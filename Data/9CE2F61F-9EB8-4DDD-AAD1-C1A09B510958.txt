2ABSTRACT
In recent years, bio-magnetic fluid dynamics (BFD) has come out to be an emerging
area in fluid mechanics investigating the fluid dynamics of bio-fluids in the presence of
magnetic field. A bio-magnetic fluid is a fluid that exists in a living creature and its
flow is influenced by the presence of a magnetic field. The most characteristic
bio-magnetic fluid is blood, which is a suspension of erythrocytes in an aqueous solution
(plasma) and since erythrocytes contain hemoglobin (a form of iron oxide) molecules,
thus it behaves as a magnetic fluid. The study of BDF has recently gained considerable
interest in view of its important applications in bio-engineering, physiological and
clinical treatments, and biomedical technology; more particularly in reducing blood flow
during surgery, separating erythrocytes (red cells) from blood, hyperthermia in treatment
of tumors/cancer therapy, and magnetic drug targeting. It is known that exchange of
heat occurs between living tissues and blood in the circulatory system. To have an in
depth knowledge for the flow and thermal behavior of bio-fluids, it is therefore worthy of
our more efforts to investigate the influence of heat transfer and magnetic field on the
bio-magnetic fluid flow.
In this study we perform an analysis of combined free and forced convection flow of
a bio-magnetic fluid over a vertical plate under the influence of a localized magnetic field,
which is generated by an electric current going through an infinite thin wire placed
parallel to the plate. Flow and heat transfer parameters, such as velocity and
temperature profiles, skin-friction coefficient, and Nusselt number are presented for
different governing parameters.
Keywords: Bio-magnetic fluid, Viscoelasticity, Magnetization, Convective heat transfer

計畫結果自評
本研究使用黏彈性流體模式描述生物磁性流體的非牛頓流動特質，探討沿著垂直板
面流動之生物流體受到導線電流所產生之感應磁場作用下的混合對流現象，建立完
整的BFD數學模式後以數值方法求解之，主要結果包括速度分佈、溫度分佈、表面
摩擦係數、紐塞爾賽等，是以可更清楚瞭解生物磁性流體在磁場作用下之熱流機制。
研究內容與原計畫相符，達成預期研究目標。本研究在醫學、生理學、及生物科技
等領域具有潛在性的應用，研究成果經整理後適合發表於國內外學術期刊。

論文摘要
Thermal Transport of an Electrically Conducting Fluid Over a
Stretching Sheet in Manufacturing Processes
Chien-Hsin Chen1, a, Y.-H. Lin2, H.-J. Lin3, Y.-R. Chen4 and Z.-N.
Chen5
1,5Department of Mechanical Design Engineering
2-4Institute of Mechanical and Electro-Mechanical Engineering
National Formosa University, Huwei, Yunlin 632, Taiwan
a E-mail: chchen@nfu.edu.tw
Abstract. The properties of the final product greatly depend on the cooling rate from
the material surface in many manufacturing processes, such as stretching sheets or
filaments that are extruded continuously from a die. Therefore, the thermal transport
behavior may play an important role in such manufacturing processes. In this paper
we present an analysis of momentum and thermal transport for the
magnetohydrodynamic (MHD) flow of an electrically conducting fluid over a
stretching sheet with prescribed surface temperature. The effects of free convection,
Joule heating and viscous dissipation are taken into consideration. The transformed
governing equations are solved numerically for this non-similar coupled flow problem.
To reveal the tendency of the solutions, typical results for velocity profiles,
temperature distributions and the local Nusselt number are presented for different
values of governing parameters.
Keywords: Manufacturing processes; MHD flow; Stretching surface; Joule heating.
