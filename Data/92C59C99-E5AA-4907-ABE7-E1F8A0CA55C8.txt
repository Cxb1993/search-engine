一. 前言 
由於衛星通訊與個人行動通訊系統需
求之快速成長，造成微波陶瓷元件如介電共
振器 (Dielectric resonator, DR) 之需求與日
俱增，典型微波介電共振器的應用包括：微
波振盪器 (Oscillator)、介電共振濾波器
(Dielectric Resonator Filter, DRF)、行動電話
及全球定位系統(Global Positioning System, 
GPS)之天線等。  
一般對於陶瓷介電共振器之材料要求
條件為高介電常數 (εr)，低損失(高Q值) 及
溫度穩定性高 (τf 幾近於零)，以結合製作
為體積小、重量輕、溫度穩定之低成本微波
濾波器。而其中，Q值為元件損耗之表現，
而在具淺力的更高頻應用時，材料的 Q值
將扮演重要之角色，故高 Q值材料的開發
顯的更為重要。 
過去在複合鈣鈦礦結構中，已有不少針
對 Ba(Co1/3Nb2/3)O3之有序化結構的觀察[1]
與微波介電性質之關聯討論[2]，而本研究主
要針對 Ba(Mg1-x/3Cox/3Nb2/3)O3 (BMCN)系
統，探討 B-site 離子有序化程度與 B-site 
離子取代時，晶體結構變化與其微波介電性
質之關係。 
二. 實驗方法 
本實驗採傳統固態反應法將 BaCO3 
(Alfa Aesar, 99.8%)、CoCO3 (Alfa Aesar, 
99.5%)、MgO (Iwantani, 99.5%) 與 Nb2O5 
(Alfa Aesar, 99.5%) 依不同化學劑量比 
(x=0, 0.1, 0.3, 0.5, 0.7, 1) 秤重，以純度
99.5%之酒精為媒介進行濕式球磨混合 6 小
時，接著以微波乾燥後，再取部份樣品粉末
利用熱差 / 熱重分析 (Differential Thermal 
Analysis / Thermo-gravimetric Analysis, 
DTA/TGA ) (Netzsch STA 409PC)，藉由吸放
熱及失重變化，決定其煅燒條件。並將各組
成 粉 末 以 每 分 鐘  10℃ 升 溫 至 
1150-1200℃，並持溫 4 小時進行煅燒，並
採自然爐冷方式降溫。隨後將煅燒粉末再以
濕式球磨以去除凝聚體。再將潤滑劑 (甘油) 
與黏結劑 (PVA, 6 wt%) 分別加入 3 wt%及
10 wt%與粉末均勻混合，然後過 50目篩並
乾燥後，以完成造粒程序。接下來再秤取各
組成之造粒粉末，以 450 MPa進行單軸壓力
成形，製得直徑：厚度為 2：1 之生坯，進
行 1400℃-1650℃持溫 4小時燒結 
性質量測方面，為將燒結體依序以
600、800、1000、1200、1500、2000與 4000
號砂紙進行表面拋光，以 Courtney 法[3]，
藉 由 網 路 分 析 儀  (network analyzer, 
HP8757D, Agilent) 配合恆溫控制器，量得
TE011 mode、TE012 mode之頻率、3dB 頻寬
及 insertion loss 等數值，輸入電腦程式以
求得其微波介電性質。 
晶體結構方面，將燒結體粉碎、研細
後，過 325目篩，然後經退火處理後，以 
0.02°/ 8 sec進行 10到 120°進行 X光繞射
實驗，並利用 Rietveld精算法以得到其晶體
結構資訊。微結構資訊則是利用 TEM觀察 
(Tecnai G2 F20)，以及  Raman光譜分析 
(Model: J. Y. Ramonor, U1000 double 
monochromator, HORIBA Jobin Yvon SAS).

計畫成果自評 
 
本計畫之主要探討方向為複合鈣鈦礦微波介電陶瓷材料的結構與性質之關聯性，對
此，本次利用 Ba(Mg1-x/3Cox/3Nb2/3)O3材料系統做為例子來研究此議題。實驗進行之方向以
及分析之方法大致皆符合計畫書所敘述，並完成了預定之目標。其中最顯著之成果即是利
用晶體結構參數 (如八面體傾斜及陽離子之有序程度) 完整的說明性質之變化 (包含了 
εr、Q*f 以及 τf值)。此重要研究結果將有助於新材料系統之開發，並可藉由事前之評估以
大幅縮短材料開發之時間。 
綜合而論，此次計畫執行成果符合原計畫書所預期，並且達成原訂之目標。這樣的研
究主要是針對基礎學術之探討，故此計畫之成果非常適合發表於國際學術期刊。

II. Experiment procedure 
The high purity TiO2 powder (99.9%, 
SBET = 143 m2/g), barium nitrate (Ba(NO3)2, 
99%, Acros), and urea (NH2CONH2, >99.5%, 
Merck) were used as the starting materials. 
First, the TiO2 powder was put in a 
polyethylene jar containing the de-ionized 
water and the yttrium-stabilized tetragonal 
zirconia polycrystals (Y-TZP) grinding balls 
(φ = 2 mm). After being milled for 4 hours, 
the TiO2 slurry was decanted from the 
polyethylene jar. Barium nitrate and urea were 
dissolved individually in the de-ionized water 
at room temperature before they were mixed 
together. Certain amount of barium nitrate, 
which was designed to form the stoichimetric 
BaTiO3 with TiO2 in later reaction, was 
dissolved in the de-ionized water. Urea was 
dissolved in de-ionized water to form 4 M 
solution. The mixture solution of barium 
nitrate and urea at various ratios were added to 
the TiO2 suspension in a rate of 2.5 ml/min. 
After that, the mixture solution was 
magnetically stirred for 10 minutes before it 
was heated to 90oC. The solution was then 
magnetically stirred at 90oC for 36h to 
precipitate the barium compound onto the 
TiO2 surface. After precipitation, the solid 
product was separated from the solution by 
filtration, and followed by a drying process in 
the oven. 
The thermal behavior of the precipitation 
powder was analyzed by differential thermal 
analysis equipped with thermal gravimeter 
(DTA/TG, STA 449C, Netzsch). A sample 
with a weight of around 50 mg was placed 
into an Al2O3 crucible pan. The measurement 
was performed in air with a flow rate of 50 
ml/min. The heating rate in the DTA 
measurement was 10oC/min and alumina 
powder was used as an inert reference 
material. The morphology of the powders was 
characterized by a transmission electron 
microscope (TEM, Tecnai G2 F20, FEI). All 
samples were examined at an accelerating 
voltage of 200 kV. The crystalline phases of 
the powders were identified by an X-ray 
diffractometer (XRD, D5000, Bruker-AXS) 
and the TEM diffraction pattern. The particle 
size distribution of BaTiO3 were use the Laser 
Diffraction Particle Size Analyzer 
(90Plus/Bi-MAS, Brookhaven instruments 
Corporation). The crystalline parameters was 
determined by the XRD pattern and software 
Cell parameter11. Raman spectrometer 
(NRS-1000, Jasco) and Fourier transformation 
infrared spectrometer (FTIR, EQUINOX 55, 
Bruker-AXS) were used to identify the trace 
of barium compound and the infrared active 
functional groups, respectively.

IV. Conclusion 
By using ultra urea, Ba(NO3)2 and TiO2 
as the starting materials, BaCO3 can be coated 
on the TiO2 surface under the experimental 
condition with urea/cation molar ratio = 30, 
reaction temperature were 90oC and the 
reaction time were 36 h. The XRD, TEM 
diffraction pattern and FTIR results showed 
that BaCO3 can be obtained from ultra urea 
and Ba(NO3)2 and without any other barium 
compound. According to the XRD pattern, the 
BaTiO3 phase can be found at 800oC for 1 h 
calcination and the BaTiO3 single phase can 
be obtained at 1000oC for 1 h calcination. The 
XRD and Raman results showed that the 
BaTiO3 were tetragonal phase and with 
tetragonality 1.0056.
