英文摘要及關鍵詞 
 
英文摘要 
 
Flash point is the most important variable used to characterize fire and explosion hazard of 
liquids. The flash point values of mixtures are not always located between those of individual 
components. It is possible to be lower than that of the lower boiling pure compound. Thus, the 
hazard of a mixture should not be evaluated by the component with the lower flash point. Several 
flash point prediction models had been proposed in the literatures for the miscible mixtures. The 
prediction capabilities of the models in literatures were compared. The results reveal that the 
models proposed by Liaw et al. are the best ones in the prediction capability, and they are 
applicable to various mixture types. 
The models, which can predict the mixtures’ flash point well, are almost based on an activity 
coefficient equation to estimate the non-ideality of liquid mixtures. However, the experimental 
binary interaction parameters necessary for these equations are not always accessible. This study 
combined the flash point prediction model proposed previously with the UNIFAC-type models, 
which do not need the experimental binary parameters, to predict the flash point of miscible 
mixtures. The validation of this combined model is done by comparing the predicted values with the 
experimental data for twenty four flammable solvents and aqueousorganic binary and ternary 
mixtures, ideal mixtures as well as Raoult’s law negative or positive deviation mixtures. All the 
binary-mixture types, which are known to date, have been included in the validated samples. 
Results reveal that the model describes the experimental data well when using the UNIFAC-type 
models to estimate the liquid phase activity coefficient. Overall, UNIFAC-Dortmund is the most 
recommended equation for applying to the model to estimate the flash point of miscible mixtures, 
due to its good predictive capability and more completed database of binary interaction parameters. 
Potential application for this approach concerns the classification of flammable liquid mixtures in 
the implementation of GHS. 
 
Keywords: Flash point; Minimum flash point solution; Maximum flash point solution; Flash point 
prediction Model; UNIFAC; Miscible mixtures

包括閉杯法(closed cup test)和開杯法(open cup test)兩種[7]，常用的標準測試方法包括：

其中開杯法所測得的閃火點通常較閉杯法所測得的值為高[7]。 
由於物質的閃火點早已成為國內外物質安全資料表(Material Safety Data Sheet,簡稱
MSDS)的重要安全資訊之一，因此，一般純物質的閃火點可由物質安全資料表直接查得，也
因此，當 GHS 實際實施時，易燃液體中的純物質於危害分類中不會有多大的問題。但對於混
合物，其閃火點隨著組成的改變而改變，而文獻上的資料卻又非常缺乏，即使是物質安全資
料表上所提供的資料，也僅為某特定組成下的數據。傳統上，會以閃火點測試儀來測試混合
物的閃火點，在台灣，以閃火點測試儀測試的成本很高，以工研院能環所的報價為例，測試
一個樣品的價格為新台幣兩萬元，而同一種混合物，工業上應用的濃度範圍往往很廣，若欲
以閃火點測試儀測試以求得完整的數據，將花費大量的金額，針對常用混合物化學品的危害
測試，歐盟估算約須花費 1.2-2.1 億萬歐元，花費 6 年時間才能完成。因此，若能有一閃火點
預測模式，則只需少許的基本數據即可快速有效而便宜的預測不同溶液及不同組成下的溶液
閃火點。 
由於利用閃火點測試儀測試取得閃火點數據所需價格很高，許多的閃火點預測模式紛紛
被提出來，尤其是互溶系統[1-3, 8-16]。以理想溶液為假設的預測模式於預測非理想溶液時往
往會出現預測不準的現象[8-11]；而利用活性係數來處理溶液的非理想性一般預測能力較強
[1-3, 12, 13, 15, 16]，後者往往利用熱力學模式來處理氣液平衡，但這些模式所用的活性係數
方程式如 NRTL、Wilson、UNIQUAC、Van Laar、Margulas 均需要相平衡的數據以迴歸其所
需參數，很不幸，由於溶液的種類繁多，這些參數往往無法有效的取得。相較之下，UNIFAC 
(Universal Quasi-chemical Functional Group Activity Coefficient)模式則不需要額外的氣液平衡
的數據迴歸求相關參數[17, 18]，UNIFAC 方程式的參數包括混合物個別分子的凡得瓦耳體積
(measure of molecular van der Waals volume), ri,、分子表面積(measure of molecular surface areas), 
qi,和 group interaction parameters, amn，這些參數已由文獻中大量的實驗數據迴歸得到。 
原始的 UNIFAC 方程式包括 Combinatorial 和 Residual 項。為了對氣液平衡、液液平衡
和過量焓(excess enthalpy)得到較佳的預測，一些對 Combinatorial 和 Residual 項作修正的模式
紛紛被提出來[1924]，如 UNIFAC-Dortmund、UNIFAC-Lyngby、Bastos’ modified UNIFAC。 
Gmehling 和 Rasmussen (1982)為最早利用 UNIFAC 模式來估算液相活性係數以求得混
合物的閃火點[15]，其模擬的混合物包括僅含易燃性物質的混合物：chloroform 與 methyl 
ethyl ketone 或 methyl acetate 混合和甲醇、乙醇、丙醇水溶液，然而，卻未包括極端閃火點
混合物(含最低閃火點溶液和最高閃火點溶液)[14, 25-28]。Vidal et al. (2006)結合廖等人的閃火
點預測模式和 UNIFAC 模式來預測高度非理想溶液,辛烷+乙醇和辛烷+1-丁醇,的最低閃火點
行為和乙醇水溶液的閃火點[25]。於上述兩份文獻，只有測試了原始 UNIFAC 模式，而沒有
比較其他的 UNIFAC 型式模式，同時，所考慮的系統未包括高閃火點溶液。 
由學術網路 Scopus 的統計結果，計畫主持人所提的混合溶液閃火點預測模式為文獻中

二、研究方法 
 
2.1 計畫執行 
第一年期計畫中對 GHS 推動中，易燃性液體分類中會造成執行人員觀念上混淆的極端
閃火點行為進行文獻回顧，並針對文獻上現有的混合物閃火點預測模式進行回顧，同時，以
實驗數據比較文獻上所提模式的預測能力。混合物的種類比較了由易燃性溶劑組合成的雙成
份溶液、雙成份有機水溶液、由易燃性溶劑組合成的三成份溶液、三成份有機水溶液。 
第二、三年期計畫則分別以互溶系統中的雙成份混合溶液和三成份混合溶液為對象，結
合之前申請人所發展的閃火點預測模式和估算混合溶液活性系數的 UNIFAC 方程式，發展不
需實驗雙成份參數的閃火點預測模式。 
 
2.2 實驗 
以 Walter Herzog GmbH 所製之閃火點測試儀,HFP 362-Tag,來量測溶液的閃火點，閃火
點測試儀利用控制裝置於接近預測閃火點附近以一特定的加熱速率來加熱樣本，在指定的溫
度區間(測試區間)，閃火點測試儀會使用點火頭自動測試。如果預期閃火點低於或等於變化溫
度(change temperature)(依 ASTM D56 的標準變化溫度取 60 oC)，則以 1 oC/min 的加熱速率加
熱，點火頭在測試區間-1 點火。如果預期閃火點比較高，則以 3 oC/min 的加熱速率加熱，點
火頭在測試間隔-2 點火。當溫度值等於預期閃火點減去起始測試溫度值開始第一次點火，當
測試溫度值超過預期閃火點加上結束測試溫度值，若仍測不到閃火點，則加熱器停止。所用
的標準測試方法為ASTM D56[29]，ASTM D56所選用的參數數值如下：起始測試溫度(start test)

三、結果與討論 
 
3.1 液體混合溶液的極端閃火點行為 
由閃火點的定義[7]，閃火點和物質的蒸氣壓有很大的關係。對於理想溶液，混合物的閃
火點會如一般人所預期位於個別成份之間且平穩的變化(如辛烷(octane)和庚烷(heptane)的混
合物，見圖 1)。但對於非理想溶液，混合溶液的閃火點則不再是平穩的變化，以正丁醇加入
正辛烷為例，混合溶液的閃火點幾乎不變，一直到正丁醇為主要成份時閃火點才明顯上升
[30]。即將少量的高閃火點物質加入一低閃火點物質中，混合溶液的閃火點幾乎不變；但若是
將少量的低閃火點的物質加入一高閃火點的物質中，混合溶液的閃火點會快速的下降。正丁
醇與正辛烷混合物的這種行為，其原因有二，一為正丁醇的燃燒下限較正辛烷為高；一為正
丁醇與正辛烷的混合溶液其行為會正偏差於拉午耳定率(Raoult’s law)[30]。

合溶液的閃火點不可能比個別成份中閃火點最低的成份其閃火點還低，這是二十世紀末國際

圖 3 甲醇(1)+乙酸甲酯(2)混合物的閃火點變化 
 
相對於最低閃火點溶液，廖等人發現環已醇+酚、環已酮+酚、3-甲基吡啶(p-picoline)+
酚、酚+苯乙酮(acetophenone)會表現出最高閃火點行為(Maximum flash point behavior)，即於
某個組成範圍內，該混合溶液的閃火點會比個別成份的閃火點還高[27]。上述混合溶液的特殊
行為並可由廖等人所提的閃火點預測模式[1]預測得到，廖等人將表現出這種特殊行為行為的
混合溶液稱為最高閃火點溶液(Maximum flash-point solution) [27]。上述四種最高閃火點溶液
的溶液行為均高度負偏移於理想溶液，其活性係數均(activity coefficient)極小於 1，此種行為
提升了該溶液的閃火點[27]。雖然，最高閃火點溶液均為負偏差溶液，但並非所有的負偏差溶
液均會形成最高閃火點溶液，環己銨(cyclohexylamine)+ 環已醇和丙醛(propionic aldehyde)+2-
丁酮即為負偏差溶液但卻非最高閃火點溶液的例子[27]。最高閃火點溶液的行為有可能能應用
於危害消減，以 3-甲基吡啶(p-picoline)為例，其閃火點值為 42.5ºC，小於 60.5ºC，依 DOT 
(department of transportation)的規定[6]，運輸時須加以包裝，但加了酚後，於 p-picoline 的莫

四、結論 
於文獻中不同閃火點預測模式的預測能力比較，顯示廖等人所提的模式為現有文獻中預
測能力較強的模式，且其適用的混合物類型較多，可應用於由易燃性溶劑組合成的雙成份溶
液、雙成份有機水溶液、由易燃性溶劑組合成的三成份溶液、三成份有機水溶液。因此，於
GHS 的推動過程中，建議採用廖等人所提的模式協助混合溶液的閃火點估計。 
針對互溶混合溶液，含雙成份、三成份溶液、僅含易燃性物質的混合物和有機水溶液，
UNIFAC 型式模式結合先前計畫主持人所發展的閃火點預測模式可有效的預測溶液的閃火點
數據。對於某些混合物，以 UNIFAC 型式模式為基礎的模式，其預測結果甚至優於以
NRTL/Wilson 模式為基礎的模式，計畫中所考慮的混合物已包括極端閃火點混合物(含最低閃
火點溶液和最高閃火點溶液)。基於 UNIFAC 型式模式不需要實驗雙成份作用參數，此結合
UNIFAC 型式模式和先前計畫主持人所發展的閃火點預測模式可應用於 GHS 的推動中，協助
易燃液體混合物的分類。

3.3  Binary AqueousOrganic Mixtures 
The measured flash points of binary aqueousorganic solution, water + ethanol, was plotted against the predictive curves of the 
curves depicted in Fig.4. It can be clearly seen that the predictive values based upon an ideal solution assumption are somewhat 
larger than the corresponding experimentally-derived data for the composition of aqueousorganic solutions apart from the case 
when the water concentration is zero, although the method is able to predict the experimental analogues successfully when the 
composition of water approaches zero. This deviation arises from the fact that these aqueousorganic solutions reveal a positive 
deviation from that of an ideal solution [2], with such behavior resulting in a reduction of the solution’s flash point from the predicted 
analogue for an ideal solution. By contrast, although there are differences between the predicted values of flash point, when using the 
NRTL or different UNIFAC-type model to estimate activity coefficients, the experimental results appear to be in much better 
agreement with such predictive curves than is the case for the analogue based upon an ideal solution assumption (Fig.4). The 
predictive curve arrived at using the NRTL equation is, as expected, in best agreement with the experimental data, with the sets of 
binary-parameter values used being selected from several ones of our previous study [2] to give the best prediction. The predictions 
based on the UNIFAC-type models, except for Bastos et al.’s modified UNIFAC one, were all in good agreement with the 
measurements, although these predictions were not as good as that based on NRTL equation. 
Overall, the prediction results of the flash-point prediction model based upon either of the UNIFAC-type models, except for 
Bastos et al.’s modified UNIFAC one, appear to agree well with the measured flash points corresponding to an aqueousorganic 
solution, especially for the model based on UNIFAC-Dortmund gave best predictions for the studied aqueousorganic mixtures.

A Model to Estimate the Inert Dilution Effect on the Flammability Limits 
LIAW Horngjang
1
, CHEN Chancheng
2
 & CHANG Chihheng
1 
(1 Department of Occupational Safety and Health, China Medical University, Taichung, Taiwan, China; 
2 Department of Safety, Health and Environmental Engineering, National Kaohsiung First University of Science and Technology, 
Kaohsiung, Taiwan, China) 
 
Abstract: Flammability limits are the most important variable used to assess fire and explosion hazard of gases and vapors. Addition of inert to a combustible 
gas is usually used to reduce the hazard of gas explosion. A model based on energy balance and adiabatic flame temperature being constant was developed for 
estimating the flammability limits of fuel–air–diluent mixtures tested at constant pressure condition. The validation of the model was done by comparing the 
estimated values with the experimental data for methane, ethylene, propane, propylene, isobutene, methyl formate with nitrogen and carbon dioxide as inert. 
Overall, the model describes the experimental data well. 
Keywords: flammability limits; inert dilution effect; adiabatic flame temperature; explosion 
 
1  Introduction 
Flammability limits is the limited range of composition a flammable gas can burn in air[1]. Below a certain concentration of the 
flammable gas, the lower flammability limit (LFL), the mixture is too “lean”; while above a certain concentration, the upper 
flammability limit (UFL), it is too “rich”. The LFLs and UFLs are also sometimes called, respectively, the lower and upper explosive 
limits (LELs and UELs). They are distinct from the detonability limits[1]. Flammability limits is one of the most important variables 
used to evaluate the fire and explosion hazard of gases and vapors. 
Flammability limits are affected by the exist of an inert gas such as nitrogen, carbon dioxide or steam [1]. Thus, addition of inert 
to a flammable gases or vapors is always used in the real process to prevent the fire and explosion hazard of gases and vapors. 
Kondo et al. modified the Le Chatlier’s rule with an empirical equation to describe the variation of flammability limits, LFL and 
UFL, with the quantity of inert added to the mixture, and estimate the unknown parameters by fitting with experimental data  [2-4]. 
Adiabatic flame temperature being constant is the frequently used concept to estimate the flammability limits. However, the literature 
studies by use of this method almost concentrate in the LFL due to the complexity of the UFL behavior. Melhem estimate the 
mixtures flammability limits by the assumption of the gas composition attending the chemical equilibrium after the explosion [5]. 
However, there are deviations between the estimation and the corresponding experimental data and the adiabatic flame temperature is 
required when using Melhem’s method. In our previous studies, the linear relationship between the LFL/UFL and inert was derived, 
and such an equation can describe the experimental data over the entire blend composition range except for the region around the 
minimum oxygen concentration [6,7]. However, the slope of the previously derived linear relationship was determined by the curve 
fitting of the experimental data. This study intends to derive a model for describing the inert dilution effect on the flammability limits, 
and predict this effect only by the basic thermodynamic data, then, validated with corresponding experimental data.

3  Results and Discussion 
3.1  Parameters Used in This Manuscript 
The proposed model of inert-gas dilution effect on flammability limit was used for methane, ethylene, propane, propylene, 
isobutene and methyl formate with inert gases, nitrogen and carbon monoxide, and validated against the corresponding experimental 
data. The values of heat of formation and heat capacity for the studied components were adopted from the literature [8,9]. The values 
of heat of formation for compounds used in this study adopted from two different sources of databank [8,9] were the same. However, 
the dependence of heat capacity with temperature from two different sources of databank [8,9] were different, and the two different 
formula were both used in this study for comparison. The flammability limit, UFL and LFL, for the pure substances used in this study 
were sourced from literatures [3,4], which tested in a spherical glass flask. 
3.2  Nitrogen dilution effect on flammability limits for methane 
Fig.1 compared the experimental data of nitrogen dilution effect on flammability limits adopted from Kondo et al. [2] and 
estimated analogues, based upon two different dependent formula of heat capacity on temperature, for methane. The experimental 
data appear to be in consistent with the two predictions, although there are differences between the predicted values, when using two 
different heat capacity formulas. 
The estimated values of LFL were almost overlapped with each other, when using the two different heat capacity equations, and 
the predicted behavior of almost constant value in LFL is in excellent agreement with the corresponding experimental data, except 
for the region approaching the intersection point of LFL and UFL, where the experimental LFL value increased slightly. In the 
estimation of LFL variation with inert, the assumption of complete conversion of the fuel into carbon dioxide was used. However, the 
fact is that there are some fuels convert to carbon monoxide because the ratio of oxygen to fuel is only slightly greater than the 
stoichiometric ratio in the region approaching the intersection point of LFL and UFL. Since the value of heat of reaction for a fuel 
converts to carbon monoxide is less than that convert to carbon dioxide, more fuel is required to keep the adiabatic flame temperature 
to be constant, and that result in the increase of LFL. 
The intersection point of LFL and UFL estimated by the two different heat capacity equation are almost overlapped with each 
other, with value of xIL =0.909 (xL =0.091), and it is only slightly greater than the experimental-derived value, 0.882 (xL =0.118). 
It was demonstrated in Fig. 1 that the estimated UFL were less than the corresponding experimental data substantially for the 
mole ratio of the inert in the blended gas greater than xU, if neglecting Eq. (32). However, this deviation improved remarkably when 
the effect of the fuel converting to carbon dioxide (Eq. (32)) was taken into account, with the prediction by use of the parameters of 
Ref. [8] superior to that of Ref. [9]. In our previous studies [6,7], the inconsistent result of our estimation with the corresponding 
experimental data nearing the FIP may be attributed to the failure of our previous assumption due to the produce of carbon dioxide in 
the UFL for the mole ratio of the inert in the blended gas greater than xU. 
3.3  Nitrogen dilution effect on flammability limits for other hydrocarbons 
The experimental data of nitrogen dilution effect on flammability limits of other hydrocarbons, ethylene, propane, propylene, 
isobutene and methyl formate, adopted from Kondo [2] were plotted against the predictive curves of the curves depicted in Figures 2-6. 
It can be clearly seen that the experimental results appear to be in good agreement with such predictive curves, although there are 
differences between the predicted values, when using different heat capacity equation. 
Vidal et al pointed that the predicted results gave the best agreement with the existing experimental data for methane and 
ethylene, when the value around 1400 K was selected as the adiabatic flame temperature [10]. The calculated adiabatic flame 
temperature for methane and ethylene at LFL in this study is 1470 K and 1393 K, respectively, which values are close to the

4  Conclusions 
This paper attempts to model the inert dilution effect on the flammability limits by use of energy balance equation. The LFL 
variation with the quantity of inert is different for different inert, with almost remain constant for nitrogen, and increase for carbon 
monoxide. This different behavior is attributed to the similar value of heat capacity for nitrogen and oxygen and different value of 
heat capacity for carbon monoxide and oxygen. Overall, the proposed model can describe the flammability limits variation with 
quantity of inert, except for the region around the point of MOC. This can be attributed to the failure in assumption of the model 
around such a point.
