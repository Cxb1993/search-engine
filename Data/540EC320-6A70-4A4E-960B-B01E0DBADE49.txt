This project is the third year of the cyro-therapy of nasopharyngeal carcinoma ( NPC) 
research sponsored by NSC. In the first year of this project, a computer code was developed to 
simulate the freezing processes of cells. In addition, experiment was conducted to measure the 
cyro-parameters of articular cartilage chondrocytes of hogs. The research results were published 
in the journal of ?Cryobiology?. In the second year, a cryo-biochip was developed and its cyro 
characteristics were investigated. Based on third-year research planning of this project, we have 
conducted a literature survey on the cryo-therapy and biological characteristics of cells under freezing 
temperature. Experiments based on the Taquchi method were conducted to investigate the effects of 
freezing parameters and the optimal freezing temperature history. Results showed that the end 
temperature had the strongest effect of the viability of the NPC cells. Based on the ANOVA 
analysis, the optimal freezing parameters in this work were: low thawing rate (4.26?0.6?/min), 
low end temperature (-80?), long hold time (15 minutes), and fast cooling rate (13.62?2.17?
/min). In terms of the effect of passage on the viability of NPC cells, the normalized viability is 
19.38?0.03%?17.55?0.04%, and 17.28?0.04% for the 9th, 10th and 11th passages, respectively. 
The small difference in the viability for different passages of NPC cells indicated the negligible 
effect of passage on the viability.
