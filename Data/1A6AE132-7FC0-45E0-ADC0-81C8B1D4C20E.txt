Abstract 
Counter weight imposed on a machine tool can both equilibrate the weight of machine head 
and delete the effect of the gravitational acceleration, g, when the head moves along the Z axis up 
and down, and a stable acceleration value set on the cutting tool path may be ascertained. However, 
the elastic jump due to the effect of the inertia of counter weight and potential energy released 
from the connected chain as the machine head makes a suddenly stop during the machining of a 
vertical-like down or up surface and subsequently a flat plane. This harmonic vibration energy 
may transfer to the machine head inducing the vibration on the cutting tool and leaving some wavy 
marks on the machined surface. 
A numerical simulation for dynamic analysis of the tool vibration induced from the movement 
of counter weight in the equilibrium of Z axis machine head of vertical machine tool will be 
performed in this project. The improvement strategies for the induced tool vibration are through 
the hardware components addition. A dynamic analysis numerical code for mechanism is utilized
