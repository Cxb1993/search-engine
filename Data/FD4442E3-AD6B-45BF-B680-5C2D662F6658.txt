中文摘要 
本計畫主要在探討氣致色變材料製程的開發及其性能研究，並延伸應用至氫氣感
測器之製備。實驗規劃主要是以 sol-gel 法及 spin-coating 技術製備 WO3 及多孔性 WO3
氣致色變鍍膜，催化劑則採用表面濺鍍 Pt 薄膜方式進行研究比較。本計畫主要是利用
類似電致色變鍍層的變色機制，以氫氣取代電致色變機制中的電位，還原氣致色變鍍
層而變色，進而利用變色程度的大小來檢測氫氣的濃度。本計畫藉由製程條件的研究
及多孔性氣致色變鍍膜的改造，提升氣致色變材料在氫氣感測性能上的變色反應速率
及靈敏度，並開發具有 100ppm~100% H2 氣檢測能力的氫氣感測器，同時也建立氣致
色變機制及 H2 氣在 WO3 膜中之質傳數學模式。  
關鍵詞： WO3、氣致色變、氫氣感測器 
Abstract 
In this research project we studied the preparation and charaterization of the 
gasochromic films and their application in the hydrogen sensor. Sol-gel method and 
spin-coating technique were adopted to prepare the WO3 and porous WO3 thin films as the 
gasochromic materials. Sputtered Pt was used as a thin film catalyst. The gasochromic 
device can be considered as a counterpart of the electrochromic device. With the exception 
that reducing gas, say H2, is used in the gasochromic device instead of electric potential in 
the electrochromic device to color the film. By measuring the degree of color change of the 
gasochromic device, we can detect H2 concentration in the gas without applying any electric 
potential. In the study, a prototype gasochromic hydrogen sensor with 100ppm~100% H2 
detection capability was developed and its gasochromism and mass transfer model was also 
extensively studied. Hopefully, the results of this project will be beneficial to the 
development of an efficient gasochromic hydrogen sensor with fast response rate and high 
sensitivity. 
Keywords: WO3, gasochromic, hydrogen sensor 
1. 前言 
由於氫氣具有潔淨無污染、永續供應、以及高熱能值等三項特質，且氫氣在燃燒
使用時只會產生水蒸氣，並無污染之虞，因此被譽稱為真正的「綠色能源」。因此，氫
氣能源被認為是未來能源的主流，所有已開發國家無不積極投入氫能平台技術的研
發，在日漸蓬勃的全球能源市場，誰能掌握氫能的關鍵技術相信將具有絕對的優勢以
及主導權。對於能源輸入達 90%以上的台灣而言，加速氫能的開發，將有助於國家能
源安全政策、降低溫室氣體之排放、以及提升台灣經濟與產業之國際競爭力。由於氫
氣的爆炸濃度範圍很廣約在 4%~80%，因此在氫氣的生產及使用環境中氫氣的洩漏安
全性問題更顯得十分重要，此意謂在氫能技術的研究發展工作中，以低成本量產製造
能檢測低濃度、高效率、及快速響應的氫氣感測器的必要性與急迫性。目前氫氣感測
器的類型主要有固態電解質式[1-3]、電化學式[4]、及半導體式[5-9]等多種。這些傳統
的氫氣感測器係將偵測到的氫氣濃度轉變成電的信號輸出，進行量化檢測，這些類型
的氫氣感測器主要是利用電位、電流、或電阻的變化原理作為信號的輸出，通常需要

迄今為止，對於氫氣引起 WO3 薄膜變色的機制主要有 3 種模型，即能級模型、價間躍
遷模型、和色心模型等，這些理論都只能從各自的角度部份地解釋 WO3 薄膜的氣致變
色現象，因此目前還沒有得到一種被統一認可的模型可以解釋所有的氣致變色機制，
至於對 sol-gel WO3 薄膜的氣致色變機制的探討更是缺乏。WO3 薄膜的氣致變色現象是
一個十分複雜的反應過程，不同製程得到的 WO3 薄膜因為晶相結構與 O/W 化學計量
不盡相同，其氣致色變機制也不盡相同，因此 WO3 薄膜的氣致變色機制顯然還有待進
一步研究。因此本計畫根據實驗研究的結果建立一套 sol-gel WO3 薄膜的氣致色變機
制，同時應用化工反應動力學及輸送現象原理探討 WO3 薄膜氣致色變的質傳現象，建
立質傳理論模式，定量描述 H2 氣在 WO3 薄膜內的質傳速率及氣致色變現象。 
4. 研究方法 
本計畫以 sol-gel 法及 spin-coating 技術製備 WO3 鍍膜，採用 W 金屬或 WCl6 作為
製備 WO3 鍍膜的前驅物，以適當組成調配成鍍液後，利用 spin-coating 技術塗佈於玻
璃片上，然後控制在 100~500℃等不同的煅燒溫度下形成 WO3 鍍膜，探討煅燒溫度對
WO3 鍍膜變色性能的影響。WO3 鍍膜的晶型結構及厚度分析，則利用 XRD、SEM 及
TEM 等儀器檢測獲得。 
為了製備多孔性的 WO3 鍍膜，本計畫採用奈米聚苯乙烯(PS)球、界面活性劑、或
其他有機物作為模板，將適量的 PS 球、界面活性劑、或其他有機物加入 WO3 的前驅
物溶液裡，然後以 sol-gel 法及 spin-coating 技術塗佈於玻璃片上。再利用高溫煅燒去除
PS 球、界面活性劑、及其他有機物而形成多孔性的 WO3 鍍膜。其中，PS 球的製備實
驗，是採用無乳化聚合的方式，分別以 KPS (potassium persulfate)及 AIBA (2,2,azobis 
(2-methylpropion amidine) dihydrochloride)兩種不同之起始劑進行合成反應，控制溫度
在 70~80℃，利用不同的反應條件及反應時間，分別製備出 50~400nm 等不同粒徑大小
之 PS 球作為模板，並探討其對多孔性 WO3 鍍膜變色性能的影響。PS 球粒徑大小之檢
測分析，利用雷射粒徑分析儀、SEM 及 TEM 分析計算得到。 
Pt/WO3 及多孔性 Pt/WO3 氣致變色元件對氫氣感測性能的測試，首先控制 H2 及
N2 氣的流量大小比率，調配不同濃度的 H2 氣通入置放 Pt/WO3 或多孔性 Pt/WO3 氣致
變色元件的反應槽中，再利用 UV/VIS 光譜儀以 400~1100nm 可見光波段測量鍍膜變色
前後之穿透率及反射率的變化量進行分析比較。 
至於氣致色變膜的質傳數學模式，本計畫根據化學平衡理論建構的氣致色變機
制，結合輸送現象的質傳理論及化工反應動力學原理，建立 H2 在 WO3 氣致色變膜中
著色、去色的的質傳數學模式，然後再根據建構的質傳機構模型所推導的相關聯立偏
微分方程式，以 Comsol 套裝軟體進行數值分析求解，探討變色過程中光學穿透率受
H2 氣濃度、WO3 膜厚度、以及 Pt 催化劑厚度變化的影響關係。

結論與建議 
本計畫利用 sol-gel 法及 spin-coating 技術製備多孔性的 WO3 薄膜，再利用表面濺
鍍 Pt 作為催化劑製成 Pt/WO3 氣致色變元件，成功開發穩定性佳、響應速率快、並具
有 100ppm~100% H2 氣檢測能力的氫氣感測器。另外，本計畫為了能定量瞭解著色與
去色的響應速率大小及穿透率變化情況，也根據輸送現象的質傳理論結合化工反應動
力學及化學平衡理論，建立氣致色變機制及 H2 氣在 WO3 膜中之質傳數學模式，成功
描述氣致色變元件在變色過程中著色與去色反應穿透率的變化現象。另外，在本計畫
研究過程中我們也發現堆積整齊的 PS 球可顯著提高氣致色變元件的反射率，因此未來
將規劃開發光子晶體結構的氣致色變元件，相信將可大幅提升元件對 100ppm 以下 H2
氣濃度的檢測能力。

In Georg’s group, extensive studies had been conducted to support
their proposed oxygen vacancy model to explain the gasochromic
mechanism of the DC-sputtered and e-beam evaporated WO3
ﬁlms [1,2,4,8,10,29].  Despite there is still no common concept
describing the mechanism of gasochromic coloration in sput-
tered or evaporated WO3 ﬁlms, most reported gasochromic studies
gave consistently experimental evidence to support the validity of
W6+/W5+ model in the sol–gel derived WO3 ﬁlms [14,17–20].
In the double injection model, it is known that the degree of
maximum absorption of blue tungsten bronze (HxWO3) is closely
related to the amount of hydrogen insertion coefﬁcient (x), which
is proportional to the concentration of colored center and usually
varies with the ﬁlm’s structure, morphology and crystallinity. How-
ever, very few studies have been found to discuss the correlation
between the hydrogen insertion coefﬁcient and the gasochromic
coloration of WO3 ﬁlms. Inouye et al. [30] reported that when the
sample became colored, the hydrogen concentration in their RF-
sputtered WO3 ﬁlm increased from 0.24 H/W (as-prepared state)
to 0.47 H/W (colored state) and the crystalline structure of the
ﬁlm changed from monoclinic to tetragonal. Chen et al. [36] stud-
ied the gasochromic effect of WO3 nanowire ﬁlm and indicated
that the gasochromism is caused by hydrogen injection, rather
than the oxygen vacancies. Furthermore, the injected hydrogen will
change the tungsten ion’s valences from +6 to +5, as well as the lat-
tice structure of the ﬁlms from the monoclinic phase of WO3 to
the tetragonal phase of H0.23WO3. Orel et al. [37] also found that
the gasochromic effect of sol–gel mesoporous WO3 ﬁlms lead to
the formation of tetragonal H0.19WO3 bronze, which differed with
respect to those colored electrochemically where the cubic HxWO3
phase is formed at x ≥ 0.21. In this work, WO3 ﬁlms were prepared
by the sol–gel spin coating technique. To obtain the informa-
tion of hydrogen insertion coefﬁcient in the gasochromic tungsten
bronze (HxWO3), the chemical reaction equilibrium approach was
adopted for the ﬁrst time, to the best of our knowledge, to
study the correlation between the hydrogen insertion coefﬁcient
and the gasochromic coloration of WO3 ﬁlms. The inﬂuence of
annealing temperature on the gasochromic property was also
discussed.
2. Experimentals
All chemicals were of reagent grade and used as received. WO3
ﬁlms were prepared via the sol–gel spin coating method. Precur-
sor solutions for WO3 ﬁlms were prepared by mixing tungsten
hexachloride (Strem Chemicals, Inc., USA, grade: 99.9% W)  with
anhydrous ethyl alcohol (Showa Chemicals Co., Japan, grade: 99.5%)
as previously reported [38]. Silica glass plates (with a dimension
of 2.5 cm × 2.5 cm)  were used as the substrates. All glasses were
cleaned with detergent soap, alcohol and de-ionized water before
coating. WO3 coatings were obtained by spin coating technique
and the process was repeated up to seven times to obtain the
ﬁlm thickness of about 700 nm.  The as-spin-coated ﬁlms were
dried under room temperature for 5 min  and then annealed in
air under 100–400 ◦C for 1 h to obtain the gasochromic ﬁlms. The
gasochromic device was then prepared by sputtering a layer of plat-
inum (Pt) over the WO3 ﬁlm. The thickness was estimated about
15 nm.  All experiments were performed under room temperature.
The surface morphology and thickness of Pt/WO3 ﬁlms were
examined by ﬁeld-emission scanning electron microscope (FE-
SEM) with energy dispersive spectrometer (FESEM-EDS, HITACHI
S4800, Japan). The crystal structure and phase identiﬁcation of the
ﬁlms were investigated by X-ray diffractometry (XRD, Bruker AXS
D8 DISCOVER Advance Diffractometer, Germany, Cu K radiation)
with a grazing incidence of angle 1◦. The accelerating voltage and
the applied current were 40 kV and 300 mA,  respectively. The col-
oration changes of the gasochromic device under various H2–N2
gas mixtures at ambient temperature were detected by UV–vis
spectrophotometer (BAL2000, Ocean Optics, Inc., USA).
3. Results and discussion
Fig. 1 gives the typical FE-SEM images of the sol–gel spin coating
WO3 ﬁlms treated with different annealing temperatures. It shows
that the ﬁlm had a uniform porous structure and was  formed of
nanoparticles with a grain size about ∼15 nm.  The thickness of all
ﬁlms prepared in this study was also estimated from the cross-
section images of SEM micrographs. It is found that the thickness
of WO3 ﬁlm changes linearly with the number of coating layer, as
shown in Fig. 2. To obtain the gasochromic device, a thin ﬁlm of Pt
catalyst was deposited over the WO3 ﬁlm with the sputtering time
of 20 s. The thickness of Pt ﬁlm estimated from the cross-section
image of SEM micrograph is about 15 nm.
X-ray diffraction (XRD) pattern of the prepared WO3 ﬁlms is
shown in Fig. 3. It can be observed that, below 200 ◦C, the XRD
exhibits a broad peak amorphous in nature while several distinc-
tive WO3 peaks can be found at 2 of 23.2◦, 23.6◦, 24.3◦, 26.6◦, 28.0◦,
28.8◦, 33.4◦, 34.0◦, 36.8◦, 41.6◦, 47.3◦, 49.8◦, 50.5◦ and 55.7◦ corre-
sponding to the triclinic phase (0 0 2), (0 2 0), (2 0 0), (1 2 0), (1¯ 1 2),
(1¯ 2 1), (2¯ 0 2), (2¯ 2 0), (1 2¯ 2), (2 2 2), (0 0 4), (1¯ 4 0), (1 1 4), and (1 4 2)
crystal planes (JCPDS 20–1323), respectively, at 300–400 ◦C. The
WO3 grains have a size of approximately 14 nm calculated accord-

coloring/bleaching processes was very fast and the time constants
(c and b) were less than 5 s.

Chih-Chieh Chan has been a professor at Feng Chia University since 1987. He
received his BS degree in chemical engineering in 1975 and MS  degree in 1977
from  National Central University and National Tsing Hua University, respectively. He
served as a chemical engineer in China Man-Made Fiber Corp. from 1979 to 1981 and
Taiwan Fertilizer Company from 1982 to 1984. He received Ph.D. degree in chemical
engineering in 1987 from National Tsing Hua University. His current research inter-
ests  include development of gasochromic and electrochromic devices, synthesis of
photocatalysts and nanoparticles, and preparation of colloidal photonic crystals.
Wen-Chia Hsu received his BS and MS  degrees in chemical engineering in 2003 and
2005 from Southern Taiwan University of Technology and Ph.D. degree in chemi-
cal  engineering from Feng Chia University in 2009. His research interests include
development of gasochromic and electrochromic devices.
Chung-Chieh Chang received his BS degree in chemical engineering in 2001 from
Tamkang University and Ph.D. degree in chemical engineering in 2007 from Feng
Chia University. Now he is a postdoctoral researcher in Institute of Physics, Academia
Sinica, Taiwan. His research interests include development of gas sensors, synthesis
of photocatalysts and nanoparticles.
Chao-Sheng Hsu received his BS degree in chemical engineering in 2001 from

ical  engineering in 2010 from Feng Chia University. His research interests include
development of gasochromic and electrochromic devices.
