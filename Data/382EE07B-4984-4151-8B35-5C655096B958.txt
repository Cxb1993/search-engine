雙重吸收微波效應下對於甲醇蒸氣重組反應產氫之影響 
Effect of microwave double absorption on hydrogen generation from methanol 
steam reforming 
 
計畫編號：NSC 99－2221－E－024－011 
執行期限：99年 8月 1日至 100年 7月 31日 
主持人：陳維新   國立台南大學 綠色能源科技學系 
Email：vwhchen@gmail.com 
 
 
1. Abstract  
Hydrogen generation from methanol 
steam reforming (MSR) with a 
CuO/ZnO/Al2O3 catalyst was investigated in 
the study; particular emphasis was placed on 
the reactions of MSR exposed to an 
environment with microwave irradiation. By 
virtue of the double absorption of 
microwaves by both the reagents and the 
catalyst, the experiments suggested that the 
MSR could be heated and triggered rapidly 
within a short time, and the methanol 
conversion from MSR with microwave 
heating was high compared to that with 
conventional heating. The obtained results 
also indicated that, when the reaction 
temperature was as high as 250°C, 
thermodynamic equilibrium governed the 
MSR, whereas the reaction was kinetically 
controlled for the temperature lower than 
250°C. Contrary to Le Châtelier's principle, it 
was noted that an increase in S/C ratio 
decreased methanol conversion. This can be 
explained by the fact that water absorbs 
microwave irradiation stronger than methanol. 
The performance of the MSR was evaluated 
based on the carbon conservation method and 
the nitrogen tracer method. It was found that 
the latter was also capable of providing an 
accurate prediction on methanol conversion, 
even though the flow rate of the product gas 
was not measured. 
Keywords: Microwave irradiation and 
heating; Methanol steam reforming (MSR); 
Hydrogen generation and production; 
Catalyst; Double absorption; Nitrogen 
tracer method.

3.2. Experimental procedure 
The experimental procedure is also 
sketched in Fig. 1. According to the operated 
condition of gas hourly space velocity for the 
reactants in the catalyst bed, the volumetric 
flow rate of the mixture of water and 
methanol was controlled by an isocraic pump 
(LabAlliance Series II Pump). Meanwhile, 
the flow rate of the carrier gas (N2) was 
controlled by a mass flow controller 
(KD-4000) in conjunction with a controller 
readout (Brooks 5850E).  With the aid of 
the carrier gas, the reagents were fed into the 
reaction tube and they were heated by 
microwaves. The functions of the mixing 
layer were to facilitate the dispersion and 
evaporation of the reactants and the mixing 
of the reactants and the carrier gas. Soon after 
the reactants flew through the mixing layer, 
MSR was triggered in the catalyst bed. To 
prevent damaging the gas analysis 
instruments, the moisture contained in the 
product gas was preliminarily and completely 
removed in the condenser and the drier, 
respectively. Following the treatment of the 
product gas, the volumetric flow rate of the 
product gas was detected by the flowmeter. 
The volumetric concentrations of CO, CO2 
and CH4 were detected by the gas analyzer, 
whereas the concentration of H2 was 
measured by the gas chromatography. The 
electrical signals from the gas analyzer and 
the gas chromatography were sent into a 
computer to measure and record the 
concentrations of CO, CO2, CH4 and H2. To 
ensure the experimental quality, prior to the 
measurements the gas analyzer was 
calibrated by a standard gas and a calibration 
curve was established before the gas 
chromatography was applied. Moreover, the 
reaction system was leak tested by sending 
the carrier gas with a fixed volumetric flow 
rate into the reaction system followed by 
measuring the flow rate at the system exit.

the methanol conversion is promoted as the 
reaction temperature is increased or the S/C

70 to 80% of methanol is consumed. Once 
the reaction temperature is as high as 250°C, 
the methanol almost completely reacts with

preceding results are very close to the

Fig. 4b; hence it is summarized that at 250°C 
the thermodynamic equilibrium is the 
dominant mechanism in MSR, whereas it is 
governed by chemical kinetic when the 
temperature is below 250°C. Fukahori et al. 
[1] also used the catalyst MDC-3 to carry out 
the experiments of MSR where the reaction 
temperature was 300°C and the space 
velocity was between 1,075 to 4,300 h-1. The 
methanol conversion in their study was 
between 65 and 90%. The comparison 
reveals that the double absorption of 
microwaves by the reactants and the catalyst 
is an effective mode to implement MSR with 
high conversion.

中文： 
本研究已成功開發出利用微波加熱及激發甲醇蒸汽重組反應
的裝置及方法。藉由所建立的裝置，利用水分子、甲醇及觸媒會吸
收微波之特性，可直接以微波加熱反應物內部，而激發蒸汽及甲醇
反應以轉化成氫氣的裝置與方法。該裝置包含一進料元件、一微波
加熱器、一微波控制箱、一反應管及一出口元件。藉由該裝置的運
轉及適當的進料控制，並配合觸媒的作用，可在極短的反應器啟動
及加熱時間下達到產生氫氣之目的，且該裝置較傳統反應器所佔空
間為小。 
 
英文： 
The present work elucidates an apparatus and method for the 
purpose of producing hydrogen. Particular emphasis is focused on a 
designed reactor, which can convert steam and methanol into hydrogen 
under the excitation of microwave on water and methanol molecules. 
The apparatus includes a feeding unit, a microwave heater, a 
microwave control box, a reacting tube and an output unit. By 
performing the invented apparatus in association with appropriate 
feeding operation and the effect of catalyst, hydrogen generation can be 
achieved in a short time after the apparatus is switched on. In addition, 
the required space of the apparatus is small compared with the 
conventional reactors.
