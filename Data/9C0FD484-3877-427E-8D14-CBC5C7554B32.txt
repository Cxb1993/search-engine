Introduction 
Because of rapid development of electronic material industries, it is required to 
develop high performance electronic products such as current central processing units 
(CPUs) and light emitting diode (LED) [1]. Assuming that high heat emitted from the 
high performance products during their operation cannot be dissipated, breakdowns of 
electronic circuits would occur [2]. Electronic materials with poor thermal 
conductivity are partially ascribed to that air-filled microgaps occupy 1-2% of 
contacting surfaces between electronic units and air has poor thermal conductivity 
(0.026 W/mK) [3]. Thermal interface materials (TIMs) such as grease, phase change, 
gels, and adhesives are filled between two solid surfaces to mate rough surfaces, 
reduce the thermal contact resistances, and eliminate the air gaps [1]. Due to poor 
thermal conductivity of silicon grease, dispersed aluminum nitride, gold, copper, 
diamond, or lead in grease can enhance efficiency of TIMs [4]. However, these fillers 
are expensive, hazardous to environment or provided high coefficient of thermal 
expansion [5]. Therefore, it is essential to find novel thermal interface materials 
(TIMs) with high thermal conductivity like greases.  
The thermal plasma technology is attracted to apply in solidification of municipal 
solid, toxic, and infected waste in recent years [6]. Inorganic matters such as heavy 
metals and radiations can be encapsulated in the plasma-melted slag which is residues 
from treated wastes in the thermal system [7]. Due to the stable slag, it can be applied 
in building and road materials [8] Plasma-melted slag may also provide good matrices 
to develop ideal TIMs. Hazardous materials can be trapped in the plasma-melted slags 
by plasma systems to generate solid wastes. Due to low leach of toxic materials from 
plasma-melted slag, stable slag is safe to be reused. Thus, TIMs with high thermal 
conductivity may be developed when plasma-melted slag are modified by 
single/bi-metal nanoparticle fillers, e.g., Al, Ag or Zn because their high thermal 
conductivities. Moreover, consumption and cost of fillers are also decreased with this 
unique process.  
X-ray absorption spectroscopy has been used in determination of elements in the 
complex environmental solids [9-11]. Metals (Ag, Cd, Cu, Pt, Ru and Zr) in the well 
defined channel/pores of zeolites has been studies by EXAFS and XANES 
spectroscopies [12-14]. Metal clusters such as (Cu3O2)n in the confined channel/pores 
of zeolites (ZSM-5) were found by EXAFS [15]. These molecular-scale data turn out 
to be very useful in revealing the catalytic reaction mechanisms. In the present work, 
EXAFS, XANES, SEM and XRD were used to reveal chemical structure changes of 
coated zinc and aluminum on the plasma-melted slags. The thermal conductivity 
efficiency of thermal grease dispersed Al2O3/ZnO-coated on the plasma-melted slag is 
also investigated.

original and Al2O3/ZnO-coated plasma-melted slag are shown in Table 2. Dispersion 
of a small amount (0.5, 1, and 5%) of the slag in the thermal grease led a little change 
of thermal conductivities at 298, 333, and 343 K. Thermal conductivities of the 
thermal grease containing 0.5, 1, and 5% of ZnO nanoparticles are increased by 
2-11% at 298 and 333 K. However, in the presence of 1 and 5% of ZnO nanoparticles, 
the thermal conductivities of thermal greases are increased by 30% at 343 K. However, 
a small increase of thermal conductivities of Al2O3 naoparticles contained thermal 
grease is observed. Due to coating processes, Al2O3 and ZnO nanoparticles can be 
observed in Al2O3/ZnO-coated plasma-melted slag. Interestingly, the thermal 
conductivies of thermal grease containing 0.5, 1, and 5% of Al2O3/ZnO-coated 
plasma-melted slag are up to about one time. Enhanced thermal conductivities of 
thermal grease containing bimetal nanoparticles on plasma-melted slag can be 
obtained.  
Conclusions 
Effective thermal conductivity of thermal grease dispersed nanosize 
Al2O3/ZnO-coated plasma-melted slag can be observed. During coating processes, the 
structures of Al2O3, ZnO, and plasma-melted slag are little perturbed measuring with 
SEM, XRD, and XANES. The thermal conductivities of thermal grease 0.5, 1, and 5% 
of Al2O3/ZnO-coated plasma-melted slag are increased with one time. Enhanced 
thermal conductivities of thermal grease with adding bi-metal nanoparticles of 
plasma-melted slag are obtained. The plasma-melted slag can be recovered to utilize 
in thermal interface materials.

1. 學術成就 
電子產品需求量增加，並朝向輕薄短小及高運轉效能和速度研發，故產品使用過程常伴隨
產生大量熱，需快速傳導至環境中，減少故障及損壞率，但各電子元件之接觸面無法達到
完全接合，空氣易進入二界面空隙，因空氣之熱傳導率不佳，將降低熱之傳輸效能，故常
以熱界面材料(thermal interface materials, TIMs)填充，隨著電提升電子元件運轉時
發熱量增加，TIMs 之效能需不斷提升。電漿融渣具穩定及安定化特性，可資源化再利用於
高單價之 TIMs，利用電漿融渣進行高熱導係數之金屬或其氧化物塗佈改質，使其含有單一
或多種高熱導性填充劑(Al 及 Zn)，增加 TIMs 的熱導係數，並降低填充劑之使用量及成本。
2. 技術創新 
選定適合顆粒大小之電漿融渣做為擔體，減少因破碎而導致有害物質大量溶出量現象，同
時將電漿融渣回收利用於高單價的 TIMs，減少 TIMs 的填充劑使用量。本實驗以溶膠-凝膠
法合成奈米顆粒，並塗佈在電漿融渣表面，可大幅降低成本，該法之步驟簡單，易合成均
質化的奈米金屬，電漿融渣塗佈二種金屬物質並添加至 TIMs，TIMs 的熱導係數提升了一
倍，該研究能進一步發展多種金屬的 TIMs 填充劑，除增加熱導係數外，多種金屬之成分
比例可減低熱膨脹現象，增加填充物多元性。 
3. 社會影響 
感染性或放射性物質量增加，以焚化方式進行處理，廢氣或灰燼可能含有戴奧辛或有害金
屬，利用電漿系統予以處理，所衍生電漿融渣為穩定化物質，常應用在建材，而應用在高
