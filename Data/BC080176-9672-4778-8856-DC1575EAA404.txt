中文摘要 
本計劃提出具有強耦合傳輸特性的Metamaterial空腔，並
且有體積小和均勻場分佈的特性。此空腔是由人工磁導
體面和良導體面所構成；其四個側邊為人工磁導體面，
頂邊和底邊為良導體面。人工磁導體面是由許多的矩形
金屬形成的週期性陣列結構所組成，並且利用具有金屬
底板的雙介質基底實現。此結構不需額外的貫孔，即可
提升其操作頻寬。為了驗證其傳輸特性，利用單極天線
當作發射與接收，並且變換接收天線的位置。在A、B、
C和D，四個位置上的插入損失量測結果分別為1.06 
dB、2.26 dB、2.02 dB 和 0.6 dB。由量測結果可驗證此
空腔在小體積下仍具有其寬頻特性。 
 
Abstract 
This study proposes a novel metamaterial-based cavity 
with a strong coupling effect between the transmitting 
and receiving devices. It is compact in size and the field 
distribution is uniform inside the cavity, which is 
realized by combining four artificial magnetic conductor 
surfaces at the four sidewalls and two metal palates at 
the top and bottom walls. The AMC consisting of a 
periodic array of square patches printed on the metal-
backed double-layer dielectric substrate without 
connecting vias to enhance its operating bandwidth. Two 
monopole-type antennas are used as the transmitting and 
receiving terminals to examine the transmission property. 
The measured insertion loss of the metamaterial-based 
with different receiving positions are 1.06 dB, 2.26 dB, 
2.02 dB, and 0.6 dB for position A, B, C, and D, 
respectively. The fabrication and measurement of the 
metamaterial-based cavity are demonstrated to validate 
its broad bandwidth and compact size.

II. METAMATERIAL-BASED CAVITY RESONATOR  
The three-dimensional geometrical configuration of the 
proposer cavity is constructed by four AMC surfaces [Fig. 1 
(b)] at sidewalls and two metallic plates at the top and 
bottom walls, as shown in Fig. 1(a). The AMC surfaces 
consist of a periodic array of square patches printed on 
double-layer dielectric substrates with a metal-backed 
ground plane. In [2], [6], the relative bandwidth of an AMC 
is proportional to the total equivalent inductances and 
capacitances of the structure. The gap between the square 
patches functions as a shunt capacitor. The metal-backed 
double-layer structure with a distance of d=d1+d2 can be 
modeled as a lumped inductor parallel connecting to the 
capacitor provided by the square patches. Thus adjusting the 
gap between the square patch and distance of the metal-
backed double-layer dielectric structure improves the 
operation bandwidth.  
The double-dielectric layer in the AMC surface adopts the 
FR-4 substrate with a permittivity of 4.4 and a thickness of 
0.8 mm as the upper dielectric layer to support the printed 
periodic array of square patch, and the air substrate with a 
thickness of 20 mm as the lower dielectric layer. The final 
dimensions of the AMC structure are D = 15 mm, w = 0.3 
mm, d1 = 20 mm, d2 = 0.8 mm,  r1 = 1, and  r2 =4.4. 
The AMC structure is characterized using high-frequency 
structure simulator (HFSS) based on finite element method. 
The reflection phase of the normal incident wave within 
90o is defined as the operating bandwidth of AMC surface. 
The calculated operating bandwidth of AMC surface is 48.6 
﹪from 0.87 to 1.41 GHz and the zero degree reflection 
phase is at 1.11 GHz. 
  The simulated electric field distributions of the dominant 
TEZ mode in the conventional metal cavity and the 
metamaterial-based cavity are shown in Fig. 3(a) and 3(b), 
respectively. For the metal cavity, the resonating frequency 
of the TEZ101 mode is 1.68 GHz, and the half-wavelength 
resonating electric fields are observed along both X- and Z-
axes. In contrast to the metal cavity, the metamaterial-based 
cavity processes a uniform electric field at the fundamental 
resonant frequency 0.86 GHz in both Y and Z direction. 
Thus the compact metamaterial-based cavity with uniform 
field distribution is verified. 
III. TRANSMISSION CHARACTERISTIC 
Two monopole-type antennas with a length of 68 mm and 
a width of 22 mm on the FR-4 substrate (thickness of 0.4 
mm ) are inserted into the cavity as the transmitting and 
receiving terminals to test the cavity performance, as shown 
in Fig. 1(a). Figure 4 shows the measured S-parameters of 
the conventional cavity and the proposed metamaterial-
based cavity with the same dimensions. According to this 
figure, results show that the transmission frequency of the 
conventional cavity is 1.79 GHz, where the frequency is 
close to the dominant resonant mode (f101=1.684 GHz). In 
the proposed cavity, the resonant frequency is centered at 
1.05 GHz with 3-dB bandwidth of 500 MHz from 0.8 to 1.3
