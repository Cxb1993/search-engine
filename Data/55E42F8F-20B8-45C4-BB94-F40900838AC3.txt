電池性質之影響
Effect of Room Temperature Molten Salt (RTMS) Additives on the
Electrolyte Conductivity and Charge-discharge property of Lithium ion
Battery

一、中文摘要：
本實驗選擇鋰離子二次電池用鋰鹽
LiTFSI 為溶質、以乙醯胺（acetamide）為溶
劑，探討較廣泛溫度及組成範圍之二元系
LiTFSI-acetamide 室溫型熔融鹽(RTMS)電解
質之輸送特性。並選擇較佳輸送特性之組成
LiTFSI-acetamide=20:80、30:70 mol％作為電
解質添加物，得知鋰離子電池的導電度隨
LiTFSI之微量添加而呈降低之趨勢。
充放電測試實驗以 LiMn2O4∣ LiPF6 in
EC:DEC(1:1 in vol.) + RTMS∣Li 之電池組
合，於商用電解質 1M LiPF6 in EC/DEC(1:1 in
vol.) 電解質中添加微量之室溫型熔融鹽
LiTFSI-acetamide，探討室溫型熔融鹽電解質
添加對於鋰離子二次電池之電容循環特性。經
由充放電測試結果顯現，當混合電解液條件為
LiTFSI-acetamide=20:80mol%以 1mol%添加入
1M LiPF6 in EC:DEC(1:1 in vol.)中，擁有較高
電容量 101.38 mAh/g 與較低電容衰退率為
3.21％(30cycle)。
關鍵詞：室溫型熔融鹽、鋰離子電池、射頻磁
控濺鍍。
Abstract：
In this study, some transport properties of the
binary room-temperature molten salt
(RTMS) system lithium bis( trifluoromethane
sulfone )imide (LiTFSI)-acetamide applied
in Li-ion battery was measured with
adapted temperature and composition. The
results would be contributived to select the better
compositions (LiTFSI-acetamide=20:80、30:70
mol％) of electrolyte for the Li-ion battery and
we known the conductivity of Li-ion battery
decreased with increasing small amount LiTFSI.
The addition of small amount of the binary
LiTFSI-acteamide room temperature molten salt
electrolytes in the commercial electrolyte (1M
LiPF6 in EC/DEC(1:1 in vol.)). The addition of
small amount of the room temperature molten
salt (LiTFSI-acetamide=20:80mol％) had
higher capacity(101.38mAh/g),lower capacity
lost(3.21%).
Keywords： Room Temperature Molten Salt,
RTMS、Li-ion battery、Radio
frequency magnetron sputtering

由於電子與資訊產業發展迅速，3C產品

密度的鋰電池二次電池材料，已成為產業的開
發重點。
鋰離子二次電池用電解液之調製是電池
性能提昇之關鍵技術。一般商用電解質係以有
機溶媒溶解無機鹽類，如LiPF6溶於EC-DEC溶
劑[1]。為提昇電池能源密度及能源效率，電解
質材料中除添加輔助溶媒(Co-solvent)之研究
報導外[2]，室溫型熔融鹽添加亦為目前鋰離子
二次電池安全性能提昇的最新研究課題[3,4]。

salts(RTMS) possess some unique properties, e.g., wide liquid-phase range, high 
thermal stability, nonflammability, unvolatility and high conductivity[2-5], therefore, 
RTMS have attracted much attention as safe and novel electrolytes. Recent researches 
note the RTMS have been widely applied to electrochemical devices such as 
lithium-ion battery[6-8], super capacitor[9-11], solar cell[12-16], fuel cell[17, 18] and 
gas sensor electrode[19-21]. It is obviously that research on RTMS has drawn 
considerable attention. Hussey[22] has noted that industrial exploitation of RTMS 
looks extremely favorable. Unfortunately, however, data of some transport properties 
for RTMS are still scarce. 
In this study, some transport properties of the binary room-temperature molten 
salt(RTMS) system lithium bis(trifluoromethane sulfone)imide (LiTFSI)-acetamide 
applied in Li-ion battery will be measured with adapted temperature and composition. 
The phase diagram will be determined by Differential Scanning Calorimeter(DSC) 
and Thermogravimetric Analyzer (TGA) analyses. The electric conductivities and 
densities of the binary melts will also be measured, the results will be contributive to 
select the better composition of electrolyte for the Li-ion battery. 
 
Experimental method 
LiTFSI(Lithium bis( trifuoromethane sulfone)imide, Aldrich, 99.5%) and 
acetamide (Acros, 99%) was used as dried with vacuuming for one week at 140oC and 
55oC, respectively. The binary molten salts with different molar ratios were prepared 
by continuous stirring for 24 hours under a high purity argon atmosphere in a glove 
box(Super(1220/750), Mikrouna). Besides, the water component of melt was 
measured by Karl-Fischer titration(DL31KF Coulometer, Mettler Toledo). 
The phase diagram of the LiFTSI-acetamide melts was obtained by measuring 
the decomposition temperatures and the melting points. The TGA and DSC analyzes 
were carried out with about 5~10mg binary melts. The TGA analyzes of the binary 
melts were performed on a platinum tray which could be heated from 273~773K, with 
a heating rate of 20K/min. A sealed aluminum disc was used for the DSC analysis, 
which was, at first, cooled to -60oc by liquid nitrogen, and the temperature was raised 
at a rate of 10K/min. The electric conductivities of melts were measured using the 
direct-current computerized measurement system designed by Hsu and Yang[23]. The 
densities of melts were also determined by using the Archimedes principle, the 
apparatus being schematically shown in [24]. 
 
Results and discussion  
The decomposition temperatures were measured by TGA analysis, the results 
reveal that the decomposition temperatures are around 473K for different 
electrolyte with several compositions is shown in Figure 8. The result shows that the 
conductivities of the binary RTMS increase with increasing temperature and 
acetamide content. The movement of ions and the molar volume of melt increase with 
temperature, resulting in the ionic mobility increasing. Guthner[27] has reported that 
the conductivity of pure molten acetamide has the characteristic of self-ionization or 
auto-protolysis, which can also be found in water.  
The reaction is 2 CH3CONH2     CH3CONH3+ + CH3CONH-. The ionization 
causes an amphiprotic acid-base character of acetamide. In this study, a decrease of 
the conductivity with increasing the content of salt(LiTFSI) has been confirmed. 
However, the increase of acetamide content increase not only the free space but also 
the mobility of the disassociated species.  The result is consistent with the 
observation reported by Hu et al.[26]. 
Figure 9 shows the conductivities of the LiTFSI-acetamide and LiTFSI-urea 
RTMS as a function of LiTFSI mole fraction at several temperatures. The result 
reveals that the LiTFSI acetamide RTMS has a bigger conductivity. The result may be 
explained in terms of the interactions among solute-solvent, owing to the acetamide 
molecule has two polar group(C=O and NH2), the interaction of acetamide is weaker 
than it of urea (three polar group), as a result the LiTFSI-acetamide RTMS have larger 
molar volume and mobility of complex species, therefore, the result as shown in 
Figure 9 is obtained. 
The interactions and bonding of LiTFSI and acetamide in the binary RTMS are 
shown in Figure 10. It is clear that the oxygen atom(O) has a larger electronegativity 
(En=3.5), the Li+ ion have a tendency to coordinate with the O(22) atoms in the C=O 
group of acetamide. Hu et al.[28] have indicated that there is a large amount of O 
atoms in acetamide interacting with Li+ ion, therefore, a stronger cation-solvent 
interaction occurs when the acetamide concentration is high. Moreover, the SO2 group 
of TFSI- anions interacts with NH2 group of acetamide via hydrogen bonding is also 
indicated. However, these interactions among solute-solvent will affect significantly 
the conductivity of binary RTMS. Besides, Hu et al. have also indicated that the ionic 
association increases with increasing salt concentration, resulting in a considerable 
amount of contact ion pairs forming. Therefore, the ionic conductivity decreases. It 
can be conclude that the ionic conductivity strongly depends on the ionic association 
in the complex system.

The chemical LiTFSI (Aldrich Inc. 99.95%) was vacuum dried at 403K for one 
week to remove the moisture. For the same reason, urea (ACROS Inc. 99%) was 
vacuum dried at 373 K for one week. These two chemicals were then mixed at 
appropriate ratio and heated to 373K. This procedure should be done in a glove box 
(Super 1220/750, MIKROUNA O2 ＜1 ppm, H2O＜1 ppm) filled with nitrogen gas.  
Moisture content was determined by Karl-Fischer titration (DL31KF Coulometer, 
Mettler Toledo). For DSC test, sample (5-10 mg, allow minimum air-contact time)   
should previously be reduced to 193 K. It was then raised to 423 K at a heating rate of 
10 K/min. Density was determined according to Archimedean principle[27-29] 
through use of a computerized system for better accuracy. The electrical conductivity 
was measured by using a direct-current method through use of a computerized system

Effect of temperature on conductivity of the binary LiTFSI-urea molten salt is 
shown in Figure 5. Conductivity increased with increasing temperature presumably 
due to increase in molar volume and the free space among molecules of molten salt 
which facilitate transport of ions. Figure 6 illustrates the effect of mole fraction of 
LiTFSI on conductivities of LiTFSI-urea and LiTFSI-acetamide. As can be seen in 
this graph, conductivity of the latter is higher than that of the former. As previously 
mentioned, this is because urea has two NH2 groups whereas acetamide has only one 
such group. As a result, the intermolecular force among urea molecules is stronger 
than that of acetamide molecules. Besides, as the solute, LiTFSI, mixed into the 
molten salt, LiTFSI-urea showed higher interaction than LiTFSI-acetamide.  
Consequently, complex ions of the former would show slower movement than the 
latter. Figure 7 shows the bonding in LiTFSI-urea complex. Li(16) interacts strongly 
forming coordinate bond with C＝O of urea as O has larger electronegativity (EN＝
3.5). Similarly, the two NH2 groups interact through Coulomb force with O(5) or O(11) 
of TFSI- . However, in LiTFSI-acetamide system, besides the interaction between Li+ 
and C＝O, there is only one NH2 group that interacts with S＝O. Hence, LiTFSI-urea 
has lower conductivity than LiTFSI-acetamide. Within the temperature range of 
313-378 K, the conductivity can be expressed as κ＝a＋bT＋cT2 where T is 
temperature in K, a, b, and c are constants, as shown in Table 2. Literature [31] has 
reported the relation of chemical bonding and shift of spectrum on LiTFSI-urea.  
Owing to resonance structure of urea and the large electronegativity of O in C＝O, O 
would coordinate with Li+. This coordination force could reduce or even destroy the 
hydrogen bond in urea and causing C＝O to show blue shifting phenomenon. On the 
other hand, NH2 group of urea would interact with O in SO2 of TFSI- and show red 
shifting phenomenon. When LiTFSI is mixed with urea, the reaction will lead to the 
formation of ［ NH2-(Li+….CO)-NH- ］ complex, whereas TFSI- reacts with   
NH2CONH2δ+ to form［NH2-CO-(NH2δ+)…TFSI-］complex. The potential utility of
