氫在金屬的特性 
一般金屬中的原子是具有規律地排列而形成晶體，在晶體內部原子間存在許多空隙
如：四面體格隙（Tetrahedral sites）或八面體格隙（Octahedral sites），一些較小的原子
如：氫、氧、氮、碳等，可進入這些空隙中，並在空隙之間運動，特別是氫原子，由於
氫原子是最小的原子，幾乎可以進入各種金屬內部。 
氫氣在和金屬粉末接觸後，氫分子先透過凡得瓦爾力在其表面上進行物理吸附，被
吸附的氫分子打斷鍵結分解成氫原子，與表面金屬形成化學性的吸附，同時也產生化學
吸附熱，被吸附的氫原子再從金屬表面滲透（Surface Penetration）至內部，進入格隙形
成固溶體α相，接著更多金屬中固溶的氫再往內部擴散，但此擴散行為需克服由化學吸
附到滲透至金屬內部的活化能障，直到α相固溶體過飽和後，在α相外圍開始產生新的β
相，即形成氫化物，此時α+β兩相共存（圖2-1）。上述的反應過程為可逆的，由β相分解
為α相的過程則為放氫反應。固態金屬（M）與氫氣反應，生成金屬氫化物（MHn）反

變化，黃色的 WO3 粉末變成藍色的 HxWO3 (x=0.35)。很久以來這個方法一直被用來檢
驗氫原子的存在。而氫分子與 WO3 反應只有在溫度大於 400℃時才有可能進行。在室溫
條件下，用 WO3 或 Al2O3+WO3 與氫氣反應均未觀察到反應的發生。然而當用 Pt/A12O3
代替 Al2O3 時，在同樣條件下卻迅速地出現了藍色化合物。顯然微量 Pt 不足以與固體的
WO3 有良好的接觸，因此用一般的催化作用難以解釋這個現象。Khoobier 提出，這是氫
溢流效應，即氫氣在 Pt 原子上發生解離且吸附，然後 H+或 H-遷移越過鋁原子表面，再
擴散通過固體粒子的介面而到達 WO3 反應。 
氫溢流發生的條件： 
(1) 催化劑能夠解離且吸附氫，也就是能夠產生原子態氫。 
(2) 原子態氫能夠順利遷移運動（如固體粒子的間隙和通道，或質子傳遞鏈）。 
氫反溢流現象也存在，即氫溢流的逆過程，儲存在載體上的原子態氫遷移到表面金
屬粒子然後以分子態氫排出。

一般來說，固體微粒分散在以水為介質的分散體系中是最為常見的。為了阻止微粒
聚集，大多數應用的是離子型界面活性劑。例如當被分散的固體是以非極性為主時，可
加入各種離子型界面活性劑，當這些表面活性劑吸附在不帶電荷的固體微粒表面時，會
使其帶有同種電荷而相互排斥，從而形成阻止粒子聚集的屏障，同時由於界面活性劑分
子在固體粒子表面上的定向作用，非極性基團指向非極性粒子的表面，極性基團指向水
相，這就降低了固液介面張力，也更有利於固體粒子在水相中的分散。這種吸附效率是
隨著疏水基團的碳鏈的增長而增加的，因此可以推測，在這種情況下，長碳鏈的離子型
界面活性劑，比短碳鏈的更有效。但是對於已帶有電荷的固體微粒分散體系，為了穩定、
均勻地分散，則一般不常用離子型界面活性劑。因為若使用的是和固體微粒帶相反電荷
的界面活性劑，則在微粒所帶的電荷被中和前，可能發生聚集而不能有效的分散，這樣
只有當中和粒子的表面電荷之後，再吸附第二層表面活性劑離子，固體微粒才能重新帶
電荷，重新分散。如果使用和固體微粒有相同電荷的界面活性劑離子時，由於界面活性
劑的極性基團只能指向相同電荷的微粒表面外，即指向水相，這種吸附狀況的固體微粒，
由於靜電的斥力而阻止微粒之間的吸附，從而達到分散的效果。並且只有當水相中離子
型的界面活性劑的濃度比較高時，才有強的吸附作用，使分散體系穩定。對帶有電荷的
固體微粒的分散所使用的一般是結構中帶有較多的極性基團的聚電解質（常稱離子型高
分子界面活性劑）。它們分子中所帶相同電荷數越多，電離能也越大。使固體微粒分散
體系趨於穩定的電能屏障作用，並非是使體系穩定的唯一因素，空間障礙也可以阻止粒
子間相互吸引，緊密靠近。因此，在很多場合下，非離子型界面活性劑對固體微粒有很
好的分散作用。

4-7 添加螯合劑與介面活性劑以水熱法製備鐵/碳複合材料 
由於奈米粒子之間的距離很小，顆粒間的吸引力使其極容易聚集在一貣，不易均勻
分散於液相中，由於奈米碳粉本身的奈米顆粒就有嚴重的聚集，這使得奈米碳粉的比表
面積會大幅下降，而在生成奈米氧化鐵時，因為氧化鐵也是奈米等級所以本身也有聚集
的效應。因此在水熱法製備鐵/碳奈米複合材料時，添加入螯合劑或介面活性劑，期望能
增加氧化鐵與奈米碳粉的分散性。 
在前面部分的實驗中，添加14%的鐵/碳複合材料均是氫氣吸附量（wt.%）最大的一
組，可見14%的添加比例是有利於氫氣吸附的。添加過少的鐵（7%）會導致表面氧化鐵
含量不足，而無法提供足夠的極性對於氫氣產生誘發極性；添加過多的鐵（56%）則會
使整體鐵/碳複合材料重量提升，而使得氫氣吸附量的重量百分比下降。所以在這部分皆
添加14%的鐵來進行實驗。

肆、結論 
1. 石墨碳和鈀皆有催化鎂鐵鈦系儲氫材料，並改善其儲氫性質的特性。 
2. 石墨碳添加的表現沒有鈀添加的好，特別是在初期的活化速率方面，但是卻可以提
升儲氫效率，因此我們推論，碳若是用在催化放氫效果不佳，但是對於儲氫量的增
加有幫助，應是來自於碳本身優秀的儲氫性質。 
3. 利用 γ-Fe2O3（磁赤鐵礦）對奈米碳粉表面進行改質，而製得 γ-Fe2O3/碳奈米複合材
料。在添加 14%鐵的 γ-Fe2O3/碳奈米複合材料中，同時具有微小孔洞與磁赤鐵礦的
存在，使得表面極性獲得提升，使得氫氣吸附量相較於原始奈米碳粉提升約 1.9 倍
（0.48 wt.%），而其它添加鐵量的 γ-Fe2O3/碳奈米複合材料在室溫下的氫氣吸附量
也都獲得提升，磁赤鐵礦的添加確實對於氫氣的吸附有所幫助。 
4. 利用 α-Fe2O3（赤鐵礦）對奈米碳粉表面進行改質，而製得 α-Fe2O3/碳奈米複合材料。
因為 α-Fe2O3（赤鐵礦）使表面微小孔洞大幅減少，且 α-Fe2O3（赤鐵礦）不如 γ-Fe2O3
（磁赤鐵礦）對於氫氣吸附貢獻來得大。 
5. 利用相轉變將 α-Fe2O3/碳奈米複合材料中的 α-Fe2O3（赤鐵礦）轉變為 γ-Fe2O3（磁
赤鐵礦），發現氫氣吸附量得到提升，證明 γ-Fe2O3（磁赤鐵礦）對於提升奈米碳
粉室溫下吸氫量優於 α-Fe2O3（赤鐵礦）。 
6. 在界面活性劑使用時無法製得鐵/碳奈米複合材料，這是因為界面活性劑的性質，它
能使奈米碳粉進行分散，但鐵氧化物生成後也受到界面活性劑的保護，使得鐵氧化
物無法在奈米碳粉表面生成，加上界面活性劑容易殘留於奈米碳粉表面阻住孔洞，
所以界面活性劑在這實驗中使用並不理想。

1. Introduction 
Hydrogen energy is an irreplaceable component in the future energy inventory. It has 
long been considered as an ideal energy carrier and the alternative of fossil fuels such as oil 
and coal due to its renewable property and zero carbon dioxide emission.
1
 Despite its 
promising perspective, there are still plenty of obstacles needed to be overcome such as the 
hydrogen production, transportation and storage. To meet the demand, four means of 
hydrogen storage has been proposed: liquefaction, compressed gas, metal hydrides, and 
adsorption.
2 
Among these nowadays available techniques, the storage in a solid-state matrix 
such as metal hydrides and adsorption is an efficient strategy especially in the mild 
temperature.
3
 Although metal hydrides have high hydrogen storage capacity through 
chemisorption, they are subjected to the issue of slow hydrogen desorption rate as well as 
high mass density. As a result, doping some transition metals with catalytic properties to 
lower desorption energies
4
 or selecting light-weight hydrogen-storage metal hydrides 
5
has 
been proposed to promote the performance of metal hydrides. 
In contrast to metal hydrides, microporous carbon-bearing materials are other brilliant 
candidates for hydrogen storage. Their properties including not only the large specific surface 
area
6
 but also the microporous texture
3
 provide a particularly favorable environment for 
hydrogen storage. Nevertheless these carbon-bearing materials adsorb the undissociated 
hydrogen molecules mainly by van der Waals forces at their surfaces. These binding forces 
are so weak that the physisorption process at room temperature is almost impeded by thermal 
motion.
3
 Again, transition metals are introduced into these carbon-bearing materials to lower 
their surface energies so to enhance the adsorption force at room temperature. For example, 
based upon the report with ab initio density-functional theory, it has been suggested that 
pillared Li-dispersed boron carbide nanotubes are capable of hydrogen storage up to 6.0 
wt.% .
7
 Doping palladium along with thermal treatment greatly increases the hydrogen uptake

2. Experimental 
2.1. Preparation of iron/carbon nanopowder composite materials 
The carbon nanopowder (CNP) (CAS 7400-44-0) and reagents including Fe(NO3)3 and 
analytical grade of ethanol were purchased from Aldrich and used without any further 
purification. The CNP was in amorphous form, made by laser decomposition with average 
particle size of 30 nm (data obtained from the instruction labeled on the bottle). To prepare 
iron/carbon nanopowder (Fe/CNP) composite materials, 0.4 gram of untreated CNP was 
mixed with desired amount of Fe(NO3)3 and with 40 ml of ethanol. Mixtures were sealed by 
Parafilm under ultrasonic water bath for one hour. After removing the seal film, samples were 
left at 60 
o
C for 24 hours for drying. 
 
2.2. Textural and structural studies 
X-ray diffraction (LabX XRD6000, Shimadzu) analyses of original and iron-spiked CNP 
samples were recorded with scan range from 10~80 degree (2 theta) and the scan rate at 4 
degree per minute with CuKα radiation (λ= 0.15406 nm) at 30 kV and 20 mA. Fourier 
Transform Infrared Spectroscopy (FTIR, Bomen DA8.3) spectra of the CNP samples were 
obtained by using transmission mode with KBr pellets with scan rate at 1.00 cm
-1
/s and with 
the resolution at 2 cm
-1
. To prepare transparent KBr salt pellets, 5 mg of the CNP powders and 
50 mg of KBr salt were ground and then hydraulically pressed. Specific surface area of the 
CNP powders was determined by 77 K N2-BET method (ASAP2020, Micromeritics). The 
pore volume and the pore size were automatically calculated by the built-in software. The 
pore volume was determined by single point adsorption with pores less than 62.5368 nm 
diameter at P/P0 = 0.96807, whereas the pore size was obtained by the adsorption average 
pore width (4V/A) by the BET method.

3. Results and discussion 
3.1. Experiments of hydrogen uptake 
Dependence of hydrogen uptake on reaction time under 37 atm and 27 
o
C (room 
temperature) is shown in Fig 2a. Since the steady states of hydrogen adsorption are reached 
after one hour reaction, their corresponding hydrogen uptake is selected for all the following 
discussion therein. As indicated in Fig 2b, a local adsorption maximum at about 0.48 wt % is 
obtained with 14 wt % of iron spiked, whereas the hydrogen uptake inversely decreases as the 
iron content increases. This hydrogen storage capacity is comparable with the adsorption 
capacity in palladium- and vanadium-doped carbon nanotube (~ 0.69 wt.%)
10
 and is also 
comparable with that in activated carbon (~ 0.53 wt.%)
9
 but is much superior to that in 
copper-doped activated carbon fibers (0.28 wt.% under similar conditions) .
1
 In spite of the 
decrease when the spiked iron content exceeds 14 %, the overall hydrogen adsorption 
capacity is in general promoted about 20~60 % in comparison with original CNP by the 
addition of iron. This observation clearly indicates that the addition of iron is a promising 
mean to improve the hydrogen adsorption capacity in carbon-bearing materials. 
3.2. The textural and structural properties 
To interpret the difference in hydrogen adsorption capacity, the textural and structural 
properties of iron/carbon nanopowder composite samples are taken into consideration. FTIR 
spectra of iron-spiked samples with the best performance before (14FeCPN-B) and after 
hydrogen adsorption (14FeCPN-A) are shown in Fig 2c. No C-H stretching vibration with 
wavenumber around 2800 ~ 3100 cm
-1
 is observed after hydrogen storage.
11
 This might imply 
either that the amount of adsorbed hydrogen molecules by chemisorptions is not enough to 
activate the C-H stretching vibrations or that there is eventually no chemisorption taking 
place. 
102 
 
the other hand, although a larger pore (>1 nm) could hold more hydrogen molecules by the 
formation of two adsorption layers, the binding force is so weak that would be readily 
compensated by the thermal motion at room temperature. Accordingly, dependence of 
porosity would be limited and the observed gravimetric adsorption would only depend on the 
specific surface area of samples.
3
 
As indicated by the BJH differential adsorption pole volume (dV/dD) shown in Fig 4b, 
the CNP sample mainly contains the meso- (centered at 2.5 nm) and the macroporous surface 
(centered at 75 nm), which is consistent with the aggregated feature observed in the SEM 
image (Fig 4a). The addition of iron species would dramatically rearrange the original porous 
texture toward microporous morphology distribution by the growth or aggregation of 
Fe-bearing particles (insert Fig 4a). When the iron content exceeds 14 wt.% (14Fe/CNP 
samples), the CNP surfaces are fully covered by Fe-bearing particles and thus the micorpore 
is blocked and the surface texture transfers toward mesoporous environments due to the 
formation of large iron clusters. The addition of iron content overall increases the specific 
surface area of these Fe/CNP samples to some extent, which is responsible for the higher 
hydrogen uptake. Although the porous texture transition is difficult to be recorded due to the 
limitation of SEM resolution, it could significantly reflect in the N2-BET 
adsorption/desorption isotherm and calculated by the BJH method.
13
 However, the argument 
of increased specific surface area alone could not fully explain why the maximum hydrogen 
adsorption does not happen in 7Fe/CNP samples, which show the largest specific surface area. 
To answer this observation, it is necessary to take into the consideration of the role of iron 
content in the Fe/CNP samples and to be discussed in the next paragraph. 
3.3. Effects of surface polarization 
As indicated by the XRD patterns, the spiked iron contents would finally transform into 
the maghemite cluster in the Fe/CNP samples (Fig 5). Maghemite can be considered as a fully 
104 
 
become hematite phase and then the hematite is oxidized to transform into magnetite.
12
 The 
maghemite obtained following this track usually has the specific surface area less than 20 
m
2
g
-1
, which supports our previous discussion that the measured surface area mainly 
contributed from the CNP content. 
Accordingly, the following possible statements was proposed to describe the promoted 
hydrogen adsorption observed in this work. In the case of 7Fe/CNP samples which exibit the 
largest specific surface area, their mild hydrogen uptake might be attributed to that there is no 
sufficient maghemite clusters embedded into CNP skeleton. Under this circumstance, only the 
nearby carbon nanopowder surfaces are able to adsorb the polarized hydrogen molecules and 
the rest carbon nanopowder surface contribute little in hydrogen adsorption due to the higher 
surface energy barrier and poor spillover reactions.
8
 This also explains the lowest normalized 
hydrogen adsorption capacity as shown in Fig 3. For 14Fe/CNP samples with the maximum 
hydrogen adsorption, the maghemite clusters might probably be well dispersed over the entire

the specific surface area since these deposited maghemite might also block the surface pores. 
Beyond 14 wt % of iron spiked, additional iron species form larger maghemite grains on CNP 
surface which not only block the surface pore to reduce the surface area but also to increase 
the weight of Fe/CNP samples. Consequently, the gravimetric storage capacity drops but the 
normalized adsorption capacity is very close to the starting CNP samples.
