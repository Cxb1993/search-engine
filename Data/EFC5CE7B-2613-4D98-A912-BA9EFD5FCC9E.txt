摘要 
本研究執行高速公路隧道行駛車輛之空氣污染物排放係數實場調查，獲取高
速公路之行車錄影及速度資料、氣象參數及空氣污染物數據，並運用於高速公路
行駛車隊平均排放係數之估算，期能獲取本土化高速公路行駛車隊排放係數值之
範圍，有助更精確地掌握移動污染源排放係數、排放量之估算。 
根據研究結果顯示：行駛車輛平均車速於 85～105 km/hr下，實場量測 NO、
NOX、CO及 CO2之平均排放係數範圍為 1.6～1.8、2.4～2.7、8.0～8.8及 490～
550 g/km-veh；以 1 hr為分析時距統計迴歸求得小型車、大型車及聯結車之 NO、
NOX、CO及 CO2四種污染物排放係數分別為 0.70、2.55及 12.03 g/km-veh；1.00、
4.21及 18.30 g/km-veh；6.00、7.00及 12.66 g/km-veh；83.49、1295.38 g/km-veh
及 3554.13 g/km-veh。另由高速公路隧道連續 25小時量測顯示：NO、NOX、CO
及 CO2 污染物平均排放係數之最大值均出現於夜間之行車時段，平均排放係數
呈現夜間高於日間之趨勢，主要成因係該時段車隊組成之差異所導致。 
 
關鍵字：排放係數、隧道實驗、高速公路、空氣污染物。 
 
 
Abstract 
The field measurement was conducted, including the investigation of air 
pollutants, meteorological factors, vehicle speed and video records of motor vehicles 
through highway tunnel study, and the data was utilized to calculate the average 
emission factors. However, the data from highway tunnel study was analyzed and 
applied on the estimation of highway emission factors. The data was utilized to 
further calculation and verification through the model simulation and statistics 
methods. It was helpful to obtain the confidence interval of emission factors and 
accuracy vehicle’s emission in Taiwan. 
 Results confirmed that this measuring plan in highway tunnel was a worth 
developing method for investigating motor vehicle’s emission factor. In field 
measurement, the average emission factors of NO, NOX, CO, CO2 were 1.6~1.8, 
2.4~2.7, 8.0~8.8 and 490~550 g/km-veh, respectively. In addition, the range of 
emission factors of NOX, CO and CO2 were 0.8~1.9, 3.5~4.2 and 240~260 g/km-veh 
using simulation of fuel-based, respectively. Emission factors of NO、NOX、CO、CO2 
were1.4~1.6、2.3~2.4、6.3~6.5 and 410~430 g/km-veh by statistics regression, 
respectively. Continuous 25 hours monitored in highway, emission factors obtained in 
nighttime were larger than those of in daytime. This phenomenon indicated the higher 
emission factor in nighttime was related to the constitution of motor vehicles in 
highway.  
 
Keyword: emission factor, tunnel measurement, highway, pollutant emission.

Measurements of the speciation of ozone precursor from Kaoping 
petrochemical/industrial area in Taiwan 
 
Control #: 857 
 
Chin-Hsing Lai*, Yee-Lin Wu, Ching-Ho Lin, Shu-Hsing Yeh 
 
*Department of Environmental Engineering and Science, Fooyin University, 151 
Ching-Hsueh Road, Ta-Liao Hsiang, Kao-Hsiung Hsien 831, Taiwan. 
 
INTRODUCTION 
Ozone in the troposphere is produced in the daytime by photochemical reactions 
initiated by its precursors, nitrogen oxides (NOx) and volatile organic compounds 
(VOCs), in the presence of solar ultraviolet radiation(1)~(4). Important factors that 
influence the ground-level ozone concentration include precursor emissions, 
atmospheric chemistry, meteorology and deposition(5)~(7). 
According to Taiwan Environment Protection Agency (Taiwan EPA) researches, 
ozone episodic day can be classified into two groups in southern Taiwan, including 
regional ozone pollution and local ozone pollution. Local ozone pollution is usually 
ozone concentration at one monitoring site over the air quality standard. Local ozone 
episodic day is related to the high concentration of ozone precursors and 
meteorological factors at a specific pollution area(8)(9). 
The Kaoping air basin, in the southwest of Taiwan, is an important 
petrochemical/industrial region. Most of the petrochemical manufacturer and 
petroleum refineries of Taiwan are distributed in this area. Intensive industrial 
manufacturing emissions are believed to cause serious air pollution there, especially 
ozone pollution (10)~(12). 
In Kaoping area, the bad control of ozone pollution causes by many reasons, 
such as VOCs emission sources and emission characteristics of VOCs speciation do 
not understand(13). This work investigated the vertical distributions of NMHC, VOCs 
concentration and VOCs species analysis of ozone precursors via vertical profile air 
sampling at an important petrochemical/industrial area in Kaoping air basin. Vertical 
distribution of VOCs species in ambient was analyzed and investigated by gas 
chromatography with flame ionization detection (GC/FID). VOCs species from two 
locations sampling and analysis were identified with emission databases from Taiwan 
EPA.

Lin-Yuan petrochemical/industrial area was selected to investigate the vertical 
distribution of VOCs species region, it is one of the large petrochemical/industrial 
areas in Taiwan, is located at the Southern Coast of Taiwan. There are over 20 large 
petrochemical factories in this area, including petrochemical manufacturer and 
petroleum refineries. Two sampling locations were selected at Lin-Yuan 
petrochemical/industrial area, one is at the center position, and another is at the

in the emission sources from petrochemical/industrial area in the future. 
 
3. High Photochemical Ozone Creation Potential VOCs Species 
Ozone production rate is affected by its precursors, including NOx and VOCs. 
VOCs species and concentration in ambient effected on the ozone photochemical 
reactions. In general, the maximum incremental reactivity (MIR) and photochemical

Observation of the nocturnal variation of ozone reservoir layers in southern 
Taiwan 
Extended Abstract # 1103 
1,*Ching-Ho Lin, 1Chin-Hsing Lai, 2Yee-Lin Wu, 3Ming-Jen Chen 
 
1Department of Environmental Engineering and Science, Fooyin University, Kaohsiung, Taiwan. 
2Department of Environmental Engineering, National Cheng-Kung University, Tainan, Taiwan. 
3Department of Occupational Safety and Hygiene, Fooyin University, Taiwan 
 
INTRODUCTION 
Ozone is expected to be uniformly distributed in the convective mixing layer in the afternoon. 
After sunset, surface cooling forms a nocturnal boundary layer, and the decay of turbulence 
within the previous mixing layer forms a residual layer. The residual layer can serve as an 
ozone reservoir layer because ozone levels in this layer can remain as high as that in the 
previous mixing layer, due to a lack of ozone depleting sources, such as dry deposition and 
NO titration. The following morning, the ozone in the ozone reservoir layer can be mixed into 
the growing mixing layer and increasing the surface ozone levels. This scenario has been 
presented as an important mechanism as overnight ozone accumulation1-3. However, evidence 
in this respect is still limited because routine measurements of aerometric conditions and 
ozone concentrations aloft are lacking. 
Southern Taiwan is suffering from ozone pollution due to intensive heavy industries and 
petroleum refineries there. As well, previous studies in this area indicated that ozone episodes 
in this area are frequently associated with the presence of an ozone reservoir layer4. Therefore, 
this work aims to experimentally investigate the characteristics of ozone reservoir layers in 
this area. 
 
EXPERIMENT 
A tethered ozonesonde system was installed at an inland site (22.826° N, 120.529° E, 10 m 
above sea level) in southern Taiwan. The system comprised a helium-filled balloon 3 m in 
diameter, tethered to a 2 km long Kevlar line; an electric winch was used to control the ascent 
and descent of the balloon. A meteorological tethersonde and ozonesonde were tethered on the 
tethered line. During the ascent and decent of the balloon, vertical profiles of meteorological 
parameters and ozone were acquired. The meteorological tethersonde (TTS111, Vaisala) can 
accurately measure pressure, temperature, humidity, and wind speed and direction. Their 
resolutions are 0.1 hPa, 0.1 oC, 0.1 %, 0.1 m/s and 1o, respectively (Vaisala, Finland). The 
ozonesonde is based on the SPC (Science Pump Corporation, USA) model 6A ECC 
(Electrochemical Concentration Cell). The accuracy of the ECC ozonesonde was about ±6 % 
in the lower troposphere. Tethered soundings were performed daily from 29 October to 12 
November 2006 and intensively performed during a high-ozone period, 8-11 November. On 
days of high ozone concentrations, soundings were performed every 2-4 h, typically reaching 
altitudes of 1400-1600 m when the meteorological conditions permitted.
