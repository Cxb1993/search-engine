2. NANOPARTICLES AND LUBRICANT  
 
The morphology and structure of TiO2 and 
CuO nanoparticles were studied with scanning 
electron microscopy (SEM). Fig. 1 shows a 
typical SEM morphology of TiO2 and CuO 
nanoparticles. The main properties of the 
nanoparticles used in the experiments are 
listed in Table.1. TiO2 and CuO nanoparticles 
(Fig.1) were added into greases (LB80102 
lithium grease made in CPC Corporation) with 
ethanol as dispersant, mixed by mechanical 
stirring for 60 mins in triple mill. The physical 
properties are shown in Table.2. TiO2 and CuO 
nanoparticles were separately dispersed in the 
lubricant at oncentrations from 0.5wt.% to 
2.0wt.%. 
 
3. ANTI-WEAR TEST METHO 
 
The anti-wear properties and friction of the 
greases with TiO2 and CuO nanoparticles as 
additives in lithium grease at different 
concentrations were evaluated on a friction 
and wear testing machine set for pure sliding 
contact with a ball-on-flat configuration as 
shown schematically in Fig.2. The stroke 
length of the reciprocating motion is 10mm. 
All settings of the friction and wear testing 
machine followed the ASTM G133 standard 
method. In this study, all test-specimens were 
cleaned ultrasonically with eptanes for 5 min, 
rinsed in ethanol and dried with hot air before 
and after tests. The 10 mm diameter ISI 52100 
steel balls with a hardness of about 63HRc 
were run against a 50mm diameter, thickness 
mm 2024-T3 Aluminum alloy counterface flat 
with hardness of 75HRb. The Chemical 
compositions of Specimens are list in Table 3. 
All tests were run for 60 min at oscillating 
frequency of 4.0 Hz under a load of 200N 
applied via a cantilever. All tests were 
conducted for each suspension at this load, and 
wear was quantified by weight loss (±0.1 mg). 
The friction coefficient was recorded 
throughout each test by means of a load 
transducer positioned to measure the lateral 
force on the flat specimen. Wear surfaces on 
flats were characterized by using SEM and 
EDS to detect the elements present on the flats. 
 
4. EXPERIMENTAL RESULT 
 
4.1 Friction tests 
To reveal the mechanisms of additives under 
the lubrication condition, the lithium greases 
with TiO2 and CuO nanoparticles compared to 
pure lithium grease are shown in Fig.3. It can 
be seen that the friction coefficients of the 
lithium grease + TiO2 and CuO nano-particles 
as additives were lower than those of the 
lithium grease. In contrast, the lowest friction 
coefficient was obtained at 1.5 wt.% for the 
lubricant with CuO nano-particles and 1.0wt.% 
for the lubricant with TiO2 nano-particles, 
indicating that there may be a best additive 
 5
from the results presented above: 
1. TiO2 nano-particle additives can efficiently 
improve lubricating properties of the lithium 
grease. The lithium grease with 1.0 wt.% TiO2 
nano-particle provide the excellent tribological 
behavior, and significantly reduce the friction 
coefficient of the rubbing interface about 40% 
in the friction tests. 
2. The lithium grease with CuO nano-particle 
additives had best anti-wear performance in 
this study. The CuO nano-particle additive can 
easily penetrate into the rubbing face and form 
the adsorption film to protect the rubbing face 
in the friction tests. The wear of the lithium 
grease + 2.0wt.% CuO nano-particles was 
reduced about 60% in comparison with the 
wear of the lithium grease. 
3. There is an optimal content of nano-particle 
additives in lubricating grease, which gives the 
best friction behaviors and anti-wear ability. 
4. Different nano-particle can provide different 
advantage to improve the lubricating ability of 
the lubricants. In this study, both TiO2 and 
CuO nano-particles exhibit different 
tendencies of friction and wear behaviour 
depending on additive content. Shape ,size and 
hardness should be considered when the 
additives are solids.
