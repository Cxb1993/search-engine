中文摘要
本研究首先發展熱電致冷器模組的三維數值理論模式，可用來
分析熱電致冷器暫態之熱反應。既有文獻通常將 P及 N型半導體元
件結合成一體來討論分析，本報告提出非定電流模組，其將 P及 N
元件分開來個別探討，而得以觀察到 P型與 N型熱電元件之間的差
異。此模式主要將熱電致冷器分成七個區塊，分別為鋁質導片、P
與 N型熱電元件以及陶瓷基板，分析三維熱電模組的電場解與溫度
解，以及各部份之暫態溫度變化。針對熱電模組的各物理幾何參
數進行性能之計算。為了驗證數值模擬的準確性，同時建構了一
實驗系統進行冷熱端溫度變化的量測，經由實驗與模擬的結果互
相比對，來驗證理論模式。
另一方面，亦同時以矽晶圓為基材，利用微機電技術如離子
佈植、乾式蝕刻、濕式蝕刻及曝光顯影等製程來製作薄膜式微熱
電致冷元件，找出薄膜式微熱電致冷元件之最佳製程。另外，並
建立實驗系統量進行微熱電致冷元件溫度測試，且建構商用軟體
(ANSYS)分析模組模擬微熱電致冷元件性能，藉由將量測數據與
模擬計算結果比較，可以驗證模擬之準確性。利用數值模擬針對
不同參數所造成之影響進行分析，探討各個參數對熱電效率之影
響。
此外，本研究使用電化學沉積法將碲 -鉍 -銻薄膜沉積於 ITO

ABSTRACT
Simulation model has been developed and used to predict transient thermal
behavior of the thermoelectric coolers. The present models amend the previous
models, in which the P-N pair was simply treated as a single bulk material so that the
temperature difference between the semiconductor elements was not possible to
evaluate. To improve the defects, a non-constant current model has been proposed in
this report. In the non-constant current model, the conductive aluminum plates
connected with the P-type and the N-type elements are taken into consideration and
the theoretical module is divided into seven major blocks, namely, aluminum plates,
P-type and N-type thermoelectric elements, and the ceramic plates. Solutions for the
three-dimensional electric fields in the P-type and the N-type semiconductor elements
as well as transient temperature variations in the cold and the hot ends have been
carried out. To verify the numerical predictions, experiments have been conducted to
measure the temperature variations of both the cold and the hot ends. Close
agreement between the numerical and the experimental data of the temperature
variations has been observed.
On the other hand, a thin-film microscale thermoelectric cooler is fabricated in
the study. The fabrication technique for the thin-film microscale thermoelectric cooler
is developed by adopting MEMS manufacturing procedure. Meanwhile, a simulation
module with the commercial software ANSYS is built for predicting the performance
of the thin-film microscale thermoelectric cooler. The simulation module is then
applied to carry out a parametric study to investigate the effects of the parameters.

第五章 結果與討論
研究歸納總結論敘述如下：
1 .在數值模擬方面，本研究重點為發展一預測三維熱電模組的電場解與
溫度解，以及各部份之暫態溫度變化。由能量平衡的理論且考慮 P及 N
元 件 的 相 異 性 ， 推 導 出 一 熱 電 致 冷 器 的 數 值 理 論 模 式 ， 得 到 P型 與 N
型熱電材料的溫度分布圖與冷熱端的暫態溫度變化。
2 .建 立 了 bu lk熱 電 致冷 器 實 驗 系 統 與數值 模 擬 相 比 較 ，結果 驗 證 了 模 擬
與實驗結果互相重合 ，證實熱電模組理論模擬架構的正確性。
3 .在微機電製程加工積體化薄膜式微熱電致冷器方面，本研究先以微機
電製程加工找出微熱電元件製程之最佳操作參數，並同時架設實驗系
統設備，用以量測實驗結果以驗證微熱電致冷器模擬之準確性。並使
用 AN SYS軟體來進行微型熱電致冷器之模擬，藉由不同參數 P、N材料
幾何尺寸、冷端冷卻負載及輸入熱電元件之電流值來進行模擬分析以
得到一最佳效能之微型熱電致冷器設計參數。。
4 .由 研 究 結 果 得 知 ， 散 熱 對 於 熱 電 模 組 具 有 相 當 的 重 要 性 ， 熱 對 流 係
數，會使兩冷熱端板的溫差降低。若散熱不佳，則容易造成元件的損
害，反而使效能降低甚至失去功能；而電流越大本應增加熱電材料效
能，卻因焦耳熱隨電流的增加而造成兩端基板溫度皆上升，反而使效
率降低

Numerical Model for Predicting Transient Thermal Behavior of Thermoelectric Coolers 
 
Chin-Hsiang Cheng and Shu-Yu Huang 
Department of Aeronautics and Astronautics 
 National Cheng Kung University 
Tainan, Taiwan 70101, R.O.C. 
Abstract 
The present model amends the previous models, in which the P-N pair is simply treated as a 
single bulk material so that the temperature difference between the semiconductor elements was 
not possible to evaluate. Based on the present simulation model, the thermoelectric cooler is 
divided into a number of major regions, including cold end, hot end, and the P-type and N-type 
thermoelectric elements. Solutions for the three-dimensional temperature fields in the P-type and 
the N-type semiconductor elements and transient temperature variations in the cold and the hot 
ends have been carried out. The performance of the thermoelectric cooler has been calculated in 
wide ranges of physical and geometrical parameters. The simulation model is adopted to perform 
a parametric study to investigate the effects of the parameters such as electrical current (I), 
cooling load per unit area (q), heat transfer coefficients on the surfaces (h and ha) and 
geometrical parameters such as total area of heat sink (As) and height of semiconductor elements 
(L). To verify the numerical predictions, experiments have been conducted to measure the 
temperature variations of both the cold and the hot ends. Close agreement between the numerical 
and the experimental data of the temperature variations has been observed. 
 
Introduction 
Applied current causes charged carriers in the material, whether they are electrons or holes, 
to diffuse from the cold side to the hot side. These mobile charged carriers migrating to the hot 
side leave behind their oppositely charged and immobile nuclei at the cold side, thus giving rise 
to a temperature difference. Since a temperature difference creates a driving potential for the 
phonons to drift back to the cold side resulting in heat conduction at equilibrium. Improving the 
performance of a thermoelectric materials involves controlling the motion of phonons and 
electrons/holes. The most important performance index of the thermoelectric materials is the 
figure of merit (ZT) which is defined by

performance of the thermoelectric micro-cooler with a nearly three-dimensional model which is

According to the literature survey [16], it is recognized that mostly the previous theoretical 
models are limited to the one-dimensional problems. However, in these existing models, the P–N 
element pair is simply treated as a single bulk material so that the difference in thermal behavior 
between the two semiconductor elements was not possible to evaluate. Only a few studies were 
conducted for the three-dimensional ones, for example, the model of Lee and Kim [15]. 
In practices, the P-type and the N-type single crystal silicon elements are made by doping 
boron and phosphorous, respectively, into the silicon wafer. According to the existing 
information, the properties of the P-type and the N-type silicon are dependent on the material 
doped, dopant density and doping time. For example, Beadle et al. [17] addressed that the 
electrical resistivity of N-type silicon is dependent on the dopant density. When the N-type 
silicon is doped at a dopant density of 5×1017 cm-3, its electrical resistivity can reach 2.3×10-4 Ω 
m.  The properties of the two semiconductor elements can be rather different under different 
doping conditions. As a result, it may lead to significant difference in temperature response in 
the P-type and N-type elements. Under these circumstances, Cheng, Huang, and Cheng [18] 
presented a three-dimensional model that treats the P-type and the N-type semiconductor 
elements as two separate parts and considers the difference in temperature between the two 
elements. However, in the model of Cheng, et al. [18], electric current is assumed to be uniform 
in all the components of the thermoelectric cooler. Hence, in the equations of the model, the 
currrent is treated as a constant thoroughly. As a matter of fact, the constant-current model is 
valid only for the cases with a sufficiently long P-type and N-type elements. For those with short 
or even thim-film elements, the constant-current assumption is no longer valid. This is the major 
concern of the present study in which the existing model [18] is modified and extended. By 
introducing a variable-current concept, the distributions of the electric potential and the current 
vector are firstly predicted and then introduced to the energy equation for solution of temperature 
distribution. The model is used to predict the transient thermal behavior of the thermoelectric 
coolers under various parameter combinations.

Experiments  
Figure 3 shows the experimental apparatus of this study. The major material of wind tunnel 
is SS41. The blower has a 100 mm-diameter circular exit used to activate the fluid and is 
connected with the rectangular entrance of expansion section with a flexible tube. The maximum 
volume flow rate is 25 m3min-1, and the maximum static pressure is 250 mm of water. The rotary 
speed of the blower is controlled by the frequency inventor which is Fuji FRENIC 5000G11 to 
alter the air velocity. The next part after the diffusion section is corner with corner vanes inside 
to make the fluid flow smoothly into the settling chambers. The honey straightener below the 
corner is made from straws to effectively eliminate the swirl and reduce the lateral variation of 
mean velocity and the turbulence intensity. Wire screens are placed every 50 mm to decrease the 
turbulence intensity and to provide an average flow field. It has four layers with different sizes to 
create three-segment tunnel. The Omega T-type thermocouples (copper/copper-constantan alloy) 
are installed for temperature measurement, whose error is within ±0.5 °C and the measuring 
range is -250°C to 350°C. For monitoring the hot and cold ends temperatures, one thermocouple 
is placed at on the hot-end ceramic plate, and one on the cold-end ceramic plate. Two of the 
thermocouples are installed for measuring the ambient temperature which one is placed at 5 mm  
 
 
 
Figure 3 Experimental apparatus 
 
 
before the exit of measurement section and the other is besides the thermoelectric module to 
attain the average value between them. Differential pressure transmitter (Omega Model-PX653) 
is adapted to conduct pressure measurement. It could used to measure pressure and pressure 
differences whose error is within ±0.25% and its measuring range is 0 to 0.1 inches height of 
water column. The tubes from two pressure-measurement rings located at the entrance and exit 
of the contraction section are linked to differential pressure transmitter to acquire the pressure 
differences between two pressure-measurement rings and the mean velocity at the exit of 
contraction section could be obtained. Fluke NetDAQ 2620A Data Logger is used for 
temperature data collection from the T-type thermocouples and also the voltage data from the 
differential pressure transmitter to get pressure differences and the flow velocity further. An 
RS232 serial interface is used to connect the data logger to a personal computer for real time data 
acquisition. 
The experiment is started after the power of the frequency inventor is turned on to activate 
the blower until the flow field is stable, and then the DC current is applied steadily by the power

As stated earlier, the constant-current model is valid only for the cases with a sufficiently

Figure 6 shows the effects of applied current on the transient variation in temperatures at 
steady state for the cases with I=0.5 and 1.0 A. As shown in this figure, the temperature of the 
hot end and the temperature difference between the hot and cold ends both increase with the 
applied current. This is because the heat sink term due to Thomson effect is proportional to 
current while the heat source term due to Joule heating is proportional to the square of current. 
As a result, once the value of current is elevated, the heat source term gradually dominates, and 
hence the slope of increase in the temperatures of the P-type and the hot end are both higher.  
Figure 7 shows the effects of heat transfer coefficient on the surface of the hot end on 
transient temperature variation. Basically, to increase heat dissipation by convection one may 
increases the magnitude of h, if the temperature difference and the heat transfer area are kept 
constant. It is found that the temperatures are decreased as the magnitude of h is increased.  
Figure 8 conveys the effects of heat transfer coefficient on the side faces of the P-type and 
N-type elements on transient temperature variation for base-line case. A heat loss from the 
surfaces of the P-type and N-type elements increases with the side-face heat transfer coefficient. 
It is observed that as ha is elevated from 5 to 15 Wm-2K-1, both Th and Tc are decreased. However, 
the temperature difference is only slightly influenced by a change in the heat transfer coefficient.  
 
Effects of the height of the semiconductor elements are exhibited in Figure 9. In Figure 9, 
the value of L is assigned to be 0.0008 and 0.0018 m.and it is found that the temperature 
difference between the two ends increases with L. The influence of an increase in element height 
is actually involved because it increases the thermal resistance against heat conduction from the 
hot end to the cold but also increases electrical resistance as well as the Joule heating effects.
