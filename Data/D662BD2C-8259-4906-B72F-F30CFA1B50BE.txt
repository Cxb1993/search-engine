In order to solve the increasing problems produced by the CPU in the computer. Some 
designs embed round heat pipes into the base of the heat sink to alleviate spreading resistance. 
The other development is concentrated on the vapor chamber heat sink. The vapor chamber is a 
vacuum vessel with a wick structure lining the inside walls that is saturated with a working 
fluid. The manufacturing process starts with copper stampings that provide precision flatness 
and, when applicable, multiple heat-input surfaces at different heights to accommodate 
components of different thickness. A copper powder wick is sintered into place, ensuring an 
integral conduction path from the wall to the vapor/wick interface where vaporization occurs. 
This study was concentrated on the systematic study for the effect of the wick structure on the 
vaporization of working fluid by an apparatus designed in the lab. The objective of this study 
was also to study the effect of the sintering process of the wick structure, infiltration of the 
working fluid and the cooling fins on the thermal resistance of the plate heat pipe heat sink. 
The results show that the wicks sintered using gas-atomized (GA) copper at 900˚C for 2 hours 
had better wicks properties and lower vaporization resistance (about 0.031˚C/W). The flat plate 
heat pipe heat sink using gas-atomized powder had lower thermal resistance (0.33˚C/W) than 
the other tested flat plate heat pipe heat sinks. 
Keywords: flat plate heat pipe, sintering, wick property, thermal properties.
