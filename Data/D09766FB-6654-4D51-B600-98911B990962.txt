Abstract 
In this work, the various weights of polyethyleneimine (PEI) were respectively supported on hydrated 
silica fume particles by the impregnation method. The prepared silica fume solid sorbents were characterized, 
and their thermal stabilities and reactivities toward CO2 were evaluated using a thermogravimetric analyzer. 
All the solid sorbents exhibited a type IV isotherm with a hysteresis loop of type H3. Due to the blocking of 
micropores and mesopores, the specific surface areas of the sorbents were significantly reduced when PEI was 
supported. The CO2 adsorption reaction was very rapid in the initial period (<3 min) but leveled off before 20 
min, thereafter the CO2 capture capacity had been nearly equal to its maximum value. Those PEI/silica fume 
sorbents prepared with the weight ratios between 1.0/1 and 1.4/1 had a larger CO2 capture capacity of 3.3 
mmol/g sorbent at 85℃. However, the sorbent prepared at a weight ratio of 0.7/1 was found to have the 
highest CO2 capacity per unit weight of PEI (6.7 mmol/g PEI) at 75℃ . Furthermore, the multiple 
adsorption/desorption cycling results indicated that the PEI/silica fume solid sorbents exhibit a good 
thermochemical stability and can be fairly regenerative in the temperature swing adsorption/desorption 
process. The results of this research may contribute to the development of amino-modified silica fume solid 
sorbents, and could be the basic data for further design of the CO2 absorption process.
