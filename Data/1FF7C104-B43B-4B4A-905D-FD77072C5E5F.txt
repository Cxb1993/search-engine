3.2 Water Quality and NOMs fractions 
The water qualities of raw water, rapid sand filter (SF) and UF membrane processes 
effluents are shown in Table 1. The DOC concentration of raw water was 6.54 mg L-1, which 
was higher than the national standard of source water. Specific ultraviolet absorbance (SUVA) 
is an index of aromatic contained in humic substance in surface water can be defined as a ratio 
of UV absorbance at 254 nm wavelength (UV254) to the concentration of DOC in water. The 
SUVA values for the above selected sample ranged from 1.21 to 1.70 L mg-1 m-1. It suggests 
that the raw water needs to be treated by the advanced treatment process, for instance, using 
the membrane technology to meet the drinking water quality standard. 
Table 1 presents the NOMs fraction and characterization of raw water, SF effluent and 
UF membrane process effluent, which indicated that the fraction of hydrophobic component 
was higher than that of hydophilic and transphilic components, based on a mass balance in 
terms of mg-C. The hydrophobic material percentages were 58.4%, 56.7% and 61.8% in raw 
water, SF and UF effluents, respectively. It means that the hydrophobic component was the 
predominant NOMs fraction, which also implies that pre-treatment processes not affected the 
NOMs fractionation in the treated water significantly. Besides, it was observed that the 
components of hydrophobic and hydrophilic NOMs were reduced 20.2% by RF treatment 
process and 54.3% by UF membrane treatment process, respectively. It suggests that the UF 
membrane process could reject the hydrophilic NOMs higher than the hydrophobic NOMs, as 
opposed to the RF treatment process. Besides, the removal efficiencies of transphilic and 
hydrophobic fractions by the UF process were 25.7% and 31.3%, respectively, which were 
higher than that by the SF process (14.3% and 20.2%). In general, the removal efficiency of 
NOMs fraction was much better by the UF process than that by the SF process. Furthermore, 
it also demonstrated that the pre-treat process can not remove the NOMs compositions 
specifically.

3.4 Determination of the Optimum Operating Conditions 
Figure 14 demonstrates the 3-D relationship among the cross-flow velocity (0.15 to 0.60 
m s-1), transmembrane pressure (75 to 150 psi) and the rejection ratio of DOC by NF270 
membrane. The rejection ratio of DOC increased slightly with increasing the cross-flow 
velocity (0.15 to 0.60 m s-1) at the constant pressure (below 110 psi). While the applied 
pressure controlled from 110 to 120 psi, the rejection ratio of DOC changes in cross-flow 
velocity was not significant. However, if the transmembrane pressure controlled at higher 
(over 120 psi), the DOC rejection ratios decreased slightly with increasing the cross-flow 
velocity. Generally, while NF270 membrane filtrated at the applied pressure from 85 to 115 
psi and the cross-flow velocity from 0.15 to 0.60 m s-1, the higher rejection ratios of DOC 
(over 94%) were obtained. 
The trend of UV254 reduction ratio related among the cross-flow velocity (0.15 to 0.60 m 
s-1) and the transmembrane pressure (75 to 150 psi) was shown in Figure 15. When operating 
at lower cross-flow velocity (below 0.25 m s-1), the reduction ratios of UV254 were over 92% 
regardless of the transmembrane pressure from 75 to 150 psi. While the cross-flow velocity 
operated over 0.30 m s-1, the reduction ratio of UV254 increased with increasing the 
transmembrane pressure but tended to be constant at higher pressure (over 110 psi), especially 
operated at higher cross-flow velocity (over 0.45 m s-1). On the contrary, the UV254 reduction 
ratio decreased with increasing the cross-flow velocity when operating at constant 
transmembrane pressure. When the transmembrane pressure controlled below 100 psi, the 
reduction ratios of UV254 decreased from 94 to 86% with increasing the cross-flow velocity 
from 0.15 to 0.60 m s-1. The trend of the UV254 reduction ratio with changing the cross-flow 
velocity (0.15 to 0.60 m s-1) seems to be similar to the case operated at higher pressure 
(over130 psi). However, if the transmembrane pressure operated from 105 to 115 psi, the 
reduction ratio of UV254 changes in cross-flow velocity was not significant. Generally, while 
NF270 membrane filtrated at the applied pressure from 100 to 140 psi and the cross-flow 
velocity from 0.40 to 0.60 m s-1, the higher rejection ratios of UV254 (over 92%) were 
obtained. Moreover, if the filtration conditions operated at the lower cross-flow velocity from 
0.15 to 0.30 m s-1, the rejection ratios of UV254 were also obtained approximately over 92% 
no matter what the transmembrane pressure operated (75 to 150 psi). 
By integrating the experimental results in this investigation, it is possible to determine 
the optimum operating conditions for reducing the DOC and UV254 in Kin-men raw water. 
Running the NF270 membrane filtration at lower cross-flow velocity (0.15 to 0.30 m s-1) was 
preferred owing to the expected lower energy cost. In other words, the better operating 
conditions for efficiently reducing the UV254 were the cross-flow velocity from 0.15 to 0.30 m 
s-1 regardless of the transmembrane pressure. Thus, supposing that operated at the 
transmembrane pressure from 90 to 115 psi and the cross-flow velocity from 0.15 to 0.45 m 
s-1, the better rejection ratio of NOMs (DOC and UV254) by NF270 was obtained. Besides, if 
the NF270 membrane filtrated at this range of conditions, the permeate flux was obtained 
around 8 to 10 cm3/cm2 per hour. 
Although the cross-flow velocity operated at 0.45 m s-1 could get the better DOC and 
UV254 reduction ratios, which was 98.4 and 95.8% at 100 psi and 115 psi, respectively. The 
energy consumption might increase if increasing the cross-flow velocity and the 
transmembrane pressure. Thus, based on above results, it is suggested that the transmembrane 
pressure (100 psi) and the cross-flow velocity (0.30 m s-1) was the better operating conditions. 
It could be gotten the better reduction ratios of DOC and UV254, which was 93.8 and 94.3%,
