by System Dynamics Modeling

Abstract: In order to improve efficiency and minimize inventory levels in the supply chain, a system dynamics modeling 
methodology is presented to express the dynamic complexity. The objective of the paper is to investigate, how system dynamics 
modeling can be supportive for strategy implementation in improving performance of supply chain. Besides conceptual 
considerations with real smartphone demand pattern, in several of scenarios: step demand pattern, pulse demand pattern, 
demand-information sharing, and lead time are simulated to observe the system’s behavior and the influence on the supply 
chain’s performance as well. This study also investigates the what-if sensitivity analyses of lead time interval and decision with 
respect to product variation and modular parameters. The model is useful in understanding the relationship in dynamic supply 
chain. We proposed the model on managerial decision-making and eliminated or reduced system chaos. Finally, by providing 
suggestion for improvements are addressed. 
 
 Keywords: Supply chain; System dynamics; Simulation modeling 
 
 
1. Introduction 
Supply chain dynamics is the interaction processes of 
the participants form different departments and companies 
(Higuchi & Troutt, 2004). The objective of the supply chain 
is to gain competitive advantage, by improving overall 
performance through taking a holistic perspective of the 
supply chain (Angerhofer & Angelides, 2000). Overall 
logistics control is to be designed that all “partners” are 
beneficiaries. In order to be efficient and cost effective 
across the entire system, total costs have to be minimized 
and activities have to be aligned from the strategic level 
through the managerial to the operational level (Simichi-
Levi & Kaminsky, 2000). Increasingly, the supply chain 
becomes the mechanism for coping with uncertainly 
demand, long lead time and capacity limed et 
al.( Forrester,1961; Lee et al.,1997; Angerhofer,2000; Toru 
& Marvin, 2004; Bernhard & Marios, 2006; Mustafa & 
Theopistic & Melek, 2007); so that it is often inefficient for 
any partner among the supply chain.  
The system dynamics modeling approach has been 
widely used to study the structures of complex systems, 
such as supply chain networks (Beamon, 1998). In this 
work, we employ the system dynamics (SD) methodology, 
well known and proven in strategic decision-making, as the 
major modeling and analysis tool in this research. The plan 
of the paper is as follows: the introduction, literature review, 
model description, results of simulation and conclusions 
sections.

3.2 Design of scenarios 
Two performance measures are used in the simulation 
model: total inventory cost and total shortage cost (Simichi-
Levi & Kaminsky, 2000). The first two scenarios explore 
on the effect that a step function and pulse in demand will 
have on the system’s performance whereas the third and 
fourth scenarios focus on two lead time options; in 
particular it assumes the worst case i.e. that of lengthened 
lead time and its impact on the supply chain network. The 
fifth scenario concern changes in manufacturer capacity 
that the impact of efficient information sharing on the 
supply chain performance. 
 
3.2.1 Demand pattern 
Two types of demand patterns are involved, i.e., a step 
function and a broad-pulse function (H. Brian & Na Xie, 
2008). A step demand function (Fig. 4(a)), for example, 
may occur as event of price reductions or the promotion 
policy. On other hand, whether the demand increase occurs 
momentary, thus the demand exists only for a short period 
of time; the demand function will appear like a broad pulse 
(Fig. 4(b)), i.e. for some pop culture.
