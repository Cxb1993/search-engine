1. Introduction 
A switched system is composed of a family of subsystems and a switching signal that specifies which 
subsystem is activated to the system trajectories at each instant of time [1].  Switched systems are often 
encountered in many practical examples, such as automated highway systems, automotive engine control 
system, constrained robotics, robot manufacture, and stepper motors.  Many complicate system behaviors, 
such as multiple limit cycles and chaos, are produced by switching signal in systems [1-14].  It is also well 
known that the existence of delay in a system may cause instability or bad performance in closed control 
systems [15-18].  Time-delay phenomena are usually appeared in many practical systems, such as AIDS 
epidemic, chemical engineering systems, hydraulic systems, population dynamic model, and rolling mill.  
Hence stability analysis and stabilization for discrete switched systems with time delay have been studied in 
recent years [2-4, 6-7, 9-10, 12-14]. 
 There are three basic problems in dealing with the stability of discrete switched systems: (1) Find the 
stability or controller design of switched systems under an arbitrary switching signal [3-4, 8-9, 13]; (2) 
Identify the useful stabilizing switching signal for switched systems [14]; (3) Construct the stabilizing 
switching signal for switched systems [6-7].   
 In the recent years, H  concept was proposed to reduce the effect of the disturbance input on the 
regulated output to within a prescribed level [4, 19-22].  In [22], H  finite time boundness for discrete 
switched system was considered.  In [2, 4, 10], H  controls were proposed for uncertain discrete 
switched systems under arbitrary switching signal.  The H  controls were investigated by identified the 
switching signal via dwell time approach [21].  Linear fractional perturbations in [16, 19, 23] are the 
generalized perturbed forms for parameter perturbations in [4, 9-10, 20-21]. 
It is interesting to note that the stable property for each subsystem cannot imply that the overall system 
is also stable under arbitrary switching signal [4].  Another interesting fact is that the stability of a switched 
system can be achieved by choosing the switching signal even when each subsystem is unstable [6-7].  
Although many important results have been proposed to design switching signal of switched time-delay 
systems, but there are only few reports concerning the switching signal design of discrete switched 
time-delay systems [6-7].  In the first part of this project, a new scheme for switching signal design is 
developed to guarantee global exponential stability of switched time-delay system.  In the second part of 
this project, the switching control and switching signal will be designed to guarantee H  performance of 
the discrete switched systems with interval time-varying delay and linear fractional perturbations.  Some 
additional nonegative inequalites are used to improve the conservativeness for the obtained results [4, 18].  
Interval time-varying delay and linear fractional perturbations of system are also included in our considered 
problem.  By the proposed approach, our results are shown to be less conservative than some recent reports 
in our denomonstrated numerical examples.

Conference on Mechatronics and Automation」，主要目的是發表研究成果論文，題目為「Passivity

各地，多位知名學者發表機電與自動化領域方面的學術論文，與會過程中學習到相當多的技術知識，

8月 5日當天從高雄搭乘遠東航空公司的班機直飛四川成都，由於航班關係到達已經是晚上七點
多，即入住飯店準備隔天的報到註冊和論文發表。在 8月 6日前往會議舉辦地點望江賓館報到，並聽
取來自美國加州聖地牙哥大學的 Miroslav Krstic 教授以“Source Seeking for Nonholonomic Vehicles

Automatic Control and Automatica 資深編輯，同時也是 IEEE及 IFAC 的 Fellow，在學術領域具有相當
聲望。從來源追蹤(Source Seeking)對於未知系統的穩定性分析與探討是相當重要的，且以輸出回授控
制方式進行研究，可應用於現今機器人產業上所面臨的控制問題，同時更輔以 Deterministic微擾法及
Stochastic 微擾法進行數值分析。從研究結果顯示，以這兩種方式之數值模擬結果具有良好的收斂性
與準確性，且同時應用於模擬機器人進行實際狀況測試，並與數值模擬比較控制效果之優劣。

的學者發表相關著作和研究成果，其中印象最深刻的是，來自中國哈爾濱大學一群研究學者所發表有

 Abstract - The passivity analysis and switching signal design 
for uncertain discrete switched systems with interval 
time-varying delay and linear fractional perturbations is 
considered in this paper.  Some LMI criteria are proposed to 
design the switching signal to guarantee the passivity of discrete 
switched time-delay system.  Some nonnegative inequalities are 
introduced to reduce the conservativeness of the proposed 
conditions.  Finally, a numerical example is illustrated to show 
the main proposed result. 
 
 Index Terms-Switching signal; passivity analysis; internal 
stability; discrete switched system; interval time-varying delay; 
nonnegative inequality. 
 
I.  INTRODUCTION 
Switched systems are often encountered in many practical 
systems including automated highway systems, automotive 
engine control systems, chemical process, constrained robotics, 
power systems and power electronics, robot manufacture, and 
stepper motors [1-12].  A switched system is a hybrid system 
which consists of several subsystems and a switching signal 
that handle the switching among them [8].  Switching among 
systems may produce many complicate nonlinear system 
behaviors, such as multiple limit cycles and chaos [8].  It is 
also well known that the existence of delay in a system may 
cause instability or bad performance in closed loop control 
systems [1-7, 9-14].  Time-delay phenomena are usually 
confronted in many practical systems, such as AIDS epidemic, 
chemical engineering systems, hydraulic systems, inferred 
grinding model, neural network, nuclear reactor, population 
dynamic model, and rolling mill.  Hence stability problem for 
discrete switched systems with time delay have been 
investigated in recent years [1-7, 9-12]. 
There are two interesting facts in switched system: the 
first one is that the stable property for each subsystem cannot 
imply that the overall system is also stable under arbitrary 
switching signal [2, 7-8, 10-11].  The another one is that the 
stability of a switched system can be achieved by choosing the 
switching signal even when each subsystem is unstable [3, 5-6, 
8].  In [5], a switching signal design technique is proposed to 
guarantee the asymptotic stability and stabilization of discrete 
switched systems with interval time-varying delay.  In [11], 
the switching signal is identified to guarantee the stability of 
discrete switched time-delay system.  In recent years, 
passivity analysis technology was provided in diverse aspects, 
such as complexity [15], fuzzy control [16-17], neural 
networks [18-19], and signal processing [20].  The passivity 
theory was firstly introduced in circuit analysis.  The 
passivity theory is a promising approach to keep the internal 
stability of systems.  In the paper, the switching signal is 
designed to guarantee passivity performance of the discrete 
switched systems with interval time-varying delay and linear 
fractional perturbations.  Linear fractional perturbations in 
[12-13] are the generalized perturbed forms for parameter 
perturbations in [2, 7, 18].  Some nonegative inequality terms 
are used to improve the conservativeness for the obtained 
results [2-3].  A numerical example is provided to 
demonstrate the use of obtained results.  From the simulation 
result, our proposed approach provides some less conservative 
results.

Two issues can be considered in passivity analysis of 
switched systems: 
1. Under arbitrary switching signal, passivity analysis and 
passive control can be investigated to guarantee the 
performance for uncertain discrete switched systems. 
2. Design a switching signal to guarantees the passivity 
property for uncertain discrete switched systems. 
Note that the matices 1A  and 2A  in this example are not 
Hurwitz ( have at least one eigenvalue larger than 1), the 
results in [2] and [7] cannot find any feasible solution to 
guarantee the stability for discrete switching systems for any 
arbitrary switching signal.  To the best of author’ knowledge, 
there are few results considering the passivity analysis of 
discrete switched time-delay systems.

IV. CONCLUSIONS 
  In this paper, the switching signal design to guarantee 
the passivity property for uncertain discrete switched systems 
with interval time-varying delay and linear fractional 
perturbations has been considered.  Some nonnegative 
inequalities and LMI approach are used to improve the 
conservativeness of the proposed results.  The obtained 
results are shown to be less conservative via the numerical 
example.
