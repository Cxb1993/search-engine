一、中文摘要
普通混凝土存在的界面過渡區其力學
性質一般皆有較弱情況發生，本計畫擬利
用不同種類之輕質骨材所拌製而成之輕質
骨材混凝土(LWAC)，探討其因不同吸水率
之輕質骨材而造成之界面過渡區性質變
化。
經由 4 種實驗驗證結果顯示，當不同
種類之輕質骨材拌製而成之輕質骨材混凝
土，因為輕質骨材具有較高於常重骨材之
吸水率，其高吸水率之輕質骨材周圍並未
出現較弱之界面過渡區。由微硬度試驗結
果更可以發現，愈接近輕質骨材之界面過
渡區域其強度愈佳，代表其輕質骨材混凝
土之界面過渡區域較強於常重混凝土者。
而添加卜作嵐材料對於輕質骨材混凝土之
界面過渡區強度提升有限，但對於常重骨
材界面過渡區之弱面改善較為明顯。
關鍵詞：輕質骨材混凝土、界面過渡區、
吸水率
Abstract
A phenomenon that frequently exists in
ordinary concrete is the weaker mechanical
properties of its interfacial transition zone
(ITZ). For this reason, in this research, light-
weight aggregate concretes (LWAC) made
from various types of lightweight aggregate
are used to investigate the changes of ITZ
properties caused by different water absorp-
tion.
The results verified by 4 kinds of ex-
periments show that, when lightweight ag-
gregate concretes (LWAC) are made from
various types of lightweight aggregate, since
the higher water absorption of lightweight
aggregate compared to normal aggregate,
there is no weaker ITZ appeared around the
of lightweight aggregate with high water ab-
sorption. It could be found from microhard-
ness tests that, the closer the region to ITZ of
lightweight aggregate is, the higher the
strength possessed. This means the ITZ of
LWAC is stronger than that of normal con-
crete. The effect of the adding pozzolanic
materials to increase the ITZ strength of
LWAC is limited. However, it is obvious to
improve the weak phase for the ITZ of nor-
mal aggregate.
Keywords: Lightweight aggregate concrete,
Interfacial transition zone, Absorption

(1)數位微觀檢測儀及 SEM 觀察－
本研究試驗係利用掃瞄式電子顯微鏡
進行 SEM 之觀測，如照片 1-3 所示，由這
些照片可發現，無論輕質骨材或常重骨材
混凝土，當中界面過渡區都存在著許多微
小的微裂縫，此些微裂縫皆是造成骨材和
漿體之間界面過渡區弱面的主因。
根據 SEM 所拍攝照片發現，常重骨材
混凝土的微裂縫分佈於骨材表面周圍，而
輕質骨材混凝土則是分佈距骨材表面約
10μm至 30μm處，主要輕質骨材的吸水特
性可將骨材表面周圍之漿體變的較緻密，
而微裂縫則是於弱面處生成，因此輕質骨
材混凝土當中界面過渡區之微裂縫，則是
於緻密漿體之後方生成。而常重骨材混凝
土由於骨材本身吸水率較小，於混凝土灌
製的過程中，水分容易聚集於骨材之周
圍，因此骨材周圍之水膠比高，漿體較不
緻密，微裂縫易於骨材周圍生成。

倍時，可以發現界面過渡區存在著較多未
反應完全的飛灰顆粒。然而飛灰本身屬卜
作嵐材料，主要是和水泥水化產物 CH 結
晶體反應生成 C-S-H 膠體。而常重骨材混
凝土當中，骨材和漿體之間界面過渡區蘊
藏了大部份的 CH 結晶體，可充分和過渡
區當中之飛灰顆粒進行卜作嵐反應，將飛
灰反應為具膠結性之 C-S-H 膠體。反觀組
別 LF 之過渡區影像，推測輕質骨材混凝
土，界面過渡區當中較無 CH 結晶體的堆
積，造成未反應飛灰顆粒較多的情況發生。

Evaluating Drying Shrinkage of
Self-Compacting Concrete in a Caisson
Using Numerical Methods
by T. H. Liu, H. J. Chen, H. W. Liao and C. I. Lin
Synopsis: Self-compacting concrete (SCC), characterized by the high flowability and
high resistance to segregation, is due to the high amount of paste (including cement and
mineral admixtures) in contrast with normal concrete (NC). However, the high amount of
paste will limit the volume fractions of coarse aggregate and reduce the tendency of
coarse aggregate to suppress drying shrinkage deformations. For this reason, SCC can be
expected to produce higher values of drying shrinkage than NC.
In order to assess the drying shrinkage of SCC quantitatively for application to
offshore caisson foundations, the formulas presented in the literature (ACI 209 and
CEB-FIP) are used to predict the values of drying shrinkage in SCC according to the
corresponding mixture proportioning. Additionally, a finite element (FE) model, which
assumes concrete to be a homogeneous and isotropic material and follows the actual size
and environmental conditions of the caisson, is utilized to simulate situations of stress
distribution and deformations in the SCC caisson resulting from the drying shrinkage.
The likelihood of cracking and the behavior of drying shrinkage of the SCC caisson are
drawn from the analytic results calculated by the FE model proposed in this paper.
Keywords: caisson foundation; drying shrinkage; self-compacting concrete

RELIABILITY OF NUMERICAL CALCULATION
The precision of numerical calculation depends on whether the result is
convergent after calculation. Different mesh methods were used to test the precision of
the numerical calculation. The actual dimensions of the caisson, a rectangular block (18
m in height, 16 m in width) with 16 rectangular hollow parts are shown in Fig. 1(a). The
thickness of internal and external walls is 25 cm and 60 cm respectively. Considering the
symmetric deformations of the actual caisson resulting from drying shrinkage, the
numerical model is regarded as a 1/8 caisson (as shown in Fig. 1(b)). The symmetry of
the structure is significant and essential for FE analyses because the mesh of symmetric
partition can reduce the number of elements drastically and improve the efficiency
simultaneously with the same precision as that of an entire structure. As a result, the
boundary condition of the numerical model is treated so that the bottom is only
constrained in the Z direction and the symmetric plane is only constrained in the normal
direction. That is to say, the center of the bottom is fixed in three directions (X, Y, and
Z-axis) and which is also a center of symmetry when the caisson contracts. Besides, the

NUMERICAL ANALYSIS
Deformation of Caisson
The drying shrinkage strains, averaged from the results of the three prediction
formulas (see Fig. 3), were used to predict the drying shrinkage behavior of the caisson.
The shape of the caisson model suffering from drying shrinkage deformation is shown in
Fig. 4. The displacement of nodes ranges from 0.11~0.38 mm in X, Y-axis direction, and
0.68~0.89 mm in Z-axis direction. The figure shows that the bottom slab provides
resistance to restrain the caisson from deforming in X, Y-axis direction. Therefore, the
transverse deformation of the caisson increases with height and the maximum occurs in
the top layer. However, the influence of the bottom slab on the vertical deformation of the
caisson is comparatively insignificant, and primarily depends on the axial stiffness of
walls the in Z-axis direction. Obviously, the external wall possesses higher stiffness than
the internal wall, and therefore brings about the lower corresponding values of
displacement.
As mentioned above, the constraints contributed from the bottom slab and the
drying shrinkage will be stopped for concrete in water due to the full supply of moisture,
the duration of drying shrinkage before the caisson placed into water (7 days) should be
consider as shrinkage time. The 7-day compressive strength of SCC is 26.7 MPa, and the
corresponding tensile strength ranging from 1.7 to 3.4 MPa is used to simulate the actual
situation of caisson. As shown in Fig. 7, the maximum tensile stress caused by shrinkage
is 0.94 MPa (region 9), and which is under the values of tensile strength of SCC
estimated at 7 days. This indicates that, for the case of this study, SCC is able to attain the
requirements for the application to offshore caisson foundations in spite of the larger
drying shrinkage than traditional concrete due to its high cement paste content. In
addition, even if the tensile stress caused by shrinkage is higher than the tensile strength
of SCC, the depth of cracks can still be determined according to Fig. 7.
CONCLUSIONS
A numerical method proposed in this paper is used to simulate the behaviors of
drying shrinkage and to predict the probability of cracking in a SCC caisson foundation.
The conclusions can be summarized as follows:
1. The numerical calculation results show that a better convergence can be achieved if
the aspect ratio (length, width and height) of the element is within 4 and the mesh
angle of the element is between 45° and 135°. The maximum deviation of principal
stress or principal strain is within ±0.5％.
2. Based on the deformation situations of the caisson, the larger variation of
deformation resulting in the higher tensile stress is found in the internal wall close to
the bottom slab and the external wall, and the procedures to calculate real stresses
from the FE model after analysis can be simplified.
3. For the stress distribution in the caisson, the maximum tensile stress is 0.94 MPa
(region 9) and which is under the values of tensile strength of SCC (1.7~3.4 MPa).
