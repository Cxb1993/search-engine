台灣附近海域及西太平洋海水面變化 
 
一、前言 
因為交通和生活方便之利，世界上多數人口均聚居於海岸附近，因此許多大城市都位於沿海。距
今約 21000 年前為 Last Glacial Maximum (LGM)時期，海水面比現今低了約 125 公尺，紐奧良和邁阿密
等都市不似今日緊鄰海邊，海岸線比現在向海洋多了數百公里。最近研究指出平均海水面上升每年約
為 1.5 到 2.4 公厘[e.g., Douglas, 2001; Mitrovica et al., 2001; Church et al., 2004]，因此人口密集的可居住
地正逐漸被海水淹沒。Leatherman 等人(2000)指出，當全球海水面上升時， 侵蝕海岸速率為海水面上
升速率的幾十至幾百倍，使得人類居住區域越來越受到嚴重侵害，而暴風浪也因海水面上升而增強，
造成沿岸之建築物及人類生命財產巨大損失。另外，Ericson 等人(2006)研究成果顯示如果全球海水面
變化以 2 mm/yr 速率上升，2050 時，全球有 40 三角洲上的 8,710,000 人會遭受到因海水面上升所產生
的災害。 
台灣為一海島型國家，平原區域僅佔全島面積之 30％~40%，而大部分人口均居住於靠海岸旁的低
海拔平原區域，因此生活與海洋息息相關，受到海洋災害的威脅也比較大。相對於其他國家，海水面
上升所造成的低窪地區之洪水泛濫、海岸地區海灘和岩壁之侵蝕、海水入侵陸地表面及地下水層等等
的災害， 以及暴風浪造成瞬時人民生命財產的損失皆比較嚴重，以台灣西海岸為例，地形梯度約為
1/1000，因此海水面若上升 1 公分，海岸線將退縮 10 公尺。另外，生態環境會受到嚴重破壞，例如七

Taiwan coastal ocean 
 
Chung-Yen Kuo1, Huan-Chin Kao1, Hyongki Lee2 
 
1Department of Geomatics, National Cheng-Kung University, Tainan, Taiwan

provided by data centers such as TOPEX/Poseidon (T/P) and Jason-2 (J-2) Geophysical Data Record 
(GDR) and the Sensor Geophysical Data Record (SGDR). In order to improve or retrieve data near coasts, 
a waveform retracking technique, which re-calculates the deviation of the midpoint of the leading edge 
from the onboard tracking gate, has to be implemented.  
 
Various retrackers have been developed in previous studies (e.g., Deng et al., 2002), including NASA V4 
(β-) (Martin et al., 1983), Off Center of Gravity (OCOG) (Bamber, 1994), original (Davis, 1997) and 
modified threshold (Lee et al., 2008), ocean, and ice retrackers (OSTM, 2009) and successfully applied in 
difference areas with diverse surfaces including the improvement for retrieving altimetric SSHs near 
shorelines. Deng and Featherstone (2006) retrieved ERS-2 SSHs using waveform retracking to obtain more 
reliable data around Australian shorelines. Fenoglio-Marc et al. (2008) found that waveform-retracked T/P 
and ENVISAT showed significant agreement with the tide gauge records in the coastal regions of the 
Mediterranean Sea. Lee et al. (2010) evaluated J-2 measurements over California coastal oceans retracked by 
a set of retracking algorithms and showed that the criterion for selecting a suitable retracker is straightforward 
by analyzing the shape of waveforms returned from different characteristics of surfaces. 
 
In this study, we followed the approach by Lee et al. (2010) and reprocessed T/P and J-2 measurements in 
oceans around Taiwan using a set of waveform retrackers to improve the quality and the number of observed 
SSHs and assessed the retracked data in order to find the optimal retracking algorithms. In addition, we 
validated retracked J-2 SSHs by comparing the data with Anping tide gauge records. By contrast with the 
study areas of the previous studies, oceans around Taiwan are a ideal testing bed for waveform retracking 
algorithms, the conclusion of which presents a general and advisable reference for the use of future researches, 
due to their drastically different geophysical features; for example, various sea states including high, medium, 
and low tides (Matsumoto et al., 2000), and rapidly changing bathymetries (Liu et al., 1998). A detailed 
description of the study area is presented in the next section.   
 
2. Study areas 
   
Taiwan is a small island in a north-south direction surrounded by the Philippine Sea, the Luzon Strait, the 
South China Sea, the Taiwan Strait, and the East China Sea in clockwise order starting from the east, in the 
western Pacific region. As shown on the left side of Figure 1, the M2 ocean tide (Matsumoto et al., 2000) and 
the bathymetry (Amante and Eakins, 2009) around Taiwan are extremely complicated. The amplitude of the 
M2 tide in the Taiwan Strait is relatively large at 200 cm compared to that in the Philippine Sea (40 cm). The 
largest amplitude appears in the middle of the Taiwan Strait and decreases toward the north and the south. Sea 
bathymetry around Taiwan is highly complex and can basically be separated into two parts: one is the Taiwan 
Strait, which is shallow with an average depth of 100 m, and the other is the Philippine Sea, where bathymetry 
changes dramatically, in some places reaching 1000 m at less than 5 km from shorelines. The various sea 
states and bathymetry of oceans around Taiwan make it an excellent region for evaluating the accuracy of 
retracked satellite altimetry. 
 
There are two ground tracks of altimeters T/P and J-2 passing the study area, namely ascending pass 51 
 16
records, which were de-tided using harmonic analysis (Pawlowicz et al., 2002).

could perform better than other retrackers to optimally retrieve SSHs with an IMP of 68.0% and STD of 0.229 
m. As mentioned above, ocean tides around Taiwan are very complicated and difficult to be modeled, so the

Abstract 
    In this study, five campaigns with a Global Positioning System (GPS) buoy near a tide gauge at Anping, 
Tainan, Taiwan, were processed with Precise Point Positioning (PPP) technique using four different satellite 
orbit products from International GNSS Service (IGS), including the final, the rapid, and the ultra-rapid 
products (observed half and predicted half). The GPS buoy data were also positioned by the differential GPS 
(DGPS) with an additional GPS receiver as a reference station on shore. The average Root Mean Square Error 
(RMSE) differences of the GPS buoy with PPP and with DGPS changes depending on the accuracy of IGS 
products. In addition, in order to absolutely verify the results, real water level data from Anping tide gauge 
were introduced. The comparison shows the average RMSE difference of the GPS buoy with DGPS scheme 
and tide gauge records is around 3-5 cm. When using the aforementioned IGS orbit products for the buoy with 
PPP technique, its average RMSE differences to the tide gauge records are 5-8 cm, 8-13 cm, decimeter level, 
and decimeter–meter level, respectively. It is concluded that with the current capabilities of IGS orbit products, 
the accuracy for a GPS buoy with PPP technique has the potential to monitor the sea surface variations to 
several cm.  
 
Keywords：GPS buoy, precise point positioning, tide gauge, sea level variation

4. Conclusions 
 
Five GPS tracking stations were used one at a time as the reference stations to solve ellipsoidal heights of 
the GPS buoy with DGPS algorithm. The solutions were compared to that of S810, which is a GPS control 
point and is 1.5 km away from the buoy, as the ground truth to test the impact of baseline length on accuracy. 
The analysis shows that positioning accuracy degrades with increasing baseline between the fixed station and 
the GPS buoy. 
 
The coordinates of the GPS buoy estimated with S810 as the reference station using DGPS technique, 
were considered as the ground truth for comparisons. The RMSE difference of the GPS buoy is about 1-4 cm 
in the horizontal component and 7-11 cm in the vertical when the IGS final product is used for kinematic PPP. 
This accuracy satisfies most positioning needs for a highly varied sea surface. When the IGS rapid product is 
used for PPP, the RMSE difference is 4-7 cm in the horizontal component and 11-18 cm in the vertical, which 
agrees the computation of Chen et al. (2004). When the observed half of the IGS ultra-rapid product is used, 
the RMSE difference is 9-27 cm in the horizontal component and 23-44 cm in the vertical. This accuracy is 
suitable for detecting larger sea level variations, such as tsunamis or storm surges. When the predicted half is 
used, the RMSE difference is < 1 m in the horizontal component and <2 m in the vertical, which is not ideal 
for sea level observations. 
  
 If the tide gauge records are considered as the true sea level variations, the average RMSE difference of 
the GPS buoy with DGPS is 3-5 cm. When the IGS final product, the rapid product, and the ultra-rapid 
products (observed and predicted) are used for the buoy with PPP, the average RMSE differences of the sea 
level variations are 5-7 cm, 7-13 cm, decimeter level, and 1-2 m, respectively. Therefore, the high-sample sea 
level variations can be potentially monitored by implementing a GPS buoy with PPP using the IGS final 
product instead of tide gauge stations. In this study, GPS buoy were deployed in the unideal location for the 
five campaigns, where GPS signals would be blocked by the breakwater, so the positioning accuracy can be 
improved if deployed in open oceans to avoid cycle slips. 
 
 The receivers used in the experiments do not support L2C signal by that time. If using a receiver 
supporting L2C format with sufficient number of satellites, the positioning accuracy should be further 
improved. In addition, more accurate real-time orbit and clock products, provided by the Jet Propulsion 
Laboratory (JPL) with charge, could improve the positioning accuracy as well (Chen, 2004). Because the PPP 
process engine applied in this study does not support that real time orbit and clock products at the moment; 
therefore, the scope of this study is limited to investigate the potential of using PPP for sea level monitoring, 
instead of proposing a real time monitoring system. Because of this future improvement, the GPS buoys with
