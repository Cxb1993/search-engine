Abstract
In this study, Mn ion was implanted into Ge wafer. Post-annealing was performed using 350
keV He+ ion with the sample kept at 300oC. The structural properties were characterized by Near
Edge Analysis (XANES). Magnetic properties of GeMn thin films were investigated as a function
of temperature and magnetic field.
Introduction
GeMn is one of the group-IV diluted magnetic semiconductors (DMSs) has received
remarkable attention both in experiment and theory in recent years due to its compatibility with
the main-strain silicon technology[1-4]. But due to the poor solubility of transition metal ion
(~1E15 #/cm3 at room temperature for Mn in Ge substrate [5]) in the conventional
semiconductors, there are only few methods to prepared GeMn thin film. Ion implantation is a
flexible tool for doping these ions into semiconductors, especially at non-equilibrium condition.
So we tried to fabricate GeMn thin film by ion implantation and following ion beam annealing,

2. SQUID
The magnetic properties of Mn-implanted samples were measured using a superconducting
quantum interference device magnetometer (SQUID). Figure 2 and 3 show the filed dependent
magnetization, M, at 5K and 300K for the samples of Mn concentration respect 0.25% and 10%.
Clearly elongated hysteresis loop are observed in figure 2 and 3. The diamagnetic background
was subtracted from the data. It is interesting that the saturated magnetization of low temperature
(5K) is almost the same value compared with room temperature saturated magnetization for the
sample with Mn 0.25%. The 10% Mn sample was not shown the sample properties, its room
temperature saturated magnetization is smaller than low temperature ones. Figure 4 shows the
remnant moment, it was measured after 1T filed cooling process. The Mn 0.25% sample has a Tc
