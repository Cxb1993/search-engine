reliable to evaluate the ecological effects of
heavy metals on aquatic environment with the
total concentration of heavy metals in the
sediment. The purposes of this study are to
investigate the effects of sediment
characteristics on bioavailability of heavy
metal in the sediment, and to assess the
relationship between bioavailability and
speciation of heavy metals in the aquatic
environment. In this study, field collected
sediments and biota (shrimps) were
determined for physicochemical
characteristics and metal (Cu, Zn, Pb and Ni)
concentrations. The speciation of metals in the
sediment was also quantified by a sequential
extraction method. Meanwhile, the metal
bioavailability and bioconcentration factor
(BCF) in the sediment were calculated to
evaluate their relationship with sediment
characteristics. Based on these results, several
metal bioavailability models were established
in this study.
The results indicated that the dominant
speciation of Cu in the sediment was organic
matter/sulfide-bound and residual forms, and
minor Cu was associated with carbonates.
Zn mainly existed as Fe/Mn oxide-bound and
residual speciation. Pb was also found in high
percentage in the exchangeable and residual
speciation. Ni was predominantly found in
residual form of sediment. The environmental
impacts of metal speciation in the sediment
depend on the behavior regarding
remobilization and bioavailability. It was
observed that Zn and Pb in the sediment had
high potential bioavailability and mobility.
Except Ni, bioavailable metals (Cu, Zn and Pb)
highly depended on the cation-exchange
capacity (CEC), carbonates content and
Fe-Mn oxides concentration in the sediment.
The significant relationships were also found
between metals (Zn and Pb) in the biota and
mobile metals (exchangeable,
carbonate-bound and Fe/Mn oxide-bound) in
the sediment. High contents of Cu and Zn
were found in the shrimp. The contents of Pb
and Ni in the shrimp were very low. It is
obvious that Cu and Zn in the shrimp
increased during the cultivation period. Six
metal bioavailability models established in
this study were useful to describe the
relationships between metal bioavailability
and metal characteristics.
Keywords: bioavailability, bioconcentration,
heavy metal, sediment, sequential extraction.
二、緣由與目的
近年來因工業廢水、廢氣、廢棄物之不
當處置，以及農藥、肥料過度使用，使得土
地及水源品質日益惡化，也使養殖戶取用到
受重金屬污染土地及水源之機會大幅增
加。由此可知，在重金屬鹽類藥物於水產養
殖池廣泛且長久持續施用以及水土品質日
益惡化之下，重金屬過量存在於水產養殖池
中，造成養殖池之嚴重污染及老化問題，同
時更進一步使重金屬過量累積在水產品體
內，影響水產品品質與人體健康安全，甚至
對生態環境產生衝擊。水產生物一旦持續生
活於污染之水域環境中，其可能藉由生物濃
縮、生物蓄積與生物放大等作用，將水及底
泥中之重金屬蓄積在體內各組織內，甚至對
水產生物造成生理與毒性影響。由此可知，
水產養殖池底泥的物理、化學及生物特性與
水產養殖生物的產量及品質有密切的關係。
以重金屬總量探討底泥重金屬對水體的
污染程度、可移動性、風險評估或對水生生
物的影響，常無法得到有效且合理的評估，
因此需針對底泥重金屬的鍵結合型態加以
研究。若以環境風險評估的觀點為考量，底
泥中重金屬鍵結型態通常比總重金屬量更
能反應出，底泥中重金屬對於水中生物的毒
性 影 響 及 其 生 物 有 效 性
(bioavailability)(Rensing and Maier, 2003)。重
金屬與底泥之鍵結愈強，愈不會釋出至水中
造成生物危害性，其存在狀態亦愈穩定。然
而隨著工業廢水的排放，重金屬多以離子態
與溶解態等鍵結性較弱之型態進入水中並
累積於底泥，此部分之重金屬，易因環境變
動或物化條件的改變而釋出至水中，對水體
生物產生負面之影響 (Maiz et al., 1997)。本
研究主要之目的即為探討水產養殖池在受
重金屬污染狀況下，研究水產養殖池的底泥
重金屬鍵結型態特性，並深入探討其與對水
產生物的生物有效性間之相互關係，以提供
未來水產養殖池底泥管理及復育時之重要
參考。
分析重金屬含量 (NIEA C303.01T)。

3. 底泥中重金屬結合型態
由重金屬在水與底泥顆粒間之主要鍵結機
制可知：底泥中可交換態金屬的成因是底泥
顆粒對金屬離子的吸附作用所產生。若金屬
與水相中的碳酸根結合成不溶解的碳酸
鹽，鍵結型態則定義為碳酸鹽結合態。鐵錳
氧化物結合態金屬係指金屬離子受鐵錳的
氧化物吸附或產生共沈澱，及不溶性的金屬
氫氧化物兩部分。有機物/硫化物結合態則
指金屬與硫離子形成硫化物沈澱或是與有
機物形成錯合物沈澱。而殘存態之金屬主要
存在於底泥礦物晶格中，為天然存在者
(Calmano et al., 1993)。圖 1 為 A 養殖場底
泥中重金屬鍵結型態含量之分布情形，由圖
得知，此二養殖池底泥中所有鍵結型態含量
總和值於每次採樣分析結果皆約略相同，但

含量之變化時可知，此二養殖池底泥中銅之
各鍵結型態含量於第二次 (May-06) (分別
是 99.38 mg/kg、97.41 mg/kg) 至第四次
(Dec-06) 採樣之分析結果 (分別是 110.92
mg/kg、121.61 mg/kg) 與第一次採樣分析結
果 (分別是 186.65 mg/kg、212.13 mg/kg) 相
較之下，明顯比第一次分析結果來得低。鋅

4. 底泥中重金屬之生物有效性及移動性
若將五種結合型態予以分類為生物可
利用相、生物不可利用相亦或化學性之移動
相、穩定相則可更清楚評估重金屬可能造成
之生物毒性。由於弱鍵結相易脫附至水體導
致生物毒性，因此將之歸類為為生物可利用
相 (Van Ryssens et al., 1999)。以重金屬五種
結合型態而言，重金屬在有機物/硫化物結
合態中，大多與硫化物和有機物形成鍵結力
強的金屬硫化物和金屬螯合物，若再扣除上
述之殘存態，可將離子可交換態、碳酸鹽結
合態及鐵錳氧化物結合態認定為生物可利
用相。在本研究中，依重金屬結型態之特性
將重金屬之可交換態、碳酸鹽結合態、鐵錳
氧化物結合態等三個金屬鍵結型態之百分
比加總，定義為生物有效性 (Lu et al.,
2003)。圖 3 為 A 養殖場底泥中重金屬之
生物有效性之情形，從圖得知，二養殖池底
泥 重 金 屬 之 生 物 有 效 性 最 高 為 鉛
(0.21-0.91)，其次是鋅 (0.11-0.63)，而銅
(0.08-0.27)、鎳 (0.04-0.18) 則是最低。而從
時間變化趨勢觀察出，此二養殖池底泥中銅
之生物有效性於第二次 (May-06) (分別是

圖 6. B 養殖場底泥中重金屬之移動性
整體而言，此二養殖場底泥中金屬移動
性以鉛最高，其次為銅、鋅，而鎳則是最低，
因此，由金屬之移動性定義顯示，底泥中金

而從時間變化得知，底泥中銅與鋅之移動性
有減少之現象，但鉛之移動性則是呈現增加

動性，此二養殖場大都是以鉛最高，其次為
銅、鋅，而鎳則是最低，從文獻得知鉛為人
為汙染的可能性較高以外，並會容易隨著環
境的變化而再度移動釋放至水體之中
(Sekhar et al., 2003)，銅、鋅次之，而鎳大部

Abstract
Because of having a high reduction potential, the zero-valent iron (ZVI) is often applied for the 
remediation of wastewater or groundwater with heavy metals.  The purpose of this study was 
aimed to investigate the reaction behavior of heavy metals with ZVI nanoparticles in the 
wastewater. The affecting factors, such as initial pH, dosage of nanoscale ZVI and initial 
concentration of heavy metal, on the removal efficiency of heavy metals by ZVI in the wastewater 
were examined by the batch experiments in this study. It was found that the removal of heavy 
metals was affected by initial pH. The rate and efficiency of metal removal increased with 
decreasing initial pH. Greater than 90% of the heavy metals were removed when the initial pH 
was controlled at 2. In addition, the rate and efficiency of metal removal increased as the dosage 
of nanoscale ZVI increased. The removal efficiency of heavy metal was higher than 80% when 
2.0 g/L of ZVI was added in the wastewater. On the other hand, the slow rate and low efficiency 
of metal removal from the wastewater treated by nanoscale ZVI was found in the wastewater with 
high concentration of heavy metal.
Keywords
Affecting factors; heavy metal; nanoparticles; wastewater; zero-valent iron
INTRODUCTION
Many industries such as electroplating, metal finishing, electronics, leather tanning, etc., frequently 
generate large quantities of wastewater containing heavy metals.  Heavy metals are released or 
transported into the environment either in industrial effluents discharged to surface water or as 
sludge applied to land.  Because heavy metals are generally toxic and non-biodegradable, they can 
be accumulated in living organisms and pose a risk to the ecosystem and human health.  At present 
time, there are many conventional methods for removing heavy metals from industrial wastewater,
including chemical precipitation (Lin et al., 2005), oxidation-reduction (Chang, 2005), ion exchange
(Eom et al., 2005), adsorption (Mohan and Chander, 2006), membrane filtration (Zhong et al., 2007)
and electrolytic methods (Feng et al., 2007).  However, these methods have the limitations of their 
low efficiency, high operational cost and sludge generation (Chen and Wang, 2008).  Thus, it is 
necessary to develop economical and effective technologies for treating the industrial wastewater 
containing heavy metals.
In the course of developing a suitable metal-containing wastewater, zero-valent iron (ZVI) is found 
to be an alternative approach. Because of having a high reduction potential, the ZVI is often 
applied for the remediation of wastewater or groundwater with several kinds of reducible 
contaminants.  Most of research mainly focused on the treatment of organic contaminants, such as 
halogenated hydrocarbons and nitro aromatics, with ZVI in the wastewater or groundwater (Huang 
and Hoffmann, 1998).  Recently, some studies have been performed on the reduction of Cr(VI) and 
nitrate by ZVI (Ponder et al., 2000; Choe et al., 2004).  However, the investigation regarding with 
the reduction of aqueous heavy metals by ZVI is very few (Bartzas et al., 2006).  Although the 
chemical reactions with ZVI proceed quickly, the rate of ZVI with water near neutral pH is 
relatively slow (Weber, 1996).  Generally, ZVI is considered as an excellent metallic material for

RESULTS AND DISCUSSION
Effects of initial pH
As can be seen in Figure 1, with the exception of Cr, the maximum efficiency of metal removal was 
below 30% after 480 min of reaction time when the initial pH of wastewater was controlled at pH 4 
and pH 5.  The rate and efficiency of metal removal were obviously improved in the wastewater 
with initial pH of 3.  The efficiencies of metal removal were 96%, 77%, 19% and 99% for Cu, Zn, 
Pb and Cr, respectively.  However, at initial pH of 2, greater than 90% of the heavy metals (Cu, Zn, 
Pb and Cr) were removed at time of 480 min and especially almost all the Cu and Cr in the 
wastewater were removed with 30 min.  These results showed that the removal of heavy metals was 
affected by initial pH.  The rate and efficiency of metal removal increased with decreasing initial 
pH.  The removal efficiency of heavy metal was in the decreasing order: Cu = Cr > Zn > Pb.  When
the wastewater with high initial pH, precipitates of iron oxyhydroxide are generated, and this causes
the immediate surface passivation of ZVI particles.  The low initial pH results in the corrosion of 
ZVI particles and therefore the metal removal is enhanced (Huang et al., 1998; Chen et al., 2005).

Effects of ZVI dosage
Figure 4 presents the effects of ZVI dosage on the removal of heavy metals by ZVI nanoparticles in 
the wastewater.  The efficiency of metal removal with ZVI dosage of 0.5 g/l was 76%, 14%, 78% 
and 51% at 480 min for Cu, Zn, Pb and Cr, respectively.  By using the ZVI dosage of 2.0 g/l, it 
appears that more than 80% of heavy metals were removed from the wastewater.  Complete 
removal of Cu and Cr can be achieved within 30 min when ZVI dosage was increased up to 2.0 g/l, 
whereas Zn and Pb was completely removed from wastewater within 240 min as the ZVI dosage 
higher than 3.5 g/l.

Effects of initial metal concentration
Figure 7 illustrates the metal removal depend on the initial metal concentration.  Cu was nearly 
completely removed from wastewater within 240 min for all initial concentration of Cu.  When the 
initial Cr concentrations were less than 100 mg/l, all Cr were removed within 120 min.  The 
removal efficiency of Cr was only 86% at the initial concentration of 100 mg/l of Cr.  However, the 
removal rate and efficiency of Zn and Pb were apparently affected by the initial metal concentration.  
The complete removal of Zn and Pb was only achieved as the initial metal concentration was less 
than 34 mg/l.  Generally, the slow rate and low efficiency of metal removal from the wastewater 
were found in the wastewater with high concentration of heavy metal, especially for Zn and Pb.  
This is because the metal removal by ZVI is a surface-mediated process (Weber, 1996) and the 
reaction active sites are limited by adding the constant ZVI dosage in the wastewater.

As previously discussed the pH and dissolved iron increased during the removal of heavy metal by 
ZVI nanoparticles.  For example, the pH was found to increase from 2.0 to about 4.1-4.5 after 480 
min of reaction regardless of initial Cu concentration (Figure 8).  In addition, the dissolved iron 
increased up to about 330-370 mg/l and was not affected by initial Cu concentration.  The ferrous

Cu were observed for Zn, Pb and Cr.
