Self-Organizing Map neural network (SOM) is one of the most typical unsupervised neural 
network paradigms. Nevertheless, some limitations still exist in the conventional SOM such as the way 
of selecting the winning neuron and the predetermination of network structure for the topological layer. 
Therefore, this year‚Äôs research project extends the previous year‚Äôs research project (NSC 94-2213-E211-
009): ‚ÄúAn Ant-Based Self-Organizing feature Map neural network (ABSOM) for Clustering Analysis‚Äù. 
The proposed research methodology integrates the Ant Colony System (ACS) algorithm, which 
possesses the merit of jumping off local minima during the optimization searching process, with the 
Self-Organizing Map neural network (SOM), which enables to map data into a low-dimensional format 
to facilitate data visualization. Thereafter, this methodology improves the clustering performance of the 
conventional Kohonen‚Äôs SOM. Furthermore, an Ant-based Growing SOM neural network (AGSOM) 
was realized. From the clustering results to three databases, the AGSOM performs better than other two 
SOM-related algorithms. 
 
Keywords: Self-Organizing Map, Ant Colony Optimization, Clustering Analysis, Variant SOM, K-
means
