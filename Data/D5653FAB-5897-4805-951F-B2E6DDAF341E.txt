一、中文摘要
網路商店雖將在未來經濟發展中，扮演重要角色
與指標，並獲不少學者，從商店本質、網路特性與價
值等不同角度，提供各種促進網路商店成功發展之意
見與作法。卻仍無研究系統化鏈結網路商店中，發展
策略、商品價值及營運績效等三項重要關鍵課題。
為此，本計劃將完成與底下三主題有關之論文：
網路商店「營運績效」、「營運績效評估模式」與「經
營策略與發展地圖」。相信，計劃主持人本年度的研
究成果，將不僅協助國內網路商店企業的發展，同時，
也能助其在無國界網路商店中，建立另一項台灣奇
蹟。而此同時，參與本計劃的研究同仁與學生，也能
因此打開另一通往國際競爭的機會及能力。
關鍵詞：網路商店、營運策略、營運績效、商品價值
Abstract
There are a lot of economic scholars and
enterprise’s leaders have already predicted that the 
e-shops will play an important role and indicator in
economic development in the future. But there is not a
systematic research for chaining the development
strategy, products value, operation performance.
For this reason, this plan will finish three articles of
the follow related topics: The comparisons of the
operation performance indices for e-shop,” “The
evaluation model of operation performance for e-shop,”
and “The Success Strategy and Road-map for E-store.”
We believe that this host's efforts and the results of the
research of the past two years not only help the
development of enterprises of domestic e-shop but also
set up another Taiwan miracle in the borderless e-shop in
the same time. Meanwhile, the research colleagues and
students who have participated in this plan also can open
another chance and ability of leading to international
competition.
Keywords: e-shop, operation strategy, operations
performances, products value

Zhao et al. (2008)以四階段探討電子商務價值，在資訊階段：提供高品質訊息給潛在顧客，促進買賣達成；
談判階段：提供方交易管理/服務項目，協助顧客決定購買；付款階段：風險管理控制及彈性付款方式；傳遞階

成本(阿里巴巴網路公司，2007)。另外，Hsiao (2009)從貨幣價值觀點，觀察線上購物發現，節省交貨時間，使
供應商能提供相對低價產品，是網路商店吸引顧客主因。而網路商店投資動態分配，可監控需求和庫存，以降
低系統成本，提升規模效益及銷售額(Mahar et al., 2009)。
2.2 滿意度觀點
Kolter (1991)認為滿意的顧客通常會再度光臨，愉悅地談論產品，忽略競爭品牌的廣告，或購買公司其他的
產品。Szymznski and Hise (2000)以線上購物經驗的滿意程度(Satisfaction)和愉悅程度(Pleasure)來衡量整體顧客滿
意度。Janda et al. (2002)以網站功能、滿足性、資訊內容等構面，討論網路商店服務品質對消費者行為意圖之影
響。Mckinney et al. (2002)利用 Spreng and Olshavsky (1993)的認知性和情感性要素發展滿意度衡量項目，作為資
訊品質、系統品質及整體滿意度衡量標準。另外，Yang et al. (2004) 則以網路券商為對象，並以回應性、服務
可靠性、勝任性、安全性、時效性、存取能力、易用性、系統可信賴等準則，來衡量網路服務品質與顧客滿意
度關聯。Shih (2004)認為影響顧客接受網路購物之顧客滿意度可依資訊、系統及服務的表現等三方面來看，並
可依此預測消費願意與網路購物行為。Chan (2007)認為，網路商店使用自動協議智慧代理人之工具，可提高顧
客滿意度及忠誠度。Huang and Lin (2007)亦表示在網路商店嵌入銷售代理人可增加賣家的盈餘，且增加買家的
產品價值、願意支付及提升對網路商店之滿意度。Souitaris and Balabanis (2007)以網路商店之策略及行銷區隔的
角度來看，產品組合、網站設計、方便性及差異化顧客服務是讓顧客滿意的主因。Aboolian et al. (2009)認為網
路服務供應商之設施地點、顧客分佈及伺服器數量，會明顯影響其績效及滿意度。
2.3 Kano二維品質模式
Kano et al. (1984)引用 Herzberg et al. (1959)的工作動機「M-H理論(motivator-hygiene theory)」，來說明商品
品質的「M-H性」，並建立具有「充足度」與「滿意度」之二維 Kano model。經 Schvaneveldt et al. (1991)、Matzler
and Hinterhuber (1998)、Chien and Chen (2008)等多位學者的引用後，證明此模式在掌握商品與服務的品質特性
時，能發揮有效且明確的策略性作用。又因 Kano model具理論特性且能滿足實際做法，同時能掌握顧客預期心
態及滿意因素，故常被學術界與實務界所使用(Jane and Dominguez, 2003)。此模式以「充足度」正、反兩向(充
足、不充足)來設計問卷，並收集被訪者對該狀況的「滿足度」差異，以判定問卷中各需求項目之品質特性。其
中，(1)魅力品質要素(attractive quality element)具有大幅提升滿意度的作用；(2)一元品質要素(one-dimensional
quality element)顯示使用者滿意度與此要素之充足度高低成正相關；(3)當然品質要素(must-be element)展現此要
7的「價值」感。故本研究將量測個人使用者的「滿意」觀點，轉換為量測企業法人業務的「價值」觀點，而提
出價值觀點下之 Kano修正模式(圖 1)。藉此，企業可精確掌握網路商店成功經營關鍵因素之價值特性、重要度
及改善方向，以降低導入過程中毫無脈絡之窘境。

響衡量網路購物因素應作全面性的考量，才能獲得廣面的網路購物成功模式(Verma et al., 2004)。Juang (2005)認

綜合上述學者之研究成果，本研究共獲得 73項成功經營關鍵因素。而經 Focus group interview之因素簡化
及其歸類過程，專家認為：(1)基礎架構、主要應用、支援功能、企業組織等 4大構面，可合理解釋網路商店導
入成功與否；(2)而此 4大構面，則可以 19類別予以呈現。
至於，各構面下之類別，則可分述如下：(1)「系統架構」有系統品質、資訊品質、系統客製化、系統執行、
系統穩定性等系統環境條件相關之 5個類別(Janda et al.,2002; Udo and Marquis, 2002; DeLone and McLean, 2003;
Bharati and Chaudhury, 2004; Huang and Lin, 2007; Jaisingh et al., 2008)；(2)「主要功能」則有行銷廣告、網站設
計、產品特性、行銷組合、顧客服務等 5個主要功能條件類別(Kauffman and Walden, 2001; Ngai and Wat, 2002;
Souitaris and Balabanis, 2007; Lian and Lin, 2008; Faria et al., 2009; Qiu and Malthouse, 2009)；(3)「企業組織」有企
業策略、組織運作、網站技術、相關資源協助等 4個企業組織條件類別((Kauffman and Walden, 2001; Ngai and Wat,
2002; Zhao et al., 2008)；(4)「決策功能」則有顧客心理、顧客滿意、產業經濟、公共政策、市場研究等 5個經
營環境類別(Kauffman and Walden, 2001; Ngai and Wat, 2002)。
4.1.2 成功經營關鍵構面、類別與因素之過濾
為過濾簡化 73項成功經營關鍵因素，並使其名稱具一致性，本研究運用 Delphi method，並邀請前 4家提
供網路商店平台業者及其 8家合作企業之主管等 12位專家，進行第一階段之問卷調查。在此過程中，專家對問
卷中衡量語意不清、無法判別等處進行修改，以利閱讀及避免引導性問題，且歸納出 4構面與 12類別。同時，
專家也將原先 73項因素過濾/篩選/修改/合併成 41項，並將各項因素歸納到適當的構面與類別上。
其中，「系統架構」共有系統運作、系統功能、資訊品質等 3類別，且分別有 3、5、3等共 11個因素；「主
要功能」則有行銷廣告、產業特性、顧客服務等 3類別，且分別 3、3、4等共 10個因素；「企業組織」有企業
發展、服務機制、技術能力與企業策略等 4類別，且分別有 4、3、3、3等共 13個因素；「決策功能」則有顧
客需求、產業經濟等 2類別，且分別有 4、3等共 7個因素(請參考附件表一)。
4.2 成功經營關鍵因素之確認
為確認此 41項因素之可靠性與正確性，本研究以 Likert五點尺度來設計問卷，並進行第二階段問卷調查，
以瞭解其信度(reliability)與效度(validity)。在 4家網路商店平台業者及 8家合作企業之協助下，本研究共對 330
位有網路購買行為之使用者發出問卷，並回收 308份，且有 284份(約 92.2%)的有效問卷。
在衡量資料穩定性與一致性之過程中，本研究採用 Cronbach's α係數，來檢定問卷之信度，並得知其整體
信度之Cronbach's α係數值為 0.868 (0.70＜Cronbach's α≦0.90：很可信)。這顯示本研究所選定之 41項成功發展
關鍵因素，具有高度可信任度。另外，本研究採用 Kaiser (1958)所提出之MSA(Measure of Sampling Adequacy)
指標，來進行效度檢定，以判斷是否適合進行因素分析。另外，為使判斷更具公信力，本研究也採用 Bartlett
檢定法，來進行佐證。結果顯示，在效度檢定中，MSA＝0.761＞0.5；且在 Bartlett檢定法中，其顯著水準小於
0.05。故本研究各項因素滿足穩定性與一致性之要求。

圖 2. 網路商店成功經營策略圖
若進一步以第 12項「促銷活動與降低價格」之個別因素來說，此因素因具「魅力」之高 Kano價值特性重

重要度值。這樣的結果顯示，第 12因素的處理，雖僅需短期資源，卻對企業價值具有高度影響能力。故第二區
域之因素，具「補強經營」之特性。

間的因果關係；(2)檢視企業目前作為的合理性；(3)了解經營過程中應有能力與資源；(4)明確各階段改善方向與

5. 研究結論
儘管，過去學者提出許多關於網路商店經營與導入之相關論文，但單純強調成功關鍵因素、經營程序或注
意事項等概念性訊息，並無法有效協助企業在經營過程中順利成功。為此，本研究建立網路商店成功經營策略
圖與地圖，以協助企業擺脫過去毫無經營脈絡之困擾，進而具有省視自身能力與資源、建立階段策略目標與改
善方向之能力，以掌握成功經營網路商店之最佳解決方案。
5.1 研究成果
為順利建立網路商店成功經營策略圖與地圖，本研究從價值觀點中之價值與效益兩角度，蒐集過去學者對
網路商店經營價值與關鍵成功因素，作為網路商店經營策略之決策項目基礎。另外，本研究也從滿意度觀點，
掌握學者研究網路購買行為之使用者之滿意相關項目，以呈現系統使用者之期待與感受。
而在 Focus group interview下，本研究將所蒐集到的 73項成功經營關鍵因素予以簡化成 41項，並在修正
Kano 二維品質模式中之「滿意度」座標，而以「價值」座標取代後，建立價值觀點下之 Kano 修正模式(Kano
價值模式)。透過此模式之問卷調查，本研究可對各項成功經營關鍵因素，進行分類及賦予「Kano 價值特性及
其重要度」。同時，本研究也透過 Delphi method將 41項因素歸類成 12類與 4大構面；並在 FAHP技術下，獲
得各構面與類別之「FAHP重要度值」。

How Can We Successfully Implement The ERP Activity?
Chien, Te-King *
Department of Information management, National
Formosa University
64, Wun-hua Rd., Huwei, Yunlin, Taiwan, R.O.C.
tkchien@nfu.edu.tw
Tseng, Huei-Shan
Department of Information management, National

Abstract---Verified by many evidences, an unsystematic
guidance process may cause an improper sequence not
followed by an enterprise finally having failures
confusedly despite of practitioners’recognition and
scholars’research achievements for the issue to
successfully implement and develop Enterprise Resource
Planning (ERP). Focus on the execution construction
categories and the items, ownerships and the causal
relation, this research, based on PDCA processes,
provides the implementation map of ERP successfully
through the quantitative analysis and qualitative analysis
technology. For the sake of ensuring this implementation
map’s value and feasibility, we invite four enterprises to
join our study for further observation. The result shows
the corporation followed the rule of implementation map
has the optimal performance. Therefore, the
implementation map of ERP, not only be able to minimize
the developing time and failure possibility but also
contribute to enterprises to implement activities and
resource allocation in precision to achieve the purpose of
implementing ERP.
Keywords-ERP activity; Implementation Map; PDCA.
I. INTRODUCTION
As an important issue for one enterprise’s
management, how to successfully implement
Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) to accurately plan
and manipulate its resources have been found in
diversified research achievements expressed with
different forms such as opinion, methodology, and
model offered by scholars in many countries. Still, one
business operator’s pressures caused by high failure
rates of managing an enterprise do not descend
significantly [10, 13]. Investigating root causes, we
deem the reason is an enterprise failing to control
systematic steps (implementation map) in one activity.
To supply a measure solving this challenge,
we have the following rational strategy: (1)
Key factors and the process for successful
implementation of an ERP activity induced
from literature reviews; (2) Process, dimensions, and
factors for implementation of an ERP activity
summarized from scholars’research; (3) Setup of a
structure for successful implementation of an ERP
activity according to the PDCA management cycle; (4)
Manipulation of an expert interview technique like the
Delphi method to verify necessity, casual relationships
and definite authority and duty for dimensions,
categories, and factors; (5) All items’importance
verified with descriptive statistics; (6) Stability for the
implementation structure and casual relationships
verified with the Focus Group Interviews and the
Fuzzy Delphi Method; (7) Setup of an implementation
map for a successful ERP activity based on above
achievements; (8) Verification of this map’s rationality
and values via cooperation with four enterprises.
II. LITERATURE Reviews
Literature reviews in this section to summarize
key factors and the necessary process for successful
implementation of ERP are able to interpret the
rationality constructed by the map for successful
implementation of ERP and functions and applications
about the Delphi method and the Fuzzy Delphi method
as a basis of the methodology in this study.
A. Key factors for successful implementation of ERP
Based on Oracle’s [11] argumentations, key factors
for successful implementation of ERP are composed of
high-level executives’ supports and promises,
educational training joined by relevant staffs,
conditions for software and hardware, a task force’s
knowledge for one system, task force members’
participation, acceptance of reform, test program, setup
of solutions, verification of a final strategy, effective
training for users, correct test operations, coordination
for the release process, and a system company’s
supports for a released process. Finney and Corbett [6]
found (1) Factors in a tactic: high-level executive’
support and promise, prospect and planning, budget,
relevant tactic, project management, member in one
project, reform of management cultures, system’s
function, and management for reform; (2) Factors in a
strategy: outstanding staff, communications program,
authorized policy maker, group morale and
in this tentative program. In this stage, the original
dimensions and categories in this study were simplified
to the second program with 4 dimensions and 25
categories for PDCA. In addition, the authority and
duty assigned to eight objects such as high-level
executive, sector executive, project manager, education
specialist, mechanism specialist, advisor, system
vendor, and supplier for different items were acquired
during investigations. In this regard, the authority and
duty integrated with the concept of the PDCA
management cycle became the basis of the
implementation map for successful ERP.
With the technique of focus group interviews,
experts’or executives’ recommendations were to
modify items with implication unclear or determination
unavailable and mergence of highly-relevant items for
friendly reading and exclusion of leading questions.
Finally, under the technique of the Delphi method,
experts or executives strongly agreed the concept of
PDCA management cycle and the structure for
implementation adopted in this study.
Matching the major structure of the PDCA
management cycle, the structure for successful
implementation of ERP argued in this study covers 25
categories shown as follows: (1) Initiation of a project;
(2) Selection of an advisor; (3) Control of current status;
(4) Verification of targets; (5) Establishment of a group;
(6) Explicit authority and duty; (7) Selection of a
vendor; (8) Evaluation of one project; (9) Setup of
indicators; (10) Discovery of differences; (11) Rule for
an activity; (12) Assignment of modules; (13) Planning
for training; (14) Educational training; (15) Data
screening; (16) Verification of a paradigm; (17) Data
transfer; (18) Verification of a system; (19) Declaration
for release; (20) Supervision of achievements; (21)
Verification of performance; (22) Praise for
achievements; (23) Discovery of root causes; (24)
Amendment of targets; (25) Mechanism for
amendment.
Compared with opinions offered by the
above-mentioned scholars, these results derived from
the structure for successful implementation of ERP in
this study create not only advantages guiding an
enterprise’s development and pointing out critical
points for business in different stages but also a
constant-improvable and stage-planning blueprint for
one enterprise.
C. Items in the structure for implementation of ERP
With the Delphi method, 132 items to be executed
are categorized into different phases of P, D, C, or A
according to their different features. In this regard, 63
items planned and deliberated prior to setup and
implementation of ERP belong to PLAN, e.g.,
establishment of an ERP planning committee,
high-level executives’explicit promise, verification for
conditions of selecting an advisor, analyses for
advantages/disadvantages of current status, etc.; 38
items categorized to the DO dimension and
indispensable for successful implementation of ERP
are interpretation for a learning organization and
structure, implementation of training in a specific
sector, etc.
Verified and assessed after the DO phase and
analyzed for differences between a plan and
achievements to find problems as well as the optimal
paradigm, these 16 items categorized to the CHECK
dimension are summary of abnormalities from different
sectors, declaration of solutions for abnormality,
summary of the current progress made by each sector,
etc. 15 items classified to the ACTION dimension are
summary of factors causing different performance,
analyses of root causes for different performance,
forecast of follow-up effects from root causes, etc.,
which should be reviewed item by item for discovery
of solutions correcting deviations and the overall
performance, enhancing experience at implementation,
and fulfilling decided targets.
D. Verification of items for implementation
To further enhance and perceive reliability and
validity of these 132 items having verified by experts
and executives, a questionnaire based on the 5-point
Likert scale was designed for the second-stage
investigation. With assistance of 4 vendors for ERP
systems and 8 cooperative enterprises, 334
questionnaires of 450 issued to ERP system users were
responded, and 316 in 334 (94.6%) were effective.
To begin with, the Descriptive Statistics adopted
in this study to evaluate and estimate an individual
item’s importance as well as its average was used for
simplification of items. Owing to results indicating
points for 23 items less than the average (=3.6), 109
items were preserved for analyses of reliability and
validity in this study with“expectation and requirement
for one system”, “response time and speed of one
system”, “supply of relevant knowledge to a supplier”,
etc. deleted from the questionnaire.
Next, to evaluate an item’s stability and 
consistence, the Cronbach’s α coefficient was adopted
to assess reliability of the questionnaire and the value
of 0.871 (0.70＜Cronbach’s α≦0.90: Very reliable)
from results indicated high reliability of 109 items
chosen for the structure of implementing ERP in this
study. In addition, to decide the feasibility of
conducting a factor analysis, the MSA (Measure of
Sampling Adequacy) indicator argued by Kaiser [8]
was employed for validity tests. Furthermore, as one
option to prove our determinations’ public credibility, 
“Definite authority and duty and implementation”and
“Explicit direction for improvement”and improvement
of one enterprise’s overall efficiency and users’
ascending satisfaction degree for ERP; decided in a
company meeting, an item to be improved makes ERP
users express least satisfaction. By all accounts, it can
be rationally induced that the“implementation map for
successful ERP”has functions of not only explicitly
designating definite authority and duty for one
enterprise and verifying this enterprise’s current
capacity but also clearly defining a direction and
targets for improvement in different stages.
VI. CONCLUSIONS
A. Research achievements
The key factors and the process for successful
implementation of ERP are induced with literature
reviews, and the structure for implementation is built
with the concept of the PDCA management cycle. On
the other hand, 4 dimensions, 25 categories, 109 items
and definite authority and duty are acquired with
analysis techniques such as the Delphi method,
interviews with experts, and the descriptive statistics.
Next, the causal relationships among dimensions,
categories, and items and the process’s stability are
ensured with the Focus Group Interviews and the
Fuzzy Delphi Method. Finally, with three important
information such as “dimensions, categories, and items
for implementation”,“definite authority and duty”, and
“causal relationships”, the “implementation map for
successful ERP”is constructed to assist an enterprise
reviewing the rationality of the current process used for
implementation and is taken as a referral for targets to
be implemented in different stages.
Implication in management
First of all, with cooperation of four enterprises in
the long run, the achievements shown in the
“management application dimension”have functions in
guidance, test, and approval and are critical referrals
for verification, evaluation, and improvement.
Secondly, the achievements acquired in the
“management activity dimension”can be taken as one
criterion to examine the performance or defects of an
enterprise’ERP activity for any measure drifted to
adjust targets and reduce possibilities causing failures
of one activity. Thirdly, the achievements acquired in
the “management mechanism dimension”can assist an
enterprise’ executives to manage relevant staffs’
authority and duty or a mechanism available to an
activity for enhancement of an ERP project and
effective manipulation of resources.
