摘要 
 本文提出一具鎖相迴路 (PLL)控制之
CLL諧振式直流電源轉換器。藉由偵測輸入
電壓與變壓器二次側輸出電壓的相位訊
號，鎖相控制可使電路之操作頻率連續地追
隨CLL諧振槽之諧振頻率。並以此諧振頻率
為基準，進而調整操作頻率以達到所需的電
壓增益。然而，因為操作頻率不會低於CLL
諧振槽之諧振頻率，因此開關皆是操作在零
電壓切換(ZVS)，可降低開關的切換損。本
文提出一200W之具鎖相迴路控制的CLL諧
振式直流電源轉換器 ，經由設計與實做來
驗證本文所提之電路的性能與可行性。 
關鍵字：CLL，鎖相迴路，諧振槽，直流電
源轉換器。 
 
Abstract 
This paper proposes a capacitively 
loaded loop (CLL) DC/DC resonant converter 
with phase-locked loop (PLL) control scheme. 
The PLL control scheme enables the switching 
frequency of the converter to continuously track the 
resonant frequency of the CLL resonant tank by 
detecting the phase signals of the input and 
transformer secondary output voltages of the 
CLL resonant tank. Based on this resonant 
frequency, the switching frequency is adjusted 
to obtain the required voltage gain. However, 
because the operating frequency will not be 
lower than the resonant frequency of the CLL 
resonant tank, the switches of the inverter are 
both operated in a zero-voltage-switching 
(ZVS) condition so that the turn-on switching 
losses are reduced. The proposed 200W CLL 
DC/DC resonant converter with PLL control 
scheme is then designed and implemented to 
validate and demonstrate the performance and 
feasibility of the proposed DC/DC converter. 
Keywords: capacitively loaded loop (CLL), 
phase-locked loop (PLL), resonant tank, 
DC/DC converter.

技術特點 
1. 對於CLL諧振式直流電源轉換器輸入電壓與輸出阻抗之變動
量有較高的容忍度。 
2. 由於鎖相控制使得電路開關之操作頻率高於諧振頻率，所以開
關皆可操作在零電壓切換以降低開關之切換損失，進而達到高
效率。 
3. 降壓變壓器具有一輔助繞組，可用以偵測變壓器漏感上的電壓
相位訊號

Auxiliary Power Supply for the Control IC of a Buck Converter 
 
Ray-Lee Lin and Hung-Ming Chen 
Department of Electrical Engineering 
National Cheng Kung University 
Tainan City, Taiwan 
rayleelin@ee.ncku.edu.tw 
 
ABSTRACT – This paper presents a charge-pump auxiliary 
power supply for sourcing the control IC of a buck converter 
without any additional magnetic device. The prototype of a 
48V buck converter with this charge-pump auxiliary power 
supply is implemented to validate the performance of the 
proposed circuit. The application of the proposed charge-pump 
auxiliary power supply can be extended to other converters, 
such as the buck-boost and the Zeta. 
 
 
I. INTRODUCTION 
 
In order to supply the required DC voltage for the control 
IC in a buck converter, two conventional schemes have been 
developed: The independent DC voltage source and the 
winding-type auxiliary power supply are briefly introduced 
as follows. 
Fig. 1 shows an independent DC voltage source that 
supplies the required DC voltage Vcc for the pulse-width 
modulation (PWM) IC in a typical buck converter. This 
independent DC voltage source is unpopular with the 
industry because of its high cost.

V. CONCLUSIONS 
 
This paper has proposed a new auxiliary power supply 
circuit for the control IC of converters.  The proposed 
auxiliary power supply for the control IC of a buck 
converter gains a stable operational voltage by utilizing the 
new circuit. Based on the application to a buck converter, 
buck-boost and Zeta converters are also shown to benefit 
from this circuit. 
In addition, experimental results show that the proposed 
circuit achieves a lower level of turn-off switching loss.  
The output voltage of the buck converter is not changed by 
different load sizes or input voltages. The input power of the 
buck converter using the proposed auxiliary power circuit is 
similar to that of the buck converter with a DC-source 
auxiliary power supply. The efficiency of a buck converter 
with the proposed auxiliary power circuit was found to be 
93.4% at the heavy-load condition.  Thus, the new 
auxiliary power supply is shown to be advantageous for use 
in the converter’s control IC.

. CONCLUSIONS 
 
This paper has proposed the three-terminal charge-pump 
cell to provide auxiliary power to the control IC of a boost 
converter.  By using the proposed three-terminal 
charge-pump cell, the auxiliary power supply for the control 
IC of converters provides a stable DC voltage. Also, with 
the three-terminal charge-pump cell, the application to the 
boost converter can be extended to other converters, 
including Cuk, Sepic, forward, flyback, half-bridge and 
full-bridge converters. 
Additionally, experimental results illustrate that the turn-off 
switching loss is reduced and that the input power of the 
boost converter with the proposed auxiliary power circuit is 
similar to that of the boost converter that uses a DC-source 
auxiliary power supply. During the operation of the boost 
converter with the three-terminal charge-pump cell, the 
efficiency was found to be 94.8% even at the heavy-load 
condition.

ABSTRACT – This paper presents a charge-pump auxiliary 
power supply for sourcing the control IC of a buck converter 
without any additional magnetic device. The prototype of a 
48V buck converter with this charge-pump auxiliary power 
supply is implemented to validate the performance of the 
proposed circuit. The application of the proposed charge-pump 
auxiliary power supply can be extended to other converters, 
such as the buck-boost and the Zeta.

V. CONCLUSIONS 
 
This paper has proposed a new auxiliary power supply 
circuit for the control IC of converters.  The proposed 
auxiliary power supply for the control IC of a buck 
converter gains a stable operational voltage by utilizing the 
new circuit. Based on the application to a buck converter, 
buck-boost and Zeta converters are also shown to benefit 
from this circuit. 
In addition, experimental results show that the proposed 
circuit achieves a lower level of turn-off switching loss.  
The output voltage of the buck converter is not changed by 
different load sizes or input voltages. The input power of the 
buck converter using the proposed auxiliary power circuit is 
similar to that of the buck converter with a DC-source 
auxiliary power supply. The efficiency of a buck converter 
with the proposed auxiliary power circuit was found to be 
93.4% at the heavy-load condition.  Thus, the new 
auxiliary power supply is shown to be advantageous for use 
in the converter’s control IC.
