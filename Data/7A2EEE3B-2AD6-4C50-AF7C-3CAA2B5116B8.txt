ABSTRACT 
The data-driven automatic speech recognition (ASR) system is mainly based on the hidden 
Markov model (HMM) technique. It can be considered as a knowledge-ignorant modeling 
method. For a language, its speech recognition system can be developed using the large text 
corpus and speech corpus, even without knowing the language. This approach can not attack 
the real problems behind the system and the improvement is limited. Recently, many 
researchers started to think about the integration of phonetic and linguistic knowledge into the 
data-driven system, so that the performance of ASR can be further improved. This project 
attempts to develop a knowledge-based data-driven ASR system by the cooperation of 
researchers from universities and research institutes in Taiwan. A resource sharing platform 
for system design and assessment is constructed. It allows researchers to use databases and 
programs developed by this project. The major research topics are (1) the detection of speech 
attributes and events, (2) the merge of speech events and relevant knowledge, (3) the 
verification of events and evidence, (4) the design of knowledge-bases, speech corpora, 
speech and language models, and software tools, and (5) the resource sharing platform for 
system design and assessment.  
Keywords: automatic speech recognition (ASR), speech attributes, speech event detection, evidence 
verification, resource sharing platform
