Abstract
P type SiGe thin films were prepared by means of RF magnetron sputtering in which a Si target
attached with Ge pieces was used. The area proportion of Ge pieces was adjusted to produce thin
films with Si80Ge20 composition. The substrate temperature and annealing temperature were
changed in order to prepare a crystalline SiGe thin film. Thin film properties were characterized
by means of FE-SEM, ESCA, XRD, Hall measurement and an in-house measurement system for
thermoelectric power. Experimental results show that the thin film is amorphous as substrate
temperature goes up to 500℃. The thin film possesses a low carrier concentration and low
electrical conductivity. The largest Seebeck coefficient 2.88 V/K occurs at a substrate temperature
of 300℃, while the power factor is 3.68×10-4 W/K2·m. The thin film remains non-crystalline as
annealed at 600-800℃. It becomes crystalline after annealed at 900 ℃. The carrier mobility
increases and electrical resistivity decreases when the crystallinity appears. The thin film
annealed at 600 ℃ has the largest Seebeck coefficient 0.79 V/K and the highest power factor
3.17×10-5 W/K2·m.

4. 結論
隨著基板溫度增加，由於原子移動速率增加以及電漿密度上升，薄膜厚度與沉積速率
是隨之增加的。但由於基板溫度仍不足，原子無法移動至較佳晶格點位置，因此由 XRD 分
析可知沉積之 SiGe 薄膜仍未明顯結晶。在電性方面，由於溫度不足使得摻質未活化，造成
薄膜之載子濃度很低。相對地，載子移動時彼此干擾的機率降低，故載子遷移率相當高。

Introduction
SiGe alloys are substitutional solid solutions and covalent semiconductors. SiGe
has some features like high carrier mobility and adjustable band gap. SiGe has been
considered as the modern band gap engineering material. Because SiGe thin films
possess lower crystallization temperatures than that of silicon and SiGe processes are
compatible with those of silicon, the trend that the poly-Si thin film is replaced by
poly-SiGe thin film is on-going. The applications of SiGe thin films include
high-speed devices, optoelectronic materials, thin film transistors, thermal radiation
detectors, thermoelectric materials and solar cells, etc. The development in techniques
of depositing poly-SiGe thin films has drawn many attentions recently for reducing
cost and widening applicability.
Usually, the silicon wafer with an oxide passivation layer is used as the substrate
for depositing SiGe thin films. However, the high cost of silicon wafer and the step of
passivation raise the overall manufacture cost. Industrially, cheap substrates like glass
or polymer used as the substrate for depositing SiGe thin films have the economical
benefit on production. Traditionally, poly-SiGe thin films are prepared by means of
CVD including LPCVD and PECVD [1,2,~3,4,5]. The SiGe thin films prepared by CVD are
normally amorphous and therefore further heat treatment for recrystallization is
in a SiGe thin film strongly influences the crystallinity of the film. Higher Ge content
means lower recrystallization temperature. However, no study has discussed the
relationship between the film composition and proportion of Ge on a Ge-attached Si
target. In this study, glass substrates were used for depositing SiGe thin films by
means of a RF magnetron sputtering system. The cross-section, film composition,
crystallinity, optical property of these thin films was investigated by means of Field
Emission SEM, X-ray Photoelectron Spectroscopy, Raman Spectrometry and UV-Vis
Adsorption Spectrometry, respectively.
Experimental
In this study, SiGe thin films were deposited on glass substrates by means of a
RF magnetron sputtering system with Ge-attached Si targets. The type of glass
substrate was Coring 1737 with a size of 15×15 mm2 and a thickness of 0.7 mm. The
in-house RF magnetron sputtering system was constructed with a ULVAC DP vacuum
system and a Dressler Cesar 136 RF generator. The optimal and the working pressure
was 2.6×10−5 and 2.6×10−1 Pa, respectively. The RF power density was 4.93 W/cm2.
The deposition time was 30 min and the target-to-substrate distance was 7 cm. The
area proportions of Ge on Si targets were varied as 0, 1/8, 1/4 and 1/2, while the
thin films deposited at various proportions (AGe) of Ge on Si targets are plotted in Fig.
1. It can be seen that the intensity of the Si2p peak decreases, while those of Ge3d,
O1s and C1s peaks increase with increasing Ge proportion. The O1s and C1s peaks
found in the film from a pure Si target (AGe = 0) may result from the surface
adsorption layer or the residual gas trapped in the chamber. The positions of these
peaks also change with increasing Ge proportion. The splitting spectra of the Si2p,
Ge3d, O1s and C1s peaks are shown in Fig. 2. The Si2p peak (Fig. 2a) can be split
into two peaks corresponding to the Si−Si bond (Eb = 98.8 eV) and Si−O bond (Eb =
101.8 eV) [18]. The Ge3d peak (Fig. 2b) purely comes from Ge elemental bonds. The
spectrum in Fig. 2c can be split into two peaks. The peak at 530.4 eV results from O’
containing many possible bonds and most probably the bonds around single O atoms,
as proposed by Hijikata et al. [19]. Another peak at 532.4 eV may result from the
O2−Si bond (Eb = 533.0 eV) or Ge L3M23M23 (Eb = 534.0 eV) [20]. These two binding
energies are too close to distinguish. However, the peak at 530.4 eV should mainly be
contributed from Ge L3M23M23.In Fig. 1c, one can see that only the O’ peak shows in 
pure Si film, while the peak at 532.4 eV appears in the SiGe film and arises with
increasing Ge content. The inference that the peak at 530.4 eV should mainly be
contributed from Ge L3M23M23 is quite reasonable. The C1s peak (Fig. 2d) is

Si−Si and Si−Ge bonds become unidentifiable. However, the peak at 290 cm−1 arises

found that the peak for the Si-Si bond moves from 495 cm−1 for AGe = 0 to 454 cm−1
for AGe = 1/4.
3. Band Gap
The optical transmittance of the SiGe films deposited at various proportion of Ge
on Si targets is plotted against the wave number in Fig. 5. Since the band gap of Si
(1.1 eV) is wider than that of Ge (0.67 eV), the optical absorption edge of a Si thin
film slants toward the short wavelength region in comparison with that of a SiGe thin
film. Therefore, increasing AGe, increasing Ge content in the thin film causes the
absorption edge of a SiGe thin film move toward long wavelength and the
transmittance in the visible region descends.
