In this study, the surface condition of Pt50Au50 alloy NP has been manipulated by CeO2 addition and N2 
heat treatment synergistically. CeO2, which has been frequently applied as an oxygen-containing species, 
texture promoter, and surface modifier,15-19 can be used as the surface condition manipulator and the activity 
promoter of ORR for the PtAu alloy NPs.20 Yu et al. have reported that the CeO2 has kinetic (Ce3+-Ce4+) 
and textural properties for the cathode catalysts.15 Wei et al. have found that for the Pd-Co catalysts, the 
addition of CeO2 acting as a textural promoter and surface modifier effectively enhances their catalytic 
activity due to the promotion of the Pd-Co NPs dispersion, enhancement of Pd surface enrichment, and 
formation of a Pd shell/alloy core structure.19 Similarly, in the Pt-Au system, the addition of CeO2 decreases 
the particle sizes, and enhances the surface segregation of Pt, which result in an alloy surface with a 
moderate oxophilicity on catalysts.20 In addition, heat treatments in inert atmospheres such as N2 or Ar have 
also been extensively utilized to tune the surface composition, promote the activity, improve the degree of 
alloying, and remove the residual surfactants of the catalysts and alloy NPs.18,21,22 For the PtRu/C anode 
catalysts with post-heat treatment in Ar, it is demonstrated that the catalytic activity of the as-synthesized 
PtRu colloidal catalyst can be enhanced due to the improved PtRu alloying level and the removal of the 
surfactant from the catalysts.21 Besides, for the PtRu/C, N2 treatment may serve as an adjustment process for 
surface composition and structure of the alloy catalysts. After heating in N2 at 570 K, the surface Pt 
composition and surface species is optimized to display the best catalytic activity toward methanol 
decomposition among all heat-treated samples.22 When the N2 treatment is applied in the CeO2-modified 
PtRu/C, the synergistic effect of CeO2 modification and N2 treatment promotes the dispersion, PtRu alloying 
degree, surface Pt enrichment, and catalytic performance of the catalysts.18 Herein, PtAu/C with CeO2 
additions (PtAuCe) were prepared by the DP process and then further treated at 520-620 K under N2. To the 
best of our knowledge, this is the first time that such synergistic effect on the ORR performance and the 
surface segregation of PtAu/C cathode catalysts has been highlighted. Besides, the surface segregation 
profile of the PtAuCe alloys and surface-activity correlation has been elucidated systematically by use of the 
surface analysis and electrochemical characterization. By means of surface engineering through CeO2 
addition, the electrocatalytic activities can be further promoted by manipulating the surface structure and 
state of Pt-Au NPs. 
2. Experimental Procedure 
2.1. Preparation of catalysts.  
 The 10 wt % of PtAu/C alloy catalysts with the Pt/Au ratio of 1:1 modified by CeO2 (15 wt %) were 
prepared via the DP method. In detail, aqueous solutions of PtCl4 (Merck), HAuCl4 (Aldrich), and Ce(NO3)3 
(Fluka) were blended with deionized water. The mixtures were codeposited at 340 K onto the 75 wt % of 
commercial carbon black (Vulcan XC-72R) at pH = 7-9. The as-deposited catalysts were subsequently 
stirred at 298 K for 24 h, washed, dried at 320 K for 24 h, reduced in a flow of H2/N2 (10/90 vol %) gas at 
470 K for 1 h, and stored as fresh Pt50Au50/Ce15C (PtAuCe) catalysts. Further heat treatment of fresh 
catalysts was applied for 1 h at given temperature under flowing N2 gas. The alloy catalysts treated at 520,

For the as-prepared system, the surface is Pd enriched for Pd80Ni20 alloy due to the strain energy effect. In 
contrast, the surface is Ni enriched for as-prepared PdxNi100-x alloy with x = 20, 30, and 70 due to the 
reduction potential effect. After heating, surface segregation driven by surface energy and strain energy is 
observed. The lower surface energy of Pd and larger size of Pd atoms compared to Ni gives rise to Pd 
surface segregation. As a result, the Pd surface segregation lowers the surface energy of the alloy and allows 
the elastic strain in the lattice to be relaxed.24 
 
 
 
In the PtAu system, surface depletion of Au and surface of Pt is noted for various as-prepared 
PtxAu100-x alloys catalysts.10 For the Pt25Au75 alloy catalysts, the surface composition of Pt is 39 atom %, 
much higher than the nominal composition (25 atom %). For the Pt50Au50 and Pt75Au25 alloys, the surface 
composition of Pt is about 70 and 90 atom %, respectively. All PtAu alloy catalysts have structures with a 
Au-rich core and a Pt-rich shell. The surface enrichment of Pt on various PtAu alloys is inherent due to the 
reduction potential effect, especially for the Au-based alloy.10 Besides, it has been reported that the surface 
states of the Pt-Au NPs can be calculated based on the thermodynamic models.25 The results show that 
Pt-Au NPs with Au core/Pt shell, Pt core/Au shell, solid solution, or eutectic like structures all undergo 
surface Au segregation and structural transformations to form Pt core/Au shell structure identically at 800 K 
to achieve their most stable state.25 In other words, Au enrichment and Pt depletion on the surface will form 
inherently when Pt-Au NPs are heated at high temperatures. However, on the contrary, when T > 520 K, the 
Pt surface composition gradually increases and approaches to 82 % for the N620 sample. Here the surface 
Pt segregation in N2 at T > 520 K may be attributed to the CeO2 effect. Figure 1 summarizes the evolution 
of surface Pt composition of Pt-Au with/without CeO2 addition. For PtAuCe at 620 K, the circle with white 
shell/black core represents the significant Pt shell/Au core structure where the surface Pt composition is 
about 82 %. On the other hand, for the unmodified PtAu, a Pt shell/Au core structure is also formed due to 
the reduction potential effect. However, its surface Pt composition is lower than that for modified PtAu 
(PtAuCe), suggesting the addition of CeO2 enhances the surface enrichment of Pt.20 Surface segregation of 
Au is subsequently dominated at 520 K for both PtAuCe and PtAu samples. Nevertheless, at T > 520 K, 
surface segregation of Au consecutively takes place for the unmodified PtAu which is consistent with the 
DFT prediction while for PtAuCe,23 instead, Pt segregation occurs. Addition of CeO2 alters the inherent Au 
segregation property at high temperatures, suggesting it may modify the surface energies of Pt and/or Au 
consequently. Accordingly, the CeO2-induced Pt surface segregation and surface reconstruction takes place. 
It has been proposed that in the PtRu/C catalysts, incorporation of CeO2 increases the atom % of Ru in its 
oxidation state when compared with that of the unmodified PtRu/C because the oxygen atoms released from 
the CeO2 oxidize unalloyed Ru atoms while annealing under N2 at 620 K.26

4. Conclusions 
 In this study, the surface condition of Pt50Au50 alloy NP has been manipulated by CeO2 addition and N2 
heat treatment synergistically. Addition of CeO2 modifies the surface energies of Pt and/or Au and 
consequently alters the inherent Au segregation property at high temperatures. The ORR performance of 
N2-treated PtAuCe is importantly and proportionally related to their surface conditions. By means of surface 
engineering, the electrocatalytic activities can be further promoted by manipulating the surface structure and 
condition of Pt-Au NP.
