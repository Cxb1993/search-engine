三、研究目的 
本研究計畫是針對不同幾何形狀構件
在不同頻率與不同振幅之高頻諧和力作用
下隨時間及溫度變化的應力波和應力分佈
做一完整的研究。對構件施加超音波產生
之高頻諧和力後，輔以高解析度的數位攝
錄放影機觀測其瞬間之光彈應力波條紋，
再經由影像擷取軟體定格後，擷錄所需的
光彈影像圖檔，並引進 TDI (Time Delay 
and Integration)數位相機，同時依據光黏彈
本構方程式撰寫程式，建立一套完整的數
位動態光黏彈系統。 
 
四、相關文獻蒐集及整理 
在工程結構上的許多問題，比如固體
推進劑（Solid Propellant）的結構完整性
（Structural Integrity）、加熱金屬和陶瓷
結構的變形及高分子聚合物結構受力之變
形[1]、黏彈拘束層阻尼器[2]及接觸介面變
形[3~7]等，皆可視為線性黏彈的（Linear 
Viscoelastic）應力分析問題。在文獻中，
邊界元素法 (Boundary Element Method, 
BEM) 和 有 限 元 素 法 (Finite Element 
Method, FEM)曾被用來分析滾動黏彈接觸
應力[3~7]，其中在BEM領域中，黏彈接觸
應力首先由Hunter[3]和Morland[4]投入對
一個剛性圓柱滾輪滾過一個黏彈半平面體
之研究，而其分析都限制於未考慮摩擦之
正向接觸應力。Panek和Kalker[5]進一步考
慮三維黏彈正向接觸問題。黏彈切線接觸
問題首先由Goriacheva[6]解出，然而該解
僅適用於同材料的圓柱且該情形下的數值
解非完全解(Complete Solution)。Papat和
Batra[7]應是最先使用FEM來探討有關黏
彈橡膠包覆滾輪者，其分析仍限制於無摩
擦之正向接觸應力。Oden和Lin[8]則使用
FEM來研究廣義幾何高彈性和黏彈圓柱
的非線性滾動接觸，並考慮摩擦和慣性力
之效應。 
黏彈分析時因材料性質係隨時間及溫
度變化，若再加上邊界條件不單純時，其
應力分析是一相當複雜的數學問題，然
而，若採用由實驗力學中的光彈法
（Photoelasticity）所衍生出來的光黏彈法
（Photoviscoelasticity）則可由實驗方式求
解。1852年麥克斯威爾(Maxwell)提出應力
-光學定律(Stress-Optic Law)後，即奠定了
光彈法的理論基礎，光彈法從此被廣泛的
應用於實驗力學研究和工程實務中。它是
採用具有雙折射性的透明材料，製成與實
際構件形狀相似之模型，並施加與實際構
件負載相似之外力，置於偏振光場中，由
於光的相位差或光程差的干涉作用，形成
明暗相間的條紋，這些條紋代表著模型內
部的應力分佈情形，經由光彈理論加以分
析，便可確定模型內部各點應力，再依據
模型相似理論，換算成實際構件中之應
力。Pih[9]在1963年利用應變計量測結果與
光彈實驗結果相比較，驗證了動態光彈應
力 - 光 學 定 律 (Dynamic Photoelastic 
Stress-Optic Law) 的 正 確 性 。 Clark 和
Sanford [10]比較了光彈材料的靜態和動
態材料性質，得到光彈條紋常數(Fringe 
Constant)值在動態應力下高於靜態應力的
10﹪~30﹪。 
塑膠和橡膠等高分子聚合物是屬於非
晶體的固體，它們的阻尼行為是因為具有
相變和分子擴散等耗能物理現象，耗能的
能力則與振動頻率及溫度變化關係密切，
因為材料本身的微結構會隨溫度而改變
[11]。此外，高分子聚合物的彈性係數會
隨著振動的頻率增加而提高，所以在設計
時，必須特別注意工作溫度範圍，因為溫
度過高會影響材料之耗能行為。通常阻尼
器做成夾層放置於振動頻率機構中以達耗
能減震之目的。

是Mindlin[12]，他假設雙折射的發生是來

彈實驗的技巧是由Williams和Arenz[13]首
先提出。Dill和Fowlkes[1]則介紹了光黏彈
理論及測試的標稱試片和程序。Arenz等人

六、實驗裝置 
本計畫所架設的光黏彈系統分為兩部
份，第一部份是為了得到材料之黏彈性
質，以及利用拉伸機及光彈儀比對出光黏
彈系統的光學係數，以便於日後判讀光黏
彈條紋，而超音波之光黏彈系統與一般靜
態光彈實驗裝置之差別在於影像擷取方
式，因為超音波射入光黏彈試片後會迅速
產生熱應力，本實驗乃採用數位攝影機來
記錄整個動態光彈影像，再經由影像擷取
卡將訊號轉換成數位訊號，並利用影像處
理軟體擷錄成靜態影像圖，加以分析。 
第一部份材料實驗之實驗裝置架設示
意圖如圖一所示，圖二為其實景圖，主要
裝備為光彈儀、工業技術研究院所提供之
拉伸機、加溫爐、及各類影像擷取設備，
所使用之設備說明如下： 
 
6.1.1. 光彈儀實驗裝置 
為了便於攜帶及限於工研院內有限之
空間，在材料實驗部分採用大陸製教學型
光彈儀，配件含一片偏振片(Polarizer)、兩
片四分之一波片(Quarter-Wave Plate)及一
片檢視鏡(Analyzer)。 
 
6.1.2. 光彈儀光源箱 
為手工製小型鈉光燈燈源箱，內含四
根飛利浦製鈉光燈管連接 220V 電源，及
一小型風扇連接 110V 電源，主體構造為
木板，前方窗戶採用壓克力板使光均勻分
布。 
 
6.1.3. 硬碟式數位攝影機 
光彈影像拍攝採用 SONY 公司所生
產之數位攝影機，型號為 DCR-SR80，視
頻訊號為符合美國國家電視系統委員會
(National Television System Committee, 
NTSC)的彩色影像訊號，並合乎電子工業
聯盟(Electronic Industries Alliance, EIA)的
標準，能達最高畫素為 107 萬個像素。 
 
6.1.4. 拉伸試驗機 
採用工研院 Instron 4465 型拉伸試驗
機做拉伸測試，其具有手動式控制面板及
IEEE 裝置連線至桌上型電腦控制，所利用
的軟體為 Instron 所提供之 Series 9。 
 
6.1.5. 拉伸試驗用加溫爐 
使用詠欣公司製造之加溫爐，加溫爐
為特殊規格，為配合工研院拉伸試驗機及
光彈影像需求而特別訂做。加溫爐可加熱
至 300℃之高溫，且可提供拉伸機夾頭進
出，前後都有玻璃窗可供光彈儀光源穿透。 
 
6.1.6. CCD 及鏡頭 
CCD 是由德國公司 New Electronic 
Technology (NET) 所 生 產 ， 型 號 為
FO432/SB 最高解析度可達 145 萬畫素，
並搭配 18-108mm 變焦鏡頭，訊號傳送方
式係透過 IEEE 1394 插槽，兩者皆是向肯
定公司購買。 
 
6.1.7. 筆記型電腦及軟體 
筆記型電腦必須具備 IEEE 1394 之接
頭用來連接 CCD，軟體使用肯定公司提供
之取像軟體。 
 
6.1.8. 校正用溫度計 
為向詠欣公司購買型號 YS-1300K 的
溫度計，使用溫度範圍為 50℃-300℃，並
送到台証科技股份有限公司校正過，量測
不確定度為 0.48℃。

7.4 超音波實驗 
超音波設備具有自動追頻之功能，可
自動調整輸出的頻率至喇叭(Horn)的共振
頻率，因此以超音波設備做為高頻諧和力
的輸出源，並搭配光彈儀與錄影機記錄動
態應力條紋。光彈儀的光源則選用鈉光
燈，並調整偏振片與四分之一波片的角度
至圓偏振光場。 
實驗前先將光彈試片以 Letbond 公司
所 生 產 編 號 4210 接著劑與連接桿
(Connecting Rod)黏合，再將連接桿以止付
螺絲(Socket Set Screws)與超音波換能器
連接。接著調整錄影機的焦距與曝光量，
一切就序後即可進行實驗。 
在實驗過程中同時以錄影機拍攝試片
內部應力成長過程，並擷取超音波導入 1
秒、2 秒、3 秒、4 秒與 5 秒等 5 個時間點
的影像進行分析。另外，為了探討幾何形
狀對應力分佈的影響，共選用了 4 種不同
形狀的試片，包含矩形、直線形、階梯形
與垂鏈形，以頻率為 20 KHz 的超音波設
備進行實驗，相同的，也擷取上述 5 個時
間點的影像分析。接著，分別導入 15 KHz
與 20 KHz 兩種頻率的超音波於矩形試片
內，研究不同頻率對於應力條紋的影響。
最後，將頻率 20 KHz 但不同振幅大小的
超音波導入矩形試片，探討不同振幅造成
的應力條紋變化，而振幅的大小根據控制
面板上的刻度分為三個等級，包含最小振
幅、中等振幅與最大振幅，三種振幅對應
的實際大小利用測微儀量測而得，如表四
所示。

7.5 有限單元法之分析結果 
此部分結果主要是與超音波及光彈實
驗相互比較，所以此節也分成三個小節來
敘述。有限單元法分析是使用市售軟體
ANSYS 模擬試片受超音激振時的應力分
佈，由於環氧樹脂材料試片受溫度影響
後，材料性質包含彈性模數與蒲松氏比皆
會改變，且因為溫度並不是均勻的上升，
所以材料參數也不是均勻的改變，這導致
在前處理時輸入材料參數的不易，因此目
前受到此限制只能分析單純只有駐波產生
的應力分佈而無法分析熱應力波的影響，
也就是只能模擬超音波激振 0.1 秒時的光
彈條紋影像。另外因為光彈條紋代表的是
主應力差，所以 ANSYS 描繪的應力結果
為應力強度(Stress Intensity)分佈。 
 
7.4.3 不同振幅 
圖二十三為 20KHz 中等振幅超音波
激振時與停止瞬間的光彈條紋，試片受超

5 秒鐘的實驗影像。與圖十八的 0.1 秒實驗
影像相互比較後可以發現，當輸入最小振
幅的超音波時，節點的條紋級次大約為 1.5
級，而輸入中等振幅的超音波時，節點處
約為 2 級，帶入 (10) 式計算後約為
6.00MPa，此結果是因為振幅變大而導致
應力值上升。另外由圖二十三(b)至(e)發現
熱應力增加的速率較最小振幅的超音波激

八、結論 
本研究使用數位光彈法首先探討光黏
彈材料之力學與光學性質，成功求得鬆弛
應變雙折射係數主曲線與逆鬆弛應力雙折
射係數主曲線，套入光黏彈法理論，針對
不同形狀的構件以及在不同頻率與振幅的
高頻諧和力作用下的應力分佈做一探討，
並另外使用有限單元法軟體 ANSYS 與光
彈法分析結果相互比較，可以看到兩種方
法之分析結果相當吻合。 
 
九、研究成果自評 
本研究之研究內容完全符合原計畫之
規劃，研究成果具學術與應用價值，適合
在學術期刊發表論文。 
    本計畫經過長時間之嚐試及失敗，得
到了一個最好的方法可以進行量產試片，
目前產出之試片有均一性，可以提供實驗
所需。 
而在材料性質的測試方面，雖然實驗
程序相當繁複，分析程序也相當複雜，不
過已經成功求得力學與光學主曲線，可以
代入光黏彈法理論中進行黏彈材料之應力
分析。由高頻諧和力實驗也可以觀察到四
種幾何形狀的應力分佈皆呈現三個同心
圓，但是應力最大值的位置會改變；若改
變超音波的頻率則會造成節點位置的改
變；若使用越大振幅的超音波會產生更大
的應力。另外也可發現熱應力與超音波激
振時間成正比關係，激振時間越久熱應力
越大，試片內的溫度也會隨著激振時間增
加而上升，因此溫度越高則熱應力也越大。 
當初計畫規劃時低估了試片製作的困
難度，造成計畫進度的拖延，其實試片製
作的困難突破之後，整個計畫的進行即大
為順利，且投入較多的人力，最後將計畫
內的進度順利完成。

Experimental and Numerical Analysis of Hygrothermal Deformation 
of Backlight Modules 
 
Wei-Chung Wang*, Hsuan-Hao Hsu, Ya-Hsin Chang  
Department of Power Mechanical Engineering, National Tsing Hua University 
Hsinchu, Taiwan  
ABSTRACT 
The main function of backlight modules (BLMs) of thin film transistor liquid crystal displays (TFT-LCDs) is to provide 
steady and intensive illumination for the whole monitor.  Warpage of BLMs can produce non-uniform brightness in 
TFT-LCDs and result in the so-called Mura effect.  In this paper, a warpage inspection system of BLMs was assembled.  
Experiment was designed to simulate the real working conditions of temperature and humidity, i.e. hygrothermal 
working conditions, of BLMs.  Phase-shifting technique was adopted to improve the resolution of shadow moiré fringes.  
The surface morphology under different working conditions of BLMs was then obtained and plotted.  The out-of-plane 
displacement fields of BLMs under the same working conditions was also analyzed by the commercially available finite 
element software ANSYS and compared with the experimental results.  Finally, thermal stress distribution of BLMs can 
be calculated from the modified ANSYS model. 
 
Keywords: TFT-LCD, backlight module, warpage, shadow moiré method, hygrothermal working conditions 
 
1. INTRODUCTION 
Along with advantages of lower power consumption and less space needed comparing to traditional cathode ray tubes 
(CRT), flat panel displays (FPDs) have almost replaced CRTs completely.  In variety of FPDs, TFT-LCDs take the 
leading role in global market share.  In order to maintain continuous market superiority, the evolution of TFT-LCDs has 
focused on creative research and development to enhance better color performance such as brightness, saturation and 
smooth dynamic range of images in TFT-LCDs.  Several techniques for inspection of components used in TFT-LCDs are 
available so far.  However, those methods are primarily employed to check whether electrical and optical properties of 
assembled glass substrates (the upper one called array and the lower one called cell) are good or not.  The inspection 
methodology for accurate and efficient measurement of deformation of components is urgently needed. 
Besides array and cell substrates, the backlight module (BLM) is the other essential component of TFT-LCD.  The 
structure of a BLM includes a light guide plate (LGP), optical films such as reflect sheets, diffuser plates, prisms; and the 
cold cathode fluorescent lamp (CCFL) or light-emitting diodes (LEDs) as light source.  The main function of the BLM is 
to provide steady and intensive illumination for TFT-LCDs.  Since TFT-LCD will be used in different parts of the world 
and worldwide climate differs significantly in different regions, the dependability and durability of TFT-LCD are very 
challengeable.  The cylindrical projection images of global temperature and relative humidity fields at 850 hpa [1] are 
shown in Figs. 1 and 2, respectively.  It’s obvious that temperature and relative humidity vary quite significantly from 
region to region. 
Since the whole assembly of the BLM is placed in a very restrictive space, after a TFT-LCD is put into service for a long 
period of time, high temperature near light source occurs.  Warpage of the adjacent LGP and optical films is occasionally 
occurred due to thermal stress.  In fact, the BLM itself is not perfectly hermetical in TFT-LCDs; humidity is definitely 
another factor need to be considered in reducing the warpage.  Typical Mura phenomenon [2] produced by warpage of 
the LGP and optical films is shown in Fig. 3.  
Fig. 4 is the schematic diagram of warpage in the BLMs.  Earlier researches towards improvement of warpage occurred 
in BLMs focused on invention of new materials.  For example, under certain hygrothermal conditions, warpage was 
taken place when polyethylene terephthalate (PET) was used as the material of diffuser plates.  Kim [3] proposed to use 
polymethyl methacrylate (PMMA) to replace PET.  In 2005, Kim et al. [2] employed three layers of composite materials

3.2 Shadow moiré system 
The phase-shifting shadow moiré system is depicted in Fig. 8.  The light source is an AC regulated fiber optic illuminator 
(Techniquip Corp., U.S.A.).  Its quartz halogen bulb provides flicker-free illumination; furthermore, the fiber tube can be 
freely rotated to any direction for the convenience of operation.  The grating of size 11in x 11in and pitch 100μm is made 
of chrome-plating glass.  The function of the grating holder is not only to hold the grating but also to connect the grating 
and the stepping motor outside the oven for phase-shifting.  The use of the stepping motor not only provides the accurate 
but also rapid control of the movement of the grating holder.  The accuracy of the movement of the stepping motor is 
0.02 mm.  The rapid movement of the grating holder is essential for executing the three step phase-shifting in the real-
time inspection.  The grating holder is also manufactured from Invar.  Invar is a material of high density and there is a 
weight limit of the grating holder that the stepping motor can be supported.  A photograph of the grating holder used in 
this paper is depicted in Fig. 9.  Weight reduction was achieved by removing the unnecessary parts from several parts of 
the grating holder.  Micrometer head located at each corner of grating holder can be individually adjusted to make sure 
that the grating is parallel to the specimen.    
The temperature and humidity of the oven is controlled by a microcomputer.  To ensure the image was taken when the 
temperature and humidity achieved stable condition, the image was taken two hours after the specimen went through the 
specific hygrothermal loading.  The original image, image of shifting by one-third pitch and image of shifting by two-
third pitch were sequentially taken and then superposed together into one phase map.  By using the unwrapping 
algorithm PhotoMechanics Tools [9], the whole-field phase of the specimen was extracted.  Typical three step phase-
shifting fringe maps of the BLM specimen under temperature  35℃ and humidity 40%RH are shown in Fig. 10.  In fact, no 
significant difference can be observed from those three fringe maps by naked eyes.  Superposed map and un-wrapped 
phase map of the three step phase-shifting fringe maps are shown in Fig. 11.  Finally, the surface morphology of the 
specimen under hygrothermal loading was reconstructed by MATLAB [12]. 
The AUO used the thermal couple to measure internal temperature of a LCD and discovered that the temperature near 
the light source could reach 90°C under working conditions.  Based on this information, four hygrothermal conditions 
were selected in this paper: (a) Humidity maintained at 40%RH and temperature range was set at 35°C~85°C, 
experimental data was taken at every 10°C; (b) Humidity maintained at 60%RH and temperature range was set at 
35°C~85°C, experimental data was taken at every 10°C; (c) Humidity maintained at 80%RH and temperature range was 
set at 35°C~85°C, experimental data was taken at every 10°C; (d) Temperature maintained at 85°C and humidity range 
was set at  30%RH~80%RH, experimental data were collected at every 10%RH. 
 
 
 
 
4. RESULTS AND DISCUSSIONS 
4.1 Experimental fringe maps distribution of hygrothermal condition (a)  
Phase maps and surface morphology of specimens of hygrothermal condition (a) are shown in Fig. 12.  Maximum out-
of-plane displacements of specimens of hygrothermal conditions (a) under different temperatures are listed in Table 1. 
Variation of maximum displacements of Table 1 is also shown in Fig. 13.  Obviously, left side of the BLM, i.e. the side 
farther away from the light source, deformed larger than the right side under hygrothermal loading.  A typical schematic 
distribution of surface pattern of the BLM is shown in Fig. 14, i.e. the size of the surface pattern is inversely proportional 
to the distance from the side of LED [13].  This larger deformation may be generated because of the lower spacing of 
surface pattern of the LGP close to the left side.   
Based on the observation on Fig. 12, fringes appeared near to the right side started to bend toward the bottom of the 
BLM specimens when the temperature increased to 55°C.  The change of fringe shapes may be because the absorption of 
moisture of the internal components and thus non-linear elastic deformation was produced.  From 35°C to 55°C, 
maximum warpage of the specimen gained about 0.15mm linearly when rising 10°C every time.  From 55°C to 75°C, 
hygrothermal deformation of internal components of BLMs changed from elastic to plastic state.  Hence the maximum 
warpage of the specimen grew slowly and the maximum value stayed at 0.6mm.  Finally, the polymer materials of the 
specimen entered into the strain hardening region.  This can be observed from the fact that the maximum out-of-plane 
displacement was increased from 0.625mm at 75°C to 0.7342mm at 85°C.  The permanent out-of-plane deformation of 
BLMs after undergoing the hygrothermal loadings can be observed by dismantling the specimens from the clamp device.  
It was found that the out-of-plane deformation of the specimens remains even one day after disassembling from the 
clamp device.

4.5 ANSYS model 
Three assumptions were made in designing the ANSYS model of specimens used in this paper: 
1. Environmental temperature is uniformly distributed; 
2. Convective heat transfer is ignored; 
3. Material properties do not change with temperature. 
Element Shell 99 of ANSYS with eight nodes was adopted to simulate the structure of BLMs.  To approach four-side 
clamped boundary condition of specimens, out-of-plane displacements of four sides were set to zero.  The starting 
temperature was chosen at 25℃ and the aforementioned selected thermal loadings were used.  
The initial out-of-plane displacement field obtained from ANSYS at temperature 65°C is shown in Fig. 21.  The result is 
distinctly different from the experimental one.  As mentioned earlier, as shown in Fig. 14, the size of the surface pattern 
of the BLM is inversely proportional to the distance from the side of light source.   Therefore, as shown in Fig. 22, the 
finite element model of the BLM was divided into four parts with different mechanical properties called real constants 
[11] in ANSYS to simulate the distribution of surface pattern.  The initially adopted ANSYS model was then modified 
by considering the variation of the density of micro pattern.  Out-of-plane displacement fields of modified ANSYS 
model are depicted in Fig. 23.  The fashion of deformation of Fig. 23 is similar to those of experimental results as shown 
in Fig. 12.  Von Mises stress fields of BLMs under hygrothermal loadings can thus be calculated.   
Von Mises stress fields of BLMs at different temperatures are shown in Fig. 24.  It is obvious that the max von Mises 
stresses of all BLMs occured at two corners of the left side.  This result explains why, as mentioned earlier, different 
layers of BLMs slightly stick together at corners under hygrothermal conditions.

5. CONCLUSIONS 
Deformation and stress fields of BLMs under four hygrothermal loadings were investigated in this paper.  The phase-
shifting shadow moiré system was used to measure the whole-field warpage of BLMs.  Careful design of the components 
of the system, e.g. the weight reduction of the grating holder, the tightening sequence of the screws of the clamp device, 
the use of the Invar and the stepping motor, etc. ensures the accuracy of the system and the success of the experiments.  
The ANSYS model was then modified based on the results of the shadow moiré method.   
The experimental results show that maximum ratio of out-of-plane displacements of specimens increases linearly before 
the temperature reached to 55°C.  It is very possible that the hygrothermal deformation of specimens already exceeds the  
elastic limitation and the plastic deformation was obtained at 65°C, therefore the maximum out-of-plane displacements 
didn't ascent any more.  Strain hardening occurred when the temperature achieved 65°C and the out-of-plane 
displacements kept on going up.  For specimens went through the second hygrothermal loading, polymer materials inside 
BLMs softened while the humidity increased and the surfaces of BLMs appeared another inverted bowl shape.  Density 
of micro pattern on LGPs and other parameters of every optical film significantly influenced the out-of-plane 
deformation of BLMs.  Therefore, the designing of ANSYS model of BLMs should take special consideration to the 
variation of density of the micro pattern of LGPs to increase the accuracy of the numerical analysis.   Furthermore, under 
the assumption of the uniform distribution of the environmental temperature, four hygrothermal loadings were adopted to 
simulate real working conditions of BLMs.  However, the fact of non-uniform temperature distribution due to the local 
intensification of the high temperature from the light source of BLMs should be considered not only in the experiments 
but also in the numerical simulation.

ABSTRACT 
In recent years, artificial mesh has been widely used in medical applications, in particular, the repair of pelvic organ 
prolapse.  However, not only before but also after implanting, the mechanical properties of the artificial mesh have 
not been well investigated.  In the past, because of the rather special structure of the artificial mesh, experimental 
measurement cannot be easily performed.  In this paper, both the tensile test and three-dimensional digital image 
correlation (DIC) method were employed to determine the deformation and mechanical properties of the artificial 
mesh before implanting.     
Keywords: Artificial Mesh, Digital Image Correlation, Deformation, Mechanical Properties, Implanting. 
 
1. INTRODUCTION  
As many as 50% of all women who have given birth vaginally have some degree of pelvic organ prolapse (POP) [1].  
Even women who haven’t given birth are at risk [1].  Once women enter menopause, they are also at risk for POP 
[1].  In the United States [2], 11% of women require surgical correction for POP or urinary incontinence. Definitely 
the quality of life of a patient suffered from POP is greatly affected.  A conventional repair surgery with tissue 
Three types of most commonly used AM in repair of POP were used as the materials for test specimens in this 
paper, i.e. Gynemesh [11], Marlex [12] and Mersilene [13].  Photographs of those three types of AM are shown in 
Fig. 1.  Two kinds of specimens were prepared from each type of AM by careful cutting along the knitting axis (KA) 
and across knitting axis (CKA), respectively.  Therefore, there are six arrangements of the test specimens.  They 
are called Gynemesh KA, Gynemesh CKA, Marlex KA, Marlex CKA, Mersilene KA and Mersilene CKA, 
respectively.  Photographs of those six arrangements of test specimens are shown in Fig. 2.  Five specimens of 
each arrangement were prepared.  For each arrangement, the five specimens were named by number, e.g. 
Gynemesh KA-1, Gynemesh KA-2, etc.  The Flower Silkware Foundation [14] was brushed on the surface of the 
test specimen to reduce its reflection.  Speckle pattern of the test specimen was produced by using the fine black 
marker pen making dots randomly on the surface.   
Before placing the test specimen into the clamping mechanism of the micro-tensile test machine, rubber end tabs 
were adhered onto the two ends of the test specimen.  The adhesive used was cyanoacrylate [15].  The size of 
the test specimen is 25 mm (length along the tensile load direction) x 20 mm (width).  The displacement rate of the 
test machine used in the experiments was 1mm/min.  An image was taken at every 3 seconds by the VIC-3D 
system.  The load applied for each image taken was recorded simultaneously on the laptop.  Photograph of the 
tensile test setup with the VIC-3D system are shown in Fig. 3. 
Maximum stress and Young’s modulus of the test specimen along KA and CKA were determined by the tensile test, 
respectively.  Average values of maximum stress and Young’s modulus of the five test specimens of each 
arrangement were calculated and used as the nominal values of maximum stress and Young’s modulus of that 
particular arrangement.  Deformation of the tensile test specimen was also measured by the 3D-DIC method.  
Young’s moduli obtained by the tensile test and 3D-DIC method were then compared. 
 
3. RESULTS AND DISCUSSIONS [16, 17]  
Figure 4 shows the stress-strain diagrams obtained from the tensile test specimens of arrangements KA-1 and 
CKA-1 of three types of AM.  Since the AM is manufactured by interweaving several fibers together along both KA 
and CKA, one or more of the fibers may be broken at certain load stage during the tensile test while the rest fibers 
can still resist the load.   That is the reason why there are several abrupt drop and rise parts appear in the 
stress-strain diagrams of three types of AM, in particular in the Gynemesh specimens.  The first linear portion of 
each stress-strain diagram as indicated in red color was selected to determine the Young’s modulus.  A linear 
least squares fit was performed for this linear portion and an equation was obtained.  The slope of the equation is 
defined as the Young’s modulus of the corresponding test specimen.  The followings are the strain ranges of the 
specimens shown in Fig. 4: 0.25～0.35 (Gynemesh KA-1); 0.3～0.4 (Gynemesh CKA-1); 0.4～0.6 (Marlex KA-1); 
0.4～0.6 (Marlex CKA-1); 0.2～0.3 (Mersilene KA-1); 0.35～0.45 (Mersilene CKA-1). 
As shown in Fig. 4(b), frequent drops and rises of the stress-strain curve occur between the maximum stress and 
of AM (except Mersilene KA) to the conventional isotropic homogeneous materials.
