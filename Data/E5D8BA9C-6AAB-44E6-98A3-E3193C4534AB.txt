反應的系統基本上也成立。為達到此目的，
除了材料技術與燃燒技術的改進外，葉片冷
卻技術的提升更是重要。目前應用的渦輪機
典型的進口溫度已高達 1900K【1】，遠超過
葉片材料可承受的溫度，這使得渦輪機前幾
級動葉片及靜葉片(或導向葉片)暴露的環境
更嚴酷，也必須要有比以往更可靠的冷卻設
計以保護葉片。膜冷卻(或稱薄膜冷卻)(film 
cooling)是保護氣輪機葉片的一項重要冷卻
技術。適當的膜冷卻可提供一層冷空氣薄
膜，將葉片與熱環境隔離而達到保護葉片的
效果。提供膜冷卻之方法乃是從適當設計的
葉片壁面與端壁(endwall)壁面膜冷卻孔陣
列(film cooling hole array)噴出二次冷卻流
體(secondary coolant)注入沿壁面的邊界層
流【2】。當二次冷卻流剛從膜冷卻孔噴出
時，如果噴射率(blowing rate)不會大到將邊
界層吹離(blow off)，則冷卻空氣將貼著葉片
表面前進，直接把葉片與外部熱氣隔離。當
冷卻空氣繼續往下游前進而與熱氣混合
時，也有減低邊界層氣體溫度的效果，因此
燃燒室(combustion chamber)出來的熱氣加
諸於葉片的熱負荷(heat load)就會減低，葉
片受熱應力而發生潛變破壞的機率將會降
低，葉片的壽命得以延長。膜冷卻設計良好
的時候，葉片內部冷卻(internal cooling)的需
求也相對降低。而當膜冷卻失效時，內部冷
卻之負荷加重，葉片溫度分佈更不均勻，熱
應力加強，葉片潛變破壞的機率就會提昇。 
 
二、文獻探討 
Wanda Jiang and Han【3】利用 5 個葉
片的線性串級，以實驗方法研究膜冷卻孔之
孔列位置對於葉片膜冷卻有效性之影響。膜
冷卻流體則採用空氣或二氧化碳氣體以模
擬溫度場造成的密度比。陳建維【4】利用
奈揮發的質傳技術，以圓柱模擬葉片之前
端，探討高紊流強度下之質傳與薄膜冷卻之
影響。謝文憲【5】也是利用奈揮發的質傳
技術，探討高紊流強度對蓮蓬頭式薄膜冷卻
之影響。實驗結果顯示，在固定吹氣比時二
次流對葉片表面直接影響的區域隨紊流強
度的增加而減小，而熱(質)傳則隨紊流強度
的增加而增加。Goldstein and Chen【6,7】對
於近端壁區域之膜冷卻有詳細的研究。他們
使用單排【6】與雙排【7】膜冷卻孔，改變
吹氣率與密度比，以及使用質傳方法進行實
驗。結果顯示，由於端壁的存在造成的三維
流場，與二維流區域比較，壓力面的膜冷卻
有效性僅有微小的影響，但是在吸力面有一
個三角型區域，在那裡膜冷卻幾乎是完全失
效的。由流動觀察顯示，該三角型區域大致
相當於通道渦漩沿著吸力面掃過的區域。
Wu and Chang【8】的結果顯示，在低紊流
強度下，利用雙排孔形成交叉噴流的膜冷卻
所形成的渦漩如果與葉片通道中的通道渦
漩同方向時，兩者的交互作用有助於增加膜
冷卻的保護面積。田智元【9】的實驗顯示，
兩噴注撞擊後可產生大的擴散範圍，沿流動
方向切面為一扇形面積，而且，藉由控制兩
噴注不同的動量，可改變噴流衝擊後的偏移
角(shift angle)。雖然此技術尚未被用在氣體
衝擊流上，但是理論上對於膜冷卻薄膜的形
成應該是一個很好的應用。主要原因是兩噴
注互相衝射後，雖然會形成鐘形(bell shape)
的擴散區，當它靠近牆壁時，由於壁面的阻
擋，將促使該擴散區沿壁面往橫方向擴展。
Ahn et al【10】提出類似的做法，他們以平
板直線流的情形作實驗發現在高吹氣比
時，兩交叉噴射膜冷卻噴注的交互作用變得
更重要。

Abstract. Jet impingement heat transfer on a target plate covered with a thick porous layer with or 
without a cylindrical center cavity is experimentally investigated using the transient liquid crystal 
technique. Based on the results of jet impingement on a bare flat plate, heat transfer enhancement due 
to the attachment of porous medium is assessed. The varying parameters in the experiments include 
the nozzle-to-plate distance, jet Reynolds number, jet-to-cavity diameter ratio, and the cavity depth. 
Results of Nusselt number distribution, stagnation-zone Nusselt number, and averaged Nusselt 
number over a region of 3 times the hole diameter are documented. Experimental results show that 
the attachment of the porous layer with a center cavity can either hamper, or effectively enhance the 
jet impingement heat transfer over a flat plate. The maximum enhancement occurs at jet Reynolds 
number of 12400 when the cavity is a through hole and the cavity has the same diameter as the jet. 
The stagnation-zone Nusselt number increases 58.3% and the averaged Nusselt number increases 
77.5% at the maximum enhancement condition. On the other hand, the addition of the thick porous 
layer without a center cavity gave rise to severe adverse effect on jet impingement heat transfer. 
Introduction 
Jet impingement on surfaces is an effective way of providing local, elevated heat (and/or mass) 
transfer. To enhance convective heat/mass transfer coefficient for a large area, multiple jets in the 
form of an array can be used. Applications of jet impingement include industrial drying process for 
textile composites [1], electronics cooling [2], and internal cooling for turbine blade leading edge 
regions [3,4], to name just a few. For further enhancement in impingement heat transfer, modification 
to the target plate using attached porous layer has received growing interest. In this regard, some 
investigations were conducted numerically (e.g. [5]), but experimental heat transfer data was 
relatively scarce. 
Currently there is a very large body of literature regarding jet impingement on flat or modified 
surfaces, thereby a thorough review is not possible. Including the above-mentioned literature, only a 
few are further noted below. Lytle and Webb [6]  utilized LDV and infrared thermal image technique 
to measure the distributions of flow velocity, turbulence intensity, and Nusselt number for cases with 
small nozzle-to-plate distance (H/dj < 1). The jet Reynolds number was between 3600 and 27600, and 
the velocity at the jet exit was fully-developed pipe flow profile. Their results showed that the 
stagnation Nusselt number was inversely proportional to (H/dj)0.191. When H/dj ≦  0.25, the 
stagnation Nusselt number formed a local minimum. Two peaks were seen in local Nusselt number 
distribution along radial direction. The inner peak was attributed to fluid acceleration from stagnation 
region, and the outer peak was attributed to highest turbulence intensity at that location. In the study 
of Baughn and Shimizu [7], nearly fully developed pipe flow was established at the jet exit. The data 
at Re = 23750 was presented from which it was seen that two peak values of local Nusselt number 
occurred for H/dj =2. For all other higher H/dj values (6, 10, and 14), the stagnation Nusselt number 
was always the maximal point. The two-hump phenomena were also seen in the results of Bizzak and 
Chyu [8] using short nozzle and non-fully-developed flow at jet exit. Kim et al. [2] attached a 
9mm-thick layer of aluminum foam or pin fins on a flat plate in a multi-air jet impingement. They

The transient liquid crystal technique was used in

and 10 at Re = 17900. When the jet-to-plate distance increases, the maximal velocity decreases (Fig.

than H/dj = 6. Since the jet diameter is the same as the hole diameter in these runs, the spent gas in the 
case with H/dj = 6 may escape from the cavity opening as explained above. The case with H/dj = 8 
already has slight lateral expansion in jet diameter as it reaches the opening of the cavity. It may help 
to reduce escape of the fluid, thereby increase heat transfer rate. 
To investigate the effect of jet-to-hole diameter ratio, data was taken with fixed hole diameter (dh = 
10mm), jet-to-plate distance (H/dj = 6), cavity depth (Hh/Hp = 0.8), and jet Reynolds number (Re = 
17900). The results of comparison for dj/dh = 0.5, 1, and 2 are shown in Fig. 8 in Nu against r/dh. In 
the case of dj/dh = 0.5, the spent gas can easily escape from the cavity opening, causing a remarkable 
decrease in Nusselt number. Comparing to the case with dj/dh = 1, the case with dj/dh = 2 has lower 
stagnation Nusselt number due to smaller centerline velocity. However, since the jet diameter is twice 
of the hole diameter, part of the jet fluid impinges on near-hole porous surface. The prolonged higher 
Nusselt number in the far region may be attributed to the wider jet coverage area. 
The thermal performance of jet impingement on a plate with and without a porous layer is further 
compared in terms of stagnation-zone Nusselt number (Nusz) and averaged Nusselt number (Nuavg) as 
summarized in Table 1. The stagnation-zone Nusselt number is averaged from the region directly 
below the jet (r/dj < 0.5), while the averaged Nusselt number accounts for the region for r/dj < 3 since 
the contribution from the data outside that region is much less significant. The average procedure 
takes the radial distribution of Nu data each of them weighted by its representative ring area. This 
calculation is reasonable because the Nu distribution is basically axisymmetric. Table 1 shows again 
that attachment of a thick porous layer without a center cavity (Cases 7-9) gives very poor thermal 
performance compared to flat plate (FP) cases 1-6. It can be calculated to show that the attachment of 
a porous layer with a through hole at Re = 12400 (Case 18) gives the highest enhancement over a bare 
plate. If the results are compared to the flat plate data at the same H/dj and the same Reynolds number 
(Cases 4-6), the enhancements of Case 18 are 102% on stagnation-zone Nusselt number, and 181% 
on averaged Nusselt number. However, it would be more practical and conservative to base on Cases 
1-3 in the comparison when the jet nozzle is allowed to set close to the target plate. Even for this 
comparison, the enhancements of Case 18 are 58.3% on stagnation-zone Nusselt number, and 77.5% 
on averaged Nusselt number.
