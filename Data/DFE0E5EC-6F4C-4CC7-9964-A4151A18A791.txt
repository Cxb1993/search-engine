Transmission electron microscopy (TEM), a magnetostrictive-meter setup, and a superconducting quantum interference
device (SQUID) magnetometer were used to investigate the microstructures, saturation magnetostriction, and magnetic
properties of bulk Fe81Ga19 and Fe73Ga19Ni8 (at.%) ferromagnetic shape memory alloys (FSMAs). In the Fe81Ga19 FSM alloy,
after solution treatment (ST) at 1100 ℃ for 4 h and quenching in ice brine, the antiphase boundary segments (APBs) of the
D03 domain were observed in the A2 (disordered) matrix. When the Fe81Ga19 FSM alloy was ST and aged at 750 ℃ for 24 h
and then furnace cooled (FC), the A2+D03 phase transformed into A2+B2+D03 structures, accompanied by coarse B2+D03
precipitates in the A2 matrix. This result decreased the saturation magnetostriction of the aged Fe81Ga19 FSM alloy. However,
adding more Ni to the Fe81-xGa19Nix (x = 8 at.% Ni) FSM alloys enhanced the formation of B2+D03 structures, further
decreased the saturation magnetostriction, and destroyed the ferromagnetic shape memory effect (FSME) in Fe73Ga19Ni8
FSM alloy ST, aged at 750 ℃ for 24 h, and FC. © 2013 American Institute of Physics.
