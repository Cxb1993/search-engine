ABSTRACT 
 
Traditionally, the bearing capacity of shallow foundation and the active or the 
passive earth pressure of retaining wall are obtained from limit equilibrium method  
based on the mechanics of continuum. However, the geo-material is mainly composed 
of discrete particles, and it often exhibits flow behavior, which is different from the 
continuous solid. Therefore, this research aims to realize the mechanical behavior of 
granular material using the software PFC2D based on the distinct method (DEM). 
 Firstly, the micro parameters of granular material were verified by the direct 
shear test. Then, the influences of the particle size, the packing, the friction coefficient 
of particle and the stiffness on the bearing capacity and the earth pressure were 
explored. The simulation results show that: (1) For particles with simple packing and 
random packing, the bearing capacity of foundation is lower than that with dense 
packing; (2) an increasing friction coefficient increase the bearing capacity; (3) the 
larger size and the higher stiffness will lead to the higher strength. Among the factors, 
the influence of stiffness is more apparent than that of other factors. For the results of 
earth pressure, aforementioned tendencies are also observed. Furthermore, triaxial 
tests of granular material are simulated. The results shows that the failure envelope is 
nonlinear and the plastic deformation reveals non-associated flow.  
 
Keywords: distinct element method, PFC2D

1.2 研究目的 
基於顆粒材料之物理性質眾多，如：粒徑大小、排列方式、勁度、鍵結力、幾何
形狀等，然而最直接影響其力學行為之參數，應為與顆粒接觸最直接相關之參數，故
在此研究中探討顆粒之勁度(正向勁度與剪力勁度)、排列(緊密排列、簡單排列、隨機
排列)、粒徑大小及摩擦角大小對整體宏觀行為之影響，另在接觸模式上，除了現有
之理論可直接使用外，初步嘗試一些非線性之接觸模式，以期更擬合我們所需之力學
行為。

2.3.1 數位影像相關係數法(Digital Image Correlation，DIC) 
DIC(Digital Image Correlation)於 1980 年代被提出，之後由 Chu 等人(1985)，結
合變形理論、數位影像相關係數法，以及內插理論來擴展此技術之應用範圍，以提
高其實用性。數位影像在拍照時或多或少都會有些雜訊，Sutton et al (1991)提出一
個在分析時降低雜訊影響之方法，另提及數位影像相關係數法量測資料之雜訊小於
0.01 個像素(pixels)。Vellinga et al (2000)將數位影像相關係數法結合掃瞄式電子顯微
鏡(SEM)，觀測到微小應變，其位移尺度大約是微米等級。Raffard et al (2001)用數
位影像相關係數法量測岩石夾泥之變形行為，以對其力學行為有了更精確的認識。
Dost et al (1999)用原子力顯微鏡擷取影像，其尺度約為奈米等級，配合數位影像相
關係數法技術，可觀察奈米級位移或奈米級裂縫。 
此段簡單介紹數位影像之理論基礎 (郭瑞昭等, 2005)，數位影像相關係數法為一
非接觸性量測，可量測物體表面之位移，其量測原理為解析物體表面之紋路或記號，
概念為物體受力變形後，其表面之紋路定會些微改變，然在分析前需先將照片灰階
化，藉由前後二張相片之比對可得其相關性，分析其表面上之位移與應變。以圖 2.6

3.4.4 使用者自訂模式(user define model, UDM) 
PFC 除內建模式外還提供使用者自訂模式，此模式可以讓使用者撰寫任何形式
之接觸模式。在使用此模式前需先用 C++編譯出動態執行檔(*.dll)，其 C++ code 需符
合 PFC manual 之規則，然模型建立之流程皆跟內建模式相同，但此模式可以依使用
者自訂參數及運算規則，自由度相當大。

第五章 大地工程應用－基礎承載力 
本章主要探討不同幾何、力學性質之顆粒材料對於條形淺基礎承載力之影響。根
據第四章驗証所得之材料參數，作為本章基本輸入參數，後針對各參數規畫一系列之
分析，用以探討淺基礎承載力之變化，以及塑性破壞區之發展。 
5.1 數值模型

分析時採用半側之模型尺寸(土層長 0.9 m、高 0.5 m，如圖 5.1 所示)。土層材料採用
無凝聚性顆粒，以緊密排列顆粒為例，其高有 86 列，每列有 150 顆圓盤元素，總元

底部粗糙，在重力狀態下以位移控制方式(速率 0.01 m/s)，探討基礎受垂直等速加載
之承載行為。 
5.2 分析規畫 
規畫上分成二部分，一為探討顆粒幾何參數之影響，另一部分為顆粒之力學參數
之影響，詳下述： 
5.2.1 顆粒幾何參數 
根據前人研究顯示，淺基礎破壞型態可歸納成三類：全面剪力破壞、局部剪力破
壞、貫穿剪力破壞。不同的破壞型態，其承載力迥然不同，差異極大。由於破壞型態
發生與土層之緊密程度密切相關，因此本研究探討顆粒之幾何性質變化，包括顆粒粒
徑及顆粒排列兩部份，對承載力之影響。

 Life cycle design considerations of hydraulic tunnels based on 
inspection and maintenance cases

Summary 
 
This study addresses lessons learned from maintenance experiences of hydraulic tunnels in 
Taiwan. The characteristics of structural safety inspection are firstly discussed, and common 
anomalies for hydraulic tunnels are illustrated. The repairing and rehabilitation issues are then 
reviewed based on practical examples. Based on the experience learned from hydraulic tunnels 
maintenance and incorporating the account of life-cycle point views, the planning and design of a 
hydraulic tunnel need to consider in detail the long-term stability issues and necessary 
maintenance tasks during its service life, and reserve all necessary preparatory facilities, as well as 
the clearance possible required for repairing works. In construction stage, it is important to 
incorporate pre-processing measures on the potential problems likely to occur during tunnel 
operating, and also to conduct the initial inspection and to establish maintenance database as 
soon as the completion of tunnel construction. 
 
Keywords: Hydraulic tunnel, life cycle, design consideration, inspection, maintenance, lining 
anomalies 
 
1. Introduction 
 
Management and maintenance are of importance after the completion of infrastructures. Safety 
inspection is regularly utilized to timely detect extraordinary phenomenon related with structural 
stability and function of public works. Necessary repairing and reinforcement are applied while 
potential hazard is reported by the inspection task. Nowadays, the lesson learned from safety 
inspection and repairing and reinforcement are usually fed back on the planning, design and 
construction of infrastructures to reduce the possible maintenance in operational period. Of which, 
safety inspection for hydraulic tunnels are difficult since they are often characterized by a longer 
length, smaller cross section and larger gradient, and are typically designed and construction 
under the purpose of water conveyance only, which results in lacking of necessary ventilation and 
lighting system for inspection. The prime time for maintaining a hydraulic tunnel is sometime 
missed since associated extraordinary phenomenon of the tunnel are late reported by reason of 
the existing difficulty for inspection. 
 
This study investigates the inspection characteristics of hydraulic tunnels based on the seven 
inspection cases in Taiwan. Among which some tunnels are successive inspected and/or 
repeatedly repaired and reinforced. The lining anomalies are reported and classified accordingly. 
The difference between hydraulic tunnels and traffic ones for safety inspection and lining 
anomalies are discussed. The effects of repairing and reinforcement measures for hydraulic 
tunnels are reviewed. Life cycle design considerations are addressed in accordance with the 
lessons learned from these inspection and maintenance cases. 
 
2. Inspection cases and associated characteristics of inspections 
 
The term hydraulic tunnel refers to tunnels used for water conveyance, water irrigation and water 
 2.2 Characteristics of hydraulic tunnel inspections 
 
Compare with traffic tunnels that serve to provide vehicle and pedestrian passages once built, 
hydraulic tunnel built in earlier days usually focused on the passage of water flow, with less 
consideration on probable safety inspection and repair needs after their completion. In recent years, 
the concept regarding structural safety inspection and necessary maintenance for operating tunnel 
has gradually matured and results in large number of practical projects, yet the inspection and 
rehabilitation ratios on hydraulic tunnels are still apparently lower than that of traffic tunnels. One of 
the reasons lies in hydraulic tunnels’ unique inspection and rehabilitation characteristics, which are 
further examined as follows: 
 
(i) Inaccessibility and difficulties in passage 
 
The intake and outlet of a hydraulic tunnel are often equipped with water gate and/or trash rack to 
control the water discharge and prevent debris from entering. Nevertheless, such water gate and 
trash rack obscure the entry and exit of the inspection and rehabilitation personnel. Some of the 
hydraulic tunnels have the elevations of intake and outlet rather near, or even lower than the year-
round water table of the running creeks, but without incorporating any water gate, which prevent 
the entry of general personnel to conduct the inspection work. In addition, as some hydraulic 
tunnels that run rather long in length, installing water gates merely at the intake or outlet hinders 
discharging the water flow through the entire line, or requires an extended time to discharge the 
water, where the inspection personnel need to ride on a raft or a small boat to enter and exit the 
tunnel in order to gain timely efficiency. In case the tunnel has an excessive gradient, it could even 
require using safety cables for connection (Fig. 1). 
 
(ii) Lacking the lighting and ventilation equipment, rendering the inspection and rehabilitation 
difficult 
 
As hydraulic tunnels do not have lighting or ventilation equipment installed, prior to performing the 
inspection work, it often requires draining the water flow out of the tunnel by gravity or mechanical 
pumping, and chances are that the drainage cannot be done completely; and also because of 
water accumulation and leakage, a hydraulic tunnel is often very humid and full of water vapour, 
which can obscure visual inspection (Fig. 2).  Moreover, a hydraulic probably exist harmful gasses 
such as methane, or the phenomenon of underrated oxygen concentration. Thus, prior to 
conducting inspection, there is a need to dispatch personnel attired in safety equipment,  carrying 
oxygen butanes and harmful gas detectors, to first inspection and verify the safety level for the 
operating environment before the inspection work may proceed. 
 
(iii) Lacking a mileage marker, rendering documentation difficult 
 
With the landscape inside a hydraulic tunnel being rather bland, one being in it can easily lose their 
way around without any specific markings for identification purposes, or even losing orientation to 
the direction to intake or outlet of the tunnel. This can cause enormous difficulties in safety 
inspection work as it not only make the inspection process difficult, but result in less easy to 
recheck the lining anomalies. As such, prior to conducting the safety inspection work, it often 
requires measuring the mileage markers first to facilitate the operation and documentation. 
 
(iv) Debris build up and percolated crystallization on the lining surface, impurities obscuring the 
lining surface 
 
The lining surfaces of wastewater and sewage tunnel are prone to draw clay deposit formation and 
debris adhesion (Fig. 3), even more; a lining where leakage occurs tends to form carbonic acid 
calcium, i.e. efflorescence, on lining surface in case the groundwater has a higher concentration of 
calcium ions, or causes hydroxide calcium to dissolve over time from lining concrete. These can all 
obscure the anomalies on lining surface. Since efflorescence may result from the movement of free 
water within concrete, when it is concentrated in a specific area, it is probable that the lining 
concrete has begun to turn into a honeycombed-spots deterioration of the formation of cracks, 
even cannot be identified visually.

3.2 Characteristics of hydraulic tunnel rehabilitations 
 
The available time for safety inspection and rehabilitation works of a hydraulic tunnel is usually 
rather short since these works is often conducted during the annual maintenance period. It is often 
a mere days to tens of days. For some hydraulic tunnels with a greater length or a complex branch 
network, or with partial section of reversed siphoning style, it is unfeasible to complete the 
inspection and rehabilitation work in one annual maintenance period, and needs to arrange these 
works by year. The maintenance of hydraulic tunnels not only shares the characteristics of traffic 
tunnels, but also has the following ones. 
 
(i) The time for inspection and rehabilitation is rather short, which results in these works all in a 
hurried state by typically temporal measures 
 Due to limited available time, the design and working drawings for rehabilitation often cannot 
identify the detailed repairing and mending dimensions, neither the comprehensive items for quality 
control. In addition, the measures adopted for repairing and mending tasks are often of sheltered 
structure types. These factors, couple with the unique characteristics for inspection and 
rehabilitation, cause the quality control task for repairing and mending works very difficult. As a 
result, the assessment on the effect of hydraulic tunnel rehabilitation remains largely on a 
qualitative to semi-quantitative level.

(v) To reserve a clearance in the cross-section design preparing for possible rehabilitation works 
 
Besides the minimum requiring cross-section space, the modern tunnel design usually reserves a 
radius clearance of a few to tens of millimetres for construction allowance. Yet the rehabilitation 
examples in Taiwan reveal that to fulfil the requirement for strengthening the damaged tunnel a 
0.1-0.3 m thick clearance is necessary for reinforcement such as additional lining. To ease the 
limitation on the reinforcement materials and rehabilitation technology, it is suggested in life cycle 
point views that the existing clearance should be reviewed and be expanded in an appropriate 
amount, for instance, to one hundred millimetres or even more. 
 
(vi) To install mileage markers and abrasion indication markers 
 
The mileage markers provide important and convenient documentation reference in the inspection 
and rehabilitation works. To avoid the markers being flushed and damaged by the water flow, it is 
suggested to adopt an embedded type for the markers on tunnel sidewall. In addition, to effectively 
monitor the level of lining abrasion, a metal gauge or a wear-resistant indicator marker can be set 
into or imbedded at bottom or invert of a hydraulic tunnel. The shape and size regarding the gauge 
or marker can be designed according to water flow velocity, and be modified through results of 
hydraulic model tests. 
 
(vii) To conduct an initial inspection as soon as the completion of tunnel construction and establish 
a maintenance database accordingly 
 
Lining deficiencies such as concrete cold joints sometimes have already existed while the 
completion of tunnel construction. These inevitable construction defects should be mended before 
tunnel operating. To effectively identify these construction defects from the lining damages caused 
by variation of tunnel surrounding and time-dependent deterioration of lining materials, which both 
turning out in operating stage, it is very important to conducting an initial inspection as soon as the 
tunnel completion. The goal of initial inspection not only serve to provide essential information for 
mending construction defects, but also play as valuable information to establish an maintenance 
database. The data regarding tunnel investigation, design, and construction should be involved into 
the database, so as the maintenance records during tunnel operating stage.
