英文摘要 
Recent research trends draw much attention 
to organic semiconductor based thin film 
transistors (TFTs) due to dramatic performance 
improvements observed in this technological 
domain. However, parameters that governing 
transport properties and electronic stability of 
organic devices still remain unclear. Moreover, 
the correlation between supramolecular structure 
of organic materials and characteristics of 
organic transistors at the microscopic level is 
still lacking.  
This project presents a study of electronic 
transport properties and device characteristics of 
polycrystalline pentacene-based TFTs at the 
microscopic level. We will investigate the 
crystal structure and supramolecular 
organization of pentacene thin films deposited 
on different dielectric surfaces. The 
corresponding carrier transport properties and 
electronic stability of pentacene TFTs will be 
investigated to explore microscopic coupling 
effect between pentacene molecules. We expect 
to find crucial factors defining potential for good 
organic TFTs device performance. 
Thickness-dependent crystal structure, surface 
morphology, surface energy, and molecular 
structure and microstructure of a series of 
polycrystalline pentacene films with different 
film thickness ranging from several monolayers 
to the several hundred nanometers have been 
investigated using X-ray diffraction (XRD), 
atomic force microscopy (AFM), contact angle 
 對有機薄膜分子微結構以及相關的載子傳輸
特性的影響非常重要。 
本研究計劃希望能開發出高性能且穩定
的有機薄膜電晶體，除了元件製程改良與各組
件材料與界面的研發外，也希望從有機半導體
元件與固態物理出發，研究載子於有機分子半
導體的傳輸機制，找出真正限制載子移動的原
因，建立理論與模型，希望能獲得突破的進
展。研究聚焦於利用熱蒸鍍技術製備高品質的
有機分子半導體（採用五苯分子）薄膜，進行
結構解析，包含結晶結構、形態學、與微觀分
子結構，並探討其關連性，結晶結構的研究將
使用 x-ray 繞射儀，形態學使用原子力顯微
鏡，微觀分子結構使用微拉曼光譜，並搭配偏
振片研究不同方向的微觀分子結構，我們將進
行五苯分子的幾何優化，利用密度泛函理論計
算有機分子振動光譜，並比較至各種不同成長
條件的五苯薄膜，希望建立研究微觀分子結構
的方法。同時，我們也製作薄膜電晶體元件，
使用不同絕緣材料當介電層，包含無機氧化物
與有機高分子材料，藉由電晶體元件通道內控
制五苯分子結構排列，將有助於從微觀分子角
度進行載子傳輸的行為的研究。 
三、 研究方法及成果 
本計畫研究的主軸，在於探討各種不同五
苯薄膜結晶結構－晶粒大小－分子微結構的
關係，並考量這些結構因子與載子傳輸的相關
係，並製作相關的有機薄膜電晶體元件，希望
找到影響載子傳輸的關鍵因素，提供分析分子
微結構的簡單分析方法，達成製作高性能有機
薄膜電晶體元件的目標。 
本研究計劃中，我們提供一個深入分析複
晶系五苯薄膜內數種結晶相的演化，並探討五
苯薄膜的成長機制。實驗的五苯薄膜係利用真
空熱蒸鍍沉積技術製作，成長於數種基板，如
thermal grown SiO2, PECVD grown SiO2, 高分
子介電材料，其厚度從數個分子層到數百奈
米，研究在不同基板上成長的五苯薄膜的多形
態與超分子結構。首先，一個完整的厚度相關
結晶相的轉變與層狀結構的演化模型被提
出；第二，各種不同厚度的五苯薄膜的表面形
態學與表面特性被研究，我們提出一個成長模
型描述五苯分子是如何成長在各種不同基板
特性的表面，此模型也被應用去解釋五苯分子
如何成長在高分子介電層的表面；第三，我們
進一步分析結合理論計算與實驗分析五苯分
子結構與微結構於複晶薄膜內的演化；第四，
我們討論結晶結構資料與分子振動特性的關
連性在一系列不同厚度的五苯薄膜；第五，我
們建立這些薄膜結構的資訊與載子傳輸效率
的關係；最後，我們分別使用無機二氧化矽與
高分子為介電層，至作對應的有機薄膜電晶體
元件，應用上述的分析來解釋獲得電特性的差
異。我們相信研究有機薄膜分子結構與微結構
與載子傳輸性質的相關性是一個很重要的領
域，而且是有趣的。 
本研究提出的五苯薄膜的成長模型，如圖
一所示，在早期成長的階段，五苯分子被沈積
在較平坦的表面，因此五苯分子傾向垂直的站
在基板上，因此容易形成正方晶系，隨著成長
厚度的增加，晶粒與晶粒邊界逐漸形成，因此
產生陡峭的斜坡，此時五苯分子必須成長於斜
坡上，導致五苯分子站在基板的傾斜角增加，
因此，於高厚度時，容易形成三斜晶系的薄膜
相與單晶塊材相，由於此結果，晶粒尺度逐漸
成長。此模型可應用於五苯成長於無機氧化層
的表面，也可應用至高分子介電層的表面。

Studies of blue organic electroluminescent devices using the 
polymer/dopant systems as a light-emitting layer 
 
Fu-Chiao Wu, Horng-Long Cheng*, Wei-Yang Chou 
 
Institute of Electro-Optical Science and Engineering, National Cheng Kung University,  
              No.1 Ta-Hsueh Rd. Tainan City 701, Taiwan 
 
ABSTRACT 
In this study, blue organic light-emitting diodes (OLEDs) using polymer-dopant systems as a light-emitting layer were 
fabricated. The light-emitting layer was produced via a spin-coating process. The materials used in the light-emitting 
layer included: poly(9-vinylcarbazole) (PVK) as a matrix material, and blue-emission 4,4’-bis(2,2-
diphenylvinyl)biphenyl (DPVBi) as a light-emitting material. The blue OLED was fabricated by blending PVK and 
DPVBi according to the proportion of mass as specified for a light-emitting layer. Process relevant photophysical 
mechanisms and energy transfer phenomena were studied using absorption, photoluminescence (PL), photoluminescent 
excitation, and electroluminescence (EL) spectra. We have learned that there was energy transfer phenomenon between 
PVK and DPVBi. More specifically, the EL spectrum of PVK:DPVBi was significantly different from the PL spectra of 
PVK, DPVBi, and PVK:DPVBi, shifting to a longer wavelength and showing multiple boarding emission peaks 
(maximum emission at 465 and 486 nm). The electro-optical characteristics of the blue OLED and the possible origin of 
the difference between the EL and PL spectra were discussed.  
 
Keywords: Organic light emitting devices, Blue-emitting materials, Polymer/dopant system, Organic film photophysics 
 
 
1. INTRODUCTION 
 
In the last few years, studies in organic light-emitting diodes (OLEDs) have been increasing expeditiously because it is 
expected that OLED is the most promising candidate for the next generation of display technology. OLED displays 
possess the following advantages: fast response time, wide viewing angle, self-light-emitting, good contrast ratio, low 
drive voltage, broad operating temperature range, suitable for flexible substrates, large light-emitting area, thinness, 
light weight, easy and low cost fabrication, etc. 
There are three conventional techniques as shown in Fig. 1 for full color OLED displays: (1) red, green, and blue 
OLEDs are arrayed side by side to generate full color, called parallel trichromatic independent method,1-3 (2) a blue 
OLED is used as a backlight to transfer energy to red and green light-emitting materials to produce full color, called 
color conversion method,3 and (3) a white OLED is used as a backlight to generate full color via a color filter, called 
color filter method.3-5 In method (1) and (2), method (2) especially, blue OLEDs play a crucial role because the 
performance of blue OLEDs affects the qualities of full color OLED displays. Therefore, the study of correlative 
photophysical mechanisms and electro-optical characteristics of blue OLEDs is very important. 
The systems used as the light-emitting layer of blue OLEDs include polymer,6-9 small-molecule,10-13 and polymer-
dopant14,15 systems. In polymer-dopant systems, small-molecule blue-emitting materials can be blended with polymer 
matrix materials to form high quality films by solution processes, e.g. spin-coating, ink-jet printing, etc.

3. RESULTS AND DISCUSSION 
 
3.1 The optical absorption characteristics of PVK, DPVBi, and PVK:DPVBi thin films 
 
Figure 3 shows the absorption spectra of PVK, DPVBi, and PVK:DPVBi (10:1) thin films. The absorption spectra of 
PVK and DPVBi thin films are in accordance with the reported data.16-18 The absorption positions of PVK and DPVBi 
start at 350 and 425 nm, respectively. As shown in Fig. 3, the absorption spectrum of PVK:DPVBi is composed of the 
absorption spectra of PVK and DPVBi simply. The position of the shoulder in the absorption spectrum of PVK:DPVBi 
locates between 350 and 425 nm, which is the absorption position of DPVBi. This result indicates that blending PVK 
and DPVBi does not generate new ground states in PVK and DPVBi.

3.3 The PL spectrum of the PVK:DPVBi thin film and the EL spectrum of the PVK:DPVBi blue OLED 
 
Figure 6 shows the PL spectra of DPVBi and PVK:DPVBi (10:1) thin films (λexc of 330 nm) and the EL spectrum of the 
PVK:DPVBi blue OLED. There are two differences between the PL and EL spectra of PVK:DPVBi. One difference is 
that the short wavelength emission peak of the EL spectrum of PVK:DPVBi has a 12 nm red-shift in comparison with 
the emission peak of the PL spectrum of PVK:DPVBi. This 12 nm red-shift might be because the range of the PL 
mechanism is smaller, which makes the range of motion of excitons limited. Thus, excitons would lose less energy, 
resulting in a shorter wavelength emission. In the EL mechanism, the transport of carriers must be considered, making 
the range of motion of excitons extensive. Consequently, excitons would lose more energy, resulting in a longer 
wavelength emission. 
The other difference is that, as compared with the PL spectrum profile of PVK:DPVBi, the EL spectrum profile of 
PVK:DPVBi has an additional long wavelength emission peak. As shown in Fig. 6, the PL spectrum profile of DPVBi 
is not symmetrical. Therefore, we infer that the PL spectrum profile of DPVBi consists of three emission peaks. We 
used the Gaussian function fitting procedure to test our inference. Figure 7 shows the result of curve fitting carried out 
by using the Gaussian function fitting procedure to fit the selected three emission peaks to the PL spectrum profile of 
DPVBi. The simulated three emission peaks are 432, 453, and 482 nm. The simulated curve profile is similar to the PL 
spectrum profile of DPVBi. For the sake of understanding the physical meaning of the simulated three emission peaks, 
the PLE spectra of the DPVBi (λem = 432, 453, and 482 nm) thin film were measured and are shown in Fig. 8. The 
relative intensity of the PLE spectra of the three emission peaks of DPVBi are almost the same, indicating that the 
electrons in the three emission positions are from the same excited state; not from other new excited states resulting 
from interchain interaction, but transit to the different ground state bands. Hence, we suggest that DPVBi could have 
three emission positions corresponding to the 00

4. CONCLUSIONS 
 
We have successfully fabricated the blue OLED by using the polymer-dopant system of PVK:DPVBi in a 10:1 mass 
ratio as a blue-emitting layer via a spin-coating process. We found that blending PVK and DPVBi does not generate 
new ground states in PVK and DPVBi by the absorption spectra. Through the PL and PLE spectra, we have learned that 
PVK would transfer almost all the energy to DPVBi. Moreover, we found that the PL and EL spectra of PVK:DPVBi 
were significantly different. By curve fitting and the PLE spectrum, we suggest that DPVBi could have three emission 
positions corresponding to the 00
0
1 SS − , 1001 SS − , and 2001 SS −  transitions. The PVK:DPVBi blue OLED has a 
brightness of 1578 cd/m2 at a voltage of 9 V, a luminance efficiency of 1.02 cd/A with an applied current of 153 
mA/cm2, and CIE coordinates of (0.159, 0.210).
