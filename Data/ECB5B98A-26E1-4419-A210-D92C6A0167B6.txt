homogeneous alignment layers. We also studied the 
optical and electrical properties of ordered organic 
semiconductor thin films. We demonstrated a novel 
thin film composed by ordered oligofluorene which 
exhibits a polarization-sensitive amplified 
spontaneously emission characteristic (dichroic ratio 
up to 10X). It would be a potential candidate for 
active layer of laser devices. We also revealed that 
the much enhanced mobilities in ordered organic 
semiconductors to a significant degree can be 
ascribed to enhanced electronic coupling, due to more 
ordered intermolecular arrangements.

I. 分子排列技術之建立 
I.1 溫度配向技術 
為了探討有機材料有序排列後的光電物理特性，首先必須建立穩定的有序排列樣品製備
技術。於之前的研究經驗得知，具中間態的有機材料具有很高的可塑性，可藉由分子自構
裝或分子配向技術形成有序且無晶界缺陷的分子凝集[1-2]，我們認為藉由溫度的調控將
有機會對分子的排列造成影響，因此選用一具有盤狀結構中心的有機分子(由中山化學系王
志偉教授所開發，但材料結構尚未發表，故尚無法揭露於成果報告中)為研究主體，以下將
簡稱此材料為 Vora-10。以一具可填充乾燥氮氣之溫度控制平台之偏光顯微鏡做為控制與
觀測系統，探討樣品溫度得時變率對於分子排列的影響，期能藉由溫度控制來對分子進行
配向。 
由(DSC)量測可知 Vora-10的玻璃轉換溫度(Tg)為 86℃，熔點在 210℃，我們將 Vora-10
於乾燥氮氣環境中加熱至熔點，再利用毛細原理將材料製成樣品，並使用偏光顯微鏡觀
察，接著透過降溫速率的控制，探討其對分子排列的影響。結果如圖一所示。

Packaging of High-Thermal Stability and High-Humid Resistance Phosphor-
Converted   
White-Light-Emitting Diode Modules Employing Novel Ce:YAG-Doped Glass   
 
Li-Yin Chen and Wood-Hi Cheng 
Department of Photonics National Sun Yat-sen University, Kaohsiung, Taiwan 
                         
ABSTRACT  
 
Intensity degradation of high-power LEDs is a well-known issue, which has been until recently mainly 
attributed to the degradation of the chip material. With the introduction of new improved chip materials the 
problem has shifted to the LED packaging and association with reliability. However, the high-power LED 
packaging and association with reliability have been less investigated. In this study, the reliability study of 
thermal and humid tests for high-power white-light-emitting diode modules (WLEDMs) incorporating 
Ce:YAG doped glass, instead of conventional Ce:YAG doped silicone, as a phosphor layer is presented. 
Comprehensive comparison study of lumen loss, chromaticity shift, and transmittance loss of high-power 
WLEDWs with Ce:YAG doped glass and Ce:YAG doped silicone under thermal aging, thermal shock, and 
damp heat tests was performed. The thermal aging results showed that the WLEDs with the doped glass at 4 
wt% concentration exhibited two times less lumen loss, chromaticity shift, and transmittance loss than with 
the doped silicone. The damp heat results showed that the WLEDWs with doped glass at 2~8 wt% doping 
concentrations exhibited 67~69%, 49~65%, and 35~67% better improvements than doped silicone for the 
lumen loss, chromaticity shift, and transmittance loss, respectively. However, the performance of the 
WLEDMs did not significantly show difference between doped glass and silicone under thermal shock tests. 
The glass as Ce:YAG doped layer is used for WLEDMs due to the glass having five times higher glass 
transition temperature (Tg) of 750oC compared to conventional silicone of 150 oC that can exhibit high 
thermal stability and humidity resistance. The new technology of glass as a phosphor host is essentially 
critical to the application of high-power white-light-emitting diode modules in the area where absolute 
reliability is required and where silicone simply could not stand the thermal, humidity, or other deteriorating 
factors due to its low thermal stability of only 250 oC.
