中文摘要 
    磁冷凍方法是一種以磁性材料為工質
的冷凍技術，其基本原理是借助磁性材料的
可逆磁熱效應（Magneto-caloric Effect，
MCE），即磁性工質被等溫磁化時會對外界
放出熱量，而在絕熱退磁時會對外界吸收熱
量，以達到冷凍之目的。本計劃是以發展一
高效率、低成本的室溫區磁冷凍技術為目
標，研究內容包含磁冷凍循環系統的設計規
劃、強磁場永磁磁路之設計、磁熱效應量測
分析，並探討磁工質在不同幾何尺寸、環境
溫度和磁場強度的狀態下的磁熱效應。 
 
關鍵字:磁致冷、磁熱效應、磁工質 
Abstract 
 
Room temperature magnetic refrigeration is a 
new refrigeration method with great 
application potential. It has advantages such 
as environment-safe and high efficiency based 
on the phenomena of magneto-caloric effect. 
Although mature in low temperature field, 
magnetic refrigeration is still in the state of 
exploration in general. Therefore, the purpose 
of this project is to develop a magnetic 
refrigeration system of room temperature, 
which has the low cost need, and the high 
efficiency. The research contents include the 
design of high field strength permanent 
magnetic circuit, geometry design of the 
magnetic refrigeration medium. The magneto- 
caloric effect of magnetic refrigerant material 
is discussed under different size, the field 
strength of magnetic circuit and the state of 
Environment temperature.  
Keywords：magnetic refrigeration, magneto- 
caloric effect, magnetic refrigerant medium

Uniform surface polished method of complex holes in abrasive flow 
machining 
 
Abstract 
Abrasive flow machining (AFM) is an effective method that can remove the 
recasting layer produced by wire electrical discharge machining (WEDM). However, 
the surface roughness will not easily uniform when a complex hole is polished by this 
method. So a CFD method is aided to design the passageways to find the smooth 
roughness on the complex hole in AFM. For finding the uniform roughness in entire 
surface, shear force acting on the chain hole or flow property of the medium in AFM 
must be gotten. In this research, the effect of shear rates on medium viscosities can 
utilize to set up a power law model first. Then the velocity, the strain rate and the shear 
force of the medium acting on the surface will be obtained in constant pressure by CFD 
software. Finally, optimal mold core put into the complex hole will be designed during 
these results. The results show that the shear forces and strain rates change a lot on 
complete surface if no mold core is inserted into the complex hole. However, the shear 
forces or strain rates are only little difference at whole surface when the core shape is 
similar to the complex hole. From the experiments, the results also show that, the

2.1 Power law 
Since the abrasive geometries are not uniform, the flow path (or motion) of each 
abrasive in the polymer gel cannot predict exactly in AFM. Therefore, it is difficult to 
describe AFM abrasive mechanism by the micro viewpoint. Polymer gel is always used 
as the base material of the abrasive medium in AFM. Since the gel is semi-solid, it can 
easily deform and mix uniformly with all kinds of abrasive. It means the gel must have 
high viscosity and large deformation ability to produce good polished effect in AFM. 
Hence, the behavior of the abrasive medium in AFM can look like as a non-Newtonian 
flow in the macro viewpoint. It becomes simple and effective to describe the abrasive 
mechanism in AFM when such flow is used as the working model. Due to the medium 
viscosity is drastically reduced when the temperature has a small increase in AFM [12]. 
Therefore, the relationship between the viscosity and the temperature must be set up in 
this model. Power law of the non-Newtonian flow shows the relation of the viscosity, 
the shear rate and the temperature in CFD-ACE+. Thus, this equation is appropriate to 
state the motion of the abrasive medium in AFM. Equation of the power law is

3. Results and discussions  
The main purpose of this research is to find the appropriate passageway, which can 
make uniform roughness in the complex surface. Hence, a numerical method is aided to 
simulate the motion of the abrasive medium in the chain hole, to find the relationships 
between the flow properties of the abrasive medium and the polished roughness in AFM. 
In this case, velocities, strain rates and shear forces of the abrasive medium will be 
found during the simulated results. Moreover, find the roughness in different position on 
the chain hole when mold core or no mold core is put into the hole in the experiments. 
Working area of the abrasive medium in AFM and mesh diagram using to simulate the 
motion of the abrasive medium is shown in Fig. 4. In Fig. 4 (b), up and down cylinders 
are the medium barrels, passageways and workpiece are set in the middle section.
