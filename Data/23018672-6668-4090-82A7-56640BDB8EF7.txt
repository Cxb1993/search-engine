Abstract 
    This project is to develop the PV system with the intelligent glass curtain for next generation. 
By using the solar cell combined with the glass curtain, electricity can be generated and 
maximum power tracking can be done spontaneously by multiple modules. For avoiding the 
shading effect, system is parallel connected. Because of the parallel structure, it must need a high 
transformed ratio circuit. The output power can be injected to the DC bus. And preventing not to 
affecting outer look, the control circuit can be integrated monolithically for reducing volume. 
Moreover, the converter can be embedded into the alumni frame which well designed by thermal 
current and thermal electricity analysis for improving the thermal dissipation problem and 
increasing the circuit efficiency and lifetime. Finally, the electricity energy can be parallel 
connected with utility and the analysis as well as evaluation of improvement effect of electricity 
generated system and energy saving of architecture will be done. The project is cooperated by 
multiple fields and it can be categorized by four subprojects: 
 
(1)In the first subproject, a photovoltaic system with high efficiency converter will be done. 
The maximum power point trackers are needed for making sure that every solar generating 
glass window can generate the maximum power. 
 
(2)In the second subproject, the purpose is to improve the heat dissipation problem of the 
solar PV glass curtain by applying both the technique of thermal-flow integral design and 
numerical analysis method. 
 
(3) In the third subproject, we are proposing to develop the IC Design technique of MPPT 
(Maximum Power Point Tracking) for advanced grid connected photovoltaic inverters on 
intelligent glass curtain.  
 
(4) In the fourth subproject, the purpose is to establish solar radiation data base for solar 
energy simulation of buildings in Taiwan. 
 
Keywords: PV system, MPPT, Multiple converters parallel connected, Cooling system, Glass 
curtain, thermal-flow integral design, numerical analysis, IC design, BIPV, Solar radiation
