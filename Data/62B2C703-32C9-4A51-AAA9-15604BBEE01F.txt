具有殘值之租賃設備在生命週期內的維修策略 
與租期長度對利潤影響之研究(I) 
Effects of Maintenance Policy of Leased Equipment with Residual Value and 
Length of Lease Period for Profit within Lifecycle 
計畫編號：NSC 99-2221-E-469-001 
執行期間：99 年 08 月 01 日至 100 年 07 月 31 日 
主持人：張文亮 (chang@ctcn.edu.tw)   
共同主持人：  
計畫參與人員：李文庭、陳柏志 
 
一、中文摘要 
本計畫主要探討具殘值的可維修租賃設備在
預防保養之年齡回溯法下的最佳維修策略與租期
長度。對於租賃設備的維修保養服務、設備租金與
租期長度為出租者與承租者簽訂租賃契約條款時
的重要項目。一般而言，設備出租者與承租者在簽
訂租賃契約時會明訂出租者須負擔維修責任且若
設備故障之維修時間超過契約內規定的時間值
時，出租者須額外付給承租者懲罰性賠償。因此，
出租者對設備實施適當的預防保養可有效的降低
設備在租賃期間內的故障次數，進而減少支出成
本。另一方面，新租賃設備有較長的租期長度時，
可以穩定出租者的收入，但租賃設備故障頻率會隨
使用時間的增加而遞增，且設備殘值亦隨之遞減。
因此，出租者在設計新設備的租賃契約時，應將設
備之租期長度、保養措施與殘值一同考量。有鑑於
此，本論文以出租者立場針對可維修的租賃設備，
考慮租期內設備故障時採小修方式處理，而當設備
年齡到達一門檻值時，則出租者為設備實施預防保
養。在此前提下，本計畫衡量設備出租期間的維修
成本、維修逾時的懲罰成本(賠償金)、預防保養成
本、設備殘值與設備租金收益，以建構租期內設備
之總利潤數學模式。依據建構之利潤模式尋求最佳
維修策略與租期長度，並以數值分析結果說明最佳
維修策略與租期長度對利潤模式之影響。 
關鍵詞：預防保養、門檻值、租期長度、設備殘值。 
Abstract 
In this project, the optimal length of lease period 
and maintenance policy for leased equipment is 
investigated. In order to have steady revenue, the 
lessor (owner) of the equipment may provide a 
discount to encourage the lessee to sign a contract 
with a longer lease period. However, as the lease 
period increases, the maintenance cost also increases 
due to the deterioration or usage. Therefore, there is a 
need to derive an optimal length of lease period and a 
corresponding maintenance policy for the leased 
equipment from the viewpoint of the lessor such that 
the expected total profit is maximized. In this project, 
the following maintenance scheme is considered. 
When the leased equipment fails, a minimal repair is 
conducted to bring the equipment back to an 
operating condition. Furthermore, when the age of 
the equipment reaches a specified level (called as 
control-limit), an imperfect preventive maintenance 
(PM) is carried out to avoid possible failures. Under 
this maintenance scheme, the mathematical model of 
the expected total profit is developed and the optimal 
length of lease period and the corresponding optimal 
maintenance policy are derived. Finally, numerical 
examples are given to illustrate the effects of the 
number of lease periods and the maintenance policy 
on the expected total profit. 
Keyword: Preventive Maintenance, Preventive 
Replacement, Number of Lease Periods, 
Minimal Repair, Threshold Value

四、結論與討論 
    本研究是以出租者的角度，針對具有殘值之租
賃設備，將預防保養考慮於租賃契約中並建構租期
內設備之總利潤數學模式，進而尋求最佳維修策略
與租期長度的相關性質，以利出租者在設立租賃契
約時參考。在研究過程中，發現租賃期長度的決策
受租金、殘值與維修相關成本等三者的相關性影
響，此結果顯示在制訂設備之租賃合約時，租賃期
長度和維修策略應同時考量。而透過數值範例的分
析，也發現保養變動成本愈高或租賃設備之失效率
函數遞增程度愈高時，租賃契約中加入預防保養措
施行動會為出租者帶來較高的利潤。 
五、計畫成果自評 
    本研究完成第一年具有殘值之租賃設備的單
階段維修策略與租期長度對利潤模式之影響。對租
賃設備所建構之利潤模式，推導出設備具殘值的最
佳維修策略與租期數的性質，透過數值分析結果，
獲致相關於租賃契約內容決策制訂之重要性質，佐
證維修策略與租期數對利潤模式之影響。

1. The impact of used products on channel 
coordination and quality decision 
Ruey Huei Yeh (National Taiwan University of 
Science and Technology); Hui-Chiung Lo 
(Tamkang University); Wen Liang Chang (Cardinal 
Tien College of Healthcare & Management); Jung-
Jung Lin (Tamkang University) 
Abstract: In the supply chain management, 
channel coordination and greening environment are 
two important issues. Better channel coordination 
can result in higher performance of the supply 
chain and increase the overall profits. Integrated 
channels and decentralized channels are two 
extreme cases of channel coordination and the 
difference in decision-making between these two 
cases is investigated. For the greening environment 
issue, used products are gradually considered to 
reduce the purchasing cost or to conform to the 
concept of environmental protection. As a result, 
the demand of new products may be affected by the 
emergence of the used products. Therefore, profit 
models for the integrated channels and the 
decentralized channels of the new products are 
established to investigate the effect of the used 
products on the pricing and quality decisions in this 
paper. 
 
2. The value of information sharing on the 
bullwhip effect with risk averse consideration 
Xiutian Shi (Nanjing University); Qing Ding 
(Singapore Management University); Houcai Shen 
(Nanjing University) 
Abstract: We study the bullwhip effect with risk-
averse consideration in this paper. The literature, 
given assumption that the decision makers are risk-
neutral, on information sharing and bullwhip effect 
in the business press is proliferation, while modern 
supply chains are becoming increasingly complex 
and sensitive to uncertainties. In the field of 
finance, a significant operational approach, mean-
variance analysis, is widely used to deal with risk 
aversion. Based on these research, we start with 
developing a model of two-stage supply chain 
consisting two risk averse members, one retailer 
and one manufacturer. This problem is similar to 
the risk-averse agent's optimal inventory problem. 
Then, we prove the risk averse decision maker's 
utility function, which combines the expected and 
variance of profit in the coming period, is unimodal 
in the order-up-to level. With that, we extend the 
proposition by considering two cases differentiated 
by information sharing. Moreover, our analysis and 
numerical examples provide further illustration of 
our work. 
 
3. Optimal policy for an inventory system under 
convex variable ordering cost 
Miao Song (The University of Hong Kong); Ye Lu 
(City University of Hong Kong) 
Abstract: We study the optimal policy for a 
periodic-review inventory system where the variable 
ordering cost is defined by a piece-wise linear 
convex function. The optimal policy is fully 
characterized for the case without the fixed ordering 
cost. For the system with a fixed ordering cost, we 
fully characterize the optimal policy of the model by 
introducing the notion of strong K,c,q-convexity. 
We also identify some sufficient conditions under 
which the (s,S) policy is optimal. 
 
4. Why Pull system is so difficult to be accepted 
in a make-to-stock production plant --an 
experimental study 
Chia-Ling Huang (National Chiao Tung University); 
Kuo-Jung Yuan (Oriental Institute of Technology); 
Yunn-Jin Huwang (National Chiao Tung 
University); Rong Kwei Li (National Chiao Tung 
University) 
Abstract: Two obstacles to pull systems 
implementation: One is lack of understanding a pull 
system. The other is lack of confidence in the ability 
of it to handle demand variability. This research 
designs a MTS game to help managers to determine: 
(1) whether they were using push or pull system; (2) 
whether the pull is better than the push system. 
Results support most participating managers still 
using push system and pull system is better. We 
expect the findings to raise the willingness of MTS 
companies to re-examine whether both obstacles 
exist in their companies, should they determine such 
a move suitable.

Optimal controlled-limit and preventive maintenance policy within 
life cycle of products 
 
Wen Liang Chang 
 
Department of Information Management, Cardinal Tien College of Healthcare & Management, 
Taipei, Taiwan 
 
Abstract 
This paper investigates controlled-limit and preventive maintenance (PM) policy. When the 
product fails, the failed product is rectified using minimal repair. In order to reduce the number of 
product failures, the seller performs imperfect PM actions when the age of the product reaches a 
controlled-limit. After the base warranty period expires, the seller gives a discount of purchasing 
extended warranty (EW) expense if the consumer purchases the EW for products. Under this 
maintenance scheme, the profit model is constructed and then the optimal policy is obtained. 
Finally, numerical examples are given to illustrate the influences of the optimal policy for profit 
model. 
 
Keyword: Preventive maintenance; Life cycle; Extended warranty; Controlled-limit; Minimal 
repair; Discount
