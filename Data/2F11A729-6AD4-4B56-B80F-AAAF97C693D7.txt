ABSTRACT 
Chatter is a self-excited vibration during machining that causes violent vibrations between 
tools and workpieces. It causes remarkable waviness and inacceptable roughness on the 
workpiece surfaces. The occurrence of chatter strongly depends on the dynamic 
characteristics of the cutting tool and the workpiece. In this study, an actively controlled tool 
holder is designed by using a piezoelectric actuator. A controller is designed to change the 
dynamic characteristics of the cutting process for chatter suppression. Chatter models for 
piezo-actuated cutting tools in turning operations are developed. Stability charts of chatter are 
calculated via simulations. The effectiveness of the piezo-actuated tool holder for chatter 
suppression is investigated experimentally. The experimental results of turning slender 
workpieces are compared under the same cutting conditions for traditional tool holder, 
uncontrolled and controlled piezo-actuated tool holders. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Keywords: piezoelectric, piezoelectric actuator, turning, cutting, chatter, control
