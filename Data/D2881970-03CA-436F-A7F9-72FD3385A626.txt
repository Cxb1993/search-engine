的 PBS放入試片，為期 24小時的浸泡，完成
浸泡的程序。並利用感應偶合電容放射光譜儀
測得銅粒子溶解量。
3.3試驗菌液製備及抗菌流程
將矽晶圓試片裁切剪成1cm×2cm大小浸
泡於PBS後24小時候進行抗菌實驗。試驗菌液
之製備，將大腸桿菌(E.Coli)菌株與金黃色葡
萄球菌(S.aureus)放入液態培養基內，液態培養
基置於培養箱恆溫37°C培養至隔夜，在以新的
液態培養基繼續培養3小時，利用離心方式將
上清液去除，將離心完所剩細菌加入PBS(緩衝
溶液)調製成含有108 cfu/ml之試驗菌液。抗菌
流程將試片放入含有108 cfu/ml之試驗菌液，置
於37°C恆溫培養箱內其轉速為210rpm，於固定
時間(1小時、3小時、6小時、24小時)進行取樣，
每次取樣取出約0.5ml之菌液，加入4.5ml PBS
經序列稀釋後取出1000µl菌液，塗佈於固態培
養基上，置於恆溫37°C培養箱培養至隔夜並計
算菌落數目。

五、結論
1.經由 ICP與抗菌結果得知銅與銀離子在相同
的濃度下，銅離子對於金黃色葡萄球菌有傑出
的抗菌特性。反之，銀離子對於大腸桿菌有良
好的抗菌性質。
2.銅離子濃度愈高則在短時間內對於大腸桿
菌與金黃色葡萄球菌有顯著的抗菌性質。
3.藉由 DSC與 FE-SEM結果證實在 TaN薄膜
內同時添加銅與銀時，可以在低溫退火條件下
使得銅與銀析出於薄膜表面並且團聚成奈米
顆粒。然而顆粒尺寸將隨著退火溫度的增加而
增加。
4.TaN-(Cu,Ag)薄膜之硬度將會隨著退火溫度
的上升而增加。此外，摩擦係數與磨耗損失率
將著退火溫度的上升而下降。
5.由 XRD與 TEM結果得知，TaN-(Cu,Ag)薄
膜在退火後可以發現到單獨的銅與銀之結晶
相存在，並且證實退火可以使得銅與銀分離成
單獨的兩相。
6.TaN-(Cu,Ag)薄膜在低溫退火(250℃)時，對
於大腸桿菌與金黃色葡萄球菌有 99%以上之
抗菌效率，並且隨著退火溫度愈高，則抗菌效
果愈有顯著的效果。
7.TaN-(Cu,Ag)薄膜經過退火後，對於大腸桿菌
與金黃色葡萄球菌皆有協同的抗菌效果。

Keywords: co-sputtering, TaN–Ag, TaN-Cu, nanocomposite thin films, Anti-bacteria,
Abstract. TaN–Cu and TaN-Ag nanocomposite films were deposited by reactive
co-sputtering on Si. The films were then annealed using RTA (Rapid Thermal
Annealing) at 400C for 2, 4, 8, 15 minutes respectively to induce the nucleation and
growth of metal particles in TaN matrix and on film surface. The samples were then
tested for their anti-bacterial behaviors against Gram-negative and Gram-positive
bacteria. The results were compared with the solubility of Ag and Cu in a buffer
solution. Under similar ion concentrations, it is concluded that Ag ion or TaN-Ag is
more effective against E. Coli (Gram-negative), while Cu ion or TaN-Cu is more
effective against Staphylococcus aureus (Gram-positive).
Introduction
Bacteria exist in every corner of the nature world. They belong to the group of prokaryotic
micro-organisms which do not contain a nucleus as distinguished from the eukaryotes. Their DNA
molecules do not have a nuclear membrane to separate them from the cytoprism. Their sizes range
from 0.5 to 5 m. Based on the different staining effects on cell wall components by the Gram stain,
bacteria can be divided into Gram-positive and Gram-negative organisms. Staphylococcus aureus (S.
aureus) is a representative of Gram-positive bacterium, which has a classical cell wall (peptidoglycan
polymer) of 20-80 nm in thickness. Escherichia coli (E. coli) is a typical Gram-negative bacterium,
which has a cell wall of 6-18 nm. Peptidoglycan layer is only 2-6 nm in thickness.
It is widely known that materials containing silver or copper may have promising antibacterial
performance against some bacteria [1-3]. When Ag or Cu atoms transform into ions and enter the
environment, the multiplication of bacteria may be stopped, probably due to their engagement with
bacteria’s DNA structure [4]. More importantly, these positively charged ions may interact with cell
wall membrane (slightly negative), leading to the breakage of cell wall [4]. The term “anti-bacterial
performance”in this study is then defined as the effectiveness of TaN-metal films to stop the
multiplication of the bacteria and to kill the bacteria. However, the detailed mechanisms are still not
clear yet.
Recently, it was reported that the mechanical properties of Ag- or Cu- doped TaN films could be
improved after rapid thermal annealing (RTA), due to the formation of metal nanoparticles in the
matrix [5]. Ag and Cu, when used as dopants, are proved immiscible with TaN, which makes the
synthesis of TaN -Ag or -Cu nanocomposite thin films possible [6]. In the previous study [7], it was
also shown that Ag and Cu particles could form on the surface of TaN, which consequently made these
TaN-metal nanocomposite promising antibacterial films.
TaN was chosen as the matrix in this study because that Ta metal and alloys are known to have
excellent bio-compatibility, which makes TaN an excellent protective coatings in bio-related
applications [8]. Besides providing mechanical protection, one of the proposed advantages of using
these TaN-metal nanocomposite films over simple Ag or Cu films is that the release rate of Ag and Cu
ions is dependent on the size of surfaced particles [7].
In this study, TaN -Ag and -Cu nanocomposite thin films were prepared by a hybrid process
combining reactive co-sputtering and rapid thermal annealing (RTA). After the RTA processing, metal
nano-particles would emerge in TaN matrix [5] and on film surface. The objective of this work is: (1)
to study the dissolution rate of Ag and Cu ions, as function of annealing and original contents, and (2)
to compare the anti-bacterial efficiency of TaN-Ag and TaN-Cu against E. coli and S. aureus.

In general, the particle size should increase with the increase of annealing time
and original metal contents. According to the variation of particle size and total
surfaced atoms, the dissolved metal ion concentration is supposed to be different, and
hence the anti-bacterial behaviors. The existence of Cu and Ag phases was also
confirmed in XRD study. Without annealing, no metal phase can be detected, which
implies that the as-deposited metal atoms are trapped in the TaN structure.
Immersion tests
Table 2 presents the concentration of Cu and Ag ions dissolved in the PBS buffer solution, as a
function of time. Apparently, the amount of dissolved metal ions can be controlled by the annealing
conditions and deposited (original) metal contents, which would directly control the particle size and
the total surfaced atomic amount of Ag and Cu. Generally speaking, the total surfaced amount of metal
and the particle size should increase with the increase of annealing time and original metal contents.

Antibacterial tests
Figure 4 shows the antibacterial efficiency of Samples TaN-Cu(2.2)-AN15 (#4),
TaN-Ag(16)-AN2 (#9), and TaN-Cu(11)-AN15 (#8) vs. time, against E. coli. Figure 5 shows the
pictures of the incubators after antibacterial testing against E. coli. TaN-Cu(11)-AN15 produced the
highest Cu concentration and hence the highest antibacterial efficiency, which is used as a reference in
the study. Figure 6 shows the same samples against S. aureus. It can be seen from Figure 3 that
TaN-Cu(2.2)-AN15 and TaN-Ag(16)-AN2 (#9) produced similar metal ion concentration. It is now
obvious that the Cu particles formed on TaN-Cu dissolve faster than Ag particles do. By comparing the
anti-bacterial behaviors of TaN-Cu(2.2)-AN15 and TaN-Ag(16)-AN2 in Figures 4 and 6, it can be
observed that Ag ion is more efficient against E. coli (Gram-negative), while Cu ion is more efficient in
killing S. aureus (Gram-positive). The reason for this is not clear at the present time, and definitely
need more study. From the performance of TaN-Cu(11)-AN15, it is found that it is possible to produce
a sample with quick and lasting antibacterial behavior. According to the previous study, this sample
should be wear resistant as well [11]. Figure 6 also shows that after 24 hrs testing in the solution, the
efficiency of the samples may drop comparing with that of the samples been tested after 6 hrs. This
implies that S. Aureus may rejuvenate quickly once the metal ions lose the activity.
