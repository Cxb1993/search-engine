摘要 
本計畫提出數種光學架構來達成數位全像之影像/資訊保密，其中主要選用
聯合轉換相關器與振幅/相位式數位全像光學加密方式來達成雙隨機相位編碼作
用以使得數位資料得以光學方法實現加密與解密。同時，本計畫也提出結合數位
全像與液晶空間光調制器技術以達到數位全像三維影像顯示之目的，未來可望結
合數位浮水印方法以達到數位影像之鑑別與保密。在本計畫中，提出以利用聯合
相關轉換器架構之光學加/解密系統，使其以複數編碼之方式設計出金鑰遮罩，
而複數編碼的金鑰遮罩則可藉由兩個耦合液晶空間光調制器使其分別操作於振
幅與相位模式來加以實現。在實際光學系統中，本計畫發展出一種修正型反覆傅
氏轉換演算法以設計出可顯示於液晶複數調制下最理想的複數金鑰遮罩。在系統
設計方面，本計畫內探討了各種器件限制條件，並分析了可能解決之道。 在光
學實現方面，考慮了聯合相關轉換器架構下的金鑰遮罩寬度上的限制，並以液晶
空間光調制器實現了線上操作的聯合相關轉換資訊保密系統，亦探討其雙隨機相
位編碼與其資訊保密特性。 另一方面， 本計畫研究了振幅/相位式數位全像保
密系統，探討隨機相位編碼的特性及其數位全像的加密方式，並進一步地利用雙
隨機相位編碼於全相位式數位全像加密方式、理論架構及其相關特性。 在理論
模擬方面，計畫內驗證了此種新穎的加密方式的可行性，並且分析輸入影像的相
位編碼方式及隨機相位編碼與加/解密特性。接著，我們探討了液晶空間光調制
器實現隨機相位編碼的可行性，並透過調制範圍的選定以獲得類似於隨機相位編
碼的調制特性。在光學實驗方面，本計畫內架設了全相位式數位全像加密實驗系
統以進行相關的二位元影像加密實驗，利用液晶空間光調制器實現線上可程控的
全相位式數位全像加密方式，並透過輸入隨機相位編碼圖形的不同，可以改變隨
機相位編碼的特性，以獲得較佳的實驗結果。上述研究工作的理論、電腦模擬、
實驗架構與系統及相關結果將於本計畫報告中加以詳述。

第一章 緒論 
1.1 數位全像資訊保密技術的發展與現況 
數位全像加密是近幾年所發展出來的一種光學加密技術，藉由數位全像術擁有
完全記錄物體資訊的特性，搭配隨機相位編碼對於振幅或相位的資訊進行加密的動
作。數位全像加密所運用到隨機相位編碼的概念是 Philippe Refregier 和 Bahram Javidi
在 1995 年於 Optics Letters 中所提出[1]，其概念為利用隨機相位在輸入及傅氏平面對
於輸入振幅影像進行編碼的動作，其加密後的影像可以達到單一白雜訊特性。雖然
此概念能夠達到影像的保密，但是在於金鑰的取得上有一定的困難，使得此概念無
法真正實際的應用。 
為了實現上述的概念 Takanori Nomura 和Bahram Javidi 在 2000 年於 Optics Letter
將隨機相位編碼的加密方式搭配數位全像術[2]，以獲得加密及金鑰資訊，其可以完
整記錄了加密影像及傅氏平面的金鑰資訊，最後經由數值計算即可還原原始影像，
驗證了雙隨機相位編碼加密的可行性，並且引入了數位全像術做為記錄的媒介，拓
展數位全像的應用性。 
有了這個概念後，Enrique Tajahuerce, Osamu Matoba, Steven C. Verrall, and Bahram 
Javidi 在隔幾個月後於 Applied Optics 提出利用相移式干涉儀來進行影像的加密[3]，
該系統在物光及參考光臂分別擺上隨機相位編碼，透過兩道光干涉來達到加密的效
果，而且金鑰的獲得及解密的過程也相當的容易，成功的將雙隨機相位編碼與數位
全像術的特性加以融合應用。 
隨機相位編碼的加密方式除了能夠對於二維影像的輸入影像加密之外，也被應
用於三維的輸入物體加密[4, 5]。此外 G. Unnikrishnan, J. Joseph, and K. Singh 於 2000
年在 Optics Letters 提出利用部份傅氏轉換結合隨機相位編碼的對於資訊進行加密
[6]，使得傅氏轉換的參數也成為加密步驟的一環，而在  2004 年 Naveen Kumar 
Nishchal, Joby Joseph, Kehar Singhru 在 Optics Communications 將此方法結合數位全像
術[7]，使得記錄影像資訊更為快速、簡便。 
Guohai Situ and Jingjuan Zhang 將隨機相位編碼放置於 Fresnel 平面進行加密
[8]，有別於以往放置於傅氏平面，提供了不同平面的加密方式。而 Sang Keun Gil, Seok 
Hee Jeon, Nam Kim, Jong Rae Jeong 在 2006 年[9]及 2007 年[10]利用空間光調器來產生
二位元的金鑰，對於輸入資訊進行資訊加密，提供了不同的加密金鑰。此外，Li-Chien 
Lin, Chau-Jern Cheng, Chiung-Liang Chen 在 2006 年於 Optics Communications[11] 提
出 JTC 加密方式中，如何設計理想金鑰的方法，並在 2008 年於 Optical Engineering[12]
將此設計方式，運用在複數編碼金鑰的加密方式，並且實現其光學加解密實驗。 
此外有另一派的學者從密碼學的角度出發去探討隨機相位編碼的加密方式，對於攻
擊者來說被破密的可以性或實際進行破密的方式作了一些介紹，其中有自選明文攻擊法
(chosen-plaintext attack)[13]、已知明文攻擊法(known-plaintext attack)[14]和已知明文嘗試
錯誤攻擊法(known-plaintext heuristic attack)[15]…等等，都說明了振幅式隨機相位編碼
被破密的可行性及實行方式。以上提到的文獻輸入振幅影像被加密後都為強度的表示
方式，而任何的強度偵測器都能擷取振幅的影像，因此有人提出將輸入振幅影像轉

1.2 研究動機及背景 
數位化時代的來臨，資訊保密可以說是目前生活中重要的一環，舉例來說：在護照、
通行證、身分證、信用卡…等等，上方皆記載著個人私密的資料，如何防止這些資料的
遭竊取或窺視，將成為一個重要的課題，因此本文提出一套影像加密技術。數位全像
加密結合了光學平行處理及數位化的特性，能夠快速對於輸入影像加密，並且獲得
其解密金鑰，傳輸到使用者手中。然而此一技術依然存在值得探討的地方。舉例來
說：更高的安全性、資料的傳輸及失真、實際應用…等等，在此本文主要針對於隨
機相位編碼加密有何改善空間及如何利用液晶空間光調制器(spatial light modulator, 
SLM)來實現隨機相位編碼作進一步的研究。 
針對上述的研究方向將有可能會遇到下列的挑戰： 
1. 雙隨機相位編碼加密方式 
數位全像加密運用兩個隨機相位編碼來進行，使其加密影像能達到單一白雜訊
的特性，然而加密的方式都是將輸入振幅影像轉換成強度的形式，容易被光偵測器
所偵測，因此有學者提出全相位的加密方式，提高資料的安全性，但是此加密方式
只使用一把金鑰來加密，並非完全利用雙隨機相位編碼方式，因此本文以此為出發
點試著發展出全相位式雙隨機相位編碼的加密方式，讓資訊的安全性提升，然而另
一把金鑰的增加方式或解密的運算程序都是需要不斷研究的地方。 
2. 液晶空間光調制器加密 
數位全像加密的隨機相位編碼在實驗中，都運用毛玻璃來或其他元件來實現，
但是其編碼的特性都不能隨意變動，因此本文以此為出發點嘗試利用液晶空間光調
制器來實現，但是一般的液晶空間光調器並非為純相位的調制，所以如何透過控制
輸入到液晶空間光調器的影像，使其入射光通過後的特性能夠趨近於理想的隨機相
位編碼，將是需要不斷研究的重點。

2.1.3 電荷耦合裝置 
數位全像術與全像術的不同就在於記錄的介質，因此在本小節將深入介紹其發
展緣由及在數位全像使用上的限制。 
電荷耦合裝置是於 1969 年由美國貝爾實驗室(Bell Labs)的維拉·波義耳(Willard 
Boyle)和喬治·史密斯(George E. Smith)所發明的。一種類似積體電路的半導體電子元
件。主要的功能可以把光轉換成電，而電的信號經過各式的電路就可以成為數位信
號,配合現今的電腦加以處理。目前常用於監視器、攝影機、傳真機、掃瞄器…等等。
以下幾個重要的參數是用於數位全像時必須要了解的： 
1. 像素大小(pixel size)、晶片大小(chip size)及每像素對應的位元數(bits per 
pixel):像素大小影響重建影像的解析度，現今的產品大約最小為 2μm2，而晶片大
小影響偵測的範圍及重建影像的品質，在此舉出兩種不同規格的 CCD 實際拍攝
的結果，一個為 Teli CCD，像素值為 640×480，像素大小為 7.4μm×7.4μm，，晶
片大小 4.88mm×3.66mm，另一個為 SONY α100，像素值為 3872×2592，像素大小
為 6.095μm×6.095μm，晶片大小 23.6 mm×15.8mm，兩者的像素位元數都設定為
8bit。圖 2.6 為不同 CCD 的重建之影像左圖大小為 640×480 右圖為 2500×2500，
距離分為 140mm 及 185mm，重建方法都為 Fresnel 轉換法，實際骰子大小約為

第三章 數位全像保密系統 
3.1  聯合轉換相關器資訊保密 
Various image security techniques using optical information processing have been 
proposed and demonstrated [27-33]. Refregier and Javidi [1] proposed a double random 
phase optical encryption system with two independent random phase masks on both the input 
plane and the Fourier plane. For a real-time optical processing system, one must use a spatial 
light modulator (SLM) to encode the complex encrypted image under the VanderLugt 
correlator scheme. However, the phase and amplitude at each pixel of the SLM cannot be 
controlled independently and simultaneously. To address this problem, Javidi et al. [27] used 
only the partially binarized phase for the reconstruction of the encrypted image. Optical 
encryption using joint transform correlation (JTC) architecture was proposed by Nomura and 
Javidi [28] to alleviate the alignment problem. Nomura et al. proposed the use of a simulated 
annealing algorithm for designing an optimal real-valued key mask [29], and the use of a 
iterative Fourier transform algorithm (IFTA) for designing a phase key mask [30] in the joint 
transform encryption scheme. In their approaches, the real-valued key mask and the phase 
mask are directly encoded into a SLM with amplitude mode and phase mode, respectively. 
However, to perfectly reconstruct the encrypted image from a JTC encryption system, a 
complex key mask is required in the input plane. This will result in the Fourier transform of 
the key mask to be a phase-only function in the Fourier plane to pass the spectrum energy of 
the input image [28, 31]. Overcoming this problem is difficult in the current practical system 
since a commercially available SLM [34] can only modulate either the phase or the amplitude 
distribution of the wavefront individually. Therefore, in this paper, we propose and 
demonstrate a modified iterative Fourier transform algorithm (IFTA) with mapping 
procedures for designing a complex key mask in the input plane, and a cascaded twisted 
nematic liquid crystal spatial light modulators (TNLC-SLMs) module as a complex-encoding 
device to implement the optical JTC encryption system. To the best of our knowledge, this is 
the first research endeavor using a complex-encoding modulator [35-42] for practical optical 
encryption under JTC architecture. In this study, the partially complex modulation 
characteristics of the TNLC-SLM module, dimensional mismatching between the input plane 
device (TNLC-SLM module) and the Fourier plane device (CCD sensor), and width 
constraints of the key mask are carefully considered to alleviate the distortion and 
overlapping of the decrypted images for practical optical implementation. To overcome these 
challenges, we propose a modified iterative Fourier transform algorithm with mapping 
procedures for the key mask design in order to achieve optimal recovery. The performance of 
the proposed encryption system is illustrated, and the shift-invariance property of the JTC 
architecture is also confirmed in experiments.

then truncated by the size constraints as indicated in Eq. (4.8).

variance is less than a preset threshold, the IFTA converges and creates a Fourier plane 
key mask for the mapping procedure by Step 4. Otherwise, the IFTA continues and 
returns to Step 2. 
Step 4: The complex key mask is obtained by the inverse Fourier transform of the Fourier 
key mask derived from Step 3, and the complex key mask is then added with a small 
value to shift the initial circle. The Fourier transform of the key mask with the shifted 
circle is clipped to limit the magnitude of the Fourier spectrum.  
Step 5: If the shifted circle isn’t matched with the encoding circle, then the mapping 
procedure returns to step 4. Otherwise, the shifted circle should be completely overlapped 
with the encoding circle and Step 6 should be done.

shifted circle and encoding circle. Based on the optimal encoding points, the optimal

4.4  結論 
A practical complex-encoding optical encryption system based on joint transform 
correlation architecture has been investigated and experimentally demonstrated. To achieve 
the optical realization, we construct a complex modulation module by cascading two 
TNLC-SLMs to encode the complex key mask in the input plane for the encryption and 
decryption. However, the complex-encoding device cannot achieve full-range complex 
modulation due to the coupled modulation properties of commercially available liquid crystal 
panels. Thus, a modified iterative Fourier transform algorithm with mapping procedures is 
developed to design the optimal key mask for image reconstruction. This algorithm 
iteratively adjusts the distribution of the key mask to satisfy pattern size constraints in both 
the input and the Fourier planes and to accommodate the partial range complex modulation of 
the cascaded TNLC-SLM module. By applying the designed key mask to the optical 
encryption process, better recovery can be achieved and flexibility of encrypted image 
properties is enhanced. We also demonstrate that key mask shifting results in precise 
decryption and that the shift positions of the complex key mask and the decrypted images are 
related by a linear function. Future research will focus on improvement of the complex 
modulation range of the spatial light modulators and applications of the complex-encoding 
for optical security.

The experimental setup of the proposed digital holographic microscope (DHM) system 
for in situ measuring the light-induced refractive index gratings is shown in Fig. 6.10, which 
mainly consists of the optical holographic recording and digital holographic microscope 
schemes. The holographic recording architecture is constructed by an off-axis interferomtric 
configuration, in which two writing beams coming from a coherent diode-pumped solid state 
(DPSS) laser with a wavelength of 532 nm can interfere and create the refractive index 
gratings with different spacing in the epoxy resin photopolymer holographic medium by 
selecting the intersection angle between the writing beams. The digital holographic 
microscope is a modified Mach–Zehnder interferometric architecture with a PZT-driven 
mirror in the reference arm and a CCD image sensor (pixel number: 640 × 480; pixel size: 7.4 
× 7.4 μm2). The probing beam coming from a He-Ne laser with a wavelength of 633 nm 
transmitted through the epoxy resin sample and interferes with the reference beam to form a 
interference fringe pattern on the CCD camera. The PZT-driven mirror introduced four-step 
phases (i.e., 0, π/2, π, 3π/2) in the reference beam to implement the phase-shifting digital 
holographic technique. The phase-shifting algorithm was used to remove the DC terms and 
twin image and the whole procedure of image reconstruction can be down to one second in 
order to observe the grating formation inside the epoxy resin photopolymer. 
The epoxy resin photopolymer sample under test was deposited to 100 μm thickness on 
a glass substrate by Doctor blading process and the refractive index of the epoxy resin 
photopolymer is about 1.50. The intersection angle of two writing beams is about 2.6° which 
will create the grating spacing about 7.8 μm so that the thin hologram approximation will be 
fulfilled. As shown in Fig. 6.10, a broadband beam splitter (BS2) was used to couple the 
writing beams from DPSS laser into DHM and the target was cast by an imaging lens (NA= 
0.1) on the epoxy resin sample to form an image hologram so that the objective (NA= 0.4) 
can easily to collect the transmitted light diffracted from the index gratings. A laser line color 
filter (CF) with high transmission at 633 nm wavelength was inserted in front of the CCD 
camera to detect the probing beam and to block the strong writing beams along with stray 
light. In such way, one can in situ observe and measure the light-induced refractive index 
gratings inside holographic recording medium during the writing exposure procedure. 
First of all, we measure the light-induced refractive index grating by casting two beams 
on the epoxy resin photopolymer plate with average power 2.8 mW/cm2. The experimental 
results of refractive index grating in epoxy resin by numerical reconstruction of DHM is 
shown in Fig. 6.11, in which 6.11 (a) is the phase map derived from the reconstructed image 
of DHM and 6.11 (b) is the 3-D profile of the index grating. One can see the fine structure of 
the index grating in the microscopic point of view. The grating index change is about 46 10−×  
and the grating spacing is about 8 μm, which is almost the same as the result of 2.6° derived 
from the estimated intersection angle between the writing beams.

We have proposed and demonstrated an in situ measurement technique for 
three-dimensional imaging of the light-induced refractive index gratings in an epoxy resin 
holographic recording medium by use of digital holographic microscopy (DHM). The 
aperture effect on image reconstruction is derived theoretically. The DHM system with large 
numerical aperture can collect the higher spatial frequencies of the sample under test and 
directly measure the fine grating structures of the light-induced index hologram. The 
proposed measurement approach has been applied to detect the three-dimensional refractive 
index profile of the epoxy resin photopolymer during the holographic recoding and 
reconstruction processes, which offers the possibility of direct observation and better 
understanding of grating formation and dynamic behaviors of the light-induced refractive 
index gratings in the holographic recording medium. Also, grating formation and 
reconstruction properties in the holographic recoding and reconstruction processes will be 
further investigated by the microscopic view of point in the future study.

Abstract: This work describes a novel digital holographic Fresnel encryption scheme using computer-
programmable random phase key masks, which are performed by electrically addressed liquid-crystal spatial 
light modulators for securing data via on-line optical processing.

1. Introduction 
Optical security is one of the most potential applications in the optical information processing community. 
Double random-phase encoding technique [1] uses two independent random-phase masks in the input plane and 
Fourier plane to convert a primary image to be encrypted into stationary white-noise data. Double random-phase 
encoding in the Fresnel domain [2] has been proposed to increase encryption dimensions and data security. As an 
image encrypted by the double random-phase encoding method involves complex data that can be stored or 
transmitted using a holographic technique, optoelectronic information encryption [3, 4] based on phase-shifting 
digital holography [5] has been proposed. Moreover, phase encoding for optical encryption with a random-phase 
mask (such as ground glass or a phase mask film) can ensure high information security and is robust against 
counterfeiting [6-10]. In this paper, we propose and demonstrate a novel full-phase digital holographic encryption 
Fresnel scheme using electrically addressed liquid-crystal spatial light modulators (LC-SLMs) [11,12] for on-line 
optical processing.  The LC-SLMs are operated in phase-modulation mode [13] to represent the phase-encoded input 
object and the double random-phase masks in the object and reference arms. Due to the fact that a primary image to 
be encrypted is phase-encoded into a phase object, the random-phase mask in the object arm can be treated as the 
roles of a phase diffuser and the other key mask for optical encryption. Furthermore, double random-phase masks 
using LC-SLMs can be electrically addressed during encryption procedures to overcome alignment problems 
encountered in practical optical encryption. This allows an encryption system to be programmable and provides 
potential applications with high data security via on-line processing. 
 
2. Principle 
Figure 1 shows a conceptual diagram of the proposed full-phase Fresnel encryption scheme with double key 
masks by phase-shifting digital holography, where one random-phase key mask is attached to the phase-encoded 
image in the object arm and another random-phase key mask is inserted into the reference arm. Based on the 
Fresnel-Kirchhoff diffraction approach, the intensity distribution of Fresnel holographic interference coming from 
the object and reference arm can be recorded on a CCD. By applying the four-step phase-shifting technique [8], a 
series of phase-shifted interference patterns is obtained for synthesizing into a complex digital hologram. As the 
double random-phase key masks are inserted into the reference and object arms for hologram recording, the intensity 
patterns of the complex digital hologram resemble random noise. Notably, one cannot retrieve information from the 
primary input object without using the double phase key mask and phase-encoding transformation for decryption. 
The full-phase encrytion and decryption procedures can be expressed as follows. For encryption, an input 
object is first phase-encoded into a phase-only image and multiplied with a random phase mask. The resultant 
Fresnel diffraction field in the object arm is interfered with by another diffracted field coming from another random-
phase mask in the reference arm. Hence, holographic interference fringes are phase-shifted and recorded on a CCD 
camera to generate an encrypted complex hologram. The encrypted amplitude and phase terms resemble random 
noise. To decrypt information and retrieve the primary image, one must use the correct double key masks 
simultaneously from the reference and object arms. The preceding encrypted holograms are addressed by the double 
key holograms in the reference and object arms, and the distribution of the decrypted image can be obtained using 
numerical reconstruction. Additionally, the distribution of the decrypted image should be phase-decoded into an 
amplitude image for direct visualization.

一、參加會議經過 
    2009 年「第 8 屆國際顯示全像術研討會」 (8th International Symposium on Display
Holography)於 7 月 13-17 日為期五天在中國深圳市隆重召開。今年主辦單位(中國泛彩溢公
司, Shenzhen AFC Technology Co., Ltd)依循往例邀請世界知名學者專家與藝術家與會發
表最新研究論文與全像藝術作品，交流與討論顯示全像術領域的現況及未來發展趨勢，並現
場展示許多各種尺寸大小的顯示全像片作品與全像攝影器材。 本次會議 議程主要包含顯示
全像術相關之材料、元件、系統、應用、藝術創作與教育等主題，與會各國學者專家逾百人
(全體與會者大合照如下圖)，總共發表約 90 餘篇論文，可謂盛況空前。 本次會議共安排五
天議程，其主題論文分別依序安排如下各單元⎯第一天：History, Culture and Education 
(Part A & B) (晚間:Banquet)；第二天：Art Concepts and Techniques (Part A,B,C & D),
(晚間:Art Forum) ；第三天： Recording Materials, Processing and Color Holography 
(Part A & B), Technical Applications (Part A & B) (晚間:Poster Session)；第四天：
Electronic, Digital, and Computer Generated Holography (Part A, B, C & D) (晚間:Tour 
the Window of the World and Watch the performance of“Splendors of the Past＂)；
第五天：(7) The Business of Holography (Part A & B)等。 由於本次會議安排於深圳市
郊海上田園度假村(Water Land)， 距市區相當遠、交通亦非便利，惟主辦單位除了白天議
程的學術論文發表以外，並於每天晚間安排不同的學術交流活動包括全像作品展覽會、科技
與藝術論壇等，皆引起了與會者十分熱烈迴響。

況，本次台灣有 6 位教授與 9 位學生共 15 人與會，參加人數僅次於地主國，筆者獲邀擔任
International program committeeman ，並於 “Report from nation”時段簡報日前甫舉辦
「臺日國際顯示全像術研討會」概況，讓與會者對於台灣的全像科技發展留下深刻印象。
