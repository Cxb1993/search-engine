(2)英文摘要 
The three Barnett-Lothe tensors SL,  and H , appearing  frequently in the 
investigations of the two-dimensional deformations of anisotropic elastic materials, are mostly 
expressed explicitly in terms of the reduced elastic compliance in the literature. Only the tensor 
H  presented by Ting (J. Elasticity 47, 1997) is expressed in terms of both elastic stiffness and 
reduced elastic compliance. In this paper, new expressions of the three Barnett-Lothe tensors 
SL, and H  are all expressed explicitly in terms of the elastic stiffness for general anisotropic 
materials. Results for the special cases of monoclinic materials with a plane of symmetry at 
03 =x , 02 =x , and 01 =x are all deduced. The special case of orthotropic materials expressed 
in terms of elastic stiffness in the literature may be recovered from present general results. 
Key words: Stroh formalism, Barnett-Lothe tensors, anisotropic elasticity, monoclinic materials, 
orthotropic materials.

bstract
The three Barnett–Lothe tensors L, S and H , appearing frequently in the investigations of the two-dimensional deformations
anisotropic elastic materials, are mostly expressed explicitly in terms of the reduced elastic compliance in the literature. Only
e tensor H presented by Ting [Ting, T.C.T., 1997. New explicit expression of Barnett–Lothe tensors for anisotropic linear elastic
aterials. J. Elasticity 47, 23–50] is expressed in terms of both elastic stiffness and reduced elastic compliance. In this paper, new
pressions of the three Barnett–Lothe tensors L, S and H are all expressed explicitly in terms of the elastic stiffness for general
isotropic materials. Results for the special cases of monoclinic materials with a plane of symmetry at x3 = 0, x2 = 0, and x1 = 0
e all deduced. The special case of orthotropic materials expressed in terms of elastic stiffness in the literature may be recovered
om present general results.

Introduction
It is known that Stroh formalism is mathematically elegant and technically powerful in determining the two-
mensional deformations of anisotropic elastic solids (Stroh, 1958; Ting, 1996). The three real matrices L, S and H ,
lled Barnett–Lothe tensors appear often in the solutions of many anisotropic elastostatic boundary value problems.
ue to their importance, the explicit expressions of Barnett–Lothe tensors have been investigated by many researchers.
orks having been done with special symmetry plane assumed in the literature were Dongye and Ting (1989) for or-
otropic materials, Ting (1992) for monoclinic materials with the symmetry plane at x3 = 0, Tanuma (1996), and
akamura and Tanuma (1996) for transversely isotropic materials. However, all their representations of the three real
atrices L, S and H are essentially expressed in terms of the reduced elastic compliances except the work done by
ongye and Ting (1989) who presented the components of L, S and H directly in terms of elastic stiffness. As to the
ost general anisotropic materials without any symmetry plane assumed, only two works, Wei and Ting (1994) and
ng (1997), have been published up to now for L, S and H . In the former (Wei and Ting, 1994), the eigenvectors
nstructed are based on the Lekhnitskii formalism (Lekhnitskii, 1963). Therefore their explicit expressions of L, S
