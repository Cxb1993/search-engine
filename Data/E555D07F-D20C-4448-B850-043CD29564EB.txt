研發成果名稱 （英文）Apparatus and method of irregular bone defect detection of 
dental implant 
成果歸屬機構 中央大學 發明人 (創作人) 潘敏俊、莊瀚伯、李士元
（中文） 
本發明針對牙科植體術後骨整合程度差異，所造成不規則骨缺損
型態，提出一非侵入式檢測評估方法、及相關檢測裝置設計製作
方式。利用植體與個別不規則骨缺損型態之結構動態特性差異，
能定量評估植體與齒槽骨之骨整合狀態。透過所發展檢測裝置與
方法，期能改善臨床常用 X 光二維影像判讀之不足，以及所見之
檢測儀器僅呈現植體整體穩固度之限制，有效得知骨缺損量、方
位與深度，藉此協助醫生適當評估病患植體與齒槽骨之整合狀
態，必要時進行骨整合強化後續處置，提高植牙成功率。 
技術說明 
（英文） 
The present invention detects an irregular bone defect of a dental 
implant. Resonance frequency is produced and received to recognize a 
quality, a direction and a depth of the irregular bone defect. Thus, an 
osseointegration is evaluated, and the present invention is of great help 
to dental diagnosis and osseointegration surgery. 
產業別 牙科膺復建產業、骨整合檢測裝置 
技術/產品應用範圍 
本發明之目的在提出一個檢測並評估牙科植體不規則骨缺損的裝
置與方法；考量到發展檢測方法之需要，所設計製作之檢測儀器，
具備四種檢測探頭可供替換。欲進行檢測時，先把金屬延伸結構
鎖附在牙科植體上，再將檢測探頭套在金屬延伸物上，由檢測探
頭內之聲波產生器發出音波激振，由音波激振金屬延伸物，訊號
則透過檢測探頭內之激振接收器感測接收，並傳至檢測儀主機，
透過頻譜分析獲得共振頻率值，藉由激振接收器所處方位，測得
之結構共振頻率差異，用以辨識不規則骨缺損量、方向與深度，
亦可評估骨整合穩固程度。

技術/產品應用範圍 
目前於植牙術後骨整合檢測技術方面，主要以振動方式評估骨整合優劣，故本
發明之目的在提出一種以超音波技術評估術後植體骨整合與檢測不規則骨缺損
之裝置與方法，設計一超音波齒科承座鎖附於植體，齒科承座裝載若干壓電換
能器，當啟動超音波訊號產生器，齒科承座外緣之壓電換能器發射超音波(縱波
或橫波)，透過齒科承座向植體-齒槽骨結構傳遞，待接觸植體-齒槽骨結構時反
射其音阻抗，再由壓電換能器接收反射波與回傳訊號，以此訊號評估界面骨整
合與檢測骨缺損深度、方位。此設計與其它植牙術後檢測裝置不同處，為發射-
接收端位於同一換能器，且待測物不會產生共振。

組織和植體界面組織切片(Histologic Technique)的觀察是最好的檢測方式[10]，但是在試片製作過程
中，植體和周圍樹脂包埋(Resin-Embedded)組織之間物理性質不同，容易造成植體與骨組織間無法取得
正確定位，所以在評估上有很大的困難[2]；超顯微結構(Ultrastructural)的研究可以從穿透式電子顯微鏡
(Transmission Electron Microscopy, TEM)觀察到植體周圍膠原纖維(Collagen)排列堆積的情況，不過TEM
試片製作要先脫鈣，所以其缺點是對於脫鈣後的切片，很難用來觀察植體周圍礦化物質的比率[2]。 
  第二種是移除扭力法，Morgan 等人的研究指出，在植體與骨整合間力與力矩的分佈情形，力矩的
量遠大於軸向應力(即力矩在整個結構中的影響較大)，因此可藉著量測破壞骨整合界面時力矩產生的扭
力值來評估植體與骨骼間的穩固程度[11]；而 Goheen 等人則表示以手控(Hand-Held)方式測量所得到的
差異高達 48%，同時移除扭力容易對周圍組織造成傷害[12]；Brånemark 的研究指出，在做扭力測試時
施予一小扭力，就會造成受測植體的變形(Plastic Deformation)，且結果只得到全或無(All or None)的答
案，無法得到骨癒合與骨成形的過程[13]。此外，當骨骼條件不理想時需要較長時間來完成骨整合，這
時斷然使用移除扭力法來測試，會引起植體組織的傷害，進而造成臨床上的失敗[2]。 
  在臨床上利用非侵入式的測試方法，來評估植牙穩固度和骨整合情況有比較高的成效與滿意度，
所以在非侵入式檢測方面，第一種是敲擊法，利用敲擊植體或基台(Abutment)所產生的聲音，來判斷植
體穩固度，主要目的是得到植體的共振(Resonance)與阻尼(Damping)，不過由於每個人對聲音的敏感度
不同，這種方法就略顯主觀[2]；Salonen 等人曾利用輕推方式來判定植體穩固度，但所得到的結果可能
是植體本身黏彈性所產生的現象，跟穩固度無關[14]。 
  第二種是 X 光法，這是臨床上最常使用的方法，利用 X 光影像的灰階密度差異做為植體植入後的
評估。Leholm 等人利用 X 光來判斷骨密度與骨整合狀況[15]；Sunden 等人發現要有 30%以上的骨流失
量(Bone Loss)才能在 X 光上清楚觀察到[16]。 
  第三種是撞擊法，這是ㄧ種利用觀察植體受力後的位移(Displacement)來做為判斷穩固度的方法。
Oka 等人以衝擊鎚去撞擊牙科植體，並偵測其回撞感測器的時間，當植體動搖度(Mobility)越大，則回
撞時間越長，藉此判斷植體穩固度，並發展出一套設備 Periotest® (Siemens AG, Germany)，用以偵測牙
周韌帶狀態與牙齒動搖度[17, 18]，但是這樣的方法會受到施力大小及方向所影響，即使可以設計成固
定激發力量的器具，若施加在牙科植體上的方向不同，就會得到不同的結果，因此以衝擊鎚撞擊牙科
植體使其產生位移，然後偵測回撞時間，來判斷牙科植體穩固度，做為檢查植體邊界狀態的方法，雖
然是目前最常見的檢測方式，卻仍有限制與缺點[19]。 
  第四種是共振頻率(Resonance Frequency, RF)法，利用共振頻率振動理論做為牙科植體穩固度的
檢測依據，近年來除了工業材料與結構方面的檢測外，也被廣泛運用在生物醫學的領域上，其中在口
腔醫學研究方面，利用該方式檢測，可以直接反應牙齒周邊組織或骨整合結構好壞與頻率變化的關係，
Meredith 等人設計一 L 型(11mm*5mm*15mm, 重 12 克)換能器(Transducer)激發受測物，利用貼於兩側
壓電材料所產生壓電效應之振動，產生一個穩態的頻率，然後連接在植體上，可量測其共振頻率，以
監測牙根周圍骨質生長的情形，若骨整合逐漸完成時，植體周圍組織的剛性會逐漸增加，植體穩固度
增加，共振頻率也隨之增加[20]，進而開發出一套設備 OsstellTM (Integration Diagnostic, Savedalen, 
Sweden)，藉由共振頻率換算之 ISQ (Implant Stability Quotient)值來測量牙科植體穩固度；Huang 等人利
用衝擊法激發人工牙根共振，並以非接觸式麥克風接收振動訊號，體外實驗結果顯示，人工牙根的共
振頻率與骨夾持高度和骨整合程度有關，夾持高度越高或夾持力越大則共振頻率越高，但是衝擊法為
一次激發待測物的所有頻率，無法觀察外力改變下系統振動狀態的改變，對植體癒合前期牙根四周新

計上是有意義的；而完整側頻率為 1 次激振量測結果，主要用於比較缺損與完整側之頻率變化差異。

六、計畫成果自評 
本計畫結案報告之研究結果，已由體外人造骨塊之骨缺損(牙科鑽頭研磨之間隙骨缺損)模型實驗，
測得之共振頻率變化，與有限元素分析及市售骨整合檢測儀器相互驗證，歸納出缺損結構與頻率變化
關係，建立術後體外骨缺損檢測機制；並進行初步體內白兔脛骨實驗，證明經由共振頻率量測，可有
效評估骨整合發展與監測植體-齒槽骨結構穩固度變化。而本計畫使用有限元素分析，進行共振頻率法
於植牙檢測之模擬驗證，藉由有限元素模態分析結果，觀察植體受外力時在骨塊中之運動行為後得知，
提供聲壓負載於連接體，造成骨塊與植體間產生應力，適當之應力將有助於骨整合發展。

operator demodulation, the support vector machine and the

However, how to select an appropriate IMF for subsequent 
envelope analysis to characterize bearing faults has not been 
explored and addressed in the above literatures. It is noted that 
the component of IMF 1 was always used in the envelope 
analysis subsequently without any explanation. Moreover, 
when the conventional envelope analysis was used along to 
detect bearing faults, all the resonant frequency bands need to 
be examined. Therefore, the above approaches are not suited 
for practical applications. This paper demonstrates the 
procedure with an insight concept that combines the EMD 
with a swept-sine excitation to select a resonance frequency 
band (or more) for the subsequent envelope analysis of 
rolling-bearing fault detection. As examples, both the dual-
fault and the tri-fault are considered and used to justify the 
proposed idea. Further, this detection procedure superior to the 
conventional envelope analysis in the bearing fault diagnosis 
is compared and discussed. 
 
2 EXPERIMENTAL SETUP 
An experiment test bench, including a servo-motor, a coupling, 
and a shaft with two rotor-disks and two ball bearings (ASAHI 
UCP-204), is shown in Fig. 1. Various fault types, such as 
unbalance, misalignment and bearing faults, can be created 
using this platform. In this paper, the platform was employed 
to investigate bearing-fault detection techniques. The 
geometric parameters of the bearing are the number of rolling 
balls, n = 8, the contact angle, α= 0° , the ball diameter, 
d=7.8mm and the pitch diameter, D=33.5mm. Two 
accelerometers were mounted on the bearing seats to measure 
vibration signals translating to the bearings. The digital 
tachometer was used to measure the shaft speed. In the 
experimentation the left-hand-side bearing remained normal, 
and the other one varied with different conditions (normal, 
dual-fault and tri-fault). The dual-defect bearing has the outer-
race fault with 1-mm diameter hole and the inner-race with a 
slot of 0.2-mm width and 1-mm depth. The tri-defect bearing 
has the ball and cage fault with 1-mm diameter hole and the 
inner-race fault with a slot of 0.2-mm width and 1-mm depth.

3 PROPOSED PROCEDURE TO DETECT BEARING 
FAULTS 
This study proposes a detection procedure for rolling-bearing 
fault diagnosis. An insight concept is exploited as the 
decomposed IMFs through the EMD computation possess a 
band-pass filtered nature. Combining the EMD with a swept-
sine excitation is able to select an appropriate IMF that 
contains a resonance frequency band (or more) modulating 
fault characteristic frequency. This frequency exhibiting a 
specific faulty condition can be eventually characterized by 
the envelope analysis. 
 
3.1 Empirical Mode Decomposition: a Sifting 
Process 
The empirical mode decomposition is an adaptive signal 
decomposition method, which is able to decompose non-linear 
and non-stationary data into a sequence of amplitude-
modulation/frequency-modulation (AM/FM) components or 
alike. These independent components to be obtained are called 
intrinsic mode functions, which must satisfy the two 
conductions [14]: (1) In the whole set data, the number of 
extrema and the number of zero-crossings must either be equal 
or different at most by one. (2) At any point, the mean of the 
envelope defined by local maxima and the envelope defined 
by the local minima are zero. 
The decomposition procedure of EMD called the sifting 
process (SP) is briefly described below [14, 15]. 
(1) Find out all the local extrema, Emax(t), and then couple with 
all the local maxima by a cubic spline as the upper envelop, 
u(t) . 
(2) Repeat the procedure (1). The local minima, Emin(t), 
produces the lower envelope, l(t). 
(3) Compute the local mean, m(t)= (u(t)+ l(t))/2. 
(4) Subtract m(t) from the original signal, x(t), and then the 
first component, h1(t), can be obtained. If h1(t) satisfies the 
condition of IMF, h1(t) is designed as c1(t).

Yu et al. [11] showed local Hilbert transform and local Hilbert 
marginal transform based on EMD for the fault diagnosis of 
roller bearing. Qi et al. [12] demonstrated the use of EMD for 
rubbing fault diagnosis from steady state response. Tejas H. 
Patel [13] indicated rub happen at its initial stage. 
For the interpretation of rub phenomena, most researches 
focused on vibration responses at a constant revolution speed. 
It was seldom noticed to detect fluid-induced instability and 
rub with startup or shutdown vibration signals. This study 
revealed that the HHT was a useful tool to analyze the startup 
signals and was effective for interpreting fluid-induced 
instability. HSA showed that oil whirl had a frequency 
component tracking the rotating speed, and that oil whip had a 
fixed frequency equal to a natural frequency of the rotor 
system. However, HSA is insufficient for revealing 
phenomenon of dry whip. In this paper, a Hilbert spectrum 
combining a full spectrum [14] was developed into the Full 
Hilbert spectrum (FHS) which is an effective method to 
observe the coexistence of oil whip and dry whip. 
 
Hilbert Huang Transform 
In general, frequency is defined as numbers of oscillations 
per unit time. However, for nonstationary and nonlinear 
signals encountered in machinary diagnosis, this definition is 
ambiguous and loses its effectiveness. In this situation, 
instantaneous frequency is viable. It has been widely studied 
when attempts to extract meaningful instantaneous 
frequencies from multicomponent signals, instantaneous 
frequencies are both physically invalid and erroneous. The 
key to the applicability of instantaneous frequencies is to 
decompose mulicomponent signals into some individual 
monocomponents to which the basic definition of 
instantaneous frequency can be applied.  
The Hilbert Huang Transform consists of two main steps, 
first, empirical mode decomposition (EMD)─to generate the 
intrinsic mode functions (IMFs). And second, the Hilbert 
transform to the intrinsic mode functions.  
It is an empirically based data-analysis method. Its basis of 
expansion is adaptive, so that it can produce physically 
meaningful representations. 
An intrinsic mode function is a function that satisfies two 
conditions: (1) in the whole data set, the number of extrema 
and the number of zero crossings must either equal or differ at 
most by one; and (2) at any point, the mean value of the 
envelope defined by the local maxima and the envelope 
defined by the local minima is zero.
