2.Experimental procedures 
2.1 TSV formation by ns laser pulses 
The laser source used in this study was a 
commercially available Q-switched, 
diode-pumped, solid-state industrial ns green 
laser that delivers an average power of 34 W 
with 532-nm wavelength at 100 kHz with 
28-ns pulse width. The laser beam was focused 
through a telecentric lens of 100-mm focal 
length to achieve a 35-µm spot size with an 
approximately 600-µm depth of focus in order 
to efficiently drill through a thick wafer. A 
two-axis galvanometer was used for quickly 
positioning the laser beam from point to point 
on the X-Y plane. The trepanning process with 
ns laser pulses was used in this study. Laser 
trepanning uses a laser beam that is smaller 
than the target diameter and cuts out the holes 
by moving the laser beam in a circular motion. 
Trepanning with ns laser pulses can afford 
TSVs having the desired specifications, 
namely, well shaped, 85°–90° taper angle, and 
100-µm diameter. The tested samples were 
single-side polished, p-type, single-crystal 
silicon wafers with (100) crystal orientation, 
200-mm (8″) diameter, 400-µm thickness. The 
processed TSVs were measured and evaluated 
for top surface quality, sidewall quality, and 
TSV morphology using a Hitachi-4800 
scanning electron microscope. The 
specifications of the laser system, drilling 
materials, process parameters, and target 
values in the present research are summarized 
in Table 1. 
2.2 Wet chemical etching 
WCE of silicon with HF–HNO3 mixtures is a 
widely used process in the semiconductor 
manufacturing industry and in the production of 
solar cells because this process enables the 
removal of work damage and the effective 
reduction of roughness [11–12]. H2O and 
C2H4O2 are commonly used as diluents to 
control the etching rate and etching selectivity 
of the etchant. The basic etching mechanism for 
isotropic silicon etching using HF–HNO3 
mixtures is summarized by chemical reaction 
Eqs. (1)–(4); reactions Eqs. (1)–(3) show how 
HNO3 oxidizes Si to form SiO2, and Eq. (4) 
shows how HF etches the resultant SiO2.

In this study, the green ns laser drilled TSV 
wafers were fixed to the designed 
wafer-holding fixture, laid horizontally and 
etched in the WCE equipment with the polished 
surface facing upward, and the etchant was 
maintained at 20°C. The experimental 
equipment for WCE used in this study is 
commercially available system. The mixture 
ratio of the etchant solution, consisting of 49% 
HF, 70% HNO3, and 100% H2O, was 
optimized to reduce the TSV sidewall damage 
caused by ns laser pulses. Experiments were 
conducted to investigate the resulting deep 
TSVs. The results are presented and discussed 
in the following section.  
3. Experimental results and discussion 
3.1 Bosch DRIE process 
The Bosch DRIE process can be used to 
fabricate deep, steep-sided TSVs with a high 
aspect ratio of 10–20 and a nearly vertical 
sidewall (i.e., the taper angle is around 90°). 
The Bosch DRIE process is the most widely 
used process to fabricate reliable drilled TSVs 
 5 
3.4 TSV fabrication using ns laser pulses with 
WCE process compared to Bosch DRIE 
TSV fabrication using ns laser pulses with 
WCE process is flexible and can be utilized for 
both via-first and via-last machining 
approaches to be adopted. Moreover, the 
proposed approach gives developers a rapid 
prototyping tool that enables them to iterate 
designs quickly from design modifications to 
machined wafer. In this study, a simplified 
process for achieving excellent TSV sidewall 
quality with the diameter of greater than 100 
µm on a 400-µm-thick wafer is introduced. 
 With respect to TSV sidewall quality, 
although the Bosch DRIE process can produce 
a scallop size within 100 nm, the etching speed 
has to be maintained at very low rate, less than 
5 µm/min, which is not economically viable for 
industrial production [5–10]. In contrast to the 
Bosch DRIE, the green ns laser with WCE 
process generates a scallop-free sidewall 
quality. Fig. 4(a) shows that the green ns laser 
with WCE process can indeed be used to 
achieve a scallop-free sidewall, and Fig. 4(b) 
shows a scallop measuring 99 nm on a sidewall 
fabricated by the Bosch DRIE process [5]. 
With regard to drilling efficiency, the 
processing time of Bosch DRIE for 400-µm 
deep TSV etching is theoretically constant, 
independent of the number of TSVs on one 
wafer. Currently, the highest etching rate 
corresponding to 100-nm sidewall roughness is 
approximately 5 µm/min (that is, a 400-µm 
deep TSV is completed in 4800 s) [5–10]. The 
TSV drilling speed of green ns laser with WCE 
process for a 400-µm thick wafer is 5.42 
TSVs/s. In Fig. 5, the TSV drilling speed is 
plotted as a function of the number of TSVs on 
one wafer. The hybrid process that combines 
green ns laser drilling tool and WCE was found 
to greatly improve a TSV drilling efficiency, 
namely 160% higher than that of the Bosch 
DRIE process for 10000-TSVs fabrication. The 
green ns laser with WCE process is faster than 
the Bosch DRIE process as long as the number 
of the TSVs on one wafer is less than 26016.  
Considering etch-profile deviation of TSVs, 
uniformity is first taken into account when 
qualifying the diameter deviation in industrial 
applications. The non-uniformity of Bosch 
DRIE in the formation of TSVs is largely 
caused by temperature differences across the 
wafer. The centre is always hotter than the 
edges of the wafer due to the bending of the 
wafer by the helium backside and the limited 
heat removal. The TSV diameter uniformity of 
Bosch DIRE can vary as small as ± 3% across 
the 200-mm wafer [6–7]. In contrast to Bosch 
DRIE, non-uniformity of green ns laser with 
WCE process in the formation of TSVs is 
largely determined by the shot-to-shot laser 
energy stability and the WCE process 
parameters. Fig. 6 shows the results of TSV 
diameter deviation at five wafer positions 
(approximately ± 5% uniformity in 150-µm 
TSV size). Certainly, both the Bosch DRIE and 
the green ns laser with WCE processes obtain 
satisfactory uniformity results. The proposed 
approach is very stable in the formation of 
TSVs. 
Concerning cost of ownership, the use of 
green ns laser process eliminates the need for 
various expensive lithographic steps such as 
mask, coating, exposure, development, and 
photoresist stripping of Bosch DIRE process. In 
addition, the green ns laser with WCE process 
uses low cost consumables and meets low 
maintenance requirements. Conclusively, the 
proposed hybrid approach has a significant cost 
advantage over Bosch DRIE process.  
4. Conclusions 
This research proposes an effective process for 
fabrication of deep TSVs. In this research, a 
green ns laser was used to quickly drill through 
deep TSVs and a WCE process was 
incorporated to enhance the sidewall quality of 
the laser drilled deep TSVs. It is found that the 
sidewall roughness of the ns laser drilled TSVs 
can be improved from micrometer scale to 
nanometer scale by incorporating an optimized 
WCE process. The drilling speed of the green 
ns laser with WCE process for forming 10000 
TSVs on a 200-mm wafer was found to be 
approximately 160% greater than that of the 
Bosch DRIE process. As seen in the analysis, 
the green ns laser with WCE process is more 
efficient than the Bosch DRIE process as long 
as the number of the TSV holes on one wafer is 
less than 26016. In this way, the 
semi-conductor manufacturers have the benefits
