Chapter 1 Introduction 
 
1.1 Background 
 
Measures of both technical efficiency (a transformation of outputs from inputs) and service 
effectiveness (a transformation of consumptions from outputs) for storable commodities are 
essentially the same because the commodities, once produced, can be stockpiled until consumed. 
Nothing will be lost throughout the transformation from outputs to consumptions if one assumes 
that all the stockpiles are eventually sold out. Namely, conventional measures for ordinary 
commodities assume perfect sale effectiveness. However, technical efficiency and service 
effectiveness for non-storable commodities, such as transport services, represent two distinct 
measurements because one can never store the surplus service capacity at low demands (off-peak 
hours) for the usage at high demands (peak hours). When such non-storable commodities are 
produced and a portion of which are not concurrently consumed, the technical effectiveness (a 
combined effect of both technical efficiency and service effectiveness) would be less than the 
technical efficiency. To evaluate the overall performance of any non-storable commodities in an 
impartial way and to gain insights of lack of efficiency or lack of effectiveness, it is imperative to 
analyze their efficiency and effectiveness altogether. 
 
Taking the passenger transport as an example, once the transport outputs (seat-miles) are 
transformed from such inputs as vehicle, fuel and labor, the seat-miles must be consumed 
immediately by the passengers; otherwise, they are exhausted and wasted forever. Thus, both 
technical efficiency and service effectiveness must be jointly measured so as to reflect the fact that 
not all the seat-miles are fully utilized in practice. The technical effectiveness depends not only on 
how well the outputs (seat-miles) being transformed from the inputs but also on how well the 
consumptions (passenger-miles) being transformed from the outputs. In other words, to assess the 
system performance for non-storable commodities, it would become more informative if one could 
jointly measure the efficiency and effectiveness. 
 
To explain this concept, Fielding (1987) first introduced three performance measures for a public 
transit system by defining cost-efficiency as the ratio of outputs to inputs, service-effectiveness as 
the ratio of consumptions to outputs, and cost-effectiveness as the ratio of consumptions to inputs. It 
should be noted that if the input factor prices are not known, one could not measure the cost 
efficiency or cost effectiveness. Nonetheless, one can still measure the technical efficiency or 
technical effectiveness. Likewise, if the sale prices are not known, one cannot measure the 
revenue-related effectiveness; but one can measure the service- or technical-effectiveness. Lan and 
Lin (2006) used the same concept of distinctive performance measurements with technical 
effectiveness, technical efficiency, and service effectiveness for rail transport services. By this 
distinction, any poor performance of transport services should be attributed to either poor technical 
efficiency or poor service effectiveness or a combination of both. Without separation of technical 
efficiency and service effectiveness measurements, it is difficult to scrutinize the sources of poor 
performance. Therefore, this study will treat technical efficiency and service effectiveness as two 
distinctive performance measures for non-storable commodities such as transport services.

3.3.2 Uniqueness 
 
To show the uniqueness of joint efficiency measurement of the ICCR model, we have to prove that 
the virtual multipliers of u, v, and w determined by the ICCR model are a global optimum, not a 
local optimum. For a nonlinear programming problem, only with a convex or concave objective 
function over a convex feasible region (i.e. sufficient conditions) would the solutions obtained via 
the Karush-Kuhn-Tucker (KKT) conditions (i.e. necessary conditions) guarantee a global optimum. 
In other words, the convexity or concavity of objective function together with the convexity of 
feasible region must be examined.

模式，包括固定規模報酬下之 IFCCR 模式及變動規模報酬之 IFBCC 模式。兩個 IFDEA 模式
整合不同 α 截集下模糊投入項與產出項之上、下限值，以產出一條明確之效率前緣，不必再

Envelopment Analysis, IRFDEA) 模式，包括用於固定規模報酬下之 IRFCCR 模式及用於變動
規模報酬之 IRFBCC 模式。IRFDEA 模式主要在同時評估路線別及公司別之績效值，主要分
為三階段，第一階段是利用整合式 IFDEA 模式評估公司別之技術效率及服務效果，其中將變
