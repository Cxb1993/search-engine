1.前言 
本二年期計畫「以反應式雙極非對稱脈衝直流磁控濺鍍系統鍍製氮化鉻基質奈米多
層薄膜之製程參數與薄膜性質研究」延續96年之研究主題，將繼續以反應式雙極非對稱
脈衝直流磁控濺鍍法，搭配電漿放射光譜儀進行電漿診斷來進行沉積 (1)相異結晶結構
-金屬/氮化物多層薄膜之Cr/CrN 與Cu/Cr2N 系統(97年度計畫)與(3)相同結晶結構(fcc)
多層薄膜-CrN/CrSiN 與CrN/TiAlN 系統(98年度計畫)的製程參數與薄膜性質的研究。 
2.研究目的 
本計畫之97年度(第一年計畫)將根據最佳製程參數，以電漿放射監控系統 (PEM)
進行電漿診斷與控制來沉積具有相異結晶結構 -金屬 /氮化物多層薄膜之Cr/CrN 
(bcc/NaCl 結構)與Cu/Cr2N(fcc/hcp 結構)薄膜系統；本年度之重點是探討不同金屬薄膜
種類與雙層週期厚度(Λ)對多層薄膜硬度、磨潤性、抗腐蝕與抑菌(以Cu/Cr2N 為主)等性
質的影響，並微調製程參數，以分析各製程參數對奈米多層薄膜之成分、生成晶相、微
結構、抗蝕、抑菌與機械性質等相關特性的影響性，並與奈米多層薄膜之強化機制進行
分析比對與驗證，以做為調整製程參數之依據。 
本計畫之98年度(第二年計畫)將根據最佳製程參數，以電漿放射監控系統 (PEM)
進行電漿診斷與控制來沉積具有相同結晶結構多層薄膜-CrN/CrSiN 與CrN/TiAlN(皆為
NaCl 結構)薄膜系統；本年度之重點是討論不同薄膜種類與雙層週期厚度(Λ)對多層薄
膜硬度、常溫抗蝕性、高溫抗氧化性等性質的影響，並微調製程參數，以分析各製程參
數對奈米多層薄膜之雙層週期厚度(Λ)、成分、生成晶相、微結構、抗腐蝕、抗氧化能

3.1.1相同結晶結構多層薄膜 (Isostructural multilayer) 
對於相同結構多層薄膜的研究都集中於各種高硬度之過渡金屬氮化物，例如 TiN、
VN、NbN等的多層薄膜結構。Helmersson等人[16]曾經報導當雙層週期厚度(Λ)僅 5.2nm
時，TiN/VN(001)奈米多層薄膜的硬度可高達 55.6 GPa，是單層薄膜硬度的 2.5倍之多。
然而再降低Λ厚度時，硬度反而下降。在 TiN/VxNb1-xN奈米多層薄膜的雙層週期厚度(Λ)
對硬度的影響研究發現[17,18]，薄膜之間的晶格不匹配度對整體硬度上升的貢獻極少。
根據 Shinn與 Barnett[19]對於 V0.6Nb0.4N/NbN奈米多層薄膜的研究結果顯示，當薄膜之
間的晶格不匹配度達 3.5%，而薄膜之間的剪模數差異不大時，硬度增加量反而不大；
在 TiN/V0.6Nb0.4N [18]與 TiN/NbN[20]奈米多層薄膜的研究亦有相同之結果。根據上述之
研究可以確知，奈米多層薄膜之間的剪模數差異度是氮化物薄膜硬度增加的主因。 
其他多晶之氮化物多層薄膜，例如 TiN/MoN[21]、TiN/CrN[22]、AlTiN/CrN[23,24]
等結構之硬度都可達到 30 GPa以上，其中(111)指向之 TiN/CrN[22] 奈米多層薄膜的硬
度在雙層週期厚度(Λ)降至 2 nm時，硬度達到 36 GPa。由以上這些研究結果可知，只在
某些氮化物多層薄膜的材料組合與特定之結晶指向才會觀察到極大的硬度增加現象。 
3.1.2相異結晶結構多層薄膜(Non-isostructural multilayer) 
由於磊晶穩定度的問題，亦即兩種不同結晶構造薄膜在沉積過程不易形成整合界
面，而傾向在另一層薄膜表面生成一個介穩定(metastable)結構而形成整合界面，因此相
異結構多層薄膜的強化現象比較複雜。由於此種相異結構多層薄膜有非常高的界面能，
因此在雙層週期厚度(Λ)小於 2 nm時，這種介穩定(metastable)結構常被觀察到。然而由
於其機械性質並不被大家熟知，因此這種相異結晶結構多層薄膜的硬度不易預估。相異
結晶結構多層薄膜的種類又可區分為氮化物/氮化物多層薄膜與金屬/氮化物多層薄膜兩
大類。 
 
3.1.2.1氮化物/氮化物多層薄膜 
數種相異結構之氮化物 /氮化物多層薄膜，例如 TiN/Cr2N[25]、TiN/AlN[26]、

已經到達其理論強度而限制了多層薄膜的硬度無法繼續提升有關[39]。 
這種金屬/氮化物多層薄膜的高溫穩定性亦是受矚目的重要性質[15]，例如Mo/NbN
互不固溶，因此在 1000℃高溫 3小時仍然維持其高溫硬度[41]。因此雖然金屬/氮化物
多層薄膜的硬度比氮化物/氮化物多層薄膜者還低，但是前者在高溫的結構穩定性卻由
於後者，因此金屬/氮化物多層薄膜在具有高溫之產業，例如應用於玻璃模造、塑膠射
出或是鋁合金壓鑄等熱加工模具表面的高硬度與耐熱鍍膜應該極具潛力。 
 
3.2奈米多層薄膜之強化原理與機制 
根據 Holleck的研究[42]，超晶格(superlattice)或奈米多層薄膜結構的雙層週期厚度
(Λ)經過最佳化調整之後，薄膜具有最大之薄膜硬度/韌性比值、疲勞強度與附著性。而
有關奈米多層薄膜之可能強化機制[15]包括下列因素：(1)差排之影像力(image force)，
(2)Hall-Petch晶粒細化強化理論，(3) 差排在交替變化應變場的移動理論與(4)超模數效
應(super-modulus effect)。然而根據實驗證明，目前僅(1)差排之鏡像力(image force)與
(2)Hall-Petch晶粒細化強化理論對多層薄膜強化有直接之影響力，另外兩個強化機制的
效果則非常微不足道[15]。茲將差排之影像力(image force)效應、薄膜內的差排滑移與
Hall-Petch晶粒細化強化理論說明如下。 
 
3.2.1差排之鏡像力效應(Image effects) 
Koehler於 1970年發表解釋多層薄膜機械性質的論文[43]，他提出利用兩種不同的
金屬，A與 B，其彈性係數 E B>E A，同時兩種金屬之熱膨脹係數相近且都具有強鍵結。
這兩種金屬薄膜的厚度必須非常小使得差排源無法在任一層薄膜內產生。如果在較軟金
屬 A 的某一個差排受外加應力作用時，此差排會往 A/B 界面移動。在較高彈性係數的
B 薄膜層會因為彈性應變作用而產生排斥之鏡像力，因而造成差排滑移通過界面的阻
礙。如此一來便使得多層薄膜機械性質比混合法則(rule of mixture)所預期者高了許多。
換句話說，在多層薄膜結構中，需要較大的應力才能將差排自彈性係數較低的 A 薄膜
材料移動到 B薄膜材料；當薄膜厚度低到一個臨界值 tC時，薄膜已無法藉由 Frank-Read

4.研究方法 
4.1 實驗流程規劃 
本研究為二年計畫，第一年(97 年期)計畫鍍製包括鍍膜實驗(I)：鍍製十種氮化鉻/
銅奈米多層薄膜(使用 Cr靶與 Cu靶)、鍍膜實驗(II)：鍍製五種氮化鉻/鉻奈米多層薄膜(使
用雙 Cr靶)，第二年(98年期)計畫鍍製包括：鍍膜實驗(III)：鍍製十種氮化鈦鋁/氮化鉻
矽奈米多層薄膜(使用 CrSi 靶與 TiAl 靶)、鍍膜實驗(IV)：鍍製七種氮化鉻矽/氮化鉻奈
米多層薄膜(使用 Cr靶與 CrSi靶)。 
上述鍍膜實驗(I)、(III)與(IV)共分為兩個階段，其中第一階段鍍製雙層厚度(Λ)的多
層薄膜，藉由調整試片停留於雙靶前的時間來控制試片之週期厚度(Λ)值，本階段的鍍
製過程中雙靶材的配置採用 180o面對面放置，試片則採 360o旋轉且控制試片停留於靶
前的時間以獲得成份均勻之多層薄膜。薄膜的總厚度皆控制 1~1.3μm，因此只要控制於
每一次的旋轉中銅靶材與鉻靶材前的停留時間便能分批鍍製不同雙層週期厚度的多層
薄膜。其試片與靶材配置則如圖 4-1與圖 4-2所示。

4.3.4 硬度試驗 
本實驗的薄膜試片使用奈米壓痕硬度機(Triboscope , Hysitron,USA)測量薄膜之硬
度與彈性係數，該儀器所使用之Berkovich，其形狀為三角錐，其三錐線與中心對稱軸
夾角為77.05°，錐面中線與中心軸夾角為65.3°，因可將壓痕最大深度控制在鍍層厚度的
10％以內，因此可避免基材效應產生，且藉由Oliver與Pharr之壓痕深度測量法推算出硬
度與彈性係數值[63]，因此可避免直接觀察壓痕所造成之人為誤差。 
 
4.3.5 附著性試驗 
薄膜與基材表面之間的附著性為該薄膜性質上的重要指標，通常附著性試驗會使用
的有洛氏C(Rockwell-C)壓痕試驗法[65-66]與刮痕試驗法[67-69]兩種。 
 
4.3.5.1 洛氏 C壓痕試驗法 
本實驗所用之洛氏C壓痕試驗法源自於德國，其測試法於1991年VDI3198規範所標
準化[67]，可測試鍍膜在基材上的附著性。 
測試方法如下：首先將鍍製於碳化鎢鈷硬質合金基材表面的薄膜試片以洛
C(Rockwell)硬度試驗機進行壓痕試驗，該試驗使用0.2吋鑽石圓錐壓痕器，施加荷重為
150Kgf，持重時間為30秒，並於每個試片中測試3點，以測試其附著強度品質。壓痕試
驗後，將每個壓痕周圍的薄膜附著情形將使用掃描式電子顯微鏡觀察其壓痕形貌以評估
該薄膜之附著強度。其薄膜附著強度依VDI3198規範可分類為HF1~HF6等六種類型，其
分類外形如下圖4-4所示，其中HF號數越小則表示該薄膜之附著性越佳。

5.III.1.2.2 破斷韌性分析 
本實驗使用微小維克氏試驗機對鍍覆於單晶矽晶片的薄膜試片進行壓痕試驗，針對
不同雙層週期厚度的薄膜進行破斷韌性之評估。 
根據Musil [70]等人對奈米薄膜的韌性研究提出，薄膜經壓痕試驗後是否會產生裂
痕依底材的不同有不同的評估方式，依本研究中矽晶片為底材而言，薄膜的有效彈性係
數( *fE )與底材的有效彈性係數( *sE )的比值 *fE / *sE ≥ 1.3，及薄膜的厚度(h)產生的壓痕之
深度(d)為 d/h ≥ 0.5，則會產生裂痕，而裂痕產生之形態與薄膜的硬度(Hf)與底材的硬度
(Hs)有關，若 fs HH 5.0≥ 則會產生如放射狀的裂痕，若反之，則會產生圈型的裂痕，而
上述之關係列於表 III-5-4，由表中可得知，薄膜經壓痕試驗後皆有產生放射狀裂痕，故
皆符合上述之條件。不同雙層週期厚度的多層薄膜之壓痕裂痕形貌 BEI 影像如圖
III-5-21 所示，皆可觀察到明顯的放射狀的裂痕。而不同雙層厚度週期的薄膜之 KIC值
如表 III-5-5與圖 III-5-22所示，由表 III-5-5與圖 III-5-22中可發現，各個不同雙層週期
厚度之 KIC值皆高於單一鍍層的 TiAlN與 CrSiN薄膜，雙層厚度週期在 6nm到 12nm，
破斷韌性值保持平緩的趨勢，當大於 12nm時，其值持續上升，在 30nm有著最大的破
斷韌性值 2.85 MPa m，雙層週期厚度大於 30nm時，則開始下降。

6.結論 
6.I.鍍膜實驗(I)- Cr2N/Cu奈米多層薄膜 
根據本實驗對氮化鉻/銅奈米多層薄膜各項研究結果，可得到以下結論： 
1. 本研究使用脈衝直流磁控濺鍍系統，藉由調整試片停留於鉻與銅靶電漿前的時間來
控制試片之雙層週期厚度(Λ)，成功鍍製出 Λ＝5nm 至 40nm 的 Cr2N/Cu 奈米多層
薄膜。以及固定停留電漿前時間，調整鉻與銅靶的功率，成功鍍製出相同雙層週期
厚度為 12nm，具不同雙層週期厚度比例 Cr2N:Cu為 9:1、7:3、5:5與 3:7的 Cr2N/Cu
奈米多層薄膜。 
2. 當薄膜雙層週期厚度為 12nm 時，其硬度、彈性係數具有最大值，分別為 17.3GPa
及 210GPa，而當雙層週期厚度小於或大於 12nm 時，薄膜的硬度、彈性係數都呈
現下降的趨勢，然而多層薄膜之破斷韌性則有相反之趨勢；而不同雙層週期厚度比
例的薄膜硬度與彈性係數，隨著銅層厚度的增加，硬度與彈性係數則下降。 
3. 在雙層週期厚度為 20nm 及 30nm 時，多層薄膜的破斷韌性皆為 1.38MPa m，為
最大值；而雙層週期厚度固定為 12nm的多層薄膜，隨著銅層厚度的增加，破斷韌
性則隨之增加，而雙層週期厚度比例 Cr2N:Cu＝3:7時，薄膜則不產生壓痕破裂。 
4. 當薄膜雙層週期厚度在 8 nm 時，薄膜的具有最低的磨擦係數及較低的磨耗率，分
別為 0.41及 4.04×10-5 mm3/N/m；而具不同雙層週期厚度比例的薄膜，磨擦係數與
磨耗率隨著銅層厚度的增加，由於薄膜中銅層厚度的增加，使磨潤性則更優異。 
5. 由 HRC壓痕試驗及刮痕試驗結果評估多層薄膜的附著性發現，當雙層週期厚度為
12nm 及 40nm 時，薄膜具有較佳的附著性；而具不同雙層週期厚度比例的薄膜，
附著性隨著銅層厚度的增加而更佳優異。 
6. 由抑菌實驗的結果發現，當薄膜雙層週期厚度為 20nm 時，薄膜有 100%之抑菌效
果；且當多層薄膜的雙層週期固定為 12nm 時，薄膜之殺菌效果隨著銅層厚度增加
而增大。 
7. 由腐蝕實驗評估多層薄膜的抗腐蝕性能發現，當雙層週期厚度越小，則有較佳之抗
腐蝕性質。 
週期厚度為 20nm的試片，具有最佳的抗菌性能及硬度，可應用於生醫領域的抑菌防刮
硬質薄膜；而以硬度及耐磨耗性能來說，週期厚度為 8nm 的試片有最佳的表現，可做

5. 由 HRC壓痕試驗及刮痕試驗結果評估多層薄膜的附著性可發現，隨著週期厚度增
加其附著性逐漸變差，在雙層週期厚度為 6nm及 20nm之試片具有較佳的附著性。 
6. 由腐蝕實驗評估多層薄膜的抗腐蝕性能發現，較低雙層週期厚度 L6 及 L10 之試

6.IV鍍膜實驗(IV)- CrSiN/CrN奈米多層薄膜 
1. 本研究以脈衝直流磁控濺鍍法成功製備七種 CrSiN/CrN奈米多層薄膜，其雙層周期
厚度自 5nm至 40nm。CrSiN/CrN奈米多層薄膜皆都具有 CrN(200)的優選取向。 
2. 由表面粗糙度可知週期厚度為 20 nm的試片其 Ra值高於其他試片，推測應該與該
試片之含氧量較高有關。  
3. 當薄膜雙層週期厚度為 40nm 時，其硬度、彈性係數具有最大值，分別為 18.5GPa
及 278.0GPa，而當雙層週期厚度小於 40nm 時，薄膜的硬度、彈性係數都呈現下
降的趨勢，綜合而言，L30試片具有高硬度值與高的破斷韌性，應具有極佳之應用
潛力。 
4. 經電化學腐蝕實驗結果得知，週期厚度為 6 nm與 8 nm的試片有著最低的腐蝕電流
密度，所以抗腐蝕性最佳，而週期厚度 40 nm的試片有著最高的腐蝕電流密度，所
以抗蝕性較差。  
5. 在較小的週期厚度時，薄膜具有較多的總層數，腐蝕溶液需要較長的時間才能由表
面極小的缺陷逐層向下穿透至底材。

Microstructures and Mechanical Properties Evaluation of TiAlN/CrSiN Multilayered Thin Films 
with Different Bilayer Thickness  
Jen-Ming Kao1, Jyh-Wei Lee* 2, Li-Chun Chang2, Shin-Pei Chen1 
1 Institute of Mechatronic Engineering, Tungnan University, Taipei, Taiwan 
2 Department of Materials Engineering, Mingchi University of Technology, Taipei, Taiwan  
 
Abstract 
The Ti0.45Al0.55N/Cr0.75Si0.25N nanoscale multilayered coatings were deposited periodically by a 
bipolar asymmetric pulsed DC reactive magnetron sputtering technique. The structures and bilayer 
period of multilayer coatings were characterized by an X-ray diffractometer. The surface and 
cross-sectional morphologies of thin films were examined by a scanning electron microscopy (SEM) 
and transmission electron microscopy (TEM), respectively. The surface roughness of thin films was 
explored by an atomic force microscopy (AFM). A nanoindenter, a micro Vickers hardness tester and 
pin-on-disk wear tests were used to evaluate the hardness, fracture toughness and tribological 
properties of the thin films, respectively. Six coatings with bilayer period ranges from 6 nm to 40 nm 
were produced in this work. It was observed that the hardness increased with increasing bilayer period 
and reached the maximum at 12nm and then leveled off at periods larger than 12 nm. An optimal 
hardness, plastic deformation resistance and tribological behaviors were found on the coating with a 
critical bilayer thickness of 12 nm. 
Keyword: pulsed DC reactive magnetron sputtering, TiAlN/CrSiN multilayer coating, bilayer period, 
fracture toughness 
1. Introduction 
Since the TiN-based superhard heterostructured multilayered coatings with hardness higher than 
50 GPa was developed by Helmersson et. al, [1] in 1987, researchers and industries have devoted lots 
of efforts to investigate and produce the tribological multilayered coatings due to their pronounced 
strength enhancement, optimal hardness/toughness ratios and excellent wear resistance.  
TiAlN coating has been characterized for its excellent mechanical properties and oxidation 
resistance [2, 3] and becomes a widely used protection coating in cutting tools industries. Chromium 
nitride is a typical transition metal nitride coating with adequate wear, corrosion and oxidation 
resistance. The researches on the TiAlN/CrN multilayered coatings revealed that excellent mechanical 
properties and oxidation resistance [4, 5] were achieved owing to the combination of nanoscale 
multilayered features. The hardness enhancement and the decrease with further decreasing of the 
bilayer period were also observed [5]. The addition of Si to CrN films to improve the mechanical 
properties of Cr-Si-N nanocomposite coatings have been explored successfully [6,7]. For Cr-Si-N 
coating with 10.1 at.% Si, a sufficient mechanical and nanotribological properties including an average 
hardness of 24.6 GPa and friction coefficient of 0.08 has been achieved in previous work [6]. However, 
almost no report on the multilayered TiAlN/CrSiN coatings was revealed. In addition, the dependence 
of the bilayer periods on the mechanical properties and toughness of TiAlN/CrSiN coatings has not yet 
been studied. In this study, TiAlN/CrSiN multilayered thin films were synthesized by a reactive pulsed 
DC magnetron sputtering system. The influence of bilayer periods on the microstructures, mechanical, 
toughness and tribological properties are discussed in this paper. Suitable bilayer period to develop a 
multilayer thin film with adequate mechanical properties and tribological performance are also 
proposed.

wt.%) alloy targets were in opposite positions and substrates were mounted on two sides of a rotating

3.2 Mechanical properties evaluation of TiAlN/CrSiN multilayered thin films 
Figure 6 illustrates the hardness and elastic modulus of multilayered coating as a function of 
bilayer period. The hardness values of single layer TiAlN and CrSiN coatings are also inserted. 
Apparently, the hardness levels off at Λ= 20-40 nm and increases with decreasing bilayer period and 
reaches a maximum value at Λ= 12 nm and, followed by a drastic decrease when the bilayer periods 
decreases to less than 12 nm. A maximum hardness, 35 GPa, was observed for the L12 coating with 12 
nm bilayer period, whereas a minimum hardness, 32.3 GPa, was found for the L10 coating with 10 nm 
bilayer period. On the other hand, the elastic modulus of each multilayered coating is almost similar to 
that of the single TiAlN coating and higher than that of the single CrSiN thin film. The tendency of 
hardness versus bilayer period is not found on the elastic modulus values. 
It is obvious that hardness values of all multilayered coatings are higher than these of single layer 
TiAlN and CrSiN coatings, which are 28.5 GPa and 30.1 GPa, respectively. The plastic deformation 
resistance values, H3/E*2 of TiAlN/CrSiN multilayered coating versus the bilayer periods and single 
layer TiAlN and CrSiN coatings are also shown in Fig. 6, where E* and H are reduced elastic modulus 
and hardness of multilayered coating, respectively. It is found that the same tendency is also observed 
in the relationship between the plastic deformation resistance and bilayer period. The maximum plastic 
deformation resistance, 0.38 GPa, was reached when the bilayer period was 12 nm. Again, the plastic 
deformation resistance value of each multilayered coating is also higher than that of single layer TiAlN 
or CrSiN coating.  
According to previous work, optimal hardness values of Ti1-xAlxN [13,14] and Cr1-xSi1-xN [6] 
coatings can be reached when the x value is around 0.5-0.6 and 0.2-0.25, respectively, which are also 
 12
adjacent to the wear tracks, which are tungsten-rich oxide wear debris identified by EDS. The 
transferred particles and oxides from the steel counter part embedded on the wear track of TiAlN/CrN 
multilayered coating was also reported elsewhere [23]. The entrapment of cemented tungsten carbide 
(WC/Co) ball debris into the hard coatings has been revealed in the wear test of CrN coating in 
previous work [24,25]. Similar result is also found on the L20 and L30 coatings. Lots of cemented 
tungsten carbide (WC/Co) ball debris are trapped in the wear track due to its lower hardness as 
compared with that of the multilayered coatings. It is suggested that the transferred layer, i.e., cemented 
tungsten carbide ball debris, is entrapped and accumulated in the wear track and even breaks up into 
minute fragments due to tribo-oxidation [26]. The transferred wear debris also becomes abrasive 
particles to pull up the WC/Co ball during sliding wear. Therefore, the sliding wear will be changed 
into the plough wear and the COF is thus increased. It is important to notice that almost no spallation or 
cracking of films in the wear tracks were found after pin-on-disk wear tests on all multilayered 
coatings. 
It is reported [27,28] that mechanical properties and toughness of thin films can be improved by 
multilayer structure designs including the type of films, the number and thickness of interlayers, the 
thickness ratio of different interlayers and etc. In this work, the optimal hardness/toughness/tribological 
properties combination is achieved for the Ti0.45Al0.55N/Cr0.75Si0.25N multilayered coating at Λ=12 nm. 
4. Conclusions: 
A series of Ti0.45Al0.55N/Cr0.75Si0.25N multilayered thin films with bilayer periods ranging from 5 to 
40 nm were prepared by the bipolar asymmetric reactive pulsed DC magnetron sputtering system 
successfully. It was observed that the hardness and plastic deformation resistance reached a maximum 
at Λ=12 nm, then leveled off with increasing bilayer period and decreased with decreasing bilayer 
periods. For the multilayered coating at 12 nm bilayer period, an average hardness of 35 GPa, elastic 
modulus of 334 GPa and low COF of 0.43 have been achieved in this work. Meanwhile, good fracture 
toughness of 4.8 MPa m  was obtained for coatings at Λ= 30 nm. It is concluded that the 
Ti0.45Al0.55N/Cr0.75Si0.25N multilayered coatings at 12 nm bilayer periods, a combination of excellent 
mechanical properties, plastic deformation resistance and tribological performance has been achieved 
in this work.

Abstract 
The Cr-Zr-N and Cr-Zr-Si-N thin films with various Zr contents ranging from 0.49 at.% to 13.58 at.% 
were deposited by a bipolar asymmetric pulsed DC reactive magnetron sputtering system. The 
crystalline structure of coatings was determined by a glancing angle X-ray diffractometer. 
Microstructures of thin films were examined by an atomic force microscopy (AFM), scanning electron 
microscopy (SEM) and transmission electron microscopy (TEM), respectively. A nanoindenter, a micro 
Vickers hardness tester and pin-on-disk wear tests were adopted to evaluate the hardness, fracture 
toughness and tribological properties of the thin films, respectively. The columnar structure and grain 
size refinements and the mechanical properties enhancements were observed due to the addition of 6.98 
at.% Si to the Cr-Zr-N coating. A texture evolution was observed when the Zr contents of Cr-Zr-Si-N 
film increased. The hardness and plastic deformation resistance increased with increasing Zr contents 
in the Cr-Zr-Si-N coatings. The Cr-Zr-Si-N coatings with 10.42 at.% Zr content exhibited a 
combination of high hardness, good mechanical properties and tribological performance in this study. 
Keyword: Cr-Zr-Si-N thin film, Cr-Zr-N thin film, pulsed DC reactive magnetron sputtering, 
nanoindentation, fracture toughness, wear 
1. Introduction 
Chromium nitride coating has been characterized for its good oxidation resistance, adequate wear 
performance and mechanical properties. Recently, according to the concept of superhard 
nanocomposite hard coatings proposed by Vepřek and Reiprich in 1995 [1], the addition of Si to form 
amorphous silicon nitride phase has been adopted to improve the mechanical properties and oxidation 
resistance of binary chromium nitride coatings successfully [2-5]. On the other hand, the addition of Zr 
to the chromium nitride films also provides an alternative of improving the mechanical properties [6], 
corrosion resistance [7,8] and high temperature oxidation [9] performance of Cr-Zr-N coatings. The 
research work on the effect of further addition of Si on the characteristics of Cr-Zr-Si-N coatings is 
limited [10,11]. The influences of Si on the thermal stability, tribological properties and corrosion 
resistance of Cr-Zr-Si-N thin films were explored [10,11]. Nevertheless, the effects of Zr contents on 
the microstructures, mechanical and tribological properties of Cr-Zr-Si-N coatings have not yet been 
investigated. 
In this work, the ternary Cr-Zr-N and quaternary Cr-Zr-Si-N thin films with various Zr contents 
were prepared by a pulsed DC magnetron sputtering system. The influences of Si and Zr on the phase, 
microstructure, mechanical and tribological properties of Cr-Zr-Si-N films were investigated. 
2. Experimental Procedure 
The Cr-Zr-N and Cr-Zr-Si-N thin films were deposited on a p-type (100) silicon wafer substrate 
by a bipolar asymmetry pulsed DC reactive magnetron sputtering system. Detailed description of the 
sputtering system has been reported elsewhere [5]. The pure Zr (99.99 wt.%) and a 90Cr-10Si (both in 
wt.%) alloy targets were adopted to place in the same side of the chamber to co-deposit the Cr-Zr-Si-N 
coatings, whereas the pure Zr and Cr (99.99 wt.%) targets was used to deposit the Cr-Zr-N film. A 
pulse frequency of 20 kHz was used during sputtering. A pulsing bias of -100V and a pulse unit with 2 
kHz frequency was applied to substrates. No attempt was made to coordinate the pulse cycles. Sample 
 20
Cr-Zr-N and Cr-Zr-Si-N coatings versus the Zr content is depicted in Fig. 2. The lattice constant, a0, 
increases from 0.419 nm to 0.430 nm as the Zr content increases from 0.49 to 13.58 at.%, which is 
attributed to the substitution effect of Zr to the positions of Cr atoms in CrN crystal lattice since the 
lattice structures of CrN and ZrN are the same (fcc) and the atomic radius of Zr (0.161 nm) is larger 
than that of Cr (0.136 nm). 
It is obvious that the lattice expansion of #A specimen is about 0.005 nm larger as compared with 
that of pure CrN, 0.414 nm. Clearly, the addition of Si atoms to the CrN film shows limited effect on 
the lattice expansion. It is suggested that the addition of Si concentration up to 6.98 at.% is only 
partially dissolved in the CrN lattice and the rest of Si atoms will form the amorphous SixNy phase [11]. 
Sandu et al. [14] proposed a film formation model for the solubility limit of Si atoms in the fcc MeSiN 
(Me= Ti, Cr, Nb) ternary system. For Si contents less than 4 at.%, Si atoms substitute Nb atoms in the 
fcc NbN lattice. While the solubility limit of Si atoms is exceeded (4 ≤ Si content ≤7 at.%), some Si 
atoms start to segregate at the crystallite surface [14]. In previous work [5], a comparable solubility 
limit of Si atoms in the Cr-Si-N thin films around 3.1 to 4.1 at.% was also reported. On the other hand, 
for the #A and #C coatings, the lattice constant values are 0.4220 nm and 0.4224 nm, respectively, 
indicating the lattice expansion is not strongly related to the Si addition in #C specimen. The average 
grain size of Cr-Zr-N and Cr-Zr-Si-N coatings on growth direction versus the Zr content are shown in 
Fig. 3. The grain size of Cr-Zr-N film (#A) is 7.99 nm, whereas the grain size values of Cr-Zr-Si-N 
coatings decreases from 5.37 nm to 4.63 nm as the Zr at.% increases from 0.49 to 13.58 at.%. 
Compared with the grain size of #A and #C coatings, it is clear that the grain size refining effect by 
addition of Si is obvious, which has been reported elsewhere [4, 5,14,15].  
3.2 Microstructures of Cr-Zr-N and Cr-Zr-Si-N thin films 
The AFM surface morphology of each coating illustrates a typical fine granular structure. In 
general, the surface roughness of Cr-Zr-Si-N coatings decreases with increasing Zr content, which is 
also listed in Table 1. Similar result was also reported in the Cr-Zr-N coatings [6]. The cross-sectional 
SEM images of #A, #C and #E coatings are shown in Figs. 4(a) to (c), respectively. The thickness of 
each coating is around 850 nm. In Fig. 4(a), a typical coarse columnar structure is observed in the #A 
coating without Si addition, whereas a more dense structure is found in the #C coating containing 6.98 
at.% Si in Fig. 4(b). A dense and compact cross-sectional morphology is clearly revealed in the #E 
coating containing 10.42 at.% Zr shown in Fig. 4(c). Effects of Si addition on reducing the width of 
columnar structure and further formation of nano crystallites surrounded by amorphous matrix in 
Cr-Si-N [3,5,14,16] and Cr-Zr-N coatings [6] have also been discussed clearly. In this work, the effect 
of reducing width of columnar structure on Si addition to the Cr-Zr-N coating is also confirmed.  
Detailed cross-sectional microstructures of #C coatings were further revealed by TEM images 
shown in Figs. 5. Very fine columnar structure and dense CrZrSi interlayer are observed in Fig. 5(a). 
Fine and compact columnar structure around 20-30 nm wide is further revealed in Fig.5 (b). Two (111) 
arcs of electron diffraction patterns shown in the upper right corner in Fig. 5(b) indicate a strong 
preferred orientation of the film structure. 
3.3 Mechanical properties evaluation of coatings 
The relationship between average hardness, elastic modulus and Zr content of the films are 
shown in Fig.6. For the Cr-Zr-N coating (#A), the hardness is around 18.9 GPa, whereas the 
hardness value of Cr-Zr-Si-N #C specimen containing the same Zr amount and 6.98 at.% Si is 
21.7 GPa. Apparently, the addition of Si to the Cr-Zr-N film will enhance the hardness effectively. 
It is suggested that the hardening effect is caused by the grain boundary refining, i.e., the 
Hall-Petch relation [17,18] and the fine columnar structure shown in Figs. 4(b) and 5(b).  
Meanwhile, the hardness of Cr-Zr-Si-N coatings increases from 21.7 GPa to 24.0 GPa as the 
Zr content increases from 0.49 at.% to 13.58 at.%, which is attributed to the solid solution 
strengthening effect of Zr. Similar results was reported in the Cr-Zr-N [6] and Cr-Zr-Si-N [11] 
films. However, the effect of Zr content on the elastic modulus of Cr-Zr-Si-N thin films exhibits 
 22
fracture toughness and plastic deformation resistance were also obvious. For the Cr-Zr-Si-N films, a 
texture change from CrN(111) to (200) was observed when the Zr contents increased from 0.49 to 
13.58 at.%. The lattice constant and hardness values of Cr-Zr-Si-N coatings increased with increasing 
Zr contents owing to the substitution effect and solid solution strengthening effects of Zr atoms, 
respectively. Good tribological property was achieved for the Cr-Zr-N and Cr-Zr-Si-N coatings. No 
delamination or film crack was observed besides the wear track and only some tungsten-rich oxide 
wear debris was found on the wear tracks. 
For the Cr-Zr-Si-N coating with a Zr content of 10.42 at.%, good mechanical and tribological 
properties including an average hardness of 24.0 GPa, plastic deformation resistance of 0.4 GPa and 
friction coefficient of 0.51 was achieved in this study.

Influences of Annealing on the Microstructures and Mechanical Properties of Cr2N/Cu 
Multilayered Thin Films 
Chia-Lin Li1, Li-Chun Chang2, Jeh-Wei Lee1, 3, Jen–Ching Huang1, 3, 4 
1Institute of Mechatronic Engineering, Tungnan University, Taiwan. 
2Dept. of Materials Engineering, Mingchi University of Technology, Taiwan. 
3Dept. of Mechanical Engineering, Tungnan University, Taiwan. 
4Research Center for Micro/ Nanotechnology, Tungnan University. 
Abstract 
Nanostructured Cr2N/Cu multilayer coatings with bilayer thickness ranges from 5 nm to 40 nm 
were deposited periodically by a bipolar asymmetric pulsed DC reactive magnetron sputtering 
technique. A rapid thermal annealing at 450oC and 500oC for 8 min were further applied, respectively. 
The structures of annealed multilayer coatings were characterized by an X-ray diffractometer. The 
cross sectional morphologies of annealed thin films were examined by a scanning electron microscopy 
(SEM). The surface roughness of thin films after annealing was explored by an atomic force 
microscopy (AFM). The nanohardness and elastic modulus of annealed multilayer coatings were 
investigated by means of a nanoindenter . The fracture toughness values of annealed coatings were 
further evaluated by microhardness tests. The coalescence of Cu nano layers into Cu particles was 
observed after annealing. It is found that hardness and toughness of coatings were influenced 
effectively by the annealing temperature. A hardness enhancement was found on the coating after 
annealing at 450oC, and decreased after annealing at 500oC. The toughness of multilayered coatings 
seriously decreases after annealing. The influences of annealing on the microstructures and mechanical 
properties of Cr2N/Cu multilayered thin films were discussed in this paper.  
1. Introduction 
One of many important properties on the industrial applications of superhard coating is thermal 
stability [1]. To protect tools or dies in high temperature applications, chromium nitride coatings are 
good choice [2-3] due to their excellent thermal properties. On the other hand, many researchers have 
devoted much effort to metal/transition metal nitride multilayered coatings, such as Cr2N/Cu [4-5], 
Cr/CrN [6] and Mo/NbN [7] were also prepared to obtain both high hardness and toughness, in which 
the soft metal and the hard nitride provided ductility and hardness, respectively. In previous work [4], 
the Cr2N/Cu multilayered coating with bilayer thickness ranges from 5 nm to 40 nm exhibited a 
combination of high hardness, good tribological and anticorrosion performance. Moreover, efforts have 
been devoted to study the microstructures and mechanical properties after annealing on the such 
multilayered coatings [8,9]. In previous report [8], the structures of the W/ZrN multilayer coatings after 
annealing at 1000oC for 1h were quite stable, and the hardness increased around 8GPa after annealing 
at 750oC for 1h. Although lots of efforts have been devoted to study the microstructures and 
mechanical properties on the multilayered coatings [5,8-10], a fundamental understanding of the 
toughness of such multilayered coatings is still lacking. 
In this study, the Cr2N/Cu multilayered thin films with different bilayer period were synthesized 
by a reactive pulsed DC magnetron sputtering system. The influences of annealing on the 
microstructures and mechanical properties of Cr2N/Cu multilayered thin films were discussed in this 
paper. 
2. Experimental Procedure 
The nanostructured Cr2N/Cu multilayer thin films were deposited by the bipolar asymmetry 
reactive pulsed DC magnetron sputtering system. The processing details were revealed in previous 
work [4]. Some deposited films are annealed for 8 min at 450 oC and 500 oC respectively using a rapid 
RTA system (SJ, ARTS-150) with ramping rate at 40 oC/s. The annealing temperature was determined 
according to a report by Hsieh et. Al [10] on the TaN-Cu nanocomposite thin films. They found that a 
suitable temperature at which the as-deposited Cu atoms should diffuse and form particles in the matrix 
 30
annealing on the mechanical properties of Cr2N/Cu multilayered thin films. The hardness increases 
after annealing at 450 oC and reaches a maximum value at Λ= 20 nm. The hardness enhancement is due 
to the higher degree of crystallization of films after annealing at 450 oC. However, after annealing at 
500 oC, the hardness decreases and almost levels off at 16GPa on each coating. In previous research on 
the W/ZrN multilayered thin films by S.A. Barnett et. Al [8], it also can be found similar results. And 
they suggested a reason that the hardness values were decreased at higher annealing temperature due to 
a relaxation of compressive stresses.  
3.2.2 Fracture Toughness 
The fracture toughness of as-deposited Cr2N/Cu multilayered coating and annealed at 450 oC and 
500 oC were also shown in Fig. 4, respectively. The KIC values of L5 and L6 coatings were unable to 
evaluate after annealing at 500 oC, and there was no mark in Fig. 4. This was because the radial crack 
length was too long and messy to calculate the values, even if the applied indentation load was smaller 
than 0.3 N. The surface morphologies of radial cracks on as-deposited L40 coating and annealed at 450 
and 500 oC with 1N , 1N and 0.3N indentation loads are illustrated in Figs. 5(a), (b) and (c), 
respectively. It can be observed that the crack lengths are getting longer after annealing at 450 and 500 
oC. In previous work, the toughness of Cr2N/Cu[4] and TiC/Cu[12] multilayered coatings increased 
with increasing bilayer period due to a greater deformation tolerance provided by the softer and ductile 
Cu metal layers. In this study, it is found the toughness of multilayered coatings seriously decreases 
after annealing. It might be attributed that a part of the soft Cu layer in the multilayered structure is 
diffused to induce that there is not enough Cu layer to bear the deformation. 
4. Conclusion 
The Cr2N/Cu multilayered thin films exhibit good high-temperature stability in the multilayered 
structure. And after annealing at 500 oC, the multilayered thin films are with the higher degree of 
crystallization, inducing that the surface roughness increases. It is observed that the hardness increases 
after annealing at 450 oC and decreases at 500 oC. The fracture toughness seriously decreases after 
high-temperature annealing. It presumes that a part of the soft Cu layer in the multilayered structure is 
diffused to induce that there is not enough Cu layer to bear the deformation.
