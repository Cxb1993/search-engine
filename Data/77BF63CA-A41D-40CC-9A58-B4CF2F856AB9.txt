ABSTRACT 
This study firstly prepares lightweight aggregate (LWA) with simulated reservoir 
sediment by combining and thoroughly mixing together six major components of 
reservoir sediment to understand the role of major bloating compounds.  The LWA 
preparation process was carried out at 9001150oC for 20 minutes.  Secondly 
feasibility of sintering/bloating the mixtures of municipal waste incinerator (MSI) fly 
ash additive and the reservoir sediment to prepare LWA with concomitant heavy metal 
immobilization was investigated.  The sintered/bloated samples were studied with 
inductively coupled plasma atomic emission spectrometry (ICP-AES), multipurpose 
X-ray powder diffractometry (XRD), environmental scanning electron microscopy 
(SEM), thermal gravimetric analyzer/differential scanning calorimeter (TGA/DSC), 
X-ray absorption spectroscopy (XAS), and others.  The results indicate that the 
simulative sediments could be bloated and sintered readily at 1100?1150oC as 
evidenced with SEM and TGA/DSC.  X-ray absorption near edge structure (XNAES) 
simulation of the LWA samples shows that iron oxide might have chemically reacted 
with potassium oxide, sodium oxide, as well as with calcium oxide to form new Fe 
species.  Apart from that, portion of Fe2O3 was reduced to Fe3O4.  It is suggested 
that silica and alumina are inert to the chemical reaction with Fe2O3.  Results of 
molecular structural parameter based on the inverse of Fourier transform evidence the 
existence of Fe-O-K coordination shell.  Other than the chemical reaction with Fe2O3, 
both Na2O and K2O lower the sintering temperature during the LWA preparation 
process.  Inverse of Fourier transform from high-temperature (1100 oC) reaction 
product of Fe2O3 + Na2O or CaO samples fails to confirm the existence of Fe-O-Na 
or –Ca coordination shell.  For the effect of MSI fly ash on sintering/bloating
