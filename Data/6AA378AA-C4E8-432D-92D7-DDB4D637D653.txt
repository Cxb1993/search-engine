  
研究計畫中英文摘要：  
本計畫是總計畫”高性能鎂基奈米/非晶質材料”的四個子計畫之一,被設計來針
對下列這幾項提供一個第二代持續三年的系統性之探討,: 
(1)新的先進合金系統之合金設計 
(2)以熱力學的相圖,分子動力模擬在原子混合/原子半徑分佈方程,壓縮或奈米 
   壓痕的彈性回饋,等方面進行數值化模擬。 
(3)針對高解析的原子排列特性描述,微結構的演變,熱力學上的反應,機械性質, 
   成型性/加工性。 
(4)脆性鎂基奈米晶質和非晶質合金或複合材料的韌化 
(5)超塑性黏滯流動行為,微細成型,press forming,粉末製作/固結成型及濺鍍。 
(6)尋找開發出之高性能鎂基材料的新應用。 
 
被選出來探討之合金系統為 Mg-Cu-Gd 和與其相關的系統。如:(1) Mg-Cu-Gd or 
Mg-Cu-Ag-Gd, (2) Mg-Cu-Y or Mg Cu-Ag-Y, (3) Mg-Cu-Nd or Mg-Cu-Ag-Nd, and (4) 
Mg-Al-Zn 等。合金成份組成的範圍上,嘗試由 Mg50-70-Cu(Ag)20-35-Gd(Y,Nd)5-15 開
始進行尋找。 
 
本子計畫將著眼於以噴覆成型和粉末固結製作高性能鎂基奈米非晶片板狀複合
材料。在第一年,＂ThermoCalc Analysis＂被用來為所有子計畫進行合金設計以及其
他相關之目的。使用我們最新研發的美國專利＂雙氣流霧化噴覆成型＂的技術,來直
接製作奈米晶質和非晶質複合片板狀材料。噴覆成型合金的熱性質和微結構之特性將
被分析。噴覆成型製程及其他子計畫以不同製程所製造的材料之微觀結構及機械性質
和成型性/加工性將會被探討。 
 
在第二年, ＂ThermoCalc Analysis＂被用來為所有子計畫進行合金設計以及其
他相關之目的。使用我們最新研發的美國專利＂雙氣流霧化＂的技術,來直接製作奈
米晶質和非晶質複合片板狀材料。粉末固結成型合金的熱性質和微結構之特性將被分
析。粉末固結成型製程及其他子計畫,以不同製程所製造的材料之微觀結構及機械性
質和成型性/加工性將會被探討。 
 
對於片狀材料, press forming是一種極為有效地,可將其製成各種適合人類使用
的形狀之技術。例如:鎂合金的手機外殼已在商業上利用 press forming 來製作。因
此,在第三年,先前兩年以噴覆成型和粉末固結成型的片狀材料,將利用 press-forming
製成雛型產品,並探討其 press formability。 
 
濺鍍靶材將被用來作為前兩年以噴覆成型和粉末固結成型製作的板狀材料的應用。這是因
為各式的靶材均已經在商業上可由板狀材料製作。此外,本計畫所開發之合金所製作的靶材
亦將被濺鍍為厚膜與薄膜的材料。因此,本計畫發展出之大塊的材料及厚/薄膜材料均能在
此三年的整合型計畫裡被廣泛的探討。

成型利用高壓氮氣瞬間將熔湯霧化成半固態之液滴，而飛行中的液滴再沉積於旋轉中的銅
基座上。最後液滴之熱量再經由旋轉之銅模以及液態氮導出，而達到快速凝固之目的冷卻
速率(>100oC/s)。利用噴覆成型製程，由於材料成形過程沒有受到模具尺寸上的限制，因此
能夠形成較大尺寸的板狀材料。成形大尺寸板狀材料之優勢在於能夠在機械性質測試上以
及材料成形性測試上作更多的探討及實驗研究。並且在未來更能夠製作成份均勻之非晶質
合金粉末以及靶材。 
 本研究課題著重在高溫非晶質的成型性(workability)，分為兩種形式的材料同時進行：
(a)利用噴射鑄造法(copper mould injection casting)所得到的棒材；(b)本實驗室專利之先進高
冷卻速率氣體霧化設備將熔融之合金經由高壓氣體（ex:N2）霧化成細小顆粒狀，而又以液
態氮冷卻機制，使得冷卻速率可達 101～102K/s，易於製作非晶質粉末。因為衡量製作非晶
質塊材的冷卻能力受限鑄造設備，故非晶質粉末以粉末冶金（Powder metallurgy, PM）的方
式製作出塊狀非晶質材料（Bulk Metallic Glasses, BMG）可以克服尺寸上的限制，而典型粉
末冶金製程常見有擠型（Extrusion）、熱壓（Hot pressing）和燒結（Sintering）[18]，均可
運用上述技術得到高密度非晶質塊材。故非晶質粉末部份以熱壓製作生胚後再以擠型獲得
良好機械性質之非晶質/結晶複合材。

2.1 利用噴射鑄造法製作 Mg65Cu25Gd10 棒材與擠型試驗 
2.1.1 Mg65Cu25Gd10 棒材 
Mg65Cu25Gd10 的鑄錠是首先將純度 99.9%銅跟 99.9%釓在氬氣保護下利用電弧融煉混
合在一起，將 Cu-Gd 圓錠翻面，重覆融煉 4 次。將純度 99.95%Mg 跟 Cu-Gd 放在石墨坩堝
內，再利用高週波爐通入氬氣保護下進行預鑄，再把 Mg-Cu-Gd 的預鑄錠敲碎，秤取適量
的料放於石英管內利用噴射鑄造法製作直徑為 6mm 的棒材。非晶質玻璃相的鑑定由 x-ray 
diffraction(XRD)利用 Cu 的單色 Kα輻射光(monochromatic Cu Kα radiation)來決定。熱性質
包括玻璃轉移溫度(Tg)、過冷液體區(△Tx)、開始結晶溫度(Tx)、開始熔化溫度(Tm)及結束熔
化溫度(Tl)則是用示差掃描卡計(differential scanning calorimetry，DSC)，升溫速率為 0.67 
k/s，並計算判斷玻璃形成能力的參數 T△ x、Trg = (Tg/Tl)、γ = Tx/(Tg+Tl)[19]、γm = (2Tx-Tg)/Tl 
[20]，以及利用 TA Instruments Univeral Analysis 軟體計算完成結晶所放出的熱。利用 Hitachi 
Table-1000 SEM 觀察 Mg65Cu25Gd10 表面型態。微硬度量測由 Micro-Vickers hardness，測試
條件為荷重 300gf，鑽石壓頭接觸時間 15 秒，並以 SEM 的 BEI 影像觀察 Vickers 壓痕周圍
尖端裂縫長度，以三個以上的壓痕出現裂縫長度作為平均的裂縫長度，可得破裂韌性
（Fracture toughness, Kc）。熱膨脹(收縮)性質則是用熱機械分析儀(Thermal mechanical 
analyzer, TMA)固定荷重 50mN，以升溫速率 5℃ /min 至 300℃下進行；茲簡述
TMA(Thermomechanical analyzer)使用原理，一般 TMA 可用來檢測金屬材料(1)玻璃轉移溫
度( Tg ) (2)膨脹係數 ( CTE ) (3)軟化點 ( Softening temperature ) (4)應力應變 ( Stress & 
Strain) (5)機械黏彈性質 (Modulus/Viscosity) (6)潛變復原 ( Creep & Relaxation)。TMA 儀器
其檢測原理: 在非振動性的壓力下測量樣品隨著溫度變化所產生的形狀變化之熱分析法。

4mm 及高度 8mm 的棒材進行不同應變速率(5×10-2、5×10-3、5×10-4)。不同位置的硬度使用

毫米，並且再用 XRD 檢測其是否為非晶質。

內部，為粉末結合強度不良區域，見 Fig 49 為示意圖標示出粉末結合不佳區域，Fig 49(b-c)
可明顯對比出粉末之間的邊界，依據物理冶金知識，明顯擴散發生約需在熔點一半溫度以
上才足以使粉末產生冶金化學鍵結。因此即使 BEI 無法觀察到粉末結合良好區之粉末邊
界，粉末仍以機械鎖合方式堆積而已。造成粉末之間邊界情況差異為溫度，因內部粉末受
熱不如外部多，外部粉末已達容易變形之過冷區，但內部粉末熱量不足，造成變形較困難，
因此變形容易之外部粉末在此成型壓力下可彼此變形堆積成一緊密胚體，而內部粉末可能
僅稍微變形，粉末之間仍存在孔隙跟細縫，此也可以用以解釋為何高度較低熱壓胚體其密
度較高，因在有限加熱時間內，較少粉末可以讓粉末結合不良區域減少，如此可增加胚體
密度。 
Fig 50 為高度 20mm 胚體室溫壓縮測試，strain rate=5×10-4 s-1，壓縮強度約為 130MPa，
與一般鎂基非晶質材料 800MPa 差距甚大[6]，Fig 51 為壓縮測試後胚體為劈裂方式碎裂，
並非為非晶質材料特有之 45°斷裂，此也再度證明粉末結合性並不佳，其壓縮測試展現強度
推測為高緻密度時之機械鎖合現象。 
將高度 10mm 胚體軸向截面做維氏硬度測試，荷重（Load）300gf，下壓時間（Dwell 
time）15s，結果見 Fig 52，圖上數字代表該點之 Vickers hardness。靠近胚體下部分所承受
之熱壓成形壓力，因粉末與模具之間摩擦力抵銷下部分胚體成形壓力，產生壓降（pressure 
drop），造成下部分胚體其緻密度較差，此也反應出 Vickers hardness 結果見 Table 3。即使
是胚體下部分之 Vickers hardness 較低約為 257.8 Kg/mm2，與文獻上此組成（Mg65Cu25Gd10）
之 Vickers hardness＝232Kg/mm2[25]比較仍然高出了約 25.8 Kg/mm2，說明粉末冶金高密度
製程優勢，也再一次證實粉末彼此之間有產生密切之機械鎖合現象。Fig 53 為維氏硬度壓

圖可看出亮灰色且不規則形顆粒狀由未擠型區域往擠型區域有明顯增多趨勢，推測此顆粒
可能結晶，符合上述應變誘發結晶推測，但在 BEI 下觀察並不如 DSC 下這麼明顯，此可能
原因為部分結晶相組成與基地相類似，使得在 BEI 下對比不明顯，或是結晶相尺寸為奈米
等級以 BEI 解析度並無法觀察到，因此改以 HR-SEM 觀察，見 Fig 57，發現不少的結晶相。 
     對不同擠型部位做維氏硬度測試，各部位取打五點取平均做圖見 Fig 58。可明顯看出
維氏硬度從熱壓胚體約為 263.5 Kg/mm2 提高到 R＝4 時 Unextruded 部分 315.3 Kg/mm2，Die 
angle 部分 320.1 Kg/mm2，extruded 部分 346.8 Kg/mm2，R＝11.1 時 Unextruded 部分
332.3Kg/mm2，Die angle 部分 350 Kg/mm2，extruded 部分 368.8 Kg/mm2，較塊材擠型維氏
硬度 320 Kg/mm2 高出 7～13%，可看出粉末冶金優勢。再由維氏硬度壓痕（Fig 59），平均
各菱角裂紋長度 C。利用式(1)求 extruded 之破裂韌性 Kc，列表於 Table 14 比較之，當 R＝
4 時 Kc＝0.64 MPa/m2，R＝11.1 時 Kc＝0.75 MPa/m2，均較熱壓塊材 Kc＝0.41 MPa/m2 高，
其原因可能為所產生結晶相阻礙了 shear bands移動，且此結晶特性為一韌性，此可能為XRD
觀察到之具有延性鎂或其合金造成，但並不包含 Mg2Cu 相，因其特徵為脆性無法使材料變
韌[32]。因此在非晶質基地內引進適量之結晶相，會有助於提昇非晶質材料之韌性。 
     上述製程先熱壓在擠型，雖加工時間均控制在結晶孕核時間內，雖基地相結晶趨勢已
經很明顯，但卻能有助於增加材料韌性。 
 
4 結論 
4.1 噴射鑄造法製作 Mg65Cu25Gd10棒材 
4.1.1 噴射鑄造 d=6mm 棒材的 XRD pattern 在繞射角(two theta)30°~40°出現寬化的峰值，
可以確定為非晶質的結構。 
4.1.2 DSC 曲線顯示噴射鑄造 d=6mm 的棒材的 Tg= =432 K 及 Tx=490 K，△Tx、Trg、γ、
γm 依序為 58、0.600、0.420、0.756，與[7]( △Tx、Trg、γ、γm 依序為 69、0.570、
0.423、0.757)比較，GFA 非常接近，顯示本研究 Mg65Cu25Gd10 棒材玻璃形成能力
良好。 
4.1.3 噴射鑄造的棒材其平均微硬度為 276.3(kg/mm2)，噴射鑄造棒材壓痕平均對角線長
依序為 44.84μm、40.01μm。 
4.1.4 噴射鑄造的棒材其 Kc 經計算為 0.74 MPa/m2 
4.1.5 噴射鑄造 Mg65Cu25Gd10 棒材，藉由 TMA 量測，其熱膨脹係數 αL=1.5*10-5 m/m k. 。 
 18 
 
Mg65Cu25Gd10 非晶質粉末。 
4.4.2 利用 P/M 熱壓製程可成功製作出直徑 10mm 高度 20mm 仍為非晶質結構之棒材。
其維氏硬度 257.8 Kg/mm2，破裂韌性 0.41MPa/m2 
4.4.3 將熱壓後塊材進行熱擠型，已可成功製得 5mm 及 3mm 的非晶質/結晶複合棒材，
對應其擠型比分別為 4 和 11.1。其結晶相主要為 Mg2Cu，以及 Mg。 
4.4.4 對擠型比 4 而言，其維氏硬度由熱壓材 257.8 Kg/mm2 提高到擠型材 348.8Kg/mm2，
其破裂韌性則由 0.41 MPa/m2 提高到 0.64 MPa/m2。對擠型比 11.1 而言，其維氏硬
度由熱壓材 257.8 Kg/mm2 提高到擠型材 368.8Kg/mm2。 
4.4.5 在非晶質基地內引進適量之結晶相，會有助於提昇非晶質材料之韌性。 
 
計畫自評表 
藉由去年所得到的經驗，由噴射鑄造棒材及粉末熱壓與擠型製程了解粉末加工的成形
性。找出去年擠型所發生的問題並加以修正跟改造，並且今年利用在本特殊製程實驗室所
擁有的專利—噴覆成型來製作出的非晶質鎂基塊材來進行擠型試驗，在模具上更由正向擠
型轉換至背向擠型，帶著這幾年本實驗室在非晶領域不斷增加的經驗，以及實驗室各個成
員的努力，藉由不同的分析設備，找到了適當地成型範圍，更瞭解了物理及其機械性質，
加廣學術上的研究，找到了適當地成型性，也在應用方面前進了一步。

Rene Baadjou, Federik Knauf and Gerhard Hirt gave a talk on “Investigations on 
Thermal Influences for Thixoforging and Thixojoing of Steel Components,” which is 
mainly concentrated on forming of semi-solid precursor material of the steel grades 
X210CrW12 and 100Cr6. One important task is the precise temperature for the 
composition of solid and liquid phase fraction in the preheated billet. Experimental 
measurements and numerical simulations show significant heat losses during transport 
of the billet and after its insertion into the die. These developing temperature gradients 
influence strongly the resulting temperature field in the formed component.  In case 
of the forming tools the critical increase of the temperature depending on the tool 
material is shown. As fundamental research in the field of thixojoining the 
temperature development of the inserts is analysed and demonstrates the feasibility of 
joining higher and lower melting materials into the semi-solid matrix. 
 
Hagen Frank, Norbert Hort, Hajo Dieringa and Karl ulrich Kainer gave a talk on 
“Influence of Processing Route on the Properties of Magnesium Alloys.”  They 
indicated that Magnesium alloys had gained an increasing interest in recent years due 
to their promising property profile for light weight constructions. They offer drastic 
advantages in weight reductions in automotive industries compared to steel or even 
aluminum. Therefore they can be used to decrease the emission of green house gases 
as requested by the EU directive for the reduction of CO2 emissions, and moreover, 
due to their recyclability they also help to fulfill the requirements from the EU 
directive regarding the end of life of vehicles. But still there are some limitations with 
regard to strength, mostly at elevated temperatures above 130℃. To overcome these 
limitations alloy development as well as process optimization has to be done for 
further enhancement of the range of magnesium applications. This paper shows and 
discusses the property profiles of the standard magnesium alloy AZ91D compared to 
the recently developed, heat resistant magnesium alloy MRI153. The alloys have been 
processed using normal high pressure die casting (HPDC), New Rheocasting (NRC) 
and Thixomolding (TM). As methods of investigation tensile and creep tests have 
been applied. The creep properties have been determined in the temperature range of 
135-150℃ and loads of 50-85 MPa. All these trials have been accompanied by 
metallographic observations (light optical metallography, SEM) and density 
measurements to investigate the influence of the processing routes on microstructure 
and the porosity of the materials. It will be shown that the differences in the property 
profile of the chosen alloys are dependent on their different chemical compositions as 
well as on different microstructures tha are obtained by the different processing 
p. 7 of 10 
conventional parameters, such as globule size and shape factor, and the solidification 
range, by means of differential scanning calorimetry. To complete the characterization 
of this new alloy and to evaluate its applicability in industrial production, the shear 
rate and time-dependent flow behavior of the alloy in the semisolid state was studied 
in a Searle-type rheometer. A future aim of the present research is to try to use 
rheology testing as the tool to optimize the chemical composition, in order to design 
an alloy characterized by good mechanical performances and easy processability. 
Considering the strong influence of the solid fraction content on semisolid alloy 
viscosity, the rheology tests were interrupted after a certain time and the alloy was 
deeply freezed using vaporized liquid nitrogen, in order to fix the microstructure and 
verify the correctness of the thermodynamic simulation. 
 
H. Mehrara, M. Nili-Ahmadabadi, B. Heidariam, S. Ashourl and J. Ghiasinejad 
gave a talk on “Modeling of Inclined Cooling Plate Semisolid Processing by Model 
Alloy.”  They indicated that continual improvement of product quality has been a 
long challenge to Semi-Solid Metal(SSM) technology. By conventional semi-solid 
processes, this might be attained at the expense of economical production. The advent 
of Inclined Cooling Plate (ICP) process has already realized the development of 
non-dendritic SSM while satisfying qualitative, quantitative and economical 
requirements collectively. In spite of its potential advantages, functional mechanisms 
of this process are not yet clearly understood that makes its optimal utilization 
obscured.  Basically, such understanding needs a picture of the process. As the first 
stop, this picture is pursed through physical modeling of the ICP process i.e. direct 
observation of an analog system by virtue of transparent character of a model alloy 
(succinonitrile-acetone). Based on this phenomenological model, a picture of the 
process is presented as follows: flowing molten alloy down ICP, multiple regions 
form typically on the plate i.e. a chilled layer at the vicinity of the plate surface, a 
two-phase mushy zone on the chilled layer and ambient liquid far from the plate 
surface. In this process, interaction of the liquid forced-flow with mushy zone 
separates solid particles from the stationary mush on the plate resulting in a two-phase 
mixture which is responsible for the formation of slurry i.e. SSM. 
 
 
 
Sumio Sugiyama and Jun Yanagimoto gave a talk on “Feasibility of Recycling 
Aluminum Alloy Scrap by Semisolid Extrusion,” in which the feasibility of recycling 
machining grindings of aluminum alloys by semisolid process has been investigated. 
Machining grindings of A2011 aluminum alloy produced experimentally by lathe 
machining were used. The material is put into a metal mold and compressed up to

semi-solid slurry in the ladle during the ladle transfer to the shot sleeve. The 
semi-solid slurry is then poured directly into the shot sleeve. This paper presents the

 S. Muenstermann, R. Telle, F. Knauf and G. Hirt gave a talk on “Semi-solid 
Extrusion of Complex-shaped Steel Rods Using Ceramic Dies,” in which isothermal 
semi-solid extrusion experiments using steel grade X210TCrW12 as walk material 
were performed on an industrial forging machine. An improved and up-scaled tool 
design was applied, based on the concept of self-heating ceramic dies tested in 
previous laboratory-scale studies and allowing for die preheating temperatures of up 
to1400℃ . Steel rods of complex cross sectional geometry were formed at low 
extrusion forces. Shape accuracy of as-formed rods is accurate and metallurgical 
examination yields no evidence of liquid phase separation. With a view on the 
intended industrial implementation construction of ceramic tools has to be improved 
in order to avoid rupture of ceramic parts due to thermomechanically induced stresses 
and benefit from the advantages inherent to ceramics in metal forming. 
 
D. J. Browne, M. Scanlan and A. Bates gave a talk on “Functionally Gradient 
Materials via a Casting Process involving Partial Solidification,” in which a novel 
technique for the production of functionally gradient materials, developed by the 
authors, is presented. The process is known as Cast-Decant-Cast or CDC process, and 
involves a partial solidification step. This process takes expensive and 
time-consuming factors out of the production of functionally gradient materials by 
enabling utilization of standard foundry equipment, and the process is carried out in a 
single multi-step casting operation. The CDC process involves simultaneous but 
separate melting of two alloys of different composition in order to produce a gradient 
in material properties. Details of the process are presented. It is shown that the 
outcome depends on partial local remelting and alloy mixing, which results in a 
gradual change or gradient between the first and second alloys in the as-cast condition, 
hence production functionally gradient materials. The process has been adapted to 
conventional casting methods such as gravity casting and low pressure casting. It is 
the method of decanting the first material from the mould that differentiates the 
process variants. The decanting step for the low pressure method is controlled by 
pressure application and release on the molten alloys within their respective, but 
separate, sealed holding chambers. Decanting the gravity casting method as a physical
