ABSTRACT 
This work shows the analysis for free vibrations of functionally graded skewed thick plates 
with considering the effect of stress singularities based on Reddy’s third-order plate theory. 
Accurate natural frequencies are determined by using the Ritz method. The admissible functions 
include a set of polynomials, which are from a mathematically complete set of functions, and corner 
functions, which account for the singular behaviors of stress at the reentrant corner. Material 
properties are assumed to vary continuously in the thickness direction with a power-law distribution. 
The corner functions show the significant effects on the convergence of frequency parameters for 
skewed plates with large skew angle due to the severe stress singularities at the corner. Frequency 
parameters are presented for different aspect ratios ( ba / ), chord ratios ( bc / ), skewed angles (β ), 
and the values of material nonhomogeneity parameters.  
 
 
Keyword: Functionally graded plate; Vibration; Skewed plate; Stress singularity; Ritz method

None of the aforementioned works has investigated the behaviors of a FGM plate 
having stress singularities. The present work first investigates the vibrations of a 
cantilevered thick skewed functionally graded plate, which has stress singularities at its 
reentrant corner, based on Reddy’s third-order plate theory. The Ritz method is used 
and its admissible functions include two sets of functions. They are a set of polynomials, 
which are from a mathematically complete set of functions, and a set of corner functions, 
which were developed in Huang [2002] and account for the singular behaviors of stress 
at the reentrant corner. Convergence studies are performed to demonstrate the effects of 
corner functions on the accuracy of the solutions. Frequency parameters are also 
presented for different aspect ratios ( ba / ), chord ratios ( bc / ), skewed angles (β ), and 
the values of material nonhomogeneity parameters.

4. CONCLUDING REMARKS 
This work has demonstrated a novel Ritz procedure to determine accurately the 
natural frequencies of skewed cantilevered FGM thick plates based on Reddy’s 
third-order plate theory. The proposed procedure incorporates a mathematically 
complete set of admissible polynomials in conjunction with admissible corner functions 
that not only properly describe the singular behaviors of stresses at the re-entrant 
clamped corner.  
The effects of adding corner functions to the admissible set of polynomials in the 
Ritz method on the determination of the frequencies of a plate were investigated 
through convergence studies for various plates with different shapes. It was shown there 
that use of corner functions accelerates the convergence of the solutions significantly, 
thereby permitting one to obtain accurate frequencies from smaller eigenvalue 
determinants, and reducing numerical ill-conditioning. 
Some accurate numerical results on nondimensional frequencies have been tabulated 
for plates with different m  (0, 0.5, and 5), bc /  (0.5 and 1), β  ( o0 , o30 , o45 , o60 , 
and o75 ), and bt EE /  (2 and 5).  More results will be obtained in the near future.
