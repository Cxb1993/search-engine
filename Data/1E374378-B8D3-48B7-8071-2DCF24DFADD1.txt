摘要 
    生物力學相關的研究近幾十年來成長迅速，主要在整合生物學和材料力學，
而由於奈米壓痕技術的發展，生物硬組織（如牙齒）內奈米結晶微結構組成物質
的尺寸、形狀、排列方式被發現後，奈米結構生物複合材料的微觀力學模型建立
及分析成為一重要研究課題。 
  本研究以有限元素法模擬探討人體牙齒內部微米假設的琺瑯質及象牙質之
RVE 力學模型及其最佳化探討，利用複合材料力學基本理論為基礎，來探討生
物體組織中有關結構力學的問題，希望藉此研究能對生物材料結構有更進一步的
瞭解，以加強仿生複合材料的基礎研究。 
  而由結果顯示，（1）琺瑯質桿柱與桿柱間彼此接觸面的形狀不同會影響正向
應力的大小，曲面能減少正向應力的產生，（2）琺瑯質襯套能有效的吸收因剪應
力而產生的能量，因而降低琺瑯質桿柱的剪應力值，（3）象牙質小管的深度對於
象牙小管的應力分佈趨勢並無明顯影響，（4）應變能吸收值和黏彈性效果會隨著
角度增加而減少。 
 
  關鍵詞：前臼齒、奈米結晶微結構、有限元素法、機械行為。 
Abstract 
In the last decades, biomechanics research integrating biology and material 
mechanics grew rapidly. Only recently, with the development of nanoindentation 
instruments, it has been possible to investigate the size, shape and orientation of 
nanocrystalline micro-structural constituent material of bio-hard tissue (such as tooth). 
Therefore, the mechanical analysis and modeling of nanostructured bio-composite 
become a very important research topic. 
This research assumed RVE microstructural mechanical model of the enamel will 
be established, using finite element method analysis. Using the basic mechanical 
theory of composite materials to investigate mechanical problem of bio-tissue could 
enhance the basic research of biomimetic composite materials.

三、 研究方法 
  綜合文獻分析的結論，本研究將針對牙齒內的兩項結構-琺瑯質組織及象牙
質組織研究其最佳化排列現象，根據這兩項組織分別皆為微米級假設的單一琺瑯
鑰匙「六角柱」及「四角柱」及單一象牙小管週期性堆疊而成，將以 RVE理論及
有限元素方法對單一琺瑯鑰匙孔柱及象牙小管進行分析與模擬，並與第一年的
「鑰匙孔」琺瑯角柱模擬結果做比對；其中琺瑯鑰匙孔柱內的蛋白質晶體有特定
方向的規則排列，蛋白質晶體為奈米等級，其尺寸須和整體琺瑯鑰匙孔柱相差
1000倍，蛋白質有緩衝作用，因此將考慮其具有黏彈性質，而礦物質及蛋白質
的有效材料參數的決定，將參考各篇文獻所得之生物材料參數，以及象牙質的有
效材料參數的決定，觀察這些微奈米組成之結構對於壓力在各形狀內的分部及整
體堆疊材料的影響。

五、 結論 
本研究主要利用 ABAQUS 軟體模擬以下內容： 
1. 比較彈性分析與黏彈性分析下，鑰匙孔狀、六角形與四角形的琺瑯質桿
柱在受到壓應力與剪應力時，其琺瑯角柱與琺瑯襯套的軸向應力分佈
情況。 
2. 比較彈性分析與黏彈性分析下，中層(DEJ到牙髓腔部分約 2.54μm)和
深層(靠近牙髓腔約 2.8μm)的象牙質小管受到壓應力與剪應力時，其
ITD與 PTD 的軸向應力分佈情況。 
3. 比較彈性分析與黏彈性分析下，0度、45度和 90度堆疊的象牙小管模
型在受到壓應力時，象牙小管軸向的 Mises應力分佈情況。 
4. 結合琺瑯質、DEJ與象牙質為牙齒試棒，並且施予正向壓應力，觀察牙
齒試棒的堆疊角度、彈性分析與黏彈性分析彼此之間的關係。

 Abstract : The present research studies the experimental datum of the coefficient of 
friction, wear weight loss, surface roughness, hardness, and wear mechanism after wear 
experiment of magnesium alloy composite AZ91D + SiCp. The present specimen is made of 
AZ91D matrix and reinforcement of SiCp in different particle size. Specific load is applied 
at different sliding speed during wear experiment, as indicated in Table.1. As either 
particle size of SiCp is increasing, the coefficient of friction, the hardness and surface 
roughness are increasing, but the wear loss is decreasing, as shown in Fig.2 to Fig.5. The 
results are consistent with those of reference [1] and [2].   
Keywords : Metal matrix composites, wear mechanism, magnesium alloy, silicon carbide 
(SiC). 
 
 
1. Introduction  
Magnesium (Mg) alloys are gaining more 
importance as a lightest structural material for 
light-weighted application, owing to their low 
density and high stiffness-to-weight ratio. Even so, 
Mg alloys have not been used for critical 
performance applications owing to their lower 
mechanical properties compared to other 
engineering materials. Hence, many researchers 
attempt to fabricate Mg-based metal-matrix 
composites (Mg MMCs) by varied methods to 
obtain lightweight materials with excellent 
mechanical properties [3-6]. The present research 
studies the experimental datum of the coefficient 
of friction, wear weight loss, surface roughness, 
hardness, and wear mechanism after wear 
experiment of magnesium alloy composite 
AZ91D + SiCp.  
Regarding to Mg-based metal-matrix 
composites, Ugandhar et al. [7] successfully 
synthesized Mg MMCs with sub-micron size SiC 
particulate reinforcements using an innovative 
Disintegrated Melt Deposition technique followed 
by hot extrusion. Sinha et al. [8] and Abachi et al. 
[9] investigated the wear behavior of Mg/SiC, 
Mg/SiC/Ti and SiCp/QE22 magnesium alloy 
matrix composites. Their results showed that their 
wear resistance of the matrix alloy could be 
increased by incorporation of SiC particles.  
Modal and Kumar [10] investigated the wear 
behavior of a creep-resistant AE42 magnesium 
alloy and its composites reinforced with Saffil 
short fibres and SiC particles in various 
combinations. It was found that the presence of 
SiC particles in the hybrid composites improves 
the wear resistance because the particles remain 
intact and retain their load bearing capacity even 
at the highest load applied, they promote the 
formation of iron-rich transfer layer and they also 
delay the fracture of Saffil short fibres to higher 
loads. Semenov et al. [11] presented the 
tribological contact characteristics of R18 tool 
steel in interface with AZ91D magnesium alloy 
hardened with SiC dispersed powder filler and by 
severe plastic deformation (SPD)— specifically, 
equal-channel angular pressing (ECAP). SPD of 
the original material leads to reduction of the 
molecular component of the friction coefficient. 
Lee et al. [12] investigated the microstructures 
and wear property of friction stir welded (FSW) 
AZ91 Mg alloy/SiC particle reinforced composite 
(AZ91/SiC/10p). The initial microstructures of the 
AZ91/SiC/10p were composed of irregularly 
distributed β-phases (Al12Mg17) and agglomerated 
SiC particles, while the friction stir weld zone was 
characterized by the homogeneous distribution of 
SiC particles, the recrystallized grain structure and 
the dissolution of β-phase. Lim et al. [6] studied 
the wear behavior of Mg MMCs with SiCp during 
sliding. The composites exhibit slightly superior 
wear resistance under the low load, but the effects

4. Conclusion  
The present material could be provided as 
material for tribological usage in the future, such 
as a cylinder of engine, a bearing, a gear wheel, 
cutting tools and the joints of human body. 
This study firstly proposed and investigated 
the particle size effect of micro-sized SiC on the 
tribological behavior of SiCp reinforced AZ91D 
Mg-based MMCs. The present Mg-based MMCs 
was fabricated by the melt-stirring technique, and 
performed excellent wear resistance properties. 
Based on the experimental results, the following 
conclusions and some important novelties could 
be drawn: 
1. The incorporation of SiC particles could 
improve the AZ91D matrix alloy wear 
resistance for most of the sliding conditions. 
2. The hardness of MMCs’ after wear tests 
increases with increasing particle size of SiCp 
both for 10N and 50N loading conditions at 
low rolling speed of 250, 1000 and 1500 rpm, 
except for the particle size of 15μm. 
3. The coefficient of friction increases with 
increasing particle size of SiCp both for 10N 
and 50N loading conditions at any specific 
particle size, except for the condition of 1500 
rpm under loading 50N. 
4. The weight loss decreased with increasing 
particle size of SiCp for moderate sliding 
conditions at any specific rolling speed of 250, 
1000 rpm.
