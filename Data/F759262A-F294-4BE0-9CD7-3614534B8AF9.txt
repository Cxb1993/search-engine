此可以限制一些可能製造出碳微粒與 NOX 的化學反應的發生。二、乳化燃料受熱時會發生
液滴爆炸的情況，這是由 Ivanov 與 Nefedov [2]首先發現的，他們將這個現象命名為微爆
(micro-explosion)，微爆現象使液滴碎裂為更細小的液滴，在燃燒場中更迅速地氣化燃燒，
因此微爆又稱為二次霧化，由於微爆在提升燃燒效率及減少污染上頗具潛力，因此近年來
已有相當多的研究成果。
在理論研究方面，1977 年，Avedisian 與 Anders [3]研究碳氫化合物與水混合之乳化燃
料的微爆現象，提出一個可以計算雙液體系統氣泡成核率的模型。乳化燃料在一大氣壓下
的過熱極限溫度與理論預測有相當好的一致性，而乳化液的過熱極限皆低於個別純物質的
過熱極限，但要讓乳化液發生微爆，乳化液的過熱極限必須低於油料的沸點溫度，而在一
大氣壓之下只有正十六烷(n-hexadecane)與水的乳化燃料符合這個條件。1978 年，Law [4]
提出多成分油滴的蒸發燃燒模型，他指出當容易蒸發的成分被包覆在液滴的中心時，快速
地加熱液滴可能導致液滴中心均勻或非均勻成核 (homogeneous or heterogeneous
nucleation)，而隨著內部壓力的增加將導致液滴碎裂。
在實驗研究方面，1984 年 Wang 等人[5]，以噴墨(ink-jet)方法產生低雷諾數、多成分的
自由飛行液滴，他們認為微爆現象受到液體組成濃度的影響，與液滴產生方式的穩定性影
響。1985 年 Wang 與 Law [6]，探討環境壓力對微爆現象的影響，其實驗結果顯示提高壓力
會促進微爆現象的發生，對於沸點差異越大的混合液，促進微爆的效果越顯著。
1998 年 Tsue 等人[7]，將液滴懸掛於石英絲線，以自由落體的方式研究重力對於微爆
現象的影響。以統計的方法分析實驗結果，結果顯示等待時間（定義為液滴開始加熱至發
生微爆經過的時間）的分佈與平均等待時間，皆不受重力的影響。微爆的發生率同樣不受
重力影響，但隨著水的過熱程度的增加呈指數增加。
2000 年 Segwa 等人[8]研究以十六烷為基礎油，加 10% ~ 40%的水混合成的乳化燃料液
滴。實驗結果發現在微重力狀態下，乳化燃料被加熱時會產生相分離的情況：原本分散的
水滴聚集在液滴中心，而外面被一層基礎油包覆；在相分離之後基礎油會被蒸發逐漸減少，
而水量不變；相分離的過程開始與結束時液滴的溫度不隨水分的含量而變，液滴的微爆溫
度則隨液滴原始水量的增加而減少。
2003 年 Wang 等人[9]實驗以兩種可互溶的液體碰撞黏合產生的自由飛行液滴，探討其
燃燒與微爆現象，結果顯示沒有微爆時的燃燒特性，如點火延遲(ignition delay)，火焰內縮
(flame shrinkage)，燃燒率(burning rate)等特性，大致上與預先混合好的相同成分與比例的液
滴相同。但是相同成分與比例的液滴，只有以碰撞方式產生的液滴出現微爆現象，他們認
為是碰撞的初期將空氣包覆形成內部氣泡，提供內部成核所需的基點。
由文獻中可知前人對於乳化燃料的研究已有深入的瞭解。在理論方面可以計算兩種成
分的乳化燃料的過熱極限。在實驗方面透過自由飛行液滴法，與懸吊液滴法兩種研究方法，
前人對於乳化液滴的微爆現象，燃燒的特性等相關的機制，都已經累積了相當的數據與知
識。但是對於複合液滴的研究在文獻中仍然不多見。2004 年陳正文等人[10]首先對柴油與
水複合液滴的微爆現象進行探討，本研究則針對純十六烷與純水組成的複合液滴的受熱情
況及微爆現象進行研究，複合液滴的特性是完全不加入界面活性劑，利用燃料與水本身的
物理特性，形成一顆油滴包覆單一水滴的複合液滴，如圖一所示。

3.2 複合液滴的微爆模式
由高速攝影機所拍攝的影像，將複合液滴的微爆現象依照液滴碎化情況分為模式 1、
模式 2、模式 3 三個模式。模式 1 是直接微爆的液滴碎化情況。模式 1a（參照圖六），液滴
在微爆之前從進入熱環境到 4.530sec 之間，都沒有發生任何變化，液滴一直保持穩定的狀
態，而在 4.531sec 液滴發生微爆時，首先發現有氣體噴出的現象，下一個瞬間 4.532sec 時
只見液滴碎裂為細小液滴，不見大碎塊，屬於微爆強度最強的狀況，微爆的時間顯然小於
1ms。模式 1b（參照圖七），液滴同樣直接發生微爆，而微爆後殘餘大碎塊的情況，在 3.999sec
液滴下方可見有部分氣體衝出，在 4.000sec 時畫面中可見液滴脹開的輪廓。微爆過程在 1ms
之內完成，但是液滴裂為大碎塊，此現象的微爆強度與微爆後無碎塊的情況相比是較弱的。
模式 2 是液滴未完全炸開，而後有部分液滴沾黏於熱電偶情況。參照圖八，在 3.112sec
時可見液滴開始膨脹，3.113sec 液滴由下方爆開，3.114sec ~ 3.117sec 可見爆炸的能量將液
滴往上方推擠；3.120sec ~ 3.134sec 可見被推擠至上方的液滴又因重力而落下，有部分液滴
分離，部分液滴仍然附著在熱電偶上。此種現象的微爆強度比微爆後仍有大碎塊的情況更
弱，爆炸期間比模式 1 要長，其能量不足以將液滴全面炸開。
模式 3 是複合液滴受熱之後沒有發生劇烈微爆，而只是稍微地脹大的情況。參照圖九，
在 2.871sec 時可見液滴撐開，2.872sec 可見液滴膨脹，2.873sec 可能有氣體衝出帶出一道液
柱，但是力道不足以拉斷液柱，因此液體又黏回液滴本體，黏回的動作造成液體不穩定的
上下晃動，在 2.883sec 時液滴再也無法沾黏於熱電偶上因此掉落。此種現象微爆的強度比
微爆後沾黏（模式 2）的情況更弱。
3.3 液滴溫度變化
圖十是在不同的環境溫度下，模式 1 微爆模式，液滴溫度隨時間的變化圖。縱座標代
表熱電偶的量測的液滴溫度，橫座標代表進入熱環境之後所經過的時間，圖上四條曲線分
別表示在不同環境溫度下，液滴溫度隨時間的變化。液滴微爆前溫度變化與純十六烷液滴
類似，Tsur = 503℃時，液滴在溫度上升過程中微爆，微爆溫度 228℃，微爆時間 0.9sec；Tsur
= 400℃的情況，升溫過程延伸的時間較長，微爆溫度約 255℃，微爆時間約 2.5sec。當 Tsur
= 320℃左右，液滴發生微爆時，溫度變化曲線經常出現瞬間溫度下降然後上升的狀況，如
圖上實心圓點線段在 t = 7.6sec 時，溫度下降將近 10℃，推測原因是複合液滴中原屬過熱狀
態的水滴，微爆時汽化成為 100℃的水蒸氣，熱電偶量測的溫度便瞬間下降。但是並非在
環境溫為 320℃時的微爆情況都出現溫度下降的狀況，圖中空心圓點線段即為一例。影響
溫度下降因素，可能與水滴汽化的狀況有關係；而在環境溫度大約為 400℃或 500℃時也很
少觀察到溫度下降的狀況，推測環境溫度較高時，環境與液滴的溫度梯度較大，液滴氣化
時下降的溫度相對較不明顯。
圖十一是模式 2、模式 3 的溫度變化情況。由圖中模式 2 溫度變化曲線發現，在膨脹
同時，約 t = 3.1sec 出現溫度下降的狀況，而後又因有液滴沾黏，溫度曲線又回復升溫狀態，
但此時液滴中水分已經很少，液滴沒有再次微爆的能力，不斷的蒸發直到蒸發完畢，溫度
於熱電偶上；模式 3 為液滴僅膨脹非微爆。由複合液滴的溫度變化可以知道液滴的微爆時
間與微爆的溫度，但由溫度變化無法判斷微爆強度。 在環境溫度為 400℃或 500℃左右，
實驗發現，微爆時間越長微爆的溫度越高，但在環境溫度為 300℃左右，趨勢較不明顯。
複合液滴的微爆強度受到微爆時間與微爆溫度的影響。微爆時間越長，微爆溫度越高，微
爆強度就越強，其中的機制可能與水過熱吸收能量，然後在蒸發時釋放有關。但複合液滴
中水滴的大小與微爆強度沒有明顯的關係。

The possibility of fire extinguishment or personnel rescue is usually determined by the time from 
ignition to flashover.  In consideration of a fire safety compartment, this time must be long enough 
for the fire fighting system to operate properly.  Peacock et al.1 analyzed the occurrence of flashover 
from the results of full-scale fire tests and concluded that the flashover usually happened when the 
upper layer temperature reached 600oC or when the radiation flux to the floor reached 20kW/m2.  
Many researchers have studied the flashover phenomenon. 
 
Babrauskas2 modeled the flashover by balancing the thermal energy items in the upper layer and 
derived an equation for the minimum heat release rate necessary to induce flashover.  The heat loss 
was thought to be mainly controlled by the ventilation factor, i.e. the area and height of the 
compartment opening.  McCaffrey et al.3 presented a correlation based on more than one hundred 
compartment fire test results.  Their correlation related the heat release rate, opening area, wall 
conduction, and ambient conditions to the temperature of the upper layer.  Mowrer and Williamson4 
placed the fire source near the wall or the corner and observed higher upper layer temperatures.  The 
wall and corner effects could speed up the flashover time by 50 to 70 per cent.  Modeling of 
flashover was presented by Jolly and Saito5 and Holborn et al.6 and compared well with experimental 
results.  Graham et al.7 derived an equation for the temperature of the upper layer and the flashover 
criterion emphasizing the thermal inertia of walls.  Based on Graham et al.’s results, Novozhilov8 
studied the suppression of flashover by sprinklers for various degrees of fire intensity.  And the role 
of thermal radiation, openings, and wall conduction on the initiation of flashover was studied in Yuen 
and Chow9. 
 
Flashover is an important stage in the growth of a compartment fire.  Although there have been a 
great amount of research in the past, the applicability of these results has to be verified in order to 
obtain a localized fire code because of the specific building materials and furniture used in the locality.  
This study presented an effort by the Architecture and Building Research Institute of Taiwan in 
assessing the influencing factors for flashover, such as fire load, temperature, heat release, radiation, 
in a typical Taiwanese office room fire. 
 
EXPERIMENTAL SETUP 
 
A typical office room in Taiwan was chosen as our full-scale fire test model.  Figure 1 and 
Table 1 display the types and arrangement of the instrumentation and furniture in the office room.  
The office was 6m long, 5m wide, and 2.4m high.  The walls were constructed with 0.2m thick ALC 
(aerated lightweight concrete) plates.  The office had one door each at the north-east and the 
south-east corner.  The doors were both half-closed; the bottom half of the south-east door and the 
upper half of the north-east door were covered.  Next to the room at the south was a 10MW fire 
products collector, with a suction hood of 7.62m×7.62m in size and an exhaust rate of 30m3/s, which 
was capable of analyzing the heat release rate by the oxygen consumption principle. 
 
The furniture in the room included six units of workstation (desk, chair, side panel), and ten wooden 
cabinets of various height placed near the north and east walls of the room.  No additional 
combustibles, such as paper or computers, were placed in the room; therefore the room represented a 
new office.  The fire load was about 390MJ/m2.  Pictures of the setup are shown in Figure 2. 
 
The broken-lined circle represented the location of the fire source, which was a 30kW (square, 
40cm×40cm) propane gas burner.  Since there was no paper or other easily-ignited combustibles near 
the fire source, the gas burner was left ignited for about 1600 seconds until the fire was fully grown 
and maintained.  The instrumentation of the fire test room included thermocouple trees (TC1~TC7), 
single thermocouples (T1~T8), radiation flux meters (R1~R5), gas analyzer probes (G1 & G2), 
air-cooled in-furnace camera system, and differential pressure transducers (BDPIn & BDPOut).  There 
was a bi-directional probe at each door opening to measure the rate of the incoming or outgoing gas 
flow.  Their respective numbers and locations are listed in Figure 1.  The dashed-line rectangle at 
the lower right corner of the figure represents the section of the furniture which we burned separately 
outside the room to evaluate its heat release characteristics.

Figure 5 shows the variations of CO and O2 concentration inside the office room.  Before flashover 
occurred, the concentrations of CO and O2 were roughly 0.1% and 20% respectively.  After flashover, 
CO concentration suddenly increased to over 0.8% and O2 concentration dropped to almost zero.  
There was some oxygen remained at the location of G2 because fresh air came in from the north-east 
opening. 
 
Figure 6 displays the measurements of radiation heat flux at five locations, R1~R5.  Note that all 
heat flux meters faced horizontally towards the inside of the room except R3, which faced up towards 
the ceiling.  There was almost no radiation flux before the fire grew quite large near 1260s.  At the 
time of flashover, all heat flux meters measured about 20kW/m2.  After flashover, R5 had the highest 
value because it was nearest to the flame coming out of the south-east door.  R1, R2, and R4 could 
all reach more than 50kW/m2 but R3 did not exceed 20kW/m2 before the flashover happened.  Due 
to cooling water problems, all heat flux meters burned down some time after flashover. 
In summary, the flashover of the full-scale office fire was determined to occur at 1600s by observation.  
At the time of flashover, the temperature of the upper layer was roughly 600oC and the radiation heat 
flux at the lower layer was around 20kW/m2.
