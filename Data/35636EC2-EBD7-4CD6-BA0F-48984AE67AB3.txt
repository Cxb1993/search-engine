Keywords: SPT, spilt core samplers, penetration force, pore pressure.  
The Standard Penetration Test (SPT) has been used widely for measuring the resistances of the 
soil to penetration through the blow count, N, and many useful correlations model have been 
established between the SPT-N values and soil properties. In recent years some engineers have also 
developed various corrections model to account for the effects of N-Values such as method of 
drilling, how clean boring is, hammer location, hammer type, overburden pressure, and various 
other factors of less importance. While these corrections are so many, their use has led to confusion 
regarding which correlations are right and which are wrong. To avoid that confusion, a new 
technique has been performed for detecting penetration force and pore pressure on the spilt core 
samplers during the SPT in this study. 
The research methodology is installed a high frequency response of small load cell and pore 
pressure sensors on the spilt core sampler of SPT. Besides a high frequency and large flash memory 
AD/DA acquisitions card are also be set up between sensors and the IPC in this system for quickly 
catching and recording the penetration force and pore pressure data. The result shown the system 
is available from in-situ test results.
