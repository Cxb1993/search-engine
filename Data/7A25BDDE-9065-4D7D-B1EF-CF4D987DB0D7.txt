II. RESONANT CHARACTERISTICS OF THE QUINTUPLE-MODE 
RESONATOR 
Fig. 1 shows the layout of the proposed quintuple-mode 
resonator. It consists of a symmetric SIR with two stepped-
impedance open stubs tapped at its center and two uniform 
stubs at the junctions of the low- and high-impedance sections. 
The SIR consists of two high-impedance sections on both 
sides and one low-impedance section in between, and can be 
treated as a triple-mode resonator [8]. The larger the 
impedance ratio of the high- to the low-impedance section is, 
the closer proximities the three resonances have. The two 
stepped-impedance open stubs make the tap point equivalent 
to a cascade of two K-inverters and provide additional 
transmission poles and zeros [9-11]. By adequately adjusting 
the stub lengths, one can allocate the transmission zeros at 
both the upper and lower edges of the passband. The 
symmetric side stubs serve as tuning stubs to slide all the 
resonant modes [13] as shown below.  
Fig. 2 investigates the resonant characteristics of the 
proposed quintuple-mode resonator. The transmission 
response is obtained by feeding the resonator with capacitive 
coupling through small gaps at its two ends. The circuit 
substrate has a relative permittivity εr = 2.2 and thickness = 
0.508 mm. In the simulation, the dielectric and conductor are 
assumed lossless so that the resonant peaks can be detected 
accurately. It can be seen from the |S21| response that the 
resonator possesses five transmission poles (f1 through f5) and 
two transmission zeros (fz1 and fz2). Herein, the resonance f3 = 
2.45 GHz is defined as the center frequency of the BPF and 
the corresponding guided wavelength is denoted as λ. 
Fig. 3(a) plots their dependences on the normalized stub 
length (lc1 + lc2)/(λ/4) for investigating the properties of the 
poles and the zeros. It is worth mentioning that the resonance 
f5 occurring near λ/4 [9-11] is created from upper tapped stub 
when (lc1 + lc2)/(λ/4) > 0.725. Note that only f3, f5 and fz2 shift 
down to lower frequencies as lc1 + lc2 is increased. The zero 
fz2 goes down more rapidly than the resonance f5, and fz2 < f5 
when (lc1 + lc2)/(λ/4) is larger than 0.82. Also, the resonance 
f3 moves to lower than f2 when (lc1 + lc2)/(λ/4) > 1.08.

會議三：電路的部分，一大部分是 CMOS 元件，有 DGS縮小化的設計、高隔
離度的 T/R 切換開關、低相位雜訊 VCO 設計、CMOS 放大器（35 – 45 GHz）、
Direct Injection-Locked Frequency Divider、CMOS Phase array、Broadband Balun（23 – 
35 GHz）、60 GHz CMOS 功率放大器 （PA）、W-band Gunn Diode 的製作、以 MMIC 
設計（8 倍頻）77 GHz 雷達 VCO，利用InPGunn Diodes 製作 94 GHz 波導管 
VCO、濾波器、60 GHz 單極天線的設計、實現與量測、封裝 Vias 的實驗等效電路
模型、30 GHz 與 77 GHz 之 SIW 天線等等。 
議程中另一大部分就是系統的實現，日本的研究還是比較務實，做出系統才能有
更多的發揮，研討會中很多的論文都在討論系統層面實現時遇到的問題，如何解決。
可以看到有很多的實例。茲舉實例如下：以 120 GHz 頻段從事 2008 北京奧運的轉
播，以點對點方式作無線傳輸，發射機與接收機中，天線的輻射（3dB）角度為 0.5
度，傳輸距離為 400 m 左右，MIMIC自行設計，測試 10 Gbits/s。技術上 120 GHz 的 
MMIC 已經很不容易了，增益本來就不高，所以「點對點」的通訊似乎是唯一的選
擇，這也可以解釋為何需要輻射（3dB）角度為 0.5 度的天線。 
會議四：在 4/21（議程第二天）晚會中，也邀請 Dr. Ohmori （大森）做專題演

二、與會心得 
    筆者現在亦兼任 IEEE Transactions on Microwave Theory and Techniques （IEEE 
TMTT） 期刊的 Associate Editor，也受到應有的尊重。筆者亦受邀參加韓日兩國代
表在 4/22 早上開會研商本研討會的舉辦事宜，例如明年後年的主辦國家等等。 
    筆者參加此次會議的目的，除了發表論文之外，也想藉此機會認識更多的專家，
以協助 IEEE TMTT 的學術論文審稿工作，通常一篇論文至少有四位才算嚴謹或合乎
要求，筆者都會請五位協助，如果其中一位無法處理或延遲時，仍可以如期完成審稿
的工作。這一次遇到多位韓日專家，交換名片，言明需要協助審稿，反應全部都很正
面。 
    聽完大森博士的演講，知道機器人要有智慧，從家庭無線網路擷取生活所需資
訊。但從另一方面想，人很像都不需要智慧了，因為機器、網路都已經設想到了。這
似乎也是另一種隱憂，人類未來會變成如何？出門還要機器人檢查或提醒什麼東西忘
記帶；當然，機器人知道主人什麼時候出門（含星期幾）是要做什麼，都已經有 database 
可以協助，是很不容易的系統整合，但是人類過度依賴機器人的結果，人不需要大腦
了？以後的世界會不會變成很聰明的（設計這些電腦、網路、機器人的人），跟很沒
有腦筋的兩大族群？
