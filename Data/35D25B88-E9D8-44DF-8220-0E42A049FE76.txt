一、中文摘要
本計畫全程三年，旨在探討卜作嵐材
料粗細程度對高性能混凝土破裂韌性
（fracture toughness）之早期及晚期影響。
第一年研究針對爐石，本（第二）年研究
則以飛灰為試材。試驗用之高性能混凝土
採用水膠比 0.35，飛灰取代水泥量分 0％、
10％及 20％三種，飛灰細度分別為通過
No.175、No.250 及 No.325 篩等三種，於
齡期到達時進行相關試驗。研究上以三點
彎矩試驗來測量預裂樑的最大載重，再根
據量測的載重與變形關係，計算出破裂能
量（GF）值，以此值來代表混凝土破裂韌
性大小。
試驗結果顯示，飛灰細度愈高者，由
於其填充效應與活性愈佳，不論取代 10％
或 20％水泥，均顯示晚齡期之強度與破裂
能量值都有提高的效果。在早齡期且相同
細度下，飛灰取代率 10％者有較高的抗壓
強度。不論在早、晚齡期，飛灰細度愈高
時，混凝土的破裂能量顯現有愈大的趨
勢。觀察晚齡期（56 天）試驗結果可發現，
飛灰取代率 10％者之強度與 GF 值均較
高。以飛灰取代水泥後，將造成混凝土晚
齡期強度與破裂能量的明顯提昇，惟取代
率不宜過大，因過大的取代率反而造成折
減，因此建議以 10％為宜。
關鍵詞：卜作嵐材料、飛灰、細度、破裂
韌性
Abstract
The project aims to study the effect of
the pozzolanic material particle size on the
early and late age fracture toughness of high
performance concrete (HPC). This is the
second year report of the 3-year project. The
pozzolanic material adopted for experiments
in this year is the fly ash. In all HPC
mixtures, the water/binder ratio is 0.35; the
cement replacement ratios are 0%, 10% and
20%; the particle sizes of fly ash are three
types that pass through sieves No. 175, No.
250 and No. 325. Three-point-bending test
was adopted to measure the load-deflection
relations, the maximum loads and the
toughness energies (GF).
The test results show that the smaller
the particle size of fly ash the better the
late age strengths and GF values no matter
what the replacement ratio is 10% or 20%. If
the particle size is the same, the early age
strength of concrete with 10% of fly ash is
higher than that with 20%. At late age the
strengths and GF values of concrete with
10% fly ash are all higher than those with
20% fly ash. This result indicates that
although the fly ash can improve the late age
concrete properties, too much fly ash may be
disadvange to the concrete. The replacement
ratio 10% is suggested in this project.
Keywords: pozzolanic materials, fly ash,
fineness, fracture toughness
44.2 硬固混凝土性質
各組試驗混凝土抗壓強度值如表 3 所
示。由表中可看出，各組混凝土，不論是
純混凝土或飛灰混凝土，其 28 天後強度皆
大幅超越 560 kgf/cm2，達高性能混凝土要
求。又由表中觀察可知，在早齡期（7 天）
時，純混凝土的強度最高，所有添加飛灰
之混凝土，其強度都低於純混凝土。在同
一細度條件下，混凝土強度與飛灰取代率
呈負相關，這是由於參與水化反應之水泥
減少，造成早期強度降低。在相同取代率
下，F3 各組強度最高，F2 組次之，而 F1
組最低，可見在添加細度較高的飛灰後，
會將混凝土內部填充得更為緻密，強度可
因而提昇。齡期 14 天之混凝土強度趨勢則
與 7 天時相同。
但自28天起，所有飛灰混凝土強度即
大幅成長，表示飛灰的卜作嵐反應在此時
趨於明顯；其中，細度較高的F2與F3各
組，其強度皆已超越純混凝土者（A0），
這是由於細度較高者之反應活性越佳，使
強度之提昇愈為顯著。至56天時，由於卜
作嵐反應更為完全，各組飛灰混凝土除
F1A2外，其強度皆已高於純混凝土；且細
度較高者其強度有愈高的趨勢。此外，由
各組混凝土強度值還可發現，不論細度為
何，均以取代水泥10%者效果較佳。
4.3 高性能混凝土之破裂性質
各組試驗混凝土之破裂能量值（GF）
與其成長百分比如表4所示，其中GF值為
利用繪圖軟體Grapher所求得。三點彎矩試
驗結果顯示，就早期（14天）之破裂能量
值而言，F1各組仍低於純混凝土，但F2、
F3各組則與純混凝土相當。至齡期56天
後，各組飛灰混凝土除F1A1、F1A2外，
其破裂能量值均明顯超越純混凝土。由此
可知添加飛灰於高性能混凝土時，除可對
抗壓強度有所提昇外，對於混凝土之破裂
能量也有正面助益。
圖2、圖3分別表示所有混凝土在14天
與56天之破裂能量值。在早齡期（14天）
的時候，不論取代率為10%或20%，各組
混凝土之GF值均以F1最小，F2次之，而F3
最大。再以GF成長百分比來看（圖4、圖
5），發現細度越高對於GF值之發展越好，
且以F3最佳，其14天之GF值可達56天GF
值的83%。至於齡期56天時（圖3），在相
同取代率下，細度越高者其GF值越大。
由表4還可看出，在早齡期（14天）且
同細度下，飛灰取代率對於破裂能量之大
小與發展並沒有一定的規律。但觀察晚齡
期（56天）的試驗結果則可發現，飛灰取
代率10%者之GF值較高；加上同細度之各
組混凝土其抗壓強度差異不大，所以可
知，不論飛灰細度為何，其破裂能量值均
以取代10%者較高。綜合前述可結論出，
當飛灰細度為過No.250篩、取代水泥量為
10%時（F2A1），可以得到最高的破裂能
量值。
五、結論
1. 添加部分飛灰於高性能混凝土中，不論
是取代 10%或 20%，對於晚期強度皆有
明顯助益，其中又以取代 10%者效果較
佳；此外，添加細度較高之飛灰可得到
較大之混凝土強度。
2. 不論在早、晚齡期，當飛灰細度越高時，
混凝土破裂能量值（GF）有越大之趨勢。
3. 以飛灰取代水泥後，將造成混凝土晚齡
期破裂能量的提昇，惟取代率不宜過
大，因過大的取代率反而造成破裂能量
值的折減，因此建議以 10%為宜。

INTRODUCTION
Compressive strength has been always regarded as an important index of concrete
quality. To ensure the construction safety and economy in engineering practice, one needs
to know the strength development of concrete at early ages. Hence, the strength
prediction of concrete is necessary. The pozzolanic materials have been used widely in
concrete construction because of many advantages (1, 2), such as less cost, decrease of
heat of hydration, and improvement of water tightness. The effects of pozzolans on the
properties of concrete will differ greatly depending on the kind of pozzolanic material, its
quality, its percentage replacement of cement and the curing methods. Prediction of the
strength of concrete containing pozzolanic materials is much more complicated, and the
current strength prediction equations can not provide accurate results. In addition to
water-to-binder ratio and capillary porosity, this research also considers the substitute
ratio of the pozzolanic materials as a parameter to investigate the relations of each
parameter and the strength of concrete, and accordingly to establish the strength
prediction equation for pozzolanic concrete.

Materials
ASTM Type Ⅰ portland cement and ASTM class F fly ash are used, the
maximum size of coarse aggregate is 20 mm, the fineness modulus of fine aggregate is
2.97, and the superplasticizer of HICON HPC1000 which meets the requirements of
ASTM C494 G-Type is used. The chemical and physical properties of cement and fly ash
are listed in Table 1 and Table 2.
Substitute Ratios of Fly Ash and Compressive Strength
It can be seen clearly from Table 4 that the substitute ratios of fly ash directly
affects the strength development of concrete. The bigger the substitute ratio of fly ash is,
the less the product of cement hydration will be at an early age, and cause a lower
strength. Considering the ages of concrete under 7days, the strength decreases apparently
with the increase of substitution ratio of fly ash. At 28 days, the strength of fly ash
concretes approaches to that of plain concrete, implying that the pozzolanic reaction of
fly ash results in strength enhancement. After 56 days, the strength of fly ash concrete is
almost equal to that of the plain concrete, except the one with 30% replacement and those
with lower W/B are even higher. These results reveal that adding fly ash in concrete is
advantageous to the strength, but the substitution ratio should be under 30%.
Pore Structure and Compressive Strength
Figure 6 illustrates the regression curve of strength versus total pore volume. The
derived equation is: S=73.12 -907.61Vt +2931.65Vt2; R2=0.940, where S=compressive
strength (MPa) and Vt=total pore volume (c.c/g). Figure 7 shows the regression curve of
strength versus capillary pore volume. The derived equation is: S=71.79
-982.32Vc+3562.48Vc2; R2=0.954, where S=compressive strength (MPa) and
Vc=capillary pore volume (c.c/g). Comparing the results of the two relation curves, the
capillary pore volume has the closest correlation with the strength of fly ash concrete
with a correlation coefficient up to 0.954. It means that the capillary pore has the most
direct influence to the strength of concrete.

INTRODUCTION
High-strength high-performance concrete frequently has high cement content,
particularly when it contains silica fume, which enhances the heat of hydration. This may
cause increased plastic shrinkage that results in cracking (1). The temperature distribution
within the mass dictates the thermal gradient that causes tensile stresses to develop. If the
developed tensile stress exceeds the tensile strength of concrete at any specific points,
unnoticeable internal cracks may develop (2, 3). Drying shrinkage is also a common
problem associated with the amount of water and the binder material used in the concrete
mix. The loss of water causes a volume reduction in concrete of which the non-uniform
volume change in a stereo perspective triggers cracks in concrete (4).
In this research, a high-strength high-performance concrete containing silica fume
and fly ash was taken as the primary material to investigate the thermal deformation and
shrinkage deformation at early age. An appropriate testing method with suitable test
variables was selected. In addition to the thermally sensitive resistors for the
measurement of temperature development, an optic sensor system was also developed to
measure the shrinkage deformation of high-strength high-performance concrete. From the
serially measured data, the developing process of temperature and shrinkage and the time
effect of the influence factors were analyzed.

The test results of the 8 thermally sensitive resistors are shown in Fig. 7. The
temperature development is divided into 5 stages, as discussed below:
1. Initial dropping temperature stage: From 1 to 4 hours, the curve of
temperature shows a period of rapid dropping.
2. Temperature rising stage: From 4-12 hours, the interior temperature of the
specimen increased, and reached a higher temperature, with the temperature
differences between top surface and bottom layer being about 3-15℃.
3. Second dropping temperature stage: At 12-24 hours, the concrete appeared
to have sufficient initial strength due to setting. The temperature then began
to decline. The accompanying shrinkage deformation resulted in tensile
stresses allowing the concrete to crack because of low concrete strength.
4. Slow dropping temperature stage: At 1-4 days, the interior temperature of
the specimen decreased slowly, and it will be influenced evidently if change
the ambient test temperature and curing methods at this stage.
5. Steady temperature stage: After 4 days, the interior temperature of concrete
approached to the ambient test temperature.

CONCLUSIONS
1. Using thermally sensitive resistors and optic fiber sensors to monitor the deformation
of high-strength high-performance concrete can provide accurate measurements,
which is helpful to understand the deformation behavior of concrete accompanying
the temperature effect at early and very early age.
2. The tensile deformation of high-strength high-performance concrete containing silica
fume and fly ash at 1 hour age reached a strain of 20×10-6 (mm/mm), or a tensile
stress of 2.4×10-3 MPa, which may induce surface cracking. At the stage from 3 to 12
hours, the shrinkage deformation increased to a strain of 278×10-6 (mm/mm), which
may induce surface cracking. After age of 12 hours, the temperature development and
the volume change of concrete tends to a stable situation, and the shrinkage crack
will develop only when the shrinkage stress becomes greater than the corresponding
tensile strength of concrete.
