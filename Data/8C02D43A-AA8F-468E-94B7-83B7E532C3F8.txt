Abstract 
The objective of this study is to 
investigate the effects of processing variables, 
e.g., the processing method, processing 
temperature, type of the mold material, 
annealing condition, and extension ratio, on 
the surface related properties of the 
polyhydroxyalkanoates (PHAs), i.e., the 
degree of crystallization and the chemical 
composition on the outmost surface layer of 
PHAs.  The results of ATR indicate that 
there is a crystallinity distribution on the 
outer 2 microns thick surface layer.  The 
extent of crystallinity follows the order of 
KRS-5(60o) > Ge(60o) > KRS-5(45o) for all 
PHAs studied and the outer surface layer 
(~one micron penetration depth) have a 
greater crystallinity than the inner layer.  It 
is also obtained that the effect of annealing in 
enhancing the crystallization of PHB is more 
significant in the surface layer.  The types of 
the film forming method and the substrate 
contacted affect the surface crystallinity of 
the PHB material because of the changes in 
nucleating and crystal growing conditions.  
The results of TGA and DSC indicated that 
the purified PHB had a better thermal 
stability, a faster crystallization rater, and a 
higher melting temperature than the pristine 
PHB.  PHB degraded after a short exposure 
at the elevated temperature and which 
resulted in the decreases of the molecular 
weight, the melting temperature, and the 
crystallization rate.  The thermal stability of 
the PHB was significantly enhanced by the 
MA grafted.  Additionally, the faster 
crystallization rater and higher melting 
temperature but different biodegradation 
rates were obtained from the MA grafted 
PHB.  The PHB grafted with the 
mechanical grafting methods had the best 
thermal stability than those prepared by other 
two methods. 
Keywords: PHA, urface crystallinity, 
biodegradation, surface analysis
