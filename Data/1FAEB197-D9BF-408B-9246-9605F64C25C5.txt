mechanism at sufficient dosage due to the formation of 
Al13 aggregates with nearly zero charge. The structure 
of flocs formed by enmeshment or sweep flocculation 
and electrostatic patch becomes more compact with 
dosage, in which the breakage of flocs increases the 
fractal dimension of flocs. On the contrary, flocs 
coagulated by PACl-Al13 become looser with dosage. The 
results have shown that less dosage is required for 
organic coagulation by PACl-Al13 than that by PACl-C 
at pH ranging from 4 to 6, at which the optimum 
dosages of PACl and PACl-Al13 were observed due to Al-
HA complexation as well as charge neutralization by 
monomeric Al and Al13. At pH 6, the optimum dosage for 
PACl-Al13 is lower than for PACl-C, indicating that 
charge neutralization is the predominant mechanism at 
acidic pH. WSEM images also have illustrated that the 
PACl-C floc turned into a linear-shaped structure 
while the PACl-Al13 floc was slightly curled. In 
addition, a few monomeric Al as well as oligmeric Al 
is generated via the depolymerization of Al13 in PACl-
C and PACl-Al13 folcs. The reduced residual turbidity 
was caused by the superior filterability of the 
supernatant resulting from larger flocs with strong 
structure in response to high rapid-mixing intensity, 
especially for low-turbidity water. For IFM 
coagulation, the in-line IFM with intensive mixing (G 
> 5000 s-1) substantially improved the removal of 
turbidity and DOC, which is due to the formation of 
larger floc by IFM coagulation. The removal of 
turbidity and DOC by coagulation in surface water 
treatment can be effectively improved by intensive 
rapid mixing, especially by in-line IFM.
