高效率 III-V族太陽能電池，是由不同吸收波段的子太陽能電池(subcell)串接(cascaded)
整合，配合太陽光譜，以達到高轉換效率。 
 
這個計畫是想用 III-V族氮化物，主要為氮化銦鎵(InxGa1-xN) 來做單接面
(single-junction)與雙接面(dual-junction)太陽能電池。只需變動銦的成份比，子太陽
能電池能隙就可從 3.4eV(GaN)變化到約 0.7eV(InN)，從紫外光到紅外光，能組合成涵蓋
整個太陽光譜的太陽能電池，有潛力得到最高的效率。但此材料系統有幾個問題。首先是
晶格常數不匹配，在磊晶基板與氮化鎵，和氮化鎵與氮化銦鎵之間。另一個問題是相分離，
主要發生在高銦成份的氮化銦鎵磊晶。而太陽能電池需要高銦成份的氮化銦鎵來吸收長波
長的光，並需要足夠厚度以利吸收，增加光電流。但當銦成份比較高、膜層較厚時，愈易
產生相分離問題。我們希望研究這個問題，並提出幾個可能解決的方法。 
 
這個研發計畫包括材料生長與元件製作。我們用 MOCVD來研製高銦成份的氮化銦鎵。在元件
方面，我們研製表面結構包括透明導電層與表面粗化，增加集光率與光電流以提高外部量子
效率。

Conclusion 
 
We used MOCVD to grow In-rich InxGa1-xN films by 
varying growth temperature from 625°C to 725°C in 25°C 
steps. For a layer thickness of about 100nm, uniform InGaN 
films of Indium composition of 42±2%, 38±2%, 38±2% and 
23±2% were successfully grown at 625°C, 650°C, 675°C 
and 725°C, respectively, while phase separation occurred at 
700°C attributed to unbalanced thermal processes. When the 
thin films grew thicker, the one grown at 725°C with 
relatively lower Indium composition appeared to show phase 
separation earlier than the one at 650°C. The explanation 
needs further investigation. In conclusion, the lattice constant 
of InGaN film increased with decreasing growth temperature 
and with increasing layer thickness if no phase separation 
occurred. InxGa1-xN films of x ratio larger than 0.4 and of 
thickness up to ~500nm were successfully grown that 
exhibited uniform surface morphology with no Indium 
droplets and had single X-ray diffraction peak with only 
~1000 arcsec FWHM in 2θ value.

Simulation results 
     
The ray-tracing simulation was carried out with scatter size 
varying from 0.1 to 2 µm for four different input wavelengths: 
300nm, 550nm, 900nm and 1200nm, and the transmitted light 
rays were collected by detectors in 10 divisions. Figure 3 shows 
a representative plot of the transmitted light intensity versus 
angle for an input wavelength of 550 nm and scatter sizes: 0.15, 
1 and 2 µm normalized to the same total amount of transmitted 
light. Apparently the scatter size of 0.15µm among the three 
produces the largest angular spreading/diffusion of the 
transmitted light, resulting in a Haze number of 95.9%. When 
the angular spreading gets smaller, so does the Haze number. 
Figure 4 shows the resulted Haze value vs. scatter size for the 
four input wavelengths. There exists an optimized scatter size 
associated with the light wavelength to achieve a maximum of 
Haze that agrees with Mie scattering theory. And the larger the 
wavelength, the larger is the optimized scatter size. For scatter 
sizes larger than the optimized one, the corresponding haze gets 
smaller gradually. The decreasing slope is larger for shorter 
wavelength. In contrast, when scatter sizes are smaller than the 
optimized one, the corresponding haze drops quickly 
(approximately in Rayleigh scattering regime). As a result, a 
scatter size of ~0.5µm in diameter is appropriate to achieve 
high Haze for all four wavelengths that cover the absorption 
spectrum of silicon thin-film solar cells.  
 
Conclusion 
     
In conclusion, we carried out a ray-tracing simulation to 
understand the relationship between Haze values and scatter 
size of a microsphere-textured TCO glass. Increasing the Haze 
value would increase the optical path length in thin-film solar 
cells and thereby enhance the conversion efficiency. The 
simulation results imply a scatter size of ~0.5µm in diameter 
would be appropriate for efficient silicon thin-film solar cells. 
In the future, we will alter other parameters not taken into 
account this time to study their effects.

I. INTRODUCTION 
Optical coherence tomography (OCT) has evolved as a 
powerful diagnostic modality for non-invasive medical 
applications [1]-[3]. With the great success of OCT in recent 
years, high resolution and high image fidelity are getting more 
importance. The axial resolution is inversely proportional to the 
light bandwidth. So far, several approaches have been proposed 
to achieve high axial resolution, such as multiplexed 
superluminescent diode, continuum generation from photonic 
crystal fiber, and xenon illuminator. In general, these 
broadband light sources either exhibit bumpy spectrum or have 
very limited 3-dB width. In contrast, spontaneous emission 
from some transition-metal and rare-earth ions can generate 
broadband and near-Gaussian spectrum, but the power level is 
often low. A glass-clad crystal fiber (GCCF) growth technique 
has been developed to guide and amplify the spontaneous 
emission. Using active medium as the single-crystalline core, 
the GCCF can maintain the high cross section of the crystalline 
environment, and effectively collect the ASE. 
By means of the codrawing laser-heated pedestal growth 
method [4], single crystalline fiber cores of various active 
media were obtained. Active ions of Ce

, Ti
3+
, and Cr

attempted to generate broadband light sources from visible to 
near infrared wavelengths. The core sizes are from 5 to 40 μm 
and the fiber lengths are from a few millimeters to tens of 
centimeters depending on applications [5]-[8]. 
II. GLASS-CLAD CRYSTAL FIBER PREPARATION 
Single crystalline fibers of various active media and 
diameters were first obtained from 500-μm-diameter source 
rods with a few diameter-reduction steps to become 30 ~ 100 
μm in diameters. They were then gone through a codrawing 
LHPG process to form double clad fibers (DCFs). During the 
codrawing process, the DCF cores were further reduced to 5 to 
20 μm depending on applications.
