摘要
一般燃料電池膜電極的組成元件有陰陽極、電解質、觸媒層與燃料傳導層等。觸媒層對
於燃料的催化效率為控制電池效率的重要因素。一般觸媒均為貴重金屬，所以有效增加觸
媒之批覆量，以及均勻控制觸媒之粒徑大小，為極需解決之重要議題。奈米碳管為導電性
良好之材料，其比表面積大可吸附較多觸媒粒子，對酸性電解質抗腐蝕性佳等特性，可作
為燃料電池電極材料之極佳載體。
本研究室過去採用微波聚醇法可以快速且均勻批覆鉑顆粒於奈米碳管，所合成之鉑粒子
約 4.1nm，粒徑分佈集中且均勻分散於奈米碳管表面。但在後續之電化學氧化還原反應中,
鉑會有毒化之現象，為過去之專題研究極需改進之問題。為改善鉑觸媒電化學氧化還原反
應中之毒化之現象，本專題研究仍採用快速之微波聚醇法，微波輔助加熱可快速還原氯鉑
酸(H2PtCl6)與氯化釕(RuCl3)溶液內之金屬觸媒粒子。探討微波聚醇法之最佳反應溫度、最
適當之醇類、以及最佳之界面活性劑，以達成快速且均勻還原鉑釕等金屬觸媒並均勻被覆
於碳基電極材料上，
研究結果顯示，採用微波聚醇法，以乙二醇為還原劑，所合成之鉑釕粒子約 4.5 nm，添
加界面活性劑，可將鉑釕合金粒子均勻分散並被覆於奈米碳管上。微波合成時間僅需 10
min，即可被附覆約 56 wt%之鉑釕觸媒在奈米碳管上。所合成之鉑釕粒子的原子數比最接
近 1:1，此為甲醇電化學反應之最理想之鉑釕原子數比值。鉑釕合金呈現 FCC 結構顯示釕
原子部份取代鉑原子。微波聚醇法合成鉑釕粒子還原性佳，可將大部分鉑釕前驅物還原成
零價鉑釕奈米粒子，鉑基觸媒之電化學活性面積為 281.88 m2/g，電流密度約達 5.5 A/cm2。。

一、前言
直接甲醇燃料電池(Direct methanol fuel
cells，DMFCs)由於可以微小化，無環境污
染問題，相關材料及其製程的開發吸引許多
學者投入探討。影響甲醇燃料電池工作效能
的主要因素是觸媒層的效能，鉑觸媒對於燃
料的作用為 Pt 觸媒粒子會分解 H2 分子為 H
原子吸附在碳電極材料上，為控制電池效料
的重要因素，而由於鉑價格相當昂貴，能夠
有效控制鉑顆粒之大小，增加鉑粒子被覆量
及均勻分散性，為發展直接甲醇燃料電池之
重要技術。
二、文獻探討
甲醇在陽極電化學反應中，鉑觸媒會與中
間物一氧化碳(CO)產生反應，隨著質子和電
子的產生與遷移，使甲醇脫氫，即與一氧化
碳形成毒化現象。在甲醇電催化氧化反應機
制上，當最後一個質子離開鉑(Pt)粒子後，
會與一氧化碳(CO)鍵結形成 Pt-(CO)ads，造
成鉑觸媒毒化現象(Poison effect)。此時鉑即
失去活性無法再繼續進行電催化反應。通常
添加第二金屬觸媒釕(Ru)以協助水分子的
活化與解離，藉此提供活性氧以促進 CO 氧
化成 CO2 後離開鉑觸媒表面，如此鉑粒子便
可繼續進行電催化反應。許多研究顯示鉑釕
雙元合金觸媒的原子數比為 1:1 時具有最佳
的電催化特性[1-3]，以雙元合金觸媒來進行
電催化反應過程中，甲醇之電催化動力的分
工 機 制 稱 為 雙 功 模 式 (bi-functional
mechanism)，包含了鉑粒子活化 C–H 鍵以
利於甲醇釋放質子與電子，氧化活性較高的
釕粒子進行低電位的水吸附活化反應並修
飾 鉑 粒 子 表 面 電 子 特 性 的 電 子 效 應
用水溶性界面活性劑來改善其分散問題，因
此於溶劑中添加適量之去離子水可幫助界
面活性劑之完全溶解，添加去離子水有助於
界面活性劑發揮分散作用。

四、結論
以微波聚醇法可以快速將鉑釕先驅物還
原成鉑釕合金粒子被覆於純化之奈米碳管
上。純化後奈米碳管可增加比表面積，提升
鉑釕奈米粒子被覆量。乙二醇介電常數和介
電損失較高，因此在微波輻射下能快速被加
熱，使用乙二醇為還原劑溶解氯鉑酸-氯化
釕，並添加等比例之界面活性劑，可將鉑釕
合金粒子均勻分散並被覆於奈米碳管上。最
佳合成溫度為 140℃，微波反應時間約 10
min，所合成之鉑釕合金粒子被覆量即可達
約 56 wt %，鉑釕合金粒子粒徑約 4-5 nm。
以微波聚醇法合成鉑釕粒子還原性佳，可將
大部分鉑釕前驅物還原成零價鉑釕奈米粒
子，鉑與釕氧化物與鹵化物含量極少。因鉑
釕奈米粒子均勻被覆於奈米碳管上，鉑吸附
一氧化碳(CO)毒化現象降低，其鉑活性面積

CHARACTERIZATION OF PREPARATION Pt-Ru ALLOY CATALYSTS ON CARBON
NANOTUBES BY MICROWAVE POLYOL METHOD.
Mi Chen1, Su-Chen Lu1, Horng-Show Koo2
1Department of Chemical and Materials Engineering, Minghsin University of Science and Technology,
Hsinchu, Taiwan ,R.O.C.
2Department of Optoelectronic System Engineering, Ming-Hsin University of Science and Technology, Hsinchu,Taiwan,
R.O.C.
Abstract
The loading amounts and uniform dispersion of catalyst particles are the major bottlenecks in the technical development of
methanol fuel cells. In this article, PtRu nanoparticles on purified carbon nanotubes (CNTs)were prepared by microwave polyol
method which provided a more homogeneous temperature gradient for the quick nucleation and isotropic loading of PtRu particles
on CNTs. The technique has advantages of simplified processes, enhanced adsorption capability, high efficiency of reduction
reaction and low-cost operation, compared to those of the processes of physical sputtering and chemical sol-gel method. CNTs were
oxidized after HNO3 acidic treatments and purifications to enhance the anchoring and dispersion of metallic nnanoparticles. In
microwave system, Metal chloride salts can rapidly dissociate and reduce metal catalyst particles, and then deposit on CNTs.
However, process temperature, reaction time and additives are important factors that affect the reduction rate of metallic particles
from metal chloride salts. There is 18wt% Ru atom substitution Pt and formation FCC PtRu alloy structure.
Key Word: Pt-Ru, nanoparticles, MWCNTs, purification, microwave polyol
I. Introduction
Direct methanol fuel cell (DMFCs) provides a clean and
mobile power sources with high conversion efficiency. It is well
known that the activity of metal catalysts is highly dependent on
particle sizes and size distributions [1]. Owing to the expensive
costs of platinum, items concerned include the usage reduction
of platinum catalysts, the adsorption enhancement and the
homogenous dispersion of Pt- based catalysts. The promotion of
reduction rate on anode electrodes are the major bottlenecks in
commercial developments of DMFC [2-4]. CNTs possessing
high stability, good porosity and high surface area are suitable
materials for electrodes and catalyst supports in DMFC[5-6]..
The Pt-based particles have been fully supported on CNTs by
generalized chemical reduction techniques. On the other hand,
Pt-Ru alloys are currently regarded as one of the most active
catalysts for methanol oxidation. Literature about the deposition
of Pt, Ru, Pt-Ru on CNTs were reported [7-11]. A great number
of reports focused on the chemical modifications of CNTs,
which have been shown to improve the metallic particle
dispersion [12-14]. Several research groups studied the
synthesis of metallic catalysts with the generalized microwave
oven by polyol process [15-16] These reports showed that the
microwave synthesized metal nanoparticles were uniform in
size, and well dispersed on support material within short
reaction time. But a household microwave oven is hard to be
controlled temperature. So, the loading amount (20~30 wt%)
and the dispersion density of Pt particles on support are still
lower than there of convention methods. The activity of those
metallic catalysts has been indicated to be dependent on
synthetic conditions [17]. Uniform Pt metallic nanoparticles
were rapidly and efficiently supported on CNTs by microwave
polyol within so short a duration as 1.5 min [18] in our previous
work.
In this work, a professional microwave assisted heating
system was used to load Pt-Ru alloy nanoparticles on purified
MWCNTs by polyol process. The microwave polyol technique
has advantages of simplified processes, high-efficiency
reduction reaction, low operation cost over the processes of
physical sputtering and chemical sol-gel synthesis. MWCNTs
were purified by the microwave digestion process. After acidic
purification treatments, CNTs surfaces have been functionalized
with functional groupies [19-21] of excellent affinity for
precious metal catalysts. Highly dispersed Pt-Ru nanoparticles
were obtained by additive addition in this work. The effects of
processing temperature, additive addition and surfactant are also
investigated in this article. The surfactant addition may account
for the evident dispersion and loading enhancements.
II. Experimental procedures
MWCNTs with the diameter from 40~60 nm were used as
supporting materials. The raw CNTs were purified by
microwave digestion acidic treatment method reported
previously [22,23].. MWCNTs were oxidized after HNO3
acidic treatments and purifications to enhance the anchoring and
dispersion of metallic nnanoparticles[12-14].
H2PtCl6 and RuCl3 were dissolved in ethylene glycol (EG),
and purified MWCNTs samples were put into a TFM
microwave closed vessel. The poly vinyl pyrrolidone (PVP),
sodium dodecyl sulfate (SDS), dodecyldimethyl (3-sulfo-propyl)
nanoparticles were obtained by additive addition of surfactant of
SDS and SB-12 which can modify the surface characteristics of
Pt-Ru nanoparticles and CNTs. It may account for the evident
dispersion and loading enhancements
3.2.Effect of reaction temperatures on supporting and
particle size of Pt-Ru/MWCNTs
Fig 2 shows the XRD spectra of Pt-Ru/MWCNTs synthesized
at diferrent temperature with addition of SDS and SB-12. The
reaction temperature were 80, 100, 120, 140, and 160 ℃. The
major diffraction peak at 2θ = 40.1°, 46.5°, 68.1°, 82.1° and
86.5°, corresponding to the reflection planes of (111), (200),

39.27 ° [24]. The XRD pattern of Pt-Ru/CNTs also indicates
peaks consistent with the face-centered cubic structure. It can be
observed that there is only a weak signal found in the sample
synthesized at 80 and 100 ℃ at 2θ = 40.1°. It is indicated that
there is no Pt-Ru particle loading on CNTs of 80 ℃ and 100 ℃..
When the temperature keep increasing above 100 ℃ the peak
become much stronger in the sample of 120,140 and 160 ℃
Pt-Ru nanoparticles disperse non-uniform and the loading
amount is non-ideal at 120 ℃. The TEM image of Figure 1(d)
shows a higher supporting and uniform dispersion of Pt-Ru

3.3 Effect of processing time on the loading amount of Pt-Ru
particles
The ICP-AES analysis of the loading amount of Pt-Ru was
conducted with different synthesized of 3 min rising time, 3min,
5 min, 10min and 30 min at 140 ℃. Table 2 shows ICP-AES
analysis of the Pt-Ru loading amount by various loading time.
The results indicated that the loading amounts was 48.3 wt%
with only 3 min rising time. As the reaction time increases, the
loading amount of Pt-Ru also increases slightly.
The loading amount of Pt-Ru increases to 58.6 wt% with 30
min of reaction time. However, the long treatment period is not
only time and energy consuming, but also yields double or triple
stacking layer of Pt-Ru particles. These results were consistent
with TEM image of Fig.1(d). Fig. 3 shows TEM images of
microwave polyol synthesized Pt-Ru/CNTs nanoparticles at 140
℃. for10min. It indicated that 10 min processing time can
achieve homogeneous dispersion and high loading amount of
Pt-Ru nanoparticles and the atomic composition of Pt-Ru was
close to one. So, the optimum reaction time is 10 min which the
loading amounts of Pt-Ru particles will approach of 55.5 wt%.
The atomic ratios of Pt/Ru in Pt-Ru/CNTs are 1.06 with
10min and 1.1 with 30 min reaction time. The atomic ratio of
Pt/Ru was chosen to be close to Pt50-Ru50 the most active
composition for methanol electro-oxidation reaction[26] While
some atomic ratio are less than one. This indicated that a
segregated of Pt and Ru On the surface of CNTs. However, we
can efficient loading Pt-Ru nanoparticles on purified CNTs with
only 10 min by microwave polyol mehtos.

IV. Conclusions
In this article, microwave polyol can efficient achieve high
dispersion and optimized loading amount of Pt-Ru nanoparticles
on purified MWCNT. Higher reaction temperatures can
obviously enhance reduction rate and rapidly synthesize Pt-Ru
nanoparticles. But the higher temperature nanoparticles are
prone to the formation aggregation deteriorate dispersion and
supporting characteristics. The appropriate temperature is
shown to be 140℃. The addition of SDS and SB-12 surfactant
species for the prepared samples can achieve complete reduction
and homogeneous dispersion and the high loading amounts of
Pt-Ru nanoparticles with 4 nm on purified MWCNTs. The
loading amount of Pt-Ru increases to 58.6 wt% with 30 min of
reaction time. However, the long treatment period is not only
time and energy consuming, but also yields double or triple
stacking layer of Pt-Ru particles. The optimum reaction time is
10 min which the loading amounts of Pt-Ru particles will
approach of 55.5 wt%. The atomic ratios of Pt/Ru in
Pt-Ru/CNTs are 1.06 . There is 18wt% Ru atom substitution Pt
and formation FCC PtRu alloy structure.
