 3
此類型磨料加工時容易飛散，降低加工的穩定性；另一類為結合式磨料(bounded magnetic abrasives, 
BMA) [6-9]，此種磨料為燒結成型，利用化學或其它程序將導磁性粒子與磨粒燒結而成，因此一般加
工產業在使用此類型磨料時需與特殊廠商訂購，導致加工成本的增加。Yamaguchi 等人[3]利用 UMA，
將磨料以強磁吸附於圓管內壁上，並運用迴轉與震動的方式對氧化鋁陶瓷管做拋光，20 分鐘後可將表
面粗糙度降至 0.02µm。而 Wang 等人[6]則是利用 BMA，介由磁力吸附磨料於管內壁上以迴轉的方式，
對鋁合金、黃銅及不鏽鋼管內部進行研磨拋光，4 分鐘後表面粗糙度由 0.8µm~1.5µm 降至 0.1µm 以下。
為了改善上述缺點，Wang 等人[10]進而使用了矽膠膠體混合導磁性粒子與硬質磨粒，製成導磁性膠體
磨料，進行膠體磨料磁力研磨加工(magnetic finishing with gel abrasive, MFGA)，加工時不僅利用迴轉運
動，還增加了軸向運動，且在短時間內使表面粗糙度從 0.65µm 降至 0.029µm。膠體磨料磁力研磨加工，
加工時不但不易飛散，也幾乎不受工件之幾何外形所限制，大幅提高加工效能。但上述實驗只使用單
一膠體，且只對管外壁加工，所以本實驗將三種膠體磨料運用在 SUS304 不鏽鋼管內壁的研磨拋光，
探討表面粗糙度與材料移除率的變化。 
2. 研究原理與方法 
2.1 導磁性膠體拋光研磨之基本原理 
    導磁性膠體磁力研磨加工以較簡單的敘述方式敘述其加工原理如圖一所示，係將一管材放置於磁
極與導磁性製具間，在導磁性製具與管材之間置入由膠體、導磁性粒子與硬質磨料結合成的導磁性膠
體磨料，而磁極與導磁性製具會形成一個封閉的磁場迴路，將導磁性膠體磨料吸附於製具上，並沿著
磁力線方向而產生相互的連結，向外延伸形成可自由分離與連結的磁刷（Magnetic Brush）[11]如圖二
所示。當在高速迴轉研拋時，磁性磨料與工件產生相對運動，磁刷尖端因導磁性粒子擠壓硬質粒子於
管材內壁上而產生相互摩擦之作用，藉由摩擦力對表面較為凸出的部份進行微量的移除。而一般研磨
加工中為了讓加工表面達到均勻且平滑的表面，研磨加工時幾乎都採用多方向運動，故本實驗所使用
之磁力研磨加工機除了圓周方向的運動還添加了軸向的運動來得到光滑且平整的表面。而磁力研磨除
了一般運動行程外還需要一個磁力研磨特有且主要的裝置即磁力產生設備，為達磁力研磨設備上的需
求，所以特別開發本次實驗所使用的設備。圖三為磁力研磨加工機，此加工機包含磁力控制系統、轉
動系統，往復系統和控制面板。磁力控制系統採用兩個感應電磁設備讓磁極產生磁場。旋轉系統採用
直流馬達（M1）將工件夾至夾頭進行旋轉運動。往復系統利用偏心凸輪、往復馬達（M2）和變頻器
產生往復運動。而控制面板是調整電流、電壓、旋轉速率與往復頻率。 
 
2.2 實驗材料 
    本實驗利用膠體作為結合介質，將金鋼砂磨料與鋼砂充分混入膠體內，作為研磨拋光中的導磁性
膠體磨料。而實驗中所使用之膠體分別為矽膠膠體、水性噪音膠(豆膠)、水性沙皮膠(豆膠)，係將膠體
混合了碳化矽磨料及鋼砂如圖四所示，運用在磁力研磨加工中，因膠體磨料加工時，加工表面流動性
良好，所以磨粒不斷更新就會產生自銳的效果，而大幅提高加工效能。而三種膠體之黏滯係數如下所
示： 
(1) 矽膠膠體之黏滯係數為      ：170 Pa．s 
(2) 水性噪音膠體之黏滯係數為  ：9.51 Pa．s 
(3) 水性沙皮膠體之黏滯係數為  ：1.24 Pa．s 
 
本實驗為研磨拋光加工，所選用的研拋材料為 SUS304 不鏽鋼管，因 SUS304 不鏽鋼管含有鎳(Ni)
所以抗腐蝕，且耐熱性佳，硬度值為 HV200，是目前廣泛運用的材料。而本實驗所選用的工件直徑為
ψ32mm、長度為 80mm。因工件選用 SUS304 不鏽鋼管，所以選用硬度較高的金剛砂磨料，粒徑大小
 5
的情況下，加工 30 分鐘後可以發現，因膠體磨料具有較多的水份，又因研拋時溫度逐漸上升，使膠體
內部水份快速釋出，所以研拋之表面具有較佳的流動性，又因磨料更新的速度較快，所以具有較佳的
研拋效果。 
 
3.3 不同鋼砂比例對研磨結果的影響 
    圖八為不同鋼砂比例對表面粗糙度與材料移除量的影響，由此圖可見三種不同鋼砂比例經過研拋
30 分鐘後，表面粗糙度都有明顯的下降，而與材料移除量也有明顯的上升。但比例為 1:0.5 時，即碳
化矽為 10g 及鋼砂為 5g 時，加工 30 分鐘後，其表面粗糙度僅從 0.657μm 降至 0.453μm，材料移除量
也只有 0.00613g 加工效果較 1:1 與 1:1.5 差。而比例為 1:1.5 時，即碳化矽為 10g 及鋼砂為 15g 時，加
工 30 分鐘後，其表面粗糙度可從 0.641μm 降至 0.0733μm，材料移除量也達到 0.0786g，加工效果明顯
較好。其主要因素為 1:0.5 之膠體磨料，膠體內鋼砂含量較少，此膠體磨料在研磨加工過程中，膠體磨
料因受磁場磁力作用後對表面的作用力較低，而膠體磨料所接觸管內壁的面積也相對較少，所以研磨
30 分鐘後表面粗糙度改善效果較差。而反過來說明，比例為 1:1.5 之膠體磨料，因鋼砂含量較多，在
研磨加工過程中，磁場磁力對管壁表面的作用力相對的較大，所以研磨 30 分鐘後在較大的拋光力量與
適當的水分潤滑下，對工件表面的拋光效果也可以有效提升，因此表面粗糙度改善效果較佳及材料移
除量也相對較高。 
 
3.4 不同碳化矽粒徑對研磨結果的影響 
    本實驗中為了要了解碳化矽粒徑的重要性，而利用三種不同的碳化矽粒徑大小進行磁力研磨加
工。這三種碳化矽粒徑大小分別為#2000、#4000 及#8000。圖九為不同碳化矽粒徑，對表面粗糙度與
材料移除量的影響。由此圖先從表面粗糙度觀察後得知，#8000 之膠體磨料在加工 30 分鐘後，表面粗
糙度僅能降至 0.156μm，而#4000 之膠體磨料，加工 30 分鐘後，能將表面粗糙度降至 0.0718μm。再由
材料移除量方面觀察得知，#8000 之膠體磨料加工 30 分鐘後，材料移除量僅能達到 0.075g，而#2000
之膠體磨料加工 30 分鐘後，材料移除量則有明顯的提升。從此圖可以了解，#8000 之膠體磨料，加工
30 分鐘後，研拋效果較差，表面粗糙度僅能降至 0.156μm，而#4000 之膠體磨料，加工 30 分鐘後，能
將表面粗糙度降至 0.0718μm，但材料移除量卻沒#2000 之膠體磨料多，而#2000 之膠體磨料，在加工
五分鐘前表面粗糙度改善效果略佳，但加工 10 分鐘後表面粗度改善效果較#4000 之膠體磨料差，加工
30 分鐘後材料移除卻能達到 0.075g。其主要原因為，#8000 之磨料，因粒徑較小研磨初期因無法對較
粗之表面紋路磨平，又因磨料粒徑較小，所以水分無法有效的滲透至加工面，導致溫度較高，且膠體
磨料溫度較高會導致膠體快速硬化，所以研拋效果較差。而#2000 之磨料研拋初期表面粗度較大，粒
徑較大之磨料能把較粗的表面紋路快速磨平；又因為粒徑較大，所以材料移除量明顯的隨著時間延長
而增大，但當表面較粗之紋路被磨平之後，大粒徑的碳化矽磨料無法研拋出更細緻的表面，所以研拋
效果受到限制，其溫度方面，因磨料粒徑較大，水分較易滲透至加工區域，且研拋力量較大，所以溫
度較低於#8000 之膠體磨料卻高於#4000 之膠體磨料。而#4000 之磨料介於#2000 與#8000 之間，在研
磨初期雖然效果沒有#2000 之磨料好，但研磨 10 分鐘後效果明顯提升，表面粗糙度優於#2000 之磨料，
主要原因是加工 10 分鐘後，因表面較粗紋路被磨平，且#4000 之磨料粒徑較#2000 之磨料粒徑小，可
以對較細的表面紋路做研磨，所以效果明顯較佳，其研磨後溫度變化可以觀察到#4000 之膠體磨料溫
度最低，其原因為#4000 之膠體磨料的流動性較#2000 之膠體磨料流動性佳，而水分滲透到研磨表面的
效果也比#8000 來得好，所以#4000 之膠體磨料具有較佳的穩定性。

中文: 
磁力研磨是一種快速且高精度的拋光方法，但在研磨非導磁
性材料SUS304不鏽鋼管時，會降低導磁性磨料之磁力，並且，因
產生的離心力易將磨料拋離加工區域，進而降低拋光的穩定性。
因此，本實驗開發不同的膠體當結合劑，將導磁性磨粒與硬質磨
粒拘束於膠體內部，形成導磁性膠體磨料，因膠體磨料具有黏滯
性，不但可以將磨粒包覆住，讓磨粒不易飛散，且膠體磨料可以
緊密貼附在管壁表面，增加拋光的穩定性。 
英文: 
    Magnetic abrasive finishing(MAF) is a fast and high-precision 
polishing method. However, the magnetic force of abrasive particles 
will drop when polishing non-permeability material such as SUS304 
stainless steel tube. Moreover, the abrasive particles will be moved off 
the surface of machining area due to the centrifugal force of rotation,
and reduce the stability of polishing. Therefore, this study developed a 
series of gels as the bonding material to combine the permeability 
abrasive particles and hard abrasive particles, then consisted a series of 
permeability abrasive gels. Generally, those abrasive gels have higher 
viscosity to dominate the flow property that will constrain uniform 
motion of the abrasive particles in MAF, and the abrasive gels can be 
tightly contacted to the wall surface to increase the stability of 
polishing.

3. Results and discussion 
The re-sticky phenomenon of the tungsten carbide in 
EDM was discussed in the following section. Polarity of 
electrode, kinds of tungsten carbide and rotation of the 
electrode were three variables that used to examine 
whether the debris will re-stick on the surface or not. 
MRR and the micrograph from SEM were two indexes 
to show the re-sticky effect in EDM. Besides, the 
solution of remove the re-sticky phenomenon by the 
machining parameter of jump distance and special shape 
electrode were introduced in this study. 
 
3.1 Polarity 
Polarity of the electrode (or workpiece) will dominate 
the working efficiency in EDM, so it is worth discussing 
the re-sticky phenomenon of the polarity first. In this 
experiment, tungsten carbide (K-20) was used as the 
workpiece and the electrodes had 200 rpm rotational 
speed in EDM. Fig. 1 shows the effects of the polarities 
on MRR. The results demonstrated that no matter what 
kinds of electrode were used, large MRR could be found 
in the negative polarity of the electrodes. Since the 
copper had good thermal conductivity, the results also 
presented that the cupper electrode produced better MRR 
than the tungsten electrode did. The electrodes wear on 
the end surface, photographed from SEM, is presented in 
the Fig. 2. These Fig. revealed that the end surface of the 
electrodes had a regular wear in the positive polarity, but 
the end surface of the electrodes was penetrated a hole 
by the re-sticky debris in the negative polarity. These 
results displayed that only negative polarity caused the 
debris of the tungsten carbide to re-stick on the 
workpiece. The re-sticky phenomenon happening in the 
working surface was that the tungsten carbide contains 
three main elements, such as tungsten, carbon and cobalt, 
where the cobalt was used to combine the tungsten and 
carbon. The melting point of the tungsten and the carbon 
exceeded three thousand degrees centigrade, but the 
melting point of cobalt only exceeded one thousand 
degrees centigrade. The cobalt was easily vaporizable or 
melting than the tungsten and the carbon in the discharge 
process. Therefore, the debris of the tungsten carbide 
might re-stick on the working surface when the cobalt 
was vaporized or melted but the tungsten and carbon 
were not. Moreover, the negative polarity of the 
electrodes was an electronic flow machining in EDM, 
the machining times were too short when the tiny 
electrons were hitting on the workpiece. Therefore, parts 
of melting debris had not enough time to vaporize during 
the machining process and this debris would re-stick on 
the working surface again. Since the electrode had 
rotated motion in EDM, so the re-sticky debris would 
accumulate in the center of the working area and caused 
the hole wear in the center of the electrodes. However, 
the positive polarity of the electrodes was an ionized 
flow machining in EDM, the ion is larger than the 
electron, so there have enough time to let the ion to 
fabricate the workpiece. It made the debris had enough 
time to vaporize and take away from the discharge gap in 
EDM. Hence, the electrode has regular wear in the 
machining process. However, the depth of the hole in the 
cupper electrode was about 150 μm, and the depth of the 
hole in the tungsten electrode was near 250 μm, so wear 
of the tungsten electrode was larger than the wear of the 
cupper electrode.

was investigated in this study. Dielectric in a small gap

on the machined surface, SEM photograph indicated 
height of the hills after different jumping distance. 
According to this Fig., fine circulation of dielectric 
reduced the height of re-sticky debris with increasing in 
the jump distance. However, slight material re-sticky on 
machined surface was still generated.

3.4 Effect of special shape electrode on re-sticky 
debris 
 
Adjusting jump distances as an approach to remove the 
re-sticky debris was not worked effectively. As a result, 
special shape of electrode was used to further improve the 
re-sticky effect on machined surface. A piece of electrode 
was cut 5 mm long from the bottom of the electrode in the 
axial direction, as shown in Fig. 8, widths of the electrode 
after cutting were 0.5D, 0.6D, 0.7D, 0.8D and 0.9D, here 
D indicated the diameter of original electrode. Fig. 9 
presents the effect of electrode with special shape on 
material removed rates of tungsten carbide. According to 
the results, material removed rate of tungsten carbide was 
decrease with increasing in the width of electrode; 
material removed rate was remarkably reduced when 
width of electrode exceeded 0.8D. During the discharge 
process, if the shape of electrode was not circle, not only 
increases the side gap between the electrode and the 
material, but also elevates the stirring effect of the 
dielectric; therefore, debris of tungsten carbide was easily 
circulated out of the discharge area, which resulted in 
discharge concentration and therefore increased 
machining efficiency. Fig. 10 displays the change of 
re-sticky debris on machined surface with using different 
width of electrode. These figures showed that debris 
re-sticky on the machined surface gradually reduced in 
EDM when the width of electrode was decreased; debris 
of tungsten carbide would not re-stick on the machined 
surface with utilizing 0.6D width of electrode. However, 
debris would re-stick on the machined surface when with 
of electrode reduced to 0.5D. This is largely owing to big 
side gap between workpiece and electrode providing good 
circulated effect of dielectric, therefore, debris of tungsten 
carbide was easily removed in discharge process; however, 
parts of debris easily accumulated in the dead-center point 
and re-stuck on the center of the machined surface if 0.5D 
width of electrode was used. Compared with adjusting the 
jump distance to eliminate re-sticky debris, electrode with 
width 0.6D directly prevented the re-stick phenomenon 
occurred on the workpiece.
