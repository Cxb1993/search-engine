Abstract 
The semiconductor supply chains are long in 
geographical distance, segmented in ownership, 
varied in business objectives, and demanding in 
collaboration. The challenges of supply chains lie 
in effective collaboration between engineering and 
manufacturing, service monitoring and control, and 
achieving reliable delivery performance. The new 
paradigm of manufacturing services requires new 
methods of operation control. This project proposes 
to develop enabling methodologies for 
collaborative planning and scheduling in 
semiconductor supply chains. In this project, fluid 
models are used to represent the dynamics of 
supply chains. A dynamic control model has been 
developed and used to analyze the value of 
production information. It was found that the gains 
of the control system can be utilized as a surrogate 
measure of information value. Finally, nonlinear 
programs are developed to estimate the synergy of 
collaborative production control. 
Keywords : supply chain control, fluid models, 
information value
