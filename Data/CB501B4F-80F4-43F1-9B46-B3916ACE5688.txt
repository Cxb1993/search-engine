Abstract 
This main work of this proposal in the first year was on the surface modification of 
silica particles and the monolayer behavior of these modified particles on air/liquid 
interface. Silica particles are hydrophobized either by chemical graft of alkyl chains or by 
physical adsorption of cationic surfactants, alkyltrimethylammonium bromide. The effects 
of the two modification methods on the monolayer behavior of silica particles at the 
air/water interface are studied,as well as the packing structure of the particulate films. The 
results show that the hydrophobicity of particles chemically modified by octanol(SiO2-C8) 
and dodecanol (SiO2-C12) are similar and higher than that modified by butanol (SiO2-C4). 
The monolayer composed of particles with higher hydrophobicity shows a large lift-off 
area, higher compressibility, and significant hysteresis due to the higher particle–particle 
interaction.s a result, the particulate films exhibit 2-dimensional (2D) aggregative domains 
of closely-packed structure, but with particle free regions presenting among the domains. 
The monolayer prepared by SiO2-C4 shows a contrary behavior resulted from the higher 
particle–water interaction. The particles modified by adsorption of cationic surfactants 
have an amphiphilic property at the air/water interface. Such monolayer exhibits lower 
compressibility and hysteresis, higher re-spreading characteristic, and a lower collapse 
pressure compared with those of the chemically modified particles. A particulate film with 
high uniformity and closely-packed structure can be obtained by using the 
octyltrimethylammonium bromide (OTAB) modified particles. When the alkyl chain of 
surfactant increases, the packing of the particles becomes looser. Such phenomenon is 
probably caused from the higher probability for the long-chain surfactants to stay at the

第一章 緒論 
1.1 前言 
    近年來，奈米粒子在材料化學的領域上已經引起相當廣泛的興趣，不管是理
論方面的探討或是實際上的應用。由單一分散奈米粒子所組成高度規則排列的材料更
是受到特別的重視。2-D 規則排列的微米或奈米結構薄膜在光學性質方面有相當好的
表現(Tieke et al. ; Dimitrov et al., 1999; Goodwin et al., 1980)，可應用於抗反射塗層
(Yoldas et al., 1984; Hinz et al., 1986)、生化觸媒 (Huang et al., 1999)、微透鏡成像
(Hayashi et al., 1991)。高度規則排列的材料在物理上的特殊結構可使光波在物質中的
電磁特性改變，Yablonovitch 及 John 在 1987 年同時指出當週期排列之粒子大小為電
磁波波長之 1/2 時，也就是遠紅外線與可視光之波長大小之 1/2（300～700nm），我們
只需要改變材料中介質的排列結構及介電常數，就可以控制電磁波在材料中的行為，
而不需要改變介質的化學結構。僅需在介質粒徑的大小以及光子能隙進行設計便可製
造出具有不同光特性的產物，此種新式的人工晶體稱為光子晶體(photonic crystal)，是
具有高度潛力的新一代光電材料。光子晶體是在一維、二維或三維尺度上具有高度規
則排列的材料。一般所謂的光學多層膜即是一維架構的光子晶體，已被廣泛地應用在
光學鏡片上。而具有二維或是三維高度秩序排列的結構則是目前在光子晶體領域中最
受重視的一環。為了得到具有以上光學特性的 2-D 粒子薄膜或 3-D 光子晶體，除了要
製備出粒徑大小均一的膠體溶液外，也要能夠精確的控制粒子的排列結構。 
在最近幾年的研究中，有許多製造 2-D 粒子薄膜或 3-D 光子晶體的方法被開發出
來，例如電泳沉積法、沉降法、溶劑蒸發法、LB 沉積法或自組裝。當然，每個方法
都有它的優點和缺點，有些可以簡單的製造出光子晶體，可是組裝速度上卻很慢，有
些很快的完成製造，但在粒子的排列上卻不好控制。

第二章  文獻回顧 
2.1 SiO2奈米粒子的製備 
根據 Stober et al.(1968)所提出的方法，在乙醇、水、氨水的環境下，將 TEOS 進
行水解反應，粒徑分佈大小從 50nm~1um 的二氧化矽粒子已經被成功的合成出來
(Badley et al., 1990; Zhan et al., 2003)，而粒子粒徑的大小及分佈主要受到反應物的濃
度、溶液的 pH 值所影響。在 Zhan(2003)的研究中發現，TEOS 在乙醇中的濃度對二
氧化矽粒子的形狀及大小有相當大的影響。稀釋過的 TEOS 溶液可以減少成核成長中
核的數目及粒子聚集的現象，可以增加溶液的單一粒徑分散性(monodisperse)並簡化
純化的程序。 
2.2 SiO2表面改質 
為了讓親水性的粒子均勻地分散在非極性有機溶劑中，可以在粒子表面進行化學
反應或物理性吸附的表面修飾，使粒子表面呈疏水性或帶電。二氧化矽粒子表面會帶
有氫氧基，屬於親水性，在表面修飾上，可以利用此氫氧基(-OH)作為化學改質的反
應基團，以得到不同的化學或物理特性(Vansant et al., 1995)。針對二氧化矽表面改質
最常用的反應劑為矽烷(silane)，其化學式可以表示為:R4-nSiXn(n=1-3)，而 X 可為 Cl 、
甲氧基(-OCH3，-OMe)、乙氧基(OC2H5，OEt)。反應時，X 會發生水合作用和粒子表
面的氫氧基產生共價鍵結合(圖 2-1)，而 R 是反應後暴露在基板表面的基團，通常可
以利用 R 來控制表面的特性(Plueddeman et al., 1991)。不過使用矽烷(silane)作改質劑
時，反應過程副反應發生的可能性較大，整個反應結果容易受到反應條件影響。只要
n>1，改質劑(silanes)彼此就可能發生化學反應而影響整個基板的表面覆蓋率(Vallant et 
al., 1999)及均勻度。Chen et al., (2001) 研究溶液中水的含量對基板表面矽烷改質的影
響，他們發現當溶液中有太多水分 (35±2ppm) 存在，反應溫度在 31±1℃下，溶液中
 11
情況。另外，也有文獻提到路易斯鹼的催化下，對矽氧烷基(Si-OR)水解反應的影響
(Ossenkamp et al., 2001)。 
Ossenkamp et al. (2001) 的研究中特別對酯化反應的覆蓋率、水解穩定性及粒子
表面特定官能基的導入作探討。他發現粒子表面的覆蓋率可以經由 Langmuir 等溫吸
附模型進行預測，達到表面覆蓋率精確控制。酯化反應完的二氧化矽粒子再經
hexamethyldisilazane 處理過後，可以有效的改善水解穩定性(hydrolytic stability)，使
粒子較不容易發生水解反應(hydrolysis)。選擇適當的改質劑(例如:HS(CH2)n OH)可以
使粒子表面帶有含硫的官能基(-S)，而這些官能基可以做進一步的化學反應，不過實
驗也發現擁有這些官能基會使得水解穩定性變差。 
另外一種表面改質的方式為物理性吸附法(Van Duffelet al., 2002; Szekeres et al., 
2002)，將界面活性劑吸附到親水性粒子的表面，使其能穩定的分散在有機溶劑中(例
如:chloroform、cyclohexane、toluene)。這種物理性的表面改質方法不像化學性改質法
一樣會有副反應的發生而使粒子產生聚集。只須配合機械震盪並加入適當量及適當種
類的界面活性劑，即可得到單一粒徑的分散液(monodisperse)。 
2.3 粒子親疏水性的量測與粒子在氣液界面的行為 
粒子於溶液中的分散技術在許多方面都扮演相當重要的角色，例如:化妝品工
業、油漆、建築材料、特殊塗佈、去泡劑及石油工業。而膠體粒子在溶液中的分散情
形決定於粒子和溶劑間的潤濕性、粒子的表面帶電量、粒子的大小等。粒子和溶劑的
潤濕性越好、粒子表面電荷密度越高可以得到較穩定均勻分散的膠體溶液。而粒子的

2.4 CdS 量子點的合成 
關於各種奈米粒子的合成方法與技術，Clemens Burda 學者在 2005 年發表的文
獻已經有非常詳盡的分類與整理(Burda et al.2005)，其整理出的奈米粒子合成方法包
含液體溶膠法（sol process）、溶膠凝膠法（sol-gel process）、微乳化法（microemulsion 
process）、化學沈澱法（chemical precipitation）、水熱法（hydrothermal synthesis）、熱
解法（pyrolysis）與氣相沈積法（vapor deposition）。本研究中主要是合成 CdS 量子點
粒子，其中 CdS 奈米粒子隨粒徑減小至 2.5 nm 時，其熔點會由 1750℃快速下降至
400℃，此外，CdS 的成核（nucleation）及成長（growth）速率必須進一步控制才能
得到較高品質純相與粒徑均勻的量子點。為了得到適當粒徑大小分佈的 CdS 量子點，
以濕式溶膠法及微乳化法可得到較且粒徑均勻的量子點，以下針對液體溶膠法及微乳
化法作介紹。 
2.4.1 液體溶膠法 
液體溶膠法是藉由添加有機分子螯合劑作為保護劑，與反應物先形成錯合物，此
類螯合劑分子通常能均勻的分散在適當溶劑中，形成均勻分散的小簇集（cluster）以
包覆反應物。此小簇集的尺寸一般都是在奈米級範圍。在反應進行時，由於反應空間
 15
往外朝向水相，如圖 2-2(b)所示。以 o/w 系統為例，當界面活性劑加入溶液中時，剛
開始界面活性劑會存在於水相與油相的界面處，隨著界面活性劑的增加，在達臨界微
胞濃度（critical micelle concentration，CMC）時，界面活性劑將以親油基朝向油相，
親水基朝向水相並自發性聚集而形成微胞（micelle）結構，油相則被微胞的結構包覆
而分散在水相中。若是在 w/o 時，水相則被包覆而分散在油相中，形成逆微胞（reverse 
micelle）結構。 
在逆微胞合成奈米粒子的方法是將陰、陽離子反應物水溶液分別包覆在逆微胞
中，如圖 2-3 所示(Harruff et al.2003; Khomane et al.2003)。當此兩逆微胞溶液混合時，
陰、陽離子會在不同微胞內發生交換，微胞中的陰陽離子一旦相遇則會發生快速反應
而生成奈米粒子，粒子的生成過程皆是在逆微胞結構內完成，因此所合成的粒子大小
就會受到逆微胞內水相的體積所侷限。由此可知，欲合成奈米粒子大小可由逆微胞系
統中的油/水比例來調整，進而控制量子點的大小。整體而言，微乳化法可利用簡單
界面化學系統合成所需的量子點，微乳化法具有簡單控制粒徑分佈的優點，除此之
外，其材料便宜、合成步驟簡單、可於室溫下操作且產率較高，本研究即是採用微乳
化法合成 CdS 量子點組裝薄膜。

3.1 化學改質 SiO2粒子薄膜性質 
3.1.1 等溫曲線 
將200μl 的溶液灑在氣液界面，為了讓粒子能夠均勻分散到氣液界面，避免3D
結構的產生，因此二氧化矽粒子溶液必須持續保持震盪。灑在氣液界面的粒子數約為
5.1×10-8 umole，待10分鐘後氯仿揮發，阻隔棒以5mm/min的速度進行壓縮。在未壓縮
前，可以觀察到SiO2-8C和SiO2-12C粒子在氣液界面上有明顯的聚集區域，而SiO2-4C
粒子則觀察不到此現象，判斷應該和粒子在氣液界面上的接觸角有關，對一雙親性分
子而言，親水基與水面的作用力是促使此分子在氣/液界面擴張的主要因素。由於
SiO2-4C粒子在氣液界面的接觸角較小(親水性較強)，因此此類粒子在水面上的擴張能
力佳，無明顯的聚集體存在氣/液界面。但SiO2-8C和SiO2-12C粒子，由於疏水性較強，
在氣液界面上的接觸角較大，即此粒子與水面間的作用力小，擴張能力較弱，因此可
以明顯的看到粒子在氣/液界面上聚集的紋路。 
在等溫線的圖形上(3-1)，可以看到不同親疏水性粒子薄膜，它們的崩潰面積相當接
近，都在接近六方最密堆積的面積(1.96×105 nm2/particle)時發生崩潰。SiO2-4C的等溫
線在靠近崩潰面積時，急劇上升，代表此粒子薄膜硬且穩定。而SiO2-8C和SiO2-12C
粒子薄膜在壓縮至面積3×105nm2/particle時，膜壓已開始緩慢上升，接近面積
1.98×105nm2/particle時發生崩潰。而利用文獻中提到等溫線崩潰壓和接觸角的關係
Πc=πγgl (1+cosθ)2/[2(3)1/2] (Clint et al., 1992)，Πc 為薄膜崩潰壓，γgl 為氣液界面張力，
θ 為粒子在氣液界面接觸角。SiO2-4C、SiO2-8C、SiO2-12C粒子薄膜的崩潰壓分別為
26.69mN/m、31.02mN/m、35.88mN/m，所對應的接觸角分別為68∘、108∘、105 (∘ 如

3.2 化學改質 SiO2 粒子薄膜表面型態 
分別將 SiO2-4C、SiO2-8C、SiO2-12C 粒子製備成膠體溶液撒在氣液界面上，在

SiO2-4C 膠體溶液的分散性較差，因此粒子在溶液中可能有部分已經聚集，而

3.4 物理改質 SiO2粒子薄膜性質 
3.4.1 等溫曲線 
將吸附陽離子型界劑的 SiO2 粒子灑到氣/液界面時，無法由肉眼觀察到明顯的白
色聚集區域，顯示粒子在氣/液界面上有極佳的分散性。如果只是把界劑分子灑到氣/
液界面上，在阻隔棒壓縮的過程中，不會有明顯的膜壓出現，這是因為這些界劑的水
溶性極佳，因此界劑容易溶於副相(水)中，相反的，將改質後的 SiO2 粒子灑到氣/液
界面，在壓縮過程中，就可以觀察到明顯的膜壓。將不同界劑改質後的 SiO2 粒子灑
在氣/液界面上，進行粒子薄膜 π-A 等溫線的量測，粒子薄膜的等溫線圖形如圖 3-8，
以膜壓對粒子佔據面積做圖，所有的薄膜其等溫線圖形都是一樣的形狀。和一般單分
子薄膜不同，粒子薄膜的等溫線圖形只有三個相區，分別為無明顯膜壓變化的氣相
(gas phase, I)，膜壓陡峭且直線上升的固體相(solid phase, II)，以及最後膜壓緩慢上升
的崩潰相(collapse, III) (Szekeres et al.2002)。在不同界劑和不同粒徑的系統下，等溫線圖
皆和 3-8 相似，唯一的不同在於它們的崩潰壓不一樣(Table3-2)。 
 
3.4.2 遲滯現象 
遲滯曲線是將粒子薄膜壓縮至一特定膜壓再擴張時的膜壓變化。由遲滯曲線可以
了解粒子薄膜在氣液界面上的再分散情形。本文中遲滯現象的探討，將粒子薄膜壓縮
至膜壓為 10mN/m 後，再擴張至起始面積。對於再分散性佳的粒子薄膜，其擴張曲線
會沿壓縮曲線擴張。在物理修飾的系統下，可看到對不同的修飾界劑不同粒徑的粒子
以及不同的設定膜壓，粒子薄膜的擴張曲線都會沿壓縮曲線擴張，顯示這些粒子薄膜
的再分散性極佳(圖 3-9)，也代表這些粒子與副相(水)間有良好的親和性。此一結果亦
顯示，之後 LB 膜的沈積，使用親水性的基板，會有不錯的轉移效果。

3.6 CdS 量子點性質 
3.6.1 等溫曲線 
由文獻中已知當 CdS 量子點以整齊方式排列時，量子點間的導帶與價帶會互相
連結形成迷你傳送帶（mini-band）結構，因此本研究利用 LB 沈積技術可製備規則排
列的單層粒子結構的特點，嘗試製作 CdS 量子點薄膜，而利用 LB 沈積技術製作 CdS
量子點薄膜的首要條件即是 CdS 量子點要能穩定存在於氣液界面上。因此，此一章
節將利用 CdS 量子點等溫線來探討 CdS 量子點在氣液界面上的特性。 
本研究藉由 C12SH 化學改質 CdS 量子點與 CTAB 物理吸附 MSA-CdS 量子點，並
藉由等溫線分析不同表面改質對 CdS 量子點性質的影響。 
在等溫線的量測上，我們首先必須確定未改質的 CdS 量子點是否能存在於氣液
界面，因此必須先分析未改質的 CdS 量子點的等溫線，已知未經改質處理的 CdS 無
法穩定分散於氯仿溶液中，因此我們將未改質的 CdS/氯仿溶液以超音波劇烈震盪後
立即分散於氣液界面上。在等溫線的量測上，我們發現未改質的 CdS 量子點，但在
整個壓縮過程中都未產生明顯表面壓變化，因此我們判斷在阻隔棒壓縮過程中，CdS
易受擠壓而沈入水中而無法穩定存在氣液界面上。為了使 CdS 能存在氣液界面，我
們將未改質的 CdS 量子點純化後分散於 3000 ppm CTAB/氯仿溶液中，帶正電荷的
CTAB 會吸附於帶微弱負電荷的 CdS 表面，形成物理性改質（CTAB-CdS），其等溫
線如圖 3-11 所示。首先，由圖 3-11 曲線 1 可知 150μL 3000 ppm CTAB 等溫線曲線在
整個壓縮過程中，表面壓僅由 1.6 mN/m 上升至 3.8 mN/m，這主要是因為 CTAB 在壓
縮過程中可解離而溶於水中，由此可知，過量的 CTAB 並不會影響 CdS 等溫線的判
斷。我們發現 CTAB-CdS 在粒子分散於氣液界面後即有高的表面壓，這代表物理改
質的 CTAB-CdS 可存在於氣液界面上，CTAB-CdS 與過量 CTAB 的存在導致較高的
 61
水性碳鏈而能存在於氣液界面，但卻也因碳鏈間的凡得瓦耳力作用而導致粒子無法形
成緊密排列的二維結構。而 CTAB-MSA-CdS 則在面積減少使得 CdS 粒子相互靠近
時，以電荷作用力吸附的 CTAB 會自由脫離（如圖 3-12 所示），使 CdS 粒子能夠相
互靠近而形成一緊密排列的粒子薄膜。另外，在氣液界面上 CdS 粒子佔據面積的比
較上，由等溫線曲線切線可求得 C12-CdS 與 CTAB-MSA-CdS 的極限面積分別為 722 
cm2/mg 與 537cm2/mg，經由每個 CdS 粒子重量與體積計算後可知，每個 C12-CdS 與
CTAB-MSA-CdS 粒子的佔據面積分別為 39.2 nm2 與 29.2 nm2。而理想的球型粒子膜
是以六方最密堆積（HCP）方式排列成二維的結構，因此每個粒子理論佔據面積應為
2√3r2，以直徑 6 nm 的 CdS 而言，理論佔據面積應為 31.2 nm2。由此可知，以 CTAB
物理吸附 MSA-CdS 的方法在氣液界面形成的粒子膜接近緊密排列狀態，但我們發現
實驗值低於理論佔據面積值，推斷其為 CTAB-MSA-CdS 在氣液界面上並不穩定及所
合成粒子非完全球狀所構成之誤差。另外，C12-CdS 則因粒子間無法相互靠近而有部
分空隙位置存在，所以實驗所得的佔據面積值較大。整體而言，利用等溫線分析可瞭
解 C12-CdS 會因表面碳鏈間吸引力較強而形成較鬆散粒子膜，而 CTAB 物理吸附配合
MSA 化學改質的 CTAB-MSA-CdS 量子點可形成緊密排列的粒子薄膜。 
3.6.2 遲滯曲線 
遲滯曲線是將 CdS 量子點粒子薄膜的分佈面積壓縮至一特定表面壓後，再將面

了解粒子薄膜在氣液界面上的再分散情形。本研究遲滯曲線分析是將 CdS 量子點粒
子薄膜壓縮至 15 mN/m 後，再擴張至初始面積，觀察表面壓隨粒子佔據面積的曲線
變化。

3.8.3硬酯胺/MSA水溶液分子層 
    當水溶液中含有不同濃度的MSA分子時，其對ODA單分子層的行為有顯著的影
響，此可由圖3-21的π-A等溫線得知。由於MSA為二質子酸，其pKa1及pKa2分別為
3.30、4.949。不同濃度的MSA溶液，會有pH值上的差異，本實驗中將副相的pH值控
制於7。由圖3-22所示，可知當硫醇濃度小於2.8×10-8M時，ODA單分子層的等溫線無
明顯的變化，可知當硫醇分子濃度很低時，對ODA單分子層分子間的作用力影響很
小，MSA分子吸附在氣/液界面的數量並不多，等溫線幾乎和ODA的等溫線相似。當
硫醇濃度再增加時，ODA等溫線往右偏移程度變大，這應是MSA吸附在氣/液界面的
數量增多，造成ODA單分子層擴張(expanded)的情況更明顯。當MSA分子吸附於ODA
單分子層，造成ODA分子的頭基變大，改變了ODA分子間的作用力，除了使得等溫
線的起始面積變大外，ODA碳鏈間不易緊密的排列，因此單分子膜傾向於較為擴張
的狀態。

吸收的強度也有增加的趨勢。

多未改質的金奈米粒子以靜電力吸附於單分子層，但是粒子的吸附密度有限，且ODA分子
易損失於氣液界面；若金奈米粒子經過MSA的修飾，可以藉由控制MSA的添加量，得到較

ODA分子帶電的頭基上，造成金粒子的吸附密度不佳。故本實驗探討了ODA單分子層在

在MSA和MSA-Au溶液上等溫線和TEM影像圖的比較，可知金粒子有較佳吸附量時MSA的
添加範圍。由鬆弛實驗可知，單分子膜在吸附金奈米粒子後，穩定性會下降，但若單分子
層有分子損失，則會使得鬆弛行為變的較不明顯。將單分子層換成PDPA高分子，副相為改
質後的金奈米粒子溶液，金粒子以靜電力吸附PDPA後，利用LB沉積技術將之轉移至固體
基板上，可在高分子上形成規則排列的單層金奈米薄膜。可提高高分子的導電度和分散性，
大大提高有機/無機混成材料的應用潛力。

water interface. The surface of silica particles are chemically grafted by alkyl
chain (a), or modified by adsorption of surfactant (b).
The effect of surface hydrophobicity of silica particles on the
property of particulate monolayer has been studied in the liter-
ature and the film quality was found to be strongly dependent
on the degree of surface hydrophobicity [22–27].
For spreading the particles at the air/water interface, appro-
priate solvent with high spreading ability should be used so that
the dispersion particles can be dragged away by the spread-
ing of solvent and distribute uniformly on the subphase. After
evaporation of solvent, the compression–expansion behavior of
the particles is a competition result between interactions of
particle–particle and particle–water. For a particle chemically
grafted by hydrocarbon chains, the alkyl groups distribute uni-
formly on the surface and the particle has a different character-
istic from that of an amphiphilic molecule. When such particles
stay at the air/water interface, the particle–water interaction
is weak as shown in Fig. 1a and the particles tend to aggre-
gate due to the higher particle–particle interaction. Obviously,
the particle–water interaction may be enhanced by decreasing
the particle hydrophobicity. The particle–particle interaction,
however, may decrease as a result. The behavior of particulate
monolayer at the air/water interface and its dependence on the
hydrophobicity of particles are important subjects being studied
for precise control of the particulate organization [22–27].
Contrary to the chemical graft method, the hydrophilic par-
ticle can also be modified by physical adsorption of surfactant.
The polar group of a surfactant adsorbs on the silica surface
with alkyl chain faces outward, so that the particles can disperse
well in a non-polar solvent and stay at the air/water interface.
Due to the reversible adsorption of surfactant molecules on par-
ticles surface, a physically-modified particle therefore behaves
like an amphilphile on water surface as schematically illustrated
in Fig. 1b. As a result, a bare silica surface can be exposed interface Science 296 (2006) 233–241
the water-contacting region of the particle and a higher particle–
water interaction is expected. The monolayer behavior of such
particles should be different from the particles modified by
chemical graft. Surprisingly, one found very few applications
of the physical capping method in fabricating colloidal crystal
in the literature [28,29].
The main purpose of this work is to compare the mono-
layer behaviors and film structures of chemically and physically
modified silica particles. Different degrees of hydrophobicity
for particle surface were made possible by surface reaction with
three alcohols and physical adsorption of three cationic surfac-
tants, respectively.
2. Experimental
2.1. Materials
Silica particles of 500 nm in diameter were purchased from
Lancaster. The particles were nearly monodispersed with a
polydispersity of 11.1%. The particles were free from or-
ganic contamination and easily dispersed in aqueous medium
and iso-propanol. 1-Butanol (99.5%) and 1-octanol (99.5%)
were obtained from Riedel–de Haen, and 1-dodecanol (95%)
was obtained from Fluka Switzerland. These alcohols were
used as surface modification agents to prepare silica parti-
cles with various surface wettability. Cationic surfactants of
various chain lengths including Octyltrimethylammonium bro-
mide (C8, OTAB, >98% purity, Fluka), Dodecyltrimethyl-
ammonium bromide (C12, DTAB, 99% purity, Sigma), and
Hexadecyltrimethylammonium bromide (C16, HTAB, 99% pu-
rity, Sigma) were used as physical modification agents. Chlo-
roform (99.9%) and iso-propanol (GC 99.8%) were supplied
by J.T. Baker and Fluka, respectively. Pure water was used as
subphase. The water used was purified by means of a Milli-Q
plus water purification system with an electric resistance of
18.2 M.

and is a characteristic of a particulate monolayer, no matter
what kind of the modification method is used.

Cadmium sulfide (CdS) quantum dots (QDs) were prepared and surface modified by dodecanthiol or
mercaptosuccinic acid (MSA) to render a surface with alkyl chains (C12-CdS) or carboxylic acid groups (MSA-
CdS), respectively. Due to the hydrophobic property of C12-CdS, the nanoparticles disperse well in chloroform
and stay stable at the air/water interface. However, 3-dimensional (3D) aggregative domains and particle-free
pores were formed in the monolayer due to poor particle-water interaction. For the MSA-CdS nanoparticles,
the surface was hydrophobized through physical adsorption of a cationic surfactant, cetyltrimethylammonium
bromide (CTAB). The capped MSA on the CdS plays an important role in enhancing the adsorption of CTAB
and improving the stability of the QDs at the air/water interface. Due to the reversible adsorption of CTAB
on MSA-CdS, a hydrophilic area can be exposed in the water-contacting region of a nanoparticle when it
stays at the air/water interface. Thus, the CTAB-MSA-CdS QD behaves as an amphiphilic compound at the
air/water interface and has properties superior to those of C12-CdS QDs in fabrication of layer-by-layer 2D
structure of particulate films. The distinct behaviors of the two QDs at the air/water interface and the related
effect on the properties of LB films were studied using a number of methods, including pressure-area (ð-
A) isotherm, relaxation and hysteresis experiments, in-situ observation of Brewster angle microscopy (BAM),
the postdeposition analysis of atomic force microscopy (AFM), and UV-vis spectroscopy.
Introduction
Semiconductors quantum dots (QDs) have recently attracted
extensive interest due to their unique photoelectrochemical
properties that arise from the quantum-size effect.1-3 The
photoelectronic and catalytic properties of the QDs can be
adjusted by changing the size of nanoparticles4,5 or by changing
the functional groups of molecules capping on the nanopar-
ticles.6,7 To be utilized in solid devices, the QDs should be
immobilized onto solid substrates. Therefore, their application
potentials rely largely on the available techniques employed to
assemble the nanoparticles.
Various attempts to immobilize the quantum-size particles
onto solid substrates have been reported, including the direct
synthesis of nanocrystals on solid surface8-10 or immobilization
of preprepared nanoparticles through the use of a self-assembly
monolayer11-15 or by way of the Langmuir-Blodgett (LB)
technique.16-20 Although self-assembly techniques have the
advantage of being versatile and fast, LB deposition offers the
possibility of fabricating particulate films into a compact,
ordered, and controllable structure.
The applications of LB technique to fabricate nanoparticulate
films may be approached through three strategies: (1) by
postdeposition treatment (heat treatment or exposure to H2S)
of the LB films containing precursors of QDs;16,21-23 (b) by
utilizing the electrostatic interaction between a Langmuir
monolayer and the preprepared particles dispersed in the
subphases;24-26 (c) by the direct spreading of surfactant-capped
particles at the air/liquid interface.27-30 The first approach has
been extensively used to fabricate quantum dots of II-VI
semiconductors through the interaction of a fatty acid monolayer
with the metal ions in subphase. The metal ions containing LB
film were then exposed to dihydrogen chalcogenides (H2X, X
) O, S, Se, Te) to form the semiconductor chalcogenide
nanoclusters (e.g. CdS). The possibility of constructing organic/
inorganic LB films with controlled molecular level thickness
offers the possibility of fabricating a wide range of nanostruc-
tures. Nanoclusters with shapes of spheres,31-33 thin sheets,34
disks,16,35 and ellipsoids36 have been reported. The numerous
structures described in the literature imply the high complexity
of the formation kinetic in the LB matrix and the difficulty in
controlling the shape and size of the nanoclusters through this
approach. As an alternative, the other two strategies provide
effective methods to control both the structure and size of QDs,
although these strategies have been studied less extensively. For
the third method, the direct spreading and compression of the
QDs at the air/liquid interface could result in 2-dimensional (2D)
monolayer films. This assembly technique not only has the
advantage of easy manipulation but also the possibility in
controlling the interparticle distance and establishing a closely
and regularly packed structure.
For a particulate monolayer at the air/liquid interface, the
surface wettability of particles is a critical parameter in
determining the monolayer behavior and the 2D arrangement
of the particulate film. Particles with highly hydrophilic proper-
ties are difficult to keep stable on the surface of an aqueous
subphase, and therefore, surface hydrophobization is required
to obtain a stable monolayer of particles. The surface hydro-
phobization is always accomplished by capping alkyl chains
on the particle surfaces, either via chemical graft37-39 or by
physical adsorption.40,41 The monolayer behavior and the quality
of particulate film have been reported to be strongly dependent
on the degree of surface hydrophobicity, as well as the capping
method used. Those studies were performed mainly by using
silica particles (with diameters of hundreds nanometer) and
novel metals (with quantum size diameters). Only a few studies

Results and Discussion
After surface modification, the dodecanethiol-capped CdS
(C12-CdS) QDs were dried and redispersed in chloroform.
Satisfactory dispersion was confirmed by the transparency of
its yellow solution and by the observation of TEM. A similar
condition can also be obtained by dispersing the MSA-capped
CdS (MSA-CdS) QDs into chloroform containing 2000 ppm
of CTAB. Without the capping of MSA, or in the absence of
CTAB in chloroform, a transparent solution cannot be obtained.
Although CdS QD was reported to have negative œ potential
(ca. -20 mV),24 the adsorption of CTAB from chloroform to a
bare CdS surface was not able to perform well in this work.
This result is attributable to the low potential of the CdS surface
because the CTAB adsorption does perform well on silica
nanoparticles which have a higher œ potential (ca. -57 mV).43
Therefore, the MSA capped on the CdS surface plays an
important role in adsorbing the CTAB molecules, a conclusion
also confirmed by the higher œ potential of MSA-CdS (ca. -55
mV). Figure 2 shows the size distributions of the CdS QDs
determined by TEM images. The TEM micrograph of C12-CdS
is also shown as a representative. The mean diameter of the
QDs is 4.9 nm for bare CdS, 5.8 for MSA-CdS, and 6.1 nm for
C12-CdS. The size dispersion of CdS QDs increases slightly after
the capping process, probably due to the growth of QDs in the
long capping process. However, since the sizes of C12-CdS and
MSA-CdS are very close, the size effect is negligible in
comparing the monolayer behaviors of the two QDs discussed
below.
The UV-vis adsorption spectra of the CdS QDs dispersed
in solution were normalized to their respective absorbance at
350 nm and are shown in Figure 3. In general, the CdS QDs
have nearly identical spectral features both before and after
capping. The adsorption edge, obtained from the intersection
of the sharply decreasing region of the spectrum with the
baseline, is ca. 472 nm for the bare CdS, 482 nm for CTAB-
MSA-CdS, and 486 nm for C12-CdS, corresponding respectively
to average diameters of 4.4, 4.8, and 5.1 nm, as estimated by
Henglein’s empirical curve.48 The mean particle size increases
slightly after surfactant capping, consistent with the result
determined by TEM. The slight deviation of particle sizes
estimated by TEM images and the UV-vis spectrum may be
caused by analysis error of the two instruments or from a
discrepancy in Henglein’s theory.
A comparison of the FT-IR spectra among pure MSA, bare
CdS, C12-CdS, and MSA-CdS is shown in Figure 4. For the
spectrum of bare CdS, the weak peaks which occur in the region
of 1000-1750 cm-1 are ascribable to the contaminants after
the purifying process. For C12-CdS, the appearance of peaks at
2954 cm-1 (îas(CH3)), 2920 cm-1 (îas(CH2)), and 2850 cm-1
(îs(CH2)) indicates the presence of alkyl chains and the
formation of dodecanthiolate was further confirmed by the
stretching vibration of C-S linkage in the 700-600 cm-1
region. The spectrum of pure MSA in this work is similar to
the result reported in the literature.49 When MSA molecules
adsorb on CdS QDs, the peaks of carboxylate vibration appear
at 1562 cm-1 (in the range 1500-1650 cm-1, asymmetric) and
1389 cm-1 (in the range 1350-1450 cm-1, symmetric). This
result indicates not only that there is attachment of MSA to the
CdS but also that the carboxylic hydrogen was dissociated and
the MSA is present in the form of a carboxyl anion.49 However,
the weak peak at 1705 cm-1 (carbonyl strecting vibration)
suggests that few MSA molecules exist in the form of dimmers.

The carboxylic acid groups on 4-CTP or the cysteine-capped
nanoparticles enable the adsorption of the particles on a positively
charged monolayer such as octadecylamine (ODA). The com-
plexation of the surface-modified nanoparticle with the monolayer
was reported to be a function of charge on the particle and
monolayer, which may be controlled by varying the pH of the
subphase.30
Although previous reports show that the adsorption of
nanoparticles on an ODA monolayer was enhanced by the
modification of the nanoparticles, the adsorbed particle density
is still limited within a monolayer. The reason is probably that
the higher pH (8-10) required to ionize the carboxylic acids
on the particles is detrimental to the protonization of amines.
The contrary effect of the pH value on the ionization of the
carboxylic acid and amine groups limits the interaction between
the particles and an ODA monolayer. To solve this problem,
mercaptosuccinic acid (MSA) was used as a capping agent to
modify the gold nanoparticles in this work. Because two
carboxylic acid groups are present in a MSA molecule, MSA has
a lower pKa (pKa1 ) 3.30). The high degree of MSA ionization
in an aqueous solution has been shown to enhance the adsorption
of a cationic surfactant on the MSA-modified CdS nanoparticle.31
It is also expected that MSA-modified gold nanoparticles (MSA-
Au) should have a stronger interaction with an ODA monolayer.
Various concentrations of MSA were used to modify the gold
nanoparticles (Au NPs). The effect of MSA concentration (the
degree of the surface modification) as well as the competitive-
adsorption effect of free MSA molecules on the incorporation
of MSA-Au on an ODA monolayer were studied using the
characteristics of the surface pressure-area (ð-A) isotherm and
transmission electron microscopy (TEM) observations.
Experimental Section
Materials. Octadecylamine (ODA, >99%, Aldrich), chloroform
(>99%, J.T. Baker), trisodium citrate dihydrate (>99%, J.T. Baker),
hydrogen tetrachloroaurate (HAuCl4, >99%, Alfa Aesar), and
2-mercaptosuccinic acid (HOOCCH2CH(SH)COOH, MSA, >97%,
Aldrich) were used as received. Doubly distilled water purified with
a Milli-Q apparatus supplied by Millipore (resistivity g18.2 M¿
cm) was used for all the experiments of this study.
Au Colloid Solution Preparation. An aqueous solution (200
mL) containing 57.6 mg of chloroauric acid (HAuCl4) was boiled,
and then another 200 mL aqueous solution of 14.7 mM sodium
citrate was added. By continuously boiling this mixture, a gold
hydrosol formed after 15 min. After cooling the hydrosol, the
capping of the gold clusters was performed by adding MSA into the
hydrosol solution without further purification. Various amounts of
MSA were used to study the concentration impact. The hydrosol pH
was adjusted to 7 using ammonia.
Mixed ODA/Au Monolayer and LB Film Preparation. The
monolayer experiments were performed in a computer-controlled
film-balance apparatus (KSV minitrough) constructed by KSV
Instruments Ltd., Finland. A Teflon trough with a working area of
32  7.5 cm2 was placed on a vibration isolation table and closed
in an environmental chamber. The film surface pressure at the air/
water interface was measured by using the Wilhelmy plate
arrangement attached to a microbalance. ODA was dissolved in
chloroform to prepare a stock solution with a concentration of 1
mg/mL. The stock solution was spread on the subphase of the gold
hydrosol using a microsyringe (Hamilton Co., U.S.A.). After allowing
a waiting period for solvent evaporation and for the adsorption of
the Au nanoparticles, the monolayer was compressed at a rate of 5
mm/min. A surface pressure-area (ð-A) isotherm was obtained by
continuous compression of the monolayer using two moving barriers.
To examine the adsorption situation of Au NPs in the monolayer,
the monolayers were transferred onto a carbon-coated copper grid
by vertical deposition (LB deposition) at a dipping speed of 1 mm/
min. The LB films were observed using a high-resolution transmission
electron microscope (Hitachi H-7500).
Results and Discussion
Adsorption Behavior of Unmodified Au NPs. Figure 1 shows
the ð-A isotherms of the ODA monolayer on the aqueous
subphases with or without gold nanoparticles. In the absence of
Au NPs, the ODA monolayer exhibits highly condensed and low
compressible characteristics, resembling the results reported in
the literature.12,25,32 The limiting area estimated by extrapolating
the constant-slope region of the isotherm to zero surface pressure
is 21 Å2/molecule, which corresponds to the cross-sectional
area of a hydrocarbon chain.33 In the presence of Au NPs, the
position of the isotherm is dependent on the interval of the
adsorption period allowed before compression. For a waiting
time of 0.5 h, the isotherm exhibits a right shift and a more
expanded state in comparison with that of the pure ODA
monolayer, ascribable to the adsorption of nanoparticles. When
the adsorption time of Au NPs was prolonged to 2.0 or 3.0 h,
the ð-A isotherm was found to shift left when compared to the
pure monolayer. This phenomenon is unusual since an increase
in the adsorption time is supposed to increase the amount of Au
NPs adsorbed and thus result in a more expanded monolayer.12
A left shift of the isotherm implies that fewer ODA molecules
are present at the air/liquid interface. One possible reason for the
loss of ODA molecules is the high solubility of ODA in the
water subphase.34 This effect is excluded here because no
significant shift of the isotherm was found when the pure ODA
monolayer was compressed after a 3.0 h waiting period (results
not shown). This result also implies that the solubility of ODA
molecules in the aqueous solution of pH ) 7 does not significantly
affect the ð-A isotherms.

Cadmium sulfide (CdS) quantum dots (QDs) were used as a sensitizer to 
enhance the light harvest of a TiO2 film. Various assembly methods, including 
self-assembly monolayer (SAM), chemical bath deposition (CBD), and SAM/CBD

films. For the SAM technique, bifunctional linker molecules including 
mercaptosuccinic acid (MSA), 3-mercaptopropyl trimethoxysilane (MPTMS), and 
3-aminopropyl-methyl diethoxysilane (APMDS) were used to incorporate QDs onto 
the TiO2 surface. For the dye-sensitized solar cell (DSSC) application, MSA was 
proved to be an efficient linker. However, the incorporated amount and coverage ratio 
of CdS-QDs on the TiO2 surface is not sufficient due to the high resistance for QDs to 
transport in the mesopores. Therefore, CBD was introduced to replenish the 
incorporation of CdS-QDs. The pre-assembled CdS-QDs act as nucleation sites in the 
CBD process, forming a CdS nanofilm with an interfacial structure capable of 
inhibiting the recombination of injected electrons. An efficiency as high as 1.35% was 
achieved using the SAM/CBD coupling technique. In an alternative CBD process, 
alcohol, instead of water, was used as a solvent for the in-situ synthesis of CdS-QDs 
onto mesoporous TiO2 films. Due to low surface tension, the alcohol solutions have 
high wettability and superior penetration ability on the mesoscopic TiO2 film, leading 
to a well-covered CdS QDs on the surface of mesopores. The efficiency of a CdS 
QDs-sensitized solar cell prepared using the present method is increased to 1.84 % 
under the illumination of one sun (AM1.5, 100 mW/cm2). The CdS-modified TiO2 
film was also used as a photoanode in a photoelectrochemical cell for water splitting. 
The saturated photocurrent under the visible light illumination (100 mW/cm2) was ca. 
5.5 mA/cm2 at an applied potential 0.6 V versus OCP, which is equivalent to a 
conversion efficiency of 3.67 %. The hydrogen generation rate obtained at this 
condition is 95.5μmol/h.
