I中文摘要
由於網路技術的快速發展，有越來越多消費行為透過網路商店來進行，3D 虛擬技術的導
入，更增加網路 3D 虛擬商店的發展性，讓消費者能夠突破在傳統 2D 網路商店瀏覽商品的
限制與失真性；對於行動不便的銀髮族更可透過網路，自由進出網路商店，盡情瀏覽商店
的一景一物，並且可以鉅細靡遺的從各角度品玩商品。然而 3D 虛擬商店在瀏覽過程中，
仍存在許多使用上的問題，其中如：定位導覽、空間迷向及商品辨識能力的問題，其中尚
有非常多待研究與討論的空間。若能有效評估與分析高齡人口在使用 3D 虛擬商店時，所
產生空間迷向及商品辨識能力的問題，透過有效導覽模式、商品群聚模式與商品資訊呈現
模式，必能提高 3D 虛擬商店對於高齡使用者的實用性。故本研究的目的，主要可歸結以
下六點：(1)探討高齡人員空間知識的學習過程與 3D 虛擬商店導覽認知決策的關係；(2)空
間知識對於尋物績效的影響評估；(3)建構最佳化之虛擬商店展示空間辨識導覽輔助模式；
(4)探討商品群聚性之最佳分類與佈置模式，以提高高齡使用者的空間知識力；(5)發展商品
資訊的呈現策略，以提高高齡使用者的商品辨識力；(6)發展高空間知覺性與高商品辨識性
之 3D 虛擬商店雛型。本研究為二年期計畫，第一年針對前三點目的，以發展最佳化之虛
擬商店展示空間導覽輔助模式為主，並獲致以下結論：最佳化之虛擬商店空間辨識導覽輔
助模式，應包括(1)模仿實體環境的涵構與氛圍，將虛擬空間實體化；(2)3D 及 2D 地標結合，
可快速建立空間認知地圖。第二年則以後三點目的進行研究，期以建構符合高齡人口使用
之高空間認知性與商品辨識性的 3D 虛擬商店。

4.1 商品群聚分類之設計
誠如先前所做的討論，群聚(clustering)是把有形或抽象的物件歸類到類似物件類別的過
程。虛擬商店就如同一空間商品資料庫，每一商品即為資料庫中的單元資料，當商品種類
繁鎖，有效將商品分群，對於在虛擬商店侷限視野空間中，能否快速進行商品辨識與尋找，
是虛擬商店能否名副其實成為省時省事的新購物模式之重要因素。以剪刀為例，就包括：
學生剪刀、事務剪刀、園藝剪刀、魚尾叉剪刀、裁縫剪刀、剪裁剪刀、美工花邊剪刀、鋸
齒剪刀、廚房剪刀、繡剪、小泉剪刀…等不同種類，此尚未考慮衍伸出造型、大小、材質
等細部分類，如：有的商家將其歸類於文具、有的歸類於事務用品、有的則歸類於五金，
當展示空間大小不一，如：將 5 個展示櫃改為 10 個展示櫃，其商品分類與陳列方式必然不
同，這就有賴商品群聚模式的導入。本研究以群聚演算法中的分割方法，並試著以 k-means
分割演算法與單一連結聚合演算法結合，進行商品群聚分類，並與階層法進行比較。
4.2 商品群組隱喻模式之建構
過去在導覽輔助介面的相關設計中，常常加入隱喻式的設計理念與因素，以提升導覽輔
助性[4]。這種隱喻設計，包括我們所熟知的桌上型隱喻(Desktop metaphor)，在網際網路中
也有許多效果不錯的隱喻式設計，如社區隱喻(Community metaphor)。部份電子商務網站也
運用了購物隱喻(Shopping mall metaphor)，如消費者在網站上可經由購物車、收銀台、客服
中心等購物中心的經營元素，進行交易或服務連結。本研究的重點是放在商品群組隱喻建
構之可行性分析，當完成前階段商品群聚之後，在虛擬商店的商品陳列，是否可透過群聚
商品的屬性隱喻，在櫥窗僅顯示該群商品隱喻，然後以超連結的方式進入單一群聚商品的
展示空間，其目的是減少虛擬商店現場瀏覽空間複雜性與迷失(當所有商品在同一空間同時
呈現時)，並加速其選定商品速度與正確性。本研究為應用群聚商品隱喻之雛形設計，在櫥
櫃上顯示相關商品的群聚隱喻；當操作者進入本瀏覽情境，可點選該櫥櫃的商品隱喻，以
超連結方式進入該群商品詳細的展覽空間，以期減少選擇商品的複雜度。但是商品隱喻以
抽象隱喻或實物圖片何者為佳？如：園藝剪刀包括：花剪、柑剪、樹剪、採果剪，採用實
物圖片固可讓人聯想該群聚商品的內容，但當花剪、柑剪、樹剪、採果剪外型有較大差異
時，應採用哪一種園藝剪刀當為群聚商品的隱喻，就有其困難度，此時若以抽象隱喻是否
有較佳的理解性(如圖一)？
4.3 商品資訊呈現模式之設計
當網路瀏覽者進入虛擬商店，透過商店展示櫥窗選定瀏覽商品的類別屬性，若要進入商
品深度瀏覽時，如何讓瀏覽者對於商品資訊易於尋找與理解，本研究以二個構面，進行探
討與設計：
(1) 階層設計概念
誠如先前針對虛擬商店商品資訊呈現原則所做的討論，階層式概念設計，可降低空間資
訊的複雜度，讓商品資訊商易於被尋找，故本研究以三階段樹狀結構，建立商品瀏覽路徑，
即第一層為群聚商品櫥窗的展示，第二層為相關產品資訊的展開，第三層為個別商品 3D
動態的展示，瀏覽方式如圖二、圖三所示。
(2) 雙重編碼設計
在商品個別資訊的呈現，本研究將以雙重編碼設計的概念，將商品的圖片與商品資訊同
時呈現，當瀏覽者進行商品資訊決策時，可透過視像符碼與語文符碼三個階段：展現、連
結與交互作用，進行資訊儲存與提取；當圖像與文數字交互存取時，所形成意像編碼形式
將更易儲存與提取，以增加使用者對於商品的認識度與決策資訊。
44.4 實驗設計
(1)自變項：包括商品群聚分類法(分割方法；階層方法)、商品資訊呈現方式(圖片與文字；
文字)，針對商品群聚分類法及隱喻設計進行評估。
(2)依變項：包括效率指標(商品選購時間)；有效指標(商品回憶能力)；主觀偏好(商品資訊
呈現的偏好)。
(3)受試人員：年齡為 55 歲至 65 歲間的高齡人口。16 位受試者將以隨機分配的方式，平均
指派到商品群聚分類×商品資訊呈現方式四種水準中。
(4)實驗環境設計：本實驗環境透過 Photoshop 軟體建構 2D 商品圖像、3ds MAX9 軟體建構
3D 商品圖像、MAYA8.0 建構 3D 虛擬商店景物，並以 Virtools 4.1 為虛
擬操作平台。
5. 結果與討論
針對二因子：商品群聚分類法(分割方法；階層方法)及商品資訊呈現方式(圖片與文字；
文字)，在三個依變項(效率指標(商品選購時間)；有效指標(商品回憶能力)；主觀偏好(商品
資訊呈現的偏好))，所進行的變異數分析。由結果推知以下結論：
(1)商品群聚分類法中分割方法對於商品選購時間，顯著較階層方法來的短；對於商品的回
憶能力亦顯著較階層方法為佳。
(2)商品資訊呈現方式以圖片與文字搭配的方式，受試者有較佳的商品資訊呈現的偏好。
依此結果，當商品種類繁鎖，有效將商品分群，對於在虛擬商店侷限視野空間中，將有助
於快速進行商品辨識與尋找，其中又以分割方法之分類方式，較易形成空間知識力；而將
商品以圖片與文字搭配的方式，呈現商品資訊，較能有效提高高齡使用者的商品辨識力。
6. 結論
本計畫為二年期之研究計畫，第一年研究主題，係針對高齡使用者在進行 3D 虛擬商店
瀏覽過程中，導覽策略與物品間展示分類，對於空間認知與尋物時間的評估分析，經由實
驗驗證，獲致以下結論：
隱含地標(物品間展示分類)和實體地標對於 3D 虛擬網路商店空間知識形成皆有顯著的重
要性，而當實體地標為 2D 或 2D 搭配文字或 3D 之地標型態，可產生較佳且較一致的空間輔
助資訊，較易於建立空間知識，並有效降低尋物時間。
第二年研究計畫的重點為：如何將物品進行群聚性的分類，發展出商品佈置、陳列與資
訊顯示的策略，以提升空間知識的建構；及如何適當充分的顯示與提供商品資訊，以提升
虛擬商店的實用與吸引性。綜合此項研究計畫的結果，可獲至以下的結論：
3D 虛擬網路商店對於高齡使用者欲獲致較佳的空間知識、商品知識及導覽效果，物品的展
示分類有其需要性，其中以分割方法進行商品群聚分類，此將形成重要的隱含地標空間知
識，此時若搭配 2D 或 2D 搭配文字或 3D 之實體地標型態，則高齡使用者將有最佳的空間知
識與尋物能力；若在商品資訊以圖片與文字搭配的方式，則高齡使用者對於虛擬商店之商
品回憶能力或主觀偏好，皆有較佳的結果。

展，當前主流的圖形使用介面／窗像單點（GUI／WIMP）介面不斷地遭受批評。隨著人機互動模
式的推陳出新，新一代人機互動模式被要求包含虛擬實境、語音辨識與合成、手寫體與手勢辨

礎之一。因此，在人與電腦之介面環境中，人所扮演決策的角色已越趨重要，而非僅是電腦系
統之運作、執行與顯示的硬體功能，若人的作業環境未予以適當的考量，則人所獲致的決策訊
息必定是片斷而不完整，此時人所做出的決策判斷往往是錯誤的，而電腦資訊環境的發達，可

Absract. The internet 3D virtual store has received wide attention from
researchers and practitioners due to the fact that it is one of the most killing
applications customers can feel in a real shopping environment and possibly
increase satisfaction. Though numerous studies have been performed on various
issues of the internet store, some research issues relating to the spatial cognition
of the elderly when immersed in a 3D virtual store still await further empirical
investigation. The objective of this study was to examine how elderly users
acquire spatial cognition in an on-screen virtual store. Specifically, the impact
of presence and absence of goods-classification on the acquisition of route and
survey knowledge was examined. Since landmarks are associated with both
route and survey knowledge, we expected to observe the impact of different
types of landmarks with both presence and absence of goods-classification. The
experimental results indicated that the presence of goods-classification was
more important in constructing route knowledge than in absence, and the time
of duration of goods-finding would be shorter. However, we also found that the
measuring scores of survey knowledge in presence of goods-classification were
not significantly larger than in absence. In addition, the measuring scores of
route knowledge were the largest and the time of duration of goods-finding was
shorter while the presence of goods-classification combined with landmark in
the type of alphanumeric + 2D picture + 3D object. Simultaneously, it could be
found in absence of goods-classification. Therefore, while the goods-
classification is absent, the landmarks could be seemed as redundant codes for
goods-finding in 3D virtual store.
Keywords: 3D virtual store, Goods-finding, Goods-classification, Landmarks,
Route knowledge, Survey knowledge.

In general virtual environment, the ability of orientation influences the efficiency
and effectiveness of wayfinding. Wayfinding is a term that can refer to a rather
narrow concern: That is, how well people are able to find their way to their particular
destination without delay or undue anxiety [15]. It is knowing where you are in a
building or an environment, knowing where your desired location is, knowing how to
get there from your present location, and used in the context of architecture to refer to
the user experience of orientation and choosing a path within the built environment.
Travelers find their way using landmark knowledge, route knowledge, and survey
knowledge [16]. The first two types of knowledge are more specific knowledge
representing sensory experience [17]. Landmark knowledge records the visual
features of landmarks, including their shape, size, texture, and so forth [18]. For a
structure to be a landmark, it must have high imagability; that is, it must be distinctive
and memorable [19]. Most often, landmark knowledge is an acquired first when
encountering and learning to know a new and unfamiliar environment. The
recognition of landmarks becomes a part of the construction of route knowledge,
where the landmarks are the points that make up the routs. Later, landmarks are the
objects and elements in the survey knowledge and they are part of constructing the
layout and relational configuration of the elements in the environment [20][21].
Survey knowledge provides a map-like, bird’s-eye view of a region and contains
spatial information including locations, orientations, and sizes of regional features
[22]. Each type of knowledge helps the traveler construct a cognitive map of a region
and thereafter find their way using that map [23][24]. In study of orientation, the
notion of a “cognitive map”has received much research attention [25][26]. At its
most general, a cognitive map is a mental construct which we use to understand and
know the environment [27]. The term assumes that people store information about
their environment which they use to make spatial decision [25]. Because of the
importance of landmark knowledge for constructing a cognitive map, much research
was devoted to the impact of landmarks on orientation. Several researchers have
studied the value of 2D landmarks (i.e. textual and 2D images) for wayfinding.
Witmer et al. used verbal directions and photographs to study route learning [28].
Waller et al. applied cardboard numeral, images of stuffed animal and arrow to study
real-world task training and found that long exposure fostered good spatial knowledge
under several VEs [29]. Additionally, several researches have been done on the
effects of 3D landmarks for wayfinding. Elvins found that 3D thumbnails make better
guidebooks for 3D worlds than do text and 2D thumbnail images [22]. Parush and
Berman discussed the impact of navigation aids and 3D landmarks on the acquisition

2.3 Experimental design and procedures
The study involved a 2 (Goods classification: absence or presence) × 8 (Types of
landmarks: none, alphanumeric, 2D, 3D, alphanumeric + 2D, alphanumeric + 3D, 2D
+ 3D and alphanumeric + 2D +3D) between-subjects experiment, resulting in a full-
factorial design with 16 treatment conditions. Each participant was randomly assigned
one of the eight conditions of landmarks in presence or absence of goods-
classification to do the task of goods-finding. Therefore, there were two participants
was randomly assigned to one of the sixteen conditions. In other words, there were
two participants assigned to each condition.
During the exposure period, participants were asked to search for and confirm
some goods in the store. There were eight target goods, two in each showroom, need
participants to search for. When they found the target, s/he should move the cursor on
the object and click the left button of mouse twice to respond ‘hit.’There were four
stages in the experiment. In each stage, participant was randomly assigned two target
goods to find and asked to attempt to recall the location of target goods and complete
an oral questionnaire of route knowledge when two target goods had been found.
When all eight target goods had been found, s/he was asked to complete a
questionnaire of survey knowledge.
