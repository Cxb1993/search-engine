電子模封樹脂廢料之資源回收利用以製備中孔洞氧化矽 
及中孔碳材料之特性分析研究 
 
計畫編號：NSC 95-2221-E-131-020 
執行期間：95年 8 月 1 日 ~ 96年 7 月 31 日 
主持人：劉宗宏  明志科技大學化學工程系 
 
摘要 
 
1. 中文摘要 
本研究原屬於多年期計畫，由於研究經費只有核定一年，故僅完成第一年計畫—中孔
洞氧化矽製備的探討。 
中孔洞氧化矽材料由於高表面積、高孔隙度、粉末超微細和具有較大孔徑，在吸附、
過濾、催化、燃料電池及半導體材料等方面有重要的應用前景。隨著半導體晶圓廠的擴充，
電子構裝業之年成長率達 40 %以上，由於需求量逐年增加，而製程中所產生之模封樹脂廢
料也日益增加，不但造成處理麻煩，也帶來許多環保問題的產生，本研究擬針對此一問題，
尋求一合理的解決途徑。由於電子模封樹脂廢料中富含氧化矽等物質，本研究以此原料利
用化學沈澱法探討各項反應包括 pH 值、水熱溫度、水熱時間及煅燒溫度等條件對中孔洞
氧化矽製備的影響。所得樣品利用感應藕合電漿質譜分析儀、元素分析儀、掃描式電子顯
微鏡、霍式紅外光譜分析儀、比表面積及孔徑分析儀、X-ray 繞射儀及熱重分析儀等，進
行樣品之化性及物性分析。實驗結果發現，利用 X-ray 繞射儀檢測產物之孔洞為六角晶格
對稱之MCM-41結構。樣品的比表面積最高可達 1250 m2/g以上，中孔洞之孔徑約在 3 - 10 
nm，優於一般利用商用原料製備的MCM-41。其中，樣品的比表面積隨水熱溫度及水熱時
間等條件之增加而減少；而對 pH值及煅燒溫度等條件有一最佳值產生。當 pH=1時之樣品
形態為圓球狀顆粒，而當 pH=11時之形態為長條狀顆粒。熱分析顯示當 pH小於 7時，樣
品有 2個主要的分解溫度；當 pH大於 7時，只有 1個主要的分解溫度。本研究之成果可
提升國內對電子材料等廢棄物之利用價值，並可應用於其它各種 IC電子廢料之處理及各項
矽材料的開發，將有助於我國在半導體工業及精密化學品科技方面的提升。 
 
關鍵字：電子模封樹脂廢料、中孔洞氧化矽、特性分析、資源回收與利用

二、研究目的 
 
    目前除少數構裝業者將模封樹脂廢料粉碎後，委託廢棄物處理業予以固化或清運掩埋
外，大多數業者還是將廢料儲存於廠內。因此，相關業者對於模封樹脂廢料之處理實屬刻
不容緩。由上表可知，電子模封樹脂材料為一富含氧化矽之物質，經由本研究除了可以有
效解決環境污染問題外，更可以經由廢棄物製備出高附加價值的奈米材料，充分有效達到
資源回收與利用之目的。 
 
三、文獻探討 
 
雖然目前尚未發現利用電子模封樹脂廢料製備中孔洞氧化矽之製程方面的研究，然而
由於本計畫之研究方法是先將模封樹脂廢料碳燒，再以鹼洗方式將氧化物轉變成矽酸鹽以
進行中孔洞氧化矽的製備反應。因此，可利用以矽酸鹽為原料，以化學沈澱法製備氧化矽
之相關文獻作為本計畫研究的參考。茲將幾篇較相關的文獻敘述如下：  
Wang et al. [3] 曾經探討利用原料性質與合成溫度控制之方法製備中孔洞氧化矽。結果
發現當水熱溫度小於 75 oC時，中孔碳之六角結構規則度較差，但若將水熱溫度提高至 95 oC
以上時，會得到立方形之中孔洞氧化矽（MCM-48）。水熱溫度的高低會影響碳先驅物與矽
化物之交互作用，進而影響到中孔洞氧化矽之結構變化。Kong et al. [4] 使用混合型界面活
性劑（共三種：陽離子、陰離子、非離子型）製備中孔洞氧化矽，結果發現可得到窄殼
（hollow-shell）型之MCM-48。實驗使用混合型界面活性劑可以有效減少界面活性劑用量、
促進微包（micelles）生成、增加比表面積與提高三維之立體結構組織等。

5.3 樣品之孔徑及吸附特徵分析 
 
5.3.1 樣品的吸附特徵分析 
圖 5為樣品利用不同 pH值進行反應，所得產物之氮氣吸脫附實驗結果。不同 pH值對
吸脫附實驗有非常明顯的影響，吸脫附曲線分成兩個階段，剛開始吸附量增加很快，到後
來中間有遲滯迴路現象產生，可知實驗所得樣品主要是屬於中孔洞物質 [11]。樣品之體吸
附(volume adsorbed)值隨 pH值增加而有一最低值，亦即當 pH=5時，體吸附值最小；而當
pH=1或 3時，有較高之體吸附值。 
 
5.3.2 樣品的孔徑分析 
圖 6為樣品在不同 pH值反應，所得產物利用 BJH（Barrett-Joyner-Halenda）模式進行
孔徑分布之分析結果。不同 pH值對孔徑分布有明顯的影響，當 pH=5時，樣品之孔徑最大，
pH=3時次之；當 pH=11時，樣品之孔徑最小。實驗所得的樣品皆是屬於中孔徑範圍的物
質。

5.4 樣品之物性分析 
 
5.4.1 樣品之 x-ray繞射儀分析 
圖 7及圖 8為樣品之 x-ray繞射儀分析結果。圖 7為不同水熱溫度所得氧化矽產物之樣
品，其中 XRD之繞射峰位置符合{100}、{110}、{200}及{210}等面之反射，是屬於六角晶
格（hexagonal lattice）對稱之MCM-41結構[12]。由圖可知，XRD之繞射峰強度隨水熱溫
度增加而增加，表示反應溫度越高，孔洞之六角結構越完整，此結果與Wang等人[13]使用
TEOS (tetraethylorthosilicate)製備MCM-48所得之結果相似。

{200}及{210}等面之反射，證明實驗所得樣品為六角晶格之MCM-41結構。由圖可知，XRD
之繞射峰位置隨水熱時間增加而略偏左移，而且強度隨水熱時間增加而增加，表示反應時
間越長，孔洞之六角結構越完整。

八、計畫成果自評 
 
電子構裝材料在今日 IC半導體工業上佔有一極重要的角色，構裝產業的蓬勃發展為國
人帶來許多商業契機，但在構裝過程中所產生之廢料亦相對增加環境成本之負擔。中孔洞
氧化矽材料及其衍生物在奈米材料的應用領域非常廣泛，其原料來源若由電子材料之廢棄
物中取得，不但能廢物利用而減少原料成本的支出，且對國家經濟具有提升作用。本研究
計畫之成果可提供電子模封樹脂廢料製備高表面積之中孔洞氧化矽材料之可能最佳反應操
作條件、建立中孔洞氧化矽材料之化學及物理性質測試、及取得中孔洞氧化矽材料製備之
基本數據，供程序設計之參考。此項技術也可應用於其它含塑膠廢棄物之處理上，對於類
似廢棄物的處理技術亦有莫大的幫助，故此為一值得吾人開發的領域，本研究之成果非常
適合發表在學術期刊或相關專利上。

KINETICS AND PREPARATION OF RICE-STRAW BASED 
POROUS CARBON BY SODIUM HYDROXIDE ACTIVATION 
 
Tzong-Horng Liou*, Shwu-Jer Chiu, Long-Chung Wu 
Department of Chemical Engineering, Mingchi University of Technology,  
Taipei 24301, Taiwan, e-mail: thliou@mail.mit.edu.tw 
 
ABSTRACT 
High surface area and porous carbons were prepared from rice straw using sodium 
hydroxide as activating agent. The effect of preparation parameters including the impregnated 
salt concentration, activation temperature, and pre-treatment process on the properties of 
carbon were studied. Experimental results indicated that pre-carbonization treated specimens 
show higher surface area and adsorption capacities than un-carbonozation treated specimens. 
The pyrolysis process during activation was observed with a thermal gravimetric analyzer 
technique. A mechanism was proposed to explain the activation process. NaOH acted as both 
the activating agent and cleaner. The results will be useful in preparing highly porous carbon 
from agricultural by-products. 
 
KEY WORDS: rice straw; activated carbon; preparation; chemical activation; pore structure 
 
INTRODUCTION 
Activated carbon is a high-porosity and large adsorption capacity material. It has been 
widely used as an adsorbent for filtration and purification [1], and in catalyst supports and 
battery capacitors, etc [2,3]. There are several hundred million tons of rice being produced 
annually. Rice straw accounts for about 50 % of the total weight of rice plants. Rice straw is 
the by-product of the rice milling industry. Its constituent is complicated and contains an 
abundance of lignocellulosic biomass and ash. Approximately 10 – 15 wt % of the ash is 
obtained from burning rice straw in the air. The ash contains round 75 wt % silica [4].The rice 
straw is bulky, and only a few parts are used as fuel. This creates a disposal problem in terms 
of the amount increasing year by year. Because the rice straw can provide more advantages, 
such as being inexpensive and widely available, it is a very good candidate to prepare 
activated carbon.  
Basically, there are two methods noted for the preparation of activated carbon: physical 
activation and chemical activation. Physical activation consists of carbonization of a precursor 
using a gaseous activating agent such as steam or CO2 [5]. In chemical activation, the 
precursor is mixed with a chemical activating agent such as H3PO4, ZnCl2 or alkali 
hydroxides (NaOH, KOH), after which, it is heated in an inert gas [6]. The preparation of 
activated carbon from coals, resins and lignin-cellulose materials (coconut shell, pistachio-nut 
shell, hardwood), have been published by many researchers. However, the preparation of 
activated carbon from rice straw by activation with NaOH has received little attention. This 
research investigates the effect of preparation conditions including the one- or two-stage 
procedure, impregnation ratio of activating agent and activation temperature on the pore 
structure of the specimen in order to produce a high-porosity activated carbon. An activation 
mechanism is also developed to account for the experimental results. The method proposed in 
this study not only has the benefit of preparation of activated carbon from rice straw, but also

Analysis of reaction characteristics 
   The TG curves in Fig. 4-(1) show the remaining weight (W/Wo) for the one-stage 
activation of rice straw at various impregnation ratios. The results indicate that the mass loss 
of rice straw activated in nitrogen occurs in four temperature zones. The curves show an 
initial mass loss of up to ~425 K due to the elimination of physically absorbed water in the 
samples. The mass loss occurring in the range of 425 – 575 K may be associated with the 
decomposition of organic materials. The temperature range of 575 – 950 K corresponds to the 
activation process, which may be attributed to further reaction between the activating agent 
and the carbonaceous precursor [11]. The mass loss in the range of beyond 950 K may be 
associated with the evaporation of metal sodium where the boiling point of sodium is 1156 K. 
The final remaining weight of rice straw activation is about 35 to 75 wt %, increased by 
increasing the impregnation ratio. The TGA plots of two-stage activation of rice straw 
obtained from various impregnation ratios are shown in Fig. 4-(2). The mass loss of 
pre-carbonized rice straw samples occurs in three temperature zones, namely 325 – 425, 425 – 
950 and 950 – 1150 K. These results are similar to those of the one-stage procedure. The final 
remaining weight is about from 48 to 78 wt %, increased by increasing the impregnation ratio. 
The peaks in the two-stage procedure are much more narrow and sharp than that of the 
one-stage procedure. Simultaneously, the peak temperatures for the activation reaction in the 
two-stage procedure are also slightly higher than that of the one-stage procedure. 
 
Activation mechanism 
   The overall reaction which occurs during activation could be [12,13]: 
4NaOH + 2C J 4Na + 2CO +2H2O                      (1) 
or  
6NaOH + 2C J 2Na + 3H2 +2Na2CO3                    (2) 
It is well-known that NaOH at the surface of the carbon precursor is firstly decomposed 
and forms the Na2O intermediate [14]. Thus the activation reaction can be divided into two 
groups. i.e. 
Na2O generating: 
2NaOH J Na2O + H2O                                (3) 
Na or Na2CO3 forming: 
2Na2O + C J 4Na + CO2                               (4)
