(一)前言: 
1962 年 Clark 採用葡萄糖氧化酵素 GOD 為感測層，製作全世界第一個安培計量型生物
感測器[1]；1970 年 P. Bergveld 提出離子場效電晶體 ISFET 的概念嘗試測量生物神經系統訊
號[2]；1976 年 J. Janata 首次提出生物場效電晶體 BioFET(酵素場效電晶體 EnFET)的概念
[3]；1980 年 Caras & Janata 正式實現第一顆 EnFET，用以量測盤尼西寧[4]；1980 年 J. F. 
Schenck 提出偵測免疫細胞的場效電晶體 ImmunoFET[5]；1981 年 D. T. Jobling 成功的將細
胞與 MOSFET 耦合[6]；1991 年 P. Fromherz 製作全世界第一顆神經細胞電晶體 CPFET[7]；
1997 年 S. Schütz 製作’甲蟲/晶片’ BioFET，能偵測甲蟲觸鬚之天線訊號[8]；1997 年 E. 
Souteyrand 嘗試第一個偵測 DNA 雜交的實驗[9]；2001 年 Charles M. Lieber 在 Science 首先
發表矽奈米線生物感測器[10]。由於有這些先驅研究，再結合生物科技、電化學、固態物理、
表面物理、生物工程、積體電路與資料處理的成熟技術，使具有專門性、選擇性、靈敏性及
可靠性的新一代奈米型感測器得以實現。近十年來拜奈米科技的蓬勃發展，奈米顆粒、奈米
棒或奈米線製程技術趨於成熟，更使得這些源於場效電晶體與酵素催化感測概念的生物感測
器有了爆炸性的發展，偵測極限皆已達單一蛋白質分子、單一 DNA 分子、單一抗體/抗原分
子或單一病毒等。這些感測器廣泛的使用於監測環境中的毒物、水質或人體血液中的禁藥成
分、疾病細胞的生長等；而各種利用奈米金屬或半導體顆粒連結生物受體分子形成的特殊功
能化奈米粒子更是對生物醫學的研究與人類疾病的治療影響既深且遠，再結合基因工程的進
展也使癌症的終極治癒目標露出曙光。 
在所有的生物感測器中，以血糖感測器最具代表性，乃因血糖偵測在臨床診斷與生物
科技上相當重要，且葡萄糖氧化酵素 GOD 便宜，在 2003 年以前所發表有關酵素場效電晶體
EnFET 的文獻中，血糖感測器約占了 1/3。以場效電晶體為基礎的血糖感測器可概分為安培
計量型( Amperometric biosensor)與電壓計量型( Potentiometric biosensor)。由於安培
計量型血糖感測器使用電化學電解增強氧化還原反應，所以較之於電壓計量型有較大的動態
工作範圍與線性區間及低的偵測極限。但由於電解反應效率與電解電極板面積成正比，加大
電極板面積顯與電晶體縮小化製程相違背，而電壓計量型血糖感測器就沒有這種困擾。在
2004 年以前所發表的血糖感測器，由於受限於反應所需的氧分子在水中的低溶解度，使得
動態工作範圍一直停留在 1.5 mM 左右，且線性區域窄，靈敏度低，偵測極限大；這個時期
最佳的文獻數據元件線性區域約 0.01-0.7 mM，靈敏度 10μAcm-2mM-1 (電解電流) ，偵測極
限 10 μM。這個瓶頸直到 2004 年開始，奈米顆粒、奈米棒或奈米線製程技術趨於成熟，藉
由奈米顆粒或奈米線超高的表面積/體積比，將之運用於安培計量型血糖感測器上，在小區
域的電解金屬極板上製作具備高表面積的電極板，使得安培計量型血糖感測器偵測效能不斷
推升，在 2005 年開始相關文獻呈爆炸性成長，單一感測器整體效能已達約線性區域 0.5μM 
- 5 mM，靈敏度 60μAcm-2mM-1 (電解電流) ，偵測極限 0.1 μM。 
如前述，由於奈米顆粒、奈米棒、奈米線等生產製程技術趨於成熟，利用奈米材料製
作生物感測器引起許多研究學者的投入。目前我們的研究團隊擬進行以多晶矽奈米線製作生
物感測器，希望藉由血糖氧化還原反應產生的氫離子，或細胞分子本身在成長過程中陰電性 
變化，影響多晶矽內部電場分佈，進而造成多晶矽傳導率的改變，用以偵測相對的待測物濃
度，其原理與酵素場效電晶體相同。在文獻上雖已有研究利用矽奈米線來偵測血糖或腫瘤細
胞的辨識，但到目前為止，利用奈米線製作生物感測器在偵測蛋白質分子、DNA 分子、抗體
/抗原分子或病毒分子等有豐碩成果，但對於血糖的感測尚無很好的結果。

Polysilicon Wire modified with 3-
aminopropyltriethoxysilane and Polydimethylsiloxa- 
ne-treated Hydrophobic Fumed Silica Nanoparticle 
Mixture Coating

Since the genetic information carried by an organism 
is inscribed in DNA, how to determine the DNA sequence 
has been an important task for biochemical or biomedical 
scientist. Typically, radiochemical, enzymatic and 
fluorescent [1] technique based on detecting different 
labels or reagents added in the molecules under test have 
been used for the DNA detection. These techniques, 
however, are proven to be time-consuming, expensive and 
complicated to be carried out. It has been reported that 
using silicon nanowire (SiNW) can effectively detect the 
hybridyzation and mutation of DNAs [2]. In this work, 
instead of using SiNW, we used polysilicon (poly-Si) 
wire coated with 3-aminopropyltriethoxysilane (Ȗ-APTES) 
plus polydimethylsiloxane-treated hydrophobic fumed 
silica nanoparticle (NPs) for sequence-specific and label-
free single-strained (ss) homopolymer DNAs detection.  
A phosphorous-doped poly-Si wire with 200nm width 
and 2ȝm length was patterned by e-beam lithography and 
its detailed fabrication process can be found elsewhere [3]. 
Figure 1(a) depicts SEM picture of the poly-Si wire. The 
sequence-specific ss homopolymer DNAs, poly(A), 
poly(T), poly(C), and poly(G) with various lengths (4, 6, 
8 and 10 bases) were prepared. The DNA solutions were 
then transferred separately onto the poly-Si wire surface 
by using a focus-ion-beam (FIB) milled blunt CAFM tip 
(Fig. 1(b)) through the capillary effect.  The current 
flowing through the poly-Si wire channel was then 
determined by using a semiconductor parameter analyzer 
Agilent 4156C.  
Fig.1: SEM pictures of poly-Si wire and the FIB-milled 
CAFM tip. 
Fig.2 shows the poly-Si wire channel current changes 
̴ I before and after dropping the test solution as a 
function of ss homopolymer DNA concentrations 
measured at drain-source voltage (VDS) 5V. In which, the 
Ȗ-APTES mixed with and without polydimethylsiloxane-
treated hydrophobic fumed silica NPs were compared. It 
was found that the sensitivity, linear detection region and 
lowest detection limit of the poly-Si wire had much 
improved if it was coated with Ȗ-APTES and NPs mixture. 
Further improvement can be achieved if the coated Ȗ-
APTES and NPs mixture membrane was treated with UV 
illumination. As observed, the linear detection region is 
extended from 10-14 M to 10-5 M, and the lowest detection 
limit can be down to 10-15 M. In addition, for the same ss 
homopolymer concentration we found that the ̴I is as 
follows: poly(T) > poly(C) > poly(G) > poly(A). Fig.3 
shows that the ̴ I also increases linearly with DNA 
length, and the lowest detectable DNA length can be 
down to 4 bases for poly-Si wire sensor modified with Ȗ-
APTES and NPs mixture after 90s UV illumination.  
Fig.2: Detection limit and linear region tested for the 
DNA solution dropped onto poly-Si wire sensor 
surface at a drain-source voltage of 5 V as a function 
of DNA concentrations. 
Fig.3: Channel current changes ̴I of the poly-Si wire 
sensor measured at a drain-source voltage of 5 V 
as a function of DNA length with concentrations 
fixed at 10fM. 
In conclusion, the poly-Si wire coated Ȗ-APTES and 
NPs can achieve excellent device performances on the 
DNA detection after UV illumination treatment process. 
Not only the linear detection region can be extended, but 
also the lowest detectable length is minimized to 4 bases.  
From considerations of low cost and easy-fabrication, 
poly-Si wire coated with Ȗ-APTES and NPs mixture plus 
UV treatment is a promising sensor for DNA detection.
