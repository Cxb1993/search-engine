Abstract 
Generally speaking, collagen molecules could be 
reconstituted into varies forms of collagen fibers by 
hydrophobic interactions and ionic forces under 
different environments. In other words, we could 
prepare different morphological structures of 
collagen fibers by varying collagen concentration, 
reconstitution temperature, pH value and solution 
ionic strength. In the present study, the collagen 
fibers exhibited narrower diameter distribution and 
possessed clearer characteristics D-banding under 
the high-voltage electric field treatment, as 
compared with the collagen fibers that reconstituted 
under normal condition. Furthermore, we could 
prepare nano-sized collagen II particles under 
certain experimental settings- under electric field 
treatment and the temperature sets under 25 ?.  
Unfortunately, this high-voltage electrostatic field 
system was not suitable in pasteurization application 
due to discharge happening at higher field strength 
setting and increased collagen solution temperature, 
before the optimum experimental setting of 
electrostatic field parameter were found. 
 
Key words: collagen, electrostatic field, 
nano-particle, pasteurization
