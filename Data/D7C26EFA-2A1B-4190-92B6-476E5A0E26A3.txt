Keywords: Single Point Diamond shaping, elliptical micro lens array 
Abstract. Micro lens array is widely used in photonics and telecommunication products, 
including back-light module of flat panel display devices and white-light LED illumination. 
The ultra precision diamond shaping method together with tool servo technique was studied 
and developed in this research to generate elliptical micro lens array. Each elliptical micro 
lens has radii in X and Y direction of 1.0 mm and 1.5 mm respectively. A high filling-factor 
(100 %) elliptical micro lens array with form accuracy better than 0.15µm and surface 
roughness around 5nm Ra was successfully fabricated. 
Introduction 
In recent years, the micro lens array plays an important role in various areas, such as 
optical coupling, optical interconnection and beam homogenizers. A variety of processes for 
the fabrication of micro lens have been developed, for instance, the photolithography 
process[1], E-beam lithography on sol-gel materials[2,3], gray-tone[4] and Focus Ion 
Beam[5]. But these methods have drawbacks of low form accuracy and poor surface 
roughness. Though the ultra precision diamond cutting with fast tool servo[6] can improve the 
surface roughness, the boundaries between micro lenses are frequently rounded due to the 
limitation of frequency response of fast tool servo. This could have very negative effect on the 
achievable filling-factor of the obtained micro-lens array. In the present research, the ultra 
precision diamond shaping approach was employed to generate micro lens array with 100% 
filling-factor and sharp boundaries.  
Elliptical micro lens array generation 
The elliptical micro lens array can do much better in converting the Gaussian beam’s 
elliptical waist to a circle waist than normal spherical micro lenses [8]. As shown 
schematically in Fig. 1, an elliptical micro lens array with radii in X and Y direction of 1.0 
mm and 1.5 mm respectively were designed to be generated in this study using a 4-axis ultra 
precision machine tool. The pitches of lenses in X and Y direction are 0.2 mm and 0.25mm. A 
diamond tool having nose radius of 1.0 mm was used to generate the curve of lenses in X 
direction. As to the curve of lenses in Y direction, it had to synchronize both Y and Z motions 
of the machine tools to finish.

mold inserts. To minimize the chemical reaction so as to prolong the service life, various protective

interfacial chemical reaction between various optical glasses, different anti-stick coating designs 
and several mold materials. The results showed that glass composition, coating design (composition, 
microstructure, thickness..), environment (vacuum, air or in protective gas), reaction temperature 
and time could all have profound effects on the interfacial chemical reaction.

Conclusions 
(1) The arrangements of the coatings have profound effect on their achievable results.  
(2) Thicker coating not necessary better: as demonstrated in the 67 layers/4nm and 97 layers/4nm 
cases, increasing the number of layers did not mean better performance. 
(3) There were strong links between high proportion of certain network modifiers and high 
tendency of inter-diffusion and sticking problems. Besides, intermediate like F also played an 
important role in triggering the interaction between glasses and protective coatings. 
(4) The results obtained in molding tests conformed to those in wetting tests.
