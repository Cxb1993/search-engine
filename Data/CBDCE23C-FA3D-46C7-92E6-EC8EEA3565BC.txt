摘要 
本研究採用選擇性雷射膠凝法成型多孔性生醫
支架結構，應用二氧化矽粉末以及溶膠，依照適當比
例混合為漿料。以25W之CO2為成型光源，選用適當
之雷射功率密度為成型條件，製作出厚7.2㎜ ×寬 7.2
㎜ ×高 7.2㎜孔洞大小為400µm之多孔性生醫玻璃
(Bio-glasses)支架結構。本研究為了使生醫支架更貼
近人體骨骼海綿結構，因此將海島結構以及多孔平板
堆疊成互連通孔結構。由實驗結果可知其適當成型參
數為掃描速度125㎜/s、雷射功率2.8W、掃描間距0.1
㎜、雷射頻率10KHz、掃描次數2次，成型時間為90
分鐘。本研究所製造之生醫支架結構具有發展組織工
程(Tissue Engineering, TE)陶瓷骨骼支架的潛能。 
 
關鍵字： 快速原型、生醫支架、選擇性雷射膠凝 
 1. 前言 
醫療科技的進步，進而提升平均國人壽命年
齡，因此相關醫療之研究逐漸受到重視。尤其是近十
幾年來生醫支架(Scaffold)於臨床中廣為運用，傳統自
體骨骼移植雖可以治療其骨骼缺陷問題，但植體本身
汙染之問題可能造成二次發病機率，進而導致療程冗
長之缺點[1]。為了避免以上之缺點，因此發展組織工
程(Tissue Engineering, TE)。組織工程是集結了細胞
(Cell)、工程材料(Engineering materials)、化學因子
(Chemical factor)而成，用於生成人造器官或修補受損
組織的技術[2]。而傳統之生醫支架製造方式多為鹽析
法 (Particulate Leaching) 、 冷 凍 乾 燥 法 (Freeze 
Drying)、纖維鍵結法(Fiber Bonding)、相分離法(Phase 
Separation) 和 海 綿 浸 漬 法 (Sponge Soaking) 等
等…[3]。以上幾種生醫支架製造方式雖可得到較高程
度之孔隙率(Porosity)，但其支架內部結構較難以控
制，例如：孔洞大小 (Pore Size)、孔洞形狀 (Pore 
Shape)、內通孔結構(Interconnectivity)等等…[8]。生
醫支架需植入人體使用，因此需要良好的生物活性
(Bioactivity)以及生物相容性(Biocompatibility)，防止
人體產生排斥進而病變之危險。而常用生醫材料
(Biomaterial) 約 可 分 類 為 金 屬 (Metal) 、 高 分 子
(Polymers)和陶瓷(Ceramic)三種[4]。 
快速原型(Rapid Prototyping, RP)技術近年來發
展迅速，因快速原型不受外型限制，可成型傳統加工
無法製造之特徵形狀[5]。因此有許多學者皆運用此技
術於生醫支架上，藉由其技術優勢成型所需之形狀，
其孔洞大小、表面形貌、支架外型皆可有效控制。快
速原型成型黏結方法主要有：光固化(Photo-curing)、
融化(Melting)、黏結(Bonding) [6]。快速原型可應用
之材料廣泛，大致可分類為液態 (Liquid)、固態
(Solid)、粉末(powder) [7]。因此可成型一般傳統製程
難以加工之陶瓷材料，不僅可透過繪圖軟體設計其元
件外型，甚至內部連通孔之形狀也可輕易成型，進而
達到輕量化、低成本之目的。 
本研究採用自行發展的選擇性雷射膠凝
(Selective Laser Gelling, SLG)法成型多孔性生醫支架
(Porosity biomedical scaffold)，透過solid works立體繪
圖軟體繪製互連通孔生醫支架後，運用自製快速原型
機台建構支架結構。 
2. 文獻回顧 
近幾年來生醫支架研究越來越多，尤其是結合
快速原型技術運用於生醫支架上更是受到矚目。因生
醫支架需要適當的孔隙率、孔洞大小和孔洞結構才可
提 供 較 佳 環 境 讓 細 胞 成 長 ， 以 便 細 胞 附 著
(Attachment) 、 增 生 (Proliferation) 、 分 裂
(Differentiation)，進而形成新的組織(Tissue) [9]。2003
年Leong[10]提到，快速原型製造技術為製造骨組織
(Bone Tissue)最好的選擇之一，其原因為快速原型具
相當高之可靠度(Reliability)、重複性(Reproducibility)
及可克服傳統加工限制。 
2012年Hong等人[11]，運用實體自由曲面成型製
造技術(Solid Freeform fabrication, SFF)成型立體結
構。將聚己內脂(Polycaprolactone, PCL)/ 聚乳酸-甘醇
酸(Polylactic-co-glycolic acid, PLGA)至130℃溶融混
合後，利用噴嘴通以壓力擠出預成型之形狀，其線徑
寬高為150μm和100μm。其結構圓柱型，直徑4.5
㎜、高 2.0 ㎜，孔洞大小為 300 μ m 、孔隙率
(Porosity)66.7%。2006年Modrinos等人[12]，運用Drop 
on Demand Printing(DDP)成型互連通孔生醫支架，以
聚己內脂和磷酸鈣(Calcium Phosphate)為材料，利用
製孔劑(Porogen)作為支撐材，建構完成後浸泡乙醇溶
解。其孔洞大小為200μm，抗壓強度(Compressive 
strength)為24.8±1.3Mpa。2006年Li等人[13]，以自製
快速原型機建構生醫支架，其製程近似於熔解沉積

5. 實驗結果與討論 
5.1 製程參數分析 
透過實驗結果得知其較佳之成型參數，以及可
成型之最小方孔尺寸，為長7㎜ ×寬 7㎜ ×厚 0.8
㎜。當功率密度值小於0.4 J/㎜2熱能不足以將漿料內
之水分蒸發，以致無法固化成型。而使用功率密度值
大於0.6 J/㎜2，雖可順利固化成型，但因熱能較高使
其水份快速蒸發。使其造成表面材料凸出而粗化，於
刮板回刮時容易碰觸表面造成破裂，以致成型失敗。
如圖6所示，為雷射功率與掃描速度關係圖。由實驗
結果得知，功率密度於0.4~0.5 J/㎜2為最佳成型參
數，此時固化成型完整，於後處理時容易清除剩餘殘
料。於孔洞大小測試部分，當功率密度值為0.45 J/㎜2
時較佳。如圖7所示，為孔洞尺寸測試平板成品圖，

In this study, using selective laser gelling method 
to form the porosity Biomedical scaffold structure. The

25W of CO2 light source with a suitable laser power 
density to produce a dimension of thick 7.2 mm × width 
7.2 mm × height 7.2 mm pore size of 400µm of porosity 
Bio-glasses scaffold model. In order to fabrication 
biomedical scaffold closer to the human skeleton sponge

this study interconnecting pore structure. The

follows: a scanning speed of 125 mm / s, laser power of 
2.8W, scanning pitch of 0.1 mm, the laser frequency 
10KHz, 2 times the number of scans. The total

  1. Introduction 
 Biomaterials can generally be classified into four categories: polymers, ceramics, metals and composites. 
Polymers are deemed to be ductile and not stiff enough and ceramics are too rigid and brittle [1-3]. 
Biomedical metals are commonly used in joint replacement, bone plates and screws, and dental root implants 
due to good mechanical properties. Nowadays titanium is popular in biomedical metal due to its low density, 
good biocompatibility – biological and chemical inertness and mechanical properties – toughness and fatigues 
strength [4-5]. 
Rapid prototyping (RP) technique is a non-conventional fabrication process for biomedical engineering 
field based on the advanced manufacturing and information technology [6]. The outstanding advantage of RP 
techniques is to fabricate complex artifact directly from computer model rapidly. This computer model can be 
constructed according to the data of magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) or computerized tomography (CT) of 
patient. Thus, RP techniques have been utilized in biomedical engineering increasing tremendously [7-8]. For 
examples, a direct metal rapid prototyping technique was utilized to fabricate porous Ti6Al4V parts with 
internal pore architecture [9].  
Many RP processes can be used to fabricate polymer scaffolds due to suitable bioactivity, but their 
mechanical properties is insufficient. Indirect fabrication can product metal scaffold for these category RP 
methods with polymer material [10-12]. Through the reverse lost mold process, first these type of RP 
machines are applied to build a negative replica that is filled with ceramic slurry to form a casting mould and 
burnt away. Then molten metal was poured and filled in this casting mould to produce scaffold. Fewer RP 
techniques are used to produce metal scaffolds directly [13. Therefore, this study presents a fabrication 
process of bone scaffold model with titanium biomedical materials by rapid prototyping (RP) technique.

2.4 Experimental process 
 The process to fabricate of biomedical titanium bone scaffold is described as follows [15,16].  
(a) A layer of biomedical titanium slurry is paved on the layer elevating platform and scrapped to control 
height (thickness) and flatness by the layer scrapper. 
(b) The paved layer of biomedical titanium slurry is selectively radiated by Nd:YAG laser beam and bonded 
to form a solidified layer. 
(c) The layer elevating platform is moved down one pitch of layer thickness (0.1mm) by a servo-motor and 
ball-screw. 
(d) Repeating steps (a) to (c). A number of solidified layers are stacked to build biomedical titanium part. 
(e) The un-bonded slurry is removed and washed by water and a biomedical titanium bone scaffold (green 
part) can be constructed. 
  
3. Results and discussion  
 
3.1 Experimental conditions 
 For this complex biomedical titanium bone scaffold can be fabricated successfully by selective laser 
sintering. There are a number of process conditions must be carefully controlled. These process conditions are 
the viscosity of this slurry, the thickness of each paved layer, and the laser power, laser scanning speed and 
hatch spacing distance. [2] 
 Although, the thickness of each paved layer can be varied from 0.05 to 0.20mm, for well fabricating 
these bone scaffolds, the constant layer thickness 0.1 mm is determined in this study. 
In this work, sintering the metal slurry needs only low laser power below 28 W. The laser power was 
adjusted from 12 to 28 W to generate a specimen. The relationship between laser power and laser scanning 
speed in laser sintering processes is indicated in Figure 3. When the laser power was less than 12 W, the 
sintered structures in the slurries were difficult to form due to insufficient laser power density for sintering 
silica. As laser power was increased from 12 to 28 W, the laser scan speed increased from 90 to 130 mm/min. 
That is the laser power increased with scan speed. When the laser power exceeded 28 W, the sintered layer 
could not be formed because the laser power density was too large for building layer.
