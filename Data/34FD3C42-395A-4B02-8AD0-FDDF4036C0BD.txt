一、中英文摘要 
本計畫之目的在於改進在下丘腦區域的核磁共振影像。從先前研究中已知下丘腦區域與帕金森氏症病
程發展有關。因此，本計畫針對下丘腦發展之高解析度及高對比的磁振成像技術，對於帕金森氏症之
臨床診斷及深腦刺激手術導引之改進深具潛力。本計畫將著重於磁振成像技術之發展。換言之，本計
畫將發展多迴訊穩定態自由旋進技術(multi-echo SSFP)；並且，此多迴訊穩定態自由旋進技術將改進平
衡穩定態自由旋進成像技術(balanced SSFP)因磁場不均勻而在下丘腦區域常見之帶狀假影。相較於傳統
T2*權重梯度迴訊技術，多迴訊穩定態技術將在下丘腦區域提供更高之對比雜訊比(此部分正在第二年
計畫發展中)。 
 
關鍵詞：磁振造影、穩定態自由旋進、帕金森氏症、下丘腦。 
 
Abstract 
The goal of this project is to improve fast MRI (Magnetic Resonance Imaging) in subthalamic region of 
human brain. Human subthalamic region is known to be related to the disease progression of Parkinson’s 
disease (PD). Therefore, the high resolution and high contrast MRI developed in this project will have great 
potential to benefit clinical diagnosis or even deep brain stimulation of PD. This project will focus on 
technical development and evaluation of MRI methods. A multi-echo Steady-state Free Precession 
(multi-echo SSFP) MRI will be developed. multi-echo SSFP will be free from banding artifact, which is 
commonly seen in balanced Steady-state Free Precession (bSSFP) MRI. Compared to conventional 
T2*-weighted gradient echo method (i.e., SPGR, spoiled gradient recalled), multi-echo SSFP will present 
higher contrast-to-noise ratio in the subthalamic region within same scan time (in the second part of the 
ongoing proposal). 
 
Keywords: Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI), Steady-state Free Precession (SSFP), Parkinson’s Disease, 
subthalamus.

Using the multi-scheme reconstruction method, these echoes were decomposed and placed in three

Figures 4b to d show images reconstructed from echo 1, 2, and 3 of multi-echo SSFP, respectively. Note that

coherent pathways. By summing those echo images, the generated composite multi-echo SSFP (Figure 4e) 
provides improved gray-white-matter contrast, and higher SNR, in comparison to the conventional balanced 
SSFP. 
 
The proposed multi-echo SSFP improves previously reported methods in two ways: (1) Pulse sequence is 
designed so that multiple SSFP echoes are acquired in a controlled way (i.e., both number of echoes and echo 
spacing). (2) The proposed echo separation and multi-scheme reconstruction methods significantly improve 
the SSFP image quality. In multi-echo SSFP, artifact-free and high SNR SSFP images can be achieved with a 
single acquisition. Furthermore, multi-echo SSFP data from multiple coherent pathways enable higher T2/T2* 
contrast compared to conventional T2* SPGR technique which can be used to improve contrast in the 
subthalamic region (in the second part of the ongoing proposal).
