 I 
摘要 
本計畫研究方向以主持人過去幾年研究成果為基礎，持續進行基於知識本體(Ontology)之
智慧型代理人(Intelligent Agent)於能力成熟度整合模式(Capability Maturity Model Integration, 
CMMI)知識管理(Knowledge Management, KM)之應用，以更進一步強化 Ontology 架構及運用
智慧型代理人等技術研發支援高階 CMMI Assessment 之知識管理代理人核心技術。第一年研
究成果包括: CMMI 技術需求、學習導向技術解決方案知識管理、專案規劃代理人及基於知識
本體模糊代理人。第二年進一步導入組織流程專注(Organizational Process Focus, OPF)、組織流
程定義 (Organizational Process Definition, OPD) 、組織流程績效 (Organizational Process 
Performance, OPP)及量化專案管理(Quantitative Project Management, OPM)等流程領域(Process 
Area, PA)，並以大學研發實驗室為應用案例，建構知識本體應用與軟體工程實驗室流程改善資
產館，以達成量化專案管理。另一方面，本計畫與義大利 Vincenzo Loia 教授及其研究團隊合
作，使用模糊標記語言(Fuzzy Markup Language, FML)說明應用案例之知識庫及規則庫。同時，
我們也使用網絡本體語言(Web Ontology Language, OWL)說明應用案例知識本體。除此之外，
本計畫亦與英國 Hani Hagras 教授合作嘗詴將第二型模糊集合(Type-2 Fuzzy Set, T2FS)導入知
識本體(Ontology)模型中。最後，根據所建置之知識本體及模糊推論結果，以語意方式呈現流
程改善的結果，讓組織可以很容易地得知流程改善優缺點。由實驗結果可知，所提出之方法可
以有效地應用在 CMMI 之知識管理。 
 
關鍵詞: 能力成熟度整合模式，知識本體，組織流程定義，知識管理，模糊標記語言

Mellon University)的軟體工程學院 (Software 
Engineering Institute, SEI)所進行的一項研究
成果。其目的是用來評估及改善軟體發展公
司之軟體開發過程及軟體開發能力，並且協
助軟體開發者持續改善軟體流程成熟架構及
軟體品質，進而提昇軟體開發專案及軟體發
展公司之軟體開發管理能力，達成軟體發展
之功能正確、縮短開發時程、降低成本及確
保品質等目標[1]。SEI 於 2000 年 12 月公佈能
力成熟度整合模式(Capability Maturity Model 
Integration, CMMI)，更進一步將能力成熟度
模式整合，逐漸取代現行之 CMM 標準[1]。 
CMMI 是 SEI 繼 CMM 成功發展後的新修
訂版本，目的在發展一個共通性之整合架
構，以支援整合不同專業領域之特定能力成
熟度模式及相關產品，其不僅提高每一級別
成熟度要求之門檻，亦同時擴充能力成熟度
評鑑適用範圍，使得軟體工程、系統工程之
專業領域及整合性產品與流程發展之環境，
皆能運用 CMMI 為軟體開發流程提供持續改
善的指引，對軟體生產力與品質的提升亦有
顯著的實質效益，並確保所有發展的產品，
能與國際標準組織／國際電技協會 (ISO／
IEC)15504 軟體流程評鑑技術報告相容並一
致[1]。CMMI 之流程領域是一組相關的執行
方法，用以達成一般與特定目標。流程是組
織持續改善的要素之一，由於軟體的發展與
其管理有著極大的關係，CMMI 中的流程管
理規範、完整的品質改善與評鑑方法等觀念
對於軟體發展與維護提出了一套完整的架
構。CMMI 整合各產官學界中的經驗與知識，
透過驗證的方法彙整出一套標準的流程架
構 ， 可 分 為 連 續 式 表 述 (Continuous 
Representation) 與 分 段 式 表 述 (Staged 
Representation) [1]。 
從哲學角度來看，知識本體(Ontology)是
處理生命體或現實事物的本質；從人工智慧
的角度來看，Ontology 是一個概念化的明確
陳述[2]，也是一個可以存在知識代理人智慧
中的實體理論；以知識為基礎的觀點上看，
Ontology 可視為是一個字彙概念系統[3]。
Ontology 中最常用的幾個組成分子分別是物
件(Object)、值(Value)、關係(Relation)和函數
(Function)。其中物件可以代表 Concept，而
Concept 可以是一個說法、想法或是實體。在
這些實體中可以給予值，使得其具備屬性-值
(Attribute-Value)的資料型態。而在物件間的相
互關係裡有關係(Relation)予以定義，並且在
物件間有特殊化 (Specialization) 與一般化
(Generalization)的關係存在。而特殊化與一般
化可以讓物件間的推演找到更為明確或是模
糊的解釋。例如物件間的繼承(Inheritance)或
是物件間的兄弟關係。而透過這些關係上的
定義，將能得到更多所需的訊息[2][3]。 
國內學者亦有多人進行 Ontology 相關研
究，包括: 中央研究院、資策會、工研院、台
大、清大、交大、成大、中央、中正、台科
大、大同、台南大學，高雄大學，….，等學
術研究機構及大學皆有計畫在進行。Ontology
於不同領域上的應用，可充分地呈現領域知
識。因而，本研究計畫針對 CMMI 中之流程
資產、度量儲存及各流程領域之工作產品，
建構出高階 CMMI Ontology 架構，更進一步
地由 Ontology 架構及智慧型技術來研發支援
高階 CMMI 管理知識平台，語意搜尋機制及
驗證確認度量分析進行相關知識的分享及傳
遞。 
 
3. 文獻探討 
知識本體經常被用作為語意網的一種標
準知識表達方式。然而，典型知識本體的概
念化表示並不能充分表示在許多應用領域中
的不確定資訊，模糊知識在許多需要面對不
精確且大量的知識及資訊的領域扮演一個重
要的角色，例如: 文件探勘、多媒體資訊系統
及人類自然語言處理。為了解決這類型的問
題，一種可能的解決方法就是將模糊邏輯與
知識本體做結合去處理不確定性的資料，模
糊知識本體有足夠的能力可以處理模糊知
識，而且在文件及多媒體物件表示及探索是
有效的[7]。知識本體可用於表達資訊及其語
意，它可以被視為是一種知識表達的工具
[12]。

出之智慧型健康飲食規劃多代理人可以有效地進行健康飲食之規劃。未來本研究會更進一步加
入基因演算法及Type2 Fuzzy Set，以改善及加強目前研究成果。 
Good eating habits can make human beings to live in a healthy lifestyle. When a person constantly 
eats too much or too little, it will have a high risk of causing a disease for him. Therefore, developing 
healthy and balanced eating habits is important for most people to stay away from diseases. This study 
proposes an intelligent healthy diet planning multi-agent (IHDPMA), including a personal profile 
agent, a nutrition facts analysis agent, a knowledge analysis agent, a discovery agent, a fuzzy inference 
agent, and a semantic generation agent for healthy diet planning. The IHDPMA provides a semantic 
analysis of healthy diet status for people based on the pre-constructed ontology by domains experts and 
results of fuzzy inference. With the generated semantic analysis, people can get healthy information 
about what they eat and make it easier to eat a balanced and healthy diet. The experimental platform 
has been constructed to test the performance of the IHDPMA. The results indicate that the IHDPMA 
can effectively work for healthy diet planning.

Abstract—Good eating habits can make human beings to live 
in a healthy lifestyle. When a person constantly eats too much 
or too little, it will have a high risk of causing a disease for him. 
Therefore, developing healthy and balanced eating habits is 
important for most people to stay away from diseases. This 
study proposes an intelligent healthy diet planning multi-agent 
(IHDPMA), including a personal profile agent, a nutrition facts 
analysis agent, a knowledge analysis agent, a discovery agent, a 
fuzzy inference agent, and a semantic generation agent for 
healthy diet planning. The IHDPMA provides a semantic 
analysis of healthy diet status for people based on the 
pre-constructed ontology by domains experts and results of 
fuzzy inference. With the generated semantic analysis, people 
can get healthy information about what they eat and make it 
easier to eat a balanced and healthy diet. The experimental 
platform has been constructed to test the performance of the 
IHDPMA. The results indicate that the IHDPMA can 
effectively work for healthy diet planning.1

III. INTELLIGENT HEALTHY DIET PLANNING MULTI-AGENT 
The IHDPMA comprises six agents, including a personal 
profile agent, a nutrition facts analysis agent, a knowledge 
analysis agent, a discovery agent, a fuzzy inference agent, 
and a semantic generation agent, which are described below. 
A. Structure of the IHDPMA 
Fig. 5 shows the structure of the IHDPMA. First, the food 
data are gathered from the Internet and convenient stores in 
Taiwan. The processing mechanism then deals with the 
collected food data and transforms them into the information. 
Next, the extracting mechanism mines the information to the 
important knowledge and sends them into the acquiring 
mechanism. Fourth, the acquiring mechanism obtains the 
important knowledge and passes them to the domain experts. 
Fifth, the domain experts construct the FML and OWL using 
the FML editor and the protege [15], respectively, and store 
both of them into the ontology repository. Sixth, based on 
the pre-defined ontology repository, the IHDPMA finds out 
the user’s personal profile, analyzes the nutrition facts of the 
meal records, calculates the percentage of calories from 
nutrients, discovers the necessary knowledge from the 
ontology repository, infers the possibility of healthy diet 
status, and eventually generates the semantic sentences. At 
last, the inferred results are stored into the healthy diet status 
repository and the domain experts then plan the healthy diet 
for the user on the basis of the inferred results. Fig. 6 shows 
the communication sequence among the sub-agents of the 
IHDPMA. 
B. Personal Profile Agent 
The personal profile agent plays a role in retrieving the 
user’s personal profile, such as age, sex, height, weight, and 
BMI, from the profile ontology. Additionally, the past 
personal meal records also could be found by the personal 
profile agent. 
C. Nutrition Facts Analysis Agent 
In this study, the nutrition facts analysis agent is 
responsible of examining the number of carbohydrate, 
protein, and fat grams contained in one portion of the 
collected meals according to the pre-constructed food 
ontology. Meanwhile, how many calories are contained in 
one portion is also acquired.

Interestingly, some significant improvements in chess have 
been achieved with ―forced moves‖ [14]. Forced moves are 
moves that are almost mandatory for a player. A 
combination is a sequence of such moves when an opponent 
can only play forced moves. Via forced moves, one can 
increase analysis depth. In chess, according to some scholars, 
this technique is efficient and programs with forced moves 
can announce checkmate and victory far in advance. In 
computer Go, forced moves are a difficult concept. 
Cazenave [15] produced notable results using forced moves; 
however, forced moves are only forced in the sense that they 
are necessary for some particular goals, not for a complete 
victory. In Go, strong players never absolutely try to keep 
some groups alive. If the opponent can use many stones to 
kill one group, letting the opponent reach his target is fine, 
as during that time, influence is extended to another part of 
the goban, increasing the amount of territory won. Via the 
MCTS approach, very deep sequences can be produced, 
allowing computer Go to reach the same complexity as the 
computer chess despite a lack of forced moves. 
A. Monte Carlo Evaluation 
Brugmann proposed an original evaluation function based 
on Monte Carlo exploration [11]: the estimate is the 
probability of winning estimated by a random player. The 
All-Moves-As-First (AMAF) value of a move improves 
Monte Carlo evaluation by using statistics on permutations 
of games: when one simulation is performed, several 
permutations of this permutation are considered as well. 
These simulations provide statistics for several moves, other 
than the first move of the initial simulation. In the MCTS 
setting, AMAF values are usually called Rapid Action Value 
Estimation (RAVE) values [17]. The Monte Carlo 
evaluation can be used as an evaluation function in an 
alpha-beta engine. However, a recent and considerable 
improvement is the incremental construction of a tree on top 
of the Monte Carlo evaluation function. Random simulations 
are still performed from the initial node, but they are 
adaptively modified in the part which is in the tree. Outside 
the tree, the simulation uses the default random policy, 
whereas in the tree, simulations choose the move that 
maximizes a score combining two criteria: (1) Exploration 
criterion: moves that have not been simulated often should 
be more intensively simulated; and (2) Exploitation criterion: 
moves that lead to high probabilities of winning should be 
simulated more often. In this incremental construction, the 
tree is expanded in the direction of interesting moves as 
estimated by the Monte-Carlo evaluation. A well-known 
implementation of this idea is Upper-Confidence Tree (UCT) 
[13]. The overall MCTS algorithm [12] is presented in 
Algorithm 1. Moreover, this MCTS is parallel: in Fig. 1, 
each computation node builds his own tree in memory, and 3 
times per second all nodes ―share‖ their trees with other 
nodes by averaging statistics, as if the algorithm was a 
simple Monte-Carlo algorithm.

Abstract 
This paper proposes a novel type-2 fuzzy ontology, 
including a type-2 food ontology and a type-2 
FML-based ontology, for diet assessment. In addition, 
the paper presents a type-2 fuzzy diet assessment agent. 
In the proposed approach, firstly, the nutrition facts of 
various kinds of food are collected from the Internet 
and the convenience store. Next, the domain experts 
construct the type-2 fuzzy ontology, then the involved 
volunteers are requested to input eaten items. Finally, 
the proposed agent displays the diet assessment of the 
input eaten items based on the constructed type-2 fuzzy 
ontology. The experimental results show that the 
proposed approach is feasible for diet assessment. 
1. INTRODUCTION 
Ontology is an explicit specification of a 
conceptualization and a formal specification of a 
shared conceptualization [1]. The use of ontologies to 
provide interoperability among heterogeneous data 
sources has been applied in many domains, including 
medical information systems [2]. However, it is widely 
pointed out that classical ontology is not sufficient to 
deal with imprecise and vague knowledge for some 
real world applications. On the other hand, fuzzy 
ontology can effectively help to handle and process 
uncertain data and knowledge [3]. The agent 
technology is a key area in the field of artificial 
intelligence research [4]. The intelligent agent system 
has been applied to many research domain [5]. 
Fuzzy Logic Systems (FLSs) have been credited 
with providing an adequate methodology for designing 
robust systems that are able to deliver a satisfactory 
performance when contending with the uncertainty, 
noise and imprecision attributed to real world 
environments and applications [6]. As a result, FLSs 
have been used in wide range of applications including 
fuzzy ontologies. Several researchers have explored the 
use of fuzzy ontologies where Lee et al. [7] proposed a 
fuzzy ontology and applied it to news summarization. 
Knappe et al. [8] proposed a fuzzy ontology-based 
query enrichment. 
This paper combines the type-2 fuzzy sets and the 
ontology model to propose a type-2 fuzzy ontology. 
Moreover, we apply the type-2 fuzzy ontology to diet 
assessment domain to propose a fuzzy diet assessment 
agent for people with the average levels of physical 
activity. The experimental results show that the 
proposed method is feasible and the diet assessment 
can be provided as a reference for the involved 
volunteers after validation by domain experts. The 
remainder of the paper is organized as follows. Section 
2, describes the type-2 fuzzy ontology, including the 
type-2 fuzzy set, type-2 fuzzy food ontology, and 
type-2 fuzzy markup language (FML)-based ontology. 
Section 3, introduces the type-2 fuzzy diet assessment 
agent. The experimental results are shown in Section 4. 
Finally, conclusions are drawn in Section 5.

Abstract 
It is well-known that classical ontologies are not 
sufficient to deal with imprecise and vague knowledge. 
On the other hand, fuzzy ontologies can effectively 
solve data and knowledge with uncertainty, most 
importantly, if they are integrated with innovative 
methods for developing agents’ intelligence as the 
Fuzzy Markup Language (FML). In this paper, an 
FML-based ontology-based intelligent fuzzy agent and 
a semantic decision making mechanism are proposed 
to apply to the semantic decision making for diabetes 
domain. The FML-based definition is considered 
modeling the knowledge base and rule base of the 
fuzzy objects and inference operators. The 
experimental results show that the proposed method is 
feasible for diabetes semantic decision-making. 
1. INTRODUCTION 
The existing technology for creation of information 
resources in the Internet does not enable to use 
effectively opportunities of a global network in 
application areas as the medical diagnosis. For this 
reason, the W3 Consortium makes reorganization of 
web service of the Internet into the Semantic Web [l] 
for automation of services in the Web with using of 
software agents. Today, several specifications of 
markup languages for creation of information 
resources as, eXtensible Markup Language (XML), 
Resource Description Framework (RDF) and Web 
Ontology Language (OWL) are exploited to 
hierarchically and ontologically depict the real world 
knowledge used by agents to infer additional 
information and provide advanced services to 
end-users. The joint use of agent concepts [1] and 
information ontological models enable suitable 
knowledge representation methods, and automated 
intelligent processing capabilities compared with 
traditional human manual, as well as subjective 
evaluation, and more [2][3]. Nevertheless, the classical 
ontologies are not sufficient to represent the vague and 
imprecise information featuring the real-world and, 
therefore, novel tools as the Fuzzy Markup Language[4] 
and the fuzzy ontology are more suitable to handle this 
type of knowledge. 
This paper presents a healthcare application based on 
agent systems, FML and fuzzy ontology for modeling 
a diabetes semantic decision-making. In details, in 
order to achieve the aforesaid goal, the our work 
exploits a combination of two semantic representations: 
(1) The first is mainly used to define the key features 
of diabetes through a taxonomic structure based on 
Web Ontology Language (OWL) modeling. (2) The 
second is based on an alternative vision of fuzzy 
controller representation based on FML. 
The remainder of this paper is organized as follows. 
Section 2 introduces the diabetes pathology and gives a 
system’s overview. Section 3 introduces the fuzzy 
diabetes ontology. Section 4 describes the diabetes 
knowledge for intelligent agent based on FML. The 
experimental results are shown in Section 5. Finally, 
conclusions are drawn in Section 6. 
2. DIABETES AND SYSTEM OVERVIEW 
Diabetes is a disease in which your body cannot 
properly use the food you eat for energy. The goal of 
treating diabetes is to keep the glucose level as near to 
normal as possible. Nowadays, with the increasing 
number of developing diabetes around the world, more 
and more researchers in different kinds of field are 
involved in the studies on this topic. 
In this paper, we apply fuzzy logic principles and 
ontology techniques to build up an ontology-based 
intelligent fuzzy agent (OIFA) to apply to diabetes 
semantic decision making domain. The structure of the 
proposed approach consists of an ontology-based 
intelligent fuzzy agent, a Pima Indian diabetes database, 
a fuzzy diabetes ontology, a diabetes knowledge & rule 
base repository, and a diabetes decision making 
repository. The domain experts first retrieve the 
experimental samples, Pima Indians Diabetes Database 
(PIDD), from the UCI machine learning database

4.1 FML and Transparent Fuzzy Agents 
Fuzzy Logic Controller (FLC) lets controller 
designers to describe complex systems using their 
knowledge and experience by means of linguistic rules. 
But, in spite of these unquestionable advantages, the 
real design of controllers strongly depends upon the 
methodologies modeling the application domain 
knowledge. The Fuzzy Markup Language enables an 
alternative vision of fuzzy controllers implementation, 
allowing the designer to model the controllers in an 
independent methodology way. This vision is named 
Transparent Fuzzy Control and it is based on the 
integration among fuzzy systems, labeled tree theory, 
and XML methodology. Fig. 2 shows the tree structure 
of FML. 
 
Fig. 2 Structure of FML. 
 
An FML implementation is based on the 
employment of tags to model different parts of fuzzy 
controller. The root of the structure of FML models the 
Controller node and the FML tag 
<FUZZYCONTROL> is used. The FML left sub-trees 
uses the FML tag <KNOWLEDGEBASE> to model 
the Knowledge Base Component. On the other hand, 
the FML right sub-trees define the Rule Bases 
Component and the FML <RULEBASE> tag is used to 
describe the controller rule base. A more detailed tags’ 
description is given in [4]. Fig. 2 shows the multiple 
rule base representation (Rule Base 1, Rule Base 2, ..., 
Rule Base n) offered by the latest version of FML 
which allows a single FML program to code several 
rule bases and evaluate them in a parallel fashion in 
order to guarantee good results for each system’s input. 
4.2 FML Diabetic Knowledge 
As depicted in previous sections, diabetic knowledge 
can be opportunely modeled by means of a taxonomic 
approach, which is capable of focusing on different 
features characterizing the diabetic pathologies and on 
the relations among them. This approach results in a 
knowledge graph offering a static view about diabetic 
features and symptoms that can be coded by means of 
the well-known OWL languages. In this way, the 
knowledge's designers are able to express their medical 
awareness by means of imprecise structure such as 
fuzzy variables. But, this OWL-based fuzzy graph is 
not capable to directly model fuzzy operators and 
fuzzy inference engines. On the other hand, FML 
focuses on fuzzy mechanism inferring novel 
information starting from predefined knowledge and 
rule bases. Hence, the joint use of OWL fuzzy diabetes 
ontology and FML description represents the most 
suitable way to model medical concepts and fuzzy 
relationships using the taxonomical knowledge. The 
usage of this combination of methodologies of 
knowledge representation strongly highlights the 
benefits offered by the XML representation. 
4.3 Diabetic Ontological Architecture 
The domain expert retrieves the PIDD from the 
Internet and stores them into the Pima Indian diabetes 
database. After that, the diabetes domain experts 
construct the fuzzy diabetes ontology according to the 
PIDD. Next, the domain expert uses the interface 
provided by the FML generating mechanism to edit the 
FML-based definitions based on the built fuzzy 
diabetes ontology and stored them into the diabetes 
knowledge base and rule base repository. The FML 
parser then analyzes the FML-based definitions to 
translate them into the desired knowledge file format to 
be the source of the fuzzy inference mechanism. 
Afterwards, the fuzzy inference mechanism infers the 
possibility of suffering from the diabetes for each 
instance stored in the ontology. Finally, the semantic 
decision making mechanism produces the semantic 
sentences according to each inferred result and stores 
them back to the fuzzy diabetes ontology. 
With the proposed agent, the diabetes semantic 
decision-making results can be provided for the 
medical staff as a diagnosis reference to alleviate their 
workload. The FML generating mechanism is an 
interface to enable the diabetic knowledge extraction 
based on the constructed ontology and then to translate 
the knowledge into the FML-based definitions [6]. Fig. 
3 shows the screen shot of the FML tree and 
FML-based definition of the fuzzy controller. The 
function of the FML parser is to extract the knowledge 
from the FML-based definition and then translate them 
into the desired knowledge file format.

本人之學生吳閔詳同學報告應用模糊標記語言於會議排程決策支援系統之研究，在這個模糊推論
系統它有5個輸入模糊變數，其分別為(a)參與者的重要程度(User Priority, UP); (b)參與者對於會議
主題之感興趣程度(Meeting Subject Preference, MSP); (c)在同時段內參與者其他事件之重要程度
(Meeting Time Preference, MTP); (d)召集人對於其他參與者之強迫程度(Meeting Forcing Degree, 
MFD)及(e)參與者對於會議本身時間長短之感受(Meeting Time Long, MTL)。Hani Hagras教授提到
“參與者的重要程度(User Priority, UP)所代表的意義為何?”及“所提到這些模糊變數T2FS是如何得
到?” Hani Hagras教授認為T2FS非常適合應用到會議排程，因為每個人有不同的參與者的重要程度
及召集人對於其他參與者之強迫程度也不同。因此Hani Hagras教授建議可以採用大量調查方式得
到每個人對參與會議必需要考慮因素的T1FS，然後再進一步得到其相對應的T2FS。

良同學完成報告後，Hani Hagras教授提出“BMI所代表的意義為何?”及“729條模糊規則是如何設計
