Crystallization and microstructure of binary crystalline blends

本計畫第二年研究重點包括兩個主
題：(i) sPS/iPS 混摻之熔融結晶，(ii) sPS/iPS
混摻之冷結晶；主要為組裝架設小角度光
散射(SALS)儀器設備以研究高分子結晶動
力學，研究 iPS 於侷限空間內之結晶行為
與晶體形態，sPS/iPS 在冷結晶時結晶速率
之差異，sPS/iPS 冷結晶時球晶與 lamella
之尺寸與形態，sPS/iPS 相分離與結晶之競
爭比較。因此本報告包含第一年流變研究
成果，主要分為三大部份即：(1)sPS/iPS 混
摻流變行為，(2) sPS/iPS 混摻熔融結晶，
與(3) sPS/iPS 混摻冷結晶。 
 
一、以流變特性分析 sPS/iPS 混摻相行為 
    將對排聚苯乙烯(sPS)及同排聚苯乙烯
(iPS)分別秤取適當重量比例，以溶液混摻
的方式製備出不同比例 sPS/iPS (7/3, 5/5, 
3/7)混摻 sample。取適量的 sPS/iPS 混摻樣
品，在 280oC 下熱壓 10 min 製備流變儀量
測所需 sample 尺寸。sample 置於 290~300 
oC 的 Advanced Rheometrics Expanded 
System (ARES)流變儀中並在氮氣環境
下，量測 storage modulus (G’) 和 loss 
modulus (G”)，測量範圍由 0.1 至 100 rad/s。 
   將 sPS/iPS 在 290 與 300 oC 不同頻率下
所量測所得 G’與 G”值，以 log G’ vs log G”
做圖，即為 Cole-Cole (或 Han) plots，如圖
1~4。由圖 1與圖 2可以發現純 sPS在 300oC
其斜率為 1.66，而在 290oC 的斜率為 1.67。
純 iPS 在 300oC 其斜率為 1.65，而在 290oC
的斜率為 1.66。然而 sPS/iPS=5/5 sample 在
290 與 300 oC 時的斜率並不是 sPS 的 1.67
與 1.66，或 iPS 的 1.66 與 1.65。且由圖 1
發現 sPS/iPS 5/5 sample 於 290oC 時在較低
數值的 G’與 G”區域(低頻區)，出現一平坦
區，其斜率由 15 min 的 0.47 改變至 140 min
的 0.06；在 300oC 時在較低數值的 G’與 G”
區域，亦出現一平坦區，其斜率由 5 min
的 1.04 改變至 140 min 的 0.39，如圖 3 所
示。sPS/iPS=3/7 sample 結果如圖 2 所示，
發現在290oC時在較低數值的G’與G”區域
亦出現一平坦區，斜率由 6 min 的 1.03 改
變至 140 min 的 0.12；在 300oC 時也出現一
平坦區，斜率由 5 min 的 1.51 改變至 120 
min 的 0.20，如圖 4 所示。理論上單一均勻
相之流體(homogeneous fluid)若 log G’ vs 
log G”做圖可得一精通曲線且在低頻區斜
率趨近於 2.0，對混摻而言若 log G’ vs log 
G”曲線偏離了精通曲線且在低頻區出現
G’之高原區(plateau)時，logG’-logG”之斜率
漸趨近於 0，此意謂著兩相流體之形成，且
其相分離結構為一 bi-continuous 形態。因
此由流變數據顯示 sPS/iPS=5/5 與 3/7 混摻
流體在 290 到 310 oC 的溫度範圍均形成了
相 分 離 結 構 ， 且 為 一 類 似 spinodal 
decomposition 之相分離過程。

sPS/iPS 相分離隨時間的變化時，雖然控制

    在本研究中使用兩種實驗方法(SALS及
POM)來量取球晶半徑，但兩者得到的球晶
半徑物理意義有些許不同，SALS所得到的
數據是一入射光徑(beam diameter)範圍內
所有球晶之半徑之平均；而POM則是直接
對單一球晶成長變化做觀察，就兩者而言
SALS數據為範圍性平均較具客觀性，但由
POM直接觀察到球晶變化，可作為SALS
間接觀察的補足。圖16為neat iPS在130oC
冷結晶過程SALS的Hv影像，可觀察到由球
晶所造成的Hv散射圖形(four-leaf-clover)，
隨著時間的過去，four-leaf-clover圖形範圍
逐漸由大變小，沿著方位角45o方向量測其
散射光強度，可以發現有一光強度極大值
存在；如圖17中以散射向量q來表示與圖形
中心點距離，可發現散射光強度極大值所
對應的地方，逐漸由大q向小q位置移動，
且散射光強度也不斷增強，由散射光強度
極大值所對應的散射向量qm 以及公式 qm 
× R = 4.09，可算出球晶半徑R。將neat iPS
球晶半徑對時間作圖，斜率即為晶體成長
速率。隨著等溫結晶溫度的降低，球晶成
長速率趨於緩慢，且由於在低溫成核數較
多，結晶完後球晶半徑有較小的趨勢。 
    sPS/iPS=5/5 在 300oC 等溫 90 分鐘後
quench成amorphous狀態，於135oC冷結晶
過程不同時間之SALS的Hv影像，如圖18；
此時Hv影像卻看不到sPS球晶所造成的
four-leaf-clover pattern。圖19為sPS/iPS=5/5
在300oC等溫90分鐘後quench成amorphous
狀態，於135oC冷結晶過程，不同時間所測
得的FTIR圖形，由實驗數據可以發現5分鐘
時就有1224 cm-1 的TTTT 吸收峰出現，表
示此時有sPS 晶體存在，從圖19中可明顯
的看出在80分鐘時則有920 cm-1 的iPS 吸
收峰產生，而sPS的晶型是α型為主。 
    由SALS可得混摻經 300oC等溫 10~90
分鐘熱處理後，於不同溫度(Tc)進行冷結
晶過程中 iPS 的晶體成長速率，若對 iPS
重量分率(φiPS) normalize 可得圖 20，從圖
中可發現 sPS/iPS=5/5混摻在 300oC等溫 90 
分鐘後，iPS 晶體成長速率比經熱處理 10
分鐘還要快，且與 neat iPS 重疊在一起，
此表示混摻經 300oC 等溫 10 分鐘與 90 分
鐘後是處於不一樣的狀態，導致 iPS 晶體
成長速率的不同。sPS/iPS=3/7混摻在300oC
等溫 10 分鐘與 90 分鐘的 iPS 晶體成長速
率相當，且與 neat iPS 重疊在一起，但高
結晶溫度時(140~160oC)，經 300oC 等溫 10
分鐘的 iPS 晶體成長速率比 neat iPS 還要
慢，並沒有重疊在一起的情形。

Philadelphia)舉辦，感謝國科會計畫經費補助，使筆者能順利前往出席此次國際重要會議，並

學生M. Raab發表)，海報論文一篇(共同作者為筆者博士班學生簡煥聲，亦出席此次會議)。

米纖維，在製程發展與纖維應用上之最新研究成果。 
本人的口頭論文報告安排於 polymer chemistry division 中的 8th international bio-related 
polymers symposium: Nanostructure and Biomaterials Applications時段，於 8/20上午 8:55~9:15
發表，題目為 Viscosity effects and internal structure of electrospun poly[(R)- 3-hydroxybutyrate] 
fibers，報告內容引起部分與會學者相當興趣，於會場有相當程度的討論與意見交換。 
因本實驗室與 University of Pennsylvania生化系的 Prof. Discher有研究合作關係，而賓大
校園亦位於費城市中心，距會議地點不遠，因此借此機會亦順道拜訪 Prof. Discher實驗室，
進一步進行學術交流，Prof. Discher研究主要是利用本研究室所製備的高分子奈米纖維膜，進
行幹細胞的培養研究，現階段已取得不錯的研究成果。

2. 過程 
According to the ACS president, there are more than 8000 papers in 717 half-day oral sections 
and 93 poster sections during the conference.  About 10,000 persons were believed to attend this 
“big conference”.  In fact, the presentations for each division were spread out in 17 different 
locations (hotels and convention center) around the Philadelphia downtown area.  Fortunately, 
those presentations in both POLY and PMSE divisions are scheduled in the same location: Sheraton 
Philadelphia City Center.  Therefore, we did not have to run across the streets to attend various 
seminars that we felt interested to listen.     
Since there was no direct fly from Taiwan to Philadelphia, I took the JAL airline from 
Kaoshiung to Tokyo and made a connection flight by American airline to reach the conference site.  
After registration on Monday, I checked the location of the conference room for my presentation; 
it’s about 15 minutes walking from the main venue (Philadelphia Convention Center).  I attended 
the Sci-Mix Interdivisional poster section on Monday evening.  It’s a very good opportunity to see 
all kinds of chemistry-related, especially polymer science, researches in one location since they 
were arranged in the same huge room.  The atmosphere was quite nice, and people were always 
discussing what they have obtained and discovered new things.  To attend the poster section is an 
enjoyable thing, very much like shopping in the department store; you may pick up the topics that 
you feel interested and ask the presenters nearby the poster some questions, and usually friendly 
response will come to you.  In my opinion, it’s kind of “fast food of knowledge” to be swallowed 
in a short time.   
On Tuesday afternoon, I attended the section D of POLY division which was contributed to the 
winner of “Paul J. Flory Polymer Education Award”, Dr. Frank Kelly, who was the dean of 
Polymer Science and Polymer Engineering College when I was a PhD graduate student in 
University of Akron in 1988~1992.  In 2007, Dr. Kelly stepped down from the position because of 
his retirement.  In addition to Dr. Kelly, I also met Dr. S. Z. D. Cheng and Dr. F. Harris; both are 
the faculty member from Akron University.  Dr. Cheng now becomes the Dean of the Polymer 
Science and Polymer Engineering.  To congratulate Dr. Kelly’s achievements in the polymer 
education, Dr. Cheng also gave a presentation of “Future directions of the polymer program at the 
University of Akron”.  It’s a touching and wonderful talk since many things, such as the 
development of new research fields and collaborations, are involved.  Some of them are connected 
to the past that I was there for 5 years, and some of them show the strength and potential role of my 
mother university.  At the end, everyone came towards to Dr. Kelly to congratulate what he did to 
promote the polymer education in the past twenty years.  Indeed, Dr. Kelly earned his PhD degree 
from Akron U. in the early 1960, and served as a faculty member since ~1975.  He is the key

二、與會心得 
1. 心得 
    It’s always good to share the research findings and idea with others, especially in an 
international conference such as ACS meeting that I attended this time.  By attending the 
conference, some new concepts and research progress around the world will be absorbed and soon 
might become the new driving force and idea for my research career.  Another good opportunity is 
to meet the people around the world who have a similar expertise and research interest.  By 
discussing in the conference and further communicating after the conference, a strong bond relation 
will be built up for the later collaboration.  Therefore, I usually go abroad and attend the 
international conference at least once in a year to further broadening and strengthening my research 
level. 
    I sincerely appreciated NSC for partially supporting my trip to the States to attend this ACS 
meeting.  Indeed, some good response in the meeting and further collaboration with Dr. Discher of 
University of Pennsylvania are undoubtedly expected.      
2.建議事項 
    To attend the conference held abroad is a good chance not only for the university faculty but 
also for the graduate students, especially those in the PhD program, to realize the new research 
progress and achievements in the world.  Under this consideration, the priority and financial 
support for attending international conference should be raised and announced to encourage 
researchers for going abroad.  At least, people in the conference will recognize more about Taiwan 
and where National Cheng Kung University is!
