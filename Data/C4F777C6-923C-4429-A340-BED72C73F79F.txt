一、前言 
 
研究計畫之背景及重要性： 
隨著科技的發展，能源，軍事，生物，醫療，生物科技等對超低溫的要求越來越高，
尤其是在生物科技方面，生物細胞的保存技術是影響移植成功的關鍵；生物細胞的保存

技術主要有三種：1. 短期4℃冷藏保存 2. 消耗性液態氮保存 3. 直接-80℃冷凍保存.

文獻回顧: 
 圖 1.1 為內複疊冷凍系統的示意圖，以及圖 1.2 為對應之壓焓圖。今以 R23/R134a
混合冷媒作為工作流體為例 [9]，由於 R23 和 R134a 在不同濃度配比下有不同的沸點
或冷凝點。因此，此兩種冷媒混合後形成非共沸混合冷媒。其工作循環流程如下：從
壓縮機排出的高溫高壓 R23(30%)／R134a(70%)混合冷媒蒸氣進入冷凝器，在其中冷
卻冷凝。冷凝後再與低溫低壓的冷媒在迴熱器中熱交換使的液態和氣態的溫度再次下
降讓分離效果更好。下降溫度後的 R23/R134a 混合冷媒進入相分離器因為重力分離所
以分為兩路，一路是富含 R23(16%)／R134a(84%)的液態混合冷媒，經膨脹閥膨脹後
進入蒸發冷凝器吸熱蒸發；另一路是富含 R23(58%)／R134a(42%)的氣態混合冷媒，
在蒸發冷凝器冷卻為液體後，然後經膨脹閥進入蒸發器，在其中吸收被冷卻物的熱量，
實現製冷。最後，富含 R23 的蒸氣與富含 R134a 的蒸氣混合後進入迴熱器中與冷凝器
出口的中溫高壓的冷媒熱交換後被壓縮機吸入、壓縮，從而完成整個冷凍循環。 
 
有關內複疊冷凍系統的研究已有許多學者提出探討，研究方向大部分是對冷媒種
類的選取及濃度配比的研究居多，在發表後轉成專利居多。 
在專利研究部分: 美國的 Podbielniak[11]就提出 ACR 的構想，後來 Ruhemann[12]
提出了以 R13/R22 冷媒混合成的冷媒混合物做為二級串疊 ACR 系統的工作流體，最
低溫度為-65℃，但是他們都沒有獲得實際應用。俄羅斯科學家 Keemenko 首先提出將
採用多級串疊，多種冷媒混合而成的冷媒混合物 ACR 系統的工作流體應用於天然氣
液化構想。同年 Keemenko[13]設計出單台壓縮機、二級串疊 ACR 系統，以 R13/R12
冷媒混合成的冷媒混合物做為二級串疊ACR系統的工作流體，當蒸發壓力為0.1MPa、
壓縮比為 11 的時候，得到了-80℃的最低蒸發溫度，在低溫的應用上得到突破，讓 ACR
的冷凍系統在 60 年代末期開始進入工業應用，後來迅速發展為液化天然氣工業的最主
要的型式。美國的 Fuderer[14]提出二級串疊 ACR 系統，採用垂直布置的冷凝管，利
用重力達到分離效果。研究結果顯示當採用 R13/R12 冷媒，混合比例為 50/50 混合成
的冷媒混合物、蒸發壓力和排氣壓力為 0.2/2.5MPa 時，可以達到-116℃的最低蒸發溫
度、改採用 R50/R12 冷媒，混合比例為 20/80 混合成的冷媒混合物、雙級壓縮，冷凝
溫度 32℃時，可以達到-156℃的最低蒸發溫度。 
美國 Polycold 公司的 Missimer[15]為改善 Fuderer[14]研究中壓力高、穩定時間長
的問題，提出了具有旁通管路的 ACR 系統。該系統用於冷卻容積為 127L 低溫儲藏箱，
該系統採用 R503/R114 冷媒混合成的冷媒混合物，當最終蒸發溫度為-73.5℃時，無旁
通管路的 ACR 系統的壓力為 2.4MPa，到達最終蒸發溫度需要 90 分鐘；有溫度控制旁
通管路的 ACR 系統的壓力為 1.9MPa；到達最終蒸發溫度需要 45 分鐘；有壓力控制旁
通管路的 ACR 系統的壓力為 2.07MPa；到達最終蒸發溫度需要 60 分鐘。Missimer[16]
針對 Missimer[17]提出的單級壓縮多級串疊的 ACR 系統做修改，在每次液氣分離後，
將一部分的高壓冷凝液態膨脹後與前一級的低壓回流的液氣混合物進行熱交換，其餘
的高壓冷凝液氣混合物進入蒸發器制冷，可得到-40℃~-100℃的低溫。 Missimer [18]
又提出含 CFC 冷媒混合物及不含 CFC 冷媒混合物自由混合的轉換的研究，並比較「傳
統製冷系統用的共沸、混合等小滑落溫差流體」與「自動串疊系統所需的大滑落溫差
非共沸冷媒」之間的差異，使用的設計軟體具有 Carnahan-Starling-De Santis 與
Peng-Robinson 狀態關係方程式與基於 Raoult’s law 的成分關係。研究結果顯示使用不
含 CFC 混合冷媒的卡諾效率為 17.1％，使用含 CFC 混合冷媒的卡諾效率為 14.9％。

Numerical Study on the Airflow Uniformity and Heat Transfer 
Performance of Innovative Air-Cooled Condensers with 
Variant-Rows Configuration 
The above-mentioned literature demonstrates that research on improving the uniformity of airflow over 
condenser coils is still lacking. The objective of this study was to design three innovative variant-rows 
configuration (VRC) condenser coils without increasing the area for heat transfer, the number of copper 
tubes, or the number of fans. These designs address the phenomenon of uneven airflow through condenser 
coils in air-cooled liquid chillers. Using CFD airflow simulation and heat transfer analysis, the influence of 
the three VRC condenser coils on the distribution of coil airflow and heat transfer performance was 
investigated to discover the optimal configuration of the condenser coils. These results could serve as an 
important reference in the improvement of chiller performance (energy efficiency) in the future.

To address the issues above, this study designed three innovative condenser coils (Cases 2 to 4) in light of 
the distribution defects in Case 1 on the condition that the numbers of copper tubes and fans remained the 
same. To improve the uniformity of airflow, Case 2 and Case 3 specifically increased the number of tube 
rows in the areas of higher flow velocity and decreased the number of tube rows in the rest. Adjusting the 
number of tube rows changed the pressure drop and flow velocity of airflow over the coils and further 
addressed the uneven airflow distribution in Case 1. Also, to overcome the issue of velocity difference 
between inner and outer coils, an asymmetric coil design was adopted in Case 4. The airflow simulation 
results for the three VRC condenser coils are shown in Figures 6(b), 6(c), and 6(d). The airflow distribution 
at the central cross-sectional plane in Case 2 (Fig. 6(b)) shows that compared to Case 1, the velocity 
increased at the top of both the inner and outer coils; also, the area covered by the low velocity zone at the 
bottom reduced. Obviously, the average flow velocity in Case 2 is higher than that in Case 1 and airflow was 
distributed more evenly. The airflow distribution in Case 3 is shown in Figure 6(c), revealing a similar 
distribution with Case 2; both configurations improved the issue of uneven distribution seen in Case 1. 
Figure 6(d) provides the airflow distribution in Case 4. A close comparison reveals that in addition to the 
advantages of Case 2 and Case 3, the average inlet velocity of air at the inner coils in Case 4 is significantly 
higher than the other three. Furthermore, the area occupied by the low velocity zone at the bottom of the 
coils in Case 4 is less than those in Case 2 and Case 3. 
 
From the combined results of the four simulations, the three innovative VRC condenser coil arrangements 
designed by this study all improved the uneven airflow at the top and bottom on the same side of a coil as 
well as reduced the velocity difference between both sides of the coils. Of the three designs, Case 4 
displayed the best overall performance. This indicates that variant rows of configuration and asymmetric 
design concepts are successful and feasible.

4. CONCLUSIONS 
The objective of this study was to design three innovative VRC condenser coils to address the issue of 
uneven airflow in air-cooled water chillers without increasing total area of heat transfer, number of copper 
tubes, and number of fans. The effects of the three VRCs on distribution of coil airflow and performance of 
heat transfer were analyzed using CFD airflow simulation and heat exchanger analysis. The results showed 
that the designs of the three VRCs could all effectively improve the uniformity of airflow distribution in V-V 
configuration coils in addition to increasing average flow velocity and total heat transfer rate, of which an 
asymmetrical VRC design (Case 4) showed the best improvement. In Case 4, the overall average flow 
velocity and heat transfer rate were higher than the conventional case (Case 1) by 10.3 % and 5.3 % 
respectively. The findings of this study can be applied in relevant air-cooled condensing units and serve as an 
important reference for work on increasing the energy efficiency of air-cooled water chillers.
