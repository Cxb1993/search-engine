中文摘要 
近年來生醫材料的處理上已趨向利用不同化學組成和微圖案，控制或觀察細胞的貼附
與生長，進而發展為組織再生的依據。在微圖案製造上，傳統的光學微影方法經常會在處
理過程中破壞或污染生物活性分子；微觸轉印法很是方便，但是僅限於可以形成自組裝單
層的材質，且僅可轉印小分子及蛋白質。研究即是利用軟印刷製程進行高分子轉印，可在
平坦或已有微圖案的基材表面快速的轉印具有生物活性的親水性高分子。轉印過程應用氧
電漿活化 PDMS 圖章表面，及利用磷脂質層雙極性特性增加對親水性高分子的吸附，以減少
轉印不均勻或線寬收縮的情形，增加轉印的完整性。 
   為了製作單細胞感測電極，必須利用此轉印技術進行細胞平台的製作和表面修飾。首
先在平台上需製作細胞貼附層，可在立體微圖案的表面上利用滾輪轉印不同的親水性高分
子，經由掃瞄式電子顯微鏡(SEM)以及原子力顯微鏡(AFM)觀察分析，晶片平台上可被轉印
一層高分子薄膜(膜厚約 350nm)；再藉由能量分散光譜儀(EDS)及化學分析電子能譜儀(ESCA)
分析平台上下元素含量的不同，可證明確實有親水性高分子被轉印至平台的表面。我們嘗
試轉印 chitosan、gelatin、hyaluronan 和 alginate 等幾種細胞培養貼附的高分子。細胞
不沾粘層（平台下）的製作則是利用自組裝單層的方法，將 PEG 寡聚物接枝在平台下。利
用細胞貼附層及抗細胞沾黏層的製作，即可將細胞引導貼附於特定之位置，而能更進一步
的針對特定細胞進行觀察及監測。 
   使用單細胞平台進行細胞的貼附實驗，以探討細胞與材料及微圖案的關係。經由貼附
後細胞數量計算，在所轉印的親水性高分子中以 gelatin 的貼附情況較佳。單細胞晶片平
台有不同的微圖案，利用不同的微圖案平台觀察微圖案與細胞的影響，則以五邊形及六邊
形的貼附率較高。利用 3T3 纖維母細胞和許旺細胞經過三天的培養，以光學顯微鏡(O.M)
觀察到，神經細胞在平台上易成單一細胞貼附於平台表面上，並伸出突觸以連結各單細胞，
顯示細胞與細胞間會有交互作用影響的現象。後續之研究將可利用微電極配合單細胞平台
做細胞即時觀測，針對單細胞可藉由量測代謝物過氧化氫(H2O2)的產生，藉以進一步可分析
細胞的活性。

一、前言 
在微機電與生物技術迅速發展的現今，結合微製程製作、微流體控制與生物培養技術
的生物感測器已廣泛地受到重視。生物感測器發展的趨勢是微型化，其中又以細胞做為辨
識元件之微小化生物感測器更進一步被發展成細胞測試平台。因為細胞是構成人體之最小
基本單位，亦是細胞做為測試最重要的原因之一。針對單個細胞進行研究，可以獲得反映
細胞生理狀態和過程的更準確、更全面的資訊，還可以使人們能更好地瞭解細胞群體中某
些特殊的細胞功能，更深入地認識細胞個體差異、細胞間相互作用和資訊傳遞，以及神經
傳遞物質、藥物或毒物刺激的生理影響等更深層次的資訊。 
細胞感測器的偵測原理，主要是透過量測細胞代謝率的變化或是偵測細胞膜電位的改
變，以量化細胞在外源性因子刺激下的生理反應。藉由微製作流程與細胞培養技術的整合，
將細胞當做量測的感應器，並且把細胞產生的訊號透過微電極介面（neuron-microelectrode 
interface）結合，在生物領域上，將有機會提供一個高感度的細胞感測器，可使微小化細胞
感測器能具有快速檢測、試劑微量化與同時多功能檢測之特性。 
生醫用微奈米圖案在生醫工程上之應用，過去的作法是利用光學微影(Photolithography)
的半導體製程，其成本高、費時、且材料大多限於矽晶片或玻璃等材質，對於細胞的形態
觀察，相當受限。利用高分子轉印(polymer inking)及滾輪壓印(Roller imprinting) 技術，可
以快速方便及大量進行微圖案的製備，配合微電極以量測細胞的變化，未來在製作多層組
織結構及研究細胞電生理行為時較具優勢。 
在本研究中，利用高分子轉印技術及滾輪壓印技術製備細胞貼附層，以及自組裝單分
子層(Self-assembled monolayers；SAMs)表面修飾的方式製作細胞不沾粘層。以親水性高分
子做為細胞貼附層的材料，將其轉印在特定位置，吸引細胞的附著，其餘區域則以其他物
質進行抗細胞貼附之處理，故可將細胞定位貼附於特定位置之上。後續之研究可利用單細
胞平台技術配合過氧化氫(H2O2)微電極的偵測，可監測H2O2的變化做為分析，利用H2O2
的變化可做為細胞活性變化分析。

目前對於單細胞的研究，有成熟的膜片箝制技術（patch clamping）、場效應電晶體陣列
(Field effect transistor; FET)、微電極陣列(Microelectro-ode array; MEA）、光尋址電位感測器

1.膜片箝制技術（patch clamping） 
膜片箝制(Patch Clamp)技術，偵測方式是使用玻璃吸管移動到要紀錄的細胞上，此方
式可得到正確且精準的資訊，但卻需要非常熟練的操作技術，且相當耗費時間卻只能得到
少量的紀錄資訊，且基本上選取細胞屬於任意，較難對一細胞作操作和跟監。 
2.場效應電晶體陣列(Field effect transistor; FET) 
將細胞培養於FET 表面，利用外加光、電、或藥物等的刺激，使細胞電位產生變化，
訊號通過很薄的一層電解液耦合到偵測表面上。測量細胞下對應FET閘極上的電壓變化，
即可實現胞外電壓或離子通道信號傳導途徑的測量。 
3.微電極陣列（Microelectrode array; MEA） 
通過基材底部設計的微電極來檢測胞外電壓和電信號在細胞間的傳導。微陣列可以提
供在細胞外數十個點同時量測，藉此得到細胞外與位置相關的量測數據。 
4.光尋址電位感測器(Light- Addressable potentiometric sensor; LAPS) 
利用具有電解液絕緣層半導體結構的LAPS 系統，利用半導體受一定波長光照射下產
生光電流。在流動裝置中加入藥物後，可以利用雷射聚焦在細胞生長位置，細胞在藥物作
用下發生生化反應，引發動作電位，這種變化通過電解液耦合到LAPS上，相當於在LAPS
的偏壓上又疊加一個微小信號，使得裝置可以偵測到訊號。 
5.掃瞄式電化學顯微鏡(scanning electrochemical microscopy, SECM) 
SECM是把樣品基材和微探針電極，置於含電解質溶液的電解槽中，當探針電極慢慢
靠近基材表面時，發生電化學反應探針電極可作三維掃描，同時把法拉第電流轉化，而得
知基材表面形貌，並可推導相關電化學反應。 
6.電化學式細胞感測器 
電化學式微電極主要設計以測定細胞外鄰近的信號，因細胞鄰近的濃度與外溶液的濃
度不同，所以必須將電極作在細胞附近，且必須有快速的應答速度，則細胞可以被即時監
測。一般測量單細胞的變化，以代謝反應為主。常測量的有氧、二氧化碳、pH 值和葡萄
糖等因子。已有研究團隊針對單細胞利用微電極以電化學方式偵測其胰島素、一氧化氮及
葡萄糖的代謝。

3.3微圖案化技術 
3.3.1高分子轉印技術(Polymer Inking) 
在目前的微圖案製作中，除了以光學微影技術外，其中微觸印刷(Microcontact Printing；
µCP)和奈米壓印蝕刻技術(nanoimprinting lithography, NIL) 被發展為克服光學微影製程
(photolithography)解析度上的限制，二者是為高生產率和低價格的圖案化技術。後來又由
Guo 團隊(Bao et al., 2003)發展出另一種圖案化技術，稱為高分子轉印(polymer inking)技
術。此高分子轉印技術在基材上亦可產生微米和奈米圖案，是利用µCP 原理，讓轉印材料
從印章的凸面轉印到基材上，但不同的是，高分子被當做轉印的材料而非小分子。 
高分子轉印流程如圖3-14所示。高分子轉印技術優於µCP 的是，可選擇不同的高分子
進行轉印，因為利用微觸印刷技術可以輕易轉印小分子，因為被吸附在PDMS上的小分子
處在液態分佈狀況，分子間作用鬆散，不會有嚴重的橫向拉力存在。但是若以微觸印刷轉
印高分子時，則因為高分子相對處在固態，分子間纏繞之橫向作用力極大，故轉印時經常
連不欲轉印的區域也有圖形，或是圖形轉印不完全，造成圖案失真。

4.2 利用滾輪壓印技術製備立體微圖案細胞平台

胞感測器，其所觀察或監測到細胞是為多個細胞群或細胞團所表現出的形態或訊息，故可
能會錯失掉許多的資訊。發展單細胞平台，可將細胞單一的分離固定，有助於單細胞形態
變化進行觀察。

5.5 細胞貼附培養 
在製備立體微圖案細胞平台這部份的實驗中所使用的微圖案平台有不同的形狀，經由
初步的貼附統計後似乎以五邊形及六邊形的貼附狀況較好，且一般貼附型的細胞被懸浮起
來後大都呈圓形狀，且在許多的研研究中大部份所使用的微圖案是為方形或圓形，因此針
對不同微形狀對細胞貼附的影響探討之實驗，決定使用較常見的方形、圓形，五邊形及六
邊形等四種不同的微圖案形狀做為細胞貼附平台進行探討。 
細胞貼附層則以 gelatin、haluronan、alginic acid 及 chitosan 四種親水性高分子做為探討
細胞貼附之影響。在實驗過程中所使用的細胞為 3T3 細胞及許旺氏神經細胞。

5.5.3 單細胞貼附培養的探討 
    在本實驗過程中，利用細胞貼附於平台表面上後，將未貼附的細胞沖洗掉後再續培養

楚看到細胞會伸出其偽足呈紡錘狀的從凸面表面跨越凹面生長貼附到另一個凸面表面生

會形成長形紡錘狀的彼此皆觸，並跨越平台表面的續生長。 
貼附培養的過程中發現，許旺神經細胞貼附微圖案平台上，經過 3 天的培養後，觀察
到神經細胞間會有彼此相接觸生長的情形，若單一神經細胞若未與另一神經細胞有接觸生
長，則易在微圖案平台上貼附形成單一細胞生長，而 3T3 細胞經一段時間的培養後其生長
是較無方向性，因此要在單細胞平台上研究單一細胞的反應或生長，神經細胞或許更易定
位以利研究。

Summary 
A “patch clamp” microelectrode modified on the surface for single cell adhesion and growth was 
developed using a novel polymer-transfer technique to deposit cell-adhesive polymer layers on 
the substrate platform. Several hydrophilic polymers—polyvinyl pyrrolidone, chitosan, alginic 
acid, and gelatin were successfully transferred onto the platform in order to enhance cell adhesion. 
The process was developed to apply a PDMS stamp pre-adhered with a phosphorylcholine (PC) 
layer on the protruding areas of the pattern, followed by adsorbing an adhesive polymer to 
imprint the adhesive layer onto the platform. Plasma activation on the stamp, adsorbed polymers, 
and/or the substrate was evaluated to provide an accessible method to obtain a nanothick (about 
180 nm) polymeric film without any residual layer. XPS characterization on the platform verified 
that the bioactive polymer was only transferred and coated on the convex surfaces of the pattern. 
From observing the cell adhesion on the chitosan-modified platform, the number of adhered cells 
on the chitosan layer was found to be greater than that on the original poly(glycidyl methacrylate) 
(PGMA) layer, corresponding with the prediction that chitosan can serve as the cell-adhesive 
layer. The modified patterns were found to confine a single cell on each convex top of the 
platforms. 
Introduction 
Single-celled platforms can act as a part of cell-based biosensors, such as planar electrodes for 
patch clamping, field-effect transistor arrays, microelectrode arrays, electrochemical 
microsensors and so on [1], for which the patch clamp technique is the main tool for studying ion 
channels by measuring a voltage change of ionic current in a small patch of cell membrane [2,3]. 
Patch clamp recording provides an advantage in facilitating drug screening, and can also directly 
assay the function of the genes that encode ion channels and transport proteins. However, the 
method is not currently practical for high throughput because it requires a skilled operator to 
manually manipulate the pipette onto the cell [4]. Recent efforts to develop micromachined 
electrode arrays have been successfully fabricated on silicon wafers [5-7], however, there is still 
little literature about the platform surface modification for cell adhesion. 
In order for cell-based biosensors to be effective in their analysis, it is necessary that viable cells 
are incorporated into the platform that will house them. The deposition should be compatible with 
high-throughput processes and the cells should be kept alive. As for cell culturing environments, 
appropriate scaffolds have been developed to regenerate cells and tissues for wound healing, 
organ reconstruction, and tissue repair. No matter in a human body or in laboratory, the scaffold 
should be able to induce cells’ adhesion, locomotion, and growth. Generally speaking, the 
scaffold should: be biodegradable, have connective pores, cell-adhesive surfaces and 
cell-growing stimulants, and so forth. Furthermore, bioactive patterns occurring on micro or nano 
scale provide an alignment effect for cell spreading and a confined area for cell growth, so the 
fabrication of micro/nano patterns plays a significant role in developing the platform as a cell 
scaffold. Inspired by these concepts, a cell-adhesive and biodegradable material has been 
engineered to coat a platform surrounded by cell-repellent areas to form a micro/nano pattern, 
and then found to control cells’ adhesion and guide their growth [8-10]. The engineered tissue or 
cell patch was used to fabricate a suitable electrode platform as a scaffold to house a single cell in

Results and Discussion 
PDMS was first tested to adsorb PC molecules from OTS-treated wafer directly without O2 
plasma activation. The OTS-pretreated silicon plate assisted in PC transfer from the plate to the 
convex of the PDMS pattern, causing the water contact angle to change from 120.7o to 50.5o. In 
contrast, in another test with a freshly-cleaned silicon plate (no OTS treatment), the PC layer 
strongly adhered to the plate, and could no longer be transferred. Optical microscopic observation 
was carried out to ensure the PC transfer pattern, as Figure 1 shows. Also, the PC-adhered PDMS 
was tested to print PC molecules onto a clean silicon substrate, giving a pattern suffering from 
line shrinkage, but still clear (photograph not shown). It is interesting that the PC layer can be 
transferred below 0 oC, indicating that the frozen gel state of the PC layer very easily adhered to 
the PDMS stamp. However, only part of the pattern was transferred in the process. Complete PC 
adhesion and transfer of the full pattern was thought to enhance polymer transfer; accordingly, 
the plasma activation on PDMS surfaces was adopted in the following conditions. One minute of 
plasma irradiation resulted in efficiently hydrophilized surfaces on the PDMS stamp, assisting in 
homogeneous absorption of PC molecules.  
As for the imprinting conditions, imprinting temperature, imprinting pressure, and wetness of 
polymers were main issues affecting the quality of pattern transfer. When we tried the imprinting 
process at room temperature and under 1 atm of pressure, the wetness of chitosan was found to 
dominate the transfer effectiveness. When we decreased the pressure to 0.37 atm, the transferred 
figures on the pattern shrank and became disconnected, owing to non-conformal contact between 
the polymer layer and the wafer. After being heated up to 200 oC, the imprinted chitosan became 
soft enough to transfer. (While PVP was operated at 140 oC.) High temperature was held to 
achieve the glass transition temperature of the polymer during the imprinting process; however, 
the operating temperature was so high for those polar polymers that it was harmful to plastic and 
bioactive substrates. Accordingly, low temperature and suitable pressure in this inking process 
were considered preferable. Because of the high molecular weight property of chitosan, the 
polymer entanglement tends to form a thick film across the micropattern, leading to ineffective 
chitosan transfer from PDMS to the silicon substrate. By absorbing a sufficient amount of water 
to soften the polymer chains for conformal contact between PDMS and the substrate, chitosan 
was able to transfer at the room temperature. The similar situation can be found in PVP, alginate 
and gelatin transfer. Alternatively, chitosan adsorbed on the protruding part of the PDMS stamp 
was activated by oxygen plasma before contact with the substrate. The result showed a 
completely transferred pattern for the chitosan polymer having
