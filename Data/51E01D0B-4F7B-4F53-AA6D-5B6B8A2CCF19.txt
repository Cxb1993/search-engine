計畫英文摘要： 
In-Mold Decoration (IMD) is a newly developed technology. The pattern printed film 
(with/without thermoforming) is embedded in mold cavity followed by the injection of plastics to 
form a decorating part. Such process avoids the secondary operations such as painting/coating 
treatment also provides a surface protection for molded parts thus results in variety of cosmetic 
appearance with relative cost. Despite the advantages of IMD, the process is now limited to the 
usage of mobile phone keypad due to the complicity of the process. Systematic studies on the 
IMD form academic side have not started yet. 
Molded Interconnect Devices (MID) are injection molded thermoplastic components, 
selectively plated with conductive metals to form circuit paths and features. These parts are used 
in electronic and electromechanical packaging to combine both the mechanical functions of 
plastic or metal parts and the electrical interconnect of rigid or flex circuit boards, connectors, 
wires and various other discrete elements into a single component. It can produce 
three-dimension meshed structure for PCB board and this superior capability influences 
conventional PCB market very significant. The application of MID for 3D PCB products also 
enhances the economic benefits by minified size, processing simplification, material saving and 
recycling. The new process once matures enough for production will simplify the PCB 
manufacturing process greatly and reduce the cost significantly. Among MID, two-shot injection 
molding is considered as the most effective method for mass production. Again, the process is 
still new to industry and the relevant research is very insufficient.  
The major goal of the project is to investigate both IMD and MID in systematic manner. In 
the first year, main work will focus on the IMD process of planar optical PC and PET film 
injection molded with LCP, PC and PMMA. The process characteristics and the influence from 
associated parameters will be investigated in details. In the second year, material such as LCP, PC, 
ABS and the associated metallized plastics also will be used for MID process via rotation-mold 
and two-component injection molding under suitable interlocked design for interface. Material 
compatibility concerning LCP, PC and ABS with metallized plastics was measured 
experimentally.  Meanwhile, thermoformed film will be used in investigating IMD process. 
Process of film forming will be studied as well. In the last year, based on the combination of IMD 
and MID, both technologies will be applied to a LED display panel. The micro-featured film 
which can scatter the LED light sources will be attached to display panel via IMD process. The 
circuit required for LED will be molded using MID process. The new design not only can reduce 
fabrication time; saving production costs and increasing productivity thereby strengthen the 
sustainable global competitiveness of existing plastic industry but also provides the new 
manufacturing technology for many similar display panel products. 
 
Key words：In-Mold Decoration (IMD), Thermoforming, Film Supplyment & Positioning, 
Molded Interconnect Devices(MID), Two-Shot Molding, Metallizing, Micro-featured LED 
Display

(2) 模具溫度對金屬薄膜沖刷之影響 
在模內貼合射出成型中，塑料成品與模面中隔著一層 PC 薄膜，使得成品在
模穴中溫度的分佈不易控制。所以模溫的控制與穩定性對成品成型性、界面接合
性有關。由於模溫的改變，研究中發現鍍膜金屬的被沖刷現象隨著模溫增加而升
高，與料溫提高有相同的現象。當模溫增加時使鍍膜金屬同時受熱，因此薄膜與
鍍膜金屬間的接著性會有下降的趨勢，使得鍍膜鋁金屬被沖刷的現象有增加的趨
勢。由曲線圖圖 4.a與圖 4.b可看出趨勢，以 X軸金屬沖刷量較多。 
(3) 塑料溫度對金屬沖刷之影響 
  發現料溫的提高，其熱量影響了鍍膜鋁金屬與 PC 薄膜異質的接著效果；所
以當料溫提高而帶來的熱量超過了冷卻系統所能帶走和控制的臨界溫度時，沖刷
現象就與料溫成正比關係了。進一步探討，當料溫由最小可射出之設定溫度值漸
漸調高時，可明顯的觀察出其鍍膜鋁金屬沖刷量的變化關係。Y 軸的部分圖 5.a
與 X 軸的部分圖 5.b 皆是，當塑料溫度提升時，金屬沖刷量也增加尤其在塑料溫
度超過 280℃時更為明顯。 
(4) 射出速度對金屬沖刷之影響 
由於射出速度的改變，研究中發現鍍膜鋁金屬的被沖刷現象會隨著射出速度
的增加而升高。因射出速度增加的影響，使薄膜與熔膠間剪切率上升，鍍膜鋁金
屬被沖刷會有增加的趨勢。當射出速度由最小設定值漸漸調整加大時，可明顯的
觀察出其沖刷量的變化，由 Y軸的部分圖 6.a與 X軸的部分圖 6.b，可了解最慢速
往高速調整，沖刷量的增大隨著射出速度的提高而增加，當射出速度繼續增高時，
沖刷量的增加卻減緩下來，不再有明顯的變化。 
綜合以上的成型參數，以射出速度影響金屬沖刷值最大，其次是塑料溫度，影響
最小是模具溫度這是針對成型參數；以金屬沖刷量來說以 X軸方向沖刷值最大，Y軸
影響最小，因此可以知道當在成型薄膜電鍍金屬膜時，要將模具溫度、塑料溫度與射
出速度降到可充填於模具的條件。
