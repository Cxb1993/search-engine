!!!!This study adopted the novel magnetic roller soft mold embossing technique of the soft 
mold preparation method, embossing, combined with the existing gas-assisted UV-curable 
technique, to develop an innovative magnetic-assisted continuous roller platform. Using the 
advantages of uniform and accurate control of applied pressure of the magnetic disk, this study 
used the magnetic roller soft mold embossing and UV-curable technique for replicating 
microstructure patterns, resulting in a more mature micro-nano embossing technology and 
application. The experimental results showed that the new magnetic roller soft mold preparation 
method can accurately cast and replicate micro-nano structures of PDMS composite magnetic 
iron powder, and the magnetic disk can impose uniform pressure to effectively implement 
complete filling during the rolling process. This innovative process provides a simple and rapid 
molding mechanism, which significantly improves the microstructure pattern replication 
technique.!

Under the advancement of micro-optical-electro-mechanical (MOEMS) technology, 
photolithography has become essential to various emerging technological fields, including the 
manufacturing of micro-nano components, such as IC chips, CD storage units, micro camera lens, 
micro-electrophoretic chips, micro-mechanic mechanism structures, and micro-brakes [1-3]. The 
lightweight and miniaturization design is achieved by the continuous progress and development 
of micro-components. Although photolithography can successfully create a high-precision 
structure, it has the restrictive disadvantages of high material and equipment costs, as well as 
complicated processes, rendering it unsuitable for mass production [4-11]. Therefore, under the 
demand of accurate, efficient, and rapid-speed microstructure component replication molding 
technologies, many rapid-speed rolling mold process technologies have been proposed.   
    Roller embossing technology has features continuity and fast speed. Besides reducing the 
processing time, cntinuous roll-to-roll process technology can be applied to achieve full 
processing at a fast speed. The roll-to-roll concept has been extensively applied to various fields, 
including opto-electrics, printing, electron, packaging, etc. In the current rolling process, a rigid 
roller has minimum contact area between the mold and substrate material during rolling, thus, the
