第一章  緒論 
1.1 研究背景 
材料與人類文明之歷史關係相當密切，因為材料是容易被人類取得的物
質，並且被加工製成器物，多年來人類與材料之間之關連已經擴張到每年有數
十噸原料自大自然取出，而這些材料是各種工業建設的根本，而機械工業為一
切工業的基礎[1]。 
本實驗主要探討以氧化鋁作為增強體來強化複合材料，使複合材料得到優
異的機械性能，進而在高溫結構的使用下與許多惡劣化學環境下得到更廣泛的
應用。現今材料製備的方法有很多種方式，一般常見的製程方式為：熔鑄法、
粉末冶金法、熱壓燒結法、機械合金化技術(MA)，此外化學氣相沉積法(CVD)、
物理氣相沉積法(PVD)、電漿熔射技術等也常被拿來用在製備複合材料的領域
中，但是這些方法需要較長時間以及較高的反應溫度，使得許多方法較不符合
經濟效益。而本次實驗所使用之方法為自持傳遞高溫合成法(Self-Propogating 
High-Temperature Synthesis)，又稱為自蔓延高溫合成或簡稱 SHS。自持傳遞高
溫合成法，係利用反應物在反應過程中會釋放出大量能量的特性，來提供後續
反應所需能量，使反應物形成一連鎖反應，故只需在反應物ㄧ端施以高溫熱源
即可使反應物反應，高溫熱源的種類有如鎢絲線圈、乙炔氧焰、雷射或微波等。
SHS相較於其他合成法，除了反應物只需提供克服活化能所需之能量，能量消
3 
生成物的熔點。例如：金屬鋁(Al)與三氧化二鐵(Fe2O3)的反應可以達到超過
3000℃的溫度，這已超過生成物鐵與氧化鋁的熔點。也因為有大量的放熱能
量，所以使得鋁熱反應會產生局部燃燒，而反應過程中持續的熱量釋放進而變
成自持性反應。事實上，許多經由鋁熱反應產成的生成物主要是由大量的金屬
相與少許的氧化物組成，可藉由重量的不同將兩者區分出來，進而運用在多樣
化的金屬冶金應用上。近年來鋁熱反應已受到普遍的重視，並實際應用在合成
複合材料與耐高溫陶瓷[6]。 
1.2 文獻回顧 
1.2.1固相燃燒合成之相關文獻 
探討地球能量問題已成為目前國際間最重要的議題，也是極具潛力需要深
入研究的技術，其中將氣液態燃料的燃燒轉變為地球能量已是生活上不可或缺
的重要來源。在過去的研究發展中，氣體、液體燃燒所產生的能 量與動力，已
成功應用在各種領域上，但固體燃燒的應用，卻因為許多瓶頸無法突破不如氣
液體燃燒之發展迅速。 
西元 1967年，俄國的科學家 Borovinskaya、Skiniro 和Merzhanov[7]等三
人在研究 Ti和 B混合壓實燃燒時發現固相-固相燃燒以自持性的方式進行，開
啟了高溫自持傳遞合成反應之研究，西元 1980 年後，美國與日本的研究人員
也開始投入此相關的研究，這些研究人員都有一些共同的研究心得，如其燃燒
5 
身的優點而改善其缺點，或不具有反應元素的優缺點而含有更佳的優越特性。
介金屬化合物，如 TiAl, NiAl, Ni3Al, CoAl…等，其中以鈦鋁介金屬的應用範圍
最廣[9]，因其具有高熔點、低密度、且高溫下抗潛變能力佳、具良好熱導體
等優異特性，被視為取代目前使用之超合金的高溫結構用材料。 
由文獻中得知鉻鋁介金屬在穩態下分別有 Cr2Al、Cr5Al8、Cr4Al9、Cr Al4、
Cr2Al11、Cr Al7這六種，而鋁含量愈多其強度與硬度隨著增強，在高溫時，由
於其耐腐蝕性與抗氧化性可阻隔在鋁合金中，故此材料可應用於燃氣渦輪葉片
上。 
以 Nb為基礎的合金擁有高熔點以及優越的機械性能，所以在高溫環境下
是結構材料中具有具前瞻性的選擇。可惜的是，它們糟糕的抗氧化性是眾所皆
知的。因此，想要在高溫環境下使用 Nb的合金，便需要高抗氧化塗層。NbSi2
的熱擴散係數近似於 Nb 的合金。如果 Si 的有選擇性氧化作用在 NbSi2中發
生，那麼，它將展現良好的抗氧化性及 NbSi2塗層將提供 Nb合金氧化作用良
好的改善[10]。而 Nb5Si3擁有高熔點及低密度等特性，並有傑出的熱化學穩定
性、在室溫下具有抗破壞力，以及當燃燒溫度上升至1500 ℃時仍然維持相當
高的鍵結力存在等等[11,12]，良好的機械、物理性質以及潛在的高溫應用，使
Nb5Si3也被使用在高溫結構材料中，而在 Nb-Si的系統裡產物得不到單相矽化
物，而是由多項材料組成，包含：Nb3Si、NbSi2、α-Nb5Si3以及 β-Nb5Si3 [13]。

1.2.4 金屬碳化物之相關文獻 
二元鈮碳化物 NbC、Nb2C 等在應用上最引人注意的特點包括高熔點、高

耐火陶瓷材料，用於商業工具鑽頭如切削工具且可作為碳化物硬質合金添加
劑，也可製作紫色人造寶石。

於磨具塗層時可生成低摩擦表面。除了可耐高溫且其硬度也相當高(莫氏硬度

Formation of Cr-Al and Cr-Si Intermetallics with Al2O3 Additions 
by Combustion Synthesis 
Chun-Liang Yeh, Jia-Zhan Lin 
Department of Aerospace and Systems Engineering, Feng Chia University 
100 Wenhwa Rd., Seatwen, Taichung 40724, Taiwan 
 
 
Abstract 
Formation of chromium aluminides (Cr2Al, Cr5Al8, and Cr4Al9) 
and silicides (Cr3Si, Cr5Si3, CrSi, and CrSi2) was achieved by 
self-propagating high-temperature synthesis (SHS) involving 
the thermite reaction of Cr2O3 with Al. Compressed samples 
were prepared from the powder mixtures of Cr2O3-xAl (with x 
= 3.0–7.0) and Cr2O3-2Al-ySi (with y = 0.67–4.0). The 
combustion process takes advantage of the reaction heat 
released from aluminothermic reduction of Cr2O3 to reach 
self-sustaining and to facilitate the formation of Cr-Al and 
Cr-Si intermetallics. Due to a decrease in the reaction 
exothermicity, the flame-front velocity and combustion 
temperature decreased with increasing Al and Si contents in the 
reactant mixtures. Based upon the XRD analysis, three 
aluminides, Cr2Al, Cr5Al8, and Cr4Al9, were identified along 
with Al2O3 in the products. Aluminide phases highly rich in Al, 
like CrAl4, Cr2Al11, and CrAl7, were not produced, because 
reaction exothermicity of the sample with Al/Cr2O3 ≥ 8.0 was 
insufficient for combustion to occur. For the Cr-Si compounds, 
Cr3Si and CrSi2 were produced in a single-phase form with 
Al2O3. The other two silicides, Cr5Si3 and CrSi, were detected 
in the products containing multiple silicides.  
1  Introduction 
Combustion synthesis in the mode of self-propagating 
high-temperature synthesis (SHS) has drawn wide interest in 
the production of a variety of materials, including borides, 
carbides, nitrides, silicides, intermetallics, and composites on 
their bases [1,2]. The SHS process takes advantage of the 
self-sustaining merit from exothermic reactions between 
reactants. Formation of transition metal aluminides, like TiAl, 
NiAl, and Ni3Al, from metallic powder compacts has been 
studied by combustion [3,4]. Many metal silicides, including 
Ti5Si3, Nb5Si3, Ta5Si3, and MoSi2, were also fabricated from 
their constituent elements by the SHS route [5,6]. Of particular 
interest for this study are chromium aluminides and silicides. 
According to the phase diagrams, there are six aluminide 
phases (Cr2Al, Cr5Al8, Cr4Al9, CrAl4, Cr2Al11, and CrAl7) in the 
Cr-Al system and four silicide compounds (Cr3Si, Cr5Si3, CrSi, 
and CrSi2) in the Cr-Si system.  
Owing to excellent oxidation resistance, chromium aluminides 
are suitable for the protective coating on the low-alloy steels 
operating in chemically reactive environments at temperatures 
exceeding 500 oC [7]. On the other hand, chromium silicides, 
Cr3Si and Cr5Si3, are outstanding wear- and corrosion-resistant 
materials for mechanical moving components working under 
aggressive or high-temperature conditions [8]. Chromium 
aluminides (Cr2Al and Cr5Al8) were often produced from 
elemental powders by mechanical alloying with subsequent 
annealing at temperatures of 400-850 oC [9]. Chromium 
silicides have also been fabricated by mechanical alloying, 
during which CrSi2 is always the first phase to form in the 
sequence of producing CrSi and Cr5Si3 [10]. Besides, a laser 
melting process has been applied to prepare Cr3Si, Cr5Si3, and 
CrSi from elemental powder blends [11]. 
However, because of low formation enthalpies for chromium 
aluminides and silicides [12,13], formation of Cr-Al and Cr-Si 
intermetallics from constituent elements by SHS is not feasible 
in the absence of external heat inputs. Instead of starting from 
the elemental powders, this study aims to obtain Cr-Al and 
Cr-Si intermetallic compounds with Al2O3 additions by 
thermite-based combustion synthesis from the Cr2O3-Al and 
Cr2O3-Al-Si systems, respectively. High exothermicity of the 
thermite reaction between Cr2O3 and Al is beneficial for solid 
state combustion. Moreover, the by-product Al2O3 serves to 
improve the fracture toughness, oxidation resistance, and 
mechanical strength of the composite materials. Specifically, 
the effects of sample stoichiometry on combustion 
characteristics are studied, including sustainability of the 
combustion process, combustion temperature, and flame-front 
velocity. The correlation of the Al and Si contents in the 
reactant mixtures with the aluminide and silicide phases formed 
from combustion is explored. 
2  Experimental Procedures  
Starting materials of this study include chromium oxide Cr2O3 
(Showa Chemical Co., 99% purity), Al (Showa Chemical Co., 
< 40 µm, 99% purity), and Si (Strem Chemicals, < 45 µm, 99%) 
powders. Reaction (1) represents the thermite reaction between 
Cr2O3 and Al under an exact stoichiometry.   
3232 22 OAlCrAlOCr +→+                (1) 
which is highly exothermic with the reaction heat of –541 kJ. 
The adiabatic reaction temperature of Reaction (1) is 2381 K 
[14]. For the production of Cr-Al intermetallics by 
thermite-based combustion synthesis, more Al than that in 
Reaction (1) is required to react with the reduced Cr. In view of 
six aluminide phases (Cr2Al, Cr5Al8, Cr4Al9, CrAl4, Cr2Al11, 
and CrAl7) present in the Cr-Al system, the powder blends were 
prepared by a molar ratio of Cr2O3:Al = 1:x with x varying from 
3 to 16. On the other hand, the addition of Si into the reactant 
mixture of Reaction (1) was adopted for the synthesis of 
chromium silicides. According to the stoichiometries of Cr3Si, 
Cr5Si3, CrSi, and CrSi2, the proportion of Cr2O3, Al, and Si was 
set to be Cr2O3:Al:Si = 1:2:y with y = 0.67 (2/3), 1.2, 2, and 4. 
The reactant powders were dry mixed in a ball mill and were

It is interesting to note that no presence of chromium 
aluminides in the XRD patterns of synthesized products from 
the Cr2O3-2Al-ySi samples. The Cr2O3-2Al-ySi reaction system 
possesses a constant molar ratio of Cr2O3:Al = 1:2, which 
exactly matches the stoichiometry of the thermite reaction 
between Cr2O3 and Al, as expressed in Eq. (1). Because of its 
high exothermicity, the reaction of Cr2O3 with Al plays an 
initiation role in the combustion sequence of the Cr2O3-2Al-ySi 
sample. Therefore, there was almost no Al left to react with 
reduced Cr. Instead, the interaction between elemental Si and 
Cr took place and yielded chromium silicides. This explanation 
was further justified from the thermodynamic point of view by 
calculating the changes in the Gibbs free energy (∆G) for the 
reaction of Cr2O3 with Al and that of Cr2O3 with Si [12,13].  
3232 22 OAlCrAlOCr +→+  ∆G = –469.7 kJ (2) 
232 5.125.1 SiOCrSiOCr +→+  ∆G = –299.7 kJ (3) 
According to ∆G of the above two reactions (2) and (3), the 
reaction of Cr2O3 with Al is more favorable to occur. 
Furthermore, among all Cr-Al compounds Cr5Al8 has the 
largest negative ∆G (= –182 kJ/mol). The Cr-Si phase with the 
largest negative ∆G is Cr5Si3 (= –202 kJ/mol). Therefore, it is 
believed that in the Cr2O3-2Al-ySi system Al tends to react with 
Cr2O3 and the reduced Cr prefers to react with Si.
