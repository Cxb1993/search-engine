Abstract 
 Eutrophication is an accelerated growth of 
algae caused by excessive nutrients flowing 
into the aquatic system, especially nitrogen 
or phosphorus. Eutrophication   brings 
phytoplankton of quantity anomalistic 
increase and death. The result decreases 
biodiversity and deteriorates water quality, 
further hazard on human health. 
 Cladoceran plays a very important role in 
aquatic ecosystem.  It takes phytoplankton 
as food and makes the aquatic system clean.  
Several studies investigated this natural 
predator-prey relationship and tried to 
implicate this as a biological control for 
eutrophication in lakes. The objective of this 
study is to investigate the grazing potential 
for two cladoceran Daphnia magna and 
Moina macrocopa on common present algae 
in blooming under control conditions, 
moreover investigate different instar of 
filtering constant form M. macrocopa. 
Filtering experiment showed both species 
of cladocera (Moina macrocopa and 
Daphnia magna) can control the algae 
growth by filtering Chlorella spp.. Moreover, 
large amount cladocera can immediately 
decrease algae concentration. Factors affect 
the algae growth by cladocera filtration 
includes: (1) The algae species, algae size, 
algae concentration, and the algae 
characteristic. (2) The cladocera species and 
the cladocera instar. These factors play 
important roles for application of 
micro-invertebrate as a media to control and 
mitigate eutrophicatoin model.
