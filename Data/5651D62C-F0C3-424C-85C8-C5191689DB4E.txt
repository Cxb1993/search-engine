Abstract 
Solder alloys have been widely used as the joining material of 
electronic devices.   These solder joints might result  in the 
formation of whiskers.  The whiskers possess a composition near  
pure tin and a length over hundreds um, which could lead to the 
short circuiting and failure of electronic products.  The 
parameters for the whisker growth include: temperature 、
humidity、 compressive stress and applied current.   The growth 
mechanisms of tin whiskers have been reported by some 
researchers, which are quite inconsistent.  
        In general, the tin whiskers grow very slowly and the 
observations require much time, it  is difficult for the study of 
whisker growth mechanism. Our laboratory found for the first  time 
that  the phenomenon of amazingly rapid growth occurred on the 
surface of rare earth- elements containing solders.   The growth 
velocity at room temperature attains 0.86nm/sec, which is mech 
higher than rates reported in the literature (0.001 to 0.01nm/sec).   
The rare earth elements-containing solder alloys provide very 
convenient experimental system for the investigation of tin 
whiskers.  
 
Keywords: lead-free solder, rare-earth element, whisker

二、 計畫摘要 
本計畫第一年針對添加稀土元素 (Rare-Earth Element)之錫銀銅
(Sn-Ag-Cu)銲錫合金所產生的異常錫鬚成長現象進行研究並尋找抑制的方
法。於銲錫合金內添加稀土元素所出現的異常錫鬚成長現象經研究後指出
此乃因為稀土介金屬化合物(RE Intermetallic Compound)氧化所造成的結
果，而針對銲錫合金而言添加鍺(Ge)元素可有效的抑制其表面氧化的行
為，故本研究嘗試於含稀土之錫銀銅銲錫合金內添加不同含量的鍺元素並
觀察其錫鬚成長行為的變化。研究結果指出於Sn3Ag0.5Cu0.5Ce銲錫合金中
添加0.5wt.%以上的鍺元素可有效的抑制室溫中的錫鬚成長與高溫中的錫
堆出現，但於Sn3Ag0.5Cu0.5La銲錫合金中添加鍺元素則效果並不明顯，推
測此與於含稀土之錫銀銅銲錫合金中添加鍺元素可有效降低稀土介金屬化
合物CeSn3的氧化速率，但對LaSn3則無明顯的影響有密切的關係。 
本計畫第二年針對錫鋅鉍 (Sn-Zn-Bi)合金系統添加稀土元素
(Rare-Earth Element)後，發生的異常錫鬚成長現象進行研究並尋找抑制的
方法。於銲錫合金內添加稀土元素所出現的異常錫鬚成長現象經研究後指
出此乃因為含稀土介金屬化合物氧化所造成的結果，而針對銲錫合金而言
添加鍺(Ge)元素可有效的抑制其表面氧化的行為，故本研究嘗試於含稀土
之錫鋅鉍(Sn8Zn3Bi0.5Ce)銲錫合金內添加少量的鍺元素並觀察其錫鬚成
長行為的變化。研究結果指出於 Sn8Zn3Bi 銲錫合金中添加 0.5wt.%鈰(Ce)，
因為生成大尺寸的(Ce, Zn)Sn3介金屬化合物，於大氣環境下室溫與 150℃
儲存後，會有錫鬚成長的現象。當 Sn8Zn0.5Ce銲錫合金中之鉍(Bi)含量由
3 wt.%降至 1~2 wt.%時，可重新獲得因 Zn添加而細化(Ce, Zn)Sn3相的有利
效應，進而顯著地降低錫鬚的發生。另外，於 Sn8Zn3Bi0.5Ce 銲錫合金中
添加 0.5 wt.% Ge，可以增進(Ce, Zn)Sn3介金屬化合物的抗氧化性，故可以
有效地抑制錫鬚生成。

的氧化。由以上的結果可知於Sn8Zn0.5Ce銲錫合金中添加3 wt.% Bi時，Zn元素
對於錫鬚抑制的作用更顯得無用。

系統中，在氧化的 CeSn3 團聚顆粒表面上有大量的纖維狀錫鬚均勻地分佈。其
長錫鬚直徑約 0.1-0.3 µm，長度超過 100 µm。然而也有發現短的錫鬚，其直徑約
1 µm，長度則有數 µm。由圖 2可發現，Sn3Ag0.5Cu0.5Nd銲錫合金表面的錫鬚
形貌可區分成三種形式。形A(圖 2(a))與形B(圖 2(b)，右側)分別為由氧化的NdSn3
介金屬相表面擠出的長纖維狀錫鬚與微小的發芽。在玫瑰花瓣狀的 NdSn3 相中
間生成之單一的錫鬚則歸類為形 C(圖 2(b)，左側)與圖 2(c))。圖 3所示為圖 2相
同區域在大氣環境下室溫儲存時間增長至 969 小時後的金相表面，由圖 3可發現
隨時間增長，錫鬚形貌並未改變。值得注意的有短纖維與微小的發芽在經過長時
間儲存後，並未進一步的成長。 
Sn3Ag0.5Cu0.5Nd 銲錫合金經研磨拋光後，置於大氣下室溫(約 25℃)中，其
稀土介金屬化合物(氧化物)隨著時間增加之原子組成變化，如圖 4 所示。由圖 4
可發現氧含量隨儲存時間增長而增加，當試片儲存超過 150小時後，其氧含量達
到飽和約 55 at.%。在先前的 Sn3Ag0.5Cu0.5Ce 銲錫合金研究中，CeSn3 氧化要
達到飽和氧含量需要大約 450小時。所以 Sn3Ag0.5Cu0.5Nd銲錫合金中之 NdSn3
玫瑰花瓣狀介金屬相的氧化速率大約是 Sn3Ag0.5Cu0.5Ce 銲錫合金中之團聚狀
的 CeSn3 介金屬相的 3倍。事實上，Nd 的化學活性較 Ce 稍低。但是 NdSn3 化
合物的氧化速率卻是高於 CeSn3 化合物，造成此現象的原因為玫瑰花瓣狀的
NdSn3 化合物與 Sn3Ag0.5Cu0.5Nd 銲錫合金基地的界面較團聚狀的 CeSn3 化合
物與 Sn3Ag0.5Cu0.5Ce 銲錫合金基地的界面大。在大氣儲存期間，氧原子透過
RE-介金屬/銲錫界面擴散遠高於透過 RE-介金屬的晶格擴散，進而促進含 Nd 的
Sn3Ag0.5Cu 銲錫合金氧化反應。因 Sn3Ag0.5Cu0.5Nd 銲錫合金中的 NdSn3 的快
速氧化，導致在 NdSn3 外表面迅速生成緻密的氧化層，如此即可避免在 NdSn3
表面錫原子的擠出，除了中心位置處。 
為了確認 Sn3Ag0.5Cu0.5Nd 銲錫合金之錫鬚成長機制，對氧化的玫瑰花瓣
狀的 NdSn3 化合物進行橫截面金相觀察。以 EPMA對氧化的 NdSn3 相的各個位
置進行化學成份分析，結果如表 2 所列。圖 5 為一典型的橫截面金相，由圖 5
可發現在嚴重氧化的區域，NdSn3 變成多層的結構。在多層結構下方的區域氧化
較不嚴重(位置 6與 7)，其氧含量大約只有外層氧化層之 1/3(如表 2所示)。可發
現(Nd, Sn)O層與純 Sn層作交叉堆疊。純 Sn 層的厚度與纖維狀的錫鬚直徑相近，
而(Nd, Sn)O層則較純 Sn層稍厚。純 Sn層的生成主要是 Sn3Ag0.5Cu0.5Nd 銲錫
合金中的 NdSn3 相在儲存過程中發生氧化：2NdSn3＋O2→2NdO＋6Sn。由圖 5
中亦可發現純 Sn 層彼此間都有互相連接，如此可當作氧化過程錫鬚成長純 Sn
原子的傳輸途徑。氧化的 NdSn3 相，除了在中心位置外，最外層之(Nd, Sn)O 氧
化層亦無裂紋生成。此現象可進一步的證明為何在 Sn3Ag0.5Cu0.5Nd 銲錫合金
中只有在每一個氧化的 NdSn3 化合物中心位置生成單一的錫鬚。此外，由表 2
可發現，在嚴重氧化區域的(Nd, Sn)O層的 Sn 含量隨著深度增加而減少，而 Nd
有明顯的改變，故抑制錫鬚生成的效果並不如預期。 
第二年： 
於Sn8Zn3Bi0.5Ce銲錫合金中，(Ce, Zn)Sn3介金屬化合物沒有細化的效果，
所以會引起錫鬚的快速成長。會出現大尺寸的(Ce, Zn)Sn3介金屬化合物可能是銲
錫基地中富Bi顆粒可提供適當的成核及成長位置所致。當Sn8Zn0.5Ce銲錫中的
Bi含量降至1~2 wt.%時，富Bi顆粒消失不見，則造成(Ce, Zn)Sn3介金屬化合物細
化與錫鬚抑制作用。Sn8Zn3Bi0.5Ce銲錫合金的快速錫鬚成長可透過0.5 wt.% Ge
的添加來避免，因為Ge的添加可以改善(Ce, Zn)Sn3介金屬化合物的抗氧化性。 
第三年： 
於 本 研 究 中 發 現 Sn3Ag0.5Cu0.5Nd 銲 錫 合 金 之 錫 鬚 成 長 模 式 與
Sn3Ag0.5Cu0.5Ce銲錫合金之錫鬚有顯著的差異，造成此現象的原因為
Sn3Ag0.5Cu0.5Nd銲錫合金中的介金屬相為玫瑰花瓣狀的NdSn3化合物，而
Sn3Ag0.5Cu0.5Ce銲錫合金中的介金屬相為團聚狀的 CeSn3化合物。當
Sn3Ag0.5Cu0.5Nd銲錫合金於大氣環境下室溫或是150℃儲存，在大部分的情形
都會在氧化的NdSn3介金屬表面中心位置生成單一的纖維狀錫鬚或是微小的發
芽。於150℃儲存下，也會在氧化的NdSn3化合物上生成土堆狀的錫鬚。總結
Sn3Ag0.5Cu0.5Nd銲錫合金的錫鬚生長，可區分成四種形式：長纖維狀(long 
fibers)，短纖維狀(short fibers)，微小的發芽(tiny sprouts)與土堆狀(hillocks)。各式
的錫鬚形式主要是受到玫瑰花瓣狀的NdSn3化合物的不同的金相橫截面與暴露
面積有關。透過氧化的NdSn3介金屬的橫截面觀察，可發現NdO/Sn多層的結構。
多層的純Sn層互相連接，可當作Sn原子的傳輸途徑，進而促成含RE銲錫的錫鬚
成長。並提出連續壓縮應力模型對此NdO/Sn多層結構的錫鬚成長機制作解釋。

Oxidation Induced Tin Whisker Growth on the 
Surface of Rare Earth Containing Solders 
T. H. Chuang, C. C. Chi and C. C. Jain 
Institute of Materials Science and Engineering,  
National Taiwan University, Taipei 106, Taiwan 
 
ABSTRACT 
The rapid growth of tin whiskers on the surface of rare earth (RE) containing 
solders was firstly observed by Chuang and Yen
1
.  It has been reported that the 
mechanism for such kind of tin whisker growth is the predominate oxidation of 
RE-intermetallic clusters existing in these alloys, which leads to a compressive stress 
that extrudes the tin atoms resulted from the oxidized process out of the specimen 
surface
2
.  Further studies on the cross sections of the oxidized RE-intermetallic 
phase CeSn3 in the air-exposed Sn-6.6Ce alloy at room temperature, a “successive 
compressive stress model” has been proposed. 
 
INTRODUCTION 
     Tin whiskers on electrodeposited Sn and Sn-alloys has been found for over 50 
years
2
.  Several models for the growth mechanism of tin whiskers including 
dislocation theory
3
 and recrystallization theory
4
 have been suggested.  However, 
certain researchers have reported that tin whisker growth is related to the oxidation 
process on the Sn surface.  For the tin whisker growth on a bimetallic Cu-Sn thin 
film specimen, Tu proposed that a biaxial compressive stress existed in the Sn film 
accompanying Cu6Sn5 formation at the Cu-Sn interface, which extrudes the tin stoms 
from the outer oxide layer on the Sn surface
5
.  Sheng et al. further observed that 
(EPMA).  Certain specimens were cut with a diamond saw across the oxidized 
CeSn3 intermetallic clusters. The cross sections were also observed by SEM and 
analyzed with EPMA. 
 
RESULTS AND DISCUSSION 
     The microstructure of the as-cast Sn-6.6Ce alloy contains many cluster-shaped 
phases with an average size of 30μm, as shown in Figure 1.  EPMA analysis 
indicates that the composition (at.%) of these clusters is Ce:Sn= 24.4:75.6, which 
corresponds to the CeSn3 intermetallic phase.  Figure 2 is the larger magnification of 
the metallographic picture, which shows many white particles with a consistent size 
of about 0.8μm dispersed in the CeSn3 intermetallic clusters.  The chemical 
composition of these fine dispersions in the CeSn3 phase is near pure Sn. 
     After air exposure at room temperature for 2 days, Figure 3 shows that the outer 
surface of the CeSn3 intermetallic clusters are covered with many long threadlike 
whiskers with a chemical composition of pure Sn.  The diameter of these long fibers 
was near the size of the white particles dispersed on the surface of CeSn3 phase in 
Figure 2.  It implies that the white dispersions in Figure 2 are initial sprouts for the 
growth of long fibrous tin whiskers in Figure 3. 
     In order to clarify the mechanism for the growth of tin whiskers in this alloy, 
the specimens were cut through the air-exposed CeSn3 intermetallic clusters.  Figure 
4 indicates that the outer region of the CeSn3 phase became a multilayer structure.  
EPMA analyses show that the multiple layers are interspersed with pure Sn 
layers(white in color) and CeO oxides (black in color).  The thicknesses of these Sn 
layers are close to the diameter of fibrous tin whiskers in Figure 3, while those of the 
CeO layers are slightly larger than those of the Sn layers.  It is suggested that the 
appearance of pure Sn layers is attributed to the oxidation of CeSn3 intermetallic 
temperature
1
.  Through cross-sectioning the oxidized CeSn3 intermetallic clusters in 
a Sn-6.6 Ce alloy, a multilayer structure interspersed with CeO oxide layers and pure 
Sn layers has been observed.  The Sn layers are connected to each other, which acts 
as the transporting channel for the Sn atoms released from the oxidation of CeSn3 
phase.  The compressive stress accompanying with the oxidation reaction extrudes 
these Sn atoms through these transporting channels to form the long fiber-shaped tin 
whiskers, which can be interpreted with a “successive compressive stress model” 
proposed in this paper.
