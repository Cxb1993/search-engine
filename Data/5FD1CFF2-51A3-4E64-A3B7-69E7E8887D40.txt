In this study, we are successfully synthesized ZnO nanorods/TiO2 nanoparticles structure and 
used in the dye-sensitized solar cells (DSSCs). Hydrothermal process was developed for growing 
ZnO nanorods with the mixed solution of zinc nitrate hexahydrate and diethylenetriamine (molar 
ratio 1:1), which were 0.01M, 0.025M, 0.05M, 0.1M, 0.2M, and 0.4M, respectively. The growth 
temperature was about 85?. The size and morphology of the ZnO nanorods were observed by 
scanning electron microscopy (SEM). The crystallographic structure of ZnO nanorods was analyzed 
by an X-ray diffractometer. The ZnO rod diameters are found that increased as the concentration of 
growth solution was increased. The dye-sensitized solar cell fabricated with the ZnO nanorods 
and/or ZnO nanorods/TiO2 nanoparticles structure using the present ZnO/ tetrakis porphine and/or 
ZnO nanorods/TiO2 nanoparticles/ tetrakis porphine as a photoanode under a 100 mW halogen lamp. 
The solar cell conversion efficiency, short-circuit current density, open-circuit voltage, and 
full-factor of solar cells were to analyze. The solar cell conversion efficiency of ZnO nanorods/TiO2 
nanoparticles structure DSSC was five time than the ZnO nanorods DSSC. 
 
Keywords: ZnO nanorods/TiO2 nanoparticles structure, dye-sensitized solar cells (DSSCs), solar 
cell conversion efficiency
