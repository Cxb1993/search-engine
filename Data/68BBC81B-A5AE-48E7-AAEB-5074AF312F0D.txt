3. 文獻探討 
錫銀銅合金是電子封裝常用之無鉛焊錫材料，但因為該焊料是一新興材料，其機械性質
之量測並無一定規範，在使用不同試件、測試方式下所得之機械性質差異性很大[2]。本計畫
擬採用壓縮實驗數據建構材料本構模型。相關之實驗研究可參考 2003 至 2004 年，Viaco 等人
[3~5]利用 Sn3.9Ag0.6Cu 圓柱試件，所進行之一系列等應變率和潛變壓縮實驗。文中除量測
Sn3.9Ag0.6Cu 之壓縮機械性質，如楊氏模數(Young＇s modulus)、熱膨脹係數(Coefficient of 
Thermal Expansion, CTE)及蒲松比(Poission＇s ratio)外。Viaco 等人也觀察各實驗試件之微觀
組織，觀察發現在 75℃和更高溫度下微觀組織中細小之銀三錫(Ag3Sn)會漸往較大之銀三錫移
動、結合，使銀三錫粗大化也導致巨觀潛變行為之變化。最後 Viaco 等人利用潛變壓縮實驗，
利用雙曲正弦應力函數對各潛變實驗之穩態潛變率進行曲線擬合，建立雙曲正弦函數形式潛
變方程式。 
為描述潛變行為，早期主要透過單軸潛變實驗，在定溫，定應力狀況，觀察潛變曲線趨
勢建立半經驗模型[6]。由實驗觀察可發現潛變應變與應力和溫度升高有強烈相關性，又潛變
是與時間相關之材料行為，所以一般將潛變應變定義為應力、溫度和時間的函數，且主要是
描述穩態潛變之行為。另外，若要同時表現暫態潛變和與時間無關之塑性變形，常見之方法
乃是採用疊加的方式，將非彈性之變形分為塑性變形、暫態及穩態潛變變形相加成疊加型黏
塑本構模型。近年來仍有許多利用潛變或疊加型模型來描述焊錫高溫行為之研究，如 2002
年，Ohguchi 和 Sasaki[7]利用獨立之應變硬化型潛變模型和彈-塑性模型組合成疊加型本構模
型，用來描述錫鉛之彈-塑-潛變行為。其中之潛變模型之應力項採用多項式形式。而彈-塑性
模型採用的是 Ramberg-Osgood 彈-塑性模型。2004 年，Déplanque 等人[8]利用錫鉛作剪力實
驗發展潛變模型，其潛變模型乃是由暫態潛變方程式和穩態潛變方程式疊加而成。2005 年
Clech[9]提出一包含暫態、穩態和加速潛變之潛變模型用來描述無鉛焊錫由潛變開始至失效之
材料行為。 
上述將塑性、潛變分別以獨立構成式組合而成之模型稱之為疊加型黏塑性本構模型。之
後又有將潛變、塑性變形合而為一黏塑性變形，可捨去疊加型模型在分離各種非彈性變形之
困難性，也可表現出內部微結構演化對巨觀材料行為之影響，是較周全之材料本構模型。黏
塑性本構模型將潛變變形和塑性變形結合為單一黏塑性變形所發展之本構模型，其特色是能
同時描述與時間相關之潛變變形和與時間無關之塑性變形。欲建構黏塑性本構模型需有描述
應變率與應力關係之流動方程式，以及與材料微結構變化相關的狀態變數(state variables)進化
方程式[10]。1975 年，Bonder 和 Parton[11]定義了最早之黏塑性本構模型。此模型被用來描述
材料應變硬化之行為。Bonder 和 Parton 利用此本構模型描述鈦金屬在高溫下之材料行為得到
不錯之結果。自 1975 年後，有許多黏塑性本構模型被提出，如 1978 年，Miller 本構模型[12]、
1982 年，Robinson 本構模型[13]等。綜觀大部分黏塑性本構行為可歸納流動方程式的形式為
三型，分別為冪次函數型、指數函數型及雙曲正弦函數型。而狀態變數可概分成兩類，分別
為具方性向之狀態變數:背應力(back stress)及等向性之狀態變數:牽曳應力(drag stress)。 
在焊錫材料本構模型方面，最常用來描述焊錫材料之黏塑性本構模型為 1982 年 Anand
所提出之 Anand 模型[14,15]。其為一組能描述材料在高溫下與時間相關之變形行為的方程
式。當材料工作溫度超過熔點(絕對溫度)二分之一時，Anand 利用單一狀態變數稱之為變形阻
抗來代表高溫下材料微結構對塑性流之平均阻抗。Anand 模型之流動方程式形式為雙曲正弦
函數形式；其狀態變數進化方程式包含硬化率項和動態回復率項。Anand 模型因為僅包含一

5.3  模型參數擬合  
作為內連接用途之無鉛焊錫在實際使用狀況下常受到低頻率之疲勞應力作用。為了使求
出之模型參數適合分析焊錫實際使用狀況下之反應。求取參數所需之等溫定應變率壓縮實驗
之應變率範圍取較慢速之 4E-4 至 2E-6 速率，以求由實驗數據擬合得之新型模型參數能用於
描述無鉛焊錫作為內連接時實際之潛變行為。本文將藉林俊吉碩士論文[28]內無鉛焊錫之等
溫定應變率壓縮實驗數據進行參數擬合。 
修正 Armstrong-Frederick 塑性本構模型參數求取步驟共分為三步： 
一為求取飽和應力，飽和應力為定變率實驗中應力達到穩態時之應力值。 
二為求取模型穩態參數，決定穩態參數前需先決定飽和背應力。飽和背應力方向會與負

6.2 循環負載行為 
循環負載形式是在溫度 75°C 作 15MPa 和 3MPa 之間循環負載實驗之負載形式。其加、
卸載時採應力控制，時間皆為十秒鐘，並維持負載應力值五千秒鐘。由實驗應變與時間的關
係，如圖 9.所示，可發現當由 15MPa 降至 3MPa 時實驗之應變隨之減少，當再次加載至 15MPa
時潛變應變率再次增加。所以錫柱試件在循環負載下，其應變歷程有移動硬化行為產生。但
由 Anand 模型模擬的結果可發現，當負載降至 3MPa 時，應變幾乎維持定值，並無實驗中應
變減少之現象，且再次加載時也無再出現暫態潛變行為。Anand 模型所表現之潛變行為明顯
與實驗不合。反觀修正 Armstrong-Frederick 本構模型，因使用背應力為狀態變數，可模擬材
料在循環負載下之移動硬化行為。當負載下降至 3MPa 時，由模型所計算之潛變應變跟著下
降，與實驗趨勢符合，但其下降之應變量較實驗少。當負載再次升至 15MPa 時，所模擬之應
變曲線也有暫態潛變行為發生。由模擬結果與實驗比對可見修正 Armstrong-Frederick 本構模
型所表現之潛變行為與實驗趨勢相符。

三、 計畫成果自評 
本計劃目的是先針對錫銀銅合金進行材料實驗求得其應力與應變關係及其潛變行為，並
參考其金相變化建立一個與微結構相關之黏塑性本構模型。計劃已完成內容則包含： 
1. 收集無鉛焊錫合金相關文獻,包括微觀和巨觀之研究以了解其機械性質、高溫潛變變形機
制、高溫微結構演化及與錫鉛合金的差異性。並以此思考建立本構模型時適當之實驗條
件。 
2. 收集金屬本構模型相關文獻，包括疊加形式的本構模型及黏塑性本構模型，用以了解高
溫金屬潛變行為之力學模型進展及研究趨勢。 
3. 收集現有用於描述焊錫合金潛變行為之本構模型，尤其著重在黏塑性本構模型。並加以
探討模型核心方程式的形式，即狀態變數進化方程式，了解此方程式對應之微結構狀態，
及對巨觀變形之影響。 
4. 建立一符合無鉛焊錫非線性潛變行為之材料本構模型。並與Anand模型和實驗相比對，結
果發現新建立之本構模型確實比Anand模型更符合實驗趨勢，能更精確描述材料的潛變行
為。

therefore, it may be concluded that heatsink compressive load 
would close solder joint fatigue crack faces and slow down the 
crack growth. From the perspective of the electrical open 
failure mode, compressive loads lead to improved board-level 
reliability, and the standard board-level reliability test 
approach that doest not include the influence of heatsink 
compression can still serve as the worse-case reliability test 
for failures related to fatigue crack growth. However, when 
the heatsink compressive load level increases, the overall 
reliability of the assembly can degrade as a result of the 
emergence of electrical shorts induced by solder joint creep 
collapse and bridging.  
It is well known that the creep deformation is a strong 
function of temperature, and that the solder joint creep 
collapse under typical service condition would be more 
significant than the collapse during the lower temperature 
regime in the T/C test. Consequently, the standard T/C test 
would not be suited, and were not used for assessing solder 
joint reliability associated to bridging failure. Instead, the 
compressive reliability of BGA solder joints was investigated 
via the board-level aging of BGA solder joints under elevated 
temperature and compressive load.

Finite Element Simulation 
The creep collapse of BGA solder joints under heatsink 
compression and the related reliability was modeled by using 
finite element software ANSYS. The numerical analysis was 
first performed and was compared with the accelerated aging 
test results for validating the modeling methodology. 
Additional finite element analyses were then applied to predict 
the solder joint compressive creep reliability over extended 
time. 
Since the board-level solder joint aging test configuration 
shown in Fig. 1 is relatively complex and the analysis for 
solder joint creep collapse requires iterative solution 
procedure, a global-and-local modeling approach was used for 
analyzing the solder joint creep deformation. In this modeling 
approach, it was assumed that the non-uniform compression 
distribution in the BGA region does not change significantly 
over time. A global model that takes into account the board-
level assembly is used to determine the non-uniform 
distribution of compressive load in the package substrate, 
BGA and test board levels at time zero. A local model is then 
applied to determine the creep deformation of the solder joints 
in the high compression region over the extend time period.  
A typical global finite element model for simulating the 
aging condition is shown in Fig. 7. The model considers only 
the package-board assembly sandwiched by the middle 
compression plate and bottom backing plate. Due to the 
symmetry of the whole test assembly, the model considers 
only a quarter of the assembly. The compressive load was 
modeled such that the pressure on the middle plate is assumed 
to be uniformly distributed over the contact regions between 
the load-controlling springs and the middle plate. The out-of-
plane displacement is zero at the location where the loading 
bolt is connected to the backing plate. The material 
constitutive models for the test assembly were assumed to be 
linear elastic and temperature dependent, except the solder 
joint, which was considered to be viscoplastic.

Conclusions 
In this study it is shown that the heatsink compressive 
loads applied on BGA packages has strong influence on 
system-level solder joint reliability. Under temperature 
cycling conditions, electrical open failures induced by solder 
joint fatigue cracking is retarded. However, depending on the 
level of compression, electrical short failure associated with 
solder joint collapse is likely to become the dominant failure 
mode under service conditions. A numerical approach based 
on global-and-local finite element analysis is proposed for 
predicting solder joint creep collapse and the associated BGA 
joint bridging reliability. The analysis procedure involves 
determining the application-specific pressure distribution in 
the package substrate, and using the local finite element model 
or a simplified power-law model to obtain the time to bridging 
failure for solder joints that are under the pressure 
concentration region. A reverse analysis may also be applied 
to determine the allowable heatsink compression load if the 
service life for the BGA package is specified.
