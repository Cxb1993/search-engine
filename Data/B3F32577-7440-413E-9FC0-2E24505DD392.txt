添加劑對無鉛厚膜電阻電性影響之研究 
許志雄 國立聯合大學材料科學工程系 
 (NSC95-2221–E -239-013) 
 
 
本研究以氧化釕（RuO2）與硼矽酸鈣玻璃依不同體積分率調製無鉛厚膜電阻油墨。電阻油墨以
網版印刷法印製於氧化鋁基板製作表面後燒式電阻，並使用晶片電阻製程製作 0805 （0。08 英
吋 × 0。05 英吋)晶片電阻。電阻油墨中，玻璃添加 6 種金屬氧化物作為電阻溫度係數（TCR）
調整劑，分別為 Nb2O5、MnO2、MoO3、Fe2O3、TiO2和 CuO。不同種類的 TCR 調整劑和添加量
會造成玻璃物理性質改變，電阻層中因玻璃分相而形成相異顯微結構。本實驗無鉛晶片電阻經過
環境測試後，所有電阻皆通過短時間超負載（STOL）測試以及超過 85%的電阻通過負載壽命測
試。無鉛電阻油墨藉由不同種類與添加量的 TCR 調整劑，可製作片電阻率為 45~10016 Ω/□，
TCR 值趨近於零的厚膜電阻。 
關鍵字：無鉛厚膜電阻、電性、玻璃 
 
(1) 前言 
後燒式電阻廣泛地應用於混成微電子與晶片電阻領域
裡[1]，電阻所使用的油墨通常以 RuO2 或 Ru 基複合物作為
導體材料[2-4]。除了導體粉末外，電阻油墨還包含了有機黏
結劑以及非傳導性的玻璃作為無機黏結劑。有機黏結劑的添
加是為了使電阻油墨能網印化。電阻燒結過程中，有機黏結
劑會燒除，玻璃則會軟化而黏結電阻層與基板層。厚膜電阻
的顯微結構顯示電阻層中，導體粒子圍繞著玻璃顆粒形成環
狀排列，導體粒子之間又被薄玻璃層隔開，多重的環與顆粒
間接觸而形成鏈狀傳導網絡[5]。傳導網絡中，電子以穿隧
方式穿越玻璃層，一般認為電阻值高低是受到薄玻璃層所影
響[6]。商用電阻油墨中廣泛地應用硼矽酸鉛玻璃作為無機
黏結劑，因為它的熱膨脹係數接近氧化鋁基板以及有良好的
耐久性等優點。然而，鉛是一種有毒材料，在 700~900℃之
間會有鉛蒸氣產生。近年來，有害物質危害禁令已正式實
施，基於環保問題，電子產品往無鉛化發展，因此電阻油墨
中硼矽酸鉛玻璃和高阻值電阻作為導體粒子的 Pb2Ru2O6.5
需尋找新材料替代以達到除鉛目的。RuO2 基無鉛厚膜電阻
已有多篇文獻發表[7-11]，並已被申請專利[12-13]。Fukaya
等人[14-16]製作無鉛電阻油墨系統，並應用於 LTCC 裝置
中。極少數的文獻針對 TCR 調整劑對於無鉛電阻電性的影
響做研究。本研究以硼矽酸鈣玻璃製作 RuO2 基無鉛厚膜電
阻油墨，並對於不同的 TCR 調整劑以及燒結製程參數對後
燒式電阻電性與顯微結構的影響做探討。 
 
(2) 實驗方法 
厚膜電阻油墨包含 RuO2、硼矽酸鈣玻璃與有機黏結
劑。玻璃是由試藥級 SiO2、B2O3、CaO、K2CO3、Na2CO3、
MgO 和 Al2O3 粉末混合成為主成份，再添加不同含量的
Nb2O5、MnO2、MoO3、Fe2O3、TiO2 和 CuO 粉末作為電阻
的 TCR 調整劑。混合好的粉末置入白金坩堝於 1500℃持溫
2 小時熔融，再將玻璃淬火於去離子水中得到玻璃塊。玻璃
塊經研磨後，將玻璃平均粒徑控制於 1.5~2.5 μm 之間，過
325 目篩網濕篩後烘乾，即得到自製電阻油墨之玻璃細粉。 
電阻油墨中，玻璃與 RuO2 依體積比 10/90 和 15/85 配製，
與有機黏結劑混合後置入自轉公轉混合攪拌機(THINKY 
AR-100，日本)初步混合，再利用三滾筒(EXAKT 50，德國)
混合電阻油墨。銀鈀導電油墨(Dupont 6277，美國)以網版印
刷法印製於 96%氧化鋁基板作為端電極。 
    本研究使用 2 種電阻線路圖案，測試圖案與晶片尺寸圖
案。測試圖案為 10mm 長，1mm 寬；晶片電阻則為 0.95mm
長，0.7mm 寬。在 180℃乾燥後，表面後燒式電阻以 880℃，
持溫 30 分鐘燒結;晶片電阻則在 850℃持溫 10 分鐘燒結。晶
片電阻上印製 2 層保護玻璃層，分別於 590 以及 580C 燒結。 
 燒成後的電阻以 2 點法於 25℃、125℃和-55℃量測電阻值
而求得 HTCR 及 CTCR。電阻層顯微結構觀察之試片經由切
割，研磨，拋光，化學腐蝕等步驟處理後，以掃描式電子顯
微鏡(SEM， JEOL JSM5600，日本)觀察。電阻層結晶相由
X 光繞射儀(XRD， Rigaku D/MAX-2200，日本)分析。晶片
電阻環境測試遵循相關規範，包括短時間超負載(STOL， 
JIS-C-5202， 5.5)與負載壽命(JIS-C-5202， 7.10)。測試功
率設定為 0805 晶片電阻額定功率 0.125W。 
 
(3) 結果與討論 
未添加 TCR 調整劑的無鉛玻璃(G)密度為 2.4 g/cm3，當
不同的 TCR 調整劑添加入 G 玻璃後，玻璃的密度為
2.38~2.43 g/cm3。 
圖一為無鉛玻璃的熱機械分析結果。不同的 TCR 調整
劑造成玻璃熱膨脹係數，玻璃化轉變溫度(Tg)和玻璃軟化溫
度(Td)改變。玻璃熱膨脹係數改變對於電阻值不會有很大影
響[17]。TMA 圖上彎曲的點為 Tg或 Td，如圖一所示。添加
0.5wt% Nb2O5 的玻璃發現有 2 個斜率轉變於 550℃及 660
℃，此玻璃具有 2 個 Tg。雙 Tg玻璃也可在未添加 Al2O3 的
GnAl 玻璃上發現。本研究中，G1Mo，G0.5Fe，G0.5Cu，G1Nb2Ti
等玻璃皆具有雙 Tg。這些玻璃中包含二個不同成份的玻璃
相，玻璃相對於熱膨脹的影響是獨立的，因此能發現 2 個 
表二為添加 1 wt% Nb2O5 與其他 TCR 調整劑複合
添加的電阻電性。RuO2 與玻璃體積比為 15/85 時，
添加 1 wt% 的 MnO2、MoO3、Fe2O3 或 TiO2 至 G1Nb
玻璃使 TCR 值往負方向移動，表面電阻與晶片電
阻皆有相同趨勢。在表面電阻中，G1Nb 添加 1 wt% 
MnO2 或 TiO2，其阻值高於未添加 MnO2 或 TiO2 的
電阻。MnO2、MoO3、Fe2O3 或 TiO2 添加至 G1Nb
玻璃對於電阻電性的影響與 MnO2、MoO3、Fe2O3
或 TiO2 添加至 G 玻璃相同。當這些添加劑應用於
晶片電阻製程中，晶片電阻的電阻值會增加，TCR
值則下降。當 1wt% CuO 添加至 G1Nb 玻璃，晶片電
阻的電阻值降低，TCR 值則增加。 
圖三為 G0.5Nb 15/85 晶片電阻截面的顯微結構，分
散於玻璃相(灰色背景)的亮點為 RuO2團聚體，其在
玻璃母相上互相連結形成傳導路徑，本研究玻璃粉
末粒徑大小為     1.5-2.5 μm。電阻層中，RuO2
團聚體圍繞著玻璃顆粒形成鏈狀結構。圖 4 (a)中，
受到稀釋後的氫氟酸腐蝕液蝕刻後，GnAl 晶片電阻
層中玻璃的顯微結構顯現 spinodal 結構。在三維空
間中，因 2 種玻璃相濃度相當，故每相皆存在著連
續路徑形成此結構。圖 4 (b)中，G1Nb 晶片電阻受到
蝕刻後，發現有球狀孔洞。此洞可能存在另一玻璃
相，而非燒結氣孔。因未受腐蝕的試片並沒有發現
任何孔洞。由此證實這類玻璃是成核與成長相分離
型態。

(4) 結論 
本研究中探討 TCR 調整劑，如：Nb2O5、MnO2、
MoO3、Fe2O3、TiO2 和 CuO 對無鉛厚膜電阻電性
的影響。添加 Nb2O5、MnO2、MoO3、Fe2O3 和 TiO2
會使電阻 TCR 值往負方向移動。CuO 則是六種添
加劑中唯一會使電阻 TCR 值往正方向移動的 TCR
調整劑。添加 Nb2O5 和 TiO2 會增加電阻值；當
MnO2、MoO3 和 Fe2O3 添加入玻璃時，電阻值並沒
有受到很大的影響；CuO 的添加會造成電阻值下
降。電阻層顯微結構中發現不同的玻璃分相行為，
玻璃分相沒有影響導電相中鏈狀結構的排列。因
此，本研究並沒有發現玻璃分相會對於電阻電性造
成影響。不同種類與不同含量的 TCR 調整劑可控
制厚膜電阻 TCR 值至趨近於零。所有的無鉛晶片
電阻經過環境測試後，皆通過短時間超負載
（STOL）測試以及超過 85%的電阻通過負載壽命
測試。

Abstract 
The diffusion of the silver inner electrode that occurred 
during sintering of commercial low temperature 
co-fired glass ceramic substrate made the dielectric 
surface become light yellow, when the silver ion 
content of the dielectric was higher than 2wt%.  
However, the samples with silicon oxide (SiO2) powder 
added maintained their white color.  Silicon oxide 
powder was used to modified the sintering behavior 
and silver ion diffusivity of LTCC ceramics.  The 
alumina particles and pores in the LTCC substrates 
could be regarded as the diffusion barrier of silver ions.  
The activation energy for silver ions diffusion in the 
LTCC substrates was 101 kJ/mol, when 5wt% SiO2 
powder was added into the substrate the diffusion 
activation energy of silver ions became 145 kJ/mol.  
At a sintering temperature of 1180K the diffusion 
coefficient of silver ions in the LTCC ceramic 
substrates with and without additional SiO2 were 8.88×
10-13 cm2/s and 1.08×10-12 cm2/s, respectively. 
Keywords: Glass-ceramics, LTCC, Diffusion, Silver 
electrode 
 
1. Introduction 
In recent years, low temperature co-fired ceramic 
(LTCC) substrates integrated with passive devices have 
been used extensively for high-density packaging 
module[1-3] and high performance wireless 
components[4]. High conductivity metals such as Cu 
and Ag have been widely used as interconnection 
materials in LTCC components and modules. Since it 
can be sintered without atmosphere control, Ag metal 
and its alloys have been considered as highly effective 
electroding materials in LTCC manufacturing. 
Adhesion and shrinkage match between the electrode 
and substrate, cambering behavior of the sintered 
modules, and the diffusion of silver ions during 
sintering are three major issues in the metallization of 
LTCC components and modules. The camber behavior 
of electroded multilayer structures has been intensively 
studied by Jean et al.[5,6] Diffusion of Ag ions in the 
high glass content LTCC substrates is considered to 
influence the reliability and performance of the LTCC 
modules with regards to increasing leakage current and 
decreasing insulation resistance[7] Although migration 
of silver ions in various electronic components and 
hybrid electronics has been investigated[8,9], the 
diffusion of silver ions in high glass content LTCC 
substrates has seldom been reported. However, the 
reaction kinetic and mechanism between silver 
electrodes and ceramic-filled glass substrates[10], as 
well as silver diffusion and microstructure development 
in LTCC systems have been previously 
investigated[11]. 
 
In this study, the diffusion kinetic of silver ions in a 
commercial LTCC substrate material was examined. 
Silica powder was used as an additive for the LTCC 
substrate in order to change the diffusivity of Ag ions 
in the substrates. 
 
2. Experimental procedure 
 
LTCC substrates were prepared using a commercial 
powder of ceramic filled glass (L8M, Ferro, San 
Narcos, CA, USA) and the same powder was combined 
with 5 wt% amorphous silica (SiO2, Nippon Chemical, 
Japan). The powders were mixed with an organic 
binder (B-73305, Ferro, San Narcos, CA, USA) at 
50:50 ratio and were ball-milled using high-purity 
alumina balls before being tape-cast to the desired 
thickness. Silver conducting paste (I-8985, EPI 
Technology Industries Inc., Taipei, TAIWAN) was 
printed on the green tape with area of 2mm x 1mm and 
dried at 130oC for 30 min. The printed green tapes were 
laminated at 60oC under 20MPa pressure for 3 min in 
an isostatic pressing chamber. The laminated sample 
was, then, diced as chips with a size of 2.24 mm x 
1.36mm x 1.1mm. The diced samples were heated to 
450oC with a 10oC/h heating rate for binder burnout 
before co-firing was conducted at temperatures 
between 1050 and 1180K for 15 to 120 min at a 
heating rate of 4oC/min. The samples were cut 
perpendicular to the substrate surface along the 
longitude of the conducting layer. Cross-section 
samples were then polished using diamond films to 
1μm surface roughness before etching with diluted 3 
vol% HF + HCl solution. Analysis of microstructure 
was performed with scanning electron microscopy 
(SEM, JEOL 5600, Tokyo, Japan). Interdiffusion 
between the resistors and LTCC substrate was 
examined using energy dispersive X-ray spectroscopy 
(EDS, 6587, Oxford, England) equipped with SEM.

In a previous investigation, LTCC material with

was about 100K difference between first and 
second shrinkage temperatures. However, there 
was only one stage shrinkage behavior of the 
LTCC material without SiO2 addition[13]. The

the inner electrodes in the chips with SiO2 added

SiO2, the x and y direction shrinkages of former 
were smaller than the latter. Due to the 
constraining effects, the SiO2 added chips 
continuously shrunk in the z direction after first

that of the L8M LTCC chips’. Therefore, the 
chips without SiO2 had higher porosity than the 
chips with, as shown in Table 1. When the chips 
were sintered at a temperature between 1050 and 
1150K, the chips with or without SiO2 reached 
an equilibrium value, and the shrinkage rate only 
increased slightly with increasing the sintering 
temperature or time in this temperature range. 
The chips with SiO2 reached second stage 
shrinkage when they were sintered at 
temperatures higher than 1050K. The glass 
phase in the chips became soft and the chips’ 
sizes expanded when they were sintered at 
temperatures higher than 1150K. After sintering 
at a temperature of 1180K, the chips with or 
without SiO2 had lower shrinkage than that 
sintered at 1150K.

4. Conclusions 
 
In this study, we demonstrate an easy method to 
decrease the diffusivity of silver ions in LTCC 
dielectric materials by adding 5 wt% SiO2 to the 
dielectric raw materials. This process effectively 
improved the coloring of LTCC chips by 
decreasing the silver ion content in the glassy 
phase of the dielectric. When the silver ions 
content was lower than 1.59 wt%, Ag-O bonds 
were not detected in the dielectric LTCC 
material and it maintained its white color. When 
the silver ion content was higher than 2.76 wt%, 
Ag-O bonds were characterized from the 
dielectric LTCC material, and their formation 
made the dielectric LTCC layer become light 
brown. Alumina grains in the LTCC dielectric 
layer behaved as diffusion barriers of silver ions 
in the dielectric layer, and these reduced the 
diffusivity and increased the diffusion activation 
energy of silver ions in the dielectric layer.
