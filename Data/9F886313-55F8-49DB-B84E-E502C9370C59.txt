陰極之研究

一、 中文摘要 
本研究以燃燒合成法(GNP)成功
製備 La2NiO4+δ及 LaNiO3之陰極粉末， 
La2NiO4+δ 及 LaNiO3 透過燃燒法需再
經由 1000°C 及 800°C 煆燒 2 h 及 8 h
得以形成。本實驗以 La2NiO4+δ為陰極
材料，在其中添加不同莫耳比之
LaNiO3 在經過 1200°C 6 h 燒結後將探
討其物性及化學穩定性。結果顯示於
La2NiO4+δ 中添加 LaNiO3 可大幅提升
其導電性質，以及發現燃燒法粉體具
有降低燒結溫度特性，日後期望應用
於中溫型固態氧化物燃料電池陰極。 
 
關鍵字：固態氧化物燃料電池、甘胺
酸 硝 酸 鹽 燃 燒 法 、 複 合 陰 極 、
La2NiO4+δ、LaNiO3 
 
Abstract 
In this study, La2NiO4+δ and 
LaNiO3 powder were prepared by 
Glycine-Nitrite process (GNP) method. 
La2NiO4 can be directly synthesized 
when GN=1.5, however LaNiO3 have to 
be calcined at 800°C for 8 h. 
In this experiment, we regard 
La2NiO4 as cathode. Adding various 
ratio of LaNiO3 and calcined at 1200°C 
for 6 h. The experimental results 
demonstrated that adding LaNiO3 into 
La2NiO4 increased conductivity and 
decreased the sintering temperature. 
La2NiO4+δ-LaNiO3 composite cathode 
meets for application in intermediate 
temperature of SOFC. 
 
Keywords：Solid oxide fuel cell, Glycine 
nitrate process, Composite cathode, 
La2NiO4+δ, LaNiO3 
 
二、 緣由與目的 
固態氧化物燃料電池(SOFCs)運
作方式是將燃料透過電化學反應，使
燃料的化學能轉換成電能和熱能[1]，
它就像小型的發電機它的運作溫度通
常在 600~1000 °C。在如此高的工作溫
度下，發電效率隨著電化學反應提高
相對也提升。但是高溫度對於 SOFC
不僅影響材料的選用，且造成電池陰
極與連接板之間熱膨脹應力過大及增
加商業成本[2]。然而降低工作溫度會
使其效能下降，這主要取決於陰極材
料的關係，因此發展高效能的陰極材
料應用在中溫型固態氧化物燃料電池
(IT-SOFCs)中顯得非常重要[3]。 
優良的陰極材料必須擁有高的電
子及離子傳導性[4]、良好的電催化活
性以及與電解質穩定之熱膨脹特性。

Preparation of delafossite CuFeO2 coral-like powder using a 
self-combustion glycine nitrate process  
Te-Wei Chiu* and Ping-Shuo Huang  
Department of Materials and Mineral Resources Engineering, National Taipei University of  
Technology, 1, Sec. 3, Zhongxiao E. Rd., Taipei, 106 Taiwan 
 
__________________________________________________________________________________________________________ 
Abstract 
This study employed a glycine nitrate process to prepare porous, stoichiometric, homogeneous CuFeO2 powders without 
post-annealing treatment or ambient control. In this method, a precursor solution was prepared by mixing glycine with an aqueous 
solution of blended (Cu–Fe) metal–nitrates in their stoichiometric ratios. The glycine-mixed precursor solution was first heated in a 
beaker to evaporate excess water and form a viscous, bluish, and semi-transparent gel. The beaker was then covered with a metallic 
mesh, and the temperature increased slowly to 250 °C to auto-ignite the material. The combustion was self-sustaining and extremely 
rapid, producing gray powders. The powders showed a large surface area of 11.38 m2/g, as determined according to BET surface area 
measurements. SEM observations of these powders confirmed their coral-like traits and porous structures.
