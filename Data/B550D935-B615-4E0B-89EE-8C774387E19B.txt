Abstract  
This project has successfully demonstrated a world-first GaN based monolithic transceiver 
module at the wavelength of 510nm for the plastic fiber communication.  The success of these 
modules and devices also greatly improves the technology of active devices for the applications 
of plastic fiber communication and free-space wireless linking by use of UV light.  In the 
second year of this project, we also demonstrated high-speed green-blue LED, which has an 
around 120MHz bandwidth and its coupled optical power into POF fiber is high enough for data 
transmission.  The success of this module and device also improves the technology of POF fiber 
communication greatly. Finally, in the third year, we use a combination of n-type doping and 
un-doped MQW, and a 76?m in diameter current-confined aperture structure we can obtain an 
extremely high 3-dB bandwidth(~330MHz), which is limited by the spontaneous recombination 
lifetime of the MQWs. We also use cascade method to minimize the current crowding problem 
and degradation of RC-limited bandwidth in large active area LED.      
           
 
Keywords: Photodetectors, high-speed photodetectors, high-power photodetectors, light-emitting 
diode (LED), cascade light-emitting-diode, GaN
