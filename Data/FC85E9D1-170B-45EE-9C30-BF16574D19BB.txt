Development in discovering new energies has been the major topic for all the countries in
the past few decades to minimize the impact from oil-based fuel crisis. It is generally admitted
that solar energy is the most prospective new energy. And, the development of solar cells has
been directed to the cost reduction of materials and the enhancement of power efficiency. This
leads the development of solar cells moving to the era of using multi-junctions and
nanostructures. In the development of nanostructures for solar cell applications, carbon nanotube
(CNT) also plays an important role as for other optoelectronics since its discovery in 1991. The
mixture of CNT and organics forms a compound material and has been intensively employed for
enhancing the short circuit current of solar devices. Recent researches reported that MWNTs
could generate larger amount of photocurrent in the region between visible light and near UV
compared with SWNTs. Besides, the use of MWNTs is more cost-effective due to the ease of
fabrication and purification processes. Hence, the development of solar cells involving the
MWNTs is a topic of interest.
The CNT films were synthesized using ECR-CVD from the mixture of methane and
nitrogen gases. The surface morphologies of CNTs films were observed using an ultrahigh
resolution scanning electron microscope. The photoconductivity measurements were performed
under white light illumination. The results showed the ECR-CVD grown MWNTs revealed
vertically aligned characters. The current-voltage characteristics had an Ohmic behavior in both
dark and white light illumination conditions. A pretty high photosensitivity of 0.19 was obtained.
It can be explained by the fact that the charge transport is more effective along the axis of
concentric cylindrical graphitic tubes characterizing the vertically aligned MWNTs.
Keywords: Multiwalled carbon nanotube, Photoconductivity
