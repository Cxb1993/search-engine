Keywords: solid oxide fuel cell, fuel-cell stack, 
flow distribution, electrochemical analyses 
    In fuel cell stack design, one of the 
engineering problems encountered is how to 
configure gas-flow manifolds, numbers of fuel 
cells and the overall pattern of gas flow to get 
uniform flow distribution and stable cell 
operation. Therefore, geometrical structure 
and the manifolds size are the important 
parameters in design. When design manifolds 
a planar type fuel-cell stack, we need to 
choose internal manifolds or external 
manifolds at first. External manifolds are 
simpler and less costly than internal manifolds 
but need higher sealing technology and only 
applied for cross type flow. In this project, 
internal manifolds are adapted. In the past 
researches, flow distribution in specific stack 
and electrochemical analyses in a fuel cell are 
study respectively, and seldom integrated. In 
this project, both effects are united to study. 
    The theoretical analyses method was 
proposed for planar solid oxide fuel-cell stack 
to study pressure variation in manifolds and 
flow distribution in all cells in this research. 
Gas flow in the individual cell and in the 
manifolds will be simulated with Darcy’s law 
and macroscopic mechanical energy balance 
including wall friction and geometrical effect, 
respectively. The permeability in the cell is 
obtained by using commercial software and it 
was provided for the future theoretical 
analyses. Then, the electrochemical model and 
heat model are used to proceed operating 
performance analyses with flow distribution in 
stack obtained previously, including output 
voltage, output power and temperature 
distribution. The results could be provided for 
the design of layers of stack and manifold 
measure. In addition, the variation of flow 
distribution on outlet with rising temperature 
due to exothermic reactions could be obtained 
by outlet average temperature.
