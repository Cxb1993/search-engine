By appropriated designing the recording architecture, we can combine the lens and mirror function in single 
holographic element. These two recording waves are incident with 90 degree (so-called 90 degree hologram) to 
record a volume hologram in a piecewise fashion in the medium (shown as the green region in Figure 1(a)). The 
recording medium is shifted and the recording is repeated until all the pieces of strings are recorded. After recording 
process, a plane wave (which represents the sunlight) as the reading wave is incident into this hologram with 
different incident angle in Figure 1(b). The diffracted wave will always come out from the edge of the recording 
medium and it will be a plane wave even the reading beam incident at different angle. It means that this hologram 
has the ability for large angle solar energy concentrator. The collection angle of solar energy is depend on the 
converge angle of the cylindrical wave during the recording process.  
Figure 2(a) show the optical experimental setup for recording volume hologram. The recording material is PQ 
doped PMMA photopolymer with 10mm x 10 mm x 2mm [5]. The focus length of cylindrical lens is 100mm and 
the width of incident beam is 3cm. Thus, the converged angle of the cylindrical wave for recording hologram is 
about 8 degree. After recording process, we use a plane wave with different incident angle to reconstruct the 
hologram and measure the diffraction efficiency. The experimental results for angle deviation of reading wave is 
shown in figure 2(b). The curve with green color shows the angular deviation for the hologram which record with a 
plane and cylindrical waves. The collected angle can be increase to 6 degree as our expect. Here, the curve with blue 
color is the diffraction efficiency for the hologram recorded with two plane waves, and the angular deviation is 0.01 
degree as previous description. The experimental results show that the collected angle can be significant increased 
from 0.01 degree to 6 degree by using our recording architecture. In addition, the collection angle can be increased 
with increasing the numerical aperture of the cylindrical lens. For example, we can use the lens with N.A. = 0.5 to 
reach 90 degree collected angle. In addition, we also can use double or triple expose to record the hologram for 
approaching the nearly all-angle collection.  
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  
 
3.  Conclusion 
In this paper, we propose a simple technique to realize a compact solar energy concentrator with large-angle 
collection using volume hologram. This volume holographic element has the ability of lens and mirror functions in 
one single device, and it can easy to integrate with solar cell. The optical experimental results show that the 
collected angle can be significantly increased by our design and is depend on the converged angle of cylindrical 
wave during the recording process. In addition, the collection area of the volume hologram is 5 time of the area of 
the solar cell. It means that our concentrator with volume hologram can have up to a fivefold concentration of 
energy per unit area of photovoltaic material. Hence, our holographic approach can offer an exceedingly high solar-
concentration, a universal solution that can be easily integrated to any solar cells.
