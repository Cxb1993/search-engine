二、緒論 
Anatase TiO2 (a-TiO2) is a wide band-gap semiconductor that attracts much interest in the 
application of transparent conducting oxide, chemical sensor, as well as high-dielectric and 
high-refractivity optical materials. Especially, the anatase titania is more focus on being a 
photocatalyst. Extensive studies have been made on growing thin-film anatase crystals and on 
evaluating their photocatalystic activities. The general crystal forms of TiO2 are rutile, anatase and 
brookite. Rutile is a stable phase crystallized at high temperature. Rutile is also the most-studied 
and best-understood TiO2 phase with an energy gap of ~3.03 eV. Although the anatase TiO2 is a 
very useful phtocatalyst for industry and human use the experimental understanding on the optical 
and electronic property of a-TiO2 is still rather incomplete. In this letter, the optical parameters of 
indirect gap and direct interband transitions of three a-TiO2 films with different deposition angles 
of 0°, 53°, and 86° tilted to the substrate normal of Si (001) have been characterized by TR. Optical 
anisotropy and electronic structure for the anatase TiO2 with different axial orientations are 
discussed. 
 
 
三、實驗 
Three a-TiO2 samples were grown by ion-assisted electron beam evaporation with different 
deposition flux angles of 0°, 53°, and 86° tilted to Si (001) substrate normal. The evaporation 
source is a 99.95% rutile TiO2 crystal. X-ray diffraction and Raman spectroscopy confirmed the 
anatase phase of the as-deposited films. TR experiments were carried out by an indirect heating 
manner with a gold-evaporated quartz plate as the heating element. A closed-cycle cryogenic 
refrigerator with a thermometer controller facilitated the temperature-dependent measurements. 
 
四、結果與討論 
As shown in Fig. 1 are the side views of scanning-electron-microscope (SEM) image of the 
three as-deposited a-TiO2 films with different tilted angles of (a) 0° (sample S1), (b) 53° (sample 
S2), and (c) 86° (sample S3), respectively. The samples of S1 and S2 seem to have a planar 
continuous anatase {001} surface grown on Si, whereas the sample S3 presents many of the tilted 
anatase nanowires on Si (001), where the side planes of {010} and {100} for the tetragonal anatase 
are manifest. The growth mechanism of the a-TiO2 films approximately follows the rule described 
between the flux angle of deposition source (α) and the column angle of deposites (β) expressed as 
β=α-sin-1[(1-cosα)/2]. The higher flux angle will render the higher column angle for the deposites 
to form the high porosity nanowire structures in the films.

Temperature-dependent TR spectra of a-TiO2 of (a) S1, (b) S2, and (c) S3 are displayed in Fig. 
3. The transitions of Egind, Ak||c, and Bk||c demonstrate an energy red-shift behavior with increasing 
the temperatures from 30 to 300 K. The values of indirect (Egind) and direct (Ak||c) gaps of a-TiO2 
obtained from Fig. 3(a) are 3.325±0.020 and 3.811±0.015 eV at 300 K. As shown in Fig. 3(c), the 
features of E1ex and E2ex present energy red-shift behavior with the increase of temperatures from 
30 to 180 K, whereas they are ionized and undetectable when the temperature reaches near 300 K. 
It indicates that the E1ex and E2ex are excitonic transitions with a binding energy lower than that of 
room temperature (~26 meV). Besides, considerable difference in the line shape and spectral 
period of the interference fringes is observed in Fig. 3(c) for S3. It is due to not only smaller 
thickness but also the contribution of Fabry-Perot resonant interference coming from the tilted side 
planes of {010} and {100} in S3. The refractive indices probed from the top (S1 and S2) and side 
planes (S3) of a-TiO2 are different, which vary the spectral line shape of the interference fringes. 
Temperature dependences of transition energies of Egind, Ak||c, and Bk||c obtained from the TR 
spectra of Fig. 3(a) are depicted in Fig. 4 with representative error bars. The solid lines are the 
least-square fits to a Bose-Einstein empirical expression of E(T)=E0-S⋅< Ωh >⋅[coth(< Ωh >/kT)-1], 
where E0 is the transition energy at 0 K, S is a dimensionless coupling constant related to the 
strength of electron-phonon interaction, and <hΩ> is an average phonon energy. The obtained 
fitting parameters are E0=3.403±0.002 eV, S=2.6±0.4, and < Ωh >=25±6 meV for Egind, 
E0=3.852±0.002 eV, S=1.7±0.4, and < Ωh >=26±8 meV for Ak||c, and E0=4.253±0.002 eV, 
S=1.7±0.4, and < Ωh >=26±8 meV for Bk||c, respectively. The value of S for the indirect gap Egind is 
larger than that for the direct transitions of Ak||c and Bk||c due to the faster temperature-energy shift 
of the indirect transition. It is similar to that observed in the layered ReX2 (X=S, Se), where they 
simultaneously present the indirect and direct band-edge transitions.

包含有熱調制光譜 TR(發表於 2004 年 RSI)，PR-PL 之共用量測技術(發表於 2005 年 OpEx)，

一、參加會議經過 
本次參加的國際會議是具有悠久歷史 TMS協會所舉辦的第五十一屆 2009年電子材
料會議(EMC 2009)，會議的地點在美國的賓州州立大學，由於國內的航空公司並沒有直
接飛到賓州，我全程所搭乘的是西北航空的班機經二次轉機後才抵達賓州州立大學，首
先在 6/22先北上在台北住一晚然後隔天搭乘 09:35的西北 NW22班機於 13:55抵達東京
成田機場再於 15:05轉機搭乘 NW26班機到達底特律，等待 7個小時後轉機 1小時到達
State College Airport 即賓州州立大學城的飛機場，到達居住處所已是美國時間 23 日晚
11:55。EMC大會於 24日早上 8:20開始，它是一個完整 3天超過 45個 Session的全 Oral 
presentation會議，個人在場聆聽多場包括III-V GaN與II-VI ZnO元件結構與低維度結構
成長與特性探討的演講，其中並與來自 Carnegie Mellon Univ 的演講者在會中討論有關
Hydrogen 檢測對 AlGaN/GaN 元件之導電率與二維電子氣濃度之影響，此外也吸收了有
關一些光電與電子材料之相關量測與分析的資訊，我的 Oral 是在 6/26 早上的
Non-Destructive Testing and In-Situ Control Session, 為 Late News: PP5, 演講之後並和來
自美國 Air Force Research Laboratory的 Dr. K. Eyink及另外一位聆聽者討論有關以光學
方式研究半導體元件內建電場及元件結構成長等相關問題，大會並於中午過後結束；我
於 6/27早上搭乘 06:15 State College飛往 Detroit的 NW2579隨後轉乘 NW11到東京成田
機場再轉 NW21回桃園國際機場，並於 29日凌晨抵達台北，結束此 EMC 2009國際會議
之旅。 
 
二、與會心得 
這次的第 51屆 EMC會議，其實有很多會議的主軸是放在 Nitride及 Zinc Oxide的研
究上，這個會議應該是一個還蠻先進的討論園地，就自己本身在會議中之所見所聞，其
實個人覺得我們臺灣在這方面的研究不管是奈米亦或是元件，與其他與會之來自全球各
地的研究者相較，應屬於相當不錯的層級，不過我想積極觀察並獲取新知，才能夠持續
地在這塊研究園地中有著重要的貢獻，這也是個人應當有的認知與努力。
