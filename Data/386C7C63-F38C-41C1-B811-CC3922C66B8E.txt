6期末報告內容:
Study of the precipitation method to prepare conductive tin oxide powder
摘要
在本期末報告中，導電性良好的氧化錫粉末可由沉澱法扮隨在還原氣氛下的煅燒而被成功地製備
出，由實驗結果顯示，最佳的沉澱方式為在 pH=2 所用的 reverse strike 方法(以 NH4OH 為沉澱劑，並
逐滴低入 SnCl4 溶液直到 pH=2)，在較高的 pH 值下，氯化銨會被沉澱出而污染氧化錫粉末的導電
性，所合成的導電性氧化錫經過 600℃ 下活性碳的還原氣氛下煅燒 1 小時，有最小的電阻係數為 44
±4.2-cm，在本報告內容中並討論滴定方法與煅燒條件對粉末電阻係數的影響。
關鍵詞: 導電性、氧化錫、奈米、沉澱
Abstract
In this final report, the conductive tin oxide powder was prepared successfully by a precipitation method with
heat treatment in a reducing atmosphere. According to the experimental results, the best precipitation method
is the reverse strike (RS) process at pH=2 (NH4OH solution as the precipitant which added drop by drop to
the SnCl4 solution until pH=2). The ammonium chloride would be precipitated at high pH value precipitation,
which pollutes the conductivity of tin oxide. The resistance of tin oxide powder has a minimum value of 44±
4.2 -cm after calcining at 600 for 1 h℃ in an active carbon atmosphere. The effect of the precipitation
method and the calcination condition on the resistivity of the tin oxide powders were discussed in this final
report.
keywords: conductive, tin oxide, nano, precipitation
Introduction
Tin oxide, SnO2, is an n-type semiconductor with a crystalline structure of rutile type and has been used in a
wide range of sciences, technologies and industries. Therefore, motive to study SnO2 powder is due to its
applications as a solid state gas sensor material, oxidation catalyst, and transparent conductor [1, 2]. There are
several methods to prepare nano size SnO2 powder, including the sol-gel method [3, 4], the emulsion method
[5], the gas condensation method [6, 7], the homogeneous precipitation method [8-10], and the precipitation
method [2, 3, 11]. Most of the papers regarding tin oxide powder primarily focus on preparation of the high
surface area SnO2 powder for the gas sensor application. Although several reports indicated that SnO2 is a
conductive material, there are a few papers that discuss its conductivity. The researchers who developed the
conductive SnO2 powder mainly focused on the Sb-doped tin oxide (ATO) powder [12-14]. However, the cost
of this powder is still very expensive. A lower cost and higher conductive SnO2 powder (without any doping)
was developed by the precipitation method with a reducing atmosphere calcination process. The details
are presented in this final report.
Experimental details
Preparation of the tin oxide powder
The precipitates were formed by mixing the two reactants of the 0.3M Sn 4+ solution and the 1 M ammonium
hydroxide solution together. The Sn4+ solution was prepared by dissolving SnCl4 5H‧ 2O to DI water and then
stirred at least 30 min for the hydrolysis reaction of SnCl4 to form the clear Sn4+ solution. The precipitation
process is similar to our previous study [15] in which the process was adopted to synthesize the YAG powder.
The reverse strike (RS) process was performed by titrating Sn4+ solution into an ammonium hydroxide
solution. The precipitate was formed in a basic condition. The titration rate was kept at about 3 ml/min. On
the other hand, the normal strike (NS) process was performed by titrating an ammonium hydroxide solution
8reaction [3] adjusted in the range of pH=5-10. In this study, the ammonium chloride powder was found, which
mixed with the precipitate of SnO2 precursor. According to the experimental result, it can be concluded that
the precursor of SnO2 is almost completely precipitated in the first stage of the RS process. Figure 4 shows the
TGA curves of the precursors collected at pH~2 and pH~9 of the NS process. The weight loss below 200℃ is
the evaporation of water and the OH group of precursor. The great weight loss at about 300℃, which is the
decomposition of ammonium chloride, was observed in the pH~9 precursor curve but it is not significant in
the pH~2 precursor curve. Continuous titrating to pH~2 in the NS process, the acid Sn4+ solution helps to
dissolve the precipitated ammonium chloride and makes the precipitate more pure. We can conclude that the
pure SnO2 precursor can be collected at pH~2, which is during the first stage of the RS process or the final
stage of the NS process. The calcinated powder prepared by the NS process and the RS process are all tin
oxide with rutile structure, as shown in Fig. 5. The crystalline size of the calcinated powders are listed in
Table 1, which is increased by increasing the calcination temperature. The powder made by the RS process is
slightly smaller, but not significantly, than that made by the NS process. The morphologies of the calcinated
powder made from the RS process and the NS process are shown in Figs. 6 and 7, respectively. The particle
sizes were all less than 50 nm. The particles size is increased by increasing the calcination temperature. The
powder made by the RS process is smaller than that made by the NS process. It is consistent with the
crystalline size measurement shown in Table 1.
The results of the resistivity measurement are listed in Table 2. The resistivity of powders collected at pH~8 is
worse than that collected at pH~2. The existence of ammonium chloride is the pollutant of the conductivity of
powder. The resistivity of the SnO2 is decreased and then increased by increasing the calcination temperature.
The decrease of resistivity is from the grain growth to reduce the grain boundary resistance of particles. The
increase in the resistivity for calcining above 600℃may be caused by the decrease of tin oxide distortion.
Cirera etc. [19] reported that the distortion of SnO2 lattice disappears at 700℃, which is a decrease of the
oxygen vacancies. The minimum resistivity is about 44±4.2-cm as calcinated at 600℃. The resistivity of a
commercial nano ITO powder, which was measured by the same method, is about 25±3 -cm. The
conductivity of SnO2 is relatively good. In addition, the resistivity of the powder made by the RS process is
smaller than that formed by the NS process. Although the forming ammonium chloride by the NS method
was dissolved by the adjusting pH~2, the residual pollutant (ammonium chloride) affected the resistivity of
the product. The RS process seems the best precipitation method to form the conductive SnO2 powder.
Conclusions
The highly conductive SnO2 was successively formed by the precipitation method. The best pH value of the
precipitating solution is pH~2. The high pH value would induce the precipitation of ammonium chloride,
which is a pollutant of the conductive SnO2 powder. The conductivity and the particle size of the SnO2
powder made by the RS powder are higher and larger than that made by the NS method, respectively.
Increasing the calcination temperature, the resistivity of SnO2 powder is decreased and then increased. The
minimum resistivity of SnO2 powder, which was calcinated at 600℃ for 1 h by the precipitation with RS
process, is about 44±4.2-cm.
