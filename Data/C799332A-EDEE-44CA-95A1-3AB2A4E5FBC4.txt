voltages of the electrodes embedded beneath the 
liquid-solid interface. Such modulation would form a 
specific time-dependent and nonuniform distribution 
of zeta potentials at the conduit＇s walls and 
subsequently creates the required microvortex flows 
when subjected to external parallel electric fields. 
The analytical solution can derive two microvortex 
patterns. They are, respectively, a single vortex 
circulating across the entire cell and four counter-
rotating vortices of equal size. Simulation studies 
show that chaotic advection for particle mixing can 
be induced by periodically alternating between the 
two flow patterns, while the best mixing efficiency 
can be achieved by properly selecting the period and 
the slip velocities at the conduit＇s walls. In 
addition, this project investigates the control and 
trapping of suspended particles in semicircular and 
square microchannels, and proposes a feedback control 
strategy to trap multiparticles at designated points 
or track their trajectories by adjusting the 
electroosmotic flow. The proposed feedback control 
strategy calculates the required particle＇s velocity 
according to the deviation between each particle＇s 
position and its designated trajectory, and hence 
acquires the optimal voltages. This work also 
addresses the problem of trapping particles at the 
surface stagnation point. By combining the feedback 
control strategy with a short-range force field, the 
efficiency of particle trapping can be enhanced and 
the invalidity of the short-range force for particles 
at a distance can be overcome. The concept is that 
the former can drive the converging electroosmotic 
flow to bring the particles fast to the neighborhood 
of the stagnation point, whereas the latter can 
ensure the capture of the particles at that point.

1. Introduction 
Electroosmotic flow is the fluid motion over a 
charged surface under the action of an applied 
electric field. Its origin lies in the existence of a 
thin electric double layer of 1-100 nm in 
thickness adjacent to the surface. When an 
electric filed is applied, this mobile charge sheath, 
which contains mostly counterions, will exert an 
electric force to drive the fluid therein, dragging 
the fluid bulk along with it. In a macroscopic 
point view, the phenomenon would look like as if 
the surface were sliding (Probstein, 1994). 
On a uniformly charged surface, 
electroosmotic flow usually suffers mixing 
deficiencies and lacks an ability to manipulate 
the fluid motion because of its irrotational flow 
nature (Chang and Yeo, 2010). These 
shortcomings can be remedied by nonlinear 
electroosmotic flow to generate rotational 
microvortex flow with nonzero vorticity needed 
for engineering processes (Ben and Chang, 2002; 
Qian and Bau, 2002; Brunet and Ajdari, 2006). 
Nonlinear electroosmotic flow can be created by 
nonuniform surface charges or  potentials under 
an applied electric field (Dukhin and Miller, 1991; 
Ajdari, 1995; Long et al., 1999; Stroock et al., 
2000). In this case, the electrokinetic slip velocity 
on a surface is nonlinear, thus causing vortex 
flows of diverse features. 
Electroosmosis provides an attractive means 
to mix fluids or suspended particles in 
microdevices. Qian and Bau (2002, 2005) 
proposed the design of a chaotic electroosmotic 
stirrer. They derived analytic solutions for 
electroosmotic flows in open straight conduits 
subjected to specific boundary conditions at 
walls. With the aid of patterned electrodes 
embedded in the top and bottom walls, chaotic 
advection could be induced to achieve efficient 
stirring by alternating between two or more flow 
patterns. The performance of the stirrer is, 
however, limited because the controlling 
electrodes embedded only in the top and bottom 
walls are ineffective in perpendicular fluid flows. 
This project is intended to furnish a design 
method for particle mixing processes using 
electroosmotic flows in confined rectangular 
conduits. Starting with designated electrodes 
embedded in the conduit’s walls, we derive 
two-dimensional analytical solutions for two 
patterns of steady microvortex flow. Through the 
alternations of the two flow patterns with an 
appropriate period, we identify the emergence of 
chaotic advection based on Poincaré sections of 
particle flow. A further study reveals that the 
induced chaotic advection can provide highly 
efficient mixing of suspended particles in the 
rectangular cell. It is demonstrated that the 
proposed mixer design is superior to Qian and 
Bau’s mixer by virtue of the uniformity of the 
particle distribution.

4. Efficiency of the Chaotic Mixer 
To describe the proposed mixing process in a 
quantitative manner, we introduce in the center 
region of the conduit a square blob of 2500 
randomly distributed particles and track their 
trajectories, i.e. the deformation of the blob, for 
15 periods. The period of the alternations is 
chosen to be 4, which ensures highly chaotic 
behavior. The particle trajectories are recorded at 
the end of each period in Figure 4.  
It is observed that in the proposed mixer, the 
blob undergoes a series of rapid stretching and 
folding—a characteristic of chaotic behavior. 
Eventually, the blob distributes uniformly 
throughout the entire mixer in less than 12 
periods of time. For a comparison, the mixer 
presented by Qian and Bau (2002) is also 
investigated with a blob of 2500 particles in a 
rectangular cell under the same operating 
conditions. The results are given in Figure 5. It 
appears that chaotic advection induced by Qian 
and Bau’s mixer could not carry particles to some 
exclusion zones and hence provide less efficient 
mixing. 
To give quantitative measure of the mixing 
efficiency, we divide the conduit into 5000 small 
cells of the same dimension. The ideal mixing is 
that each small cell consists of a single particle. 
Two indices are developed to evaluate the mixing 
efficiency: occupation ratio and standard 
deviation. The index of occupation ratio is to 
calculate the ratio of the small cells occupied by 
at least one particle. The standard deviation is 
simply a measure of uniformity of the particle 
distribution. Figure 6 compares the proposed 
mixer design with the design of Qian and Bau by 
virtue of the two indices. Obviously, the proposed 
mixer is far more superior to Qian and Bau’s 
mixer.

本計畫建立矩形與半圓形區域內電滲流的解析解，能夠準確且快速地計算流體的運動速
度，適合用於微粒子操控程序之設計。有關微混合器設計的成果已發表於中國大連市之
AMN2012 研討會，有關粒子運動控制的成果將於 101年化工年會進行海報展示，期刊論文
亦在撰寫中。 
 
針對矩形微管道內懸浮粒子，我們利用時變電滲微渦流產生之混沌對流來建構微混合器。
此解析解可推導出兩種微渦流類型，分別為環繞整個管道截面的單一渦流以及同等大小反
向旋轉的四個渦流。週期性地變換此兩種微渦流場類型，可以產生混沌對流以混合粒子，
而最佳混合效率可藉由選擇適當的週期大小和管壁滑移速度來達成。 
 
針對微流道內懸浮粒子的控制與捕捉，我們提出利用電滲流的調整進行多粒子定點捕捉或
指定軌跡追蹤的回饋控制策略。所提回饋控制策略根據粒子位置與指定軌跡的偏差來計算
所需粒子速度，進而獲得最佳的電壓改變量。我們亦結合回饋控制策略及短距作用力場來
