Estimation of the Variation of Bearing Coefficients in Rotating

一、摘要 
 
油膜軸承之軸承係數隨轉速而變，由
Tiwari 等人所推導之不平衡反應方程式中
得知，未知數數目為方程式數目的 2 倍，
無論量測數目的多寡，皆須以至少兩次試
運轉的方式鑑別出軸承係數，亦即必須停
機做第二次不平衡配重之調整配置，以獲
得足夠之振動訊號。本研究假設在某一轉
速區間內的軸承係數曲線為二階多項式，
再利用此轉速區間內若干個轉速的不平衡
振動反應，計算出該多項式的係數。其優
點符合以軸承係數為轉速之函數的假設，
以及在一次試運轉的情形下鑑別出軸承係
數，不必停機，可進行線上鑑別軸承係數
之作業。同時可降低雜訊效應，又有利於
內插法求取其他特定轉速下之軸承係數。 
關鍵詞：軸承係數，油膜軸承，不平衡反
應，鑑別 
  
Abstract 
 
It is well known that the bearing 
coefficients of oil film journal bearings are 
speed dependent.  From the derivation of 
Tiwari et al., the numbers of unknown 
coefficients are twice the number of the 
equations relating to unbalance responses.  
That means at least two unbalance test runs 
are necessary to solve the bearing 
coefficients, no matter how many 
measurements are made, and the rotating 
machines have to stop once to adjust the 
unbalance configurations for the second test 
run.  In this study, second order 
polynomials are assumed to fit the bearing 
coefficients in a specific speed range, and 
unbalance measurements are made at several 
rotating speeds in that speed range to solve 
for the polynomial coefficients.  The 
advantages of this approach are that it fulfills 
the conditions of speed-dependent 
coefficients, and the bearing coefficients can 
be obtained with only one test run.  It is not 
necessary to stop the machines, and on-line 
identification of bearing coefficients can be 
achieved.  Noise reduction and interpolation 
for the bearing coefficients at other speeds 
can also be easily attained. 
Keywords: bearing coefficient, oil film 
bearing, unbalance response, identification 
 
二、緣由與目的 
 
在旋轉機械中，轉子軸承系統是一個
重要的動態系統。在工業界常遇到的問題
是數學模型之分析結果與旋轉機械的振動
量測結果之間有相當大的差異。其原因是
在建構轉子軸承系統數學模型的精確性不
足。轉子軸承系統之數學模型中的參數
值，必須能相當精確的代表旋轉機械之實
際運轉狀態。在轉軸與轉盤分析方面，數
學模型之模擬精確性可說相當地好[1, 2]。
軸承方面，過去雖然已經有許多鑑別軸承
動態係數方法的發表，但在實際使用這些
軸承係數時，發現有很大的誤差[2, 3]。主
要的原因是，分析或鑑別這些軸承係數時
之各種條件與軸承實際使用狀況不同。 
一般軸承的動態係數只考慮 4 個阻尼
係數與 4 個勁度係數，這 8 個係數之性質
會隨著軸轉速而變化[4, 5]。

在較寬的轉速區間內，以高階多項式逼近
軸承係數是比較適當的。但若以太高階的
多項式來逼近軸承係數曲線，會造成未知
數係數過多的情形，同時曲線內易有多次
振盪(oscillation)。在一般使用上，不須求
解所有轉速下之軸承係數，只需得知在某
特定轉速區間內的軸承係數即表可。故本
研究採用二階多項式來逼近在某轉速區
間內之軸承係數曲線，可避免以高階多項
式鑑別造成未知數過多之情形。綜合以上
觀點可發現，逼近在某一轉速區間內之軸
承係數近似曲線以二階多項式較適當。

IDENTIFICATION OF SPEED-DEPENDENT BEARING COEFFICIENTS FROM  
UNBALANCE RESPONSES IN ONE TEST RUN 
 
 
Tachung Yang*    Kai-Shang Lin 
Department of Mechanical Engineering, Yuan Ze University, 
Chung-Li, Taoyuan, Taiwan, R.O.C. 32026 
mety@saturn.yzu.edu.tw 
 
 
ABSTRACT 
It is well known that the bearing coefficients of oil film journal 
bearings are speed dependent.  From the derivation of Tiwari et al., 
the numbers of unknown coefficients are twice the number of the 
equations relating to unbalance responses.  That means at least two 
unbalance test runs are necessary to solve the bearing coefficients, 
no matter how many measurements are made, and the rotating 
machines have to stop once to adjust the unbalance configurations 
for the second test run.  In this study, second order polynomials are 
assumed to fit the bearing coefficients in a specific speed range, and 
unbalance measurements are made at several rotating speeds in that 
speed range to solve for the polynomial coefficients.  The 
advantages of this approach are that it fulfills the conditions of 
speed-dependent coefficients, the bearing coefficients can be 
obtained without stopping the machines, and on-line identification 
of bearing coefficients can be achieved.  Noise reduction and 
interpolation for the bearing coefficients at other speeds can also be 
easily attained. 
INTRODUCTION 
Parameter identification of bearing coefficients plays an 
important role in the modeling of rotor-bearing systems.  It is well 
recognized that bearings can be modeled as 4 stiffness and 4 
damping coefficients, and the bearing coefficients are functions of 
rotating speeds [1]。 
Methods of obtaining bearing coefficients of hydrodynamic 
bearings can be roughly categorized into (1) solving Reynolds’ 
lubrication equation [2]; (2) calculated by CFD codes [3]; and (3) 
by experiment.  The first two approaches are fast and convenient.  
However, many uncertainties exist in the operating conditions of the 
bearings, e.g., manufacturing tolerance, assembled clearance, 
temperature variations of oil film, thermal conduction and 
convection [4-6].  Hence, estimating bearing coefficients based on 
the vibration signals under operating conditions is the most precise 
approach that reflects the true conditions of the bearings [7].  
Tiwari et al. [7] gave an extensive review on the experimental 
identification of bearing coefficients. 
Among the experimental methods, which can be further 
divided into of non-periodic and periodic excitations [7]: the 
non-periodic excitations include static excitation [8, 9], and impulse 
excitation [10-12], whereas the periodic excitations include 
harmonic excitation [13-15] and unbalance [16-23]. 
It seems infeasible that applying external excitations directly 
on large industrial rotating machines, like steam turbine-generators, 
since complicated and expensive experimental equipment is 
required for the impulse excitation and exciter’s excitation.  Using 
unbalance masses to excite the rotor seems the most practical and 
inexpensive method so far. 
From the previous literature [16-23], at least two unbalance 
configurations and two test runs, each for an unbalance 
configuration, are required to estimate the bearing coefficients for 
each individual rotating speed.  Tiwari et al. [22] identified the 
speed-dependent bearing coefficients based on two test runs.  
From their derivation, the unknown coefficients are twice the 
number of the equations established, no matter how many the 
bearings are.  Therefore, at least two independent unbalance 
configurations and two test runs are required to establish the 
equations. 
Although experimental approaches can reflects the true 
operating conditions of industrial machines, several limitations still 
exist.  For example, the maintenance schedules are always tight, 
no sufficient test time for the experiment, and the test runs should 
be kept to minimum.  Also, the field people hope to have the 
capability of on-line monitoring.  The major advantage of 
identification in single test run, either run up or run down, is that 
stopping the machines for the setup of the second unbalance 
configuration is not necessary, and the identification work can be 
completed by varying the operating speeds in tolerably operational 
conditions. 
San Andes and De Santiago [23] discussed the formulation of 
the identification equations of bearing coefficients and conclude 
that two independent unbalance sets are required.  But only one 
test run is conducted by assuming null cross-coupling forces and 
only the direct terms of bearing coefficients are identified.  Sinha 
et al. [24] identified the foundation coefficients in single test run.  
To obtain sufficient number of equations, they set up several 
narrower speed ranges and used the responses at multiple speeds 
within each speed range to identify the foundation coefficients by 
assuming constant coefficients in each speed range.  Although this

From Tables 5, 7, 8, it can be found that several false 
nodes are identified (nodes 4 and 8).  The discussion for 
these results is as follows, 
1. When unbalances occur at adjacent nodes, e.g., node 3 and 5, 
node 4 is recognized if it is identified in early search rounds.  
Nevertheless, nodes 3 and 5 are still to be identified later 
and the accuracy of the unbalances at nodes 3 and 5 is not 
affected, since the unbalances at nodes 3, 4, and 5 is 
recalculated at each subsequent search round.  The 
unbalance at node 4 will be negligible and can be removed 
easily. 
2. When large noise is added into the signals, e.g., 
NSR=10%, some false nodes will be selected (e.g., node 
8), which causes the index δ  not able to converge
