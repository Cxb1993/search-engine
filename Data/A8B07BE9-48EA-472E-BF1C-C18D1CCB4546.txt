摘要 
本計畫採用不同的奈米製程技術來製作金
屬奈米結構，透過表面聲波元件，來達到主
動操控表面電漿波在奈米金屬波導中的傳
播方式；希望利用微機電製程技術，進一步
將表面電漿子元件整合到積體光學光路
中，達到主動式調變表面電漿光波的線性以
及非線性現象。本計劃成功的利用奈米壓印
及製作出週期排列的金屬奈米結構也利用
自組裝製程將金屬奈米結構整合到表面聲
波元件上希望能藉由簡單的製程設備及元
件來展現利用表面聲波操控近場光學現象。 
關鍵詞:金屬奈米結構，表面聲波元件，近
場光學。 
 
Abstract 
We proposed to use different ways to achieve 
the fabrication of periodically arranged 
metallic nanostructure. An actively 
controllable plasnonic device could be 
fabricated by the combination of metallic 
nanostructure and surface acoustic wave 
(SAW) device. We hope to integrate the 
actively controllable plasmonic device into the 
integrated optical circuit by the MEMS 
fabrication. The results shows that the metallic 
nanostructures could be successfully 
fabricated using the nanoimprint technology 
and self-assembly way combined with the 
fabrication of metallic nanoparticles, which 
was deposited onto the SAW device. We hope 
that an actively controlled near-field optics 
could be demonstrated by the SAW-controlled 
plasmonic device. 
 
Key words: metallic nanostructure, surface 
acoustic wave (SAW) device, near-field 
optics.

2 EXPERIMENTAL 
The solid core, large mode area PCF of different core diameter was used in the experiment. For example, a commercial 
name of LMA-35 (Crystal Fibre A/S Denmark) has solid core of 35μm in diameter, hollow cladding of 283 μm in 
diameter, and air-hole of 11.5 μm. A LMA PCF selection chart and a cross-sectional image of LMA-8 are shown in Fig. 
1. The LC mixture used to infiltrate the PCF is a photoresponsive LCs that contains an azobenzene LC in which the RI 
modulation of LC is achieved by the photochromatic deformation of the azobenzene under an applied external optical 
field. The homogeneous mixture of namatic LC, azobenzene LC and chiral dopant was filled into the air-hole cladding 
by capillary force at the temperature of 90°C. No pre- or post-aligning treatment was applied in the LC or LC-filled PCF. 
Fig. 2 shows the two schematics of optical setup to characterize the optical properties from photoresponsive LC-filled 
PCF.

All-optically switchable photonic crystal fiber  
 
 
Yu-Chang Lin, Sheng-Che Huang, Vincent K. S. Hsiao*  
Dept. of Applied Materials and Optoelectronic Engineering, National Chi Nan University, Nantou, 
Taiwan 54561 
 
*kshsiao@ncnu.edu.tw; phone 886-49-291-0960; fax 886-49-291-2434 
ABSTRACT 
We have achieved an all-optical tuning in photonic crystal fiber (PCF) by filling the photoresponsive liquid crystal (LC) 
into the air-hole cladding. The photo-induced phase transformation of the photoresponsive LC modulates the effective 
refractive index of the photoresponsive LC-filled air-hole cladding, thereby creating an optically tunable environment. 
Under the laser irradiation the output intensity of guided light can be modulated by the photoresponsive LC-filled 
photonic bandgap structure. The tuning behavior of the guided light is independent on the polarization direction by using 
the linear-polarized He-Ne laser as probe. We also demonstrate the potential use of photoresponsive LC-filled PCF in 
all-optical communication device using an optical spectrum of wide bandwidth amplified from an erbium-doped fiber 
amplifier (EDFA) system. 
 
Keywords: all-optical tuning; photonic crystal fiber; photoresponsive liquid crystal 
 
1 INTRODUCTION 
Photonic crystal fiber (PCF), an intriguing design which was first demonstrated in 1991, guides light by 
incorporating a photonic bandgap (PBG) located in the cladding structure [1]. The PBG is created by introducing a 
hollow fiber core and a periodically arranged air-void cladding in which Light cannot propagate through the fiber-void 
interface within the cladding body due to the forbidden bandgap. Another design of PCF is using modified total internal 
reflection (TIR) that the PCF is consisted of a solid fiber core and a periodically arranged air-void cladding. No matter 
which kind of PCF the intriguing design of hollow structure in core or cladding extends the use of optical fiber from 
optical fiber guidance [2,3] into high power fiber laser [4,5], nonlinear optics [6,7], sensing [8,9], and tunable fiber 
devices [10-12] by separately filling gas, liquid, and solid materials into the voids. Especially, the Infiltration of liquid 
crystal (LC) into the voids efficiently creates an actively tunable environment and has demonstrated potential application 
in optical devices, such as tunable switches and filters [13, 14]. The tunability of LC-filled PCF is due to LC’s unique 
properties of large and tunable birefringence under the external field, such as thermal, electrical and optical field [15]. 
The external field changes the average refractive index (RI) of the LC-filled core or cladding and modulates the optical 
properties of the LC-filled PCF. 
In this proceeding, we demonstrate an all-optical tuning PCF achieved by infiltrating photoresponsive LC [16] into 
the air-void cladding using a solid-core, large mode area PCF. The light guiding mechanism of solid-core PCF is similar 
as conventional step index guiding fiber where the RI of air-hole filled cladding is smaller than the solid core [5]. After 
LC infiltration, we observed that the guiding ability of LC-filled PCF was still valid. The filled LC increases the RI of 
cladding and changes the guiding mechanism of solid-core PCF from modified TIR into PBG guiding in which the LC-
filled cladding regions act as a scattering center to modulate the output intensity. The behavior of intensity modulation is 
independent on the polarization direction of guided light which is from linear-polarized 633 nm laser. We also try to 
connect the photoresponsive LC-filled PCF of different core size into the conventional single mode fiber (SMF) using 
fusion splicer and to verify their optical performance. The occurrence of PBG from the LC-filled PCF indicates the 
change of guiding mechanism from modified TIR into PBG guiding by using the continued optical spectrum (1520 nm – 
1550 nm) from the erbium-doped fiber amplifier (EDFA).

Fig. 5 shows the output beam profile from LMA-35 PCF captured from CCD camera using H-Ne laser as probe 
before (Fig. 5a) and after (Fig. 5b) LC infiltration. Before LC infiltration, the PCF guides few part of probe light in the 
solid core but most part of probe light scatter within the air-hole cladding areas. Our observation is confirmed from 
company’s data sheet that the LMA-35 has huge attenuation in the visible wavelength (> 100 db/km). After LC 
infiltration, even though some parts of probe light were observed outside the cladding area; however, most of the probe 
light was guided within the solid core comparing the light in the air-hole cladding. For the common solid core PCF, the 
fiber core (pure silica, nsilica=1.45) has a higher RI than the cladding (nave=1.38, mixed silica and air holes, nair=1) which 
was calculated by the volume percentage of air-hole (15%) and silica (85%) areas according the geometry of commercial 
solid-core LMA-35 PCF. After photoresponsive LC infiltration, the RI of cladding changed from 1.38 to 1.47 which was 
calculated by using the average RI of nematic LC as 1.61 (for TL213, no=1.527, ne=1.766, nave=2/3no+1/3ne) [28]. 
Because the average RI of cladding is larger than the solid core after LC infiltration, the mechanism of modified TIR 
guidance is not suitable to explain the guidance of probe light in photoresponsive LC-filled PCF. Therefore, we speculate 
that the LC-filled cladding regions in PCF create a PBG and the light cannot propagate inside the LC-filled cladding
