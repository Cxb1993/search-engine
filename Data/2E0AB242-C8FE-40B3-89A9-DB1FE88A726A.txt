Measurements＂, which has a document 
number of IEEE 802.11-09/0995r1. The 
authors are myself, my students 
(Wen-Heng Lin, Jian-Zhong Lo, Xun Lan), 
and MediaTek engineers (James Wang, 
Vish Ponnampalam, Alvin Hsu). We try to 
study the transmission and reflection 
characteristics of different common 
construction material in 60 GHz.  
Material under test includes Plastic 
Partition, Plywood, Thick Wood Board, 
and Tempered Glass, We have two 
different polarization loss study. For 
normal incident wave, the reflection 
loss is the same for all polarization.  
Away from the normal incident 
direction, the polarization with 
e-field parallel to the interface 
surface will have less reflection loss. 
Nevertheless, the polarization with 
e-field not parallel to the interface 
surface will have more reflection loss, 
and a high loss angle. This is a 
complete study result for 60-GHz 
material.

second paper with a title of “A

Transmit/Receive Switch in 90nm CMOS 
Technology A 0.7-V 60-GHz Low-Power 
LNA with Forward Body Bias Technique in 
90 nm CMOS Process＂. This paper is  
a low voltage and low power 60 GHz low 
noise amplifier (LNA) using 90 nm 
standard MS/RF CMOS technology By 
using forward-body-biased our LNA can 
operate at 0.7-V and 4.9mW while 
maintaining a peak gain of 13 dB at 55 
GHz and 12.6 dB at 60 GHz with a compact 
chip size of 0.351 mm2. The 1-dB 
frequency bandwidth ranges from 54.5 
to 61.0 GHz and the minimum noise 
figure is 6.3 dB at 64 GHz. We use the 
ratio GBP to power consumption to 
compare with recent publications. Our 
paper can achieve the highest ratio of 
GBP to dc power, especially it only 
operates such a low supply voltage at 
0.7 V. After the presentation of my 
paper, questions about the device size 
selection is raised, we gave a 
guideline of device selection.

proposes two techniques to minimize 
the impact of RF impairments on the 
performance of 60-GHz multi-gigabit 
system: (1) An example of 60-GHz 65-nm 
CMOS power amplifier pre-distortion 
linearizer is proposed for the 
optimization of multi-gigabit RF 
system linearity; (2) A 60-GHz 
sub-harmonic local oscillator 
topology is also proposed for the 65-nm 
CMOS phase noise optimization. 
65nm CMOS PA linearizer can boost 
60-GHz linear power 2-3dB under the 
same linearity requirement and DC 
power consumption. Fig. 2 shows the
