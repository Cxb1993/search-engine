Abstract
Auto Focusing (AF) is a common but important feature both for still image photography and
video photography. Traditionally, focusing sensors are utilized for auto focusing systems; we
propose a new auto focusing mechanism which uses the image sensor directly for measuring and
estimating the focal distance in this project. For a given scene, we will first fix the shutter speed
and iris value, then capture series of pictures with different focal distance. After that, we partition
each picture into blocks and perform spectrum analysis on each block. Finally, we can estimate
the correct focal distance for each series of blocks by selecting the block with highest energy for
high frequency component. In this project, each Region of Interest (ROI) of each image can be
analyzed to design auto focusing for each scene through Discrete Cosine Transformation (DCT).
It can help to take a good composition for each photographer. In addition, the proposed approach
can be applied to digital camera and all the research results for this project will be distributed.
Key words: Auto Focusing, Discrete Cosine Transformation (DCT), Region of Interest (ROI),
Still Image Photography
