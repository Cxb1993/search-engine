 
利用綠色乾燥及萃取技術應用於陳皮香氣及活性成分保留與萃取 
Green drying and extraction technology will be used to maintain and extract the aroma and 
active compounds of Chen peel 
摘要 
 
目前有不少農產品進行日曬乾燥，因其能改變乾燥物中特殊成份之生成，在台灣日
曬乾燥無法生產高品質的產品,其主要原因是受到天候、環境衛生等因素所影響。本計畫
利用已取得專利之密閉式光觸媒乾燥系統進行陳皮間歇乾燥之研究，探討乾燥期間熱傳
及水分之變化，分析其乾燥陳皮之物理化學特性，並探討不同製備方式(日曬乾燥、熱
風間歇乾燥、密閉式光觸媒間歇乾燥)，利用超臨界CO2萃取分離出陳皮中香氣及活性成
分及評估其抗氧化能力。本研究顯示，太陽能密閉式間歇乾燥法製作陳皮，必須要32天
才能將陳皮乾燥至13％以下，但此種緩和之脫水模式可增加乾燥產品之風味與口感，並
有利於延長保存期限。生菌數部分，太陽能密閉式間歇乾燥系統配合光觸媒之塗佈能有
效降低陳皮之生菌數至5 cfu/g，有助於延長保存期限及食品衛生安全。超臨界萃取後之
陳皮精油之產量為陳皮乾重之5.63％，較其他間歇乾燥法高約1倍。因此經由研究後確認
此方式可達至高品質、活性、香氣之乾燥模式。經田口分析後可得到最佳化之陳皮色澤
間歇條件，乾燥相對溼度設定為20%、乾燥溫度40±3℃以及乾燥風速2 m/s，可得到較高
品質之陳皮。經品評之結果，在外觀、表皮色澤部分，因密閉式日曬間歇乾燥乃採低溫
乾燥之方式進行，較易減緩陳皮之酵素性褐變反應產生，色澤較鮮艷。在陳皮之香氣部
分，密閉式日曬間歇乾燥時其乾燥溫度及速率較為均勻且緩和，香氣成分不易於高溫時
隨著產品本身水分之蒸散作用而揮發，所以較能保留香氣，優於其他間歇乾燥法。最後，
抗氧化成分與能力分析部分，密閉式日曬間歇乾燥的總酚與類黃酮含量後雖較熱風間歇
乾燥來的低，但因密閉式日曬間歇乾燥可保留較多的含量，慢慢釋出，因此在儲藏3個
月之後，其抗氧化成分較熱風乾燥來的高，且進一步影響到DPPH清除自由基能力，使
其清除率較熱風間歇乾燥高。 
綜合上述，可確知本研究以創新之間歇乾燥方法配合密閉式乾燥系統，利用控溫調
時之脫水模式，即可獲得較高品質和香氣之陳皮與其抗氧化成分。故本研究間歇日曬之
密閉式乾燥方法，可適用於連續乾燥之工業，提供農業產品或食品脫水一個保留有效抗
氧化成分之乾燥模式。 
 
關鍵字:日曬乾燥、陳皮、間歇式乾燥、超臨界、田口法、香氣 
 
一、前言 
陳皮為常用的中藥材之一，又稱青橘皮，屬於芸香科(Rutaceae) 柑橘屬(Citrus) 植
物的外果皮或幼小果皮乾燥品；功能有健胃、顧脾、行氣、化淤、發汗散寒及祛痰；在
中醫上主治胸腹脹痛、慢性肝炎、氣鬱脹痛、消化不良、胃痛及乳部痛，為常使用的理
氣藥之一；另【本草綱目】也有記載：青皮主治胸膈逆氣、協痛、小腹疝氣、消乳腫、
疏肝膽、瀉廢氣，苦辛溫無毒。陳皮具有特殊風味適於食品化，但是尚未有飲料或健康
食品形態出現市面。本實驗將研究國產各種橘皮(芸香科(Rutaceae)柑橘屬(Citrus))。柑橘
類包括：椪柑(Citri Rubrum Pericarpium)、桶柑(Citrus tankan fortunella hairi)、文旦柚
(Citrus grandis Osbeck)、斗柚、柳橙(Citrus grandis Osbeck)、檸檬(Citrus limonum)、葡
萄柚(Citrus paradisi Macfaden)及其他雜柑類(如金桔 Citrus microcarpa Bonge)，生產面積
共 33527 公頃，年產量達 547814 公噸。可利用各種橘皮數量可觀(方 1994)。 
柑橘類在台灣種植面積近五萬公頃，年產量五萬五千公噸，台灣每年每人平均消費
新鮮柑桔 26 公斤，連同進口的柑桔及果汁，每人平均超過 30 公斤左右，可見柑橘類食
品在日常飲食中佔極重要之地位，其運用範圍非常廣泛，例如果汁飲料、香料、色素、
精油、果醬等。桶柑含有氮物質；在火烘的製造過程中總氮量變化不大，而胺基態氮含
量則隨著蒸煮時間增加而有減少之趨勢。胺基態氮於陳皮製造過程中所減少的主因為柑
橘類在蒸煮過程中其細胞受蒸氣蒸漲而流失，或於蒸煮過程中有梅納反應之參與，而導
致此等胺基態氮的消耗（Lin 1988），營養成分不易保存。本研究選擇以桶柑作為製作陳
皮之原料，其主因為；柑橘含有多種類酚酸、Limonene、維生素及類黃酮。維生素含量
高達 126.88-146.83 mg/100g，果膠質含量 0.49-0.93 g/100g 較其他水果豐富，總類胡蘿蔔
素含量介於 0.036-0.743 mg/100g，其果皮總多酚含量為果肉的 4-5 倍，4.953- 6.988 g/100g
（Bocco 1998）。 
另外，陳皮在加工過程中，顏色變成黑褐色，除了 Maillard 反應外，酵素性褐變亦
扮演重要角色，Polyphenol oxidase 將 Tyrosine 之類成份含 hydroxy group 之 bezene ring，
先進行 hydroxylation 再氧化，本研究擬藉乾燥技術控制發展之專利技術，調整水活性及
溫度等參數，生產具有活性官能基之 Nobiletin 衍生物。 
傳統日曬乾燥法中太陽光有助於柑橘乾燥之品質提升，但在其日曬過程中，開放式
的空間下或氣流中，無法兼顧品質與安全的衛生問題，乾燥時間也隨著氣候而改變，以
致無法生產高品質之乾燥產品且香氣無法保存(Basunia 2001)。在傳統烘乾製程中通常是
以熱風或滾筒式乾燥機烘乾，雖能有效的達到脫水乾燥之目的，但溫度過高易破壞活性
成分，而且產品微量成分不易合成，香氣較無法保留（Watmuff J. 1977, 陳 1996）。本
研究室由國科會計畫補助已開發之密閉式光觸媒乾燥機（中華民國專利 No219833；美
國專利 6,941,673），是模擬傳統日曬乾燥，利用密閉式乾燥取代開放式環境，利用低溫
乾燥進行間歇式乾燥脫水及配合溫度與時間之控制，使柑橘中之酵素與基質結合達到有
效活性成分之合成，並能有效保留住香氣及營養成分(陳 and 黃 1999)。

2. 分析檢測： 
a.陳皮含水率測定 
   水分含量之計算 :將新鮮之柑橘放入乾燥機中乾燥至恆重為W，利用公式
(W/Wi)*100%，(三重覆)求固型物率。並採無因次水分含量，即將已知新鮮柑橘重量為
Wi，乾燥後重量變為W，固型物重Wo，此時之水分含量為(W-Wo) /(Wi-Wo)*100%。以
1hr為一單位，紀錄柑橘乾燥時之水分含量之變化，並以曲線圖表示之。 
b.水活性測定: 
將乾燥完成之陳皮剝取1cm2放置於水活性測定儀（AQUA LAB，CX-2 ，Japan）中
讀取其Aw 值與Temp℃(水活性測定儀之測定溫度)，每樣品測定三重覆，並計算其平均
值。 
c.色澤測定： 
剝取1cm2~2cm2之陳皮置於色差儀鑑定其顏色差異。以Hunter “L”，”a”，”b”值表示，
其L值表示亮度，100時為全白，0時為全黑，a值表示紅黃度，b值表示藍綠度，每樣品
正反面各測兩點，取5個共10次(三重覆)，以平均值作為分析之依據。並於測定前以標準
白板及黑板作其校正。 
白度值(W.I)用以評估乾燥製品之褐變程度之指標，白度值越高表示褐變程度越低，其計
算公式如下： 
W.I = 100-【（100-L）2 + a2 + b2】0.5
d.香氣成分萃取： 
乾燥樣品取3g，利用固相微量萃取進行萃取，先將萃取器具進行去吸附，樣品置
於萃取瓶中進行吸附約20-30 min。 
e.香氣分析鑑定： 
乾燥樣品取3g，以固相微量萃取其揮發性成分後，使用GC-MS「氣相層析質譜儀」
(Hewlett-Packard 6890 SERIES II, Japan) 測定之。偵測器為氫焰離子偵測器 (flame 
ionization detector, FID) 溫度設定為250℃。放置管柱之烘箱(oven) 初始溫度設定為 40
℃，升溫速率為5℃/min，溫度升至250℃後，維持10 min (Friedman et al. 2000)。 
f.官能品評：

再以 10 mL、80%甲醇重複上述萃取步驟。待萃取完畢後，收集上清液，以濾紙 (No. 4, 
Whatman, England) 過濾並定容至 25 mL。 
c.抗氧化物質分析： 
(a) 總酚測定： 
參考Taga 等 (1984)方法，測定萃取物中總酚含量。原理為在磷鉬酸化 (phospho-
molybdotungstis acid) 複合物(Folin-Ciocalteau’s phenol reagent)中，苯酚 (phenol) 可以還
原鉬(molybdenum) ， 當樣品中的苯酚環愈多時，酚基與 Folin-Ciocalteau phenol reagent 
反應之後，可以產生愈多的藍綠色複合物 (Ancos et al. 2000)。 
將標準品沒食子酸及適量的甲醇萃取物分別以 80%甲醇溶液配製至一定溶度。各取
0.2mL 萃取液，加入 1mL Folin-Ciocalteau’s phenol reagent 及 7.5%碳酸鈉溶液 0.8mL，
混合均勻後，在室溫下以避光方式靜置 30 分鐘，以分光光度計在 750nm 下測吸光值。
由沒食子酸(gallic acid)的標準曲線計算樣品總酚含量。樣品之總酚含量以萃取物中每
100 公克陳皮粉樣品乾重中所含沒食子酸當量(gallic acid equivalent, GAE)毫克數表示。 
(b) 類黃酮測定： 
參考Dowo (1959) 及Quettier-Deleu 等 (2000) 之氯化鋁(AlCl3)方法測定萃取物中
類黃酮含量。由文獻指出，氯化鋁針對黃酮(flavone)及黃烷醇(flavonol)兩類化合物進行
衍生化，作用機制為氯化鋁可與C3 或A 環之C5 及B 環中C3’或C4’位置上帶有羥基
(-OH)，及碳環中C4 位置為酮基之類黃酮物質進行衍生化，其於特定波長下具有吸光特
性。取0.5 mL 萃取液，加入1 mL 2% AlCl3‧6H2O 甲醇溶液，混合均勻靜置10分鐘，
在430 nm 下測吸光值。 
由 quercertin (3,3’,4’,5,7-Pentahydroxyflavone) 的標準曲線計算樣品類黃酮含量。類黃
酮含量以萃取物中每 100 公克陳皮粉乾重，或中所含 quercertin equivalent(QE)毫克數表
示。 
 
7.陳皮抗氧化能力分析： 
a.清除 1,1-diphenyl-2-picrylhydrazyl(DPPH)自由基能力測定： 
參考Shimada 等 (1992) 方法。由於抗氧化劑在抑制油脂氧化之機制上，通常是抗
氧化物當氫離子供應者(hydrogen doner)或自由態游離基接受者來清除油脂過氧化物自
由基，故使用DPPH (1,1-diphenyl-2-picrylhydrazyl) 來檢測抗氧化劑提供氫之能力。DPPH 
為一相當安定的自由基，其甲醇溶液在517nm 附近有強的吸光值，當被抗氧化劑還原，
或與另外一個自由基結合時，吸光值會減低，藉以判斷樣品捕捉自由基之能力 
(Brand-Williams et al. 1995) 。其吸光值愈低表示樣品之捕捉自由基之能力愈強。 
DPPH + AH → DPPH‧ ：H + A‧ (violet) (decolorized) 
取不同濃度樣品甲醇萃取液2 mL，加入新鮮配製DPPH (0.8 mg/mL)之甲醇溶液2 mL 
均勻混合，以黑布遮蓋靜置30 分鐘，測定波長517 nm 之吸光值。吸光值愈低表示樣品
捕捉自由基能力愈強。配製80%甲醇溶液作為對照組。 
計算公式： 
清除能力(%) = (1–樣品 OD517 值/控制組 OD517值) ×100

4.4 陳皮色澤分析 
    在不同乾燥下，產品褐變反應也會有所差異，其原因極有可能為非酵素性或部分於
酵素性褐變反應。經研究結果；日曬密閉式間歇乾燥、熱風間歇乾燥與傳統日曬乾燥之
白度值分別為；27.36、35.83、26.79 ，如表 2 所示。經研究證實熱風間歇乾燥法隨著乾
燥溫度大幅度的提昇或降低，使得表皮油囊氣孔皺縮導致水分去除較不穩定，其外觀色
澤則呈深色；其為溫度達到某一範圍以上時，物料中所含水分、醣質及有機酸易發生化
學反應所致。日曬密閉式間歇乾燥之製程溫度屬低溫範圍，所發生化學反應較熱風間歇乾
燥緩和，雖然日曬密閉式間歇乾燥也受陽光中紫外線照射，但其乾燥室極具密閉性，得以
減少陳皮中之類黃酮與氧化接觸之機會，所以能使乾燥後之陳皮顏色較為明亮，且香氣
較易保留。 
 
4.5 陳皮生菌數之探討 
食品開發完成後，第一個檢測重點為產品之微生物數量，不同的食品檢測的菌種也
有所不同，大多數的微生物對人體無害或有益，但卻有少數具有致病性的病原菌存在，
例如細菌、真菌、放線菌等，當人體抵抗力低時，食品中的細菌及真菌所產生的毒素亦
會引起食品中毒，或誘發腫瘤。 
利用太陽光紫外線配合光觸媒達到殺菌效果並與熱風間歇乾燥法、傳統日曬間歇乾
燥法作比較，產品在乾燥完成階段，陳皮所含之生菌數，依 CNS 中華民國國家標準檢
驗檢測之，如表 3、圖 4 所示，利用日曬密閉式間歇乾燥之陳皮平均生菌數為 5 CFU/g (稀
釋 10倍)較熱風間歇乾燥平均生菌數 170 CFU/g及傳統日曬間歇乾燥平均 200 CFU/g低。 
 
4.6 陳皮微細構造之探討 
    植物性食品材料整體或其中任何一部分，經採收(拔、摘、割等)後，由於水分、礦
物質及醣類、激素等小分子有機成分的供應或輸送停止，該植物組織依舊維持生命與各
種生理活性，但卻在代謝路徑上發生很大的變化。例如光合作用幾乎完全停止，而其它
細胞成分的合成及轉變反應雖依舊維持著，但其活性卻有很大的變化，而其結果對該植
物體組織之可食性或食用品質而言，則利弊互見，不一而定。現以不同乾燥條件之陳皮，
如圖 5 所示，利用電子顯微鏡 (SEM) 觀察其纖維與油囊氣孔之組織。經電子顯微鏡觀
察陳皮之油囊與纖維狀態，如圖 6、7 所示。以熱風間歇乾燥與傳統日曬間歇乾燥後之
陳皮纖維組織結實緊密，較不利於乾燥期間酵素與基質間之結合，且熱風間歇乾燥因高

織較為寬鬆可利於酵素與基質間之結合。間歇密閉式乾燥於乾燥期間其油囊呈現非封閉
狀。由於低溫之故一些高溫易揮發之物質不易於乾燥期間由表皮油囊釋出，使得揮發性

生，色澤較鮮艷。在陳皮之香氣部分，密閉式日曬間歇乾燥時其乾燥溫度及速率較為均

USING GREEN DRYING TECHNOLOGY TO MAINTAIN HIGHER LEVELS OF 
ACTIVE COMPOUNDS: AN EXAMPLE OF ROSELLE (HIBISCUS SABDARIFFA 
L.) DRYING 
 
HO-HSIEN CHEN  
Department of Food Science, National Pingtung University of Science and Technology, No1, 
Shuehfu Rd., Neipu, Pingtung, 912, Taiwan ,email: hhchen@mail.npust.edu.tw
 
 CHIH-SHENG CHOU 
Department of Food Science, National Pingtung University of Science and Technology, No1, 
Shuehfu Rd., Neipu, Pingtung, 912, Taiwan ,email: e9036009@mail.npust.edu.tw
 
In this research, a novel patented Solar Energy-assisted Photocatalytic Closed-type Dryer (SEPCD) was used to dry roselle (Hi-
biscus sabdariffa L.). It was designed to replace traditional sun drying and hot-air drying, in which the qualities of dried products 
are easily affected by meteorological conditions and sanitary environment. The operation of SEPCD is to advance the concept of 
green drying technology in terms of energy saving, active compounds maintenance and bactericidal effect during drying process. 
The results showed that, by using SEPCD at 45℃, the total bacterial count of dried roselle was average below 5 CFU/g which is 
much lower than that dried by commercial hot-air-drying. The concentrations of anthocyanins, total polyphenols, and flavonoids 
were 6.31±0.03, 22.90±0.99 and 8.07±0.06 mg/l, respectively, which are higher than those by hot-air drying.  
 
1. Introduction 
Roselle（Hibiscus sabdariffa L.）is originated in India and is widely distributed in tropical and subtropical 
regions as a potential new food crop. In Taiwan, most roselle is cultivated in Taitung County, Eastern Taiwan. The 
total cultivated area is about 150 ha, mainly in Gingfeng Shiang, Peinan Shiang, Taimali Village and Luyeh Village, 
in which the Victor, a popular and superior cultivar, is planted on hillside areas from sea level up to 800 m. Usually, 
roselle is propagated by seeds or cuttings and grows on sandy soil. The ideal planting time in Eastern Taiwan is from 
April to May with an annual mean temperature between 19 and 23℃. Blooming occurs in September and October, 
and calyces are ready for harvest in November and December [1]. Roselle extract contains natural active compounds 
such as anthocyanins, polyphenols and flavonoids, which provide protection against oxidative stress [2]. They are 
potent free radical scavengers and tend to decrease lipid peroxidation and damage to cells [3]. Recent pharmacology 
research reports these active compounds in extracted roselle provide antioxidant resistance against hepatoxicity, hy-
perlipidemia and atherosclerosis, according to animal studies [4] [5]. Because of the potential benefits for human 
health, roselle processing draws the attention of many food manufacturing sectors in Taiwan and their related prod-
ucts are becoming popular in the market. Products in the market that are made form roselle or roselle extracts are 
roselle preserves, juices, jellies and jams, wines, vinegars, tea bags and functional capsules. In additional, roselle is 
used as an ingredient in many foods to increase color, due to its substantial natural pigment content. 
In this study, an example of roselle drying is given to demonstrate the application of green drying. Meanwhile, 
a novel design Solar Energy-assisted Photocatalytic Closed-type Dryer (SEPCD, Taiwan patent, No 228585 and 
USA patent No 6941673) which is the scale-up system by Chen [6] [7], is also introduced to practice roselle drying. 
Three main active compounds, anthocyanins, total polyphenols, flavonoids, and total bacterial count produced under 
SEPC drying and hot-air drying were investigated. The purpose of this paper is to promote the concept that func-
tional and hygienic dried products without additional chemical additives （such as preserving agents or color reten-
tion agent ）can be produced by green drying technology. 
 
2. Materials and Methods 
2.1 Materials  
The samples, roselle calyces, were collected from Taitung County in Eastern Taiwan during November and 
December 2006. The roselle calyces were prepared by making a cut around the tough bases of the calyces and then 
were washed and blanched. Samples were dried by a conventional hot- air dryer at 60℃ and at 45℃ by SEPCD. The 
drying processes were carried out until the water content reached about 6％. 
2.2 System Description of SEPCD 
SEPCD uses the heat from sunshine that passes directly through the walls of the drying chamber（150 kg ca-
pacity）. The drying chamber （ 2×1×2 M） has white glass windows in the front, top, left and right sidewalls, which 
enables solar thermal energy to be used to dry foods or the like. A photocatalyst, titanium dioxide (TiO2), was coated 
on the inside wall and catalyzed by ultraviolet, from sunlight or ultraviolet lamps, to have a bactericidal effect. Mir-
rors were attached to the inner surface of the rear wall to provide maximum sunlight reflection in the chamber. Two 
heat pumps（1.5 hp for each）and an air blower（2 hp）were connected to the air duct. Moisture can be condensed from 
the forced hot-wet air flow from outlet of chamber by passing it through finned-tubes of the evaporator in the duct. 
Prior work [7] had indicated that the optimal dying temperature of SEPCD is between 40-45℃, which utilizes 
only the transferred heat from the condenser without switching on the heating system. Therefore, it is energy saving.  
The drying characteristic curves for roselle dried by hot-air dryer at 60℃, and dried by SPECD at 45℃ are shown in 
Figure 2. It shows that roselle drying by SEPCD reduced the water content to 6％ in about 14 hours, whereas drying 
by hot-air dryer took about 23 hours to reach the same level of water content. For color measurement, Table 1 indi-
cates that samples dried at 60℃ by commercial hot-air dryer were darker based on Hunter color values and white 
index（WI=16.57±0.14）which is higher than did the SEPCD drying （WI=18.63±0.40. Dried samples can be ground 
to powder for further processing purposes. Concentrations of active compounds of samples dried by SEPC drying are 
higher, as shown in Table 2. It proved that low temperature drying can maintain more antioxidants. For total bacterial 
count, the samples dried by SEPC got average below 5 CFU/g which is much lower than that dried by commercial 
hot-air-drying which was between in 85-100 CFU/g and lower than raw samples without drying which was between 
in 112-120 CFU/g. In other words, it demonstrated that in this example study photocatalyst （TiO2）took effect in 
closed-type cabinet to produce more hygienic dried raw material. 
 
4. Conclusions 
SEPCD was designed to advance the concept of “Green Drying”. It showed good performance in energy sav-
ing, maintenance of active compound, and sterilizing. If the raw materials were obtained by organic cultivation, the 
process would be called “Organic Green Drying”. A similar operation can be extended to other aquacultural or 
agro-food products to obtain high quality and dried products safely.

INTRODUCTION 
Pomelo (Citrus grandis Osbeck) is belong to the Citrus family which is a seasonal fruit in

hypolipidaemic, hypoglycaemic efficacies (Jung et al., 2006 [2]). 
It has been reported that hydroxylated flavanones of citrus flavanones produced from hes-
peridin or naringin by Aspergillus saitoi, have demonstrated higher antioxidant capacity (Miyake 
et al., 2003 [8]; Kim et al., 2009 [9]) as well as higher antidiabetic activity (Kim et al., 2009 [9]) 
than hesperidin and naringin.

and Hashinaga, 2005 [10]) and antimicrobial activity (Mokbel and Hashinaga, 2006 [11]). It has 
been proved that some citrus species such as orange, the peel has higher antioxidant bioactivity 
than the pulp (Carme Garau et al., 2007 [12]). 
As such, in some regions, citrus peels are sun dried for consumption or used as spices, food 
ingredients etc. The peel of pomelo after drying is rich in limonene, pinene, myrcene, caryo-
phyllene, germacrene-D, and other aroma components which has been confirmed by some pre-
liminary experimental works. It is noteworthy that some of these compounds do not exist in fresh 
pomelo peel or exist in a small amount in the fresh peel, but are induced and enriched during 
drying. This is due to the fact the thermal stress occurs during drying tends to shrink and crack 
the peel, which in turn breaks the oil glands in the peel. The breakage of oil glands releases aroma 
compounds [12,13]. These aroma compounds are then formed other functional aroma derivatives 
resultant from epidermal enzyme reaction . These aroma components also have a wide range of 
biological effects mentioned above. As such, it is vital to the pomelo peel processing industry to 
apply suitable technique to retain the aroma components which are mostly volatile and tends to

Pretreatment 
Three pretreatment methods were employed namely perforation of pomelo fruit, hot water 
blanching and heat pretreatment using steam. 
 Perforation of whole pomelo fruit was carried out by punching a 3.0 cm diameter hole 
along the central axis through the pomelo fruit using a stainless steel puncher. The hole at the 
center of the pomelo fruit allowed faster water removal. Hot water blanching of whole pomelo 
fruit was carried out in a hot water bath at 80 oC. for 10 min. Hot water blanching shorten the 
pomelo peel and may induce more volatile compounds. High temperature pretreatment of pomelo 
fruit was carried out using superheated steam for 5 min. The purpose to apply superheated steam 
pretreatment is also to induce more volatile compounds. All treated pomelo fruits were then sub-
jected to drying.

Currently, there are lots of agricultural products still uses suns 
drying for drying their product. One of the reasons is that some com-
pounds can be induced during sun drying. However, due to the mete-
orological and sanitarian conditions of sun drying in Taiwan, high 
quality of dried products could not be obtained. In this work, a patented 
solar energy-assisted photocatalytic closed-type dryer (SEPCD) was 
used to study water content and drying rate, chemical characteristic of 
dried Chen Pee under different drying process such as sun drying, 
hot-air drying and SEPCD intermittent drying. Finally, a super critical 
carbon dioxide extraction was used to investigate the aroma content, 
compounds and their antioxidant capability. The results showed that it 
took 32 days to dry Chen Pee with the water content below to 13% by 
SEPCD intermittent drying. This process can moderate the drying 
process and can enhance the flavor and taste of the dried products. It 
also would help the shelf life. For the bacterial count, by coating with 
photocatalyst on inner glasses of drying chamber, it did reduce the 
bacterial count below to 5 cfu/g, in which that would contribute to ex-
tend the shelf life and ensure the food sanitary. 5.63％ essential oil 
content of Chen Pee was extracted by super critical carbon dioxide, 
which is higher than other drying processes. Therefore, it is sure that 
SEPCD intermittent drying can be regarded as a fine model for dried 
products with high quality, activity and aroma. By Taguchi method, a 
set of optimal conditions, RH 20%, drying temperature 40±3℃ and 
flow speed 2 m/s, was obtained for high quality Chen Pee. For sensory 
evaluation on the appearance and color, less browning effect caused by 
SEPCD drying. For aroma, due to low temperature drying by SEPCD, 
more fragrance can be maintained instead of evaporation in hot-air 
drying process. For the antioxidant analysis, though the content of total 
phenol and flavonoids are less than the hot-air drying, more these 
compounds will be released after three-month storage. In other words, 
the longer the storage, the higher the antioxidant and higher capability 
of free radical scavenging ability than it was by hot-air drying. Sum-
mary, by using SEPCD drying with intermittent method, high quality 
with fragrance dried product could be obtained. This process can be 
used for other agri-food drying to obtain high antioxidant products.

Pretreatment 
Three pretreatment methods were employed namely perforation of pomelo fruit, hot water 
blanching and heat pretreatment using steam. 
 Perforation of whole pomelo fruit was carried out by punching a 3.0 cm diameter hole along 
the central axis through the pomelo fruit using a stainless steel puncher. The hole at the center of the 
pomelo fruit allowed faster water removal. Hot water blanching of whole pomelo fruit was carried 
out in a hot water bath at 80 oC. for 10 min. Hot water blanching shorten the pomelo peel and may 
induce more volatile compounds. High temperature pretreatment of pomelo fruit was carried out 
using superheated steam for 5 min. The purpose to apply superheated steam pretreatment is also to 
induce more volatile compounds. All treated pomelo fruits were then subjected to drying. 
 
Drying 
Two drying methods were employed in this study. In intermittent hot air drying, hot air dryer 
(Sdom Apparatus Mfg. company, Taiwan, 220 V, 33 A) was used. Temperature range of the dryer is 
between room temperature to 200 oC. The intermittent hot air drying was carrie out at three 
temperatures namely 40oC, 50oC and 60oC. In the intermittent heat pump drying, a solar assisted 
heat pump dryer (Taiwan patent no. 219833, USA patent no. 6941673) was used. Figure 2 illustrates 
Devices Inc., Pullman, Washington, USA). Aw was measured hourly by removing a sample from the 
dryers until it reached 0.4 
 
COLOR ASSESSMENT 
Hunter L*a*b* values of dried samples were assessed by a color meter (CDM-08, Laiko Co., 
Ltd., Tokyo, Japan). The L* value is used as an indicator of brightness, a* to indicate chromaticity 
on a green (-) to red (+) axis; and b* to indicate chromaticity on a blue (-) to yellow (+) axis. The 
calibrations of color difference meter for L*a*b* are 90.43, 0.52 and 3.25, respectively. Meanwhile, 
these values could be converted into a white index which formula is which is given in eq (1). The 
white index is used to indicate the degree of browning after drying.

AROMA COMPOUNDS ANALYSIS 
 
All Solid-phase microextraction (SPME) fibers and holders used in this study were commercially 
available from Supelco (Bellefonte, PA, USA). To ensure there was no carryover of analysis from 
previous extractions, blank runs were completed at least once daily before use of any fibers for 
sampling. These fibers were exposed to the air with Pomelo powder for 30 min in the bottle, and 
was analysis by Gas chromatography-mass spectrometry (GC-MS, Hewlett-Packard 6890 SERIES 
II, Japan ). The injected temperature is 200 ℃,and raised rate 5℃/min to test.

the pomelo (Fig. 5). The different volatile aroma compounds of dried pomelos were extracted by 
solid phase micro-extraction (SPME) and analyzed by gas chromatography mass spectrometry 
(GC-MS) (Fig 6). The result shows that the dried pomelo without a hole punched and dried by 50℃ 
hot-air with superheated steam pre-heat treatment at 145℃ for 5 min was rich in aroma component 
than the sample dried at 60℃. Superheated steam was used for the purpose of antibacterial action 
and broken oil glands. Under this operation conditions, dried samples also contain higher amounts 
of caryophyllene and germacrene-D. The result also indicates that pomelos dried at a higher 
temperatures, might lose aroma compounds. The dried pomelo under above operation conditions by 
intermittent drying could be used for further processing to make various pomelo teas.
