摘要 
 
本研究主要探討使用 5KW 二氧化碳雷射對 Ti-6Al-4V 及 Ti-15V-3Cr-3Al-3Sn 合金板材進
行銲接，比較板材與銲件經不同時效熱處理之試件，評估兩種鈦合金在不同溫度環境下其缺
口拉伸性質之變化情形。 
在室溫、150℃、300℃及 450℃環境下，對 Ti-6Al-4V 母材與經不同時效熱處理之銲件，
進行缺口拉伸試驗。結果顯示，Ti-6Al-4V 母材隨環境溫度的升高，其缺口拉伸強度隨之下降
及延性增加； Ti-6Al-4V 銲後原始狀態與經 482℃與 704℃時效處理之銲件，整體高溫缺口拉
伸強度之趨勢與母材相似，缺口拉伸強度隨環境溫度升高而下降及延性增加。由缺口拉伸破
裂面微觀特徵顯示，Ti-6Al-4V 母材呈現延性細小窩穴狀破裂，隨環境溫度升高，有較大且深
之窩穴狀破裂特徵，Ti-6Al-4V 銲後原始狀態與經 482℃時效處理銲件於室溫下為延性穿晶與
部分類劈裂破壞，隨環境溫度提高，破裂型態轉變為延性穿晶破裂，而經 704℃時效處理銲
件，室溫下穿晶窩穴狀及沿晶破裂，隨環境溫度提升，沿晶破裂型態有增加之趨勢。 
Ti-15V-3Cr-3Al-3Sn 經不同時效熱處理之板材與銲件在室溫下，除銲後原始狀態外，其餘
時效處理之板材缺口拉伸強度皆高於時效處理銲件，經 593℃時效處理之試件有最高之缺口
拉伸強度；環境 450℃時，板材經時效處理之缺口拉伸強度皆高於經時效處理之銲件缺口拉
伸強度。從缺口拉伸破裂面顯示，室溫下 Ti-15V-3Cr-3Al-3Sn 原始板材為類劈裂脆性破裂與
銲後原始狀態之延性穿晶窩穴狀破裂不同；在高溫 450℃時，各時效處理之板材與銲件其破
裂型態主要皆為延性穿晶窩穴狀破裂。 
關鍵字:雷射銲接、缺口拉伸試驗、銲後熱處理、Ti-6Al-4V、Ti-15V-3Cr-3Al-3Sn 
 
Abstract 
Notched tensile tests of Ti-6Al-4V laser welds, which were subjected to post-weld heat treatments 
(PWHTs) at 482 or 704
o
C, were carried out in air at 25, 150, 300 and 450
o
C.  The results were 
compared with those of mill-annealed base metal (MB) specimens tested at same temperatures.  
Generally, the notched tensile strength (NTS) of all specimens decreased with increasing 
temperature.  Moreover, the NTS of the MB specimen was about 11% higher than that of the 
welds at room temperature, but became much lower at 450
o
C.  For the welds, the presence of 
thicker grain boundary α promoted intergranular fracture at elevated temperature accounted for the 
lower NTS of the weld with the PWHT at 704
o
C. 
 
Keywords:  Notched tensile test; Ti-6Al-4V; Laser weld; Post-weld heat treatment. 
 
(1)前言 
鈦與鈦合金為用於航太之結構金屬而開
發，其鈦合金本身優異的比強度、耐蝕性佳
及優良機械性質，近年來已廣泛用於生醫、
石化、車輛等工業上。雙相鈦合金同時具有 α
型與 β 型合金之特性，Ti-6Al-4V 為市場上最
廣泛應用之鈦合金。 
α＋β 雙相鈦合金可藉由不同熱處理程序
得到不同強度，但其熱加工性差、冷加工性
差、硬化率低及超塑性的加工溫度太高等缺
點 。 準 穩 定 型 β 單 相 鈦 合 金
Ti-15V-3Cr-3Al-3Sn 有較佳冷加工性、良好硬
化能、低鍛造成型溫度及強度，經由時效處
理，其最大強度可提升至 1200~1400 MPa，
 4 
而影響銲道品質。Ti-6Al-4V 雷射銲接參數為
功 率 3200W 、 銲 速 800mm/min ；
Ti-15V-3Cr-3Al-3Sn銲接參數為功率3000W、
銲速 1000mm/min。 
2.2 時效熱處理 
鈦合金於高溫下極易氧化，進行時效熱
處理或銲後熱處理時，環境須在高真空下進
行。Ti-6Al-4V 銲件及 Ti-15V-3Cr-3Al-3Sn 板
材與雷射銲件分別進行時效處理，Ti-6Al-4V
銲件時效時間為持溫 1 小時，其試片時效條
件為室溫、482℃(900℉)、704℃(1300℉)；
Ti-15V-3Cr-3Al-3Sn 板材與銲件時效時間為
持溫 8 小時，其試片時效條件為室溫、
371℃(700℉)、426℃(800℉)、482℃(900℉)、
538℃(1000℉)、593℃(1100℉)。試片皆在持
溫後空冷至室溫。 
2.3 高溫缺口拉伸試驗 
測試試片為 Ti-6Al-4V，條件分別為原
始母材( Mill-Annealed )與銲後原始狀態及銲
件經 482℃/1hr 及 704℃/1hr 二組不同時效溫
度熱處理；Ti-15V-3Cr-3Al-3Sn 條件為原始板
材、銲後原始狀態與板材和銲件經 371℃/8hr、
426℃/8hr、482℃/8hr、538℃/8hr、593℃/8hr
五組不同溫度熱處理試片。測試溫度為室溫、
150℃、300℃及 450℃四種溫度區間拉伸試驗，
拉伸速率為 0.75mm/min。

Notched tensile tests of Ti-6Al-4V laser welds, which were subjected to post-weld heat treatments (PWHTs) at 482 or 704oC, were carried out in air at 25, 150, 
300 and 450oC. The results were compared with those of mill-annealed base metal (MB) specimens tested at same temperatures. In general, the notched tensile 
strength (NTS) of all specimens decreased with increasing temperature. The results indicated the NTS of the MB specimen was obviously higher than that of the 
welds at room temperature, but became much lower at 450oC. It was deduced the presence of thicker grain boundary α promoted intergranular fracture at elevated 
temperature accounted for the decreased NTS of the weld with the PWHT at 704oC. 
 
Keywords:  Notched tensile test; Ti-6Al-4V; Laser weld; Post-weld heat treatment. 
 
1. Introduction 
Titanium alloys of α + β type can be heat-treated to 
achieve different mechanical properties for various industrial 
applications.1) It is known the microstructures play an 
important role in determining the mechanical properties and 
fracture behavior of titanium alloys. 2) Ti-6Al-4V, an α + β 
alloy, is by far the most widely used titanium alloy and 
accounts for more than 50% of the industrial titanium usage. 
After beta heat treatment, a coarse acicular structure leads to 
higher fracture toughness but considerably lower tensile 
ductility than the equiaxed structure.3)  Heat-treating 
Ti-6Al-4V in the α-β field results in an increase in toughness 
with a slight loss in ductility and strength relative to the 
mill-annealed condition.4) In the case of Ti-6Al-4V welds 
with suitable post-weld heat treatments (PWHTs), coarse α 
platelets in the matrix, along with thick α films at the grain 
boundaries, are beneficial to the fracture toughness of the 
welds.5)   
In general, the use of titanium alloys for aerospace and 
military applications is below 550°C. The maintenance of 
high notched strength at elevated temperature is necessary to 
avoid embrittlement under loading. In previous study, the 
Ti-6Al-6V-2Sn welds with high hardness showed notch 
brittleness at room temperature.6) The presence of grain 
boundary α layer promoted grain boundary shear, thus, 
reduced its notch brittleness at room temperature for the weld 
with the PWHT at 704°C.6) This study examined the effect of 
test temperatures on the notched tensile strength (NTS) of 
Ti-6Al-4V laser welds and the results were compared with 
that of the mill-annealed base metal (MB) specimen. The 
decline in NTS and change in fracture characteristics of the 
specimens with temperatures were also correlated with the 
fracture features of the specimens.   
 
 
2. Material and Experimental Procedures 
Ti-6Al-4V plate with a thickness of 3.0 mm in 
mill-annealed condition was used in the experiment. The 
chemical composition of the alloy in weight percent was 5.90 
Al-3.95 V-0.11 Fe-0.01 C-0.07 O- 0.01 N and a balance of Ti.  
Tensile properties of the MB specimen include an ultimate 
tensile strength of 962 MPa, yield strength of 830 MPa and 
elongation of 17%. A CO2 laser was utilized for bead-on-plate 
welding in the keyhole mode in one pass at room temperature. 
The welding parameters included a laser power of 3.2 kW 
and a travel speed of 800 mm/min. A copper mirror with focal 
length of 200 mm was utilized for laser welding of Ti-6Al-4V 
specimens. The focal point of laser beam was positioned at 
0.5 mm below the specimen surface. All specimens were 
welded in the mill-annealed condition with the welding 
direction normal to the rolling direction. The welded 
specimen in the as-welded condition was named as the AW 
specimen. PWHTs were performed on the AW specimen at 
482 and 704°C for 3 hr in vacuum, then followed by 
argon-assisted cooling to room temperature; such specimens 
were designated as the W-482 and W-704 specimens, 
respectively.   
Double-edge notched specimens7,8) were used to 
determine the NTS of the welds. For the welds, the notches 
were located at the center of the fusion zone (FZ) to ensure 
the crack growth along the weld centerline during tensile 
loading. Notched tensile tests were carried out in air at a 
constant displacement rate of 1.0 mm/min. For 
high-temperature tests, the specimens were placed in a 
furnace and heated to the predetermined temperature (150, 
300 and 450°C), then held at that temperature for 30 min 
before testing. The NTS results are presented as the average 
of at least three specimens for each testing condition. The 
fracture surfaces of distinct specimens were examined using a 
scanning electron microscope (SEM), with attention paid to 
the change in fracture features.
