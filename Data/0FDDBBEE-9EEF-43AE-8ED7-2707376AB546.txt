摘要 
 
本研究運用傅立葉轉換紅外線光譜儀，原位即時研究二氧化碳吸附於純二氧
化鈦與負載過渡金屬銅的二氧化鈦上，經紫外光照射後所產生的光觸媒催化反
應。利用改良式溶膠凝膠法水解鈦的醇氧化物製備二氧化鈦，以及負載銅金屬之
二氧化鈦。由 X 光繞射分析、紫外光－可見光光譜分析可證明觸媒結構以及對紫
外光吸收的能力。 
在 500℃通入空氣進行前處理之後，觸媒表面偵測到大量過氧化物以及氫氧
基。通入二氧化碳氣體後，發現二氧化碳會移除表面的水份與部分氫氧基，並與
觸媒表面原有的氫氧基、氧空缺分別產生碳酸氫根與碳酸根。 
觸媒照射紫外光後受激發所產生的電洞，與水解離出的氫氧離子結合形成氫
氧基，或與二氧化鈦的氧離子結合形成氧分子，氧分子自觸媒表面脫附形成氧空
缺；二氧化碳再與光反應產生的氫氧基、氧空缺結合形成碳酸氫根與碳酸根，吸
附型式的二氧化碳有明顯的減少。觸媒在紫外光照射下受激發所產生的電子，將
與水解離的氫離子結合形成氫原子，進一步將二氧化碳依序還原成甲酸鹽、甲
酸、甲醛、甲醇等有機產物，但是其傅立葉轉換紅外線光譜訊號並不明顯，由此
推論光催化還原的效率或反應步驟不易由紅外線光譜儀觀察得到。 
由傅立葉轉換紅外線光譜分析的結果，可知二氧化碳光催化還原反應並不明
顯，不過仍可見其還原後中間產物與生成物的微弱訊號，進而推測出二氧化碳在
純二氧化鈦與負載過渡金屬銅的二氧化鈦上，進行光觸媒催化反應的可能機制。

2-1 研究背景相關文獻回顧 
2-1-1 二氧化碳循環簡介 
    地球的碳循環主要仰賴光合作用把二氧化碳中的無機碳轉化成有機物，稱為
「初級生產」，部分死亡的植物細胞與動物排泄物中的一些碳會形成沉積岩，更
少部分沉積碳變成石油或天然氣蘊藏，即現今工業所需的初級燃料；除了呼吸與
分解作用會釋出二氧化碳，自然界中隨著板塊碰撞運動能使沉積碳與含碳岩石陷
入地球內部，以極高溫與高壓使岩石熔化，並隨著火山爆發釋放出二氧化碳；近
百年來人類燃燒石化燃料使得二氧化碳回到大氣中的速度加快，大約是火山作用
的 100 萬倍[1]。 
根據位於夏威夷茂納羅亞火山 ( Mauna Loa ) 大氣觀測站的紀錄，自 1958 年
至今，二氧化碳的濃度持續增加當中，與甲烷等氣體吸收從地表反射向外的太陽
長波輻射，使大氣與地表不斷升溫造成全球暖化的現象，即溫室效應 ( greenhouse 
effect ) ，自 1900 年起的近一世紀增溫了 0.6℃，是 1000 年來最溫暖的世紀[2]。 
目前除了減少運用或提升使用石化燃料 ( fossil fuel ) 的效率之外，1997 年
12 月百餘個國家在日本京都召開聯合國氣候變化框架公約締約國大會，會中通過
京都議定書 ( Kyoto Protocol ) ，於 2005 年 2 月起正式生效，適用至 2012 年，用
以控制與降低二氧化碳的排放[3]。地球暖化與人類以石化燃料為主要能源有著密
切的關係，石油消耗約占世界原始能量的 40%，根據現在的生產量來估計，大約
40 年後石油將開始枯竭，所以尋求其他乾淨的替代能源將是人類現今最重要的課
題[4]。如能把環境中過多的二氧化碳轉化成有經濟效應的化學原料與燃料，將是
一舉數得的方法。

上的甲酸鹽類氫化，在TiO2上的甲酸鹽類將會遷移到Cu-TiO2的界面或Cu上之後
再被氫化，但是同時氫氣在Cu上裂解成氫原子後，快速地溢滑到TiO2上，TiO2表
面佈滿氫化物，卻抑制了TiO2吸附碳酸鹽類與甲酸鹽類的能力。由於Cu與TiO2的
協同作用大大提升氫氣活化的能力，一但CO2吸附在Cu上很快就會被氫化成甲酸
鹽類，之後接著氫化成CO或甲醇，所以其TOF最高，但是甲酸鹽類進一步氫化的
步驟，由於Cu與TiO2之間某種協同作用(synergistic)，使得反應趨向生成CO而抑
制了生成甲醇。

(baseline correct)的步驟；僅 XRD 圖譜稍微作平滑化。本實驗所論及觸媒表面的
物種結構與吸附情形將在附錄二有清楚的呈現。 
 
4-1 原位傅立葉轉換紅外線光譜儀偵測二氧化碳的光催化 
4-1-1 前處理 
首先將KBr粉體在 400℃溫度下導入He流通 3 小時後，所偵測到的IR圖譜作
為背景圖譜(background)。圖 4-1 為TiO2經過前處理步驟的IR偵測圖譜，如圖所示
在 975cm-1與 3800-2500cm-1可以觀察到兩個強的吸收波段，另外其他在
2358cm-1、2343cm-1有下凹的訊號，在 1800-1200cm-1等波段也有吸收訊號。在導
入Air流通下進行升溫處理，溫度依序增加至 500℃，觀察其圖譜變化可以發現
3550-2500cm-1與 1800-1200cm-1吸收波峰消失，取而代之的是 3800-3550cm-1新的
吸收波段產生，此外 2358cm-1與 2343cm-1在強度上有些微的增加。 
在 975cm-1強的吸收波段是屬於二氧化鈦晶格的振動 (lattice vibration) 
Ti(O2)[47,48,68]，另外從 3800-2500cm-1範圍的寬峰是與水鍵結(以H-bonded)的
TiO-H中ν(OH)伸縮振動，以及表面的非定域化質子(delocalized protons)所致
[49,69,70]。假如有很多水分子吸附在表面時，將會以氫鍵鍵結形成聚集，並在
3000cm-1以上的波段產生寬峰[69]。A. A. Davydov[70]認為在氧化物的觸媒表面包
含高度氧化態的陽離子，形成布忍司特酸基中心(Bronsted acid centers)與非定域化
質子(多重中心質子multi-centered protons)，擴大ν(OH)波段範圍，並移動到較低
頻率的波段範圍 4000-2000cm-1。而 1800-1200cm-1波段是由於H2O與CO2的吸附作
用，或有機污染物吸附表面所致[71]。至於 2358cm-1與 2343cm-1歸類於νas(CO2)，
即大氣中二氧化碳的非對稱伸縮振動[72]。

4-1-2 二氧化碳吸附作用 
各類觸媒進行二氧化碳吸附作用的步驟如下：觸媒經過前處理步驟處理之
後，在 25℃下以每分鐘 60 毫升的流速通入純二氧化碳氣體，氣體通入時間為
20min。分別以KBr與進行前處理程序之後的觸媒當作背景圖譜，以便進行更明確
的比較。由KBr背景圖譜可知觸媒表面TiO2振動訊號很強，這會影響我們觀察吸
附於觸媒表面物種的分析，因此另外取前處理完當作背景圖譜，能扣除TiO2表面
振動頻率波段與使用不同觸媒本身差異的因素。 
 
4-1-2-1 觸媒TiO2進行二氧化碳吸附作用 
圖 4-7 是二氧化碳吸附在TiO2的紅外線圖譜(以KBr作為背景圖譜)。圖中同樣
仍可見TiO2表面振動，未結合型態的H2O平移至 1618cm-1。在 2400-2200cm-1有很
明顯的吸收峰，此為氣相的二氧化碳所致[59]，由於通入的是高純度的二氧化碳，
因此很容易偵測到氣相的二氧化碳；另外在 3750-3550cm-1同樣出現兩個雙峰的訊
號，在這附近的位置可能為OH基的吸收峰，但是在通入高純度二氧化碳之後，
很快就產生很大的訊號，根據文獻[63]指出此為吸附觸媒表面的CO2與氣相CO2所
結合產生的振動頻率(combination tones)所致；在 1700-1200cm-1在通入二氧化碳之
後有明顯的變化，由前處理完為背景圖譜來分析將更清楚。圖 4-8 使用前處理完
的觸媒作為背景圖譜，可觀察到 3500-2500cm-1波段H2O的寬峰有略為減少，推測
是由於高純度的二氧化碳含水量很低，相當於對觸媒表面進行乾燥的動作，或者
二氧化碳移除原本吸附H2O的位置，導致寬峰減少。另外由圖 4-9 針對
2200-1000cm-1波段放大來看，1000cm-1以下由於雜訊太大於此不作討論；在
1675、1427cm-1有明顯的吸收峰推測為碳酸氫根(HCO3)所致， 1248cm-1、1222cm-1
較弱的吸收訊號亦是碳酸氫根(HCO3)所致，1581、1357cm-1是碳酸根(CO3)的訊
號；碳酸氫根與碳酸根的吸附速度很快，在通入二氧化碳 1 分鐘之後隨即偵測訊
號，可知HCO3與CO3迅速吸附在觸媒表面上；2076、2062cm-1疑似CO吸附觸媒的
訊號，可能是CO2鋼瓶含帶少量的CO氣體。

吸附圖譜強度與純TiO2相當，不過 1wt%Cu/TiO2所觀察到碳酸根的吸附訊號仍較
純TiO2略為明顯。另外利用商用觸媒Wako-TiO2與P25 與溶膠凝膠法製備的TiO2作
比較訊號位置大致相同，且商用觸媒的碳酸根訊號強度強於sol-gel製備的TiO2，
因此產生兩支新的明顯碳酸根的訊號(1520、1311cm-1)，推測商用觸媒的表面積
較大，能吸附較多與不同型式的碳酸(氫)根，但增加的效果仍不及溶膠凝膠法製
備的 2wt%Cu/TiO2，可知摻入適當含量的銅金屬，經由吸附物的遷移作用可以促
使觸媒表面吸附足量的碳酸(氫)根。 
因此二氧化碳吸附於觸媒表面，大致上有三種型式，第一則是與觸媒表面的
OH基結合形成碳酸氫根[92]，第二是與氧缺陷結合形成碳酸根[85]，第三即是CO2
直接吸附於觸媒表面[63]。如圖 4-22 所示。

關於二氧化碳進行還原反應的部分，在文獻中利用紅外線光譜偵測二氧化碳
還原的情形，大部分都是加氫氣進行氫化反應，碳酸(氫)根吸附於觸媒表面之後，
在與氫氣裂解後的氫原子進行氫化反應，依序氫化還原為甲酸鹽、甲醛、甲醇甚
至甲烷[59,60,62,92]。另外他人先前的研究對於光催化還原二氧化碳作 FTIR 偵測
圖譜分析，會添加其他用以抓取電洞的有機物質(sacrificial electron donor)如甲
醇、異丙醇[63,98]，其目的是還原物質提供電子與電洞結合，避免電洞進行氧化
反應，如此無法確知其是否為有機物與水的重組反應，或是光觸媒催化二氧化碳
反應。因此本研究單純以純二氧化碳與觸媒表面上所含的水份進行光催化反應，
以確保其有機產物為二氧化碳還原的結果。 
本研究藉由還原CO2後的有機產物微弱訊號，可以推論CO2還原反應可能機
制。還原產物中 2wt%Cu/TiO2觸媒上的H2CO與H2COO、1wt%Cu/TiO2觸媒上的
H2COO、Wako-TiO2觸媒上的H2COO、P25 上的HCOO等訊號較明顯，藉由觀察
到以上中間還原產物，推論光催化還原反應與氫化二氧化碳反應類似，即從吸附
型式的CO2依序氫化為HCOO、H2COO、H2CO、CH3O-M等，FTIR也有觀察到
HCOOCH3的HCOO訊號(1403cm-1)，應為還原產物進一步產生的酯化反應。基於
較明顯中間產物排序出還原程度依序為：2wt%Cu/TiO2觸媒>1wt%Cu/TiO2觸媒
>Wako-TiO2觸媒>P25 觸媒>TiO2觸媒，推測其中銅金屬扮演的角色是延長電子電
洞再結合時間，並幫助電子傳達至反應中間物上[96]，或銅金屬抓取電子後與表
面的H+形成H原子，再藉由Cu與TiO2的協同作用使得表面反應中間物更具活性，
促使光催化還原二氧化碳反應的進行，即Cu上連結H原子，促進二氧化碳與反應
中間的氫化，如圖 4-26 所示。

A Novel Optical-fiber Photoreactor for CO2 photocatalytic Reduction 
 
Tzu-Hua Wua, Jeffrey C. S. Wu*, 
Department of Chemical Engineering 
National Taiwan University, Taipei, Taiwan 10617 
 
 
Abstract 
 
An optical-fiber photoreactor, comprised of 216 catalyst-coated fibers, was designed and 
assembled to transmit and spread light uniformly inside the reactor. The power loss of light 
transmission inside an optical fiber was calculated using beam propagation method. The 
optimum length of optical fiber was estimated to be near 11 cm long in order to entirely 
spread out light energy over surface catalyst. Vapor-phase CO2 was photocatalytically 
reduced to methanol using the photoreactor under UV irradiation in a steady-state flow 
system. The solutions of metal-loaded titania were prepared by thermal hydrolysis method. 
Metal-loaded TiO2 film was coated on optical fibers by dip-coating method. TiO2, Cu/TiO2 
and Ag/TiO2 films were uniformly on the fibers and their thicknesses ranged from 27 to 33 
nm. The films consisted of very fine spherical particles with diameters of 10-20 nm. The 
XRD spectra indicated anatase phase for all films. Methanol yield increased with UV 
irradiative intensity. Maximum methanol rate was 4.12 µmole/g-cat•hr using 1.0 wt%-
Ag/TiO2 catalyst at 1.13 bar of CO2, 0.03 bar of H2O pressures, and 5000 seconds mean 
residence time under 10 W/cm2 UV irradiation.

Theoretical Light transmission in an Optical-Fiber 
 
Figure 1 shows that light is transmitted into an optical fiber. The light is split to two 
beams when hitting the internal surface of fiber, due to the difference of refraction index 
between the TiO2 film and the quartz core. Part of the light is reflected and transmitted along 
the fiber, while the rest penetrates and excites the titania layer at the interface. The light 
gradually spreads and diminishes to the end of the fiber. 
 
Beam propagation method (BPM) is used to calculate the transmission loss on an optical 
fiber [14]. Figure 2 shows the power loss in a 5 mm long TiO2-coated optical fiber. The 
transmission loss only slightly increases with increasing TiO2 thickness from 50 to 350 nm. 
The power loss is due to the photo energy absorbed by TiO2 film, which decays exponentially 
along the optical fiber. Base on this result, it is estimated the remaining power decrease to 1% 
in a 11.4 cm long optical fiber. Thus the length of optical fiber is determined to be 11 cm so 
that all photo energy can be transmitted and absorbed by the surface TiO2 film in a 
photoreactor.

Results and Discussion 
 
Characteristics of TiO2 film 
 
Figure 5 shows the SEM cross-sections of TiO2, Cu/TiO2 and Ag/TiO2 films on top of the 
fibers. The TiO2, Cu/TiO2 and Ag/TiO2 films are uniform and their thicknesses are 31, 27 and 
33 nm, respectively, on the surface of optical fiber. The films are transparent, colorless and 
uniformly flat without cracks. Figure 5 also shows that the all films consisted of very fine 
close-packed particles with diameters of 10-20 nm.  
 
The XRD spectra in Figure 6 shows the diffraction patterns of TiO2, Cu/TiO2 and Ag/TiO2 
films. Thermal treatment at 500oC for 5 hours results in well crystallized anatase-type TiO2. 
No other diffraction peak is observed in the XRD spectra indicating that metal (Cu or Ag) 
oxide is finely dispersed on TiO2. Figure 7 shows the UV-visible absorption spectra of films. 
The TiO2 films absorbs light below a wavelength of 380 nm in the UV-Vis spectra. The UV-
visible absorptions of Cu/TiO2 and Ag/TiO2 catalysts are similar to those of pure titania.  
 
Table 1 summaries the characteristics of films included crystalline sizes, band gaps and 
specific surface area. The average crystalline sizes of all films ranged from 14 to 17 nm which 
are consistent with the SEM observation (Figure 5). In general, the crystalline size is 
primarily influenced by calcined temperature only, and metal loading has negligible effect. 
The band gaps ranged from 3.5 to 3.6 eV indicating that the metal loadings do not affect the 
band structure of TiO2.

The heterogeneous photo reduction of CO2 involves two procedures, photo activation and 
catalytic reaction on the catalyst. The factors of photo activation include (1) the excitation of 
electron-hole pair by photon may not be efficient. Most of photon convert to thermal energy 
and is dissipated ; (2) the recombination of electron and hole could be substantial; and (3) the 
effective electrons for the catalytic reduction of CO2 may be only a small portion of total 
electrons due to migration loss. The factors of catalytic include (1) the adsorption of CO2 may 
be limited on catalyst surface; (2) one of the elementary reaction steps is rate-limiting ; and (3) 
reverse reaction may be occurred. Considering the above factors together, therefore, the 
overall apparent quantum efficiency can be very low in the CO2 photo reduction. 
 
Summary 
 
An optical-fiber photoreactor is designed and applied to the photo reduction of CO2 with 
H2O using TiO2, Cu/TiO2 and Ag/TiO2-coated optical fibers. The photo reduction of CO2 is 
one of the best routes to renewable energy similar to photosynthesis. So far, the maximum 
methanol yield was 4.12 µmole/g-cat•hr under 365 nm UV irradiation. Compared with a 
traditional packed-bed reactor, an optical-fiber provides a medium to transmit light uniformly 
throughout a reactor. In addition, a higher processing capacity is possible because the catalyst 
can disperse on a large external area of optical fibers in a given reactor volume. The 
advantage of photo-driven reaction is clearly benefited from the un-limited solar energy. A 
high-efficient photoreactor is the first step toward a commercial-scale application to produce 
chemicals.

2Abstract
A novel boron nitride (BN) supported Pt-Sn catalyst was used for the oxydehydrogenation of
propane. BN is a graphite-like inert support which provides negligible interaction with metals.
The Pt-Sn/BN catalysts were prepared by co-incipient wetness impregnation with various Sn
loadings. A commercial support γ-Al2O3 was chosen to compare with BN. PtSn alloys were
formed due to partially reduced Sn in Pt-Sn/BN catalyst in H2 at 400oC. Furthermore, the
crystalline phases of PtSn and SnPt3 alloys were also observed from the XRD spectra of Pt-
Sn/BN catalysts. However, PtSn alloys were not detected in Pt-Sn/γ-Al2O3 by XRD. The Sn
addition clearly improved the activity and propylene selectivity of Pt-Sn/BN at 600oC. The
more Sn loading, the higher the selectivity and the yield of propylene were. A maximum yield
of propylene (38.3%) was achieved on Pt-Sn(0.75wt%)/BN catalyst at the start of reaction.
The catalysts, Pt-Sn/γ-Al2O3, were deactivated more rapidly than Pt-Sn/BN. The activity and
selectivity enhancement are attributed to the formation of PtSn and/or SnPt3 alloy particles on
BN support. Compared with the hydrophilic -Al2O3, the hydrophobic BN surface can expel
the H2O during the oxidation of hydrogen resulting in the activity increase.
Keywords: Oxidative dehydrogenation; Propane; Propylene; Boron nitride; Pt; Sn.
Introduction
Propane dehydrogenation has been well studied for a long time in chemical industry.
However, this reaction is limited by thermodynamic equilibrium, and requires heat due to
endothermic dehydrogenation. In practice, elevated temperature helps to increase conversion
but also decreases the propylene selectivity and causes coking on the catalysts. Traditional
Houdry process used three reactors to switch between dehydrogenation, decoking and purge.
Oxydehydrogenation (or oxidative dehydrogenation) offers remarkable advantage by no
thermodynamic limitation and overcomes the heat supply by its exothermic oxidation. The
4Materials traditionally used as supports are insulating oxides such as SiO2, -Al2O3, V2O5,
TiO2 and various zeolites. These oxides possess large surface area, numerous acidic/basic
sites, and metal-support interaction that offer particular catalytic activity for many reactions.
Metal oxides have also been thoroughly studied and employed in the chemical industry for
decades. On the other hand, non-oxide materials hold many unique properties unlike metal
oxides, such as high thermal conductivity, acid-base resistance, hydrophobicity and possibly
negligible metal-support interaction. Boron nitride (BN) has been used as catalyst support
recently [9, 10]. The graphite-like hexagonal BN is the most stable BN isomer under ambient
conditions [11]. In general, BN is inert material for catalytic reaction. In a supported metal
system such as Pt/BN, BN has been shown to have a negligible interaction with Pt in the
catalytic oxidation [9]. The easy migration of Pt particles occurred on the crystalline face of
BN due to the weaker adhesion between the crystalline face and Pt [9, 10]. Such effect may
promote the metal sintering and lead to the formation of bimetallic alloy, a favorable active
site for the selective hydrogenation of,-unsaturated aldehyde into unsaturated alcohol [12,
13]. Our previous study indicated that PtSn alloy on BN support significantly enhanced the
selectivity toward crotyl alcohol in crotonaldehyde hydrogenation [14]. In this study, the
oxydehydrogenation of propane is selected to explore the enhanced selectivity toward
propylene by the bimetallic clusters on the unique BN support.

Oxydehydrogenation of Propane
Fresh catalyst (0.3 g) was charged in the middle of a straight-tube quartz reactor with a 10-
mm ID. The catalyst was reduced for 2 hours at 400oC, using pure hydrogen (99.999%) in the
reactor and then increased to reaction temperature under He purge before switching to the
reactant mixture. The reactant mixture was composed by mixing pure propane and air. The
molar ratio of propane/air mixture was maintained at 8/2 and passed through the reactor at
23.8 ml/min (WHSV=6.8 hr-1) under atmospheric pressure. The concentration of propane in
the reactant mixture was accurately adjusted by tuning the flow meter of propane. The
concentration was further confirmed by an on-line GC (Agilent GC6890) before reaction.
The reaction temperature was maintained at 600oC in a tubular furnace. A thermocouple was
placed in the center of the catalyst bed to record the reaction temperature and to control the
furnace. The products of oxydehydrogenation were measured by the on-line GC equipped
with a 30m GS-Alumina capillary column using FID and TCD detectors in series.

Conclusion
This study has presented favorable findings for the oxydehydrogenation of propane into
propylene by employing BN supported PtSn catalysts. Compared with traditional -Al2O3
support, BN exhibits an unique property of minimum metal-support interference thus PtSn
alloy can be easily formed during H2 reduction. The selectivity of propylene is greatly
improved without sacrificing the conversion in oxydehydrogenation. Coking is possible
reduced on BN due to the lack of acidity. Therefore, boron nitride offers a promising support
for oxydehydrogenation catalyst.

Results and discussion 
Figure 3 shows the methanol yields versus light intensity at 75oC. The yields increase with 
light intensity in the range of 2 to 10 W/cm2. Pure TiO2 catalyst gives the least methanol 
yield, while metal-loaded TiO2 catalysts improve the yields. The activity of Cu/TiO2 is 
slightly higher than that of Ag/TiO2 when light intensity is lower than 8 W/cm2. The 
maximum yield of methanol reaches 4.12 µmole/g-cat•hr using 1.0 wt% Ag/TiO2 catalyst 
under a light intensity of 10 W/cm2. TiO2, Cu/TiO2 and Ag/TiO2 films were uniformly on the 
fibers and their thicknesses ranged from 27 to 33 nm. The films consisted of very fine 
spherical particles with diameters of 10-20 nm. The XRD spectra indicated anatase phase for 
all films. Table 1 summaries the characteristics of films included crystalline sizes, band gaps 
and specific surface area. The average crystalline sizes of all films ranged from 14 to 17 nm. 
Figure 4 shows the SEM cross-sections of TiO2, Cu/TiO2 and Ag/TiO2 films on top of the 
fibers. The TiO2, Cu/TiO2 and Ag/TiO2 films are uniform and their thicknesses are 31, 27 and 
33 nm, respectively, on the surface of optical fiber. The films are transparent, colorless and 
uniformly flat without cracks. Figure 4 also shows that the all films consisted of very fine 
close-packed particles with diameters of 10-20 nm.

Conclusions 
The advantage of photo-driven reaction is clearly benefited from the un-limited solar energy. 
Current design of metal-loaded TiO2 coated optical-fiber photoreactor exhibits a good 
photocatalytic activity for converting CO2 to methanol, as a renewable energy. A light-
intensified photoreactor is the first step toward a commercial-scale application.
