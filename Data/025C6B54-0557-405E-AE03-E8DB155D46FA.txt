中文摘要 
微製程技術在目前的科技發展中扮演著重要的地位，在三維微結構的設計製
作過程中，適當的運用電腦輔助設計將可大幅度減少重新設計與試做的過程，並
建構虛擬原型的開發與迅速地評估設計變更的效果及產品的製造與效率，得以加
速實體成品的開發時間。在目前的微機電製程中，主要是利用半導體製程的方式
來製作，但其加工形狀受到較大的限制，不易製作出三維微結構；而近來發展出
許多新興的微製程技術，如電子束微影及準分子雷射微細加工等，其可直接製作
出精度高且任意曲面之三維微結構。但在此兩項技術上，由於必須考量其本身的
製程特性，在獲得一產品前常需經過多次的試做或是以簡單的模型來指引，其將
影響產品開發的時間且提高成本。所以，若要製作出更精細複雜之結構並減少試
做的過程，則必須藉由電腦輔助設計來加速元件的製作；但是目前尚無明確針對
此兩項製程技術之製程模擬模組。因此，本計畫擬將發展一功能完善的微製程模
擬器並針對電子束微影製程技術進行三維微結構之製程模擬，並導入 Digital 
Convolution 理論結合其製程特性以提高模擬之精確度，並配合實驗製作以驗證
模擬的準確性，以提供使用者在微結構設計製作上的參考。除此之外，隨著積體
電路線寬不斷縮小，晶圓表面高低起伏對微影聚焦能力的影響很大，使得晶圓平
坦化的要求不斷提升。由於化學機械研磨涉及化學蝕刻與機械研磨相互的複雜過
程，必須考慮各研磨參數與條件之影響，因此在本計畫中將運用細胞自動化理論
之特性進行 CMP 製程模組之建構，以提供 CMP 技術之發展並獲得最佳之研磨
效果。 
 
 
關鍵字：微製程、製程模擬、電腦輔助設計、細胞自動機(CA)、電子束微影(EBL)、
化學機械研磨(CMP) 
ㄧ、前言 
由於工程系統及裝置愈來愈複雜，功能愈來愈多使得微小化更為重要，除了
以往的半導體產業外，更衍生了微機電系統與奈米技術等製程技術；由於微奈米
系統工程設計的多面性和複雜性，在一般的微製程系統製作上，從設計開始到真
正的最後滿意的產品完成，常常牽涉到無數次的重新設計與試做(trial and error)
過程，這代表著時間與成本的浪費。因此選擇並且適當的運用電腦輔助設計
(CAD)[1]是相當重要的，其可建構虛擬原型的開發與環境測試，得以縮減實體成
品的開發時間。 
近來有許多新興的微製程技術可用來製作 3D 微結構，如準分子雷射微細加
工(Excimer Laser micromachining)[2,3]及電子束微影(Electron Beam Lithography, 
EBL)[4]。由於在傳統的微加工技術上，其加工形狀受到較大的限制，不易製作
出 3D 微結構，而在此兩項新興的技術上，除了可加工出精度高之微結構外，其
可直接製作出任意曲面之 3D 微結構，將可應用於光學元件的微透鏡及背光模
組，或是生醫元件中的微流道上。除此之外，其後續的元件製作，亦可透過電鑄、
模造與奈米壓印等技術，以縮短製程時間並提高產量，為一具有研究性且有潛力
的製造技術。 
2004 年 Lin 等人[5]利用 EBL 技術以 SU-8 光阻製作出微透鏡與微流道等 3D
微結構，並以奈米壓印技術將圖樣轉換到另一個高分子材料上，已獲得良好之結
果。然而，由於 EBL 製程技術較為複雜且缺乏適當的 CAD 工具，使其仍難以利
用此技術建構出要求之結構，在獲得一產品前常需經過多次的試做或是以簡單的
模型來指引，其將影響產品開發的時間且提高成本。所以，若要製作出更精細複
雜之結構並減少試做的過程，則必須藉由 CAD 來加速元件的製作，因此，本計
畫將藉由實驗結果並導入 Digital Convolution[6]發展一 EBL 製程模組，進行 3D
微結構之製程模擬，並與實驗驗證模擬的精確性，以提供使用者在微結構設計製
作上的參考。 
除此之外，在半導體製程技術中，晶圓表面高低起伏對微影聚焦能力的影響
很大，使得晶圓平坦化的要求不斷提升。化學機械研磨(Chemical Mechanical 
Polishing, CMP)[7]於半導體元件製程技術中已被公認為最有效之全面性平坦化
技術，但由於其涉及到固體力學、流體力學、材料與化學蝕刻等複雜過程，必須
考慮各研磨參數與條件之影響，因此，在本計畫中整合各領域之分析結果，考慮
運動學、有限元素力學分析、流體壓力與實驗參數等，配合細胞自動機理論
(Cellular Automata, CA) [8]針對 Wafer level CMP 與 Device level CMP 以電腦輔助
設計及視覺化之模擬進行 CMP 製程模組之建構，以提供 CMP 技術之發展。 
在本計畫中主要針對 EBL 與 CMP 兩製程技術進行模擬模組之建構。第二章
為 EBL 製程之介紹與製程實驗。第三章為 EBL 製程模擬模組之建構。第四章為
CMP 製程之 FEM 應力分析。第五章為 CMP 製程模擬模組之建構。第六章為結
論與未來工作。第七章為相關之發表論文。 
由於電子束的鄰近效應會造成曝光圖形的影像失真且其製作速度較慢，若藉
由電腦輔助設計進行製程模擬將可避免不必要使試誤實驗浪費；因此，本研究將
利用電子束劑量的疊加及電子束劑量和光阻高度的關係，來模擬預測所製作出的
3D 結構表面輪廓，透過實驗建立電子束劑量和光阻高度的關係以建構一快速且
精確之模擬模組。 
 
 
2.2 電子束微影實驗 
本研究從 Microchem company (MA, USA)獲得 SU-8 光阻，並施加電子束於
SU-8 光阻上來製作所設計之 3D 圖形。SU-8 光阻的成份為 50–70% epoxy resin，
25–50% γ-butyrolactone 以及 1–5%的 propylene carbonate and triarylsulfonium 
hexafluoroantimonate salts。SU-8 顯影劑的成份為 98–100% 1-methoxy-2-propyl 
acetate solution。電子束曝光系統為 Leica Weprint Model-200 stepper (Jena, 
Germany)，其可變式形狀電子束(shaped electron-beam)的能量為 40KV，電子束
的形狀尺寸可為 20nm~4μm，電子束的電流為 40 A/cm2。  
首先將 SU-8 光阻以 6000rpm 旋轉塗佈於六吋晶圓上，厚度約為 17μm，接
著再以 65oC 進行軟烤 10 分鐘，接著再以 90oC 軟烤 2 分鐘。接下來將完成 SU-8
光阻塗怖的晶圓送至電子束曝光系統，藉由改變電子束的直徑尺寸以及電子束的
劑量在 SU-8 光阻上曝光出不同的高度形狀，但不做曝後烤，再以顯影劑將圖形
顯影出來，並以 150oC 進行 5 分鐘硬烤，即可完成 3D 微結構之製作。 
接下來針對微結構進行量測，藉由表面輪廓量測儀(surface profile) (Profiler 
P-10) (TENCOR, USA)來獲得不同的電子束尺寸和不同的電子束劑量與 SU-8 光
組的高度關係，如圖 2.2 所示。利用電子束劑量與 SU-8 光阻的高度關係，將表
面輪廓轉換成電子束 spot size 的劑量分佈的核心函數(kernel function)，如圖 2.3
所示，如此即可獲得模擬模型所需要的參數。

z 電子束移動距離與電子束尺寸對結構表面粗糙度之影響 
在EBL製程中，電子束之移動將可能影響到結構表面形貌與製程效率。因
此，電子束之移動距離為一關鍵之決定參數。如圖3.7所示為一線結構之製程模
擬，其改變不同之電子束移動距離與電子束尺寸探討其對結構表面粗糙度之影
響。由模擬結果可發現，隨著移動距離的增加，其表面粗糙度則愈差，其主要是
因為電子束劑量亦會隨移動距離的增加使其分佈愈不均勻所導致。 
此外，由圖3.7b可知，當Ns*超過0.8時其表面粗糙度會快速增高，因此若要
得到較低的表面粗糙度，則其電子束移動距離必須小於其所對應之電子束尺寸之
80%。 
 
z 電子束曝光時間對結構表面粗糙度之影響 
除了電子束移動距離外，電子束之曝光時間亦是一重要參數，其將影響電子
曝光強度。如圖3.8所示為一線結構在不同曝光時間下之製程模擬。由模擬結果
可知，當曝光時間愈短且移動距離愈大時其表面粗糙度則愈差。其主要原因為曝
光強度較弱，導致電子束劑量分佈不均勻。因此，根據模擬結果，當Td*大於1且
Ns*小於0.8時可獲得較佳之表面形貌。 
 
 
 
 
3.4 本章結論 
在本計畫中，以 Digital Convolution 法進行 EBL 製程模擬模組之建構，並利
用電子束劑量的疊加及電子束劑量和光阻高度的關係建立模擬資料庫，進行模擬
預測所製作之 3D 微結構，目前已獲得良好之模擬結果，期望能提供使用者在微
結構設計製作上的參考。本研究於 EBL 製程模擬模組所發表之論文列於第七
章，詳細內容請參考附錄 A。 
 
 
 
提升 CMP 製程技術以獲得最佳化之製程參數。 
在本章中，將針對 CMP 研磨進行時晶圓與研磨墊之間的接觸形態在不同負
載壓力下考慮不同之機械參數影響接觸面之間平坦化程度進行參數研究，以充分
瞭解 CMP 之研磨機制，發展並評估其理論模型。利用有限元素方法(Finite 
Element Method)建立 CMP FEM 模型，以探討研磨墊不同材料與幾何形狀參數之
影響，並建構製程資料庫作為 CMP 製程模擬模組發展之依據。 
 
 
4.2 CMP 製程之接觸力學理論分析 
CMP 研磨中要考慮的參數很多；若機械性質的參數值(如：相對線速度、分
佈壓力)過大，則研磨模式主要由機械作用主導，其可能晶圓拋光表面易有薄膜
剝落或粗大的刻痕(Scratch)，會導致材料移除率變小；若化學性質的參數值(如：
蝕刻能力)過大，則研磨模式主要由化學作用主導，其可能會使晶圓拋光表面粗
度變大，與出現過度研磨現象。 
CMP 製程最早的研磨模型為 Preston’s Equation，於 1927 年由 Preston[10]所
提出，其表示式為： 
 
VPKMRR p 0=                                                   (4.1)  
 
其中，MRR 為晶圓材料移除率(Material Removal Rate, MRR)，P0 為晶片與研磨
墊之接觸壓力，V 為研磨墊與晶圓之相對速度，而 Kp 為 Preston 常數。本章主要
探討分佈壓力施加在晶圓上對其晶圓與研磨墊之間接觸壓力均勻度現象，因此在
前提假設下將 Preston 常數 Kp 與相對線速度 V 視為常數，考慮改變不同負載壓力
對 CMP 之效應影響。 
一般而言晶圓上給予均勻分佈壓力其結果在晶圓與研磨墊之間將造成不均
勻之分布接觸壓力。Fu and Chandra 探討以一個彈性與彈性基材之接觸問題，此

力，會造成中央部份呈現壓縮現象，邊緣部份呈現拉伸現象[11]，如圖 4.2 所示，
此現象形成所謂的邊緣效應，使晶圓外圈造成過研磨的情形，影響平坦化處理。

5.1 前言 
在目前的 CMP 技術中，其因接觸壓力分佈不均，所造成之平坦度與均勻度
不佳的問題，一直是代工大廠所困擾的問題；而此方面的研究，在運動學領域，
其僅針對晶片與研磨墊間之相對速度對研磨率的影響，而在材料力學領域卻又著
重於壓力分佈的影響；因此，若能同時考慮各種不同之製程參數，並以電腦輔助
設計及視覺化模擬，其將有助於 CMP 製程技術之發展。本研究即將發展一 CMP
製程模擬模組，整合各製程參數及視覺化之模擬，提升 CMP 製程技術以獲得最
佳化之製程參數。 
由於在 CMP 技術進行晶圓平坦化之製程中，必須考慮到諸多的問題與細
節，在實際的製程時，更是造成許多的困擾，因此亦有許多的 CMP 研磨機制模

六、結論與未來工作 
適當的運用電腦輔助設計(CAD)，其可建構虛擬原型的開發與環境測試，得
以縮減實體成品的開發時間。在本計畫中，分別運用 Digital Convolution 法與細
胞自動機(CA)法進行製程模擬模組之建構。 
在本計畫第一年部分，以 Digital Convolution 法進行 EBL 製程模擬模組之建
構，並利用電子束劑量的疊加及電子束劑量和光阻高度的關係建立模擬資料庫，
進行模擬預測所製作之 3D 微結構，由模擬與實驗結果之比較，可獲得良好之模
擬結果，並針對相關之製程參數進行探討，亦可獲得合理之結果，期望能提供使
用者在微結構設計製作上的參考。 
在計畫第二年部分，以 CA 法建立 Wafer-level CMP 製程模擬模組，同時考
慮運動學、有限元素力學分析與流體壓力等因素，針對溝槽效應與晶圓翹曲等情
況，以電腦輔助設計及視覺化模擬，已獲得良好的結果，並由微觀角度初步建立
device-level CMP 製程模擬模組，探討接觸壓力與研磨率之變化，期望將有助於
CMP 製程之良率提升並降低成本。 
未來在 EBL 製程技術方面，將持續針對其製程特性與加工方式進行模組之
建構與改善，期望能達成快速且精確之模擬。在 CMP 製程技術方面，將持續針
對其製程方式進行建構，如多區段背壓、decive-level CMP各階段之研究以及CMP 
Pad dressing 等製程技術進行探討。除此之外，亦可將製程模擬方法進行延伸，
針對其他三維微結構製程技術進行探討，如準分子雷射製程技術製程模擬等，以
提供一電腦輔助設計平台，給予設計者在微製程設計或半導體製程技術上之重要
參考。

附錄 A： EBL 製程模擬模組相關發表論文 
 Development of a Digital-Convolution-Based Process Emulator for 3D 
Micro Structure Fabrication Using Electron-Beam Lithography 
 
Hsiu-Ming Yeh, and Kuo-Shen Chen 
 
 
Abstract—Although electron-beam lithography has 
been demonstrated to be feasible in creating 3D 
micro-polymer structures, the proximity effect due to 
forward-and-backward scatterings usually makes it difficult 
to precisely determine the distribution of electron irradiation.  
The process design for creating the desired shape still 
largely depends on a trial-and-error basis.  Therefore, in 
order to reduce the cost and to accelerate product 
development, it is important to utilize computer-aided design 
(CAD) tools.  A method, called as element growth method- 
(EGM), which is based on digital convolution approach, is 
developed and presented under an OpenGL environment to 
reduce the cost and the developmental period for fabrication.  
By using such a convolution approach, this emulator 
converts the processing parameters into a final spatial 
dosage distribution and subsequently into the final geometry 
of structures.  In addition, a physically-based kernel 
function is also proposed and used.  Examples of 3D 
micro-structures such as the micro-lens are presented.  By 
these tools, it is possible to provide guidelines for optimizing 
the fabrication process and to reduce the cost for the related 
e-beam lithography-based 3D fabrication. 
 
Index Terms—Electron beam lithography, CAD, Micro 
fabrication, Process simulation.  
 
 
I. Introduction 
 
Microelectromechanical Systems (MEMS) technology 
represents an opportunity for improving various applications 
in electronics, actuators, and sensors [1-4].  Traditionally, 
due to the restriction in semiconductor fabrication routes, 
MEMS fabrication usually achieves 2-D extruded shapes.  
Recently, with the development in optoelectronics and 
bio-fluidic applications, such as micro lenses and channels 
[5-6], three-dimensional MEMS structures and their 
associated fabrication technologies become increasingly 
important.  In order to create real 3D microstructures, 
several methods have been proposed and demonstrated, 
including gray-scale lithography [7], focus ion beam (FIB)

electron-beam direct-write lithography (EBL), originally 
used for creating masks with sub-100 nm resolution for 
microelectronics applications, has also provided attractive 
solutions for prototyping new generation techniques to open 
many new possible applications in electronics, MEMS, and 
optoelectronics [11-15].  Recently, by utilizing the 
scattering phenomenon during the electron-material 
interaction and a linear superposition approach, Chen, et al. 
[5] have demonstrated the feasibility of using EBL to create 
3D objects by using SU-8.  They then used this primary 
mold to obtain PDMS replicas by subsequent 
nano-imprinting lithography (NIL) [16] and PDMS casting 
processes.  
Although Chen, et al. have developed and demonstrated 
the feasibility of creating micro-3D structures using such an 
EBL/NIL approach, there is still a considerable gap between 
their achievement and the real industrial needs.  That is, 
only the forwarding process (i.e., from a given fabrication 
process design to predict the final geometry of the object) 
has been established, while the inverse process (given a 
desired 3D geometry to find the corresponding fabrication 
process parameters) is still underway.  Practically, the most 
critical part of the previously-developed EBL/NIL process is 
the fabrication of accurate SU-8 molds by EBL.  For 
inverse design, this largely depends on a trial-and-error 
process, in which the cost would be formidable.  In order to 
work on the inverse design, one should get a better control 
of the forwarding process.  In order to achieve this, a CAD 
utility must be developed.   
CAD utilities provide a virtual environment for 
allowing users to design, fabricate, and update their products 
on a computer screen prior to real fabrication.  This allows 
a tremendous cost reduction for design iteration to accelerate 
the product development [17].  A successful process 
emulator can effectively simulate the complicated 
fabrication process and to perform parametric studies 
efficiently.  This feature is especially important for the EBL 
3D fabrication process because this process is extremely 
expensive and very difficult to be accurately predicted.  
However, due to the three-dimensionality and highly 
discrete nature of the EBL process, the widely used 
geometrical approach in MEMS process emulation [17] 
cannot be applied.  Instead, the highly meshed cell-based 
simulation, based on physical laws, should be used.  In this 
article, a digital convolution method [18] based EBL process 
emulator is development and presented.  With the aid of 
this emulator, one could perform design iterations on screen 
to find the optimal solution, and this approach would 
significantly reduce the cost and number of trials in process 
optimization.

B. EBL Emulator Architecture 
The above algorithms were subsequently realized in 
order to form the core of the proposed EBL emulator.  This 
EBL process emulator was written via a C++ Builder 
programming language under an OpenGL environment, to 
provide an user-friendly visualization environment.  In this 
study, cells were generated to define the thick photo resist, 
and the process data in our previous experiment [5] would 
be used as the database for the purpose of demonstration and 
verification.  In conjunction with the physical basis of the 
e-beam process, the database would then be used to create 
the transition rules for EBL process simulation.  
The functional block diagram of the EBL process 
emulator is shown in Figure 8.  First, an user-interface was 
designed for creating 3D structural layouts, including both 
geometric and process settings.  After filling in the 
necessary input data, this emulator automatically calculates 
the required parameters for the EBL process, including a 
stacked-up dosage distribution and the summation of dosage 
distribution, and then transforms the calculated data into a 
structural configuration by using this database.  Finally, the 
results are visualized by the OpenGL environment.  The 
required parameters for setting up the EBL emulator, such as 
the spot size, the dosage level, and the moving step size, are

for the purpose of illustration. Users can setup their layout 
via the layout editor to determine the cell number, the 
cellular size, the trajectory plan, and other associated 
parameters for a particular processing simulation.  By using 
the database and the trajectory design, the program would 
automatically calculate the dosage accumulation and use this 
interface for visualizing the final results.

VI. Discussion 
 
In this paper, the EBL simulation module was 
developed by combining the above proposed experimental 
results and the digital convolution method.  Due to the 
three dimensionality and highly complicated nature of the 
EBL process, the digital convolution method is extremely 
suitable for providing highly-meshed cells-based simulation 
based on physical laws and this approach does not require a 
complex calculation than other numerical methods such as 
the Finite Element Method (FEM), the Finite Difference 
Method (FDM), and the Boundary Element Method (BEM). 
(In fact, these methods cannot even be used in this type of 
application.) 
It must be pointed out here that the accuracy of the 
kernel functions of the EBL emulator is a critical issue for 
achieving success.  In this paper, the polynomial kernel 
function in Section III was used firstly to verify the 
simulated approach, but it may not be adequate due to the 
lack of physical insights.  Therefore, a new 
physically-based kernel function is proposed, based on the 
distributions from forward-and-backward scatterings.  This 
approach provides a universal form for different e-beam 
resists.  By experimental characterization, it is possible to 
extend this form to other materials based on a standard 
experimental procedure.  
In EBL technology, the final shapes of structures 
depend on the process parameters such as the moving step 
size, dwelling time, and spot size.  According to above 
parametric studies, the parameters of the moving step size 
and dwelling time not only affect the structure profile 
roughness but also influence the structure height.  In 
addition, there are many other process parameters which 
may also effect the final structural configuration, such as 
dosage distribution, substrate, and resist materials.  The 
result indicates that without a detailed simulation, the cost of 
the trial-and-error procedure could be very expensive and 
time-consuming.  
Finally, we would like to emphasis that this approach 
(the Element Growth Method) can also be extended to 
model other processes, such as excimer laser 
micromachining and chemical-mechanical polishing (CMP).  
For example, in the CMP process, the wafer can be modeled 
as a set of discrete cells.  One of the key states of these 
cells (i.e., the removing rate) can be determined by a 
transition rule (i.e., by Preston’s equation [31]: in which the 
states of the neighborhood cells, such as the kinematics, 
stress, curvature, and chemical concentration must be used 
to determine the removal rate of this specific cell).  
Therefore, once the kernel functions of other manufacturing 
technology can be found, it is possible to perform its 
simulation result by using the method proposed in this work.

2. The 3D EBL Process
The electron beam lithography process is mainly an
interaction between highly energetic electron beams and
polymer materials. Polymers used in EBL are negative
resists (such as SU-8) and the interaction between poly-
mer macromolecules and electrons causes the formation
of cross-links [4]. However, as schematically shown in
Figure 1, the interaction between electrons and materials
causes scattering as an electron beam enters a polymer
film. Consequently, the expected uniform dosage distri-
bution becomes a complicated spatial distribution. As a
result, the developed resist image is not only wider than
expected, but also forms 3D shape. If both the dosage
distribution and the correlation between the absorbed
dose and the final structure height for a single spot e-
beam irradiation can be decided, one could use a con-
volution (or Green’s function) approach to find the spa-
tial distribution of the material subjected to a series of
e-beam irradiations by linear superimposing the overall
absorbed dose and consequently, the final structure con-
figuration. By using the correlation between the dosage
level and the final structure height, it is possible to con-
vert the dosage spatial distribution to the final geometry.
Figure 2 shows the experimental data on the relation-
ships between the e-beam irradiation dose and the spatial
dosage distribution for SU-8 [5]. A bell-shape distribu-
tion with a wide tail is observed. Theoretically, the tail
could be very long [4] but it can be truncated once the po-
sition is beyond a certain distance apart from the center
for the purpose of performing mathematical simulation.
This simplification could accelerate the modeling process
with a reasonable accuracy.

the remaining height of SU-8 structure after develop-

Finite-element analysis on wafer-level CMP contact stress:
reinvestigated issues and the effects of selected process
parameters
K.-S. Chen & H.-M. Yeh & J.-L. Yan & Y.-T. Chen

# Springer-Verlag London Limited 2008
Abstract Contact stress uniformity is a key issue for the
performance of wafer-level chemical–mechanical planariza-
tion (CMP) and has been extensively studied during the past
two decades. However, contact-stress-related issues are not
consistently presented in the literature. In addition, a number
of topics remain to be addressed in wafer-level contact
analysis. The objective of this article is in twofold. First, it
aims to provide a more detailed discussion and stress
analysis of the inconsistent issues, including the definition
of CMP uniformity, the stress indicator, and the effect of
carrier films. Second, contact stress analyses of several
important but rarely touched problems are also investigated.
Topics to be investigated include: the effects of material
hyperelasticity, the effects of a grooved pad, the effects of
wafer warpage due to residual stress, and finally the possible
advantages of a multizone loading manner. For the first
category, this work proposes a new definition of CMP
uniformity based on the width of the relatively flat zone. In
addition, the contact stress distribution in terms of both von
Mises and normal stress are also investigated and their
relationship is qualitatively established. Furthermore, the
importance of the carrier films is reinvestigated, and the
conclusion indicates that their importance is not as signifi-
cant as previously reported. The hyperelasticity of pad
material primarily affects the pad deformation. The presence
of pad grooves results in a net increase of contact stress, but
the global tendency is unchanged. A warped wafer signifi-
cantly reduces the contact stress uniformity. By contrast, the
multizone loading manner can effectively improve the
uniformity of stress distribution. Finally, the stress analyses
presented are integrated with a graphic user interface to form
a CMP computer-aided design system for further applica-
tions. The issues addressed and the conclusions obtained are
important for improvement of CMP performance.
Keywords CMP. Contact stress . Finite-element analysis
1 Introduction
The chemical–mechanical polishing (CMP) process is a key
fabrication route in state-of-the-art integrated circuit fabri-
cation processes [1]. The global uniformity of the material
remove rate (MRR) is critical for short-wavelength optical
lithography and copper fabrication processes as well as for
device yield considerations. The schematic plot for a CMP
process is shown in Fig. 1. It consists of a wafer carrier, a
carrier film, and a pad attached on a rigid platen. When
planarizing the wafer surface, it is placed on the platen with
a uniform external applied load. A chemical slurry is
sprayed continuously on the pad. Both the wafer and the
pad rotate and their relative motion brings the wafer in
contact with aggregates in the slurry, which generates
multiple actions, including the mechanical shearing and
chemical reaction, to remove the material. The detailed
mechanism of the CMP process is quite complicated and
not yet fully described due to the complexity of the
interaction between the structural, chemical, fluid, and
material domains. Significant efforts have been conducted
to develop a coupled model by investigators such as Kim
et al. [2]. However, most work still relies on traditional
phenomenological observation.

1.2 New process parameters to be discussed
For the second category, the models proposed by Fu and

practical parameters. This is addressed in “Section 4.1.”

patterns for the purpose of slurry transportation. The

discussed since this issue is highly case sensitive. In this

worth performing a numerical analysis to provide a basis
for process optimization that acts as a guideline for
engineers. This issue is investigated in “Section 4.4.”

3.1 Definition of uniformity
Traditionally, the performance index for measuring the wafer-
level uniformity is theWithin Wafer Nonuniformity (WIWNU).
For a particular performance indicator J, WIWNU is defined
as (maximum J−nominal J)/nominal J within a wafer. A
typical normal contact stress of wafer in contact with a CMP
pad is schematically shown in Fig. 2b. Using such a
definition, the WIWNU for the MRR of the CMP process
can be found by using the stress at the wafer center as the
nominal stress and the stress at the edge as the maximum
stress. However, it is well known that a singularity exists at
the wafer edge. That is, the theoretical stress at the edge is
actually infinity and the definition of the WIWNU for contact
stress is thus questionable [11,12]. Here, we propose a new
definition of the performance index for evaluating the contact
stress uniformity. That is, instead of counting the percentage
of stress variation as discussed above, it is also possible to
evaluate the range of the relatively flat zone shown in Fig. 7
as the measure of the contact stress uniformity. However,
since the curve at the “flat regime” is actually not completely
flat but changes gradually, a stress variation range should be
defined. For example, a 5% variation range could be used as
the basis to find the range of the flat zone. The performance
index is defined as the normalized flat zone length, which is
defined as the radius of the flat zone, r*, divided by the wafer
radius, a, shown in Fig. 7. In this approach, in order to
increase the CMP MRR uniformity, instead of seeking
solutions to minimize the contact stress difference between
the wafer center and the edge, the loading manner should be
designed to enhance the range of the normalized flat zone.

material properties of the carrier film have a slight impact
on the contact stress distribution. However, the level of the
effect is not as severe as that found by Lin and Lo [11]. We
believe that this result is reasonable since the carrier, carrier
film, wafer, and pad can be treated as a serially connected
spring system and the reaction force is faithfully transmit-
ted. That is, the total loading acted on the carrier film–wafer
interface should approximately equal the load acted on the
carrier regardless of the selection of carrier films (within a
reasonable range). This argument is supported by the finite-
element simulation results shown in Fig. 9. Another
possible reason for the discrepancy is due to different
definitions of WIWNU. For improper meshes, different
carrier films may result in different edge stress (which is not
correct) and, according to the definition of Lin and Lo, the
stress nonuniformity would be enhanced. However, as
addressed before, the results are mesh dependent. For these
reasons, we believe that the importance of carrier films on
the contact stress uniformity, as Lin and Lo argued, is
exaggerated. The effect of the presence of both carrier and
carrier film is minor, and the carrier film has been replaced
by an air bag to faithfully transmit the applied load acted on
the carrier onto the wafer surface for many state-of-the-art
CMP equipments. Based on this study, the effect of carrier
film is not important and the benchmark problem for
evaluating CMP contact stress can be reasonably reduced to
a uniformly loaded wafer contact with a polishing pad (i.e.,
model A).

VI. CONCLUSION  
The quality of CMP process is critical for modern IC 
fabrication and an efficient CAD system is certainly highly 
demanded. This work briefly presents the basic theory, the 
development, and a primary demonstration of a device-level 
CMP CAD module.  By integrating the phenomenological 
material removing relation such as Preston’s equation, 
contact stress analysis, finite element method, with a cellular 
automata environment, this CMP CAD is aimed to predict 
the rounding, dishing, and erosion effects commonly 
observed in modern copper CMP processes.  Primary 
results indicate this CAD can essentially catch the 
characteristics of a typical polishing process.  Further 
modification of this CAD would enhance its accuracy for 
industrial applications to improve the quality and reduce the 
cost.

5. 結論 
    在本研究中，建立了 CMP 研磨墊之修整製程模
擬模組，針對鑽石修整器之修整路徑與修整面積恢復
率進行分析，考慮修整器與研磨墊之相對速度關係、
修整器之擺放位置以及修整器之運動方式等參數之影
響。由模擬結果可知，當修整器與研磨墊之轉速比為
0.858 時，可獲得較佳之修整路徑分佈及較高之面積恢
復率。藉由製程模擬之建構，將有助於提升研磨墊研
磨效果並延長研磨墊的使用壽命，未來將持續針對其
他製程參數，如鑽石修整器之種類、修整器之作動方
式、研磨墊之形式等進行探討，以增進 CMP 製程技
術之發展。

Development of Pad Conditioning 
Simulation Module in CMP Process 
with Diamond Dresser 
Hsiu-Ming Yeh and Kuo-Shen Chen 
Department of Mechanical Engineering, National 
Cheng-Kung University 
NSC Project No.: NSC-95-2221-E-006-399-MY2 
  
Abstract   
During a CMP process, the pad surface will be 
grazed in absence of conditioning and the pores and 
concaves on pad surface will be filled up with removed 
materials, abrasive grits and chippings from pad itself, 
which results in abilities deterioration in material 
removals.  The pad surface can be regenerated by 
proper conditioning, and the surface structure and 
roughness are determined by conditioning process.  
However, the effectiveness of pad conditioning relies on 
many factors such as grit arrangement, external forcing, 
and motion path planning.  It is important and 
convenient to use a simulation tool to integrate all 
information for performing global analysis.  This paper 
presents the development of pad conditioning simulation 
module.  This model focuses on dresser polishing 
trajectories and pad recovered area ratio.  Moreover, it 
is also possible to link this module with the wafer level 
CMP CAD module previously developed for a more 
comprehensive CMP simulation.  By the tool developed, 
it is possible to provide guidelines for enhancing CMP 
performance and reducing fabrication cost. 
 
Keywords: CMP, diamond dresser, pad conditioning, 
process emulation.
