Abstract 
Extensively used in industry, ammonia is an 
important chemical product with diverse uses. 
It is eliminated in many ways, such as in the 
semiconductor industry, ammonium nitrate 
production industry, livestock feedlots, the 
nitrogen fertilizer application industry, and 
fossil fuel combustion or petroleum refineries. 
Industrial wastewater containing ammonia is 
generally either toxic or has concentrations or 
temperatures such that direct biological 
treatment is unfeasible. This investigation 
used aqueous solutions containing more of 
ammonia for catalytic liquid-phase oxidation 
in a trickle-bed reactor (TBR) based on 
nano-sized copper/cerium carbon fiber 
composite catalysts, prepared by 
co-precipitation method. The experimental 
results indicated that the minimal ammonia 
was removed from the solution by the wet 
oxidation in the absence of any catalyst, while 
approximately 95.0% ammonia removal was 
achieved by wet oxidation over the nano-sized 
copper/cerium carbon fiber composite catalyst 
at 463 K with oxygen partial pressure of 3.0
