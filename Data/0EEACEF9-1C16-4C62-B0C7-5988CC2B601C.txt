IV 
 
Abstract 
Spinal fusion surgery is a standard surgical procedure for unstable spinal motion 
segment such as spondylolisthesis and other spinal disorders. The fusion technique 
provides good support at the cost of restricting motion of spinal column. The spinal 
“dynamic stabilization system” is a new device for spine arthroplasty. It stabilizes and 
also remains motion of spinal column. The representative device of “dynamic 
stabilization system” is the Dynesys system. The Dynesys was proved to reach good 
clinical outcomes. The success rate is similar to the convention fixation devices. 
Dynesys is not covered by the national health insurance program at this moment. 
However the amount of implantation is increasing. The clinical and biomechanical 
research of Dynesys is much less than the convention spinal fixation devices. There is 
an emergent need for the study of dynamic stabilization system for the reasons of 
national health care and management of health resources. 
 The in vitro human cadaveric model will be used for this study. We designed one 
software and four apparatus for this research. The medical image analysis software 
will be used to examine the specimen properties and surgical protocol before the 
experiment. The Fatigue Loading Apparatus will be used to generate fatigue loading 
on the spine. The Spine Hybrid Testing Apparatus will be used to evaluate the efficacy 
of dynamic stabilization system. The Impact Testing Apparatus will be used to find 
the dynamic properties of instrumented spinal column. The Tensile-Compressive 
Testing Apparatus will be used to find the pull out strength of pedicle screw. We first 
find the relationship between the “bone-screw motion” and screw pull out strength 
using two level motion segment, and then use Hybrid Testing Apparatus and Impact 
Testing Apparatus to find the efficacy and dynamic properties of dynamic stabilization 
system using four-level motion segment model. 
 The results of this study will help us to understand the biomechanical properties 
of dynamic stabilization system. It helps to find the reason of surgery failure, and may 
provide the rehabilitation strategies for patient with implantation. 
 
 
Keywords: spinal dynamic stabilization system, biomechanics, fatigue loading, 
hybrid test, impulse test

脊椎融合手術會造成病患鄰近節過多的代償而造成鄰近節的提早退化，縱然

第三年：脊椎動態式穩定系統之動態性質測定 
本實驗使用 8 副約六個月大 120 公斤重的新鮮豬隻腰椎(L2~L5)進行力學測
試，每副試樣包含四節椎骨與三個椎間盤。本實驗依試樣的狀態可分為四個區
間，分別為健康(intact)、模擬傳統脊椎融合手術(rigid-0%)、活動度限制狀態
(constrain-20%、40%、60%、80%)、模擬受傷(injury-100%)。在試樣未受任何破
壞健康狀態時使用轉矩控制，扭力大小設定為在試樣上方施與正負 8 牛頓-米的
純扭矩使試樣產生前彎與後仰的動作，接著利用健康狀態下受到 8 牛頓-米所量
測到的活動角度範圍對模擬受傷狀態、模擬傳統融合手術狀態以及活動度限制狀
態做角度控制，來觀察各個狀態下的操作節狀況和鄰近節的代償情形。本實驗利
用角度限制裝置來控制試樣操作節的靈活度，定義為將操作節模擬受傷狀態時的
活動角度到模擬傳統融合手術狀態時的活動度分成五等份，模擬受傷狀態時的角
度定義為 100%，而模擬傳統脊椎融合手術時的角度定義為 0%，然後調整角度
限制器至 20%、40%、60%、80%(圖十)，並且量測各個狀態時操作節和上、下
鄰近節的活動度、椎間核壓力、椎間孔面積、旋轉中心以及中性區間(圖十一)。

研究計畫之討論 
第一年：脊椎動態式穩定系統之穩定性測定 
本研究使用人體屍骨實驗，接近真實情況，就目前實驗結果發現，當椎弓根
螺釘與椎骨間出現 2mm 的間隙時，其拉出強度顯著的下降，代表此椎弓根螺釘
和椎骨間的咬合力減弱，有較高的機會鬆脫。且比較非破壞性的介面剛性強度、
振動頻率與標準常用的破壞性拉出強度兩兩間的關係，結果發現介面剛性強度、
振動頻率皆與拉出強度呈現中度到高度正相關，顯示可以利用非破壞性的介面剛
性強度及震盪頻率來預測出破壞性的拉出強度測試結果，並證明皮質骨與椎弓根
的面積比與骨釘拉出強度高度相關，相較於過去的文獻資料，對骨釘拉出強度有
更好的預測力。皮質骨與椎弓根的面積可由病人的電腦斷層掃瞄影像獲得，又電
腦斷層掃瞄為脊椎融合術前的常規性準備工作，並不會造成病人額外放射線曝照
的問題，因此很適合做為醫師於術前評估骨釘脫落的風險。皮質骨與椎弓根的面
積比和骨釘振盪頻率相對於骨釘拉出強度所建立的平面型複回歸模型，與骨釘拉
出強度有顯著的線性度，判定係數達 0.75，比過去僅有骨釘振盪頻率單一參數的
線性迴歸方程式，對骨釘拉出強度有更好的解釋力。此平面型複回歸模型可應用
在未來骨釘植入生物試樣後的生物力學測試上，降低生物試樣個體差異所造成的
誤差與試樣損耗。 
 
第二年：脊椎動態式穩定系統之靜態性質測定 
 由於目前對於動態脊椎穩定系統的需求有增加的趨勢，而臨床報告顯示，該
系統對於改善鄰近節椎間盤提早退化的問題並不如預期，故本實驗室自行開發一
套可調整脊椎動態穩定系統活動範圍的裝置，並透過力矩控制及角度控制的力學
測試方法，觀察植入椎節的前彎/後仰活動度對於鄰近椎節代償行為的影響。實
驗結果發現，鄰近椎節的前彎或後仰活動度隨著植入椎節活動度被限制的程度增
加而上升，此現象可能是脊椎融合手術造成臨近節椎間盤提早退化的原因。若脊
椎固定器容許植入椎節的活動度達限制前的 60~70%，則鄰近椎節的活動度可接
近正常狀態。此實驗結果可提供未來進行椎間核壓力變化量測時的實驗方向，並
可提供骨科器材設計人員未來改良脊椎固定系統的參考資料。

二、與會心得 
本人研究領域是脊椎生物力學，所以照例對脊椎的論文情況做了小小的統計。
今年大會與脊椎相關的專題討論有四場，分別為脊椎總論(Spine: #6)、頸椎
(Cervical spine: #4)、椎間盤生物力學(Intervertebral disc biomechanics: #6)、椎間
盤生物學(Intervertebral disc: #6)，還有一場為優選壁報論文而辦的 Spine short 
talk(#9)。壁報論文的分類則沒有將頸椎特別分出，計有脊椎總論(Spine: #29),  脊
椎與椎間盤生物學 (Spine and disc biology, #66),  脊椎生物力學 (Spine 
biomechanics: #67)，椎間盤生物力學(Intervertebral disc biomechanics: #33)，共 213
篇論文。本實驗室每年皆有多篇投稿獲選為壁報論文。椎間盤生物力學的研究大
部分以「椎間盤退化」這個大前題下所衍伸出來。椎間盤基質有三個主要成分：
蛋白多醣、膠原蛋白、透明質酸。蛋白多醣的減少、變性是椎間盤退化的重要特
徵之一，也是椎間盤是否退化或疑似退化的指標。產生椎間盤的退化性動物模型
的方法目前有二，一為穿刺模型，二為酵素降解模型。降解酵素的施打在於降低
蛋白多醣的含量，目前似乎統一使用軟骨素脢(ChABC)。但有個弔詭之處：測量
蛋白多醣含量的方法為量測 GAG 含量，但各種降解酵素的施打都能降低 GAG 
含量。目前也未有研究顯示軟骨素脢僅降解蛋白多醣，而不會影響其他椎間盤基
質的成份。在海報論文區有篇研究以老鼠的活體實驗顯示，穿刺模型的效果不如

Motion Compensation of Adjacent Motion Segment Secondary to Dynamic Spine Stabilization 
1Chou, W K; 1Wang, C W; 1Liu C H; 2Chueh, S C; 2Weng, Y S;+1Wang, J L 
+1 Institute of Biomedical Engineering, National Taiwan University, Taipei, Taiwan; 2Industrial Technology Research Institute, Hsinchu, Taiwan 
Senior author: jlwang@ntu.edu.tw 
INTRODUCTION 
Laminectomy with screw-rod fixation surgery is a common surgical 
procedure for unstable spinal column and assorted spinal disorders. The 
fixation provides strong stabilization at the cost of spinal normal motion. 
The immobilization of fused motion segment may induce the motion 
compensation at the adjacent level and then cause early degeneration. 
The dynamic stabilization system, a flexible spinal fixation device, is 
designed to preserve the normal spinal motion and avoid adjacent level 
degeneration. However, cases of adjacent level degeneration after 
dynamic stabilization are reported, which indicates the current 
stabilization system may still be too rigid. The extent of constraints from 
the stabilizer can be tricky. If it is too soft, this system cannot provide 
good stability, but if is too rigid, this system would behave like 
traditional fixation instrument. The ultimate flexibility for the dynamic 
stabilization system is not yet concluded. The purpose of this study is to 
find the motion compensation of adjacent motion segment at different 
extent of fixation constraint. The results of this study may be helpful for 
the parametric design of current or new stabilization system.  
 
MATERIALS AND METHODS 
Specimens Preparation. Eight 4-level lumbar motion segments were 
dissected from 6-month old pigs. All soft tissues except the surrounding 
ligaments and facet capsule were carefully removed. Specimens were 
wrapped in saline-soaked gauze and stored in the freezer until the 
experiment. Instrument. A home-made dynamic spinal stabilizer was 
designed to control the range of motion (ROM) of implanted motion 
segment. This stabilizer is able to control the ROM of motion segment 
by adjusting the block and shaft for linear and rotary constraints (Figure 
1A). Experimental protocols. The ROM of total and individual level 
(including the mid level and adjacent cranial level) of intact and injured 
lumbar spine under 6 Nm flexion and extension pure moment were first 
calculated from the motion of flags attached to the vertebrae (Figure 1B). 
The injury was created by damaging the mid level bilateral facet joints 
and surrounding ligaments. After the injury, the injury level was 
implanted by a tradition rigid fixation device. Then, an angular 
displacement controlled rotation was applied to find the individual level 
ROM. The magnitude of angular rotation is the one of total ROM 
recorded at injury condition. The difference of ROM of mid level at 
injury and rigid fixed conditions was divided into 9 internals. Then, the 
home-made dynamic stabilizer was implanted to tune the ROM of mid 
level to these 9 intervals at the same displacement controlled rotation. 
The ROM of adjacent cranial level of these 9 setting was recorded and 
compared to the ROM of mid level. Statistics: The ROMs of mid level 
were compared to the one of the adjacent cranial level at intact, injury, 
rigid, and 9 constrained levels by using the independent T-test. The 
alpha value was set as 0.05. We want to find the situations that the 
ROMs of mid level are similar to the ones of adjacent cranial level.

RESULTS 
 During the flexion, the intact and injury specimens were both under 6 
Nm bending moment. After the facet injury, the ROM of mid level 
increased from 4.0 (SD 0.8) to 8.0 (SD 2.3) degree, but the ROM of 
adjacent level remained similar. The loading conditions for the rigid and 
the 9 stabilization constraints were 17.2 (SD 3.4) and 9.7 (SD 1.2) 
degree for flexion and extension angular displacement control. After the 
rigid fixation, the ROM of mid level decreased to 2.4 (SD 0.7) degree, 
but the ROM of adjacent level was compensated (i.e., increased) to 6.7 
(SD 1.7) degree. As the ROM of mid level increased with the loose of 
stabilizer, the ROM of adjacent level decreased. It is found that, the 
ROMs of mid level and adjacent level were similar when the stabilizer 
was set from 40% to 80% looseness (Figure 2A). During the extension, 
the trend was similar to the one during extension, except that the ROMs 
of mid level and adjacent level were similar when the stabilizer was set 
from 30% to 60% looseness (Figure 2B).

DISCUSSION 
This study proved the concept of tuning a dynamic spine stabilizer to be 
feasible. The degree of motion compensation of adjacent level is 
contradicted to the looseness of dynamic stabilizer. We found the 
optimal looseness to be from 40% to 80% for flexion and from 30% to 
60% for extension. The looseness is defined as the difference of ROM 
between injury and rigid fixation. The ROM of rigid fixation is usually 
small and can be concluded to a consensus magnitude. However, the 
ROMs of injury spine largely depend on the degree and type of injuries. 
In order to make the suggest range useful for clinical application, a 
classification of ROM on spine injury may be needed. We also find the 
constraint of flexion is different from extension. A design that constrains 
the flexion and extension in different angles can be considered for new 
implant. Few limitations should be addressed. This study used porcine 
spine instead of human cadaveric spine. The stability of porcine spine is 
usually better than the human one. It is better to verify this experiment 
again using human cadaveric spine. This study constrains the spine 
motion by limiting linear and angular motion of stabilizer. However, the 
current clinical available stabilizer constrains the spine motion by 
varying the stiffness of rod. The next generation of this device should 
consider the tuning of linear and rotary stiffness.
