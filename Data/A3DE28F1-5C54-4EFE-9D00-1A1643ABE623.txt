      混合再生能源發電系統等分散式發電
(Dispersed Generation, DG)，CIGRE 將其定義
為「非經規劃的或中央調度型的電力生產方
式，通常與配電網連接直接加入配電負載
側」。未來 DG 在微電網內的大幅普及，將
影響傳統集中發電的電力系統，對遠距離輸
電線的需求將減少。未來配電系統新增的負
載，相當部分可由分散式發電予以滿足，經
由適當的控制進行諸如削峰填谷、轉移負載
等管理策略，使發輸電設施的利用率有效提
高。 
    太陽能與風力皆具有間歇且不易預測的
特性，易造成供電的不穩定。增加儲能或其
他發電設備來確保電力供應的穩定，除可擴
展再生能源發電系統的應用場所及供電彈性
外，未來智慧電網內加入分散式電源的微電
網架構中，當成電壓控制或功率調節的設
備，皆使此類含儲能系統的混合再生能源發
電系統受到相當的關注。可靠的分散式發電
單元和儲能裝置的有效結合，也是解決諸如
電壓脈衝(Impulse)、突波(surge)和驟降(sags)
和暫態缺電(outage)等動態電能品質問題的
有效方法之一。 
        放射狀的配電饋線網路在接入一定容量
DG後，各負載節點電壓大小會有一定的變
化，對電壓分佈的特點、DG的接入位置以及
注入容量的限制，有諸多相關研究且有嚴謹
的理論依據可進行分析。研究結果顯示，從
穩定饋線電壓的角度而言，DG接入配電網的
地點和容量，是規劃混合再生能源發電系統
等分散式發電最應注意的課題。 
        在掌握了再生能源供電系統發電量易隨
天候變化的特性後，考量儲能系統加入後對
系統不同運轉狀況及電壓穩定性，並兼顧混
合式再生能源供電系統運轉的可靠度下，如
何規劃出最佳的混合式再生能源供電系統裝
置容量，將是本計劃研究重點。 
三、研究方法 
        設備最佳容量規劃化方面的研究在於有
效地分析不同求解最佳化技巧的優缺點後，
進而利用成本目標函數結合不同的系統可靠
度及其他限制函數，來探討有限制條件之非
線性最佳化。傳統的最佳化方法利用數學微
分演算，主要包括如線性、混合整數規劃等，
此類方法常運用多次疊代求解最佳化問題，
收斂慢或甚至不收斂是常見的限制。較新的
智慧型啟發式計算，包括模擬退火法、基因
演算法、粒子群演算法、蟻群演算法等。此
類演算法主要經由模仿自然界中某類生物在
演化或生存中某種自然適應特性來進行最佳
化。此類演算法限制條件較傳統數學最佳化
法少，因此運用廣泛，但收斂速度一般較慢
且對模型的建立、參數的變化有很大的依賴
性。 
        最佳化方法最終結果只是求出的解為局
部最佳化解或擬全局最佳解，而避免陷入局
部最佳化解的可能，需要增加計算時間為代

3.3.2基因演算法 
    基因演算法是由密西根大學 John Holland 
教授根據達爾文「物競天擇、適者生存」自
然界理論所提出，基因演算法應用於最佳化
問題之工程運算，以隨機方式同時產生多組
解，較佳的解被留下運算，經過多次疊代後，
方可求出最佳解，就如同模擬生物間之競
爭，不適合環境物種自然被淘汰，而適者得
以生存並繁延後代。 
    基因演算法以物競天擇、適者生存的觀
念，模擬生物進化遺傳的過程，因此以隨機
的方式決定初始族群，並以複製、交配、突
變來進行最佳化之搜尋，相關過程如圖 4 所
示。本計劃將利用此法求解混合式再生能源
系統之最佳配置解。

4.2  可靠度分析測試 
    設計規劃混合太陽能－風力發電系統

4.2.1  系統可靠度之容量組合模擬範圍 
        在進行可靠度分析時因臺灣各地區之日
照量與風速皆有所不同，必須依日照量與風

設定合理的太陽能、風力發電機組混合容量
與蓄電池容量配置模擬範圍，以使所規劃出
的容量解為全域最佳解。風速與日照量較好
的地區測試範圍為最小，反之氣候條件較差
的地區則測試範圍則需要加大。 
4.2.2  系統可靠度之取樣點分析

五、結論 
     本計劃探討在不同太陽日輻射量、風速
等氣象條件下，考慮了不同系統可靠度需
求，求解更貼近全域最佳解之再生能源混合
發電系統最佳容量配置狀況。為因應再生能
源隨氣候變化易變的特性，調整儲能系統設
置的容量以達相同的系統可靠度設計需求
及控制電壓的彈性較大，此亦彰顯未來在微
電網運轉中儲能系統所佔有的角色。 
模擬結果顯示，若以增加供應負載量及
提昇整體系統可靠度，對照減少系統建置成
本比率的方式進行分析，含燃料電池的混合
式再生能源發電系統配置將比不含燃料電
池的配置型式似乎更佔優勢；此優勢在太陽
能/風能互補狀況好的區域產生的效益更為
明顯。上述結果彰顯未來微電網系統中，結
合了燃料電池的再生能源發電系統架構是
令人值得期待的。

II. THE OPTIMAL SIZING PROBLEMS 
 
A. The reliability analysis of a SPV generation 
To access the available solar generation of a PV system 
in candidate region is one of the most important parameters 
before installation. Because of the intermittent solar 
radiation characteristics, which highly influence the 
resulting energy production, power reliability analysis has 
been considered an important step in any power system 
planning and design process. A reliable electrical power 
system means a system has sufficient power to feed the load 
demand during a certain period or, has a small loss of load 
hours. 
Reliability index evaluated in the simulation is the total 
loss of load hours. LOLH is the summation of loss of load 
expectation events expressed in hours over a specified time 
(usually one year).  At these time, a SPV system is unable to 
meet the load requirements due to lack of power at an 
instant. LOLH is a feasible measure of the system 
performance for assumed or known load distribution.  
LOLH equal to 0 means that the load will always be 
satisfied. Larger LOLH means that customer will be 
suffered from a higher probability of losing power, 
especially in a bad solar radiation year. It is a very useful 
customer-side index in the generation system planning.

solution can make the best compromise between the two 
significant issues: the system power reliability and system 
installed cost.  The objective function of the optimal 
capacity planning problem is to minimize the total cost 
while satisfying the reliability requirements of the SPV 
system.  The objective function can be expressed as the 
installed cost of a SPV system:

      Using the eight LOLH curves shown in Fig. 4, the 
influence of LOLH on the planning of PV/BTY capacity of 
a SPV system can be identified.  Considerable installed PV 
and BTY capacity reductions occur as LOLH varies from 0 
to 400 hour.  It is obvious that different locations possess 
different photovoltaic features.  
     The Anpu site which has the poorest solar radiation 
among these six tested sites causes the installed capacity of 
PV/ BTY to be very large.  Some other locations, e.g., 
Keelung, the curve of smaller LOLH will disappear because 
the PV and battery capacity don’t meet enough capacity 
shortage status in the simulation range. On the other side, 
the sites  at  Chiayi and Tainan  characterize  a  much  richer 
 
 
solar radiation result implying to a smaller installed capacity. 
Comparisons of the simulation results at 25 sites, the Chiayi 
site has the best solar resource in Taiwan. With a similar 
range of PV/BTY capacity, the Dongjitao site is also 
characterized by  a good solar potential.  
      The LOLH curve shown in Fig.4 can be roughly divided 
into two blocks. In the first (left) block, increasing of 
smaller PV installed capacity lead to a remarkable BTY 
capacity reduction, especially at the site of Anpu.  In the 
second (right) block, the BTY capacity decreases gradually 
with larger increasing of PV installed capacity. The optimal 
solution of a SPV system planning occurs at the turning 
point of a LOLH curve, it is located at the overlapping part 
of these two blocks.  
     Analysis of the relationship of PV/BTY capacity in terms 
of LOLH can determine the optimal capacity allocation 
status. Due to the unit cost of a PV component as much 
larger than that of BTY, the total installation cost of PV 
significantly dominates the final optimal cost. A system 
with a large PV size and small BTY size can be prone to 
quite fast charging/discharging of the batteries. For this case, 
the reliability of system would be largely dependent on the 
solar radiation alone. Such design exposes the system to 
instantaneous variations in the solar radiation. Even though 
the LOLH can be reduced by providing a larger PV size, the 
degree of LOLH may be quite high depending upon the 
radiation characteristics.  On the other side, a system with a 
small PV size and large BTY size will result in slower 
charging/discharging rates in the batteries. Most of the 
converted energy will be stored when the instantaneous 
generation is in excess of the load. This LOLH in the design 
may be large due to a small solar array size.  
      Selecting the right size of PV/BTY of a SPV system 
with least installation cost is important for planner. The 
issue will be presented and discussed in the next section.

      The solar radiation is a significant parameter for a SPV

radiation to the optimal size were analyzed at six weather 
sites of Taiwan, Chiayi, Tainan, Tawu, Dongjitao, Lanyu 
and Anpu during the years of 2003 to 2009. the 
relationships of solar radiation and the corresponding 
optimal size of a SPV system can be identified in Fig. 6.

red line represents optimal size for battery bank and blue 
line for solar array.  Two extreme degrees of reliability, 
LOLH equal to 0 and 400, at six specified sites will be 
discussed to demonstrate their influences.
