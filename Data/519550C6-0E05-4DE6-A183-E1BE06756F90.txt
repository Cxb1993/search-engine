試體，分別變化其箍筋配置與箍筋間距，探討上述參數對於撓剪破壞柱之影響。

第二年的研究係為了探究老舊中高樓層建築物之動態倒塌行為，遂參考 80 年代台灣之醫院建築
物，建構四座 1/2.25 縮尺之兩層兩跨鋼筋混凝土構架，在國家地震工程研究中心（NCREE）執行振動
台倒塌實驗，並採用 921 地震歷時作為地震力輸入[2, 3]。本實驗除了可以觀察到鋼筋混凝土構架之倒
塌行為之外，亦可觀察到結構之梁、柱和梁柱接頭的互制作用。四座試體設計變化參數分別包括柱之
配筋形式、接頭圍束情形及柱軸力差異。透過實驗配置之預軸力加載以及側向慣性力的施加，可以觀
察中高樓層建築物其一、二樓構架之動態倒塌行為，以模擬中高樓層受震崩塌之行為。 
試驗結果之照片如圖 2 所示。試驗結果顯示 HCFS 試體（高軸力加載）外柱在內柱崩塌後，中間
柱下沉使得梁版帶給外柱的接頭處承受極大的彎矩，使得外柱需承受內柱所傳遞過來的彎矩，最後外
柱產生撓剪破壞。HCFS 試體之中央柱破壞速度較外柱來得快，不若 MCFS 試體（低軸力加載）之三
根柱子幾乎同時破壞，確實高軸力之施加使構架之破壞序列發生改變。 
此外，MUF 試體（韌性 RC 柱-非圍束接頭）與 MUFS 試體（非韌性 RC 柱-非圍束接頭）之接頭
均發生嚴重破壞，但兩者柱體型式不同，故導致柱體之破壞模式有所差異。MUF 試體之柱體為韌性配
筋，破壞主要集中在接頭上，相較 MUFS 試體，前者接頭變形量占構架整體位移之比率要比後者高。
故就本實驗而言，接頭變形對層間位移之影響不可輕易忽略。

補強，可增加原有柱體之強度與勁度，亦可能使原有之強梁弱柱之破壞模式轉成強柱弱梁之破壞模式。
因此本補強工法適合用於強梁弱柱或具有軟弱底層之結構型式，當柱以剪力破壞模式來抵抗側向力
時，此補強方法可藉由提升柱剪力強度來提供足夠之側向承載力；當柱以撓剪破壞控制且其韌性不足
時，或補強柱韌性對於耐震性能亦無有效提升時，此補強方法即適用於改善結構系統，將破壞機制由
柱轉到梁上，進而提升整體結構之耐震能力[5]。 
本研究進行試驗來驗證手冊[5]建議的翼牆補強方法，是否能夠達到預期之強度與相對應之位移能
力[5]。此試驗包含兩座試體，第一座為單柱原型試體，第二座為翼牆補強試體。單柱原型試體係為對
照翼牆補強試體之用，以確認翼牆補強之效果。單柱原型試體之設計儘可能與老舊既有校舍柱一致，
以模擬老舊校舍柱體之真實行為。翼牆補強試體之翼牆設計理念盡量與手冊[5]建議之設計一致，期能
有效驗證手冊[5]建議補強方法之有效性。 
 
圖 4  翼牆與柱體之界面脫離[6] 
另一方面，文獻[6]之非韌性鋼筋混凝土構架震動台實驗顯示，採用翼牆補強之非韌性配筋柱體在
實驗過程中，翼牆與柱體產生界面脫離之情況（圖 4），因此翼牆與柱體整體撓曲行為無法有效之發展
出來。故採用手冊[5]建議之補強方法進行翼牆之施作是否會發生翼牆與柱體之界面脫離，亦是本研究
所要探究之處。柱牆界面之破壞情況應會影響補強柱體之強度發展與強度後之位移能力，甚至是最終
之倒塌行為。 
 
研究方法與目的 
本專題計畫係執行一座 RC 單柱與一座 RC 翼牆補強反覆載重倒塌實驗，而其研究目的如下所示： 
1. 本研究將驗證手冊[5]建議翼牆補強方法之有效性，其是否能夠達到預期之強度與相對應之位移能
力。 
2. 觀測試體在反覆載重與固定軸力之作用下，柱體與翼牆界面之破壞情況。 
3. 根據本次實驗結果，對手冊[5]建議之翼牆補強方法提出評論與建議，以供參考。

試體製作 
本研究之兩座試體均在 NCREE 外面之空地進行組模、綁鋼筋及混凝土澆置之作業。待接近實驗
室安排之測試日期，才將試體移進實驗室內。本研究先製作兩座完全一樣之單柱試體，然後在將其中
一座試體之柱兩側增設翼牆，因此沒有增設翼牆之試體為 COL 試體，而增設翼牆之試體為 CWW 試體。
為了模擬實際校舍補強的情況，CWW 試體翼牆並未與柱體在同一時間施工，而是等柱體澆製完成約
三個半月之後，才開始翼牆之施工，如圖 13 所示。 
   
圖 13  CWW 試體翼牆施工 
為了模擬校舍實際現場施作的情形，兩座試體均採用立式之施作方式，意即先進行基礎梁之鋼筋
組立、組模及混凝土澆製，再進行柱體與上端梁之鋼筋組立、組模及混凝土澆製。試體所有的混凝土
均採由上而下之立式澆製。

結論與建議 
本研究為了要探討翼牆補強之效果而進行兩座鋼筋混凝土柱之倒塌試驗，一座試體為模擬老舊校
舍之單柱桿件，另一座試體為在柱桿件之兩側進行翼牆補強之施作。就本試驗之結果可得出以下之結
論與建議： 
1. 翼牆補強對於試體的強度有顯著的提升，但在強度後乃至於最終倒塌之位移能力並沒有正面之效
益。 
2. 手冊[5]建議之補強方式在原有柱體與新增翼牆之交界面僅有輕微之裂縫發生，並無顯著之破壞。
此外，在翼牆補強過程中，界面間之打毛與足夠的植筋埋置深度是必頇以確保界面剪力能夠有效
傳遞。 
3. 手冊[5]建議在上下端翼牆與梁體之水平面需增加額外之錨定鋼筋應不必配置。 
4. 基礎的轉動讓柱體反曲點的位置下移，使柱桿件撓曲強度所對應之側力強度降低。 
5. 翼牆水平剪力鋼筋的連續與否以及翼牆圍束鋼筋之細部設計與施工，這兩者對於強度後行為之影
響，仍需要進一步之實驗研究加以確認。 
6. 在評估剪力破壞點與軸力破壞點位移之參數考量上，如式(5)中之   與 cf ，式(6)中之 stA 、 ytf 與
cd ，建議採用原有柱體之數值以避免高估含翼牆柱體之位移能力。

ABSTRACT 
 
Recent reconnaissance reports revealed that elementary and high school buildings are the particularly 
vulnerable structures in Taiwan. Therefore, enhancements to the seismic capacities of the school buildings 
through retrofitting are urgently required. However, there are 3,621 elementary and high schools in Taiwan, 
and the total number of buildings may be as high as approximately twenty thousands. Without careful 
planning, the budget could easily be exceeded due to the large number of buildings. Adopting an effective 
strategy using economical technologies and systematic prioritization is essential for this school retrofitting 
project to be successful. The government of Taiwan has launched a project to upgrade the seismic 
performance of school buildings within three years, and a total of $17.6 billion NTD (New Taiwan dollar) was 
budgeted from 2009 to 2011. The objective of this paper is to report on the strategy, technology, and progress 
of this seismic project for school buildings in Taiwan. 
 
INTRODUCTION 
 
School buildings are public constructions which are densely utilized. The number of casualties that can 
be caused by a collapse due to an earthquake is unimaginable. Consider the 7.6 Richter scaled earthquake that 
took place in Pakistan on October 8, 2005 at 8:50 a.m. (Fig. 1). This earthquake occurred during class time, 
and the collapse of the school buildings resulted in the death of 19,000 students. Among the 87,000 deaths in 
this earthquake, a staggering twenty two percent were students. Pakistan lost a generation of young people in 
this earthquake, a huge loss that can never be recovered. A similar tragedy also occurred during the Chinese 
Wen-Chuan earthquake in May of 2008 (Fig. 2), and it is essential that we learn from these experiences and 
ensure that a similar tragedy does not happen again. 
 
The 921 Chi-Chi earthquake demonstrated that the safety level of school buildings in Taiwan is of great 
concern. During this earthquake, more than half of the school buildings in Nantou County were either 
partially or fully destroyed (Fig. 3). Therefore, it is without a doubt that the seismic capacity of the school 
buildings in Taiwan should be a cause for concern, and that the seismic capacity of the school buildings needs 
to be urgently improved through retrofitting. However, there are 3,621 public elementary, junior, and senior 
evaluation are useful for determining the retrofitting priority of each building. These procedures identify 
school buildings with inadequate seismic capacity, and by using seismic performance indices, the retrofitting 
priority for each building is determined. The detailed evaluation and retrofitting design would then undergo 
reviews to ensure the quality of the analysis and the design, and the retrofitting construction would be 
inspected by engineers to ensure the quality of construction. The results for each stage are submitted into the 
Taiwan School Buildings’ Seismic Performance Data Bank. By analyzing this data, it is possible to 
understand the progress of the project, the quality and specific characteristics of the operations, and to provide 
information for references in decision making. All the stages for upgrading seismic performance of school 
buildings are described separately in the sections below.

Both detailed evaluation of seismic capacity and the design of retrofitting should go through the review 
process. The reviews are conducted at the beginning and at the completion of the job. The review at the 
beginning of the job is conducted so that members of the review committee, the design personnel, and the 
school representatives agree with the planning and extent of the job. The review at the completion of work is 
an inspection to ensure that all the required work by the contract is fulfilled. The review committee consists of 
three members, all of which are selected from the Reviewer Archives of the Ministry of Education. The 
committee’s opinion of the review is written by the coordinator of the committee. 
 
The retrofitting project was carried out by the use of economical, effective, and traditional technologies. 
The overall retrofitting budget for unit floor area was limited to be $4,000NTD/ m2, 20% of NTD $20,000/ 
m2, which was the cost of the reconstruction of the building. The project was mainly for seismic capacity 
retrofitting of the structures. Thus, the budget for repair of the building should not exceed 30% of the 
retrofitting budget. 
 
Retrofitting Construction 
The quality of the school buildings’ seismic retrofitting project should be inspected by the three-level 
public engineering work administrative system. The first level is a self inspection by the constructor; the 
second level is conducted by the engineering unit of the user of the building together with the inspection 
contractor; and the third level is conducted by the government agency responsible for the project. In school 
buildings’ retrofitting project, the school is the user of the building and it does not have engineering unit. 
Therefore, the effort of the inspection contractor is quite important. 
 
School campuses are densely populated public areas, and safety is an important factor during the 
retrofitting construction on campuses. Therefore, before starting construction, a public hearing should be 
conducted in which the constructor explains the construction plan, the work schedule, and the plans for safety 
and environmental administration. The inspection contractor explains the plan for construction inspection. 
The purpose of this is to let the teachers, students, and their parents understand the safety measures during the 
period of construction. 
 
The current status of the retrofitting plan is shown in Table 3. The preliminary evaluation, detailed 
evaluation, and retrofitting design have already been performed on the targeted number of buildings, and the 
completed retrofitting constructions have reached 870 buildings with the rate of completion at approximately 
62%. The present task is to continue the effectiveness of the project, to improve the quality of each stage, and 
to generally evaluate the completion rates of the seismic capacity evaluation and retrofitting work to avoid 
any oversight.

DATA COLLECTION 
 
The establishment of a data bank to record results from seismic evaluation and retrofitting of all school 
buildings in Taiwan is helpful in improving the quality of the plan and the schools’ safety administration of 
campuses. The currently available data of Taiwan’s school building seismic performance data bank are shown 
in Table 4. It is seen from Table 4 that there is room for improvement on the data input to the data bank. 
 
At the beginning, the plan on seismic upgrading of school buildings was started in a rushed fashion, and 
there were school buildings in all cities and counties left out of the planning process. These buildings should 
be quickly identified and added to the plan. For this purpose, the Project Office for Seismic Upgrading of 
School Buildings started gathering basic information about seismic capacity for all elementary and high 
school buildings. Undergraduate civil and construction engineering students from several universities are 
carrying out this census of school buildings as their practical work experience during winter and summer 
vacations. The rate of completion at the present stage is shown in Table 5. The census is expected to be 
completed in 2011.

The school building seismic upgrading plan in Taiwan requires that the expenses of the seismic 
retrofitting construction be within $4,000NTD/m2, and that the budget for reconstruction is $20,000NTD/m2. 
Therefore, in Taiwan’s school building upgrading plan, the project is considered economical only when the 
retrofitting expenses do not exceed 20% of the reconstruction cost. Table 6 shows that Taiwan’s retrofitting 
construction cost was $2,207NTD/m2, which was only 11% of the reconstruction cost. 
 
CONCLUSIONS and REMARKS 
 
Taiwan’s plan for school building seismic evaluation and retrofitting has been carried out smoothly. The 
current task is to continue the work effectively, and should be evaluated to improve the quality. The most 
important task is to quickly finish the census of the basic seismic capacity data for all of the public elementary 
and high schools so that the requirements of improving the seismic capacity of all school buildings can be 
accomplished. 
 
It is hoped that, by seismic evaluation and retrofitting of school buildings, the general public of Taiwan 
would understand the importance of seismic retrofitting. This work may be continued and extended to other 
existing buildings in order to create a more secure homeland.
