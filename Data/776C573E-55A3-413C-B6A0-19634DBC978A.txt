  
一、緣由與目的 
水體因工業廢水之任意排放而污染嚴重，其中重金屬污染因其毒性常危害動植物生存，
因而更加受到重視。以往，處理水中重金屬，包括化學沉降、離子交換、薄膜分離、溶劑萃
取及吸附等方式，但這些方式常受成本高、去除不完全、產生有毒污泥、及能量需求高，而
有所限制(Mehta and Jaur, 2005)。近年來，以生物吸附去除重金屬之方式，極受重視。可利用
之生物吸附劑，包括細菌、酵母菌、真菌及藻類，而這些生物吸附劑之中，又以吸附容量較
大及鍵結力較高之藻類，最具發展潛力(Chojnacka et al., 2005)。 
生物吸附重金屬係利用細胞表面存在之官能基，結合金屬離子而進行之吸附去除程序。
一般細胞表面之細胞壁包圍著蛋白質、多醣體及脂肪等物質，這些物質可提供特定之
carboxylate, hydroxyl, thiol, sulphonate, phosphate, amino 及 imidazole 等官能基，此等官能基可
與重金屬進行吸附，以達到重金屬去除之目的。 
研究指出(Chen and Yeh, 2005)，高錳酸鉀對細胞破壞程度不大，其誘導藻體釋出之胞外
物，以附著於藻體表面為多。藻體細胞在高錳酸鉀氧化之後，藻體細胞外表有釋出物生成，
藻體細胞並有凝聚現象。高錳酸鉀能促使藻細胞沉降率提高，係由於高錳酸鉀誘導出之胞外
物提供細胞凝聚之機制，加上胞外物吸引更多之 MnO2 膠體吸附，使藻體密度增加，利於沉
降所致，而當有鈣離子存在時，鈣離子可作為生物膠體之架橋，藻體沉降性更好。 
因此，利用高錳酸鉀作用於藻類，以誘導出之胞外物，可能有利於藻體細胞對重金屬之
吸附。本研究目的，在於瞭解高錳酸鉀作用藻類之動力變化，一方面希望有助於淨水廠操作
時，選擇高錳酸鉀添加點之應用。另一方面，探討高錳酸鉀應用於綠藻之重金屬生物吸附研
究，希望藉高錳酸鉀預期誘導細胞釋出胞外物之作用，以上述凝聚現象為基礎，探討高錳酸
鉀前處理對綠藻生物吸附水中重金屬之影響。 
二、研究方法 
2.1 純藻培養 
本研究試驗藻種為 Chodatella sp.，屬於綠藻的一種。Chodatella sp. 培養方式如下：將配
製好之營養鹽 500 mL (Norris, 1955)置入 1 L 之血清瓶中，之後利用高溫高壓殺菌釜進行滅菌 
(1.5 atm, 121 ℃, 30 min) ，取出營養鹽降溫後，植入綠藻母液並於培養箱培養，綠藻培養箱
為恆溫光照箱(SGC-10, Taiwan)，溫度控制為 28℃，光照時間 24 hr/day，光照強度 10000± 2000 
lux/m2。綠藻培養過程中，每天加入約 10 mL CO2 氣體，做為綠藻所需之碳源。 
2.2 人工純藻混合液 
純藻培養至 7 天之後，自恆溫光照箱取出藻液，隨即分裝於離心管，以 4000 rpm (3000×g) 
轉速(Universal 320/320R，Germany)，離心 5 分鐘，之後將上澄液棄置，再以模擬實際原水離
子強度之 0.015 M NaClO4 溶液洗下藻體，使藻體懸浮於 NaClO4 溶液中，並調整成適當藻類
濃度之人工純藻混合液，作為試驗之用。 
2.3 藻體細胞計數 
藻體為橢圓型，計數方法以分光光度計 (GENESYSTM10 Spectrophotometers, USA) 配合
顆粒計數器 (Model 8000A, HIAC, Pacific Scientific Instruments, USA)偵測其濃度。掃描綠藻之
最佳偵測吸收波長為 684 nm，配製不同濃度之綠藻溶液，進行光學密度(Optical Density, OD684)
之偵測，並配合顆粒計數器之結果，繪製綠藻濃度相對於 OD684 吸光值之檢量線。本研究藻
體濃度為 OD684=0.2，相當於 5.6×107 cells/mL。 
2.4 高錳酸鉀氧化試驗 
取固態高錳酸鉀 (Merk, Germany) 10 g，以加熱溶解方式溶於 1 L 純水中，靜置 24 hr 後，

三、結果與討論 
3.1 細胞破壞程度 
前氧化劑之添加，於水處理觀點，應以不破壞藻體細胞為原則，以避免細胞釋出有機物
而增加淨水負荷。為此，本研究分別以鉀離子濃度，葉綠素-a 濃度及溶解性有機物濃度釋出
變化，探討高錳酸鉀對藻體細胞之作用程度。圖 4 為 1 mg/L 高錳酸鉀劑量下，水中鉀離子、
溶解性有機物釋出及葉綠素-a 濃度變化。鉀離子在藻體細胞中主要作為酵素活化劑(Enzyme 
activator)，大部分儲存在液泡內，細胞壁表面亦存有少量(Macrobbie, 1974)，因此利用 K+之
釋出程度，可以判定藻體細胞受破壞程度(Peterson et al., 1995)。由圖 4 觀察，在 1 mg/L 高錳
酸鉀劑量下(1.8×10-11 mg/cell)，對細胞破壞程度相當微小。

3.2 細胞表面觀察 
前氧化劑作用對細胞表面結構之影響，可藉由電子顯微鏡加以觀察。圖 5 為經高錳酸鉀

m，藻體四周有刺毛，藻體細胞璧表面為網狀結構。由圖 5 觀察，高錳酸鉀氧化後之藻體細
胞，表面細胞璧網狀結構並未受到破壞，藻體刺毛亦未有斷裂情形。顯示在 1 mg/L 高錳酸鉀
劑量下，對細胞破壞程度相當輕微。 
3.3 重金屬生物吸附動力現象 
若欲瞭解重金屬之生物吸附現象，首先應探討 Pb 與 Cu 之單溶質生物吸附動力現象。圖
6 為 Pb 與 Cu 之生物吸附動力曲線，Pb 之生物吸附速率，極為快速，約 30 分鐘之吸附時間，
即可達吸附平衡。一般而言，重金屬與生物吸附劑達到動力吸附平衡時間，約 1 小時即可完
成，其中前 30 鐘吸附速率相當快速(Tűzűn et al., 2005; Gong et al., 2005)。而重金屬生物吸附
分兩階段，第一階段，重金屬以快速方式(數秒~數分鐘)吸附於藻類表面，此階段與細胞代謝
作用無關，之後，進入第二階段，重金屬離子傳輸進入細胞膜內，此階段與細胞代謝作用有
  
置 。 Raungsomboon 等 (2006) 研 究 藍 綠 藻 (Gloeocapsa gelatinosa) 表 面 之 capsular 
polysaccharide(CPS, 胞外多醣體)，對 Pb2+之生物吸附容量比未有 CPS 者，高出 5.7 倍，因此，
高錳酸鉀誘導藻體釋出胞外物，提升重金屬之生物吸附容量(Raungsomboon, 2006)，此為可能
原因之ㄧ。另一可能原因，為高錳酸鉀之還原態產物 MnO2，由於此 MnO2 之等電位點介於
2.8~4.5 之間，以本研究水樣 pH 5而言，MnO2可能對 Pb 與 Cu 之吸附去除，亦有所貢獻(Morgan 
and Stumm, 1964；Posselt and Anderson, 1968)。

四、計畫自評及後續研究重點 
研究初期，建置細胞培養設備、細胞濃度放大工作、重金屬與細胞間之合理質量吸附比
例，曾使研究遭遇困難，目前已一一克服。截至目前，高錳酸鉀有助於 Pb 與 Cu 之生物吸附，
已有一些結果。但 Pb 與 Cu 之生物吸附上之差異，及 MnO2 之貢獻度，則有待儘快釐清。另
外，胞外物是否於生物吸附上之貢獻，亦期望早日確定。因此利用 FTIR 及 EDX 等工具，以
確定如上問題，則為後續重點，而建立適當之吸附模式，亦為後續重點研究項目。

Materials and methods 
Algal culturing and preparation of the algal suspension 
A common green algae species, Chodatella sp. isolated from raw water, was cultured in a 
media according to Norris et al. (1955). Axenic cultures of Chodatella sp. were grown in 
batches in a 1-L modified serum bottle containing 600 mL sterilized algal media. The bottles 
were placed in an incubator at 28℃ and continuously illuminated with 10000 lux of light. 
Carbon dioxide was supplied to the cultures daily. Cultures were harvested during the log 
growth phase after 7 days. Individual cell size was 3 - 5 μm. 
Conventionally cultured Chodatella sp. cells were obtained by centrifugation at 4000 rpm 
(3000×g) for 5 min. Precipitated cells were then washed and resuspended in 0.015 M sodium 
perchlorate (NaClO4) solution, simulating the ionic strength of source water. Cell 
concentrations in suspension solution were determined with a particle counter (Model 
8000A, HIAC, Pacific Scientific instruments, USA) and by measuring absorbance at 684 nm 
(OD684) with an ultraviolet/visible spectrophotometer (Model U-2001, Hitachi, Japan). 
Aliquot suspensions of constant cell concentrations (4×107 cells/mL) were prepared for 
experiments. Figure 1 shows the schematic diagram of experimental procedure. 
Preoxidation test and sedimentation test 
Preozonation of algal suspension water samples were performed in semi-batch ozonation 
method. Ozone from an ozone generator (Welsbach T-816 laboratory ozonator, Welsbach 
Ozone Systems Corp., San Jose, CA, USA) was applied to a sample contained in a 1-L gas 
washing bottle. Constant gas phase concentration and flow rate were maintained and required 
ozone dosages were obtained by varying the ozone injection time. After dosing, water 
samples were immediately sealed headspace-free and mixed with a magnetic stirrer. Transfer 
efficiencies were determined by the determining the difference in the mass of ozone applied 
to a sample and the mass of ozone in the off-gas. Applied and utilized ozone concentrations 
were determined using the classic iodometric method (APHA, 1995).

potassium permanganate reagent in Milli-Q water and then filtered through a 0.2-μm 
membrane filter (Advantec MFS Inc., USA) to remove MnO2 colloids. This stock solution

Carbohydrate and Protein. 
Carbohydrate concentration was measured with a modified anthrone method (Raunkjær et 
al., 1994). Glucose was used as the standard. One aliquot of sample was mixed with anthrone 
reagent. Samples were placed in a 100℃ water both for 15 min and then cooled in a 
refrigerator at 4℃ for 5 min. Sample absorbance was measured at 625 nm. Measurement of 
protein concentration utilized a modified Lowry method (Lowry et al., 1951). Bovine serum 
albumin (BSA, Merck, Germany) was used as the standard. The samples and standards were 
measured spectrophotometrically at 750 nm. 
Potassium. 
Cell membrane damage was quantified by measuring potassium content before and after 
preoxidation. Samples were filtered through a 0.2-μm membrane filter (Advantec MFS Inc., 
USA). Filtrate was then acidified with hydrogen chloride to pH<2 for potassium analysis 
with an atomic absorption spectrometer (Model Z-5300, Hitachi, Japan). 
Dissolved manganese.
Manganese dioxides adsorbed by cells were quantified by measuring dissolved manganese 
concentration before and after permanganate preoxidation. Samples were first filtered 
through 0.2-μm membrane filter (Advantec MFS Inc., USA) then acidified to pH<2 with 
HNO3. Dissolved manganese concentrations were determined using inductively coupled 
plasma spectroscopy (ICP spectroscopy: JOBIN YVON, JY 38 PLUS, Division 
d’Instruments, S.A.). 
Zeta potential. 
The zeta potential measurements of cells in this study utilized a zeta meter (Zetasizer 
2000HSA, Malvern Instruments, U.K.), which measures zeta potential using the principle of 
photo correlation spectroscopy (PCS). Samples with adequate cell concentrations were 
injected slowly into the zeta meter and measured at least 3 times and an average value was 
calculated. 
Scanning electron microscopy

Effect on cell morphology 
Figure 8 shows SEM micrographs of algae cell surface morphology before and after 
preoxidation. The algae tested have four symmetrically disposed bristles. The cell body is 
ovoid in shape and about 3×5 μm in size. Under preozonation, the bristles broke off and the 
cell body shape was changed. Most cell fractions shrivelled. Edzwald and Paralkar (1992) 
demonstrated that cell surface alterations, with simultaneous NPDOC increase in the aqueous 
phase, can be observed following preozonation. With permanganate preoxidation, however, 
no significant lysis or alteration of cell walls was noted. Colloidal MnO2 was adsorbed on the 
reticulate layer on algae cell surfaces. This experimental result is also in agreement with the 
suggestion that EOM was secreted after permanganate preoxidation, as shown by coating on 
the reticulate layer on cell surface.
