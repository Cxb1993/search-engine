Abstract
The technology roadmap toward smaller structures and thinner layers in semiconductor manufacturing 
directs attention more and more toward yield-affecting influences from the air quality of manufacturing 
environment. The so-called airborne molecular contaminants (AMCs) have become a key issue related to 
production yield. A useful method for reducing AMC is purging of wafer with nitrogen gas. In this study, 
parametric studies on purging a front opening unified pod (FOUP), a wafer box for handling 450-mm wafers, 
are studied by theoretical analysis, CFD simulation and experimental measurement.Moisture and oxygen are 
perhaps the most important homogeneous impurity in wafer FOUP used in semiconductor manufacturing. A 
difficulty associated with moisture and oxygen are their strong adsorption on FOUP and its slow desorption 
due to a very high activation energy. In this study, the mechanism of moisture and oxygen desorption from 
FOUP are studied. A unique experimental setup is designed and utilized to determine desorption under gas 
flow conditions by monitoring the moisture and oxygen concentration at the system outlet. A simple 
analytical technique is presented to analyze the desorption data so acquired, in an effort to normalize the 
results and thus making them system independent. Desorption rate constants and drydown time for FOUP 
surfaces are calculated using this model at a constant temperature.
Keywords: FOUP; Purge; Drydown time; Moisture; Oxygen
