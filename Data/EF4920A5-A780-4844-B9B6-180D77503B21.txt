1. Introduction 
Aromatic and aliphatic compounds are two important commodity chemicals. The separation of aromatic 
compounds (benzene, toluene, and ethylbenzene) from short-chain aliphatic compounds (C6~C8) mixtures is 
challenging since these compounds have boiling points in a close range and several combinations form 
azeotropes. Therefore, an effective alternative separation technique is highly required in industry. The 
conventional processes for the separation of these aromatic and aliphatic mixtures are liquid extraction and 
extractive distillation. These techniques require additional distillation steps to separate the extraction solvent 
from the main solvents with additional investments and energy consumption. Replacement of traditional 
solvents in the separation of aromatic and aliphatic compounds by ionic liquids is an effective method. Ionic 
liquids have been widely employed as potential green solvents to replace volatile organic solvents in 
electrochemistry, reaction, and separation processes due to they are almost zero vapor pressure (Earle et al., 
2006; Zaitsau et al., 2006). To apply these ionic liquids in the chemical separation process, phase equilibrium 
data are very important. The focus of this paper is placed on the phase behavior of LLE for five ternary systems 
of ionic liquids (1-ethyl-3-methylimidazolium bis(trifluoromethylsulfonyl)imide, [EMIM
+

used to determine the LLE binodal curve data.

Extended Abstract 
 
Liquid-liquid equilibrium (LLE) data for the ternary systems water + (N-methyl-2-pyrrolidone, dimethyl 
sulfoxide) + (methyl methacrylate, butyl methacrylate) were determined at atmospheric pressure over a 
temperature range of 283.15 K to 323.15 K. The reliability of the experimental tie-line data was determined 
through the Othmer-Tobias [Othmer and Tobias, 1942] and Bachman [Bachman, 1940] plots. The 
experimental data were also compared with the values calculated by the NRTL [Renon and Prausnitz, 1968] 
and UNIQUAC [Abrams and Prausnitz, 1975] models. Good quantitative agreement was obtained with these 
models. LLE data for the ternary systems were measured by an apparatus similar to that of Peschke and 
Sandler [Peschke and Sandler, 1995]. The composition of sample was analyzed by a GC with TCD and using 
high purity helium (99.99%) as a carrier gas.  A stainless steel column packed with 10 % Porapak Qs 60/80 
(2 m1/8") can clearly separate the constituent compounds of the samples.  Figures 1 to 3 are the phase 
diagrams for the ternary systems of water + N-methyl pyrrolidone + methyl methacrylate, water + dimethyl 
sulfoxide + methyl methacrylate, and water + dimethyl sulfoxide + butyl methacrylate at 283.15 K and 
323.15 K, respectively.  The LLE calculation procedure with the NRTL and the UNIQUAC models was 
detailed in Walas [Walas, 1985].  In general, the average deviations from the NRTL model are slightly 
smaller than those from the UNIQUAC model.  Figures 1 to 3 also compare the calculated binodal locus 
and tie-lines from the NRTL and UNIQUAC models with the experimental results.  As seen from Figures 1 
to 3, good agreements between the calculated results from the solution models and experimental values have 
been obtained for the three investigated systems.
