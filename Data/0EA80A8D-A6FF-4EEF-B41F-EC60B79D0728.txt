Abstract 
    This research is proposed to conduct an 
experiment of building constructions and 
environmental control system based on an 
open building experimental platform. The 
building is designed to provide adaptable 
use. The environment control systems are 
integrated with ground cooling, radiant cool 
ceiling panel, and natural ventilation. The 
results will improve the understanding of the 
feasibility of optimizing a hybrid control 
system. To cooperate with the planning of 
the newly-built project of the "Taiwan 
Building Technology Center", this project 
plans to build and construct an "open 
experimental platform" to conduct 
integrated analysis of  multiple  active and 
passive thermal control systems, hence to 
fine tune the case that maintains the best 
indoor thermal comfort while consumes the 
lowest energy. First, this research combined 
the variety systems that have potential in 
energy saving, according to the principle of 
"open building" to develop the U-Deck 
system and then establish the scale model 
and 3-D model to examine the feasibility of 
various collocations. At the same time, 
ground temperature was measured and was 
used further as the basic data for simulating 
the ground-source heat pump system to 
reduce energy and heat released to the urban 
atmosphere. A radiant cool ceiling system 
was installed and tested to examine the 
performance in terms of both thermal 
comfort and energy consumption. In 
addition, this research adopted buoyancy 
mechanism of ventilate using scale model 
test and simulation.  The relation among   
heat source height, the height of neutral 
level and heat flux intensity were analyzed.
