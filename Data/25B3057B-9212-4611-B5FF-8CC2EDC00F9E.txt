中文摘要
本研究主要利用太陽光做為光源，探求 TiO2 光催化程序氧化處理光電業彩色濾光片廢
水之最適加藥組合，再藉由附加 Fresnel lens 聚光片在太陽光下進行 TiO2 光催化程序，以促
進廢水 COD 去除率、TOC 去除率和 BOD5/COD(生物可分解性)。探討內容除了以統計實驗
設計-反應曲面法(RSM)探求反應參數-廢水 pH 值、TiO2 添加量和反應時間，達到 COD 去
除率 35%、TOC 去除率 25%和廢水生物可分解性達 0.15 以上之最適組合，並探討不同因
子(廢水 pH 值、TiO2 添加量、太陽光能及太陽光熱能)對於反應去除彩色濾光片廢水去除率
及生物可分解性之影響，更進一步探討附加 Fresnel lens 在太陽光 TiO2 光催化程序中所產生
之效應。實驗結果經反應曲面法最適化分析，顯示彩色濾光片廢水於 pH 7.3~8.7、1.5 g TiO2/L
及反應時間 3 小時之反應條件下，可達到本研究之設定目標。在最適條件下進行附加 Fresnel
lens 聚光片之太陽光/TiO2 光催化程序，於反應 1 小時，COD 去除率及 TOC 去除率分別可
提升至 37.38%和 25.83%，生物可分解性更提升至 0.15 以上，優於使用太陽光模擬光源反
應 3 小時之處理效率，此乃因 Fresnel lens 聚光板具有光熱之聚集效應，可有效促使太陽熱
能提升廢水溫度 15-25 ℃，及太陽光能更可提升至無添加聚光板之 2-3 倍，進而提升廢水
的處理效果。此結果顯示應用 Fresnel lens 可有效地提升廢水的處理效率，降低後續之處理
成本，進而提高太陽光/TiO2 光催化程序於實廠廢水之應用可行性。
關鍵字: Fresnel lens 聚光板、太陽光 TiO2 光催化, 光電業廢水, 反應曲面法, 生物可分解性

二、實驗設備與材料方法
2.1實驗材料
本研究乃選用苗栗地區某光電產業廢水，然光電產業廢水之種類繁多，於本研究乃選
定難以被生物分解之彩色濾光片廢水為本研究處理對象，該廠之彩色濾光片廢水由於生物
可分解性較低，常需合併 DMSO 廢水及其他廢水進行處理。為切確掌握實廠廢水之現況及
性質，本研究及對該光電業廢水進行基本分析，在生物可分解性方面，主要以 BOD5/COD
為參考依據作為參考因素之考量。
另 Arslan-Alaton et al.(2009)研究中指出廢水中若含有過高之硫酸根離子、氯離子等，
會與目標汙染物搶奪氫氧自由基，進而影響反應系統之去除率，故本研究室為了解廢水中
所含之離子特性，將廢水以 0.45 μm 之濾膜過濾後，以離子層析儀(ICS-1000 及 ICS-1100
with AS)進行分析。
2.2 實驗設備及程序
本研究之實驗流程主要分為四階段，第一階段主要以表 1 所示之三因子三階層中心旋
轉試驗設計來進行實驗後並探求 pH、g TiO2/L 及反應時間之最適組合。實驗乃利用瓶杯試
驗（1 L 反應瓶，每個反應瓶內裝 200 mL 廢水，光照射面積 113 cm2，液面深度 2.5 cm，
磁石攪拌速度 200 rpm，廢水水溫則利用循環水浴將水溫控制於 25 0C）來進行，光源則由
太陽光模擬試驗機(Suntest CPS+ model, ATLAS Co., USA)裝置一支 1500 W 之 Xe 燈來提供
反應過程所需要之光輻射(300800 nm)能量，功率設定為 500 W/m2 (UV290-390: 35.5 W/m2)，
此約等於實際太陽光 1,100 W/m2 中波長 300800 nm 之光輻射能量，並選用 Degussa P25
TiO2 作為本研究之光觸媒材料。第二階段則分別探討廢水 pH 和光觸媒添加量對於色度、
COD 和 TOC 去除率之影響，第三階段分別探討太陽光能及熱能對於彩色濾光片廢水處理
效率之影響。最後由反應曲面法所探求出之最適操作條件進行實際太陽光(光強度為
926.2~958.7 W/m2，其中 UV290-400 為 52.5~ 57.4 W/m2，UV290-320 為 48.6~53.4 W/m2，UV254
為 4.46~5.46 W/m2，UV365 為 13.8~16.6 W/m2)及附加 Fresnel lens 對彩色濾光片廢水處理效
率影響之探討。
2.3 樣品分析方法
依設定時間點取樣之樣品，以 0.45 μm 之濾膜過濾後以下列方法進行分析。
(1) COD分析：利用HACH Co.之COD加熱器，溫度設定150 0C，加熱120 min後；再以
HACH-4000綜合水質分析儀，方法編號2720，波長設定620 nm進行比色法分析，量
測出廢水之COD值。
(2) BOD5分析：依照環檢所公告之標準操作方法( NIEA W510.54B )進行分析，菌種未
經馴化。分析中所用之營養鹽，乃以 HACH 公司生產之 BOD Nutrient Buffer調配
而成。另以Y.S.I廠牌(USA)，型號為 Model 5100 之溶氧測定儀進行水樣DO值之分
析，再依稀釋比例計算出廢水之BOD5值。
3.2 TiO2添加量對彩色濾光片廢水處理效率之影響
圖 4 為彩色濾光片廢水於廢水 pH 值 7.5，反應時間 3 小時，改變光觸媒添加量之廢水
去除效率及生物可分解性。由圖 4 可知當光觸媒添加量為 0.5 g/L 時，其 COD 去除率及 TOC
去除率僅有 28.43%和 18.59%，主要是因為 TiO2 添加量少，使得氫氧自由基生成量不足，
進而導致廢水去除率低。當TiO2添加量由 0.5 g/L增加至 1.5 g/L，廢水COD去除率由 28.43%
提升至 32.90%，TOC 去除率由 18.59%提升至 24.41%，BOD5/COD 亦由 0.082 提升至 0.139，
此乃因較多之光觸媒添加量，可吸附較多汙染物，亦可產生較多之氫氧自由基與汙染物反
應，故能提升反應效率，有效的將大分子分解成小分子，增加生物可分解性。但 TiO2 添加
量由 1.0 g/L 增加至 1.5 g/L 時，可發現廢水之 COD 及 TOC 去除率增加減緩，主要可能有
兩種原因，其一可能為 TiO2 添加量過多，反而會形成遮光效應導致部分光催化效果降低、
去除效率增加不如預期，而於 Silva et al. (2007)研究中發現當 TiO2 添加量過多，會導致光
催化效果降低、去除效率減少，於 Sun et al. (2008)和 Huang et al. (2008)研究中亦也有此發
現，且指出當添加劑量超過最佳添加量時，可能會導致光催化劑聚集結塊，部分光觸媒因
此而不能吸收光子生成氫氧自由基，使得去除率下降；另一原因則為添加較多之 TiO2 雖可
產生較多電子-電洞對，進而生成較多之氫氧自由基，但若水中提供的氧量(電子捕捉劑，如
式(4)-(5)所示)不足，導致電子-電洞對快速地再結合，減少氫氧自由基(HO·)及氫過氧自由
基(HO2·)生成之機會，導致去除率增加減緩。

3.6 太陽熱能暨廢水溫度對於彩色濾光片廢水處理效率之影響
於 Daneshvar et al. (2004)研究中提及理論上溫度對於光催化反應之影響不大，因為溫
度的提升可能會改變光觸媒的吸附量，甚至使電子電洞對再次重組。本研究仍為探討太陽
熱能暨廢水溫度對反應效率之影響，將光強度固定於 500 W/m2，改變廢水溫度 15、25 或
35 0C 進行實驗。由實驗結果(如圖 10 所示)可發現當廢水溫度由 15 0C 提升至 35 0C 反應 3
小時，其 COD 去除率由 28.16%增加至 36.84%，TOC 去除率由 19.75%增加至 27.98%，由
結果可知廢水溫度對於光催化反應之影響力仍大，提升廢水溫度有助於反應進行，從熱力
學觀點，增加溫度，其分子熱能及震動能增加，與其他反應物質之碰撞頻率亦隨之增加，
提升反應效率，於 Barka et al. (2008)研究中亦利用光催化反應分解染料廢水，發現將廢水
溫度由提升 20 0C 至 40 0C，有助於提高去除效率。
表 5 為彩色濾光片廢水於不同廢水溫度下之擬ㄧ階反應動力常數，由表 4 & 表 5 之反
應動力常數，可發現各反應條件之 kCOD及 kTOC順序為：15 0C (500 W/m2)< 250 W/m2 (25 0C)<
500 W/m2 (25 0C)< 750 W/m2 (25 0C)< 35 0C (500 W/m2)，光照強度與廢水溫度皆是光催化反
應之重要影響因子，但其中以廢水溫度之影響較大。另由表 5 之數據可知當廢水溫度由 15
0C 提升至 35 0C 時，其反應半衰期可由 4.95 小時縮短至 2.95 小時。

In this study, one of advanced oxidation processes (AOPs) –Fenton-like process with
promotion by dye-photosensitization, namely photosensitization Fenton-like process, was used to
treat carbofuran rinsate. The photosensitization Fenton-like process has several advantages,
mainly an increase of the degradation rate and lower Fe-containing sludge generation. Basically,
the photosensitization Fenton-like process starts with the reduction of Fe3+ through the following
reactions.
(a) When irradiation (< 450 nm) is involved, the added Fe3+ or Fe3+ generated by Fenton’s 
reaction (eq. (1)) is continuously reduced to Fe2+ as described by equation (2-3):
Fe2++H2O2 → Fe3+ + OH + OH‧ - (1)
Fe3++H2O→ Fe(OH)2+ +H+ (2)
Fe(OH)2+ +hν → Fe2+ + OH‧ (3)

excited dye into Fe3+ (eq. (4-5)), leading to the catalytic cycling of Fe3+/Fe2+ (Ma et al., 2006).

To understand intensively the feasibility of the photosensitization Fenton-like process for
treating carbofuran rinsate towards reuse, the effect of type and dosage of photosensitizer - dye,
and initial dosage of Fenton-like reagents ([H2O2]0 and [Fe3+]0) on the efficiency of carbofuran
decomposition, COD degradation and TOC mineralization were investigated.
MATERIALS AND METHODS
Materials
Carbofuran (C12H15NO3) with a purity of 99.0 % was provided by ChemService Co., PA, USA
and was used without further purification. Carbofuran rinsate with a concentration of 100 mg/L
(0.452 mM) was prepared for experiments. Two dye photosensitizers, including methylene blue
(MB) (C16H18ClN3S, λmax: 668 nm) and alizarin red S (ARS) (C14H7O7SNa, λmax: 556 nm), were
selected to be used in this study for their clearly different molecular structures with or without a
quinone structure unit. Hydrogen peroxide (H2O2) with a concentration of 35 % was supplied by
Panreac Co., USA. Fe2(SO4)3 (Panreac Co., USA) with a purity of 75 % was used as a source of
ferric ion. HPLC-grade methanol (J.T. Baker Co., USA) was employed as eluent. All other
chemicals used in this study were analytical grade and used as received.
Procedures
All experiments were carried out in a batch mode. A 1-L glass beaker containing 200 mL of
carbofuran rinsate was used and maintained at a temperature of 25 0C in a water bath during the
experiments. The initial pH of the solution was adjusted to pH 3.0 ± 0.1 using 0.1 M H2SO4. The
concentration of hydrogen peroxide adding to pesticide rinsate was in the dosage of 5.0, 10.0, or
15.0 mM. The amount of Fe3+ ion used for each run was 0.2, 0.33, or 1.0 mM to make a initial
ratio of [H2O2]/[Fe3+] being 5 75. Concentrations of photosensitizer added were 1 %, 2 % or 3
% of that of the initial concentrations of ferric ion in water. The prepared rinsate was then placed
into the photoreactor and irradiated by artificial solar light. Artificial solar light irradiation with
intensity of 500 W/m2 was supplied by an ATLAS Suntest CPS+ solar simulator (ATLAS Co.,
USA). During the experiments, the pH of the solution was monitored using a pH meter
(SP-701LI 120, Suntex Co., Taiwan) equipped with a glass electrode. Samples were withdrawn
from the reactor at preset time intervals (5, 10, 20 min), tested for H2O2 consumption according
carried out at different initial H2O2 concentrations. The carbofuran, COD and TOC removals are
shown in Fig. 1. The H2O2 concentration declined significantly, which led to a significant
carbofuran degradation. Almost all of carbofuran molecules were decomposed into smaller
intermediates in 20 min reaction time. However, considering the COD and TOC degradation, the
COD removal efficiency were 24, 38, and 42 % for 5 mM, 10 mM, and 15 mM of H2O2,
respectively while the TOC removal efficiency was less affected by increasing the initial H2O2
dosage. The phenomenon was also stated in the study of Al Momani et al. (2004) for
2,4-dichlorophenol with treatment of photo-Fenton reaction. This may be due to not all the H2O2
degraded to produce hydroxyl radicals, about 40 % of the initial H2O2 dosage remained in
solution. Moreover, Fe2+ formation and accumulation decreased after 10 min reaction time,
leading to less Fe2+ reacting with H2O2 to produce hydroxyl radicals.
The effect of the initial Fe3+ dosage on the photo-Fenton-like process was tested by carried
out experiments with various amount of Fe3+ and a fixed H2O2 initial concentration of 10 mM.
The removal efficiency as function of initial Fe3+ dosage was shown in Fig. 2. As a result of iron
photo-reduction reaction and formation of hydroxyl radicals, the carbofuran removal increased by
increasing the initial Fe3+ dosage. However, the COD and TOC removals increased slightly. This
may be due to more dosage of Fe3+ did not lead a much higher H2O2 utilization and then no more
hydroxyl radicals produced or did additionally scavenge the generated .OH. Moreover, starting
photo-Fenton-like reaction at higher Fe3+ dosage (1mM) led to the formation of brown turbidity
in the reactor, and no more improvement in the degradation was noticed. Ghaly et al. (2001)
remarked that this turbidity decreased the penetration of light in solution and then decreased the
reaction of Fe3+-photoreduction, resulting in the generation of .OH decreased. Basically,
increasing initial H2O2 dosage was much more beneficial for increasing degradation of pesticide
rinsate than increasing initial Fe3+ dosage. Among the conditions studied, the optimum treatment
condition of photo-Fenton-like reaction for carbofuran rinsate was with a [H2O2]0/[Fe3+]0 value of
10/0.3 (3035).
Effect of type and dosage of photosensitizer
Under the operation of optimal dosage of Fenton-like reagents, the effect of adding dye
photosensitizer on degradation efficiency of carbofuran rinsate was shown in Table 1. The COD
degradation efficiency of rinsate could be promoted to 61 % and 66 % from 37 % by an addition
of methylene blue (MB) and alizarin red S (ARS), respectively. Meanwhile, the mineralization
efficiency of wastewater could be achieved to 29 % and 57 % from 21 % through the assistance of
adding MB and ARS, respectively. This could be due to effective electron transfer from visible
light-excited dyes into Fe3+, which leads to regenerate Fe2+ (eq. (4)-(5)) (Ma et al., 2005).
Moreover, ARS showed much more effective acceleration effect on the mineralization of
carbofuran than MB. It has been reported that quinone/hydroquinone analogues could greatly
catalyze the Fenton degradation of organic compounds (Chen and Pignatello, 1997). Therefore,
when a dye with a quinone-like molecular structure (hydroquinone or catechol) have been found
to greatly catalyze the Fenton degradation of organic compounds (Ma et al., 2005), which is
attributed to the fact that they could reduce Fe3+ to Fe2+ rapidly at low pHs and hence make a rapid
regeneration of Fe2+ bypassing eq. (2), the slow step of the Fenton reaction. The quinone structure
can rapidly react with .O2H generated through the following reaction (eq. (6)), and thus the
quinone cycle is built up.

hydroquinone to recycle Fe2+ from Fe3+, resulting in one more catalytic effect on the reduction of

The effect of dye dosage, in terms of [Dye]0/[Fe3+]0, on degradation efficiency of carbofuran
rinsate was reported in Table 2. Concentrations of dye added were 1 %, 2 % or 3 % of that of the
initial concentrations of ferric ion in water. It was found that except for carbofuran rinsate with 1
% MB addition, COD and TOC degradation efficiency of carbofuran rinsate was generally
enhanced by the addition of dye, even with a result of lower utilization efficiency of H2O2.
Moreover, results showed that a better degradation efficiency was found with a [Dye]0/[Fe3+]0 of 2
%, especially for the addition of ARS. The lower COD and TOC degradation efficiency for
samples with more dye (3 %) in presence of rinsate was possibly resulted from the color-shielding
effect of dye and then reduced light irradiation. A similar phenomenon was also found in solar
TiO2 photocatalysis/photosensitization of carbofuran (Kuo et al., 2006). As a result, the optimum
dosage for the complete destruction of carbofuran molecular structure was found with a
[H2O2]0/[Fe
3+]0 ratio of 30-35 and a [Dye]0/[Fe3+]0 ratio of 2 % under 500 W/m
2 irradiance after
treating 20 min. Under the optimal conditions, it was showed that carbofuran molecules could be
decomposed quickly to lower-molecular-weight intermediates and even mineralization by
attacking of hydroxyl radicals. Carbofuran was found to be decomposed to carbofuran phenol,
3-oxo carbofuran phenol, and 3-hydroxyl carbofuran phenol initially, and then further be degraded
to smaller molecules, such as NO3-, CH3COOH, (COOH)2, and finally be mineralized.
CONCLUSION
Based on the results obtained in this study, it was believed that a great increase in COD
degradation and mineralization efficiency of pesticide rinsate in addition of pesticide molecule
totally be decomposed could be achieved by adding photosensitizer - dye with an appropriate ratio
into Fenton-like system. The dyes such as ARS which has a quinone-like structure showed much
more promotion effect on mineralization efficiency of pesticide rinsate. Consequently, the
Fenton-like process along with the aid of a photosensitizer, such as ARS, could be a feasible and
potential technology for the treatment of pesticide rinsate towards reuse.
