???? ???A real-time hardened red blood cell detecting chip and the 
method of operating principle are provided. The real-time 
hardened red blood cell detecting chip comprises the first 
substrate and at least a parallel electrode array. The first 
substrate comprises at least a micro-channel. Electrodes are 
parallel arranged, and the parallel electrode array is disposed in 
the channel on the first substrate and is composed with a 
plurality of electrodes. Another real-time hardened red blood cell 
detecting chip comprising the first substrate, at least one of 
parallel electrode array and the second substrate having at least 
one of a micro-channel, is provided. The parallel electrode is 
disposed on the first substrate and is composed with a plurality 
of electrodes. The electrodes are also parallel arranged. The 
second substrate, comprising at least one of a micro-channel, is 
disposed on the first substrate. And the micro-channel passes 
through the parallel electrode array. By using the present 
real-time hardened red blood cell detecting chip, the red blood 
cells can be manipulated to adhere or move off the area of the 
parallel electrode array.

Abstract 
The results of a study on a real-time hardened red blood cell (HRBC) separating chip are 
presented in this paper. This separating chip was made by using MEMS fabrication process with 
simply two photo masks. By using oxygen-plasma direct bonding method, the assembly of an 
electrode chip and the micro-channels will form a set of HRBC separating chip. A syringe pump 
is used to push the fluid into the micro-channels in different flow rates. The HRBC will be 
operated by dielectrophoresis force. When the blood fluids flow through the testing section, the 
normal RBCs move forward and the HRBCs move to the branch channel. 
In this experiment, the performance of the separating chip was evaluated by measuring inlet 
flow rate. The input voltage ranged from 5 to 20VPP, and frequency ranged from 500kHz to 
10MHz. The effects on the geometry of the micro-channel were also study in this paper. The 
results show that the separating performance of the Y-type micro-channel was enhanced 
compared to that of the Branch type. The inlet flow rate of the Y-type device can attain the 
maximum of 333µm/sec. The results indicated that the present separating chip can fulfill the 
demands for most micro-fluidic systems. Moreover, this device can be easily applied to complex 
systems with Bio-detecting system in the future. 
Keywords: HRBC, DEP, particle-manipulating, separator
