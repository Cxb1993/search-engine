Kinematics of rock slope induced by seismic excitation. 
計畫編號：NSC 97-2221-E-002-187 
執行期限：97/08/1-98/07/31 
計畫主持人：黃燦輝  台灣大學土木工程學系 教授

摘要 
分析地震所引致的順向坡滑動或是岩塊滑動，通常以滑動面的摩擦角或摩擦係數做為滑
動門檻值。然而，使用傾斜台是一個簡便可獲得摩擦角的試驗方法，此試驗結果是否可直
接作為邊坡受震時塊體滑動的門檻值需進一步探討。 
本年度計畫研究中，進行靜態傾斜台與動態振動台模擬邊坡基盤受震導致上部塊體滑動
情形，並比較兩種方法所量得的塊體滑動門檻值之差異，且進一步探討造成此差異的原因。
首先，採用三種材料(砂岩、鋁、模擬岩塊)分別進行實驗，結果顯示，透過傾斜台所量得之
靜摩擦係數 tµ 均高於所量得的瞬時臨界摩擦係數 ( ( ))c i ctµ µ= (tc為塊體初始滑動的時刻)；若
進一步改變邊坡基盤的受震頻率(4Hz、6Hz及 8Hz)，發現較高頻率時 cµ 值也較高，顯示在
受震條件下， cµ 並非單一固定值；此外，改變塊體施加於滑動面上之正向應力，亦發現當
滑動面的接觸應力愈大時，所對應的 cµ 愈低，顯示 cµ 與正向應力也有相關；本研究除了探
討塊體之滑動門檻值，也針對滑動歷程中瞬時摩擦係數 ( )i tµ 的改變作了分析，發現模擬岩
塊受震滑動的摩擦行為與理想材料的 Coulomb摩擦模式有所不同，其瞬時摩擦係數與塊體
滑動過程中的相對速度及相對位移具有函數關係。 
最後，針對兩種實驗方法所得結果的差異特性進行討論，發現滑動面上的正向應力分佈
對於塊體之滑動門檻值有一定程度的影響，當正向應力分佈愈不均勻時，塊體愈容易滑動。
然而，當基盤受震時，滑動面上正向應力的分佈相對地較靜態條件時分佈更不均勻。 
透過本計畫研究成果，已初步的釐清傾斜台量測結果的限制性，若要得到塊體之受震滑
動門檻值，仍需透過動態的模擬邊坡受震實驗。本計畫未來將進一步的發展岩塊受震滑動
的力學模式，以期可作為實際案例分析與設計之參考。 
Abstract 
Earthquake induced dip-slope sliding or rock-block sliding is usually analyzed using friction 
angle or friction coefficient measured at the sliding interface.  Tilt test is a convenient test for 
measuring the required friction angle.  However, tilt tests is a test under static condition, and 
the applicability of measured friction parameters to analyses under dynamic excitation requires 
further discussion. 
This study conducts static tilt test and shaking table test to simulate block sliding with base 
excitation, compares differences on measured sliding thresholds, and discuss the cause of this 
difference.  First, three different materials (aluminum, sandstone, and synthetic sandstone) are

一、前言 
國際隧道安全論壇 (International Tunnel Safety Forum)係由國際隧道管理協會
(Tunnel Management International, TMI)鑑於 1990 年代末期數起國際交通隧道事故釀成
重大災難所主辦的專業型國際研討會，第一屆於 2005 年在香港舉辦，第二(本)屆於法
國里昂舉辦。 
此次舉行之第二屆國際隧道安全論壇進一步針對公路及鐵路隧道安全議題深入探
討(Second International Tunnel Safety Forum for Road and Rail)，吸引了法國、瑞士、義大
利、香港、美國、英國、加拿大、西班牙、澳大利亞、芬蘭、印度、新加坡、荷蘭、德
國、瑞典等各國之隧道專家共同與會，研討範圍涵蓋了檢視過去隧道營運管理的經驗，
以及討論其對於未來隧道管理可能產生的影響。此類議題對於隧道所有者(政府或私人
單位)、營運管理者具有特別的重要性，並且對於隧道設計者、火災緊急救援單位、風
險管理者、保險業者、立法者、研究人員、使用者、隧道設備儀器製造商和車輛設計者
有著直接的關聯。營運中隧道安全的議題相對於過去著重於新建技術的隧道工程範疇而
言屬於較新的課題，以往在國內討論較少。參加此次國際會議，可瞭解世界各先進國家
不論在公路隧道或是鐵路隧道之營運管理、防救災及安全維護等各方面之發展，並可藉
此機會能與世界各國之學者專家進行技術與學術交流。

4. Causes for anomalies and potential failure patterns 
4.1 Possible causes accounting for anomalies 
Identifying the cause accounting for the anomalies on tunnel lining is extremely important for 
follow-up evaluation on tunnel safety and necessary measures for maintenance.  However, it is 
very difficult due as the relationship between an anomaly and a potential cause may not be a simple 
one-to-one case.  Not only the anomalies may vary with time, but also the corresponding causes 
accounting for the anomalies may be transferred step by step.  Consequently, for the time being 
identifying the cause for tunnel anomalies remains a tough task for tunnel inspection and safety 
evaluation. 
Japan Society of Civil Engineers (JSCE) (20003) classified the possible causes for tunnel anomalies 
into two categories, the exterior reasons and the interior ones, as listed in Table 1.  The exterior 
reasons imply the variation of tunnel circumstance, which may lead to a change of external force 
acting on tunnel lining.  And the interior reasons represent the variation of lining itself, such as the 
deterioration in strength or stiffness of support. 
4.1.1. Exterior reasons 
After completion, there are many factors may lead to the change of external force acting on tunnel 
lining.  Among which, three typical types, i.e., the plastic pressure, gravitational earth pressure, 
and the inclined pressure, have been well identified (Fig 3).  The plastic pressure implies an extra 
overstress zone associated with an incremental stresses induced by the time-dependent behavior or 
squeezing behavior of tunnel surrounding rocks been equilibrium state under construction once.

or rocks on crown area of tunnel.  Since the gaps in-between tunnel lining and surrounding rocks

5. Maintenance consideration and strategies 
5.1 Goal of maintenance 
Deciding the goal of tunnel maintenance is probable the first thing and the most important one for 
the rehabilitation of tunnels in operation, especially for outdated tunnels.  It is absolute a tough 
task since the design standards and associated service levels may have been modified from the stage 
of tunneling to the moment of maintenance, or even more, the target life cycle for tunnel service.  
In addition, the volume of traffic, or the demand of tunnel transportation, may also change.  In 
case the modern standard is requested to be satisfied for tunnel built in accordance with early days’ 
criterion, it may be necessary to be reinforced to update the strength, stiffness as well as service 
level of the tunnel.  However, old tunnels usually prepared limited tolerance space, which result in 
insufficient room for tunnel updating.  On the other hand, if the maintenance work aims to keep 
tunnel stability to maintain its function, the mission could be accomplished by means of 
uncomplicated repairing measures. 
The lessons learned from a series of railway tunnel improvement projects in 1990’s in Taiwan show 
that a big challenge for tunnel maintenance is to keep a smooth transportation during repairing or 
reinforcing work, especially for the later.  Furthermore, the cost for reinforcing the outdated tunnel 
to updating standard is more or less unreasonable, compared to that of building a new tunnel.  
Consequently, maintaining the function as usual is preferable for tunnel maintenance work 
nowadays.  There are two hydraulic tunnels have been repaired to keep their functions for decadal 
years, following up with new tunneling projects (CECI Engineering Consultants, Inc., 1997; United 
Geotech, Inc., 2004).

1. Introduction 
Tunnel inspection and corresponded safety evaluation have been paid much attention in early 
1990’s in Taiwan since some renovation projects aimed to enhance the service function on 
operational tunnels and to extend lift-span of which.  Various anomalies have been reported on 
many tunnels, such as lining crack, spalling, leakage, displacement, etc.  Valuable lessons have 
been learned from these inspection projects accordingly.  However, due as it is still short of 
well-ordered standards or guidelines to be referred to, the adapted techniques and corresponded 
inspected procedure in these projects, even in similar stage between two tunnels and for 
alike-objectives to be inspected, are sometimes distinct.  As a result, inspection outcomes between 
two tunnels with similar conditions, for instance, the necessity for rehabilitation or the strategy for 
subsequent inspection, could be diverse in some cases.  Therefore, a research aiming at improving 
the inspection approach and building up inspection guidelines for operational traffic tunnels was 
launched at 2005 (Huang et al., 2008).  First, the inspection projects are reviewed and the founded 
anomalies are classified accordingly.  Based on these anomalies, the objectives to be inspected are 
discussed and identified, and corresponded inspection techniques are proposed as well.  Thus, the 
requirements for tunnel maintenance are taken into account to set up guidelines for tunnel 
inspection.  Furthermore, an integrating approach for potential section with repair necessity, 
comprised of visualization technique, information integration, and necessary long-term monitoring, 
is proposed. 
 
2. Inspection projects and lining anomalies 
2.1 Tunnel inspection 
The modern tunnel inspection project lunched at 1993 in Taiwan.  Meanwhile more than 500 
km long tunnel have been constructed and put into operation.  The tunnel inspection has been 
paying more attention after the 1999 Chichi Earthquake due to about 30 tunnels damaged (Wang et 
al, 2001).  Up to now, there are more than 100 tunnels with total length of more than 200 km have 
been inspected.  Among which there are 3 railway tunnels, 4 highway tunnels, and 5 hydraulic 
tunnels.  There are approximate one sixth tunnels in length in Taiwan have been inspected (Wang 
and Huang, 2007; Lin et al., 2007). 
The concept on operational tunnels inspection and corresponding technologies has made a big 
progress in the past decade.  Not only the quantity of inspected tunnels increase rapidly, some 
important tunnels have been inspected successively in certain interval, such as the tunnels of Taipei 
Mass Rapid Transit System.  Moreover, sustained inspection for repaired tunnel has also carried 
out on 70 years old hydraulic tunnel (Lee et al., 2006).  These practical inspection projects provide 
valuable data and the lessons learned have pushed relevant researches ahead.

3.2 Suggested techniques 
The subjects to be inspection are classified into three categories, i.e. the lining surface, the 
lining soundness and the cavity behind the lining.  The inspection items in lining surface are 
usually visual, including the distribution of cracks, the width of cracks and its developing, lining 
deformation, leakage, spalling of lining materials, and drop of sealing mortar for masonry tunnels.  
The inspection items of lining soundness and the cavity behind the lining are usually non-visual.  
The former includes defect of lining self, thickness of lining, crack depth, strength and deterioration 
of lining materials, rebar corrosion and cover thickness, and the deterioration of sealing mortar of 
masonry tunnels.  And the latter involves any cavity or vacant space existing in-between the 
boundary of tunnel lining and surrounding rocks, as well as the loosening zone of tunnel

stages (Huang and Jeng, 1997, 1998).  For ordinary inspection stage, the influence of anomalies on 
the passer’s safety was of concerned and the safety level of tunnel was leveled into three ranks.  
The evaluated objects included not only tunnel lining, but also the portal and nearby slope, 
pavement, and relevant accessories for tunnel furnish (e.g. ventilation and lightening).  The

5. Conclusion 
The end of construction is the start of maintenance, and the period of maintenance is usually 
tenfold or even greater to the construction time.  In this manuscript a new framework for tunnel 
inspection and safety evaluation has been proposed according to the lessons learned from practical 
projects in the past two decades.  The objectives to be inspected are discussed and identified in 
various inspection stages, and corresponding techniques are proposed as well.  The purposes for 
various inspection stages and the requirement for tunnel maintenance are taken into account to set 
up guidelines for tunnel inspection.  Comparing with the conventional approaches, the cause 
accounted for the anomalies and possible influent factors are more emphasized in the proposed
