一、中文摘要 
 
在傳統的正交多頻分工系統(OFDM)中，藉由加入足
夠的冗餘資料量(Redundancy) 於連續傳送的個別資
料區塊中，便可以完全去除由通訊頻道所引發之區
塊干擾(Inter-block-interference; IBI) 所造成的影響。 
在本計畫中，首先在假設通道資訊 (Channel site 
information)已知下，結合非正交投影(Oblique projection)
技術，我們探討利用循環字首(Cyclic prefix; CP)及補零
(Zero-padding; ZP)正交多頻分工系統的傳送接收器
(Transceiver)架構。在無雜訊的環境下，所設計之接收
器，可以用具零階有限脈衝響應濾波器組 (FIR 
filterbanks)迫零等化器(Zero-forcing equalizer)來實現，以
達到零區塊干擾(IBI-free)與符碼間干擾(ISI-free)之效
益。然而，在加入的冗餘資料量不足(Insufficiency)
情況下時， 本計畫提出一個以虛擬隨機後置編碼
(Pseudo-Random-Postfix; PRP)正交多頻分工系統(OFDM)
的新傳送接收機架構。 在此，我們除了提出新的半
盲蔽式(Semi-blind)通道估測法外，更結合最大縮短通
道長度法(Maximum shortening signal-to-noise ratio scheme)
及貝氏決定迴授等化器(Bayesian DFE)，以有效消除區
塊干擾(IBI)及符碼干擾（ISI）。最後，在系統效能比
較上，則以位元錯誤率(Bit-error-rate; BER)來呈現。經
由電腦模擬，我們證實在所有討論的情況下;我們所
提方法在和傳統方法相同的假設下，皆具較佳的位
元錯誤率系統效能。除此之外，在無足夠的冗餘資
料量系統下，本計畫所提之傳送接收器系統架構皆
能有效地用於實現正交多頻分工系統，可以降低傳
接機的設計複雜度。 
關鍵詞:  
多載波分碼多工、非正交投影、迫零等化器、縮短
通道法、半盲蔽式通道估測、貝氏決定迴授等化器。 
 
英文摘要 (Abstract) 
 
In conventional Orthogonal Frequency Division 
Multiplexing (OFDM) systems, the effect of 
inter-block-interference (IBI) can be completely 
removed by inserting sufficient redundant symbols 
between successive transmission blocks. For sufficient 
redundant symbols, in tradition the Pseudo-Random- 
Postfix (PRP) of PRP-OFDM system was applied for 
semi-blind channel estimation, with order-one statistics 
of the received signal. In this project, based on the 
PRP-OFDM transceiver framework, we propose a 
modified semi-blind channel estimation scheme for 
insufficient redundant symbols, where the length of 
redundant symbol (PRP) is less than the channel order. 
The PRP-OFDM capitalizes on the advantages of 
zero-padding (ZP)-OFDM; it replaces the guard 
interval contents of ZP-OFDM with pseudo-randomly 
weighted postfix sequences. Unlike the CP-OFDM, it is 
not sensitive to frequency channel nulls that are close to 
the subcarriers, as that using the ZP-OFDM systems.  
To further reduce the complexity of receiver, the 
maximum shortening signal-to-noise-ratio time domain 
equalizer (MSSNR TEQ) is developed for suppressing 
the inter-block-interference (IBI). After that the 
Bayesian decision feedback equalizer (DFE) is adopted 
for suppressing the ISI to further improve the system 
performance. The merits of the proposed scheme with 
Bayesian DFE is confirmed via computer simulation, to 
achieve better performance, in terms of bit error rate 
(BER), over the conventional block-based equalizers, 
viz., the zero-forcing equalizers and the minimum mean 
square error DFE. 
 
Keywords: OFDM, oblique projection, zero-forcing 
equalizer, Semi-blind channel estimation, channel 
shortening, Bayesian DFE.

Blind Adaptive DS-CDMA Receivers with Sliding 
Window Constant Modulus GSC-RLS Algorithm  
Shiunn-Jang Chern, Chun-Hung Sun and Chih-Chi Chang 
Department of Electrical Engineering  
National Sun Yat-Sen University, Kaohsiung 80424 Taiwan 
Tel: +886-7-5254171, Fax: +886-7-5254199 
E-mail: chern@ee.nsysu.edu.tw 
 
 
 
 
Abstract— The adaptive filtering algorithms, based on the 
sliding window linear constrained recursive least squares (SW 
LC-RLS) approach, is very attractive to a violent changing 
environment. In this paper we propose a novel SW constant 
modulus RLS (SW CM-RLS) algorithm, based on the Min/max 
criterion, to deal with the narrowband interference (NBI) for DS-
CDMA system over multipath channels. For reducing the system 
complexity, the generalized sidelobes canceller (GSC) structure is 
employed, and is referred to as the SW CM-GSC-RLS algorithm. 
The aim of the SW CM-GSC-RLS algorithm is used to alleviate 
the effect of NBI, and suppressing the MAI and ISI, 
simultaneously, thus achieves desired performance.  
I. INTRODUCTION 
The increasing demand for wireless communication systems 
has led to the development of advanced multiple access 
schemes such as direct-sequence code division multiple access 
(DS-CDMA) to increase the capacity of current generation 
wireless systems. In the DS-CDMA systems all users transmit 
messages through identical channel at the same time with 
same frequency band, but assign distinct signature waveforms 
(or spreading codes), which allows signal separation at the 
receiver. However, the DS-CDMA framework faces some 
potential impediments in the wireless communication systems. 
The major impediments for signal detection at the physical 
layer are the effects of multipath fading, multiple access 
interference (MAI), near-far problem, narrowband interference 
(NBI), mismatch effects and channel noise.  
Linear detectors for DS-CDMA systems have been an 
active area of research because they offer promising MAI 
suppression and robustness to multipath propagation with 
reasonable complexity. In particular, minimum-output-energy 
(MOE) detector or linearly constrained minimum variance 
(LCMV) approach [1][2][4] exploit knowledge of the desired 
user’s spreading code to operate in a blind manner. The MOE 
detector minimizes the receiver’s output energy subjected to 
one or more linear signal preserving constraints. Under ideal 
conditions, it has the performance closed to the optimal 
minimum mean-square error (MMSE) detector without 
requiring training sequences [1][4]. Nevertheless, the LCMV 
approach is extremely sensitive to inaccuracies in the 
acquisition of the desired signal user’s signature code and 
timing, referred to as the mismatch effect [4]. Therefore, many 
robust blind adaptive algorithms have been proposed, which is 
based on linearly constrained constant modulus (LCCM) 
criterion. It is very robust to code and timing inaccuracies 
because the aim of the constraint is not to protect the desired 
signal from being cancelled by the adaptive algorithm, but to 
avoid the capture of an interference [4]. In addition to alleviate 
the effects due to MAI and ISI, in this paper, the CM approach 
associated with the sliding window (SW) RLS algorithm is 
developed based on the Min/max criterion to deal with the 
narrowband interference (NBI) for DS-CDMA system over 
multipath channels. For reducing the system complexity, the 
generalized side-lobe canceller (GSC) structure presented in 
[3] is employed. In [5][9] it has been proved that the use of the 
RLS algorithm with the sliding window (SW) approach could 
achieve better tracking property over the conventional RLS 
algorithm. With this approach, the sliding rectangular window 
is used to discard old data to reduce the influence of the past 
on the estimate. In fact, based on the Min/max approach [1]-
[3] with SW CM GSC-RLS algorithm, the constrained 
optimization can be performed to combine all multipath 
component of the signal of interest, and jointly minimize MAI, 
mismatch effect as well as NBI effect, while maximizing the 
signal component at the receiver’s output. The detail will be 
introduced in what follows.

III. COMPUTER SIMULATIONS AND CONCLUSIONS 
In this section, we would like to demonstrate the merits 
of the novel proposed SW CM GSC-RLS algorithm, based on 
Min/max criterion, via computer simulations. In the computer 
simulations, the up-link DS-CDMA system is considered and 
the BPSK modulation is adopted. Ten users, including the 
desired user, are considered where user 1 is assumed to be the 
desired user. Gold sequences of length 31 (P=31) are used to 
generate the code sequences. In what follows the effect due to 
the narrowband interference (NBI) is investigated, while the 
multipath fading and channel mismatch are considered, 
simultaneously.  The desired user is assumed to have power 
that is 10 dB weaker than other nine users (with equal-power). 
In addition, the length of the sliding window and the forgetting 
factor of GSC-RLS as well as CM GSC-RLS algorithms are 
chosen to be 400 (or 200)L =  and 0.998λ = , respectively. We 
assume that the receiver is synchronized to the desired user, 
while signals received from other users are arriving at the 
receiver with arbitrary delays jτ ( j=2,…, 10) between q and P 
chip periods, where the channel order is q=2. Also, channel 
parameters for all users are generated randomly [2].  
The performance, in terms of output signal to interference 
plus noise ratio (SINR), is evaluated. It is thus compared with 
the GSC-RLS (MOE) and CM GSC-RLS algorithms, based on
