一、摘要 
 
中文摘要 
 
化學物質的污染包括重金屬在內，有絕大部
分是經由人類的活動有心或無意所導致的污染物
堆積而形成的問題。許多生物需要微量的某些重
金屬作為其生長成分，然而在較高的濃度時，這
些重金屬對生物仍具有毒性。具強烈毒性的重金
屬如鎘及鉻等在大部分的情況下則均是具抑菌或
殺菌的作用。然而，有些細菌能發展出抵抗及去
除這些重金屬毒性的機制。環境中重金屬污染的
量的提高直接影響到其微生物在數目與種類的組
成。本實驗室在過去幾年來專注於重金屬污染環
境中微生物族群與多樣性之的研究，包括利用未
經培養方式直接以分子生物技術探討該環境中微
生物多樣性，也從遭重金屬鎘污染的環境分離出
本土之耐高濃度重金屬之菌株。本實驗室執行「重
金屬污染環境中微生物抗重金屬與類金屬之相關
基因及分子生物機制之探討與在生物整治的應
用」三年期計畫，乃是延續過去幾年探討遭重金
屬污染的場址之微生物多樣性之實驗結果進行之
深入研究。針對這些菌種進行生長條件的研究，
評估它們在含高濃度的鎘及其他重(類)金屬的培
養基中生長之情形，同時以分子生物學方式針對
這些菌株進行抗重(類)金屬基因與機制做進一步 
 
 
 
 
 
的探討。篩選與這些本土重金屬耐受性高之微生
物與其抗重金屬能力相關的基因，針對這些基因 
的功能做進一步的探討。除了闡明污染環境微生
物多樣性的情形之外，關於這些微生物如何能在
高含量的重金屬環境中存活，以及其如何降解或
沉澱這些污染物的機制，將是本計畫探討的重
點。本研究計畫在過去三年的研究聚焦於：(1) 利
用分子生物學方法探討重金屬污染環境之微生物
多樣性；(2) 從環境中分離並鑑定能抵抗重金屬
之微生物；(3) 培養抗重金屬之微生物及(4)研究
其抵抗重金屬之生理及生化機制；(5)利用微生物
應用於重金屬污染環境之生物整治之研究 。 
 
 
關鍵詞：抗重金屬微生物、鎘、亞碲酸鹽、碲、
重金屬污染、微生物多樣性、環境生物科技 
 
 
Abstract 
 
Pollution due to chemicals, including heavy 
metals, caused by anthropogenic activities by either 
intentional or unintentional deposition of the 
contaminants has become a major environmental 
 3
專一性離子輸出系統 (specific ion efflux 
systems)，將毒性金屬離子排除到細胞外【Nies and 
Silver, 1995】。 
 
除了重金屬外，有些類金屬如碲(tellurium) 
在其氧化態如亞碲酸鹽(tellurite)對微生物有極強
的毒性【Zannoni et al., 2008】，曾經在使抗生素發
明前被用來作為殺菌/抑菌物質【Taylor, 1999; 
Zannoni et al., 2008】。然而即使如此，跟對重金屬
的反應一樣，許多微生物仍可發展出抵抗並且 
生存於含有這些毒性物質的環境中【Pages et al., 
2008; Chien et al., 2007; 2011; Chien and Han, 
2009；Hu and Zhao, 2007; Prado Acosta et al., 
2005】。 
 
一般而言，微生物對抗高濃度重金屬的機制
大概可分為以下幾種：(1)利用主動外流 (active 
efflux) 的作用將毒性金屬離子排出細胞外；(2)
利用酵素 (通常利用還原金屬離子)來降低金屬
毒性及 (3)利用生物累積 (bioaccumulation) 或隔
離 (sequestration) 金屬離子的方法降低重金屬對
細胞的影響【Nies, 2003; Mejare and Bulow, 2001; 
Silver and Phung, 1996】。例如有些微生物可將較
具毒性的六價鉻還原成較不具毒性且通常以氫氧
化物沉澱的三價鉻 【Vainshtein et al., 2003】。另
外也有許多微生物包括格蘭氏陽性菌和格蘭氏陰
性菌及一些酵母菌能夠吸附重金屬【Silver and 
Phung, 1996; Teizel and Paresk, 2003】。藉由探討在
這些受污染的土壤中的微生物種類，我們可以了
解其微生物的多樣性以及微生物族群與污染源的
相關性等。另外在這些污染場址中也能夠分離出
許多利用不同機制來抵抗重金屬的微生物，這些
微生物將是具有發展利用微生物整治方法來清除
重金屬污染潛能的候選者。 
 
利用微生物對環境污染進行生物整治是目前
生物科技應用於環境保護最重要的一環，也日建
引起社會大眾以及科學界的關注。探討微生物的
族群在對環境壓迫（如重金屬堆積）的反應對於
瞭解這些污染源對生物多樣性以及其對人類健康
的威脅是非常重要的課題。分離及鑑定由重金屬
污染的樣品中的微生物亦將有助於發展生物性整
治技術。另外土壤若含有重金屬的污染時，常常
也會同時遭受有機污染物質如多環芳香烴 
(Polycyclic Aromatic Hydrocarbons, PAHs) 的污
染。再整治重金屬污染時，不論應用何種方式，
均需要先移除/分解這些有機污染源【Dermont et 
al., 2008】， 方能進行後續的整治。 因此研究微
生物抵抗重金屬的生理與基因及相關代謝機制，
也將對這些有機污染物的生物整治法有所助益。 
 
 
三、研究目的 
 
本實驗室長期以來投注於重金屬汙染與環境
微生物的研究，最終的遠程目標乃是廣泛的探討
台灣幾個重要的重金屬污染環境場址中的微生物
生態，並闡明這些微生物的生理與生化特性。進
一步開發利用微生物生物科技應用於重金屬污染
的生物整治 (microbial bioremediation)。過程中除
了累積學術研究成果與發表國際論文外，同時也
持續分離純化台灣本土耐受重金屬之各種不同菌
株並加以妥善保存於本研究室或寄存於財團法人
食品工業發展研究所，這些菌株將可提供給所有
學術界同仁研究使用，也有機會實際應用於各種
環境污染之微生物生物整治的開發。 
 
本計劃為延續本實驗室探討遭重金屬污染的
場址之微生物多樣性之實驗結果進行之深入研
究。本實驗室已從這些環境的土壤中分離出許多
不同的微生物菌種。並針對這些菌種進行生長條
件的研究，主要評估它們在含高濃度的鎘及其他
重(類)金屬的培養基中生長之情形。目前本實驗
室已由兩個受污染場址分離出數株可生長在含高
濃度鎘及(或)碲之培養基中，充分表現出在污染
環境中微生物的多樣性。已經收藏之對高濃度重
(類)金屬具有抗性的本土微生物菌株包括：格蘭
氏陰性菌 Stenotrophomonsa sp. (strain CD02 
[BCRC17926])，Shewanella spp. (strain TeB )， 
Pseudomonas spp. (strain TeU [菌株寄存食研所準
備中] 以及 strain EJ01)，Enterobacter sp. (strain 
CD02, 04 以及 08)，Cupriavidus taiwanensis (strain 
EJ02) 以及格蘭氏陽性菌 Paenibacillus sp. (strain 
TeW)等， 這些菌株在發表論文後將寄存於菌種
保存中心(食品科學研究所)提供國內外學術同仁
們研究使用。本計畫同時也將以分子生物學方式
針對這些菌株進行抗重(類)金屬基因與機制做進
一步的探討。

7. Pseudomonas sp. EJ01 抗鎘及其他重(類)金屬
之最小抑制濃度測試 
 
測試 Pseudomonas sp. EJ01 培養於在含各種
重(類)金屬培養基的耐受性，我們將
Pseudomonas sp. EJ01 培養在添加各種不同
濃度之重(類)金屬的 LB medium ，添加濃度
為 500 μM 至 8 mM，測試的重金屬種類則包
括鎘(cadmium)、鉻(chromium)、鎳(nickel)、
鋅(zinc)、鉛(lead)及銅(copper)與類金屬碲(亞
碲酸鹽)。 
 
 
8. Pseudomonas sp. EJ01 移除培養基中
cadmium 的測量 
 
將 Pseudomonas sp. EJ01 培養在含鎘之 LB 
培養基中，於其生長過程中，每隔一定時間
取出樣本，經離心去除細胞後，上清液過 0.22 
μm 的濾膜，將每個樣品調成金屬離子濃度 5 
ppm 以下、體積 20 ml，進行火焰式原子吸收
光譜儀(Flame Atomic Absorption 
Spectrometry)之分析【Liu et al., 2003】。 
 
 
9. 利用轉位子( Transposon )插入突變法探討
Pseudomonas sp. TeU 與亞碲酸鹽還原及鎘
的耐受性相關之基因 
 
利用引子進行 PCR 來確認轉位子在突變株
之位置。並藉由倒轉聚合酶連鎖反應，利用
Tn5 轉位子兩端設計的引子：KAN-2 forward 
primer 與 R6KAN-2 reverse primer，將含有轉
位子且已自體黏合之 DNA 片段放大。反應
條件使用 extensor PCR master mix 說明書提
供之 program，如此可找出 Tn5 轉位子插入
之基因。使用之解序策略為：以限制酶截切
出包含 Tn5 轉位子之染色體 DNA 片段，利
用接合酶將其頭尾自體黏合，再利用倒轉聚
合酶連鎖反應放大該片段，經由瓊脂凝膠電
泳將 DNA 萃出後，使用 TA 黏合、熱休克、
轉型等實驗，最後以 M13 引子將之定序以取
得 Tn5 轉位子外部的未知序列。 
 
 
10. Pseudomonas sp. TeU 與轉位子突變株 
(AU08 與 BU21) 基因插入確認 
 
利用 EZ-Tn5™ <R6K ori/KAN-2> Tnp

Transposon 隨機插入 細胞的 genomic DNA

11. Strain TeU 與突變株抗重金屬鎘(Cd) 與類金
屬亞碲酸鹽之測試 
 
在培養基中加入不同濃度的鎘(Cd)或亞碲酸
鹽(TeO32-)，利用測量光學密度(Optical 
density；OD600)來作為菌體生長之數量，已決
定其生長特性與生長速度。 
 
 
12. Tellurite (K2TeO3)於培養基中濃度變化之測
定 
 
Medium 中 tellurite 的含量測定是利用試劑
Sodium diethyl dithiocarbamate(DDTC)與
Medium 中的 tellurite 形成 complex，在 340 
nm 會有最大吸收波長，tellurite 濃度愈高則
吸收值愈高。偵測 tellurite 的濃度最高為 100 
µM【Turner , 1992】。實驗流程先將待測菌液
裝入 1.5 ml 微量離心管，經由 13000 rpm 離
心一分鐘，取上清液 100 µl,加入 300 µl 0.5 M 
(pH 7.1)的 Tris HCL 和 100 µl 10 mM 的
diethyldithiocarbamate reagent (DDTC)，均勻
的混合後置於石英比色管中，經由 U-2800 
Spectrophotometer 定點波長 340 nm 測量。

5.  Pseudomonas sp. EJ01 抗鎘及其他重(類)金屬
之最小抑制濃度測試 
 
實驗結果顯示利用含不同濃度之 CdCl2 (1000 
µM、2000 µM、3000 µM、4000 µM、5000 µM、
6000 µM、7000 µM)的 LB 培養基培養，結果發
現 EJ01 單獨只有 LB 及含 1000 µM CdCl2 的生
長情形最為快速且 OD 值最高，但隨著 CdCl2
濃度提高，菌株培養的 lag 期的時間逐漸拉
長，OD 值也越來越少(圖二十)。在其他實驗結
果顯示 Pseudomonas sp. EJ01 在抗銅、鋅、鉛
的最小抑制生長濃度皆為 6000 μM，鉻的最小
抑制濃度為 2000 μM，鎳的最小抑制濃度為
3000 µM，而類金屬碲的最小抑制濃度只有 250 
µM (表三)。 此外在這些測試當中也發現到
EJ01 在生長時期中細菌會聚集成小顆粒狀的
現象，而這現象在控制組中不曾發現過，因此
在後續實驗當中想進一步了解這現象與重金
屬有何關聯。

Tellurite resistance and removal by bacterial culture
Cells of Paenibacillus sp. strain TeW were grown in 250-
ml Erlenmeyer flasks containing 50 ml of LB medium sup-
plemented with different concentrations of K2TeO3 (500–3,000
mol/L) to determine strain TeW’s tolerance to the maximum
concentrations of tellurite. Cultures were incubated at 30C
with shaking and the growth was determined as optical density
at 600 nm (OD600).
To test for the inducibility of tellurite resistance, Paeni-
bacillus sp. strain TeW was grown in LB medium with or
without K2TeO3 (1,000 mol/L). The cells were collected and
washed once the cultures reached the midexponential phase
(OD600  0.6) by centrifugation (8,000 g for 15 min) and then
resuspended in LB medium with or without K2TeO3 to access
growth kinetics of the transferred cell populations.
Removal of tellurite ions (K2TeO3) was evaluated by de-
termination of the concentration of K2TeO3 left in the medium
during bacterial growth. Diethyldithiocarbamate was used for
quantitative determination of K2TeO3 [16]. Cells grown in the
medium containing 100 mol/L or 1,000 mol/L of K2TeO3
and the supernatants were collected by centrifugation at 18,000
g at successive time intervals. The assay condition was as
follows: 100-l supernatant was added to 300 l of 0.5 M (pH
7.1) Tris HCl, followed by addition of 100 l of diethyldi-
thiocarbamate reagent (10 mmol/L stock in ddH2O, prepared
freshly before assay). The absorbance of this reaction mixture
was measured at 340 nm after mixing using a U-2800 spec-
trophotometer (Hitachi, Tokyo, Japan). The residual K2TeO3
in the supernatants were determined by the colorimetric meth-
od using diethyldithiocarbamate as described by Turner et al.
and the detection limit was 1 to 50 g/ml (4–200 mol/L)

RESULTS
Strain identification
Strain TeW is a gram-positive, endospore-forming, rod-
shaped bacterium and is identified as a Paenibacillus sp. ac-
cording to its morphology, biochemical traits, and the sequence
of 16S rDNA analysis. The phylogeny of Paenibacillus sp.
strain TeW and some known related bacteria based on 16S
rDNA sequences are shown in Figure 1.
Resistance and reduction: Paenibacillus sp. strain TeW
Paenibacillus sp. strain TeW was able to grow in the LB

DISCUSSION
Potassium tellurite is known to be highly toxic to both
prokaryotic and eukaryotic cells and has been used as both an
antimicrobial and a therapeutic agent [1,11]. Tellurite has also
been used in selective media for the isolation of pathogens
that are naturally resistant to tellurite [1]. Although tellurium
and its soluble salts are both considered rare in nature, tel-
lurium compounds can still be found in relative high concen-
trations in industrial waste discharge [8,21,22]. Concentrations
of Te were significantly higher (more than 100 mol/kg)
around hydrothermal vents [22]. Microorganisms residing in
environments contaminated by industrial pollution are often
resistant to multiple pollutants and therefore may need to use
different strategies for the resistance to heavy metals and met-
alloids [5,12,13,23]. Reduced uptake or increased efflux mech-
anism are usually not the main mechanisms of resistance in
microbes resistant to tellurite [1,5,24–27]. Instead, in the case
of K2TeO3, bacteria usually convert it to a less toxic form by
aerobic reduction, ultimately resulting in the formation of in-
tracellular precipitations of black metallic tellurium [28,29].
Reductases such as nitrate reductase have been studied for the
tellurite reduction in Escherichia coli, Rhodobacter, and Ba-
cillus stearothermophilus [30–32]. The formation of black
precipitates during growth in a medium containing tellurite is
the result of the reduction of the colorless K2TeO3 to black
metallic tellurium and has been reported in many bacteria
[25,28,32]. This is applicable to the fact that the Paenibacillus
sp. strain TeW isolated in the present study was resistant to
high concentrations of tellurite but was sensitive to cadmium.
An inducible tellurite resistance activity has been observed
in several microorganisms, including E. coli and Natrono-
coccus occultus [4,21]. We have also isolated two strains of
Shewanella spp. possessing inducible tellurite resistance
(www.serc.msstate.edu/publications2008.html). In contrast,
for this Paenibacillus sp. strain TeW, the mechanism of tel-
lurite resistance is constitutive. Moreover, the growth patterns
(generation, time, etc.) of Paenibacillus sp. strain TeW were
also the same, regardless of the cells grown in the medium
with or without the addition of tellurite. A constitutive, high-
level tellurite resistance trait has also been noted in photo-
synthetic purple, nonsulfur bacteria Rhodobacter sphaeroides
[3,9].
Although aerobic reduction has been suggested as the
mechanism for some microbial species to resist high concen-
trations of K2TeO3 [4,8,22], examples of anaerobic tellurite
resistant or reduction have also been reported [10,33–35]. In
Paenibacillus sp. strain TeW, the amount of tellurite reduction
or removal from the culture medium increased when cells were
incubated statically compared to the cells grown with aerobic

The bacterium was isolated from the sediment by using Luria
Bertani medium (trypton 10 g/L; yeast extract 5 g/L and NaCl
5 g/L) containing 1,000mMK2TeO3 and incubated on an orbital
shaker at 2.5 g at 308C for 48 h. A pure isolate was obtained by
selecting the colonies formed after 24 h growth on the same
medium solidiﬁed by addition of 1.5% (w/v) Bacto agar (BD
Diagnostics). Aseptic techniques were used to avoid any exog-
enous microbial contamination, and the growth of the enrich-
ment cultures was monitored by turbidity.
The isolate was identiﬁed by a combination of 16S-rRNA
gene sequence analysis and its basic physiological and bio-
chemical characteristics. The DNA of strain TeU was extracted
using the Tissue and Cell Genomic DNA Puriﬁcation Kit
(GeneMark, Hopegen Biotechnology Development Enter-
prises). The 16S-rRNA gene of strain TeU was ampliﬁed
with bacterial universal primers: Forward primer F24,
50AGTTTGATYMTGGCTCAG30 and reverse primer F25
50AAGGAGGTGWTCCARCC30, as described [33]. Puriﬁed
polymerase chain reaction (PCR) product (1.5 kb) was then
sequenced and compared with the 16SrRNA gene sequences
available in the database at the National Center for Biotechnol-
ogy Information (NCBI) by using their Web site and the Basic
Local Alignment Search Tool (BLASTn) algorithm. The phy-
logenetic analyses based on 16S-rRNA gene sequences of strain
TeU and related taxa were performed by the ClustalV method
developed by Higgins and Sharp included in Lasergene soft-
ware (DNASTAR) [34].
Transposon mutagenesis
The EZ-Tn5 <R6Kgori/KAN-2> Tnp Transposome Muta-
genesis kit (Epicentre Technologies) was used to generate Cd-
sensitive or reduced tellurite reduction mutants. To obtain a Cd-
sensitive mutant or a reduced tellurite-reduction mutant, we
screened several hundred mutant clones after EZ-Tn5 muta-
genesis. To prepare electrocompetent cells of Pseudomonas sp.
TeU, cells were grown to mid exponential stage, as determined
by an optical density measurement at 600 nm (OD600 0.6) and
then collected and washed with 15ml 10% ice-cold glycerol
three times. The washed cells were then resuspended in 10%
ice-cold glycerol in aliquots of 100ml and were ready for
electroporation of transposon DNA. A 100-ml aliquot of elec-
trocompetent cells of strain TeU was mixed with 1ml EZ-Tn5
<R6Kgori/KAN-2> Tnp Transposome and then subjected to
electroporation with an ECM399 electroporation system (BTX
Harvard Apparatus) in 0.1-cm cuvettes at 1,800V. Themixtures
were then resuspended in 1ml Super Optimal Broth with
catabolite repression [35]. Transformants were then plated on
LB plates containing 30mg/ml kanamycin (LB/Km). Colonies
formed after 2 to 3 d incubation at 308C were selected and
transferred onto LB/Km medium supplemented with CdCl2
(200mM) or K2TeO3 (1000mM). Cells unable to grow in the
medium containing CdCl2 (200mM) or failing to form black
colonies in the presence of K2 TeO3 (1,000mM)were picked for
further study.
Cadmium and tellurite tolerance
To test the extent of heavy metal resistance in the mutants,
cells of strains TeU, AU08, and BU21were grown in 250-ml
Erlenmeyer ﬂasks containing 50ml LB medium supplemented
with different concentrations of Cd (100–1,000mM CdCl2) and
tellurite (200–6,000mM K2TeO3). Cultures were incubated at
308C with shaking to maintain aerobic conditions. Cell growth
was determined by the measurements of optical density at
600 nm (OD600).
Tellurite reduction/removal by bacterial strains
Reduction/removal of tellurite ions (K2TeO3) was evaluated
by determining the concentration of K2TeO3 left in the medium
during bacterial growth. Diethyldithiocarbamate was used for
quantitative determination of K2TeO3 [36]. Cells from strains
TeU, AU08, or BU21 were grown in LB medium containing
200mM, 500mM, or 1,000mM K2TeO3, and the supernatants
were collected by centrifugation at 18,000 g at successive time
intervals. The assay conditions were as follows: 100ml super-
natant was added to 300ml 0.5M Tris HCl (pH 7.0), followed
by addition of 100ml diethyldithiocarbamate reagent (10mM
stock in ddH2O, freshly prepared before assay). The absorbance
of this reaction mixture was measured at 340 nm after mixing,
using a U-2800 spectrophotometer (Hitachi). The residual
K2TeO3 in the supernatants were determined by the colorimet-
ric method, using diethyldithiocarbamate as described, and the
detection limit was 1 to 50mg/ml (4–200mM) [36].
Gene identiﬁcation
Genomic DNA extracted from mutant strains AU08 and
BU21 was digested with different restriction enzymes that do
not cut within (AscI, BglII, EagI, EcoRI,NcoI, SacII) or only cut
the EZ-Tn5 transposon one time (BamHI,HincII, PstI). Restric-
tion fragments were then self-ligated using T4 DNA ligase. The
self-ligated fragments were used as PCR templates for inverse
PCR using Extensor Hi-Fidelity PCR Master Mix (Thermo
Fisher Scientiﬁc) for the ampliﬁcations of the segments ﬂanking
the transposon with KAN-2-FP and R6Kgori/KAN-2-RP as
primers. The signiﬁcant results from the PCR reaction (which
was obtained from a BamHI-digested DNA of strain AU08
and HincII-digested DNA of strain BU21) were cloned into
pGEM-T Easy vector (Promega), and the cloned segments were
then sequenced and compared with the sequences available in
the database at NCBI. The genes of strains AU08 and BU21
affected by EZ-Tn5 insertion were identiﬁed by sequencing the
DNA regions ﬂanking the transposon.
RESULTS
Microbial isolation and identiﬁcation
The bacterium strain TeU isolated from the sediment of the
Er-Jen River in Tainan County, Taiwan [32], is a gram-
negative, rod-shaped, strictly aerobic bacterium and is identiﬁed
as a Pseudomonas species. The sequence of the 16S-rRNA gene
of strain TeU was compared with the sequences available in the
database at NCBI and were shown to be closely related to the
genus Pseudomonas. The phylogeny of Pseudomonas sp. TeU
and some known Pseudomonas spp., based on 16S-rRNA gene
sequence alignment, is shown in Supplemental Data, Figure S1.
Metal/metalloid resistance of Pseudomonas sp. TeU
Pseudomonas sp. strain TeU was able to grow in the LB
medium containing Cd ion (Cd2þ) up to 500mM (Fig. 1). As for
tellurite, the bacterium can tolerate up to 3,000mM K2TeO3 in
the medium and is also able to reduce tellurite to tellurium,
resulting in the formation of black precipitates in cultures grown
in medium containing tellurite. Using diethyldithiocarbamate as
an indicator, we demonstrated that Pseudomonas sp. TeU was
able to reduce or remove more than 90% of tellurite from the
growth medium within 48 h when grown in the LB medium
supplemented with 200mM, 500mM, or 1,000mM K2TeO3.
Concentration of tellurite was not reduced/removed in the
uninoculated medium (results not shown). Figure 2 shows

Pseudomonas sp. strain TeU also can resist concentrations of

the bacterium’s ability to reduce tellurite, because it still could
reduce 80% of tellurite compared with strain TeU (Fig. 6). In the
case of strain AU08, although the ability to reduce tellurite was
greatly reduced in this mutant, no signiﬁcant difference was
seen in the growth patterns between this mutant and wild-type
(strain TeU) in LB medium with or without Cd. The mecha-
nisms for tellurite reduction, therefore, are not the same as for
Cd resistance in this bacterium. The measurements of growth by
optical density at 600 nm in LB containing tellurite up to
1,000mM were comparable for all three strains examined.

amounts of black precipitate (Te) than the other two mutants
