Abstract 
In this study we report on the influences of processes 
parameter for depositing PZT (Lead zirconate titanate) thin 
film on the silicon based Ti/Pt bottom electrode prepared by 
PMS(Pulse Magnetron Sputter) using single metallic target 
with a post deposition rapid thermal annealing. The process 
parameters of pulse frequency, duty cycle, O2/Ar flow rate 
ratio, and post annealing time and temperatures has been 
discussed. The crystalline of film is analyzed by X-ray 
diffractiometer and the morphology, surface structures and 
deposition rates of films is analyzed with SEM.The 
experiments exhibited that the perovskite structure of PZT 
thin film can be obtained with post annealing treatment at 
650℃ for 40s by PMS process at 100℃. The X-ray 
diffraction intensity of perovskite structure decreases with 
annealing time but increases with annealing temperature 
until 750℃. About PMS parameters, the lower pulse 
frequency, say 10kHz, enhance the deposition rate and X-ray 
diffraction intensity of perovskite PZT structure. Also, 
adjusting duty cycle to 80~85% and lower O2/Ar flow rate 
ratio have enhancement on the deposition and X-ray 
intensity. The enhancement of perovskite PZT structure by 
increasing the film thickness is confirmed by SEM and XRD. 
The perovskite single phase of PZT thin film can be 
obtained with pulse frequency under 33kHz, or film 
thickness over 400nm, or O2/Ar flow ratio under 1/10. The 
deposition rate reach 9.4nm/min at 60W input power. 
However, the film cracks and lift-off caused by stress 
appearing as film thickness larger than 550nm, and hillock 
phenomenon of Ti/Pt(50nm/150nm) bottom electrode both 
cause the capacitor devices PZT thin film electrically short. 
 
Key word: Pulse magnetron sputter, PZT thin film, Pulse 
frequency, Duty cycle Deposition rate, Crystalline. 
摘要 
本實驗採用脈衝磁控濺鍍(Pulse magnetron sputter, 
PMS)，以金屬單一靶材通以氧氣反應將鋯鈦酸鉛（PZT）
薄沈積於矽基鉑金屬底電極上，並藉由快速退火使薄膜
結晶產生鈣鈦礦相（Perovskite Phase）。藉由調變 PMS
製程的的脈衝頻率、能率循環、氧氣流量，及退火的時
間與溫度，分析其對薄膜的結晶相、沈積率、以及微結
構的影響。薄膜性質主要以 X-ray 繞射儀進行薄膜結晶相
分析，以 SEM 量測沈積率及觀察薄膜結晶型態及薄膜表
面形貌。實驗結果得知：以 PMS 製程製備 PZT 薄膜，在
100℃的沈積溫度下並無結晶相產生，需再以後退火製程
於溫度 650℃以上始得到鈣鈦礦相，在 750℃獲得最佳之
結晶性。調變不同退火時間於 40 秒至 160 秒，隨退火時
間增加，薄膜之焦綠石相繞射強度亦逐漸增加。脈衝參
數方面，在氧/氬氣流量比為 1:1，沈積溫度 100℃的環境
下，脈衝頻率從 10kHz 至 100kHz，隨脈衝頻率的降低，
薄膜沈積率逐漸增加，且經退火後薄膜鈣鈦礦相的 X-Ray
繞射強度增加並減少焦綠石相（Pyroclore phase）產生。
而隨著能率循環至 95%下降至 80%，會使薄膜沈積率及
鈣鈦礦相的 X-ray 繞射強度增加。當薄膜厚度到達 400nm
以上時，脈衝頻率 33kHz 以下、氧/氬氣流量比為 1:1、
能率循環 90%，或氧 /氬氣流量比為 1/10、脈衝頻率
50kHz、能率循環 90%，皆可獲得單一鈣鈦礦相之 PZT
薄膜。隨膜厚增加可助於鈣鈦礦相生成，且具有較高之
沈積速率，以 60W 濺鍍功率其最高沈積速率可達
9.4nm/min。當薄膜厚度達 550nm 以上，薄膜內應力較大
就會產生明顯浮凸及剝離現象，而在 Ti/Pt（50nm/150nm）
的底電極經快速退火後會產生凸丘（Hillock）現象，使
電容結構之 PZT 薄膜產生短路。 
關鍵字：脈衝磁控濺鍍、PZT 薄膜、脈衝頻率、能率循
環、沈積率、結晶性 
 
1. Introduction 
Lead zirconate titanate(Pb(ZrxTi1-x)O3, PZT)has a 
perovskite structure and is currently the most frequently 
used intelligent ceramics material. Through the use of its 
properties such as piezoelectrics, ferroelectrics, pyroelectrics 
etc., it can be used as many important components such as a 
transducer[1], a film bulk acoustic resonator (FBAR)[2], an 
actuator [3,4] such as an inkjet head [5,6], a micro pump 
[7,8], an optical fiber communication component [9], a 
non-volatile memory [10], a transistor, an infrared sensor 
[11], etc. 
As compared to other thin film deposition technologies, 
PZT piezoelectric thin film made by the sputtering method 
has advantages such as: dense thin film quality [2], mature 
process technology and easy integration. However, thin film 
made by this method has the drawback of a low deposition 
rate commonly seen in the use of ceramic target in the 
sputtering [12]. The use of metallic sputtering target material 
to replace the currently commonly used ceramic target has 
advantages such as: higher deposition rate, easier fabrication 
of metallic target of larger area, higher thermal conductivity 
than ceramic, the capability to be applied directly in the DC 
sputtering [13,14] and RF sputtering [15]; the deposition rate 
can reach 0.48µm/h. However, the use of DC sputtering 
method to deposit metallic oxide has issues such as arc 
discharge and target poisoning; therefore, pulse DC power 
technology is currently the most effective method to solve 
this problem [16]. When the pulse frequency is larger than 
10kHz, almost all the depositions of oxide can be maintained 
for long-term stability [17]. Currently, the use of single 
metallic target and pulse DC sputtering method to deposit 
PZT thin film on the bottom electrode and optical fiber of 
silicon substrate, a film thickness of 1.7µm and of dense and

3.2 Deposition rate analysis 
Fig. 3 illustrates the thin film deposition rate obtained 
at different pulse frequencies, duty cycle 90% and oxygen 
argon flow rate ratio of 1:1. It can be seen from the figure 
that as the pulse frequency gets reduced (pulse period gets 
increased), thin film deposition rate will rise linearly along 
with the pulse width. The wide pulse period provide a more 
stable situation that results primarily from the ratio of metal 
to oxygen arriving at the surface of the growing film (where 
the oxidation reaction takes place) than a narrow one, and 
hence the tendency toward perovskite phase growth, 
therefore, the higher deposition rate is obtained. Fig. 4 
illustrates the thin film deposition rate at different duty 
cycles and at pulse frequency of 50kHz and at oxygen and 
argon flow rate ratio of 1:1. It can be seen from the figure 
that the duty cycle range between 75 and 80% have a better 
deposition rate than the other conditions. Meanwhile, when 
duty cycle exceeds than 80%, the reverse time is shorter, 
lower deposition rate is obtained due to more serious poison 
from target surface. In other words, when the duty cycle is 
lower than 80%, more clean period is introduced during 
deposition process, and result in decreasing deposition rate. 
Fig. 5 illustrates the relationship to deposition rate under 
different oxygen flow rates and at pulse frequency of 50kHz 
and duty cycle 90%. It can be seen from the figure that once 
the oxygen enters the system, the deposition rate becomes 
greatly reduced, then it will enter the oxide mode with a 
deposition rate of about half of the metallic mode; when the 
oxygen flow rate ratio is increased to 50%, its deposition 
rate is about 0.2 times that at the metallic mode. The major 
reason is because the sputtering rate of the oxide is lower 
than that of metal and the ionization rate of the argon ion is 
reduced after the pass of oxygen [12]. It can be seen from 
Fig. 5 that under oxygen and argon flow rate ratio of 3.3% 
(1:30), the currently reached deposition rate can be as high 
as 9nm/min.

3.3 Crystalline phase analysis of the thin film 
Fig. 6 illustrates the X-ray diffraction patterns at 
different pulse frequencies and duty cycle 90%, oxygen and 
argon flow rate ratio of 1:1 and film thickness controlled at 
400±30nm. It can be seen from the figure that as the pulse 
frequency gets reduced, the X-Ray diffraction intensity of 
perovskite phase will increase, which means that lower pulse 
frequency is helpful to the formation of perovskite phase of 
PZT thin film; however, with film thickness of under 400nm 
and a frequency of below 25kHz, the deposited thin film 
shows single perovskite phase. The reason might be because 
that the lower pulse frequency (wide pulse period) 
deposition condition provide a more stable situation that 
results primarily from the ratio of metal to oxygen arriving 
at the surface of the growing film (where the oxidation 
reaction takes place) than a higher one, and hence the 
tendency toward perovskite phase growth. Fig. 7 shows the 
X-ray diffraction patterns at different duty cycles and at a 
pulse frequency of 50kHz, oxygen and argon flow rate ratio 
of 1:1 and film thickness of 400±30nm. It can be seen from 
the figure that a better perovskite phase diffraction pattern 
can be obtained at duty cycle from 75% to 85%, the amount 
of oxygen incorporated into the coating at constant oxygen 
flow rate could be substantially lower at these duty periods 
than at 90%, where the arrival of metal flux at the substrate 
is slower due to serious poison from target surface, and 
result in decreasing plasma density. Therefore, low 
deposition rate and weak perovskite phase peak are obtained. 
Fig. 8 shows the PZT thin film X-ray diffraction patterns 
obtained under different oxygen and argon flow rate ratio 
and at pulse frequency of 50kHz, duty cycle of 90kHz and 
deposition time of 1 hour. It can be seen from the figure that 
when the oxygen and argon flow rate ration is 1:1, 
perovskite phase and pyrochlore phase will be formed

計畫成果自評 
本研究之旨在利用直流脈衝磁控濺鍍法（Pulse 
Magnetron Sputter, PMS）沈積 PZT 壓電薄膜於矽基鉑/鈦
金屬電極基板上，相異於傳統 PZT 陶瓷濺射靶而選用單
一 Pb/Zr/Ti 之合金金屬靶材，並通氧氣作為反應性氣體。
對濺鍍製程各參數進行濺鍍參數測試實驗，藉以瞭解其
參數設定範圍對製程之影響及可行性，並找出快速退火
製程之最佳參數並歸納出下列幾點可行性後續研究: 
1. 本研究使用單一金屬靶材通氧氣做反應性濺鍍，未來
可精確針對氧氣流量對薄膜沈積率之探討，瞭解脈衝
磁控反應性濺鍍於金屬模式高速堆積至可行性，降有
助於 PZT 應用於厚膜致動器之發展。 
2. 本研究以控制相同鍍膜時間，發現 PZT 薄膜之鈣鈦
礦結晶性與膜厚有正相關，未來將以控制相同膜厚，
進一步瞭解脈衝參數對薄膜特性之影響。 
3. 以金屬靶濺鍍 PZT 薄膜，靶材所含鉛因低融點特性
致使工作溫度與功率有所限制，未來可以氧化鉛與鋯
鈦金屬製作靶材之探討，以提高製程窗口與穩定性。 
4. 本研究發現脈衝參數皆對 PZT 結晶性質有所影響，
應進一步瞭解脈衝參數對電漿性質之影響，直接瞭解
電漿特性對 PZT 結晶性質之影響機制。 
5. 本研究發現 PZT 及底電極之薄膜應力可能破壞薄膜
品質，未來可針對製程參數探討降低 PZT 薄膜應力，
或增加 Pt 底電極金屬厚度以避免凸丘現象產生。

一、 會議簡介 
本次會議是在新加坡舉辦的 Thin Films 2008國際研討會。本次會議的時間為 2008
年7月13日至7月16日，分類如下： MPF; OFN; NNF; ETF FPT; CCE; OPF; SND; 
TCR; BCT; ODF; ONF等12個大主題，本次會議提供了一個相當好的知識交流平
台，提供與會的研究者交換最新的研究成果，並討論薄膜與奈米科技在未來的研
究方向與趨勢。整體而言，會議所涵蓋的範圍包含了薄膜與奈米的機械、製程、
材料、及元件應用等等，參與的學者橫跨物理、化學、材料、機電、生物、能源
等領域的大師，大家齊聚一堂共同討論薄膜與奈米科技在各種不同領域的最新研
究成果與應用，對於未來薄膜與奈米的研究也有很大的助益。 
本次會議的投稿論文約為 800篇，總共錄取的篇數為 660篇，約為 82.5%。總共
的作者人數為 660人，分佈在30個不同的國家，包含澳洲、加拿大、中國、法國、
德國、日本、印度、台灣.. 等等，被錄取的文章中，數量最多的是我國，約佔總
論文數之35%。在大會中口頭發表 240篇，海報發表為420篇。在本次會議中，來
自於美國的文章，及歐洲國家文章並不多。此行最大的收穫為獲得生物及能源（太
陽光電）薄膜相關研究上最新的發展趨勢，對於今後研究計畫的執行與未來研究
方向的規劃將有莫大的助益，本次會議由於主題眾多，因此分為四天舉行，同時
