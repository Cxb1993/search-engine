(Distribution System)對成本占有相當的影
響。Simchi-Levi et al. (2007)指出在美國大
約有 10％的國內生產總值(gross domestic 
product, GDP)與供應鏈相關活動有關，而
在個別的產業中，將產品從原物料到最後
顧客手中的所有配送過程，其成本比例往
往超過 10％。一個物流配送系統的設計與
許多決策相關，其中包含設施區位問題
(facility location problem, FLP)與車輛途程
問題(vehicle routing problem, VRP)等。 
因 區 位 途 程 問 題 (location routing 
problem, LRP)是一個包含途程規畫的設施
區位決策，其考量的是整合性的物流配送
規畫，因此求解將更為複雜。一般是將區
位途程問題分解為設施區位問題、場站與
顧客的分配問題與車輛途程問題進行求
解，且甚少考量顧客的時窗(time window)
限制。因此，本研究將針對時窗限制多場
站車輛途程問題(multiple depots vehicle 
routing problem with Time Windows, 
MDVRPTW)進行探討。 
近年來 VRP 為實體物流研究中一個
重要的管理議題，而 MDVRPTW 問題為
VRP 的延伸。實務上，物流系統中往往具
有多個物流中心，且須在給定的時窗限制
下對顧客進行服務。因此多場站車輛途程
問題(multiple depot vehicle routing problem, 
MDVRP) 與 時 窗 限 制 車 輛 途 程 問 題
(vehicle routing problem with time windows, 
VRPTW)同為 VRP 中兩個重要的變形問
題。在 MDVRP 的網路中顧客可由數個物
流中心場站進行服務，每個場站有各自的
車隊，車隊由所屬場站出發，服務多個顧
客後再回到原場站。另一方面，VRPTW
中，每個顧客有其時窗的限制，顧客須在
最早開始服務時間(earliest start time)與最
晚開始服務時間(latest start time)的區間內
開始被車輛服務。此兩類變形問題的研究
多是採分別求解方式，因此，本研究計畫
同時將場站的分配、車輛途程及時窗限制
規畫等問題，一併納入演算法求解，已期
望達到更好的求解品質。 
MDVRPTW 問題可定義為一個運輸
網路中，由一個物流中心(場站)提供具相
同車容量的配送車隊對已知需求量的顧客
群進行服務。透過決定每輛車的服務顧客
與配送路線，達到總配送成本最小的目
標。此外，每條路線僅由一輛車進行服務，
每輛車須從場站出發，最後再回到場站；
每輛車所服務顧客的需求量總和不得超過
車容量限制，亦或採用每輛車總服務時間
必須小於時間上限的限制；每一需求點皆
必須被服務，且僅能被一輛車服務一次，
再者，車輛到達需求點之開始服務時間，
須在顧客所提出的時窗區間內。若車輛比
最早開始服務時間還早到達顧客點，則須
等待至最早開始服務時間，始可進行配送
服務；反之，若晚於最晚開始服務時間到
達顧客點，則違反時窗限制為不可行解。 
MDVRPTW 屬於 NP-hard 的問題，確
定解方法並不易在合理時間內求解較大型
的問題。近年來，巨集啟發式演算法
(meta-heuristic algorithm)崛起，其中蟻群
最佳化演算法 (ant colony optimization, 
ACO)在求解 VRP 上有非常突出的表現，
然而在多場站車輛途程問題的應用並不
多，因此，本研究計畫之目的是針對具容
量及時窗限制的車輛途程問題提出一個多
蟻群演算法 (multiple ant colony system, 
MACS)，將採用二階段(兩蟻群)之初始解
建構準則，第一階段為顧客點指派準則，
第二階段為分別對各場站的途程建構準
則，藉此有效求解 MDVRPTW 問題。

3.3 MACS 區域搜尋法 
每次產生建構解後，MACS 採用兩種
區域搜尋法來改善建構解，第一種為兩場
站間的插入法：將一顧客點從原本的場站
的途程中移除插入至另一個場站的途程
中；第二種為兩場站間的交換法：將兩個
屬於不同場站的顧客點彼此交換至對方的
途程中。為節省計算成本，ACS 於求解
VRPTW 之區域搜尋法將只應用於迭代最
佳解上，然而 MACS 的建構解步驟中包含
ACS 演算法，計算較為耗時。因此，本研
究的 MACS 將只使用較少數量的蟻群以
節省計算時間，而對每組建構解皆進行區
域搜尋法改善，進一步提升求解品質。

 given n demand nodes, flows between all origin-destination pairs and the required number of 
hubs (p), the objective is to find the hub locations and minimize the total transportation cost 
for serving all flows. The p-hub median problem is NP-hard, even if the locations of hubs are 
fixed, the allocation part of the problem remains NP-hard (Love et al., 1988). The hub 
location problem (HLP) is extended from p-hub median problem in that the number of hubs is 
endogenous and the HLP considers the fixed cost for establishing a hub. This paper deals with 
the uncapacitated single allocation hub location problem (USAHLP) whose objective is to 
decide the number and locations of the hubs and allocate the non-hub nodes to hubs so that 
the total transportation cost is minimized. 
 
The hub location problem was first introduced by O’Kelly (1992) with the fixed cost for hubs 
making the number of hubs as a decision variable. He formulated a quadratic integer 
programming model for this problem. Until 1994, Campbell presented the first linear 
programming formulations for the uncapacitated/capacitated single/multiple allocation hub 
location problems. 
 
For uncapacitated single allocation hub location problem (USAHLP), several studies have 
been published with different approaches to solve the problem. Abdinnour-Helm and 
Venkataramanan (1998) proposed a new quadratic formulation, which is based on the idea of 
multi-commodity flows in the network. They solved this problem by using the branch and 
bound approach. Unfortunately, the computational time is very consuming except for the 
small scale instances. They also proposed the other heuristic, a genetic algorithm, for solving 
the problem to meet the shorter computation time requirement. 
 
Abdinnour-Helm (1998) proposed a new hybrid heuristic which combined the genetic 
algorithm and tabu search. First, the genetic algorithm is used to determine the number and 
locations of hubs, and then allocate the non-hub nodes to its closest hubs to form an initial 
solution for tabu search to improve and obtain the optimal solution. CAB data set results by 
this hybrid algorithm were compared with the genetic algorithm of Abdinnour-Helm and 
Venkataramanan (1998). The new hybrid heuristic leads to much better solutions than using 
genetic algorithm alone. 
 
Although the previous studies have tested the hybrid genetic heuristic and found it to be better 
than the genetic algorithm, Topcupglu et al. (2005) proposed another genetic algorithm for the 
USAHLP. Their genetic algorithm outperformed the hybrid genetic heuristic proposed in 
Abdinnour-Helm (1998) with respect to solution quality and computational time. Unaware of 
the work by Topcupglu et al. (2005), Cunha and Silva (2007) proposed a new genetic 
algorithm combined with simulated annealing algorithm. Their hybrid heuristic outperformed 
the genetic algorithms of Abdinnour-Helm and Venkataramanan (1998) and Abdinnour-Helm 
(1998). From the review of above studies, all of them proposed population-based heuristic to 
solve USAHLP. Chen (2007) proposed the simulated annealing algorithm (SA) with tabu lists 
for the uncapacitated hub location problem (SATLUHLP). The results of this new heuristic 
outperformed the heuristic presented by Topcupglu et al. (2005) both in computational time 
and solution quality.

MSTS-3) and a two-stage tabu search (HubTS). In their study, they compared these two

2. RELATED LITERATURE 
 
Schefcyzk (1993) showed that for internal 
benchmarking, traditional ratio approaches correlate with 
simple DEA models. The case study of sixteen warehouses 
performance evaluation showed that simple cost-based 
measures to be suitable for internal benchmarking. Cohen 
et al. (1997) conducted an industrial survey of service parts 
logistics and defined relevant performance measures and 
measured the achieved values of such measures. However, 
the comparative results were based on a number of 
normalized ratios of specific financial and service 
performance variables. 
Hackman et al. (2001) argued that ratio-based 
performance measures are inaccurate and inappropriate for 
warehousing. Data envelopment analysis (DEA) is 
regarded as an appropriate tool for this task because of its 
capability to capture simultaneous all the relevant inputs 
(resources) and outputs (performances), to construct the 
best performance frontier, and to reveal the relative 
shortcomings of inefficient warehouses. Ross and Droge 
(2002) measured 102 distribution centers’ productivity, and 
identified distribution centers with consistent best 
performance using facet analysis, and detected performance 
trends using DEA window analysis of 4 years data. 
An internet-based DEA system (iDEA) for 
warehouses was designed by the Keck Lab at Georgia Tech 
(McGinnis et al., 2002). Because of price sensitivity and 
service requirements among customers, increasing 
efficiency is of critical importance. Thus, DC managers in 
the competing 3PL environment would like to identify 
improvements through the development of standards for 
comparison to similarly situated DCs. Johnson et al. (2010) 
described the development of large-scale internet 
benchmarking instance, iDEAs-W and their findings from 
this ongoing collaboration between academia and the 
warehousing industry.  
Hamdan and Rogers (2008) proposed a new weight-
restricted DEA model with four inputs, labor, space, 
technology cost and material handling equipment cost, and 
three outputs, shipping volume, order filling and space 
utilization, to evaluate 19 warehouses. Zhou et al. (2008) 
developed two DEA models to measure the operational 
efficiency of ten leading 3PL in China with multiple year 
data. Min and Joo (2006, 2009) also applied DEA on 
evaluating the 3PL providers in USA. De Koster and Balk 
(2008) evaluated the efficiency of 65 European distribution 
centers (EDCs) and compared results for years 2000 and 
2004. They found that public EDC warehouses run by 
logistics service providers are more efficient than own-
account EDC warehouses. Manrodt and Vitasek (2010) 
identified common metrics that impact both distribution 
and manufacturing. They found that 3PLs are being 
required, measured and rewarded on driving performance 
for value for their customers. Senior management is paying 
more attention to performance measures to gain better 
understanding of the turmoil in the economy and cut costs 
through efficiencies. Gu et al. (2010) reviewed the 
benchmarking and analytical models for warehouse 
performance evaluation in terms of cost, throughput, space 
utilization, and service provides. 
In this paper, we adopt both the Charnes, Cooper and 
Rhodes (CCR) model and Banker, Charnes and Cooper 
(BCC) model among several different types of DEA 
models. The CCR model differs from the BCC model in 
that the former considers constant returns to scale, whereas 
the latter considers variable (decreasing or increasing) 
returns to scale and thus mitigates the impact of economies 
of scale on the efficiency.  
 
3. METHODOLOGY 
 
DEA is a non-parametric mathematical programming 
approach for measuring relative efficiencies of comparable 
DMUs with respect to multiple inputs and outputs in a 
specific situation. With the use of DEA, the most 
appropriate set of weights for all the inputs and outputs are 
obtained so that the resulting efficiency scores are less than 
or equal to one. Consider a set of n DMUs, with each DMU 
j (j = 1, …, n), using m inputs xij (i = 1, …, m) and 
generating s outputs ykj (k = 1, …, s). In the absence of 
priori knowledge on weights, viand uk, that are associated 
with input i and output k, the DEA model allows each 
DMU to choose the weights of its inputs and outputs in 
order to maximize its own efficiency score with respect to 
the others. Charnes et al. (1978) solved the particular non-
linear programming problem to obtain the appropriate 
weights for a given DMU. Specifically, the CCR model for
