植物根系對土壤強度影響之研究。 
在 Wynn 與 Mostaghimi 的研究中，提出土
壤侵蝕是一種複雜的現象，主要在於土方的密
度，而冷凍/解凍循環、土壤先前的含水量、直
徑 2~20 mm 的根系密度、土壤質地、土壤孔隙
水與河水的交互作用等均對土壤的侵蝕與臨界
剪應力有明顯的影響—亦即土壤之形態。相對
於其他的土壤物理與化學參數，本研究首次以
現地測試植被河岸之侵蝕性，並提出植被在河
岸侵蝕影響之量化分析（Wynn and Mostaghimi, 
2006）。 
綜由以上文獻回顧，可知植栽對於裸露河
床與護岸而言，具有一定的保護作用，但是卻
很少從土壤本身的抗流反應機制來看其耐受極
限，也說明本研究的意義。 
三、實驗設備與研究方法 
3.1 研究方法 
本研究擬探究裸露河床土壤自身的耐沖刷
範圍，以及從不同流速變化下土壤被沖刷的粒
徑大小比較，探討不同流速變化下裸露河溪之
土壤沖刷反應，裸露土壤實驗結果將與植生實
驗水道之沖刷反應做比較，以瞭解河溪土壤本
身抗沖刷之耐受極限與反應機制，以及植被對
河床或護岸土壤產生的保護作用，可以更精確
掌握河溪整治工程中正確的河床與護岸處理技
術，提供河岸邊坡工程設計施工時的參考依據。 
3.2 實驗水道設備及實驗

表性類型為實驗水道設計依據。以現地常見流

屯溪河床周邊土壤為栽培介質，儘可能以現地
栽培介質為主。

3.5.1 篩分析實驗

monitor the performance of a structural BMP and the occasional sampling data might 
be not sufficient to reflect the complete BMP situation. Once a structural BMP runs 
stable, a validated model is helpful to replace human works and serves as an 
automatic monitoring tool with minimum cost. Modeling can provide continuous 
predictions for the future performance of BMPs if particular weather scenario is 
determined. In this study, BMP ToolBox model developed by Tetra Tech and George 
Prince’s County in Maryland, USA, was employed to evaluate a realistic BMP site in 
Taiwan. The case study was designed to remove nonpoint pollution sources from tea 
gardens to protect the water quality in Feitsui Reservoir. The BMP ToolBox model 
was calibrated and verified with two year sampling data and the one-factor-at-a-time 
method (OFAT) was applied for sensitive analysis of the model. The modeling results 
were satisfied, in which the coefficient of determination (R2) is 0.87 and 0.8 for 
calibration and verification, respectively. Several rainfall types were used as future 
weather scenario for prediction of the BMP performance and the results showed that 
the total phosphorous is removed up to 76% when the return period of rainfall is 25 
years and the duration is 4 hours. The application of BMP toolbox in this study 
demonstrates a good experience and the study process can be benefit for evaluation of 
the current and future performance of a structural BMP entity.
