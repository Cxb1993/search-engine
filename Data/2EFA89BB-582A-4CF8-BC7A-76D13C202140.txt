3.1 晶片製作與接合 
    本實驗採用CNC微加工技術製作流體晶片，選用
方便加工與工作流體不易產生反應的壓克力PMMA
為基材。旋轉微流體晶片的基材事實上為直徑10 cm
的圓形光透明壓克力光碟片。PMMA對於偵測光學與
螢光有良好的光學性質，其折射率約為1.49，比重在
1.18至1.20。微流體晶片上片壓克力厚度為0.5 mm，
下片壓克力厚度2 mm，上下片壓克力以熱壓接合方
式接合。

本研究以旋轉方式產生離心力驅動微量血液，並
設計特殊微流道結構使血液分離，探討微流體晶片當
作血球容積計與觀察活體型態之可行性。本實驗利用
光學顯微鏡觀察血球在流體晶片中之型態，並同時以
螢光染劑在螢光顯微鏡下檢測血球是否為活體，再利
用Hct實驗機制同步拍攝動態影像，計算其像數當作
血球容積計使用。本微流體晶片同時可以檢測四組不
同之檢體。在螢光偵測時，則先以DPBS溶液將血液
稀釋成30倍，再混合濃度1 mM的螢光染劑在螢光顯
微鏡放大150倍下來判定血球是否為活體細胞，其血
球型態則可以進一步用來檢測肝病與腎病。目前此實

The two fluid streams for mixing were ferric 
chloride (FeCl3‧6H2O) and ammonium thiocyanate 
(NH4SCN) dissolved in deionized (DI) water at the 
same concentration of 0.2 mol/kg. The ferric chloride 
solution is pale yellow while the ammonium 
thiocyanate solution is colorless. When these two 
solutions come into contact, the ferric ions bind with 
the thiocyanate ions instead of the chloride ions to 
produce blood-red color (Shakhashiri 1983). The 
intensity of the red-blood colour may represent the 
amount of fluids that mixed and reacted and was 
quantified as the index of mixing efficiency between 
the two fluids. 
Figure 2 shows the schematic diagram of 
experimental setup for visualization of mixing in the 
micromixer on a rotating disk. The apparatus used 
for the flow visualization experiments included the 
rotation platform, the micro-image-capturing unit and 
the light source. The microfluidic disk was spun by a 
step motor having a speed accuracy of ±0.2%. The 
micro-image-capturing unit was composed of a CCD 
camera (CV-M71CL, JAI, 768 × 576 pixels, 60 
frames/s), a frame grabber (Metero-II/CL, Matrox), 
and a microscope (2× objective) above the disk. In 
order to synchronize the image capturing unit with 
the rotational motion, the CCD camera was triggered 
through a photo diode (wavelength 320-730 nm). A 
He-Ne laser (wavelength 633 nm) of 5 mW was used 
as the triggering light source. A halogen light (MHF-
G150LR, Moritex) was placed beneath the disk to 
produce sufficient light for illuminating the flow field 
undergoing a rotation up to 1200 rpm. In 
synchronization with the rotational motion, the CCD 
camera could be triggered to allow only one shot of 
the targeted object on the rotating disk per revolution.

RRESULTS AND DISCUSSION  
In addition to the visualization experiments, numerical 
simulations were performed to reveal the flow patterns 
of fluids A and B in the rotating U-shaped channel. We 
employed computational fluid dynamic package Fluent 
(version 6.2.16) to compute the flow field and 
concentration distribution from the continuity, Navier-
Stokes, and species equations. Figure 3 shows the 
simulation result of concentration for the disk rotating at 
600 rpm counterclockwise (ccw). The two fluids were 
taken to have the same properties as water but with 
different concentrations. At the inlets, red color 
represents fluid A of species concentration1.0, and blue 
color for fluid B of concentration 0.  As the two fluids 
fully mix, the color turns green representing a 
concentration of 0.5. It can be seen that Coriolis begins 
to influence the interface in the straight channel region, 
pushing fluid A toward upper and lower walls and fluid 
B toward sidewall A to form a “C-shaped” interface in 
section 1 at x = 4.2 mm. Subsequently, the 90° curves 
appear to stir the fluids by flipping fluids A and B. 
This can be seen first in section 3 changing from AB 
to BA and then in section 4 where fluid A splits to 
sandwich fluid B forming ABA. In addition to flipping, 
interface folding also becomes more significant as 
the flow turns to section 4. After flowing through 
these curves, the two fluids are well mixed in section 
5 (x = 4.2 mm). Figure 4 shows the mixing efficiency

is well predicted by the simulations with a constant 
diffusion coefficient of 3×10-10 m2/s. The measured 
mixing efficiency for the single-U mixer increases 
from 48% at Ω = 360 rpm to more than 80% at Ω = 
720 rpm and near 86% at Ω = 900 rpm. Counter-
clockwise rotation is slightly higher than clockwise 
rotation by about 5% at Ω = 480 rpm. But at Ω = 600 
rpm, clockwise rotation jumps to lead the counter-
clockwise rotation by approximately 10%. The 
measured mixing efficiency for the double U-mixer is 
apparently higher than that for the single U-mixer by 
an amount of 10-20%. The efficiency increases from 
60-65% at Ω = 360 rpm to reach nearly 99% at Ω = 
720 and 900 rpm for both clockwise and counter-
clockwise cases. The simulations that cover a 
rotational range of 120-1200 rpm show a good 
agreement with measurements. Overall, the 
simulated efficiencies appear slightly lower than the 
measured values with an average difference of 4%.  
Moreover, the simulations show no discernable 
difference between clockwise and counter-clockwise 
cases.
