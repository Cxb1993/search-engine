中文摘要 
本研究係以二水磷酸氫鈣(CaHPO4‧2H2O，DCPD)為原料，利用水解法在氫氧化鈉水溶液(NaOH(aq))
中合成氫氧基磷灰石(Ca10(PO4)6(OH)2，HA)，改變製程參數以尋求水解條件的最佳化，並達成降低燒
結溫度的目標。在75℃之2.5 M NaOH(aq)中水解合成1 h可獲得奈米低結晶化粉末，寬50 nm，長100 nm，
顯現{0 0 2n}面的奈米 HA異向性結晶粉末。添加陽離子型介面分散劑十六烷基三甲基溴化銨(CTAB)
參與反應時，在 1×10-3 M濃度形成微胞，阻止了晶粒成長，可將 HA晶粒大小降至 20 nm寬，50 nm
長。 
將不同之 Ca/P值(1.0~1.8)的 HA粉末壓胚並在 800oC進行 4 h燒結，其{0 0 2n}之異向性結晶現象
在 Ca/P>1.5後消失，且密度約達 2.8 g/cm3，此乃因 rhenanite相(NaCaPO4)相隨 Ca/P比之增加而增加所
致。此外，並可在晶界上觀察出非晶相的形成。 
將 Ca/P=1.5之 HA粉末壓胚並經 800oC燒結 4 h後的燒結體，在 37oC之生理食鹽水中進行長期浸
漬測試，發現隨浸漬時間增加至 2周後有焦磷酸鈣相(CPP)結晶，並因 CPP的形成使燒結體表面硬度下
降。另於體外以皮膜纖維母細胞 3T3在 37oC進行細胞培養實驗，4 d後發現周圍細胞的分裂延展以及
析出的 CPP結晶，顯示在水解時，添加碳酸鈣所合成之 HA粉末在 800oC的低溫燒結後，有良好的生
物相容性。 
 
關鍵字：氫氧基磷灰石，水解，低溫燒結，浸漬試驗，焦磷酸鈣

第一章 簡介 
1-1 前言 
近年來醫學越來越發達，人的壽命也因此延長，但人體生理組織還是會隨著年齡增長而退化甚至
死亡，所以對於可植入人體並替代器官的生醫相關材料越來越熱門。 
生醫材料的發現為二戰時因飛機碎片彈射入人體內沒被發現取出，隨著這名士兵年老照射X光後，
發現原來人造化合物可以長期植入人體而沒有引發任何的不適反應，此現象被發現後，科學家紛紛投
入生醫材料的研發。因此生醫材料的定義為：植入人體後，持續或間斷的與體液接觸，以達成所需的
生理與醫療用途。由於生醫材料長期植入人體，所以研發生醫材料時除了要考慮材料本身性質外，更
要兼顧在生物體內的適應性，即生物相容性(Biocompatibility)。常見條件為[1,2]： 
(1) 無毒性、無刺激性、無血栓性、無致癌性等，不產生對生物體有害的作用。 
(2) 具有組織親合性，能和骨骼或組織緊密結合。 
(3) 具有與生物體相近的機械性質，不造成破壞或腐蝕。 
(4) 能長期處於生物體或被生物體吸收，不因時間而改變其性質。 
(5) 易於製造、加工及後續滅菌處理。 
而本研究探討的是生醫材料中的人造骨頭－氫氧基磷灰石。 
氫氧基磷灰石是脊椎動物硬組織中的自然無機成分。是生物材料中具生物活性的磷酸鈣陶瓷材料，
常被用來修補非病理性骨缺損[3]。氫氧基磷灰石具有良好的生物相容性，不會引起發炎反應或排斥反
應[4]，與骨頭有強大的結合能力，可與骨頭功能性的合而為一，於非骨組織中，不會誘導新的骨頭生
成，也不會刺激骨頭生長加快，並且有特殊的骨傳導 (osteoconductive)特性[5]，提供一種具引導性生
長性質且適於新骨沉積的物理性基質而使骨細胞生長進入原來不會生長的區域之中[3]。 
在大部分的研究當中，合成氫氧基磷灰石製程上都需要高溫熱處理或是繁複的程序，便於製作出
高結晶度的氫氧基磷灰石，但也會因高溫而使的氫氧基磷灰石晶粒過度成長，失去了天然骨骼的結構
並間接的降低了生物吸收性與反應性，因此合成奈米級氫氧基磷灰石並改質是我們的研究重點。 
1-2 研究目的 
本計畫第一年實驗中，已成功製備出了奈米級氫氧基磷灰石，第二年研究主要目的在氫氧基磷灰
石摻雜其他微量元素來進行改質。在人體骨骼中主要成分為鈣磷氧化合物，還有其他的微量元素如氟、
鋁、鉻、硒、錳、鍶...等，其中元素鍶可以減緩骨骼的溶解速率，並可以增加成骨細胞的成長速率，
故在醫藥上可用來預防並治療骨質疏鬆症。因此我們在這一年的研究中，我們希望透過摻雜元素鍶進
行氫氧基磷灰石的改質，希望改質後的氫氧基磷灰石能在植入初期透過元素鍶的刺激，使成骨細胞增
生並增加其生長速率，加速骨骼傷口的癒合。 
本研究選用與前一年相同的製程方式，透過水解法並搭配微乳化法，在水解法製程中加入介面活性劑
及逆微胞技術，提供並限制其奈米微粒的成核環境，進而控制奈米微粒的粒徑與其幾何形狀。

2-2-5 熱行為分析及燒結製程 
    熱行為之分析亦分為兩部分，一為粉末煅燒處理，探討粉末受熱後之基本性質變化，以作為燒結
探討之參考；二為壓胚燒結處理，探討以不同鈣磷比製備的粉末，在不同溫度下壓胚燒結後之差異，
並探討其最佳條件。 
本部分研究首先取用上一部分所製備之Ca/P=1.0 (DCPD之鈣磷比)以及Ca/P=1.67 (HA之鈣磷比)兩
種粉末進行測試，將兩種粉末在大氣中以1oC/min之升溫速率，分別加熱至600oC，800oC及1000oC並持
溫4 h以進行煅燒處理，而後爐冷至室溫，研磨過篩(300 mesh)待測。 
所得之不同鈣磷比(Ca/P=1.0~1.8)的粉末，秤量0.25 g，於直徑10 mm的模具中以23 MPa壓製成錠，
再將其置於大氣中以2oC/min之升溫速率，分別加熱至600oC-1000oC之間的選取溫度持溫4 h，經燒結後
之試片爐冷至室溫，並與熱差分析儀所獲得之結果相互比對。 
2-2-6 生物性質分析 
    有關於本研究的生物性質分析分為三部分，一為燒結體經浸漬不同時間後之差異，二為粉末萃取
液之細胞培養，三為燒結體之細胞培養。 
首先取Ca/P=1.5所合成的HA粉末，於800oC進行4 h之燒結體，以20 ml之生理食鹽水(NaCl 0.3mg/L)
在37oC分別浸漬2 d，4 d，7 d，14 d，28 d與42 d後，以去離子水洗掉表面殘留的生理食鹽水但不破壞
表面狀態，並在60oC乾燥待測，而後對浸漬後的燒結體與生理食鹽水進行各項分析，並比較浸漬前後
之性質。 
粉末萃取液之細胞培養部份，採用皮膜纖維母細胞3T3為目標，選定Ca/P=1.0與1.5的粉末進行體外
細胞存活率研究，並以Merck商業用HA粉末進行比較。由於所合成的粉末相當細小，細胞長在粉末上
的現象較難觀測，因而以萃取液培養細胞，再以新鮮的培養液同步培養作為控制組。粉末於121oC經過
20min的高壓滅菌後，將粉末浸漬於培養液(culture medium)中一段時間(4 d，7 d，14 d，21 d)後，取其
萃取液100 μl注入96孔的培養盤，每孔再植入1x104個3T3細胞，分別進行1 - 4 d的培養後，以pH試紙量
測培養液之pH值，再吸去所有的萃取液與培養液，後以MTT測定法[3-(4,5-dimethylthiazol-2-yl)-2,5- 
diphenyltetrazoleliumbromide]量測培養盤中細胞數量，將實驗組細胞數量除以控制組細胞數量則為生存
率，以比較生存率的方式顯示細胞成長的趨勢。 
最後，再取Ca/P=1.5粉末於800oC燒結4 h的燒結體進行體外細胞培養，將燒結體表面磨薄拋光後置
入直徑80 mm的培養皿內，經過75%酒精水溶液滅菌浸漬24 h後，去除酒精後以培養液清洗，再以新鮮
的培養液完全浸蓋燒結體，注入1x106個3T3細胞，在37oC的CO2環境中進行貼附實驗，每2 d換一次培
養液以提供細胞足夠的養分，經過4 d的培養後以標準程序脫水固定，再進行觀察。

2-3-10  MTT分析 
MTT是3-(4,5-dimethylthiazol-2-yl)-2,5-diphenyltetrazolelium- bromide之縮寫，為一黃色染劑，加入
細胞培養液中，擴散進入細胞中，經粒線體之琥珀酸去氫脢(succinate dehydrogenase)去氫還原後形成藍
紫色的formazan結晶，用DMSO將formazan結晶溶出，可得到藍紫色溶液，於540 nm測吸光值。 
    MTT轉變僅在活細胞中進行，並堆積在細胞內，且 formazan 形成量與細胞數目呈正比。由於細
胞還原MTT的能力，代表了細胞粒腺體的活性，因此可做為細胞存活率的指標。由於擁有快速、經濟
和無放射性污染之優點，常被使用為細胞存活率及巨噬細胞吞噬能力之測定，經比較MTT呈色法所測
定細胞存活率，與放射性核酸併入法之結果相關性可達 0.8。

3-1-3 NaOH濃度變化的影響 
    圖3-4所示者為在不同之NaOH濃度下，於75oC進行1 h水解合成所得粉末的XRD圖。由圖中之結果
發現，在NaOH濃度為0.1 M時合成所得的HA粉末，除其繞射峰呈現嚴重寬化的現象外，(002)面的繞射
強度亦相當低，顯示大部分產物仍以ACP型態存在。HA的形成並不完整，但隨著NaOH(aq)濃度的增加，
除(002)繞射峰的強度亦隨之逐漸增加外，(211)與(300)繞射峰的分離程度亦隨濃度之增加而益發明顯，
顯示的HA結構逐漸完整。在NaOH溶液濃度為2.5 M時分離程度最為明顯，而後在NaOH(aq)為5 M時又
開始降低，而當NaOH(aq)的濃度在 10 M時則不再有HA的形成，反而轉為八鈣磷酸鹽相
[Ca8H2(PO4)6•5H2O, Octa-calcium phosphate, OCP]的形成。 
    圖3-5所示者為在不同濃度時水解所得之粉體的FT-IR光譜，圖中之各光譜均顯示在563與605 cm-1

3-5 燒結製程分析 
3-5-1 不同鈣磷比之 HA粉末的高溫相變化 
以不同鈣磷比之 DCPD與 CaCO3混合粉末，於 75℃之 2.5M NaOH的水溶液中水解合成所得之粉
末經 800oC燒結 4 h後，所獲得之 XRD繞射圖分別如圖 3-24與圖 3-25所示。由圖 3-24發現，當 HA
之 Ca/P之值由 1.0到 1.4時，所有的燒結體皆顯示以良好結晶化之 HA為主相，並伴隨少量 rhenanite
相的形成。此外，並無 CaO或β-TCP的存在。 
圖 3-25顯示 Ca/P=1.5至 1.8之粉末在 800oC經過 4 h的燒結後，仍以良好結晶化之 HA為主相，
伴隨少量的 rhenanite相的形成，而最強繞射峰(211)在 Ca/P=1.5時之強度則為本系列試樣中最低者，在
Ca/P=1.6時卻又突然增強，然後再隨 Ca/P之增加而遞減。此外，在 Ca/P值大於 1.6之後，{0 0 2n}的
異向性結晶現象則不存在，表示當 Ca/P比接近 s-HA之 1.67時，其結構亦傾向形成 s-HA，因而 HA的
相強度突然增加，但隨著 CaCO3添加量的增大，HA結構再次因缺陷的形成而使得強度遞減，但仍無
CaO或者β-TCP相出現。 
圖3-24所示者為以不同鈣磷比所得之粉末，經800oC燒結4 h後之燒結體密度及線性收縮率，其結果
指出燒結密度由Ca/P=1.0時的1.59 g/cm3，隨著Ca/P比值之增加而增加，而在Ca/P值由1.5到1.8之間達到
2.8 g/cm3左右。與HA的理論密度3.155 g/cm3相較，燒結體的密度值僅有理論密度的90%左右，而
a-NaCaPO4相的理論密度為2.890 g/cm3，顯示a-NaCaPO4相的形成有助於HA在800oC時的燒結而使密度
上升。線性收縮率與燒結密度有相同的趨勢。此外，收縮率由5.15%增加到約21%時即趨於穩定，並在
Ca/P=1.5時可趨穩定。 
由不同Ca/P比的合成粉末於800oC燒結4 h後的SEM微結構影像如圖3-27所示。圖3-27(a)所示者為未
添加CaCO3時(即Ca/P=1.0)之顯微結構，可見其仍未呈現緻密化之現象，且圖中的顆粒仍以析出物的型
態存在。圖3-27(b)所示者為Ca/P=1.3時之微結構，發現雖已有緻密化行為發生，但仍有針狀析出物的
殘存，表示對在Ca/P=1.3所得之粉末，於800℃仍不能完全燒結。圖3-27(c)所示者為Ca/P=1.5之粉體在
800oC燒結4 h後之微結構，所有的HA粉體已成長成大顆粒，亦即CaCO3的添加的確有助於燒結的進行。
圖3-27(d)所示者為在Ca/P=1.67所合成之粉體經800oC燒結4 h後之微結構，其晶粒較之圖3-27(c)者更能
穩定成長，此結果與在圖3-26中所得之密度的結果相符。 
圖3-28所示者為由圖4-24與25中之HA的(002)計算所得之晶粒大小，在Ca/P=1.0時，HA的晶粒大小

圖3-29(a)為Ca/P=1.5時之燒結體的明視野相，可發現數個明顯的晶粒及晶界，經以EDS鑑定並以Ca/P

3-5-2 燒結條件與碳酸鈣添加之關聯 
圖3-30所示者是以Ca/P=1.5水解合成所得的HA於不同溫度燒結4 h後之燒結體的XRD圖。為確認在
燒結體內部是否會因受熱較不均而有CaO之形成，乃將燒結體表面磨掉後再進行XRD測試。此外，由
上節之結果知道HA粉末在600oC即有良好的結晶度，因此燒結亦從600oC開始，而後每上升100oC進行
一次燒結實驗直至1400oC為止。從圖3-30的結果可以發現在600oC燒結4 h後，仍僅呈現HA相，但至700oC
時則有CaO相，於800oC時，除HA及CaO外，尚有NaCaPO4相，但NaCaPO4相到1200oC則完全消失，而
CaO則一直到1300oC才消失，在此期間並無β-TCP相生成。在1200oC時雖仍以HA為主相，但α-TCP與
TTCP相已形成，而HA的繞射峰亦隨溫度的上升而逐漸往低角度偏移，強度則隨著α-TCP相的增加而遞
減。 
以Ca/P=1.0及1.5所合成之HA粉末及Merck商業用HA粉末分別於不同溫度燒結4 h之燒結體的密度
結果如圖3-31所示。圖中以三角形標示者代表Ca/P=1.5粉末之燒結結果，在600oC即有足夠強度可供置
於水中進行密度量測，且其在700~1100oC間之密度維持在2.8 g/cm3左右，而在1100oC達到最高值2.85 
g/cm3後下降，此最高值雖僅為HA之理論密度3.155 g/cm3的90.33%，但卻接近a-NaCaPO4的理論密度
2.890 g/cm3。方形與圓形之標示分別代表Ca/P=1.0所得之HA粉末與商業粉末之燒結體密度，其結果顯
示在800oC時兩者雖皆有可量測之數值，但卻較Ca/P=1.5所得之粉末在600oC燒結的密度為低，尤以商
業粉末之燒結體密度更低。此乃因Ca/P=1.0所得之粉末在800oC燒結時，其燒結體中存有少量之NaCaPO4
相而使燒結溫度略為降低，並使其密度較商業粉末所得者為高。而後兩者之密度皆隨燒結溫度之升高
而逐漸提升，直到1200oC後方才高於以Ca/P=1.5所得之粉末的燒結體密度，此時商業粉末的燒結密度
也大於以Ca/P=1.0所得之粉末的燒結密度。 
此外，由圖3-31的結果亦發現三種粉末燒結體之最大密度出現的溫度分別為商業粉末(1200oC，理
論密度之96.20%) > Ca/P=1.0之粉末 (1300oC，理論密度之92.76%) > Ca/P=1.5之粉末 (1100oC，理論密
度之90.33%)。而由密度達到最大值之燒結溫度可知CaCO3的添加能降低燒結溫度達200oC之多，但過
量之CaCO3的添加亦形成過多之NaCaPO4相，而使燒結體密度之最大值在1100oC，而此溫度亦為
NaCaPO4相在高溫消失的溫度，因此在燒結溫度高於1100oC之後，密度就急速下降。 
燒結體之表面硬度與燒結溫度之關係如圖3-32所示，其測試方法是將表面磨去後再拋光，量測十

3-6 生物適應性分析 
    經過先前的實驗，對所合成之HA粉末的基本性質予以探討，然而由於生醫材料與生物體之間的反
應亦相當重要，因此將分別以粉末及燒結體的形式進行生物適應性分析。 
3-6-1 燒結體之長期浸漬 
就本研究而言，係選定以Ca/P=1.5合成之HA粉末於800oC燒結4 h後的燒結體進行生物性質分析研
究。首先在生理食鹽水中經長期浸漬實驗，由於HA燒結體被用於生物體內時，會長期處於體液環境之
中，雖然HA是結構非常穩定的材料，但具有生物降解性(bio-degradable)，再加上本實驗用之HA的原始
Ca/P比除低於HA的理論值1.67之外，更含有許多容易降解的ACP與d-HA，且加上燒結後所產生的
NaCaPO4相之結構也不穩定，若在體液環境中釋放過高離子濃度而對人體造成傷害，將違反作為生醫

第四章 結論 
1. 二水磷酸氫鈣(DCPD)，在75℃之2.5 M NaOH(aq)中水解反應1 h後，可獲得奈米級的氫氧基磷灰石(HA)
粉末。 
2. NaOH的濃度升高，雖可加速DCPD轉變成HA的反應，但過高的濃度亦會導致晶粒破碎並轉變為八鈣
磷酸鹽(OCP)，因此NaOH的濃度以2.5 M為最佳。 
3. 在水解過程中Ca/P值的改變，會對所得之HA粉末的結構造成影響，而形成ACP與缺鈣型HA(d-HA)。
藉由添加碳酸鈣(CaCO3)以改變Ca/P值，而使所形成之HA中有CO32-取代部分結構位置，而形成更高
比例的碳酸化HA (CHA)。 
4. 陽離子型介面分散劑十六烷基三甲基溴化銨(CTAB)的添加，會在濃度 
   1 × 10-3 M時形成微胞，抑止HA的晶粒成長，並將其晶粒小降至20 nm寬與50 nm長，及造成a軸向晶
格常數的縮減。 
5. 以 Ca/P比在(1.0~1.8)之 HA的粉末經壓胚後，並在 800oC燒結 4 h後，{0 0 2n}之異向性結晶現象，
隨CaCO3之添加量的增加(即Ca/P>1.5)而逐漸消失；而 rhenanite相之量則隨Ca/P比值之增加而上升，
而燒結密度在 Ca/P>1.5者趨於 2.8 g/cm3之穩定值。 
6. Ca/P=1.5的 HA粉末在 800oC燒結 4 h可獲得最高之密度 2.77 g/cm3，但在 800oC燒結更長之時間則
其密度反而下降，顯示在 800oC之最佳燒結時間為 4 h。 
7. 以Ca/P=1.5之HA的粉末於800oC燒結4 h的燒結體，浸漬於37°C的生理食鹽水中經2~42 d後，其表面
在浸漬14 d後發現有新生成之焦磷酸鈣(CPP)結晶，且其量隨浸漬時間之增加而增加，其形成係來自
於HA燒結體表面在浸漬時所釋出之鈣磷離子的重新結合而析出。 
8. 浸漬不同時間後，生理食鹽水中之Ca2+的濃度從零增至2 d後的181 ppm，而重量損失為1.34%/cm2；
而後隨著浸漬時間之增長，Ca2+濃度迅速下降，表示溶液中的離子重新在燒結體表面形成CPP，但
至28 d後便逐漸穩定，係因達成CPP，ACP析出及回溶的平衡。此外，因CPP的形成而導致燒結體表
面硬度之下降。 
9. 取以 Ca/P=1.0與 1.5水解合成的 HA粉末進行萃取液體外細胞存活率研究，並與Merck商業粉末進
行比對，其存活率大小為 Ca/P=1.5> Ca/P=1.0>Merck，顯示此結果係因在水解合成過程中少了於大
部份合成法中可能出現的多餘離子，而使對於細胞的生物相容性略為提高。而浸漬時間越久，三組
HA粉末之萃取液的存活率差異就越小，亦即對細胞存活的影響也越小，表示本研究所合成之粉末較
之商用粉末能在更短時間內促進細胞的存活率。

Two mol% Y2O3-PSZ precursor powders for dental applications were synthesized using ZrOCl2·8H2O and
Y(NO3)3·6H2O by a co-precipitation process at pH 7 and 348 K for 2 h. The thermal properties and phase
transformation of 2Y-PSZ nanocrystallite powder have been investigated using a thermogravimetric and
difference scanning calorimeter (TG/DSC), Fourier transform infrared spectroscopy, X-ray diffraction,
transmission electron microscopy (TEM), selected area electron diffraction and dilatometric analysis. Two
weaker broad exothermic peaks appear at around 618 and 718 K were explored in DSC results. The TG
analysis shows that minor weight loss occurs from 298 to 348 K, followed by three major weight losses at
610, 630 and 773 K. Calcinated at 773 and 1273 K, the crystallized phases are composed of the major
phase of tetragonal ZrO2 and minor phase of monoclinic ZrO2. TEM reveals that the tetragonal ZrO2 with
an average size of less than 20.0 nm is mainly aggregated into the secondary aggregates with a size of
small than 30.0 nm. The sintering curve of the compact pellet has a signiﬁcant shrinkage with a linear
rate of 18.5% at about 1341 K. Maximum densiﬁcation rate happened at 1473 K, demonstrating the good
low temperature sinterability for dental applications.

1. Introduction
Recently, the development of advanced dental ceramics has led to
the application of partially stabilized zirconia (PSZ) in restorative
dentistry, which can be produced in combination with computer-
assisted design/computer-aided manufacture (CAD/CAM) techniques
[1,2]. The use of zirconia-based ceramics for dental restoration has risen
in popularity due to their superior fracture strength and toughness
compared with other dental ceramic systems [3].
Three types of ZrO2 ceramics are available for dental application,
namely 3 mol% Y2O3-tetragonal ZrO2 polycrystals (3Y-TZP), ZrO2-
toughened alumina (ZTA) and magnesium partially stability ZrO2
(Mg-PSZ) [1]. The properties of ZTA are due to the stress-induced
martensitic transformation t-ZrO2 is combinedwith the alumina(Al2O3)
matrix [4,5]. Thesematerials are of interest due to their potential for use
as bioceramics [6]. The addition of 33 vol% of 12 mol% ceria-stability
ZrO2 (12Ce-TZP) to In-Ceran® Alumina.® from the In-Ceran® Zirconia®
for developedother commercially dental product have been reported by
Guazzato et al. [7] The slip-castingandmachiningof In-Ceran®Zirconia®
materials reveals a very similar microstructure with large Al2O3 grains
(6 μm long and 2 μm wide) together with the less than 1 μm of ZrO2
grains. The crack paths were consistently transgranular for ZrO2 and
intragranular for Al2O3 [1]. Recently, itwas shown that ZTA promote the
uniform dispersion of ﬁne ZrO2 grains in an Al2O3 matrix, leading to
excellent mechanical properties that are suitable for medical applica-
tions [7–9]. However, the use ofMg-PSZ for biomedical applications, has
not been successful, due to the porosity, associated with a grain size of
30–60 μm, which can cause wear [10].

3Y-TZP ceramics above a critical grain size of about 1 μm become less
stable and more susceptible to the transformation from t→m. However,
when the grain size is less than 1 μm, then a lower transformation rate

ceramics is less than about 0.2 μm, then the phase transformation cannot

Zr(OH)4·xH2O sheets which came together in an ordered fashion
leading to the ﬂuorite structure, while fast precipitation resulted in a
random polymer. Upon heat treatment, the ordered polymer results in
the tetragonal or cubic form, while the random polymer results in the
monoclinic form of zirconia. A detailed investigation of the ZrO2
particle formationmechanism fromZrO(NO3)2 solution as a functionof
pH under hydrothermal conditions is presented by Denkewicz Jr. et al.
[32]. They indicate that the existence of the controlling regimes for
crystallization of zirconia dissolution/precipitation at low pH (pHb2),
a solubility controlled at intermediate pH values (pH from2 to 11), and
a gel chemistry controlled regime at highpH (pH from11 to 14). On the
other hand, the formation mechanisms of m-ZrO2 or t-ZrO2 are
dissolution/precipitation or in situ crystallization, respectively, from
amorphous Zr(OH)xOy gels.
As mentioned above, it has been generally observed that the
precipitation conditions, i.e. pH, time, and temperature, affect the
phase of ZrO2 crystal formation [18,19,21,32]. However, in the present
study, according to the TG/DSC results, it can be suggested that the
precipitates of [Zr(OH)4(OH2)2](H2O)2 rather than that of Zr(OH)4.
From the results of Figs. 1 and 2, it is found that the appropriate
calcination temperature for the greenprecursor powders is above773 K,
at which an exothermic reaction is observed. Fig. 2 indicates that the
radical species of the NO3- , OH- and C=O bands are still residual in the
precursor powders, and for calcination temperatures below 773 K the
content of residual radical species gradually decreases as the temper-
ature rises. When the calcination temperature reaches 773 K, the
residual radical species are largely absent. This result is similar to that in
Lee et al. [27]. Therefore, the exothermic peak at 761 K is attributed to
the transformation of Zr(OH)4 andY(OH)3 into ZrO2 andY2O3, forminga
2Y-PSZ solid solution.
4.2. The mechanism of ZrO2 formation
Since the metastable t-ZrO2 is known to exist at room temperature
only in crystallites of less than ~30 nmdiameter, the stability in this case
is causedby the lower surface energy of the t-ZrO2 phase comparedwith
m-ZrO2 [30]. The surface energy of the m-ZrO2 (γm=1.13 J·m-2) is
higher than that of the t-phase (γm=0.77 J·m-2) [33,34].
Wu et al. [16] have pointed out that fraction of the t-ZrO2 decreases
from 52 to 33% when calcination temperature increases from 773 to
973 K for a hold time of 1 h. However, in the present study, the variation
trend of the t-ZrO2 content in 2Y-PSZ powders is not in agreementwith
their results.
4.3. Sintering curve of 2Y-PSZ nanocrystallite powders
The fast densiﬁcation of 2Y-PSZ powders at low temperaturemay be
due to two reasons: one is the effect of particle size [35], and the other is
that the lattice and grain boundary transport are frozen out [36].
The importance of particle size in determining sintering rate can be seen
in the inverse dependencies of the sintering equation on particle radius
r: [35]

where x is the radius of the neck of particles together, the constant
contains the surface tension and the relevant diffusion coefﬁcient, n is
between 2 and 6, and p is between 2 and 4.
In the present study, the crystallite size of the 2Y-PSZ powders was
less 20 nm, and although 2Y-PSZ nanopowders exist as agglomerates,
the size of agglomeration was still less than 30 nm. Hence, the effect of
particle size in densiﬁcation of the sintered 2Y-PSZ compact is a
dominant factor at lower temperatures.
Grain boundary and lattice diffusion have a steeper temperature
dependence, and tend to dominate at higher temperatures, thereby
promoting densiﬁcation. In contrast surface diffusion and evaporation-
condensation have weaker temperature dependencies, and often
dominate at lower temperatures, when lattice and grain boundary
transport is inhibited [35]. The principle behind fast-ﬁring is that, by
rapid heating to bypass the coarsening regime, densiﬁcation can be
promoted [36].
A major problem in the processing of nano-size powders is the
existence of agglomerates, which occurs spontaneously due to van der
Waals force. While small interparticle pores are easily closed during
sintering, large interagglomerate pores need a high sintering temper-
ature or long sintering time to be eliminated.
5. Conclusions
The thermal behavior phase and sintering curves of 2Y-PSZ
nanocrystallite powders prepared by a co-precipitation process for
dental applications using ZrOCl2·8H2O and Y(NO3)3·6H2O as staring
materials have been investigated. DSC curves indicate that the ﬁrst
weaker broad exothermic peak at around 618 K is due to the
decomposition of Zr(OH)4(OH2)2 into the Zr(OH)4 and H2O. The second
weaker broad exothermic peak at around 761 K is caused by the
transformation of Zr(OH)4 into the crystalline ZrO2. TG curves reveal
that the ﬁrst weight loss of about 6% is caused by the evaporation of
absorbed water on the surface of the precursor powders. The second
large weight loss of about 21% between 348 and 610 K is mostly due to
the loss of absorbedwater in the interior of the precursor powders. The
third largeweight loss of about 36% between 610 and 630 K is attributed
to dehydration and zirconium hydroxide Zr(OH)4 formation. The last
large weight loss of about 51% between 630 and 773 K is due to the
Zr(OH)4 transition to ZrO2 and H2O. When the 2Y-PSZ precursor
powders are heated from 773 to 1273 K for 2 h, the crystallized samples
are composed of themajor phase of t-ZrO2 and aminor phase ofm-ZrO2.
The sintering curve reveals that the densiﬁcation course was complete
at approximately 1673 K, with the total shrinkage rate of about 50.0%.

As mentioned above, structural and textural studies on ZrO2-Y2O3
powders for samples prepared by heating in air or under vacuum have

isothermal transformation from t→m. Wu et al. [16] pointed out that

enhance the stability of the t-phase. Moreover, Vasylkiv and Sakka [17]

zirconium chloride and urea sol followed by a washing-drying

obtained. Li and Guo [18,19] heated ethanol-aqueous salt solutions to
synthesize 3Y-TZP powders and investigated the related compacting
and sintering behavior. However, an extensive search of the literature
found that the 2Y-TZP nanocrystallite ZrO2 powders prepared at pH 7
using a co-precipitation process have not yet been studied in detail.
In the present study, high purity zirconium oxide chloride
(ZrOCl2·8H2O) and yttrium nitrate (Y(NO3)3·6H2O) were used as
starting materials for the synthesis of 2Y-PSZ nanocrystallite powders
via a co-precipitation process. Thermogravimetric and difference
scanning calorimeter (TG/DSC) analysis, Fourier transform infrared
(FT-IR) spectroscopy, X-ray diffraction (XRD), transmission electron
microscopy (TEM), selected area electron diffraction (SAED) and a
dilatometric analyzer (DA) were utilized to characterize the thermal
behavior and sinterability of 2Y-PSZ precursor powders prepared at
pH 7.
The purposes of this investigation were: (i) to study the thermal
behavior of the 2Y-PSZ precursor powders prepared at pH7, (ii) to study
the phase of the 2Y-PSZ precursor powders post-calcined at various
temperatures for 2 h, (iii) to observed the microstructure of the 2Y-PSZ
precursor powders post-calcination, and (iv) to evaluate the sinterabil-
ity of the 2Y-PSZ precursor powders.
2. Experimental procedure
2.1. Sample preparation
Zirconium (IV) oxide chloride (ZrOCl2·8H2O, purity≧99.5%, supplied
by Riedel-de Haën, Germany) and yttrium nitrate (Y(NO3)3·6H2O,
supplied by Acros, New Jersey, USA)were used as the startingmaterials.
ZrOCl2·8H2O and Y(NO3)3·6H2O were dissolved in a deionized water-
ethanol solution in a volume ratio of 1:5. The molar ratio of Y2O3 to
(Y2O3+ZrO2) was ﬁxed at 2.0 mol% and labeled as 2Y-PSZ. 1 wt%
polyethylenglycol (PEG, extra pure reagent, molecular weight 6000 Da,
supplied by Nippon Shiyaku Kogyo K. K., Japan) was added into the
mixture as a dispersant. The solutionwas stirred and heated to 348 K in
a thermostatic bath to obtain white precipitates and the ammonium
hydroxide (NH4OH, purity 28%, supplied by Nihon Shiyaku Reagent,
Japan) was added into the solution as a buffer to control the pH value
at 7. After precipitation, the precipitates were repeatedly rinsed with a
large amount of deionized water and tested with AgNO3 solution to
make sure noAgCl precipitation had occurred, and then the precipitates
were freeze-dried at 218 K in a vacuum. Calcining of the samples was
carriedout in air usingamullite crucible. Powderswere cooled in air and
each sample was characterized by XRD.
2.2. Sample characterization
TG/DSC analysis (Universal V 4.5 A TA Instruments, USA) was
conducted on a 50 mg precursor powder sample at a heating rate of
10 K·min-1 in air with Al2O3 powder as a reference material.
The chemical behavior and molecular bonding structure of the 2Y-
PSZ precursor powders before and after calcination at various
temperatures for 1 h were evaluated with an FT-IR spectrometer
(Perkin-Elmer Spectrum One FT-IR Spectrometer, Boston, USA), using
absorption spectra obtained with a spectral resolution of 4 cm-1. Each
sample was mixed with KBr (sample to KBr=1:10 in mass ratio) and
pressed into a 200 mg pellet 12 mm in diameter to obtain infrared
absorption spectra at a frequency range of 400–4000 cm-1. A composite
spectrum for each sample represented an average of 64 scans,
normalized to the spectrum of the blank KBr pellet.
The sinterability of 2Y-PSZ precursor powders calcined at 773 K for
2 h was studied with a dilatometric analyzer (DA, Setsys Evolution,
Setaram) with a heating rate of 5 K·min-1 from room temperature to
1673 K. Samples for dilatometric tests were cut from the pressed disk
and shaped into a bar of 20×2×2 mm3. Sintering was conducted in an
alumina tube furnace containing an alumina push rod. The push rod
applied a minimal pressure of 414 Pa to the sample during sintering.
TEM (Hitachi FE-2000, Tokyo, Japan) was used to determine the
morphology and crystal structureof the calcinedpowders at 200 kV. The
crystal structure of the 2Y-PSZ powder was determined by SAED. The
TEM sample was prepared by dispersing the 2Y-PSZ powders in an
ultrasonic bath and then collecting them in a copper grid.

3.1. Thermal behavior of 2Y-PSZ precursor powders prepared at pH 7
The TG/DSC analysis curves for the 2Y-PSZ precursor powders at a

transformation of Zr(OH)4 into the crystalline ZrO2 lattice [21].

3.4. Sintering curve of the 2Y-PSZ nanocrystallite powders
Fig. 6 shows the sintering curve of the compact pellet uniaxially

pellet has very good sinterability, where a signiﬁcant shrinkage rate is
observed at about 1341 K, with a linear shrinkage of 18.5%. The
densiﬁcation course is complete at approximately 1673 K, at which the
total shrinkage of 50% is recorded. The shrinkage remains at 18.5% for
the sintering curve between 1341 and 1373 K, due to the relatively large
amount of tetragonal transition to monoclinic ZrO2 that occurred. The
maximumdensiﬁcation rate is found at 1473 K, demonstrating the good
low temperature sinterability of the 2Y-PSZ nanocrystallite powders.
