一、前言 
    近年來光學製品產業的蓬勃發展，尤其是各種精密光學元件如非球面透鏡、CD 和 DVD 
光學讀取鏡頭、光纖耦合器上聚焦鏡片的使用等，成為我國發展光學製品產業之關鍵零組件，
如圖 1.1 所示[1]。而此類光學元件配合光電產品之發展趨勢，講求精密化與輕薄短小。隨著非
球面光學應用的逐漸成熟化，用以解決球面像差與縮小產品尺寸的功能，模造玻璃光學元件
的需求性日益增強。模造製程技術之關鍵技術分別為光學設計、預形體製作、模仁超精密加
工、模仁表面硬膜披覆、模造製程與精密量測等。並且在 1982 年，日本就已有小規模的模
造玻璃量產能力，然而玻璃模造技術產品面臨塑膠射出鏡片之競爭相當明顯。時至約 2000 
年，數位相機產品大行其道，帶動高階產品因消費需求而誕生，諸如 300 萬畫像素以上高階
數位相機等一再突顯塑膠元件耐候性、耐熱性、耐濕性不良之缺點，必須使用穩定性高的玻
璃元件。根據工研院光學產業調查資料顯示，我國非球面模具成形鏡片發展自 1992 年工研院
開始非球面與精密光學零組件技術開發計劃，從事模具成形玻璃鏡片量產技術開發至今，我
國的模具成形鏡片生產大多轉往大陸做相機鏡頭的組裝。目前我國既有的照相機產業以非球
面模具成形鏡片提供龐大之市場需求，而市場上的需求快速成長，我國可發展之產業包括非
球面光學讀寫頭、傳真機、光學掃瞄器及條碼閱讀器使用之鏡片。 
 
圖 1.1 國內光學玻璃相關產業之零組件[1] 
 
    玻璃模造技術是最有機會製造出高解析度、穩定度佳，且成本較低廉的非球面玻璃透鏡，
同時市場上對於光學產品的解析度及穩定性的要求更趨於嚴苛，亦帶動了光學鏡片材料的開
發和製程技術的提升，而非球面光學玻璃元件的成型技術可概分為兩大類，分別為模造玻璃
及精密研削。其中，模造玻璃技術被視為最有機會製造出高解析度、穩定性佳、具量產性且
低成本非球面玻璃透鏡的前瞻技術。在模造玻璃的過程中，將玻璃預形體置入精密加工的模
具內壓製成型。然而成型模具於高溫、高壓及高應力的工作環境下，模具表面易生成氧化物，
並與玻璃材料產生沾粘現象，在此狀況下反覆進行製程，不但易導致模具表面因黏著磨損而
降低壽命，更直接影響玻璃元件的品質。 
    要使玻璃與模具之間抗沾黏磨損的鍍膜種類選擇很多，其中包括類鑽碳薄膜( DLC )、貴
重金屬薄膜( Pt-Ir 合金)、一般常見的氮化物薄膜( Nitride )和氧化物薄膜( Oxide )等。而本研
究選用物理氣相沉積(Physical Vapor Deposition, PVD)技術中的陰極電弧電弧沉積(Cathodic 
Arc Deposition, CAD)系統製備氮化物薄膜對材料表面進行改質後，再針對氮化物薄膜進行金
屬電漿源離子植入法( Metal Plasma Ion Implantation, MPII )，進行更深入的表面改質，由於金
屬電漿源離子植入使用較高的加速電壓(40kV)將靶材原子植入基材內，能使薄膜結構更為緻
密，並且改變其差排結構，降低薄膜內應力。此外，金屬電漿源離子植入之植入深度將取決
於基材之微結構及硬度，若是基材結構較為緻密、硬度較高，被植入之原子需要更高的能量
才能往基材內移動。因此，相同的加速電壓與各種植入元素會有不同的植入深度。故本研究
藉由金屬電漿源離子植入更進一步增加氮化物薄膜之結構，達到提昇模具表面抗氧化、沾粘
及磨損的能力，以延長披覆薄膜之工件使用壽命。 
    本研究利用本實驗室自行設計之高溫接觸角量測儀，以觀察高溫時光學玻璃球與模仁鍍
膜間之表面接觸角變化，隨後使用熱場發射掃描式電子顯微鏡觀察光學玻璃球與模仁鍍膜間
之反應狀況，以做為設計模仁鍍膜之參考。同時將所沉積之薄膜進行高溫氧化行為、機械性
質及磨潤性質之分析。

三、結果與討論 
3-1 薄膜微結構分析 
3-1-1 薄膜成分分析 
    本研究之成分分析使用歐傑電子能譜儀(AES)來對 CrAlSiN/CrN 鍍膜以及植入後之樣品
做成分分析，表 3.1 為各樣品之 AES 元素分析。一般之 EDS 為大範圍成分分析，其偵測深度
大於 1µm，由於本研究之離子植入深度只有數十奈米，因此 EDS 較不適用於植入後之成分分
析。而歐傑電子偵測範圍非常微小，偵測範圍可小於 1nm，可對樣品表面做定性及定量分析，
故本研究採用 AES 之成分分析。 
    成分分析後得知，CrAlSiN/CrN 之成分為鉻 21.9 at%、鋁 18.7 at%、矽 8.6 at%及氮 50.8 
at%；植入劑量為 Mo 1E17 其成分為鉻 22.8 at%、鋁 21.4 at%、矽 9.1 at%、氮 34.2 at%及鉬
12.5 at%；植入劑量為 V 1E17 其成分為鉻 22.2 at%、鋁 20.7 at%、矽 7.7 at%、氮 36.4 at%及
釩 13 at%；植入劑量為 C 1E17 其成分為鉻 20.7 at%、鋁 18.6 at%、矽 8.7 at%、氮 35.8 at%及
碳 16.2 at%；植入劑量為 C 2E17 其成分為鉻 19.2 at%、鋁 17.3 at%、矽 7.9 at%、氮 32.9 at%
及碳 22.7 at%；植入劑量為 Mo 1E17+C 1E17 其成分為鉻 16.3 at%、鋁 13.2 at%、矽 4.3 at%、
氮 34.4 at%、鉬 12.7 at%及碳 19.1 at%；植入劑量為 V 1E17+C 1E17 其成分為鉻 16.7 at%、鋁
15.4 at%、矽 3.1 at%、氮 33.6 at%、釩 13.3 at%及碳 17.9 at%；植入劑量為 Mo 1E17+C 2E17
其成分為鉻 15.4 at%、鋁 13.3 at%、矽 3.4 at%、氮 28.2 at%、鉬 13.6 at%及碳 26.1 at%；植入
劑量為 V 1E17+C 1E17 其成分為鉻 16.4 at%、鋁 15.5 at%、矽 4.7 at%、氮 31.5 at%、釩 12.1 at%
及碳 19.8 at%。 
    由 AES 成分分析發現，植入劑量為 Mo 1E17、V 1E17 及 C 1E17 氮含量有減少的趨勢，
當植入劑量超過 2 E17 時(如 C 2E17、Mo 1E17+C 1E17 及 V 1E17+C 1E17 等)，鉻、鋁及矽含
量有開始逐漸減少的趨勢。由這些現象發現，最先被取代的原子為氮，其他原子被取代的情
形不是非常嚴重；當植入劑量增加時，尤其是 Mo 1E17+C 1E17、V 1E17+C 1E17、Mo 1E17+C 
2E17 及 V 1E17+C 2E17 這四組，其他元素才有原子被取代的現象發生。 
    植入後氮含量下降較多的原因是由於氮的活性大，CrAlSiN/CrN 薄膜內氮原子較易與其
他金屬原子(鉻、鋁)形成離子鍵，而此種離子鍵又比鉻與鋁之間形成的金屬鍵或是氮與矽形
成的共價鍵來的強。話雖如此，但離子植入的能量非常大，導致部分的鍵結被打斷，而鍵結
被打斷後之原子就以原本的型態存在於薄膜中，而 CrAlSiN/CrN 薄膜多以離子鍵為主要鍵
結，當此鍵結遭到破壞後，鉻與鋁就可能以非晶結構存在於薄膜中，氮就變成氣體分子被消
耗掉，離開原本的晶格位置，而植入原子就會佔據氮元子之晶格位置，導致薄膜內氮含量下
降。這些現象主要是發生於表面近 50nm 的地方，原子被取代的現象會隨著植入深度越深而
遞減，越深層的鉬、釩及碳成分就越少，因此植入深度越深層，其原子被取代的現象即會減
少。

Glass  molding  technology  is  progressively  attentive  for  the

In  this  study,  ion  implanted  multicomponent  thermal  stable

performance  of  the  deposited  multilayered  coatings  will  be 
investigated in details. It’s a one year program that is being conducted

Nanocomposites usually consist of either nanocrystallites in an
amorphous matrix or a mixture of nanocrystalline phases. Such
materials have been produced with superior mechanical properties.
Previous studies on ternary or quarterary nanocomposites containing
amorphous carbon (a-C) such as TiO2/a-C and silver doped a-C

chemical vapor deposition (PECVD) had been conducted to show good

determined by the plasma process parameters and the kinetics of the
deposition process.
Even though ZrN and a-C coatings have been conﬁrmed that it can

The objective of this work was to study the effect of carbon contents on

vitro study [18]. The adherence of A. actinomycetemcomitans to the

biocompatibility and cell proliferation activity of the ZrCN/a-C coatings,
the attachment and growth behavior of normal human gingival

investigated.
2. Experimental details
ZrN and ZrCN/a-C coatings were deposited on pure Ti plate samples
(20 mm×20mm×1 mm, Surface roughness Ra=0.1 μm, bio-grade 2,
Uniti Titanium, Moon Township, PA, USA) by using a cathodic arc
evaporation system. Circular Zirconium targets (100 mm in diameter)
were arranged on the chamber wall to deposit the ZrN and
nanocomposite ZrCN/a-C coatings. A dc arc current of 70 A was applied
between the anode and the cathode using a welding power supply
(Miller XMT 304 CC/CV). The samples were placed on a rotational
substrate holder for the deposition of the ZrN and ZrCN/a-C coatings.
Prior to deposition, a base pressure prior to deposition was less than
1×10−3 Pa. Ar and reactive gas (N2 andC2H2)were introduced through
a conducting duct around the target to enhance the reaction of the
plasma and reduce the macroparticles produced during the deposition
process. The temperature of the sample during the deposition was
measured by a thermocouple located near the sample to be within the
range of 100±20 °C. Substrate bias voltage of−80 Vwas used. The ZrN
coating was deposited at a N2 pressure of 1.5 Pa. For the deposition of
ZrCN/a-Cﬁlms (ZrCN-10, ZrCN-20 andZrCN-50), a ZrNwasdeposited as
an interlayer (~150 nm). At a total gas pressure of 1.5 Pa, a mixture of
reactive N2 and C2H2 with different C2H2 ﬂow rate from 10 sccm to
50 sccmwas introduced into the chamber to form the ZrCN/a-C coating
with different carbon contents. The coated ZrCN-10, ZrCN-20 and ZrCN-
50 sampleswere depositedwith a C2H2 ﬂow rate of 10, 20 and 50 sccm,
respectively. The total thickness of the coatings was 0.5–0.6 μm, which
was controlled by a deposition time of 8 min.
The characteristics of composition and chemical binding of the
deposited ﬁlms were identiﬁed by using an X-ray photoelectron
spectroscope (PHI1600 XPS) with non-monochromatized Mg Kα
radiation. It was performed with 3 kV Ar ions to sputter the surface
oxide layer for 1 min and reveal the chemical composition of the
deposited coatings. The survey spectra in the range of 0–1000 eVwere
recorded for each sample followed by high-resolution spectra over
different elemental peaks, fromwhich the compositionwas calculated.
The spectral ranges at 182±12 eV, 400±10 eV, and 287±12 eV,
corresponded to the binding energies of Zr3d, N1s and C1s,
respectively. Energy calibration was made by reference to the Au
4f7/2 peak from a clean gold surface at 83.8 eV. Curve ﬁtting was
performed after a Shirley background subtraction by a Gaussian ﬁtting
[19]. The surface morphology of the deposited coatings was examined
in a Joel JSM-7000F high resolution ﬁeld emission scanning electron
microscope (FESEM). It was operated at an accelerating voltage of
15 kV, and a working distance of 10 mm. Glancing angle X-ray
diffractometer (PANalytical X'pert Pro) with a high resolution ψ
goniometer and Cu radiation was employed for phase identiﬁcation.
The diffractometer was operated at 40 kV and 30 mA with a glancing
angle of 1°–2°.
The static contact angle was measured by using an instrument for
measuring contact angles (FTA-125, First Ten Angstroms, Portsmouth,
VA, USA). The obtained images were analyzed to calculate the contact
angle of the deionized water of each sample at room temperature. Each
reported contact angle is the mean of at least three independent
measurements.
The retention of bacteria on the coated sampleswas determined by a
ﬂuorescence staining method employing Syto9 (Molecular Probes,
Eugene, OR, USA) as described previously [10]. Brieﬂy, 500 μl of A.
actinomycetemcomitans suspension (2×107 colony forming units
(cfu)/ml) was added onto the sample surface. After incubation for 6 h
at 37 °C under a relative humidity of 96% with avoiding light exposure,
the sample surface were rinsed three times with PBS, then the retained
bacteria was ﬁxed with 4% paraformaldehyde (Sigma-Aldrich, St. Louis,
MO, USA) and stained with 10 μM of Syto9 for 30 min at room
temperature. The adhered bacteria on the coated samples were
measured using a ﬂuorescence detected at 488 nm by an ELISA
(enzyme-linked immunosorbent assay) reader (Synergy HT, BioTek
Instrument, Winooski, VT, USA). The results were determined from
three independent experiments performed in duplicate, and quantiﬁed
in relative ﬂuorescence intensity.
The retentive bacteria on the surfaces of coated samples were also
examined using scanning electronmicroscopy (SEM). Each sample was
immersed in the aqueous solution of 3 ml of the cultured A.
actinomycetemcomitans (5×108 cfu/ml) in LB broth, followed by
incubated for 3 h at 37 °C. The tested samples were rinsed three times
with Phosphate buffer solution (PBS) and immediately ﬁxed in 2.5%
glutaraldehyde for 2 h. Prior to SEM, the tested samples were rinsed
with PBS again, and were immersed in distilled deionized water for
10 min, and then were dehydrated in an ethanol series (50, 70, 90, 95,
and 100%, each for 10 min). The tested samples were ﬁxed and
subsequently dried by using critical point drying with CO2, which has
its critical point at 31.1 °C and 7.39 MPa, using a Samdri-PVT-3D
apparatus. The chamber is pre-cooled (10 °C) to allow it to be readily
ﬁlled with liquid CO2 from a gas cylinder. The chamber is then heated to
just above the critical temperature with subsequent critical pressure
being achieved. Immediately after critical point drying, samples were
coated with Pt and then observed in secondary electron (SE) detection
mode.

samples were also assessed by bacterial viability test [20]. A total of
200 ml LB agar (5 g LB broth and 1.6 g agar plus with 193 ml glass-

3.2. HGF cell proliferation
The optimal transgingival implant surface should improve the
sealing of the connective tissue by achieving a tight connection of the
ﬁbrous tissue to the implant surface. By modifying the surface texture
of the implant materials, the tissue-implant attachment can be
enhanced, resulting in a material that should be at least as good as
normal titanium [36]. It is necessary to examine the HGF cell viability
for the application of dental implants. In this study, an MTT assay was
used for evaluation. The optical density of the formazan produced by
HGF cells wasmeasured after 72 h (Fig. 4). The optical density value of
the uncoated (control sample) Ti was 0.263±0.007. Previous studies
showed that smooth Ti favors human oral ﬁbroblast attachment and
soft tissue growth [37,38]. The biocompatibility of Ti is attributed to
surface oxide spontaneously forming in air and/or other surface
treatments (such as thermal oxidation or anodic oxidation). It is
believed that the cellular behavior including proliferation, adhesion,
and spreading are greatly inﬂuenced by this oxide layer.

(中文) 光學玻璃與模具鍍膜之介面反應決定光學模造玻璃於高溫的模造成形特性與可靠
性，因此本技術在光學玻璃模具上進行離子植入奈米複合(nanocomposite)結構
之高溫穩定氮化鉻鋁矽鍍膜， 並探討此鍍膜與光學模造玻璃於高溫的模造成形
特性。本技術所設計之鍍膜乃利用本計劃之鍍膜製程採用鉻與鋁矽合金靶材，利
用單一及雙重金屬電漿源離子(Mo、V及C)植入氮化鉻鋁矽多層奈米複合鍍膜，藉
以在氮化鉻鋁矽多層鍍膜表面形成高溫穩定之離子植入非晶質(amorphous)混合
層，獲致具有高硬度、高附著力、抗高溫氧化、與光學玻璃低介面反應且緻密之
鍍膜。本技術同時設計建置高溫接觸角量測系統(RT~800 oC)，針對光學玻璃模
具合金鍍膜進行模具鍍膜與光學玻璃之抗沾黏特性探討，建立光學玻璃於高溫的
模造成形特性模式。藉由光學模造玻璃於高溫成形之抗沾黏特性與高溫機械性能
研究，獲得最佳化光學玻璃模具離子植入合金鍍膜及模具材料之組合設計。
(英文) Glass molding technology is progressively attentive for the production of high precision 
optical elements with aspheric surfaces. The working surfaces of these tools are exposed 
to the chemically active molten glass and also subjected to mechanical and thermal cyclic 
operations. In practice, the non-sticking performance of glass to the die surface (or 
coating) is the key property requirement for glass molding dies. 
In this study, ion implanted multicomponent thermal stable coatings are used to provide a 
chemically inert surface on the molding dies at high processing temperature. Single or 
duplex Mo, V and C ions are implanted into CrAlSiN/CrN films to form an amorphous 
layer with non-sticking performance. AlSi alloy and Cr targets are used for the deposition 
of multilayered films.
