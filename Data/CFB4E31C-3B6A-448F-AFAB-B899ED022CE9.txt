Abstract. In this study, mechanical behavior and failure pattern of composite concrete deck were 
investigated. A unique dimension of steel concrete deck was fabricated and tested in four-point 
bending. Two series of decking were also designated with bolts at a spacing of 15 cm and 30 cm, used 
to compare with those without bolt. Totally, 9 composite concrete decks were tested in a close-loop 
servo-hydraulic testing system (MTS) using displacement control. Load versus displacement curves 
as well as failure patterns of the specimens were studied to figure out the mechanical behavior of this 
type of composite structure. 
Introduction 
Recently, several catastrophic earthquakes have taken place around the countries along the 
Pacific-Rim region during the past years. The new high-rise building constructions in these areas have 
dominantly adopted steel reinforced concrete (SRC). In consequence, the use of composite concrete 
with steel decking becomes significant and necessary.  The fabrication of floor or slab is first to place 
steel decking across the girders on steel frame to assemble a floor form, then to place concrete on the 
top of steel decking with limited steel mesh in it.  The advantages include rapid construction and 
saving costs from labors and support materials. It also provides a highly efficient methodology for 
modern building construction.  From the literatures, although Eurocode 4 [1] provides limited design 
procedure for concrete composite slab with steel decking, rare researches have been reported dealing 
with the composite behavior under loading.  Porter (1988) [2] studied the ultimate state of the 
composite slab and provided some suggestions for design.  Evans and Wright (1988) [3] reported that 
the concrete strength contributes little to the load capacity of the slab.  Calixto (1998) [4] found the 
bolt screwed on the steel decking could strengthen the load capacity of the composite deck.  Sebastião 
(2004) [5] used styroform and bolt in the composite slab to reduce the weight and strengthen the load 
capacity.  However, the behavior of the composite slab has not explored clearly.  It seems valid and 
necessary to figure out the mechanical behavior of this composite structure, in particular, the stress 
transfer mechanism of the interface between concrete and steel decking.

4. The composite mechanism of concrete composite deck is valid only when the first peak load is not 
reached.  However, the post peak behavior of the composite slab still reveals a conservative 
failure type.
