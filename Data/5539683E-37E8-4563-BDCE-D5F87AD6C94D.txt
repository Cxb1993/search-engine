英文摘要 
This sub-project focuses on the approach that senses the corresponding voltage with ac 
signals. This method is suitable for enzyme analysis and sensing. Compared with the dc signal 
sensing approaches, its main advantage is that its output is less affected by the noise. In addition, 
by using this ac signal sensing method, it can simultaneously get several different analysis plots 
of measured solution, such as Nyquist Plot and Electrochemical Impedance Spectroscopy (EIS).  
The main goal of this sub-project is to design a low-power and low-noise amperometrical 
sensing circuit to detect the small sensed signal, and then to amplify it for further processing. 
Moreover, we will also develop a low-noise, high-accuracy, and high-efficiency power 
management circuit to convert time-varying battery voltage to a stable and low-noise supply 
voltage for powering the whole sensing circuit and circuits developed by other sub-projects.

 1
前言 
人類社會醫療體系的技術發展及健康保險制度日益健全，先進國家平均壽命也大幅增
加，加上人口出生率大幅降低，造成社會的許多衝擊，而高齡化的社會儼然成形。但是，
人體免疫能力會因老化而降低，且基因突變的機率大增，帶來醫療與照護支出增加以及生
活品質低落等社會問題。除此之外，由於M型化的社會現象日益嚴重，如何以低成本的方
式，維持最佳的身體狀況，創造最佳的工作環境和情緒，成為現代人重視的課題。在癌症
治療過程中或器官移植後，身體的免疫能力會遭受嚴重的破壞，若能有效、快速地監測人
體的免疫或抗氧化能力，對相關醫護處置亦能提供即時性的資訊，以利後續之治療或追蹤。
隨著積體電路與微機電製程技術的不斷精進，結合積體電路、微機電與生物醫學技術，設
計且製作具有微小化、快速、平行處理能力的生物及醫療用檢測元件，在微小面積上快速
進行大量生化感測與分析，已日漸成為智慧型感測系統發展的主流，將對於環境、對人體
的影響及高齡化社會的到來，有著無比的潛力。 
本子計畫主要是負責研發電流式感測晶片設計，為了因應未來市場需求及趨勢，本子
計畫的目標是微小化生醫感測平台與系統，以降低測量系統成本，並朝系統可攜式發展，
使得整個檢測系統成為人人可以輕鬆取得並攜帶，藉此達到疾病預防的目的，為滿足此目
標，首先開發製作適合感測之 CMOS Bio/MEMS 平台，減少量測端的體積；二是研發低功
率、低雜訊的電流式感測電路，將 CMOS Bio/MEMS 平台上感測到的微小信號，放大轉換
為可處理之類比訊號，降低功率消耗使電路可使用攜帶式電源，並能長時間攜帶量測；三
則是開發適合可攜式生醫感測晶片之低雜訊、高精準度、高效率的電源管理電路，將隨時
間變化之電池電壓轉換成穩定且低雜訊之電壓，供整個感測系統及其他子計畫之電路使用。

文獻探討 
有關電化學感測器之信號處理系統的研究，國外方面包括 Turner 提出一個 CMOS 積體
電路所設計的感測電路，主要應用來發展微小化的葡萄糖感測器，該感測器是利用固定葡
萄糖的氧化電極製作。Fidler 提出一個電壓控制電流源方式的電化學量測儀，做為氣體感測
器的信號處理電路，可以應用在環境污染的監控方面。Reay 則提出一個積體化 CMOS 恆電
位儀作為微小化的電化學分析儀器，與市售的電化學量測儀相較之下，他具有尺寸小、低
功率及價格便宜的優點。Kakerow 提出一個低功率的單晶片式的 CMOS 感測電路。Frey 設
計了一個積體化電化學量測電路，應用在 CMOS 生物感測晶片上。Breten 整合了資料轉換
電路用於電化學感測器的電流測試達到微小電流(pA)。Bandyopadhyay 設計了一個 VLSI 感
測電路陣列用來紀錄分散式的電化學神經系統。Martin 提出了一個低電壓化學感測介面：
全差式恆電位儀，應用於系統單晶片方面。Narula 提出一個 VLSI 恆電位儀，應用於電化學
解析分析之電流量測。最近 Gore 又提出了一個多通道的恆電位儀，應用在量測極微小電流
(fA)的生物醫學感測器之應用，Hassibi 提出一個可規化的電化學感測陣列晶片，應用在生
物醫學感測器之應用。 Liao 提出一個可攜式的電化學量測電路並整合手機通訊技術，達成
一個無線感測之量測環境。 
國內方面，台大電機、台大醫院、與台大應力所合作，結合半導體與生醫技術，成功
開發出一檢測 C-反應蛋白(C-Reactive Protein, CRP)之無線檢測系統。另外，清大、交大和
成大也有團隊作類似之研究，而我們的研究團隊也提出一個可攜式的電化學量測儀，並整
合應用於膽紅素感測器之量測。

圖 2、電極模擬擺放圖 
 
圖 3 為用三個電極的電流式感測方法之示意圖。三個電極分別稱為參考電極(Reference 
electrode)、對電極(Counter electrode)和工作電極(Working electrode)，其基本運作方式是在
工作電極與對電極之間給予一固定電流，並連接參考電極到放大器的輸入端，以感測參考
電極上的電位。在圖 3 中，分別以 ZeC、ZeR、ZeW分別代表對電極、參考電極和工作電極與
待測液介面所產生的不同等效阻抗值，而 Zm則是待測液的等效阻抗值。由圖 3 可知，用此
種方法量測到的阻抗值，除了待測液的等效阻抗值 Zm外，還包括了 Working 電極介面的等
效阻抗值 ZeW。一般在電化學應用上，此種感測方法所使用的三個電極通常是由不同的金
屬材質作成的，如金電極、白金電極、銀/氯化銀電極(Ag/AgCl)，但在 CMOS/MEMS 製程
中，我們目前可製作的只有金電極一種，所以目前我們打算這三個電極都用金電極製作。
理論上，待測液濃度不同，則等效阻抗值 ZeW 就會跟著變化，且其所造成的訊號變化量會
遠高於因電導係數所造成的變化量。

圖 6、儀表放大器系統圖 
 
 計畫第二年，我們將感測系統晶片化，晶片主要由四個部分組成，首先是提供固定電
流/電壓訊號的訊號產生器(Constant Current/Voltage Generator)，能夠同時輸出兩組固定且相
同振幅、相位的交流電流/電壓訊號，利用其中一組訊號量測已知模組得到一組Reference訊
號，另一組相同訊號則提供給檢測電極使用，透過兩組量測資料之間的相位差及振幅差來
推算檢體的阻抗值，其中更要有抑制DC訊號產生的電路，避免溶液中有DC電流產生，會
造成溶液單方向的氧化還原反應，改變溶液中濃度的分佈，是本檢測方式不期望產生的現
象；第二部分是可以調整放大倍率以及隔絕DC訊號的儀表放大器(DC Reject Instrumentation 
Amplifier)，用來量測電極上的訊號，由於訊號源已經抑制DC訊號的產生，所以將DC訊號
隔絕可以避免不必要的DC雜訊，只萃取交流差動訊號，另外也可以透過並聯的電容大小不
同來調整放大器的放大倍率，將不明顯的訊號放大；第三部分為振幅偵測器(Magnitude 
Detector)，用來偵測振幅的大小，並用DC訊號的方式輸出；最後一部分則是相位偵測儀
(Phase Detector)，比較實際量測訊號與Reference之間的相位差，並用DC的方式表示，其整
體系統架構圖如圖7所示。

(2)交換電容式直流-直流轉換器(Switched-Capacitor-Based DC-DC Converter) 
一般在無回授的交換電容式直流-直流轉換電路架構中，開關的切換頻率(switching 
frequency)及其責務比(duty ratio)都是固定的，對電容充電時，也沒有控制其充電電流
大小的機制，因此，此種電路的精確度及穩定度不佳，輸出電壓會隨著輸入電壓及負
載變化而變化，且電壓轉換比例也會被電路架構所限制住。在使用電池的儀器或電子
產品中，電池的電壓會隨著使用時間增長而下降，若使用無回授的交換電容式直流轉
換電路架構，其輸出電壓無法維持在一定值，所以一般只有在非使用電池的產品或對
輸出電壓精準度要求不高的應用中，才會採用無回授的交換電容式架構。 
交換電容式架構的這些缺點都可經由回授控制電路的加入而改善，一般而言，有
回授電路的電源轉換器，是利用回授的誤差訊號，來控制三種機制—切換頻率、責務
比、電容充電電流大小—的其中一種，以達到穩定的輸出電壓值。改變並控制責務比
的控制方法，稱作脈波寬度調變(Pulse-Width Modulation, PWM)；改變並控制切換頻率
的控制方法，稱作脈波頻率調變(Pulse-Frequency Modulation, PFM)；而另一種方法則
是以控制充電電流大小的方法，調變充電後的電容電壓值，來達到穩定的輸出電壓值
[36-38, 40-42]。 
除了穩定精準的輸出電壓要求外，也希望能夠充分使用電池電力以延長使用或待
機時間，因此，我們提出一個能夠操作在升壓或降壓模式的交換電容式新架構，如圖 
26(a)所示，在電池電力飽滿且其電壓高於所需電壓時，電路操作在降壓模式(step-down 
mode)，一旦電池電壓低於所需電壓時，電路轉成操作在升壓模式(step-up mode)，充分
使用電池電力，延長電池使用壽命[35]。如圖 26(b)所示，在降壓模式時，φ3永遠導通
且φ4永遠不導通，φ1和φ2則分別導通以對電容充放電。此外，在電路中加入回授電路
來控制電容充電電流大小，以提高Line Regulation和Load Regulation，達到穩定而精準
輸出電壓的目標。

Conductance catheter system is used to estimate real-time ventricular volume by measuring

論文英文摘要: 
For cardiac gene researches, a method to quantify the cardiac functions is needed. 
Ventricular pressure-volume loop analysis is a standard tool to evaluate and quantify the 
myocardial and cardiac functions. The ventricular volume of transgenic mice is typically 
estimated by using the conductance catheter measurement system, which measures instantaneous 
ventricular conductance and then converts it into volume. The classic conductance-to-volume 
conversion equation was proposed by Baan et al. in 1984, but its accuracy is limited. The volume 
estimation accuracy of Baan’s equation versus different possible mouse left ventricular volume 
ranges is investigated in this work. The results shows that the error of volume estimation is 
closely related to end-diastolic volume: the error increases with increasing end-diastolic volume. 
Therefore, it may not be appropriate to use Baan’s equation on the mice with the disease of 
congestive heart failure, which results in larger end-diastolic volumes.

II. METHODOLOGY 
A. Finite Element Models  
In the previous literature, in vitro experiments with 
known-volume KCl solutions placed in cylindrical insulated 
Plexiglas chambers were used to examine the performance of 
the two conversion equations [8]. Therefore, finite element 
models were constructed using COMSOL Multiphysics® to 
mimic the in vitro KCl solution experiments.  
The construction of FEM models is described briefly as 
follows. Create a cylinder with insulated boundaries to 
represent the miniaturized cylindrical Plexiglas chamber. The 
conductivity of the cylinder is set to 1.26 S/m to represent the 
used KCl solution. Except for the exposed electrodes, the 
mouse catheter is insulated. Hence, empty a 0.2-mm-radius 
thin cylinder inside the first cylinder to represent the inserted 
mouse catheter. The modeled catheter copies the size of a 
commercial mouse catheter, SPR-839, manufactured by 
Millar Instruments, Houston, Texas, USA, which has four 
0.25-mm-height platinum ring electrodes with a radius of 0.2 
mm. The interelectrode center-to-center spacing is 0.5, 4.5, 
and 0.5 mm, respectively, as illustrated in Fig. 3. The 
0.25-mm-height platinum ring electrodes are modeled by four 
small solid cylinders with the conductivity of 9661000 S/m to 
reduce the complexity of the model and the computation time. 
The outer pair of electrodes is used to provide a 30 μA current, 
so one of them is grounded, while the other one has a current 
density of 238.7 A/m2 on one 0.2-mm-radius circular 
boundary. The inner two electrodes are to model the floating 
voltage-sensing electrodes, so all their boundaries are 
insulated. The used finite elements are quadratic, and a linear 
iterative solver with drop tolerance of 10-5 was used.  
B. Radial and Longitudinal Position of the Catheter 
As it is stated above, the approach to insert catheter 
through LV apex generally does not place the catheter along 
the radial central line of the LV. Moreover, it is difficult to 
determine where the exact apex of the LV is, and whether or 
not the proximal electrode is just within the myocardial wall. 
Therefore, it is important to investigate the effects of the 
catheter position deviations on the measured conductance.  
The catheter position within the cylinder-like environment 
can be indicated by its radial and longitudinal positions. 
Accordingly, two terms are defined: the catheter radial 
distance is defined as the distance between the catheter and 
the radial central line of the chamber; the catheter depth is 
defined as the distance between the proximal electrode and 
the myocardial wall, which corresponds to the distance 
between the top electrode and the solution surface in KCl 
solution experiments or FEM models. Fig. 2 illustrates the 
definition of these two terms in the FEM models.  
The effects of the catheter position deviation on the 
measured conductance are examined by performing 
simulations with different catheter radial distances and depths. 
The conductance measured between the two inner electrodes 
can be calculated from Multiphysics® simulation results [4, 8]. 
Moreover, since LV volume changes with the heart beating, 
six models with different sizes were created to represent 
different LV volumes in the cardiac cycle: the largest one 
corresponds to the LV volume at diastole, while the smallest 
one corresponds to the LV volume at systole. Besides, we 
locate the catheter in 4 different radial positions for each 
model. Label the maximal possible catheter radial distance as 
r, and then the 4 investigated catheter radial distances are 0 
(the catheter is placed along the radial central line of the 
cylinder), r/2, 3r/4, and r (the catheter is placed next to the 
cylinder border), respectively, as illustrated in Fig. 3. Hence, 
totally 24 simulations are performed. 
 
C. Conductance-to-Volume Conversion 
An independently-measured SV is needed to determine the 
calibration factors, 1/α and γ, by eqns. (1c) and (2c). In this 
study, SV is defined as the true volumes difference between 
the largest and smallest cylinders among the 6 FEM models, 
while the true volume of the cylinder is defined as the 
solution volume between the two inner sensing electrodes. 
Once the calibration factors are calculated, the measured 
conductance from the simulations can be converted to its 
corresponding volume by using the classic and the nonlinear 
conversion equations, eqns (1b) and 2(b), respectively. Then, 
compare the resulting volumes with their true volumes.

A. Catheter Position Deviation v.s. Conductance 
Figure 4(a) shows the relationship between the catheter 
radial distance and the measured conductance in a cylinder. 
As the catheter moves from the center to the peripheral, the

On the other hands, the conductance-volume relationship 
derived from the nonlinear conversion equation tracks the 
true conductance-volume relationship very well, as shown in 
Fig. 5(b). As the catheter radial distance increases, both 
relation curves move to the left with a similar rate. Therefore, 
one can conclude that the nonlinear conversion equation can 
calibrate and compensate the errors induced by the catheter 
position deviation, while the classic conversion equation fails 
to do so. 
For the ideal in vivo catheter placement, both the catheter 
radial distance and depth should be zero. However, in in vivo 
experiments, physicians cannot know the exact catheter 
position without aided imaging tools. Therefore, the catheter 
is typically not placed in its ideal position in vivo, which 
changes the conductance-volume relationship. According to 
Fig. 5(a) and (b), if the classic equation is used to convert the 
measured conductance to volume, the converted volumes get 
from the true volumes, as the catheter moves away from its 
ideal position. Contrarily, if the nonlinear equation is used, 
the volume-estimation accuracy remains almost the same, no 
matter whether or not the catheter is placed ideally.

V. CONCLUSION 
The effects of catheter radial and longitudinal position 
deviations on the measured conductance were investigated. 
Contrary to the common assumption of center-located 
catheter in previous literature, FEM models with the catheter 
placed in different positions were constructed and simulated. 
The simulation results show that the measured conductance 
does change with varied catheter position. 
However, these position-deviation-induced errors should 
be capable to be compensated more or less by the calibration 
factors of the conversion equations. The error-calibrating 
capacities of the classic and nonlinear conversion equations 
were compared. According to the simulation results, the 
nonlinear conversion equation can calibrate the errors 
induced by the catheter position deviation, while the classic 
conversion equation fails to do so.

II. METHODOLOGY  
A. Potassium Chloride Solution Experiments 
Seven cylindrical holes were drilled in thick blocks of 
Plexiglas, whose conductivity is essentially zero. Those holes 
were filled by the KCl solution with the conductivity of 1.26 
S/m. A miniaturized commercial mouse conductance catheter, 
SPR-839, made by Millar Instruments, Houston, Texas, USA, 
was used to measure the solution conductance in Plexiglas 
holes. The mouse catheter consists of four 0.25-mm-long 
platinum ring electrodes with 0.2 mm radius. The 
interelectrode center-to-center spacing is 0.5, 4.5, and 0.5 mm, 
respectively (see Figure 1(b)). The true volume is defined as 
the solution volume between the two inner voltage-sensing 
electrodes. The true volumes of those 7 cylindrical holes range 
from 7 μl to 80 μl, which is about the possible LV volume 
range of mice [3-4, 6]. The catheter was inserted into the 
center of the cylindrical hole and fixed using a mechanical 
holder. 
The two outermost electrodes is used to provide a constant 
10 kHz, 30 μA peak excitation ac current, and the voltage 
difference between the two inner electrodes is sensed and 
measured. The magnitude of the sensed voltage is proportional 
to the resistance between the inner electrode pair. This signal 
is then amplified and rectified to remove the carrier frequency, 
10 kHz. The reciprocal of this signal was taken to yield the 
conductance-magnitude-proportional voltage output, which is 
then calibrated to conductance [4, 6-7]. Figure 2 shows the 
block diagram of this measurement system.  
 
 
 
 
Figure 2.  The block diagram of conductance catheter measurement system 
 
B. Data Analysis 
The LV volume of a normal mouse may ranges from 8 μl to 
50 μl [6]. For a mouse with CHF, its EDV gets even larger, 
while its ESV also gets larger than that of a normal mouse [3]. 
Therefore, the data of the KCl solution experiments may be 
partitioned into several different sets: a set with small EDV and 
small ESV represents the LV volume range of small or young 
mice, a set with large EDV and small ESV represents the LV 
volume range of large or mature mice, and a set with large 
EDV and large ESV represents the LV volume range of mice 
with CHF. Let each set have at least three data, and thus there 
are totally 15 possible data sets.  
The SV of each set is defined as the volume difference 
between its EDV and ESV. As a result, the calibration factor (α) 
of each data set can be calculated by Eq. (3). In addition, since 
the Plexiglas is insulated, the measured conductance comes 
totally from the solution, which means that GP in Eq. (1) is 
essentially zero. Therefore, Eq. (2) is used to convert the 
measured conductance to volume. The average volume 
estimation error of each set, Eave(i), is calculated by

V. CONCLUSION 
The volume-estimation accuracy of the mouse conductance 
catheter system with respect to EDV, ESV and SV were 
analyzed. The results show that the average error by using 
Baan’s equation is closely related to EDV. When EDV gets 
larger, the volume estimation error gets more significant. 
Therefore, for mice with CHF, it may not be appropriate to 
adopt Baan’s equation for converting the measured 
conductance to volume.

IV. MEASURED RESULTS 
A 5V/3.3V step-down SC-based converter with PI control 
scheme was implemented by using the 0.35µm mixed-signal 
2P4M polycide 3.3/5V process. Both CM and CL were 1μF, 
and D was 0.5. According to Eq. (12), the low limit of the 
switching frequency was 72.4 kHz, and the switching 
frequency was set to 100 kHz. The core area was 588×293 
μm2, and the whole testchip area was 1.195×1.304 mm2. Fig. 
6 shows the transient response of the output voltage by 
changing the load current from 50 μA to 7.5 mA. 
Theoretically, the conversion efficiency of this type of 
converters is no more than 66% [1, 2, 4]. The measured 
efficiency was 65% for a 7.5 mA load current, and 57% for 
1mA. Table I summaries the measured results. 
 
V. CONCLUSION 
This work presents an approach to design a step-down SC-
based DC-DC converter for low-power applications. An 
integrated 5V/3.3V step-down SC-based DC-DC converter 
was implemented using the 0.35µm mixed-signal 2P4M 
polycide process. The measurement results show that the 
converter achieves excellent line and load regulations.

In the DSP chip, the digitized signals were filtered by 30-
kHz bandpass filters (BPFs) to remove the noise outside the 
signal band, and then the magnitude and phase of the sensed 
voltage were detected. Some digital signal processing 
techniques were used to reduce the data rate of the signals,

measured signals. As a result, the real-time pressure-volume 
relations can be observed from the LabVIEW graphic user 
interface.

White female Wistar rats were used in the experiment,
