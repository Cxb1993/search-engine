中文摘要
高熔點合金硬膜 Mo-Ru 已應用在模造玻璃用模仁表面保護硬膜，以延長模仁壽命。在
實際模造環境氣氛中含有氮氣以及殘留約 10 ppm 的氧氣，因此表面保護膜在量產製程中的
抗氧化能力成為重要議題。本研究探討 Mo-Ru 硬膜在 600 oC 含氧環境中的氧化行為。結果
顯示氧成分進入 Mo-Ru 膜層中，影響相穩定性以及機械性質。在四小時熱處理後，由穿透
式電子顯微鏡顯示出高 Mo 含量的鍍層有 MoO3 氧化層以及表面下的 Mo 流失區出現。由
XPS 驗證 Mo 在氧導入後有價數的變化，顯示氧傾向與 Mo 結合形成氧化物。另ㄧ方面高
Ru 含量的合金鍍層有較好的抗氧化能力，氧吸收的量少、硬度提高，為適當的保護膜。然
而隨著熱處理時間增長，可觀察到三個氧化階段。最初四小時熱處理，少量氧的導入造成
固溶強化以及再結晶現象使得膜層硬化，硬度值自 13 GPa 提高至 19 GPa。熱處理至八小
時可觀察到島狀氧化物的形成，同時因為 MoO3 的高揮發性，Mo 向外流失，使得表面粗糙
化。最後在 20 小時熱處理，觀察到裂痕在膜層中擴展。
在 10ppmO2-N2 氣氛熱處理，表面粗糙度因為島狀氧化物的形成而顯著地增大。然而在
220ppmO2-N2 氣氛熱處理，緻密的氧化層形成而使表面相對平滑。分別以穿透式電子顯微
鏡以及 Auger 電子光譜縱深曲線分析顯微結構以及組成變化，顯示氧化層是由優養與缺氧
層交替組成的層狀結構。此部份後續研究仍持續進行中。
關鍵字： Mo-Ru、氧化、機械性質、玻璃模造
Abstract
Mo-Ru coatings are used as protective hard coatings on the top surface of glass molding dies
to prolong the lifetime of die materials. In a realistic molding environment, the atmosphere
consists of steadily purged nitrogen and residual oxygen, around 10 ppm O2. The oxidation
resistance of surface coatings in mass production becomes a critical issue. In this study, the
diffusion of oxygen into the Mo38.1Ru61.9 coatings annealed at 600oC in an oxygen containing
atmosphere was investigated. Oxygen was absorbed into the Mo-Ru coatings, which affected the
phase stability and mechanical properties. The X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy proved the
valence variation of Mo in the near surface, accompanied by the introduction of oxygen ions,
which implied the formation of molybdenum oxides. After 4 hrs annealing, a scale of MoO3 and a
Mo-depleted transition zone near the surface were pointed out by the transmission electron
microscopy investigation on the Mo-rich coating. On the other hand, the Ru-rich coatings
absorbed oxygen in a less content and behaved higher hardness after annealing. Solid solution
strengthening and recrystallization due to the introduction of oxygen raised the surface hardness
31. 前言
玻璃模造技術逐漸成為高精度非球面
光學元件的關鍵生產技術[1-5]，尤其是應用
在手機鏡頭、數位相機鏡頭等民生消費產業
裡。在模造製程中，模仁必須忍受自室溫到
600 °C反覆式的熱循環製程。因此，模仁衍
生成模仁材料與表面硬膜的搭配組合。模仁
材料必須能被加工成光學面，並具有優異的
抗熱震與老化能力，如SiC以及WC。而硬膜
材料則必須在模造溫度下不與玻璃反應以
及有足夠硬度以避免被刮傷。然而鍍膜製程
免不了造成的缺陷，提供模仁局部暴露與氧
化的管道，模仁表面形狀精度因而降低，使
得壽命受限[6]。在量產製程中，兼顧製程產
能、成本、運作下，係透過揮發液態氮充填
入模造腔體以形成保護氣氛，故實際仍存在
ppm等級的氧含量。因此，保護膜的抗氧化
能力成為重要的議題。貴金屬合金膜，如鉑
合金[7]，銥合金[8]，以及碳膜[6]等，已被
廣泛使用以及見諸於專利說明書中。氮化物
如TiAlN[9,10]，CrWN[11]及TiAlN/ZrN [12]
做為模仁硬膜的學術研究也曾被報導。然而
在實際模造環境含氧氣氛中硬膜的耐氧化
效應仍未有具體的研究，而這將會影響到硬
膜的使用效能，也成為本研究的目標。
在之前的研究中，已發展Mo-Ru合金硬
膜[13]以取代昂貴的鉑合金、銥合金等習知
玻璃模造用保護硬膜。利用在以Ru為主的基
材裡，生成Mo5Ru3相可以有效抑制熱效應
造成的晶粒成長，在真空環境中實施600°C
四小時熱處理後，仍能具有足夠的硬度、適
當的表面粗糙度以及維持相的穩定，具備成
為模仁硬膜的條件。因此本研究進一步在實
際玻璃模造機中建構含氧氣氛以評估
Mo-Ru硬膜的抗氧化能力。

4. 結論
Ru 含量較高的 Mo-Ru 合金硬膜抗氧化
性較佳，適宜做為模造玻璃模仁表面保護硬
膜。在之前的研究顯示 Mo-Ru 鍍層在真空
中 600°C 熱處理 4 小時表面硬度由原來的
11-13 GPa 略降低至 11-12 GPa[13]，主要的
原因來自晶粒大小略微增大；而在 12± 2
ppm O2-N2 氣氛下熱處理，Ru 成分較多的
Mo-Ru 鍍層表面硬度由 13-16GPa 增大至
18-20 GPa，這是因為氧導入至 Mo-Ru 鍍層
後產生固溶強化以及 Mo-Ru 鍍層再結晶的
效果。而在高氧含量氣氛中，由於層狀結構
的出現，形同複合材料，表面硬度自熱處理
前的 16.0±0.4GPa 提高至 19.2 ±2.4GPa，而
且形成有效的氧擴散阻滯層。
由於在低氧濃度模造環境中歷經較長時
間，即使是 Ru 含量較高的 Mo-Ru 膜仍會氧
化，故在實際應用時應注意模造成型機內氧
分壓的控制，或者發展受損膜層剝除及再生
的技術[27]，以利本計畫研究成果之實際應
用。此外開發適當的氧擴散阻滯層亦是另一
個途徑，如本研究發現的相分離現象，期待
未來能在此方面有進一步突破。

3. Results and discussion
3.1. Chemical composition variations
As annealed at 600 °C under amolding atmosphere containing 12±
2 ppm oxygen, the Mo38.1Ru61.9 coatings were oxidized to different
extents as listed in Table 1. Nitrogenwas also considered as the possible
incorporation of the coatings since the annealing was conducted
in the nitrogen containing atmosphere. However, the contents of
nitrogen were analyzed to be 0–0.3 at.%, and thus were ignored.
Apparently, coatings annealed for longer times absorbed more oxygen
atoms. The atomic ratio ofO/(Mo+Ru) analyzed by FE-EPMAon the top
surface increased from 0.16, to 0.29, to 0.39, to 0.45, to 0.41 as the
annealing timewas increased from4, to 8, to 12, to 16, to 20 h. The slope
of O/(Mo+Ru) vs. annealing time decreased as the time increased as
shown in Fig. 1, whichdemonstrated that a diffusion controlled reaction
should be investigated. Moreover, the volatility of MoO3 should
be discussed, since its volatility at temperatures above 550 °C in
10.1 kPa of oxygen [13] or in air [14] is well-known. The variation of
the Mo:Ru concentration ratio related to the absorbed oxygen content
after annealing was neglected.
The Auger depth proﬁles of the 16 and 20 h annealed Mo38.1Ru61.9
coatings are shown in Fig. 2a and b, respectively. 5 at.% of oxygen was
observed at depths of 40, 110, 170 and 270 nm for the 4, 8, 12 and 16 h
annealed coatings, respectively. The Mo/(Mo+Ru) ratios were in the
range of 0.32–0.37 and 0.34–0.39 in most regions of the 16 and 20 h
annealed coatings, respectively. Though oxygen diffused into the
normal Mo–Ru regions, both Mo and Ru contents decreased, however
the Mo/(Mo+Ru) ratios remained constant. Outside the normal Mo–
Ru regions, aMo-depleted zonewas observed beneath the free surface
except for the sample annealed for 4 h only.
3.2. Structure variations
The as-deposited Mo38.1Ru61.9 coating consisted of Mo5Ru3 and
minor Ru phases. TheMo5Ru3 phase possesses a strong (202) peak and
apparent (411), (422) and (433) reﬂections as seen in Fig. 3. The main
peak, Mo5Ru3 (202) corresponding to the strong texture of columnar

3.3. Surface quality and mechanical properties
By AFM analysis, the surface roughness of Mo38.1Ru61.9 coatings
annealed in the molding atmosphere for 4 h is lower than that of as-
deposited coatings as listed in Table 2, which means that annealing,
without forming an obvious Mo-depleted zone or oxide scale, could
smooth the surface as the annealing temperature 600 °C was higher
than the deposition temperature 550 °C. An increase of surface
roughness accompanied the formation of a Mo-depleted zone after
longer annealing treatment in the molding atmosphere. The AFM
observations showed similar trends in two different scanned areas,
2 µm ×2 µm and 10 µm×10 µm.
The nanoindentation test was performed under 3000 µN load for
as-deposited Mo38.1Ru61.9 coatings, 4000 µN load for 4, 8 and 12 h
annealed ones, and 5000 µN load for 16 and 20 h annealed ones. To
prevent the substrate effect, a suitable ratio of indentation depth to
ﬁlm thickness should be allowed to acquire the precise nanohardness
and Young's modulus of ﬁlms [19]. The indentation depth of the
Berkovich tip is within the range of 73 to 88 nm as can be seen in
Table 2. Since the Mo–Ru coatings exhibited a thickness of 0.77 µm,
the indentation depth to ﬁlm thickness was around 1/10. The
hardness of as-deposited Mo38.1Ru61.9 coatings was 13.1 GPa. After
being annealed in the molding atmosphere for 4 to 16 h, the hardness
raised to 18–19 GPa. Accompanied by the introduction of oxygen,
solid solution strengthening and recrystallization of Mo–Ru grains
resulted in hardness enhancement. By the XRD analysis, the grain
dimension calculated by Scherrer's formula [20] was 37 nm for the as-
deposited sample. Using the Mo5Ru3 (202) Kα1 reﬂection of the
annealed Mo38.1Ru61.9 coatings, the grain dimensions were around
25–31 nm. Since the crystalline feature of Mo38.1Ru61.9 coatings
revealed a columnar grain structure, the calculated grain dimension
should be relative to the cross-section dimension of the columnar
grain. The grain dimension variation was small between the annealed
samples, which implied the annealed coatings possessed similar
hardness values. For the 20 h annealed Mo38.1Ru61.9 coating with
60 nm oxide scale, a hardness of 22 GPa was recorded. The Young's
modulus data were sustained at the level around 210–240 GPa except
for the 20 h annealed Mo38.1Ru61.9 coating, which exhibited an oxide
scale and incorporated cracks, which revealed a distinct decrease of
Young's modulus to 180 GPa only.
4. Conclusions
Three stages were veriﬁed for the diffusion of oxygen into the Mo–
Rumatrix under a 12 ppm oxygen containing atmosphere at 600 °C. In
the beginning, the oxygen atoms diffused into theMo–Ru coatings at a
low level and the diffusion out of Mo or Ru was ignored, which
strengthened the surface hardness and became a valid protective
coating for the die material with high surface quality. In further heat
treatment, the preferential oxidation of Mo and high volatility of
MoO3 resulted in the presence of a Mo-depleted zone and roughened
the surface gradually, which limited the reliability and lifetime of the
Mo–Ru protective coating. Finally, cracks occurred in the oxide scale
and propagated in the Mo-depleted zone and the depth of the Mo-
depleted zone spread into the deeper Mo–Ru coating.
