1摘要
軟焊為微電子產品、與光電產品中最主要的連接技術。錫鉛合金為目前最常見的
銲料，然而因為鉛對環境及身體健康具有危害，無鉛銲料要取代錫鉛銲料，已是短期
內將實現的現實。本計畫以 Sn-Ag-Cu、Sn-Ag、Sn-Cu、與 Sn-In 合金，與 Ni、Ag 與
Cu 基材之軟焊連結為研究之標的，擬探討的性質主要為相關材料多元系統的相平衡，
並用以探討銲料與基材間的溼潤性與界面反應特性、以及銲料合金固化的特性。而其
中多元系統相平衡的研究，是本計畫中最具特色、同時也是本計畫的研究重心。本計
畫除了將繼續進行相平衡之實驗量測外，並將進行多元相圖之計算，以建立能進行實
驗也能進行計算，十分具特色的相圖研究中心。本計畫將首先評估上述三種四元系統
的溼潤性、相平衡與界面反應資料，針對文獻中不足或欠缺之處，進行更深入之探討
研究。
關鍵字：無鉛銲料、相平衡、濕潤性、界面反應、固化
Abstract
Soldering technology is the primary joining technology in the microelectronic and
optoelectronic products. Pb-Sn alloys are the most popular commercial solders nowadays;
however, owing to the concerns of the threats of Pb to the environments and human health,
replacing the Pb-Sn alloys with Pb-free solders will be a reality in the very near future. This
proposal targets on the related soldering issues between the promising Pb-free solders, Sn-Ag-Cu,
Sn-Ag, Sn-Cu and Sn-In alloys, and the commonly encountered Ni, Ag, and Cu substrates. This
proposal plans to evaluate the literatures of the wetting, interfacial reactions, solidification and
phase equilibria of the systems mentioned above, and then carry out studies on the insufficient and
no data systems. With the very preliminary literature surveys, this proposal has selected quite a
few topics as shown in the working items to work with. However, we will try hard to reposition
our studies to focus on more important issues during this project, so that we can maximize our
academic impacts with limited funding sources. One thing for sure and very important is the effort
to work on phase equilibria. Phase diagram can be determined either by experimental
determination or thermodynamic calculation. A very significant ambition of this proposal is not
only to determine the phase diagrams of the important solder systems but also to establish a phase
diagram center in Taiwan. This phase diagram research center will have the ability to carry out
both experimental determination and thermodynamic calculation.
Keywords: Lead-free solder, Phase equilibria, wetting, interfacial reaction, solidification

光電產品中，其待連結的物件表面通常會有表面處理。如在近年中最受到矚目的銲料
凸塊(bump)覆晶(flip chip)接合技術，係以銲料來連接積體電路線路與覆晶基板[11]。
為了避免銲料與積體電路導線間的界面反應，於凸塊與積體電路間須有稱為凸塊下金
屬化(UBM，under bump metallization)的表面處理，所以與銲料直接接觸者為這些表面
處理所生成的金屬層。較常見的基材為 Ni、Ag、Cu 與 Au。
除了上述的 Sn-Ag-Cu、Sn-Ag、Sn-Cu 與 Sn-Zn 等銲料外，Sn-In 銲料應亦為具有
良好應用潛力的銲料。雖然銦的價格高昂，含銦合金在微電子產品上一般並不被認為
具有大量使用的可能性。Sn-Ag-Cu 等合金雖受到推薦，但是這些合金皆具有一項顯著
的缺點，就是它們的熔點偏高。Sn-Ag-Cu 的共晶溫度在 217oC[12]，Sn-Ag 與 Sn-Cu
的熔點則更高，皆高於目前所用 Sn-Pb 共晶合金的熔點 183oC。較高的熔點代表著較
高的軟焊操作溫度，以及須要較高玻璃轉換點(glass transition point)的基材，二者皆代
表著較高的成本。而 Sn-In 共晶銲料的熔點為 120oC，遠低於上述所提的數種具潛力的
合金。Chen 等人[13]提出了於凸塊表面上鍍銦之複合銲料專利方法，此種專利方法藉
由降低銦的用量以降低銦價昂的缺點，其所獲得的利益則為較低的軟焊溫度。如果此
專利確實可行，Sn-In 銲料將可能異軍突起，成為主流的無鉛銲料。此外 Sn-In 合金與
一般基材的溼潤性良好，而且具有很好的疲勞性質[14-16]，In 與 Sn-In 合金在光電的
產品上已有部份的應用。所以在微電子產品與光電產品的軟焊探討中，除了
Sn-Ag-Cu、Sn-Ag、Sn-Cu 與 Sn-Zn 合金外，Sn-In 合金亦具很高的探討價值。
雖然無鉛銲料已是箭在弦上，然而仍有許多基本資料尚未清楚。本計畫的目的即
在於針對上述最具應用可能性的無鉛銲料與基材系統，根據計畫主持人的學術專長對

對 Sn-Ag、Sn-Cu、與 Sn-Ag-Cu 銲料，與 Ag、Cu、Ni 之基材連結，其相關的四

Ag-Ni、Cu-Ni、Sn-Ag-Cu、Sn-Ag-Ni、Sn-Cu-Ni、及 Ag-Cu-Ni。其銲點的可能組合為
Sn-Ag/Ag、Sn-Ag/Cu、Sn-Ag/Ni、Sn-Cu/Ag、Sn-Cu/Cu、Sn-Cu/Ni、Sn-Ag-Cu/Ag、
Sn-Ag-Cu/Cu、及 Sn-Ag-Cu/Ni。針對各材料系統，其溼潤性、界面反應、以及相平衡
的文獻整理如下。
7Sn-3.5wt%Ag 合金與 10μm 厚的 Ag 之界面反應，研究了從 250℃至 325℃溫度範圍之

Sn-Ag/Cu界面反應
London等人[31]研究共晶組成的Sn-Ag合金(Sn-3.5wt%Ag)在240至360℃下與Cu的
界面反應，發現界面生成兩個介金屬相，1-Cu6Sn5與1-Cu3Sn。Choi等人[32]也在70
至180℃間，探討共晶Sn-Ag合金與Cu基材的界面反應。發現界面也僅有1相與1相的
生成。Vianco等人[33]研究了Sn-3.5wt%Ag與Cu基材在70到205℃之間的界面反應，發
現了1相與1相在界面生成。此外Vianco等人利用1相與1相厚度的比例，推論Cu原
子為此反應系統中擴散速率較快的原子 [34]。Flanders等人 [35]研究110到208℃
Sn-3.5wt%Ag與Cu基材的固相-固相界面反應，也在界面發現生成1相與1相。但
Flanders等人在介金屬相界面發現Sn-3.5wt%Ag合金在固化時所析出的2-Ag3Sn相會隨
反應時間延長而遠離Cu界面，向合金方向移動。如此的現象，相當於Kirkendall效應中
的marker。因此推論Sn原子為此系統中擴散速率較快的原子，異於Vianco等人[34]的結
果。
本實驗室曾對Sn-Ag/Cu系統進行較完整的探討，除了研究Sn-3.5wt%Ag外，亦探
討了Sn-25wt%Ag與Sn-74wt%Ag合金與金屬Cu的反應[36]，反應的溫度則包括了240oC
與450oC。Sn-3.5wt%Ag/Cu在240oC下，反應結果與之前之文獻類似，亦發現1相與1
相於界面生成。雖然各研究團體所發表的結果不盡相同，但均有一個共同的特點：即
在共晶Sn-Ag合金與Cu的界面僅生成兩介金屬相，1相與1相，並無其他二元或三元的
介金屬相被發現。此外，介金屬相的成長也都符合拋物線定律的關係式，顯示介金屬
的成長機制為擴散所控制。對Sn-25wt%Ag/Cu與Sn-74wt%Ag/Cu在450oC下，其界面生
成相分析較為複雜，主要的原因在於Sn湯於固化過程中亦會產生生成相。為了釐清界
面反應之反應控制元素，除了液-固之反應外，本實驗室亦曾製備了1-(Ag3Sn)/Cu之反
應偶[36]。結果顯示Sn原子為系統中擴散最快的原子，與Flanders等人有相同的結論。
此外亦確定了在上述反應偶中，Ag是擴散最慢之元素。
Sn-Ag/Ni界面反應
Jang 等人[37]研究 Sn-3.5wt%Ag 無鉛銲料與 UBM 上無電鍍 Ni-P 的反應。實驗分
為兩種方式，其一是在 260oC 迴銲 10 次，另一則是置於 150oC 與 170oC 下反應 500-1000
小時。反應結果均觀察到在界面處只有 Ni3Sn4 介金屬相，Sn-Ag 合金處則有 Ag3Sn 相。
93 次的迴銲後，發現在 Cu6Sn5 與 Cu 基材間會有新的 Cu3Sn 介金屬生成。Lee 等人[43]
對 Sn-0.7wt%Cu/Cu 界面反應做過探討，在一開始迴銲時，在界面有扇貝狀之 Cu6Sn5
生成，而接下來在三種固態溫度(125、150、170℃)與不同時效時間(0~1500 小時)下進
行觀察，在一開始時間為 0 時，上述的三種溫度下並未觀察到有 Cu3Sn 介金屬生成，
而只有 Cu6Sn5 生成，但是時間拉長到 500、1000、1500 小時，則有 Cu3Sn 生成，並且
發現了在迴銲形成的扇貝狀介金屬相轉變成層狀的介金屬相，且此界面反應之介金屬
相成長機制為擴散所控制。Yoon 等人[44]也曾對共晶 Sn-0.7wt%Cu/Cu 界面反應做過
探討，討論了在 255℃的迴銲溫度下，不同反應時間對界面所產生的影響，發現在反
應時間為 1s、1min、10min、30min 時，均可看到 Cu6Sn5 介金屬生成，隨著迴銲時間
的增加，原本圓形扇貝狀之介金屬轉變成較長之扇貝狀或棒狀之形狀，而且介金屬層
的厚度也隨之變厚。
Sn-Cu/Ni界面反應
Frear[45]等人對 Sn-0.7wt%Cu/Ni 的界面反應進行研究，其實驗步驟是將
Sn-0.7wt%Cu/Ni在 260℃下迴流(reflow)一分鐘，僅在界面處發現一層Ni3Sn4的生成相。
Chen 等人[41]發現 Sn-0.7wt%Cu/Ni 界面反應，會生成 Ni3Sn4 相。但當通入電流，加入
電遷移的效應之後，會再發現 Cu6Sn5 的生成相。Chen 等人[46]對 Sn-Cu 合金/Ni 系統
的界面反應在 250℃下進行研究，發現在 Sn-0.2wt%Cu 會生成 Ni3Sn4 相，
Sn-0.7wt%Cu/Ni 及 Sn-1wt%Cu/Ni 會生成 Cu6Sn5 相，而在 Sn-0.4wt%Cu/Ni 時會生成
Ni3Sn4 相及 Cu6Sn5 相。
本實驗室，也曾對 Sn-0.7wt%Cu/Ni 分別在 200℃及 250℃進行固-固及液-固的界
面反應。在 200℃下的固-固界面反應，在反應 12 小時至 27 天均生成兩層的生成相，
分別為 Ni3Sn4 和 Cu6Sn5，且 Cu6Sn5 的厚度約是 Ni3Sn4 的 5 倍。此兩生成相的總厚度
和反應時間的平方根作圖，呈線性關係。在 250℃下的液-固界面反應，僅有看到一生
成相，為 Cu6Sn5，當反應至 2 小時，此 Cu6Sn5 相會在中間出現裂痕，進而發生層狀剝
落。Sn-微量 Cu/Ni 在 200℃下進行反應，均會生成 Cu6Sn5 相及 Ni3Sn4相，此和 Sn 對

研究方法及實驗步驟
本計畫的目標在於提供 Sn-Ag-Cu-Ni、Sn-In-Cu-Ni、與 Sn-In-Ag-Ni 四元相關系統
的溼潤性、界面反應、合金固化與相平衡之基本資料。研究進行的方法為首先進行文
獻的蒐集與分析，以評估現有文獻中不足與不全之處，並選擇擬探討研究之對象。溼
潤性是以溼潤天平(wetting balance)進行量測。界面反應是以反應偶的實驗方法探討，
並配合相關系統的相圖以探討反應之路徑與機制。合金固化是以微差熱分析儀(DTA,
differential thermal analyzer)量測加熱與降溫曲線，再配合相平衡的知識，探討固化的
路徑與機制。相平衡的探討為本計畫之重心，將包括相平衡的實驗與相圖的計算。相
圖的計算將採用 CALPHAD 的方法，並應用商用的 Pandat 軟體。研究的進行步驟如下：
(A) 溼潤性量測
本研究將以濕潤天平，量測無鉛銲料之潤濕性。首先秤取適量之 Sn-In 合金，置於
高溫爐中熔融之後，再將此熔融合金倒入濕潤天平的銲錫槽中。所使用的基材為 Ag
片、Cu 片、及 Ni 片三種，將之用鑽石切割機裁成長為 15mm，寬為 1mm 的試片，使
用壓片機壓平，經清洗後並浸於助銲劑中數十秒，以達清理基材表面之目的。將處理
過表面的基材用夾具夾住，並沾以少量的助銲劑，掛於濕潤天平的基材懸掛處，此時
並將在此位置所受的力歸零。設定好所有的測試參數後（操作條件則是參照
MIL-STD-883D Method 20022.2, 1987），就可以啟動濕潤天平系統量測。對每個基材而
言，主要分成二個部分的實驗：在改變處理基材之助銲劑及改變銲料的溫度下，以濕
潤天平量測在不同環境下，不同基材與不同銲料間潤濕現象的變化情形。
(B) 界面反應實驗
本計畫主要利用反應偶的技術來探討電子構裝銲接點相關系統的界面反應。反應
偶，顧名思義，是將兩種不同的材料接在一起以進行反應。在此材料的選擇，一為無
鉛銲料相關元素或合金，另一則為基材，兩者接觸界面所發生的反應(界面反應)即為
本實驗主要觀察的區域。

反應室中氧氣濃度降至最低，所以必須抽真空並反覆通入高純度的 Ar gas，排除反
應室中的空氣，避免金屬氧化。合金融熔、凝固的過程至少 3~ 5 次以上，每次電弧
熔合時間約為 3 秒，以確保合金能均勻混合。金屬熔合前後均須秤重，以確定質量
損失率在 0.5%以內，不會對合金組成造成明顯的影響。
之後將配製好的合金置於石英玻璃管中，在管內壓力低於 10-5bar 下真空密封，
避免合金氧化。將封好的合金樣品置於 1000℃高溫爐中反應 6 小時，目的在使金屬能
均勻混合。6 小時後取出合金以冰水淬冷。再將這些合金置入 210℃的高溫爐中進行熱
處理，熱處理時間為 6 星期~12 星期，使合金達相平衡。
在完成退火程序之後，將合金取出後以冰水迅速冷卻。再以鑽石切割機(Buehler,
ISOMETTM, U.S.A.)將熱處理過後的合金切成兩半，一半先以熱埋機(Buehler)將合金
與導電電木粉鑲埋，之後取出合金試樣，先以 1200 目的碳化矽砂紙進行合金試樣的研
磨，再以 2400 目的砂紙進行細磨，由小到大依序進行研磨，最後再以1.0μm及0.3μm
的氧化鋁粉進行拋光的步驟，以使合金表面光滑無刮痕。拋光完畢後，再利用超音波
震盪器去除殘留合金表面的氧化鋁粉；之後將完成上述程序之合金使用光學顯微鏡
(OM, Olympus, BH, Japan)及掃描式電子顯微鏡(SEM, Hitachi, S-2500,Japan)進行金相
觀察；若觀察發現不同相間的對比不明顯，可將合金浸入溶液組成比例為
CH3OH:HCl:HNO3=93:2:5(ml)的蝕刻液中一分鐘，可使對比更加明顯。
而另一半的合金則可利用銼刀或研缽敲碎後研磨成粉末，並以 X 光繞射儀(X-ray
diffractometer, XRD; Scintac, XDS-2000 V/H, U.S.A)分析以確定合金的生成相。
繞射儀所使用的光源為Cu Kα之 X 光入射源，電壓和電流分別為 40kv 與 35mA，
繞射角度為2θ從20˚至90˚。之後將分析合金的繞射圖譜與國際粉末繞射委員會(Joint
Committee on Powder Diffraction Standard ,JCPDS)所建立的標準繞射圖譜加以比對，就
可得知化合物或元素之繞射峰值及繞射峰彼此之相對強度，藉此找出符合的相。
並將 XRD 的結果與能量分散光譜儀 (Energy Dispersive spectrometer, EDS)或電子
微針探測儀(Electron Probe Microanalysis, EPMA; JEOL, JXA-8600 SX, Japan)所分析的
組成加以對照，以確認各種金屬相的種類。在利用電子微針探測儀進行分析時，合金
應避免蝕刻，以確保其組成及平面的完整性。

Friction-stir spot welding of dissimilar metals
Yu-chih Huang1, Young-ki Yang2, Sindo Kou2* and Sinn-wen Chen1
1 Department of Chemical Engineering, National Tsing Hua University, Hsin-Chu,
Taiwan

kou@engr.wisc.edu
Friction-stir welding (FSW) is a relatively new metal interconnection process. This
technique invented at The Welding Institute in 1991 is considered a revolution in
welding technology. Although it is regarded as a solid-state joining process, recent
studies have shown that liquid phase can be formed during friction-stir spot welding
(FSSW). The plastic deformation of stirred material can be affected significantly by
the formation of liquid phase. In this study, we examined several systems including
Al1100-Mg and simple eutectic Sn-Pb, Sn-Zn, Ag-Cu using FSSW. Working
material stuck to and removed by the tool when the tool was withdrawn at the end of
welding in Sn on Pb and Al1100 on Mg couples. No eutectic phase was found near
the keyholes of Sn-Pb, Sn-Zn systems. Except for Zn on Sn with long tool pin, there
was no mechanical mixture of two base metals in the resultant joints of Sn-Pb and
Sn-Zn. On the contrary, the materials mixture through mechanical mixing was
considerable in Al1100-Mg and Ag-Cu systems.
alloys [11-16].
The liquation of stirred material can have significant effect on plastic
deformation in FSW and deteriorate the strength of welding joint. Yang et al. [17]
have demonstrated and explained the differences in tendency to liquate among several
Al and Mg alloys in FSSW. In addition, a simple binary Al-6.3Cu alloy has been
used to investigate liquid-film formation induced by liquation in FSSW and its effect
on the resultant weld [18].
In this study, investigations into the resultant welds of Al1100-Mg, Sn-Pb,
Sn-Zn and Ag-Cu were carried out to examine the possible solidified eutectic phase or
intermetallic compounds after FSSW.

RESULTS AND DISCUSSION
1. Sn-Pb
Figure 2 and 3 are the photos of the Sn/Pb and Pb/Sn resultant joints welded with
long pin by FSSW. The keyhole morphology of Sn/Pb joint is different from that of
Pb/Sn joint. It can be attributed to the stirred material removed by the tool pin.
When the work-piece was composed by Sn on Pb, an amount of stirred material stuck
to and removed by the tool pin after FSSW, as shown in Figure 4. This phenomenon,
however, is not observed in the case of Pb on Sn.
Microstructural examinations under optical microscopy showed that neither
mechanical mixture of material nor eutectic phase is observed in the resultant welds
of Sn-Pb system. There is a possibility that the two base metals were too soft to
have enough resistant heat for forming liquid phase. In addition, the material
mixture was stuck to and removed by the tool pin. Thus the resultant welds look like
that the two base metals were just squeezed together without being mixed with each
other through the pressure of the tool pin.
2. Sn-Zn
Figure 5(a)-5(d) are the cross-section photos of the Sn-Zn welds. Owing to the
different mechanical properties such as hardness, the joint morphologies are different
between Sn/Zn and Zn/Sn work-piece. Table 6 shows the mechanical properties of

joint welded with long pin. The high resistance heat generated from welding
resulted in a ring pattern around the joints. Figure 13(a)-13(d) are the cross-section
photos of the Ag-Cu welds.
CONCLUSIONS
No eutectic phase is only found in the Sn-Pb and Sn-Zn welds. It is likely that
the Sn-Pb and Sn-Zn work-pieces are too soft to have enough resistant heat for
forming liquid phase. The resultant welds look like that the two base metals were
just squeezed together without being mixed with each other through the pressure of
the tool pin. In Al1100-Mg and Ag-Cu welds, more material mixture is observed.
Further investigations are needed to examine the possible intermetallic compounds
and eutectic phase.

Solidification Simulation Using Scheil Model in Multicomponent Systems
Shuang-Lin Chen1,2, Ying Yang1, Sinn-Wen Chen3, Xiong-Gang Lu2, Y Austin Chang4

Abstract
Solidification paths simulated by Scheil model have been analyzed. Since the Scheil
model assumes that the already solidified solid phases are “frozen”, the simulated 
solidification paths must follow the eutectic type paths and cross over the peritectic
type boundaries to satisfy the mass balance requirement. Examples are given for
comparison between the Scheil simulation results and the experimental observations
for alloys in Sn-Ag-Bi and Mo-Ti-Si systems.
Keywords: solidification simulation, calculation, phase diagram.
1. Introduction
We have learnt that in an isobaric binary system two basic types of reactions exist
during solidification: eutectic and peritectic. Binary eutectic and peritectic reactions
can be easily understood from the binary phase diagrams. However, reactions become
much more complex in multi-component systems and it is not so easy to understand
the reactions in multi-component systems, even in a ternary system. This paper details
geometrically the eutectic and peritectic types of reactions in a ternary system during
solidification simulation with Scheil model and then extends it to multi-component
systems. Examples will be presented to compare the prediction from simulation with
experimental results. We start from binary system so that the similarity among binary,
ternary and higher order systems can been revealed.
The phase formation during solidification is a kinetic process. In this paper, a simple
at any temperature can be easily identified. However, the solidification of a ternary
alloy is more complex and will be discussed in the following.

multi-component phase equilibrium calculation [2002Chen, 2003Chen, 2009Cao].
Pandat also calculates the reaction equations for non-invariant reactions.

xC=0.229, where the reaction is a eutectic type ( L    ). Since the eutectic
reaction satisfies the mass balance condition in the Scheil model, the eutectic

Two examples are given below: the Sn-Ag-Bi and Mo-Si-Ti ternary systems. The
Scheil simulation results will be compared with the experimental observation.
5.1 Sn-Ag-Bi System
Sn-Ag-Bi (SAB) is an important base ternary system for lead-free solders (Sinn-wen:
can you cite one reference here?). Figure 7(a) is the Scheil solidification path of the
SAB alloy superimposed on the liquidus projection of the Sn-Ag-Bi ternary system.
The solidification path is -Agε-Ag3Snε-Ag3Sn+Sneutectic (Sn+Bi+ε-Ag3Sn).
The region of the ternary eutectic (Sn+Bi+ε-Ag3Sn) is enlarged in Fig. 7(b). Figure 8
is a BSE micrograph of the alloy SAB, Sn-60wt%Ag-7.5wt%Bi. The alloy was
melted at 800C, and then cooled down in the air. The primary solidification phase is
the dark -Ag phase. Although it is very difficult to differentiate metallographically,
compositional analysis results clearly indicate the phase on the outer ring of the dark
-Ag phase region is theε-Ag3Sn phase. The bright phase is the Bi phase and the gray
phase is the β-Sn phase. As shown in Figure 8, there are Sn-Bi eutectic dark-white
mixture regions. The gray region with very fine bright phase is theβ-Sn phase with Bi
precipitate distributed into it. The Bi precipitation in the β-Sn phase is a result of
decreasing solubility of Bi with lower temperature. From the solidification path, it is
clear that the liquid phase cuts through the peritectic-type monovariant line between
the -Ag and the ε-Ag3Sn phase, and then it follows the eutectic-type monovariant
line between theε-Ag3Sn phase and the Sn phase.

Figure 9 shows the calculated liquidus projection of the Mo-Si-Ti system. The solid
lines are the monovariant liquidus lines on the liquidus surface and the green and red
lines represent the simulated solidification paths of two alloys using the Scheil model.
Aloy “1” has a composition of Mo-0.1Ti-0.3Si and aloy “2” Mo-0.23Ti-0.32Si (both
in mole fraction). The label “m”denotes a transition point from the eutectic reaction
LMo5Si3+Mo3Si for Mo-rich alloys to the peritectic reaction L+Mo5Si3Mo3Si on
the right hand side. The primary phase of solidification of aloy “1” is Mo5Si3
followed by eutectic solidification of Mo5Si3+Mo3Si in binary Mo-Si. During the
eutectic solidification, the liquid composition stays on the monovariant eutectic valley
of LMo5Si3+Mo3Si until the binary eutectic. On the other hand, aloy “2” starts with 
the primary solidification of Mo5Si3. When the liquid composition reaches the
monovariant liquidus of the peritectic type, the liquid composition does not stay on
the monovariant line and goes into the primary solidification region of Mo3Si instead.
Then, the Mo3Si phase is solidified until the liquid composition reaches another
eutectic valley of L(Mo)+Mo3Si. The subsequent solidification stays on the eutectic
valley of LMo3Si+(Mo) and eventually to the eutectic valley of LTi5Si3+(Mo)
[2003Yang]. The Scheil simulation predicts the mole fraction of the solid phase
resulted from the primary solidification of Mo5Si3 and Mo3Si is more than 95%,
which indicates the final solidified eutectic of Mo3Si+(Mo) and Mo5Si3+(Mo) may
not be easily detected from experimental study of the solidified aloy “2”. Figures 
10(a) and 10(b) show the as-cast microstructures of aloys “1” and “2”, respectively, 
using Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM) in Back Scattered Electron (BSE) Image
mode. As-cast microstructure of aloy “1” is featured bythe Mo5Si3 primary dendrite
and the eutectic of Mo3Si+Mo5Si3 in between the primary dendrites. In aloy “2”, no 
eutectic of Mo3Si+Mo5Si3 was observed. This alloy is featured by the solidification of
Mo5Si3 followed by the solidification of Mo3Si. This example demonstrates that the
Scheil simulation on solidification path is a very useful tool to help understand some
complicated as-cast microstructures.

6. Conclusion
Solidification paths calculations using the Scheil model have been analyzed using a
hypothetical ternary system. It has been shown that the Scheil simulated solidification
paths must follow the eutectic type paths ( L     ) and cross over the peritectic
type boundaries ( L     ). There are two reasons for taking such a special
solidification path: the mass balance requirement and the assumption that the already
solidified solid phases are “frozen” and no difusion in solids is taken into account. 
The calculated paths of solidifications of Sn-Ag-Bi and Mo-Ti-Si alloys agree with
those obtained experimentally. The present method of calculation can be utilized to
calculate the paths of higher order alloys provided that thermodynamic databases are
available.
