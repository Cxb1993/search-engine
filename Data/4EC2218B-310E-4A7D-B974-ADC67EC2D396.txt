前言 
在眾多的能源儲存技術包括氫氣、蓄電池及壓縮氣體中，氫氣普遍被認為是
最佳能源載體（energy carrier），因為當再生能源暫時短缺時，氫氣可以替代燃油
的功能，可運送與發電功能。氫氣可利用管路傳送或在地生產，作為燃料電池的
燃料來驅動車輛（能源效率可達 42 km/l）或作為其他用途的電源（如推動火車
或船舶的推進馬達），化學能轉化為電能的效率可高達 83%，而且幾乎是零排放
－僅純水而已。氫氣作為燃料電池車或動力機械的燃料指日可待，就等價格合理
的燃料電池來臨的一天[2]。 
在眾多產氫技術中，如：(1)熱分解產氫（thermolysis）；(2)利用太陽能將生
質產氫；(3)風能和太陽能電池結合電解水（electrolysis）技術產氫，係結合永續
的風能和太陽能所發的電與高效率（目前約 60-73%、最高可達 80%）的電解水
技術，是未來最具前景的氫氣量產技術，其理想效率可達 24%（以太陽能電池的
30%及電解水的 80%效率來估算）[2]；(4)太陽能水解產氫（photolysis）係將發
電與電分解二個過程結合為一，可直接應用太陽能分解排放污水或海水產氫來發
電、或直接儲存氫能源，具有永續性與低成本的潛力，除傳統氫能的無污染與無
溫室氣體排放外，尚包括較高的太陽能/氫轉換率的潛能、產製與應用相對安全、
產氫製程安全無污染、純水依賴性低、甚至可能直接水解海水或廢水等特色。利
用光觸媒水解產氫的製程又稱綠色製程，即是著名的「Honda-Fujishima effect」
[1]，係利用二氧化鈦光觸媒的氧化及或還原能力來進行化學合成反應：一為利
用光觸媒的氧化能力，與水反應後產生氧氣，另一種則是利用光觸媒的還原能
力，將水的H＋還原成氫氣。依此原理觸媒水解產生氧氣和氫氣，氫氣可用來做
燃料電池的潔淨能源。 
雖然二氧化鈦光觸媒有水質淨化、自淨、殺菌環保領域及水解產氫等新興能
源領域之應用，但不可諱言，二氧化鈦的工業應用的普及性目前受限於二個主要
因素：(1)電子-電洞的再結合、(2)可見光（太陽光）的利用率太低。目前的研究
重點著重於：光生載子（photo-generated carriers）遷移速率的提升，如製備新結
構的TiO2奈米線（nanowires）、奈米柱（nanorods）、奈米條或環（nanobelts or 
陽能或室內燈源的環境淨化的新應用，同樣是非常重要的研究領域。在MAO製
備的多微米孔TiO2/Ti厚膜的正面上製備Pt或C奈米粒，構成高比表面積的柱狀-
微米孔的Pt-TiO2/Ti複合薄膜觸媒，因載子能有效率地經由奈米孔隙網格傳遞、
減緩電子－電洞再結合速率；而高比表面積能吸收更多的光子能量，增加觸媒效
能，可以結合太陽光或室內燈源的降解污染物的應用；或製成類似電解產氯之形
狀安定電極（dimensionally stable anode, DSA），此濺鍍白金的Pt-TiO2/Ti與石墨的
C-TiO2/Ti的產氣電極，無論在船體減阻與防污或在無電解質添加與無白金陰極的
光電氧化（PEC）反應，皆有實用價值。 
二、文獻探討 
最近幾年有關TiO2薄膜的製備，最特殊的應是C.A. Grimes團隊開發出超高的
面積體積比的TiO2奈米管，無論是用於氫的感應元件、產氫電極、觸媒薄膜以及
生醫材料等領域的應用，皆有重大的進展[12]。由於TiO2奈米管與鈦底材的結合
不良問題，導致電子傳遞至外部（白金或電極）的路徑阻力增加，因製程的複雜
性引起的結構與電路問題。因此，結構較堅強、但觸媒效率可以比擬TiO2奈米管
的奈米孔薄膜，逐漸受到重視。例如今年（2008）以來，A. Fujishima [13]與中
國大陸中科院的研究團隊合作已發表階層微米 -奈米孔（ hierarchical 
micro-/nano-porous）TiO2，分別降解氣體acetaldehyde及液體phenol，獲得顯著的
提升（效率分別提升約40 %及70 %）[19]。因為階層微米-奈米孔薄膜可以增加
觸媒的光吸收效率、提升了光子進入TiO2內部的機會，證明除電子-電洞分離效
果外，比表面積對光觸媒效率的提升是有很大的助益。其他研究團隊亦陸續開發
出微米-奈米孔薄膜，如規則排列奈米孔（highly ordered nanopore arrays）薄膜

甲基橙（methyl orange, MO）效率高約7 % [16]。Y. Liu等學者的測試結果證明，

ABSTRACT 
 
To enhance the visible-light photoelectrochemical (PEC) 
response, the heterostructured TiO2/ITO electrodes were 
codoped with nitrogen (N) and carbon (C) ions grown on 
ITO glass substrate as a layered N,C-(TiO2/ITO)ITO 
(Ti/TO) electrode. Under AM 1.0 simulated solar light 
irradiation and 1.5 V applied bias, the layered Ti/TO 
electrode yields simultaneously a hydrogen production rate 
of 12.0 μmol/cm2 h and pollutant degradation rate of 0.0126 
1/cm2 h, whereas the N,C-ITO electrode shows little 
activity of pollutant oxidation. One can concludes that the 
overlaid N,C-TiO2 layer enhances not only the photocurrent 
response of the layered Ti/TO electrode at entire applied 
potentials, but also the flat band potential; a shift of about 
0.1 V toward the cathode, which is desperately beneficial in 
PEC process. The PEC activities are attributable in part to 
the columnar film surface of the layered Ti/TO electrodes. 
 
Keywords: solar hydrogen, TiO2, ITO, PEC degradation 
 
1 INTRODUCTION 
 
The photoelectrochemical (PEC) splitting of water using 
solar energy has attracted substantial attention as a means 
of producing hydrogen as a clean and renewable resource 
[1]. Researchers have sought suitable photocatalysts for 
splitting water since a pioneering work of Honda and 
Fujishima by employing TiO2 semiconductor as 
photoanode in 1972 [2]. As an anatase TiO2 photoanode is 
excited by incident light with wavelengths shorter than 387 
nm, electrons and holes can be generated. In short, the 
photogenerated holes oxidize and decompose water, even 
some organic or inorganic substances in aqueous solution, 
at the photoanode while the electrons can interact with 
hydrogen ions into hydrogen at the counter platinum (Pt) 
electrode. It has been shown that hydrogen production can 
be enhanced by irradiating Pt/TiO2 suspensions with 
simultaneous degradation of azo-dyes [3]. Moreover, 
photoelectrolytic cleavage of biomass wastes, such as urine, 
ethanol and glycerol, in water is much more efficient for 
electricity generation [4] and hydrogen production [5]. 
For efficient PEC splitting water, the n-type photoanode 
is required to have narrow band gap around 2.0 eV, suitable 
negative flat band potential, good stability and high 
quantum efficiency [1]. Among various semiconductor 
photocatalysts, TiO2 is one of the most popular catalysts 
because it is environmentally friendly and chemically stable 
in electrolyte solution with high quantum efficiency. 
However, the use of TiO2 is limited by its wide band gap 
(~3.2 eV). Alternatively, semiconductors with smaller band 
gaps, such as CdS (2.4 eV), Fe2O3 (2.3 eV) and Cu2O (2.2 
eV), commonly suffer from photocorrosion in electrolyte 
solution and fast recombination of the photogenerated 
carriers [1]. It is well-known that metal or semiconductor 
with a high work function coupled with other 
semiconductor can significantly enhance the oxidation 
ability of photogenerated holes in the semiconductor, due to 
efficient carriers separation. A heterostructured film, such 
as TiO2/ITO, TiO2/WO3, CdS/TiO2 or TiO2/SnO2, have 
been proposed for providing a potential driving force for 
photogenerated charge carriers separation [6-9]. Dai et al. 
have demonstrated that the superior photocatalytic (PC) 
reactions of the TiO2/ITO film are mostly associated with 
the photogenerated holes, because ITO has a higher work 
function (~4.7 eV) than TiO2 and a Schottky barrier can 
form at the TiO2/ITO interface, where the ITO thin film 
accepts electrons [7]. One should note that in 
heterostructured film not only the proximity of their 
conduction band to conduction band [10], but also the 
proper redox positions of heterostructure plays significant 
roles on charge carriers transfer and thus photocatalysis. 
Recently, both N-doped In2O3 and ITO (N-ITO) and C-
doped In2O3 electrodes were demonstrated to be promising 
photocatalysts with favorable PEC properties, especially 
under visible light (λ>378 nm) [11-13]. However, both ITO 
and N-ITO films exhibit no PC activity in degradation of 
methylene blue solution [12], though some PC degradation 
of azo dyes has been reported [14]. In our previous work, 
we found that an applied bias could serve as a highly 
efficient way to suppress hole-electron recombination of the 
N-doped ITO electrode, where the generated photocurrent 
density sharply increased with its applied bias potential. 
This has been ascribed mostly to better electrical 
conductivity and proper positions of the flat band potentials 
[11,15] as a result of suppression of InN and SnO2 phases in 
the domain-structured N-ITO electrode which is  prepared 
at a low N-doping content [13]. 
Titania codoping with anionic species, such as C and N, 
appears to have very promising visible-light PC properties

3.2 Photoelectrochemical Properties 
 
Fig. 4 shows a set of current-voltage (IV) characteristics 
recorded on three samples and e-ITO electrode in dark and 
under illumination of simulated solar light. Under 
illumination, the photocurrent increases sharply as the 
applied potential reaches 0.5 V and 0.3 V vs. SCE on N,C-
ITO and Ti/TO electrodes, respectively. There is no 
saturation of photocurrent observed in both two samples, 
which indicates efficient charge separation under 
illumination, while the saturation of photocurrent can be 
seen on N,C-TiO2 sample. Under illumination, however, the 
e-ITO electrode exhibits no gain on photocurrent with a 
breakdown point at about 0.9 V vs. SCE. This indicates that 
the N and C dopants can enhance the PEC properties of 
pristine ITO film. That is a shift of the flat band potential 
from -0.70 to -0.82 V vs. SCE is achieved on N,C-ITO 
sample, which was estimated from the I-V measurements in 
Fig. 4 [25]. The relatively high photoactive response of the 
Ti/TO electrode is attributable to, at least in part, the 
synergetic effect of N,C-codoping on band gap narrowing 
and photosensitizing [16,21]. Moreover, the overlaid N,C-
TiO2 layer (300 nm thick) enhances not only the 
photocurrent response of the layered electrode at the entire 
applied potentials, but also the flat band potential from -
0.82 to -0.91 V vs. SCE; a shift of negative flat band 
potential is desired for facilitating the PEC process [1,25]. 
Under irradiation of AM 1.0 simulated solar light and 
1.5 V applied bias, the layered Ti/TO film electrode has the 
highest photocurrent density of 0.42 mA/cm2 and hydrogen 
yield rate of 28.8 μmol/cm2 h in 4.0 N Na2CO3 solution 
(pH~11). Comparatively, the N,C-ITO electrode has a 
photocurrent density of 0.35 mA/cm2 and a hydrogen yield 
rate of 24.6 μmol/cm2 h, respectively. However, the N,C-
TiO2 electrode exhibits a photocurrent density of 0.09 
mA/cm2 and a relatively high hydrogen yield rate of 25.0 
μmol/cm2 h, respectively.

(英文) This invention is related to a device that features a reduction in frictional resistance and 
antifouling hull surface of seagoing ships which are activated electrochemically by 
microporous hull surfaces. The device includes of a series of anodic microporous plates 
and cathodic microporous plates on the hull surfaces of which are electrically connected 
to a direct current (DC) power supply. The DC power supply provides an adequate DC 
bias to the hull surfaces where hydrogen and oxygen microbubbles are electrochemically 
nucleated herein. As a result, the microbubble-induced superhydrophobic surfaces are 
formed and the reduction in frictional resistance and antifouling hull surface are obtained.

alloys via a micro-arc oxidation or anodic oxidation technique. For an aluminum hull ship 
the same scheme is also effectively applied as the method is claimed above.
