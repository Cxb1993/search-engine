一、中文摘要 
在紡織研究領域，人們越來越關心有關觸覺，視覺，嗅覺和聽覺的感官評價。以往
文獻討論了客觀的測量，如織物懸垂性，觸感，光澤和氣味，而織物的聲音也影響了感
官舒適的服裝，但目前較少出現在文獻中。實驗使用織物摩擦聲音品質產生器進行織物
摩擦，經由頻譜分析儀收集並記錄織物摩擦聲響之時域信號，並應用快速傅立葉分析轉
換織物摩擦聲響為頻域信號，藉此可得知織物摩擦時的最大分貝值。使用 22 種市售之
蠶絲織物進行摩擦聲響實驗，其中包含 17 塊平紋及 5 塊斜紋。由頻譜圖發現，本實驗
之蠶絲織物最大聲響頻率範圍藉由 2015Hz 至 4825Hz，摩擦聲響範圍藉由 41dB 至
68dB。而織物的摩擦聲響會因織物本身之力學性質及基本組織有所差異。平紋織物與
斜紋織物相比之下，平紋織物之摩擦聲響皆大於斜紋織物。從力學性質方面則發現，表
面粗糙度(SMD)剪切剛度(G)及厚度(T)皆為影響織物摩擦聲響之重要因素。表面粗糙度
越大則摩擦聲響越大，剪切剛度越大則摩擦聲響越大，而厚度則為相反，厚度越厚摩擦
分貝則較小。 
關鍵字：織物摩擦聲音品質產生器、聲音頻譜分析、快速傅立葉分析、力學參數、頻域
訊號 
 
Abstract  
   In the textile research field, there is growing interest in sensory evaluations related to 
friction, vision, olfaction, and audition. Fabric objective measurements such as drape, tactility, 
luster, and odor have been discussed in previous papers, but fabric sound, which also affects 
the sensorial comfort of clothing, does not currently appear in the literature. With the use of 
fabric friction sound collection equipment in experiment, their rustling time-domain signals 
of friction sounds are recorded from sound spectrum analysis. Their frequency-domain signal 
obtained from fast Fourier transform (FFT) spectra can indicate the maximum dB value by 
using the frequency-domain signal. Twenty-two commercially available silk fabrics were

3-5 頻譜圖處理程序 
(1) 將織材裁成邊常 20 cm 的正方形織物，使用織物摩擦品質產生器進行摩擦聲響之時
驗，將摩擦之聲響經由麥克風接收傳至頻譜分析儀處理訊號，再經由訊號處理軟體
匯出成 CSV 檔，接著使用 Microsoft Office Excel®軟體將 CSV 檔開啟並轉成數據
及圖檔成為原始圖樣。 
(2) 使用 Origin®軟體將原始圖樣進行分析及處理，並將原始圖樣轉換為以時間及帕斯
卡所呈現之時域訊號。 
(3) 再經由 Origin®軟體將時域訊號經由快速傅立葉轉換(Fast Fourier Transform FFT)。
轉換為以分貝及頻率為座標之頻域訊號。 
(4) 最後再將頻域訊號進行平緩化及其它訊號分析處理，將頻域訊號轉換為最終之平緩
化之圖型。如圖 3 所示。 
(5) 藉由以上之步驟，可將織物摩擦之聲響做進一步之轉換及分析。經由時域訊號可看
出摩擦時之時間及該時間內之 pa 值，而轉換成為頻域訊號將可以看出在不同頻率
之下，該織物之摩擦聲響之分貝值，以及可看出該織物之摩擦聲色。最後將頻域訊
號做平緩化之處理，可以更清楚看出其分貝高低差以及各織物之聲譜圖差異。

五、結論 
由實驗可以發現，藉由織物摩擦品質產生器可以量測出織物摩擦時之摩擦聲響值，
並可使用頻譜分析儀紀錄其摩擦聲響。使用快速傅立葉轉換可將織物摩擦聲響之時域訊
號轉換為頻域訊號，以利觀察其不同頻率時振幅之變化及各織物最大頻率及分貝。 
經研究發現，蠶絲織物之摩擦聲響之最大頻率約 2015Hz 至 4825Hz，而在 4800Hz
至 7000Hz 開始會呈現出一平緩區域，由圖 4、6、8 可以發現，厚度在 0.2 以下時，平
緩區約在 7000Hz 開始，厚度在 0.2 至 0.25 時，平緩區約在 5500Hz 開始而厚度在 0.25
以上時，平緩區約在 4800Hz 開始。由此可得知厚度越厚則越早進入平緩區域。 
由分類之頻譜圖可以發現，織物之厚度會影響織物摩擦聲響之大小。其原因為，在
相同面積之下厚度越厚則織物摩擦時所造生之摩擦聲響會被織物本身吸收，以至於摩擦
聲響降低。不同織物組織，如平紋斜紋對聲響也具有影響，由此實驗可看出平紋組織聲
響皆大於斜紋組織，斜紋組織雖厚度不同但卻擁有相近之聲響，其原因為不同織物組織
擁有不同基本聲響。由以上實驗結論，織物組織及厚度皆會影響織物摩擦之聲響，織物
組織為主要之影響，而厚度為次要之影響。

本體朝向夾合本體之凹槽另一側貼近，即可完成夾合固定。

fiber-reinforced composites. Some researchers thus proposed valuable investigations relative to 
buckling. Jang et al. [11] investigated the influence of different processing methods on the buckling of 
toughened epoxy prepregs with carbon fiber reinforcement. Hashemi and Koohgilani [12] studied the 
fracture behavior of polypropylene reinforced with short glass fibers. In their study, buckling was 
assessed in the directions parallel and perpendicular to the mold fill direction. The results indicated 
that buckling in the transverse direction was higher than in the melt flow direction. Czigany [13] 
and Denault [14]also did a similar investigation on the buckling of fiber-reinforced composites. 
The observed anisotropy buckling was explained in terms of fiber orientation and distribution. The 
studies of fiber-reinforced PBT have yet been undertaken. A recent study, reported the effect of 
injection molding process parameters on the mechanical properties of fiber-reinforced PBT, was 
carried out by the author and coworkers [15-16] of this study. However, the integrated influence of 
injection molding process conditions, specimen morphology, and fiber amount on the buckling is still 
not very clear for PBT material. 
Method 
Materials. The materials used in experimental work are neat PBT, 15 wt%, and 30 wt% short glass 
fiber-reinforce PBT resin. These materials’ commercial brands are Valox 310SE0, DR51, and 
420SE0, respectively, which are products of General Electric Inc. Their material densities are 1.39, 
1.41 and 1.63 g/cm3, respectively. The average fiber diameter is 12 µm. The fiber length is small than 
0.5 mm. The material general specifications are listed in Table 1. Before the injection molding 
process, the plastic particles were heated to 120°C for 4 hours to remove moisture in materials. 
Specimen preparation. The buckling specimens were prepared by setting three injection molding 
process conditions which following the C-Mold simulation of mold design and filling process. Three 
design parameters are filling time (1 sec, 2 sec, 3 sec), melt temperature (240°C, 260°C, 280°C) and 
mold temperature (65°C, 80°C, 95°C). The packing pressure is controlled constant pressure of 42 
MPa. The detail injection molding conditions are listed in Table 2. 
The specimens were molded in a computerized reciprocating screw injection molding machine, 
with a maximum injection pressure of 60 MPa. The shape and size of the ping gate are also simulated 
by C-MOLD for an effective filling process. The pin gate is determined to be a rectangular inlet of 2 
mm x 1 mm. The configuration of cavities and runner is shown in Fig. 1. 
Experiment procedure. The process followed the modified ASTM D695-02a specification. The 
test was performed using a 100 kN computerized MTS (Model 810) closed-loop servohydraulic 
system at 0.5 mm/s in room temperature.  The specimens’ two ends were fixed 10 mm length with 
fixture and had 105 mm free length for buckling test. The specimens were monotonically loaded in 
compression until fracture. The software controlled procedure and continuously recorded the load and 
compliance displacement. The compression stiffness is obtained from the linear portion of the 
load-displacement curve. 
After test procedure, the microstructure and fracture surface were investigated with a SEM. All 
specimen fracture surfaces were gold sputtered before observation.

Conclusions 
A study was made on the buckling of fiber-reinforced PBT composites for various injection 
molding process parameters and fiber amounts. The experimental observations are summarized as 
follows. 
1. The addition of short glass fibers can significantly strengthen its capability of fracture crack 
resistance and thus increase the buckling load of the neat PBT. The difference caused by the addition 
of fibers is obvious. The buckling load depends on the amount of fibers as the amount of fibers 
dominates the capability of fracture crack resistance.  
3. The buckling compress displacement of neat PBT is larger than that of fiber-reinforced PBT, 
demonstrating that the neat PBT is the softest than the addition of fibers PBT. The maximum buckling 
load is obtained at 30wt-% fiber-reinforced PBT. The T9 condition is the best condition in PBT 
injection mold condition. 
4. The fiber pull-out dominating the failure can be seen across the fracture surface of 15%-wt 
fiber-reinforced PBT specimens. The 15%-wt fiber-reinforced PBT specimens fracture mechanisms 
are fiber pull-out and fiber break.
