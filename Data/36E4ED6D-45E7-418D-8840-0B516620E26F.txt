壹、背景及目的 
 
一、智慧化居住空間之發展 
 
「智慧型建築（IB, Intelligent Building）」，係配合建築空間與機能，從人員、環境、
設備角度整合，將建築物內之防災、電氣、給排水、空調、通訊及輸送等設備系統與空
間使用之運轉、維護管理予以自動化，使建築物功能與品質提昇，以達到建築之安全、
健康、節能、便利與舒適等目的。功能上強調建築設備與其他相關智慧型工程技術的結
合，包括建築自動化系統與技術、建築使用空間、運轉管理制度，以及如何導入高性能
建築控制機能，使空調、照明、防災等設備達到安全、省能及環保效果，且能維持良好
之室內環境（溫琇玲，1999）。「行政院 2005 年產業科技策略會議(SRB 會議)」海外顧
問 Clements-Croome 教授對於智慧型建築，則是賦予更深層的定義，他認為：「Intelligent 
Building 並非全然是高科技建築，它應該是可以隨著使用者的需求而改變的「智慧化」
建築，它必須是高度永續性的，必須可以提供居室者互動性空間性能，以提升生活效能
的」。因此，如何把建築由「自動化」提升至「智慧化」成為探討下世代居住規格時，應
予以高度聚焦的課題。 
 
「智慧空間（Smart Space）」則是智慧型建築的組成基本單元，如同細胞之於器官，
是相當重要的操作元素。（Okoshi et al, 2001）對於 Smart Space 提出他的概念，如圖 1
所示：人類對於隨身型電子產品擁有高度的智慧控制能力，而所屬空間卻無法相對支援；
未來，此窘境將會隨著『智慧型空間』的產生而有所改變，居室人員對於空間性能、環
控設備、家電能夠擁有更多的智慧控制以及行動計算能力。目前，在智慧空間的組成上，
多在探討：(1)裝置異質性(Device Heterogeneity)、(2)裝置的互相察覺(Device Awareness)、
(3)隨到隨用(Come and Use)、(4)裝置資源侷限(Limited Resource)、(5)服務管理整合
(Integrated Multiple Service Management)、(6)分享式居家服務(Home Service Sharing)、(7)
安全(Security)、(8)服務品質(QoS, Quality of Service)、(9)使用者付費問題(Pay per use)、
(10)使用者認證(User Authentication)、(11)喜好(Preference)等問題。希望藉由「人-網路-
設備」的整合，提升居家設備之智慧化。

二、自然智慧與室內環境控制 
 
人工智慧（Artificial Intelligence, AI）與人體腦部理解力的運作，存在有高度的類比
性質。然而，動物整體的反應與認知行為的研究結果告訴我們，建構建築智慧（Building 
Intelligence）理論基礎的方式，以效法動物反應與認知行為模式應為較可行的，吾人得
以在生物族群中精選出某些值得注視的生物行為，而這些被稱為「自然智慧（Natural 
Intelligence）」的動物反應與認知行為與前述建築智慧有著極高的類比性，例如：人體皮
膚對於外界溫度的調控機制、哺乳類動物的季節性毛皮變化、花朵對應日照的閉合動作
等（Wigginton and Harris，2002）。如同人的五官對應於自然環境的五感，如何應用自然
智慧，設計出具備「順應自然」、「自主調控」的建築外皮與室內建材，創造更舒適、健
康的室內環境，已成為下一階段建築環控領域的突破點。 
 
三、建築物開口部與環境控制 
 
根據過去研究顯示，以辦公大樓為例，建築物耗電量以空調耗電量所需最多，約佔
48%，其次為照明耗電量，佔 43.7%，兩者合計佔總建築耗電量之 90%若能利用建築物
外殼開口部引進適當的自然光源，或者結合遮陽設施以阻擋太陽輻射熱侵入，將可減少
建築物室內空調與照明的負荷，大幅減輕建築物耗電量的使用，而達到節約能源之目的。
以建築物室內耗電量而言，照明用電將是耗電的來源之一，在所有的照明省能手法上，
若利用建築物自然採光，可節省建築物平均耗能的 25%左右，亦可降低尖峰用電時刻的
建築耗能 30%，自然採光的運用不但可以降低照明用電量，也可以降低因照明器具所產
生之熱負荷而造成空調用電，且外部遮陽對空調的節能效果佔了 40%左右(游義琦，
1990)，由此可知，在照明及空調的省能效益上，開口部的外遮陽設施及晝光之運用對建

五、本計畫之研究主題 
 
對於各式遮陽設施對應之適用方位及相關影響因子之研究成果已相當充分，但都以
固定式之遮陽設施為主要研究。隨著每日之太陽高度角、方位角及季節不同之影響，此
類的遮陽設施將無法完整兼顧室內採光及溫熱環境之需求；研究顯示，過大的開口部會
帶來大量的陽光，使室內過分炎熱；欲減少陽光進入室內而採用外遮陽設備，又將阻礙
採光、通風和視野，因此，對於室內熱環境的要求和自然採光、通風、視野上的需求，
實是相互矛盾的。 
 
本研究採用市面販售水平式活動百葉之鋁百葉窗，研發具備自然智慧之被動式建築
環控系統，以時序概念調整百葉角度對應不同時段的太陽高度角；百葉角度秉持阻擋太
陽直射光線並且擁有最大可視範圍之晝光的控制模式稱為「時序控制百葉窗」；百葉維持
水平狀態稱為「水平固定百葉窗」，進行相互比較，探討時序控制下的百葉窗對於室內光
與溫熱環境之影響及節能效益。並利用縮小模型實驗、原型開發、足型實驗現地檢測等
方式，觀測此創新系統之建築環控性能， 「與人工智慧型環控設備搭配之整體綜效」、「時
序概念」、「節能效益」等層面相互印證，以修正精進本研發系統。

3.1.2 智慧型百葉窗之控制機制 
足尺實驗時序控制型百葉窗之控制機制則與上一階段實驗相同，為了使遮陽設施能夠隨
著太陽運行軌跡的變動而有相關之對應，使遮陽設施能有效的遮住所有直射光線，因此採用
市面販售之水平式活動百葉窗(以下簡稱百葉窗)，加入時序控制概念，也就是依據一日中時
間的變動也是太陽高度角的變化來調整百葉角度，以達成此遮陽設施對於室內環境能有效「屏
除長波日照所產生之熱得，並可引進最大可視光頻帶之晝光」之功效。 
此外因應本階段實驗設計，依據太陽一日之運行軌跡在高度角及方位角之變化下，經由
太陽日照對室內光環境及溫度環境有相當程度之影響，故以太陽不同方位角之特性，在不同
的座向，對應的百葉控制方式也不同，經由上述得知太陽運行軌跡對建築物立面的現象，依
據此現象設計百葉調整角度對應不同座向時之時序控制的方式。

3.1.4 實驗步驟流程 
實驗可分為實驗組與對照組，除了室內以Hobo記錄室內溫度及照度變化，每分鐘一筆，
室外採用Testo、照度計、光照計分別量測室外溫度、照度、日射量及風速之數據，每小時記
錄一筆外界環境影響相關因子的變化。對照組為關燈＋關空調狀態下主要探討在自然光環境
下對室內光與溫熱環境的影響。實驗組為開燈＋開空調狀態下，探討晝光+照明及空調作用下
對室內光與溫熱環境的影響及節能效益，除記錄外界環境影響因子外，須加計每小時室內關
燈狀態下之照度，在依據作業面的照度標準500LUX，進行燈具開燈控制，以開燈最少之狀態

PERFORMANCE ASSESSMENT OF THE ROOFTOP TURBINE VENTILATOR 
POWERED BY HYBRID WIND AND PHOTOVOLTAIC ENERGY 
Tzyy-hwang Shieh1, Che-ming Chiang1 and Chi-ming Lai2 
1 Department of Architecture, National Cheng-Kung University, Taiwan 
2 Department of Civil Engineering, National Cheng-Kung University, Taiwan 
A conventional rooftop turbine ventilator is a helpful part of a building’s ventilation system.  The innovative Hybrid 
Ventilator, powered by hybrid wind and photovoltaic, can enhance the ventilation performance of a conventional 
ventilator.  In this study, the performance differences between Hybrid Ventilators and conventional ventilators will be 
assessed through a series of experiments.  Then, CFD (Computational Fluid Dynamics) simulations shall be applied to 
survey the indoor ventilation efficiency of the Hybrid Ventilators and conventional ventilators.  The results show that a 
Hybrid Ventilator provides approximately 4-times the exhaust capacity of a conventional ventilator.  As for the 
installation of the Hybrid Ventilator, the central position on the slanted rooftop is highly recommended. 
Keywords: ventilator, ventilation, wind, photovoltaic, CFD (Computational Fluid Dynamics) 
 
INTRODUCTION 
Turbine ventilators are widely installed in Taiwan to 
enhance building ventilation (especially with spaces like 
bathrooms and kitchens where negative pressure is 
highly demanded) and the ventilation of factories.  
Theoretically, the proper combination of turbine 
ventilator and natural ventilation will help tremendously 
the indoor air quality while it will also decrease the 
reliance on air-conditioning and thus reduce the energy 
consummation. 
Previous works [1] [2] examined the potential of 
installing a new common roof turbine ventilator onto an 
existing bathroom ventilation system serving fourteen 
bathrooms, and assessed its overall ventilation 
performance.  The experimental results demonstrate 
that the combination of the roof turbine ventilator and 
bathroom ventilation successfully achieved adequate 
air change rate in bathrooms.  Therefore, this 
ventilation design alternative is a promising means of 
improving the indoor air environment in bathrooms.  
The design concept in a following study [3] was aimed 
at increasing the operation and energy efficiency of the 
rooftop turbine ventilator and then developing a 
prototype of the rooftop turbine ventilator powered by 
hybrid renewable energy (wind and PV energy).  A 
low-speed wind tunnel experiment was performed to 
investigate this innovative prototype’s ventilation 
performance.  The experimental results indicated that 
installing an inner fan at low outdoor wind speed (0m/s, 
5m/s) increases the ventilation rate.  The ventilation 
rate was not improved by installing an inner fan at a 
high outdoor wind speed.  A rated rotation speed close 
to 1500 rpm was highly recommended when installing 
the inner fan. 
Obviously, a conventional rooftop ventilator is a 
helpful part of a building’s ventilation system.  The 
rooftop turbine ventilator powered by hybrid wind and 
photovoltaic energy, or simply “Hybrid Ventilator” for 
short, can enhance the ventilation performance of a 
conventional ventilator.  But what are the differences 
between conventional ventilators and the Hybrid 
Ventilator faced with the weather conditions in Taiwan?  
So far, we have not found any related research being 
conducted.  Therefore, in this study, the performance 
differences between Hybrid Ventilators and 
conventional ventilators will be assessed through a 
series of experiments. 
 
RESEARCH METHOD 
Experimental Method  
 
Previous investigations [1] [2] confirmed that the 
installation of roof turbine ventilators successfully 
achieved the required ventilation level.  Considering 
this finding, this study aimed to enhance the ventilation 
efficiency using renewable energy, without consuming 
additional electricity.  Therefore, without altering the 
ventilator’s main structure of the ventilator, a 
16-inch-diameter inner fan was installed in the neck of a 
20-inch-diameter ventilator, replacing the existing inner 
vane (the previous works has showed that a turbine 
ventilator exhausts better with inner vanes than without 
them; however, the difference between the two is not 
significant).  This DC inner fan’s electricity demand 
was met by PV panels.  Fig. 1 illustrates this rooftop 
turbine ventilator powered by hybrid wind and PV.

CFD simulations 
 
Fig. 4 shows that the flow patterns at X=3.75m (Fig. 
4(a)), X=5m (Fig. 4(b)) and interior height of 1.5m (Fig. 
4(c)) of Mode 5, with the Hybrid Ventilator installed at

Fig. 4 Flow patterns of the interior of Model 5 at 
X=3.75m, X=5m, Z=1.5m respectively 
 
In Fig. 4(a) we can see that the outdoor air was 
flowing at a velocity of about 0.86m/s into the factory 
through the lateral openings, A1, at the bottom of both 
walls, and met at the middle of the bottom, A2, to then 
form an upward airflow.  Besides the outdoor airflow 
that pushes this flow up, the Hybrid Ventilator also 
induces the air to move upward.  Thus, as seen in the 
diagram, the air flows to the left.  The airflow reaching 
A3 was directed along the slanted ceiling and drawn by 
the airflow from the bottom A1, and lastly created a 
clockwise air circulation, B1, and a counterclockwise air 
circulation, B2, on both sides of the interior. 
Fig. 4(b) shows a flow pattern similar to Fig. 4(a), 
except that the higher air velocity, induced directly by 
the Hybrid Ventilator, can be found near the ridge (A3) 
in Fig. 4(a).  In Fig. 4(c), similar to Mode 1, an upward 
airflow is generated in the middle of the interior space, 
A4.  The air circulation flows at a higher velocity than 
that on the two sides.  As for the both sides, marked 
as A5, the air flows feebly, at a velocity less than 
0.3m/s. 
As a result of the comparison, we can see that as a 
machine is installed in Models 1, 2 and 3, the air 
velocities and paths of air flow for the three Modes do 
not vary much.  However, when the ventilators are 
installed on the center of the slanted rooftop, as in 
Model 2, the average indoor air velocity is a little higher 
than the other two modes.  It appears to be the same 
with the Hybrid Ventilators in Models 4 and 5, where a 
better indoor ventilation is created by placing the Hybrid 
Ventilator at the center position on the slanted rooftop 
(as in Model 5). 
 
CONCLUSION 
When the outdoor air velocity ranges between 
0.3m/s and 1.2m/s, the ventilation rate of a 
conventional ventilator is about 86~259 CMH, while 
that of a Hybrid Ventilator is about 2159~2633 CMH.  
As the outdoor air velocity reaches 3.1~5.3m/s, a 
conventional ventilator can generate a ventilation rate 
of 130~518 CMH, and a Hybrid Ventilator 2029~2849 
CMH.  Consider the ventilation “quantity” (rate), a 
Hybrid Ventilator provides approximately 4-times the 
exhaust capacity of a conventional ventilator.
