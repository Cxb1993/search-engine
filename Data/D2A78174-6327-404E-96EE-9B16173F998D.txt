乃是異質原子對既有晶格原子的取代與置換，藉由不同價電數的匹配，形成 P型或 N型摻雜，
但是有機半導體中組成晶格的基準乃是分子，因此摻雜對於有機半導體之意義完全不同。舉
例來說，最簡單的對有機半導體的摻雜約莫可分成兩大類，一個是元素體的摻雜，另一類則
是分子體的摻雜，以五環素(pentacene)為例，元素體的摻雜可為碘(I)、溴(Br)、鐵(Fe)、鈉(Na)、
鈣(Ca)、或氧(O)等等，其中部分摻雜可形成 P型五環素，其他則可形成 N型五環素，但是形
成的機制與無機摻雜不同，主要乃是利用不同元素的電子親和力，造成容易吸引電子的類受
體(acceptor-like)能態與容易釋放電子的類施體(donor-like)能態，如圖一。但由於其與原受摻
雜之分子並未形成取代架構，僅依靠摻雜後形成的類似缺陷能帶結構，吸引或釋放載子，因
此其數量與傳輸機制皆須更進一步的驗證。至於另一種摻雜行為更為複雜，乃是兩種分子間
的摻雜，例如在五環素中加入不同有機分子，比如十六氟銅苯二甲藍(F16CuPc)，利用 pentacene
較易傳導電動的特性與 F16CuPc易傳導電子的特性，製造出具雙載子傳導性的主動層，如圖
二，Oteyza等使用MBE分別同時蒸鍍 Pentacene與 F16CuPc其結構示意圖如圖，但以此方式
製作出之有機薄膜，兩種分子會互相干擾與影響，對於電子電洞的導通沒有幫助，反而製造
更多的能障。因此對於分子類的摻雜，需要更謹慎與小心的控制其排列組合，方能達到混摻
的目的。

載子傳導度，降低阻抗 [1] 。

Abstract 
A threshold voltage tunable pentacene based thin film transistor was reported through iodine 
doping with a wide modulation range over tens of voltages. Thus, the TFT could be modulated from 
enhancement type to deep depletion type. The iodine doping could be controlled by a simple way 
with varying post heating temperature and maintaining time. A light iodine doping would slightly 
enhanced the pentacene TFT mobility from 0.11 cm2/vs to 0.18 cm2/vs, but a heavy doping would 
degrade its mobility about 4-10 times lower than the undoped sample. All the device Ion/Ioff current 
ratio was over 105. 
  
Pentacene based transistors have drawn many interests due to its low cost, high performance 
and relative stable properties in recent years. However, its high driving voltage, poor air stability, 
and unintentional interface issues need be further solved for commercial application. In order to 
apply to electronic circuits, we need to precisely control the device properties, such as field effect 
mobility, sub-threshold slope and threshold voltage. Here, the threshold voltage is a key factor since 
it governs the operation voltage and transistor type. The threshold voltage has been modulated 
through multiple techniques, for instant, different device configurations [1], control of interface 
dipole [2], space charges storage [3] and etc... Nevertheless, the skills mentioned above still meet 
the issue that the threshold voltage was fixed after the device has been fabricated, and rarely could 
be tuned after the finish of device. Therefore, we developed a post treatment skill to modulate the 
threshold voltage of pentacene transistor through iodine doping. The iodine doping would not only 
affect the threshold voltage, but also the conductivity and mobility of pentacene, which is similar to 
impurity doping theory in silicon. Iodine doped penatcene has been investigated earlier and its 
characteristics was found slightly change with undoped pentacene. The iodine act as acceptor states 
in pentacene. Iodine would interact with pentacene, which lead to the formation of positively 
charged pentacene and negatively charged iodide counter anions (I3-, I5-) [4]. This procedure 
provides more free holes, thus change the electrical properties of pentacene transistor. In this article, 
the modulation range of threshold voltage could be tens of voltage and mobility could be enhanced 
almost twice to 0.18 cm2/vs with undoped sample. The on/off current ratio was all over 105.  
The iodine doping skill was developed on a bottom gate bottom contact (BGBC) device first, 
its configuration as shown in Fig. 1(a). The device had an indium tin oxide (ITO) gate and 
source/drain electrodes with a thickness of 150 nm. Its gate dielectric layer was TEOS SiO2 with a 
thickness of 300 nm and fabricated by plasma enhanced chemical vapor deposition (PECVD). 
30-nm-thick pentacene was evaporated on it. Finally, a silver layer (100 nm) was deposited on it 
and patterned as source/drain electrodes. The iodine would act with silver; therefore they could not 
be doped after the silver process. The experiment result was shown in Fig 3. As pristine, the 
characteristics of the BGTC TFT behaves like a varistor within -40-40 V. After heating with 70 ℃, 
the curves did not change much, but the current increased. That was attributed to the iodine 
diffusion into up and down pentacene layer, therefore increased the pentacene conductivity. Then, 
the 90 ℃ heating process leads the TFT to function work as an enhancement mode transistor with 
Vth -7.7 V. After 110 ℃ heating process, the TFT shows the characteristics like an undoped BGTC 
TFT with a Vth -13 V. The BGTC TFT has an Ion/Ioff ratio over 105 and a higher mobility ranged 
from 4.2×10-2 to 1.8×10-1 cm2/vs than the BGBC TFT. Comparing to an undoped BGTC TFT, the 
mobility of the iodine doped sample increase to almost two times. 
In summary, we succeed to modulate the threshold voltage of pentacene TFT through the 
doping of iodine. The modulation range is the largest as we knew to date. This is a fantastic work 
for the commercialization of organic TFT due to the request of precisely control of device 
parameters. The authors like to thank the financial support of national science council in contract no. 
of NSC 98-2221-E-018-017.
