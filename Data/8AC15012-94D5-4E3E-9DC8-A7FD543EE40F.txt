4. CONCLUSION 
 
An extension of Golay’s two pair construction to general 
complex alphabets is described.  Golay’s block 
concatenation and block interleaving constructions can be 
viewed as special cases in a unified parameterized 
construction.  The construction is flexible since there are no 
restrictions on the alphabets, structures and lengths of the two 
component complementary pairs.  Existing and new QAM 
Golay complementary sequences can be obtained from this 
construction. Zeros can be inserted to the Golay sequences by 
adjusting the parameters.

======= Review 1 ======= 
> *** Strengths: What are the key strengths of this paper? 
The paper provides examples of Golay pairs over the 16-QAM alphabet for lengths 7,9, and 15, where no Golay pairs over the 
QPSK alphabet exist. 
> *** Weaknesses: What are the major weaknesses of this paper? 
The major weakness of the paper is that it essentially only contains an exhaustive search for 16-QAM Golay pairs of length 7 and 
partial search results for lengths 9 and 15. Apart from Proposition 2, which is fairly straightforward, I cannot see any new results 
beyond this search. 
> *** Comments and Recommendation: Please give the reasoning for your  
> overall recommendation and 
any additional comments you wish to add. 
I am not really agreeing with the main motivations of this paper. 
1. The authors remark on p.1 that "The complete set of all sequence lengths where Golay complementary sequences exist is still 
unknown." 
If the goal is to find Golay sequences over the reals or the complex numbers, then at least when the length is even, there is a 
simple construction using Huffman sequences [Huffman, IEEE vol. IT-8 (1962)]. These are sequences with only one nonzero 
out-of-phase aperiodic autocorrelation (at the extreme shift). When the length is even, we can negate every second element to 
get a second sequence, which is complementary to the first. I guess, with a little thought one can also get a construction for odd 
lengths. Hence the above question is only meaningful if the alphabet is restricted. 
2. In Section 3, the authors raise the question of construction sequences with PMSEPR strictly less than 2 asymptotically. While it 
is true that the proposed construction gives sequences with PMSEPR less than 2, the limit, as the length tends to infinity, is 2 
again. So this does not answer the question raised by the authors. 
In contrast, Huffman sequences of length n have PMSEPR at most 1+2/n and there are good bounds on the ratio of the largest 
and the smallest magnitude of the sequences elements. 
Furthermore, there are sequences with unimodular elements, whose PMSEPR tends to 1 as the length tends to infinity. These 
correspond to certain polynomials, commonly known as "ultraflat polynomials".
