一、 中文摘要 
 
本計畫使用新方法製作 OTFT 元件電極，
利用微接觸的方式在金屬表面沾上一層導電
聚合物 PEDOT，之後再連金屬一起轉印至有
機半導體上，過程中不需要金屬濕蝕刻，不但
可解決導電聚合物的導電度問題，也能兼具降
低接觸電阻的優點，製作出更穩定且特性良好
的 OTFT 元件電極，達最佳化之效果。由實驗
結果顯示，使用 Au/PEDOT 堆疊式的電極，將
可有效促進元件的臨界電壓、開關比、載子移
動力、與源/汲極接觸電阻等特性，未來若整
合捲軸式壓印技術進行量產，將可製作出兼具
低成本與良好特性的軟性電子元件。 
關鍵詞: 奈米壓印, 有機薄膜電晶體 
英文摘要 
In this project, we will work on the new 
fabrication technique for the OTFTs electrode. 
With the micro contact inking of PEDOT on 
metal surface, the two-layer (metal/PEDOT) 
structure will be transferred to the surface of 
pentacene. The high conductivity and low 
contact resistance electrode will be fabricated for 
the future reliable and high performance OTFTs. 
In our results, the Au/PEDOT stacked 
source/drain electrodes of OTFTs were 
fabricated by combining the micro-contact 
inking and reversal imprinting. The PEDOT was 
inked on the mold by the micro-contact process 
and the Au/PEDOT stacked layer was 
transferred on pentacene by imprinting 
technology. The threshold voltage, and on-off 
ratio, carrier mobility, and source/drain contact 
resistance of organic TFTs were all improved by 
the proposed process. 
 
Keywords: OTFT, PEDOT, and Imprint 
 
二、 計畫的緣由與目的 
The organic thin film transistors (OTFTs) 
have become the potential candidates for 
low-cost and flexible electronics application 
[1-3]. To prevent the solvent and reactive-ion 
etching damage of organic semiconductor in the 
photolithography, the shadow mask is often used 
in the pattern process. However, the resolution is 
limited, minimum down to ~30μm, and it is 
difficult to apply the method over a large area.  
The imprinting lithography is a cost-effective 
solution as conventional projection lithography 
reaches its limits [4-7]. In 2002, L. J. Guo et al. 
[8-9] developed a reversal imprinting technique. 
It was demonstrated that the polymer structure 
can be fabricated onto the flexible substrates by 
using this technique. In 2008, the nano-scale Au 
line was successfully fabricated on flexible 
substrate by reversal imprinting and lift-off 
process by Henry J. H. Chen et al. [3]. Besides, 
the imprint and transfer printing technique had 
been demonstrated to fabricate the electrodes for 
OTFTs. The organic interlayer, poly

To find the optimized imprinting condition of 
Au/PEDOT stacked electrodes transferring on 
organic semiconductor material (pentacene), 
there were several test imprinting processes 
performed on the flexible polyimide substrate. In 
our experiments, the imprinting conditions of 
stacked electrodes transferring for these two 
materials are similar. Here, the electrode was 
imprinted on the pentacene with the condition of 
90-120 oC, 100-300 psi, for 3min. The OTFTs 
channel width/length is about ~150-750μm/ 
~3-75μm.  
The imprinting results were investigated by 
optical microscope (OM). The contact resistance 
was extracted by using the transmission line 
method (TLM) [11]. The output (I-V) 
characteristics, and transfer characteristics were 
measured by the semiconductor parameter 
analyzer HP 4156.

There are several unpredictable issues may 
violate the performance of OTFTs in the 
proposed process. For example, in the imprinting 
process, the high temperature process and the 
pressure applied by hard Si mold may influence 
the structure or the molecular arrangement of 
organic materials, PEDOT and pentacene. 
Besides, the exposure in the air may also damage 
the quality of organic materials. In this paper, the 
process temperature has been reduced. Reducing 
the process cycle time, process pressure or using 
the flexible mold within the imprinting technique 
should be considered for the further 
investigations.

結論: 
The Au/PEDOT stacked electrodes of 
pentacene TFTs were fabricated by inking and 
imprinting technology. The yield of electrodes 
transferring on pentacence was about ~90%. The 
threshold voltage, and on-off ratio, carrier 
mobility, and source/drain contact resistance of 
OTFTs with the Au/PEDOT electrode were 
better than those of OTFTs with Au electrode.
