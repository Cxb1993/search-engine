Multi-objective optimal algorithm of Economic Dispatch Problems 
for the Large-Scale Power System Considering Emission

摘要 
本計畫提出一套新的多目標演算法應用
於考慮污染之大型電力系統經濟調度問題
上，可提供給台電及民營系統業者規劃同時考
慮發電成本（Cost）與污染排放量（Emission）
的最佳電力經濟調度使用。「改良型基因演算
法」（Improved genetic algorithm）的運算過程
中，配備了改良型進化方向運算子（Improved 
evolutionary direction operator）與遷移運算子，
可以有效搜尋及主動探索出最佳解。另外運用
「 參 數 更 新 技 術 」（ Multiplier updating 
technique）將能在求解過程中，避免造成擴展
拉 格 朗 其 函 數 （ Augmented Lagrangian 
function）的變數空間扁平化（Deforming）而
導致的求解或搜尋困難。同時採用「ε-限制式
技術」（ε-constraint technique）來處理此多目
標的最佳化問題。因此本計畫已整合了「ε-
限制式技術」、「改良型基因演算法」及「參數
更新技術」，提出「多目標最佳化演算法於考
慮污染之大型電力系統經濟調度」解決方案。 
本計畫所提出的演算法具有下列優點；1)
可自動調適的處罰參數以處理限制條件、2)僅
需使用少量族群來求解可降低運算時間、3)考
慮發電污染影響具多目標規劃、和 4)可適用於
大型（Large-scale）電力系統的經濟調度問題
上。經過四個例題測試比較，驗證本計畫的成
果應用於此問題上，確實具有效能及實用價
值。 
 
關鍵詞：污染，多目標最佳化，參數更新技術，
電力經濟調度。 
 
 
 
 
Abstract 
This project has proposed a new 
multi-objective optimal algorithm of economic 
dispatch problems for the large-scale power 
system considering emission, aiming to 
minimize two objectives - cost and emission. 
This project would like to provide for the 
Taipower Company and many private power 
enterprises to program the multi-objective 
optimal economic dispatch of large-scale power 
systems. The improved genetic algorithm (IGA) 
equipped with an improved evolutionary 
direction operator and a migration operation can 
efficiently search and actively explore solutions. 
The multiplier updating (MU) technique will be 
introduced to avoid deforming the augmented 
Lagrangian function and resulting in difficulty to 
solution searching. To handle the multi-objective 
problem, the ε -constraint technique is 
employed. The proposed approach has integrated 
the ε -constraint technique, the improved 
genetic algorithm, and the multiplier updating 
technique. 
The proposed method have merits: 1) 
automatically adjusting the randomly given 
penalty to a proper value； 2) requiring only a 
small-size population; 3) considering emission; 
and 4) applicable to the large-scale power system. 
Compared results of four test systems show the 
superiority of the proposed method over earlier 
researches, and reveal that this project has both 
of the efficiency and the practice for solving the 
problem of large-scale power system considering 
emission. 
  
Keywords: Emission, Multi-objective 
optimization, Multiplier Updating technique, 
Power economic dispatch.

3.4 本計畫演算法的流程 
本計畫所提具自動調適處罰函數的參數
更新技術將可有效避免 ALF的求解困難，圖 1
為本計畫整合ε-限制式技術、IGA 與 MU 的
演算法流程圖。在此圖中具有兩個迴路：（1）
在內迴路中本計畫應用 IGA，以由外迴路處得
到的處罰參數及拉格朗奇乘積子，求解 ALF。
（2）然後將搜尋得到的 ALF解答，傳送至外
迴路朝La（x, ν,υ）上邊界（Upper bound of La）
更新。當內外迴路都疊代足夠的世代數後，ALF
將會收斂至對偶問題（Dual problem）的鞍點
（Saddle-point）[30]。

5. 結論 
本計畫已提出一套「考慮污染之大型多目
標電力系統經濟調度」最佳化演算法，可應用
於同時規劃發電成本與污染排放量的最佳電
力經濟調度問題上。本計畫所提之整合型最佳
化演算法，整合了「ε-限制式技術」、「改良型
基因演算法」及「參數更新技術」。並具有下
列優點；1)可自動調適的處罰參數以處理限制
條件、2)僅需使用少量族群來求解可降低運算
時間、3)考慮發電污染影響具多目標規劃、和
4)可適用於大型電力系統的經濟調度問題上。
經過四個例題測試比較，驗證出本計畫的成果
應用於大型多目標電力系統經濟調度問題
上，確實具有效能及實用價值。

3. The Proposed Algorithm 
 
3.1. The IGA 
 
The GA [12] is a parallel and global searching-
technique that emulates natural genetic operations. GA 
can find a global solution after sufficient iterations, but 
has a high computational burden. To enhance GA’s 
computational efficiency, an improved evolutionary 
direction operator (IEDO) modified from [13] and a 
migration operator [14] are embedded in GA to form 
the IGA. The IEDO includes choosing the three best 
solutions in each generation to perform the 
evolutionary direction operator algorithm to find a new 
solution superior to the original best solution. The IGA 
has been applied successfully to constrained 
optimization problems such as: nonlinear mixed-
integer optimization problems [15] and the combined 
heat and SPED problem [16]. The IGA requires only a 
small population, and it is more efficient than GA. 
 
3.2. The Multiplier Updating (MU) 
 
Michalewicz et al. [17] surveyed and compared 
several constraint-handling techniques used in 
evolutionary algorithms, and showed that the penalty 
function method is among the most popularly methods 
for managing constraints. Powell [18] noted that 
classical optimization methods with a penalty function 
have certain disadvantages that become severe when 
using large penalty parameters, because the penalty 
function becomes “ill-conditioned”, making good 
solutions difficult to obtain. However, a situation in 
which the penalty parameters are too small makes it 
impossible for the constraint violation to contribute a 
high cost to the penalty function. Therefore, selecting 
suitable penalty parameters is not trivial. Herein, the 
MU [15] is introduced to manage constrained 
optimization problems. Such a method can eradicate 
the ill-conditioned property of the objective function.

constrained optimization problems is defined as:

3.3. Solution Procedures of the Proposed 
Algorithm 
 
Figure 1 displays the flow chart of the proposed 
algorithm, which has two iterative loops. The 
augmented Lagrange function is used to obtain a 
minimum value in the inner loop with the given 
penalty parameters and multipliers, which are then 
updated in the outer loop toward producing an upper 
limit of La. When both inner and outer iterations 
become sufficiently large, the augmented Lagrange 
function converges to a saddle-point of the dual 
problem [15]. Advantages of the proposed method are 
that the IGA efficiently searches the optimal solution 
in the economic dispatch process and the MU 
effectively tackles system constraints.

5. Conclusions 
 
Few reports have been made at units considering 
both valve-point effects and multiple fuels for the 
realistic SPED problem, which always exist in real 
power systems simultaneously. Herein, this research 
proposes the IGA to solve the practical SPED 
operation. The realistic SPED problem is complicated 
because both valve-point loadings and change fuels 
must be considered. The IGA helps the proposed 
algorithm to efficiently search and actively explore the 
solution, and the MU helps the proposed method to 
efficaciously handle constraints of the system. One of 
the contributions of this research is the inclusion of 
valve-point loadings and multi-fuel sources into a 
frame. The other contribution of this study is the 
combination of the IGA and the MU to solve the SPED 
problem. The comparative results demonstrate that the 
proposed algorithm has shown the effectiveness for 
solving the real world SPED problem.

3. The Proposed Algorithm 
 
3.1. The IGA 
 
The GA [12] is a parallel and global searching-
technique that emulates natural genetic operations. GA 
can find a global solution after sufficient iterations, but 
has a high computational burden. To enhance GA’s 
computational efficiency, an improved evolutionary 
direction operator (IEDO) modified from [13] and a 
migration operator [14] are embedded in GA to form 
the IGA. The IEDO includes choosing the three best 
solutions in each generation to perform the 
evolutionary direction operator algorithm to find a new 
solution superior to the original best solution. The IGA 
has been applied successfully to constrained 
optimization problems such as: nonlinear mixed-
integer optimization problems [15] and the combined 
heat and SPED problem [16]. The IGA requires only a 
small population, and it is more efficient than GA. 
 
3.2. The Multiplier Updating (MU) 
 
Michalewicz et al. [17] surveyed and compared 
several constraint-handling techniques used in 
evolutionary algorithms, and showed that the penalty 
function method is among the most popularly methods 
for managing constraints. Powell [18] noted that 
classical optimization methods with a penalty function 
have certain disadvantages that become severe when 
using large penalty parameters, because the penalty 
function becomes “ill-conditioned”, making good 
solutions difficult to obtain. However, a situation in 
which the penalty parameters are too small makes it 
impossible for the constraint violation to contribute a 
high cost to the penalty function. Therefore, selecting 
suitable penalty parameters is not trivial. Herein, the 
MU [15] is introduced to manage constrained 
optimization problems. Such a method can eradicate 
the ill-conditioned property of the objective function.

constrained optimization problems is defined as:

3.3. Solution Procedures of the Proposed 
Algorithm 
 
Figure 1 displays the flow chart of the proposed 
algorithm, which has two iterative loops. The 
augmented Lagrange function is used to obtain a 
minimum value in the inner loop with the given 
penalty parameters and multipliers, which are then 
updated in the outer loop toward producing an upper 
limit of La. When both inner and outer iterations 
become sufficiently large, the augmented Lagrange 
function converges to a saddle-point of the dual 
problem [15]. Advantages of the proposed method are 
that the IGA efficiently searches the optimal solution 
in the economic dispatch process and the MU 
effectively tackles system constraints.

5. Conclusions 
 
Few reports have been made at units considering 
both valve-point effects and multiple fuels for the 
realistic SPED problem, which always exist in real 
power systems simultaneously. Herein, this research 
proposes the IGA to solve the practical SPED 
operation. The realistic SPED problem is complicated 
because both valve-point loadings and change fuels 
must be considered. The IGA helps the proposed 
algorithm to efficiently search and actively explore the 
solution, and the MU helps the proposed method to 
efficaciously handle constraints of the system. One of 
the contributions of this research is the inclusion of 
valve-point loadings and multi-fuel sources into a 
frame. The other contribution of this study is the 
combination of the IGA and the MU to solve the SPED 
problem. The comparative results demonstrate that the 
proposed algorithm has shown the effectiveness for 
solving the real world SPED problem.
