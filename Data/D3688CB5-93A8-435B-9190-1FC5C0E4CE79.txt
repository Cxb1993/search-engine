ABSTRACT 
This sub-project is to develop a numerical method for modeling or 
predicting the performance of a micro-channel reformer and a micro-PEMFC 
for design purposes.  The processes in a PEMFC include momentum, energy 
and mass transports together with chemical and electro-chemical reactions.   
 
The first year project goals are  
(1) to establish the transport model and computer code for a reformer with 
micro-channel catalyst layer. 
(2) to establish the transport model and computer code for MEA of a 
PEMFC with micro-channels. 
The second year project goals are  
(1) to model heat and mass transfer in a reformer with micro-channel 
catalyst layer. 
(2) to perform modeling for MEA of a PEMFC with micro-channels. 
The third year project goals are  
(1) to perform performance evaluation for a reformer with micro-channel 
catalyst layer. 
(2) to perform performance evaluation for a PEMFC with micro-channels. 
(3) to accomplish a numerical method for modeling and predicting the 
performance of a micro-channel reformer and a micro-PEMFC. 
 
The computer modeling of heat and mass transfer in a methanol micro-
channel reformer has been finished.  Two conference papers one in 3rd 
IECEC Conference and one in 5th Joint ASME/JSME Fluids Engineering 
Conference have been published.  A numerical method for modeling a 
micro-PEMFC has been established.  The results are in good agreement with 
the experimental data of the sub-project one. 
 
Keywords: PEMFC, numerical modeling, micro- channel heat and mass transfer, 
PEMFC reformer with micro-channel catalyst layer.

Abstract 
 
Methanol micro-channel reformer is an important device to generate 
hydrogen. Fuel produces hydrogen and other chemical substance through a 
reformer in the catalytic reaction. The catalyst is adopted to reduce the 
activation energy and speed up the reforming reaction. The molecules of 
methanol mixture steam will collide more frequently in micro-channel 
configuration. In this study, the performance of a methanol micro-channel 
reformer with Cu/Zn and Pd/ZnO catalyst is investigated numerically with 
various channel height, channel length, gas inlet velocity, and the catalyst 
density.  And the rates of hydrogen production in various conditions were 
evaluated. The results show that the methanol conversion is increased while the 
channel length increased, the channel height increased, the inlet temperature 
increased, the inlet velocity decreased, and the catalyst density increased. The 
results of this study provide the need for the fuel cell system design. 
 
keywords?Fuel cell reformer, Micro-channel reactor, Numerical simulations, 
Methanol steam reforming, Micro-channel of heat and mass transfer.

Effect of the channel length 
Channel length is varied from 800 to 6000 µm.  Results are 
given in Figure 2.  It is shown that the results are the same at

89.73%, 95.61%, 98.13% and 99.21% for L=2000, 3000, 4000, 
5000, and 6000 µm, respectively.  As expected, longer channel 
length provides longer time for the reaction to take place and 
results in higher conversion ratio.

Effects of inlet velocity 
Ten inlet velocities of 0.00001, 0.00005, 0.0001, 0.0005, 0.001, 
0.005, 0.01, 0.02, 0.04, and 0.05m/s are studied and the results 
are given in Figure 4. As expected, the conversion ratio is 
higher for lower flow rate since lower flow rate allow longer 
time for reforming to take place.  For inlet velocity of 0.05 
m/s, 74% methanol conversion has been reached.  When inlet 
velocity is 0.01 m/s, the conversion ratio increased to more than 
95%. The results show that 98% conversion ratio can be 
reached when the inlet velocity is lowerd down to 10-4 m/s.  It 
is worth to mention that H2 production rate is propotional to the 
flow rate, therefore one should consider both the conversion 
ratio and the production rate simultaneously to match the 
capacity in design purpose.

CONCLUSIONS 
This study provides a design reference for the application 
of micro-structure in a fuel cell reformer.  The channel length 
is varied from 800 to 6000 µm and the channel height varies 
from 50 to 400 µm.  The inlet flow velocity varies from 
0.00001 to 0.002 m/s, and the inlet temperature varies from 250 
to 350 oC. The density (area fraction on the wall) of catalyst 
varies from 25 to 100 %.  The results indicate that: 
1. the higher the inlet velocity, the lower the methanol 
conversion ratio 
2. the variation of the conversion ratio is almost linear with 
the flow rate 
3. the methanol conversion ratio increases with the increase of 
inlet temperature  
4. the higher the catalyst desity, the higher the conversion 
effieciency. However, the present results indicate that the 
reduction of the catalyst density by a half only sacrifices 
5% performance 
5. the conversion efficiency decreases with the increase of the 
channel height, but increases with the increase of the inlet 
temperature.
