中文摘要 
肌肉痙攣(spasticity)是一個常見於
脊髓損傷或中風等中樞神經系統之疾病，
為了建立神經保護新藥或新式治療，了解
此痙攣症狀之漸進式運動能力(如步態表
現)就顯的格外重要，我們先前研究著重於
評估肌肉牽張反射與運動功能恢復的時程
改變，我們使用牽張反射與BBB 步行量表
來評估脊髓損傷受傷前後之改變。本研究
繼續延伸先前研究，並藉由觀察痙攣大白
鼠的步行功能之時程發展變化。本研究目
的除了脊髓損傷的模式，本年度以缺血性
中風之動物模型，發展一缺血性中風鼠之
步態分析系統，我們可以定量在中風後進
行步態時程分析。當大鼠在透明步道上行
走時，我們可以使用高速隻數位相機捕捉
大鼠足印之影像，藉由離線影像分析可以
確認痙攣大白鼠行走時的後腳之時間與空
間步態參數，例如步行速度、步距、跨步
長、擺盪與著地時間為主要評估參數，結
果顯示與術前比較下，痙攣大白鼠的步行
速度減少，並在雙腳之步距和跨步長中觀
察到步態不對稱情形，擺盪期與著地期時
間也在術後雙腳中顯示同步增加。這個結
果指出此步態分析可以在缺血性中風痙攣
大白鼠提供各種運動功能之詳細資訊，未
來也可使用此工具來探討經顱磁刺激於痙
攣大白鼠或人類中風的治療研究。 
 
關鍵字：缺血性中風、運動功能恢復、步
態模式、步行、痙攣大白鼠。 
 
Abstract 
Muscle spasticity of limbs is a common 
impairment in patients suffering from central 
nerve system such as stroke or spinal cord 
injury. In our previous study, we focused on 
assessing the time-course changes of spastic 
hypertonia in electrophysiology and 
locomotor function for animal following 
spinal cord injury (SCI) of T8 hemisection. 
To develop future neuroprotective drug 
research or novel therapies, the progressive 
changes in the motor performance such as 
gait have become increasingly important to 
provide insight into the time-course 
development of locomotor function. 
The aim of the study of this year is to 
extend the SCI to middle cerebral artery 
occlusion (MCAO) in stroke rats for 
observing the time-course change locomotor 
functions of spastic rats. A gait analysis 
platform using image analysis tool was 
developed using high-speed commercially 
available camera for automatic measurement 
of gait parameters of spastic rat. The images 
of foot prints of rats walking on the 
transparent walkway can be captured using a 
high-speed (300 frames/sec) but inexpensive 
digital camera. Image analysis of the 
footprint can be performed off-line to 
identify the temporal and spatial indices of 
bilateral hindlimbs in MCAO rats during 
walking. The parameters of walking speed,

(B) Video-based gait analysis system 
A similar walking track for observing 
the footprint image [4, 8, 9] was adopted and 
modified in this study (Fig. 1). As depicted 
in Fig. 1, the footprint image acquisition 
system consists of three major components: 
waking track, light source with reflection 
mirror, and digital camera. 
The walking track apparatus consists of 
a Plexiglas chamber 80 (l) × 6 (w) × 12 (h) 
cm. Because uniform distribution of light is 
essential for image analysis, we place LED 
light source in front and parallel of the 
mirror just next to the camera. A mirror with 
45 degrees of tilting angle was placed under 
the walking track, which would reflect the 
appearance of the soles of the rat to the high 
speed (300Hz samples per sec, 512 × 384 
pixel) and high resolution (60Hz, 2816 × 
2112 pixel) digital camera (EX-F1, Casio), 
which is placed in front of the mirror. 
 
Figure 1. Schematic diagram of walking 
track apparatus equipped with a high speed 
digital camera placing 80 cm in front to give 
a direct side view of lateral joint motion 
trajectory and a reflected plantar view of the 
footprint image on the track. 
 
(C) Measurement of footprint Indices 
Among the various footprint parameters, 
five spatial and three temporal footprint 
indices were calculated from the acquired 
footprint image, as depicted in figure 2. The 
first five spatial indices generally measure 
step length, stride length, base of support 
(BOS), foot angle, and print length (PL) of 
foot. These spatial parameters were 
calculated with high resolution image, 
corresponding to 0.18 mm resolution in the 
field of 50 cm walking track. The other three 
indices, relate temporal parameters of gait 
pattern, containing the swing phase time, 
stance phase time and walking speed. 
Among them, the swing and stance phase for 
each hindlimb were determined from side 
view at 300 Hz video sampling. The walking 
speed also calculated from the 
distance/duration under automatic rat’s eye 
catching as the distance between beginning 
and terminal points. 
 
Figure 2. Tracking of hindlimb footprints for 
determining spatial indices of gait. 
 
After acquiring the footprint image, the 
digital image processing was applied to 
detect the boundary of the soles and 
determine the critical points for deriving the 
paw indices. By a simple threshold technique, 
seven red markers to represent horizontal 
and vertical frame can be separated and used 
for transformation of the unit from pixel 
number to real length. Similarly, the foot sole 
can be differentiated from the contrast for 
further extraction of footprint indices. A 
series of image processing can be taken to 
determine and further calculate the several 
spatial parameters from sequential images.

IV. Conclusions 
In current study, quantitative 
time-course changes of gait pattern in 
MCAO rats can be obtained from an 
inexpensive digital video system. The results 
demonstrated that temporal and spatial gait 
parameters can be accurately analyzed by 
our proposed system. Our findings provide 
objective assessment for spastic rats of 
hemi-MCAO animal. The integration of 
muscle tone assessment system developed in 
first two years and current gait analysis 
should provide quantitative locomotor 
function indices for assessing the rTMS 
treatment of CNS lesion including SCI and 
stroke in the long run  
計畫成果自評 
本研究計畫大致完成原研究計畫之構
想發展經顱磁刺激於痙攣大白鼠的治療研
究。先前兩年以經顱磁刺激及微小化肌張
力評估系統為主，本期主要成果為成功建
置在評估痙攣大白鼠之步態分析系統，利
用數位影像分析得到精確完整之步態參
數，並以腦部缺血性中風之痙攣大白鼠模
型中進行時程分析，評估由受傷後至恢復
期之步態模式改變，建立大白鼠單邊中風
後自主復原的自然發展，由此資訊可在新
式經顱磁刺激於治療等動作行為之量化評
估工具，建立未來長期探討經顱磁刺激治
療研究之基礎。

二、與會心得 
今年會議 Invited Speaker為Alison Mercer, University of Otago, New Zealand “Manipulating 
dopamine pathways in the bee brain: Impacts on brain function and behaviour” 主題為蜂王如何以
荷洛蒙得釋放去調節工蜂的學習能力及行為，而這些又與蜜蜂Dopamine的濃度及後續的學習
行為相關。其理論是蜂王壓抑工蜂的學習行為，使其能”臣服”於蜂王之下從幼蜂的餵養、環
境的清潔、築蜂巢到最後擔任守衛而終其一生。真佩服這些動物學家的觀察能力。 
 
與本研究直接相關，互相輝映的研究為 “Modulation of intracortical inhibition by low 
intensity theta burst stimulation,” Michael Ridding, The University of Adelaide, Australia 
 
其似乎與我們研究的作伙伴(長庚黃英儒醫師)師出同門，皆為研究經顱磁刺激(TMS)中陣
發型磁波 (theta burst stimulation, TBS)的刺激模式對腦塑性之探討。經顱磁刺激是一種無痛、
非侵入性，且已廣泛運用在人腦科學研究的新興實驗與評估工具。由於大腦中的神經傳導需
要電流的改變，經顱磁刺激就藉由這個特性，以一種非侵入性、無痛而且安全的方式，利用
金屬線圈，直接對腦中特定區域發出強力但短暫的磁性脈衝，與腦部引發微量的電流來興奮
刺激之區域，研究者首度在不須將大腦打開的情況下，成功的利用磁刺激誘發人類的運動皮
質區，並且紀錄到動作誘發電位(motor evoked potential, MEP)，因此，初期經顱磁刺激之研究
皆以評估神經傳導路徑興奮性居多。 
     
近幾年，使用連續輸出的磁刺激模式開始發展，目前研究中不僅評估皮質脊髓徑路之神
經生理，同時也是一個非常好用的治療神經學和精神疾患的工具。過去許多研究顯示重複性
經顱磁刺激的對於神經生理的影響依賴於輸出的頻率。低頻(低於1Hz)的重複性經顱磁刺激模
式傾向於減少運動皮質的興奮性，高頻(高於1 Hz)重複性經顱磁刺激則增加皮質興奮性。且這
個興奮性改變會持續一段時間，這種後續效應(after effect)可能來自於運動皮質產生長效興奮
(LTP, long-term potentiation)或長期抑制(LTD, long-term depression)。由於長效興奮或抑制對於
人類的學習、記憶或大腦塑性改變等扮演一個很重要的過程，因此，使用重複性經顱磁刺激
來引發人類腦中的長效興奮或抑制的改變將可應用於治療神經學疾患，來增強大腦神經的塑
性(plasticity)，幫助運動功能之提升。 
     
最近，另一種可以在大腦造成長期效應的重複性經顱磁刺激模式被發表出來，稱之為陣
發型磁波刺激(theta burst stimulation, TBS)，此一模式比過去傳統磁刺激更快速也更有效率去
改變運動皮質的興奮性。陣發型磁波刺激基本組成模式為低強度(80%主動動作閥值, AMT)、
高頻的Theta叢集脈波(三個脈波於50Hz釋放)，並在每200毫秒重複輸出；且刺激模式分為連續
性陣發型磁波刺激(cTBS)和間歇性陣發型磁波刺激(iTBS)，過去研究顯示使用20秒的連續性
陣發型磁波刺激可以持續降低動作誘發電位(MEP)20至30分鐘，因此連續性陣發型磁波刺激
會產生持續性的運動皮質抑制，間歇性陣發型磁波刺激則為長時間的促進運動皮質興奮。 
     
由於陣發型磁波刺激可以造成運動皮質興奮性長時間的改變，為了去確認陣發型磁波刺
激在運動皮質內部神經活動機制的改變，可以在陣發型磁波刺激前後，使用成對磁波刺激造
成短間隔皮質內抑制(short interval intracortical inhibition, SICI)以及皮質內促進(intracortical 
facilitation, ICF)等參數當作評估指標。短間隔皮質內抑制(SICI)使用成對的磁脈波，兩個脈波
