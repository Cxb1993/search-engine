Analysis of Temperature Compensation 
Mechanism of Fiber Bragg Grating 
Packages  
 
Abstract 
Optical fiber Bragg gratings (FBG) are important 
components in optical communication systems. But the 
1nm shift in Bragg wavelength over a 100 ?  
temperature range for an uncompensated grating can be a 
serious problem. The wavelength shift of an 
uncompensated FBG usually doesn’t meet the 
requirement for 100GHZ channel spacing. (wavelength 
shift ?0.8nm). 
In this paper, we studied two temperature 
compensated mechanisms for fiber Bragg grating 
packages, and the parametric study for thermal issues of 
FBG was also be performed, finite element models were 
constructed in addition to analyze fiber grating 
temperature compensated package, and direction toward 
better design was proposed. 
Key word: fiber Bragg grating, temperature 
compensation, channel spacing.
