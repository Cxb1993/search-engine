Abstract
This project completed the analysis of the hybrid compensation strategies for the reconstructed image
degrading effect due to the recording material thickness change for the off-axis holographic data storage
system. The hybrid compensation methods can reduce the recording material’s quality request, which
indirectly decrease its manufacturing cost. Based on the first Born approximation theory, we analyzed and
simulated the optical intensity distribution of the reconstructed image under various recording conditions and
reconstruction conditions in which we simultaneously modulated two or more reading parameters. Our studies
can afford to deeply understand the reconstruction mechanism of the off-axis holographic data storage system
and the compensation performance by different hybrid strategies for the degrading effect of the reconstructed
image due to the recording material thickness shrinkage.
