中英文摘要 
 
關鍵詞：ANAMMOX (Anaerobic Ammonia Oxidation)，AO (Anaerobic Oxic)，脫硝，活性
污泥模式，生物膜模式 
 
許多同時去除氮、磷的生物去除營養鹽系統的第 1 段為厭氧段，其主要目的為厭氧釋
磷。然而，當進流水中含有硝酸氮的情況下，將使得厭氧段轉變成缺氧情況，而影響除磷
功能。因此，本研究提出 Enhanced ANAMMOX (Anaerobic Ammonia Oxidation) Biofilm 
Denitrification Anaerobic Oxic (EABD-AO) 系統，以克服此問題。本研究首先操作 EABD-AO
活性污泥系統，於進流水中添加 2 mg L-1 亞硝酸氮、1 mg L-1 碳酸氫鈉。pH 維持於 6.5 至 7
之間 (提供 H+)、污泥停留時間維持於 15 天。最後，以本研究團隊所發展的台灣活性污泥
模式 NO.1 (TaiWan Extended Activated sludge model NO.1, TWEA1)、生物膜模式，併同
ANAMMOX 動力模式，建立 EABD-AO 模式，並將演算所得之結果，與模型廠實驗值進行
比較，以確認模式之準確性，評估處理效能。與 A2O 比較可知，EABD-AO 之總化學需氧
量 (total chemical oxygen demand, TCOD) 和溶解性化學需氧量 (soluble chemical oxygen 
demand, SCOD) 去除率較低，TCOD 和 SCOD 去除率分別低於 A2O 之去除率 3.61 %及 2.05 
%。雖然 EABD-AO 之碳去除率較低，但在氨氮 (NH4+-N)、總氮 (total nitrogen, TN)、PO4-3
之去除率方面反而較高，分別高於 A2O 之去除率 0.93 %、4.23 % 和 10.0 %。據推測，應
該是 EABD-AO 系統強化 ANAMMOX 反應所致。ANAMMOX 菌族群之動力參數標定方
面，實驗結果顯示，EABD-AO 系統內， maxABμ 之值約為 0.09 d-1，接近 Koch et al. ( 2000) 所
提出的 0.072 d-1。出流溶解性成份的模式模擬值與實驗值相當一致。由於一致性良好，可
推測系統中存在 ANAMMOX 行為，但仍需進一步藉分子生物等方法分析微生物族群。 
 
Keywords：ANAMMOX (Anaerobic Ammonia Oxidation), AO (Anaerobic Oxic), denitrification, 
activated sludge model, biofilm model

main purpose is for phosphorus releasing under anaerobic condition. When influent wastewater 
contained nitrate, anaerobic stage was switched into anoxic condition, it resulted in worse 
phosphorus removal. Therefore, an enhanced ANAMMOX (Anaerobic Ammonia Oxidation) 
Biofilm Denitrification Anaerobic Oxic (EABD-AO) process was proposed in this study to 
conquer this problem. The reactions in EABD-AO system were observed. The major operational 
parameters included the ratios of different components in influent, dissolved oxygen, sludge 
retention time, the removal efficiencies and degradation rates of carbon, nitrogen and phosphorus

二、研究目的 
 
本研究之研究目的如下： 
1.操作 EABD-AO 活性污泥系統，視情況於進流水中添加硝酸氮、亞硝酸氮、碳酸氫
鈉等，加強 ANAMMOX 反應。 
2.探討懸浮性活性污泥與固定生物膜之間的硝化、脫硝、ANAMMOX 等反應，並可求
得其計量關係。 
3.藉由模廠操作，探討在不同操作條件下監測碳、氮、磷去除率、降解速率等。除可
了解微生物動力行為，並可探討系統各種成份、細菌轉化、傳輸過程及成分之轉化。 
4.併同 TWEA1、生物膜模式、ANAMMOX 動力模式，自行以 FORTRAN 程式語言撰
寫程式，建立 EABD-AO 數學模式

3.4 國內缺氧/厭氧/好氧處理程序及 ANAMMOX 程序的發展現況 
國內對缺氧/厭氧/好氧處理程序之相關研究較少，且本研究擬於第 1 段的缺氧槽中加入
生物膜擔體，並視情況添加 NaNO3、NaNO2、KHCO3 等，以加強 ANAMMOX 反應，可視
為創新之程序。許多 BNR 系統第 1 段為厭氧段 (例如 A2O)，其主要目的為厭氧釋磷。然
而，當進流水中含有硝酸氮的情況下，將使得厭氧段轉變成缺氧環境，而影響除磷功能。
本研究提出創新的 EABD-A 程序與傳統 BNR 之差異為：於第 1 段的缺氧槽中加入生物膜
擔體，可增加微生物之停留時間，避免生長速率較低的 ANAMMOX 族群遭到 wash out。
在好氧槽的硝化上澄液，藉由外部循環回到缺氧槽。由於硝化上澄液中含有亞硝酸氮，與
進流水中之氨氮，以及脫硝所產生的無機碳源作用，應可行 ANAMMOX 反應。故本研究
認為，XAOB、XNOB、XAB 之反應途徑如圖 3.1 所示。

4.4 檢驗方法 
1. 檢驗項目 
檢驗項目可分為線上監測記錄以及採樣檢驗數據。線上監測記錄包括 DO、pH 值、氧
化還原電位 (ORP)。採樣檢驗項目包括 COD、NH4+、NO3-、NO2-、PO43-、懸浮固體物 
(suspended solids, SS)、SVI (sludge volume index) 等。 
2. 各實驗項目之檢驗方法 
分析項目中少部份檢驗項目如 DO、pH 值、ORP 於實驗進行中即進行記錄外，其他檢
驗項目則於實驗後進行之。採樣檢驗項目包括 COD、NH4+、NO3-、NO2-、PO43-、SS 等。
揮發性脂肪酸的測定則藉由滴定法進行分析。所有的分析方法則參考 Standard Methods 
(APHA, 1995)。 
 
4.5 模式架構及演算 
本研究之模式模擬，以下列 3 項模式為主要工具：(1) TWEA1 (Pai, 2007; Pai et al., 2009a, 
b, c, d, e)，(2) ANAMMOX 模式(Koch et al., 2002)，(3) Biofilm model (Pai et al., 2004)。在
缺氧槽中，參與作用的微生物包括 XH、HPAO、XAB，且分為懸浮性及附著性微生物，因此
必須採用 TWEA1 的厭氧程序速率式、ANAMMOX 模式、Biofilm model 等 3 項模式進行
模擬。在厭氧槽中，參與作用的微生物包括 XH、XPAO，因此採用 TWEA1 的厭氧程序速
率式。在好氧槽中，參與作用的微生物包括 XH、XPAO、XAOB、XNOB，因此必須採用 TWEA1 
的好氧程序速率式。

The development of irrigation canals and its influence on agricultural 
society in Taiwan from 1624 
 
Yi-Ti Tung *, ** and Tzu-Yi Pai ***

Taichung, 40201, Taiwan

Abstract In this study, the development of irrigation canals and its influence on 
agricultural society in Taiwan from 1624 were surveyed. During Holland-Spain Period 
and Ming-Cheng Period (1624 – 1683), only ponds and small scale irrigation canals 
were built up. In Qing Dynasty (1683 – 1895), four important irrigation canals including 
Babao Canal, Babuza Canal, Liukon Canal, and Tsaokon Canal were established. 
During Japanese Occupation Period (1895 – 1945), Taoyuan Canal and Chia-Nan Canal 
were established. After Taiwan Retrocession, the representative Shihmen Canal was 
constructed. Through the spatial cognition of irrigation system, different agricultural 
social groups formed their own life style, characteristics, and even culture. In addition, 
the irrigation range enforced the identity and territoriality of the social group. From 
Qing Dynasty, irrigation canal also played a main role in social agricultural construction 
and classification of social strata. 
 
Keywords Taiwan, irrigation canals, agricultural society, social strata, space cognition 
 
Introduction 
Like the blood vessels sending oxygen and nutrients throughout the human body, 
irrigation canals transport essential agricultural water resource across the field for ages. 
These canals enhance the agricultural productivity by providing stable water supply for 
the area where precipitation is low and unstable. After hundred years of operation, the 
irrigation canal system makes major contributions to the development of Taiwan 
agricultural economy. These investments to the irrigation infrastructures establish a 
concrete foundation for rapid growth of agricultural production as well as commercial 
and industrial activities. 
In addition to the function of supplying agricultural water resource, these irrigation 
system and irrigated land also provide ecological services including clean water, energy 
regulation, climate balance, and nutrient recycling. These systems also provide habitats 
in which organisms live and corridors that connect two adjacent habitats to allow 
migration of organisms from one to another (Wade, 1995; Lam, 2006). The irrigation 
canals and fields joint system prevent the soil from erosion. For example, some rice 
paddies in southeastern Asia have been cultivated for hundreds or even thousands of 
years without any significant loss of productivity (Cunningham and Cunningham, 
2008). 
In order to distribute the water resources, agricultural citizens living in the same village 
have to keep connecting with each other regularly for ensuring everyone, from the 
origin to the end of the river, will be able to share the water all together. The irrigation 
canals not only breed the surround of village, but also nourish the public relationship. 
The irrigation canals establish the social relationship, territorial space, and even the

Because the Qing government intended to enforce cultivation affairs in Taiwan, General 
Shih-Bang Shih proposed a project for establishing an irrigation canal at Changhua 
Plain of middle Taiwan in 1709. Ten years later, i.e. 1719, this canal was finished and 
called Babao Canal in which the water from Choushui River was taken for irrigation. 
More than 20,000 hectares of farmlands were irrigated by Babao Canal (Fig. 1). From 
that time, Changhua area was famous because of mass production of rice. Until now, the 
Babao Canal with total length of 566 km still works for breeding people in the middle 
Taiwan. In addition to middle Taiwan, the Qing government also enhanced the 
cultivation affairs in northern Taiwan. In 1718, some immigrants from Mainland 
collected capital to build up a canal. Several years later, Longen Canal was established 
for irrigating Hsinchu area. Another ancient irrigation canal was Tonler Canal which 
was built up in the same period for irrigating Tainan area of southern Taiwan.

During the Qing Dynasty, the settlers increased significantly. In addition to the Babao

In middle Taiwan, one settler of Han people negotiated with the chief of aboriginal 
people and built up a canal for irrigating Taichung area in 1723. Therefore, this canal 
was called Babuza which was the name of that aboriginal people tribe.

During Japanese Occupation Period, Taoyuan Canal and Chia-Nan Canal were two 
important irrigation canals for irrigating Taoyuan Plateau and Chia-Nan Plain, 
respectively. 
In 1912, Taoyuan Plateau of northern Taiwan suffered the most sever drought among the 
past 50 years. The Japanese and local farmers cooperated to establish the Taoyuan Canal 
and finished in 1924. A famous Japanese engineer, Yoichi Hatta, designed this canal 
which irrigated more than 22,000 hectares of farmlands. 
Yoichi Hatta also involved in planning and design of the Chia-Nan Canal and 
Wushantou Dam in southern Taiwan. The Chia-Nan Canal was finished in 1930 and 
became the largest agricultural hydraulic facility of Asia at that time.

During the Japanese Occupation Period, the Japanese government assembled the private 
irrigation canals and organized the public irrigation canal systems called “Hydraulic 
Assemble”. The government invested a part of finance for establishing irrigation canals 
meanwhile a part of finance was still supported by private farmers. Its goal was to 
optimize the management of public irrigation and supervise agricultural community 
welfare and development. The organization was enormous, with branches set up in each 
county government. 
After Taiwan Retrocession, the “Hydraulic Assemble” was reorganized as “Hydraulic 
Committee”. After 1956, the “Hydraulic Committee” underwent another re-organization, 
becoming “Irrigation Association” to handle liaison and collaborative affairs. To more 
effectively utilize irrigation waters, 18 Irrigation Associations are set up around Taiwan 
presently to supervise water supplies, maintain irrigation facilities and drainage systems 
within each of their jurisdictions for farmland irrigation. Fig.4 shows the distribution of 
Irrigation Association presently in Taiwan.

The irrigation systems in Taiwan are reportedly among the most effective in the world 
(Weissing and Ostrom, 2000). Lam (1996, 2001) examined the successful experience of 
irrigation governance and management in Taiwan as a means of understanding how 
joint efforts could be established and sustained through institutional arrangements. The 
results indicated several principles for institutional design, including a careful definition 
of the scope of farmers’ participation, complementarity of interests between individuals, 
reduction of asymmetries involved in the use of authority, and the existence of domains 
of autonomy are identified as instrumental to the success in Taiwan.
