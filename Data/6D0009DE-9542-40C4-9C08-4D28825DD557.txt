1.4. 光、改變空間視覺上的形式 
    美國 70年代以來最别開生面的光與空間的藝術藝術家，詹姆斯·特瑞爾
（James Turrell），在最極簡主義的美學中，建立光線層次變化的展現，經由「空
間環境感覺」在科學及心理學感知上，分析各種不同空間及光線相互之影響，他
的作品為在既定的空間框架內，利用光的對比與色彩在視覺上的引導，改變空間
框架在視覺上的輪郭，在一 10×5×4m 的空間中對牆面天花及地板投影藍色的光
(figure11)，使空間的每一面之間的輪廓因為色彩的一致而消失，三維的空間在視
覺失去了第三的維度，也就是空間的深度，而呈現二維的平面畫面(figure 12)，
此外 James Turrell 也試圖利用光在牆面做色彩的分割，如圖所示，在一個狹長的
走廊上，用色彩模糊其邊界，空間的軸線因顏色重新定義其位置，使走廊的空間
尺度在視覺上被擴寬(figure13)。 
    1967 年「Alta(White)」，是經由光線投射在對角牆及地面所形成立面三角形
之環境空間裝置作品，強烈的光使人眼忽略空間牆體的存在感而專注於幾何的發
光形體，使平面投影於牆角的平面圖形，看似像五面體存在於空間之中(figure 
14)(fugure 15)，James Turrell 的作品表現出實體空間的空間造型，是可以利用光
來改變其輪廓，在視覺上產生空間尺度的改變。 
2. 空間超連結 
建築空間滿足了人類居住上隱私的需求，實體的牆面阻斷了外界窺視我們的
生活之同時，也阻斷我們對外界環境的感知途徑，在實體空間中，開窗似乎是提
供屋子內的人與外界連結的最佳方案，隨著人類群居密度越來越高，開一扇窗，
看見喜歡的景色的機會越來越少，窗戶變成了室內通風採光的工具，人們變得不
太在乎窗外所看見的景色，更在乎的是生活的隱私不因開窗位置的錯誤而暴露於
外在的環境。此外，室內空間配置也受到「可開窗位置」所牽制，空間越靠近建
築核心位置越是封閉，因失去與外部自然環境連結的機會而成了建築裡負面空
間，而必須在這類型空間作業的人也無法自然而然的感受到時間與環境的變化。 
    當電腦資訊技術普遍的存在於我們生活環境之中，資訊技術打破了時間、空
間的隔閡，人類對空間資訊的認知也逐漸的受到電腦資訊傳遞技術的進步而有所
改變，過去十年人們已經透過電腦設備進行人、時間、空間的連結，透過觀看過
去的紀錄影像可以使現在的人與過去的人們產生連結，我們靠時鐘了解時間的變
化，網路傳遞儀器所偵測到之氣象數據使我們得知或者預知外面的氣候環境，網
路視訊的傳遞更可以使兩個遠端的人們面對面的溝通，這些資訊充斥在我們所處
的空間中，成為人們感受外部環境與遠端空間的習慣方式。 
空間超連結的概念是以視訊的影像，結合空間設計的手法，讓遠端或外部環
境的資訊可以合理的成為所處空間之一部分，如同我們透過窗戶看見外面的環
境，因為窗外的植物延伸進入屋內，或是風和光的導入，使我們感覺到外面空間
的存在，而不是看見平面的畫面，virtual window的裝置(figure 16)(figure 17)，將
大型顯示器與窗戶結合，窗框距離顯示器一個窗台的距離，窗台上刻意擺了個盆
栽，顯示器的畫面為大樓窗外透過視訊攝影機所看到的市區景象，影像裡置入一
顆 3D虛擬的植栽，當人的觀看位置改變時，我看見的景象也會跟著改變，而 3D
的虛擬植栽透過臉部偵測，亦會隨著觀看者位置的改變，而呈現對角線的移動，
因此，大樓的影像、植栽與窗框的位置錯位使原本平面的影像有了空間的深度，
而窗戶內部設有風扇，當風重窗戶吹出時觀看使觀看者有觸覺上的感受，窗框上
的光影暗示了光線的方向是從外面傳的入室內的，將這些因素結合成為空間設計
的手法，使觀看者感受到平面畫面中的空間是存在的。 
兩個分隔的空間，可以藉由共通的元素之延伸入彼此空間，使兩個空間產生
延續的關係， (Figure 18)(Figure 19)為一個已空間超連結為概念的裝置藝術作品
(巫明璋 鄭世民 2007)，將兩空間之視訊影像利用”梯子”串連，梯子即為一種空
間設計的手法，兩端空間皆有一座實體的梯子，並且延伸進入彼此虛擬視訊影像
中，並且墊高地板的厚度，強調地板後方空間的存在，利用其中一端為長走廊的
空間特性，使其成為地板開口中的空間。

3.1用語定義 
 輸入影像(圖形) : 指的是輸入電腦的圖形影像。 
 投影影像(圖形) : 投影機將輸入電腦的圖形影像投影於空間中之影像圖形。 
 變形修正影像(圖形) : 輸入電腦的圖形影像，依據投影機所投影的影像圖形
結果對輸入之圖形做修正後所得到之圖形。 
 投影面: 投影機投影影像的物質表面。 
 輸入之 RGB值 : 再針對輸入電腦的影像之 RGB值調整所輸入之值。 
 輸出之 RGB值 : 圖形影像投影於物體表面後，透過攝影機所偵測到之 RGB
值。

意選擇我們喜好的景色，並且裝上虛擬的窗框，與實體的窗台，而兩者的存在使灑入室內的光斑產生
了陰影，陰影變化不但強調光線的方向的，且更加強調太陽的位置，而光斑形狀及位置的變化則顯示
光線是會隨著時間而變化的(figure35)，使室內空間的人們自然而然的感受時間的改變，窗框上會有飛
鳥的停駐，因為窗外正是一片美好的自然環境。

四、 結論 
 
   窗戶、除是建築空間與空間之間連結的媒介，我們需要建築圍塑來定義我們的生活領域，但更需要
窗戶讓我們感受外界的氛圍。藉由窗戶可以提升我們生理及心理上的健康(Moore, 1981; Ulrich, 1984).
而在建築空間中人們大多也喜歡有開窗的空間(Kaplan, 2001)。就空間設計師的立場而言，創造空間是
空間設計的目標。而創造空間的方法與程度則受到時代與的科技的影響。因此在資訊科技發達的現在，
在探討空間形式設計已不再單方面侷限於其人與其所處的空間，而是更進一步探討人與遠端的空間設
計。 
   當然，透過資訊技術來作為空間設計的手段意味著空間中我們所見的不一定是真實的，人們一直認
為眼前所見物體存在是因為真實的存在。然而空間中所見的皆為光影在物體上反射之後所呈現的成
像。本研究現階段成果以透過光影改變空間實體結構表面之色彩，並結合空間元素的暗示使虛擬影像
空間存在于實體的空間中。成果展示一種新時代的空間設計方法，突破以往實體的空間裝修的週期。
本研究可以分為設計技術與科技技術的兩的層面探討，而目前所完成的部分主要為設計技術階段。 
    在設計技術方面，本研究呈現透過投影機再實體空間牆面或天花板上開啓一開口，裝上一扇窗戶。
穿戶外的空間可以是一個我們喜愛的空間，一段我們喜愛的回憶影片或者是遠端家人的客廳即時影
像。而更重要的是透過空間元素的表現，例如經過透視變形設計過後的窗框、牆壁斷面的厚度，讓我
們感覺開口結構的合理存在，而窗外的陽光或鳥、植栽攀爬進入室內暗示我們視覺上窗框裡的空間是
可及的。更重要的是虛實結合的掌握，使真實空間與虛擬空間真實串聯一起。   
    而在科技技術方面為本研究後續可以繼續深入的方向，針對透視變型影像如何在各種視角皆能夠
合理的隨著觀看者即時變形，使觀看位置不受角度上的限制，亦能透過互動技術及時偵測觀看者位置
及視覺方向，改變其樣貌達到互動的效果。此外，讓透過資訊進行空間設計的概念落實到軟體設計的
界面，使空間設計者可以容易操控軟體在空間中進行空間設計，使空間設計不再以實體破壞性的裝修
為主，漸漸地局部由虛擬的投影影像取代為後續發展的目標。

  Abstract- Where to dwell has been a question since the 
beginning of human existence.  In this paper we return to this 
everlasting issue because rapid advances in information 
technology have created bright new avenues for spatial design. 
Technology can be the means to create new dwelling spaces.  
The question is how new technology can lead us to better lives. 
     We propose a spatial hyperlink which integrates 
information technology with spatial design to create a virtual 
connection between spaces.  The objective is to create a sense of 
connection for people who live separately, to make it possible for 
people who live in enclosed spaces to connect with nature, and to 
connect  people’s  past, present and future territorial identities.  
Two design cases are presented to demonstrate how spatial 
hyperlink works.  The first case creates a place that connects 
people who use it at different times.  The second case creates a 
place that connects people to nature. 
 
I.    INTRODUCTION 
A.     Spatial hyperlink  
Following Norberg-Schulz’ (1980) [1] concept of the 
“existential place” in architectural phenomenology, 
architecture should be directed to the “meaning” as well as the 
physical properties of the environment. Rather than being 
solely an abstraction or mental construction, a “dwelling” can 
be associated with the emotional experience of mortals on this 
earth, connecting them with the earth, the sky, and the divine, 
bringing all together into one harmonious whole [2]. In his 
review of a broad range of approaches to “place making”, 
Sime (1983) [3] raised the question of whether we are 
“Creating places or designing spaces?” when we bring 
architects, planners and researchers together to integrate 
design and research issues. Where to dwell has been a 
question since the beginning of human existence.  In this 
paper we return to this everlasting issue because rapid 
advances in information technology have created bright new 
avenues for spatial design. Technology can be the means to 
create new dwelling spaces.  The question is “what kind of 
new dwelling spaces do we wish to create?” 
 
B.    Place, dwelling and information 
 A “dwelling”, for its occupants, is a space with meaning. 
There is a link between that particular location with 
experience and emotion. To make a space one’s dwelling 
means to remain there, to stay in place, free of fear and danger 
[2].  A dwelling is not merely a shelter built for safety at one 
specific moment.  A dwelling provides protection and shelter 
in the future. Information which links the present to past 
experience and to one’s predictions for the future is the key to 
establishing a sense of security. Information is also the 
foundation of social relations, without which an isolated 
person would have little chance of survival.  The need for a 
safe dwelling is deeply rooted in our evolutionary emotions 
and intuitions.  Modern technology may provide us with 
physical safety and comfort, but it may not answer our need to 
feel that where we live is our “dwelling”.  Places have to be 
seen as wholes (Norberg-Schulz, 1980.)  Creating a space 
which has the feeling of being a “dwelling” involves more 
than measurements and analysis. To create that feeling, spatial 
designers in the era of information technology need to think 
less the way scientists and engineers think, and more with the 
inspiration of poets. 
    A place is defined by things that express meaning, or in 
other words, things that we perceive and interpret based on the 
information which we associate with them.   A person 
defines the place where they are situated by what they can see, 
hear and touch.  Spatial designers are information engineers 
who manipulate information flow by building walls and 
windows to arrange for the passage of light and sound.  The 
way they configure space defines the communication patterns 
between the space’s occupants. The circulation patterns which 
the space allows establishes the probability of chance 
encounters and the ease of social engagement. A space also 
creates links to the past and to the future, for any activity in 
the space may leave physical traces that carry information 
across time. 
     For thousands of years humans have defined the 
relationship between themselves and the earth, the sky and 
eternity by the way they built the walls and roofs of their 
dwellings.  From the viewpoint of information engineers 
these structural elements served to filter the flow of 
information transmitted by light and sound, to create unique 
insulated environments. The configuration of entrances, spaces 
and passages also fostered or discouraged social engagement. 
The age of information technology gives modern spatial 
designers new tools not only to connect dwellers to each other 
in their own space, but also to connect them with spaces which

A.    Project 1: Locus — dining to gather 
   Spatial Hyperlink creates a new way to communicate.  
Instead of sending messages to other people, we can leave 
messages in physical contexts to indirectly communicate with 
other people.  
 
 
 
Fig.2 Locus- people who cannot have dinner together dine together 
 
The objective of this project was to create a feeling of living 
together for family members who cannot synchronize their 
daily schedules with one another.  Traditionally, the dining 
table is a place which facilitates family union.  In modern 
society it is more difficult to bring all family members 
together for dinner at the same time.  In this project the 
shadows of people and dishes engaged in a previous activity 
show up to join someone sitting alone in front of the dining 
table.  At the same time, a sound recording of the previous 
activity is played to create the atmosphere of being together.  
The current activity also leaves a new shadow and sound 
recording for whoever sits at the table next.  Fig. 2 shows a 
person eating with the shadows of people and objects from the 
previous activity. 
 
 
 
Fig.3 Sequence of activites 
Our feelings, perceptions, memories of our home can be 
shaped by seeing the ‘locus” (the previous shadow recording) 
which has been left on the table.  The purpose of this project 
was not to transfer direct information, but to assemble the 
right kinds of spatial atmospheres for the space where 
communication takes place. 
    Fig.4 shows the installation of the dining table.  The 
installation consists of a dining table with silhouetted videos 
of prior activities projected onto the table top to create the 
feeling of being together with the past and future selves of 
other family members who are in another time and/or space.
