英文摘要 
This project targets on development of intelligent and tele-operated cranes for 
improving safety and efficiency of construction erection operations through 
multi-disciplinary research collaboration. The multi-disciplinary technologies 
integrated involve tele-operation of construction cranes, augmented reality, and 
automatic motion planning.  
The goal of the first year (totally three years) is on development and integration of a 
laboratory crane and tele-operation platform, including both hardware and software 
aspects. An industrial robot arm (KUKA CR 16) has been integrated as a prototype 
crane. By mounting IP cameras on the left, right, global and top sides of this robot 
arm, the environment information around the arm can be retrieved and multiple view 
angles of the rigging objects can be covered. Also, for providing operation guidance 
to operators, this work utilizes Augmented Reality and Motion Planning technologies 
to generate a virtual path which is then registered on the images from the cameras. 
With the guidance information and multiple screens display, participants attended in 
usability tests provided positive feedbacks and consider the research directions are 
worthy to promote. 
The goal of the second year is on system integration and test. Beside the integration of 
hardware platform already finished in the first year, a method has also been provided 
for classifying crane operation information into three types of views: focused view, 
ambient view, and alert view, which are displayed in the user interface for 
tele-operators. According to the result of the usability test conducted this year, it is 
proved that the possibility of collisions during erection activities can be decreased 
using the proposed user interface and the information classification strategy used for 
assisting tele-operations can effectively reduce the information loading of the 
operators while the operation efficiency is maintained. 
The goal of the third year is on erection scenario studies. By conducting usability test 
for crane operators, the feasibility of implementing the proposed interface at the field 
is identified. Most of crane operators completed test tasks with fewer errors than that 
done by students. And they provided positive feedbacks and suggestions especially on 
augmented rigging paths. Based on these suggestions, we improved the algorithms for 
generating rigging paths by considering three feasibility factors: Visibility, Control 
Features and Dynamic Environment. A virtual environment for evaluating the 
efficiency of developed algorithms has been built. It proved that developed algorithms 
can generate rigging paths in real-time for users to make decisions efficiently.

1 前言 
在現今的工程建設中，由於設備規模的日益提升與預鑄工法的盛行，大多數
的案例使用吊車機具進行工項的施作，使吊車成為工地最常使用的施工資源   
（Liu, 1995），此類施工方式，需要處理吊裝作業的規劃與實行，由實務角度來
看，吊裝作業扮演著關鍵性的角色，尤其在預鑄工法與鋼構件的案例中，約有
27%的總工程成本直接或間接地與其有關（Carter, et. al. 2000），諸如工地材料、
設備與元件的安裝和運輸，都是藉由吊裝機具才得以快速完成，並且作業的內容
通常屬於危險性較高、工程結構的主要構件等等重大工項，因此，吊裝作業所影
響的將是整個工程時程規劃、成本分析與安全考量的主要因素，而如何有效增加
施作效率與安全、減少成本支出，都是吊裝作業研究所需面對的方向。 
近年來，如何有效率地執行吊裝作業並提昇其安全性，已成為施工環境中最
被關心的課題之一，由如何規劃吊裝路徑、吊車型式的選定、工地配置到吊車手
訓練上，皆已發展出許多理論架構及自動化的輔助技術。然而，在改進傳統吊裝
施作形式、整合現今技術至實際操作與實體機具的研究方面，尚有充裕的發展空
間。利用現今的電腦輔助技術，吊裝作業已不再受限於以往的操作模式，除了控
制領域的技術可以加強吊裝操作的穩定性外，諸多在機器人領域中的感知、定位
技術也具有極大的應用潛力，可用來輔助操作人員更加快速地了解工地環境，進
而做出更正確有效的判斷來，若再加入上述吊裝規劃技術，更能將吊裝作業由吊
車手進行繁複的操作與經驗判斷，提昇至更安全、更有效率的施工方式，此即為
本計畫之緣起。 
2 研究目的 
為增加吊裝作業的施作安全，降低工程施工時產生危害的機率，並且延伸應
用於特殊吊裝情境中，本研究將發展在吊裝過程中，提供吊裝輔助及環境感知等
額外資訊的智慧型遠端控制平台，藉由機器人視覺感知技術與運動規劃的演算法，
提供遠端操作環境進行吊裝作業，系統將呈現施工場景影像，結合感測資訊提供
操作者更多元的資訊，加上自動化的路徑指示，來協助吊車手在操作吊車時進行
更精準、更具效率的定位與移動，避免因為動態工地的不確定性，以及吊車手的
錯誤判斷，產生對於公共安全的重大危害。

3.3 視覺化輔助技術 
視覺化輔助技術領域的進展日新月異，值得注意的發展如擴增實境技術
（Augmented Reality，簡稱 AR），其意指運用攝影機（或其他視覺感知設備）
視點內的幾何資訊來分析感測環境，以獲得除了影像光譜屬性外的額外資訊，進
而輔以虛擬資訊進行呈現的相關技術，諸如影像中物體位置（Localization）、攝
影環境三維重建（Klein and Murray, 2007）、及虛擬物件於真實環境貼合（Mapping）
等等，這些研究中的視覺資訊不是只由感光元件反映的影像資訊而已，而是附加
了經過分析、運算後，所得到的定位資訊，並將額外的虛擬物件放進去的結果，
目前可見的應用如視覺化教學、設備操作即時說明及施工過程展示等等，如圖
3-4 列出的使用情境，在辦公桌與停車場等真實影像中，置入了虛擬的動畫人物
及塔吊，會隨著鏡頭移動而不斷更新位置，呈現猶如真實存在的視覺感受。

3.5 吊裝路徑規劃 
目前熱門的吊裝路徑規劃作法是使用到運動規劃（Motion Planning）技術，
Kang 與 Miranda（2006）首先利用此技術即時產生吊裝路徑於虛擬環境中，作為
吊裝規劃的先行模擬之用。而當這樣的模擬情境或產生的吊裝路徑越真實，這些
資源便越能在實際施工前發揮沙盤推演的效用（Hung and Kang, 2009）。 
在這路徑規劃技術的領域中，四大方法是最常被使用的，第一種為潛在力場
域法（Potential Field Method），其方法將空間視為一個力場作用的區域，目標
終點位置具有吸引力、障礙物則具有排斥力，如此可透過力的加總，讓欲移動至
終點的物件依時間和位置產生相對應的路徑（Khatib, 1986；Song and Kumar, 2002；
Daily and Bevly, 2008）；第二種方法則是基因演算法（Genetic Algorithm），利
用每種路徑解之間互相的配對、變種與錯置來產生子代路徑解，而經過評估若呈
現較好效果的路徑解則保留下來，以此持續產生更多子代解來（Goldberg and 
Holland, 1988；Melanie, 1998）；而第三種方法則稱為可視性圖解法（Visibility 
Graph Method，Liu, 1992；Oommen, 1987），將障礙物視為路徑點（Path Node）
並以之連接來找出路徑解，此方法的限制在於障礙物不是能平滑的（Smooth）；
最後，第四種方法是機率地圖法（Probabilistic Roadmap Method，簡稱 PRM）
（Latombe, 1996；Choset et al., 2005），此方法經常利用於機器人領域中，是先
在空間中進行點位的取樣，將點位間做連結成圖，再來，檢視每兩點間的連線，
相連後找尋是否有最佳的路徑解，此方法在本研究亦被使用，其以被證實是非常
有效率的路徑規劃法（Ladd, 2004）。

4.2 多視角遠端操控 
本系統架構中的工地資訊觀測器由複數攝影機組成，用來提供多角度的吊裝

4.4 以注意力為主之吊裝資訊分類 
結合工程規劃器所提供的吊裝資訊，本研究在介面呈現上考慮注意力集中程
度的人類因素，提出以注意力為主的系統介面，透過將吊裝所需的相關資訊進行
分類呈現的動作，讓需要不同注意力程度的各項資訊皆以不同方式呈現，希望達
到減輕人感知上的負擔及提昇操作效率的效果。 
如表 4-1 所示，本研究初步將吊裝所需的相關資訊歸納為兩大種類：吊裝過
程資訊及吊裝限制資訊。吊裝限制資訊指所有會減緩、妨礙吊裝進行而必須注意
的資訊，諸如：吊車工作範圍、荷重能力、碰撞情況及人員安全等等；吊裝過程
資訊則泛指除了吊裝限制資訊以外，吊車手能依憑來完成任務的所有相關資訊，

注意力相互影響的曲線分布如圖 4-5 所示，本研究將此曲線切割為三部分用
以將吊裝資訊歸類為：持續注意型、即時參照型及警示型。第一類吊裝資訊屬於
持續注意型（Focus View），代表其訊息需持續顯示於吊裝過程中，吊車手之施
作將以此為主並時時刻刻注意著，此類資訊便如工地實際影像、各角度吊物影像
等等；第二類為即時參照型（Ambient View）資訊，該類資訊多半為吊車手需要
時再行取得即可的輔助訊息，像是吊車姿態、吊物特性等等；而最特殊的算是第
三類資訊：警示型（Alert View），吊裝限制的資訊多屬此類，平時以即時參照
的方式呈現，而在狀況緊急的時刻，將具有取得轉移吊車手全副注意力的顯著改
變，諸如，當吊物太過接近周遭建物或瞬時風速過大等情況發生時，此類資訊會
以警示形式展現來吸引吊車手的注意。表 4-1 的資訊分類便是以此方式進行，並
且分別決定優先順序。

吊車手可視性（Visibility） 
 在遠端操控環境下，吊車手可能因為感知設備（例如攝影機）架設位置的不
同，產生不同程度上的視覺死角，或是因為遠距離因素而造成對實際施工環境的
疏離感，因此，吊裝的指導路徑若是產生在這些不易讓吊車手做判斷的位置上，
將實際降低吊車手參考與利用的意願，也等於發揮不了實際的作用，例如圖 4-8
的狀況，即使吊車手實際上在現地進行操作，他的視線亦會被周圍的建築物或障
礙物所阻擋，產生視覺上無法獲取資訊的死角。 
是故，本研究發展的演算法針對可視性問題進行判斷，考慮到路徑產生的即
時性與效率，採用被動的做法，利用 PRM 算出吊裝路徑後，路徑上的每個點位
都會與遠端攝影機（若非遠端操控的情況，則為吊車手所在的駕駛艙）的位置進
行光線追蹤（Ray Tracing）測試，倘若光線遭到遮蔽或是無法被複數以上的攝影
機所追蹤到，亦即視線有所阻礙或無法令吊車手產生對環境的立體感，則藉由
PRM 重新選取新的路徑，其中設定一個重新找尋次數的門檻值α，到達門檻值
後尚未找到合適的路徑，即放棄找尋並告知使用者在該工地環境的複雜度下，無
法提供路徑引導，此時使用者可控制調整遠端攝影機的位置並再次嘗試。

動態環境（Dynamic Environment） 
施工環境的複雜程度與動態特性，對於吊裝行為有極大的影響，吊車手必須
依據環境的變化更改既定的吊裝路徑，以經驗及駕駛技術來完成吊裝任務，在遠
端控制的吊裝行為中，這項能力便更加重要了，工地中的人物、車輛等等皆是會
移動的個體，程式產生的吊裝路徑指示若不具備有隨著環境狀況更新的即時性
（Real-Time），很容易便不能在實際情況中派上用場。 
為了達成路徑及時更新的特性，需考慮在卡式座標中會隨時間改變位置的障
礙物，但由於PRM在程式執行之初便花時間將整個C-Space及路徑圖建構完成，
而此步驟不具有重新建構的即時性，故若要因為工地中局部物體的移動，而在每
個時間點（Time Step）下更新整個 C-Space 是無法即時做到的事情，在此，本研
究提出一種利用八元樹（Octree）空間結構來局部更新 C-Space 的方法，其使用
方式（PartialUpdate）如表 4-7 所示。 
八元樹空間的架構是將整個空間做切割，並以階層關係的方式相互連結，以
卡式座標空間為例，整個空間可視為是八元樹的根節點（Root Tree Node），分
別在其空間中的Ｘ、Ｙ及 Z 軸上一分為二，切割為八個子空間，每個子空間由一
個子節點（Child Tree Node）代表，再對每個節點空間進行一樣的切割動作，以
此類推地增長這棵八元樹直到預期的子空間大小為止。

6 測試驗證

 現行介面（Conventional Interface）：由現今塔吊操作介面上可知，鳥瞰視

 介面 A（User Interface A）：這是本研究提出的兩種新型態操作介面之一，
在此介面下，受測者將能看到四種不同角度的模擬場景影像，這些影像分別
來自架設於機械手臂的頂端、左側與右側攝影機，也包含一台可看見整體機
械手臂與模擬場景的廣域攝影機，這些攝影機將欲吊裝物的各個角度包覆，
提供受測者避開視線死角的資訊，在利用這個介面施作的同時，工作人員是
不會提供口頭指示的，受測者需自行利用多視角的視覺資源來完成任務。 
 介面 B（User Interface）：此介面是本研究提出的另一個方案，其視覺資源
包含介面 A 的四個視角，並在視覺影像上面提供對應位置的虛擬吊裝路徑
建議，該資訊由 AR 技術進行定位，動態地呈現在頂端視角的影像中，隨著
受測者操作手臂的移動，此路徑也會跟著視角移動而改變，就如同存在於真
實世界中一般。除了路徑建議以外，利用 AR 定位的資訊，本研究也可判斷
出吊物目前所在的高程資訊，亦將該資訊以及目標地的高程資訊一併提供給
受測者，讓受測者可依據精確數值的資訊來判斷現在吊裝高程是否容易發生
碰撞。與介面 A 相同之處在於使用本介面進行任務時，工作人員並不會以
口頭提供指示，一切由受測者以遠端視角並沿著 AR 路徑去完成吊裝任務。

7.2 以注意力為主介面之使用性測試結果討論 
在計畫的第二年度，本研究進行了以注意力為主介面的使用性測試，整體測
試結果分述於下列圖表中。圖 7-4為 30名受測者完成任務所花費的時間分布圖，
由結果來看，僅使用多視角（Only Camera Views）介面的平均花費時間為 275.5
秒（標準差 65.5），而使用以注意力為主介面的平均花費時間則為 297.6 秒（標
準差 63.9），代表利用多視角介面完成任務較有效率，但以統計顯著性來看
（t=-2.03，p=0.52），兩種介面的花費時間並未達到顯著地不同。 
依據結果來看，多視角介面的效率較高，這並未是本測試所預期的結果，可
能的原因在於多視角介面提供的資訊量較少，受測者並不需要去花費精神解讀視
覺以外的訊息或是受到警示訊息影響，因為這些訊息並沒有提供在介面上，相對
而言，使用以注意力為主之介面便需注意到這些資訊，做出對應於警示訊息的動
作，來避免發生安全意外，並且在測試過程中也發現，許多受測者嘗試依照吊裝
路徑建議去施作，一邊參照一邊動作的速度也較為緩慢，比較起來是較花時間的
行為，但安全性也因此提高（安全性部分可參考下個項目的結果討論），故在花
費時間上會有此現象發生。然而，此現象並未具有顯著性，相信在更複雜的模擬
吊裝任務中，視覺以外的資訊需求增加時，情形便有可能會反過來了。

在技術驗證（第一次）測試中，可發現受測者使用多視角與 AR 輔助的介面
來完成模擬吊裝任務所花費時間，比使用現行介面為短，平均是 341（多視角）、

在測試後的訪談中，受測者認為多視角資訊在遠端系統內有其必要性，且 AR 技
術提供的吊裝路徑建議與高程資訊也十分有用，雖然相對於多視角來看，提昇的
效果較不明顯，但有多數認為有其發展的價值並值得改進。 
在以注意力為主介面的測試（第二次）結果中，使用以注意力為主介面所花
費的時間較多視角介面略高（297.6 秒與 275.5 秒），但其差別在統計上不顯著，
可知雖然資訊量增加但並不怎麼加重受測者的負擔，再者，以能否降低不安全吊
裝行為的發生機率來看，以注意力為主介面便發揮效用了，其對比於多視角介面，
能有效降低%的不安全行為，可以看出將資訊進行分類後，更能有效地為受測者
應用與理解，在各資訊類型使用率與吊裝流程間，也看出一定的規律性，如起吊
或放置階段，受測者會較頻繁地使用即時參照型資訊，而在一般情形下，則僅使
用持續注意型資訊而已，這與本研究預期之現象符合。 
在吊車手（第三次）的測試中，本研究將其結果與學生的部份進行比較，發
現吊車手在施作所花費的時間較學生略短，但操作的正確性明顯增加，其發生不
安全行為的機會更加降低了，以測試和訪談結果來看，吊車手給予系統正面的評
價，無論在多視角、AR 技術或是整體系統上，皆認為其具有導入現地使用的潛
力，模型平台也被認為是合於模擬真實吊裝環境，但在吊裝路徑建議這項資訊上，
認為其使用性不高，因為並不符合吊車手的操作特性，這是可以再行改進的地
方。 
依照吊車手測試最後的改進建議，本研究針對吊裝路徑的規劃演算法追加了
改進設計，提出三種可行路徑的因素：可視性（Visibility）、駕駛特徵（Control 
Feature）及動態環境（Dynamic Environment），考慮上述三項因素來改進系統
中的路徑規劃演算法，該演算法已利用新建置的虛擬環境平台進行效果展示與效
能測試，考量到工地環境的複雜性，在應因動態物件位置的改變，而需重新規劃
路徑的速度上，本演算法平均可在 161 毫秒內完成，符合吊車手認為在工地真正
可行的路徑建議。

紀：另外問一下，你碰過或聽過吊車發生意外通常是甚麼情況？ 
林：如果用行走式的吊車來講的話，很多都是地面鬆軟造成吊車下
陷，工地現場有很多地方的地面可能是回填土，在硬土與回填土邊
界的地方，吊車開上去就有可能坍下去，或是左右不平衡，有時候
還是沒吊掛東西的時候地面很正常，等到吊起東西重量增加後，才
陷下去的也有，所以大部分都是因為地基鬆軟造成的。 
紀：那還有沒有其他意外常發生的？ 
林：像是觸電也常發生，一般來講，電線也不是說不容易注意到，
但是即使注意到也很容易發生意外，譬如說在高壓電線附近，規範
上是要離 3m 遠來作業，若是接近到 3m 以下，有可能把吊索吸過
去，吊車內的吊車手不會有事，因為吊車有接地，但是吊車附近的
人就有可能會有危險，我自己就觸電了幾次，但是在危險操作前就
已經先叫附近的人先遠離了，所以也沒發生嚴重的事。 
紀：所以說有時候還是得要危險操作囉？

阿！有時候很難提防的。

Abstract 
This paper proposes an approach for generating real-time rigging path guidance for tele-operated 
cranes. This approach is developed to fulfill both the operational safety and efficiency requirements, 
and the operational feasibility requirements for crane operators. This approach utilizes feedback from 
real crane operators obtained during a pilot test using a prototype of a tele-operated crane in order to 
develop a feasible rigging path guidance system. Firstly, the probabilistic roadmap method (PRM) 
coupled with a sampling-based searching method in configuration space is employed as the core path 
planning algorithm to provide the operator with possible collision-free rigging paths. The paths 
suggested by the PRM are then further modified to account for three operational feasibility factors: 
visibility, control features, and the dynamic environment. This finally leads to paths with workable 
and safe trajectories for tele-operated crane usage. The suggested rigging paths are displayed and re-
planned in a real-time manner, in consideration of the dynamic objects on construction site. 
Furthermore, a validation with a tower crane simulation system in a virtual environment has been 
developed to demonstrate the feasibility and usability of the proposed approach. Further evaluation 
and benchmarking of the approach will be needed in future research. 
Keywords: tele-operation, path planning, probabilistic roadmap method, c-space. 
1 Introduction 
Tele-operation, the remote control of machines, has in recent years been increasingly applied in 
practice and been the subject of research (Bernold, 2007). Tele-operation allows for a reduction of 
human presence at a site, and hence significantly reduces safety risks. An increasing number of 
researchers are focusing on integrating tele-operation technologies in order to improve construction 
efficiency (Wang and Dunston, 2006; Wang and Zhu, 2011). As increasing number of current 
construction projects involve the use of large-scale cranes with a longer associated operation time, 
operational safety and productivity requirements are becoming critical issues that need to be carefully 
addressed (Daniel, 2009). In order to improve the operability of the tele-operated crane, this paper 
focuses on generating feasible rigging path guidance on remote monitors for the operators’ reference. 
During the last two decades, a significant amount of research in the area of rigging path-planning 
problems for construction cranes has been aimed at using computers to automatically generate the 
rigging path solutions. The resulting paths must be guaranteed to be collision-free for safety purposes 
and they must also be used for construction site planning, shared space workload analysis, schedule 
conflict detection, safety and risk identification, and operator training purposes. Sivakumar et al. 
(2003) used heuristic functions to seek out collision-free paths for dual-crane cooperative erection,

Five professional crane operators were recruited and participated in an in-depth user test to verify the 
usability and feasibility of the developed UIs. All the operators held professional licenses from the 
Industrial Safety and Health Association (ISHA) of Taiwan, and each had over ten years of 
experience. The testing times ranged from 63 to 92 minutes. The operators were asked to execute the 
simulated rigging tasks previously designed, and were then interviewed after completing the tasks. 
From the observations of the tests and the subsequent interviews, it was found that one suggestion 
was most often provided by the operators, which was that the virtual rigging path guidance should be 
improved. The path taken during erection depends on the style of each operator. However, all 
operators agreed that paths are not simply composed of straight lines, and must be generated 
according to the natural movement of the crane so that they can be easily followed. Guidance paths if 
possible should not be generated over the heads of field workers or existing buildings, and should be 
kept at low elevations. The view angle can also limit an operator’s depth perception if the guidance 
path is only shown on the top and global views. Considering the feedback from all test participants, it 
was concluded that three feasibility factors need to be considered to improve the current mechanisms 
of path guidance generation: visibility, control features, and the dynamic environment. 
The tower crane was assumed as a rigging platform to develop an improved approach. A 
configuration of the tower crane is given by (r, θ, l), which is a 3-DOF manipulator. The symbol r 
denotes the radius of the boom rotation as well as the current distance between the mast and the 
trolley, θ represents the rotation angle of the tower crane, and l represents its current hoisting length. 
The feasible nodes are generated in three-dimensional C-space using PRM. The A* search algorithm 
(Hart et al., 1972) is used as the path searching method in this research. 
2.2 Visibility 
Viewing limitations of cameras on the tele-operated crane need to be considered and eliminated from 
the C-space, so that usage of a tele-operated crane may be improved. The view of crane operators, 
using conventional construction techniques (i.e., operating in the crane’s cabin) as shown in Figure 2, 
is usually limited or obstructed by the buildings on the site. This makes it impossible to follow path 
instructions that lead behind the buildings on the site, relative to the position of the operator. This type 
of problem may be solved by using extra cameras to monitor the blind spots. However, the depth 
perception of operators may still be lost when there is only single camera surveillance at target region.

In addition to safety concerns, human control capacity must also be considered during crane 
operations. Crane operators can manipulate, at most, two DOFs of a crane simultaneously. For 
example, though manipulations like rotating the boom while hoisting allow for a decreased operation 
time, they are subject to the operators’ perception capacity. However, the paths generated by the PRM 
may not take this factor into consideration and can sometimes involve the manipulation of more than 
two DOFs. This may not be feasible in practice and operators need to create an alternative rigging 
plan by themselves. The value of a path planned for safety may be lost in these kinds of situations. 
To deal with the capacity problem, it is proposed to use a grid-like sampling method in C-space 
when searching for a feasible path. The edges of the graph can only be linked to adjacent nodes. 
Nodes must be linked by creating a rectangular shape for a single DOF configuration, and nodes can 
be linked diagonally for a two-DOF configuration. The path found on this graph is guaranteed to be 
within the capacity of human manipulation to perform by using combinations of the control sticks. 
2.4 Dynamic environment 
When considering the complexity of a modern construction site, it is very likely that rigging tasks can 
be interfered with by the surrounding elements, such as other construction vehicles or machines. This 
is potentially a very serious problem which needs to be specially handled in tele-operation scenarios. 
Without a human presence, it is difficult to handle any unexpected scenarios if the construction plans 
for the tele-operated machines on the site are not well-established. The participants of the test also 
pointed out the fact that the re-planning ability of rigging plans is a requirement of a tele-operation 
system to handle the dynamics of a construction.  
Bahnassi (2010), in consideration of the real-time path re-planning problem, developed and 
implemented re-planning methods based on the rapidly randomized tree (RRT) method. Considering 
the usage of PRM of this paper, it is computation power consumed for handling dynamic objects. The 
large computational demand is because once the status of an object is updated, the entire C-space 
needs to be rebuilt in order to identify any potential collision regions. To improve the computational 
efficiency, the Octree structure (Meagher, 1982) for collision detection was integrated. This can be 
used to partially rebuild those regions of space that have potentially changed due to dynamic objects. 
In the redesigned C-space, each element (r, θ, l) contains the information on the relative Cartesian 
position (x, y, z) and is categorized according to the geometric relationship of the Cartesian space. 
Firstly, all the elements in the C-space are inputted into the tree root. The space is then split into 
eight octants, segmenting the related Cartesian space, and the elements covered in each octant are 
recorded into the corresponding child structure. Secondly, each octant is split and the elements are 
recorded in the same way, and this is repeated until the desired level is achieved. The layout of the

The vertical axis of Figure 4 represents the average calculation time spent on the re-planning process,

and usability tests are needed to validate this approach. The evaluation of the weighting mechanism 
developed as part of this research is ongoing work and still in need of further investigation. 
4 Conclusions and Future Work 
A path-planning approach modified from the PRM and using a search algorithm was developed in this 
research. By conducting a pilot test with crane operators on a tele-operated crane prototype, three 
operation feasibility factors were identified: visibility, control features, and the dynamic environment. 
In consideration of the visibility issues, the guidance path can only be generated within regions 
monitored by at least two cameras in order to ensure the user’s operability when they are following 
the paths. A weighting mechanism for control features to adjust the travel cost between every C-space 
node pair was developed as well. By adapting the selection mechanism, the guidance path can avoid 
crossing over existing objects, maintain rigging directions, and maintain low elevations as far as is 
possible. In consideration of the dynamics of a construction site, the Octree structure for partially 
rebuilding C-space is integrated to speed up the re-planning process.
