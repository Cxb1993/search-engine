INTRODUCTION
Incineration has become the most important method to reduce the volume of municipal
solid waste (MSW) in Taiwan now. Seventeen mass-scale MSWI plants treated over
80% of municipal solid waste incineration (MSWI) in 2001, among them, sixteen plants
equipped with a semi-dry (SD) scrubber and a fabric filter (FF) for air pollution control
(APC) of the flue gas. In a real plant, two streams of SD residue and FF ash in a MSWI
plant are usually collected and mixed together into the stream of APC residues with a
random mixing ratio. The collected combined ash is called as MSWI fly ash, in order to
distinguish from the coal fly ash (IAWG, 1997a). The properties of Taiwan’s MSWI fly
ash, such as the chemical compositions, the pH of ash, the particle size distribution and
the leachability of heavy metals, have been reported well (Huang and Chu, 2003; Huang
and Chu, 2005; Huang, 2006) over the past twenty years. The total generation rate of
combined ash (a mixture of MSWI fly ash and bottom ash) in Taiwan was 2000 tons per
day in 2001 and 1800 tons in 2003 (Huang and Chu, 2003). A common phenomena seen
in most MSWI plants is that an on-line collected MSWI fly ash has a high fluctuation in
the concentration of lead in TCLP leachate. For the first time the fluctuation in TCLP
concentration of lead has been suggested that it would be strongly depending on the
mixing ratio of FF ash to SD stream mixture.

CONCLUSIONS
This study characterized the chemical composition and the leaching behaviors of
lead (II) of two APC residues from MSWI plants, SD residues and FF ashes, and
their binary mixtures. Using the TCLP leaching test, lead (II) starts to leach
significantly at a CaO/SiO2 ratio > 2.3 for SD residues and a CaO/Al2O3 ratio > 0.5
for FF ashes. TCLP leachate concentrations of lead of three series of synthetic APC
residues increased significantly for ashes with 60wt% FF ash. This result provides
an explanation for the high fluctuation in lead leached from on-line generated APC
residues.
