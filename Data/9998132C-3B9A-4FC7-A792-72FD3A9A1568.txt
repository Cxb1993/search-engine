壓電風扇冷卻平板型散熱片之熱流特性研究(I) 
         計畫編號：NSC 98－2221－E－211－017－ 
         執行期限：98 年 8 月 1 日至 99 年 7 月 31 日 
         計畫主持人：李弘毅  華梵大學機電工程學系

摘要 
 
本計畫以實驗方式探討壓電風扇冷卻平
板型散熱片之熱傳特性，實驗中探討壓電風
扇擺放之位置及散熱片幾何尺寸等參數對於
散熱效益之影響。實驗結果顯示壓電風扇葉
片位於散熱片正中央時可獲得最佳之熱傳效
益，多數情況下壓電風扇位於散熱片前端較
位於後端能獲得較好的熱傳效益。無鰭片時
熱傳效益最差，增加鰭片數目可增加熱傳效
益，在壓電風扇葉片位於散熱片前端時效果
最為明顯。 
 
關鍵詞：壓電風扇、平板型散熱片、電子冷
卻、紅外線熱影像 
 
Abstract 
 
  In this project, an experimental method is 
used to investigate the thermal characteristics 
of plate-fin heat sinks cooled by the 
piezoelectric fan. The effects of the position of 
the piezoelectric fan and the heat sink 
dimensions are discussed. The experimental 
results show that the thermal performance is the 
best with the blade tip of the piezoelectric fan 
in the center of the heat sink. In most 
circumstances, the thermal performance is 
better with the blade tip of the piezoelectric fan 
in front of the heat sink than in back of the heat 
sink. The thermal performance is the worst for 
the heat sink without any fin. The thermal 
performance enhances as the fin number of the 
heat sink increases. Additionally, the 
improvement is more obvious with the blade tip 
of the piezoelectric fan in front of the heat sink. 
 
Keywords：Piezoelectric Fan, Plate-fin Heat 
Sink, Electronic Cooling, Infrared 
Thermography 
 
一、緣由與目的 
 
隨著微機電製程技術的進步，電子產品
朝微型化趨勢發展，如筆記型電腦、行動電
話、PDA與GPS等，而在體積逐漸縮小且內
部電子元件功率不斷提升的影響下，使得電
子元件運作時產生之熱通量不斷增加，為了
確保電子產品能維持穩定工作，不會因溫度
過高造成壽命減短或是損壞的情況，因此散
熱系統設計也需微小化並更加具有效益。 
長久以來空氣強制對流冷卻皆為電子產
品散熱技術之主流，其方便、便宜及可靠等
優勢為廣受青睞之主因。為了達到提升冷卻
效益的目的，常見使用旋轉風扇加上散熱片
來進行冷卻，其主要原理是將電子元件運作
時所產生的熱量經由傳導方式傳至散熱片，
再利用旋轉風扇等強制對流裝置驅動冷卻流
體流經散熱片進行熱交換，將熱藉由對流方
式帶走，以達到降低元件溫度的目的。然而
電子產品微型化發展的趨勢下，空間受限已
無法再讓大型的旋轉風扇與散熱片同時裝置
於產品內部，為了有效提升散熱裝置的冷卻
性能，近幾年來在散熱模組的設計與應用上
亦有很多不同的發展，為了能使熱量加速傳
遞，液態冷卻（Liquid Cooling）、相變化冷
卻（Phase-change Cooling）、熱管（Heat Pipe）
與蒸汽腔體（Vapor Chamber）等技術也導入
散熱片設計中。此外，多孔介質（Porous 
Medium）與渦流產生器（Vortex Generator）
等相關散熱技術的應用，亦有許多學者與研
究人員進行相關議題之研究。而在改善風扇
體積方面，具有體積小、消耗功率低與低噪
音等優點的壓電風扇（Piezoelectric Fan）便
受到散熱模組設計上之考量。 
本計畫之研究主要探討壓電風扇應用於
電子元件散熱，研究中改變壓電風扇擺放位

由上述研究文獻中可得知，壓電風扇具有應
用於微電子散熱的可行性，其重量輕、低耗
能、安靜與使用壽命長等優點，且可製作成
微小尺寸元件，應用於各種需要散熱的環境
中，對於目前最常見的旋轉風扇無法發揮冷
卻效能的地方，以及需要局部散熱的部分，
壓電風扇皆可提供有效且可靠的散熱效果。
然而壓電風扇應用於電子元件散熱相關文獻
仍尚缺乏，研究文獻大多出自於特定研究團
隊所發表，且部分文獻偏向於壓電風扇之材
料與特性研究，因此壓電風扇結合電子散熱
技術尚待探討之各項參數頗多，是一值得深
入研究之主題，在此將針對壓電風扇擺放位
置與散熱片幾何尺寸等因素進行比較與討
論。

五、結論 
 
本研究使用紅外線測溫技術，探討在不
同條件下壓電風扇冷卻平板型散熱片之熱傳
效能。由實驗之結果，歸納成以下幾點結論： 
1. 壓電風扇葉片位於散熱片正中央時可獲
得最佳之熱傳效益，多數情況下壓電風
扇葉片位於散熱片前端較位於後端能獲
得較好的熱傳效益。 
2. 無鰭片時散熱片熱傳效益最差，增加鰭
片數目可增加熱傳效益，在壓電風扇葉
片位於散熱片前端時效果最為明顯。 
3. 壓電風扇僅對局部區域可產生明顯效
果，因此前方有鰭片進行熱交換才能顯
現出其效能。此時鰭片寬度增加可增加
熱傳面積，使散熱片熱阻值減少，但過
寬仍會影響其效能。

A review of the pertinent literature indicates that the vapor 
chamber can spread heat rapidly and have the advantages of 
thinness and lightness. Most related works have emphasized 
the heat transfer characteristics of vapor chambers. However, in 
practice, a vapor chamber is commonly combined with heat 
sinks or fins to increase the area of heat transfer through which 
heat is transferred and improve cooling. This work studies the 
effects of the Reynolds number, the fin width and the fin height 
on the performance of vapor chamber heat sinks using infrared 
thermography. 
2  EXPERIMENTAL APPARATUS AND METHOD 
This study experimentally investigates the thermal 
performance of vapor chamber heat sinks. The experimental 
apparatus, schematically depicted in Fig. 1, consists of a wind 
tunnel system, a heating system, devices for measuring flow 
velocity and temperature, vapor chamber heat sinks, and an 
infrared thermography system. 
 
Figure 1. Experimental apparatus for measuring performance 
of vapor chamber heat sink.

section and a blower. The blower, which has a maximum flow 
rate of 14 m3/min, sucks the required cooling air though the test 
section via a centrifugal fan that is regulated by an inverter. 
The air flows steadily across the heated vapor chamber heat 
sink, which removes heat continuously.

3.2 Thermal Resistance of Aluminum Heat Sink and 
Vapor Chamber Heat Sink 
Figure 4 plots the thermal resistance of the aluminum heat 
sink and the vapor chamber heat sink with W =0.5mm, H = 
10mm and n = 20 at various Reynolds numbers. The overall 
thermal resistance Ro of the vapor chamber heat sink is lower 
than that of the aluminum heat sink. Therefore, the vapor 
chamber heat sink outperforms the aluminum heat sink. 
Notably, the spreading thermal resistance Rs of the vapor 
chamber heat sink is less than that of the aluminum heat sink 
because, for the vapor chamber heat sink, the generated heat is 
transferred by the liquid-vapor phase change of the working 
fluid inside the vapor chamber to the top of the base plate, 
while for the aluminum heat sink, the heat is transferred by 
conduction through the aluminum plate. Since the high heat 
transfer capacity of the phase change, the vapor chamber 
transfers the heat more uniformly over the base plate and 
improves the performance of the heat sink. As specified by Eq. 
(5), the convective thermal resistance Rc is the reciprocal of the 
product of the overall heat transfer coefficient and the 
convective heat transfer area. Therefore, the convective thermal 
resistances of the aluminum heat sink and the vapor chamber 
heat sink are almost equal for a particular Reynolds number 
and given fin dimensions. The convective thermal resistance 
declines as the Reynolds number increases, and the rate of this 
drop falls as the Reynolds number increases.

3.3 Effects of Fin Width on Thermal Resistance of 
Vapor Chamber Heat Sink 
Figure 5 plots the effects of the fin width on the thermal 
resistance of the vapor chamber heat sink with H = 10mm and 
n = 20 at various Reynolds numbers. A vapor chamber heat 
sink with narrower fins has a lower spreading thermal 
resistance of the base plate. The spreading thermal resistance of 
the vapor chamber heat sink with a constant fin width at Re = 
10000 exceeds that at higher Reynolds numbers, indicating that 
increasing the velocity of the cooling air can increase the 
transfer of heat to the base plate and reduce the spreading 
thermal resistance. 
As discussed above, the convective thermal resistances of 
the vapor chamber heat sink is almost the same for a specified 
Reynolds number and fin dimensions. The convective thermal 
resistance increases with the fin width, because a heat sink with 
wider fins has a smaller inter-fin spacing, and therefore allows 
less flow of the cooling air into the heat sink for cooling. 
Increasing the Reynolds number causes more cooling air to be 
forced into the inter-fin spacing to exchange heat, reducing the 
effect of the fin width on the convective thermal resistance. 
Adding the spreading thermal resistance and the 
convective thermal resistance yields the total thermal 
resistance, as displayed in Fig. 5. The total thermal resistance 
of the vapor chamber heat sink with the narrowest fins is lower 
than that of those with the other two fin widths. Moreover, the 
effect of the fin width on the total thermal resistance declines as 
the Reynolds number increases.

3.4 Effects of Fin Height on Thermal Resistance of 
Vapor Chamber Heat Sink 
Figure 6 plots the effects of the fin height on the thermal 
resistance of the vapor chamber heat sink with W = 1.0 mm and 
n = 20 at various Reynolds numbers. The results indicate that 
the spreading thermal resistance of the vapor chamber heat sink 
with a given fin height remains constant when Re ≥  30000. 
Additionally, the spreading thermal resistance increases with 
the fin height for a fixed Reynolds number, because the 
accumulation of heat in a heat sink with higher fins is greater. 
This effect subsequently affects the transfer of heat at the 
condensation end of the vapor chamber and degrades the 
thermal performance of the vapor chamber. The spreading 
thermal resistance of the vapor chamber heat sinks with H = 
10mm or H = 20 mm tends to increase as the Reynolds number 
declines to 10000, indicating that the Reynolds number must 
increase as the fins of a vapor chamber heat sink become 
shorter to improve base plate performance.
