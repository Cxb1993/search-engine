摘要 
 
關鍵詞：零價鐵、氧化鐵、填充床、流體化床、活性碳、正己烷。 
 
    本計畫首創以「兩段式三價鐵 / 零價鐵金屬併同處理燃燒尾氣之
NOx/CO/HC/Soot」的概念，整合氧化鐵(Fe2O3)及零價鐵(ZVI)系統，發展兩段式
氧化還原之污染物處理技術，並探討ZVI與NO/HC或NO/CO或NO/Soot及Fe2O3與
NO/CO或NO/Soot之影響。首先於研究ZVI與NO/CO在填充床管柱之試驗中，結
果指出穿透時間會分別隨著反應溫度、零價鐵粉添加量及CO進流濃度提高而增
加，其中反應溫度為影響穿透時間長短之主要因子。而利用ZVI流體化床併同處
理NO/Soot (活性碳粉)之實驗中，在反應溫度773 K，NO進流濃度240 ppm，鐵粉
添加量0.5 g及氣體進流率0.3 L/min的條件下，增加碳粉量其單位鐵粉處理NO能
力成線性比例增加；此外，當煙道氣中O2進流濃度增加時，其NO處理效率將隨
著O2進流濃度增加而降低。研究ZVI與NO/HC (正己烷, n-Hexane)在流體化床管柱
之試驗中，隨著n-Hexane進流濃度的改變，未加入n-Hexane氣體之穿透時間比有
加入n-Hexane氣體較長，其原因為當加入n-Hexane氣體時，n-Hexane會與NO氣體
競爭消耗零價鐵，生成Fe2O3、Fe3O4及FeC等物質。而於Fe2O3填充床管柱內處理
NO/CO之實驗中，在CO進流濃度1910 ppm以下時，由於CO提供的莫耳流率較
低，導致氧化鐵還原量不足，進而NO去除率較差；當提高CO進流濃度至1910 ppm
以上時，系統對於NO的處理能力可達95%，CO的利用率隨著CO進流濃度增加而
略有提昇，但CO利用率的提升對於NO去除率則沒有顯著的影響。研究
Fe2O3/NO/Soot之填充床管柱試驗中，反應溫度773 K，NO進流濃度240 ppm，O2
進流濃度3%，鐵粉添加量3.0 g及氣體進流率0.3 L/min條件下，在碳粉添加量為
0.5 g以上時，碳粉即能和O2反應產生充足的還原性CO氣體，故可以完全去除
NO，且NO反應穿透時間隨著碳粉添加量之增加而延長；探討改變O2進流濃度對
穿透時間之影響中，穿透時間隨著O2進流濃度增加而減少，為一負相關之遞減關
係。  
 I
一、前言 
國內外目前柴油引擎尾氣處理設備大多數針對Soot、HC及CO進行處理，且
使用較為複雜的觸媒系統(包含還原型觸媒及氧化型觸媒)，此外，柴油引擎尾氣
處理所使用的觸媒包含了貴重金屬(鉑、釟及銠)，不僅增加製造成本，也產生廢
觸媒後續處理之問題，就經濟及資源面而言，此一觸媒系統有再考量的必要。 
    依本研究團隊過去三年研究結論，以零價鐵(ZVI)處理NO，經反應後XRD分
析證明零價鐵本身氧化為三價之α-Fe2O3[1]，而三價鐵在有還原劑(如CO及Soot
等)存在時，三價鐵被還原後，具有可再還原NO的能力[2]，顯示三價鐵在一定之
還原性氣體環境下，具有同步去除NO及其它還原性污染物的能力，非常適合於
柴油引擎燃燒尾氣之處理。因此本研究整合三價鐵及零價鐵系統，以「兩段式三
價鐵/零價鐵處理燃燒尾氣系統」之概念進行設計，第一段係先將還原性污染物
以氧化觸媒進行處理及無害化，第二段則利用零價鐵強大還原能力將氧化性污染
物還原，進一步將燃燒尾氣加以淨化。本研究整理比較現行處理技術與三價鐵/
零價鐵處理技術對柴油尾氣引擎之NO/HC/CO/Soot其去除效率比較列於表1.1，
就處理效率而言，併同三價鐵/零價鐵處理技術除去除率高於其它方法，更具有
併同處理還原態污染物(HC/CO/Soot)及氧化態污染物(NO)的優勢，未來極具競爭
之潛力及價值。

四、結果與討論 
4.1 ZVI 與 NO/CO 之間的反應 
4.1.1 反應溫度效應 
起始研究為求得添加 CO 氣體前，零價鐵與 NO 氣體之最低反應溫度條件，
反應溫度由 573 K 開始試驗，發現零價鐵對 NO 去除效率不佳，迅速達到穿透點
(ZVI/NO/CO 試驗中，NO 出流濃度為 150 ppm 時，視為穿透)，故將反應溫度提
高至 623 K，雖然實驗剛開始時 NO 去除率可達 100%，但隨即又達到穿透點，
顯示去除效果不佳，故排除於後續研究，所以後續實驗主要以 673 K、723 K 和
773 K 三個反應溫度來進行。 
圖 4.1 為 NO 進流濃度 400 ppm，鐵粉添加量 0.5 g，氣體進流率固定在 0.2 
L/min 條件下，探討不同反應溫度(673 K、723 K 及 773 K)之 NO 出流濃度穿透
曲線圖。由圖 4.1 顯示，反應剛開始即出現百分之百之 NO 去除效率，並且隨著
反應溫度由 673 K 增加至 723 K 時，其 NO 出流濃度達穿透點之時間由 4640 秒
增加至 8500 秒，穿透時間延長了 3860 秒；若將反應溫度由 673 K 增加至 773 K
時，其 NO 出流濃度達到穿透點之時間則由 4640 秒增加至 11360 秒，穿透時間
延長了 6720 秒，因此從實驗結果可以發現隨著反應溫度的提高，操作時間隨之
增加。

4.1.3 CO 進流濃度效應 
圖 4.3 為在反應溫度 773K，鐵粉量添加量為 0.4 g，氣體進流率為 0.2 L/min
條件下，探討不同CO進流濃度之NO及CO出流濃度穿透曲線圖。從圖 4.3(a)發現
隨著CO進流濃度由 0 ppm增加至 1000 ppm時，其NO出流濃度之穿透時間由 8630
秒增加至 12060 秒，穿透時間延長了 3430 秒；若將CO進流濃度由 0 ppm增加至
1500 ppm時，其NO出流濃度之穿透時間則由 8630 秒增加至 16110 秒，穿透時間
延長了 7480 秒。由上述結果得知，隨著CO進流濃度增加，NO出流濃度的穿透
時間也隨之增加，其原因是因為CO氣體能將被NO氧化成之Fe2O3還原Fe3O4，而
被還原的Fe3O4便可重新再與NO進行反應，使得系統穿透時間得以增加，由圖
4.3(b)中CO出流濃度的減少，證實過程中CO確實會參與反應；而CO濃度的增加，
可使CO氣體分子在反應區間的擴散碰撞能力增加，有利於CO氣體分子擴散吸附
於鐵氧化物表面，以進行還原反應，達到增加穿透時間之效果。

4.4.1 CO 進流濃度對 CO 利用率及 NO 去除率之影響 
本節將針對不同CO進流濃度進行實驗，圖 4.11 為反應溫度 773 K，NO進
流濃度 240 ppm，氣體進流量 300 mL/min及氧化鐵粉添加量 3.0 g條件下，不
同CO進流濃度(955、1910、2865 及 3820 ppm)之CO利用率及NO去除率關係
圖，當CO進流濃度為 955 ppmv時，NO去除率僅達 4.45%，顯示在低CO進流
濃度下，由於CO提供的莫耳流率較低，導致氧化鐵還原量不足，進而影響了
NO去除率。當CO進流濃度達到 1910 ppm時，NO去除率自 4.45%提升至 95%，
增加了 21 倍，對照CO進流濃度 955 ppm之實驗結果，可以觀察到當CO進流
濃度增加 1 倍時，由於氧化鐵表面經CO還原的還原態鐵量大幅提升，因此系
統對於NO的處理能力隨著氧化鐵還原率增加而增加。當CO進流濃度持續增加
至 2865 及 3820 ppm時，NO去除率仍維持 95%左右，顯示在此濃度範圍下，
CO的利用率雖然隨著CO進流濃度增加而略有提昇，但CO利用率的提升對於
NO去除率則沒有顯著的影響，推測此一現象發生的原因，應與NO在氧化鐵表
面的吸附量有關，當NO進流濃度未改變的情形下，NO吸附於氧化鐵表面的總
量為一常數，雖然具還原能力的FexO隨著CO利用率增加而增加，但由於NO
的吸附量維持固定不變，因此造成NO去除率在CO進流濃度範圍 1910~2980 
ppm時，並未隨著CO利用率之增加而增加，而是維持NO去除率為 95%。

5.2 建議 
    由實驗結果中可以證明本研究對於分開各段(三價鐵/零價鐵)處理煙道氣中
NO/CO/HC/Soot 均能有良好之處理效率，甚至對 NO 去除率可達 95%以上，但
是對於實廠上應用仍需要進一步試驗，並更深入探討使其普及化且達到經濟上之
價值；此外，建議利用工業產生之廢鐵屑來替代氧化鐵粉，或以擔體觸媒取代直
接使用氧化鐵粉，透過實驗測試，進一步計算反應所需的氧化鐵面積公式，提供
未來發展新型觸媒之可行性研究。

一、參加會議經過 
 
The article titled “NOx Removal by Carbon Gasification using Fluidized Zero Valent 
Iron Process” was presented in the A&WMA’s 101st ANNUAL CONFERENCE & 
EXHIBITION (07/24/2008-07/27/2008) in Oregon Convention Center, Portland, Oregon. 
The presented article was the result from project number 96-2221-E-027-018 supported 
by NSC, ROC. In this study, three important parameters, temperature, carbon quantity 
and oxygen concentration were determined for the high temperature NO/ZVI/carbon 
reaction. Under the ZVI quantity of 0.5 g and carbon quantity of 0.5 g at flux of 0.6 
L/cm2.min for temperature 573 K, there was no NOx removal efficiency. At temperature 
of  623 K, NO removal efficiency was only about 39.7%. At temperature of 673 K, 723 K 
and 773 K, complete reductions of NOx were achieved with capacity of ZVI of 1.56 mg 
NO/g ZVI, 6.53 mg NO/g ZVI, and 7.89 mg NO/g ZVI, respectively. The results indicate 
that NOx removal efficiency and the ZVI capacity increases with the increase of 
temperature. The addition of carbon also plays an important role. When ZVI powder 
quantity is fixed and when the carbon powder quantity increases, capacity of ZVI (as mg 
NO/g ZVI) is proportionally increased as the carbon quantity increases. In addition, the 
increase of oxygen concentration decreases the efficiency for NOx removal. Under the 
environment of high-temperature and low oxygen, carbon gasification provided reducing 
agent CO to assist the reduction of either NOx or iron oxide resulting from the oxidation 
of ZVI.

INTRODUCTION 
Nitrogen oxide (NOx) is generated during combustion process, and about 95 % of NOx 
from stationary combustion sources is emitted as nitrogen monoxide (NO). The presence 
of NOx in the environment has been a serious problem, causing photochemical smog in 
our environment. Therefore, reduction or even zero discharge of NOx from the flue gas 
out of industrial or power plants has always been a critical issue. There are several 
technologies available for flue gas NOx treatment. Both selective catalytic reduction 
(SCR) and selective noncatalytic reduction (SNCR) can all remove NOx well; however, 
these techniques are all with their drawbacks for excess use of chemicals or high cost for 
catalyst in SCR process1. ZVI is a promising technology capable of removing NOx. There 
is no chemicals needed and no other waste generated since used iron can be regenerated. 
Moreover, the required operating temperature in ZVI process may not be as high as the 
operating temperature of SCR. From operational and economical points, ZVI does 
possess advantages over these traditional processes. 
The earliest reference for ZVI/NOx reaction is US Patent No. 43259252, where NOx 
more than 270 ppm was mentioned to remove by elemental iron for water volume of 
1-15% and temperature below 473K for sufficient contact time. Some literatures also 
reported ZVI/NO reaction for temperatures around 873-1273 K3-5; 8-10. Hayhurst and 
Lawrence3 conducted the NO and N2O removal at temperatures of 973K and 1173K in a 
fluidized bed using ZVI particle with diameter of 139-400µm for influent NO 
concentration of 1350-1470 ppm. ZVI can remove NO and N2O up to 80 % at 
temperature of 973 K and 1273K. Lissianski et al.5 also reported that NOx removal 
efficiency for concentration between 600ppm and 1000ppm was increased 30 % by 
adding metallic ZVI (diameter<10μm) in a 300KW of Boiler Simulator Facility at 
temperature 1590K. Chen et al.6 studied the effects of temperature and empty bed contact 
time (EBCT) for ZVI/NO reaction in a packed bed column process. For six controlled 
temperatures of 573 K-773 K and 400 ppm of NO influent concentration for EBCT of 
0.0225 sec to 0.0679 sec, NO was completely removed. Moreover, the amounts of NO 
reduction (as mg NO /g ZVI) increased as temperature increased. ESCA (Electron 
Spectroscopy for Chemical Analysis) and XRD (X-Ray Diffraction) were used to 
determine the reaction mechanisms by analyzing the oxidation state and crystal structures 
of the reacted ZVI. Two resulting equations were reported in their study:

Effects of Temperatures  
The experiment started with 573 K, but there was no NO reduction. Apparently, higher 
temperature is needed. The breakthrough curves for these four working temperatures (623 
K-773 K) are shown from Figure 2. Breakthrough here is defined when the effluent 
concentration is higher than the allowable concentration, which is commonly 20 % of the 
influent concentration or 50ppm in this study. When the temperature was increased to 623 
K, 100 % removal was achieved, but only lasted for very short time. With every 50 K of 
temperature increases, in addition to complete NO removal, the breakthrough time was 
also increased.  
 
NO removals in term of mass NO reduction were calculated based on Equation (3). These 
numbers can be utilized as a reference to estimate the actual operational time when scaled 
up. For 673 K, the NO reduction (as mg NO/g ZVI) was 1.56 mg NO/g ZVI. Increasing 
the temperature to 723K and 773K, the NO reduction was increases to 6.53 mg NO/g ZVI 
and 7.89 mg NO/g ZVI, respectively. Consequently, the reactivity can be ranked as 
follows: 773K > 723K > 673K > 623K> 573K. Two possible reasons could be explained 
for higher reactivity in higher temperature: (1) higher diffusivity for NO to diffuse into 
the surface layer of ZVI in higher temperature (2) higher equilibrium constant at higher 
temperature if the reaction is endothermic.

Effects of Oxygen Content 
The breakthrough curves for these three working oxygen content (3%, 6% and 9%) are 
shown from Figure 7(a)-(c). The same ZVI/carbon dosage of 0.5 g/0.5 g and the oxygen 
content was from 3% to 9%, 100 % removal was achieved, but only lasted for very short 
time. With every 3% of oxygen content decreases, in addition to complete NO removal, 
the breakthrough time was also increased. The addition of carbon also plays an important 
role. When iron powder quantity is fixed and when the carbon powder quantity increases, 
capacity of ZVI (as mg NO/g ZVI) is proportionally increased as the carbon quantity 
increases. In addition, the increase of oxygen concentration decreases the efficiency for 
NOx removal. Under the environment of high-temperature and low oxygen, carbon 
gasification provided reducing agent CO to assist the reduction of either NOx or iron 
oxide resulting from the oxidation of ZVI.

XRD analysis and reaction mechanism determination 
In order to determine the reaction mechanism among ZVI, carbon, O2 and NO, XRD was 
conducted to analyze the crystal structure of the reacted ZVI for ZVI, carbon, NO, O2 
from previous experimental condition (Temperature 773K, ZVI/carbon dosage of 0.5 
g/0.5 g, same flux of 0.6 mL/min.cm2). Since the reaction is a heterogeneous reaction 
which only occurred on the ZVI surface instead of bulk of the ZVI3, 4, the reacted ZVI 
remained on the surface can be used for characteristic identification to understand its 
byproduct and the reaction mechanism. According to Hayhurst & Ninomiya4, the 
byproducts of ZVI could be either FeO, Fe3O4 or Fe2O3. FeO is the predicted remaining 
iron oxide according to a model based on a shrinking core of iron surrounded by an 
outside layer of oxide, but it was risky without any chemical identification. XRD was 
used for identifying the species, and the XRD spectra were shown in Figure 8. In addition 
to the highest peak (ZVI), the rests were determined as Fe2O3 and Fe3O4.
