報告內容 
前言: 
目前台灣廢棄物處理以焚化、掩埋、堆肥與資源回收為主。因土地取得不易，掩埋比例日
益減少，而漸以焚化爐為主。焚化爐可減少廢棄物體積，並可增加蒸氣與電力之能源回收。
但焚化爐處理垃圾亦會產生空氣污染與焚化灰燼，對環境之衝擊可謂不小。空氣污染可由
空氣污染防制設備加以控制，唯其可能致癌之戴奧辛與多環芳香烴碳氫化合物等，仍需加
以特別控制處理。而另一可能衍生之污染物則為焚化後所產生之灰燼，如焚化爐床所產生
之底灰與污染控制設備所產生之飛灰等。底灰與飛灰因含有金屬、戴奧辛與多環芳香烴碳
氫化合物等致癌物，因此需特別加以處理，以避免二次環境污染。底灰與飛灰之處理目前
以固化、骨材利用、填土、土壤改良與掩埋等方式為主。灰燼雖可利用覆土掩埋或厭氧消
化添加，但其反應機制則仍不是很清楚。因此有必要進行實驗室厭氧消化槽之實驗，將實
驗結果推廣至掩埋場或厭氧消化槽使用參考。 
研究目的: 
本研究主目的主要探討都市固體廢棄物焚化爐灰燼作為掩埋場覆土或厭氧消化添加之可行
性。利用厭氧消化槽(Landfill bioreactor)模擬掩埋場或厭氧消化槽，以探討了解不同灰燼比
例對廢棄物分解之影響。並探討灰燼之特性含量，不同環境條件如 pH 與滲出液之參數等，
探討可能溶出有害物質如重金屬濃度等對垃圾分解之影響。最主要目的為找出灰燼最適添
加比例以作為現場實務之使用參考。 
文獻探討: 
有關焚化爐灰燼研究甚多，有灰燼特性分析(Capuano et al., 2005)，灰燼的利用如骨材、路
基、磁磚或磚塊等建材利用、土壤改良等(Huang et al., 2007; Kuo et al., 2007; Lin and Lin, 
2005; Shih et al., 2005; Wang et al., 2005; Asavapisit et al., 2005; Tay and Show, 1997)。灰燼含
有多種金屬、戴奧辛與多環芳香烴碳氫化合物等，為了解其作為各項利用之可行性，溶出
試驗是重要步驟之一，如毒性溶出實驗(TCLP)等，可了解溶出物質濃度對其資源利用與環
境或厭氧生物分解之影響。以灰燼作為掩埋場覆土或厭氧消化添加之研究不多(Lo, 2005; Lo 
and Liao, 2007)。因灰燼含有鋁、矽等礦物成份，因而適合水泥之添加以為骨材等利用，若
以高溫加以加熱使達 1500℃以上，則可將灰燼加以製成磁磚或磚塊等建材利用，經濟價值
更高。唯其加熱所耗能源應加以考量其成本，並計算其益本比與效能，使具推廣價值性。
因灰燼作為掩埋或厭氧消化添加，所耗成本少，又可就當地處理，因此可行性很高。唯其
所含金屬、戴奧辛與多環芳香烴碳氫化合物等潛在有害物質，需加以特別處理，以利其掩
埋或其它利用。雖然灰燼含有金屬、戴奧辛與多環芳香烴碳氫化合物等潛在有害物質，唯
其溶出後濃度可能吸附在垃圾基質上，造成其溶出濃度有可能對垃圾分解抑制潛勢降低，
甚至金屬濃度有可能成為垃圾分解所需要之營養鹽元素，而有利於垃圾之厭氧生物分解。
因此，本研究乃探討灰燼最適添加垃圾比例，以作掩埋覆土或厭氧消化添加之現場操作利
用參考依據。

結論與建議: 
本研究旨在探討灰燼添加垃圾共同掩埋之可行性。由結果可知，底灰部份，產氣量大小為
100 > 200 > 400 > 600 > control g/L 底灰/垃圾比，可知少而適量之底灰添加垃圾共同掩埋，
有促進垃圾分解與增加氣體產量效益。至於飛灰部份產氣量大小為 20 > 40 > control > 60 > 
100 g/L 飛灰/垃圾比，結果顯示大於 60 g/L 以上飛灰/垃圾比對垃圾分解有抑制作用，而小
於 40 g/L 飛灰/垃圾比之飛灰添加，相反地，則有產氣量增加，顯示此飛灰/垃圾比之添加，
有利於垃圾之生物分解。由此可知，灰燼之添加，其金屬化合物等含量與其溶出濃度對垃
圾分解中之微生物有其一定之影響，其促進或抑制程度視金屬離子濃度與 pH 之貢獻情形
而定。其它參數之影響大致相同，沒有對垃圾分解程序造成特別之衝擊。 
本研究以合成垃圾作為基質進行探討，為求與實際垃圾更為符合，未來擬進行採集掩埋場
實際垃圾作為基進行實驗，垃圾基質之含水量擬由 6%增為 6%，35% 與 50% 三種，以使
垃圾基質與真實垃圾更為接近。另垃圾分解之微生物，擬將以分子生物科技進行甲烷菌與
產氫菌等之鑑定，以了解微生物族群對垃圾分解之影響，而垃圾分解所產生之沼氣，亦將
以 GC(TCD)分析甲烷與氫氣等，以作為能源再利用參考。

The release of metals from MSWI ashes containing more than thirty metals species1,14 might show 
synergistic or antagonistic effects2,15 on MSW decomposition. Thus, understanding the individual metal 
effect on MSW digestion is important for landfill co-disposal of MSW and MSWI ashes. Thus, this study 
aims to investigate the effects of selected individual heavy metals such as Cd, Cr, Ni, Zn on anaerobic 
digestion in the MSW and MSWI co-disposal process, as well as in anaerobic bioreactors contaminated 
with heavy metals.  
 
METHODS AND MATERIALS 
MSW Substrate 
Synthetic MSW (6% TS) containing office paper (30%), newspaper (30%), hay (35%) and food waste 
(5%) was prepared following Lo14 to conduct the experiment. The physical composition is representative 
of typical organic fraction of MSW. In addition, the chemical constituents of C, H, O, N and “others” of 
MSW were measured to be approximately 46%, 6%, 41%, 1.4%, and 5.6% respectively. Content of 
carbon was also similar to the typical value of MSW. The MSW with higher water content was prepared 
as such to make the MSW more easily degraded by the microorganisms via seeded anaerobic sludge from 
a municipal wastewater plant. MSW was prepared and stored in a freezer before experimental use. 
Seeded Sludge 
Seeded sludge was added into the reactors to initiate and develop the MSW anaerobic digestion. It was 
obtained from the Fu-Tien municipal wastewater treatment plant located at Taichung City in central 
Taiwan. The plant collects 50,000-55,000 CMD sewage and use an aerobic biological treatment process 
based on the activated sludge process (HRT, 6 hrs). Sludge from the primary (HRT, 1.5 hrs) and 
secondary sedimentation tank (HRT, 4 hrs) is sent to the gravity thickener (SRT, >12 hrs) and then sent to 
the anaerobic digester (>30 days) for anaerobic digestion. The digested sludge is then dewatered by 
pressure filter. Dewatered sludge (75 % water content) is landfilled or given to agricultural uses after a 
metal content screening test. After the secondary sedimentation tank, the effluent is disinfected with 10 % 
NaOCl in a retention tank for 20 minutes and discharged. Seeded sludge was obtained from the anaerobic 
digester. The metal content of sludge can be found in Lo and Liao’s investigation14. 
Experimental 
The experiment was conducted by mixing 200 mL of seeded sludge completely with 200 mL MSW 
substrate and metals in the 500 mL bioreactors. Metal contents were prepared the three initial 
concentrations of 5, 50 and 100 mg l-1 (metal weight/0.4L effective reaction volume) respectively. 
Selected metals for the experiment were the heavy metals Cd, Cr, Ni, and Zn. These reactors were 
maintained within a homeostatic oven kept at a constant temperature of around 35℃. Over the digestion 
period of ~50 days, reaction MSW and sludge in the reactors were filtered to analyze the soluble metal 
concentrations about once per week (actually eleven measurements were made). The total reactors for 
each metal including three different initial levels were 12*3=36 (each with one reactor for gas monitoring 
and 11 reactors for metal measurement) and the total reactors for the four metals were 36*4 = 144 plus 
the 12 control reactors without metal addition (1 reactor for gas monitoring and 11 reactors for 4 metals 
levels measurement for blank information) totally equal to 156. During the digestion process, gas 
production was recorded by gas collectors with water drainage daily. Each soluble metal concentration 
after filtration was measured by ICP-OES (Thermal Electron Corporation).  
Kinetic study 
Cd in the initial 50 and 100 mg l-1 reactors appeared to be reduced before day 45 and 51 respectively (Fig. 
1A). However, gas accumulation seemed to be the same in the control and the initial 50 mg l-1 metal 
added reactor from day 46 to day 51 whilst that in the initial 100 mg l-1 metal added reactor remained at 
only 370 mL after MSW digestion completion. All Cd soluble concentrations in the reactors were found 
below 0.0065 mg l-1 (Fig. 1B).  
Similarly, gas accumulation with Cr was found to increase after day 34 in the initial 5 mg l-1 reactor 
compared to that of control one and the other two metal added reactors (Fig. 2A). The gas accumulation 
order was initial 5 mg l-1 reactor > control reactor > 50 mg l-1 reactor > 100 mg l-1 reactor. Before day 33, 
the gas accumulation trend was found to be similar to that of both the initial 5 mg l-1 reactor and control 
reactor. The soluble concentrations of Cr were all below 0.25 mg l-1 and were apparently found in the 
order of 100 mg l-1 reactor > 50 mg l-1 reactor > 5 mg l-1 reactor > control reactor (Fig. 2B). From the gas 
accumulation trend and soluble levels, it might be noted that ~0.02 mg l-1 shows a stimulatory effect 
whilst > ~0.05 mg l-1 appears to exert an inhibitory effect on MSW digestion. The corresponding Cr 
contents of ~0.36 mg g-1 VS in the initial 5 mg l-1 reactor might show beneficial effects while potential 
detrimental effects by >~4.31 and ~7.77 mg g-1 VS in the initial 50 and 100 mg l-1 reactors were also 
observed (Fig. 2B).

scenarios of metal soluble levels, metal content in VS and tolerance of different bacterial 
communities2,3,18. In other studies, Cu, Cd and Cr concentrations of 2.4, 1.6 and 4.0 mg l-1 were found to 
achieve the maximum methane production7. However, concentrations higher than these levels were found 
to inhibit the cattail anaerobic degradation resulting in gradually lower methane production. The levels 
causing beneficial effects7 were found to be higher than similar beneficial results in this study by Cd and 
Cr of 0.0001-0.0004 and 0.02-0.05 mg l-1 respectively. This result appears to indicate that the water 
content or total solid of MSW substrate might affect the metal transport, adsorption and bacterial 
performance resulting in the different inhibitory effects. In addition, soluble metal concentrations (and 
total content) such as Cu, Cr(VI), Ni and Zn of 0.5 and 0.7 (50-70), 3.0 (200-260 and 520), 1.6 and 2.0 
(30 and 70) and 0.1 and 1.0 (341) mg l-1 were reported to be strong inhibitors to anaerobic digestion2. The
