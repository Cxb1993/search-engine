2摘要
本研究主要目的在探討有風速存在的條件下，醇類存在於溶液中對不同水溶解度有機物揮發速率
之影響，選擇的醇類包括甲醇、乙醇，丁醇，戊醇與庚醇為實驗對象，風速選擇包含 0、0.2、0.5、0.8、
1.0、2.0、4.0 與 6.0m/s 等 8 種風速，選擇有機物之水溶解度範圍由 1780 致 0.56 mg/L，藉由這些有機
物在未加入醇類與加入不同醇類後在靜置與有風情況下質傳係數(KOL)變化，討論醇類對有機物揮發之
影響。所得結果顯示雖然所選擇有機物具有不同的水溶解度，但除了溶解度較低的三種氯苯外，加入
醇類或在風速下有機物的揮發速率變化皆具有近似的趨勢，但加入醇類不同則對揮發速率產生不同之
影響效果，低碳數的醇類如甲醇與乙醇對有機物的揮發會產生抑制作用，高碳數的醇類如丁醇、戊醇
及庚醇對有機物的揮發會產生加速作用，此結果被認為是低碳數醇類對溶液中的有機物會產生增溶作
用，增加溶液與有機物之親和力，致使有機物揮發速率略微降低，高碳數醇類可能聚集於溶液表面，
同時吸引有機物接近交界面，導致有機物的揮發量增加，此現象被稱之為”界面共同揮發作用”，且
此作用在有風速存在的條件下此作用將更加明顯，此結果亦證明有機物在交界面的濃度是決定有機物
揮發之重要因子。
關鍵詞:揮發干擾、醇類、有機物、共揮發作用。
Abstract
The objective of this study is to investigate the effects of alcohols in the solution on volatilization rates of
organic solutes. The selected alcohols include methanol, ethanol, butanol, pentanol and heptanol with added
concentration ranged from 100 to 500 mg/L. The target organic compounds with water solubility from 1780
to 0.56 mg/L mixed with alcohols to test volatilization rate constant (or mass transfer coefficient KOL) under
0~6.0 m/s wind speeds. The effects of the alcohols on emissions of organic solutes were determined via the
obtained KOL values. The result indicates except three kinds of chlorobenzene the selected organic compounds
exhibit the similar change in KOL values as the presence of alcohols. The KOL values for three chlorobenzenes
with low water solubility show relatively higher increase or decrease. The presence of alcohol with less
carbon number, such as methanol and ethanol could reduce the KOL values of the organic compounds. The
reason is ascribed to that the alcohols can enhance the solubilities of the test organic compounds. On the
contrary, presence of alcohol with high carbon number, such as butanol, pentanol and heptanol could increase
the KOL values of the organic compounds. The reason of increased volatilization rates of solutes was
considered as alcohols accumulate in the interface, which affects the volatilization of organic solutes. This
phenomenon is called as co-volatilization. Moreover, the result demonstrates the interaction of organic
compounds in the interface relative to solution is a dominant factor to determine emission of the organic
solutes.
Keywords: Alcohol, Co-volatilization, Two-film theory, Organic solutes
