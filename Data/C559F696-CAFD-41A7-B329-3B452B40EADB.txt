Abstract 
 
This study developed a steady 
combustion numerical model to simulate a 
cylindrical solid fuel burning in a quiescent, 
nature convective environment. The model 
includes two-dimensional continuity, full 
Navier Stokes’ momentum, energy, and 
species equations with a one-step overall 
chemical reaction and second-order finite-rate 
Arrhenius kinetics. In combustion the 
temperature of the entire cylindrical solid fuel 
were maintained at a constant temperature. 
The fuel gases assumed eject uniformly from 
the upper half circular of the cylinder while 
the down half circular were coated. The model 
was solved numerically using a body-fitted 
coordinate transformation and the SIMPLE 
algorithm. The temperature distribution and 
the velocity field were obtained and used to 
explain the flame structure. The results show 
that, in a nature convective environment the 
steady flame over a cylindrical solid fuel was 
a wake flame. Also, the flame becomes 
weaker as the gravity increased. 
Keywords: cylindrical, solid fuel, flame
