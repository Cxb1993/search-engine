Abstract: 
Laetiporus sulphureus is one of the fungi used traditional medicinal mushrooms with curing 
mammary glands cancer and prostate gland cancer. The research demonstrated that the polysaccharide 
and extracted materials have many biological activities to including anti-oxidizes, anti-tumor, 
increasing immunity ability and declining blood sugar, etc. This research is to optimize the submerged 
culture conditions to produce the exopolysaccharide of Laetiporus sulphureus with respect to several 
operating parameters in shake flask fermentation, and also examining its immunity ability properties. 
The experimental results exhibited that glucose and yeast extract and soya peptone were the most 
suitable carbon and nitrogen sources for exopolysaccharide production, respectively. Their optimal 
concentrations were 10 g L-1 of glucose, 5 g L-1 of yeast extract and 3 g L-1 of soya peptone, 
respectively. The optimal temperature, initial pH and trace element concentration for 
exopolysaccharide production by Laetiporus sulphureus in shake flask culture were found to be 31 ℃?,

表 Y04 
Presentation type:  Poster presentation 
 
In vitro antioxidant properties of Phellinus igniarius by submerged cultures in 20-L 
stirred tank bioreactor 
 
Ming Yeou Lung* and Kai Wun Deng  
Department of Department of Chemical and Materials Engineering, Minghsin University of Science and 
Technology, Hsin Fong, Hsin Chu, Taiwan 304, R.O.C. 
*E-mail: mylong@must.edu.tw 
 
Phellinu igniarius, a basidiomycete belonging to the family Polyporaceae, is a medicinal 
basidiomycetous fungus belonging to the Hymenochaetaceae, with excellent remedy of anticancer and 
antioxidant activity. The mushroom has been used as traditional medicines for the treatment of 
cardiovascular disease, tuberculosis, liver or heart diseases, bellyache, bloody gonorrhea, and diabetes. 
However, the limited production and market shortage have been attributed to the slow growth and the 
difficult collection of fruiting body as well as the rare nature resources. The problem can be solved through 
the effective approach of submerged culture to yield antioxidant-related products. Evaluated in this study are 
accordingly antioxidant properties of both ethanol extracts from mycelia and mycelia-free broth, hot water 
extracts from mycelia, intracellular polysaccharide from mycelia  and  exopolysaccharide from 
mycelia-free broth by P. igniarius submerged cultures in a 20-L stirred tank bioreactor. Antioxidant 
properties of five extracts including antioxidant activity by the conjugated diene method, reducing power, 
scavenging abilities on DPPH (1,1-diphenyl-2-picryl hydrazyl radical), chelating ability on ferrous ions and 
scavenging superoxide anion activity were determined with various test systems. 
The fermented product extracts (i.e. ethanolic extract from mycelia, exopolysaccharide from 
mycelia-free broth, ethanolic extract from mycelia-free broth, hot water extract from mycelia and 
intracellular polysaccharide from mycelia) showed high antioxidant activity, reducing powers, scavenging 
effects on 1,1-diphenol-2-picrylhydrazyl radicals, chelating effects and scavenging effects on superoxide 
anion. The lowest EC50 values for antioxidant activity were 2.22±0.24 mg mL-1, 0.52±0.14 mg mL-1 for 
reducing powers, 5.41±0.61 mg mL-1 for scavenging effects on 1,1-diphenol-2-picrylhydrazyl radicals, 
4.61±0.14 mg mL-1 for chelating effects and 3.99±0.11mg mL-1 for scavenging effects on superoxide anion. 
Results confirm that extracts with powerful antioxidant properties from fermenting products by P. igniarius 
are potential good antioxidant sources and can be applied to antioxidant-related functional food and 
pharmaceutical industries.
