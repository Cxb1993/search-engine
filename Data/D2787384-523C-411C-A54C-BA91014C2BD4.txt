Abstract  
 
This study presents an imaging technique combining multi-functional dark field microscopy (DFM) 
with spectroscopy to image the nanostructures of different formats of optical disks and identify their 
optical properties by absorption spectra. The resolution of this DFM system is determined using 
one-dimension nanostructure with lengths from 160 nm to 600 nm. The pattern sizes are confirmed by 
atomic force microscopy (AFM). The results show that DFM resolution is about 160 nm with the 
current setup, and the resulting DFM image size appears to be slightly larger than an AFM image. We 
then deposited a 50 nm gold film on top of these nanostructures and measured their absorption spectra 
to identify the relationship between surface plasmon resonance (SPR) spectra and one-dimensional 
nanostructures. The immobilization of streptavidin on the surface of the gold-coated nanostructure 
causes changes in the absorption spectrum ranging from 450nm to 650nm. One the other hand, we also 
study the local field enhancement due to the SPR of gold nanorods, which can be possibly conjugated 
on the protein molecules to magnify the signal. Compared to gold nanospheres, the SPR frequency of 
gold nanorods can be modulated by changing the aspect ratio of rods. The SPR- induced local field 
enhancement will enhance the optical process, such as absorption, scattering, and fluorescence. We 
herein investigate the two-photon excited fluorescence efficiency of gold nanorods, and verify the 
theoretical estimation of the local field enhancement through the experimental data. 
 
 
 
 
 
Keywords—surface plasmon, dark field microscopy, two-photon fluorescence, local field enhancement, 
gold nanorods

I. Background and Purpose 
 Researchers have recently conducted several studies on the plasmonic phenomena induced by 
optical excitation, finding applications in optoelectronics, sensors, and biomedicine [1, 2]. The 
interaction between incident photon energy with free electrons on a metallic surface is subject to the 
effects of nanostructure pattern and feature size. These effects can result in distinct optical and 
electronic properties caused by surface plasmon enhancement [3, 4]. For example, in surface plasmon 
resonance (SPR), gold film approximately 50 nm thick is extremely sensitive to changes in the 
refractivity index, and can be used as a sensing layer to detect very low concentrations of biomoleculars 
[5]. Moreover, nanostructures, such as nanoholes and nanoslits, deposited with gold film have also been 
proven to induce surface plasmon resonance [6, 7] with improvements in sensitivity and resolution.  
Optical techniques provide a unique opportunity for studying the properties of nanostructures, and 
spectral information is usually provided as surface plasmon characteristics of a noble metal layer. For 
example, the absorption spectrum of gold nanopaticles represents the resonance bands of its surface 
electrons caused by light field coupling. Surface enhancement Raman scattering (SERS) is used to 
observe the Raman shift of molecular vibration bands caused by approaching the surface Plasmon of 
noble metal nanostructures [8]. Optical methods also have a lot of advantages for biological applications, 
including non-invasive and in-vivo measurement, providing qualitative and quantitative analysis, and so 
on. Optical microscopy was developed long ago to aid in the observation of biological structures such as 
cells and tissues, and microscopic components, such as objective lens with large numeric aperture, have 
been improved to achieve high spatial resolution.  
 Researchers have developed more and more advanced optical microscopy combined with 
spectroscopy to obtain multi-functional information of in-situ specimens. These approaches include 
confocal microscopy, two-photon microscopy, diffraction microscopy, and dark field microscopy. In 
fluorescent microscopy, many fluorescent probes that can be labeled to target molecules are very 
powerful tools to enhance the signals of biomolecular interaction under optical microscopy [9]. 
Fluorescence enhancement and SERS are result of electrons on the surface of gold or silver 
nanoparticles interacting with biomolecules [10], which amplifies the optical signals of emission 
intensity and vibrational band, respectively. Consequently, to acquire information on molecular 
interactions enhanced by metal or semiconductor nanostructures inside a cell or tissue, it is necessary to 
implement optical microscopy with ultra high spatial resolution and spectral function. Besides, 
researchers can also use dark field microscopy (DFM) to observe the optical properties of individual 
gold and silver nanoparticles and nanorods because DFM block the interference of background signals 
and improve overall image contrast. 
This study combines optical microscopy with spectroscopy to identify the optical properties of 
one-dimensional nanostructures and evaluate the performance of this system. Various sizes of 
one-dimension nanostructures were imaged with white light and 390 nm laser light to determine the 
resolution of dark filed microscopy. The absorption spectra of these one-dimensional structures coated 
with a thin gold layer were then measured for comparison with the plasmonic absorption properties. On 
the other part of this study, we measure the two-photon fluorescence efficiency of gold nanorods and 
estimate it from a theoretical model. The gold nanorods are of interest to us because they can be 
conjugated to the detected biomolecules and serve as contrast agents either for the dark field 
measurement (proposed in the first part of this report) or in nonlinearly excited fluorescence. In recent 
years, there has been a renewed interest in fluorescence from noble metal nanoparticles. Much of the

III. Conclusions 
In summary, this study proposes constructing a DFM and combining a spectral measurement system to 
observe intracellular structures and classifies biomolecular interactions by nanostructure coated with 
noble metal. To test the resolution of this optical image system, linear pattern nanofeatures measuring 
160 nm to 600 nm long were imaged by DFM, and the pattern size was confirmed by AFM imaging. 
Results show that the current resolution of DFM is 160 nm, and the size of the DFM image is greater 
than the AFM image. Nanofeatures with different pitch sizes were deposited with a 50 nm gold layer by 
an e-beam evaporator. The absorption spectra were measured to identify the surface plasmon properties 
produced by the thin gold film. Moreover, specimens of ovary cancer cells and adenocarcinoma were 
also imaged by DFM, and the structure of the nuclei and some cellular organs can be clearly recognized 
using a 100X objective lens and EMCCD. Based on the current results, this optical system has the 
capacity to create images of biomolecular interaction occurring inside a cell. 
This report contains a separate study on the plasmon-enhanced fluorescence of gold nanorods; the 
nonlinear fluorescence efficiency is shown to be primarily dependent the LSP mode of gold nanorods. 
With the coupling of incoming light wavelength to the LSP mode of gold nanorods, the two photon 
fluorescence(TPF) can be magnified due to the local field enhancement. This mechanism, likewise, can 
be applied to other optical process such as scattering in DFM measurement, and the gold nanorods can 
potentially be exploited as a contrast agent in either DFM or TPF.

flashing  報告與 poster，所以，三分鐘的口頭報告準備，的確也是讓我覺得
很不同，如何在如此短的時間更精簡地傳達研究的成果。而在 poster 
section，時間是 1.5小時，其中不斷地回答未知的問題，感覺還真的不同於
以往 oral presentation，poster是近距離跟人互動，可能要重複講很多次自己
的研究，而口頭報告卻是練膽量，講一次接著回答問題，但是也考驗自己在
壓力下的應變能力(雖然自己常常會在講下一張投影片想到上一張漏掉什麼
而分心接著就造成緊張)。 
      在交談方面，尤其在此次有很多機會與國外的學者與學生講話的機會
裡，發現自己在表達研究上，可以很順地敘述，因為詞彙方面會很熟悉，但
是要聊到日常的生活，常常會卡在忘了某個字怎麼說，然後就開始結巴。不
過，因為在一旁都會觀察老師跟對方互動時聊的話題，慢慢地就會跟著問問
別人，生活的情況，氣候差易，交通差易….等等，雖然還是會常常陷入不知
道接著要講啥，但是已經是個開始，尤其自己是那種對於同語言都不見得一
見面就很能聊的個性。另外，還是覺得自己對於對方講話講很快時，還是會
有壓力。 
      在作會議記錄部分，以往在自己的國家都是用同種語言，會議記錄都只
須要寫個簡短的字或詞，回來就可以憑記憶在把整個議題紀錄下來，然而，
當大家講的是英文時，就很不一樣了，當下要努力聽得懂，大腦也在組織，
還要再寫下來，真的很不容易，等自己想好要寫什麼，大家又繼續往下講，
所以只能記錄幾個重點，幾個相關的專有名詞或術語，記錄寫起來就顯得很
不充實，經過此次，深深覺得此部分需要在找方法精進。 
      最後，謝謝國科會的贊助與老師的帶領，讓慧歆在此趟參訪的過程中受
益良多。
