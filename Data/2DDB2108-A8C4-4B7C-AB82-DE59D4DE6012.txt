3. Experimental Procedure
A single target magnetron sputtering system
was used to sputter ZnO-doped
Nd(Co1/2Ti1/2)O3 thin films from a
3-inch-diameter, 0.5 wt% ZnO-doped
Nd(Co1/2Ti1/2)O3 powder target which was
synthesized via a conventional solid-state
reaction. Those starting materials (ZnO,
Nd2O3, CoO and TiO2) were weighted
according to the composition ZnO-doped
Nd(Co1/2Ti1/2)O3 and placed in polypropylene
containers with milling media for 24 h in
distilled water. The mixture was dried at
100C, then thoroughly milled before it was
calcined at 1250C for 2 h. The calcined
powder was sintered at 1350C for 4 h. The
sintered powder was ground and sieved through
100-mesh screen to produce ZnO-doped
Nd(Co1/2Ti1/2)O3 target powder.
All of the ZnO-doped Nd(Co1/2Ti1/2)O3 films
were deposited at a fixed substrate temperature
of 150oC and an operating pressure of 5 mTorr.
With different Ar/O2 ratios (100/0 ~ 80/20) and
rf powers from 250W and 350W, the deposited
films were sputtered at the sputtering time of 5
min on the Indium Tin Oxide (ITO)/Glass
substrate. The crystalline phases of ZnO-doped
Nd(Co1/2Ti1/2)O3 film were identified by X-ray
diffraction (XRD, Rigaku D/Max . ) at 30ⅢⅤ
kV and 20 mA with Cu K radiation. The
morphology of the film surface was determined
using an atomic force microscope (AFM,
Digital Instrument DI3000).
The electrical properties of the ZnO-doped
Nd(Co1/2Ti1/2)O3 thin films were evaluated by
capacitance-voltage (C–V) and current-voltage
(I–V) measurements. The test samples with
metal-insulator-semiconductor (MIM)
capacitor structures were prepared on
ITO/Glass substrates. The sample devices
(Pt/ZnO-doped Nd(Co1/2Ti1/2)O3/ITO/Glass)
were fabricated by symmetrically depositing Pt
electrodes (diameter 0.8 mm) on top sides of
the ZnO-doped Nd(Co1/2Ti1/2)O3/ITO/Glass
structure. The C-V and the I-V curves of the
MIM capacitors were measured by using a
HP4192 Impedance Analyzer and a HP4155
Parameter Analyzer, respectively.
The radio frequency measurement technique
described here requires that the dielectric film
be deposited on a metal layer and capped with a
top metal electrode layer. The structure of
sample is Pt/ZnO-doped
Nd(Co1/2Ti1/2)O3/ITO/Glass structure (Fig. 1).
The top electrode was prepared by using rf
magnetron sputtering and patterned into suitable
test structures by photolithography. A
ground-signal probe is connected to vector
network analyzer to measure the capacitance of
the sample structures. The probe makes
contact to the center circular patch of metal
which functions as the top electrode of the
capacitor, and the metal outside the ring which
functions effectively as the ground. The metal
patterning of the dielectric is avoided because of
the probe is not directly contact to bottom
electrode. The electrical path to the bottom
electrode of the capacitor is made through the
large capacitor formed between the bottom
metal layer and the large top ground [12]. The
dielectric properties of ZnO-doped
Nd(Co1/2Ti1/2)O3 films will be evaluated with
using the capacitance value.

Fig. 5 shows a type J-(E)1/2 curves of the
ZnO-doped Nd(Co1/2Ti1/2)O3 films deposited on
ITO/Glass substrate at different Ar/O2 ratios
and rf powers obtained from 44 nm-thick to 56
nm-thick ZnO-doped Nd(Co1/2Ti1/2)O3 films
with positive bias ( from 0V to 1V ) to the top
electrode. When leakage currents of
ZnO-doped Nd(Co1/2Ti1/2)O3 films are plotted
as a function of electric field, it is observed that
they follow either Schottky emission or
Frenkel-Poole emission. Schottky emission
occurs at the interface between the films and
the electrode where a Schottky barrier is
formed. It is shown that the leakage currents
are almost proportional to the electrical field.
This effect was attributed to the absorption
current due to the dielectric relaxation
phenomena of the ZnO-doped Nd(Co1/2Ti1/2)O3
capacitors. Schottky emission occurs at the
interface between the films and the electrode
where a Schottky barrier is formed. This result
indicates that the I–V characteristics of
ZnO-doped Nd(Co1/2Ti1/2)O3 capacitors can be
explained by Schottky emission in the external
electric field region [13,14].
The C-V curves of ZnO-doped
Nd(Co1/2Ti1/2)O3 films were measured by
Pt/ZnO-doped Nd(Co1/2Ti1/2)O3/ITO/Glass
(MIM) structure, and the applied DC voltage
was swept from -5 to 5V in the measurement at
1 kHz. By using the fiat-band characteristic
caused by the measuring process itself, the
dielectric constant of the ZnO-doped
Nd(Co1/2Ti1/2)O3 films will be calculated form
the values of the capacitance. The dielectric
constants of the ZnO-doped Nd(Co1/2Ti1/2)O3
films deposited at different Ar/O2 ratios and rf
powers are shown in Fig. 6. The dielectric
constant is influenced by the grain uniformity
and crystallinity. The dielectric constant of the
ZnO-doped Nd(Co1/2Ti1/2)O3 films decreased
with increasing Ar partial pressure and rf power.
With an Ar/O2 ratio of 80/20 and rf power of
250W, a dielectric constant of 28.6 was obtained.
It may be understood the dielectric constant
depends on the microstructure of the
ZnO-doped Nd(Co1/2Ti1/2)O3 thin films and is
related with to the process parameters.

Nd(Co1/2Ti1/2)O3 thin films with different Ar/O2

Mechanical properties of double-walled carbon nanocoil : A molecular dynamics study
Meng-Hsiung Weng1, Jenn-Sen Lin2*, Ji-Jun Zhao3, Ming-Chang Chen1, Hsu-Cheng Hsing 4, Yimu Lee4, Chun-Hung Lai4,
Pai-Chuan Yang4
1 Department of Mechanical and Electro-Mechanical Engineering, National Sun Yat-sen University, Kaohsiung, 804
Taiwan
2 Department of Mechanical Engineering, National United University, Miao-Li, 36003, Taiwan
3 School of Physics and Optoelectronic Engineerin, Dalian University of Technology, Dalian city, Liaoning Province
116024, China
4 College of electrical engineering and computer science, National United University, Miao-Li, 36003, Taiwan
*Corresponding author (jsenlin@nuu.edu.tw)
Keywords: Nanocoil, Nanotube, mechanical properties, molecular dynamics.
Abstract: Carbon nanocoils have unique 3D structure with great potential applications as mechanical components such as resonating
elements and nanosprings [1,2]. In this study, the molecular dynamics is employed to investigate the mechanical properties of the
(5,5)@(10,10) double-walled carbon nanocoils. The second reactive empirical bond order (REBO) potential is employed to model
the interaction between carbon atoms. The yielding stress, maximum tensile strength, and Young’s modulus can be determined from 
the tensile test. We used the calculation of slip vector to observe the dynamic behavior and structure situation of atom of carbon
nanotube in strength process. The mechanical properties of (5,5)@(10,10) double-wall carbon nanocoils also compared with the that
of (5,5) single-wall carbon nanocoils and (10,10) single-wall carbon nanocoils.
