  III
Abstract 
This project researched a 100-lbf liquid rocket for ab upper-stage rocket in 
conjunction with the development of the launch vehicle for satellite.  In the third year’s 
project, the sub-project “Research of a 100-lbf Hypergolic Liquid Rocket Injector Plate” 
designed and manufactured the injector plate of the ground-test liquid rocket.  The hot 
fire test results showed that, at the designed operation condition, the combustor pressure 
of the liquid rocket engine was 153.2 psi, and the measured sea-level thrust was 62.1 lb, 
and the characteristic exhaust velocity reached 1429.2 m/s.  And the calculated 
vacuum thrust was 99.8 lb, and the vacuum Isp was 295.8 s, which met the project’s 
objective.  The wall temperature of the combustor revealed that the film cooling 
mechanism of the injector plate worked well during the operation of the rocket. The 
highest temperature region was observed at the nozzle throat (~1000K).   
“Computer Simulation Analyses of a 100-lbf Liquid Rocket Combustor” has 
developed a three-dimensional spray-combustion simulation model. The simulation 
results showed that, increasing the combustor length expands flame combustion time 
and may reduce the combustion efficiency. Larger nozzle inlet angle would leads to a 
significant friction loss on wall structure. Higher combustor pressure may result from 
the reduction of the throat diameter. 
“Combustion Instability Analyses of a 100-lbf Liquid Rocket” focused on the analysis 
of the suppression mechanism for combustion instability. The results showed that 
baffles reasonably suppressed tangential sound-wave resonance, and the height of the 
baffle’s significantly enhanced the effectiveness of suppression. Compare to the 
quarter-wave resonator, Helmholtz resonator showed a better suppression effect.    
The project also designed the combustor of the ground test liquid rocket engine. In the 
hot fire tests, low-frequency chamber pressure oscillations were observed. The 
corresponding pressure oscillation was less than 0.3 psi, which had no observable 
influence on the performance of the designed rocket engine. 
 
 
 
Keywords: liquid rocket, propellant, injector plate, combustion simulation, combustor
