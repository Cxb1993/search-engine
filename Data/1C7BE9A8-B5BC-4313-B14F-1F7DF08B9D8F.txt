  
2.研究目的 
差溫成形的金屬流動容易，若能適當的控制溫度的分佈，就能以簡單的方法鍛壓出複雜
之零件的初步形狀，進而實現零件近淨成形。目前對於電氣成形的零件加工，主要用於高徑
比大的零件加工，如汽機車之軸盤類汽門零件。電氣鍛粗技術具有設備投資較低、鍛件桿部
尺寸精度較高、各項品質優異等特點。缺點是變形方式為自由鍛粗，變形區周向應力為拉應
力，對一些塑性較差的材料變形不利，容易產生裂痕等缺陷，製品不良率高。 
本研究的動機是為了能夠獲得更多差溫成形的相關資料及電氣成形之多元化零件的成形
加工，因此以有限元素模擬軟體及實驗進行相關參數之研究，增加有關於差溫成形及電氣成
形的相關資訊，期望能對產業界與學術界提供有價值的參考依據。具體言之，主要研究的目
的有：探討電氣加熱時，電壓及接觸電阻率等參數之影響。探討不同管材幾何形狀之差溫電
氣端鍛成形後，應力、應變、微觀組織等變化。藉由擴口成形製程，以了解電氣成形用於其
他零件加工之可行性。 
 
3.理論基礎 
3.1 差溫電氣端鍛成形原理 
差溫原理為希望金屬材料在某個區域的流動性能夠特別顯著，因此在塑性成形區域進行
局部加熱，使局部加熱區域產生溫度差異，提高局部的溫度與塑性變形能力如圖 1 所示，使
成形加工較容易及成形負荷降低，此差溫成形技術可節省多道次模具的製造費用，降低生產
成本及時間。差溫電氣端鍛製程技術廣泛應用於高徑比大的端鍛成形(例如氣門製造)，此種
成形法係利用材料本身之電阻，通以低電壓大電流，使電流通過處產生熱量進而達到加熱之
效果，其原理及配置方式如圖 1 所示。 
3.3 管材擴口成形理論[2] 
管材的塑性加工與板材塑性加工相比，雖然從變形性質、變形特點方面來看，有許多相
似之處，但在製造方法、製造參數選擇及防止產生品質缺陷而採取的製造措施等方面，都存
在很大的不同。管材的塑性加工中，往往容易產生品質缺陷。管材的擴口成形係將管胚端部
直徑擴大的一種成形加工。根據管件的使用要求，擴口加工可製造出管端為錐形、筒形或其
他形狀的零件。管材擴口在管件連接中，得到了廣泛應用。常見的擴口形狀如圖 2 所示。如

6.結論 
本文針對差溫電氣成形中主要之製程參數進行研究分析，以有限元素軟體 DEFORM-2D
進行參數的模擬，並將所得之結果進行實驗比較分析，比較模擬與成形之形狀及探討微觀組
等影響與變化。從結果之探討與分析可歸納出下列幾項結論： 
1. 在差溫電氣加熱過程中，加熱的速度主要受到電壓、接觸電阻率及幾何形狀等影響。當
接觸面積著增加其接觸電阻率也會增加，加熱至目標溫度所需加熱時間也愈久。 
2.  當電壓值過高而管材厚度過薄時，材料則會因為電壓過大使加熱速過快，導致晶粒粗大
化甚至材料融化。因此，當管材厚度減少時，加熱電壓也需要降低，以防止缺陷之產生。

2. RESEARCH METHODS 
2.1 Finite element method 
FEM is a powerful analytic method with universal applications and geometrical adaptability in metal 
forming, which shows great superiority and practicability in the predictions of forming load, shape and 
dimension variations, and stress and strain distributions in deformed billets, as well as the determination of 
optimal process parameters. Moreover, utilizing FEM numerical simulation results for differential heating 
process analysis could enable us to comprehend metal plastic flow behavior, local thermal parameters and 
analyze the variation processes of the microstructure inside the work piece.  
2.2 Analysis of grain refinement

air-cooled for 4sec (the time to move from the heater to the forging machine), differential heating forging

3.3 Average grain size 
Figure 9 shows the distributions of grain size and strain with a height-diameter ratio 3.33, and under different 
compressing amounts of 25%H, 50%H, and 75%H. When the compressing amount was 25%H, the large 
strain area was concentrated at the blank center, thus, the grain refinement area at the blank center was 
significant, with a minimum average grain size 10.6μm. As the pressing amount increased to 50%H, the 
large strain area would move to the outer edge of the blank, and the grain refinement area would move as 
well, with a minimum average grain size of 8μm. As the pressing amount increased to 75%H, the large strain 
area was at the blank center and the outer edge, respectively, and the grain refinement area would move to 
the blank center and the outer edge, with a minimum average grain size of 4.5μm, under sufficient 
temperature conditions.  
Figure 10 shows the average grain size upon being heated under the same heating power, but with different 
height-diameter ratios, and differentially forged at 25%H and then water quenched. As seen, as the height-
diameter ratio decreased, the grain refinement area moved from the blank center to the end edge, since the 
maximum strain area gradually moved to the blank end edge. As seen in Figure A, the minimum average 
grain size was 11μm; in Figure B, the minimum average grain size was 10.6μm; in Figure C, the minimum 
average grain size was 10.6μm, while in Figure D, the minimum average grain size was 11.6μm. 
  
pressing amount was 50%H, the profile shape factor was the highest; when the pressing amount was 
75%H, the profile shape factor would decrease. When heated at the middle range, under the same 
diameter but different heating powers, since the middle-range temperature was high and coincided with 
that in the middle-range expansion area of free forging, the profile shape factor was the highest when 
the pressing amount was 75%H. 
3. Regarding the average grain size, when the height-diameter ratio was 3.33 and compression amount 
was 25%H, the grain refinement area was concentrated at the blank center. When the pressing amount 
increased to 50%H, the grain refinement area moved to the outer edge of the blank. When the pressing 
amount increased to 75%H, the grain refinement area moved to the blank center and the end edge, 
respectively. Under sufficient temperature, the grain refinement area would be at the maximum 
equivalent effective strain area. As the strain amount increased, the grain refinement would be more 
significant. When heated with different height-diameter ratios, but under a fixed heating power, 
differentially forged at 25%H and then water quenched, as the height-diameter ratio decreased, the 
grain refinement area moved from the blank center to the end edge, since the maximum strain area 
moved gradually to the blank end edge, resulting in the movement of the grain refinement area.
