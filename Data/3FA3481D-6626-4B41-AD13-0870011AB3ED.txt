中文摘要 
小牛血清蛋白是免疫學分析以及食品科學研究裡最重要的蛋白質。食物在加工過程中熱能的輸
入，會造成蛋白質的熱變性，產生物理、化學以及生物性的變化，導致其結構上的改變並造成食品品
質問題，甚至是食品衛生之安全問題。因此如何即時觀察蛋白質之熱變性過程，並推測其結構的變化，
例如展開與摺疊，是一重要的課題。目前許多量測蛋白質結構的方法如蛋白質電泳法、X-ray晶格繞
射、圓的二色性光譜法，皆無法完整研究 BSA快速的熱變性現象。在本研究中，我們將發展一套角度
放大率可增進十倍以上的光學外差式偏光儀，以及致冷晶片之加熱系統，以量測蛋白質對於溫度改變
之旋光角度的即時變化及雷射穿透功率的改變，來探討 BSA詳細的熱變性過程，並搭配蛋白質電泳法
來佐證其結果。 
關鍵詞：小牛血清蛋白; 熱變性; 旋光量測 
英文摘要 
Bovine serum albumin (BSA), a highly complex protein, plays important functions in food systems and 
in immunology analysis. In food processing, heat treatments are frequently required for a variety of purposes 
and they can change the physical, chemical, biological and functional properties of proteins affecting the 
quality and acceptability of food products. Therefore, during thermal processing it is important to understand, 
monitor and control protein changes, particularly protein unfolding. There are many methods to detect protein 
structures, such as X-ray diffraction crystallography, circular dichroism spectroscopy and optical rotation 
dispersion. However, these methods are difficult to monitor the denaturation process of BSA. In this study, we 
propose an optical heterodyne polarimeter capable of more than 10-fold of amplification factor in measuring 
the optical rotation angle and a thermoelectric cooler in controlling the sample temperature. We will observe 
thermal denaturation of BSA by measuring the real-time changes of optical rotation angle, and then use 
electrophoresis to further study the thermal effects of BSA. 
Key words: Bovine serum albumin; thermal denaturation; optical rotation meassurement 
 
前言 
小牛血清蛋白(Bovine Serum Albumin, BSA)，存在於牛乳乳清蛋白中[1-4]，是免疫學分析以及
食品科學研究裡最重要的蛋白質之一。其二級結構包含了 50-55%的α螺旋(α-helix) 、15-18%的β
平板(β-sheet) 以及 27-35 %的隨機圈(random coil) [5]，但是 1989 年 Carter 等人使用 X-ray 晶
格繞射之技術研究 BSA之二級結構，卻指出 BSA中不存在β-平板的結構 [6]。BSA為一種大型橢球狀
蛋白質，其分子量為 66.430 kDa，根據序列分析其結構包含三個功能性區域(domain)，而每一個區域
由兩個大的與一個小的迴圈(loop) 結構所組成[7]。圖 1(a) 為 BSA 的立體構造圖 1(b) 中管狀的地方
即為α螺旋[8]。

BSA 是免疫學分析以及食品科學研究裡最重要的蛋白質，在受到各種物理、化學、以及生物性的
外在因素作用下，會造成結構上的改變，其中以熱變性現象最被廣泛研究。如前所介紹，目前已有諸
多應用於研究 BSA 的熱變性的方法。蛋白質電泳法只能得到 BSA 熱變性後的最終結果，無法探討 BSA
詳細的變性過程以及精確的變性溫度。而圓偏振二色性光譜可得知 BSA 的結構在攝氏 60~70 度區間內
有較明顯的變化，並將此區間定義為變性溫度區間，但精確的變性溫度無法得知。差分掃描量法以及
旋光量測可根據實驗曲線上的轉折點，推測 BSA 之結構應在此特定溫度下發生劇烈的改變，並定義其
為變性溫度，除此之外，亦可針對 BSA詳細的熱變性過程作深入研究。 
然而後現已被發展出的旋光量測系統-偏光計，多建立在以量測強度訊號為主軸，但強度量測常會
遇到波動(fluctuation)而造成訊雜比下降的缺點。為了使所能解析到的訊號更好，干涉技術於是被利
用來作為量測的方法，此時以相位作為量測訊號才被發展，但是一般的干涉儀仍會受到環境擾動而使
得訊號模糊。為了改善這樣的情形，共光程外差干涉技術 (optical common-path heterodyne 
interferometry)於是被採用了。在本計畫中，我們將引用了一相位可調式波片(phase-variable 
waveplate, δ-WP)，來達到斜率放大的目的，使得旋光角度的訊號量測能更靈敏，並可利用相位可調
式波片來選擇所要放大的倍率，使得系統更為簡單，且將以實際量測到的數據來評估系統的解析能力，
之後系統的發展上，更可利用茲曼雷射(Zeeman laser)來取代目前的外差光源，使得系統更為小巧。
國內在研究蛋白質結構的熱變性尚是處女地，根據筆者有限之認知並無此方面之研究。尤其是利用可
放大旋光角之光學外差式方法，不論在國外或國內均應屬首創。 
研究目的 
本研究之目的是要建構能放大旋光角改變的倍數之光學外差式偏光儀，並搭配自行架設的加熱系
統，設計各種不同的 BSA 加熱流程，利用量測 BSA旋光角度的即時變化以及雷射穿透功率的改變，來
深入探討 BSA詳細的熱變性過程，並研究熱變性後其結構上的可逆性與不可逆性。除了物理性的光學
方法，本研究並搭配蛋白質電泳法，來直接驗證 BSA在熱變性後的結構變化情形。

Investigation of the thermally structural denaturation of bovine serum 
albumin by a home-made optical heterodyne polarimeter 
 
Chien-ming Wu  
Department of Biomedical Engineering and Environmental Sciences, National TsingHua University, Hsinchu, 
Taiwan, R.O.C. 
ABSTRACT   
Bovine serum albumin (BSA) solutions (2.5 %, PH 7.2) under heat treatment can result in structural changes. Some of the α
-helices are transformed at 67℃ to random coils resulting in an increased rotation angle. With subsequent heating, the transformed 
random coils may once again transform to non-native β-sheets and restore the optical rotation angle. These two states are all 
reversible. However, when the heating temperature goes up to 69℃ the denatured BSA starts to transform into a rigid network and 
becomes an irreversible state. The reversible and irreversible temperatures of BSA were determined and discussed. 
 
Keywords: Heterodyne polarimeter, bovine serum albumin, thermal denaturation. 
1. INTRODUCTION 
Bovine serum albumin, a highly complex protein, is one of the most frequently studied proteins in food systems and in immunology 
analysis [1-5]. Previous studies indicate that the secondary structures of BSA contain about 50-55% α-helix, 15-18% β-sheet and 
27-35% random coils [6]. In contrast, based on X-ray crystallography, there are no β-sheets in the structure of the native form [7]. 
When heat is applied to BSA, the kinetic energy and the molecular vibration increase, and as a result, the hydrogen bonds and 
non-polar hydrophobic interactions are interrupted [8-9]. Harmsen and Braam [10], using infra-red and optical rotation dispersion 
(ORD) spectra concluded that alkali or heat denaturation caused a partial loss of alpha-helical structure with no formation of 
β-sheets. However, from their infra-red spectra, a shoulder appeared for BSA heated above 72°C at 1620 cm-1, which is indicative 
of beta-sheet formation. Clark et al. [11] using infra-red and Raman spectra reported that gels formed from heat and that chemically 
denatured proteins arose from the interaction of regions of beta-sheets. They also noted that a shoulder was visible on their infra-red 
spectra at 1620 cm-1 for BSA heated to 75°C and above. Further studies have indicated that BSA when heat-treated, goes through 
two structural stages. The first stage is reversible while the second stage is irreversible, but it does not necessarily result in a 
complete destruction of the ordered structure [12-13]. Results from circular dichroism (CD) and infra-red (IR) spectroscopy 
indicated that β-sheets were formed when albumin was heated above 65°C [14], 70°C [13]. Recently, Teepakorn et al. [15] using 
optical rotation (OR) and transmitted light (TL) methods reported that the denaturation temperatures (Td) obtained from OR 
measurements were consistent with reported differential scanning calorimetry (DSC) values.  
In the present study, instead of measuring the sample’s spectra by IR [10-14], CD [13], DSC [15], as mentioned above, or other 
methods such as X-ray diffraction crystallography [16-17], and optical rotation dispersion [18-19], we proposed a novel method to 
measure the changes in the optical activity of the sample. We used a home-made optical heterodyne polarimeter, which could
