Here we select the 3-UPU parallel manipulator [19] as 
the topological structure of the new knee ligament arthrometer. 
As shown in Fig. 7, the 3-UPU parallel mechanism is a spatial 
mechanism with three degree-of-freedom, and there are three 
identical limbs connecting between the upper platform and the 
lower (fixed) platform. Each limb consists of one upper 
member and one lower member, which are connected by a 
sliding joint (or prismatic joint, denoted as P). The 
upper/lower member of the limb is connected to the 
upper/lower platform by a universal joint (denoted as U). 
Hence in each limb the sequence of the joints becomes U-P-U. 
We may simplify the structure by using two limbs only, that is, 
2-UPU, and the kinematic behavior of the parallel manipulator 
remains the same.
