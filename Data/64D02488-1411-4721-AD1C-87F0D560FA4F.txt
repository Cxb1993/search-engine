研究計畫中文摘要  
人類對能源的需求與日俱增，本研究旨在嘗試行動再生能源的開發，透過發電鞋機構
與實驗的設計，從事步行發電相關特性之研究。藉著開發步行發電鞋具，讓穿戴者能夠邊
行走邊發電。本計畫順利完成以下: 設計三款發電鞋具，用以實現步行發電; 建構步行發
電數學模型，藉由步態分析數據與發電機特性，描述合理之步行發電量; 執行發電鞋具實
測，分析所設計之發電鞋發電量；另設計一足踝模擬器，係一台三自由度運動之機器腳，
做為鞋具評估工具，可穿戴發電鞋具於矢狀面上週而復始仿真人行走或踩踏，藉此定量的
評估發電鞋具之特性。最後嘗試開發之能源分享平台一項，受限於發電鞋發電功率，仍有
待更進一步之研究與改進。 
 
關鍵字: 行動再生能源、步態生物力學、發電鞋 
 
研究計畫英文摘要 
Retrieving the mobile and renewable energy is innovative and practical for people with their 
portable devices that are more and more widely used. With the design of power generating shoe 
(PG shoe) and experimental setup, this study aimed to retrieve the renewable energy from human 
walking. The PG shoe has been developed to convert the walking mechanical energy to the 
electric energy with the driving force during heel strike. Several issues have been done as follows: 
A PG shoe was developed and tested their performance. A mathematic model of PG shoe was 
established. This mathematic model is constructed by the gait analysis data and the formula of 
power generator. An ankle-foot simulator was designed and set up for testing the PG shoe with 
the simulation of gait pattern. The simulator could measure the power generation and failure 
mechanism of PG shoe objectively. However, to increase the efficiency of retrieving energy from 
walking is needed for further design of Energy-Sharing Platform. 
 
 
Keywords: mobile renewable energy, gait biomechanics, power generating shoe

(2) 步行發電數學模型建構與特性分析 
 
不同的鞋底設計將影響步態，因此本研究透過專利分析彙整出之模擬發電鞋(圖 1)(圖 
6)，分析其對應的步態與對應的合理發電功率。「步行發電數學模型」整合了 1. 步態-發電
機做功轉速、 2. 發電機發電特性兩者。 
 
步態-發電機做功轉速 
本研究收集了 7 位健康年輕人，隨機穿著三款模擬發電鞋(圖 1a, c, d)，透過動作分析
系統 Vicon370 量測步行站立期之發電機做功轉速。如前文提到，三款模擬發電鞋並無實際
之發電機，因此間接量測其鞋底彈簧處之垂直形變速度，再反推發電機做功轉速。鞋具
markers 貼法如(圖 6)所示。從先前的研究得知，發電鞋鞋底垂直外凸型 (圖 1b)，其垂直
上下的運動軌跡對步態運動學影響甚大，行走時受測者主觀描述亦很不舒服，因此實驗初
期即捨棄分析該型鞋具。

建構、發電鞋特性分析、足踝模擬器設計等。藉由步態分析，瞭解發電鞋設計潛在對人步

運動學資料可以發現，TYPE 1 與 TYPE 2 明
顯比 TYPE 3 與 TYPE 4 於各關節角度相較控制

態。此外，於步頻、步速、跨步長及跨步時間之
四個參數亦同。 
會有上述結果，研判是 TYPE 1 與 TYPE 2
都是鞋底明顯外凸形式，類似穿著高跟鞋，導致
受試者腳跟提早著地，各關節自然補償所造成。 
TYPE 3 與 TYPE 4 均屬於鞋底挖空形式，鞋
底板接近水平，兩者差異在於鞋底挖空量。兩者
於擺盪末期、腳跟著地期以及腳跟承重期於踝關
節角度上與控制組均達顯著差異，會有如此結
果，研判是鞋底挖空與彈簧反彈雙重效應影響，
於腳跟著地期與腳跟承重期，彈簧反彈力量加速
了腳跟滾動(heel rocker)的過程，造成踝關節背屈
角度減少約 5°。造成擺盪末期的踝關節背曲角度
減少的原因仍不明，研判可能與鞋底板組裝時雖
要求水平，但受限內嵌彈簧的長度，仍有些許外
凸偏差(<3 度)所致，或與鞋子重心的改變有關。 
於步頻、跨步長、跨步時間和單腳站立期時
間上，受試者穿戴 TYPE 3 的表現相較控制組未達
顯著差異。於步速、單腳站立期時間受試者穿戴
TYPE 4 的表現相較控制組未達顯著差異。 
TYPE 3 與 TYPE 4 兩組於運動學角度上，相
較控制組達到顯著差異之時期相同，然於 TYPE 
3，在步頻、跨步長、跨步時間的表現相較 TYPE 4
是更接近控制組。 
 
 
五、結論 
 
現有發電鞋設計可簡化歸類成鞋底外凸式與
鞋底挖空式，實驗結果顯示，鞋底外凸式(TYPE 1
與 TYPE 2)會造成使用者多處關節角度及步頻、
跨步長、步速、跨步時間和單腳站立期時間的改
變，影響到原步態行為。鞋底挖空式(TYPE 3 與
TYPE 4)於踝關節角度亦有改變，但相對於外凸式
的鞋款，整體而言更接近控制組步態。 
雖然穿戴 TYPE 3、TYPE 4 鞋子於實驗數據
中無法明確指出何者較接近控制組步態，但由於
TYPE 3 鞋底的挖空量較 TYPE 4 少，鞋底外型更
接近控制組鞋子，理論上可以提供較佳之足底支
撐穩定性。

Chi-Wei Chou1, Hung-Jen Lai1, Yi-Hung Chen1, Wen-Chuan Chen1, Chung-Huang Yu2, 
Cheng-Kung Cheng1  
1Institute of Biomedical Engineering, National Yang-Ming University, Taipei, Taiwan      
2Department of Physical Therapy and Assistive Technology, National Yang-Ming University, Taipei,

Introduction: Power generation in human biomechanics, especially in walking, has been proposed 
in recent years. Most power generating shoes are equipped with mechanisms at heels where the 
maximal ground reaction forces (GRF) act. However there were few researches about the 
efficiencies of the mechanism designs because of the technical difficulties in measuring sole 
deformation in gait analysis. This study compared (1) deformations of springs mounted at medial 
and lateral heels of the specialized shoes, and (2) GRFs with the controlled shoes. Methods: Six 
subjects underwent gait analysis including three-dimensional kinematics and GRFs in random order 
of wearing the specialized shoes and the control shoes. The laterally extruded bars prevented the 
markers from shielding and provided an even level for measuring the spring height. Vertical 
deformations of the medial and the lateral springs were calculated respectively. Results: The 
average deformations of medial and lateral springs were 15.6mm and 17.8mm. The occurrence of 
the first, second peak GRF were 34msec, 20msec later than that of the controlled shoes. The first 
peak GRF was 11% lower in wearing the specialized shoes than in the controlled shoes. Discussion 
and conclusion: The sole deformations of the power generating shoes were correlated with the 
theoretical power. The results showed greater sole deformations in the lateral side could provide 
information to power generating shoes designs in the future. Nevertheless, the spring effect might 
provide shock absorption in the heel contact phase. Therefore the peak GRF was lower and delayed 
comparing to the GRF in the controlled shoes.

III. RESULTS 
 
The developed walking robot could provide a new 
method of testing AFOs by simulating subject’s walking 
pattern with their gait data. Figure 4 showed robot 
walking on a forceplate, from heel-strike to toe-off, then 
return for next cycle. Comparing to the target gait data, 
three parameters of robotic gait were analyzed. The RMS 
accuracy ± mean SD, (accuracy range) and (SD range) of 
robotic “vertical ground reaction force” were 80.5N ± 
9.5N, (-234.4N ~ 102N) and (1.6N ~ 36.5N) respectively 
[Fig 5]. Also the parameter of robotic “tilting angle of 
foot” were 1.9° ± 0.5°, (- 4.4° ~ 1.6°) and (0.0° ~ 2.3°) 
[Fig 6]. The parameter of robotic “angle of ankle joint” 
were 2.1 °  ± 0.3 ° , (-3.3 °  ~ 1.9 ° ) and (0.0 °  ~ 1.4 ° ) 
respectively [Fig 7].

In this study, the peak knee adduction moment significantly reduced up to 29.6% and 20.0% 
in type II and type III shoes. The first peak of GRF also significantly decreased 8% and 13% 
respectively. These results supported the idea that the peak knee adduction moment in walking 
could be decreased by a shock absorber designed on the heel of shoes.  
The first peak of GRF represented for the maximum reaction force given in the loading 
response phase where the center of pressure was under heel pad. Type II and type III shoes both 
utilize the spring deformation to lower the GRF. The mechanism of type II shoes could store the 
reaction force until the heel-off phase and decrease the knee adduction moment in the mid-stance 
phase. In walking trials with type I shoes, some subjects reported that the discomfort from the 
elevated heel. This matched with the results of previous researches about the high-heel shoes: the 
higher heel caused the higher knee joint load in walking. 
The results also revealed that the reduction of knee adduction moments were diverse in 
wearing three designs of shoes due to several variables affecting the moments through the bearing 
joints were not investigated in this study, such as walking speed, tibial rotation angle and toe-out 
angle. Future studies should focus on figuring out the relations of factors that affect the peak knee 
adduction to differentiate the subjects. The design of intervention shoes should also modified for 
OA patients to see the outcomes and effects. 
 
CONCLUSION 
      
Wearing an intervention shoe with spring based outsole can reduce the peak knee adduction 
moment in healthy subjects during walking.
