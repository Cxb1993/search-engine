2Computer Simulation
Ala and Lac were simulated using GAVA 
software package [4] using the following 
parameter. doublet resonance at 1.47 ppm and a 
quartet at 3.78 ppm; Lac: doublet  resonance at 
1.33 ppm and a quartet at  4.10 ppm; The spectra 
were simulated under 1.5 Tesla and PRESS 
localization module with following parameters: 
bandwidth = 2500 Hz,  sample points = 4096, 
TE =35/135/270 msec. In order to investigate 
the effect  of different  Full-Width-Half-
Maximum (FWHM), two FWHM of 0.02 ppm 
and 0.033 ppm were used for simulation. For 
each setting, the concentration of Ala is 
continuously adjusted to match the ratio of Ala 
to Lac from 1:1, 1:2, 1:3 to 1:10. A total of ten 
[Ala] to [Lac] ratios were simulated. 
MRS quantification
The simulated spectrum with different FWHM 
at different TE was then analyzed by LCModel. 
Two basis sets were applied to fit the simulated 
spectrum. The first basis set is provided by 
LCModel (LCM), which was generated by 
metabolic solutions in vitro. The second basis 
set was generated using GAVA containing 15 
metabolites (GAVA), in order to be consistent 
with the basis set of LCModel. Spectral fitting 
with LCModel was restricted to fit only Ala and 
Lac for simulated spectrum. Concentration 
ratios of Ala to Lac were then calculated. The 
accuracy  was evaluated by the comparison of 
fitted Ala/Lac ratio versus real Ala/Lac ratio. We 
also calculate the errors of fitted Ala/Lac ratio to 
real ratio.

involved in two BASIS sets.  The 1.5T BASIS 
spectra acquired from in-vitro solution 
phantoms provided by  LCModel is shown in 
(A).  Spectra simulated by GAMMA with the 
same parameters of (A) were shown in (B). 
Only nine of 15 most used metabolites are 
shown here.   The lineshape of metabolites: Ins, 
NAA+NAAG, Gln, Glu, GABA,Cr, and PCH

J-coupling resonances of Lac and Ala in 
simulated spectra (Fig.2B) are more obvious 
than the spectra acquired from solution 
phantoms (Fig.2A).
 The concentration ratios of Ala/Lac 
obtained by LCModel using solution BASIS set 
and GAVA simulated BASIS set with three 
different TEs: 35, 135, and 270 ms are shown in 
Table 1 for FWHM  0.02 ppm and in Table 2 for 
FWHM  = 0.033 ppm respectively.   The input 
spectra of Ala and Lac were simulated by 
GAVA with FWHM of 0.02ppm.
 Figure 3 shows error estimation of the 
concentration ratios for the spectra simulated 
with FWHM 0.02 ppm while FWHM 0.033 
ppm in figure 4, and analyzed using solution 
BASIS set and GAVA simulated  BASIS set 
with TE 35, 135, and 270 ms respectively. 
Analysis using GAVA simulated BASIS set 
achieved lower error on average, which is 
reasonable because the input spectra were 
identical with the Ala and Lac spectra in GAVA 
BASIS set.  For the analysis using LCModel 
solution BASIS set, spectra of TE 135 have 
achieved better performance than other TEs.
Our result shows that the TE 135 may 
provide more reliable quantification result  for 
Ala/Lac identification.  The choice of BASIS 
sets can be also very  important for subtle 
quantification of metabolites which the 
resonance are overlaid  by each other. 
Therefore, investigation on those issues should 
be further studied in order for more accuracy on 
diagnosis of abscesses.
