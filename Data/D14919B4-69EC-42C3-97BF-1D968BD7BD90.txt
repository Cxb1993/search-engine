Abstract 
Chitosan is the second most abundant nature polysaccharide having a similar structure as 
glycoaminoglycans (GAGs) which are the main components of extra cellular matrix (ECM). Due 
to its relatively lower cost as compared to GAGs, many researches had tried to look for the 
application of chitosan in tissue engineering. The amino group at C2 of chitosan is thought as the 
major cationic functional group with special biological characteristics in cell culture. In addition, 
studies have indicated the incorporation of sulfonic or phosphonic functionalities would be 
beneficial to the growth of certain cells. However, no study has explored the effect of 
incorporation of both abovementioned anionic functionalities onto the chitosan structure. 
In this study, we have surface-phosphorylated the polyelectrolyte membrane formed by 
chitosan and water soluble sulfonated chitosan with an aim to incorporate phosphonic and 
sulfonic functionalities into the membrane surface. Surface analyses by ESCA and ATR-FTIR 
have shown that these two functional groups have been successfully grafted onto the surface. And 
the ratio of P/S was dependent upon the weight ratio of phosphorylation agents added. Although 
the surface contact angle value was increased after surface phosphorylation, surface of these 
polyelectrolyte complexes remained to be hydrophilic and smooth. 
Despite the plasma recalcification time was decreased after surface phosphorylation, these 
chitosan polyelectrolyte complexes remained less blood-coagulative than the non-treated chitosan 
and chitosan that has been directly surface-phosphorylated. In addition, these phosphorylated 
polyelectrolyte complexes showed similar or slightly less platelet-reactivity than the 
non-phosphorylated counterpart. In contrast, significant platelet activation and adhesion were 
noted on the direct-phosphorylated chitosan. This implicated the incorporation of sulfonic acid 
onto the chitosan surface can increase the platelet compatibility. Adipose-derived stem cell 
incubation study has demonstrated that the incorporation of both phosphonic and sulfonic

Abstract  
 
Glycosaminoglycans (GAGs) are the main components of extra cellular matrix (ECM). 
Studies have indicated that scaffolds modified by GAGs could improve cell proliferation and 
differentiation. Chitosan, the second most abundant nature polysaccharide, has a similar structure 
as GAGs Due to its relatively lower cost as compared to GAGs, many researches had tried to 
look for the application of chitosan in tissue engineering. With an aim to imitate the low 
molecular weight glycoaminoglycan such as chondroitin sulfate or keratin sulfate in structure, 
this study tried to directly sulfonate the hydroxy groups at C6 of chitosan. At the same time, 
protection strategy was used to keep the amino groups at C2 of chitosan intact, which was known 
as the major positive functional group of chitosan with special biological characteristics in cell 
culture. Various surface chemical characterization results as well as surface zeta potential 
measurement have indicated that both sulfonate/sulfonic and amino functionalities were 
coexistent on the deprotected sulfonated chitosan specimen. In vitro platelet adhesion testing has 
shown that such a deprotected sulfonated chitosan membrane can increase the amount of platelet 
adhesion while keep those adhered remained unactivated. In addition, to imitate the character of 
phospholipids in cell membrane, surface phosphorylation has been attempted in this study to 
incorporate phosphoric groups on the C6 of chitosan. The optimized conditions of 
phosphorylation have been studied, and the content of grafted phosphoric groups has been 
improved.

Abstract  
 
Chitosan, the second most abundant nature polysaccharide, has a similar structure as 
glycosaminoglycans (GAGs) which are the main components of extra cellular matrix (ECM). 
Due to its relatively lower cost as compared to GAGs, many researches had tried to look for the 
application of chitosan in tissue engineering. In our previous study, protection strategy had be set 
up to keep the amino groups at C2 of chitosan which was known as the major positive functional 
group of chitosan with special biological characteristics in cell culture. Based on the fruitful 
results, we tried to phosphorylate the hydroxyl groups at C6 of chitosan in the cause of imitating 
the zwetterionic characteristics of phospholipids in cell membrane. Surface analysis of modified 
chitosan samples had indicated the success of phosphorylation. The blood compatibility of 
modified chitosan samples had evaluated by platelets adhesion test and plasma recalcification 
time, and shown that phosphorlated chitosan would improve the activation of platelet, and 
shorten the plasma coagulation time.
