Abstract 
The correlation and interaction of physiologies, dynamics, and bio-informatics associated with 
the Cerebral Autoregulation (CA) mechanism are extremely complex and fascinating. The 
integration of a completed CA system generally involves the cerebrovascular, cardiovascular, 
cardiopulmonary, and respiratory control system. The bio-informatics engaged in the CA system 
also includes CBV, CBF, CBFV, !VMCA of blood autoregulation, CPP, SAP, MABP, ICP, CSF of 
intracranial pressure, HR and BR of heart beat related signals, and PaCO2/ PaO2, PCO2/ PO2, PETCO2, 
PEECO2 of metabolism activity and gas transportation. The CA mechanism aims to maintain a steady 
cerebral blood flow within a normal range regardless the variations of blood pressure and other 
bio-information. Since the blood has mainly been the controlled signal and mainly flow through all 
the system organs through blood vessels, the model which depicts the behavior of blood in the 
cerebrovascular system has inevitably become important. The intended CA system model is 
modified to consider only the correlation of the cerebrovascular control mechanism and is is aimed 
at fulfillment of a CA simulator as the bio-informatics involved in the interaction of cerebrovascular 
mechanism is the main concern. The integration of a CA model from the viewpoints of Cerebral 
Hemodynamics and Intracranial Hemodynamics based on the cerebrovascular control mechanism 
and related bio-informatics is firstly objected. In current project, the feedback control model is 
proposed to integrate the CA with cerebrovascular control mechanism based on the modeling and 
simulation results of past researches. The CA simulator will be proceeded on the LABVIEW® and

一︾參加會議經過 
本次論文發表主題為「Comparative Studies of the Optimal Airflows and Ventilation Settings 
under Continuous Resistive and Elastic Loadings」，本研究以PB7200
持續命令式之強制換氣模式、並以常用之三種呼吸器氣流型態為對
象，包含正弦波、方波、漸減波，進行呼吸耗能最小化的最佳波形
研究。針對設定之病患呼吸機械特性與化學層面之通氣需求，產生
最佳化的氣流波形。進一步以病患可能之呼吸機械特性改變，如呼
吸道阻抗及肺彈性變化下，產生最佳化的通氣頻率、潮氣容積、尖
峰氣流量等通氣設定參數，及推導出每分鐘通氣量、吸/吐氣時間比
等呼吸範型，計算出其和正常呼吸機械特性時之改變量。同時也將
這三種通氣波形的最佳化設定參數，兩兩比較分析，以期提供呼吸
治療師臨床設定之參考。 
CESSE2011會議是由美國資訊工程研究院（IERI , Information 
Engineering Research Institute, USA）主辦的EI index國際研討會。會議宗旨主要是邀請國際上在電
腦、電機、系統科學、和工程的學者及研究人員，從跨領域的觀點，提出實際應用性的研究成果，
並針對個人研究中所面對的挑戰，和可能的解決方案，交互研討觀摹。上一屆的會議(CESSE2010, 
Mar 29~31) 是在Rio de Janeiro, Brazil(巴西) 舉辦。本次之CESSE2011會議，原定4月10~11日於中
國湖南省武漢市舉辦，然而會議主辦單位卻在會議的前一週，才以擴大會議規模為原因，臨時將
時間地點更迭為4月16~18日於中國廣東省廣州市的健力百合酒店(Guangzhou Jian Li Harmony

0.04, and 0.03 (Ers=20, 25, 33.3, respectively). For 
the case of Crs=0.05 (Ers=20), it was indeed 
considered as elastic assistance for a soft lung.

5. Conclusions 
In this paper, three airflows, which were 
generally utilized in respiratory therapy under 
mechanical ventilation, is optimized along with 
breathing patterns of ventilator settings. The optimal 
respiratory control is employed to minimize the 
effort of breathing. A remarkable character of the 
proposed optimization model is its ability to mimic 
the ventilatory and breathing responses to CO2 
inhalation, under muscular exercise, and under 
mechanical loadings. The results of current study 
shown to be consistent with previous researches and 
demonstrate the potential innovative design of a 
mechanical ventilator with optimal airflow and 
ventilator settings that tailored to meet patient’s 
physiological need. The comparative study 
performed for any two airflows under loaded and no 
load breathing is valuable for possible clinical 
reference in respiratory therapy.

Comparative Studies of the Optimal Airflow Waveforms and Ventilation Settings 
under Respiratory Mechanical Loadings 
S.L. Lin1, S.J. Yeh2, H.W. Shia1 
1 Feng Chia University/Department of Automatic Control Engineering, Taichung, Taiwan 
2Department of Neurology, Cheng Ching Hospital, Taichung, Taiwan 
 
In this study, the sinusoidal, square, and descending waves, 
which are usually applied as airflow waveforms for mechanical 
ventilation, are modeled and optimized with minimum effort of 
breathing under continuous mandatory ventilation (CMV). With 
respect to possible changes in respiratory mechanics, the optimal 
airflow waveforms are simulated and optimized. The breathing 
pattern and ventilation settings are derived from the neuro-
mechanical effector of control model. Comparisons of breathing 
patterns between loaded and no load breathing are performed 
and comparative studies between three optimal airflow wave-
forms are achieved. 
Ventilator; airflow, optimization; respiratory mechanics. 
I. INTRODUCTION  
A mode of ventilation for mechanical ventilator is a par-
ticular set of control and phase variables to breath, including 
either mandatory or spontaneous mode. When first selecting 
ventilator settings, the primary object is to stabilize the patient 
by assuring adequate oxygenation and ventilation. There are 
numerous support modes provided by modern mechanical 
ventilators, however, all variations can be summarized into 
three categories: (1) continuous mandatory ventilation (CMV), 
(2) intermittent mandatory ventilation (IMV), and (3) sponta-
neous mode. As may be targeted by either volume or pressure, 
CMV is a full ventilation support mode in which mandatory 
breaths are triggered according to a preset time interval re-
gardless of patient’s effort [1]. Although CMV mode is poorly 
tolerated by most patients because it generally increases the 
work of breathing (WOB) and the oxygen consumption of the 
respiratory muscles, it still has a proper place in the support of 
selected patients in respiratory failure especially if the pa-
tient’s primary problem is inadequate alveolar ventilation [2]. 
Ventilators of new generation also allow the clinician to 
choose either a volume-targeted or pressure-targeted approach. 
The use of volume-targeted approach is guaranteed by a min-
imum minute volume, even as the patient’s compliance and 
resistance are changing. During CMV mode and volume-
targeted breaths, the therapist can set both magnitude and 
pattern of inspiratory airflow waveform. The inspiratory flow 
pattern will affect the inspiratory time and resultant time pa-
rameters. If the patient-triggered ventilations are of our con-
cerns, the inspiratory flow and improper ventilator settings are 
primary factors increasing the patient’s the WOB, which can 
often lead to muscle fatigue and hypercapnia [3,4]. 
Most ventilators operating in the flow-limited mode gener-
ally offer a choice of several different inspiratory flow patterns 
including sinusoidal, square, descending ramp, and ascending 
ramp. Although it was found that current literature provides 
conflicting perspectives on the relative benefits of these vari-
ous flow waveforms [4~6], guidelines have also been devel-
oped for selecting the flow waveform during mechanical ven-
tilation. Among these, sine or square-wave flow pattern is 
probably adequately adopted for most patients requiring un-
complicated or short-term ventilatory support [7]. 
In earlier researches [8], it was suggested that normal venti-
latory responses to CO2, exercise inputs, and mechanical load-
ing could be predicted by the minimization of a controller 
objective function consisting of total chemical and mechanical 
cost of breathing. The optimal respiratory control model was 
later proposed and verified by optimizing a quadratic inspira-
tory neural drive [9]. The optimal instantaneous airflow and 
lung volume were derived based on a lumped-parameter RC 
model [10] for the relation between respiratory neural and 
mechanical outputs. 
Three usually seen airflow, including sinusoidal, square, 
and descending wave, in mechanical ventilation are modeled 
as the inspiratory flow pattern during CMV mode and volume-
targeted mechanical ventilation in this paper. Instead of opti-
mizing the pressure profile, the airflows waveforms will be 
optimized in current study through the optimal respiratory 
control model. 
II. MATHEMATICAL MODEL 
A. The Optimal Respiratory Control Model 
Among those respiratory control models under studied dur-
ing the past years, possible optimality principle has found to 
be existed in the modeling of the respiratory control [8,9]. The 
optimal chemical-mechanical control model, which success-
fully demonstrates the ventilatory responses to chemical stim-
uli as well as muscular exercise, was previously proposed and 
simulated by Poon and Lin [9], respectively. They suggested 
that it is possible to minimize a combined controllers cost, 
consisting of the total chemical challenges and mechanical 
work that can be simulated through a neuro-mechanical effec-
tor with the form of an electrical RC model. The basic hypoth-
esis is that all respiratory responses, including breathing pat-
tern and ventilation, may be direct consequences of the 
optimization of an instantaneous respiratory neural output. 
Thus, rather than assume a hierarchy of control mechanisms 
for the various levels of the control outputs, the model propos-
es and verifies the profile of the neuromuscular drive, which 
can successfully relate the neuro-respiratory output to the 
resultant mechanical airflow.

square, and descending airflow model, respectively. Total 
ventilation ( ), breathing frequency (F), tidal volume (VT), 
peak flow (PF), mean flow (MF), peak pressure (A), inspira-
tion/expiration time (Ti, Te), and duty cycle (Ti/T) were de-
rived and optimized through the optimal control model. The 
percentage changes of the breathing pattern from no load were 
calculated for CRL and CEL. In Fig. 5 of sinusoidal airflow 
model, the resistive loading have shown substantial effect on 
the decreased F and increased VT, however, the total ventila-
tion was depressed (≈-20%). For the case of CEL in Fig. 5, a 
stiffer lung (increased Ers, or decreased Crs) resulted in the rise 
in both and F, while VT was suppressed. Both peak flow 
and mean flow were also found to be increased, and it is also 
noteworthy to figure that peak pressure was increased to ≈40% 
with Crs=0.03.  
In Fig 6, the result of CRL on the breathing pattern of

except in the case of R=10, for which the imposed resistance

on , VT, and F were significant in CRL. The increases of 
peak pressure were significant in both CRL and CEL. 
In contrast to the sinusoidal and square airflow, the im-
posed resistive loading seemed to have critical effect on the

shown in the top of the Fig. 7. The CRL breathing of the de-
scending wave exhibits similar influence to the sinusoidal 
airflow on all breathing pattern but with larger percentage 
changes in magnitude.  Meanwhile, the results of CEL breath-
ing of descending wave were also found to have comparable 
pattern with that of square airflow model.

本計畫從腦血管控制機制的層面，分別從腦血流動力學與顱內動力學的觀點、
腦血管控制之 CA 生物資訊分析，進行腦血管系統模式之整合，整理出 CA 的控
制模型。並嘗試利用先前完成的「生醫系統模擬 e-Learning 平台」上，裝置一
個 Wed-based 開放架構的 CA 模擬器。所完成的 CA 模擬器，具體功能將以重複
實現文獻上腦血流調控之相關實驗與模擬為主 Web-Based 模擬器提供簡易的人
機操作界面，從輸入呼吸生理參數，到模擬軟體系統分析之輸出，強化 CA 模擬
控制的工程應用訓練。模擬器導入原「醫學工程在逢甲」之數位學習平台中，
使用者打破區域與時間之疆界。
