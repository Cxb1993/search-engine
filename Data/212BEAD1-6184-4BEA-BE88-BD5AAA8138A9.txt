中文摘要 
本研究提出以八羥奎林鎵鹽奈米線(Tri-(8-hydroxyquinoline) gallium, GaQ3)
為沉積模板方式，利用原子層沉積技術(atomic layer deposition, ALD)製備鋁參雜
氧化鋅奈米管， 鋁參雜氧化鋅(Al-doped ZnO, AZO)係利用 ALD 層層原子堆疊
概念，將氧化鋁與氧化鋅交互沉積於八羥奎林鎵鹽奈米線之上，固定每一層氧
化鋁與氧化鋅複合膜成長所需 ALD 總循環次數，藉由控制氧化鋁與氧化鋅複合
膜中 ALD 循環次數比例，可獲得具不同鋁摻雜濃度 AZO，接著在 350 oC 大氣
環境下加熱試片即可移除 GaQ3奈米線，獲得 AZO 奈米管。研究結果顯示鋁摻
雜可加速氧化鋅在 GaQ3奈米線成核成長速率，並且獲得光滑平整奈米管，同時
奈米管的管狀結構亦隨著鋁摻雜濃度增加而更清楚；根據光致發光 
(photoluminescence, PL) 光譜結果，隨著鋁摻雜濃度增加 AZO 奈米管的 PL 發光
峰能量增加但強度隨之減少；總言之，我們可藉由控制氧化鋁與氧化鋅複合膜
ALD 循環次數比例，獲得所需鋁摻雜濃度 AZO 奈米管，並控制其光致發光特
性。

Abstract 
    An easy and versatile route to prepare Al-doped ZnO (AZO) nanotubes by using 
the template-based process is reported.  Tri-(8-hydroxyquinoline) gallium nanowires 
(GaQ3 NWs) prepared by thermal evaporation were used as the deposition template 
of AZO.  ZnO/Al2O3 bilayer prepared by atomic layer deposition (ALD) was 
stacked on GaQ3 NWs to form the AZO shell.  After heating to 350 oC to remove the 
GaQ3 NWs, AZO nanotubes were obtained. The Al doping level can be controlled by 
changing the fraction of ALD cycle number of Al2O3.  Both pure ZnO and low Al 
content AZO nanotubes have a nanotube structure with connected nanocrystals.

Atomic layer deposition is a well-known thin film growth technique allowing 
excellent thickness control and conformal coating on complex structures.[14]  ALD 
has been applied to prepare core-shell nanowires with the use of 1D nanostructure 
templates, such as carbon nanotubes,[15,16] ZnO NWs[5,17] and SiOx NWs.[18]   
Previously, we reported a low temperature ALD process to prepare smooth and 
conformal alumina nanotubes by using the GaQ3 NW template.[19]   Different from 
Al2O3, ZnO deposited by ALD tends to form separated nanoparticles in the beginning, 
and then grow to irregular and rough nanotubes.[16,18,19]  Kim et al. proposed that 
Al2O3 can serve as a surface functionalization layer to improve adhesion of ZnO on 
CNT  surface.[16]    With the aid of a buffer layer, a continuous and relative clear 
ZnO nanotube can be grown by using ALD.   Some research groups also 
demonstrated that ALD could be applied to grow alloy or doped films by alternating 
the deposited materials.[20,21]  However, success in fabricating alloy or doped 
nanotubes by means of ALD has been rarely reported so far.   Therefore, it is of 
great interest to grow smooth doped nanotubes by using ALD and NW template.  In 
this study, we report a novel technique to grow ZnO and AZO nanotubes by using 
GaQ3 NW template.  This technique provides an easy and flexible route for 
preparation of doped nanotubes with desired composition. To prepare pure ZnO 
nanotubes, sometimes an Al2O3 buffer layer was predepoisted with 30 cycles of ALD 
to assist the nucleation of ZnO.   
 
Experimental 
GaQ3 NWs were deposited on a silicon substrate by thermal evaporation, as 
reported in our previous work.[22]  DEZ and TMA were used as precursors of zinc 
and aluminum in the ALD reaction, respectively.  Water vapor was served as the 
oxygen source.  One typical ALD cycle consisted of 0.2 s metal precursor pulse 
followed by 5 s N2 purge to remove excess precursor and by-products, then 0.2 s 
water pulse, and finally another 5 s N2 purge.  During the ALD process, the silicon 
substrate was maintained at a background temperature of 50 oC.  ZnO or Al2O3/ZnO

Typically, an incubation period of about 30-40 ALD cycles is needed for nucleating 
ZnO on Si or sapphire substrate.[23]  However, an even longer incubation period is 
needed for ZnO nucleation on GaQ3 NWs.  Full coverage with a particle-like ZnO 
coating was formed after 400 ALD cycles, as shown in Figure 3b.  The wall 
thickness of ZnO tube prepared by using 400 cycles of ALD is about 30 nm, which 
suggests an estimated average growth rate of 0.75 Å/cycle.  Apparently, the 
nanotube is composed of many connected ZnO nanocrystals, which grow along the 
radial direction of GaQ3 NW.  The nucleation, growth and coalescence of ZnO 
nanocrystals suggest that deposition of ZnO by ALD on GaQ3 NWs follows the 
island growth mode.[24]  Hence, it is very difficult to grow smooth ZnO nanotubes 
on GaQ3 NWs.   
With the use of an Al2O3 buffer layer, i.e., predeposition with 30 cycles of Al2O3 
prior to deposition of ZnO, however, GaQ3 NWs were seen to be already covered 
with many ZnO nanocrystals by 200 cycles of ALD, as shown in Figure 3c.   The 
size and density of ZnO nanocrystals are larger than those without the Al2O3 buffer 
layer.  The wall thickness of ZnO nanotube is also larger than that without the buffer, 
as shown in Figure 3d.   Obviously, the nucleation and growth of ZnO on GaQ3 is 
assisted by the Al2O3 buffer.
