前言 
以燃燒控制或煙道氣處理做為氮氧化物(NOx)的控制技術已相當成熟，一般燃燒控制 NOx 的方法不
外燃燒調整、使用低 NOx燃燒器(LNB)與整體燃燒系統的改善[19]。在煙道氣處理技術多以選擇性觸媒
(SCR)與非觸媒(SNCR)方法還原 NOx為氮氣。其中以燃燒控制方法所能達到的 NOx處理效率有限，對於
愈來愈嚴格的 NOx排放標準，多數製程均須加上煙道氣處理方法，才能符合排放標準。而煙道氣處理方
法中 SNCR 法在系統負載變動時，其煙道氣溫度亦會隨著變動，最佳氨氣或尿素噴入位置常不易掌握，
因此 SNCR 法最佳的 NOx去除率也大約只有 65%[3,4]。至於 SCR 法雖具較高的 NOx處理效率(約 95%去除
率)[20,27]，是目前煙道氣處理 NOx最常用的程序。但因 SCR 設備中的觸媒床，會造成高壓損，增加操
作能源投入，並且觸媒須更換處理，會衍生二次污染處理負擔，因此，以吸收處理 NOx的程序逐漸被研
究開發。由於 SCR 或 SNCR 程序所依據的反應，都是在氣相中的還原可逆反應，其部份反應路徑是循環
的，無法達到對 NOx完全的處理，而 NOx Scrubber 吸收程序是將氣相的 NO，最終處理成在液相中的離
子態，為不可逆反應，理論上有可能達到 100%的去除率。但因煙道氣中所含的 NOx成分多為水溶性極
低之 NO，不易以一般的 Scrubber 吸收處理，本研究團隊曾提出以兩段式 NOx Scrubber 程序解決此問
 5
溶液為電解液，產生 Ag(II)中介物用以同時處理 NOx與 SO2的整合吸收程序，但該研究使用鉑與鈦金屬
或合金作為電極，在實場應用上，將會有電極損耗與極化與的問題發生。並且其使用的硝酸銀 Ag(I)
電解液在成本考量上，亦不具經濟競爭性。因此無論電解液的選用或電極材料的選擇，均值得進一步
進行探討。為簡化電氧化操作程序，並有效降低電力負荷，在應用上電壓或電流是相當重要之操作參
數，反應中應避免活性、濃度及電阻極化現象產生。故如何設計一個簡單、易操作、且電力消耗是經
濟可行的電氧化程序，以用於電化學氧化還原劑產生/再生器之整合系統，並測試其在應用之操作參
數，是值得研究的重要課題。而本研究團隊在電極材料選用與如何防止極化現產生也有研究經驗與成
果。 
由以上文獻回顧可知，兩段式 NOx洗滌吸收系統已經多位學者在理論與 Laboratory Scale 實驗證
實其功能，並經本研究團隊過去許多模廠測試確認其可行性。而以電化學反應程序處理工業廢水的研
究，本研究團隊過去也曾以電混凝程序，成功地處理含油泥與金屬殘渣之工業切削油廢水，並嘗試以
電混凝與電氧化程序，測試處理含紡織染料廢水與造紙加工廢水之效果，獲得相當正面的效果。在以
電化學反應器產生氧化劑做為電氧化程序處理工業廢水研究中，由於在電氧化反應器中加上薄膜後，
氧化劑與還原劑會分別在陽極與陰極產生，且從其氧化還原電位可預判其具有很高的氧化力與還原
力，可用在兩段式 NOx洗滌吸收系統。又此電化學反應器中有薄膜阻隔，使氧化劑與還原劑不會互相混
合，造成彼此消耗的損失。因此，本研究考慮以此做為氧化還原劑的再生器，應用於兩段式 NOx洗滌吸
收程序的氧化還原劑的供應與再生器。 
本此基礎，本團對希望進一步將兩段式 NOx洗滌吸收程序與電化學反應再生氧化還原劑程序結合，
發展一可以現地產生氧化還原劑的 NOx洗滌吸收程序。本研究將發展一個結合兩段式 NOx Scrubber 與電
化學氧化還原劑再生器之整合系統，探討此系統之功能性，以突破兩段式洗滌吸收 NOx程序，在氧化劑
與還原劑的儲存與消耗及電極材料選用的關鍵技術，提供工業界一套具經濟效益且符合處理效果的 NOx
處理系統。 
 
研究方法 
實驗設備與流程 
本研究將測試結合兩段式 NOx Scrubber 與電化學產生/再生氧化還原劑技術之整合系統，探討該整
合系統的操作參數。實驗參數包括: 氧化洗滌塔內 NO 氧化率效率、還原洗滌塔內 NO2還原效率、各洗
滌塔內氣相質流率、液相質流率，電化學反應器內氧化劑與還原劑之溶液 pH 值、氧化還原電位(ORP)、
電解質濃度、電流密度、電流效率、導電度，並測試不同極板材料之抗腐蝕性、極化程度等。 
本計劃將自製電化學電解反應槽，槽體將採用 PVC 為材質，電極將測試幾種非金屬與金屬氧化物為極
板，以探討比較其極板材料之抗腐蝕性、極化程度對電流效率的影響。反應槽中間以薄膜隔開氧化槽
與還原槽，薄膜將選用不同材料與孔徑之薄膜，以探討比較其對電流效率與氧化還原劑再生能力的影
響，選出最佳適合的薄膜材質與孔徑規格。反應槽外接直流電供應器以提供所需電壓，並量測記錄電
壓、電流。

ABSTRACT 
Greenhouse gases (GHGs) emission from power sector accounts for a high proportion of global 
GHGs emission.  How to improve the accuracy in counting GHGs emission of a power plant 
becomes significant.  In this study, life cycle assessment was taken into consideration in the 
GHG inventory of a coal-fired power plant.  To include life cycle assessment in the GHGs 
inventory for a power plant, the GHGs emission in the phases of the fuel mining, transportation, 
and consumption was considered.  The GHGs inventory of a Taichung coal-fired power plant 
with life cycle assessment was demonstrated.  In accordance with GHGs inventory guidelines 
and the system definition of the scope, this study include GHGs emission from the coal mining 
from every imported source country, the coal transportation by every fleet and port capacity, and 
coal combustion in the electricity production phases.  The proportion of GHGs for the 
considered phases of coal mining, transportation and combustion were obtained from similar 
inventory conducted by Japanese Central Research Institute of Electric Power Industry.  The 
GHGs inventories with and without considering life cycle assessment is compared.  The 
inventory of coal-fired power plant is also compared with other types of power generation.  This 
study estimated that the direct GHGs emissions (Scope 1) is 38749,113 tons and the indirect 
emissions (Scope 2) is 24,608 tons, while the total GHGs emission is 38,773,721 tons, or and 
873 (g-CO2/ kwh) for Taichung coal-fired power plant.  A 1.1 ratio for similar Japanese GHG 
emission with and without LCA of coal-fired power plant study was applied to GHG emission 
estimation for Taichung power plant.  This concludes that the GHG emission for Taichung coal-
fired power plant with LCA is 960 (g-CO2/kwh).

Scope Definition and Institution Boundary 
According to ISO 14064-1, GHG Protocol, the GHGs are defined as CO2, CH4 (GWP=23), N2O 
(GWP=296), HFCs (12-12,000), PFCs (GWP=124-14,800) and SF6 (GWP=22,200).  The GHGs 
emission in this paper are all converted by the GWP and expressed as CO2 equivalent 
symbolized as (CO2-e).  All activities include combustion, chemical use and relevant activities 
associated with GHG emission are inventoried.  The GHG emission inventory performed for 
Taichung fossil power plant in Taiwan based on data of year 2008.  The LCA items considered 
in this study include GHG emission of the plant construction, coal mining, mining utility, coal 
transportation, methane leakage, fuel consumption, and combustion ash disposal.

CH 4 Emission＝Annual imported coal amount× Emission factor × GWP 
The emission factor for coal mining is derived from the ratio of coal imported countries and

calculated by the formula below: 
Emission Factor＝[Coefficient of surface mining × Σ(import country ratio×surface mining 
ratio) + Coefficient of underground mining × Σ(import country ratio×underground mining 
ratio)]/1000 
Neglecting the amount of coal imported below 5%, the surface mining coefficient is set as 92

CONCLUSION 
1. This inventory considered fuel cycle, plant construction, deploy and waste disposal.  The fuel 
cycle includes coal mining, transportation, and combustion stages in the power generation 
process.  
2. the GHG emission for mining stage in Taichung coal-fired power plant is counted as 2.08 (g-
CO2-e /kwh). 
3. The GHG emission of Taichung fossil power plant incoal  transportation stage is calculated 
as 7.99 (g-CO2-e /kwh). 
4. The GHG emission of Taichung fossil power plant in operational stage is calculated as 
873.03(g-CO2-e /kwh). 
5. Combined the amounts of GHGs emission from scope 1 and scope 2, we concluded the total 
GHG emission of Taichung fossil power plant is  873.1(g-CO2-e /kwh). 
6. When compared the GHGs emission 873.03 (g-CO2/kwh) of Taichung coal-fired power plant 
with 886.81(g-CO2-e/kwh) of coal-fired power plant in Japan, Taichung plant emit less GHG 
than those in Japan.   
7. A 942.9 (g-CO2/kwh) GHG emission was estimated if LCA is considered for Taichung coal-
fired power plant when the ratio 1.08 of GHGs emission with and without LCA in similar 
coal-fired power plant in Japan is used to extrapolate.
