Abstract 
The objective of this project was to 
design and fabricate a 3D digitizer based on 
grinding technique, which has four main 
functions: (1) removing material of the part 
layer by layer, (2) capturing cross-sectional 
images of the part, (3) applying the image 
processing techniques (threshold, edge 
detection and segment) to the captured 
images, (4) stacking the processed images 
and rebuilding a CAD model. Using the 3D 
digitizer, users can obtain the inner and outer 
structure of a part under measurement and 
rebuild its CAD model. 
 
To achieve the goal, a proper machine 
tool and related hardware were selected, such 
as an instrument-control computer, stepping 
motors, a CCD camera, lens, a circular LED 
light, an air unit and a dust cover. Next, 
motor adaptor flanges, a fixture of imaging 
system, and an air unit were designed and 
fabricated. Then the motors to control 
moving stages and a CCD camera to capture 
images were installed. Then the software for 
linear scale signal feedback, stepping motor 
motion control and image capturing was 
developed by using LabVIEW. Finally, a 
performance analysis was conducted and the 
applications of 3D digitizer were studied. 
 
From the results of the performance 
analysis, it was obtained that the maximum 
working space was 245×105×70 mm, the 
accuracy was 0.029 mm, the resolution was 
0.005 mm, the precision was 0.026 mm and 
the minimum layer thickness was 0.005 mm. 
The Gage R&R percentage of the 3D 
digitizer was 25.99%. The performance of
