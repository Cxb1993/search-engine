Keywords: Tangible User Interface, HumanComputer Interaction, Metaphorical Design, 
Ambient Display, Open Innovation, Living Laboratory, Sustainable Awareness 
 
In this paper we present iAWN (short of Interaction, Aesthetics, Wellbeing and Natural) a 
straw-like tangible interface that use emotion metaphor to convey meaningful interaction and 
evoke aesthetic experiences in everyday life. iAWN provides a new touchable-grass interaction 
interface. iAWN evokes aesthetic user interaction experience, allowing emotional associations. 
The work is based on the cultural probe method by collecting primary visual data about domestic 
settings. We take a design probe approach to exploring design alternatives. The design probe 
includes mock-up experiments, functional sketch, and script writing. The current version of the 
iAWN prototype is equipped with innovative reminding technology, which combines the 
Livindex Cloud with the Google Calendar service. Through an openminded ideation process, 
users could provide their feedbacks and suggestions to our gestural interface design in an 
interactive smart space, and enrich the characters of the iAWN far beyond the capabilities of a 
single design team. As of currently, the achievements from this study have actually provided us 
with an adjustable environment to improve our understanding of issues related to 
actionperception coupling and gesture meanings. Future work is to make iAWN devices as a 
seamlessly service platform for awareness of energy consumption, environmental quality, and 
personal health conditions.  
 
The outcome and research work in progress in the previous year include the following: 
1. The iAWN prototype suggests natural interaction by a gestural interface which enhances 
the aesthetic, wellbeing and social networks in our living environment 
2. The Livindex system, which currently supports the reminder services in the space, will 
become a platform for all future services 
3. The successful experience of this study encourages a new design method, not only for a 
gestural interface, but also for a service design process mediated by a gestural interface. 
4. A research paper titled “Methodology for Ideation of Interaction Design” has been 
accepted for publication in the CAADRIA in 2009.  
5. Developing awareness tools with easy-to-use interfaces to persuade users to behave in a 
more sustainable way. 
6. Tracking ecological footprints and making information transparent. 
7. Applying green materials to sustainable interaction design that minimizes the negative 
impact on our environments. 
8. Information into users’ affective state is critical for a good evaluation of their experience 
when interacting with the systems. To explore effectiveness of physical metaphors in our 
interactive design, we try to explore brain imaging as a measure that more directly 
quantifies the cognitive utility of our interfaces.
