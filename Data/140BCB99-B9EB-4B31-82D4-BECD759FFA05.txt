Growth mechanism of carbon nanotubes by the catalyst of nickel silicide 
 
中文摘要 
 本工作之目的在利用矽化鎳作為催化劑，藉由常壓式熱化學氣相沉積法合成碳奈米

 Abstract --- The purpose of this work was to investigate the growth mechanism of 
carbon nanotubes (CNTs) by the catalyst of nickel silicide (NiSix).  Catalytic nanoparticle 
enclosed in the tip of a CNT and defective catalyst enclosed in the midway of a CNT indicate the 
tip growth mechanism of CNTs by NiSix catalysts.  The whole NiSix compound as the catalyst 
for CNT growth was attributed to the much higher Ni-Si binding energy of about 850 eV than the 
Si-C binding energy of 285 eV.  The bottom metal thickness affects the formation of catalytic 
nanoparticles and thus growth density of CNTs greatly.  The lateral CNT device with the metal 
stack structure of 200 nm TiN / 7 nm Ni / 200 nm Ti was fabricated at 800 oC and atmospheric 
pressure.  With one bridged CNT, the total resistance is about 5 kΩ.  Therefore, near Ohmic 
contact between the CNT and metal electrodes was demonstrated. 
 
 Keywords: growth mechanism, carbon nanotube, nickel silicide, bottom metal thickness,  
Ohmic contact atmospheric thermalCVD, RTA 
 
INTRODUCTON 
Carbon nanotubes (CNTs) are attractive as nano-size bricks with which devices can be 
constructed in a bottom-up fabrication process.  Silicides play a significant role in modern 
device technology [1].  The operation of electronic devices heavily relies on the specific 
properties of metal-semiconductor interfaces.  Therefore, the purpose of this work was to 
investigate the growth mechanism of CNTs by the catalyst of nickel silicide [2, 3].  CNTs were 
synthesized by atmospheric thermal chemical vapor deposition (thermal CVD). 
 
The unique properties of carbon nanotubes (CNTs) have attracted a broad scientific 
community and become the promising 1D structures in many fields of the nano science.  
Notably, they are excellent candidates as nano-component in electronics, nano electro-mechanical 
systems (NEMS), and bio-sensing [4-6].  It is desired to electrically contact suspended carbon 
nanotubes for various fundamental studies and potential applications.  The fact that a suspended 
nanotube is free of van der Waals interactions with a substrate makes them appealing for 
mechanical, electrical, and electromechanical measurements.  A prerequisite for such integration

15 and 30 minutes, respectively.  Raman analysis was utilized to evaluate the quality of grown 
CNTs.  Field emission scanning electron microscopy (SEM) is used to observe the morphology 
of Ni catalytic particles and grown CNTs. 
 
RESULTS AND DISCUSSION 
At the process temperature of 750 oC, the pretreatment of nickel silicide catalytic film was 
performed with hydrogen flow of 500 sccm.  The gases of acetylene (C2H2), hydrogen, and 
nitrogen of 20, 300, and 200 sccm, respectively, were then flowed into the furnace to synthesize 
CNTs.  The  pretreatment and deposition times were 10 and 20 minutes, separately. Figure 2(a) 
shows the SEM image of one-bridged CNT device.  The linear current-voltage (I-V) 
characteristic of the 2-terminal device indicates that the bridged CNT is metallic, as shown in 
figure 2(b).  The low current of 10-7 A at ±5 volts might be due to the poor contact between the 
CNT and Ti electrode. 
 
To explore the probable phase change during CNT growth, furnace annealing of NiSix film 
for 30 min. at 750 oC in N2 environment was carried out.  The corresponding XRD patterns were 
presented in figure 3.  The Si-mono silicide (NiSi) peaks of samples with the RTA temperature 
of 500 oC, indicated by the diffraction peaks (2θ) of 31.6 o, 36.1 o, and 45.8 o, weakened while 
three new diffraction peaks at 28o, 47 o, and 56o appeared.  These three peaks signify nickel 
disilicide (NiSi2) phase.  It suggested the phase change of nickel silicide during the CNT 
synthesis process. 
 
The TEM grid was prepared by sonicating a CNT-covered substrate in a solvent and then 
drop-drying the suspension on the copper grid.  Figures 4(a) demonstrates the TEM image of a 
CNT.  It reveals center hollowness and multiwall structure of a CNT.  The EDS analysis of the 
catalytic nanoparticle enclosed in the tip of a CNT indicates the composition of Ni rich silicide 
(Ni1.41Si) or NiSi-like silicide, as shown in Fig. 4(b).  Figure 5(b) indicates the Si rich silicide 
(NiSi2.64) or NiSi2-like composition of defect nanoparticle enclosed in a CNT, as shown in figure 
5(a).  It implies the tip growth mechanism of CNTs by nickel silicide catalysts, similarly to those 
by Ni catalysts [10].  Also, the whole NiSix compound is the catalyst for CNT growth although 
Si element doesn’t have catalytic activity.  It was attributed to the much higher Ni-Si binding 
energy of about 850 eV than the Si-C of 285 eV [11, 12].

The multi-electrode array (MEA) recording systems have long been demonstrated as a 
powerful platform for the investigation of excitable biological systems.  The desired increase 
in the effective area of recording electrodes has been established by various surface treatment 
methods, such as electroplating [typically platinum, iridium or titanium nitride] and 
electrochemical etching.  Carbon nanotube coatings offer a new and exciting route to further 
improve the performances of MEAs due to their many beneficial properties such as mechanical 
stability, chemical durability, good electrical conductance and their bio-compatibility.  Figure 1 
shows the schematic of the electrode micro-fabrication process [1].  The micro-fabricated CNT 
MEA is shown in figure 2 [1].  As-prepared CNT electrodes were packaged and then submitted 
to CV tests conducted at scan rates between 20 and 300 mVs-1 [Fig. 3(a)]. [1]  The oxidation 
current versus the scan rate data, derived from the data in figure 3(a) and presented in figure 3(b) 
[1], exhibit a clear linear  dependence corresponding to a DC capacitance of 500 nF, derived 
from the relation: i = C(dV/dt) in which i is the oxidation current, C is the linear capacitance, 
and dV/dt is the scan rate.  The specific capacitance of 10 mF cm-2 of CNT MEAs is larger 
than the DC specific capacitance of commercial TiN micro-electrodes of 2.5 mF cm-2.  The 
extremely high area specific capacitance of the CNT electrodes is thus a direct consequence of 
their effective three-dimensional nature.

The AC impedance magnitude of the CNT based micro-electrodes was determined by 
Electrochemical Impedance Spectroscopy (EIS) experiment under equilibrium conditions over 
the range of 1 Hz to 20 kHz.  Figure 4 shows the frequency dependence of the impedance of a 
typical CNT electrode [1].  Two main features are apparent in figure 4.  First, is the low 
impedance of the CNT electrode, 10 kΩ  at 1 kHz.  The second important attribute is the 
weak frequency dependence of the CNT electrode impedance.  A power law fit according to Ze 
= Rofm [dashed line, figure 4(a)] yields m values ranging between -0.1 and -0.4 for CNT 
electrodes.  These values are lower than typical values obtained with platinized electrodes 
which vary between -0.6 and -0.8, depending on the amount of electro-deposited Pt.

三、目次 
 
壹 目的 
使用化學氣相沉積來製作單壁碳奈米管場效電晶體，以應用至感測器化之用途。藉由元件製
作的參數控制，使作為電晶體通道的碳管連接數目僅為數根，並量測碳管與電極的接面電阻，
與碳管的電阻。 
並藉著本次參訪學習的機會，學習認識一下對方團隊的製作方法與技術，以增廣自我見聞。

第一步驟-基板之準備： 
 
基板分為兩種，一種為經過圖案化製程用來製作元件有結構的試片，另一種則是用來做為控
管碳管結構生長之控制試片，兩種的差別為是否擁有金屬電極的結構。而當兩種基板準備好
了以後，則是要準備將催化劑層以浸泡-附著(dip-coating)方式來附著於準備好之基板上。 
為了製作場效電晶體元件，我們初始選用 P-type 的 test Silicon wafer，而 P 型參雜的基
板剛好可以作為電晶體之背閘極(back gate)，接著採用常壓化學氣相沉積(APCVD)的濕式氧
化法(wet-oxide)沉積一層厚度不等之二氧化矽層(SiO2)作為電晶體之絕緣層，與催化劑之附
著層，此處二氧化矽層厚度之控制為實驗參數之一，最後採用 500 nm 之厚度。 
接下來再使用 E-gun 沉積一層 300 nm 之鈦(Titanium, Ti)並圖形化以作為電晶體之電極，如
圖二所示。

概述 
    本實驗主要比較以鎳(Ni)和矽化鎳(NiSix)作為催化劑，並控
制不同的溫度以及載氣對於成長碳管會有不一樣的影響，在此實驗
利用疊狀結構並經過圖形化(Pattern)來做出電極，目的是為了製
作出奈米碳管元件(CNT-FET) ，再經過熱化學氣向沉積法
(Thermal-CVD)製程來成長碳管。 
藉由控制不同的製程參數，並經過 I-V 電性量測系統，判斷出
可能跨接碳管的位置，接著經過電子顯微鏡(SEM)找出真正探管跨
接的位置，並由得到的電性以及碳管的特性，判斷此電流為漏電流

兩端且準直，加以分析並改變製程參數來探討漏電流產生的原因和

總結:  
(1)在經過化學氣相沉積(CVD)成長碳管的製程後，以鎳作為催化劑的基版很難找到碳管。 
(2)在改變製程溫度(750 °C ~ 850 °C ) 之下，溫度不是改變漏電流的主要因素。 
(3)從結果看來，載氣的改變對漏電流有影響。 
(4)在使用 NDL 的機台成長碳管的試片並不會有漏電流問題，但使用 CNM 的機台有此問題，表
示可能是 CNM 機台的問題。 
(5)對於空氣預處理(air pretreatment)的製程下成長的碳管，把氬氣(Ar)換成氫氣做預處理
並把時間從 30 增加到 90 秒對於成長碳管有助益。 
(6)從 EDX 的結果看來，我們無法證明漏電流是因為鈦(Ti)金屬的擴散。 
(7)當我們使用空氣作為預處理 CVD 的製程條件下(CNT-A01~A03)漏電流的問題得到改善。 
(8)真正解決漏電流的關鍵，是(Ar/H2)預處理的時間變少(因為加入 air-pretreatment 所以
(Ar/H2)的預處理時間變少)，或是因為中間有短暫的抽真空，從結果無法判斷。

三、目次 
 
壹 目的 
使用化學氣相沉積來製作單壁碳奈米管場效電晶體，以應用至感測器化之用途。藉由元件製
作的參數控制，使作為電晶體通道的碳管連接數目僅為數根，並量測碳管與電極的接面電阻，
與碳管的電阻。 
並藉著本次參訪學習的機會，學習認識一下對方團隊的製作方法與技術，以增廣自我見聞。

第一步驟-基板之準備： 
 
基板分為兩種，一種為經過圖案化製程用來製作元件有結構的試片，另一種則是用來做為控
管碳管結構生長之控制試片，兩種的差別為是否擁有金屬電極的結構。而當兩種基板準備好
了以後，則是要準備將催化劑層以浸泡-附著(dip-coating)方式來附著於準備好之基板上。 
為了製作場效電晶體元件，我們初始選用 P-type 的 test Silicon wafer，而 P 型參雜的基
板剛好可以作為電晶體之背閘極(back gate)，接著採用常壓化學氣相沉積(APCVD)的濕式氧
化法(wet-oxide)沉積一層厚度不等之二氧化矽層(SiO2)作為電晶體之絕緣層，與催化劑之附
著層，此處二氧化矽層厚度之控制為實驗參數之一，最後採用 500 nm 之厚度。 
接下來再使用 E-gun 沉積一層 300 nm 之鈦(Titanium, Ti)並圖形化以作為電晶體之電極，如
圖二所示。

所以在此推測漏電流的問題很可能是 RTA 的製程腔體內含金屬離子汙染所造成。

9 金屬鈦電極並非是造成漏電流問題的汙染源

概述 
    本實驗主要比較以鎳(Ni)和矽化鎳(NiSix)作為催化劑，並控
制不同的溫度以及載氣對於成長碳管會有不一樣的影響，在此實驗
利用疊狀結構並經過圖形化(Pattern)來做出電極，目的是為了製
作出奈米碳管元件(CNT-FET) ，再經過熱化學氣向沉積法
(Thermal-CVD)製程來成長碳管。 
藉由控制不同的製程參數，並經過 I-V 電性量測系統，判斷出
可能跨接碳管的位置，接著經過電子顯微鏡(SEM)找出真正探管跨
接的位置，並由得到的電性以及碳管的特性，判斷此電流為漏電流
或者是碳管導通所產生的電流，以及觀察碳管的形貌是否跨接電極
兩端且準直，加以分析並改變製程參數來探討漏電流產生的原因和
對碳管成長的影響。
