Abstract (??)?   
Nine kinds of tool, i.e., TiN-coated, TiCN-coated and uncoated K type carbide tools with 
chamfered main cutting edge (CMCE) were used in turning of high-strength 
glass-fiber-reinforced plastics (GFRP) materials to study the cutting forces and mechanism of 
chip formation.  Also, the ideal tool geometries, the relationship between the shapes of 
secondary chip and tool geometries were observed.  According to the overall performance, the 
coated CMCE tools were better than the uncoated CMCE tools.  In regards to the various 
cutting forces and current consumption, the surface roughness of the workpiece and the 
temperature of the tip surface, the levels of coated CMCE tools were all smaller than for the 
uncoated ones.  The cutting force for the TiCN-coated CMCE tools was smaller than that of the 
TiN-coated ones.  From the surface roughness of the workpiece, the TiN-coated CMCE tools 
showed better results than TiCN-coated and uncoated CMCE tools. 
Key Words: Silver white chip, GFRP, composites, chamfered main cutting edge
