二、Abstract
The current study aims to construct a flight
simulation system for Taiwanese people
based on the results of the previous studied.
This study included three main parts: 1) a
survey of technological and ergonomic
literatures on ultralight planes, 2) design and
construct a flight simulation system, and 3)
test and evaluation of the constructed
simulation system. The results can be
summarized as following: 1) distances
between the main controllers and SRP on the
simulation system were adjustable, so that
the subjects can adjust the controllers to fit
their dimensions, 2) the adjustable items
included: the seatback angle and rudder
angle; the X-, Y-, and Z-distance from the
throttle to SRP; the X-, Y-, and Z-distance
from the elevator to SRP; and the distance
between the rudder to SRP, and 3)
experimental results showed that the
learning performance of the new system
constructed by the study were better than
that of the previous one. The conclusion
could be used as a reference for the
ultraplane design and development.
keywords: ultralight plane, learning
performance, flight simulation, system
design.

7. 結論與建議
從本研究的研究成果可歸納以下幾點：
1) 本研究新建構的飛行模擬系統為可調
整式，其可依駕駛者的體型和操作需求調
整至其舒適的狀態。調整的項目有座椅靠
背傾角及方向舵角度；SRP 至油閥的長寬
高度、SRP 至升降桿的長寬高度和 SRP 至
方向舵的長度。
2) 在本研究所建構的飛行模擬系統中，學
習低速與高速輕航機的飛行績效以低速學
習效果較佳。
3) 比較本研究建構的飛行模擬系統與先
前的模擬飛行系統雛形中，駕駛者學習低
速與高速輕航機的學習效果差異。結果顯
示，在本研究建構的模擬飛行系統中，學
習低速與高速輕航機其學習效果皆優於先
前的模擬駕駛艙。此結果可說明一個符合
人舒適的駕駛艙可以提升學習效果，減少
學習的時間及降低意外的發生。本研究成
果可提供一套適合台灣人的飛行駕駛座
艙，以作為台灣超輕航機設計的參考。

Abstract Using computer as a tool for office workers has progressed in recent years. Workers spent more and
more hours in their office for both their proper business and leisure works. Providing comfortable furniture for
computer office workers has been becoming a critical issue. This study surveyed critical body dimensions and
working habits of 41 office workers employed in a manufacturing company. The relative sizes of their desks
and chairs were also measured as well. The patient questionnaires were investigated to test the corrections
between them. The results have: 1) 92.8% keyboard-carried planes were higher than the recommendation
height (67 cm) for computer works in Taiwan. 2) the most three frequent disorder symptoms were shoulder
muscle, cervical vertebra, and low back pain. 3) the distance between keyboard and elbow height was most
strongly corrected to the three symptoms. 4) the pain degrees of the large distance group were greater than
that of the small distance group in the three symptoms. And 5) the working hours also had a significant effect
on disorder symptoms. The results can be used as design guidelines for building computer office environment.
Keywords: computer worker’s symptoms, body dimension, furniture design, and working habit.
1 Introduction
Using computer as a tool for office workers has progressed in recent years. Workers spent more and more
hours in their office for both their proper business and leisure works. Sitting for long periods of time the
muscles must do much physical work. Without keeping correct posture and sufficient breaks easily gains
body’s disorders. Providing comfortable furniture for computer office workers has been becoming a critical
issue for proprietors.
Computer office workers are potentially to have Repetitive Strain Injury (RSI). RSI is a general term used to
describe a disorder in which people develop symptoms such as pain, numbness, stiffness, and weakness as a
result of sustained repetitive work, often done under adverse conditions. Not only repetitive keyboard use or
other kinds of repetitive manual activities are the major causes, but long work hours without sufficient breaks
and meeting deadlines under stress are also contributors. Furthermore, poor lighting, poor ventilation,
crowding, and other undesirable work environmental issue are additional factors. In addition, poor posture and
physical fitness, lack of exercise, deficient diet, and irregular sleep patterns can all be contributory. In the
scientific community, the terms cumulative trauma disorders (CTDs) and repetitive motion disorders are often
used. Usually the pain or other symptoms affect their work and other normal activities. RSI is a widespread

The investigation result of working habit including five items. The results showed that the average year of
employed age was 7.72 years (SD= 6.25 years). This can be seemed as a normal distribution from the spread
range. The mean working hours with computers per day was 9.88 hours (SD= 1.47 hours). It is longer than
that of Taiwan’s standard working hours(8 hours). Subjects usually take a break after working about 2.02
hours (SD= 0.76 hours). The period of time is longer than that of suggestion working hours (1 hour) (NIOSH,
1997). However, the duration for taking breaks is 6.52 min (SD= 2.90 min), shorter than 15 min which was
suggested by NIOSH (1997). The average exercise hours is 1.02 hours per week (SD= 0.24 hours). This is
shorter than suggestion hour 2.5 hour a week (NIOSH, 1997).

The investigation result of disorder symptom showed that the most five severe symptoms from most to least
were shoulder muscle pain, cervical vertebra pain, eye problem, low back pain, and backache with the average
pain point of 2.54, 2.39, 2.29, 2.17, and 2.10, respectively. The average pain points of the all five symptoms
were great than 2. This meant that the pain level of those five symptoms were in the light to middle levels.
While the average pain point of the remained other four symptoms, headache, wrists pain, buttocks pain and
thighs numbness or pain were less than 2 representing light pain levels.
The correlation coefficients between each other of the all the nine symptoms and the total pain index were
showed in Table 2. The table showed that the most great values among all of the correlation coefficients were:
0.69 (cervical vertebra pain and shoulder muscle pain), 0.68 (buttocks pain and thighs numbness or pain), 0.67
(cervical vertebra pain and low back pain), and 0.63 (shoulder muscle pain and low back pain), respectively.
The four symptoms, cervical vertebra pain, shoulder muscle pain, backache, and low back pain were strongly
corrected each other. This may because of their relatively physical anatomy. In total, the symptom of eye
problems was most correlated to the other eight symptoms. Furthermore, the total pain index was strong
positively correlated to all the nine symptoms and can be used for evaluating total pain conditions.
