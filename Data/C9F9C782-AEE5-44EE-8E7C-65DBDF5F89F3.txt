摘要 
本研究以 Ti6Al4V 合金為材料，研究經由氫化條件處理過後，其微結構與機械性質的變化。
X-ray 繞射分析顯示，層狀微結構 α 相內皆有晶粒細化的現象，SEM 微結構觀察亦發現 α 相
內，晶粒經過相變化的過程，α 相區域內的晶粒，形成薄片狀奈米結構，尺寸介於 50 至 100 nm
之間。此細化效果幫助了合金硬度及抗壓強度的提升。但一旦生成 δ 氫化鈦相後，皆因氫脆
裂痕導致硬度下降，且氫化程度越高裂痕越寬。 
 
關鍵字：Ti6Al4V、氫化、晶粒細化、機械性質。 
 
Abstract 
This project is focused to study the grain refinement and mechanical properties of Ti6Al4V alloy by 
means of isothermal hydrogenation. X-ray diffraction analyses showed that grain refinement occurs 
in α phase of Ti6Al4V. Scanning electron microscopy (SEM) observations showed that the major 
microstructural feature with the nanostructures (like strips) with the thickness from 50 to 100 nm 
within α phase. Formation of the nanostructures helps to increase the hardness and compressive 
strength of Ti6Al4V. However, formation of δ hydride in Ti6Al4V will resulted in embrittlement. 
The width of crack caused by embrittlement increased with the amount of δ hydride formed. 
Keywords: Ti6Al4V, hydrogenation, grain refinement, mechanical properties. 
 
一. 前言  
鈦合金一般可利用傳統的熱機處理
(thermomechanical treatment)方法來細化晶粒
[1-3]。簡述其細化機制，材料在機械加工過
程中，透過滾壓(rolling)、擠型(extrusion)或
鍛造(forging)等方式，將應變能以大量差排形
式儲存至晶體內，之後予以適當的熱處理，
晶粒再結晶與成長形成細小晶粒。但此製程
材料外形易受侷限，不適用於已成形或具形
狀設計的樣品。如近期 Saitova 提出之研究成
果[3]，等軸晶 Ti64 合金經由等徑轉角擠型
(equal channel angular extrusion，ECAE)加工
後，予以熱處理，其晶粒大小由 5-7 μm 細化
至 0.2 μm，而降伏強度則由 900 提升至 1100 
MPa。 
近 年 來 新 型 的 熱 氫 製 程 (thermal 
hydrogenation processing，THP)，亦提供鈦合
金晶粒細化的途徑。傳統觀念上認為氫對「結
構材料」是一種有害元素，易使材料氫脆[4,5]，
但隨著研究進展，利用氫做為鈦合金的臨時
性(暫存性)元素，已可有效改善合金的製程以
及改變其微結構形貌，進而達到提升合金機
械性質的效果。熱氫製程細化晶粒的原理為
利用鈦合金與氫元素的多樣特性(i)鈦合金本
身對氫有很高的親和力；(ii)氫分子接觸合金
表面分解成原子後，可固溶進入合金內充當
臨時性的 β 或 δ穩定元素；(iii)降低鈦合金的
β-transus 溫度點等，利用上述特性即可藉由相
變化達到細化晶粒的效果。 
 
二. 研究目的 
熱機製程雖可達到細化 Ti64 合金晶粒的
效果，但材料會因此產生變形，不適用於已
成形或具形狀設計之樣品。因此，對於生醫
材料(例如：人工骨骼)與民生製品(例如：高
爾夫球頭)等，已商業化的產品，熱氫製程可
以在不影響材料外形之下，進一步細化合金
晶粒提升其機械性質，相當具有優勢。 
實驗步驟設定的動機與欲想達到的目的
說明如下：鑒於以 PCI 儀器進行之等溫吸放
氫的熱氫製程，目前已知如前述 Perng 文獻的
成果之外[6]，較少人從事此類製程分析，但
其特色：(i)在等溫環境下，藉由調控鈦合金的
充氫量達到控制其相變化區域，不同於傳統

其主特徵峰 α(1 0 2)面半高寬皆較未熱氫處

之現象，且隨合金吸放氫處理量的增加，半

因此，吸放氫 0.7 與 0.9 H/M 樣品不是晶粒成
長變大，而是後續金相觀察到之合金氫脆現
象，釋放了晶格的內應力，令半高寬變窄，
但還是高於未吸放氫處理樣品。β 相主特徵
峰(2 0 0)面半高寬則無明顯規律變化，因此較
難比較。

合金在雙相 αH +βH區域內，吸放氫 0.1 H/M

5.3 機械性質測試 
吸放氫處理令層狀Ti64合金晶粒細化後，
此效果反應在機械性質變化上，首先，硬度
變化如圖四(a)所示，商購 Ti64 合金熱處理生
成層狀相分佈後，硬度略微降低至 308 Hv，
但合金歷經 αH +βH相變化(0.1 H/M)後，硬度
提升至 329 Hv，並在歷經單相 βH處理區段時，
隨吸放氫處理量增加，硬度亦呈現線性增加，
並到達最高值 394 Hv，提升約 28 %；然而一
旦生成 δ相的 0.7 與 0.9 H/M 樣品，皆因金相
觀察到之裂痕，而降低了合金硬度。 
此外，吾人取吸放氫 0.5 H/M 處理之層狀
樣品做橫截面硬度測量，檢查合金內部硬度
值是否與表面相同，意謂觀察吸放氫處理造
成之晶粒細化效果是否內外相同，顯見合金
內部晶粒細化效果與程度是和合金表面相當，
證明本論文的吸放氫處理，對 Ti64 合金的影
響是內外均勻的。 
層狀 Ti64 合金硬度下降原因，在於 SEM
與低倍率 OM 觀察下，即發現合金一旦生成 δ

鈦之應變能相當大，α → δ與 β → δ相變化過

EFFECT OF INTERSTITIAL CARBON ON THE MONO- AND DI-HYDRIDES OF 
Ti25V35Cr40 ALLOYS 
 
Chia-Chieh Shen1,2,3,*, Kwen-Chou Wu2,3, Hsueh-Chih Li2,3  
1Department of Mechanical Engineering, Yuan Ze University, Chung-Li, Taiwan 
2Fuel Cell Center, Yuan Ze University, Chung-Li, Taiwan

ABSTRACT 
The presence of interstitial carbon atoms was 
found to affect the hydrogenation properties of 
Ti25V35Cr40Cx (x = 0, 0.1, and 0.5) alloys over the 
range T = 30–300°C.  Heat treatment at T = 1200°C 
was used to homogenize the distribution of carbon 
atoms throughout the specimens.  X-ray diffraction, 
P-C isotherm curves and thermodynamic 
measurements, including van’t Hoff plots and 
temperature-programmed desorption spectra, were 
used to study the destabilization of mono- and di-
hydrides in annealed Ti25V35Cr40Cx due to the 
presence of interstitial carbon atoms.  The 
experimental results showed that the interstitial 
carbon atoms introduced both obstacles and carbon-
induced strain into the structure, which destabilized 
di-hydrides and enhanced the plateau pressure.  The 
hydrogenation capacity at T = 30–80°C was also 
reduced.  Surprisingly, destabilization of the mono-
hydride was less pronounced in the presence of 
interstitial C atoms at T = 150–300°C.  The 
destabilizing behavior of interstitial carbon atoms 
in Ti25V35Cr40 hydrides was ascribed to a lower H 
content in the saturated mono-hydrides as 
compared with the saturated di-hydrides, as well as 
strain relaxation at higher temperatures. 
Keywords: TiVCr; Hydrogenation; Carbon; 
Pressure-composition isotherm 
 
1. INTRODUCTION 
Body-centered cubic (BCC)–TiV alloys show 
good hydrogenation capacities of 1.8 H/M 
(equivalent to 3.6 wt%) [1-9].  BCC alloys reach to 
their capacities through a phase transformation 
from the low-pressure mono-hydride to the high-
pressure di-hydride.  However, the effective 
hydrogen desorption capacity under hydrogen 
pressures above 0.1 MPa at 30°C is only 1 H/M 
because mono-hydrides (for example, 
Ti25V35Cr40H0.6) are very stable, as shown in Fig. 1 
[2].  Destabilizing mono-hydrides to increase the 
effective hydrogen desorption capacity of BCC 
alloys is important for applications. 
Traditionally, the substitution of transition 
elements (Mn, Zr, Fe, etc.) with titanium has been 
widely used to increase the hydrogenation capacity 
[1-9]; however, few studies have examined the use 
of interstitial alloys.  Uno et al. studied the effects 
of interstitial boron on hydrogen solubility in BCC 
Ti37V38Mn25 and Ti31V39Cr30 alloys [10].  Later, 
Cho et al. reported the influence of interstitial boron 
on the hydrogen absorption/desorption properties of 
Ti32V25Cr43 alloys [11].  Unfortunately, both cases 
demonstrated that the maximum hydrogen storage 
capacity decreased upon addition of boron, 
probably due to the presence of micro-segregation 
in the specimens.  A very small amount of carbon 
has been successfully doped interstitially into TiFe, 
and this carbon significantly promoted its hydrogen 
plateau pressure [12].  We demonstrated that 
interstitial doping of boron or carbon into 
Ti25V35Cr40 alloys is an effective way to control the 
hydrogenation properties of di-hydride in 
Ti25V35Cr40 during annealing.  Under optimal 
doping conditions, the effective hydrogen 
desorption capacities increased for Ti25V35Cr40B1 
and Ti25V35Cr40C0.1 at T = 30°C, respectively [13].  
In this study, we examined the effects of interstitial 
carbon atoms on the destabilization of the mono-
hydride and di-hydride in Ti25V35Cr40 over a wide 
temperature range of 30–300°C by X-ray 
diffraction, P-C isotherm curves, and 
thermodynamic measurements. 
 
2. EXPERIMENT 
Ti25V35Cr40CX (x = 0, 0.1, 0.5) alloys were 
prepared.  Elemental powders of Ti (purity, 99.7%), 
V (purity, 99.9%), Cr (purity, 99.5%), and C (purity, 
99.5%) were mixed at the desired stoichiometries 
and were arc-melted on a water-cooled copper 
hearth under an argon [6 N] atmosphere at a flow 
rate of 25 L/min.  The samples were sealed in a 
quart tube under vacuum (10–2 Torr) applied by a 
mechanical pump.  Samples were then annealed at 
1200°C for 2 h to eliminate segregation [14].  The 
hydrogenation properties of the annealed specimens 
were studied.  A typical Sievert's apparatus was 
used for the hydrogenation test, which has been 
described previously [15].  All specimens were 
activated at 600°C and a hydrogen pressure of 0.5 
MPa.  To completely remove the residual hydrogen, 
the hydride specimens were degassed under 
vacuum at 700°C for 2 h.  After several cycles of

by the strain caused by the presence of carbon

high temperatures, remarkably eliminating carbon-
induced strain, which destabilized the mono-
hydride.  In addition, since fewer H atoms became 
trapped in the saturated mono-hydride, H atoms 
preferentially occupied the larger interstitial sites.  
For example, they occupied sites close to small 
parent metal atoms, such as V and Cr (the atomic 
radii of Ti, V, and Cr are 1.45, 1.32, and 1.25 Å, 
respectively).  The P-C isotherm curves of the 
mono-hydride below an H content of 0.5 H/M were 
very similar for all specimens.  However, the di-
hydride (FCC) formed through a phase transition 
from the mono-hydride (BCC), as shown in Fig. 9 
[9].  Interstitial carbon atoms acted as obstacles to 
retard the structural transformation when the lattice 
constant along the c-axis expanded from BCC to 
FCC.  Thus, a high hydrogen pressure was required 
for the structural change to proceed.  A fraction of 
the larger interstitial sites were already occupied by 
H atoms in the form of a mono-hydride.  Incoming 
H atoms inevitably occupied the smaller interstitial 
sites close to larger bigger parent metals, such as Ti 
and V in the BCC lattice, to form a di-hydride.  The 
smaller sites were more sensitive to the carbon-
induced strain than the larger sites.  Therefore, the 
extent of destabilization in the di-hydride due to the 
presence of carbon obstacles and strain was more 
pronounced as the number of carbon atoms 
increased, as observed in the extreme separation of 
the P-C isotherm curves of the di-hydride at T = 
30°C for Ti25V35Cr40C0.1 and Ti25V35Cr40C0.5.  The 
presence of interstitial carbon atoms significantly 
raised the equilibrium absorption pressure of the di-
hydride compared to that of the mono-hydride. 
 
4. CONCLUSION 
In this study, the hydrogenation properties of 
Ti25V35Cr40Cx (x = 0, 0.1, 0.5) were studied at T = 
30–300°C.  After annealing at 1200°C for 2 h, all 
specimens displayed a BCC structure, but a small 
amount of precipitate was present for 
Ti25V35Cr40C0.5.  The presence of interstitial carbon 
atoms and carbon-induced strain dramatically 
destabilized di-hydride in Ti25V35Cr40 alloys at T = 
30–80°C.  However, this effect became less 
pronounced in the destabilization of mono-hydride 
at T = 150–300°C.  The different behavior of 
interstitial carbon atoms during formation of the 
BCC Ti25V35Cr40 hydrides was ascribed to the lower 
saturated H content in the mono-hydride compared 
to that in the di-hydride, as well as strain relaxation 
at higher temperatures.
