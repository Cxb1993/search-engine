中文摘要 
 
由於近年來分子生物學的進展，癌症生成的機制已逐漸明朗。癌症是由於控制細胞生
長、分化及凋亡的基因的一系列遺傳突變所造成。癌症基因具有高度的變化性，而影響一
個基因的表現方式，最重要的機制即人類基因體選擇性裁剪(Human Alternative splicing)。 
 
本計畫主要包含兩個研究目的。第一，是建立一個適合生物學家查詢特定基因的相關
資訊。第二，則是期望能夠找出與選擇性裁剪相關的機制。根據影響選擇性裁剪的因素，
本研究從三方面著手：尋找與特殊裁剪(組織專一性與疾病專一性)相關之特徵序列；探討
RNA 二級結構與裁剪機制之關係；探討疾病與裁剪機制之關係。 
 
本計劃所研發出之選擇性裁剪資料庫 Avatar 已經擁有人類與其他五個物種的
Alternative Splicing 以及 Tissue Specific 資料。透過此資料庫，對於癌症相關基因的蛋白質
產生機制以及特定的基因結構將可更為了解，同時並可進一步進行相關生物實驗，利用
PCR 等方式針對特定基因片段的蛋白質產物做偵測，以進而了解癌症成因。 
 
關鍵字: 選擇性裁剪，癌症基因，組織專一性 
 
Abstract 
 
In resent years, the mechanism of cancer source is more obvious in molecular research. 
Cancer can cause by abnormal expressed of a set of genes that control the cell develop, disunite 
and apoptosis. The cancer-related gene has highly variability, but the most important mechanism 
in this is the alternative splicing.   
 
This project includes two main propose: first, to establish a database to provide the 
information of specific genes to fit the need of biologists. Second, we hope to discovery the 
mechanism related to the alternative splicing. According to the factor which influences 
alternative splicing, we develop this project in three areas: to find the specific sequence related 
to alternative splicing (in tissue specific or in disease specific), to discovery the relationship 
between the RNA second structure and the mechanism of alternative splicing and discuss the 
relationship between the cause of disease and alternative splicing. 
 
In this project, we successfully develop an alternative splicing database: AVATAR. This 
database contains the alternative splicing and tissue specific data in human and other five 
species’ gene data. Through this database, we can more understand the specific protein product 
and cancer-related gene structures how to cause the cancer. We can also according these data to 
do the PCR experiments, and detect the protein product of the specific genes to figure out the 
source of cancer. 
 
Keywords: AVATAR, cancer-related gene, alternative splicing, database

圖 1　 選擇性裁剪類別 
 
人類選擇性裁剪的機制，緊密的影響了組織專一性(Tissue-Specific)以及細胞發展時期
的變化。因此，選擇性裁剪的表現型與細胞的功能有緊密的相關，更有可能為疾病的因子
之一。許多癌症相關的基因都受選擇裁剪的調控，雖然多數的選擇性裁剪表現型尚未被完
整的定義，但是已知與細胞的凋亡有密切相關。以著名的癌症相關基因 CD44 來說，單一
基因就有 15 種以上的表現型，其他如 BRAC1 更高達 17 種。當我們由人類選擇性裁剪的
角度對基因進行分析時，我們可以偵測出一個基因有多少種的表現形，然後挑出具有多種
表現型的基因做為候選的癌症相關基因以進行分析以及預測。 
 
另外，以肝癌的相關基因 Dlc 來說，目前已知有兩種不同的表現形式。透過 NCBI EST
資料庫的資料以及其選擇性裁剪表現型加以分類後，我們由統計結果可以明顯發現罹癌者
與正常者分屬兩個族群，且與表現形式具有高度相關性。因此，透過此一研究，我們可鑑
定出更多具有專一性的選擇性裁剪表現形式。 
 
許多癌症相關基因都可以在 mRNA 上發現特殊的表現方式，而在 mRNA 上的突變更
可進一步影響蛋白質的功能，進而成為癌症發生的特定因子。根據這些突變發生在 mRNA
上的位置，我們可以進而將這些資料加以分類，並使用統計方法加以檢定，以確認哪些基
因有可能是癌症相關基因。 
 
癌症相關基因的特性是一個基因通常具有多種的表現方式。若能針對擁有多種選擇性
裁剪表現方式的基因加以偵測，即可篩選出可能的候選基因，提供我們進行下一步的研究。

性裁剪的研究，有漸為熱門的趨勢。有許多研究者開發建立選擇性裁剪資料庫，包括 ASAP 
(Alternative Splicing Annotation Project ) [17]、ASDB (Alternative Splicing Database) [18]、
ASMamDB (Alternative Splicing Database of Mammals) [19]、SpliceDB [20]、PALS db 
(Putative Alternative Splicing Database) [14]、ISIS (Intron Information System) [21]。這些研究
包含有選擇性裁剪資料的線上查詢、註解實驗資料、分類不同的選擇性裁剪類型。目前的
研究顯示 35%~60%的人類基因會發生選擇性裁剪 [8]。其中，卡夾式選擇性裁剪是最常見
的類型[22, 23]。

Miyaso 等學者[33]研究疾病 SMA（Spinal muscular atrophy，脊髓性肌肉萎縮症）與選
擇性裁剪的關係。一般正常人的兩條第 5 號染色體皆有兩個同源性很高的運動神經元存活
基因（SMN gene）：包括靠近染色體末端（telomeric gene）的 SMN1 基因與靠近染色體
中節（centromic gene）的 SMN2 基因。這兩個基因僅在 3’端的區塊有 5 個 b.p.的差異，利
用位於第七及第八個表現序列（外顯子 7、外顯子 8）的核苷酸差異，可以區分 SMN1 及
SMN2，並進行 DNA 的分析。SMN1 及 SMN2 基因都會進行轉錄，SMN1 基因轉錄轉譯出
來的為穩定且具完整功能之蛋白質；而 SMN2 基因轉錄出來的 mRNA 大部分缺少外顯子 
7，也就是有選擇性裁剪的現象產生，造成外顯子 7 被移除掉，故轉錄出來的多為不穩定
之蛋白質，也因此會造成疾病的產生。這篇論文發現 SMN1 在其外顯子 7 後方的內含子上
面同樣存在有枝幹環的 RNA 二級結構，是造成 SMN2 其外顯子 7 在基因轉錄的時候被裁
剪掉的原因 。 
 
RNA 二級結構與裁剪機制之關係 
 
RNA 的二級結構由 A、G、C、U 這 4 種鹼基組成的聚合體稱謂 RNA 分子的初始結
構。與 DNA 的雙鏈結構不同，RNA 通常都是單鏈結構，形成局部的鹼基配對。A-U、C-G
通過氫鍵形成比較穩定的鹼基對，稱作 Watson-Crick 對；有時也會考慮較弱的 U-G 對（比
C-G 對少一個氫鍵），上述鹼基對都是規範鹼基對。 
 
一個 RNA 分子的二級結構是其三維結構中一組鹼基對的集合。RNA 分子二級結構的
預測與 DNA 序列比對具有一定的相似性，只是 RNA 單鏈通過互補鹼基進行自身折叠。

研究方法 
 
在生物資料庫部分，我們勘測了從 EST 分析的現有選擇性裁剪資料庫。有二種不同方
法為選擇裁剪的資料庫建築。第一個, 他們使用 UniGene 群資訊(或其它 EST 使成群的方
法) 裝配 ESTs 在同樣群。他們通常使用 mRNA 在群作為參考序列。第二種方法, 他們對
人的染色體排列所有 EST 序列。然後, 他們分析 ESTs 的被排列的資訊, 對同樣基因地點
排列。第一方法是以為 ESTs 在同樣 UniGene 群應該對應於一個唯一基因。因為對應的基
因知道, 而不是排列所有 ESTs 對整體染色體, 我們能對對應的 mRNA 排列 ESTs 。所以, 
它節省很多計算時間。 
 
目前使用 EST 預測選擇性裁剪位置的方法主要有兩種：第一，先將 EST 做分群的動作，
一個群相當於一個基因的代表，然後判斷這些基因是否有不相同之處，及選擇性裁剪之位
置。例子: PALS db 採取最長的 mRNA 序列在各 UniGene 群因為與 ESTs 和 mRN 選擇性
裁剪 被排列在同樣群預言選擇裁剪的站點的參考的序列。[14]第二，則是直接將所有的EST
對其到基因體上，之後再判斷選擇性裁剪的位置。由於 EST 數量龐大(目前約七百四十萬
筆)，且基因體序列冗長（三十億個鹼基）因此是一件耗時耗記憶體的的運算過程。本研究
團隊同時提出一演算法解決此問題，並建立一資料庫 AVATAR[2]。 
 
Avatar(A Value Added TRAnscriptome database)資料庫是被本研究團隊發展出來的資料
庫。其 Avatar 資料庫內包含了繪製人類序列中 ESTs 和 mRNA 序列圖。目前 Avatar 資料庫
通過大規模分析出，共包含的基因選擇性裁切事件共 10,194。且包含 3’端、5’端的人類選
擇性裁切事件、卡夾式選擇性裁剪、保留的內含子及互相排斥選擇性裁切事件[24]。 
 
我們透過針對序列的資料進行分析，以及使用智慧代理人技術，建構出一個完整的選
擇性裁剪資料庫。此資料庫除擁有人類的選擇性裁剪表現形式資料外，同時可針對新舊版
資料的差異進行分析並針對有變動的資料重新計算，因此與傳統方法相比，大幅減低了運

無法準時參加會議。 
 
若有未來同樣需至該國開會之同學，請特別注意簽證與住宿之問題。該國簽證辦理時間較一般國
家要長，三到四周是較為保險之辦理時間，且盡量避免透過中共北京使館代辦簽證。除辦理時間
較長之外，北京使館明確表示須本人親自取件，無法代為寄回台灣，且申請時須出具該國當地人
士之保證函(保證準時回國且無非法滯留情事)。台灣同學可透過新加坡，日本或維也納大使館代
辦即無須保證函(台灣護照與申請文件需以快遞寄送至該國使館，完成申請後會代為寄回)。另外
則是住宿之問題，台灣目前沒有旅行社可代辦訂房事宜，須親自與旅館接洽或透過歐洲之旅行社
代辦。申請簽證時需附住宿證明(一般經驗上至少要包含三分之二入境時間)，該國旅遊局網站提
供了國內所有經認證之民宿以及星級旅館，若想到當地再找旅館可會因無法出具住宿證明而遭大
使館拒絕簽證申請。

1 Introduction 
Eukaryotic genes contain stretches of DNA that usually do not 
code for protein products. These non-coding stretches, called 
introns, must be spliced out of pre-mRNA transcribed from the 
gene before the RNA can be translated into the protein. The 
protein coding parts of this splicing process accurately joins 
genes, called exons, segments into a continuous stretch of 
protein-coding mRNA. Alternative splicing (AS) the result is 
occurred when the splicing machinery (a spliceosome) 
produces different mature mRNAs from the transcript of a 
gene [1-3]. 
Comparative analysis of ESTs and cDNAs with genomic 
DNA predict a high frequency of alternative splicing in human 
and mouse genes. However, the extent of alternative splicing   
conservation among mammals is still being discussed [4-6]. If 
an alternative splicing event is conserved in both human and 
mouse, and thus more likely to be biologically significant and 
provides some advantage to the organisam. However, if an 
alternative splicing event in one species is not conserved in the 
other species, it can also be functional, representing exons 
created after the divergence of the human and the mouse 
lineages. It is interesting to assess the level of conservation of 
alternative splicing. 
 The conservation of alternative splicing events in human 
and mouse can be classified according to comparison result. 
We would like to discover three types of comparison result. If 
an alternative splicing event in one species and it also exists in 
other species, the event is called a conserved alternative 
splicing event (CAS events). If there is an alternative splicing 
event in one species and there is only one form in other species, 
the event is called a non-conserved alternative splicing event 
(NCAS events). We combined with the human exons and 
mouse exons to discover the cross-species alternative splicing 
events (CSA events). The cross-species alternative splicing 
event means event that in orthologous genes, there is no 
alternative splicing event in both species and their splicing 
forms are distinct. On the other words, if we combine exons 
and splicing events of two species, it looks like there is one 
alternative splicing event in this combination.  
 
2 Materials 
The all EST sequences were downloaded from NCBI 
(ftp://ftp.ncbi.nih.gov/repository/dbEST/gzipped/dbEST.repor
ts.date.no.gz). As of FEB 14, 2004; the dbEST database has 
nearly 5.1 million human ESTs and 3.7 million mouse ESTs.  
Human and mouse genomic sequences retrieved from NCBI 
contig build 34(genbank format) and build 31, respectively 
(ftp://ftp.ncbi.nih.gov/genbank/genomes). Gene information 
and mRNA sequences were fetched from NCBI RefSeq 
project [7]. 
We used the information about alternative splicing from 
Avatar (a value added transcriptome database) [8]. Briefly, the 
database filters the expressed sequences that may be 
contaminated, so only ESTs alignments with similarity scores 
greater than 94% and contained canonical introns were used 
for further alternative splicing analysis. In Avatar, authors 
used the alignment result from expressed sequences aligning 
to the genome generated by MUGUP [9] to detect the 
alternative splicing sites.  
The five classes are including exon skipping, mutually 
exclusive, alternative 3' splice site, and alternative 5' splice site. 
Using these data, there were 4,124 and 1,351 alternative exon 
skipping sites; 536 and 131 mutually exclusive sites; 11,479 
and 6,260 alternative 3' splice site; 8,010 and 3,682 alternative 
5' splice site and 16,267 and 3,518 retained intron identified in 
human and mouse orthologous pairs, respectively. 
We used the orthologous pairs between human and 
mouse genes from a reciprocal best match relationship 
according to annotation in the Homology Map database [10]. 
There are 9,136 orthologous pairs in the Homology Map 
database. 
 
3 Methods 
Our method is carried out in three main steps: First we choose 
carefully the orthologous genes pairs for further analysis. 
Second we identified orthologous exons between human and 
mouse by the results of the first step. Then we discovered 
CAS events  NCAS events and CSA events by the results of 
the second step. Due to page limitation, we only describe our 
method for exon skipping events. Methods of other types of 
alternative splicing events are similar and omitted. 
 
3.1.Preprocessing phase 
In the preprocessing phase, we would show how to choose the 
orthologous genes pairs for further analysis. Based on the 
Homology Map catalog of gene pairs that are orthologous 
between human and mouse, we searched for these 9,136 gene 
names in Avatar (fetched from NCBI RefSeq project) and 
7,078 human genes can be found.  
For those genes were not found in Avatar, 324 matched with 
18,729 HGNC gene aliases file [12] and were thus taken as 
additional genes sets. The genes corresponding to these 7,402 
(= 7,078 + 324) pairs were examined for occurrence in mouse 
gene name sets (obtained from NCBI RefSeq project). We 
searched 7,402 human gene names in Avatar and 6807 human 
genes can be found in mouse gene lists.

To identify orthologous exons between human and mouse, we 
used pairs of genes reported as reciprocal best matches in the 
Homology Map. For all target orthologous gene pairs, we first 
identified their corresponding exons in Avatar.
