摘要
面對未來地球資源無可避免的匱乏和消耗，建築設計納入「環境效率」思惟為必然之趨勢。整
合計畫的 WLC 環境效率整合平台，是以 ISO 的環境效率思維所建構之從外環境到室內環境的整體
尺度架構。本計畫在其中主要探討智慧健康營居環境中的室內裝修綠建材性能課題。
台灣屬於熱濕氣候，在探討濕氣環境時缺乏了建材的濕氣物性基礎資料。建材的濕氣物性資料
不只可應用在調濕設計上，在探討建築空間的熱舒適性、結露、建材劣化與生物性污染等議題時，
均須理解各種建材對於濕氣的反應。另一方面，許多標榜著「高性能」的進口建材藉著國外的標章
制度，在國內市場上產生了相當強的競爭力。但當台灣的建材業者有意進行研究開發時，卻苦無國
內的標準測試法，也造成國內建材產業難以掌握關鍵技術的問題。
本計畫主要建立室內裝修建材濕氣物性測試法與資料庫、台灣典型住宅空間型態與使用行為的
分析，以及在本土邊界條件的建構後進行室內空間整體的數值模擬，以建構智慧健康營居環境室內
本土建材濕氣環境之性能預測與評估。
關鍵詞：濕氣物性、標準測試法、綠建材
Abstract
Nowadays, the continuous consuming of the earth resources is unable to avoid, thus the thinking of
environmental efficiency becomes a crucial and major issue for architectural design. The objective of the
main proposal is to establish the WLC integrated framework of environmental efficiency, which is based
on the definition of ISO. The framework includes the environmental subjects from outdoor to indoor
living spaces. In this sub-research, we will study the environmental control via materials for living
environment regarding intelligent and healthy meanings.
Taiwan is located in high temperature and humidity region. Humidity control is absolutely an important
subject; however, the hygrothermal properties of interior materials could rarely be found in references.
The hygrothermal properties of materials can be used not only for moisture buffering studies, but also
researches related with thermal comfort, condensation, microbial polution…etc. Furthermore, plenty of 
imported interior materials, which are claimed as high performance, stimulate the industries of the interior
design market in Taiwan. The experimental standards for hygrothermal performance of interior materials
should be established for local researches and industries; therefore the local material manufacture could
obtain more chances of being more competitive in the market.
The objectives of this project is to establish the experimental standards for hygrothermal properties of
interior materials in Taiwan, to analyze the spatial structure and usage of typical residential housing in
Taiwan, and to evaluate the environmental control performance of interior materials via simulation with
the boundary conditions which are provided by the experiments.
Keywords: Hygrothermal Properties, Experiment Standard, Green Material

1.2 室內裝修建材的濕氣物性資料
台灣屬於熱濕氣候，在進行建築熱環境的探討時，理所當然地需要建材的熱物性來做為
基礎資料；但目前在探討濕氣環境時，卻缺乏了建材的濕氣物性基礎資料。建材的濕氣物性
資料，不只可應用在調濕設計上，在談討建築空間的熱舒適性、結露、建材劣化與生物性污
染等議題時，均須理解各種建材對於濕氣的反應。
在建立建材濕氣物性資料庫之前，不可或缺的是必須先建立建材濕氣物性的標準測試
法。在國外許多的文獻當中可發現，歐洲與日本均已建立了標準測試法，並進行許多建材的
量測以建立濕氣物性資料庫。而台灣目前並無國內的標準測試法，也造成國內建材產業難以
掌握關鍵技術的問題。
然而，國內對於建材的基本濕氣物性資料相當地缺乏，建築物理環境相關書籍中刊載的
濕氣物性僅有透濕係數（或稱為透濕係數或水蒸氣擴散係數），建築設計者不但難以從中得
到建材調濕性能之資訊，這些資料許多更是國外 40 年以前的測試資料（王錦堂，1968；中
村泰人、松尾陽等，1984；江哲銘，1997；賴榮平、林憲德等，1991）。因此，面對濕氣問
題日漸受到重視的台灣，標準測試法與建材濕氣物性資料庫都是急待建立的基礎研究。

2.2.2 平衡含水率曲線之測試法
平衡含水率曲線的定義為建材在不同相對濕度的環境之下，達到平衡時之含水率。因此
必須在 0%-98%的環境濕度之間（相對濕度範圍需考慮台灣氣候決定）取 4~5 個點進行測試。
圖 2 為 JIS A1475 所規範的測試用恆溫恆濕箱。利用恆溫恆濕箱建立環境溫濕度後，使用電
子秤量測材料的重量變化直到達到平衡。藉由乾燥材料重量即可求得建材在不同相對濕度下
之含水率。然而，相對濕度的實驗點須符合當地室內氣候的變動範圍，相對於國外採用
20%~95%的範圍，本研究考慮台灣室內相對濕度變動範圍，預定建構可控制相對濕度於
40~95%範圍的恆溫恆濕箱。
2.2.3 透濕係數之測試法
建材的透濕係數測試法，所需設備與平衡含水率類似，同樣是採用恆溫恆濕箱來控制環

過的水蒸氣質量。圖 3 為量測材料表面的物質通量（flux, kg/m2/s）所採用的透濕杯法（cup

三、相關研究文獻回顧
3.1 熱濕氣候下的住宅室內微生物污染問題
室內的高濕度環境是塵蟎與黴菌等微生物適於生長的環境。塵蟎的代謝物與黴菌孢子除
了引起氣喘等過敏性疾病外，Wessén 等研究者（1996）發現細菌與黴菌等微生物由來的揮
發性有機化合物，稱為 MVOC（Microbial Volatile Organic Compounds），是造成過敏、感染
性疾病、中毒症狀，甚至導致癌症之原因（Leino et al., 2003）（Fischera et al., 2000）。Clarkea
et al.（1999）根據黴菌培養實驗，發現室內溫度於 15~30°C，相對濕度超過 75%適合好乾性
黴菌生長，而相對濕度過 85%適合好濕性黴菌生長；而 Abe（1996）提出黴菌指數（fungal
index），同樣指出 15~30°C，相對濕度大於 75%之環境易形成黴菌生長。由文獻可得知，相
對濕度超過 75%之環境，產生生物性污染之風險相對提高。
Wu et al. （2000）在進行實際量測後指出，台灣地區在夏季時空戶室內真菌濃度高於美
國、芬蘭等國家，顯示台灣的室內氣候較適合真菌的生長。可見台灣高溫多濕氣候下室內生
物性污染問題是相當重要的課題。

3.4 建築型態分析
根據中華民國內政部營建署之資料，台灣地區住宅總數為 6,378,836。其中連棟式住宅(長
型街屋)約佔而公寓大廈約佔 32%(資料統計至西元 2005 年)(圖 10). 此二類型之住宅建築佔
超過住宅總數的一半，可代表目前台灣本土典型住宅型式，故本研究以此二類住宅型式為探
討對象。
3.4.1 連棟式住宅(長型街屋)
因為整體都市規劃，都市中住宅分區基地形狀多為長方形，故建築物配置多迎合長方形
基地變化，而形成長軸向的建築配置型態。在此配置下，建築物的開口則多出現於建築物的
短軸向面上，而長軸向面則幾乎不開窗(如圖 11)。此種建築型態導致建物中心之空間形成封
閉不通風的空間型態，而這些封閉的空間(如浴廁空間)則形成住宅中濕氣累積不散的區域。
3.4.2 公寓大廈型住宅
近年來因為都市化的關係，公寓大廈為台灣社會中一種普遍的住宅型式。此種住宅型式
的基本平面中，樓梯間以及電梯梯廳多位於建築物之中心，其周圍圍繞 2-6 戶不等的住宅單
元，每一戶單元皆有不同的方位朝向(如圖 12)。於是為了達到最佳的居住品質一般皆將居室
空間(如客廳、臥室等)設置於建築物開窗處，以獲得較好之光線以及通風。故如浴室以及廚
房等濕氣較容易產生累積的空間，則設置於建物內部剩餘的空間，造成光線以及通風的死角。

本計劃模擬以 DOE(Department of Energy)所提供之台北全年逐時氣候資料，於

如表 6 所示。模擬結果以 Case 1~5 逐步確認調濕建材對室內溫度與濕度的影響。
Case 1 為無使用調濕建材下室內濕氣環境模擬，其目的在於確認無調濕建材時室內濕度

Although Taiwan government understands the importance of 
developing renewable energy, the speed of development still 
grows slowly. The target that the Ministry of Economic Affairs 
declared is that the production of the renewable energy should 
achieve 8% in total power generation systems before 2050[6];
however, the statistical data reveals only 0.42% in August 2010
(Fig. 2). Therefore, even if the target is more conservative than 
advanced countries, it is still a distant goal.
The total area of Taiwan is 36000 km2 where contains the 
mountains higher than 3000 m [7] [8]. There are 23.22 million 
people; the density of the population is 640 people / km2, which 
represents a dense population area. In other words, this 
geographical condition is not conducive to large-scale 
renewable energy development such as wind farm. Therefore, 
in order to reach the target of the renewable energy 
popularization, building-scale renewable energy policy such as 
ZEB becomes more important. In Taiwan, if the zero energy 
houses could be a positive policy, it would be an effective
catalyst to develop renewable energy.
Taiwan is located at the subtropical area, which presents a 
hot and humid climate phenomenon. Thus, the appropriate 
design strategies of ZEB are different from that of Europe and 
America where they focus on the building insulation and 
air-tightness. The different areas express their unique landscape, 
local building materials, and construction methods. Therefore, 
how to develop a suitable locally design strategy is an 
important issue. Taking into consideration on the local climate 
data with the feasibility of the renewable energy, the purpose of 
this study is to propose appropriate residential energy-efficient 
design strategies, and further to suggest the models of the ideal 
zero energy farmhouses. A detached house in rural area in 
Chiayi (120.6E 23.5N), located in the southern Taiwan and on 
the Tropic of Cancer, was being selected as the study case. In 
the process for attaining the goal of this study, the following 
tasks should be considered:
z Based on the weather data in Chiayi, to analyze the 
potential of the renewable energy for houses design.
z To assess the residential energy consumption, and to 
propose the design strategies of zero energy home.
z To suggest the design proposals of zero energy home.
II. ZERO ENERGY HOME
ZEB was academically also called Net Zero Energy Building 
(NZEB), which does not mean that there is no energy 
consumption in the building but is considered that the net value 
of the energy budget is positive. In other words, the ZEB which 
allows the income of energy is over the expenditure. 
Residential is one kind of ZEB, usually and academically called 
Zero Energy Homes (ZEH) or Zero Energy Residential 
Building (ZERB).
Recently, there are many ZEH proposals being published.  
For improving the ZEH quality, the studies on these proposals 
may be separated into simulations and field measurement.  For 
instance, Reference [2] demonstrated two models that were 
simulated in different climates, and which further prove that the 
appropriate different types of residential buildings are needed 
in different climates. Therefore, weather conditions are 
important factors in designing ZEH. Another simulation study 
illustrates that a house with passive design is more 
energy-saving than the house with curtain-glasses [9]. The 
studies on numerical simulation usually focus on development 
of renewable energy and the evaluation on designing. Moreover, 
the simulations of solar thermal power, solar photovoltaic 
power, and wind power were widely conducted in these studies. 
For example, the solar thermal power is most appropriate to be 
used for heating in temperate and frigid zones, and the wind 
power is suitable for coastal areas. However, Taiwan presents a 
different climate condition from temperate and frigid zones. 
Thus, the end-use of the power and the method of 
building-scale power generation should be carried out in 
different concepts.
According to the supplemental options of renewable energy, 
Marszal et al. [10] suggested five supply options of ZEB. The 
first three supply options have been considered to be that the 
renewable energy being produced in the jobsite is suitable for 
meeting architectural design regulations; the rest two supply 
options are classified into large-scaled or centralized energy 
production in zero energy community or urban planning. In this 
study, the selected case is a single residential building; 
therefore, the first three supply options that Marszal et al. [10]
mentioned would be applied as calculation methodologies.
III. METHOD
A. The Case Study Object
In order to propose the suitable ZEH design strategies for 
architectural design process, a detached house in rural area in 
Chiayi (120.6E 23.5N) was selected as the study case. Chiayi is 
a county located in southern part of Taiwan and on the Tropic 
of Cancer, and with the population of about 538,000 and area of 
about 1903km2. Agriculture is the main local industry, and 
farm house is the main building type in this region. Besides, 
Chiayi is hot and humid climate in subtropical zone. Plains is 
the main topography, and the residential type is detached house.
The total area of the jobsite is 916 m2. The basic design draft
of this house has been completed, which shows in Fig. 3. It is a
common and typical residential house in local. The total floor 
area is 276 m2 with 7 people (3 generations) living in, including
5 bedrooms, 3 bathrooms, 2 balconies, 1 living room, and 1
dining room.
B. The estimation of housing energy consumption
To estimate the future power consumption of this residence, 
the simulation model of residential electricity consumption that 
Kuo[11] published was applied in this study. This simulation 
model is based on the measurements of 62 residential end-use 
data with regression analysis during 3 year. This model 
summed up the influential factors, including different life 
patterns, rooms, and appliances, and developed a simple 
operation interface of the simulation program for the architects 
and designers to estimate the energy consumption of residence 
(Fig. 4), regardless of whether their having equipped with a 
high level engineering knowledge or not.

IV. RESULTS
A. The result of calculate housing energy consumption 
The case in this study  is a single house in southern Taiwan. 
The total floor area of this case is 276m2; and the residents

V.CONCLUSION
Due to the global climate change and fossil fuels depletion, 
ZEB became a trend of necessity in the global communities. 
Even though there have been many ZEB researches in western 
world, the direction of research is not on same track in Taiwan,
due to its geographical location with hot and high humidity 
weather. For building ZEH, the conditions of geography and 
society in Taiwan are not conducive to large-scale renewable 
energy development. Therefore, the small-scale development, 
such as the residential renewable energy policy, becomes more 
important and practical to Taiwan situation. As such, how to 
develop a suitable locally design strategy is an important issue.
A residential consumption evaluation system is used to 
evaluate the power consumption of a detached farmhouse in 
Chiayi. According the solar power reduction algorithm, the 
results demonstrate that at least 14 pieces of solar panels should 
be installed to supply all the demand for power consumption of 
5088kWh. In addition, when the tilt angel is between 20° to 30° 
and solar panels face toward south direction, the most effective 
solar radiation could be produced. Therefore, three proposals 
were carried out in this study, which were combined the solar 
panels with the roof, to create the optimal conditions for 
achieving the zero energy house demands.
To conclude, this study purported to find out the optimal 
solutions of building ZEH in Taiwan. The solar power is 
analyzed and considered to perform the key point for producing 
the residential power generation. However, in this study, the 
energy production is solved but the energy consumption 
relating to the whole energy-saving efficiency has not been 
clearly discussed. Moreover, the design proposals are focused 
on the shape of the roof with the solar panels for the best solar 
power generation; but the design of the main housing building 
is not considered, which might affect the energy consumption.
Therefore, the result of this study will be the preceding of the 
completely integrated study, and will be turned into the 
parameters of the whole ZEH energy-efficiency simulation to 
obtain the final housing model for Chiayi or similar zone in the 
future. The authors hope the final result in future would not 
only be the ZEH, but also improve comfortableness and the 
healthiness of the occupants, and also provide an advanced 
reference for the related industries in Taiwan.

In this research, the simulation bases on the EMPD 
(Effective Moisture Penetration Depth) method, also called 
lumped parameter approach [11] or buffer storage humidity 
model [12], which is a method assumed that the moisture 
penetration only happened in a limited depth from the building 
material surface [13]. In this paper, the EMPD was performed 
by EnergyPlus, which was developed by DOE (Department of 
Energy) of the U.S. 
Therefore, the objectives of this study is to compare the 
moisture buffering performance of pinewood under different 
moisture condition setup, to confirm feasibility and 
practicability that the EnergyPlus applied to moisture buffering 
simulation of interior materials, and to suggest an ordinary, 
reliable, and useful simulation tool with accuracy regarding 
moisture buffering to architecture related industries for 
supporting building design process. Researchers expect that the 
results could be developed to the practical reference for 
architects, interior designers, or other related industries. 
II. METHODS 
A. EMPD 
EMPD method is a simplified algorithm method to simulate 
the moisture absorption and desorption of building material 
surface. This is assumed that the moisture transfer phenomena 
only could reach a certain depth from the surface in building 
material, which could be calculated only by the permeability 
and sorption isotherm. Compared with the HAM model, certain 
material properties, such as porosity, are not needed in EMPD. 
Therefore, EMPD could be applied to simulation and reduce 
the calculating time.  
B. Equation

environment is extremely complicated. The detailed 
information of geometry and heat and moisture material 
properties will be needed, such as building materials, coatings, 
furniture, and all the objects which show hygrothermal 
response. The property information is usually not available or 
difficult to obtain. Thus, the simplified method to simulate 
moisture exchange between air and materials could be used to 
alter.

D. Indoor air quality evaluation 
Humidity is an important parameter, which causes serious 
problems to human and buildings. Accordingly, 
moisture-buffering effect should be carried out to solve the 
issue. Table 4 demonstrates the percentage of relative humidity 
appears during a whole. The researchers also studied in 5 cases 
in the previous paragraphs. 
According to Table 4, the RH in all cases is mostly around 
70-99%, which is much higher than the 40-70% standard. 
Compared Case 03 and 05, the results show that moisture 
buffering materials reduces the percetage of 100%RH from 
18.98% to 10.27%, which presents moisture buffering effect 
performance of the material. However, even moisture buffering 
reduce the percentage of the highest RH, the RH still remains 
high, where mostly around 90-99%RH (43.82% of the year). 
The comparison of Case 02 and 04 is in the similar situation. 
The results mean that to achieve the effective moisture 
buffering effect should not only use moisture buffering 
materials but the other building passive design dtrategies will 
be needed, such as ventilation. This result also shows that 
vnetilation is extremely important, which will be the future task 
of the research. 
V. CONCLUSION 
Taiwan locates in subtropics, which represents a high 
temperature and high humidity climate phenomenon. The high 
temperature and humidity here present a serious indoor air 
quality issue, which will cause bio-pollution and harmful for 
human health; it will also cause construction problem, such as 
the decay and ruin of building materials. Therefore, how to 
apply moisture buffering materials practically into the indoor 
environment should be carried out.  
In this research, the simulation bases on the EMPD method 
which was performed by EnergyPlus, a simulation tool 
developed by DOE. The researchers attempt to compare the 
moisture buffering performance of pinewood under different 
moisture condition setup, to confirm feasibility and 
practicability that the EnergyPlus applied to moisture buffering 
simulation of interior materials, and to suggest an ordinary, 
reliable, and useful simulation tool with accuracy regarding 
moisture buffering to architecture related industries for 
supporting building design process. 
First, though the reference reviews, the researchers verify the 
EMPD method and the correlation factors in the simulation. 
Secondly, the simulation model is based on typical housing 
room unit built as a one-zone model; the moisture production is 
based on the typical daily schedule of occupants, which is 
8g/m3h at 6 a.m. to 8 a.m. and 4 g/m3h at 4 p.m. to 10 p.m. The 
simulation is operated as 5 cases with and without EMPD.  
Finally, researchers discuss the results of the 5 case studies. 
Therefore, according to the reference reviews, EMPD is an 
effective method of calculating moisture content, and could be 
operated easily to simulate the moisture variation of indoor 
environment by supporting program. Also, EnergyPlus is 
verified as a feasible tool to carried out the EMPD, and it is a 
intermediate-level tool compared to other HAM tool, which
