 I
中文摘要 
光纖與光纖元件是光纖通訊與感測系統最重要的基本元件之一。為了保有高機械強
度，傳統上在製造光纖的過程中會在玻璃光纖外面加上塑膠被覆。然而，加上塑膠被覆的
光纖對於抵抗水氣入侵而仍能保有長時間高機械強度之抗疲勞性並非很好。因此，被覆碳
薄膜材料之高抗疲勞碳密封鍍層光纖引起廣泛的研究，並被認為是光纖通訊的重要技術之
一。倘若碳密封鍍層光纖也能具有導電性，在未來的應用上將會更為廣泛。具導電性的碳
密封鍍層光纖不僅具有良好的密封性，同時也可以用來傳輸光和電的訊號。 
本二年期計畫將以化學氣相沉積法研製具導電性碳密封鍍層光纖，以作為發展密封鍍
層光纖被動元件與智慧型感測元件的基礎。計畫第一年是使用電漿輔助化學氣相沉積法和
熱化學氣相沉積法，分別通入前驅氣體甲烷、乙烯、乙炔和丙烷等碳氫化合物，並在沉積
碳鍍層的過程中同時摻雜氮原子，以研製具導電性碳密封鍍層光纖。本計畫第二年仍使用
電漿輔助化學氣相沉積法和熱化學氣相沉積法，但在沉積碳鍍層的過程中同時摻雜鋁金屬
元素，來研製具導電性碳密封鍍層光纖。本計畫的目標除了使碳鍍層具有高密封性外，並
期望在第一年能使其導電率達到 5.0×105 ohm-1·m-1 而在第二年其導電率能趨近於金屬導體
的導電率。 
本專題研究計劃經第一年的努力執行，已完成熱化學氣相沉積用感應耦合電漿系統的
建置和許多實驗結果。其中，以熱化學氣相沉積碳鍍層且沉積溫度為 900℃，工作壓力為
575±25 torr， CH4/Ar 比為 7，總流量為 32 sccm，沉積時間為 120 分時，可以得到碳鍍層
的最佳導電率為 5×105Ω-1∙m-1。另外，本專題研究計劃經第二年的努力執行，已完成熱化
學氣相沉積系統用真空抽氣系統的建置和許多實驗結果。其中，以熱化學氣相沉積碳鍍層
且沉積溫度為 975℃，工作壓力為 30±5 torr，CH4/N2 比為 5，總流量為 20 sccm，射頻瓦數
為 100 W，沉積時間為 10 分時，可以得到碳鍍層的最佳導電率為 1×106Ω-1∙m-1，趨近於金
屬導體的導電率。本專題研究計劃也將相關研究成果發表於 7 篇 SCI 期刊論文。 
 
關鍵詞：光纖、非晶質碳、薄膜材料、化學氣相沈積

綜合申請人過去的經驗[1-8]，利用熱化學氣相沉積法所製備的碳鍍層會呈現近石
墨的結構，所以碳鍍層的導電率可以達到 5×104 ohm-1·m-1；而利用電漿輔助化學氣相
沉積法製備的碳鍍層會呈現含氫非晶質碳(Hydrogenated amorphous carbon)的結構，在
導電過程中電子會受到非晶質結構缺陷的侷限，使得碳鍍層的導電率約為 10-9-10-12 
ohm-1·m-1。若欲得到導電性較好的類石墨化碳鍍層，含氫非晶質碳需經過高溫退火處
理才可達成[18-23]。已有數篇研究指出[24-26]，假若在碳鍍層結構中摻雜(Doping)氮(N)
或磷(P)等元素，氮或磷將會扮演電子施體(Donor) 的角色，進而提升碳鍍層的導電性。
因此，藉以氮或磷元素的摻雜可以達到研製具導電性之碳密封鍍層光纖的目標。本研
究計劃第一年考慮在以電漿輔助化學氣相沉積法和熱化學氣相沉積法研製碳密封鍍層
時，同時添加高純度氮氣(99.995%)[以氮氣為主要考量]，並設法將氮氣解離成氮元素
(N)摻雜到碳鍍層中。因為電漿輔助化學氣相沉積法所製備的碳鍍層導電率相對較低，
所以製程後還需要對碳鍍層施以退火處理。另一方面，碳鍍層中若摻雜金屬元素也是
另一種提高碳鍍層導電性的方式。表 1 為不同金屬元素在室溫下的導電率[27] ，表中
所列之金屬元素導電率皆大於 2×106 ohm-1·m-1。在碳鍍層中摻雜微量金屬元素將有助
於提升碳鍍層的導電率。因此，本研究計劃第二年考慮在以電漿輔助化學氣相沉積法
和熱化學氣相沉積法研製碳密封鍍層時，同時將金屬[以鋁元素為主要考量]離化並摻雜
到碳鍍層中。對於電漿輔助化學氣相沉積法製備的碳鍍層，在製程後仍需要對碳鍍層
施以退火處理。本二年期專題研究計畫構想內容(目的與方法)，整理於表 2。

 7
本計畫第一年是使用電漿輔助化學氣相沉積法(設備自有)和熱化學氣相沉積法(設備
自有)，分別通入前驅氣體甲烷、乙烯、乙炔和丙烷等碳氫化合物，並在沉積碳鍍層的過
程中同時摻雜氮原子，進行具導電性碳密封鍍層光纖之研製。在電漿輔助化學氣相沉積
製程中是利用通入不同碳氫化合物、氫氣與氮氣的混合比例，並且改變製程中的射頻功
率、工作壓力、沉積溫度和外加偏壓等參數，然後再利用高溫快速退火來製備具導電性
碳密封鍍層光纖。另外，熱化學氣相沉積製程是利用通入不同碳氫化合物與氮氣的混合
比例，其中氮氣必須先利用感應耦合電漿輔助裂解成為氮原子，並且改變熱化學氣相沉
積法中的沉積溫度、沉積壓力、沉積時間等參數，來製備具導電性碳密封鍍層光纖。隨
後進行碳鍍層的各種特性量測分析，配合具導電性碳密封鍍層特性分析結果找出最佳製
程條件。除了碳鍍層的密封性外，導電性是本年度計畫重點努力的目標。在計畫的第一
年，期望能使碳密封鍍層的導電率達到 5.0×105 ohm-1·m-1。 
本計畫第二年則參考並延伸第一年的計畫，使用電漿輔助化學氣相沉積法和熱化學氣
相沉積法，在沉積碳鍍層的過程中同時摻雜金屬元素(以鋁元素為主要考量)，進行具導電
性碳密封鍍層光纖之研製。隨後進行碳鍍層的各種特性量測分析，配合具導電性碳密封鍍
層的特性分析結果找出最佳製程條件。藉由特定金屬元素的添加，預期除了可以使碳密封
鍍層導電性增加外，亦可降低化學反應活化能，且使得碳鍍層對水接觸角上升，製備具有
高導電性且阻水性更佳之具導電性碳密封鍍層光纖。碳鍍層的導電性亦是本計劃第二年重
點努力的目標，期望能使碳密封鍍層的導電率趨近於金屬導體的導電率。 
3. 結果與討論 
    本專題研究計劃經第一年的努力執行，已完成熱化學氣相沉積用感應耦合電漿系統的
建置和許多實驗結果。其中，以熱化學氣相沉積碳鍍層且沉積溫度為 900℃，工作壓力為
575±25 torr， CH4/Ar 比為 7，總流量為 32 sccm，沉積時間為 120 分時，可以得到碳鍍層
的最佳導電率為 5×105Ω-1∙m-1。 
另外，本專題研究計劃經第二年的努力執行，已完成熱化學氣相沉積系統用真空抽氣系
統的建置和許多實驗結果。其中，以熱化學氣相沉積碳鍍層且沉積溫度為 975℃，工作壓
力為 30±5 torr，CH4/N2 比為 5，總流量為 20 sccm，電漿瓦數為 100 W，沉積時間為 10 分
時，可以得到碳鍍層的最佳導電率為 1×106Ω-1∙m-1，趨近於金屬導體的導電率。 
本專題研究計劃也將相關研究成果整理成 7 篇 SCI 期刊論文和 8 篇研討會論文 (詳列於
計畫成果自評，期刊論文摘要請參閱附錄)。

The effect of substrate sizes on characteristics of carbon films deposited on 
glass plate substrates by thermal chemical vapor deposition 
 
Po-Yu Chen1, Sham-Tsong Shiue1*, and Hung-Yi Lin2 
1 Department of Materials Science and Engineering, National Chung Hsing University, 250 Kuo 
Kuang Road, Taichung 402, Taiwan. 
2 Mechanical and Systems Research Laboratories, Industrial Technology Research Institute, 
Hsinchu 310, Taiwan. 
Abstract 
When carbon films are prepared using thermal CVD, properties of carbon films are affected by 
many factors, such as the precursor gas, deposition temperature, and working pressure. It is 
believed that the substrate size is also an important factor in preparing carbon films by thermal 
CVD. Hence, this study investigates the effect of substrate sizes on the property of carbon 
coatings that are deposited on glass plate substrates in a quartz tube furnace using thermal CVD. 
Experimental results show that the deposition thickness decreases with increasing the silica glass 
plate width, indicating that the deposition rate of carbon coatings decreases with increasing the 
deposition area of the glass plate substrate. Meanwhile, the ordered degree and crystallite size of 
carbon films increase with increasing the glass plate width, while the number and size of particles 
on carbon coating surfaces, surface roughness, and electrical resistivity of carbon films decrease. 
Keywords: chemical vapor deposition, thin film, substrate size, Raman spectra.

The effect of rapid thermal annealing on characteristics of carbon coatings on 
optical fibers 
 
Jen-Feng Yu, Tsuen-Sung Chen, Hung-Chien Lin, Sham-Tsong Shiue* 
Department of Materials Science and Engineering, National Chung Hsing University, 250 Kuo 
Kuang Road, Taichung 402, Taiwan. 
 
 
Abstract 
 
Carbon films are deposited on silica glass fibers by radio-frequency plasma enhanced 
chemical vapor deposition, and the properties of these optical fibers are improved by rapid 
thermal annealing. The annealing temperatures are set to 100, 200, 300, 400, 500, 550, 600, and 
700 oC. Experimental results show that the thickness and surface roughness of carbon films 
decrease with increasing the annealing temperature ranging from as-deposited to 500 oC, while 
the sp2 carbon bonding, sp3 CH3 bonding, optical band gap, and water contact angle of carbon 
films increase. As the annealing temperature increases from 550 to 700 oC, the parts of carbon 
films are delaminated. The sp3 CH3 bonding in carbon films is shifted to the sp3 CH2 bonding, and 
the sp3 CH2 bonding is subsequently transferred to the sp2 CH bonding. Meanwhile, the amount of 
the sp2 carbon bonding in carbon films increases, while the optical band gap decreases. Based on 
the evaluation of water-repellency and low temperature morphology of carbon films, the carbon 
film annealed at the temperature of 500 oC is the best for production of carbon-coated optical 
fibers. As compared to conventional thermal annealing, rapid thermal annealing is more effective 
to improve the properties of carbon-coated optical fibers.

Effects of different acetylene/nitrogen ratios on characteristics of carbon 
coatings on optical fibers prepared by thermal chemical vapor 
deposition 
 
 
Ren-Hung Lee, Liang-Shin Lai, Sham-Tsong Shiue* 
 
Department of Materials Science and Engineering 
National Chung Hsing University 
250 Kuo Kuang Road, Taichung 40227, Taiwan 
 
 
Abstract  
The effects of C2H2/(C2H2+N2) ratios on characteristics of carbon coatings on optical fibers 
prepared by thermal chemical vapor deposition are investigated. The C2H2/(C2H2+N2) ratios are 
set to 60, 70, 80, 90, and 100%. Additionally, the deposition temperature, working pressure, and 
mass flow rate are 1003 K, 133 kPa, and 40 sccm, respectively. The deposition rate, 
microstructure, and electrical resistivity of carbon coatings are measured. The low-temperature 
surface morphology of carbon-coated optical fibers is elucidated. Experimental results indicate 
that the deposition rate increases with increasing the C2H2/(C2H2+N2) ratio, and the deposition 
process is located at a surface controlled regime. As the deposition rate increases, the electrical 
resistivity of carbon coatings increases, while the ordered degree, nano-crystallite size, and sp2 
carbon atoms of the carbon coatings decrease. Additionally, the low-temperature surface 
morphology of the carbon coatings shows that if the carbon coating thickness is not smaller than 
289 nm, decreasing the deposition rate is good for producing hermetic optical fiber coatings. 
 
Keywords: Thermal chemical vapor deposition, Carbon coating, Optical fiber, Microstructure.

Effects of deposition parameters on characteristics of carbon coatings on 
optical fibers prepared by thermal chemical vapor deposition 
 
Sham-Tsong Shiue,* Po-Yu Chen, Shin-Shueh Chen, Ren-Hung Lee, Tsuen-Sung Chen 
Department of Materials Science and Engineering 
National Chung Hsing University 
250 Kuo Kuang Road, Taichung 402, Taiwan 
Hung-Yi Lin 
Mechanical and Systems Research Laboratories 
Industrial Technology Research Institute 
Hsinchu 310, Taiwan 
Abstract 
The effects of deposition parameters on characteristics of carbon coatings on optical fibers 
prepared by thermal chemical vapor deposition are investigated. The deposition parameters are 
selected as follows. The CH4/(CH4+N2) ratio is in the range between 20 and 100%; the 
temperature is set from 1173 to 1248 K; the working pressure is arranged between 50 and 100 
kPa, and the residence time is ranging from 1.47 to 7.37 s. The deposition rate, microstructure, 
and electrical resistivity of carbon coatings are measured. The low-temperature surface 
morphology of carbon-coated optical fibers is elucidated. Experimental results indicate that the 
deposition rate increases with increasing the CH4/(CH4+N2) ratio, deposition temperature, 
working pressure, and residence time. The activation energy (= 456 kJ/mole) of carbon 
deposition from methane was shown to correlate to the activation energy of methane dissociation. 
The deposition rate is proportional to about first-order of partial pressure of methane, and thus, 
the deposition process is mainly controlled by the process to create mono-carbon species in the 
carbon film. As the deposition rate increases, the size and number of particles on the carbon 
coating surface and electrical resistivity of carbon coatings increase, while the ordered degree, 
nano-crystallite size, and sp2 carbon atoms of the carbon coatings decrease. Additionally, the 
low-temperature surface morphology of carbon coatings shows that as the carbon coating 
thickness is large enough to sustain the thermal loading, decreasing the deposition rate is good for 
producing hermetic optical fiber coatings. 
Keywords: Thermal chemical vapor deposition, Carbon coating, Optical fiber, Microstructure.
