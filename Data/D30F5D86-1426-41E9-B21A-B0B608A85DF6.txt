ABSTRACT 
A nano-patterned tungsten oxide nanowire (TONW)-based hydrogen sensor was developed by 
UV nanoimprint lithography (UV-NIL) to effectively improve sensing performance. Nano-scale 
arrays of patterned TONWs with 500 nm of spacing were fabricated by UV-NIL and thermal 
oxidation. The nano-patterned TONWs were deposited using interdigitated platinum (Pt) electrodes 
to form hydrogen sensors. Planar and nano-patterned TONW sensors were compared in various 
hydrogen concentrations from 5 to 400 ppm at room temperature, with results indicating that the 
TONW sensors are more sensitive and respond more quickly to H2 gas than those of planar TONW 
sensors. 
 
Keywords：Tungsten oxide nanowire, hydrogen sensors, nanoimprint lithography 
 
1. Introduction 
The development of transitional metal oxide nanostructures has prompted a variety of research 
projects in developing device-oriented nanostructures to take advantage of their outstanding 
properties. Tungsten oxide is a wide-binding energy n-type semiconductor which makes it attractive 
for electrochromic devices, photo detectors and gas sensors Tungsten oxide is widely in 
electrochromic devices [1], photo detectors [2] and gas sensors [3] and its electrical and optical 
properties have thus attracted considerable attention.  
One-dimensional nanostructures with a high surface-to-volume ratio and small grain size have

structures with particular nano-patterns to obtain advanced gas sensitivity, which should be very 
promising for gas sensors applications. The hydrogen detection capabilities of the nano-patterned 
TONW sensors were measured electrically. The gas sensing performances of the planar TONW and 
nano-patterned TONW sensors were studied and compared to verify the influence of nano-patterned 
structures on sensing properties.  
 
2. Experimental 
A quartz master mold with periodic nanoline arrays with a feature size of 500 nm in the 
nanoimprint process was fabricated by the e-beam lithography and lift-off process (see [17] for 
details). UV-NIL was performed using a NX-2000 (Nanonex, USA). A master mold was gently 
placed in contact with the n-type silicon substrate and sandwiched between the two membranes of 
the NX-2000, thus providing optimal uniformity over the whole imprinted field. Prior to UV-NIL, 
the substrate was coated with UV curable resin PAK-01-200 (TOYO GOSEI, Japan) at 2500 
rpm/min for 30 s, producing 300 nm films, and then baked at 80 °C for 120 s to remove the solvent. 
Nanoimprint pressure of 200 psi for 30 s was followed by 10 s of UV exposure at room temperature. 
The residual layer was then removed under an O2 : Ar (8:1) mixture with an RF power of 160 W for 
95 s by reactive ion etching (RIE). Finally, the tungsten metal film was deposited on the 
nano-patterned resistant layer by DC magnetron sputtering and then lifted off in a sulfuric acid bath, 
resulting in the nano-patterned tungsten film to synthesize the TONWs.

Figure 3 shows the SEM images of the nano-patterned TONWs fabricated by using UV-NIL. 
After imprinting into the photo-resist incorporated with the quartz master mold, the master mold 
pattern is transferable which allows for high replicability  without residuals. The surface 
morphology of the patterned TONWs is similar to that of the planar TONWs, which is 
well-distributed with an average length and diameter of approximately 350 nm and 25 nm, 
respectively. Following UV-NIL and thermal oxidation, the spacing of the nano-patterned TONWs 
is wider (about 600 nm) than a master mold which probably causes partial nano-pattern distortions.

Figure 4(a) presents the hydrogen sensing performance of the planar TONW and patterned 
TONW sensors with different gas concentrations from 5 to 400 ppm. When the hydrogen is 
introduced, the resistance decreases with time and is saturated at a value that depends on the gas 
concentration for both of the sensors. The resistance of TONWs is known to decrease with 
[(Rair-RH2)/Rair] ×100, where RH2 is the resistance of the hydrogen sensor exposed to H2 ambient and 
Rair represents the resistance in a dry air environment. In general, sensors exhibit a higher sensitivity 
response when they are exposed to hydrogen with higher concentrations. Both the planar TONW 
and the nano-patterned TONW sensors can detect hydrogen molecules in concentrations as low as 4 
ppm with an observable signal (0.43% and 0.66%, respectively). Figure 4(b) shows sensor 
sensitivity measured in environments with hydrogen at different concentrations. The planar TONW 
sensors exhibit sensitivity of 0.85 % with a high hydrogen concentration of 400 ppm, while the 
nano-patterned TONW sensors exhibit a much higher sensitivity of 1.13% under the same 
conditions. This indicates that, in the various H2 concentrations, the nano-patterned TONW sensors 
are much more sensitive to H2 gas than the planar TONW sensors, which is consistent with several 
researchers’ reports. Wan et al. [21] and Gao et al. [22] respectively reported that patterned SiNW 
sensors outperformed thin film sensors fabricated for NO2 and NH3 detection. A significant 
improvement in sensitivity was found for the NW devices, especially for the narrower one due to its 
larger surface-to-volume ratio. Consistent with these reports, the nano-patterned TONW sensors 
show better gas sensing properties than the planar TONW sensors and the nano-patterned gas 
sensors are fabricated by UV-NIL, which efficiently increases the surface-to-volume ratio to 
improve gas sensing performance.

奈米技術的進展，使得能夠在不同材料上，以奈米甚或以原子尺度之準確度製成各種奈米
結構的需求不斷增加。而各式各樣的奈米製作技術也因此相繼的被積極探討與開發。 
    在眾多發展中的下世代微影技術裡，電子束投影微影(EPL, electron-beam 
projection lithography)、極紫外光微影(EUV)及 X 光微影等技術都具有相當優異的解析
度足以應付未來的需要，但是這些技術除了有各自需要解決的問題之外，還有個共通的缺
點，那就是成本太高。 
    奈米壓印微影技術因其只要使用單次之微影步驟，即可在大面積的晶圓基板上，以單
一模具重複進行相同奈米圖樣轉移與奈米結構之製作，而被視為是在己公開可用以製作各
種奈米尺度極大型集成系統(ULSI Systems)技術中，具有低成本、高產比率的潛在優勢，
於未來有亮麗前景的一種技術。 
    奈米壓印之成型機制異於其他利用光子、電子束改變阻劑之化學結構來定義圖形的微
影技術，其解析度不會受限於光的繞射、散射等光學效應，而且省去了光源以及複雜光學
系統的昂貴費用，使其設備成本大幅降低，同時又能兼顧產率而適合量產。其應用的範圍
相當廣泛，包括奈米電子、光學元件、高密度儲存裝置、奈米電磁裝置、生物裝置、感測
器及奈米機電元件等之製作。
