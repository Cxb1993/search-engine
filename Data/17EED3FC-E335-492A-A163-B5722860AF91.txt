1前言
由於影響微生物燃料電池產電量之因子甚多，如何將這些可能影響因子納入考量並建立一套適合特定微生
物燃料電池之產電模式，將有利於對產電系統操作穩定性之控制。本研究乃以污水處理系統之應用為例，固定
某些影響因子，以進行其它影響因子與產電量模式或相關性之研究。另外，也設置一套去氮除磷污水處理系統，
以探討應用該處理系統之厭氧槽作為燃料電池陽極槽可行性。希望能建立具應用性之產電模式或成果，利用這
些成果得以有效控制系統之產電能力及污水處理效率，期能在污水系統處理污水之同時，能順便穩定產電。
因此，本專題研究以污水廠實際操作之可行性為考量，第一階段為探討質子交換膜面積與陽極之
連接斷面積比例及石墨碳纖電極板陰陽極距離與產電量之關係；第二階段乃在固定某些影響因子情況
下，以實驗模組測試其它各種可能影響因子之變化造成產電值之差異性，並取得較完整之數據，再以
統計方法或其它方法嘗試建立某些產電模式或相關性模式，以期能實際應用於污水廠；第三階段則嘗
試以 A2O 去氮除磷生物處理系統之實驗模組(Anoxic-Anaerobic-Aerobic)，並以厭氧槽作為陽極槽，以
探討其結合微生物燃料電池之可行性。
研究目的
本研究之目的如下：
(1)建立某些產電模式或相關性模式，期能應用於污水廠生物處理系統，以作為評估其生物燃料產電系
統設置可能性之參考依據。
(2)探討質子交換膜面積與陽極之連接斷面積比例與產電量之關係。
(3)石墨碳纖電極板陰陽極距離與產電量之關係。
(4)依據前述第(2)及(3)項，嘗試建立其相關性模式。
(5)配合 A2O 去氮除磷污水處理系統之實驗模組，探討其結合微生物燃料電池之可行性。
文獻探討
基本上，影響 MFC 產電效率之因子相當多，例如電極材料(Park and Zeikus, 2003)、電極的表面積
(Ghangrekar and Shinde, 2007)、陰陽極距離(Cheng et al., 2006)、質子交換膜(Liu and Logan, 2004; Min et
al., 2005; Liu and Li, 2007; Sun et al., 2009)、反應槽構造(Fan and Liu, 2007; Daniel et al., 2009)、pH (Gil et
al., 2003; Behera and Ghangrekar, 2009; Jiang et al., 2009)、溫度(Liu et al., 2005)、陽極槽介質(mediator)
種類(Park and Zeikus, 2000; Logan et al., 2005)、陰極電解質(Jiang et al., 2009)、基質強度、性質及負荷
(Liu et al., 2005a and 2005b; Jiang et al., 2009; Zhang et al., 2009; Mohan et al., 2010)、微生物(Rabaey, 2004;
Mathuriya and Sharma, 2009)等等。
一般微生物燃料電池通常包括有陽極槽及陰極槽，其間以特殊質子膜隔開兩槽(Min et al., 2005)；
微生物則在陽極槽內氧化分解基質燃料(電子供應者)而產生電子及質子，電子則經由電路傳輸至陰極
槽，而質子則經由特殊膜傳輸至陰極槽；然後在陰極槽內則因氧還原成水之還原反應而消耗掉電子及
質子(Gil et al., 2003)；換句話說，將細菌與電子接受者分開才可使電子傳至電極，此時也必須有等量之
質子傳至陰極(Logan and Regan, 2006)，此意謂著陽極與陰極的距離將會影響產電反應，Cheng et
al.(2006)之連續進流單槽式 MFC(流向由陽極流經陰極)研究結果即顯示陽極與陰極的距離愈短產電量
愈高，在其它相同操作條件下，陰陽極距離僅 2cm 者之最大功率密度約為距離僅 4cm 者之 2 倍。雖然
尚未得知是否有探討質子交換膜面積與水流斷面積比例與產電量關係之相關研究，然而由上述似乎可
推測質子交換膜與水流斷面積比例愈接近者理論上應該會有較高之產電量，此項乃為本專題研究計畫
3操作溫度控制在 22–25.2C，陰極溶氧控制在 6.23-7.25 mg/L 範圍，人造廢水採連續進流。因此，本階
段假設在固定之溫度範圍、陰陽極槽尺寸及容積、電極板尺寸及距離、質子交換膜材質及面積、陰極

MFC design and operational condition
Fig. 1 shows the schematic diagram of MFC. Two same-sized graphite carbon electrodes (CCM-400C; Central
Carbon Co., Ltd., Taiwan) were used for both anode and cathode (6.3 cm length4 cm width3 mm thickness). The
electrodes were fixed in the respective anodic and cathodic chambers by fully inserting them into the liquids in both
chambers. Both anodic and cathodic chambers were made of Plexiglas acrylic sheets, and each chamber had an effective
water volume of 797 cm3 (8.3 cm side water depth 9.8 cm width 9.8 cm length). All covers and connections were
sealed with silicone to achieve anaerobicity in the anode chamber during operation.
A peristaltic pump was used to pump a fixed amount of artificial wastewater into anodic chamber continuously. A
constant flow rate of 1.0 l day-1 was applied through this study. The inflow and outflow of anodic chamber were located
1 cm above the bottom and 1 cm below the top of the chamber, respectively. The effluent of the anodic chamber first
flowed into a water seal box, and then overflowed into an intermediate chamber so that anaerobicity was maintained in
the anode chamber. The liquid in the intermediate chamber was periodically discharged. Before fresh anodic chamber
effluent (overflow from the water seal box) is collected for analysis, the liquid in the intermediate chamber should be
discharged first. For oxygen supply to the electrochemical reaction, air was purged into cathodic chamber by an air
stone connected with a blower. The dissolved oxygen (DO) concentration was always maintained at 7.0–7.5 mg l-1. The
cathodic electrolyte used in the cathodic chamber was a mixture of 30.8 mM KH2PO4∙H2O solution (adjusted to pH ~7.0
by 1.0 N HNO3) and aerated water. Sludge mixing in the anodic chamber was performed using a stirrer, whereas mixing
in the cathodic chamber was only achieved through aeration. The mixing in anodic chamber was very slow only to
reduce the substrate gradient and to provide effective contact between the substrate and the sludge.
The anodic and cathodic chambers were separated by a PEM (Nafion 117, Dupont Co., USA). The membrane was
installed in a Plexiglas acrylic pipe connecting the anodic and cathodic chambers. The anode and the cathode were
connected externally with a concealed copper wire, and power production was measured at the external load resistance
of 1000 Ω adjusted using a resistor box. The distance between the anode and the cathode and the PEM surface area were
decided by the size and the length of Plexiglas acrylic pipe. In this study, distances between electrodes of 6, 10 and 18
cm were used for PEM surface area of 20.25 and 49.00 cm2. And, distances between electrodes of 15.5, 24 and 34 cm
were applied for PEM surface area of 1.77 cm2.
The MFC was operated at an ambient temperature of 24–26 C in our laboratory. The hydraulic retention time
(HRT) of anodic chamber was 13.3 h when the flow rate of 1.0 l/day was used. The synthetic wastewater (influent) and
the effluents from the anodic chamber were collected for analysis. The performance of MFC at each operational phase
was considered stable when both COD removal efficiency and power output are stable. For example, in the first phase
