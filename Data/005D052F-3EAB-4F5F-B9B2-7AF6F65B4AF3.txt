2.3.Gas permeability measurement  
Gas permeability was determined on a membrane cell equipped with a digital flow meter. The gas flow was 
converted into Barrer (10-10cm3cmcm-2s-1cm Hg-1) by taking into the account of effective membrane area, 
gas pressure difference, and film thickness. The ideal selectivity was defined as the ratio of two corresponding 
gas permeabilities. To model the gas permeability and selectivity of the composite as a function of zeolite 
loading, the Maxwell and modified Maxwell equations were used [1].

3. Results and Discussion 
3.1. Free volume property of PDMS-zeolite composites 
The pristine PDMS exhibited four positron and positronium (Ps) lifetimes. The first lifetime at 0.1 ns was due 
to p-Ps annihilation and the second at 0.4 ns originated from free positron annihilation. The longer lifetime 
components at 1.3 and 3.7 ns were due to polymer free volume. For zeolite powders, the longer lifetime 
components at 2.8 and 43.5 ns were associated with the voids between nano-particles (Fig. 1(a)). 
 
As zeolite was added into the PDMS matrix, the longer lifetimes slightly decreased with lower intensity than 
that of the pristine PDMS (Fig. 1(b)). The result is the decreases in fractional free volume in the composites: 
the fractional free volume decreased from 20.9% to 14.1% as the zeolite content increasing from 0 to 40%

3.3. Gas separation using PDMS and composite membranes  
The gas permeabilities of N2, CO2, and SF6 in PDMS membrane were determined. The permeability increased 
with upstream pressure for CO2, as shown in Fig. 5. That was due to the swelling phenomena caused by the 
higher CO2 solubility. It was shown that the CO2 solubility was 14-30 times higher than that of N2 in PDMS 
[2,3]. The polymer chain mobility and the free volume might have been increased from this effect and 
promoted gas transport. However, the solubilities of N2 and SF6 in PDMS were not high enough to initiate 
swelling and the permeabilities decreased with gas pressure (Fig. 5). The membrane compaction at high 
pressure might have caused less free volume available for N2 and SF6 permeation.  
 
The selectivity of each gas pair was calculated as a function of pressure as shown in Fig. 6. The SF6/N2 
selectivity was between 1.2 and 1.8. The results were consistent with literature value (1.2-1.5 [2,3]). The 
CO2/N2 selectivity was higher (2.6-9.7), supported by Hirayama et al. [3].

3.3. Mass transfer modeling  
The gas permeability and selectivity of PDMS with 0-40% ZSM zeolite loading were measured and estimated 
using Maxwell equation. The Maxwell equation overestimated the gas permeability and underestimated the 
gas pair selectivity (Fig. 11). The modified Maxwell equations were employed which takes into account the 
polymer rigidification around the zeolite and the partial pore blockage (Fig. 12). The modified equations 
yielded better permeability prediction compared with the experimental data for (Fig. 11). The separation 
selectivity was adequately estimated using the modified model (Fig. 13).
