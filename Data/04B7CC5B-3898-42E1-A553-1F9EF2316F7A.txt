ABSTRACT
Methyl tertiary butyl ether (MTBE) has emerged as the latest contaminants to
threaten the human health, quality of groundwater aquifers and drinking water
reservoirs. Due to the increasing incidence of MTBE in the environment and the
apparently low rates of MTBE natural attenuation, it is important to investigate the
effects of environmental factors on microbial communities dynamics in a laboratory
controlled conditions, for future application in the soil and groundwater
bioremediation.
Overall, the research project is to examine the MTBE removal efficiencies and
environmental factors in a laboratory-scale suspended growth bioreactor.
Additionally, evaluation the bacterial community dynamics during biodegradation in
MTBE contaminated groundwater is detected by PCR-Single Strand Conformation
Polymorphism (PCR-SSCP) based genetic profiles. The proposed research is to be
completed in two years. The objectives of the research are to investigate the
bioremediation capability in an MTBE- and BTEX-contaminated bioreactor, and to
evaluate substrate removal efficiencies under biostimulation and bioaugmentation
conditions. In addition, a relationship between removal efficiencies and
concentrations of nitrogen source and oxygen will be determined by using response
surface analysis. Exploring microbial community changes under various
environmental conditions (concentrations of nitrogen source and oxygen) by using
SSCP profiles of PCR-amplified 16S rDNA genes and linking the changes with
simultaneously diminishing substrate concentration in the microcosms are the goals of
this research.
Results of the research show that: (1) the removals for MTBE and BTEX
decrease in the order of ethylbenzene, p-xylene, toluene, benzene, and MTBE; (2)
additional 10–30 % removals for both MTBE and BTEX were observed via.

1第一章 前言
汽油中主要之含氧添加劑為醚類(MTBE、ETBE、TAME)與醇類，其中
以甲基第三丁基醚(Methyl tert-butyl ether，MTBE)之物化特性與汽油較相
似，因此最為石油公司所接受；同時，MTBE 具有高蒸氣壓，若加油站未設
有油氣回收裝置或加油槍使用方式不當時，則 MTBE 容易直接逸散於大氣
中，最後因濕沉降而進入地表水。另一方面，若儲油槽之滲漏而污染土壤時，
則 MTBE 易由未飽和之土壤層中擴散，進而傳輸至地下水層；且由於 MTBE
之溶解度極高(48,000 mg/L 於 20℃下)，於水體停留時間長，故淺層地下水中
之 MTBE 亦會隨著時間而進入深層地下水中，造成對地下水源極大的威脅。
此外，MTBE 在未攪拌之水溶液中溶解度大於苯與甲苯之 2~4 倍，而乙苯與
二甲苯則大於 7 倍以上，但在攪拌之水體中，則 MTBE 之溶解度則與 BTEX
相近，亦凸顯出 MTBE 之特性(林哲昌，2003)。近年來美國加州地區已陸續
發現加油站周邊區域之地下水體已遭受到 MTBE 之污染，因此美國政府已擬
定禁用添加 MTBE，以避免地下儲油槽之洩漏而影響地下水體，同時也針對
加油站地下儲油槽之設置與使用採更嚴格之檢查程序。反觀在台灣方面，依
據環保署於民國 90 至 91 年 4 月間抽樣調查全國有關汽油污染之地下水、自
來水或河川水水樣中得知，在 70 件樣本中有 27 件水樣遭受油品污染，其中
14 件為 MTBE 污染事件(環保署，2002)。此外，台灣地區之地下儲油槽總數
超過 7,000 座以上(僅以加油站油槽而言)，據估計超過半數以上之地下儲油槽
有洩漏之可能，而這些潛在危機將對地下水源構成極大的威脅。
有關 MTBE 健康影響方面，美國之相關研究發現 MTBE 將危害人體，
當人體暴露於高濃度 MTBE 中時，易引起噁心、嘔吐、頭暈與意識不清等症
狀，並對動物造成致癌性。Duffy et al. (1992) 研究 MTBE 之毒性動物試驗亦
有類似的結論；Joseph (1997) 發現自從美國賓州費城(Philadelphia, PA) 使用
含有 MTBE 之汽油後得到氣喘病之病患急速增加。由於諸多實驗數據證實
MTBE 不利於人類之健康，因此 MTBE 已被美國環保署暫列為可能之人類致
癌物質(USEPA,1996)。我國環保署目前已將 MTBE 列為疑似有毒性化學物質
之第四類，並於近期內研擬將其修正為有立即危害人體健康之第三類有毒性
化學物質，屆時管制 MTBE 的方式將更為嚴格。

3第二章 研究目的
本研究目的第一年乃在於探討模擬現地地下水中之單一環境因子對地下
水中微生物族群結構與消長狀態，同時亦比較其 MTBE 去除效率變化；單一
環境因子包括：(1)基質濃度、(2)複合碳源、(3)系統之環境 pH 值，及(4)系統
緩衝能力等，其試驗將先以批次瓶方式進行以得知單一環境因子對地下水中
微生物族群結構與消長狀態，同時亦比較 MTBE 去除效率。並於第二年研究
以受 MTBE 與 BTEX 污染之人工模擬實驗系統進行生物復育測試，評估進行
生物刺激及生物強化後對基質之分解效率，並利用統計學之回應曲面法(RSM)
建立各基質最佳去除效率之氮源與溶氧的操作條件，同時獲得回應曲面之模
型。故其研究目的為：
(1) 基質種類與濃度對 MTBE 之去除效率與菌群結構變化之影響；
(2) 不同 pH 條件下對 MTBE 之去除效率與菌群結構變化之關係；
(3) 複合碳源對 MTBE 之去除效率與菌群結構變化之影響；
(4) 金屬離子（Cu2+、Pb2+、Zn2+、Cr3+、Mn2+）種類與濃度對 MTBE 之去除
效率與菌群結構變化之影響；
(5) 探討由回應曲面法所求得於不同溶氧量及氮源量下，對生物復育槽處理
MTBE+BTEX 之去除效率；
(6) 探討添加 MTBE 與 BTEX 分解純菌株於生物復育槽內對去除效率及菌群
結構之影響；
(7) 以 PCR-SSCP 技術探討不同操作條件下之菌群結構變化及其生菌數消長
關係。
5則可從 PCR 產物中證實，即當每一種菌落群具有獨立之特徵表現，因此從獨
立特徵中，找尋特異之條帶(band)即可得到菌群間之差異變化點，並可由差
異點中來定量菌群群落數，且 SSCP 之分析方法是非常靈敏(可偵測低於 1.5%
之菌群群落結構)，因此由 16S rRNA 搭配 PCR 與 SSCP 技術，為一分析菌群
結構與菌群間之差異之最佳方式。
Foucher et al. (2003)以 16S rDNA 序列方式建構污泥餅中之微生物族群數
目，並採用 SSCP 分析技術與 PCR 擴大方法來求取批次搖瓶震盪培養與固定
生物膜培養間之菌群結構變化。由研究結果顯示，菌群結構會隨反應槽間之
溶氧增減而產生變動；若以反應器主體探討菌種變化時，則批次搖瓶震盪之
菌相異於固定式生物膜。
(3)地下水生物復育之相關研究
Chapman et al. (1997)在某一處曾受地下儲槽洩漏的區域，建立一模場試
驗，其中 BTEX 的平均濃度最高達 60 mg/L。場址中共設置 7 口的處理井及
36 口的監測井，處理井直徑為 20 cm，每口處理井內灌注 13.5 kg 的慢釋氧物
質(oxygen release compound, ORC)與砂的混合物。在處理 182 天後，處理井
周圍溶氧濃度介於 1.4~4.3 mg/L，採樣分析發現通過處理區域的地下水中的
BTEX 可達 70 %的去除率。
Park (1999)以連續回分式活性污泥法(sequencing batch reactor，SBR)處理
含 MTBE 廢水之生物降解研究，研究結果顯示，利用 SBR 系統馴養 MTBE
分解菌時，可以將難以分解之污染物質(MTBE)之微生物馴養成功。MTBE
於生物降解過程中會產生 TBA 等代謝產物，當與其他低濃度基質(BTEX)共
存下可將 MTBE 快速代謝完畢。且發現 SBR 操作條件在 HRT= 0.1~4 天，及
MTBE 濃度為 2~20 mg/L 時，為最佳之操作條件。
Zoeckler et al. (2003)採用好氧性微生物進行現地地下水之 MTBE 處理，其
結果顯示經過 58 天處理時程，MTBE 之濃度可由 2.7 mg/L 降至 0.28 mg/L。
Kao et al. (2003)研究以雙層生物牆處理受 PCE 污染之地下水層，結果顯
示，由實驗室模擬地下水污染所組裝之管柱狀反應器可將 24μg/L 之 PCE 去
除達 99 %。
(二) 國內研究情形
(1) MTBE生物分解之相關研究
高等人(2002)研究 MTBE 在好氧分解與共代謝條件下之生物降解情形，其
結果顯示，現地微生物可在好氧條件下，以 MTBE 為單一碳源及能量源進行
降解，而產生之降解副產物第三丁基醇(tert-butyl alcohol, TBA)亦可被現地好
氧微生物降解；若添加 BTEX 為替代碳源時，可增進 MTBE 之降解速率，且
7反應槽中之微生物菌群結構產生變化。
(3)地下水復育相關研究
陳(2001)研究結果顯示，在受 2,4-二氯酚污染場址之整治上，若場址為好
氧狀態，則場址中之微生物可在不添加額外碳源之情形下，以氯酚為主要基
質進行降解；若場址為厭氧狀態，則氯酚污染物之去除方式有二：(1)以加強
式厭氧生物復育技術，添加適當碳源如污泥餅，使氯酚在厭氧下以還原脫氯
反應去除；(2)以加強式好氧生物復育技術，提供場址充足之氧氣，使氯酚在
好氧下進行降解。
莊等人(2001)研究受多環芳香族化合物(Polycyclic Aromatic Hydrocarbons,
PAHs)污染場址中的微生物相，探討污染場址中氧氣含量對於 PAHs 於各馴化
菌株進行生物降解過程之影響性。研究結果顯示，受 PAHs 污染場址進行生
物復育整治工作時，污染場址的氧氣供應量及方式(例如，添加慢性釋氧物
質、灌入純氧及加入雙氧水等)可能為影響生物復育成效的關鍵因素。
吳(2002)配合現地的狀況以空氣壓縮機產生空氣，並以 40 L/min 的流量直
接注入受石油污染地下水之場址中，以增加其溶氧量(由 0.2 mg/L 增至 1.15
mg/L)來促進微生物的活性進而增加菌落數(總菌落數由 102 CFU/mL 增至 107
CFU/mL)，且於實施生物曝氣法(biosparging)後，對地下水監測井中之各種污
染物(例如，苯、甲苯、乙基苯、二甲苯等)之去除率皆達 60 %以上，同時 BTEX
之濃度約由 75.5 mg/L 皆降至到 25.5 mg/L 以下。
9一、微生物之總體 DNA萃取方法
微生物樣本 DNA 來源取自於各試程中採樣所得之水樣，並於-20 ℃之環
境下進行菌體保存。待欲以分子生物技術進行分析時，將先前所保存之菌體
再以改良式玻璃珠抽取法(bead-beating system)(黃，2003)，進行菌體之總基因
體 DNA (genome DNA)萃取。所萃取之菌體總基因體 DNA，即為 PCR 鏈鎖
反應中所使用之模版 DNA (template DNA, tDNA)，並經萃取後所得 tDNA 濃
度範圍約為100 ng/μl～200 ng/μl間，為使 PCR 反應時 tDNA 能充分進行放
大，因此需將 tDNA 濃度稀釋到50 ng/μl範圍內再行 PCR 反應。
二、聚合酶鏈鎖反應
聚合酶鏈鎖反應(PCR)為一種大量增殖某一特定目標 DNA 序列之方法，
其特異性在於所選用之寡核苷酸引子(primer)會與 DNA 相對股上之互補序列
進行雜交進而放大其 DNA 序列。本研究所使用之寡核苷酸引子(primer)序列
分別選自於大腸桿菌(E. coli.) 16S rRNA 中 V3 變異區(variable V3 region)之上
下游保守序列，此對引子經由 PCR 放大後所得之片段相當於 16S rRNA 中第
334 至 514 鹼基部分，並可經由定序方式與比對核酸序列資料庫(GenBank)
後，確定絕大部分細菌之屬名或種名。聚合酶鏈鎖反應中所使用之溫度程式
則先分別以 100 ℃加熱 10 分鐘進行雙股 DNA 變性(denature)成單股 DNA
後，再進行一連串之鏈合(annealing)、延伸(extension)動作，反應完成後之 PCR
產物再以 4℃環境下冷藏保存備用。
三、單股 DNA構形多型性分析步驟
單股 DNA 構形多型性(SSCP)分析技術係修改自 Schwieger & Tebbe
(1998)與 Lee et al. (1996)之報告。SSCP 分析過程中所使用凝膠聚合物為丙烯
醯胺(acrylamide)與雙丙烯醯胺(N,N'-methylene-bis acrylamide)所共同凝聚合
成之聚丙烯醯胺膠體，聚合過程中需配合添加過硫酸銨(ammonium persulfate,
APS)與四甲基乙二胺(tetramethyl ethylene diamine, TEMED)以進行自由基產
生與催化反應，而聚丙烯醯胺凝膠主要之特性在於不需添加任何變性劑下即
可進行凝膠電泳且具重複性，故又可稱為非變性凝膠電泳。實驗中所使用凝
膠濃度均為 8 % acrylamide/Bis；電泳跑膠時間為 100 分鐘，電壓設定為 300
V，並同時控制循環水溫度於 4 ℃下進行電泳。電泳中所使用的 PCR 產物均
與等體積變性劑(loading dye, 95 % formamide, 10mM NaOH, 0.02 % bromophenol
blue, 0.02 % xylene cyanol, 20 mM EDTA)混合均勻後，於 100 ℃下進行 10 分鐘
加熱變性動作，最後再將樣本置於冰盒中冷卻。電泳完畢後之凝膠則以硝酸
銀(AgNO3)進行染色，其染色之原理係利用銀離子與 DNA 作用之特性，使銀
離子與電泳膠上的 DNA 片段結合後，將銀離子還原並使銀離子顯色，即可
得到硝酸銀染色後之電泳膠片，並可藉以判讀 DNA 圖譜。最後再將硝酸銀
染色後之膠片以玻璃紙方式進行自然風乾後保存(Bassam et al., 1991)。

六、MTBE biodegradation and degrader microbial community dynamics in
MTBE, BTEX, and heavy metal contaminated water(詳如附錄六所示)
Exploring microbial community changes under various environmental
groundwater conditions (single substrate, mixed substrates, and the presence of
heavy metals) and linking the changes with simultaneously diminishing substrate
concentration in the microcosms are the goals of this research. Most
microorganisms from environmental microcosms or wastewater treatment plants
cannot be cultivated artificially. Capturing microbial community fingerprints
therefore requires applying a molecular biological technique. By using SSCP
profiles of PCR-amplified 16S rDNA genes, it was demonstrated that, with the
repeated addition of substrates during long-term acclimation, substrate-utilizing
populations in a microcosm gradually increased to become the dominant
constituent. Conversely, the presence of metals inhibited community
development and differentiation. It was also shown that substrate degradation
rates increased under co-substrate conditions, with substrate-degraders easily
adapting to the environment. They become dominant bacteria, a phenomenon
attributed to the propensity of the fittest species to outgrow their competitors
when presented with suitable substrates.

前言
汽油中含氧添加劑種類常包括有
甲基第三丁基醚(methyl tert-butyl
ether, MTBE)、乙基第三丁基醚
(ethyl tert-butyl ether, ETBE)及第
三戊基甲基醚 (tert-amyl methyl
ether, TAME)等，其主要功用在於
提高汽油中之辛烷值與含氧量，以
促進引擎完全燃燒而減低一氧化
碳(CO)之排放；此外汽油中其他主
要成分則包括苯(Benzene, B)、甲苯
(Toluene, T)、乙苯(Ethylbenzene, E)
及二甲苯(Xylenes, X)所構成之芳
香烴化合物 (BTEX)及其他烯烴
(Olefins)類化合物等。因此，若儲
存於地下貯油槽或輸油管線中所
含之含氧添加劑(ETBE、MTBE、
TAME)或芳香烴化合物(BTEX)造
成之洩
漏、逸散等，將直接或間接污染土
壤、空氣或隨雨水流佈而擴及整體
地下水。故本研究將針對汽油中多
重基質添加劑下對菌種之降解影
響，亦即探討含氧添加劑與芳香烴
化合物在單一基質與複合基質及
多重基質添加下，對生物之分解特
性與菌群之結構分佈。

前言
傳統菌群結構分析方式多數以電子顯
微鏡或光學顯微鏡方式觀察菌體外觀
結構變化而可初步得知菌體之群屬(桿
菌、球菌或鏈球菌…等)或利用平版培
養與試管培養方式來觀察菌落之外觀
特徵(圓、凸、濁度…) 等差異來獲取菌
群；除此之外亦可以微生物之生理生化
反 應 來 獲 知 菌 群 種 類 ， 如 以
API(analytical profile index)試管鑑定方
法等亦可鑑定菌群之種屬名， 但上述
方式常需花費較多時間且受限於可培
養之菌種，生化特性相近之種類亦不易
區分。因此近年來多數學者以生子生物
技術之方式進行菌群結構分析，而主要
之分析技術則包括有：變性梯度凝膠電
泳 ( d e n a t u r i n g g r a d i e n t g e l
electrophoresis, DGGE)、熱梯度電泳凝
膠(thermal gradient gel electrophoresis,
TGGE)、單股 DNA 構型多形性(single
strand conformation polymorphism,
S S C P ) 及 螢 光 原 位 雜 交 法
(florescence in situ hybridization, FISH)
等，這些分析技術雖皆大同小異， 但
以 SSCP 所需花費較少，且具有重複性
及靈敏度佳等特性，故本研究乃採取此
分析技術以分析其菌群結構。
在菌群結構分析方法上，依據
Almeida et al.[1]、Lee et al.[4]及 Schwieger
& Tebbe[5]研究結果顯示，以 SSCP 技術
分析菌群結構時，亦可搭配聚合酶鏈鎖
反應(PCR)之優點來放大菌群之特定序
列。在進行 SSCP 分析中所得到菌體總
DNA 樣本時，可經由加熱變性處理後
得到兩股單股 DNA(正負股)，而所得兩
股單股 DNA (dsDNA)亦可經由核酸外
切酶作用切除正負兩股中之其中一
股，故於 SSCP 圖譜上所呈現之色帶
(band)即可代表為一種菌群，進而獲取
初步之菌群種類分佈情形。此外，在
SSCP 圖譜分析過程中亦可以額外使用
添加引子方式進行，使其單股構型所形
成之正負股其中一股以跳躍式之方式
拉開兩股間之距離而加以區別[7]。

Abstract
The influence of zinc, manganese, and nickel on the degradation of MTBE
(methyl tert-butyl ether), and BTEX (benzene, toluene, ethylbenzene, and xylene)
and MTBE degradation by an aerobic MTBE-degrading strain, Pseudomonas
aeruginosa, were investigated. The maximum degradation rate for
Pseudomonas aeruginosa to degrade MTBE and individual BTEX followed the
order of MTBE > benzene > toluene > xylene > ethylbenzene. The result
showed that unlike previous findings, P. aeruginosa was able to degrade MTBE
through direct metabolism when MTBE was present as the only carbon source.
The degradation rate of MTBE was rapid, completed within 80 hrs. MTBE was
more easily degraded by P. aeruginosa than BTEX. MTBE biodegradation by
this strain was stimulated at low concentrations of Zn2+ (1–10 mg l-1) and Mn2+
(1–10 mg l-1) but inhibited at high concentrations of Zn2+ (20 mg l-1), Mn2+(20 mg
l-1), and Ni2+ (1–4 mg l-1). Kinetic parameters for BTEX and MTBE degradation
in the presence or absence of metals by the strain were obtained through nonlinear
parameter regression and a least-square minimization procedure. In all cases, a
good agreement was achieved between kinetic simulations and experimental
results.

2. Materials and methods
2.1. Bacterial strain and culture condition
An aerobic MTBE-degrading strain, Pseudomonas aeruginosa, was purified
from a mixed culture obtained from a petrochemical wastewater treatment plant in
Southern Taiwan and acclimated subsequently in a 1.5 liters bioreactor fed with
MTBE as a sole carbon source for a period of time. The composition of mineral
salt medium used in this study was as described elsewhere (Chiu et al., 2006).
After a series of screening and isolation procedures, a pure culture was identified
as P. aeruginosa through comparing sequences extracted to the GenBank database
by use of the BLAST facility of the National Center for Biotechnology
Information (NCBI) (http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/BLAST/). The pure strain
was inoculated to 250-ml screw cap amber glass bottles containing 100 ml of a
mineral salts medium and moderate MTBE, sealed with Teflon Mininert valves
for incubation at 30 ℃ under constant shaking at 150 rpm. After the incubation,
the pure P. aeruginosa was kept frozen at -70 ℃ in mineral salts medium
containing 50% glycerol (v/v).
2.2. Batch experiments
MTBE and BTEX biodegradation rates were determined using batch
incubation studies with or without the addition of metal ions (Zn2+, Mn2+, and
Ni2+). Desired concentrations of metal solutions required were obtained by serial
dilutions of concentrated stock solutions of the heavy metals (1000 mg l-1; Merck)
with distilled water. Studies were carried out in 250-ml serum bottles containing
100 ml of mineral salts medium or mineral salts medium spiked with the metals
and appropriate concentrations of MTBE or BTEX by a gas-tight syringe. The
headspace of each bottle was added with equal volume pure oxygen to prevent
oxygen from becoming a limiting factor. The bottles were incubated in the dark
at 30 ℃ shaken at 150 rpm. The variation of biomass and substrate
concentrations within each bottle was monitored periodically. Media and
materials were all sterilized and blank controls were included in all experiments.

give a normalized index for evaluating the degradation efficiency of each

3. Results and discussion
3.1. Degradation of MTBE and individual BTEX components
The pure strain, Pseudomonas aeruginosa, isolated from the mixed culture
acclimated in a 1.5 L bioreactor fed with MTBE as sole carbon source was
capable of degrading MTBE and BTEX. P. aeruginosa was unique as the strain
could degrade MTBE directly when MTBE was a sole carbon source. The result
was contrary to the findings of Garnier et al. (1999), Dupasquier et al. (2002), and
Morales et al. (2004) which indicated that P. aeruginosa could degrade MTBE
only through cometabolism with other substrates.
Figs 1a and 1b illustrate MTBE and BTEX degradation rates and biomass
productivity rates for P. aeruginosa under various substrates concentrations. The
degradation rates of MTBE, ethylbenzene, and xylene, as well as the biomass
productivity rates increased with increasing substrate concentrations. When
increasing the concentrations of MTBE (28.8 to 305.6 mg l-1), ethylbenzene (14.2
to 66.1 mg l-1), and xylene (7.1 to 40.3 mg l-1), the biomass productivity rate of P.
aeruginosa increased from 0.06 to 2.34 mg l-1 hr-1, 0.02 to 0.10 mg l-1 hr-1, and
0.02 to 0.15 mg l-1 hr-1, respectively. Similar results were also found in the
relationships between MTBE, ethylbenzene, and xylene concentrations and their
biomass productivity rates. This behavior indicates that within the
concentrations of these three substrates, the substrates did not exhibit inhibitory
effect on P. aeruginosa activity and biomass was the rate-determining factor.
Increasing the concentrations of the substrates increased the impact frequency of
substrates and biomass, and the biodegradation rates of the substrates and their
biomass productivity rates.
Unlike MTBE, ethylbenzene, and xylene, the degradation rates of benzene
(Fig. 1b) decreased with increasing benzene concentrations. This result reveals
that the benzene concentrations used in this study were toxic to P. aeruginosa.
The association of degradation rates of toluene with the biomass productivity rate
(Fig. 1b), was also found. The degradation profile of toluene is apparently
different from those of MTBE and BEX as it shows a two-phase pattern.
Toluene degradation rate increased up to a toluene concentration of 17.3 mg l-1,
whereas it decreased with toluene concentrations higher than 17.3 mg l-1.
Comparing the maximum degradation rate among these substrates, their
degradability by P. aeruginosa followed the order of MTBE > benzene > toluene
> xylene > ethylbenzene. The result for this strain contradicts the finding by

Table 1 summarizes the kinetic parameter evaluation results for BTEX ,
MTBE , and MTBE degradation in the presence of the metals used. The values
ofμm for MTBE degradation by P. aeruginosa were higher in the presence of the
metals than in the absence of the metals except for those with the presence Ni2+ at
concentrations of 3 and 4 mg l-1. The introduction of metals at low
concentrations (Zn2+, Mn2+, and Ni2+) could stimulate the growth of the strain and
consequently improve the degradation rate of MTBE. The stimulation was
turned into inhibition when the concentration of was for Zn2+ (20 mg l-1), Mn2+
(20 mg l-1) and Ni2+ (1-40 mg l-1) was high. The phenomenon may be
interpreted by the values of Ks.
As can be seen from Table 1, the Ks values for MTBE degradation with
addition of metal were higher than without. While the value of Ks could be an
index of the substrate affinity to cells, the increasing value of Ks with the addition
of Zn2+, Mn2+, and Ni2+ reflected decreasing substrate binding to cell. It is
suggested that the decrease in MTBE degradation rate with the addition of
elevated concentration of Zn2+ (20 mg l-1), Mn2+ (20 mg l-1), and Ni2+ (1–4 mg l-1)
resulted from the adsorption of these metals into bacteria cells, or the precipitation
of the metals on the cell surfaces, thereby modifying the active-sites for MTBE
metabolism. Also, as theμm is a function of X measured gravimetrically, the
higher values ofμm shown in the presence of low concentrations of Zn2+ and Mn2+
(1–10 mg l-1) may actually be due to the stimulation in biomass growth. Thus, it
may be concluded that the stimulatory effect of metals at moderate concentrations
on MTBE biodegradation was primarily contributed from the improvement in cell
growth and enzyme synthesis, whereas the inhibitory effect was attributed to the
decrease in substrate affinity.
The values of a (Table 1) for Zn2+, Mn2+, and Ni2+ were 0.26 to 1.86±0.09,
0.29 to 2.55±0.09, and 0.24 to 2.11±0.40, respectively. They were lower than
the minimal inhibitory concentrations observed in this study. It is possible that
the medium may contain certain metal-bind or metal-precipitating matters that can
reduce the solution-phase metal concentration in medium. Therefore, the
amount of metal ion concentrations available to affect the biodegradation of
MTBE by P. aeruginosa is lower than the amount added. Similar result also
could be found in the literatures reported by Hughes and Poole (1991), and
Poulson et al. (1997).
Fig. 3 shows the time courses of BTEX and MTBE degradation by P.
aeruginosa with or without additional metal introduction. As can be shown in
this picture, model predictions all fit very well to the experimental data. This
indicates that the parameters estimated and the models selected in this study could
be successfully applied to predict BTEX and MTBE biodegradation by P.
aeruginosa.
4. Conclusions
Pseudomonas aeruginosa isolated in our laboratory have demonstrated its
ability to degrade MTBE through direct metabolism, in contrast to the early
findings that showed the degradation of MTBE by co-metabolism only. The
maximum degradation rate for Pseudomonas aeruginosa to degrade MTBE and
individual BTEX in order was: MTBE > benzene > toluene > xylene >
ethylbenzene. In the presence of low concentrations of Zn2+ and Mn2+ (1–10 mg
l-1), MTBE degradation rate was stimulated. High concentrations of Zn2+ (20 mg
l-1), Mn2+ (20 mg l-1), and Ni2+ (1 to 4 mg l-1) inhibited the degradation of MTBE.

Abstract
Methyl tertiary butyl ether (MTBE) is commonly used as fuel oxygenates to
improve gasoline combustion but MTBE is also found to cause soil and ground
water pollution and influences the health of the human body indirectly. In the
microcosms of the natural environment or the wastewater treatment plant, most
microorganisms are unable to be cultivated artificially. To capture the
fingerprints of the microbial community, it’s necessary to apply the molecular
biological technique. The purpose of this research is to explore the change of the
microbial community under various environmental conditions in the groundwater,
and to compare with its remaining concentration of MTBE in the microcosms
simultaneously. Environmental factors include concentration of substrate, and
mixed substrates. The result of study shows: (1) Some substrate was degraded
rapidly in a co-substrate condition, indicating the substrate-degraders were easy to
adapt environment and became dominated bacteria. (2) If mixed cultures
contained a known pure culture, the specific strain can be determined via a
jumping position of SSCP gel by addition of a primer in mixed cultures. (3) By
using the analysis of single-strand-conformation polymorphism (SSCP) profiles
of PCR-amplified 16S rDNA genes fingerprint, the microcosms were found to
change gradually to the dominated microbes of MTBE-degraders under repeated
addition of MTBE during the long-term acclimation. (4) MTBE-degraders have
been detected from the mixed cultures using the analysis of
single-strand-conformation polymorphism (SSCP) profiles of PCR-amplified 16S
rDNA genes.

2. Materials and Methods
2.1 Bacterial strains and batch microcosm experiments
The bacterial cultures used in this study were obtained from a petrochemical
wastewater treatment plant. They were initially grown in a liquid medium and
were adapted to the targeted compounds and working conditions before the
biodegradation experiments. After a series of screening and isolating procedures,
four pure cultures were identified as Ralstonia sp. (YABE411), Pseudomonas sp.
(YATO411), Pseudomonas sp. (YAET411), and Pseudomonas aeruginosa
(YAMT521). The microorganism was grown at pH 6.8±1 in a mineral salt
medium as described elsewhere (Lin and Li, 2005). Media and materials were
sterilized by autoclaving at 121℃ for 20 minutes. The media were then
inoculated under sterile conditions. The inoculated media were incubated at
30±1 ℃ in a thermostat rotary-shaker for future use. The pure culture was
provided as a mark in some bacterial community study.
During the experiments, screw cap amber glass bottles of 250 ml volume,
each containing 100 ml of an autoclaved phosphate-buffered mineral salts solution,
sealed with Teflon Mininert valves were kept in the dark under constant shaking
at 150 rpm and a temperature of 30±1 ℃. A stainless steel needle fitted to a
gas-tight syringe was used to inject MTBE, and the bottles were then re-sealed
and shaken for 12 hours. This procedure ensured complete dissolution of the
MTBE and equilibrated the headspace with the solution. Subsequently, the
inoculum of mixed cultures was then introduced using the same final biomass
concentration in each bottle. Substrates (MTBE, ETBE, TAME, BTEX) were
added as needed to provide a determining concentration in the bottles. Test
cultures and uninoculated control cultures were prepared in triplicate.

liquid phase than discussing intrinsic biological reaction rates. Therefore, use of
gas-phase measurements for biodegradation experiments satisfactorily captures
the substrate concentrations in the liquid. In all biodegradation experiments, gas

3. Results and Discussion
3.1 Effects of acclimation periods on microbial community
Figure 3 shows that after a 30-day acclimation period, different substrate
types resulted in different microbial community structures. Lanes L1, L2, and L3
in Figure 3A represent communities developed in bi-substrate cultures of MTBE +
toluene, MTBE + ethylbenzene, and MTBE + benzene, respectively. The
numbers of bands in these profiles are similar to each other, indicating similar
species compositions in the various environments. However, the substrate
removal efficiency decreases in the order of MTBE + toluene > MTBE +
ethylbanzene > MTBE + benzene. The community grown with MTBE and
toluene also generated the most intense bands, followed by the culture with
MTBE and benzene. Removal efficiency is therefore a good indication of the
quality of the substrate in supporting the growth of dominant microbial species, as
revealed by the increases in band intensity.
3.2 Effects of mixed substrates on microbial community
Growth in bi-substrate systems of MTBE and m-xylene, p-xylene or
o-xylene resulted in similar community structures. In addition, communities in
xylene-containing cultures tend to generate greater numbers of bands than
benzene-containing cultures. Bands from the former also tend to appear in
greater intensities (Fig. 3-B, lanes L1, L2, and L3). Therefore, communities
from xylene-containing cultures can be placed in one group (Fig. 3-B, the dashed
box on lanes L4, L5, and L6) based on their structural similarity, and those from
benzene-containing cultures in another (Fig. 3-B, box on lanes L4, L5, and L6).
Figure 3-(b) shows the similarity indices and clustering relationships between
these communities. Communities from xylene- and benzene-containg cultures
again fall into two separate groups with similarities within each group as high as
0.88.

frequently generate communities with simpler structures, probably by favoring the
growth of a few dominant species. In Figure 4, the appearances of bands a and b

2. Materials and Methods
2.1 Bacterial strains and batch microcosm experiments
The bacterial cultures used in this study were obtained from a petrochemical
wastewater treatment plant. They were initially grown in a liquid medium and
were adapted to the targeted compounds and working conditions before the
biodegradation experiments. After a series of screening and isolating procedures,
four pure cultures were identified as Ralstonia sp. (YABE411), Pseudomonas sp.
(YATO411), Pseudomonas sp. (YAET411), and Pseudomonas aeruginosa
(YAMT521). The microorganism was grown at pH 6.8±1 in a mineral salt
medium as described elsewhere (Lin and Li, 2005). Media and materials were
sterilized by autoclaving at 121℃ for 20 minutes. The media were then
inoculated under sterile conditions. The inoculated media were incubated at
30±1 ℃ in a thermostat rotary-shaker for future use.
During the experiments, screw cap amber glass bottles of 250 ml volume,
each containing 100 ml of an autoclaved phosphate-buffered mineral salts solution,
sealed with Teflon Mininert valves were kept in the dark under constant shaking
at 150 rpm and a temperature of 30±1 ℃. A stainless steel needle fitted to a
gas-tight syringe was used to inject MTBE, and the bottles were then re-sealed
and shaken for 12 hours. This procedure ensured complete dissolution of the
MTBE and equilibrated the headspace with the solution. Subsequently, the
inoculum was then introduced using the same final biomass concentration in each
bottle. Substrates (MTBE, ETBE, TAME, BTEX) and medal ions (Al3+, Mn2+,
Zn2+) were added as needed to provide a determining concentration in the bottles.
Test cultures, control cultures with added metals, and uninoculated control
cultures were prepared in triplicate.
2.2 Analytic methods
Because MTBE is a volatile organic compound (VOC), mass transfer rates
between the gas and aqueous phases of MTBE are rapid, and gas concentrations
approach equilibrium with the liquid phase. Changes in liquid phase
concentrations are slow compared to the rate of mass transfer between gas and
liquid phase than discussing intrinsic biological reaction rates. Therefore, use of
gas-phase measurements for biodegradation experiments satisfactorily captures
the substrate concentrations in the liquid. In all biodegradation experiments, gas
samples were periodically collected from the headspace of each bottle to monitor

3. Results and Discussion
3.1 MTBE removal in the presence of metal ions
Fig. 1 summarizes the effects of Al3+, Mn2+, and Zn2+ on MTBE degradation
with metal concentrations of 5 and 10 mg l-1. In all cases the MTBE removal
rates were higher without metal ions in the solution. The inhibitory effect of
adding metals was obvious, with the continuously decreasing value of MTBE
degradation rate in the order of Zn2+, Al3+, and Mn2+. Fig. 1 shows that there
were no concentrations of Al3+, Mn2+, and Zn2+ that caused any stimulating effect.
This result is in agreement with previous report by Amor et al. (2001), who
showed that microorganisms exhibit various growth rates and degradation rates in
the presence of heavy metal with different types and various concentrations. As
also can be seen in the figure, zinc exhibited the highest toxicity. This finding
was inconsistent with previous results, showing that Bacillus thuringensis was not
affected negatively by Zn2+ concentration at 10.98 mg l-1 (Hassen., 1998).
3.2 Microbial community structure in the presence of metal ions
Figure 2 shows the SSCP profiles, similarity indices, and the result of cluster
analysis of communities under the influences of various metal ions. Figure 2(b)
indicates that communities grown in the presence and absence of metal ions bear a
similarity of 0.68, revealing a change in community structure due to the ions.
This result agrees with the study of Jacek (2001) which demonstrated the
differences in microbial community structure between a heavy
metal-contaminated site and a non-contaminated one.
In the presence of Mn, Al or Zn ion, community structures can be divided
into two groups based on ion concentrations. At the concentration of 5 mg l-1,
the similarity between Al- and Zn-treated communities was 0.88. The similarity
dropped to 0.83 at the concentration of 10 mg l-1. Similar results were observed
for Mn-treated communities. These results indicate that metal ions negatively
affect the structure similarity between communities. Our findings are consistent
with those of Jacek (2000) who studied the changes in microbial communities
facing different concentrations of heavy metal ions. In this study the author
found that communities from sites contaminated with high concentrations of
heavy metal (1380 mg kg-1 of Pb, 23.3 mg kg-1 of Cd, and 2390 mg kg-1 of Zn)
was much lower in species diversity than communities from less-contaminated
sites (160 mg kg-1 of Pb, 4.4 mg kg-1 of Cd, and 330 mg kg-1 of Zn).
