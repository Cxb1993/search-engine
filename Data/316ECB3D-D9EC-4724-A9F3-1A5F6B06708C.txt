英文摘要 
In the project, the surface-conduction electron-emitter (SCE) was fabricated with e-beam lithography and 
photography on Eagle2000 substrate. The pattern of SCE was an equilateral triangle. The SCE used three apex 
angles to emit electrons to the anodes and produce secondary electrons from the anodes to the upper electrode at the 
same time. The basic material of the cathode and anode was carbon nanotube (CNT). Before the CNT formation, the Al 
and Fe films were deposited on the Eagle2000 substrate by e-beam evaporation. Subsequently, the Fe/Al/Eagle2000 
substrate was introduced into a thermal chemical vapor deposition (CVD) system to grow the vertically aligned CNTs. 
To enhance the field emission characteristics of the cathode, we deposited ZnO and RuO2 nanostructures onto the 
CNTs by thermal CVD and metal-organic CVD, respectively. The threshold electric field could be lowered and 
the stability of field emission could be improved. As for the growth of CNTs, we successfully grow the vertically 
aligned CNTs on Eagle2000 substrates. By analytical equipments, the vertically aligned CNTs had a high number 
density and high crystallinity. From the SCE measurements, it had a relatively higher SCE current for the ZnO-coated 
CNTs. On the other hands, the RuO2-coated CNTs had a comparatively lower threshold electric field and a more stable 
SCE current. The results of the SCE project can provide a further promising application of SCE. 
  
Keyword: Surface-conduction electron-emitter display, Carbon nanotube, Secondary electron, Field emission, 
Thermal chemical vapor deposition, lithography 
 
 
Acetylene (C2H2) gas as the carbon source was introduced into the thermal CVD system under a pressure of 
about 266.6 Pa. The growth time for the CNT formation was 3 min. 
To enhance the amount of electrons emitted from the SCE, ZnO and RuO2 nanostructures were coated 
onto the surface of CNTs.  
(1) ZnO nanostructures formation 
   After CNT synthesis, the CNT/Eagle2000 samples were placed into another thermal CVD to growth 
the ZnO nanostructures. The Zn source was Zn(CH3COO)2•2H2O (700 mg). The furnace was ramped up 
to the annealing temperature at a ramp rate of about 30°C/min, while the base pressure was kept to about 
1333 Pa. Air was introduced into the thermal CVD system. The ZnO nanostructures growth temperature 
was 400°C and the growth time was 60 min. 
(2) RuO2 nanostructures formation 
The CNT/Eagle2000 samples were transferred into a metal organic CVD (MOCVD) system for RuO2 
nanostructures growth. Bis(ethylcyclopentadienyl)ruthenium(II), Ru[(C2H5)C5H4]2, was used as the Ru 
source and was carried by O2 sprayed onto the CNT/Eagle2000 samples under a pressure of about 200 Pa 
at a growth temperature of 550°C for 60 min.  
Both the thermal CVD and MOCVD systems spontaneously cooled down to room temperature.   
The surface morphology and internal structure of the synthesized samples were observed by scanning 
electron microscopy (SEM) and transmission electron microscopy (TEM), respectively. The microstructural 
information of CNTs and RuO2 nanostructures was conducted by Raman spectroscopy with an Ar laser 
exhibiting an excitation wavelength of 514.5 nm. Photoluminescence (PL) analysis was used to observe the 
composition of the ZnO nanostructures with a 325 nm He-Cd laser as the excitation source. The field 
emission measurement apparatus consisted of parallel plates, with the ZnO nanostructures coated CNTs and 
RuO2 nanostructures coated CNTs as the cathode and a stainless plate as the anode, which were separated by 
125 μm. The secondary electron emission (SEE) measurement was conducted in a high-vacuum system with a 
pressure of about 10-5 Pa. The SEE measurement apparatus consisted of parallel plates, which were separated 
by 125 μm. The schematic illustration and the photo were shown in Fig. 2. 
 
結果與討論 
The CNTs were grown with the design patterns on Eagle2000 substrates. Figures 3(a) and 3(b) show the 
SEM images of the CNTs SCE patterns formed by e-beam lithography and photolithography, respectively. All 
the synthesized patterns were accorded with the designs of e-beam lithography and photolithography. The 
synthesized CNTs were vertically aligned to the Eagle2000 substrates. The insets of Figs. 3(a) and 3(b) were 
the enlarged cross-sectional SEM images. The number densities of all the samples were approximately 109 
cm-2. The vertically aligned CNTs with high number density could be regarded as a single field emitter [10,11]. 
Figure 4 shows the TEM image of the synthesized vertically aligned CNT. The diameter of the vertically 
aligned CNT was about 15 nm, and the vertically aligned CNT had a hollow and multi-wall structure, 
indicating that the synthesized vertically aligned CNT exhibited the metallic properties. In addition, a Fe 
nano-particle was observed at the tip of the vertically aligned CNT, indicating that the CNTs growth occurred 
by the tip growth mode [12]. To enhance the field emission and secondary electron emission, the ZnO and 
RuO2 nanostructures were deposited onto the vertically aligned CNTs. Figures 5(a) and 5(b) show the TEM 
images of ZnO and RuO2 nanostructures uniformly coated onto the vertically aligned CNTs, respectively. The 
shape of ZnO nanostructures was taper-like and the size was about 20 nm. On the other hand, the RuO2 
nanostructures were uniformly coated onto the CNT. Some RuO2 nanostructures appeared on the RuO2 film. 
The thickness of the RuO2 nanostructures was about 20 nm.

二、與會心得 
  本次會議有很多關於石墨烯製作與應用之討論，與各國學者互相交流學術議見
與實驗心得，使本人學習到許多石墨烯新的製程方式與應用，並對於本實驗室在研
究石墨烯方面有很多的幫助與建議。此外，藉由會議討論也認識許多國外學者，互
相學習與交流以達到提升語言能力與專業知識之訓練。
