此，局部放電的檢測和評估即成為高壓設備絕緣狀況監測的一種重要手段。 
過去局部放電檢測技術受限於檢測儀器無法量化等問題，因此一直無法成為發展的重
心，直到學者Barry H. Ward[2]在1992年利用電腦進行量化，加上隨著電腦科技的進步，使
量化過程與儲存的局部放電檢測技術都更上一層。至今在量測的相關訊息中，以局部放電
圖譜最具參考價值，其相關訊息需要儲存圖譜為資料庫，但儲存圖譜所需的容量過大，導
致無法有效的儲存資料，因此發展出萃取特徵的方式。常被引用萃取特徵的方式為：統計
運算、分形維數、韋伯函數等，然後利用萃取特徵的方式進一步結合辨識系統。常見的辨
識方法中以模糊辨識、類神經網路(Artificial Neural Network)與機率分類法等做為辨識系
統，隨後局部放電圖譜辨識的自動化就此展開。 
本研究運用脈衝電流法做為老化實驗之檢測方法，並且使用具部份瑕疵但仍合乎正常
使用標準及IEC局部放電限制規範之XLPE電力電纜為樣品進行老化試驗，經過一段時間監
測絕緣狀態的變化，由統計運算萃取特徵參數且紀錄資料，其特徵參數的資料經由整理過
程，即可分析特徵參數之變化趨勢，並對特徵數據之老化趨勢做深入探討，由特徵參數之
變化趨勢來分類絕緣老化狀態，最後輸入特徵資料於Matlab中的類神經網路做絕緣狀態之
分類辨識，以期能預防性的診斷絕緣老化狀態。 
由於目前台電於地下配電系統所使用之XLPE電力電纜，其額定電壓等級分為15kV、
25kV兩級，其中15kV級適用於電壓等級為11.4kV之地下配電系統；25kV級則適用於電壓等
級為11.4kV及22.8kV兩種地下配電系統。但台電公司為簡化購料、庫存管理、及提升供電
品質等考量，自民國77年開始，地下配電系統電壓等級已全面採用25kV級電纜。而在線徑
方面則分為：主幹線使用之500MCM銅線，其安全電流為600A，及分歧線使用之美規#1號
線，可承受安全電流為200A。本計畫使用台電使用頻率及數量較多之25kV級500MCM單芯

2.1 脈衝電流法的原理 
此脈衝電流檢測法，為局部放電所產生的電荷交換，使得被試物兩端出現放電電荷，
然而放電電荷無法直接量測，因此利用耦合裝置、檢測阻抗等構成的試驗迴路，如圖2所示
的試驗迴路，被試物兩端的放電電荷就會通過試驗迴路形成脈衝電流，在檢測阻抗兩端可
檢測出脈衝電壓信號，該信號正比於被試物的放電電荷，則可以通過適當的定量方法，並
利用檢測阻抗上檢測出瞬間電壓來量視在放電電荷量。因此，檢測方法的適用性與精確度
在局部放電上將顯得格外關鍵。 
 
 
圖2. 局部放電檢測電路 
 
局部放電的檢測電路主要是由儀器測量系統與試驗迴路兩大部分組成，其中儀器測量
系統主要包括耦合裝置、傳輸系統（例如連接電纜和光纖）及測量儀表。試驗迴路主要由
被試物、耦合電容、檢測阻抗(Zd)、高電壓源與高電壓之接線所組成，如圖2所示。以下為
其相關裝置的敘述。 
(1) 被試物 
除長電纜和有繞組的被試物外，通常可看作集中參數電容Cx。 
 
(2)耦合電容

以保證測量裝置能安全工作；另一方面將被試物的局部放電訊號耦合到檢測阻抗上。耦合
電容器對商頻交流電壓應呈現高阻抗，對於高頻的局部放電脈衝應呈現低阻抗。耦合電容

2.2.3 試驗樣本 
本試驗用的被試物為台電標準25kV，XLPE地下電力電纜，500MCM線徑，安全電流
600A，長度約為2.5M，電纜結構示意圖如圖7所示。其採用交聯聚乙烯(XLPE)為絕緣材料，
體積小絕緣能力佳，由於在正常使用下，若為無瑕疵品，可使用年限可長達約20年，因此
並不易量測到其老化時之特徵，因此，本研究採用一條自製之瑕疵電纜，先刻意破壞其XLPE
絕緣層之厚度，並量測其於正常電壓下不至於產生局部放電之情況，予以長時間加壓，觀
測其老化之現象，如圖8所示。 
由於當設備在製造過程中可能會有一些異常雜質，或者搬運過程中因碰撞而產生裂
縫，則造成電纜提前發生老化並產生局部放電的主要因素之一。導致絕緣老化因素是由於
其內部出現微小缺陷所引起[4]，而絕緣材料缺陷可能存在有空洞、金屬突出物、焊錫突出
物與裂縫等，其中絕緣老化存在空洞最受廣泛研究[4,5]，因此本計畫亦先行製作絕緣材料
內存有空洞之被試物。

3.絕緣老化局部放電量測及特徵的萃取 
3.1 直接擷取之物理量 
藉由實際量測被試物(電力電纜)所得到之局部放電資訊，將其儲存在儀器內部的資料
檔，由於每筆資料所包含的數據仍然非常龐大，並不適用於辨識或分析使用，因此本計畫
將每筆龐大的數據，透過適當的統計運算，以有效的縮小數據之數量。 
 
3.2 特徵萃取之統計量 
分析模組之指紋圖譜特徵參數，係由最大放電量Hqmax(ψ)、平均放電量Hqn(ψ)、脈衝
次數Hn(ψ)、振幅次數H(q)及放電能量次數H(p)與以下參數所組合，搭配統計分析包括：偏
態，峰態，不對稱，交叉關聯等四個統計方式，固總共可以產生20種可能特徵值。 
(1) 偏態(Skewness)

徵值為正值。應用偏態計算轉換資料時，是分別計算正、負週期的偏態值，如圖14所示。

圖24. 辨識老化狀態類型之類神經網路模型 
 
由於類神經網路對於其初始權重值及偏權值之狀態非常敏感，初始值若能設定的好，
將可以避免網路陷入區域最佳解，進而提高類神經網路的辨識成功率，而傳統類神經網路
之訓練法則-倒傳遞(BP)訓練法則，其初始值設定大多採用隨機變數選取方式，因此，對於
辨識率之提升較為有限，訓練過程中亦容易陷入區域最佳解。基於此，本計畫利用一些最
佳化法則，融合進入類神經網路的訓練邏輯中，以取代隨機變數式之初始值選取，進而有
效提升其辨識成功率。 
粒子群最佳化演算法（Particle Swarm Optimization，PSO）是一種基於群體的進化計算
技術，是Kennedy和Eberhart 於1995 年在對一個簡化社會模型進行模擬的過程中提出的。
它最早是作為一種非線性連續函數最佳化的方法，但也可以擴展到離散二進制情況和應用
於組合最佳化問題。從方法論的角度看，PSO主要源於人工生命理論以及鳥類或魚類的群
集現象；從社會認知學的角度看，PSO應用了如下的簡單道理：即群體中的每個個體都可
以從以往經驗和鄰近個體的發現中受益，其理論基礎主要包括3個基本因素，刺激的評價，
與近鄰的比較，及對領先近鄰的模仿。 
PSO演算法的規則很簡單，其特點是：每個粒子都有一個速度和位置；這個位置的目
標函數是已知的；它自身所經歷的最佳解和族群所遇到的最佳解的目標函數值是己知的。

An artiﬁcial identiﬁcation system to classify the insulation aging status of cast-resin transformer through
current impulse method of partial discharge (PD) is proposed. The aging phenomenon of insulation mate-
rials belongs to a natural property and has strongly inﬂuences with the stability of power systems. There-
fore, an effectively insulating identiﬁcation technology plays an important role to enhance the system
operating reliability. Since PD is a well known evidence of insulation degrading, a series of high voltage
test with acceleration aging process to collect PD signals for identiﬁcation system are conducted. Some
selected statistical PD features instead of waveform are then extracted from these experimental PD sig-
nals as input data of the identiﬁcation system. Also, an artiﬁcial neural network that combined particle
swarm optimization is presented as the effectively identiﬁcation tool. To demonstrate the effectiveness
and feasibility of the proposed approach, the artiﬁcial identiﬁcation system is applied on both noisy
and noiseless circumstance. The experiment showed promising results with over 94% identiﬁcation rate
and even with 30% noise per discharge signal, an 85% successful identiﬁcation rate can still be achieved.

1. Introduction
The insulation status of electrical equipments directly inﬂuences
the stability of power system. Degraded insulating property of elec-
tric equipments that result from aging process will lead to serious
accident and great loss for the utilities and customers. However,
the aging phenomenon of insulationmaterials during running peri-
od is a natural property (Agarwal, 1995). Thus effectively insulating
diagnostic technologyplaysan important role toenhance thesystem
operating reliability. Since the performance of power supply is
closely related to electric equipments, it is possible to improve con-
siderably the stability of power supply if prevent examination of
electric equipments is made available. Cast-resin transformers are
widely used in the modern distribution system, especially for some
high technology factories and intelligence building. Unlike the oil
immerse transformer, Dissolved Gas Analysis (DGA) is not available
for cast-resin transformers. Since transformer is one of electric
equipments most closely associated with power consumption, the
lifespan of transformer depends upon the insulating strength of
insulation materials. Therefore, monitoring the aging status of the
transformer will be helpful to prevent system failure (Lin, Wu, &
Chung, 2008). Since digital PD detection is developed (Ward, 1992)
hasextremelyhighdiagnosis accuracy for insulatingmedium(James
& Phung, 1995; Tanaka, 1995), it is an efﬁcient way to well identify
the aging status of cast-resin transformer for maintenance or
replacement to further prevent insulation breakdown (Farag et al.,
1999; James & Phung, 1995; Karthikeyan, Gopal, & Venkatesh,
2008; Tanaka, 1995; Yue, Chen, Cheng, Song, & Xie, 2006).
When the insulation of equipments has some defection or
aging, the local partial discharge might be happened on rate volt-
age service. If the PD waveform can be collected and analyzed, it
should be possible to identify the nature of failure or the aging sta-
tus, thus making breakthrough contribution on defective detection.
Any breakdown of running transformer will lead to dramatic loss
and negative inﬂuence. In an attempt to prevent and identify occa-
sional accident, degrading diagnosis and aging status identiﬁcation
of transformer are urgently required based on a safe, real-time and
accurate solution.
Recently, the use of the global optimization technique called
particle swarm optimization (PSO) (Kennedy & Eberhart, 1995),
to solve real world problems have aroused researchers’ interest
due to its ﬂexibility and efﬁciency. Limitations regarding the form
of the objective function employed and the continuity of variables
used for the classical greedy search technique can be completely
eliminated. Considering the complexity of error curve of back
propagation (BP) neural network and its sensitivity to initial
weights (Amir & Chuanyi, 1997), this paper applied PSO as the ﬁrst
optimization stage to get a better initial weights and bias for BP
neural network. When the initial weights and bias are set from
PSO, the artiﬁcial neural network with BP training is used as the
identiﬁcation system. This identiﬁcation system combining PSO
and BP for neural network training is then called PSO–BP hybrid
algorithm.

cast-resin transformer with no accident history. The accelerated

4.1. Brief review of PSO
The PSO ﬁrst introduced by Kennedy and Eberhart, is one of the
modern heuristic algorithms. It was developed through simulation
of a simpliﬁed social system, and has been found to be robust in
solving continuous nonlinear optimization problems (Clerc & Ken-
nedy, 2002; Kennedy & Eberhart, 1995). The PSO technique can
generate a high-quality solution within shorter calculation time
and stable convergence characteristic than other stochastic meth-
ods (Eberhart & Shi, 1998; El-Zonkoly, 2006; Shi & Eberhart, 1999).

far and its position. Moreover, each individual knows the best va-
lue in the group Gbest among Pbest, namely the best value so far of
the group. The modiﬁed velocity of each individual can be calcu-
lated using the current velocity and the distance from Pbest and
Gbest as shown below:

4.2. The PSO–BP hybrid training algorithm for artiﬁcial neural network
The process of PSO–BP hybrid algorithm is divided into two
phases:
Step 1: Initial settings with PSO
Firstly, PSO optimum algorithm is used to obtain the ini-
tial weights and bias of neural networks. The weights and
bias of feed-forward neural network are taken as the opti-
mum, i.e. the coded particle position vector during PSO
process. Next, PSO algorithm is used to search the opti-
mum. The objective function is to minimize mean square
error (MSE) of all training samples. PSO algorithm is
stopped if PSO convergence conditions are met. In such
case, the near optimum solution from PSO is taken as ini-
tial weights and bias of neural network.
Step 2: BP training procedure
Back propagation training is performed using the initial
weights and bias that close to optimum obtained from
Step 1. When the convergence conditions of back propaga-
tion are met, the calculation is stopped in order to
increase the probability of converging to global optimum
during the training process. Therefore, the ﬂowchart of
proposed process with the extracted features of PD signal
on cast-resin transformer to process the aging periods
classiﬁcation has been shown in Fig. 9.

2. AE technology
AE refers to natural phenomenon in many situations, such as
earthquake, rock burst, thunder, crack of tree branches, and frac-
ture of glass or pottery. In ancient times, the quality of pottery
ware was identiﬁed with AE principle; at present, the maximum
load of automobile components is tested with the same principle.
The ASTM standard E610-82 gave a detailed description as ‘‘AE is
a transient elastic waves generated by the rapid release of energy
form localized sources within a material”. PD detection is basically
considered as a pulse phenomenon of energy release, and the sub-
sequent sound release is named AE from the perspective of super-
sonic wave. Therefore, an ideal way to judge the performance of
transformer online is based on computer-aided AE method for PD
detection. The following are three highlights of AE methods in PD
detection for transformer (Boczar & Zmarzly, 2004; Chen, Tsao, &
Lin, 2005; Grossmann & Feser, 2005; Gupta & Ramu, 2001;
Lundgaard, 2001).
(1) Online and real-time supervision: AE can be detected online
without switching off, or any special arrangements of
equipments.
(2) High safety: AE can be detected from a contact-free or out-
side contact manner, thereby helping to improve greatly
the safety of maintenance personnel compared with high
voltage testing.
(3) Perfect trouble shooting: In the case of new or unknown
troubles of electric equipments, only AE data shall be
required for future identiﬁcation and prevention.
Therefore, in this paper, applying AE principle to analyze PD of
transformers which is difﬁcult to diagnose online by other meth-
ods is intended.
2.1. Acquisition of AE signal
PD data, if obtained from computer simulation testing, may dif-
fer from those derived from ﬁeld test research. Therefore, in this
paper, we use ﬁeld experiment, and employ epoxy-resin trans-
formers with designated pre-faulty to get the defective types pat-
tern of PD. Five types of defects are designed, which are mostly
faulty in ﬁeld include model A, improper protrusion (non-smooth-
ing) on high voltage terminal; models B and C, improper solder or
wire on grounding and high voltage coil terminal; and models D
and E, improper bubble within insulating material near low voltage
and high voltage coil. To reduce interference from noise and to
make a very high level of dependability, the experiment is con-
ducted mainly in an electron-magnetically shielding room. The
hardware structure of PD measurement for AE is shown in Fig. 1.
When the rate voltage is applied on the experiment epoxy-resin
transformer, PD signal will be generated and will result in AE phe-
nomenon. A voltage value will be obtained from AE sensor and will
then be transmitted through a preampliﬁer to the data acquisition
card performing A/D conversion. Finally, the digital data pattern is
stored in the database of PC.
2.2. Parameters setting for AE signal sampling
There are some parameters that should be set properly to
perform AE measurement system, since AE sensors are very sensi-
tive to noise and reﬂected wave. The noise and reﬂected wave pre-
venting and processing often dictate the performance of AE
measurements and ﬁnal recognition. Therefore, if the AE system
concentrates only on maximum sampling rate without considering
other issues, the data may contain many noise and reﬂected wave
interferences. It will be impossible to identify the defective types of
equipments perfectly. In such a case, to prevent noise interference
and reﬂected wave while sampling the relevant AE parameters
shall be taken into account for optimized setting and utilization.
2.2.1. Parameters for waveform sampling
Reﬂected wave is a most challenging issue in the identiﬁcation
of AE data, since it has characteristics similar to original AE wave-
form. In this paper, addressing the reﬂected interference is at-
tempted using waveform characteristic ﬁltering method, and AE
waveform is analyzed with three commonly deﬁned parameters
to obtain a complete waveform.

3.1. Brief review of PSO
The PSO, ﬁrst introduced by Kennedy and Eberhart, is one of the
modern heuristic algorithms. It was developed through the simula-

in solving continuous nonlinear optimization problems (Clerc &
Kennedy, 2002; Kennedy & Eberhart, 1995). The PSO technique
can generate a high-quality solution within shorter calculation
time and stable convergence characteristic than other stochastic
methods (Eberhart & Shi, 1998; El-Zonkoly, 2006; Shi & Eberhart,
1999). Searching procedures by PSO based on the above-men-

best value so far of the group. The modiﬁed velocity of each indi-
vidual can be calculated using the current velocity and the distance
from Pbest and Gbest as shown below:

6. Conclusions
Most of the PD detection methods can be applied on ofﬂine per-
iod of equipment. By using AE, the real-time and online detection
could be easily performed. Therefore, a top-down experimental
procedure of epoxy-resin transformers for defective types recogni-
tion by PD is developed in this paper. High voltage test on pre-
faulty transformers is conducted and PD signals are measured for
defect recognition and type classiﬁcation. Five selected features
are used in this paper to increase the successful probability of rec-
ognition rate. A PSO–BP approach is also provided for better recog-
nition results. The whole proposed procedure can be easily
extended to other electrical equipments for online defect detection
and type classiﬁcation. To show the effectiveness of the proposed
approach, several classiﬁcation and identiﬁcation simulations are
used to evaluate. The results show that the recognition rate is as
high as 90–96% under noise-free circumstances. It should be high
enough for practical use to reduce system failure probability.
Although, noise may interfere with or overlap the PD pattern,
and likewise inﬂuence the recognition rate. In our research, even
on 30% noise-added situation, at least 80% recognition rate can
be guaranteed. These encouraging results show that this paper
can provide a feasible and effective way to early detect the possible
failure of epoxy-resin transformers and can also determine the
types of failure to help utility for maintenance needed.

III. TIME SERIES INDEX 
How to discriminate PD with noise is generally relied on 
experienced professionals. By measuring wave amplitudes and 
power-cycle phases, an ellipse is often drawn and the PD 
event needed to be manually identified. However, AEs are 
easily distorted by external noise and interference, so 
misjudgments often occur in manual identification. 
The time series index is proposed on GIS PD analysis, which 
places importance on characteristics of frequencies occurring 
in PD signals, rather than on the waveform. It effectively 
increases the possibility of correct determination of GIS PD by 
avoiding problems caused by wave distortion and interference. 
The proposed time series index is explained below: 
For the GIS devices, the correct differentiation between noise, 
corona, or internal PD in measured AE signals is very 
important. If the measured signal is a noise signal, the GIS 
would not have to undergo any maintenance work, where as if 
the measured signal was a corona, then the GIS must be 
checked to see if any charged electronic parts are exposed, or 
if the dust level is too high, when internal PD is detected, then 
it is vital to schedule a time to turn off the device in order to 
repair and maintain it, to prevent complete power failure. 
Therefore, this paper focuses on the 3 topics of internal PD, 
coronal discharge and noise as its research topic. 
According to references, the 3 phenomena patterns are drawn 
in Fig. 2 [11]. Observation concludes that different types of 
PD signals have different discharge phase angles, occurrence 
frequencies and distribution.

V. CONCLUSION 
To prevent economic and social loses caused by power 
outages, preventive measures must be taken to ensure that 
power devices are always functioning properly. Currently, PD 
is an effective detection method. An electrical device under 
long hours of operation without good maintenance would 
easily accelerate the deterioration of insulation material. This 
influences operating performance and shortens the lifespan of 
the device. This paper proposes a waveform feature filtering 
method and combined with time series index based on the AE 
measurement method in order to reach higher safety standards, 
better identification, and online detection of a PD fault 
detection system. Using data collected from self-made 
equipment and onsite measurements, this paper concludes that 
the idea proposed can effectively distinguish the internal PD 
phenomenon occurring in GIS, and distinguish between 
different types of PD signals, thus proving the feasibility and 
effectiveness of this research. 
Measurements can be performed by the online detection 
system without any additional processes to the power device, 
or having to turn off the power devices. In addition, the 
concept of time series index used for distinguishing PD signals 
is also present in the results of some of our test cases. This 
concept clearly distinguishes the differences in internal 
discharge, coronal discharge, and noise, especially in the case 
of the onsite data, since the devices to be measured were 
defected and repaired for comparison. Following the results, 
there is even more confirmation of the feasibility of this 
method. By using the approach proposed in this paper, we can 
effectively detect flaws and failures in GIS beforehand, and 
avoid loses due to power outages, while increasing the safety 
and reliability of electrical systems.

集的出現在第三象限，然而會隨著其嚴重程度慢慢向外
擴散，且幅值會變大。 
(3).雜訊: 
  雜訊一般來自於我們測量的環境，可能是運轉中機
器發出的噪音，或是機械震動，而其出現在橢圓軌跡圖
上便會是雜亂沒有規律的出現。 
由以上的幾種放電類型圖可得知，其局部放電的頻率成
份相對於電壓頻率各有所不同，因此我們就可以依放電
頻率與電源頻率之間的關係來辨識局部放電類型的基
準。本論文認為假使是有發生部份放電，就會產生音射
訊號，因此我們認為所量測到所有的音射信號之頻率，
就會就會像局部放電所產生的電流脈衝之頻率與設備
電壓的頻率有關係，基於此分析，本文以所收集到的音
射訊號之間的相互關係來作為辨識的基礎，以台灣來說
其商用電壓頻率為六十赫茲，其電源週期為 16.67ms，
本文為了可以計算出 120 赫茲，因此是以 8.3ms 為基準
來做辨識，因此可以用公式(3.1)來作為計算音射訊號間
的頻率關係。

  然而我們在本文中比較關心的GIS的放電類型為內
部放電以及電暈放電；內部放電和電暈放電的電流脈衝
圖在一個 360 度的相位內會出現許多次電流脈衝，將接
收到的音射訊號之時間記錄下來再用公式 3.1 可得到頻
率，若是依據我們觀察到的局部放電電流脈衝圖，內部
放電的圖譜所顯示在第一與第三象限中，所以公式計算
出來應該會固有定頻率落在 120HZ 上，如果放電不嚴重
的話放電就會集中在一個象限，那麼就會有固定頻率落
在 60HZ 上，但是兩象限內會有多次放電造成計算後會
有高頻的成分，不過並不會影響到最後的結果因為一個
週期內會有兩次分析出的頻率會落在 120HZ，電暈放電
的放電圖譜所顯示在第三象限中，因此以公式計算出來
應該會有固定落在 60HZ 上，但也會和內部放電一樣會
計算出高頻的成分不過並不會影響到最後的結果，因為
一個週期內還是可以會有一次分析出的頻率落在
60HZ，假使是為雜訊的話，並不會有固定出現在任何一
個象限內所以分析出來並不會有固定的頻率，因此就可
以作為區分。
