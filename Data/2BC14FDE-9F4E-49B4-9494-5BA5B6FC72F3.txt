of In0.06Ga0.94N/GaN multiple-quantum-well 
ultraviolet light emitting diodes (UV LEDs) with 
emission wavelength of 400 nm has been investigated 
and compared with blue (470 nm) LEDs. Based on their 
injection current-dependent characteristics under dc and 
pulsed operation, it can be concluded that carrier 
overflow is the dominant factor that affects the external 
quantum efficiency of UV LED before thermal effects 
take over. It is experimentally shown that increasing the 
number of quantum wells is necessary to alleviate the 
carrier overflow issue and improve the luminescence 
efficiency of the UV LEDs. In the topic (2), it is found 
that adding slight amount of Al into GaN for a barrier 
layer does not degrade the crystal quality. Instead, it 
leads to better carrier confinement in In0.06Ga0.94N 
quantum well. The maximum output power and 
external quantum efficiency of a 300×300 µm2 
packaged chip is 13.4 mW and 21.7 % under 
continuous wave current injection of 20 mA, 
respectively. In the topic (3), Ti/WSi/Ti/Au and 
Pd/NiO/Al/Ti/Au, low resistance, high optical 
reflectivity and high thermal stability n and p-type 
ohmic contacts for flip-chip light-emitting diodes, have 
been demonstrated, respectively. In the topic (4), the 
improved material quality and good electrical 
conductivity of crack-free 6 µm Al0.1Ga0.9N film is 
realized on a simple strain reducing structure by 
controlling its growth parameters. In the topic (5), 
based on the obtained high quality thick Al0.1Ga0.9N 
buffer layer and optimized Inx1Ga1-x1N/ 
Alx2Iny2Ga1-x2-y2N multi-quantum wells, output 
power larger than 2 mW is obtained for 365 nm UV 
LEDs. In the topic (6), in order to get further 
improvement on the performance of 365 nm UV LEDs, 
LED structure was grown on SiON stripe-patterned 
sapphire substrates. It is found that the PL emission 
intensity is improved substantially by about 10 times 
than the conventional one. This improvement is 
attributed to both defect reduction and light extraction 
enhancement by the SiON stripe patterns. 
In the subproject II, the research topics include (1) 
solid state lighting package, (2) development of UV 
transparent lens and (3) development of high thermal 
dissipation package technology. In the topic (1), the Si 
lamp is formed by using isotropic and anisotropic dry 
etching in the Si lamp structure. We also simulate the 
optical performance of the lamp with and without 
phosphorous-coating.  In the Pb free solder, we also 
find that the bonding ability and thermal conductivity 
between the solder and Cu matrix are good. In the topic 
(2), two methods for UV transparent lens are used, 
which contain (a) photoresist-reflow method and (b) 
Si-molding substrate. In the subtopic (a), 
photoresist-reflow is used to fabricate the photoresist 
lens, and then transfer them to a quartz substrate.  The 
shape of the quartz lens can be optimized by choosing 
appropriate etching selectivity between the photoresist 
and quartz. In the subtopic (b), Si-molding substrate is 
used, and silicone or sol-gel glass is used as the lens 
materials. Si lens mold is developed by using an 
isotropic dry etching method.  For the Pb-free solder 
process, we have found that the bonding ability and 
thermal conductivity between In solder and Cu matrix 
are good, too. In the topic (3), high thermal dissipation 
package technology is developed. A miniature heat pipe 
is used to solve the problem of solid-state lighting 
system. We have developed Si and Al base miniature 
heat pipes which can work in solid-state lighting 
system. 
In the subproject III, an optical model is developed 
for simulating light extraction efficiency of GaN-based 
LED chip. We propose to introduce a periodic inverse 
pyramid structure on the interface between the sapphire 
and the n-GaN of a GaN-based LED to obtain a light 
extraction efficiency as high as 78%. On the other hand, 
based on the Monte-Carlo ray tracing simulation, the 
light extraction efficiency of GaN-based LEDs as a 
function of the position of the light source over the 
active layer is studied. Several parameters, including 
chip dimensions, absorption coefficients and packages 
are analyzed. Besides, the light extraction efficient 
characteristic of a Thin-GaN LED is studied with 
respect to a sapphire-based LED.
