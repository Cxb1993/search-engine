一、中文摘要 
本實驗利用射頻磁控濺鍍法製備ZnO薄膜於C軸藍寶石單晶基板上，實驗內容主要分成兩
個部份： (1) 探討添加緩衝層之氧化鋅磊晶薄膜的磊晶品質及緩衝層效應， (2)稀磁性
Zn1-xMxO(M=Co,Mn)磊晶薄膜，期望獲得室溫稀鐵磁性ZnO薄膜； 
從實驗結果顯示，氧化鋅薄膜的最佳磊晶條件為，基板溫度為500℃，濺鍍時間為30分鐘，
有較好的結晶性及最低電阻率2.53×104 Ω.cm；且由XRD之Phi-scan證明其氧化鋅薄膜具有磊晶
性 質 ， 並 由 TEM 分 析 結 果 得 知 ， 此 氧 化 鋅 薄 膜 呈 C 軸 成 長 ， 其 磊 晶 關 係 為
[0001]ZnO//[0001]Sapphire。藉由改變成長溫度及時間於藍寶石單晶基板濺鍍低溫同質氧化鋅緩衝
層，再於高溫成長氧化鋅薄膜發現氧化鋅薄膜為優選方向的成長，非磊晶結構。 
摻雜之氧化鋅磊晶薄膜方面，鈷元素之摻雜量對氧化鋅薄膜之最佳條件；根據實驗結果顯
示，在摻雜量為 8 at%時，氧化鋅薄有較好的結晶性及最低電阻率 1.19×105 Ω.cm；由磁性分析
得知，在低溫時 8 at%、10 at%及 15 at%皆有鐵磁性，而摻雜量為 10 at%及 15 at%時在室溫下
具有鐵磁性質。錳元素之摻雜量對氧化鋅薄膜之實驗結果顯示，在摻雜量為 6 at%時，氧化鋅
薄有較好的結晶性及最低電阻率 6.37×104 Ω.cm；由磁性分析得知，在低溫時 6 at%、10 at%及
15 at%皆有鐵磁性，而摻雜量為 10 at%時在室溫下具有鐵磁性質。 
關鍵詞: 磊晶，射頻磁控濺鍍法，稀磁性氧化物，摻雜 
 
Abstract   
This study focused on the preparation of the epitaxial zinc oxide thin films on sapphire substrates 
by radio-frequency magnetron sputtering. The experiment is mainly categorized into two parts. The 
first part explores the buffered ZnO films on the epitaxy of the pure ZnO thin films and the second 
concerns the feasibility of Co-or Mn-codoped ZnO films.  
From the experimental results, the ZnO film deposited by sputtering, the growth temperature and 
the flow ratios of argon to oxygen were shown to significantly influence the crystalline quality and 
surface morphology of ZnO films. The optimal conditions for the epieaxial growth are those films 
grown at 500 ℃ with Ar/O2 ratio to be 1:1. The epitaxy of ZnO films were confirmed by both TEM 
and Phi-scan results by XRD. The epitaxial relationship is [0001]ZnO//[0001]Sapphire and (0002) ZnO 
//(0006) Sapphire. However, the introduction of the buffered ZnO films is not effective to enhance the 
epitaxy of ZnO films in this styudy.

四、結果與討論 
    由以上的實驗規劃大致上將實驗結果分為兩部分(添加緩衝層之氧化鋅磊晶薄膜、稀磁性
Zn1-xMxO(M=Co,Mn)磊晶薄膜)，並分述其濺鍍實驗參數及氧化物摻雜量對薄膜微結構光學性
質、電性質及磁性質之影響。  
4.1 添加緩衝層之氧化鋅磊晶薄膜 
4.1.1 於藍寶石基板上製備氧化鋅磊晶薄膜 
本實驗以射頻磁控濺鍍系統，濺鍍氧化鋅薄膜於藍寶石基板上。適當地改變控制濺鍍參
數，如基板溫度，藉此了解這些製程參數，對於結晶結構、表面型態、光學性質、電性、微結
構的影響，找出適合磊晶薄膜之濺鍍條件。 
圖 1 為氧化鋅薄膜改變基板溫度之 XRD 繞射圖，從圖中可觀察出(002)與(004)之氧化鋅
薄膜的繞射峰，以及 Sapphire 基板的繞射峰，並未看到其他相產生。其中(002)面的相對強度
較高，為主要的優選取向，且(002)面與(004)面皆呈 C 軸成長。Norifumi Fujimura[6]等人指出，
使用射頻濺鍍氧化鋅薄膜通常為多晶結構，且呈 C 軸成長，原子會以最低表面自由能的平面
來沉積，而(002)面之表面能為 0.099 eV/Å2，所以氧化鋅薄膜會往[002]方向成長。由 XRD 繞
射圖得知，隨著溫度的增加，(002)面的強度有隨之增強的趨勢，而半高寬也隨著溫度的增加，
有逐漸變窄的趨勢。文獻指出[7]，隨著基材溫度的增加，會給予原子能量，使得原子有足夠的
動能進行移動與擴散，而使結晶性變好，而當增加到 550℃時，(002)面的繞射峰相對的降低，
且半高寬也有增加現象。這是因為過高的溫度，反而會破壞結晶性[8]。 
圖 2 為氧化鋅薄膜改變基板溫度之 SEM 圖。從型態上看來，從室溫到 400℃，其晶粒
有明顯變大的趨勢，在 350℃時可以觀察到有大小不一的晶粒，而到了 400℃時，其晶粒有明
顯變大，在 350℃時其薄膜有些許的缺陷孔洞，而到了 400℃時孔洞也消失不見。這是由於溫
度的增加，原子有足夠的動能進行擴散及移動，所以會使得晶粒成長變大，也使得薄膜的結構
越緻密，結晶性越好。而從 400℃到 500℃，隨著溫度的上升，表面的平整度有逐漸趨於平坦，
在 500℃時能得到最為平整的表面；而在 550℃時，其表面型態從平面成長轉為島狀成長，呈
現一多晶的表面形態。 C.C. Chang[8] 指出，薄膜成長品質與沈積時欲鍍粒子到達基板表面時
的擴散能力遷移率有關，當擴散能力不足，其所成長之薄膜會有坑洞產生，而擴散能力太高，
則會發生再結晶的現象。所以太高的溫度，反而會使二維成長轉為三維成長，所以在 500℃時
為最佳之基板溫度。

對氧化鋅薄膜之(11-22)及Sapphire (11-26)進行Phi-scan，從圖中可以發現氧化鋅都是每隔60o就

4.2 稀磁性 Zn1-xMxO(M=Co, Mn)磊晶薄膜及 P-N 接面製作 
4.2.1 稀磁性 Zn1-xCoxO 磊晶薄膜 
    本實驗以射頻磁控濺鍍系統，濺鍍 Zn1-xCoxO 薄膜於藍寶石基板上。適當地改變控制濺鍍
參數，如 Co 元素之摻雜量，藉此了解這些製程參數，對於結晶結構、表面型態、鍵結組態、
光學性質、電性、磁性的影響，找出適合之濺鍍條件。本實驗固定基板溫度為 500℃，通入氬
氣 2 sccm、氧氣 8 sccm，沈積時間為 30 分鐘，濺鍍功率 100W，濺鍍時之工作壓力為 5mTorr，
改變不同 Co 元素之添加量，探討薄膜中不同 Co 添加量對於 Zn1-xCoxO 薄膜之影響。 
圖 10 為 Zn1-xCoxO 薄膜改變摻雜量之 XRD 繞射圖，從圖中可觀察出(002)與(004)之氧化
鋅薄膜的繞射峰，以及 Sapphire 基板的繞射峰，並未看到其他相產生。由 XRD 繞射圖得知，
隨著摻雜量的增加，(002)面的強度有隨之下降的趨勢，而半高寬也隨著摻雜量的增加，有逐
漸變寬的趨勢。根據 Sejoon Lee 等人的看法[9]，薄膜結晶性越好對磁性質越有幫助，因此，具
有良好的(002)優選方向的薄膜對本實驗是很重要的。 
圖 11 為 Zn1-xCoxO 薄膜改變摻雜量之 PL 圖，使用光致螢光光譜(Photoluminescence，PL) 
觀察薄膜中的缺陷。由圖中得知，隨著摻雜量的增加，其 PL 之峰值有逐漸下降的趨勢。由文
獻[10]得知在 2.8eV 和 3.0eV 之峰值，分別對應的是鋅間隙(Zni)和鋅空缺(VZn)，隨著摻雜量增
加，其峰值強度明顯變弱，鋅空缺之峰值也隨摻雜量的增加而變弱。推測可能為隨著摻雜量的
增加，其結晶性也逐漸變差，其整體峰值強度也明顯下降。

五、結論

Cr additive effects on the nanostructure, magnetic properties and 
ordering kinetics of FePtCu thin films
Guo-Ju Chena,*, Yung-Hui Shih*, Yee-Shin Chang**, Jason Shian-Ching Jang*, Sheng-Rui Jian*
*Department of Materials Science and Engineering, I-SHOU University, Kaohsiung, Taiwan
** Graduate Institute of Electro-optic and Material Engineering, National Formosa University, Yunlin

agjchen@isu.edu.tw, magggggg2@gmail.com
Keywords: Alloy films, DC sputtering, magnetic property, nanostructure
Abstract.
In this study, the (FePt)94-xCu6Crx (x=0, 6.5, 8.3, 11.1) alloy films were prepared by sputtering
deposition. The effects of Cr addition content and heat treatment on the nanostructure and magnetic 
properties of the polycrystalline FePtCu films are reported. Previous experiments showed that the 
ordering temperature of the (FePt)94-xCu6 films reduced to 320 ℃, which is much lower than that of 
the FePt alloy. However, the grain growth after heat treatment limited the practical application in 
recording media.
By adding the Cr content in the FePtCu film, the grain sizes of the films can be adjusted from 48 to 
12nm, even for the films annealed at temperature as high as 600℃. DSC traces of as-deposited 
disorder films at different heating rates, to evaluate the crystallization of the order phase, revealed that 
the addition of Cr enhanced the activation energy of ordering from 87 kJ/mol to 264 kJ/mol for the 
(FePt)94-xCu6Crx (x=0 and 6.5, respectively) films. The (FePt)94-xCu6Crx (x=6.5) film showed a 
coercive force of 12.8 kOe and the magnetization of 658 emu/cc.

  The  (FePt)94-xCu6Crx (x=0, 6.54, 8.33, 11.11) alloy thin films were prepared using a DC magnetron 
sputtering. Thin films were deposited by a composite target on the Corning 7059 substrate at room 
temperature. The film thickness was about 120 nm. Film compositions were adjusted by the numbers 
of the Cu and Pt chips on Fe target. Prior to growth the base pressure ranged from 5 to 7x 10-6 torr and 
the working pressure was fixed at 4 mtorr. The DC power kept at 30 W. The as-deposited films were 
subsequently annealed in the temperature range of 300~600  for one hour in vacuum at 1x 10-6 torr.
The crystal structure of the samples was determined by glancing incident X-ray diffraction 
analysis (GIXRD) using Cu-Kα radiation at an incident angle of 0.5o. The microstructure of the films 
was observed by transmission electron microscope (TEM). The compositions of the films were 
examined by electron probe microanalysis (EPMA). Magnetic properties were studied using a 
superconducting quantum interference device (SQUID) at room temperature with a maximum 
magnetic field of 50 KOe.
3. Results and discussion
Figure 1(a) shows the X-ray diffraction patterns of the  (FePt)94-xCu6Crx (x= 6.54) alloy films on 
glass as-deposited and annealed at the temperature range of 300  to 600℃ for one hour. The 
as-deposited and the samples annealed at lower than 400 has a disordered γ (Fe, Pt) phase (Fig.1 
(a)). The XRD pattern shows mainly the (111) reflection, since the lattice plane is the most 
close-packed plane, which has lowest surface energy. There is no impurity phase except for the γ (Fe, 
Pt) phase. After heat treatment at higher temperatures, order L10 FePt phase that accompanied the 
(001) and (011) superlattice reflections appear while the annealing temperature exceeded 500. In 
previous study, the ordering temperature of the FePtCu films was reduced to 320[12]. According to 
the literature [13], a solid solution phase (Fe, Cu)Pt might form at low temperature which could 
reduce the ordering temperature of FePt phase. Since the (Fe, Cu)Pt phase belongs to the space group 
of P4/mmm (JCPDS card no.: 42-1359) ,which is the same space group as that of ordering FePt phase. 
phase, Tx, for the (FePt)94-xCu6Crx (x=0~11.11)films were measured by DSC. The crystallization 
temperatures for the films with Cr content ranged from 6.54~11.11 at.% are shown to be higher than 
that without Cr addition. The ordering temperatures are estimated to increase from 450℃ to 578℃
with the Cr content x=0~11.11. The increasing ordering temperature might result from the difficulty 
of atomic diffusion inside the FePt grain and diffusion through microstructural defects, such as 
microcracks, grain boundaries and Cr wetting layers.   
The activation energy for crystallization of the ordering (FePt)94-xCu6Crx films was determined 
by means of the Kissinger plot[16],

the excess Cr atoms will diffuse out of the FePt. Meanwhile, the Cr atoms in the FePt grains obstacle
the diffusions of Fe and Pt atoms during ordering. Therefore, the ordering process requires higher 
activation energy to proceed.

L10 FePt is hard magnetic. Cr layer between FePt grains causes some effects, such as fining grain size, 
hindering inter-granular magnetic coupling, which might affect the magnetic properties of the 
(FePt)94-xCu6Crx films. Especially, the Cr wetting layer change the surface energy of FePt grains, thus,

proves that the Cu additive effectively enhanced the ordering of FePt phase and obtained high 
coercivity even with the Cr addition.
In summary, the coexist of Cu and Cr additives may cause two effects in the FePt films : (i) Cu
atoms keep the low ordering temperatures and high ordering parameters, in spite of Cr addition, and 
(ii) excess Cr atoms form wetting layers around FePt grains, which fining grain size and modifying
the morphology of the FePt phase. The former increases the fraction of order FePt. The later limits the 
grain growth of FePt grain during high temperature annealing. The coercivity of the films may 
enhance due to the domain wall pinning effect and the fraction of the order FePt phase. In this 
experiment, the maximum Hc of about and 12.8 kOe and the magnetization of 658 emu/cc were 
achieved in the film direction. 
4. Conclusion
In the study, the polycrystalline (FePt)94-xCu6Crx (x=0~11.11) thin films on glass were prepared by 
DC magnetron sputtering method. Experimental results indicate that Cr addition in the films 
influences the formation of ordered phase. Cr atoms also form wetting layer between FePt grains. The 
activation energy of the ordering of the (FePt)94-xCu6Crx (x=6.54 ~11.11) thin films enormously 
increases to the range of 264~305 kJ mole−1. The (FePt)94-xCu6Crx (x=6.54) film annealed at 600 
oC
for 1 hour shows the maximum Hc of about and 12.8 kOe and the magnetization of 658 emu/cc.
