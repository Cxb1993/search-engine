摘要 
本研究基於精實服務原則，嘗試建立供給與消費流程之客觀分析架構，並找出供給與消
費流程中可能的顧客接觸點，提出不同的合併設計，再以最小化總浪費成本為目標，希望找
出不同顧客接觸程度下之最佳化之服務設計。合併後之流程複雜，不易以解析方式處理，本
研究運用模擬方式進行流程分析，並初步研究運用模擬最佳化策略於尋找最佳的合併價值溪
流設計。本研究先就顧客接觸之程度加以分類，確立後續需建立之接觸點範圍，降低接觸點
分析之複雜度；在供給與消費溪流的合併上，則先建構合併影響模式，再以模擬分析合併後
對於服務整體之影響。本研究除健康食品，並舉若干服務業說明不同顧客接觸程度下之可能
接觸範圍，再以洗衣業進行模擬驗證。研究發現服務設計若先確定顧客接觸程度，再進行合
併設計，不僅有效降低設計複雜度，亦可藉由模擬分析，規劃與驗證服務之績效。 
關鍵詞：精實服務、流程模擬、模擬最佳化 
Abstract 
We proposed a lean-based service flow analysis framework. The framework first identifies the 
range that customer participations can have upon the service provision flow, should they be on the 
upstream, middle-stream, and/or downstream. The identified range helps the designer to focus on 
important customer contacts upon the provision flow and facilitates the combined designs of both 
service provision flow and service consumption flow. With mostly undetermined parameters and 
complex process structure, simulation is used for the effectiveness analysis of various flow designs 
and for seeking the optimal combined design by using a more efficient simulation strategy. In 
addition to health food service process, some service design cases are drawn to describe how to 
identify the range of customer participation. A simulation case is demonstrated for the laundry 
cleaning service to show the effectiveness when comparing various combined designs of laundry 
cleaning service. 
Keywords: Lean Service, Process Simulation, Simulation Optimization

三、研究方法 
3.1 顧客參與之角色與深度 
本研究將顧客參與定義為：顧客與服務企業之間的接觸行為，不管在訊息接觸或是體力
上的接觸，若是顧客有在流程中提供意見或是親身參與流程的發展，或是在最後接受到服務
的部份均視為顧客參與的一種。 
服務的過程可分為 3 階段：顧客輸入、服務過程與顧客接受服務的結果。我們定義低度
參與為顧客只需接受服務；在中度參與部份，顧客不僅接受服務結果，且應驅使服務者依顧
客需要來創造或更新服務；在高度參與部份，則顧客需與服務者共同經應服務過程。 
3.1.1 參與深度與服務流程設計 
服務是一連串的活動，流程設計的好壞會直接影響服務模式的效能與效果。服務流程以
往較偏向先由服務者先行定義清楚服務流程，再讓顧客依服務者所規劃之服務流程來執行，
至於是否能回應顧客需求無法確定。 
本研究依不同顧客參與程度來發展不同的流程設計，首先對顧客角色加以確定，再依據
定義完的顧客角色來判定顧客屬何類型參與的顧客，依據不同類型的顧客來確定顧客對於服
務流程所著重的部分，再依據所定義顧客參與的程度來設計服務流程。設計好服務流程架構
後，如圖 3.1。

2. Previous Study 
Project activity cost varies with its duration and resource inputs. On the other hand, different 
resource inputs 專 and costs will vary the activity duration as well. This is categorized as 
Time-Cost Trade-Off Problem (TCTP). Kelly (1961) assumes the relationship between activity 
time and cost to be linear to reduce the difficulty in solving the TCTP. Meyer and Shaffer (1963), 
Patterson and Huber (1974) suggest to use mixed integer programming technique to handle the 
TCTPs that have discrete and linear relationship between time and costs.  
Project execution is full of many uncertain factors. Examples include unforeseen bad weather that 
causes the interruption of the project progress; the project owner may request scope change and 
the change order due to unknown job site conditions. Even the labor and the equipment have 
inherent variability. The materials may be delivered irregularly or not sufficient materials when 
needed. The simplest method to process the uncertainties is to use Monte Carlo simulation (Diaz 
and Hadipriono, 1993). Other approaches also include probabilistic TCTP or the combination of 
both deterministic and probabilistic TCTP (Laslo, 2003; Ke and Liu 2005) and heuristic methods 
(Daisy et al. 2004, Yang 2006). Yang (2006) introduces PSO heuristic in the project crashing 
analysis. Total minimized project costs can be found efficiently under specific time window 
should the activities be crashed in three forms: discrete, piecewise-linear, and non-linear.  
 
3. Research Methodology 
The successful implementation of PSO heuristic into project crashing analysis with various types 
of activity encourages the authors to further the study into the analysis of optimal project time 
and costs by performing the combined Monte Carlo simulation and PSO heuristic method. This 
combined method is executed according to the following three steps.

In the beginning, xij and the velocity vectors are generated randomly. Equation (6) is composed 
of three parts: the inertia of particle’s previous behavior, as indicated by inertia weight ω; the 
cognitive consistency part, as indicated by fine-tuning the position (solution) based on the current 
position and the best fitness value of the particle; the social influence part, as indicated by the 
imitating behavior of the particles. Φi equals to c1*rand() and φg equals to c2*rand(), where c1 
and c2 are accelerating constants; rand() stands for the random number generated from the 
uniform distribution between [0,1]. Equations (7), (8) and (9) update the position and velocity 
vectors. 
 
Since the generated velocity vectors may be too large to control, a maximum velocity Vmax is 
defined to limit the range of the velocity vectors. In addition, the xij value should be kept 
between 0 and 1; therefore, the value of Vmax cannot be too large to reduce the solving power of 
the algorithm and the slower convergence. 
 
4. Check the stop criterion 
In order for the algorithm to better approximate the optimal value, the number of iterations plays 
a crucial role. Here the number of iterations is the stop criterion and more iteration is preferred; 
however, the efficiency may be compromised. Various iteration choices can be experimented to 
compare the convergence speed.

4.1 Case Analysis 
The completion probability, as seen in Table 1, shows the chance of successful completion under 
certain time/cost constraint. Often, the project performer is more concerned about the profit 
margin, but overlooks the risk of its inability to carry out the project under tight time constraint. It 
is understandable that the more resources being put in the project, the faster the project can be 
carried out. However, as can be seen from Table, there is a region where hardly any time/cost 
combination can generate higher completion probability, or higher chance of success completion 
of project. This probability matrix can be further divided into three regimes: the first regime with 
cell values very close to zero and it indicates the situation that hardly these time/cost 
combinations should exist since the success chance is minimal; the second regime includes the 
cell value between (>0) and 0.6 and it indicates the success chance varies a lot from minimal to 
improving. Finally, the third regime includes cell values over 0.6 and it indicates very high 
success chance and promising to complete the project within these time/cost combinations. 
Therefore in the studied project, in order to have a successful and promising result, the time 
should be above 280 days and the costs should be above 39,100 thousand NT. 
 
5. Conclusion and Recommendation 
The study proposes a framework incorporating the PSO heuristic and Monte Carlo simulation to 
analyze the project completion probabilities among various time/cost constraints. From the 
numerical case study, it can be found that in the most cases, the project direct costs from PSO 
under time constraint are in the lower completion probability regime. Often the project direct 
costs derived from PSO are usually very optimistic in executing the projects.
