摘要
TiC 作為增強相添加於工具鋼等金屬基材，得以提升金屬基複合材料(MMC)之磨耗性及
強度等機械性質。本計劃嘗試利用機械合金法使 Ti 與奈米碳粉在遠低於傳統製程溫度下，
以行星式球磨使原料粉末在固態反應製備原生 TiC。研究結果顯示，球磨 Ti50C50於球磨轉
速 400rpm 及球磨時間 8 小時以上，當球磨能量累積達到足以反應時，Ti 與 C 生成 TiC 之
反應瞬間發生，可成功製備出細緻且球形的原生 TiC，其機構為機械誘發自蔓延反應
(MSR)，即以機械合金法使原料粉末在瞬間反應為化合物。本研究接著探討基地相球磨處理
後對於增強相分佈之影響，並分別使用真空燒結及熱均壓製程製備工具鋼-TiC 複合材料，
觀察試片顯微組織及增強相的分佈情形。經由熱均壓後之工具鋼-TiC 複合材料，其緻密性
良好且基地相與增強相接合情形優異，顯示熱均壓製程能有效接合異質材料。而利用以機
械合金法製作的原生 TiC，與經過球磨之後的工具鋼粉能夠有效提升 TiC 分散的效果，真
空燒結所形成之複合材料，即使 TiC 添加量高達 32.5wt% (43vol%)的分佈情形仍相當良
好，以此複合粉末接續進行熱均壓，可得到性質更優異的複合材料。
關鍵詞：原生 TiC、機械合金法、工具鋼、金屬基複合材料、熱均壓
ABSTRACT
TiC is often added to improve wear-resistant properties and hardness of tool steels and
metal matrix composite (MMC). In present study, synthesized TiC is formed via mechanical
alloying (MA) by planetary ball milling Ti and nano-carbon powders. It is observed that very
fine and spherical in-situ TiC powders can be synthesized by high energy ball milling longer than
8hr at 400 rpm. According to XRD analyses, TiC is formed at an instant via a mechanically
induced self-propagating reaction (MSR). The accumulated mechanical energy attains a critical
value to trigger such synthesizing reaction. To manufacture TiC containing composites, processes
including sintering under vacuum and hot isostatic pressing (HIP) are surveyed. Excellent
densification and good interface between refinement and matrix are observed in HIPped
composite. The in-situ TiC and tool steel powders are mixed and ball milled before sintering
under vacuum. It is found the fine in-situ TiC particles distribute uniformly with the ball milled
tool steel powders. The amount of TiC can be added up to 32.5wt%(43vol.%). The properties of
vacuum-sintered composites are expected to further improve using HIP process.
Keywords: in-situ TiC, mechanical alloying, tool steel, metal matrix composite, hot isostatic
pressing

粉末冶金法為目前使用最廣泛的方法，主要是將 TiC 粉末直接與氣噴或水噴的鐵基粉
末混合之後，以冷均壓、真空燒結[11]或熱均壓[12]的方式來製造。此種製程可以用相對
於熔鑄製程較低的溫度來進行燒結，且 TiC 增強相之添加量以及尺寸亦較容易控制，添加
量甚至可以達到 50%體積百分比，並可經由混粉製程來確保均勻的混合，為目前商用 TiC-
鐵基複合材料的主要製造方式。
利用粉末冶金製備之金屬基複合材料，由於其增強相添加量較高，因此具有極高硬度
的性質，且可以直接形成近淨形(near-net-shape)的成品，但往往由於粉末之尺寸與密度
差異限制其生產，使增強相如 TiC 粉末偏粗，與母材結合性稍差，脆性因此較高。商用的
TiC 強化工具鋼的生產成本，也會受到特殊設備與製程技術以及原料，如 TiC 粉末的影響
因而偏高。
本研究係以原生之方式製作 TiC，其製程包括化學反應、自蔓延高溫合成(SHS,
Self-propagating High temperature Synthesis)、機械合金法(MA, Mechanical Alloying)
等。機械合金法是將原料粉末經由高能量機械研磨，製備均質合金粉末之技術，其應用包
含平衡相、非平衡相(如：過飽和固溶體、準結晶、非晶質、奈米晶等)、複合材料等。此
技術藉由機械研磨粉末，使粉末反覆混合、冷焊、破裂，產生之能量使其活性化引發化學
反應，因此可在室溫或遠低於一般製程溫度下操作，為固態反應製程。由於上述特性，機
械合金法可製備出傳統製程不易製備之合金或化合物[8]。Ma 等人[14]利用機械合金法成
功製備出原生(In-situ)Al4C3，即以 Al 與 C 經由球磨，使其在固態反應製程下生成 Al4C3。
本計劃即利用機械合金法製備原生TiC，並以粉末冶金法作為工具鋼-原生TiC之實驗製程。
四、研究方法

製程處理後，燒結體的相分佈及性質。

四.3. 燒結製程
四.3.1. 真空燒結製程
由於原始工具鋼粉末為球形，且粉末粒度與原生 TiC 粉末差異大，此現象對於粉末咬
合與分散較不利，為改善此問題，本計劃使用不銹鋼之球磨罐(50ml)與磨球(10mm)以 BPR
10：1，球磨轉速 450 rpm，球磨時間 2小時經由球磨將其由球形改變為多角形粉末並降低
粉末粒度。將混合後之工具鋼-TiC 粉末添加 4wt% PVA，以研缽將其研磨後，以 200mesh
標準篩過篩，將過篩後之粉末以 40mm×6mm 模穴之模具進行壓胚，其壓力為 2400MPa 持壓 5
分鐘。冷壓後之生胚進行真空燒結，燒結溫度為 1250℃，持溫一小時後爐冷，爐內真空度
控制在 4.0×10-5~5.0×10-5torr 之間。
四.3.2. 熱均壓製程
本計劃在熱均壓製程部分所使用之基地相粉末為未球磨之工具鋼粉，將混合後之工具
鋼-TiC 粉末進行封罐及熱均壓處理，首先將混合後之粉末裝入罐體中，在粉末填充後進行
封蓋焊接，接著將罐體抽真空，達到 10-3torr 以下之真空度後將抽氣管以油壓封口，避免
氣體滲入，最後將封罐完成之罐體進行熱均壓製程。其中本實驗所使用之熱均壓機為 Avure
QIH-21 型，封罐時真空度為 10-2torr，熱均壓條件則為 1250℃，120MPa 持溫持壓 2 小時。

六、結論
1. 本計劃利用機械合金法，以行星式混練機將 Ti 與 C 在固態反應製程下，使用 10：
1 的 BPR，並控制球磨轉速及球磨時間兩項製程參數，在球磨轉速 400rpm、球磨
時間超過 8小時後成功製備出原生 TiC 粉末。
2. 由 Ti50C50之 XRD 分析結果顯示，當球磨能量累積至足以發生反應之活化能，反應
在瞬間發生，其機構類似機械誘發自蔓延反應(MSR)，即以球磨之應變能觸發初使
反應，接著以串聯方式使原料粉末在瞬間完全反應成化合物。
3. 經由球磨後之 Ti50C50 粉末，隨球磨時間增加，其粒徑經過冷焊、破碎與精練等三
階段，在長時間球磨發生 TiC 反應之後，使原本的角形顆粒產生近似球形的最終
顆粒。
4. 工具鋼-TiC 複合粉末經由熱均壓製程所得之複合材料表面無明顯空孔，表示其緻
密性良好，且基地相與增強相之介面接合情況優異，顯示熱均壓製程可有效接合
異質材料。
5. 工具鋼粉經過球磨處理後粉末粒度可有效降低，將其與原生 TiC 粉末混合並進行
真空燒結，即使當 TiC 添加量高達 32.5wt%(43vol%)，仍能有良好分佈情形。由
上述結果預期若使用此複合粉末，經由熱均壓製程後可得到性能更優異之複合材
料。
6. 利用機械合金法製備原生 TiC，可使製程步驟簡化，而粉末冶金法製備複合材料
亦可降低製程溫度，因此結合原生反應與粉末冶金之優點，能夠有效降低生產難
度，進而減少生產成本。

Mechanical Properties of TiC-Strengthened Cr/V Containing Tool Steels
J.K.Chen1*, S.F.Chan2, M.Y.Liu3, S.H.Chang3

ABSTRACT
Two different sizes (~2um and ~20um) of TiC powders are added to Cr and V containing steel
powders to form metal matrix composite tool steels. Mixtures of 0~25wt% TiC and steel powders are
encapsulated and hot isostatically pressed for densification. Their density and mechanical properties
are characterized. Product densification of over 99.9% theoretical density is achieved. Its hardness is
found to increase with the amount of TiC addition. Product with over HRc65 hardness is obtained with
addition of 25 wt% coarse TiC particles in which the matrix and strengthening particles intermix
uniformly. The fine TiC particles distributed as packs at the inter-particle regions of the matrix phase
and give less hardening effects. The flexural strength has shown to be controlled by crack initiation
and less by crack propagation, therefore it reduces with TiC addition which causes stress concentration
in microstructure. For impact test, brittle fracture is induced by high strain rate which initiates the
cracks with ease in both matrix and hard phases. However, fine particles are more effective in
blocking crack tip propagation under high strain rate. Therefore, the impact toughness increases
slightly with TiC.
INTRODUCTION
Titanium carbide strengthened tool steel has, since the 1970s, been characterized as having good
combined thermal stability and mechanical properties [1]. Many products, such as FERROTiC™,
TiCALLOY™, and FERRO-TITANIT™, are developed and commercialized [2]. The processing
routes of TiC-strengthened steels include powder metallurgy, conventional melting and casting, and
in-situ production of dispersoids [3], etc. Among these, powder metallurgy by hot isostatic pressing
(HIP [4] has been most widely adopted [5]-[6]. Up to 45vol.% of TiC can be embedded in high alloy
cooling.

Although different both in the amount of TiC addition and in cleavage size, the difference of flexural

wt% coarse TiC containing sample has the lowest flexural strength of all. It may be explained by

CONCLUSION
Effects of TiC size and fraction on microstructures and the mechanical properties of a Cr/V containing
composite tool steel are presented. Fine TiC particles distribute as packs within the inter-particle
regions of the matrix phase to reduce packing porosity, whereas the coarse TiC particles distribute
uniformly among matrix phase. Although the hardness of composite increases with TiC additions, the
hardening effects are quite different for fine and coarse TiC particles. Distribution of fine TiC particle
packs is similar to large TiC particles distributed at larger spacing, therefore the increase of hardness is
not as much as that provided by coarse TiC particles. Ratio of matrix and dispersoid particle size
apparently plays an important role in hardness performance.
In three point bending tests, flexural strength drops greatly due to TiC additions, because TiC induces
brittle fractures while non-TiC added specimen demonstrates ductile fracture. Crack propagation
appears to be relatively easy in TiC added composite, therefore the flexural strengths are less sensitive
than hardness to the fraction of TiC particles.
All four composites bear low impact energy, since both matrix and hard phases demonstrate brittle
fracture in high strain rate testing condition. It is observed that the addition of fine TiC particles
increases the impact energies more effectively than coarse particles. The higher total particle cross
section area in fine particle added specimens should provide more barriers to crack tip propagation and
thus demonstrate higher impact energies.
