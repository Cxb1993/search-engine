easily, making a portable MS analyzing system with low power consumption.  
This paper presents a compact, low-cost and high efficient system for generating 
atmospheric pressure DBD plasma utilizing high-frequency pulse DC electric field in 
a micro-device. The miniaturized DBD plasma system is used as the ion source for 
mass spectrometry analyzing of biosamples. The discharge performance and the ion 
intensity stability of the proposed DBD plasma system are experimentally evaluated 
using commercial MS equipment. Solid biosample of garlic slice and gas sample of a 
commercial perfume are used to test the performance of the proposed system. 
 
2 Experimental 
 
Figure 1 shows the experimental setup for the proposed miniaturized DBD 
generator for MS applications. The DBD plasma device was powered by a home-built 
electronic circuit composed of a coil (1:200) as the voltage transformer, a bipolar 
junction transistor as the pulse generator, a function generator for frequency control 
and a DC power supply. The DBD plasma generator was made with a quartz tube 
with the dimension of 1.5 mm (I.D.) x 3.0 mm (O.D.) x 45 mm (length). A copper wire 
with 1 mm in diameter was used as the inner discharge electrode and a 200 m thick 
copper foil outside the quartz tube was used as the outer discharge electrode. The 
length for generating the plasma discharge was 10 mm. Helium, argon, nitrogen or 
air can either be used as the carrier gases for plasma generation with the proposed 
system. The frequency and the power range for generating the plasma discharge 
were 24-40 kHz and 0.6-2.4 W, respectively. A sample chamber was placed in front 
of the plasma generator for sample loading and connected to the inlet port of the MS 
machine. For practical MS appellations, the operation conditions for the plasma 
generator were set at a pulse frequency of 34 kHz, a DC voltage of 2 V, a helium flow 
rate of 4 cc/min, resulting a power consumption of 1.36 W. Figure 2 shows a photo 
picture for the experimental setup of the DBD-MS system. A commercial available 
MS machine (Quattro Ultima, Micromass, U.K.) was used in the current study for the 
proposed DBD-MS detection.
