Abstract 
    In this research, the effect of equal channel angular extrusion (ECAE) on microstructures 
and mechanical properties of the as cast magnesium alloy ZA85 (Mg-8wt%Zn-5wt%Al) with the 
initial grain size of about 150µm was investigated. The microstructures were examined by optical 
microscopy (OM) and scanning electron microscopy (SEM). Rockwell Indenter and Instron 8501 
were used for mechanical tests and superplastic behavior. 
    During ECAE, the mechanism of grain refinement was dynamic recrystallization. At higher 
ECAE temperature of 250℃?, the average grain size was reduced to 13.5µm by two-passes. 
However, with the further passes up to four, the average grain size was increased to 19.7µm due 
to the grain growth of high temperature effect. At lower ECAE temperature of 180℃?, the grains 
were refined significantly from 150µm to 4.2µm by six-passes.  
Both the strength and ductility of ZA85 alloy could be remarkably improved by ECAE. The 
best parameter of this research was ECAE at 180℃? through six-passes. The hardness of the as 
cast alloy was promoted from HRB 19 to HRB 46. The UTS at room temperature were increased 
from 175MPa to 402MPa. The elongation was also increased from 2.3% to 6.4%. At high 
temperature tensile tests, the UTS and elongation at 200℃? were increased from 105MPa and 
5.1% to 249MPa and 28.5%, respectively. Furthermore, the ZA85 alloy after ECAE showed 
superplastic behavior at 250℃? with the strain rate of 10-4/s. The elongation could be reached to 
185%. When testing at 300℃?, the elongation of 125% and 400% could be obtained at the strain 
rate of 10-3/s and 10-4/s, respectively.  
From the results of the present study, ZA85 alloy has better high-temperature properties than 
that of the commercial AZ and AM alloys. Besides, ZA85 alloy possesses superplastic behavior at 
250℃?. Consequently, ZA85 alloy has a good potential for automobile parts application.
