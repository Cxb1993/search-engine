Keywords?Millimeter wave, Low Temperature Cofired Ceramic, LTCC, System-On-
Package, Filter
    A thorough lecture and research review of milimeter-wave system on package (SOP) and
waveguide structures was conducted during the project period. Non-radiation modes of embedded
LTCC dielectric waveguides were employed to realize the filters. The permittivity characteristics
of available LTCC material systems were evaluated covering a frequency range till about 30 GHz,
and a kind of material characterization fixture was also developed to extend the frequency range.
The transition structures for exciting LSE01 and LSM01 modes were proposed and the detail 3D
electromagnetic analysis confirmed their performance. These structures were successfully
integrated in the synthesized filter structure. For filter synthesis theory, the mathematical
relationships between resonant frequencies of direct-coupled synchronous or asynchronous
resonators and their coupling coefficient were derived with the aids of equivalent π-network and
T-network. With the aids of measured or simulated data, these models can further relate the
coupling coefficient and corresponding physical space between coupled resonators. Then these
mathematical models were applied to synthesize the desired filter responses. A multilayer DECT
ceramic filter with frequency response similar to elliptical type was designed and realized with
good theoretical and experimental consistency.
