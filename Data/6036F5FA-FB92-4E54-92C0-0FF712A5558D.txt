微細化的 3C 製品，此種人工研磨方式會衍生出品質不穩定的問題。因此，本研究將鎂合金置於有
機酸溶液中，進行浸泡、超音波震盪或化學機械研磨三種方式處理，比較三種處理方式後的移除量、
表面光學性質及表面形貌，並討論有機酸和鎂合金間的反應機制。 
 
研究目的 
在鎂合金成型產業急遽發展的同時，其成型件表面處理技術的改良亦同樣地重要，先前吾人已
提過關於成型件作出後必須進行人工研磨與整平的步驟，以及化成處理、化學鍍/電鍍處理或陽極
處理均需要在平整的物件表面進行，才能增加表面處理與後續塗裝的均勻性、平滑性與光澤性，而
產生良好的物件品質。然而，傳統的人工研磨方式需仰賴大量的人力，耗時又有品質不穩定的問題，
並且研磨過程所產生的金屬粒子具有易燃性，不但危害作業環境，更對人體有極大的傷害。有研究
者以噴沙機（Blast）試圖達成成型件表面平整的目的，但是其效果有限，而且達不到量產所需之
自動化要求。因此，本研究利用化學法進行鎂合金表面前處理，以無鉻、無氟、低腐蝕性的有機酸
溶液進行鎂合金表面整平、淨化及亮化的步驟，由於有機酸溶液無危害環境及人體之虞，鎂合金置
於溶液中又可解決研磨粉塵的問題；另外，加入可自動化操作的機械式加工系統，包含超音波震盪
清洗以及機械研磨處理，可在前處理程序中，達到更好的整平、淨化及亮化之效果；同時，提升處
理品質的穩定性，達到縮短處理時間與自動化生產等目的。另外，此製程對於量產鎂合金的連續自
動化具有相當大的潛力。

ABSTRACT 
The anodic characteristics of copper in phosphoric acid were studied by linear 
sweep method. A combined model including acceptor and salt film views was 
proposed to explain copper electropolishing at the limiting current. In addition to a 
diffusion layer, CuH2PO4 was found in the resistive film during copper 
electropolishing in 85 wt% phosphoric acid by XPS with Ar+ etching technique. This 
passive layer could protect depressions more than protrusions on copper surface 
because more copper ions accumulated due to a steric hindrance effect and thicker 
CuH2PO4 formed at depressions. Moreover, the dissociation of polymeric phosphoric 
acid depending on the amount of copper ions might be important to a fine 
electropolishing. Because more copper ions were restrained in depressions than in 
protrusions, more phosphate-containing anions would be dissociated from polymeric 
phosphoric acid to form passive salt products, which consumed more H2O. Thus, the 
protective action of this passive layer at depressions was higher than at protrusions, 
and less H2O existing in depressions than in protrusions could hydrate copper ions. 
The microscopic difference between protrusions and depressions results in an 
electropolishing with high performance.      
 
Electrochemical planarization of copper in phosphoric acid  
One is a diffusion-limited transport of acceptor species to the anode. The 
acceptor species would complex with the copper ions at the anode, enabling their 
transport toward the bulk solution. Many investigators suggest that the acceptor 
species are water [7-9], but some researchers found the acceptor species might be the 
anions in electrolyte [10].  
Another important electropolishing model is related to the diffusion-limited 
transport through solid films on anodes. In 1979, Ismail proposed a mass-transfer 
anodic film model for surface brightening in phosphoric acid. In their model, surface 
brightness is obtained when the mass-transfer rate for the dissolution products is 
exceeded, causing oxide formation at the anode [11]. A similar mechanism was 
proposed upon dissolution the concentration of copper ions increases at the anode 
until the solubility limit of a copper salt, at which point a salt film precipitates on the 
anode instead of oxide [12, 13]. 
Some researchers proposed the brightening seems to occur when the surface is 
covered by a protective layer, salt film or viscous liquid, controlling the rate of 
dissolution [14]. This view is close to Jacquet’s view. However, the composition of 
this protective layer is very complex, with various authors proposing a copper 
hydroxy salt [15], or copper phosphate [16] as probable compositions in Cu-H3PO4 
system.  
The electropolishing mechanisms of copper in 85 wt% phosphoric acid have 
been extensively studied using electrochemical techniques and in-situ or ex-situ 
surface characterization techniques such as optical microscopy, ellipsometry, X-ray 
photoelectron spectroscopy, Auger electron spectroscopy and impedance 
spectroscopy. However, in addition to the uncertainty concerning the transport-limited 
species, debate persists in the presence of a film on the anode surface. Some 
investigators claim that a film does not exist, while the others have reported evidence

CONCLUSIONS 
 
This study shows a dual-resistive film covered on copper surface when copper 
was electropolished with phosphoric acid in the limiting current plateau. One of the 
resistive films is the diffusion layer, and the other is the passive layer. As the applied 
potential increased to the region of oxygen evolution, the oxygen bubbles would 
destroy the resistive film locally, but the passive layer still partly covered on copper 
surface to produce the difference of reaction rates between protrusions and 
depressions. Therefore, a bright and smooth surface could be obtained, even though 
the pits would be found on copper. In addition, the dissociation of polymeric 
phosphoric acid highly depended on the amount of copper ions, or the position of 
protrusions and depressions in 85 wt% phosphoric acid than low-concentration one. 
Thus, copper electropolishing in 85 wt% phosphoric acid obtained better quality. In
