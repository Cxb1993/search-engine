加多酚類化合物於甜菜色素中，已發
現有類似的色素穩定效果，可能也具
有與花青素共色作用相似的保色機
制，但目前仍不明確。近年研究指出
在 pH 4及 pH 6下添加苯甲酸或兒茶
素於火龍果皮甜菜色素中，經色素加
熱再生後，比起添加香豆素、漂木酸、
肉桂酸及沒食子酸具有較高的色素殘
留量，而添加某些酚類化合物如兒茶
素，在火龍果皮色素會產生類似共色
作用的效果，推測貯藏期間形成甜菜
色素-兒茶素複合物，因而增加了甜菜
色素的貯藏穩定性 (蕭 2009)。但酚類
對於台灣藜甜菜色素是否也有如此的
保色效果，目前尚無資料。
本研究以紅色台灣藜為原料，分
別就添加不同種類酚類化合物，且於
不同 pH值模擬系統，探討對於台灣藜
甜菜色素貯藏穩定性。進一步添加屬
酚類化合物之天然花青素，期望能提
升台灣藜甜菜色素的應用性及改善商
品價值。

Materials and methods 
1. Sample preparation 
Betanin from pitaya peel was extracted by solvents and then concentrated 
by evaporatory vacuum and freeze dried. The pigment powder were subjected 
to a model system with pH 4 or pH 6 (0.1 M citric acid, 0.2 M Na2HPO4). After 
added with the phenolics with different structure for 1 hour at 25℃, pigment 
were heated at 80℃ for 1 hr and regenerated at 4℃ for 24 hours and then 
storage at 15℃ and 35℃. Their changes in color qualities, pigment retention, 
half-live, sulfide resistance, and antioxidant capacities were investigated.   
 
2. Colour Measurement (Tsai & Huang, 2004) 
The wavelength of the pigment samples was measured by a Hitachi 
spectrophotometer U-2001 (HITACHI, Tokyo, Japan). The betanin content was 
estimated by absorbance at 538 nm (A538) and the A538 of original system is A0，
while the A538  after heating and regeneration were A. The pigment retention % 
were calculated as A/ A0 *100 
3. sulfide resistance of betanin 
The pigment powder was subjected to a pH 4 model system with SO2 (0.005M). 
After added with the different kinds of phenolics and storage at 25℃, the 
changes of half-life of the pigment was investigated. 
4. HPLC analysis 
Samples were filtered by 0.45 µm PTFE (polytetrafluoroethylene) membrane 
and 20 µl of the filtrate were injected to HPLC (Kanner, 2001). The condition of 
HPLC was as followings: Column : Lichrosorb RP-18 ( 250 × 4.6 mm ) 
Results and Discussion 
1. Change of color (by pigment retention %, half life of pigment) 
The color changes of pitaya peel pigment with different phenolics 
addition were accessed on the basis of the pigment color after regeneration. 
As illustrated in Figure 1, the pigment residue of pH 4 samples was highest 
in catechin (26.48%) and benzoic acid (17.71%), while that of control were 
16.25% only after storage at 15℃ for 95 days. They were much lower if 
stored at 35℃. For example, after 10 days storage, the pigment residue was 
18.83, 13.74 and 13.29% for catechin, benzoic acid and control samples. 
According to Cai et al., (1985), the half life (t1/2) of betacyanin of amaranthus 
was 23.3 months in form of dried powder when compared with that in form of 
water solution (1.04 months). But if stored at 4℃, they were 77.9 and 14.6 
months respectively. Apparently, low temperature favors the mantainence of 
betacyanin. The similar trends were observed in the pH 6 samples. One 
exception was observed in the chlorogenic acid samples. They showed a 
high A530 and turned to be yellow color. This is similar to the thermal 
induced high A520 in mulberry heating system samples (Tsai et al., 2005).  
    As to the effect of polyphenolics (Table 1), catechin samples showed the 
highest half-life of pigment followed by benzoic acid and coumaric acid 
samples. For example, the half-lif were 93.30, 67.39, 65.21 and 59.48 days in 
the pH 4, 15℃ samples for catechin acid, courmaric acid and control samples. 
This is more evident in the pH 6 samples (25.66 and 12.80 days for catechin 
and control).  
   The bleaching effect of sulfate on betacyanin was also investigated in a 
model system with 0.005 M sulfate at pH 4. As shown in Figure 2, all 
polyphenolics addition increased the half life of pitaya pigment system. 
Especially for the catecinin (66.47 hrs when compared with 52.39 hrs that of 
control). This is similar with the copigment effect of polyphenols on the 
anthocyanin resistance to sulfate bleaching (Tsai et al., 2008).  
changes in FRAP can e observed. Therefore, no significant correlation 
between changes of pigment and FRAP of pitaya peel exsisted. The FRAP 
might not be attributed to the pigment of pitaya peel. Tsai et al., (2008) 
mentioned the increase of FRAP in the anthocyanin-polyphenol system was 
majorly come from the phenolic compounds. It seems the same situation for 
the betanin-polyphenol system in this study. 
 
Conclusion 
The addition of catechin significantly increase the pigment retention, 
longest half-live and the best SO2 resistance of betanin During storage. It 
confirmed the possibility to stabilize this natural pigment in food products by 
adding natural polyphenols. HPLC confirmed the existence of a new 
compound in the catechin samples, which might be a betanin-catechin 
complex and their existence was confirmed through LC/MS. The pigment 
retention of betanin and betanin-catechin was increased as catechin 
concentration increased.
