Abstract 
In this project, we design a human behavior 
understanding system under the conditions of 
mutual occlusion between those interactive 
people. One problem to recognize human 
behavior is how to properly segment human 
body into meaningful body parts such as the

challenging due to the ambiguities caused by

properly segment body parts, say

Finally, a human behavior recognition

applied to understand the interaction among

Abstract: 
Given the rapid expansion of car ownership worldwide, 
vehicle safety is an increasingly critical issue in the automobile 
industry. The reduced cost of cameras and optical devices has 
made it economically feasible to deploy front-mounted 
intelligent systems for visual-based event detection. Prior to 
vehicle event detection, detecting vehicles robustly in real time is 
challenging, especially conducting detection process in images 
captured by a dynamic camera. Therefore, in this paper, a 
robust vehicle detector is developed. Our contribution is 
three-fold. Road modeling is first proposed to confine detection 
area for maintaining low computation complexity and reducing 
false alarms as well. Haar-like features and eigencolors are then 
employed for the vehicle detector. To tackle the occlusion 
problem, chamfer distance is used to estimate the probability of 
each individual vehicle. AdaBoost algorithm is used to select 
critical features from a combined high dimensional feature set. 
Experiments on an extensive dataset show that our proposed 
system can effectively detect vehicles under different lighting 
and traffic conditions, and thus demonstrates its feasibility in 
real-world environments. 
Keywords: Vehicle detection, Haar-like feature, eigencolor 
1. Introduction 
Car ownership is expanding rapidly worldwide, further 
raising the importance of vehicle safety. The reduced cost of 
cameras and optical devices has made it economically 
feasible to deploy front-mounted intelligent systems for 
visual-based event detection. Prior to vehicle event detection, 
detecting vehicles robustly in real time is challenging, 
especially conducting detection process in images captured 
by a dynamic camera. While innovative and light-weight 
applications have been developed for mobile platforms, the 
relative lack of computing power on mobile devices still 
poses an important obstacle to tasks related to video signal 
processing, such as vehicle detection. The increasing maturity 
of mobile device hardware and newly developed operating 
systems (e.g. Android) have put a new focus on this issue, 
particularly on real-time applications. Therefore, in this paper, 
we investigate in dynamic vehicle detection on mobile 
devices in real time. Dynamic vehicle detection involves two 
major steps: feature extraction and classification. The feature 
extraction process derives a set of features from the video 
stream. The second step analyzes the extracted features in 
order to make an appropriate classification decision. A 
variety of machine learning classification techniques have 
been investigated for surveillance tasks, e.g., support vector 
machines [13], naïve Bayes classification [14], and AdaBoost 
[5]. AdaBoost is especially suitable for such tasks since it has 
achieved high detection rates in real-time using simple 
Haar-like features. 
To the best of our knowledge, few approaches are 
proposed for dynamic vehicle detection on mobile devices. 
The limited computing resources make real-time vehicle 
detection a challenging problem. In addition, to robustly 
detect vehicles, noises from cluttered environment, such as 
traffic signs, shadows and buildings, should be prevented 
from detection. Therefore, in the literature, some researches 
focus on vehicle detection based on shadow features [1], 
corner detection [2], edge based [3], and Haar-like features 
[4]. Weak features such as Haar-like features have been 
proved to be effective for object detection [5]. With applying 
integral image, Haar-like features can be computed in an 
efficient manner. However, detecting vehicles may generate
