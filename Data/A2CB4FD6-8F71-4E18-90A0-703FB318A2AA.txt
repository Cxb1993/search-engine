IV
Abstract
The causes of cardiovascular diseases are highly related to the change of physiological
parameters. Stroke can be resulted from unstable cerebral blood flow due to the cerebral
autoregulation (CA) and baroreflex mechanisms being unable to work in effect. CA and baroreflex
are important mechanisms for protection in human body. Stroke might influence the coordinated
ability (metastability) of the brain and it is still unclear. Therefore, if the related physiological
parameters cab be monitored as well to make the coupling among metastability, CA and baroreflex
clear, it would be helpful for diagnosing of stroke in clinical practice. The main purpose of this
research is to integrate the analysis of brain wave, blood pressure, cerebral blood flow and heart rate
using linear and nonlinear approaches, such as Hilbert-Huang spectrum, delay vector variance,
cross-correlation function, baroreflex sensitivity, independence of complexity and independence of
predictability, to evaluate the coupling relationship among metastability, CA and baroreflx for
developing a quantitative assessment system. For the present now, the results of blood pressure and
cerebral blow velocity values in stroke are higher than those in healthy persons (p<0.05). Low
frequency power of blood pressure and cerebral blood flow velocity are also decreased more than
those in healthy subjects (p<0.05), it might be the effect of sympathetic nerve system in stroke
patients. Max CCF values in healthy subjects are higher than those in stroke subjects (p<0.05), it
might indicate the correlation between blood pressure and cerebral blood flow velocity are higher in
healthy persons. Baroreflex analysis by using CCF between stroke and healthy subjects indicate
max CCF index is also significantly different (p<0.05). It might reveal the different regulation
function of baroreflex. The nonlinear parameter, K2, stands for “chaoticness”. K2 values of cerebral 
flow velocity and mean heart rate in stroke subjects are higher than those in healthy subjects
significantly (p<0.05), it might indicate cerebral flow and heart rate in stroke are more chaotic. The
results of linearity by using DVV analysis can indicate the change of MABP difference (p<0.05)
between healthy and stroke patients.

三、文獻探討
1981年Akselrod等人發現心率變異之頻譜分析結果可以反映出交感與副交感神經的作
用情形[1]，即時心率變異分析便廣泛的應用於臨床檢測及研究上，如 Pomeranz 等人於 1985
年發現，心率變異之頻譜分析結果會隨著姿勢的改變而變化[2]，這是因為站立與平躺時，
自律神經系統調控作用的方式不同。而 De Boer 等人[3]提出一描述心跳與心跳間變動的數
學模型，此模型加入血壓對心率變化之影響。此模型包含血壓對心率的感壓反射(Baroreflex)
控制及呼吸對血壓影響，並解釋了心率與血壓產生 LF 與 HF 變動原因。2007 年 Hess 等
人研究亦顯示感壓反射靈敏度(baroflex sensitivity)在血壓正常的健康人中靈敏度較高，而血
壓較低者，心率變異分析值較高[4]，由此可知感壓反射的狀況可作為一判斷中風預兆的一
項生理回饋指標。而在姿勢性低血壓的年長病人中亦發現感壓反射靈敏度作用較低，顯示
血壓的變異與感壓反射的關係[5]，甚至於腦幹的作用都可以用感壓反射靈敏度來評估[6]。
由 De Boer 的研究知道舒張壓及收縮壓可以影響心跳的間隔，舒張壓趨勢經頻譜分析後與
心率變動頻譜僅在 LF 頻帶有相關，而收縮壓趨勢頻譜分析後與心率變動頻譜在 LF 與 HF
頻帶皆有相關。藉由 HRV 的頻譜分析已經增加了解神經控制在竇房節的調控效果，迷走神
經輸出致動主要的促成頻帶在高頻 High Frequency (HF: 0.15-0.5Hz)，而低頻帶 Low
Frequency (LF: 0.04-0.15 Hz)的成分被當作是交感神經調控的指標之一[7]。目前一般認為動
脈壓低頻部份與交感神經活性有關[8-9]，亦有學者們認為低頻成份可能源自中樞神經系統
經交感神經傳出纖維作用於血管的調控[3]。 除此之外血壓的變異與呼吸也有其相關性的存
在，Pitzalis 等人發現，以每分鐘呼吸 6 次、10 次和 16 次，其頻譜不管收縮壓或心率變異
的頻譜，呼吸的速度越慢它相對應的頻率振幅是增加的[10]。這指出緩慢的呼吸並不只是單
純的讓肺的活動力提高。更進一步的是影響到自主神經的功能，而自主神經主要負責全身
血液循環中調控各個臟器與肌肉循環的血管收縮與舒張。更慢速的呼吸速率會導致心臟輸
出量的震盪和收縮壓的震盪；因此必須有更靈敏的感壓反射(Baroreflex)的控制才能夠包容

本評估方式研究成果以線性方法評估心血管系統之調控外，另可以

nonlinear approaches. Moreover, coupling degree of subsystems of
cardiovascular can be also evaluated. By this technique, the correlation
of bio-signals can be observed but the effect of cardiovascular diseases
on physiological regulation also can be evaluated. It can be a way to
offer the reference in clinical practice to help the diagnosis.
