Abstract
The reverse-bias operation of the InGaN 
light-emitting diode (LED) device can reveal 
device reliability problems. This study uses 
optical characterization techniques, 
including surface temperature 
measurements, two-dimensional (2D) x-ray 
fluorescent (XRF) element analysis, 2D 
electroluminescence (EL) images processed 
by Matlab, and electrical measurements to 
visualize thecurrent leakages around the 
metal contact of the device. Connections 
between the device performance 
and the reverse-bias EL current distribution 
have been established. This paper attributes 
the origin ofthe reverse-bias emission to a 
high electric field caused by weak structures 
during process variations.  Hot 
electron-induced emissions due to a leakage 
current may be a mechanism of the  
reverse-bias emission. Furthermore, 
reverse-bias stress on the devices is 
performed on the LED devices to 
investigate reliability issues. The 
reverse-bias light emission is relevant to 
reliability problems because of its 
combination of optical characterization and 
electrical performance. These techniques 
provide a screening tool that wil correlate 
device failures with the fabrication process 
for industrial applications

Fabrication and Characterization of ZnO Nanorods on
Polished Titanium Substrate by
Electrochemical-Hydrothermal Methods
Yih-Min Yeh 1 and Hsiang Chen *2,
1WuFeng University No. 117, Sec 2, Chiankuo Rd, Minhsiung, Chiayi County 62153,
Taiwan, R.O.C.
2National Chi Nan University No. 1 University Road, Puli, Nantou County 54561,

Tel:886-49-6002820 Fax:886-49-2912238 E-mail:hchen@ncnu.edu.tw
ZnO nanorods have been deposited on the polished Ti substrate by
electrochemical-hydrothermal methods. The titanium substrate was first
electro-polished to remove the oxide and obtain an ideal flat Surface. Then, a seed
layer of ZnO was first electrochemically deposited in both ZnO and ZnCl2
solutions. On top of the seed layer, ZnO nanorods were hydrothermally deposited
in Zn(NO3)2 and HMTA solution. To examine the polishing and growth conditions,
atomic force microscope (AFM), X-ray diffraction (XRD) analysis and optical
images under microscope were used to measure the surface roughness, crystalline
structure, and film morphology. Well-crystallized nanorods could be grown on top
of Ti susbtrate with a suitable polishing voltage and sufficient growth time. The
high quality ZnO nanorods on top of Ti can function as photo-catalyst for
biomedical anti-bacteria applications.

Effects of Saccharin Addition on Electrodeposited

Yih-Min Yeh1、Song- Min Liu1 and Hsiang Chen* 2, Chuan Hao Liao2, Chun Wei Lin2

Abstract: CuInS2 with a proper band gap and high absorption rate can function well as an
absorptionlayer in a solar cell. In this research, we fabricated a high quality CuInS2 film on top of the
ITO by a 2-step method of electrodepostion and sulfurization. In contrast to previous studies [1-3],
saccharin was added to improve the microstructures of the Cu-In precursor thin films. With a
proper saccharin concentration and current density, a well-crystallized Cu-In precursor film could be
formed. Then, after the precursor film was sulfurized in a suitable condition, a high-quality CuInS2 film
for an abosoprtion layer in a solar cell could be fabricated.
Introduction : In this study, conducting glasses (ITO) was used as the work electrode to
electrodeposits the Cu-In alloy thin films in a plating solution containing CuCl2, InCl3 solution at a
constant-current. In addition, saccharin was added to the plating solution to improve the
microstructures of the Cu-In thin films. The results reveals that the current density and saccharin
concentration affect the surface morphology of Cu-In alloy thin films. Through X-ray diffraction (XRD)
and scanning electron microscopy (SEM) analysis, we found that when the concentration of the
saccharin was 0.004M and the current density was 1 mA cm‧ -2, the Cu-In alloy thin films, preferred
orientation (200) with well crystalline structure could be obtained. In this condition, the SEM photos
showed that the Cu-In thin films had the least micro cracks and the lowest surface roughness as shown
in Fig. 1 (a), (b), and (c).
