3.4 研究方法 
3.4.1 研究對象 
 為了確保本研究具有足夠的樣本數以符合信度之要求，本研究團隊利用
Chang 等人 20於 1998 年-2008 年針對某航空製造業員工進行前瞻性世代研究所計
算出之高血壓發生率，在暴露組與非暴露組比例為 1：1，顯著水準為 0.05 且檢
力為 80%的條件下，估計出達統計上顯著差異所需之最少樣本數為 1646 人。本
研究團隊藉由台灣區機械工業同業公會之會員名冊取得國內機械設備製造業的
廠商名單後，主動以電話及電子郵件與廠商聯繫，以徵詢產學合作的可能性。在
多次的溝通與協調後，中部地區共有四家機械設備製造業願意參與本研究。因此
本研究以這四家廠商總共 1843 名勞工作為研究對象，在取得廠商及員工同意的
前提下進行本研究。本研究在正式執行前，亦先獲得中國醫藥大學附設醫院人體
試驗委員會的同意與許可才開始進行。我們在對合作廠商及員工舉辦四次的說明
會後，總共有 1028 名勞工自願參加本研究，但在排除 8 名生活習慣問卷填寫不
完整及 18 名進到該公司或該部門前即患有高血壓之勞工後，建立 1002 名勞工完
整的資料檔。 
3.4.2 現場訪視 
 為了瞭解各公司勞工的作業環境概況，本研究團隊使用 TSE-1358 八音度音
頻分析儀和 P-TRAK 超微粒子計數器針對各公司廠區所有製程進行全面性的現
場訪視(Walk Through)，量測每個廠區不同製程之均能音量及粒數濃度。並且蒐
集各公司現場相關資料，包括廠區配置圖、環境監測資料、勞工作業型態及內容、
潛在的危害因子及暴露人數以了解勞工噪音與懸浮微粒的暴露狀況。

3.4.5 相似暴露族群之建立 
 我們根據現場訪視及個人採樣之結果，將所有研究對象之噪音強度與懸浮微
粒質量濃度分佈以中位數為分界值，進行不同暴露類型的分組，包括噪音暴露組
(noise≧80dBA、noise＜80dBA 及非暴露組)、可呼吸性粉塵暴露組
(PMM≧75μg/m3、PMM＜75μg/m3 及非暴露組)及噪音與可呼吸性粉塵共同暴露
(noise≧80dBA and PMM≧75μg/m3、noise＜80dBA and PMM≧75μg/m3、
noise≧80dBA and PMM＜75μg/m3 及非暴露組)。 
 此外，我們也依據環境噪音頻譜分析、懸浮微粒質量濃度及粒數濃度之測量
結果，以所有研究對象在各個噪音頻率(125Hz、250Hz、500Hz、1000Hz、2000Hz、
4000Hz、8000Hz)、不同粒徑大小(PM10、PM2.5、PM1)及總粒數濃度之中位數(分
別是大於或者等於 52dB、59dB、65dB、68dB、68dB、69dB 與 66dB、大於或者
等於 94μg/m3、66μg/m3 與 38μg/m3 以及大於或者等於 241595 P/cm3)為分界值，
將研究對象區分為高暴露組、低暴露組及非暴露組。 
3.4.6 統計分析 
 本研究藉助 EXCEL 2010 版進行研究對象之健康檢查資料、生活習慣問卷資
料、環境與個人噪音值、粒數濃度與質量濃度之建檔，且利用 SAS 9.3 版統計套
裝軟體來分析不同相似暴露族群在人口特性、生活習慣、健康狀況、環境噪音、
個人噪音、粒數濃度及質量濃度是否有顯著差異。對於連續變項我們先使用
Shapiro-Wilk test判斷其是否呈常態分布；如果呈常態分布則使用 t-test和ANOVA

本研究之優點為使用世代研究設計進行研究，避免橫斷式研究設計在探討暴
露與疾病關係的時序性問題，因此可以研究職業噪音與懸浮微粒共同暴露對於高
血壓發生率的因果關係。此外，除了環境噪音與環境懸浮微粒之監測，我們也進
行了個人噪音和個人可呼吸性粉塵的監測；此方法能更清楚瞭解勞工在工作期間
噪音與可呼吸性粉塵暴露程度的真實情況。 
然而本研究屬於回溯性的設計，因此容易發生健康工人效應的情況。即暴露
到高分貝噪音或高濃度懸浮微粒的勞工可能因為得到疾病而死亡、離開公司或調
往低分貝噪音或低濃度懸浮微粒之製程，因而使得本研究結果在頻率 125、250
及 500 Hz 的高噪音暴露組勞工以及高可呼吸性粉塵暴露組勞工相較於低暴露組
勞工有明顯較低的高血壓相對危險比。 
綜合以上的結果，我們認為職業噪音≧80 dBA 和可呼吸性粉塵質量濃度≧
75 μg/m3 共同暴露可能會增加高血壓之風險。單獨暴露於 80 dBA 以上的職業噪
音也可能增加勞工的高血壓發生率。因此我們建議目前國內及國際上對於高噪音
作業環境(大於以及等於 85 dBA)所採取的勞工聽力保護計畫，應該將規範標準降
低至 80 dBA，以避免勞工發生高血壓的風險。此外，對於具有噪音與懸浮微粒
共同暴露之勞工，應該加強監督聽力防護具及呼吸防護具的使用情況，以減少勞
工噪音與懸浮微粒之暴露，來保護勞工的健康。

transient changes of blood pressure, but their combined effects were unclear. 
Objectives This repeated-measures study investigated the single and combined effects

Methods We recruited 33 males and 33 females aged 18-32 years as study subjects.

Introduction 
Epidemiological studies have reported that exposure to road traffic noise, 
particulate matter with aerodynamic diameter less than 2.5 μm (PM2.5) or nitrogen 
oxides (NOx) was associated with the transient effects of blood pressure. Noise 
exposure above 55 dBA may increase systolic blood pressure (SBP) and diastolic 
blood pressure (DBP) in adults aged 18-32 years 25. Short-term exposure to PM2.5 was 
associated with the increased SBP 26, 40. A doubling of NOx exposure during one year 
preceding enrollment was associated with a decreased SBP 41. 
However, combined effects of noise, PM2.5 and NOx on ambulatory blood 
pressure are not clear. The possible biological mechanisms for the effects of PM2.5 on 
blood pressure have been suggested to include local and systemic inflammation and 
oxidative stress, autonomic imbalance and endothelial dysfunction 42-44. Noise may 
activate the autonomic nervous and endocrine systems to produce the irregular 
endocrine and metabolic dysfunction that develop the hypertension 45-46. Since PM2.5 
and noise exposures may affect the same target system, which is the autonomic 
nervous system, it is interested in investigating whether there was a synergistic effect 
due to the combined exposure. Therefore, the purposes of this study were to 
investigate the single and combined effects of noise, PM2.5 and NOx on 24-hour 
ambulatory blood pressure.

regression models.

Introduction 
Epidemiological studies have reported that exposure to road traffic noise, 
particulate matter with aerodynamic diameter less than 2.5 μm (PM2.5) or nitrogen 
oxides (NOx) was associated with the transient effects of blood pressure. Noise 
exposure above 55 dBA may increase systolic blood pressure (SBP) and diastolic 
blood pressure (DBP) in adults aged 18-32 years (Chang et al. 2009). Short-term 
exposure to PM2.5 was associated with the increased SBP (Brook et al. 2011; Dvonch 
et al. 2009). A doubling of NOx exposure during one year preceding enrollment was 
associated with a decreased SBP (Sorensen et al. 2012). 
However, combined effects of noise, PM2.5 and NOx on ambulatory blood 
pressure are not clear. The possible biological mechanisms for the effects of PM2.5 on 
blood pressure have been suggested to include local and systemic inflammation and 
oxidative stress, autonomic imbalance and endothelial dysfunction (Brook and 
Rajagopalan 2009; Brook et al. 2010; Mills and Snabre 2009). Noise may activate the 
autonomic nervous and endocrine systems to produce the irregular endocrine and 
metabolic dysfunction that develop the hypertension (Babisch 2011; Ising et al. 1999). 
Since PM2.5 and noise exposures may affect the same target system, which is the 
autonomic nervous system, it is interested in investigating whether there was a 
synergistic effect due to the combined exposure. Therefore, the purposes of this study

Subjects 
The recruitment and selection of participants in detail have been mentioned 
previously (Chang et al. 2009; Chang et al. 2012). Briefly, we recruited young adults 
through an on-campus advertisement at China Medical University in 2007. There 
were 69 students to respond to the advertisement, but only 66 volunteers were 
included because three adults reported to have a diagnosed hypertension on a 
questionnaire. No participants had any cardiovascular or pulmonary diseases. The 
main source of environmental noise was from the road traffic because all study 
subjects lived at least 10 km from any airport. 
In addition, we used a standardized questionnaire to collect individual 
information related to potential risk factors of hypertension during the study period. 
These factors included personal age, height, weight, exercise habits, cigarette smoking, 
alcohol drinking, tea consumption, coffee consumption and a family history of

alcohol, tea and coffee drinkers. We defined regular exercisers as those who had

Noise exposure measurements 
We measured personal noise exposure continuously by using a personal noise 
dosimeter (Logging Noise Dose Meter Type 4443, Brüel & Kjær, Nærum, Denmark.), 
which can report 5-minute continuous equivalent sound levels (Leq) at an exchange 
rate of 3 dBA and the time-weighted-averages (TWA) of noise doses. The range of 
50–120 dBA was used to measure all subjects’ noise exposure with 5-minute readings 
over 24 hours. As noise exposure levels were measured below the detected limit 
during the nighttime, we used 35.4 dBA (the 2

order to investigate the current and time-lagged effects of noise exposure, we 
summarized 5-minute exposure measurements into 60-minute and 120-minute 
time-moving-average segments for further analysis.

We used a portable, non-invasive, automated monitoring and recording system

subject’s ambulatory SBP and DBP over 24 hours. The measurements of ambulatory

regression has both fixed-effect and random-effect covariance parameters. Variables 
belonged to the fixed effect in this mixed-effects model included sex, age, BMI, 
cigarette smoking, regular exercise, exposed levels of noise, PM2.5 or/and NOx 
and family history of hypertension. These variables were potential confounding

We also used the linear mixed-effects regression to compare the within-period

noise, PM2.5 and NOx exposures during the daytime and nighttime. The covariance 
structure of compound symmetric type was chosen instead of the first-order 
autoregressive type due to the smaller value of Akaike’s Information Criterion in both 
ambulatory SBP and DBP (Cnaan et al. 1997). This study applied the MIXED 
procedure containing fixed and random effects in SAS 9.1 version (SAS Institute 
Incorporation, Cary, North Carolina, USA) to implement the linear mixed-effects 
regression models.

Discussion 
We found that acute noise exposure was associated with transient increases in 
ambulatory SBP and DBP. The results were consistent with the findings in a previous 
study (Chang et al. 2009). The acute effects of noise exposure on ambulatory SBP and 
DBP persisted from the current to 1-hour and 2-hour time lag exposures indicated that 
the transient increases in ambulatory blood pressure may cause the sustained elevation 
in blood pressure to develop the hypertension after the repeated and prolonged 
exposure (Chang et al. 2011; Leon Bluhm et al. 2007; Barregard et al. 2009; van 
Kempen and Babisch 2012). 
Exposure to PM2.5 also had the increased effects on ambulatory SBP and DBP in 
the present study. Our findings supported the results in a population-based prospective 
cohort study that observed the long-term effects of PM2.5 exposure on arterial SBP 
and DBP (Fuks et al. 2011). But only the increased SBP was reported in two past 
studies (Brook et al. 2011; Dvonch et al. 2009). The inconsistent results might be the 
different approach in exposure assessment and the different strategy in blood pressure 
measurements (i.e. resting blood pressure vs. ambulatory blood pressure). The future 
study should consider these factors to investigate the association between exposure to 
PM2.5 and variation in blood pressure.

The strengths of this study included the use of repeated measures analyses to 
explore the variation in ambulatory blood pressure caused by instant noise or air 
pollutants exposures. In addition, such study design explained the variance between 
and within individuals. The larger number of time-series data of exposure and 
outcomes solved the temporal problem between exposure to noise, PM2.5 or NOx and 
the changes in ambulatory blood pressure.  
The limitation was the use of monitoring data instead of personal sampling to 
represent individual exposure to PM2.5 and NOx. The use of individual noise-exposure 
measurements but station-based PM2.5 and NOx monitoring levels may have the 
greater effects of noise exposure observed on ambulatory SBP and DBP compared 
with the results from PM2.5 or NOx exposure. Therefore, the smaller effects on 
ambulatory SBP and DBP caused by air pollutants exposures were found in this study. 
In conclusion, exposure to noise or PM2.5 may be associated with the elevation of 
ambulatory blood pressure in adults. In addition, the interaction of noise, PM2.5 and 
NOx exposures may contribute to the underestimation of noise exposure on 
ambulatory SBP and DBP, PM2.5 exposure on ambulatory SBP and NOx exposure on 
ambulatory DBP but the overestimation of PM2.5 exposure on ambulatory DBP.

本研究為國際上首次以機械設備製造業為基礎之職業世代研究，透過回溯性的研究設計，
探討噪音與懸浮微粒之單獨及共同暴露對勞工高血壓發生率影響的因果關係。我們除了發
現職業噪音暴露與勞工高血壓發生率增加有關外，也觀察到中、高頻(1000、2000、4000
及 8000 Hz)的噪音暴露與高血壓風險有顯著的劑量-反應關係。此外，本研究也發現到可
呼吸性粉塵質量濃度及總粉塵粒數濃度與高血壓發生率有關。機械設備製造業勞工在噪音
與可呼吸性粉塵的共同暴露下，有顯著較高的高血壓發生率風險，但是兩者暴露並沒有顯
著的協同作用。 
     
    本研究成果除了已經發表的一篇 SCI 論文外，預計還可以再發表二篇以此職業世代為
基礎的 SCI 論文。 
   
    透過本研究結果，我們發現國內職業安全衛生法規對於勞工在噪音作業場所的保護可
能不足。目前法令要求雇主對於勞工實施的「聽力保護計畫」是針對噪音大於及等於 85
分貝的工作場所，但是本研究發現在大於及等於 80 分貝的作業環境就可能會增加勞工的
高血壓發生率風險。因此我們建議未來在修訂相關法令中，將噪音大於及等於 80 分貝的
工作場所列為必須實施勞工「聽力保護計畫」的地點，以保護勞工的健康。
