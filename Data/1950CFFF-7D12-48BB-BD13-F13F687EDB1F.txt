will be forced to face the shortage problem of one of 
its constituent element, indium (In) in the future. On 
the other hand, zinc oxide (ZnO) shows high optical 
transmittance (greater than 85%) and electrical 
conductivity (resistivity about 10-4 Ω-cm), and also 
good thermal conductivity. It also shows a small 
difference in lattice constant (1.9%) compared to that 
of GaN and a superior stability at high temperature. 
Particularly, compared to ITO, the material resource 
of ZnO is ample and no after-use worry concerned with 
the environmental protection is required. In this 
research plan, we focus on enhancing the light output 
of GaN-based LED by using aluminum-doped ZnO (AZO) as 
the electrode, which is transparent to the 
electroluminescence of LED. The atomic layer 
deposition method was used to grow n-type AZO films. 
Finally, optical and electrical properties of three 
GaN-based LEDsincluding the two using AZO and ITO 
transparent conductive layers (TCLs), respectively, 
and that without TCL were analyzed and compared. We 
found that the GaN-based LED using AZO as the TCL 
presents the most homogeneous distribution of 
injection current, demonstrating a relatively 
homogeneous luminescence area.

究，來提升穿透係數，藉此提升發光強度。第三則是針對歐姆接觸深入探討，找
到較佳的金屬組合來改善其電流對電壓特性。
