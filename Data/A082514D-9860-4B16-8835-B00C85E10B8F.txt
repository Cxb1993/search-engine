Human face recognition has received unprecedented interest in recent years. However, rigorous 
tests with real-world data have revealed many shortcomings of existing approaches. In short, the 
current systems still cannot fulfill the performance requirement for practical applications. A 
majority of current face recognition approaches make use of 2D facial images. Nonetheless, 2D 
technologies are not reliable due to illumination and pose variations in 2D facial images. Also, the 
appearance of a face can be altered dramatically through make-up efforts. On the other hand, the 
fundamental difficulties in 2D face recognition can be overcome by using 3D shape of a face. In 
particular, the 3D facial surfaces are invariant to illumination changes and the pose of a human face 
can be corrected easily by using 3D data. 
 
The principal investigator has already proposed a systematic approach for constructing 
geometrically invariant features. We verify the recognition performance of the proposed invariant 
features by conducting face recognition experiments on the Face Recognition Grand Challenge 
(FRGC) version 1.0 dataset. A significant performance improvement over the FRGC baseline 
algorithm is observed. However, there are no expression variations in FRGC version 1.0 dataset. A 
practical face recognition system must be robust to facial expressions that might appear in 
real-world scenarios. 
 
In our previous 3D face recognition system, we observed significant performance degradation due 
to expression variations. In this research project, we will overcome this limitation and develop an 
expression- invariant 3D face recognition system. In particular, the emphasis will be placed on the 
development of the Multi-Region Face Recognition Algorithm, where multiple facial sub-regions 
are combined by fusion technologies. The main idea behind the proposed method is that smaller 
facial regions are more invariant across different facial expressions. In order to speed up the 3D 
computation, we will build a parallel computing system consisting of 10 personal computers. The 
design of the proposed 3D face recognition system is scalable and the ultimate goal is to meet the 
performance requirement for a high-volume authentication system, e.g. customs system in an 
international airport. Finally, the experimental validation of the proposed expression- invariant 3D 
face recognition system will be done by using FRGC version 2.0 dataset, which is the largest 
publicly available face image dataset. 
 
 
 
Keywords: biometrics, 3D face recognition, FRGC, facial expression.
