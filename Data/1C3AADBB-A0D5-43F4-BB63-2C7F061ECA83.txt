本研究利用 RF 磁控濺鍍技術並搭配電鍍 Ni-P/Al 複合靶材，藉添加第三元 Al 至 Ni-P 系統中製
作出三元 Ni-P-Al 濺鍍薄膜。經 X-ray 相鑑定分析顯示，結晶溫度可由 400℃(二元 Ni-P)提升至
475℃(三元 Ni-P-Al)。Al 元素之添加扮演著抑制 NixPy系列化合物析出的角色，不僅提升了薄膜
結晶溫度且同時改善其熱穩定性。在製程控制上，利用高溫製程及低 Ar 流率之參數控制，薄膜
結構趨於結晶化；相反地，低溫製程及高 Ar 流率之參數控制，薄膜結構則偏向非晶質化。進一
步分析薄膜之表面形貌並比照其結晶行為，可證明製程溫度愈高，結晶物愈多之薄膜表面形貌所
呈現的波浪形起伏越明顯且粗糙度也越大。 
關鍵字：Ni-P、Ni-P-Al、濺鍍、結晶溫度、熱穩定性。 
 
1. 前言 
利用電鍍製程[1]鍍製 Ni-P 合金材料系統是常見的表
面處理技術之一，Ni-P 合金薄膜[2]具有製作簡易、硬度
高、抗腐蝕性強、耐磨耗性佳等優點，常為工業界作為表
面保護性膜層之使用。以物理氣相沉積製程(PVD) [3]添加
第三元元素於 Ni-P 鍍膜形成 Ni-P 基三元合金鍍層，為提
升相關性質之有效且常用的方法。若以熱穩定性[4~7]與機
械性質為考量，第三元元素可選擇如 W[8~10] 、
Cu[11~12]、Al[13]、Cr[14]等。其中 Al 比重約 2.7，有質
輕且堅，耐蝕性佳等優點，其 FCC 結構可與 Ni 等同結構
之元素形成高強度且高耐蝕的 Al 合金。亦有文獻指出利用
濺鍍技術添加 Al 至二元 Ni-P 系統不僅能提升整體再結晶
溫度，延緩析出物析出，熱穩定性亦有效提升[15~16]。故
可知利用濺鍍技術將 Al 元素添加至 Ni-P 系統將是一十分
可行之方式。本研究以電鍍製程製作濺鍍用三元 Ni-P/Al
複合靶材[17]，複合靶材同時擁有電鍍 Ni-P 與未鍍覆 Ni-P
之 Al 基材表面，搭配單一濺鍍源之方式，期望可於磁控濺
鍍製程[18]達成一靶共濺鍍三元 Ni-P-Al 合金薄膜[15]。並
利用製程溫度及 Ar 輸入流率等濺鍍製程控制參數，進而
研究 Ni-P-Al 合金薄膜之相轉變、熱穩定性及表面性質。 
 
2. 實驗流程 
本研究利用 RF 磁控濺鍍技術鍍製 Ni-P-Al 三元合金薄
膜，並分析其相轉變、熱穩定性與表面性質。濺鍍製程使用
之靶材係利用 Brenner type 鍍浴[19~20]之電鍍製程將 Ni-P
二元合金薄膜鍍覆於 Cu 或 Al 基材上而成一複合靶材。若
考慮圖一所示靶材因濺鍍鎗上之磁石裝置所造成表面侵蝕
之強弱效果，必須將欲鍍製之第三元元素面積設計於靶材中
間環狀區域，以獲得最佳之第三元元素濺鍍效果。圖二為靶
材設計圖，係於純度 99.7%之 Al 基材上貼覆四個直徑 5mm
對稱圓面積之抗酸鹼膠帶，覆蓋面積約為 0.8 cm2，並相距
10 mm。選用高電流密度 100 mA/cm2、鍍覆時間 6 小時以
及 55℃電鍍製程溫度，預期得到較高之膜厚與未鍍覆 Ni-P
膜之 Al 面積可同時進行濺鍍。將以電鍍製作的 Ni-P/Cu 或
Ni-P/Al 靶材裝置到濺鍍鎗上，並將試片基材固定於試片基
座再安裝至腔體內，便可進行腔體抽真空程序。待腔體壓力
下降至 2.0×10-6 torr 時，即可開始設定濺鍍製程條件，表一
為濺鍍製程參數。製程設定條件如下，(1)濺鍍源與基材距
離約為 120 mm，(2)輸出功率設定為 50 瓦，(3)輸入 Ar 氣體
流率為 10、15 及 20 s.c.c.m，(4)濺鍍製程時間設定為 1 小時
及 4 小時，(4)分別於製程加熱基材至 150℃、250℃、350℃、
450℃、500℃及 600℃下進行三元 Ni-P-Al 與二元 Ni-P 濺鍍
實驗。然而設定不同的 Ar 氣體流率，將影響濺鍍製程時的
壓力，其大約介於 1.5×10-3~3.1×10-3 torr 間。隨著真空腔體
輸入 Ar 氣體後，即可觸發電漿並濺鍍複合靶材。由複合靶
材 Ni-P/Cu(或 Ni-P/Al)濺鍍出各元元素(Ni、P 及 Al)所匯集
而成的穩定原子流鍍覆至基材的表面。待鍍覆時間結束後，
關閉靶材輸出功率，待腔體溫度降至室溫即可洩真空取出試
片。利用場發射式電子微探儀(FE-EPMA, JXA-8500F, JEOL, 
Japan)於 15 kV 加速電壓下進行合金薄膜之成份分析。定量
分析之標準元素試片分別選擇用 Ni、InP、Al、Cu、Si。薄
膜相鑑定以 X-ray 繞射儀(Shimadzu, XRD6000, Japan)分析
圖五、不同製程溫度所鍍製之 Ni-P-Al 薄膜 X-ray 繞射圖 
圖六為 Ni-P-Al 薄膜於 500℃、550℃、600℃與 Ar 流
率為 10、15 及 20 s.c.c.m 的製程下之 X-ray 繞射圖。隨著製
程溫度的提升與增加輸入 Ar 氣體流率的方式，Ni-P-Al 之結
晶趨勢與結晶產物皆有不同之結果。由圖中(1-1)~(1-3)的
X-ray 繞射圖可觀察到，在 500℃以下薄膜皆為非晶質結構，
隨著基材溫度增加到 550℃，開始出現清晰且較為尖銳的
Ni(111)繞射峰，也可看出一些析出物將要形成的趨勢。提
升到 600℃後，可發現 Ni 結晶、Ni-P 及 Ni-Al 析出物的繞
射峰都非常明顯。另外在(2-1)~(2-3)的 X-ray 繞射圖中，雖
可發現(2-3)的繞射峰較(1-3)尖銳，但製程溫度 550℃時，45°
之繞射峰的半高寬有略為縮小，整體結晶反應並無明顯變
化。提升到 600℃時，此時才可發現到明顯 Ni 基、Ni-P 及
Ni-Al 的析出物結晶繞射峰。推論原因為電漿環境中隨著 Ar
流率之上升，濺鍍輸出的功率固定下，Ar 游離化形成 Ar
離子之驅勢亦會增加，基材受到粒子撞擊的程度比低 Ar 流
率下來得高，有效抑制了析出物之產生，進而對基材表面沉
積之結構產生修飾的效果。對照 Ar 流率為 20 s.c.c.m 的
(3-1)~(3-3)繞射圖的結晶趨勢則可再次驗證 Ar 流率對相轉
變之影響。同時由於本實驗使用三種元素之單一濺鍍源靶
材，所濺鍍出之元素在電漿環境中相互混合，隨著 Ar 流率
上升，不僅元素粒子與 Ar、Ar 離子及電子碰撞機率增加，
其混合結果亦造成薄膜明顯之非晶質混合現象。換言之，提
升 Ar 流率，可使不同元素共沉積的晶體結構有修飾之作
用，並可促進元素間的混合，使結構更易形成非晶質狀態。

21. Introduction
Ni-Al based alloy films are well known for its good performance as a protective coating 
for high temperature fields and relative low cost manufacture process in versatile industrial 
applications [1,2]. In order to improve the mechanical properties of the Ni-based coatings, 
the strengthening mechanisms of solid solution and precipitation hardening are frequently
employed [3]. The Al atoms not only can dissolve into Ni matrix homogeneously, but also 
form specific metastable nickel aluminide compounds with Ni when sufficient activation
energy propelled [4]. The Ni-Al based intermediate phases possess good physical and 
mechanical behaviors, including low density, high tensile strength and yield point, high 
temperature creep resistance, and excellent corrosion and oxidation resistance [5,6]. The 
magnetron sputtering technique is commonly utilized to fabricate various functional metallic 
alloy coatings owing to its process variety [7]. Through the control of various process 
parameters of sputtering, the composition, microstructure, phase, and the related 
characteristics can be manipulated for the coatings of interests [8]. The Ni-Al alloy coatings 
with specific phases and microstructures were synthesized by using the magnetron sputtering 
process [9]. However, correlations between fabrication process and related microstructural 
and mechanical properties are not fully described.  In this present study, we focused on the 
dependency of input powers on composition, microstructure, phase, and correlated
mechanical and morphological behavior of the sputtering Ni-Al coatings. The evolution in 
4nano-indentation tester (Nano Hardness Tester, CSM Instrument, Switzerland) with a 
Berkovich diamond indenter. The applied load was 5 mN for all test with a loading and 
unloading rate of 60 mN/min and dwelling time of 5 seconds. The surface morphologies of 
the coatings were investigated by atomic force microscopy (AFM, Solver PRO-M model, 
NT-MDT, Russia) using the typical Si probe in the tapping scan mode. The surface 
roughness, Ra and Rq were calculated from the AFM images.
3. Results and Discussion
3.1. Composition evaluation
The composition and film thickness versus input power control of various Ni-Al coatings 
are summarized in Table 1. All the coating thicknesses were controlled between 1.3 to 2.0 
m for reliable morphological and mechanical investigations.  At a fixed 100W for Ni, the 
Al content in the coating was increased proportionally from 5.7 to 41.9 at.% with the increase 
in input power of Al from 25 to 100W. Theoretically, Ni exhibits a higher sputtering yield 
under the identical plasma environment and input power [10]. The sputtering rate of metals 
deduced from DC input power was also larger than that from RF input power [10,11]. Thus, 
the deposition rate of DC input power on Ni target was higher than that of Al under identical 
RF input power in this study. In summary, the Ni-rich Ni-Al coatings could successfully be 
6solid solution structure with an (220)-preferred orientation. As the sputtering power 
increased, a higher plasma energy was provided and more ionized Ar gas ions with superior 
ion energy was generated.  The higher plasma energy enhanced the surface mobility of 
deposited atoms to move along the surface of the deposits [12,13] and to attain the lowest 
surface energy state [14,15]. As a result, the preferred orientation of Ni-Al films changes 
from (111) to (220). 
On the other hand, when the Ni input power was increased from 100 to 125W with a 
fixed Al power at 100W, an amorphous-like structure was observed for sample E. With 
further increase of Ni input power at 150W, sharp peaks for Ni(Al) (111), (200, and (220) 
were rediscovered.  Moreover, it was interesting that distinct peaks for AlNi3(110) and (210) 
were observed.  This indicated that AlNi3 intermetallic compound phase precipitations [16]
coexisted with metastable Ni(Al) solid solution matrix.  It was argued that the higher ionic 
energy and impingement momentum transfer supplied in plasma enhance the formation of 
Al-Ni compounds. The grain size of Ni-Al coatings was calculated by Scherrer’s formula 
[17] from the XRD diffraction patterns. The Ni(Al)(111) peak was selected for calculation 
of the grain size. The correlation between grain size and Al contents in the Ni-Al coatings is 
plotted in Fig.2. The grain size ranged from 67 to 178 nm as the Al content in the coatings
varied from 41.9 to 5.7 at.%, respectively. It appeared that the grain size decreased 
significantly with the Al content in the Ni-Al alloy coatings deposited under fixed Ni input 
8structure and the supersaturation induced lattice distortion for compressive stresses in the 
coatings. The higher nano-hardness of sample F as compared to A and B was due to the 
AlNi3 precipitation in the metastable Ni(Al) matrix. To further demonstrate the 
strengthening effect, sample F could be put into quantitative analysis. The stoichiometric 
composition derived structure was close to 30Ni(Al)+10AlNi3. This structure predicted that 
the Ni-Al coatings contained Ni(Al) solid solution and AlNi3 compound phases. According 
to Ni-Al binary alloy phase diagram [18], sample F should transform into AlNi+Al3Ni5 if 
adequate heat treatment was applied.  Nevertheless, in present case the coatings were 
deposited in the unheated condition that it was not preferable to form the AlNi and Al3Ni5
phases in stead of Ni(Al) and AlNi3.
3.4. Surface morphology and roughness analysis
Figure 5 shows the surface morphology of binary Ni-Al films evaluated by AFM.
Surface roughness values of as-deposited Ni-Al films are summarized in Table 3.  The dense 
and granular structure on the surface was revealed for all coatings. With increasing Al 
content in the coating from 5.7 to 41.9 at.%, the surface roughness degraded from 19.3 to 1.0 
nm in Ra. It was argued that the granular surface caused by larger grain size might be 
attributed to the rougher coating morphology. Nevertheless, the calculated Ra value was in 
the nanometer scale for various Ni-Al coatings, implying that a smooth morphology could be 
obtained using dual-gun magnetron sputtering process. It was interesting to point out that 
10
approximately 11 GPa was measured for nano-crystalline Ni58.1Al41.9 and Ni71.5Al28.5 films. 
The grain size decreased with the Al content in the Ni-Al coatings, while hardness increased 
with the Al content due to the reduction in grain size. The Ni59.7Al40.3 coating with 
metastable Ni(Al) nano-crystallites and AlNi3 precipitations also showed a superior
mechanical property as compared to the Ni94.3Al5.7 and Ni83.3Al16.7. The AFM survey 
indicated that the surface morphology of as-deposited Ni-Al coatings exhibited a nano-scale 
profile ranged from 1.0 to 19.3 nm in Ra. The surface roughness of the Ni-Al coatings 
presented a proportional relationship with respect to grain size and related Al contents in the 
coatings. Consequently, it was demonstrated that the Ni-Al coatings achieved satisfactory
composition, structure, and surface characteristics, including roughness and hardness, with 
respect to fabrication power controls.

心得報告: 
 冶 金 鍍 膜 及 薄 膜 國 際 會 議 (International Conference on 
Metallurgical Coatings and Thin Films) 係由美國真空協會(American 
Vacuum Society, AVS)主導，由其下之先進表面工程部門(Advanced 
Surface Engineering Division, ASED)所主辦之大型薄膜與冶金鍍膜相
關國際會議，今年為第三十六屆，此國際會議固定於美國加州聖地牙
哥市舉行，每年約有一千多位世界各地之學者、研究人員以及相關業
界人士參加，發表論文總數可達 700多篇之譜，可謂薄膜工程及相關
技術與研究界最盛大的一個國際會議。每年發表之論文亦轉載至 Thin 
Solid Films及 Surface and Coatings Technology兩本表面處理及鍍膜技
術重要國際期刊中。 
 今年參與人數約近八九百人，論文總數亦近 700 篇，羅列於 12
項主題中。共可分為大會主要議題 8 個 symposium 及 4 個最新相關
symposium:

B. Sputtering Coatings and Technologies 
C. Optical Thin Films 
D. Carbon and Nitride Materials: Synthesis-Structure-Property 
Relationships 
E. Tribology and Mechanical Behavior of Coatings and Thin Films 
F. Characterization: Linking Synthesis Properties & Microstructure 
G. Applications, Manufacturing and Equipment
