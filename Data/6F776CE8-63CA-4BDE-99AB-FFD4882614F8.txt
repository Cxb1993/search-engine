一、前言
根據聯合國世界衛生組織的定義，一個地區中的 65歲以上人口比例超過總人口數的 7%時，即為
高齡化社會。近年來由於台灣的生活環境逐漸由農業社會進入工業社會，醫療品質及制度也日益精進
完善，少子化的情況愈來愈明顯，根據內政部網站 96年估測統計，台灣目前男性的平均壽命為 75.09
歲，女性的平均壽命為 81.09 歲，因此人口結構自 82 年起邁入高齡化社會，65 歲以上老人所佔比例
於 96年底更是已達 10.2%。高齡者在身體機能衰老及慢性疾病影響行動下，對於行動輔具的依賴程度
將日益升高。高齡者經常使用的移動類行動輔具有手動輪椅、電動輪椅、電動代步車等，使用範圍距
離及速度都非常有限，而且常見高齡者將電動輪椅或電動代步車開到馬路上，險象環生。雖有復康巴
士提供叫車服務，但只限於較大的縣市區域。
但是非都會區之鄉鎮老年人口比例很高，有其習慣之生活方式，不願意被安置於都市生活空間。
若在偏遠地區或是臨時有個人移動距離較長的需求時，分析國內市場的解決方案後發現，目前仍缺乏
一個有效的解決方案。對於稍具行動能力的高齡者，或許還可騎乘標準型改裝機車(如圖 1 所示)；但
對於需完全依靠移動類行動輔具的高齡者，目前只有子母車型改裝機車可供使用，但是改裝機車在使
用上潛藏著許多不便及危險。由於國內市場較小，業者多半不願意投入資金與人力去開發真正適合高
齡者之移動載具。前幾年開始進口日本豐田所推出福祉車，主要是在汽油車上加裝可轉位伸出之座
椅、及可降落至地面的電動輪椅子車，由於是少量休閒車種，價格昂貴，高齡者未必有能力可購買。

針對高齡者的未來使用情境，依據其遠距行動自主性與安全性的需求，本計畫將規劃一種小型有
全罩車頂，且可搭載現有輪椅或電動代步車之高齡者智慧型輕量移動載具(以下簡稱本載具)。為了使
輪椅或電動代步車可輕易上下本載具，本計畫經由田野調查、高齡者觀察與訪談、車室空間規劃與設
計、與移動載具外觀設計研究後，將採用後進前出的創新進出模式，如圖 2所示，以降低老年人在上
下載具的危險性，減少斜坡恐懼。並由於本載具四輪皆為車輪馬達獨立驅動之設計，搭配線控技術與
各子計畫的工程技術，得以實踐此創新構想，並可大幅降低底盤高度限制，提升輪椅或電動代步車與
本載具之結合性。高齡者可駕駛本載具行駛較長距離，到達目的地後再將輪椅或電動代步車開出，以
方便在社區道路及室內空間移動，大幅提升其行動能力。
本計畫將配合政府的長期照顧十年計畫，規劃公有營運模式，利用鄉里辦公室或者是聯誼中心作
為營運點的架設，借助高齡者強烈的互助連結，由志願駕駛者駕駛本載具，運送有需求的高齡者。本
計畫並將同步開發私有營運模式，以滿足高齡者須能自行駕車的需求。公有營運模式可利用營運點作
為充電站，電量過低的電池可在出發前換成充飽電的電池。若碰到中途電量耗盡的狀況，由於為公有
營運模式，可由營運點派遣電池急救車替換電池。

子計畫四：Toyota上市 Previa與 Altis兩款福祉車[1] (如圖 8所示)，其著重於提供老弱殘疾民眾

Chrysler, GM or Toyota Vans改裝)[4] (如圖 10所示)。但對於人口密集道路擁擠的國內路況，龐大的車
體，使上下載具空間增大，停車位難求，尋找合適上下載具的停靠位置更是不易。為了達成結構強度、
安全性和輕量化的目的，本載具之主結構將採用鋁管式空間桁架(Aluminum Space Frame, ASF)。ASF
的最大特點為具有高剛性與輕重量[5]，結合現有車輛之車底架，再加以結構優化以達車體輕量化目的。

研究方法與工具
本研究透過田野調查、現場觀察及深度訪談等研究工具，探討高齡者使用行動輔具，如電動代步
車等，瞭解高齡者之操作狀況、問題與需求，以使用者之需求開發移動載具，期能提出可行的方向與
策略。建構符合搭載電動輪椅與電動代步車的車室模擬環境，考量高齡者的身心理發展的限制，規劃
車室空間實體模擬平台，透過高齡者進出空間的活動行為，詳實地作觀察紀錄，經由分析與歸納並提
出最佳化的車室空間，提供開發團隊最佳的參考依據。
子計畫二：智慧型車輛動態控制之研發
循跡防滑控制系統
本研究所提出之 TCS其架構如圖 11所示。首先利用 AutoSimTm軟體建構一個包含懸吊系統效應
之五自由度非線性縱向車輛動態模型，提供具有垂直負載轉移之車輛動態響應用來模擬驗證。將三相
永磁同步馬達透過推導轉換為方便分析的兩相位車輪馬達模型[12]，做為載具動力源。將電門手把與
駕駛者動力需求之關係透過指數函數建立成查表，可調整其指數的數值來改變動力需求變化的敏感度
[13]。TCS 主要分為三個部分，首先，根據輪胎力模型為基礎使用系統參數估測演算法來估測路面狀
況[6]，並以一運算方程式設計滑差搜尋法，配合兩組門檻值來搜尋最佳的目標滑差；接著，適應性模
糊滑動控制器則是以滑差的誤差及誤差的變化量來定義滑動平面，做為控制器輸入，並以一修正方程
式調整控制器的模糊知識庫，決定一個介於 0~1之間的電流增益命令，透過限制車輪馬達驅動電流達
到防止打滑的效果；最後，適應性電流控制會利用最小平方法估測馬達參數，自動調整控制器增益值，
確保車輪馬達在長時間運轉的情況下，依然能良好地跟隨目標電流。

車艙理論數值模型、3-D模型建立與流場模擬分析
本年度主要模擬車艙空調內的空氣流場分佈，將針對高齡者的人因生理需求為主要考量之出發
點，透過出風口及吸風口來控制冷氣風量、角度及溫度等參數，使空氣流場保持在控制區域內且風量
與風速不需太大，並考慮到在高齡者乘坐的狀況下車艙內外之溫差不能太大，以達到區域性及舒適性
之溫度的調控，首先需要建立車艙空間數值模型的數學模型以用來了解與分析空間之氣體流場。
在進行車艙區域性空調熱流場模擬分析之前，須先建置車艙空間3-D solid model(與子計畫一相互
配合)並轉換成CAE(Computer Aided Engineering，電腦輔助工程)的模型，並以Gambit軟體建立非結構
性的網格，再經由Fluent軟體進行室內3-D熱流場的模擬分析，並涵蓋太陽輻射熱及人員生理熱、出及
吸風口的口徑、角度及位置及風扇的風量等資料，將依實際狀況融入在模擬分析中。
區域性空調系統之參數研究與分析
區域性空調系統在實驗設計時，如果遺漏會影響空氣流場結構的控制因子，將會造成系統嚴重的
錯誤，在此將針對影響空氣流場結構的各項參數進行分析探討（僅於關鍵性參數進行概略性說明）。
針對關鍵性之參數進行要因分析，本研究初步設定以風口角度、風扇風速、溫溼度、風口距離、出吸
風口數量及出風口壓力等因子為關鍵因素，對空氣流場結構的完整性進行測試，並透過Fluent軟體進
行數值模擬分析與驗證，以確認空氣流場結構的成形，以期能找出此等因子間最合適之參數組合，例
如加強些風速、改變出、吸風口角度及出吸風口間的位置與距離等可使空氣流場結構不被破壞，這些
參數研究結果在未來可加入在系統控制內，使車艙區域性空調系統更加可靠。

循跡防滑控制響應、點線為 TCS-2是除了有電子煞車的循跡防滑控制響應、粗實線為目標滑差。圖 26(a)
是車輪馬達輸出的扭力響應，從圖可知當車輛進入低摩擦係數路面時(1.5 sec)，TCS 能迅速的偵測到
路面環境的改變並搜尋出較佳的目標滑差，並即時降低驅動扭力，避免滑差過大。圖 26(b)是車輪馬
達的滑差響應，沒有控制的滑差會超過穩定的範圍(穩定範圍約在 0.1~0.18)高達 0.9左右，這表示已經
發生嚴重打滑，驅動性能嚴重下降，其末速明顯的小於有 TCS控制的車輛。而有 TCS控制的滑差會
隨著扭力下降而跟隨著目標滑差，落在穩定的範圍內，可提高車輛對路面環境突然改變的穩定性能。

滑差回到穩定區域的時間。

Conclusion 
An experimental approach to the evaluation of thrust force caused by core-candlestick drill using 
design experiments was proposed in this study. The experimental results were summarized as 
follows: 
(1) The diameter ratio (P = 82.3 %) and feed rate (P = 17.0 %) are seen to make the largest 
contribution to the overall performance, which present a statistical significance test F > F 0.05. 
(2) For a given feed rate, the thrust force increase with the increasing inner diameter of 
core-candlestick drill. Moreover, the thrust force decreases with the increasing spindle speed, which 
lowers feed rate per revolution of drill.

SHIUH-JER HUANG, FU-REN YING 
Department of Mechanical Engineering 
National Taiwan University of Science and Technology

Abstract: - Here, a TMS320C6416 DSK board is integrated with two CMOS color image sensors to construct a 
new stereovision platform instead of current PC based or multi-CPU combination structures for detecting 
moving objects. A novel moving object detecting strategy is proposed by combining the image temporal 
differencing and the sum of absolute difference (SAD) matching schemes. This system can detect and track any 
moving object without object color and shape limitations. The subtraction image processing technique is 
employed to detect the moving object first based on single image. Then, the edge and SAD stereo matching 
schemes are used to establish the coordinate relationship of left-right two images. Finally, the depth of a 
moving object can be estimated for 3D position calculation based on stereo geometry relationship. 
Experimental results are used to evaluate this system dynamic performance.  
 
 
Key-Words: - Stereo vision, CMOS image sensor, and moving object detecting strategy. 
 
1 Introduction 
Machine vision has been widely applied in a lot 
of 2D fields accompanied with the quick 
progressing of image processing technique[1].  The 
basic principle of human eyes can distinguish near 
and far objects is that human brain utilizes the view 
difference of two eyes images to convert into object 
depth information. Hence, two or more video 
cameras are selected to simulate two eyes function 
for constructing stereo vision systems [2].  Stereo 
vision system acquires two images from two 
corresponding cameras simultaneously. Specific 
mathematical operations software programs are 
designed and loaded into image processing CPU to 
simulate the images view difference calculation 
process. Then, the object depth information in an 
image can be derived from the sum of square 
difference (SSD) calculation of two images. 
How to extract the moving object from two 2D 
images becomes an important stereo image 
processing technique. Bae et. al [3] developed a 
stereo object tracking system by using two video 
cameras. The depth distribution information of both 
images can be calculated based on minimum mean 
of absolute difference scheme to establish disparity 
motion vector. Yoneyama et. al [4] used the motion 
vector information of Mpeg Video Stream to detect 
moving object location. Background subtraction 
scheme was used to identify a moving object [5] by 
comparing the current captured image with a pre-
input background image. The area with subtraction 
residue will be the coming or moving object. 
However, the basic background subtraction scheme 
has strict limitation on fixed background 
environment to achieve accurate identification. 
Hence, mathematical modeling analysis technique 
was employed to construct the background model 
and the mapping of environmental change [6]. Since 
background modeling needs complicate 
mathematical derivation and huge computing 
operations, the continuous images subtraction 
method is proposed to simplify this modeling 
analysis process. The new extracted image is used to 
compare with last captured image without input 
background information. The difference zone 
between those two images is defined as moving 
object location [7].  
However, how to develop an ingenious stereo 
vision system with simple structure and low cost for 
stand-alone application is still a challenge work. 
Here, TMS320C6416 DSK board is integrated with 
two CMOS color image sensors to construct a stereo 
vision platform for acquiring and locating moving 
objects. A novel moving object detecting technique 
is proposed by combining the image temporal 
difference and the sum of absolute difference (SAD) 
matching schemes. Experimental results are used to 
evaluate the feasibility and reliability of this stereo 
vision system.

MODEL CAR ROADSIDE PARALLEL AUTO-PARKING MOTION 
CONTROL USING SELF-ORGANIZING FUZZY CONTROLLER 
Shuh-Jer Huang, Guan-Yu Lin and Cheng-Chi Yu* 
Department of  Mechanical Engineering, National Taiwan Univ. of Science d Technology 
No. 43, Keelung Road, Sec. 4, Taipei, Taiwan

Abstract  
Vehicle auto-parking process can be divided into relative position detection between ego-car and surrounding 
parking space, parking trajectory planning and real-time vehicle motion path estimation and trajectory tracking 
control.three steps. Here, an appropriate parking trajectory is planned and converted into desired state variables 
trajectories of X and Y components and frontal wheel steering angle based on sinusoidal function. A model-free 
intelligent self-organizing fuzzy control (SOFC) strategy is proposed to design auto-parking path tracking 
controller by regulating a selected state variable deviation. The output command is the real-time frontal wheel 
steering angle. This intelligent controller has system learning mechanism without expertise knowledge or 
trial-and-error process. It is implemented on a 1/6 model vehicle for experimental study. The experimental results 
are compared with that of a nonlinear feedback linearization controller.  
1.  INTRODUCTION  
Vehicle parking assistant systems can be classified into parking assistant and auto-parking two types. The parking 
assistant system can provide driver more vehicle surrounding information by using additional sensors to help the 
driver complete the parking action, i.e. ultrasonic sensor, CCD camera and radar system. Mercedes Benz parking 
assistant system [1] can provide the parking space image of vehicle backside on car A/V screen when driver shifts 
the transmission system into backward parking gear, R. When driver shifts the transmission into R gear, Lexus 
parking assistant system [2] actuates the vehicle backside video camera and displays the vehicle backside view 
image on the electronic multi vision (EMV) screen to improve the driver visibility. 
Three main steps of auto-parking process are the relative position between ego-car and parking space constructing 
by using appropriate sensors; optimal parking path analysis and planning based on available space size; and 
vehicle motion trajectory estimation and parking path tracking control. Auto-parking system employs camera or 
radar system to detect the appropriate parking space and relative location with ego-car first, then it can 
automatically plan a parking trajectory for auto-parking system to track when driver starts the parking action. 
Driver still can control the brake and vehicle backing speed. This idea was firstly proposed by Volkswagen 
company [3] in 1992. Volvo [4] and BMW developed an auto-parking system, too. It includes electronic power 
steering system (EPS), ultrasonic environment detecting system and auto-parking trajectory planning and tracking 
control system. 
There have certain researches worked on the parking path tracking control by using car-like mobile robot based on 
visual based environment information [5,6]. Fuzzy logic control algorithm was employed to design the parking 
controller [7,8] based on model car or simulation work. Daxwanger and Schmit [9] employed fuzzy and neural 
network schemes to develop a visual based model car backward parking controller by generating the on-line 
auto-parking steering angle command. Zhu and Rajamani [10] proposed a nonlinear state feedback controller for 
roadside auto-parking. Previous researches focused on the mobile robot motion avoidance and parking path 
planning with visual based or sensors fusion system or only focused on small model car simulation study. The 
vehicle dynamics had not been concerned. In addition, the fuzzy controller must be designed based on expertise 
and trial-and-error work or needs complicate vehicle dynamic model. It is not convenient for practical application. 
Here, a model-free intelligent self-organizing fuzzy control strategy is proposed to design auto-parking path 
tracking controller based on system learning mechanism without expertise knowledge or trial-and-error process. 
This control system is implemented on 1/6 model vehicle for experimental study.

4.  AUTO-PAKING CONTROLLER DESIGN  
Since the auto-parking control process has non-autonomous property and uncertainty behaviour. Each 
parking trajectory and initial/final positions are different. The real state variables are un-measurable and the 
motion dynamics has internal dynamics behaviour. Usually, the 2D parking path tracking control purpose is 
executed by monitoring the frontal wheel steering angle only. The X and Y axes parking path components are not 
measurable and controlled state variables. The controller only generates the steering angle command for 
indirectly regulating the motion trajectory. It is an indirect and in-complete control problem. In addition, the 
complicate vehicle dynamic model is not well known for state estimation. Hence, the simplified 2D bicycle 
model is employed to design a nonlinear input-output feedback linearization controller proposed by Zhu and 
Rajamani [10]. Here, a model-free self-organizing fuzzy control scheme is proposed to design an intelligent 
controller for a 1/6 model vehicle auto-parking monitoring process. Its parking path tracking control performance 
is compared with that of a recent nonlinear input-output feedback linearization controller study [10]. Both control 
strategies only generate the frontal wheel steering angle command. Then the vehicle needs an appropriate 
controller to quickly regulate the steering angle for tracking the desired command. Here, PI controller is designed 
for this purpose. The basic control algorithms of both controllers are explained shortly in following sections.  
Fuzzy logic control has been successfully employed in many industrial applications. However, there have 
been no definite procedures for designing an optimal fuzzy controller. The parameters of the membership 
functions and fuzzy rules need to be set by an expert or based on experience. Usually these parameters are designed 
by trial-and-error work to obtain appropriate performance, which is often a time-consuming process. Therefore, 
the intelligent fuzzy controller with learning ability was proposed to facilitate the implementation process. 
The main difference between a SOFC and a traditional fuzzy controller is the properties of their database 
and fuzzy rules. The database and fuzzy rules of a traditional fuzzy controller are fixed after the design step. 
However, the database and fuzzy rules of a SOFC are accumulated or modified continuously based upon a learning 
strategy during the control processes to improve the system output precision. The SOFC was first proposed in [14] 
and its learning structure was modified in [15,16]. Originally, the learning scheme was based on a performance 
decision table. However, the design of a performance decision table is as difficult as the design of a fuzzy rule table. 
Then the output error and error change were employed directly to modify the linguistic fuzzy rules table. The fuzzy 
rules table of this SOFC can be started with zero initial fuzzy rules. Here a real-time linguistic SOFC control 
strategy shown in Fig. 3(b) is proposed by using two parameters to take care of the function of the performance 
measure instead of the performance decision table.

ABSTRACT Regional air conditioning mechanism (RACM) system is an energy saving equipment,
which can control the airflow in a room for the purpose of achieving a regional steady-state temperature. With
this concept, each staff in a different area of the location can be satisfied with respect to their own local
environment. The RACM was constructed of a main duct inlet-outlet round ports. The purpose of this paper is to
study the effect of air inlet velocity, outlet vacuum pressure, and distance between outlet port and floor surface to
obtain an optimal airflow circulation cell, temperature distribution, and thermal comfort indices in occupied zone
of the room. Moreover, the computational fluid dynamics (CFD) model was validated on the basis of the
experimental results. It was found that the simulation results were in good agreement with the experimental data.
The CFD can predict very well the RACM and reveal the effect of operation parameters on the airflow
circulation cell that is directly related to its performance. Besides, the RACM inferred more energy efficient than
a typical air conditioning system. The results will be valuable bases to research and design the RACM system to
all kinds of regional air conditioning in any enclosed space.
Keywords: Energy-saving; Thermal comfort; Computational fluid dynamics (CFD); Airflow circulation cell
1. INTRODUCTION
As the standard of living is increasing, more and
more people are looking for an independent temperature
zone controlled in a workroom that can be more pleasant,
comfortable, healthy, and safety, energy efficient. In these
respects, a theory of personalized air conditioning systems
has been proposed and quickly paid much attention as a
central concern in designing intelligent workplaces since
the early 1990s [1].
Zeng et al. [2] investigated personalized ventilation
systems (PVS), whose design allows each occupant to
control their own airflow direction, temperature and select
options according to their personal thermal comfort
demands. In their study they showed that the perceived air
quality of PVS is superior to the conventional mixing
ventilation system given the same amount of supply air.
Cho et al. [3] presented a personal environment
module (PEM), which was constructed of two diffusers
connected by flexible ducts, a main fan, a mixing box with
two variables, speed fans, and filters. The PEM as an
alternative air conditioning system for improving thermal
comfort in workspaces has been studied. Comparisons with
a typical under floor air conditioning (UFAC) system were
conducted. The advantages of the PEM are not only that it
can be controlled flexibly and easily, but also that it offers
significant potential for improving thermal comfort in
workspaces.
Fanger and Melikov [46] proposed that
personalized ventilation (PV) can improve occupant’s 
thermal comfort, the perceived air quality and decrease the
intensity of sick building syndrome (SBS) symptoms as
compared to mixed ventilation. Occupants will use the
provided individual control of airflow rate and positioning
of the air terminal device to obtain their preferred
micro-environment in rooms where the air temperature is
within the range recommended by indoor climate standards.
They recommend development of more efficient air
terminal devices.
Melikov [7] focused the studies upon personal
ventilation aims to supply clean and cool air at low velocity
and turbulence direct to the breathing zone of occupants.
Each occupant may be provided with control of the
supplied flow rate and/or supplied air temperature. The
users prefer in their personal workplace and reduce energy
use from thermal stratification, reduce static pressure, and
increased economizer operation.
Huang and Tuan [8] further studied and analyzed the
concept of regional air conditioning mechanism (RACM) in
the workroom using the computational fluid dynamics
(CFD). Based on three case studies, the numerical
simulations showed that the airflow occupation appeared to
provide the best thermal comfort according to occupants’ 
preferences and also reduced much of the energy loss for
systems if intake air velocity was set at 0.8 m/s. Other
simulation results have also been presented by the authors
[9], who investigated steady state flow and heat transfer
within a modeled room using the RACM system. Many
different parameters were studied with different inlet air
temperatures, air supply flow rates, and inlet port positions.
Relative to the optimizing building’s energy-saving,
ensuring the occupants’ comfort, and preserving air quality
in conventional air conditioning, there were some studies
those were pointed out that such as: Novel evaporative
cooling system with an automatic wind and automatic
wind-tracking device, the origination, development and
current status of chilled ceiling, displacement and
density mesh at the inlet and the outlet flow region using
Gambit software [13]. A computational model of the room
with the RACM system was constructed using the CFD
software of FLUENT Inc. (Fluent 6. 3). By using
solution-adaptive refinement such as gradient adaption of
velocity, the model can be refined by adding cells at
occupied zone to enable the physical feature of the
independent velocity field to be better resolved and coarsen
the rest of model room. When adaption is used properly, the
resulting mesh is optimal for the flow solution. In other
words, computational resources are not wasted by the
inclusion of unnecessary cells in the rest of model, as in the
structured grid approach. Therefore, the mesh quality of
gradient adaption seems to better than initial mesh in the
same k  turbulence viscous model, and then the gradient
adaption has been chosen for further simulation. Thus, the
mesh had 11555 elements in total.

5. RESULTS AND DISCUSSION
Based on the theory that the airflow convection
effect is much stronger than the diffusion effect, this aim of
this research is to limit the air energy to a certain region by
means of creating an airflow circulation cell. The inlet flow
speed acts as the inertia and outlet vacuum pressure
provides the centrifugal force to form a circulation cell.
Therefore, the RACM can make the office room into a
region with different temperature sections without any
partitions. In this study, a total of ten simulations set in a
hypothetical summer cooling period were carried out by
using FLUENT. The simulated room has dimensions of 3.2
m height (h) and 10 m diameter (D) as shown in Fig. 2. The
left corner of the modeled room (length = 0.11.2 m; height
= 01.2 m) is intended to correspond to the occupied zone
of the room as depicted in Fig. 3.
5.1 Temperature and velocity field
CFD simulations of temperature and velocity
distributions in an unsteady state during a three hundred
seconds simulation were carried out for all three groups.
Figs. 9 and 10 depict the thermal environment in the
simulated room. In the group one, the intake air blows in
and it trends go straight down, most of uptake air is sucked
back to outlet by the vacuum pressure to form the
circulation cell at the occupied zone. The air inlet velocity
magnitude is high on top occupation and getting reduced
when it flows downward to occupation below as depicted in
Fig.10. On the other hand, there is some airflow that slowly
changes to horizontal direction and moving further away
from the occupied zone. The airflow loss increases as air
inlet velocity magnitude increases. The reason is likely that
as the inlet air velocity increases, proportionally more of
cooling airflow inlet enters the room which then makes the
areas of the airflow loss become larger and larger in room.
For the group two, the simulated conditions are shown with
the variation of Pout. Basically, the airflow circulation cell is
still kept inside the occupied zone, however, the airflow
pattern and temperature distribution areas narrower and
narrower in the occupied zone as the Pout increases. The
reason for this is that, presumably, the more airflow was
sucked back to the outlet port as the Pout increases. It is also
seen from the figures of group three that the circulation
airflow zone is less uniform as the L2 is deviated from that
of case*. We explain that, the room background effect aid
significantly affects to the establishing airflow circulation
cell process. If the L2 is shorter than that of the case*, the
cooling air inlet reaches the room background quickly, as a
result, the cooling airflow much changes to a horizontal
direction that may cause more airflow loss in the room. If
