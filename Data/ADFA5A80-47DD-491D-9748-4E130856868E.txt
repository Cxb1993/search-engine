Abstract 
We demonstrated a hybrid transflective device by vertically 
integrating a PDLC and an OLED with OPV as the reflective and 
transmissive mode, respectively. Such an OPV in an OLED 
absorbs light and converts into electrical power, which reduces 
the power consumption and also acts as the integrated sensor.  
1. Introduction 
Transflective LCD, which consists of transmissive (T) and the 
reflective (R) parts in a subpixel, is one of the most popular 
mobiles displays because the T- and the R-modes exhibit a high 
ambient contrast ratio (A-CR) under low and high ambient light 
conditions, respectively [1]. However, the planar integration of T- 
and R-modes of the transflective LCD limits the aperture ratio 
which, in turn, degrades the display resolution and brightness. A 
tandem structure which vertically integrates an OLED with 
reflective LCD (RLCD) can not only increases the aperture ratio 
but also enhances the A-CR, as compared to the conventional 
transflective LCD [2-4]. The objective of this paper is to integrate 
an organic photovoltaic (OPV) device in this tandem display. 
Such an embedded sensor can adjust the operation condition for

2. Results 
In this study, an OPV-embedded OLED was used for integration 
with PDLC. In this OPV-OLED, we used N, N-Bis (naphthalen-1-
yl) -N, N-bis (phenyl) benzidine (NPB) as the hole-transport layer 
(HTL), tris-(8-hydroxyquinoline) aluminum (Alq3) as the emitting 
layer (EML) and the electron-transport layer (ETL), LiF as the 
electron injection layer (EIL) materials for OLED. N,N'-Bis (2,6-
diisopropyl -phenyl) -1,7-bis (4methoxyphenyl) perylene-
3,4,9,10-tetracarboxydiimide (MPPDI) sandwiched by electrodes 
was used as a single layer OPV device. Device structure OPV-
OLED is: NPB (50 nm)/Alq3 (40 nm)/LiF (0.5 nm)/Al (2.5 
nm)/Ag (2.5 nm)/MPPDI (75 nm)/LiF (0.5 nm)/Al (150 nm).

The semi-transparent metal consisted of a double metal Al/Ag, 
which provides efficient destructive interference and an electron 
injection pathway resulting from the large difference of the lowest 
unoccupied molecular orbital (LUMO) value between Alq3 (2.7

for better electron injection from the cathode into MPPDI. A 
control OLED without OPV was also fabricated for comparison: 
NPB (50 nm)/Alq3 (40 nm)/LiF (0.5 nm)/ Al (150 nm). Our 
PDLC consists of UV-curable monomer NOA65 in a nematic LC 
host (E48, Δn=0.231 at λ=589 nm). The concentration of NOA65 
is 40%. The LC/monomer mixture was injected into an empty 90° 
twisted cell in the isotropic state, followed by UV exposure 
intensity at I=60 mW/cm2 for 15 min at T=20 °C. Fig. 2 shows the 
J-V curves of the control device and the OPV-OLED. Driving 
voltages of OPV-OLED was about 0.55V higher than control 
device due to the increase of the OPV thickness. Although 
thickness of OPV-OLED was 84% thicker than control device, the 
driving voltages of these devices rose by only 8%, which proves 
the high conductivity of MPPDI. In OPV-OLED, an Al layer is 
essential with the structure Alq3/LiF/Al for better electron 
injection from the cathode to the ETL.

Abstract 
Combining the absorption effect of Ag-doped organic matrix and 
micro-cavity effects of metal-organic-metal structure as the black 
cathode, a low reflectance (between 4.5 and 17%) organic light 
emitting diode (OLED) within entire visible range (from 400 to 
800 nm) was achieved without anti-reflection coating. This device 
looks “black” at all the viewing angles (from 0o to 75o). J-V 
characteristic and operation lifetime of the “black” OLED is as 
good as the control device with Al cathode. 
1. Introduction 
Ambient-contrast-ratio (A-CR) of organic light-emitting device 
(OLED) decreases dramatically with increasing the ambient 
intensity, which limits its outdoor applications, due to the high 
reflectance of the metal electrode [1]. Commercially available 
circular polarizer film, consisting of a linear polarizer and quarter 
wave retarder, was laminated on OLED for improving the A-CR 
[2]. However, it results in an extra cost and complexity in 
fabrication. An alternative is to insert a “black” layer inside the 
OLED by means of absorption or destructive interference effect 
for eliminating the ambient intensity [3-6]. Cavity design of 
destructive interference is sensitive to wavelength and viewing 
angle which limits its view angle performances and A-CR at the 
whole visible spectrum. On the other hand, there are still 
difficulties in finding a suitable “black” material over the whole 
visible range compatible to the OLED fabrication without 
affecting its electrical performances. 
The objective of the paper is to design a low reflectance OLED 
over the whole visible range and different viewing angles by 
means of a broadband absorber, combined with a suitable cavity 
design, which is called absorptive destroyed interference black 
cathode (ADIBC). This broadband absorptive thin film consists a 
matrix N,N'-Bis(2,6-diisopropylphenyl)-1,7-bis(4-methoxyphenyl) 
perylene-3,4,9,10-tetracarboxydiimide (MPPDI) and Ag dopant.[6] 
MPPDI exhibits a broadband absorption with double peaks at 420 
and 567 nm, respectively. By doping Ag inside MPPDI matrix, 
the absorption increases dramatically due to the nanoparticle 
scattering and surface plasmonic resonance. For electrical 
characteristics, MPPDI is a perylene derivative which exhibits 
high electron mobility and is commonly used for organic 
photovoltaic applications. With the incorporation of Ag, the 
conductivity of the ADIBC further increases which shows an 
identical J-V characteristics with the device using Al electrode.
