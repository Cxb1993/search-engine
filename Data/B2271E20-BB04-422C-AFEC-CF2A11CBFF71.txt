4. 結論 
本研究探討白金奈米纖維(Pt/NF)的製
作技術以及應用於質子交換膜燃料電池電
極的製作方法與性能測試分析，同時考慮
Pt/NF混合白金碳載體製作陰極側觸媒層對
PEMFC性能造成的影響。實驗研究成果顯
示，使用單一的白金奈米纖維製作觸媒層無
法達到提升燃料電池性能的目的，必須藉由
混合白金碳載體來提升燃料電池的性能。從
性能極化曲線、交流阻抗頻譜、以及迴圈伏
安法量測結果皆顯示，陰極觸媒層中白金觸
媒的混合比例以0.4mg/cm2 白金碳載體和
0.1mg/cm2白金奈米纖維時，能得到最佳的性
能。利用白金奈米纖維的高導電性與白金碳
載體的高反應比表面積特性，使兩者的優點
在適當的比例下結合互補，即可得到最佳的
燃料電池性能，未來進一步的研究重點在於
電極的耐久性測試，驗證使用Pt/NF與Pt/C混
合觸媒是否可達到延長電極壽命的效果，達
到提升PEMFC性能的研究目的。

ABSTRACT 
In this study, the performance of a polymer electrolyte 
membrane (PEM) fuel cell with double-faced microporous 
layer (MPL) coating on gas diffusion layer (GDL) is 
investigated experimentally. A standard commercial SGL® 
10BA carbon paper is used as the substrate and it is coated 
with MPL on both sides of the paper with different 
composition. Three different carbon powders are used in 
the experiments, including Vulcan XC-72R, Acetylene black, 
and Black Pearls 2000. The effect of PTFE content is also 
considered. A single cell testing apparatus is constructed to 
measure the cell performance and evaluate the effect of 
GDL with double-faced MPL coating. Accordingly, the 
optimal fabrication parameters of double-faced MPL could 
be determined. The result shows that under the same 
operating conditions, the performance of fuel cell using GDL 
with double-faced MPL is better than that using general 
single-faced MPL. The Acetylene black is found to give the 
best cell performance than the others. The optimal 
composition of MPL on the surfaces facing to the catalyst 
layer and flow-channel plate are 1.25 mg/cm2 and 0.25 
mg/cm2, respectively. Besides, the optimal PTFE content is 
the same on both sides of MPL which is found to be 20 wt%. 
 
Keywords: microporous layer, gas diffusion layer, carbon 
loading, PTFE, PEMFC

1. INTRODUCTION 
A coating of MPL on surface of GDL facing to the catalyst 
layer is helpful for the water management problem in a 
PEM fuel cell especially in the cathode side. Accordingly, the 
GDL with MPL coating have been widely used in PEM fuel 
cells. The MPL is a thin layer coated on the GDL surface 
which consists of carbon powder and hydrophobic agent. 
The employment of MPL can enhance the remove of liquid 
water, avoid flooding phenomena, and thus improve the 
cell performance. Numerous studies have devoted to this 
research field and propose many different ways to raise the 
PEM fuel cell performance by the utilization of MPL [1-23]. 
Recently, Wang et al. [11] used carbon paper (Toray TGP-H-
30) as the substrate and first test the effect of GDL with 
double-faced MPL coating. The carbon powder in the MPL is 
made of composite carbon black which consists of AB and 
BP2000. They found that the composite carbon black may 
enhance both the transport of air and liquid water in the 
cathode. Particularly, the double-faced coating of MPL on 
the substrate could form a porosity gradient in the GDL 
which is beneficial for the transport of reactant gas and 
product. Hence, the PEM fuel cell generally exhibits better 
cell performance. However, the optimal type and loading of 
carbon powder are not discussed in their work and the 
optimal content of hydrophobic agent like PTFE in the MPL 
is also unclear, which motivates the present investigation. 
In the present study, the effect of GDL with double-faced 
MPL coating on the performance of a PEMFC will be 
evaluated experimentally in details. Three different types of 
carbon powder are employed in the experiments including 
AB, Vulcan XC-72R, and BP2000. The optimal carbon loading 
on each side of GDL is determined and the best content of 
hydrophobic agent PTFE in the MPL is tested as well. The 
technique of electrochemical impedance spectroscopy (EIS) 
is also used to explore the variations of polarization losses, 
ohmic losses, and concentration losses with different GDLs 
in the cathode side fabricated by a double-faced MPL 
coating. The results will benefit the further improvement in 
PEMFC performance.

transfer resistance with the carbon loading on the surface 
facing to the flow channel side are displayed in Fig. 5 for the 
typical case using AB at 500 mA/cm2. Apparently, the charge 
transfer resistance is more significant than the other two 
resistances at moderate current density condition and the 
ohmic resistance is always slightly lower than the gas 
transfer resistance. Particularly, the case with MPL coating 
0.75/0.25 mg/cm2 always gives the minimum value in each 
resistance and thus exhibits the best cell performance. The 
results show that a double-faced MPL coating on GDL in the 
cathode side may not only reduce the membrane resistance 
but also increase the triple-phase interface in the catalyst 
layer and enhance the gas transport performance in GDL. 
However, the amount of carbon loading on the surface 
facing to the flow channel should be appropriate and it is 
generally less than that on the other surface facing to the 
catalyst layer. 
Note that in above experiments the total carbon loading 
on both surfaces of GDL is fixed at 1.0 mg/cm2. In order to 
evaluate the effect of carbon loading on each surface, we 
first fix the carbon loading on the flow-channel side and 
adjust that on the catalyst-layer side to test the variation in 
cell performance. The result of a typical case is shown in Fig. 
6 in which the AB is used as the carbon black in MPL. The 
carbon loading on the flow-channel side is fixed at 0.25 
mg/cm2. We observe that the cell performance increases 
gradually when the carbon loading on the catalyst-layer side 
increases from 0.5 to 1.25 mg/cm2. Once the carbon loading 
increases further to 1.5 mg/cm2, the cell performance falls 
abruptly giving the worst performance in all the tests. Such 
a result indicates that an appropriate amount of carbon 
loading in MPL on the catalyst-layer side of GDL is quite an 
important factor to affect the cell performance. An over 
coating of carbon loading on this side of GDL surface could 
significantly lower the cell performance. The influence of 
carbon loading on the flow-channel side is also evaluated in 
a similar way. On the catalyst-layer side the carbon loading 
is fixed at 0.75 mg/cm2 and on the flow-channel side four 
cases are considered with carbon loadings 0, 0.25, 0.5, and 
0.75 mg/cm2. The results show that the case with 0.25 
mg/cm2 on the flow-channel side still gives the optimal cell 
performance. Accordingly, we may conclude that the set of 
carbon loading 1.25/0.25 mg/cm2 for a GDL with double-
faced MPL coating gives the best cell performance. Similar 
results are also observed in the cases using Vulcan XC-72R 
and BP 2000 as the carbon black in MPL. However, it is 
found that the case using AB in MPL still demonstrate the 
best performance. The effect of PTFE content in MPL is also 
an important factor which may affect the cell performance 
profoundly. In order to determine the optimal PTFE content 
for a GDL with double-faced MPL coating, we have 
performed a series of tests which are similar to those for 
the determination of optimal carbon loading in each MPL. A 
typical test result is shown in Fig. 7. The cases of PTFE 
content considered in the experiments include 5%, 10%, 
20%, 30% and 40%. We first fix the PTFE content in the MPL 
on the flow-channel side at 20% and adjust that in the MPL 
on the catalyst-layer side. We can observe that when the 
PTFE content increases from 10% to 20%, the cell 
performance is improved significantly. Once the PTFE 
content exceeds 20%, however, the cell performance begins 
to decrease. Similar phenomena were also observed in the 
tests that the PTFE content in the flow-channel side was 
varied. Thus, we may conclude that the optimal PTFE 
contents in both MPLs for a double-faced MPL-coating GDL 
are the same that is 20 wt%. 
4. CONCLUSIONS 
In this work, the effect of cathode GDL with double-faced 
MPL coating on the performance of a PEM fuel cell has been 
tested in details. The purpose is to evaluate whether it is a 
possible way to improve the cell performance by employing 
a GDL with double-faced MPL coating to replace that with 
generally single-faced MPL coating. The results show that a 
GDL with double-faced MPL coating indeed may improve 
the cell performance significantly when the amounts of 
carbon loading in both MPLs are appropriate. Different 
types of carbon black were used in the experiments and the 
influence of PTFE content was also examined. The tests in 
each type of carbon black always show that the set of 
carbon loadings 1.25/0.25 mg/cm2 gives the optimal cell 
performance with the same PTFE content 20% in both 
MPLs. Particularly, the result using AB in MPL displayed the 
best cell performance. EIS analyses also showed that in such 
a condition of cathode GDL, the fuel cell exhibits lowest 
ohmic resistance, charge transfer resistance, and gas 
transference than those of other cases. That is, a small 
amount of carbon loading on the surface of GDL facing to 
the flow-channel side with appropriate concentration of 
hydrophobic agent should be helpful for the improvement 
of PEM fuel cell performance.
