Abstract 
A novel framework for treating dynamic 
system problems using bootstrap filter (or 
Particle filter) is studied and implemented in 
this project. We propose a special Sequential 
Monte Carlo method, the mixture bootstrap 
filter (MBF), which is essentially a moving 
bank of Kalman filters or extended 
variable-structured multiple model (VSMM) 
estimator with Monte Carlo sampling method. 
The MBF is derived for state-space models in 
special form, namely, conditional dynamic 
linear model, conditional linear Gaussian 
model, or partial linear Gaussian model. The 
characteristic of these models are linear and 
their formulation depends on extra random 
variables, called latent. The Monte Carlo 
sampling is working in the space of latent 
variables instead of in the space of the state 
variable.  
Given the indicator variable, the MBF 
provides a sufficient statistical characterize- 
tion of the system dynamics. Meanwhile, the 
MBF relies on the conditional Gaussian 
property and uses a marginalization operation 
in order to improve the efficiency and 
accuracy of the sequential Monte Carlo 
estimation technique. Compared with a few 
available filtering methods including 
conventional Monte Carlo ones, the 
effectiveness provided by the MBF will be 
evaluated. Additionally, examples in target 
tracking are also given to demonstrate the 
proposed procedures and applications.  
 
Keywords: Mixture bootstrap filter, Target 
tracking, Multiple model, Variable structure.
