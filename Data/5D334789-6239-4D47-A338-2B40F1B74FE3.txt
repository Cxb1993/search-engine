中文摘要 
    為了深入了解人工溼地中淡水螺與水體環境間的交互關係，本研究利用嘉南藥理科技大
學校園中之人工溼地，依據溼地中 BOD(Biochemical Oxygen Demand)高至低之不同，劃分四
個區塊，進行軟體動物相及各項水質因子的調查。經因素分析分析結果顯示，總凱氏氮、總
磷、磷酸鹽、硝酸鹽、亞硝酸鹽以及水中含氧量(溶氧量)等水質參數為影響人工溼地水體狀
況的主要因子。再應用判別分析得知，人工溼地中總凱氏氮及磷酸鹽逐升高，溶氧量下降時，
福壽螺(Pomacea canaliculata)之生物量會隨即升高；當水中總凱氏氮、磷酸鹽及 BOD 削減
後，台灣網蜷 (Melanoides tuberculatus formosensis)及網蜷 (Melanoides tuberculatus)的族群生
物量會有上升的趨勢。本研究結果顯示，人工溼地淡水螺群聚會隨著水質變化改變而改變其
群聚之結構，期許此結果可以提供日後人工溼地設計與維護之憑藉。 
關鍵字：人工溼地、軟體動物、指標生物、環境因子 
 
Abstract 
The purpose of this study was to investigate the water factors effect of freshwater snail in 
constructed wetland.  We divided four study sites, according to wetland BOD in Chia Nan 
University constructed wetland.  Our results showed the major factor, total Kjeldahl nitrogen 
(TKN), total phosphorus (TP), phosphate (PO42+), nitrate (NO3-), nitrite (NO2-) and dissolved 
oxygen (DO), affect water quality in constructed wetland.  Discriminant analysis showed that 
biomass of apple snail (Pomacea canaliculata), Melanoides tuberculatus formosensis and 
Melanoides tuberculatus shift to follow the water parameter such as TKN, PO42+,DO and 
Biochemical oxygen demand(BOD) constructed wetland Our result this should can offer design 
and maintain according with constructed wetland in the future.  
Keywords：constructed wetland、mollusk、biological indicators、environment factors 
 
一、 前言 
     
溼地水體品質的化學與物理性差異反應出水生生物種類與豐度上的差異，人工溼地中的
水域環境亦有相同的狀況(羅等，2005)。溼地中的軟體動物(mollusk)為溼地中的大型底棲動
物。由於軟體動物行動緩慢，再加上它們對水體物理及化學性質相當敏感之特性，許多研究
利用其族群組成之變化來監測水體環境變化的狀況(趙，2000)。人工溼地亦可經由軟體動物
之變化來作為管理的參考依據(彭等，2007)。然而，過去軟體動物與水體狀況之參數鮮少出
現於人工溼地，在管理層次上亦缺少有利的參考資料(荊與黃，2007)。因此，為了建立人工
溼地之底棲軟體動物與水質變化的資料，進而提供相關管理上之參數，本研究針對人工溼地
中水質因子與淡水軟體動物(淡水螺)的相互關係進行研究。 
 
二、 研究目的 
    本研究利用嘉南藥理科技大學中之人工溼地(後簡稱校園人工溼地)為研究地點，進行底
棲軟體動物之採集與水質分析，並經由各項水質分析結果、生物群聚指數、主成分分析、皮
爾森相關係數與判別分析，探討淡水軟體動物群聚與水質環境因子之關係，再配合各樣點所
採集之底棲淡水軟體動物，建立以底棲軟體動物為水質指標生物的相關資料及尋求可辨認水
質狀態之指標生物。

四、 研究方法 
    本研究利用嘉南藥理科技大學中之除污型人工溼地 (後簡稱：校園溼地)為研究地點。根
據以往基本資料 (黃，2003；彭 等，2007)分別依據 BOD 之高低於校園溼地中之 FWS 設定
四個樣點，其中樣點 1、2 為 BOD 高之區域，樣點 3、4 為 BOD 低之樣點(圖 1)，每樣點大
小為 50x50cm。並於 2007 年 11 月、12 月以及 2008 年 1 月、2 月、3 月、4 月、5 月，上午
8:00 至上午 11:00 於每樣點進行各項水質因子測定與底棲軟體動物調查。 
水質因子測定分為現場水質測定及實驗室水質分析四大類。現場水質測定主要以
Hyerolav (mini sonde 4A, USA)測定現場水體溫度(後簡稱：溫度)、溶氧 (DO, dissolved 
oxygen)、酸鹼值 (pH)、導電度 (conductivity)及氧化還原電位 (ORP, oxidation reduction 
potential )等水質資料。 
實驗室水質分析主要將樣區水樣採回後做進一步的水質檢測分析，其檢測項目包含濁度 
[turbidity；行政院環境保護署環境檢驗所(後簡稱 環檢所)，2005a]、總懸浮固體 (TSS, total 
suspended solids；環檢所，2003a)、化學需氧量 (COD, chemical oxygen demand；環檢所，
2007a)、生化需氧量(BOD；Biochemical Oxygen Demand；環檢所，2001)、葉綠素 (Cha, 
chlorophyll a；環檢所，2004)、總磷 (TP, total phosphorus；環檢所，1994) 、總凱氏氮 (TKN, 
total kjeldahl nitrogen；環檢所，1997)及大腸桿菌數 (E.coil, Escherichia coli；環檢所，2007b)
等水質重要指標。除此之外為了有效了解水質與底棲軟體動物的關係，亦針對水中氨氮 
( NH+4 ；環檢所，2005b)與硝酸鹽 (NO-3)、亞硝酸鹽 (NO-2)及磷酸鹽 (PO+4；環檢所，2003b)
進行分析。 
底棲軟體動物調查主要以 50 x 50 平方公分之定量框做為樣區範圍。先以目視方式撿拾
可直接看見之淡水螺，再以鏟子將框內之底泥鏟起，以篩網篩除細泥後，再從剩餘的底質中
挑揀淡水螺，採集之軟體動物隨即進行物種鑑定，並測量重量後，便放回原採集地。底棲軟
體動物調查結果除了計算密度及生物量 (biomass)外，同時利用向農—魏佛指數 (Shannon 
and Weaver, 1949; Wilhm and Dorris, 1968)方式計算多樣性指標 (species diversity index)。

七、 計畫成果自評 
本研究在討論不同樣區水質變化情形時，屬於物理性質之環境因子，以及植物覆蓋度對
葉綠素含量的影響，皆無顯著差異，未來可進ㄧ步探討，造成此原因是因為樣點過於接近，
還是有其他因素造成未來能針對其他人工溼地及自然溼地進行相關研究，進ㄧ步探討人工溼
地與天然溼地中軟體動物組成與環境因子間的關係，以利蒐集更完整的相關資料，期許能建
立更完善的軟體動物生物指標，以利用於判別人工溼地水及自然水體水質狀況。另外本研究
協助一碩士班研究完成學位論文，題目為：人工溼地淡水螺的分布與環境因子關係之研究。

Using Constructed Wetlands  
 
Ying-Feng Lin*, Shuh-Ren Jing and  Hsuan-Yu Sui 
Department of Environmental Engineering and Science, Chia-Nan University of Pharmacy and Science, Tainan 
717, Republic of China (Taiwan)
 
*E-mail: yflin@mail.chna.edu.tw; yflin@ms12.hinet.net 
 
 
Abstract 
This study established farm-scale constructed 
wetlands (CWs) integrating with earthen ponds, using 
existing fishpond areas, with a wetland to pond ratio 
of 0.086 for shrimp culture. The CWs were used as a 
practice for aquaculture water and wastewater 
treatment so as to regulate water quality of shrimp 
ponds and to manage diffuse pollution from pond 
effluents. Results of water quality monitoring for 
influent and effluent showed that constructed 
wetlands significantly reduced TSS (59~72%), 
turbidity (55~65%), chlorophyll a (58~72%), BOD5 
(29~40 %), and COD (13~24%) from pond water. 
The wetland treatment sufficiently regulated water 
quality of the recirculating fishpond, which was 
significantly (p<0.05) better than that in a control 
fishpond without connection of CWs. Furthermore, 
the wetland-treated effluent always satisfied the 
national effluent standards for aquaculture farms (i.e. 
90% of the collected samples < 30 mg TSS /L, 100% 
of the samples < 30 mg BOD5/L and 100 mg COD 
/L). Accordingly, three kinds of wetland treatment 
applications were proposed to implement the best 
management practices (BMPs) to reduce diffuse 
pollution from aquaculture farms based on the results 
of this study and conventional aquaculture operations 
conducted in Taiwan. 
Keywords: constructed wetlands; recirculating 
aquaculture; shrimp; pond effluent; pollution 
management 
 
1. Introduction 
World aquaculture continues to grow more rapidly 
than all other animal food-producing sectors [1]. In 
Taiwan during 2007, aquaculture provided about 0.32 
million metric tons of food production that 
represented a mean market value of about one billion 
US dollars annually [2]. Total areas for pond 
aquaculture were more than 55,000 ha, of which 75% 
were conducted by inland earthen pond culture. 
However, aquaculture has also caused significant 
impacts on environments and natural resources. Of 
these impacts, water pollution by aquaculture effluent 
is one of the serious concerns from publics [3]. 
Management of pollution from effluent is therefore 
crucial to achieve environmental responsibility and 
sustainability for aquaculture in future development. 
Pond effluent is normally produced when ponds are 
drained during harvest or when water is exchanged in 
response to deterioration of water quality of ponds. It 
typically contains particulates, organic matter, 
nitrogen, and phosphorous. Aquaculture effluent is 
characterized by relatively dilute concentration of 
pollutants and very large amount of volumes when 
discharging irregularly at various points, seeming like 
diffuse or non-point source pollution rather than point 
source pollution [3].   
To manage such type of pollution, some nations 
including Taiwan and non-governmental 
organizations have developed water quality standards 
for aquaculture effluent (Table 1) [3, 4]. However, it 
is difficult for aquaculture farms to comply with these 
effluent standards because most of farms in the major 
aquaculture nations are small and operated by 
individuals with little technical knowledge and 
finance. Therefore, development of technically 
efficient and low-cost treatment methods is 
imperative for effective management of aquaculture 
effluent in compliance with effluent standards. 
Application of best management practices (BMPs) is 
another approach and could be affordable for farmers 
to improve the quality and reduce the volume of 
aquaculture effluents [3]. Some BMPs have been 
suggested to minimize nutrient loads in effluents, 
including not using water exchange or reducing water 
exchange as much as possible, discharging pond 
draining effluent through a settling basin, and reusing 
water where possible. Still, more technically and 
economically efficient ways to implement these 
principles of BMPs are required.  
Constructed wetland (CW) is an efficient and cost 
effective ecotechnology for water pollution control. 
Since early 1970s, the technology has been used to 
treat a wide variety of wastewater including domestic 
wastewater, acid mine drainage, industrial 
wastewater, agricultural and urban storm runoff, and 
effluent from livestock operations [5]. For the past 
decade, several researches have demonstrated the use 
of constructed wetland for treatment of water and 
wastewater from aquaculture operations, including 
fishpond effluent [6-13], shrimp pond effluent [4, 14], 
saline aquaculture effluent [15], recirculation

2.2 Water sampling and analysis 
Water samples were taken weekly from the two 
shrimp ponds, and from the influent and effluent of 
the CW. The sampling was usually performed at 
around 10 A.M. on each sampling date. The samples 
were analyzed for total suspended solids (TSS), 
turbidity, and chlorophyll a, while filtered samples 
were analyzed for 5-day biochemical oxygen demand 
(BOD5), chemical oxygen demand (COD), NH4-N, 
NO2-N, NO3-N, total Kjeldahl nitrogen (TKN), and 
total phosphorous (TP). Temperature, pH, 
conductivity, and dissolved oxygen (DO) of water in 
the sampling locations were also monitored.  
Several variables, including reduction percentage of 
influent-effluent concentration of pollutants, area 
removal rate, and first-order removal rate constant (k), 
were further calculated according to the methods 
reported in previous studies [16-17] so as to evaluate 
the performance of the CW. 
2.3 Statistical analysis 
All statistical analyses were performed using a 
statistical graphic system (Origin version 6.0) by 
Microcal Software, Inc. Paired t-test was used to test 
for significant difference in water quality between 
influent and effluent of the CW. One-way ANOVA 
was used to determine significance in water quality 
between two shrimp ponds. 
 
3. Results and Discussions 
Average flow rate of recirculating water ranged from 
306 to 473 m3/day during the three experimental 
phases, which corresponded to a relatively high 
hydraulic loading rate of 2.92~4.51 m/d to the CW 
and a very short hydraulic retention time of 
4.54~7.02 hr in the CW (Table 2). Unlike CWs for 
domestic or industrial wastewater treatment that are 
normally operated under low  HLR less than 0.1 m/d 
[5], recent studies have exhibited aquaculture effluent 
and recirculating aquaculture water can be efficiently 
purified by CWs under extremely high HLRs of 
1.54~2.82 [17-18], 3.03 [19], and even up to 10.6-
28.9 m/d [12-13]. Implementation of high HLR 
operation suggests that wetland area required for 
pollution management of aquaculture water and 
effluent may be potentially reduced.

3.6 Planning of CW application to BMPs  
Base on the results of the study and conventional 
aquaculture operations conducted in Taiwan, three 
kinds of wetland treatment applications to implement 
the BMPs for reducing diffuse pollution from 
aquaculture farms are proposed.  
Because uncontaminated surface water or 
groundwater resource has become scarce for decades, 
one major portion of inland culture farms conduct 
aquaculture with limited discharge or discharge only 
when pond water exchanging. Under such situation, 
the CW can be integrated with the pond to operate a 
recirculating aquaculture mode (Figure 2(a)), similar 
to the studied site of this paper. Wetland treatment 
and recirculation would be beneficial to maintain 
good water quality for fish or shrimp culture and to 
reduce farm effluent or even achieve zero effluent 
during culture. Thus, water exchange to improve 
pond water quality would be not necessary. During 
harvest or regulation of pond water quality, effluent 
can be safely discharged to receiving water body 
from the treated effluent of CW (Figure 2a), which 
could always comply with the effluent standards. 
Furthermore, when a new batch culture is initiated, 
the CW can act as a biofilter to purify the inflow 
water resource that may be somewhat contaminated. 
The extent of water recirculation is determined by 
farmers with considering pumping cost and pond 
water quality expected. 
Another major portion of aquaculture farms sprawl 
on the coastal area of Taiwan, exploiting seawater for 
culture when tide rising, and discharging the effluent 
when tide ebbing. For such flow-through aquaculture 
farms, the CW can be used simply to receive and 
purify the pond effluent before discharging to water 
body of sea via conduits (Figure 2b). In case the 
inflow saline water is also somewhat contaminated, 
part of the CW can be designed to treat the inflow 
water with another part to remediate the pond effluent 
(Figure 2c). 
The wetland treatment applications could fulfill the 
principles of BMPs that are mentioned above, 
including not using water exchange or reducing water 
exchange as much as possible, discharging pond 
draining effluent through efficient treatment, and/or 
reusing water. 
 
4. Conclusions 
The Results of this study show that constructed 
wetlands effectively and consistently reduced TSS 
(54~78%), turbidity (52~65%), chlorophyll a 
(54~68%), and BOD5 (27~46 %) from the 
recirculating shrimp culture water, resulting in a 
treated effluent with good quality that is suitable for 
recycling and reuse to shrimp ponds and in turn 
satisfactory to regulate water quality of the pond 
when pond water would not to be exchanged or 
renewed. These benefits are demonstrated by the 
findings that water quality (e.g. TSS, turbidity, 
chlorophyll a, BOD5, COD) of the recirculating pond
