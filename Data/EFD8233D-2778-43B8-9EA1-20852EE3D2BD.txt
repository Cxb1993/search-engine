中文摘要 
摻和醇胺(三乙醇胺/對二氮已環)水溶液吸收二氧化碳之反應動力學量測研究 
NSC 96-2221-E-033-020 中原大學化工系 李夢輝 
關鍵詞：二氧化碳，三乙醇胺，對二氮已環，摻合醇胺水溶液，酸性氣體吸收，反應動力學 
本研究為探討摻和醇胺(三乙醇胺/對二氮已環)水溶液吸收二氧化碳之反應動力學量測研究，
所探討之系统為：CO2/TEA/H2O；CO2/PZ/H2O；CO2/PZ/TEA/H2O。所量測之系统濃度範圍：
CO2/TEA/H2O將為1.0，1.5 及2.0 kmol/m3 TEA水溶液；及CO2/PZ/H2O將為0.05，0.1、0.2、0.3及0.4 
kmol/m3 PZ水溶液；CO2/PZ/TEA/H2O則為0.1、0.2、0.3及0.4 kmol/m3 PZ分別加入1.0及1.5 kmol/m3 
TEA水溶液中。溫度範圍將為30、35及40 oC。所量測之吸收反應動力學數據由擬一級快速反應模
式來描述CO2與醇胺之反應動力行為，而對於PZ、TEA則皆使用一級反應的反應動力學模式來探
討，同時也由簡化結合質傳及化學反應平衡動力學模式來表示。 
二氧化碳與醇胺水溶液之比吸收速率將由溼壁柱來測量。醇胺水溶液之物性數據如密度、黏度
以及氧化亞氮在混合醇胺水溶液之亨利常數、擴散係數等也同時量測，而二氧化碳在混合醇胺水溶
液之亨利常數、擴散係數將由氧化亞氮相似定律估算。所量測之吸收反應動力學數據由擬一級快速
反應模式及簡化結合質傳及化學反應平衡動力學模式來表示。所得之二氧化碳與醇胺PZ、TEA之
反應速率常數適用於CO2/TEA/H2O、CO2/PZ/H2O、CO2/PZ/TEA/H2O，應可完整描述摻和醇胺及單
一醇胺水溶液吸收二氧化碳之反應機構。 
近年來使用摻和醇胺水溶液可有效加速與二氧化碳之反應速率及增加二氧化碳之吸收負載已
為相關學者們所得之共同結論。PZ/MDEA/H2O在文献上已被認為新類型之酸性氣體吸收配方，而
PZ/TEA/H2O具有相似之吸收特性，故應為另一具潛力值得開發之酸性氣體吸收配方。本研究之成
果可提供CO2/PZ/TEA/H2O之反應動力學基礎數據及相關之反應速率常數等，可作為設計吸收設備
之依據。

通常三級醇胺會比一級(或二級)醇胺使用較低之蒸氣用量(Kohl and Nielsen, 1997a)。 
摻和醇胺可擷取各醇胺之優點以截長補短，應用上藉著組成的自由度，對氣體吸收
負載、吸收速率、氣提速率、能源損耗，皆可就特有之酸性氣體吸收程序加以有效設計，
以適當比例混合應用。Chakravarty et al. (1985)發現利用摻合醇胺處理天然氣、精製氣、
合成氣時，會使程序中的吸收效果得到明顯的改善，同時在汽提上節省很多能源需求。
Chakravarty等提出了MEA/MDEA摻合醇胺的建議。Chakravarty等指出了在27 wt % 
MDEA + 3 wt % MEA之摻合醇胺在吸收塔中可達到30 wt % MEA吸收酸性氣體之效
果；而同時在汽提塔中也僅需如MDEA使用較低之蒸氣用量。摻合醇胺由於多了一個組
成的自由度，以適當比例混合，其吸收效果對吸收容量、吸收速率、氣提速率、能源損
耗，皆有明顯改善。 
有效設計氣體吸收相關設備如吸收塔、汽提塔、蒸氣用量、循環量等需要仰賴基礎
吸收數據之量測及預估計算。基礎吸收數據包括：氣液平衡，亨利常數，擴散係數，醇
胺與酸性氣體之反應動力學數據，醇胺比熱，與酸性氣體之反應熱，密度，黏度等。近
年來工業界及學術界對於摻和醇胺之研究皆著重於各基礎數據之量測，以建立完整之氣
體吸收基礎數據，作為設計氣體吸收塔及汽提塔之依據。摻和醇胺與酸性氣體二氧化碳
之反應動力學數據之量測及預估計算，在計算質傳速率及設計吸收程序所必須。Versteeg 
et al. (1990) 提出以摻和醇胺來吸收二氧化碳之質傳速率以聯立之可逆反應式來表示，
並以數值解法解出以薄膜理論(film theory)或滲透理論(penetration theory)之反應速率常
數及加强因子(enhancement factor)。在一級醇胺(MEA)滲和三級醇胺(MDEA、TEA)系統
方面；Glasscock et al. (1991)以流動式之液體攪拌槽反應器來量測MEA/MDEA/H2O及
DEA/MDEA/H2O吸收二氧化碳之反應速率。以攪拌槽反應器來量測MEA/MDEA/H2O及
MEA/TEA/H2O吸收二氧化碳之反應速率，並以擬一級反應(pseudo first order)來描述摻和
醇胺吸收二氧化碳之反應機構(Rangwala et al., 1992)。Hagewiesche et al. (1995)以層流液
體噴射吸收器(laminar liquid jet absorber)量測MEA/MDEA/H2O在25oC吸收二氧化碳之反
應速率，並以滲透理論為主，反應視為平行可逆反應，而以數值方法解得比吸收速率及
加强因子。以層流液體噴射吸收器及液體攪拌槽反應器來量測DEA/MDEA/H2O吸收二
氧化碳之反應速率，並以一嚴謹完整之理論模式來描述二氧化碳之吸收(Rinker et al., 
2000)。以圓盤支柱吸收器(disk column)來量測DEA/MDEA/H2O吸收二氧化碳之反應速

反應速率。Zhang et al. (2001)也以圓柱吸收器來量測PZ/MDEA/H2O吸收二氧化碳之反應
速率，同時以反應機構以piperazine carbamate存在之下來表示。除了PZ摻和MDEA水溶
液外，Seo and Hong (2000)也以溼圓球汽液吸收設備來量測PZ/AMP/H2O吸收二氧化碳之
反應速率，反應機構以複合離子反應機構來表示二氧化碳與AMP及以擬二級反應來表示
二氧化碳與PZ之反應。Sun and Li (2005)以短溼壁柱來量測PZ/AMP/H2O在温度30 – 40 
oC吸收二氧化碳之反應速率，並以快速擬一級反應及複合離子反應機構來分別描述二氧
化碳與PZ及AMP之反應速率。文献上所得之PZ摻和醇胺水溶液和二氧化碳之反應速率
常數各學者所决定之值未必完全相同(25 oC, 4096 – 53700 m3/(kmol s)) (Bishnoi and 
Rochelle, 2000; Seo and Hong, 2000)，但PZ可加速醇胺 (MDEA或AMP)水溶液和二氧化
碳之反應速率則是一致的結論。

(1982)提出了一漸近解析解來解得聯立徧微分-代數式，其法為在任何一點之平衡濃度以
時間平均濃度來漸近，以求得吸收二氧化碳之加强因子。Hagewiesche et al. (1995)以一

轉換為聯立一階(lst order)對時間之微分方程式，此法用來探討摻和醇胺MEA/MDEA水

用Hagewiesche et al. (1995)之數值方法，結合質傳及化學反應平衡模式也被應用在探討
DEA水溶液吸收二氧化碳之反應動力學(Rinker et al., 1996)。利用類似之數值方法，結合
質傳及化學反應平衡模式也被應用在探討DEA/MDEA水溶液吸收二氧化碳之反應動力
學(Rinker et al., 2000)。修正Hagewiesche et al. (1995)之數值方法，結合質傳及化學反應
平衡模式也被應用在探討DEA/AMP水溶液吸收二氧化碳之反應動力學(Mandal et al., 
2003a)。結合質傳及化學反應平衡動力學模式之優點，包括其為一嚴謹而完整的描述二
氧化碳吸收入液相中所發生之各種反應，故所得之結果可正確代表二氧化碳和醇胺反應
的各種情形；唯此模式須先以數值法解出聯立徧微分-代數式，再和實驗數值來對照，
以决定出正確的二氧化碳與醇胺之反應機構，故此法中正確的數值法為必備的重要工
具。另外在計算過程中，也需要一些正確的熱物性質例如物理溶解度(亨利常數)、二氧
化碳在溶液中之擴散係數、醇胺在溶液中之擴散係數及各反應之平衡常數等。在使用正
確的相關熱物性質，結合質傳及化學反應平衡動力學模式應為嚴謹而完整的計算方法。 
TEA(triethanolamine)為最早被使用之酸性氣體吸收液，雖然屬於三級醇胺有較高之
二氧化碳吸收負載量，但由於其與二氧化碳反應速率較慢，限制了其廣泛的使用(Kohl 
and Nielsen, 1997a)。以PZ加入與二氧化碳反應速率較慢的三級醇胺醇胺 (如MDEA)或
立體障礙醇胺(如AMP)，以加速其吸收反應速率，已發展為新的酸性氣體吸收液配方(Xu 
et al., 1992; Seo and Hong, 1999, 2000; Zhang et al., 2001; Sun et al., 2005)；TEA和醇胺
MDEA或AMP相較，同樣有高的二氧化碳吸收負載量特性，若加入PZ應可有效改善其吸
收特性，但目前文献上尚未有學者探討以PZ/TEA/H2O為酸性氣體吸收液以吸收二氧化
碳。

 (E) PZ + TEA + H2O 吸收二氧化碳之比吸收速率量測 
二氧化碳吸收速率(單位時間，單位面積)量測所使用的氣液接觸設備是如同量測擴
散係數的濕壁柱裝置。二氧化碳的吸收速率可對醇胺水溶液取樣，滴定分析其反應前後
loading (每莫爾醇胺吸收二氧化碳的莫爾數)的差值，並與醇胺水溶液的體積流率、醇胺
濃度的乘積得知，或是直接由連接進出口的兩支泡沫流量計的流速差而得。 
溼壁柱測量氣體在吸收溶劑中之吸收速率(單位時間，單位面積)步驟如下： 
1. 恆溫水槽之溫度由加熱器控制至所欲量測溫度。此恆溫水循環流經溼壁柱外之
套管以保持溼壁柱於一定溫度。 
2. 吸收溶劑槽之溫度由加熱器控制至所欲量測溫度，系統加入吸收溶劑前，先通
入吸收氣體，系統出口保持開啟，以使吸收氣體連續進入系統，約十分鐘，以
清除系統內可能存在之微量氧氣，並使被吸收氣體充滿系統。系統加入吸收溶
劑，並調整流量於3≈6 ml/s，並使溼壁柱之液膜形成層流。 
3. 出入囗氣體流量可由泡沫流量計測得，並調整氣體流量於15 ≈ 20 ml/s。

(E) 氧化亞氮類比理論(N2O Analogy) 
因為二氧化碳會與醇胺水溶液產生化學反應，無法單純量測物理溶解度。首先由Clarke 
(1964) 提出，使用具有與二氧化碳相似分子結構、分子體積與電子組態的N2O氧化亞氮，取代
CO2作為量測的氣體，根據類比理論的方法，決定二氧化碳在醇胺水溶液中的物理溶解度。 
Laddha et al. (1981)量測數種不與CO2及N2O產生化學反應的有機醇和純水的物理溶
解度，發現定溫 25℃時二氧化碳與氧化亞氮，對於各個不同的有機醇類的溶解度具有
一固定比值1.37，證實了類比理論。因此CO2在醇胺水溶液的物理溶解度量測在此理論
下大量應用，也推廣至CO2在醇胺水溶液的擴散係數的估算。

(A) 擴散-反應之聯立徧微分-代數方程式之電腦計算模式建立 
本研究是以結合質傳及化學反應平衡動力學模式之計算程式，結合初始條件及邊界
條件，並代入之正確的熱物性質例如物理溶解度(亨利常數)、二氧化碳在溶液中之擴散
係數、醇胺在溶液中之擴散係數及各反應之平衡常數等可以求得反應膜內各成份之濃度
和時間、位置之函數解，由二氧化碳在溶液中之濃度，計算鄰近汽液界面之濃度斜率對
時間積分由0至汽液接觸時間θ，可得平均比吸收速率，再進而求得醇胺吸收二氧化碳之
加强因子，此平均比吸收速率再與實驗所量測之比吸收速率作比較，進而決定二氧化碳
與PZ及TEA之反應速率常數。計算時須首先計算各成分反應物在吸收二氧化碳前之起始
濃度，以為計算中之起始條件。由式(27)至式(36)計算出之吸收二氧化碳前之起始濃度
和溫度、醇胺起始濃度及二氧化碳反應前之吸收負載。在30oC，AMP濃度 2.0 kmol．
m-3 ，PZ分別為0.1, 0.2, 0.3, 0.4( kmol⋅m-3 )時所解出之初始濃度 01u ~ 010u 計算結果為列於
Table 41中。在實際動力學數據中吸收負載為二氧化碳反應前之量測值，求得反應速率
常數時將使用為起始條件。 
二氧化碳反應動力學測試部份，對於文獻中己知系統CO2/DEA/AMP/H2O由10個偏

Measurements of Ternary Diffusion Coefficients of Aqueous Blended 
Alkanolamine systems: Monoethanolamine + 
N-methyldiethanolamine + Water 
 
 
Chih-Chiang Ko, Po-Hsun Lin, and Meng-Hui Li 
 113
Diffusion coefficients, viscosity, and density are thermophysical properties and are 
needed in the evaluation of mass transport properties. The rate of molecular diffusion in liquid 
is normally the rate-determining factor in unit operations, such as the absorption of acid gases 
in alkanolamine solutions and heterogeneous gas-liquid chemical reactions. Diffusion 
coefficients are also useful for investigating the structure of liquids and developing theories of 
liquid states (Tyrrell and Harris, 1984). The mutual diffusion coefficients in aqueous 
alkanolamine solutions have been reported in the literature such as MEA, MIPA 
(monoisopropanolamine), DEA, DIPA, DGA, EDA (ethylenediamine) and TEA (Hikita et al., 
1980, 1981); MEA, DEA, MDEA, and DIPA (Snijder et al., 1993); MDEA (Rowley et al., 
1997); TEA (Leaist et al., 1998); DGA, TEA, AMP, and 2-PE (Chang et al., 2005). The 
Taylor dispersion technique had been used frequently for measuring binary diffusion 
coefficients of various solutions (Taylor, 1953; Alizadeh et al., 1980; Baldauf and Knapp, 
1982; Leaist et al., 1998). In the Taylor experiment a narrow band of solution is injected into 
a laminar carrier stream of different composition which flows in a long capillary tube. The 
injected solutes spread out as they flow along the tube. A differential refractometer monitors 
the broadened distribution of dispersed solutes as they flow out of the tube. The Taylor 
dispersion technique for measuring binary diffusion was extended to three- and 
four-component systems (Deng and Leaist, 1991). Ternary diffusion coefficients in liquids 
which have been reported in the literature are catalogued into four classes of systems, 
electrolytes (e.g., LiCl-NaCl-water), electrolyte-non-electrolyte mixtures (e.g., 
KCl-glycine-water), non-electrolytes in water (glycine – sucrose - water), and organic liquids 
(e.g., acetone-benzene-cyclohexane) (Cussler, 1976). Employing the advantages of each 
amine, the aqueous blended amines have been proposed to be used for acid gases removal in 
the absorption processes (Chakravarty et al., 1985). Blended amines such as MEA + MDEA + 
H2O, MEA + AMP+ H2O have been suggested for acid gases removal (Kohl and Nielsen, 
1997). Some thermophysical properties such as density, viscosity, Henry’s constant, 
diffusivity of a gas in liquid, and reaction kinetics data for the CO2 absorption into aqueous 
blended alkanolamine have been reported in the literature. However, the ternary mutual 
diffusivity coefficient of aqueous blended alkanolamine solutions has not yet been reported in 
the literature. Thus, it is the objective of this research to measure the ternary mutual 
diffusivity coefficients in MEA + MDEA + H2O systems.

(monoisopropanolamine), DEA, DIPA, DGA, EDA (ethylenediamine) and TEA (Hikita et al.,

The Taylor dispersion technique had been used frequently for measuring binary diffusion 
coefficients of various solutions (Taylor, 1953; Alizadeh et al., 1980a; Baldauf and Knapp, 
1983; Leaist et al., 1998). In the Taylor experiment a narrow band of solution is injected into 
a laminar carrier stream of different composition which flows in a long capillary tube. The 
injected solutes spread out as they flow along the tube. A differential refractometer monitors 
the broadened distribution of dispersed solutes as they flow out of the tube. The Taylor 
dispersion technique for measuring binary diffusion was extended to three- and 
four-component systems (Deng and Leaist, 1991). Ternary diffusion coefficients in liquids 
reported in the literature were catalogued into four classes of systems, electrolytes (e.g., 
LiCl-NaCl-water), electrolyte-non-electrolyte mixtures (e.g., KCl-glycine-water), 
non-electrolytes in water (glycine – sucrose - water), and organic liquids (e.g., 
acetone-benzene-cyclohexane) (Cussler, 1976). 
Employing the advantages of each amine, the aqueous blended amines have been proposed to 
be used for acid gases removal in the absorption processes (Chakravarty et al., 1985). Blended 
amines such as MEA + MDEA + H2O, MEA + AMP+ H2O have been suggested for acid 
gases removal (Kohl and Nielsen, 1997b). Some thermophysical properties such as density, 
viscosity, Henry’s constant, diffusivity of a gas in liquid, and reaction kinetics data for the 
CO2 absorption into aqueous blended alkanolamine have been reported in the literature. The 
ternary mutual diffusivity coefficients of MEA + MDEA + H2O were reported using the 
Taylor dispersion technique (Ko et al., 2006). However, the ternary mutual diffusivity 
coefficient of DEA + MDEA + H2O has not yet been reported in the literature. Thus, it is the 
objective of this research to measure the ternary mutual diffusivity coefficients in DEA + 
MDEA + H2O systems.

5. Conclusion 
Ternary diffusion coefficients of aqueous blended alkanolamine systems diethanolamine (DEA) + 
N-methyldiethanolamine (MDEA) + water using the Taylor dispersion technique have been measured 
for temperatures 30, 40 and 50 oC. The systems studied were aqueous solutions containing the total 
amine concentrations of 2, 3, and 4 kmol⋅m-3 with three (or four) molar amine ratios. For a DEA + 
MDEA + water system, D11 was found to be increase as the temperature and the molar ratio increase. 
Both of D11 and D22 decrease as the viscosity of solution increases. The ratio of D12 to D11 varies from 
-0.132 to -0.219 while the ratio of D21 to D22 about 0.003 to 0.022. Thus, for DEA the cross diffusion 
effect due to the concentration gradient of MDEA is larger than that for MDEA due to the 
concentration gradient of DEA. The predicted values from the Onsager phenomenological coefficients, 
the activity, and partial molar volume of component are generally follows the trend of the 
experimental values. The predicted values can usually provide good initial estimates values.

In this study, the mutual diffusion coefficients of some ionic liquids in water at infinite dilution 
for temperatures (303.2 to 323.2) K were measured using the Taylor dispersion technique 
(Alizadeh et al., 1980b; Snijder et al., 1993b; Kao and Li, 2006a; Su et al., 2007). The 
investigated ionic liquids were as follows: 1-ethyl-3-methylimidazolium dicyanamide 
[Emim][C2N3], 1-ethyl-3-methylimidazolium tetrafluoroborate [Emim][BF4], 
1-ethyl-3-methylimidazolium ethylsulfate [Emim][C2H5SO4],  1-ethyl-3-methylimidazolium 
trifluoromethanesulfonate [Emim][CF3SO3], and 1-ethyl-3-methylimidazolium 
2-(2-methoxyethoxy) ethylsulfate [Emim][MDEGSO4]. The present diffusion coefficients data 
were correlated using two different approaches: (a) the diffusing component was treated as 
an organic solute using the Wilke−Chang equation (Wilke and Chang, 1955) and (b) as an 
electrolyte solute using the Nernst−Haskell equation (Robinson and Stokes, 1965). The 
Wilke−Chang equation (Wilke and Chang, 1955) underestimated the present data of diffusion 
coefficients, thus, it was modified by introducing a temperature-dependent empirical 
parameters and the results were satisfactory. The Nernst−Haskell equation (Robinson and 
Stokes, 1965) requires infinite dilution conductances of ions and to this end, the molar 
conductivities of several infinitely dilute concentrations of aqueous ionic solutions for the 
temperatures (303.2 to 323.2) K were also measured using a commercial conductivity meter. 
The Nernst−Haskell equation (Robinson and Stokes, 1965) successfully predicted some 
diffusion coefficients using the obtained infinite dilution conductances. 
 
The Taylor dispersion apparatus used in this study was described previously by Kao and Li 
(Kao and Li, 2006a). In this work, the mutual diffusion coefficients of the investigated ionic 
liquids in water were measured at infinite dilution. The samples of dilute solutions of ionic 
liquids in water were injected into a carrier fluid of pure deionized water. The concentration of 
the solution greatly affects the diffusion coefficient. To this end, the concentration of the 
injected samples were kept as dilute as possible so that the measured diffusion coefficients 
were not influenced by concentrations of the samples, but still a steady detectable signal

Since the vapor pressure of ILs is very small, the most likely way for them to pollute the 
environment is in the waste water stream. To predict the behavior of a chemical substance in 
the waste water stream, it is important to know such physical properties as partition and 
sorption coefficients and solubility in water. For several imidazolium-based ILs, Ropel et al. 
(2005), reported the partition coefficients (index of bioaccumulation in an aquatic environment) 
to the mixture of 1-octanol and water. The study of Wong et al. (2002) reported the solubility of 
[emim][PF6] and [bmim][PF6] in water.  
Aside from the previously mentioned properties, another important property that can be used 
to determine the ability of IL to spread in the environmental stream (water) is the diffusion 
coefficients. In the past years, a number of measurements of diffusion coefficient for different 
species such as carbon dioxide and several hydrofluorocarbons, in ILs were reported by 
different research groups (Every et al., 2000; Every et al., 2004; Morgan et al., 2005; Shiflett 
and Yokozeki, 2005; Shiflett and Yokozeki, 2006). However, there were few data of diffusion 
coefficients of ILs in solvents, even in water. Richter et al. (2003) reported the diffusion 
coefficient measurements of molten salts and ionic liquids in methanol using digital image 
holography. Su et al. (2007) reported diffusion coefficient measurements of alkylimidazolium 
tetrafluoroborates and hexafluorophosphates in water at 303.2 K using Taylor dispersion 
experiment. 
Thus, in this present work, we reported the measurements of diffusion coefficients of five 
[Emim]-based ILs for the temperatures ranging from (303.2 to 323.2) K using Taylor 
dispersion method. The studied ILs were [Emim][C2N3], [Emim][BF4], [Emim][EtSO4], 
[Emim][CF3SO3], and [Emim][MDEGSO4]. The present diffusion coefficients data were 
correlated using two different approaches: (a) the diffusing component was treated as an 
organic solute using the Wilke−Chang equation (Wilke and Chang, 1955) and (b) as an 
electrolyte solute using the Nernst−Haskell equation (Robinson and Stokes, 1965). In the 
prediction of diffusion coefficients using Wilke−Chang equation, the density values of the 
solute (IL) and viscosity of the solvent (water) were needed, so the densities and viscosities at 
the studied isotherms, (303.2 to 323.2) K, were also determined. The densities of ILs were 
measured while the viscosities of water were calculated from a reliable correlation (Hsu and Li, 
1997a). The use of Nernst−Haskell equation requires an infinite dilution conductance of 
individual cations and anions, thus, the molar conductivities of the investigated ILs in water at 
various concentrations were also measured.

Measurements of Ternary Diffusion Coefficients of Aqueous Blended 
Alkanolamine systems: Monoethanolamine + 
N-methyldiethanolamine + Water 
 
 
Chih-Chiang Ko, Po-Hsun Lin, and Meng-Hui Li 
Diffusion coefficients, viscosity, and density are thermophysical properties and are 
needed in the evaluation of mass transport properties. The rate of molecular diffusion in liquid 
is normally the rate-determining factor in unit operations, such as the absorption of acid gases 
in alkanolamine solutions and heterogeneous gas-liquid chemical reactions. Diffusion 
coefficients are also useful for investigating the structure of liquids and developing theories of 
liquid states (Tyrrell and Harris, 1984). The mutual diffusion coefficients in aqueous 
alkanolamine solutions have been reported in the literature such as MEA, MIPA 
(monoisopropanolamine), DEA, DIPA, DGA, EDA (ethylenediamine) and TEA (Hikita et al., 
1980, 1981); MEA, DEA, MDEA, and DIPA (Snijder et al., 1993); MDEA (Rowley et al., 
1997); TEA (Leaist et al., 1998); DGA, TEA, AMP, and 2-PE (Chang et al., 2005). The 
Taylor dispersion technique had been used frequently for measuring binary diffusion 
coefficients of various solutions (Taylor, 1953; Alizadeh et al., 1980; Baldauf and Knapp, 
1982; Leaist et al., 1998). In the Taylor experiment a narrow band of solution is injected into 
a laminar carrier stream of different composition which flows in a long capillary tube. The 
injected solutes spread out as they flow along the tube. A differential refractometer monitors 
the broadened distribution of dispersed solutes as they flow out of the tube. The Taylor 
dispersion technique for measuring binary diffusion was extended to three- and 
four-component systems (Deng and Leaist, 1991). Ternary diffusion coefficients in liquids 
which have been reported in the literature are catalogued into four classes of systems, 
electrolytes (e.g., LiCl-NaCl-water), electrolyte-non-electrolyte mixtures (e.g., 
KCl-glycine-water), non-electrolytes in water (glycine – sucrose - water), and organic liquids 
(e.g., acetone-benzene-cyclohexane) (Cussler, 1976). Employing the advantages of each 
amine, the aqueous blended amines have been proposed to be used for acid gases removal in 
the absorption processes (Chakravarty et al., 1985). Blended amines such as MEA + MDEA + 
H2O, MEA + AMP+ H2O have been suggested for acid gases removal (Kohl and Nielsen, 
1997). Some thermophysical properties such as density, viscosity, Henry’s constant, 
diffusivity of a gas in liquid, and reaction kinetics data for the CO2 absorption into aqueous 
blended alkanolamine have been reported in the literature. However, the ternary mutual 
diffusivity coefficient of aqueous blended alkanolamine solutions has not yet been reported in 
the literature. Thus, it is the objective of this research to measure the ternary mutual 
diffusivity coefficients in MEA + MDEA + H2O systems.

2. Measurements of Ternary Diffusion Coefficients of Aqueous 
Blended Alkanolamine systems: Diethanolamine + 
N-methyldiethanolamine + Water 
theories of liquid states (Tyrrell and Harris, 1984). The mutual diffusion coefficients in 
aqueous alkanolamine solutions have been reported in the literature such as MEA, MIPA 
(monoisopropanolamine), DEA, DIPA, DGA, EDA (ethylenediamine) and TEA (Hikita et al., 
1980; Hikita et al., 1981); MEA, DEA, MDEA, and DIPA (Snijder et al., 1993a); MDEA 
(Rowley et al., 1997); TEA (Leaist et al., 1998); DGA, TEA, AMP, and 2-PE (Chang et al.,

The Taylor dispersion technique had been used frequently for measuring binary diffusion 
coefficients of various solutions (Taylor, 1953; Alizadeh et al., 1980a; Baldauf and Knapp, 
1983; Leaist et al., 1998). In the Taylor experiment a narrow band of solution is injected into 
a laminar carrier stream of different composition which flows in a long capillary tube. The 
injected solutes spread out as they flow along the tube. A differential refractometer monitors 
the broadened distribution of dispersed solutes as they flow out of the tube. The Taylor 
dispersion technique for measuring binary diffusion was extended to three- and 
four-component systems (Deng and Leaist, 1991). Ternary diffusion coefficients in liquids 
reported in the literature were catalogued into four classes of systems, electrolytes (e.g., 
LiCl-NaCl-water), electrolyte-non-electrolyte mixtures (e.g., KCl-glycine-water), 
non-electrolytes in water (glycine – sucrose - water), and organic liquids (e.g., 
acetone-benzene-cyclohexane) (Cussler, 1976). 
Employing the advantages of each amine, the aqueous blended amines have been proposed to 
be used for acid gases removal in the absorption processes (Chakravarty et al., 1985). Blended 
amines such as MEA + MDEA + H2O, MEA + AMP+ H2O have been suggested for acid 
gases removal (Kohl and Nielsen, 1997b). Some thermophysical properties such as density, 
viscosity, Henry’s constant, diffusivity of a gas in liquid, and reaction kinetics data for the 
CO2 absorption into aqueous blended alkanolamine have been reported in the literature. The 
ternary mutual diffusivity coefficients of MEA + MDEA + H2O were reported using the 
Taylor dispersion technique (Ko et al., 2006). However, the ternary mutual diffusivity 
coefficient of DEA + MDEA + H2O has not yet been reported in the literature. Thus, it is the 
objective of this research to measure the ternary mutual diffusivity coefficients in DEA + 
MDEA + H2O systems.

5. Conclusion 
5.1. Ternary diffusion coefficients of aqueous blended alkanolamine systems diethanolamine (DEA) + 
N-methyldiethanolamine (MDEA) + water using the Taylor dispersion technique have been measured 
for temperatures 30, 40 and 50 oC. The systems studied were aqueous solutions containing the total 
amine concentrations of 2, 3, and 4 kmol⋅m-3 with three (or four) molar amine ratios. For a DEA + 
MDEA + water system, D11 was found to be increase as the temperature and the molar ratio increase. 
Both of D11 and D22 decrease as the viscosity of solution increases. The ratio of D12 to D11 varies from 
-0.132 to -0.219 while the ratio of D21 to D22 about 0.003 to 0.022. Thus, for DEA the cross diffusion 
effect due to the concentration gradient of MDEA is larger than that for MDEA due to the 
concentration gradient of DEA. The predicted values from the Onsager phenomenological coefficients, 
the activity, and partial molar volume of component are generally follows the trend of the 
experimental values. The predicted values can usually provide good initial estimates values.

In this study, the mutual diffusion coefficients of some ionic liquids in water at infinite dilution 
for temperatures (303.2 to 323.2) K were measured using the Taylor dispersion technique 
(Alizadeh et al., 1980b; Snijder et al., 1993b; Kao and Li, 2006a; Su et al., 2007). The 
investigated ionic liquids were as follows: 1-ethyl-3-methylimidazolium dicyanamide 
[Emim][C2N3], 1-ethyl-3-methylimidazolium tetrafluoroborate [Emim][BF4], 
1-ethyl-3-methylimidazolium ethylsulfate [Emim][C2H5SO4],  1-ethyl-3-methylimidazolium 
trifluoromethanesulfonate [Emim][CF3SO3], and 1-ethyl-3-methylimidazolium 
2-(2-methoxyethoxy) ethylsulfate [Emim][MDEGSO4]. The present diffusion coefficients data 
were correlated using two different approaches: (a) the diffusing component was treated as 
an organic solute using the Wilke−Chang equation (Wilke and Chang, 1955) and (b) as an 
electrolyte solute using the Nernst−Haskell equation (Robinson and Stokes, 1965). The 
Wilke−Chang equation (Wilke and Chang, 1955) underestimated the present data of diffusion 
coefficients, thus, it was modified by introducing a temperature-dependent empirical 
parameters and the results were satisfactory. The Nernst−Haskell equation (Robinson and 
Stokes, 1965) requires infinite dilution conductances of ions and to this end, the molar 
conductivities of several infinitely dilute concentrations of aqueous ionic solutions for the 
temperatures (303.2 to 323.2) K were also measured using a commercial conductivity meter. 
The Nernst−Haskell equation (Robinson and Stokes, 1965) successfully predicted some 
diffusion coefficients using the obtained infinite dilution conductances. 
 
The Taylor dispersion apparatus used in this study was described previously by Kao and Li 
(Kao and Li, 2006a). In this work, the mutual diffusion coefficients of the investigated ionic 
liquids in water were measured at infinite dilution. The samples of dilute solutions of ionic 
liquids in water were injected into a carrier fluid of pure deionized water. The concentration of 
the solution greatly affects the diffusion coefficient. To this end, the concentration of the 
injected samples were kept as dilute as possible so that the measured diffusion coefficients 
were not influenced by concentrations of the samples, but still a steady detectable signal

Since the vapor pressure of ILs is very small, the most likely way for them to pollute the 
environment is in the waste water stream. To predict the behavior of a chemical substance in 
the waste water stream, it is important to know such physical properties as partition and 
sorption coefficients and solubility in water. For several imidazolium-based ILs, Ropel et al. 
(2005), reported the partition coefficients (index of bioaccumulation in an aquatic environment) 
to the mixture of 1-octanol and water. The study of Wong et al. (2002) reported the solubility of 
[emim][PF6] and [bmim][PF6] in water.  
Aside from the previously mentioned properties, another important property that can be used 
to determine the ability of IL to spread in the environmental stream (water) is the diffusion 
coefficients. In the past years, a number of measurements of diffusion coefficient for different 
species such as carbon dioxide and several hydrofluorocarbons, in ILs were reported by 
different research groups (Every et al., 2000; Every et al., 2004; Morgan et al., 2005; Shiflett 
and Yokozeki, 2005; Shiflett and Yokozeki, 2006). However, there were few data of diffusion 
coefficients of ILs in solvents, even in water. Richter et al. (2003) reported the diffusion 
coefficient measurements of molten salts and ionic liquids in methanol using digital image 
holography. Su et al. (2007) reported diffusion coefficient measurements of alkylimidazolium 
tetrafluoroborates and hexafluorophosphates in water at 303.2 K using Taylor dispersion 
experiment. 
Thus, in this present work, we reported the measurements of diffusion coefficients of five 
[Emim]-based ILs for the temperatures ranging from (303.2 to 323.2) K using Taylor 
dispersion method. The studied ILs were [Emim][C2N3], [Emim][BF4], [Emim][EtSO4], 
[Emim][CF3SO3], and [Emim][MDEGSO4]. The present diffusion coefficients data were 
correlated using two different approaches: (a) the diffusing component was treated as an 
organic solute using the Wilke−Chang equation (Wilke and Chang, 1955) and (b) as an 
electrolyte solute using the Nernst−Haskell equation (Robinson and Stokes, 1965). In the 
prediction of diffusion coefficients using Wilke−Chang equation, the density values of the 
solute (IL) and viscosity of the solvent (water) were needed, so the densities and viscosities at 
the studied isotherms, (303.2 to 323.2) K, were also determined. The densities of ILs were 
measured while the viscosities of water were calculated from a reliable correlation (Hsu and Li, 
1997a). The use of Nernst−Haskell equation requires an infinite dilution conductance of 
individual cations and anions, thus, the molar conductivities of the investigated ILs in water at 
various concentrations were also measured.

The diffusion coefficients of five [Emim]-based ionic liquids in water at infinite dilution 
were measured using Taylor dispersion method from temperatures of (303.2 to 323.2) 
K. The diffusion coefficients were well correlated by a modified Wilke−Chang equation 
in which a temperature-dependent correction factor was introduced to the 
Wilke−Chang correlation. The molar conductivities of the investigated ionic liquids 
were also measured to predict the diffusion coefficient using the infinite dilution 
conductances of the ions via the Nernst−Haskell equation. The results of the 
calculation of diffusion coefficients using the Nernst−Haskell equation were 
reasonably accurate.
