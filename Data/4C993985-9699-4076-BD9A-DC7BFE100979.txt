3.2 石英震盪天平(QCM) 
在這些量測方法中，可以有效監控生物膜質
量（厚度），準確度高，且逐漸受到重視者，係利
用石英晶體微量天平（quartz crystal microbalances, 
QCM）等壓電晶片（piezoelectric crystal）來進行
量測（Nivens 等，1995; Reipa 等，2006），其偵
測極限為每平方公分 3×105 cells。QCM 的原理係
將石英晶片特殊切割後，沿其晶片表面垂直方向
加入一震盪電場，則晶體內部會產生一機械振
盪。震盪的頻率與厚度、密度和接觸介質（例如
空氣或水）有關，若石英晶體的厚度一定，則此
機械振盪將以一固定的頻率產生，若晶體表面有
生物膜附著，或其他鍍層，則其震盪頻率將會改
變。早在 1959 年 Sauerbrey 即發現，其震盪頻率
的改變量Δf 與晶體上面累積的質量改變量Δm
成正比，亦即

購，許多套件已應用在生物晶片與生物感測器之
研發領域。 
 
3.3 抗菌薄膜製備 
2006 年本研究團隊本研究使用雙極反應式非
對稱脈衝直流磁控濺鍍法在矽晶片表面披覆氮化
鉻銀（Cr-Ag-N）薄膜，藉著改變銀靶功率，獲得
銀含量自 10%至 21%的三種 Cr-Ag-N 鍍膜。本研
究參酌相關規範，設計抑菌試驗，檢測不同銀含
量之薄膜對 E.coli、P. aeruginosa 之抑菌能力。實
驗結果顯示，不同銀含量、不同結構組成之含銀
奈米薄膜，對抑菌效果有相當明顯之差異。試片
之含銀量越高，大致可得到較佳之抑菌效率（張
育傑等，2006）。

銅含量自 1.6%至 24.4%的 Cr-Cu -N 鍍膜。試片之
含銅量越高，大致可得到較佳之抑菌效率，唯含

5.1.2 連續進流試驗 
在完成接種試驗後，本研究開始以配製的無
菌 MHB 生長基質進流到 QCM 模組中，此時，未
附著於 QCM 上之微生物，將會受進流基質帶出
系統，此舉將會使 QCM 系統的質量減輕。而在
接種期間已成功附著在 QCM 上的微生物（或形
成生物膜），將會因進流新鮮基質，而促進微生物
的增殖反應，從而使 QCM 系統質量增重。 
圖 4 為未經任何處理的 QCM 晶片，接種試
驗後，連續進流 MHB 的震盪頻率監測結果。可
以觀察得知，有別於接種期間的震盪頻率變化，
該 QCM 的震盪頻率隨時間而降低，顯示 QCM 上
附著的生物質量逐漸增高。在 100,000 秒的期間
內，其震盪頻率降低 1,200 Hz。 
將 QCM 晶片置換為本研究濺鍍、含銀量 34%
的 CrNAg 晶片，在接種完隔夜後，以 MHB 連續
進流，監測其震盪頻率變化，得到如圖 4 結果。
由圖 5 可觀察得知，隨著開始進流，QCM 震盪頻
率便緩慢增高，隨著時間增長，逐漸趨緩。推測
應是一開始進流，QCM 槽內的細菌，被連續進流
的基質沖出所致。隨後便趨於穩定。 
比較圖 4、圖 5 的頻率變化，可以發現，含
銀量 34%的 CrNAg 晶片，具有明顯的抗生物積垢
能力。生物膜不易在接種期間附著在 QCM 表面，
因而在進流 MHB 後，QCM 頻率變化顯示，並無
明顯生物增殖現象。

5.5 結論與建議 
本計畫主要所探討者，為微生物在金屬表面的附
著特性，以及利用抗菌金屬覆膜，對生物積垢的
抗積垢能力評估。研究結果顯示，利用石英震盪
天平的特性，可以有效且即時地監控微生物與金
屬介面之間所發生的反應。本研究所製備的含銀
奈米薄膜與含銅奈米薄膜皆可以此方式進行監
控。由於生物膜的形成過程中，生物膜的附著是
關鍵步驟，因此，本系統的即時監控功能，可為
防積垢材料的開發技術，提供很多關鍵資訊，對
於抗菌、抗積垢的材料開發，亦有相當大的助益。 
透過本研究，亦歸納出利用 QCM 評估薄膜表面
處理技術的抗積垢成效評估之限制：(一)主要限制
在於薄膜材料是否本身即有足夠抗化學腐蝕的能
力，特別是在生物培養條件下，與培養基混合所
處之高氯鹽環境。(二)其次為薄膜與 QCM 基材間
附著之機械強度，是否足以承受 QCM 之高頻震
盪。(三)最後，其中 QCM 成本較高，以及將材料
覆膜在 QCM 電極上，部分製程會有較大障礙等。

Abstract: 
TiO2 thin films were coated on 100 meshes stainless steel 304 (SS304) sieves by using the sol-gel 
method followed by 200oC thermal treatment in this study. The SEM-EDS analysis of the TiO2 
coatings showed the thin films were composed mainly of TiO2 and SS304 substrate, no significant 
levels of impurities were found using our coating process. The prepared TiO2-coated sieves were then 
employed to set up a modified version of out self-designed photocatalytic reactor to evaluate their 
bactericidal abilities. Little information about the ability of TiO2 thin films to kill bioaerosols has been 
reported before. The results show that the bactericidal efficiency rate improved from 42.2 to 70.6 % 
when the relative humidity values of the air decreased from 99 to 57% with a 20 l/min gas feed flow 
rate. Most bacteria (> 94%) can be removed after they have been treated by the designed 
photocatalytic reactor under a high fed flow rate (12.5 l/min). Compared to control experiments (UV 
OFF test) and to a high humidity test, the photokilling abilities of the developed TiO2 coated sieves, at 
low humidity, were demonstrated. It was also obvious that the removal efficiency was consistent when 
the reactor operated continuously for 3 hours. With the advantages of a large contact area with the 
target bioaerosol, low headloss, durable substrate, and easy maintenance, the TiO2-coated sieves 
possess high potential for application in the photocatalytic deactivation of airborne pathogens. 
Keywords: Bioaerosols, TiO2 thin films, Sol-gel method, Stainless sieves

2.3 Design of Photocatalytic reactor with TiO2-coated SS sieves.  
A specially designed phtocatalytic reactor with 6 cm inner diameter, 20 cm in length, was adopted in 
this work as shown in Fig. 1, which allowed the UV lamp (15W, Sankyo, Japan) with a maximum 
wavelength of 365 nm to be placed at the center of the reactor. Fig. 1 is a schematic diagram showing 
the proposed TiO2 thin films photocatalytic reactor system for evaluating the photokilling abilities of 
the developed photocatalytic reactor. The experimental system included a pure air supply unit (Fig. 
1a-d), a bioaerosol generation unit (Fig.1e, f), a photocatalytic reactor with TiO2 coated SS sieves 
(Fig.1h), and a bioaerosol sampling unit (Fig.1j, k). The bioaerosols used in the experiments were 
generated by a Collision six-jet nebulizer (BGI, USA) with an air flow of 20 l/min. The diffusion 
dryer is an optional setup for decreasing the humidity in the fed air (Fig.1g). A TiO2 coated sieve was 
rolled to form a cylindrical space between the UV blacklight lamp and the phtocatalytic reactor to 
force the bioaerosols to pass through the meshes of the TiO2 coated sieve (Fig. 1h). The photoreactor 
was constructed of glass with an effective volume of around 1.0 liter. A light flux of 2 mW/cm2 was 
measured using an industrial UV meter (UVR-T1, Topcon, Japan). The effective surface area of 
TiO2-coated sieve in the photocatalytic reactor is 168 cm2.

3.2 Photocatalytic reactor tests.  
After packing the TiO2 coated SS sieves around the UV lamp, the photocatalytic reactor (Fig. 1h) 
was connected directly to the bioaerosol nebulizer (Fig. 1f) to evaluate its bactericidal abilities on the 
airborne pathogens.  Figs. 3(a) and (b) show airborne CFUs on TSA medium, collected from the 
photocatalytic reactor exhausts when the UV lamp was off and when the lamp was on, respectively. 
The calculated bactericidal efficiency is 42.2% with a 20 l/min gas feed flow rate which was 
generated directly by a nebulizer with a 1.0 × 105 cells/ml cell suspension. Figs. 3(a) and (b) show 
the airborne pathogens in the inflow gas were obviously decreased by the photocatalytic reaction, 
demonstrating the TiO2 coated SS sieves can be used to treat bioaerosols under a flow velocity as high 
as 4.4 m/min. Since the photocatalytic reaction efficiencies often can be improved by decreasing the 
relative humidity in the air [17], a diffusion dryer (Fig. 1g) was connected to the nebulizer to decrease 
the relative humidity of the bioaerosol-containing air. The calculated bactericidal efficiency was 
improved from 42.2 to 70.6 % while the relative humidity values of the air decreased from 99 to 57% 
(Figs. 3c, d). It is also suggested that the reactor remove the bioaerosols mainly by the photocatalytic 
reaction caused by the TiO2 coated SS sieves, not by mechanical action.

4. CONCLUSIONS 
The SUS304 sieves were coated TiO2 thin film for airborne pathogens control in this work. A good 
uniformity and thin thickness of coating by a sol-gel method were achieved in this work. The prepared 
TiO2-coated sieves were employed to set up a photocatalytic reactor to evaluate their bactericidal 
abilities on bioaerosols. The results show that a photocatalytic reactor, packed with TiO2 coated SS 
sieves can effectively and continuously decrease bioaerosols for a certain time after the UV lamp has 
been switched on for 5 mins. Since the photokilling efficiencies of this reactor can be enhanced easily 
by either increasing the number of TiO2 coated sieves, decreasing the gas feed flow rates or 
decreasing the operation humidity. Thus this practicable photocatalytic reactor composed of TiO2 
coated SS sieves is suitable for deactivating bioaerosols. With the advantages of low cost preparation 
method, low headloss [13], high flow rate, long wavelength (365 nm) UV lighting, and an ability to 
flexibly apply the SS sieves, TiO2 coated sieves have been demonstrated to be a good alternative for 
airborne pathogen control.
