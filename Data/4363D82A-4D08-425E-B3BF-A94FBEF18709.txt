A Low-power Subharmonic Injection-Locked Oscillator using E/D-mode GaAs 
PHEMTs for Ka-Band Applications  
Fan-Hsiu Huang, Chi-Hsien Lin, Hong-Yeh Chang, Yi-Jen Chan  
Department of Electrical Engineering, National Central University  
Chungli, 32001 Taiwan, R.O.C.  
Tel: +886-3-422-7151 ext.34469, Fax: +886-3-425-5830, E-mail: 92541002@ee.ncu.edu.tw 
 
 
Abstract –  A subharmonic injection-locked oscillator (SILO) implemented by using 0.5 µm 
E/D-mode PHEMT process is demonstrated to achieve a Ka-band low-power and low phase noise 
source in this paper. Based on a 15 GHz cross-coupled oscillator, a signal having the frequency 
around 30 GHz can be generated in this SILO circuit through the push-push technique. A cascode 
amplifier is also arranged to be the output buffer for gaining the output amplitude of 30 GHz signal. 
The total power consumption of SILO is 24.5 mW under 2.5 V supply voltage. The measured 
free-running phase noise is -101 dBc/Hz at 1 MHz offset. With injecting a 2nd-order sub-harmonic 
signal (7.5 GHz) into this ILO, the output phase noise can be further improved to -127 dBc/Hz. The 
overall frequency multiplication factor is 4. 
 
Index Terms - E/D-mode GaAs PHEMT, Injection-Locked Oscillator, cascode amplifier.  
 
I. Introduction 
    Low-voltage and low power consumption operation are the important consideration in microwave 
and millimeter-wave applications. The E/D-mode (Enhanced and Depletion) GaAs PHEMT technique 
has been developed for high-speed digital or high-frequency rf communications [1-4]. Owing to the 
different threshold voltages of the D-mode and the E-mode devices, the circuit complexity and the power 
consumption can be improved significantly as compared to the conventional D-mode design. For 
millimeter-wave wireless systems, the local oscillator is typically based on an rf phase locked loop (PLL) 
architecture. However it is generally difficult to achieve a low phase noise PLL with low power 
dissipation in millimeter-wave band. To overcome the phase noise issues, the injection-locked PLL 
(ILPLL) containing a sub-harmonic injection-locked oscillator (SILO) has been proposed [5-6]. The 
injected clock signal can be generated from a low-frequency PLL to synchronize the SILO, so the phase 
noise performance is determined by the low-frequency PLL. Hence the SILO can only be designed 
focusing on the characteristic of wide locking range with a low power consumption.  
In this study, we used the 0.5 µm E/D mode GaAs pHEMT technology to achieve the low-power 
Ka-band SILO. With a supply voltage tuning from 1.8 V to 2.5 V, this oscillator demonstrates a low 
be 12 dB, i.e. -128 dBc/Hz. However the measured 30 GHz phase noise suppression has the noise 
degradation of 1 dB compared to the injected signal due to the intrinsic noise in the devices [9]. 
 
III. Conclusion 
 We have presented a Ka-band sub-harmonic injection-locked oscillator fabricated in 0.5 µm 
E/D-mode GaAs pHEMT technique. This circuit demonstrates the performances of low-power 
consumption and low phase noise at 30 GHz under the free-running and the injection locking state. The 
SILO can be further adopted for the Ka-band PLL MMIC integration.
