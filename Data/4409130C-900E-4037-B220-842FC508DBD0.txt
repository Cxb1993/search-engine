一、前言 
依據行政院主計處統計資料顯示，2006 年我國機械產業總產值達新台幣 7,830 億元，較 2005
年成長 9.3%，其中工具機產值新台幣 1,270 億元、機械零組件為 1,250 億元、光電半導體設備
產值則為 540 億元，而在上述產業當中關鍵零組件技術研發則扮演相當重要的角色，尤其是精
密高速轉子軸承系統。現今工業科技和微加工技術發展迅速，使儀器和設備元件的超精密加工
和量測技術更發展到了奈米級領域，不僅量測精度需求大幅提昇外，更需要奈米級的位移和定
位。為符合此量測技術的需求，諸如要求測量儀之旋轉精度達到奈米級、更要求保持高度的穩
定特性，因此關鍵零組件當中之非圓形氣體軸承的應用便日趨受到重視。在精密加工機、半導
體晶圓切割機、雷射印表機、DLP 背投影電視、高轉速硬碟機等與其他需潔淨、超高速、準確
定位的系統中皆有大量的應用。由於氣體軸承具有無接觸的特性、摩擦力極小，軸承幾乎是在
無摩擦的狀態下運轉，因此轉子與軸承間的磨耗極低，使軸承系統的壽命較其他軸承高。同時
由於氣體具有可壓縮性、黏滯性極低，使流體的剪力極小，因此無須太多功率即可達到非常高
的轉速，而且不易發熱，即使在高溫的環境中運轉也不會影響其動態性能。 
非圓形氣體軸承本身所使用的潤滑劑為空氣，不僅不會受到環境的污染，且可以在極高旋
轉速率、高溫或低溫的場所下進行。而氣體的均化效應，更可以有效的補償了原件表面加工的
誤差，且可大幅度地提高軸承迴轉精度和平移精度，因此可以作為圓度基準、直線基準以及超
精度加工技術和微加工技術的母機元件，而受到國內外學術界和工業界的重視，其生產和製造
水準，代表人類在超精密加工技術領域的研究水平。目前在許多要求軸心定位精度高、轉速高、
低摩擦及特殊環境要求的儀器設備內，均大量採用空氣軸承來取代傳統滾動元件或液膜潤滑軸
承。 
 
二、研究目的 
由於氣體軸承轉動時之摩擦力極小，在一般的情況下，軸承幾乎是在無摩擦的狀態下運轉。
因為氣體軸承本身所使用的潤滑劑為空氣，不僅不會受到環境的污染，且可以在極高旋轉速率、
高溫或低溫的場所下進行。然而氣體的均化效應，可以有效的補償了原件表面加工的誤差，且
可大幅度地提高軸承迴轉精度和平移精度。因此可以作為圓度基準、直線基準以及超精度加工
技術和微加工技術的母機元件，而受到國內外學術界和工業界的重視，其生產和製作水準，代
表人類在超精密加工技術領域的研究水準。目前在許多要求軸心定位精度高、轉速高、低摩擦
及特殊環境要求的儀器內，均大量採用氣體軸承來取代滾動元件軸承。因此本計畫主要目的乃
針對非圓形氣體軸承分析其運轉時的操作條件，並判斷是否會使該系統造成非線性不穩定現象
產生，進而降低其定位誤差等問題。 
 
三、文獻探討 
氣體潤滑的數學理論在 1886 年，由 Reynolds [1]首先推導出有關於壓力、密度、相對運動
與速度的偏微分方程式，此方程式即為著名的雷諾方程式，從此建立了流體潤滑理論的基礎。
1904 年，由 Sommerfield [2]簡化雷諾方程式而求得軸頸軸承之解析解，1905 年，Michelle [3]求
得止推軸承的解析解，前述二位學者均假設流體為不可壓縮流體。直到 1913 年，Harrison [4]針
對可壓縮流體發展出雷諾方程式的近似解。1962 年，由 Dowson [5]提出了廣義的雷諾方程式，
此方程式是針對 Reynolds [1]所提出的雷諾方程式稍作修改，這兩個方程式最主要的差別在於前
者考慮到沿著滑動方向，乃通過薄膜厚度方向之黏度與密度的變化以及對潤滑薄膜壓力分佈的
影響。由於雷諾方程式在可壓縮流體的假設下，必為非線性方程式，當時亦無電腦計算設備，
因此限制了當時氣浮軸承理論分析的發展，也因此氣浮軸承的發展沈寂了一段時間。後來，在
1950-1970 年代之間關於穩態氣體靜壓軸承的設計與分析，有 Tang 與 Gross [6] 發表軸承性能之
應用便越來越受到重視。非圓形軸承在設計時便已具有多個偏心距（e，eccentricity）及偏心率
（er，eccentricity ratio），包括高偏心及中低偏心率，高偏心率可帶來具有較佳的承載能力、剛
性也較強，因此當運用在旋轉機構需要不同範圍的承載力或高剛度需求時，便可滿足多樣性的
需要，同時藉由不同偏心率的設計可提高系統的穩定性，降低軸承產生非線性運動的可能性。 
本計畫擬採數值分析的方式將非圓形氣體軸承的相關特性做一詳盡的探討。由於在實際轉
子軸承系統中轉子是具有撓性的，為了能讓動態方程式更接近真實系統，本計畫亦考慮撓性轉
子之非圓形氣體軸承系統。同時，為了能快速求解雷諾方程式，擬採微分轉換法求解並與常見
的數值方法如有限差分法、連續過鬆弛法（successive over relaxation method, S.O.R.）等做比較。
此外，為進一步瞭解軸頸與轉子中心的運動情況，在此也將利用動態軌跡、頻譜響應、龐卡萊
映射圖與分岔圖等來分析軸頸與轉子中心的動態行為。

討及驗證，並與 S.O.R 方法相互比較。

技術說明 
非圓形氣體軸承與一般圓形氣體軸承不同，主要在於非圓形
軸承的曲率中心位置與半徑不盡相同，使偏心率也不同。由於一般
氣體軸承在低偏心率的情況下其承載能力較差，同時在某些低偏心
率範圍時，系統會產生嚴重的不穩定現象。而為改善此缺點，非圓
形軸承在設計時便已具有多個偏心距（e，eccentricity）及偏心率
（er，eccentricity ratio），包括高偏心及中低偏心率，高偏心率可帶
來具有較佳的承載能力、剛性也較強，因此當運用在旋轉機構需要
不同範圍的承載力或高剛度需求時，便可滿足多樣性的需要，同時
藉由不同偏心率的設計可提高系統的穩定性，降低軸承產生非線性
運動的可能性。 
此外系統在某些參數條件下，其旋轉過程中會產生非週期性
運動，而這些不規則的運動可能將使系統產生微結構的疲勞破壞；
為能了解系統在工作的過程中何種狀況下會產生非週期的現象，以
及盡量避免產生較不規則的振動並減少振動振幅，以降低對軸承系
統的不良影響，並期提供較適當的工作環境。因此本技術主要針對
具修正雷諾方程式以混合法求解，同時驗證數值結果之正確性。之
後，再利用系統的軌跡圖、龐卡萊映射、頻率響應和分岔圖來分析
系統在不同的操作參數之下，轉子中心的動態行為。進而分析轉子
在軸承支撐之下所具有之動態特性，並預估與控制系統的非線性動
態行為所產生的時機。

Abstract—This paper employs a hybrid numerical method combining 
the differential transformation method and the finite difference method 
to study the dynamic behavior of a rigid rotor supported by a micro gas 
journal bearing system. The analytical results reveal a complex dynamic 
behavior comprising periodic, sub-harmonic, and quasi-periodic 
responses of the rotor center. Furthermore, the results reveal the changes 
which take place in the dynamic behavior of the bearing system as the 
rotor mass is increased. The current analytical results are found to be in 
good agreement with those of other numerical methods. Therefore, the 
proposed method provides an effective means of gaining insights into the 
nonlinear dynamics of micro gas film rotor-bearing systems. 
Keywords- hybrid method, micro gas bearing, quasi-periodic motion 
I.  INTRODUCTION 
Micro gas journal bearings have been extensively used for 
a wide variety of mechanical engineering applications over the 
past few decades, and are continuing to attract considerable 
attention due to their potential for use in Micro 
Electromechanical systems and high-precision instrumentation. 
In 1961, Gross [1] presented perturbation solutions to steady, 
self-acting, infinitely long journals and plane wedge films. The 
perturbation solutions were valid for all ranges of geometrical 
parameters and were highly accurate. Castelli & Elrod [2] 
presented a method for solving the stability problem in 360-
degree self-acting, gas lubricated bearing systems. The 
proposed method involved integrating a set of nonlinear 
equations to obtain the rotor center orbits corresponding to any 
set of geometrical, operating or initial conditions. 
In 1994, Zhao et al. [3] investigated the sub-harmonic and 
quasi-periodic motions of an eccentric squeeze film damper-
mounted rigid rotor system. The authors noted that for large 
values of unbalance and static misalignment, the sub-harmonic 
and quasi-periodic motions generated at speeds of more than 
twice the system critical speed were bifurcated from the 
unstable harmonic solution. Brown et al. [4] applied short 
bearing theory to develop a simple model of a rigid, 
hydrodynamically supported journal bearing and showed that 
the journal behaved chaotically when the rotating unbalance 
force exceeded the gravitational load. Based on theoretical and 
experimental evidence, Adiletta et al. [5-6] reported that a rigid 
rotor supported by short bearings behaved with sub-harmonic, 
quasi-periodic or chaotic motion at particular values of the 
system parameters. Sundararajan & Noah [7] proposed a 
simple shooting scheme integrated with an arc-length 
continuation algorithm for the investigation of periodically 
forced rotor systems. 
The remainder of this study is organized as follows. 
Section 2 develops a mathematical model describing the time-
dependent motions of the rotor center of a rigid rotor supported 
by two micro gas journal bearings. Due to the nonlinearity of 
the gas film pressure in this bearing system, determining the 
Reynolds equation solutions is very difficult. Accordingly, 
Section 3 develops a hybrid method combining the finite 
difference method (FDM) and the differential transformation 
method (DTM) to obtain the required solutions. The solutions 
are then compared with those obtained using the SOR 
(Successive Over Relation) method. Section 4 presents the 
simulation results obtained using the proposed hybrid method 
for the vibrations of the rotor center for various rotor masses. 
Finally, Section 5 draws some brief conclusions.

B. Dynamic orbits and phase portraits 
Figs. 1(a)~1(d) show that the orbit of the rotor center is 
regular at a low value of the rotor mass (mr =0.012), but 
becomes irregular and performs quasi-periodic motion at mr 
=0.015. At rotor mass values of mr =0.12 and 0.146, the rotor 
center performs 2T-periodic and quasi-periodic motion, 
respectively. 
C. Power spectra 
Fig. 2 shows the dynamic response of the rotor center as a 
function of the rotor mass in the horizontal direction. At a low 
rotor mass of mr =0.012, the power spectra indicate that the 
rotor center performs T-periodic motion. As the rotor mass is 
increased to mr =0.015, it can be seen that the rotor center 
motion transits to quasi-periodic motion in the horizontal 
direction (Fig. 2(b)). However, as the rotor mass is further 
increased to mr =0.12, the orbits revert to 2T-periodic 
(subharmonic) motion. Finally, at rotor mass values of mr 
=0.146, the power spectra indicate that the rotor center 
performs quasi-periodic motion(Figs. 2(d)). 
D. Bifurcation diagrams and Poincaré maps 
The bifurcation diagrams in Fig. 3 plot the rotor center 
displacement against the rotor mass mr for values of mr ranging 
from 0.01 to 0.16. Figs. 4(a)~4(d) present the Poincaré maps at 
mr =(a)0.012, (b)0.066, (c)0.074 and (d)0.15, respectively. 
Observing Figs. 3(a) and 3(b), it is seen that the rotor center 
performs T-periodic motion in both the horizontal and the 
vertical directions at low values of the rotor mass, i.e. mr 
<0.015. This is confirmed by the Poincaré map shown in Fig. 
4(a) for a rotor mass of 0.012. However, at mr =0.015, the T- 
periodic motion transits to quasi-periodic motion. From Fig. 3, 
it is seen that this quasi-periodic motion persists over the rotor 
mass range 0.015≤ mr <0.047. However, at mr =0.047, the 
quasi-periodic motion is replaced by T-periodic motion in the 
horizontal and vertical directions. This T-periodic motion is 
maintained over the rotor mass interval 0.047≤ mr <0.066, but 
reverts to 2T-periodic (subharmonic) motion over the rotor 
mass range of 0.066≤ mr <0.072 as shown in Fig. 4(b). As the 
motor mass is increased from 0.072 to 0.073, the subharmonic 
motion is replaced by T-periodic motion in both the horizontal 
and the vertical directions. 
Figs. 3 and 4(c) show that the T-periodic motion over mass 
range 0.72 ≤ mr ≤ 0.73 reverts to quasi-periodic motion in the 
rotor mass range 0.074≤ mr <0.077. At mr =0.077, the quasi-
periodic motion is replaced by T-periodic motion in the 
horizontal and vertical directions. This T-periodic motion 
persists over the interval 0.077≤ mr <0.119, and is then 
replaced by 2T-periodic motion in the horizontal and vertical 
directions as the rotor mass is increased to mr =0.12. The 2T-
periodic motion is maintained in the interval 0.12≤ mr <0.138. 
Figs. 3 and 4(d) indicate that the rotor center performs T-
periodic and quasi-periodic motion as the rotor mass is 
subsequently increased in the interval 0.138≤ mr <0.146 and 
0.146≤ mr ≤ 0.16, respectively.

Abstract—This paper employs a numerical method combining the 
differential transformation method and the finite difference method 
to study the bifurcation behavior of a rigid rotor supported by a 
micro gas bearing system. The numerical results reveal a complex 
dynamic behavior comprising periodic, sub-harmonic, and 
quasi-periodic responses of the rotor center. Furthermore, the 
results reveal the changes which take place in the dynamic behavior 
of the bearing system as the bearing number is increased. The 
current analytical results are found to be in good agreement with 
those of other numerical methods. Therefore, the proposed method 
provides an effective means of gaining insights into the nonlinear 
dynamics of micro gas film rotor-bearing systems.

The remainder of this study is organized as follows. Section 2 
develops a mathematical model describing the time-dependent 
motions of the rotor center of a rigid rotor supported by two micro

results obtained using the proposed hybrid method for the 
vibrations of the rotor center for various rotor masses.  
 
I. BACKGROUND 
icro gas bearings have been extensively used for a wide 
variety of mechanical engineering applications over the 
past few decades, and are continuing to attract considerable 
attention due to their potential for use in Micro Electromechanical 
systems and high-precision instrumentation. In 1961, Gross [1] 
presented perturbation solutions to steady, self-acting, infinitely 
long journals and plane wedge films. The perturbation solutions 
were valid for all ranges of geometrical parameters and were 
highly accurate. Castelli & Elrod [2] presented a method for 
solving the stability problem in 360-degree self-acting, gas 
lubricated bearing systems. The proposed method involved 
integrating a set of nonlinear equations to obtain the rotor center 
orbits corresponding to any set of geometrical, operating or initial 
conditions.

Abstract—This paper employs differential transformation 
method (DTM) to study the dynamic behavior of a gyroscope 
control system. The analytical results reveal a complex dynamic 
behavior comprising periodic, sub-harmonic, quasi-periodic, 
and chaotic responses of the center of gravity. Furthermore, the 
results reveal the changes which take place in the dynamic 
behavior of the gyroscope control system as the external force is 
increased. The current analytical results by DTM are found to 
be in good agreement with those of Runge-Kutta method 
(RKM). Therefore, the proposed method provides an effective 
means of gaining insights into the nonlinear dynamics of 
gyroscope systems.  
I. INTRODUCTION 
yroscope control system dynamics is a very interesting 
nonlinear system which has been intensively studied 
during the last decades. The gyro has attributes of great utility 
to navigational, aeronautical and space engineering, and has 
been studied by many authors. From the recently researches, 
it can be seen that different types of gyros with linear or 
nonlinear damping have been found with rich dynamic 
behavior such as sub-harmonic and chaotic motions [1]-[5]. 
In 2005 [6], Ge et al. apply adaptive control theory to 
synchronize two identical two-degree-of-freedom heavy 
symmetric gyroscope system. However, the synchronization 
scheme in the above-mentioned paper is derived on the basis 
of the ideal assumption of control input. In fact, the 
implement of control inputs of practical systems is frequently 
subject to nonlinearity as a result of physical limitation. It has 
been shown that input nonlinearity, including saturation, 
backlash and dead-zone, can cause a serious degradation of 
the system performance, a reduced rate of response, and in a 
worst-case scenario, system failure if the controller is not well
