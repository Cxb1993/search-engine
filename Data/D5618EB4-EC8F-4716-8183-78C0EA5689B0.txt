要。此外，衛星編隊飛行所需要的發射成本較高，衛星控制與任務操作技術都較
困難，相對的需要較長的研發時間，成本也相對增加。基於上述理由，福衛七號
相較於福衛三號之改進重點，在於如何改變星系之軌道部署，以增加探測數據量，
使探測數據達到最大值。至於衛星編隊飛行的重要性，根據福衛三號的經驗，將
是在福衛七號部署的初期，也就是衛星儀器調校期間，衛星與衛星的儀器之間可
以相互校正，達到最佳之調教結果，提升探測數據之準確性，增進福衛七號之探
測成效。

I. INTRODUCTION
The concept of satellite formation flying (SFF) was started in the middle of 1990s and
has about 15 years of history so far. Main concept of SFF is that multiple satellites can
work together in a group, as a formation or cluster or constellation, to accomplish the
objectives of one much larger and more expensive satellite. In many cases the group is
even more powerful. Coordinating small or even micro satellites have many benefits
over large satellites including simpler in design, faster in building, and cheaper in
replacement. Usually, depending on the applications, there are three types of formation
flying: trailing formation flying (TFF), cluster formation flying (CFF), and satellite
constellation flying (SCF). [1]
Trailing formations are formed by multiple satellites orbiting on the same path. [1]
They are displaced from each other at a specific distance to produce either varied
viewing angles of one target or to view a target at different times, and are especially
suited for meteorological and environmental applications such as viewing the progress
of a fire, cloud formations, and making 3D views of hurricanes. Notable pairs are

After launched on 15 April 2006, 6 satellites of FORMOSAT-3/COSMIC carry
propellant to raise themselves to 6 different orbital planes at 800 km altitude so that a
constellation with global coverage is deployed. Onboard each satellite there is a GPS
occultation experiment (GOX) receiver. The occultation occurrences of the constellation
can be assimilated into numerical weather models for real-time weather prediction.
Refer to Figure 7 for the phenomenon.
According to the mission requirement, the constellation is deployed in six orbital
planes with 800 km altitude and 30 deg right ascension of ascending node (RAAN)
apart. The six satellites were launched together to a parking orbit with 516 km altitude
and 72 deg inclination. With each satellite raises itself to higher altitude one by one, six
orbital planes have been formed after a period of about 18 months with different RAAN
utilizing the nodal precession due to the oblateness effect of the Earth.
Difficulties of the constellation deployment are five folds: 1) the first factor is
the large attitude errors near polar region due to geomagnetic stabilization; 2) the second
factor is the satellite entering the safe mode due to overheating of the battery in large
solar beta angle; 3) the third factor is the on-board coarse attitude sensors and lack of
rate sensor may sometimes cause thrusting be aborted due to control system unstable; 4)
the fourth factor is the constraints of orbit transfer duration for the phasing of RAANs
and the arguments of latitude (AOL); and 5) the fifth factor is the long duration of
constellation deployment which imposed high load to the mission operations team.
Through comprehensive team works, the operations team has eventually achieved
success in the constellation deployment. Figures 8 and 9 show the final results.

The Formosa Satellite #3 (FORMOSAT-3 or FS3) Program belongs to the SCF.
Six microsatellites with 62 kg each are deployed in 6 orbital planes with 72 deg
inclination angle and 800 km altitude to form a “constelation observing system for 
meteorology, ionosphere, and climate” (COSMIC). Since its successful launch on 15 
April 2006, hundreds of institutes from near 50 countries have registered to use the data
collected. Therefore, it is known as FORMOSAT-3/COSMIC internationally. The
design life of FS3 is 7 years, in other words, its end-of-life (EOL) is on 15 April 2013.
Based on system simulation, the currently FS3 constellation can obtain about 2,415 GPS
occultation data daily.

III. Optimal orbit inclination for data maximization and even global distribution
From Table 1, the orbit inclination angle to give the maximum number of data
acquisition of the FS3 type constellation is i=154 deg. However, as shown in Figure 4, it
