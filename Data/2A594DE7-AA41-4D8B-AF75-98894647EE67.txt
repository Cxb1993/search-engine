ABSTRACT 
The squeeze film behavior of MEMS torsion mirrors 
is modeled, analyzed and discussed.  Effects of gas 
rarefaction (1st-order slip-flow model with 
non-symmetric accommodation coefficients, ACs) and 
surface roughness are considered simultaneously by 
using the average Reynolds type equation (ARTE).    
A simple coordinate transformation, just stretching or 
contracting the axes by referring to the roughness flow 
factors, is utilized to transform the linearized ARTE into 
a diffusion type modal equation.  The dynamic 
coefficients (stiffness and damping coefficients) are 
derived and expressed in analytical form.  The results 
show that the tilting frequency (or 0Γ : squeeze number), 
roughness parameters ( γ : Peklenik numbers, σ : 
standard deviation of composite roughness) and gas 
rarefaction parameters (D: inverse Knudsen number, 
1 2,α α : ACs) are all important parameters on analyzing 
the dynamic performance of MEMS torsion mirrors. 
1. INTRODUCTION 
The dynamical behavior of movable parts in 
MEMS is largely affected by the ambiance due the 
presence of gas engulfing the microstructure, e.g. low 
vacuum conditions for micro-accelerometers [1], 
ultra-thin gas film between magnetic heard/disk 
interfaces [2], and tilting micro-mirrors in DLP type 
projectors [3, 4].  The main effect occurs in these 
examples is the squeeze film.  The energy losses in the 
system is lowered by many methods (lowering the 
ambient pressure [1], perforations [5]) to increase the 
quality factors of the vibrating devices.   
In micro devices, the effects of gas rarefaction 
(including surface accommodations effects) and 
surface roughness become important [6] under the 
conditions of low spacing or low ambient pressure.  
Three important parameters in micro devices are: (1)the 
Knudsen number ( 0/Kn hλ= ), (2)ACs ( 1 2,α α ) and 
(3)the film thickness ratio ( 0 0 /SH h σ= ).     
For the present squeeze film problem, only 
Poiseuille flow exists.  The non-symmetric conditions 
for ACs ( 1 2α α≠ ) could be discussed.  The flow factors 
can be expressed as functions of non-symmetric ACs 
( 1 2α α≠ ), inverse Knudsen number, and roughness 
parameters ( 0SH  and γ).  The effects of non-symmetric 
ACs, inverse Knudsen number and surface roughness 
parameters on the flow factors and the dynamic 
coefficients of torsion micro-mirrors are discussed.
