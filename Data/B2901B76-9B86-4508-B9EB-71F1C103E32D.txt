plating layer and contact applications. 
The purpose of this research (sub-project 3) is to 
extend the applications of PEALD for dual damascene 
Cu interconnect technology in 65, 45, and 32 nm 
technology node and beyond. The innovative all-PEALD 
thin films processes is developed for various metals 
and metal nitride layers including Ta2N ,TaN, Ru and 
Cu which are integrated on applications for diffusion 
barrier and Cu directly plating Cu interconnect 
(Ta2N/TaN/Ru/Cu). Various key materials properties 
including conformability, microstructure, and more 
importantly the diffusion barrier properties have 
been investigated. Additionally, a novel plasma-
enhanced atomic layer deposition ultra thin（<3 nm）
mixed-phase/nanolaminate Ru–TaN barrier has been 
investigated, and it will confirmed that the copper 
diffusion barrier and direct-plate characteristics of 
the mixed-phase barrier can be modulated by varying 
the metal ratio in the film. The mixed-phase material 
which is combines the robust barrier properties of 
TaN with the direct-plating characteristics of Ru. It 
is apparent that the mixed-phase liner 
characteristics should be a strong function of Ru/Ta 
ratio in the film which can be controlled by 
modulating the number of deposition cycles of each 
individual material. Also, the integration of all-
PEALD thin films processes with porous low-k 
dielectric (PMSQ, k<2) and pore-sealing effect by 
plasma treatment are studied.
