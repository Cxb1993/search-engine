應用於微投影機之雙面微透鏡陣列元件開發 
Development of Double Side Micro Lens Array for Micro 
Laser Projector Application 
計畫編號：NSC 98-2221-E-492 -001 
執行期間：98 年 08 月 01 日至 99 年 07 月 31 日 
計畫主持人：陳峰志 
共同主持人：郭慶祥 
中文摘要 
近年來微透鏡陣列廣泛應用於光學系統之勻光用途，諸如微投影機、液晶顯示器等成
像系統，皆需藉此類光學元件進行光源的整束與能量的均勻化。本研究整合光學設計、超
精密加工技術及塑膠射出成型技術致力於微透鏡陣列元件的量產技術開發。光學設計的部
分以純量繞射理論進行雷射勻光微透鏡陣列元件的模擬，模擬結果顯示具雙面微透鏡陣列
元件可將紅、綠、藍三色之雷射光同時均勻化並混出可應用於微投影機光源之白光。依照
光學設計之結果，以慢刀伺服加工的方式進行模仁製作，並結合塑膠射出成型技術，製作
出雙面微透鏡陣列光學元件。實驗結果證實，本研究所開發之雙面微透鏡陣列光學元件具
備投影系統所需之均勻光特性，並可實際應用於微投影系統。 
 
關鍵字：微透鏡陣列、微投影機、超精密加工、慢刀伺服加工 
Abstract: In this paper, the development of micro lens array for the application to micro laser 
projectors is introduced. For the purpose of commercial mass production, it is necessary to 
investigate the concurrent engineering of optical design, mold fabrication, and plastic injection 
molding at once. The design of the micro lens array was based on the scalar diffraction theory. 
The double side micro lens array proposed in this study can simultaneously shape red, green, and 
blue laser beams to a uniform projection pattern. The mold was fabricated by an ultra precision 
diamond turning machine using slow tool servo method. The optical design constraints were 
considered from the feedback of mold fabrication and plastic injection molding. The experiment 
was also to verify the projection performance and examine the application feasibility to micro 
laser projectors.  
Keywords: micro lens array, diamond turning, slow tool servo, micro laser projectors.

The mold was made of STAVAX with a Ni-P plate and fabricated by an ultra precision 
diamond turning machine with slow tool servo method, the scrape process based on simultaneous 
X-Y-Z motion stirring. To reduce the vibration and improve the surface quality, the scraping path 
planning was distinguished into the even and odd lenses, as shown in Fig. 4.  The diamond 
turning machine and the scrape path planning are shown in Fig. 4. 
 
Fig. 4. Diamond turning machine and the path planning 
The micro lens array came into shape by the plastic injection molding. The lateral shift 
alignment of the mold surfaces, the repeatability of the mold alignment (which was less than 3 
μm), the thickness and the lenses dimension determined the completion of the injection molding, 
while the lenses dimension and the injection parameters were examined by the mold flow 
analysis. The mold flow software, Autodesk® Moldflow® Insight was used here. The analysis 
result served as the reference in the design phase. 
3. Result and discussion 
The mold and the implemented PMMA micro lens array are shown in Fig. 5.  The design 
was according to case (a) in Fig. 3.  The thickness of this micro lens array is 0.48 mm.  The 
thickness should be as small as possible based on the simulation. To reduce the thickness down 
less than 0.3 mm, the injection force and the sprue were controlled to 50 tons and 50 mm by the 
mold flow analysis. The lenses profile was examined by the confocal microscope, as shown in 
Fig. 6, while the misalignment was measured by the lateral shift on surfaces of different sides.  
The displacement error of 11 μm in x direction and 4 μm in  y direction was obtained due to the 
molds misalignment from different sides.
