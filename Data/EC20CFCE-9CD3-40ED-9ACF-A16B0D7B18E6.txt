Abstract 
In this study, we apply several models to simulate wave transformation, including shoaling, 
refraction, diffraction, reflection, wave breaking and energy dissipation from deep water to 
shallow water. 
The integral project could be categorized into the following sub-parts: 1. Applied 
Boussinesq Equations in the wave transformations of the nearshore, 2. Characteristics of 
nonlinear waves generated by directional wavemakers, 3. Simulation of water waves by higher 
order Mild-Slope Equation, 4. Applied Boundary Element Method to study wave deformation, 5. 
Applied Dual Reciprocity Boundary Element Method to study wave deformation by Mild-Slope 
Equation and 6. Experimental study on 2-D wave field deformation. The validity of the models is 
examined through comparisons with experiments. 
Keywords: Boussinesq Equations, directional wavemakers, Mild-Slope Equation, Dual 
Reciprocity Boundary Element Method, experiment.
