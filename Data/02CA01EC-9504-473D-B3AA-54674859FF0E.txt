八、Conclusion
In this study, the oxide coating on AZ91D alloy was anodized by pulse reverse plating through 
a MAO process. When the up-limit potential of pulse reverse plating was set  at  240 V, a regular 
current density peak could be found. In addition, the homogenous spark discharges formed a dense 
and smooth MAO coating from the inner layer to the outer layer, with an improved thickness 
growth of 6.06 μm/min. 
 The up-limit potential of pulse reverse plating influenced the MgO crystalline intensity. In 
addition, the hardness of the oxide coating had the same trend as the MgO crystalline intensity. 
Moreover, the maximum hardness value in this study  was 1120 Hv. The corrosion resistance of the 
oxide coating prepared at the up-limit  potential of 240 V had a passivity  potential interval of 0.83 V 
and a corrosion current density of 1.2 × 10–4 A/cm2. 
Consequently, pulse potential and reverse potential in an MAO process are set as 240 and 90 
V with 200 ms period time, which is a suitable parameter to anodize an MAO coating with a high 
corrosion resistance.
