認知地圖(cognitive map)。市面上現有之 GPS 導航系統對於這類的視覺空間資訊多採用多視窗 2D 圖像
介面設計，而受限於時間與空間的影響，常發生電子圖資與實際環境無法互相對應的問題。而影響使
用者認知空間的重要因素就是主觀的心智旋轉。心智旋轉即是當使用者對於方位的認知與導航資訊有
落差時，此時所產生的認知修正現象。而心智旋轉將 exocentric view(以環境為中心的 global view)轉換
成 egocentric view(以自我為中心的 local view)的過程會影響使用者對於導航資訊的決策。所以傳統電子
圖資的設計方式有以下兩種[5]： 
1. 朝北導航(north-up)：最常見的電子圖資顯示方式是以北邊朝上方式，是一種 exocentric view 的參
考架構；因此當使用者的前進方向與導航圖資所呈現的方向並不一致時，使用者的 egocentric view
認知架構與導航系統所提供資訊會相衝突。使用者必須透過心智旋轉動作，轉換導航地圖或是認
知地圖的資訊，以辨別目前的行進方向。 
2. 載具導航(track-up)：此類的電子圖資的顯示方式是與使用者的行進方向互相對稱，是以使用者為
中心的 egocentric view 認知架構，亦即導航系統地圖朝上的方式即是使用者前進的方向，故使用
者無須心智旋轉，即可判斷下一個轉彎的方向。 
從心智旋轉的觀點來看，雖然載具導航可以減少使用者的心智負擔，而上述這兩種方式對於使用者在
認知空間的影響上仍存在未臻一致的結果。例如，[6]的研究指出朝北導航系統可以降低使用者在尋找
與辨識特定地標時的困難度；而[7]則是反證載具導航系統較不容易導致導航錯誤。針對心智旋轉問題，

本研究的主要工作目標是研究『智慧型實景導航系統』之感知定位功能，也就是要利用 GPS 資料來推
論使用者的行為，進而利用感知使用者行為來進行修正定位誤差的動作，本研究稱此技術為

context-aware 能力則必須能擷取環境資訊(context)、解譯資訊、且讓 context 資訊適用於當下狀態。設
計此種系統最大挑戰是擷取、呈現與處理 contextual data 的複雜度。周遭感知(context-aware)技術目的
於讓系統”智慧化”，並讓系統能針對使用者狀態主動採取動作，以降低使用者於認知空間的負擔。 
本研究即在探討如何針對硬體感測器誤差與導航介面需求的問題，透過周遭感知(Context-aware)與擴增

VI. 研究方法 
要實現實景導航系統，就必須要先解決導航過程所需的準確位置與方位資訊問題，然後進一步運用擴
增實境介面整合呈現導航資訊，以提供使用者直覺與便利的導航服務，因此，實景導航系統架構可如
圖四所示區分為，系統平臺層 (platform)、周遭感知服務層 (context-aware services)及應用程式層
(application)等三層，首先是系統建構平臺層(platform)，此層包含相關硬體、作業系統及相關函式庫
(Libraries)與虛擬引擎 (virtual machine)；其次為建構於平臺上的 context-aware services 層，包括
Position-aware service 運用位置感測器(例如 GPS 全球定位系統)以周遭感知技術常見的 Behavior 
Learning 與 Position Data Perception 等方法來降低感測器誤差，來提供使用者準確的地 位置資訊；而
使用者方位資訊是由方位感測器(例如電子羅盤、重力感測器及陀螺儀)來提供，但因方位感測器易受到
環境干擾的影響，所以需要其他不同輔助感測器來與方位感測器進行相互校正，以提供正確的方位資
訊。而電子羅盤由於微型化技術的進步，現已常見於各式行動裝置(例如智慧型手機)上，故可運用影像
處理技術擷取周遭動態影像進而推論出相對運動方位資料，因此，Orientation-aware 感知服務可以引用
Magnetism Analysis及Orientation computation等方法將分別由電子羅盤與PGPS所推論出的方位資訊進
行相互校正，以提供使用者面對方位的正確資訊；在獲得正確的位置與方位資訊的前提下，在
waypoint-aware 方面，則是運用周遭感知模式來解析地理資訊系統之 Route Planning 功能所提供的中途
點資訊，以推論(reasoning)使用者的行進意圖，找出下一個中途點資訊，最後應用層整合擴增實境技術，
用 Label Placement 及 Registration 方法將虛擬導航指標清楚標示於導航器螢幕，提供使用者動態實景導
航服務。經由感知服務的 context 交互運用與整合，將可以解決 position inaccuracy，orientation inaccuracy
及導航標示問題，提供使用者直覺與便利的實景導航服務。

位資訊，所以電子羅盤等方位硬體感測器易受周遭電磁環境影響而有磁性誤差的問題。要解決此問題
必須找出另一個物理效應的資訊來與電子羅盤方向資訊交互驗證，以正確推出使用者的面對方向。換
句話說，Orientation-aware service 與 Position-aware service 一樣，皆是利用兩種不同物理效應交互驗證，
配合使用者合理行為狀態來進行使用者行為推論。在 Position-aware service 中，PGPS 是利用
Pseudo-range 與 Doppler Effect 等不同物理效應，將 GPS raw data 中的位置與速度資訊進行交互參考，
配合使用者合理行為狀態進行推論出合理位置資訊。相同原理，Orientation-aware service 如運用單一霍
爾效應，則無互相參考依據，而從 PGPS 研究中，發現使用者的剛體轉向運動是遵循牛頓物理定律，
故可以運用牛頓定律與洛倫茲力交互驗證，紓解方位硬體感測器的問題，並據以建構出推論使用者旋
轉方位的認知技術，協助系統獲得準確的方位資訊。 
然而在電磁分析方法中所面臨的最大技術挑戰是在於載具移動中對於電磁信號的偵測與判讀，對於這
個問題，由於人的轉向是屬於連續運動，故電子羅盤所產生的電子信號應屬於平滑的連續信號，單位
時間內並不會有巨大落差，故可藉由單位時間內信號差距獲知電磁信號是否受到干擾。故在上述的研
究基礎上，Orientation-aware service 可以運用 Magnetism analysis 及 Orientation computation 等方法獲得
實景導航系統的相對位移資訊。在 Magnetism analysis 方法中所面臨最大的技術挑戰在於電磁信號中的
信號快速判讀單位時間內前後電磁信號的變化值。而在 Orientation computation 方法中，可透過電子羅
盤在連續信號的前後比對，找出連續信號中的差異資訊，來與 GPS 的方位資訊進行交互驗證，便藉此
可推算出使用者的精確方位資訊。如此一來，Orientation-aware service 可讓使用者在運動的狀態下，偵
測陀螺儀(gyroscope)或電子羅盤(magnetic compass)所產生的累積誤差，為實景導航系統帶來精確方位
資訊的可行方案。 
 
4.3 Waypoint-aware service(路徑感知服務) 
受到心智地圖與導航地圖無法一致的影響，導致現有的導航系統在面臨使用者行進路徑與系統規劃路
徑不同時，系統所提供的解決方案，都是呆板的迴轉提示，好讓使用者回到原來的規劃路徑上。而此
情況實際發生的原因，除了可能是使用者對於所在區域不熟而走錯路之外，另外亦有可能是使用者比
導航地圖更熟悉目前區域的地理位置，因此選擇走自認為更有效率的路徑。故本研究所提出之實景導
航系統與傳統導航系統差異，除了透過 Position-aware service 與 Orientation-aware service 可以獲得使用
者目前的正確位置與方位資訊之外，更大差別是 Waypoint-aware service 是以 Waypoint Tracking 方法
針對地理資訊系統所規劃的導航路徑及使用者的行進資訊來推論使用者行動意圖，進而推導出合理的
下一個 waypoint 資訊，以提供使用者適性的路徑導航資訊。由於地理資訊系統所提供的空間資訊(spatial 
context)通常區分為三種類別，路標資訊(landmark context)、路徑資訊(route context)及環視資訊(survey 
context)，這些空間資訊皆可以用中途點(waypoint)來表示。所謂的路標資訊是指地理系統內存的 POI
資訊；而路徑資訊是指由地理資訊系統所規劃之從目前位置到目的地(destination)的理想路徑所構成的
中途點清單；另外還有環視資訊是指目前位置之特定圓周範圍內的所有路口資訊。Waypoint-aware 
service 將善用地理資訊系統所提供空間資訊來進行使用者行進意圖推論。 
對於 Waypoint-aware Service 而言，感測資料來源包含「Position-aware Service」、「Orientation-aware 
Service」及「地理資訊系統的路徑規劃功能」，Waypoint-aware service 即是根據上述「感測」的 raw data
進行使用者行進意圖判斷。根據人類的心智地圖特性，當人類走錯路時，會下意識降低行走速度，以
利觀察周遭環境，反之，當他很確定自己的行走路徑時，即使旁人一再提醒走錯路時，他還是按照自
己步伐速度前進，因此可以利用此心智地圖特性，從上述所感測 raw data 判斷出使用者行為意圖，進
而在確認使用者走錯路時，適時提供警示提示。

5.3 討論與結論 
本研究是從 PGPS 研究成果與 AR 研究的最新發展，針對傳統 GPS 導航系統方向指引不明確的問題，
提出以多項硬體感測器來進行 Position-aware 與 Orientation-aware service，以提供精確的位置與方位資
訊，同時佐以 Waypoint-aware service 方法，追蹤使用者於導航過程的行動意圖，並將這些資訊整合於
擴增實境介面上，以實現實景導航系統，大幅提升使用者對於方位導航的直覺與便利性，在雛形系統
驗證的過程中部分，本研究發現以下研討議題，提供未來相關研究經驗參考： 
l Limited waypoint 
 在雛形系統開發的過程中發現，由於中途點清單(waypoint lists)是由地理資訊系統所提供，故中途
點清單的詳細度，直接影響到使用者的導航精細度，每一段中途點清單的相關資料越齊全，則導航指
標將越清楚，但是相對所需之儲存空間越大，推算每一個 waypoint 所需時間越長，如何在導航精細度
與執行效能之間取得平衡，將是實景導航的重要議題。 
l Training time 
 由於本文所提出實景導航系統是運用周遭感知技術針對使用者習性進行訓練與觀察、學習，而使
用者與環境互動的複雜行為如何進行觀察？雖然本研究提出許多方法作為觀察使用者的參考，但是是

Context-Awareness Technique for GPS Positioning 
 
Jiung-yao Huang1, Chung-Hsien Tsai2, and Shih-Yen Wei3 
Computer Science and Information Engineering Department 
National Taipei University 
San Shia, Taipei, 237 Taiwan 
jyhuang@mail.ntpu.edu.tw1, keepbusytsai@gmail.com2, arrny1224@gmail.com3 
 
 Abstract - The previous researches of GPS positioning for 
navigation all focus on performing preliminary GPS data 
ranging error filtering by Kalman filter first and then 
calibration with the map matching method. However, this 
approach requires the accuracy of the GIS map. This paper 
proposes a GPS data calibration technique for navigation 
which entirely depends on human behavior to further calibrate 
GPS data from Kalman filter without map information under 
any unprepared environment. The study adopts the context-
aware approach to fully explore user’s behavior and rectify 
GPS data from Kalman filter accordingly. The presented 
method is coined as Perceptive GPS (PGPS). It starts with 
extracting user’s feature data from Kalman filtered GPS data 
to classify his current state. Newton Markov Model (NMM) is 
then introduced from Hidden Markov Model to capture the 
user’s motion state. Based on NMM, PGPS technique records 
the GPS carrier’s behavior into a Transition Probability 
Matrix (TPM). This TPM is then used to infer the behavior of 
the GPS carrier from the online received GPS data and 
calibrate GPS carrier’s position accordingly. Finally, a series 
of experiments to validate PGPS technique are conducted and 
fully discussed at the end of the paper. 
 
 Keywords: Augmented Reality, Context aware, Hidden 
Markov Model, Entropy. 
 
1.  Introduction 
 The GPS navigation system in the market is composed by 
positional signal module, geographic information system(GIS) 
and user interface module[1]. The legacy GPS systems use the 
map matching technique to calibrate GPS data during 
navigation. The map matching approach always tries to fit the 
GPS data to a road that has shortest perpendicular distance. 
However, when there are two nearby parallel roads, the map 
matching approach will hop between these two roads so as to 
cause the serious cognitive disorder. Furthermore, owning to 
human cognitive errors caused by those traditional 2D map 
guiding, the live-view GPS navigation system has been 
proposed and also attracts much attention[2]. The live-view 
GPS navigation system adopts Mobile Augmented 
Reality(MAR) technology to overlay directional arrow on the 
live-view image to provide intuitive navigational information. 
Due to the lack of 2D map display, the map matching 
technique does not apply to live-view GPS navigation system. 
Along with the rapid evolvement of silicon technology in 
recent years, most of mobile devices, such as smartphone, 
nowadays are often equipped with camera, wireless network, 
the Global Positioning System (GPS) receiver and a variety of 
sensors. Such smart mobile devices allow users to 
interactively perceive their environmental information, such as 
location, personal ID, temporal or spatial information. Hence, 
it is a booming trend in the recent years to employ smart 
mobile device as a platform for developing the live-view GPS 
navigation system. For example, Wikitude Drive[3], AVIC-
VH09[4] and Route 66 Maps+[5] systems are three well-
known systems which have released their demo videos in 
YouTube. 
 However, since such built-in GPS receiver is susceptible 
to the environmental interferences, most of MAR applications 
utilize auxiliary equipment such as inertial sensors to calibrate 
GPS ranging error. One of the earliest MAR navigation 
applications that used GPS as a positional sensor was Touring 
Machine[6]. To get the accurate signals in urban street 
canyons, it redesigned vessel-based GPS receiver with a bulky 
wearable computer.  
 Fong et al.[7] in 2008 applied Differential GPS Carrier 
Phase technique to meet high accuracy requirements. They 
proposed a relative position measurement technique from GPS 
carrier phases of two GPS receivers, one is stationary and the 
other is mobile, for high precision positioning toward outdoor 
MAR applications. Such differential GPS carrier phase 
technique required at least a three-dimensional coordinates of 
reference receiver to stay fixed on a known control point. 
Therefore, the practicability of this approach is still not 
adopted by most of the mobile augmented reality systems 
applications yet.  
 Most existing commercial GPS navigation systems are 
still restricted by the inaccuracy of built-in GPS chip and 
employ Map matching algorithm[8] to solve GPS ranging 
error problem. For live-view GPS navigation system, because 
2D electronic map is unable to be displayed on the screen, the 
Map matching algorithm becomes worthless to calibrate GPS 
data. We must rely on the cognitive user’s behaviors to 
calibrate ranging error during live-view GPS navigation 
process. 
 In this paper, the technique has been proposed[2] to 
perceive user’s behaviors for live-view GPS navigation 
system. We explore the context-aware approach to perceive 
user’s behavior to calibrate the GPS ranging error accordingly 
and the presented algorithm is named as Perceptive GPS 
(called PGPS thereafter). PGPS extracts user’s feature data 
from the received GPS data to classify his current state. The 
modeling method of PGPS is then introduced to infer a 
plausible motion state of the user. This perceived motion state 
is then cross-referenced with the classified current state to 
motion from GPS data such as speed over ground(SOG), 
position and course over ground(COG) information. The 
extracted feature data include user’s displacement, velocity 
change, and the course difference between two consecutive 
GPS data. Based on the momentum changes, the state 
classification stage then uses feature data to classify GPS 
carrier’s behavior into pre-defined modeling states. The 
classified state is coined as the current state(CS) of GPS 
carrier. 
 
Fig. 1. PGPS process diagram 
 
 Perception needs to know the past history of the GPS 
carrier. The proposed technique is composed of learning phase 
and perception phase to perform the online perception. During 
the learning phase, outputs of state classification are analyzed 
by Newton’s Laws of Motion to compute the state transition 
probability among modeled states. These state transition 
probabilities are then collected into a Transition Probability 
Matrix (TPM) to represent the habitual behavior of the user on 
that period of time. In other words, the value of TPM is filled 
in the form of momentum change of user’s motion. This TPM 
then becomes inference foundation of the perception phase 
when the user performs online perception next time. Hence, in 
order to distinguish TPMs between the learning phase and the 
perception phase, subscript t of TPMt represents new TPM 
derived from the current learning phase while subscript t-1 of 
TPMt-1 denotes TPM from the previous learning phase. In 
short, during the online perception, new TPM, i.e. TPMt, is 
interactively built to record user’s current habitual behavior 
and previous TPM, i.e. TPMt-1, is used to forecast the most 
likely current status of the user, called the perceived state(PS). 
Finally, the amendment stage fine-tunes of the GPS positional 
and course data are based on the differences between PS and 
CS. Detail description of each stage layout is displayed at the 
following sessions.

 Since each GPS data is the snapshot of GPS receiver at an 
instant of time, the study assumes that the GPS carrier is a 
rigid body and the sequence of received GPS data is the 
trajectory of GPS carrier. Under the assumption of Newton’s 
motion physics, this study applied Newton’s second Law of 
Motion, ( ) /F d mv dt , to calculate the momentum of GPS 
carrier and to analyze the motion of GPS carrier accordingly. 
Based upon Newton’s second Law of Motion, the momentum 
change is induced by the displacement, velocity change and 
course difference. Since the location information embedded in 
GPS data is denoted in latitude and longitude, Haversine 
formula[16] is adopted to compute the displacement ∆x. 
Subsequently, the displacement (Δx), the velocity difference 
(Δv) and the course difference (Δθ) of two consecutive GPS 
data are extracted as the feature data to detect the behavior 
change of GPS carrier. 
3.2 State Classification 
 After the feature data are extracted, they can be used to 
estimate current state of GPS carrier. The question is how the 
GPS carrier’s behaviour is classified from the above feature 
data? Newton's second Law of Motion tells us that the 
momentum change may be caused by the alteration of mass or 
the variation of velocity. Under the assumption that the GPS 
carrier is a rigid body and its mass is fixed, the momentum 
change is dominated by the variation of velocity. 
Consequently, ∆v should be the first element to classify GPS 
carrier’s behaviour from the received GPS data. Further, there 
is no doubt that the ranging error of GPS data is indeed 
embedded in the value of displacement ∆x which naturally 
becomes the second classification key. Finally, the difference 
of course, ∆θ, then turns into the last component for 
classification. Hence, with the help of Newton's Laws of 
Motion, the computed feature data Δv, Δx, Δθ can classify 
GPS carrier’s behaviour into 7 reasonable states:  
stationary(Ss), linear cruise(Slc), linear acceleration(Sla), linear 
deceleration(Sld), veering cruise(Svc), veering 
acceleration(Sva), and veering deceleration states(Svd). In other 
words, based upon Newton's Laws of Motion, this research 
assumes that the behaviour of GPS carrier can be modelled by 
the set S = {Ss, Sla, Slc, Sld, Sva, Svc, Svd}. In order to 
accommodate the innate ranging errors reside in GPS signal,

the past experiences recorded in TPM to infer the most likely 
current state, yet, this computation still has some problems. 
For example, when Ai,j,k is 0 or the computed maximum 
probability is not an unique value, the computed state would 
become ambiguous. Furthermore, when the perceived state, 
PS, from Eq. (5) is not the same as the current state, CS, from 
Table 2, there is no absolute conclusion on whether it means 
the received GPS data has ranging error or the GPS carrier is 
changing its habitual behaviour. 
 After conducting a series of experiments, the above 
problems can be summarized into the subsequent four possible 
scenarios. First, the GPS signal has the ranging errors caused 
by the environmental effects. The second case is that, GPS 
carrier is changing from one state into another during activity. 
The third scenario which we suppose it could be the prediction 
of current state is incorrect. The final situation is the habitual 
behaviour of GPS carrier has changed. Hence, the solutions to 
these four cases are discussed as below. 
(1) When GPS signal has the ranging errors caused by the 
environmental effects, the received GPS data will not 
follow Newton's Laws of Motion. Hence, the received 
GPS data have to be amended according to the 
perceived state; so that it can be used to infer the 
following state with the stable TPM. 
(2) The second case is that the GPS carrier is currently 
changing its state during activity. Since stable TPM 
records the inertial behaviour of GPS carrier, the 
original inertia may also be changed when the GPS 
carrier is switching from one state into another. In this 
case, the perceived state from Eq. (5) is wrong. In 
order to distinguish this case from the first case, a 
mechanism is designed to detect the change of inertial 
status of GPS carrier. Since the inertia has the 
characteristics of continuity, a posture threshold 
parameter, pt , is designed to count the number of 
consecutive state change. That is, 1pt pt    when 
the perceived state from Eq. (5) is continuously 
different from the classified state by Table 2. An 
inertial threshold φ is further defined for each GPS 
carrier so that when pt  , it implies that the GPS 
receiver is changing its state. The system will then 
believe that the state of GPS receiver has changed, and 
trust the classified state from the received GPS data as 
the accurate current state. If pt  , we assume the 
system is in the first scenario and amend GPS data 
accordingly. 
(3) The third situation is the perceived state computed 
from Eq. (5) is inaccurate. Since the maximum 
possibility inferred from Eq. (5) maybe 0 or not an 
unique value, it implies the state transition from the 
previous state to current one is undecided or there are 
more than one possibilities of such transition exist. 
Under such circumstances, the perceived state thus will 
become distrusted and another approach is required to 
verify the accuracy of online received GPS data. 
Hence, a cross reference scheme between two 
consecutive feature data sets is designed to achieve this 
goal. If the difference between two consecutive feature 
data sets is within the range of predefined threshold, 
the classified current state from online received GPS 
data is then accepted. Otherwise, the previous 
perceived state is trusted based upon the inertial 
principle. 
(4) The final case is the change of habitual behaviour of 
GPS carrier. Different habitual behaviours will produce 
respective TPMs for online behaviour inference. 
Hence, the corresponded stable TPM for state 
inference and thresholds for state classification are 
switched for further state perception process.

format, this estimation must meet the Earth's geographical
