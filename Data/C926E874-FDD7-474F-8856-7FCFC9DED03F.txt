二、前言 
資訊科技運用已經為現代的戰爭型態帶
來全新的變化，並且使得戰爭理論被導入一個
新的思維模式之中，隨著軍事科技的進步，人
類所能控制的範疇逐漸擴大，時間也因此受到
壓縮，戰爭將朝「以速度爭取時間」、「以時
間掌握空間」的方向來發展，在進入資訊化的
時代，「數位化戰場」勢必是未來戰爭的型態。
黃偉傑[1]指出一向執世界軍事牛耳的美國軍
方，在建構全新的21世紀部隊(Force XXI)時便
將步兵視為其數位化戰場想定下的一環，試圖
讓情資反應能貫徹到以往被置於指管通情環
節最末端的部隊基層，讓戰場上的不同友軍單
位能彼此分享即時情資，這也就是美軍在資訊
化戰場中所謂的資訊水晶球 (Information 
Crystal)概念，這樣不但能讓具有科技優勢的美
軍充分發揮火力遂行打擊，更可以消弭長久以
來最致命的戰場迷霧。而在美國軍方的帶動
下，現在英、法、德及俄羅斯等國亦急起直追，
對數位化戰士的概念進行研究和發展。在數位
化戰場中，結合衛星導航定位系統(GPS)及地
理資訊系統(GIS)的電子地圖實不可或缺，而電
子地圖必須憑藉顯示器來展現。然而作戰中的
環境極端惡劣，因此體積大、耗電又脆弱的一
般顯示器在戰場中並不適用，更無法運用於數
位化戰士的頭盔顯示器上。隨著第三代(3G, 
Third Generation)的技術成熟及產品化之後，對
於顯示面板的應用，低耗電、高解析及全彩顯
示的達成已指日可待。在可預見的未來，在輕
薄短小、精緻靈敏、色彩鮮豔及低耗電等需求
下，像紙張一樣厚度且可撓曲的顯示螢幕將會
上市，而這其中有機電激發光顯示器(Organic 
Electroluminescence Display)將可能扮演非常
重要的角色，此一顯示器簡稱OLED顯示
器，係利用有機電激發光二極體 (Organic 
Light-Emitting Diodes)發光原理所製造之
新一代平面顯示器。 
高分子型有機電激發光面板，不需要背光
板、彩色濾光片、偏光板等。因此，材料成本
比液晶(Liquid Crystal)面板低，製造及組裝工
程亦很單純。尤其是可以使用塑膠基板的特
點，使其受到相當的注目，在厚度與重量上都
可以減低到終極的水準。因為其可以使用在彈
性變形的面板，故可適於軍事用途。 
三、研究目的 
有機發光二極體依其使用之有機薄膜發
光材料的不同，目前計有兩種系統，第一種為
以 染 料 或 顏 料 為 主 的 小 分 子 系 (Small 
Molecular-based Device)作為有機發光層，泛稱
為OLED；另一種是以π共軛性高分子系
(Polymer-based Device)作為有機發光層，是一
種摻雜後具有螢光性及導電能力的導電性高
分子，在紫外線照射下可以發出可見光，利用
不同百分比的調配方法製作出不同的光色，俗
稱為PLED (Polymer Light Emitting Diode)或稱
P-OLED。就材料而言，PLED的熱穩定性與機
械性質較佳，具有可溶液性；在製程方面，
PLED的驅動電壓較低(3～4V)；在設備投資方
面，PLED較小分子OLED便宜。目前OLED主
要適用於小面板的顯示器，而PLED的反應時
間幾乎是即時，符合動態影像的顯示需求，是
未來發展成大平面顯示器的重要技術，因此全
彩化技術的改進、提升，才能使OLED及PLED
真正取代液晶顯示器的時代來臨。PLED的製
作可用旋轉塗佈及噴墨列印等方式，而噴墨列
印方式主要可分為： (1)熱汽泡式噴墨法
(Thermal Bubble Ink-Jet Printing) 與(2)壓電式
噴墨法(Piezo Ink-Jet Printing)兩大類。但壓電
式噴墨印頭的結構較重，控制系統較為複雜。
因此本實驗研究僅針對熱汽泡式噴墨印頭，模
擬PLED高分子元件製程中的液滴落點、液滴
衝擊現象，觀察不同溶液自熱汽泡式噴墨頭噴
出後其噴流形態、碎裂情形、主液滴與衛星液
滴之數量與飛行速度、噴射角、液滴落地點精
準度及基板之不同表面粗糙度對液滴衝擊基
板液力行為的影響，進行有系統的分析比較，
尋求在液滴產生與衝擊過程的影響因素，作為

六、結果與討論 
1.液柱長短對落地點範圍之影響 
當噴墨頭內之加熱器對流體加熱產生薄
膜沸騰及微汽泡時，推動流體噴出而產生液
柱，液柱會因流動的不穩定性、外界環境的干
擾及流體本身的黏滯力、表面張力等因素，而
拉伸、頸縮並產生粒徑較大的主液滴，之後主
液滴與液柱分離。本文定義主液滴與液柱分離
且液柱尚未完全脫離噴嘴時的最大長度為液
柱長度(等同碎裂長度)。本實驗以63Hz拍攝頻
率對各工作流體各拍攝4,092張影像，各取其最
長的液柱如圖5所示，依長度排序為：(1)1:3糖
水，(2) 1:2糖水，(3)2:3甘油水，(4)純水。與
本研究第一年成果表1各工作流體的落地點範
圍相對照，可看出液柱愈長的流體，其落地點
範圍愈小。此結果與Parrado & González [4]成
果推論：⎡液滴群中的液滴所受空氣阻力遠小
於單一球體⎦一致，亦即稀疏液滴群的單顆液
滴所受空氣阻力之干擾大於密集液滴群的單
顆液滴，另外流體噴出後較快形成液滴者，其
所受空氣阻力影響較久，以致落地點範圍較
大。又衛星液滴的動量小於主液滴，所受環境
影響更顯著，致使衛星液滴偏離主液滴路徑造
成落地點範圍擴大。但流體性質對液柱長短的
影響如表1所示，並非單一受黏滯力所影響，

2.不同流體衛星液滴的比較 
如前一節所述：主液滴後方跟隨的衛星液
滴，當彼此的間距靠近時，其所受之阻力係數
是遠小於流體力學中阻力係數方程式所計算
的結果；故可推論衛星液滴因為主液滴在前方
排除氣流而所承受的空氣阻力較小，尤其是當
衛星液滴數量多且彼此間的間距較為緊密
時，愈後面的衛星液滴會因有前方液滴開路而
受空氣干擾愈小，所以定位較為精準。相較之
下，當衛星液滴數量較少時，衛星液滴彼此間
的間距就較為疏鬆，衛星液滴受空氣干擾愈
大，所以精準度較差。圖6為工作流體所產生
的衛星液滴數量統計圖，若與圖5對照比較，
可見液柱越長者衛星液滴數越多。再與表3對
照比較，可見衛星液滴越多者落地點範圍越
小。 
3.觀察液滴衝擊玻璃平板時的流體動力行為 
有關液滴衝擊壁面的研究其數量為數已
眾多，本實驗室研究群亦有所得如[7]與 [8]。
一般而言，液滴在接觸玻璃平板前，其所擁有
的能量為動能、位能及表面能，當液滴開始附
著於玻璃平板時，在慣性力的作用下，液滴產
生變形並以液膜的型態往外延展擴張，並同時
受黏滯力、表面張力的影響。當延展到最大值
時，由於表面張力的作用，液膜開始回縮，沿
著原來的延展線往回爬升，爬升到液滴所具備
的位能受重力影響而釋出後，聚集中央的液滴
再次往外延展擴張，但由於能量經過耗損，延
展擴張的半徑不如之前，如此反覆震盪，最後
完全停滯於玻璃平板上。當液滴衝擊玻璃平板
前，速度、粒徑較大者，所擁有的能量較多，
其延展擴張的半徑也較大；另一方面，黏度係
數、表面張力愈大，其延展擴張的半徑愈小。

的距離，液滴衝擊後為液體延展與液面波
動，但不至於產生飛濺，與前人相關成果符

5.由本研究之經驗得知，製作PLED所用的高分
子物質不易溶解，而其溶劑通常具有易揮發
性且具毒性，對製作過程中操作人員易生不
良影響。另外由於溶劑具有高揮發性，在製
作PLED的噴射過程中，極易將噴孔堵塞而使
噴頭損毀與製程中斷。因此溶劑的選用或調
配是熱氣泡噴墨應用PLED製作待突破的另
一課題。 
(二)自評 
    藉由本實驗可以清楚了解到，高分子元件
製作時其成敗之關鍵，製程中液滴噴射行為的
特性，如何控制或改善液滴噴射時之穩定以及
其影響的程度。若能掌握或控制液滴噴射之穩
定，此不但可以降低高分子元件製作之成本，
亦可提升噴墨列印之品質或精確度，不但在科
技產業上是一大幫助，對於國軍電子地圖的印
製亦可提升其精確度。

2.3 Parameter Setting and Fluid Properties 
The voltage and duration of pulse current through the 
heater in thermal ink-jet print head were 14.6 V and 2.6 µs, 
respectively. The frequency of pulse current resulting in ink 
injection was set in 1.0 kHz. The location of collection 
optics from forward scatter in the PDA system is 73 degree 
where the effect of refractive index changing on the PDA 
measurement is not sensitive; more details described in our 
previous work [9]. The tolerance of un-spheroid was set 
at 30%. To be consistent, all measurements were kept in the 
same nozzle in the print head in this experiment. An 
ensemble of at least 40％ data was collected for each 
measurement. The statistical uncertainty in estimating the 
mean velocity from randomly sampled data was estimated 
to be below 2%, according to Yanta [12]. As far as the size 
measurements are concerned, according to Tate [13] the 
maximum deviation from the actual cumulative distribution 
is expected to be below 2.5%. 
To identify the effects of the fluid properties such as 
viscosity and surface tension on the injecting, five fluids 
which are water, water-sugar solutions, water-glycerin 
solution and PF solution were used in this study (only four 
fluids were used in the PDA measurement). Table 1 shows 
their physical properties.

3.2 Drop Placement Accuracy 
 
According to the results of our previous [15] and this 
works mentioned above, whenever the liquid emits into the 
ambiance it always shapes into a column and then the 
column becomes longer as time progresses; and its tip 
gradually becomes more spherical in shape and then the 
main drop detaches itself from the column which integrates 
into some satellites. For the application of ink-jet printing, 
all the drops including the main and satellite are expected to 
be placed at the target. However, the instability of the 
integration of the liquid column will affect the placement 
accuracy.   Based on our experience, the instability will 
result in a variation of drop velocity, size and number of 
satellite. The variation of the radial velocity is much 
expected to affect the placement accuracy, and the size is 
expected as well.  Therefore, the axial and radial velocities 
and size of the main drop and satellite were measured by 
using PDA and the injection angle relative to the axis of the 
nozzle hole was derived. The spray angle was statistically 
estimated by using the standard deviation of the injection 
angle. Finally, involving the drop size the area and accuracy 
of the drop placement were evaluated.

3.3 Injection Angle and Spray Angle

placement accuracy by statistically counting dropping 
places one by one. In this work, the placement is estimated 
by using the spray angle (called θ) which is derived from 
the injection angle. Based on the assumption that the drop

3.7 Stability of Injection 
The stability of injection for different nozzle hole in 
the same purposed built print head was also investigated by 
using the water and measuring drop velocities and sizes. 
The results of different nozzle hole reveal that the mean 
size ranges from 25 to 36 µm and the smallest and largest 
standard deviation of the injection angles (σ) are 0.91° and 
3.24°, corresponding to the spray angle with 5.46° and 
19.44°, respectively. This fact indicates that there is 
variation in the injection character for different nozzle in 
the same print head. Therefore, the following measurement 
was kept at the same nozzle hole for different fluids. 
 
3.8 Injection Characters of Different Injecting Rates & 
Fluids 
At the same nozzle hole, measurements were carried 
out for different injecting rates and fluids, respectively. The 
results reveal that the standard deviation of the injection 
angle (σ) is affected by the injecting rate; it will be much 
larger especially in the case with 5 kHz injecting rate. 
For size, the mean diameter is not affected much by 
the injecting rate for the case of water-sugar solution with 
lower viscosity. But it will be affected for the case of higher 
viscosity such as the water-sugar solution (33.3%wt) and 
water-glycerin solution (40%wt); the mean diameter will 
decrease when the injecting rate reaches 3 kHz and more. 
This result is consistent with the result of Parrado and 
González’s work [14]; they attributed this fact to the timing 
being not enough to fill the fluid into the injection chamber.

4. CONCLUSION 
 
In this paper, the effects of physical properties of at 
least 4 fluids on the injection character were investigated 
the feasibility of the manufacturing P-OLED by using the 
bubble ink-jet printing was assessed. The results of this 
study are summarized as follows: 
The drop placement accuracy of injection was estimated by 
alternatively using the spray angle which was derived from 
the measurements of vertical and horizontal velocities of 
the droplet. The Particle Dynamic Analyzer (PDA) was 
used to systematically measure the droplet velocity and size 
for three fluids in this work. The macro-photography was 
also used to visualize the detachment process of the liquid 
jet at the exit of the injector. The typical results are as 
follow: 
1. It is practical to estimate of the drop placement accuracy 
of injection by deriving from the PDA measured axial and 
radial velocities and droplet size. 
2. In this work, the key factor which controls the accuracy 
of jet targeting is the breakup length of the injection. The 
longer breakup length is the more number of satellites. The 
situation that one satellite follows one closely will induce 
more stable flying, which results in high accuracy of jet 
targeting. 
3. Under the control of creating acceptable breakup length 
of injection by selecting suitable physical properties such as 
the viscosity and surface tension of the working fluid, the 
manufacturing P-OLED by using the bubble ink-jet printing 
is feasible.
