英文摘要 
  In this study, we used NaCl particles as porogen and genipin as coupling agent to prepare a 
macroporous composite membrane of chitosan/silica with 45.38% porosity and 107.18μm mean 
diameter via sol-gel method. Chitosan acidic solution (1 wt%) was coated on the surface of a alumina 
membrane to prepare chitosan/alumina macroporous membrane with porosity of 36.1% and mean 
diameter of 1.26μm . Some amino acids such as L-Histidine ( His ), L-Cysteine ( Cys ), L-Aspartic 
acid ( Asp ), L-Tryptophan ( Trp ), and L-Glutamic acid ( Glu ) were grafted onto the prepared 
membranes. Five different heavy metal ions (Fe3+、Co2+、Ni2+、Cu2+、Ag+) were chealated to amino acid 
grafted membranes and to check their oxidative activity on catechol (tyrosinase-like property). All 
heavy metal ion carrying membranes exhibited the oxidative behavior, converting catechol to quinine, 
but the His carrying membranes showed a highest oxidative power and the heavy metal ions 
immobilized on chitosan/silica membranes had higher oxidative activity than on chitosan/alumina 
membranes. 
   Besides the five amino acids mentioned above, another two substrates of tyrosinase 
L-3,4-dihydroxyphenyl-alanine (L-DOPA ), and L-Tyrosine (Tyr), and two tyrosinase inhibitors 
p-Aminobenzoic acid ( BA ), p-Aminophenylacetic acid ( PA ) were grafted onto chitosan/silica 
membranes too. The prepared chitosan membranes were aimed to purify tyrosinase from Agaricus 
bisporus. After working at different pH environments, the p-Aminobenzoic acid grafted chitosan 
exhibited the excellent adsorbing capacity by a 0.1 M PBS buffer as the extracting solution of Agaricus 
bisporus acetone powder at pH 4. The p-Aminobenzoic acid showed a competitive inhibition with 
tyrosinase. The analysis of the purified tyrosinase by SDS-PAGE showed only one band on the 
electrophoresis gel, this identified the selectivity of the BA grafted chitosan toward tyrosinase. The 
molecule weight of the purified tyrosinase is 111 kD which is almost the same as the molecule weight 
of the Sigma prepared tyrosinase (119 kD). The maximum adsorption capacity of the BA grafted 
chitosan toward tyrosinase reached 145 mg/g at 10℃, so this BA grafted chitosan membrane can be 
served as an affinity membrane on tyrosinase for commercial application. 
    The thermodynamic data showed the adsorption of tyrosinase by the BA grafted chitosan 
membrane obeyed Freundlich isotherm. The negative value of the adsorption ΔG and ΔH indicated the 
adsorption behavior is a spontaneous and exothermic process, and the adsorption capacity will decrease 
as the operation temperature increase. The negative value of the adsorption ΔS showed the arrangement 
of the protein molecule was more regulated on the BA grafted chitosan membrane than on the solution 
state. The kinetic data showed the adsorption of tyrosinase by the BA grafted chitosan membrane can 
be well expressed by pseudo second order kinetics. 
 
Keywords: macroporous chitosan membrane; grafting amino acid; chealating metals; tyrosinase-like; 
ligand; affinity membrane; tyrosinase purification.
