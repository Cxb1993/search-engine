 In the past 
several years, various 1-D metal-peapodded 
nanostructures have been successfully 
synthesized and reported.
 9-10
 Hu et al. found that 
Au nanopartilces encapsulated in silica 
nanowires exhibit a wavelength-dependent photo 
response due to the SPR effect from which it can 
be applied to the optical switching devices.
5 
Hsieh et al. reported the fabrication of gold in 
Ga2O3 peapodded nanowires where the Au peas 
were induced by the formation of twinned 
structures in the Ga2O3 pods. Qin et al. described 
the formation of Cu nanoparticle chains 
encapsulated in Al2O3 nanotubes with 
well-controlled manner by Rayleigh instability. 
Key parameters to be controlled are the size, 
shape, interparticle distance, and the surrounding 
matrix of the nanomaterials. Hence, to precisely 
control the assembly of nanoparticles is a crucial 
step. Several approaches, such as the colloidal 
chemical method, lithographical method and 
template agents have been applied to prepare 
these ordered structures.
13-16
 In this paper, we 
demonstrate a simple synthetic method to 
produce the one-dimensional Au/Ga2O3 
nanowires by the silica-enhanced and post 
annealing in situ two-step process. The 
Au/Ga2O3 core-shell nanowires were synthesized 
by a reaction of Ga, Au, and silica substrates via 
VLS growth mechanism. Subsequently, the 
Au/Ga2O3 core-shell nanowires were annealed at 
the same temperature for additional 3 to 30 min. 
During the growth of the oxide nanowires, gold 
was drawn into the Ga2O3 nanotube through 
capillarity attraction.
17
 Subsequent annealing of 
the as-synthesized core-shell nanowires led to 
the formation of gold nanoparticles periodically 
encapsulated in the monoclinic single crystal 
Ga2O3 nanowires by the mechanism of Rayleigh 
instability. 
For further studying the optical properties of 
these nanostructures, such as the plasmonic 
behavior; the mechanism of surface plasmon 
polarizations in sub-wavelength metal particles 
is that when the incident photon frequency is 
resonant with the coherent oscillation of the 
conduction electrons in nanoparticle of the metal, 
surface plasmons can be excited. It radiates 
electromagnetic wave which results in the 
unique color that is not present in the bulk metal. 
This phenomenon is known as localized surface 
plasmon resonance (LSPR), or particle plasmon, 
it is due to the charge density oscillation and 
confined between the metal nanoparticles, as the 
particle size is significantly smaller than the 
wavelength of incident light. As long as 
localized surface plasmons are excited, intense 
light absorption, light scattering and local 
electromagnetic field enhancement of the metal 
particle would occur. The features strongly 
depend on the composition, size, shape, and the 
surrounding local dielectric environment of the 
particles. In the present study, Au/Ga2O3 
core-shell nanowires were synthesized by 
thermal annealing treatment. The fabrication of 
well-organized nanoparticle chains confined in 
nanotubes was observed by in-situ TEM. A 
single nanowire optical device was designed and 
its photonic characteristics were investigated. A 
remarkably high on/off photo-current ratio 
 5 
observed that with the increasing annealing time, 
the embedded gold particles become larger. 
Furthermore, we did also obtain gold-peapodded 
nanowires with controllable embedded particle 
size as the core-shell nanowires were annealed at 
800 °C for 2 and 5 min. 
Embedded gold nanoparticles with different 
average diameters are prepared as described 
above. SEM images of the gold-peapodded 
Ga2O3 nanowire with 50, and 76 nm embedded 
nanoparticles are shown in Figure 3, parts a, and 
b, respectively. The shape of nanoparticles is 
nearly spherical. The size histograms of the 50 
and 76 nm gold particles corresponding to the 
SEM pictures are given in the insets of parts a, 
and b of Figure 3, respectively. Figure 3 (c) 
shows the scattering spectra of two different size 
gold nanopartilces. We find that with the 
increasing particle size, the corresponding 
spectra are red-shifted (λmax = 532, and 550 nm 
for the 50, and 76 nm embedded particles ). In 
other words, a strong size dependence of the 
plasmon absorption band has been observed. 
 
Figure 3. SEM images of the 
gold-peapodded Ga2O3 nanowire with (a) 50  
and (b) 76 nm embedded nanoparticles. The 
corresponding size histograms are given in the 
insets of parts a, and b, respectively. (c) 
Scattering spectra of gold-peapodded Ga2O3 
nanowire with 50 and 76 nm embedded 
nanoparticles. 
In addition to the size dependence of LSPR 
wavelength, another interesting effect has also 
been observed in our investigation. In general, as 
gold nanoparticles increase in size, their 
scattering spectra are red-shifted. Moreover, 
change to the shape of gold nanoparticles has a 
significant effect on their observed light 
scattering spectrum. Shape-dependent light 
scattering for gold nanoparticles is shown 
qualitatively in Figure 4. Figures 4 (a) and (b) 
are TEM and HAADF images of one single 
gold-peapodded Ga2O3 nanowire, showing that 
the gold particles embedded in nanowires are 
rod-like with an average aspect ratio of 2 and a 
corresponding scattering spectra of the nanowire 
is shown in Figure 4 (c). While gold spheroids 
embedded in Ga2O3 nanowires have a single 
spectra peak wavelength at ~ 530 nm (Figure 3 
(c)), the plasmon resonance peak splits into two 
peaks: located at the wavelengths of 500 nm and 
640 nm, respectively. Compared to the results 
obtained by Roorda et al.  on aligned gold 
nanorods in silica, the extinction spectra shows 
surface plasmon resonance shifts characteristic 
for anisotropic gold nanorods. According to 
Gans’ theory, The high-energy band absorbing at 
around 500 nm corresponds to the oscillation of 
the electrons perpendicular to the major (long) 
 7 
axis, while the red peak is along the major (long) 
rod axis. These results suggest that such 
controllable one-dimensional Au-peapodded 
Ga2O3 nanowires are excellent candidates as 
functional building blocks for nanoscale 
wavelength-dependent photo switches and 
optoelectronics.

一、 國際合作研究過程 
      我於 4/16/2011經台北抵達洛杉磯，並於 4/17/2011前往匹茲堡。經由 Georgia 
Institute of Technology的王中林教授介紹，於匹茲堡與 University of Pennsylvania
材料系的 I-Wei  Chen教授會面，在瞭解彼此的研究方向與成果後，洽談未來國際學
術合作的可能與內容。之後於 4/21/2011 – 4/23/2011轉往芝加哥的 University of 
Illinois at Urbana-Champaign與 Northwestern University的 Robert Chang教授
見面，並就彼此國際合作的可能作進一步的討論，在此一合作中，Robert Chang教授
希望能借重我們實驗室在高分辨電子顯微鏡上的專業對奈米材料作進一步的微分析。
同時間，也與 University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign材料系的 John Rogers
教授會面，他也希望能透過國際學術合作關係，運用本實驗室在高分辨電子顯微鏡的
專業，利用 EELS能譜的分析技術在材料分析與鑑定上有所突破。之後在 8/28/2011 – 
9/14/2011也再度前往芝加哥與作更進一定的細節討論與合作內容的洽談。4/24/2011 – 
4/25/2011則轉往波特蘭 Oregon State University對其研究環境作一短暫參訪，當天
也立即前往舊金山 Stanford大學與 Yi Cui教授會面，並相約加強彼此學術合作及交
流。之後，於 4/28/2011 – 5/1/2011轉往西雅圖與 University of Washington材料
系張一平教授會面，了對彼此研究內容後，也相約建立國際學術研究合作關係。同樣
在 7/7/2011 – 7/26/2011也再度前往西雅圖 University of Washington確認進一步
合作的方向及形式。在 15天的參訪及與國際著名學者會面洽談國際合作中，與眾多傑
出同行相談甚歡，也建立起和許多國際傑出研究團隊的合作橋樑與學術交流，自覺收
穫豐富，實屬不可多得的機會，感謝國科會的全額補助此次的行程，讓本人這趟學術
交流之旅豐富且圓滿。
