中文摘要 
 
關鍵詞：工程滿意度、學校工程、品質機能展開 
 
對於工程設施的管理者與使用者而言，要提升整體工程的滿意度，除了需慎選規劃設計建築師之外，營
造廠商的篩選亦頗為重要。在「921 災後校園重建工程」完工幾年後，許多學校均發生重建設施使用性欠
佳情形，引起社會大眾對於部分工程設施的需求性以及使用成效的質疑，可見改善及提升學校工程與設
施的整體滿意程度已成為一個重要的課題。本研究探討如何提升學校工程及設施管理者暨使用者(如總務
主任)之整體滿意度。首先，採用本計畫提案人將發展完成的營建工程整體滿意度評估模式，針對 40 所
學校的工程及設施之管理者暨使用者進行評估與調查，並彙整出學校工程及設施整體滿意度欠佳之項
目。其次，運用導入品質機能展開法辨識出工程及設施使用者的真正需求，並將其轉換成簡單易懂的改
善技術及策略。最後，透過模糊品質機能展開法之運用，評估並研訂改善技術策略之優先施行順序，以
供相關單位及人員進行決策之參酌。 
 
Abstract 
The overall performance of designer and contractor (including their engineering staffs) of construction project 
not only affects the success of the project significantly but also determinates the satisfaction of the project owner. 
It is thus very important for the project owner of the constructed facility to select good designers and contractors 
to accomplish the highly overall satisfaction of the construction project. After the completion of campus-rebuild 
projects due to Chi-chi earthquake, most facilities users of the earthquake-damaged schools are unsatisfied with 
the overall construction quality of relevant facilities. It is obviously that enhancing and improvement of school 
building construction and related facilities is a critical issue. This research focus on school building construction 
and related facility as the research object, probe into the improvement of the school building construction 
satisfaction. Firstly, the unsatisfied items of school building construction and related facilities will be gathered 
using the overall construction satisfaction assess model developed by the pre-research carried out by the 
investigator. A total of forty completed reconstruction projects will be evaluated to extract construction items 
with low satisfaction. QFD (Quality Function Deployment) will be used to extract the actual needs of the school 
building construction and facilities end users. Such needs will then be transferred into improvement technical 
and strategies in easily understood forms. Finally, the proposed improvement technical and strategies will be 
ranked based on various fuzzy degrees.  
 
Key words: Construction satisfaction, school construction project, QFD

模糊理論 
模糊集合理論(Fuzzy Set Theory)主要是用來處理事件範圍中模糊不清的問題。模糊理論允許「是否屬
於中間的中間狀態」，以隸屬函數概念代表模糊集合，允許領域中存在「非完全屬於」和「非完全不屬於」
等集合的情況。「模糊語意變數」是一種針對人類語意程度的不同所相對應的變數，其是以自然語言或人
工語言來表示，亦即將人類的自然語言(文字或字句)或人工語言中不同程度的詞語視為變數值 (如圖 1)。

圖 4 品質機能展開的四個階段 
 
QFD 的架構看起來像一間房屋，故又名品質屋(如圖 5)。QFD 透過一連串的品質屋矩陣分析，來達
成品質設計的目標。品質屋是實施 QFD 的主要工具，其是將顧客的需求轉換成生產技術上可完成的品質
要素，並使該設計達到某種程度的目標值。只要實施 QFD，一般都會把品質屋作為品質計劃的流程圖。
品質屋是由六大部份所組成： 
1. 左牆「需求品質」：列出顧客的心聲或顧客對產品的全部需求。 
2. 天花板「品質要素」：列出工程上的技術和生產條件（如工程特性、設計參數等）。 
3. 右牆「企劃品質矩陣」：列出顧客需求的優先次序。 
4. 屋內「關係矩陣」：標示顧客需求與工程技術的關係。 
5. 地板「技術矩陣」：列出工程上的技術和生產條件的優先次序。 
6. 屋頂「相關矩陣」：標示各工程技術間的關係。

研究結果與討論 
學校工程滿意度評估 
    透過「學校工程滿意度」評估因子之權重，進而擬定工程滿意度之評估表，以建置學校工程滿意度
之評估模式。設計、施工滿意度及服務滿意度共 53 項評估因子，為衡量工程滿意度項目。滿意度得分是
依受訪者對工程設計、施工品質滿意程度與服務品質滿意程度之認定計算而得（即滿意度得分=評估因子
權重*滿意度評分）。最後加總各評估因子之滿意度得分，即為各評估因素之工程滿意度分數。學校於興
建時往往忽略了使用者及管理者在使用上的要求，因此若能在學校規劃興建或改建時，參考使用者對學
校的意見，並將其納入整體建築中，將增加使用者的方便及提昇學校工程之滿意度。 
    根據設計、施工與服務三個滿意度評估結果，找出學校使用者目前使用上出現不滿意之處。不滿意
項目係低於整體平均值。所以個別項目離整體平均值越低，表示未來在興建學校建築工程需更加注意，
也是建築師與施工廠商必要改善的項目。反之，當個別評估項目高過整體平均值，代表學校建築完工後
在使用上並未讓使用者覺得不滿意，該評估項目即已達到學校使用者的要求。未來興建學校工程時只要
維持平常運作模式，設法維持該項目的滿意程度即可。 
    總結設計、施工及服務三階段使用者需求項目如表 4，清楚地顯示使用者對學校建築物之設計、施工
不滿意項目與服務不滿意項目共有 25 項。將來在新建學校工程時，這 25 個項目必為建築師與營建廠商
考量的重點，而如何改善此 25 項不滿意設施項目的技術策略，提高設計、施工品質滿意度與服務品質滿
意度甚為重要。

滿意度技術改善策略 
本研究藉由文獻回顧、訪談以資料收集，分別對設計、施工及服務三構面不滿意之項目提出改善策略，
期藉建築師、營造廠商所提出的改善策略來提高使用者滿意度。 
滿意度機能展開屋 
    使用者對學校建築不滿意項目及其改善策略，在找出改善技術策略時，再根據使用者需求及品質技
術展開之結果，進行品質屋的展開而品質屋中結構之主要內容為關係矩陣的描述。關係矩陣由品質展開
及技術策略展開組成，用以描述兩者間之關連程度，而利用品質屋找出技術策略構成要素優先之執行順
序，以決定技術改善的策略。關係矩陣之產生方式是由建築師、營造廠商及學校等各相關單位共同協商，
而矩陣中數據的取得乃是屬於建築師、營造廠商之主觀經驗判斷。 
工程設計滿意度藉 34 份問卷之設計品質機能展開屋總表(表 5)得知，如要改善其使用者不滿意的設
計項目，其改善技術策略如下：首先要改善的技術「考量實用性」，其餘依序為「規劃設計要點及保養維
修方式」、「配合當地地形設計位址」、「設計考量施工」、「配合環境佈置之材質」。最不需重視的依序為「更

2 Build the COA Adjudgment System 
2.1 Cases Base Reasoning 
CBR is a problem-solving paradigm for solving new 
problems based on previously solved problems[13-14]. 
CBR simulates the human thinking and problem solv-
ing strategies. With the case incensement in the case 
database, CBR provides a more accurate solution[15]. 
The basic principles of CBR is as a group of endless 
cycle, as the 4 REs shown in Figure 1 and described as 
follows. At the beginning, the new problem is defined 
and described as a new case, and is compared to the 
past cases in the case database to select the most simi-
lar one(s) trying to find out the possible solutions. If 
there are still differences between the new case and se-
lected similar case, the possible solution is revised to 
fit the new problem. Finally, via experts’ assessments 
and evaluations, the feasible solution is stored back to 
the case database for future use; on the other hand, the 
failure solution is deleted to avoid the same mistakes 
taken in the future. The concept of CBR has been ap-
plied to various construction research areas[16-18]. 
The procedure to develop the COA adjudgment sys-
tem is shown in Figure 1. The procedure can be di-
vided into five steps namely: (1) Cases collection and 
case database development, (2) Attribute weight as-
signment, (3) Case search, (4) Case adaptation, and (5) 
Case evaluation and feedback.

3 Case Search and Evaluation 
3.1 Case Search 
Once the case base is formed, the most important 
mechanism of the system is to find an appropriate case 
from the case base to provide reference to a new prob-
lem. In this case search status, the Filter Rule (FR) and 
the Nearest-Neighbor Retrieval (NNR) methods were 
used as the main schema. When a user inputs the prob-
lem attributes of a new query into the system, the FR 
will filter the cases in the case base to prevent unsuit-
able cases being retrieved from the case database. The 
NNR then calculates the similarity of the query and 
each case passing the FR by Eq. 4[9]. The similarity 
ranges from 0% to 100%; the higher the value is, the 
more similar the two cases are. While the most suitable 
case was found, the system provides the values of solu-
tion attributes of the most suitable case for reference.

3.2 Case Adaptation 
During the case search procedure, the query may dif-
fer from the cases. Thus, the FR and the Adapt Rule 
(AR) were designed to search and modify, if needed, 
cases, respectively. The FR is used to filter the cases 
that are totally irrelevant with the query to obtain the 
RC. The AR is applied to modify the RC, if necessary, 
to make the RCs satisfy the need of the query. 
Among the Solution Attributes, law (the gist law of a 
case), which directly influences the degrees of the im-
prisonment and the forfeit of the defendant, is an im-
portant analytic result of this system. Therefore, the 
Filter Rules use this attribute as the main filtering fac-
tor in this system, which means that the laws of the Re-
trieval Cases passing the Filter Rules should be the 
same with or reasonably similar to the law of the query. 
The citing of a law is influenced by four other Problem 
Attributes, defendant, charge, death, and injuries. Us-
ing the concept of Induction Tree, the cause-effect rela-
tionships are between the above four Problem Attrib-
utes and the Solution Attribute law.  
In addition to the law that the defendant will be pun-
ished upon, the defendant may receive extra punish-
ment according to his/her action after the accident 
happened. The “forthwith reporting of the accident, 
and the maintenance of the accident scene” is an ap-
propriate action, while “postponing the report of the 
accident” or “altering the accident scene” are all inap-
propriate action, and are against the Law. From the 
case study, there are ten cases with inappropriate ac-
tions. It is found that for inappropriate action, different 
punishments are applied on different defendants. If the 
defendant is a ”corporation and representative”, he/she 
may receive extra fines. If the defendant is an “em-
ployer”, he/she may receive on the average an extra 
one month in prison. According to these rules, the ARs 
based on the defendant’s action are established. 
3.3 Case Evaluation and Feedback 
This research developed the framework of the COA 
Adjudgment Decision System based on CBR. The us-
ers will play the evaluation roles as most the CBR sys-
tem does. If the user evaluate that the solution for the 
case is feasible, the solution case is fed back and re-
stored into the case database; on the other hand, the
