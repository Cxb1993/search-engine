I摘要
由於全球輕量化、省能源與環保的趨勢下，使得鎂合金成為產業
界重用的金屬材料。本研究計劃針對裝載腳踏車架體與鎂板殼件等之
鎂合金產品零組件進行研究與開發。在研究過程中擬使用 DEFORM
3D 軟體進行成形性之模擬分析用來協助擠製與鍛造模具之設計，並
運用田口法直交表進行實驗規劃，此外並透過模糊理論結合田口法來
尋求成品之多重品質特性之最佳製程參數組合，且以 ANOVA 變異數
分析來了解各製程參數對成品機械性質之影響程度。
本研究計劃第一年針對鎂合金腳踏車攜車架熱間擠製加工進行
研究與開發。在研究過程中使用 DEFORM 3D 軟體進行攜車架成形性
之模擬及協助擠製模具之設計，並應用模糊田口方法探討 AZ31 與
AZ61 鎂合金腳踏車攜車架之熱間擠製加工，並考慮多重品質特性指
標之情況下尋求最適化製程參數，及探討 AZ31、AZ61 鎂合金與 6061
鋁合金腳踏車攜車架之機械性質差異。
第二年針對鎂合金薄板殼件之鍛造成形加工之製程研究，採用擠
製所得之鎂合金板材來進行薄板殼件鍛造成形之實驗及其成形性之
探討，另外也使用 DEFORM 3D 軟體進行薄板殼件鍛造成形之模擬分
析。希望本研究之成果，能提供鎂合金成形加工之學術界與產業界之
研究人員參考。

41.2 鎂及鎂合金
鎂是所有結構用金屬及合金材料中密度最低的，與其他金屬結構
材相比，鎂及鎂合金具有比強度、吸震性佳、電磁遮蔽和抗輻射能力
強，容易切削加工，容易回收之多項優點，在汽車，3C、航太、自行
車等領域具有極重要的價值和發展前景，是繼鋼鐵和鋁合金發展起來
的結構材。隨著很多金屬礦產資源的日益枯竭，鎂以豐富的資源逐漸
受到重視，尤其是結構輕量化的技術及環保問題的需求更加速的鎂合
金工業的發展[4]。
1.2.1鎂合金特性
鎂合金的特色是質輕，僅有鐵的 1/4，鋁的 2/3。以相同體積之金
屬量測其重量，鎂約為銅的 38%，為純鋁的 60%。鎂合金的機械性質，
受到加工技術和取樣位置的影響，ㄧ般而言，鎂合金的特點如下[5]。
1. 所有金屬結構材中鎂合金的密度最低。
2. 比強度較高。
3. 在高壓下有良好的可鑄性。
4. 使用純度越高的鎂可提升抗腐蝕能力。
5. 若與塑膠材料相比有以下優點：
a. 鎂合金有較佳的機械性質
6鎂及鎂合金對於大多數的鹼類水溶液，以及有機化學品有耐蝕的
能力，但實際上除了鉻酸與氫氟酸外，所有普通的酸對鎂都有侵蝕性。
鹽類水溶液，也會侵蝕鎂，因此鄰近海水的鎂合金製品，都需鍍上保
護膜以防止腐蝕破壞。鎂合金中，含有少量的鐵或鎳，就會降低合金
的耐蝕能力，因此必須儘可能清除這些雜質[8]，避免鎂因受到腐蝕而
造成破壞。因此在儲存和使用過程中，必須採取適當的措施防止鎂的
腐蝕，如表面處理和塗油、塗裝等。
1.2.2 鎂合金命名規範
鎂合金之命名根據 ASTM-B275 標準規定，其符號名稱分為四部
分，如表 1-2 所示[9]，其第一部分表示二種主要添加合金的元素；第
二部分表示二種主要添加合金元素的含量；第三部分表示區別除二種
主要合金元素外其他元素之不同；第四部分表示合金狀態及性質。
以 AZ31B－T4 為例，如表 1-3 所示，第一部份「AZ」，表示鎂合

表示鎂合金中鋁與鋅的含量分別約為 3wt﹪及 1wt﹪；第三部份「B」，
表示第二種標準成分，第四部份「T4」，表示鎂合金經固溶處理。
81.3 文獻回顧
近年來由於國際環保意識抬頭，所以如何使能源的消耗滅少及產
品再回收性的利用即成為必要的課題，而鎂合金之比重為 1.74 g/cm3，
比鋁合金比重 2.7 g/cm3及其它鋼鐵材料比重還要輕，鎂合金之其它優
點則有比強度佳、防電磁干擾、散熱快及吸震性佳等等。目前而言鎂
合金之成形法還是以壓鑄(Diecasting)為主，Louis Braun [10]對於熱室
機及冷室機運用於鎂合金製造方面進行相關之研究。Tong 等[11]以量
測鎂合金壓鑄加工之模穴壓力與監控成形過程，來提升薄殼手機外殼
零件之品質。Huang 等[12]以壓鑄加工製作鎂合金試棒，其近澆口處
的砂孔狀態最少且緻密，而在試棒中間段則最差，但隨著增加壓
鑄加工的壓力則可以降低砂孔狀態。Zhang 等[13]探討 ZA104(0.3-0.6
Ca)鎂合金在壓鑄加工之產品的機械性質與微結構組織。Lee 等[14]研
究 AM50 鎂合金在高壓壓鑄加工中，其製程參數對多孔性分佈之影響。
傳統壓鑄加工成形後，產品常產生裂縫缺陷與顯微組織結構
不穩定，造成產品良率的下降，且缺乏球化晶狀分怖，因此發展出
半溶融壓鑄(Semi-solid die-casting)法，有助於改善壓鑄法之缺陷問
題。Du 與 Zhang [15]研究 AZ91D 鎂合金經半溶融壓鑄之顯微組織
與機械性質之行為，在 SEM顯微組織觀察可以清楚看出α-Mg globules
均勻分佈在基材中，且透過拉伸試驗得知經由半溶融壓鑄之 AZ91D

鎂合金之擠製成形極限，並以實驗進行驗證。Hsiang 與 Kuo [22]曾探
討 AZ31 與 AZ61 鎂合金熱間擠製成薄板後之成品特性與加工條件之
影響。Chen 等[23]探討擠製比對 AZ31 鎂合金之顯微組織與機械性質

之材料加熱溫度在精密鍛造對成形極限的影響，材料加熱溫度在 200

擠製加工依材料變形時的溫度，可區分為冷間擠製加工(Cold

度高於再結晶溫度，則稱為熱間擠製。由於冷間擠製加工無需加熱裝
置，所以整體設備較為簡單且材料無熱漲冷縮問題，因此工件的尺寸
精度與表面光度較高，而且模具壽命也較長，不過因為材料會有加工
硬化的現像，所以常需要進行製程退火，而且所需擠製負荷也較熱間
擠製來的高。由於熱間擠製加工時，所需之擠製負荷較冷間擠製低，
故可使用較大的擠製比以及較高的擠製速度，以提高單位時間的產
量，降低成本；同時成品斷面形狀可較複雜，所以在工業界大都採用
熱間擠製加工。

3.2 田口方法
日本品質工程專家田口玄一博士所創品質工程方法，由於成效良
好，甚受國際品質界人士的重視，美國品管界稱其為田口方法（Taguchi
method）。田口博士結合技術與統計方法，使產品設計和製造過程能達
到最佳條件，而迅速改進成本與品質[37]。
田口品質規劃法是運用在工程設計、製造程序與經濟原理的科學
方法，其目的為提升產品之品質。田口品質規劃法將可控制因子以直
交表進行規劃組合，在不失實驗結果的準確性之條件下，可大幅減低
實驗的次數，完成最有效的實驗。

3.9 結論
本計劃第一年主要結合田口方法與模糊邏輯探討 AZ31 與 AZ61

4.2 有限元素軟體簡介
本文中所使用有限元素分析軟體 DEFORM 3D V6.01，它是適用
於金屬成形中冷鍛、溫鍛、熱鍛之固體成形，聚合物之固體成形，多
孔金屬成形，靜態壓縮及鑄造凝結等製程之模擬。
DEFORM 全名為 Design environment for forming 本軟體是美國
SFTC ( Scientific forming technology corporation )之產品，其前身為
Battelle columbus lab.為美國空軍所研發之 ALPID 程式，專為解析大
變形塑性加工問題之有限元素分析電腦模擬軟體。相對於 ALPID，
DEFORM 增加了自動網格佈建程式( Automatic mesh generation )及自
動網格重建(Automatic remesh )縮短了網格佈建所需的時間，還有解決
網格在經過大量變形後，過度扭曲而產生負的 Jacobian 的問題[42]。
DEFORM的數值解析方法是以有限元素法求解，其基本概念係將
一複雜連續體分割成許多小元素，DEFORM軟體可將網格分割成三節
點四面體元素或八節點六面體元素之網格，但DEFORM自動網格佈建
功能僅適用於三節點四面體元素，然後導出各個元素的構成方程式
(Constitutive equation)，導入邊界條件形成勁度矩陣(Stiffness matrix)
後求解非線性方程式，可得應力、應變、應變率及負荷等詳細資料，
適合做多用途及軸對稱或平面應變及複雜模具邊界的分析，所以一般
套裝軟體大都使用此法。

第五章 結論
本計劃為二年之個別型計劃，其研究主要針對裝載腳踏車架體與
鎂板殼件等之鎂合金產品零組件進行研究與開發。其所得之成果如下：
1. 鎂合金圓柱壓縮實驗中，在室溫與低溫之間材料無法成形，必須
要在 200°C 以上才可以成形，其成形溫度範圍在 200°C 至 350°C
之間。
2. 鎂合金圓柱壓縮實驗中，隨著溫度越高，塑流應力越低，材料越
容易成形。另外隨著應變率越大，塑流應力越高。
3. 田口方法結合模糊邏輯理論之分析也可以同時滿足擠製負荷、壓
平強度與 T 槽破壞強度三種品質特性，應用此一方法嘗試去尋求
以較低之擠製負荷條件下可獲得較高攜車架之壓平強度與 T 槽破
壞強度。
4. 運用有限元素分析軟體 DEFORM 進行模擬，將模擬分析與實驗結
果做比較，模擬的結果與實際鍛壓的成品有一致性，故可知本模
擬模式可正確地模擬材料之流動狀況。此外，鍛造負荷與模擬分
析進行比較，兩者的差異在 12.63％以內，由此可知本有限元素分
析軟體 DEFORM 所建立之程式在預估鍛造負荷方面有不錯的準
確性。

2.  TAGUCHI METHOD AND FUZZY LOGIC ANALYSIS 
The Taguchi method is an efficient and successful experimental method for acquiring optimal 
process parameters. However, the Taguchi method only focuses on a single quality characteristic when 
analyzing optimal process parameters; this always leads to a mutually paradoxical situation among 
optimal process parameters for different quality characteristics. Therefore, fuzzy logics should be 
applied to look for optimal process parameters of the MPCI. By presetting quality goals, the optimal 
combination of process parameters can be analyzed and adjusted using the intelligent inference model. 
 
2.1  Taguchi Method 
The experimental planning method of Orthogonal Arrays (OA) proposed by Dr. Genichi Taguchi 
in the 1950s markedly reduces the number of experiments needed to achieve good reproducibility for 
acquired experimental results.  Via analysis by Measure of Quality, the optimal combination of 
process parameters of a single quality characteristic can be obtained. Through Analysis of Variance 
(ANOVA), one can analyze and evaluate experimental error, and significance different factor effects 
relative to experimental error.

4.2  Construction of the Fuzzy Logic System of MPCI 
This study uses Matlab software to construct the inference model of the MPCI. The S/N 
ratios for sheet tensile strength and extrusion load are taken as the input variables for the fuzzy 
logic system, whereas the MPCI is taken as the output variable. The acquired MPCI is used to 
perform statistical analysis of factor effects; the optimal combination of process parameters for 
the MPCI can then be acquired. 
 The two input variables are defined as the following 5 levels: very small (VS), small (S), 
medium (M), large (L) and very large (VL). The output variables of the MPCI are defined as the 
following 9 levels: tiny (T), VS, S, small-to-medium (SM), M, medium-large (ML), L, VL and 
huge (H). The fuzzy rule base adopts Mamdani’s fuzzy rule. The relationship between system 
input and output is expressed by an “If-Then” type . Table 5 shows the formulated fuzzy rule 
table. This table has 25 fuzzy rules, which are as follows:

The membership functions of both the input variable and output variable adopt a

4.4  Analysis of Variance (ANOVA) 
The purpose of ANOVA is to evaluate the significance of the factor effect on an experiment. The 
factor effect is not cited in the prediction formula until its significance has reached a certain level. 
Table 6 presents the ANOVA table of the MPCI for the hot-extrusion process for AZ31 sheets. Since 
each group of experiments has only one MPCI value, ANOVA evaluation cannot be calculated.  
Therefore, the small variances are integrated as non-influential factors for continuous calculation by 
ANOVA. Since the variance of billet heating temperature is 0.002, which is very small compared with 
the variances of other factors, billet heating temperature is integrated as a non-influential factor. The 
significant process parameters influencing the MPCI of AZ31 sheet extrusion are initial extrusion 
speed, container temperature and lubricant. 
Table 7 shows the ANOVA table of the MPCI for the hot-extrusion process for AZ61 sheets. Due 
to the very small difference between variances of billet heating temperature, initial extrusion speed

5.  CONCLUSIONS 
By combining the Taguchi method with fuzzy logics, the study investigates optimal process 
parameters for the MPCI of the hot-extrusion process for AZ31 and AZ61 magnesium alloy sheets, 
and improves the mutually paradoxical situation among optimal process parameters for different 
quality characteristics. Based on the results of this study, we conclude the following. 
1. The optimal combination of process parameters for the MPCI for the hot-extrusion process of 
AZ31 sheets are billet heating temperature of 320°C, initial extrusion speed of 3 mm/sec, 
container temperature of 400°C, and lubricant of boron nitride. 
2. The optimal combination of process parameters for the MPCI for the hot-extrusion process for 
AZ61 sheets are billet heating temperature of 340°C, initial extrusion speed of 3 mm/sec, 
container temperature of 300°C, and lubricant of boron nitride. 
3. Based on ANOVA results, the process parameter most significantly influencing the MPCI of both 
materials is initial extrusion speed. 
4. Combining the Taguchi method and fuzzy logics facilitates simultaneous acquisition of a low 
extrusion load and high tensile strength. 
5. The difference between predicted values and experimental values for the optimal process 
parameters for the MPCI for the hot-extrusion process for the two sheets is very small, 
demonstrating the feasibility of prediction, indicating that the Taguchi method and fuzzy logics 
can be combined and applied to optimal process parameters.

ABSTRACT 
 
This study investigated the mechanical properties and forming characteristics of flanged parts 
made of AZ61 magnesium alloys via hot working. The bearing cover of the gearbox in cars was 
selected as a carrier in hot forging to probe into the formability of magnesium alloys. A high-speed 
metal test machine was used for compression tests under different forming temperatures and strain 
rates to obtain stress-strain curves. The stress-strain data are applied to the Finite Element Method to 
analyze the formability of the bearing cover.   
Finally, based on the comparison of simulation and experimental results, we conclude that under 
the same billet heating temperature and low strain rate, the forming load was small and no cracks 
developed on the flanges of forged parts. However, under the same condition, the microstructure of 
the part was coarse. This study also attempted to identify a method for manufacturing a bearing cover 
with a low forging load, and determined how temperature influences hardness and microstructure of 
the bearing cover.

4.  Conclusions 
      This study performs cylinder compression experiments of the AZ61 magnesium alloy under 
different forming temperatures and different strain rates to identify the effects of forming temperature 
on magnesium alloy forging and forming, and establish the stress-strain curve. Additionally, this study 
conducts simulative analysis using finite element software under different forming temperatures and 
strain rates to obtain the distribution of forming load, stress and strain. Furthermore , after the 
experiment in which the magnesium alloy bearing cover is forged, the best forming conditions were 
acquired. Finally, via the hardness tests and metallographic observations, this study evaluated the 
effects of forging and forming under different temperatures on material compression strength and 
microstructure. The following conclusions are based on experimental and simulation results. 
1. In the magnesium alloy cylinder compression test, as temperature increased, flow stress 
decreased, and the ease of forming the material increased. Additionally, as strain rate increased, 
flow stress increased. 
2. The ring compression test applied to the magnesium alloy under the same forming temperature 
and compression volume, the constant shear friction factors of different lubricants in descending 
order  were as follows: dry friction > boron nitride > graphite > molybdenum disulphide. 
3. In the experiment in which the magnesium alloy bearing cover was forged, material forming 
was poor at room temperature and a low temperature. The best forming temperature range was 
270–320°C. As temperature increased, the ease in forming the material increased. However, 
when the temperature exceeded 320°C, flash and surface oxidization were created. 
4. Simulation results demonstrate] that, as the bottom of the bearing cover has significant 
deformation, its strain value is great.  Additionally, as temperature and strain rate increased, 
the strain value increased. 
5. As forming temperature increased, the hardness value decreased.  The reason for this 
phenomenon is that when temperature is low, fluidity is poor, and the crystalline grains extrude 
with each other, thereby generating an extreme strain, and a high hardness value. Since 
deformation at the bottom of the bearing cover was increased, the hardness value increased, and 
the crystalline grains in the acquired finished products were small.

5. CONCLUSIONS 
Combining Taguchi method with fuzzy logic theory, this study investigates the optimal process 
parameters for the MPCI of hot extrusion process of AZ31 and AZ61 magnesium alloy carriers, 
improves the mutually paradoxical situation of the optimal process parameters among different 
quality characteristics.  As a result, some conclusions are drawn as follows: 
1. The combination of Taguchi method and fuzzy logics enables the acquisition of higher 
flattening strength and T-slot fracture strength of carrier when the extrusion load is lower. 
2. There is a very small difference in the predicted value and experimental value of the optimal 
process parameters for the MPCI of hot extrusion process.  It reveals that it is feasible to apply 
the combination of Taguchi method and fuzzy logics to the prediction of optimal process 
parameters. 
3. The aluminum content in AZ61 magnesium alloy is higher than that in AZ31 magnesium alloy, 
so the mobility of material is worse, leading to a higher forming load.  However, the flattening 
strength and T-slot fracture strength of AZ61 magnesium alloy are higher than AZ31 magnesium 
alloy.

Introduction 
 
Under the trend that the whole world is paying higher attention to the concept of environmental 
protection, the recyclability of products and the ways to decrease energy consumption gradually 
become important issues.  Therefore, the use of light alloy, which is recyclable and able to 
decrease energy consumption, has become a main trend of today in the development of new 
materials.  Magnesium alloy not only has the excellent properties of light mass, high strength ratio, 
recyclability and reuseability, but also does not cause too much burden to the environment in the 
entire life cycle from product production to declaration of product scrap. Therefore, speaking of the 
pursuit of thin, light, compact, small, environmental protection based high-tech products, 
magnesium alloy shall be the most popular metallic material across the centuries.  
In 2004, Balendra et al. [1] developed the injection forging process, which enables the forming

conducted simulation and real extrusion tests, and demonstrated that cylindrical hot compression

Conclusions 
 
The study conducts compression test of AZ31 and AZ61 magnesium alloy cylinders under 
different forming temperatures and different strain rates, intending to know the effects of 
forming temperature on the forging fabrication of magnesium alloy, and establish a stress-strain 
curve.  The acquired stress-strain relationships of AZ31 and AZ61 are substituted in the finite 
element software, DEFORM, to perform simulation and analysis so as to find the data of punch 
head displacement, load, stress and strain.  Besides, the hot forging experiment of AZ31 
magnesium alloy bearing cover parts is conducted, hoping to find the optimal forming conditions.  
Finally, through hardness test and metallurgical observation, the study evaluates the effects of 
forging fabrication under different temperatures on the strength and micro-structure of the forged 
finished products.  After collation and analysis, the following conclusions are drawn: 
1. In the compression experiment of magnesium alloy cylinder, the material cannot undergo 
forming between room temperature and low temperature.  Only at above 200°C can forming 
be undergone.  Hence, a better forming temperature range is between 200°C and 350°C. 
2. In the compression test of magnesium alloy cylinder, as the temperature is higher, the plastic 
flow stress is lower, and the forming of material can be undertaken more easily.  Besides, as 
the strain rate is getting higher, the plastic flow stress will be higher. 
3. The material of AZ31 magnesium alloy cannot undergo forming in the first kind of die 
(having a spherical hole of 5mm depressed at the bottom) between room temperature and low 
temperature.  Its optimal forming temperature range is at around 340°C.  With the

FRICTION MODEL OF PIEZOELECTRIC ACTUATING 
TABLE 
The friction force components of this PZT actuating table can be 
divided into static friction and stricbeck effect viscous friction. If 
the control voltage is increased slowly from zero, the control 
voltage used to overcome the mechanism stick friction and preload 
can be found when it begins to move. This starting control voltage 
has nonlinear characteristic, and it is function of position, moving 
direction and temperature. It is called the dead-zone offset voltage 
in this study. If the experimental payload is small comparing to the 
36N nominal preload, and the specified moving speed is much less 
than the 250 mm/sec maximal allowable velocity, the slide motion 
speed will be linearly increased with respect to the control voltage 
increasing. Hence, this dead-zone stick friction control voltage can 
be measured by experimental testing. Fig. 3(a) and 3(b) are the 
measured motion speed with respect to control voltage of axes x 
and y, respectively. It can be found that the offset voltage of axis x 
is about 0.61V to 0.65V and –0.67V to –0.75V, and axis y is 
around 1.80V to 1.93V and –1.68V to –1.74V in both moving 
directions, respectively. When the actuating voltage exceeds the 
dead-zone offset voltage, the motion of X-Y table becomes very 
sensitive to the control voltage. If the actuating voltage exceeds 
the offset voltage, each 0.1V extra control voltage will cause about 
7mm/sec velocity change in axis x, and about 4.6mm/sec velocity 
change in axis y, respectively. Hence, it can be concluded that 
most of the control voltage is used to overcome this dead-zone 
offset control voltage. Besides, the dead-zone offset voltage has 
time-varying characteristic, it may change with respect to the 
operating position, the temperature and humidity. How to deal 
with this offset compensation is the key factor of this PZT 
mechanism motion control investigation.  
A PC based controller and experimental layout of this 
positioning system is constructed and shown in Fig. 2. This X-
Y table has two independent axes x and y actuated by two 
different piezoelectric actuators. This long range piezoelectric 
table can be used in micro or/sub-micro level positioning 
applications. The control voltage is calculated in PC, which is 
converted from digital to analog signal by a D/A interface card, 
and sent to the piezoelectric actuator’s driver unit to actuate a 
piezoelectric motor. Both axes displacements are measured by 
linear encoders, and sent back to PC through encoder card for 
closed loop control. The resolution of linear encoder is 0.1μm, 
and the maximum measuring range is 100mm.
