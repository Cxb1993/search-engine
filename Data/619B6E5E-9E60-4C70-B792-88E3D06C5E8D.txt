二、微接觸印刷（µCP）上墨機制分析 
2.1 印刷製程之重要性以及µCP 與其他印刷技術之性能比較 
由於與無機材料特性的不同，軟性電子除了以真空蒸鍍方式製作以外，還可
以使用溶液式的印刷製程；三星手機配備的 AMOLED 便是以真空蒸鍍方式製
作。真空製程具有較高解析度以及較穩定製程條件等優點，但也面臨大真空腔體
不容易製作與使用以及大面積蒸鍍均勻度不易達成等挑戰；相較下，印刷製程雖
然高解析度不容易達成且高品質印刷製程條件嚴苛，但由於其大氣操作環境以及
適合大面積製作之優點，許多研究單位與產業界也都積極進行印刷製程研發，其
中包括現階段以真空蒸鍍技術為主的三星公司。未來究竟誰會成為軟性電子的
de factor 製程，或者未來製程會是真空與印刷混合的製程仍有待時間的考驗；當
然若印刷製程可以達到高解析度以及高品質大面積均勻轉印的話，挾帶大氣與大
面積製作優勢之印刷製程將非常有可能成為未來軟性電子的主要製程技術。以下
便先對包括本團隊所提出之創新 µCP 印刷技術做一簡介，接著與現有印刷技術
做性能評估與比較，探討 µCP 在軟性電子製作之可行性與潛在優勢。 
圖一為 µCP 的製程示意圖，主要包括了上墨與轉印兩過程。µCP 最早由
Whitesides 的研究團隊於 1993 提出[8]，它採用自組裝單分子層（self-assembled 
monolayer，SAM）技術將墨水分子以化學吸附方式轉移至基板；為了拓廣 µCP
應用的多樣性，採用物理吸附技術之 µCP 也隨之陸續被提出；本團隊的創新 
µCP 便是利用凡德瓦力（van der Waals force）物理吸附 µCP 的一種。其概念與
日常生活使用圖章來簽章類似，也與軟性凸版印刷相似：以一個表面刻有圖案的
印模（stamp）將墨水分子（ink molecules）轉印到基板上，達到在基板上形成所
需圖案的目的。但它與一般蓋章和凸版印刷的差異在於 µCP 的墨水轉印為乾轉
印，凸版印刷與一般蓋章則屬於濕轉印；乾轉印使我們得以精準控制膜厚與均勻
度，但濕轉印則不容易對膜厚與均勻度有精準的控制。促成 µCP 可以乾轉印的
主要因素在於它使用了具有極低表面自由能的 poly(dimethyl siloxane)（PDMS）
矽膠印模；PDMS 的 rubber-like 性質使得即便基板表面並非完全平整時，印模也
可以和基板完全貼合（conformal contact），再加上其極低的表面自由能，而達到
墨水分子可以以乾轉印的方式完全轉印至基板。但是，雖然 µCP 的一切優點來
自於使用了低表面自由能的 PDMS 印模，但也因為 PDMS 的緣故造成許多高難
度的上墨挑戰，本計畫的第一項研究成果便是對 PDMS 上墨機制的探討與分析。
在詳細敘述上墨分析成果前，我們先對現有印刷技術與本團隊的 µCP 做一性能
評比。

3.2 高分子半導體溶液準備條件最佳化研究 
在針對P3HT場效應電晶體的研究過程中，發現P3HT的溶液溫度與使用的溶
劑對載子遷移率有極大的影響，因此對此現象作了一系列的實驗分析，成功地以
UV-vis吸收光譜結合P3HT backbone twisting的理論解釋了最佳溶液處理方式的
存在。圖六整理了主要的發現。首先，如大家所預期的，溶劑對P3HT的溶解度
好壞與載子遷移率成正相關，例如使用較好的溶劑甲苯比起較差的溶劑
p-xylene，可以得到較高的載子遷移率。同時當提高溶液溫度時，因為溶解度的
提升，也造成載子遷移率的提升。不過由於溫度的提升將造成P3HT backbone的
twisting，使得P3HT的共軛長度（conjugation length）變短，造成載子遷移率的負
面影響，因此有最佳溶液溫度的存在；實驗得到使用toluene或p-xylene溶劑時，
最佳溫度皆為65oC。在使用較佳溶劑甲苯並選擇最佳溶液溫度（65oC）所製作之

差異可以達到十倍。

Introduction 
Large-area, Organic & Printed Electronics – Convention 2012 (LOPE-C 2012) is 
the fourth annual international convention on organic and printed electronics 
organized by OE-A (Organic Electronics Association). Its objective is to facilitate 
establishment of a viable organic and printed electronics industry by providing a 
platform where international experts from academia and industry can exchange their 
latest R&D results and discuss the possibility of collaboration and integration. 
Because of the successful integration of AMOLED (active-matrix organic 
light-emitting diode) in Samsung smart phones, the actual market of organic display 
surprised every market predictions by a very big margin (Fig. 1). As a result, we 
observed a substantial increase in participation at LOPE-C 2012. Specifically, with 
3,500 m2 of exhibition space (an increase of 40%), LOPE-C 2012 hosted 1,560 
visitors (an increase of 36%) from 46 countries (an increase of 58%) and 104 
exhibitors from 17 countries (an increase of 14%). LOPE-C 2012 comprises a 
business conference, a technical conference, and a scientific conference; the business 
conference required a separate registration. We attended the technical and scientific 
conferences on the second and third days, and delivered an oral presentation at the 
scientific conference. The title of the oral presentation was titled “A novel liquid iris 
based on electrowetting technology.” 
This is our second participation at this important international conference. In the 
following, highlights of recent research and business development of organic and 
printed electronics are summarized.

A Novel Liquid Iris Based on Electrowetting Technology 
Cheng-Chen Yu*, Jeng-Rong Ho, and Jung-Wei John Cheng 
Department of Mechanical Engineering, National Chung Cheng University, Chia-Yi 621, Taiwan, R.O.C 
Chang-Pen Chen and Yeh-Min Lin 
Metal Industries Research and Development Centre, Kao-Hsiung, Taiwan 
*Author to whom correspondence should be addressed. E-mail: arthur1106@gmail.com 
Abstract 
With the market blooming of consuming electronics, the requirement of tunable 
lens has been increasing. As the adaptive liquid lens develops, many new types of the 
liquid iris have been presented. According to their driving mechanism, liquid irises 
can be classified into two major types: fluidic-pressure drive and dielectric-force drive. 
The former has a relatively larger tunable range. But t an extra pump system is needed 
to produce the pressure and regulate the fluid. It is, thus, relatively difficulty to 
control lens deformation precisely. Moreover, the operating time is long that restricts 
its applicability in today’s state-of-the-art optical devices. The dielectric force type, 
however, has the merits of accurate regulation and quick response time. Usually, it 
needs two immiscible dielectric liquids with different dielectric constants, and the 
larger difference in dielectric constants, the better tenability of the iris. Nevertheless, 
to obtain liquids with appropriate dielectric liquids is difficult that limits its 
development. In this study, we propose a novel configuration of tunable liquid iris. It 
consists simply of two immiscible liquids and two flat ITO glasses as electrode. The 
two immiscible liquids are transparent salt solution and opaque oil. The top electrode 
was pre-coated with a 3 μm-thick PDMS film as the dielectric layer. The surface of 
the bottom electrode was specially treated using UV irradiation to define hydrophobic 
and hydrophilic areas. The iris’ aperture diameter was turned out to be regulated 
easily by varying the DC bias voltages between the two electrodes. Results show the 
aperture diameter can be continuously changed from –1.5 mm at voltage-off state to 
3.5 mm at 350 V. This liquid iris has the features of low-cost fabrication, fast 
response time, low electrical power consumption, and easy reversibility without the 
need of any mechanical movable parts.
