第一節：前言 
美國聯邦通訊委員會於 1994 年首次使用同時多回合（simultaneous multi round，簡稱 SMR）
拍賣方式釋出頻譜執照。SMR 是由賽局理論學者設計，在執行前也使用實驗研究方法來檢驗
其各種細部規定。該次拍賣結果十分成功，被許多國家尊為典範經驗。台灣於 2001 年舉辦第
一次頻譜拍賣，該年交通部電信總局釋出五張第三代行動通信 (third generation 簡稱 3G) 執照。
2007 年時成立未久的國家通訊委員會 (National Communications Committee，簡稱 NCC) 舉辦
台灣的第二次頻譜拍賣，釋出六張無線寬頻接取( wireless broadband access，簡稱 WBA)執照。 
台灣 3G 執照拍賣採用標準的 SMR 拍賣設計，其細節與國際常用作法相同，六家廠商競
標五張執照，政府總收入高達 489 億元，整個釋照過程堪稱順利成功。雖然有此次成功執行
SMR 拍賣的經驗，但是 2007 年時 NCC 採用的拍賣設計卻與標準 SMR 拍賣有很大的差異；例
如採用百分比而非金額投標，增加投標前審議階段，對拍賣回合數訂定上限等等。這些特殊的
拍賣設計都是國際釋照經驗中前所未見，也正是本文研究的主題。 
為求正確瞭解當時採用與眾不同拍賣設計的原因，我們必須先認識台灣科技政策與產業政
策背景。本文第二節先介紹與此次釋照過程相關的科技政策與產業政策背景，第三節以台灣
3G 拍賣為例說明標準 SMR 設計的意義，第四節討論 2007 年釋照拍賣的特殊設計，第五節則
以實驗方法檢驗對拍賣回合數設定上限會造成那些影響，結論報告於第六節。

第三節：台灣 3G 執照拍賣--標準 SMR 設計 
交通部電信總局於2000年3月成立「第三代行動通信業務開放規劃小組」，規劃議題包含：
3G 執照開放時機，核發頻寬，執照數，以及發照方式採評審或拍賣制等等。為妥善進行台灣
的第一次執照拍賣，經建會也於同期間委託野村總合研究所進行「第三代行動通信執照競價釋
出整體規劃研究案」。參酌各方意見後，交通部於 2001 年 10 月 15 日公布「第三代行動通信業
務管理規則」（以下簡稱 3G 管理規則），其中敘明： 
鑒於頻率資源的有限性，無線通信業務之執照釋出時，均採對所佔用之頻率繳交頻率
使用費作為獲得該公共財之代價，由於行動通信業務的迅速發展，對頻率資源的需求
大增，在供不應求的情況下，許多國家紛紛經由市場機制決定頻道商業價值的作法，
藉以達成無線執照及頻率的釋出。基於政府財政資金需求、釋照透明性、經營業者的
風險評估及顧及消費者權益與市場有效競爭維持下，本業務執照採公開競價機制方式
釋出，以充分反映執照之合理經濟價值。

定標價增額下限 1%是為了確保標價逐漸上升，下限定的越高標價上升的速度就越快，有

第三節：2007 年 WIMAX 執照拍賣 
NCC 於 2007 年 3 月公布「無線寬頻接取業務管理規則」（以下簡稱為 WiMAX 管理規則），
開放北區三張（A1、B1、C1）以及南區三張（A2、B2、C2）共計六張執照。同年 4 月 12 日
至 6 月 11 日受理申請，計有大同電信、中華電信、遠傳電信，旺旺電通、威邁思電信、台信
聯合數位六家廠商對南北兩區均提出申請；大眾電信、創一投資、匯僑三家僅申請北區執照；
中華聯網寬頻、亞太固網寬頻、威達有線、寰宇通等四家僅申請南區執照，總計有十三家廠商
提出申請。 
因為 WiMAX 執照具有本文第一節所述之特殊政策背景，所以此次拍賣之制度設計與 3G
拍賣有甚大差異，以下分項討論。 
1. 決定優先執照 
為配合WiMAX產業鏈發展，故六張無線寬頻執照中有兩張指定用IEEE802.16e技術規
格(即通稱之WiMAX標準)且申請人不得為第一類電信事業市場主導者（也就是電信業的三
大廠商：中華電信、遠傳、以及台灣大哥大），以下將此二張稱為優先執照。NCC原本將
南、北的C頻道規劃為優先執照13，但是聽證會時業者認為C頻道比較好而有不同意見；如
果抽籤決定又怕公平性遭到質疑。所以最終決定由申請人填寫對六張執照之優先順序表，1

3. 實質審查： 
早期世界各國曾經採用審議制來分配頻譜，但是審查計畫書就和審美一樣，很難有清
楚客觀的標準，故審議制有時也被稱為選美比賽(beauty contest)。申請廠商都會試圖做出一
份看來最好的計畫書，但是計畫書寫的最好卻不一定是最有效率的廠商，因此各國政府大
多改用拍賣作為分配頻譜的方法以求達到資源之有使用。台灣3G拍賣規則也明訂釋照程序
採形式審查，拍賣前審查之目的只是確保廠商符合競標資格而不是進行實質審查。 
WiMAX管理規則中沒有明白指出將進行採實質審查，但是第八條敘名：「得設審查作
業小組；其審查項目及配分比重，由本會訂定公告之」。WiMAX執照於4月12日開始受理
申請，NCC遲至申請開始的當日才公告審查項目，每一申請案件之評分總分為100分，包含：
（一）事業計畫書書面審查：網路布局及技術能力（28分）、服務經營規劃（23分）、財
務規劃（23分）、用戶權益保障規劃（6分）、及加分項目（5分）等共計五項。以上加總
共計85分，但規定書面審查總分超過80分者，仍以80分計。（二）面談（20分）：邀請申

大同與威達也可能想降低其標價，也就是他們會有誘因背離表 4 的結果。 
世界各國電信廠商在參與頻譜執照拍賣時常會過於激動的競相提高標價，原因可能是對
新科技的評估過於樂觀，或是有必須參與新技術的市場壓力等等。NCC 基於照顧電信業者的
立場希望拍賣得標價不要太高，此原則值得肯定，但是 WiMAX 拍賣結果顯示單回合拍賣並
不一定能誘發業者理性投標，也無法保證標價不會飆高。 
5. 回合數限制 
為縮短拍賣進行時間並避免標價持續上升，WiMAX 管理規則第十四條規定「競價程
序至多進行十回合」。外生限定回合數的拍賣設計同樣也是前所未見的創新設計，以下將此
設計稱為同時限制回合拍賣（simultaneous limited round auction，簡稱 SLR 拍賣）。 
據我們所知，唯一類似 SLR 拍賣的是 Klemperer（2002）所提出的 Anglo-Dutch 拍賣
制度（以下簡稱 AD 拍賣）。AD 拍賣是對K 件同質（homogeneous）商品進行兩階段拍賣，

為單位，起始報價數值不得低 1.5%，第二到第九回合標價增額無上下限規定。為了避免競
標者在前九回合都隨便出價而到第十回和突然提高標價得標，故規定第十回合報價上限為
（第九回合暫時得標乘數比值 1.10× ）。因為第二到第九回合增額無上限，故如果依照
WiMAX 管理規則進行多回合拍賣，有可能標價乘數比會上升的很快。如果希望達成「標
價不要太高」的目標，似乎還是應該訂定增額上限。 
第十回合是拍賣的最後一回合，也是賽局理論反向歸納的起點，必須做詳盡規劃。暫
時得標者在第二到第九回合不能出價，但是暫時得標者如果在第十回合仍然不能出價，就
會落入「只能被搶標卻無法還擊」的不公平處境。因此 WiMAX 管理規則敘明除了填寫競
價標的及報價數值外，競標者在第十回和還要「再填寫一張單列投標標的之競價標的單，
並裝入另一信封內。…第十回和報價結束後，應先行開啟單列投標標的之競價標的單，供
第九回合結束後開標所決暫時得標者考量是否再對同一競價標的再為報價」。接著再依照以
下順序開標：「先行開啟非暫時得標者之報價單，其報價為有效報價時，方開啟第九回合所
決暫時得標者之報價單」。 
以上規定與一般同時行動(simultaneous move)密封標的精神有所不同。一般情況是競標
者在選擇標的並決定願付標價時，並不知道是否有其他人也對此標的投標，這就是所謂的
同時行動賽局。而依照 WiMAX 管理規則，第十回合是「順序行動」(sequential move)競標，
而非同時行動競標。暫時得標者不必先表達自己提高標價的意願，而是在知道有人想搶標
之後再決定新標價，故暫時得標者可能居於較有利地位。

Taiwan’s Wimax License Auction –  
A Case Study of Government’s Role in Technology Progress  
 
Chinn-Ping Fan* 
  
 
Abstract 
 
The government in Taiwan promoted the WiMAX standard in 2005, and leaped boldly into the 
unknown future. The WiMAX program was especially delicate in the sense that it required 
coordinated efforts from many government units. NICI was responsible for initiating the new 
technology; the main concern of the Ministries of Economic Affairs was the manufacturing and 
exporting of ICT products; and NCC was responsible for spectrum management and awarding 
WiMAX licenses. This paper analyzes the simultaneous limited round (SLR) design of the WiMAX 
auction with experimental method. Our experimental data show that limiting the number of rounds 
in an auction will not decrease the efficiency.  
 
Technology progress is a highly risky process; the official 4G winner has yet to surface. But 
still, Taiwan’s ICT industry has accumulated valuable learning experience in the past years. Even if 
it were not the leading 4G standard, Taiwan’s WiMAX policy still achieves at least part of the goal 
of technology progress.  
.

STAG’s work is been divided into 6 units, each led by a Director. Among them, the National 
Information and Communication Initiative Committee (NICI) is responsible for initiating national 
set by leading countries before 2005. From GSM, 3G, to WiFi, international standards and 
commonly used spectrum were first established by leading foreign firms, and then Taiwan followed. 
Also, manufacturing and exporting ICT equipment came before providing domestic 
telecommunication services. When releasing the first generation GSM licenses, there were no 
mobile telecommunication firms in Taiwan. This is also the case for the 3G industry. Firms began 
to manufacture mobile phones only after there was an established international standard. Within this 
process, Taiwan’s firms could not control the essential IPR, had to pay huge amount of royalty to 
foreign firms, and were forced into a competitive disadvantage. 
The first attempt to resolve this predicament could be traced back to 1998, when National 
Science Council proposed the First Stage National Science and Technology Program for 
Telecommunications. This program supported individual research projects from government 
agencies, telecommunication firms, and academic community. It is also a platform for researchers 
to coordinate their efforts and learn from the progress of other projects. The First Stage lasted for 
five years, from 1998 to 2003, with satisfactory results, and was succeeded by the Second Stage 
Program, from 2004 to 2008.  
Scientific researches were followed by industrial policy. To cope with the National S&T 
Programs for Telecommunications, a new agency, The Committee of Communications Industry 
Development, was founded under The Industrial Development Bureau of The Ministry of Economic 
Affairs. This committee had direct contact with ICT firms, it’s responsibilities included the 
following: organizing workshops on new technology, providing an open bulletin board for foreign 
and domestic firms to post business information, and gathering ICT industry statistics.  
With all the preparations completed, NICI proposed the “M (Mobile)-Taiwan Program” in 
2005, and officially declare that WiMAX would be the preferred 4G technology in the M-Taiwan 
program.  Figure 2 presents the complete structure of M-Taiwan program  
1. Form a complete WiAMX ecosystem, as shown in Figure 3 below, which includes chipset, 
CPE, base station, network elements, system integration, applications and commercial 
operation.  
2. Develop differentiated applications, e.g., IPTV Broadcasting over WiMAX.  
3. Design a coupled WiMAX/Wi-Fi network to leverage strength of Taiwan Wi-Fi industry.   
4. Leverage government sponsored research & development projects for core technologies, e.g., 
the WiMAX acceleration project.  
5. Participate in international standards activities and collaborate with international organizations 
or players to speed up product development, manufacturing, and market development.

Table3 shows that the variations of the winning bids were huge, and only one of the Telecom 
Big Three was among the winners. In fact, many details of the WiMAX auction were very different 
from the usual SMR auction, as listed below. 
1. The crucial proposal review  
Many countries used to allocate spectrum licenses through proposal review, but this 
mechanism have been called beauty contest since it lacks objective standards. There are currently 
also a review stage before bidders compete for telecom licenses, but the purpose of the review is to 
make sure that only eligible corporations could participate in the auction. And since the main 
mechanism for allocation of licenses is through auction, so usually all eligible corporations would 
pass the review and be allowed into the auction stage automatically. In the 2002 3G auction, there 
was also a review stage which worked just like this; to make sure only eligible corporations could 
submit bids.  
But the situation was very different for the WiMAX auction. The review stage turned out to be 
the most crucial stage for the licenses allocation. Thirteen firms submitted written proposals which 
were reviewed by a team of experts according to the following criteria: networking and technical 
capability (28 points), service management planning (23 points), financial planning (23 points), 
user’s right protection planning (6 points), and also, other extra credit (5 points). These items added 
up to 85 points, but it was regulated that the maximum grade for the written proposal was 80 points; 
the other 20 points were determined by an oral interview. The total points from the written proposal 
huge; the lowest winning bid (4.18%) was by FET, the only winning Big Three. And this figure was 
only one third of the highest bid (12.89%) submitted by First International. First International used 
to provide PHS services for a very small proportion of the market. But after years of slow growth 
and low profit margins, in 2007 First International was in the legal process of corporation 
restructuring. So again, judging from hindsight, the social benefit of that high bid of 12.89% 
seemed doubtful. One of the advantages of a multi round auction is that participants could begin 
from low prices and increase them gradually. It is likely that the dispersion of the winning bids 
would be smaller if the auction were multi round.  
3. The SLR Auction rule 
Article 14 also stated that even if more than ten firms survived the proposal reviewing stage, 
the auction would last for at most ten rounds. We call this design a simultaneous limited round 
(SLR) auction. This design is again peculiar. Why limit the length of the auction? Why ten? As of 
today, we could find no country that has similar design or theoretical support for it. Section 4 of this 
paper presents an experimental study of the SLR auction design. 
4. Bid increment rule 
The 3G and WiMAX auctions also had different bid increment rules In the 3G auction, Article 
25 regulated the upper and lower bounds on bid increment for every round. The bid increment was 
at least 1% and at most 7%, i.e., the bid submitted must be greater than or equal to 1.01 times the 
leading bid of the previous round, and it must be smaller than 1.07 times the leading bid. This 
design assured that the winning bids will ascend but only gradually. But the regulation of the 
WiMAX auction was different. The initial reservation bid was 1.5%, there were no upper 
constraints on bid increment for the second to the ninth round. And for the tenth and final round the 
bid increment is at most 10%. But since the actual auction was only single round, so these rules for 
the latter rounds was never implemented, but it seems that without limitation, the bids could 
increase to unhealthy high levels in the first nine rounds..

procedure for spectrum auction design. The 2007 WiMAX auction has many special designs; 
among these, I will study the SLR design with experimental method.

Subject first complete in a standard SMR auction. Each bidder is allowed to obtain only one 
license, and they can freely choose which one to bid on. The payoff of a winning bidder is his 
valuation minus the price he pays. For example, we expect B3 to win license X with a price of 1274, 
and hence she would have a payoff of 1296-1274=22. These payoffs were actually awarded to the 
subject as incentive to insure they would participate the auction seriously. 
There are actually no theoretical solutions for SMR auction, but we could solve for the 
complete information case. If the valuation of Table 4 is common knowledge, we would expect B3, 
bidder with highest valuation, to win license X, with a price of 1274, the second highest value. At a

pay any price above 1274. Therefore, B3 could pay 1274 and win the auction of license X. With the

How should we evaluate the successfulness of Taiwan’s WiMAX program? If we take “do 
nothing” as the baseline of comparison, I would say that it had been a correct move to promote the 
M-Taiwan and WiMAX program in 2005. Even in the worst case scenario, that WiMAX would 
cease to exist ten years from now, the learning that Taiwan accumulated in the past few years is still 
a valuable experience. This experience would make it easier for telecom equipment firms to 
approach other 4G technology. And also, the worst case may not happen. It could be that WiMAX 
and LTE will both survive, but in different parts of the world.  
A delicate part of the WiMAX program was that it required coordinated efforts from many 
government agencies, the NICI, Ministries of Economic Affairs, and NCC. Taiwan needed a healthy 
infrastructure for the WiMAX standard to grow upon. And in 2007 the very young NCC was the 
agency responsible for spectrum management and licenses awarding. Many of the details of the 
WiMAX auction design appear peculiar at first glance. But we must remember that the nature of the 
WiMAX auction is very different from other spectrum auction. As an emerging technology, there 
simply does not yet exist criteria to identify efficient firms. Therefore, it would be nearly impossible 
for the WiMAX auction to achieve the usual goal of maximizing efficiency. And with the general 
purpose of promoting new technology, revenue maximizing was also not a major concern.  
According to our experimental data, the performance of the SLR design was be very close to 
SMR, limit the rounds of an auction does not reduce efficiency. However, the actual WiMAX 
auction data show that the winning bids were very much disperse, and hence, it may not be a Nash 
equilibrium, i.e., some of the winning firms may want to reduced their bids after the outcome of the 
auction was announced. I think this problem lies not within the SLR design, but in the confusion on 
the possibility of single round auction. As stated earlier, some firms were expecting a multi-round 
auction; others might expect a single round auction. If the structure of the auction game is not 
common knowledge, it would hardly be surprising that the result was not an equilibrium.  
When the government promoted the WiMAX standard in 2005, Taiwan boldly leaped into the 
unknown future. Even if WiMAX were not the leading 4G standard worldwide, Taiwan’s WiMAX 
policy still achieves at least part of the goal of technology progress. In this process of promoting 
technology growth, the participations of telecom firms were necessary for the success of ICT 
industry. The telecom firms had duely played their role. Currently, there are 6 WBA licenses 
together serving a very small part of Taiwan’s telecom market. Considering the market share, the 
number of firms may be too large. It may be a good time for NCC to consider the future of these six 
licenses and the possibility of consolidation.
