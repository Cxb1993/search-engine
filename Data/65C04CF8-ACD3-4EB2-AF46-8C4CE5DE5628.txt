之一。換言之，其理念是基於旅客需求，
整合大眾運輸系統，盡可能提供較具吸引
力的旅運方案。於是，無縫旅行(Seamless 
Journey)的概念，出現於運輸領域。在倫
敦市政廳對未來二十年的運輸規劃中，也
強調要將簡單(Easy)，舒適(Comfortable)，
安全(Safe)及可靠(Reliable)，整合於無縫
旅行中(Authority, 2009)。我國交通部大力
推動無縫運輸(Seamless Transportation)，
期望透過空間無縫、時間無縫、資訊無縫
及服務無縫之系統規劃與服務，提升國家
競爭力。在政策執行力層面，目前規劃編
列三年新台幣約 150 億，並於 98 年度通
過三分之一經費，提昇各縣市無縫運輸軟
硬體設備。 
由上述研究背景可知，無縫運輸是一
件被主管機關高度期許的進行式工作，政
府也投入龐大預算支持。要順利推動無縫
運輸，其重要成敗關鍵在於如何明確化其
績效架構與準則？目前的績效為何？有
多少的提升空間？而其如何進行量化？
這三個問題是最為主管機關關心且注重
之問題。因此，本研究之研究目的為下述
三點： 
(1). 建構無縫運輸之定義，所延伸之績效
評估架構為何？特別是實際操作上的
準則？各主管機關是否有共識？ 
(2). 現今的績效值為何？與目標值(理想值)
的落差為何？我們該如何縮小兩者差
距？ 
(3). 針對必然會出現的不完全動態績效矩
陣，如何能以合乎邏輯之學理求解？ 
 
三、結果與討論 
   
明顯地，無縫運輸是針對大眾運輸系
統，特別是都會區大眾運輸系統。於討論
路網層級前，本研究先描述單一路線之無
縫運輸指標，而後由該區域內各路線之加
總，並配合路網層級之指標，即可獲得該
區域之無縫運輸績效。 
針對路線層級之指標，經由文獻回顧
及訪問調查，以某一地區的大眾運輸無縫
運輸的績效指標可定義為：空間無縫 x1(車
站平均距離 x11，停車轉乘設施是否足夠
x12、轉乘步行距離 x13、計程車派遣是否充
足 x14)、時間無縫 x2 (等車平均時間 x21、轉
乘平均次數 x22、與私人運具的旅行時間比
值 x23)、資訊無縫 x3 (運具資訊充足性 x31、
運具資訊正確性 x32、即時到站資訊 x33、行
前旅行資訊是否充足 x34、票證整合是否完
備 x35)及服務無縫 x4 (票價 x41、弱勢(含年長
者)之特殊需求是否滿足 x42、駕駛服務態度
x43、客訴反應 x44、環境整潔 x45、行李托運
x46)。經由回饋式對 12 位運輸背景之博士及
5 位高雄、台南及屏東交通局處之公職先進
進行調查，上述對於路線層級之無縫運輸
的績效指標涵蓋了對無縫運輸之滿意度為
79％以上。但於訪查的同時，受訪者未能
提出更多且明確之指標。 
對於路網層級之指標，除了前述路線
層級之指標外，於空間無縫 x1 構面尚須大
眾運輸工具覆蓋率 x15。 
若是不僅考量都會區，考慮偏遠地區

Abstract—The serious game and its application in e-education 
are more important for now and near future. In this paper we 
discuss the concept, scenario, and operation of an online 
serious game Chain Stone Master (CSM). By collecting the 
data from different participants’ database, we have the pricing 
operation of filtered 78 games for CSM. In perfect information 
transparency, decision makers analyze the business operation 
and price the suitable value for different goods. Price 
reduction, sustained, and increasing in different game stages 
and competition condition are formed a pricing strategy 
matrix. Actually, the matrix shows the pricing strategy of 
participants in this non-cooperative game. The decision tree 
method is used to identify the winners’ decision for each item 
in competition behind and superiority. By comparing pricing 
operation with the winners and other participants, we find that 
the winners try to make price steadiness (±13.5%) in different 
competition conditions, and it is smaller than other 
participants’ decision. But the decision tree can not explain the 
pricing logic completely.  
Keywords- Pricing strategy; chain store management; 
decision tree; serious game 
I.  INTRODUCTION  
Based on the mature network environment and the 
changing educational, online serious games or game 
learning are more important in the last decade. Serious 
games are designed for the purpose of solving a learning 
problem. David proposed that game developers how to tap 
into the rapidly expanding market of serious games. He also 
explored the numerous possibilities that serious games 
represent such as the ability to teach military training in a 
non-lethal environment and the ability to convey a particular 
political viewpoint through a game's storyline. Based on this 
book, a overview of all of the major markets for serious 
games, including the military, educators, government 
agencies, corporations, hospitals, non-profit organizations, 
religious groups, and activist groups are described [1]. Chan 
advocated that the government must invest more money in 
serious games [2]. De Freitas and Jarvispresents proposed 
the development of the Serious Games-Engaging Training 
Solutions. The interest in how immersive digital games can 
be applied effectively in learning and training contexts, 
along with the evidence that games-based learning show 
some initial evidence of accelerated learning, increasing 
motivation and supporting the development of higher 
cognitive skills were all bases for the development of the 
project. It is comprised of game demonstrators and 
evaluation techniques to measure the effectiveness of game-
based learning [3]. Nadolski et al. described the a 
methodology and generic toolkit for developing and 
delivering scenario-based serious games that are aimed at 
the acquisition of complex cognitive skills in higher 
education [4]. Westera et al. also presented a framework for 
serious game design, which aims to reduce the design 
complexity at conceptual, technical and practical levels. The 
approach focuses on a relevant subset of serious games 
labeled as scenario-based games [5]. These three literatures 
focus on the developing platforms of serious games. Kim et 
al. explored the effects of the meta-cognitive strategies on 
the academic and gaming achievements based on 
MMORPG Gersang. The participants, consisting of ninth 
graders, played the game until they all reached the third 
level to ensure that they have the same gaming ability prior 
to gaming for the study. Three meta-cognitive strategies 
were developed: self-recording, modeling and thinking 
aloud. Those strategies are specially related to gaming 
activities and applied in pre-gaming activities, gaming 
activities, and post-gaming activities. The results of their 
study imply that a commercial game playing in conjunction 
with meta-cognitive strategies can be an effective way to 
increase students' performance both in learning and gaming 
by keeping them involved. Talking and observation 
activities such as thinking aloud and modeling are more 
effective than writing activities in enhancing the students' 
achievements both in learning and gaming [6]. As the above 
literatures reviews, the students can learn more easily and 
quickly based on great design series games, and the 
supervisors also can know the students’ learning condition 
more easily. But we believe that we can find some 
interesting decision rules based on series games as 
simulating system. 
In this paper, we focus on the winners’ pricing strategy in 
a series game Chain Store Master (CSM) based on decision 
tree clustering method. Section 2 and 3 illustrate the goals, 
scenarios, operation steps, and judgments criteria of CSM. 
The data collection and pricing strategy matrix are shown in 
section 4. The analysis, discussion, and conclusions are in 
section 5 and 6.

B. The processing stage 
Once players are satisfied with their decisions, players 
can implement them for the following season (e.g. from the 
first season to the second). Once players finish one season, 
players cannot change their decisions or the results of that 
season. Players can only apply their experience to the 
following season. In other words, once players process the 
decisions of a season, no matter how much players want to 
change them, the new decisions can only be implemented in 
the next season.  
 
C. The result stage 
In organizational life, decisions must be obeyed. For 
example, the marketing department of Wal-Mart can test a 
model to determine the influence of a change in a price 
strategy (e.g. higher price, less goods) on business profit. 
They may prefer testing various prices and sales. After 
gamer finishing input decision data, the system will 
calculate the profit for each participant based on the 
designed formula in the server. The information from the 
server can be referred to players for next stage decision 
making. 
IV. 4. DATA COLLECTION AND BUILDING THE PRICING 
STRATEGY MATRIX 
A. Data collection 
The series game CSM is developed and used to train 
business college students over 5 years. We collected data 
from 92 games. Based on the total revenue is positive, we 
filter data of 78 games that each game has 5 teams and each 
team prices over 4 seasons. Based on the collecting data, we 
have 312 winners’ pricing decision sets and 1248 losers’ 
pricing decision sets in different simulation condition.  
B. Building the pricing strategy matrix 
We design a matrix called pricing strategy matrix. The 
columns include the ranking in last stage competition, 
market share, competitors’ prices advantage, and inventory 
levels. The rows include price adjustment ratio and purchase 
quantity. The matrix can be defined as a multiple dimension 
data sets.
