2Abstract
The development for red cell substitutes has been intensified due to the increased prevalence
of the blood-borne infections including hepatitis and human immunodeficiency virus (HIV).
Although hemoglobin and non-hemoglobin based blood substitute have been developed in
order to deliver oxygen and sustain life, problems remain with the products safety and
efficiency. Free unmodified hemoglobins can not be used as a red cell substitute because (1)
the large amount of hemoglobin dimers via the dissociation of free hemoglobins will damage
the kidney, (2) hemoglobins without 2,3-diphosphoglycerate (2,3-DPG) will lose their ability
to unload suitable oxygen in the tissue, and (3) free hemoglobins will scavenge nitric oxide to
cause hypertension. In our previous study, we conjugated the erythrocytic band 3
N-terminus derived peptide to lysines of hemoglobin surfaceusing the activated
polyethyleneglycol (PEG). The modified hemoglobin showed a lower p50 value, indicating a
better oxygen releasing behavior. We characterized that the peptide conjugation on Lys 95
may be responsible for the reduced oxygen affinity of Hb. For the past year, we had
successfully constructed the E. coli expression system to produce and purify recombinant
hemoglobin. In the future, we are going to change Lys 95 to cysteine using site directed
mutagenesis and then conjugate 2,3-DPG effectively to hemoglobin using the activated PEG.
Keywords: red cell substitute, hemoglobin
