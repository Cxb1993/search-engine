一、 前言 
 經濟發展與人類無窮的慾望，造就日常
所需的紡織品不斷地創新及改良，但其中使
用的有害物質，也引起環境問題甚至危害人
體健康。因此，近年來各國政府紛紛開始重
視化學物質的使用，其中，以歐盟最為積極，
對紡織品實施了嚴格的保護措施，並陸續公
告一系列的禁用染料與化學品的法規。 
 歐盟基於保護人類健康與環境安全的長
遠考量，將舊有公告的指令翻修與彙整，並
於 2006 年建立一個統一的化學品管理系統，
亦即要求化學物質需進行一系列的註冊、評
估、授權與限制的法規，稱為 REACH
（Registration, Evaluation, Authorization and 
Restriction of Chemicals），其目的在於全面性
對化學品及其化學物質之生產與進口進行監
管。本計畫藉由訪談檢驗單位與紡織廠商得
知，紡織產業鏈屬於化工業的下游產業，在
紡織供應鏈中，受 REACH 衝擊影響最大的為
織布/染整一貫化作業廠(統稱織染廠)，與原
先計畫提案的成衣廠有所出入，原因在於織
染廠擁有特殊的產業特性，不僅占台灣紡織
業廠商的 80%，其所出口的成品布亦占整個
紡織品產值的 60%，台灣紡織品近三年的出
口情形如圖一所示。

結果，其年度營業總額普遍高於人纖廠與成
衣廠，原因在於供應鏈需求的變化與產業環
境改變，導致染整技術提升，紛紛將低附加
價值的產品移轉至成本低廉的國家生產。而
我國則生產高附加價值產品或能快速反應的
系列產品，並將重心著重於機械自動化、安
全環保化與產品多元化等新環保形象[5]。因
此，在面臨 REACH 法規的要求下，織染廠無
論是直接將布料銷售至歐盟，或先轉售至別
的國家製成成衣及其相關紡織品，再由品牌
廠商直接銷售至歐盟，所受衝擊影響均大，
應立即尋找應變措施，否則將損失歐盟這塊
龐大市場。 
 爲此，本計畫以織染廠為對象，透過「綠
色管控」的觀念，發展因應 REACH 的管控作
業流程，希冀能從廠內原物料、化學品的採
購開始至生產製造端，達到有效的控管。 
二、 研究目的 
 REACH 法規基本的訴求原則為「No 
Data, No Market！」，說明沒有物質數據資
料，就無法進入歐盟的市場，沒有遵循
REACH 法規的腳步，織染廠將造成嚴重損
失。REACH 法規對於紡織產業化學物質之管
制，認定布料生產供應鏈中，所有參與者都
有責任來保證化學物質的安全使用。但經訪
廠詢問得知，台灣紡織業尚無統一標準作業
可循，REACH 法規除了造成內部管理作業負
擔增加外，更衍生出與供應商之間溝通瓶頸
產生，在製造過程中亦會生產不符要求的產
品。 
 爲此，本研究認為除了對紗線、染料/
助劑的來源要做控管外，在製程上使用到的
添加劑以及生產與加工過程中接觸到的化學
物質也需做一產品履歷的監督控管與追溯，
以確實地對歐盟(或經由品牌商)進行成品通
報高度關注物質(SVHC)候選清單含量、噸數
與進行成品中意圖釋放化學物質註冊事宜，

ECHA 將申請者之註冊繳交資料送至歐
盟會員國主管機關，判斷此化學物質是否需
進行化學物質評估，一般評估作業可分為文
檔評估與物質評估兩種。文檔評估由會員國
主管機關進行，審核申請者提供之技術檔案
是否符合法規要求，若文檔評估不通過，將
進行物質評估，並決定是否要求進一步的試
驗。當會員國主管機關判定此化學物質可能
對人體健康與環境構成影響，即可要求進行
物質評估。 
Restriction 限制 
由文獻[18][4][15]得知，若化學物質於評
估階段，經 ECHA 判定此化學物質有危害人
體健康和造成環境污染時，歐盟執委會或成
員國將會限制某些危險物質及配製品的製
造、銷售及使用，並對相關的物質頒布全歐
盟最嚴厲的禁用與限用規定，其限制等級內
容可以包括某些產品內的物質使用、消費者
使用，甚至全面禁止該物質的使用等。據文
獻[22]了解，ECHA 彙集了歐盟舊有的化學物
質指令，並已在 2009 年 6 月 1 日歸併至
REACH 法規附錄 17 之下，其項目目前為 59
項，未來將可能陸續增加。 
Authorization 授權 
 Blainey等學者[7]認為授權的目的是爲
了保證化學品在市場內受到良好的管控，並
保證受極高關注的化學物質(SVHC)在風險
控管上可得到充分的控制或者是採用適當的
替代物質或技術以取代之。在授權下的化學
物質均屬SVHC候選清單，ECHA衡量適當使
用SVHC時所承擔的風險和使用該危險化學
物質帶來的社會經濟效益，對危險化學物質
進行規範，希望有害物質之風險能夠適當地
被控制，以利銷售與消費者的使用[4]。 
Notification 通報/Communication 告知 
 通報為代表歐盟境內的成品製造或進
口商對已正式公告的 SVHC 候選清單，其總
量超過每年一公噸與 SVHC 候選清單在成品
中之濃度超過 0.1％(w/w)以上者，就必須進
行通報作業，目的為有效地管控化學物質的
流向。不僅如此，申請者同樣必須提供客戶
或消費者化學物質的安全使用資訊，以強調
化學物質資訊傳遞的重要性，並對製造或進
口商提出最嚴厲的管控。

五、 結果與討論 
 本計畫採用TNS架構之A-B-C-D步驟程
序作為此研究方法的核心，提出一套可彈性

幾點為本計畫所歸納出的計畫結果與建議： 
y 以「管控綠色產品」為主軸，發展一套系
統化的綠色產品管理系統架構，以供相關
產業做為建構新系統或改善原系統之比較
參考。 
y 面對 REACH 法規要求下，對台灣織染廠
的影響及其現況因應作法做進一步分析，
以研擬可行的方案，供尚未因應 REACH
法規或對此法規不熟悉之產業參考。 
y 現今，REACH 法規所規範的 SVHC 與附
錄 17 仍陸續在增修中，若未來 REACH 法
規所規範的物質高達數萬時，可視本研究
初期規劃的管理作業流程為基礎，延伸出
更嚴謹、複雜的細部作業流程。 
y TNS 之 A-B-C-D 步驟程序所提倡的觀念
與 P-D-C-A 雷同，都具循環改善的意味，
希冀未來在因應不同法規時，可採相同手
法延伸範疇，以規劃紡織品的整體管控程
序作業。 
y 本計畫所發展的綠色產品管理系統架構，
僅提出作業開發的流程及塑模，並未實際
根據該架構產生出具體的程式碼及系統樣
貌，此部分尚待未來延續研究。

A Green Control System for Production 
1. INTRODUCTION 
With the development of technology, 
people are surrounded by all types of electronic 
products. The shortening life cycle of these 
electronic products implies rapidly increasing 
amount of waste products and then causing a 
heavy burden to the environment. In recent 
years, many countries have started to concern 
about serious issues from polluting the 
environment with hazardous substances. 
European Union (EU) has been the most active 
one to recognize the need for a commitment to 
environmental protection. In February 2003, 
the EU proposed the RoHS directive, which 
regulated six substances of Lead, Cadmium, 
Mercury, Hexavalent Chromium, 
Polybrominated Biphenyls (PBB) and 
Polybrominated Diphenyl Ethers (PBDE) that 
are used in the electronic products exported to 
the EU. This directive stipulates that the 
concentrations of these substances should not 
exceed certain limits (Chen 2004)(EU 2003). 
Although it is one of the many sets of 
regulations made as a result of environmental 
issues, but RoHS directive particularly affects 
the manufacture of electronic products and 
related business operations and practices (Shina 
2008). Companies that export products to the 
EU need to make sure that their products meet 
the requirements stated in the RoHS. To 
comply with RoHS standards, a basic method 
that brandoriented companies have used is the 
establishment of a green supply chain 
management system. The purpose of the green 
supply chain is to produce green products and 
to ensure that the content of hazardous 
substances is held in check as per regulations 
and client requirements during the process of 
making the materials and components into 
products. 
In general, a product’s life cycle can be 
divided into six stages of product planning, 
design, verification, trial run, mass production, 
and after-sales service. The control of green 
products needs to be designed around all stages 
of a product’s life cycle, from selection of 
materials, product design, manufacturing, to 
packaging and finally product usage and waste 
disposal to ensure that the product meets the 
expectations of clients and complies with 
regulations (Ahmed et al. 2003)(Gong and 
Chen 2008). Production is an important part in 
this whole control process. Even if components 
are RoHS compliant, the final product still may 
not be a green product unless the production 
process meets the RoHS criteria. 
Firms, such as the systems integrators, 
improve their production technologies (such as 
lead-free) in their factories to produce green 
products, and at the same time the production 
management mechanism within the factories is 
adjusted accordingly to ensure products’ green 
quality. The production management should 
cover all bases, from quality control of 
incoming materials from suppliers, and 
continue on to the production process and 
ending with final product examination. This 
ensures that no hazardous substances remain in 
the products (SGS 2008). If the potential 
problems occurred during production are 
ignored and a product with high risk is not 
identified, it would be a great blow to the 
reputation of the firms while customs find out 
that the products do not comply with green 
regulations. 
Because of restrictions on hazardous

management, manufacturing process 
management, supply chain management, and

improve product quality and management of 
hazardous substance pollution caused by

activities. According to the QC 080000 HSPM, 
the following production related issues are

production flow control mechanism to monitor 
and measure the production process of

discussed. The QC 080000 HSPM will be

3. GREEN PRODUCTION 
As revealed in past studies (Tan et al. 
2002)(Zhu and Sarkis 2006), the definition of 
green production should include eliminating 
waste, conserving energy, efficient use of 
resources, and regulations’ compliance test. To 
minimize the pollution caused during 
production, we should start from fundamental 
issues of hazardous materials removal from the 
production process to lower the toxicity of 
waste and then to lessen the impact on the 
environment (Ahmed et al. 2003)(Fiksel 2006). 
This implies that a lead-free process must be 
specified first, which includes regulations made 
for leadless solder, heating equipment, and the 
reverification of lead-free process conditions as 
examples in a printed circuit board industry. 
Components that are compatible with leadless 
solder must also be clearly specified and 
component procurement will have to comply 
with RoHS standards as well. All manufactured 
products have to go through comprehensive 
testing and verification before they can be 
labeled as being compliant with RoHS and 
meeting client requirements.  
However, Handfield et al. (2005) showed 
that firms had mostly paid attention to product 
verification in compliance with the RoHS 
directive. Firms were often under the 
impression that they could claim effective 
green control in RoHS as long as the inspection 
reports and surveys submitted by suppliers 
were accepted. Even today, this circumstance 
still exists. Most firms stress management of 
suppliers but ignore the necessary information 
links during each process in the production 
stage. Once the information is not transparent, 
this may cause management problems related 
to the RoHS directive. For example, if during 
the procurement stage, there is no exchange of 
information with the production stage, then 
when there are component incompatibilities 
found during materials inspection or production, 
procurement personnel cannot receive 
immediate notification of the incompatible 
with the required specifications. It is always an 
important issue to make sure that the finished 
products are complied with green directives 
and customer’s requirements. During the 
product design stage, the content of hazardous 
substances in products has already been 
decided, but during mass production, the 
hazardous substance’s concentration still needs 
to be verified in order to ensure that products 
meet design standards. Thus, verifying whether 
components from suppliers comply with 
regulations, effective use of suppliers and 
component information during the design stage, 
ensuring that information can be used in 
production in a timely and correct fashion and 
providing production personnel with a 
reference have all become important facets.  
 
4.1 Incoming Quality Control Workflow 
Before production, the quality control 
department (unit) should go through the 
process of Incoming Quality Control (IQC) to 
ensure that the components used in production 
comply with environmental regulations and that 
the concentrations of restricted substances in

4.2 Equipment Inspection Workflow 
Firms need to make sure that production 
processes follow environmental standards. 
Firms also need to prevent normal materials 
from physical or chemical changes during 
production that causes them to exceed 
limitations in restricted substance’s 
concentration. Firms should have separate 
production lines and equipment to prevent 
crosscontamination of materials. Thus, every 
time a production line switches products, the 
machines and equipment need to be cleaned, 
the signs that apply to the materials last used on 
the production line need to be changed to avoid 
mixing of materials. Products that are made 
after the shift also need to be checked and the 
records of the checkups must be saved for 
tracing reference. Another issue is the lost link 
with the use of some consumable materials, 
because their use does not show up in the BOM. 
Examples of commonly used consumables on 
production lines, such as solder paste, solder

Pre-production and postproduction (prior to

preproduction equipment meets regulatory 
requirements. However, once post-production

In summary, the production risk

4.4 Final Product Quality Control Workflow 
When finished products are sent to QC 
units for quality control, QC personnel first key 
in work order’s serial numbers to retrieve data 
on the products’ production risk assessment 
and set a sampling ratio based on the 
assessment results. This step is followed by 
XRF (X-ray fluorescence) analysis. QC 
personnel perform FQC following standard 
operation procedure. Products passing 
verification tests are recorded and stored for 
future delivery. Products failed of test require 
more detailed follow-up inspection procedures, 
such as having them sent to inspection agencies 
for more precise and complete procedures. 
Products that pass such follow-ups are made 
ready for delivery while those that fail must go 
through defect analysis, and in such cases, 
manufacturing units should be notified and 
instructed to execute pertinent tasks, such as 
cleaning the production lines. A final product
