ABSTRACT 
 
Speech event detection and speech attribute identification for automatic speech recognition 
(ASR) become more attractive in the international community. The data-driven method for 
automatic speech recognition (ASR) is based on the speech corpus to model the speech units 
and language. It has shown to be an effective method for ASR. However, the performance is 
still unsatisfactory for many applications. The combination of knowledge- based approach to 
the data-driven models becomes more interested to researchers. How to integrate the 
knowledge of speech and linguistics into data-driven model is an important step to solve 
ASR problems. This project intends to investigate the evidence and verification of speech 
events. The sequential hypothesis testing will be the tool for the verification of multiple 
event sequences. Speech event detection and attribute identification are under investigation.   
 
Keywords: automatic speech recognition (ASR), speech event detection, evidence 
verification, sequential hypothesis testing, sequential probability ratio testing (SPRT)
