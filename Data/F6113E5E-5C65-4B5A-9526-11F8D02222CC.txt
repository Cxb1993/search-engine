一、前言 
氧化鋅(ZnO)由於具有寬能隙(3.2 eV)以及直接能隙優異的光學特性，因此跟 GaN 一樣，可以製作
出藍光及紫外光等發光二極體(Light emitting diode, LED)及光偵測(photo-detector)元件。由於 ZnO 擁有
較高的激子結合能(Exciton Binding Energy, 60 meV)遠高於 GaN (25 meV)，也因此有機會在室溫環境下
產生更高效率的雷射激發。再加上氧化鋅的成長溫度遠低於氮化鎵磊晶成長的溫度，另外還有無毒性，
材料來源充足，所以是非常理想的下一世代環保光電材料。由於氧化鋅本身優異的光電特性，相關奈
米結構成長及性質研究，已經吸引大量的注意。將奈米材料實際整合應用到先進光電元件，成長規則
排列奈米結構將會是非常關鍵的技術。另外現階段製作氧化鋅光電元件最大挑戰仍在於 p 型氧化鋅摻
雜不易。本年度專題研究成果將分成二維週期性層狀奈米結構氧化物磊晶薄膜成長及非極性摻雜銻氧
化鋅磊晶成長。 
二、研究目的及文獻探討 
1. 二維週期性層狀奈米結構氧化物磊晶薄膜成長 
RAO3(MO)m (R:Sc,Yb,Lu；A:Ga,Al,Fe,In；M: Zn,Mg；m:為整數)氧化物晶格結構是由 RO2-層以及
AO(MO)m+層沿著[0001]晶體方向兩層週期性交替堆疊而成。其而空間群分別為 R 3 m (m 為奇數)和
P63/mmc (m 為偶數)[1]，AO(MO)m+區域寬度與 m 值有關係。這樣規則自我排列的奈米層狀結構，可以
表現出的帶電載體的空間的侷限性[2]，被視為一個的自然形成的超晶格或自然量子井結構，並擁有獨
特的物理、光電、電、熱電及自旋特性[3-6]。然而高品質單晶或磊晶 RAO3(MO)m薄膜，為探討這些自
然形成超晶格氧化物的基本物理現象和性質相當重要。因此有不少研究團隊嘗試不同方法來成長各種
不同RAO3(MO)m磊晶層[3, 6, 7]。Ohashi提出利用自我緩衝磊晶技術來進行 In2O3(ZnO)5磊晶薄膜成長，
其 In2O3(ZnO)5 磊晶層在經過退火處理呈現良好的結晶性[6]。Hosono 提出可以透過結合脈衝雷射蒸鍍
系統(PLD)和反應式固相磊晶(R-SPE)技術可以成長高品質 InGaO3(ZnO)5 的磊晶薄膜，且該薄膜呈現優
異的電性[8]。 
在本研究中，我們嘗試著結合濺鍍沉積和 R-SPE 技術來分別進行 YbGaO3(ZnO)5 (YGZO5)及
InGaO3(ZnO)5 (IGZO5)薄膜成長。其中濺鍍系統而言，相對於昂貴的 PLD 系統，不僅使成本減少也可
以提供大面積濺鍍沉積的效果，進而成長大面積YbGaO3(ZnO)m (YGZO)或 InGaO3(ZnO)m (IGZO)薄膜。
另外嘗試在自製氧化鋅坩堝中進行 R-SPE 藉此來抑制在 R-SPE 高溫處理時，高蒸氣壓元素的揮發所造
成的成分變化。在本研究中分別以 XRD 及 TEM 觀察，將對於不同 R-SPE 製程條件對於 YGZO 及 IGZO
磊晶薄膜成長之影響進行研究。 
2. 非極性摻雜銻氧化鋅磊晶成長 
ZnO 已經被視為具有寬能隙半導體材料，它有紫外發光二極管（UVLEDs）、雷射二極管（LD）、
光偵測器[9-11]，由於 ZnO 優異的光學特性如在室溫具有較高激子結合能(60 meV)和在可見光內有高的
光學透明性，然而成長 p型摻雜氧化鋅仍然是一個挑戰任務，且成為 ZnO光電元件發展的主要障礙 [12]。

2. 非極性摻雜銻氧化鋅磊晶成長 
利用 RF（13.6 MHz）磁控濺鍍在 r-平面藍寶石基板上進行 Sb 摻雜 ZnO 薄膜沉積。陶瓷靶製備是
透過氧化鋅（純度為 99.9995％）和三氧化二銻粉末（純度 99.99％）均勻混和，經過球磨及燒結而製
備而成，其中所含的銻濃度分別為 0 at.%、1.5 at. %、2.0 at.%。濺鍍前會先將 r 面藍寶石基板切成 10 x10 
mm 尺寸，並在空氣中進行熱處理 1200℃退火 3 個小時，試片清潔用乙醇、丙酮、去離子水放置超音
波清潔震盪，清洗後以氮氣吹乾放置。進行濺鍍前，先將腔體壓力抽到 3x10-5 torr 後，再通入氬氣及
氧氣來進行製程，其氬氧比為 1:9，濺鍍壓力為 8 mtorr 基板溫度在 400-500 ℃下進行，RF 濺鍍功率為
100 W。試片退火處理則是採用 ULVAC MILA-5000 鹵素燈退火系統來進行。退火前先將退火爐抽到
10-3 torr，再通入氧氣並維持在 2 torr，退火溫度在 750 oC 為 3 分鐘。磊晶薄膜的微觀結構的利用 X 光
繞射儀（XRD）來進行分析；掃描電子顯微鏡（FESEM，HITACHI S-4700 用來觀察薄膜的表面形貌；
並利用 Philips Tecnai 20 穿透式電子顯微鏡(工作電壓為 200 kV)進行薄膜橫截面顯微結構觀察。ZnO:Sb
磊晶薄膜霍爾效應電性測量(Hall effect measurement)則是使用 HL 5500 PC 來進行，以 van der Pauw 配
置方式並在室溫及磁場強度為 0.32 T 下進行量測。

將是獲得 p 型半導體電性關鍵。

Reactive solid phase epitaxial growth of superlattice YbGaO3(ZnO)m thin 
films 
Hou-Guang Chen*, Yung-Shiang Lin 
Department of Materials Science and Engineering, I-Shou University, Kaohsiung 840, Taiwan 
 
This article describes the epitaxial growth of superlattice YbGaO3(ZnO)m (where m is an integer) thin films on 
(111) plane yttria-stabilized zirconia (YSZ) substrates through a combination of sputtering and reactive solid 
phase epitaxy processes. Homologous oxides YbGaO3(ZnO)m (YGZO) featuring nanoscale multilayered 
structures are known to display exceptional optoelectronic properties. Our fabrication process involved thin 
ZnO epilayers deposited through sputtering onto the YSZ substrates, YbGaO3(ZnO)5 thin films deposited at 
room temperature, and then high-temperature annealing (>1200 °C) of the bilayer structures, consisting of 
epitaxial ZnO seed layer and amorphous YbGaO3(ZnO)5 film. To suppress vaporization of ZnO from the films, 
high-temperature annealing was performed in a box made of ceramic ZnO. We used X-ray diffraction and 
transmission electron microscopy (TEM) to analyze the thin films annealed at various temperatures and for 
various durations. Accordingly, we established the epitaxial relationship between the YbGaO3(ZnO)5 thin film 
and the YSZ substrate as (0001)YGZO5//(111)YSZ and [11 2 0]YGZO5//[110]YSZ. TEM images revealed that 
high-quality YbGaO3(ZnO)5 epilayers could be synthesized through a suitable annealing process in the 
ceramic ZnO box.

2. Experimental procedure 
YGZO thin films were fabricated by combining radio-frequency sputtering deposition and R-SPE treatment 
on (111) plane yttria-stabilized zirconia (YSZ) substrates. The YbGaO3(ZnO)5 (YGZO5) ceramic target was 
prepared from Yb2O3, Ga2O3, and ZnO powders, which were mixed homogenously at a molar ratio of 1:1:10. 
The mixed powder was dry-pressed and then sintered at 1350 °C for 12 h to obtain YbGaO3(ZnO)5 (JCPDS 
No. 40-1351) ceramic disc, which was examined using powder XRD [Fig. 2 (a)], revealing that the 
YbGaO3(ZnO)5 target contained a small amount of the Yb2O3 phase. Prior to R-SPE processing, a thin ZnO 
epitaxial layer (thickness: ca. 20 nm) was sputtered on the YSZ substrate at 500 °C for use as a seed layer for 
the solid phase epitaxy reaction. Next, an amorphous or polycrystalline YbGaO3(ZnO)m thin film was 
deposited at room temperature on the epitaxial ZnO seed layer. Finally, R-SPE was performed through 
post-annealing at 1200–1300 °C for 30 or 60 min. To compensate for the evaporation of zinc from the 
deposited film, the bilayer samples were introduced into a box made of ceramic ZnO during high-temperature 
(>1200 °C) R-SPE treatment. The formation of YGZO films through this modified R-SPE process is 
illustrated schematically in Figure 2(b). 
XRD characterization of YGZO epitaxial films was performed using a Philips X’Pert diffractometer and 
a Cu K radiation source. Cross-sectional transmission electron microscopy (XTEM) was performed using a 
Philips Tecnai 20 microscope operated at 200 kV. The XTEM specimens were prepared using focused ion 
beam (FIB) technique. Optical transmission spectra were obtained by a JASCO V-670 double beam 
UV-Vis-NIR spectrometer. 
3. Results and discussion 
Figure 3(a) presents XRD 2/-scan data for YGZO thin films formed on YSZ substrates under various 
improved through appropriate R-SPE treatment. We evaluated the in-plane twist of the YGZO5 film through 
an off-axis skew symmetric -scan measurement [9]. Figure 3(c) reveals that the FWHM of the off-axis XRC 
profile for the YGZO5 10 1 10 reflection was 0.25°, suggesting that the epitaxial YGZO5 film had a larger 
lattice distortion resolved into the in-plane component. 
To further characterize the microstructures of the YGZO epitaxial films, we recorded XTEM images of 
samples of the YGZO thin films annealed at 1200 °C for 30 min (sample A) and 60 min (sample B). Figure 4 
presents bright-field (BF) TEM images of the YGZO film annealed at 1200 °C for 30 min; we observe a 
two-layer structure. Figure 4(a) presents the BF image taken along the YSZ [ 1 10] zone-axis; the inset 
displays the corresponding selected area electron diffraction (SAED) pattern taken from the interfacial region 
between layer 1 and the YSZ substrate, confirming that layer 1 is the YbGaO3(ZnO)5 phase with 
(0001)YGZO5//(111)YSZ and [2 1 1 0]YGZO5//[ 1 10]YSZ epitaxy on the (111) plane YSZ substrate. Remarkably, 
layer 1 and the YSZ substrate appear with dark contrast in the BF TEM image; these regions are oriented 
parallel to a low-index zone-axis ([ 1 10]YSZ and [2 1 1 0]YGZO5), which can lead to strong diffraction. Tilting 
the specimen allowed us to perform diffraction analysis of layer 2; for example, we obtained a YbGaO3(ZnO)5 
[2 1 1 0] zone axis diffraction pattern from layer 2 after tilting the specimen away from the [ 1 10]YSZ zone 
axis [inset to Fig. 4(b)]. Layer 2 appeared with dark contrast in the corresponding BF TEM image [Fig. 4(b)], 
indicating that it was a YbGaO3(ZnO)5 phase having no epitaxial relationship with the underlying structures.

contributed by these non-epitaxial YGZO5 grains.

Epitaxial growth of Sb-doped nonpolar a-plane ZnO thin films on

Hou-Guang Chen*, Sung-Po Hung 
Department of Materials Science and Engineering, I-Shou University, Kaohsiung 840, Taiwan 
 
In this study, the epitaxial growth of Sb-doped nonpolar a-plane (11 2 0) ZnO thin films on 
r-plane (1 1 02) sapphire substrates was performed by radio-frequency magnetron sputtering. 
The influence of the sputter deposition conditions and Sb doping concentration on the 
microstructural and electrical properties of Sb-doped ZnO epitaxial films was investigated by 
X-ray diffraction (XRD), scanning electron microscopy (SEM), and Hall effect measurement. 
The measurement of XRD phi-scan indicates that the epitaxial relationship between ZnO:Sb 
layer and sapphire substrate is (11 2 0)ZnO//(1 1 02)Al2O3 and [1 1 00]ZnO//[11 2 0]Al2O3. The 
out-of-plane a-axis lattice parameter of ZnO films reduces monotonically with increasing Sb 
doping level. The cross-sectional transmission electron microscopy (XTEM) observation 
confirms the absence of any significant antimony oxide phase segregation across the 
thickness of the Sb-doped ZnO epitaxial film. However, the epitaxial quality of the films 
deteriorates as the level of Sb dopant is increased. The electrical properties of ZnO:Sb film is 
closely correlated with post-annealing condition and Sb doping concentration.

4. Conclusion 
In this study, we report that antimony-doped a-plane ZnO epitaxial films grown on 
r-plane sapphire substrates by RF sputtering technique. The influence of deposition 
temperature and Sb doping concentration on the microstructural properties of ZnO:Sb 
epitaxial films was systemically studied by SEM, XRD, and TEM. The out-of-plane a-axis 
lattice parameter of ZnO films reduce monotonically with increasing Sb doping level. Based 
on XRD measurements and TEM observations, the absence of any other secondary phases in 
the ZnO epitaxial films was identified till 2 at. % Sb doping. Remarkably, the incorporation 
of antimony dopant with high doping level can make a negative impact on film crystalline 
quality and p-type conductivity.
