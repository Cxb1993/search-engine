ABSTRACT 
    The ionic functional polymer microsphere was prepared by using soap-free emulsion 
polymerization in this study. After the metal ions, Co2+ and Fe3+, were adsorbed on the polymer 
microsphere, the polymer with magnetic nano-particles shell were successfully prepared by 
in-situ chemical co-precipitation. The magnetic crystalline grain would be changed to 54.1 nm 
from 8.5 nm when the calcinations temperature was raised. Furthermore, silica was coated on the 
surface of the magnetic nanoparticles by sol-gel method.The hollow cobalt ferrite-silica spheres 
possess superparamagnetic property. Therefore, as the surface of the core-shell nanocomposite 
was modified by amino-silane to prepare the functional magnetic nanoparticles. In adsorption of 
heavy metal, the result shows that the amount of the metal ions absorption increased with 
adsorption times. That adsorption behavior is following the Langmuir isotherm. The absorption 
amount of single layer of saturation (qm) is 16.18 (mg/g) and Langmuir isotherm constant (KL) is 
0.07 (l/mg).  
 
Keywords: chelating functional polymer, nanocomposite,core-shell spheres, superparamagnetic   
 
中文摘要 
    本文利用無乳化聚合法製備出表面具有金屬螯合官能基之高分子微米粒，利用其表面
帶有之羧酸官能基與 Co2+、Fe3+行離子鍵結。接著搭配化學共沉法以及水熱法，成功在高
分子表面生長出鈷鐵氧磁性奈米微粒(CoFe2O4) 磁性晶粒更因溫度提升，粒徑由 8.5 nm 變
大至 54.1 nm。進ㄧ步地，本文利用 SiO2 層保護核-殼型磁性粒子，藉由反應溫度的增加，
水解縮合的速率由室溫反應 72 小時，縮短到 80℃反應 12 小時，成功製備高溫燒結型的中
空(Hollow)磁性奈米複合粒子，並利用矽烷耦合劑對其表面進行改質。由實驗結果得知所合
成之核-殼型及中空型磁性材料皆具有超順磁態之特性。量測功能性多層型複合微粒
（PMA/CoFe2O4/   SiO2/APTMS）吸附金屬離子方面，對不同重金屬離子有不同吸附量，
吸附量隨時間增加而增加，到一定吸附量則趨於平緩。由實驗數據得知功能性多層型複合
微粒較符合 Langmuir 等溫吸附模式，其單層飽和吸附量(qm)為 16.18 (mg/g)，實驗常數(KL)
為 0.07(l/mg)。 
 
關鍵字：無乳化劑乳化聚合、核殼型複合微粒、中空型磁性粒子

結 論 
本文成功的以功能性高分子乳液為模板，利用高分子表面所具有之羧酸官能基與金屬離子
行離子鍵結，並利用化學沉澱法及水熱法將 CoFe2O4 磁性粒子成長在高分子表面，得到一
具有核-殼型結構的磁性粒子。由其結果發現此核殼型粒子具有超順磁特性。接著再被覆
SiO2 搭配高溫煅燒製備出中空型複合微粒。此外，本實驗並對多層型複合粒子（polymer/ 
CoFe2O4/ SiO2）其表面進行改質，利用矽烷耦合劑（APTMS）重新賦予複合粒子新的功能
並保有舊有特性，將之應用於微波吸收、生化分離與金屬吸附。綜合以上的實驗與數據，

In this scope, a preliminary task was to analysis the characterization and kinetics of the 
polymerization reaction for specific operating conditions. Generally, the classical radical 
polymerization kinetics of MMA applies at low conversion. When then the viscosity of the reaction 
mixture increase, the termination reaction becomes diffusion controlled. Then the auto-acceleration 
of the reaction (Trommsdrorf effect or gel effect) leads to higher monomer conversion was expected. 
For industrial polymerization process, the auto-acceleration of the reaction is a source of a serious 
problem that control of the viscosity and the temperature is critical to obtain the desired molecular 
weight distribution. Furthermore, the reaction must be kept under control to avoid thermal runaway 
of the reactor. 
The target of this work was to investigate the characterizations and kinetics for the 
combination of CL and RSH as initiators. The effects of reaction temperature, CL, and RSH 
concentrations on the kinetics, gel fraction were reported respectively. 
Experiments Part 
Materials 
Methyl methacrylate (MMA) monomers were doubly distilled under a reduced pressure and 
stored below 5oC. ε-Caprolactam (CL) (Aldrich, EP grade), and n-dodecyl mercaptan (RSH) 
(Aldrich, EP grade) were used as received. 
Polymerization and Kinetics 
The polymerization was performed in a 4-neck Pyrex reactor. The whole polymerization was 
controlled under nitrogen atmosphere. Monomer conversion was measured by precipitating the 
polymer with methanol and dried in a vacuum oven. The samples were taken at particular 
time-intervals for kinetics study. Kinetics measurements were performed using the initial-rate 
method. For all rates of polymerization (Rp) calculations, initial rates were considered and 
determined from time-conversion plots. 
Measurements 
Fourier-transfer infrared (FT-IR) spectra were recorded using the BIO-RAD spectrometer with 
KBr pellets. 1H-NMR and 13C-NMR spectra were recorded with a Bruker 400-MHz FT-NMR 
instrument, and chemical shift were recorded in part per million with tetramethylsilane as internal 
standard. For ESR characterizations, the polymerization was performed directly in graded ESR 
tubes of quartz. The spectra were recorded with 100 scans over a magnetic field range of 150 gauss. 
The time constant of the spectrometer was 10 ms, and the sweep time for each scan was 5 second. 
Results and Discussion 
The polymerization of MMA initiated by CL and RSH has not been well study yet, therefore, it 
is necessary to exam the basic kinetics. In the absence of CL and RSH, no polymerization is noted, 
but trace of CL and RSH makes the reaction initiated at the same reaction condition. Apparently, CL 
and RSH pairs can cause the polymerization. Notably, no polymerization occurred when the 
polymerization consists of only MMA monomer and RSH. It is generally believed that the RSH 
cannot be used solely to polymerize the vinyl monomer. Instead, RSH is used as the chain transfer 
intensity of RSH is found in the polymer chain end, but the end group intensity decreases linearly 
with an inverse ratio of molecular weight. Thus, the terminal group, RSH, should exist in the 
polymer chain end. 
For a combination of HMQC and HMBC walked, the carbon and the proton resonance could 
then be assigned. These spectra provided additional proof for the exact chemical shifts, the end 
group structure were then identified unambiguously. 
Conversion-Time plots 
Figure 4 is the comparison of conversion versus reaction time at 78oC, and different 
concentration of RSH. As can be observed, the presence of RSH in the reaction medium modified 
the overall rate of polymerization. The bulk polymerization (line 4(A)) initiated with AIBN 
exhibited a large auto-acceleration rate of polymerization. The present of a chain transfer agent (line 
4(B) ~ 4(D)) in the reaction medium decreased the conversion at the same reaction time. The 
concentration of RSH little influences on the initial polymerization rate, but affect the gel-effect 
apparently. Besides, in the later stage of polymerization, the rate increases with enlarge of RSH 
concentration. 
Line 4(E) ~ 4(H) are the polymerization used CL and RSH as initiators. At low conversion 
regions, the chain transfer agents have effect on the initial rate of polymerization. The initial 
polymerization rate increases with the concentration of RSH. At high conversion regions, the 
concentration of RSH affected both the onset of the gel-effect and the increased of the 
polymerization rate. In addition, these conversions increased smoothly with reaction, even though 
the conversions are high. When RSH was used as chain transfer agent /or initiator, the 
polymerization reaction was mild, and hence the conversion was shifted to a lower value in the 
whole conversion range. 
Kinetics 
The conversion was calculated using the data of weight conversion. The initial polymerization 
rate, Rp, was determined by the conversion and reaction time data. The reaction order of the 
initiator and the effect of reaction temperature on polymerization have been carried out. Figure 5(a) 
~ 5(d), respectively, revealed the conversion-time plots for the various concentration of CL and 
different reaction temperatures (78, 83, 88, 93 oC). The initial polymerization rate increased with 
the CL concentrations. We further deal with the initial rate to CL concentrations to get the internal 
order. The plot of lnRp versus ln[CL] was displayed in Figure 6. From the slope of the straight line, 
the order of CL was evaluated to be 1.0. 
The influence of the concentration of RSH on Rp was also examined.  Figure 7(a) and 7(b) 
were the polymerization rate at a fixed CL concentration (3.37×10-2M), and different RSH 
concentrations at different temperatures (78, 83oC).  As it could be observed the initial rate 
increased with RSH concentrations, and the polymerization rates were indeed influenced at high 
conversions. Similarity, the plot of lnRp versus ln[RSH] at different temperature was given in 
Figure 8.  From the slop of the line, the order of RSH was 1.0, i.e. Rp changed with the 
concentration of R-SH.  In this system, thiol played a double role, i.e. as initiator initiating the 
polymerization at lower conversion, and as the chain transfer agent at higher conversion. 
temperature polymerization. The minimum was shifted as the temperature increased, and the onset 
shifted to higher conversion, too. In conclusion, the temperature changes had little effect on the gel 
effect, but RSH did. 
Conclusion 
The polymerization of MMA with ε-caprolactam and RSH proceeded by a living radical 
polymerization mechanism. By initial rate method, we had studied the rate of MMA polymerization 
and obtained a rate expression. The rate was slower than the conventional radical polymerization 
rate initiated with AIBN, the activation energy was calculated to be 15.20 kcal/mol and the 
frequency factor to be A=3.67x107 were both lower that of the AIBN system. During the 
polymerization, R-SH served double roles: as initiator in the early stage, and as transfer agent in the 
later stage, which could slow down the gel effect at high conversion. While the change of 
temperature effects little on the gel effect.
