1. ABSTRACT 
Fumed silica (FS) was physically blended 
with polyvinyl alcohol (PVA) in order to en-
hance the chemical, thermal and mechanical sta-
bilities. Methanol permeability was suppressed 
in the PVA/FS composites membrane.  The 
PVA and PVA/FS membranes were immersed in 
KOH solution to prepare hydroxide-conducting 
electrolyte. The conductivity of PVA/FS/KOH 
electrolyte immersed with FS concentration of 
PVA/FS composites membranes was examined. 
Direct methanol fuel cell performances were 
evaluated employing these electrolytes. The 
generated voltage and power density were stud-
ied as a function of temperatures, KOH concen-
tration, methanol concentration and oxygen hu-
midity. The PVA/FS/KOH electrolytes exhibited 
higher cell performance than PVA/KOH. The 
open circuit voltage of 1V and maximum power 
density of 39 mW/cm2 were achieved using 
PVA/FS(20%)/KOH at 40 oC, 6M  KOH the 
anode feed, and humidified oxygen into the 
cathode. The performance was higher than the 
literature data with the hydroxide-conductive 
solid electrolytes.

2. INTRODUCTION 
Fuel cells convert the chemical energy of 
methanol into electrical energy and provide al-
ternative attractive power sources as the crude 
oil price surges.  Among the various fuel cells, 
the direct methanol fuel cell (DMFC) has at-
tracting attention due to the high power density, 
low emission of pollutants, and the use of liquid 
fuel that can be easier to store and transport than 
hydrogen. DMFC is especially suitable in port-
able electronic equipment, such as laptop com-
puters, two-way radios, mobile phones, personal 
digital assistant (PDA), etc.

4.2 Ionic conductivity 
The conductivity values of PVA and PVA/FS 
composites membranes immersed in 2M KOH at 
different temperatures were analyzed. The ionic 
conductivities increased with increasing the FS 
concentration and temperatures in PVA/FS com-
posites membrane (Fig. 2). In our previous work2, 
we reported that the higher concentration of FS 
creates more free volume in the PVA membrane 
which could help water to easily diffuse into the 
membrane.  The higher bound water content 
also helps ionic conductivity at elevated tem-
peratures. Our previous report3 revealed that the 
bound water concentration in PVA/FS (10%) and 
PVA/FS (20%) membranes are 28, 33 and 44%, 
respectively.  
 
The data show that the conductivity values of 
PVA/FS composites at different temperatures and 
KOH concentrations. The maximum conductiv-
ity value i.e., 0.0859 S/cm obtained for PVA/FS 
(20%) composites membrane in 2M KOH at 60 
oC where as, 0.0539 S/cm acquired for PVA 
membrane in 2M KOH at 60 oC. Cheng et al., 
reported that the ionic conductivity values for the 
alkaline PVA/HAP (Hydroxyapatite ceramic 
filler) composite polymer membrane with 5 and 
10 wt.% HAP fillers are about 0.0251 and 
0.0442 S/cm at 30 oC, respectively.  Yang et 
al.4 obtained the conductivity values of the alka-
line blend PVA/TiO2 composites polymer mem-
brane is 0.0181 S/cm at 30 oC. The ionic conduc-
tivity values of above both cases showed the 
lower values than PVA/FS composites mem-
branes (0.0757 S/cm at 30 oC).

4.3 Effect of fumed silica concentration on cell 
performance 
The polarization curves and electrode potentials 
were obtained for PVA and PVA/FS (20%) com-
posites membrane in 1M MeOH/KOH solution 
at 60 oC. It clearly showed that the PVA/FS (20%) 
composites membrane exhibit the better cell 
performance and potentials than PVA membrane. 
It is believed that this finding is due to the tortu-
ous path present in the PVA/FS composites to 
prohibit methanol crossover in the membrane. 
The incorporation of inorganic nanoparticles can 
also decrease the membranes swelling in metha-
nol. The methanol solubility in PVA was 0.198 ± 
0.020g/g at room temperature. It decreased to 
0.030 ± 0.006 g/g in PVA composites containing 
20% FS. The combined effect of less methanol

5. CONCLUSION 
A direct methanol alkaline fuel cell membrane 
consisting of PVA and FS nanoparticles were 
successfully prepared by a solution casting 
method. Ionic conductivities and cell perform-
ance of these membranes were dependent on the 
silica content in the membrane matrix as well as 
the temperature and concentration of methanol 
and KOH electrolyte. Observed methanol per-
meability of PVA/FS (20%) composites mem-
brane was decreased to 2.575 ×10-6 cm2/s at 30 
oC. In addition, relatively  maximum conduc-
tivity and maximum peak power density of the 
PVA/FS/KOH direct methanol alkaline fuel cell 
is about 0.0757 S/cm at 30 oC and 30 mW/cm2 at 
40 oC, respectively. Besides its lower methanol 
permeability, superior conductivities and cell 
performance, the PVA/FS/KOH electrolyte 
membrane could be some of the potential mate-
rials used as fuel cell electrolyte membranes.
