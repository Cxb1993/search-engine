中文摘要 
磁旋行波管是藉由電子迴旋頻率的都
卜勒頻移而使電子束的能量轉換為毫米波
的高功率振盪器，並具有寬頻調節特性。
本 計 畫 探 討 使 用 散 佈 式 損 耗
(distributed-loss)磁旋行波管並操作在不同
的電流下，模擬結果顯示操作電流低於自
由起振電流，輸出功率隨著輸入功率線性
增加而增加且相位差為定值，屬於 amplifier
的操作區段。另一方面，當操作電流超過
自由起振電流時，非線性分析模擬結果發
現磁旋行波管在不同的操作下會有三種不
同的操作區間，分別是 amplifier、mode 
competing 和 injection locking 區間。在橫向
模式競爭方面，結果顯示當放大器操作於
phase-locking oscillation 區間，其輸出功率
再提昇操作電流或增加作用長度，因此將
此一機制運用於低電壓操作之磁旋行波放
大器，此放大器將具有高操作電流或長作
用長度的特性，如此可彌補因操作電壓降
低所造成作用效率下降。 
 
Abstract 
 
A gyrotron backward wave oscillator 
and traveling wave tube are a promising 
source of coherent millimeter wave radiation 
based on the electron maser instability. 
However, the requirement on phase 
coherence is strict for applications. In this 
plan, using distributed-loss Gyro-TWA and 
operating at different current, the simulation 
results of gyro-TWA driven by an external 
signal show that there are three roots exist. 
When the beam current is below free-running 
start oscillation current, the results of the 
nonlinear simulation are consistent with the 
results of the amplifier’s linear theory. This 
is an amplifier regime. When the beam 
current is above start oscillation current, 
there are three different operating regimes, 
amplifier regime, mode competing regime 
and phase-locking oscillation regime in a 
gyro-TWA driven by an external signal. In 
the mode competing, when the traveling 
wave tube operating in the phase-locking 
oscillation mode can effectively suppress the 
oscillation of non-operating mode. Using this 
mechanism in low-voltage operation of the 
Gyro-TWA, will have high current and 
intention length of long, so can compensate 
for low operating voltage drop caused by the 
efficiency of decrease. Beyond the 
start-oscillation current of the gyro-TWA, the 
amplification transits to the nonlinearly 
driven oscillation where the driven frequency 
approaches to the start-oscillation frequency. 
The performance of the gyro-TWA can be 
enhanced in the nonlinearly driven 
oscillation. 
 
Keywords: gyrotron traveling wave 
amplification, nonlinearly oscillation 
 
I. INTRODUCTION 
The gyrotron traveling wave amplifier 
(gyro-TWA) is a high-power, broadband, 
millimeter-wave amplifier. An interaction 
structure with distributed wall losses was 
recently shown to be effective in suppressing 
spurious oscillation. A Ka-band 
distributed-loss gyro-TWT was 
experimentally demonstrated at NTHU to 
produce a 93 kW saturated peak power at 
26.5% efficiency and 70 dB gain with a 3 dB 
saturated output power bandwidth of 3 GHz

C. Mode competition regime 
The simulated results show that multi 
solutions are obtained in an injected power 
range in Fig. 2. Figure 4 presents a 
phase-space diagram of the driven 
gyro-BWO. Based on nonlinear oscillation 
theories [3], [5], if the initial amplitude does 
not exceed the amplitude threshold (unstable 
limit cycle C1) in region I in Fig. 4, then the 
system enters the stable origin (P) where no 
oscillation occurs. It is elucidated that the 
oscillation does not initiate in the 
amplification regime in Fig. 1 unless the 
injected power exceeds a critical drive power 
of 0.0005 W.  
In contrast, if the oscillation mode is in 
either region II or III in Fig. 5, then the 
system moves to the stable limit cycle C2. 
Based on nonlinear oscillation theories [3], 
[5], the steady states [dashed line in Fig. 2] 
are similarly unstable states as like the 
unstable limit cycle C1 in Fig. 4. It is 
elucidated that two stable modes exist, 
amplification mode and nonlinearly driven 
oscillation mode [solid line in Fig. 2] for

the stable modes compete with each other in
