2Abstract
The alternative fuel vehicles not only make substantial pollution reduction, but also lead to
actual economic, environmental and health benefits to the society. The study developed an
"Environment and Health Co-benefits Model" to quantify the integration of economic and health
benefits (including the increase of average life expectancy and the reduction of annual medical
treatment expenditure) of current alternative fuel vehicles on the market. Analyzing consumer
preferences and willingness to alternative fuel vehicles through questionnaires, the cost-benefit of
subsidies mechanism was evaluated to provide recommendations for decision-makers.
The results show that the electric vehicle have more benefit than other alternative fuel vehicles,
with a saving of medical treatment expenditure NT$1,082/year*vehicle, and increasing the
average life expectancy 5,786 person*day/vehicle; followed by the new diesel engine vehicle that
can save NT$1,041/year*vehicle and increase 5,631 person*day/vehicle; the hybrid vehicle saves
NT$942/year*vehicle and increases 5,031 person*day/vehicle; with the least benefit, the
liquefied petroleum car save NT$831/year*vehicle and increase 4,730 person*day/vehicle. The
questionnaire results showed that consumers willingness to pay (WTP) NT$20,000、$60,000、
$100,000、and $100,000/year*vehicle respectively when they know the economic benefits for
LPG, new diesel vehicle, hybrid vehicle, and electric vehicle. The amounts of WTP are
NT$30,000、$80,000、$125,000、and $150,000/year*vehicle respectively when they know the
health benefits for these vehicles. WTP increased by 25% to 50% after knowing the health
benefits than that for knowing the related economic costs. It shows that people would pay more
attention to the environmental and health benefits.
The best cost-benefit ratio of alternative fuel vehicles (instead of gasoline vehicles) is -0.4 for
LPG vehicles, followed by 1.02 for new diesel engine vehicles, 0.10 for electric vehicles, and 0.
13 for hybrid vehicles. The main reason is that LPG vehicles have the lowest investment cost
among all alternative fuel vehicles (NT$50,000~70,000 for the refit expense from gasoline
vehicles). For hybrid cars and electric vehicles with less than 1.00 cost-benefit ratio, car
merchants subject to the technological improvements and mass production to lower the costs by
NT$562,426、(hybrid cars), NT$387,709 (electric vehicles) per vehicle, together with the
government subsidies NT$445560 , NT$622700 per vehicle, respectively, then the cost-benefit
ratio of government subsidy is 1.00 at this time which shows the relatively equal to the
effectiveness of feedback. After the activation of energy tax in the future, liquefied petroleum gas
vehicles can save running cost NT$4696/year*vehicle, it is the best among four types of
environmental-friendly vehicles. However, gasoline vehicles, diesel vehicles, hybrid vehicles and
electric vehicle will have increased cost of NT$5200, 3682, 903, 2520/year*vehicle. This makes
no difference to the. Besides, after the activation of energy tax, the liquefied petroleum vehicle is
the best for cost-benefit, and all of cost-benefit ratio will increase. Therefore, it shows that the
introduction and activation of energy tax will benefit the society in real terms.
Keywords—Air pollution, Greenhouse gas, Health benefit, Subsidy, Willing-to-pay.

技術說明
(中文) 1. 整合空氣污染物及溫室氣體減量效益內部成本及外部成本，同時分析包括財
務成本、措施之投資成本及國民健康的量化效益，呈現我國於各車輛替代能源補
貼及獎勵制度等政策下之經濟效益。 
2. 以問卷調查，統計消費者對於各車輛替代能源之偏好屬性與願付金額差異，
並且計算其在減量行動下之成本效益分析。 
 
評估車輛替代燃料補貼及獎勵制度所帶來之社會減量效益，並對其政策實施量化
分析及改善建議，可做為提供政府各相關部門補貼獎勵及相關配套措施參考依據。
(英文) The study developed an "Environment and Health Co-benefits Model" to quantify the 
integration of economic and health benefits (including the increase of average life 
expectancy and the reduction of annual medical treatment expenditure) of current 
alternative fuel vehicles on the market. Analyzing consumer preferences and willingness 
to alternative fuel vehicles, the cost-benefit of subsidies mechanism was evaluated to 
provide recommendations for decision-makers.
