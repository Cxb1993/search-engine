Abstract 
The spatial and temporal distributions of emission are both non-uniform.  The emission 
contributed by motorcycle exhaust is not simply determined by emission factors and traffic 
information.  In roadways, the atmospheric flow and temperature gradient between exhaust and 
ambient form the major mechanism to drive the complex pollutants’ dispersion within short 
distance.  However, this issue has not been studied extensively.  In addition, this spatial scale 
is comparable with the scale to evaluate exposures for motorcycle drivers and passengers-by.  
This topic is not only importance to the academic achievement, but also meaningful for 
environmental management. 
 
In order to properly resolve the pollutant distribution around vehicles, this study is focused 
on the dispersion of exhaust stream under the influences caused by the roadway environment.  
Computational fluid dynamics and turbulence model are combined in this study to develop a 
atmospheric dispersion model.  The field measurement is also designed to provide information 
for refining model parameters.  Based on the developed model, the flow field, temperature field, 
and pollutant concentration field are solved for several scenarios.  Based on the developed 
model, the flow field, temperature field, and pollutant concentration field are solved for several 
scenarios.  The results show that the sharp changing manners in flow and temperature fields 
result in the high concentration gradients.  For cases simulated in this study, the emission from 
motorcycles driving near the roadside cause significant influence on pollutant concentration in 
walking areas and bus stops for the bus-only roads which are inside than the motorcycle roads.
