 一、前言 
目前國內各大機車廠莫不致力開發一種稱為UV(Utility Vehicle)的汽車，UV是一種多功能用途車
輛，主要應用於off road，例如，可載人/運輸貨物，農產品/休閒娛樂使用/工務使用等，該車型也常
用於拖曳，故美國市場也稱其為TUV (Trail Utility Vehicle)。基本上，UV乃是由高階ATV，再加上
若干汽車式配備(例如頂棚、方向盤、防滾籠、後車斗)的衍生商品，因功能及用途極為廣泛，近來
為歐美市場接受，世界大廠更爭相投入開發。台灣在全球ATV佔有重要地位，主力廠商包括光陽、
三陽，宏佳騰，鼎力，合麒，益通，金蜂等，不但有自有品牌，成功銷售歐美市場，也是國際大廠 
OEM/ODM重鎮，我國UV產品在國外的形象是，台灣產品有日本大廠的品質，價格卻更為低廉。 
 
UV的銷售對象主要為較高經濟能力的歐美農場主人，以及注重休閒運動的白領階級，是我國少數
自有品牌的利基車輛產品，更是我國機車產擴展領域的最佳切入產品。然而，面對大陸的低價競爭，
小型低階的ATV產品市場，台灣已經逐漸喪失優勢，必須朝高附加價值的UV發展，例如低污染與
綠色節能，並且配合歐洲最新通過的的LV法規UV可以上路之趨勢，使其延伸至on road 使用。然
而必須提升動力性能、舒適性能、與功能，方能為市場所接受，使其延伸至on road 使用。 
 
本計畫主要與光陽公司及金屬中心合作，以三年的時間，將該公司自主設計的UV改型並提升等級，
以改善其原有因為限於農用搬運車而具備耗油與操縱性能不佳之缺點為目標，將原有之汽油引擎燃
料系統，以模組化串聯式之雙軸複合動力系統取代，並且研發各項創新模組與關鍵技術，使其提昇
等級稱為省能源與低污染之「多功能智慧型輕型電動車(MPI-EV)」，使UV除了原用途之外，更可
延伸為on road 使用，未來化都會短行程替代工具以及零污染農場或高爾夫球場之應用，以作為我
國機車業與UV製造產業之下一代主力產品，應用情境如圖1.1所示之情境動畫的部份分鏡圖。主要
規格為空車重小於600kg，平地極速65km/hr，最大起步加速力0.42g，前後軸各配置1組288V 5kW
的一體式交流同步馬達/發電機(Integrated Motor/Generator, IMG)，自動手排變速箱(AMT)，整車規
格如表1.1所示。

 三、文獻探討 
 
雙軸驅動電動車曾由 Kazama et. Al.[1]提出探討，且證實可以經由前後輪之動力分配控制，動態地
分配前後輪之驅動力矩，於加速時，避免車輪打滑的現象產生，而改進汽車驅動性能、操控性能、
及穩定性。然而該研究採用 CVT 傳動系統，與本研究之 AMT 不同。Colli et.al [2]以單輪之滑動率
控制為例，提出以模糊法則為基礎的控制法。此一研究對滑動率控制之物理意義非常有啟示，但限
於單輪之研究。 
 
在車內通訊網路方面，我國在 CAN Bus 上的應用發展已有數年，剛開始散見於各大學院校零星的
研究[3,4]。而有系統的研究及發展，則肇始於四年前，例如，虎科大的 CAN 收發模組與 CAN-LIN
收發模組，屏科大的 CAN 收發模組設計與驗證的平台[5]等。 
 
在輔助動力系統之建立方面，HCCI 燃燒有高效率，無節流損失和低 NOx 和 PM 的排放，以及均質
混合氣等優點。汽缸內的 HCCI 燃燒為多點燃燒[6]。HCCI 燃燒時燃燒控制困難，而且必要時要對
混合氣加溫，如此會導致進氣密度降低形成容積效率變差，以及在混合氣較濃時，爆震的聲音會更
強烈，這些都是 HCCI 引擎所需面對與處理的問題[7]。 
 
在能量回收型主動式懸吊系統，文獻[8]提出負載端為各種電源電壓時之充電電路設計方案，以提
供高效率之輸出電流，文獻[9]利用倒階控制設計主動式懸吊系統，並利用里亞普諾夫證明其穩定
性。文獻[10]則利用適應性控制法則設計具有克服參數不確定性之智慧型控制架構。 
 
在車體結構建模與分析方面，國內有關車輛結構之研究，均是利用有限元素法來分析探討 [11]，
且大多是大客車之研究為主。Baskin 等人[12]利用有限元素模型來分析汽車結構之輕量化及最佳
化，結果可降低車重 6%至 15.3 %。Yamamoto 等人 [13]則是結合 topology 最佳化以及形狀最佳化
來探討汽車結構之最佳化設計。Kuo [14]等人則指出，接點強度及主結構之斷面形狀是主要影響其
靜態及動態剛度之主要因素。 
 
在停車輔助系統方面，Peterson[15]利用 Burmester theory 合成具死點構型之平面四連桿起落架收放
機構。Artobolevsky [16]提到許多起落架收放機構。 
 
在冷氣空調系統方面，現今內燃機車輛之冷氣空調系統大多以皮帶帶動壓縮機，車內冷氣空調系統
之性能高低對燃料的消耗或電池續航力都有極大的影響。過去關於各式冷氣空調系統的研究與應用
當中可以發現壓縮機的動力來源、壓縮機性能、冷媒選用、管路設計、控制與熱交換閥桶的加入等
元件與系統的調整與改裝皆會對冷氣空調系統之性能表現具有關鍵性之影響[17]。 
 
有關車廂散熱之研究，針對密閉空間內之氣流運動與溫溼度控制之研究方面，國內、外均有相關的
研究報告。過去幾十年來，在室內氣流運動方面研究方式主要分為實驗量測及電腦模擬兩種，研究
重點著重於室內流場之速度分佈、溫度分佈、紊流特性及人體舒適度研究等，如 Wu 等人以太陽輻
射和強制對流為邊界條件，用數值模擬客車之隔熱箱體結構溫度及速度分佈[18]，Chow 等人以模
擬方式研究火車車廂的空氣品質及太陽熱量對乘客舒適度影響[19]，Fan 等人提出一暫態熱負荷模
擬計算法，模擬飛機座艙暫態熱負荷，並將結果與傳統方式做比較[20]。 
 
有關汽車消費需求心理之分析，目前尚就讀大學階段的年輕族群將是十年後汽車的主要消費者，由於
其異於目前世代的特殊文化背景，未來購買未來車之需求可能會和本世代消費者之模式有所不同。購
車評估準則可分為四大主要因子，分別為經濟性、功能性、安全性及外觀性，陳仕煥[21]。陳韋仁[22]
研究 24-35 歲之年輕族群之生活型態與首購房車決策，分析出不同生活型態之集群人口對購車之決策
考量方向有所差異。

 1.2 車內控制通訊網路建構 
本項工作是建立 MPI-EV CAN-BUS 車用通訊網路。本年度已經完成網路架構與制定各模組間的通
訊協定，並且完成收發模組之製作。車內通訊系統主要是進行連結 MPI-EV 整車控制器( Vehicle 
Control Unit, VCU )以及各個子計劃模組和感測元件之間訊號溝通網路以及配合偵測各模組之加入
/取出以及其狀態，如車上驅動馬達之耗電量、電池模組之殘電量、模組化輔助動力系統之燃油儲
量及發電量、智慧型空調系統模組之耗電量等資訊，並傳送相關之控制命令給各相關模組以控制其
作動情況。具體的作法是以國內車用通訊網路架構規劃方式，進行 MPI-EV CAN-BUS 通訊網路軟
體架構之規劃，然後根據 SAE J1939/71 標準規範，規劃如圖 1.2.1 所示之通訊網路架構為基礎之
CAN BUS 通訊網路上，每個 CAN 訊號之通訊協定，並且依照 SAE J1939/71 標準，制定應用層格
式資料之訊號通訊管理表。 
 
 
 
圖 1.2.1 通訊網路架構 
 
接著建構 CAN Bus 收發模組，並進行各收發模組間資料的傳輸驗證。圖 1.2.2 為本研究所發展
之 CAN Bus 收發模組架構圖，左邊是最基本的架構，由 89C51 單晶片、Philips 公司的 SJA1000 
CAN 控制晶片及 PCA82C250 CAN 收發晶片所構成，也是本研究第一年所要建構的系統及技
術。右邊的架構則是由 Atmel 公司的 T89C51CC02 單晶片及 PCA82C250 CAN 收發晶片所構成，
其中 T89C51CC02 晶片基本上是將 CAN 控制晶片整合至 89C51 的單晶片，因此其體積更小，且
構造更為簡單，這是本研究在第二年期所要發展的架構，並作為整合至本智慧型電動車各次系
統的基礎。目前已完成 CAN Bus 通訊收發模組之電路設計與製作，並已完成測試平台之功能測
試，此一實驗平台由三個模組所構成，測試驗證的過程如下，由 A 模組感測開關 ON 後，將訊
號藉由 CAN Bus 傳至 B 模組，由 B 模組控制馬達 ON 並計算轉速值在傳至 C 模組，C 模組將轉
速值由七段顯示器顯示後，在將一個訊號傳至 A 模組以點亮某個 LED。完成之 CAN Bus 通訊收
發模組在麵包板上之電路之後，圖 1.2.3 為 CAN Bus 通訊實驗平台之兩個收發模組，圖 1.2.4 為
 用條件。以馬達驅動引擎，在適當轉速下產生壓縮熱再導入燃氣以幫助燃燒。以火星塞式壓力計量
測汽缸內部壓力，藉壓力-體積關係計算輸出功率及了解壓力變化。研究過程中導入 EGR，以閥門
調整 EGR 導入引擎的比例，並藉由 EGR 溫度來加熱進入到汽缸內的混合氣，使其在壓縮行程時能
達到自燃溫度。排放之氣體將導入廢氣分析儀內以量測各污染物數值。引擎測試架構與實驗平台如
圖 1.3.1 與圖 1.3.2 所示。 
 
本年度目前之成果為，由於所選用的試驗引擎壓縮比偏低，進氣溫度雖經加熱至 350℃，仍無法順
利讓燃燒持續，但由汽缸壓力變化情形，已可觀察到壓縮點火之現象-最高壓力大於未燃燒時之氣

2.1 車體結構建模與分析：本計畫將原 UV 之內燃機引擎以複合動力系統取代，並加入各項創新模

 3.綠色與節能空調系統 
本子計畫主要進行綠能車廂散熱模組開發與模組化電動汽車空調系統開發兩項工作，詳細研究方法
如下所述： 
3.1 綠能車廂散熱模組之開發：本項工作之目標在於發展一種裝置於車頂的車廂散熱模組，以便於
高日曬量之環境下，不需要藉由啟動冷氣，只須利用太陽能電池，或者車廂內外溫度梯度能源，驅
動史特靈引擎，就可以使車廂內溫度在日曬下控制在 35oC 以下，以便使冷氣需求可以降至最低。
本項工作主要利用 CFD 分析工具，配合實驗數據進行散熱系統設計與效能評估。 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
(a)車頂通風管道與外型之設計：本項工作主要利用計算流體力學套裝軟體(Computational Fluid 
Dynamics, CFD)透過數值模擬方法進行之，外型設計的重點主要在於減少車頂通風管道與外型對車
子行駛升阻力係數的影響，通風管道設計的重點則主要在於減少氣流流經其間有最少的壓力損失。
本年度先以 Matiz 為研究對象，最後再拓展至光陽 UV。研究之初，首先探討加裝散熱模組裝置對
車子行駛升阻力係數的影響，首先量測實際 Matiz 外型與尺寸，再使用 Gambit 繪出轎車外型及建
造網格，最後使用 Fluent 進行模擬與分析。另一方面，通風管道流場，同樣先使用 Gambit 繪出其
管道設計以及完成其網格的建造，之後也是透過 Fluent 進行模擬與分析。根據模擬分析結果，最
佳化的車廂散熱模組之內部通風管道與外型如圖 3.1.2 所示。該通風管道熱空氣入口端連接車頂內
部，容許車廂內熱空氣流入管道中，在管道的另一端則安裝兩個抽風風扇，以提供動力將熱空氣經
由管道從車廂內排放到外界。 
 
(b)散熱驅動來源之評估與設計：經實驗研究發現，史特靈引擎體型略為巨大且單價過高，同時不
適合長期遭受震動，否則活塞定位非常容易造成偏移，造成活塞裝置內的摩擦力增加，史特靈引擎
效能降低，不利應用於本系統之抽風散熱。如圖 3.1.3 所示，本試驗在未遭受晃動之前與之後，在
不同溫差的驅動下所產生的旋轉速度。明顯可見，經過晃動後的史特靈引擎若安裝在汽車上，極有
可能因為汽車行駛時無法避免車體晃動而導致史特靈引擎效率嚴重變差。因此，本計畫擬以光電機
制(太陽能晶片)取代史特靈引擎作為驅動抽風風扇之動力源。 
太陽能薄膜晶片一般常見的選擇性不少，分成單晶、多晶跟非晶。根據產業界資料顯示，單晶
的價格每瓦約 240 元，多晶每瓦約 180 元，而非晶每瓦約 160 元，但這都非絕對值，因為隨著整塊
太陽能板瓦數的不同，單位價格也都不一樣。

National Pingtung University of Science and Technology13456, National Taiwan Normal University2

Abstract-This paper presents a dynamic simulation model for 
a hybrid electric scooter (HES) designed by NPUST in Taiwan. 
The HES is built in a parallel hybrid configuration with a 24V 
370W auxiliary power electric motor, a 24V 600W generator, a 
24V15AH battery, and an electronically controlled fuel injection 
internal combustion engine (ICE). Because the system is complex 
and nonlinear in nature, the simulation model utilizes 
mathematical models in tandem with accumulated experimental 
data. The methods used to construct the model and to prepare 
experimental data are fully described. Efforts are made to 
accurately predict key system parameters including engine speed, 
velocity, CVT gear ratio, battery state of charge (SOC), and fuel 
consumption rates during an ECE-40 driving cycle test. The 
efficacy of the model was verified experimentally with a scooter 
chassis dynamometer; parameters predicted by the simulation 
model are seen to be in excellent agreement with those of 
experiments. 
 
I. INTRODUCTION 
Hybrid vehicle technologies have been the subject of 
extensive research interest in recent years, and are now being 
integrated into scooters in the interests of improving fuel 
economy while reducing exhaust emissions and noise pollution. 
A hybrid electric scooter (HES) has been designed by NPUST 
in Taiwan. Because it comprises two power sources, the HES 
requires an energy management system (EMS) for optimal 
power distribution under various driving conditions. In the 
fabrication of an HES, both the design and parametric tune-up 
of the EMS are crucial. Determining whether or not the results 
of the tune-up are satisfactory is an involved process; currently, 
the only verification procedures available require a series of 
time consuming tests entailing substantial capital costs. 
Numerical simulation may provide a suitable alternative to 
hardware tests during this verification procedure. To this end, a 
dynamic simulation model for the HES is developed here. 
Efforts are made to accurately predict key system parameters 
including engine speed, continuous variable transmission (CVT) 
gear ratio, battery state of charge (SOC), motor current and fuel 
consumption rate on the ECE-40 drive cycle test. This enables 
an evaluation of the performance of a tuned HES system. 
Several studies have been carried out with a focus on 
modeling and analyzing the subsystems of scooters. However, 
an overall vehicle dynamic model for the HES studied here is 
rarely presented in the literature. The work of Lin [1] 
introduced a fuel cell scooter design concept and developed a 
computer simulation to examine the overall vehicle design. 
However, the internal combustion engine (ICE) and CVT 
components were not included in this system. With regard to 
scooter subsystems, Carbone et al. [2] presented an 
experimental study of V-belt CVT dynamics and Francoa et al. 
[3] proposed a real-time engine brake torque estimation model 
using the instantaneous measured engine speed as the model 
input. These two studies rely wholly on sophisticated 
mathematical models operating under a number of specific 
parameters, and are difficult to apply in the simulation of an 
overall vehicle. We have previously presented a simulation 
model [8] for urban scooters with a mechanical-type CVT. 
However, scooters incorporating hybrid power systems have 
not yet been considered; in light of this, the present study is 
focused on HES modeling. 
The hybrid electric scooter used in this study is comprised of 
an electronically controlled fuel injection ICE, a mechanical-
type CVT, a clutch, an electric motor, a generator, final drive 
gears, and a battery module. In the interests of simplification 
and a reduction in computational overhead, mathematical 
models and look-up tables are employed in the construction of 
a hybrid dynamic simulation model. The methods utilized to 
construct this simulation model and prepare experimental data 
are described, and a PID based driving model is derived for 
control of the scooter model within energy management design 
specifications. Experiments are also carried out to verify the 
effectiveness of the model using a chassis dynamometer under 
ECE-40 drive cycle conditions.

By using the simulation model derived above and outlined in

Rch=Rdch=0.039Ω, ηbat= 0.96 for charging, and ηbat= 0.96 for

In the HES system, the generator is designed to charge the 
battery when the engine speed exceeds 3000 rpm, with an 
amount of current mediated by the EMS. Under EMS 
command, the electric motor provides auxiliary power to the 
scooter over a low velocity range, as can be seen from the 
discharge currents provided in Fig.9(f). Based on the energy 
management policy, the power of the scooter is solely provided 
by the electric motor in the first mode of the drive cycles. 
Fig.9(c) can be used to ensure that the CVT gear ratio retains 
desired initial conditions for both simulation and experiments. 
The battery voltage and SOC are affected by charge/discharge 
currents in the generator and motor, as can be seen in Fig.9(f)-
(h). It is clearly observed that all the parameters in the ECE-40 
drive cycles estimated by the simulation model are well in 
agreement with those of experiments. This suggests that the 
proposed dynamic simulation model is capable of accurately 
reproducing the characteristics of actual behavior in HES 
systems.  
Such a model is important in the design stage of an EMS 
strategy for the HES, as simulated fuel consumption rates, 
CVT gear ratios, engine speeds, and battery SOC values can be 
used to evaluate the performance of an EMS strategy 
numerically without the expense of fabrication and testing. 
 
V. CONCLUSION 
This paper presents an idea for constructing a dynamic 
simulation model for hybrid electric scooters on drive cycles. 
The method employed makes joint use of mathematical models 
and accumulated experimental data to simplify the resulting 
model. Efforts are made to accurately estimate key system 
parameters such as the fuel consumption rate, CVT gear ratio, 
engine speed, and battery SOC of the HES on ECE-40 drive 
cycles to evaluate the performance of an EMS strategy 
numerically. By comparison with experimental data, it was 
shown that the proposed simulation model can accurately 
characterize the actual behavior of the HES system.

In the paper presented by Dr. Bea Maes/ Greet Lambrechts, Katholieke Universiteit 
Leuven, Belgium, on the topic “Staff reactions to challenging behaviour: An observation 
study Prof.” concluded as following: 
 
Previous research has identified the need for more studies about staff reactions in 
naturalistic settings that reflect the client’s typical environment and involve caregivers with 
whom the client is familiar.  Therefore, the main goal of this study was to assess staff 
reactions to challenging behaviour in naturalistic situations. 
 
We analysed video recordings of the reactions of ten staff members towards challenging 
behaviour of clients with severe or profound intellectual disabilities who displayed 
self-injurious behaviour, stereotyped behaviour and/or aggressive/destructive behaviour.  
The main hypotheses of the study were that staff mostly use verbal strategies in response to 
challenging behaviour and that they often are inclined to immediately stop the behaviour.  
Furthermore, we examined if behaviours as attention, offering or taking away materials or 
tasks, and giving orders occur and to what extent they occur.  Finally, by means of sequential 
analyses, we studied the sequential succession of challenging behaviour and staff reactions, 
while making also a distinction between the three types of challenging behaviour. 
 
To analyse the video recordings, we developed a coding system by means of a pilot case 
and used event counting during continuous recording and recorded each discrete occurrence 
of staff behaviour and client’s challenging behaviour.  For the sequential associations, 
logistic multilevel analysis was used.

How could this film catch and retain her attention? 
 
As Ephi proved capable of looking so intensely at those films, her previous lack of 
attention was not caused only by impairement, but maybe it was principally a matter of will.  
So I was led to the assumption that: 
- In her mind, previously, looking intensely was too difficult 
- But what she could see in these films produced an irresistible desire to look and that 
overcame her fear. 
 
The film may have fulfilled these dual requirements: providing an irresistible desire to 
look, and easing the effort needed for attention. 
 
Bringing an irresistible desire to look 
She must have recognized herself in the film, which was not a foregone conclusion.  
The film selected all her most familiar situations, with plenty of landmarks (places, objects, 
people), do , what she could see on the screen could match situations stored in her memory, 
and she was liable to recognize herself as the main character of the scene. 
 
Working as a ‘replay’ of desires and pleasures she had felt before, the film became itself 
and object of desire and a source of pleasure.  Also, her curiosity may have been an 
‘attention trigger’. 
 
The film included abundant marks of her desires and pleasures.  For that it was 
necessary to know exactly what she liked, and how it was possible to create the corresponding 
situations to film them.

In the paper presented by Dr. Jill Porter, University of Bath, England, on the topic “Using 
mathematical awareness to inform simple actions and problem solving” concluded as 
following: 
 
Studies with typically developing infants suggest that quantity is an important property 
that can inform anticipation, prediction and more sophisticated problem solving (e.g. Straus & 
Curtis 1984; Starkey 1992; Brannon et al 2004; McCrink & Wynn 2004; Feigenson et al 2004; 
Wynn et al 2005).  However these studies are largely carried out in experimental situations 
with the researcher a manipulator of events.  The paper takes date from three children who 
were selected by teachers in their special school as amongst the most difficult to provide 
appropriate number learning opportunities because of their profound and multiple learning 
difficulties.  
 
Drawing on the research with typically developing children, context were developed that 
promoted awareness of number through requiring it to inform simple actions and problem 
solving behaviour.  Rather than developing standard contexts, we had to tune into situations 
which were motivating to the individual child and which led to sustained attention.  We also 
had to look at the repertoire of responses that each child had at their disposal and to prompt 
these responses to a situation where number was relevant and meaningful.  This paper 
reflects on this earlier work in the light research.  It presents for consideration the 
implications for future research and practice with pupils with profound learning difficulties. 
 
In the paper presented by Dr. Jean Ware, Bangor University, Wales, on the topic “Thinking 
skills for pupils with PMLD” concluded as following: 
 
In Wales, as in England, the National Curriculum is seen as an entitlement for all pupils, 
thus for teachers of pupils with PMD there is a potential tension between appropriateness and 
inclusion.  Education is a devolved area in Wales and although the Curriculum in Wales is 
very similar to that in England, Wales sets its own priorities, and has given a particular 
emphasis to pupils with PMLD, although there is also a strong inclusion agenda. 
 
Between 2001 and 2006, in response to a need expressed by teachers for an assessment 
tool suitable for pupils with PMD an assessment specifically for this group was developed.  
It was published in 2006 by The Welsh Assembly Government as ‘Routes for Learning’ At 
about the same time, the emphasis in the curriculum shifted towards a greater emphasis on 
skills as somewhat away from academic subjects.  The Skills Framework outlining four 
essential skill areas (Thinking, ICT, Number and Communication) seemed to provide an 
approach which was more suited to learners with PMD, and so potentially provided an 
opportunity to at least partially resolve the tension between appropriateness and inclusion.

In the paper presented by Dr. Krysti DeZonia, University of California, USA, on the 
topic “Research related to family and teacher perspectives on adult life for PMD” 
concluded as following: 
 
This study examines parent, teacher, and institutional formulations of adulthood for 
students, aged 16-22 years, with profound developmental disabilities.  Using data collected 
from interviews with eight parent and teacher pairs (16 participants), special education 
artifacts, and community resources, this study uncovers the similarities and differences in 
parent and teacher thinking regarding a student’s adult life and the factors that influence the 
content of student Individual Education Plans (IEPs) and Individual Transition Plans (ITPs).  
These factors include community and institutional perceptions of adulthood; the role of 
context in thinking and decision making; the influence of social and cultural expectations; and 
the disparity in the mental models of parents, institutions, and teachers. 
 
The study reveals that parents, teachers, and institutions have different perspectives 
regarding the adult lives of individuals with profound disabilities.  Parent thinking is focused 
on securing a fulfilling and nurturing future for their child and teacher thinking on active 
participation in a range of activities in institutional settings, while institutional formulations of 
adulthood revolve around work and independence.  Parents are better able to envision an 
adult life for their child when provided life planning support. 
 
Teachers are more concerned with disability label than parents, and are more focused on 
the role of institutional settings in a student’s adult life.  IEP and ITP goals are more 
consistent with teacher perspectives regarding a student’s adult future than with parent or 
institutional perspectives.  These disparities in conceptualization may account for the poor 
adult outcomes that research has documented for individuals with PMD.  Context emerged 
as one of the most significant factors that influenced parent and teacher thinking regarding a 
child/student’s life after graduation.  This study supports the critical need for establishing 
model transition programs and providing education to parents and teachers regarding the 
options available to individuals with profound disabilities when they reach adulthood.  
Implications for parents, teachers, and institutions are reviewed and recommendations for 
further research are provided. 
 
During the presentation, parents are informed of a new model of family support that has 
been developed as a result of this study.  This includes assistance with developing a Life 
Quality Plan for their children and the establishment of an online training program for Special 
Needs Life.

external magnetic field is applied at the first, third, and fourth quadrant, the fluid actually 
experience less resistance to flow through the region without external magnetic field.
