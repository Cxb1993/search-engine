The volatile copper(II) complex CuII(OCHMeCH2NEt2)2 was synthesized. By the use of this 
CuII(OCHMeCH2NEt2)2 as a MOCVD precursor, highly conductive, conformal, continuous, dense,

1.1 深次微米元件金屬導線的需求 
金屬化(metallization)是將構築在矽晶片上之電子元件，以金屬導線相連接而形成完整電
路(circuit)之製程
【1】
。隨著元件尺寸不斷縮小、運算速度不斷提升及製程技術快速的發展，製
程技術由大型積體電路(Very-Large Scale Integration, VLSI)邁入超大型積體電路(Ultra-Large 
Scale Integration, ULSI )的世代，而這使得金屬化成為一個非常重要的關鍵技術。 
當導線的線寬變小，導線截面積將因此縮小，造成通過之電流密度大幅提高(109A/cm2)，
電子流之運動使得導線金屬本身原子沿著電子流之推力方向，而於晶界(grain boundary)移動
造成所謂的電子遷移(electromigration)現象，隨著電流密度大增，電子遷移效應將持續嚴重，
甚至將導致金屬導線的斷線。 
除了電子遷移效應外，在縮小法則下，電晶體閘極長度的縮減是有助於速度的提升與元
件功能的改善。但導線之 RC(即電阻與電容的乘積)延遲時間的增大(如圖 1-1)卻可能將元件縮
小所得到功能改善的效果抵消甚至變差。因為電路中信號傳遞的快慢，是受到電阻(R)與電容
(C)的乘積所左右，RC 乘積越大，速度就越慢；反之 RC 乘積越小，信號傳遞速度就越快。

1.2 金屬導線材料的選擇 
隨著半導體製造技術的演進成長，元件傳輸速度也隨之大幅增加。在晶片單位面積上的
電子元件密度增加。在自然界中適合做金屬導線材料之金屬有鋁(Al)、銅(Cu)、銀(Ag)、金(Au)
等四種，而其各種性質則列於表 1-1。 
由於金(Au)本身具有較低的電阻值及良好的抗腐蝕性與抗電子遷移性，因此在早期的 IC
工業上金金屬導線材料亦曾被使用。然而，其缺點為低溫下易與矽金屬形成一復合
(recombination)中心，且不易進行乾蝕刻處理。至於銀(Ag)金屬雖可提供更低的電阻值(1.59 
μΩ-cm)，但在一般的環境下極容易被腐蝕，因此也被排除於 IC 工業的金屬導線材料應用之
外【1】。 
金屬鋁(Al)是積體電路中一直使用的導線材料，因為技術成熟、附著力強、易蝕刻、且
與 N+/P+易融合成極佳之歐姆接觸(ohmic contact) 【3】。但是矽在鋁中有一定之固態溶解度(solid 
solubility)，使得在多層金屬內連線(multilevel interconnect)的鋁與矽接面(junction)上產生尖峰
(spiking)現象，增加了接面的漏電流(leakage current)；鋁金屬內連接線另一重要問題是電子遷
移(electromigration)現象的發生，此現象是當鋁導線處於傳導電流的狀態時，鋁原子受電場的
影響將沿著晶粒邊界(grain boundary)隨著電子的流動而移動，而若此現象過於劇烈時，將導
致鋁金屬線的斷路(open)。在以往鋁製程中，通常都加入 TiN/Ti 或 TiW 所構成之擴散阻障層
(diffusion barrier)以及採用鋁銅矽金屬導線來克服尖峰與電子遷移現象。但是在 0.15µm 及以
下元件，鋁的電阻值與抗電子遷移能力及 TiN 的障壁能力皆己不符合要求。於是，尋找一個
低電阻值(resistivity)、高抗電子遷移能力(electromigration resistance)之金屬導線材料是相當重
要的。

3.1 銅金屬薄膜的成長方法 
金屬薄膜的製備方法一般可分為三種：第一種為物理氣相沈積(physical vapor deposition, 
PVD)，其原理為在蒸鍍室裡，對被蒸鍍物提供不同形式之能源，如高溫加熱或是離子撞擊，
藉以增加被蒸鍍物的蒸氣壓，並將其所產生的氣態粒子沈積在基材表面上，製成薄膜。依據
所使用的能源形式不同，物理氣相沈積法(PVD)又可大略分為真空蒸鍍(vacuum evaporation)
及濺鍍(sputtering)兩種。第二種為電鍍法(electroplating)，電鍍法又可分為電鍍(electrolytic)和
無電極電鍍(electroless)。電鍍為一種電沈積過程(electro-deposition process)，是利用電極
(electrode)通過電流，使金屬附著於物體表面上， 其目的是在改變物體表面之特性或尺寸。
而無電鍍不需外加電流，即可在被鍍物上析鍍出所需的金屬鍍層。在無電鍍中，主要是利用
鍍液中添加的還原劑進行氧化還原反應，使溶液中的金屬還原成金屬。而第三種製備薄膜的
方法為化學氣相沈積(chemical vapor deposition, CVD)，化學氣相沈積是藉由氣態之分子化合
物(precursor)經由吸附、擴散、化學反應及揮發性產物脫附等步驟來沈積薄膜於基材表面上。
圖3-1為化學氣相沈積原理之示意圖【8】。其原理程序大致說明如下： 
(1) 運送先驅物到基材。 
(2) 吸附先驅物於基材表面上。 
(3) 先驅物在基材表面上發生化學反應並產生鍵結。 
(4) 反應物、反應中間物及產物在基材表面上擴散(diffusion)。 
(5) 基材表面上反應物的長晶(nucleation)及薄膜成長。 
(6) 金屬中心旁的官能基以有機分子及副產物形式從基材表面脫附。 
(7) 將官能基和副產物有機分子傳送出反應器。

3.3 銅先驅物之文獻回顧 
在銅薄膜之化學氣相沈積中，選擇一個合適的有機銅金屬化合物為先驅物是
化學氣相沈積的首要之務。可以被選擇做為銅薄膜之化學氣相沈積的先驅物，應
具有下列特性：(1)高揮發度(volatility)及高蒸氣壓；(2)好的熱穩定性，銅先驅物不
會在揮發成蒸氣及傳送至銅薄膜沈積腔體過程中，發生裂解；(3)低的分解溫度，
亦即銅薄膜化學氣相沈積的化學反應，必須在基材溫度不高的情況下發生，促使
銅先驅物之官能基，在低溫下可以裂解脫離化合物的銅中心原子，脫附出基材表
面，因而可以得到不含碳和氧等雜質的銅薄膜；若是發生化合物裂解所需的溫度
太高，可能會破壞基材本身的性質；(4)安全性的考量，此銅先驅物必須沒有毒性，
容易處理，在空氣中可以穩定儲存，不會反應或分解掉。由於目前已知的銅先驅
物，沒有完全符合上述這些特性，因此目前銅膜製程研發的重要課題之ㄧ，便是
利用各種化學理論知識，重新設計合成新的銅先驅物；除此之外，從之前已有的
先驅物改善製程方式更是具有經濟和時效性的價值。目前，銅的化學氣相沈積一
般是採用本身為有機金屬化合物的先驅物(precursor)作為沈積銅膜的氣體源。而銅
先驅物之發展主要可分為兩類： 
(1) 銅正一價(Cu1+)化合物的化學氣相先驅物： 
銅正一價的先驅物大致可分為七大類，如圖3-3【18】所列。其通式分別為： (1)

[(OR)CuL]n；(6) (β-ketonate)CuL2及(7) ClCuL2。而目前最常用的銅正一價先驅物為
第二類和第六類。其一般通式可表示為(β-diketonate)CuLn。其中Ln為一有機團，大
多為路易士鹼(Lewis base)，包括PMe3【19-21】，烯類取代基的1,5-COD【22-25】，
VTMS (trimethyl- silane)【26-30】，和炔類(Alkynes)取代基的2-butyne【31-33】等；
β-diketonate通常為hfac (hexafluoroacetylacetonate)，tfac (trifluoropentanedione)，acac 
(acetyl-acetonate)等取代基，這些取代基上，各有數量不同的氟原子。根據
 15
到純的銅膜，否則銅膜所含的雜質多，相較於銅正一價先驅物的不穩定性，銅正
二價化合物在空氣中相當穩定，可以保存較長久的時間，而不必擔心暴露在空氣
中，會隨時分解的問題，樣品處理起來，就容易許多。另一個不同的是，先驅物
熱分解生成銅膜的沈積溫度，銅正二價較銅正一價化合物高出一些。這乃是因為
化合物本身結構很穩定，則相對的，此化合物要分解時，便須獲得多一些的能量。
這是銅正二價先驅物較不被半導體產業青睞的原因之ㄧ。另一項銅正二價先驅物
的缺點，乃是這類化合物蒸氣壓太低，而至其汽化所需的溫度相當高。如Cu(acac)2
的汽化溫度為180 ~ 200℃，而Cu(dmp)2為220℃，含氟原子的Cu(hfac)2則為97 ~

溫度可以知道先驅物至少必須在此溫度(185℃)以上，才會發生進行熱裂解反應，進而裂解成金

1. Introduction 
Chemical vapor deposition of copper has been extensively studied for interconnect metals of 
ultra large scale integration (ULSI) circuits. Copper exhibits a lower resistivity (1.67 μΩ-cm) than 
aluminum which has been the choice for the interconnection metal. Low resistivity of interconnect 
metal can yield significant reduction in (resistance x capacitance), or RC time delay [1]. For Cu 
metallization in ULSI processing, electroplating is the cost-effective technique for the deposition of Cu 
damascene structures. However, this technique requires a thin Cu seed layer before electroplating of a 
thicker Cu layer can be started. Chemical vapor deposition (CVD) has now been seriously considered 
as the best replacement for sputtering in the deposition of seed layers due to its superior capability in 
facilitating deposition of thin conformal Cu layers in small trenches/vias. 
Precursors for Cu-MOCVD can be categorized into two groups, i.e. Cu(I) and Cu(II) compounds. 
Cu(II) compounds such as CuII(β-diketonate)2 were initially used as a source material, which include 
Cu(acac)2 (acac = 2,4-pentanedione), Cu(hfac)2 (hfac = 1,1,1,-5,5,5,-hexafluoro-2,4-pentanedione), 
Cu(fod)2 (fod = 6,6,7,7,8,8-heptaflouro-2,2-dimethyl-3,5-octane-dionate), and Cu (dmp)2 (dmp = 
2,2,6,6-tetramethyl-3,5- heptanedione)[2-4]. In the Cu-MOCVD process with Cu2+(β-diketonate)2 
precurors, it is necessary to use an external reducing agent such as H2 gases to get a good quality thin 
film of copper metal without significant carbon and oxygen contamination. A new volatilizable 
precursor, CuII(amino-alkoxide)2 compounds such as Cu(OCH2CH2NMe2)2, has been successfully 
synthesized by Goel et al.[5]. Their results show that the amino-alkoxide ligand in this compound 
serves as a reducing agent, releasing copper metal at temperature as high as 300 ºC. Young et al.[6] 
indicated that the generation of copper metal is accompanied by the development of an aldehyde 
species, namely (2-dimethylamino)acetaldehyde and one equivalent of (2-2-dimethylamino) ethanol. 
Becker et al.[7] have pointed out that copper films deposited at 230 and 260 ºC consisted solely of 
metallic copper with no detectable contaminants. Furthermore, copper films obtained from

procedure reported by Goel et. al.[8]. Starting materials not available form commercial sources were 
synthesized according to the literature, i.e. Cu(OMe)2[9]. FTIR (Bio-Rad, FTS-3500) was used to 
identify the function groups and purity of synthesized CuII(OCHMeCH2NEt2)2. Sublimation studies 
were accomplished on a Dynamic TGA-2950 instrument in a nitrogen (99.99%) atmosphere under 
ambient pressure.  
TaN layer was firstly prepared on (100) p-type silicon wafer by rf-sputtering, which were 
deposited under sputtering with an energy output of 250 W for 15 min accompanying inflows of argon 
and nitrogen gases. A horizontal, low-pressure, cold-wall reactor was used for the MOCVD 
experiments. In general, for each MOCVD experiment, 1.5 g CuII(OCHMeCH2NEt2)2 was loaded in to 
the bubbler in an argon glove box. The TaN substrate was mounted on the base of deposition chamber. 
Afterwards, the bubbler was attached to the MOCVD apparatus and the whole system was evacuated 
(less than 10-3 torr) and heated (200~240 ºC). A stream of nitrogen gas (99.9999%, 10 sccm) was 
passed through the system, and the air bath was adjusted to the vaporizing temperature (60 ºC) approx. 
for 3 to 5 min to stabilize the partial pressure of precursors. The deposition was then initiated by 
opening the bubbler valves and closing the by-pass valve. The deposition pressure of the system was 
kept constant at 0.1 torr. After designated time (0.5-10 min) was reached, the deposition process was 
stopped and the substrates were slowly cooled down to room temperature by ambient environment. 
XPS experiments were conducted with a Thermo VG Scientific Theta Probe X-ray photoelectron 
spectrometer equipped with a monochromatized Al Kα line 1486.6 eV. The crystalline structures of 
copper films were characterized using a Rigaku RTP 300 X-ray diffractometer (XRD). The film 
resistivity was calculated from the sheet resistance measured by a Napson RT-7 four-point probe and 
the thickness measured by cross-sectional SEM(FESEM, JOEL JSM-6500F), which was also used for 
examining the surface morphology of deposited copper on trench substrates.

Fig. 4 illustrates the XPS spectra of the core levels of Cu 2p (Fig 4a), Cu LMM (Fig. 4b), and C 
1s (Fig. 4c) in the Cu films deposited at different temperatures. The binding energies of the core levels 
of the Cu 2p and Cu LMM in the Cu films were consistent with the data found in the work by Kirsch 
and Ekerdt[15]. Fig. 4a confirms the existence of Cu and Cu1+ species in the deposited film, since both 
of them have the same characteristics peaks of Cu 2p3/2 and Cu 2p1/2 binding states locating at 932.7 
and 952.5 eV[15]. Besides, Cu+2 species were supposed to be absent in the deposited film, because no 
Cu+2 satellite peaks around 942.2 and 962.0 eV in Cu 2p core levels was observed[15]. Due to the 
higher sensitivity in the variation of X-ray-induced Cu LMM Auger spectrum, Cu LMM Auger peak 
can be used to identify the the distinction of the chemical states between Cu and Cu1+ species. The 
peak of elemental Cu and Cu2O should be loacted at 568.2 and 570.1 eV, respectively[15]. As shown 
in Fig. 4b, none of the Cu LMM Auger spectra shows any Cu1+ peak at 570.1 eV, indicating that the 
deposited films were composed of only metallic copper. This is consistent with the XRD result in 
Figure 3. Fig. 4c shows the C 1S spectra of deposited films at various temperatures and no distinct 
peak was observed. It is supposed that the carbon contaminant in the Cu films is below detection.  
Fig. 5 depicts SEM micrographs of the Cu films deposited for 5 min at various temperatures. The 
grain size of Cu films was measured by obtaining the surface area of fifty Cu grains with SigmaScan 
Pro image analysis software (V5.0, SPSS Inc.). At 200 ºC (Fig. 5a), the surface morphology of the Cu 
film was smooth and continuous.  The average size of Cu grain formed at the deposition temperature 
of 200 ºC is 102.76 nm, while it was increased to 145.72 nm at 220 ºC. Surface morphology of the Cu 
films significantly changed as the deposition temperature was raised from 200 to 220 ºC. A marked 
increase in the grain size (202.84 nm) was observed when the deposition temperature was up to 240 ºC. 
The difference in the surface morphology for three temperatures is due to the fact that high deposition

supposed to have better quality than those deposited at other conditions. Therefore, the following 
MOCVD experiment with a trench TaN substrate was intentionally conducted under this optimum 
condition. 
 
3.3. Cu films on a trench TaN substrate 
    Fig. 9 is a cross-sectional SEM micrograph of Cu film deposited on a trench TaN substrate at 200 
ºC with a deposition time of 5 min. As shown in Fig. 9, the as-deposited Cu film was smooth, 
continuous, and conformal. The thickness of the film was uniform in the field, near the top, along the 
sidewall, and at the bottom of the trench. For electroplating, a smooth seed layer is highly desired, 
especially within trenches/vias. Both SEM and AFM micrographs (Figs. 5, 6, and 9) have evidently 
indicated that copper films produced by MOCVD with CuII(OCHMeCH2NEt2)2 as a precursor can be 
used an ideal seed layer for electroplating in Cu metallization.  
 
4. Conclusion 
   The feasibility of using the MOCVD with CuII(OCHMeCH2NEt2)2 as a self-reductive precursor to 
achieve smooth, continuous, and conformal copper seed layers have been demonstrated. The precursor 
was successfully self-synthesized in this work, which was proved to deposit metallic Cu film without 
an external reducing agent. Furthermore, the XRD spectra indicated that the deposited Cu films have 
an fcc structure with (111) preferred orientation. The surface morphology of Cu films observed by 
SEM and AFM revealed that a smooth and continuous Cu film could be produced on a TaN substrate 
at 200 ºC. The resistivity of the Cu film deposited at 200 ºC with the deposition time of 5 min was as 
low as 2.58 μΩ-cm, which is slightly higher than that of bulk copper metal. The experimental result of 
MOCVD with a trench TaN substrate implies that this technique is able to manufacture the copper

2、研究方法 
本研究採用 p-type <l00>晶向之矽晶片(Si 
wafer)，並且以濺鍍法(RF-sputter)在矽晶片上
成長氮化鉭 TaNx 薄膜做為基材。銅先驅物使
用自行合成之(hfac)CuI(COD)。所使用之系統
為一自行設計組裝的低壓冷壁式有機金屬化
學氣相沉積系統。將銅先驅物放入玻璃蒸發器
儲存瓶內，並且將銅先驅物儲存瓶加熱維持在
設定溫度。以超高氮(N2)為載氣，質量流量控
制器(MFC)控制氣體流量為 10sccm，沉積壓力
控制在 0.2 torr，而滯留時間為 0.86 秒。改變
不同沉積溫度及先驅物蒸發器溫度利用有機
金屬化學氣相沉積方式於 TaNx/Si 基材上成長
銅薄膜。 
 
3、結果與討論 
本研究以 TaN0.82 薄膜為基材，探討沉積溫
度及先驅物(hfac)CuI(COD)分壓對沉積速率的
影響。首先我們探討沉積溫度對沉積速率之效
應，在此部分將先驅物蒸發器溫度固定在
70°C，改變沉積溫度為 120～300°C，沉積時
間為 6～12 分鐘。Fig.1 是利用 FESEM 對銅
膜橫截面所拍攝的圖，由此本實驗在不同沉積
時間下所得到的銅膜膜厚度可以得知。Fig.2
為在不同沉積溫度，銅薄膜厚度與沉積時間之
關係圖，且由此數據可求得在不同沉積溫度時
的銅薄膜沉積速率，將沉積速率取對數後對
1/T 作圖所得之沉積速率對沉積溫度的阿瑞尼
斯圖(Arrhenius plot)如 Fig.3 所示，由圖中發現
到在沉積溫度 190°C 之前沉積速率隨著沉積
溫度改變有明顯的變化，因為表面反應速率對
溫度是非常敏感的，因此沉積速率對溫度依賴
性高，所以在 190°C 及更低溫度時應為表面反
應控制區(surface reaction limited regime)。然而
在較高沉積溫度(大於 190°C)時，由於表面反
應速率已高，沉積過程中的速率決定步驟轉換
為氣相分子運動至待沉積表面的質傳步驟，而
質傳速率對溫度的依賴性低，故沉積速率在溫
度變化時沒有太大的改變，因此當沉積溫度大
於 190°C 時為質傳控制區(mass transfer limited 
regime)。之後利用 Arrhenius equation 計算其
活化能。計算得到的活化能在表面反應控制區
約為 18.32 kJ/mol，而在質傳控制區約為 0.625 
kJ/mol。 
在沉積過程中，實驗發現需經過一段沉積時
間後才會有明顯的薄膜成長(如 Fig.2 所示)。
一般這與所謂的薄膜成長潛伏期有關。潛伏期
的長短將會影響薄膜成長速率。而本研究對潛
伏期的定義是採取將薄膜厚度對沉積時間作
圖，外插至膜厚等於零的時間，此時間即為潛
伏期。影響銅薄膜成長之潛伏期的因素有：反
應先驅物的反應特性、基材表面的特性及沉積
溫度等。Fig.4 為潛伏期與沉積溫度之關係圖，
由圖中可觀察到在沉積溫度 230℃及較低溫度
時，潛伏期皆大約為 3.0 分鐘，潛伏期都非常
短，推測這與銅先驅物(hfac)CuI(COD)具有高
蒸氣壓、好的熱穩定性及低的分解溫度，以及
所沉積的基材 TaN0.82 有較佳的導電性，提供
較多的吸附位置(adsorption sites)，以利所吸附
的先驅物進行自身氧化還原反應，及適當沉積
溫度有著密不可分的關係。然而在 300℃時則
有非常短的潛伏期。

多文獻中得知，先驅物一價銅(hfac)CuIL 中的
L為一僅具些許離子鍵(Cu-C鍵結)特性的中性
根團，而 hfac 卻是一個具有弱酸性根團，相較

裂解，進而使得基材表面必須提供電子，使得
平衡中心金屬離子的正電荷、滿足金屬離子的
配位需求，以增加先驅物的安定性，因此，在
此提出的看法，是以式[4]中先驅物分子吸附於
基材表面的一瞬間，即把弱鍵結力的中性根團
裂解帶走，進而因為電子轉移使得(hfac)CuI更
為穩定，而不是因為先驅物分子先吸附基材表
面再裂解的反應。在熱力學的觀點來看，式[4] 
因為電子轉移使得(hfac)CuI更為穩定，所需的
能量必定比進行自身氧化的不對稱化學反應
低的許多，因此自身氧化還原之不對稱化學反
應(式[5])才是整體反應的速率決定步驟。
