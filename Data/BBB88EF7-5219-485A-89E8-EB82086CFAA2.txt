1.1 膠體分散溶液 
 
本計畫探討懸浮於電解質溶液中的膠體粒子之電動力學現象。膠
體粒子（分散相，disperse phase）的粒徑範圍介於 1 mn （ 910− m）
至 1 mμ （ 610− m）之間，分散於溶劑分子（分散介質，dispersion 
medium）中而形成膠體溶液。膠體溶液是日常生活中相當常見的溶
液系統，舉凡化妝品（ cosmetics）、乳液（ emulsions）、藥物
（pharmaceuticals）、食品（ foodstuffs）、油漆（paints）以及染料
（dyestuffs）等，都是膠體溶液實際應用的範疇[1]。 
 
因分散相與分散介質可分別為固體、液體與氣體等，扣除兩者皆
為氣體的情形，可組成如表 1-1 所示共八種型態的膠體溶液[2]；其
中，溶膠（sols）與乳液（emulsions）是目前最重要、應用最為廣泛
的膠體分散溶液。

面積／體積），而分散相與分散介質間之交界面的特性扮演了決定整

1.4 關於沉降之文獻回顧 
 
    當一群帶電粒子在電解質溶液中受到重力場的驅動而下沉，流體
流經帶電粒子週遭將攜帶反離子自流體的上游處朝流體的下游處移
動，造成在流場下游處有過多的反離子累積，而同時在流場上游處留
下過多的同離子，整體可視為粒子周圍的電雙層發生變形而生成一個
誘發電場（一般又稱為沉降電場、沉降電位梯度或沉降電位）。由於
誘發電場的方向與帶電粒子的運動方向相反，因此將作用相反於其運
動方向的作用力而造成沉降速度下降，此一效應稱為沉降電場效應。

中下沉時所發現，因此後來又被稱為 Dorn 效應[3]。Smoluchowski[43]
針對此現象提出理論的研究，在電雙層厚度很小的情形下，沉降電場

2.7 力積分與電泳動度的計算 
 
    高分子液滴因表面調節而帶負電，於電場中受到電力的作用，高
分子液滴流體與電解質溶液流體皆具有黏度，液滴在溶液中電泳時受
到拖曳力的作用。此二力同時作用於液滴上，初期液滴受電力作用而
產生一加速度，使得液滴的速度發生變化，此時由於流體的黏度產生
一相反於速度方向的拖曳力，當速度成長到使拖曳力與電力相等時，
液滴達擬穩態，此時液滴所受外力之合力為零，液滴之加速度亦為
零，速度達其終端速度（terminal velocity）。以下將分別探討液滴所
受之電力和拖曳力。

微分方程式。本論文的研究以一正交多項式—Chebyshev 多項式—為

A. 就電泳而言：

拖曳力，故卡羅液滴的電泳動度最大，牛頓液滴次中，硬球粒子

因此有較大的電泳動度。

6. 密集度 H (a/b)的影響：無因次電泳動度隨液滴於溶液中之密集度 H

附錄: 本次會議論文 
 
 
Electrophoresis of Charged Polymeric Liquid Droplets 
through a Porous Membrane 
 
 
Chia-Ping Chiang, Wen-Li Cheng, and Eric Lee* 
Department of Chemical Engineering, National Taiwan University, Taipei, Taiwan 106, ROC 
*ericlee@ntu.edu.tw 
 
Abstract—Electrophoresis, the motion of colloidal particles under the influence of an externally applied electric field, has 
been the focus of research interest in colloid science, which has great potential in practical applications. Most of the 
traditional studies have been limited to suspensions of rigid particles, while there are many important applications involving 
liquid droplets, such as the micro-emulsion systems, where the polymeric liquid droplet is non-Newtonian in general. 
Moreover, porous membrane is often encountered in biochemical and biomedical applications. 
Here the electrophoresis of charged polymeric liquid droplets immersed in an electrolyte solution through a porous 
membrane is investigated theoretically. The polymeric fluid inside the droplet is described by the Carreau model as a 
non-Newtonian shear-thinning fluid. Charge regulation of the particle surface is considered as well, in that the actual surface 
potential of the colloid is treated as a function of the concentration of the electrolyte ions surrounding it. 
A pseudo-spectral method based on Chebyshev polynomial is employed to solve the resulted general electrokinetic 
equations. This method has proven to be a powerful and efficient tool in the field of electrokinetic phenomena. The 
electrophoretic mobility, the velocity of the droplets under unit electric field, is calculated for various situations. 
Key parameters such as the double layer thickness surrounding the droplet, the voidage of the porous membrane, and the 
rheological properties of the polymeric fluid inside the droplet are examined to understand their effects on the overall 
electrophoretic phenomena under consideration. 
It is found, among other things, that the electrophoretic mobility is higher than that of the corresponding Newtonian drops in 
general, owing to the shear thinning effect, which has a tendency to decrease the viscosity hence the hydrodynamic drag force 
retarding the drop motion. And the smaller the voidage of the membrane is, the slower the electrophoretic mobility of the 
droplet. 
 
  The system under consideration is illustrated in Fig. 1. 
A nonconducting, spherical, charged polymeric liquid 
droplet of radius a is placed at the center of a spherical 
cavity of radius b, and a uniform electric field E in the z  
 
 
 
direction is applied. The cavity model proposed by 
Zydney[3] is adopted, and the voidage is defined by 
1-(a/b)3. The polymeric liquid droplet comprises an 
incompressible Carreau fluid.
