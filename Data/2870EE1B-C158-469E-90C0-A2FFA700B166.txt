技術/創作名稱 金屬-絕緣層-半導體電晶體式氫氣感測器
發明人/創作人 劉文超、陳慧英、林坤緯
技術說明
中文：本發明是在研製一種高靈敏度之金屬-絕緣層-半導體電晶體
式氫氣感測器。此結構利用有機金屬化學氣相沈積法成長在半絕緣
型砷化鎵基板上，此結構包含 5000A 半導體緩衝層，以及 n型半
導體主動層薄膜，其濃度及厚度分別為 1x1016cm-3及 3000A。在磊晶
之後接著利用真空蒸鍍的技術，於 n型半導體薄膜表面蒸鍍一層金
鍺鎳合金做為電晶體之汲極以及源極。並且在 n型半導體薄膜表面
成長氧化層，並在此氧化層上蒸鍍鉑金屬，作為二極體式感測器之
蕭特基極。由於鉑金屬對氫氣具有良好的觸媒活性，當氫氣分子吸
附於鈀金屬表面時會被解離為氫原子，而大部分氫原子將會擴散穿
透鈀金屬，並於鈀金屬與氧化層薄膜介面造成極化作用形成偶極矩
層，此一偶極矩層將改變鈀金屬與氧化層介面之電場，進而降低了
金屬-半導體蕭特基能障高度。因此，當接觸氫氣後，將改變電流
電壓特性。經由實驗結果顯示，本感測器在含有不同氫氣濃度的空
氣環境中，其順向及反向電流將會隨氫氣含量增加而增加。在室
溫，9090ppm 氫氣濃度中，飽和靈敏度高達 561。此外，在瞬間暫
態反應中，9090ppm 氫氣濃度中反應的時間不到一秒鐘，此即說明
本發明實為一種具有高靈敏度特性的二極體式氫氣感測器。

Semiconductor hydrogen sensors, utilizing
catalytic metals such as Pd and Pt, are
generally fabricated with a thin oxide layer to
suppress the Fermi-level pinning effect
resulting from the high-density surface defects.
In this work, the Pd- and Pt/oxide/InGaP
Schottky diode hydrogen sensors with
high-sensitive hydrogen detection over wide
temperature regimes are studied and
presented.
四、研究方法
The studied Pd-In0.49Ga0.51P and
Pt-InGaP Schottky diodes were grown by a
metal organic chemical vapor deposition
(MOCVD) system on (100) oriented
semi-insulated (S.I.) GaAs substrates. The
epitaxial structure consisted of a 5000Å-thick
GaAs undoped buffer layer and a 3000Å-thick
n-In0.49Ga0.51P (n=21017cm-3) active layer.
Conventional photolithography, vacuum
evaporation, lift-off, and wet etch techniques
were used to form electrode patterns. The
ohmic contacts were deposited on the n-InGaP
active layer by AuGe/Ni metals and alloyed at
450℃ for 1 minute. Then, the mesa etching
process was used to etch the wafer into the
substrate by solutions of HCl : H2O = 1 :1 for
InGaP and NH4OH : H2O2 : H2O = 5 : 3 : 100
for GaAs, respectively. The native oxide on
the wafer was removed by the solution of HF:
H2O=1 : 1. Then, the samples were placed in a
thermal oven with O2 flow at 360℃ for
35minutes to form a thin and fresh oxide layer
on the surface. Finally, the evaporated Pd and
Pt metals produced Schottky contacts. The
Schottky contact area was 8.510-4cm2. For
hydrogen adsorption and desorption
measurement, the devices were placed in a
closed stainless-steal reaction chamber
connecting to a gas flow tube with a
regulating valve. Different concentrations of
hydrogen gas in air were employed in this
study. The chamber was maintained in one
atmosphere condition and the continuous and
stable gas flowing with a 500 sccm flow rate
was introduced into the chamber.

1. Current-Voltage (I-V) characteristics
Figures 1(a) and (b) show the
current–voltage (I-V) characteristics of
studied Pd-InGaP and Pt-InGaP MOS
Schottky diodes measured in air and exposed
to hydrogen contained ambient with hydrogen
concentrations of 48, 937, and 9090ppm
H2/air at different temperature, respectively.
The forward currents of both studied devices
are substantially increased with increasing the
hydrogen concentration and temperature. This
is caused by the decrease of barrier height
resulting from the presence of dipole layer at
metal-oxide interface and reduction of
semiconductor energy-gap. The current
variations of InGaP Schottky diode based on
Pd metal are more sensitivity than those of Pt
metal under low hydrogen concentration (<
937 ppm H2/air) and low operating
temperature (T< 400 K) regimes. On the other
hand, at high operating temperature, the
Pt/InGaP sensor has better detecting properties.
Particularly, at 600K, the current variations of
Pt/InGaP Schottky diode are significantly
higher than those of Pd/InGaP Schottky diode.

六、結論
In summary, the hydrogen sensing
performances of Pd- and Pt-InGaP MOS
Schottky diodes have been systematically
studied and compared under steady-state
condition at different temperature. The
Pd-InGaP Schottky diode exhibits better
current variation and larger change of barrier
height under low hydrogen concentration
ambient. On the contrary, the Pt-InGaP
Schottky diode shows better high-temperature
performances and larger hydrogen detection
regimes. According to the Lamgmuir form of
response and van’t Hoff equation, under
steady-state, the initial heat of adsorption
related to equilibrium constant can be
calculated. The initial heat of adsorption of
Pd- and Pt-InGaP Schottky diodes are 355 and
364.8meV/atom, respectively.
