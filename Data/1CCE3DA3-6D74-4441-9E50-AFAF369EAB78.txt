摘要 
在一般的金屬研磨製程中，研磨界面的溫度會因為研磨界面的磨擦現象及刮除現象而昇
高，這種現象會影響到金屬工件本身的溫度及尺寸，進而對研磨結果產生影響。本研究將由理
論模式的建構與實驗的驗証同時著手來探討金屬研磨製程之研磨溫昇現象。針對金屬研磨製
程的研磨界面溫昇理論模式的建立，將由研磨界面間的界面現象出發，利用微觀的表面接觸力
學來描述金屬研磨製程中研磨界面間的接觸現象，探討研磨顆粒、研磨墊表面粗度與金屬工件
表面材料三者間的交互關係，並依此來建立針對金屬研磨製程且考慮到耗材影響之研磨溫昇理
論模式，最後利用所開發的金屬研磨溫昇理論模式來探討不同耗材使用及操作參數對於研磨界
面溫昇的影響，以得到一具實用性的理論模式。  
關鍵字：金屬研磨、研磨溫昇、界面現象、瞬閃溫度、微觀接觸 
  
1. 前言 
在目前的工業界中，機械製造技術正朝著提高精度與生產率兩個方面迅速發展。在提高生
產率方面，提高自動化程度是各國致力發展的方向，近年來，從C N C數控工具機到電腦整合
製造系統的發展迅速，在特定專業範圍內得到了極佳應用。而提高精度方面，從精密加工演進
到超精密加工，這也是世界各主要工業國家致力發展的方向。其加工精度從微米到次微米，甚
至奈米，其應用範圍日趨廣泛，在各類工業中都有所應用。其中金屬研磨製程因可以製造出表
面粗糙度極佳的被加工表面，且幾乎所有的金屬工件都可以用此方式進行加工，因此乃是目前
超精密加工中最主要的加工方法之一。由於研磨製程不僅要能提供鏡面級的表面粗糙度，還要
保證獲得精確的幾何形狀和尺寸，因此研磨製程的關鍵加工條件是避免振動的研磨運動、潔淨
的環境、高品質的研磨劑及精密的溫度控制，這些條件中，又以精密的溫度控制最為重要。在
一般的平面拋光製程中，研磨界面的溫度會因為研磨界面的磨擦現象及刮除現象而昇高，這種
現象會影響到金屬工件本身的溫度及尺寸，進而對研磨結果產生影響。所以，想要完整瞭解研
磨拋光製程的現象，研磨溫昇效應的探討是不可忽略的，此外，在實際的製程應用上，要對研
磨界面的溫昇機制有充分的瞭解，才有可能對其效應做進一步的控制，以確保研磨製程之品
質。[1-13] 
在金屬研磨的製程中，因為機械磨耗的作用而產生摩擦熱，進而使研磨界面間的溫度升
高，研磨界面間的接觸溫度不只對磨潤性能影響甚巨，對於金屬工件尺寸熱變形的發生亦有很
大的影響。一般而言，研磨界面間溫度的分佈狀況是正向力，研磨滑動速度與研磨界面材料性
質及表面粗糙度的函數[14]，因此現在已有學者針對研磨製程研磨溫昇現象進行探討 [15-24]，

在此一階段的研究中，為了金屬研磨界面溫昇理論模式的推導及方便對所發展模式進行
驗證的工作，整個模型將假設在金屬研磨的製程中，金屬工件表面與研磨墊的表面為兩近乎
平行的表面，且研磨顆粒經由研磨漿的流動，不斷地被帶進研磨界面間，使研磨界面一直保
持浸潤的狀況。研磨墊表面的粗糙峰高度分佈及研磨漿中研磨顆粒的直徑分佈均假設為高斯
分佈，且研磨顆粒的形狀為球體。 
為了能順利推導金屬研磨製程研磨界面溫昇理論模式，本研究先以微觀表面接觸力學來
估算金屬研磨製程進行時，研磨界面間單一有效研磨顆粒上所受的平均力量及研磨顆粒/金屬
工件接觸界面間實際接觸面積，在藉由瞬閃溫度的概念，求得研磨界面間的研磨溫昇。

高的金屬材料進行研磨時，因所生成的摩擦熱較容易散失，所以其單一有效研磨顆粒的研磨
溫昇會較低。 
 
5. 結論 
在本研究中，針對金屬研磨製程的研磨界面溫昇理論模式的建立，由金屬研磨界面間的界
面現象出發，利用微觀的表面接觸力學模式來描述金屬研磨製程中研磨界面間的接觸現象，探
討研磨顆粒、研磨墊表面粗度與金屬工件表面材料三者間的交互關係，並依此來建立針對金屬
研磨製程且考慮到耗材影響之研磨溫昇理論模式，最後並利用其來探討不同耗材使用及操作
參數對於研磨界面溫昇的影響。 
在一般金屬研磨製程中，較大的下壓力會增加研磨界面間的接觸率及有效研磨顆粒數，
因此材料移除率也會增加，此外，較高的下壓力也會增加接觸面間的摩擦熱，使研磨溫昇上
昇。較大的研磨顆粒與金屬工件表面的穿透量較深，接觸面積的半徑也較大，所以單一有效
研磨顆粒的研磨溫昇會隨顆粒平均直徑增加而升高。使用研磨顆粒粒徑標準差較小的研磨顆
粒，會增加研磨界面間的有效研磨顆粒數目，因為顆粒尺寸標準差越小，在研磨界面間，有
效研磨顆粒數目佔實際接觸面中全部顆粒數目比率越高，因此研磨顆粒粒徑標準差較小會有
較佳的材料移除率，且研磨溫昇也會降低。此外硬度較高或熱傳導係數較低的金屬工件材料
會導致較高的研磨界面溫昇。

摘要 
在一般的金屬研磨製程中，研磨界面的溫度會因
為研磨界面的磨擦現象及刮除現象而昇高，這種現象
會影響到金屬工件本身的溫度及尺寸，進而對研磨結
果產生影響。本研究將由理論模式的建構與實驗的驗
証同時著手來探討金屬研磨製程之研磨溫昇現象。針
對金屬研磨製程的研磨界面溫昇理論模式的建立，將
由研磨界面間的界面現象出發，利用微觀的表面接觸
力學來描述金屬研磨製程中研磨界面間的接觸現象，
探討研磨顆粒、研磨墊表面粗度與金屬工件表面材料
三者間的交互關係，並依此來建立針對金屬研磨製程
且考慮到耗材影響之研磨溫昇理論模式，最後利用所
開發的金屬研磨溫昇理論模式來探討不同耗材使用及
操作參數對於研磨界面溫昇的影響，以得到一具實用
性的理論模式。  
關鍵字：金屬研磨、研磨溫昇、界面現象、瞬閃溫度、
微觀接觸 
  
1. 前言  
在目前的工業界中，機械製造技術正朝著提高精
度與生產率兩個方面迅速發展。在提高生產率方面，
提高自動化程度是各國致力發展的方向，近年來，從
C N C數控工具機到電腦整合製造系統的發展迅速，在
特定專業範圍內得到了極佳應用。而提高精度方面，
從精密加工演進到超精密加工，這也是世界各主要工
業國家致力發展的方向。其加工精度從微米到次微
米，甚至奈米，其應用範圍日趨廣泛，在各類工業中
都有所應用。其中金屬研磨製程因可以製造出表面粗
糙度極佳的被加工表面，且幾乎所有的金屬工件都可
以用此方式進行加工，因此乃是目前超精密加工中最
主要的加工方法之一。由於研磨製程不僅要能提供鏡
面級的表面粗糙度，還要保證獲得精確的幾何形狀和
尺寸，因此研磨製程的關鍵加工條件是避免振動的研
磨運動、潔淨的環境、高品質的研磨劑及精密的溫度
控制，這些條件中，又以精密的溫度控制最為重要。
在一般的平面拋光製程中，研磨界面的溫度會因為研
磨界面的磨擦現象及刮除現象而昇高，這種現象會影
響到金屬工件本身的溫度及尺寸，進而對研磨結果產
生影響。所以，想要完整瞭解研磨拋光製程的現象，
研磨溫昇效應的探討是不可忽略的，此外，在實際的
製程應用上，要對研磨界面的溫昇機制有充分的瞭
解，才有可能對其效應做進一步的控制，以確保研磨
製程之品質。[1-13] 
在金屬研磨的製程中，因為機械磨耗的作用而產
生摩擦熱，進而使研磨界面間的溫度升高，研磨界面
間的接觸溫度不只對磨潤性能影響甚巨，對於金屬工
件尺寸熱變形的發生亦有很大的影響。一般而言，研
磨界面間溫度的分佈狀況是正向力，研磨滑動速度與
研磨界面材料性質及表面粗糙度的函數[14]，因此現
在已有學者針對研磨製程研磨溫昇現象進行探討 
[15-24] ， 然 而 目 前 研 究 大 多 是 從 現 象 邏 輯
（Phenomenological）上來進行研究，因此需要進行大
量的實驗，於實際的應用上亦有著許多限制。 
研磨溫昇相關的研究方面，學者專家們主要由瞬
閃溫度的觀點來進行。於1937年，Blok[15]首先提出了
有關單一粗糙峰與一平面在邊界潤滑的狀況下之研磨
界面溫昇的相關理論，他以兩相對表面的運動狀況來
描述瞬閃溫度，並求出了接觸區分別在高低Peclet 
Number下的最大瞬閃溫度（Flash Temperature）。
Jaeger[16]在1942年以Blok的理論為基礎，將接觸溫昇
的模式拓展至兩滑動接觸的表面，並求出介於高低
Peclet Number中間區域的瞬閃溫度近似解。在1959年
Achard[17]轉換Blok的模式，強調接觸溫度對接觸面積
之平均溫度的重要性。Francis[18]於1970年推導出兩物
體相對運動時，在Hertzian Contact狀況下達到穩態時
的界面溫度場的溫度分佈，其考慮的的接觸點熱源是
橢圓形式，並得到一個在彈性接觸時的界面溫度模
式。而Gecim與Winer[19]更進一步地考慮兩快速運動
之接觸面間的溫度傳遞速率，探討表面粗糙度的影
響。Bhushan[20-21]於1987年建立起一套考量摩擦熱與
表面尖峰接觸界面溫度模式。Kuhlmann-Wilsdorf[22]
於1987年利用Jaeger於1942年所提出的方法，成功地預
估兩滑動摩擦表面之橢圓接觸點在不同速度參數下的
最 大 瞬 閃 溫 度 。 隨 後 McCool[23] 更 結 合 了

The Effects of Abrasives during Post-CMP Cleaning 
 
Pay-Yau Huang1 and Yeau-Ren Jeng2
1Assistant Professor, Dept. of Mechanical Engineering, WuFeng Institute of Technology 
E-mail: py.huang@mail.wfc.edu.tw 
2Professor, Dept. Mechanical Engineering, National Chung-Cheng University 
E-mail: imeyrj@ccu.edu.tw   
 
Abstract  
In order to understand the single particle adhering 
and removing mechanism of post-CMP cleaning process, 
the interfacial phenomena between an abrasive particle 
and wafer surface were investigated in this study. The 
micro-contact model was used to predict the 
particle-wafer contact area and contact force in order to 
find the required force that removes the particles. This 
study suggests that under the same applied down force 
during CMP process, the different penetration depth can 
be achieved with different particle size. Smaller particle 
size has lower friction force between the particle/wafer 
contact interface and easier to be removed during 
post-CMP cleaning process. 
Keywords—chemical-mechanical polishing (CMP), 
Post-CMP cleaning, abrasive particle, removal 
 
1  Introduction 
Chemical Mechanical Polishing (CMP) is the method 
of choice in the semiconductor industry to planarize 
inter-level dielectrics and damascene metal wiring for 
on-chip multi-level interconnects. As the size of 
microelectronic device decreases, planarization of both 
front and backend layers becomes vital for interconnect 
smaller than 0.5 μm [1-3]. Due to the characteristic of 
CMP process, large amount of contamination will leave 
on the polished surface, such as residual abrasive 
particles, metallic contamination and a damaged layer. 
Therefore the post-CMP cleaning process has become a 
standard manufacturing step. Depending on the type of 
CMP and the polishing conditions, the abrasive particles 
in the slurry can be physically adsorbed at the wafer 
surface or partially embedded in the substrate due to the 
mechanical pressure exerted by the polishing pad. It has 
been accepted that the van der Waals force plays an 
important role in the adhesion process. To overcome the 
adhesion force, the mechanical scrubbing from the brush, 
or chemical loosening using solution or additives are 
necessary. Chemical additives are used to control the 
electrostatic repulsion in order to prevent particle 
readhesion. This can be done by controlling the charges 
on the substrate and particles, which can be altered by 
modifying the solution pH or the addition of surfactant. 
The objective of using surfactant is that it can be used to 
modify the apparent charges on particle and substrate 
surface. Another important characteristic is that particles 
can be kept apart during the occupation of the organic 
surfactant molecules when attached to the particle. 
To date the CMP and post CMP cleaning have become 
standard processes for local and global planarization. 
Researchers have attempted to approach them from 
different point of views in accordance to their needs. 
These studies consist of evaluating the effect of 
hydrodynamic force [4,5], modeling parameters to study 
the adhesion force [6-11], studying the lubrication 
behavior and friction forces [12], using additives and 
different chemicals during cleaning [13,14]. These 
results, among others, have discussed the mechanical 
nature of surface material removal in a micrometer 
length scale and larger. The abrasive particles used in 
CMP are in nanometer. Interesting questions arose when 
sizes considered become smaller. Thus, understanding 
the contact phenomena between a single abrasive particle 
and wafer surface is the motivation of this research.  
In order to understand single particle removal, the 
current study starts from a particle embedded process 
during CMP. The knowledge of how the abrasive particle 
embedded in the wafer surface helps to understand 
particle removal mechanisms. In the polishing process, 
we make the following assumptions: the uniform load 
distribution during CMP process, the uniform 
distribution on pad asperities, a direct contact between 
pad and particles, and a direct contact between substrate 
and particles. Previous researches show that 
hydrodynamic force is limited for effective cleaning 
[12,15]. Therefore, this work focus on the direct contact 
force in order to see the effectiveness of the particle 
removal. Consequently, for the cleaning process, the 
method used in this study is a direct contact with the 
substrate surface through brush sweeping.

In order to understand particle removal mechanisms, it 
is important to know how particles are adhered to wafer 
surfaces during polishing. The numerical modeling here 
is to illustrate how particles are adhered to wafer surface 
under different loading conditions.

4  Conclusion 
This research investigated a single particle adhesion 
and removal mechanisms during CMP and post-CMP 
cleaning process with numerical analysis. The current 
result is consistent with the experiment conducted 
previously. The present study has presented a 
consideration of the interface phenomena and provides 
an insight into the physical effects of particle size in the 
post-CMP cleaning process and opens areas of future 
investigation in understanding roles of surfactant 
additive and how it works in the post-CMP cleaning.

Abstract. The objective of this research is to better understand 
the mechanisms of material removal in the polishing process.  
This study is aimed to establish a material removal rate model 
for metal polishing to predict the result of metal polishing 
process. This research conducts theoretical study to investigate 
the material removal rate and its mechanism during the metal 
polishing process. The present study proposes a material removal 
rate model for metal polishing based upon a micro-contact model 
which considers the effects of the abrasive particles located 
between the polishing interfaces. In addition to such operational 
parameters as the applied down force, the present study also 
considers the consumable parameters rarely investigated by 
previous models, including workpiece surface hardness, abrasive 
particle size and the standard deviation of abrasive size 
distribution. The current results show that the material removal 
rate increases as the average abrasive particle size. In addition, 
the smaller standard deviation of abrasive particle size 
distribution can increase the efficiency of metal polishing 
process. This study also provides physical insights into the 
interfacial phenomena not discussed by previous models, which 
ignored the effects of abrasive particles between the polishing 
interfaces during force balancing. 
 
Keywords: metal polishing, material removal rate, micro-contact 
mechanics, interfacial phenomena 
1.1 Introduction 
Due to the trend for high precision, the requirements for 
the surface roughness of metal workpiece are becoming 
stricter. Polishing  is the method of choice to planarize the 
metal surface to a certain tolerable range. In the metal 
polishing process, workpiece surface roughness is 
minimized by passing slurry with abrasive particles 
between the workpiece and the polishing pad [1].  Figure 
1.1 shows the schematic view of the metal polishing 
plateform. The effectiveness of the metal polishing 
process is determined by a variety of process control 
parameters, however, due to the complex nature of the 
inter-relationships between these parameters, control of 
the polishing process currently remains at an empirical 
stage. In order to improve the efficiency of the polishing 
process and the quality of its polishing result, many 
researchers have explored the effects of the process 
parameters from a physical perspective and by considering 
experimental results [2-5].  
  Xie et al. investigated the free abrasive polishing 
process experimentally and theoretically [6].  Experiments 
were carried out to understand the effects of particle size, 
polishing pad and nominal contact pressure on the wear 
rate and surface roughness of the polished surface. A 
theoretical model was also developed to predict the 
relationship between the polishing parameters and the 
wear rate for the case of hard abrasive particles 
sandwiched between a soft pad and a workpiece.  
Chauhan et al. observed polished ceramics and metals by 
microscopic, which indicated that material removal occurs 
as a consequence of repeated sliding or rolling of the 
abrasive particles against the workpiece surface [7].  Luo 
and Dornfeld proposed a solid-solid contact model of the 
CMP process based on plastic contact over the wafer-
abrasive interface and pad-abrasive interface [8]. The fluid 
effect is attributed to the number of active abrasives, but 
the effects of slurry particles under hydro-dynamical 
contact were not investigated.

1.3 Results and Discussions 
The current parametric study explores the influence on the 
material removal rate of operational parameters such as 
the applied down force and of consumable parameters 
such as abrasive particle size, the standard deviation of 
abrasive particle size distribution, and workpiece surface 
hardness.  
Figure 1.5 presents the variation in the material 
removal rate with applied down force magnitude for 
various average particle diameters. The simulation results 
indicate that for a constant average particle diameter, the 
material removal rate increases with increasing down 
force magnitude.  Furthermore, for a constant magnitude 
of down force, the material removal rate also increases 
with increasing average particle diameter.  According to 
the developing model, both larger the abrasive particle 
size or the applied down force can increase the penetrating 
depth of the abrasive particle/workpiece contact interface.  
Consequently, the material removal rate increases. These 
observations are consistent with the results of previous 
experimental studies conducted by Xie et al.[6]  Equation 
(1.9) reveals that the number of effective particles is a 
function of the average particle diameter and the real 
contact area of the polishing interface. However, the real 
contact area of the polishing interface increases as the 
magnitude of the down force increases, and hence the total 
number of effective particles increases with increasing 
applied down force or with decreasing average particle 
diameter. But, the material removal rate in the metal 
polishing process is influenced more strongly by the 
penetration depth than by the number of effective particles, 
and consequently, the material removal rate increases as 
the average particle size increases.
