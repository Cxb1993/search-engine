    體外震波碎石術，在泌尿科已行之多年，且有穩定而確鑿的臨床實證功效[1,2]；
如能利用同一原理，尋求適當頻率跟強度的震波[3]，以便選擇性地破壞脂肪細胞而
保留其餘組織[4]，且利用 RO 逆滲透水當作震波傳遞之介質，以期達到更高之效率
[5]，未嘗不是一項有益人類的技術創新。其明顯優點當然是免除了手術創疤，又因
其相對的非侵入性，期望可大幅減輕其併發症之頻率與嚴重度，以此觀之，這項技
術應頗值得開發。

4.1.1 凝膠假體組織厚度能量耗損實驗

能量強度。

4.2.2 兔子的實驗 
A.1 體外震波消脂的基本情形 
震波擊發條件為操作電壓 10kV，擊發震波 1000 次，擊發頻率 1Hz，焦點位置
在皮下 1cm，大約位於肌肉層與脂肪層之間。圖二十二顯示施打震波 1000 次、10kV、
1Hz 下，大白兔表皮受到震波消脂後的切片放大四倍圖，圖顯示表皮與真皮組織沒
有受到傷害，位於皮下的脂肪層也不在聚焦區內所以也沒有受到震波的破壞。圖二
十三顯示施打 1000 下、10kV、1Hz 在聚焦區邊緣的大白兔肌肉層的切片放大四倍
圖，內含 2-3mm 脂肪，也有受到些許的震波傷害，不過血管、肌肉組織等幾乎沒有
受到傷害。圖二十四是大白兔脂肪層的切片放大四倍圖，圈圈處裡面是脂肪細胞有
遭到震波破壞的區域。 
 12
由以上分析可知，體外震波消脂確實可以破壞震波聚焦區的脂肪細胞，在震波
聚焦區主要會產生兩種破壞情形：一種是條狀的破壞，由高壓力所造成。另一種是
不規則的形狀的破壞，是在更高壓力的情形下產生了空蝕效應，這大多在擊發電壓
10kV 的震波時產生。而在震波聚焦區中其他的組織如表皮組織、肌肉組織也會受到
些許的傷害，不過受損的情形和範圍都較脂肪組織來的少。切片中在震波聚焦區外
的組織幾乎不會受到震波的影響而受損。

B.6 副作用分析 
  在體外震波消脂的動物實驗中，有幾項副作用也相伴而生，在巨觀和微觀的檢
驗中都有被發現。這些副作用在將來臨床實驗的應用，會產生關鍵的影響而降低震
波消脂的效果，甚至危害受試者的生命。 
B.6.1 表皮出血現象 
  在十數次的動物實驗中，不論實驗動物是兔子還是猪，在表皮都很容易會產輕
微的出血現象。面積大約是直徑 1-1.5 公分的圓形。主要成因是因為在震波消脂實
驗中我們定位深度不夠深。大約是皮下 1cm 大約與震波聚焦的範圍差不多，導致一
些震波聚焦到表皮，使得表皮的微血管受到空蝕效應和高壓的影響而破裂，造成微
出血滲出表皮，這在巨觀與微觀的觀察都可以看到此一現象。此一症狀在兔子的實
驗中，可以在一個禮拜左右完全復原而不留下疤痕。這些出血在精準的定位以及調
整輸出能量之後可以減低出血症狀甚至沒有出血症狀。以下圖三十七顯示受到震波
而造成的表皮出血的情形。圖三十八顯示受到震波而造成的表皮出血檢體的切片

B.6.2 脂肪以外的組織的破壞 
  在微觀的觀察中，切片裡有些結締組織和和肌肉組織以及表皮的微血管都有受
到些微的傷害。此一傷害在連續的震波實驗中觀察，亦可發現這些受到傷害的組織。
根據我們切片的觀察可以在一週後復原。脂肪組織較其他組織來的柔軟脆弱，一般
而言較容易受到傷害，尤其是在體外震波碎石的療程中脂肪細胞受到的破壞遠較其
他組織大。圖四十是受到震波破壞的其他組織的切片放大四倍圖，圖中除了血管周
邊的脂肪組織被破壞外，靜脈血管也受到些許的破壞。 
 
5、結論 
本計畫已完成了 in vitro 和 in vivo 之動物實驗，證明震波消脂是可行的。目前
使用體外震波於溶化脂肪細胞上有三大優點: (1)非侵入性，(2)對皮膚、血管、神經
皆未有損傷，(3)脂肪無燒傷現象產生。雖然動物實驗結果證明震波消脂是可行的，
然而應用震波消脂於人體脂肪，要等待人體臨床成功才能算真正的成功，此項工作
是未來努力目標，亦是一項挑戰工作。

Fat is an efficient form of excess energy (calorie) storage, because it has about twice 
the caloric density of carbohydrates and proteins. Excess fats in the subcutaneous layer 
often occur in several areas, such as the abdomen, hips, thighs, the waist and the chin or 
neck. These excessive fats, the fat layer, between the skin and muscle can deform body 
contour and plague the patient. To improve body contour, invasive lipoplasty (liposuction) 
has been used as a plastic surgery by removing excessive subcutaneous fats. Traditional 
liposuction, such as the negative-pressure type [1], was an effective but dangerous 
surgical technique for removing subcutaneous fats because of the use of a cannula that 
was inserted through a small skin incision. The cannula tip, moved back and forth to 
sculpture fat cells, was connected to a suction device for suctioning out sculptured fats. 
Consequently, traditional liposuction might incur some problems such as bleeding, nerve 
damage, normal tissue damage, formation of blood clots, infection, and skin depression.  
In order to improve the traditional negative-pressure liposuction, tumescent 
liposuction [2, 3], ultrasound-assisted liposuction [4-7], and power-assisted liposuction [8] 
have been invented. Power-assisted liposuction is basically a negative-pressure lipoplasty, 
but possibly causes less bruising, swelling and discomfort. These new devices and 
concepts can accelerate fat removal, resulting in reduced operation time and less labor, 
less blood loss and greater safety. However, there are some unsolved deficiencies, such as 
possible pulmonary edema, lidocaine toxicity for tumescent liposuction, and bruising 
problems and skin burn risk for ultrasound-assisted liposuction. 
Studies on reducing the operation risks and the side effects of invasive lipoplasty have 
continued. In 2005, a new technique was developed in Israel [9-11], which used focused 
ultrasound waves for breaking down fat deposits. Fat cells were non-invasively destroyed 
with a device named the Contour I system (UltraShape). This method caused no 
additional stress on the kidneys or the liver and did not damage the blood vessels, 
peripheral nerve fibers and lymphatic vessels in the treated adipose tissue. The Contour I 
device used the principle of ultrasound focusing. After this treatment, disrupted fat cells 
are metabolized and travel to the liver for recycling to meet the continuing demands of 
the body via the circulatory system [12]. Moreover, skin in proximity of the treated area 
does not collapse after treatment with the Contour I system.  
Extracorporeal shock wave (ESW) has been used for the treatment of renal stones 
for over 20 years [13]. This is so-called extracorporeal shock wave lithotripsy (ESWL). 
The principle of ESWL is to produce a high pressure through shock waves focused at a 
focal point where the target such as a kidney stone is located. The treated stone is broken 
by the compression effect due to focused compressive shock waves, the spalling effect 
due to expansion waves, and the impact effect of micro-jets due to cavitation [14-16]. 
Outside the focal area, the pressure is dramatically lower, causing almost no damage on 
the kidney tissue. In general, stone fragments of less than 2 mm can naturally drain 
through the ureters. Recently ESW was extended to the treatment of musculo-skeletal 
disorders [17-20]. This is so-called extracorporeal shock wave therapy (ESWT). The 
principle of ESWT is to produce a high pressure at the target for fracturing the calcified 
tissue or inducing neovascularization via tissue damage. A new ESW therapy for lysing 
fat cells, named as ESW lipolysis, is proposed in this study. ESW lipolysis is different

3. Results  
3.1 Values of parameters associated with shock wave administration 
    Since there were no published reports regarding the result of lipolysis by shock 
waves, we had to determine the least energy density flux that was necessary to disrupt fat 
cells and other parameter values associated with our ESW machine. These parameters 
associated with shock wave administration were the operation voltage, number of shock 
waves, and firing frequency of shock waves. Thus some experiments were conducted for 
determining these parameter values.   
(a) Operation voltage: The voltage of the ESW machine in our laboratory could be 
operated from 5 kV to 12 kV. Starting from 7 kV to 10 kV with increments of 1kV, 
several experiments were conducted to observe, by eye, visible damage to the treated 
adipose tissue. A further study involved detailed microscopic examination. Several 
experiments were conducted within a range of 7-12 kV to understand the extent of 
damage to the treated fat tissues. In consideration of the operation time, four numbers 
of shock waves – 650, 1000, 2000, and 3000 were studied at the same voltage of 10 
kV. The 10kV voltage produced a peak pressure of 47.2 MPa with a standard 
deviation of 2.6 MPa, corresponding to an energy flux density of 0.249 mJ/mm2 with 
a standard deviation of 0.0361 mJ/mm2. 
(b) Frequency of shock firing: Two different frequencies of shock wave firing were 
studied – 1 Hz and 1.5 Hz. It was found that the shock-wave-firing frequency was not 
a significant parameter. Hence the frequency of 1Hz for shock wave firing was used, 
except where specifically noted.  
(c) Wave propagation medium: Two media were used in this study – tap water and 
reverse osmosis (RO) water. It was found that the RO water could effectively cause 
damage on the treated fat samples with about 30% greater effect. So only the results 
of the RO water experiments are reported except for special mention. 
(d) Erosion rate of bronze electrodes: It was found that the erosion rate of the electrodes 
depended on the medium used. For bronze electrodes, the erosion rate was about 
0.1-0.2 mm increase in electrode gap for 200 shock firings at the operation voltage of 
10 kV in tap water. Thus, after 200 shock wave firings, the electrode gap was 
adjusted to retain its optimum gap.

4. Discussions 
 Since the electrode gap will be increased with the number of shock wave firings, the 
increasing gap results in non-uniformity of the produced shock wave strength, causing 
difficulty in accurately estimating the energy flux density of shock wave. On the other 
hands, the electrode gap is related with the electrode material. In this study, the electrode 
material used was bronze, which has a good conductivity but easily erodes because of 
high temperature. An alternative approach for accurate estimation of the energy needed 
for lysing fat cells is to use an electro-magnetic shock wave generator or a piezoelectric 
shock wave generator, which does not have the problem of electrode erosion. Thus, more 
researchers have been encouraged to do this research to find an accurate energy needed 
for lysing fat cells. 
 The present study is a pioneer study of lipolysis by shock waves. To the authors’ 
knowledge, there have been no other related works published. Therefore, no other results 
were included in this paper for comparison with the present results except for the reports 
on the UltraShapeTH, which were already mentioned in the introduction. Basically, a 
shock wave contains much more energy than does an ultrasound wave. The number of 
shock waves needed for lipolysis is much less than that for ultrasound treatment, resulting 
in a shorter treatment time. 
 A basic question is what happens to the destroyed fat cells after shock wave 
treatment in clinical application. Brown [12] reported that the destroyed fat (free fatty 
acids and glycerol molecules) is transported through a series of metabolic pathways to the 
liver or other tissues. The broken cell membranes (debris) are cleared by the process of 
phagocytosis. The authors believe that a lot of destroyed fat cells occurring at the same 
time may induce embolism in the liver. This problem needs to be answered by an in vivo 
study.             
5. Conclusions 
A non-invasive therapy for lysing adipose tissue by shock wave treatment has been 
proposed and studied. An in vitro experiment has been conducted on pig skin/fat tissues 
treated by shock waves in order to confirm the proposed ESW lipolysis. An 
electro-hydraulic shock wave generator with automatic gap adjustment was set up for 
generating shock waves. The parametric studies associated with shock wave 
administration have been made.  
It was found that the erosion rate of electrodes depends on the medium used. For 
bronze electrodes, the erosion rate is about 0.1-0.2 mm increase in the electrode gap for 
200 shock wave firings at the operation voltage of 10 kV in tap water. The 10 kV voltage 
could produce a peak pressure of 47.2±2.6 MPa, corresponding to an energy flux density 
of 0.249±0.0361 mJ/mm2 at the second focal point. Moreover, the erosion rate of bronze 
electrodes in RO water was faster than that in tap water, resulting in greater damage to 
adipose tissue. The damage to adipose tissue was not clearly visible from
