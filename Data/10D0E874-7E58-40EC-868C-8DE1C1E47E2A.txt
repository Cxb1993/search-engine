氣泡在固化中陷入固體形成氣孔之過程 (3/3)

一、中文摘要 
 
   本計劃理論分析與實驗量測微米氣泡在固化
界面生成,為固體吸收,形成不同形態氣孔之過
程. 不同於氣泡因過熱,固體內氣孔之產生肇因
於固液界面之成分濃度偏析而成過飽和. 製造
或材料加工等過程工件中通常發生氣孔,氣孔之
分佈,尺寸大小,及形狀決定了工件之美觀,更直
接影響加工品質.不幸地,氣泡因移動固液界面
濃度偏析,過飽和而產生,然後為界面吸收,固體
內形成不同氣孔,其相互影響之研究極為缺乏. 
本研究延伸已往成果,除了以顯微鏡,照相機,全
像干涉儀,觀察量測純水非穩態固化過程,單一
及多個微小氣泡界面成核, 成長及為固體吸收, 
形成長形及蟲穴形氣孔之輸送過程外,並提出更
深入理論模式與現象解釋. 由於研究範圍深且
廣, 此計畫分為三年,除了以實驗觀察量測氣孔
之形成,理論工作第一年為理論探討氣泡形成圓,
長形氣孔成長過程; 第二年研究是解釋常見之
氣孔形成原因; 最後一年為數值計算熱流效應
對於不同氣泡氣孔形成之影響. 本計劃結果顯
示,證明,並奠定完成首次氣泡形成過程有系統的
指引與瞭解.  
 
關鍵詞：氣泡動力學, 氣孔, 製造與材料加工
成品之瑕疵 
 
Abstract  
 
 Mechanisms of a microbubble emanated and trapped in 
solid as different types of pores during solidification are 
experimentally observed and theoretically analyzed in this 
project. Rather than the familiar nucleate boiling due to 
superheat, bubble formation results from supersaturation of 
the dissolved gas in the liquid ahead of the solidification 
front. Qualities and microstructure of materials are strongly 
affected by shapes, distributions and shapes of pores in 
solids, which are widely encountered in almost all the 
micro-electro-mechanical systems (MEMS), manufacturing 
and materials fields. Unfortunately, understanding of the 
entrapped bubble and pore behavior in different pattersns 
during solidification is still lacking. In view of the 
complexity of the process, this proposal is a three-year 
project involving experimental and theoretical analysis of 
the microbubble and pore growth in the solid. Experimental 
equipments include zoom microscopy, cameras and 
interference holograph had been established as before. The 
first year for the theoretical analysis is to model the 
development and departure of a spherical and columnar 
microbubble or pore in solid; the second year is to simulate 
the growth and shape of the observed hole; and the last year 
is to simulate a microbubble and pore formation affected by 
fluid flow. This work provides the first deep insight and 
future directions into different types of pore shapes in the 
solid. 
 
Keywords: Bubble dynamics, pore formation, defects 
encountered in manufacturing and materials processing 
 
二、緣由與目的 
    
 Porosity is one of the most serious problems commonly 
occurring in welding and materials and manufacturing 
processing [1-3]. Pore formation in solids is a consequence 
of the bubbles trapped by the solidification front. Pore 
formation can be categorized by gas entrapped in the molten 
layer [2-4], and solute rejection from the solid phase during 
solidification [5-7]. The gas entrapment in the molten layer 
is closely related to instabilities of the free surface and fluid 
flow. Pores are in sizes of millimeters. Pores resulted from 
solute rejection at the solidification front are in sizes of 
micrometers. Two mechanisms, however, can be modeled 
by considering a growing or decaying bubble entrapped by 
the solidification front in supersaturated and supercooled 
liquids. The bubble entrapped as a pore in a supersaturated 
liquid is treated in this work.

六、結論 
 
 The present work provides analytical and numerical 
methods to simulate the measured growth and entrapment of 
a microbubble by a solidification front in a supersaturated 
liquid. The model satisfies conservation of mass, 
momentum, energy and thermodynamic and physico-
chemical equilibriums at the bubble surface. The bubble 
shape above the solidification front is systematically and 
self-consistently determined by solving Young-Laplace 
equation. The computed results are confirmed by comparing 
with experimental data. Further detailed and complicated 
phenomena related to pore formation are undertaken. This 
is the first study of pore formation on  the world.
