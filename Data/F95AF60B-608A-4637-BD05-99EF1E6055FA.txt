Non-transparent materials, e.g. metal and silicon were adopted into the current
silicon-based thin film transistors, the aperture ratio of light output of displays was
limited. Oxide based semiconductors were the most important materials for
currently studying transparent thin film transistor. ZnO thin film transistor
possesses better carrier mobility than IGZO, but IGZO had some merits, e.g., large
area manufacturing and low temperature process. Nevertheless, lack of rare metal
-indium in the earth’s crust to manufacture IGZO; Therefore un-doped ZnO TTFT
has large developing potentials. Therefore, in this research project, the main
focuses will emphasize the processing development of ZnO transparent thin film
transistors. By means of ceramic powder metallurgical processes of targets
un-doped ZnO and doped ZnO with Ga2O3 and In2O3 to form the ZnO and IGZO
active thin film layer by adjusting processing parameters of radio frequency
magnetron sputtering. Devices characteristics of ZnO and IGZO thin film
transistors will be investigated by p-type Si wafer structure and processes. To
develop the lower threshold voltage and off state current of transparent thin film
transistors, the optimum ZnO and IGZO active layer will be adopted by means of
RF sputtering deposition.
Especially, the research analyzing the impact of film characteristics by
adopting metallurgical process parameters with different IGZO powders on
electrical properties is the most important, including film resistivity, Hall mobility,
and carrier concentration. The ternary compound phase of ZnGaO4 becomes stable
at 1000 according to XRD diagrams at different calcining temperatures.℃
Therefore, the calcining temperature of the powder was set at 1000 . An analysis℃
of the XRD diagrams of different compositions of IGZO powders shows that the
main peak (101) of the crystallization phase, InGaZnO4, appears clearly at an
atomic ratio of In：Ga：Zn = 1:1:1. Increasing the Zn mole ratio enhanced the main
peak intensity of InGaZnO4. A comparison with the XRD data of IGZO powders
with different atomic ratios indicates that the optimum chemical composition
occurred at an atomic ratio In: Ga: Zn = 1:1:2 for IGZO powders. Ceramic targets
with different compositions were used to deposit the a-IGZO films. The Hall
conductivity and carrier concentration in the a-IGZO films increased as the gallium
ion content decreased or the zinc ion content increased.

六.結果與建議
(1) 在製靶的過程中，雖然我們自製的靶材都有製備出成果，但是壓靶仍是一個
我們必須面對之問題，因為壓靶須以 60,000-70,000 kg/cm2 的壓力在 1250℃
持溫 1 小時的環境下燒結製成陶瓷靶材，然而學校之設備並無法達成此效
用，因此目前仍委託廠商幫忙壓製而成，不過後續為了減少成本，我們仍會找出
方法解決，例如加入 PVA 幫助集結成塊。
(2) 針對自製靶材 IGZO、ZnO，由於受到製作時的環境影響，粉末內之成份可
會受環境影響而變質，為了驗證自製靶之純度，我們也購置商用靶作為對照組。
(3) 由於 ZnO及 IGZO 透明薄膜在沉積時通入氧氣，會使得薄膜電阻率急遽上升，
造成薄膜之電阻率無法藉由四點探針量得，尤其在未退火時更是無法測得，
之後須藉由量測元件上的測試鍵而得薄膜阻值。
(4) 製程中，由於元件是採用矽背閘所成之電晶體，因此在採用逆向蝕刻法時，
必須非常小心，因為元件都為氧化物所組成，因此使用氫氟酸蝕刻時，必須 控
制蝕刻之選擇比，以蝕刻出最佳之圖形。

ABSTRACT
Recently, more studies have been done on the relationship between the oxygen vacancies and the
numbers of the conduction carrier in the transparent thin-film transistors (TTFT). In this work, we
found that the number of oxygen vacancies may decrease with increasing resistivity, responsible for the
lack of conduction carriers in lower annealing temperature.
(keyword: TTFT, annealing temperature, oxygen vacancy)
INTRODUCTION
Wide bandgap transparent oxide semiconductor (TAOSs) ZnO thin films, In the past years, was
widespread adopted to the solve the problems associated with conventional Si based TFTs such as their
low field effect mobility and opacity. Moreover, the great important advantage given to replace the Si
based TFT is the flexibility[1-4]. the radio frequency magnetron sputtering is most often used due to its
high growth rate and large area uniformity[5].
In the condition of un-doping, the main carrier concentration originated in the oxygen vacancies
of its own material. The oxygen vacancies release free electrons to conduction in the channel which are
trapped by oxygen vacancies. Owing to the high chemical activity of Zinc, Zinc possesses highly
oxidation effect regardless of in the atmosphere or in the manufacture process. The less the oxygen
vacancies are, the higher the resistance is. Therefore, in order to acquire the electrical characteristic of
ZnO film, it must to proceed annealing. It is desirable to reduce the oxygen content to form the more
oxygen vacancies by annealing. In this work, the electric conductivity of ZnO with different annealing
temperature was investigated by the materials behavior and optics experiments.
MATERIAL AND METHOD
The ZnO films were deposited on glass substrate by RF sputter with RF power 175W at fixed
working pressure of 5m torr in room temperature. The deposition condition employed in the sputtering
deposition was the argon of 50 standard cubic centi-meter per minute (sccm) .After preparing the ZnO
film, the different annealing temperature was varied from 200-400°C for an hour in the N₂ambient.

ABSTRACT
ZnO is the center of attention in recent years, mainly used in TTFT. The radio frequency
magnetron sputtering is most often used due to its high growth rate and large area uniformity. We show
that the appropriate glass substrate temperature is helpful. When the 300 ℃, can have a better quality
film. Because of Oxygen vacancies, reduction of oxygen will reduce the transmittance of ZnO thin
films. If using ZnO film as the channel layer of TTFT, the best film depositing condition will be set at
room temperature.
Key words：ZnO、radio frequency magnetron sputtering、glass substrate temperature、TTFT.
INTRODUCTION
Wide bandgap transparent oxide semiconductor (TAOSs) ZnO thin films , In the past years, the
ZnO film of transparent thin film transistor (TTFT) was widespread adopted to the solve the problems
associated with conventional Si based TFTs such as their low field effect mobility and opacity.
Moreover, the great important advantage given to replace the Si based TFT is the flexibility[1-4]. the
radio frequency magnetron sputtering is most often used due to its high growth rate and large area
uniformity[5]. The carries of the ZnO film were produced by the lack of oxygen atoms, which are
denoted as the oxygen vacancy. Generally, the valance electrons in the metal oxide compound would be
attracted to oxygen atoms, because the electrical negativity of oxygen was larger than that of metal.
Therefore, once the Zinc atoms located beside the oxygen atom, the electrons of Zinc which would be
captured by the oxygen might become the conductive carriers. In this work, the study was focused on
the impact ZnO film deposited with different substrate temperature.
MATERIAL AND METHOD
The ZnO ceramic target was used in the experiment. The ZnO target was formed in into the
sputter target with the pressure of 60,000-70,000 kg/cm2 at 1200℃ in Ar ambient. The ZnO film was
deposited on the glass of SCHOTT B270 by RF magnetron sputtering system. In the deposition process,
the flow rate of Ar was fixed at 50 sccm and the working pressure was controlled at 5E-3 Torr. Also,
the sputtering conditions were set on different temperatures such as room temperature, 100 , 150 ,℃ ℃
200 , 300 . After deposition,℃ ℃ the samples would be annealed at 400℃ with 5E-3 Torr working
pressure for 1 hour in N2 ambient.
The electrical parameters were measured by the Hall measurement, which used the van der Pauw
method by HL5500PC (Bio-Rad). the surface morphology of the samples were investigated by the
Scanning Electron Microscopy (JOEL JSM 6500-F) and Atomic Force Microscope (Veeco Dimension
5000 Scanning Probe Microscope). The crystallinities of ZnO film were also analyzed by XRD
(PANalytical X'Pert Pro) with Ni-filtered CuK(λ=1.5418Å) source in θ-2θ scanning mode. The 
transmittance were scanned by the UV–Visible spectrum system (LAMBDA UV Lambda 900).
