measurement results show that the deposition pressure on the 5, 6 
and 7 torr, the cells have the high current density that is typical 
characteristics of microcrystalline phase. The structure of 7.5-torr 
cell is mixed-phase of microcrystalline and amorphous phase that 
the current density has the middle value. The 8-torr cell is fully 
amorphous and has the lower current density that is the typical 
characteristic of amorphous phase. There have a certain percentage 
of microcrystalline phase for the solar cells deposited at 5 ~ 7.5 torr, 
therefore it has a lower open-circuit voltage of about 0.45 V. The 
open-circuit voltage of the amorphous phase of the 8-torr cell is 
increased to 0.84 V. These results illustrate that transition region 
from microcrystalline to amorphous occurred from 7 to 7.5 torr. The 
solar cell deposited at 7 torr is at boundary of transition region, and 
has the best performance and conversion efficiency. Good mc-Si:H 
solar cells must be fabricated at the boundary of transition region 
from microcrystalline to amorphous, which is generally controlled 
at high-pressure condition. 
 
 
(一) 中文摘要 
 
本研究使用 40.68 MHz超高頻電漿增強化學氣相沉積系統，改變 i層沉積壓力及 p層氣體摻雜比
例製作氫化微晶矽(µc-Si:H)薄膜太陽電池。藉由拉曼光譜儀、X 光繞射儀、掃描式電子顯微鏡、電流-
電壓量測儀及量子效率測量儀，分析太陽電池的結構及電學特性變化。 
 
p型µc-Si:H薄膜以改變摻雜氣體比例 (B2H6 / SiH4 = 0.25%至 1% )製作。由結果顯示當 p層摻雜氣
體比例為 0.25%時，結晶比例高，易誘發後續 i層薄膜析出結晶，孵化層較薄，相反的當 p層為非晶矽
結構，抑制 i層析出結晶，p/i介面載子傳導不佳，使得太陽電池特性下降。適當的 p型微晶矽結構有
助於 i層的結晶成長及品質改善。 
 
    固定 p層摻雜氣體比例為 0.25%，改變 i層沉積壓力 5至 8 torr製作太陽電池。壓力於 5、6及 7 torr 
時太陽電池結晶結構呈現垂直柱狀，而沉積壓力增加至 7.5 torr時孵化層變厚，結晶結構呈現倒圓錐狀，
最後於 8 torr時為全非晶矽結構。由電流-電壓結果顯示當沉積壓力於 5、6及 7 torr時，具有高的電流
密度為典型微晶相之特徵，壓力於 7.5 torr時，薄膜結構為微晶轉非晶的混合相，電流密度介於中間值，
為微晶與非晶混和相所造成之結果，而壓力為 8 torr時，具有明顯低的電流密度，此為非晶相的特徵。
壓力為 5至 7.5 torr時之太陽電池具有一定比例的微晶矽結構，故具有較低的開路電壓約為 0.45 V，沉
積壓力為 8 torr的太陽電池僅具有非晶矽結構，開路電壓明顯上升至 0.84 V。這些結果指出，沉積壓力
於 7至 7.5 torr為微晶至非晶轉介區，介於轉介區邊界的 7 torr太陽電池有最佳的薄膜特性及轉換效率。
良好的氫化微晶矽太陽電池必須控制在微晶轉非晶的轉介區邊界，一般發生在較高壓的條件。

(三) 報告內容 
 
一、前言 
氫化非晶矽(hydrogenated amorphous silicon: a-Si:H)薄膜太陽電池主要面臨的兩個問題，一為轉換
效率低，另一為薄膜內因光照後生成的缺陷與斷鍵所造成的光照衰退，目前國際間均針對這兩個問題
積極研究其解決之道。在改善轉換效率方面，1994年瑞士 Neuchatel大學的微技術研究所(Institute of 
microtechnology: IMT)發表了 a-Si:H/µc-Si:H (氫化微晶矽 hydrogenated microcrystalline silicon: µc-Si:H)
的疊層太陽電池結構[1](此結構被稱為 micromorph)。此種疊層太陽電池的上電池由 a-Si:H所組成，由
於 a-Si:H有較大之能隙，負責短及中波長光之吸收；下電池則由µc-Si:H所組成，µc-Si:H的能隙較小
負責中及長波長光之吸收，這兩個電池堆疊後可以增加太陽光譜的運用，進而達成提高轉換效率。目
前此種結構是矽薄膜太陽電池提昇效率及穩定性最重要的發展之一，日本的 Kanaka公司利用此種結構
製作太陽電池，其實驗室轉換效率已可大於 15%[2]，而日本的MHI及 Sharp等國際大廠也相繼採用此
結構來改善轉換效率[3, 4]。 
由於µc-Si:H薄膜負責長波長波段(紅光及紅外光)的光吸收。當光波長大於730 nm(即光子能量小於
1.7 eV)時，µc-Si:H薄膜的光吸收係數大於a-Si:H薄膜的光吸收係數，但其數值小於103 cm-1，因此以
μc-Si:H薄膜做為矽薄膜太陽電池之光吸收層時，需要有較厚的厚度來吸收紅光及紅外光，其厚度為使
用a-Si:H(厚度約0.2至0.3 µm)做為矽薄膜太陽電池之光吸收層的5至10倍，約為1.5至2.0 μm。若以a-Si:H
薄膜的沉積速率製作μc-Si:H薄膜，將使得生產速率嚴重降低，因此如何快速的沉積出品質良好的
µc-Si:H薄膜，為製作μc-Si:H薄膜及a-Si:H/µc-Si:H micromorph疊層太陽電池的重要關鍵之一。 
氫化微晶矽薄膜的結晶形態有許多不同的結晶成份、結晶尺寸及結晶方向，且強烈的依賴氫氣稀
釋等不同製程的影響，如何選擇製程條件及製作何種結晶形態才能有較佳的太陽電池性能，仍是國際
間努力的課題。 
 
二、研究目的 
為找出最佳的p型µc-Si:H薄膜結構及將本質µc-Si:H薄膜控制於轉介區內，本計畫以超高頻(40 MHz)
電漿加強化學氣相沉積(VHF-PECVD)，在p型µc-Si:H薄膜的製備以調變不同氣體配比，製作不同p層結
構的µc-Si:H薄膜；在轉介區的本質層µc-Si:H薄膜的製備以氫氣稀釋法搭配變化沉積壓力，建立一詳細
有系統的p層結構對i層結構的影響，並找出適當p層結構及位於轉介區的本質層改善太陽電池性能。 
 
三、文獻探討 
國際間典型製作氫化微晶矽薄膜之研究，一般採用氫氣稀釋法。所使用之氣體為矽烷(SiH4)及氫氣
(H2)，即提高氫氣對矽烷的稀釋比例，使得薄膜沉積受氫原子蝕刻，造成薄膜的矽氫合金比例及矽氫鍵
結形態的改變。利用氫氣稀釋法製作氫化微晶矽薄膜，隨著提高氫氣對矽烷的比例(R = H2/SiH4)，使得
薄膜內含有矽奈米晶粒，並逐漸成長成柱狀結構。 
µc-Si:H薄膜處於轉介區具有較佳的特性，但µc-Si:H薄膜隨著薄膜的厚度增加其結晶比例及晶粒
大小隨之增加。Baojie Yan等人[5]的研究成果指出，µc-Si:H薄膜太陽電池隨著本質層厚度的增加，增
加了µc-Si:H的結晶比例及晶粒大小，而晶粒的成長造增加了晶界的產生，進而影響太陽電池轉換特性
中的短路電流隨之減少。圖一為 Baojie Yan等人以不同的本質層厚度製作太陽電池的量子轉換效率圖。
由圖中所示，當本質層厚度為 1340 nm時有最佳的量子轉換效率，隨著厚度的上升至 1930 nm時，其
量子轉換效率下降。因此 Baojie Yan等人提出，以逐漸減少氫氣稀釋比例沉積本質層，來抑制 µc-Si:H
 
圖三、不同 P層沉積時間對太陽電池特性圖 
 
由上述可知，在 p-i-n μc-Si:H薄膜太陽電池，p層 μc-Si:H薄膜會強烈的影響 i層的 μc-Si:H薄膜的
結晶特性，進而影響太陽電池的光電轉換效率。因此找出適當的 p型 μc-Si:H薄膜結構及將 i層的 μc-Si:H
薄膜的結晶狀態控制於轉介區內，才能製作出特性良好的 μc-Si:H薄膜太陽電池。 
 
四、研究方法 
本研究使用 40.68 MHz 超高頻電漿加強化學氣相沉積系統製作 μc-Si:H 薄膜及太陽電池。分析
μc-Si:H薄膜特性所使用的基板為 100 mm × 100 mm × 0.7 mm Corning Eagle XG 玻璃，而製作太陽電池
所使用的基板為 100 mm × 100 mm × 1.1 mm的 Asahi U-type 玻璃。本質氫化微晶矽薄膜製程條件為固
定電極距離 1 cm、加熱板溫度 200 ℃、射頻功率密度 0.6 W/cm2、氫氣稀釋比例 (R = H2 / SiH4)為 40，
調變腔體壓力為 3、5、6、7、7.5及 8 Torr沈積本質氫化微晶矽薄膜氫化微晶矽薄膜；p型製程條件為
固定電極距離 1 cm、加熱板溫度 200 ℃、射頻功率密度 0.6 W/cm2、氫氣稀釋比例 (R = H2 / SiH4)為
150、腔體壓力 3 Torr，並調變摻雜氣體比例(B2H6/SiH4)為 0.25、0.5及 1%。以、以拉曼光譜分析結晶
比例及 SEM測量薄膜的微結構來描述這些不同結構的本質及 p型氫化微晶矽薄膜。探討 p型 μc-Si:H
薄膜結構及 i層的 μc-Si:H薄膜的結晶狀態控制，及其對太陽電池性能的影響。 
本質及 p型氫化微晶矽薄膜的結晶比例、結晶方向及微結構分別由拉曼螢光顯微鏡、高解析 X光
繞射儀量測及掃瞄式電子顯微鏡分析。太陽電池效率的量測是試片在 300 W ELH燈泡照明下量測，ELH
燈泡的光強是 Standard cell (PV Measurement PVM 250)校正至約一個太陽的光強度。 
 
五、結果與討論 
為了了解 p 層結構對 i 層結構的影響，並找出適當 p 層結構及位於轉介區的本質層改善太陽電池
性能。以 VHF-PECVD系統在 p型µc-Si:H薄膜以不同摻雜氣體比例，製作不同 p層結構的µc-Si:H薄
膜；而本質層µc-Si:H薄膜的製備則以氫氣稀釋法搭配變化沉積壓力，調變不同製程壓力，製作 μc-Si:H
薄膜並找出µc-Si:H薄膜的轉介區。分析不同結構及 i層製程壓力對 p-i-n μc-Si:H太陽電池的結構、光

Abstract 
 
In this work, hydrogenated microcrystalline silicon 
(μc-Si:H) p-i-n solar cells deposited on different crystal 
volume ratio (XC) of p-layers are fabricated by very-high 
frequency chemical vapor deposition (VHFCVD). The XC of 
p-layers is strongly depended on the hydrogen dilution ratio 
(RH=H2/SiH4). Increasing RH can obtain p-layers of high 
crystal volume ratio (XC) with high dark conductivity and low 
activate energy. Increased XC of p-layer can induce the crystal 
growth of the following i-layer, which resulting in the μc-Si:H 
solar cell performance with increasing short-current density 
(Jsc) but reducing open-circuit voltage (Voc) and fill factor (FF). 
The i-layer crystal growth is influenced by the crystal phase of 
p-layers, and the performance of the solar cell is determined by 
the crystal structure of i-layer. 
(Keywords: Hydrogenated microcrystalline silicon, 
hydrogen dilution ratio, crystal volume ratio)  
 
Introduction 
     
Hydrogenated microcrystalline silicon (μc-Si:H) thin film is 
a promising material for solar cell because better stability for 
light soaking and higher absorption in the infrared compared to 
hydrogenated amorphous (a-Si:H) thin film. The structure of 
μc-Si:H films are high sensitivity to the deposition conditions 
and the substrates morphology. The fabrication process of 
p-i-n μc-Si:H thin-film solar cell, p-layer is deposited before 
μc-Si:H i-layer. As the previous researches [1-5], the structure 
of i-layer is influenced by μc-Si:H p-layer, thus the μc-Si:H 
solar cell characteristics are also affected. In this work, 
different crystal volume ratio (XC) of p-layers is mainly 
changed by variation of the hydrogen dilution ratio 
(RH=H2/SiH4) are investigated. 
 
Experimental 
 
The μc-Si:H p-i-n solar cells are fabricated by 40.68 MHz 
capacitive-type VHFCVD with fixed substrate temperature at 
180℃ and electrode distance is 10 mm. The deposition 
pressure of p-layers is 3 torr, a power is 35 W, doping gas flow 
ratio RB (B2H6/SiH4) is 0.5% and varying hydrogen dilution 
ratio (H2/SiH4) from 50 to 100. The i-layers are deposited with 
a gas pressure of 6 torr, a power 60 W, and hydrogen dilution 
ratio (H2/SiH4) of 60. The crystal structures of p-layers and 
i-layers are measured by Raman spectrometer and scanning 
electron microscopy (SEM). The performance of solar cells is 
evaluated by I-V measurements.

phase is also called as the incubation layer. In fact, the protocrystalline phase is a gradual changing phase 
from the amorphous phase of the initial film growth to the microcrystalline phase of the onset of crystal 
grain nucleation. Increasing hydrogen dilution can reduce the thickness of incubation layer. The gradual 
changing phase from the amorphous phase to the microcrystalline phase will happen in a thin-thickness 
region. Some reports show that the pc-Si:H structure can be used to improve the performance of solar 
cells [4-7]. However, for different hydrogen dilution, the formation of protocrystalline phase in a same 
fixed thickness within incubation layer is totally different. Therefore, to understand the influence of the 
pc-Si:H films on the performance of solar cells shall carefully identify the difference of the formation of  
protocrystalline phase of the films. 
  
In this work, we gradually change the hydrogen dilution ratio (R = H2/SiH4) to prepare a series of 
various protocrystalline phase of pc-Si:H films. Since the protocrystalline phase can only exist in the 
incubation region. In order to maintain the protocrystalline phase, pc-Si:H/a-Si:H multilayers were 
fabricated, in which 26-nm-thickness pc-Si:H sublayers and 13-nm-thickness a-Si:H sublayers are 
periodically deposited. The role of a-Si:H sublayers is to interrupt the continuous growth to limit the 
thickness of pc-Si:H sublayers, thus to maintain the protocrystalline phase [8-10]. The controlling of 
various protocrystalline phases of pc-Si:H/a-Si:H multilayers is obtained by a series changing of 
hydrogen dilution ratio R for deposition of pc-Si:H sublayers. The changing of protocrystalline phases of 
these pc-Si:H/a-Si:H multilayers are analysed by Raman spectrometer and spectroscopic ellipsometer (SE) 
[11, 12]. The I-V characteristics of the p-i-n solar cells with the i-layer of these pc-Si:H/a-Si:H 
multilayers are used to identify the influence of the different protocrystalline phase on the performance of 
solar cells and photodegradation effects.   
2. Experimental 
2.1. Sample preparation 
a-Si:H and pc-Si:H single-layer films, pc-Si:H/a-Si:H multilayers, p-i-n solar cells with i-layer of 
single-layer of a-Si:H or pc-Si:H, and of pc-Si:H/a-Si:H multilayers were prepared by a single-chamber 
13.56 MHz capacitive-type plasma-enhanced chemical vapour deposition (PECVD) system. The 
substrates used for films and solar cells characterisation were deposited on Corning 1737F glass and 
Asahi U-type glass, respectively. The substrate temperature, peak RF power and pressure for i-layer 
deposition were kept at 200oC, 20 W and 1 Torr, respectively. The pc-Si:H/a-Si:H multilayers were 
deposited with computer control of periodically changing hydrogen dilution ratios (R = H2/SiH4). The R 
ratio for pc-Si:H sublayer deposition was changed from 6, 8, 16 and 28 to control the formation of 
different protocrystalline phase. The R ratio for a-Si:H sublayer deposition was fixed at 4. In order to 
compare the variation of protocrystalline phase, the thickness of pc-Si:H sublayer is fixed at 26 nm, and 
that of a-Si:H sublayer is fixed at 13 nm. The total number of layers is 10, and the total i-layer thickness is 
390 nm. The thicknesses of p-layer and n-layer are 15 and 30 nm, respectively. The area of the p-i-n solar 
cells is 7.07×10-2 cm2. 
2.2. Sample measurements 
Raman spectra were used to characterise the crystal phase change of the a-Si:H and pc-Si:H single-
layer films and pc-Si:H/a-Si:H multilayers, which were measured by a spectrometer of NT-MDT 
MS5400i with 632.8 nm wavelength and 2.27 mW output power. The dielectric function, optical energy 
band gap were measured and analysed by spectroscopic ellipsometer, which were carried out by a J.A.

3.2. Optical properties of a-Si:H and pc-Si:H single-layer films 
Figure 2 displays dielectric functions of the i-layer a-Si:H (or pc-Si:H) films deposited with R ratio 
from 4 to 28. The peak position and peak height of dielectric constants of real part (ε1) and imaginary part 
(ε2) are summarised in Table 2. Increasing R ratio reduces peak intensity and shifts peak position toward 
higher energy of ε1 and ε2. Cabarrocas et al. [14] pointed out that the film with low film density would 
result in the peak movement of ε1 and ε2 toward high-energy and decreasing of the peak height simul-
taneously. High R ratio induces the formation of more protocrystalline phase in the films, which have low 
film density. As shown in the insets of Fig. 2 and data of Table 2, the lower R ratios from 4 to 8 films 
have similar low-energy peak positions and high peak height of ε1 at about 2.79 to 2.82 eV and 22.9, and 
of ε2 at about 3.66 to 3.70 eV and 25.4 to 25.5.  In addition, the higher R ratios of 16 to 28 films have 
high-energy peak positions and low peak height of ε1 at about 2.83 to 2.87 eV and 22.4 to 21.4, and of ε2 
at about 3.68 to 3.75 eV and 24.9 and 23.8 eV. The results demonstrate that low R ratio films exhibit 
more amorphous phase, and high R ratio films contain more protocrystalline phase, resulting in low film 
density. The reduction of peak intensity at low-energy side of ε1 and ε2 corresponds to the low absorption 
of long-wavelength photons. High-energy peak positions and low peak height of dielectric constants 
correspond to the Raman peak shifted to high wavenumber.

3.6. Photodegradation  properties of the solar cells 
Figure 6 presents the quantum efficiency (QE) curves before and after photodegradation of single i-
layer solar cells. The difference of QE between before and after photodegradation is at the long-
wavelength region from 500 to 700 nm. The photodegradation effect is mainly due to the recombination 
of carriers generated by long-wavelength photons. Notably, for low R4 and high R28 cells, the 
degradation of QE is more serious than that of the cells of middle R6 and R16. Since the QE is measured 
under short-circuit condition, the degradation of QE is corresponding the degradation of JSC. The results 
indicate that there is an optimised protocrystalline structure for reducing the degradation of JSC.

4. Conclusions 
The gradual change the amount of protocrystalline phase could be well controlled by the pc-Si:H/ 
a-Si:H multilayers. The Raman spectra and dielectric function and optical properties measured by 
spectroscopic ellipsometer can sensitively identify the variation of protocrystalline phase of the films. 
Increasing R ratio can obtain more protocrystalline structure in the films with high optical energy gap and 
low film density, which results in low photodegradation rate but low initial energy efficiency. Therefore, 
the optimised performance of pc-Si:H/a-Si:H multilayers solar cells for obtaining good stability and 
efficiency shall be controlled by suitable protocrystalline phase.

Abstract 
A series gradual changing of protocrystalline phase of hydrogenated protocrystalline silicon/hydrogenated amorphous 
silicon (pc-Si:H/a-Si:H) multilayers is fabricated by periodically changing hydrogen dilution ratios (R = H2/SiH4). 
Protocrystalline phase can be well identified by Raman peak shift and the variation of dielectric function measured by 
spectroscopic ellipsometer. Increasing R can effectively increase the protocrystalline phase, optical energy gap, and 
reduce the density of films. More protocrystalline phase can suppress the photodegradation effects of the pc-Si:H/a-
Si:H multilayers solar cells, but reduce the initial efficiency. Therefore, the performance of cells could be optimised 
by suitable protocrystalline phase for obtaining good stability and efficiency.

1. Introduction 
Hydrogenated protocrystalline silicon (pc-Si:H) film is a promising materials for solar cells, which 
structure is gradually changed from amorphous phase to the boundary of amorphous and microcrystalline 
(a + µc) mixed phase along the film growth direction. This gradually changed phase will stimulate the 
formation of nucleation sites, thus, which is named as protocrystalline phase [1-3]. Due to the incubation 
of the formation of nucleation sites and the following crystal grain growth, this region of protocrystalline
