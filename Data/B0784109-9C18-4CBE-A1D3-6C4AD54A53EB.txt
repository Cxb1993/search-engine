1. 前言
現今豆腐的製作過程，舉凡傳統工法，亦或自動化一貫化流程，尚未達到自動監測其品質的階
段；豆腐於成品時，也沒有一套標準的檢驗機制供檢測時的參考依據，因此本實驗以檢測、控制生
產線豆腐品質為目標，進行各種實驗驗證與理論分析，以達到低成本、高品質的豆腐輸出。
豆腐由大豆蛋白質加熱後並且與凝固劑混合進而凝結所形成的膠狀組織，因此蛋白質是構成膠
狀組織的主要成分，其餘成分還包括脂肪、水、與氣泡 (Kohyama et al., 1995; Saowapark et al.,
2008)。而可將豆腐的膠狀組織視為其質地(texture)，原因在於膠狀組織的堅實度會深深地影響其口
感( Jackson et al., 2002)，而非其成分(Kohyama and Nishinari, 1993)。豆腐的口感、產率及品質等特
性，取決於豆漿的濃度、凝固劑種類及比例、凝結成豆腐的過程時間及火候的掌控等重要操作條件
(deMan et al., 1986; Hou et al., 1997; Cai and Chang, 1998)，影響著豆腐最後質地的特性以及口感、
風味。
超音波的傳遞特性深受介質的影響；因超音波在傳輸時，會因穿透過不同的介質而造成衰減或
波速改變(McClements, 1995)，因此受到凝固條件所影響的豆腐，其質地疏密程度可透過超音波傳
遞受介質影響的特性加以檢測出來(Gan, 2006)。此外，豆腐的膠狀組織特性還可用顯微鏡觀察，其
機械特性可以採質地分析(textural analysis)或流變分析(rheological study) (Kohyama et al., 1995;
Toubal et al., 2003; Wang et al., 2005; Berry et al., 2006; Arltoft et al., 2007; Saowapark et al., 2008)，因
此經由超音波傳輸的特性，對照顯微影像分析不同疏密的質地(Saowapark et al., 2008)，來驗證宏觀
的理論與模型。
在顯微影像分析上，由於近年來隨著顯微鏡技術的進步，解析度涵蓋了由光學顯微鏡的微米
(μm)，到電子顯微鏡的奈米(nm)範圍，幾乎已經接近到原子層次的解析度(Bloch et al, 2001)，因此
可利用高解析度的顯微鏡，辨別出嫩豆腐與板豆腐的結購與成分差異。嫩豆腐與板豆腐因其先天混
合形成豆腐所需的凝固劑不同，使得豆腐的口感有所差異，所以運用顯微技術來探討其微結構組織
與成分的不同，如此將進一步的助於我們辨別嫩豆腐與板豆腐的差異。因此本實驗運用SEM所呈
現的豆腐網狀結構的顯微影像，如孔洞大小、整體孔洞的完整度，以影像處理的方式作質地分析並
且估算出其孔洞部分的形狀面積、直徑長度等方式來更精確的分類出不同質地的豆腐有何差異。而
豆腐的膠狀組織成份可在共軛焦雷射顯微鏡(confocal scanning laser microscope, CSLM)下呈現由蛋
圖 1 CaSO4．2H2O 豆腐的概念性網路結構模型(Kohyama et al., 1995)。

3.2 影像處理分析
3.2.1 掃瞄式電子顯微鏡
豆腐微結構組織資訊的取得，是由 SEM(TM-1000，Hitachi，日本)來做研究試驗之用，此型號
最大的優點為，樣本不需要經過繁複的前處理手續，而且軟體操作相當地簡便。對於豆腐結構組織
得以依不同的需求，來做進一步的縮放。
3.2.2 掃瞄式電子顯微鏡之觀察
本實驗利用顯微影像來分析豆腐顯微組織結構與其機械特性，以及豆腐微結構與超音波穿透
豆腐時所提供之資訊的關聯性。因此，本實驗所要進行的分析為根據不同配方的市售嫩豆腐以及板
豆腐來建立其膠狀組織與影像結構的關係，例如：結構的完整度與網狀孔洞的大小
以下所示為顯微影像之取得實驗方法流程：
1. 將市售豆腐切取一長、寬、高分別為 10 mm×10 mm×2 mm 之豆腐切片，並且置於圓形鋁座上。
2. 將豆腐切片置入液態氮(零下 196℃)裡面，浸泡約五分鐘。基於 SEM(TM-1000，Hitachi，日本)
不能處理含水率過高之軟質樣本，所以先以液態氮做前處理的工作。根據嘗試下的結果，浸泡
的時間不能超過三分鐘，否則會使豆腐切片生成冰晶之塊狀結構，影響顯微影像的呈現，並進
並依上述調配染劑之方法所得的染劑滴於 5m 的樣本，並蓋上蓋玻片，蓋上玻片時避免空氣進入
與破壞樣本結構，致使染劑不完全覆蓋在樣本上，導致無法分辨成分的正確位置與無法得知完整的
結構。
3.2.5 影像處理分析
經由電子顯微鏡所照出不同質地豆腐的網狀結構，再以影像處理的方式取得孔洞大小與形狀的
數據來分辨不同質地豆腐。由於二值化影像可從灰度值之影像經過處理而成，且較容易儲存、處理
與辨認，因此本實驗利用二值化處理影像以達到辨別孔洞與計算孔面積，即算出幾個點
(pixel)(Chang and Wang, 1997、Ilya Blayvas,2006)。圖 5 代表二值化之圖形影像分析的方塊圖，方

6 結論
嫩豆腐與板豆腐的口感之所以會有差異，根據顯微影像分析與超音波檢測分析的實驗結果，可
得知大部分的板豆腐孔洞是大於嫩豆腐的，而在超音波的衰減係數中，板豆腐的衰減比嫩豆腐的衰
減大。因此，證明出超音波在豆腐中傳播時，會受到豆腐內部膠狀組織的關係影響其物理特性。例
如：因為板豆腐的結構比較鬆散，孔洞比較大，所以超音波的衰減係數較大，而嫩豆腐則恰好相反。
基於實驗材料取決於市售豆腐，因此，其每一種嫩豆腐與板豆腐會因為各家廠商經驗法則的不
同而有所不同，站在物理結構意義的角度來探討豆腐的微結構組織，撇開其組成成份所造成的機械
參數誤差，就現階段的機械參數實驗結果而言，具有可以參考的價值。因此，本研究的貢獻在於使
用超音波以及物性分析的方式，來探討其微結構組織所對應的物理相關性。豆腐微結構孔面的大小
與超音波衰減係數呈相關性，可以建立起靜態超音波檢測模型，提供固態豆腐一完整且客觀的檢測
方式。

Abstract: The purpose of this study was investigated the relationship between the qualities of

INTRODUCTION

USE OF ULTRASOUND FOR CHARACTERISING THE GELATION PROCESS IN
HEAT INDUCED GLUCONO--LACTONE (GDL) TOFU CURD
Feng-Jui Kuo1, Cheng-Chang Lien1, Ching-Hua Ting1*
1 Department of Biomechatronic Engineering, National Chiay University, Taiwan
*Corresponding Author, Tel +886-5-2717642, Email: cting@mail.ncyu.edu.tw
Abstract: Tofu is a gel-network product made by coagulating soya milk proteins followed by
heat or pressure treatment after the addition of a coagulant. This paper uses a hypothesis tofu
gelation model for ultrasonic validation. The aggregation and the ripening processes in tofu
gelation can be identified with ultrasonic measurements. Mechanical evolution of tofu curd in
gelation was investigated using low-power ultrasound and textural analysis. Two independent
ultrasonic parameters, velocity and attenuation, were measured at the frequency 1 MHz as a
function of time after addition of the (glucono--lactone) GDL coagulant to heated soya milk.
The responsive ultrasonic velocity has a plateau in the beginning of gelation and tends to a
lower steady state after the formation of tofu gels. Ultrasonic attenuation exhibits first-order
kinetics that matches the development of firmness revealed by textural analysis. Low-power
ultrasound explores the formation of tofu gels in the aspects of pre-gelation processes, protein
aggregation in gelation, and mechanical evolution in the gel at the post-gelation stage. This
allows the use of ultrasonic attenuation as an alternate to textural or rheological analysis in
characterisation of tofu gel. Ultrasonic measurement is non-contact and non-destructive and
hence it could be possibly used as a real-time indicator of tofu maturity on the production line.
Key Words: Tofu, Gelation, Textural analysis, Ultrasonics, Firmness
INTRODUCTION
Addition of the calcium sulphate (CaSO4.2H2O) or the (glucono--lactone) GDL coagulant
into heated soya milk triggers the formation of tofu gels. The formation is a two-step
mechanism (Kohyama et al., 1995): soya proteins are denatured and then aggregated by the
coagulant. Microscopy of tofu gel demonstrates that the spread of proteins, fats, water, and air
throughout the network structures correlates with the gelation process (Saowapark et al., 2008),
i.e. the structure of tofu gel varies in gelation. Ultrasonics, a good means in identifying the
structural variation of a medium (Llull et al., 2002; Kuo et al., 2008), has previously been used
in this laboratory in characterising the gelation process in heat induced CaSO4.2H2O tofu curd
(Ting et al., 2009).
The previous study (Ting et al., 2009) revealed some results in adoption of ultrasonics in
characterising CaSO4.2H2O tofu gelation. The aggregation and the ripening process in tofu
gelation can be identified with ultrasonic measurements. The progress of tofu ripening was
explored using the ultrasonic power attenuation which follows first-order kinetics, the same as
the penetration test. This allows the use of ultrasonic attenuation as an alternate to textural or
rheological analysis in characterisation of tofu gel. Ultrasonic measurement is non-contact and

Abstract: Tofu is a gel-network product made by coagulating soya milk proteins followed by heat or
pressure treatment after the addition of a coagulant. The firmness of the network determines the
texture and hence the sensation of tofu. The structure of a tofu network can be observed with
microscopic analysis and the mechanical properties are obtainable through textural analysis or
rheological study. These approaches are destructive and sometime prohibitively expensive and hence
they are unsuitable for on-line measurement of tofu properties in a production line. Hygiene and
efficiency are an essential requirement in automated food processing. The best way to avoid
contamination in quality measurement of food processing is to use non-contact and non-destructive

calibrators. Experimental results show a positive correlation existing between the area percentage of

INTRODUCTION
Tofu is usually considered as a salt- or acid-coagulated water-based gel, with soya lipids and proteins
and other constituents trapped in its gel networks (Kohyama et al.,1995). The taste of tofu is
significantly effected by its final texture (Jackson et al., 2002), i.e. the firmness of the tofu gel
structure. The textural property is governed by soya milk concentration, coagulant type and
concentration, gelation pressure and temperature, and gelation time (Hou et al., 1997; Cai and Chang,
1998).
Microscopy of tofu gel demonstrates that the spread of proteins,fats, water, and air throughout the
network structures correlates with the gelation process (Saowapark et al., 2008), i.e. the structure of
tofu(Ting et al.,2009), gel varies in gelation. Ultrasonics has been demonstrated to be a good means in
identifying the structural variation, as the behaviour of an ultrasound wave travelling in a medium
correlates with the structure of the medium (McClements, 1995; Gur and Ogel, 2001; Llull et al.,
2002; Kuo et al., 2008). Thus, it is the objective of this study to exploit ultrasound for nondestructive
measurement of tofu firmness with scanning electronic microscopy for (SEM), and textural analyses
as the calibrators.

Abstract: In food processing, sweetness and viscosity are two important scientific sensory
indices for food quality. These two sensory indices, if measureable, can be used in automatic
control of food processing to obtain the best product quality. In this study, based on previous
static ultrasonic measurements, we use ultrasound as an instrumental indicator in dynamic
measuring and as a sensor in system feedback control of juice production. The product quality

INTRODUCTION
Fruits play an important role for people in daily life. Besides eating directly, people often
squeeze fruits to fresh juice for drinking. Fresh juice may be stored as a concentrated solution
and later delivered to the market in a reconstituted form. Reconstituted juices may be blended
with additives, diluted with water, or have added sugar to arrive at a specific,
consumer-favored flavor. The sweetness is a taste sensation that is difficult to measure
scientifically. Practical methods currently used for measuring the sugar contents in a fluid
include Brix gauge probing and chemical analysis (Harker et al., 2002; Lynnworth, 1989). But
it can only be operated manually which is unacceptable in automated food processing.
Hygiene and efficiency are an essential requirement in automated food processing. The best
way to avoid contamination in quality measurement of food processing is to use non-contact
detectors. Low-power ultrasound and infrared which are emitted from equipment isolated
from the target material are a well-studied candidate in food processing, as they do not
introduce extraneous contamination nor alter the properties of the substance (Cartwright,
1998a; Bhardwj, 2001).The ultrasonic variable most suitable for the determination of sugar
content is the propagation velocity (Kuo et al., 2008). In static fluids, it shows a good linear
correlation with sugar contents in solution denoted by Brix. Hence ultrasonics can be used to
measure the sugar content of solution through suitable mathematical calibration (Kuo et al.,
2008).
The previously set-up model is confined to static fluids. However, a fruit juice production
system deals with dynamic fluids. The Reynold number is an important parameter in
characterising the behaviour of a flowing liquid (Mott, 2006). In a dynamic fluid, the Doppler
effect occurs in the fluid when the wave source produces relative velocity to the observer.
Hence the juice quality model obtained from static measuring should be revised to adapt to a
dynamic scenario.

DISCUSSION
This study utilizes the low-power ultrasonics for measure ultrasonic velocity as a linear
function of sugar concentration in real-time. The linear regression equation has a R-square
value of 0.99.
The measurement system used in this study has a standstill receiver and the emitter is
perpendicular to the direction of the fluid flow. Hence the transducer does not introduce
effective velocity or Doppler shift, according to Eq. (4) and (5). Fig. 3 and 4 reveal that
ultrasonic velocity is robust to flow rate variation, i.e. both static and dynamic scenarios have
the same velocity profile.
By using Eq. (6), the Reynold number of the experimental rig is larger than 4000. Fluid flow
at such a high Reynold number has complicated behaviours because of bigger friction, heat
transfer, and mass transfer. The experimental results show that the ultrasonic velocity is only
dominated by the density and viscosity of the fluid but not by the Reynold number.
