As the integrated circuit technology scales 
down, more and more functions can be 
integrated into a system-on chip (SOC). 
High-speed, high-efficiency and low-cost 
interconnects are needed to access deeply 
embedded cores in such high-density and 
high-volume SOC. Various wrappers and test 
access mechanisms (TAMs) have been proposed. 
Currently, the interconnects are hard-wired metal, 
whose performance are limited by RC delay and 
crosstalk noise resulted from the shrinkage of 
metal size. In the same time, on-chip bandwidth 
increases while off-chip bandwidth is limited 
due to the package issues. To alleviate these 
interconnect bottlenecks, several non-direct 
contact interconnect technologies, which are not 
new technology but new employment, have been 
proposed. Among these technologies, capacitive 
AC coupling and wireless RF are compatible 
with standard CMOS process. Hence, this 
project is going to study and implement these 
two interconnect technologies, and apply them in 
SOC testing.
