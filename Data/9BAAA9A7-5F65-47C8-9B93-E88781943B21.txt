摘要 
 
近年來光觸媒應用在室內空氣品質改善上以越趨普及，並受到各研究單位相當程度上的重視，本
研究目的就以光觸媒表面沉積型態與光催化之關聯性做探討，光觸媒製成以電漿輔助化學氣相沉積法
結合模板合成法的方式，以二乙基鋅(DEZn)做為前驅物，並以陽極氧化鋁(AAO)薄膜做為模板基材，
嘗試能製備出表面型態不同之氧化鋅光觸媒，依不同沉積之結果進行光觸媒之光催化測試，使光觸媒
在應用上能有較佳效率。 
在光觸媒分析方面，對於氧化鋅光觸媒進行化學與物理特性之分析，並利用氣相異丙醇作為污染
物，以紫外光對於光觸媒進行照射，藉以評估光催化分解之效果，由光催化分析實驗中可得知，光催
化時光觸媒含量會影響污染物分解效率，但經由表面結構不同之光催化效率比較發現，就算光觸媒含
量不同，但因光觸媒表面的型態不同，觸媒型態為管狀之光觸媒似乎可以增加光催化效果，進而導致
光催化效率與沉積量較多之光觸媒相近。 
關鍵字：氧化鋅表面型態、高頻電漿法、二乙基鋅、陽極氧化鋁。

3. 氧化鋅光觸媒製備技術 
目前製備奈米尺度(以粒狀體為主)光觸媒之方法包括溶膠凝膠法(Sol-gel method)、水熱法
(Hydrothermal process)、濺鍍法(Sputter)、離子輔助蒸鍍法(Ion beam assisted deposition)、模板合成法
(Template synthesis method)、化學氣相沉積法(Chemical vapor deposition, CVD)等製備方式為普遍使用來
製作光觸煤的技術。 
溶膠凝膠法(Sol-gel method) 
溶膠凝膠法是觸媒製備方法中應用最為廣泛的方法，在張氏(2008)論文中指出溶膠凝膠法可以用來
製備粉體(Powder)、塊狀(Monoliths)、薄膜(Film)；所謂溶膠(Sol)是指極小的膠體分子受凡得瓦爾力(Van 
der Waal’s attraction)與電雙層作用下，因布朗運動均勻的分佈在液相當中。凝膠(Gel)是指經由水解
(Hydrolysis)及縮合(Condensation)反應後，分子單體逐漸鍵結形成大分子的半固體凝膠。 
水熱法(Hydrothermal process) 
    通常使用金屬鹽類為前驅物，並添加觸媒進行反應，在高溫、高壓的水溶液或蒸氣等流體中進行
合成，合成後可得懸浮的光觸媒微小顆粒。其優點是︰成本低廉、操作簡易、可進行大量合成。

電漿系統具有以下幾種特性︰ 
1. 電漿中具有約等量的游離電子與離子，在帶電的粒子之間是以庫倫引力在交互作用。 
2. 在電漿中粒子的移動速度相當的快，並帶有很高的熱動能。 
3. 在能量較低的電漿中所有物質並非是完全解離的狀態，除游離電子與離子外，還有處於基
態與激發態的中性原子與分子。 
4. 在電漿當中粒子與粒子之間的作用並非呈現一線性系統，在不同的區間會有不同的穩定與
非穩定性。 
而產生電漿的方法包括直流放電(Direct- current- discharge)、高頻(Radio frequency)以及微波
(Microwave)等方式，其中以直流放電電漿之成本最低，微波電漿之設備成本最高，但是電漿產生效率
卻以微波電漿之產率最高，直流放電電漿效率最低，高頻電漿則介於兩者之間，且近年來其頻率分配
技術業已成熟，因此其產生電漿之品質亦受肯定，本實驗使用之電漿產生方式為高頻電漿產生法，取
決於經濟效率成本之中間值。 
在電漿輔助化學氣相沉積法中，原料以分子的形態進入反應腔體後在電漿區域被活化，經擴散作
用均勻分佈在基材上方，當原料分子在基材上方移動時會以適當的能量相互碰撞後產生反應，生成氣
態生成物與在基材上沉積成固態生成物(薄膜)。

出現六角晶體的結晶構造。

進入可見光波長的範疇內(λ> 400 nm)。

過程中所使用的紫外源的波長及強度也會影響光催化的效率，光觸媒活性所需能階3.2 ev約等於380 
nm，只要提供足夠的能量就能將鍵結打斷使污染物分解，由文獻可知254 nm較365nm 較可有效降解污
染物，另外當光強度增加時降解污染物的效率也是增加的。本研究使用目標污染物異丙醇，探討在不
同相對濕度、不同紫外燈光強度、不同初始進流污染物的濃度、二氧化鈦披覆量實驗參數對光催化效
率的影響。

四、結果與討論 
本研究使用電漿輔助化學氣相沉積法搭配二乙基鋅作前驅物，配合使用氰氣(Ar)與氧氣(O2)混合沉
積氧化鋅光觸媒薄膜，而在文獻當中有指出使用金屬輔助化學蝕刻法搭配陽極氧化鋁進行實驗，可將
陽極氧化鋁做為模板，並在沉積後將模板去除可得到管狀氧化物沉積，故引用在本研究上，期盼能結
合本實驗室現有高頻電漿產生器設備，並參考過去本實驗室製作二氧化鈦光觸媒經驗，在製備氧化鋅
光觸媒技術上有所突破。 
為求氧化鋅光觸媒製備後在光催化應用上能有精進，本實驗設計上結合膜板法與電漿輔助化學氣
相沉積法來進行光觸媒製備，並且把利用模板法製備在AAO模板上方沉積之氧化鋅光觸媒進行光學特
性、表面結構、沉積量進行一連串分析以及比對，最後再使用氣相異丙醇作為污染物配合紫外光照設
設備進行對光觸媒光催化實驗，以觀察各不同條件製備下的觸媒在光分解上有何差異。 
在本系統設定當中把氰氣、氧氣、前驅物等三個操作變數做為實驗參數，在蔡氏(2002)的研究論文
指出，光觸媒沉積膜之特性以及沉積之速率均與電漿之密度有相當大的關係；而電漿密度又可從輝光
之強弱來得知，故其輝光所代表的就是電漿密度的分佈狀態，在本研究當中就以三種氣體流入腔體，
以產生輝光均勻程度來取捨流量的控制範圍，如圖4-1。

2. 沉積量與沉積參數分析 
    在沉積物進行定量的方面，由於在本實驗室以往使用CVD進行光觸媒沉積時，均無法對於所沉積
出的光觸媒重量進行定量，導致對於光觸媒分析上無法有個依據，也無法得知沉積效果為何，故嘗試
藉由分析在土壤中金屬總量之方法來分析沉積在AAO膜上之氧化鋅沉積量，所參考依據為環保署公告
方法編號NIEA R355.00C「土壤、沈積物、污泥及油脂中金屬元素總量之檢測方法－微波消化原子光譜
法」之方式，並且依照沉積量經消化後在溶液中可能之濃度而選擇使用火燄式原子吸收光譜儀(FAAS)
來對於在AAO膜片上之氧化鋅光觸媒之沉積物進行定量。 
    此實驗當中所使用之光觸媒樣品操作條件為使用RF-PECVD方法進行光觸媒沉積，控制基板溫度
在150 ℃、RF電漿輸出功率為150 W、沉積時間控制在30分鐘、反應系統壓力為0.20 kPa、前驅物DEZn
流量為400 ml/min；而在Ar與O2氣體流量上進行不同參數改變之沉積實驗，再將經RF-PECVD沉積後之
沉積膜進行消化處理，並搭配火燄式原子吸收光譜儀(FAAS)進行在沉積膜上方之氧化鋅含量測定。 
從圖4-11為經由控制不同的氰氣與氧氣來進入RF-PECVD當中進行之實驗，從圖中可觀察到，不論
所輸入氧氣的流量為多少，其氧化鋅沉積量只要氰氣流量越高，就會產生較多之氧化鋅光觸媒，反觀，
輸入氧氣流量改變卻對於沉積量無明顯的增加或減少驅勢；在本實驗背景測試為使用空白AAO膜片進
行空白實驗時，所得吸光值為-0.003 Abs.，經檢量線(附錄一)換算後為0 ng。

4. 氧化鋅光催化效果分析 
    光觸媒之光催化效果測試為使用氣相異丙醇做為污染物，使用波長365 nm之紫外光對於U字型光
反應器進行照射，以批次式實驗進行光催化測試，當光催化反應進行中，藉由火燄式離子氣相層析儀
分析異丙醇在氧化鋅光催化反應下其濃度的變化情形。 
直接光解背景測試 
    圖4-13為不放置光觸媒在反應器中，並直接注入污染物後進行照射UV光之空白實驗，在此實驗中
先將液相異丙醇注入U型反應器中，以批次反應進行，將反應器置放在恆溫板上，以50 ℃做為反應固
定溫度，避免異丙醇因冷凝在玻璃壁上造成實驗誤差，且注入異丙醇後先行靜置使其因布朗運動可以
完全揮發於反應器中，再對無放置光觸媒之反應器以波長365 nm之紫外光光源與0.6 mW/cm2之光強度
進行照射，觀察異丙醇在此光照條件下是否會受短波長光照而分解。 
在圖4-14中可發現，揮發性有機物異丙醇在此條件下進行光照並無呈現濃度下降之情況，表示本
實驗使用之紫外光365 nm與0.6 mW/cm2之光強度照射下，是無法將異丙醇之鍵結打斷使其分解礦化。
此結果與楊氏(2003)論文研究當中所指出異丙醇之結構主要為C-C鍵、C-H鍵與O-H鍵，要打斷C-C鍵結
需最大波長為346.1 nm，要打斷C-H鍵與O-H鍵則各要有最大波長289.7 nm與243.3 nm之結果相符，可
更加確定本實驗採用之光波長365 nm紫外光之光能量無法直接將異丙醇之鍵結打斷。

%，當二氧化鈦披覆量提高為 4.0 mg/cm2 時，異丙醇光催化效率約 35 ± 2 %。另外，探討甲苯光催化效
率，當進流濃度約為 15 ppmv、當披覆量為 1.5 mg/cm2 時，甲苯光催化效率達約 19±7%，而當提高披

由以上研究結果顯示添加 4.0 mg/cm2 與添加 1.8 mg/cm2 光觸媒光催化效率差異不大，顯示添加越
多的光觸媒可以增加越多比表面積，但是披覆過量的二氧化鈦光觸媒會使觸媒顆粒彼此之間會造成堆
疊，使得紫外光無法透射光觸媒而產生光遮蔽效率，反而無法有效利用光觸媒的活性位置。在李育群40
的研究將兩種二氧化鈦光觸媒( P-25、ST-01) 披覆於紙質、不織布基材上進行光分解異丙醇實驗，在探
討不同二氧化鈦添加量對光分解效果之影響，結果發現二氧化鈦添加越多 BET 表面積越大對異丙醇的
轉化率有提升，但添加量為 13 wt % 時異丙醇的轉化率趨於帄緩，即使添加到 22 wt % 也無太大的變
化，推論當二氧化光觸媒紙質基材之比例為 13 - 22 wt %，光觸媒之光分解能力即達到一穩定值，即使
增加光觸媒的比重，也無法有效增加光催化分解能力，其原因為添加過多的觸媒會使顆粒產生堆疊，
而產生遮光效應，反而不利反應進行。

identify odor emission sources from a complex industrial
district

Introduction
Emission of odorous compounds has been one of the most frequent causes of complaints
in Taiwan, where industrial districts are often in close vicinity to residential and schools due
to the land limitation in urban development. It also presents a difficult task for regulatory
authorities because the extents of odor relies heavily on subjective individual olfactory senses,
as well as the broad spectrum of variability (e.g., time of occurrence, duration, meteorological
conditions) that make source-tracing even more challenging.
The investigative odor tracing technique usually involves olfactory analysis or chemical
component analysis from air samples. Chemical analysis is obviously important because it
gives detailed and conclusive identification of chemical compounds in the sampled air,
however the concentrations of the identified compounds do not necessarily reflect the cause
of the odors. In addition, chemical identification and quantification analyses are highly
temporal and spatial dependent, thus obtaining representative sampling is problematic for
periodic and/or occasional episodes. Consequently, extrapolation of the odorants measured
using chemical methods has rarely been successful. Conversely, olfactory analysis takes
priority to human sensory function over instrumentational analysis, but its role as a regulatory
basis of odor episodes is often controversial because it lacks scientific evidence to confirm the
cause of the episodes.
The open-path Fourier Transform infrared spectroscopy (OP-FTIR) is an emerging
monitoring of exhaust emissions to locate the most probable emitters according to the
available information, including the stationary source pollutants, the manufacturing
characteristics, and the raw chemicals and materials used in the manufacturing processes. This
confirmation step is conducted by using a close-cell Fourier Transform Infrared (CC-FTIR) at
the exhausts of the suspect sites, whose analytical results are subsequently compared with the
ex-situ GC/MS analysis of air samples.

Results and Discussion
1. Receptor odor monitoring by OP-FTIR in the step-1 investigation:
Tables 1 summarizes the results of the step-1 investigation. The OP-FTIR monitoring
showed the continuous presence of dimethyl formamide (DMF), toluene, butanone, ethyl
acetate, acetone, methanol, ammonia, isopropanol, and some chlorofluorohydrocarbons.
Among them, the concentration of DMF clearly exceeded the emission ambient standard,
whereas DMF, toluene and ethyl acetate exceeded their odor threshold values. Furthermore,
the occurrences in terms of both frequency and concentration for most of these compounds
were highly correlated with SW prevailing wind. We also found that the most frequent
complaints occurred when southern wind prevailed, implying that emission of pollutants such
as ethyl acetate, DMF, and butanone was possibly the cause of odor complaints from the
university. In particular, the correlation coefficients between DMF, toluene, and butanone
were nearly 0.6, suggesting that these three compounds originated from the same sources.

3. On-site CC-FTIR analyses in Step-3 investigation
The objective of the step-3 investigation was to uncover the most probable emitters,
using the capability of CC-FTIR to continuously monitor the exhaust concentration, aided
with GC/MS analyses as a final verification procedure. Among the four targeted factories, the
emission from Factory F were consistently to those found in step-1 investigation (DMF,
toluene, butanone, and ethyl acetate), and their emission concentrations were consistently
high during operation. As a result, Factory F was deemed as the primary source of emitter
causing the odor in the university campus.
As a summary, a stepwise investigative method featuring the monitoring capability of
the OP-FTIR was successfully applied to locate the responsible emitter causing frequent odor
complaints from a nearby university. Factory F, which situates in the southwestern direction
of the odor“hotspot”, was found responsible for the odors on the basis of the emission pattern
and concentrations. Factories A, B, and G were partly responsible for the emission of toluene,
butanone, and ethyl acetate. As a conclusion, the OP-FTIR coupled with the systematic
procedure that methodically narrows the potential emitters provides a rapid and effective tool
for tracing emission sources.

Welcome 
Welcome to the A&WMA International Specialty Conference “Leapfrogging Opportunities for Air 
Quality Improvement.” The goal of this conference is to provide an opportunity for the scien-
tific, regulatory, industrial, and environmental communities to share their worldwide experi-
ences in different aspects of air quality assessment and management. 
 
China and other Asian countries need to balance rapid economic growth with environmental 
preservation. Asian air quality management strategies often follow the patterns set by Europe 
and North America, even though more effective methods have been proposed and demon-
strated. Leapfrogging opportunities abound in the areas of multi-pollutant standard setting, 
emissions characterization, air quality monitoring, source and receptor modeling, quantifying 
and valuing adverse effects, emission reduction technologies, and implementation strategies. 
 
Major changes are difficult to implement in a long-established regulatory setting due to a large 
investment in the current infrastructure and established culture. As environmental manage-
ment investments are just being made in many Asian countries, it is possible that these nations 
can demonstrate more efficient and effective air quality management practices and set a fu-
ture standard for countries with more established systems. 
 
The 295 abstracts/extended abstracts from 34 countries included in this Technical Program 
and the Proceedings provide original and recent information on five major topics: 
 
• Ambient Measurements 
• Emission Inventory and Characterization 
• Effects on Health, Visibility, and Ecosystems 
• Air Quality Modeling 
• Energy and Air Quality Management 
 
As a follow-up, authors are encouraged to submit papers by June 30, 2010, for peer-reviewed 
publication in a dedicated issue of the Journal of the Air & Waste Management Association in 
2011.

Track 4: Database Management for Long-Term Visibility Networks – Dr. Bret 
Schichtel, U.S. National Park Service, USA and Prof. Rudolf Husar, Washington 
University, St. Louis (YongNing Ballroom 1) 
The value of air quality data increases as it gets more and proper use in data and modeling analyses and 
assessments. Several Web-based decision support systems (DSS) have been developed to increase distribution and use of air quality 
data. This course presents an overview of how DSS can be used to support real-time and other air quality monitoring networks—
improving program management, communication, and collaboration with users. The availability and integration of data from multiple 
networks and data analysis and visualization tools can be used to enhance and increase the efficiency of scientific investigations and 
implementation of air quality regulations. The course will use the IMPROVE (http://vista.cira.colostate.edu/improve/), VIEWS  
(http://vista.cira.colostate.edu/views/), WRAP Technical Support System (http://vista.cira.colostate.edu/tss/), and other DSS as examples 
with hands-on demonstrations. A brief overview of metadata standards for describing and sharing air quality data and database architec-
tures for storing data and implementation technologies will be presented.  
Bret A. Schichtel received the D.Sc. degree in mechanical engineering from Washington University, St. Louis, Missouri, in 1996, and 
currently is a research physical scientist for the National Park Service, Air Resource Division. He has been involved in air quality research 
for more than 25 years where he has participated in the design and development of Web-based decision support systems facilitating the 
integration, visualization, and distribution of air quality, modeling, and meteorological data. 
Rudolf Husar is a professor at Washington University and director of the Center for Air Pollution Impact and Trend Analysis, for the past 
27 years. He pioneered the application of environmental informatics to air quality issues. 
 
Track 5: Quality Assurance for Air Quality and Environmental Monitoring 
Programs – Prof. Richard J. Tropp and Mr. Richard Egami, Desert Re-
search Institute, USA (YongNing Ballroom 2) 
This course gives an overview of the necessary elements of a total quality assurance program for 
environmental monitoring, with an emphasis on air quality monitoring. The course will discuss the 
major elements of quality assurance and quality control, including infrastructure elements necessary to sustain a viable monitoring pro-
gram that produces data of known and consistent quality. Elements of a quality assurance program to be discussed include: 1) policy and 
objectives; 2) organization; 3) planning; 4) training; 5) preparation and procurement; 6) preventive maintenance; 7) monitoring, sample 
collection, and analysis; 8) calibration and standards; 9) audit procedures and intra- and inter-laboratory comparisons; 10) data  
processing and validation; 11) statistical analysis of data; and 12) management oversight and reporting.  
 Richard J. Tropp is an Associate Research Professor in the Division of Atmospheric Sciences at  the Desert Research Institute. He has 
more than 30 years of management and research experience. Dr. Tropp has taught quality assurance/quality control principles to the 
Republic of China EPA and the Canadian AES. He is currently the Chair of A&WMA's Technical Council.  
Richard Egami is a Sr. Research Scientist emeritus at the Desert Research Institute. He has more than 35 years of experience and has 
led many special air quality studies. He started working in air pollution projects in the late 60s and has seen many changes over the 
period.  
Language Facilitator: Prof. Zhipeng Bai, a Professor at the College of Environmental Science and Engineering, Nankai University,  
Director of the China State Environmental Protection Key Laboratory of Urban Ambient Air Particulate Matter Pollution Prevention and 
Control, and Director of the Research Center for Environmental Science at Nankai University.  
Track 6: Exposure Assessment and Risk Management – Dr. David Harlos, Advantek Con-
sulting, Inc., USA (An Yuan Room 3) 
This course gives an overview of modern exposure assessment methods and a review of risk management approaches. Air 
pollution exposure types, magnitudes, extents and impacts are reviewed as a function of human energy use rates, beginning 
with Paleolithic human habitations. Assessment tools, including modern measurement systems applied in similar cultural  
settings along with archaeological inference, modeling, and climatic evidence are examined. Modern risk management  
strategies include risk evaluation and the precautionary principle. Human warfare and its paradoxical outcome of altruism, provides a 
basis for the risk management approach (and explains its persistent failures). Religious beliefs and moral hazards support the  
precautionary approach. The roles of human psychology, cultural norms, technological innovation, and exponential population growth 
have both enhanced and controlled risk in the continuing attempt to improve risk management success.  
David P. Harlos is a private consultant with Advantek Consulting, Inc., based in Gainesville, FL. He received his Sc.D from the Harvard 
School of Public Health and has 36 years air pollution measurement experience in government, academic, and consulting settings. He 
has worked in the areas of indoor environmental quality, human exposure assessment, air toxics sampling, ambient air monitoring, design 
of air pollution surveys, source emission testing, and development of air sampling methodology.

Mount Cui-

Park 
Located ~20

CuiHua Moun-

King of the Western Zhou Dynasty. The highest peak,

Well-maintained, easily traversed trails with interpre-

Southern Wutai 
 
Located 30 km south of 
Xi’an, southern Wutai is one 
of the most sacred Buddhist 
shrines. This lush and  
beautiful mountain consists 
of five peaks: Qingliang, 
Wenshu, Shenshen, Lingying, 
and Guanyin. Climbing the 
mountain on foot offers  
outstanding views. There are many streams and 
bridges traversing through bamboo with views of the 
temple resting on the mountain’s slope. Ancient 
 edifices that can be visited include the Shengshou  
Temple, Shengbao Spring, ZhiZhu Temple, Guanying 
Dais, and Wufu Palace. It takes 20 to 30 minutes to 
reach the summit by car. Southern Wutai served as a 
resort for the emperors of several dynasties and is 
now part of the Zhongnanshan National Forest Park.

Big Wild Goose Pagoda and the North Square 
 
Located in downtown 
Xi’an, the Big Wild 
Goose Pagoda, is an 
architectural marvel 
standing 60 meters 
high (seven stories). 
The square pyramid 
pagoda, was built with layered bricks and no mortar in

A.D., at the age of 28, Zhang traveled to India to

The North Square, adjacent to the Big Wild Goose  
Pagoda, is divided into nine levels. The terraced 
Square stretches from north to south and includes the 
largest fountain and waterscape square in Asia, Tang 
cultural sculptures, decorated archways, and gardens.

collection includes highlights of Shaanxi province  
culture and documents development of Chinese  
civilization over 7,000 years. The museum collection 
includes 113,000 historic and cultural artifacts  
unearthed in Shaanxi.

Terra–cotta Warriors and Horses Museum 
Located 30 km east of Xi’an, Emperor Qin’s Terra-
cotta Warriors and Horses Museum, “the Eighth Wonder of the World” is a 
world cultural heritage site. It is among the top archaeological excavations of 
the 20th century.  
 
Constructed more than 2,200 years ago to protect the afterlife of the  
Emperor Qin Shi Huang, the terra-cotta army includes > 7,000 life-sized  
warriors, horses, chariots, and weapons. It was discovered by peasants  
digging a well in 1974. The original paint has worn off, and the bronze-age 
weapons (including swords, arrows, and lances) have eroded. The most  
impressive aspect of the site is the scale of the collection even though only 20% of the statues 
have been unearthed. The unique faces of the warriors are said to be modeled after the artists 
who sculpted them and the Imperial Guards of the time. 
 
Han YangLing Museum 
Located ~30 km east of Xi’an near the Xianyang airport, Han  
YangLing is the mausoleum and garden of Liuqi, the fourth emperor 
of the Western Han Dynasty. It has been dated to 2,000 years ago, a 
time regarded as the golden age of Imperial China. The  
archaeological investigation at YangLing started in 1970, but the  
explorations were enhanced during the 1990s when highways were 
built to the Xianyang airport. The  
museum, built in 1999, houses a large number of precious relics 
inside hermetically sealed enclosures. 
 
This large-scale underground museum uses advanced technology 
to protect the cultural relics. The pits are encased with special 
glass that maintains the environment similar to what it was  
before excavation. Suspended glass corridors allow visitors to 
take a closer look at the painted pottery warriors, animals, chari-
ots, and horses.
