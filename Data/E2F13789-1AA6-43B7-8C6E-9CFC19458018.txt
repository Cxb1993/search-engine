Abstract 
Perfluorocompounds (PFCs) are commonly used as etching gas in semiconductor and 
photoelectric industry. However, due to their high global warming potential, PFCs are assigned as 
greenhouse gases and shall be abated immediately.  
The experimental results showed that the addition of additives and the elevation of power 
increased the SF6 conversion. The higher SF6 conversions were achieved, reaching 51.0%, 49.2%, 
48.9%, and 36.0% at the H2/O2/SF6, H2O(g)/SF6, H2/SF6, and O2/SF6 ratios were 5/5/1, 5/1, 5/1, and 
5/1, respectively, at [SF6]in = 2000 ppm, 700 torr and 1.6 kW. Adding additive and increasing 
applied power could elevate the conversion of CF4. At 700 torr and1.6 kW, the CF4 (0.2%)/N2 
mixtures with the inlet molar ratio of H2/O2/CF4 = 5/2/1, H2O(g)/CF4 = 5/1, H2/CF4 = 5/1, or O2/CF4 
= 2/1 had a higher CF4 conversion, reaching 42.6%, 41.3%, 39.5%, and 37.0%, respectively. 
In SF6-containing system, the main product was F2 with trace amount of SiF4 when added 
additive. In addition, toxic HF and sulfur depositions were also found when H2 was added. The 
addition of O2 yielded SO2F2, SOF2 and SOF4, as well as trace amount of SO2 and NOx. When H2 
and O2 were added simultaneously, the formation of SO2F2, SOF2, and SOF4 were suppressed to 
result in SO2 was the only major product (except F2), to reveal that a higher conversion and fewer 
toxic byproducts were achieved. In CF4-containing system, the main product was also F2 with trace 
amount of SiF4 whatever additive was added. In addition, when H2 or C2H4 was added, toxic HF and
