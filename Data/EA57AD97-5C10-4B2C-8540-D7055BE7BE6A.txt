利用迴旋加速器來製作放射性同位素
是為現今國際間最為廣用的方法，其係藉
由迴旋加速器產生具有適宜能量的入射離
子，並與適當的靶材進行核反應來獲致所
需的放射性同位素。目前，國內核能研究
所的同位素應用組擁有一台 TR30/15 迴旋
加速器（compact cyclotron in the Institute
of Nuclear Energy Research，INERCC），
可提供電流為 400A、能量為 15-30 MeV
的質子射束，以及電流為 150A、能量為
8-15 MeV 的氘離子射束。可生產多種醫
用放射性同位素。基本上，在臨床診斷
上，由於目前碘系列核醫藥物的發展相當
成熟，再配合正子照影技術，將十分便利
於臨床診斷的使用。其中 124I 放射性同位
素（t1/2 = 4.18 d； Iβ+= 25%，Eβ+= 2.13
MeV；EC = 75%，E= 602.7 keV，I=
60.5%；E= 1691 keV，I= 10.4%；），
因其在具備正子衰變的碘系列同位素中，
為半衰期較長者，加上其正子的能量為次
低者（僅高於 121I），同時伴隨有兩種能
量的光子，因此利用 124I 核醫藥物，除有
充裕的時間可對患者進行結合 PET/CT 的

行特定的放射治療，因此為近來常用於診
斷與治療的核醫藥物。進而在放射治療
上，因 124 I 具有較強的放射性、較短的半
衰期、以及適於局部區域的放射性量測等
優點，致使其在放射治療的應用上遠優於
131 I。此外，由於 124I 半衰期較短，相較
於反應器生產的 131I（t1/2 = 8.04 d）也具有
較強的放射性，適於局部的放射治療，而

療時間，使得 124I 在放射治療的運用上，
未來將優於 131I，可預期其需求量將與日
俱增 [1]。這也是目前對於 124 I 的需求量
遽增的主因。其中，由於碘的 SPECT 診
斷用同位素 123I 及正子放射性同位素124I，

照射（生產 123I 需使用 124Xe 的氣態靶，

2. Materials and methods
INER’s compact cyclotron (INERCC) TR30/15 is a medically oriented cyclotron built in 1993. The
accelerating beam current has a nominal intensity of 400μA for protons in the energy range of 15–30
MeV and 150 μA for deuterons in the energy range of 8–15 MeV, respectively. There are four
beamlines designed for different production purposes. The present solid target configuration for 124I
production is shown in Fig. 1. It comprises a copper body with cooling-water ports, a silver
supporting plate, and a thin platinum target with a central recess (about 700 μm thick) to plate target
material of 125TeO2. The target material, which is 50 mg/cm2 with 125Te purity of 99.57%, is mixed
with 2% Al2O3 and then coated uniformly on a silver supporting plate within a 40×16×0.7 mm3
recess with platinum-backing for proton bombardment. A cooling water jet is design to cool at the
back of the silver supporting plate. Thus the heat can be carried out during isotope production and
the melting of the oxide layer, which can cause the loss of expensive target material by sublimation
during the irradiation, can be avoided. A series of grooves is machined into the silver plate to form
cooling fins. When the plate is soldered onto the target body these grooves form 16 rectangular
channels (0.8 mm × 3 mm), through which the 293 K cooling water is forced at a high velocity 19
l/min. Usually, the proton beam with selective energy, which hits the target surface at a glancing
angle of 70, can be stopped in a relatively thin layer where it deposits all its energy. The heat
generated in this layer close to the target surface is then conducted to the water-cooled back of the
target plate and into the cooling fins. From there the heat is transferred to the cooling water and
removed.
To significantly reduce the personnel radiation dose during isotope production, the target was
automatically transported into a hot cell in the radiochemical laboratory after irradiation. The
distillation tube is made of quartz and is located in an oven. It is warmed up to 1023 K in steps and
maintained at that temperature for some time. At this temperature the radioiodine was removed from
the tellurium dioxide in a stream of dry air. After some time, the maximum distillation yield is
reached; longer distillation does not lead to higher activities. The distilled radioiodine was trapped in
a row of traps each, two containing 1.0 ml of 0.02 M NaOH solution, and the third one solid CO2.
The radioiodine released is carried with the air stream and is deposited mainly in the first trap (~98%);
a small fraction is found in the second trap (~2%). A detailed dry distillation technique can also be
found in [4].

4. Results and discussion
Fig. 2 presents the calculated excitation function of 124I as a function of incident proton energy.
The results of measured cross sections given by Hohn et al., 2001 are also shown for comparison.
For most of the reactions the agreement between experiment and theory is remarkably good; in the
high energy regions, however, some discrepancies are observed, presumably due to inaccuracy in
estimation of the precompound effect in model calculations. A suitable energy window for 125Te(p,
2n)124I reactions is Ep = 2014 MeV in this work. If the cooling is sufficient, the theoretical yield
over this energy range amounts to 71.6 MBq/μA∙h, which is 10% less than the Hohn’s result over the
energy range Ep = 2115 MeV [2]. The deviation could be mainly from the different excitation
functions between ALICE-IPPE and ALICE-91. Moreover, the decay factor during isotope
separation contributes 3% in this work. Fig. 3 shows the 124I production yield and 125I /124I activity
ratio of as a function of incident and proton energy, respectively. The expected impurities (as % of
124I-activity) are 123I (7.2%) and 125I (0.39%) which are less than Hohn’s results (123I-7.4% and 125I-
0.9%). The 123I impurity will decay quickly for its short half-life. The level of 125I impurity is a
little higher than that for the 124Te(p, n)-reaction (<0.1%). However, the much higher 124I yield
(about 5 times higher yield of 124Te(p, n)-reaction) in the 125Te(p, 2n)-reaction makes this route very
attractive for production purposes. Fig. 4 is the irradiation on-time/off-time schedule analysis. The
results revealed that the highest 124I-yield and lowest 125I-impurity is fabricated under an 8/4 hours on-
time/off-time process.
The 125Te(p, 2n)124I process was actually performed in INERCC under a beam current of 3 μA, but
only 11.1 MBq/μA∙h could be achieved. Obviously, significant amounts of the target material might
be lost from the front face of the target due to the heat accumulated in the target. Using ANSYS, the
highest temperature of the target material is calculated to be 421.1 K. This temperature is still far
below the melting point of TeO2 (948 K). Therefore, it is supposed that some other factors, such as
air gap existed between 125TeO2 material and Pt target, reduced the cooling ability of the system. The
thermal effect of air gap is verified with temperature distribution calculation by ANSYS. Two
models are used to calculate the temperature distribution. Model 1 consider the gap only existed
between TeO2 and Pt in the beam current direction, while Model 2 include the gap around TeO2. The
initial environment temperature in the calculation is 293 K. According to our previous analysis [12],
at the very first 6~7 seconds, the target temperature will increase very fast and then reach its maximum
temperature around 25 seconds. The same results in the case without gap (i.e. gap = 0.0 mm) of
Model 1 and Model 2 prove the calculation is correctly set. The maximum temperature of target is
found near the center of TeO2. Fig. 5 presents the maximum temperature of target material as
function of gap size. As can be seen, the maximum temperature of target is increased with increasing
gap size. When the gap is 0.3 mm, the maximum temperatures are 881 K of Model 1, and 934 K of
Model 2, respectively. The temperature is very close to the melting point of TeO2. In addition, the
