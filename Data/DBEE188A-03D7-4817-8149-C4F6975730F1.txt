(二)英文摘要 
A single crystal GaN thin film was successfully grown on a Si (111) substrate by means of 
metalorganic chemical vapor phase epitaxy sysytem. (MOVPE). The intermediate layer served 
as the buffer layer consisting of AlN、AlN/AlGaN and AlN/AlGaN/GaN Superlattice was adopt 
to improve the quality of GaN on Si. It was found that pits and cracks were not observed on the 
GaN surface and a mirror-like surface was obtained by using AlN/GaN as the buffer layer. The 
full-width at half maximum (FWHM) of the photoluminescence (PL) was 58.1 meV, which was 
the best result between these samples. The background concentration and mobility of this GaN 
thin film measured by Hall measurement were 4.2x1016cm-3 and 350cm2/Vs, respectively. Then, 
the GaN thin film on Si substarte was grown by using the AlN/AlGaN buffer layer and GaN 
metal semiconductor field effect transistor (MESFET) was fabricated by using general process 
technology. The drain current density of GaN MESFET was 20.2 mA/mm with the bias 
conditions of Vgs = 1V and Vds = 6V. This result can be attributed to the better crystal quality 
of GaN on Si substrate by using such an AlN/AlGaN intermediate layer. The maximum 
transconductance (gm) is about 8.01 mS/mm when the gate-source voltage was -0.8V and the 
drain saturation current density can be achieved to a maximum value of 42 mA/mm. And the 
gate-source breakdown voltage of GaN MESFET on Si substrate was about 80V.

(一)前言 
Ⅲ-Ⅴ氮化物半導體是一個直接能隙半導體材料，其可應用的範圍很廣泛，包含發
光元件及電子元件。近年來由於通訊、網路等高科技技術快速發展，在高頻通訊、高
功率微波元件及光電元件等領域的規格與需求皆大大地提昇，因此使得氮化鎵金屬/
半導體場效電晶體(GaN MESFET)與氮化鋁鎵 /氮化鎵高電子移動率場效電晶體
(AlGaN/GaN high electron mobility transistors, HEMT)在高頻、高功率微波領域非常地
熱門。由於氮化鎵材料的化學惰性較大、熱穩定性佳且鍵結力強，故在高溫及高腐蝕
的環境下有較好的抗高溫與抗腐蝕性。在電性方面，儘管氮化鎵的電子遷移率(~1500

(1) 寬的直接能隙(wide bandgap)：Eg = 3.4eV 
(2) 高峰值電子速率(high peak electron velocity)：Vpeak = 2.5×107 cm/s

早期研發氮化鎵材料磊晶技術十分困難，主要瓶頸是因為沒有適合之基板可以來
成長氮化鎵薄膜材料，因此基板與氮化鎵薄膜之晶格常數並不相同，造成界面晶格匹
配相當困難。目前，氮化鎵大部分仍成長於氧化鋁基板(Al2O3)上即 sapphire 基板，選
擇 sapphire 基板作為成長氮化物之基板的原因是因為它可以耐高溫，且可以承受腐蝕
性氣體的侵蝕，價格便宜且容易獲得。然而，氮化鎵薄膜成長在 sapphire 基板上，也
進而造成了一些限制，諸如絕緣的特性、無法製作大尺寸、極端的堅硬等。使用 sapphire
基板的缺點如下所述： 
(1) 價格昂貴，使得磊晶原物料成本提高。 
(2) sapphire 為絕緣材料，製作半導體元件時 P 型與 N 型電極需在同一面，如此一來，
元件使用面積會被迫增加且成本也相對較高。另外使用絕緣的 sapphire 基板亦會
使得元件對於靜電(ESD)的容忍度及抵抗能力大幅下降。 
(3) 大面積的 sapphire 基板製造不易，目前產學界所普遍使用之 sapphire 基板多以 2
吋基板為主，基板面積較小亦會使得 GaN 元件的製造成本提高，目前 sapphire 基

以獲得穩定的蕭基接觸特性，並使元件在長時間熱處理之下仍可以保持良好的穩定不受任
何的影響，所以本計畫仍需針對此 Mg-doped GaN 埋入層應用於金半接面場效電晶體

本計畫係使用Aixtron 2400G3與Emcore D180等商業用MOVPE系統來磊晶成長GaN
磊晶薄膜於 Si 基板上，其中 TMGa(三甲基鎵)、TMAl(三甲基鋁)以及 NH3(氨氣)分別作為
Ga、Al、N 原子的來源。首先，分別使用 AlN、AlN/AlGaN intermediate layer、AlN/AlGaN/GaN 
Superlattice 作為成長氮化鎵(GaN)薄膜在矽基板上的緩衝層(buffer layer)，其中 Al 含量為
0.25，圖一為其示意圖。接著使用光學顯微鏡觀察其表面是否有裂痕缺陷；使用雷射光激
發光譜(PL)量測其發光特性與材料品質；使用 X 射線繞射儀量測其晶格不匹配程度與晶
格應變參數；最後使用霍爾量測儀測量其未摻雜背景載子濃度以及霍爾移動率
(mobility)。由表一可知，使用 AlN/AlGaN 作為緩衝層所成長的 GaN 薄膜沒有任何微細缺
陷，表面呈現鏡面狀態；而其 PL 量測的 GaN 主波長位置為 365nm 處的半寬高為 58.1

AlN/AlGaN 作為緩衝層來成長 GaN，其所產生的晶格應變為最小，故其磊晶品質為最佳，
此種優越的材料結晶特性才會展現於上述的光電特性量測之中。

四、計畫成果自評 
本計畫第一年度利用 MOVPE 磊晶法於 Si 基板上成長氮化鎵 GaN 系列場效電晶體元
件，並成功地利用 AlN/AlGaN intermediate layer 作為緩衝層材料並順利成長氮化鎵薄膜在
矽基板上，而也順利製作出 GaN on Si MESFET，此 MESFET 的三極區與飽和區特性相當
穩定，當閘源極電壓 Vgs = 1V 以及汲源極電壓 Vds = 6V 時，其汲極電流密度將可達到
20.2 mA/mm，這樣的電流密度證實了成長在 Si 基板上的 GaN 之磊晶品質是相當不錯的；
另外，當閘源極電壓 Vgs = -2.0V 時，其汲極電流密度幾乎為零，這顯示了此 GaN MESFET
元件可視為”N 通道空乏型”FET 元件。當閘源極電壓 Vgs = -0.8V 時，轉移電導 gm 會有
最大值為 gm = 8.01 mS/mm，而汲極飽和電流密度最大可達到 42 mA/mm，而此 GaN on Si 
MSFET 元件的閘源極崩潰電壓將可達到 80V 左右，因此確實達成我們所預期之目標。 
儘管如此，在此 GaN on Si 基板的 FET 電子元件研發方面仍有許多地方值得我們繼續
去研究與探討。首先，本計畫年度我們使用 AlN/Al0.25Ga0.75N 作為複合緩衝層來磊晶成長
所需 MESFET 之元件磊晶結構，由結果來看特性均相當不錯，但是使用 AlN/AlGaN/GaN 
Superlattice (超晶格)作為緩衝層的元件特性結果卻不如預期，猜測應該是我們還沒有抓到
此超晶格結構之最佳化條件，因此下一年度會在進一步改善 GaN on Si 基板的薄膜品質。
另外，閘極控制能力似乎仍有改善的空間，本計畫下一年度擬使用 MISFET 或者 MOSFET
來改善此一問題，以上種種皆是我等研究團隊未來須研究與注意的。 
本計畫第二年度利用 MOVPE 磊晶法於 Si 基板上成長氮化鎵 GaN 系列場效電晶體元
件，並成功地利用 AlN/AlGaN intermediate layer 作為緩衝層材料並順利成長氮化鎵薄膜在
矽基板上，另外本計畫亦使用 p-GaN 埋入層作為通道阻絕層，以進一步改善 GaN MESFET
元件特性，具有此一 p-GaN 埋入層之 GaN MESFET，此 MESFET 的三極區與飽和區特性
相當穩定，當閘源極電壓 Vgs = 1V 以及汲源極電壓 Vds = 6V 時，其汲極電流密度將可達
到 32.5 mA/mm，這樣的電流密度證實了成長在 Si 基板上的 GaN 之磊晶品質是相當不錯
的；另外，當閘源極電壓 Vgs = 1V 時，轉移電導 gm 會有最大值為 gm = 13.2 mS/mm，而
汲極飽和電流密度最大可達到 58mA/mm，因此使用此 p-GaN 埋入層可有效改善磊晶品質
與降低元件漏電流，進而改善元件漏電流與崩潰特性、提升元件飽和電流、增進轉導增益
(Transconductance)以及改善元件操作可靠度特性，因此確實達成我們所預期之目標。

2. EXPERIMENTS 
    In this study, p-i-n structures were grown by metalorganic vapor-phase epitaxy (MOVPE) on c-face (0001) 
sapphire substrates. During the growth, trimethylgallium (TMGa) ammonia (NH3) and bi-scyclopentadienyl 
magnesium (Cp2Mg) were used as the gallium, nitrogen and magnesium sources, respectively.For the p-i-n 
samples, a 250Å-thick GaN nucleation layer was first grown on sapphire and followed by a 1μm-thick Si-doped 
GaN layer (nd～1×1018cm-3), a 50 nm-thick In0.06Ga0.94N absorption layer (n～5×1016 cm-3), and a 1μm-thick 
Mg-doped GaN layer(na～5×1017 cm-3). The 1μm-thick p-GaN layer was grown to prevent the depletion punch 
through, because of the low concentration which usually existed in p-GaN layer. Before the fabrication of p-i-n 
photodetectors, Ni catalyst is applied in our samples to enhance the p-GaN Ohmic property. The 2 nm-thick Ni 
films were deposited on the surface. Then, the samples with 2 nm-thick Ni films were annealed in N2 ambient for 
10 min. at 600 ℃ to activate the p-GaN layer. After annealing, the HCl solution was used to remove the thin Ni 
films on our samples. To define the device geometry, the epi-layers were etched down to the sapphire forming a 
variety of devices with different diameters. Then, the mesa was also defined by using inductively coupled plasma

(10/80/10/50 nm) was deposited as the Ohmic contact on n-GaN surface. Finally, the contact pad (Ni/Au = 10/40 
nm) is deposited on both p and n GaN surface. Both p-type and n-type contacts were then alloyed in N2 ambient to

4. CONCLUSIONS 
In summary, the p-GaN/i-InGaN/n-GaN photodetectors have been fabricated in our experiment. By means of 
the Ni catalysts technology, the p-GaN Ohmic contact can be efficiency improved due to the increase of hole 
concentration. The large forward current and low turn-on voltage of the p-i-n photodetectors could be achieved by 
using this Ni catalysts technique. The peak responsivities corresponding to 375nm at 1V is around 0.02 A/W for 
p-i-n photodetectors with Ni catalysts.

1. Introduction 
    Since the first efficient double-layer organic 
light-emitting devices (OLEDs) were developed, many 
efforts have been made to improve the performance of 
OLEDs [1]. It is generally realized that the optimization of 
charge injection and transport is of critical importance for a 
bright and efficient OLED. Towards this goal, the thin lay-
ers of insulating buffers, which consist of LiF, CsF, SiOxNy, 
SiO2, NaCl, sodium stearate, were often introduced to en-
hance the current injection and lower the operating voltage 
between indium-tin-oxide (ITO) electrodes and the organic 
layers [2-7]. 
    In this letter, the (N'-bis(1-naphthyl)-N,N'-diphenyl-1, 
1'biphenyl4,4'-dimaine (NPB) and LiF superlattice layers 
were served as the hole transport layer, which can balance 
the charge injection and transport. The OLEDs with these 
NPB/LiF superlattice layers were also fabricated. The hole 
injection efficiency of OLEDs with NPB/LiF superlattice 
layers can be enhanced and driving voltage was also de-
creased with this insertion of NPB/LiF superlattice layers. 
2. Experimental 
    Table 1 shows the device structure. Devices were fab-
ricated with (α-NPB) as the hole transporting layer (HTL) 
and tris-(8-hydroxyqunoline)aluminum (Alq3) as the emit-
ting and electron transporting layer (EML, ETL). ITO as 
transparent anode electrode and Al as reflective cathode 
were used. In this structure, we inset LiF layer as buffer 
layer in order to improved hole and electron injection and 
reduce operating voltage. In order to enhance the perform-
ance of OLEDs, the LiF and NPB superlattices were intro-
duced between the ITO and Alq3 layer. The superlattice 
structure for device A was LiF/NPB (0.3nm/45nm) which is 
called “one pair”. For the purples of fixed hole transport 
layer thickness, the superlattice structures for device B and 
C were 2 pairs LiF/NPB (0.3nm/22nm) and 3 pairs 
LiF/NPB (0.3nm/15nm), respectively. 
    In all of our experiments, the ITO glass substrates with 
sheet resistivity of 10 Ω/sqr were used and ultrasonically 
cleaned with Acetone, IPA, and de-ionized water. Then, the 
ITO substrates were pre-cleaned with O2 plasma treatment 
for 25min. The organic layer were sequentially deposited at 
a rate 1~2Å/s onto substrates by thermal evaporation at 
base pressure of 1×10-5 Torr. The active area of devices is 
4×4mm2. All devices were encapsulated in glove box. For 
all devices, the current density-luminance was measured 
with Kiethley2600 and DT101 luminance meter at each 
constant current from 0 to 50mA with 0.2mA step. All 
measurement was performed in dark box. The current den-
sity-operating voltage was measured with HP 4156 at each 
constant current from 0 to 20V with 0.1V step. 
    Figure 1 shows the current-voltage (I-V) characteris-
tics of different devices. It was found that when the lumi-
nance density was kept to 1000 cd/m2, the driving voltage 
of device A, B, and C were 4.5V, 3.8V, and 3.1V, respec-
tively. The decrease of driving voltage can be due to the 
fact that the barrier height formed by the insertion of buffer 
layer between ITO and NPB can be decreased with in-
creasing the pair numbers of NPB/LiF superlattice layers. 
When the hole injection barrier height was decreased, the 
recombination rate of holes and electrons can be easily en-
hanced such that the driving voltage can also effectively 
reduced. 
    Figure 2 shows the spectrum of these three devices. It 
can be found that the wavelength of device B was increased 
so as called “blue shift”. Such effect was attributed to the 
lower barrier height caused by the NPB/LiF superlattice 
buffer layers which increased the transport speed and num-
ber of holes. Then the few recombinations can be found 
near the interface between NPB and Alq3. The larger lumi-
nance intensity of device C was also found in Fig.2. The 
tunneling effect of holes due to the more pairs of NPB/LiF 
superlattice layers can result in the large amount of recom-
bination in the Alq3 layers and also in the larger luminance 
intensity. 
    Fig.3 shows the current-luminance density (I-L) for 
these three OLED devices. Maximum brightness in device 
C reaches 25000 cd/m2 while that in device A and device B 
can reach their maximum brightness at 16000 cd/m2 and 
23000 cd/m2, respectively. The slope of B-I curves corre-
sponds to current efficiency (cd/A) .We can see that current 
efficiency of device C is highest among all the devices 
while that of device A and device B are slightly lower. The 
luminance and transport efficiency were both enhanced 
with the increase of the pair numbers of NPB/LiF superlat-
tice layers. The related studies, which focused on more 
NPB/LiF superlattice pair and other new structures to real-
ize the optimum contact electrode for hole transport are 
now under the way.

In a separate paper, Waki et al. used a thin Ni layer to enhance hydrogen desorption on the surface of p-GaN, since 
Ni is a commonly used material to enhance hydrogen desorption in hydrogen storage alloys [9]. With the Ni layer, they 
found that hydrogen concentration was significantly reduced. In other words, Ni could indeed act as catalysts to enhance 
hydrogen desorption on the surface of Mg-doped GaN. In the enhancement results from our study [10], the measured 
hole concentration for the Mg doped GaN was as a function of annealing temperature deposited with and without the Ni 
catalytic films. We clearly proved that the hole concentration has a great increase with 2 nm Ni catalysts at 600 ℃ and 
the Mg doped GaN sample with a 2 nm-thick Ni catalytic film activated at 600 ℃ could provide the largest current. Thus, 
my recent efforts aimed at the activation of the p-type layer by using Ni catalyst technology to enhance the performance 
of p-i-n photodetectors. In this study, the p-i-n structure photodetectors with a p-GaN layer, an InGaN active layer and an 
n-type GaN layer were grown by metal-organic vapor phase deposition (MOVPE). The Ni catalysts technology was 
applied in p-GaN layer to enhance ohmic characteristics of p-type layer. The optical and electrical properties will be 
discussed in the following.  
 
2. EXPERIMENTS 
In this study, p-i-n structures were grown by metalorganic vapor-phase epitaxy (MOVPE) on c-face (0001) 
sapphire substrates. During the growth, trimethylgallium (TMGa) ammonia (NH3) and bi-scyclopentadienyl magnesium 
(Cp2Mg) were used as the gallium, nitrogen and magnesium sources, respectively.For the p-i-n samples, a 250Å-thick 
GaN nucleation layer was first grown on sapphire and followed by a 1μm-thick Si-doped GaN layer (nd～1×1018cm-3), a 
50 nm-thick InGaN absorption layer (n～5×1016 cm-3), and a 1μm-thick Mg-doped GaN layer(na～5×1017 cm-3). The 
1μm-thick p-GaN layer was grown to prevent the depletion punch through, because of the low concentration which 
usually existed in p-GaN layer. Before the fabrication of p-i-n photodetectors, Ni catalyst is applied in our samples to 
enhance the p-GaN Ohmic property. The 2 nm-thick Ni films were deposited on the surface. Then, the samples with 2 
nm-thick Ni films were annealed in N2 ambient for 10 min. at 600 ℃ to activate the p-GaN layer. After annealing, the 
HCl solution was used to remove the thin Ni films on our samples. To define the device geometry, the epi-layers were 
etched down to the sapphire forming a variety of devices with different diameters. Then, the mesa was also defined by 
using inductively coupled plasma (ICP) etching to expose the underlying n-type layer. After conventional cleaning 
procedure, Ni/Au (2.5/5 nm) was deposited as semi-transparent metal contacts on p-GaN surface by thermal evaporation. 
Besides, the Ti/Al/Ti/Au (10/80/10/50 nm) was deposited as the Ohmic contact on n-GaN surface. Finally, the contact 
pad (Ni/Au = 10/40 nm) is deposited on both p and n GaN surface. Both p-type and n-type contacts were then alloyed in 
N2 ambient to form the Ohmic electrodes. 
 
3. RESULTS AND DISCUSSIONS 
Figure 1 shows the measured atomic force microscope (AFM) photograph, which can be used to analyze the surface 
condition of Ni films before and after catalysts annealing. It was found that surface of the Ni catalytic film was smooth, 
which indicated that as deposited Ni film was uniformly. After furnace annealing in nitrogen ambient, it can be seen 
clearly that surface of the sample became rough with numerous island structures. The formation of islands is probably 
advantageous for Ni catalyst, since hydrogen atoms might desorb easier with a larger surface area. The root-mean-square 
roughnesses (Rrms) of samples are 0.748 and 15.259 nm for Ni film before and after annealing, respectively. Fig. 2 shows 
the I-V characteristic of p-GaN layer. We observed that a larger current can be realized after Ni catalysts with a 2 nm-
thick Ni film. The enhancement of p-GaN layer Ohmic contact can be attributed to the improvement of hole 
concentration in Mg doped GaN. After Ni catalysts, the semi-transparent metal contact Ni/Au was deposited onto the p-
GaN layer. Then, we annealed the Ni/Au metal contact in O2 ambient for 5 min. at 550  to improve the transparency 
for Ni/Au. In the literature, NiO, p-type in nature, can be formed by annealing in O2 ambient. Thus, a p-GaN Ohmic 
contact has been fabricated in our experiment. However, the optimal Ohmic characteristic could not be realized since the 
current decreased when annealed above 5 min.

2. EXPERIMENTS 
We first cleaned the ITO glass substrates, with a sheet resistivity of 10 Ω/sqr, by acetone, isopropyl alcohol, and de-
ionized water. The chemically cleaned ITO glass substrates were subsequently pre-cleaned with O2 plasma treatment at 
10 Pa for 25min. OLED devices were then fabricated with NPB as HTL and tris-(8-hydroxyqunoline) aluminum (Alq3) 
as the emitting layer (EL) and ETL. To fabricated the devices, we thermally evaporated the organic layers and metal 
layers at 1~2Å/sec onto the substrates at base pressure of 1×10-5 Torr. The deposition rate and thickness of the layers 
were measured by a crystal sensor, quartz oscillator combined with frequency meter. The active area of devices is 
4×4mm2. All devices were encapsulated in glove box. Table 1 lists the whole device structures. ITO and Al were used as 
transparent anode electrode and reflective cathode, respectively. In this structure, we inset LiF layer as buffer layer in 
order to improve hole and electron injection and to reduce operating voltage. In order to enhance the performance of 
OLEDs, the LiF and NPB multiples were introduced between the ITO and Alq3 layer. Three devices with different 
multiple structures were fabricated. The multiple structures for device A consists of one pair of LiF/NPB (0.3nm/45nm). 
The multiple structures for device B consists of two pairs of LiF/NPB (0.3nm/22nm) while that of device C consists of 
three pairs of LiF/NPB (0.3nm/15nm). It should be note that total thickness of the HTL in these three devices is almost 
identical.

3. RESULTS AND DISCUSSIONS 
Figure 1 shows schematic energy level diagram of the OLEDs with three pair NPB/LiF multiple layers. It can be 
seen that the highest occupied molecular orbital (HOMO) and the lowest unoccupied molecular orbital (LUMO) levels of 
NPB are -5.2 and -2.4 eV, respectively, while LiF serves as an insulator material. It has been reported in the literature 
that LiF with proper thickness deposited on ITO anode can enhance hole injection into NPB [14] since hole injection 
depends strongly on the initial barrier height (IBH) existing at the ITO/NPB interface. The introduction of the LiF buffer 
will enhance hole injection when the IBH is large. These phenomena are explained in terms of tunneling model [15]. In 
addition, electrons and holes recombine in the Alq3 layer near the interface of NPB/Alq3; and the hole is the majority 
carrier due to the fact that hole mobility in NPB is much larger than electron mobility in Alq3, leading to the unbalance 
of holes and electrons in the device. Therefore, we should be able to enhance the emission efficiency by decreasing the 
hole mobility in the device. As shown in figure 1, holes need to travel through the NPB/LiF multiple layers before they 
can finally reach the Alq3 layer where electrons are confined owing to the existence of potential barrier at NPB/Alq3 
interface. In the proposed devices, holes are accumulated and confined in the NPB layers due to the existence of 
potential barrier at the NPB/LiF interface. As a result, we should be able to reduce hole mobility in the device, achieve a 
better balance of holes and electrons, and thus enhance light emission efficiency of the OLEDs.
