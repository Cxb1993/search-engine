中文摘要 
本研究係三年期計畫，主要針對新的鐵基(Fe-Mn-Si)形狀記憶合金做一系統性之研究與
實驗，於目前熟知的 Fe-Mn-Si 與 Fe-Mn-Si-Cr 記憶合金中，適當調整其化學成分比例並添加
適量合金元素來提高其形狀回復率及回復應變量並適度調降形狀回復溫度。研究中應用 VIM
熔煉爐配製不同成分之 Fe-Mn-Si 系列形狀記憶合金，包括 Fe-Mn-Si，Fe-Mn-Si-Cr，稀土型
(Fe-Mn-Si-Cr-Re，Fe-Mn-Si-Cr-RE)形狀記憶合金，間隙型(Fe-Mn-Si-Cr-C、Fe-Mn-Si-Cr-N)
形狀記憶合金與置換型(Fe-Mn-Si-Cr-Ni，Fe-Mn-Si-Cr-Ni)形狀記憶合金等，並探討合金基本
性質、形狀回復特性與熱機處理效應等。 
實驗結果顯示：在FeMnSi(Cr)形狀記憶合金中添加微量稀土元素可有效提升其形狀記憶
效應。添加之稀土元素量不可大於0.2wt%，具最佳記憶性能之稀土元素含量約為
0.10~0.16wt%。含鉻元素之FeMnSi基記憶合金較容易於熱處理時產生晶界相而降低其形狀記
憶效應，添加微量稀土元素可以抑制晶界相的產生，因而呈現較優異的形狀記憶特性。
Fe69Mn25Si6Re0.11與Fe64Mn25Si6Cr5RE0.11二種合金具有最佳之形狀回復率，(Re：錸元素，RE：
稀土混合元素)，經過500~600℃之熱處理後，回復率高達96~97%。熱機訓練可以有效提高
合金之形狀記憶效應，在3%應變及回復溫度600℃下進行熱機訓練第三次後即可得到近乎
100%的形狀回復率。對於間隙型鐵基形狀記憶合金(Fe-Mn-Si-Cr--IA)，當C及N元素添加量
約為0.05~0.2wt%時，Fe-Mn-Si-Cr--IA合金可呈現最佳記憶效應，Fe-Mn-Si-Cr--IA合金於熱
輥壓狀態，因合金內部存有過多差排，其記憶效應未達60%，經過500~700℃之熱處理後，
熱輥壓殘留之內應力逐漸消除並產生再結晶現象，因而大幅提高其形狀回復率至90~92％。
但當熱處理溫度高於800℃時，晶粒快速成長，且由於晶界相析出與合金內部存有較多的ε
麻田散鐵相，因而形狀回復率降低許多；對於置換型鐵基形狀記憶合金(Fe-Mn-Si-Cr--SA)，
於Fe-Mn-Si-Cr形狀記憶合金中添加置換型元素Ni及Cu，合金呈現優良的形狀記憶效應。
Fe-13Mn-5Si-12Cr-5Ni熱輥軋試片經600℃時效2小時後，析出M2C碳化物以及(Fe-Si)Cx奈米
析 出 物 ， 這 些 析 出 物 有 助 於 提 升 合 金 之 記 憶 效 應 。 Fe-13Mn-5Si-12Cr-5Ni 與
Fe-13Mn-5Si-12Cr-5Cu熱輥軋試片經600℃時效2小時後，具有最佳記憶效應，分別達到99%
與96%。 
 
關鍵詞：鐵基形狀記憶合金、熱機訓練、晶界相、熱機處理 
 
Abstract 
The purpose of this three-year project is to systematically investigate the FeMnSi(Cr) shape 
memory alloys. By suitable adjustment of chemical compositions and addition of some alloying 
elements, we aim at improving the alloy’s shape memory effect, recoverable strain and reducing 
the recovery temperature. These Fe-Mn-Si-based alloys, including the Fe-Mn-Si-Cr--RE, 
Fe-Mn-Si-Cr--IA Fe-Mn-Si-Cr--SA, were prepared by using the VIM technique. Their basic 
properties, shape recovery performance and effects of thermo-mechanical treatments have been 
studied. 
Experimental results show that the shape memory effect can be effectively improved by slight 
addition of Rare earth (RE) elements. The adding amount of Rare earth elements should be less 
than 0.2 wt%, and the optimal adding amount ranges in 0.10~0.16wt%. Cr element in the 
Fe-Mn-Si-based shape memory alloys will enhance the formation of grain-boundary phase during 
heat treatment and degrade their shape memory ability. However, slight addition of Rare earth 
elements can inhibit the formation of grain-boundary phase and hence the alloys can exhibit 
excellent shape memory effect. The Fe69Mn25Si6Re0.11 and Fe64Mn25Si6Cr5RE0.11 alloys exhibit the 
optimal shape recovery ratio, 96~97%. Thermo-mechanical training can effectively raise the 
alloy’s shape memory ability. The shape recovery ratio can almost achieve 100% after three cycles

Conclusion 
1. The normalized specimen of SAE 8620 steel exhibits a two-phase structure of α-ferrite and pearlite. 
The 300~500°C tempered specimens exhibit a microstructure of fine tempered martensite. Some 
carbide particles can be observed within the martensite laths for the 400°C and 500°C tempered 
specimens. 
2. The 400°C tempered specimen of SAE 8620 steel is only susceptible to local attack of pitting 
corrosion and can exhibit an excellent corrosion resistance. This 400°C tempered specimen has a 
small weight loss during immersion in a 3.5% NaCl solution, and its polarization curve exhibits a 
high corrosion potential with a low corrosion current. 
3. The tensile strength and elongation of the normalized and 300~500°C tempered specimens will be 
reduced when tested in NaCl solutions. Meanwhile, the elongation of these specimens decreases 
significantly, although their tensile strength does not change obviously if the testing temperature 
rises up to 80°C. 
4. The normalized specimen can inhibit effectively the crack-growth under constant loading because 
of its excellent ductility. Due to the different extent of brittleness, the 300~500°C tempered 
specimens have a sequence of crack-growth rate under loading as 300°C > 400°C > 500°C.

Introduction 
In light of their low cost and excellent workability, the Fe-Mn-Si-based shape memory alloys 
have attracted much attention recently. In contrast to the Ti-Ni and Cu-based shape memory alloys, 
the Fe-Mn-Si-based alloys exhibit a non-thermoelastic martensitic transformation. Their SME arises 
from the reverse transformation of stress-induced ε martensite (HCP structure) into γ parent 
austenite (FCC structure) upon heating [1]. In the past decades, extensive studies of the 
Fe-Mn-Si-based alloys were made focusing on the transformation behaviours [2-4], physical 
properties [3-5], effects of thermo-mechanical training [6-10] and composition dependence of SME 
and corrosion resistance [11-19]. Also, effort is made to increase the use of these alloys, especially 
the “heat-to-shrink” pipe coupling [20]. 
T.Y. Hsu et al. reported that slight addition of rare earth (R.E.) element could improve the 
shape memory performance of Fe-Mn-Si and Fe-Mn-Si-Cr alloys [21-23]. The authors suggested 
that the slight addition of R.E. into these alloys might reduce the stacking fault energy, enhance the 
formation of thermal or stress-induced martensite, lower the TN temperature, strengthen the 
austenite and refine the grain size. These possible effects were considered to improve the shape 
memory performance of Fe-Mn-Si and Fe-Mn-Si-Cr alloys. However, the R.E. always comprises 
many coexisting elements, such as La, Ce, Pr, etc. These coexisting elements might have their 
individual contribution on the alloy’s properties, but their quantities could not be easily and 
precisely controlled. Hence, high purity of Rhenium (Re, atomic number: 75) was used to replace 
the R.E. elements in the present study. The efficacy of Re element is expected to be similar to that 
of R.E. element on the shape memory performance of Fe-Mn-Si-based alloys. By precise addition 
of a single element, instead of comprising many uncertain elements, the exact effects of Re addition 
can be clearly illuminated, and it should be more beneficial for the real applications of 
Fe-Mn-Si-based alloys. Hence, in this study, by slight Re addition into a Fe-25Mn-6Si-5Cr shape 
memory alloy, we aim at investigating systematically the crystal structure, shape memory

Thermo-mechanical training of Fe-25Mn-6Si-5Cr and Fe-25Mn-6Si-5Cr-0.13Re alloys 
To enlarge the effects of thermo-mechanical training, the 900℃ annealed Fe-25Mn-6Si-5Cr and 
Fe-25Mn-6Si-5Cr-0.13Re alloys with a worse ability of shape recovery are selected to execute the 
experiment of thermo-mechanical training. Figure 4 (a-c) and (d-f) show the stress-strain curves of 
the 1~6 training cycles with 2~4% tensile strains and 500℃ annealing for the Fe-25Mn-6Si-5Cr and 
Fe-25Mn-6Si-5Cr-0.13Re alloys, respectively. The variation of these stress-strain curves is 
consistent with that reported in a previous paper [10] for a Fe-30Mn-6Si-5Cr shape memory alloy. 
In the earlier stage of tensile strain, the ε martensite can be induced from the γ austenite by the 
applied stress, through the formation and movement of Shockley’s partial dislocations and 
associated stacking faults. But after a critical quantity of ε martensite has been produced in the later 
stage of tensile strain, the plastic deformation may come from the slip of perfect dislocations 
introduced in the γ austenite. During 500℃annealing for 15 minutes, part of Shockley partial 
dislocations and stacking faults introduced by thermo-mechanical training can still exist in the γ 
austenite although most of them will be annealed out. These residual Shockley partial dislocations 
and stacking faults can be used as the effective nuclei to induce the ε martensite in the subsequent 
deformation. Namely, these effective nuclei will increase the stacking fault probability and reduce 
the critical stress of inducing ε martensite from the γ austenite. This phenomenon is more obvious 
for the Fe-25Mn-6Si-5Cr-0.13Re alloy. But after the starting of stress-induced ε martensite, the 
further formation of ε martensite is more difficult than that occurs in the region around the nuclei. 
Besides, the residual perfect dislocations will also hinder the further formation of stress-induced ε 
martensite and hence the flow stress continues to rise up in this stage. The more the training cycles, 
the higher the flow stresses, and the cyclic hardening phenomenon occurs. As compared in Figure 
4(a-c) and (d-f), the needed stress to induce ε martensite is found to decrease more obviously for 
Fe-25Mn-6Si-5Cr-0.13Re alloy than that for Fe-25Mn-6Si-5Cr alloy. This indicates that the 
Fe-25Mn-6Si-5Cr-0.13Re alloy has a more obvious efficacy of residual nuclei to induce the ε 
martensite than Fe-25Mn-6Si-5Cr alloy. Meanwhile, Fe-25Mn-6Si-5Cr-0.13Re alloy will have less 
amount of residual perfect dislocations to hinder the further formation of stress-induced ε martensite 
and hence the cyclic hardening phenomenon is not obvious as that for Fe-25Mn-6Si-5Cr alloy.

stronger effect of thermo-mechanical training. Namely, the improvement of shape recovery of 
Fe-25Mn-6Si-5Cr-0.13Re alloy is rapider and more effective during the thermo-mechanical training. 
This phenomenon is ascribed to that the efficacy of effective nuclei for inducing ε martensite is 
more obvious for Fe-25Mn-6Si-5Cr-0.13Re alloy. This feature has also been illuminated in Figure 
4(d-f), where the needed stress for inducing ε martensite in Fe-25Mn-6Si-5Cr-0.13Re alloy is found 
to decrease rapidly with increasing training cycle. Meanwhile, the as-called “cyclic hardening 
phenomenon“, which is related to the residual perfect dislocations, does not occur for 
Fe-25Mn-6Si-5Cr-0.13Re alloy even tested at a tensile strain of 3%. Hence, the 
Fe-25Mn-6Si-5Cr-0.13Re alloy can exhibit a more striking efficacy of thermo-mechanical training 
than the Fe-25Mn-6Si-5Cr alloy.
