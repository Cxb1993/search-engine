Since the gas phase and liquid phase have different 
velocities, particularly for a large density difference between 
the gas and liquid phases, more realistic models and 
additional parameters are needed for the pressure recovery 
calculation. Based on an annular-mist flow model 
accompanied with mass and momentum balance, Schmidt 
and Friedel [11] proposed a rather complex formula. 
Attou and Bolle [12] simplified the jet line emerging 
from a sudden expansion as a straight line, and then the 
central flow is confined inside a conical diffuser. By 
applying the momentum balance within the boundary of the 
conical jet for an incompressible and adiabatic flow, they 
obtained a correlation for the two-phase flow pressure 
recovery from a sudden expansion. Their correlation is 
particularly good for small mass velocities, but it is 
inapplicable to high quality flow.   
Recently, Abdelall et al. [13] investigated air-water 
pressure drops caused by abrupt flow area expansion in 
small tubes. The large and small tube diameters were 1.6 
and 0.84 mm, respectively. The measured two-phase 
pressure difference indicated the occurrence of significant 
velocity slip. By assuming an ideal annular flow regime and 
adopting the minimum entropy production (slip ratio S = 
(ρL/ρG)1/3) leads to a reasonable agreement between the data 
and the predictions.    
The foregoing equations were developed for the 
conventional tubes. Up to now, there were no relevant data 
concerning with the two-phase pressure loss data for the 
narrow rectangular channels subject to sudden enlargement 
and contraction. In addition, a detailed physical description 
of two-phase flow across the abrupt flow area change in 
smaller rectangular channels is still not available. Notice 
that two-phase flow across this configuration is very 
common in compact heat exchangers. In this sense, a 
reliable method to predict the abrupt flow area change are 
very needed for the design of compact heat exchanger. 
Therefore, the objective of this study is to study the flow 
pattern change and frictional pressure loss pertaining to the 
sudden flow area expansion in the small rectangular 
channels. The influences of the aspect ratio and area ratio, 
gas quality and mass flow rate are examined.

另外第二場演講是由 Dr. Eckhard A. Groll 發表題目 Performance of Micro-channel Heat Exchangers as Outdoor 
Coils in Unitary Heat Pumps，內容為比較室外機的冷凝器採用 Micro channel 與傳統 fin -and- tube 之性能表
現與評估。實驗測試件為兩台能力為 3.6kW 之單體式熱泵，其中室外機部份採用採用 Micro channel 與
傳統 fin -and- tube 之冷凝器，在不同條件下運轉並比較性能優劣。結果發現採用 micro-channel 之熱泵體
積比傳統式減少約 20%，熱傳能力在操作條件為室內溫度 25 度，室外溫度 7 度時表現最好，原因是因
為該操作條件下熱泵之管內熱傳量最大，故提高整體熱傳效率；而當操作條件為室內溫度 25 度，室外
溫度 0 度時鳍管式熱交換器表現能力較好，原因是因為該條件下 micro-channel 嚴重結霜導致熱傳能力
低於鳍管式熱交換器，因此並不一定是換上 micro-channel 就能提升效率解決問題，而是要多面配合熱
泵才能發揮最大效益。第三場主題為 CFD Modeling of Industrial Ventilation System Making the Most of 
Ventilation in Industrial Facilities，主講人為 Dr. Ray Sinciair。CFD 為近年來研究熱流的新方法，採用模擬
的方式能減少不必要的測試條件，降低實驗的測試成本。而在冷凍空調領域 CFD 也是設計通風工程或
是評估建築物能源損耗的強力工具，該主講人介紹目前 CFD 應用在解決通風工程的問題，並且簡化問
題的複雜程度，達到省時省力。在工業界成本觀念是相當重要的，越能節省成本的公司就能獲得更大
的利潤。

Simulation of Net-Zero Energy Building－Cool Roofs and Cool Gaps in Low Energy Buildings 主講人 Dr. 
William Miller。在北美地區有許多地方以建造所謂綠色建築物和節能建物(NET ZERO BUILDING)，時間
已經有數十年，並且有許多專家利用實驗與理論方式去估算，如何使建築物更節能，例如使用節能建
