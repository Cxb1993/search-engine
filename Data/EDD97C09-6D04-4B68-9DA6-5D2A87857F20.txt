Dielectric characteristics and orientation of piezoelectric (PbZrxTi1-xO3, 
PZT) films fabricated by sol-gel spray and spin techniques 
Chin-Chi Cheng · Tsai-Tien Ho  
 
Received: 13 May 2009 / Accepted: 16 May 2009 
© Springer Science+Business Media, LLC 2010 
 
Abstract The paper presents PbZrxTi1-xO3 (PZT) 
thick/thin films fabricated by spray and spin coating 
technologies. The PZT sol-gel is prepared by mixing PZT 
powder and PZT solution, and fabricated on the 
aluminum and nickel substrates for several layers. After 
drying, firing and annealing, the films were polarized by 
the corona poling technique. The average thicknesses of 
fabricated films were 3 and 10μm for spin and spray 
coating techniques, respectively. The dielectric 
characteristics and orientation of films had been measured 
by LCR meter, XRD for comparison. The crystallization 
of PZT structure fabricated by spin coating technique was 
denser than that by spray coating technique, especially for 
thicker layers. The dielectric constant increased with 
coating layers, and the dissipation factor decreased with 
coating layers. The films fabricated by spray coating 
technique had better performance on these two parameters 
than those by spin one. 
Keywords: PZT film · Spray · Spin · Dielectric · 
Orientation

1. Introduction 
Lead zirconate titanate (PZT) films (>1mm thick) are 
important for microelectromechanical system (MEMS) 
applications [1]. PZT films, thicker than 10 mm, are 
typically deposited by screen printing, spray coating, 
composite film technology and electrophoretic deposition. 
PZT thick films in the range of 1–10 mm thickness are 
usually deposited by sputtering, spin coating, pulsed laser 
deposition, metal organic chemical vapor deposition or 
chemical solution deposition (CSD). Among these 
techniques, spray coating technique was often used due to 
that the PZT films can easily be produced at desired 
locations through a shadow mask made of even paper 
board [2]. The spin coating technique was utilized due to 
its simplicity and excellent reproducibility [3]. In this

2.   Experimental procedures  
2.1 Preparation of sol-gel and fabrication procedures  
The piezoelectric PZT powder was purchased with 
an average dimension of 0.6 μm and dispersed into the 
lab-made PZT solution by the ball milling method to 
achieve the gel [4]. PZT powder was chosen because of 
its high piezoelectricity. The sol-gel was fabricated on 
various substrates (Al plate and Ni membrane) by spray 
and spin coating methods. Fig. 1 presents the flow chart of 
fabricating procedures for these two techniques.   
An air gun was used to spray the sol–gel composite 
directly onto the samples. For spin coating method, the 
sol-gel was dropped onto the substrate and spun at 800 
rotation per minute (rpm) for 10 s and 2500 rpm for 30 s. 
After coating, several thermal treatments, such as drying, 
firing and annealing, were carried out at temperatures of 100, 
430 and 650℃, respectively, with the optimal time duration. 
These steps were used for the fabrication of each layer. 
Multiple layers (1, 3 and 5 in this study) were made in order 
to reach the desired thickness. 
The films were then electrically poled using a corona 
discharging technique [5]. The corona poling method is 
chosen because it can pole the thick piezoelectric ceramic 
film of a large area and on curved surfaces. Finally a top 
electrode is fabricated on the PZT film.  We use a silver 
paste to form the top electrode at room temperature 
instead of vacuum sputtering reported in [6]. Four 
fabricated PZT films on Al plates and Ni membranes 
were shown in Fig. 2. The sizes of Al plate and Ni 
membrane were 35mm-square, 0.63mm-thick and 35mm-
square, 50μm-thick, respectively.   One top electrode was 
put on the Ni membrane surface for the characteristic test. 
The average thicknesses of PZT films for spray and spin 
coating techniques were 10 and 3μm per layer, 
respectively. The thickness difference caused the color 
variation for various samples. In Fig. 2, the film 
fabricated by spray coating technique contained more 
porous structure.

In order to further inspect the effects of fabricating 
methods and layer thickness on the orientation of PZT 
crystallization, the intensity of XRD on PZT (110) was 
illustrated in Fig. 5. In Fig. 5, the intensity of PZT (110) 
on Al substrate increased with coating layer from 1 to 3, 
and then decreased from 3 to 5. The decreasing tendency 
of film by spray coating technique was larger than that by 
spin coating technique. The increasing intensity may be 
due to the increasing PZT crystallization, and the 
decreasing one may be due to the loose structure of 
thicker films. This phenomenon was especially significant 
on film fabricated by spray coating technique.

3.2 Layer thickness effect  
It is also our interesting to investigate how the layer 
thickness would affect the characteristics of PZT film, 
especially on dielectric constant and dissipation factor. 
These two parameters are important for film to indicate 
the charge storage capacity and energy transfer efficiency. 
The dielectric constant and dissipation factor with respect 
to coating layers on Al substrate are presented in Table 1, 
and redrawn in Figs. 6 and 7. From Table 1 and Fig. 6, the 
dielectric constants of PZT film fabricated by two coating 
techniques increased with coating layers monotonically, 
indicating that the charge storage capacity increased with 
layer thickness. From Table 1 and Fig. 7, the dissipation 
factor of PZT film fabricated by two coating techniques 
decreased monotonically, indicating that the energy 
consumption during operation would decrease with layer 
thickness.  For these two parameters, the performances of 
films by spray coating technique is better than those by 
spin one.

4.  Conclusion 
PZT sol-gel was fabricated on Al and Ni substrates by 
spray and spin coating techniques. The PZT films passed 
heat treatments for crystallization. Then, corona polling 
was applied on the film. Finally, the top electrode was put 
on the film surface by silver paste at room temperature.  
From the results of XRD, the variety of substrates 
did not affect the forming of PZT crystallization. The 
intensity of PZT (110) decreased when the number of 
layers was larger than 3. This indicated the loose structure 
of thicker film. The dielectric constant of film increased 
with coating layers, indicating the increasing charge 
storage capacity. The dissipation factor of film decreased 
with coating layers, indicating the decreasing energy 
consumption during transformation. The films fabricated 
by spray coating technique perform better on these two 
parameters than those by spin one.
