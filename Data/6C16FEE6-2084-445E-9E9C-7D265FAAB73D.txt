二、緣由與目的 
工程專案計畫可藉由各個工作項目施
工方案的不同、作業流程的更動及資源（人
力機具等）的配置使得工期有所選擇，一
般而言，工期越長則間接成本越高；而工
期越短則可能代表應用較新通常也較貴的
工法或短期投入更多的資源，因此也意味
著直接成本更高。總括而言不同的工期付
出的成本不同，決策者必須依據自身財務
狀況與計劃所需之完成日期做一權衡評估
後，進而決定專案的預定施工時間與成
本，所以產生了工程專案的時間-成本權衡
問題（Time-Cost Trade-Off Problem）。因
為不同工作項目可能有數項施工方式或方
法可供選擇，而個別的施工法可能又有數
種資源配置方式，兩者組合下產生不同施
作成本與工期，若工作項目眾多或資源組
合複雜則決策者在專案的時間-成本的權
衡考量必然費盡心力。 
離散事件模擬技術有著成本低及效率
佳的優點，符合營建業離散事件及難以實
做之型態，長期以來被應用於建構施作程
序模型以檢驗不同工法的施作速率與成
本，並可進一步測試不同資源配置對工期
或成本的影響，對施工方法新穎通常也較
無經驗時或資源組合較為複雜時，離散事
件模擬技術可做為決策者判斷工期及成本
上的依據，此外由於選定施工方式與相對
的資源配置尚屬於專案的施工規劃階段，
因此許多不確定因素諸如施作時間及成
本，甚至可用的資源數量可能並非相當確
定，而離散事件模擬技術正適合模擬此種
情況下可能產生的各種專案時間與成本，
故可適用於搜尋時間-成本權衡曲線。唯傳
統的離散事件模擬技術用於搜尋時間-成
本權衡曲線時分別存在幾項缺失，第一是
大多數的離散事件模擬技術僅能以單目標
做為考量，如工期（生產速率）或成本等，
進而在單一方案中分析不同資源配置與工
期或成本的變化關係，以達到最佳化配置
之目標，當決策者需同時考量時間與成本
並有所拿捏時，使用者需逐次測試每個可
行解，再藉由模擬的結果一一篩選時間-成
本權衡曲線，此種模式的模擬結果並無法
有效支援決策者立即在資源上做出精確的
配置。第二是少數能同時考慮工期與成本
權衡的離散事件模擬技術僅能針對單一施
工方案中，分析資源配置組合對專案工期
及成本造成的影響，換言之，當工程專案
中有不同施工方案或作業流程可供選擇
時，亦需分別建構網圖模型獨立分析，而
後靠人工方式去分析比較不同模型產出的
權衡解，進而繪製出最終權衡曲線以決定
最佳方案，此類模擬技術雖可有效同時考
慮時間與成本權衡問題，唯當專案可供選

3.3、時間-成本權衡問題之文獻 
時間成本權衡問題自西元1960年被提
出至今，已有許多研究利用不同方法進行
求解，其中包括利用啟發式（Heuristic 
Methods ） 、 數 值 規 劃 （ Mathematical 
Programming Models）及基因演算法等方
法，而上述提及的方法可依據其限制式不
同而有所分類，比較典型的假設類型是將
模型中每個離散作業中的時間成本曲線假
定呈線性關係，而大部份類似的模型皆假
設如此，相關研究分述如下： 
1. Perera（1980）以要徑法配合線性規劃
模型以求解最佳化趕工排程問題，其模
型可考慮作業的可趕工量、專案預定完
成時間、網圖迴路限制等。但該研究有
兩種基本假設，一為作業的工期與直接
成本呈線性關係，二為無論在趕工或任
何情況下，網圖要徑均不得改變， 
2. Demeulemeester（1996）等人利用動態
規劃的觀念求解時間成本問題，該研究
提出新的求解步驟，有有效降低搜尋排
程網圖路徑的次數而得到時間成本曲
線。 
3. Burns 等人（1996）提出混合線性規劃
與整數規劃，先以線性規劃方法找出時
間成本權衡曲線外圍下限值，而後以整
數規劃求解對應於目標工期的最佳
解。然而該研究建立的模式所搜尋到的
外圍曲線，其曲線上各點並非一定在專
案所有的作業選項組合中，亦即其外圍
曲線並不保證是符合實際的時間成本
權衡曲線。 
4. Liu 等人（1996）提出傳統 CPM 排程技
術是在要徑作業上增加資源或資源配
置以縮短專案工期，並在不延長工期的
情況下於非要徑作業上減少資源數量
或選擇較經濟的資源進而降低成本，然
而資源選取及配置是一個複雜的組合
性問題，因此提出結合整數線性規劃及
線性規劃的機制求解工程專案時間成
本權衡問題。 
5. Leu（1999）運用基因演算法發展一個
重複性排程最佳化模型，該模型可在考
慮資源限制條件及資源分享的模型下
求解生產時間、資源數量及最佳化總時
程。 
6. Sakellaropoulos 及 Chassiakos（2004）
提出利用整數線性公式在考慮各作業
的前後邏輯關係及時間限制下，求解時
間-成本權衡曲線並最小化專案成本，
其模型在專案成本的計算方面可考慮
直接成本、間接成本、提前完工獎金及
逾期罰款等因素。 
上述提及之數學規劃技術雖可直接的
求得解時間成本權衡問題的最佳解，但有
著數學模型建構過程費時且容易出錯的缺
點，一般而言，數學規劃技術較適用於求

4.1、基因演算法 
基因演算法是1975年由美國密西根大
學的 John Holland 教授所提出，主要的理
論基礎是根據達爾文進化論的「物競天
擇，適者生存，不適者淘汰」的精神進行
演化。而其在遺傳演化的根據，乃是取其
母體（Population）由於受制於周遭的環境
所能提供的狀況，因而導致適應力較好的
將較有機會成為用來繁衍下一代的親代；
而通常較佳的後代都是遺傳於親代雙方，
而在第二代適應力較好的則亦較有機會被
選 擇 進 行 交 配 （ Crossover ） 及 突 變
（Mutation）。經過不斷的進行演化，適應
力較差的將會被淘汰；適應力佳的將不斷
的演化進而產生更好的後代，而如此的繁
衍進化乃符合達爾文進化論的精神。 
有關基因演算的流程如圖 1 所示。一
般進行基因演算法之計算流程步驟主要是
由編碼、產生初始解（初始化）、評估目
標值、複製選取、交配、突變所構成。而
基因演算法的演算流程除了一開始的編碼
與產生初始解的步驟外，其餘的步驟則是
不斷地重複進行演化的動作，直到符合演
算的終止條件為止。 
 
4.2、多目標最佳化 
時間成本權衡問題與傳統單目標求解
不同，需同時考量時間及成本兩個目標
值，是屬於多目標最佳化問題。一般為了
簡化求解過程大都將多項目標量化成單一
目標以利求解，較為典型的方法可歸納為
多目標權重法（multi-objective weighting）
與效用函數（utility function）兩種。權重
法是求解多目標中最簡單的方法，由決策
者主觀判斷各目標的重要性而給予適當權
重值，進而將多目標轉化成單一目標，但
此法易受決策主觀之影響求解結果；而效
用函數主要是將目標值轉換為同等單位的
屬性以進行計算，而目標的屬性一般都經
由樂透性問題得知，但樂透性問題一般定
義較困難且大多無法由不同決策者得到一
致的答案【Feng et al., 1997】。

5. 突變(Mutation) 
由於子染色體並不一定繼承上一代
之所有特性，而以隨機的方式使得子染
色體的某一基因值隨意的變換成另一值
的情形，稱為突變。而突變在基因演算
的流程中雖只居於次要地位(主要為交
配)，但卻可以引進新的基因樣式，使得
求解空間跳脫局部最佳解，防止了演算
過早收斂，而較容易搜尋到全域最佳
解。一般而言，若為二進位形式之基因
字串，若原基因值為 0 則改變為 1；原
基因值若為 1 則更改為 0。本研究進行
突變之機制如圖 6 所示，進行突變前為
P1、P2，突變後為 O1、O2 之染色體字串。

六、結果與討論 
營建專案之時間-成本權衡問題，是專
案決策者規劃專案時，所需考量之重要問
題之一。但營建計畫所需之工期與成本，
會隨各工作項目的施工方案、作業流程及
資源配置之不同而改變。且若欲選擇較無
實際經驗的新工法時，專案之時間-成本權
衡分析則存在著較大之風險。此外，較大
規模或複雜之營建專案計畫，也就需針對
較繁雜之時間-成本權衡問題，進行模擬分
析。 
因此，本研究計畫依據「混凝土預鑄
構件作業」案例之模擬分析過程與結果，
可歸納出以下幾點結論： 
1. 建構結合基因演算法與離散事件模擬
技術之機制 
使用離散事件模擬技術模擬營建專案
計畫於不同施工方法與資源配置所產
生之工期與成本，再結合基因演算法之
搜尋技術，對於求得專案計畫之權衡
解，確實有其效益與可行性。  
2. 基因演算法搜尋機制 
以基因演算機制取代傳統窮舉法一一
求解之方式，更有效率篩選優良指派方
式與資源數量，縮短最佳化搜尋時間，
提昇求解效率。 
3. 多項施工方案自動化 
電腦模擬程式-GACOST 可自動化讀
取分析多項施工方案之時間-成本權
衡問題，替營建專案計畫決策者提供
了一項實用的模擬工具。 
 
七、計畫成果自評 
在本年度研究計畫中已成功將基因演
算法與電腦模擬技術相結合，以求解時間
-成本權衡問題，並在系統整合方面經測
試亦無缺失。

In order to correctly select the probability distribution for activity duration,

methodology to practical construction application. Moreover, most of the cases, 
historical data for fitting PDF of activity duration can not be accessed. Thus, the 
deterministic duration of activities is adopted occasionally for running simulation 
and thus the uncertainty of activity duration is scarified. 
On the other hand, however, the subjective judgment of activity duration from 
experts is usually used for the estimation of the length of duration in simulation 
experiments. The subjective statements used by experts in estimating task duration 
normally contain some sort of imprecision that is not easy to be modeled in 
stochastic way but is more easily to be represented in form of fuzzy data. In 
addition, there are two merits for adopting fuzzy data in the task of duration 
estimation: (1) no huge amount of historical data are required to be collected; (2) no 
tedious data-fitting tasks need to be performed. However, how to cope with fuzzy 
activity duration in running discrete event simulation is not well explored. Thus, this 
paper proposes the mechanism that fuzzy number can be used to represent activity 
duration when running discrete-event simulations. In addition, the proposed 
mechanism is implemented via reprogramming Construction Operation Simulation 
Tool (COST), which is the discrete-event simulation computer program, for 
facilitating the simulation with fuzzy duration. Details of this new simulation 
mechanism and examples demonstrating the mechanism are provided in the 
following sections.

Conventionally, modeling the uncertainty of activity is usually performed by 
selecting the PDF that best fits based on sample data. AbouRizk and Halpin [2] 
investigated 71 sample durations of construction activities and concluded that a 
flexible distribution such as beta distribution can be used for accurate modeling of 
construction duration data. In addition, Farid and Koning [7] confirmed that beta 
PDF is a better choice for closely fitting the actual truck load and travel times in 
earthmoving operations. Moreover, AbouRizk et al. [4] proposed the moment

square minimization method produces better fit compared to the other two

3.1 Fuzzy number  
 
Fuzzy sets theory was developed to deal with the uncertainties that are not 
naturally statistical [9]. Fuzzy sets are expressed by membership functions. The 
membership degree of an element that is certainly belonging to or absolutely not 
belonging to a fuzzy set can be defined as unity or null, respectively. However, if an 
element partially belonging to a fuzzy set then its membership degree should be 
between 0 and 1. 
A fuzzy set whose membership function has only one distinct peak where 
membership value is equal to one is called fuzzy number. Membership functions of 
a fuzzy number can be assumed shapes or forms. One of the most widely used 
membership function is in triangular shape [15]. The fuzzy number with triangular 
shape membership function is named as triangular fuzzy number (TFN) which is 
adopted in this study for the estimation of activity duration.

8  Conclusions 
 
Simulation should incorporate the uncertainties in task durations that are due to 
uncertainties in the project environment. Traditionally, these uncertainties are 
modeled using PDF by data fitting processes and are highly influenced by the 
availability of historical data. A new simulation mechanism that copes triangular 
fuzzy numbers with simulation is proposed in this paper. This approach allows 
experts estimate task duration subjectively while data fitting process can not be 
performed. Meanwhile, the ambiguity of task duration caused from expert’s 
judgment is maintained while using fuzzy numbers. The effects of different 
defuzzification methods for advancing simulation clock and outputting system 
performance are compared. Examples are illustrated how the proposed mechanism is 
applied. It is found that the results generated are meaningful and show some degree 
of certainty for the estimation of production rate. In addition, the reprogrammed 
computer simulation program, COST, simplifies the processes of simulation with 
fuzzy duration modeled by triangular shape membership fuzzy numbers.

Abstract 
 
This paper presents a mechanism integrating genetic algorithms with discrete 
event simulation technique to solve construction time-cost trade-off (TCT) problem. 
Discrete event simulation technique is used to model construction methods, 
operation processes, and together with the associated resources used in the 
operations. Then, the genetic algorithm (GA) is applied to screen out those 
alternatives with both higher cost and longer duration for a project. Case study 
shows that the proposed GA-simulation mechanism can efficiently locate the 
solutions for TCT analysis. Additionally, this new mechanism is computerized for 
facilitating the task of TCT analysis. 
 
Keywords: discrete event simulation, genetic algorithms, time-cost trade-off, 
multiobjective optimization, construction operations modeling, computer simulation. 
 
 
1  Introduction 
 
Construction project is composed of a network of activities. To perform the 
activities of a project, planners usually have to decide the construction methods or 
technologies, operation processes and the associated resources, which are used in the 
operations, including crew sizes and equipment from different alternatives. 
However, different construction methods and related resource combinations for 
performing an activity create various durations and costs for that particular activity 
and therefore influence the total cost and duration for a project. Hence, planners 
have to face the decisions of finding the most cost effective way to complete a 
project within the desirable duration for a project. These decisions are usually made 
based on the so-called time-cost trade-off (TCT) analysis. The purpose of such 
analysis is to reduce the project cost but not to impact the project duration. The 
results of TCT analysis provide a TCT curve that the associated duration and cost for

3  Genetic Algorithms 
 
Genetic Algorithms (GA) are search algorithms developed by Holland [17], 
which are based on the mechanics of natural selection and genetics to search through 
decision space for optimal solutions. The metaphor underlying GA is natural 
selection. In evolution, the problem each species faces is to search for beneficial 
adaptations to the complicated and changing environment. In other words, each 
species has to change its chromosome combination to survive in the living world. In 
GA, a string represents a set of decisions (chromosome combination), a potential 
solution to a problem. Each string is evaluated on its performance with respect to the 
fitness function (objective function). The ones with better performance (fitness 
value) are more likely to survive than the ones with worse performance. Then the 
genetic information is exchanged between strings by crossover and perturbed by 
mutation. The result is a new generation with (usually) better survival abilities. This 
process is repeated until the strings in the new generation are identical, or certain 
termination conditions are met. Fig. 1 demonstrates the general structure of Genetic 
Algorithms. Goldberg [18] and Gen and Cheng [19] are good references for details 
regarding genetic algorithms. 
GA has also been known for its flexibility in hybridizing with other 
methodologies to obtain better solutions [19]. Hence, the GA is adopted to integrate 
with the simulation methodology to find the optimal solutions in TCT analysis. The 
following sections introduce the GA-simulation mechanism and describe the 
components and process of the GA for optimizing the scheme of the simulation 
model in detail.

8  Summary 
 
Time-cost trade-off analysis is one of the most important aspects that planners 
have to face for planning a construction project. Time and cost are traded off by 
selecting construction technologies, operation processes, and the associated resource 
combinations among different alternatives. However, many construction 
alternatives, especially those for the new construction methods, have not been 
actually practiced before they are selected as the construction method. It is risky to 
directly apply them in the TCT analysis. In addition, the more complicated the 
project, the larger the scale has to be faced for the TCT analysis. Using discrete 
event simulation technique and genetic algorithm, the authors successfully 
simulating construction alternatives to obtain more reliable data used in the TCT 
analysis and moreover, the TCT curve is efficiently located by genetic algorithm. 
Additionally, the computer program, GACOST, which automates the execution of 
the new approach, provides a practical tool for construction project planner to apply 
GA-simulation mechanism to practice.
