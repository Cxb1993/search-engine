本年度預計之工作為針對磁性粒子之基本

2.1 實驗裝置架設與理論模式 
2.1.1 實驗裝置架設 
 本實驗設備有光學顯微鏡平台、可程式
電源供應器、高斯計、磁性流體、溶媒、
Helmholtz 線圈、深為 0.43mm 凹槽載具、
凌鏡、電腦。將本研究實驗設備簡單繪製成
圖 2-1 所示，利用此架構可以有效觀察到磁
性流體液滴浸入於可互溶流體薄膜層中且
施加均勻垂直磁場時，會產生三維的尖峰和
二維的擴散界面不穩定現象同時存在。本研
究中主要目的為了有效觀察到磁性流體液
滴浸入於可互溶流體薄膜層中且施加均勻
垂直磁場時，會產生三維的尖峰和二維的擴
散界面不穩定現象同時存在，如圖 2-2 所
示。 
    最初磁性流體液滴直徑為 d，中心厚度
最大約為 0.2 mm，其液滴被置入於深度
h=0.43 mm 透明壓克力凹槽且裡面充滿可互
溶非磁性流體，以至於磁性流體液滴能夠完
全浸入薄膜流體層中。所使用磁性流體為輕
礦油基 EMG901，是由 Ferrotec Corporation
所生產的，此磁性流體的黏滯度 µd =10 cp、
密度ρd =1.53 gm/ml 和飽和磁化率 Ms=660 
Oe。然而非磁性可互溶流體是使用特定的礦
物油，也就是由 Ferrotec Corporation 所
提供的 EMG901 之載基流體，其黏滯度 µm =4 
cp 和密度 ρm=0.89 gm/ml。均勻垂直磁場
是藉由可程式電源供應器提供固定電流給
一對 Helmholtz 線圈所產生，而電源供應器
在開啟的瞬間會產生一均勻的磁場，其電源
供應器之電流是維持固定，因此線圈所產生
的磁場強度為 H=755 Oe。可互容的兩個流
體在擴散界面發展過程中，藉由 CCD 攝影機
從正視方向直接錄製整個運動情形，除此之
外，還利用凌鏡的反射可以觀察到側面的羅
森史維格尖峰。然而在 CCD 攝影機上加裝光
學鏡頭，以至於能夠清晰得觀察到微細界面
運動，最後經由傳輸線連結到電腦，將不穩
定現象利用 MATLAB 程式語言來直接量測觀
察分析，以得到精準的實驗數據結果。

2.2 第一年度成果與討論 
2.2.1 實驗成果與討論 
本研究我們主要探討以磁性流體液滴
浸入於可互溶流體薄膜層中且施加均勻垂
直磁場，會產生一個尖峰和複雜性不穩定的
混合類型現象。然而在實驗過程有兩種類型
同時被探討的，對於不同條件影響因素之
下，所產生不穩定現象分別以定性和定量上
分析之：（I）自由表面流場類型，也就是允
許羅森史維格尖峰衝出可互溶非磁性流體
層。（II）受限制流體流場，在流體表面似
乎加一個蓋子，此流場空間形成受限制，主
要觀察探討羅森史維格尖峰受到抑制之情
形。

2.3 第一年度結論 
我們實驗研究可互溶磁性流體液滴浸
入於薄膜流體層中且施予固定垂直磁場之
界面不穩定現象，實驗結果明確地指出彼此
相互影響關係，是關於羅森史維格尖峰的形
成和流體間的互溶自然現象、以及同時發生
在小厚度薄膜流體層裡的可互溶磁性流
體，導致一個新的磁性流體不穩定現象混和
類型同時產生，且揭開一個令人有趣的動態
現象變化和大量紋路形成的神秘面紗，我們
觀察到在系統裡的擴散、流動、對流和磁場
之物理過程似乎影響紋路結構形成。然而，

Instabilities of Ferrofluids Immersed in Fluid Layer

1 Introduction and experimental setup  
Ferrofluids are colloidal suspensions of nanometer-sized magnetic particles suspended 
in a nonmagnetic carrier fluid. They behave superparamagnetically and can easily be 
manipulated with external magnetic fields. The interesting interplay between 
hydrodynamic and magnetic forces makes ferrofluids a fascinating material to study a 
variety of interfacial instabilities and pattern-forming processes[1]. When a ferrofluid 
is subjected to a sufficiently strong vertical magnetic field, an array of visually 
striking peaks is formed on its free surface. This effect is known as the Rosensweig 
instability [2-7]. However, if the ferrofluid is trapped between two narrowly spaced 
glass plates and a vertical field is applied, highly branched, effectively 
two-dimensional structures arise, characterizing the so-called labyrinthine instability 
[8-13]. Despite the rich physics and the great variety of situations explored both 
theoretically and experimentally in the study of the peak and labyrinthine instabilities 
in ferrofluids, they are commonly presented as two compelling, but disjointed 
physical phenomena. Recently, an ingenious experiment had been performed in which 
peak and labyrinthine patterns coexist and share a coupled dynamic evolution [14]. 
This has been done by immersing a ferrofluid drop in a miscible nonmagnetic fluid. 
Under such miscible circumstances a peak first grows rapidly, and then gradually 
decays, to ultimately reimmerse into the surrounding nonmagnetic layer. This unique 
scenario implies in the simultaneous emergence of peculiar labyrinthine structures 
within the thin nonmagnetic layer. The discovery defines a new hybrid-type interfacial 
instability in ferrofluids. In order to better understand this new hybrid 
ferrohydrodynamic instability, in this work we present more detailed experimental 
results under the situations of different fluid combinations, which include both in a 
miscible and an immiscible environment.

condition, numerous peaks of equal sizes are generated. In addition, the peaks would 
drift outward due to the magneto-repulsive forces. The formation of peaks by different 
means, such as a major single peak of the re-immersed ferrofluids within the fluid 
layer and numerous tiny peaks from the residual ferrofluids on the surface, leads to 
another kind of hybrid instability, or dual Rosensweig instability, when the field is 
reapplied.  
3. Summary 
We present a systematically experimental study of a different pattern-forming 
instability occurring when a ferrofluid droplet is immersed in a thin layer of a 
nonmagnetic fluid, and subjected to a uniform perpendicular magnetic field. For 
miscible droplets with various initial diameters, the development of a hybrid-type 
ferrohydrodynamic instability, where peak and labyrinthine ferrofluid patterns coexist 
and share a coupled dynamic evolution, is confirmed. The patterns would evolve from 
an in-plane labyrinthine dominant regime for a smaller droplet to a prominent third 
dimensional Rosensweig instability with multi-peak formation for a larger droplet. On 
the other hand, labyrinthine instability is not observed under the present experimental
