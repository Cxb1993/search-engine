2 Materials and Method 
2.1 Subjects 
Twenty-four female university students participated in the study. Their mean age is 
20.6 years (range 19-27). All subjects were right-handed and non-touch typists. All 
had normal or corrected visual acuity of 1.0 arcmin-1 or better and were right-handed. 
Their demographic data were summarised in Table 1.

2.2 Apparatus 
An adjustable VDT workstation (see Fig. 1) was employed in this study. The desk 
height and desk tilt can be electrically adjusted. The adjustable range for the desk 
height is from 80 cm to 120 cm. The adjustable range of the desk tilt is between 0 (flat) 
to 30 degrees. 
Three single/twin-axis goniometers (Biopac, USA) were used to record the neck 
flexion, elbow flexion and wrist extension angles dynamically. Muscular loads were 
registered by a surface electromyography (sEMG) (Telemyo, Noraxon USA Inc.) on 
right extensor carpi ulnaris, right biceps, deltoideus (both sides) and trapezius (both 
sides) [5]. Gaze angle to monitor centre and the other workstation preferred settings 
were measured manually. 
2.3 Experimental Design 
The explored factors including monitor size (MS) of laptop (two levels, 12 and 14 
inch), initial desk height setting (IDHS) (two levels, i.e. from the highest/lowest 
position), and initial desk tilt setting (IDTS) (three levels, from 30 to preferred, flat, 
from 0 to preferred). All the three independent variables were treated as within-
subjective variables.
