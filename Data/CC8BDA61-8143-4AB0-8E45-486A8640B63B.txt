3.2 個人健康記錄
個人健康記錄或個人健康歷（Personal Health Record, PHR），是由紙本病歷、電腦化病
歷、電子病歷演變而來[14]，目前有多種解釋，但都類似於以個人為中心的健康記錄。麥思
馬提卡政策研究公司（Mathematica Policy Research Inc.）[15]認為個人健康記錄是一個廣泛
的紙本或電子式系統，用於記錄個人的健康有關的訊息。醫療提供者和病人雙方可以輸入
訊息到記錄中。病人擁有個人健康檔案和控制存取的權利。馬可基金會（Markle Foundation）
[16]認為是一種電子應用，個人可以在一個安全和保密的環境中，評估、管理和共享他們的
健康訊息。它使人們能夠存取和調整他們畢生的健康信息，和提供適當的部分給需要它的
人
Tang[17]認為個人健康記錄包括由個人所管理的健康資訊，是一種可以幫助維持健康的
工具。個人健康記錄可以在病患授權下，於健康照護者、病患所使用的系統間自由的流通。
Sittig[18]認為以網際網路為基礎的個人健康記錄，包括可由網路存取的任何應用，它使得病
患或其照顧者能夠檢閱、註解、建立、維持和他健康相關的任何記錄，例如藥品、醫療問
題、過敏、疫苗接種史、病歷或與醫療提供者通訊。美國衛生及公共服務部（Department of
Health and Human Services）[19]則提出了個人健康記錄與個人健康記錄系統的潛在利益，如
更容易了解健康問題、提高控制健康的意識、加強個人健康訊息的獲得、及時的支援、適
當的預防保健服務、加強與醫療提供者的溝通、驗證醫療提供者記錄的準確、支援家庭監
控慢性疾病、理解和適當使用藥物、保險福利管理和理賠、避免重複檢驗、減少藥物不良
相互作用和過敏反應等。
3.3 服務導向架構
SOA是以企業的服務流程為考量的系統架構，SOA代替了過去以純物件導向元件的系
統開發方式，將企業的應用程式和資源，改採Web Services來進行，系統元件間利用介面進
行呼叫存取，並使用標準的訊息做資料交換，可隨時依企業的流程服務的改變來做組合。
SOA近年來已被用來整合異質平台，例如：Microsoft的.NET、SUN的J2EE，都可以透過Web
Services的HTTP、SOAP、XML等標準協定來彼此溝通、相互存取交換資料、降低系統的開
發成本[20]。
網路服務(Web Services)透過Web 通訊協定及資料格式的開放式標準如HTTP、XML
6告與網頁內容明顯區隔[26]。每年衛生署會根據上述這些標準來評斷健康網站是否符合標
準，若符合會給予優良健康網站標章，並根據網站種類分為六大類為一般健康資訊網站、
大型醫療院所網站、小型醫療院所網站、醫療院所單科網站或主題網站、診所及個人網站、
基金會、學會或協會網站、縣市衛生局網站、衛生署所屬機關及其分局網站[27]。同時衛生
署會將得獎健康網站設置在優良健康網站入口網裡，讓民眾方便點選。
Web Spider網路搜尋可說是網路時代不可或缺的一項便利服務，各大軟體廠商莫不投入
巨額的資金和人力，相繼開發功能更強的搜尋引擎（Search engine），使得全文檢索變得更
加方便迅速。例如Google、Yahoo!奇摩、AltaVista等國內外著名網站，都是利用類似技巧來
建立搜尋引擎，提供使用者搜尋服務。一般的資訊搜尋策略普遍使用的是Web Spider又別名
為Ants、Automatic Indexers、Bots、Crawlers、Web Robots及Web Scutters是一種會有條理次
序地來『自動瀏覽網路』的電腦程式[28-30]。Web Spider能攀爬到各個網站，並搜尋其所需
的資訊。最初，Web Spider會從種子網頁（Seed URL）地址上獲取鏈結，然後使用這些鏈結
作為下一組要進行處理的頁面參照，而他下載了這些網頁之後，就會得到更多鏈結。Web
Spider下載的第一個頁面被稱為第一滲透層（The First Penetration Level）。在接下來的每一
個滲透層中，Web Spider都會根據前一層所得到的鏈結，繼續下載更多的網頁直到它達到了
最大滲透層的極限為止。但實際上，基於儲存和處理技術問題，頂多抓取整個網站內容的
百分之四十左右[31, 32]。Web Spider擷取到的網頁資料會先被複製到特定伺服器上的資料庫
儲存，接著建立網頁索引，然後依據關鍵詞對網頁進行分類、歸檔、最後依該網頁被鏈結
檢索的頻率加以排序。此外，Web Spider程式必需比對目前和庫存網頁內容，如果相同檔名、
但內容有所異動者，即會將新的網頁回傳以更新資訊，讓用戶能搜尋到最新的網頁資料。
而Page Rank又別名為網頁級別、Google左側排名或Page Rank排名。Google創辦人Larry
Page將Page Rank定義為「能做到確實瞭解使用者想要的東西，並確實提供對應的資訊」
[33]。由上述可知網頁資訊取得的重要性與需求性，使得減少使用者在網際網路搜尋資訊時
的時間。而Google的Page Rank演算法是當A頁面連結到B頁面時，A頁面投票給B頁面去做
計算。並根據超連結的關係，當連結到B頁面越多時，B頁面的等級相對提高，反之，等級
越低或沒有等級。因此等級越高時，代表搜尋的價值越高，越容易被放置在搜尋結果上的
頂端。但是此種演算法有一個缺點為舊網頁的PR會比新網頁的PR來的高，即時新網頁的價
值比舊網頁高還是一樣比舊網頁的PR低。在學者Hany M. Harb研究有關搜尋引擎和網路排
名結果中提到Page Rank的排名方式不同於Google排名演算法，是以使用者的資訊與關鍵字
資訊來納入考量的計算方式。同時參考網頁的點擊率和關鍵字的出現間隔[34]。
3.5 科技接受模型
Technology Acceptation Model（TAM）科技接受模型[35, 36]，又稱技術接受模型，由
Davis於1989年所提出，常用於預測及解釋使用者對於新系統的接受程度，該理論模型是以
理性行動理論和計劃行為理論發展而來。理性行動理論（Theory of Reasoned Action, TRA）
[37, 38]主要用於預測與解釋人類的態度和行為。Fishbein及Ajzen認為人類的行為會受他的
行為意圖（Behavioral Intention）所影響，而行為意圖又會同時受到個人的行為態度（Attitude
toward Behavior）與主觀規範（Subjective Norm）二者的影響。Ajzen又基於理性行動理論，
提出計劃行為理論（Theory of Planned Behavior, TPB）[39, 40]，認為人的行為是由個人的意
志力控制，人的行為意圖會再受到第三個因素－感知行為控制（Perceived Behavioral Control,
PBC）所影響。

5.3 網路健康知識查詢系統
本研究第三階段，除了進行系統評估之外，也依據使用者的需求，繼續修改擴充系統，
其中一個主要的功能，就是連結外部網站，協助個案或是個案管理師能夠獲得健康知識。
使用者在登入系統後，可以使用關鍵字查詢的功能。系統會連上伺服器並提供使用者
介面，當使用者在使用者介面輸入關鍵字後按下查詢按鍵，系統會將使用者輸入的關鍵字
傳到 Refine Agent。其主要功能是將使用者輸入的字串將不具意義的字去除，成為真正的關
鍵字，如：的、了、嗎等。Refine Agent 得到精簡過的字串後，再將關鍵字送往搜尋引擎和
資料庫。本模組為整個搜尋過程的核心，系統會先比對資料庫裡是否有建立符合關鍵字的
索引值，索引表內的欄位應包含 URL 和從網頁的<head>內<title>和<body>標籤內抓取的關
鍵字和內容，若沒有則會回應到網路程式，並回傳連結網址至 Ranking Agent 以五大標準及
網址點擊次數、關鍵字出現字數、健康優良網站六大類來排名網址。平時 Web Spider 程式
也會定時上網爬行檢查是否有新的 URL，如果發現新的 URL 會新增資料庫的索引值。
22
Codes 來找尋醫療資訊網站。同時，也會有 Page Rank 方式來呈現給使用者。以達到正確及
快速的醫療資訊查詢方式。
在開發 Web Spider 程式時會遇到有些網頁文字編碼的問題。通常編碼有 Big-5 碼、utf-8
等。很少用到西歐語系的編碼。當 Web Spider 遇到這種西歐語系編碼時，會無法存入資料
庫，並且超出執行時間。因此對於這種編碼的網頁，當執行過久，就會選擇下一個網頁繼
續取得網頁內容，而資料庫會刪除此網頁的索引值。在下一次在執行 Web Spider 程式時，
遇到西歐語系的編碼時會選擇避開此編碼。
5.4 系統評估
本研究對系統使用者進行簡易滿意度評估。主要分為兩個部分：上線初期評估及使用
一年後之評估，在建置初期先對使用者進行量性評估，了解使用者的滿意度，建置一年後，
再進行開放式問卷對使用者進行評估。
本研究在建置初期對於使用者進行評估，評估方式為使用者使用系統一段時間後，請
使用者填寫問卷，從問卷中了解社區式之遠距照護服務系統，是否會影響使用者使用健康管
理系統的意願與滿意度。由於填寫問卷皆為長者，因此問卷在設計上與題數上已精簡為主，
每一假說設計以兩題為主，在文句敘述皆淺顯易懂，使長者不會有壓力及不悅感，評估方法
針對上述來設計問卷，對使用者施測，問卷題目設計採用李克特（Likert）五點尺度量表，
對每一假說設計出數道題目，讓使用者填寫，無法填寫者，請當地管理人員以口述方式進行，
抽樣對象為實際使用過本系統的社區居民。
本研究在建置約一年後對於使用者進行評估，評估方式為開放性問卷方式，詢問使用
者遠距照護服務系統的易用性、有用性、使用態度、使用意願、隱私權及照顧品質，以深入
了解使用者對於遠距照護服務系統的接受程度。由於填寫開放性問卷大多為長者，研究人員
訪談受測者(長者)以口述方式進行，向受測者統一說明問卷內容，將受測者口述之訪談內
容，利用錄音設備記錄並由本研究人員整理書寫成訪談資料。
本研究於 2009 年 5 月進行問卷發放， 評估對象與一般自願配合填寫問卷的對象不同，
需考慮長者的配合程度與耐心，在問卷設計與題數上以精簡為主，問卷題項分配與內容包括
系統特性 2 題、感覺有用性 2 題、感覺易用性 2 題、使用態度 2 題和使用意願 3 題，共計
11 題，問題如表一。因高齡長者從未使用過電腦，評估方式為長者量測血糖血壓時，由研
究者代為操作個人健康記錄整合系統，展示其歷史記錄、趨勢圖給長者觀看，並由個案管理
師詢問長者最近的健康狀況，及注意事項後，由研究者以口述詢問方式，請長者配合做問卷
調查。

Abstract—Population aging is an important issue in Taiwan.
The percentage of elderly members of the population has been
growing quickly during recent years. The requirements of
long-term care for chronic and functionally impaired patients
are rapidly increasing. Beside medical treatment, the needs of
long-term care facilities are also continuously increasing.
Among the variants of long-term care institutions, the quality
of healthcare service varies widely and lacks an objective
assessment indicator. In order to manage healthcare quality,
Taiwan Long Term Care Association has defined six quality
indicators including number of falls, nosocomial infections,
use of physical restraints, non-planned weight gain or loss,
pressure ulcers and non-planned transfers to acute hospitals.
In order to facilitate, collect and calculate the indicators, the
Association cooperates with us to implement a service system.
The architecture of the system has four components including
Data Collection Subsystem, Report Generation Subsystem,
Aggregation and Analysis Subsystem and System
Administration Subsystem. In this paper, we introduce and
share our experience of the system's development.
Keywords long term care; nursing home; quality
indicators; web-based system.
I. Introduction
The United Nations has defined an “aging society” as a 
population in which the percentage of people over 65 years is
greater than 7 percent; when that percentage increases beyond
14 percent, the population is deemed an "aged society." The
population of Taiwan is about 23.4 million. According to a
government estimation report [1], the number of senior
citizens over 65 accounted for 10.7 percent of the nation's total
population in 2010. The report also predicted that the
proportion would exceed 14 percent by 2017, meaning that the
nation will become an aged society at that time, and then
would exceed 20 percent by 2025. The average life expectancy
is 76.1 years for men and 82.7 years for women in 2010; the
report predicted that the average life expectancy would be 78.3
years for men and 84.9 years for women in 2020. The burden
of supporting the healthcare expenses for the elderly
population is significant. According to statistical data from the
Bureau of National Health Insurance of Taiwan, in 2006, the
average amount spent by the elderly population for health
insurance was about 7 times that of other age groups [2]. In
2010, there are 1089 long-term care and elderly care facilities
in Taiwan with about 41000 residents with an average of 2.15
residents per staff [3].
The Healthcare Indicator is the measurement for patients' care
quality. According to the Joint Commission on Accreditation
of Healthcare Organizations (JCAHO), an indicator is a
quantitative measure of an aspect of patient care [4]. Because
the quality is difficult to quantify, using more objective
evidence for monitoring is necessary. Although the quality of
healthcare is difficult to measure, it can take certain problems
into account. The development of long-term care quality
indicators is based on the JCAHO and the Taiwan Quality
Indicator Project (TQIP) definitions. The Association
continuously modified the evaluation items. The latest version
was finished in 2009 and was promoted as the basis of the
institution's quality assessment.
There are many studies in the quality of care for nursing home.
Chia-Ling Yang suggested seven important indicators of
quality of nursing homes including environment, nursing
professional competence, quality assurance, basic human
rights, direct care attitude, social satisfaction [5]. Ming-Nan
Kao investigated on aged care quality of nursing home [6].
Chen Li-Chuan used observable indicators of nursing home
care quality instrument to measure quality of nursing home
care [7]. Noemi C. et al. tested an Internet report card
containing information about quality indicators and assessed
consumer satisfaction and quality in nursing homes [8].
Carolyn J. Greena et al. suggested that Information system
support is a critical success factor for chronic disease
management [9].
The Taiwan Long Term Care Association has defined six
indicators for monitoring long-term care quality including
number of falls, nosocomial infections, use of physical
restraints, non-planned weight gain or loss, pressure ulcers and
non-planned transfers to acute hospitals [10, 11]. The
indicators are reviewed frequently and have been the
institution assessment requirements since 2010. Each indicator
has a number of items that are reported each month. The data
were entered into MS-Excel worksheets and collected through
Email or CD-ROM initially. It always took some time in
routine data entry and report gathering each month; in order to

B. Data Collection Subsystem
The raw data of indicators are input from the Data Collection
Subsystem. All of the indicator definitions and formulae are
shown on the screen to avoid user error. The subsystem may
identify where the user comes from to prepare the data entry
environment. Because each indicator is only saved on one
record per month, the system will check the system time and
retrieve the data from the previous month from the database
and display it on the screen. Users may modify the indicators,
generate monthly reports or input new data directly. Users may
also choose to calculate, confirm or cancel each data entry.
The calculate function calculates the ratios for each indicator
followed by predefined formulas. Because each ratio could not
be larger than 100 percent, the system may check each ratio
item and alert the mistake values. When the confirmation
button is clicked, the system checks if the month's data exists
in the database and perform a data update or append a database
record.
C. Report Generation Subsystem
After entering the indicator items, the system may generate
reports. We followed the report format defined by Taiwan
Long Term Care Association to design the system. When users
login to the system, he/she may select a yearly report or a
monthly report to be generated. The system retrieves indicator

Abstract—Population aging is an important issue in Taiwan. The 
percentage of elderly members of the population has been 
growing quickly during recent years. The requirements of long-
term care for chronic and functionally impaired patients are 
rapidly increasing. Beside medical treatment, the needs of long-
term care facilities are also continuously increasing. Among the 
variants of long-term care institutions, the quality of healthcare 
service varies widely and lacks an objective assessment indicator. 
In order to manage healthcare quality, Taiwan Long Term Care 
Association has defined six quality indicators including number 
of falls, nosocomial infections, use of physical restraints, non-
planned weight gain or loss, pressure ulcers and non-planned 
transfers to acute hospitals. In order to facilitate, collect and 
calculate the indicators, the Association cooperates with us to 
implement a service system. The architecture of the system has 
four components including Data Collection Subsystem, Report 
Generation Subsystem, Aggregation and Analysis Subsystem and 
System Administration Subsystem. In this paper, we introduce 
and share our experience of the system's development. 
Keywords long term care;  nursing home; quality indicators; 
web-based system. 
I.  INTRODUCTION 
The United Nations has defined an “aging society” as a 
population in which the percentage of people over 65 years is 
greater than 7 percent; when that percentage increases beyond 
14 percent, the population is deemed an "aged society." The 
population of Taiwan is about 23.4 million. According to a 
government estimation report [1], the number of senior citizens 
over 65 accounted for 10.7 percent of the nation's total 
population in 2010. The report also predicted that the 
proportion would exceed 14 percent by 2017, meaning that the 
nation will become an aged society at that time, and then would 
exceed 20 percent by 2025. The average life expectancy is 76.1 
years for men and 82.7 years for women in 2010; the report 
predicted that the average life expectancy would be 78.3 years 
for men and 84.9 years for women in 2020. The burden of 
supporting the healthcare expenses for the elderly population is 
significant. According to statistical data from the Bureau of 
National Health Insurance of Taiwan, in 2006, the average 
amount spent by the elderly population for health insurance 
was about 7 times that of other age groups [2]. In 2010, there 
are 1089 long-term care and elderly care facilities in Taiwan 
with about 41000 residents with an average of 2.15 residents 
per staff [3]. 
The Healthcare Indicator is the measurement for patients' 
care quality. According to the Joint Commission on 
Accreditation of Healthcare Organizations (JCAHO), an 
indicator is a quantitative measure of an aspect of patient care 
[4]. Because the quality is difficult to quantify, using more 
objective evidence for monitoring is necessary. Although the 
quality of healthcare is difficult to measure, it can take certain 
problems into account. The development of long-term care 
quality indicators is based on the JCAHO and the Taiwan 
Quality Indicator Project (TQIP) definitions. The Association 
continuously modified the evaluation items. The latest version 
was finished in 2009 and was promoted as the basis of the 
institution's quality assessment. 
There are many studies in the quality of care for nursing 
home. Chia-Ling Yang suggested seven important indicators of 
quality of nursing homes including environment, nursing 
professional competence, quality assurance, basic human rights, 
direct care attitude, social satisfaction [5]. Ming-Nan Kao 
investigated on aged care quality of nursing home [6]. Chen Li-
Chuan used observable indicators of nursing home care quality 
instrument to measure quality of nursing home care [7]. Noemi 
C. et al. tested an Internet report card containing information 
about quality indicators and assessed consumer satisfaction and 
quality in nursing homes [8]. Carolyn J. Greena et al. suggested 
that Information system support is a critical success factor for 
chronic disease management [9]. 
The Taiwan Long Term Care Association has defined six 
indicators for monitoring long-term care quality including 
number of falls, nosocomial infections, use of physical 
restraints, non-planned weight gain or loss, pressure ulcers and 
non-planned transfers to acute hospitals [10, 11]. The indicators 
are reviewed frequently and have been the institution 
assessment requirements since 2010. Each indicator has a 
number of items that are reported each month. The data were

B. Data Collection Subsystem 
The raw data of indicators are input from the Data 
Collection Subsystem. All of the indicator definitions and 
formulae are shown on the screen to avoid user error. The 
subsystem may identify where the user comes from to prepare 
the data entry environment. Because each indicator is only 
saved on one record per month, the system will check the 
system time and retrieve the data from the previous month 
from the database and display it on the screen. Users may 
modify the indicators, generate monthly reports or input new 
data directly. Users may also choose to calculate, confirm or 
cancel each data entry. The calculate function calculates the 
ratios for each indicator followed by predefined formulas. 
Because each ratio could not be larger than 100 percent, the 
system may check each ratio item and alert the mistake values. 
When the confirmation button is clicked, the system checks if 
the month's data exists in the database and perform a data 
update or append a database record. 
C. Report Generation Subsystem 
After entering the indicator items, the system may generate 
reports. We followed the report format defined by Taiwan 
Long Term Care Association to design the system. When users 
login to the system, he/she may select a yearly report or a 
monthly report to be generated. The system retrieves indicator 
items from the database, calculate ratios, and display the results 
on the screen. Users may print the report directly or copy and 
paste the data to other software for further use. The subsystem 
also has six modules; each indicator is an independent module 
and may generate monthly and yearly reports automatically. 
The screen of monthly reports is similar to the Data Collection 
Subsystem. The yearly reports are relatively complicit. The 
program shall define a two-dimension buffer, retrieve 12-
month records from the database, calculate the ratios for each 
indicator item, and then display the values of the two-
dimension buffer on the screen or print them out to a paper. 
D. Aggregation and Analysis Subsystem 
The Aggregation and Analysis Subsystem is designed for 
Association users to analyze cross-institution indicators 
collected from other sites. Users may select parameters of 
period, institution and aggregation functions as condition and 
query from database. Uses may also compare each institution's 
indicator performance.
