一、中文摘要 
本研究使用超臨界流體微細發泡射出
成型探討聚乳酸/蒙脫土奈米高分子複合材
料機械和熱性質之影響。以二氧化碳﹙CO2﹚
作為發泡劑，比較聚乳酸(PLA)添加不同蒙
脫土含量經傳統射出和微細發泡射出的拉
伸和衝擊試片，進行機械性質和熱性質之
研究。研究結果顯示，傳統射出和微細發
泡拉射出的機械性質方面，PLA隨著蒙脫
土含量的提高而有降低抗拉、抗衝擊和耐
磨耗的趨勢，在發泡方面氣泡尺寸變大且
氣泡密度降低，此乃因 PLA加入蒙脫土後
加速 PLA之裂解現象，在熱性質方面添加
蒙脫土會降低結晶溫度但對熔點無決定性
之影響，XRD結果顯示蒙脫土含量增加，
黏土層間距離有被撐開之現象，TEM結果
顯示，蒙脫土有均勻分散在聚乳酸內。 
 
關鍵詞：蒙脫土，超臨界流體微細發泡，
聚乳酸，奈米高分子複合材料 
  
Abstract 
 
This study investigated the influence of 
content of montmorillonite (MMT) on the 
mechanical/thermal properties of 
microcellular injection molded  
Polylactide(PLA)/clay nanocomposites. The 
carbon dioxide﹙CO2﹚was the blowing agent. 
The PLA/MMT nanocomposites were 
prepared by twin screw extrusion. The results 
showed that as MMT content is increased, 
tensile strength, impact strength, and wear 
resistance decrease. This is caused by the 
speed degradation of PLA due to the addition 
of MMT. For the thermal property, MMT 
would decrease the crystallization 
temperature but have no major effect on the 
melt temperature of the nanocomposites. The 
XRD results showed that the layer spacing of 
clay is increased as the MMT content is 
increased. TEM pictures showed that MMT 
is well dispersed on the PLA matrix. 
Keywords: PLA, CO2, Microcellular 
Injection Molding, Clay, Nanocomposites. 
二、緣由與目的 
超臨界流體微細發泡射出成型是近幾
年來一種新興的塑膠射出成型技術。超臨
界微細發泡最早是在 1980年代由MIT的
Nam P. Suh [1,2]教授所研發之技術，後來
美國 Trexel公司將這項新技術商業化並稱
為Mucell®微細發泡技術，如圖一所示。微
細發泡射出成型製程是將惰性氣體（N2或
是 CO2）經由超臨界流體產生器在高壓的
狀態下注入螺桿內，惰性氣體在螺桿內高
溫高壓的“超臨界”狀態下與聚合物熔膠混
合產生一個單相溶液，為了在射出之前維
持單相溶液而快速促進氣體與聚合物熔膠
的均質混合，背壓通常保持在 8~20MPa之
間，比傳統射出成型製程之背壓 0.3~3MPa
之間還高[3]。射出成型期間，單相溶液流
經射嘴注入模具內，模内的壓力突然的下
降會引起熱力學之不穩定性，而形成眾多
數量的成核點（比傳統發泡之數量還更
多），如圖二所示， 
氣體開始從單相溶液形成眾多的微小氣
泡，最後發泡製品經冷卻後而完成最終成
品。然而微細發泡之氣泡大小約在
10~100µm 之間比在傳統射出成型製程之
氣泡大小 250µm以上要來的小。藉由氣體
代替塑膠能夠節省材料和減輕零件重量。
氣泡成長時由於氣體填充在聚合物分子空
隙之間和降低發泡時之內應力，故能有效
改善翹曲、收縮和凹陷且不需保壓壓力[4]。 
高分子聚合物是現代的社會生活所屬
必備品也是世界向永續性發展一個很重要
的角色。目前預估現今已有 15億噸的聚合
物經由石油能源而來，且生產產能每年以
四至五個百分比持續增加，這個成長是因
塑膠的許多優勢，包括重量輕、運用廣、
且加工技術越來越成熟。但因塑膠及其運
用上的優勢也引起許多人開始對石化來源
物質的關心，特別是近來因石油的資源有
限，而造成生產費用漸漸升高，另許多塑
膠的耐久性也造成廢棄處理上很大的問
題；目前塑膠回收速率仍無法趕得上使用
的消耗速度，在一些狀況下回收時會造成
污染集中也產生新的回收問題。所以生物
可分解材料 PLA的誕生及發展替社會提供
一個有好處的塑膠，同時減低對環境的衝

熱性質 
  在高分子奈米複合材料中，因為樣品
本身存在著許多不同的物質，爲了明確知
道在這些材料中其熱穩定性，所以必須透
過 TGA 來測定，在溫度連續變化時，物質
質量的變化情形。如圖十的結果分析，藉
由高溫造成高分子和水分的分解，在當溫
度達到 600℃所得到的物質幾乎可以確認
為蒙脫土，且可以發現 PLA奈米材料隨著
黏土比例增加，其裂解溫度也稍微增加，
這是因為黏土會限制高分子鍊之移動。 
圖十一顯示 DSC分析之 PLA蒙脫土奈米
複材微細發泡射出成型之結晶溫度分析，
很明顯的可以看出用 CO2當發泡劑及添加
蒙脫土會改變結晶溫度。但是熔點分析(圖
十二)，發現添加奈米對奈米複材熔點影響
不大。 
    氣泡結構 
  微結構分析經 SEM來分析試片的斷面
結構與試片的機械性質有關聯，能夠看見
不同材料與成型條件也會影響微細發泡
PLA的氣泡成長型態，在圖十三所示比較
PLA添加不同比例的蒙脫土對微結構的影
響，而發現隨著蒙脫土的比例上升氣泡尺
寸逐漸變大且氣泡密度逐漸變小，影響氣
泡大小之因素主要決定於融膠強度和黏度
係數，因 PLA融膠強度不大，故會使小氣
泡相通而變成大氣泡 另外因為黏土有阻
絕氣體之功能，而變成大氣泡。 
 
結      論 
微細發泡射出成型對 PLA添加不同比
例蒙脫土的奈米複材探討機械性質的分
析，會隨著蒙脫土比例增加而抗拉、抗衝
擊和耐磨耗性都是呈現下降趨勢，而氣泡
尺寸逐漸變大且密度逐漸減少，此乃因
PLA加入蒙脫土後加速 PLA之裂解現象。
微細發泡射出成型對PLA奈米複材的DSC
之結晶溫度分析，添加蒙脫土可降低結晶
溫度，在經微細發泡射出中使用二氧化碳
作為發泡劑，可以幫助結晶成長率，故可
明顯降低結晶溫度，但對熔點較無影響。

Results and Discussion 
 
 Although there are 4 cavities in one mold, only one 
cavity (#1 in Fig. 5) was taken for the measurement in 
order to get consistent data. The rim thickness was the 
average of the five samples. The experiment was carried 
out by short shot first and showed that the runner system 
has unbalanced melt flow (Fig. 5). Some portions are 
filled whereas some are only partially filled. The injection 
molding process was done by the conventional method 
first, and then the microcellular process was introduced as 
the foaming molding method. Fig. 6 shows the rim 
thickness of the roller by conventional molding. The 
thickness variation is more than 0.10 mm, and points 4 & 
5 have the least thickness.   
 
The effect of process conditions on rim thickness of 
the textile roller of PBT with glass fiber 
 
Fig. 7 shows the rim thickness variation of the glass 
fiber filled PBT by microcellular injection molding. The 
thickness variation is around 0.04 mm, which is on the 
margin of the specification, and the thinnest occurs at 
point 5. The thickness trend is similar to that of solid 
molding, but the thickness variation is smaller for 
microcellular molding.  
 
The effect of process conditions on rim thickness of 
the textile roller of PBT with mineral fiber 
 
Figs. 8-13 show the rim thickness variation of 
Wollastonite filled PBT by microcellular molding. From 
the curves observed, the thickness variation of 
Wollastonite filled PBT is smaller than that of glass fiber 
filled PBT, and the curves of the Wollastonite filled PBT 
are smoother than those of glass fiber filled PBT. For the 
process conditions used, only the MPP has the trend 
where as the MPP is increased, the rim thickness variation 
is decreased. MPP is the driving force to make cells 
smaller. 
Figs. 14 & 15 show the microstructure of the cell 
near the gate of glass fiber and Wollastonite fiber filled 
PBT. The cell structure of Wollastonite fiber filled PBT is 
more uniform than that of glass fiber filled PBT, and the 
cell size is around 10 μm.  
 
The large rim thickness variation of glass fiber 
filled PBT may be attributed to the non-uniform cell 
structure. For the shrinkage rate, the average thickness of 
solid glass filled, foamed glass fiber filled, and foamed 
Wollastonite filled PBT is 6.004, 6.243, and 6.256 mm 
respectively. The thickness on the mold is 6.40 mm. In 
turn, the shrinkage rate is 6%, 2.4%, and 2.2% for solid 
glass filled, foamed glass fiber filled, and foamed 
Wollastonite filled PBT respectively.   
 The method to improve the rim thickness variation 
is changing the runner design as shown in Fig. 16. By this 
design, the melt has more balanced flow characteristics. 
Because the mold is too complex to modify, computer 
simulation, Modex3D, is used to simulate the flow 
characteristics of the old and modified design. Fig. 17 
shows the shrinkage rim of the original and modified 
designs. It shows that the modified design has less 
shrinkage compared to the original design.   
 
Summary 
 
The effect of the process parameters on the rim 
thickness of glass fiber and Wollastonite filled PBT has 
been conducted.  It has been found that: 
 
(1) For the rim thickness, microcellular injection molding 
has smaller thickness variation than conventional 
molding. 
(2) The Textile roller has more uniform thickness with 
Wollastonite filled PBT than glass filled PBT. 
(3) Parts have less shrinkage by microcellular injection 
molding compared to conventional molding. 
(4) Wollastonite filled PBT has more uniform cell 
structure but lower shrinkage rate than glass filled 
PBT.

SHRINKAGE STUDY OF TEXTILE ROLLER MOLDED BY THE MICROCELLULAR 
INJECTION PROCESS  
 
Shyh-Shin Hwang1, Peming P. Hsu2, and C.-W. Chiang 1 
1Mechanical Engineering Dept., Ching Yun University, Taiwan, ROC 
2 Department of Computer Application Engineering, Far-East University, Taiwan, ROC. 
 
 
Abstract 
There are several advantages of the microcellular 
injection molding process. One of the advantages is that 
the shrinkage of the part can be reduced. This project 
investigated the effect of the process parameters on the 
shrinkage of the textile roller. Polybutyleneterephthalate 
(PBT) materials with 30 wt% glass and Wollastonite fiber 
were used. The results showed that the shrinkage by 
microcellular injection molding is less than that of 
conventional injection molding. Glass fiber filled PBT has 
more shrinkage than Wollastonite fiber filled PBT due to 
the non-uniform cell size of the glass fiber filled PBT. 
 
Introduction 
The microcellular process was first introduced by N. 
P. Suh [1-2] as a batch process in 1980. In this process, a 
polymer sample is housed in a high pressure chamber. An 
inert gas like CO2 or N2 is introduced into the chamber, 
diffusing into the polymer until saturation. Then, the 
pressure is rapidly reduced while the polymer temperature 
is simultaneously increased, producing a thermodynamic 
instability that lowers the gas solubility and creates cell 
growth. The disadvantage of the batch process is that a 
long period of time is required for the polymer to become 
saturated with the gas, due to low diffusions rates at room 
temperature. To avoid this problem, microcellular 
extrusion was developed. It reduces the time necessary for 
the gas to saturate the polymer by introducing the inert 
gas into the barrel while the polymer is still molten. The 
diffusion rate is high because the temperature in the barrel 
is high. Unfortunately, as parts become more complex, 
microcellular extrusion cannot be used to produce them. 
In response, microcellular injection molding was 
developed[3] and commercialized by Trexel Co. Ltd. as 
the Mucell@ process. The key insight of this process is the 
application of a supercritical fluid. The supercritical fluid 
is injected during the injection stage cycle, creating 
millions of micron-sized voids in otherwise solid 
thermoplastic polymer parts. 
Several studies have investigated the shrinkage and 
warpage of injection molded parts. Bushko et al.[4,5] 
studied the effect of processing conditions on shrinkage, 
warpage, and residual stresses of a thermal viscoelastic 
melt. Their results showed that a higher packing pressure 
resulted in less shrinkage. Liao et al.[6] investigated 
optimal process conditions for shrinkage and warpage in 
thin-wall parts. They showed that the optimal process 
conditions differ for shrinkage and warpage in injected 
thin-wall cellular-phone covers. Recently, Kramschuster 
et al.[7] studied the shrinkage and warpage behavior of a 
grocery box in microcellular and conventional injection 
molding. They showed that the SCF level and injection 
speed are the most important factors affecting the 
shrinkage and warpage of microcellular injection molded 
parts.  
The textile roller(Fig. 1) in the textile machines is a 
worn part which should be replaced after a certain time. 
The material of the roller is PBT. PBT has good 
dimensional stability, mechanical strength, stiffness, and 
fire retardant characteristics. To improve the mechanical 
strength, most of the plastics are filled with glass fiber. In 
this study, both glass and Wollastonite fiber filled PBT are 
investigated. Wollastonite is a calcium metasilicate 
(CaSiO3) which can improve thermal and dimensional 
stability at elevated temperatures[8].  
In this study, we have investigated the effects of 
process parameters on the variation of the rim thickness 
(Fig. 3) by conventional and microcellular injection 
molding. The specification of the rim thickness is ±
0.02mm. If the rim thickness tolerance is out of 
specification, there is a running noise from the roller, and 
the textile roller should be replaced after a certain time.

Mold 
A three-plate-mold with 4 cavities of a textile roller 
was used in this study. Each cavity has six gates around
