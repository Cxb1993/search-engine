Abstract 
In this study, the dynamic gear electrical 
erosion testers with the supply electricity 
instrument and load cell is used to investigate 
the effects of experimental parameters such 
as rolling speed ,supply current, supply 
voltage and oil film thickness , on the electric 
erosion mechanism and tribological behavior 
of the gear tooth contact surface. 
Experimental results show that when the 
electrical arc occurs, the interface voltage 
increases with increasing oil film and supply 
current thickness at a certain rolling and 
supply voltage. However, the interface 
impedance and the interface voltage increase 
with increasing supply voltage under certain 
rolling speed, oil film thickness and supply 
current. Furthermore, the erosion width 
increases with the increasing supply current 
and voltage. With the decrease of the rolling 
speed and the increase of the interface power, 
the melting phenomenon is more obvious, 
and the erosion width and friction force 
becomes larger. 
 
Keywords: gear, current, shaft voltage, 
electric erosion, tribological behavior
