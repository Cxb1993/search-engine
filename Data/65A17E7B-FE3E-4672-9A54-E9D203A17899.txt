Abstract 
 
In this work, self-assembled monolayers of various phosphonic acids with 
different chain lengths and surface groups were formed on TiO2 nanoparticles to 
modify photoanodes of dye-sensitized solar cells (DSSCs). After SAM’s modification, 
the work function and surface property of a TiO2 photoanode changed, and this 
influenced its charge transport ability and thus the device efficiency. The 
characteristic parameters of the DSSC devices after SAM’s modification, such as Isc, 
Voc, FF and efficiency, etc., were compared with those of the standard device. It was 
observed that when a phosphonic acid is with a shorter chain length, there is more dye 
adsorption, leading to more light absorption and thus higher device performance. On 
the contrary, if a phosphonic acid is with a longer chain length, there is less dye 
adsorption and light absorption, so a lower device efficiency would be resulted. The 
effects of the dipole moment of a phosphonic acid on charge transport and device 
efficiency are not clear now. More phosphonic acids with different surface groups will 
be selected and used to clarify this point.  
 
 
Keywords: self-assembled monolayer (SAM), phosphonic acid, dye-sensitized solar 
cell (DSSC)

(英文)  Formation of Amorphous 1D CuPc Nanostructures and Their 
Crystallization and Transformation by Heat Treatment 
附件四 
 3
CuPc vapor molecules possessed higher kinetic energy and had excess energy to adopt nanowires with a 
larger specific surface area once they reached the cold substrate. Three temperatures lower than the melting 
point of CuPc were applied to study the effect of working temperature. However, the influence of various 
working temperatures on the morphology of the nanostructures was inconspicuous. Both XRD and thermal 
analyses demonstrated that the 1D CuPc nanostructures were amorphous. To acquire more information from 
the UV-vis absorption spectra of the nanostructures, the absorption edges were analyzed and their optical 
band gaps could be derived. The red shift of the B-band absorption peak might be ascribed to the size 
variation effect.  
 
Compared with the thin film, 1D CuPc nanostructures exhibit a wider absorption region which is more 
beneficial for application in organic PV cells. The nanostructures can also provide a larger interface for 
carriers generation, leading to higher efficiency of the devices. Nevertheless, crystalline CuPc may be 
preferable for photoelectronic applications. To achieve crystallization of the nanostructures, heat treatment 
was performed at appropriate temperatures for various lengths of time. Straight crystalline needle-like 
nanostructures with a smooth surface could be formed when the CuPc molecules gained enough thermal 
energy from heat treatment. As the heating time was further extended, coarsening phenomenon would occur 
to decrease the surface energy of the nanostructures. Longer time or higher temperature for heat treatment 
enabled the molecules to rearrange and adopt a more ordered way, giving rise to increasing crystallinity. On 
the other hand, a higher temperature for heat treatment could cause reduced intensity of the UV-vis absorption 
spectra and red shift of the optical band gap. The lower absorption intensity was due to looser distribution of 
the nanostructures, whereas the red shift was probably resulted from the phase transition and size variation 
after heat treatment.
