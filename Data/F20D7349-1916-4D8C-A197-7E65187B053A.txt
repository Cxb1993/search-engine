Abstract 
This paper investigates electrical AC transient 
responses of a piezoelectric buzzer excited by 
mechanical impact forces with low frequencies 
and their applications to generating a DC power 
after rectifying and filtering processes. 
Dimensions and material characteristics of the 
buzzer are first expressed. Then, a direct 
piezoelectric effect is used to elucidate an effect 
of an external mechanical stress on an electric 
field of a piezoceramic body inside the buzzer. 
An equivalent circuit model of the buzzer with 
thickness vibration mode is analyzed. Moreover, 
a piton-like mechanical impact system is 
constructed to generate an impact force for 
striking the buzzer, and a piezoelectric DC 
power generator is designed and fabricated. 
Finally, effects of magnitudes and frequencies of 
impact forces on the AC transient responses are 
measured, and effects of a rectifying circuit and 
filtering capacitances on output DC voltages are 
discussed. 
Keywords: piezoelectric buzzer, piezoelectric 
effect, impact force, DC power 
generator
