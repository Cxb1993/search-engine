中文摘要 
 
隨著數位照相機與數位攝影機的普及，有許多人將其拿來記錄生活上與週遭上
所發生的點點滴滴。在觀看拍攝的作品時常發現，我們對於拍攝的地點往往無法很
正確的描述。本計畫建構一個以網格運算(Grid computing)為基礎的視訊場景定位系
統。根據網格資源分享與網格無縫(seamless)存取服務的特性來有效的分配運算負載
並動態平衡網格的資源。本系統包括以下伺服器：網頁伺服器，視訊片段分派伺服
器，關鍵畫面擷取伺服器，區塊擷取伺服器，建築物偵測伺服器，詮釋資料伺服器，
建築物辨識伺服器，場景定位伺服器。我們開發佈建於網格上之視訊場景定位技術，
其中包含網格運算下的建築物偵測、建築物辨識及場景定位等技術的開發。透過本
系統，影像與視訊的提供者將可更方便的分享其作品；觀賞者也可更容易的得到與
視訊畫面相關的空間資訊與時間資訊。 
 
關鍵詞：網格運算、視訊場景定位、建築物偵測、建築物辨識 
 
  
 4
一、前言 
 
隨著數位照相機與數位攝影機的普及，
有愈來愈多人將其拿來記錄生活上與週遭
上所發生的大小事情：如旅遊見聞，特殊
事件等。然而在觀賞拍攝的作品時常會發
現，大部分的作品我們只能就拍攝時的人、
事、時、物清楚的描述，對於拍攝的地點
卻無法很正確的描述。觀察目前的影片
可以發現，影片中大部分都會出現拍攝
點的地標或者建築物。因此，若能辨認
出場景中地標或代表性建物所在位置，
就能知道拍攝時的地點。目前已有許多
關於建築物辨識的研究出現[1-14]。本
實驗室於 98 年度，提出了建構「以視
訊內容為基礎之視訊場景定位系統」的
計畫用「一般化機率決策類神經網路
(GPDNN)」 [15-18] 辨識出場景的地標
或代表性建物，最後透過資料庫的查詢
得到此建築物的相關資訊：如名稱，經
緯度等，進而達到自動化視訊場景定位
之目的。 
隨著同時上線使用視訊場景定位系
統的使用者以及其所上傳需定位視訊資
料的快速增加，如何能在可接受的時間
內快速的分析、處理、與定位上傳者所
上傳視訊的場景是此系統所需開發的重
要關鍵技術。為了讓所提出的視訊場景
定位系統能在網際網路上快速的分析處
理多個輸入的視訊，本年度擬建構一個
以網格運算(Grid computing)為基礎的視
訊場景定位系統。 
 
二、研究目的 
 
基於以下網格運算的特點： 
1.資源分享，網格是分散式的運算架構。
在網格中所有為了完成某一共同目標的個
體、團體與資源會動態結合成虛擬組織
(virtual organization: VO)。在同一個虛擬組
織中的網點(node)皆可以協同分享彼此的資
源。 
2.無縫(seamless)存取，網格運算將分散於
網點上的運算、資料、與資源(如處理器、
網路頻寬與存儲容量等)虛擬化，並對使用
者提供這些資源的無縫 (seamless) 存取服
務。也就是說在網格運算中，使用者看到
的是一台大型的電腦，而不是隱藏於系統
內部複雜的中央處理伺服器、資料伺服器
與網路運行方式。系統會於內部動態的挑
選適宜的電腦作運算以達到有效率的網格
資源分配。 
本計畫將以網格運算為基礎來設計視訊場
景定位系統， 
 
三、研究方法 
 
本計畫所要完成的視訊場景定位系統
包含了七種伺服器： 
1. 網頁伺服器 
提供使用者透過網頁瀏覽器連到以網
格運算視訊場景定位系統網站，並上傳分
享的視訊或瀏覽系統網站上所分享的視訊。 
2. 視訊片段分派伺服器 
分析當前網格的系統使用資訊與情況，
動態的將使用者所上傳的視訊分段，並將
其所切割的視訊片段分派到網格上適合的
關鍵畫面擷取伺服器上執行關鍵畫面擷取。 
3. 關鍵畫面擷取伺服器 
此伺服器主要是抽取由視訊片段分派
伺服器所派送過來的視訊片段的關鍵畫面。
主要工作就是判斷哪些畫面是相似的畫面，
並在這些畫面中抽取一張代表性的畫面。 
4. 區塊擷取伺服器 
針對關鍵畫面擷取伺服器所送過來的
畫面抽取出影像區塊。若抽取的影像區塊
愈精確，則後續在偵測區塊是否為建築物
區塊時的結果會愈正確。 
5. 建築物偵測伺服器 
判斷區塊擷取伺服器所傳送過來的圖
型區塊是否為建築物。在建構建築物偵測
伺服器時，我們以上年度開發之類神經網
路技術為基礎，開發一架構於網格運算中
之建築物偵測技術。

been developed, such as Diverse Density[8], Bayesian-kNN and Citation-kNN
algorithms[14], EM-DD algorithm[17], etc., and successfully applied to many ap-
plications [9, 18, 3, 5, 1, 7, 15]. More works on MIL can be found in [19].
The robustness, adaptation, and ability to automatically learn from examples
make neural network approaches attractive and exciting for MIL. When the notion
of MIL was proposed, Dietterich et al.[4] indicated that a particular interesting issue
in this area is to design multiple-instance modifications for neural networks. Ramon
and De Raedt [12] presented a neural networks framework for MIL. Zhang and Zhou
proposed a multi-instance neural network named BP-MIP[20, 16], which extended
the popular BP [13] algorithm with a global error function defined at the level of
bags instead of at the level of instances. How to construct a neural model structure is
crucial for successful recognition. All the above neural networks for MIL are based
on the all-class-in-one-network (ACON) structure, where all the classes are lumped
into one super-network. The supernet has the burden of having to simultaneously
satisfy all the teachers, so the number of hidden units tends to be large.
In this paper, a Multiple Instance Decision Based Neural Networks (MI-DBNN)
is proposed for MIL. The proposed MI-DBNN is a probabilistic variant of the Deci-
sion Based Neural Networks (DBNN) [6]. The MI-DBNN inherits the one-class-in-
one-network (OCON) structure from the DBNN. For each concept to be recognized,
MI-DBNN devotes one of its subnets to the representation of that particular concept.
Pandya and Macy [11] compared the performance between the ACON and OCON
structures, and observed that OCON model achieves better training and generaliza-
tion accuracies. Beside, the discriminant function of the proposed MI-DBNN is in
a form of probability density, which yields high accuracy rates compared to other
approaches, as discussed in Section 4.
The reminder of this paper is organized as follows. In the next section, the pro-
posed discriminant function is presented in detail. Then, in Section 3, the imple-
mentation of the proposed MI-DBNN and its learning scheme are introduced. Ex-
perimental results are presented and discussed in Section 4. Finally, Section 5 draws
some conclusions and future works.

MIL constraint. That is, the discriminant function of MI-DBNN is designed to cap-
ture the nature of MIL. Given a set of i.i.d. feature patterns x= {x(t); t = 1,2, · · · ,N}
extracted from the instances in a bag B, we assume that the likelihood func-
tion p(x(t)|ωi) for the concept ωi is a linear combination of component densities

3.2 The Recognition Phase
The goal of the MI-DBNN recognition phase is to obtain instances from the input
unlabelled bag, to compare them with the concept models learned before, and to
find the concepts the input unlabelled bag belonging to.
As shown in Fig. 3, each subnet receives the input patterns extracted from the
unlabelled bag, and computes the discriminate function as shown in (2). Then, the
results of (2) are compared with the threshold Ti. According to the different applica-
tions, the unlabelled bag may be labelled as one concept or multiple concepts. The
vector V in MI-DBNN is the recognition vector showing which concepts unlabelled
bag belonging to. The ith element of V is set to 1 if the output of the ith discriminate
function is larger than Ti, which implies that the given bag belongs to the concept
ωi. Otherwise, the ith element of V is set to 0 implying that the given bag does not
belong to the conceptωi. From the recognition vector, one can recall which concepts
the given bag belonging to.
4 Experiments
In order to show the proposed MI-DBNN ability to deal with the MIL problems, the
MUSK data sets available from UCI Machine Learning Repository [10] are used in
the experiments.
The MUSK data sets include MUSK1 and MUSK2. The data sets consist of
descriptions of molecules. The target protein is a putative receptor in the human
nose, and a molecule binds to the receptor as positive if it smells like a musk. The
MI-DBNN is trained to learn what shape makes a molecule musky. MUSK1 has
92 molecules (bags), of which 47 are positive, with an average of 5.17 shapes (in-
stances) per molecule. MUSK2 has 102 molecules, of which 39 are positive, with
interesting issue for future works. Furthermore, it is also interesting to employ fea-
ture selection techniques to test if feature selection can improve the performance of
MI-DBNN .
