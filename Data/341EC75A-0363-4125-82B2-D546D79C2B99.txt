五、結論  
本研究雙爆震源相互間影響之數值模擬，是以有限體積法為基礎，解算無黏性非定常可壓縮流，模擬過程
中改變爆震源位置配置及產生爆震源時間間隔，並將結果作定性及定量上的分析、探討，以了解雙爆震源爆炸
效益。研究結論歸納以下列幾點： 
1.當兩爆震波接觸時，因移動方向不同，產生相互衝擊之交互作用的現象，進而形成衝擊區域之暫態壓力升高。 
2.由偵測點中可發現，震波在不同位置及不同產生時間下所記錄到的暫態壓力值不同，顯示因震波發生的位置
及時間會影響震波的交互作用現象。 
3.兩爆震源於相同垂直高度位置時，水平距離越大，所產生之暫態壓力對近地面的影響越大，但兩者水平距離
超過三倍爆震源寬度(CaseB4)後便不再增加；而兩爆震源於不同垂直距離下，垂直距離愈大，所產生之暫態壓
力對近地面的影響也越小。  
4.兩爆震源於不同時間產生震波間之交互作用時，採用爆震源有時間間隔之配置，其造成之暫態壓力比同時間
產生爆震為高，且兩者爆震間隔為(CaseD1)時所產生之爆震效益最大。而對接近地面所產生之影響也最大。 
 
5.1 未來研究方向 
  本研究已針對非定常之雙爆震源間交交互作用影響作一系列分析比較，對流場的結構特徵及物理性質也作
了詳加的描述。但所模擬之算例乃假設爆震源氣體與外界氣體相同，僅狀態不同。同時並未將爆炸燃料與化學
反應考慮進去。因此，後續研究可針對爆炸的化學反應與加入氣體黏性，以期能獲得最接近實際爆炸的模擬結
果。且本研究為假設 4.1=γ 之理想狀態，然而爆震產生時其γ 值已並非為理想狀態，故爾後研究爆震行為時應
將其列入考慮。然而實際地面為一粗糙面，會造成震波反射時並非正規反射，故考慮反射面的情況也是未來研
究的領域，最後期能藉由上述各種方式來獲得最接近實際爆炸的模擬結果，進而提供工業上爆炸震波之效應及
軍事戰場震波防制之運用參考。

ABSTRACT  
In this study, a numerical computation of unsteady 
flowfield of a NACA0012 Cascades wing at low Reynolds 
number is carried out. The numerical scheme, using the 
preconditioning finite volume method, is introduced to 
solve the governing flow equation. The grid is generated 
employing the unstructured method. The trailing edge 
separation at different angle of attack is predicted and the 
resulting lift-drag ratio is computed. It found that cascades 
wing remain the lift with high angle of attack (α≧30 
degree). The area of vortex interaction between upper and 
lower trailing edge moves forward when the angle of 
attack is increasing. 
Keyword: Cascades Wing, Low Reynolds Number, High 
AOA, Preconditioning, Finite volume  
 
INTRODUCTION 
Micro Air Vehicle (MAV) is developed since 1993. The 
definition employed in DARPA's program limits these craft 
to a size less than 15 cm (about 6 inches) in length, width or 
height. MAVs must be capable of staying aloft for perhaps 
20 to 60 minutes while carrying a payload of 20 grams or 
less to a distance of perhaps 10 km. [1] However, the low 
Reynold Number (104~105) aerodynamic problem need to 
deal with for MAV development. It is the critical Reynold 
Number area in Re=104~105. The lift-drag ratio is 
becoming to very low in this area[2]. The Laminar Kinetic 
is not overcoming with downstream back pressure, and the 
separation is induced. Therefore, the shear and transition 
flow is going to turbulent flow.[2-4] The flow over airfoil is 
of major interest for the aerodynamic design of aircraft 
wing and turbomachinery components. This lifting devices 
usually attain optimum performance at the onset of 
separation.[5] Therefore, separation phenomena must be 
include if the analysis is aimed at practical application. 
Separation results at high angle of attack. With the 
development of high-speed computers, Computational 
Fluid Dynamics (CFD) is emerging as an equally important 
tool as compared to experiments. Rhie and Chow[6] used 
numerical method to solve two-dimensional Navier-Stokes 
equations for incompressible flow. The k-εmodel was 
applied to turbulent flow with and without trailing edge 
separation. It was shown that CFD results agree with the 
experimental measurement for NACA 0012 airfoils for 
attached flow at Re=2.8×106 and Re=3.8×106. Davidson 
and Rizzi [7] carried out a study to predict stall over a 2D 
airfoil. The useed a standard explicite Rung Kutta time 
marching code. They showed that Baldwin Lomax model 
failed to predict the stall whereas Algebraic Stress Model 
(ASM) did predict the stall at AOA=16 degree which was 
found to be in good agreement with experimental 
observations. Many attempts have been made to solve 
attached flow over airfoil using Navier-Stoke equations, but 
little progress has been made in the solution of flow with 
separation. Most of the exiting method incorporate one 
equation model turbulence models which are not applicable 
to turbulent separating flow.[8] In general, the k-εmodel has 
been applied successfully to flow around a single airfoil.[9] 
The present study is the unsteady flowfield of a 2D 
NACA0012 cascades wing at low Reynolds number. The 
effects of angle of attack (α) on the flowfield are computed, 
and k-εmodel is employed to take account for the 
turbulence while parametric study is conducted to 
investigate the effects of the angle of attack on lift-drag 
ratio. 
 
COMPUTATIONAL GRID 
Because of the complexity of airfoil leading edge, trailing 
edge, and passage, it is necessary to stretch grid in that 
region. Unstructured grids are used to cover the flowfield, 
and the stretching transformation clusters are made near the 
boundary layer. The total number of cells is 43301 with 
respect to the 2-D airfoil as shown in Figure 1. The 
govering equations are discretized on an unstructured grid 
with triangular elements using a node-centered, finite-
volume technique and solve through a time-marching 
scheme. The grid points close to the solid surface are 
considered to be lying within the viscous sublayer, i.e. grid 
points y+≦5 are in the viscous sublayer. The law of the 
wall assumption is considered for the grid points y+≦100.

8. CONCLUSIONS 
A simulation of flowfield around the cascades wing 
NACA0012 airfoil is carried out using a preconditioning 
Navier-Stokes equation solver. Separated and vorticity 
flow haves been computed using RNG k-εturbulence 
model. Lift-drag ratio have been determined for low 
Reynold number. The conclusions derived for the 
flowfield studied are as following: 
1. Vortex periodicity shedding occurs on the trailing 
edge of the upper airfoil for high angle of attack (α≧
30 degree). 
2. The trailing edge separated flow of the lower airfoil 
is sucked by the anti-clockwise vortex of the upper 
airfoil shedding. 
3. The area of vortex interaction between upper and 
lower trailing edge moves forward when the angle of 
attack is increasing. 
The conclusion obtain from the lift-drad ratio maybe 
listed as follow: 
1. Lift-drag ratio reduces as AOA increasing for high 
AOA (α≧20 degree), but it still with positive value. 
2. The amplitude of the Lift-drag ratio vibration is 
increasing for the AOA increasing. 
3. Lift-drag ratio is going to zero suddenly when the 
AOA large than 40 degree.
