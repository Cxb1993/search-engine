摘要 
多晶矽太陽能電池目前仍是市佔率最高的太陽能電池。太陽能電池發電為達
到市電平價（grid parity）的目標，必須提升晶片品質並降低生產成本。在多晶矽
晶片中常見高濃度的碳與氧成分，會造成差排與熱施體，碳與氧是主要影響晶碇
品質的雜質。 
本研究以有限體積法（FVM）模擬定向固化系統（DSS）爐體內熱流場、氧 
雜質、碳雜質與氧化矽氣體濃度場。而且模擬預估碳與氧雜質濃度，與長晶業 
者提供實驗量測多晶矽晶碇內的碳與氧雜質濃度相當一致。模擬結果顯示熔湯 
對流主要由浮力所造成，隨著生長過程對流形態會改變。本研究提出修改爐體 
熱場之設計，可增加熔湯對流的強度，使碳雜質分佈較均勻。修改熱場爐體長 
成晶片製作之太陽能電池，其效率較原設計爐體所製成太陽能電池效率提昇 
1.8%。 
晶碇固化時隨固化分率增加，熔湯與坩堝接觸面積會減少，使熔湯內氧濃度 
降低。低固化分率時，高爐壓下熔湯內氧濃度較低爐壓者高。高固化分率時，因 
為氬氣冷卻自由表面熔湯的效果，使高爐壓下熔湯內氧濃度較低爐壓者低。因此， 
爐壓在晶體生長過程可適度調整達到更佳的控氧目的。增加氬氣流量可將更多自 
由表面氣化的氧化矽氣體帶至爐外，而降低氧濃度。本研究在不增加氬氣流量情 
形下，藉由簡單的氬氣導流設計增加自由表面上方氬氣流速，可獲得之降氧濃度 
效果與增加25%氬氣流量時相似，也可達到控氧與改善晶碇品質的效能。 
 
 
 
關鍵字: 太陽能、多晶矽、數值模擬

一、 前言 
1-1 研究背景與文獻回顧 
    太陽能電池屬於太陽光伏（Photovoltaic，簡稱PV）產業。太陽能電池發電必
須朝向「發電成本等於或低於傳統發電成本（亦即市電同價，grid parity）」努力，
未來才可能取代傳統發電方式。在太陽能電池原料選擇方面，由於矽是地球上蘊
含量第二豐富的元素，其性質無毒性，而且矽在半導體工業的發展，已具有深厚
的基礎，因此目前太陽能電池仍以矽為主要材料。Muller et al. [1]預測2020年全球
太陽光電對矽原料的需求量預計將達18萬頓以上，詳如圖1-1，約為目前需求量之
三倍，未來矽原料仍是太陽能電池廣泛使用的原料。但全球生產矽原料的廠家很
少，容易發生壟斷市場的現象。2007 年矽原料產量不及蓬勃發展的太陽能電池需
求，矽原料價格大幅上漲，提高結晶矽太陽能電池成本，而促使薄膜矽太陽能電
池近年來逐漸受到重視。Sark et al.[2]研究發現薄膜矽太陽能電池市佔率逐年提昇 
，結晶矽太陽能電池市佔率雖受排擠效應而逐年減少，迄2010年結晶矽太陽能電
池市佔率與產量（capacity）分別為74%與4.8GW，仍均遠高於薄膜矽太陽能電池
的22％與1.2GW，詳如圖1-2所示。Hoffmann[3]則預測迄2030年矽薄膜太陽能電
池產能才有機會超越結晶矽太陽能電池的產能，詳如圖1-3所示。因為矽薄膜太陽
能電池光電轉換效率遠不如結晶矽太陽能電池，短期內矽薄膜太陽能電池產能仍
難以取代結晶矽太陽能電池的產能，使結晶矽太陽能電池研究仍受到相當的重視
[1, 4-6]。 
    結晶矽太陽能電池市電同價必須朝兩個方向努力：增加電池效率與降低生產
成本。矽材料對陽光中紅外線光吸收能力限制是影響矽太陽能電池效率的原因，
再加上反射光的損失，使光電轉換效率受限無法超越50％。增加電池效率的方法
有提升矽原料純度與改善晶片品質。改善晶片品質等同於降低晶片缺陷（defect），
常見矽晶片的缺陷有：點缺陷、線缺陷（dislocation）、雜質沈澱物（precipitate）
與晶界（grain boundary）。摻雜元素造成的點缺陷會使不同軸向位置晶片的電阻

積於晶界上，使復合(recombination)活性增強[12]，載子生命週期與擴散長度則變
短。因此，多晶矽晶片藉由吸附[13-22]或鈍化(passivation)[17, 21, 23, 24]方式，
可以改善晶界附近沈積金屬雜質污染對太陽能電池電性的傷害，但吸附與鈍化的
程序都增加了額外的生產成本。 
由於矽原料(raw feedstock)中含有大量雜質，純度愈高的原料價格愈高。矽
原料依純度可區分為冶金級矽(metallurgical grade Si)其矽純度約99% (2N)與電
子級矽(electronic grade Si)其矽純度約99.999999999% (11N)。冶金級矽每公斤約
1-1.5 美金，而電子級矽原料依市場供需情況而變動，每公斤約為35-200美金[25]。
高矽純度著眼於避免雜質引響載子壽命週期。若原料中雜質成分過高超過矽熔湯
之溶解度，或生長過程中受到雜質污染，這些雜質便擴散進入晶體中，降低少數
載子（minority carrier）存活期(lifetime)與遷移率(mobility)，這是太陽能電池轉
換效率受限的原因之一。圖1-4 顯示冶金級矽原料製成太陽能電池後，若含有少
許金屬雜質，如鈦(Ti)與釩(V)，可使效率降低60％以上[26]。但冶金級矽原料中
金屬雜質多為過渡元素，其偏析係數都很小，可於冶煉原料製程中以純化方式去
除，而提升矽的純度[27]，因此，太陽能級矽原料的專屬規格[25, 26]便經折衷後
而產生，目前太陽能級矽(solar grade Si)原料之純度約為99.9999%(6N)。 
太陽級矽原料中雜質以硼、氧、碳、鐵、鉻、銅與鎳等為主[25]，太陽級矽
原料製成多晶矽晶碇後，晶片中以碳與氧成分濃度最高，因此，多晶矽晶碇內碳
與氧成分濃度是重要品質指標之一。為掌握碳與氧成分在晶碇中濃度，通常量測
氧與碳濃度時，會將多晶矽晶碇切割為不同區域之晶棒(brick)，各區域晶棒製成
晶圓後以FTIR檢測雜質濃度，再以光束誘導電流Light Beam-induced current(LBIC)
量測主、次要載子擴散長度、存活期與遷移率與電阻值等數據，便可評定太陽能
電池晶碇品質。

1-1-2 定向固化法生長多晶矽晶碇研究概況 
雖然傳統生長半導體晶片之柴氏長晶法（Czochralski method，簡稱CZ法）仍
普遍用於生長單晶矽太陽能電池晶片，然而受限於單位時間與設備之產量較生長
多晶矽太陽能電池晶片之定向固化法（directional solidification method，簡稱DS
法）小，單位生產成本較高，而不具市場優勢，目前結晶矽太陽能電池晶片仍以
定向固化法生長之多晶矽晶片為市場主流。定向固化法係藉由控制溫度使晶體朝
向某一方向生長，晶粒生長的方向與熱流傳遞方向相反。它被普遍使用於冶金
（metallurgy），如鑄造法（casting method）生長鋼鐵材。多晶矽晶片初期發展便
引用鑄造法（爐體）生長多晶矽晶碇（ingot），隨後也有運用布氏法（Bridgman 
method）、熱交換器法（HEM method）、電磁鑄造法（electromagnetic casting）
及定向固化系統法（DSS method）等。上述方法生長多晶矽晶碇過程均為將坩堝
內多晶矽原料加熱融化後，運用爐體熱場（hot zone）特性建立垂直之縱向溫度梯
度，使矽熔湯（silicon melt）冷卻，而由坩鍋底部向上凝固生長為晶碇。基於建
立垂直之縱向溫度梯度方法不同，而有前述不同的爐體設計概念[41]。 
定向固化法爐體熱場（hot zone）設計，除了建立與控制垂直之縱向溫度梯度
使晶碇生長外，另一個要點是控制使降低生長界面附近水平方向溫度梯度變。這
些建立或保持溫度梯度的重要元件，可以歸納為加熱、取熱與保溫裝置。加熱裝
置主要功能為熔化坩堝內原料，取熱裝置則是提供固化（solidification）所需之溫
度梯度，而保溫裝置則是保持晶碇生長所需的溫度梯度。三者必須相互配合，

現不論是何種外型的坩堝，熱傳方向均由熔湯頂部經固液界面向晶錠底部傳送，

低，而碳雜質濃度隨爐壓變化較不明顯。

三、結果與討論 
3-1 標準型 DSS 多晶爐模擬與實驗結果 
    模擬計算之不同生長階段爐體整體溫度分佈詳如圖 3-1(a) – (f)。在 3-1(a) – (f)
圖中左半邊為等溫線圖，等溫間隔為 10K，右邊係速度分佈圖。在長晶過程由於
絕熱層開啟，使絕熱層內部熱傳向爐壁（經由取熱塊（heat sink）與支柱（support）
傳向爐壁（wall）），爐壁內有冷卻水以保持約 300K 壁溫。 
    由於固化時固液界面會釋放潛熱，熱傳方向與固液界面垂直，換言之，晶粒
生長方向會垂直固液界面，但熱傳方向與晶粒生長方向相反。由於熔湯內徑向熱
傳與自然對流的浮力作用，不同固化分率下，固液界面形狀（變形）亦不同。圖
3-2 為實際 DSS 爐體生長晶錠右半剖面圖，將 11%固化分率之模擬固液界面形狀
與剖面圖重疊比對，發現模擬固液界面形狀大多與晶粒生長（熱傳）方向互相垂
直。過冷度(undercooling)指熔湯低於固化溫度，而未凝固的現象。通常熔湯低於
固化溫度將在晶種（seed）處「成核」（nucleus）結晶，但多晶矽生長通常未使
用晶種成核，在缺乏晶種成核情形下，熔湯可能會發生以晶體發生均勻成核之較
低的固化溫度現象。溫度過冷為控制結晶方向的主要因子，較低的過冷度，因固
液界面移動速度慢，晶粒向橫向擴展，而較高的過冷度則向軸向發展。比較圖 
3-1(a)-(f)不同固化分率模擬結果與圖 3-2 剖面圖，發現 DSS 爐體長晶過程，在晶

測晶碇中央區域沿軸向之平均碳雜質濃度，紅線係相同區域模擬結果，模擬結果
與實驗結果有相當高之一致性，且誤差在 10%內屬合理可接受範圍。由以上模擬
研究發現，標準型爐體在相同水平高度下，晶碇內碳雜質分佈較修改型爐體不均

是由於熔湯的強烈熱對流所造成。從堝壁溶解矽熔湯內的氧雜質，被熔湯內兩對
渦漩分別沿自由表面與固液界面帶至熔湯中央區域。而接近坩堝側壁處的熔湯流
速快，使該處氧雜質等濃度線隨流動趨勢而非常扭曲。溶解於矽熔湯內的氧在接
近自由表面處會氣化成為氧化矽氣體，依循矽熔湯上渦漩自坩堝側壁（高氧濃度）
向坩堝中央區域流動的特性，使氧濃度自坩堝側壁沿自由表面向坩堝中央區域降
低。而在接近自由表面處未氣化的氧在矽熔湯內隨熔湯流動在中央區域轉而向下
輸送。因此，氧雜質等濃度線在接近自由表面處是凹向自由表面的。此外，另有
部分氧雜質是被熔湯下渦漩帶著沿固液界面傳輸。因為氧於矽熔湯的偏析係數 k0 
約為 1.05，沿固液界面流動的部分氧雜質會偏析入晶體內，未偏析入晶體內者將
隨熔湯轉而向上傳遞。依循矽熔湯下渦漩自坩堝側壁（高氧濃度）向坩堝中央區
域流動的特性，使氧濃度自坩堝側壁沿固液界面向坩堝中央區域降低。受到上渦
漩在坩堝中央區域的向下流動與下渦漩在坩堝中央區域的向上流動的相互作用，
下渦漩在接近中央區域的向上流動動量較弱，故接近固液界面的氧雜質等濃度線
是凸向自由表面的，且在坩堝中央區域上、下渦漩合併後流向堝壁，使該處氧雜
質等濃度線形狀是較平直的。故低氧濃度區將出現在熔湯中央區域。在自由表面
氣化的氧化矽與氬氣合成混和氣，由於氬氣大渦漩流動特性，使氧化矽等濃度線
是凹向中心軸。最高氧化矽濃度出現在坩堝側壁上之三相點。

中央區域晶碇品質較四周邊緣晶碇品質重要。 
    本研究將模擬的固液界面區分為兩段：中央區域與堝壁區域，兩區域交接點
為固液界面「最低點」位置，通常此位置也是雜質容易聚集處。中央區域固液界
面形狀通常為凸向熔湯，本研究以「凸率」表示該區域固液界面形狀凸向熔湯的
程度；而堝壁區域固液界面形狀通常為凹向熔湯，「凹率」表示該區域固液界面
形狀凹向熔湯的程度。模擬結果顯示原設計爐體晶體生長時，固液界面堝壁區域
之凹率隨固化分率增加而增加，中央區域的凸率隨固化分率增加而呈現先減少而
後增加。觀察實際晶碇縱剖面圖，與模擬結果也是一致的。為提昇中央區域晶碇
品質，我們提出修改原爐體熱場設計（修改型 DSS 爐體），控制固液界面在固
化時之側向熱流，使中央區域固液界面凸率增加，最低點位置向坩堝壁方向移動。
觀察實際修改型爐體長成晶碇之縱剖面圖，與我們預測的結果一致。 
    多晶矽原料含有多種固有雜質，但多數雜質可隨偏析過程排出至熔湯中，殘
留晶體中雜質以氧與碳雜質濃度較高。碳雜質濃度高容易生成差排，當碳雜質濃
度超過矽熔湯的溶解度會生成碳化矽，使切割晶碇時容易斷線。氧雜質會生成熱
施體，使晶片產生假性低電阻，還會使太陽能電池發生光衰，碳與氧雜質均影響
多晶矽晶碇品質與降低太陽能電池的性能，這是多晶矽晶碇研究中常見探討碳與
氧雜質的主要原因。「擴散」是重要的雜質傳遞機制，但是氧與碳雜質在矽熔湯
內的薛密特無因次參數（Schmidt number）分別均大於 1，表示這些雜質在矽熔湯
內傳遞時，「對流」機制的影響也很重要，但目前研究卻罕見探討熔湯對流流動
形態對雜質分佈的影響。本研究利用數值模擬，分析 DSS 爐體生長多晶矽晶碇過
程之熱流場與雜質輸送現象，獲得很好的結果，熔湯流動形態是影響雜質分佈的
重要機制。 
    熔湯對流主要由浮力引起，隨者固化分率增加，熔湯對流形態會隨著改變。
在低固化分率時，熔湯內有上、下兩對渦漩。上渦漩因自由表面處熱毛細力會強
化浮力對流的效果。原設計 DSS 爐體內的熔湯在這個生長階段，上、下渦流的流
動使碳雜質等濃度線為「鐘型」形狀，最高碳雜質濃度出現在熔湯中央區域。當

2. Mathematical model
A schematic representation of the industrial DSS furnace used
in this study is shown in Fig. 1. The temperature of the furnace
wall is kept constant by a cooling system, and the side insulation
moves upward during ingot growth. The mc-Si ingot grows from
the bottom to the top of the crucible by the dissipation of heat
from the bottom of the crucible to the furnace wall.
An axis-symmetric furnace is assumed in the numerical
simulation. The melt is assumed to be a Newtonian ﬂuid and
the deformation of the free surface is neglected. The buoyancy
convection may be generated by the temperature and concentra-
tion gradients in the melt. The Lewis number Le, which is deﬁned
as the ratio of the thermal diffusivity a to the mass diffusivity D,
represents the relative strength of the buoyancy force induced by
the non-uniform temperature to that generated by the non-
uniform concentration. The Lewis number Le for the carbon
concentration in the silicon melt is about 1000. Therefore, the
buoyancy due to the non-uniform concentration can be neglected
in the present study. The material properties of silicon are shown
in Table 1[2,8,12,13].

A simulation of the carbon concentration distribution in
the melt during the growth process in an industrial DSS furnace
is carried out. Convection is induced in the melt by buoyancy
and thermocapillary ﬂow. The vortex pattern in the melt
convection changes during the growth process. When the
solidiﬁcation fraction is small, the ﬂow pattern of the melt
convection mainly consists of upper and lower vortices coupled to
each other. The intensity of the lower vortex is higher than that of
the upper one. The motion of the coupled vortices generates bell
shaped carbon concentration isolines. At this point a higher
carbon concentration appears near the central region of the melt.
When the solidiﬁcation fraction becomes higher, the upper vortex
moves gradually towards the corner region near the crucible side
wall and the lower vortex separates into two vortices whose
motion induces the higher carbon concentration to move from
near the central region of the melt towards the side wall of the
crucible.

Oxygen impurities can reduce the carrier lifetime of mc-Si solar cells. In this study, simulations of the
transient temperature, velocity and concentrations of oxygen and silicon oxide are carried out in order to
clarify the transport mechanism of oxygen impurities in the silicon melt and silicon oxide through argon
gas, in a directional solidiﬁcation system (DSS) furnace. As the solidiﬁcation fraction enlarges, the oxygen
concentration in the melt diminishes, because of the reduction in the amount of crucible surface
immersed below the siliconmelt. When the solidiﬁcation fraction is small, two pairs of vortices appear in
the melt. Oxygen originating from the crucible is carried towards the free surface by the upper vortex.
Oxygen concentration is higherwith a higher furnace pressure rather thanwith a lower one due to the low
SiO evaporation at the free surface.When the solidiﬁcation fraction increases, the upper vortex gradually
disappears. The lower vortex occupies almost the whole of the melt, with the exception of a small upper
central regionwhere a small vortex forms because of the cooling effect of the argon gas. Oxygen impurities
carried by the lower vortex along the crystallization front towards the central region are obstructed by
this small vortex. The size of the small vortex increases as the solidiﬁcation fraction increases. Since the
small vortex is strongerwhen the furnace pressure is higher, the concentration is lower around the central
region. This means that the oxygen concentration is smaller when the furnace pressure is higher rather
than lower. The simulation results agree well with the experimental results.

1. Introduction
Directional solidiﬁcation systems (DSS) are frequently
employed to fabricate large-size multicrystalline silicon (mc-Si)
ingots for use in solar cells. The efﬁciency of the solar cells depends
on the quality of the mc-Si ingot, which is sequentially determined
by the impurity concentration and the crystallization process
[1–3]. Oxygen impurity is one of the most signiﬁcant types of
impurities affecting solar cell wafers and may cause light-induced
degradation [4] or creation of thermal donors [5] in solar cells. The
oxygen in a DSS mainly originates from the heated quartz crucible
and the feedstock [6]. Some of the dissolved oxygen in the melt
reacts with Si atoms to form silicon oxide (SiO) at the free surface,
which is carried away by the argon gas above the surface, and the
remaining quantity is incorporated into the ingot. The walls of the
quartz crucible in the DSS furnace are coated with a layer of silicon
nitride (Si3N4), which leads to a signiﬁcant reduction in the
dissolution rate (activity) of oxygen from the quartz crucible
through the Si3N4 layer into the Si melt. To control the oxygen
concentration in a mc-Si ingot, it is necessary to clarify the
transport mechanisms of oxygen impurities in the silicon melt
and silicon oxide in argon gas during the directional solidiﬁcation
process.
Many investigators have studied the transport phenomenon of
oxygen impurities in a silicon melt in a Czochralski growth system
numerically [7–15]. Recently Gao et al. [16] performed a numerical
studyof the effect of the argon gas ﬂow rate andpressure onoxygen
concentration in a unidirectional solidiﬁcation furnace. They
showed that oxygen concentration could be reduced at a speciﬁc
point in the crystal growth by using a higher argon ﬂow rate and a
higher furnace pressure. Machida et al. [9,10] showed experimen-
tally that the effects of the argon ﬂow rate and furnace pressure on
the distributions of oxygen concentrations in a sc-Si crystal are
dependent on the furnace structure. The oxygen concentration in
sc-Si crystal taken from the open-type hot zonemay be determined
by the evaporation of SiO on the free surface. As for the gas-
controlled hot zone, the oxygen concentration in a sc-Si crystalmay

higher axial position,which corresponds to the interface position of

amount of oxygen impurities released from the crucible into the
melt is controlled by the area of immersed surface of the crucible
wall. This decreases for higher solidiﬁcation because the surface
area of the crucible wall that is immersed lessens. From Figs. 2–4,
we can see that the oxygen concentration in the melt also depends
on the furnace pressure, the ﬂow pattern in the gas and melt
ﬁelds and the SiO evaporation. The SiO evaporation dominates in
the smaller solidiﬁcation fraction, while the melt-ﬂow pattern
becomes the governing factor for higher solidiﬁcation fractions.
This explains why, as seen in Fig. 5, the oxygen concentration is
higher when the furnace pressure is high, than when the latter is
low, for in the smaller solidiﬁcation fraction but is lower in the
higher solidiﬁcation fraction. On theother hand, the variation in the
oxygen concentration with axial position is less signiﬁcant with
a low furnace pressure than it is at high pressure. However, bubbles
may be generated on the free surface when the furnace pressure
is lower than the saturated vapor pressure of SiO [15]. Hence, an
excessively low furnace pressure has to be avoided in mc-Si ingot
growth in a DSS furnace.
4. Conclusion
Numerical simulations of the transient distributions of tem-
perature, velocity and concentrations of oxygen in themelt and SiO
gas in argon gas are carried out. The process of mc-Si ingot growth
takes place in a DSS furnace under four different furnace pressures.
Regardless of the furnace pressure, as the solidiﬁcation fraction
increases, the oxygen concentration reduces due to the reduction in
the amount of crucible surface immersed in the silicon melt. At
higher furnace pressures, the evaporation of SiO at the free surface
is lower. Therefore, for smaller solidiﬁcation fractions, the magni-
tude of the oxygen concentration in the melt is higher when the
furnace pressure is higher than when it is lower. For higher
solidiﬁcation fractions, the upper vortex in the melt disappears
and a small vortex appears in the upper central region due to the
cooling effect of the argon gas. The effect of furnace pressure on SiO
evaporation is less signiﬁcant since the ﬂowmotion is controlled by
the lower vortex. The small vortex blocks the transfer of oxygen
impurities carried by the lower vortex towards the central region.
Therefore, when the small vortex increases in size, the oxygen
concentration in the central region is signiﬁcantly reduced. This
occurswhen the solidiﬁcation fraction increases. Since the strength
of the small vortex is higher at a higher furnace pressure, the
oxygen concentration is lower in the central region.

1. Introduction 
Multicrystalline-silicon (mc-Si) wafers are commonly used in silicon solar cells.  Oxygen, 
one of the most important types of impurities, can cause thermal donor [1] and/or light-induced 
degradation [2] in solar cells.  During the process of mc-Si crystal growth, any oxygen impurity 
from the heated quartz crucible and the feedstock [3] is dissolved in the silicon melt to either 
become incorporated into the ingot or react chemically to silicon monoxide (SiO) gas and then 
evaporate at the free melt surface [4].  In the furnace, the argon gas is guided at a predetermined 
flow rate through the gas pipe and then carries the evaporated SiO gas away from the furnace.  
The oxygen concentration distribution in the melt and the SiO concentration distribution in the 
argon are affected strongly by the flow pattern because the Schmidt number (the ratio of 
viscosity diffusion rate to the mass diffusion rate) is larger than unity for oxygen impurities 
mixed in the silicon melt and SiO impurities mixed in the argon gas. [5, 6].  Therefore, the 
oxygen impurity originated from the crucible is carried by the melt motion towards the free melt 
interface and then the evaporated SiO gas is taken away by the argon gas.  The rate of 
evaporative oxygen lost at the free melt surface is affected by the oxygen concentration gradient 
in the thin diffusion boundary layer of Si melt and Ar gas near the free melt surface [7].  
Therefore, the oxygen concentration in the melt depends on the evaporated SiO gas 
concentration at the free melt surface [8, 9] and the flow speed of the argon gas near the free 
surface [10-12].  In order to increase the argon gas flow speed, the gas pipe is extended and 
connected to a gas flow guidance device above the free surface [11].  Therefore, a lower oxygen

Conclusion 
A series of simulations have been carried out to investigate the effect of a gas flow guidance 
device on the oxygen concentration in the melt during a DSS multicrystalline silicon growth 
process.  When the gas flow guidance device is installed, the gas flow motion near the free 
surface is enhanced and the gas diffusion boundary layer is decreased.  It carries more 
evaporated SiO gas outside the furnace.  Therefore, the oxygen concentration in the melt 
decreases.  In addition, the heat transfer near the free surface is also enhanced by the stronger 
gas flow, which produces a stronger thermo-capillary flow near the free surface.  As the 
solidification fraction increases, the melt vortex is easier to separate in comparison to that which 
occurs without gas flow guidance device.  The oxygen concentration in the melt has a strong 
blocking effect on the separated vortex near the central region of the melt-crystal interface.  
Therefore, the oxygen concentration in the melt can be decreased more significantly by the 
reduction of the vertical distance between the guidance device and the free surface.  The argon 
gas flow can be sped up near the tri-junction point by enlarging the horizontal length of the 
guidance device; the maximum oxygen concentration always appears near the tri-junction point.  
Hence, the oxygen concentration in the melt can be remarkably reduced when the gap between 
the crucible sidewall and the tip of the guidance device is smaller.

1. Introduction
The oxygen impurity incorporated in single crystalline-silicon
(sc-si) wafermay cause light-induced degradation in solar cells [1].
In the Czochralski (CZ) growth system the oxygen primarily
originates from the heated quartz crucible and the feedstock [2].
The higher the temperature at the quartz crucible wall, the higher
oxygen concentration will be dissolved in the silicon melt [3].
During the CZ growth, some of the dissolved oxygen impurity in the
melt will become incorporated into the ingot, while the rest of the
dissolved oxygen reacts chemically with the silicon oxide (SiO) gas
and vaporizes at the free surface to be carried away by argon gas
above the free surface. The Schmidt number, which represents the
comparison of the ability of the momentum diffusion to the
concentration diffusion, is approximately 10 and 3.5 for oxygen
impurity in the silicon melt and SiO impurity in the argon gas,
respectively. This means that the oxygen impurity in the silicon
melt and the SiO impurity in the argon gas are effectively
transported by the ﬂow motion. It is obvious that to maintain a
lower oxygen concentration in the as-grown silicon crystal, the
temperature of the crucible should be kept as low as possible so as
to minimize the oxygen generation. The ﬂow motion inside the
melt should bring oxygen impurity originating from the crucible
wall towards the free surface for vaporization. A lower furnace
pressure should be utilized to enhance the evaporation rate of
the oxygen, and a higher argon velocity along the free surface
should help to carry away the SiO gas.
Machida and coworkers [4,5] carried out experiments to
examine the effect of the argon gas ﬂow rate and furnace pressure
on the oxygen concentration in a CZ silicon crystal by installing a
gas controller in the furnace and the transverse magnetic ﬁeld.
Their results showed that the oxygen concentration (with a gas
controller) is proportional to the ﬂowvelocity of argon gas. This can
be achieved by either increasing the argon gas ﬂow rate or
decreasing the furnace pressure. The trend is reversed when the
transverse magnetic ﬁeld is applied to the CZ growth system. They
conjectured that the oxygen concentration might be controlled by
the melt ﬂow pattern. Togawa et al. [6,7] measured the oxygen
concentration in as-grownsilicon crystals producedby aCZ furnace
system using different crucible and crystal rotation rates. The
results showed that the oxygen concentration decreases as the
crucible rotation rate is increased and the crystal rotation rate is
ﬁxed but it varies insigniﬁcantly with the variation of crystal
rotation rate for a ﬁxed crucible rotation rate. Li et al. [8] have

4. Conclusion
In this study, numerical simulations of the growth of 8 in silicon
crystal have been carried out so as to ﬁnd the optimum combina-
tion of crucible and crystal rotation rates when the furnace
pressure and gas ﬂow rate remain constant.
When the crystal rotation rate is ﬁxed (13 rpm) and the crucible
rotates in the opposite direction to the crystal rotation, Taylor–
Proundman vortices appear in the region under the crystal. When
the crucible rotation rate increases, the suppression of the diffusion
of oxygen impurity from the crucible wall to the crystal–melt
interface rises because of the higher strength of the Taylor–
Proundman vortices. Because the heat transport from the crucible
wall to the crystal–melt interface is blocked by the Taylor–
Proundman and secondary vortices, the temperature on the
crucible wall rises for the higher crucible rotation rate. The
evaporation of oxygen impurity on the free surface decreases
due to the reduction of the size of the buoyancy–thermocapillary
vortices. Therefore, the amount of oxygen impurity diffused into
the melt towards the crystal–melt interface increases as the
crucible rate increases. When the crucible rotation rate is lower,
the suppression from the Taylor–Proundman and secondary
vortices is higher than the enhancement of the oxygen impurity
diffusion. For the higher crucible rotation rate, the trend is reversed.
Therefore, there is a crucible rotation rate for obtaining a lower and
more uniform oxygen concentration when the crystal rotation is
13 rpm. The lowest oxygen concentration in the crystal can be
achieved by adjusting the rotation rate of both crucible and crystal.

1. Introduction 
Oxygen impurity in single-crystallization silicon (sc-Si) solar cells can lead to 
light degradation or thermal donors, decreasing the efficiency of the solar cell.  The 
oxygen impurity mainly originates from the heated wall of the quartz crucible and the 
feedstock.  Some of the dissolved oxygen in the melt will be incorporated into the 
crystal, while the rest reacts chemically to form SiO gas, which then evaporates from 
the free surface and transferred outside the furnace by the argon gas. 
To improve the quality of sc-Si solar cell wafers, the oxygen concentration has to 
be managed, to prevent it from being incorporated into the Si crystal during the 
crystal growth process.  Heat shields and gas controllers are the major methods for

controller increases as the argon gas flow rate decreases.  They mentioned that the 
argon gas flow along the free surface can be speeded up by modification of the 
geometry of the heat shield to carry a larger quantity of silicon oxide gas away from 
the furnace.  The oxygen concentration in the melt can be reduced by speeding up 
the movement of argon gas along the free surface.  Oxygen impurities initiated from 
deformation of the free surface is not considered.  The differential equations

oxygen concentration isolines for three different heat shield shapes are similar, but the 
magnitude decreases for the case with the lower value of d.  From Figure 2, we can 
see that the maximum temperature of the crucible wall is the same (Tmax = 1710 K) 
for the three cases.  When d increases, the area of the melt-gas interface covered by 
the higher argon gas speed increases, and the speed of argon gas near the crucible wall 
temperature.  On the other hand, there is a reduction in the strength of the 
buoyancy-thermocapillary and Taylor-Proundman vortices (Figs. 2 and 4).  The 
transport of oxygen impurity originating from the bottom wall of the crucible towards 
the melt-crystal interface is prevented by the motion of the Taylor-Proundman vortex. 
The oxygen impurity originating from the sidewall of the crucible is carried towards 
the free surface by the motion of the buoyancy-thermocapillary vortex to then react 
chemically with the SIO and be vaporized into the argon gas [4].  There is a 
reduction in the preventive ability of the oxygen impurity towards the crystal-melt 
interface when the length of crystal increases.  When L > 300 mm, the average 
oxygen concentration at the melt-crystal interface increases for greater crystal length.  
This may be due to the reduction in the preventive ability of oxygen impurity towards 
the melt-crystal interface which is more significant than the reduction in the ability to 
release the oxygen impurity from the crucible wall.     
4. Conclusion 
In the present study, the effect of the heat shield shape on the oxygen 
concentration in the melt is investigated numerically.  The argon gas flow near the 
crucible wall can be sped up by enlarging the horizontal length of the heat shield.  
The maximum oxygen concentration always appears near the crucible-melt-gas 
interface due to the stronger thermocapillary-buoyancy convection in the melt.

   In this study, we perform numerical simulations to analyze the effect of the position 
of the heater on the thermal and flow fields and the oxygen concentration distribution 
during the industrial Cz silicon crystal growth process. The amount of oxygen released 
from the quartz crucible to the silicon melt during the growth process can be lowered by

crystal lengthens, there is a significant decrease in the gradient of the oxygen

reduction in the average oxygen concentration at the melt-crystal interface for longer 
crystal lengths because of the lower wall temperature, lessening of the contact surface 
between the crucible wall and the melt and the stronger Taylor-Proundman vortex.
