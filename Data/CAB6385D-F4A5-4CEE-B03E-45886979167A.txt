Biosensor Fabrication by Using Focus-ion-beam Milled Capillary Atomic-Force-Microscopy Tip”. In 
2009, our research team proposed an ultra-sensitive glucose sensor using a 
3-aminopropyltriethoxysilane (γ-APTES) and polydimethylsiloxane (PDMS)-treated hydrophobic 
fumed silica nanoparticle (NPs) mixture as the sensing membrane, based on a sub-micrometer scale 
polysilicon wire structure. The γ-APTES and fumed silica NPs mixture was directly coated onto the 
poly-Si wire region with the help of a focus-ion-beam (FIB) processed capillary 
atomic-force-microscope (C-AFM) tip. The glucose sensor showed an extremely wide linear-detection 
range (100 nM to 10 mM) with a channel current sensitivity as high as 5.33AmM
−1
cm
−2
 (or a channel 
conductance sensitivity of 70 μSmM−1), and a detection limit as low as 10 nM. The poly-Si wire sensor 
performed well over 100 successive trials. These results were submitted to Sensors and Actuators B: 
Chemical, and was accepted after a little over two months. (Sensors and Actuators B 142 (2009) 
273–279). In 2010, we reduced the line width of the polysilicon wire to 100 nm and investigated the

ultralow lowest detection limit of 32 pM. We found that the channel current disturbance caused by 
interference was below 7% for interference-to-glucose concentration ratios up to 600:1, and the leakage 
current of the organic/NPs nanocomposite membrane was reduced by three orders. It could be reused 
more than 1600 times without much sensitivity degradation. These results were published in Biosensors 
and Bioelectronics.(Biosensors and Bioelectronics (2010) 273–279 ) 
 
In 2009 and 2010, the main contributions of our plan were as follows: 
1.We were the first research team to apply a PDMS-treated fumed silica NPs and γ-APTES mixture in 
producing biosensors with poly-Si wires. 
2.We were the first research team to use the FIB-milled CAFM tip to accurately scan/coat an ultra-thin 
enzyme membrane of the silica NPs+γ-APTES mixture onto poly-Si wire. The silica NPs were well 
distributed in cured γ-APTES. 
3.By combining the use of γ-APTES and NPs, a sub-micrometer-scaled (0.75μm) polysilicon wire could 
achieve ultralow lowest detection limits and highly sensitive performance comparable to that of the Si 
nanowire. 
4.Our proposed biosensor is dry type, with no working and reference electrodes, and the interferents 
were not electrolyzed during glucose detection. Excellent selective detection ability was observed. 
5.The PDMS-treated silica NPs could tightly bind with the γ-APTES, achieving a stable 
crossing-linking structure which could resist the erosion of the hydrogen ions and hydrolysis. Good 
performances were obtained even after more than 1000 consecutive trials.

B. 漂移與長時間的穩定度方面 
一般所熟知的漂移是離子場效型生物感測器在感測過程中，當固定汲極與源極間的電
流時，閘極參考電位會隨時間改變，或謂起始電壓不穩定稱之。漂移的一般性解釋是電場強
化絕緣層內部離子遷移，以及在絕緣層/電解液界面非平衡電化學情況所引起。電解液在強
大的陽極電催化作用下，造成電子注入，在絕緣層內部形成空間電荷區及慢速表面效應。眾
所熟知，絕緣感測層，例如二氧化矽在水溶液中其表面會進行化學改質 ( chemical 
modified )，水會與二氧化矽表面反應形成 Si-OH 鍵結，造成表面有高濃度未橋接氧 
( non-bridging oxygen )鍵結，尤其當二氧化矽薄膜暴露在電解溶中情況會更嚴重。這使
得絕緣層表面的化學改質更容易進行，加速漂移。 
1998年，Shahriar Jamasb 提出 s-模型，可完整定量解釋 Al2O3、Si3N4、SiO2、Ta2O5
等閘極絕緣層的漂移現象，如圖 C012-2-6 所示。其假設與水相關的物質的傳輸受絕緣層表
面化學改質層中的捕捉缺陷所限制，而以跳躍/捕捉的傳輸方式緩慢的到達絕緣層反應介
面。以等效的電路來看，由於表面受水相關物質化學改質，介電常數下降，整體電容值下降，
為了要維持相同的 IDS 所需的通道電荷量，閘極電壓必須增加形成漂移。一般觀察時間在
20小時以內可以看到明顯的效應。 
酵素場效型生物感測器長時間的穩定度問題，主要由酵素感測薄膜與感測絕緣層或電
解電極在長時間的工作下會剝離，造成感測能力退化。一般評估的方式是在 4℃環境將酵素
薄膜浸泡於 pH 值 7 的生物緩衝溶液中，經過長時間後觀察其感測能力。以文獻上所發表的
結果來看，浸泡 30天，感測能力維持 80% 就算穩定。 
 
(二)研究目的 
本 99年度計畫主要目的在於研究多晶矽線乾式生物感測器的漂移與長時間的穩定度特
性，以及干擾源對於元件特性的影響，並將元件推進至奈米級領域，期能研製具備更低偵測
極限、更靈敏、更寬線性工作區間，同時擁有對葡萄糖極高的選擇性檢測能力，以及低漂移
與長時間的穩定特性。 
本計畫選擇以多晶矽奈米線為元件，於線上建構 GOD/γ-APTES/SiO2 酵素感測膜的結
構，製作高靈敏度生物感測器。本計畫所探討的奈米線係指以電子束蝕刻線寬 100nm的多晶
矽線。為了克服能將酵素感測材料精確的塗佈於多晶矽奈米線上，我們以特別訂製的平坦式
圓錐型傳導式電子顯微鏡 CAFM探針，利用聚焦式離子束 FIB加工研磨成毛細管形(如圖 1)，

鍵結，再利用傳導式電子顯微鏡將γ-APTES 定位、塗佈並固定於多晶矽奈米線上；次利用
毛細管形探針將極微量葡萄糖氧化酵素塗佈於γ-APTES 上產生交聯形成血糖酵素感測層。

Abstract 
 
This study investigates the line width dependence on 
glucose analyst characteristics of PSW with a surface 
modified by 3-aminopropyltriethoxysilane mixed with 
polydimethylsiloxane-treated hydrophobic fumed silica 
NPs plus ultra-violet illumination (-APTES+NPs+UV). 
It is found that the sensitivity increases as the PSW line 
width decreases. The improvement in sensitivity due to 
UV illumination tends to saturate at exposure time 120 s, 
which is independent of line width. The channel current 
density change J versus surface to volume ratio of the 
PSW with different line-widths showed linear behavior 
for all of the coated membranes. The interference 
immunity tends to rise as the line width increases. For a 
500 nm wide PSW, the disturbance caused by 
acetaminophen (AP) interference can be kept below 
3.5% of the original sensing current density for 
AP-to-glucose concentration ratios up to 600:1 if the 
surface is modified with -APTES+NPs+UV. 
 
 
1. Introduction  
 
Glucose level monitoring is important in clinical 
diagnostics for patients with diabetes mellitus, and there 
is a significant need for a highly sensitive and reliable 
glucose detector. Various detection methodologies such 
as amperometric, potentiometric and impedimetric 
glucose sensors have been developed [1-6]. In our 
previous paper, we successfully combined the 
applications of the NPs to the PSW glucose sensor. We 
demonstrated the coating of a -APTES and NPs mixture 
layer onto the surface of a PSW with the help of a 
capillary atomic-force-microscope (C-AFM) tip [7]. The 
resulting sensor showed an excellent linear glucose 
detection range from 0.1 μM to 10 mM, with a 
low-detection limit down to 10 nM. In the present paper, 
we report our investigation on the line-width dependence 
characteristics of the PSW glucose sensor, including 
sensitivity, the effects of ultraviolet light illumination, 
and glucose selective detection in the presence of an 
interferent. 
 
 
2.  Results and Discussion 
 
Figure 1 shows the SEM images of the PSW. The line 
widths are 100, 200, 300, and 500 nm, respectively, and 
the channel lengths are all 3μm long. The sensing 
membranes γ-APTES or a mixture of the γ-APTES with 
polydimethylsiloxane-treated hydrophobic fumed silica 
nanoparticles (γ-APTES+NPs) were coated on the 
surface of the PSW with a FIB processed C-AFM tip. 
The details of the  fabrication processes were presented 
in our previous work [7]. Figure 2 compares the current 
density change J as a function of glucose concentration 
for the PSW with different line-widths coated 
respectively with -APTES only, -APTES+NPs, and 
-APTES+NPs plus a 90s UV illumination. The 
sensitivity increases as the gate-width decreases. The 
-APTES+NPs plus UV illumination process showed 
excellent performance for sensitivity, linearity and low 
detection limit.

3. Summary 
 
In this paper, we reported that PSW glucose sensors with 
smaller line-widths exhibit higher sensitivity. The good 
linearity in J versus the surface to volume ratio plot 
indicates that we can obtain uniform membranes with 
well-distributed NPs by the C-AFM coating. The PSW 
with the wider line-width has a smaller channel electric 
field and tends to exhibit better immunity to interference. 
The high interference immunity is believed to be due to 
the fact that the PSW is dry-type and that the membrane 
quality is improved by the introduction of NPs.
