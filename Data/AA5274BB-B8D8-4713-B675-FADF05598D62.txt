摘 要 
本研究將不同Fe2O3含量添加於Li2O-Fe2O3-MnO2-CaO-P2O5-SiO2（LFMCPS）
系玻璃陶瓷中，探討 Fe2O3 添加量對玻璃陶瓷結晶相種類、顯微結構、磁特性、
發熱現象及生物相容性的影響。由 DTA 結果得知，LFMCPS 系玻璃塊材與粉末
樣品之結晶放熱峰溫度不同，樣品呈現表面結晶現象，結晶放熱峰溫度則因
Fe2O3 添加量改變而有不同的趨勢。由 X-光繞射分析結果顯示，LFMCPS 系玻
璃陶瓷經熱處理溫度 850℃持溫 4 小時後，所形成結晶相有錳酸鋰、矽灰石、矽
酸鋰、Ca(Mn,Ca)Si2O6、Li2Ca4Si4O13等。添加 Fe2O3之 LFMCPS 系玻璃陶瓷除
了上述結晶相外，亦生成 (Li,Mn)Ferrite 及 Li2FeMn3O8二種含鐵相。由 SQUID
及 EPR 的分析結果可知，LFMCPS 玻璃陶瓷的 Fe2O3添加量為 4 wt%時，經溫
度 850℃熱處理樣品呈現超順磁性與鐵磁性混合，Fe2O3 添加量高於 8wt%時，
樣品呈現鐵磁性。由 LFMCPS 玻璃陶瓷的發熱現象得知，SAR 值隨含鐵量增高
而上升，當含鐵量為 24wt%時 SAR 值則呈現下降趨勢，且與磁滯損失有相關性。
從生物相容性測試中發現含有 Li、Ca 離子的結晶相隨著浸泡時間的有被溶出的
趨勢，Fe、Mn 離子則未被檢出。 
 
I. 緒論 
1-1 前言 
磁性材料及磁原理廣泛地被應用於醫療上用途，磁場及磁性材料應用在腫瘤
治療已成為現今磁療技術的主要項目之一，磁性粒子於醫療上的應用主要在癌
細胞的高溫療程方面，根據研究顯示，癌細胞比正常細胞不耐熱，由於癌細胞
組織缺乏微血管及神經組織散熱系統，癌細胞約在 43 ℃溫度下會死亡，而正
常細胞不會產生損傷。臨床高溫療程所使用的能量通常為微波、超音波等，是
以高能量微波直接照射腫瘤部位來破壞腫瘤組織，但能量穿透正常組織到達腫
瘤處時，有部分能量會被正常組織吸收而使強度減弱，影響高溫療程的效果。
若改用鐵磁性材料則可避免能量損失的現象，特別是鐵磁性玻璃陶瓷，其治療
原理是將鐵磁性玻璃陶瓷注射至腫瘤處，施予一交變磁場，利用加磁、去磁過
程產生磁性損耗，此過程產生的熱能來加熱腫瘤組織來達到療效。鐵磁性玻璃
陶瓷成份的選擇會影響到後續治療過程的良劣，因此可以藉由玻璃成分、熱處
理條件對其物理、化學、磁性性質作更進一步的控制[1-9]。 
常被使用於高溫療程之鐵磁性玻璃系統成分為 Fe2O3-CaO-SiO2，經結晶化熱
處理後所生成的磁性相為赤鐵礦(Hematite)或磁鐵礦(magnetite)。1983 年 A. A. 
Luderer 等人以 Fe2O3-P2O5-Li2O-Al2O3-SiO2系列玻璃陶瓷，經熱處理後生成赤鐵
礦，應用於高溫療程相關之研究[1]。Y. K. Lee、S. Y. Choi 等人在 1996 年則是
利用 Fe2O3-CaO-SiO2 系列玻璃陶瓷，經結晶化熱處理後，產生的主要磁性相為
磁鐵礦[2,3]。Y. Ebisawa 等人則是研究 FeO-Fe2O3-CaO-SiO2系列玻璃陶瓷經結
晶化熱處理後，磁性相磁鐵礦之性質[4-6]。為獲得磁性質更優良的玻璃陶瓷系
統，亦有添加成核劑的相關研究出現，其中常使用的成核劑為 P2O5。如 K. Singh
等人所使用的 SiO2-Na2O-Fe2O3-CaO- P2O5-B2O3系列玻璃陶瓷，其研究結果顯示
成核劑 P2O5的添加能使玻璃陶瓷系統具有較佳的生物相容性與結晶性[7-9]。含
鐵相晶粒尺寸控制在單磁區轉換成多磁區之臨界尺寸時，系統具有最優良的鐵
磁特性，其矯頑磁力值最強，若含鐵相晶粒尺寸小於此臨界尺寸時，則呈現超
順磁特性，其矯頑磁力值趨近於零，故控制含鐵相之晶粒尺寸，可獲得磁特性
優良的磁性材料[1-9]。 
 
1-2 研究動機與目的 
本研究探討不同的結晶化溫度及 Fe2O3 添加量對於 LFMCPS 系列玻璃陶瓷
之結晶相種類、顯微結構、磁特性及發熱現象的影響，並初步觀察此系列玻璃
陶瓷相容性的關係。  
2.1 玻璃熔製 
本研究所用玻璃原料為 Li2O-Fe2O3-MnO2-CaO-P2O5-SiO2系玻璃，分兩系
列：一為 24.724g Li2CO3添加，變化 Fe2O3含量；一為添加 10g Li2CO3，變化
Fe2O3含量，其組成如表 1 所列。原料稱重後以球磨機混合 2 小時，混合後原料
傾置於 1200℃預熱之白金坩堝，再升溫至 1350℃持溫 1 小時，使原料熔解均勻，
熔融的玻璃膏倒入鐵板模型，再將注入玻璃的模型置於 450℃的退火爐中持溫 2
小時，以消除因急速冷卻而產生的殘留應力。待冷卻至室溫後即可獲得黑色長
柱狀玻璃。利用慢速切割機將長條玻璃切成厚度約 3mm 的塊狀，並做結晶化熱

3.2.2 F2 成分玻璃陶瓷之 X 光繞射分析 
圖 6 為含 4wt﹪Fe2O3之 LFMCPS 系玻璃塊材樣品在不同溫度下，分別經 4
小時結晶化熱處理之 X-光繞射圖譜。F2-600-4h 樣品之 X-光繞射圖譜，除了非
晶質繞射峰外，出現與 F1 樣品相同的錳酸鋰繞射峰。當結晶化溫度上升至 640
℃時，在 2θ角度為 30°位置出現矽灰石微弱的繞射峰。結晶化溫度上升至 660
℃時，除了錳酸鋰及矽灰石之繞射峰外，還出現 Ca(Mn,Ca)Si2O6 結晶相。當結
晶化溫度為 720℃時，結晶相種類與 F1 成分樣品相似，錳酸鋰之繞射峰強度明
顯減弱，矽灰石、Li2Ca4Si4O13、Ca(Mn,Ca)Si2O6 結晶相析出。在結晶化溫度為
850℃時，矽酸鋰繞射峰出現，在 2θ角度為 18.7°位置有一明顯的繞射峰存在，
經鑑定應為 Li2FeMn3O8之結晶相。 
    將此 F2 玻璃樣品研磨成粉末，結晶化熱處理 600℃時，主要為錳酸鋰結晶
相為主。結晶化溫度 640℃時，出現矽灰石結晶相，溫度再升高至 660℃時，樣
品除了出現矽灰石繞射峰外，有 Li2Ca4Si4O13、Ca(Mn,Ca)Si2O6等結晶相析出。
720 ℃之結晶相與 660℃相同外，尚發現有微弱之未知繞射峰，與 F1 成分玻璃
陶瓷之 X-光繞射分析結果比較推測，可能為含鐵相之繞射峰。含鐵相之繞射峰
強度明顯變強，經鑑定發現含鐵相可能為 LiFe5O8(Lithium Ferrite)、
MnFe2O4(Manganese Ferrite)或是 FeFe2O4 (Magnetite)結晶相，其繞射峰亦有偏移
現象，需與其它分析結果對照，方能確認其含鐵相。熱處理 850℃時，除了和
720℃一樣有矽灰石、Li2Ca4Si4O13、Ca(Mn,Ca)Si2O6外，錳酸理結晶相消失，生
成新的矽酸結晶相。

3.2.4 F4 成分玻璃陶瓷之 X 光繞射分析 
F4 系列玻璃樣品於結晶化溫度 600℃下，除了非晶質繞射峰外，亦有明顯之
錳酸鋰繞射峰。結晶化溫度 640℃時，仍以錳酸鋰為主相，矽灰石之繞射峰逐漸
增強。當溫度上升至 660℃時，F4 樣品之結晶相有錳酸鋰、矽灰石、Li2Ca4Si4O13、
Ca(Mn,Ca)Si2O6等。溫度持續上升至 720℃時，錳酸鋰繞射峰稍微減弱，除了有
矽灰石、Li2Ca4Si4O13、Ca(Mn,Ca)Si2O6 之結晶相存在，亦發現有微弱含鐵結晶
相之繞射峰。在 850℃時，矽酸鋰繞射峰出現，2θ角度為 18.7°位置有一明顯的
繞射峰存在，經鑑定應為 Li2FeMn3O8之結晶相。 
     將此 F4 玻璃樣品研磨成粉末，結晶化熱處理 600℃時，主要為錳酸鋰結
晶相為主。結晶化溫度 640℃時，出現矽灰石結晶相，溫度再升高至 660℃時，
樣品除了出現矽灰石繞射峰外，有 Li2Ca4Si4O13、Ca(Mn,Ca)Si2O6等結晶相析出。
720 ℃之結晶相與 660℃相同外，含鐵相之繞射峰強度明顯變強，經鑑定發現含
鐵相可能為 LiFe5O8(Lithium Ferrite)、MnFe2O4(Manganese Ferrite)或是 FeFe2O4 
(Magnetite)結晶相，其繞射峰亦有偏移現象，需與其它分析結果對照，方能確認
其含鐵相。熱處理 850℃時，除了和 720℃一樣有矽灰石、Li2Ca4Si4O13、
Ca(Mn,Ca)Si2O6外，錳酸理結晶相消失，生成新的矽酸結晶相。 
3.2.5 F5 成分玻璃陶瓷之 X 光繞射分析 
F5 系列玻璃樣品於結晶化溫度 600℃下，除了非晶質繞射峰外，亦有明顯之
錳酸鋰繞射峰。結晶化溫度 640℃時，仍以錳酸鋰為主相，未知相之繞射峰逐漸
增強。當溫度上升至 660℃時，F5 樣品之結晶相有錳酸鋰、矽灰石、Li2Ca4Si4O13、
具有表面結晶的現象。LFMCPS 系玻璃陶瓷樣品經結晶化熱處理溫度 720℃持
溫 4 小時後，樣品剖面呈現雙層結構，中間層為玻璃區，玻璃相外圍則為結晶
區。不論 Fe2O3添加量多寡，在此熱處理溫度條件下，LFMCPS 系玻璃陶瓷樣
品具有雙層結構。經結晶化熱處理溫度 850℃持溫 4 小時之樣品剖面則未產生雙
層結構，由此得知，在 850℃熱處理條件下，LFMCPS 系玻璃陶瓷樣品已具有
良好結晶性質。LFMCPS 系玻璃陶瓷樣品結晶相成長是由表面往樣品內部成
長。綜合 DTA 曲線分析、XRD 繞射分析以及樣品剖面觀察，推論 LFMCPS 系
玻璃陶瓷具有表面結晶的現象。

4.7 Fe2O3含量對 LFMCPS 系玻璃陶瓷磁特性影響 
 根據各種磁性量測結果，添加不同 Fe2O3含量之 LFMCPS 系玻璃陶瓷樣品，
其系統中含鐵相的生成與樣品之磁特性有直接的影響。當結晶化熱處理溫度 720
℃時，F2、F3 樣品之 X-光繞射圖譜中可發現含鐵相(Li,Mn)Ferrite 的微弱繞射訊
號，隨含鐵量增多，含鐵相繞射訊號越顯著。但含鐵相之生成未使系統的磁特
性產生大的改變。從磁滯曲線來看，F2、F3 樣品皆呈弱鐵磁性，根據 M. Hayashi
等人之研究指出[16]，此系統中磁性相數量少，磁性粒子均勻分佈於基地中，其
交互作用力微弱所致，而 F4、F5 因含鐵量多使得鐵磁性現象較顯著。磁滯損耗
與發熱量可看出相關性，對於添加 24.724g Li2CO3 玻璃陶瓷樣品，當含鐵量小
於 8wt%時，由於含鐵相(Li,Mn)ferrite 結晶相量少，呈現微弱鐵磁性，磁滯環面

4.結論 
一、Fe2O3添加量影響 LFMCPS 系列玻璃陶瓷的結晶行為。LFMCPS 系玻璃陶
瓷經結晶化溫度 850℃持溫 4 小時後所形成的結晶相有錳酸鋰、矽灰石、矽
酸鋰、Ca(Mn,Ca)Si2O6、Li2Ca4Si4O13。添加 Fe2O3樣品除了上述結晶相外，
亦會生成含鐵相(Li,Mn)Ferrite 及 Li2FeMn3O8二種含鐵相生成。 
 
二、Fe2O3的添加量會影響 LFMCPS 系玻璃陶瓷之磁性行為。含 4 wt%Fe2O3，
經結晶化熱處理溫度 850℃持溫 4 小時樣品呈現超順磁性與鐵磁性的混合
行為，超順磁性源自於粒徑使吋在 nm 級之(Li,Mn)Ferrite 含鐵相，含鐵量
越多，(Li,Mn)Ferrite 結晶相之 Mn/Fe 比值越高，使得(Li,Mn)Ferrite 顆粒尺
寸達μm 級而使磁性發生改變，呈現鐵磁性。 
 
三、LFMCPS 系玻璃陶瓷之含鐵相對發熱現象之影響。當含鐵量增加至 16 wt%
時，磁滯環面積有變大的趨勢，SAR 值亦有增大的現象，然而當含鐵量達
24 wt%時，磁滯環面積減小，SAR 值隨之變小。 
 
四、LFMCPS 系玻璃陶瓷成分對生物相容性的影響。樣品中生成之含鐵相在 SBF
溶液中並沒有溶出的現象，然而 Li、Ca 元素會隨著浸泡天數而溶解出來。

Abstract 
Magnetic properties of Li2O- MnO2-CaO-P2O5-SiO2 (LMCPS) glasses doped with various amount 
of Fe2O3 were investigated.  There is a dramatic change in the magnetic property of pristine 
LMCPS after the addition of Fe2O3 and crystallized at 850 °C for 4 hours.  Both the electron 
paramagnetic resonance (EPR) and magnetic susceptibility measurements showed that the 
glass-ceramics with 4 at.% Fe2O3 exhibited the coexistence of superparamagnetism and 
ferromagnetism at room temperature. When the Fe2O3 contents were higher than 8 at.%, the 
LMCPS glasses showed ferromagnetism behavior.  The complex magnetic behavior is due to the 
distribution of (Li, Mn)ferrite particle sizes driven by Fe2O3 content.  The thermal induced 
hysteresis loss of the crystallized LMCPS glass ceramics were characterized under an alternating 
magnetic field.  The energy dissipations of the crystallized LMCPS glass ceramics were 
determined by the concentration and Mn/Fe ratios of Li(Mn, Fe)ferrite magnetic phase formed in 
the glass ceramics. 
phases of these glass ceramics of different iron oxide contents were characterized by X-ray 
diffractions (XRD) and the corresponding magnetic behaviors were studied by superconducting 
quantum interference device (SQUID) magnetometer and paramagnetic resonance (EPR). The 
hyperthermia behaviors of the LMCPS glass ceramics were investigated as well. 
 
2. Experimental Procedure 
Reagent grade powders of Li2CO3, MnO2, Fe2O3, CaCO3, Ca3(PO4)2 and SiO2 were used for 
preparing the LMFCPS glasses. The glass consisting of 25 at.% Li2O, 8 at.% MnO2, 20 at.% CaO, 
2 at% P2O5 and 45 at.% SiO2 were doped with different amounts of Fe2O3. They were labeled as F1, 
F2, F3, F4 and F5 for 0, 4, 8, 16 and 24 at.% Fe2O3 addition, respectively. Detail procedures for the 
LMCPS glass preparation were described in the previous report [15]. The LMCPS glasses were 
heat-treated at different temperatures for 4 hours.  
The crystalline phases formed in the LMFCPS glass ceramics were analyzed using an X-ray 
diffractometer (Rigaku, Ultima IV, Japan) with Cu Kα radiation and a Ni filter at a scanning rate 
(2θ) of 2◦/min. Thin foils for scanning transmission electron microscopy (STEM, JEOL 3010, 
Japan) measurements were prepared via the conventional technique: the sample was sliced into a 
thickness of ~200 μm using a diamond-embedded saw, and then lapped to a thickness of ~30 μm 
mechanically followed by ion beam thinning to electron transparency. Chemical microanalysis was 
conducted using an energy-dispersive X-ray spectrometer (EDS) (Link eXL-II, Oxford, UK) 
equipped with STEM. The accelerating voltage for STEM was 200 kV. The EPR spectra were 
recorded with an X-band (9 GHz) EMX-10 spectrometer (Bruker E580, Germany) in the 
temperature range of 4 – 300 K. 
Magnetic measurements were carried out using a superconducting quantum interference 
device (SQUID, Quantum Design MPMS5, USA) magnetometer with the applied field up to 6T 
and magnetic field frequency of 13.6 Hz. Zero-field-cooled (ZFC) and field-cooled (FC) curves 
were taken between 5 and 400 K, for different values of cooling field HFC (10 Oe, H ,70 kOe). The 
annealed LMCPS glass ceramics were sealed inside quartz tubes for conducting the electron beam 
to be detected in the powder form, either. Nevertheless, LiMn2O4 and Li2FeMn3O8 phases were 
formed on the surface of the LMCPS glass ceramics showing the surface nucleation behaviors of 
those crystalline phases.  
Fig. 1 depicts the EPR spectra taken at 77 K and 300 K for F1, F2, and F3 LMCPS glass 
ceramics crystallized at 720 °C.  The room temperature EPR spectrum of F1 showed a single 
Lorentzian line shape with a line width (ΔHpp) of ~ 570 Gauss and a g-value of 2.05±0.01.  
Adding 4 at.% and 8 at.% Fe2O3 into F1 resulted in similar EPR spectra with ΔHpp (g-value) of 583 
(2.03±0.01) and 585 Gauss (2.01±0.01), respectively (Fig. 1(a)).  By lowering the temperature to 
77 K, there was negligible change in the spectrum line shape, ΔHpp and g-value for each sample, as 
comparing to its spectrum recorded at room temperature (see Fig. 1(b)).  The ion species and their 
surrounding environment causing the resonance absorption in EPR measurements were similar to 
those of 720 oC heat-treated F1, F2, and F3 LMCPS glass ceramics. Thus, the crystallization 
condition employed had subtle effect on developing new magnetic phase with Fe ions.  According 
to the x-ray diffraction (XRD) results, the major crystalline phases formed in 720 oC heat-treated 
F1, F2 and F3 LMCPS glass ceramics were LiMn2O4, CaSiO3, Li2Ca4Si4O13, and Ca(Ca, Mn)Si2O6.  
LiMn2O4 and (Li, Mn)ferrite were the minor phase for both F2 and F3.  By comparing the double 
integration of EPR spectra in Fig. 1(a), there was less than 8% increase in the additional iron ions at 
8 at% addition of Fe2O3.  Therefore, the measured absorption lines originate primary from Mn4+ 
ions.  Iron ion with valence states, Fe3+ and Fe2+, were present in the glass ceramic.  Only Fe3+ 
showed the EPR signal at room temperature though both Fe3+ and Fe2+ were paramagnetic [18,19]. 
The Fe3+ resonance typically characterized at g-values of ~ 4.3 and ~2.1 [20-25] had negligible 
contribution to the EPR spectra of F2 and F3, suggesting that insignificant amount of Fe3+ ions 
were incorporated into a crystallite.  Thus, the magnetic properties were similar in those three 
compounds regardless of the addition of Fe2O3. 
Since adding Fe2O3 into the pristine F1 LMCPS glassy ceramic and crystallized at 720 °C 
showed no apparent changes in magnetic properties, we further rise up the crystallization 
temperature to 850 °C for the same time period.  Fig. 2 exhibits EPR spectra of individual 850 oC

The distinct narrow component observed at g = 2.05 in the room temperature EPR absorption 
line of F2 crystallized at 850 °C was a signature of superparamagnetic resonance [29,30].  The 
superparamagnetism is due to thermally induced fluctuations of the directions of magnetism in 
single-domain particles below a certain “critical size.”  The single-domain particles exhibit 
paramagnetic property in the thermal energy dominating regime while those shows ferromagnetism 
as spin-spin interaction prevails.  The transition of paramagnetism to ferromagnetism can be 
distinguished from the response of magnetic susceptibility under zero field cooled (ZFC) and field 
cooled (FC) conditions.   
Fig. 3 exhibits magnetic susceptibility (χ) as a function of temperature (T) for F2 and F3 in 
temperature range of 5 K–300 K under the conditions of ZFC and FC.  There was a significant 
difference in the behavior of χ (T) between ZFC and FC conditions in F2.  The measured magnetic 
susceptibility under field cooled (χ FC) decreased as T raised, while that obtained in the zero field 
cooled (χ ZFC) increased with the increase in T and gradually reached a maximum at 270 K before 
decreasing.  The observed transition behavior of χ ZFC at 270 K was a typical characteristics of 
superparamagnetism [31-35] with blocking temperature Tb = 270 K.  Above Tb, thermal 
fluctuations predominated over anisotropic magnetic interaction resulting in a paramagnetic 
behavior.  The application of external magnetic field above Tb overcame the thermal fluctuations 
to line up the spin orientation leading to the breaking of magnetic anisotropic interaction below Tb 
causing a dramatically enhancement in χFC.  The observed temperature-driven transition of 
magnetic susceptibility under magnetic field free condition confirmed the detected 
superparamagnetism at 300 K in EPR absorption line and explained the absent of the 
superparamagnetic resonance below 300 K in our measuring temperature steps.  
For F3 crystallized at 850 °C, χ FC decreased rapidly initially followed by a linear decrease 
with the increasing T. χ ZFC(T) exhibited the similar behavior as χ FC(T), showing the paramagnetism 
of the glassy ceramic.  The result was also demonstrated in EPR measurements showing 
size of F4 ad F5 glass ceramics were less than 1 μm, and the grains were in single domain state[39]. 
Energy dissipation of single domain grain is proportional to the applied field frequency, 1.0αP f . 
The heating power of those samples can be deciphered from the area of the hysteresis loop and 
applied field frequency[40]. Although F5 glass contented 8 at% Fe2O3 more than that of F4 glass, 
Both F4 and F5 had approximately the same saturated magnetization and similar coercive forces. 
The result indicated the crystallite size of ferrite phases were comparable [38]. For measurements 
with applied frequency 13.6 Hz, the energy losses of each hysteresis loops were 234, 1238, 1986, 
and 1890 erg/g for F2, F3, F4, and F5 samples, respectively. The F4 had the largest coercive field 
and energy loss among the LMCPS glass ceramics. As reported [41], the magnetization(M) and 
remanent magnetization(Mr) increased with increasing Mn content in the Ni-Zn-Mn-Fe-O ferrite. In 
this study, F4 had relatively high Mn/Fe ratio than F5 did. The magnetizations of F4 were higher 
than that of F5. 
Fig. 6 is the SAR characterizations of the bulk forms of LMCPS glass ceramics under different 
heat-treatment conditions. The heat-treatment-free LMCPS glass ceramic had SAR values as low as 
pure water. After heat-treatment, the SAR values increased due to the formation of Li(Mn,

heat generation calculated from the magnetization curves. 
 
Conclusion

at.% into the glass-ceramic.  Heat-treating these compounds at 850 °C for 4 h promotes the

glass-ceramic.  Based on the TEM images, those (Li,Mn)ferrite particles have a range of particle 
size distribution and the concentration of Fe2O3 determines the average size of those particles.

crystallization LMFCPS glass. 
 
2. Experimental procedure 
2.1 Sample preparation 
  The reagent grade powders Li2CO3, MnO2, Fe2O3, Ca3(PO4)2, CaCO3 and SiO2 were used for 
preparing the LMFCPS glass. The glass composition was (by weight percent) 10.0% Li2O, 9.0% 
MnO2, 16.0% Fe2O3, 25.0% CaO, 5.0% P2O5, and 35.0% SiO2. All samples were 50 to 100 g 
batches of the specified composition that were accurately weighed and well premixed. The batches 
were transferred to a mullite crucible and melted at 1450oC in an electric furnace for 2 h. After 
  The typical DTA curve of a LMFCPS glass powder heated at a rate of 10oC/min in a static air 
is shown in Fig. 1. It was found that the glass transition temperature, Tg, was around 506oC. Single 
glass transition temperatures may suggest the existence of a compositionally homogeneous glass 
state. Yanacopaulos and Kasap [16],who used DSC heating and cooling on bulk and film 
amorphous selenium, pointed out that Tg refers to the onset of glass transition. On the other hand, 
Ray [17] also reported that Tg corresponds with the temperature at which the network acquires 
mobility and changes from a rigid into a plastic structure. Moreover, Fig. 1 also reveals that 
LMFCPS glass has one broad exothermic temperature peak at 674 oC, and that the highest sharp 
exothermic peak is at 764oC.  The heat-treated temperature selected corresponded to the DTA 
result. 
3.2 Crystal structure of LMFCPS glass 
  The XRD patterns of LMFCPS glass crystallized at various temperatures for 2 h are shown in 
Fig.2. The effect of heat treatment temperature on the progressive development of phase 
transformation are noted as follows: (a) the glassy state was still maintained from 550 to 650oC, (b) 
the lithium silicate (Li2SiO3), β-wollastonite (β-CaSiO3) and lithium orthophosphate (Li3PO4) 
phases occurred when the sample crystallized at 750oC, and (c) when heat-treated at 850oC, the 
lithium silicate, β-wollastonite, and Li3PO4 phases were still remarkably sharp. Moreover, the 
magnetite (FeFe2O4) was first evident appear and a minor crystalline phase, triphylite 
[Li(Mn0.5Fe0.5)PO4], also shows up. 
  Fig. 3 shows the XRD pattern of LMFCPS heat-treated at 850oC for various amounts of time. It 
shows that the same phases as those in the sample crystallized at 850oC for 2 h. i.e. The crystallized 
phases in the LMFCPS glass-ceramics did not change with dwelling time when heat-treated at the 
same temperature. 
  The crystallization kinetic and magnetic properties of iron oxide containing 
25Li2O-8MnO2-20CaO-2P2O5-45SiO2 glass (LMCPS, 0≦Fe2O3≦8 at% extra addition) 
heat-treated at various temperatures for 4 h were reported by Hsi et al. [18]. They pointed out that 
0.5CaF-xFe2O3 (5≦x≦20wt%) glasses were heat-treated at 1050oC for 3 h, hydroxyapatite, 
manetic, and wollastonite phases were identified in all crystallization samples. An additional phase 
of akermanite (Ca2MgSi2O7) appeared in crystallized samples with high Fe2O3 content. A 
comparison of the results of present study with those of previous studies [11,19] shows that, except 
for the finding that the magnetic and wollastonite were the same in all crystallized samples, all 
phases in the present study were similar to those in previous studies [11,19]. This phenomenon was 
also caused by differences in glass composition and heat-treatment temperature, which created the 
different crystalline phases in the glass-ceramic samples. 
 
3.3 Microstructure of a LMFCPS glass ceramics 
The SEM microstructure of a LMFCPS glass crystallized at 850oC for various amounts of time 
and EDS results are shown in Figs. 4 and 5, respectively. Fig. 4(a) shows the morphology of a 
LMFCPS glass crystallized at 850oC for 2 h, which reveals that the microstructure can be divided 
into three areas: the light-gray region, the dark-gray region, and dispersed particles included in the 
light-gray region. Fig. 5 shows the EDS results for the three regions shown in Fig. 4(a). It can be 
seen that the light-gray region primarily contains the elements Si, Ca, and O; the deep-gray region 
primarily contains the elements Si and O, and dispersed particles are (Fe, Mn, P)-rich phase. 
According to the results of the XRD and EDS, the light-gray region is β-CaSiO3, the deep-gray 
region is Li2SiO3, and the dispersed particle region is FeFe2O4 and Li(Mn0.5Fe0.5)PO4. Fig. 4(a) 
shows that the lath forms of β-CaSiO3 and Li2SiO3. Some constitutent parts of sample had unique 
direction growth which are indicated by arrows. On the other hand, Fig. 4(a) also shows many 
particles dispersed around the boundaries of the β-wollastonite matrix. The small and large particle 
sizes are about 150 nm and 2.0-3.0 μm, respectively. The SEM morphology for a crystallization 
time increase from 2 to 8 h is shown in Fig. 4(b). It shows that the lath structure of β-CaSiO3 has 
interrupt and become shorten. In addition, the lath Li2SiO3 and β-CaSiO3 were intersection at 
interrupt point.

β-CaSiO3 matrix. Many Fe-rich particles dispersed around the boundaries of the β-wollastonite
