   There are six major topics investigated in the subproject I. It contains (1) luminescence 
efficiency of InGaN multiple-quantum-well ultraviolet light-emitting diodes, (2) optimization of 
InGaN-based 400 nm UV LED structure, (3) low resistance, high optical reflectivity and high 
thermal stability ohmic contacts applied for flip-chip ultraviolet light-emitting diodes, (4) the 
development of high crystal quality and conductivity AlGaN epilayer, (5) optimization of 365 nm 
UV LED structure and (6) 365 nm UV LED structure grown on SiON stripe-patterned sapphire 
substrates. 
In the topic (1), the electroluminescence efficiency of In0.06Ga0.94N/GaN multiple-quantum-well 
ultraviolet light emitting diodes (UV LEDs) with emission wavelength of 400 nm has been 
investigated and compared with blue (470 nm) LEDs. Based on their injection current-dependent 
characteristics under dc and pulsed operation, it can be concluded that carrier overflow is the 
dominant factor that affects the external quantum efficiency of UV LED before thermal effects 
take over. It is experimentally shown that increasing the number of quantum wells is necessary to 
alleviate the carrier overflow issue and improve the luminescence efficiency of the UV LEDs. In 
the topic (2), it is found that adding slight amount of Al into GaN for a barrier layer does not 
degrade the crystal quality. Instead, it leads to better carrier confinement in In0.06Ga0.94N quantum 
well. The maximum output power and external quantum efficiency of a 300×300 µm2 packaged

the topic (3), Ti/WSi/Ti/Au and Pd/NiO/Al/Ti/Au, low resistance, high optical reflectivity and
