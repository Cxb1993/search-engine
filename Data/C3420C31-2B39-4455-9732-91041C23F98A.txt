摘要 
 本研究中所使用的材料為鎂-鋰-鋁-鋅作為基本成份並添加微量鈧金屬之合金，對
LAZ1141+0.1wt%Sc 與 LAZ1191+0.2wt%Sc 鎂合金進行冷加工以及自然時效處理等製程，並在實驗中
使用了軋延機、OM、Vickers 硬度機、拉伸詴驗機等儀器完成所需的製程及觀察各製程所產生之微結
構與機械性質上的變化。 
    實驗結果顯示，兩種材料皆能藉由冷加工處理來達到強化，且在冷加工量 20％之製程中，其降伏、
抗拉強度所提升的效果最為明顯。如果對 LAZ1141+0.1wt%Sc 施予更大的加工量，其強度不會有所提
升但其延伸率將會逐漸增加；另外，由於發現 LAZ1191+0.2wt%Sc 合金可產生 AlSc 相，所以其在降伏、
抗拉強度上的表現也較為出色。經由冷加工能夠使 LAZ1141+0.1wt%Sc 及 LAZ1191+0.2wt%Sc 硬度達
到提升，且能夠使自然時效處理的尖峰時效(peak aging)由原先的第 2 個小時延遲到第 4 個小時才出現，
因此推測冷加工對此材料具有抑制析出的效果。 
 
關鍵詞：鎂鋰合金, 時效處理, 冷軋延 
 
 
Abstract 
The alloy design and aging treatment are used to strengthen the Mg-Li alloys. The Mg-Li-Al-Zn alloy 
composition, by changing the different amount of Al and Sc addition were investigated. Two kinds of 
LAZ1141+0.1 wt% Sc and LAZ1191+0.2 wt% Sc alloys were cold rolled and natural aged. The optical 
microscope, scanning transmission electron microscope, Vickers hardness tester and tensile testing 
machine were used to evaluate the microstructure and mechanical properties. 
Experimental results show that, both alloys can be strengthened by cold rolling. Especially, the yield and 
tensile strengths will be upgraded obviously when the rolling reduction is 20%. However, the higher rolling 
reduction can not improve the strength of LAZ1141+0.1 wt% Sc more, but the elongation will be increased 
gradually. In addition, AlSc-phase were found in LAZ1191+0.2wt%Sc rather than LAZ1141+0.1wt%Sc, 
therefore the yield and tensile strength of the former is better than the latter. Although the cold-rolling process 
can increase the hardness of LAZ1141+0.1 wt% Sc and LAZ1191+0.2 wt% Sc alloys, the successive the 
natural aging will prolong the peak aging time from 2h to 4h. That is because the cold rolling can delay the 
precipitation of α-phase. 
 
Keywords：Mg-Li magnesium alloys, aging treatment, cold rolling.

This research studies a brand new Magnesium-Lithium alloy, LAZ1151, with trace Sc element 
additions. Cold rolling was conducted on the material to improve its mechanical properties. 
Microstructure and mechanical behaviors were analyzed after processing. 
 
Experimental Procedures 
The super light Mg-Li alloys of interest is Mg-11wt%Li-5wt%Al-1wt%Zn-0.034wt%Sc 
(LAZ1151). The alloy was melted in vacuum under Argon gas protection, and then formed into 200 
mm (8”) cylindrical ingots, later sliced into 15 mm thick plates. The chemical compositions were 
analyzed by Induction Coupled Plasma – Atomic Emission Spectrometer (ICP-AES) and Spark 
Optical Emission Spectrometry (Spark-OES).  
Some specimens were prepared for room temperature aging, while others were prepared for cold 
rolling. The cold rolled specimens were rolled to reduce the thickness by 30%, 60% and 90%, 
respectively. Each rolling pass adopted an approximate thickness reduction of 10% of the former 
thickness. 
The specimens’ microstructures were analyzed with optical microscope (OM) and X-ray 
diffraction (XRD). Tensile tests and Micro Vickers hardness tests were carried out. Tensile tests 
were conducted with cross head speed of 1mm/min, and specimens were made according to 
ASTM-B557M. For Micro Vickers hardness tests, 100g load was applied for 10 sec and each 
plotted datum was obtained by averaging 15 measured values. 
 
Results and Discussions 
Microstructures. 
Fig. 1(a) shows the optical microstructure of the as-cast LAZ1151, in which the β phase average 
grain size is about 169μm; the white spots in the graph are the Mg-rich α phase precipitates whose 
volume fraction is about 1.8%. α phase precipitate mainly on grain boundaries and some inside the 
grains. XRD analysis, Fig. 2, confirms this (α+β) duel phase structure. An explanation according to 
Song et al. [7] is that the Li content quantity is near the (α+β)/β boundary that solidification-induced 
segregation results in a (α+β) micro-structure. XRD also confirms the existence of θ (MgLi2Al) and 
AlLi phases in the specimens. The effect of the two phases on the alloy is unknown and is currently 
beening studied. However, Hsu et al.[8] popose that due to the size of the θ phase there is little 
strengthening effect.  
The LAZ1151 specimens were left in room temperature for aging. Fig. 1(b) and (c) shows the 
micro-structures for 3 month and 6 month aging, with average grain sizes of 172μm and 199μm, 
respectively. An increase in α phase precipitations were observed, α phase increased from 4% to 
8.7% in duration of three months, XRD pattern (Fig. 3) indicate the same trend for α phase 
increment. According to Leu et al.[9], α phase precipitations leads to age softening of the Mg-Li 
alloys.

An approach to strengthen the material via the use of cold rolling was conducted. Fig. 4 shows 
the optical microstructure of the 6 month aged LAZ1151 with 30%, 60% and 90% cold rolling. Fig. 
5 shows the XRD patterns for each rolling reduction. An obvious trend of grain refining was 
observed (227μm→147μm), which is consistent with the works of Chiang et al [10] who also used 
cold rolling on Mg-Li alloys. On the other hand, α phase precipitations also increases with 
increasing rolling reductions (8.8%→13.3%). This is due to the meta-stable nature of the β phase 
matrix. When external energies are induced to the matrix, in our case the strain energy accumulated 
in the material caused by rolling, the β matrix would expel Mg atoms to precipitate α phase [10].

Summery 
A brand new Mg-Li alloy, LAZ1151, was prepared for research. The mechanical properties and 
microstructures of the as-cast and six month aging specimens were observed and analyzed. Typical 
age softening was observed, probably due to α phase precipitations and grain growth. Cold rolling 
was used to strengthen the material. A maximum tensile strength of 180 MPa was achieved, mainly 
due to strain hardening.

Mg-Li alloys are seldom applied in engineering field due to their relatively low strength, low 
thermal and creep resistance, etc. and thus ternary/quaternary β-phase alloys are designed to 
overcome the disadvantages. In 1955, Jones studied the strength and work-hardening properties of 
ternary magnesium β-alloys containing lithium with the composition of Mg:Li being 88:12. He 
pointed that alloys with Cd, Zn or Al produce optimum properties in the cast and rolled condition 
but are not stable at ordinary temperature, and alloys with Si, Cu, Sn or Ce (or mischmetal) have 
less good mechanical properties [5]. In 1957, Clark and Sturkey investigated the age-hardening of 
Mg-19.6Li-18.5Zn β-alloy and found that it age-hardens rapidly at room temperature and reaches 
the hardness peak for 20~30 hrs aging by the formation of MgLi2Zn precipitates which are the 
transition structure of the equilibrium LiZn phase [6]. In 1980, Alamo and Barchik studied the 
precipitation phenomena of Mg-11.4Li-1.4Al β-alloy and confirmed that, for specimens 
solid-soluted and then aged at room temperature, phase reaction follows β→β+θ+α and the hardness 
reaches its maximum value when the presence of θ precipitates. Here the θ is a metastable phase of 
the equilibrium LiAl one [7]. In 1997, Saito et al. investigated the aging behavior and mechanical 
properties of Mg-16.0Li-0.7Al-0.5Cu β-alloy and found that, for specimens solid-soluted at 450°C 
for 1 hr and then quenched in iced-water, the maximum tensile strength of 198 MPa with the 
elongation of 15% can be obtained when the solid-soluted specimen was aged at room temperature 
for ≈ 1400 hrs to the hardness peak [8]. In 2002, Takuda et al. studied the tensile property of 
Mg-12Li-1Zn β-alloy thin sheets which were annealed at 350°C for 0.5 hr and then hot-rolled at the 
same temperature to a thickness of 0.6mm. They observed that the β grains are elongated with 
textural change and have sufficiently high ductility at low strain rates. The tensile strength of the 
specimen in the rolling direction tested at the room temperature is 125 MPa with the elongation of 
56% [9]. In 2006, Chang et al. studied the mechanical properties of Mg-11Li-1Zn β-alloy with a 
little of α-phase processed by equal channel angular extrusion and found its ultimate tensile strength 
is 175 MPa with the elongation of 35% [10]. In 2008, Hsu et al. investigated the room temperature 
aging characteristic of Mg-11.2Li-0.95Al-0.43Zn β-alloy which was extruded at 210°C with an 
extrusion ratio of 29.4:1, solid-soluted at 400°C for 0.5 hr and then quenched in water. Its tensile

3.1 As-homogenized LZ141 alloy

found to be 1.379g/cm
3

Figure 1(a) shows the OM image of as-homogenized LZ141 alloy which reveals an equiaxial 
microstructure with uniform grain size distribution. The average grain size is calculated by using 
ASTM E112-88 standard and found to be 264μm. Figure 1(b) is the SEM image of Fig. 1(a) in 
which some white, spherical-like particles can be found along grain boundaries and in the grains. 
Figure 2 shows the XRD pattern of as-homogenized LZ141 alloy. As seen from Fig. 2(a), LZ141 
alloy is mainly β- -phase. This characteristic can be confirmed indisputably from 
Fig. 2(b) which is the enlarged profile of Fig. 2(a) in the 2θ range of 30° to 45°. The XRD result of 
Fig. 2 indicates that the white particles shown in Fig. 1(b) are α-phase. From the Mg-Li binary 
phase diagram [3], Mg-14Li binary alloy only displays a single β phase. However, Figs. 1(b) and 2 
show that a trace of α phase still remains in LZ141 alloy. This result implies that it is difficult to 
-phase completely in ternary Mg-Li-X alloys. A similar phenomenon was also mentioned 
on the study of LA141 alloy but the cause of α-phase presence had not been provided [15]. 
The hardness of as-homogenized LZ141 alloy is found to be 41.6 Hv which is a little lower 
than that of Mg-16Li-2Zn alloy (LZ162), say ≈50Hv [16]. The intrinsic low hardness is due to its 
high Li-content and coarse grain size, as well as the lack of the aluminum strengthening effect. 
Crawford et al. [17] mentioned that, even LA141 alloy has the solid-soluted Al atoms for 
strengthening, its strength improvement is insignificant but its elongation is much sacrificed.

of severely cold-rolled one, but it will not change for specimen SS at 400°C×40min. The SS LZ141 
alloy, as compared with as-homogenized one, has its tensile strength and elongation to be 146MPa 
and 38%, respectively, due to α-phase particles solid-soluted in the β-matrix. The grain size of SS 
specimen is 259μm which is almost the same as that of as-homogenized one. If SS specimen is 
further cold-rolled to 80% thickness reduction, the tensile strength, elongation and hardness can 
reach 227 MPa, 33% and 61.0 Hv, respectively. Experimental results show that duplex 
strengthening effects caused by solid-solution with subsequently severe cold-rolling can 
significantly improve mechanical properties of LZ141alloy.

升並無貢獻。 
 
INFLUENCE OF TMT ON MECHANICAL PROPERTIES OF 
MgLiAlZn ALLOYS 
Jian-Yih Wang  Chang-Chuan Hsu  Sy-Cherng Yang 
 
ABSTRACT 
MgLi series alloys have excellent formability at room temperature, but their mechanical 
properties are worse than general magnesium alloys. The effect of work hardening is little for the 
rolling at room temperature of the extruded MgLiAlZn alloy, mainly because β matrix phase 
generates recrystallization and the stress promotes the precipitation of coarse α phase. Therefore, 
the 90% reduction in rolling after the solid solution treatment, due to the effects of both solid 
solution strengthening and work hardening, the tensile strength and elongation are 276 MPa and 
20% respectively. This shows the rolling of MgLiAlZn alloy after solid solution treatment can 
obtain high strength and high ductility. 
For the natural aging treatment of this alloy, peak aging reaches at 48hrs and precipitates the 
pellet shape pre-θ phase of 2~3nm wide and about 10nm long. The longer the aging time, the pre-θ 
phase disappears, θ phase forms and α phase precipitates at the same time. However, precipitation 
of coarser α phase at a longer time has no contribution to enhance its mechanical properties.

Keywords: Magnesium-Lithium alloys, Mechanical behaviors, Cold rolling, Age softening. 
Abstract. This research studies a brand new Magnesium Lithium Alloy, LAZ1151, 
with trace Sc additions. The mechanical properties and microstructures of the as-cast 
and alloys after three and six month aging were observed and analyzed. 
Microstructure and XRD confirms the existence of α phase precipitates (Mg rich) in 
both as-cast and aging specimens of the alloy. Grain growth was observed in the alloy 
after room temperature aging. The tensile strength of the as-cast LAZ1151 is 147 MPa; 
the value is decreased to 135 MPa after six month aging, showing typical room 
temperature softening. Strengthening via the use of cold rolling was tried; a maximum 
tensile strength of 180 MPa was achieved after 90% rolling reduction, which is 
mainly due to strain hardening. 
 Introduction 
Lightweight magnesium alloys have the lowest density of any structure materials 
together with high specific strength, high heat conduction and high electro-magnetic 
wave shielding capacities. Therefore it is commonly used in aerospace, automobile 
and 3C industries [1-4]. However, the applications for Mg alloys are limited due to its 
hexagonal close-packed (HCP) structure, which leads poor formability at room 
temperature. According to the Magnesium-Lithium binary phase diagram, a HCP and 
BCC dual-phase structure exists when the Lithium (BCC, body center cubic, structure) 
content is between 5 to 11wt%. This not only further decrease the alloys’ weight but 
also improves the formability at room temperature. However, relatively low strength 
is the main drawback with Mg-Li alloys. Two methods can effectively refine grain 
size and improve mechanical behaviors. One is severe plastic deformation (SPD) 
the size of the θ phase there is little strengthening effect.  
The LAZ1151 specimens were left in room temperature for aging. Fig. 1(b) and (c) 
shows the micro-structures for 3 month and 6 month aging, with average grain sizes 
of 172μm and 199μm, respectively. An increase in α phase precipitations were 
observed, α phase increased from 4% to 8.7% in duration of three months, XRD 
pattern (Fig. 3) indicate the same trend for α phase increment. According to Leu et 
al.[9], α phase precipitations leads to age softening of the Mg-Li alloys.
