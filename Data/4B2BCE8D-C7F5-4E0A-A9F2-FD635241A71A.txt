結果與討論 
一、酚等逆境誘導台灣藜新甜菜色素
的最適濃度 
    預備試驗顯示，台灣藜在逆境下
發芽，其發芽狀況大多比控制組 (水) 
來的差，尤其兒茶素和沒食子酸等酚
逆境，更是隨著酚逆境濃度之增加，
芽的生長就越差。為取得較理想的芽
體新色素，將逆境濃度的範圍縮小，(各
逆境 H2O2、兒茶素、沒食子酸、酪胺
酸和鹽分的濃度個別為 0-588mM、
0-1mM 、 0-1mM 、 0-1.1mM 和
0-342mM)，再比較逆境下發芽芽体水
萃液之甜菜色素含量 (A540)。結果如圖
1 所示，台灣藜在 H2O2、兒茶素、沒
食子酸、酪胺酸和鹽分等逆境下發芽 5
天，其芽中甜菜色素含量大多高於控
制組，顯示在台灣藜發芽過程中提供
H2O2、兒茶素、沒食子酸、酪胺酸和
鹽分皆可能有助於甜菜色素的形成，
且各逆境誘導的最適濃度，在 H2O2、
兒茶素、沒食子酸、酪胺酸和鹽分，
各為 58.8 mM、0.5mM、0.3mM、
0.28mM和 85.5 mM。 
 
二、逆境處理對台灣藜芽色澤品質之
影響 
在彩度 (chroma) 方面，當彩度值
愈高，表示顏色愈濃。結果如表 1 所
示，除了在 H2O2和鹽分逆境者之外，
控制組和在其他逆境者皆隨著發芽天
數的增加，彩度也隨之上升，顯示紅
色濃度增加。至第 11天時其彩度值大
致分布於 75.20-77.52，之後彩度下
降，芽體開始褪色。整體而言，以沒
食子酸逆境者的彩度值較高，亦即所
誘導的色素彩度較高，顏色較濃，在
鹽分逆境者最低。 
在色相角(hue angle) 方面，色相
角是利用角度的不同來呈現顏色的變
化，在 0-90°由紅色轉為黃色，90-180°
由黃色轉為綠色，180-270°由綠色轉為
藍色，270-360°則由藍色轉為紅色。本
研究中各芽體色素液之色相角大致分
佈在 331.64°到 358.44°，亦即呈現紅偏
紫色。發芽第 5 天後，所有逆境者之
水萃液，色相角皆呈現下降的趨勢，
由  344.99°-358.44°下降至第 11 天
332.83°-336.79°之間，除了在鹽分逆境
者之外，其它逆境者第 13天色相角顯
著上升，亦即紅色顯著增加。 
該結果顯示，逆境台灣藜芽在發
芽第 11天時色素含量最高，導致水萃
液顏色彩度最高，色相角偏低，顯示
該時期所誘發的色素最濃，且偏向紅
紫色。

Effect of varieties, seasons and matrix substance on the bioactive 
compounds and antioxidant capacity of Djulis 
 
Pi-Jen Tsai＊, Pei-Huwa Wu and Kanjana Narkprasom  
Department of Food Science, NPUST 
 
Abstract 
    Djulis, full of color, is a traditional cereal plant in Taiwan. Besides high contents 
of protein and starch, they also possess higher dietary fiber than most of the cereals. 
On the other hand, it has been used for inflammation and insect repellant, which was 
believed to be attributed to its antioxidant capacity. The aim of this study is to 
elucidate the effect of different varieties (red and yellow), harvest seasons (summer 
and winter) and matrix additives (alcohol and acid) on the antioxidant composition 
(pigments and polyphenols content) and antioxidant capacity (DPPH, FRAP) of 
Djulis. Results showed that samples from red variety showed higher DPPH 
scavenging activity than that from yellow varieties. Winter harvested samples 
exhibited higher antioxidant capacity, pigments and polyphenols content than that 
from summer harvested samples. Statistic analysis revealed that reducing power was 
highly correlated with A480 and total phenol content, while DPPH scavenging activity 
with A530. Further purification confirmed that the major antioxidant composition of 
Djulis was betanin. In addition, organic acids especially citric acid and acetic acid 
were found to show protection effect on the pigment, while the alcohol significantly 
bleached it. The discrimination through PCA revealed the effect of varieties and 
growth seasons on the bioactive compounds and antioxidant capacity of Djulis.

 The Djulis grains for this study was obtained locally from Taiwan, and then

water for 24 hr then the sample was freeze dried. Then frozen dried pigment extract was 
dissolved in buffer solutions with different alcohol (10, 20, 30, 40, 60, and 80%), model 
(pH 3.5, 5.5), and subjected to heating at 60°C for 30 min and storage at 25°C for 28

citric acid and ascorbic acid) were used to evaluate the color protection of the organic 
acid on the betanin in the alcohol system. Djulis was conducted to PCA analysis to see 
if the samples from different season and variety can be classified based on the items 
tested including Color quality (A530, A480, Hunter a, Chroma), antioxidant capacity 
(FICA, FRAP, DPPH), antioxidant components (Total phenol, betacyanin).

Results and Discussion 
Results showed that the Djulis contained high protein (average 16.32%), dietary 
fiber (average17.64%), starch (average 52.23%) and fat (average 4.05%) (Table 1). 
Samples of red variety contained more starch, crude fat, calcium, sodium, amino acid, 
total polyphenols and betacyanins. While yellow variety samples showed higher content 
of dietary fiber, crude protein, zinc and iron, flavonoid, phenolic acids (chlorogenic acid, 
coumaric acid and ferulic acid) and betazanthins (Table 2). The betacyanin content in 
the winter red sample were 1.59 folds that in summer samples. Total phenol also much 
higher in the WR samples (1.74 folds as compared with SR). As to the antioxidant 
capacity, FRAP reducing power and ferrous ions chelating ability are higher in yellow 
variety than that in the red one, but DPPH scavenging activity in red variety was better 
than that in yellow one. For the harvest season, winter harvested samples exhibited 
higher nutrition components, antioxidant compounds, color quality and antioxidant 
capacity than summer harvested samples. Further analysis through PCA showed that all 
samples were discriminated well according to varieties and seasons (data not shown). 
 
As to the pigment color, djulis water extract exhibited red color in the pH ranged 
from 2 to 6. Significant regeneration was only found in the pH 2 system in this study. In 
the acid added Djulis pigment model systems, organic acids were found no color 
hyper-chromic effect on Djulis. But they exhibited color protection both in the pH 2 and 
pH 4 model systems, especially after heating and regeneration. Only samples with citric 
acid and acetic acid remained their color after storage for 26 days, but not the one with 
ascorbic acid. It revealed that during storage, the effect of acid addition depends on the 
pH. Citric acid and acetic acid may exhibit better color protection in the pH 2 and pH 4 
systems, while ascorbic acid in the pH 6 system (Table 3). It suggested that the 
protecting function on pigment, in the former, was that the acid as the chelating agent to 
neutralize betanin electrophile and reduce nucleophilic substitution; in the later, it might 
be attributed to the effect of the betanin-acid complex.
