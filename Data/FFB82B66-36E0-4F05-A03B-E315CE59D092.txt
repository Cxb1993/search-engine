應用O2/RFG技術於流體化床焚化系統中污染物之生成特性與控制研究(3/3) 
 
國科會研究計畫編號：NSC95-2221-E-241-017 
執行期限：95 年 8 月 1 日至 96 年 7 月 31 日 
主持人：陳志成 弘光科技大學環境工程系 
計畫參與人員：陳志明、郭建鑠、張鳳儀 
 
中文摘要 
本研究應用流體化床焚化系統與 O2/RFG 燃燒技術，探討不同進氣組成與煙氣回流率下
燃燒廢氣中 CO2、SO2、NOx、HCl 酸性氣體、鉛鋅銅鉻鎘五種重金屬、及 PAHs、苯(氯苯)、
酚(氯酚)有機污染物之排放特性，並搭配 NaOH 濕式洗滌塔探討 CO2 與其他污染物之控制效
果。實驗結果顯示，燃燒廢氣中 CO2、CO、NO 與 SO2 濃度隨著進氣氧濃度的提高明顯
增加，煙氣回流亦可增加 CO2 的排放濃度，實驗最高值可達 64.9%，比一般空氣燃燒所產生
CO2 濃度增加 4 倍以上；酸性氣體 SO2 與 HCl 之濃度隨著煙氣回流而明顯增加，但 NO 之濃
度則無顯著影響。應用 O2/RFG 燃燒技術可增加中高揮發性重金屬於底灰之濃度並減少袋式
集塵灰之濃度，有助於提升後續空氣污染防治設備對重金屬之控制效果。隨著進氣氧濃度升
高與煙氣回流率增加，中高環數 PAHs 之生成濃度亦有減少趨勢。 
此外，實驗結果發現應用 O2/RFG 燃燒技術搭配 NaOH 洗滌塔對 CO2 之控制效率明顯增
加，甚至達到 100%去除控制；由於 NaOH 洗滌塔在一般空氣燃燒系統中對 SO2 和 HCl 酸氣
之控制效果即為良好，因此 O2/RFG 燃燒對此兩種酸性氣體之控制效率影響不大；CO 與 NO
氣體於部分條件下之去除效率不高，且去除率與氧氣濃度和煙氣回流率之變化關係無明顯趨
勢，僅有當氧氣濃度為 60%與 80%時，NO 去除率有增加情形。NaOH 濕式洗滌塔對 PAHs
之控制效果不明顯，但高氧濃度與煙氣回流時，氣固相酚類與氣相烯類及苯類有機物之去除
率皆為良好。綜合研究結果證實，O2/RFG 燃燒技術可減少廢氣量、改變不同污染物之濃度與
分布特性，進而提升 CO2 與各種污染物之控制效果，值得進一步開發應用。 
 
關鍵詞：廢棄物焚化、氧氣/煙氣回流燃燒、二氧化碳、酸性氣體、重金屬、有機物、控制效
率 
 
Abstract 
This study applies a fluidized bed incineration system and O2/RFG combustion technology to 
investigate the emission characteristics of acid gases (CO2, SO2, NOx, HCl), heavy metals (Pb, Cr, 
Cd, Cu, Zn) and organic pollutants (PAHs, phenols, benzenes) at different feed gas compositions 
and ratios of recycled flue gas. Moreover, a NaOH wet scrubber was set up to investigate the 
control efficiencies of CO2 and the other pollutants. Experimental results indicate that the 
concentrations of CO2, CO, NO and SO2 in the exhaust gas increase with the raising O2 
concentration in feed gas. The recycling of exhaust gas can also enhance the concentration 
of CO2, the highest value is 64.9%, which is four times of the CO2 concentration in general air 
combustion system. The concentrations of acid gas SO2 and HCl also increase with the exhaust gas 
recycling, but that is not obvious for NO. Using this O2/RFG combustion technology can enhance 
the concentrations of semi to high volatile heavy metals in bottom ash and decrease their 
concentrations in baghouse ash, and these are beneficial for the control of heavy metals in APCDs. 
As the O2 concentration in feed gas and ratio of recycling flue gas increase, the concentrations of 
medium to high rings PAHs formed in waste incineration system are also decreased. 
Additionally, experimental results show that using this O2/RFG combustion incorporated with 
NaOH wet scrubber has great control efficiency of CO2, and even 100% removal is achieved. The 
improve of acid gas SO2 and HCl control in such system is not obvious due to the NaOH scrubber 
in general air combustion system originally has very good control efficiency for them. The control

(b)氧氣濃度 
    由於高濃度的氧含量，可增加氧分子和有機可燃物的接觸，並且由於廢氣量減少，可延
長氣體停留時間，使有機有害物質的破壞去除率大幅提昇，且於高濃度的氧含量下，廢氣產
生量減少，亦可使輔助燃料的耗用量顯著降低；在 1300℃的排氣溫度，2 %的排氣氧含量的
情況下，以純氧取代空氣可以節省補助燃料達 60 %，對於處理濕度大、熱值低的廢棄物而言，
輔助燃料的節省是一筆很可觀的數目。由於二次燃燒爐所需加熱氣體量的減少，和燃料使用
效率的提高，二次爐的燃燒器亦節省許多輔助燃料。 
Kimura et al.(1995)研究O2 / RFG燃燒指出，當氧濃度低於30 %時，未燃燒碳含量會較高，
若將氧濃度提高至 42 %時，理論上絕熱火焰溫度會與空氣燃燒系統相同，噴入氧氣量增加，
可改善燃燒效率，亦可減少未燃燒碳含量；Nozaki et al. (1997)指出，將氧直接噴入燃燒爐，
可改善燃燒效率，O2/CO2 燃燒之氣體溫度較低(與空氣燃燒比較)，但若增加直接噴入燃燒爐
之氧量，氣體溫度亦增加；Yamada et al. (1998)發現，氧濃度增加時爐內可利用熱亦增加，當
氧濃度達 30 %時，可利用熱與空氣燃燒系統相似，供氧濃度增加可使燃燒爐尺寸減小，隨著
氧濃度增加，其火焰擴散速度亦隨之增高，而火焰擴散速度之比較發現：O2 / Ar > O2 / N2 > O2 
/ CO2；Croiset et al.(2001)指出，在進料氣中氧濃度較高者，火焰溫度增加，當氧濃度為 28 和
35 %時，煙道氣回流燃燒模式之 SOx 排放率較 O2/CO2 一次燃燒模式低，在較高氧濃度中，
其氣體通過系統之總體積流率較低。 
 
(c)操作溫度 
    O2 / RFG 此種富氧燃燒雖然很容易產生局部溫度過高和熱氮氧化物的形成，但是經噴入
氧氣與回流廢氣的混合作用，可減低火焰溫度，促進燃燒室內氣體再循環，使燃燒室內溫度
平均分佈，避免局部熱點的出現，減少熱氮氧化物的形成。另外，均勻的溫度分佈亦使得間
歇式進料焚化爐的溫度升降循環之尖峰溫度下降，谷底溫度上升，亦即縮小溫度變化的振幅。 
 
(d)煙道氣回流量 
煙氣回流除了具有上述抑制富氧燃燒所造成溫度過高與熱氮氧化物形成的作用外，亦具
有熱能回收之效果，Nozaki et al. (1997)利用 O2 / CO2 模式研究粉煤燃燒指出，燃燒氣體回流
可將部分熱能帶回燃燒室，增加煤燃燒之氣體溫度，提高熱效率，進而改善粉煤之燃燒效率。 
 
(5) O2 / RFG 燃燒模式之整體熱效率與經濟成本評估 
O2 / RFG 燃燒模式使廢氣體積減少，使既有二次空氣污染防治設備的廢氣處理量減少而
更具效用，降低操作、維護費用，又由於氧氣傳送至固體廢棄物表面的機率和速率增加，有
機物揮發速度加快，氧氣與可燃物碰撞機會提高，燃燒速度和熱效率更大，燃燒之灰燼也因
燃燒更完全，而使灼燒減量達到最小值，且由於燃燒之廢氣體積的減少，廢棄物被更完全和
迅速地破壞，若運用於實廠中，既存焚化爐的最高處理量可因而加大。 
Yamada et al.(1998)指出，O2 / RFG 燃燒模式因燃燒氣體中無氮氣混合，故熱損失較低，
鍋爐效率增高，整體熱效率較傳統空氣燃燒模式高，然而電廠供電量因為部份電力用於氧氣
製造與 CO2 處理而減少，因此其淨熱效率由原本之 39 %(未控制 CO2 時)減少為 30 %，但傳統
之燃煤電廠若結合醇胺吸收設備處理 CO2 時，因需要大量蒸汽用於再生醇胺，其供電量低於

則會增加 Pb、Cd 及 Cr 於砂床中的濃度。 
 
(b)重金屬分佈與顆粒粒徑之關係 
許多學者研究指出重金屬濃度分佈與生成微粒粒徑具有相關性，多數重金屬如鉛、鎘、
鋅、鉻、砷、銻、鎳等，隨顆粒粒徑減小有增加其濃度分佈的特性，尤其以具揮發性之重
金屬更加顯著，而揮發性較低之重金屬化合物則大多分佈在大粒徑之飛灰或底灰中。 
Wey（1998d）於重金屬濃度與粒徑分佈之關係中指出，在粗粒飛灰中，鉛、鉻、鎘之
分佈較無規律；細粒飛灰中鉛、鎘濃度趨向分佈於小粒徑微粒；而添加有機氯與無機氯均
增加重金屬氯化物揮發量，有降低重金屬粒徑分佈範圍之趨勢。而 Querol 等人（1995）指
出，As, Be, Cd, Co, Cr, Cu, Hg, Ni, Pb, Sb, Se, Tl, Zn較易富集於細小顆粒上，且濃度可達 2-20
倍，但 Al, Ba, Fe, K, Mg, Mn 之濃度分佈與顆粒粒徑大小較無相關性。Ontiveros 等人（1989）
研究指出，焚化效率越高，較小粒徑的飛灰所佔的比例就愈高，且隨灰渣顆粒粒徑降低，
重金屬濃度會隨之增加。而 Kauppinen（1990）研究燃煤及醫療廢棄物焚化產生飛灰微粒其
質量與元素分佈情形，發現微粒質量與元素分佈皆成雙峰分佈（Bimodal），其中 Pb, Ca, Cu, 
Sr, Cd 在細粒飛灰中含量極高，而重金屬 Cd 分佈明顯趨向於粒徑較小顆粒。焚化系統中，
隨著水冷卻系統溫度的降低，細小飛灰（0.1~15.72 μm）上之重金屬總量會增加，然而，隨
著半乾式系統溫度的減低，細小飛灰上之重金屬總量則會降低（Wey et al., 1999b）。 
 
(c)焚化條件對重金屬分佈及生成物種之影響 
除了重金屬物種本身之物化特性外，焚化操作條件亦是影響重金屬分佈之主要因素，
包括燃燒室溫度、停留時間、供應空氣流量、廢棄物物理化學組成、廢棄物進料量與重金
屬含量、廢氣處理系統、氣體冷卻速率等等，其中以燃燒溫度、廢棄物含氯種類與含氯量、
廢氣處理系統之影響最大（Barton, 1990）。

(D)有機污染物 
有機污染物亦會對人體有嚴重之危害性，尤其是列入管制的高分子環狀芳香化合物
（PAHs, PCDD, PCDF 等）（Wey, 1998b；Charles, 1992；蔣，1991），這些污染物皆具毒性，
PAHs 及 Dioxin 等有機污染物多為脂溶性，如果不加以分解破壞而任其溶入人體內，則到
達細胞中核酸的 DNA 時，便會產生致癌性（Benestad, 1990；Charles, 1992），因此，如何
有效的來控制這些二次污染物成為焚化爐操作設計上的重要因子。 
 
(a)半揮發性有機物（PAHs）的特性 
PAHs 是指由兩個或兩個以上苯環所組成的化合物，大多具高有機碳分配常數、高生物
濃縮因子及高沸點等特性，在 25℃時的飽合蒸汽壓皆小於 10-1 mmHg，在常溫下大多以液
相存在，且揮發性小，環境中 PAHs 存在於土壤、空氣、底泥、水乃至食物中，而焚化操
作過程是 PAHs 產生源之一。Charles 等人（1992）研究指出，焚化產生的多環芳香族碳氫
化合物對生物具有高致癌性和致突變性，且焚化爐所排放的有機污染物其致突變能力與
PAHs 的量有正相關性（Benestad, 1990）。 
 
(b)有機污染物（PAHs）的生成及行為 
焚化過程中 PAHs 的生成受到了多項操作條件的影響，包括有燃燒溫度、進料組成、
燃燒滯留時間及空氣量的多寡等，在燃燒溫度方面，於低溫下燃燒 PVC、PS 及 PE 時會有
大量的 PAHs 生成（Thomai et al., 1996），而固體物焚化時因攪拌不均勻而造成局部低溫時
（400~500℃），易形成低分子量有機物及三、四環的 PAHs，而較高溫度下（700~900℃），
由於高溫促進環狀物的形成，以五、六環的 PAHs 濃度較高（Chiang, 1992）；在進料組成的
影響方面，若進料中含有重金屬存在時，會催化或抑制 PAHs 的生成（Wey, 1998b；1998c），
而焚化中若以柴油為輔助燃料時，柴油即是生成 PAHs 的一大主因（Li, 1994）；有關空氣量
影響方面，燃燒 PVC 時，若在氧氣存在的情況下，則 PAHs 的生成量會因此而降低（Thomai 
et al., 1996），在空氣量不足時是傾向於裂解狀態，有機物無法完全氧化，易生成有機的污
染物；而高溫的滯留時間亦為一重要因子，需要有足夠的反應時間才能將有機物完全破壞。 
多環芳香族碳氫化合物及其衍生物，在不完全燃燒時會形成氣相的 PAHs，離開高溫火
焰反應爐後，因氣流逐漸冷卻，經由冷凝（Condensation）、結核（Nucleation）及吸附
（Adsorption）等機制而存在於固相之中，其中又以吸附為最主要的機制。 
 
(c)飛灰與有機物（PAHs）的關係 
焚化操作所產生的有機污染物並非皆直接排放至大氣中，部分因在煙道時降溫而吸附

五、六環的 PAHs 則大多出現在較大粒徑的飛灰。影響 PAHs 在飛灰上的吸附因子包括飛灰

3.不同操作條件下酸性氣體之控制效率  
本研究除了探討不同操作條件下各種氣體之生成排放特性外，更進一步應用濕
式洗滌塔探討不同氣體之控制效率，並和一般空氣燃燒系統之空污防治效率比較。
廢氣控制單元採用 10% NaOH 為洗滌液，洗滌塔高 0.7m、  直徑 0.1016m、填充物
尺寸 0.55m、洗滌液噴霧量為 2.76 L/min。  
由圖4顯示不同氣體在洗滌塔入口端與出口端之濃度變化，可發現當進氣氧濃度為21%
且煙氣不回流時，NaOH 洗滌塔對 CO2 之控制效率可達 40%左右，但隨著氧氣濃度提高與
煙氣回流率增加，在相同 NaOH 洗滌塔操作條件下，CO2 去除效率明顯增加，甚至達到近
乎 100%去除控制，其原因有兩項，一為 CO2 濃度的提升促使洗滌液與 CO2 吸收反應速率
與去除率增加，另一項原因為氧濃度提高與煙氣回流可大量減少排放廢氣量，導致液氣比
提高(但 CO2/NaOH 計量比不變)，因而增加氣液接觸機率而提升去除效率。因此，藉由適
當氧氣煙氣回流燃燒控制，燃燒廢氣中 CO2 即可達到 100%完全控制或回收。

2. 煙氣回流對重金屬濃度之影響 
圖6分別顯示不同煙氣回流率下(21/0、21/80、40/0、40/70、60/0、60/50)下重金屬Cd、

度是否會因進煙氣回流燃燒而產生累積增濃效應，並進而提高整體之控制效率。 
結果顯示高揮發性重金屬鎘與鉛於進氣氧濃度21%時，煙氣回流(RFG= 80%)可減少底

回流(RFG= 50%)可顯著增加底灰之濃度並減少袋式集塵灰之濃度，有助於鉛、鎘之控制。
至於中度揮發性重金屬鋅與銅，在不同進氣氧濃度時煙氣回流對其濃度分布變化情形亦和

發性重金屬鉻於不同進氣氧濃度時，煙氣回流可減少底灰之濃度，但增加旋風集塵灰或袋
式集塵灰之濃度，這是由於回流煙氣高溫促使重金屬鉻揮發程度增加，而隨煙氣帶至旋風
集塵器與袋式集塵器所致。

圖 8 顯示不同進氣氧濃度條件下 PAHs 有機物於氣相與固相之濃度分布，本研究

應所致；此外，進氣氧濃度提升所形成之增濃效應(總氣體量減少導致濃度增加)，亦
是其濃度增高的可能原因。對於固相 PAHs 而言，其生成趨勢亦和氣相 PAHs 一致，
隨著進氣氧濃度提升而有明顯增加的趨勢。

五、結論 
本研究針對 O2/RFG 燃燒技術搭配空氣污染控制設備進行各種污染物之控制去除探討，
綜合實驗結果可得到以下幾點結論： 
1. 燃燒廢氣中 CO2、CO、NO 與 SO2 濃度隨著進氣氧濃度的提高明顯增加，煙
氣回流亦可明顯增加 CO2 的排放濃度，其最高值 64.9%出現在進氣氧濃度 60%與煙氣回流
率 50%時(理論值可達 71.43%)，當氧濃度 80%與煙氣回流率 25%時，CO2實驗值亦可達到
64.3%，比一般空氣燃燒所產生 CO2 濃度增加 4 倍以上。酸性氣體 SO2與 HCl 之濃度隨著
煙氣回流而明顯增加，但 NO 之濃度則無顯著影響。  
2. 隨著進氣氧濃度提高與煙氣回流率增加，NaOH洗滌塔對CO2之控制效率明顯增加，
甚至達到 100%去除控制，至於酸性氣體 SO2 和 HCl，由於 NaOH 洗滌塔在一般空氣燃燒系
統中對酸氣之控制效果即為良好，因此提升氧氣濃度與煙氣回流率對此兩種酸性氣體之控
制效率影響不明顯，幾乎皆可達 100%完全控制。而 CO 與 NO 氣體，由於本身與 NaOH 反
應性不佳，因此部分條件下之控制效率不高，且去除率與氧氣濃度和煙氣回流率之變化關
係無明顯趨勢，僅有當氧氣濃度為 60%與 80%時，NO 去除率有增加情形。  
3. 提升氧氣濃度與煙氣回流率可增加中高揮發性重金屬於底灰之濃度並減少袋式集

combustion efficiency and save the energy by changing the oxygen 
concentration in feed gas and ratio of recycling flue gas. The amount of 
exhaust can be significantly decreased and the capacity of following 
APCDs can be reduced. The concentrations and partitioning 
characteristics of various pollutants are also changed; the control 
efficiencies of CO2 and other pollutants are therefore much improved.

Chiang et al. [18] indicated that the formations of organic compound with cyclic structures are enhanced at high

rings are usually present in gas state while PAHs with four rings are co-presents in solid and gas states. BbF and
BaP were the major compounds of solid PAHs, and PA and AcPy are the major compounds of gas PAHs. The
presences of heavy metals may enhance or suppress the formations of PAHs. Lead and zinc may enhance the
formations of gas PAHs with low rings, while chromium and cadmium may enhance the reactions of low-ring PAHs
and precursors to form high-ring PAHs [20-22]. Thomai et al. [17] studied the combustion of plastic PVC and found
that the formations of PAHs can be reduced by supplying sufficient or excess oxygen. With insufficient air, the PVC
was under pyrolysis and more organic pollutants were formed at such incomplete oxidation conditions. With longer
retention time and higher excess air factor, the formations of PAHs become less because the feed waste is
combusted and destroyed more completely [23].
PAHs have high boiling points and low vapor pressure, they tend to migrate to the condensed phase via nucleation,
condensation and adsorption. Previous studies showed that the amount of PAHs adsorbed on fly ash were affected
by the specific surface area, particle size, heavy metals and the amount of carbon in the fly ash [20-22,24]. Most
low–ring PAHs (with three and four rings) are present in the fly ash with smaller particle size while most high-ring
PAHs are present in larger fly ash. Lee et al. [25] pointed out that PAHs adsorbed by fly ash were proportional to the
amount of carbon. Wey et al. [20-22] indicated that heavy metals (Cr and Cd) in the fly ash would provide activity
sites required for the reactions between the lower-ring PAHs and PAHs precursors and promote them to form
higher-ring PAHs.
Since O2/RFG combustion technology is still in development and most researches applied it for CO2 emission
control in power plants, the applications of this technology in waste incineration and the emission characteristics of
different air pollutants along with O2/RFG waste incineration need further investigations. The emission
characteristics of organic pollutants in general air combustion processes are well studied; however, the effects of
flue gas recycling on the formations of different organic compounds are seldom investigated. Therefore, this study
applies the O2/RFG combustion technology for waste incineration and investigates the emission characteristics of
different organic pollutants at different operating conditions.
EXPERIMENTAL
Experimental Apparatus
Figure 1 illustrates the schematic diagram of experimental apparatus. The composite feed gas with different oxygen
concentrations was prepared by pure oxygen cylinders, an air compressor, mass flow meters and a gas mixer. For a
better control of combustion temperature, a fluidized bed incinerator was used as the experimental reactor. The
fluidized bed incinerator was consisted of a feedstock feeder, a feed gas distributor, and a main combustion chamber.
The combustion chamber had 50 cm height and 5 cm inside diameter, and was made of 3 mm thick stainless steel
(AISI 310). Five thermocouples were inserted into the axis to determine the temperature profiles in the combustion
chambers and the flue pipes. Silica sands were used as the fluidized bed materials and their particle size distribution
was 15.05% 470-300μm,49.62% 300-214μm, 17.35% 214-163μm, and 12% <163μm. Because the silica sands in
the fluidized bed incinerator have higher heat capacities and better mixing efficiency, the temperature distribution in
the combustion chamber is uniform and stable. Granular coal and polyethylene (PE) plastics were mixed and used to
represent the waste, and they were fed into the incinerator by a rotary feeder on the top of fluidized bed incinerator.
As the flue gas was recycled, it was continuously introduced into the gas inlet of first combustion chamber by a
vacuum pump. The exhaust gas was treated by a baghouse, a wet scrubber and finally discharged into the
atmosphere.

RESULTS AND DISCUSSIONS
Polycyclic Aromatic Hydrocarbons (PAHs)
Tables 2 and 3 showed the concentrations of different PAH compounds emitted from the O2/RFG waste
incineration system. The O2 concentrations in the feed gas were controlled at 21% and 40% and the ratio of recycled
flue gas were controlled at 0, 15, 35, 55 and 75%. There were sixteen PAH compounds analyzed and discussed in
the following. With 21% O2 concentration in the feed gas, the major PAH compounds in solid-phase samples were
Chr, B(a)A, B(b)F, DbA, B(a)P and InP, they all belong to the PAH compounds with middle to high benzene rings.
On the other hand, the major compounds in gas-phase samples were high-ring DbA and low-ring NaP. As the ratio
of recycled flue gas increased, the emission concentrations of PAH compounds with middle to low rings tended to
increase. However, the concentrations of high-ring PAH compounds were increased then decreased. The total
concentrations of solid-phase PAHs were thus not significantly varied with the ratios of recycled flue gas. For
gas-phase PAH compounds, all the concentrations of PAH compounds with low to high rings were increased and
then decreased. The total concentrations of gas-phase PAHs also had the same trend. Therefore, increasing the ratio
of recycled flue gas can reduce the emission of PAH compounds. The highest total emission concentration of PAHs
was occurred at 35% recycled flue gas and the lowest was occurred at 75% recycled flue gas.
As the flue gas was recycled to combustion chamber, most incompletely decomposed organic compounds including
PAHs were also recycled to combustion chamber. The concentrations and resident time of organic compounds in
combustion chamber were both increased, and the combustion efficiency and destruction efficiency were therefore
increased. However, these high concentrations and long resident time of PAHs in the combustion system also
increased the probabilities of gas-phase PAHs transforming to solid-phase PAHs through heterogeneous
condensation and nucleation. Therefore the concentrations of solid-phase PAHs were increased with the ratio of
recycled flue gas. Moreover, introducing the recycled flue gas into the combustion chamber may cause partial
oxygen lean or temperature fluctuation, and these were also the possible reasons for the increase of solid-phase
PAHs with the ratio of recycled flue gas.

concentration of gas-phase PAHs were increased as the O2 concentration in feed gas was increased from 21% to
40%. The increase of O2 concentration in the combustion system can promote the transformation of solid-phase

Phenol Compounds
Tables 4 and 5 showed the concentrations of different phenol compounds emitted from the O2/RFG waste
incineration system. The O2 concentrations in the feed gas were controlled at 21% and 40% and the ratio of recycled
flue gas were controlled at 0, 15, 35, 55 and 75%. Twenty two phenol compounds were analyzed and discussed in
this section. With 21% O2 concentration in the feed gas, the major phenol compounds in gas-phase and solid-phase
samples were 2,4-dinitrophenol, phenol and dinoseb. The other chlorinated phenol compounds, such as
chlorophenols, dichlorophenol, trichlorophenol, tetrachlorophenol, and pentachlorophenol, could also be found; but
their concentrations were very low.
The concentrations of solid-phase phenol compounds were increased with the ratio of recycled flue gas, but that of
gas-phase phenol compounds were increased then decreased or decreased directly. Moreover, the concentrations of
gas-phase phenol compounds were higher than that of solid-phase phenol compounds. This finding indicated that
phenol compounds had higher volatilities and they were hard to attach or condense on solid-phase samples.
Comparing the data in Tables 4 and 5 found that the kinds and concentrations of solid-phase and gas-phase phenol
compounds were significantly decreased as the O2 concentration in feed gas was increased from 21% to 40%. The
major phenol compounds in gas-phase and solid-phase samples were 2,4-dinitrophenol and phenol. The other
chlorinated phenol compounds, such as chlorophenols, dichlorophenol, trichlorophenol and tetrachlorophenol, could
also be detected at different ratios of recycled flue gas; but their concentrations were very low. As the ratio of
recycled flue gas increased, the concentrations of solid-phase phenol compounds were increased gradually while
that of gas-phase phenol compounds was decreased. These trends were similar with that of PAHs. Therefore, the
effects of O2/RFG combustion on the formation and control of organic pollutants were more significant as the O2
concentration in the feed gas was increased.

Alkene and Benzene Compounds
Tables 6 and 7 showed the concentrations of different alkene and benzene compounds emitted from the O2/RFG
waste incineration system. The O2 concentrations in the feed gas were controlled at 21% and 40% and the ratio of
recycled flue gas were controlled at 0, 15, 35, 55 and 75%. Six alkene compounds and ten benzene compounds were
analyzed and discussed in this section. With 21% O2 concentration in the feed gas, the major alkene compounds in
solid-phase samples were allyl chloride and methacrylonitile, and the major benzene compounds were benzene,
toluene and 1,2,3-trichlorobenzne. As to the gas-phase samples, all the sixteen organic compounds were be detected
but their concentrations were very low. Most chlorobenzenes, dichlorobenzene and trichlorobenzene compounds
were present in gas-phase samples. The kinds and concentrations of alkene and benzene compounds present in
gas-phase samples were more than that in solid-phase. This phenomenon is related to the higher volatilities of these
compounds. As the ratio of recycled flue gas increased, the concentrations of alkene and benzene compounds in
solid-phase samples increased while that in gas-phase sample increased and then decreased. This trend was similar
with that for PAHs and phenol compounds as mentioned above.
The kinds and concentrations of alkene and benzene compounds were increased as the O2 concentration in feed gas
was increased from 21% to 40%. The major alkene compounds in gas-phase and solid-phase samples were still allyl
chloride and methacrylonitrile. The major benzene compounds were benzene, toluene, ethylbenzene, m-xylene,
o-xylene (BTEX) and dichlorobenzene. Since BTEX, chlorobenzenes, dichlorobenzene and trichlorobenzene were
all volatile, most of them were present in gas-phase samples. Comparing the data in Tables 6 and 7 showed that
increased the O2 concentration in feed gas would increase the formations of gas-phase alkene and benzene
compounds and decrease their concentrations in solid-phase samples. As the ratio of recycled flue gas increased, the
concentrations of alkene and benzene compounds in solid-phase samples slightly increased while that in gas-phase
sample were increased and then decreased. As the O2 concentration in feed gas was 40% and ratio of recycled flue
gas was 30%, the concentrations of alkene and benzene compound were the highest.
