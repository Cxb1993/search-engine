2A Study on Light Attenuation in Reservoirs
Abstract
Studies concerning limiting factors on algal growth in reservoirs in Taiwan have
primarily focused on nutrients such as nitrogen and phosphorous. However, based on large
amount data acquired during the past decade, the levels of algal density in these reservoirs are
considerably lower than the nutrient levels have suggested. The Carlson Trophic State Indices
(CTSI) based on secchi-depth are considerably higher than those based on nutrient levels.
This phenomenon suggests that factors other than nutrients may be limiting the growth of
algae in these reservoirs, and light limitation was the primary suspect.
In the present study, light attenuation properties in two reservoirs in southern Taiwan
have been investigated and the possibility of light limitation on algal growth evaluated.
On-site surveys were conducted on February and July of 2007 representing accordingly dry
and wet seasons. Results indicate that Nan-Hua Reservoir had stable light attenuation
properties with a reservoir-average light attenuation coefficient of 1.25m-1 for both dry and
wet seasons. Light attenuation in Wu-San-Tou Reservoir was more variable with
reservoir-averages of 1.64 and 1.18m-1 for dry and wet seasons respectively. A light
attenuation regression model based on chlorophylle-a, suspended solids and absorbance of
colored dissolved organic mater (CDOM) has been developed using pooled survey data from
the two reservoirs. Predictions using the model have indicated that suspended solid was by far
the most prevalent factor contributing to the light attenuation in two reservoirs.
Light limitations on algal growth have also been evaluated using Steel function. Results
indicated that half-saturation light intensity occurred at depths between 2.8 and 4.3m
depending on seasons and reservoirs. Deep photic zones in these two reservoirs suggest that
due to high intensity of solar radiation in this region, light limitation is unlikely to be a
limiting factor for algal growth in spite of relatively significant light attenuation in these water
bodies. Further studies are necessary to identify factors leading to the differences of algal
intensity among reservoirs and between seasons in this region.
