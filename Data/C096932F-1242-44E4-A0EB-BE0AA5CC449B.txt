In the study, organic-inorganic hybrids are composed of biodegradable polyesters and titanium oxide (or 
silica) grafted carbon nanotube (CNE). Two types of nanocomposed (i.e. (A). BDP polyester/CNT-graft-SiO2 
nanocomposites, (B) BDP polyester/CNT-graft-TiO2 nanocomposites SiO2 grafted CNE/polyester 
nanocomposites and TiO2 grafted CNE/polyester nanocomposites) will be analyzed. To enhance absorption of 
multi-walled carbon nanotube (CNB) onto polyester, silica and titanium oxide, carboxyl acid and 
amino-functional modification are applied to the CNB via chemical modification using nitric acid and 
hexamethylene, respectively. A construction of sol-gel sheaths onto the surface of CNB will be carried out 
with the assistance of ultrasound. The ultrasound assistance prevents an unregulated agglomeration induced 
by the hydrophobicity of CNB and phenyl-containing sols, leading to a selection construction of sol-gel 
sheaths on the nanotube surface. With the aim of understanding and controlling physical and structural 
properties of the nanocomposites, different synthetic conditions are investigated to assess their influence on 
the final hybrid behavior.

前言 
生物分解性高分子具有一般高分子材料的功能，而且使用後在自然界受到細菌、黴菌、藻類等微
生物的作用，能逐漸分解成二氧化碳、水及其他無害的殘留物質，不致對人類唯一的地球村帶來污染，
追求人與自然之和諧。目前生產生物分解性聚酯材料的國家以美國、日本、德國為主要，各國對分解
性聚酯材料的研發非常的重視，及致力於技術研發與生產高性能、高附加價值、低成本及多元化用途
的分解性聚酯材料。隨著生活水平之提升，兼顧特殊機能性與環境親和性之特殊功能性生物可分解及
可再生性之聚酯材料，乃為國內刻不容緩之研究課題。 
研究目的 
本研究計畫之目的為拓展生物分解性聚酯材料之新應用，研究與製備特殊功能性與強化性之生物
分解型聚酯奈米複合材料—「強化型生物分解性聚酯/奈米碳管複合材料」：奈米碳管接枝金屬氧化物
SiO2 與 TiO2 奈米微粒，此生物分解性聚酯奈米複合材料中之奈米碳管在接枝 SiO2 與 TiO2 奈米微粒後，
不僅在結構上可強化此生物分解性聚酯奈米複合材料塊材，更可在導電性、導熱性與抗輻射等特殊功
能性上，強化此生物分解性聚酯/奈米碳管複合材料之特殊性能。將此生物可分解及資源再生性聚酯與
米碳管在接枝 SiO2 與 TiO2 奈米微粒混成，強化此生物可分解聚酯奈米複合材料的特殊功能性。

相同的特徵峰：位於 1350 cm-1 的 D band 和位於 1580 cm-1 的 G band。G band 形成原因與碳原子 sp2 鍵
結相關，表示高頻率電子第一階 E2g 模式；而 D band 的生成是由於碳管上的缺陷所造成。藉由兩特徵
峰的比值，可以知道碳管上的缺陷多寡。 將圖八中各個特徵峰取強度最高值，將 D band 除以 G band
算其比值，得到表一，由表可看出官能化碳管的比值最大，表示酸化後的碳管會使缺陷增加。而接上
TiO2 後的碳管比值均比純碳管和酸化碳管兩者要小，顯示出 TiO2 奈米粒子能夠填補碳管的缺陷位置，

Abstract 
 
Crystallization and characterization of the poly(l-lactic acid)/silica and poly(l-lactic acid)/titania 
nanocomposites which use the sol-gel method  were investigated by differential scanning calorimetry 
(DSC), polarized optical microscopy (POM), Fourier transform infrared spectroscopy (FTIR), 
transmission electron microscopy (TEM), electron spectroscopy for chemical analysis (ESCA) and 
wide-angle x-ray diffraction (WAXD). The result of DSC analysis at the two systems confirmed that 
the crystallization rate increased with the trend by adding silica and titanium dioxide which can be 
formed nuclei to improve the PLA crystalline. TEM observed inorganic particles distribution of 
composite materials. By polarized optical microscopy (POM) to observe poly(l-lactic acid)/silica and 
poly(l-lactic acid)/titania spherulite, the two systems spherulite growth rate is also significantly 
upgraded, especially in the poly(l-lactic acid)/ titanium dioxide composite materials, could be 
observed the ring-band that original PLLA spherulite does not exist. Showing titanium dioxide and 
PLLA have a special force, resulting in changes in spherulite patterns. Fourier transform infrared 
spectroscopy (FTIR) analysis showed that poly(l-lactic acid) / titanium dioxide composite materials to 
generate new functional groups in the Ti-O-C absorption peak, the force with the hybrid material of 
polylactic acid and titanium dioxide, relative volume of the composition. Electron spectroscopy for 
chemical analysis (ESCA) to further verify the binding force of the changes in chemical bonds, 
causing the force confirmed the occurrence of the reaction mechanism for the transesterification. 
Supplemented by wide-angle x-ray diffraction (WAXD) composite materials confirmed the 
esterification reaction due to pay the original chain poly(l-lactic acid) molecular chain and change the 
way with crystallization, the crystallization of part of absorption peak decreased and the destruction of 
the crystallization peaks.
