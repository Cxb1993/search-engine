快速原子力顯微鏡應用於動態奈米加工與奈米壓痕之分析與實
驗研製(3/3) 
 
計畫編號：NSC 96-2628-E-150-005-MY3 
執行期限：98年8月1日至99年10月31日 
主持人：方得華 國立高雄應用科技大學 機械工程系 
共同主持人：劉建宏 國立虎尾科技大學 光電與材料研究所 
計畫參與人員：洪正翰、康紹輝、黃彥鈞、詹啟佑 
 
 
摘要 
本研究利用快速原子力顯微術(High speed atomic force microscopy, HS-AFM)應
用於奈米加工技術與分析。實驗以高頻音叉與AFM的動態系統結合，並應用在PET
高分子材料的表面加工，以不同溫度與力量下去分析機械特性。結果發現快速頻率
的刮痕加工於PET材料加溫至20~60 °C，接近Tg點時，材料因溫度升高並快速軟化
進而提升了加工的深度。隨著溫度的升高，會造成PET材料剛性與表面能下降，進
而提高材料的移除率，但是也造成黏滯力的提升，同時增加黏滯區域。快速奈米加
工的單位面積速度可達到31.25 Hz (單點約62.5μs)加工PET材料，經加溫在30~50 nN
的受力下，移除率為251.85~2422.66 μm3相較於在室溫下為26.60~70.30 μm3移除率提
升許多。快速接觸與非接觸式奈米放電加工的加工深度分別為24~145 nm和17~128 
nm，研究並得到快速放電加工移除材料於不同時間的移除率。非接觸式加工0.018
秒瞬間的電流量為7 nA，與接觸式加工電流量為 2.5 nA。而快速微陣列加工平均電
流為2~11nA，已經達到新型快速放電奈米加工技術的應用。 
 
關鍵詞: 快速原子力顯微鏡、PET、黏滯力、奈米壓痕、奈米刮刻

2. 實驗方法 
2.1材料特性 
    實驗材料分為二個部份，第一部份為PET材料，PET單體分子式為C10H8O4，玻
璃轉移溫度（Tg）介於75～80℃之間，楊氏模數(Young’s modulus, E)為2.8~3.1 GPa，
且具有良好的透明性及光澤度，並廣泛應用於生活與工程應用中。另一個實驗材料
為氧化鋅，此氧化鋅薄膜以ITO為基材，利用濺鍍機(sputter)鍍上氧化鋅膜。在鍍膜
前置作業，先以超音波震盪於酒精中清洗表面，去除附著在二氧化矽表面上的微粒
和有機物。濺鍍系統流程為：(1)放置靶材及清洗乾淨的基板，調整好靶材與遮板位
置。(2)以機械幫浦(pump)粗抽至3x10-2 Torr的真空度，再以擴散幫浦細抽至3x10-6 
Torr的真空度。(3)通入高純度Ar氣體，使得工作壓力保持在5x10-2 Torr的真空度。
(4)RF裝置控制器的設定，開啟控制器後進行濺鍍。(5)預鍍10分鐘，以去除靶材表
面的雜質及附著物。(6)預鍍完畢後，移開遮板正式濺鍍薄膜。(7)濺鍍結束後，關閉
遮板、通入氣體與控制器。(8)待降溫後，取出試片。最後將所鍍之薄膜表面應用於
奈米放電加工與特性分析。

進一步去量化PET高分子材料在不同溫度下的加工深度分析，如圖6結果得知，
當溫度分別在20、30、40、50、60 °C時，加工深度分別為 14~37 nm、35~72 nm、
93~153 nm、140~240 nm和 239~363 nm。這個現象是由於加溫接近Tg點時，造成材
料的快速軟化並提升了加工時的深度。因此，加工的移除量隨著加熱溫度而提升。
由以上結果看出，我們可以進一步深入探討材料移除率，如圖7為快速刮痕加工移除

PET材料造成軟化與表面能下降，並提高移除率提高的能力。 
 
3.2 黏滯力分析 
因為PET材料受到加熱開始軟化，加工過程PET材料容易附著在探針表面，進
而產生黏滯力，所以為了探討此效應，我們利用微小的AFM針尖來量測材料表面的
黏滯力。圖8(a)為在20~60 °C下，在不同的探針力量(不同深度)的黏滯力，當溫度由
20 °C升到60 °C，黏滯力會隨著加熱溫度升高而提高，這個現象是因為材料軟化後，
探針刮過去時會帶著大量的PET原子進而產生相當大的阻力，黏滯力因此上升。圖8 
(b)-(c)為PET在不同溫度下探針的黏滯曲線圖，隨著溫度的升高會影響增加黏滯區
域，但較高的黏滯力會造成探針的損傷。 
從圖9探討PET材料的硬度與黏滯力的功函數分析。黏滯力的功函數是估算黏滯
力與作用位移獲得的。由圖9(a)中發現當材料隨溫度提升導致材料的硬度與剛性從
75 MPa下降至6 MPa，並造成黏滯力的功函數從1.4 nJ增加到11.3 nJ。因此由結果得
知，溫度愈高時材料的剛性會下降，但是黏滯力的功函數會增加。圖9(b)為PET材料
在不同溫度的剛性參數。此分析可提供探針在適合的硬度與黏滯力的功函數下加工。

3.7 奈米放電加工的電流特性 
圖18為實驗擷取接觸與非接觸式的動態放電加工電流，可分析瞬間加壓在材料
表面的電流與電荷量。在8Hz，0.018秒所形成的非接觸式瞬間即發的電流量為7 nA;
而以接觸式瞬間加壓於0.018秒所形成電流量為2.5 nA。然而利用快速放電的陣列加
工所形成的電流為2~11 nA。微小的快速電流加工能在瞬間加熱移除材料，可應用在
半導體材料的微加工技術。 
 
4. 結論 
本研究為原子力顯微鏡動態加工應用與分析。獲得結果如下說明： 
(1) AFM在快速頻率的刮痕加工於PET材料於20~60 °C，分別施加30~50nN力量，得
到加溫接近Tg點造成材料的快速軟化並提升了加工時的深度。 
(2) PET材料料移除率為251.85~2422.66 μm3，經加熱的PET材料造成剛性與表面能下
降，並提高移除率提高的能力。 
(3) PET材料受到加熱開始軟化的表面的黏滯力呈現出材料隨溫度與力量增加的特
性，並同時增加黏滯區域，較高的黏滯力會造成探針的損傷。PET材料隨溫度
提升而使硬度與剛性下降75~6 MPa，並造成黏滯力的功函數提升σ =1.4~11.3 nJ。 
(4) 快速微刮刻在62.5 μs /point 加工PET材料。由黏滯力分析發現，當施加壓力低於
9nN力量下加工可以得到低黏滯壓力加工條件。經加工的結構可應用在硬碟儲
存。 
(5)快速接觸與非接觸式放電加工，非接觸式加工深度為24~145nm，而接觸式加工深
度為17~128 nm，可得到快速放電加工移除材料於不同時間的移除率。 
(6)非接觸式加工瞬間電流量為7nA與接觸式加工電流量為2.5nA。而快速微陣列加工
平均電流為2~11nA，達到快速微電流加工技術。

這方面的機械工業化與實務算是一流的、研究起步甚早，應用成果頗為豐碩。
而本計畫主持人方得華與英國密德塞克斯大學(Middlesex University)是位於倫敦
北邊郊區一所規模很大的大學。密德塞克斯大學的產品設計與工程系的科技團
隊，在過去已經有初步的相關研究合作經驗。本研究計畫案也需要參考與藉助
該單位的設計與實際應用，因此主持人於2009年8月短期前往，赴英國位於倫敦
(London)的密德塞克斯大學校本部進行學術參訪與討論未來相關合作。 
這裡介紹英國協助研究之機構，其研究設備及人力配合情形暨特色。密德
塞克斯大學是英國倫敦最佳研究指標的國際化大學之ㄧ，擁有 100 多年歷史，
五個大校區，學校吸引來自 100 多個不同國家，超過 3,000 多名國際學生，總

Slippage and Direction Sensing Based on a Flexible 
Tactile Sensor with Structural Electrodes 
 
Cheng-Hsin Chuang* and Chen-Tai Lu 
Department of Mechanical Engineering 
Southern Taiwan University 
Tainan, Taiwan 
chchuang@mail.stut.edu.tw

Abstract— A novel flexible tactile sensor for sensing the incident 
slippage and its direction were designed by introducing the 
concept of structural electrodes on a piezoelectric film (PVDF). 
The structural electrodes consisted of an elastomeric column on 
the top of PVDF film with the distributed microelectrodes 
underneath the elastomeric column. As an object was placed 
upon the elastomeric column and pushed by an external force, 
the occurrence of slippage can be detected by the output voltages 
from the distributed microelectrodes due to the corresponding 
bending stresses distributed on the PVDF film. In addition, two 
opposite output voltages read out from different microelectrodes 
can differentiate the direction of slippage while the elastomeric 
column was bent by the friction force between the object and 
column surface. As the experimental results, two peak voltages 
happened simultaneously in either compressive-stress area or 
tensile-stress area when the slippage occurred. Thus, the 
slippage between the object and elastomeric column can be 
detected. Besides, the peak value of slippage voltage depends on 
the height of column and the weight of object. This paper 
successfully demonstrated the concept of structural electrodes 
for sensing the incident slippage based on a piezoelectric tactile 
sensor. 
I. INTRODUCTION 
Grasping an object of unknown weight is the most 
important demand for a robot, however, the control of 
grasping force needs the detection of slippage to prevent the 
object fallen from the robot finger. The global slippage usually 
occurs after a sequence of small vibrations due to the partial 
slippage within local rough surface; therefore, a fast and 
dynamic response is significant for sensing slippage. Early, 
Cutkosky et al. [1] utilized a couple of accelerometers to 
detect the incipient slippage by sensing microvibrations 
caused by the propagation of the slip regions. However, the 
signal of accelerometer is difficult to correlate with fiction 
force. Gaetano et al. [2] proposed a tactile sensor to detect the 
incipient object slippage using the normal and shear stress 
information from a PVDF transducers array. They 
demonstrated the slippage can be found by a trained neural 
network with the signal patterns of normal and shear stresses. 
But, the accuracy for detecting the global sliding slippage is 
not high enough due to the flat surface of device. Maeno et al. 
[3, 4] developed an artificial finger skin with silicone ridges 
and embedded a pair of PVDF transducers inside the ridge for 
grasp force control. Another similar design can be found from 
the work of Kang et al. [5]. A PVDF strip is arranged along 
the direction normal to the surface of finger of the robot hand, 
thus, the incipient slip can be detected. Although PVDF film 
has many advantages for tactile sensor such as high 
piezoelectric voltage sensitivity, flexible and light-weight thin 
film material, responsiveness over a wide frequency range, 
ruggedness and inertness to chemical agents. But, the 
fabrication processes of PVDF-based sensors usually cannot 
be compatible with planar micromachining technology, which 
makes almost impossible to reduce the sensor size and to 
implement the massive production for industrial applications. 
In this study, we introduced an innovative idea to design a 
tactile sensor with structural electrodes for multi-axial force 
and slippage sensing. The so-called structural electrode is 
made by the combination of column-like structure and 
distributed microelectrodes based on MEMS fabrication 
process. In the following article, the electro-mechanical 
behavior of present tactile sensor was evaluated by numerical 
analysis. Furthermore, a tactile sensor with single silicone 
rubber column was fabricated and examined for the slippage 
detection here.

III. SENSOR FABRICATION 
A PVDF-based tactile sensor with structural electrodes 
was fabricated and the processes are indicated in the Fig. 4. A 
commercial 52μm-thick PVDF thin film (Measurement 
Specialties, Inc.) was utilized as the sensing material. Firstly, 
we stripped the both sides of Ag layers by acetone and 
deposited 1000 Å metal layer Cr by E-beam evaporator. Then, 
we patterned the microelectrodes array by the standard 
photolithography and wet etching with a mask design as 
shown in the Fig. 4(b). The silicone rubber column was 
molded by a metal mold and cured in a vacuum oven at 85℃ 
for 5 hours. Finally, the silicone rubber column and the 
patterned PVDF thin film were bonded together on a glass 
substrate as shown in the Fig. 4(b). The dimensions of the 
tactile sensor are the same with the simulation model in the 
Fig. 3(a). However, in order to investigate the structural 
effects on the detection of lateral force and slippage, we 
fabricated three kinds of silicone rubber columns with 
different heights such as 30, 20 and 10 mm.

IV. EXPERIMENTAL SETUP AND RESULTS 
A. Lateral Force Sensing 
For a piezoelectric tactile sensor, it is difficult to measure a 
static force due to induced charges by a static force that could 
only be detected as the force is loading. The output voltage 
will decline rapidly in a very short time, because there are no 
further charges that can be induced from piezoelectric film 
after the contact force in a static state. Therefore, a dynamic 
test system was built to obtain the output characteristics of a 
tactile sensor. The lateral force acting at the top of silicone 
rubber was excited by a shaker (Data Physics Corp.) and 
calibrated by a force sensor (PCB209C02, PCB Piezotronics, 
Inc.). Two channels of sensor output from right and left 
microelectrodes were amplified by a charge amplifier (B&K 
NEXUS2690A) with a 40Hz low-pass filter before monitored 
by an oscilloscope. As the experimental results shown in the 
Fig. 6, the outputs of force sensor and tactile sensor were 
recorded while a cycling lateral force 0.6N excited at 2 Hz. 
Two opposite signal can be apparently obtained from left and 
right electrode, hence, we can determine the direction of 
lateral force based on the the sign of VL and VR.

V. CONCLUSSION 
A novel tactile sensor with single structural electrode has 
been designed, fabricated and measured for the detection of 
lateral force and incident slippage. The force direction can be 
determined by two opposite voltage outputs form distributed 
microelectrodes under the silicone rubber column. A high 
sensitivity of slippage detection can be achieved as the height 
of column is taller, however, the detectable range of friction 
force could be limited due to the nonlinear displacement of 
elastomeric column especially for taller column. Furthermore, 
we can extend the concept of structural electrode to an array 
for multiple sensing in a large area if the size of electrode and 
column can be further reduced by micromachining technology.

2. Experimental 
Before the device fabrication, 500-nm-thick ZnO seed layers were deposited onto PET 
substrates using radio frequency (rf) magnetron sputter deposition technique. 100-nm-thick Ag 
electrodes were subsequently deposited onto the ZnO film by electron beam evaporation to serve 
as Schottky contacts while lithography was performed for defining the interdigitated contact 
pattern. The fingers of the Ag contact electrodes were 150 m long and 10 m wide with 10 m 
spacing. The active area of the whole device was 150×160 m2. Then we employed the 
photoresists in protecting the electrode patterns by lithography technique. The sample with 
photoresist-protected interdigitated electrodes was subsequently immersed in the Zn(NO3)2/ 
C4H13N3 (DETA) aqueous solution for 3 hours at 90 oC. The fabricated MSM photosensors were 
removed from the solution, rinsed with distilled water and dried in air. Finally, we removed the 
photoresists from the interdigitated electrode surface of devices. The schematic structure of ZnO 
NR MSM PD is shown in Figure 1. On the other hand, the traditional MSM PDs with 
500-nm-thick ZnO film were fabricated for comparison, where no ZnO NRs can be found. 
X-ray diffraction (XRD), micro-photoluminescence (micro-PL), and high-resolution 
transmission electron microscopy (HRTEM, JEOL JEM-3010) were then used to characterize the 
optical and crystallographic properties of the as-grown ZnO NRs. Surface morphologies of the 
NRs and NR PDs were characterized by a field-emission scanning electron microscope (FESEM, 
JEOL JSM-6700F). An HP-4156C semiconductor parameter analyzer was then used to measure 
current-voltage (I-V) characteristics of the fabricated ZnO NR PDs. Spectral responsivity 
measurements were also performed by TRAIX 180 system with a 300-W xenon arc lamp light 
source and a standard synchronous detection scheme. 
 
3. Results and discussion 
Figure 2(a) and 2(b) show the XRD and PL results of the as-grown ZnO NR arrays, 
respectively. The XRD pattern of ZnO nanorods indicates reflection peaks of hexagonal wurtzite 
structure, (002), (101), (103) and (004) correspond at 2θ = 34.42, 36.25, 62.86 and 72.56, 
respectively. They are in good accordance with the JCPDS card No. 36-1451 with lattice 
constants of a = 3.25 Å and c = 5.21 Å. The sharp diffraction peak (002) indicates that the ZnO 
nanorods have grown along the c-axis. It were found a strong UV emission centered at 380 nm 
and a broad peak in the visible region 550 nm as shown in Figure 2(b). 
 In Figure 3, HRTEM and selected area electron diffraction (SAED) pattern images show 
the ZnO NRs is structurally uniform and contains no defects such as dislocations and stacking. It 
clearly shows the lattice spacing of 0.52 nm corresponds to d-spacing of (002) crystal planes, 
indicating the growth of the crystalline ZnO NRs along c-axis direction.  
FE-SEM image with a 45o tilt angle of the ZnO NR photodetectors is as shown in Figure 4, 
the inset shows a 45o tilt angle photograph of the NR arrays. The average length and diameter of 
these ZnO NRs were around 1-2 μm and 100 nm, respectively. It was found that high density of 
well-aligned ZnO NR arrays were successfully grown on the gap of interdigitated electrodes. 
Figure 5 shows current-voltage (I-V) characteristics of the fabricated traditional ZnO film and 
ZnO NR MSM PDs with the same Ag interdigitated electrodes measured in dark and under 
illumination. Photocurrent measurements were performed by the 370-nm. With an applied bias of 
3 V, it was found that the dark current/ photocurrent for ZnO film and ZnO NR MSM PDs were 
96.4 nA/1.3 μA and 0.11 nA/19.6 μA, respectively. The photocurrent to dark current contrast 
ratios of the ZnO film and ZnO NR PDs were 9.5×102 and 3.23×103, respectively. The 
enhancement of UV light absorption could be attributed to the nanorod arrays having larger 
surface area than film in this study. Thus, the photocurrents generated from NR MSM PDs are 
increased much more than the traditional film MSM PDs.

2. Finite element indentation analysis

DEFORM-2D. An axisymmetric cone with half-included angle of 70.3° in which the 
conical indenter has the same area function as a Berkovich tip was used in this study. 
The indenter is constrained to move only in the thickness-direction of the bulk metal. 
Nanoindentation is performed under a precisely continuous measurement of the force 
(load) and the displacement (depth) during the loading and unloading process (Fig. 1 
(b)). Figure 1 (c) presents the stress-strain curve with bilinear constitutive law of a 
bulk material. Note that E is Young’s modulus; ET is tangent modulus; Y is yielding 
strength. During the analyses, the conical indenter is assumed to be rigid. The bulk 
metal is assumed to be elastic-linear work-hardening. Because of symmetry only a 
half of the conical indenter and bulk material were taken for simulation. The nodes 
along the axis of rotation can move only along the y-axis and all the nodes on the 
bottom of the mesh are fixed. The meshes near the indenter needed to be very fine to

frictionless. In this nanoindentation case, the bulk metal has a 4000 nm thickness and 
a 200,000 nm width. The model adopts 4-node tetrahedral elements for the bulk metal 
in two-dimensional simulation. The proper size of the meshes for the FE models is

4. Prediction of stress-strain behavior by experimental data 
The loading-displacement curves can be obtained by nanoindentation tests. The 
aim of this study is the stress-strain behavior of a local region of a material can be 
directly extracted by the comparison of load-displacement behavior between the 
abductive network prediction and an indentation test. The prediction model of 
abductive network can provide valuable references conveniently in prediction of the 
mechanical parameters such as E, Y and ET after nanoindentation tests. Figures 7(a) 
and (b) show the stress-strain behavior is directly extracted by the comparison of 
load-displacement behavior for Cu and Ti materials. The FEM simulation 
(DEFORM-2D) and abductive networks (AIM) are adopted to predict the mechanical 
parameters (Y and ET). The Young’s modulus E can be obtained directly by 
nanoindentation test. The value of E is 98 GPa and 140 GPa for Cu and Ti, repectively. 
The predictive values of Y and ET are 300 MPa and 15GPa by FEM simulation for Cu 
material. Moreover, the predictive values of Y and ET are 300 MPa and 16GPa by 
abductive networks for Cu material. The predictive values of Y and ET are 500 MPa 
and 13GPa by FEM simulation for Ti material. Moreover, the predictive values of Y 
and ET are 400 MPa and 16GPa by abductive networks for Ti material.
