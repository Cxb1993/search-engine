作負荷，長期在如此狀況下會增加飛安上的風險，進而增加飛機碰撞的機率，當
這樣的情形下，必頇靠著當下每架飛機飛行狀況去判斷，如何在只知道飛機彼此
之機的位置、速度等基本數據，而瞭解到未來的碰撞可能，利用運動軌跡的描述，
建立出危險的區域使飛機有能力去做出避開這些區域的動作。然而在進場前，大
部份巡航航路上的飛機會以其最有效率的速度行進，而且通常在巡航階段的飛機
都會依高度、航程選擇最有省油的等速度前進。在於管制程序上只要保持一定的
隔離，及調整速度相差過大的飛機使交通不致於圔車，將可以保持空中交通的安
全性。但是，在進場、離場等階段轉換之間飛機行進的速度都是持續改變的。雖
然以時間為基準的隔離並不是新的概念。在相同的時間隔離下飛行員將會有固定
的時間作出反應，不會因為速度加快而縮短反應時間，對於飛行安全上相信更有
幫助。例如飛機在進場的階段中，飛機與飛機之間的距離隔離只能依賴航管人員
的經驗，將後到的飛機與前方飛機的距離拉開，使前方飛機在減速進場的過程中
仍可以至少得到最小的 3海浬的隔離。然而次一架飛機也是減速進場，此時在處
理後續進場的飛機上，航管人員依然是以相同的模式進行。這樣的方式必頇依賴
航管人員的經驗及其隔離的技巧，也是容易為了保持飛航安全而造成空間浪費的
一個階段。因此本研究將會在流場描述的模型中加入能夠展現出時間隔離特性的
因子，使飛機的速度能夠達到自動化時間隔離的調整，以減輕航管人員的負擔。 
。而在透過最佳化的方法來找出飛機進場的順序，此方法主要是先找出何種排列
組合讓飛機降落所花費的時間是最少的，然後再藉由此排序找出整個系統的最少
變動時間，而後系統會提供飛機一個建議的飛行速度，利用此方法能在預測未來
會有擁擠情況發生時，先行提供解決的建議，減少機場附近空域的飛機密度，再
加上流場描述的模型中加入能夠展現出時間隔離特性的因子，使飛機的速度能夠
達到自動化時間隔離的調整，使整個航道容量可以最大化。 
 
文獻探討 
 
在[1]中指出飛機在進場到降落的過程中會因為頂風的大小影響飛機與飛機
之間的距離，進而浪費了跑道的容量。文中分別指出在不同的頂風情況下，ICAO
的標準距離隔離會因為風速的影響，使後方的飛機與前方的飛機距離加大，而造
成跑道可使用的時間無飛機使用，直接的浪費了跑道的使用率。相對於直接的距
離隔離，以時間為基準隔離飛機的方式使得航道的使用率可以提高。且飛機在跑
道上起降，仍能符合 ICAO規定的時間隔離的標準，且能避開前一架飛機所產生
的尾流，以達到飛航安全。

因為公路與航道上的載具不同，車輛跟飛機在行進時的特性也有所不同。針
對公路與航道上載具的性質差異，我們會在原本的公路模型中加上特殊的限制，
及參數上的轉換，以符合飛機在航道上行進的特性。 
公路與航道上載具之間相異的性質有： 
1、飛機不能靜止於航道上。 
2、飛機的行進速度不能超過其容許值範圍。 
3、後方飛機會受到前方飛機之尾流的影響。 
4、由於飛機速度遠大於車輛，必頇有足夠的隔離距離。 
 
本研究將修改原本的 LWR Model 以符合上述特性。針對這些需求，我們把
飛機以一類似常態分佈的波形代替平均分散的密度值，此常態分部的脈波表示航
道上的 Danger Value Distribution(DVD)。這個脈波形成的方式是以飛機所在位置
為中心，依據該飛機在航道上的速度設定，得到飛機在 N 個 time step 之後能夠
到達的時間點為危險的分佈位置。也就是假設以 10秒為單位，距離中點 10秒的
位置就是一個 sigma、20秒為二個 sigma，以此類推由中心往兩邊分佈。所以我
們可以預知，飛機以越高的速度前進，相同的 10秒所走的距離越大，則一個 sigma
的位置離中心點越遠，這個脈波也就越寬。不變的是各個 sigma 的值及中心點的
最大值。這樣的一個模型就能夠由航道上的脈波分佈情況得知飛機所處位置，並
且使得 DVD 值擁有 TCAS 中監視區域的效果，也就是越接近飛機危險值越高。 
在得知一架飛機的位置與其在航程中的速度設定之後，我們將可以知道他在
任何一個時間點的位置，則此時間與位置的關係將可以用來建立 60秒、40秒及
25秒的監視區域。當然在同一航道上飛機的行進方向相同，監視區域的大小可
以適當的調整。然而若用原始的 Eulerian model 則常態分佈的脈波會隨著速度變
快被延展，而且其峰值會隨著速度變快而降低。然而，整個脈波合依然是 1。

DVD模型應用於機尾亂流程序 
在此流場模型中，利用脈波的傳遞來表示飛機在航道上行進時對於航道上其
他飛機造成的威脅程度。然而不同的機型，各自擁有其不同的特性。代表飛機所
在位置的波峰向前延伸的部分表示該飛機在未來將造成的威脅，而且將隨著飛機
行進增加；而向後延伸的部分則是該飛機在過去造成的威脅，並且會隨著飛機行
進而降低。對應到不同機型的物理性質，大型的飛機行經的空域將留下較強的渦
流，而小型的飛機將產生較弱的渦流；對於渦流的抵抗能力，大型的飛機則能夠
承受較強的渦流，相同強度的渦流對於小型機的飛航安全則會造成較大的威脅。
因此波峰向前延伸的部分可用來描述飛機抵抗渦流的能力，而向後延伸的部分將
可以用來描述產生渦流的強度。代表大型飛機的脈波，向前延伸的部分會比小型
飛機小；而代表大型飛機的脈波，向後延伸的部分則會比小型飛機大。以 FAA
規範的機尾亂流隔離為例，飛機在連續進場時，必頇實施機尾亂流程序，避免受
到前方飛機遺留之渦流對飛安上的影響。此程序規範了各個機型飛機之間應有的
最小隔離，特別是小型機跟隨在大型機的情況下。FAA 將飛機分成 small、large、

In the USA, a system has been under development by the NASA Ames Research Center. The system, 
referred to as the Center/TRACON Automation System (CTAS), comprises of the Final Approach Spacing 
Tool (FAST), Traffic Management Advisor (TMA), and the Descent Advisor (DA). FAST provides terminal 
area controllers with heading and speed advisories to help generating an accurately spaced flow of aircraft 
onto the final approach course (Dallas and Erzberger 1995). TMA generates runway assignments, landing 
sequences, and landing times for all arriving aircraft. DA generates clearances for en-route controllers to 
handle arrival flows to metering gates. The advisories generated by these tools assist controllers in handling 
arrival aircraft starting at about 200 nm from the airport and continuing to the final approach fix (Davis et al. 
1994). This system has been operated in Dallas/Fort Worth (DFW) International Airport. 
In Europe, a similar system is done by the German Aerospace Center DLR (Hartmut et al. 2009). The 
system, referred to as 4D Cooperative Arrival Manager (4D-CARMA), will provide advisories to air traffic 
controllers. The advisories, which contain the information of landing runway, flight speed, flight altitude and 
flight trajectory, will assist controllers to handle the arrival traffic. Also, controllers can input their desired 
sequence to make the system provide the necessary advisories. This system had been simulated in Frankfurt 
Airport. 
The above two systems control the aircraft in the terminal area. If the airport capacity is greater than the 
arrival flow, then ATCs can deal with the traffic efficiently. But if the number of arrival aircraft is greater 
than the airport capacity, aircraft need to wait in the air for their landing time-slot, and longer time staying in 
the air may pose possible dangers in air travel. To solve this problem, this paper provides a concept of speed 
control in the en-route sector, which reduces the possibility of congestion in the terminal area.  
The current scheduling method for air traffic control is the First-Come-First-Served (FCFS) method, 
which means the aircraft that arrives earlier gets higher priority to land. Although easy to implement, FCFS 
method has be shown not able to use the capacities of air traffic well (Beasley 2000). 
Beasley et al. (2000) modeled the airplane landing issue by using the mix-integer formulation. Firstly, it 
uses the aircraft separation time to calculate the time windows of the landing time. Secondly, the 
mixed-integer concept and separation constraints were used to calculate the runway allocation. Since the 
separations between different types of aircraft are different, all the possible combinations of landing 
sequence have to be considered. This formulation is shown to be similar to the traveling salesman problem. 
Using this kind of formulation, the number of constraints grows with the square of the number of the aircraft 
and the time used for finding the solution grows rapidly accordingly. Hence heuristic methods are used to 
One major advantage of this research is in the formulation of the separation time constraints. A 
separation time matrix and a sequence matrix are used to express the necessary separation constraints. Using 
the purposed formulation,   aircraft will have     constraints as opposed to traveling salesman 
problem formulations which have         constraints for separation. The N-square growth in 
constraints greatly increases the time require to find the optimal solution. Therefore heuristic algorithms 
such as genetic algorithms and tree search algorithms are used in order to generate the solution within an 
acceptable amount of time. Since heuristic solutions are not guaranteed to be optimal, the answer may not 
give the ATCs the best advisory. In our formulation, the number of constraints grows linearly with the 
number of aircraft. Therefore heuristics algorithms are no longer required to find the solution within 
acceptable amount of time. Moreover, our formulation also considers the aircraft that lands prior to these 
being scheduled aircraft. Hence, the proposed formulation also works in the dynamic case. 
This paper is structured as follows. In Section 2 the mixed-integer programming problem formulation is 
presented. Section 3 discusses how to find the minimum arrival time variation of all the aircraft using 
quadratic programming. Section 4 shows how to further reduce the calculation time using position shift 
constraints. In Section 5 the historical data of the Taiwan Taoyuan International Airport was used to test our 
approach, and comparisons with FCFS method are presented.

 4. Reducing the Calculation Time 
When the number of arrival aircraft becomes larger, the calculation time becomes longer. By reducing 
unnecessary variables in the purposed formulation, the reduction in the calculation time is possible. Clearly 
the elements in the sequence matrix 𝑆 contribute the most to the number of decision variables. A careful 
examination of the possible sequences may eliminate some impossible combinations. Since the arrival time 
for each aircraft should locate in their respective arrival time windows, some sequence changes are unlikely 
to happen. Therefore, if the aircraft are pre-sorted according to their arrival time window, a banded schedule 
matrix S of width m is adequate for representing all the possible optimal solutions. After removing 
unnecessary variable,     variables in S will reduce to (2m+1)N-m(m+1) variables for N≧m+1. Using 
this technique, the number of variables grows linearly with the number of aircraft. Hence, the solution time 
required can be greatly reduced.

6. Conclusions 
When aircraft arrive at an airport, proper separations between aircraft are required. The proposed 
algorithm firstly checks if the separation time is sufficient between all the estimate arrival times of aircraft. 
The arrival time separation should meet ICAO’s regulations. If the system detects conflicts, it provides 
optimal sequences that have minimal operation time for all the arrival aircraft by using mixed-integer 
programming. After generating the optimal sequence, the gap between estimated arrival time and the 
scheduled arrival can be further minimized by utilizing the proposed velocity adjustment algorithm.  
Many existing researches are focused on scheduling aircraft in the terminal area. If the number of the 
arrival aircraft is beyond the capacity of the airport, the terminal area may become crowded. This paper 
proposes an algorithm to control the speed of aircraft in the en-route sector. Then these aircraft can have 
proper separations before entering the crowded terminal area. The proposed algorithm uses the idea of 
sequence matrix so that the constraint number is greatly reduced. With the additional variable reduction 
technique, the proposed algorithm can solve much larger problems within acceptable time.
