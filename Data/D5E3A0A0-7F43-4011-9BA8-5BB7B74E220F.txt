一、 緣由與目的 
隨著科技的進展，機械元件所能達到的功能雖然逐漸可為電氣或電子元件所取代，但是
人類社會中所需求的能量傳遞、變化的機械元件如齒輪卻不會因此改變。相反地，簡易加工、
高功率重量比、可達成不同的空間組合要求的齒輪減速機即成為新的設計要求。在一些可達
到特定應用的特殊漸開線齒輪中，為人所注目的是錐形齒輪 (conical gear 或 Beveloid gear)。 
錐形齒輪最早係由 Beam 於 1954 年所提出[4]，並以 Beveloid Gear 一詞稱呼此種齒輪。
錐形齒輪（圖 1）屬於廣義漸開線圓筒齒輪 (involute cylindrical gearing)，為變齒厚漸開線齒
輪。其主要的特性包括變齒厚、組合型態多樣化、組裝誤差敏感度低、加工容易等，因此近
年來由於齒輪傳動研究能力與加工技術的提昇，國外重要齒輪製造廠亦開始將錐形齒輪應用
在重要的動力設備，車用傳動設備[8]、船用減速機[10, 19]、機械臂用可調背隙行星減速機[16, 
51]、押出機用減速機[15]等，相關重要研究可參見表 1。

4. 實驗驗證 
本計畫將針對直交軸齒輪對進行齒面承載能力驗證實驗，以驗證所計算齒面應力值之可
靠度。實驗驗證設備係採用功率封閉式設備原理，即俗稱之背對背測試機(back-to-back test 
rig)。齒面疲勞驗證實驗係利用上述實驗設備進行不同負載（應力）－週期之測試，即根據齒
面破壞狀況以及材料之 S-N 曲線，以驗證所設計齒輪之齒面應力是否與計算值相符合。實驗
方法上除應用封閉功率式實驗設備外，亦需規劃以下事項 
 規劃負載與測試週期：根據材料之 S-N 曲線反推不同負載下的測試週期，並以此做為實驗
目標與測試週期的控制。 
 齒印調整：由於加工、組裝等誤差等影響，因此必須使齒面接觸斑落在齒面設計位置。此
位置亦需與齒輪量測檢驗數據相配合。 
 齒面破壞認定：一般對齒面破壞試驗的破壞認定標準為點蝕面積比，本計畫採用的是點蝕
面積與有效齒面總面積之比值小於 4%即宣告失效。 
本實驗由於以下因素，齒面修整將應用在正齒輪： 
 由於錐形齒輪在齒輪對中亦扮演背隙控制角色，若錐形齒輪齒面進行修整，則在背隙調整
過程中，規劃接觸點將會偏離規劃之節圓位置（圖 9），因而產生傳動誤差。若齒面修整
在正齒輪，則僅需控制正齒輪之組裝距離即可，背隙控制並不會影響到傳動精度。 
 由於齒面修整需以展成研磨方式加工，目前有合作齒輪加工廠之磨齒機無法研磨大錐角之
齒輪，更遑論齒面修整。

(2) 形狀係數之影響 
形狀係數為調整曲線外型比例的參數，一般而言，形狀係數越大時曲線彎曲幅度就增加。
圖 14表示齒面應力最大值在不同形狀係數變化，圖 15為不同曲線形狀係數下橢圓斑長軸方
向的應力分布情形。圖中可看出當形狀係數越大，即曲線越彎曲，橢圓斑是往大端方向增大。
雙曲線外形比例係數變大時，接觸斑往大端方向拓展會如圖 16之齒面應力分佈狀況所示，齒
面最大應力仍在理論接觸點，但整體接觸斑的外形會呈現類似水滴形狀。

DESIGNING SKEW CONICAL GEAR DRIVES IN APPROXIMATE LINE 
CONTACT FOR POWER TRANSMISSION  
 
Shyi-Jeng TSAI, and *Szi-Hann WU 
Department of Mechanical Engineering, National Central University 
No. 300, Jhong-Da Road, Jhong-Li, Taoyuan County 320, Taiwan 
 
Keywords: approximate line contact, profile-shifted transmission, loaded tooth contact analysis 
 
 
ABSTRACT 
 
The paper summarizes the research on the topic of 
improving the surface durability of conical gear drives for 
power transmission. The authors proposed a new concept 
for designing skew conical gear drives, which are 
designed in so-called approximate line contact combining 
with profile-shifted transmission. The contact patterns 
have a large major-to-minor-axis ratio, so that not only the 
surface durability can be increased, but also the sensitivity 
to edge contact can be reduced. A numerical method for 
loaded tooth contact analysis was also developed to solve 
the non-Hertzian contact problem of such a conical gear 
drive in approximate line contact. The feasibility of the 
design concept is verified both by FEM and by measuring 
the contact pattern on a test bench. 
 
INTRODUCTION 
 
Because conical involute gears [1-5], also known as 
bevloid gears, have many advantages, such as abilities to 
backlash control, assembly errors absorption, and also 
varieties of spatial combination and simple manufacturing, 
they become an important gear drive type for precision 
transmission. However, the conical gear drive is restricted 
in application to power transmission because of less 
surface durability due to point contact while in meshing. 
How to enhance the surface durability is therefore the 
focus of the development of such a gearing. In general, the 
Hertzian contact stress due to point contact can be reduced 
by using the following two measures: 
(1) improving the curvature relation of the engaged tooth 
surfaces; 
(2) reducing the angle between the directions of the first 
principal curvature of the engaged tooth surfaces. 
 
The concept of flank modification [6] belongs to the 
first measure. Based on the same concept, Mitome [7] 
proposed a novel type of concave-conical gearing. 
Another concept, not associated with manufacturing, but 
with design, can be found in the design of conical gearing 
for profile-shifted transmission. In this case, a better 
curvature relation between the contact points can be 
obtained by adjusting the position of the local tooth 
bearings. Tsai and Wu [8], for example, proposed a 
systematic design approach for conical gearing in 
profile-shifted transmission based on the concept of 
common operating rack. Ohmachi et al. [9] also developed 
profile-shifted conical gears with a larger tooth depth 
based on the same concept, however without considering 
the operating common rack.  
Beyond the first concept, Tsai and Wu [10] proposed a 
novel design approach for skew conical gear drives based 
upon the relations of line contact proposed by Roth and 
Tsai [4, 11]. The contact pattern of such a skew conical 
gear pair can be adjusted to form a point contact pattern 
with a larger major-to-minor-axis ratio, not necessarily 
using manufacturing measures mentioned above. The 
theoretical point of contact between the engaged teeth 
under a load spreads over the face-width. Consequently 
the contact pattern is similar to that of the line contact, and 
is therefore called “approximate line contact”. Compared 
with line contact pattern, such a gear drive has not only 
the advantage of the greater surface durability, but also 
less sensitive to edge contact caused by misalignment of 
the shaft axes. 
The goal of the paper is to present the concept for 
designing such a skew conical gear pair. A numerical 
example is also illustrated, and further simulated by using 
FEM and also verified by measuring the contact pattern on 
a test bench.

Relations for generating conical gears 
 
As well known, the tooth surfaces of the conical gears 
are in general involute helicoids with different helix angle 
on each flank. They are generated by an inclined rack with 
an angle θP with respect to the gear axe, Fig. 1. The pitch 
plane of the rack and the pitch cone of the generated 
conical gear are in tangency at the line t-t. The angle 
between the line t-t and the intersecting line of the rack 
flank with the pitch plane determines the helix angle βP. 
The gearing data for involute helicoids, such as the 
transverse pressures angle αC and the helix angle βC on 
the pitch circle can be calculated according the formulae 
in reference [2, 4, 11].

ILLUSTRATED EXAMPLE 
 
An example is demonstrated for the proposed conical 
gear drives in approximate line-contact. The gearing data 
are listed in Table 1, where the skew gear set consists of a 
conical and a helical gear. The gear set was designed for 
pitting test, so that it has a small angle η = 0.562° on the 
engaged left-side flanks (so-called “line contact”) and a 
larger angle η=6.539° for the right-side flanks (approxi-
mate line contact). The evaluation data in Table 2 is only 
valid for engagement on the right-hand flanks. Under 
consideration of the shift of line of action ΔbL (=40% 
face-width), the allowable manufacturing and assembly 
errors, as well as the specific contact stress can be 
calculated according to the proposed approach [10]. The 
allowable manufacturing tolerances for edge-contact-free 
meshing reveal the feasibility of the skew conical gear in 
practical application. 
The actual contact stresses are also calculated with a 
numerical method [12]. The calculated data show that the 
teeth engaged on the left-side flanks are more sensitive to 
edge contact than those on the right-side flanks. The 
contact stress on the right-side flanks is about 1.5 times 
greater than that on the left-side flanks (“line contact”), 
but there is also enough surface durability for power 
transmission.

The illustrated example was simulated numerically

The distribution of the contact stress over the engaged 
tooth surface was illustrated in Fig. 13. The calculated 
result shows a typical a saddle shape of non-Hertzian 
contact pattern. The comparison of the calculation with 
FEM-results is in a good agreement.  
 
Experimental Validation 
 
The measurement of the contact patterns of the 
proposed skew conical gear was conducted on a pitting
