部份互溶混合液的閃火點預測(2/3)-雙成份有機水溶液
計畫編號：NSC 96-2221-E-039-004-MY2
執行期限：96 年 8 月 1 日至 98 年 7 月 31 日
主持人：廖宏章 中國醫藥大學職安系
一、中文摘要
閃火點為判定液體火災爆炸危害的重
要參數，而部份互溶混合溶液出現在液液
萃取程序和非均勻相蒸餾程序。本研究計
畫針對部份互溶混合溶液(含雙成份有機
水溶液和多成份混合溶液)，分別發展能預
測雙成份部份互溶混合有機水溶液和由易
燃性溶劑組合成的多成份部份互溶混合溶
液的閃火點預測模式。本研究分別以水+1-
丁醇、水+2-丁醇、水+異丁醇、水+1-戊醇、
水+辛烷溶液和甲醇+甲苯+2,2,4-三甲基戊
烷、甲醇+丙酮+癸烷來驗證上述兩個模式
的預測能力。結果指出兩液相區的不互溶
行為不能忽略，於雙成份溶液，兩液相區
的閃火點幾乎為定值，於三成份溶液，兩
液相區的閃火點則不為定值，而是於個別
tie line 上，其閃火點幾乎為定值。整體而
言，當分別利用 LLE 和 VLE 參數來估算
兩液相區的範圍和閃火點，此模式可有效
的預測實驗數據，依此結果，本研究計畫
所提出的兩個模式可分別應用於含雙成份
部份互溶混合有機水溶液和由易燃性溶劑
組合成的多成份部份互溶混合溶液的火災
爆炸危害評估和本質較安全設計。
關鍵詞：閃火點、預測模式、部份互溶混
合溶液、氣液液平衡
Abstract
Flash point is the most important
variable used to characterize fire and
explosion hazard of liquids. Herein, partially
miscible mixtures are presented within the
context of liquid-liquid extraction processes
and heterogeneous distillation processes.
This project describes development of two
models for predicting the flash point of
partially miscible mixtures, including binary
aqueous-organic mixtures and ternary
mixtures of flammable solvents. To confirm
the predictive efficiency of the derived flash
points, the two models were verified by
comparing the predicted values with the
experimental data for the studied mixtures:
water + 1-butanol; water + 2-butanol; water
+ isobutanol; water + 1-pentanol; water +
octane and methanol + toluene +
2,2,4-trimethylpentane, methanol + acetone
+ decane. Results reveal that immiscibility in
the two liquid phases should not be ignored
in the prediction of flash point. The flash
point values almost remained constant in the
two liquid phases for the binary partially
miscible mixtures. On the other hand, the
flash point values almost kept constant in
each tie line for the ternary partially miscible
mixtures. Overall, the predictive results of
the two proposed models describe the
experimental data well when using the LLE
and VLE parameters to estimate sequentially
the span of two liquid phases and the flash
point, respectively. Potential application for
the model concerns the assessment of fire
and explosion hazards, and the development
of inherently safer designs for chemical
processes containing binary partially
miscible mixtures of aqueous-organic
system and ternary partially miscible
mixtures of flammable solvents.
Keywords: Flash point; Prediction; Partially
miscible mixtures;
Vapor-liquid-liquid equilibrium
二、前言
閃火點為可燃性液體釋放出足夠的
蒸氣和空氣形成可燃性混合物時的最低溫
度，於閃火點下，物質的蒸氣壓為形成其
4只適用於互溶系統，針對部份互溶系統所
發展的閃火點預測模式僅有廖等人所發展
適用於由易燃性溶劑所組合的雙成份部份
互溶系統[19]。延續之前的研究，本研究
的目的為建立能預測雙成份部份互溶有機
水溶液和多成份部份互溶溶液的閃火點預

成份部份互溶有機水溶液閃火點預測模式
中，選用了水+1-丁醇、水+2-丁醇、水+異

成份部份互溶溶液的閃火點預測模式中，
選用了甲醇+甲苯+2,2,4-三甲基戊烷和甲
醇+癸烷來驗證。
三、研究方法
3.1 實驗
以 Walter Herzog GmbH 所製之閃火
點測試儀,HFP 362-Tag,來量測溶液的閃火
點，閃火點測試儀利用控制裝置於接近預
測閃火點附近以一特定的加熱速率來加熱
樣本，在指定的溫度區間(測試區間)，閃
火點測試儀會使用點火頭自動測試。如果
預期閃火點低於或等於變化溫度(change
temperature)(依 ASTM D56 的標準變化溫
度取 60 oC)，則以 1 oC/min 的加熱速率加
熱，點火頭在測試區間-1 點火。如果預期
閃火點比較高，則以 3 oC/min 的加熱速率
加熱，點火頭在測試間隔-2 點火。當溫度
值等於預期閃火點減去起始測試溫度值開
始第一次點火，當測試溫度值超過預期閃
火點加上結束測試溫度值，若仍測不到閃
火點，則加熱器停止。所用的標準測試方
法為 ASTM D56[20]，ASTM D56 所選用
的參數數值如下：起始測試溫度(start test)
5 oC、結束測試溫度(end of test) 20 oC、測
試區間 1(test interval-1) 0.5 oC、測試區間
2(test interval-2) 1.0 oC。
水為經過 Milli-Q Plus 純化過，甲醇、
丙酮和 2,2,4-三甲基戊烷為 HPLC/Spectra
等級試藥(Tedia Co. Inc.)、1-丁醇、異丁
醇、1-戊醇和辛烷亦購自於 Tedia Co.
Inc. ， 2- 丁 醇 購 自 於 Fisher Scientific
International Inc.，甲苯購自於 J.T. Baker，
Decane 購自於 Alfa Aesar。

mixtures[4,14,15]. Garland and Malcolm[16] (2002) developed a statistical model to predict the flash point of a single organic acid-water
solutions: acetic acid + propionic acid + butyric acid + water. However, it deviated significantly from the experimental measurements
for multiple organic-water solutions[17].
Overall, application of the former models[3,11,12,16] is limited to solutions that can be assumed as ideal within the composition
range considered. The new models taking into account non-ideality of the solution through liquid phase activity coefficients have to
be used to predict efficiently the flash point of these miscible mixtures[4,5,10,13,17]. Non-ideality of the liquid phase is in particular
responsible to the occurrence of extreme flash-point behavior such as minimum and maximum flash-point behavior[14,15,18]. This is
similar to minimum boiling and maximum-boiling azeotropic behavior in vapor-liquid equilibrium. In the vapor-liquid equilibrium
area, stronger non-ideality may often result to the partial miscibility of the liquid phase, eventually coupled with the occurrence of a
so-called heteroazeotrope. We suspect that similar behaviors happen for flash point. The models for predicting the flash points of
binary partially miscible mixtures of flammable solvents and aqueous-organic system were proposed by Liaw et al.[19,20]. However, to
our knowledge no applicable model has been available for partially miscible mixtures with more than two components. In this
manuscript, a model for the multi-component partially miscible mixtures of flammable solvents was developed and investigated
using the ternary partially miscible solution: methanol + toluene + 2,2,4-trimethylpentane.
Based upon the definition of flash point [2], it is necessary to estimate the vapor-phase composition of flammable substances
from a vapor-liquid equilibrium equation in order to predict their flash point. Furthermore, it is acknowledged that partial miscibility
occurs because of significant interaction within the non-ideal liquid solution. For such solutions, liquid-phase activity coefficients
must be taken into account in the vapor-liquid equilibrium equation by means of thermodynamic models. The original Wilson
thermodynamic model[21] is not applicable for evaluating the liquid-phase activity coefficients for mixtures that exhibit a miscibility
gap[22]. Unlike Wilson’s equation, the NRTL[23] and UNIQUAC thermodynamic models[24] are applicable to both vapor-liquid and
liquid-liquid equilibria[22].
2 Experimental protocol
An HFP 362-Tag Flash Point Analyzer (Walter Herzog GmbH, Germany) was used to measure the flash points for a variety of
mixtures (methanol + toluene + 2,2,4-trimethylpentane) at different compositions. The apparatus incorporates control devices that
program the instrument to heat the sample at a specified heating rate within a temperature range close to the expected flash point. The
flash point is automatically tested using an igniter at specified temperature test intervals. If the expected flash point is lower than or
equal to the change temperature, heating rate-1 is used and the igniter is fired at test interval-1. If the expected flash point is higher,
heating rate-2 is adopted and the igniter is fired at test interval-2. The first flash-point test series is initiated at a temperature
equivalent to the expected flash point minus the start-test value. If the flash point is not determined when the test temperature
exceeds the sum of the expected flash point plus the end-of-test value, the experimental iteration is terminated. The instrument
operation is conducted according to the standard ASTM D56 test protocol[25] using the selected parameters: start test 5 ºC; end of test
20 ºC; heat rate-1 1 ºC/min; heat rate-2 3 ºC/min; change temperature 60 ºC; test interval-1 0.5 ºC; and, test interval-2 1.0 ºC. The
liquid mole fraction is determined from mass measured using a Setra digital balance (EL-410D: sensitivity 0.001 g, maximum load
100 g). A magnetic stirrer provides sufficient agitation for the test samples. The prepared mixtures were stirred for 30 minutes before
2,2,4-trimethylpentane. The prediction results were compared with experimental data. The liquid-phase activity coefficients for these
two mixtures were estimated using the NRTL[23] and/or UNIQUAC equations[24]. The binary interaction parameters of the
NRTL/UNIQUAC equations obtained from the LLE data and VLE data were both used in this study, with parameters adopted from
the literature[7,28-30]. The parameters for relative van der Waals volume (r) and the surface area (q) for the pure components needed for
the UNIQUAC equation and the Antoine coefficients are also sourced from the literatures[28-30].
The flash points for the pure substances used in this study were measured using the Flash Point Analyzer, with these values
compared with their literature-derived analogues [31-39] (Table 1). There are between-source differences in the flash-point data for
methanol, toluene, and 2,2,4-trimethylpentane. Our measurements for methanol are identical to the values reported by Oxford

4.2 Flash-point Variation of Binary Mixtures of Methanol, Toluene and 2,2,4-trimethylpentane
The flash points for the binary mixtures of methanol, toluene, 2,2,4-trimethylpentane covering their entire composition range
were tested. Methanol + toluene and toluene + 2,2,4-trimethylpentane are miscible mixtures in their flash point temperature, however,
methanol + 2,2,4-trimethylpentane is a partially miscible one.
Figure 1 compares the predicted flash points, based upon the binary parameters of binary solutions, with the corresponding
measured values for the miscible mixtures: methanol + toluene and toluene + 2,2,4-trimethylpentane. Predictions are in good
agreement with the experimental data over the entire composition range, when the NRTL or UNIQUAC equation with Gmehling et
al.’s VLE parameters[29,30] is used. However, there are remarkable deviations between the predictions and measurements when based
on UNIQUAC equation with Gramajo de Doz et al.’s LLE parameters[28], with the observed minimum flash point behavior of
methanol + toluene being not predicted and the predicted maximum flash point behavior for toluene + 2,2,4-trimethylpentane being
not observed in the measurements. One reason is that Gramajo de Doz et al.’s parameters were regressed on LLE data. It is common
knowledge in thermodynamic that use of LLE parameter for predicting VLE is usually not satisfactory[40], and the flash point
definition of being “suficient vapor to become a combustible mixture” is related to VLE. The other one is that Gramajo de Doz et
al.’s LLE parameters were assumed to be constants, and regressed on data at 30 oC, which temperature is far from the flash point
range of the solution, 10 to -10 oC. However, the substantial deviation of flash point between the prediction and measurement
indicated that the LLE parameters of the two mixtures are strongly temperature dependent. Another reason may be that Gramajo de
Doz et al.’s parameters are not appropriate. In the parameter value estimation process for the equations used to describe the activity
coefficients, the values of the parameters, which give a global minimum of one objective function, are deemed to be the parameters
2,2,4-trimethylpentane-rich region was then used to calculate the constant flash point in each tie line in this study. Table 2 indicated

4 Conclusions
The immiscible behavior in the two liquid phases should not be ignored in the prediction of flash point for partially miscible
mixtures. The measured flash points are almost kept constant in each tie line. The model for the flash point prediction of ternary
partially miscible mixtures of flammable solvents is able to represent well the experimental data over the entire composition range.
Thus, it appears reasonable to propose that this model is potentially applicable for assessment of fire and explosion hazards in
real-world environments and producing inherently safer designs for chemical processes.
