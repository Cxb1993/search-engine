一、中文摘要 
 
    可調多波長光纖雷射，不僅應用於長距離
高密度分波多工傳輸（DWDM）系統，也被
用來作為一個測試儀器光學元件。多波長光
纖雷射已廣泛使用摻鉺光纖(EDFs)來做研
究，由於 EDFs 獨特的優勢，如高增益、飽
和功率大。目前有不同的方式來實現多波長
產生的各種增益機制，包括利用半導體光放
大器（SOA）[1，2]，摻鉺光纖（EDF）[3-5]，
混合 SOA 和 EDF[6] ，布里淵摻鉺光纖 [7]，
以及拉曼和 EDF[8]。 
如今建立在 EDF 介質上的 300 條多波長
光纖[9]已被刊出，使用 Fabry-Perot etalon 產
生多波長並架構出一個簡單而具成本效益的
環型 EDF 結構也已進行了研究[10]，這些在
訊號雜訊比的表現上，皆小於 40dB。最近，
我們提出了可調式 L-Band 16×200GHz 的多
波長光纖雷射採用串聯半導體光放大器
（SOA）和光切片器(Slicer)，以提供均勻且
可調的多波長操作多達 16 個雷射信號線，
擁有超過 40dB 的訊號雜訊比和 1.6nm 的波
長間隔。

Abstract 
 
    The tunable multi-wavelength fiber 
lasers have not only been seriously considered 
for applications in long-reach dense 
wavelength-division multiplexing transmission 
(DWDM) system, but have also been used as a 
test instrument for optical components. 
Multi-wavelength fiber lasers using EDFs have 
been widely investigated due to unique 
advantages such as high saturation power and 
large gain. However, there are different 
approaches to achieving multi-wavelength 
generation with various gain mechanisms, 
including semiconductor optical amplification 
(SOA) [1, 2], erbium-doped fiber (EDF) [3-5], 
hybrid SOA and EDF [6], Brillouin-erbium 
fiber [7], as well as Raman and EDF [8]. 
Up to now, 300 uniform multi-wavelength fiber 
based on EDF gains [9] have been reported. A 
simple and cost-effective EDF ring laser 
configuration using a Fabry-Perot etalon to 
generated multi-wavelength have been 
investigated[10]. The performances of signal- 
to-noise ratio in these methods are less than 
40dB. Recently, we presented a tunable L-band 
16 × 200GHz multi-wavelength fiber laser by 
using cascaded semiconductor optical 
amplifiers (SOAs) and optical slicer to provide 
uniform and tunable multi-wavelength 
operation of up to 16 laser lines with a 
signal-to-noise ratio over 40 dB and 1.6-nm 
wavelength spacing. 
 
Keyword: SOA, multi-wavelength, signal-to- 
noise ratio, WDM, slicer.

III.  CONCLUSIONS 
A stable multi-wavelength ring fiber laser based on 
cascaded SOAs and optical slicer was propsed and 
experimentally demonstrated. By employing this simple 
scheme and through the proper controlled of the 
polarization controller, we were able to achieve uniform 
multi-wavelength operation up to 16 laser lines with the 
signal-to-noise ratio over 40dB and 1.6-nm wavelength 
spacing at the room temperature. The wavelength can be 
tuned in a range from ~1598 nm to ~1603 nm. Good 
power stability < 0.01 dB over 1598nm-1623nm in 100 
sec measurements at the room temperature have been 
experimentally shown. Such a multi-wavelength-
switchable laser source is a special structure for the 
applications of DWDM devices and systems.

Abstract--A tunable L-band 16 u 200 GHz multi-
wavelength fiber laser using cascaded semiconductor 
optical amplifiers (SOAs) and optical slicer was proposed 
and experimentally demonstrated. By employing this 
simple scheme and being through the proper control of the 
polarization controller, we were able to generate uniform 
multi-wavelength operation up to 16 laser lines with the 
signal-to-noise ratio over 40dB and 1.6-nm wavelength 
spacing at the room temperature. Moreover, the output 
stability of the multiwavelength laser has also been 
discussed and analyzed. 
I.  INTRODUCTION 
The tunable multi-wavelength fiber lasers have not  
been seriously considered to be applied in long-reach 
dense wavelength-division multiplexing transmission 
(DWDM) systems, but have also been used as a test 
instrument with optical components. Multi-wavelength 
fiber lasers using EDFs have been widely investigated 
due to unique advantages such as high saturation power 
and large gain. However, there are different approaches 
to achieving multi-wavelength generation with various 
gain mechanisms, including semiconductor optical 
amplification (SOA)[1-2], erbium-doped fiber (EDF) [3], 
hybrid SOA and EDF [4], Brillouin-erbium fiber [5], as 
well as Raman and EDF [6]. 
Up to now, 300 uniform multi-wavelength fiber based 
on EDF gains have been reported. A simple and cost-
effective EDF ring laser configuration using a Fabry-
Perot etalon to generate multi-wavelength has been 
investigated. Recently, we have presented a tunable L-
band 16 u 200GHz multi-wavelength fiber laser with 
cascaded semiconductor optical amplifiers (SOAs) and 
optical slicer. It was designed to provide uniform multi-
wavelength operation up to 16 laser lines with a signal-
to-noise ratio over 40dB and 1.6-nm wavelength spacing.  
II.  EXPERIMENTAL RESULTS AND DISCUSSION
The schematic of the experimental setup is shown 
in Fig.1. The proposed laser scheme consists of two 
SOAs, a 1×2 and 10/90 optical coupler, a polarization 
controller (P.C.), an isolator, and a slicer. The gain of the 
fiber laser is provided by two semiconductor-optical-
amplifiers (SOA-NL-L1-C-FA) which are produced by 
Kamlelian. The SOA1 and SOA2 respectively provided 
the peak small signal gain of 15.02 dB and 16.69 dB at 
1550 nm. And the polarization-dependent saturated gain 
(PDG) between the transverse electric (TE) and the 
transverse magnetic (TM) components of the SOA is 
about 0.43 dB as the SOA is biased at 400 mA and 
thermally stabilized at 20 oC.

Improvement the Light Extraction Efficiencies 
with Patterned Sapphire Substrates 
by Wet and ICP Etching Method 
Chan-Shou Wu1, Tsair-Chun Liang2, Wei-Chih Cheng, Shun-yuan Huang*, Hon Kuan*
Graduate Institute of Electro-Optical Engineering, National Kaohsiung First University  
of Science and Technology, Kaohsiung , 811, Taiwan, ROC. 
*Department of Electrical Engineering, Southern Taiwan University of Technology, Tainan County 710, Taiwan 
Tel: +886-7-6011000 ext. 2712  Fax: +886-7-6011096

2 E-Mail: tcliang@ccms.nkfust.edu.tw 
Abstract--In this study, sapphire substrates patterned by 
a selective chemical wet and an inductively coupled plasma 
(ICP) etching technique was proposed to improve the 
performance of GaN-based light-emitting diodes (LEDs). 
The light output power of LEDs on ICP-etching patterned 
substrates was better than the LED on wet-etching 
patterned substrates about 17%.
I.  INTRODUCTION 
The low extraction efficiency is primarily due to the 
large difference in the refractive indices between 
III-nitride materials and air so that the light extraction 
efficiency is limited by the internal reflection [1].!To
solve these problems, patterned substrates were 
demonstrated could improved the light extraction. Some 
articles reported the sapphire substrates patterned by dry 
etching with periodic grooves or holes [2,3].   
In this study, we prepared the sapphire substrate with 
columned patterns etched by wet etching and ICP 
etching. For comparison, we fabricated the same 
InGaN/GaN LEDs epitaxial condition on wet and ICP 
etching patterned sapphire substrates. The optoelectronic 
properties of the fabricated LEDs will be discussed. 
II.  EXPERIMENTS 
The sapphire substrates used in this study were 2 in. 
For wet etching, a 300 nm SiO2 was deposited on 
sapphire substrate by plasma enhanced chemical vapor 
deposition (PECVD). The circular photoresist array with 
a 2.5 ȝm diameter and 2.5 ȝm spacing was defined by 
the standard photolithography process on SiO2 film. 
Then SiO2 film was patterned in a buffered oxide etch 
solution using photoresist as a mask. After SiO2
patterning, the photoresist was removed and the SiO2
array was left to serve as the mask for the etching of 
sapphire substrates. The sapphire substrates with SiO2
masks were wet-etched in a mixture of H2SO4 and 
H3PO4 (H2SO4:H3PO4 = 3:1). The SiO2 mask was then 
removed in HF solution. For ICP etching, the photoresist 
array was fabricated directly on sapphire substrate by the 
standard photolithography process. Then the sapphire 
substrate was etched with BCl3/Cl2 in an ICP etcher 
using photoresist array as mask. After etching, the 
photoresist was removed. And then the samples used in 
this study were all grown on PSS by MOCVD. Details 
of the growth procedure could be found elsewhere [4,5]. 
Fig. 1 shows the epitaxial layers and the LED structures 
fabricated on patterned sapphire substrates. Photo- 
luminescence (PL) was measured at room temperature 
using a 325-nm line of a He-Cd laser as an excitation 
source. The morphologies of the wet and ICP LEDs 
surface were observed by a field emission scanning 
electron microscope (FESEM). The current-voltage (I-V)
and light output power measurement was also performed 
at room temperature by an HP4145 semiconductor 
parameter analyzer.

substrate patterned by wet or ICP etching.
