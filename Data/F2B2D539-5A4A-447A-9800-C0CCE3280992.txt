Abstract 
This paper investigates the characteristics of a 
voltage transient response, including a peak 
voltage, a time constant and an oscillation 
frequency, and their applications for a 
Langevin-type piezoelectric vibrator. The 
transient response is induced at the input 
terminals of the vibrator immediately after an 
external impulse force is applied to drive the 
vibrator. Also, the above characteristics can be 
employed to estimate the equivalent resistance, 
the quality factor and the anti-resonant 
frequency of the vibrator without any AC power 
supply. Then, an equivalent circuit of the 
vibrator with an impulse force is used to derive 
the transient response and its characteristics. 
This transient response involves an AC 
component only. Moreover, a driving-and- 
measuring system is constructed to control the 
impulse force and record the transient responses 
induced by the various impulse forces. Finally, 
the effects of the magnitude of the impulse force 
on the peak voltage, time constant and 
oscillation frequency of the transient response 
are measured and discussed. 
Keywords: impulse force, transient response, 
piezoelectric vibrator, peak voltage, 
time constant
