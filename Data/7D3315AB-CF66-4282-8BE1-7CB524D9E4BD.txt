Abstract 
Specific interactions between Lipopolysaccharides (LPS) and cells stimulate the secretion of 
active cytokines by cells, which result in toxic effects such as fever or low blood pressure and may 
even cause sepsis shock with high mortality, therefore there are researches continuing focus on the 
detoxification or neutralization of LPS. This is a two-year project.  In the 1st year, the effect of 
steric hindrance was studied by a mixed SAM system of HS-(CH2)15-CH3 and HS-(CH2)15-COOH.  
The SAM monomer was immobilized on gold substrate through chemical bond, and PEG and 
PMB were in sequence linked to the –COOH terminated SAM surface by EDC/NHS amide 
coupling. The PEG linker was introduced to reduce the steric hindrance effect and to study the 
specific and nonspecific interactions of the modified surfaces with LPS by ellipsometry and by 
SPR spectroscopy. The modified surface is characterized by FTIR, XPS, ellipsometry and contact 
angle measurements.  
 
The results indicated that for a PEG-grafted -CH3/-COOH terminated mixed monolayer, the 
nonspecific adsorption of PMB and LPS on the modified surfaces exhibit a bell-shaped 
distribution with the fraction of the –COOH on the SAM surfaces, i.e. the adsorptions are lower 
for SAM with pure -CH3 or –COOH terminal groups. The surface coverage of grafted PEG 
molecule is about one, suggesting that the long chain PEG molecule might exists in a coiled form 
on the SAM surface.  The nonspecific adsorption of PMB and LPS on a homogeneous –COOH 
SAM-PEG surface are very low and can be neglected.  By further coupling a linker molecule 
does not have substantial effects on the nonspecific adsorption.  From the adsorption isotherm, 
one obtains the equilibrium adsorption constant of LPS at 37˚C on a PMB-modified SAM surface 
which is immobilized at pH=8.4 of 3.63 * 107 M-1. Also, from the dynamic adsorption curve, one 
obtains a value of 3.26*103 M-1s-1 for the adsorption rate constant of LPS-PMB interaction. The 
results are applicable for the design of an extracorporeal LPS-removal device. By introducing PEI 
as an intermediate layer for the immobilization of PMB, the results indicate that PEI molecules 
immobilized under different pH values will result in distinct efficiencies on the adsorption of 
Lipopolysaccharide (LPS) and the efficacy of bactericidal. When PEI is immobilized at higher pH 
value, i.e. pH=10, it’s adsorption with LPS is subject to more influences from the primary amine.  
While, for PEI immobilized at pH values of 7.4 and 4, by introducing PMB will increase the 
surface amount of primary amine and therefore promote the efficiency of LPS adsorption and 
bactericidal ability. The saturated adsorption film thickness of LPS to a PEI or a PEI+PMB 
modified surface is about 0.5-0.6nm.  The bactericidal ability of various PEI modified surfaces is 
well correlated to its efficiency in LPS adsorption.  It is believed to be due to the fact that LPS is 
one of the major components of the outer membrane of Gram-negative bacteria. 
 
Keywords: adsorption, LPS, PEG, surface modification

對於特異性吸附的影響，因而針對表面的非特異
性吸附做探討。 
本實驗將 PEG 分子接枝於 Mix-SAM 表面之
COOH 官能基上，針對 SAM-PEG 表面進行 PMB 及
LPS 之非特異性吸附實驗。實驗發現 SAM-PEG 表

Au-S-(CH2)10[-COOH/-CH3]混合SAM對PMB之非特

PMB-LPS 的動態吸附行為。

估計 kd 為 8.98*10-5 s-1。

計劃成果自評 
本計畫之執行主要穫得如下成果： 
1. 成功建立可量化評估不同修飾表面與 LPS 交
互作用之平台。透過即時表面動態吸附量測系統及
理論吸附模型的建立，未來將可進一步應用作為生
醫材料表面吸附機制基礎理論探討之用。此外，本
系統亦可提供作為材料表面修飾技術開發之快速
篩選平台，對於各種新型材料表面設計之效能，提
供一個有效之檢測工具。 
2. 透過本計畫對於PEG-PMB修飾表面與LPS交互
作用性質的探討，已掌握表面 PEG 鏈段對於 PMB 及
LPS抗非特異性吸附之特性，並對於以 PEG鍵結 PMB
所提供之空間延展性，有所瞭解。此結果初步已可
達到提昇表面對 LPS 專一性吸附的效果。未來應有
應用於生物製劑內毒素移除，或體外血液淨化系統
開發之空間。 
3. PEI-PMB 修飾表面之測試，顯示透過適當改變
基材 PEI 固定化之 pH 值，可有效改變其對 LPS 之
吸附效率。發現提高 PEI 固定化處理之 pH 值，或
進一步引進 PMB 分子固定於 PEI 表面上，均可有效
提高其 LPS 吸附量。實驗結果亦顯示，PEI-PMB 表
面對 Gram negative 之大腸桿菌之抗菌成效趨勢與
LPS 吸附類似，顯示 LPS 吸附試驗系統亦可作為發
展抗菌材料之參考。 
本計畫結果大致符合預定之目標。針對 LPS
之吸附，已初步確認了平面立體障礙效應，及藉

本計畫之執行主要穫得如下成果： 
1. 成功建立可量化評估不同修飾表面與 LPS 交互作用之平台。透過即時表面動態吸附量
測系統及理論吸附模型的建立，未來將可進一步應用作為生醫材料表面吸附機制基礎理論
探討之用。此外，本系統亦可提供作為材料表面修飾技術開發之快速篩選平台，對於各種
新型材料表面設計之效能，提供一個有效之檢測工具。 
2. 透過本計畫對於 PEG-PMB 修飾表面與 LPS 交互作用性質的探討，已掌握表面 PEG 鏈段
對於 PMB 及 LPS 抗非特異性吸附之特性，並對於以 PEG 鍵結 PMB 所提供之空間延展性，有
所瞭解。此結果初步已可達到提昇表面對 LPS 專一性吸附的效果。未來應有應用於生物製
劑內毒素移除，或體外血液淨化系統開發之空間。 
3. PEI-PMB 修飾表面之測試，顯示透過適當改變基材 PEI 固定化之 pH 值，可有效改變其
