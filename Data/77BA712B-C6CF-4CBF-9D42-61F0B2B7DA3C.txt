水解分割製程之酵素表現(3/3)

中文摘要
本 研 究 延 續 前 兩 年 計 畫 由 以
(R,S)-mandelates、(R,S)-tropic acid esters、
(R,S)-α-methoxyphenylacetates為基質擴展
至 兩 相 溶 液 中 進 行
(R,S)-3-hydroxy-3-phenylpropionates 之 酵
素水解分割。首先篩選最佳的反應條件為
水相pH 6、異辛烷為有機相、甲醇為離去
基、45oC，而得到Lipase PS 之立體選擇性
VS/VR = 50.7。進一步藉由共價鍵固定化技
術修飾酵素表面胺基之正電強度，而非表
面酸基之負電強度，達到有效調整活化中
心組胺酸側基之pKa以及改變酵素活性與
立體選擇性目標，且在pH 6 水相環境下可
提高酵素立體選擇性為VS/VR = 53.6。動力
學分析顯示此固定化酵素將降低兩對掌異
構物基質之k2R 與 k2S值。這些結果並進一
步與SNSM-87酯解酵素之分割結果比較。
關 鍵 字 ： 水 解 分 割 ； Lipase PS ；
(R,S)-3-Hydroxy-3-phenylpropionates；共價
固定化
Abstract: Lipase PS from Pseudomonas
cepacia as the best lipase was screened for
the hydrolytic resolution of (R,S)-ethyl
3-hydroxy-3-phenylpropionate in biphasic
media. The optimal reaction conditions of
using isooctane as the organic phase, aqueous
pH 6, and methanol as the leaving moiety
was then selected for improving the
enantioselectivity in terms of VS/VR = 50.7.
By considering the easy enzyme recovery
and reduced influence of background
hydrolysis, Lipase PS covalently
immobilized on Eupergi C 250L but not on
Sepabeads@ EC-HA was further selected for
giving VS/VR = 53.6. Moreover, the kinetic
analysis for each enzyme preparation was
carried out, showing the main effect of
enzyme immobilization on decreasing the
kinetic constants of k2R and k2S. The results
are also compared with those by using
SNSM-87 esterase as the biocatalyst.
Keywords： Hydrolytic resolution; Lipase
PS; (R,S)-3-Hydroxy-3-phenylpropionates;
Covalent immobilization

2.5. Kinetic analysis
Unless specified, a biphasic medium
consisting of 2.83 cm3 buffer (300 mM) and
20 cm3 organic solvent containing a specific
substrate concentration was stirred with a
magnetic stirrer at 400 rpm and 45 oC.
Reaction started when 0.5 cm3 buffer
containing a given amount of free or
immobilized enzyme was added to the
resultant solution. Samples were removed
from the organic phase and injected onto the
HPLC at different time intervals for analysis.
Similar experiments without containing the
enzyme were performed for estimating the
non-enzymatic initial rate V and partition
coefficient for the substrate Kp. By
subtracting V from the measured initial rates,
the enzymatic initial rates VR and Vs were
determined and employed for estimating the
kinetic constants from Eqs. (1)-(2) that were
derived from the Michaelis-Menten kinetics:
k2R(SR)(Et)/KmR
VR =——————————— (1)
1 + (SR)[1/KmR + 1/KmS]
k2S(SS)(Et)/KmS
VS =——————————— (2)
1 + (SS)[1/KmR + 1/KmS]
Notations (Et), (SR) and (SS) denote the
initial concentrations of enzyme, (R)- and
(S)-ester in the aqueous phase, respectively.
The latter two concentrations can be related
to the initial substrate concentration in the
organic phase (SR)org as (SR) = Kp(SR)org/(1 +
KpVaq/Vorg) or (SS) = Kp(SS)org/(1 + KpVaq/Vorg).
One may delete the KmR term in Eq. (2), when
using (S)-ester as the substrate. Moreover, the
kinetic parameters parameters are defined as
k2R** = k2R*KmS*/(KmR* + KmS*), k2S** =
k2S*KmR*/(KmR* + KmS*), KmR** = KmS** =
KmR*KmS*/(KmR* + KmS*), k2i* = k2i/(1 + K3i),
Kmi* = Kmi/(1 + K3i), K3i = (k2i + k-3i)/k3i and
Kmi = (k-1iK3i + k2i)/k1i with i = R and S.
3. Results and discussion
3.1. Effects of enzyme source, leaving alcohol
and solvent
The time-course changes of the substrate
concentration in the organic phase, and hence
in the whole reaction medium such as the
time-course conversions illustrated in Fig. 1,
are employed for estimating the initial
enzymatic rates, as well as the partition
coefficient and non-enzymatic rates without
adding the enzyme. Table 1 demonstrates
effects of the lipase source on specific initial
rates, enantioselectivity in terms of VS/VR or
VR/VS, racemate conversion Xt, and eeS when
employing 1 as the substrate. Lipase PS is
then selected as the best lipase, owing to the
highest enantioselectivity although its
specific activity is inferior to that of NOVO
525F.
Effects of changing the leaving alcohol
moiety on Kp, specific initial rates, VS/VR, Xt,
and ees for Lipase PS are represented in Table
2. With 2 as the reference, an increase of the
hydrophobicity of leaving alcohol moiety of
1 or 3 may decrease Kp and hence the
substrate solubility in the aqueous phase. The
inductive parameter F of the substituent to
methanol might provide a measure of the
relative effect of chain substituent on the
electron density and nucleophilic ability of
the hydroxyl group, and hence the leaving
alcohol ability of the substrate. Yet, no
correlation between the F values (i.e. 0.01,
0.03, 0.11 to 0.13 for the leaving alcohol of
1-4) and VR/(Et) and VS/(Et) was found (Wang
et al, 2008b, 2009b). As an industrial
acceptable E value is at least 20, the methyl
ester 2 giving the highest enantioselectivity
of VS/VR = 32.5 was selected as the best
substrate.
