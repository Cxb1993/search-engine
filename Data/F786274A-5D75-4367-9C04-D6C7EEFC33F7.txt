the ratios of 1:10 and 1:15 (w/v) in the removal efficiencies of Cd (74%), Cu (62%), and 
Pb (51%) by the mixed solution of 0.02 M citric acid and 0.05 M EDTA (1:1, v/v).  
The sequence in the removal efficiency of heavy metals from polluted soils was: 0.1 M 
HCl > mixed solution of 0.1 M HCl and 0.005 M citric acid > mixed solution of 0.02 M 
citric acid and 0.05 M EDTA. This is probably because 0.1 M HCl is a strong acidic 
leaching solution (pH 1.5), while the soil pH was restored to 3.0 after the acid leached 
soil was washed with deionized water three times. The pH of the mixed solution of 0.1 
M HCl and 0.005 M citric acid was 2.5, while the soil pH was 3.8 after mixed solution 
leached soil was washed with deionized water three times. The pH of the mixed solution 
of 0.02 M citric acid and 0.05 M EDTA was 6.5, while the soil pH was 7.0 after mixed 
solution leached soil was washed with deionized water three times. Soil pH is usually 
the best indicator of residual nutrients in soil after acid leaching. The change of soil pH 
was the lowest after leaching with the mixed solution of 0.02 M citric acid and 0.05 M 
EDTA. Consequently, it is beneficial to restore the soil fertility by field engineering 
operation.  Further, there was no significant difference in the removal efficiencies of 
heavy metals with soil to solution ratios of 1:10 and 1:15 (w/v) by mixed solution of 
0.02 M citric acid and 0.05 M EDTA. The soil to solution ratio 1:10 (w/v) is thus cost 
effective. 
 
Key words: Acid leaching, reclamation of soil pollution, 0.1 M HCl, citric acid, EDTA, 
soil fertility
