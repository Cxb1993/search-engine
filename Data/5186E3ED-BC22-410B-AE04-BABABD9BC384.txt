發明人/創作人 鄭慧如、王志逢、林柏村、陳威廷、傅勝利 
技術說明 
一種具有奈米碳管高分子複合材料之軟性層疊物的製備方
法，包含：於一基材上形成一奈米碳管層；於該奈米碳管層上
形成一熱塑性高分子層；將一熱塑性高分子基材覆蓋在該熱塑
性高分子層上並施加熱壓，使該熱塑性高分子基材與該熱塑性
高分子層得以接合；及移除該基材而獲得一軟性層疊物，其具
有一熱塑性高分子基材層，與一連接於其上之奈米碳管高分子
複合材料層。本方法不但可確實獲得所欲的軟性層疊物，更具
有操作簡便、快速、成本低等優點，極適合供產業界運用。 
可利用之產業及 
可開發之產品 
可利用之產業: 化學材料製造業、通訊產業、光電產業、電子產業 
可開發之產品: 防電磁波材料。 
技術特點 
高分子材料具有質輕、價格便宜、加工成型性佳等特性，
已成為日常生活隨處可見之材料。但是，因為高分子材料的強
度及機械性質已不敷運用，且較不適於高溫下使用，在擴展使
用範圍的考量下，業界更發展出多種以高分子材料為主之高分
子複合材料，例如無機材料與高分子材料所構成之複合材料等。 
由於奈米碳管與高分子材料所構成之複合材料具有抗靜
電、電磁遮罩等功效，因而最常被使用。奈米碳管高分子複合
材料的製備方法包含原位聚合(in-situ polymerization)法、溶液蒸
發(solution evaporation)法等。溶液蒸發法是先將奈米碳管分散
至一可溶解高分子之溶劑中，以獲得一分散液，接著再將高分
子加入此分散液中，最後將溶劑蒸發，即獲得複合材料，但是
此方法的缺點在於奈米碳管於有機溶劑中的溶解度不佳，通常
需要額外添加表面活性劑等分散劑。 
在上述各種奈米碳管高分子複合材料的製備方法中，通常
需要經過一連串的收集、純化步驟，且為了使奈米碳管得以均
勻分散，另需運用超音波震盪、添加表面活性劑或以酸性溶液
氧化碳管的表面等處理步驟，但是當使用過多上述處理步驟
時，易導致奈米碳管大量的損耗，而影響最終製得之奈米碳管
高分子複合材料的產率。因此，如果可在減少製備步驟以及時
間下，同時避免奈米碳管的損耗，並得以成功製得奈米碳管高

摘要 
奈米高分子複合材料是結合了奈米材料的特殊性質如強度、硬度、光、電、
磁…等特性，與有機高分子的加工性與低介電性，使其應用大為增加，尤其是
軟性電子。然而奈米材料於高分子基材局部聚集造成複合材料的機械性質降
低，應用大為減少。因此瞭解奈米材料與高分子間的交互作用，進而加以改進
奈米結構材料/高分子複合材料特性乃是當今當務之急。本研究目的在於利用奈
米碳管以改善及增強高分子聚合物之機械強度及物理性質，並透由分子動力學
模擬了解在奈米複合材料在力學與熱學的耦合效應之下，奈米複合材料的動態
特性與行為以建立理論模型，並預測新奈米材料以及奈米複合材料於的力學與
熱學等特性與性質。本計畫利用氣相式連續聚合反應方法製備 CNT/PE 複合材
料，利用四點探針量測複材之片電阻及以拉伸試驗機量測複材拉伸強度與延伸
率，探討含 CNT/PE複料的微結構與其物理、機械性質之間的相關性。由於 PE
成長於奈米碳管上的機制不明，因此應用高速電腦中心提供之分子動力學模
擬，探討直鏈狀 PE 吸附、纏繞於奈米碳管的行為分析。CNT 選擇以固定奈米
碳管曲率與管徑為基準，以鋸齒型(9.0)、扶手椅型(5.5)與螺旋型(7.3)三種不同捲
曲方式的奈米碳管為模型。在不同溫度下，探討 PE的初始位置，對奈米碳管纏
繞的影響。並利用單體數量控制高分子剛性，發現高分子剛性對纏繞有一定的
影響。由模擬結果可知，從模擬動態圖與交互作用能的分析，PE鍊纏繞在奈米
碳管上是有規則性的。其纏繞的形貌以鋸齒型(9.0)效果最佳，其交互作用能最
穩定。而扶手椅型(5.5)碳管的纏繞效果不佳，主要是以吸附的方式，附著在碳
管周圍。

1.前言 
奈米碳管屬於一維的奈米材料，
有相當大的比表面積，並且擁有良好
的機械性質[1-3]。時常被添加並製作
成高分子基複合材料，用於加強高分
子的機械強度[4]，奈米材料具有高表
面積與高表面電位特質，因此奈米材
料的奈米碳管分散性始終不佳，學者
利用化學方法，將奈米碳管的表面經
過改質，讓奈米碳管帶上官能基產生
微量電荷，達到奈米碳管在水溶液或
有機溶液有較好的分散性。最近的研
究證據指出，不同類型的高分子，可
以使奈米碳管溶分散於水溶液與有機
溶劑中[5-9]。相信可以有效的利用物
理吸附與纏繞在奈米碳管的表面，克
服碳管間較強的凡德瓦爾力，而達到
良好的分散性[10]。 
而最近引人關注的是Baskaran等
人利用大量的實驗，利用碳管與高分
子的合成，置入不同的溶劑中，有較
強的傾向溶解於水溶液[11]。而與有機
溶劑有較弱的疏水性，從而得到高分
子纏繞在碳管的假設[12、13]。在實驗
方面，已有高分子吸附碳管的概念產
生。Kang等人利用超音波震盪，有效
的分散單壁奈米碳管於水溶液中，並
讓PPES吸附於單壁奈米碳管上[14]。
Li等人在實驗中，發現到聚乙烯結晶於
多壁奈米碳管周圍[15]，至於模擬方
面，由許多專家投入心血，期望證明
高分子吸附在碳管周圍是有秩序的。
Yang等人利用分子動力學模擬，將帶
有苯基的高分子材料，吸附於奈米碳
管，達到分散奈米碳管的效用[16]。
Maritan等人提出纏繞的構想，此方法
直接說明了具有可繞性的高分子沿著
管狀表面纏繞[17、18]。但此機制卻對
高分子沿著碳管的螺旋方向纏繞的解
釋不明。 
本計畫在實驗部分，利用化學氣相
沉積法成長奈米碳管，並在其上方成
長聚乙烯分子，目的是為了將奈米碳
管有效的分散與使用。過去的實驗方
法，奈米碳管經常先須表面改質，才
能讓奈米碳管有良好的分散，但此法
會造成奈米碳管表層上的破壞與導電
性下降，故我們提出的實驗方法即是
為了讓奈米碳管能在高分子中有良好
的分散性。由於成長聚乙烯分子作用
的時間十分短暫，並且成長上的聚
乙烯分子質量較少，所以儀器較困
難有效地量測。所以我們使用分子
動力學的模擬方式來分析上述問
題。模擬部分著重於研究不同螺旋方
向的奈米碳管，經由聚乙烯吸附與纏
繞，期望聚乙烯會依照碳管的螺旋方
向纏繞；並控制聚乙烯數量，比較聚
乙烯剛體性質的差異，是否會對纏繞
造成影響。

4.結論 
在實驗部分，利用化學氣相沉積
法，成長奈米碳管與聚乙烯後，再轉
印至聚乙烯薄片上。 
本文以分子動力學探討直鏈狀聚
乙烯與奈米碳管的纏繞行為。文中以
管徑和長度相當、三種不同類型的奈
米碳管為對象，並以直鏈狀聚乙烯、
碳管接觸角度與數量為操縱變因，在
系統溫度1K與473K兩種溫度下進行
模擬。並比較不同類型的奈米碳管，
其纏繞的難易程度(如:動態圖、交互作
用能)。 
低溫的狀態下，聚乙烯剛性較
強，活動能力較差，在聚乙烯與碳管
平行排列以及依碳管的螺旋向量放置

Introduction 
Mullite is known to be an advanced high temperature ceramic due to its 
remarkable physical and chemical properties [1-2]. Many methods are available for 
mullite synthesis. Among these developed methods, sol-gel method provides 
maximum homogeneity of alumina and silica species resulting in the formation of 
mullite at relatively lower temperatures compared to the other conventional methods 
[3-5]. To synthesize stoichiometric mullite (3Al2O3-2SiO2), boehmite (AlOOH) and 
tetraethoxysilane (TEOS, Si(OC2H5)4) are often used as precursors for alumina and 
silica, respectively. 
Carbon nanotubes, discovered by Iijima in 1991 [6], with outstanding chemical 
and physical properties have become the potential reinforced materials in various 
technological applications [1]. Many deposition methods [7-11] have been reported on 
the CNT synthesis. The chemical vapor deposition (CVD) process, one very popular 
and low cost method for growing CNTs, adopts transition metal particles such as Fe, 
Co or Ni as the reaction catalyst to reduce the growth temperature. The transition 
metal catalysts were often found to be incorporated into the silica or alumina 
framework substrate [12-14] when TEOS is selected as the precursor for silica 
substrate to synthesize CNTs. A high performance braking materials is often required 
to have good mechanical property, high wear resistance and high tribological 
performance. Our previous work has shown that the CNTs have better tribological 
performance, which stabilized the friction coefficient. Therefore, the paper intend to 
integrate both merits of the mullite which provides rather good mechanical properties 
and high wear resistance and the CNTs which can stabilize the friction coefficient 
using a simple process. Since the boehmite and tetraethoxysilane are often used as 
precursors for mullite, and Fe(NO3)3 and tetraethoxysilane are applied as the catalyst 
in substrate for growing CNT, we mixing them three at once to grow both the mullite 
and CNTs through CVD process at the same time. In this study, we prepared the 
Fe(NO3)3/TEOS/ boehmite catalyst sol-gel with different CVD growing processes to 
generate fabricate different CNT /aluminosilicate powders. The performances of these

Effect of Process Parameters on Tribological Behavior of Carbon Nanotube 
Containing Brake Lining Materials Prepared through Sol-Gel Catalyst 
Dispersion and CVD Process 
 
Kuo-Jung Lee*1, Mao-Hsiang Hsu1, Huy-Zu Cheng1 and Ping-Chuan Chang2 
1I-SHOU University, Department of Materials Science and Engineering, Kaohsiung, 
Taiwan 
2 Department of Electrical Engineering, Kun Shan University, Tainan, Taiwan 
* krlee@isu.edu.tw 
Abstract 
Focus of this study has been placed on the influences of carbon nanotube (CNT) 
on the mechanical and tribological behavior of CNT containing brake lining materials. 
Different concentration of catalyst for growing CNT was dispersed into the 
commercial friction powder by sol-gel method before fed into the CVD furnace with 
different ratio of C2H2/N2 to fabricate the CNT containing friction powder. The CNT 
containing brake lining specimens were fabricated by hot pressing these homemade 
CNT containing friction powders.  
Experimental results indicate that significant enhancement of specimen’s 
tribological and mechanical properties can be obtained when the CNT containing 
friction powders are adopted by sol-gel catalyst dispersion and CVD process. The 
CNT containing brake lining specimens prepared through 2.0 M concentration of 
catalyst and 30% C2H2 show the best tribological and mechanical performance. 
However, too many amorphous carbon clusters derived from higher concentration of 
catalyst and ratio of C2H2 /N2 would cause poor formation of specimens and reduction 
of reinforced efficiency. 
The green specimen which was fabricated by hot pressing pure commercial 
friction powders shows less stable, higher friction coefficient and larger weight loss 
during the wear test. On the contrary, the CNT containing brake lining specimens 
exhibit better tribological properties, lower friction coefficients and lower weight loss. 
Especially for the specimens prepared through 2 M concentration of catalyst and 30% 
C2H2 show the most stable and lowest friction coefficient and weight loss. 
Keywords: CNT, brake lining, friction, wear.

Experimental Procedures 
Catalyst friction powder preparation 
The iron catalysts were dispersed in the silica matrix using sol–gel technique. 
Fe(NO3)3 aqueous solutions with different concentration (0.0 M, 0.5 M, 1.0 M, 1.5 M, 
and 2.0 M) were first mixed with tetraethylorthosilicate (TEOS) dissolved in absolute 
ethanol, then the solution was mixed with the commercial friction powder (20vol% 
BaSO4 and CaCO3) and ultrasonic stirred for 30 mins. After then, the mixture was air 
dried at room temperature for 48 hours and dried stepwise in the oven at 90 oC for 30 
mins and 150 oC for 30 mins to remove excess solvent. 
 
CNT /friction powder growth 
CNT /friction powder growth was undertaken in a simple CVD setup made of a 
tube furnace and gas flow control units. The catalyst friction powder was placed in a 
graphite boat before sending to a horizontal quartz tube reactor. The catalyst friction 
powder was first calcined in the vacuum atmosphere at 450 OC for 5 hours. After then 
the catalyst powder was chemically reduced at 550 OC with a H2/N2 (10/100 sccm) 
atmosphere for 2 hours. The CNTs were finally grown at 750 OC for 30 mins by 
introducing a mixture of C2H2 /N2 into the reactor at different flow ratio of 15/85, 
30/70, 45/55 and 60/40 sccm. After the growing process, the nitrogen was fed into the 
reactor to purge the acetylene from the tube and prevent back flow of air into the tube. 
The furnace was then allowed to cool to room temperature before exposing the CNT 
/friction powder to the air. 
Two modes of process paremeters were designed to fabricate CNT /friction 
powder. One is to adopt different concentration of catalyst (0.0 M, 0.5 M, 1.0 M, 1.5 
M, and 2.0 M) with constant flow ratio of C2H2 /N2 (30/70 sccm). The other mode is 
to adopt constant concentration of catalyst (2.0 M) with different flow ratio of C2H2 
/N2 (15/85, 30/70, 45/55 and 60/40 sccm).

Results and discussion 
Microstructure 
The microstructures of CNT /friction powder are shown in Fig. 2. As indicated 
by arrow signs in Fig. 2(b), many CNTs and amorphous carbon clusters were 
generated at the same time through the CVD process. It is also noted that, many CNTs 
dispersed and entwined with a commercial friction powder particle. This result would 
facilitate CNTs to distribute uniformly in fabricated specimens when these modified 
CNT /friction powders were mixed uniformly. 
 
Open porosity 
    Fig. 3 shows the dependence of open porosity with different process parameters. 
Among all specimens, the green specimen possesses the highest open porosity (12 
vol%). The CNTs containing brake lining specimens showed lower porosity than that 
of green specimen. The A30 specimen with 2.0 M concentration of catalyst and flow 
ratio of C2H2 /N2 = 30/70 shows the lowest open porosity (7.4 vol%). One possible 
explanation for the phenomena is that CNTs of CNT /friction powder could easily fill 
those small pores between friction powders during the formation of specimens. It 
should be noted that the open porosity of CNTs containing brake lining specimens 
decreases with the concentration of catalyst. However, the specimen with higher ratio 
of C2H2 /N2 did not show lower open porosity. The phenomena can be attributed to

the same load (1.2 MPa) and speed (1000 rpm) wear test were compared in Fig. 6. 
Among all CNTs containing brake lining specimens, the A30 (or C2) specimen had 
the lowest weight losses (19 mg). The weight losses decrease further with the 
concentration of catalyst. However, the weight losses increase with the ratio of C2H2

although CNTs could improve tribological performance of brake lining specimens; 
however, larger quantities of amorphous carbon clusters in CNT /friction powder 
produced from high ratio of C2H2 /N2 would cause poor formation of CNTs 
containing brake lining specimens and reduce efficiency of tribological improvement. 
It should be noted that the weight losses of specimens decreases with their flexural 
strength and roughly proportional to their open porosity. It also shows that the weight 
losses of specimens are roughly related to their friction coefficient curves. The lower 
the friction coefficient and the smoother the curve is, the lower the weight loss is.

Conclusion 
1. The CNTs containing brake lining specimens showed lower porosity than that of 
green specimen. The CNT containing brake lining specimens prepared through 2.0 
M catalyst concentration and 30% C2H2 show the lowest open porosity and best 
properties. 
2. CNTs could improve the mechanical and tribological performances of specimens. 
However, too many amorphous carbon clusters derived from high ratio of C2H2 
/N2 or too little CNTs produced from low catalyst concentration would cause poor 
formation and reduce reinforced efficiency. It seems that the well balance in 
proper concentration of catalyst and ratio of C2H2 /N2 could provide the best 
mechanical and tribological performance of CNT containing brake lining 
specimens.
