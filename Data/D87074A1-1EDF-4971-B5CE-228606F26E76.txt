Abstract   
A PEMFC stack made with the 
traditional graphite bipolar plate is heavy, large 
volume, high cost in manufacture etc. In 
addition, the stiffness of the graphite bipolar 
plate is very high so that the large assembly 
force is necessary to reduce the contact 
resistance between bipolar plate and diffusion 
layer. However, it will make the reacting gas 
enter the reacting area hard. The effective 
reaction area will decrease greatly, especially 
in high current density. 
The heterogeneous bipolar plate used in 
this study is composed of carbon fiber and 
ordinary plastics. However, the fiber and the 
plastics are not mixed together as they did in 
the so called carbon composite bipolar plate. In 
this new design, carbon/graphite fiber bunches, 
responsible for conveying electrons between 
the electrodes of neighboring cells in a stack, 
are perpendicular to and penetrate through the 
plastic main plate body or bends slightly so 
that the design can create the better contact 
between the bipolar plate and the diffusion 
layer of the electrode. Therefore, the contact 
resistance between diffusion layer and bipolar 
plate is diminished, and the electrons can 
easily be conducted to or out of the reacting 
area. In addition, because of the diffusion layer 
without being compressed greatly, the fluid 
can easily flow into carbon fibers and diffusion 
layer, and then the reactive region uniformly. 
Therefore, both characteristics can increase the 
effective reactive area of catalytic layer 
markedly. A 4-cell PEMFC stack made with 
this new bipolar plate is built with weight 370 
g and volume 385 cm
3
 without a fan. The total 
power out of the 4-cell stack is about 30 W. 
The average power density is about 160 
mW/cm
2
, but the power density of a single-cell 
can reach a value about 220 mW/cm
2
. The 
specific power and volumetric power densities 
are 81 mW/g and 78 mW/cm
3
, respectively. 
The advantages of this stack compared to 
conventional graphite bipolar plate are light 
weight and higher performance at low inlet 
pressure. 
   
Keywords: carbon fiber bunch, bipolar 
plate, fuel cell.
