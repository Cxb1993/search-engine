Abstract 
 
Conventional polarizers are usually 
straight waveguides in which only the one 
polarization can be guided with extremely 
low loss, while the other one is radiated into 
the substrate. A lot of power of the radiation 
mode exiting at output end results in a low 
polarization extinction ratio (PER). Hence, 
for achieving a high PER, these polarizers are 
usually too long to be integrated in a compact 
chip. 
   Therefore, we propose novel polarizers 
with a multisectional bending waveguide. 
The transmissions of the devices depend on 
the amount of radiation loss at bend corners 
in multisectional bending waveguides for the 
two polarizations. But the two different 
polarized lights can propagate along a 
straight waveguide without radiation loss. In 
the proposed TE-pass and TM-pass 
waveguide polarizers, we utilized low-loss 
polymer waveguides and highly birefringent 
polyimides. They are composed of seven 
coherently coupled corner bends connected 
by straight waveguides of equal length. The 
TE-pass and TM-pass polarizers are 
fabricated to insert birefringent polyimides 
strip onto the buried ribs and both lateral 
claddings of that, respectively, such that the 
transmission functions for the TE and TM 
polarizations are considerably discrepant 
when a bending angle and a thickness of the 
polyimide strip are fixed. The simulated 
PERs are obtained 75 dB/mm and 72.1 
dB/mm for the TE-pass and TM-pass 
polarizer, respectively.          
 
Keywords: Polarizer, Waveguide, 
Polarization extinction ratio, 
Polymer.
