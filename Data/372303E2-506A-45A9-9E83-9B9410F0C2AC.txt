 一、緣由與目的 
1.1 研究背景與動機 
模具產業屬於高度分工的產業，自粗胚加工、放電加工到模具成品都需要專業委外加工服務，因
而委外供應體系的建構，相對重要。受到產品壽命週期縮短與多樣少量趨勢的影響，核心競爭能力的
確認與垂直水平供應鏈管理系統的建立，更是成為獲利的關鍵。為因應中國大陸積極扶持模具產業可
能造成的影響，台灣模具廠商開始思考同業與異業結盟的發展策略，生產系統有下列基本考量： 
1.建立協同開發機制，協助委外廠商參與開發設計或製造生產。 
2.協助產品或零組件廠商進行產品開發。 
3.以同業或異業合作觀念克服產品壽命週期縮短的問題。 
模具產業近二十年來受到工資上漲與大陸將模具列為重點發展產業影響，低價產品發展空間緊
縮，高精密模具研發逐漸成為台灣模具產業的發展方向，部份廠商並且逐漸建立台灣研發，大陸或東
南亞配合產製的供應鏈模式。委外是模具產業因應多樣少量的必要策略，以成本效能觀念建立各種供
應商在不同專業層面的協同合作(collaborative operations)，則是模具廠商必須思考的營運模式。 
委外比例高，顯示核心作業項目少，與供應商決策系統相關的作業活動比例增加，供應商數量增
加是一個以策略性評估擴增容量創造價值的思考(Fawcett 1995)。另外，產品壽命週期縮短導致產品與
技術之變更，供應鏈亦可能因此而變動，生產系統在決定委外可行性時，供應商選擇成為一項關鍵性
議題(Gules and Burgess 1996)。對模具生產製造而言，精實生產(lean production)也是因應產品壽命週期
縮短的另一項考慮，但其成功關鍵因素必須有彈性化生產與完善物流與委外作業系統之設計。其次，
委外無論是何種背景所形成，供應商評估與成本效能是決策形成的依據，將供應商選擇納入生產系統
設計，形成一種供應鏈與供應鏈系統之整合(Fahy 2002)，並具有資源基礎之策略競爭模式(Grant 1991)，
也是模具廠商委外作業的思考因素。 
以實務觀點分析，生產系統在面對此一問題時至少應有下列幾個基本考量： 
1.供應商選擇決策的要項與範圍應加以界定。 
2.區別核心自製項目。 
3.建立委外決策分析架構。 
4.建構評估自製、委外與供應商選擇作業的分析因素。 
5.建立投資效益決策方案與評估分析模式。 
供應商效益評估，須結合生產系統的成本條件，從供應鏈管理系統建立一個包括自製、委外決策
與供應商選擇的整合性成本效能模式，才能實際提供生產決之參考，這也是本研究計畫的主要動機。 
委外類別與型態很多，本計畫首先將委外條件因素歸類，再與自製基礎連結，並進一步與供應商
選擇連結，建構一個啟始成本效能分析模式，然後應用模糊多目標規劃發展成自製、委外與供應商選
擇之整合模式。 
委外生產模式係以建立競爭優勢而設計，但因供應商選擇需考慮因素包括交期、品質、成本、供
應能力，並且各因素間具有互換性(trade-offs)，所以其交互關係分析是一個值得探討的問題。另外，自
製實體設施需要較高的固定成本，且一旦投入就屬變更不易的沉沒成本(sunk cost)，決策時則尚有變動
成本的考量。因產品壽命週期縮短，變動成本估算亦經常面臨不確定的問題。另外，對自製決策而言，
生產數量規模是影響核心作業選擇與委外程度的主要因素。具生產經濟規模的作業項自製，並將經濟
規模不足、品質不夠或成本效益相對較弱的作業項目委外，是基本的考量。經濟規模，取決於需求數
量、成本與頻率，並有長短期與供應體系維持的考量。依自製與委外作業性質，蒐集相關成本資料，
系統化地建立成本分類體系，並納入不確定因素，以構成完整的決策分析模式，是本計畫的基本考量。
因有多目標與不確定因素之考量，本計畫將建構一個模糊多目標決策模式，以符實際應用之需求。 
1.2 研究目的與範圍 
本計畫針對模具委外與供應商決策議題，建立一個模糊多目標規劃模式，以模具生產系統為案例，
研究範圍包括數學規劃的推導與建立、分析模具產業生產特性以及透過案例應用與測試，提供模具相
關產業生產系統設計的參考，研究目的在於： 
1.建構一個具自製、委外決策結合供應商選擇之模具生產最佳化供應鏈決策模式，分析並架構整個模

及資源基礎(resource-based)條件與成本因素的衡量，產生自製與外購決策的問題，以及供應商能力、成
本與關係維持的供應商評估分析。 
2.3 自製與委外決策 
傳統生產系統設計，因產品數量足夠，或製程變更不易，自製比重高。現代生產系統產品變動程
度大，批量式生產導致產能利用不足、存貨偏高、整備時間與頻率增加並且造成生產系統管理困難，
委外成為因應的方式。自製與外購是生產系統長期面對的問題，任何一個企業均會有此一方面程度不
一的考慮(Linder 2004)。Offodile(2002)分析資訊與網路系統對製造策略的影響，尤其全球化趨勢的形
成，生產系統要建立虛擬化製造(virtual manufacturing)的自製與委外決策系統，思考供應鏈系統的經濟
性問題(Long 2005)。Hohli 和 Park (1994)分析多產品買賣程序的協調性，並且發展出一個決策性模式。
另外，Sinha 和 Sarmah (2007)認為委外立基於產能不足，但應注意供應鏈連繫的問題。Arnold (2000)
同樣提出委外與供應鏈有高度相關，其核心就是自製與外購決策問題之看法，並說明三個前提： 
1. 所謂外部(outside)是指可以創造價值的非企業本身擁有的個體，為一種內部組織界限(border)的改
變，或將外部夥伴結合之組織界限無形化(borderless organization)。 
2. 外部並非以其為終點，也就是要從策略觀點思考外部資源。要利用外部作為競爭基礎，供應管理應
具有來源市場分析，以獲得競爭優勢的能力。 
3. 瞭解外部資源目的在於從如何使用與為何觀點，以確保競爭地位，要落實此一觀念，供應鏈管理是
必要性的工作。 
Tayles 和 Drury (2001)分析未來產銷趨勢，認為將零組件與程序委外已經是必要的作法。但此一作
法不僅只有資本支出的影響，尚有下列兩項主要決策性問題： 
1.資本節省可用於其它地方，創造效益的決策分析。 
2.自製決策需考量預留產品變化之空間，但可能會因為資本支出增加而抵消。 
2.4 供應商選擇與評估 
自製與委外是因應產品變動衍生出來的產能規劃問題，供應商則是提供委外決策的依據，供應商
數量以及供應商供應能力、供應品質、供應成本、供應交期、供應穩定性，乃至供應商關係都是重要
考量議題(Maloni and Benton 1997；Mol 2003；Weber 2000)。從生產系統設計角度，有關供應商選擇之
探討，主要為供應商績效評估與供應商選擇程序，本計畫以研究工具相關之數學規劃加以探討。 
針對供應商選擇問題，早期研究以 Wind 和 Robinson(1968)為代表，更早之前，Dickson(1966)提出
要考量(1)符合品質標準的能力；(2)準時輸運產品的能力；(3)績效記錄等三項因素作為評估準則。隨後，
Dempsey(1978)、Willis 和 Huston(1989)、Helper(1991)、Weber et al. (1991)以及 Choi 和 Hartley(1996)均
有過探討，並且認為品質是最重要的績效評估準則。Verma 和 Pullman (1998)有鑑於供應商評估文獻雖
然不少，但從實證角度分析卻極為有限，以管理者實際選擇供應商為主題，提出一個供應商選擇程序。
Shin et al. (2000)則進一步提出以供應鏈管理導向(supply chain orientation)和供應商/購買者績效
(supplier/buyer performance)觀念，並以成本、品質、運輸以及彈性程度作為供應鏈管理系統的準則。
Water 和 Peet (2006)提出一個三階段自製委外之 AHP(analytic hierarchy process)決策模式，決策因素包
括策略目標、績效目標、績效目標的因果關係、績效目標之相關範圍、產能因素與相關範圍之功能決
策以及委外最適供應商關係之決策。 
有鑑於委外決策與供應商選擇的重要性日增，近年來許多研究從不同角度探討供應商選擇的議
題。Ricardo et al. (2007)提出一個運送績效與供應商選擇決策模式，主要考慮因素為存貨管理與前置時
間需求。Lau 和 Lau(1994)以前置時間與價格績效針對兩個供應商的供應與協調，提出一個決策分析模
式。相關研究包括 Lehmann 和 Shaughnessy(1982)提出績效、經濟、整體、適合、合乎規範等五項準則。 
Dale (1987)認為評估準則應包括品質、生產計畫與管制系統的有效性、過去的整體績效歷史、採
購項目及價格。Ellram (1990)指出廠商在選擇供應商時，除考慮成本、品質、交期等量化準則外，亦應
考慮管理相容性、目標一致性、供應商略性等相關性準則。Patton (1996)進一步提出價格、品質、交期、

(Ghodsypour and O＇brien 1989；Sucky 2007；Wang et al. 2004)。為改善此一缺失，多個研究提出 AHP
可用以解決數量與質化評估的問題，認為數量方法應與 AHP 合併使用(Huang and Keskar 2007； Hafeez

利用 AHP 求解供應商選擇之方法，通常需要與 LP 或目標規劃相結合，建立數量與質化評估準則，
大部份相關性研究均假設偏好度(preferences)或期望水準已知。舉例來說，Araz et al. (2007)提出一個模
糊多目標結合 AHP，並具有偏好結構順序法(Preference Ranking Organization Method for Enrichment 
Evaluation, PROMETHEE)的評估系統。Sucky (2007)為解決相互依賴(interdependencies)之缺失，發展一
個可以解決選擇新供應商與從現有供應商轉換至另一供應商之階層化方法。 
2.5.4 模糊多目標規劃 
模糊集理論(fuzzy set theory)是一項廣泛使用於不同評估準則資訊不足之模式建構問題，有助於實
務問題之決策。模糊集理論最早是由 Zadeh (1965)，Bellman and Zadeh (1977)隨後擴展出一個可以解決
含不確定特性(uncertain characteristics)之決策問題。Zimmerman (1978)更進一步彙整多目標規劃之應用
觀念，並且導入利用線性歸屬函數作為表達與整合每個模糊目標，然後轉換為 LP 求解形態，以解決模
糊理論應用的問題。因模糊多目標規劃觀念可以轉換為 LP 求解形態，應用性可因此而擴增。例如，
Slowinski (1986)、Teng and Tzeng (1990)和 and Chiampi(1996)就曾利用模糊多目標規劃觀念，發展出用
水管路系統、運輸計畫與電磁規劃等不同領域之應用求解模式。與 AHP 或多目標規劃研究相較，利用
模糊多目標規劃觀念求解委外問題，尚不多見，Kumar et al. (2004、2006)及 Araz et al. (2007)或可為代
表。 
委外模式一般均會假設目標函數偏好度(the preference of objective functions)、限制式或參數均屬已
知，因而最後求解所得就是決策依據。這類模式設計所含因素或參數，實務上經常存在不確定並且難
以精確估計的情形，模糊多目標規劃可用以解決此一研究缺口。針對委外管理問題，Araz et al. (2007)
和 Kumar et al. (2004, 2006)提出具整合多目標決策模式，求解過程為先決定目標，然後列出合適供應商
之順序。相關研究尚有 Nam (1995)從資訊科技應用角度探討委外問題，提出一個解決競價策略的混合
整數規劃模式。 
綜合而言，有關數學規劃在供應鏈管理的探討雖然已經累積不少，但仍存在下列缺失： 
1. 部份研究雖曾提及委外決策是一個以產能延伸(capacity extension)為基本考量，並形成自製/委外抉
擇，且具多項因素分析之決策問題。然而，針對供應商、生產與銷售及其衍生之各項因素，以往研
究模式並不多見。 
2. 產能規劃是委外決策的關鍵因素，但以往研究大都著重於供應商選擇與供應鏈間之關係，系統化數
學規劃整合模式並不多見。 
3. 彈性是委外優勢的核心，缺乏精確度是在模式建構時必須考量的因素，但在以往研究著重於多目標
或確定性模式的建構，具多目標與不確性考量之合併模式仍然不足。 
4. 以往模式建構考慮因素仍然不足，實務應用不易，例如前置時間、採購數量折扣、延誤與品質等因
素尚未充份呈現。 
5. 以往研究常以單一供應商供應單一物料或零組件為模式建構基礎，實務上多供應商供應多項物料或
零組件的情形，相當普遍。 
6. 多數研究以生產或銷售層面單獨或以其中兩者為考慮，缺乏生產系統建構供應鏈管理系統思考模
式。 
7. 多數模式以成本最佳化為考量，但實務應用相關因素考量仍然不足。 
8. 多數研究以單一目標(single objective)為模式設計架構，惟在供應鏈管理系統經常存在著投資成本、
存貨、交期或品質之互換性(trade-offs)，應以多目標(multi-objective)為模式設計思考方式。 
本計畫針對上述缺失，從供應鏈管理系統規劃觀點，建立一個具成本、品質、交期、存貨與供應
商關係等多重因素考量之多目標規劃模式，並針對關鍵性參數，建構模糊多目標模式(fuzzy multiple 
objective model)與加權應用模式。

Application of Fuzzy Multi-objective Linear Programming with 
Outsourcing Cost-effective Analysis for Mold Manufacturing 
Earl-Juei Wang*  Tai-Sheng Su  Ming Hsien Chang 
Department of Industrial Management  
National Pingtung University of Science and Technology 
Abstract 
Characterized as mother of industry, almost all industrial products require the support of mold industry. Given the development of 
high-tech industry trends toward to limited production, diverse model range, shortening product life cycle, and quick customer 
responsiveness, decisions related to mold manufacturing strategy are tending to be more complex than before in Taiwan. The 
low-end products are already no longer competitive and the high-end products need to establish a new production system pattern. 
The core concept of mold production systems design requires the application of new technologies in the phases of design and 
manufacturing to enhance the production efficiency for quick-responsive satisfying customer requirements. As high-tech 
enterprises internationalize and supply chain network systems to meet global logistics operations broadly expend, Taiwan mold 
firms must re-define their development strategies with manufacturing cost-effectiveness. This paper develops a fuzzy 
multi-objective linear programming model to analyze the cost-effectiveness between capacity planning and suppliers. Factors 
considered in the proposed model are order quantity allocation, manufacturing quantity, punishment costs, and crashing costs. 
Implementation results demonstrate the potentials for the cost effectiveness of capacity planning and outsourcing in practical 
applications for the specific mold manufacturing decisions. 
Key words: mold manufacturing, outsourcing, cost-effectiveness analysis, multi-objective linear programming model. 
1. Introduction 
Since the early 1960’s, Taiwan’s mold products yielded a certain amount in industrial exports for living industry in the past 
and for high-tech industries in recent three decades. The core concept of mold production systems design requires the application 
of new technologies in the phases of design and manufacturing to enhance the production efficiency for quick-responsive 
satisfying customer requirements (Lee et al. 1998; Li et al. 2003). As high-tech enterprises internationalize and supply chain 
network systems to meet global logistics operations broadly expend, Taiwan mold firms must re-define their development 
strategies with manufacturing cost-effectiveness. To develop a cost-effective production system, mold manufacturing firms are 
continually adjusting profitable make/buy ratios, focusing solely on core operations, and considering flexibility by setting order 
quantity on non-core operations for outsourcing firms (Ghosh and Nagi 2005; Pecas et al. 2009). 
Outsourcing strategies are often developed to satisfy demand in a given limited capacity. Capacity expansion and partial 
outsourcing are two strategies for meeting market demand (Wang et al. 2010). Mold production processes consist of various steps 
and involve many prior planning with decision making in different phases. In reality, some factors are still uncertain. Given a 
specified quality level with cost determinants, however, the mold production planner must evaluate available production options 
under the contracted requirements between the manufacturability itself and the capabilities from outsourcing firms. This work 
develops a fuzzy multiple goal model for making outsourcing and capacity expansion decisions with cost-effectiveness while 
considering multiple parts and suppliers. The specific objectives are the following: 
1. to analyze the crucial factors in the mold outsourcing strategy; 
2. to propose a cost-effective model for evaluating the manufacturability between capacity planning in homemade case and 
outsourcing suppliers in buy case; and 
3. to devise a fuzzy multiple goal model incorporating contracted objects. 
 
2. Literature review 
2.1 Outsourcing decisions  
The definition of outsourcing varies according to the perspective [15]. However, a common definition is the external purchase 
of regularly required services and parts that are currently supplied by the firm itself or that most comparable firms provide for 
themselves [9]. Outsourcing is becoming an important strategy and can result in competitive advantage as markets change Yang et 
al. [17]. However, outsourcing decisions are complex because numerous factors must be considered regardless of whether 
qualitative or quantitative approaches are used. 
Outsourcing studies initially focused primarily on strategic considerations whereas they have begun to focus on mathematical 
models during the past two decades. Since factors considered in outsourcing decisions are not limited to order allocation, 
inventory, capacity planning, and vendor selection, some studies have focused on business process outsourcing, qualitative 
systems, and vendor partnership. However, since all firms should apply quantitative approaches when making outsourcing 
decisions, the following review focuses on mathematical programming.

Outsourcing studies initially focused primarily on strategic considerations whereas they have begun to focus on mathematical 
models during the past two decades. Since factors considered in outsourcing decisions are not limited to order allocation, 
inventory, capacity planning, and vendor selection, some studies have focused on business process outsourcing, qualitative 
systems, and vendor partnership. However, since all firms should apply quantitative approaches when making outsourcing 
decisions, the following review focuses on mathematical programming. 
2.2 MAKE/OUTSOURCING DECISION PROBLEM 
Numerous studies have surveyed the literature on outsourcing, and the most recent studies have examined strategic issues [13]. 
This approach was based on lean production and was focused on creating vendor rating systems. However, in reality, most 
companies are constrained by either capacity or flexibility and must assess outsourcing possibilities to satisfy changing demands. 
Tsai and Lai [14] noted that firms often face situations in which market demand exceeds its production capacity. Thus, identifying 
core competencies and outsourcing possibilities is essential. 
Although cost is a key consideration when firms make outsourcing decisions, they must consider different aspects of 
components in different environments. Wind and Robinson [16] proposed linear models for performance measurement based on 
various cost aspects. One reason for outsourcing decisions is that labor is cheaper in less developed countries. From the 
international outsourcing perspective, Long [10] designed a model to explain incomplete outsourcing in the presence of spillover 
and concluded that firms must determine how to balance marginal gain with marginal cost. Swenson [12] further discussed the 
problem of capital intensity for overseas assembly provision and concluded that when responding to cost changes, non-capital 
intensive industries are more capable than high capital-intensive industries. Compared to low-capital-intensity firms, 
high-capital-intensity firms require specific matches, which are different to locate. For contract manufacturers, Kim [7] proposed a 
model for comparing part consignment and turnkey arrangement. The model optimized the proportion of outsourced 
manufacturing to each contract manufacturer and the additional processing needed for semi-finished units upon return from the 
contract manufacturers. 
As globalization has created complex multi-layer, supply chains that enable vendors to extend their reach globally, 
Abdel-Malek et al. [1] developed a framework for assessing the performance of multi-layered supply chains when formulating 
outsourcing strategies. 
2.3 FUZZY MULTIPLE GOAL PROGRAMMING 
Fuzzy set theory is widely used to solve real-world decision making problems when decision making criteria are unclear. 
Fuzzy set theory was first developed by Zadeh [18] and later expanded by Bellman and Zadeh [3] to solve decision problems 
involving uncertainties. Zimmerman [19] further summarized the implementation concept of multiple goal programming and used 
the linear membership function to represent and integrate each fuzzy goal, before converting the problem into a LP computable 
presence. Fuzzy multiple goal programming (FMGP) has various applications. The number of studies that have applied FMGP 
techniques to solve outsourcing problems are relatively few. Two examples are Kumar et al. [8] and Araz et al. [2]. 
Outsourcing models generally assume that preferences regarding objective functions, constraints or parameters are known. 
Thus, decision making occurs in a final decision phase. However, in reality, factors or parameters formulated in the model are 
uncertain and cannot be precisely estimated. Fuzzy goal programming (FGP) provides a solution. Araz et al. [2] further refined 
works by Kumar et al. [8] by developing an integrated multicriteria decision-making methodology for outsourcing management. 
The two phases of the proposed methodology are identifying suitable outsources. Chen and Wang [20] proposed an integrated 
VIKOR approach in a fuzzy environment to solve fuzzy multi-criteria decision- making problems. Vinodh et al. [21] presented a 
fuzzy analytic network process approach to deal with the supplier selection problem in supply chain system.

5. CONCLUSIONS  
This study proposed a FMOLP model to resolve the problem of multiple vendors, multiple components, and multiple time 
periods. The novel feature of the model is its focus on integration of capacity planning and vendor selection regarding factors such 
as finite number of time periods, order allocation, and costs associated with capacity additions, holding, and rework. Satisfaction 
degree is also measured to account for imprecision of the results when they are used for decision making. The model 
systematically analyzes the interaction among multiple goals to provide insight when identifying options. Cost-effective options 
for various options are determined by the interactions between capacity planning and vendor selection and practical 
considerations.
