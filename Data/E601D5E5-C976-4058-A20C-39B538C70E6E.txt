摘要 
 
由於超級電容器具有高功率密度、高電容量與使用壽命長等眾多特點，因此在許多
應用層面都扮演著極為重要的角色，未來的發展更是潛力無窮。 
本研究以工研院材化所提供之電紡聚丙烯腈(PAN)基纖維為原料，利用高溫管型爐
先進行氧化及碳化處理，將 PAN 基纖維初步改質為具有較佳電化學特性的超級電容器
基底材料。接著，我們在經過氧碳化處理後的 PAN 基纖維表面以循環伏安法塗佈 RuO2，
更進一步的提升電極材料的電化學特性。最後，吾人有系統的改變鍍液濃度及沈積圈數
等各種不同的 RuO2 塗佈條件，並透過各式分析方法及量測儀器，以期能夠得到具有最
佳電化學特性之超電容用複合電極。在本研究中，我們所得到具有最佳電化學特性之複
合電極，其塗佈條件為鍍液濃度為[RuCl3] = 0.01 M, [KCl] = 0.1 M, [HCl] = 0.01M 及沈積
圈數為 50 圈，而其比電容量表現在掃瞄速率 10 mV/s 時為 218 mF/cm2，在 1000 mV/s
時降為 145 mF/cm2。由此可知，比電容量在極高的掃瞄速率下仍可保有原來的 67%，具
有極佳之電化學特性。

3.2 超級電容器的種類 
超級電容器因儲電機制之不同可概括分成兩類[2]： 
(1) 電雙層電容(Electric-double-layer Capacitor: EDLC) 
這種機制藉由一外加電壓的施予，造成兩電極間產生電位差，而產生反應系統的正
負極，此時，電解液中的陰陽離子受到靜電力的作用，分別向電性相異的電極方向移動，
在電極表面形成電雙層，如圖一所示。由於電位改變造成電極表面電子能量改變，使得
電雙層的排列也相對地改變，因此形成與電容器完全相同的電荷分離現象。由於這種電
容器在電極與電解質溶液之間並沒有電荷轉移，因此沒有法拉第電流產生，只有靠靜電
力吸引而造成電荷分離，以儲存電能。這種利用電解液中離子進行物理吸附/脫附的機制
稱為電雙層儲電機制。

4.2 分析方法及量測儀器 
4.2.1 場發射掃描式電子顯微鏡 (FE-SEM) 
在表面微結構之分析上，SEM 是一非常重要的工具，藉由接收二次電子的訊號得
知表面形貌，可看出原始奈米纖維之大致的直徑，長度，密度以及均勻性並瞭解 RuO2
塗佈在纖維上之形貌及分佈狀況。本實驗採用場發射式電子顯微鏡(JEOL JSM 6500F, 
FE-SEM)，解析度為 1.5 nm，最大的放大倍率 500K。 
 
4.2.2 穿透式電子顯微鏡 (FE-TEM) 
在材料本體細部微結構之分析上，TEM 是一可得知直接證據的觀測利器，若再佐
以 EDS, NBD 與 EELS 等分析儀器則可得知材料本體之更多資訊，對欲分析之材料有一
更完善之瞭解。其工作原理是藉由熱游離或是強電場發射陰極電子以穿透並和觀測材料
之極薄表面層作用，藉以得知材料之微結構影像及晶格排列等資訊。本實驗採用場發射
式穿透電子顯微鏡(JEOL JEM 2010F, FE-TEM)，加速電壓為 200 kV，點分辨率為 0.23 
nm，線分辨率為 0.1 nm， 最大放大倍率為 1500K。 
 
4.2.3 循環伏安法(Cyclic Voltametry)  
本研究採用三電極量測系統，並採用 1 M H2SO4 溶液為電解液，將複合電極(工作
電極)、參考電極(飽和甘汞電極)及輔助電極(白金板)置入電解液中形成三極式電化學量
測系統(如下圖二所示)，並進行循環伏安法量測並可藉此計算出複合電極之比電容值，
所使用的電化學量測系統為 Princeton Applied Research 之 Parstat 2273 型。

六、結論 
由於金屬氧化物在複合電極中扮演極為重要之角色，故我們改變不同的塗佈金屬氧
化物之參數，以期能夠找出最佳的塗佈參數，而能具有最佳的電化學特性。由上述結果
可看出較為稀薄的鍍液來塗佈金屬氧化物可在奈米纖維之表面塗佈出較為均於且適量
之結果。此外，沈積圈數也是一個重要的變數，若是沈積圈數過多則金屬氧化物反倒無
法均勻分散在奈米纖維之表面，而只會局部累積在某些區域上，造成複合電極之電化學
特性的衰減。在本研究中，所得到具有最佳電化學特性之複合電極其塗佈條件為鍍液濃
度為[RuCl3] = 0.01 M, [KCl] = 0.1 M, [HCl] = 0.01M 及沈積圈數為 50 圈，而其比電容值
表現在掃瞄速率 10 mV/s 時為 218 mF/cm2，在 1000 mV/s 時降為 145 mF/cm2。由此可知，
比電容量在極高的掃瞄速率下仍可保有原來的 67%，在高頻應用時仍具有極佳之電化學
特性。

2.2. Microstructure characterizations
After thermal treatments, the surface morphology and micro-
crystalline structure of the stabilized PAN-CFs, carbonized PAN-CFs,
and activated PAN-CFs were characterized by ﬁeld-emission scanning
electron microscopy (FESEM) and Raman spectroscopy with 632.8 nm
He–Ne laser excitation.
2.3. Electrochemical measurements
The stabilized PAN-CFs, carbonized PAN-CFs and activated PAN-CFs
with exposed size of 1 cm×1 cm were used as working electrode
materials. Before electrochemical measurements, the thermal treated
PAN-CFs samples were immersed in 6 M H2SO4 aqueous solution
maintained at 50 °C for 1 h to remove impurities andmake the surface
of PAN-CFs more hydrophilic. After acidic treatments, the PAN-CFs
were attached to a current collector (Ti plate) and used as a working
electrode. Electrochemical behavior and speciﬁc capacitance of the
PAN-CFs were examined in a three-electrode cyclic voltammetry (CV)
with platinum wire and saturated calomel electrode (SCE) used as
counter and reference electrodes, respectively. And a 1 M H2SO4
aqueous solution was used as electrolyte.
3. Results and discussion
Fig. 1(A) and (B) shows the Raman spectra of PAN-CFs subjected to
the stabilization and carbonization treatments, respectively, with
curves (a) to (c) corresponding to 1 h, 2 h, and 4 h of treatment
durations in sequence. The crystalline nature of PAN-CFs can be
evaluated from the intensity ratio of G-band to D-band peak (i.e. IG/ID)
[20,21]. In both ﬁgures, we could observe IG/ID ratios increase with
increasing treatment durations. When the stabilization treatment
durationwas varied from 1 h, 2 h, to 4 h, the IG/ID ratios increased from
0.62, 0.66, to 0.77, Fig. 1(A). After the carbonization process, the
corresponding IG/ID ratios further increased to 0.82, 0.90, and 0.93,
Fig. 1(B). From these results, we could conclude that the structural
quality of the samples was improved due to the improvement in
crystallization via stabilization treatment. And the higher increments
in IG/ID ratios after carbonization treatment as shown in Fig. 1(B)
indicate that carbonization indeed further improved the structural
quality of PAN-CFs.
Fig. 2 illustrates the effects of the stabilization and carbonization
treatments on the surface morphology of PAN-CFs. The stabilization
process was carried out at 280 °C for duration of 4 h, and a similar
sample under the same stabilized condition was subsequently
subjected to the carbonization process. The average diameters of
pristine PAN-CFs, stabilized PAN-CFs, and carbonized PAN-CFs were
estimated from the low-magniﬁcation SEM images, Fig. 2(A)–(C), to be
approximately 800 nm, 500 nm, and 400 nm, respectively. As it is
indicated, the average diameter of PAN-CFs decreases after thermal
treatments, while the size distribution of PAN-CFs became more
uniform. Moreover, it could be seen from the high-magniﬁcation SEM
images, Fig. 2(D)–(F), the surface roughness of carbon ﬁbers decreased
with thermal treatments. This phenomenon resulted in electric
conductivity enhancement due to the improvement of electron
transportation over a smoother surface of carbon ﬁbers.
The surface morphology of PAN-CFs subjected to four different
activation conditions is presented in Fig. 3. When the activation
duration increased, the surface of activated PAN-CFs got rougher,
attributed possibly by the removal of amorphous carbon from the
surface of carbon ﬁbers by water vapor. We have observed that if PAN-
CFs were placed in a moisture environment for a long period of time,
the ﬁber surface became rough and the pores dispersed on the surface
would increase. Fig. 3 also indicates that the pressure of carrier gas
affected the surface morphology of PAN-CFs. In general, as the
pressure of carrier gas N2 is creased, the surface roughness of PAN-
CFs became more uniformly distributed and more pores are formed.
We postulate that when the pressure of carrier gas is higher, a greater
amount of water molecules are introduced into the processing
environment, causing a more effective etching to the surface of
PAN-CFs. Hence, the locally etched situationwas effectively decreased
and the distribution of pores created via the reaction between the
carbon ﬁbers and water vapor was more uniform.

PAN-CFs, and carbonized PAN-CFs, respectively. The stabilization
treatment was carried out at 280 °C for duration of 4 h, and samples
with the same stabilization process parameters were used as the
starting material of carbonization treatment. The CV measurements
were carried out in a potential window of 0 to 1 V with different
scan rates ranging from 100 to 1000 mV/s. The observed rectangular-

4. Conclusions
In summary, the effects of various thermal treatments including
oxidative stabilization, carbonization, and activation on the morphol-
ogy and electrochemical performances of PAN-CFs were investigated.
In general, the stabilization and carbonization treatments help to
improve the structural quality, size distribution, and surface rough-
ness of PAN-CFs, while their effects on the capacitive performance are
not obvious. By contrast, the activation treatment produces numerous
shallow pores on the surface of PAN-CFs, leading to a further increase
in the speciﬁc surface area of PAN-CFs. As a result, the capacitive
performance of PAN-CFs can be signiﬁcantly enhanced by activation
