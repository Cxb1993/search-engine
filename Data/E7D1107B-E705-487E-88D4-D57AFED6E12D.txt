Abstract 
This project is originally planned to 
work on the continuation of last academic 
year’s project, which is to investigate the 
construction and performance of the LAS 
coding scheme. The first part of this project 
emphasizes to investigate the construction 
and performance of the complementary 
coding scheme for evaluating interference 
elimination for fading channel, multiple 
access, and inter-cell for CDMA technology. 
The second part is intended to construct 
binary quadratic residue codes of block 
length p from manipulation on m-sequences, 
where p is taken from the factorization table 
of 12 −m  into primes, but not a Mersenne 
prime, in the hope of finding dmin for p>151. 
We performed and finished the project by 
concentrating the first part only based on 
reviewers’ guide line, which we figured out 
by the fact that the grant was awarded with 
budget reduction by supporting 2 
master-student-level RA’s and deleting 1 
master-student-level RA and 1 
PhD-student-level RA from the original plan. 
For the first part of this project, we 
investigate on MA code performance for the 
MC-CDMA system, which is a combinatiuon 
of OFDM and CDMA techniques. Like the 
OFDM systems, the MC-CDMA has the 
great advantage in combating inter symbol 
interference (ISI) caused by frequency 
selective fading channels. Although the 
MC-CDMA spreads the bits using orthogonal 
codes to ensure orthogonality, the 
orthogonality may however be destroyed at 
the receiver due to frequency selective fading, 
thus leading to multiaccess interference 
(MAI). The MAI problem cannot be solved 
by increasing the transmit power because 
increasing transmit power for one user will 
also increase the interference for others. To
