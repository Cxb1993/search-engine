三、計劃緣由與目的 
Recently, great efforts have been devoted 
to various designs of compound semiconductor 
high speed devices, including pseudomorphic 
high electron mobility transistors [1-2] 
(HEMTs), InP-based lattice-matched 
heterostructure field-effect transistors [3-4] 
(HFETs), metamorphic high electron mobility 
transistors [5-7], InGaAsSb dilute-channeled 
HEMTs [8], and nitride-based HEMTs [9-10]. 
Due to their large bandgap, high 
two-dimensional electron gas (2DEG) 
concentration, high saturation velocity, and 
superior breakdown characteristics. Owing to 
the intrinsic large bandgap, high 
two-dimensional electron gas (2DEG) 
concentration, high electron saturation velocity, 
and high sustaining electric field properties of 
nitride semiconductors, nitride-based HEMTs 
are suitable for the monolithic microwave 
integrated circuit applications [11-12]. In 
addition, since the device characteristics are 
directly related to gate length, efforts have been 
devoted to gate-length reduction, including 
depositing a Γ-gate upon an undercut of the 
Schottky layer [13], and forming the field plate 
separately after gate evaporation [14] or after 
the insulator deposition [15]. Though the 
formation of the gate electrode directly upon an 
undercut was studied [16] to decrease the 
fringing capacitance, the air gap existing below 
the undercut may easily cause a tear-off to the 
gate electrode during the lift-off process to 
degrade the reliability issues. The unpassivated 
surface leakage may also degrade the device 
operation.  
Therefore, this project has successfully 
investigated a SiN-passiveted Γ-gate 
Al0.27Ga0.73N/GaN HEMT design to achieve 
gate length reduction, surface passivation, and 
formation of a field-plate structure at the same 
time. Besides, temperature-dependent 
characteristics over a wide temperature range 
of 300-450 K for the present SiN-passivated 
Γ-gate HEMTs with respect to different SiN 
layer thicknesses (150, 300, and 450 Å) have 
been physically investigated. A conventional 
device with a gate length of 1.2 µm by using 
identical optical mask upon the same epilayer 
structure is also fabricated for comparison.
