一、前言與研究目的 
近幾年來氧化鋅為一熱門的紫外-藍光光電材料，由於它具有直接發光能隙在 3.37 eV，與極大的
束縛能約 60 meV (大於 GaN 的 21 meV)，此性質使得氧化鋅在室溫下操作依然有良好的穩定性，另外
氧化鋅也具有本質 n 型的高電子移動率(180~850 cm2/vs) ，並且此材料其製作方式也相當完備，例如:
近年來最為流行的水熱法、電化學沉積法 [1]，此方法的優點為低溫低成本不需考慮氧化鋅與基板之間
的晶格常數搭配之問題，因此其應用非常廣可適用於各基版，第一年的研究即以氧化鋅作為基礎，製
作氧化鋅奈米棒/有機材料異質結構的發光元件與薄膜太陽能電池。 
在發光元件的研究中，因為氧化鋅之 p 型材料目前面臨著許多問題，不容易製作且不穩定，而有
機導電材料大多都具有 p 型高傳導電洞的特性 [2]，如此將 n 型氧化鋅奈米棒與 p 型有機材料結合，製
作成無機/有機複合薄膜發光元件，可同時將無機氧化鋅高電子移動率的特性與有機材料之高發光效率
之特性整合在一起，本研究利用氧化鋅表面缺陷來產生綠-黃光波段，並搭配藍光有機材料使其無機/
有機複合薄膜發光元件可發出結合綠-黃光與藍光的白光的頻譜。 
在薄膜太陽能電池的研究中，由於全世界都即將面臨著石油能源危機，因此各國都積極發展替代
性能源，而太陽能是目前最易取得且環保的替代性能源之一，其中又以有機/無機複合薄膜太陽能電池
最具潛力，因為其成本低廉是一般矽晶圓或是三五族材料以磊晶方式製作的元件的十分之ㄧ價格，再
加上有機材料具可繞性，因此大大地延伸其應用範圍，因此在此我們研究以氧化鋅奈米棒作為載子傳
輸通道的有機/氧化鋅奈米棒複合材料的太陽能電池。 
關於第二年的研究，透明軟性薄膜電晶體為近幾年一大熱門的研究主題 [3-8]，因為不管是光電或
電子產業中，透明軟性薄膜電晶體一直為其元件的重要核心，其中氧化鋅是個被各界看好的材料，由
於它具有直接發光能隙在 3.37 eV，與極大的束縛能約 60 meV (大於 GaN 的 21 meV)，此性質使得氧
化鋅在室溫下操作依然有良好的穩定性，另外氧化鋅也具有本質 n 型的高電子移動率(180~850 
cm2/vs)，並且此材料其製作方式也相當完備，例如:近年來最為流行的水熱法、電化學沉積法，此方法
的優點為低溫低成本不需考慮氧化鋅與基板之間的晶格常數搭配之問題，因此其應用非常廣，可適用
於各基版，本研究目的為以氧化鋅作為基礎，製作氧化鋅/有機材料異質結構的光電電子元件。 
在氧化鋅薄膜電晶體的研究中，目前市面上大多數的方法為真空磊晶製作氧化鋅薄膜[9, 10]，若是
以較低成本的溶液法製作氧化鋅薄膜[11-13]，往往無法得到較佳的電晶體電子移動率[14, 15]，這是由
於以水溶液法成長的氧化鋅薄膜其表面缺陷較多並較難控制，本研究利用氧電漿對有機絕緣層進行表
面改質，使得原本疏水性的有機絕緣層轉換成親水性的有機絕緣層[16]，因此能夠讓氧化鋅種子層均勻
地成長在有機絕緣層上[17, 18]，並藉由改變氧化鋅溶液濃度，來控制氧化鋅薄膜的表面缺陷，最後我
們成功地將無機氧化鋅薄膜與有機絕緣層結合在一起製作成透明軟性薄膜電晶體。 
另一方面，在太陽能電池的發展上，在石油價格高漲及石化原料燃燒造成的溫室效應下，隨著能
源議題的發燒，替代性能源有急迫性的需要，太陽能產業持續呈現出快速成長的態勢，其中 III-V 族材
料中的砷化鎵，由於具有高吸收係數、高飽和電子速率及高電子移動率，且其能隙為直接能隙，光電
轉換效率高，使砷化鎵成為相當優異的太陽能電池材料。然而，對於目前結合半導體與高分子之混成

關於第一年的研究，在發光元件的實驗中，我們以旋轉塗佈的方式將藍光有機材料 PF 塗佈於 ITO

小時的條件退火，為了將樣品上殘留的有機溶液烤乾，之後再以水熱法的方式將氧化鋅奈米棒陣列成

ABSTRACT 
 
Silicon nanowire (SiNW)/Poly(3,4-
ethylenedioxythiophene):poly(styrenesulfonate) 
(PEDOT:PSS) solar cells were fabricated by solution 
process.  The effect of nanowire length to cell 
performance was investigated for the nanowire length 
varying from 0.73 μm to 5.59 μm.  The nanowire length 
was found to have negative effect on the power 
conversion efficiency (PCE), in the condition of the fixed 
thickness of PEDOT:PSS.  The highest PCE of 7.02% was 
obtained for the wire length of 0.73 μm. 
 
INTRODUCTION 
 
Silicon nanowires (SiNWs) have caught lots of attention in 
the field of solar cells because of the property of low 
reflectivity, which provide a chance to combine both anti-
reflection layer and active layer in a material [1-3].  
However, most of cells designed in thorough SiNWs need 
high cost and high temperature facilities and procedures, 
such as diffusion, epitaxy, deposition and dry etching, but 
obtain low efficiencies [1-3].  Therefore we turn our 
attention on the organic/inorganic structure to get low cost 
and room temperature process.  In this work, we choose 
PEDOT:PSS, and SiNWs to be a core-sheath active layer. 
PEDOT:PSS is a hole transport layer (HTL) here because 
of its highest occupied molecular orbital (HOMO) energy 
of ~5.1 eV which is near the valence band energy of 
SiNWs.  The core-sheath structure make PEDOT:PSS 
passivate the surface defects of SiNWs to prevent the 
carriers from being trapped in the SiNWs surface.  SiNWs 
array enlarge the junction surface area to produce higher 
current density [4], and make low reflectance in the range 
of solar spectrum [5].  These make SiNWs trap more 
photons than planar Si solar cells [6].  Our investigation is 
centered on the effect of length of SiNWs to the 
performance of solar cells and the performance 
improvement by the SiNWs.  The result reveals that 
shorter nanowires induce higher current density (Jsc) and 
PCE though the higher nanowires have lower reflectance.  
The PCE is improved by SiNWs about 3.3 times from 
2.12% to 7.02%. 
 
EXPERIMENT 
 
Silicon nanowires were prepared by metal-assisted 
chemical etching process.  Phosphor-doped 1-10 ohm-cm 
Si (100) wafers immersed into AgNO3 and HF solution at 
room temperature.  Ag ions reduced to Ag atoms and 
etched down with HF vertically with respect to the surface 
(100).  Here we change time for etching from 0 to 20 
minutes for each 5 minutes to obtain different length of 
nanowires.  The native oxide had been removed for the 
device of planar Si/PEDOT:PSS solar cells, which are 
going to be a reference.  After synthesizing, the array of 
SiNWs were soaked into concentrated nitric acid to 
remove residual Ag, and finally immersed into diluted HF 
to remove SiO2.  Afterwards, evaporate Ti and Ag on the 
reversed side of nanowires to be the negative contact.  On 
the other hand, spin-coat PEDOT:PSS on ITO coated 
glass substrate to form a thin film; then attach SiNWs on 
the films of PEDOT:PSS to form a core-sheath 
PEDOT:PSS/SiNW junction (see Fig. 1). 
 
The current density-to-voltage (J-V) curve were measured 
by Keithley 2400 electrometer in air at condition of both 
dark and 1 sun air mass 1.5 (AM 1.5) 100 mW/cm
2
, 
simulated by solar simulator SUN 2000, Abet 
Technologies, Inc.  The light falls normally through glass 
and ITO on the PEDOT:PSS/SiNW heterojunction 
structure.

ABSTRACT 
Many treatments such as thermal annealing, slow 
drying and adding additives had been promoted to 
increase the efficiency of polymer solar cells based on 
P3HT:PCBM blend system. However, the methods 
mentioned above are hard to control polymer material 
order. The method of using horizontally external electric 
field on polymer thin films is not only arranging polymer 
materials regularly, but also improving morphology of 
P3HT:PCBM thin films. Therefore, the device applied with 
electric field has better performance which power 
conversion efficiency (PCE) raises from 3.04% t0 3.46%. 
 
INTRODUCTION 
The polymer solar cell has become the focus of the 
energy industry due to promoting renewable devices. The 
most common and efficient material system for polymer 
solar cell is bulk heterojunction (BHJ) based on Poly(3- 
hexylthiophene)(P3HT) and [6,6]-phenyl-C61-butyric acid 
methyl ester (PCBM) [1]. There are lots of groups working 
on this material system and some of them have improved 
the power conversion efficiency (PCE) via many ways. 
Thermal annealing process is usually adopted to producing 
P3HT:PCBM thin film solar cells in order to reorganize the 
P3HT crystals and optimize BHJ morphology[2]. 
Unfortunately, the long period of annealing process incurs 
the P3HT to flow and the PCBM to aggregate [3], which 
reduces both the lifetime and PCE of the cells. In order to 
avoid inhomogeneous crystallization area and serious 
phase separation caused by annealing, some researches 
use slow drying and adding additives method 
[4][5]. Furthermore, some papers also mentioned that by 
applying external bias voltage on samples establishment of 
a large electric field (about 10
7
 V/m) [6][7], the polymer 
long chain orientation can be adjusted and then results in 
improving the low carrier mobility, which is caused by the 
insufficient diffusion distance of excitons. 
 
Instead of conducting a huge bias voltage directly on 
the electrodes of the samples, we apply an electric field 
outside of the samples to build a small laterally external 
electric field (about 10000 V/m) called “horizontally 
external electric field” in this paper. By carrying out this 
method, we gain the benefit to avoid damaging the 
electrodes of the device. This treatment enhances the PCE 
from 3.04% to 3.46% and improves the short circuit current 
density (JSC) from 9.135 mA/cm
2
 to 9.554 mA/cm
2
. In order 
to understand the changes of the polymer thin film treated 
with the electric field, we analyze the dipole moment 
direction of polymers and use AFM images to assist our 
study in morphology of thin films. In conclusion, applying 
horizontally external electric field (about 10000 V/m) to the 
process of arranging P3HT:PCBM thin film makes it 
regular and realizes the ideal BHJ, which enhances carrier 
mobility. On the other hand, electric field treatment can 
also affect the RMS roughness of an active layer, which 
increases contact surface between an active layer and an 
electrode.

acetone, and IPA. The ZnO sol-gel was then deposited on

undergoing an electric field treatment was also prepared 
as a control group. Subsequently, NiO nanoparticles were 
spun on to create an electron blocking layer (EBL) [8]. 
Finally, 250 nm-thick Ag cathode was deposited by 
thermally evaporation at a pressure of 3×10

MEASUREMENT RESULTS 
 
Fig. 3 shows the J-V characteristics of the device with and 
without NiO:WO3 interlayer under air mass 1.5 global 
(AM1.5G) irradiation of 100 mW/cm2 and the parameters 
are list in Table 1. From the experimental results, we can 
find that the Rsh of the PSCs with NiO:WO3 is improved 
from 437Ω/cm2 to 643Ω/cm2 relative to the PSCs without 
NiO interlayer. Moreover, the Voc is improved from 0.54V 
to 0.56V, the FF is increased from 59.8% to 63.7%, and 
the efficiency is promoted from 3.2% to 4.1%. These 
results verify the theoretical inference.  
From Table I, the improvements obtained by the 
mixed oxides are greater than those without oxides, 
showing that the mixed oxides better benefit photovoltaic 
performance. The high RSH indicates less leakage current 
across the cell and contributes to the improved FF and VOC. 
Because the conduction band minimum (CBM) of NiO (1.8 
eV) is higher than the lowest unoccupied molecular orbital 
level of PCBM (3.8 eV), NiO can block the reverse electron 
flow from PCBM to Ag and thereby suppress the leakage 
current. In addition, the decreased RS indicates that the 
mixed oxides are beneficial for the hole transportation. The 
hole transportation from P3HT to Ag may result from two 
possible reasons. One is the hole extraction from the 
highest occupied molecular orbital (HOMO) level of P3HT 
through the valence band of NiO (5.0eV) and then into Ag 
(∼4.9 eV, due to its oxidation). The other is the electron 
extraction from Ag through the CBM of WO3 (6.3 eV) and 
then into the HOMO of P3HT, a right reverse mechanism 
in organic light-emitting diodes with WO3. The two oxides 
improve the device performance based on different 
mechanisms, revealing that they are complementary.

 INVERTED LOW-BANDGAP POLYMER SOLAR CELLS WITH LONG-TERM STABILITY

ABSTRACT 
 
Stable polymer solar cells based on an intrinsically air-
stable conducting polymer, a-PTPTBT, in inverted 
structure are investigated.  After surface modification of 
ITO for the subsequent film deposition, the device shows a 
improvement of power conversion efficiency from 2.45% to 
3.31%, and exhibits very high stability. The device retains 
over 95% of its maximum efficiency even after 1,500 hours 
of storage in air.  All the layers between the electrodes in 
the device were processed with solution, which could 
greatly reduce the production cost and simplifies the 
fabrication process.  This strategy provides a possible 
route to achieve commercially applicable organic solar 
cells. 
 
INTRODUCTION 
 
Polymer solar cells have attracted much attention due to 
the possibility of achieving flexible, large-area and light-
weight devices with low fabrication cost [1, 2].  The most 
typical device structure of the bulk-heterojunction polymer 
solar cells employs an aluminum cathode and a poly(3,4-
ethylenedioxythiophene):poly(styrenesulfonate) (PEDOT: 
PSS)-modified ITO anode. However, solar cells fabricated 
according to this conventional configuration suffer from 
several degradation problems.  First, the low work function 
metal Al is easily oxidized in air [3].  Second, the acidic 
nature of PEDOT:PSS would degrade the device 
performance due to the corrosion to indium tin oxide (ITO) 
[4].  In addition, phase separation of donor and acceptor 
materials in the film evolution could lead to unfavorable 
morphology in this configuration [5].  An approach to solve 
these problems is to use the inverted structure of polymer 
solar cells by modifying the ITO electrode by an electron 
selecting layer and replacing the easily oxidized low work 
function metal electrode by a high work function one to 
change the nature of carrier transport.  Such inverted-type 
polymer solar cells have been widely reported to exhibit 
better device stability [6-8]. 
 
Besides the strategy of adopting a stable device structure, 
there exist inherently air-stable conducting polymers.  
These polymers have their HOMO level below the 
threshold for oxidation of air, which is about -5.27 eV 
relative to vacuum level [9], and can be stored in air 
without being oxidized.  In this work, we employ a novel 
low-bandgap, air-stable conducting polymer, a-
PTPTBT[10, 11].  This polymer material combined with the 
inverted device structure can yield devices exhibiting very 
long-term stability with acceptable power conversion 
efficiency (PCE). 
 
EXPERIMENTAL 
 
Fabrication procedure of the device is based majorly on 
solution process.  All layers between the electrodes in the 
cell were formed by application of suitable solutions or 
precursors of those materials.  The cross-sectional 
diagram of device structure of this work is shown in Fig. 1.  
ITO coated glass was pre-cleaned upon ultra-sonication 
with detergent, acetone and  isopropyl alcohol, and then 
treated with air-plasma for further cleaning and surface 
modification. The ZnO thin film layer was formed by spin-
casting a Zn(CH3COO)2 sol-gel solution onto to glass 
substrate and subsequently baking the substrate in the 
oven at 200℃  for 30 minutes.  Then the device is 
transferred into the grove box with nitrogen atmosphere.  
Subsequently, a blend solution containing a-PTPTBT and 
PC70BM with total concentration 35mg/ml was spin-coated 
on top of the ZnO thin film at 1000rpm.  The film was slow-
dried in a Petri dish to derive a slightly better morphology.  
After drying, a colloidal suspension of NiO nanoparticles 
(0.1 mg/ml), which served as the electron-blocking layer, 
was spin-coated onto the active layer at 2000rpm.  Finally, 
the silver electrode (~250 nm) was deposited on the top by 
thermal evaporation in a vacuum of 2×10-6 torr.  The 
devices were unencapsulated and stored in air.  Current 
density-voltage characteristics were measured with a 
Keithley 2400 source measurement unit under the solar 
illumination with the intensity of 100 mW/cm2.

CONCLUSION 
 
In summary, we have successfully improved the device 
performance by the formation good-quality ZnO electron 
selecting layer.  Air-plasma treatment of ITO-coated glass 
can make the sol-gel solution more compatible with ITO, 
thereby helping formation of a uniform ZnO film.  The 
photoactive blend a-PTPTBT/PC70BM based on this 
system exhibits a satisfactory PCE of 3.31% with very high 
long-term stability.  Hence, the combination of inverted 
device structure and the air-stable conducting polymers 
provides a strategy of achieving commercially practicable 
organic solar cells.

RESULT AND DISCUSSION 
 
The external electric field is used to control the 
morphology of the active layer. Two values of electric field 
were applied: 500V/cm and 1000V/cm. The surface 
variation was observed on the surface roughness, and the 
surface variation also influence the short circuit current 
density (Jsc), series resistance (Rs), fill factor (FF), and 
power conversion efficiency (PCE) of the solar cells. The 
J-V characteristics of the sample in different electric field 
treatment are shown in Table 1 and the J-V curve of 
devices are shown in figure 2. The device without electric 
field treatment has open circuit voltage (Voc) = 0.56V, 
short circuit current density (Jsc) =8.97 mA/cm2. The 
corresponding PCE is 3.02%. The sample with electric 
field treatment 1000V/cm has open voltage (Voc) = 0.56V, 
short circuit current density (Jsc) = 9.70 mA/cm2. The 
corresponding PCE is 3.35%.

CONCLUSION 
 
In summary, the device shunt resistance is increased from 
521Ω-cm2 to 813Ω-cm2, the current density is improved 
from 8.90 mA/cm2 to 9.70 mA/cm2, and power conversion 
efficiency is increased from 3.02% to 3.35% by electric 
field treatment on the polymer thin film surface. In the 
experiment, the electric field is applied externally just after 
the active layer is deposited, while the active layer solution 
is still in liquid sate, so the molecules are less constrained 
in motion. Therefore, only relatively small electric field is 
sufficient to have the same effect.
