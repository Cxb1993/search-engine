Abstract
One a-Si:H p-i-n solar cell can only
provide open-circuit voltage Voc~0.7V. To
apply in electronic paper, the Voc should be
improved. In this report, to achieve high open-
circuit voltage, the tandem a-Si:H p-i-n solar
cell is implemented to increase the Voc. Current
mismatch and junction resistance are the two
major issues of tandem solar cell which can be
eliminated by further adjustment. After using
the theoretical calculated film thicknesses for
individual solar cell and an improvement of the
process parameters, the current mismatch
problem is eliminated. The junction resistance
was also reduced with increasing doping
concentration and thickness. After these
adjustments, the open-circuit voltage of the
tandem solar cell could be increased to 1.51 V.
Then, in order to get higher Voc, the triple-
junction a-Si:H p-i-n solar cell is fabricated,
and the Voc of the triple-junction solar cell is
raised to 2.06 V.

array backplane for Sipix Microcup EPD with
the a-Si:H as active layer. The 1T1C design
will be used to cost down for the LCD foundry
to involve this market. We also consider the
electrical characteristics with mechanical strain
for applications; therefore, the design to sustain
mechanical bending will also be taken in this
array backplane and the degradation
mechanism of the electrical characteristics will
be discussed.

Abstract
The objective of this sub-project is to
develop an image processing and auto-
correcting system for electrophoretic display
(EPD). Our goal is to improve the image
quality of gray-level EPD. In addition, we will
propose a complete and feasible image
processing procedure according to the existing
SiPix EPD products, the theory of
chromatology, and the image processing
technologies. For the time being, we focus our
research on the simulation and the testing of the
dithering algorithms. In addition, we compare
the differences between the results processed
by the proposed algorithms and those exported
directly from the hardware so that we can find
out the fittest algorithm for the EPD.
