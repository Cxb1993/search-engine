摘要 
 
本研究以電漿電弧做為加熱源來蒸發鎢靶材，並藉由氣凝合成機制以及一套吹氣
裝置製備氧化鎢奈米材料，並進ㄧ步探討其場發射特性。實驗過程中，使用氬氣
(Ar)添加 10%的氫氣(H2)作為電漿氣體，控制氧分壓(O2/Ar)，並在電流 80A 以及
腔體壓力 600 torr 下蒸發鎢靶材。藉由 SEM、XRD 與 TEM 結果可得知，成功製
備出 WO3 氧化鎢奈米顆粒、W5O14 奈米棒和 W18O49 奈米棒。其場發射量測結
果顯示：WO3 奈米顆粒、W5O14 奈米棒和 W18O49 奈米棒的場發射起始電場分別
為 4.76 V/µm、3.55 V/µm和 3.54 V/µm。本研究使用常壓低溫電漿設備針對W18O49
奈米棒進行表面處理，其起始電場約為 3.27 V/μm，相較於未處理之起始電場可
降低 7.6 %。 
 
關鍵字：氣凝合成法、氧化鎢奈米材料、電漿表面處理、場發射特性 
 
ABSTRACT 
 
In the present study, plasma arc as a heat source to evaporate tungsten target, tungsten 
oxide nanomaterial could be obtained by gas condensation mechanism with a set of 
blowing gas. Further investigations on the field emission property of tungsten oxide 
nanomaterial were carried out. Plasma gas including the hydrogen（10 %）was added 
in argon gas, oxygen partial pressure (O2/Ar) and used to evaporate tungsten bulk at 
current of 80 A and chamber pressure of 600 torr. The results of SEM, XRD and TEM 
analysis show that WO3 nanoparticles, W5O14 nanorods and W18O49 nanorods can be 
prepared successfully, respectively. The field emission research shows that the turn-on 
field is WO3 nanoarticles 4.76 V/µm, W5O14 nanorods 3.55 V/µm and W18O49 
nanorods 3.54 V/µm. However, the turn-on field of pre-treatment by surface 
modification of plasma is 3.27 V/µm on W18O49 nanorods, compared to untreated 
initial electric field can reduce 7.6%.  
 
Keywords：Gas Condensation Technique、Tungsten Oxide Nanomaterial、Surface 
Modification of Plasma、Field Emission

1. Introduction 
For media storage related application, the demands for a high reflective metallic thin films is 
an important issue. Among these metallic thin films, noble metals such as gold [1], silver [2], etc. 
have been used. For instance, silver thin film exhibits the highest reflectivity and the lowest 
polarization splitting for the full infrared region and down to a wavelength of 400 nm [3]. However, 
Ag thin films experience the relatively poor environmental resistance and a decrease of reflectivity 
of the Ag thin films can be noticed [4]. In addition, the elevated temperature during usage may be 
attributed to the decrease in reflectivity.  
Nowadays for metallic thin films to be used in electronic and optic applications, cost is always 
an important issue. Noble metallic thin films are prepared by sputtering commercially available 
targets. Limited researches have been devoted to preparing targets, depositing metallic thin films, 
and evaluating its performance. Copper thin films have been widely used for interconnection in 
integrated circuits [5]. Copper is much cheaper than those of the noble metals and is a potential 
candidate to be used as a reflectance layer. However, pure copper film has limited oxidation 
resistance and suffers a significant decrease in its brightness. Doping various elements to improve 
its oxidation resistance is necessary. For instances, aluminum and titanium [6] can form a thin oxide 
layer to improve the oxidation and corrosion resistance. Titanium and zirconium [6] can refine the 
grain size within the materials. In this study, we will prepare selected copper alloy targets (Cu-Al-Zr 
and Cu-Ti-Zr), deposit corresponding thin films, and investigate their reflective performance. In 
addition, the as-deposited copper alloy thin films will experience two simulated working conditions 
(an elevated temperature and weather durability) and the degradation in reflectance of these films 
will be investigated.

Vacuum arc remelting (VAR) process was used to prepare copper alloy targets with various 
compositions including Cu-Al-Zr and Cu-Ti-Zr. The compositions for the targets prepared in this

copper alloy thin films exhibited a fine grain microstructure which is important for optical 
applications.

After experiencing the simulated working conditions, a decrease in reflective performance can 
be expected. Again it should be recalled that no significant variations can be observed for 80 oC 
heat-treated copper alloy thin films. Apparent decreases in reflectance can be observed after heat 
treating at 150 oC for 10 minutes or under a weather durability test (50 oC and 85% relative

refine the grain size, while addition of titanium into copper can increase the weathering resistance. 
4. Conclusions 
In this study, Cu-Al-Zr (CAZ) and Cu-Ti-Zr (CTZ) copper alloy targets were prepared. 
Though precipitates can be observed to segregate along the grain boundaries, copper alloy thin 
films were deposited successfully by DC sputtering. Doping Al-Zr or Ti-Zr can refine the grain size 
and oxidation resistance of the thin films. All the copper alloy thin films exhibited a surface 
roughness less than 1 nm. The reflective performance of CTZ thin films was better than their CAZ 
counterparts. The reflectance of CTZ films was ~70% and was less than 40% for CAZ films. No 
observable differences can be revealed after heat treating at 80 oC, but grain coarsening can be 
noticed at 150 oC. Only the CTZ1 film exhibited a reflectance close to 50%, while that of the other 
films decreased to less than 40%. The CTZ thin films also exhibited good weather durability, only a 
10% decrease in reflectance was observed. Among the copper alloy thin films investigated in the 
present study, copper alloy thin films with 1.5% titanium and 0.2% zirconium additions (i.e. CTZ1 
film) exhibited the best reflective performance. It is suggested that doping zirconium can refine the
