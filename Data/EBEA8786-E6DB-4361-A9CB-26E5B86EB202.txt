INTRODUCTION 
一般我們將大氣懸浮微粒依粒徑範圍分為可進入上、下呼吸道的粗粒徑微粒
（coarse particles; PM10, particles with diameters of 10 micrometers or less）、可進入
肺泡的細粒徑微粒（fine particles; PM2.5, particles with diameters of 2.5 micrometers 
or less）與次微米微粒（submicron particles; PM1, particles with diameters of 1.0 
micrometers or less）。微粒暴露造成呼吸及心肺循環系統的不良健康影響已在許
多研究中被證實。而即使PM10之 24 小時平均濃度未超過 115 μg/m3（低於本國之
空氣品質標準），相關研究仍指出會有急性呼吸道症狀、影響肺功能及增加氣喘
住院率等影響[1, 2]。此外，微粒之短期暴露會增加呼吸道症狀發生率及發作時
間、增加呼吸道疾病住院率、及氣喘現象惡化[3, 4]等，而長期暴露則會影響肺
功能[1, 5]、產生氣喘[6]、慢性阻塞性肺部疾病[7]、心血管疾病[7-10]，甚至罹患
肺癌[7, 9]、降低平均壽命[11]及提高特定疾病造成之死亡率[1, 12]。 
在心跳速率變異性（heart rate variability; HRV）與微粒空氣污染物相關之研
究方面，Liao等人的研究中收集了馬里蘭州退休者之家共 26 位年齡大於 65 歲的
自願者，量測室內外PM2.5 24 小時平均濃度與HRV，在調整了年齡、性別、心血
管健康狀況等因子後發現，高污染日(PM2.5 > 15 μg/m3)相對於低污染日（PM2.5 < 
15 μg/m3），高頻率（high frequency, HF）勝算比為 3.08。已有心血管疾病的個案，
其HF、低頻率（low frequency, LF）、5 分鐘平均RR間期標準差（standard deviation 
of normal to normal interval, SDNN）減少與PM2.5上升更有顯著的相關[13]。Pope 
III等人於美國猶他州峽谷收集了 6 位 65 歲以上及 1 位 23 歲患有心血管疾病、高
血壓、糖尿病等的患者配戴心電圖紀錄其心跳與HRV，在與當日，前一日由監測
站所得的污染物資料進行統計分析後發現PM10可能經由自主神經系統影響平均
心跳速率及HRV造成平均心跳的增加、SDNN下降與方均根RR間期標準差（root 
mean square standard deviation of NN interval, r-MSSD）的增加[14]。Gold等人於
美國波士頓收集了當地 21名居民及距離居住場所 6公里外監測站PM2.5與PM10的
資料，並測量每個人心跳速率及HRV。研究發現當受測者於緩慢呼吸的狀態下，
PM2.5 4 小時平均濃度內四分位距（interquartile）每增加 14.3 μg/m3均導致r-MSSD 
6.1 ms的減少 2[15]。Magari等人收集了美國 39 位男性鍋爐製造工人與一位管線
配裝工人並令其配戴心電圖以及進行個人PM2.5質量濃度暴露評估，結果顯示在
調整採樣時間、抽煙、年齡、心跳、尿中尼古丁等因子後，PM2.5 4 小時移動平
均（4-hour moving PM2.5 average）濃度每提高 1 mg/m3，將造成SDNN 2.66%的減
少（95% confidence interval (CI) = -3.75%, -1.58%）與心跳 1.02 %的增加 1（95% 
CI = 0.59%, 1.46%）[16]。 
事實上，近年來之研究已發現，微粒濃度與粒徑大小皆為造成不同健康影響
之主要因素。由於細懸浮微粒及粗懸浮微粒之粒徑大小不同，其來源、形成機制、
組成、於大氣中之壽命、室內室外濃度比（indoor-outdoor ratios）、空間分佈及短
時間變化等皆不相同，因此各種粒徑微粒之健康影響有進一步探討的必要性[3, 
17, 18]。

Concentrations for College Students in Taichung Metropolitan Area

The COPIA study (Characterization of Outdoor, Personal, and Indoor Air Pollutants) 
characterizes the outdoor, personal, and indoor concentrations of particulate and 
gaseous pollutants for college students in Taichung Metropolitan Area.  The overall 
goal of this study is to better understand not only the relationship between ambient 
concentrations and personal exposures to particulate matter (PM) and related air 
pollutants but also the contributing factors in different weather and culture system that 
might affect personal exposures to air pollutants.  Monitoring was performed for 18 
walking commuters and 21 motorcycle commuters for 5 consecutive days in the year 
of 2007.  During each sampling day, the participant wore a multi-pollutant sampler 
to collect simultaneous 24-hr integrated samples for each of the following pollutants: 
PM2.5 (including sulfate, nitrate, elemental carbon (EC), organic carbon (OC), and 
polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs)), PM10, PM2.5-10, O3, NO2, and SO2.  In 
addition, residential indoor concentrations and outdoor levels at a central monitoring 
site were measured simultaneously using the same multi-pollutant sampler.  Housing 
characteristic information and time-activity diaries were also obtained.  On average, 
college students commuting by walking spent 68.5% of each school day within their 
residences, while motorcycle-commuting students spent only 56.5% of their time at 
home.  Initial results showed that 24-hr mean personal exposures to PM10, for 
example, were 67.0 (+ 22.3) μg/m3 for walking commuters and 52.2 (+ 24.6) μg/m3 
for motorcycling commuters, while corresponding ambient levels were 81.0 (+ 39.4) 
μg/m3 and 57.5 (+ 27.9) μg/m3, respectively.  The mean personal PM2.5 
concentrations for students commuting by walking were 53.6 (+ 21.5) μg/m3, with the 
corresponding ambient PM2.5 concentrations of 63.7 (+ 33.0) μg/m3.  For 
motorcycle-commuting students, mean personal PM2.5 was 38.3 (+ 20.6) μg/m3 and 
ambient levels was 42.5 (+ 27.4) μg/m3.  In general, personal PM exposures were 
lower than corresponding ambient concentrations, while the motorcycling participants 
showed higher mean personal/outdoor ratios of PM as compared to those for 
participants commuting by walking.  Finally, for gaseous pollutants, outdoor
