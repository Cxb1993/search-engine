  III
Abstract 
As a part of the R&D of a 100-lbf NTO/MMH liquid rocket engine in the main 
project, this sub-project proposes to research the design of the injector plate.  In third 
year project, the design procedure of the liquid rocket injector was developed, and the 
injector plate of the ground-test liquid rocket was designed and manufactured.  
Together with the other two sub-projects “Computer Simulation Analyses of a 100-lbf 
Liquid Rocket Combustor” and “Combustion Instability Analyses of a 100-lbf Liquid 
Rocket”, a ground-test article of a 100-lbf rocket was also developed.  Hence, the fire 
tests of the ground-test liquid rocket engine were performed.  The effects of the liquid 
rocket engine design and operation parameters on the combustion and propulsion 
performance were then researched.  
From the hot fire test results, it showed that, at the designed flow rate condition, the 
combustor pressure of the liquid rocket engine was 153.2 psi, and the measured 
sea-level thrust was 62.1 lb, and the characteristic exhaust velocity reached 1429.2 m/s.  
And the calculated vacuum thrust was 99.8 lb, and the vacuum Isp was 295.8 s, which 
met the project’s objective.  As the flow rate of the propellants was decreased, the 
propulsion performed as well as the vacuum Isp of the liquid rocket engine was 
degraded because of the poor atomization and mixing of the liquid propellants.  
Further increasing the mass flow rate of the propellants to be larger than the designed 
condition, the thrust of the rocket engine was increased, but the propulsion performed as 
well as the vacuum Isp showed little improvement.  
The wall temperature of the combustor revealed that the film cooling mechanism of 
the injector plate worked well during the operation of the rocket. The highest 
temperature region was at the nozzle throat (~700K).  Besides, at the nozzle inlet and 
the injector plate were also observed the high temperature of the combustor wall.  
The hot fire test results showed the well combustion and cooling characteristics of the 
designed injector plate. The completion of the design procedure of the liquid rocket 
injector of this third-year’s sub-project provided the useful key technique for the liquid 
rocket design. Thus, the main objective of this year’s sub-project was successfully 
achieved.
