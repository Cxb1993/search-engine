Wait等人【2】使用三組不同長度的壓電
風扇進行數值模擬與流場可視化實驗，文中
討論不同共振頻率對壓電風扇產生之流場與
壓電風扇耗能的影響。結果顯示雖然共振頻
率較高可以提供複雜的流場，增加冷卻流體
混合及熱交換作用，但相對也會造成較高的
耗電，一般可攜式電子設備對於電能之消耗
極為重視，因此將壓電風扇應用於可攜式電
子設備，應選擇合適的共振頻率。 
Abdullah 等人【3】進行壓電風扇高度對
電子冷卻應用上的流動與熱傳影響研究，利
用計算流體力學軟體 FLUENT與質點影像測
速儀進行數值模擬與流場可視化實驗，研究
中在兩排熱源上方裝設一壓電風扇，探討兩
種不同壓電風扇至熱源距離對於流場分布及
熱傳效果之影響。實驗結果發現所使用之兩
種壓電風扇至熱源距離中，距離較大的情況
提供較好的熱傳效果，其最佳冷卻效能可使
熱源溫度下降達 68.9°C。 
Kimber 等人【4】利用紅外線熱影像量
測技術進行實驗，於一加熱不鏽鋼薄片前放
置壓電風扇，並改變壓電風扇振幅及壓電風
扇與加熱不鏽鋼薄片之間距，藉此討論各參
數對於熱傳效果之影響。實驗結果顯示最佳
的壓電風扇與加熱不鏽鋼薄片的間距會隨著
壓電風扇振幅不同而有所改變，當振幅較大
時，壓電風扇與加熱不鏽鋼薄片之間距越小
所得之熱傳效果越好，而最佳的間距會隨著
壓電風扇振幅變小而逐漸變大。 
Liu 等人【5】以實驗方式研究壓電風扇
水平與垂直擺放對平板表面的熱傳影響，實
驗中共使用六種不同幾何尺寸的壓電風扇，
研究結果發現熱傳增加是因為每一個振盪週
期在壓電風扇葉片尖端所產生的噴射氣流所
引起。風扇葉片垂直擺放之最佳熱傳位置由
中心處對稱分布（Symmetrical distribution）；
而葉片水平擺放則呈現不對稱分布的情形，
最佳熱傳出現在葉片尖端位於散熱片上方四
分之一處的位置（x/L=0.25）。實驗結果也發
現風扇水平擺放的熱傳性能並非都低於垂直
擺放，其與平板表面之距離也有關係。 
Kimber 與 Garimella【6】測量並預測壓
電風扇之冷卻特性，實驗是將壓電風扇擺放
在一個固定熱通量的表面，並以紅外線攝影
機獲得表面溫度資料。實驗中使用六組壓電
風扇，共振頻率範圍從 60 到 250Hz，結果顯
示風扇頻率對最大熱傳率之影響比振幅稍
大。 
Yoo 等人【7】表示在對噪音大小有要求
的電子設備中，利用壓電風扇來取代旋轉式
風扇進行冷卻是非常合適的。研究中利用黃
銅、鋁及磷青銅製成壓電風扇之葉片，並於
操作頻率 60Hz 條件下，探討不同壓電風扇尺
寸、壓電材料與驅動電壓對於壓電風扇葉片
擺動時所產生的流體速度之影響。當壓電材
料長度固定時，壓電風扇之共振頻率會隨著
葉片長度增加而變小。研究結果顯示以磷青
銅製成的壓電風扇葉片能提供較好的效益，
以鋁製成的壓電風扇葉片效能略差，但材料
成本較為節省。 
Ghazanfar 等人【8】為了研究如何穩定
與控制氮化鎵發光二極體（GaN LED）之波
長，選擇使用壓電風扇來控制發光二極體內
PN 接合點的溫度。研究中指出壓電風扇具有
低噪音與良好的空氣流動品質，對發光二極
體封裝之熱管理是一種有效的解決方案。實
驗結果顯示使用壓電風扇冷卻發光二極體上
之散熱片，其溫度比自然對流情況下降低了
37.4°C。 
Açıkalın 等人【9】將壓電風扇以平行及
垂直擺設於一加熱源上方，此外在垂直擺設
情況下，熱源上方增加一平板型鰭片，探討
這兩種結構時，改變壓電風扇振幅、壓電風
扇至熱源距離、壓電風扇長度、操作頻率與
共振頻率之偏移量及壓電風扇與熱源中心位
置之偏移量等參數對於熱傳效果的影響。結
果顯示操作頻率與共振頻率之偏移量對於熱
傳效果影響最大，因為操作頻率遠離共振頻
率時，壓電風扇葉片之振幅大幅減小，導致
氣流變弱，使得整體熱傳性能下降。當振幅
相同時，較短葉片的壓電風扇能提供較大的
冷卻流體速度，因此其散熱能力優於較長葉
片的壓電風扇。

3.2 鰭片數目對散熱片熱傳效益之影響 
圖 13 為當鰭片高度 H=15mm，鰭片寬度
W=5mm 時，鰭片數目改變對散熱片熱傳效
益之影響。圖中鰭片數目為 0 時，表示此時
僅有散熱片底板，上方並無任何鰭片，而壓
電風扇擺放高度與有鰭片時相同。結果顯示
增加鰭片數目可以增加熱傳效果，特別是壓
電風扇垂直擺放時，無鰭片情況下散熱片熱
阻值均高於 22°C/W，當鰭片數目為 2 時，熱
阻值降幅高達 76%，而壓電風扇水平擺放下
也有超過 33%之降幅，顯示壓電風扇搭配散
熱片使用可大幅提升熱傳效果。圖 14 與圖
15 分別為壓電風扇葉片垂直與水平擺放時，
鰭片數目對散熱片熱傳效益之影響。由圖中
可以得知，在相同鰭片寬度、鰭片高度、壓
電風扇擺放方式與壓電風扇位置的條件下，
皆為鰭片數目為 3 時較鰭片數目為 2 時能有
較佳的熱傳效果。因為兩片鰭片時壓電風扇
位於流道上方，而當三片鰭片時壓電風扇葉
片位於正中央鰭片上方，此時壓電風扇葉片
擺動所產生之空氣流動可立即與散熱片進行
熱交換作用，且因為氣流受到鰭片之阻擋而
進入兩側流道內，產生加速作用，能有更大
的動能到達散熱片底板進行熱交換帶走熱
量，此外，三片鰭片也較兩片鰭片能提供更
大的散熱面積，因此能獲得更好的熱傳效果。 
3.3 鰭片高度對散熱片熱傳效益之影響 
圖 14 為壓電風扇葉片垂直擺放時，鰭片
高度對散熱片熱傳效益之影響。由圖中可以
得知，除鰭片數目為 2、鰭片寬度為 4mm、
壓電風扇尖端切齊散熱片中心（X=15mm）
時，高度改變對熱阻值影響差異程度較小
外，其餘鰭片寬度與鰭片數目條件下，皆為
鰭片高度較低時熱傳效果較佳。顯示壓電風
扇垂直擺放時，因風扇葉片為左右擺動，冷
卻流體主要是以水平方向擴散，所以鰭片高
度越高，反而造成冷卻氣體無法進入流道內
部進行熱交換作用，使得散熱片之熱傳效益
變差。 
圖 15 為壓電風扇葉片水平擺放時，鰭片
高度對散熱片熱傳效益之影響，此種擺放方
式則是當鰭片數目為 2 的情況下，鰭片寬度
較薄（W=3mm 與 4mm）時，鰭片高度增加
會造成熱阻值增加，而鰭片寬度稍厚
（W=5mm 與 10mm）時，鰭片高度增加可稍
微降低熱阻。此時壓電扇葉片正位於散熱片
流道上方，而鰭片過高會造成壓電風扇距離
底板之位置也較遠，葉片上下擺動時所引起
的動能不足以驅使冷卻氣體達到散熱片底板
處進行熱交換，因此熱阻值反而升高，惟獨
鰭片寬度較厚時因高度所增加之散熱面積較
其他幾種鰭片寬度時還多，所以能使熱阻值
稍微降低。而鰭片數目為 3 時，則無論何種
鰭片寬度，皆為鰭片高度增加，熱傳效果增
強，因為鰭片數目為 3 時，葉片下方恰有一
片鰭片，壓電風扇水平擺放所引起向下的氣
流能立即與鰭片進行熱交換作用，因此鰭片
高度增加雖然也使壓電風扇與散熱片底板距
離增加，但鰭片增加的散熱面積能大幅增進
熱傳效果。圖 16 為 H=30mm、W=5mm、n=2、
Hfan=50mm、X=0mm，壓電風扇由上往下擺

四、結論 
本研究以紅外線熱像儀及質點影像測速
儀進行實驗，探討壓電風扇冷卻平板型散熱
片之熱流特性，考慮壓電風扇之擺放方式、
擺放位置與散熱片的幾何尺寸等參數對於散
熱效益之影響。 
基於實驗之結果，將結論歸納如下： 
1. 壓電風扇擺放位置：壓電風扇垂直擺放
時，最佳熱傳效益出現於風扇葉片尖端位
於散熱片中心處。而壓電風扇水平擺放
時，散熱性能則是葉片尖端位於散熱片前
緣時最佳，隨著風扇葉片往散熱片後方移
動，熱傳效益會逐漸變差。 
2. 散熱片之鰭片數目影響：因散熱片具有三
片鰭片較兩片鰭片能提供更大的散熱面
積，且三片鰭片與兩片鰭片相比，會造成
散熱片內流道寬度縮減，使冷卻流體產生
加速作用，因此也能獲得更好的熱傳效
果。 
3. 散熱片之鰭片高度影響：壓電風扇垂直擺
放時，大多為鰭片高度較低時熱傳效果較
佳。而壓電風扇水平擺放時，三片鰭片的
情況下鰭片高度增加可以提升熱傳效
益，兩片鰭片的情況則隨不同鰭片寬度變
化而有不同的效果。 
4. 散熱片之鰭片寬度影響：壓電風扇垂直擺
放時，鰭片寬度增加並無法提高散熱片熱
傳效益，在部分情況下甚至會造成散熱效
果明顯變差。而壓電風扇水平擺放時，大
多數情況皆為鰭片寬度增加可以降低散
熱片之熱阻值。

Abstract—To assess the performance of plate-fin heat sinks 
with the introduction of a shield in a cross flow, we analyzed 
the surface temperature of the base of the heat sink with an 
infrared thermograph to determine the effects of shield, fin 
height, fin width and Reynolds number on the thermal 
resistance. The introduction of a shield decreases the bypass 
flow effect and forces more coolant fluid to enter the channel 
from fin to fin to enhance the heat transfer. Increasing the 
Reynolds number decreases the thermal resistance of the heat 
sink, but that decrease tends to diminish with increasing 
Reynolds number. For a given fin width, the thermal 
performance of the heat sink with the highest fins is superior. 
For a given fin height, the optimal fin width in terms of 
thermal performance increases with increasing Reynolds 
number.

I. INTRODUCTION 
The increasing miniaturization of electronic products 
makes the transfer of generated heat to the outside more 
difficult. As the efficiency of electronic products suffers 
from an inadequate mechanism of heat dissipation, possibly 
causing damage as the temperature rises, effective thermal 
management of electronic products is of priority concern. 
Sara [1] presented the characteristics of heat transfer and 
friction and analyzed the convective heat transfer through a 
rectangular channel with pin fins of square cross section and 
attached over a flat surface; the pin fins were arranged in a 
staggered manner, with varied clearance ratios C/H, defined 
as the ratio of the clearance C between the upper surface of a 
fin and the ceiling of duct to the fin height H, and interfin 
distance ratio Sx/D, which is the ratio of the longitudinal pin 
fin spacing Sx to the thickness D of the fin. The performance 
analysis was made under a constraint of constant pumping 
power. The experimental results showed that a smaller 
interfin distance ratio (Sx/D) and clearance ratio (C/H) 
increased both the thermal performance and the friction 
coefficient. Elshafei [2] assessed, both experimentally and 
theoretically, the thermal fluid performance of a plate-fin 
heat sink under cross flow conditions through parameters for 
the mainstream velocity, fin density and clearance between 
tip and shroud. Their effects on the bypass flow, pressure 
drop and thermal performance were discussed; the mean 
heat-transfer coefficient decreased with increasing clearance 
between tip and shroud, but the effect of this clearance on the 
mean heat-transfer coefficient decreased with increasing 
Reynolds number. Jonsson and Palm [3] studied in a wind 
tunnel the thermal performance of plate-fin heat sinks and 
strip-fin heat sinks with inline and staggered arrays; they 
assessed the effects of fin height and bypass conditions on 
the thermal and hydraulic performance of various heat sinks. 
The thermal resistance increased with increasing height and 
width of the wind tunnel duct; in particular, the height of the 
wind tunnel duct contributed substantially to the 
effectiveness of heat transfer. Li et al. [4] used CFD software 
FLUENT to study the hydraulic and thermal performance of 
a plate-fin heat sink undergoing cross flow forced convection 
with the introduction of a shield; the influence of fin width, 
fin height, number of fins and Reynolds number were 
assessed without and with a shield. A shield that tends to 
decrease the bypass flow effect has a great influence on the 
variation of the thermal fluid field and the performance of 
the heat sink. Attaching a shield forces coolant fluid to flow 
into the fin-to-fin channel to enhance the heat transfer, 
increasing the pressure drop; this trend is significant at small 
Reynolds numbers. The decrease of thermal resistance due to 
the shield diminishes with increasing fin height, but 
increasing the width of the fins has a more radical effect. El-
Sayed et al. [5] varied the fin height, fin width, space 
between fins, number of fins and the distance of fin tips from 
the shroud to investigate the performance of a plate-fin heat 
sink; the pressure drop increased with increasing Reynolds 
number and fin height but decreased with increasing inter-fin 
space and fin width, increasing the clearance between the fin 
tips and the shroud decreased the mean Nusselt number, and 
the mean Nusselt number increased with increasing 
Reynolds number, fin width and space between fins but with 
decreasing fin height. Prstic and Bar-Cohen [6] placed a heat 
shield before a plate-fin heat sink in a transverse duct flow 
and analyzed the thermal-fluid performance both 
experimentally and numerically; three tested configurations 
conformed to fully shrouded, partially shrouded and shield 
conditions. The superior thermal performance achieved in 
the fully shrouded configuration is accompanied by a penalty 
of a significant pressure drop. The use of a heat shield 
decreases the influence of the bypass effect and induces 
additional fluid to enter the fin-to-fin channel of a plate-fin

III. RESULTS AND DISCUSSION 
We investigated the effects of a shield, Reynolds number 
Re of the cooling air, fin height H and fin width W on the 
performance of plate-fin heat sinks under cross flow 
conditions. Twenty-five plate-fin heat sinks with fin widths 
W = 0.5, 1.0, 1.5, 2.0 and 2.5 mm and fin heights H = 15, 30, 
45, 60 and 75 mm, were used in the experiments. The 
surface temperature of the base of the heat sink, measured 
with an infrared thermograph, is analyzed to examine the 
effects of installation of a shield, fin dimensions and 
Reynolds number on the thermal resistance. 
A. Effects of a shield on thermal resistance 
Fig. 4 depicts the thermal resistance of heat sinks of W = 
1.5 mm for five fin heights, five Reynolds numbers and with 
and without a shield [7]. The thermal resistance with a shield 
is less than without a shield at any condition of fixed fin 
width, fin height and Reynolds number. This behavior 
reflects the existence of the shield blocking the upper bypass 
fluid as in the case without a shield and forcing more coolant 
fluid to enter the fin-to-fin channel, thus enhancing the heat 
transfer of walls in the fin-to-fin channel and decreasing the 
thermal resistance. The greatest decrease of thermal 
resistance as the shield is installed is 41 % for H = 15 mm 
and Re = 10000, whereas the smallest decrease of thermal 
resistance is 17 % for H = 15 mm and Re = 50000. An 
installed shield is consequently effective in enhancing the 
heat transfer of the heat sink under a cross flow.

Fig. 6 shows the thermal resistance for an increased 
Reynolds number 20000. The variation of the thermal 
resistance due to the fin width effect is small when W ≤ 1.5 
mm. For H ≥ 45 mm, the three curves reveal that the 
minimal thermal resistances do not occur at the minimal fin 
width. They vary slightly as the fin width increases from 0.5 
mm to 1.5 mm and the thermal resistance slightly decreases 
as the fin width increases. For fin height H = 45 mm, the 
minimal thermal resistance appears at fin width W = 1.0 mm. 
For both fin heights H = 60 and 75 mm, the minimal thermal 
resistances appear at fin width W = 1.5 mm. For all five fin 
heights and W ≤ 1.5 mm, the variations of the thermal 
resistance due to the effect of fin width are less than 17 %. 
The variations are subtle and they indicate that the blockage 
effect of the shield assists the cooling air to enter the fin-to-
fin channels to exchange heat when the Reynolds number is 
slightly greater. The thermal performance hence does not 
clearly vary with decreasing width of the fin-to-fin channel 
in a certain range, but, when the fins are too wide (W > 1.5 
mm), the cooling air is impeded and the thermal resistance is 
considerably increased. For all five fin heights and W > 1.5 
mm, the variations of the thermal resistance due to the effect 
of fin width are between 40 % and 53 %. The thermal 
resistance declines as the fin height increases. The minimal 
thermal resistance at this approach speed is 0.197 °C/W for 
H = 75 mm and W = 1.5 mm, 47 % less than that at Re = 
10000. 
When the Reynolds number is increased to 30000, the 
thermal resistance is as plotted in Fig. 7. The variation of 
thermal resistance differs from that at the preceding smaller 
Reynolds number. At first, the thermal resistance drop 
slightly as the fin width increases for all five fin heights. The 
thermal resistance is minimal as the fin width increases to 
1.5 mm for heat sinks with all five fin heights. The thermal 
resistance increases as the fin width continues to increase. A 
heat sink with a shield hence decreases the by-pass effect of 
the cooling fluid that has greater kinetic energy sufficient to 
flow into the heat sink. The heat sink with a large area for 
heat transfer thus improves the thermal performance. If the 
fins are too wide, the cooling air is obstructed from entering 
the heat sink to exchange heat, so that the increase in fin 
width to improve the heat transfer is limited. Similarly, 
increasing the fin height decreases the thermal resistance, but 
the decline is not great as at Re = 20000. The minimal 
thermal resistance at this approach speed is 0.147 °C/W for 
H = 75 mm and W = 1.5 mm, 25% less than at Re = 20000.
