21. Introduction
The interest in the coating of transition-metal nitride has grown considerably during the
last 20 years. Various metal nitrides have become important in industry, science and
technology for their attractiveness and useful properties. Among the 4th group transition metal
nitrides, TiN may have the most successful application in industry. In addition, ZrN has
attracted attention for its better corrosion resistance [1,2], better mechanical properties [3,4],
and warmer golden color [5] than the corresponding properties of TiN film. The TiZrN
coating demonstrates an enhanced properties compared with the binary TiN and ZrN coatings
deposited under equivalent conditions [6,7].
Improvement in properties of thin films was obtained by the deposition of binary, ternary
and even higher component systems. TiN was a good example used as the base for many
coatings alloyed with other elements, such as Al, Si, V, Cr, Zr etc. The addition of Zr may
further improve the thermal stability of the TiN coating [8]. Similar to aluminum, zirconium
forms a stable surface oxide [9], which stabilizes the TiN monocell. Moreover, preliminary
studies on the effects of zirconium implantation have shown improved wear resistance of TiN
coatings [10]. In general, the composition of Zr0.5 Ti0.5N gave the fine mechanical properties
[11].
However, there were few researches to discuss properties and corrosion in the TiZrN
system. Furthermore, the structure of deposited films was mixed crystal TiZrN in preliminary
studies, and no literature report for TiZrN to appear in two phases of the films during
4spectroscopy (SIMS), which was used to measure the thickness of TiZrN film on 304 SS.
X-ray diffraction (XRD) was used to determine the preferred orientations of the films.
Electric resistivity of the films was measured by a four-point-probe. The composition of
TiZrN and packing density were characterized by the Rutherford backscattering spectroscopy
(RBS). Hardness was measured by nanoindentation. Potentiodynamic polarization
measurements were carried out using a PARC EG&G Potentiostat / Galvanostat model
M263A operated by M352 software. The electrolytes were 0.5M H2SO4 + 0.05M KSCN
solution. The polarization curves of all the specimens were generated after conducting the
scan from -800 to 800 mV (vs. SCE). Table 1 summarizes the experimental parameters
employed for the film deposition in the present investigation along with the results of
characterization for the films obtained.
3. Results and discussion
3.1 Microstructure of TiZrN films
Figure 1 shows X-ray diffraction patterns of TiZrN thin films. Reflection line of TiZrN
(002) peak is observed between those of TiN (002) peak and ZrN (002) peak, similarly,
reflection line of TiZrN (111) peak is observed between those of TiN(111) peak and ZrN(111)
peak. In general, the deposited films exist without second phase in mixed crystal TiZrN [13].
Both TiN and ZrN crystallize in the cubic densest sphere packing, this is also the structure of
the mixed phase TiZrN. However, there is a reflection line of TiZr phase appearing in sample
B8, the phenomenon is never displayed in others research. The reason is that the thin films of
6pure TiN (sample B1) have lower resistivity as compared with TiZrN, the reason is that
substitutional atoms of Zr atom can be regarded as impurities in the TiN film. The resistivity
increases with increasing of Zr/(Zr+ Ti) ratio gradually. Furthermore, the grain size decrease
with increasing the ratio of Zr/(Zr+Ti), and the increase of grain boundaries result in the
increase of resistivity. When the gun power of Zr target is at 400 W, the incorporation phase
TiZr is discovered. That is the reason for the sample B8 to have the highest resistivity and
lowest packing factor. Moreover, the second phase of TiZr appears in sample B8, and the
smallest grain size for TiZrN thin films is 7.9 nm.
Figure 4 shows the variation of the hardness with the ratios of Zr/(Zr+Ti). Hardness is a
convenient index for evaluating the performance of the TiZrN thin film. Some trends of
conflict between processing parameters and the hardness of thin film were reported from time
to time [18,19]. There are many factors that affect the hardness of transition metal nitride. The
high hardness coatings appear along with nano-scale structure generally, such as
nanocomposite or nanograin. Figure 5 shows the relation between hardness and grain size.
Grain size plays an important role in hardness of the TiZrN thin films. Experimentally, the
effect of grain size displays two opposite trends on hardness. In the study, the mixing process
produces nanograin size films in the TiZrN. With decreasing grain size, the multiplication and
mobility of the dislocations are hindered, and the hardness of materials increases following
the ‘Hal–Petch’ relationship. Moreover, al specimens of single phase TiZrN are 
understoichiometric (N/(Ti+Zr)＜1). In the TiN system [20], the high hardness appears in the
8hinders the flow of corrosive medium through the film. The addition of Zr can improve the
corrosion resistant of the TiN coating.
4. Conclusions
Nano-crystalline (Ti,Zr)N thin films were successfully produced using dual gun with Ti
and Zr targets in r.f. magnetron sputtering. The grain size decrease with increasing the ratio of
Zr/(Zr+Ti). The mixing process is based on the addition of Zr elements to the TiN, and
restrains grain growth to produce nanograin size, the grain size decreases with increasing the
ratio of Zr/(Zr+Ti), and increasing of grain boundaries results in the increasing of resistivity.
Moreover, with decreasing grain size, the multiplication and mobility of the dislocations are
hindered, and the hardness of materials increases. The second phase of TiZr produces
extended deformation of the matrix TiZrN with high gun power of Zr target, and the smallest
grain size for TiZrN thin films is 7.9 nm. The TiZrN with the incorporation phase of TiZr has
highest resistivity, and relief of lattice strain is the reason for decreasing of hardness. The
large numbers of grain boundaries increase the distance of corrosive medium to the 304
stainless steel in the nanograin TiZrN, and then the second phase of TiZr in TiZrN restrictes
the flow of corrosive medium. The addition of Zr can improve the corrosion resistance of the
TiN coating.
