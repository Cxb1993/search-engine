efficiency evaluation. 
This paper estimated the cumulative learning effect 
of 23 local governments of Taiwan during 2001 to 2009 
using regression analysis, and the results were then 
incorporated into DEA SBM model to evaluate the 
relative performance of MSW recycling in 2009. The 
major contributions of this paper are three-fold: 
First, this paper developed a new approach that 
incorporates organizational learning effects into the 
DEA SBM model. Second, improved policies were 
provided for local governments with inefficient MSW 
recycling. Finally, this paper found that household 
disposable income levels differ depending on learning 
methods, leading to a considerable difference in 
cumulative learning effects. In promoting recycling 
policies, local governments should pay attention to 
determining whether these policies can be 
disseminated and implemented relative to their living 
standard.

23 個縣市政府資源回收量之相對績效評估。本研究證實融入組織學習效果確實對績效具有攸

Abstract 
Many literatures document that the effect of organizational learning (OL) results in 
continuously improving the firm performance or management system over time. The cumulative 
learning effect may form as one of intellectual capital. Thus far, however, the static model of data 
envelopment analysis (DEA) does not deal with the longitudinal learning effect. This study attempts 
to incorporate the learning effect into the model of DEA for efficiency evaluation. The two-stage 
approach will be developed along with the latent learning effect is estimated from the time-series 
data, and then set the estimated learning effect as input in the DEA SBM (slacks-based measure) 
model. With regard to the issue of sustainable development, the proposed DEA model will be 
applied to investigate the organizational learning effect of MSW (Municipal Solid Waste) recycling 
4 
允許隨機誤差存在，也不必人為設定權重，可以同時處理多種投入與產出項。一般常使用的
模式包括CCR(Charnes-Cooper-Rhodes)、BCC (Banker-Charnes-Cooper)與SBM (Slack-Based 
Measure)模式。CCR與BCC模式皆假設無效率之投入或產出係以等比例調整。SBM模式則以
差額變數衡量無效率決策單位（Decision Making Unit, DMU）調整(Cooper et al., 
2007)，屬於非輻射型模式具有較高判別能力(Discriminating power)(Zhou et al., 2008)。此外，
SBM模式可透過效率值分解提供無效率決策單位改善效率的參考目標，建立有效的回饋
系統。多數績效評估相關研究著重在應用層面(Chang and Tseng, 2000)。 
最近應用在有關環境與生態議題時，多數學者將焦點關注於在生產正面產出（Desirable 
Outputs）的過程所伴隨負面產出（Undesirable Outputs）的發生(Scheel, 2001)，若未來欲降低
負面產出，必需負擔相當大的成本，此時負面產出越多效率將越差，例如：二氧化碳( )
排放量、硫氧化物（Sulfur Oxides）、有毒廢棄物等。除應用於生產過程，在探討永續發展為
政策目標時，也可能需要考慮負面產出，例如Courcelle et al.,(1998)認為在家庭廢棄物的收集
與分類的過程中，可將不可資源回收的廢棄物視為負面產出項。實務上，也有可能會發生投
入越多效率越好的情況，例如Allen (1999)在資源回收再利用系統與廢棄物處理系統的過程
中，將廢棄物假設為投入越多效率越好的情況。除考慮負面因子(Undesirable Factors)能使被
選取的學習標竿(Benchmarks)更加合理化外，其他學者也嘗試將各項品質(Quality)指標衡量方
法納入標竿評選的過程，以避免選定具有不理想品質的DMU，例如Olesen and Petersen (1995) 
的產品品質指標、Chang and Tseng (2000)的價值判斷(Value Judgement)，以及Shimshak et 
al.,(2009)的服務品質指標等等。然而，如何在資料包絡分析模式中融入組織學習效果衡量相
對效率，在過去文獻中尚未曾探討。 
有鑑於此，本研究延續 Chen and Chang (2010)的研究，探討臺灣各縣市處理資源回收的
永續議題，研究目的有下面四項:(1)嘗試發展將學習效果融入資料包絡分析模式；(2)檢定學習
效果對縣市政府資源回收效率之影響；(3)對垃圾處理與資源回收相對無效率之縣市政府提供
擬定改善目標；(4)檢定都會區與非都會區對縣市政府資源回收效率是否有差異。 
二、研究方法與步驟 
學習效果係反映過去時間所累積的成果，衡量組織學習效果必須使用時間序列資料。由
於傳統資料包絡分析是處理橫斷面資料效率評估，為了將組織學習累積的效果融入模式中，
本研究採兩階段方法進行分析：首先估計各 DMU 累積之學習存量效果，其次將各組織學習
效果加入資料包絡分析模式投入項中進行相對效率衡量。分述如後: 
第一階段 組織學習效果估計 
Wright (1936)為首位研究學習效果的學者，模擬學習效果如累計每單位的對數線性
(Log-linear)函數。從那以後，各式各樣的學習效果陸續被提出，發展至今除對數線性模型外，
著名模型還包括：S-曲線(S-curve)模型(Carr, 1946)、史丹佛-B 曲線(Stanford-B)模型(Asher, 
1956)、學習公式(Learning Formula)(De Jong, 1957; Glover, 1965)、匹配函數(Adaptation 
Function)(Levy, 1965)、指數函數(Exponential Function)(Pegels, 1969)、Upturn 模型(Knecht, 
1974)、結合產品(Combined Product)模型(Yelle, 1979)等等，其中還是以對數線性模型最廣泛
被應用(Badiru and Ijaduola, 2009; Yang and Chand, 2008)。

臺灣在過去數十年社會及經濟活動急遽擴張下，所衍生的廢棄物問題不斷出現，如何有

方向將能有效改善資源回收之績效。在資源回收績效方面，較少探討各縣市推動資源回收政

在組織學習效果估計的部分，家庭成員與回收人員可能受到地方政府政策、經驗累積與
教育等因素影響，改變其學習的態度或行為，進而提高資源回收的績效。除Chen and Chang 
(2010)將臺灣環保局訓練員工費用假設學習係數的內生變數外，尚有其他影響因素誘導或迫
使民眾配合垃圾減量與確實做好資源分類與回收，例如：地方政府推動適合當地的管理政策
(Chiang, 2001; Garces et al., 2002)、環保局至社區、學校與機關團體宣導活動與文宣(Hu et al., 
2008)、學校的教育宣導(Chiang, 2001)與在資源回收過程中所產生累積的學習經驗(Chen and 
Chang, 2010)等。目前台灣以地方政府(清潔隊)、社區、學校與機關團體為四大回收管道(EPA,

Methods 
 
Data envelopment analysis (DEA) 
 
For assessing the performance among peers, DEA is a linear programming based 
methodology, in terms of efficiency relative to these peers‟ transforming utilized 
inputs into outputs. Described by its position relative to the frontier of best 
performance, the efficiency of a Decision Making Unit (DMU) is mathematically 
defined by the ratio of the weighted sum of outputs to the weighted sum of input. The 
details of DEA, including methodology, theory, as well as the advantages and 
disadvantages of the technique, can be found in Cooper et al. [20] and Norman & 
Stoker [21]. DEA can answer what inputs and what quantity should be reduced, what 
outputs and what amounts should be increased so as to be efficient. Several models in 
DEA were applied to evaluate hospital efficiency [22-24].

Input projections and reduction rates 
 
The new target, and quantitative corrective information of SODs for safety 
improvement, can not be provided in FMEA. Consequently, there is no input 
projection and reduction rate. 
In DEA, for those modes (or DMUs) whose efficiency scores are less than one 
(1), the input projections of SODs (namely, new targets of SODs) could render these 
inefficient DMUs into the efficient frontier. However, from (Table 2), for the three 
modes (#4, #10 and #15) whose efficiency scores are one, the input projections of 
SODs are identical to the original value of SODs, in either SBM-I model or 
CCR-I-AR model. Consequently, their reduction rates are all zeros. Namely, modes 
#4, #10, and #15 construct the efficient frontier. 
 
A comparison of quantitative corrective information of risk indexes

improving effort. Namely, FMEA does not provide any new target of SODs for safety 
improvement. However, through the novel approach, the scales of improving effort 
can be provided by the differences between the original values of SODs and the 
corresponding input projections, as shown in the eighth and ninth columns of (Table 
2). These differences are exactly the quantitative corrective information of risk 
indexes (S, O, D) that thereafter reduce failure possibility.

presented by two sets of failure modes with the same RPN.  
    After the risk ranking, the new target and quantitative corrective information of 
SODs for safety improvement, are needed in taking corrective action, because of the 
concern of the improvement cost. However, the earlier study [13, 16, 17, 29] can not 
provide this information by FMEA.  
    The novel approach provides not only the risk ranking as FMEA offers, but also 
more insights into the failure modes, especially regarding SODs. The greatest 
contribution and advantage of this approach is that once the SODs are given, the risk 
ranking, the new targets and quantitative corrective information of SODs for safety 
improvement can be quantitatively provided at the same time. These insights have 
been presented, as stated earlier by an illustrative example.
