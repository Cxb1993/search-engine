英文摘要 
The objectives of the present study are to prepare gold-supported vanadium-doped tin oxide (Au/VOx- 
SnO2) nanocomposite powders and elucidate their surface functions by exploiting these powders as catalysts 
and gas sensors. The work aimed to explore the interaction between Au and V elements, obtain its correlation 
to catalytic and gas-sensing reactions and propose the surface nature of the materials. The Au-V-Sn oxide 
composite was prepared by the co-precipitation method, and their structural properties were characterized.

the presence of metallic state and vanadium in three oxidation states and also an increase in the number of

the reactor study, gold induces the formation of COx, vanadium promotes the yield of formaldehyde, and 
Au-V co-doping slightly increases the conversion of methanol. That is, basic and redox characters co-exist on 
the surface. The CO sensing study reveals that gold addition enhances gas response and shortens response 
time. Oxygen in the atmosphere promotes sensing response. In conclusion, the Au-V co-doping results in

surface.

二、研究目的 
本計畫研究目的有三點： 
1. 採用共沈澱法製備 Au/VOx-SnO2 三元物系奈米粉體，及取得儀器分析材料結構性質，包括量測粒徑
及粉體形態，分析 Au、VOx 與 SnO2 元素狀態與交互作用。 
2. 採用甲醇氧化反應器以探測 Au/VOx-SnO2 奈米粉粒體的催化活性，取得甲醇轉化率及產物選擇率，
合併關聯 Au 與 VOx 的催化效應，釐清表面化學性質及催化機制。 
3. 採用半導體式一氧化碳(CO)氣體感測器探測 Au/VOx-SnO2 奈米粉粒體的感測應答率，闡釋表面吸附
作用及電化學反應，釐清 Au 與 VOx 的感測機制。 
這些成果預期提升化工技術於材料製造，增加表面應用化學知識，在無機材料及低溫節能反應器，
應有具體貢獻。 
三、文獻探討 
二氧化錫(SnO2，能隙約3.6 eV)非常有實用性，已商業化應用於半導體式氣體感測器。此外， SnO2
比較TiO2有更高的催化活性，適用為觸媒或擔體。二氧化錫晶面(110)上的架氧橋位置有氧空缺，在加
熱或電場作用下，電子游離於表面並還原氧氣生成O2¯或O¯，進行化學反應[Kawabe et al. (2001)]。但
是操作溫度高達250-400°C是問題，造成能源消耗及材料結構惡化（例如粒徑及表面積），效能於是降
低。許多關於二氧化錫的改質研究，仍持續進行中[Korotcenkov (2007)；Barsan (2007)]。 
摻雜第二金屬是提升化學反應性及導電性的方法之一，因為表面電荷及催化中心數量都有增加。
尤其多價數過渡金屬引發之效應，在觸媒應用有相當的效果。例如在VOx-SnO2觸媒與甲醇氧化反應研
究，VOx導致反應溫度下降約125°C（同轉化率），主產物由甲醚改成甲醛，因為在V-O-Sn結構之V4+/V5+
進行快速氧化還原作用，促進選擇性氧化路徑，但TiO2-SnO2與ZnO-SnO2觸媒則無類似效應[Wang et al. 
(2010)]。摻雜金屬能提升氣體應答及降低感測溫度，原因是摻雜作用增加電子轉移能階數及增加反應
中心的數量，感測表面活性增強。文獻報導摻雜Nb5+於TiO2產生新電子組態、提高CO感測應答[Ruiz et 
al. (2003)]。同樣結論適用於MoO3-SnO2 [Ansari et al. (2002)]及Fe2O3-SnO2 [Bose (2005)]等物系。雖然有
關提升感測性能的材料研究為數不少，但用電性量測與光譜分析以闡釋氣體感測機制的研究仍顯不
足，本計畫將著重於此。 
氧化釩作為觸媒是廣泛的實例，早期以含浸法製備擔載型氧化釩觸媒，雖其有催化活性，但受限
於分散性不易控制的問題。近期研究多以共沈澱法或嫁接法以增加VOx分散率，提高催化活性。分散
型VOx比較V2O5有更高催化活性[Briand et al. (2004)]，因為VOx比較易於發生還原作用，及減弱O-O鍵
結以消耗較多量氧氣，而產生活性O¯以促進氧化反應[Besselmann et al. (2001)]。在V2O5-TiO2觸媒與乳
酸氧化反應系統，V5+是催化中心[Shyue et al. (2005)]。在VOx/TiO2觸媒與乙烷氧化反應，V4+=O是催化
中心，VO2比V2O5有較高催化活性[Tessier et al. (1999)]。Tatibouët等人(1997)研究V2O5-TiO2觸媒，認為
V5+能擴散進入TiO2晶格，啟動V4+=O →V5+=O的電荷移轉作用。Forzatti等人(1997)以含浸法製備
V2O5-TiO2觸媒與測試甲醇氧化反應，認為V=O與Ti4+進行交互作用，甲醇氧化成甲酯。最近柏克萊大
學Bell教授探討VOx/TiO2觸媒與甲醇氧化反應，發現吸附於Ti中心之CH3O基團，會逆向遷移至V催化中
心，生成HCHO，反應溫度也大幅下降[Bronkema et al. (2007)]。

六、結論 
1. 採用共沈澱法成功製備 Au/VOx-SnO2 三元物系奈米複材粉體，在材料結構性質方面，Au 以金屬態
呈現且粒徑約 3 nm，V 元素摻雜於二氧化錫晶格內形成固溶氧化物且有三種氧化態(V3+/V4+/V5+)共
存於表面。摻雜作用影響晶格結構，導致氧化物粒徑減小。 
2. 對於 Au/V-SnO2 粉體，Au 添加作用促進誘發 3d V4+或氧空缺的孤電子，亦即增強耦極性交互作用，
這是促成此三元物系材料具有實務應用的因素之一。 
3. 在 Au/V-SnO2 的催化性質方面，甲醇氧化反應結果顯示，Au-V 共摻作用微量提高了甲醇的反應轉
化率，Au 增加表面路易士鹼性，V 摻雜則增加氧化還原活性。 
4. 關於 Au/V-SnO2 的氣體感測性質，CO 氣體應答顯示，Au 添加提高應答率且縮短感測器的應答時間
及脫附時間（亦即提升感測反應速率）。AuVSn 感測器可以實施運作於空氣或氮氣環境下偵測氣體，
具有實務應用價值。

Gold-Doped Oxide Nanocomposites Prepared by Two Solution Methods 
and Their Gas-Sensing Response 
 
Chien-Tsung Wang*, Huan-Yu Chen and Yu-Chung Chen 
Department of Chemical and Materials Engineering, National Yunlin University of Science 
and Technology, 123 University Road, Section 3, Douliou, Yunlin, 640, Taiwan             
   
ABSTRACT   
 
Gold species on an oxide support possess variable electronic structures via charge 
transition so as to increase their chemical redox activity. They are also viably promising for 
use to enhance gas-sensing response when being exploited in a solid state gas sensor. The 
synthesis method of the gold-loaded materials plays a crucial role in the functionality. In 
this paper, we report two types of gold/tin oxide based nanopowders prepared by 
co-precipitation method and by deposition-precipitation method, respectively. They were 
evaluated as sensing elements in a semiconductor carbon monoxide (CO) gas sensor. 
Effects of the material type and CO concentration on sensor response were investigated. 
Their structural characterizations were done by X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy, X-ray 
diffraction and transmission electron microscopy. Results demonstrate the surface gold 
species effective to facilitate CO oxidation in gas atmosphere and promote 
low-temperature sensor performance. 
 
INTRODUCTION 
  
Semi-conducting metal oxides have been widely exploited as sensing elements in 
solid-state sensors, because they possess active sites to chemisorb molecules and catalyze 
reactions. For example, tin dioxide (SnO2) is most frequently used in gas sensors to detect 
hazardous species such as carbon monoxide (CO), ethanol, ..etc. This n-type 
semiconductor adsorbs oxygen in air and CO molecules on the surface, and the oxidation 
of both species produces carbon dioxide and electrons. A decrease in the electrical 
resistance represents the sensor response. However, the SnO2 sensors often operate at 
higher temperature (e.g., up to 400 °C) in order to achieve a higher sensing response. 
Unfortunately, this thermal effect frequently causes material instability and high power 
consumption [1].  
 Great progress has been made by many research teams with invention of noble 
metal/oxide nanocomposite materials in powder form. Among the examples, gold 
nanoparticles or clusters deposited on an oxide support can promote sensor performance, 
by reducing operating temperature or increasing response magnitude [2,3]. This kinetic 
effect arises from a high catalytic activity over the gold particles (in form of sphere or 
semi-sphere) located on the oxide support for CO oxidation [4]. The catalytic properties of 
the small gold clusters or nanoparticles supported are related to their capability to 
chemisorb small molecules at defect sites on the gold surface or at the hetero-junction 
between the gold and the oxide support [5]. The synthesis approach of the gold/oxide 
composite materials plays a crucial role to influence their structural and chemical 
properties. For example, a previous study using deposition-precipitation (DP) and

CONCLUSIONS 
 
The present work completes the preparation of gold-deposited tin oxide 
nanocomposite powders and demonstrates the promoting effect of the gold particles to 
enhance sensing response to CO gas in air. These Au/SnO2 composite powders are 
promising for practical applications in semiconductor sensors as well as catalytically active 
materials.
