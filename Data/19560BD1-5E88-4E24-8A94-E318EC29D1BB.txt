In this project, based on the post-configuration configuration (with the optical phase 
modulator set behind the test sample), we propose a novel method for measuring the optical 
activity of scattering chiral medium. When p- polarization light passes through the test sample, 
polarization light and unpolarized light are generated and mixed mutually. They go through a 
quarter-wave plate and a half-wave plate. The azimuth angle of the half-wave plate is special 
angle with respect to the horizontal axis. Then they pass through an EO phase modulator of 
which only the polarized part is modulated. Finally, they pass through an analyzer and the 
interference signal is generated. This signal includes information of optical activity being related 
to the phase difference. Since the effects of the quarter wave-plat and the half wave-plate the 
phase difference can be magnified significantly being used to determine the optical activity. In 
additions, after the interference signal passing through the band-pass filter, the undesired signal 
generated by the unpolarization light can be filtered. The desired phase difference related to the 
optical activity can be obtained accurately with the heterodyne interferometry. The obtained 
phase difference is introduced into special derived formulas to obtain the optical activity of 
scattering chiral medium. The material characteristics of the chiral medium are analyzed.
