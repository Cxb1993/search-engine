(1)正面-乾式雷射清潔 
1999 年，Halfpenny and Kane [1] 利用紫外
光雷射進行玻璃表面的清潔處理，雷射震波成
功地清除直徑 0.3 m、0.1 m 的氧化鋁顆粒。
氧化鋁顆粒因雷射加熱後膨脹及其他組合機
制，使顆粒與基板之間的黏著力降低，更易於
去除。研究中發現當雷射加載之直徑與顆粒直
徑相同時，其清潔成功率可達 100%。圖 1-2 為
氧化鋁顆粒附著於玻璃表面，清潔前後之照
片，氧化鋁直徑分別為 0.3 m、0.1m。 
 
圖 1-2 玻璃表面清潔前後之照片(摘自 Ref [1]) 
2003 年，Kim et al. [2]利用脈衝雷射去除附
著在矽晶圓表面之次微米玻璃粉末。脈衝雷射
加載過程會使周圍空氣產生振動，此一振動會
產生高強度的聲波，使玻璃粉末脫離矽晶圓表
面。若在清潔過程中輔助以氬氣，可以有效提
高聲波強度，進而提高清潔效能。 
2006 年，Hsu and Lin [3-4] 探討雷射清潔
法表面震波移除微粒的機制，對微粒與不銹鋼
基材表面的黏著力，及表面震波產生的清除力
進行分析。表面震波會引發基材表面有瞬間加
速度產生，微粒因慣性作用而彈離基材表面，
此一慣性作用可以表面清除力來代表。研究中
發現雷射加載方向垂直於基材時，其清潔效能
最高，而且雷射能量越高，可以清潔的面積越
大。 
2008 年，Kim et al. [5]以 UV 脈衝雷射加載
於矽晶圓上的遮罩層，觀察遮罩層與矽晶圓間
的黏著力，發現可以利用 UV 雷射脈衝時產生
之摩擦靜電與壓力去除掉氧化鋁粉末，同時可
以防止再次污染，且表面溫度不高。 
(2)正面-濕式雷射清潔 
1997 年，Berthe et al. [6]利用水來輔助雷射
震波之產生，以 Nd:YAG 雷射加載於金屬材料
表面，此金屬材料浸置於水中，水容器設計為
封閉式。雷射打入水中會產生極大之震波，可
以運用於雷射清潔之處理。水量之多寡不會影
響震波之生成，水僅作為傳遞震波的介質。 
1988 年，Lee et al. [7]分別利用 CO2 及
Nd:YAG 雷射來移除固體表面的微粒雜質，雷
射能量會使固體表面的水急速氣化，產生強烈
的震爆能量，其力量足以移除微粒雜質。 
(3)背面-乾式雷射清潔

2.4 表面微粒粉末附著方式 
本文使用JIS粒徑規格番號800之氧化鋁粉
末，其粒徑大小為15m，使其附著於玻璃基
板。先將氧化鋁粉末與純水以5：1的比例進行
調配後，均勻塗佈於玻璃基板；再以50C烘烤
180分鐘，如此氧化鋁粉末與玻璃基板間會有較
高的附著度。 
微粒粉末附著於基板之附著力，影響到雷
射移除粉末的效率。因此，必須預先針對微粒
粉末的附著程度，進行量測實驗。 
本文利用真空泵浦對玻璃基板進行表面微
粒吸附實驗。真空泵浦噴嘴置於玻璃基板上方
後進行抽氣，如圖 2-4 所示。以真空泵浦之壓
力值代表粉末之附著程度。噴嘴高度(噴嘴出口
與基板之距離)為 0.1mm，在抽氣過程中壓力值
到達 32cm Hg 時，噴嘴範圍內氧化鋁粉末約五
秒可被真空泵浦完全抽離，增加抽氣時間並不
會擴大可抽離範圍。

The above mentioned technique of controlled fracture is essentially a laser breaking method based 
on the stable fracture principle. The quality of laser-cut surfaces is much better than the quality of 
mechanically broken surfaces. Unlike the controlled fracture method, Tsai and Shiu [7] proposed an 
alternate method based on an unstable fracture. In their method, a diamond blade is used to scribe a 
groove along the desired cutting path in an alumina substrate. A defocused CO2 laser is applied to the 
substrate surface. As the laser moves a short distance, a short crack is generated through the edge of 
the substrate. When the laser direction is reversed, a great thermal stress is induced at the crack tip 
causing the substrate to break along the scribed line. 
Tsai and Chang [8] proposed a pursed laser breaking technique, a technique that further builds on 
the unstable fracture technique. A diamond-point scoring tool was used to scribe a grooved-crack 
along the cutting path in a glass substrate. A pulsed CO2 laser with a circular spot was then applied at 
the middle of the cutting path to separate the glass substrate. A crack developed in the grooved-crack 
after the first laser pulse, increased in length after the second pulse, and then extended unstably after the 
third.  
The aim of this paper is to propose an alternative laser breaking technique that also builds on the 
unstable fracture technique. But unlike the method of Tsai and Chang [8], the laser used was a CW CO2 
laser with a line-shape beam. The length of the line-shape was adjusted to the length of the desired 
cutting path. Furthermore, the mechanical pre-bending device proposed by Tsai and Lin [5], 
pre-scribing the substrate and the water cooling assistance were integrated applied to this technique to 
enhance the cutting quality. 
The machining parameters, i.e. laser power, length of the line-shape beam, and the cutting quality 
were discussed. The effects of the mechanical pre-bending, the pre-scribing, and the cooling assistance 
were also examined. The breaking fracture mechanism of glass is different from that of stable fracture 
technique. The phenomena of glass separation and the fracture extension process were analyzed. The 
microstructure of the fracture surface was observed using scanning electron microscopy (SEM). 
Principle of Laser Percussion Breaking 
Materials and Breaking Process. The experimental specimens were TFT-LCD Eagle2000 glass 
substrates produced by the Corning Company (Taiwan). The glass substrates are cut to the size of 
100100 0.5 mm3 and 50100 0.5 mm3. 
Figure 1 illustrates the laser percussion breaking process. The line-shape laser beam was applied at 
the desired cutting path of the substrate. The length of the laser beam is regulated to equal the length of 
the desired cutting path, i.e. the length of the substrate. The laser energy creates two separating frontier, 
i.e. crack tips, in the two reverse edges of the substrate. The crack tips will be extending unstably, 
causing the material to split into two pieces. Great tensile stress is generated along the desired 
separation plane and induces the substrate broken for an instant. 
Line-Shape Laser Beam. Because the softening temperature of the glass is low, the temperature 
increase due to the applied laser energy must be low enough. If the laser beam is focused on a narrow 
spot, the laser energy will concentrate on a local area and induce a high temperature. Therefore, the 
laser spot size must be large enough to limit the temperature and prevent the softening effect. 
The Gaussian energy profile is an elliptical shape. The laser beam must be transferred from circular 
shape to an elliptical shape. The beam delivery system is composed of a cylinder lens and a 
concave-planar lens as shown in Fig. 2. The Gaussian energy profile is flattened out to a narrow strip, 
i.e. similar to a line-shape. The length of longitudinal major axis is far greater than that of the transverse 
minor axis. The longitudinal major axis must be regulated to the length of the desired cutting path. The 
lengths of the major and minor axes are 100 mm and 3 mm for the substrate length of 100 mm,

Summary 
The laser percussion breaking technique with the assistances of pre-bending, pre-scribing and cooling 
was successfully applied to the cutting of LCD glass substrates. The mechanism of the breaking 
technique has been thoroughly examined in this study. It was found that the crack initiates at the edge 
of the substrate, and then extends along the desired separation path to the other end. The pre-bending 
assistance is the most important method to enhance the breaking performance. The surface quality 
along the cutting path is very good, and better than that from mechanical breaking. The methodology 
proposed in this study provides an opportunity to improve technologies for diamond scribing and laser 
breaking of LCD glass substrates
