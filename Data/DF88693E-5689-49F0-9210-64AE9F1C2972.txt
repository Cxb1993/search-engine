1廢棄堇青石結合改質奈米零價鐵再利用以微波誘導降解鹵素
有機物之研究
摘要
本研究以微米鐵、奈米零價鐵(商用與製備)、奈米零價銅以及銅鐵雙金屬為介質，
於微波照射下對降解氯苯水溶液活化能，降解速率以及反應機制之影響。於微波照射條
件下所有類型介質較沒有微波照射可以提高對氯苯水溶液降解速率佳及降低活化能。結果
顯示，在微波功率 250 W，照射時間 150 sec 誘導下微米鐵、商用奈米零價鐵、製備奈米
零價鐵對氯苯去除效率分別提升 13.4 (61.2% vs. 4.6%)、3.6 (65.4% vs. 18.1%)及 2.8
(76.5% vs. 27.5%)倍，活化能分別降低 34.0%、16.1%、15.1%。由於微波(Microwave, MW)
照射穿透溶液到達金屬表面，零價鐵吸收 MW 能量，促進零價鐵表面氧化，增加表面活性
位置，因而提升 CB 去除效率，降低活化能。因此以 MW 幅射處理含氯有機污染物是一個
高效益處理技術。
.
關鍵字: 微波誘導； 活化能； 零價鐵； 氯苯
3一、前言
工業上應用鹵素有機化合物已有數十年時間，如四氯乙烯(tetrachloroethylene, PCE)和三
氯乙烯(trichloroethylene, TCE)廣泛用於金屬表面油質之去除、乾洗劑與溶劑[1-3]，而氯酚
應用於木材防腐劑、殺蟲劑及殺菌劑[4-6]。含氯有機物污染物之處理技術包括：熱處理方
法、生物分解法、高等氧化化學法、光裂解法及微波降解法等等。然而、上述處理方法各
有其所需克服問題，如熱處理法，通常處理溫度需要高於1000 oC及產生更具毒性副產物[2,
3]；生物分解法必須有良好控制條件與需要較長降解時間[1]。因此、發展高去除效率、無
毒性、低成本、操作容易之處理技術[3] 是現在全球關注焦點。
零價鐵金屬做為含氯有機化合物之廢水或地下水脫氯還原反應的電子來源，經由這幾
年研究顯示是有效率。奈米零價金屬漸漸應廣泛應用在環境領域，於污染整治上應用極具
優勢。然而，零價鐵實際應用上有幾項不利之處包括表面活性維持很困難，短時間反應表
面形成氧化層及不同來源的Fe0反應度有顯著的變動等等須克服問題[7, 8] ]。
MW 屬於電磁輻射其頻率範圍介於 300MHz-300GHz[9]，一般家庭與工業 MW 通常操
作頻率在 2.54 GHz[10, 11]，相當波長 12.2 cm 及 1.02×10-5 eV 能量，為非游離輻射。MW 處
理系統中材料可分為三類：絕緣、導體及吸收體，吸收 MW 輻射的物質稱之介電體(dielectrics)
[12]。因此 MW 加熱也可稱為介電體加熱，然而、並不是所有物質皆可被 MW 迅速加熱，
而分子結構會影響物質與 MW 交互作用及傳遞能量之能力，當 MW 與不同介電體特性物質
接觸時，MW 將選擇性與比較高的耗損物質偶合[13]，所以物質介電體性質與溫度、溼度、
物質密度及幾何形狀有關[9, 14]。MW 技術近年來已應用於環境工程方面，Jou 應用微波處
理含 Cr 與 Pb 金屬污染土壤、含 PCP 與 TCE 廢水[9, 14, 15]。
本研究主要研究目的為運用零價鐵高氧化活性特性結合微波，探討於微波輻射電磁場下
對降低降解氯苯活化能之影響。研發亦符合環保、再生與經濟效益，對含鹵素有機物的整
治具有顯著的貢獻技術。

5三、結果與討論
比表面積對 CB 降解及活化能之影響
還原反應過程顆粒尺寸扮演重要角色，較小顆粒具有更多活性位置以及顆粒尺寸減少其
特定表面積增加，將可提供更多與污染物質接觸面積[6]。過去幾年，多數研究強調零價鐵
比表面積效應，一致性結論認為以零價鐵進行氯化有機物，乾淨的比表面積數量影響降解
速率。然而、根據最近幾年研究結果零價鐵的新鮮度對反應數率比表面積有較大影響，因
此影響零價鐵降解主要因素包括污染物質化學性質、零價鐵來源、品質、純度、可利用表
面積濃度以及新鮮度等因素皆顯著影響零價鐵降解反應速率[17, 18]。由於反應位置、雜質、
結構與形態學等等會有不同活化能。所以零價鐵雜質與形態學是影響污染物質降解速率主
要因素[18]。而奈米顆粒特性包括單位體積高表面面積比率[19]、高水平表面路徑(表面路徑
也稱之為高米勒指數，那通常有一個路徑結構) [20]及高表面積能量[21]相較於微米鐵。
於濃度 100 mg/L CB 溶液 40 mL 含鐵氟龍墊片中孔螺旋蓋之茶色瓶，分別加入 1 g 的微
米級鐵粉、奈米級製備與商用零價鐵，置入恆溫槽內加熱，改變恆溫槽溫度分別為 25 oC、
40 oC、50 oC 及 60 oC。靜置時間為 30、60、… 及 240 分鐘，其分別粒徑及表面積彙整於
表一，比較不同粒徑及表面積對降解速率與活化能之影響。以反應溫度 60 oC 說明。圖 2
結果顯示，反應溫度 60 oC，在相同反應時間(240 min)，微米鐵粉、奈米製備與商用零價鐵，
去除率分別為 33.3%、55.2%及 42.6%，速率常數分別為 1.5 × 10-3 min-1、3.3 × 10-3 min-1
及 2.3 × 10-3 min-1，正常化表面積反應速率常數(surface area normalized reaction rate constant,
ksa) 分別為 6.3 × 10-5 (L/min‧m-2)、3.0 × 10-6 (L/min‧m-2) 及 1.7 × 10-6 (L/min‧m-2)。
由表 4-1 資料顯示三者表面積大小依序為商用奈米鐵>製備奈米鐵>微米鐵，然而去除率及
反應速率常數依序為製備奈米鐵>商用奈米鐵>微米鐵。
雖然商用奈米鐵粒徑及表面積大於製備奈米鐵，理當有較大表面積與污染物接觸，然而
實驗觀察到因顆粒容易聚結以致於溶液中多數沉澱於底層，減少與污染物接觸面積，又因
奈米零價表面活性維持很困難，既使貯存於適度條件之下，對污染物反應度的降低是不可
避免。使用製備新鮮奈米鐵是未經烘乾濕奈米零價鐵，由於較少表面氧化或表面污染，鐵
表面能有維持較佳表面活性一個”新鮮度”金屬表面與污染物質接處而有高反應性，且零
價鐵顆粒膨鬆容易散佈於溶液中，增加與 CB 接觸而提高去除率。Cheng and Wu [17]以 10-40
mesh 未曾開封顆粒鐵及已開封幾個月鐵粉末進行 TCE 降解反應，結果顯示已開封鐵粉末
降解 TCE 經 250 小時之後，對降解反應增進幾乎沒有影響。由於零價鐵具有高氧化特性，
所以零價鐵表面”新鮮度”也是影響去除率變數之ㄧ。Hara et al., [22]以四種不同類型鐵粉
(比表面積分別為 3.9、0.47、0.2 及 0.13 m2/g)，比較溶液中每單位質量脫氯反應速率，結果
顯示較小比表面面積鐵粉，提供較多接觸活性位置，因而有較高脫氯反應速率。Lien and
Zhang [23]以微米級與製備奈米級零價鐵做為比較對四氯乙烯降解速率，觀察到微米級鐵沉
澱於瓶底，而製備奈米級鐵是散佈在溶液中，由於四氯乙烯傳遞或擴散到沉澱於瓶底微米
級能力低，致使微米級降解速率低於製備奈米級鐵。
7微波輻射不同表面積對 CB 降解及活化能之影響
物體的表面積及形狀對該物體吸收微波能源的能力有相當的影響。一般而言，帶有尖角
的物體較能吸收微波；物體暴露的表面積越大，吸收的微波越多。將濃度 100 mg/L CB 溶
液 50 mL 置入石英玻璃反應中, 分別加入 1 g 的微米鐵粉、奈米製備與商用零價鐵，直接
置入微波爐內,輸出功率 250 W。微波照射操作條件：微波輻射 10 秒，暫停微波間隔 120
秒為一循環，共循環 15 次，總微波輻射時間 150 秒。比較微波電磁輻射不同粒徑及表面積
對降解速率與活化能之影響。圖 3 結果顯示，於相同微波輻射時間(150 秒)，微米鐵粉、奈
米製備與商用零價鐵，去除率分別為 61.2%、76.5%及 65.4%。由表 1 資料顯示三者粒表面
積大小依序為商用奈米鐵>製備奈米鐵>微米鐵，然而去除率大小依序為製備奈米鐵>商用奈
米鐵>微米鐵。雖然商用奈米鐵粒表面積大於製備奈米鐵，理當有表面積較大吸收的微波能
量越多，然而因多數顆粒聚結沉澱於溶液底層，減少微波能量的吸收，以致其去除率較製
備奈米鐵低。使用製備奈米鐵是未經烘乾濕奈米零價鐵，因此零價鐵顆粒膨鬆容易散佈於
溶液中，有大面積可吸收較多微波能量，且吸收微波能量轉變成熱能於整個溶液中較均勻。
先前已印證零價鐵流體化及未流體化溶液活化能之影響，結果顯示零價鐵流體化於溶液其
去除率可提高 12.1%，活化能可降低 14.5%。微波電磁輻射微米鐵粉、奈米製備與商用零價
鐵，活化能分別為 32.7 kJ/mol、18.6 kJ/mol 及 18.3 kJ/mol。依據上述活化能數據，微米鐵

氯還原反應，質量擴散是限制步驟。因此經由微波輻射較無微波(25 oC 常溫降解)微米鐵粉、

附件一
Combining Zero-Valent Iron Nanoparticles with Microwave Energy to Treat
Chlorobenzene
ABSTRACT
In this research, MW energy is combined with nano-scale zero-valent iron (ZVI or Fe0)
particles suspended in aqueous chlorobenzene (CB) solution for enhancing the CB decomposition.
When MW energy is applied at 250 W for 300 sec to irradiate 100 mg/L of CB solution
containing 1 g ZVI particles, the CB activation energy is decreased by 8.6 kJ/mol (21.9 to 13.3
kJ/mol) so that the CB removal efficiency is enhanced 4.6 times (82.8 vs. 18.1%). The reductive
dechlorination reaction occurs at the surface of zero-valent iron particles; the organic substance in
the bulk of the solution diffuses to the iron particle surface to contact the iron particle for the
reductive dechlorination reaction to occur. When MW radiation penetrates the solution to reach
the surface of suspended Fe0 particles, the radiation accelerates the iron oxidization, and increases
the surface activity site thus enhancing the CB decomposition rate. Hence the MW-induced
zero-valent iron can reduce the CB activation energy that contributes to the enhanced rate of CB
removal.
Keywords: Chlorobenzene; Activation-energy (Ea); Microwave radiation; Zero-valent iron.
15
MW radiation is a form of electromagnetic radiation with frequency ranging from 100 MHz
to 300 GHz (Tai and Jou, 1999). The electric field interacts with polar materials, and the
magnetic field reacts with the charged material. Through the interaction between the medium
molecules and the MW electromagnetic field, the electromagnetic energy is converted into heat
energy directly in the dielectric medium. The MW heating has several advantages over traditional
heating such as lower energy consumption, shorter treatment time (Venkatesh and Raghavan,
2004), more efficient heating, no direct contact between the heat source and the material being
heated, selective heating, smaller heating equipment, and less wastes generated (Clark et al., 2000;
Mavrogianopoulos et al., 2000). In recent years, the MW technology has been applied for
treating chlorinated organic pollutants with rapid reaction rates and high removal efficiencies
(Jou, 1998, 2006; Jou and Tai,1998; Coss and Cha, 2000).
The objective of this study is to combine the high reactivity of zero-valent iron particles and
the rapid heating of microwave as a new method for treating aqueous chlorobenzene solution.
The zero-valent iron particles are suspended in aqueous chlorobenzene solution as MW dielectric
media in this study to generate heat from microwave irradiation for dechlorinating the
chlorobenzene dissolved in the solution. The influence of MW irradiation on CB activation
energy, solution temperature, and removal efficiency is investigated.
MATERIALS AND METHODS
Materials
The chlorobenzene solution was prepared by dissolving 905 μL of chlorobenzene (99.9%
purity GR Reagent, TEDIA, USA) in methanol (99.9% purity, GR Reagent, TEDIA, USA) with
the final concentration of the stock solution adjusted to 20000 mg/L. The working solution was
prepared by dissolving250 μL of the stock solution in de-ionized water(18.2 MΩ, Milipore Co, 
USA) to a final concentration of 100 mg/L chlorobenzene. The commercial nanoscale zero-valent
iron particles (ZVI, 99.9% purity, Conyuan Biochemical Technology Ltd. Company) have 55.8
m2/g specific surface area as determined using the BET method (Beckman Co., SA-300).
A household microwave oven operated at 2.45 GHz with a maximum power of 750 W was
used for generating the microwave energy. It was modified by installing a programmable PID
(proportional integral derivative) controller. The MW energy was controlled by programming
the PID (Proportional-Integral- Derivative) controller to adjust the PID feedback signal for
maintaining a constant pre-selected MW energy level. The column reactor, which holds the CB
solution to be treated, was made of low-energy-loss boron-silica glass; it was placed in the
microwave oven to carry out the experiment.

Increasing the ZVI dosage from 1 g to 2 g will further enhance the CB removal efficiency 1.3
times (24.0 vs. 18.1%). However, the reduction of ZVI leads to the production of iron oxide

Influence of Temperature
Temperature is an important parameter to influence the reaction rate; chemical processes
tend to be more temperature-dependent than physical processes (Su and Puls, 1999). The
activation energy (Ea) is a parameter to determine whether a process is temperature dependent;
Arrhenius equation is used to relate the reaction rate constant to temperature (Su and Puls, 1999;
Lien and Zhang, 2007):
ln ksa (or k) = ln A - Ea/RT (1)
Where Ea is activation energy (kJ/mol), R is the molar gas constant (0.008314 kJ/mol K), k
is the observed rate constant (1/h) and ksa is the surface area normalized reaction rate constant

The activation energy for the transformation of CB was measured in a closed batch system

Influence of MW Irradiation on CB Decomposition with Addition of Nanoscale ZVI
The MW irradiation will induce ion migration and dipole rotation causing the medium
molecules to vibrate thus bringing about thermal effect (Venkatesh and Raghavan, 2004;
Appleton et al., 2005; Sku et al., 2001). The radiation will further weaken the strength of various
chemical bonds and reduce the activation energy of the organic substance irradiated (Zhang et al.,
2007). Moreover, the existence of microwave absorbent dielectric materials can enhance these
effects. If only 250 W MW is applied for 300 sec, Fig. 3 reveals that the CB removal efficiency is
raised from 27.8% (blank MW) to 82.8%, and activation energy is reduced from 22.1 kJ/mol
(blank MW) to 13.3 kJ/mol.

CONCLUSION
In this research, laboratory data demonstrate that MW energy enhances the capacity of
nano-scale ZVI in decomposing CB, and raise the CB removal efficiency by decreasing the CB
activation energy. When MW radiation penetrates the CB solution to reach the surface of
suspended zero-iron particles, it enhances the generation of nascent hydrogen, and increases
surface activity site, thus enhancing the CB decomposition rate. If 250 W of MW irradiation is
applied for 300 sec to the CB solution mixed with 1 g ZVI, the CB removal efficiency increases
to 82.8% with average 460.3 W/cm3 power absorbed to lower the activation energy to 13.3
kJ/mol. Using only ZVI without MW irradiation for 240 min, about 18.1% CB is removal
efficiency with a higher activation energy (18.9 kJ/mol for temperature ranging from 25 to 60 oC).
Hence the MW-induced zero-valent iron can effectively reduce the CB activation energy in
addition to increasing the rate of CB removal.
