精進質子交換膜燃料電池膜電極組製備以提升電池效能 
 
 
尹庚鳴*、黃勝杰 
元智大學化工與材料科學系 
國科會編號：NSC 95-2221-E-155-080 
 
摘要 -- 本 研 究 目 的 在 於 膜 電 極 組 的 精 進 , 探 討 添 加 微 孔 層
(micro-porous layer, 簡稱 MPL)對於質子交換模燃料電池(PEMFC)的影響；實驗
結果顯示，在碳紙上添加一層微孔層，可減少陰極水氾濫，使得電池性能提升；
當微孔層中碳負載量為 0.5mg/cm2 與 C:FEP＝1:0.7，會有最佳的燃料電池性能，
因為較薄的微孔層與良好的疏水能有效的改善氣體擴散。在 FEP 含量方面，FEP
含量太多會使孔隙減少，進而影響到氣體的傳送，FEP 含量太少則無法在陰極有
效的移除水分。在碳紙上添加一層微孔層，可將反應在陰極所產生的水順利的排
出，避免因為陰極的水氾濫而阻礙氣體的擴散；當陰極進料為空氣時，在高的陰
極增濕溫度更容易看出微孔層的作用。 
關鍵字：燃料電池、微孔層、膜電極組、水泛濫
1. 前言 
  能源是發展國家經濟最重要的基
礎，擁有不虞匱乏的能源即是國家競
爭力與地位提升的保證。然而漫無止
境的開採，已使得人類所賴以生存的
能源蘊藏量銳減，同時傳統能源消耗
所帶來的環境污染問題更是遠大於其
所帶來的便利。綜觀現在開發中的新
能源裝置，燃料電池因其具有高發電
效率、低污染、易攜帶等優點，很可
能是繼水力、火力、核能之後，成為
第四種主要的電力來源。在質子交換
膜燃料電池中，因為陰極的還原反應
而產生液態水，常會阻塞氣體擴散層 
中氧氣的傳輸，使得陰極的還原反應
速率受限，而使電池性能下降。為了
避免水的氾濫而阻礙氧氣的輸送，我 
們在氣體擴散層與觸媒層之間，添加
一層具有疏水效果的微孔層，期盼可
以解決此一水管理的問題。

Analytical equations of a liquid feed direct methanol fuel 
cell are derived to correlate the literature experimental 
discharge data to the theoretical predicted polarization 
curve. The mass transport of methanol and oxygen in flow 
channels and porous carbon cloth diffusion layers of the 
respective anode and cathode compartments are described 
in association with the prescribed electrochemical 
reactions. The influence of methanol crossover through 
the membrane is accounted, and the effect of pressure 
difference between cathode and anode is included. The 
influence of permeated methanol on the cathode potential 
is justified using the mixed potential theory. The 
concentration depletions of methanol and oxygen along 
their respective flow channels can be predicted with 
simple algebraic formulas. Further, non-linear regression 
scheme is imposed on the model so that important kinetic 
parameters can be retrieved.

Abstract 
Algebraic equations of a liquid feed direct methanol fuel cell (DMFC) with 
co-current channel flow are derived to correlate the literature experimental discharge 
data with the theoretically predicted polarization curve. Mass conservation of 
methanol and oxygen normal to the membrane electrode assembly is derived, and an 
averaging procedure over the channel length is carried out to give the overall cell 
performance. The influence of methanol crossover through the membrane is 
accounted as the result of diffusion, electro-osmotic drag, and the pressure difference 
between anode and cathode. The influence of permeated methanol on the cathode 
potential is illustrated with the mixed potential theory. That is, the parasitic methanol 
oxidation consumes part of the oxygen reduction current in the cathode catalyst layer, 
that is, forming an internal short circuit, so that the resultant cell current density is 
decreased compared to the situation without crossover. The model is capable of 
predicting the concentration depletions of methanol and oxygen within the porous 
diffusion media of the membrane electrode assembly, and that along the respective 
flow channels. Nonlinear least square scheme is coupled with the algebraic model 
retrieving the parameters of methanol diffusivity in the anode backing layer, exchange 
current density of methanol on catalyst surface, and proton conductivity in the 
polymer electrolyte membrane. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Key words: Direct methanol fuel cell, algebraic model, mixed potential theory, 
crossover, flow channel.

It has to be mentioned that all the aforementioned semi-empirical models still require 
the numerical regression procedure for each set of the experimental I-V data to obtain 
empirical parameters, which may not be applicable to extensive operating region. A 
mechanistic model developed by Guo and Ma [12] includes the species mass transport 
within the individual porous medium of the membrane electrode assembly. This 
model is useful for the identification of various rate limiting steps in a cell, although 
the open circuit voltage seems over-estimated. Many computing intensive simulations 
with consideration of methanol crossover and channel flow effect are available 
[13-15]; they are perhaps too complicated to be implemented for rapid parameter 
estimation of the experimental data. The purpose of present work is to develop an 
analytical model that includes important physical features while simple enough to 
retrieve kinetic parameters for system identification. 
2 The algebraic model 
The model region is sketched in Figure 1 with the following assumptions: 
• The cell is isothermal and operated in the steady state. 
• No methanol and oxygen concentration variations are considered in the direction 
normal to the MEA face within the respective anode and cathode catalyst layers due to 
the thinness of such layers. 
• Plug flow is fulfilled in either anode or cathode flow channel. 
• Due to the large over-potential driven, the crossover of methanol is to be consumed 
instantaneously at the membrane/cathode catalyst layer interface [16]. 
• Mass transport is described by the effective diffusion mechanism through anode or 
cathode diffuser.  
• Fully flooded anode channel, anode backing layer, anode catalyst layer, polymer 
electrolyte membrane, and cathode catalyst layer are assumed; however, gaseous 
oxygen is diffused within the cathode diffuser.

3 Results and Discussion 
Figure 2 is a comparison of model simulation with literature data [21]. It is seen 
that the limiting current density is arrived much earlier and closer to the OCV as 
lower methanol concentration is used in the feed. At the highest methanol feed 
concentration of 0.625M, the mass transfer limitation is deferred and the largest range 
of discharge rate is obtained. Accompanied in the same figure is the corresponding 
methanol crossover rate expressed in leaking current density leakI . Highest methanol 
crossover is observed at the open circuit voltage where the diffusion of methanol 
across the membrane the highest. Leaking current density vanishes as the cell limiting 
current density is approached, in which methanol in the anode catalyst layer is 
depleted. Apparently, anodic process limits the cell performance.

is clear that cathode polarization is much less than that in the anode electrode. Note

Contour plots of the methanol concentrations in the anode catalyst layer and the 
channel are shown in Figure 5. The Figure is sampled at a feed concentration of 
0.375M as a function of channel length and cell voltages. It is seen that except that 
close to the inlet and near the open circuit voltage, methanol concentration in the 
anode catalyst layer is uniformly distributed along the channel length. The methanol 
concentration within the anode flow channel is decreased along the conduit as a result 
of fuel consumption. The decline of methanol concentration is more significant at low 
cell voltage or when limiting current density is approached. 
4 Conclusions 
A simple algebraic model on the direct methanol fuel cell is presented, which 
accounts the respective anodic and cathodic electrochemical reactions, the associated 
mass transport through the porous diffusion media, and the mixed-potential effect on 
cathode induced by methanol crossover. In addition, the respective anode and cathode 
channel flow effects are included in the work. This model is based on the mass 
conservation in the normal direction of the MEA while averaged along the channel 
length. The calculation procedure is efficient and parameter estimation scheme can be 
incorporated in the model to retrieve important system properties. Although algebraic 
formulation is intended for simplicity, this work is virtually a pseudo-two dimensional 
model.
