Abstract 
In the house cases of Southeast Asia, two 
basic types of wood construction systems 
may be categorized as “horizontal stacking” 
and “vertical trussing”. In “horizontal 
stacking” system, columns with equal height 
are built together to form an invisible 
platform, on which the roof was constructed. 
Horizontal platform offering a transformation 
interface is the general rule, as well as the 
rule of construction members stacking to 
form the roof structure. In “vertical trussing” 
system, columns with different heights are 
arrayed and combined with short beams to 
form a single truss, and several trusses are 
juxtaposed to construct a house’s framework 
with roofing covered. “Encroached 
construction” is another featured option for 
some Southeast Asian houses, always 
accompanied with “horizontal stacking”. 
However, Han’s house types adopt “vertical 
trussing” without any “encroached 
construction”.  
This study will present nine house types 
adopted “horizontal stacking” system, three 
adopted “vertical trussing” system. All cases 
chosen will be analyzed through construction 
process from 3D-modeling. From the 
viewpoint of basic types of wood 
construction, this study re-examines the 
house types appeared in the wide regions. 
The creativity of roof form and the 
adaptability of house scale will be proved to 
connect with the basic concepts of 
construction system.

第四章 「水平堆疊」與「垂直間架」構
造系統之比較 
4.1. 兩種不同的構造觀念   
  「水平堆疊」與「垂直間架」兩種構造
觀念的出發點不同。前者的柱子貫穿生活
空間層與屋頂層，後者的柱子僅及於生活
空間層，屋頂層是另外一套系統。「垂直
間架」在思考建造一棟民居時，柱子已被
設想成是直接承載屋頂的構件，只要經由
少數水平構件的連結便完成承載屋頂的任
務。垂直向承載系統由垂直柱與水平構件
的連結視為「桁架化」(trussing)的過程，
因此本研究將之命名為「垂直間架」。「水
平堆疊」構造觀念以 Fataluku 民居最具代
表性，該民居由下而上可分成三個部分: 下
部空間  (leg), 生活空間  (body), 屋頂 
(head)，body 疊在 leg 之上，head 疊在 body
之上，有兩個水平轉換介面。在建造下部
結構時，外人無法想像其上部結構；等高
的柱子創造出一個水平轉換介面，轉換介
面之上與之下的構造元素幾乎沒有關聯。
水平轉換介面之上，各種水平構件
(crossbeam, tie-beam)、垂直構件(post)恰似
以疊積木的方式逐次把屋頂構造完成，故
本研究將之命名為「水平堆疊」。(附錄 1.8) 
 
4.2. 屋頂造型的原創性 
    「垂直間架」的屋頂造型幾乎是固定
的，中國的抬梁式構造、台灣的穿斗式構
造民居與遠在蘇門答臘 Minangkabau house
的屋頂都是 gable roof，造型原型相同，差
別在於材料不同，尺度與比例不同，屋面
起翹方式不同，各種裝飾有別。假如放眼
世界，凡是屬於「垂直間架」構造概念的
屋頂造型、甚或房屋造型應該都相似。「水
平堆疊」構造觀念衍生的屋頂造型變化極
大，蓋因水平轉換介面之上的屋頂可以自
由發揮，無需被下部結構所限制。南島語
族興建許多船型屋頂，應該是具備這種構
造觀念所致。然而此種構造觀念並非南島
語族所獨有，泰國民居的建造者就不是南
島語族，構造觀念的傳播與分享是另一研
究課題。

Two Basic Types of  Wood Constructions in East Asian Traditional Houses: 
Horizontal Stacking and Vertical Trussing 
 
 
Chen, Yao-Ru ; Pan, Chung-Pin 
 
Associate Professor, Department of  Architecture, Cheng Shiu University, Taiwan. E-mail: yaoru.chen@gmail.com 
Graduate Student, Department of  Architecture, Cheng Shiu University, Taiwan 
 
 
Abstract 
Ethnic groups of  East Asia have built diversified house types for thousand years. Many brilliant house types can 
be seen in Southeast Asia, Japan and China. Every single house type was built with talented concepts and skills, 
which inspires our curiosity and imagination. 
In the cases we surveyed and studied, two basic types of  wood construction systems may be categorized as 
“horizontal stacking” and “vertical trussing”. In “horizontal stacking” system, columns with equal height are 
built together to form an invisible platform, on which the roof  was constructed. Horizontal platform offering a 
transformation interface is the general rule, as well as the rule of  construction members stacking to form the 
roof  structure. In “vertical trussing” system, columns with different heights are arrayed and combined with short 
beams to form a single truss, and several trusses are juxtaposed to construct a house’s framework with roofing 
covered. “Encroached construction” is another featured option for some Southeast Asian houses, always 
accompanied with “horizontal stacking”. However, Han’s house types adopt “vertical trussing” without any 
“encroached construction”.  
This study will present six house types adopted “horizontal stacking” system, three adopted “vertical trussing” 
system. All cases chosen will be analyzed through construction process from 3D-modeling. From the viewpoint 
of  basic types of  wood construction, this study re-examines the house types appeared in the wide regions. The 
creativity of  roof  form and the adaptability of  house scale will be proved to connect with the basic concepts of  
construction system. 
 
Keywords: house type, house construction, South East Asia house, horizontal stacking, vertical trussing, encroachment. 
 
 
 
1. Introduction 
House type study can be commenced from two aspects: demands and production. As the title of  Oliver’s book 
“Built to meet needs” (Oliver, 2006) implies, houses are to meet the habitants’ needs under particular 
geographical environment and climatic condition. The characteristics of  living lead to particular spatial layout 
and symbolic decoration; and spatial layout reflects the demands of  habitants fully. The production aspect of  
houses involves the ways they are built, with factors encompassing construction system of  a single house, 
expansion of  a single house, formation of  a house grouping that meets the need of  raised habitant population. 
  
3 
 
2. Method 
House construction study contains three levels: construction concept, construction system, and construction 
details. In a large region, the variety in ethnic groups results in different house types, which develop their own 
construction systems. The different construction systems might originate from the same basic type of  
construction. The basic type of  construction is a construction concept or a set of  operational principles. Based 
on the basic type, the construction concept leads to unique construction system with originality. The 
construction systems of  house type derived from different construction concepts might be diverse. The 
manufacture of  construction details or the development of  sub-system might be derived from the construction 
system afterwards. This study focuses on the investigation of  construction concept and construction system. 
Construction details are not yet discussed.(Fig.3) 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Research on traditional architectural construction is mostly assisted with section and perspective. The earlier 
construction research on Buddhist pagoda, Chinese palaces and houses were described with the section and 
perspective by Si-Cheng Liang and Duen-Zeng Liu (Liu, 1982). Funo Shuji analyzed and induced the 
construction systems of  Southeast Asia with sections in his “世界住居誌”. (Funo, 2005) In study of  Southeast 
Asia houses, construction systems are also described with section or isometric. (Nasir, 1995; Lim, 1987; Saito, 
1991; Oliver, 1997) However, section, perspective, or isometric are the ultimate presentation of  construction 
systems, instead of  description of  construction process. Thus researchers or readers cannot well understand the 
construction process of  certain house. Since the introduction of  SketchUp software, the drawing of  perspectives 
and the making of  3D-Models of  architectures have been simplified and facilitated. We collected variations of  
house type in East and Southeast Asia, built up 3D-models of  their construction system with SketchUp, 
deconstructed the 3D-models and figured out the construction process, and completely comprehended the 
construction system from the overall appearances and construction processes of  the houses. The construction 
concepts were extracted by analyzing and comparing various construction systems. Exploring the relationship 
between construction concept and construction system becomes the subject of  this study.

Minangkabau House Construction, Sumatra  
In the case of  Minangkabau House (Chen, 2008), five columns are established longitudinally first to construction 
the floor system as well as to connect with TB1 and TB2. Transversely, it is connected by CB1, CB2, and CB3, and 
the roof  ridges (RG) raised on the two ends. Finally, the roofing system is applied. (Fig.6) 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Summary 
The three construction systems, Tai-Liang, Chuan-Dow, and Minangkabau, all contain columns of  various 
heights, which decide the appearances of  gable roofs. The truss is formed by columns of  different heights 
vertically, thus the construction concept of  such construction system is named “vertical trussing”. 
The two construction systems, Chuan-Dow and Minangkabau, have almost the same column span longitudinally. 
All cross beams are placed on top of  the columns after all the tie beams are positioned  
The spans between columns situated longitudinally in Tai-Liang are various, with larger spans in the middle and 
narrower spans on two sides. Tie beam and cross beam have two layers of  systems each, which are differentiated 
with TB,tb/ CB,cb in this study. Unlike Chuan-Dow or Minangkabau, all tie beams are finished first before all 
cross beams are placed. The construction process of  Tai-Liang is (tb1, tb2), (cb1, cb2), (TB1, TB2), (CB1, CB2), cb3, 
TB3, CB3. The beam system is completed with interwoven tie beams longitudinally and cross beams transversely.

Batak Toba House Construction, Sumatra  
Fig.11 illustrates a Batak Toba house construction. The 16 columns (C1) are about 3.2 meter high. There are also 
26 short pillars in the lower part. Each C1 and short pillars are connected by tie beams, forming a stable lower 
construction to support the floor. When entering the house from the entrance, two five-column rows on the two 
sides can be seen. The column bracket (Bk) is fixed on top of  C1. Each column bracket on each C1 row supports 
two beams of  different functions, with a cross beam (CB1) inside and circular-section beam outside (labe-labe). 
The beams (2CB1 and 2TB1) surround the house and stabilize the column system, while there is only one 
labe-labe placed on each side for the purpose of  fixing the roof  shape and external wall. A mezzanine platform 
(MP) is constructed on the surrounding beams (2CB1 and 2TB) in the front- and back-ends of  the house. On 
front- and back-platform, gables (Gb1, Gb2) are fixed and balanced by cables hanging outwards through two

An initial conclusion obtained from the analysis is as follows: 
Different Beginning Point of  Two Concepts 
The beginning point of  the two construction concepts, “Vertical trussing” and “Horizontal stacking,” are 
different. The columns of  the former are arranged throughout the living space and roof  space, whereas the latter 
only on the living space and the roof  space is another system. In “vertical trussing,” columns are considered as 
direct roof-carrying elements already in thinking stage. Only a few more horizontal elements are required for 
connection until it is able to carry the roof. In a vertical supporting system, the connection of  vertical columns 
and horizontal elements is considered as the procedure of  "trussing", thus the name “vertical trussing” in this 
study. Fataluku house is the most representative type in the construction concept of  “horizontal stacking.” The 
three parts of  the house follow in the bottom-up order of  leg (lower space), body (living space), and head (roof). 
Body is stacked on top of  leg, and head on top of  body, forming two horizontal transformation interfaces. The

people, who have higher family population and thus developed house grouping to meet the needs of  the big

Here emerges an interesting question: Why has Han people, user of  Tai-Liang and Chuan-Dow constructions, 
never adopted encroachment? 
Further Study 
Superiority and inferiority of  “vertical trussing” and “horizontal stacking” are excluded from the discussion. A 
more significant investigation should be their influences. Are the spatial layouts of  house types impacted by the 
construction system derived from “vertical trussing” and “horizontal stacking”? Is it imaginable that the “one 
bright two dark” spatial layout a product of  construction system with “vertical trussing” concept? Is it possible 
to find houses with “horizontal stacking” in the territory of  Han ethnic group? Are the spatial layouts of  Malay 
house or Thai house influenced by “horizontal stacking”? What are the construction systems of  the numerous 
house types in Southeast Asia out there? These remain interesting topics for further study.
