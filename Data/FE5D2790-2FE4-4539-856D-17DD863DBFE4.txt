英文摘要 (Abstract) 
Conventional video coding technologies (e.g., the ITU-T 
H.26x and ISO/IEC MPEG-x) are mainly based on the two 
principles of predictive and transform coding to better present 
the video signals and thus to achieve a more efficient coding 
without affecting the decoded signal quality. It implies 
complex encoders and lightweight decoders, which is 
well-suited for broadcasting-like applications. It usually 
generates a result of unbalanced computational complexity 
(CC) at encoder and decoder, the CC in the encoder is 5~10 
times over decoder side. This asymmetry in complexity 
becomes a big disadvantage for a growing number of 
emerging applications, such as (1) wireless video surveillance 
cameras and (2) mobile communication devices. They are 
rather relying on an upstream model. In this case, it is usually 
advantageous to have lightweight encoding with high 
compression efficiency and resilience to transmission errors. 
Recently, a new coding paradigm, referred to as Distributed 
Video Coding (DVC) has emerged based on two Information 
theorems from the seventies (1973 and 1976) to deal with 
problem. In this project, we present a new wireless video 
surveillance system, based on our recent proposed 
padding-based DVC. It can be employed to further reduce the 
CC and hardware cost of the encoder, and the major difference 
compared with three traditional DVC codec’s is that we do not 
divide frames into Wyner-Ziv (WZ) frame and key frame, 
separately. Furthermore, unlike the conventional DVC 
wireless network framework, we adopt the technique of 
Decoding-Friendly Encoder Design (DFED) to simplify steps 
at downlink, that may be used to reduce complexity of the 
conventional video coding (such as a complex standard like 
H.264/AVC) at encoder then get a lower complexity decoder 
of terminal (portable) device. In this project, we also 
emphasize on the specific part of Mutual Bi-Directional 
Frames Coding at Decoder for the Padding-Based DVC to 
improve the quality of our previous proposed padding-based 
DVC. 
Keywords: 
Distributed video coding (DVC), Padding-based DVC, DFED, 
Video surveillance systems.

3.1. 分散式視訊編碼（DVC）簡介 
目前傳統分散式視訊編碼 (DVC) 發展的主要架構中
有三個較為突出的研究團體 ; 他們是 Stanford 大學
的 Wyner-Ziv (WZ) DVC [10]，  Berkeley 大學的
PRISMDVC [11] 和歐洲的DISCOVER [12-13] DVC。 
其中，由 Stanford 大學提出的 WZ DVC 主要是用框
架級別 (Frame level) 來編碼，並使用 turbo codes 為主
的編碼技術，也採用了反饋通道（Feedback channel）
來做流量控制（Rate control）且可以在解碼端(Decoder)
來執行（註:一般的視訊壓縮標準其流量控制皆在編
碼端執行）[27]。而 Berkeley 大學提出的 PRISM DVC
主要是用在區塊級別(Block level)來編碼，並使用
Syndrome coding 和 CRC 為主的編碼技術，但是它並
未使用反饋通道（Feedback channel）其流量控制是在
編碼端 (Encoder side) 執行。 
值得一提的是由歐洲發展的DISCOVER視訊壓縮
編解碼器（codec），它主要是源自於 Stanford 大學的
WZ DVC 延伸發展而來，它顯著的改善了許多 WZ 
DVC 的缺點。例如它的 RD performance（或 PSNR）
的 dB 值就比傳統的 WZ DVC 好很多，而且根據參考
文獻 [23-24] 二篇論文所述，它的大部分（幾乎全部）
增益值(gain)就高於 H.264/AVC 標準的 intra coding， 
尤其在 ＂Coast Guard＂這個視訊測試影片中，其增
益值甚至超過 H.264/AVC 標準的 Inter coding with 
zero-motion (或 No Motion)。傳統的分散式視訊編碼 
(DVC) 其設計以 WZ frame 的架構為主，即是 Stanford
和 DISCOVER 所採用的架構。 這個方法有二個主要
問題需要解決。其一為解碼器的複雜度過高，使之無
法在即時系統（Real time system）中實現。 在探討有
關 DISCOVER 的論文如參考文獻 [13] 中指出它的
解碼器端有著高複雜度的問題，由 參考文獻 [13] 的
表七與表八（Table 7 and Table 8）中，可以很明顯的看
出其解碼所需的時間遠比傳統 H.264/AVC intra 高出
至少百倍，且最高可達千倍以上，所以要在即時系統
中實現有其難度。 這是傳統分散式視訊編碼 (DVC)
的一個大問題。 
   因為在 DISCOVER 中採用以框架為主  (Frame 
based) 的設計方式，使得一次消失的資訊過於集中，
所以較不容易還原影像。 不像採用區塊為主 (Block 
based) 的設計方式，因其消失的資訊較為分散， 所
以還原影像比較容易。 例如，在參考論文 [13] 的圖
(六)（Figure 6）中曾提及當在 GOP 2 時， 其產生的
失真還不是太多， 但到了 GOP 8 時與 GOP 2 相比較
其失真可高達 2~6 dB (相差很大)。 倘若 GOP 數再
增加其失真量必定更為可觀， 這是一個不容忽視且
需急於解決的問題。 
 
3.2. 填充型分散式視訊編碼（Padding-based DVC）
設計 [19][20]  
由前面的討論可以瞭解傳統分散式視訊編碼技術的
發展背景與概況及其優缺點，接著我們將介紹我們所
提出的新填充型分散式視訊編碼器 (Padding-Based 
DVC)架構。 實際上我們所提出的填充型分散式視訊
編碼， 採用了以區塊為主 (Block based) 的設計方法
來進行 (而不使用傳統 DVC Frame based 的設計)，同
時我們也避開傳統錯誤更正碼的編碼方式（Turbo 
code），而改採填充型為主 (Padding based) 的方式，以
取代傳統方法。 利用填充型視訊編碼架構，相較於
傳統的分散式視訊編碼技術有較佳的 RD (Performance) 
dB 值 [19][20]。基本上，它超越了大部份傳統的分
散式視訊編碼技術所採用的解決方案，並且只需要較
低的複雜度和硬體成本。 以下簡單介紹我們最近所
提的填充型分散式視訊編碼 (Padding-Based DVC)架
構。與傳統的分散式編碼器(DVC) 架構相較有著顯
著的不同之處，將依序介紹於後。 
   填充型分散式視訊編碼（Padding-Based DVC）技
術，可以依照編碼和解碼二部分來說明，其方塊圖分
別描繪於圖(四)及圖(五)。 首先， 編碼器端是由三

Classification 功能區塊 (Function block) 又可區分為
DC 和 SAD （ Sum of Absolute Differences）的 
Classification 功 能 。 相 較 於 傳統 的 H.264/AVC 
Intra-frame 編碼器，它只增加一點額外的運算複雜度
（Computational complexity）而已。在 Classification 中，

3.4. 利用解碼友善編碼器（DFED）簡化無線視訊傳
輸網路下載 (Down link) 部分之設計 
因為 H.264/AVC 編碼器使用了太多複雜的演算法，
其實有些效果不太好的演算法是可以適當的移除，並
不會影響太多的效能；例如，它們有太多的模式選擇
（Inter mode selection），通常有十九種模式選擇。以
下我們提供一些簡單概念做參考，例如， 非可變塊
大小運動估計（Non-variable block size of motion 
estimation），只有一個參考框架（Only one reference 
frame），整點像素運動搜索（Integer pixel for motion 
search），更大面積的運算（Larger block size）…等。
而解碼友善視訊編碼器(DFED)，其目的是希望將編
碼端 (Decoder)作合理的簡化 [14-18]，使解碼端成本
降低。 
如何利用解碼友善編碼器有效的簡化解碼端的複
雜度，主要是先在編碼端找出哪些步驟被簡化是有效
的，否則就算減少了一些編碼端的步驟，解碼端的複
雜度仍然不會降低，甚至會弄巧成拙使解碼端的複雜
度升高，就像傳統分散式視訊編碼 (DVC)器一樣。
我們可以利用解碼友善編碼器（DFED）來簡化無線
視訊傳輸網路下載 (Down link) 部分之設計。 我們
所提出的填充型分散式視訊編碼（Padding-Based DVC）
[19-20] 編碼器結合解碼友善編碼器(DFED)在整個
無線視頻網路傳輸上是最佳匹配。 
 
(A) 解碼友善編碼器 (DFED) 的設計原理

可行性分析，在（1）中，因為我們是以整個網路作

對硬體設計方面有所助益，可以減少解碼端大量記憶
體的數量，但無法減少解碼端計算複雜度，而我們的
設計主要還是用在軟體設計的層面，需要的是計算複

論範圍。所以最後對於我們的整體網路設計而言，我
們採用（2）的方式，作為我們的解碼友善視訊編碼
器(DFED)的解決方案，就是在編碼端簡化半點像素搜
尋，而使得解碼端因為減少了半點像素補償的運算，
而使其計算複雜度可以減少，所以（2）應該是現階
段最適合的一種解決方案。

況 (不同 Qi 值)下採用我們或 DISCOVER 分散式視
訊編碼 (DVC)技術，其比值皆大於 1； 亦即是需要
較少的計算複雜度。在編碼端我們的方法其計算複雜
度略高於 DISCOVER 分散式視訊編碼器， 因為在編碼
端我做了不少有關低移動區塊(low motion blocks)及沒
移動區塊(zero motion blocks)搜尋。 表(二)是解碼端
計算複雜度比較，在解碼端 DISCOVER 分散式視訊
編碼器其計算複雜度高於我們的所提分散式視訊編
碼器與 H.264/AVC intra 甚多，最大甚至於達到近兩
千倍。 這使得 DISCOVER 分散式視訊編碼器很難實
現於即時處理狀況，這與我們先前所討論的
DISCOVER 分散式視訊編碼器採用 turbo code， 其在
解碼端作 turbo code 解碼時約佔了整個計算複雜度的
80%~90%。採用我們所提的填充型分散式視訊編碼
器，不會發生這種情況。當然若加上利用相互的雙向
框架 (MB-frame）的處理方式，與原來填充型分散式
視訊編碼器相較其計算複雜度約增加 30%， 但約有
0.2 dB 的視訊品質增益，可以達到我們的理想需求。

Padding Block Based DVC Coding Scheme with 
Mutual Bi-directional Frame Coding at Decoder 
 
Ted Chih-Wei Lei 
Department of Electrical Engineering 
National Sun Yat-Sen University 
Kaohsiung, Taiwan 
tedlei@ccms.nkfust.edu.tw 
Shiunn-Jang Chern 
Department of Electrical Engineering 
Tamkang University 
New Taipei City, Taiwan 
sjchern@mail.tku.edu.tw 
 
Abstract—In this paper, we apply the mutual bi-directional frame 
coding (MB-frame coding) to our previous proposed padding 
block based Distributed Video Coding (DVC) scheme for 
performance improvement. Basically, in the MB-frame coding 
approach, we adopt the mutual forward and backward video 
sequence frames to process within reference frames in each 
individual Group of Pictures (GOP) until no skip blocks could be 
padded at decoder. It is worth to noting that with this approach 
the computation complexity does not increase at encoder side, 
which is consistent with the basic concept to meet the low 
complexity DVC requirement. Indeed, it achieves the overall 
system performance improvement with the cost of increasing for 
average 30 % of computation complexity at decoder side. Via 
computer simulation we show that the experimental results with 
the MB frame coding approach has about 0.2 dB gain over the 
one which does not employ the MB-frames coding scheme. 
Keywords- Distributed Video Coding; Padding Block Based 
Distributed Video Coding; Mutual Bi-Directional Frames Coding 
I.  INTRODUCTION 
Video compression operates by removing redundancy in the 
temporal, spatial and/or frequency domains. The lossy 
compression coding is mainly with the transform and 
quantization processing, while the lossless compression is with 
entropy coding. Lossy compression is necessary to achieve 
higher compression, while lossless compression of image and 
video information only provides a moderate amount of 
compression. A coded video sequence in H.264/AVC [1] 
consists of a sequence of coded pictures. A coded picture can 
represent either an entire frame or a single field. Generally, a 
frame of video can be considered to contain two interleaved 
fields, a top and a bottom field. If the two fields of a frame 
were captured at different time instants, the frame is referred 
to as an interlaced frame, and otherwise it is referred to as a 
progressive frame. The frame is processed in units of a 
macroblock (corresponding to 16x16 pixels in the original 
image). Each macroblock is encoded in intra or inter mode. 
Basically, two frame coding structures, the intra-frame and 
inter-frame, are used in conventional video coding [1][2]. 
Intra-frame coding is mainly encoded the single frame with 
spatial domain only, it does not refer to any other reference 
frames to encoded. In contrast, the inter-frame coding 
techniques are with a temporal based video compression 
scheme, in which one or more neighboring frames are chosen 
as the reference. Also, the use of inter-frame prediction is 
considered to be as a kind of prediction; it tries to take 
advantages from temporal redundancy between neighboring 
frames allowing achieving higher compression rates. 
Distributed video coding (DVC) is a new paradigm for 
video compression based on two significant information 
theorems, Slepian-Wolf and Wyner-Ziv [3]. Unlike the 
conventional video compression standards (e.g. AVC/H.264), 
the source statistics are exploited at the encoder side; the 
complexity for video encoder is 5~10 times over video decoder. 
In the DVC codec we shift the complexity at encoder to the 
decoder side, without degrading the video quality. It is very 
suitable for some emerging applications where computational 
power is sparse at the encoder side, such as wireless low power 
video surveillance, visual sensor networks, and mobile camera 
phone. Currently, there are three major groups for the 
development of conventional DVC architectures, viz., the 
Stanford's Wyner-Ziv (WZ) video coding [3], Berkeley's 
PRISM [4] and Europe's DISCOVER [5][6], respectively. 
Basically, the WZ codec proposed by Stanford works at the 
frame level and uses turbo codes based Slepian-Wolf coding, 
which is characterized by a feedback channel to perform rate 
control at decoder. While the PRISM codec by Berkeley works 
at the block level and uses the syndrome coding, that is 
characterized by an encoder side rate controller based on the 
availability of a reference frame. The DISCOVER video codec 
by Europe is, in fact, an extension of the Stanford's WZ video 
codec, which can be used to significantly improve the RD 
performance over the conventional ones. Especially, as 
reported in [7][8], it has the PSNR gain over H.264/AVC Inter 
with zero-motion for "Coast Guard" video test video sequence, 
as well as gains over H.264/AVC Intra for most tested video 
sequences. Although the WZ codec is the most popular one 
used in the literatures among the above-mentioned DVC video 
coding schemes, which is eventually designed for lower 
transmission rate systems. It suffers from three major problems 
of DVC at present deployment. The first problem is that the 
high computational complexity (CC) at decoder of DVC is still 
hard to implement in a real time applications. Specifically, as 
reported in Tables 7 and 8 of [6] (the DISCOVER codec’s 
paper), the CC problem at decoder side is higher than 
conventional H.264/AVC intra decoding more than one 
hundred times (even up to thousand times). Also, as indicated 
in Table 9 of [6], the higher CC at decoder is mainly due to the 
turbo-code decoder, which contributed over 80% to 90% 
Directional frame coding (MB-frame coding) is introduced and

A. The I-frame, P-frame and B-frame of Cnventinal video

As described earlier, I-frame is an intra-frame coding, P-

than I-frame. The Bidirectional frame named as the B-frame 
coding is also an inter-frame coding, but it uses both previous 
and next (future) frames as (data) reference to achieve the 
highest amount of data compression [1][2]. To facilitate

B. The Enhance Funtion of PB Based DVC with MB Frame 
Coding 
As described above, in the proposed approach, MB-frame is 
different from the B-frame used in traditional approach. As 
illustrated in Figure 2, the conventional B-frame coding 
approach is processed with video sequence when we completed 
the first B-frame and continued to processing the next B-frame. 
As can be seen from figure 2, B1 frame is first referred to I0 
frame and P2 frame with motion search, and when the 
processing of B1 frame is completed, we continue to processing 
B3 frame, which is referred to P2 frame and P4 frame with 
motion search, and keep continuing to process with this order. 
But in our proposed MB-frame video coding, it is doing 
differently, since at decoder of the proposed PB based DVC, 
the PBMA algorithm is employed, where in the traditional 
video coding scheme it  used the motion estimation at encoder. 
For instances, as shown in figure 3, in the MB-frame approach, 
in the forward video sequence MB1 frame is first refereed to I0 
frame with PBMA, and following up the MB2 frame is referred 
to this decoded MB1 frame with PBMA, and keep continuing to 
process with this order until MBN-1 frame in the same GOP 
(Group Picture) is completed. After that, we begin to process 
the backward video sequence, in which MBN-1 frame is first 
referred to I0 frame as reference frame of the next GOP with 
PBMA, similarly (but in the reverse direction) MBN-2 frame is 
referred to the decoded MBN-1 frame with PBMA, and again 
keep continuing to process with backward order until MB1 
frame in the same GOP is completed. Finally, we continue to 
handling until forward and backward video frames are 
completed padded; we referred the above steps as the first cycle 
of skip blocks padded, and then we recycle this sequence order 
n times until no skip blocks are needed to pad. 
In addition, this algorithm is also different with our early 
proposed PB Based DVC scheme; the original design could 
deal with one cycle only, and did not use the mutual treatment, 
yields with the MB-frame coding approach better performance 
improvement can be achieved.  The overall performance 
improvement is around 0.2 dB, which is more closed to the 
conventional B-frame video coding design. Also, it is worth 
mentioning that with the MB frame coding algorithm, at 
encoder side no additional complexity increment is required.

Performance of the MIMO CS-PRP-OFDM Systems 
with Complementary Codes 
 
Shiunn-Jang Chern1, You-De Lee2, and Richard Hsin-Hsyong Yang3 
1Electrical Engineering Department, Tamkang University, New Taipei City, Taiwan, E-mail: sjchern@mail.tku.edu.tw 
2Electrical Engineering Department, National Sun Yat-Sen University, Kaohsiung City, Taiwan 
3Department of computer and communication, National Kaohsiung First University of 
Science and Technology, Kaohsiung, Taiwan 
 
 
 
Abstract—This paper presents a new transceiver framework of 
the MIMO-OFDM systems, with the space-time block code (ST-
BC). Unlike the conventional Pseudo-Random Postfix (PRP) -
OFDM in our proposed framework the null samples of zero-
padding (ZP)-OFDM is replaced by known cyclic postfix 
sequence (CPS), weighted by a pseudo-random (PR) scalar. Since 
the CPS is implemented by the cyclic-shift (CS) complementary 
code (CC) sequences, the proposed transceiver scheme is referred 
to as the MIMO CS-PRP-OFDM systems. By exploring the useful 
property of CC sequences, convolved with channel information, 
the receiver design associated with the semi-blind channel 
estimation of the proposed MIMO CS-PRP-OFDM systems is 
affected only by the background noise. It avoids the interference 
of the transmitted signals, and yields achieving better system 
performance, in terms of symbol error rate, compared with the 
conventional PRP-OFDM based systems, with less complexity. 
This is especially true when the signal-to-noise ratio is increased. 
Keywords- MIMO-OFDM; space-time encoder; redundant 
sequence; complementary codes; cyclic-postfix 
I.  INTRODUCTION 
Multiple-input multiple-output (MIMO) systems provide a 
very promising means to increase the spectral efficiency. 
Equipped with space-time block encoding (ST-BC) [10] in the 
transmitter and applied the intelligent signal processing at the 
receiver [6], MIMO systems can provide diversity and coding 
gains over single antenna system. The orthogonal frequency 
division multiplexing (OFDM) technique has been widely 
adopted for high-speed wireless communications, due to its 
robustness of multipath propagation [1]-[5]. In OFDM system 
to maintain the orthogonality among subcarriers and to prevent 
the inter-block-interference (IBI), a redundant sequence named 
as the cyclic prefix (CP), zero-padding (ZP) and pseudo-
random postfix (PRP), with length no less than the order of 
channel impulse response (CIR), is inserted between 
consecutive block symbols in the transmitter [1][4][8][9]. 
Eventually, the ZP-OFDM [4] system was proposed to 
circumvent the problem of channel null occurred when the CP-
OFDM system was employed, with the price of increased 
receiver complexity. Although the appended zero-samples can 
be used to eliminate the channel induced IBI, it seems a little 
waste. Hence, in the PRP-OFDM system [8][9] the PRP is used 
to replace the guard interval contents of ZP-OFDM. It 
capitalizes on the advantages of ZP-OFDM, but unlike the ZP-
OFDM it exploits additional information, i.e. pseudo-randomly 
weighted postfix sequences, for semi-blindly channel 
estimation, with order-one statistics of the received signal [7]. 
At the receiver end, by removing the added redundant sequence 
the effect of IBI could be alleviated, effectively. The PRP-
OFDM system with single antenna was extended to the MIMO 
case, by encoding the transmitted signal and postfixes vectors 
with two different space-time encoder [13][14], and is referred 
to as the ST-BC MIMO PRP-OFDM systems. With specific 
design of the space-time (ST) encoder for the PRP sequences, 
the MIMO PRP-OFDM system is capable to perform a semi-
blind estimation for the entire MIMO-channel, by exploiting 
the order-one statistics of the received signals.  
In the aforementioned PRP-OFDM systems [8][9][13][14], 
to extract the channel information it assumed that the 
transmitted signals and background noise are zero-mean, and is 
performed by averaging a number of collected signal-blocks in 
the receiver to avoid the effect due to the interfered transmitted 
signal. The zero-mean assumptions do not hold, due to the fact 
that the collection of sufficient signal-blocks may not be 
possible, in practical situations. Especially, when the signal-to-
noise ratio (SNR) is increased, the effect of the interfered 
transmitted signal becomes significant. To circumvent the 
above mentioned problem, in this paper, we propose a new 
transceiver framework of the ST-BC MIMO-OFDM systems to 
further enhance the system reliability and with less complexity 
compared to the PRP-OFDM based systems. In our proposed 
scheme, the null samples of ZP-OFDM is replaced by the 
known cyclic-postfix sequences (CPS), weighted by a pseudo-
random scalar. Since the CPS is implemented by cyclic-shift 
(CS) complementary codes (CC) [15][16], it is referred to as 
the MIMO CS-PRP-OFDM system. By exploring the useful 
property of CC sequences convolved with channel information, 
at the receiver we can enhance the semi-blind channel 
estimation, effectively. The resulting channel estimation is 
affected simply by the background noise; it avoids the 
interference of the transmitted signals, yields better system 
performance achievement. This is especially true when the 
signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) is increased.

V. CONCLUSIONS 
In this paper, we have proposed a novel ST-BC MIMO CS-
PRP-OFDM scheme, where the pseudo random cyclic-postfix 
sequences, implementing with the complementary code (CC) 
sequences, was employed to replace the PRP in OFDM 
systems. The MIMO CS-PRP-OFDM systems with 2 transmit-
antennas and 2 receive-antennas is considered to verify the 
merits of the proposed scheme. By exploring the property of 
CC sequence the semi-blind channel estimation, with first-
order statistics of received signals, could be performed, 
effectively and with lower complexity, compared with the 
PRP-OFDM based system. However, with the proposed 
MIMO CS-PRP-OFDM systems, it affected only by the 
background noise, to avoid the interference of the transmitted 
signals. Thus, when the SNR is increased better performance 
achievement could be obtained compared with the 
conventional MIMO PRP-OFDM systems. Also, we found that 
the increasing of the numbers of the receive antenna, we could 
improve the system performance of MIMO CS-PRP-OFDM, 
accordingly, compared with that using the conventional 
MIMO-PRP-OFDM systems, associated with the space-time 
block coding. This proposed transceiver could be further 
extended for insufficient redundancy case, with the blind 
channel shortening algorithm.
