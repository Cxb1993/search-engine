結合仿生學設計方法與TRIZ方法於產品創新設計研究 
 
計畫編號：NSC94-2212-E-006-045 
執行期間：94/8/1~ 95/7/31 
主持人：陳家豪  國立成功大學機械系  教授 jlchen@mail.ncku.edu.tw

摘要 
本計畫的研究目標為結合 TRIZ 創新設
計方法與仿生學設計方法, 以進行發展進
階的產品創新設計方法, 作為設計工程師
在進行產品創新設計時, 可發明出『新又有
用』的產品及製程, 以提昇我國的產品創新
設計能力及產品的競爭力。最後發展結合仿
生學創新設計方法與 TRIZ 方法的新的產
品創新設計方法的設計程序和模式，以改良
的 TRIZ 方法為基礎來發展，期望能研發出
結合兩種方法優點的新創新設計方法。並以
產品創新設計案例做驗證。 
Abstract 
The goal of this project is to develop 
advance product innovation design methods 
by combination TRIZ innovation design 
method and biomimetics design technique.  
This new method can be used as supporting 
tools for designer’s innovative design task to 
invent “novelty and usefulness” new products 
or processes. 
關鍵詞：創新設計, 發明創新問題解決方法
(TRIZ), 仿生學設計。 
一、前言 
我國科技產業發展二十餘年來，已達
到傲人的成就。展望耒來二十一世紀，我國
要維持競爭力不墜，則應該要從科技創新著
手，不應指望模仿先進國家的技術，因為先
進國家的競爭力的基礎，都來自於創新科技
與能力。而未來國內產品的競爭力則在於產
品創新。同時，產品創新的能力則根基於產
品創新設計能力，一般而言，專利權的數量
多寡是產品創新設計能力的客觀指標。因此
學習與開發各種產品發明創新設計方法，是
我國建立先進的產品創新設計能力基礎。 
創新發明的方法非常的多，而可用於技
術創新設計的方法也很多，國內也有學者提
出創新發明設計方法。其中較特別的是
TRIZ 發明創新問題解決方法，此方法由蘇
聯學者 Altshuler 於 1946 年開始發展，他從
分析專利(從早先 40 萬個到目前超過 200 萬
個專利)中瞭解到發明的原理。1980 年代被
引入美國，近 6 年來更被開發為創新發明電
腦軟體，現已廣泛用於歐、美、日等國工業
界，成功地完成許多創新設計案例。 
「仿生學」(Biomimetics or Bionics)定
義，就是模仿各種大自然生態過程及生物現
象，或從大自然的其他動植物尋找創新的靈
感(ideas)，重新利用最新科技，從製程上及
產品特性上開發出新型商品。其應用領域從
簡單的材料設計到複雜的生物醫學、航太工
程都可含蓋。例如: 有些研究機器人的學者
以動物為學習對象，研發各種仿生機器生物
如龍蝦、蒼蠅、狗、魚、蛇乃至壁虎等等。
工程師們有時會將「魚類尾鰭重新吸收流場
中的動能以增加推進效率」這一類生物運動
的原理應用到工程機械上，我們都稱這種模
仿生物的工程應用為「仿生學設計」
(Biomimetic Design)。大自然及生物現象可
提供非常多創新點子之建議與各種工程問
題之答案，因此仿生學設計已成為創新設計
靈感(ideas)來源的快速、容易取得且易掌握
之重要方法。

3.2 整合仿生設計與 TRIZ 方法架構 
要完成仿生矛盾矩陣表的建構，首先
要將仿生案例蒐集並分類到矛盾矩陣表中。 
每一個矛盾矩陣表的建議法則都有詳
細的定義和副定義，經過分析後的仿生案例
可能和這些定義相符合，因此被歸類在該建
議法則之下，但若無符合者，我們則進行下
一步驟：分析仿生案例所解決的矛盾問題是
由哪些工程參數構成的，再察看在這些工程
參數的矛盾矩陣方格內有哪些建議法則出
現，若該仿生案例解決該矛盾問題的方法與
建議法則的方法相近，則將該仿生案例歸類
於該建議法則。再者，仿生案例若無任何與
其所解決之矛盾問題相符之建議法則，則將
該案例置於第四十建議法則之後，視為第四
十一個建議法則，其流程突如圖三所示。

五、結論與建議 
由以上結果觀之，使用 TRIZ 矛盾矩陣
表來系統化仿生案例確實可以使設計者更
容易從仿生案例中找到靈感、解決問題。 
TRIZ 矛盾矩陣表提供了仿生案例系統
化的功能，但在矛盾矩陣表中依舊有空矩陣
的存在，可以使用單一特性原則方法【34】
來解決該問題，亦可在建構仿生矛盾矩陣表
時，直接就每個矛盾找仿生案例，即可解決
空矩陣的問題。 
雖然 TRIZ 強大的骨架可以提供仿生案
例系統化的捷徑，但是在蒐集案例時亦發現
有些問題，即生物界只有某些工程參數和人
類設計相通，但有些人類發展出來的工程參
數在生物界中找不到相近的，例如：＃24
資訊的損失、＃25 時間的浪費、＃27 可靠
性、＃28 量測的準確度、＃29 製造準確度、
＃30 作用於物體的有害因素、＃31 有害副
作用、＃32 製造性、＃38 自動化程度等等，
這些工程參數主要用在機械方面，因此仿生
案例幾乎無法解決這一部份產生之矛盾問
題，這是後續研究帶解決的問題。 
仿生方法的可行性，我想不言而喻，
而使用 TRIZ 矛盾矩陣表將之系統化只是方
法之一，畢竟 TRIZ 矩陣表示人類設計的整
理，而仿生方法則是經由大自然的演化得到
的結果，其間必有無法相互補足之處，因此
必定有更完善的系統化方法會出現，之後的
發展目標可朝結合多項設計方法來組成一
獨立的仿生方法系統，提供更多且更明確的
想法給設計者使用。

Link through TRIZ contradiction matrix 
The simple way to adapt the TRIZ method into sustainable innovation is using traditional TRIZ method by 
identifying a pair of contradiction parameters and finding suitable inventive principles from contradiction 
matrix (Chen and Liu, 2002; Chang and Chen, 2003a).  How to transfer from parameters of eco-design 
problems to TRIZ parameters is the gap that needs more study. 
 
Direct link to the TRIZ inventive principles 
Another approach guides the designer from the design principle, design checklist, design rule, or design 
criteria of eco-design to the corresponding TRIZ inventive principles directly (Liu and Chen, 2003). As for 
the designer only knows how to improve or avoid one parameter of his sustainable innovation problem, a 
modified TRIZ method that uses one parameter to find suitable inventive principles without the use of 
contradiction matrix was developed(Chen and Liu, 2001; Chen and Liu, 2003; Liu and Chen, 2001). 
 
Link with other eco-design tools 
Many eco-design tools were selected to link with TRIZ-based sustainable innovation processes, such as 
green QFD (Liu and Chen, 2003), design around method and extension method (Chang and Chen, 2003a), 
et al.  Recently, the concept of integrating environmental FMEA tool with TRIZ was proposed (Yen and 
Chen, 2004).  This new method provides the designer a supporting tool to develop new products or 
processes with less environmental impact. 
 
Eco-products examples for TRIZ inventive principles 
Sustainable innovation always needs inspiration.  Due to insufficient experience, designers may not easily 
solve the conflict problem.  Therefore, there is a need to provide designers sustainable innovative 
examples for 40 TRIZ inventive principles (Chang and Chen, 2003b).  These examples cover a wide 
range of products and processes.  They are novelty, creative and eco-friendly.  These practical examples 
are conducive to inspire design engineers for developing sustainable innovative products.  Sustainable 
innovative examples for 40 TRIZ inventive principles are implemented in a TRIZ-based sustainable 
innovative design computer software tool (Chang and Chen, 2004). 
 
Patterns of green evolution rules 
Green evolution rules of products provide the designer a useful tool for filtering and selecting solutions of 
sustainable innovative design problems.  These technical evolution trends determine the direction and 
character of technical revolution in the next generation.  Hence, it can help the designer forecast the future 
of the technique and concentrate research and development in the most promising directions (Chen, 2002; 
Chen and Liu, 2002).  The concepts of ideality laws and the existence of an ideal final result for 
eco-products can help the designer to locate an evolutionary direction for innovative design problem 
definition process.  Starting the ideal final result and backing away from it as little as possible offers the 
designer a different sustainable innovative design challenge (Chen, 2002). 
 
Future research direction for TRIZ based sustainable innovation methods 
The suggestions for future research direction of TRIZ-based sustainable innovation methods are as follows.  
(1) More sustainable innovation examples related to TRIZ inventive principles are required in the future for 
providing the designer more references to simulate innovation idea.  (2) Identifying other green evolution 
rules can offer the designer more opportunity to locate current product position and forecast the next 
generation eco-product.  (3) Modifying classical contradiction matrix or creating new contradiction matrix 
to include more environmental related parameters can transfer TRIZ method as a more suitable tool in 
performing sustainable innovation tasks.  (4) Finally, integrating TRIZ with other eco-design tools can 
provide the designer more sustainable innovation tools for product sustainable innovative design tasks.

Pedestrian traffic signals - animated little green man 
In 1999, the first pedestrian traffic signal (animated little green man) in the world was built up in one of 
crosswalks (the pedestrian crossings) of street intersection in Taipei city (Hobbiesplus, 2005), as show in 
Figure 3.  As the traffic light turned, a animated little green man flashed like he was walking. Above him 
was a clock that started counting down the seconds left until the traffic light turned red. You knew how fast 
you had to hurry to get across.  The new traffic lights you are seeing are made out of arrays of light 
emitting diodes (LEDs).  These are tiny, purely electronic lights that are extremely energy efficient and 
have a very long life.  Each LED is about the size of a pencil eraser, so hundreds of them are used 
together in an array.  LED bulbs might consume 15 or 20 watts instead of 100, so the power consumption 
drops by a factor of five or six.
