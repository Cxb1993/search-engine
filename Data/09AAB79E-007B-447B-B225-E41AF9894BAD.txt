抗還原層晶界效應隨溫度增加而消失。最後，將之前的共摻雜型氧化鋯電解質，搭配白金
電極製作出單電池，並利用所研製之小型燃料電池測試系統進行電池測試，於 100cc/min, 
5%H2無加濕的情況下，可得到約 82 mW/cm2的發電效率，遠優於Tosoh-8YSZ電解質之 56 
mW/cm2的發電效率。由此可知，依循本計畫所提供之設計原則所開發出之共摻雜型氧化鋯
或氧化鈰系統可降低工作溫度，並提升發電效率；同時亦成功研製出小型燃料電池測試系
統，符合我們所預期的目標。

3-1 二價(Mg2+, Ca2+, Sr2+)與三價(Gd3+)稀
土元素共摻雜於氧化鈰之結晶結構分析 
在分析所有數據之前，先針對此三種
共摻雜系統的晶格常數變化分析，雖然這三
種不同元素之共摻雜系統皆為立方晶結
構。由圖 1 所示，可發現GDC隨著Mg2+與
Ca2+的添加，因氧空缺增多造成晶體呈現較
鬆散的狀態，使晶格常數隨添加量增加而下
降；然而GDC添加Sr2+方面，雖仍有氧空缺
的產生，但是Sr2+離子半徑遠比母相大得
多，因此晶格常數會隨著添加量上升。另
外，由圖 2 可見單摻雜三價元素會使晶格常
數上升，但是單摻雜二價元素，晶格常數變
化的情形，尤其Ca2+與Mg2+皆有稍微下降的
趨勢，造成這些現象除了離子半徑差異外
(如表 2)，還有各元素對母相固溶度之差異
[4]，如表 3。由晶格常數變化趨勢，可以看
到另外一個現象，見圖 1，Ca2+與Sr2+添加於
GDC曲線的截距幾乎落於同一個點大約是
在 5.43 Å，而Mg2+添加於GDC的截距值則落
在 5.414 Å，相當接近純CeO2之晶格常數，
這原因可能是因為MgO對CeO2母相固溶量
低以及離子半徑差異所造成。藉由與母相離
子差異百分比計算，結果是Mg2+/Ce4+ (-8.2%)
是負值，Ca2+/Ce4+ (15%)與Sr2+/Ce4+ (28.8%)
均是正值。摻雜Mg2+所形成的缺陷的型態是
小型置換原子或是格隙型原子，這與Sr2+和
Ca2+加入GDC的晶格變形型式(晶格應力或
晶格應變)不同。除此之外，可從文獻[7]各
二價氧化物對CeO2固溶限可知最高的是
CaO，最低的是MgO，而且MgO的溶解反應
能又遠大於CaO與SrO，而溶解反應能取決
於晶格能與缺陷形成。故可知Mg2+所產生的
缺陷型態以及對氧化鈰基材的固溶量都與
其它兩個系統有截然不同的表現。

一般單摻雜Gd3+於氧化鈰，摻雜量 20 
mol%擁有最佳導電性，超過此含量會因摻
雜缺陷過多而導致缺陷的叢聚(defect cluster)
以及氧空缺在排列位置上形成序化現象而
使電性下降[11]。藉由Arrhenius plot其斜率
經換算可得到氧離子在電解質中遷移時所
受到的活化能障。圖 7為不同含量Sr2+與Ca2+
於GDC於 750oC時導電率與活化能關係
圖，可知摻雜濃度變化是導電率及活化能的
函數。本實驗共摻雜氧化鈰基系統於
x=0.02、0.05 mole時，缺陷含量皆較GDC來
得高，但導電率卻皆高於GDC，顯示出主要
原因可能是因為共摻雜效應抑制了氧空缺
序化的發生，因此擁有較低的活化能以及較
高的導電性，同樣現象由Yamamura[12]等人
對Ce1-x-yLaxMyO2-delt a (M=Ca、Sr)系統做研
究，發現共摻雜效應會抑制氧空缺序化的形
成。

Abstract 
Current electrical ceramic research activities in Taiwan are reviewed, especially studies on 
zirconia materials for energy-related applications. Phase transformation behavior on electrical as 
well as mechanical properties of modified zirconia materials using in-situ compression-diffraction 
experiments with synchrotron radiation and various property measurements are reported in this 
work. Experimental results exhibit that frequently mentioned tetragonal-to-monoclinic (t-to-m) 
transformation and ferroelastic domain switching may not be more influential than the newly 
found stress-induced phase transition which exhibits correlation between the cubic/t’ phase and a 
monoclinic-like phase for enhancing the fracture toughness of modified zirconia. Besides, 
modified zirconia may display either superelastic behavior or good ionic conductivity, by 
appropriate composition and phase control. Phase transitions and microstructures on mechanical 
and electrical properties of zirconia will be discussed.  
Keywords: phase transformation, mechanical properties, electrical properties, toughness, 
synchrotron radiation 
 
1. Introduction  
Both electrical and mechanical properties are important for zirconia used as an electrolyte in a solid oxide 
fuel cell (SOFC). Good ionic conductivity of an electrolyte provides an opportunity for developing a SOFC 
operating at a lower temperature. Better mechanical toughness of the electrolyte permits a higher reliability on 
intermittent operations. To gain better toughness of zirconia electrolyte, the toughening mechanisms need to be 
clarified. The t-to-m phase transformation toughening and ferroelastic domain switching in tetragonal phase are 
frequently proposed for toughening in several zirconia-based ceramic materials [1,2]. In our previous works 
[3-5], some special features associated with the cubic phase and mechanical properties of zirconia were found 
from X-ray diffraction under cyclic loading, but the relationship between mechanical properties and 
microstructural feature has not been fully understood yet. Investigations of mechanical properties, phase 
structure and crystallography relation of the stress-induced reversible phase transformations in trivalent oxides 
(RE2O3) and Nb2O5 co-modified ZrO2 (3Y) were carried out, by monitoring the phase transformation 
temperature using different dopants. Besides, the origin of the energy-absorbing mechanism of modified ZrO2 
PSZ system was also investigated in this work.
