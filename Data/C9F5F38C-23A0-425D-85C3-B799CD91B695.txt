一、參加會議經過 
 
    ICSI7 會議原訂於日本仙台舉行，本次因日本大地震，會議改為於比利時的魯汶舉
行，此會議主要研究方向以矽材料製程，以及矽鍺元件、Ⅲ-Ⅴ族半導體元件、太陽電
池、PL 等。會議長達 5 天。這個會議與 ISTDM 兩年輪辦，是個受到做矽鍺製程相關
研究的人所關注的一項會議。 
    由於會議原訂於今年 6 月舉行，因日本大地震而延後至 8 月底，其中論文轉投的
部分，在會議開始前即已展開全文論文的審查工作。個人認為可能是會議地點改變加
上是否積極參加會議已與論文轉投結果無關，所以會議的人不如想像中的多。會議安
排十分緊湊，值到晚上六點會議才結束。 
 
 
二、與會心得 
    本次會議，在口頭發表會場幾乎沒有以太陽能電池為主的主題，個人覺得十分可
惜，然而，MIT 學者 Prof. Liu 發表的 Ge on Si optoelectronics 對個人來說算是一個意外
的收穫，其演講十分引人入勝。個人在會場中遇到一位大陸武漢大學的教授，對個人
研究提了一些疑問，大多問題讓個人覺得訝異，他提出了一些很奇特的想法，直到大
陸學者問到: “非晶矽的能帶寬是多少”的時候，我才發現跟他聊天真是浪費時間。比利
時是個盛產啤酒的國家，在 poster section 中有提供免費的啤酒，我想大陸學者是喝醉
了，才會在以矽為主的會議中，問到非晶矽的能帶寬是多少的無聊問題。當然這某種
程度也代表著，大陸人勇於問問題，大陸學者問笨問題其實比台灣人羞於問問題還要
好一些。 
 
Numerical modeling of triple-junction a-Si:H/µc-Si:H/Ge solar cells 
 
 
J.-S. Huang, C.-H. Lin*, C.-M. Wang, Y.-C. Pan 
 Department of Opto-Electronic Engineering, National Dong Hwa University, 
Hualien, Taiwan, R.O.C.  
* Tel: 886-3-8634188 / Fax: 886-3-8634180 / chlin0109@mail.ndhu.edu.tw 
Abstract 
     We have investigated a a-Si:H/µc-Si:H (micromorph) two-junction tandem solar cell and a 
a-Si:H/µc-Si:H/Ge triple-junction tandem solar cell using Sentaurus TCAD computer simulations. In 
order to accurately simulate the micromorph solar cell, the simulation model includes grain boundaries 
in the microcrystalline layer. In addition, we have employed the n, k values of a microcrystalline layer 
with a crystallization level of approximately 60 %. The two-junction cell achieved a Jsc of 11.7 
mA/cm2, a Voc of 1.50 V, and an efficiency of 10.6 %. Meanwhile, the triple junction achieved a Jsc of 
11.4 mA/cm2, a Voc of 1.68 V, and an efficiency of 12.5 %. The results clearly indicate that an 
additional Ge layer could enhance the Voc of tandem solar cells.

tool, RSOFT. 
2. Simulation Model 
In this study, Poisson’s equation and the two carrier continuity equations were simultaneously 
solved using rigorous numerical techniques under steady-state conditions using the Sentaurus TCAD 
device simulator. The standard AM1.5G light spectrum was used, and important parameters that were 
used are listed in Table I. Density of state calculations included band tail states and dangling bond 
states which were respectively approximated by exponential and Gaussian distributions. 
2.1. Micromorph tandem solar cell 
Fig. 1 shows the schematic structure of the micromorph (a-Si:H/µc-Si:H) tandem solar cell and 
the typical thickness of the two sub-cells in the micromorph tandem solar cell. The structure consisted 
of a hydrogenated amorphous silicon p-i-n top cell and a hydrogenated microcrystalline silicon p-i-n 
bottom cell. A hydrogenated microcrystalline layer with an amorphous phase of about 40 % [9] was 
assumed. Therefore, we have assumed the amorphous defect density of states (DOS) at the grain 
boundary. Tunneling junctions exist between the two sub-cells; both band-to-band and trap-assisted 
tunneling can contribute to tunneling [10, 11]. Considering the relative absorption and current 
matching between the two cells, the hydrogenated microcrystalline bottom cell must be thicker than 
the hydrogenated amorphous top cell. The back reflector, Ag, can aid long-wavelength light absorption 
in the µc-Si:H layer.

Meanwhile, the triple-junction cell achieved a Jsc of 11.4 mA/cm2, a Voc of 1.68 V, and an efficiency of

cells. The triple-junction solar cells made use of the solar spectrum more efficiently, improving the 
conversion efficiency remarkably. 
The triple-junction solar cell had a higher quantum efficiency in the long wavelength region 
compared to the two-junction solar cell. The internal quantum efficiency (IQE) was studied to 
characterize the triple-junction solar cell. Fig. 5 shows the IQE of a triple-junction solar cell with a 
0.26 µm thick a-Si:H top cell, a 1.5 µm thick µc-Si:H middle cell, and a 0.5-µm-thick Ge bottom cell.

solar cell was current limited by the middle sub-cell.
