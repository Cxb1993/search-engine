Temperature Stability of a CMOS-compatible 
Single-arm Microring Optical Filter with MOS
Cross-section
Chih T’sung Shih1, 2, Zei Wei Zeng1, and Shiuh Chao1*
1Institute of Photonics Technologies, National Tsing Hua University, Hsinchu, Taiwan 30013, R.O.C.

Abstract: We introduce the numerical analysis of the temperature stability of a 
single-arm microring that has a MOS and rib-waveguide cross-section with 25.5 μm
radius of curvature. The temperature coefficient of the resonant wavelength for the 
microring was found to be 0.088 nm/0C.

1. Introduction
Devices with single-arm microring resonator play an important role in the area of silicon-photonics 
technologies such as optical microring modulator [1, 2], spectrophotometer [3], bio-sensor [4]. The 
microring devices that has MOS cross-section can perform functions of active tuning and modulation, 
moreover, its fabrication process is CMOS-compatible which allows integration of the optical and the 
electrical functions under the well-established CMOS process. The advantage of the microring 
resonator in comparison to the Mach-Zehnder type filter is its small-footprint and hence less sensitive
to the material loss, however, temperature stability of the resonant wavelength due to the high
temperature coefficient of refractive index, 1.86×10-4 /0K for silicon[5], needs to be investigated in 
order to design the device for practical application..
In this paper, we introduce our design and report our transmittance spectrum calculation results, 
under temperature drifting, of a CMOS-compatible single-arm microring optical filter with MOS 
cross-section. We found the temperature coefficient of the resonant wavelength for the microring with 
25.5 μm radius of curvature to be 0.088 nm/0C.
2. Device structure design
Fig. 1(a) shows the overview of our optical single-arm microring filter and Fig. 1(b) shows the MOS
cross-sectional view in the ring. The bus has the same cross-sectional structure but the dimension was 
optimized to have the -match with the ring. The MOS structure is comprised by a 3×1018 cm-3 p-poly 
silicon gate with 1 μm in width and 0.568 μm in height, a 12nm gate oxide, and a 3×1017 cm-3
n-crystalline silicon with 0.46 μm in height under the gate oxide. The cross-sectional structure is 
optically a rib waveguide. By using the methods in Ref. [6], we designed the microring filter based on 
the following criteria: First, the bus and ring waveguide have to be single-mode. Second, the
propagation constants (s) for both bus and ring waveguide have to be closely matched; this was 
implemented by adjusting the widths of the bus and the ring waveguide. Third, the bending loss of the 
ring has to be small. In our design, the bending loss of the ring waveguide dropped drastically around 
R=20 μm and was 0.9 dB/cm at R=25.5 μm outer radius [7]. The rib waveguide has a single TE-like 
mode around 1550 nm.

structure, together with the Sellmeier dispersion equation for the refractive index of the intrinsic
single-crystalline silicon in Ref. [8] and the empirical free carrier dispersion relations of Soref in Ref.
