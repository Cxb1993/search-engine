 1. Introduction  
The purpose of this study is to determine the several effective design factors needed to 
produce a lighting system which will allow for attaining of glareless lighting and the maximum 
lumens while still preserving uniform distribution on the observation plane. Then using “Taguchi 
Method” finds the best experiment combination. Using these factors we fabricate an illumination 
system consisting of two installed at both ends of an lighting tube. The light tube is shaped the 
same as a traditional fluorescent light tube, so can replace traditional tubes.  
Energy conservation in the illumination aspect is also an important topic. The light-emitting 
diode(LED) has the higher efficiency, lower power consumption, longer lifetime, not easier to 
damage than traditional light source. So LED is a good choice for energy conservation and 
protecting the environment. Our goal is to design a novel LED light tube. 
 
2. Statement of the design factors 
2.1 The gap y between the tube and reflective surface 
2.2 the relative distance x between lens and LED 
2.3 Characteristics of the tube surfaces (AR-coating) 
2.4 The material of the lighting tube  
2.5 Tilt angle ψ of the end surface of the tube  
2.6 The Curvature of the lens 
3. Taguchi method 
The simulation is to apply Taguchi method and optical software, TracePro, doing a design that 
provides a low-cost and high-quality objective for the photoelectric industry in LED lighting 
design and analysis. We choose the high efficient LED (EHP-AX08LS/CT01C-P03), lens and 
holder install on the two sides of the tube.  
Taguchi method will be establish the six factors such as the gap y between the tube and 
reflective surface, the relative distance x between lens and LED, the characteristics of the tube 
surfaces (AR-coating), the material of the lighting tube, the tilt angle ψ of the end surface of the 
tube, and the Curvature of the lens. First above six parameters must be determined. Next these 
parameters are divided into five levels separately. Then design six factors and their five levels are 
shown in Table 1. 
After design the factors and levels exactly, we choose the Taguchi method orthogonal arrays 
properly, Ｌ25(56). Then use TracePro to simulate and obtain 25 results. Take 25 data intoＬ
25(56) again, and use a program to find a set of optimization according to S/N diagram. 
Using a random table decides five sets of experiments to confirm the set of optimization. 
Exactly, the optimization will be larger than any experiment. So the research decreases time and 
production cost really.
