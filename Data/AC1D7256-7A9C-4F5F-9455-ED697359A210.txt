Abstract. Mg65Cu20Y10Ag5 Amorphous/ nano ZrO2 composites alloy powder were fabricated
through the combination method of melt spinning and mechanical alloying (MA). The melt spun
amorphous matrix ribbons are ground into powders and mixed with 3 vol.% ZrO2 nano particles in
the planetary mill. After then formed by hot pressing in Ar atmosphere under the pressure of 1100
MPa at the temperature of soft point which measured by TMA (Thermal mechanical Analysis). The
hot-pressed bulk composite specimens are compression tested at different temperature within the
supercooled temperature region. The flow stress was found decrease with increasing temperature
dramatically when the temperature exceeds the middle temperature of supercooled region. The
specimens after compression test were examined by X-ray diffractometry and SEM to investigate
its crystallinity and fracture mechanism.
Introduction
As a structural material, magnesium has
numerous advantages over steels, cast irons,
copper, aluminum alloys and plastics, such as
the high specific strength/density ratio and high
damping capacity [1-3]. However, since the
inherent low stiffness and low workability for
conventional magnesium alloy, the application
of magnesium alloys is limited. To increase the
yield strength and workability of the
magnesium alloy, the most effective way is
grain refinement accompanied with dispersion
strengthening by the nano-scaled mechanism
[4-13]. Recently, Mg-TM-RE (TM: transition
metal such as Ni, Cu, Zr, Zn; RE: rare earth
metal such as Y) alloys are reported to exhibit a
high compressive fracture strength up to 800
MPa [14-16], which is about twice as high as
the highest strength for conventional Mg-based
crystalline alloys. In parallel, these alloy
systems also show a large supercooled liquid
region (more than 40 K) and a high
glass-forming ability [17-19]. The critical
cooling rate for amorphous formation is about
102 K/s for these alloy systems. This high
stability of the supercooled liquid against
crystallization has enable us to produce bulk
amorphous by lowing cooling rate or to further
fabricating nano composite alloy with nano
oxide dispersoid in an amorphous matrix by
powder metallurgy. However, the Mg-based
amorphous alloys, like other bulk metallic
glasses (BMGs) suffer from low ductility
problems. In fact, the Mg-based amorphous
alloy has been found to be the most brittle in
comparison with others BMGs and often failed
in the elastic regime without any plasticity. To
solve such problem, BMG composites with
ductile metal or refractory ceramic particles as
reinforcement were developed to impede the
propagation of localized shear bands and
enhance the formation of multiple shear bands
[20-23]. Such composite alloys exhibit
significantly improved compressive strength,
plastic strains, and impact toughness compared
to monolithic BMGs.
In order to synthesize a homogeneous
nano-dispersed Mg-based composite alloy
(which contains nano-sized thermally stable
oxides and nano-grained/amorphous matrix)
with high strength and the least contamination
by the container of processing (such as milling
vial) , a new combination process route of the
rapid-quenched melt spinning with mechanical
alloying [24,25] is employed in this study. The
process includes mechanical alloying (MA) the
rapidly quenched pre-alloyed Mg-Cu-Ag-Y
ribbon with the nano-sized thermally stable
ceramic powder (ZrO2).

The structure of alloy composite powder
that mixed with Mg65Y10Cu20Ag5 alloy ribbon
and 1 to 5 vol.% ZrO2 powder by planetary mill
was revealed to keep amorphous matrix after
50 hours milling. Only a clear broaden peak
and the other ZrO2 peak present on the X-ray
diffraction pattern as shown in Figure 6. In
parallel, DSC traces also reveals a clear Tg
before crystallization for the composite alloy
powders of Mg65Y10Cu25-xAgx with 5 vol.%
nano-sized ZrO2 after 50 hr mixing, as shown
in Figure 7. This implies that the high energy
ball milling does not cause any phase change
on the amorphous matrix of these Mg-based
composite alloy powders.
Figure 8 shows the result of FEG-SEM
observation for the composite alloy of
Mg65Y10Cu20Ag5 alloy with 1 – 5 vol.%
nano-sized ZrO2 particle after 50 hours milling.
It is found that a lot of nano-scaled ZrO2
powder embedded in the Mg65Y10Cu20Ag5
matrix quite homogeneously. TEM observation
also revealed that the ZrO2 with average
particle size of 80 nm distributed
homogeneously in the amorphous matrix of the
Mg65Y10Cu20Ag5 / 5 vol% ZrO2 composite alloy,
as shown in Figure 9. In addition, the high
resolution TEM (HRTEM) image and the
selected area electron diffraction pattern
(SAED) of the Mg65Y10Cu20Ag5 /5 vol% ZrO2
composite alloy powder also revealed that the
ZrO2 dispersoid in the Mg-based composite
alloy powder is a tetragonal-structured single
crystal, as shown in Figure 10(a). According to
the HRTEM image and the SAED of the
dispersoid and the amorphous matrix as shown
in Figure 10(b), the interface between the ZrO2
dispersoid and the amorphous matrix of the
composite alloy powder exhibits a very good
bonding condition.

When increasing the test temperature to
436 K, the compression ratio can be easily to
reach 50% with strain rates of 5 x 10-2 s-1 and 5
x 10-3 s-1, respectively. However, the composite
alloy is also crystallized due to the longer
soaking time at high testing temperature, as
shown in Figure 20. The XRD pattern reveals
the Mg2Cu phase clearly after compression test
at 436 K for each kind of strain rate. Therefore,
the compression ratio just can reach to 50% for
the samples compression tested at 436 K with
strain rates of 5 x 10-4 s-1 and 5 x 10-3 s-1,
respectively. By the highest strain rate (1 x 10-2
s-1), the compression ratio can exceed 60%
without any crack appearance on the specimen.
As increasing the test temperature to 446 K,
since this temperature is very close to the
crystallization temperature (TX, 453 K) that the
incubation time will be very short and so as to
crystallize easily at the lower strain rate (5 x
10-4 s-1 and 1 x 10-3 s-1). Only the sample,
which tested at 436 K with strain rates of 5 x
10-2 s-1, can exhibit more than 50%
compression ratio.
Though most of samples partially
crystallized after the hot-compressed test, some
specimens can sustain their sound surface
without any crack, but some specimens can not.
Therefore, the particle size of the crystallized
phase may act an important role on the hot
workability during the hot forming process.
According to XRD patterns, the crystal size can
be estimated by Scherrer’s formula [27]
1. All of the Mg65Cu15-x Y10Agx alloys, x = 0,
5, 10, can be easily quenched into
amorphous state by melt spinning method.
In addition, the high energy ball milling
does not cause any phase change on the
amorphous matrix in these composite
alloy powders of Mg65Cu25-x Y10Agx alloy
with 5 vol.% nano-sized ZrO2 particle
after 50 hours mechanical alloying.
2. TEM observation revealed that the ZrO2
with average particle size of 80 nm
distributed homogeneously in the
amorphous matrix for both of the MA
Mg65Cu20Y10Ag5 /5 vol.% ZrO2 composite
alloy powder and the hot-pressed
Mg65Cu20Y10Ag5 /5 vol.% ZrO2 composite
alloy sample. In addition, the interface
between the ZrO2 dispersoid and the
amorphous matrix of the composite alloy
exhibits a well bonding condition.
3. The density of hot-pressed Mg-based
composite alloy can reach to 96 %
theoretical density under a pressure of
1100 MPa at 409K. In addition, the
hardness of the hot-pressed Mg-based
composite samples can reach to Hv 360 for
the Mg65Cu20Y10Ag5 /5 vol.% ZrO2
composite alloy, which is about 33%
higher than the amorphous
Mg65Cu20Y10Ag5 alloy.
4. The hardness of Mg65Cu20Y10Ag5 alloy
with 3 vol.% ZrO2 nano-powder is about
30% higher than the alloy without ZrO2
nano-powder. In parallel, the KIC value of
the Mg65Cu15Y10Ag10 alloy with 3vol. %
ZrO2 nano-powder also presents 6.8 %
higher than the alloy without ZrO2
nano-powder. This implies that the
nano-sized ZrO2 dispersion can obviously
increase hardness as well as toughness of
the Mg based amorphous alloy.
5. The result of X-ray diffraction reveals
Mg2Cu phase clearly crystallized for the
sample after compression test. Since the
temperature of 446 K is very close to the
crystallization temperature (TX, 453 K)
that the incubation time will be very short
and so as to crystallize easily at the lower
strain rate (5 x 10-4 s-1 and 1 x 10-3 s-1).
6. The lowest flow stress about 230 MPa was
revealed for the test condition with the
strain rate of 1*10-2 s-1 at 426 K and 436 K,
respectively.
7. As the particle size of Mg2Cu crystal
grows larger than 50 nm, the composite
alloy becomes more brittle and easier to
crack during compression test. Therefore,
the hot forming condition is suggested to
select the temperature of 436K with strain
rate of 1 x 10-2 s-1 to avoid the formation
of larger Mg2Cu crystals.

二、與會心得
由於筆者本人目前所執行之研究主題為非晶質合金材料及鎂合金奈米複合
材料，所於特別選定 Bulk metallic glasses，Magnesium alloy, 及 Ultrafine
grained materials 之演講主題為聆聽之對象。兹就聆聽後覺得較佳論文之心
得摘述如下：
(1)Recent advances in understanding of glass formation in bulk amorphous
materials, Z. P. Lu and C. T. Liu, Oak Ridge National Lab., USA
本論文之重點主要在歸納及印證一個新的指標用於預測塊狀非晶質合金之
形成能力(glass forming ability), 其所包含的概念及理論基礎包括了物質結
晶的熱力學及動力學,由於玻璃態金属之形成取決於急速冷却避過結晶成核
的臨界冷却速率,此與其 Tl 和 Tg(玻璃轉換溫度)有關，另外金屬玻璃的熱穩
定性則與其退火時之結晶溫度 Tx 有關。所於新的指標 g = 2(Tx-Tg)/Tl 可以
很準確地比較出各種成份合金之玻璃形成能力，縮短研究之時程。另外亦發
現形成非晶質合金所需之臨界冷却速率取決於其 g 值。
(2)Making Mg-based bulk metallic glasses formation easier by rare earth adding,
W. H. Wang, Institute of Physics, Chinese Academy of Science, Beijing, China
雖然鎂基非晶質合金具有輕量化的特點，但由於鎂基非晶質合金之非晶質
化能力對於其製備環境(vacuum)及雜質相當敏感，但如果利用稀土元素(如
Y, La)之添加後即可有效地抑制結晶成核並提升其非晶質化能力。本論文主
要在探討稀土元素於鎂基非晶質合金中如何有效吸附氧原子以防止異質成
核結晶之效果。
(3)BMG-based Mg alloys with GPa strength and plasticity, J. Xu, Han Ma, Y. K.
Xu, Chinese Academy of Science, Shenyang, China, and E. Ma, Johns Hopkins
University, USA
作者以(Mg0.65Cu0.075Ni0.075Zn0.05Ag0.05Y0.1)100-xFex (x=9,13)為主成份探討不互
溶固溶體析出於非晶質基地之作用，實驗結果顯示原本脆性 Mg 基非晶質合
金於形成 Fe 析出相之複合結構後，其抗壓強可高達 1GPa 且具有 2%之塑性
變形，可見其靱性由此大大地提升且具有工業應用潛力，另外，亦展示填加
硬顆粒 TiB2於 Mg0.65Cu0.075Ni0.075Zn0.05Ag0.05Y0.1合金中也可於獲得相同甚至
更好的性能。是故只要有效地阻斷 Shear band 於非晶質合金基地之傳播，
將可大幅提升非晶質合金之機械性質。
(4)Non-equilibrium arc-melted binary Ti-Fe bulk alloys with ultra-high strength
and enhanced ductility, D. V. Louzguine, H. Kato, and A. Inoue, Institute for
Materials Research, Tohoku University, Japan
利用簡單的氣氛保護電弧熔煉方式熔製 Ti65Fe35 合金，僅於水冷銅模之冷
却速率即可獲得 TiFe 初晶及次微米尺寸共晶組織之複合結構，由於 TiFe 初
晶可以有效轉移 Shear band 的傳播方向，所以可以獲得非常好的機械性質，
FS 2.2 GPa, YS 1.8 GPa, and 6.7% elongation.
