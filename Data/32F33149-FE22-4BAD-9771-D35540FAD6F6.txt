Introduction 
Magnesium (Mg) alloys are gaining more importance as a lightest structural material for 
light-weighted application, owing to their low density and high stiffness-to-weight ratio. Even so, 
Mg alloys have not been used for critical performance applications owing to their lower mechanical 
properties compared to other engineering materials. Hence, many researchers attempt to fabricate 
Mg-based metal-matrix composites (Mg MMCs) by varied methods to obtain lightweight materials 
with excellent mechanical properties [1-4]. 
Regardless of the potential of Mg MMCs, their studies have been relatively limited when 
compared to the abundance of Al MMC investigations over the last two decades. However, research 
on wear of those two materials is even scarcer. Bindumadhavan et al. [5] studied the wear and 
mechanical properties of A356 aluminium alloy dual particle size (DPS) composites reinforced with 
up to 15 vol. % of SiC particles. It revealed that the maximum effective increase in wear resistance 
occurred for the composite with about 7 vol. % SiC. Sahin and Acilar [6] developed a vacuum 
infiltration process to produce aluminium alloy composites containing various volume fractions of 
ceramic particles. In additional to aluminium powder, magnesium was used to improve the wetting 
of SiC particles by the molten aluminium alloy.  
Regarding to Mg-based metal-matrix composites, Ugandhar et al. [7] successfully synthesized 
Mg MMCs with sub-micron size SiC particulate reinforcements using an innovative Disintegrated 
Melt Deposition technique followed by hot extrusion. Abachi et al. [8] investigated the wear 
behavior of SiCp/QE22 magnesium alloy matrix composites. Their results showed that their wear 
resistance of the matrix alloy could be increased by incorporation of SiC particles.  Modal and 
Kumar [9] investigated the wear behavior of a creep-resistant AE42 magnesium alloy and its 
composites reinforced with Saffil short fibres and SiC particles in various combinations. It was 
found that the presence of SiC particles in the hybrid composites improves the wear resistance 
because the particles remain intact and retain their load bearing capacity even at the highest load 
applied, they promote the formation of iron-rich transfer layer and they also delay the fracture of 
Saffil short fibres to higher loads. Murthy et al. [10] studied the abrasive wear behavior of SiC 
whisker-reinforced borosilicate and magnesium aluminosilicate composites under different 
conditions. Their results reveal that the wear resistance increased by increasing volume fraction of 
whiskers. Semenov et al. [11] presented the tribological contact characteristics of R18 tool steel in 
interface with AZ91D magnesium alloy hardened with SiC dispersed powder filler and by severe 
plastic deformation (SPD)— specifically, equal-channel angular pressing (ECAP). SPD of the 
original material leads to reduction of the molecular component of the friction coefficient. Lee et al. 
[12] investigated the microstructures and wear property of friction stir welded (FSW) AZ91 Mg 
alloy/SiC particle reinforced composite (AZ91/SiC/10p). The initial microstructures of the 
AZ91/SiC/10p were composed of irregularly distributed β-phases (Al12Mg17) and agglomerated 
SiC particles, while the friction stir weld zone was characterized by the homogeneous distribution 
of SiC particles, the recrystallized grain structure and the dissolution of b-phase. Lim et al. [4] 
studied the wear behavior of Mg MMCs with SiCp during sliding. The composites exhibit slightly 
superior wear resistance under the low load, but the effects of the SiC particle reinforcements on

Temperature effect. Temperatures during test of MMCs versus volume fraction of SiCp in 
AZ91D MMCs loaded up to 10N and 50N are shown in Fig. 2, which is measured in side the 
MMCs at 3 mm apart from contact surface. It can be observed that for light 10N loading conditions, 
most of the temperatures are lower than 60°C for all sliding speeds. But for severe 50N loading 
conditions, most of the temperatures are bigger than 50°C for all sliding speeds. The temperature of 
MMCs increases (over 100°C) with increasing volume fraction of SiCp for the severe sliding 
condition of 50N and 1500 rpm, which can explain why the weight loss increases with increasing 
volume fraction of SiCp, and exception of the COF decreasing with increasing sliding speed at 1500 
rpm under loading 50N happened. Because of the softening and melting of the AZ91D matrix 
materials at temperature over 100°C (at the sliding condition of 50N and 1500 rpm), weight loss 
increases and COF became bigger than that of sliding condition of 50N and 1000 rpm.

Weight loss. Fig. 5 shows the weight loss of MMCs versus volume fraction of SiCp in 
AZ91D MMCs loaded up to 10N and 50N. It can be found that the weight loss decreased with 
increasing volume fraction of SiCp for light 10N loading conditions at any specific sliding speed 
(250, 1000 or 1500 rpm), which is inversely proportional to its hardness. Here shows the effect of 
addition of SiCp to AZ91D alloy on wear resistance, which is in accordance with the results of many 
MMCs’ researches. However, the weight loss increases with increasing volume fraction of SiCp for 
the severe sliding condition of 50N and 1500 rpm.  For the sliding condition of 50N and 1000 rpm, 
the weight loss first increases with increasing volume fraction of SiCp., then decreased. The reason 
of these results is associated with softening and melting of the AZ91D matrix materials caused by 
frictional heating at the sliding interface, which cause extensive material transfer to the disc. 
However, the SiCp/AZ91D MMCs exhibit superior wear resistance under lower and moderate 
sliding condition than AZ91D alloy.

the tribological behavior of SiCp reinforced AZ91D Mg-based MMCs. The present Mg-based 
MMCs were fabricated by the melt-stirring technique, and performed excellent wear resistance 
properties. Based on the experimental results, the following conclusions and some important
