摘要 
 
本文使用商用有限元素軟體 LS-Dyna 來建構震波管和複合材料平板的有限元素模型，並將震波的
初始條件、空氣和複合材料平板的材料性質等參數輸入，進而模擬震波衝擊複合材料平板的動態行為。
並利用成功大學航太所震波實驗室的震波管以及結構實驗室的訊號處理器、示波器、應變規和高速攝
影機等數據擷取儀器來進行震波管衝擊實驗，量測複合材料平板之位移以及應變。將模擬的數值與實
驗的數據做比對與驗證，驗證主要分成三大部份：1.震波驗證；2.震波馬赫數 1.3 ~ 1.6 衝擊複合材料平
板的位移量驗證；3.震波馬赫數 1.3 ~ 1.6 衝擊複合材料平板應變值驗證，最後再將驗證的結果作討論。
在震波驗證方面，利用理論與商用有限元素軟體模擬比對驗證。透過驗證可以得知震波的物理現象，
其模擬的結果理論值都非常的相似。在震波馬赫數 1.3 ~ 1.6 衝擊複合材料平板的位移驗證，利用高速
攝影機所量測到的實驗結果與 LS-Dyna 模擬的結果大致相同；而震波馬赫數 1.3 ~ 1.6 衝擊複合材料平
板的應變驗證方面，比較實驗與模擬的第一個應變峰值，其結果也非常的接近。 
 
關鍵字：LS-Dyna 有限元分析、震波、震波管、複合材料平板 
 
Abstract 
 
This study deals with numerical 3D simulation, through the code LS-Dyna, of the effects caused by shock 
wave generated by shock tube on clamped-free-clamped-free rectangular plates made of composite material. 
Then we input the initial condition of high and low pressure section, material property of air and composite 
plates to simulate the dynamic response of composite plates due to shock wave loading. For experiment, 
shock tube, signal conditioning amplifier, oscilloscope, strain gauge, and high speed camera are used to 
measure the displacement and strain of composite plates. The main output results of the simulations are 
presented and compared with the experiments preformed at the shock tube laboratory and structure laboratory, 
Institute of Aeronautics and Astronautics, National Cheng Kung University, Tainan, Taiwan. The verification 
apart from three parts：1. The shock wave. 2. The displacement of composite plates due to shock wave loading 
for Mach number 1.3 ~ 1.6. 3. The strain for composite plate due to shock wave loading for Mach number 1.3 
~ 1.6. For the verification of displacement and strain, it follows that the numerical simulations are in a good 
agreement with the experiments.  
 
Keywords：LS-Dyna FEM analysis, shock wave, shock tube, composite plate

四、 實驗方法與步驟 
 
在製作玻璃纖維強化塑膠平板時，分別使用華立公司所提供的玻璃纖維布，型號為 TGFW-600 (面
密度 areal density 885 g/m2)的 E 型玻璃纖維(E-glass fabric)。基材(matrix)的部分則使用弘鉞公司所提供
的環氧樹脂(epoxy)和硬化劑(hardener)，型號為 Araldite GY 521 和 Aradur HY 956，其兩者以重量比
100:20 混合攪拌至溶液澄清為止。製作玻璃纖維強化塑膠平板時，先剪裁 250mm x 250mm 的一片玻璃
纖維布。玻璃纖維強化塑膠平板的製作方式是採用手積層(hand layer-up)，先將此平面編織纖維布(woven 
roving fabric)放置真空袋內，再將事先混合好的基材，適量地倒入真空袋中，再利用滾筒將試片上多餘
的氣泡擠出，最後將真空袋緊密封緊，上方以重物加壓，放置室溫約 1 天使其硬化成型。再將製作完
成的玻璃纖維強化塑膠平板切割如尺寸 65.5mm × 60mm，分別在衝擊板的上下邊緣約 1cm 處，各鑽
上 4 個螺絲孔。將 TML 的應變規貼於玻璃纖維強化塑膠平板距離中央上方 1cm 。將其應變規的導線
接於訊號放大器和示波器，即可進行震波管衝擊實驗的量測。 
震波管的部分，使用均勻的聚酯薄膜(mylar)作為高低壓段的隔片，並且根據不同的馬赫數更換不
同厚度的隔片。在馬赫數 1.3 時，使用 0.06mm 隔片；在馬赫數 1.4 到 1.6 的實驗中，由於高壓段的壓
力亦造成 0.06mm 隔片的變形，因此選擇使用 0.109mm 隔片，經過上述的準備後，即可開始進行馬赫
數 1.3 到 1.6 的震波管實驗。馬赫數 1.5 和 1.6 的震波衝擊實驗，必須將低壓段的壓力降低，因此在壓
下擊發鈕前，必須先利用低壓段的抽氣機將壓力抽至設定值，即可避免因壓力太大而導致震波管實驗
儀器的損壞。 
在開始實驗之前，首先啟動壓縮機和除濕機，充氣約 25~30 分鐘，使接於高壓段後的儲氣槽，充
至所需的壓力(大約 8~10 公升)，充完氣後關閉壓縮機的開關。接著開啟連接壓力計的電源供應器
(piezontronic)和數位延遲/脈衝波產生器(digital delay / pluse generator)。在放入含有衝擊板的基座前，先
將震波管低壓段內的異物清除，如前次打完的隔片碎片，再把含有衝擊板的基座裝於震波管的低壓段
尾端並將底座與震波管的低壓段尾端鎖緊。接著，選定實驗的入射震波馬赫數，決定 P4/P1 值。 
其震波管(如圖 2 所示)的實驗過程: 
1. 將舊的隔片換下，並換上新的隔片。 
2. 將裁剪好的方格紙貼於震波管低壓段的觀測視窗。

5.2 震波馬赫數 1.3 ~ 1.6 衝擊複合材料平板的位移量驗證 
在震波衝擊的實驗過程中，利用高速攝影機拍攝玻璃纖維強化塑膠平板受震波衝擊的形變(即 z 方
向的最大位移量)，並根據所拍攝的圖片來測量玻璃纖維強化塑膠平板的位移量。因此在震波管低壓段
內的觀測視窗貼上方格紙做為量測的比例尺，其方格紙的尺寸為每格 1.5 mm×1.5 mm，使玻璃纖維強
化塑膠平板的形變量在尺寸上有個標準，並以每秒 24500 張圖片的速度拍攝。在模擬方面，只取平板
的模型，並將平板模型調整至觀測視窗相同的位置，如此可以顯示出各時間點平板的位移量。馬赫數

The SMS is an aluminum box of size 20 cm x 20 cm x 30 cm (height) and the overall mass is 24 Kg. The 
cross-shaped ULF payload box is positioned on top of the satellite body. The TCS adopts an NCKU self-fabricated 
MEMS-based temperature sensors for temperature monitoring. These sensors are mounted on appropriate positions to 
measure the temperature. In addition, patch heaters are used as active thermal control devices. The micro-satellite is 
3-axis stabilized. The ADCS provides active (magnetic coils) and passive control (gravity gradient boom) to stabilize 
the satellite. The gravity gradient boom is comprised of 16 tubular elements with total deployed length 2.96 m. The 
spacecraft pointing accuracy is in the range of < 5º. The EPS is comprised of surface-mounted solar cells, batteries 
(Li-ion) and the DRU (Distribution and Regulation Unit). An average on-orbit power of 19.8 W is being provided. The 
C&DH employs COTS (Commercial off the shelf) devices consisting of CPU, DIO (Digital Input Output), ADC 
(Analog Digital Converter), and DAC (Digital Analog Converter). About 1.4 MByte of data are being generated per 
orbit.  
The satellite payload includes a ULF payload for earthquake precursor research, a GPS payload experiment, a DSS 
(Digital Sun Sensor) payload for attitude control usage and an ECP (Experiment of Communication Payload) for the 
high frequency communication experiment. 
To verify and validate the design of the SMS, it is imperative to analyze and test the vibration response of the 
satellite during ground handling, launch, and in space. The approach and results of the vibration and impact analysis and 
test of the LEAP microsatellite circuit boards are discussed in the following sections. 
 
 2. VIBRATION RESPONSE SPECTRAL ANALYSIS 
In this study, a vibration response spectral analysis was adopted to characterize the dynamic response of a printed 
circuit board (PCB) mounted in the core module of the LEAP micro-satellite subjected to the prescribed impact/shock

multi-degree-of-freedom system and the ground excitation loads were applied in terms of the prescribed acceleration 
power spectral density (PSD) curves. Theoretical basis of the vibration response spectral analysis can be found in [4] 
and some important equations are described in the following sections.

4. PRINTED CIRCUIT BOARD (PCB) DROP IMPACT TESTS & RESULT 
This study employs a PC-104 printed circuit board (PCB) controller for dynamic structural response investigation. 
The PC-104 PCB controller is a core device mounted on the LEAP micro-satellite and is considered to be the most 
vulnerable component subjected to the impact environment. The board level drop test method of components for 
handheld electronic products, i.e. the JESD22-B111 standard, is referenced to conduct the current drop impact tests [6]. 
While most of the loading conditions follow what JEDEC regulates, the bare PC-104 PCB board is used as the test 
board and was mounted vertically instead of horizontally on a supporter. Fig. 5 shows that the PCB controller board was 
screwed at its four corners to the copper standoffs. The copper standoffs are screwed into a steel supporter which then is 
secured on the steel heavy drop table. The signals were acquired through two accelerometers after impact. 
Accelerometer #1 was mounted on an L-bracket connected to the PCB test board so that the accelerometer #1 could 
pick up signal in the direction of the drop impact. Accelerometer #2 was mounted on top of the steel drop table in order 
to pick up the current impulsive signal after impact initiated. This impulsive signal can be used as the input to the 
support of the PCB test board (i.e. the standoffs). During the drop impact test, drop table falls along the guide rods and

 6.2. MODEL ANALYSIS OF THE PCB TEST BOARD 
The numerical model describe in Sec. 5 was created to perform modal analysis. For the numerical modal analysis, 
the PCB test board is bonded with the copper standoffs and the other end of the standoff rods are modeled to be fixed at 
their roots. The PCB test board has a mass density of 1,900 kg/m3 made of glass reinforced plastic (i.e. GFRP) and the 
constitutive relationship of the PCB test board is assumed to behave transversely isotropically. The in-plane (x-y plane) 
and out-of-plane elastic modulus are 16.2 GPa and 7.4 Gpa, respectively, and its in-plane and out-of-plane shear moduli 
are 6.043 GPa and 8.042 Gpa, respectively. (See Table 1) The copper standoffs are assumed to behave isotropically with 
an elastic modulus of 110 Gpa, Poisson’s ratio of 0.33, and density of 8,900 kg/m3. After performing the modal analysis, 
the mode shapes and corresponding modal frequencies of the PCB test board were obtained but not presented here. 
Among these analyzed eigenmodes, the 18th mode is the most significant one and it has the largest modal coefficient 
suggesting that the 18th eigenmode dominants dynamic structural response.  
 
 6.3. VIBRATION RESPONSE SPECTRAL ANALYSIS 
The vibration spectrum analysis with prescribed random vibration loading type was used to analyze the dynamic
