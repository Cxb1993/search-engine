一、 前言 
如眾所皆知的，於流體力學中，描述流體的運動有 Eulerian 與 Lagrangian 兩種不同的
方式。就物理觀點而言，Eulerian 方式是於空間中各固定的位置點處，來觀測恰好通過這些
點處之流體質點的運動行為；而 Lagrangian 方式是針對個別的流體質點，觀測它們於時空
中運動所走過的軌跡上之運動行為。就數學表示而言，以 Eulerian 方式來描述流場，是直
接以時間和空間位置的函數形式表示之，即其以時間和流體質點所佔的空間位置作為獨立
變數，而流場中之各物理量為因變數；而以 Lagrangian 方式描述流場是以時間和標註流體
質點的參數作為獨立變數，而流場中之各物理量包括流體質點的軌跡位置等皆為因變數。
因此，就描述流場特性之表面直覺上的簡便性而言，直接以時空函數形式來展現的 Eulerian
方式，當然優於以參數函數形式來間接表現的 Lagrangian 方式。亦因此，Eulerian 方式於數
學解析處理上亦較 Lagrangian 方式簡單，此乃因以 Lagrangian 方式來描述流場特性時，先
不管其解析求解之困難不易，而既使求得其解後，仍須多一道將所得之參數函數再對應到
以空間位置表示之處理。雖然如此，Lagrangian 方式可完全含括整個流場所有特性的描述，
然 Eulerian 方式卻有無法直接描述流體質點的運動軌跡之虞。這兩種描述流體運動之不同
方式的優缺點，當然亦出現於流體中之自由表面波動現象的描述裡。 
由於以 Eulerian 方式來描述流體運動現象，在數學解析處理上較為容易。因此，自
Stokes(1847)以此方式利用攝動展開近似法 (the perturbation expansion approximation 
method)，來對任一均勻等深之流體中的自由表面重力波動現象進行解析描述後，以 Eulerian
方式來描述流體中波動問題的研究，就獨佔鱉頭式地由橫與縱的兩方面大大地展開。於橫
向方面的發展上，從較深時的 Stokes 波推展到較淺時的橢圓函數波(Cnoidal wave; Korteweg 
& de Vries, 1895)，甚至到極淺時的孤立波(Solitary wave; Boussinesq, 1871;Rayleigh, 1876; 
McCowan, 1891; 陳，1983)等等 ;而於縱向方面的進展上，其理論解析推展到第五階
(Skjelbreia, 1963; Fenton, 1985, 1990; 陳，1989)，而數值解甚至已計算到數十階至百階
(Cokelet, 1977; Rienecker & Fenton, 1981; Williams, 1981; 林，1988)等，以逼近至最高波的
程度。至於解析傳遞於變動水深的波動場，往昔最常用的方法為應用等水深之波動解，再
由能通量守恆原則(conservation of energy flux)階梯式地逐步處理波動隨水深變化之情形，以
解釋波高、波速及波長在水深變動過程中之變化，如 Sverdrup 等(1944)、Le Méhauté 等(1964)
及黃等(1986)等學者之論述；顯然地，此種解析方法無法適當地呈現出底床坡度的效應。對
於斜坡影響效應，Biesel(1952)在線性化自由表面邊界條件下，以深海之微小振幅波前進在
小坡度之底床上，提出了其流場結構解的近似式來闡述其流場特性的變化，但可惜的是並
未闡述該近似式如何推導出且未討論碎波之現象，致往後學者無法窺視其全貌，此可參見
陳(1992,1997)、陳等(1999)等學者之論述。而陳等(2003a,2003b,2004,2005)、楊(2004)及許
(2005)則有針對斜坡底床前進之表面波動的非線性問題作了較完整的描述。 
以 Lagrangian 方式描述流體中之波動問題研究，第一篇論文即所謂的餘擺線波
(Trochoidal wave)，是早由捷克數學家 Gerstner(1802)以 Lagrangian 方式描述下所提出的。
然因以 Lagrangian 方式描述下求解流場解，涉及到數學解析處理的高度困難性，致使以此
方式對流體中之波動現象更滿足的描述受到嚴重的延滯。Rankine(1863)亦獨自以Lagrangian
方式對流體中之自由表面波動問題描述之，且得到與 Gerstner 完全相同的解，可是此解僅
適用於深海情況之第一階線性解而已；Gaillard(l904)雖修正此解於有限深度水中，然卻並
未能滿足。Miche(1944)再以 Lagrangian 方式展開，推廣至任一均勻等深之流體中的自由表
面規則重力波動問題的描述，並且更進一步求得其流場至第二階的解。此外 Pierson(1962)

5.1 質點軌跡  
在前進波流場中，對線性波而言，其流體質點的運動軌跡為一封閉的隋圓形
狀，而對非線性波而言，其流體質點的運動軌跡為非封閉而近似橢圓的交叉旋進曲
線形狀；且此兩者之流體質點的運動軌跡，其起伏高度會隨著流體質點所在的深度
增加而遞減，至底床處時流體質點是沿著底床作前後來回的運動。誠如上述，前人
之理論，如以 Stokes 波、橢圓函數波、孤立波理論等等，來描述規則前進波傳遞
於斜坡底床上時，皆無法展現流體質點運動軌跡的橫軸會隨著其所在的深度及底床
坡度α 之變化。針對此，本文理論可給予說明之。

Lewy [4], Stoker [5] and Lowel [6] solved the problem of waves over a limited number of singular values of
sloping beach, but their solutions cannot be satisfactorily applied to arbitrary bottom conditions. Peters [7] proposed
an integral analytic form to express the surface wave propagating along the sloping bottom and the solution has
recently been calculated numerically by Ehrenmark [8]. Keller [9] derived an alternative linear form for progressive
waves on an uneven bottom, however, the solution was implicit and was valid only under the condition of deep
water wave and gentle bottom slope. Unfortunately, most of the above-mentioned theories could not present the
complete wave transformation on sloping bottom.
Biesel [10] suggested an applausive approximate method to account for the normal incident waves propagating
on a sloping plane, and he used the bottom slope α as a small parameter for expanding the velocity potential in terms
of a perturbation expansion. Peregrine and Ryrie [11] applied a numerical model to calculate the finite-amplitude
wave over a stepped bottom instead of a gentle slope, however; the effect of bottom slope was not still included
in their paper. Recently, advances have been made in mild slope equations and Boussinesq equations from a set of
equations to construct numerical models which are capable of simulating the wave propagating on a sloping beach
by Nwogu [12], Wei et al. [13], Kennedy et al. [14] and other researches. However, to date only few scholars present
analytic solution for wave transformation on a planar beach. Chen and Tang [15] modified Biesel’s [10] theoretical
model and obtained the solution to the first order of α, and then Chen [16] has initially extended to the third order
of α, which was also verified by experiments in Chen and Chang [17]. Chen et al. [18] derived an expression of the
velocity potential in terms of beach slope α to the second order for a progressive wave propagating obliquely over
a gentle plane slope.
Above description shows that a rational and explicit analytical solution has not yet been obtained on surface
wave propagating over a sloping bottom. In order to examine the effect of sloping bottom on surface waves, a
perturbation expansion was used to derive an expression of the velocity potential in terms of the wave steepness ε to
the first order and the bottom slope α to the third order. The free surface boundary conditions were first linearized,
and the physical quantities related to the wave motion were expanded in terms of the bottom slope α so that the
analytical solution of the velocity potential could be derived as a function of the bottom slope α to the third order
in the Eulerian coordinate system. Then the analytical solution of the wave profile in Lagrangian coordinate system
on a given bottom could be obtained and comparisons with the experimental data were made and discussed in this
paper.

bottom is nonlinear; hence, the nonlinear Stokes wave theory has been applied by many researchers. However,
in these previous studies the sloping bottom was approximated by a ﬁnite number of steps. The eﬀect of the
bottom slope could not be fully exhibited using such an approach.
Lewy [4], Stoker [5], and Lowel [6] described the exact solutions of the shallow water equations on beaches
of uniform slope for certain beach angles, but the linearized solutions cannot be satisfactorily applied to arbi-
trary bottom slopes. Peters [7] proposed an integral analytic form to express the surface wave propagating
along the sloping bottom and the solution has recently been calculated numerically by Ehrenmark [8]. Keller
[9] derived an alternative linear form for progressive waves on an uneven bottom; however, the solution was
expressed as an implicit form and was restricted to the short deep-water wave on a gentle sloping beach. The
above-mentioned theories cannot predict the complete wave transformation on a sloping bottom.
Biesel [10] suggested a plausive approximation method to account for the normal incident waves propagat-
ing on a sloping plane where the bottom slope a was ﬁrst considered in the velocity potential as a perturbation
parameter. To date, only a few investigators have presented analytic solutions for the wave transformation on
a planar beach. Chen and Tang [11] modiﬁed Biesel’s [12] theoretical model and obtained the solution to the
ﬁrst order of a. Chen and Yang [13] derived the expression of the velocity potential in terms of the beach slope
a to the second order for a progressive wave propagating obliquely over a gently planar beach. However, the
above analytical solutions are limited to the linearized free surface condition, thus only the ﬁrst order wave
steepness is considered.
For nonlinear waves on the beach, Carrier and Greenspan [14] gave an analytical solution for the shallow
water wave motion of ﬁnite-amplitude, non-breaking waves on a beach of constant slope. Chu and Mei [15]
and Liu and Dingemans [16] proposed perturbation solutions for weakly nonlinear waves propagating over
anunevenbottom.The problemof nonlinearwave propagationover a sloping bottom is not onlymathematically
challenging, but also involves physically curious phenomenon. Most of previous papers added the nonlinear
quantities by amethod resembling the linear solution. This approach cannot be extended to higher order nonlin-
ear solutions [8].
Based on the statements above, it is obvious that a rational and explicit nonlinear analytic solution has not
yet been obtained for nonlinear waves propagating over a sloping bottom. There are three key points concern-
ing this problem: (1) The inﬂuence of the bottom slope factor and the continuous change in water depth have
not been suitably considered. (2) The mass ﬂux conservation of the ﬂow ﬁeld, which is extended to the shoreline
of the sloping bottom, cannot be satisﬁed either. (3) The nonlinear analytic solution for the above physical phe-
nomenon has not yet been available. A linear solution of this problem was given in Part 1 [17], and the objective
of this paper, Part 2, is to present a rigorous and newmathematical derivation for nonlinear water surface waves
propagating over sloping bottoms. Based on the principle of wave motion with nonlinear free-surface boundary
conditions and the conservation of mass ﬂux in the domain bounded by sloping bottom, the later condition has
been previously neglected, the nonlinear analytical solution for the velocity potential is derived as a two-param-
eter function of the nonlinear ordering parameter e and the bottom slope a perturbed to the fourth order (eman,
m + n = 4) in the Eulerian coordinate system. The time-decoupled solution is found to estimate the mean return
current. Besides, there is a direct inﬂuence of nonlinearity and bottom slope upon the wave number and a new
second-order dispersion relation is also derived. In the limiting case where the water depth is either inﬁnite or
ﬁnite, the new nonlinear solution is reduced to those derived by Stokes [1] and Fenton [2]. Moreover, both the
asymmetry of the wave proﬁle and the set-down can be obtained. Finally, by comparing with the experimental
values for the wave asymmetry parameter and wave set-down, the theoretical results of the nonlinear analytical
solution show good agreement with experimental data for arbitrary bottom slope and wave steepness.

5.2. Transformation to the Lagrangian system
It is known that an Eulerian description at the free surface y = g can always be expressed as a Taylor
series of functions at y = 0, which implicitly assumes that the surface proﬁle is diﬀerentiable. This
assumption limits the applicability of Eulerian description in near-break condition. On the other hand,
in the Lagrangian description the free surface is represented by a ﬁxed level of the parameter of the
particle in the space of the Lagrangian domain. It is more appropriate for the Lagrangian description
of limiting free surface motion [21,22]. Thus, we could expect that the solutions in the Lagrangian form
would be able to describe eﬀects that cannot be captured by classical Eulerian solutions. As a wave
shoals, the waveform becomes asymmetric and the particle motion can be obtained by the nonlinear
transformation between Eulerian and Lagrangian coordinates. The Lagrangian velocity may be estimated
from the Eulerian velocity by [23]

6. Conclusions

this problem has not yet been treated appropriately in previous studies. In explicit form both the velocity
potential and the surface elevation are expanded as a two-parameter function of the nonlinear ordering
parameter e and the bottom slope a perturbed to the fourth order (eman, m + n = 4) in the Eulerian coor-
dinate system.

    Nonlinear Water Waves Propagating on a Sloping Bottom in Lagrangian Coordinates 
Yang-Yih  Chen1.,Meng-Syue Li1.,Hung-Chu Hsu2.,Kuei-Sen Yang1. 
1. Dept. of Marine Environment and Eng., National Sun Yat-Sen Univ., TAIWAN, China 
2. Tainan Hydraulics Lab., Research Center of Ocean Environment and Technology, National Cheng Kung Univ.,TAIWAN, China 
 
 
 
ABSTRACT 
 
A new asymptotic solution describing nonlinear water wave 
propagation on the surface of a uniform sloping bottom is derived in the 
Lagrangian coordinates. We use the two-parameter perturbation method 
to develop a new mathematical derivation. The particle trajectories, 
wave pressure and Lagrangian velocity potential are obtained as a 
function of the nonlinear ordering parameter ε  and the bottom slope α perturbed to second order. The analytical solution in Lagrangian 
form satisfies the zero pressure at the free surface. The condition of the 
conservation of mass flux is examined in detail for the first time. Then, 
the solution is used to estimate the mean return current for waves 
progressing over the sloping bottom. The Lagrangian solution enables 
the description of the features of wave shoaling in the direction of wave 
propagation from deep to shallow water, as well as the process of 
successive deformation of a wave profile and water particle trajectories 
leading to breaking. The nonlinear analytical solution is verified by 
reducing to the Lagrangian second-order solution of progressive waves 
in both the limit of deep water and of constant water. 
 
KEY WORDS:  Lagrangian approach; sloping bottom; nonlinear 
water waves; perturbation; wave breaking; particle trajectory.  
 
INTRODUCTION 
 
The motion of a fluid particle within a propagating surface wave may 
be described by either observing the fluid velocity at a fixed position or 
the trajectory of a particle that is carried along with the flow. These 
alternative descriptions are called Eulerian and Lagrangian approach, 
respectively. For an incompressible fluid, the Eulerian approach is 
clearly prefered, because of the corresponding continuity equation is 
linear. It is also well known that the Eulerian description for a free 
surface can always be expressed in Taylor series at a fixed water level, 
which implicitly assumes that the surface profile is a differentiable 
single-valued function. In the Lagrangian approach, however, the 
surface elevation is specified through the position of a surface particle, 
i.e., a particle whose vertical parameter is equal to zero. Unlike an 
Eulerian surface, which is given as an implicit function, a Lagrangian 
form is expressed through a parametric representation of particle motion. 
Hence, the Lagrangian description is more appropriate for limiting the 
free surface motion, whereas this unique feature cannot be represented 
by the classical Eulerian solutions (Biesel 1952, Naciri and Mei 1993, 
Chen et al. 2005, 2006, and Buldakov et al. 2006).  
 
The first water wave theory in Lagrangian coordinates was obtained by 
Gerstner (1802) who assumed the flow possesses finite vorticity. Miche 
(1944) proposed a perturbation method for Lagrangian solution to a 
second order for a gravity wave motion. Pierson (1962) also applied 
perturbation expansion to water wave problems with Lagrangian 
formulae and obtained a first-order Lagrangian solution. Buldakov et al. 
(2006) developed a Lagrangian asymptotic formulation up to a fifth-
order for nonlinear water waves in deep water. However, all the 
theories mentioned above are rotational and are limited to the condition 
of uniform water depth. To date, only a limited few analytic solutions 
are derived for wave transformation on a planar beach in Lagrangian 
coordinates. Among them, Sanderson (1985) obtained a second-order 
solution in a uniformly stratified fluid with a small bottom slope in a 
Lagrangian system. Constantin (2001) considered the first-order 
Lagrangian solution for edge wave on a sloping beach. Chen and 
Hwung (2000) derived a Lagrangian solution in terms of beach slope α 
to the second order for a progressive wave propagating over a gentle 
plane slope, while Kapinski (2006) studied the run-up of a long wave 
over a uniform sloping bottom in Lagrangian description. 
 
The purpose of this paper is to development a rational and explicit 
analytical solution for surface waves propagating over a sloping bottom 
in Lagrangian description. In order to examine the effect of a sloping 
bottom on surface waves, a perturbation expansion is used to derive an 
expression for the particle trajectories in terms of wave steepness ε  
and the bottom slope α  to a second-order. The analytical solutions for 
physical quantities related to the wave motion are then expanded in 
terms of the wave steepness and the bottom slope. Thus the wave 
profile obtained by vertical label (i.e., vertical displacement parameter 
in Lagrangian approach) is equal to zero at free surface in which the 
zero pressure is satisfied. Finally, discussion is drawn from the physical 
quantities obtained from the present solutions.

As the height of a wave reaches its limit, the crest is fully developed as 
a summit which can be calculated as the spatial surface profile by a 
system of Lagrangian coordinates. In this approach, the new 
displacement components of water particle x  and y  to the second 
order approximation have been obtained, such as from Eqs. (33) and 
(34). The surface wave profiles η  near the wave breaking point can be 
evaluated and the results illustrated in Fig. 4. These figure shows the 
surface wave profiles prior to breaking on different wave steepness and 
wave phase for bottom slopes of 1/5α =  and1/10 , respectively, based 
on the wave breaking criterion of / 1u c = . The breaker type is 
determined by the deep-water wave steepness and beach slope. For 
wave steepness 0,0 0 0.03k a π′ =  and 0,0 0 0.05k a π′ = , it is found that the 
surface wave profile up to second order at breaking point is similar to a 
plunging breaker on bottom slope of 1/5α = . However, the surface 
profile is similar to a spilling breaker on 1/10α = . Note that as wave 
steepness becomes larger, wave breaking takes places in a deeper water 
region. In particular, for a given wave steepness, a spilling breaker is 
usually found on a gentle bottom slope. The variation of the wave 
profile is less significant and the wave asymmetry is smaller due to the 
shorter propagation distance. That explains why spilling breaker is 
usually found for larger wave steepness. On the contrary, a plunging 
breaker is usually observed on a steep bottom slope due to the wave 
profile has a sufficient distance to develop. These results are in good 
agreement with the classification of wave breakers proposed by Galvin 
(1968). This confirms that the breaker type depends on the bottom 
slopes. 
 
Both linear (up to the order of 1 1ε α ) and nonlinear solution (up to the 
order of 2 0ε α ) are also implemented for comparison and the results are 
shown in Fig. 4. In general, the nonlinear wave profiles are higher than 
the linear solution for any wave steepness and bottom slope. Moreover, 
the breaking point predicted by the nonlinear solution occurs earlier 
than that by the linear solution. We remark here that in the comparisons 
made above, the nonlinear wave profiles prior to breaking point differ 
from the linear wave profiles in three ways: 
 
1. Breaking takes place earlier in a deeper depth. 
2. The wave height at the breaking point is much higher. 
3. After breaking point, the tendency for the wave profile to plunge 
onto the sea bed is reduced.
