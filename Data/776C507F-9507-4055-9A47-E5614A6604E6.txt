溶之效應情況下，而造成總延伸率之降低。

壞之靨渦狀破斷面(dimpled rupture)特徵。 
四、 結論 
  根據上述之實驗結果，本研究可歸納以下
幾點結論： 
1. LZ91-F材之YS、UTS、UE隨拉伸溫度升
高而降低，TE隨拉伸溫度升高而提升達約
55%。LZ91-F材之YS與UTS隨應變速率之
增加而提高，TE則隨應變速率增加而降
低。較慢拉伸應變速率具有較佳之延伸率 
2. LZ91-F經200ºC與300ºC不同條件下之退
火熱處理，發現α相在較高熱處理溫度與
長時間持溫下，會出現固溶現象而使α相
面積率降低，且α相呈現等軸晶之型態。 
3. 本研究LZ91-F鎂鋰合金攪拌改質製程最
佳參數：攪拌棒轉速2896 rpm、進給速率1 
mm/s、攪拌頭傾角1.5º、下壓力15 MPa。 
4. LZ91-F在攪拌過程受塑性流之影響會在
SZ有分散的情形出現，且在AS區中出現細
晶區；該區之硬度值也較其他區域偏高。 
5. 經攪拌製程後，LZ91-FSP合金中部分富鎂
之α相會被固溶進入富鋰之β相，並且經
50ºC熱處理後發現α相在β相晶粒內與晶
界處形成針棒狀網格析出。 
6. XRD與極圖分析結果顯示經FSP改質後，
LZ91-FSP材之ND面晶面方位顯著改變，
且發現顯示經改質後α相之六方晶格將倒
向PD與TD方向，底面會沿攪拌頭旋轉方
向排列之晶面織構。 
7. LZ91-FSP材不論是沿平行或垂直PD方向
進行拉伸，其抗拉強度皆高於LZ91-F材，
但其延伸率低於LZ91-F材。 
8. 當LZ91-FSP材拉伸試驗沿平行PD方向拉
伸時，比起沿垂直PD方向拉伸之LZ91-FSP
材具有較高之抗拉強度。

1. Introduction 
Hydroxyapatite (Ca10(PO4)6(OH)2; HA), the major inorganic constituents of human bones and 
teeth, is a widely used calcium phosphate bioceramic. HA has attracted attention for the past 
decades and considered an excellent hard tissue substitute [1,2] in dentistry and orthopedics due to 
its bioactivity, biocompatibility and excellent osteoconductive properties [3-5]. Different from the 
synthetic HA, however, natural apatite is non-stoichiometric due to the presence of trace ions [6]. 
The substitution of Mg2+, Zn2+ and F− ions in the biological apatite has effects on the crystallinity, 
dissolution kinetics, improving biological performances and clinical applications [7-10]. Relative 
reports indicated that the substitution of Zn2+ improves the thermal stability and the bioactivity of 
HA [8,11]. Since Mg is a main substitute for Ca in biological apatites, the Mg-substituted HA is 
studied and expected to have excellent biocompatibility, biological properties and long term 
stability. In vitro and in vivo studies showed that Mg2+ substituted into apatite crystals contributes to 
the acceleration of osteoblast adhesion to apatite and promotes bone formation [12,13].  
In orthopaedic and dental fields, HA is commonly used as coatings to extend the biological 
responses of metallic prostheses and to improve the adhesion of HA-coated prostheses to the bone 
[14]. Among currently approved biocompatible stainless steel, Co-Cr-based and titanium alloys, a 
HA-coated Ti-based alloy is more favorable for using as the metallic implants because of the 
combination of good osteoconductive properties and the compatibility between elastic moduli of 
bone for load-bearing bone substitutes in orthopaedic surgery [15,16]. In addition, Mg-based alloys, 
which have the lowest density (1.74 g/cm3) of constructional metals, have been attempted and 
identified as potential lightweight implants due to their high strength-to-density ratio. The elastic 
modulus of Mg alloys (41-45 GPa) is closer to the cortical bone (3-20 GPa) and much lower than 
Ti-based alloys (about 110 GPa) [17]. In vitro and in vivo studies indicated that Mg can be used as 
biodegradable medical implants [18,19]. Several Mg-based alloys, such as Mg-Al-Zn, Mg-Zn and 
Mg-RE systems [17,20,21], have been investigated and developed for biomedical applications.

proportional-integral-derivative (PID) controller (Parr 4842) with ± 1°C. The saturated steam 
pressure was 0.48 MPa at 150°C.  
2.2. Coating characterization 
The phase composition of the MAH surface layer and hydrothermally-synthesized HA coatings 
was identified by a grazing-incidence X-ray diffractometer (GI-XRD, Rigaku D/MAX2500), using 
CuKα radiation at 30 kV and 20 mA with a step size of 0.02° (2θ). Scanning electron microscope 
(SEM, Philips XL-40 FEG) was used to observe the surface morphologies of synthesized coatings. 
The X-ray photoelectron spectroscopic (XPS) analysis was performed in a VG Scientific 
ESCALAB 210 XPS system, using a standard AlKα radiation source (hν = 1486.6 eV) at 15 kV 
with a power of 300 W and a base pressure of 5 × 10−9 Torr. Survey spectra (from 0 to 1000 eV) and 
high-resolution spectra of the Mg 2p region were obtained.

Referring to the GIXRD pattern in Fig. 1(b), the diffraction peaks of MAH layer are observed and 
this hydrothermal coating is composed of HA, Mg(OH)2 and Ca(OH)2 phases. It is recognized that a 
homogeneous crystalline HA coating cannot be uniformly synthesized on the MAH layer, and they 
are hydrothermally-precipitated as agglomerated particles on the pre-treated MAH layer as 
displayed in Fig. 2(b). Although the surface stability and corrosion resistance of AZ91D are 
improved with the formation of MAH layer [25], but the synthesis and deposition efficiency of 
hydrothermally-synthesized HA on the pre-treated MAH layer are decreased. Fig. 2(c) and 2(d) 
show the surface morphologies of hydrothermally-synthesized pure HACs and Mg-HACs on the no 
MAH-coated AZ91D substrate, respectively. It can be seen that both of hydrothermal HACs and 
Mg-HACs exhibit uniform surface features. According to the XRD results, crystalline HA is the 
main phase and no diffraction peaks of Mg are observed. It indicated that uniform HACs and 
Mg-HACs can be hydrothermally-deposited on the AZ91D alloy without any surface conversion 
treatment. Fig. 2(e) displays the surface morphology of alkali solution pre-treated Ti-6Al-4V, which 
also has a uniform hydrothermally-synthesized surface coating as shown in Fig. 2(f).  
The chemical states of hydrothermally-synthesized pure HACs and Mg-HACs are analyzed by 
XPS. The survey spectra in Fig. 3(a) demonstrate that Ca, P, Mg and O elements in the coatings are 
detected. Representative high resolution Mg 2p band of various hydrothermally-synthesized HA 
coatings are shown in Fig. 3(b). It is recognized that the symmetrical single peak of Mg 2p band 
located at about 50.3 eV can be attributed to the presence of Mg in HA structure [26]. The Mg 2p 
spectra for the Mg-HACs intensifies with the substitution of Mg2+ ions in the HA crystal structure, 
demonstrating that the formation of the calcium magnesium phosphate phase (Ca7Mg2(PO4)6) 
through the hydrothermal synthesis process. However, the Mg 2p spectrum of hydrothermal 
coatings deposited on the MAH layer is broadened and shifts towards to lower binding energy. It is 
resulted from the presence of Mg(OH)2 and uncoated MAH (Mg6Al2(OH)16CO3•4H2O) layer 
because a homogeneous coating cannot be uniformly synthesized on the MAH layer.

The Weibull modulus can be used to determine which coating has higher uniformity and 
reliability. It is noted that the Weibull modulus is a dimensionless parameter, which determines the 
degree of the uniformity of a mechanical property throughout the specimen, not its absolute value. 
As a result, the Weibull statistical analysis (Table 1) demonstrates that hydrothermally-synthesized 
coatings on AZ91D and Ti-6Al-4V all show a reliable wear-out failure model (m > 3) of increasing 
failure rate (IFR) [28]. In addition, coatings with a larger Weibull modulus can be selected for 
clinical application as it may be an indicator for lesser technique sensitivity. Therefore, evident 
benefits of the cumulative failure probability (Fig. 4(a)) and failure rate (Fig. 4(d)) curves shift to a 
higher adhesive strength with a concentrated data distribution are achieved for Mg-HACs deposited 
on the AZ91D alloy. It also means that we can predict when the failures occurred more precisely.
