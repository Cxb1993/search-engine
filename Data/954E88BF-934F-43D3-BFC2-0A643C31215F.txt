一、中英文摘要 
為了整合空載光達與航空攝影測量兩者的優點俾行截長補短之效，本研究先
根據瀝青路面和道路交通標線兩區的光達回波強度的顯著差異性，設計高斯曲面
匹配(Gaussian Surface Matching, GSM)法來求得兩直交標線之交點，一方面用來估
計與改正相鄰航帶光達點雲坐標基準偏差量，另一方面用來求解空照影像的外方
位元素，俾統一兩者的坐標系統。經由真實資料測試，光達航帶點雲坐標基準經
改正後，同名交點之整體平面偏差量之均方根值從 0.650m 降至 0.286m，符合光達
點之先驗平面精度±0.25m。使用這些基準改正後的點位做為地控點(共 30 點)，施
行空照影像光束法平差，整體空三精度達到±0.67 個像元的次像元精度等級。 
在統一兩者的坐標系統之後，應用小波演算法輔助空載光達點雲過濾，使用
小波進行內插生成格網化地形初始面，以網格化資料結構結合Geodesic Dilation
法、高程差與其變化率判斷、重新篩選過濾機制，逐步將光達點雲分類為地面點
與非地面點，進一步計算產生地表數值高程模型；過程中可參考光學影像特徵(例
如NDVI或GI)，另外針對橋樑區域的光達點雲過濾，應用和橋樑走向正交的掃瞄
方式搜尋橋樑種子點，配合區域成長法偵測橋樑位置。從地形簡單的實驗區一至
地形複雜且多斷線的實驗區二的點雲分類成果顯示，分類總錯誤率分別為0.5%和
6%，成果頗佳，構想驗證為可行。 
關鍵詞：光達、小波、空照影像、分類/過濾、數值地形模型 
ABSTRACT 
For integrating airborne Lidar scanning technique and aerial photogrammetry, this 
research project firstly developed a Gaussian Surface Matching (GSM) approach which 
utilizes the laser reflectance property that the laser intensity on asphalt surface is 
significantly lower than the one on road marking lines, and determines the coordinates 
of the intersection of two orthogonal road marking lines. These intersection points are 
then used on the one hand to estimate and correct the systematic bias between two 
coordinate systems of the overlapping Lidar strips, and on the other hand to determine 
the exterior orientation entries of all aerial images. Tests are done by using real data. 
After systematic bias estimation and correction, the root mean square registration error 
is reduced from 0.650m to 0.286m in horizontal direction. These intersections are then 
used as ground control points in aero triangulation (AT). The AT with the precision of 
±0.67 pixel is achieved. 
After unifying the coordinate systems of Lidar points and aerial images, the 
wavelet approach is used for airborne Lidar data filtering. An initial digital surface 
model (DSM) is generated by wavelet approach. The method utilizes then geodesic 
dilation, height difference and second order difference of heights of neighboring grid 
points, and then re-selects Lidar points by generating a reference surface to select more

六、小波法求定數值地表高程模型 
完成前述處理並統一光達點雲和用來定義影像外方位元素的地面坐標系統之
後，便可進行數值地表高程模型 DEM 之求定工作。而以空載光達點雲製作數值高
程模型時，面臨到兩個最大的問題：(一)如何從複雜的光達點雲覆蓋面上，挑選落
於地面的光達點，(二)面對龐大的點雲資料時，如何提升過濾的品質與效率。 
為了解決上述二個問題俾以獲取測製 DEM 之地面點資料，常見的點雲過濾法
依不同的操作概念可大致分成四類：(一)斜率為基礎、(二)區塊為基礎、(三)面為
基礎、(四)群集/分塊為基礎。以斜率為基礎的操作概念是建立在非地面點與相鄰
地面點之間存有斜率異常變化或斜率驟起之關係，例如：Vosselman(2000)計算光
達點和點之間的斜率，以篩選地面光達點。以區塊為基礎的操作概念是建立在地
面點為局部區塊高程值較低的點位，例如：Petzold et. al. (1999)將搜尋罩窗的尺寸
逐步縮小，視罩窗內與最低點高程值之差值超過門檻值的光達點視為非地面點逐
一剔除，以獲取地面點。Zhang and Chen (2003)則逐漸放大搜尋罩窗，透過膨脹
(dilation)和侵蝕(erosion)迭代操作挑選地面點。以面為基礎的操作概念則是建立於
非地面點與擬合面之間具有高程異常凸起的特性，例如：Kraus and Pfeifer (1998)
以最小二乘法進行曲面擬合，將光達點高程和擬合面高程差超過門檻值之光達點
視為非地面點而予以濾除。群集/分塊為基礎的操作則是將光達點高程視為影像灰

高度做為下降參數值，從實驗的結果中發現，以 3*3 的罩窗迭代滑動，能降低 GD

八、結論與建議 
8.1 結論 
1.此研究運用擴充式最小二乘影像匹配法的原理，發展出一個可靠的新方法
GSM，從空載光達(X,Y,I)點雲匹配道路交通標線區的 I(X,Y)面函數，以解算道路
交通標線參數，進而萃取出兩交會直線之交點，提供光達航帶基準偏差之估計和
改正、與進行光達點雲和空照影像坐標基準套合之用。由光達航帶間之同名標線
交點的平面點位套合結果可知，實驗所用之光達點雲之平面系統偏差方向一致，
由西至東的航帶之坐標基準一致地往東北方向偏移，光達航帶間同名點之平均平

For registration of 3D LiDAR point clouds, another solution 
methods were developed e.g. by Akca (2003), Csanyi and Toth 
(2005), and they utilized LiDAR specific ground targets which 
can be easily recognized from a huge number of LiDAR points.  
Also, a large number of methods are developed for registering 
all LiDAR point clouds scanned at different ground-based 
stations or from multiple airborne strips. They can be roughly 
classified into (1) point-based methods, (2) line-based ones and 
(3) planar polygon-based methods. One of the most well-known 
point-based methods is the Iterative Closest Point (ICP) 
approach presented by Besl and McKay (1992), Chen and 
Medioni (1992), and improved by Zhang (1994), Masuda and 
Yokoya (1995) and Bergevin et al. (1996). Mitra et al. (2004) 
developed a registration method from a geometric optimization 
perspective for faster convergence. Gruen and Akca (2005) 
proposed a method tackling the difficult problem on 3D surface 
and curve matching in a least squares manner. Makadia et al. 
(2006) proposed a fully automatic registration method with the 
property of robustness against weak overlap. 
Stamos and Allen (2002), Stamos and Leordeanu (2003) 
presented approaches for automated feature-based range 
registration of large scale urban scenes by adopting 3D lines on 
the so-called supporting planes which make the matching task 
easy. Jaw and Chuang (2005) presented an approach for 
automatic 3D line-based matching and registration of LiDAR 
point clouds. Lee et al. (2005) used linear features for detecting 
discrepancies between LiDAR data strips. Habib et al. (2005) 
utilized straight-line segments for photogrammetric and LiDAR 
data registration. Kager (2004) used planar patch features for 
simultaneous fitting of aerial laser scanner strips. Moreover, 
both line-based and planar polygon-based methods for LiDAR 
registration are given by (Ghanma, 2006).    
 
2. TIE VOXEL METHOD 
2.1 Meaning and Idea 
Tsay (2007) presented a novel method for 3D LiDAR block 
adjustment without utilizing any accessory such as reference 
targets. Its main idea is to exploit the implicit information and 
geometric conditions on some specific types of volume 
elements (voxels) and to utilize them as “tie voxels”. The novel 
terminology “tie voxel” possesses a similar meaning to the “tie 
point” in aerotriangulation. All corresponding tie voxels try to 
“tie”, namely connect, all neighbouring point clouds to generate 
a larger point cloud in a least squares manner. In our present 
model, all points in a point cloud are assumed to have a 
common coordinate datum. 
It is well-known that diverse types of implicit features such as 
feature points, lines, curves, planes, surfaces and volumes can 
be extracted somehow from 3D LiDAR points. They are the so-
called derived features. The aforementioned implicit 
information might be then simultaneously utilized for 3D 
LiDAR block adjustment. 
 
2.2 Types of Tie Voxels 
Figure 1 illustrates an example of one type of tie voxel, namely 
tie cuboid. There are also surely different types of tie voxels 
such as buildings with pent roof, gable roof, pavilion roof, hip 
roof, hipped-gable roof, cone roof, and half-check roof as well 
as a building with cylindrical side and hemispheric roof. They 
emerge in building areas everywhere on our real world and can 
be formulated by specific mathematical functions.  
2.3 Main Sides of A Tie Voxel 
On our real world, almost always no building owns the ideal 
surface that can be described completely and exactly by the 
related mathematical function. Therefore, the 3D LiDAR points 
on the main sides of a tie voxel must be selected somehow. The 
so-called “main side” means that it is one plane (or surface) of 
the processed voxel. For a tie cuboid building, there should be 
more points well-distributed on the aforementioned main sides 
and main roof plane.

2.4 Parameters of A Tie Cuboid 
Figure 2 illustrates a simple tie cuboid with seven parameters: 
3D coordinates of its corner point S (XS, YS, ZS), width, length 
and height of the cuboid (w1, w2, h) and the orientation angle F. 
The simple cuboid is assumed to have horizontal ground side 
and roof plane. In this 7-parameter model,  the 3D coordinates 
of  any corner points, any edges and any side planes of the 
cuboid can all be formulated definitely and explicitly by these 
seven parameters XS, YS, ZS, w1, w2, h and F. Moreover, if a 
corner point is a ground control point, its observation equation 
can also be formulated by them. 
Furthermore, Figure 2 illustrates apparently that a tie cuboid 
contains point features (8 corner points), linear features (12 
edges) and planar features (6 sides). They are implicit features 
derivable from LiDAR points. After the tie voxel method 
completes the 3D LiDAR block adjustment, these features are 
also determined simultaneously.

KEY WORDS: Registration, 3D Point Cloud, Tie Voxel Method, LiDAR 
 
ABSTRACT: This paper proposes a newly developed method for registration of 3D point 
clouds acquired at different locations or in different coordinate systems. It is called “tie voxel 
method”, in which voxel denotes “volume element”. The motivation, ideas and concrete 
formulation of the tie voxel method are given. Its potential applications are discussed. On the 
one hand, the theoretical quality of the tie voxel method is studied by using simulated data 
points. On the other hand, some tests are also done by using real airborne LiDAR points. They 
verify that the tie voxel method has a good applicability potential. 
 
1. INTRODUCTION 
 
Diverse measuring methods are applicable for acquisition of the geometrical, radio-physical and 
semantic information about interest objects. For example, the light detection and ranging 
(LiDAR) system can acquire a large number of high precision 3D points on local surfaces of 
interest objects in a very short time interval. The airborne and terrestrial LiDARgrammetry 
needs the 3D points acquired respectively at multiple strips and ground-based stations to 
reconstruct complete 3D object models such as cyber city models. Also, long strips are needed 
to collect data in a larger region.  
 
2. THE NEED FOR LiDAR BLOCK ADJUSTMENT 
 
Although these direct geo-referenced 3D points of different strips and stations and in a longer 
strip as well have high precision, they still often have some various types of errors, comprising 
diverse kinds of systematic errors, blunders, stochastic errors and total errors that cannot be 
defined by the systematic error model or the stochastic error model. These errors must be 
corrected somehow.  
Moreover, those 3D points of different strips or ground-based stations must be registered 
somehow to cover an entire terrain surface or object surface, e.g. by means of a suitable datum 
transformation.     
Therefore, LiDAR block adjustment is needed for error correction, registration and datum 
transformation of different strips (stations) of LiDAR points to generate complete high-quality 
LiDARgrammetry products. 
 
3. LiDAR DATA ERRORS 
 
For designing suitable functional and stochastic models adopted in LiDAR block adjustment, the 
error sources and error models must be examined somehow. In general, LiDAR errors involve 
timing error, GPS positioning error, GPS/IMU tracking error, boresight error, errors caused by 
imperfection of LiDAR system calibration, angular resolution and mechanical vibration etc. For 
This paper proposes a new method for 3D LiDAR block adjustment without using any accessory 
such as reference targets. Its core idea is to exploit all implicit information and geometric 
conditions on some volume elements (voxels) and to utilize them as “tie voxels”. It is well-
known that 3D LiDAR points have diverse types of implicit features such as feature points, lines, 
curves, planes, surfaces and volumes. They can be derived from LiDAR points. These implicit 
information might be simultaneously utilized for 3D LiDAR block adjustment. 
    
Figure 2. Two examples of diverse types of tie voxels 
Figure 2 illustrates two examples of diverse types of tie voxels: tie cuboid and cylindrical side of 
a building with hemispheric roof. There are also surely different types of tie voxels such as 
buildings with pent roof, gable roof, pavilion roof, hip roof, hipped-gable roof, cone roof, half-
check roof etc. All can be formulated by specific mathematical functions.  
Nevertheless, there are almost no ideal cuboid buildings in the real world. The 3D points on 
their main sides and roof plane must be selected somehow. There should be more points with 
better spatial distribution on the aforementioned main sides and main roof plane of a tie cuboid 
building. 
Figure 3 illustrates a simple tie cuboid with seven parameters: 3D coordinates of its corner point 
S (XS, YS, ZS), width, length and height of the cuboid (w1, w2, h) and the orientation angle q. The 
simple cuboid is assumed to have horizontal ground side and roof plane. In this 7-parameter 
model,  the 3D coordinates of  any corner points, any edges and any side planes of the cuboid 
can all be formulated by these seven parameters XS, YS, ZS, w1, w2, h and q. Moreover, if a 
corner point is a ground control point, its observation equation can also be formulated by them.

points), linear features (12 edges) and planar features (6 sides). They are implicit features 
derivable from LiDAR points. After the tie voxel method completes the 3D LiDAR block 
adjustment, these features are also determined simultaneously.

7. CONCLUSION AND FUTURE WORKS 
The proposed tie voxel method enables simultaneous adjustment for (1)determination of 
voxel/cuboid/strip parameters (derivation of different implicit features), (2) fitting of LiDAR 
points onto voxels, (3) LiDAR point clouds registration and (4) 3D LiDAR block adjustment. 
Test results verify that the tie cuboid method provides good accuracy and reliability for airborne 
LiDAR point registration, even in case of improper point distribution on each wall plane. The
