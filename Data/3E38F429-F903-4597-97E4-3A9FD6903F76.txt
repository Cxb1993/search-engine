2.英文摘要： 
The study used the electron transport material Bpy-OXD to substitute a part of 
the electron transport layer of Alq3, to upgrade the efficiency and lifetime of the 
device to enable higher luminance efficiency in low current density, its half-lifetime 
will also increase about 162 % . 
The study reuse the spin-coating NPB and α-NPD co-doped to deposit hole 
transport material on ITO plastic substrate in proper order thermal-evaporating to 
deposit NPB on ITO substrate to make flexible OLEDs with double HTL to 
improve the smoothness of the surface of the substrate and the accuracy of the 
concentration of co-doped. The device’s structures are PET/ ITO (160 nm)/ HTL/ 
Alq3 (52 nm)/ Bpy-OXD (15 nm)/ LiF (0.5 nm)/ Al (135 nm). When the HTL is 
spun NPB+THF (37 nm)/ evaporate NPB (41 nm), the maximum luminance was 
3568 cd/m2 at 93.9 mA/cm2, and the maximum efficiency is 3.86 cd/A at 8 V； 
when the HTL is spun (NPB:α-NPD)+THF (58 nm)/ evaporate NPB (41 nm), the 
maximum luminance is 4634 cd/m2 at 9 V, and the maximum efficiency is 4.18 
cd/A at 68 mA/cm2. 
This study used the device’s structures of PET/ ITO (160 nm)/ NPB (41 nm)/ 
Alq3 (67 nm)/ LiF (0.5 nm)/ Al (135 nm)/ passivation, by using the sealing of 
thermal evaporate organic film of flexible OLEDs. In which the organic film used 
the hole inject material m-MTDATA or use the sealing lid by PET plastic without 
ITO after evaporating the sealing film. As a result, use a multi-layer sealing of m-
MTDATA (500 nm)/ PET which its half-lifetime will increase by about 545.85 %, 
when compared with the non-encapsulate device. 
Finally, this study takes the lead to use the AlN instead of glass as the substrate 
of OLED, because the AlN substrate has a good radiation feature, the thermal 
conductivity was approximately 200 W/m．K, compared with the glass of 1.38 
W/m．K, is obviously higher. As a result, when the part is operating under a high 
luminance, and a high current density, it could still effectively reduce the heat with 
the substrate dissipation, avoiding the organic material’s crystallization, which 
could make the weaken faster, to be caused by the joule heat produced by the 
interior device. Also, using PECVD to deposit SiO2 could flat the AlN substrate and 
avoid the point discharge. Then, the device could withstand 27V of driving voltage, 
and the TEOLED could produce a maximum luminance of 3661 cd/m2 and a 
maximum efficiency of 5.05 cd/A. 
 
Key Word：flexible organic light emitting diodes, efficiency, thermal evaporation, 
lighting, large-size, backlight, advertisement board, crucibles, AlN

3.2 元件基板前處理步驟 
3.2.1 ITO 塑膠基板 
將基板放入燒杯內，再將燒杯置於超音波震盪器內，再利用丙酮
(acetone)(General Chemical Corp. UN 1090)清洗 10 秒鐘（玻璃基板要震洗 8 分
鐘）與甲醇(methanol) (General Chemical Corp. UN 1230)震洗 8 分鐘，接著再
使用去離子水(deionized water)震洗 8 分鐘，最後使用氮氣(nitrogen)將基板吹
乾，並將基板置於烤箱內，以 90 ℃烘烤 8 分鐘以去除水氣。 
 
3.2.1 氮化鋁基板 
(1) 使用氮化鋁基板厚度約 0.6 mm、基板面積為 4 cm×4 cm，先以拭鏡紙
將表面異物拭去，並利用氮氣槍將殘留物吹掉。 
(2) 使用自動研磨機，將基板分別以 2000、4000 號的水砂紙各拋光 2 小
時，最後再以 0.1μ的鑽石研磨片拋光 2 小時。 
(3) 使用超音波清洗機，分別將基板浸入丙酮、甲醇、DI water 震洗，時
間分別為 10 分鐘、10 分鐘、10 分鐘，並利用氮氣槍吹乾。 
(4) 基板置入烤箱去除多餘的水氣，溫度 110℃，30 分鐘。 
(5) 基板置入氧電漿清洗機中，抽真空至 2×10-2 torr，通入氧氣 10 sccm，
並固定腔體壓力 2×10-1 torr，功率 25-30 W，時間 1.5 分鐘，利用氧電
漿做玻璃表面清洗動作。 
(6) 基板置入 PECVD 中，通入 20 SCCM TMS 及 40 SCCM O2，在真空壓
力為 3×10-1 torr 下，以 50 W 的功率沉積 SiO2薄膜於基板。 
 
3.3 基板陽極圖案化 
3.3.1 ITO 塑膠基板(下發光元件) 
在 ITO 塑膠基板上蒸鍍有機薄膜之前，先對塑膠基板上的導電 ITO 薄膜進
行圖案化。經由準確設計 ITO 圖案，將即將製作之有機發光二極體元件的正
電極（陽極）牽引至基板的邊緣，同時經由正電極的圖案化，也可以定義有
機發光二極體之發光面積大小。 
以下將敘述使用濕式蝕刻方法，來將塑膠基板上的 ITO 圖案化，其濕式蝕
刻步驟如下： 
1. 光阻塗佈：利用旋轉塗佈(spin-coating)的方法，先用 800 r.p.m.之轉速旋
轉 15 秒後，再用 1800 r.p.m.之轉速旋轉 15 秒，將光阻(photo resist)
（FUJIFILM FH-640L 之正光阻）均勻塗佈在 ITO 塑膠基板上。 
2. 軟烤：將塗佈後之基板放進烤箱中，使用 90 ℃之溫度烘烤 8 分鐘。 
3. 曝光：將光罩放置在基板上，再利用 UV 燈(AB-M Inc. Mask Alignment 
Systems)照射基板進行曝光，曝光功率為 400 W，時間為 10 秒。 
4. 顯影：將曝光後之基板放入顯影液（FUJIFILM FHD-5 四甲基氫氧化氨
(TMAH)之正顯影劑）中，使曝到光之光阻被顯影液給溶解掉，顯影之
時間為 30 秒。 
5. 定影：將顯影完成之基板放入去離子水中，使顯影動作停止，從去離子

電子傳輸層膜厚為 Alq3 (52 nm)/ Bpy-OXD (15 nm) (Device A2)。

愈大，此由空間電荷限制電流理論(space charge limited, SCL)可以推得。但是
在圖 4.1.7 比較不同的電洞阻隔層 Bpy-OXD 厚度之發光亮度對電流密度及圖
4.1.8 發光效率對電流密度特性曲線圖中，卻發現當 Bpy-OXD 厚度在最大值
15 nm 時，發光亮度及發光效率反而最大，這是因為激子的擴散長度約為 20
～30 nm，所以增加電洞阻隔層的厚度，可以使電子-電洞復合區遠離陰極，
進而防止激子擴散至陰極而淬熄，使復合率提高，而且電洞阻隔層 Bpy-OXD
的 HOMO 能階也比發光層 Alq3 來的大，因此產生很大的能障(0.86 eV) 有效
阻擋電洞在發光層與電洞阻隔層的界面，使得元件的發光亮度和發光效率能
有所提昇。

4.5 TEOLED 製作於散熱基板 AlN 之研究 
一般 OLED 元件均製作於玻璃基板，因此在高亮度或高電流密度操作下
時所產生的焦耳熱，會因為玻璃的熱傳導率較低(1.38 W/m‧K)，被積存於元
件內部，造成元件快速毀損。因此，本研究率先使用具有高熱傳導率的 AlN
基板(約 200 W/m‧K)製作 TEOLED，以利元件操作所產生之焦耳熱能經由基
板得到散逸。但因 AlN 基板的表面非常粗糙，如圖 4.5.1(a)。所以需先對基板
 36
無法再繼續注入。由圖 4.5.3 可發現當注入元件的電流密度越高時，發光亮度
會跟著變高，但是相對的，發光區域之溫度也會大幅升高，如果無法有效散
熱，將使元件燒毀。而 AlN 基板因為散熱的效果非常好，所以元件於較高的
電流密度下操作，可得到更高的發光亮度，而不至於使元件燒毀。圖 4.5.4 之
發光效率-電流密度圖中，使用玻璃基板之 TEOLED 元件有最高的發光效
率，但是隨著電流密度的提高，發光效率下降的幅度也很大。是因為電流密
度提高使元件內部溫度升高，造成元件損壞加速，所以發光效率才會迅速下
降。相對地，使用 AlN 基板之元件，由圖 4.5.4 可發現由於 AlN 基板具有良
好的散熱特性，元件之發光效率在高電流密度下，衰減幅度會比使用玻璃基
板之元件更平緩，能有效減少元件衰退之速度。由圖 4.5.2 之電流密度-電壓
曲線圖，AlN 基板上是否沉積 SiO2，並不會影響電流注入至元件，但由圖
4.5.3 與圖 4.5.4 可知，將 SiO2沉積於 AlN 基板上，可使元件特性得到顯著的
提升。這是因為以 PECVD 沉積之 SiO2 薄膜具有階梯覆蓋性，能使拋光後之
AlN 基板表面變得更平滑，避免在蒸鍍 TEOLED 陽極時，因表面的不平整，
導致陽極產生尖端放電之效應，因而得到較好的元件特性。最後綜合圖 4.5.2-
4.5.4，SiO2 不但能幫助修飾 AlN 基板的表面，對於散熱的特性，亦無明顯之
影響。而元件在最佳結構為 AlN substrate/SiO2 500nm/ Al 60nm/ Au 2nm/ m-

1. Introduction 
Conventional OLED pixels are bottom emission struc-
ture where TFT and data lines would block the passage of 
light. To improve the aperture ratio and keep the bot-
tom-layer TFT from affecting the passage of light, the use 
of top emission organic light emitting diodes (TEOLED) is 
an inevitable trend [1]. The fabrication of TEOLED re-
quires a high-reflectivity anode that must possess high 
work function to increase hole injection into the organic 
layer. Many scholars have attempted to use metallic mate-
rial with high work function as anode, while Lee et al. se-
lected Ni [2]. This paper employs double metal layers Al 
/AuGeNi as anode to achieve high reflection and high hole 
injection efficiency, and uses the multi-layer structure of 
LiF/Al/Ag [3,4] as cathode. The thicknesses of organic 
layers are also varied to obtain optimum luminescence 
characteristics. We predict that Al/AuGeNi will perform 
longer lifetime than Al/Au [5]. 
 
2. Experimental 
In this study, the OLED device is fabricated on a glass 
substrate. The glass substrate was placed in the metal 
evaporation chamber, and Al and AuGeNi (alloy ratio of 
84:14:2) were deposited under 3×10-6 torr to create an an-
ode at first. Next, the substrate was moved to the organic 
evaporation chamber for the deposition of organic thin 
films under 2×10-6 torr. The hole injection layer (HIL) was 
4,4',4''-tris(N-3-methphenyl-N-phenyl-amino)-triphenyl-
amine (m-MTDATA), the hole transport layer (HTL) was 
N,N’-diphenyl-N,N’-bis(1-naphthyl)-(1,1’-biph-enyl)-4,4’- 
diamine (NPB), and the emitting and electron transport 
layer (ETL) was tris(8-hydroxyquinoline) aluminum (Alq3). 
The device was then moved back to the metal evaporation 
chamber for the deposition of metal cathode LiF/Al/Ag. 
SpectraScan PR650 and Keithley 2400 were employed to 
measure the luminance and current-voltage characteristics.

This study used Al/AuGeNi as anode. Figure 1 shows 
the current density vs. voltage of different anode structures. 
When Al was collocated with ultra-thin high work function 
alloy (AuGeNi) in the double metal anode, the turn-on volt-

spectively luminance vs. current density and luminance 
yield vs. current density of different anodes. It is found that 
devices with thermally evaporated Al/AuGeNi anode had

pared to Au-Ge (5.1 - 5.0 eV) and Au-Sn (5.1 - 4.4 eV). As 
shown in Fig. 2 and Fig. 3, double metal layer structure of 
Al/AuGeNi is an excellent choice for OLED anode.

1. Introduction 
Organic light emitting diode (OLED) has attracted con-
siderable interest of researchers since it was successfully 
developed in 1987 [1]. Doping is one of the common tech-
niques for producing tricolor light. But the transfer of en-
ergy from host to guest in the doping system is insufficient, 
and high doping concentration would lead to quenching 
effect. Thus it has been difficult to fabricate ideal OLEDs 
that offer both full saturation (pure color) and high lumi-
nance efficiency. The experiments in this study employ 
ultra-thin separately doped OLED structure to achieve low 
turn-on voltage, high luminance efficiency and high elec-
troluminescence [2]. In the simulation part of this study, the 
photoluminescence (PL) spectrum intensity of each emit-
ting layer (Alq3 and DCJT) and full width at half maximum 
(FWHM) of its Gaussian distribution are changed to obtain 
the electroluminescence (EL) spectrum of ultra-thin sepa-
rately doped device, which is then compared to the experi-
mental data. 
 
2. Experiment 
This study employed ultra-thin separately doped struc-
ture as published previously [2]. The device structure is 
ITO(230nm)/NPB(40nm)/DCJT-dopedAlq3(5nm)/Alq3 
(5nm)/DCJT-dopedAlq3(5nm)/Alq3(45nm)/LiF(0.5nm)/Al 
(60nm). The emitting layer Alq3 was doped with DCJT by 
means of ultra-thin double-layer separate doping to produce 
red light. The energy band diagram of the OLED is shown 
in Fig. 1. In the experiment, three concentrations of DCJT 
dopant, 1, 2.7, and 3.5 % wt, were used. After the organic 
layers were deposited, the substrate was moved to metal 
evaporation chamber to deposit electron injection layer LiF 
and cathode Al. After the device was fabricated, Keithley 
2400 and SpectraScan PR650 were used to measure the EL 
spectra and light-current-voltage (L-I-V) characteristics. 
The Emissive Thin Film Optics Simulator (ETFOS) de-
veloped by Zurich University of Applied Sciences - Center 
for Computational Physics and IBM was used for simula-
tion. To run the simulation: 1. set the film thickness and 
optical parameters (e.g. n, k values) for each layer; 2. de-
fine the emitting layer, dipole location (D.L.) of emission 
area (in Table 1, D.L.=1 means the emission area was at the 
NPB-DCJT doped Alq3 interface; D.L.=0 means the emis-
sion area was at the Alq3-LiF interface) and PL spectra of 
emitting materials Alq3 and DCJT; 3. adjust the FWHM of 
PL Gaussian distribution (by the percentage of FWHM to 
the full width of Alq3 spectrum) and intensity values of PL 
spectrum of emitting materials (Alq3 and DCJT). Table 1 
shows the simulation parameters of each emitting layer. 
Finally the simulated EL spectra of devices were obtained 
and compared with the measurements obtained in the ex-
periment.

 Black film for improving the contrast ratio of organic light emitting diodes

1. Introduction 
Ambient light is incident upon a panel and reflects to 
the visual sensory organs of a user in bottom emission or-
ganic light-emitting diodes (OLED). Such design also re-
flects a scattered in addition to enhancing brightness [1], 
and thus relatively lowering the contrast ratio of a display. 
Therefore, it is necessary to take the improvement and en-
hancement of the contrast ratio into consideration to 
achieve a vivid lifelike effect.  
A common method for effectively improving the con-
trast ratio is to attach a polarizer onto an external layer of a 
display [2]. Another common method for improving the 
contrast ratio is to add a black film into the structure of the 
OLED.  
  One of adding black film methods is adopting a trans-
parent cathode on the traditional OLED to add a light ab-
sorbing layer at the rear of the cathode [3]. The other 
method adds a black film structure in front of the cathode to 
form a composite cathode [4-6]. 
It is noteworthy that when the black film is inserted 
between the cathode and the organic layer, not only the 
scattered light entering into the OLEDs is eliminated, but 
the brightness of the light reflected from the cathode is also 
lowered by this black-film. 
 
2. Experiment 
In this experiment, sheet resistors 5Ω/□ ITO glass are 
rinsed by ultrasonic cleaner in acetone, methanol, and DI 
water for ten minutes, and then cleaned by O2 Plasma for 
90 seconds. NPB and Alq3 are deposited sequentially onto 
the surface of the ITO by thermal resistance heating in or-
ganic chamber at pressure of 5x10-6. The evaporation rate 
of organic matters is controlled within 0.1~0.3 Å/sec. Then, 
the sample is sent to a metal deposition chamber for depos-
iting. Table 1 shows the parameters adopted by the OLED 
with and without black film structures, Al/CuPC.  
The OLED properties were tested under the atmos-
phere. This experiment used a xenon lamp as the back-
ground light during the measurements, and the brightness 
of the background light source was controlled at 33.5 
cd/m2.  
 
3. Results and Discussion 
Fig. 1 shows the graph of current density versus volt-

the Al/CuPC film of 4/80 nm thickness (device B). It is

similar to the devices of other thicknesses, and the maxi-

1. I
    Phosphorescent dye gains energy from the radiative 
recombination of both singlet and triplet excitons [1] to 
improve the internal quantum efficiency of fluorescent 
OLED from typically 25% at maximum to nearly 100% [2]. 
ntroduction 
   The topic of enhancing the luminance efficiency of 
phosphorescent OLED has attracted the interest of many 
researchers. The host materials that have been under study 
include Tris(8-hydroxy-quinolinato)aluminum (Alq3) [3,6], 
3-phenyl-4-(1'-naphthyl)-5-phenyl-1,2,4-triazole (TAZ) [4], 
2,9-Dimethyl-4,7-diphenyl-1,10-phenanhroline (BCP) [4], 
4,4',4''-Tris(carbazol-9-yl)triphenylamine (TCTA) [5] and 
4,4'-Bis(carbazol-9-yl)biphenyl (CBP) [4-6]; for hole and 
exciton blocking layer, BCP [6], TPBi [7], BAlq [8] and 
BPhen [9] have been employed in the attempt to enhance 
the luminance efficiency of device. 
    Electron injection materials include alkali metal fluo-
ride, e.g. LiF, NaF, and CsF. It is known from the literature 
reports that when CsF or LiF is used as electron injection 
layer, the CsF can react with Al and release the metal more 
easily than LiF [10]. 
    This study employs LiF or CsF as electron injection 
layer and compare their effects on electron injection and 
luminance efficiency, and furthermore, uses BCP or TPBi 
as hole and exciton blocking layer to compare their effects 
on the luminance characteristics of flexible phosphorescent 
OLED.  
 
2. Experiment 
The ITO substrate employed in the study was 80Ω/□ 
PET substrate. Before the deposition of organic layer, the 
patterned ITO substrate was placed in O2 plasma for sur-
face cleaning. The substrate was placed in the organic 
evaporation chamber for the deposition of organic thin 
films under 2×10-6 torr, NPB was deposited as hole trans-
port layer (HTL), CBP was deposited as host, Ir(ppy)3 was 
deposited at the rate of 0.1 nm/sec as guest, 
2,9-Dimethyl-4,7-diphenyl-1,10-phenanhroline (BCP) or 
2,2',2''-(1,3,5-Benzinetriyl)-tris(1-phenyl-1-H-benzimidazol
e) (TPBi) was deposited as holes and excitons blocking 
layer, and Alq3 was deposited as electron transport layer 
(ETL). After the deposition of organic thin films, the sub-
strate was moved to the metal evaporation chamber for the 
deposition of LiF or CsF as electron injection layer (EIL) 
under 4×10-6 torr and finally the deposition of Al cathode. 
SpectraScan PR650 and Keithley 2400 were employed to  
 
measure the luminance and current-voltage characteristics. 
 
3. Results and Discussion 
The study first examined CBP:Ir(ppy)3 of varying 
thickness in devices with the structure and energy band as 
shown in Fig. 1(a). The emitting layer thickness was varied 
to augment the electron-hole recombination region and 
enhance luminance and yield. It is found in Fig. 2 and Fig. 
3 that when the thickness of emitting layer increased from 
10 to 40 nm, luminance was raised from 4400 to 10220 
cd/m2 at 10V, and the yield also improved from 13 to 30.4 
cd/A at 6V as shown in Fig.3.
