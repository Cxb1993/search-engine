IAbstract
Metal injection molding (MIM) process is superior in making parts with
complicated shapes compared to the press-and-sinter process. When sinter-hardening
steels, which can attain high hardness and high strength without quenching treatment
after sintering, are used along with the MIM process, the process and products will
become even more competitive. The objective of this study was thus to find suitable
processing parameters and alloy compositions to improve the mechanical properties of
the current MIM products.
To optimize the alloying, the effects of adding Ni, Cr and Mo on the mechanical
properties were investigated. Different sintering temperatures and different tempering
conditions were also studied. The effect of cryogenic treatment was employed to further
improve the mechanical properties by transferring the retaining austenite to martensite.
The results show that parts after sintering can attain 52HRC hardness, 1900MPa
tensile strength, and 3% elongation. After cryogenic treatment using liquid nitrogen, the
hardness and tensile strength were further increased to 55HRC and 2000MPa, but the
elongation was decreased to about 1%. After tempering, the elongation was increased to
5% and the standard deviations of mechanical properties were also improved. The
optimum tempering conditions were 180℃ or 200℃, for 2 hours.
The results also show that the optiunm carbon content between 0.5 and 0.6wt%. The
tensile strength thus attained was about 2000MPa. When the carbon content was
excessive, and the cooling rate was slow, such as at a rate of 3℃/min, carbides would
precipitate at the grain boundaries and brittle-type of fractures were observed. Thus, the
mechanical properties were poor. But when a higher cooling rate of 25℃/min was used,
carbide precipitation did not occur unless the carbon content was greater than 0.8wt%.
These results show that using the composition of Fe-8Ni-0.8Cr-0.8Mo-0.5C, good
mechanical properties of 2000 MPa, 55HRc and 5 % elongation, can be attained using a
cooling rate of 25℃/min and tempering at 180℃ for 2 Hrs. No need of quenching is
required.
Keywords: Metal injection molding, sinter-hardening steel, cryogenic treatment, carbide,
powder metallurgy.

1第一章 文獻回顧
粉末射出成形(Powder Injection Molding，簡稱為 PIM)，為一種結合了傳統粉
末冶金與塑膠射出成形之技術，此製程主要是將金屬粉末與高分子黏結劑混合，
使其在加熱後具有類似塑膠材料的流動性，並可藉由一般射出成形機製造出具複
雜形狀的零件，射出成形後的生胚經過脫脂過程以除去高分子黏結劑後再經燒結
即可得到高密度且具有優異機械與物理性質的燒結零件[1-3]。另外此種成型方式可
藉由一套模具大量的生產出形狀複雜及尺寸精密的產品，減少了產品的二次加工
所需的費用。目前已廣泛應用於汽車零件、機械結構零件、生醫材料、鐘錶及電
磁材料等領域[4,5]，如圖 1- 1 所示。大體來說，PIM 具有產品複雜度高、可大量生
產、機械性質佳以及降低生產成本等優點[6,7]。但 PIM 的缺點是廢料比例相當的高，
廢料主要來自於模具的流道及澆道，如果有效的回收再利用廢料亦是一個相當重
要的課題[8,9]。

但 PIM 製程仍有限制，目前實際應用上所面臨的一大挑戰是試片尺寸穩定性
的控制，比起傳統冶金產品約為±0.1％的尺寸公差，目前 PIM 所能達到的最佳尺
寸公差約為±0.3％，且會隨著零件尺寸之變大而不易控制。其主因為 PIM 製程需
經由許多製程步驟，且零件由生胚到最後的產品通常會有 10~20％的線性收縮
[10-12]。
金屬粉末射出成形主要包含了四個步驟：混煉（射料的準備）、射出成形、脫
脂與燒結。
1. 混煉：將金屬粉末與高分子黏結劑結合，使混合物具有類似塑膠的流變行為，
成為具有良好流動性的射料。
2. 成形：將塑化後的射料經由射出機加熱射入模具中成形。
3. 脫脂：將成形後生胚中的黏結劑經由溶劑萃取及熱分解的方式取出。
4. 燒結：將脫脂後的生胚經由高溫燒結成產品。

1.2 鎳的影響
常溫下的鎳為 FCC 結構，對鐵而言，鎳為沃斯田鐵相的穩定化元素，可利用
固溶強化，增加肥粒鐵相的強度與韌性，並且可以降低延性、脆性轉變溫度。但
若含鎳量高時，熱處理完容易有殘留沃斯田鐵產生，因而造成強度下降。圖 1- 4
為 Fe-Ni 的平衡相圖[18]，由此圖可知，鎳的添加也會降低α→γ的相變化溫度，並
擴大α→γ共存區，也因此減緩了當α→γ變態時發生晶粒成長的現象，因而提高燒
結緻密化的現象。而當鎳以元素粉方式添加時，在燒結過程中會先偏析在晶界上，
在發生相變態時，也會抑制晶界的移動。
51.3 鉻的影響
鉻在常溫下為 BCC 結構，而圖 1- 6 為 Fe-Cr 平衡相圖[18],由此圖可知，鉻為肥
粒鐵相穩定化元素，且鉻的硬化能相當好，使得鉻添加後可使 S 曲線大幅向右移，
在慢的冷卻速率下(0.5℃/s)，仍可以得到 50％麻田散鐵組織。而且添加少量的鉻會

另外，鉻的添加可使鐵有固溶強化的效果，鉻可與碳、氮及氧形成碳化鉻、氮
化鉻與氧化鉻等具較高強度、硬度的化合物[23,24]。鉻也用在不銹鋼，因其生成緻
密的氧化鉻，在抗腐蝕方面有幫助。在磨耗方面，也有用濺鍍形成氮化鉻減少其
磨耗率的優點[25]。

1.5 麻田散鐵結構
麻田散鐵為 BCT 結構，由高溫安定之沃斯田鐵以剪變形式，無擴散之變態而
形成[30,31]，此為鐵基合金中兩種主要麻田散鐵形態之形成方法。
低合金鋼中麻田散鐵的基本變態單元的形狀如果是板條狀(Lath)，則稱為板條
狀麻田散鐵(Lath martensite)，如圖 1- 9 及圖 1- 10 所示[32]，其中每一個板條狀麻田
散鐵皆是由於均勻剪力的結果，板條狀田散鐵之生成主要有兩種方式，一個是穿
越母相沃斯田鐵產生不相鄰但平行的板條狀麻田散鐵，然後在最先形成的板條狀
麻田散鐵之間，由成核及生長產生其他平行的板條狀麻田散鐵，另一種方式為連
續的成核及生長相鄰而平行的板條狀麻田散鐵，板條狀麻田散鐵寬度不同之原因
是，較寬的板條狀麻田散鐵形成在沃斯田鐵並未受到變形時的較早期變態過程，
而細的板條狀麻田散鐵形成在變態過程晚期。
另一種形態的麻田散鐵為板狀麻田散鐵(Plate martensite)，如圖 1- 11[32]及圖 1-
12[32]所示，其變態的過程則是形成非平行的板狀單元，板狀麻田散鐵形成於晶粒
邊界及已形成之板狀麻田散鐵之間，且較晚形成之板狀麻田散鐵的長度較小，板
狀麻田散鐵的生長可能為生成新的板狀麻田散鐵或已生成之板狀麻田散鐵繼續生
長，而板條狀麻田散鐵的生長只經由生成新的麻田散鐵。
影響麻田散鐵的形態有很多因素，而主要原因為合金元素及變態的溫度。若在
合金中加入α相安定元素，如：Sn、Cr、W、V 及 Mo，會使相圖中的γ區閉合，
形成γ圈(γ-loop)，則傾向形成板條狀麻田散鐵。而合金中加入沃斯田鐵安定化元
素，例如：C、N、Ni、Pt 及 Mn 等等，會降低麻田散鐵開始變態溫度(Ms)，而有
可能形成板狀麻田散。

1.6 冷卻速率的影響
鋼料的機械性質會因加熱和冷卻條件不同而有所差異，尤其是冷卻條件的影響
最大[33-35]。在 Lindberg[33]等人的研究中，Fe-1.5％Mo-0.4C 在 1120℃燒結，當冷卻
速率為 0.5℃/s 時，金相中包含 2％的肥粒鐵和 98％的變韌鐵。而冷卻速率為 2.5℃/s
時，金相中全為變韌鐵。而根據 Youseffi[36]等人的研究，Fe-1.5％Mo-1.2C 在 1250℃
燒結，冷卻速率由 4℃/min 加快至 40℃/min 時，其拉伸強度由 515 提昇至 608MPa，
硬度由 Hv290 上升至 Hv330。由上可知，微觀組織影響及機械性質受到冷卻速率
的影響很大。而冷卻速率亦會影響晶粒的大小，進而影響機械性質，例如 Jang[37]
等人的研究，冷卻速度較快可得到較多的麻田散鐵組織以及較小的晶粒尺寸，所
以可以得到較好的機械性質。
而同時冷卻速度並不可以過快，例如 Anand[38]等人的研究，經過淬火的試片，
其機械性質並不如一般燒結製程的試片。其原因在於過快的冷卻速率使得碳化物
來不及析出，所以機械性質比一般燒結製程試片來得低。由以上可知，找到最佳
冷卻速率才能給予合金鋼最好的機械性質。

sodium metabisulfite)腐蝕 6 至 8 秒，置入酒精中以超音波震盪乾淨，烘乾後以光學
顯微鏡觀察其金相組織。在光學顯微鏡觀察下，殘留沃斯田鐵呈現白色(white)，麻
田散鐵為草色(straw-colored)，變韌鐵為藍黑色(bluish black)，未固溶的碳化物為黑
色，此腐蝕方法可分辨合金成分中殘留沃斯田鐵、麻田散鐵、碳化物以及變韌鐵
的分佈。

3.4 不同鐵粉的影響
本節利用不同鐵粉做為基礎粉，分別為 OS、OM 及 ISP 鐵粉，以比較不同鐵
粉對 Fe-8Ni-0.8Cr-0.8Mo 之機械性質的影響。
圖 3- 56 為以不同鐵粉為基礎粉時，密度及碳含量的變化，由圖 3- 56 可知，
由於 OM 鐵粉之碳含量較 OS 及 ISP 鐵粉高，且無 SiO2，故燒結後試片的碳含量
較高，約 0.8wt%，OS 及 ISP 分別為約 0.5wt%及 0.6wt%，而密度以 OM 最低。
圖 3- 57 為 OS、OM、ISP 鐵粉為基礎粉時對合金成分硬度的影響，由圖 3- 57
可知，燒結後硬度以 OS 最高、ISP 次之，而 OM 最低，此趨勢與碳含量相同，由
1.8 節的 Ms 點計算公式可知，碳含量會降低 Ms 點，則含碳量高之試片常溫時內部
的麻田散鐵量較低，殘留沃斯田鐵量較多，故 OM 試片的硬度最低。經過深冷處
理後，試片內部的殘留沃斯田鐵皆變態成麻田散鐵，故三者的硬度相當接近，而
回火後三者試片之硬度仍相當接近。
圖 3- 58 為不同鐵粉為基礎粉對拉伸強度及伸長量的影響，由圖 3- 58 可知，
在拉伸強度方面，OM 最低，約 900MPa，而 OS 及 ISP 相近，而伸長率方面，OM
最低，OS 最佳。圖 3- 59、圖 3- 60 及圖 3- 61 分別為 OS、OM 及 ISP 之拉伸破斷
面與金相分析，由圖 3- 59 可知，以 OS 為基礎粉時，拉伸破斷面為酒渦狀組織，
且金相是以麻田散鐵相為主。而當以 OM 為基礎粉，由圖 3- 60 可看出，其拉伸破
斷面為劈裂面，且由於碳含量過高，在金相分析中可看出碳化物因基地過飽合而
析出在晶界上，導致拉伸強度降低至約 900 MPa 左右。當基礎粉為 ISP 時，可由
圖 3- 61 知，破斷面與 OS 相同，為酒渦狀組織，金相亦以麻田散鐵相為主。
由本節可發現，碳含量對此成分之機械性質有相當大的影響，故下節將探討

3.5 不同碳含量的影響
為比較不同碳含量對機械性質的影響，本節在以 OS 為基礎粉之合金粉中，

而不同碳含量對硬度的影響可由圖 3- 63 得知，由圖 3- 63 可知，未添加石墨

接近。
在拉伸強度及伸長量方面可由圖 3- 64 得知，由圖 3- 64 可知，拉伸強度在碳
含量為 0.55wt%之試片最高，約 2100MPa，而伸長量隨著碳含量的上升而下降。
綜合上節與本節，碳含量過高時，試片內部的麻田散鐵量較少，且晶界上容
易析出碳化物，導致強度下降，而碳含量過低時，其機械強度亦不高，故此成分
之試片在燒結後，碳含量應控制在 0.5wt%與 0.6wt%之間，以得最佳之機械性質。

而降低，原因亦同 3.4 節及 3.5 節所解釋。深冷處理後，試片硬度提升並互相接近，
而回火後亦呈現相同結果。
圖 3- 81 降溫速率為 3℃/min 時，碳含量與密度的關係，由圖 3- 81 可知，試

圖 3- 82 為降溫速率為 3℃/min 時，不同碳含量之顯微組織，依序是由低至高，
由圖 3- 82 可知，當降溫速率為 3℃/min 時，當試片碳含量為 0.680wt%以上時，晶
界上可見明顯之碳化物。而與降溫速率為 25℃/min 及 9℃/min 比較，可發現降溫
速率 3℃/min 時，晶界析出碳化物的所需的碳含量較低，僅約 0.68wt%。
綜合此節之結果，若整理晶界上析出碳化物之碳含量與冷卻速率的關係，可
得表 3- 1，由表 3- 1 可知當降溫速率慢時，試片碳含量較低，晶界上即會出現析出
物，如當降溫速率為 3℃/min，試片碳含量為 0.6wt%至 0.7wt%時，晶界上即會析
出碳化物，而降溫速率為淬火時，試片碳含量為 1.0wt%以上時，晶界上仍不會有
碳化物的形成。由此可知，晶界上是否析出碳化物與試片碳含量之過飽合度與冷

物仍來不及析出在晶界上，而當降溫速率慢時，碳含量過飽合時，碳化物始有充
份的時間成核並成長析出在晶界上。

3.8 不同合金元素的影響
為討論不同合金元素對此合金成分的影響，本節在原始合金元素中添加入不
同的合金元素或使用不同合金元素粉，以比較合金元素對此成分之機械性質的影
響。
3.8.1 銅元素及不同不銹鋼粉與鎳粉的影響
根據前人之研究，添加銅粉時，因燒結溫度超過銅的熔點並形成液相，一般
而言，液相形成之後，燒結體會因毛細力的影響而迅速分佈在基地中，有助均質
化，推測會增進材料的機械性質，故本小節之其中一組試片為在合金系統中，加
入0.3wt%Cu。而另一組試片嘗試以17-4PH不銹鋼粉代替316L不銹鋼粉，因17-4PH
不銹鋼粉中含有少量的銅及鈮元素，估計可使材料強度上升，且此組試片亦使用
110Ni 粉替代原本的 123Ni，因 110Ni 粉之粉末粒徑較小，估計可增進材料的均質
化，本小節嘗試將這兩組試片與實驗組(control)比較其機械性質。
圖 3- 83 為不同合金成分對硬度的影響，由圖 3- 83 可知，燒結後硬度三組試
片互相接近，而深冷處理後，換成 17-4PH 不銹鋼粉及 110Ni 粉之試片硬度稍高，
回火後亦有相同的趨勢。
圖 3- 84 為不同合金成分對拉伸強度及伸長率的影響，由圖 3- 84 可知，拉伸
強度以實驗組添加 0.3wt%Cu 之試片最低，而換成 17-4PH 不銹鋼粉及 110Ni 粉之
試片拉伸強度最高，約 2100MPa，伸長率亦最高，約 4%。
圖 3- 85 為不同合金成分對密度及碳含量的影響，由圖 3- 85 可知，合金成分
加入 0.3wt%Cu 之後，密度會稍微下降，推測是因銅粉液相燒結時，潤溼鐵粉之後，
即溶入鐵基地，而原本銅粉的位置易留下空孔，導致密度下降。而碳含量方面，
合金成分加入 0.3wt%Cu 之後，因 Cu 元素對 C 元素有排斥力，故其碳含量為最低
者，進而導致較低的強度。

由於 Co 可以提高 Ms 點，在室溫時，就可得較多量的麻田散鐵，且 Fe-49Co-2V

3.10 CCT 曲線
本節利用熱膨脹分析儀，將燒結好之試片，升溫至 1000℃，持溫十分鐘，控

析是否有相變態產生，以畫出 Fe-8Ni-0.8Cr-0.8Mo-0.5C 之 CCT 曲線，如圖 3- 105
所示。
由 1.8 節的三個公式可算出，此成分之 Ms 點溫度約為 215℃、170℃及 90℃，
而由圖 3- 105 可知，此成分之 Ms 點約為 110℃。
圖 3- 106、圖 3- 107 及圖 3- 108 為降溫速率為每分鐘 1℃、0.75℃及 0.5℃之
顯微組織，由顯微組織之分析可看出，降溫速率為每分鐘 1℃及 0.75℃時，其顯微
組織仍屬麻田散鐵結構，而當降溫速率為每分鐘 0.5℃時，試片內部出現變韌鐵之
結構，可知有碰到變韌鐵之鼻部，圖 3- 105 可知，此成分之 Bs 之鼻部約為 500℃。
綜合以上所述，可知 Fe-8Ni-0.8Cr-0.8Mo-0.5C 成分之 CCT 曲線，應為圖 3- 105
所示，其 Ms 點約為 110℃，變韌鐵變態之鼻部約為 500℃。

INTRODUCTION
The material cost of PM parts in the past few years has been increasing, and there are still no
signs of a decrease in the near future. This has presented a big challenge to the PM industry. To
remain cost-competitive, the development of new alloys with less of the costly elements Ni, Mo,
and Cu has been a focus of interest. Attention has also been focused on replacing Ni, Mo, and
Cu with the less costly Si and Mn, which are also hardenability-enhancing elements [1,2].
However, these elements are difficult to sinter due to their high affinity to oxygen and water
vapor in the sintering atmosphere. Another approach to reducing the cost of the parts is to
develop sinter-hardening alloys which eliminate the heat treatment [3,4]. Yet another approach
is to develop high compressibility powders or new compacting processes so that the mechanical
properties of the PM parts can be improved through reduction of the porosity [5]. Although
these approaches show improvements on the mechanical properties of PM parts, there is still a
lot of room for improvement. One area that has not been seriously explored is that the
homogenization of the alloying elements in sintered compacts is rather poor due to the short
sintering time and the low sintering temperatures used in industrial practices. As the
homogenization is incomplete, the full benefits of the alloying elements have never been
accomplished.
Among Cu, Ni, and Mo, which are the most widely used alloying elements, Ni is the most
difficult one to homogenize since its diffusion rate into iron is the slowest [6]. Cu is not an easy
element to homogenize either, and in fact is only slightly better than Ni [6]. As a result, the areas
at sintered necks and in pore-rich regions, which are stress concentration sites, are enriched with
Ni and Cu. These areas were found to be lean in carbon and have been identified as ferrite and
austenite [7]. These phases are soft and are prone to crack initiation and propagation during
mechanical testing, as was shown by the authors using in-situ tensile tests inside a scanning
electron microscope (SEM) [7]. Other studies on Fe-4Ni-1.5Cu-0.5Mo-0.8C [8] and
Fe-2Ni-0.85Mo-0.6C [9] also suggest that the Ni-rich areas enhance the propagation of fatigue
cracks. These studies all indicate that Ni-rich areas play an important role in determining the
mechanical properties of Ni-containing PM compacts.
To alleviate this problem, Wu et al. recently reported that the addition of 316L stainless steel
powder to FD-0405 and using Fe-3Cr-0.5Mo prealloyed powder as the base powder could
reduce the amount of soft Ni-rich areas [10,11]. The addition of Mo was also found to be
effective, but to a lesser extent [7]. These improvements in homogenizing the microstructure

Effects of Alloying with Chromium
To improve the distribution of carbon and nickel in Ni-containing sintered compacts, the effects
of Cr and Mo additions were examined. Figure 6 shows that the compact of FD-0405+0.5Cr
sintered at 1250℃,
which was prepared by adding 3wt% 316L stainless steel powders to the FD-0405, consisted
mainly of bainite and martensite. Little Ni-rich/C-lean ferrite and pearlite was present. The line
scans of the Ni and C, as shown in Figure 7, further confirmed the effect of Cr on the
distribution of Ni and C. When Cr was absent, the powder core contained low Ni and high C,
while the surface regions contained high Ni and low C. When 0.5wt% Cr was added, the
differences of C and Ni content between the core and the surface region decreased. As a result,
the fracture path, as shown in Figure 8, was no longer along the Ni-rich ferrite, nor through the
pearlite in powder centers, which are typical in the sintered FD-0405 compacts shown in Figure
2 and Figure 5. The major areas that the crack passed through were identified as either bainite or
martensite using metallography examinations and microhardness measurements. It was also
noticed that a major portion of the crack path was taking the shortest distance between pores,
despite the fact that the hard and strong bainite and martensite were present. This suggested that
the fracture behavior of these materials is determined by pore shape, pore size, and pore size
distributions.

DISCUSSION
Effect of Alloying with Chromium
The above results show that the addition of Ni and Cr together could have a synergistic effect on
the mechanical properties. As shown in Table 2, the tensile strengths of the P/S
Fe-3Cr-0.5Mo-0.5C and Fe-4Ni-0.5Mo-0.5C were both lower than 700MPa, and the hardnesses
were lower than 90HRB, even when the sintering temperature was as high as 1250℃. However,
when the compact contained both 4wt%Ni and 3wt% Cr, the tensile strength increased to
1323MPa, the hardness increased to 39.2HRC, and the elongation was close to 2%. The main
reason is that the distribution of Ni and C was significantly improved due to the presence of Cr,
157
Since much better homogenized microstructure and alloying can be obtained using fine carbonyl
iron powders, the mechanical properties were much improved. The tensile strength and hardness
of the S/P Fe-8Ni-0.8Mo-0.8Cr-0.5C specimen reached 1.98GPa and 45HRC, respectively.
Similar properties were also attained using the PIM process. These properties were attained
without quenching. The cooling rate in the furnace, measured at 550℃, was only 0.1℃/s. It is
believed that with the use of finer Ni powders and a faster cooling rate, such as that in the
sinter-hardening furnace used in the PM industry, including continuous furnaces and vacuum
furnaces equipped with gas quenching capabilities, the tensile strength and hardness can be
further enhanced. Related tests are now in progress.
CONCLUSIONS
The fracture path of the FD-0405 compact was analyzed using the in-situ tensile test method.
When the compact was sintered at 1120℃ for 30 minutes, the Ni-rich ferrite and pearlite were
the main phases responsible for the failures. As the sintering temperature increased to 1250℃
and sintering time increased to 1 hour, much less Ni-rich ferrite was present, and a major
portion of the fracture passed through pearlite, which was the dominant phase in the matrix.
When as little as 0.5wt% Cr was added, most weak ferrite, austenite, and pearlite disappeared,
and the matrix consisted mainly of bainite and martensite. The main function of the Cr addition
was, in addition to its high multiplying factor for the hardenability, to help homogenize the Ni
and C, as illustrated by the line scans using an EPMA. This phenomenon can also be explained
by thermodynamic analysis using the Thermo-Calc program. With a more uniform distribution
of Ni and C in the matrix, more bainite and martensite were formed. As a result, a tensile
strength of 1323MPa and a hardness of 32.9HRC were attained for press-and-sinter
Fe-4Ni-3Cr-0.5Mo-0.5C compacts sintered at 1250℃ for 1 hour, followed by furnace cooling
and tempering. To further improve the properties, fine carbonyl iron powders and the
spray-dry-press-and-sinter process were employed, and an ultrahigh tensile strength of 1.98GPa
and a hardness of 45HRC were achieved on sinter-hardened Fe-8Ni-0.8Mo-0.8Cr-0.5C
compacts without the need for any quenching treatment. Similar properties were also attained
using the powder injection molding process.

Sinter-Hardened Ultrahigh-Strength PM Steels with Homogeneous Alloying and Microstructures
K. S. Hwang and M. W. Wu
Department of Materials Science and Engineering
National Taiwan University
1, Sec. 4, Roosevelt Road, Taipei, 106, Taiwan, R. O. C.
ABSTRACT
The mechanical properties of Ni-containing PM steels are usually quite unsatisfactory due to the
non-uniform distribution of Ni. The causes of this non-uniform alloying and its effects on mechanical
properties were investigated in this study using mini-tensile specimens that were made of diffusion
alloyed FD-0405 (Fe-4Ni-0.5Mo-1.5Cu-0.5C) powders. The fracture path and the microstructure
observations indicated that the soft Ni-rich/C-lean areas at the periphery of the iron powder and in
pore-rich regions are the main cause of the failures. With the addition of Cr in the form of 316L stainless
steel powder or in the form of prealloyed base powders, the distribution of Ni and C became more uniform,
as measured using an electron probe microanalyzer. As a result, the Ni-rich ferrite and austenite
disappeared and more bainite and martensite were formed. A tensile strength of 1323MPa and a hardness
of HRC39 were attained in the Fe-4Ni-0.5Mo-3Cr-0.5C compact that was sintered at 1250℃ for 1 hour,
furnace cooled, and then tempered. This ultrahigh strength performance was attributed, in addition to the
hardening effect of Cr and Mo, mainly to the more homogeneous alloying of Ni and C, which can be
explained through thermodynamic analysis using the Thermo-Calc Program. When fine carbonyl iron
powders were used as the base powder and the spray-dried process was employed, the mechanical
properties were further improved. A tensile strength of 1.98GPa, a hardness of 45HRC, and an elongation
of 3% were achieved in the Fe-8Ni-0.8Cr-0.8Mo-0.5C compact that was sinter-hardened during furnace
cooling without any subsequent quenching treatment. Similar properties were also attained by using the
PIM process.
INTRODUCTION

decrease in the near future. This has presented a big challenge to the PM industry. To remain
cost-competitive, the development of new alloys with less of the costly elements Ni, Mo, and Cu has been

mechanical properties of the PM parts can be improved through reduction of the porosity [5]. Although

To improve the distribution of the alloying elements, particularly Ni and C, Cr was added in two ways.
The first one was in the form of 316L stainless steel powder (PF-20F, ATMIX), which contained 16.5wt%
Cr and had an average particle size of 13.7 μm. The other method was to use the prealoyed Fe-3Cr-0.5Mo
or Fe-1.5Cr-0.2Mo powder as the base powder, to which the Ni powder (Ni-123, INCO) was added. The
characteristics of the two Cr-containing base powders are listed in Table 1.
The other way to improve the homogenization of alloying elements was to reduce the diffusion distance
by using fine carbonyl iron powders, the characteristics of which are also listed in Table 1. However, the
poor flowability of the carbonyl iron powder did not allow the use of the conventional press-and-sinter
process. Thus, two processes were employed in this study. The first method was to employ spray drying
(S/P) as a pre-treatment. A blend of carbonyl iron powder, nickel powder, 316L stainless steel powder, and
Mo powder weighed to their proportions was mixed with 1.41wt% polyvinyl alcohol, 0.07wt%
polyethylene glycol, and 26.5wt% water. The spray-dried powder obtained had spherical particles with a
size of about 45µm. The powder was then mixed with 0.1wt% ethylene bis-stearamide and pressed into
compacts with a green density of 5.3g/cm3. The debinding and sintering were carried out using the same
procedure as that of the P/S process. The second method of using fine carbonyl iron powders was to
employ the standard PIM process. The feedstock was prepared by kneading mixtures of Fe, Ni, 316L, and
Mo powders and a wax-based binder. The tensile bar specimens were injection molded and solvent
debound using heptane, with subsequent thermal debinding and sintering. For the S/P process, the
specimens were sintered for 2 hours at 1220℃, followed by tempering at 230℃ for 1 hour. For the PIM
process, the specimens were sintered at 1200℃ for 2 hours and then tempered at 180℃ for 2 hours.

DISCUSSION
Effect of Alloying with Chromium
The above results show that the addition of Ni and Cr together could have a synergistic effect on the
mechanical properties. As shown in Table 2, the tensile strengths of the P/S Fe-3Cr-0.5Mo-0.5C and
Fe-4Ni-0.5Mo-0.5C were both lower than 700MPa, and the hardnesses were lower than 90HRB, even
when the sintering temperature was as high as 1250℃. However, when the compact contained both
4wt%Ni and 3wt% Cr, the tensile strength increased to 1323MPa, the hardness increased to 39.2HRC, and
the elongation was close to 2%. The main reason is that the distribution of Ni and C was significantly
improved due to the presence of Cr, as shown by the line scan changes in Figure 7. The change in the
carbon content can also be explained through thermodynamic analysis using the Thermo-Cal program. As
shown in Table 4, the presence of Ni and Cu increases the chemical potential of carbon and thus decreases
the equilibrium carbon content in the Ni/Cu rich areas. In contrast, the presence of Mo and Cr helps
stabilize the carbon in the Ni/Cu rich areas. Thus, with more carbon retained in the Ni/Cu rich areas of
Cr-containing specimens, more bainite and martensite formed, and as a result, the mechanical properties
were significantly improved.
