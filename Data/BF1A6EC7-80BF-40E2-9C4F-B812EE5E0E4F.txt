Null-field integral equations and  
their engineering applications 
 
Abstract 
Following the success of the null-field integral formulation in the past years, the 
null-field integral equations are extended to deal with engineering problems including 
(1) torsion, (2) water wave and (3) elasticity problems in the report. In the 
mathematical model, some mathematical tools, e.g. degenerate kernel (separable 
kernel) and Fourier series, are employed in the null-field integral equations. The 
fundamental solution is extended into separable form based on the concept of 
separating the source point and field point. For the circular boundary, the boundary 
densities are approximated by Fourier series. By using the adaptive observer system 
in conjunction with degenerate kernels, all boundary integrals in the null-field integral 
equations are calculated easily and analytically free of the sense of principal values. 
The null-field integral equation yields the linear algebraic system after matching the 
boundary conditions. For the eccentric case, the vector decomposition technique for 
the potential gradient in the hypersingular formulation is considered carefully for the 
radial and tangential derivatives. First, we focus on the torsion problems with circular 
inclusions. The torsional rigidity is calculated and compared with the results in the 
literature. The stress distribution on the torsion bar is also analyzed. Second, the water 
wave problem is studied. Forces on surfaces of the cylinders are determined 
numerically. Also, the interacting potentials by cylinders are solved. Parameter study 
of wave number of water wave, impinging angle and size of cylinders are also 
discussed. Besides, the nonuniqueness problem exists of the near-trap mode is also 
our main concern. Finally, elasticity problems are considered. Stress concentration 
factor of multiple holes and/or inclusions are our main concern. Several examples are 
used to see the accuracy and efficiency of the present method after comparing with 
other available methods. 
Keywords: torsion, water wave, elasticity, degenerate kernel, Fourier series, null-field 
integral equation

1. Introduction 
For solving engineering problems, many researchers have paid more attention to 
derive the exact or analytical solution. However, the solution can be derived in some 
special cases only. Therefore, the numerical methods are always resorted to deal with 
the problems, e.g. finite difference method (FDM), finite element method (FEM) and 
boundary element method (BEM). Finite difference method and finite element 
method have been employed to solve engineering problems for a long time. For the 
finite difference method, the linear or nonlinear problems could be solved well, but 
the problems with irregular boundaries may be inconvenient in imposing the 
boundary condition. For the finite element method, it is very popular to solve many 
problems and a lot of software packages have been developed nowadays, such as 
NASTRAN, ABAQUS and ANSYS. However, there are still some drawbacks that 
mesh generation for the domain is complicated and time consuming especially for 3D 
and crack problems. Recently, an alternative, the boundary element method (BEM) or 
so-called the boundary integral equation method (BIEM), is more popular. It is 
recognized a numerical method as well as finite difference method and finite element 
method. For the boundary element method, only the boundary is needed to be 
discretized. The effort of mesh generation is reduced when the boundary element 
method is used. From the viewpoint of mesh reduction method, the boundary element 
method can be seen as one kind of generation methods of mesh reduction. However, 
even if it is the mesh-reduction method, the boundary is still needed to discretize. 
Now, the present method can be seen as one kind of meshless methods. Since 
discretization is not required but using collocation approach to determine the 
unknown Fourier coefficients. 
It is well known that BEM is based on the use of fundamental solutions to solve 
partial differential equations. These functions are two-point functions which are 
singular as the source and field points coincide. Most of the efforts have been focused 
on the singular boundary integral equation for problems with ordinary boundaries. In 
some situations, the singular boundary integral equation is not sufficient, e.g. 
degenerate boundary, fictitious frequency and spurious eigenvalue. Therefore, the 
hypersingular equation is required. Then, the calculations of singular integral and 
hypersingular integrals are not avoided in the conventional BEM as well as dual 
BEM. The applications of hypersingular equations have been summarized in the 
review article of Chen and Hong [1]. 
Unlike the conventional BEM and BIEM, Waterman [2] introduced first the 
so-called T-matrix method for electromagnetic scattering problems. Various names, 
null-field approach or extended boundary condition method (EBCM), have been 
coined from researchers of different fields. The null-field approach or T-matrix 
 3
In this report, we develop a systematic approach by using the null-field integral 
formulation in conjunction with degenerate kernel and Fourier series. To fully utilize 
the circular geometry, the fundamental solutions and the boundary densities are 
expanded by using the degenerate kernels and Fourier series, respectively. This 
approach is seen as a semi-analytical method, since the error stems from the number 
of truncation terms of Fourier series in the implementation. However, the analytical 
solution can be obtained by using the present method for the circular and annular 
problems.

of Murakami and Yamakawa may open the issue that Kuo and Conway’s result may 
be questionable. In general, the numerical analysis is developed to predict the data 
before experiment. However, we always find that the results have differences between 
the numerical analysis and experiment data. We may wonder that the numerical result 
may not be correct if the two results deviate. As we know, the mathematical model is 
established under certain assumptions. Therefore, the mathematical model is always 
simpler than the real problem. If the mathematical model has the analytical or exact 
solution, it is the basic solution for comparison with the numerical result. For the real 
problem, many uncertain conditions exist in the experiment. Valid experimental data 
need special care. The inconsistency between the experimental data and numerical 
results stems from many reasons. So, the mathematical model is continuously 
modified by adding specific consideration.

一、參加會議經過 
    第 22 屆國際理論與應用力學大會（ICTAM）於 2008 年 8 月 24-29 日在澳洲阿德萊德市舉行。此系
列會議前身係由 Oseen, Levi-Civita, von Karman 與 Prandtl 教授於 1922 開始舉辦。IUTAM 主辦的國
際理論與應用力學大會每 4 年召開 1 次，與會人士超過 1000 人，由 IUTAM 大會委員會負責組織，迄
今已經在全世界成功舉辦了 22 次，是國際力學界級別最高、規模最盛大的研討會，也是各國都在努力
爭辦的會議。大陸中國力學學會今年特別成立了申辦專家小組，由浙江大學校長楊衛院士在
ICTAM2008 大會上做了申辦 ICTAM2012 大會報告，挾申奧之餘威，2012 年第 23 屆國際理論與應用
力學大會將於北京舉行。參加本次大會中國代表團由李家春、胡海岩、白以龍、崔而傑、楊衛、胡文
瑞與鄭哲敏等院士率領約 120 位學者組成。反觀台灣方面，接受論文 29 篇，參加人員約 20 人，由馬
劍清教授向國科會提報申請九人小組組團參加，僅獲得 60 萬元補助。相較於中國大陸對此會議的看重
與支持，是值得國科會省思注意的。 
    本次會議於澳洲阿德萊德市之國際會議廳(Adelaide Convention Center)舉行。大會投稿論文 1517
篇，接受發表論文 1322 篇，可前來發表者 965 篇，以理論與應用力學為主題。六天會議中分為多個
sessions，並有幾個 mini-symposium，同時在會場內各樓層的會議室同步進行，並有 Open lecture 與 Closed

    首先，感謝國科會預算支助教師出國參加國際研討會，才能有機會參與這次的第 22 屆國際理論與

    反關當今的台灣，大專院校林立，國私立大學有一百六七十所，密度之高，堪稱世界之最。但許

國際語言。因此，對於英語的掌握，我們必須要再更努力的學習。此行對我而言可說受益良多。

三、建議 
 本次研討會給我的感覺是學術交流與國際視野開拓的重要性。國際間針對各種專業領域不乏有多

當局注意。另外，此行的四天參訪行程觀察到澳洲大學生在實作方面能力很強，且與課程緊密結合，
亦是我們可以學習之處。學校與業界互動熱絡與頻繁亦是我們不及之處。研究議題與當前時空接軌很
有特色。另本次會議，有主持人未到場，主辦單位無服務人員，導致耽誤議程。大型會議人手的調度
是相當重要的，值得國內舉辦大型會議作為借鏡。

問題主題，與會人數不到百人。除了大陸、香港與台灣三地的學者外，亦有遠從新加坡、歐洲、俄國
與美國前來參與此次盛會的學者。其中與會人士亦不乏國際知名學者，如：Cicinati 大學劉桂榮教授、

中心主任)、台大數學系王振男主任、成大數學系林景隆教授與中山大學羅春光教授及其三位學生與本

“Water wave problems using null-field integral equations: ill-posedness and treatment ”
