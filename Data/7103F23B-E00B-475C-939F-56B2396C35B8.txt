these industries. Auto-ignition temperature (AIT) is 
an important characteristic for assessing the fire 
and explosive hazard of flammable materials and it is 
the required information to decide the explosion-
proof class of electric equipments handling such 
flammable materials according to the NFPA 70 code. 
However, the AIT information is very scarce for many 
new materials (for example, ionic liquids), and the 
existing AIT data between different literature are 
also very much diverse. Thus, a systematic approach 
to elucidate the auto-ignition mechanism and build up 
the experimental results is of imperative. 
In this study the AIT of organic compounds (including 
alcohols, kentones, ethers and halogen compounds) and 
ionic liquids are measured, and an mechanism was 
proposed to eluicidate the measured AIT and the 
corresponding ignition time delay. Regarding the 
measured AIT, it is found that the measured results 
differentiate from those reported by NFPA 497 in 
Methyl Ethyl Ketone, 2-Heptanone, Isopropyl chloride 
and Dipropylene glycol methyl ether. The AITs of 
other explored compounds could be taken as the same 
as those in NFPA 497 while considering the known 
experimental uncertainties. It is also theoretically 
found that there is a linear relation between the 
inverse of ignition temperature and the logarithm of 
the ignition time delay and experimental results 
satisficatorily fits this relation.

二、前言 
自燃係指可燃物質與空氣接觸，不需要明火的作用就能發生燃燒的現象，而
自燃溫度 (Auto-ignition temperature, AIT) 的定義是指物質在空氣中無任何外在火
源時，可以發生自燃現象的最低環境溫度。根據傳統的氧化速率理論，自燃溫度
是物質發生氧化反應時，釋出熱量的速率與散失到環境的速率達平衡時的溫度
[1]。美國材料試驗協會(American Society for Testing and Materials, ASTM) 於
ASTM E659 液體化學物質自燃點的標準測試方法提到自燃溫度係指化學物質在一
大氣壓下的空氣中，沒有外界火源幫助發生熱焰燃燒，其可燃液體混合氣體因放
熱氧化反應的速率高於熱量散發速率而使溫度升高引起著火的最低溫度[2]。 
美國石油協會(American Petroleum Institute, API) 於 API RP500 Recommended 
Practice for Classification of Locations for Electrical Installations at Petroleum 
Facilities Classified as Class I, Division I and Division 2 定義自燃溫度係指在常壓
下，無需外在熱源因素可自行起火燃燒的溫度[3]。API RP505 Recommended 
Practice for Classification of Locations for Electrical Installations at Petroleum 
Facilities Classified as Class I, Zone 0, and Zone 2定義自燃溫度係指可燃物質在無外
在條件下與空氣混合可自行燃燒的最低熱表面溫度[4]。 
中華民國國家標準(Chinese National Standard, CNS) 3376 定義自燃溫度為大氣
壓力條件下，化學性蒸氣或氣體於空氣中引燃的最低溫度稱之[5]。 
工業製程中，若製程的操作溫度高於可燃性液體的自燃溫度，一旦發生外洩
即可引發火災導致重大財產損失與環境破壞。因此，自燃溫度為評估易燃物質在
火災爆炸危害的重要參考指標，對於危險物的製造、處置、使用、儲存等過程的
安全是不可或缺的重要資訊[6]。例如，在美國石油協會(American Petroleum Institute, 
API) API 581 的量化風險評估中利用自燃溫度作為物質發生洩漏時形成火災或爆
炸的判定基準，製程操作溫度高於外洩物質的自燃溫度則其洩漏後的危害後果以
火災燃燒為主，反之若製程操作溫度低於外洩物質的自燃溫度，若現場有其餘火
源存在，需謹慎考慮洩漏後可能發生爆炸危害的後果[7]。 
美國消防協會(National Fire Protection Association, NFPA)在制定防爆電氣設備
等級，其爆炸等級分類是以可燃性蒸氣的自燃溫度劃分區域的防爆等級，在 NFPA 
70 中規定「Class 1 設備在操作時表面溫度不得超過特殊氣體或蒸氣的自燃溫度」。
在NFPA 70指定採用ASTM E659試驗方法量測可燃性蒸氣的自燃溫度以評估防爆
電氣設備的所需等級[7]。

第二章 文獻探討 
雖然自燃溫度是處理易燃物質中不可或缺的重要資訊，但不同試驗環境條件
或不同試驗方法對 AIT 試驗結果皆可能造成試驗結果的差異[9]，例如：當時測試
環境溫度、濕度、壓力、測試方法等，且許多文獻上數據可能是來自不同的試驗
方法或不同的預測模式。 
陳與謝等人(2010)利用暴露艙試驗室控制環境溫濕度配合正交試驗設計[17]，
探討乙醇自燃溫度試驗的過程中的試驗條件所造成的影響。試驗結果顯示，以相
同材質的燒瓶量測化學品自燃溫度，自燃溫度會隨著環境溫度增加亦有升高的趨
勢；在相同環境溫度下，化學物質自燃溫度的量測結果顯示石英燒瓶所測得的自
燃溫度高於硼矽玻璃所測得的結果高約 20℃。 
暴露艙試驗結果可經由正交試驗設計的變異數分析(Analysis of variance, 
ANOVA)建立分析表，經分析結果發現自燃溫度試驗過程中，環境溫度及燒瓶材質
對自燃溫度具有統計上的影響，而環境溼度對自燃溫度的影響不具有統計上的顯
著性，經由統計分析結果顯示環境溫度確實對自燃溫度試驗具有影響。 
從上述結果發現，環境溫度及燒瓶材質對自燃溫度具有統計上的影響，且石
英燒瓶所測得的自燃溫度高於硼矽玻璃所測得的結果高約 20℃，以風險評估的策
略來看，在評估易燃性液體危害的安全對策上應該以較低的自燃溫度做為參考，
因此本研究試驗環境將控制在一大氣壓、25℃下，選用硼矽玻璃作為試驗的燒瓶。 
從過去文獻指出，自燃溫度之探討大都以預測模式來建立純物質的自燃溫
度，Suzuki 等人 1994[16]用化學物質的分子結構與物理性質發展模式來評估自燃
溫度，Mitchel and Jurs 1997[9]將 327 個非均勻向有機化合物用定量構效關係 
(Quantitative Structure–Property Relationship, QSPR) 發展模式評估自燃溫度，然而
試驗所測得的自燃溫度數據來源較少[18–21]。

Abstract—Auto-ignition temperature (AIT) is usually defined 
as the lowest temperature at which a substance will produce hot-
flame ignition in air at atmospheric pressure without the aid of 
an external energy source such as spark or flame. Its principal 
applications include: defining the maximum acceptable surface 
temperature in a particular area, usually for electrical 
classification purpose, to prevent fire and explosion hazards; 
determining the possible hazardous consequence associated with 
leakage of flammable chemicals in risk assessment methods. 
Although AIT is indispensable for safely handling and operating 
flammable substance, the AITs data  are, however, very much 
diverse in different data compilations. In present work the AITs 
of three chlorinated compounds are measured in compliance with 
the ASTM E659 test method. The measured AITs are (463.8 ± 
9.3) ℃, (244 ± 4.9) ℃ and (530.9 ± 10.6) ℃ for Isopropyl 
chloride, Butyl chloride and Benzoyl chloride, respectively. It is 
found that the AIT compiled in DIPPR 2009 is beyond the 
experimental reproducibility in all investigated compounds. The 
difference is found to be of 129 ℃ , 215 ℃  and 445 ℃  for 
Isopropyl chloride, Butyl chloride and Benzoyl chloride, 
respectively. The AIT reported in The Chemical Database also 
deviates from that obtained in present work with certain degree, 
and the difference is found to be of 126 ℃, 36 ℃ and 70 ℃ for 
Isopropyl chloride, Butyl chloride and Benzoyl chloride, 
respectively.

I.  INTRODUCTION  
Auto-ignition temperature (AIT) was regarded as the 
temperature to which a combustible mixture must be raised so 
that the rate of heat evolved by the exothermic oxidation 
reactions of the system will just overcome the rate at which 
heat is lost to the surroundings [1]. Thus, AIT is usually 
defined as the lowest temperature at which a substance will 
produce hot-flame ignition in air at atmospheric pressure 
without the aid of an external energy source such as spark or 
flame. Obviously, the ability of a flammable material to 
spontaneously ignite is an important index for assessing its fire 
hazards for people who handle, transport, and store such 
flammable materials. 
The principal application of AIT in industries includes: 
defining the maximum acceptable surface temperature in a 
particular area, usually for electrical classification purpose to 
prevent fire and explosion hazards. For example, article 500.8 
of NFPA 70 (also known as the National Electric Code) 
provides that “Class I equipment shall not have any exposed 
surface that operates at a temperature in excess of the ignition 
temperature of the specific gas or vapors” [2]; determining the 
possible consequence associated with leakage of flammable 
chemicals in hazard risk assessment methods. For example, 
API Publication 581 (also known as Risk-based inspection 
base resource document) requires the AIT of a flammable 
chemical to determine the consequence of a leakage is an 
explosion or a fire while a leakage of this flammable chemical 
occurring [3]. 
Although AIT of flammable materials is indispensable 
information to safely handle and operate flammable material, 
the AITs of a compound reported in different data 
compilations are very much diverse. The difference between 
data compilations is found to be up to more than 300℃ in 
many flammable liquids. Such diversity is attributed to many 
experimental factors and has been reported in the literature 
[4,5]. One of the crucial factors responsible to this diversity is 
the method for measuring the AIT of liquid chemicals is not 
unified yet. Most methods for measuring the AIT of liquid 
chemicals introduce the sample into an apparatus container 
which is preheated to a specific temperature, but both the 
container shape and container size may be different in 
different test methods [6,7]. Although ASTM E659 method 
was now widely accepted to measure AIT of liquid 
compounds, the existing method to measure AIT of liquid 
chemicals in Europe is the DIN 51794 method, Determining 
the ignition temperature of petroleum products, which adopts a 
narrow-neck conical flask having a capacity of 200 mL to be 
the ignition container [7], but the ASTM E659 used a 
spherical 500 mL flask. Results of these two existing methods 
are reported to be not comparable and it is deemed that ASTM 
E659 method will give a lower AIT because of the larger 
volume of ignition container employed in the ASTM E659 
method. However, most data compilations reports the 
experimental AIT of a chemical without offering the test 
method employed, so users are generally hard to determine 
which one is more feasible for their problems at hand while 
the reported values are inconsistent. 
Another factor possibly responsible to this diversity is that 
it was believed that most AITs of liquid chemicals reported in 
the literature were measured by the now-discontinued 
procedure of ASTM D2155 method [8]. This method adopted 
a 200 mL spherical flask as the ignition container, but the 
now-existing ASTM method of ASTM E659 used a spherical

III. RESULTS AND DISCUSSIONS 
Figs. 2 to 4 show the combustion plots of experiments for 
Isopropyl chloride, Butyl chloride and Benzoyl chloride, 
respectively. In these figures, the x-axis is the amount of 
sample added into the combustion container, and the y-axis is 
the preheated temperature of the combustion container. If the 
sample introduced generates a hot flame in ten minutes, this 
case is taken as a flammable one and denoted as a circle. If the 
sample introduced does not be ignited or it generates a cold 
flame, this case is taken as a nonflammable one and denoted 
as a cross. While varying the sample quantity, the case of the 
lowest preheated temperature of the ignition container to 
ignite the sample with a hot flame is denoted by a triangle. 
This lowest temperature is, by definition, the AIT of the 
explored compound.
