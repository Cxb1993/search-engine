一、 中文摘要 
發光二極體的亮度目前還無法大量應用於
一般照明燈的規格，除了材料特性以及封裝技術
的限制外，最主要的就是熱的問題。目前高功率
發光二極體的輸入之功率只有 15~20%會轉換成
光，剩下的 80~85%會轉換成熱，這些熱如果無
法適時排出至環境，將會影響發光二極體的發光
效率及使用壽命。我們此計畫目標在將奈米碳管
均勻混入在高功率發光二極體封裝中的膠體
中，取代原本熱傳導係數不佳的環氧樹脂。雖然
在環氧樹脂固晶膠中加入銀粉，可以有效提升熱
傳導係數，但整體的熱傳導係數還是介於 5-10 
W/mK。我們此計畫透過在固晶膠體中加入奈米
碳管，將有機會可以再次提升固晶膠體的熱傳導
係數，我們欲達到之規格為熱傳導係數可以達到
10-20 W/mK。

I.  INTRODUCTION
In the past few years, the novel frequency divider circuits based on the resonant tunneling diode (RTD) 
have been developed [1]-[4]. The frequency divider circuit is used as a basic block in communication system. 
The RTD has special current-voltage (I-V) curve with negative differential resistance (NDR) characteristic. A 
circuit consisting of such nonlinear I-V characteristic often shows the chaos phenomenon [5]. When an 
external  periodic s ignal  i s  used,  such circuit  shows an increased variety of bifurcation 
sequences. Then we can observe the long-period behavior which demonstrates the frequency divider 
characteristic. 
For the previous frequency divider design, the RTD is used as a core device. The RTD is fabricated based 
on the III-V compound technique such as the molecular-beam-epitaxy (MBE) system. Compared to silicon-
based devices and integrated circuits, the cost of MBE is more expensive and the process is not easy to be 
compatible with mainstream ULSI technology including the CMOS or BiCMOS process. In this paper, we 
demonstrate a NDR circuit made of Si-based metal-oxide-semiconductor field-effect-transistor (MOS) and 
SiGe-based heterojunction bipolar transistor (HBT) devices. A NDR-based frequency divider circuit is 
designed. We show the high-frequency simulation results under several hundred MHz using the standard 
SiGe BiCMOS process provided by the Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company (TSMC) foundry. 
We input a sinusoidal signal to this circuit. The results show that dividing ratio can be selected by the input 
signal frequency. Hence, this NDR-based frequency divider possesses wide application and operation
depending on what kind of process is used.
II.  NDR-Based Frequency Divider Circuit
The MOS-HBT-NDR circuit used in this work is made of three Si-based n-channel MOS and one SiGe-
based HBT devices, as shown in Fig. 1. By suitably designing the MOS width/length (W/L) parameters, we can 
obtain the Λ-type I-V curve with NDR characteristic. Especially, this NDR circuit possesses the ability to 
control the peak currents by the external voltage terminal Vgg.
Figure 2 shows the simulated I-V characteristics with the parameters are designed as WMN1=20μm, 
WMN2=10 μm, and LMN1,2=0.35 μm. The HBT uses the ln02 cell based on the standard 0.35μm SiGe
process provided by the TSMC foundry. When the VS is biased at 1.6 V, the peak voltage is 0.45 V, valley 
voltage is 1.1 V, peak current is 1.36 mA, and valley current is approximated to zero.
IV.  CONCLUSIONS
We have demonstrated a frequency divider using an MOS-HBT-NDR-based chaos circuit. This 
MOS-HBT-NDR circuit is made of two MOS and one HBT devices. The fabrication process of this 
MOS-HBT-NDR circuit is easier than that of the RTD device. This circuit isdesigned according to the strong 
nonlinearity of its NDR characteristic. 
The frequency dividing operation is based on the bifurcation phenomenon which appears in the 
waveforms of subharmonic oscillation. We have studied the operation margin of the circuit corresponding to 
the frequency and distortions of input signal. Clear frequency dividing operations from 1/1 to 1/20 can be 
obtained from the divider circuits,. The simulated results also show that the input signal distortion has a 
negligible influence on the frequency divider state.
The operation frequency is not only limited by the cutoff frequency of MOS-HBT-NDR circuit but also is 
determined by the characteristic frequency fLC. Therefore, a higher operating frequency is expected, if the 
frequency divider is implemented by further advanced BiCMOS integrated circuit process. Particularly, the 
MOS-HBT-NDR circuit is more convenient to integrate with other active MOS, HBT, and BJT devices and 
passive R, L and C devices in comparison with the RTD circuit. These phenomena might provide some useful 
ideas to design the NDR-based applications.
