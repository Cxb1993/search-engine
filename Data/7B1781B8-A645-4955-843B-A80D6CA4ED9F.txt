Abstract 
 
The airborne laser scanning technique (ALS) with 
GPS positioning and INS orientation is useful for quickly 
obtaining the three-dimensional data of points due to its 
high precision and resolution. The data obtained from 
ALS include the three-dimensional position data and laser 
intensity of points. To capture the feature information of 
ground, some appropriate algorithm and procedures are 
needed. In the study, the original three-dimensional point 
clouds will be applied for categorization based on a 
normalized Digital Surface Model (nDSM) and the 
Tensor Voting framework. The intensity of laser data 
used seldom in the laser data analysis will be introduced in 
the study as auxiliary data in order to achieve the more 
correct results of categorization. Finally, an effective 
method of classification for lidar data will be given. 
 
Keywords : airborne laser scanning, Tensor Voting 
algorithm, nDSM, categorization
