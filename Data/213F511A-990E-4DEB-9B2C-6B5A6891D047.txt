計畫名稱：可撓式長波長奈米/微晶矽薄膜太陽能電池之研發 
計畫主持人：莊賦祥

技術/創作名稱 利用超高頻電漿化學氣相沉績系統製作可撓式矽薄膜太陽能電池 
發明人/創作人 莊賦祥,朱維屏,蔡裕勝,謝文烽,郭鎮維 
本計畫以超高頻 (VHF)電漿增強化學沉積技術研製奈米及微
晶結構之可撓式長波長矽薄膜太陽能電池，使用 60MHz 2000W 之
電源，並設計開發 VHF-PECVD 反應腔之電極板結構，其目的在
促進 SiH4 分子高速解離以提升矽薄膜結晶性，並增快沉積速度，
以獲得最佳製程參數，以降低製作成本；本實驗主要以超高頻電漿
增強式化學氣相沉積系統(Very High Frequency Plasma Enhanced 
Chemical Vapor Deposition ; VHF-PECVD)來研製最佳化氫化微晶
矽薄膜。最後將沉積的薄膜轉貼於塑膠基板上，完成可撓式薄膜。技術說明 
Recently, due to environmental consciousness and energy crisis, 
there are many investigations of illumination focused on solid light 
with properties of portable, soft, and flexible. This proposal will 
integrate the subjects including long-wavelength nano and micro 
crystal silicon thin film solar cells. hydrogenated microcrystalline 
silicon thin films (HMSTFs) were investigated by mean of the 
very-high-frequency (VHF) (60 MHz) plasma-enhanced chemical 
vapor deposition (PECVD) under well-controlled discharge conditions.

100MHz 條件下氫含量在大約 95%開始微晶成長[10]，由於氫氣稀釋使得沉積速率下降，
滲入薄膜的雜質濃度隨之增加，所得到的奈米結構矽薄膜均具有 N-type 之導電性，這是由
於大量的氧原子雜質摻入所照成具有施子(donor)摻雜之效應所導致[11][12]。N-type 奈米結
構矽薄膜無法作為太陽電池本質(intrinsic)吸收層，為了改善氧原子雜質摻入之影響，相關
的研究指出在薄膜成長時加入百萬分之一(ppm)等級的硼原子摻雜(稱做 microdoping 技
術)，可以有效地將氧原子的摻雜影響抵消，使 n-type 之特性回復至本質，薄膜的暗電導下
降，光暗電導比上升，活化能也增加至二分之一光學能隙之數值，且經過微量參雜處理後
薄膜的光照衰退也獲得改善 [13][14]。1987 年 Curtins 等人於瑞士 Neuchtel 大學引入 60MHz
超高頻化學氣相沈積技術在高氫氣稀釋(SiH4+H2)條件製作 μc-Si:H 薄膜，使得薄膜的品質
與沈積速率的控制獲得改善。VHF-CVD 的優點具有較高的電子及離子密度，但具有較低的
離子能量，代表激發電漿時，離子密度增加可以提昇薄膜的沈積速率，而離子能量的降低
則可減少離子對薄膜轟擊的傷害[15]。1998 年 Lihui Guo 等人研究指出在 PECVD 沈積系統
中加入大量的氫氣稀釋 SiH 4 氣體，當稀釋比例超過 90％時，薄膜的非晶結構漸漸轉化成
微晶結構[16]。Shirai 等人利用氫電漿以 layer-by-layer 的方式對沈積薄膜的表面進行氫電漿
處理，也可將薄膜的非晶結構轉成微晶結構[17]。Martins 等人以 PECVD 系統藉由改變 RF 
power 來觀察薄膜結晶度的變化。以 PH3-SiH4-H2-He 為反應氣體，固定沈積溫度在 200℃
並改變 RF power 在 10～300 W，研究發現薄膜的結晶度隨著 RF power 的增加而變大，是
一個微結晶結構的薄膜[18]。2008 年 A. Chowdhury 等人分別使用 54.24 和 13.56MHz 的
PECVD，並調變總氣體流量 100~300sccm，矽濃度比 1~6.5%，並能長出 6~13nm 的晶粒，
研究指出提昇微晶結晶度，提高本質層的穩定性，有效改善光劣化效應[19]。 
4.實驗方法 
4-1 微晶矽薄膜實驗方法 
將已經烘乾之試片，以高耐熱之真空膠帶固定在石墨電極板上，在放至傳送箱(load lock)
中，做沈積薄膜前的準備。SiH4氣體混合氫氣含量60%。 
VHF-PECVD 系統之操作步驟如下： 
將附有試片之石墨電極板放進傳送箱(load lock)後，關上腔門，使用機械幫浦(rotary 
pump)粗抽真空至約 3×10-2 torr 左右後，先關閉機械幫浦，在開啟主 chamber 和 load lock 之
間的真空閥門，利用機械手臂將石墨電極板傳送到主 chamber。 
基板送至主 chamber 後，開啟加熱器調整至需要的溫度，並繼續抽高真空至 7×10-6 torr。
將製程中各實驗參數(腔體壓力、基板溫度、射頻功率、氣體流量)先設定好，再關閉細抽閥
門，切換到粗抽閥門，開啟需要沈積的氣體流量，並持續抽氣至設定的壓力到達穩定後，
再依實驗所需的沈積時間，開啟電漿開始沈積薄膜。 
薄膜在升溫製程結束後，需在腔體內自然降溫 1 小時以上，在傳送至 load lock 中降至
常溫下在取出試片，此步驟目的是為了不讓薄膜在高溫下瞬間破真空接觸大氣，致使薄膜
瞬間冷卻，使薄膜緻密性變差，避免矽薄膜容易水解或脫落。 
將已沈積完之薄膜，分別量測α-step、Raman、XRD、FTIR、UV/VIS/IR、I-V等分析儀器。

基板大小為39mm*39mm。P型薄膜使用TMB氣體進行摻雜，當薄膜沉積完成後，會送至傳

5.結果與討論 
5-1 調變氫氣稀釋比對微晶矽薄膜結晶度之影響 
本實驗是固定沈積壓力為0.7 Torr、射頻功率為300W及基板溫度為250℃的製程條件
下，改變氫氣稀釋比(SiH4/(SiH4+H2))為1 %～4 %，探討對μc-Si:H 薄膜的材料與電性之影
響。 
分析結晶度方面(XC)，圖5-1氫氣稀釋比(SiH4/(SiH4+H2)) 1~4%之拉曼分析圖顯示，當
SiH4比例越高時(SC=3.5~4%)，薄膜呈現非晶相狀態，而當SiH4比例越低時(SC=1~3%)，薄
膜則呈現微晶相狀態，代表薄膜的結晶度會隨氫氣稀釋的增加而有提高的趨勢，圖5-1以
Origin程式以高斯分佈作圖擬合算出的結晶度比，並於表5-2中顯示，在SC 1%薄膜有最高
結晶度58.3%，並發現之後SC 1.5%的結晶度(52.9%)到SC 3%的結晶度(41.6%)，薄膜隨SiH4
比例增加結晶度有降低的趨勢，由於大量的氫原子在電漿反應過程時，能夠填補薄膜之懸
浮鍵(danging bond)，並減少缺陷密度，在反應中會蝕刻薄膜內部較弱的Si-Si 鍵及Si-H 鍵，
使接下來的反應根種SiH3 能與之鍵結成較強的Si-Si 鍵的結晶結構，提昇薄膜的結晶度，
而到SC 3.5~4%時，薄膜則回非晶矽無結晶度可言。

5-2 調變基板溫度對微晶矽薄膜結晶度之影響 
本實驗目的是以上一小節中調變氫氣稀釋比(SiH4/(SiH4+H2)) 1%～4%取最佳結晶度SC 
1%的參數，表5-3為固定沈積壓力為0.7 Torr、射頻功率為300W的製程條件下，改變基板溫
度為200～400℃，並在每一道製程中間間隔50℃，探討改變基板溫度對μc-Si:H 薄膜的結晶
度之影響。 
分析結晶度方面(XC)，圖5-7稀釋比(SiH4/(SiH4+H2)) 1%改變基板溫度為200～400℃之
拉曼分析圖顯示，微晶矽的主峰值會隨著溫度漸增主峰強度有越明顯趨勢，表5-4結晶度表
及圖5-7 Origin程式以高斯分佈作圖擬合圖所示，薄膜結晶度有最高400 (~70.2%)℃ ，並發現
由溫度最低200 (~48.3%)℃ 到溫度最高400 (~70.2%)℃ ，薄膜的結晶度會隨溫度的增加而有提
高的趨勢，代表溫度的增加有助於薄膜沈積時結晶的成長。 
氫含量(CH)分析方面，圖5-8為氫氣稀釋比(SiH4/(SiH4+H2))1%改變基板溫度為200～
400℃之FTIR圖，由圖5-9中630~640cm-1的搖擺模，可以估算出薄膜中的氫含量，表5-4顯示，
在400℃薄膜有最低氫含量(3.6%)，並依序觀察有最高溫至最低溫度200℃薄膜氫含量
(~5.9%)，發現當基板溫度越高時，薄膜的氫含量反而會有下降趨勢，由此觀察可發現薄膜
的結晶度和氫含量是同步的，發現薄膜結晶度越高氫含量就越低，主要原因為化學退火機
制的反應過程當中所產生的氫原子會透過薄膜較鬆散的非晶區域進行結構的重新排列與鍵
結，促使非晶態轉變為微晶態，使得薄膜轉變成較堅固的結晶結構。

1. Introduction 
In the next generation of photovoltaic cells, the 
developments of epitaxial solar cells have increasingly 
drawn attention [1]. By the use of low cost plastic 
substrates for solar cells production, they have the 
advantages of low cost, lightweight, flexible, and simple 
producing process. For epitaxial solar cells with a large 
area, the roll-to-roll process technology can be combined to 
reduce production costs [2]. In 2005, Yang Yang et al 
treated the absorption layer of a solar cell with a for "slow 
growth" approach for drying treatment which in result 
formed a better organized assembly of molecules and 
thereby enhanced the carrier mobility and lowered the 
resistance of components in series connection. [3] In 2007, 
Sung-Ho Jin et al developed an optimal processing 
condition. By adding glycerol within the PEDOT: PSS 
buffer layer to enhance the current density in the short 
circuit, the efficiency can reach up to 4.64%[4].  
In this study, we mix two organic polymer materials, 
poly (3-hexylthiophene) (P3HT) and [6,6] - phenyl-C61 
butyric acid methyl ester (PCBM), as the component for 
active absorption layer and use spin-coating on PET 
substrate to make flexible solar cells. The surface of a 
substrate is not completely flat, especially for plastic 
substrates which often have sharp tips, PEDOT: PSS is 
used as a buffer layer for components to improve surface 
roughness. Also the addition of glycerol within PEDOT: 
PSS can increase conductivity in which improves the 
power convention efficiency (PCE%) of devices. In this 
study we also discuss the effect on PCE% by using 
different thermal annealing methods for the devices. 
2. Experimental 
The ITO substrate we applied in our research was 
polyethylene terephthalate (PET) substrate a resistance 
value of 35±5 Ω/□. The ITO thickness is 160 nm. Before 
spin coating, a patterned ITO on PET substrate was first 
supersonic cleaned by acetone (10 seconds), methanol (3 
minutes), and deionized water (5 minutes). N2 was further 
used for air drying the ITO surface, and then ovendry for 5 
minutes at temperature 90 °C. The PET substrate was then 
placed within a O2 Plasma cleaner at 30W to clean and the 
ITO surface and increase the work function. We then take 
the sample and spin coat G-PEDOT:PSS (Glycerol doped 
PEDOT:PSS) on the ITO surface at (2500 rpm/30s) to 
easily enable the generated electron holes by excited light 
transfer to ITO. The sample was further dried by air at 90
℃ for one hour, and then placed in a N2 glove box. At this 
time the P3HT:PCBM solution (2 wt% with respect to the 
weight of o-dichlorobenzene (DCB)) has been placed on a 
hotplate at 40˚C for 24 hours. We then coat the dissolved 
DCB active layer, P3HT:PCBM (1:1 ratio), by spin coating 
on the G-PEDOT:PSS layer. Once the coating of the active 
layer is complete, the sample is immediately placed within 
a saturated DCB vapor pressure (including P3HT:PCBM 
the solution) semi-enclosed container. By the self-organize 
effect, a "slow growth" mechanism occurs and the P3HT 
and PCBM molecules will self organize. We remove the 
sample until the active layer is dry (about 40 min) and 
place it on a hotplate for pre-annealing treatment at 90 ~ 
120℃ for 10min. 
We then produce the LiF/Al cathode component by 
metal vapor deposition and then post-annealed. Upon the 
completion of the devices, AM G1.5 100 mW/cm2 was 
used as sunlight simulation. By using KEITHLEY 2400 for 
measuring the current - voltage characteristics, we discuss 
the effect on PCE% by using different thermal annealing 
methods for the devices. 
3. Results and Discussion 
The structure of the devices was PET/ITO/G-PEDOT: 
PSS/P3HT:PCBM/LiF/Al in our study. The dope of 
glycerol with PEDOT: PSS can increase the conductance of 
PEDOT:PSS electrical conductors and lower the resistance 
of components in series connection [4], thus increasing the 
current in short-circuit. Table 1 shows the experimental 
parameters for components A and B. The rotation speed of 
spin coating the active layer is fixed at 600 rpm/30s. We 
compared the PCE% for PEDOT:PSS with 6% glycerol 
(device B), and with no doping of glycerin (device A ). As 
shown in Fig 1, device B with the mixture of 6% glycerol 
has a better PCE = 1.78%. However, the glycerin within 
the PEDOT:PSS can easily cause the generated excitons in 
the active layer to directly cross the PEDOT:PSS and flow 
to the ITO electrode, causing leakage current and resulting 
in a lower fill factor.
