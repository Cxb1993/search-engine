Abstract 
This study aims to develop multi-layer photoelectrode film of dye-sensitized solar cells 
(DSSCs) with high efficiency and double-layer counter electrode film with low cost, the extract 
of natural dye preparing techniques and key materials and components of gel electrolyte. Owing 
to the conventional DSSCs only can prepared on ITO and FTO and the development is restricted. 
Therefore, this study is aimed to develop high efficiency DSSCs. After being assembled, 
electronic transmission characteristics of photoelectrode and counter electrode films of DSSCs 
will be further analyzed, including electrochemical impedance spectroscopy, response time,       
effective diffusion coefficient and diffusion length. Simultaneously, open-loop voltage (Voc), 
short-circuit current (Isc), fill factor, photoelectric conversion efficiency and incident 
photon-to-current conversion efficiency are measured. The DSSCs fabricated by multi-layer 
photoelectrode films with Schottky barrier is developed in this study. Double-layer counter 
electrode films with low cost and the extract of chlorophyll and anthocyanin from natural plants 
are developed in this study. The photoelectric conversion efficiency of DSSCs is 1.44%.
