2一、中文摘要
本計畫研究以濺鍍技術及觸媒輔助成長矽奈米錐陣列，獲得濺鍍製程參數與矽沉
積物之型態的關連性。本研究發現以濺鍍技術成長矽可以區分為三個成長模式：(1)連
續矽薄膜、(2) 無薄膜沉積、(3) 矽奈米錐陣列。以濺鍍技術成長矽奈米結構的反應機
構與化學氣相沉積相似，必須控制環境為觸媒輔助成長，以及抑制連續薄膜成長。本
研究成功的成長矽奈米錐陣列，並已經刊登一篇論文於 SCI國際學術期刊，以及申請
一篇中華民國專利。主持人於本計畫執行期間也參加國際學術研討會，發表一篇海報
論文。
關鍵字：矽、奈米錐、濺鍍、觸媒
二、英文摘要
This project investigated the growth of silicon nanocone arrays by sputtering deposition
with the aid of catalyst particles. It was found that process conditions affect morphology of
silicon deposits. Three types of Si structure were derived: (1) continuous film, (2) no
deposition, and (3) silicon nanocone arrays. The mechanism for growing silicon nanocones
by sputtering technique was similar to that by chemical vapor deposition. Silicon growth
was promoted by catalyst particles but hindered on free surface. This work has been
published in an international SCI journal. A patent application has been filed to the ROC
patent office. This project also funded the investigator to give a poster presentation in an
international conference.
Keywords: Catalyst, nanocones, silicon, sputter deposition

本計畫研究於濺鍍系統中之基材溫度、濺鍍功率與氫氣濃度三項參數對矽奈米錐成長
的影響。於基材溫度方面，參考以觸媒輔助化學氣相沉積法經由 VLS機構成長矽奈米線時，
矽奈米線經觸媒輔助之軸向成長(axial growth)速率將隨溫度提高而增高 [10]；另一方面，
高溫效應同時也會促進徑向成長(radial growth)，造成矽奈米線斜度增大[11]。本計畫將基材
溫度控制於 350~450 °C區間，基材表面覆蓋奈米尺寸之金觸媒，於大濃度範圍之氫氬混合
氣體中濺鍍矽，以進行成長矽奈米錐之研究。於高氫氣含量的氣氛中進行濺鍍，可使自矽
靶材濺射出的粒子狀態由矽原子(Si)轉換成矽氫(例如 SiH、SiH2、SiH3)中間體，以及於反
應氣氛中具有高濃度氫原子(H)，進而改變沉積型態由連續薄膜轉變成矽奈米線或矽奈米
錐。

Bohr-Ran Huanga, Shyankay Joub,*, Yao-Ming Wua, Kuei-Hsien Chenc, Li-Chyong Chend

Abstract
Nanocrystalline diamond (NCD) films were deposited on Si substrates by microwave plasma-enhanced chemical vapor
deposition (MPECVD) using methane/hydrogen/oxygen (30/169/0.2 sccm) as process gases. Subsequently a thin
(0.33m) and a thick (1.01m) NCD films were irradiated with XeF excimer laser (= 351 nm) with 300 and 600 mJ
cm-2 of energy densities in air. The NCD films became rougher after laser irradiations. Fraction of graphitic clusters
decreased but oxygen content increased in the thin NCD film after laser irradiation. Opposite phenomena were observed
for the thick NCD films. Effect of laser irradiation to oxygenation and graphitization of NCD films were correlated with
structural properties of free surface and grain boundaries of the thin and thick NCD films.
Keywords: Nanocrystalline diamond; Excimer laser; Raman spectrum; XPS

Figs. 3(a) and (b) present UV Raman spectra of the as-grown thin and thick NCD films, exhibiting clear diamond
peak at 1332-1334 cm-1 and graphitic clusters (G band) at about 1580 cm-1, together with small bumps representing
diamond nanocrystals at 1160 cm-1 and disordered carbon (D band) at about 1350 cm-1 [20]. The result of the Raman
spectra indicates that the films are composed of nanocrystalline diamond, diamond microcrystals and carbon clusters.
Similar Raman spectra (not shown) were derived from the NCD films after laser irradiations. Area ratios of the
Raman peaks of corresponding nanocrystalline diamond (Ind), diamond microcrystals (Idiam) and disordered carbon (ID)
to the G band (IG) for the NCD films are listed in Table 2. The thick NCD film has higher ratios of Idiam/IG and Ind/IG
than the thin NCD film. It indicates that more diamond crystals but less graphitic carbon clusters are present in the
as-grown thick NCD film than in the thin NCD film. Ratios of Idiam/IG, ID/IG and Ind/IG for the thin NCD film increase
with energy densities of laser, indicating decrease of fraction of graphitic clusters. On the contrary, above peak ratios
for the thick NCD film decrease with the increase of energy densities of laser. Therefore, laser treatments generate more
graphitic clusters in the thick NCD film.
The C1s XPS spectra of the NCD films are shown Fig. 4. These spectra are decomposed into four components
at about (I) 284 eV, (II) 285 eV, (III) 286 and (IV) 289 eV. The peaks (I) and (II) relate to C-C or C-Hx bonds, and
peak (III) and (V) correspond to C-O and COOH, respectively [21-23]. Intensity of the peak (I) of thin NCD film
decreases but intensities of peaks (III) and (IV) increase with energy density of laser, as shown in Figs. 4(a)-(c). On
the contrary, intensity of peak (I) of the thick diamond film increases but intensities of peaks (III) and (IV) decline with
with energy density of laser. Oxygen concentration on surface of NCD film, calculated from C1s and O1s (not shown)
signals, changes from 12.3% to 24.7% for the thin film, and from 18.7% to 6.5% for the thick film after the NCD films
were irradiated with 600 mJ/cm2 of laser. The laser treatment changed oxygen content in the thin and thick NCD films
in different trends. A previous study has indicated that exposure of diamond film in air induced oxygen incorporation
to diamond surface and grain boundaries [19]. Oxygenation of carbon proceeded faster in grain boundaries than on
surface of diamond. Bonded oxygen was more difficult to remove from grain boundary than from surface of diamond.
Here we suggest that laser irradiation simultaneously removed oxygen from NCD surface and assisted oxygenation of
carbon in grain boundaries of NCD in air. The laser energies used in this study were above ablation threshold for carbon
[18]. Therefore, carbon on NCD surface would be evaporated away by laser irradiation. When oxygenated carbon was
removed from NCD surface, a graphite-rich layer would form. On the other hand, oxygen radicals were possibly created
by UV photons and then induced oxygenation of carbon [14]. The thin NCD film had more grain boundaries than the
thick NCD film. Oxygenation of carbon became significant in the thin NCD film during laser irradiation. The thick
NCD film had high surface area as compared with the thin NCD film, thus surface graphitization was prominent after
removal of C-O by laser irradiation.
XeF laser irradiation may modify NCD films in several aspects. The laser energy could anneal NCD to larger
grains and rougher surface, promote oxygenation of grain boundaries of NCD and induce graphitization of NCD
surface.
