II. OVERVIEW OF BRAIN-CONTROLLED REHABILITATION 
SYSTEM (BCRS) 
BCRS is composed of a brain-robot interface and a 
rehabilitation robot. Human can do rehabilitation exercise by 
the rehabilitation robot and control BCRS by EEG signal. 
The first part of BCRS is the rehabilitation robot and it 
consists of a rehabilitation robot (CEBOT) and a real-time 
controller (sbRIO-9642). CEBOT is used to generate three 
exercises and guide human to move. In the three 
rehabilitation exercises, the PD controllers composed of two 
different control modes (joint angle control and output force 
control) are used and realized by the sbRIO-9642. In order to 
observe the amplitude of human muscle contraction and 
detect the abnormal situation, such as the occurrence of 
spasticity, the EMG data from the active surface electrodes 
(Serial #A1447, B&L Engineering, Inc.) placed on the 
biceps and triceps are sampled by a sbRIO-9642 (National 
Instrument Inc.). Second part, the Brain-Robot interface, is 
used to detect human’s intentions and select the movement 
commands in this work. It mainly collects the EEG data, 
filters out the noise, amplifies the raw signals, segments the 
data, detects the P300 potentials, and makes decision. The 
32-channel EEG cap and electrodes (NeuroScan Inc.) are 
used in this study, an analog to digital card (USB-6212, 
National Instruments Inc.) is used to obtain the EEG signals, 
and SVM algorithm is used to detect the P300 potentials. 
The overall system scheme is shown in Fig. 1 and the details 
are described later. For testing the performance of BCRS, 
there are five normal subjects whose ages are from 20 to 29 
in this study. The brain signals are collected from electrode 
cap, the channels (Fz, FCz, Cz, CPz, C3, C4) are chosen for 
detection of P300 potentials, and the reference voltage is the 
average voltage of T5 and T6.

Finally, a scenario is developed to test online performance 
of the P300 BCI and rehabilitation system. The scenario 
includes 12 rehabilitation exercises and each rehabilitation 
exercise is composed of four commands, such as “Isotonic”,

accuracy of k-nearest neighbor algorithm (KNN), SVM, and 
MKL for each subject in the scenario. The results are shown

V. CONCLUSIONS 
This paper discussed how to construct of a rehabilitation 
robot with P300 BCI, to improve the generalization by using 
MKL, and to decrease the training time by using MKL. By 
using a new robot, CEBOT, to guide the human to do exercise 
and a new P300 panel to invoke the occurrence of P300 signal, 
human can control the robot by the brain signal. Compared 
with other human-robot interaction systems, the proposed 
rehabilitation system with P300 BCI has an important property. 
Even the human is incapable of moving, the system still can be 
used for human by EEG signal. In order to decrease the 
training time and increase the ability of generalization, MKL 
is applied and the experimental results show that the proposed 
CEBOT with MKL can increase the inter-subject accuracy and 
decrease the training time by merge the kernel to find the 
optimal hyperplane. In tradition, SVM need to using the cross 
validation to find the parameters of kernel and it needs a lot of 
time for some kernels, such as Gaussian and Exponential 
kernels. In the future, further detailed control issues, optimal 
rehabilitation exercises, decision-making algorithm, and 
methods for artifact signal removing will be addressed and 
evaluated through practical clinical trials.

於本研究之前雖然計畫主持人及其研究團隊已經有對於微流道的相當程度的研究成果，在
渦流產生器方面也有相當多的參考文獻，但在微尺度的流道中置放渦流產生器在這以前並
無較詳盡之研究，故本研究的主要貢獻在於藉微加工技術製作出流道表面粗糙度較小，且
尺寸與誤差也在極為合理的範圍內的微流道並置放渦流產生器於流道中。並且以此流道模
型進行攻角、渦流產生器對數以及流體流動方向等參數化的實驗與數值分析，解釋渦流產
生器對於微尺度流道之熱流行為之影響具有巨觀之優勢，惟必須注意在微尺度下因管道相
對較為細小，故渦流產生器對於壓降之影響也較為顯著。 
    以上結果可提供學術界以及業界在設計裝置渦流產生器之微型通道時參考，而微尺度
之渦流產生器流道若能運用在學術界將能提供部分研究成果以利未來有其他單位要設計
微流道試片時參考，不論在實驗方法以及流道的製作上都具有相當之參考性。業界若擬藉
微型通道以進行微型散熱，且藉渦流產生器將得以在經過設計後能找出比一般直管流道更
佳的散熱效果。
