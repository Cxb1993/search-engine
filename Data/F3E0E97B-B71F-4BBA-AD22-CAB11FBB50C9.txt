中文摘要
本研究主要是利用 Fresnel lens 聚光板在太陽光下進行 photo-Fenton 程序氧化光電業彩
色濾光片廢水中難分解有機污染物，以促進廢水 COD 及 TOC 分解效率，並提升其生物可
分解性(BOD5 /COD)，以利後續生物程序之處理。探討內容除以統計實驗設計-反應曲面法
(RSM)探求反應參數-[H2O2]0/ COD0、[H2O2]0/ [Fe2+]0 及廢水 pH 值等達到廢水生物可分解性
(BOD5 /COD)達易分解程度(0.3 以上)之最適組合，更進一步探討附加 Fresnel lens 在太陽光
photo-Fenton 程序中所產生之效應。實驗結果經反應曲面法最適化分析，顯示彩色濾光片廢
水在 pH 5、1 ~1.35 g [H2O2]0/ COD0、[H2O2]0/ [Fe2+]0 = 15 之加藥條件下，反應 60 min 後，
其 COD 及 TOC 之去除率可分別達 49.6 %及 30.7 %，其生物可分解性可提升至 0.3 以上。
再以此最適加藥條件進行 Fresnel lens 聚光板之效應試驗，結果發現在 Fresnel lens 聚光板的
應用下，COD 分解率則提升至 87.0 %，TOC 礦化率更提升至 69.0 %，此乃因 Fresnel lens
聚光板具有光熱之聚集效應，可有效促使太陽熱能提升廢水溫度 15-25 ℃，及太陽光能更
可提升至無添加聚光板之 2-3 倍，進而提升廢水的處理效果。此結果顯示應用 Fresnel lens
可有效地提升廢水的處理效率，降低後續之處理成本，進而提高太陽光 photo-Fenton 程序
於光電業實廠廢水之應用可行性。
關鍵字: Fresnel lens 聚光板、太陽光 photo-Fenton 程序、彩色濾光片廢水、反應曲面法、生
物可分解性

2. 實驗設備及程序
(1) 太陽光 photo-Fenton 程序實驗
本研究之實驗程序分為三階段，第一階段主要以表 2 所示之三因子三階層中心旋轉試
驗設計來進行實驗，並以 Minitab 統計軟體 R15 (Minitab Co., USA)中之 response surface
methodology (RSM)程式來探求 H2O2/COD、[H2O2]0/[Fe2+]0 及廢水 pH 值達到廢水生物可分
解性(BOD5/COD)達易分解之程度(BOD5/COD ≧0.3)之最佳組合。實驗乃利用瓶杯試驗（1 L
反應瓶，每個反應瓶內裝 200 mL 廢水，磁石攪拌速度 200 rpm，廢水水溫則利用循環水浴
將溫度控制於 25 ℃）來進行，光源則由太陽光模擬試驗機(Suntest CPS+ model, ATLAS Co.,
USA)裝置一支 1500 W 之 Xe 燈來提供反應過程所需要之光輻射(300800 nm)能量，功率設
定為 500 W/m2 (其中 UVA+B: 35.5 W/m2)，此約等於實際太陽光 1,100 W/m2 中波長 300800
nm 之光輻射能量；第二階段則分別比較[H2O2]0/COD 和[H2O2]0/[Fe2+]0添加比例對於廢水色
度、COD 和 TOC 去除率及生物可分解性提升之影響，第三階段則在廢水最適加藥條件下，
以實際太陽光（光強度約 1,000 W/m2，其中 UVA+B: 49.7～53.8 W/m2）進行附加 Fresnel lens
於處理系統中，所產生之效應探討。

2. Fresnel lens 聚光板對彩色濾光板廢水處理之效應探討
本研究依據一般太陽能電池聚光鏡之設計概念，製作一Fresnel lens聚光板（如圖5所
示），此聚光板之材質為透光性佳之壓克力(Polymethylmethacrylate, PMMA) 材質，每片尺
寸為長寬各為395 mm、厚度約2 mm、環紋距離(pitch) 0.5 mm、溝槽深度(Facet depth)約為
0.2 mm、環紋數(Fresnel circles)約395環，此聚光板經測試發現對於300-800 nm之光之透光
率達90 %以上，聚光板在廢水表面之太陽能輻射強度均勻分佈，在光圈達10 cm時（聚光板
與廢水表面之距離約16 cm），光強度增加平均可達2倍以上，意即達2個太陽(2 sun)之照射
強度，當光圈縮至1 cm時，光強度則可增加10倍以上，另對於紫外光強度（λ< 400 nm）
亦能顯著提升（如表4所示），此對於水溶液中之Fe3+還原成Fe2+，有相當大之助益。

六、計畫成果自評
本研究成果內容大致達到計畫書之預定成果，綜合本研究之結果，可知藉由太陽光之照
射，能有效地促進 Fenton 程序氧化光電業彩色濾光片廢水之效率，另附加 Fresnel lens 聚光
板進行太陽光 photo-Fenton 程序處理彩色濾光片廢水，則不僅可縮短反應所需時間 50 %以
上，且其處理效率亦可達到再提升之效果，因此 Fresnel lens 聚光板之應用，有效地提高太
陽光 photo-Fenton 程序於實廠廢水之技術及經濟可行性。

combination of H2O2 with Fe2+ (eq. (1)). The reactions involved are briefly described as:
Fe2++H2O2  → Fe3+ + OH + OH‧ - (1)
When irradiation (< 450 nm) is involved, the Fe3+ generated by Fenton’s reaction (eq. (1)) is

Fe3++H2O→ Fe(OH)2+ +H+ (2)

Even though photo-Fenton oxidation for wastewater treatment has been shown to be highly
efficient, their operation is actually quite expensive (Da Hora et al., 2005). The combination of
photo-Fenton oxidation as a preliminary treatment, followed by an inexpensive biological process,
would seem to be an economically attractive option (Lapertot et al., 2006). Similarly, most of the
photo-Fenton oxidation studies on CPs have focused on combining artificial UV light with
Fenton process, leading to a higher operation cost. The possibility of applying natural solar light
taking the place of artificial UV light in photo-Fenton process would pave a new way for
pretreatment of industrial wastewater.
In this study, the biodegradability (measured as the ratio BOD5/COD) of chlorophenols,
including 2-chlorophenol (2-CP), 4-chlorophenol (4-CP), and 2,4-dichlorophenol
(2,4-DCP), improved by solar photo-Fenton process was investigated. Moreover, to
understand the enhancing motivation of solar photo-Fenton process for biodegradability of
chlorophenol wastewater, the decomposition intermediates and average oxidation state (AOS)
variation of wastewater were also investigated.
MATERIALS AND METHODS
Materials
CPs(2-CP, 4-CP and 2,4-DCP) with a purity of 99 % were purchased from Acros Co., USA and
used without further purification. The initial concentration of CPs during all the experimental
runs was 3 mM. Hydrogen peroxide (H2O2) with a concentration of 35% was supplied by Panreac
Co., USA. Analytical-grade FeSO4.7H2O (Panreac Co., USA) was used as a source of ferrous ion.
HPLC-grade methanol (J.T. Baker Co., USA) was employed as eluent. All other chemicals used
in this study were analytical grade and used as received.
Procedures
All experiments were carried out in a batch mode. A 1-L glass beaker containing 500 mL of CPs
solution was used and maintained at a temperature of 30 0C in a water bath during the
experiments at room and solar conditions. The initial pH of the solution was adjusted to pH 3.0 ±
0.1 using 0.1 M H2SO4. The concentration of hydrogen peroxide adding to CPs solution was in
the dosage of 7.5, 15.0, 22.5 or 30.0 mM. The amount of Fe2+ ion used for each run was 0.6, 0.9,
or 3.0 mM to make a initial ratio of [H2O2]/[Fe2+] being 2.550. For solar photo-Fenton process,
experiments were conducted at National United University, Taiwan (N24011’33”, E120034’48”), 
under clear sky conditions. The solar intensity was measured with a LI-250 radiation indicator
(LI-COR Co., USA) of pyranometer LI-200SA. The irradiated solar intensity during the
experimental run was in the range of 900 1100 W/m2 (UV290-390: 30 35 W/m2). During the
experiments, the pH of the solution was monitored using a pH meter (SP-701LI 120, Suntex Co.,
Taiwan) equipped with a glass electrode. Samples were withdrawn from the reactor at preset time
intervals, tested for H2O2 consumption according to the method reported by Seller (1980) and
iron as Fe2+ ion determined by Fe(II)/1,10-phenanthroline complex at 510 nm
(APHA/AWWA/WEF, 2000) using a Hitachi (Japan) U-2001 spectrophotometer instantly, and
then quenched with sodium hydrogen sulfite to avoid further reactions. The samples were then
stored at 4 0C for the following HPLC, COD, TOC, IC and BOD5 analysis.

reagent for CPs pretreatment. Among the conditions studied, the optimum pretreatment
condition of enhancing biodegradability of CPs wastewater was with a [H2O2]0/[Fe2+]0 value of
3350 for a lower Fe2+ dosage. This value may response to an optimal
[H2O2]0/[Fe2+]0/[chlorophenol]0 ratio of 10.0/0.2/1.0, 10.0/0.3/1.0, and 10.0/0.2/1.0 for 2-CP,
4-CP, and 2,4-DCP, respectively. As a result, the value of BOD5/COD was increased from 0 for
untreated solution up to 0.231, 0.248, and 0.193 for 2-CP, 4-CP, and 2,4-DCP, respectively with a
15-min pretreatment. The biodegradability of wastewater increased more for monochlorophenol
(2-CP, 4-CP) than polychlorophenol (2,4-DCP). This could be due to that the increase of chlorine
content will block some favorable positions susceptible to hydroxyl radical attack, leading to
decrease the reactivity of polychlorophenols towards hydroxyl radicals (Tang and Huang, 1996).
Moreover, the microtoxicity of chlorophenol solution decreased more than 95 %. The COD and
TOC degradation efficiency could be in excess of 90 % on the whole as the preoxidized
chlorophenol solution treated by a following batch-type biological unit (Table 1).

Effect of AOS and intermediates on biodegradability
The degree of oxidation and produced intermediates of CPs wastewater during solar
photo-Fenton pretreatment affected significantly the biodegradability of solution. Average
oxidation state (AOS) could be used to monitor the change in the degree of oxidation (Al
Momani et al., 2006). The average oxidation state of the organic carbon was calculated by
equation (4).
AOS = 4 (TOC−COD)/TOC (4)
in which the unit of COD and TOC is mol O2/L and mol C/L, respectively. The AOS value
indicates how chemical substances in the effluent become more oxidized. A higher AOS value
implies a higher degree of oxidation. Theoretically, the AOS has a value of +4 for CO2, the most
oxidized state of C, and −4 for CH4, the most reduced state of carbon. 2-CP, 4-CP, and 2,4-DCP
has an AOS value of -0.33, -0.33, and 0, respectively. The detected intermediates of CPs
degradation such as hydroquinone, acetic acid, maleic acid and oxalic acid have an AOS value of
-0.33, 0, 0.67, and +3, respectively. Based on the oxidation behaviors and identified intermediates
of CPs wastewater, three oxidation stage could be illustrated as shown in Fig. 2. The first one
with an AOS value below 0 showed the system with insufficient oxidative power to effectively
decompose chlorophenols, leading to some benzene-ring containing compounds such as quinone,
catechol, and hydroquinone were accumulated in solution. In the second stage (AOS value was
between 1 and 2), some organic acids such as muconic acid, maleic acid, and oxalic acid were
found in solution, indicating that the phenyl-ring structure of chlorophenol was decomposed and
then produced more biodegradable aliphatic organic compounds. Finally, mineralization of
wastewater was almost complete in the third stage which was with an AOS value of 3~4. Under
the operation of optimum conditions, the oxidative state of chlorophenol wastewater could be
easily controlled into the second stage which implied a higher biodegradability stage and less
H2O2 required. Accordingly, the proposed degradation pathways of CPs along with 3 AOS stages
could be illustrated by Fig. 3.

CONCLUSIONS
The parameters including H2O2 dosage and Fe2+ dosage as well as initial ratio of
[H2O2]/[Fe2+]/[chlorophenol] in solar photo-Fenton process affected the biodegradability of
chlorophenol wastewater. Nevertheless, increasing initial H2O2 dosage was much more beneficial
for increasing biodegradability of wastewater than increasing initial Fe2+ dosage. Among the
conditions studied, the optimum condition of increasing biodegradability of chlorophenol
wastewater was found with a [H2O2]0/[Fe2+]0/[chlorophenol]0 ratio of 10.0/0.2/1.0, 10.0/0.3/1.0,
and 10.0/0.2/1.0 for 2-CP, 4-CP, and 2,4-DCP, respectively. As a result, the value of BOD5/COD
was increased from 0 for untreated solution up to 0.231, 0.248, and 0.193 for 2-CP, 4-CP, and
2,4-DCP, respectively with a 15-min treatment. Moreover, the oxidative state of chlorophenol
wastewater could be easily controlled into a higher biodegradability stage by suitable amounts of
oxidant and reaction time. Based on the results obtained in this study, it was suggested that the
solar photo-Fenton process could be operated effectively as a pretreatment step to convert
biorecalcitrant compounds into more readily biodegradable and less toxic intermediates. A
conspicuous COD and TOC removal efficiency could be achieved as the preoxidized solution
treated by a following biological process.
