附錄 A: “Simplified 6-DOF Simulation Models and Guidance 
Laws for Bank to Turn Unmanned Aerial Vehicles” 
ABSTRACT 
In this paper, simplified 6-DOF mathematical models are proposed for analyses and designs of unmanned aerial 
vehicles (UAVs). The derivations of those models can be based upon (1) design specifications of aerodynamics 
of a new UAV or (2) system identification datum of an existing UAV. Datum used are ratio of the lifting force to 
angle of attack, trim angle of fin and natural frequency for constant speed and altitude flight. Using the proposed 
method, one can perform autopilot designs, and guidance laws developments before the aerodynamic model 
obtained from wind tunnel tests or the physical UAV can be applied. A Tomahawk-like cruise UAV example 
shows simplified 6-DOF models give good approximations to the complete 6-DOF system for developing 
guidance and control laws. 
Keywords: Simplified 6-DOF models, guidance laws, UAV. 
 
1. Introduction 
An unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV) is an aircraft 
without onboard pilot. The role of unmanned Aerial 
vehicles has been increasing significantly in both military 
and civilian operations. UAVs can be remote controlled 
or fly autonomously based on preprogrammed flight 
plans or more complex dynamic automation systems. 
Global position system (GPS) and image seeker can 
provide datum for mid-course and terminal guidance 
respectively.  
The simplified 6-DOF simulation model of 
unmanned aerial vehicle [1, 2] is usually used to evaluate 
the hardware specifications in System Requirement 
Review (SRR) phase. In this phase, there is no 
aerodynamic models got from expensive wind tunnel 
tests. However, one may have design specifications of 
aerodynamics. They are derived from adjoin or 
Monte-Carlo simulations [3, 4]. They base on engaging 
requirement for moving or stationary target. The typical 
aerodynamic design specifications are (1) minimal 
maneuver capability, (2) turning rate constant, (3) natural 
frequency. (4) bandwidth of flight control system(FCS). 
Base upon those data, one can further define (1) ratios of 
lifting forces to angle of attack and sideslip, (2) trim 
angles of fins with respect to angle of attack and sideslip. 
According with these specifications and define datum, 
small-signal aerodynamic models can be derived. Then, 
one can perform analyses and designs of flight control 
system [5-7]. Adding trim conditions back to the found 
small-signal models with the found flight control system, 
then the simplified 6-DOF simulation models are derived 
and can perform 6-DOF simulation to find the hardware 
requirements and develop guidance laws. Hardware 
includes accelerometers, fin actuators, rate gyros, 
altimeter, and RF seeker. The simplified 6-DOF models 
give more information than those of found by transfer 
function. Since, simplified models give good 
approximations to the complete system and developed 
guidance and control laws can be applied to the complete 
system directly. It gives same advantage also for an 
existing datum have derived from wind tunnel testing.  
In this paper, a Tomahawk-like cruise UAV example 
is considered. Simulation verification results show 
simplified 6-DOF models give good approximations to 
the complete system model for developing guidance and 
control laws. In this work, the simplified models are 
derived from identification from the complete 6-DOF 
simulation datum. They are not derived by the small 
perturbation method from trim conditions [7]. The 
parameters of identified model are closed related to 
design specifications. 
This paper is organized as: (1) in Section 2, the 
considered system is discussed; (2) in Section 3, 
simplified equations of motion with small-signal dynamic 
models are derived; (3) in Section 4, Bank to Turn(BTT) 
guidance law are developed for lateral and vertical plane; 
(4)in Section 5, flight simulations verification with an 
illustrating example are presented.

4. BTT guidance laws 
4.0 The guidane concepts of the Tomohawk 
cruise missile[9,10]  
The concepts of the UAV’s guidance laws are based 
upon the Tomahawk cruise missile. The Tomahawk 
land-attack cruise missile has been used to attack a 
variety of fixed targets, including air defense and 
communications sites, often in high-threat environments. 
The land attack version of Tomahawk has inertial and 
terrain contour matching (TERCOM) radar guidance. The 
TERCOM radar uses a stored map reference to compare 
with the actual terrain to determine the missile's position. 
If necessary, a course correction is then made to place the 
missile on course to the target. Terminal guidance in the 
target area is provided by the optical Digital Scene 
Matching Area Correlation (DSMAC) system, which 
compares a stored image of target with the actual target 
image. The concepts of lateral and vertical guidance laws 
are shown in Fig.7 and Fig.8.

3.高度控制命令(Hc)產生方式 
    高度控制命令產生方式有(1)統計法，(2)預設法，及(3)地形追隨法，分述如下： 
(1)統計法(Statistical Method) 
統計法概念，如圖七所示，利用統計法則算出平均高度(Hmean)，標準偏差量(δH )，高度控制
命令(Hc’)是以δ H 再加上安全高度δS 而成，這種方式可以即時(Real Time)方式進行，無人載
具邊飛行邊量測相對高度(Hm)，並計算Hmean及δH，然後決定飛行HC'，此法的飛行路徑平
滑，不需要大 G 值，且不需預知地形狀況，但飛行高度較高，以提高防碰撞率。統計法比較
適合崎嶇度不高的老年地形。統計法也可以非即時(Off-line)方式，做飛行路徑的初步規劃，
統計分析事先獲得的地形資料，計算出適合的飛行高度及路徑。 
(2)預設法(Preplanned Altitude Setting Method) 
預設法的概念，如圖八所示。高度控制命令依據事先獲得的地形資料，設定控制命令，這種
方法和導航儀導航精度的關連性較強。命令的產生方法可用上述的統計法，分段計算飛行高
度後，再細部修正，使飛行高度更接近地表。圖中的 Xe必須大於導航精度(見圖四)，當 Xe小
於導航精度時，必須再提高高度控制命令，或再加寬 Xe，以避免碰撞發生，此法之優點分述
如下： 
優點：飛行高度平滑，飛行貼地性佳，操縱能力需求低。 
缺點：導航精度(橫向、縱向)需求較高，必需事先獲得地形資料。 
 
(3)地形追隨法(Terrain Following Method) 
地形追隨法，可事先不知地形的變化，以高度計量測相對高度(與預設法量取的絕對高度不
同)，直接做地形追隨飛行，這種方法的優缺點如下： 
優點：飛行高度低，貼地性佳。 
缺點：操縱能力需求高，仍需預先做路徑規劃，避開陡昇(降)率高的地形。 
 
目前的地形資料獲得容易，所以統計法可以用來做路徑及高度命令計算用，如果有較佳的導
航精度(如 GPS/INS)，可以採行的方式是結合預設法及追隨法兩種方法，以預設法預先規劃
高度控制命令方式，以追隨法防範因導航精度惡化時的高碰撞率產生。巡航高度的規劃一般
都在 50m 以上，所以高度量測的精度需求在高度控制方面不高。 
比較上述三種方法，得知地形追隨法的 G 值需求最高，所以在操縱能力分析時，將以地形追
隨法做為代表，做操縱能力、飛行高度及地形陡昇(降)坡度之間的關係，作圖做為選擇飛行
路徑及飛行高度的參考。
