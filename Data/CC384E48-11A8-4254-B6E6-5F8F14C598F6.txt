? Abstract 
This sub-project is mainly to confer the study of sharing the dynamic environmental information 
and hierarchical strategies for middle-size soccer robots. According to the rules of RoboCup, we 
exploit the omni-directional vision system to be the only sensor of the middle-size soccer robot 
(MSR). By using the fast image processing system, our robots can analyze the information about 
the surroundings and identify the positions of important features in the field. Through the 
real-time image processing and the self-localization algorithm, the absolute coordinate and the 
heading direction of each robot in the soccer field can be calculated, and the absolute coordinate 
of the ball can be also obtained based on the relationship among robots and the ball. By utilizing 
the wireless communication architecture, every MSR sends its image features to the 
hierarchically strategic center of the goalkeeper to make decisions. The hierarchically strategic 
center is composed of the environmental information analysis unit and the role-assignment 
mechanism. The offensive or defensive strategy will be determined by the environmental 
information analysis unit and the proper role of each robot will be decided by the 
role-assignment mechanism. Then, each robot combines the current image information and role 
assignment to perform the dynamic behavior with the fuzzy logic controller. The fully 
autonomous MSR possesses the individual image processing system, the wireless 
communication architecture, and strategic decision system. Finally, the efficiency and feasibility 
of the proposed system are demonstrated by practical experiments.
