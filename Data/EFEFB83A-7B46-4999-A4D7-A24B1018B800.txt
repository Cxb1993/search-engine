Abstract   
A ZnO buffer layer and AZO transparent conducting films have been deposited by 
R. F. magnetron sputtering at room temperature. Electrical, structural, 
morphological, and optical characteristics of AZO thin films grown on glass 
substrates (Corning 1737F) with 50, 150, and 300nm thick ZnO buffer layers 
were studied. In this approach, the grey relational Taguchi method analysis is adopted 
to solve the coating process with multiple deposition qualities. Experimental results 
for the AZO films with ZnO buffer layer (AZO/ZnO/glass), that electrical resistivity 
decreases from 7.721×10-3 Ω-cm to 0.675×10-4 Ω-cm and optical transmittance
