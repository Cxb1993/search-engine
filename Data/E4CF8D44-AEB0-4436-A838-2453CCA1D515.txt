2. 基於知識本體智慧型語意搜尋代理人  
基於知識本體智慧型語意搜尋代理人架構包
含四部份 : (1) 文件處理及摘要機制 (Document 
Processing/Summarization Mechanism); (2) 基因學
習機制(Genetic Learning Mechanism)及(3) 智慧型
語 意 搜 尋 代 理 人 (Intelligent Semantic Search 
Agent)。現分別說明如下: 
 
 文件處理及摘要機制 
文件處理及摘要機制主要沿用 94 年度之計畫
成果，再加入索引機制，做為使用者查詢時使用，
此機制包含五個部份： (a) 資訊擷取代理人
(Information Retrieval Agent)；(b)文件處理機制
(Document Processing Mechanism)；(c)文件摘要機
制(Document Summarization Mechanism)；(d)基因
學習機制(Genetic Learning Mechanism)及(e)索引
機制(Indexing Mechanism)。其中我們將知識庫分
為兩大部份，分別由(1) CMMI Q/A Sets、CKIP 中
文斷詞系統字典、WordNet 及 HowNet 所組成的知
識庫 ; 及由 (2) CMMI Q/A Ontology 、 CMMI 
Domain Ontology、Alternation Rule Base、Chinese 
Syntax Pattern 及 English Syntax Pattern 所組成的知
識庫。 
 
 基因學習機制 
這 裏 提 供 CMMI 需 求 發 展 (Requirement 
Development, RD) 、 需 求 管 理 (Requirement 
Management, REQM)、專案規劃(Project Planning, 
PP)及專案監控 (Project Monitoring and Control, 
PMC)等流程領域之 5W1H(Where, Who, When, 
What, Why, How)句型做為訓練資料，然後經由本
計畫所開發出來的 7 層式平行模糊推論機制產生
出 Defuzzify 結果，則再經由基因的交配與突變，
直到產生理想的結果。基因學習程序藉由編碼概要
及利用限制式初始化族群，然後記錄這些已經初始
化的族群當作目前的族群。在目前族群中之染色體
利用適配函數進行評估。如果利用適配函數評估的
結果不理想，則基因學習程序將會進行衍生。精英
法被用於學習的程序中。選擇剛開始，從目前的族
群中選出最佳的兩個染色體直接放到新的族群
中，不進行交配和突變的程序。在精英法之後，利
用輪盤法選擇父代，產生其餘的染色體，再利用生
產、交配及突變運算子產生子代，而個體具有較佳
的適配度會有更多的機會被生產。為了讓知識庫內
的染色體，能有效率且合理的競爭和淘汰，所以選
擇方法就變的很重要。 
 
 智慧型語意搜尋代理人 
使用者送出一個自然語言查詢指令，經由自然
語 言 處 理 機 制 (Natural Language Processing 
Mechanism)處理，並參考 CMMI Ontology-based 
Q/A Knowledge Base，產生 CMMI Concept。而結
構化/非結構化文件在經過文件處理及摘要機制處
理後，產生有意義的詞彙，再配合索引機制及參考
CMMI Ontology-based Q/A Knowledge Base，產生
所收集到的結構化或是非結構化的文件的檔案索
引。接著 CMMI Concept 及文件的檔案索引，經由
排序機制(Ranking Mechanism)組合產生排序結果
(Ranking Results)，這樣使用者就可以得到之前自
然語言查詢所得到的結果。同時智慧型語意搜尋代
理人會參考使用者的使用回饋記錄，自動進行基因
學習機制，調整輸出結果至最好的狀況，並將結果
輸入到 CMMI Concept Generating Mechanism，以
重新產生新的 Concept。

Ontology 中可以了解每個 Concept 之間的關聯，比
如說有些活動狀態(What)、人員(Who)以及時間
(When)是有關聯的，如圖 6(a)所示，召開研討會的

Abstract: 
This paper proposes a Customer Relationship 
Management (CRM) Ontology based on CMMI project 
planning for business application. The CRM ontology 
describes the CRM project planning knowledge based on 
CMMI. It contains three categories, including establishing 
CRM project estimates, developing CRM project plan, and 
obtaining CRM project commitment. There are various 
concepts in the class layer and some instances in the instances 
layer defined in the ontology. The proposed ontology can be 
applied to construct an intelligent CRM system based on 
CMMI project planning for business applications. 
Keywords: 
CMMI; Project Planning; Ontology; Customer 
Relationship Management 
1. INTRODUCTION 
Recently, the customer relationship 
management (CRM) has become one of the most 
important issues in the enterprise. It is the integration of 
marketing, sales and service functions through IT-enabled 
business process automation such that the value of each 
customer interaction can be maximized. It also provides fast 
and efficient transactions to help companies to acquire, 
serve, and retain the customer relationship, such as 
“potential customers development,” “marketing strategy 
reconciliation,” “customer service management,” and 
“enterprise knowledge management” [1]. Capability 
Maturity Model Integration (CMMI) is a process 
improvement approach developed by Software Engineering 
Institute (SEI) in 1999, which is a joint venture of the U.S. 
Department of Defense and Carnegie Mellon University 
(CMU). CMMI can be used in such professional fields as 
software engineering, and system engineering, integrated 
product development. Hence its processes and 
developments can be evaluated and improved. It has 
become an internationally recognized process for software 
development and system development [2]. 
The purpose of the project planning defined in CMMI 
is to establish and maintain plans that define project 
activities. The project planning process area involves the 
following issues: developing the project plan, interacting 
with stakeholders appropriately, getting commitment to the 
plan, and maintaining the plan [2]. CMMI project planning 
process area includes three specific goals, such as 
establishing estimates, developing a project plan, and 
obtaining commitment to the plan. The first specific goal is 
to establish estimates, including estimating the scope of the 
project, establishing estimates of work product and task 
attributes, and defining project life cycle. All of these 
estimates are the foundations which are used to develop the 
project plan. The second specific goal is to develop a 
project plan, including establishing the budget and 
schedule, identifying project risks, planing for data 
management, planing for project resources, planing for 
needed knowledge and skills, planing stakeholder 
involvement and establishing the project plan. The third 
specific goal is to obtain commitment to the plan, including 
reviewing plans that affect the project, reconciling work 
and resource levels, and obtaining plan commitments [2]. 
With the support of the ontology, both user and system 
can communicate with each other by the shared and 
common understanding of a domain [3]. There are many 
ontological applications that have been presented in various 
domains. For example, Lee et al. [4] proposed an 
ontology-based fuzzy event extraction agent for Chinese 
news summarization. The summarization agent can 
generate a sentence set for each Chinese news, but the 
results need to be improved to suit for various Chinese 
news websites. Lee et al. [3] also presented an 
ontology-based decision support agent for CMMI project 
monitoring and control. In this paper, we purpose a CRM 
Ontology based on CMMI project planning for business 
application. Three categories are involved in the CRM

4. CRM ONTOLOGY FOR BUSINESS APPLICATION 
In this section, we give some guidelines for applying 
CRM ontology to business application. Based on the CRM 
ontology presented in Section 3, three categories are 
defined in the knowledge model, including establishing 
CRM project estimates, developing CRM project plan, and 
obtaining CRM project commitment. Now, we briefly 
describe them as follows. 
A. Establish CRM Project Estimates 
Step1. Estimate the scope of the CRM project 
Step1.1 Establish the CRM project 
Step1.2 Establish the CRM project requirement 
Step1.3 Get the work breakdown structure (WBS) 
of the CRM project 
Step2. Define CRM project life cycle 
Step2.1 Develop the concept of CRM project 
Step2.2 Develop the CRM project plan 
Step2.3 Execute the CRM project plan 
Step2.4 Finish the CRM project plan 
Step3. Evaluate project cost and effort for the CRM  
project 
 
B. Develop CRM Project Plan 
Step1. Establish the CRM project schedule and 
budget 
Step1.1 Establish the CRM project schedule 
Step1.2 Establish the CRM project budget 
Step2. Identify the CRM project risks 
Step2.1 Identify the risk list 
Step2.2 Identify the risk impact 
Step2.3 Identify the risk probability of 
occurrence 
Step2.4 Identify the risk priorities 
Step3. Plan for the CRM project resources 
Step3.1 Plan the human resources 
Step3.2 Plan the equipment resources 
Step4. Plan for the needed knowledge and skills of 
the 
CRM project 
Step5. Project stakeholder involvement for the CRM 
project 
Step6. Establish the project plan for the CRM project 
C. Obtain CRM Project Commitment 
Step1. Review the project plan 
Step2. Reconcile project work and resource levels  
for the CRM project 
Step2.1 Reconcile the resources levels  
Step2.2 Revise the estimated parameters  
Step2.3 Revise the human resources 
Step2.4 Revise the equipment resources 
Step2.5 Revise the cost 
5. CONCLUSION 
In this paper, we propose a CRM Ontology based on 
CMMI project planning for business application. The CRM 
ontology describes the CRM project planning knowledge. 
Three categories, including establishing CRM project 
estimates, developing CRM project plan, and obtaining 
CRM project commitment, are defined in the ontology. The 
proposed ontology can be applied to business to construct 
an intelligent CRM system based on CMMI project 
planning.

4. 參與會議的照片 
  
Abstract - In this paper, several ontology applications 
developed by Ontology Application and Software Engineering 
Laboratory (OASE Lab.) of National University of Tainan 
(NUTN) in TAIWAN are introduced. The first study aims at 
developing an ontology-based intelligent decision support agent 
(OIDSA) for capability maturity model integration (CMMI) 
project monitoring and control. The evaluation shows that the 
OIDSA for CMMI project monitoring and control can 
effectively evaluate the completed percentage of progress to 
reduce the human effort and the costs of the project. The 
second study presents an intelligent fuzzy agent based on 
process and product quality assurance (PPQA) ontology for 
supporting CMMI assessment. The experiments show that the 
proposed approach can effectively operate for the 
summarization of the evaluation reports. Besides, the ontology 
technology is able to successfully apply to other domains, such 
as image processing, news summarization, meeting scheduling 
system, and healthcare. 
 
Index Terms—Ontology, CMMI, news summarization,

Ontology is playing an increasingly important role in 
knowledge management and the Semantic Web. This section 
describes a novel episode-based ontology construction 
mechanism to extract domain ontology from unstructured 
text documents. Fig. 7 shows episode-based Chinese domain 
ontology construction flowchart. Additionally, fuzzy 
numbers for conceptual similarity computing are presented 
for concept clustering and taxonomic relation definitions.

from episodes to construct a domain ontology, while 
non-taxonomic relations can be generated from episodes.

instances for ontology learning. Experimental results show 
that the proposed approach can effectively construct a

First, the nutrition facts for some popular food eaten in

experts evaluate if the recommended menus meet a balanced 
and healthy diet.

D. Real-Time Food Recommendation Mechanism

personal food ontology, the real-time food recommendation 
mechanism generates some dinner menus recommended for 
diabetics. Meantime, the recommended menus are also given 
to domain experts to confirm if they are met a balanced and 
healthy diet. Next, we show the detailed algorithm of the 
proposed personal food recommendation agent.

V. CONCLUSION 
This paper proposes an intelligent ontological agent for 
diabetic food recommendation, including an ontology 
creating mechanism, a personal ontology filter, a food fuzzy 
number creating mechanism, a fuzzy inference mechanism, 
and a real-time food recommendation mechanism. The 
needed domain knowledge is stored in the Taiwanese food 
ontology and the personal food ontology. The experimental 
results show that the proposed agent is capable of 
recommending an individual dinner menu according to the 
pre-constructed domain ontology. In the future, the Type-2 
fuzzy set would be introduced to create the membership 
functions of the Taiwanese food ontology and personal food 
ontology. Besides, the semantic inference and the adaptive 
learning capabilities will be added to the proposed agent to 
enhance the performance and the ability of the intelligent 
ontological agent.
