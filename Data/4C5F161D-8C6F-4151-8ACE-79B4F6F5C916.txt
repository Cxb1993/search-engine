以廣域量測技術應用於含大型風場電力系統之彈性交流輸電系統控制策略開發 
主持人：國立清華大學電機系 朱家齊 副教授 
執行期限： 98/8/01 ～ 99/7/31 
計畫編號： 98-2221-E-007-120- 
 
摘要 :現代電力系統中均會配置廣域量測系統
(WAMS)，未來將提昇至廣域控制系統(WACS)。其問題
之關鍵點在於需考量通訊延遲(latency)。本計畫探討彈
性交流輸電系統之線上即時控制技術開發。考慮以下兩
種應用: (1)應用廣域量測技術之電力系統穩定器即時
控制:由於電力通信設備與同步向量量測技術之精進，
廣域量測控制技術之觀念正興起。廣域量測技術雖可提
供本地控制器更多之輔助控制信號，增加系統可觀性。
但通信網路之時間延遲，亦可能破壞系統穩定度。因此
於控制器之設計上，需考量其強健性。本計畫探討如何
利用遠端信號，設計電力系統穩定器，改善電力系統區
域間振盪問題。(2) 以暫態能量函數法，進行 UPFC 線
上穩定度輔助控制:廣域量測控制技術亦促成暫態能量
函數之發展。首先將發展其解析型式。並推導線上監控
之穩定度指標。最後在依暫態能量函數發展非線性之阻
尼控制法則。並針對時間延遲之統計特性，進行加入非
線性之阻尼控制法則後，系統受擾動後之即時模擬。 
關鍵詞: 彈性交流輸電系統、即時模擬控制平台、廣域
量測控制、強健控制、暫態能量函數、非線性阻尼控制。 
Abstract:  Wide Area Measurement Systems have been 
widely deployed in modern power systems. This technique 
will be further extended to wide area stability control 
systems (WACS) with considering modern  controllers 
techniques such as power system stabilizers (PSS) and 
FACTS. Due tot ime delay in the communication channels, 
WAMS involve delays that are greater than the 
corresponding delay for local controllers. Therefore, it is 
imperative that latency should be considered inthe design 
phase. With such dedicated considerations in controller 
design, the controller is expected to be more robust even 
under latency variations. In this project,  the following 
issues will be addressed: (1)WACS-based controller design 
with considering the communication delay, and (2) stability 
enhancement by transient energy functions of power 
systems with induction generators and FACTS: 
Keywords: Flexible AC Transmission System (FACTS), 
Wide-Area Control System (WACS), Communication 
Latency, Transient Energy Functions (TEF), Nonlinear 
Damping Control 
I. 前言 
現代電力系統中，通常包含大量的量測訊號作為通
訊之用，以完成電力系統控制之目標，其架構圖如圖 1
所示，這樣的量測系統與通訊網路被稱做為廣域量測系
統(WAMS)。廣域量測系統的成功與否，主要取決於所
使用的相角量測單元，相角量測技術可以大大改善可觀
度，因此可以更精確地看到系統需求而反應。近年來廣
域量測系統已經被應用在及時系統穩定度強化上，未來
將提昇到廣域控制系統(WACS)，用以控制電力系統中之
電力系統穩定器(PSS)、高壓直流傳輸系統與交流彈性傳
輸系統(FACTS)中的輔助型控制。 
 
圖 1 含有再生能源之廣域量測電力系統架構圖 
 
在廣域控制技術中，主要的問題在於通訊延遲
(latency)。傳統上，在電力系統強健控制中，時間延遲
的影響是可以忽略的，但是在廣域控制中，時間延遲就
變成需要考慮的因素了。長時間的延遲可能會對廣域控
制的穩定性造成損害，而降低系統的強健性。由於在通
訊通道的時間延遲，廣域量測系統會涉及到超過區域量
測所造成的時間延遲，因此在設計控制器上，必須考慮
latency 的影響以及控制器也應該被預期可以在延遲的
影響下，展現其強健性。 
本計畫將結合廣域控制與彈性交流傳輸系統之即時
控制系統。考慮以下先進技術於電力系統之應用: (1)
應用廣域量測技術之電力系統穩定器即時控制:由於電
力通信設備與同步向量量測技術之精進，廣域量測控制
技術之觀念正興起。廣域量測技術雖可提供本地控制器
更多之輔助控制信號，增加系統可觀性。但通信網路之
時間延遲，亦可能破壞系統穩定度。因此於控制器之設
計上，需考量其強健性。本計畫探討如何利用遠端信
號，設計電力系統穩定器，改善電力系統區域間振盪問
題。(2)以暫態能量函數法，進行 UPFC 線上穩定度輔助
控制: 廣域量測控制技術亦促成暫態能量函數之發展。
首先將發展包含 UPFC 之解析型式。並推導線上監控之
穩定度指標。最後在依暫態能量函數發展非線性之阻尼
控制法則。並針對時間延遲之統計特性，進行加入非線
性之阻尼控制法則後，系統受擾動後之模擬。 
II. 廣域量測系統時間延遲 
在廣域控制系統溝通網路中，資料是假設成以封包
方式傳輸，這些封包分成三部分：前端(header)、資料負
載端(payload)、末端( trailer)。前端包含以下資訊：封包
長度、起點與終點位置、封包類型以及封包號碼(假如是
傳送一連串的封包)，而資料負載端是從量測端而來的資
料，而末端是在封包的最後以及包含了讓接收裝置可以
辨別是封包末端的資訊。

B. 10 Machine 39 Bus New England System
The second case study involves a reduced order model of a
New England system. This system consists of 10 generators,
39 buses (30 of them are load buses), and 89 interconnecting
lines [6]. Generator 10 is equivalent to a large part of the
interconnected power system and its behavior is similar to an
infinite bus with low equivalent impedance and high inertia.
From system operation conditions viewpoint, it would be
desirable to install the UPFC in a weak transmission line that
is connected to low voltage buses. Another important lines
for UPFC installation are those that play as tie lines. This
kind of line along with some others can form tie lines for
the system and are able to divide the system into two or more
main islands. Bus 26 can be seen as one of a critical boundary
bus and line 26-29 also has a heavily load transfer. Thus, a
UPFC are determined to install between bus 26 and bus 29
near bus 26.
In order to validate the controller performance when the
power system experiences severe disturbances, a three phase
fault is applied at Bus 26 at 0.5s. This fault is cleared at
0.61s by opening one of the two lines between bus 26 and
bus 28. Fig. 7 and 8 show the swing curve of the generator
with and without a UPFC, respectively. It can be observed that
in the multi-machine system without a UPFC, its stability is
lost shortly after the three-phase fault occurs. From Fig. 8, a
New England system remains stable with the help of a UPFC
when using the proposed control law.
VI. CONCLUSION
In this paper, we have developed a energy-based forward
neural network supplementary damping control based on struc-
ture preserving power system modes for improving transient
stability of power systems. Simulation results show that the
proposed supplementary damping strategy can indeed enhance
the system’s transient stability even when power system is
subjected by a large disturbance.

III. ANALYTICAL STUDY OF THE BOUNDARY PROPERTY
Instead of direct verifying the transversality condition of
the BCU method, we will employ tools such as the self-
indexing energy function and the structural stability to analyze
the invariant properties of the UEPs on the stability boundary
and the boundary property.
A. OMIB System
We consider the a single round-rotor synchronous gener-
ator connected to an infinite bus (OMIB) through a lossless
transmission line:

corresponding artificial, reduced-state system is defined as
δ˙ = Pm −B sin((δ)) (4)
and the corresponding one-parameter system Σ(λ) is defined
as
δ˙ = λ(Pm −B sin(δ)) + (1− λ)ω

We start the analysis of the OMIB system with zero me-
chanical power injection. The corresponding energy function
is W (δ, ω) = 12Mω
2 − B cos δ. W (δ, ω) is self-indexing, the
stable manifolds and the unstable manifolds of equilibrium
points of the same type will satisfy the transversality condition.
We can get the following invariant property.
Proposition 1: (Invariant Property of the OMIB system
Under Zero Power Injection) If the OMIB system Eq. (3)
has zero mechanical power injection, then (i) there are only
two UEPs, (pi, 0) and (−pi, 0), lying on the stability boundary
∂A(δs, 0). (ii) The UEPs on the stability boundary (0, 0) of
the one-parameter system Σ(λ) are invariant for all λ ∈ [0, 1].
Next, we show the transversality condition and the boundary
property for the OMIB system (3) with zero mechanical power
injection.
Proposition 2: (Transversality Condition and Boundary
Property) If the OMIB system (3) has zero mechanical
power injection, then (i) the stable and unstable manifolds
of the UEPs of the corresponding one-parameter system Σ(λ)
intersect transversally for all λ ∈ [0, 1]. (ii) The UEPs (pi, 0)
and (−pi, 0) lie on the stability boundary of the original system
(3). The UEPs ±pi lie on the stability boundary of the reduced-
state system (4).
Results in Proposition 2 can be extended to the OMIB
system with a small mechanical power injection and small
transfer conductances by exploring the structural stability [1].
Proposition 3: (Structural stability) If the OMIB system
(3) has zero mechanical power injection, then the correspond-
ing one-parameter system is structurally stable.
The property of structural stability for the OMIB system
(3) with zero mechanical power injection can be extended to

Proposition 5-6 of [1], the invariant properties of the UEP’s

remain true even for a sufficiently small power injection. Thus,
we can get the following proposition:
Proposition 4: (Boundary Property) If the mechanical
power injection Pm and the transfer conductance G of the
OMIB system (6) are sufficiently small with (δs, 0) being a
SEP, then (i) The stable and unstable manifolds of the UEPs of
the corresponding one-parameter system intersect transversally
for each λ ∈ [0, 1]. (ii) (δi, 0) is a UEP ∈ ∂A(δs, 0) of the
original system (6) if and only if δi is a UEP ∈ ∂A(δs) of the
reduced-state system.
Hence, Proposition 4 ensures that the OMIB system with a
small mechanical power injection and small transfer conduc-
tance satisfies the boundary property.

The intersecting stability regions of the original systems with

V. CONCLUSION
We have applied the self-indexing energy function, and
the structural stability to analyze the invariant properties of
the UEPs on the stability boundary of simple power system
stability models. In particular, for both the OMIB system and
the TMIB system, we have shown that, under certain mild
conditions, the original system and the reduced-state system
contain the same UEPs on their stability boundaries.
