摘  要 
 
    本研究主題為風力機之 FRP 葉片，就結構設計之角度切入，配合台灣地區欲開發離岸型風
力發電系統，先從常用之原材料著手研究，藉由各式風力發電機葉片常用之積層板進行靜態與
動態材料試驗，以探明各種纖維與樹脂搭配之材料性質，作為日後設計之資料庫；其次藉由流
場分析程式 Xfoil 的使用、法規之參照及搭配有限元素軟體 ABAQUS 的操作，建立一套考慮空
氣動力與彈力之流固耦合分析流程，並引入纖維配向角改變所造成的耦合效應進行討論，研究
中亦提出改善風力機發電效率之可行性方法；研究最後再將離岸型風力機之浮體錨鏈系統、塔
架與風力機系統進行運動特性之數值分析，並藉由實驗驗證離岸型浮體風力機系統受波浪作用
產生之遙晃對葉片的影響。 
 
 
 
關鍵字：FRP 葉片、材料試驗、空氣動力、流固耦合、離岸型風力機系統

第一章 序  論 
 
1.1 前言 
過去數年間，由於傳統能源的短缺與溫室效應對地球環境的影響，使得各國不得不對綠色
能源做一重新評估與運用，而風力發電於再生性能源中的發展配比更是關鍵因素之一，因此各
國多將 2010 年視為風力發電發展的階段性目標年限，以台灣地區為例，經濟部為因應世界綠色
能源使用之潮流，將再生性能源配比由現階段的 5.45%調高為 10%，其中又以風力發電需承擔
其中的 80%最為關鍵，然因應陸域型風力機開發較早、裝機量已漸趨飽和，且受限於土地取得、
噪音等環境衝擊，後續發展有限，故開發離岸風場為下一階段之趨勢；近年來，在輕量化之考
量下，風力機之葉片多以玻璃纖維強化塑膠(glass fiber-reinforced plastics-GFRP)為主要材料，然
而隨著風力機技術日趨純熟，單一風力機裝置容量亦從早期的 kW 等級，改由 WM 等級之風力
機所取代，而隨著風力機發電容量的快速提升，其整機結構尺寸亦隨之增加，重要元件之ㄧ的
葉片尺寸亦從 1MW 機型的 25 公尺成長到 2MW 的 40 公尺，伴隨而來的結構強度、力學特性、
製程與運輸等問題亦越加複雜。我國有相當成熟之 FRP 遊艇產業，已可製造長度達 130 呎之複
合材料遊艇，對如 2MW 等級大型葉片之製造與運輸已墊下良好之基礎，值得進一步發展。 
故本研究將先對風力機 FRP 葉片之常用材料做一了解，並參考葉片各部位慣用材料資訊，
進行相關之材料動靜態試驗並分析比較其結果，其次搭配翼型流場分析之工具、法規的參照及
有限元素分析軟體 ABAQUS 的分析，建立葉片實際受風場外力時之空氣彈力處理流程，並針
對纖維配向角變化所引起的耦合效應做一探討；第三年度則針對離岸型浮體式風力機進行數值
運動特性推導，並藉由實驗驗證之，從中確認離岸浪流對風力機葉片運轉之影響情形。 
 
 
1.2 文獻回顧 
    在二十世紀中葉前，金屬仍然為主要之葉片材料，Burton, T.在 Wind Energy Handbook[1]中
記載，於 1941 年美國之 Smith-Putnam 1250 kW、直徑 53 m 葉片與 1956 年丹麥之 Gedser 200 kW、
直徑 24 m 葉片皆是以金屬製作。唯隨著葉片之大型化，對重量與性能之要求更形殷切，故較重
的金屬便處於劣勢，此外，金屬尚有造型變化不易之缺點，欲以金屬製作出葉片前緣(leading edge)
以及幾何上之扭轉(twist)等較高曲率之部位均有一定之難度，導致現今愈來愈少以金屬為材料
之葉片，然而金屬仍為風機其他元件如塔架、軸轂、軸系(shaft)等不可或缺之材料。 
 7
分析時產生流固耦合的處理問題，NREL(National Renewable Energy Laboratory) [18-19]即藉由數
值方法分析 ITI Energy Barge 及 TLP 兩種浮體式風力機系統之運動響應，研究中除了考慮風力、
波浪力外，亦將機構控制理論結合其中進行整體離岸風力機之研究；Z-Z Chen [20]亦以 TLP 浮
體式風力機做為分析對象，研究中除適當簡化外力項外，亦將浮體平台及風力機做了合理的假
設，進而求解該類型風力機於海浪中之運轉特性；此外，Nielson[21]及 Henderson[22]等人均提
出，當浮體平台發生運動時，慣性力為風機葉片及塔架受力之主要影響因素，日本 Suzuki 與 Sato
先生[23]則藉由理論與實驗模擬浮體式風力機系統之運動行為，並進行風力機受力分析及葉片
疲勞之評估。由此可知，欲進行離岸型風力機系統之結構解析，應就系統中的浮體結構、錨鏈
系統及風力機進行詳細地運動特性探討。  
    由前述文獻可知，離岸型風力機系統及重要元件葉片涉及問題層面極廣，為此多數學者乃
於各領域進行深入研究，本研究將針對風力機葉片材料、流固耦合之葉片氣彈力問題及浮體式
風力機系統運動特性進行循序漸進之研究與探討。

複合材料乃由強化材與基材(matrix)所組成，其中現行風電葉片市場較常使用之種類為玻璃
纖維及碳纖維，兩者比較如下： 
(1) 在機械特性方面，玻璃纖維均無與碳纖維競爭之空間，如較碳纖維重、比強度及彈性係數
(Young’s modulus)均較弱，然而價格卻是其最大之優勢，根據 Sandia 於 2002 年之報告，多
軸針織之碳纖維(carbon stitched biaxial or triaxial fabric)每公斤價格為 23.30 美金，相同型式
之玻璃纖維(E-glass stitched biaxial or triaxial fabric)為每公斤 4.50 美金，前者為後者之 5 倍，
近年來，受到客機廠商 Boeing 與 Air Bus 在新一代飛機之設計中加重碳纖維之比例，使得於
航空業外碳纖維之供應有短缺之現象，導致其與玻璃纖維之價差更形加大，故以商業生產之
角度思考，玻璃纖維仍有其運用的價值存在。 
(2) 國際上之葉片製造大廠目前仍以玻璃纖維為主要之使用原材料，有時亦會搭配碳纖維進行
局部區域之結構補強，由國內產業之角度來看，我國大型複合材料結構製造廠商亦以玻璃纖
維產品為主力，已具相當製造能量，故玻璃纖維在現階段仍是兼具結構效能與經濟考量下不
可或缺之材料，因此我國欲發展葉片材料仍應以玻璃纖維為主體，然亦可因應大型化結構之
需求而思考搭配碳纖維以減輕重量之發展方向。 
 
    除了纖維種類使用不同外，纖維編織方向的選擇應用，亦是風電葉片設計的重點與變化性
之一，有別於台灣傳統漁船製造時使用玻璃纖維編織而成的切股氈(Mat /strand chopped mats)(圖
2-1)與紗束布(Roving /woven roving) (圖 2-2)，風電材料更強調受力方向性的確認與材料特性發
揮，所以纖維會採用非彎曲(noncrimp)之軸向型纖維，如單軸向纖維(即受力方向主要由單一軸
向纖維所支撐，實際鋪設角度則根據受力評估之需求而調整)或雙軸向纖維(即由兩層不同方向
之單軸纖維編織而成)(圖 2-3)；此外，受限於重量效率之要求，船舶領域常使用於兩層多軸纖維
間以加強接著力之 Mat 在風力機葉片中亦不被使用；再者，使用纖維之面密度(area density)亦為
葉片設計考量之變因之ㄧ，以美國纖維廠 Vectorply 為例，除了纖維的編排方向種類繁多外，重
量的挑選也是結構設計者一個思考的選項。

2.2.4 拉伸破壞模式探討 
a. 縱向拉伸部分 
(1) L900：由於纖維之組成完全以 0 度方向之單軸向纖維為主，橫向(90 度)僅以少數纖維編織固
定，其破壞模式由於單向纖維承受負載之能力較高，在實驗過程中試片之白化現象不
明顯，待初始破壞發生後，可以明顯聽見纖維斷裂之聲音，隨即觀察到初始破壞處會
有束狀白化，而剩餘支撐纖維因承受不住加載之負荷而斷裂，使白化擴及整個試片，
最後纖維從樹脂中抽出使試片呈散開狀，試驗隨即中止。

圖 2-60、DB800 縱向拉伸試片破壞圖(左：正面 / 右：側面) 
 
b. 橫向拉伸部分 
(1) L900：為原 L900 主軸旋轉 90 度後之纖維排列方式，由於橫向排列無法提供強度，負荷完
全由樹脂強度提供，強度相當弱，實驗開始不久隨即斷裂，為橫向破斷且除了破斷處
外材料體無明顯白化現象。

(2) DBL1169：為原 DBL1169 主軸旋轉 90 度後之纖維排列方式（即+45°/-45°/+90°)，其破壞模
式明顯區別為正面是±45 度之破斷，背面是橫向破斷，且同 DB800 有明顯的層
間剝離現象發生。

2.5 材料動態疲勞試驗

第三章 FRP 葉片承受氣動力作用下之流固耦合分析 
 
3.1 FRP 葉片積層材料分佈 
實際之風力機 FRP 葉片中，葉片各剖面除根部附近為圓形外，其餘均以翼形為主，且葉片
由根部沿至葉尖方向時，翼形剖面會逐漸變細並具扭轉角(twist angle)，為建造此種形狀，且須
達到應具備之強度與剛性並兼具輕量化，葉片需採用中空殼結構之設計，典型中空殼之葉片斷
面形狀，大體上可如圖 3-1 所示，分為外殼(skin)、抗剪腹板(shear web)與內縱樑蓋(spar cap)，
其中，外殼之目的在於提供所需要之翼面形狀，即使得葉片具備足夠之空氣動力特性，但單純
由翼形所組成之中空殼剖面結構，在抵抗面外剪切負荷時並非相當地有效率，因此會設計加入
內縱樑(spar)，除分擔彎曲負荷外尚可承受剪切負荷，以防止翼剖面變形及外板不易發生挫曲
(buckling)現象。 
內縱樑包含內縱樑蓋及抗剪腹板，內縱樑蓋之厚度相較於外殼而言較厚，目的在於增強抵
抗彎矩之能力，故多以方向性明顯之單向單軸纖維疊合而成之積層單板(single skin)為主，抗剪
腹板主要用來抵抗剖面之剪切負荷，並藉著與外殼組合而成之封閉剖面來加強整個剖面之抗扭
轉(torsion)特性，故以三明治結構(sandwich structure)為主，外殼部份則視設計需求不同則為三
明治結構或積層單板或兩者均包含之結構設計。 
 
圖 3-1、葉片斷面之基本結構 
 
    於纖維種類方面，內縱樑蓋係承受主要之彎曲負荷，故以單軸向纖維為主，抗剪腹板為三
明治結構，主要用以承受剪切負荷，因此面材所用之纖維以雙軸且夾±45°受力方向之纖維布為
主以補強抗剪能力，外殼部分除內縱樑蓋區域外，若考量減輕重量之緣故，多以三明治結構為
主，於面材纖維上尚需具備葉片長度方向，故以三軸向纖維為主。

3.3 FRP 葉片承受氣動力作用之流固耦合程序 
3.3.1 氣動力考量與風壓給定 
    本研究係建立一套在考慮氣動力(aero-dynamic)作用時，探討空氣流體與葉片結構間相互作

速 70m/s 作為探討條件，分析流程如上所述，應先確認該風力機之運轉狀態為何，由於此時之
風力機因超過額定運轉風速 25m/s，故已呈現停機狀態，且當風速介於 25m/s~70m/s 時，風力
機之葉片將旋轉 90 度至與葉輪旋轉平面垂直，使其受風產生之旋轉力最小，於實際風場狀況
時，偏航(yaw)系統將呈現持續吹風狀態，其中上風或下風型之系統策略又因風力機製造商而

定且垂直迎向葉輪方向，而 GL 於極限風速(EWM)規範篇章中亦假設此時極限風速之風況為穩

第四章 離岸風力機系統動搖分析 
 
4.1 離岸型浮體式風力發電機之介紹 
離岸型風力發電(或稱海上風力發電)之概念在 1970 年代由麻州大學的 William E. 
Heronemus 教授提出，由於當時的風電技術尚未成熟，且沒有發展再生能源的迫切性，因此，
離岸風電僅是一個未來可發展之概念，並沒被其他研究單位、企業界等付諸實行；直到 1990
年代中期，風力發電的技術漸趨成熟，歐洲許多企業紛紛投入風力發電的產業，陸上型風力發
電機也開始量產，風電產業進入商業化時代，且隨著風力機技術成熟的緣故，離岸型風力發電
機的概念又被重新提起，丹麥、荷蘭及瑞典等國陸續發展，丹麥於 2003 年在哥本哈根港埠入口
處附近，設立「米代爾．葛倫登」濱海風力園區，共有 20 座海上風力發電機，總設備容量達
700MW，隨後英國、德國及西班牙等也訂定發展計劃，離岸風電的概念在世界各國積極推動之
下如火如荼地展開。 
離岸型風力發電機受到世界各國的重視有其原因，除了風力發電本身之優勢以外，離岸型
風力發電具備以下之優點： 
1. 海上之風力資源豐富，比陸上之風能更為充沛。 
2. 對環境的影響較小。 
3. 安置於海上，節省陸上空間。 
目前離岸風力發電機多屬於近岸式設置，即風力機組於淺水區域(shallow water)海床上，利
用打樁方式固定之，近岸式風力發電機之技術與陸上型風力發電機大致相同，然而部份工事需
搭配海事工程進行，故設立風力機的工程亦較為複雜。 
然而，各國之海岸線長度、近岸區域有限，欲擴展海上風田、增加風力發電的發電量，勢
必須往深水區域(deep water)發展，故離岸風電之發展趨勢如圖 4-1 所示，由水深較淺、離岸較
近的區域，往水深較深、離岸較遠的區域發展，然而若進一步在深水區設立離岸風力機，將會
面臨經濟與技術層面的問題，目前深水區離岸風電處於積極發展之階段，技術層面尚未成熟，
為此設置成本仍舊居高不下，然而美國國家再生能源研究室(NREL)指出，技術的進步與成熟勢
必使得製造成本下降，且只要離岸風力機所需成本降至一定比例以下，各單位勢必積極投入於
深水區域設置離岸風力機。

式為 TLP 浮體平台。 
3. Buoyancy：此類型之浮體平台利用浮力的分佈使整體機構達到靜態穩定。較有名的型

提供適度的系統浮力與穩定度，為此多國之研究單位，如美國、挪威、日本等均著手設計該 SPAR
類型之離岸型浮體式風力機系統，故本研究亦選擇此 SPAR 類型設計概念之離岸型浮體式風力

第五章 結 論 
    本研究第一年度針對風電葉片纖維積層板進行相關材料試驗，建立相當數量之實驗資料
庫，除本身研究使用外，亦提供國內其他單位，如聯合船舶設計中心以進行風電葉片結構設計
時參考使用，材料試驗數據亦提供予國內複合材料製造廠商作為生產製造之參考依據，研究成
果對於產業技術之提升有所助益。 
    第二年度針對葉片承受空氣動力時產生空氣彈力之流固耦合問題進行解析，研究過程中除
需了解其他流體計算、控制研究領域知識外，亦需於本身之複材結構領域有所深入探討，該課
題之探討於複材結構計算領域屬於創新之研究內容，以往結構計算之外力給予並無考慮流場情
況，然本研究除進行流場壓力解析外，亦進行耦合收斂問題的處理，研究成果於學術領域頗有
價值；此外，研究內容亦提及葉片積層設計概念以提升風力機之發電效率，此研究成果若為產
業界所採納使用，應可獲得不錯之應用成果。 
    第三年度之研究著重於離岸型浮體風力機系統之運動解析，研究過程中除對浮體運動之旋
轉葉片上應變值進行數值模擬外，亦藉由實驗驗證之，過程中除累積不少實驗之寶貴經驗外，
亦整合電子控制領域研究能力建立一套無線應變量測系統，對於未來欲進行二維度運動與多維
度水槽中波浪受力試驗而言，已奠定不錯之研究基礎；此外該研究課題同時為離岸風電發展之
重要課題，整體系統運動特性之了解除有助於確保葉片安全性外，各項構件之選擇組併使用，
如 spar 型之錨鏈與浮台組裝運用，亦於其中進行可行性評估，故本研究於未來除了繼續深入探
討外，對於國內未來若欲發展離岸風力發電而言，將可為先期設置評估之參考依據。

Fluid-Structure Interactions of FRP Wind Turbine Blade
Under the Effect of Aerodynamic
Ya-Jung Lee1*, Yu-Ti Jhan1, Chao-Nan Wang1 and Cheng-Hsien Chung2
1 Engineering Science and Ocean Engineering, National Taiwan University, Taipei
2 R&D Department, United Ship Design & Development Center, Taipei
Abstract
Because structural analyses of FRP wind turbine blades must concern with aerodynamic and fluid-structure
coupling phenomena, some researchers have analogized the analysis of blades as a beam composed of several nodes in
order to account for aerodynamic and aeroelastic effects, but the calculation is not involved with the FEA of whole blade
model. Even though finite element analysis (FEA) is also used to measure whole blade model response in recent
literature, but loading condition is regarded as a concentrating force from aerodynamic calculation to avoid the effect of
aerodynamic pressure force on foil surfaces. Therefore, this develops an analytical method for calculating fluid-structure
interactions under the consideration of aerodynamic pressure effect when executing the FEA of wind turbine blade.
Improved understanding of actual blade loading response can improve blade design. In addition to evaluating turbine
power efficiency given the coupling effect, this work proposes improvements such as fiber orientation arrangement, to
enhance the power efficiency.
Keywords: Aerodynamic, fluid-structure interaction, fiber orientation.

1. Introduction
In recent years, many countries have experienced shortages of traditional energy sources or air pollution from energy
consumption. Therefore, every country continually legislates for various policies or laws to encourage and subsidize the
development of the sustainable green energy. Wind energy is a feasible alternative energy source that has well evaluation
about its power efficiency and cost-benefit by lots of countries. However, the dimensions of the wind turbine that
increase following with the power capacity raise rapidly, and the blade which is one important component of wind
turbine also grows from 750 kW turbine about 20 meters in radius to 2 MW one whose radius is approximately 40 meters.
The problems associated with fiber reinforced plastic (FRP) blades, such as structural strength, manufacturing and
transportation problems, occur spontaneously, and are more complex in larger wind turbines than in smaller ones.
The wind load on the blade surfaces not only produces the rotor motion but also causes the structure to bend out of the
rotating plane and even produces twist phenomenon in the foil sections. The twisted foil directly changes the fluid field
surrounding the blade, and the blade’s deformation condition would alter in consequence. This interactional
fluid-structure procedure is one phenomenon of aeroelastic affection, and many researches progressively investigate into
the blade’s bending-twist response under considering the aerodynamic load. Mezyk [1] considered the aerodynamic loads
by the iterative BEM method to solve the angle of attack and then executed the optimization work of blades. Lobitz [2]
and Locke [3] analyzed the bending-twist coupling effect by changing the fiber orientation in the spanwise direction to
the blade were reduced gradually along the spanwise direction from the root region to blade tip.
3. The implementation of aerodynamic loading to blade structure
3.1 Confirmation of turbine operating condition
As mentioned earlier, operating conditions of a wind turbine must be verified to gain the real and correct aerodynamic
force loaded on the blade surface when fluid flows through the blade section. Figure 3 shows some basic designing
factors relative to wind speed for the initial design of the 2MW wind turbine [7]. The data shows that the wind turbine
initially generates electricity as wind speed reaches 3m/s and starts its pitch control system when wind speed reaches
10m/s to control the rotor rotational speed, and the maximum control angle at wind speed 25m/s is about 27 degrees.
Accordingly, two operating conditions, the initial pitch control stage at 10m/s and maximum pitch angle at 25m/s, were
selected to calculate the aerodynamic load on the blade in order to assess blade performance at maximum wind speed
with and without pitch control strategy.

4.2 Fluid-structure interactional calculations
Figure 7 shows the procedure for calculating fluid-structure interactions in the wind turbine blade under aerodynamic
load. After confirming the blade geometry, the FEA model and data for composite laminate materials was constructed in
ABAQUS. The following steps from the selection of wind turbine operating condition to the calculation of regressive
pressure equations are about the deal with external force due to the aerodynamic effect that was stated previously. The
continuous steps are to execute the finite element analysis and to confirm the sections’convergence condition. If any
section does not converge, the calculations for aerodynamic load must be repeated. Finally, if the blade enters a stable
state, the FEA model’s elements would be checked the stress conditions to determine the failure situation, and the model
laminates should be reapportioned if any element achieves the failure criterion. The Tasi-Wu failure criterion was mainly
selected in this research to perform failure verification. Nevertheless, if all elements have no failure situation and then the
interactive analysis is finished.

5. Calculation results and evaluation of turbine power efficiency
5.1 Rotation angle results
Figures 8 show the fluid-structure interactional calculation results for changes in twist angle for each station given a
wind speed of 25 m/s. The horizontal axis is the interaction times, and the vertical one is the twist angle of the foil in each
station. The bending and rotational deformation near the root end about stations (5%~25%) is not obvious after the
interactional aerodynamic loading because of its high stiffness. However, the twist angle around the blade tip (95%)
reached 1.2 degrees from zero degree after three interactions, and it significantly influenced turbine power efficiency. The
bending deformation in the spanwise direction was not significantly related to blade length even at high operating wind
speed (25m/s) as Fig. 9 shows. The displacement of the tip in the 25m/s wind speed was 0.18 m in the first calculation
and swayed to 0.13 m after three times of interaction.

5.3.1 The pretwist design of blade geometry
The blade structure analysis should complete the first FEA involved with the aerodynamic pressure loading, and then
feedbacks the results of bending and torsion deformation to the blade performance and geometry designers who could
refer to these data and design the pretwist foil sections which provides better performance than the original one. However,
this method may require substantial interactions and the complex data pass. Otherwise, blade geometry may not provide
optimal power output during whole operating wind speed (3~25 m/s) of turbine, because the structure analysis was done
in one turbine operating and wind speed condition. Accordingly, blade design and analysis by this method requires
determining the typical wind speed range on the target location.
5.3.2 Correction of pitch angle control
This modification of pitch angle is relative to the turbine mechanical and electrical control. The second chart in Fig. 4
shows the original pitch angle control trajectory. Executing the fluid-structure interactions against each wind speed
derives the new modified pitch angle control trajectory directed to one whole blade. However, although this correction
improves original power output, some power efficiency is lost due to the elastic blade structure.
5.3.3 The adjustment of fiber orientation
The FRP blade is composed of fiber and matrix, and its extensional, bending and coupling stiffness depends on its fiber
orientation in accordance with Classical Laminated Plate Theory. Therefore, many researchers have attempted to enhance
turbine operating efficiency by using bending-coupling property of composite material [3] [4]. In the third improvement,
the fiber orientation in the spar cap region was changed to a 20 degree angle relative to the spanwise direction as
described in the literature. Figures 10 show the schematic figures. Table 2 shows the simulation results, and these data
were still derived after the fluid-structure interactional calculations mentioned previously. Change in the fiber orientation
to 20 degrees enabled the blade section to rotate nearly 0.8 degree versus the original designing blade and enhanced more
power about 11.5% than the original capacity 1.66MW. A notable consequence was that the different rotated direction of
fiber main-axis, positive or negative, caused the wind turbine to produce divergent operating power capacity.
