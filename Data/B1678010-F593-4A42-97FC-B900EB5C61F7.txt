摘要 
 
本研究以開發高效率太陽電池為目標，主要應用多重接面概念，結合一般單晶矽與異質材料多晶
SiGe 層，形成多接面單晶矽/Si1-xGex（x = 0 - 1）漸變異質結構型（Graded hetero-structure）太陽電池，
以突破單一接面單晶矽太陽電池的效率限制。本研究採用鋁誘發結晶法 (aluminum-induced 
crystallization, AIC)於低溫下成長多晶矽鍺薄膜於單晶矽基板上。實驗上，先將鋁及鍺薄膜蒸鍍於單晶
矽基板背面以形成 α-Ge/Al/sc-Si 結構，然後於 500°C 至 550°C 退火一段時間；X-射線繞射(XRD)顯示
於 550°C 退火 20 分鐘，非晶矽就會轉成多晶矽；由於 Ge/Al/sc-Si 結構中的鍺為固定的量，因此，多
晶 Si1-xGex 中的 x 值會隨著退火時間而增大。本研究將多晶 SiGe 層整合於太陽電池的背面，其光電轉
換效率為高可提昇至 19.1% (AM1.5G)，顯示了單晶矽太陽電池背面加入多晶 SiGe 層可使得轉換效率
增加約 7.3%。 
 
關鍵詞：多重接面、異質結構太陽電池、多晶矽鍺薄膜、單晶矽基板、鋁誘發結晶法。 
 
Abstract 
This work has aimed to develop high-efficiency solar cell technology primarily with the multi-junction 
concept. The conventional single-crystalline silicon cell was integrated with the poly-SiGe layer to form a 
sc-Si/Si1-xGex (x = 0 – 1) graded hetero-structure solar cell. In this work, the growth of poly-crystalline 
silicon-germanium (poly-SiGe) thin films on single-crystalline silicon (sc-Si) substrates by aluminum-induced 
crystallization (AIC) has been investigated at low temperatures. Experimentally, the aluminum and 
germanium films were evaporated onto the sc-Si substrates to form the α-Ge/Al/sc-Si structure and then 
annealed in the temperature range of 500°C to 550°C. The XRD results show that the initial transition from 
amorphous structure to polycrystalline structure occurs after 20 minute AIC annealing at a temperature of 
550°C. The fraction x of the poly-SixGe1-x is increased with the annealing time due to the limited Ge amount 
in the Ge/Al/sc-Si structure. A maximum photovoltaic conversion efficiency of 19.1% (AM1.5G) has been 
achieved in this work for the sc-Si solar cells with back-side p+-type poly-SiGe film. It shows that the 
conversion efficiency of the sc-Si solar cell can be increased by 7.3% with the addition of a p+-type poly-SiGe 
film on the back-side of the cell. 
 
Keywords: Multi-junction, hetero-structure solar cell, poly-SiGe, single-crystalline silicon substrates, 
aluminum-induced crystallization.

C. AES Analysis 
To understand the diffusion process during the layer exchange of the AIC process, Auger depth profiling 
of the AIC samples were examined as a function of the annealing time. Figure 6 shows the AES analysis of 
the Al/poly-SiGe/sc-Si structure after the AIC process at 550°C for 20 min, 90 min, 2hrs, and 3hrs, 
respectively. It shows that the fraction x of the poly-SixGe1-x increases with the annealing time due to the 
limited Ge amount in the Ge/Al/sc-Si structure. 
 
D. Cell Performance 
For the shallow junction structure, the junction depth of the solar cells has been controlled from 0.10 to 
0.30 μm by POCl3 diffusion at temperatures between 800 to 850 ℃. The surface concentration of the 
phosphorous impurity in the n+-type emitter of the solar cells was in the range of 4.5x1018 to 4.5x1019 cm-3 as 
measured by the spreading resistance probe (SRP) method. The conventional single-crystalline (sc) Si solar 
cells made in this work have shown a maximum conversion efficiency of 17.8% with Jsc of 41.25 mA/cm2, 
Voc of 0.60 V, and fill factor (FF) of 0.72 under AM1.5G solar illumination. As a comparison (Figure 7), the 
single-crystalline Si solar cells with back-side p+-type poly-SiGe film made in this work have achieved a 
maximum conversion efficiency of 19.1% with Jsc of 45 mA/cm2, Voc of 0.58 V, and fill factor (FF) of 0.73 
under AM1.5G solar illumination. It shows that the short-circuit current density of the cell has been increased 
by 9.1% with the back-side p+-type poly-SiGe film which enhances the long-wavelength absorption for the 
cell, although the open-circuit voltage decreases by 3.3%. As a consequence, the conversion efficiency of the 
sc-Si solar cell can be increased by 7.3% with the addition of a p+-type poly-SiGe film on the back-side of the 
cell. 
 
4. Conclusions 
In this work, poly-crystalline SiGe films with thickness over 1 μm have been successfully formed by the 
low-temperature AIC process at 500°C and 550°C for 20 min to 5 hrs. Characterizations of the specimens 
with EDS, XRD, and AES depth profiling have confirmed the formation of the poly-SiGe films. The 
poly-SiGe films formed by the low-temperature AIC process can be applied in the multi-junction solar cells to 
enhance the long-wavelength absorption. The hetero-structured solar cells with back-side p+-type poly-SiGe 
film made in this work have achieved a maximum conversion efficiency of 19.1% under AM1.5G solar 
illumination.
