中文摘要 
隨著都市快速發展與城市河川的整治，
污水處理廠每年產生之污泥量逐年增加，污
泥灰有效的再生利用係未來城市發展的重要
課題。國內大地工程不斷施作，而台灣地區
土域狹小，使用軟弱不良之地盤來開發利用
將是必然的趨勢，其中水泥是常用的土壤改
良穩定材料之一，而污泥經高溫焚化後產生
之灰渣與水泥具有相同的卜作嵐特性，故將
污泥灰應用於改良軟弱路基土壤是一項資源
有效再利用的方法。本研究採用A-4土壤來進
行穩定處理改良，其性質屬低壓縮性沉泥，
土樣分別混合污泥灰、污泥灰/水泥、污泥灰
/石灰，再添加Nano SiO2材料，其中污泥灰/
水泥與污泥灰/石灰比例為1：1、2：1、3：1、
4：1，經由土壤基本試驗及微結構觀察，以
求得改良軟弱路基土壤之工作性、強度、滲
透性及剪力強度的最佳配比。研究成果顯
示，污泥灰確實能改良軟弱路基土壤，但其
成效有一定的限度，水泥與石灰的添加都能
提升污泥灰的改良效果，且Nano SiO2的也可
提升污泥灰、污泥灰/水泥、污泥灰/石灰的
使用範圍與成效。 
關鍵詞： 奈米材料、土壤穩定處理、污泥灰  
Abstract  
With the quick development of city and the city 
river regulations, sewage sludge produced from 
wastewater treatment plants increases gradually 
year by year. How to effectively reuse sewage 
sludge ash becomes an important issue for the 
development of the future cities. On the other 
hand, with the limited land resource in Taiwan 
and the constant growing in geotechnical 
engineering applications, the development of 
soft ground becomes inevitable. Hence, cement 
is one of many stabilizers applied to improve 
the soft soil. Sewage sludge, after incinerated at 
high temperature, possesses the pozzolana as 
cement and can be applied to improve the soft 
subgrade soil. In this study, the soil sample 
collected is a cohesive soil and is characterized 
as A-4 (less-compressible clay) soil. The soil 
sample will mix with the incinerated sewage 
sludge ash, sludge/cement, and sludge/lime and 
with/without nano-SiO2 additive to carry out the 
stabilization treatment. The mix ratios for 
sludge/cement and sludge/lime are assigned at 
1/1, 2/1. 3/1, and 4/1. With the help of the basic 
soil tests and microscopic examinations, 
properties such as workability, strength, 
permeability, and shear strength of stabilized 
soft subgrade soil, as well as the optimum mix 
ratio of soil and ash would be obtained. Test 
results indicate that incinerated sewage sludge 
ash can improve properties of soft subgrade soil. 
However, the stabilization treatment by using 
sludge ash can only be achieved to certain 
extent. Yet, the additions of cement and lime to 
sludge ash can improve the results of the 
stabilization for soft soil. Furthermore, the 
addition of nano-SiO2 to sludge ash, 
sludge/cement, and sludge/lime can also help 
improve the stabilization for soft subgrade soil.  
 
Keywords: Nano-materials, soil stabilization 
treatment, sewage sludge ash

3.2.試驗材料 
本研究試驗所使用之材料包括南科高雄
園區內的土壤(A-4)、高雄市下水污泥灰
(Sludge)、品牌水泥(Cement)、奈米二氧化矽
(nano-SiO2)。其說明如下： 
1.污泥灰(Sludge)係採用高雄下水處理廠
的污泥經焚燒 800~850℃後所殘餘的灰渣，其
表面細圓無味，呈棕色；其基本試驗有 pH 值
試驗、比重、比表面積、毒性溶出試驗(TCLP)
和微觀試驗(SEM、EDX、XRD)。 
2.水泥係使用波特蘭第一型水泥，由於波
特蘭水泥水化作用，使水泥礦物組成中之金
屬元素(如 Al、Ca、Mg、Fe 之氧化物)與水中
之氧元素產共價鍵，而形成結晶結構，而此
結晶結構之共價鍵極易與矽元素，可產生高
價鍵結。水泥之成分除含少量之 MgO、
Na2O、K2O、SO3 外，主要為未化合之 CaO(即
游離石灰)、SiO2、Al2O3、Fe2O3 等四種，故
水泥可依上四種單礦物之多寡分成五種形式
(Mehta)。 
3.奈米二氧化矽(nano-SiO2)為白色粉末狀  
4.試驗用土壤乃為依 AASHTO 分類中之 A-4
型土壤，依規範試分類為低及中等塑性沉泥
黏土土壤。 
3.3 材料配比 
本試驗為了瞭解奈米氧化矽與污泥改良
土壤之情況，原土壤添加奈米氧化矽再分別
摻合污泥、污泥/水泥、污泥/石灰等後，進
行各項試驗。所有試體配合比係用重量百分
比計算。探討不同含量之摻合料對土壤之改
良。本研究土壤添加奈米氧化矽分別再以污
泥、污泥/水泥、污泥/石灰等摻合料對土壤
改良之成果，評估奈米氧化矽添加量與污泥
灰穩定處理之可行性。污泥、污泥/水泥、污
泥/石灰分別與 A-4土壤進行均勻拌和，再添
加奈米氧化矽含量為0%、1%、2%、3%，各摻
合料對A-4土壤重量比為0%、2%、4%、8%、16%。

4.3 pH 值試驗(pH Test) 
4.3.1 不同污泥灰/水泥比例 vs 奈米材料不
同添加量 
圖 7.為不同比例下之污泥灰/水泥添加
於土壤 A的 pH 值變化與養護齡期關係圖。圖
中顯示出，添加越多比例的水泥量，將會使
土壤 pH 值往上攀升，雖然各比例下的污泥灰
/水泥都會隨著養齡期的增加而下降，但各比
例的趨勢卻有明顯的不同，在拌合後靜置兩
小時其污泥灰/水泥為 1：1、2：1、3：1、4：
1的 pH 值分別為 12.20、12.15、11.96、
11.75，添加水泥量越多雖然會使 pH 值升高，
但上升的幅度卻有一定的界限存在。從養護
齡期來觀察，各污泥灰/水泥的比例都會隨著
齡期的增長而呈現下降的趨勢，其原因從 pH
值之鈣離子飽和原理與穩定處理機制發現，
鈣離子隨水化反應開始作用而逐漸被消耗，
初始土壤添加水泥為高鈣含量卻逐漸消耗鈣
離子成低鈣含量，造成土體的鈣離子飽和度
下降，因此 pH 值隨含鈣量減少而降低。污泥
灰/水泥為 4：1在齡期下的變化較為快速下
降，雖於養護齡期 7天時的趨勢較緩和，但
隨後 pH 值又有持續下降的行為發生，直到養
護齡期為 28 天時才漸漸穩定，而污泥灰/水
泥為 3：1下也有著相同的趨勢發生，但不同
的是其下降趨勢較污泥灰/水泥為 4：1為緩
慢，直到養護齡期 28 天 pH 值才有急速滑落
的情形產生，且兩者之間越來越相近，其可
能是因水泥含量增加，土體內的水分無法瞬
間提供水泥水化反應所需的水量，但隨著齡
期的增加水泥會漸漸的全部完成水化反應，
逐漸消耗鈣離子而使 pH 值降低，所以污泥灰
/水泥為 4：1與 3：1的最後差距並不大。 
污泥灰/水泥比例為1：1與2：1的情況下，pH
值雖會隨著齡期增加而下降，但變化趨勢卻
沒有污泥灰/水泥為4：1與3：1的快速與明
顯，這現象應該與土體內的水泥是否能完成
水化作用有關，土體內添加過量的水泥將造
成內部產生搶水行為，所以養護齡期達28天
時污泥灰/水泥為1：1、2：1、3：1、4：1的
pH值分別為11.82、11.32、10.36、10.02，
顯示出污泥灰/水泥為4：1與3：1較能使內部
的水泥完成水化作用，確實能將鈣離子消耗
而使pH值降低，污泥灰/水泥為1：1與2：1雖
然pH值也會隨齡期而下降，但其pH值卻無法

5.結論與建議 
5.1 結論  
1. 石灰比例含量越多並不會使土壤強度能
有效的向上攀升，在污泥灰/石灰比例為
3：1時會有較高的土壤強度出現，而Nano 
SiO2的添加對污泥灰/石灰的影響不大，
且Nano SiO2的含量太多還會使土壤強度
有負面的趨勢發生。  
2. 泥灰添加於土壤A能使其95%CBR值提升
至12~13之間，而依規範所訂定CBR≧8
為良好路基土壤，而且也比軟弱路基土
壤A之承載力高約4倍以上，再次印證了
污泥灰對土壤穩定處理的效能。  
3. 由土壤無圍壓縮強度試驗顯示，Nano 
SiO2添加至污泥灰中對於改良土壤確實
能顯現其成效，最佳添加量為1~2%之
間，過量添加並無明顯的提升，而污泥
灰/水泥與污泥灰/石灰也有此現象的發
生，故為達到成本利益，應取各配比之
最佳含量應用之。 
4. 土 壤 添 加 污 泥 灰 的 膨 脹 量 介 於
0.3~0.4%，比原始土壤減少50%之膨脹
量，由此可知污泥灰對土壤具有抑制膨
脹之作用。 
5. 石灰含量越多土壤的膨脹量越大，且石灰
與Nano SiO2之間的搶水現象會使土壤的
連結性變差，土壤的無圍抗壓強度將無
法有效提升，故如果要將汙泥灰/石灰運
用在軟弱路基上，除須考量石灰的膨脹
行為外，污泥灰、石灰、Nano SiO2間的
最佳含水量需進一步的設計。 
6. 由界達電位與PH值試驗得知，Nano SiO2
確實能藉由離子轉換之功效，取代擴散
雙層之氫鍵結力，造成雙層水厚度減
少，當雙層水厚度減低時，粘土顆粒因
為相互接近而造成彼此雙層水重疊產生
的排斥力因而降低，故容易形成所謂的
膠凝結構(flocculated)，使土壤透水係數
提升。 
7. 污泥灰添加於土壤約可使透水係數提升
8~10倍，顯示出污泥灰確實可改善土壤
透水係數的效能，而污泥灰/石灰也具有
相同的能力，但污泥灰/水泥在齡期7天的
透水係數卻已較原始土壤低，故得知水
泥將導致土體不具透水性。 
8. 污泥灰/水泥各比例的95%CBR值都相當
良好，其污泥灰/水泥比例3：1、4：1可
提升土壤20~30倍的95%CBR值，而比例
2：1的95%CBR已相當接近高承載基層與
底層碎石級配料之程度，比例1：1的
95%CBR值甚至超越了碎石級配料之等
級，由此可顯示出污泥灰/水泥對提升土
壤強度行極為顯著。 
9. 由SEM、XRD微觀結構可以得知，添加
Nano SiO2確實可以加速氫氧化鈣與

Abstract The pozzolanic characteristics of a sludge incinerated into ash were determined in this study. Lime is
commonly used as a stabilizer for the treatment of soils, while sewage sludge ash (SSA) is often applied with lime to
improve soft subgrade soil. In this study, a cohesive soil categorized as A-4 (low-plasticity clay) by AASHTO
classifications was mixed with SSA/lime in a 3:1 ratio, and nano-SiO2 was added to the soil. To determine changes in
workability, strength, permeability, and shear strength of the soft subgrade soil, basic soil tests were conducted and the
microstructure of the treated soil was analyzed. The results indicate that SSA/lime mixtures improve the properties of
soft subgrade soil and transform the soil from “poor subgrade soil” to “good to excelent subgrade soil” with a CBR＞8.
Moreover, the addition of nano-SiO2 improves the efficiency of SSA/lime treatments on soft subgrade soil. However, the
swelling behavior of the treated soil was affected by the addition of nano-SiO2 and lime. Thus, soil swelling should be
considered before lime and nano-SiO2 are applied to soft subgrade soil.
Keywords: Lime; sewage sludge; soft subgrade soil.
INTRODUCTION
Sewage systems are a fundamental infrastructure of Taiwan, and the amount of waste water treated by these
systems has increased rapidly. The sludge produced by sewage systems requires further treatment before it can
be released into the environment. Dewatered sludge is often dumped into the ocean or landfills; however, this
is not a suitable solution for an island that possesses limited land resources such as Taiwan. To reduce the
effects of dewatered sludge on the environment, the Taiwanese government has focused on the reduction and
reutilization of sludge. For instance, the incineration of dewatered sewage sludge into ash is a common
alternative to sludge treatment. After incineration at high temperatures, moisture and organic matter are
removed from the sludge. The ash generated by incineration possesses pozzolanic characteristics and can be
used as cement in engineering applications or as a treatment for soft subgrade soil. Thus, incineration
transforms sewage sludge from waste into a useful construction material.
Nanomaterials are synthesized from fine nanoparticles, which range in size from 1 to 100nm. Due to their
small particle size, unique characteristics such as optical properties, magnetism, thermal conductivity, and
chemical activity have been observed in nanomaterials. In this study, nano-SiO2 and sewage sludge ash were
added to soft subgrade soil and low-plasticity clay soils with low engineering potential to enhance their
strength and improve soil characteristics associated with satisfactory road conditions.

Soil Properties
Results obtained from the Atterberg limit test indicated that the liquid limit (LL), plastic limit (PL), plastic
index (PI), specific gravity, optimal moisture content (OMC), and unit weight of soil A (untreated soil) were
25.6-26.3%, 16.3-16.8%, 8.8-10.0, 2.72g/cm3, 13.5-13.8%, and 17.0-17.5kN/m3, respectively. The unconfined
compressive strength of the OMC was between 29.5 and 32.5kPa, which indicates that the bearing capacity of

In this study, soil classifications followed the standards specified by the American Association of State
Highway Transportation Officials (AASHTO) and the Unified Soil Classification System (USCS). As shown
in Figure 1, untreated soil was categorized as an A-4 soil with low-plasticity clay by AASHTO and as CL

hydrometer analysis. The results indicated that the untreated soil contained 95% silt and clay, where 50% to

untreated soil was not suitable for subgrade materials and required improvement before use in engineering

Compaction Test
The optimal moisture content (OMC) of untreated soil mixed with SSA/lime at an optimal dry unit
weight was obtained from compaction tests. Subsequently, the moisture content of the soil was
controlled to determine the required compactness and in-situ unit weight. Figure 2 shows the results

improved the workability of the soil; thus, the arrangement of wet soil particles was altered by the
addition of SSA/lime. However, further observation is necessary to determine the effects of particle
rearrangement on the strength of soil. The results shown in Figure 2 also indicate that the dry unit
weight of the soil treated with SSA/lime was less than that of untreated soil. This disparity may
have been caused by differences in the specific gravity of SSA, lime, and soil, which are 2.13, 2.2,
and 2.72, respectively.

Figure 5 shows the relationship between unconfined compressive strength (UCS) and the curing

The results in Figure 5 indicate that the nano-SiO2 improved the UCS of soil treated with SSA/lime
at early stages in the curing process. Furthermore, an increase in the UCS was observed with an
increase in the concentration of nano-SiO2. However, the effect of the nano-SiO2 concentration
became less pronounced at later stages in the curing process. For instance, after 91 days, the
strength of soil treated with SSA/lime and 0%, 1%, 2%, and 3% nano-SiO2 were 210.7, 213.7,
223.2, and 221.7kPa, respectively. Compared to treated soil without nano-SiO2, the strength of soil
treated with SSA/lime and 1%, 2%, and 3% nano-SiO2 was 1.4%, 5.9%, and 5.2% higher,
respectively. The results indicate that the addition of nano-SiO2 to soil treated with SSA/lime can
significantly improve the UCS; however, changes in UCS were not positively correlated to the
amount of nano-SiO2 added to the soil. Thus, to achieve the greatest effect of nano-SiO2 at the
lowest cost, an optimal amount of additive should be further determined.
Indoor Permeability Test
The hydraulic conductivity of soil treated with SSA/lime and various amounts of nano-SiO2 at
different stages in the curing process is shown in Figure 6. The results indicate that the application
of SSA/lime effectively improved the hydraulic conductivity (K, ×10-7cm/s) of the soil. At early
stages in the curing process, the value of K for soil treated with SSA/lime was 79.3, which is 14.4
times greater than the K of untreated soil. As the curing time increased, the value of K gradually
decreased. After more than 28 days, differences between the hydraulic conductivity of treated and
untreated soil became smaller, which was a result of hydration reactions between SSA and lime.
Specifically, products such as calcium hydroxide and C-S-H gel generated by hydration filled the
pores of the soil, causing a gradual decrease in hydraulic conductivity over time. The K of soil
treated with SSA/lime for more than 28 days was approximately 46.3, which is 7.2 times greater
than that of untreated soil, indicating that soil treated with SSA/lime becomes permeable after long
curing times. Thus, SSA and lime are excellent additives for improving soil permeability.
As shown in Figure 6, the value of K increased with an increase in the concentration of nano-SiO2.
Thus, the addition of nano-SiO2 improved the permeability of soil treated with SSA/lime.
Furthermore, an increase in the concentration of nano-SiO2 had a larger effect at longer curing times.
As stated above, calcium hydroxide and C-S-H gel generated during hydration fill the pores of soil;
however, nano-SiO2 decreases the thickness of the double layer and improves soil permeability.
Thus, the value of K increased with an increase in the concentration of nano-SiO2.
