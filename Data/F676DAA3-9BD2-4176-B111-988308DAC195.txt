中文摘要 
此兩年期計劃的目標是以奈米碳管來製造太陽能電池與其相關元件，並研發符合企
業需求且成本低廉的製造方式。為了解決太陽能電池效率不彰和費用昂貴的兩大問
題，本研究將著重於能量採集結構的探討。本研究將以電漿輔助式化學氣相沈積
(PECVD)來培育養成垂直向上多壁層與單壁層奈米碳管，並將這些碳管作為電極，
再以交疊型的半導化單壁層奈米碳管來製造透明的太陽能電池。這些方式將會大量
改善電極能源收集 的能力、並增加太陽能電池的效能。當垂直向上奈米碳管的純
化完成後，將可使用並作為太陽能電池的底層電極。接著，將在這層奈米碳管電極
上濺鍍出和非晶矽薄膜，這些濺渡出來的太陽能材料因為製程簡單與高效能，所以
很適合來研究奈米碳管的太陽能電池

other kinds of nanowires. Similar attempt has also been recently done by a group in 
Georgia Tech Research, in which case MWNTs forests were used as scaffold to support 
cadmium telluride heterojunction14. To contrast, our parallel study on amorphous silicon 
(a-Si:H) solar cell has achieved a more conformal coating to the MWNTs contacts, and 
individual coaxial photovoltaic devices in nanoscale. 
 
Our solar cell device fabrication process is described as follow. A Tungsten layer (80nm) 
was first sputtered onto a Si/SiO2 insulating substrate as part of the device bottom contact. 
Nickel, used as catalyst to grow MWNTs, was later deposited onto the tungsten, and 
patterned lithographically using lift-off process. Electron-beam lithography was used here 
to define the catalyst area and generate arrays patterns. The patterns were designed in a 
way that only a single standing carbon nanotube was grown on each catalyst site15-17. 
Vertically-aligned MWNTs were grown using DC plasma enhanced chemical vapor 
deposition technique (PECVD). The tungsten layer, together with those grown MWNTs, 
was served as a whole back electrode in the device. To continue the solar cell fabrication 
process, n-type (~25nm) and intrinsic a-Si:H layers (~500nm) were sequentially
