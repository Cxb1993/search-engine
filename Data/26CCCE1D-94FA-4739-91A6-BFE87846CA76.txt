二、英文摘要 
During the human life increasing year by year, early diagnosis, treatment and 
the immediate monitoring of diseases have become the main stream of medical 
service development through a high specificity, high sensitivity and also real time bio- 
sensor.  Binding a little charged biological macromolecules at the nanowire surface 
(for examples, nanowires, nanotubes and many nanomaterials), they could sensitively 
respond to change in the electrical potential of nanowire FETs.  The study of Si 
nanowire FETs has attracted highly attention on domestic and foreign research teams 
and the industrial company especially on CMOS device entered More Than Moore’s 
age because of their great potential application as label-free, real time and 
ultrasensitivity biological and chemical sensor.  The sensitivity of silicon nanowire 
becomes more obvious when size of nanowire is less than 120nm in past experiment.  
In general, the size and number of silicon nanowire could improve the sensitivity of 
bio-sensor. Unfortunately, it is difficult to study the behavior of silicon nanowires 
with sub-15nm size and aggressive line pitch for current lithography process (electron 
beam or nanoimprint, etc). 
Recently, I have developed that electron-beam assisted chemical reaction 
could deposit pattern-transferred hard mask on substrate surface directly, then to form 
the result pattern of nanowires, nanogaps and nanodots for subsequent etch on the 
unmasked substrate.  The technique is named as Electron Beam Nano Injection 
Lithography (EBNIL).  This EBNIL technique is with maskless and photoresist-free 
characteristics.  The patterning size of nanowires and nanogaps could be scaled 
down less than 10nm through reducing the electron beam diameter only.  Record 
area size of 0.039 um2 for a functional 6T-SRAM cell has been successfully achieved 
with a novel EBNIL technique (2009, IEDM). These electrical characteristics of 
小(具 5nm 成像潛力)，而線寬解析度的進一步微縮僅需要調整電子束直徑大小
便可達成，成功突破目前黃光技術受到光罩/光阻/光波長對解析度的限制。由於
奈米線場效電晶體利用少量帶電荷的生物分子結合在奈米線表面時就可造成元
件電流的強烈變化，所對應到是感測靈敏度與奈米線幾何形狀的關係，這是需要
去研究特別是當奈米線越小時(表面積/體積 ratio接近時)，理論上應有較大變
化或有特別的奈米現象，進而可能建立元件的 SPICE model，有機會來創造產業

四、研究方法及成果 
The application of nanowire and nanogap devices has demonstrated for 
ultrasensitive chemical, biological, and photovoltaic sensors1-9.  Silicon nanowire 
(SiNW) FETs have gotten consideration attention for their great potential application 
as label-free, real time and ultrasensitive biology research and genetic screening1-7.  
The high surface-to-volume and nanoscale dimension of silicon nanowires could 
response sensitively to change in the local electric potential as well as ultrasensitive 
sensors.  Similarly, the nanogaps (typically with less than 100 nm) between two 
fixed microelectrodes had been demonstrated on the electrical detection of biological 
interaction between polypeptides based on conductance variation8-9.   
In general, bottom–up and top-down approaches are pursued to fabricate the 
nanowire or nanogap devices.  Based on the metal-catalyzed chemical vapor 
deposition10-11 and vapor-liquid-solid growth1,12 of “bottom-up” technique, the 
reliable and reproducible of SiNW is still present challenge on electronic circuit 
integration.  On the top-down technique, the nanowire or nanogap devices could 
have been fabricated on several lithography approaches based on conventional 
on the pattern of hard mask.  Another advantage of this technique is to trim this 
dimension of hard mask further during reactive ion etching process, and to form 
smaller size of nanowires and nanodots than that of before this etching.  Finally, the 
remains of hard mask could be removed by wet or dry etching process. The main 
steps of this technique are schematically illustrated in Figure 1(b).  Compared to 
standard Si VLSI process, the technique could eliminate four process steps as resist 
coating, exposure, resist developing and resist stripping except with similar hard mask 
deposition.   
Used pattern-transferred hard mask approach, Figure 2a and Figure 2b are the 
cross-sectional TEM of metal (TiN) nanowire and the SEM image of silicon 
nanowires, respectively.  During hard mask deposition, a nominal electron beam has 
4.6nm diameter with a current of approximately 100pA.  The gas precursor is 
platinum metal organic ((CH3)3CH3C5H4Pt), and the typical pressures were in the 
range of 3x10-6 torr.  On high resolution mode, high aspect ratio platinum line/island 
for pattern-transferred hard mask has been fabricated well based on optimizing gas 
flux control, e-beam energy and diameter selection.  The result pattern of TiN 
nanowire was to use reactive ion etching with the chamber pressure of 8 x10-3 torr and 
Cl2 / BCl3/CHF3 reaction gas, but the silicon nanowires/nanodots/nanogaps could be 
fabricated on the same chamber pressure and HBr reaction gas.  On TEM image of 
Figure 2a, the composition of hard mask is not pure platinum materials.  It is useful 
to understand the composition of hard mask for removal of hard mask.  The EDS 
result of platinum-based hard mask is shown in Figure 2c.  The atomic percentage 
analysis of platinum-based hard mask materials is included about 15% platinum and 
73% carbon as well as metal-based organic photoresist, that is, it is compatible with 
the photoresist stripping of standard Si CMOS process.  A reactive ion etching with 
O2 plasma is to remove this platinum-based hard mask.  After removal of hard mask, 
high Ion/Ioff ratio.  The IDS-VGS characteristics of NWFETs fabricated by presented 
lithography with 14nm/18nm (W/LG) are shown in Figure 3c.  The “off” current of 
NWFETs is demonstrated on 0.1 pA (linear region, VDS = 50 mV) and 10 pA 
(saturation region, VDS = 3V), respectively.  Furthermore, it is also found that the 
on/off current ratio of NWFETs is more than 4 orders of magnitude on both linear 
region and saturation region of NWFETs operation.  Thus we find the consistent 
result and agreement between theoretical simulation (3D quantum mechanical) and 
experiment result form conventional lithography to this pattern-transferred hard mask 
technique. 
The binding of these gold nanoparticles bridges this gap and leads to readily 
measurable conductivity changes.  The antibodies were labeled by gold particles 
with an average diameter of 25 nm9.  Figure 4a shows that the critical dimension of 
nanogap capability by platinum-based hard mask. The nanogaps could be easily 
patterned to 10 nm at dense line and preferred location without the restriction of mask, 
photoresist and exposure proximity effect.  The silicon nanogaps with 18 nm have 
been fabricated on 40 nm pitch size of dense lines as shown in Figure 4b.  The line 
pitch could be scale down if thinned the thickness of platinum-based hard mask and 
optimized the selectivity between hard mask and substrate.  It is to mean that the 
minimum nanogaps could be to fill only any binding gold nanoparticle for bridging 
this micro-electrode. The technique provides a reproducible and reliable pattern 
performance of biosensor fabrication.
