行政院國家科學委員會專題研究計畫成果報告 
奇異點與不均勻厚度塗層圓形異質的熱彈性交互作用 
Thermoelastic interaction between a point singularity and a 
nonuniformly coated circular inclusion  
計畫編號：NSC 97-2221-E-252-006 
執行期限：97年 8月 1日至 98年 7月 31日 
主持人：陳富謀   南開科技大學機械工程系 
計畫參與人員：兼任助理  黃衍榕 
 
摘要 
本研究旨在探討不均勻厚度覆層圓形異質與點熱源的熱彈性交互作用。利用保角映射並結
合解析連續與交替技巧，可分別求得位於圓形異質，中間層材料與基質之溫度場與應力場級
數解。數值分析並探討材料組合對熱應力的影響，發現中間質材料之熱模數對界面應力有絕
對性的影響，適當調整中間質材料與相鄰材料之熱模數比可顯著降低複合材料之界面應力。
此外，本計劃所推導之解析解可用來模擬焊接製程及作為研究相關熱彈性異質或裂縫問題之
格林函數。 
 
關鍵詞：不均勻厚度覆層圓形異質；點熱源; 保角映射；解析連續 
 
Abstract 
Within the framework of the linear theory of thermoelasticity, the interaction between a point 
heat source and a nonuniformly coated circular inclusion is solved in this paper. Based on the 
technique of conformal mapping and the method of analytical continuation in conjunction with the 
alternating technique, the solutions to heat conduction and thermoelasticity problems are derived 
explicitly. A rapid convergent series solution for both the temperature and stress functions, which is 
expressed in terms of an explicit general term of the complex potential of the corresponding 
homogeneous problem, is obtained in an elegant form. Numerical results are provided for some 
particular examples to investigate the effect of material combinations on the interfacial stresses.  
 
Key words: Nonuniformly coated circular inclusion, point heat source, conformal mapping, 
analytical continuation

計畫名稱：奇異點與不均勻厚度覆層圓形異質的熱彈性交互作用 
計畫主持人：陳富謀 
計畫編號：NSC 97-2221-E-252-006 學門領域：應力與變形 
技術/創作名稱 奇異點與不均勻厚度覆層圓形異質的熱彈性交互作用 
發明人/創作人 陳富謀 
中文： 
本研究旨在探討不均勻厚度覆層圓形異質與點熱源的熱彈性交互
作用。利用保角映射並結合解析連續與交替技巧，可分別求得位於
圓形異質，中間層材料與基質之溫度場與應力場級數解。數值分析
並探討材料組合對熱應力的影響，發現中間質材料之熱模數對界面
應力有絕對性的影響，適當調整中間質材料與相鄰材料之熱模數比
可顯著降低複合材料之界面應力。此外，本研究所推導之解析解可
用來模擬焊接製程及作為研究相關熱彈性異質或裂縫問題之格林
函數。 
技術說明 
英文： 
Within the framework of the linear theory of thermoelasticity, the 
interaction between a point heat source and a nonuniformly coated 
circular inclusion is solved in this paper. Based on the technique of 
conformal mapping and the method of analytical continuation in 
conjunction with the alternating technique, the solutions to heat 
conduction and thermoelasticity problems are derived explicitly. A 
rapid convergent series solution for both the temperature and stress 
functions, which is expressed in terms of an explicit general term of the 
complex potential of the corresponding homogeneous problem, is 
obtained in an elegant form. Numerical results are provided for some 
particular examples to investigate the effect of material combinations 
on the interfacial stresses. Besides, the general solutions developed 
herein for heat sources can be used in engineering to model thermal 
processes in welding and in the boundary element method as Green’s 
functions for thermal problem involving inclusions and cracks.
