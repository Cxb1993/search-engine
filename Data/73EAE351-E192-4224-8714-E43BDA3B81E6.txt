雖然這種對抗或逃逸之反應以往是獵人求
生存之道，但是目前卻已較不適用於面對現代
人的壓力了。原因之一是：雖然當我們面對壓
力時，身體已準備好基本的反應行動（對抗或
逃逸），但是我們卻常處於一種無法釋放這種身
體反應能力的狀態。原因二：甚至當我們所面
臨的壓力都已解除，我們對於這些壓力源的對
抗或逃逸之反應卻仍持續地進行中，此種高度
的激發狀態便對健康產生負面的影響。更甚
者，這些本能的壓力反應也常僅在我們的預期
狀況下隨即作用，根本不須真正地面臨實際壓
力之存在。這些因素正說明現代人面對壓力的
諸多變數，非常不同於古老人類單純、本能地
反應以求得生存而已。
壓力除了意指個人之心理感受，如焦慮、
緊張、疲憊外，亦包括環境下之噪音、振動、
熱、燈光、有毒物質和重力加速度。這些壓力
源可透過個體主觀的經驗、生理的改變和表現
的折損而呈現出來。環境因素的壓力常展現在
各類工作中，個體的生理工作負荷和其工作表
現之關係。而心理層面的壓力則常與個體認知
歷程的改變有關，如注意力下滑、精神不集中。
因此為降低不良壓力之影響，往往必須透過問
題解決方案之設計、善用策略和人員訓練，以
保持或提升人員的工作表現。
壓力的負面影響
綜合而言，壓力可能導致之結果可類分為
五個層面(程千芳,2001)：
1. 生理影響：包括對於突發未預期之事件經由
交感神經系統的運作而產生之短期的改變，如
口乾、流汗、心跳加速和呼吸困難等。Selye
（1956, 1976, 1985）將之界定為一般適應症
候群（general adaptation syndrome, GAS）。
從最先的警覺反應，至第二階段的抵抗期，身
體嘗試著去適應這個壓力源。在此階段，可能
會出現一些疾病或健康問題。最後階段則為衰
竭期，個體因長期或重覆發生之壓力而造成持
續性的生理激動狀態，而付出極高的代價。當
免疫系統及身體能量耗弱而再難以進行抵抗
時，即進入此一階段。若壓力仍持續，則生理
的傷害和適應性疾病之發生率更高，嚴重者即
造成死亡。
2. 威脅健康：與壓力有關之最普遍症狀通常與
腸胃系統有關，如反胃噁心、消化不良、腹瀉
和潰瘍。而某些特定的人格類型（特別是好競
爭者、常給自己設定較困難的工作目標者和常
會因失敗而感到挫折者）是較高的危險群。其
他與健康有關之症狀包括氣喘、頭痛、睡眠失
常、性功能失常、神經質、神經過敏、和易患
感冒等。
3. 行為失常：包括不安的反應、神經質的大笑、
發抖、衝動的行為、易激動和反應時間較長等。
壓力也會導致食慾上的改變和過度的飲酒和抽
煙。
4. 認知受損：壓力使人較無法意識集中、易健
忘、決策力差。
5. 情緒困擾：如焦慮、攻擊性強、憂慮、疲憊、
冷漠的、情緒化的、緊張和不安等。

台灣民航飛行員壓力與因應的探討
台灣自 1988 年天空開放之後，部份的航空
公司即聘雇外國機師至國內工作，屆時國內部
份的航空公司即處於多元文化之工作操作環
境。不同國籍機師在文化的交會下，對於其壓
力問題與因應需求或策略的差異，直接或間接
地影響渠等之工作表現。有關不同國籍飛行員
的特性探討首推 Helmreich(1984)比較本國飛
行員與美國飛行員在座艙管理態度量表
（ Cockpit Management Attitudes
Questionnaire, CMAQ）上之反應，結果顯示本
國飛行員並未“表現”出與其他國家相同典型
之態度結構。雷同地，陸鵬舉等人(2001)之研
究結果亦顯示：國內民航駕駛員若依國家文化
進行分類，則渠等於 CRM 觀念面、投入面和執
行面上之反應，大多達統計上之顯著差異水
準。景鴻鑫等人(2001)認為由國家文化之差
異，可反應出國人的「座艙中之秩序情結」。他
們認為國人因有懼亂情結而表現出強調上下關
係之表面和諧的現象。景鴻鑫等人(Jing, et
al., 2001)則指出：中國社會的權威性格與高
的失事率間有高的相關。至此，不論國內或國
外有關國籍比較之研究結果皆反應出：個體座
艙上之行為表現，係受到國家文化等因素之影
響，而有不同的態度或行為反應。
不同的國籍機師在各類國家文化的交會
下，是否持有不同的組織文化認知和座艙操作
態度？程千芳(Cherng, 2004; 程千芳和何立
己,2005)將國內之民航機師依其國籍區分成本
國機師和外國機師，則兩組對於其公司組織文
化和座艙操作態度之認知不盡相同：外國機師
體驗到較多「公司重人情關係」、也對公司認同
較高，在飛行員的專業座艙操作態度上則較強
調標準程序的確實執行，也較願意求援；相對
地，本國機師則感受到較強之「公司講究形式
表面」，而在飛行專業座艙態度上則較強調應避
免衝突，也較同意在執行飛行任務後的解說有
助於組員間之協調合作；對於緊急狀況下，認
定自己能快速形成解決方案並下判斷；而對於
緊急狀況下表現不好者，則較會歸因於經驗不
足，也較強調應避免於操作環境下談論不相干
的話題。此等結果反應出：本國籍機師在專業
態度上較重和諧，對於自我因應緊急狀況能力
也有較高的自信，對於公司文化反應出較強「形
式表面」的負面態度；相對地，外國籍機師則
較能以公司為傲，在專業態度上則較強調程序
執行和不恥下問。

演講。他以英國的經驗，強調早期處理員工的心理健康問題，可以降低經濟上的損
失，以歐洲為例，每年直接消耗於治療憂鬱症病患的經費約 440億歐元，若含間接

轉換工作所造成的損失，則每年約消耗 259億英鎊，因此提升人員的心理健康，勢
在必行。
現今世界各國已紛紛制訂政策來解決職場上人員的各類問題，包括工作負荷，

Can civil pilots’ personality traits predict their stress, coping types, and job 
performance?
Chian-Fang G. Cherng, Ph.D.
Department of Health Psychology, Chang Jung Christian University, Taiwan, ROC.
e-mail: anna94@mail.cjcu.edu.tw
Introduction
Pilots’ work is categorized as one of the high risk job, and is expected to be sensitive to
stress. It was of importance to understand civil pilots’ baseline stress level, sources of stress, 
as well as their reactions and habitual coping behaviors. For civil pilots, stress may arise from
environmental stressors, domestic stress and family relationships, work stress including role
ambiguity and relationships with colleagues or managers, and life stress.
Kobasa, et al.,(1982) defined a hardy personality which is related to the stress-free or
stress-resisted. As shown in Kobasa, there are three components of the hardy personality, i.e.,
control, commitment, and challenge. As such, I’d like to examine the personality traits in
relation to stress and coping behavior.
The reaction under stress could be including: poor concentration, feel boredom, slowed
reaction, anxiety, depressed, irritated, fatigue, frustrated, and physical symptom. In Aldwin &
Revenson (1987)’s definition, coping encompasses cognitive and behavioral strategies used to 
manage a stressful situation and the attendant negative emotions. Havlovic & Keenan (1995),
in this regard, classified coping behaviors into 5 types: positive thinking, direct action,
help-seeking, avoidance/resignation, and alcohol use. A number of variables, including
personality, job tenure, and managerial experience, all have been found to associate with
specific coping types.
Green et al. referred stress to be a person’s evaluation downgrading his/her own ability 
to complete the perceived demand (1996). As such, stress is rather than objective but
subjective, and is individual-vulnerable. In Taiwan, a few civil airline companies have hired
foreign pilots since 1988. Did Taiwanese pilots and foreign pilots perceive different stress and
show different copying behaviors? Have they the same pilot personality? Can their stresses
and personality predict their coping behaviors and flight safety or flight performance? The
current study was to explore the constellation of civilian pilots’stress, coping strategies and
their flight performance in terms of their national culture and their personality traits.
Materials and Methods
Participants
Seven hundred and fifty civil pilots from 7 airline companies in Taiwan participated in this
study. However 29 of them (3.9%) were excluded due to missing data. Most of them are males
(99.2%), over 45 years of age (56.5%), work over 10 years in their current companies
(59.4%), 7000 flight hours and above (68%), and Taiwanese (82.7%).
Questionnaire
A researcher-made questionnaire, using a 5-point scale, consisting of 101 close-end items
related to work stress, reactions under stress, present work, personality traits, and stress
coping behaviors, was used in this study. The participants’ job performance and their 
experience of involving flight accidence or event were also evaluated.

they in a stress condition. This result implied that it is of importance for pilots to handle their
stress for safety reason.

Discussion
The current results have shown that the two groups of civil pilots in Taiwan had
different personality traits, stressor and habitually coping types. It is of importance to make
sure that pilots’ perceptions on their work situations, such as operation rules, work climate, 
and promotion are suitable to their flight performance as well as personal needs in order to
reducing the human errors and guarantee for flight safety. With respect to their different
stressors and coping types, designing suitable intervention training programs to increase the
use of adaptive or effective coping were suggested. For example, using the bio-feedback
system to assist Taiwanese civil pilots to reduce their reactions when they are stressful. It is
not only important for individual needs, but also is good for establishing well organizational
culture.
