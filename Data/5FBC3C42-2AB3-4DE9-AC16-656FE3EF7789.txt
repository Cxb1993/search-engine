(一)報告內容 
一、前言 
微電子封裝工業目前大量的以
錫球銲接的方式，將電子之件晶片
連 接 到 印 刷 電 路 板 上 ( Printed 
Circuit Board, PCB )。這些銲點組裝
後的性質直接會影響封裝後整體成
品的良率。介金屬的各種性質 (如導
電性、導熱性、機械性質、熱膨脹
係數 )也直接影響了銲點之可靠度，
因此了解介金屬化合物的各種基本
性質在封裝銲點的的影響是非常迫
切且重要的。再加上通過銲點之電
流密度將高達 104 A/cm2。電遷移效
應已會對銲點的可靠度造成影響，
我們認為要分析在銅墊層的溶解及
消耗的行為，必須透過界面的介金
屬化合物，化合物之電遷移性質將
直接影響銲點界面之電遷移現象。
綜合過去電遷移的研究，介金屬化
合物的電遷移性質都被忽略了，因
此了解介金屬化合物電遷移的研究
也是迫切且重要的。  
此 計 畫 目 標 為 成 長 介 金 屬 塊
材，並且建立測量介金屬化合物基
本性質的設備及方法，針對介金屬
本身抗電遷移能力以及與銲料的濕
論潤性做通盤的研究，並進行電遷
移效應對於銲料與介金屬界面反應
的研究。  
本計畫在一年內年，預期之目標
雖未能完全如計畫開發，但所開發
出研究成果也相當豐碩。自本計畫
開始執行以來，已有一篇論文於國
外期刊發表。

隨著電子產品輕、薄、短、小

四、研究方法 
錫球製備 
用電子天平秤取0.00015g重的銲錫，
並將錫塊放在塗滿助銲劑的蓋玻片上，並
放在加熱器上加熱250℃，形成錫球後浸泡
於酒精中，放置超音波震盪器，將錫球上
的助銲劑清洗乾淨。 
鎳及銅鎳 bi-layer通電試片製備 
本實驗所使用的銅片，由銅棒
（Aldrich, Cu rod,6.35 mm diam.,99.999%）
經鑽石刀切割而成為0.1 mm 厚，拋光至
無刮痕後，在鎳的基材上，放置助銲劑以
及錫球，加熱至250 15℃ 秒，使錫球與鎳片
連接，如圖六(a)，冷卻後，以少量酒精清
洗多餘的助銲劑。Cu/Ni bi-layer金屬試
片，是取厚度25µm之銅箔經過酒精清潔
後，用蒸鍍的方式鍍上鎳金屬，接合方式
如同上述如圖六(b) 。 
電遷移通電裝置 
將鎳通電片，置放於分別為160℃、180
℃及200℃的恆溫板加熱上，以10g銅棒當
作電極與錫球接觸，並通以電流密度
2.5x103amp/cm2電流，如圖六(c)，電子流
由鎳材流出，通過錫球，流入銅棒電極。 
通電後，將試片冷埋於環氧樹酯中，
經拋光後，將試片進行酸蝕，使得介金屬
化合物與銲料接觸之界面能清晰，最後以
電子顯微鏡取像。

Introdution: 
In electronic packing, intermetallic compounds (IMC) play an important role for the reliability.  
The physical, electrical, and thermal properties of IMC would affect  
the performance of the whole device.  As we know, the most important intermetallic reaction in the 
interconnectects is the reaction of Cu and Sn.  In the flip-chip solder joint, Cu under-bump 
metallization (UBM) will react with Sn and form Cu-Sn compound seriously.  The growth of 
Cu-Sn compounds while the solder in the solid-state is of concern.  Because Cu-Sn compound is 
brittle, it becomes a significant fraction of the solder joints, it can act as a site for crack initiation 
and propagation when the joint is deformed.  As the functionality and speed increasing, the current 
density of chip interconnection crease dramatically.  Hence, the current density in each solder 
bump will substantially increase over 104 A/cm2. [1]   Kao reported that Cu pad under bump at 
cathode side would dissolved into SnPb solder due to EM (electromigration) force. [2]  Cu atoms 
which dissolved into solder would be moved to anode side and cause Cu-Sn compound grow more 
faster at in flip-chip solder joint.  Then, the properties of Cu-Sn compound will be more important 
in the interconnect solder joint.  So, we need high quality bulk Cu-Sn compound to measure them.  
However it does not have a technique to fabricate bulk Cu-Sn compound well.  We apply 
liquid-electromigration method to grow Cu-Sn compound.  Liquid-electromigration method is the 
method in which the electric current passing through the solution-substrate interface is the driving 
force for deposition of the epitaxial layer at a constant temperature.  Two main mechanisms for 
growth of liquid-electromigration method are EM and Peltier effect.  Powder metallurgy is 
generally used to grow IMC (intermetallic compound).  Unfortunately, it can not grow IMC very 
well.  Based on several advantages, we fabricate bulk Cu-Sn compounds by 
liquid-electromigration method.  First, composition uniformity of the growth process can be 
controlled better.  Second, the current can be seen as forced mixing.  Third, the source can 
constantly supply into the solution to grow very thick crystals.  Also, we can dissolve different 
species in the solution to grow a ternary compound.  Under different process temperatures, Cu3Sn 
and Cu6Sn5 compounds can be grown, respectively.

As an electric current flows through Cu/Sn/Cu sandwich sample, a temperature difference 
would occur between the two Cu/Sn interfaces.  The amount of Peltier heat (Q) depends on current 
(j) can be expressed as: [6] 
                        Q= SAB · T· j                                (3) 
,where T is the temperature,and SAB is the difference of thermoelectric power between A and B.  
The subscript AB denote that the current flow is from A toward B.  If SAB is positive, heating 
would be occurring at the A/B interface.  This is so-called Peltier effect. 
Mechanism of Liquid-electromigration method: 
Many researchers found that the solute Cu atoms migrated to the anode in the solid state test 
condition and Cu-Sn compounds formation is enhanced at the anode side under current stressing. 
[7-9]  That agrees with our experimental with the lower current density (40A/cm2).  Fig. 2 shows 
the optical images of current-stressed Cu/Sn/Cu sample at 400℃, under current density of 40 A/cm2 
for different times.  We can find that Cu atoms were gradually electomigrated to anode with 
current stressing time.  Cu-Sn compounds are only formed densely at anode side.  
Electromigration force is dominant under lower current density to grow Cu-Sn compounds in 
liquid-electromigration method.  It represents that electromigration causes Cu atoms migrate along 
with electron flow and form Cu-Sn compounds at anode side in liquid-electromigration method.  
This result is the same with EM tests in the solid state.  But we observed different result that 
believe that Peltier effect results the Cu-Sn compound form at the cathode side.   
So, how to calculate the quantity of Peltier effect becomes a very important issue.  
Unfortunately, there is no thermoelectric power data of molten Sn and no completed data of Cu and 
Sn so far.  Based on Table 1, we only can estimate our sample at certain temperature of solid state.  
For example, we stress 100A current under 300k, peltier heat at one interface would be 0.0849J/s 
calculated from equation 3.  If we only consider heat conduction, the quantities of peltier heat at 
two interfaces can cause 1.12 ℃ temperature difference between cold and hot side by Fourier First 
Law: 
                    Q = - k · A · dT/dx                                (4) 
where k is thermal conductivity of pure Sn, 60.7 W/m·k, A is the cross-sectional area ,and dT/dx is 
temperature gradient.  In order to know the temperature difference between the anode and the 
cathode interfaces directly, we use far infrared thermal couple to measure it under different 
annealing temperatures and current densities.  Table 2 shows that temperature difference is 
increasing with annealing temperature and current density.  From Eq. 3, we can know peliter heat 
is proportional to this two factors.  Also, another coefficient in Eq. 3, SAB, would be changed by 
temperature in Table 1.  Based on temperature difference data in Table 2, we plot the Fig. 5 which 
is temperature difference versus current density under different annealing temperatures 
(100~350℃).  We can find that temperature difference increases with current density linearly at all 
annealing temperatures.  And the temperature difference at liquid state is much larger than solid 
state, especially at higher annealing temperatures of 300 and 350℃.  It means that temperature 
difference is proportional with current density as Eq. 3 showed.  Fig. 6 is the plot of temperature 
difference versus annealing temperature under different current densities (20~400 A/cm2).  All 
curves are linear and get the same trend with increasing.  It means that SAB is really enhanced by 
annealing temperature, especially in liquid state.  Hence, Peltier effect in liquid-electromigration 
method is very serious and would overcome electromigration force.
