一、中文摘要
所謂抗指紋表面即是具有自清潔功能
(self-cleaning)或具雙疏效(hydro/oleo
phobic)的薄膜。而未來具有雙疏效果的觸
碰控式顯示器面板勢必成為市場上的主
流。本計畫即著眼於觸碰控式顯示面板對
於具抗指紋功能的需求，將藉由有機/無機
混層、奈米結構改質製備超雙疏薄膜，研
究如何獲得最佳材料與結構之最佳條件。
本計畫之超疏水之機制是遵循Cassie
model與Wenzel model結構模型為理論基
礎 進 行 研 究 。 計 畫 將 採 用 共 溶 劑
(cosolvent)法製做奈米微粒，溶劑將以乙
醇(anhydrous ethanol)、二甲基甲酰胺
(Dimethylformamide) 、 去 離 子 水
(deionized water)等進行共溶，研究溶劑
的極性程度對製作SiO2奈米微粒的影響。
而超雙疏薄膜將以溶膠-凝膠法製備有機/
無機混成前驅溶液，之後與奈米SiO2混
合，以旋轉塗布在玻璃基板上，利用控制
液體的表面張力與揮發速度來調整粒子間
的距離，達到自組裝之目的。有機/無機混
成前驅溶液將選擇具疏水特性之有機物與
TEOS及耦聯劑(copolymer)、醇類溶劑等進
行混合反應。
關鍵詞：超雙疏，水接觸角，溶膠-凝膠法，
SiO2奈米微粒，自組裝
1. Abstract
The so called anti-fingerprint films is based
on the function of self-cleaningor hydro/oleo
phobic characteristics of the films. It was
well known that the applications of the
hydro/oleo phobic films on the
touch-controlled flat panel become very
urgent. This program was subjected to use
sol-gel method to prepare the
organic/inorganic hybrid thin films with
nanostructure of hydro/oleo phobic
properties and find out the optimized
synthesizing conditions. The function of
the hydro/oleo phobic was based on the
theory of Cassie model and Wenzel model.
The cosolvent method for the crystallization
of nanoparticles was proposed in this
program. The solvents such as anhydrous
ethanol, dimethylformamide (DMF),
deionized water, etc were chosen to
co-dissolving the TEOS. The condition on
synthesizing the SiO2 nanoparticles will be
studied. Organic-inorganic precursors will
be prepared by sol-gel method and mixed
with the SiO2 nanoparticles. The hydro/oleo
phobic thin films will be obtained by
spraying the precursor on the glass
substrates and the assembled distance
between SiO2 nanoparticles will be adjusted

的空間。

Abstract 
 
In this study, we prepared the multi-refraction film thin by 
ink-jet technique with sol-gel precursor. The precursors 
were prepared by using some transition metal alk-oxide and 
the tetraethylorthosilicate (TEOS) mixed separately with n-
Butyl Alcohol and PVB (Poly(vinyl butyral)).The structure 
and morphology of the resulting films were investigated by 
atomic force microscope (AFM). It is shown that the shape 
of the pattern of the films would affect the refraction 
proportion. 
 
 
1. Introduction 
 
Reflexite Collimating Film (RCF) is an optical film used to 
condition the light output of transmissive backlit displays 
and allows the management of available light more 
effectively. RCF features precise prisms on one side and a 
micro-replicated, non-smooth surface on the other side. The 
benefits of using RCF include a brighter backlight module 
and increased battery life. And microlens can be use as the 
Reflexite Collimating Film. In later years, many fabrication 
ways have been explored to obtain refractive microlens 
arrays [1–5]. These methods include photolithographic 
methods [1] micro jet technique [2], ultraviolet curing of 
polymer [3], replica molding [4], and MEMS process [5] 
are all newly developed methods for the polymeric lenses.  
In this study, prepared the reflexite collimating film by with 
microlens and the precursors were prepared by sol-gel 
technique. And the precursors can also be used for the ink-
jet technology. It becomes much easy for the pattern design 
though the ink-jet technology.  
 
 
 
2. Experimental 
 
. The equipment used is a drop-inject printer (Dimatix 
Materials Printer, DMP) with 16 nozzles in 254 μm spacing 
(100dpi). The nominal single drop volume is about 10 pico 
liter. Fluid temperature is adjustable from ambient to 70ºC 
for controlled jetting. Single and array of droplets were 
deposited on the x-y driven glass substrates and glass 
substrates coated with red organic dye. The shape and the 
contact angle of the drops are investigated by CCD camera. 
An organic-inorganic hybrid precursor was obtained by 
hydrolysis of tetraethylorthosilicate (TEOS) and some 
transition metal alk-oxide. They ratio of the some transition 
metal alk-oxide/TEOS are from 5wt% to 40 wt%.  The 
precursors were prepared by mixing the materials with 50% 
volume fraction of and n-Butyl Alcohol: PVB (Poly (vinyl 
butyral)) = 20:1. The mixture was added 10 μl HCl per 
20ml of TEOS and aged for 12 hours. After deposition, the 
specimen was baked at 200℃ for one 1h to remove 
excessive solvent. The shape and surface roughness of 
lenses have been measured by atomic force microscopy 
(AFM, Digital Instrument, NS3a-D3100).

2.3 Synthesis of amphiphobic thin films by      
organic-inorganic hybrid method 
 
In this work, the thin films are obtained from 
 the nanoparticles and low-surface energy solution.
We used surface modification for nano-spheres by
combining silica particles with low-surface energy
 solution. This process will change surfaces prop-
erty of nano-spheres from hydrophilic into hydro-
phobic. 
In this procedure, we fixed the nanopaticles su-
spension volume as 10ml, mixed with low-surface
energy solution (70wt% F4) 3, 5, 7,9, 11ml respe-
ctively. 
After that time, we spreaded modified nano-sph-
eres on glass substrate via spin coating technique. 
Finally, we put the samples into the furnace and 
heated to 50℃ for 10 hours for drying. 
When the samples were drew out from furnace,
we examined the water/oil contact angles of the  
thin films via Contact Angle Meter (Sindatek, 100
SB). The transparent ratio of the samples was co-
nducted for light with wavelength 400 ~ 800 nm 
by UV/Visible spectrophotometer (Jasco, V-560).  
The morphology of the thin films was analyzed  
by field-emission scanning electron microscope (H
ita-chi, S-4100I ).
