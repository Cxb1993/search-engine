摘要 
    本研究以電漿電弧做為加熱源來蒸發鎢靶材，並藉由氣凝合成機制以及一套
吹氣裝置製備W5O14奈米棒，並進ㄧ步探討W5O14奈米棒之氣體感測特性。實驗
過程中，使用氬氣(Ar)添加10%的氫氣(H2)作為電漿氣體，並在電流90A以及腔體
壓力400 torr下蒸發鎢靶材。研究結果顯示，在電漿氣中加入10%氫氣可成功地製
備具有單晶結構的W5O14奈米棒，其晶格間距(d-spacing)為0.38nm，且W5O14奈米
棒會沿著[010]方向成長，而W5O14奈米棒長寬比(aspect ratio)之比例為12.7。可以
透過參數作佳化的設計，可以發現W5O14奈米棒於48小時300℃的熱處理條件下
具有良好的熱穩定性; 而針對氣體感測研究方面結果顯示：W5O14奈米棒在感測
溫度為200℃下對低濃度(1~4ppm)的NO2氣體具有良好的感測性質，隨著氣體濃
度的增加其感測之靈敏度也會呈線性成長。 
關鍵字：氣凝合成法、W5O14 氧化鎢、奈米棒、氣體感測。 
 
 
ABSTRACT 
In the present study, tungsten oxide nanomaterials were synthesized by a 
modified plasma arc gas condensation technique. X-ray diffraction results showed 
that all the nanomaterials investigated in the present study were W5O14 phase. High 
resolution transmission electron microscopy observation revealed that tungsten oxide 
nanorods grown along the [001] direction with a lattice spacing of 0.38 nm. 
Photolumeinscence emission at ultraviolet-visible (365 nm) and blue (444 nm) 
regions are found in the W5O14 nanorods, which is caused by band-band transition and 
oxygen vacancies, respectively. And the band gap of tungsten oxide nanorods is 3.4 
eV.  
The thermal stability of W5O14 nanorods is good enough under the test 
environment of 300℃ and 48 hours. The sensitivity for NO2 gas with 1~4ppm is 
excellent under 200℃ and proportional to the NO2 gas concentration. 
 
Keywords：Gas Condensation Technique、W15O14Tungsten Oxide、Nanorod

五、結論 
利用電漿電弧氣凝合成法，並藉
由在電漿氣(Ar)中加入 10%還原氫氣
可成功的製備出 W5O14 奈米棒，製程

Electro-optical properties of CIGS thin films prepared by magnetron sputtering 
Cherng-Yuh Su1*, Xue-Zhen Chen1, Wei-Hao He1, Hsuan-Ching Lin1,  
Cheng-Han Wu2, and Shih-Chang Lian2 
 1Inst. Manuf. Tech., National Taipei University of Technology, Taipei, 106, Taiwan, R.O.C.

Abstract 
In this study, Cu(Inx,Ga1-x)Se2 (CIGS) thin films deposited on Mo-coated soda-lime glasses as 
absorption layers for solar cells were prepared rapidly by direct current (DC) magnetron sputtering 
with selenization process. The effects of selenization temperature and selenization time on the CIGS 
thin films were investigated. SEM, XRD and EPMA were used to indentify the morphologies, 
micro-structures and compositions of the CIGS thin films. The results indicated that the optimized 
selenization temperature and time for preparing high-quality CIGS thing films are 550℃ and 60 
min, respectively. However, with the increasing selenization temperature, the surface roughness and 
density of the CIGS thin films are decreased due to the cracks and cavities formation.

2. Experimental procedures 
A Mo film with a thickness of 700 nm was deposited on soda-lime glass (SLG) substrates with 
dimension of 1cm×1cm×1.5mm by direct current (DC) magnetron sputtering in Ar gas at a pressure 
of 6×10-3torr. Substrates were cleaned using acetone, alcohol and deionised water. Then they were 
dried with N2 gas and immediately introduced into the vacuum apparatus. 
The DC magnetron sputtering used in this study is shown in Fig. 1. The Mo film was sputtered 
from a Mo target foil (purity 99.95%) with a diameter of 50.8mm and thickness of 6 mm. First, the 
vacuum chamber was evacuated to a base pressure of 5×10-5 torr. Then Ar flow was introduced into 
the chamber. The high vacuum valve was partly throttled and the gate-valve was managed to 
achieve the desired Ar pressure. Then the power supply was turned on and sputtering power was 
adjusted to 100W. Prior to the deposition, the target was cleaned by pre-sputtering.    
Then, CIGS thin films were deposited by DC magnetron sputtering and selenization method. 
Firstly, the metallic precursors of approximately 1μm were deposited sequentially on Mo coated 
glass substrates using CuInGa (CIG) alloy target (purity 99.9%, Cu:In:Ga=38.55：48.76：12.69 
wt%,) by DC magnetron sputtering system. Secondly, the metallic precursors were selenized with 
Se vapor in a closed space shown in Fig. 2. In order to investigate the effects of selenization 
temperature and selenization time on the microstructures of the CIGS thin films, the selenization 
processes were performed by various temperatures. The selenization time were kept at 40 min and 
60 min; the selenization temperatures were varied at 530 , 550 and 580 .℃ ℃ ℃

Fabrication and Optical properties of Ti-doped W18O49 Nanorods using  
a Modified Plasma Arc Gas Condensation Technique 
 
Cherng-Yuh Su1*, Hsuan-Ching Lin1, and Chung-Kwei Lin2 
 
 
1Institute of Manufacturing Technology, National Taipei University of Technology,

Abstract 
 
In this study, Ti-doped W18O49 nanorods were prepared without any templates and 
catalysts using a modified plasma arc gas condensation technique. The structure 
investigations and optical property of the as-prepared nanorods were characterized by 
FEG-SEM, HRTEM, XRD, micro-Raman, and UV-Visible spectrometers. Experimental 
results show that the diameter and the length of the as-prepared nanorods are 
approximately 20 nm and 3 μm, respectively. As-prepared nanorods exhibited W18O49 
phase and characteristic Raman peaks can be observed. UV-Visible absorption 
investigations revealed that Ti-doped W18O49 nanorods exhibited a red-shift  
phenomenon when compared with the pure TiO2 and WO3. 
 
Keywords: plasma arc, nanorods, tungsten oxide, doped, gas condensation.

Experimental Procedures 
 
Fig. 1 shows the apparatus used for preparing Ti-doped tungsten oxide nanorods in 
this study. The target used in this study was composed of tungsten powders (particle size 
c.a 10 μm with purity of 99.95 %) and nanocrystalline titanium oxide powders (particle 
size c.a 10 nm with purity of 99.95%) with a weight ratio of 10 to 1. The target was then 
heated by a water-cooled plasma arc gun (model：PWM-300, Thermadyne International, 
USA). The chamber pressure was kept at ~760 torr. Once the plasma arc was triggered, 
the voltage and current were maintained at 34 V and 90 A, respectively. The overall
