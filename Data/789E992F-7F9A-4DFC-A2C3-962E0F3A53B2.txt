三、 研究方法 
3.1 負載預測系統 
本計畫採用模糊邏輯與倒傳遞類神經網
路之混合模擬適應性模糊類神經網路，以建
立自備發電機用戶電力負載預測系統，並達
到可自動依據用戶歷史負載資料變化趨勢，
調整預測模式之內部參數，兼顧非靜態成長
趨勢負載與靜態變化負載特性之預測精確度
的要求。自備發電機用戶電力負載預測系統
架構如圖 3-1 所示。 
適應性模糊類神經網路是一種具有學習
能力的多層前授型網路，由於自備發電機用
戶的負載使用，建置前與建置後會有所不
同，此外也會隨著外在的決策因素而影響整
體的發電調度，進而提高成本，因此，給定
歷史的訓練資料以及進行中的量測資料，透
過自我學習的方式，適當的調整模糊邏輯之
歸屬函數，以滿足模糊邏輯的輸出輸入關係。

3.2 自備發電機組系統架構與運轉成本 
自備發電機組以重油發電機組為主，燃
料以重油為主，柴油為輔。系統架構中，除
了發電機組主機設備，還有其他各種輔機設
備，輔助發電機組維持正常運作。

常契約電價×經常契約容量＋當月半尖峰契
約電價×半尖峰契約容量+當月離峰契約電價
×[離峰契約容量－（尖峰契約容量＋半尖峰契
約容量）×0.5]，當離峰契約容量－（尖峰契
約容量＋半尖峰契約容量）×0.5 為負值時，
則按零計算。 
備用電力是指綜合或電力用戶於經常供
電線路之外，另接其他供電線路，以備經常
供電線路故障、檢修、跳脫或其他緊急情況
時，由其他供電線路供電者。以特高壓方式
供應者，備用電力按經常用電適用電價另加
百分之十五計算。 
2. 流動電費： 
經常用電流動電費是以尖峰電價、半尖
峰電價、週六半尖峰電價以及離峰電價分別
乘上各個時段的用電度數加總而得。備用電
力所使用的用電度數，併入經常用電度數
內，並按照經常用電適用的電價計算。 
3. 功率因數調整費： 
功率因素調整費的目的為提高電力設備
及線路利用率，功率因數低，表示無效電力
偏大，增加設備及線路的損失，改善功率因
數可減少電費、減輕電力損失、穩定電壓及
用電品質。 
依台電電價表規定，若用戶每月用電平
均功率因數不及百分之八十時，每低於百分
之一，該月份電費應增加千分之三；若超過
百分之八十時，每超過百分之一 ，該月份電
費應減少千分之一點五。 
4. 超約附加費： 
台電投資發、供電設備，除按用戶用電
契約容量設置外，為防止機組可能發生故障
或用戶臨時增加用電，影響供電可靠度，以
應付突發負載需要之設備稱為備載容量，由
於可利用性低，故分攤之費用高，為公平起
見，多分攤於超約用電用戶而不轉嫁到全體
用戶。 
依台電電價表規定，當月用電最高需量
低於其契約容量時，以契約容量計算基本電
費，當月用電最高需量超出其契約容量時，
超出部份依各該不同供電季節、不同供電時
間之適用電價，按下列標準計收基本電費，
並概不給予功率因數及基本電費等電費折
扣。 
(1) 超過契約容量 10％以下部份按二倍計收
基本電費。 
(2) 超過契約容量 10％以上部份按三倍計收
基本電費。 
5. 契約容量調整費： 
契約容量訂定後，用戶可以依用電需求
隨時加以調整，但會依照變更的容量收取不
同的費用，用戶調整契約容量時依不同時段
及時間收取擴建補助費或供電設備維持費，
其收費標準依照台電營業規則規定。 
裝設自備發電機用戶總用電成本是本計
畫最佳化演算法之目標函數，故在計算適應
性時，先以經常、半尖峰及離峰契約容量決
定台電用電度數，而自備發電機組發電度數
即為負載需量減掉台電用電度數，有了台電
用電度數及發電機組發電度數，代入 3.2 節中
所述之計算，用戶總用電成本即為台電電費
成本加上自備發電機組發電成本的總和。 
3.4 最佳契約容量方法 
本計畫所探討之最佳契約容量方法，分
別有轉移方法、基因演算法、改良型田口法
與強化文化演算法，詳細描述如后。 
3.4.1 轉移方法[1] 
此方式為目前用戶實際的契約容量訂定
方式，為因應時間電價，尖峰及半尖峰時段
的電價遠高於離峰時段電價，因此自備發電
機在電價較高時段發電是經濟效益比較高的
操作方式，離峰時段則無利用發電機發電之
必要。 
根據台電營業規則規定，經常契約與離
峰契約可以 1 比 5 的方式轉換，不需收取任
何契約容量調整費，亦即每減少 1kW 的經常
契約容量可轉換為 5kW 的離峰契約容量。 
依預估的最高負載需量及轉移方式訂出
契約容量值後，尖峰和半尖峰時段負載最高
需量超出經常契約容量的部分即由發電機供
應。

裝設自備發電機用戶總用電成本計算亦
是本文最佳化演算法之目標函數，故在計算
適應性時，先以經常、半尖峰及離峰契約容
量決定台電用電度數，而自備發電機組發電
度數即為負載需量減掉台電用電度數，有了
台電用電度數及發電機組發電度數，代入成
本計算，用戶總用電成本即為台電電費成本
加上自備發電機組發電成本的總和。

CULTURED DIFFERENTIAL COMPUTATION ALGORITHM FOR 
OPTIMAL CONTRACTED CAPACITY OF POWER CONSUMER WITH 
SELF-OWNED GENERATING UNITS 
*Hong-Tzer Yang, Pai-Chun Peng, and Shr-Ching Guo 
 *Department of Electrical Engineering Department of Electrical Engineering 
 National Cheng Kung University Chung Yuan Christian University 
 Tainan, Taiwan Chung Li, Taiwan 
*htyang@mail.ncku.edu.tw, g9302803@cycu.edu.tw, and g9478007@cycu.org.tw 
Abstract – Contracted capacity setting is a discrete and 
nonlinear optimization problem in consideration of expen-
diture on the electricity from the utility and cost of the 
self-owned generating units (SOGUs) at the same time. 
This paper proposes the cultured differential computation 
algorithm (CDCA) to solve this problem. The cultural 
algorithm (CA) used in the CDCA extracts and saves the 
domain knowledge or problem properties during the evo-
lution process. The differential computation of the CDCA 
provides fast converging characteristics in searching the 
optimal solution through operations of mutation, cross-
over, and selection operations which are efficient and 
different from the existing generic algorithm (GA). To 
verify feasibility of the proposed method, the paper em-
ploys the real data obtained from a large optoelectronics 
factory in Taiwan. In comparison with the existing optimi-
zation methods, the proposed CDCA approach has supe-
rior results as revealed in the numerical results in terms of 
the computation time needed and the quality of solution 
obtained. 
Keywords: Self-Owned Generating Units, Optimal 
Contracted Capacity, Cultural Differential Computa-
tion Algorithm 
1 INTRODUCTION 
Requirements of power reliability are quite strict for 
the high-technology industry. In attempt to improve the 
power supply reliability, avoid voltage sag and interrup-
tion events, and reduce the corresponding impacts, often 
employed are the SOGUs interconnected with the 
power grid. The operations of the SOGUs and the con-
tracted capacities with the utility are thus highly con-
cerned. 
Determination of the contracted capacity and the op-
erations of the SOGUs is a discrete and nonlinear opti-
mization problem. The discrete feature arises from the 
utility tariff designs for different peak, half-peak, and 
off-peak periods of power consumption as well as from 
the adjusting fees of contracted capacity. The nonlinear 
characteristics results from the operation dispatch of the 
SOGUs with nonlinear cost functions. To solve such a 
discrete and nonlinear optimization problem, ap-
proaches [1-8] have often been employed. 
Heuristic translation method [1] to determine the 
contracted capacities was suggested and widely used by 
Taiwan Power Company (TPC). Mixed-integer linear 
programming [2] is a traditional approach for an opti-
mization problem with discrete variables and linear 
objective functions and constraints. As a result, the 
nonlinear objective functions or constraints involved in 
the problem need to be successively linearized. On the 
other hand, the problem has also been solved through 
mixed-integer and stochastic programming based ap-
proaches with uncertainty taken into account [3-5]. 
The evolutionary programming [6] approach is used 
to solve the capacity contract determination problem for 
industrial customers, but without consideration of the 
SOGUs. Alternatively, the genetic algorithms have also 
been applied to the similar optimization problem [7]. 
Drawbaks of the evolutionary computing approaches 
would be the long searching time needed and the quality 
of the solution searched for the capacity contract, espe-
cially for the problem with SOGUs included. 
To try to solve the optimization problem more effi-
ciently, the Taguchi method was proposed in [8]. Ta-
guchi method operates based on the analysis that reveals 
which of the factors are most effective in reaching the 
goals and the directions in which these factors should be 
adjusted to improve the results. It may enhance the 
global and local exploration abilities with good flexibil-
ity. However, the complex design for the orthogonal 
matrix included in the quality analysis may decrease the 
efficiency and the quality of outcome. 
In this paper, we propose the approach of CDCA [9] 
to effectively search the best combination of contracted 
capacities and the dispatched output of SOGUs. In the 
proposed CDCA, the differential evolution (DE) [10] 
provides fast converging characteristics in searching the 
optimal solution through operations of mutation, cross-
over, and selection operations. The operations are effi-
cient and different from the existing GA. Besides, the 
CA in the CDCA incorporates domain knowledge or 
problem properties obtained during the evolution proc-
ess to make the search process more efficient [11-13].  
Employing real data collected from supervisor con-
trol and data acquisition (SCADA) system of a large 
optoelectronics factory with SOGUs, the obtained simu-
lation results demonstrate the feasibility and effective-
ness of the proposed method in terms of convergence 
speed and solution precision compared with heuristic 
scheme [1], GA [14], and Taguchi optimization [15] 
methods. 
This paper is organized as follows. In Section 2, the 
problem description of the contracted capacity optimi-
zation is described. Next, the CA and DE algorithms, as 
well as the proposed CDCA are described in Section 3 
and 4, respectively. Section 5 presents the solutions 
obtained and the comparisons with the existing meth-
ods. Finally, conclusions of this paper are given.

3 OVERVIEW OF CULTURAL ALGORITHM 
AND DIFFERENTIAL EVOLUTION 
Evolutionary Computing methods were developed by 
observing the natural phenomena and the appearance of 
biology and life [10]. Some social researchers also have 
suggested that culture may be symbolically encoded and 
transmitted within and between populations as an in-
heritance mechanism [11]. In this section, we briefly 
describe the basics of CA. 
3.1 Basics of CA 
CA operates on two spaces shown in Fig. 1 [9]. The 
population space contains population (which is a set of 
possible solutions to the problem) generated from any 
evolutionary computing method. The other space is the 
belief space which stores the knowledge, traits, or bias 
acquired by the population during its evolutionary proc-
ess.

lished by using acceptance function and influence func-
tion. Acceptance function is applied to the population 
space to select the group of individuals, which incorpo-
rate experiences and domain properties to update the 
belief space.

4.3 Structure of CDCA 
The solution structure of the CDCA is delineated in 
Fig. 2. With the belief and population spaces initialized, 
the influence function selects the mutation operations 
based on the four knowledge sources of Eqs. (26) - 
(33), for DE in the subsequent mutation, crossover, and 
selection process. 
Among the new population generated, the acceptance 
function then selects the individuals from the population 
space. The knowledge thus acquired is then used to 
update the belief space via Eqs. (34) – (35) by determin-
ing the number of accepted individuals and the prob-
ability of the four knowledge sources to be applied to 
the next DE process. The evolutionary process termi-
nates according to the given maximal number of genera-
tions. 
5 NUMERICAL RESULTS 
To verify the effectiveness of the proposed method, 
this paper employed the real data obtained from the 
SCADA system of a large optoelectronics factory in 
Taiwan with SOGUs in September, 2004 to March, 
2005. Based on their experiences, the load demand in 
this example can be regarded as deterministic. The case 
studies serve to investigate the effectiveness of the pro-
posed method in the optimization of contracted capaci-
ties. Besides, since the consumer did not have the con-
tract with the utilities as an IPP (independent power 
producer) to sell the energy, it was supposed there was 
no energy sold in the case studies. 
The data used include the operation costs of the 
SOGUs, the predicted power load demands, and the 
power bills from the utilities. Total seven SOGUs had 
been installed with maximum power output of 8MW for 
each. The parameters used in the proposed CDCA are
