摘要 
本計畫運用整合微奈米技術與光纖通訊技術最先進之光機電高科技，將微奈米材料的高比表面積
的特性以及技術應用在光纖的感測表面上，利用多孔洞結構的二氧化矽奈米粒子(porous silica particles)
與溶膠-凝膠製程(sol-gel process)技術製作成高靈敏度之光纖感測器，針對氧氣做氣體濃度之偵測，可
設計出具有體積小、速度快、靈敏度高、可靠度高、性能佳、環境適應性強等優點之氣體感測分析系
統。本計畫首先針對多孔洞二氧化矽奈米粒子結構的感測材料製作與研究，藉由溶膠-凝膠製程技術製
作多孔洞二氧化矽奈米粒子與基材，將氧氣感測材料與多孔洞二氧化矽奈米粒子以及溶膠-凝膠基材做
結合，此時氧氣感測材料會侷限在多孔洞二氧化矽奈米粒子的奈米孔洞結構與溶膠-凝膠基材中，最後
將感測薄膜塗佈在光纖的端面進行氧氣濃度感測。經由 UV LED (405 nm)光源去激發光纖端面的氧氣
感測材料而產生放射螢光，藉由螢光光譜儀量測其放射螢光，再利用放射螢光之強度會隨著氧氣濃度
的不同而改變的特性，進行量測與監控環境中的氧氣濃度。 
 
關鍵詞：光纖、氧氣、二氧化矽奈米粒子、溶膠-凝膠。 
 
 
 
Abstract 
In this project, the high technology of nano-science and optical fiber sensors are applied and integrated. 
How to efficiently manufacture the high porous silica nanoparticles structure and mixed with dye for sensing 
gas is crucial in this project. Through a well-known manufacturing process in porous silica nanoparticles, a 
new method in manufacturing high porous silica nanoparticles will be developed. It is based upon the unique 
process in sol-gel process. The ubiquitous oxygen molecule is quite critical for the very existence of life. 
Optical oxygen sensor based on the quenching of fluorescence or phosphorescence emitted from 
oxygen-sensitive dye is our focus in this research. These optical sensors for the determination of oxygen offer 
the advantages of no consumption of oxygen, quick response time, high sensitivity and high resolution as well 
as continuous determination of oxygen concentrations in remote, hazardous, or in vivo environments. 
The technique is based on coating the end of an optical fiber with a microporous film prepared by the sol-gel 
process. The highly-sensitive oxygen sensor comprising an optical fiber coated at one end with porous silica 
nanoparticles and oxygen sensing dye embedded in the sol-gel composite xerogel. The oxygen composite 
xerogel is excited using an ultraviolet light emitting diode (UV LED) light source with a wavelength of 405 
nm. Based on the Stern-Volmer, we can monitor the oxygen concentration in the ambient environment. 
 
Keywords: optical fiber, oxygen, porous silica nanoparticles, sol-gel.

2. Theory 
Although luminophore quenching depends on several factors, in the simplest scenario of a luminophore 
in a homogeneous microenvironment, the quenching effect can be described by the Stern-Volmer equation 
[18]: 
I0 /I = 1 + Ksv[O2]                                                                        (1) 
where I0 and I represent the steady-state fluorescence intensities in the absence and presence, respectively, of 
the quencher (O2 in the current case); Ksv is the Stern-Volmer quenching constant; [O2] is the oxygen 
concentration. In the ideal case, a plot of I0 /I against [O2] is linear with a slope equal to Ksv and an intercept of 
unity, allowing the application of a simple single-point sensor calibration scheme. 
 
3. Experimental 
3.1 Fabrication of two optical fiber oxygen sensors 
The optical oxygen sensor developed in this study was fabricated using a Ru(II) complex ([Ru(dpp)3]2+) 
and porous silica nanoparticles embedded in an n-octyltriethoxysilane (Octyl-triEOS)/tetraethylorthosilane 
(TEOS) composite xerogel. For comparison, Ru(II) complex embedded in an Octyl-triEOS/TEOS composite 
xerogel was also fabricated for an optical oxygen sensor. The basic steps of synthesis of the porous silica 
nanoparticles are described in the sub-sections below. 
 
3.2 Synthesis of monodisperse SiO2 solid spheres 
Monodisperse SiO2 solid spheres 50-800 nm in diameter were prepared using a slightly modified Stöber 
 6 
prepared by mixing Octyl-triEOS (0.2 mL) and TEOS (4 mL) to form a precursor solution. Adopting an 
approach similar to that employed by Yeh et al. [10], EtOH (1.25 mL) and HCl (0.1 M, 0.4 mL) were then 
added to the sol solution to catalyze the ORMOSIL reaction. The resulting solution was capped and stirred 
magnetically for 1 h at room temperature. During the mixing process, Triton-X-100 (0.1 mL) was added to the 
solution to improve the homogeneity of the silica sol, resulting in a crack-free monolith. The oxygen-sensitive 
dye solution was prepared by dissolving 2 mg of PtTFPP in 10 mL of EtOH. The resulting solution (1 mL) 
was then added to the porous silica nanoparticles and stirred for 10 min. The solution was then added to the 
composite sol solution. Finally, the solution was capped and stirred magnetically for another 10 min. 
The sensor fabrication process was commenced by soaking a multimode optical fiber in NaOH for 24 h. 
The fiber was then rinsed with copious amounts of de-ionized water and EtOH and then dried at room 
temperature for 10 min. The composite xerogel with the porous silica nanoparticles was then deposited on one 
end of the fiber in a dip-coating operation performed at a velocity of 0.25 mm/s. Finally, the coated fiber was 
dried at room temperature and left to stabilize under ambient conditions for 1 week.

5. Conclusion 
This paper presented a highly sensitive optical fiber oxygen sensor based on a Ru(II) complex 
([Ru(dpp)32+]) and porous silica nanoparticles embedded in a sol-gel matrix. The experimental results show 
that the oxygen sensor has a linear response for oxygen concentrations in the range 0~100 % and has a 
sensitivity of approximately 26, which is considerably higher than the typical optical oxygen sensor based on 
the Ru(II) complex doped in a sol-gel matrix. The porous silica nanoparticles not only increase the surface 
area per unit mass in the sensing surface but also increase sensitivity because a substantial number of aerial 
oxygen molecules penetrate the porous silica shell. Thus, the proposed sensor can be used for applications that 
required high sensitivity.
