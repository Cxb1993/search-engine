摘要 
 
本研究旨在探討一溴二苯醚(monobromdiphenylether, MBDE)之氣相光催化分解之氧化
特性，分析不同反應參數對於 MBDE 分解速率之影響，並推導光催化分解 MBDE 之反應
動力模式。本研究採用 Pyrex 玻璃底槽及上蓋為石英玻璃所組成之皿式光催化反應器，並
以被覆二氧化鈦(Degusa P-25)粉末被覆三氧化鎢(WO3)等半導體氧化物玻璃珠作為光觸
媒，置於反應器上方之紫外線燈管及可見光光源上，藉以進行相關之光催化分解實驗。本
研究反應之操作參數包括：水蒸氣濃度、氧氣濃度、溫度等。研究結果顯示，適當的水蒸
氣濃度，有促進 MBDE 分解之特性，但當水蒸氣濃度過高時，水氣將與 MBDE 競爭活化
位址，而導致分解效率降低；氧氣濃度對於 MBDE 分解效率之影響，則呈現為典型異相觸
媒反應之反應特性；MBDE 之分解速率隨溫度增加呈現指數上升的趨勢。經由不同水氣濃
度、氧濃度等操作條件下，光催化分解 MBDE 之結果進行反應動力模式分析，結果顯示利
用雙分子競爭吸附形式之 Langmuir-Hinshelwood 方程式之預測結果，與實驗值有不錯的吻
合度。另外，就可見光光源分解一溴二苯醚之能力方面，單純以二氧化鈦為觸媒時，其在
可見見光下的梵映並不明顯，但在複合以三氧化鎢半導體後，確實可增進其在可見下之分
解效能，惟基本上，仍以在近紫外光能下，具有較快的反應速率。 
 
關鍵字：光催化、複合光觸媒、溴醚化物、反應參數、模式模擬 
 
Abstract 
Experimental results indicate that photocatalysis of MBDE basically follows a first-order 
reaction initially and the calcination temperature of WO3/TiO2 at 500 oC (between 105~600 oC) 
as well as WO3 to TiO2 ratio equal to 0.5% (between 0.5~3.0%) can achieve a better 
photocatalytic activity for the degradation of MBDE. In addition, the results show that suitable 
amount of water vapor (e.g., 5400 μM) can promote the photocatalysis of MBDE due to 
accumulating less reaction intermediates and forming more hydroxyl radicals, but the MBDE 
degradation rate is retarded in too humid conditions due to adsorption competition for active sites 
on the photocatalyst. Besides, the results show MBDE decomposition rate increasing 
exponentially with reaction temperature. A typical Arrhenius rate of relationship for the effect of 
reaction temperature on photocatalytic degradation rate of MBDE is observed. Finally, for the 
formation of reaction intermediates from photocatalysis of MBDE, both enhancing water vapor 
concentration and reaction temperature can result in less formation of the intermediates but it 
results in forming more different types of reaction intermediates. The identified reaction 
intermediates include diphenyl ether, 4-methoxycinnamic acid, 2-ethylhexyl ester, butylated 
hydroxytoluene, benzoic acid, myristic acid isopropyl ester, benzene, bromoform and 
dibromomethane. This study also demonstrates enhancing photocatalytic activity of TiO2 after 
being doped with WO3. Although consistent results of achieving higher degradation rate of 
MBDE under illumination with shorter-wavelength light sources, the results also demonstrate the 
potential application of visible light as the light sources while using WO3/TiO2 as a photocatalyst. 
The photocatalysis of MBDE illuminated by the lamp with a main wavelength of 450 nm has 
been observed in the study.

在於可延緩電子與電洞再結合的速率，或延伸可吸收光源的波長至可見光波長範圍。
Serpone 於 1995 年提出 IPET 之理論，即於具能階差異之複合半導體系統中，當光源使能隙
較低之半導體光觸媒受激發時，則所產生之光電子可藉由兩半導體電位差，以類似於「跳
島作用」，將所提升的電子傳遞至能隙較大半導體傳導帶電位上，藉以延長電子電洞之再
結合時間，以利‧OH 之生成，達到提高光活性之目的。其反應方式，乃當光源足以使兩半
導體都激發時，由於電位差之緣故，能隙小之半導體傳導帶電子將傳遞並累積在能隙較高
之半導體傳導帶上，而能隙較大之半導體價帶的電洞則傳遞至價帶中電位較低的半導體
上，使電子電洞依然能有效分離。相關的研究中，Serpone 利用 CdS/TiO2、ZnO/TiO2、
TiO2/Fe2O3、ZnO/Fe2O3 等複合觸媒對 phenol、2-chlorophenol 與 pentachlorophenol 進行光
催化反應，發現受激發後 CdS 表面電子會轉移至 TiO2 上故 CdS/TiO2 複合觸媒對有機物降
解率的提升有所幫助。本研究則製備添加三氧化物鎢複合二氧化鈦之複合光觸媒，藉以瞭
解在改質光觸媒在可見光下之反應效能。

(near-UV lamp, F15TBLB, GE)作為光源，其主要發射光之波長為350~365 nm，以提供442 
μW/cm2之光照強度。在反應器四周均留有四個開孔以便於進樣、採樣及熱電偶溫度計

2. 水氣濃度對光催化分解 MBDE 之影響 
圖 3 為不同水蒸氣濃度下，MBDE 經光催化分解後，其殘餘率隨反應時間遞變的曲線，
佈同溼度下之 MBDE 光催化分解反應，仍呈一階反應。乃將實驗所得之殘餘率變化曲線取
對數後，所得的斜率值即為各條件下的反應速率常數。圖 4 所示為不同水蒸氣濃度，對於
光催化分解 MBDE 反應速率之影響，實驗測試條件為 MBDE 初始濃度 5.45 μM，氧氣濃度
8000 μM，水氣濃度介於 0~26760 μM，反應溫度 150 oC。 
實驗結果顯示，水氣濃度之增加對於反應速率有兩種不同之影響，在反應之水氣濃度
小於 13400 μM 環境下，MBDE 的反應速率有隨著水氣濃度提高而增加之趨勢；然而，當
反應的水氣濃度提升至 13400 μM 以上時，MBDE 反應速率始隨著水氣濃度之提高而降低。
造成此現象的原因，可能主要來自反應物與水分子競爭活化位置有關(Wu et al., 2004)。當
反應物濃度固定時，反應初期時由於水分子吸附於 TiO2 表面，進而被電洞氧化而形成氫氧
自由基(•OH)，氫氧自由基為一強氧化劑，能快速將 MBDE 分解，其反應速率也因此而提
高。所以，初期水氣濃度之增加對於光催化分解反應有所幫助。不過，當水氣濃度逐漸增
加時，TiO2 表面之氫氧自由基可能藉由氫鍵(H-bond)吸附水分子，所以在不斷增加水氣濃
度之情況下，便會使水分子於 TiO2 表面造成累積，而阻礙 MBDE 之吸附，導致 MBDE 與
水分子競爭吸附於活性位址之現象， MBDE 的分解效率也隨之降低。 
另外，實驗也發現，在乾燥或低水氣濃度的情況下，觸媒表面有中間產物累積，使得
觸媒表面顏色隨著光照時間增加而逐漸變深，觸媒活性也隨著實驗的進行，而逐漸衰退；
但當有水氣的情況下，觸媒表面顏色隨著水氣濃度增加而變淺，尤其當水分子濃度增至
13400 μM (465000ppm)時，觸媒表面顏色與最初未照光前差異不大(如圖 5)，此時 MBDE
光催化反應速率也達到最高。基於此，對於 MBDE 之光催化分解反應而言，水氣濃度的增
加，可促進光催化分解反應所生成的產物，從觸媒表面脫附或反應，使觸媒活性不因產物
的吸附累積而衰退。此外，水氣的增加，加速中間產物的分解，或促進中間產物從觸媒表
面脫附，也可能是提升 MBDE 反應速率的原因之一。

Abstract 
This study investigated photocatalysis of monobromdiphenyl ether (MBDE) 
under the illumination of near-UV light by using P-25 TiO2 as the 
photocatalyst. The tests were conducted in a disk-shaped photocatalytic 
batch reactor. Several experimental parameters including water vapour 
concentration from dry to 27000 μM, oxygen concentration from 400 to 8000 
μM, and reaction temperature from 150 to 270 oC were tested.  In relatively 
dry air, increased water vapour concentration promoted MBDE degradation 
rates due to reduced accumulation of reaction intermediates on the catalysts 
and the formation of more hydroxyl radicals, but it was also observed that 
too much water vapour also could inhibit MBDE degradation due to 
competitive adsorption for active sites between MDBE and water vapour. 
The highest reaction rate of MBDE occurred at a water concentration of 
13400 μM.  Typical heterogeneous catalytic reactions were observed for the 
MBDE degradation rate versus oxygen content. A first-order reaction with 
respect to oxygen concentration was detected for the photocatalysis of 
MBDE at low oxygen concentrations, but a zero-order reaction was 
observed after the adsorption sites were saturated with oxygen.  The 
degradation rate of MBDE increased exponentially with reaction temperature, 
showing a typical Arrhenius relationship. Faster surface reaction rates are 
expected in high temperature conditions even though there is less 
adsorption of reactants by the photocatalyst.  The formation of reaction 
intermediates was reduced by both high water vapour content and high 
reaction temperature. 
Keywords: bromdiphenyl ether; photocatalysis; TiO2; degradation; water 
vapour; temperature 
 
 
1. Introduction 
Polybrominated diphenyl ethers (PBDEs), which 
have been applied as flame-retardants in plastic 
products and synthetic polymers, are emerging as 
contaminants of global concern due to their wide 
spread occurrence in the environment and 
increasing content in human tissues (Sun et al., 
2003). Beside being an excellent flame retardant, 
PBDEs are widely used in the manufacture of 
electronic products, wooden furniture, polyurethere 
foam, plastics, and textiles (Manchester-Neesvig et 
al., 2001). Many life style products, at least 
including electronic parts, TV sets and computers, 
sofa, foam-based cushion seats, and carpets 
contain PBDEs. As they deteriorate, PBDEs can be 
released from these household products to the 
environment and enter food-chains. The potentially 
adverse impacts on the environment and human 
health should be a cause for concern. 
   PBDEs can contain up to 10 bromine atoms per 
molecule and up to 209 possible congeners for 
PBDEs can occur, depending on the number and 
positions of the bromine atoms on their molecular 
structures. Some preliminary toxicity investigations 
have shown that PBDEs could induce 
immunosuppression and chloracne, disrupt the 
endocrine system, affect learning and memory 
functions, and cause cancers (Eriksson et al., 
2001). PBDEs have been found in air, water, 
sediments, wildlife (marine mammals, fish, and bird 
eggs) and humans (breast milk, serum, and 
adipose tissue) after tonnes of PBDE-containing 
products have been discarded (BGVV Press, 2002; 
Chaturvedi, 2002).  
2.3. Chemical Analysis Systems 
The chemical analysis units contained two sets of 
gas chromatographs (G.C.), GC/MSD (HP 
6890/HP5973) and GC/ECD (HP 4890 Series II 
plus), for identifying and quantifying chemical 
compositions for the reactions. The reaction 
intermediates or products were identified by the 
GC/MSD with a HP-5 separation column (Agilent 
Technologies, 60m×0.25mm I.D., 0.25μm film), and 
then were further quantified by the GC/ECD with a 
DB-XLB separation column 30 m×0.25 mm I.D., 
0.1μm film). The residual MBDE concentration in 
the photocatalytic cell was periodically measured 
with the GC/ECD by taking gaseous samples from 
the reactor, while the reaction intermediates were 
qualified with the GC/MS. Concentrations of both 
oxygen and water vapour experiments were 
estimated by calculating their dilution ratios to the 
overall reactor volume which had a total pressure of 
about 1.0 atm (101 hPa).

For the photocatalytic degradation of MDBE in 
various water vapour concentration conditions, the 
MBDE reaction rate initially increased with water 
vapour content but then fell at water vapour 
concentrations above 14,000 μM. The results 
indicate that some water vapour is beneficial for the 
degradation rate of MDBE, but it seems to have an 
adverse effect when too much water vapour is 
present. The deterioration in the degradation rate 
could be due to competitive adsorption between 
MDBE and water vapour for the active TiO2sites. 
Similar reaction patterns have been observed for 
gas-phase photocatalysis of many organic 
pollutants, which also show that the photocatalytic 
rates of organic pollutants peaked at a similar water 
vapour concentration.

Based on photocatalytic reaction kinetics (Equation 
1), TiO2 valence electrons can be promoted to the 
conduction band under the illumination of near-UV 
light, and electron-holes are left in the valence band. 
The electrons promoted to the conduction band 
tend to react with oxygen for most PCO reactions. 
In fact, it has been reported that oxygen acts an 
efficient electron capture agent in photocatalytic 
reactions. On the other hand, the residual electron-
holes in the valence bands usually can react with 
water vapour (or hydroxyl ions, OH-) to form 
hydroxyl radicals (•OH) which can further react with 
the organic species adsorbed on the photocatalyst.  
 
TiO2 + hν (light) → h+ (hole) + e–  (promoted electron)         (1) 
 
Accordingly, more hydroxyl radicals are expected to 
form in more humid environments. However, 
because water vapour and organic species 
generally compete for the same activated sites on 
photocatalysts, the degradation rates of organic 
compounds will be retarded when the water vapour 
concentration is high enough to occupy most of the 
activated sites and to interfere with the adsorption 
of organic compounds by TiO2. This may be the 
main reason why MDBE degradation rates initially 
show a typical Arrhenius type response to reaction 
temperature. In fact, some yellowish reaction 
intermediates were observed to form on TiO2 
surfaces and these reaction intermediates could 
further decompose by extending the illumination 
period. Obviously, the relatively slower degradation 
rates of the certain reaction intermediate(s) 
retarded the overall reaction rate of MDBE, 
confirming that the surface reaction is the rate 
determining step. 
3.4. Reaction Intermediates 
For the formation of reaction intermediates from 
photocatalysis of MBDE, both enhancing water 
vapour content and reaction temperature can result 
in reduced formation of the intermediates. In this 
study, several reaction intermediates were 
identified, including diphenyl ether, 4-
methoxycinnamic acid, 2-ethylhexyl ester, butylated 
hydroxytoluene, benzoic acid, myristic acid 
isopropyl ester, benzene, bromoform and 
dibromomethane. Tables 1 and 2, respectively;

photocatalysis of MDBE by changing water vapour 
concentration or reaction temperature.  
 
4. Conclusion 
This investigation studied gas-phase photocatalytic 
degradation characteristics of monobromdiphenyl 
ether (MBDE) under the illumination of near-UV 
light. An anatase crystal-form photocatalyst, TiO2, 
was applied in the investigation. Several 
experimental parameters including water vapour 
concentration, oxygen concentration, and reaction 
temperature were tested. Some important results 
are summarized as follows: 
• MBDE degradation rate could be promoted in 
the presence of a suitable amount of water 
vapour, but it was inhibited by high 
concentrations. The degradation rate peaked 
at a water concentration of about 13400 μM. 
• A first-order reaction with respect to oxygen 
concentration was detected for the 
photocatalysis of MBDE at low oxygen 
concentration, but a zero-order reaction was 
observed after the adsorption saturation of 
oxygen. 
• The rate of MBDE degradation increased 
exponentially with reaction temperature, 
showing a typical Arrhenius relationship.  
• Both high water vapour concentration and 
high reaction temperature resulted in reduced 
formation of reaction intermediates.
