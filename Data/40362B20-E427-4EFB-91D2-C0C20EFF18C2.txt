Abstract 
 
Optical fibers and their devices are the most important elements in the communication and 
sensing systems. Optical fibers usually are coated with polymeric or hermetic coatings to 
maintain their high mechanical strength. However, the hermetic property of double-coated optical 
fiber is not good enough; the stripping ability of coatings of hermetically-coated optical fibers is 
worse, and the bending flexibility of nickel-sealed double-coated optical fibers is limited. To 
withstand the harsh environment, optical fibers are demanded to simultaneously owning good 
hermetic property, stripping ability of coatings, and bending flexibility. Therefore, it becomes an 
important issue to fabricate optical fibers owning these three properties. The goal of this project is 
to fabricate the novel carbon-sealed double-coated optical fibers during two years. In the first 
year, the polymer-like carbon-sealed double-coated optical fibers will be prepared by plasma 
enhanced chemical vapor deposition using different hydrogen (or hexafluoroethane)/hydrocarbon 
ratios. In the second year, the graphite-like carbon-sealed double-coated optical fibers will be 
prepared by plasma enhanced chemical vapor deposition using different argon/hydrocarbon ratios 
at a high radio frequency (RF) power. We are expecting to fabricate a novel optical fiber with the 
excellent hermetic property, stripping ability of coatings, and bending flexibility. 
In the first year, the temperature controlling system was established, and many experiments 
were executed. One of the experimental results shows that if the deposition temperature, working 
pressure, total mass flow rate, CH4/H2 ratio, and deposition time are 298 K (25 °C), 17 Pa (0.13 
torr), 25 sccm, 4, and 8 min, respectively, the polymer-like carbon-sealed double-coated optical 
fibers have the good hermetic property, stripping ability of coatings, and bending flexibility. In 
the second year, the RF plasma source system was established, and many experiments were 
executed. One of the experimental results shows that the graphite-like carbon-sealed 
double-coated optical fibers could be prepared by raising the RF power (600 W ~ 900 W). The 
graphite-like carbon-sealed double-coated optical fibers show the excellent hermetic property, 
stripping ability of coatings, and bending flexibility. Additionally, some of these results are 
published in seven SCI journal papers and nine conference papers. 
 
Keywords: Optical fiber, Amorphous carbon, Thin film materials, Chemical vapor deposition

1. 計畫緣由與研究目的 
自從 1970 年美國的康寧(Corning)玻璃公司公開宣佈可以作為光纖通訊媒介的低傳輸
損失玻璃光纖研製成功以後，光纖通訊系統有了一日千里的發展[1]。近年來由於資訊的爆
炸，加上多媒體通訊及全球網路的需求日益迫切，而光纖通訊可提供足夠的傳輸品質，所
以光纖通訊的需求便隨著各種通訊網路建設擴充、提升而快速成長，而光纖(Optical Fiber)
及其元件在光纖通訊中為主要的基本元件。光纖相較於傳統銅線電纜而言，具有下列優
點：高頻寬(High Bandwidth)、低損失(Low Loss)、體積小、重量輕、不受電磁波干擾
(Electromagnetic Interference)和保密性高等[14]，所以非常適合作為通訊的傳輸媒介。而
隨著科技的進步，光纖除了應用在傳送語音外，亦可以使用在傳送數據和影像方面。藉由
光纖通訊系統資料傳遞能力可達傳統銅線通訊電纜的萬倍以上，使得光纖的應用範圍涵蓋
了長途電話、局間中繼電路、海纜通訊系統、用戶迴路、視訊會議、區域網路、有線電視、
國防工業及工業儀器等。光纖通訊歷經數十餘年來的發展，整個長途電信網路的光纖化已
經完成。隨著世界各國興建資訊高速公路的熱潮以及我國政府的大力推動國家資訊基礎建
設，國內各電信、資訊通訊、甚至娛樂業者紛紛投入多媒體光纖網路市場。在有線電視開
放與電信自由化等因素的配合下，光纖通訊的時代正式在台灣揭開序幕，最後將以實現光
纖到家(Fiber To The Home, FTTH)進而推向光纖到桌(Fiber To The Desk, FTTD)[5]的終極
目標，由光纖提供整體的視覺的、聲音的、數據的整體服務。未來，光纖取代銅線的地位，
和人們生活關係密切，將是必然的趨勢。 
傳統光纖結構如圖 1(a)所示，其中玻璃光纖(Silica Glass Fiber)是以折射率較低的纖殼
(Cladding)包覆著中心部份為折射率較高之纖核(Core)，內外兩層之材料同為二氧化矽玻
璃。為了增加玻璃光纖的機械強度，一般在製造過程中都會在玻璃光纖外面再加上塑膠
被覆。塑膠被覆通常包括兩層，第一塑膠被覆層是採用楊氏模數較小的材料，以做為應
力緩衝層和降低光纖之微曲損失；而第二塑膠被覆層是採用楊氏模數較大的材料，用來
承受外在機械力並保護玻璃光纖以維持光纖之機械強度[1,2]。加上雙塑膠被覆之光纖擁
有不錯的拉力強度(56 GPa)和較小的微彎曲損失(Microbending Loss)。 
然而，塑膠被覆層對於長時間抵抗水氣入侵而維持光纖機械強度之抗疲勞性並非很
好。所謂抗疲勞性是指光纖在外界環境影響下而仍能保有長時間高機械強度的能力，一
般是以 n 值表示此種能力。n 值愈大，表示光纖保有長時間高機械強度之能力愈好，而一
般塑膠被覆光纖之 n 值約在 20 與 30 之間[6]。因此，為了再提高光纖之抗疲勞性，另一
種在玻璃光纖和第一塑膠被覆層之間再加上密封鍍層(Hermetic Coating)而擁有高 n 值的
密封鍍層光纖(Hermetically-Coated Optical Fiber)(圖 1(b))引起廣泛地研究，並被視為是光
纖通訊的重要技術之一[721]。

不同所引起的殘留應力會大幅的降低。目前已被考慮過的非金屬材料以碳(氮，氧)化物和
碳為主[1416]。這些材料的緻密性高及抗化學腐蝕良好，其中以非晶質碳具有不錯的機
械強度、抗水氣能力、高緻密性、化學惰性、高機械強度等特性[29,30]。密封鍍層光纖
在製造過程中（諸如：化學氣相沉積法、熱浸鍍法或其他方法），通常需要在相當高的製
程溫度[14,15,19]下才能進行。因為在玻璃光纖上製備密封鍍層時，其表面會產生電化學
反應，造成密封鍍層光纖的機械強度驟降至與傳統的雙塑膠被覆光纖幾乎相同甚至更低
[16,31]。除此之外，密封鍍層很難被剝除[32]，所以密封鍍層光纖在光電系統中較難與其
他光電元件直接連接。 
最近，申請人研製出鎳包覆雙塑膠被覆光纖(Nickel-Sealed Double-Coated Optical 
Fibers) (圖 1(c))，除了已提出中華民國專利申請外，並將成果發表於 Journal of Lightwave 
Technology[33]。鎳包覆雙塑膠被覆光纖是藉由無電鍍法在雙塑膠被覆光纖上製備鎳薄膜
所構成。鎳包覆雙塑膠被覆光纖具有良好的導電性、高的機械強度、高的水氣阻絕性，
和較佳的抗微彎曲損失(Microbending Loss)能力。此外，鎳包覆鍍層也可保護雙塑膠被覆
光纖不被損害，具有抵抗磨耗、附著性佳以及硬度高的特性，非常符合光纖應用所需的
必備條件。除此之外，鎳包覆雙塑膠被覆光纖之被覆層很容易被以機械力剝除[33]，所以
鎳包覆雙塑膠被覆光纖在光電系統中很容易直接與其他光電元件連接。當鎳膜的厚度為
68 nm 時，鎳包覆雙塑膠被覆光纖具有極佳的溫變抵抗性（熱穩定性）。鎳包覆雙塑膠被
覆光纖相較於傳統的雙塑膠被覆光纖和密封鍍層光纖，更適用於光通訊系統。然而，鎳
包覆雙塑膠被覆光纖的可撓曲直徑最低只能到 5 公分，小於 5 公分鎳包覆層就會產生微
裂縫[33]。 
以上三種光纖相對而言，傳統光纖(圖 1(a))的密封性較差，密封鍍層光纖(圖 1(b))的
鍍層剝除性較差，而鎳包覆雙塑膠被覆光纖(圖 1(c))的彎曲可撓性較差。為了因應將來更
為嚴苛與複雜的外在環境，光纖最好能同時兼具良好的密封性、鍍層剝除性及彎曲可撓
性。因此，研製一種除了光纖本身已有的性能外，並能兼具上述三項優點的新型光纖成
為一重要課題。 
碳材料在光纖密封鍍層的應用已經有一段時間了，而且碳鍍層光纖具有相當高的水氣
阻絕性[34]。碳材料本身可以 sp1、sp2 和 sp3鍵結形式存在，不同鍵結形式的碳也可能與氫
原子相互鍵結，所以相較其他元素，碳材料可鍵結生成多變的結構。在碳薄膜的範疇內，
就以原子排列的有序程度來分類，碳薄膜可以區分為結晶態碳膜或非晶質態碳膜。由於非
晶質碳膜缺少可提供水氣擴散的晶界以及結構排列緻密的特性，在眾多不同的碳材料結構
上，非晶質碳膜往往是作為碳密封鍍層的首要之選。在一般的化學氣相鍍膜技術中，含氫
非晶質碳薄膜(Hydrogenated Amorphous Carbon films, a-C:H films)是最容易獲得到的。依鍵
結組成來區分，大致可以分成三大類：(1)類鑽石碳薄膜(Diamond-Like Carbon films, DLC 
films)，結構中富有 sp3 碳碳鍵結，含量<30 at.%，硬度值>15GPa，物理與機械特性相近於
鑽石；(2)類石墨碳薄膜(Graphite-Like Carbon films, GLC films)，結構中富有 sp2 碳碳鍵結，
氫含量<30 at.%，硬度值<10GPa，薄膜硬度與光學能隙值較趨近於石墨結構；(3)類高分子
碳薄膜(Polymer-Like Carbon films, PLC films)，結構中氫含量>45 at.%。隨著 PLC films 的生
成，薄膜硬度會遠小於 10GPa，並且光學能隙值會逐漸攀升。依據主要形成 a-C:H films 的
鍵結組成分類可以表示成為一三相圖，如圖 2 所示[30]。由於碳材料三相圖的建立，更可以
輔助研究者對碳薄膜材料做更精準的判斷，以及薄膜特性的選用。

2. 研究方法 
本計畫第一年研究方法是利用電漿輔助化學氣相沉積系統來製備近類高分子碳包覆雙
塑膠被覆光纖。在設定的射頻功率、工作壓力、與氫氣(或六氟乙烷)/烷炔類(甲烷、乙炔、
丙烷、丁烷)混合氣體比例下，藉由不同的沉積時間來控制碳鍍層的厚度。分析量測主要包
括碳鍍層特性量測及近高類分子碳包覆雙塑膠被覆光纖特性量測兩大部分，其所使用的儀
器分述如下： 
A. 碳鍍層特性量測： 
(1) 厚度：場發射掃描式電子顯微鏡(國科會貴重儀器) 
(2) 表面形貌及粗糙度：原子力顯微鏡(國科會貴重儀器) 
(3) 微結構：拉曼散射光譜儀(本系公共儀器)、傅立葉轉換紅外線光譜儀(本系公共儀
器)、X 光光電子能譜儀(國科會貴重儀器) 
(4) 表面抗水性：水接觸角量測儀(本系公共儀器) 
(5) 楊氏模數及硬度：奈米壓痕測試儀(國科會貴重儀器)

Microbending losses in double-coated optical fibers caused by axial 
strain-induced creep deformation of polymeric coatings 
 
Yu-Chun Hsueh,1 Liang-Hsun Lai,1 Tzu-Fan Tseng,2 Jeng-Yue Wu,2 and Sham-Tsong Shiue1* 
 
1 Department of Materials Science and Engineering, National Chung Hsing University, Taichung 
402, Taiwan 
2 Department of Chemical Engineering, National Chung Hsing University, Taichung 402, Taiwan 
 
Abstract 
The viscoelastic behavior of commercial UV-cured polymeric coatings of optical fibers is 
evaluated using dynamic mechanic analysis. The results indicate that relaxation of stresses and/or 
strains in these coatings occurs by creep deformation. The axial strain-induced viscoelastic 
stresses in optical fibers are derived from the exact viscoelastic behavior of the polymeric 
coatings. Compressive radial stress on the glass fiber produces microbending losses, and 
therefore, microbending losses in double-coated optical fibers that are caused by axial 
strain-induced creep deformation of polymeric coatings are investigated. These microbending 
losses can be minimized by suitably selecting the thickness and physical properties of the 
polymeric coatings, as follows. The radius, Poisson’s ratio, and strain ratio of the primary coating 
should be increased, but the Young’s modulus and relaxation time of the primary coating should 
be decreased. The radius, Young’s modulus, Poisson’s ratio, and strain ratio of the secondary 
coating should be decreased, but the relaxation time of the secondary coating should be increased. 
When the thickness and physical properties of the polymeric coating are chosen to minimize the 
microbending loss, the thickness and Young’s modulus of the secondary coatings should be large 
enough to withstand an external mechanical force.  
 
Keywords: Optical fiber, Polymeric coating, Viscoelastic stress, Microbending loss.

Effects of ethylene/nitrogen mixtures on thermal chemical vapor deposition 
rates and microstructures of carbon films 
 
 
Liang-Hsun Lai, a Ke-Jie Huang, a Sham-Tsong Shiue, a,* Jing-Tang Chang, b and Ju-Liang He b 
 
a Department of Materials Science and Engineering, National Chung Hsing University, Taichung 
402, Taiwan 
b Department of Materials Science and Engineering, Feng Chia University, Taichung 407, Taiwan 
 
Abstract 
When ethylene/nitrogen (C2H4/N2) mixtures are used to deposit carbon films by thermal 
chemical vapor deposition (CVD), effects of C2H4/(C2H4 + N2) ratios on the deposition rate and 
microstructures of carbon films are investigated. Experimental results reveal that the deposition 
rate of carbon films increases with the C2H4/(C2H4 + N2) ratio, and also, raises with the residence 
time, deposition temperature, and working pressure. The kinetics of this thermal CVD process is 
discussed. The deposition rate of carbon films is proportional to the C2H4/(C2H4 + N2) ratio with a 
power of second order, which is resulted from the adsorption of remaining precursor gases C2H4 
on the silica glass plate substrate. Few nitrogen and hydrogen atoms are incorporated into carbon 
films. As the partial pressure of C2H4 is smaller than a threshold pressure or the residence time is 
shorter than a threshold residence time, no film is formed. The activation energy (= 448 kJ/mole) 
of carbon deposition is related to the activation energy of C2H4 dissociation. The degree of 
ordering and nano-crystallite size of carbon films decrease with increasing the C2H4/(C2H4 + N2) 
ratio, while the sp3 carbon atoms of carbon films increase. Finally, the results of thermal CVD 
carbon deposition using C2H4 are compared with those using methane and acetylene. 
 
Keywords: Carbon film, Thermal chemical vapor deposition, Ethylene, Microstructure.

Effects of radio-frequency powers on the properties of carbon coatings on 
optical fibers prepared by thermal chemical vapor deposition enhanced with 
inductively coupled plasma 
 
Liang-Hsun Lai a, Sham-Tsong Shiue a,*, Hung-Yi Lin b 
 
a Department of Materials Science and Engineering, National Chung Hsing University, 250 Kuo 
Kuang Road, Taichung 402, Taiwan 
b Mechanical and Systems Research Laboratories, Industrial Technology Research Institute, 
Hsinchu 310, Taiwan 
 
Abstract 
This study investigates the effects of different radio-frequency (rf) powers on the 
characteristics of carbon coatings on optical fibers that are prepared by thermal chemical vapor 
deposition (thermal CVD) enhanced with inductively coupled plasma (ICP). Methane and 
nitrogen were used as the precursor gases, and rf-powers of ICP were set to 0, 50, 100, 200, 300, 
and 400 W. The deposition temperature, working pressure, and deposition time in the thermal 
CVD process were set to 1248 K, 4 kPa, and 2 h, respectively. Experimental results indicate that 
the deposition rate of carbon coatings increases as the rf-power increases from 0 to 200W, but 
decreases as the rf-power exceeds 200 W. The mean crystallite size and ordered degree of carbon 
coatings decrease with increasing the deposition rate. Moreover, when the rf-power increases, the 
carbon coatings have more sp2 carbon atoms and shift to graphite-like. With the assistance of ICP, 
carbon coatings can be deposited by thermal CVD at a low working pressure (about 4 kPa). 
Furthermore, the deposition rate and film properties can be adjusted by the rf-power. 
 
Keywords: Carbon coating, Thermal chemical vapor deposition, Inductively coupled plasma, 
Microstructure.

Effects of acetylene/ammonia mixtures on the properties of carbon films 
prepared by thermal chemical vapor deposition 
 
Liang-Hsun Lai and Sham-Tsong Shiue* 
 
Department of Materials Science and Engineering, National Chung Hsing University, 250 Kuo 
Kuang Road, Taichung 402, Taiwan 
 
Abstract 
Effects of the C2H2/(C2H2+NH3) ratio on the properties of thermal chemical vapor 
deposition (CVD) carbon films are investigated. Experimental results indicate that the deposition 
rate of carbon films increases with increasing the C2H2/(C2H2+NH3) ratio. Ten NH3 molecules 
will suppress about one C2H2 molecule to form carbon films, and the deposition rate of carbon 
films is proportional to the partial pressure of acetylene with a power of fourth order. A model 
description of the kinetics of this thermal CVD process is proposed. Moreover, the ordering 
degree, nano-crystallite size, and the number of sp2 carbon sites decrease with increasing the 
C2H2/(C2H2+NH3) ratio, while the electrical resistivity increases. Few nitrogen atoms and no 
hydrogen is incorporated in the carbon films. When the carbon films are very thin, the carbon 
atoms would be uniformly deposited on a silica glass plate which results in surface smoothening. 
Nevertheless, when the carbon films are getting thicker, the surface roughness increases. As the 
C2H2/(C2H2+NH3) ratio increases, the decrease of the water contact angle on a carbon film 
surface is mainly resulting from the decrease of the number of sp2 carbon sites in the carbon films. 
The deposition temperature and working pressure of thermal CVD carbon films using the 
C2H2/N2 or C2H2/NH3 mixtures are smaller in comparison to those using a CH4/NH3 mixture. 
Usually, when the deposition temperature is above 1073 K, NH3 is decomposed into NH2, which 
would suppress the growth of carbon films. However, as the deposition temperature is below 
1073 K, NH3 serves as the dilute gas in the described processes. 
 
Keywords: Carbon film, Thermal chemical vapor deposition, Ammonia, Microstructure.
