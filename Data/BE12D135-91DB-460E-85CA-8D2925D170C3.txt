1.中文摘要 
本研究利用鎢極惰性氣體遮護電弧銲接方法(gas tungsten arc welding，
GTAW)，將不同比例的鉻與碳化鉻(Cr:C=4:1)金屬粉末填料銲覆在S45C碳鋼基材
表面上，研究不同高碳高鉻合金成份的顯微結構特性。一系列的高碳高鉻合金被
成功的銲覆在S45C碳鋼基材表面上。這些硬面合金被設計為了得到不同
(Cr,Fe)23C6與(Cr,Fe)7C3碳化物之亞共晶、近共晶與過共晶顯微結構。根據X-ray
繞射分析與顯微結構觀察結果顯示，亞共晶與近共晶之顯微結構包含二種
相:Cr-Fe固溶相(α)與(Cr,Fe)23C6碳化物。過共晶(3.8%C)之顯微結構由邊緣為
(Cr,Fe)23C6環繞中心為(Cr,Fe)7C3的初晶雙相碳化物與［α+(Cr,Fe)23C6］共晶結構
所構成。過共晶(5.9%C)之顯微結構包含初晶的(Cr,Fe)7C3碳化物與［α+(Cr,Fe)7C3］
的共晶結構，而初晶與共晶的(Cr,Fe)7C3碳化物具有兩種不同的型態，包含片狀
與棒狀的型態。不同硬面合金之硬度值會隨碳含量的增加而增加，且以具有大量
初晶(Cr,Fe)7C3碳化物之硬面合金D的硬度為最高，約為HRc63.9。 
Abstract 
A series of high-carbon Cr-based hard-facing alloys were successfully fabricated 
on a substrate of S45C steel by gas tungsten arc welding (GTAW) process using

銲覆後，其顯微結構包含初晶的M7C3碳化物與［α+ M7C3］的共晶結構時，銲覆
層具有較高的硬度值與優越的磨耗抵抗能力，因為M7C3碳化物是大家所熟知擁

良的抗腐蝕能力，已經廣泛被應用來當作銲覆層中的補強相[5-11]。 
鎢極惰性氣體遮護電弧銲是一項重要的硬面銲覆加工工具，尤其是在較小與
較精密的工件表面上，而且根據研究顯示[12-14]，利用鎢極惰性氣體遮護電弧銲
所得到的硬面層顯微結構具有較細的組織，可以提升銲覆層的硬度值與抗磨耗能
力。本研究利用鎢極惰性氣體遮護電弧銲接方法(GTAW)，將不同比例的鉻與碳
化鉻(Cr:C=4:1)金屬粉末填料銲覆在S45C碳鋼基材表面上。並對這些銲覆層作晶
體結構分析、顯微結構觀察、成份分析與硬度測試。

1. INTRODUCTION 
Silver oxide (Ag2O) has being used extensively in photography and in batteries (as an 
electrode). It was also reported that a single p-type Ag2O phase with a band gap of 1.2 eV  
may be deposited using sputtering techniques [1]. This film has been studied extensively for 
the applications in electrical, optical and magneto-optical data storage industries. During the 
past years, the silver oxide electrode applied as a battery material has been developed to 
achieve better performance in voltage regulation with longer storage life [2,3]. Silver oxide 
films (Ag2O and/ or AgO) have been fabricated over the past decade using several different 
techniques including magnetron sputtering [4–6], chemical bath deposition [7], ECR oxygen 
plasma assisted e-beam evaporation of Ag [8], and post-deposition processing of Ag surface 
using oxygen plasma [9–13]. According to Abe et al. [14], the silver oxide could exist in dual 
phases. In Abe’s report, Ag2O decomposed to Ag at 200–400℃. On the other hand, Chiu et al. 
and Chuang et al.[15] reported that AgO would begin to decompose into Ag2O and O2 at 160℃, 
while Ag2O would begin to decompose into Ag clusters and O2 around 380℃. Overall, the 
thermal stability of silver oxide along with their optical and electrical properties had not been 
systematically studied. 
In the present study, silver oxide thin films were prepared by DC-reactive magnetron sputtering 
at various deposition temperatures. Since the films are not thermally stable, this study makes 
an attempt to study the films’ band gap, electrical resistivity, and optical absorption, depending 
on the films’ deposition temperatures. 
2. EXPERIMENTAL METHODS 
Silver oxide films were deposited on Corning 1737 glass substrates by a DC magnetron 
sputtering system. Each target has a diameter of 50 mm, and parallel to the substrate holder. 
The distance of target-to-substrate was 100 mm. For deposition, the sputtering system was first 
pumped down to 7x10-4 Pa and then Ar gas (18 sccm) was introduced to fill the chamber up to

showed that the AgO phase may begin to decompose with the increase of deposition

Ag2O would begin to decompose into Ag clusters and O2 at about 380℃. 
3.3 Electrical and optical characteristics 
Figure 3 shows the optical transmittance and absorptance of the samples deposited with various 
oxygen flow rate at a temperature of 160℃. According to Figure 3a, when the wavelength is 
higher than 375 nm, it is observed that the transmittance increases with the increase of oxygen 
flow rate. Apparently, this is attributed to the transformation of Ag to Ag2O (hexagonal) under 
high oxygen flow rate. By examining the absorptance spectra (Figure 3b), it is found the 
obtained AgxO + Ag films have higher absorption than that of single phase Ag2O film within 
the range of 500 and 800 nm. The increased light absorption may be attributed to the 
inordering of structure, or the existence of Ag phase. Figure 4 shows the optical transmittance 
and absorptance of the films prepared at various deposition temperatures under a constant 
oxygen flow rate at 3.5 sccm. From Figure 4a, it can be seen that the transmittance is decreased 
with the increase of deposition temperature. Apparently, this is attributed to the transformation 
of Ag2O (hexagonal) to Ag+Ag2O (cubic). By examining the absorptance spectra (Figure 4b), 
an absorption peak is observed at 410-420 nm for the sample deposited at 400 ℃. Apparently, 
this is attributed to the nucleation and growth of Ag particles which may possibly create 
plasmonic resonance and enhance the absorption.
