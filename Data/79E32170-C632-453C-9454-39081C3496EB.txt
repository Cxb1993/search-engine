渦流產生器與多孔介質應用於電子元件熱傳增強技術之研究(II) 
         計畫編號：NSC 97－2221－E－211－018－ 
         執行期限：97 年 8 月 1 日至 98 年 7 月 31 日 
         計畫主持人：李弘毅  華梵大學機電工程學系 
         計畫參與人員：姜明宏、趙上茗、王博勳  華梵大學機電工程學系 
 
摘要 
 
本計畫以實驗方式探討在風洞系統內放
置多孔介質之熱傳增強效益。多孔介質具有
大比表面積與質量輕等特性，其較大的熱傳
面積與較佳的流體擾動，可提升電子元件之
熱傳效能。研究中在固定加熱量的條件下，
藉由改變多孔介質燒結粒徑大小與多孔介質
厚度等參數，以不同冷卻流體速度探討各參
數對熱傳之影響。實驗中量測測試元件溫
度，並與未加入多孔介質之情況比較，了解
應用多孔介質於電子元件冷卻之熱傳增強效
益。 
 
關鍵詞：熱傳增強、多孔介質、燒結銅、網
目 
 
Abstract 
 
  In this work, an experimental method is used 
to investigate the heat transfer enhancement by 
inserting the porous medium in a wind tunnel 
system. The porous medium possesses the 
characteristic of large specific surface area and 
less mass. Its larger heat transfer area and better 
fluid perturbation enhances the heat transfer 
and reduce the electronic component 
temperature. Under the fixed heating power, the 
effects of the mesh number of the sintered 
copper, the thickness of the porous medium, 
and the Reynolds number on the heat transfer 
are studied. The temperatures of the blocks 
with or without the porous medium are 
measured to understand the efficiency of the 
application of the porous medium on the 
cooling of the electronic components. 
 
Keywords：heat transfer enhancement, porous 
medium, sintered copper, mesh number 
 
一、緣由與目的 
 
長久以來空氣強制對流冷卻技術皆為電
子設備散熱技術之主流，其方便、便宜及可
靠等優勢為受大眾所青睞之主因。為了達到
提升冷卻效益的目的，常見加裝散熱片的使
用方式，其主要原理是將電子元件運作時所
產生的熱量經由傳導方式傳至散熱片，再利
用風扇等強制對流裝置驅動冷卻流體流經散
熱片進行熱交換，將熱藉由對流方式帶走，
以達到降低元件溫度的目的。然而此種散熱
方式必須要有足夠的空間配置散熱片與強制
對流設備等裝置，並容易衍生噪音與震動等
問題，因此，當印刷電路板上具有多個發熱
元件，卻又無法搭配散熱片進行散熱時，如
何有效增強局部的熱傳效果，遂成為值得深
入研究的問題。 
多孔介質具有大比表面積（Specific 
Surface Area）與質量輕等特性，其較大的熱
傳面積與較佳的流體擾動，使得多孔介質近
幾年來被大量應用於航太工業、燃料電池、
電子構裝與熱交換器等需要質傳或熱傳的環
境中。銀、銅、鋁、碳等金屬或非金屬材料
皆可製作成多孔介質產品。利用多孔介質來
提升電子元件之熱傳效能，降低電子元件溫
度，為一極為可行的冷卻技術。 
本計畫之研究主要探討多孔介質應用於
電子元件散熱，研究中改變多孔介質燒結粒
徑大小、多孔介質厚度與冷卻流體速度等參
數，探討對於電子元件熱傳效益的影響。研
究中以溫度量測裝置，量測電子元件溫度，
並與未加入多孔介質之情況比較，以了解應
用多孔介質於電子元件冷卻之熱流效益。

Tzeng等人[12]以實驗方式探討三種不同

Tzeng等人[13]持續利用實驗方式在矩形
流道中置入多孔材料進行研究，探討混合熱
對流情況下其冷卻性能。研究中探討燒結銅
粒徑、雷諾數和熱通量等參數對熱傳之影響
。由較小粒徑燒結成之燒結銅與流體有較大
的接觸面積，在固定熱通量時，整體溫度會
隨著孔隙率降低而下降。 
本計畫所探討之主題為多孔介質應用
於電子元件熱傳增強技術研究，為了增加冷
卻效益，計畫中於平板上設置多個發熱源，
模擬印刷電路板上電子元件工作時之溫度升
高情形，並放置多孔介質於發熱源週遭，藉
由多孔介質具有大比表面積、較高的熱傳導
係數及較佳的流體擾動等特性，來增進熱傳
效益。本研究改變多孔介質網目數、多孔介
質厚度與冷卻流體速度等，在固定加熱量
下，探討多孔介質對於電子元件熱傳效益之
影響。 
 
三、實驗設備與研究方法 
 
3.1 實驗設備 
本研究所使用之實驗設備包含有風洞系
統、加熱裝置、溫度量測裝置、流速量測裝
置及實驗模型等部份。其整體實驗設備架構
圖如圖 1 所示。 
風洞系統主要由風洞本體與吸入式鼓風
機所組成。風洞本體包含整流段與測試段兩
部分，測試段長度為 700mm，其截面尺寸為
200mm×90mm，用以模擬電子設備內部情
形，提供不受外界干擾的實驗環境。吸入式
鼓風機可使風洞系統產生穩定的氣體流速，
並可藉由變頻控制裝置來調整轉速。鼓風機
使用CHUAN-FAN公司的TB-201 機型，最大
可提供 14m3/min的空氣流量，最大靜壓為
170mmAq ，鼓風機馬達的最高轉速為
2800RPM。 
加熱裝置包含直流電源供應器、加熱塊
和絕熱層，加熱塊材料為銅C1100，其熱傳導
係數為 391.1W/m°C ，加熱塊尺寸則為
30mm×30mm×96mm。為了有效絕熱，使熱
量往加熱塊上方進行傳遞，加熱塊周圍包覆
30mm厚的絕熱層，是由台達化學公司所生產
密度為 24kg/m3之絕熱玻璃棉（ Insulation 
Glasswool ），該絕熱棉之熱傳導係數僅
0.037W/m°C。直流電源供應器採用GW公
司、型號GPR-6030D，TOPTEK公司、型號
TK-2303D與ABM公司、型號 9303D的電源供
應器，分別調整電源供應器以提供每個加熱
塊均為約 5.63W的相同功率。 
流速量測裝置之作用為測量冷卻流體進
入風洞測試段之流速，因風洞系統之流速是
由鼓風機轉速頻率來控制，因此須先利用流
速量測裝置進行測量，以調整出各流速所對
應之鼓風機轉速。實驗中使用 Furness 
Controls 公司生產，型號為 FCO510 的微電腦
微壓流速計（Low Pressure Microprocessor 
Micromanometer），搭配皮托靜壓管（Pitot 
Static Tube）使用，其量測的流速最高可達
18m/s，並具有 0.25%之精確度。 
溫度量測裝置是由熱電偶與資料擷取器
所構成，用以量測實驗模型中之測試元件表
面溫度及風洞入口之冷卻流體溫度。實驗中
使用線徑 0.127mm 的 T 型熱電偶搭配
YOKOGAWA 公司製造，型號為 DC100 之資
料擷取器，其具有 0.5 秒快速掃瞄取樣，可
量測電壓、電流、溫度及應變等物理量，所
擷取之數據經由 RS-232 介面傳至電腦，可配
合套裝軟體進行分析。 
實驗模型包含測試元件與多孔介質。測
試元件之尺寸為 30mm×30mm×4mm，材料與
加熱塊相同，皆為銅C1100。其下方與加熱塊
接觸，使加熱塊所產生的熱能直接傳遞到測
試元件，並在測試元件頂部表面分別埋置四
個T型熱電偶，用以量測表面平均溫度Tave，
位置如圖 2 所示。多孔介質為Ecka-granules
公司[14]編號 89/11 AK的磷青銅(Phosphor 
Bronze)銅粒，並經由燒結製成多孔介質實驗
模型。實驗中分別使用 2mm及 4mm厚度的多
孔介質，並選用四種銅粒規格，如表 1 所示。
網目數 [15]是篩選銅粒所用的篩網之網目大
小，會影響所選用的銅粒大小尺寸。圖 3 為
相同尺寸下實驗中所使用的不同多孔介質實
在冷卻流體速度達到 Re=40000 時，多孔
介質對測試元件溫度的影響，如圖 8 所示。
由圖中顯示，此時除 t=4mm、網目數 170-250
的多孔介質(PM4)，其餘的多孔介質改變網目
數已無法增加熱傳效益，不論放置何種網目
數或厚度之多孔介質，皆會使整體效果變
差。表示隨著冷卻流體流速的增加，增加網
目數對測試元件熱傳改善效果已經趨於平
緩。第二列測試元件的溫度變化趨勢與
Re=30000 時相似，放置網目數 10-20(PM1)
與網目數 30-40(PM2)之多孔介質使得元件溫
度高於未放置多孔介質情況，此乃因前方測
試元件經對流作用所帶走的熱量，在流道後
方累積，導致溫度上升。 
在冷卻流體速度增加至 Re=50000 時，多
孔介質對測試元件溫度的影響，如圖 9 所
示。與 Re=40000 的測試元件溫度相比，此速
度下網目數10-20(PM1)與網目數 30-40(PM2)
之多孔介質造成的溫度上升幅度更大。因為
此時對流效果相對較好，放置多孔介質反而
會阻礙流體直接對測試元件散熱，導致散熱
較差。只有在放置網目數 170-250(PM4)且較
厚(t=4mm)之多孔介質，測試元件溫度略低於
無多孔介質時，其餘多孔介質網目數的情況
下測試元件溫度皆高於無放置多孔介質時。
這是因為網目數 170-250 之多孔介質(PM4)
其等效熱傳係數最大且與測試元件接觸面積
最大，熱傳效果最佳，而放置其他較小網目
數之多孔介質反而會使測試元件溫度上升。 
在冷卻流體速度增加至 Re=60000 時，多
孔介質對測試元件溫度的影響，如圖 10 所
示。由圖中可以看出多孔介質網目數改變對
測試元件溫度之影響幅度與 Re=50000 時相
似，僅放置 t=4mm 網目數 170-250 之多孔介
質(PM4)的情況下，測試元件溫度比起無放置
多孔介質時會有些微的下降，其餘情況放置
多孔介質時測試元件之溫度均較無放置多孔
介質時為高，加入多孔介質反而使測試元件
熱傳效益變差。因此在高雷諾數的流體速度
下不宜置入多孔介質，以節省成本，並避免
熱傳效益變差。網目數 170-250 的多孔介質
不論在低雷諾數或是高雷諾數情況下，增加
厚度皆能使散熱效益增加。 
 
五、結論 
 
本研究在風洞系統中，探討冷卻流體的
雷諾數、多孔介質網目數與多孔介質厚度等
參數，對於整體測試元件熱傳效能之影響。
由實驗之結果，歸納成以下幾點結論： 
1. 冷卻流體流速對多孔介質散熱有著絕對
的影響。當冷卻流體流速增加，明顯的
造成測試元件溫度的下降，但是隨著流
速增加，溫度下降的趨勢也會逐漸趨
緩，代表冷卻流體流速對多孔介質散熱
的熱傳效益有其極限存在。 
2. 當多孔介質厚度 t=2mm 時，在低冷卻流
速(Re=10000)的情況下，有優於無多孔
介質時之散熱效果，且隨著多孔介質網
目數增加，效果越好。而在 Re=20000
時，只有最大網目數 170-250 之多孔介
質其熱傳效果優於無多孔介質的情形。
當 Re≥30000 後，無論加入何種網目數
之多孔介質均無法有效降低測試元件溫
度，多數情況下甚至造成熱傳變差。 
3. 在多孔介質厚度 t=4mm 下，於較低冷卻
流體流速 (Re=10000) 時，放置多孔介
質熱傳效果皆明顯優於無放置多孔介
質，且隨著網目數增加，效果也越好。
但是在 Re=20000 及 30000 時，只有網目
數60-80與170-250之多孔介質熱傳效果
優於無放置多孔介質的狀態。其餘冷卻
流體流速下，僅有網目數 170-250 的多
孔介質散熱效果優於無多孔介質時。 
4. 在低流速(Re=10000)下，放置多孔介質
均能有效增加散熱效益使溫度降低。但
是隨著冷卻流速增加，網目數較少與厚
度較低的多孔介質反而會造成溫度上
升，流速越大則越大的網目數之多孔介
質能有較佳的散熱效益，可降低測試元
件表面溫度。本研究中多孔介質網目數
170-250、厚度 t=4mm 時，不論在各種
流速下，皆有最佳的散熱效益。

Keywords : Impingement cooling; Plate-fin heat sink; Thermal resistance; Infrared thermography. 
 
 
ABSTRACT 
   This work experimentally studies the performance of 
plate-fin heat sinks under impingement cooling by adjusting the 
impinging Reynolds number, the impingement distance, and the 
fin dimensions. The results show that the heat transferred by the 
heat sink increases with the impinging Reynolds number. The 
performance can be improved significantly even at low 
impinging Reynolds number. However, the improvement 
becomes indistinct as the impinging Reynolds number increases. 
The thermal resistance declines as the impingement distance 
increases, and is minimal at Y/D=20 for various impinging 
Reynolds numbers. Additionally, the thermal resistance 
increases as the impingement distance increases further. 
Increasing the fin width can effectively reduce the thermal 
resistance. However, as the fin width increases beyond a 
particular value, the thermal resistance increases dramatically. 
Reducing the thermal resistance by increasing the fin height 
depends on a suitable impinging Reynolds number and fin 
width. Therefore, the effect of the fin height is weaker than that 
of the impinging Reynolds number or the fin width. 
 
1. Introduction 
As semiconductor technology has developed rapidly, the 
performance and the power of electronic components have 
improved. The need for light and miniature products has led to 
high heat fluxes in electronic components. Therefore, electronic 
cooling has become a critical problem. Cooling technology for 
high-density electronic components has been extensively 
discussed in recent years. Although many methods have been 
proposed, such as phase-change cooling, thermoelectric cooling 
and liquid cooling, the cooling method that combines the 
impinging jet with the heat sink offers the advantage of rapid, 
direct and local cooling. It provides an excellent heat transfer 
mechanism in a limited space, making it effective in the cooling 
of electronic components. This cooling approach has been 
studied by many earlier works. Ledezma (1996) addressed the 
optimization of the heat transfer from pin fins under an 
impinging air flow; they elucidated correlations for optimal 
fin-to-fin spacing and maximum thermal conductance. Sathe 
(1997) studied fluid motion and heat transfer from a pin-fin heat 
sink under an impinging flow. The results indicated that the 
temperature of the substrate was low directly under the nozzle 
and increased in the outward direction. Brignoni (1999) 
considered the heat transfer of a confined jet flow that impinges 
on a pin-fin heat sink; using a fixed nozzle-to-target spacing, 
they varied the flow speed, the diameter of the nozzle and the 
nozzle arrays and determined that at a fixed air flow rate, a 
nozzle with a smaller diameter provided a higher impinging 
velocity and a lower thermal resistance. Maveety (2000, 2002) 
presented experimental and numerical results on the 
performance of pin-fin heat sinks under impingement cooling. 
They revealed that the fin dimensions strongly affected the 
thermal resistance. Additionally, the effect of the impingement 
distance on the thermal performance decreased as the Reynolds 
number was increased. Sathe (2004) studied the optimized 
performance of the plate-fin heat sink under a rectangular 
impinging jet. Their results indicated that cutting the fins under 
the impingement zone reduced the pressure drop without 
sacrificing the heat transfer. Bhopte (2004) adopted a 
commercial package to study the heat transfer of a plate-fin heat 
sink under impinging air cooling. The Nusselt number was 
found to depend on the Reynolds number, the Rayleigh number, 
the nozzle diameter, the impingement distance and the heat sink 
geometry. Shah (2006) numerically investigated the effects of 
the cuts in the end fins, the fin number, and the fan hub on the 
performance of the plate-fin heat sink with impingement 
cooling. Thermal and hydrodynamic performance improved as 
material was cut from the end fins. Increasing the number of 
fins slightly improved thermal performance and markedly 
increased pressure drop. 
A review of the literature indicates that the cooling 
performance of electronic devices can be improved by 
combining the impinging jet with a heat sink. However, only a 
limited amount of work is available on plate-fin heat sinks 
under impingement cooling. The aim of this work is to study 
experimentally the performance of plate-fin heat sinks under 
  
impingement distance (Y/D), the fin width (W/L) and the fin 
height (H/L). These parameters are set to Re = 5000, 10000, 
15000, 20000, 25000, Y/D = 4, 8, 12, 16, 20, 24, 28, W/L = 
0.08125, 0.1, 0.11875, 0.1375, 0.15625, and H/L = 0.375, 
0.4375, 0.5, 0.5625, 0.625.  
 
4.1 Effect of impinging Reynolds number on thermal 
resistance of heat sink 
   Three heat sinks with a fixed fin width of W/L=0.11875 and 
various fin heights of H/L=0.375, 0.5 and 0.625 at an 
impingement distance of Y/D=8 are adopted to investigate the 
effect of the Reynolds number of the impinging jet on the 
thermal resistance of the heat sinks, as presented in Fig. 3. The 
experimental data show that the thermal resistance of the three 
heat sinks with different fin heights declines by an average of 
34.96% as the impinging Reynolds number is increased from 
5000 to 10000. This result indicates that the performance is 
clearly improved even when the Reynolds number of the 
impinging jet is low. As the impinging Reynolds number is 
increased to 15000, 20000 and 25000, the thermal resistance 
slowly declines to average values of 20.22%, 18.15% and 
18.02%, respectively. Restated, the effect of the impinging 
Reynolds number on the thermal resistance is limited. 
The thermal resistance of a heat sink with a fin height 
H/L=0.375 at Re=25000 is close to that of a heat sink with 
H/L=0.625 at Re=15000. Therefore, the heat sink with higher 
fins can be introduced not only to reduce the pumping power of 
the impinging jet but also to meet the cooling requirement. 
 
4.2 Effect of impingement distance on thermal resistance of 
heat sink 
A change in the impingement distance alerts not only the 
range of spread of the impinging jet but also the velocity of the 
flow as it reaches the heat sink. Therefore, the impinging 
Reynolds number and the impingement distance together, 
determine the heat transfer efficiency. The experimental results 
in Fig. 4 indicate that, when the heat sink (W/L = 0.1, H/L = 
0.5) is used with various impinging Reynolds numbers and 
impingement distances, the thermal resistance declines as the 
impingement distance increases and the thermal resistance is 
lowest at Y/D =20. However, the thermal resistance increases 
with the impingement distance beyond this threshold (Y/D 
=20). 
Although the optimal impingement distance was found to be 
Y/D=20, the maximum decreased ratio in the thermal resistance 
as the impingement distance is varied varies with the impinging 
Reynolds number, as presented in Fig. 5. When the impinging 
Reynolds number is less than 10000, the maximum decreased 
ratio in the thermal resistance is caused by increasing the 
impingement distance from Y/D=4 to Y/D=8. The maximum 
decreased ratio in the thermal resistance is associated with 
increasing the impingement distance from Y/D=8 to Y/D=12 
when Re is 15000. Finally, when Re exceeds 20000, the 
decreased ratio in the thermal resistance is maximized by 
increasing Y/D from 16 to 20.

4.3.1 Effect of fin width on thermal resistance of heat sink 
The fin width affects the interfin spacing of the heat sink, 
and the interfin spacing is critical in determining whether the 
impinging jet can enter the heat sink easily to exchange heat or 
not. Figure 6 shows the effect of the fin width on the thermal 
resistance for various fin heights and impinging Reynolds 
numbers with Y/D =8. When Re is 5000 and H/L is 0.375 or 0.5, 
the effect of the fin width on the thermal resistance becomes 
weaker as W/L increases from 0.08125 to 0.11875. The thermal 
resistance is lowest at W/L=0.11875, and increases as W/L 
continues to increase further. Additionally, the thermal 
resistance is more affected by the fin width when Re is 5000 
and H/L is 0.625. The thermal resistance is lowest at W/L = 
0.1375. As Re increases to 10000, the fin width only slightly 
affects the thermal resistance for any of the three fin heights 
(H/L = 0.375, 0.5 and 0.625) when W/L is in the range 0.08125 
to 0.11875. Furthermore, the thermal resistance is lowest at 
W/L=0.1375 for all fin heights. As Re increases further to 
25000, the thermal resistance reaches its minimum at W/L=0.1, 
and is largely independent of the fin width for the smallest fin 
height H/L =0.375. However, the effect of the fin width on the 
thermal resistance is greater at other fin heights (H/L = 0.5 and 
0.625); the thermal resistance is lowest at W/L=0.11875.
