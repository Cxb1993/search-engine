Preface 
全球各地籠罩氣候變遷衝擊之下，皆釀成嚴重災情及人員生命財產損傷。近
年氣候變遷下所釀成的極端氣候，多以過冷、過熱或者暴雨、乾旱等類型出現。
為減少氣候變遷對自然或人類系統所造成的風險，需要更多減緩（mitigation）
或調適（adaptation）的行動來因應（IPCC, 2007）。「減緩」寄望於衝擊盡量不要
發生，盡可能降低可能需要因應的衝擊程度，並避免毀滅性的衝擊；「調適」則
是在災害已無可避免的情況下，針對不同類型衝擊做好整備以降低災損（葉欣誠，
2010；蕭代基，2008）。其中，水患問題更是日趨嚴重，即使水利工程技術先進
的歐洲國家也不例外，學界針對水患問題進行相關評估與研究報告，認為土地與
河川不當開發使用等人為因素造成水患越來越嚴重，同時，全球性的氣候變遷使
得降雨更難以預測，其防洪設施基準也縮小期間距。至今全球已經有許多調適策
略正在進行，例如提高堤河道防等工程性減災措施。但對於氣候變遷下，突然的
暴雨經常超出預設的防洪頻率標準，築堤束洪方式已難以有效遏止水患發生。相
對的，一連串的水災問題亦間接反映以結構式減災為主的防洪策略實難以對抗未
來毀滅性的災害。因此，非結構性的防洪策略配合地方的地理環境與都市特質，
共同打造一座「與水共存」的城市已是一個必然需要推動的工作。 
面對水患問題，許多的檢討都指向自然環境變遷及土地與河川過度開發。自
然環境部分，由於氣候變遷影響，環境脆弱度（vulnerability）增加，災害事件
不斷頻傳。人為部分，都會區過度開發與土地使用型態的轉變，包含土地超額開
發、都會區不透水面積增加導致逕流量大幅減少，或抽取地下水造成地層下陷等。
目前，已有非常多的研究提出透過國土規劃來重新治理淹水問題，但卻鮮少探討
地方政府應如何運用城市之地理特質來面對水患？又該有何種調適策略？因此，
本研究欲以埤塘做為主體，期望：一、檢視淹水潛勢危險地區，藉由其地形環境
特質與調整策略之運用，運用網絡方式減緩水患災害之衝擊；二、思考水的流動
特性應如何結合都市開放空間，提供防災治理之對策；三、建立以埤塘做為非結
構性防洪調適策略的可能方法。

1.3.2 降雨及暴雨管理 
    在 20 世紀前，許多國家都依賴降雨而儲存水來使用，而到目前為止，許多
未開發地區或是鄉村地區仍依賴著雨水的儲存來做為灌溉或生活使用，以及調節
洪患。時至今日，過往所開墾的雨水儲存設施設置仍有其必要性，與河川整修一
樣，是因應因開發而產生變化的雨水之流出機構的一種方法。有關雨水儲存設施
的計劃，不僅要考量到流出調節功能，也必須從再次認識開發、治水，以及與河
畔環境有關的現狀來開始。因此如何降低洪峰量體之綜合治水對策應被同時考量，
其中雨水截留系統設置( rainwater harvesting system)於國內外之評估，結果皆認
為對都市洪峰有抑制效益，其可減輕地表逕流及延緩洪峰集中時間（陳賜賢）。 
    美國土木工程師學會（ASCE, American Society of Civil Engineers）於 1985
年提出傳統對雨水之處置法係將城市暴雨帶來之雨水經由人工下水道，快速地往
下游方向排送，故可能將天然水路改建成較大型之人工渠道；但是正確的雨水經
營法則儘量將暴雨之雨水滯流(detention)、截流(retention)在地表與輔助入滲
(infiltration)至地下，使得該集水區下游之天然的小水路可予保留使用，不須改建
成較大的人工水路，該方法也符合「生態工法」或近自然工法之法則。雨水滯流
係提供空間，暫時容納雨水，俾減下游的逕流量之尖峰值但增長逕流延時。雨水
截流則係攔截及貯蓄雨水，以供利用、入滲至地下或自然地蒸發回大氣中（王茂
興，2003）。

1.4 台灣埤塘相關研究及特性 
1.4.1  桃園縣境內埤塘之特性 
埤塘不僅反映先民開拓定居的過程，同時也呈現人類依附自然，與大地互動
共存的例證。埤塘的型態區分為上築的築堤式陂塘以及下挖的開挖式埤塘。築堤
式陂塘指山間及丘陵地堵住山谷所建造之埤塘；開挖式埤塘係指在平地的窪地周
圍築堤所建造之埤塘。這兩種埤塘的水質、水生植物與棲息生物依其立地條件不
同而有所差異，但皆可藉由溝圳及地形高低差達成灌溉和調節功能（農田水利會
聯合會，2006）。所以，原有的埤塘設計是以水利機能為主。 
桃園縣因台地內所流經的溪流短促，雨水貯留時間甚短，故居民多鑿地築池，
利用天然雨水貯存以供灌溉，最高曾有多達 8,846個埤塘，素有「千塘之鄉」之
名，目前僅剩下約 2851 口。埤塘做為平時儲水的場所，實可避免旱災的發生；
再者，每逢颱洪或豪雨，倘若供應水源的水庫其水質混濁或淨水場無法正常處理
原水，所導致民生用水暫停供應亦可利用埤塘的留水功能來儲水以備用。亦即，
在颱風豪雨來臨前，若可將水庫內乾淨的水源預先引至選址埤塘內進行蓄存，當
發生水庫水源因濁度過高而無法取用時，則可將埤塘所蓄之水源引至淨水場，以

淹水潛勢模擬部分，本研究之降雨雨型分析採用簡單尺度不變性高斯馬可夫
雨型（Simple Scaling Gauss–Markov, 簡稱 SSGM）建立，進行定量降雨 24小時
累計雨量 150~1200公釐之淹水潛勢模擬，為一符合隨機碎形特性與高斯馬可夫
歷程的無因次（dimensionless）雨型（鄭克聲等，1999）。SSGM雨型（許恩菁，
1999）以非定常性一階高斯馬可夫歷程（nonstationary first–order Gauss–Markov 
process）描述無因次年最大值事件，具備馬可夫歷程特性，滿足尖峰降雨量統計
特性，且具有最大概似度，其優點為： 
 符合尖峰降雨百分比之統計特性。 
 降雨量之時間分佈與年最大暴雨事件之歷程特性一致。 
 雨型因暴雨類型而異。 
 經適當之尺度轉換後，雨型可適用於不同延時之設計暴雨。 
 建立雨型所使用之降雨事件與建立降雨強度延時–頻率曲線所使用之降
雨事件大致相同。

2.2  Flood Risk Management 
A number of studies have been conducted in the field of flood risk management(e.g., De 
Bruijn, 2005; Zerger and Wealands, 2004; Klijn et al., 2004; Hall et al., 2003; Plate, 2002; 
Saul, 1992; Rossi et al., 1994). The research results are divided into 4 categories: the 
identification of natural and social factors in flood risks; basin-based flood case investigation 
coupled with analysis of local natural and social conditions; establishment of flood risk 
models; and correlation between flood risks and structural and nonstructural measures, and 
actions and methods for risk diversification devised from it. The 4th category is further 
divided into structural and nonstructural measures. The structural measures refer to the 
defense against or elimination of floods, prevention or reduction of losses, whereas the 
nonstructural measures are the adjustment of human activities to mitigate floods. In general, 
structural measures require extensive funding, and often bring dramatic changes in existing 
landscapes as well as an illusion of safety that may come true, as floods that go beyond the 
design of waterways or dikes are frequent. The nonstructural measures are divided into 2 
subcategories: one is the changes in adaptability to floods or resistance to losses, which 
includes training, improvement of disaster prevention awareness and capability, flood plain 
management, pre-flood season preparation and response plans, flood warning and alarms, 
flood regulation, flood retention, flood water storage, land use configuration, and soil 
geologic engineering; the other is the changes in loss sharing patterns, including 
community-based disaster prevention actions, flood response and flood insurance. Further, the 
risk diversification focuses on the distribution of risk or losses among various stakeholders, 
and this is one of very important means for risk treatment in risk management. There are 2 
major methods, one being to break down the source of risks into manageable pieces, and the 
other being the reduction of losses to risk receivers. This can be the transfer in time and space 
through engineering means, or a full-scale analysis on reduction of flood prevention and 
mitigation facilities that are currently existing from the perspective of bring out the best of 
disaster mitigation resources, or it can be the transfer of objects exposed to floods, utilization 
or facility layout for land use, or reduction of losses to risk receivers through insurance. 
2.3  Stormwater Management and Regulation 
The new flood prevention and mitigation thinking in recent years is to see floods from 
the concept of risk management, adjust the relationship between human and water and 
consider what role rainwater plays though the ideas of resources, i.e. turning from “flood 
control” to “stormwater management.”

flood control as standard. In Taiwan, the definition of Central Weather Bureau for storm (or

infiltration, treatment and reuse of runoffs instead of handling rainwater in a simple and rapid 
fashion. This practice has the benefits of reducing flood impacts to cities, removal of 
pollutants, enhancement and protection of natural environment, as well as helps reduce the 
risks in traditional stormwater management (Elliott and Trowsdale, 2007; Waters et al., 2003).

The idea and method are to increase the detention of water in an urban setting, absorb 
stormwater in an urban area, slow down runoffs and decrease and delay the rainwater flows 
with natural environment and infrastructures, which provides greater flexibility for discharge. 
The similar idea and method are observed in the ponds developed by early people in Taoyuan 
County when they established their settlements.  However, these ponds can evolve further 
from reservoirs to flood detention and regulation, and that will probably provide a chance to 
minimize impacts of draughts and floods to local people, and it is the issues to be investigated 
in this study.   
FPs of Taoyuan County were investigated for the feasibility of using them as tools for 
flood regulation. Geographically, Taoyuan County slopes down from southeast to northwest. 
The southeast part is mostly hills, terraces and mountains ranging from 300m to 2,000m or 
more in altitude. The further to the southeast we go, the higher the terrains are; the terrains are 
flatter in the northwest and consist of mostly hills and alluvial terraces ranging from 320m at 
southeast to 30m at northwest. The geography is mostly mountains (34%), terrace (50%) and 
coastal alluvial plains (16%). The blue ribbon system comprises of coasts, rivers, irrigation 
systems consisting of FPs, and reservoirs. At the coasts, there are approximately 43km of 
coastlines and protection forest; rivers are short and radiating outwards, and they are local 
rivers of drainage (Figure 2.). 
Due to the terrace geography of Taoyuan, early people stored rainwater for irrigation by 
exploiting the natural geography and geology. There were once as many as 8,846 FPs in 
Taoyuan and that won the name “country of thousand ponds.” However, there are only 2,836 
of them left, and they are scattered very evenly across the local terraces (Figure 2.; 錯誤! 找

The supply and demand of water resource and water quality are put to the test whenever 
there is a flood. Facing the impacts coming from climate change, the world is doing its best to 
find out response strategies, and the efforts have won supports from both governmental and 
private sectors. For extreme weathers or strong downpours that are possibly the results of 
climate change, it is necessary to develop strategies that fit local conditions, and the overall 
environment has to be re-evaluated in order to come up with the suitable regulation solutions. 
For Taoyuan County, FPs are important assets that need to be connected and put into better 
use. Therefore, the focus of this study is to investigate: are the existing FPs fit for flood 
detention and retention? How are we going to put them into good use? And what are there for 
us to do? These are the three aspects of investigation:  
(1) Knowledge of the relationship between local environmental information and flooding 
potential: field surveys were conducted to learn the current status of FPs in Taoyuan 
and find out their relationship through inundation potential simulation; investigation 
was carried out to collect information about the number and size of these FPs, 
current use of surrounding lands and their actual locations, and the connections 
between trenches were studied to realize how the water levels are related and how 
the level differences are regulated; and flood simulations were performed with 
different daily precipitations at 150, 300, 450, 600, 900 and 1,200mm.   
(2) Investigation of relationship between FPs and floods and the application of flood 
remediation: the network connection between the FPs and trenches across Taoyuan 
County and the flood history were investigated.   
(3) Localized regulation strategy and new thinking of nonstructural flood prevention 
measures: the local geographic environment was combined to propose a regulation 
strategy that fits the local conditions. 
As a result, field research was conducted to find out the current status of FPs, 
information was compiled and combined with GIS for spatial evaluation and analysis, and 
finally image overlay was introduced to interpret the results.   
Literature review consists of the definition of research topic and the review of literatures 
related to FPs. First, it was investigated how regulation strategies and associated cases were 
developed under the impacts of climate changes in previous studies in order to strengthen the 
basis and theoretical foundation of the regulation strategy. For field research, current status 
investigation was conducted on the FPs in Taoyuan County. The field research is a research 
method that allows researcher for in-depth knowledge by providing them an opportunity to go 
into the social phenomenon they are studying or to observe some things and objects. In 
addition, in-depth interviews were performed in the hope that the interviewees could provide 
information related to the study, such that related to the open spaces in Taoyuan County. The 
face-to-face interviews told us what the interviewees thought or how they described the 
conditions.

4.1  Inundation Potential

tidal information, the 40m×40m numeric topographic elevation maps of Taoyuan were 
collected for the need of inundation potential simulation. Also, the inundation potential areas 
of daily precipitation of 150~1200mm were mapped for Taoyuan County. It is found in the 
study that primary flooding areas are coastal towns.  In addition, the areas along some

with concentration along rivers, particularly along Nankang River. Therefore, flooding is

Guanyin. In total, all 4 coastal towns of Taoyuan are prone to flooding, covering a very large

5.3  Investigation of Farm Pond Carrying Capacity of Precipitation Percentage:  
As the accumulated precipitation increased from 150mm to 1,200mm, FPs’ carrying 
capacity of precipitation within the inundation potential area increased slowly from 190% to 
716%. On the other hand, for FPs within 500m outside of the inundation potential area, the 
percentage increased from 805% to 1,592% and then to 3,081% when the accumulated 
precipitation increased from 600mm to 1,200mm.  This rapid increase might come from the 
sheer number and density of FPs in the inundation potential area.  With each of these FPs at 
the center, there were overlaps in a radius of 500m. Therefore, there were plenty of FPs in the 
neighborhood of flooded areas for flood detention, and that may attribute to this rapid 
increase.   
6.  Conclusions  
The following conclusions were reached based on the data analysis and discussions 
above:  
(1) FPs serve as water storage facilities to prevent draughts;  
(2) The water storage function of the FPs may provide a relief for utility water shortage 
whenever there is a typhoon or storm that causes murky water in the water-supplying 
reservoirs or renders water purification facilities unable to process raw water. That is, 
if the clean water in reservoirs is discharged into selected FPs for storage before a 
typhoon or storm is going to strike, the water stored in these FPs can be directed to 
water purification plants for emergency relief when the turbidity is so high in 
reservoirs that the water is not usable.   
(3) The analysis shows that the FPs located within the inundation potential area still 
functions as flood detention facilities no matter the accumulated precipitation is 
150mm or as high as 1,200mm; i.e. FPs are tools for risk allocation.   
(4) Once the FPs in the inundation potential area are saturated, channels and trenches 
can be used to direct excessive rainwater to neighboring FPs for further relief of 
stormwater pressure.   
The study is an ongoing effort. Next, storm drains, local drainage systems and other 
drainage facilities will be included for consideration to allow more accurate knowledge of 
inundation potential and the investigation of actual benefits that the FPs can provide.
