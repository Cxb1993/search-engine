Results and discussion 
Within 35 min, >90% of coated copper on surfaces of scrap ITO glass was extracted into the RTIL-I. 
By TEM, the grain sizes of extracted copper from scrap ITO glass in the RTIL-I are about 80 and 90 nm, 
respectively (Figures 1(a) and (b)). In Figure 1(c), the crystal plane calculated diffraction pattern shows 
that nanosize Cu are suspended in the RTIL-I. Differently, by diffraction pattern, nanosize CuI is an 
α–phase of cubic structure in the RTIL-I (see Figure 1(d)). 
The XANES spectrum of coated copper on surfaces of scrap ITO glass (Figure 2(a)) shows the main 
features at 8982, 8996 and 9003 eV, which suggest the existence of metallic copper. However, the 
pre-edge XANES spectra (in Figures 2(b)) of extracted copper from scrap ITO glass into the RTIL-I 
exhibited 1s-to-4pz and 1s-to-4px,y transitions at 8985-8988 eV and 8994-9002 eV, respectively, which 
indicated the existence of the Cu(II) species.  
The XANES spectra in the energy range 8970-9020 eV can be fitted with a linear combination of 
XANES fit vectors. The metallic copper is the main compound on surfaces of scrap ITO glass (Figure 
2(a)). In Figure 2(b), metallic copper converts to CuI (64%) and CuI2
- (26%) during extraction. About 
10% of Cu is also observed. It is clear that Cu strip from surfaces of scrap ITO glass and is extracted into 
the RTIL-I. The reacted equations for extracted copper in the RTIL-I can be express as I2 + I
