1.前言 
近年來，台灣地區經濟快速發展，造成都市化人口集中的現象，在許多都會地區到處
可見，同時具有二種以上的用途場所，彼此佔有一定比例的樓地板面積，並在管理與使用
上構成相對關係，而組成的同一棟建築物，即是本研究所指的複合用途建築物 [1]。由於此
類建築物內部同時結合住宅、營業、工廠等其他用途場所，常因各類用途場所的火災危險
因素不同，造成建築物防火管理與火災預防上的漏洞。如在 2001 年 5 月 12 日台北縣汐止
東方科學園區大樓火災為例，該建築物共有 220 個經營單位，地下層有大型量販店屬於營
業用途場所，1 至 3 樓主要為小型商店、速食店、住家等屬於營業與住宅用途場所，4 樓
至 26 樓則為辦公室、工廠、倉庫等屬於工廠與其他用途場所，該建築物屬於大型的複合
用途建築物；而該次火災事件主要發生原因，為 3 樓住宅用火不慎引起火災，大量高溫的
濃煙經由管道間流竄至 16 樓，造成二次燃燒並快速向上延燒，由於該建築物的消防幫浦
故障後，無人重視而未能即時修復，以致火災發生時，建築物的自動撒水設備無法發揮功
能，加上該棟建築物高度超出雲梯車救援能力，雖然火災發生在晚上未造成重大傷亡，但
整場火災歷時約 43 小時，造成新台幣 50 億元以上的損失。尤其台灣地區有許多類似東方
科學園區大樓的複合用途建築物，因此，此類建築物火災的相關研究實在刻不容緩 [2][3]。 
回顧台灣地區防火安全評估法的發展過程，首先由 Chen, D.C. and Jian, X.W.於 1995
年參考日本特定建築物的防火評估法 [4]，並利用專家問卷調查的方法，以營業場所建築物
為研究標的物，提出台灣地區第一套的建築物火災危險評估法 [5]。在 1999 年 Guo, J.T.and 
Xiong GH. [6]直接引進加拿大火災危害度與成本分析的電腦評估法 FIRECAM TM，針對住
宅與辦公室用途的建築物，期望引進加拿大的經驗與資料庫，建立本土化電腦模擬的參
數。而內政部建築研究所自 2000 年起，對於建築物防火安全等級較高者，給予防火標章 [7]
予以鼓勵，但其認證評估的對象則以旅館類建築物為主。由於上述評估方法的對象，皆以
單一用途的建築物為主。因此，目前有必要研擬一套完整且被各界接受的複合用途建築物
防火安全評估法。本研究在內政部建築研究所提供經費支持下，邀請各界共同參與，並截
取其他評估方法的優點，引用政府公報的火災統計資料，經過適當匯整後，再利用矩陣運
算方式，建構一套本土化的複合用途建築物防火安全評估法，將各項量化的評分數據反映

2.建築物的用途分類 
過去很少針對複合用途建築物進行相關研究，可以引用的數據或資料甚少，本計劃收
集台灣地區建築物的相關火災統計之數據或資料，用來表示複合用途建築物的內部各類場
所之火災特性，並視各類場所的火災危險程度彼此獨立，不考慮相對的交互影響作用；因
此在進行複合用途建築防火安全評估的相關研究前，必須對於建築物進行分類。由於目前
台灣尚無一致的建築物分類方式，而且在政府公佈的火災統計資料之中，亦無法將建築物
細分為更多類型，況且本研究以矩陣運算方法作為複合用途建築物的防火安全評估工具，
乃期望未來能有足夠的分類標準與統計資料，能將建築物分成數十種以上，再利用矩陣運
算的方式擴充評估方法之內容，使其更能反應建築物的火災特性。所以，本計劃根據目前
所收集的火災統計資料並參考 Lin [8]的研究成果，暫時將建築物用途分為住宅類、工廠類、
營業類、其他類等 4 種類型，並以此四類作為後續研究的分類依據： 
1).住宅類：此類場所通常具有一個或一個以上的居住單元，每個單元之居住者擁有該單元
之全部的所有權，若有共同使用的部份則由所有居住單元之所有權人共同持有。另外，
由於台灣地區寸土寸金，許多家庭將建築物一樓當作商店經營，二樓以上則作為住宅使
用，然此類建築物由於可燃物眾多且有營業使用的行為，增加許多火災發生的風險性，
本計劃亦將此種住宅兼作店舖使用的建築物歸於此類。 
2).營業類：此類場所通常為具有相當面積比例的商業活動空間，提供消費者休閒、娛樂、
購物、飲食等需求。由於近年來國民所得提高，各種商業活動的方式亦趨向於多元化，
除了大型的百貨公司、購物中心、量販賣場以外，三溫暖、觀光理髮店、KTV、MTV、
PUB、卡拉 OK、餐廳、酒廊、茶藝館、保齡球館等場所到處可見。然而，此類場所的
經營者常以經營獲利為考量，而忽略火災的危險性，不但經常無照違規使用，甚至破壞
原有建築物防火措施與消防設備、大量採用易燃隔間材料、封閉逃生梯、外部廣告物阻
斷逃生等情形，因此，一旦發生重大火災事件時，皆會造成相當嚴重的死傷 [4]。 
3).工廠類：此類場所通常為具有相當面積比例的作業空間，利用各種生產技術與設備，以
自動化或人工的方式，將原物料變成各式各樣的產品。由於其生產過程需經由相當複雜
的製造過程，且人力資源成本過高，採用自動化的高標準作業系統，以及使用昂貴的設
備器材與原物料，已經成為現代化工廠作業的新趨勢；由於經常需要使用各種電器、瓦
斯、鍋爐等加熱設備，以及大量的易燃性、爆炸性、高揮發性等危險物品，造成此類建
築物火災危害日益嚴重，甚至已成為國際上共同的趨勢(Yang) [9]。 
4).其他類：前述 3 類以外的建築物，如醫院、診所、學校、老人安養中心等，本計劃皆將
其歸於此類。

5.風險比重 
由於建築物防火安全評估的目的，在於獲得建築物內部火災發生的潛在危險程度，根
據 Holborn,P.G. [14]的研究，建築物的火災危險程度與起火機率成正比例的關係；因此，在
進行複合用途建築物防火安全評估時，先假設各類場所的火災危險程度彼此獨立，再利用
內部各類場所不同的起火機率，來表示彼此之間在複合用途建築物所佔的風險比重。而在
探討建築物的起火機率時，除了以時間區間為考量因素外，亦可將研究對象的樓地板面積
納入考慮。因此本計劃根據 Holborn,P.G. [14]與 Li [15]的研究成果，將火災發生頻率定義為‘單
位時間火災發生次數’，時間單位定為一年，即建築物火災發生頻率為‘每年火災發生次
數’，其單位為次/年，建築物火災的起火機率為‘單位面積的火災發生頻率’，其單位為次/
年．平方公尺；另外，亦根據 Li [15]的研究，定義建築物火災發生週期為‘每次火災發生時
間的間隔’，即為火災發生頻率的倒數，其單位為年/次。 
5.1 各類建築物火災的起火機率、頻率與周期 
本計劃根據 Lin [8]與內政部消防署最新公佈的火災統計資料，獲得 1985 年至 2008 年
歷年來各類建築物起火次數的統計資料；再根據內政部營建署的資料，獲得台灣地區歷年
來合法完工取得執照的各類建築物所佔之樓地板面積，至於，違法興建的建築物則不在統
計範圍內，並假設建築物在被統計的時程內，不會因地震、洪水或人為等因素而拆除，而
獲得 1985 年至 2008 年歷年來台灣地區各類建築物所佔樓地板面積的統計資料。最後，本
計劃將前述兩項統計資料合併考量後，得到 1985 年至 2008 年歷年來各類建築物火災的起
火機率如表 5。 
由表 5 可知，每年各類建築物火災的起火機率並不相同，以工廠類為例 1986 年
2.79×10-5 次/年．平方公尺為最高，2008 年 2.12×10-6 次/年．平方公尺為最低，兩者相差高
達約 13 倍。如前述研究所知，建築物火災的發生頻率，是以建築物火災的起火機率乘以
樓地板面積而來；所以，如何選取表 5 中各類建築物火災的起火機率，對於後續研究有決

率；其中以工廠類建築物 9.90×10-6 次/年．平方公尺為最高，住宅類建築物 7.01×10-6 次/
年．平方公尺為次之，其他類建築物 2.96×10-6 次/年．平方公尺為再次之，營業類建築物
2.08×10-6 次/年．平方公尺為最低。

Jen-Hao Chi1*   Ji-Jun Yang2   Chin-Chun Chang3

Abstract 
Statistical data over the past 24 years detailing the number of fires and building floor areas published by the 
government was employed to acquire the fire probability, frequency, and cycle for each building category. By 
applying a matrix calculation, the fire probability, frequency, cycle, and risk ratio for each functional area within a 
multi-purpose building were obtained. With assistance from the government, the fire case investigation and 
statistical data for building fires were established. By adopting the risk ratio concept, the weight values for 20 fire 
safety assessment criteria and 4 fire safety strategies for a multi-purpose building were acquired to indicate the 
possible causes of fires and the quantitative extent of fire influence. 
Keywords: Fire Probability, Multi-Purpose Building, Risk Ratio, Fire Safety Assessment Criteria 
 
 
1. Introduction 
Rapid economic growth has caused multi-purpose buildings to become commonly seen in many Taiwan 
metropolitan areas in recent years. In this research, a multi-purpose building is defined as a building serving two or 
more functions, with each containing a certain amount of floor area and the facility management and usage having a 
relationship between all use areas [1]. Because of the combined functions a multi-purpose building serves, and each 
functional area has different fire risk characteristics, many loopholes exist in the building fire safety management. 
There are many buildings similar to Hsichi’s Eastern Science Park Building in Taiwan, emphasizing the importance 
of related fire safety research [2,3]. 
It is necessary to develop a complete and accepted fire safety assessment method for multi-purpose buildings. 
With the participation of all social circles and financial support from the Architecture and Building Research 
Institute, Ministry of the Interior (MOI), the strengths of other assessment systems were adopted. Statistical fire data 
published by the government were applied to a matrix calculation. This research set up a domestic fire safety 
assessment system for multi-purpose buildings. The evaluated data can be presented to relevant personnel to 
understand the fire safety grades and improvement directions for building fire safety rapidly, to reduce the casualties 
and damage caused by fire. 
2. Fire safety assessment criterion and fire safety strategy 
The building fire safety assessment criterion is closely related to the causes of fires. It plays an essential role in 
the development of a fire safety assessment system [4]. Because the building safety assessment criteria coverage 
must be broad and complete [5], this research selected adequate fire safety assessment criteria based on: 
(1)Assessment Methods for Fire-Safety Functions in Special Buildings in Japan, (2) the Fire Safety Valuation (Point) 
Scheme of Edinburgh, Scotland, and (3) the opinions of domestic specialists and scholars. 
 
2.1 Fire safety assessment criteria  
In the research of Pan [6], and Chen [7], most fire safety assessment research in Taiwan is based on the 34 fire 
safety assessment criteria mentioned in the “Assessment Methods for Fire-Safety Functions in Special Buildings in 
Japan”, due to its extensive scope and geographical and cultural similarity to Taiwan. The selection of 34 fire safety 
assessment criteria comes from the reference to Japanese architectural laws and fire causes and a statistical analysis 
conducted by 20 scholars and building fire experts. Its coverage includes the building interior and exterior, fire 
safety software and hardware equipment. Therefore, according to the 34 fire safety assessment criteria from Japan, 
this research incorporated the current regulations in Taiwan regarding building fire safety and combined or 
eliminated criteria that had similar names. According to Marchant’s research [4] on the “Fire Safety Evaluation 
(Point) Scheme of Edinburgh, Scotland”, it was concluded that fire rescue capability was tightly connected with the 
fire damage. Hence, the two fire safety assessment criteria, “essential equipment for fire-fighting” from Japan and 
“fire brigade” from Scotland, are combined into the assessment system defined here as one of the fire safety 
assessment criteria: “fire rescuing ability”.

5. Remarks and suggestions 
This building fire safety assessment system for a multi-purpose building by matrix calculation, with joint 
participation by the government, private commercial corporations, and academic institution, includes fire safety 
assessment criteria, fire safety strategy, weight value, risk ratio and assessment value in its frame. Because the 
weight value of each fire safety assessment criterion representing the full marks of that item, it is necessary to 
ascertain the practice degree of each fire safety assessment criterion within the assessed building during actual 
assessment, which means the weight value of each criterion needs to be multiplied by the above-mentioned 
assessment value. The assessment value can be in 0 to 100% of the ranges according to the very good or bad degree 
of the assessed building. Because the grading standard refers to the relevant regulations, the purpose of the building, 
the subjective consciousness of appraiser, and many other conditions, the argument about assessment value was not 
discussed in this text. Referring to the frame of this research, a follow-up study may establish the grading standard 
according to characteristics of assessed buildings and contents of regulations. 
The matrix calculation method used here can serve as the foundation for a future computerized assessment 
system. By doing so, the up-to-date weight value, fire probability, fire frequency, and fire cycle of the building fire 
safety assessment system can always be adopted to accurately indicate the building fire safety grading based on a 
future questionnaire and statistical data of fire cases and building floor area with the new fire case included. Not 
only does it assist the engineer in promoting the building fire safety to the building investor, but also provides 
assistance to the insurance company when setting up the insurance rate it also serves as an assessment tool in issuing 
the “Fire Safety Building LOGO” to the building.  
The fire probability used in this research is the average value collected over the past 24 years. During the 
research time, experts from the insurance industry suggested that from the public safety viewpoint the highest fire 
probability should be adopted to calculate the fire frequency and cycle for each building category. That is, the fire 
probability of 2.79×10–5 times/year m2 in 1986, as being the highest, should be applied for the factory building to 
demonstrate the hazard of building fire. However, after discussion, it was considered that many factors would be 
influential in building fire probability, such as both internal and external building fire safety software and hardware 
measures and related regulation revision as an implicit influence. Therefore, it is suggested that future users of the 
fire safety assessment system should selectively choose an average value representing a certain year or a certain

經常造成重大傷亡與財物損失。本計劃引用政府公報 24 年的建築物
火災次數與樓地板面積等資料，得到各類用途建築物火災的起火機
率、發生頻率與週期；再以矩陣運算方式，來計算複合用途建築物
內部各類用途場所火災的起火機率、發生頻率、週期與風險比重等。
並在政府部門協助下，建立台灣地區建築物火災案例調查與統計的
相關資料，再引用風險比重的觀念，獲得複合用途建築物 20 項防火
安全評估因素與 4 項防火安全對策的權重數值來表示複合用途建築
物可能的火災發生因素，與其影響的量化程度。

Among all of the research related to buildings fire safety 
assessments, only a few are focused on multi-purpose buildings. 
However, there are many multi-purpose buildings in metropolitan areas. 
Because of the unique fire characteristics of multi-purpose buildings, 
fires in these buildings often cause severe loss of life and damage. 
Statistical data over the past 24 years detailing the number of fires and 
building floor area published by the government has been used to 
acquire the fire probability, frequency and cycle for each building 
category. By applying a matrix calculation, the fire probability, 
frequency, cycle, and risk ratio for each functional area within a 
multi-purpose building are obtained. With assistance from the 
government, the fire case investigation and statistical data for building 
fires were established. By adopting the risk ratio concept, the weight 
values for 20 fire safety assessment criteria and 4 fire safety strategies 
for a multi-purpose building were acquired to indicate the possible 
causes of fires and the quantitative extent of fire influence.

2.2 Fire safety strategies  
Consider the “Assessment Methods for Fire-Safety Functions in Special Building in Japan” [6, 7, 9] as an example. 
The subjects of this assessment system are large-scale department store buildings. The key fire safety design feature in 
such buildings is the ability to control the speed of fire spreading within the building to allow the safe evacuation of all 
shoppers. This assessment system not only looks at the total assessment score but also focuses on the fire spreading 
prevention capability when evaluating the building. Three fire safety strategies are proposed: fire prevention strategy, 
enlarged prevention strategy, and refuge-escape strategy. Based on the research conducted by Bukowski [9], Pan [4], 
and Chen [5], this research adopted those three fire safety strategies from Japan and acquired one more from the “Fire 
Safety Building LOGO” recognition system [15] promoted by Architecture and Building Research Institute, Ministry of 
the Interior (MOI), as the result of incorporation of current domestic policy into the essential strategy. Table 1 concludes 
and shows the relevancy of the 20-fire safety assessment criteria and the 4-fire safety strategies.

3. Risk ratio of multi-purpose building 
The goal of building fire safety assessment is to learn the potential risk level for fire occurrence within a building. 
Based on Holborn et al.’s [10] research, the building fire risk level and its fire probability are directly proportional. 
Because of that, under the assumption that the fire risk of each functional area was independent, the risk ratio of every 
functional area in the same building can be presented by its fire probability while conducting the fire safety assessment 
on the multi-purpose building. In discussing the building fire probability, both factors of ‘time’ and ‘floor area of the 
building’ are taken into consideration. According to Holborn et al.’s [10] and Li’s [11] research, fire frequency is “the 
number of fire occurrences per time unit”. By setting the time unit to one year, fire frequency is described as “the 
number of fire occurrences within one year” with the unit being “times/year”. In addition, the building fire probability is 
defined as “the fire frequency per area unit” with the unit being “times/year·m2”. Moreover, according to Li’s [11] study, 
the building fire cycle is defined as “the time interval between two fires”, which is the reciprocal of fire frequency with 
the unit being “years/time”.

4. Weight analysis of multi-purpose building 
The objective that building fire safety assessment is to acquire involves the possible factors of fire occurrence and 
their quantitative extent of influence, which means the weight value of building fire safety assessment criteria. These 
values are regarded as the assessment basis of the fire risk level of the building. Fire safety assessment criteria and the 
weight values of single purpose buildings have already been obtained with expert deliberation and the Delphi Method in 
the above research, the question is how to acquire the similar data of a multi-purpose building. By applying the matrix 
calculation, supposing the fire risk level of each functional area was independent between different functional areas 
within multi-purpose building, the above-mentioned risk ratio of a multi-purpose building is multiplied by the fire 
safety assessment criteria weight values for each category building. This represents the influence degree of each fire 
safety assessment criterion within the multi-purpose building. Here, the weight values of 20 fire safety assessment 
criteria listed in Table 1 are incorporated in sequence into the matrix of Equation (10):

means the weight value of each criterion needs to be multiplied by the above-mentioned assessment value. The 
assessment value can be in 0 to 100% of the ranges according to the very good or bad degree of the assessed building. 
Because the grading standard refers to the relevant regulations, the purpose of the building, the subjective consciousness 
of appraiser, and many other conditions, the argument about assessment value was not discussed in this text. Referring 
to the frame of this research, a follow-up study may establish the grading standard according to characteristics of 
assessed buildings and contents of regulations. 
The matrix calculation method used here can serve as the foundation for a future computerized assessment system. 
By doing so, the up-to-date weight value, fire probability, fire frequency, and fire cycle of the building fire safety 
assessment system can always be adopted to accurately indicate the building fire safety grading based on a future 
questionnaire and statistical data of fire cases and building floor area with the new fire case included. Not only does it 
assist the engineer in promoting the building fire safety to the building investor, but also provides assistance to the 
insurance company when setting up the insurance rate it also serves as an assessment tool in issuing the “Fire Safety 
Building LOGO” to the building.  
The fire probability used in this research is the average value collected over the past 24 years. During the research 
time, experts from the insurance industry suggested that from the public safety viewpoint the highest fire probability 
should be adopted to calculate the fire frequency and cycle for each building category. That is, the fire probability of 
2.79×10–5 times/year m2 in 1986, as being the highest, should be applied for the factory building to demonstrate the 
hazard of building fire. However, after discussion, it was considered that many factors would be influential in building 
fire probability, such as both internal and external building fire safety software and hardware measures and related 
regulation revision as an implicit influence. Therefore, it is suggested that future users of the fire safety assessment 
system should selectively choose an average value representing a certain year or a certain time period in Table 2 as the 
statistical base of assessment.
