 1
鋼筋混凝土柱受剪破壞之 
耐震行為曲線研究 
Study on the Seismic Performance Curves of Reinforced 
Concrete Columns Failed in Shear 
一.中文摘要 
    鋼筋混凝土建築物受剪破壞而倒塌，在九二一地震勘災中屢屢可見。從受損
的柱發現多數為非韌性配筋，意即柱箍筋間距過大或不具備耐震 135 度彎鉤，而
使得這些柱可能無法具備良好之消能與變形能力。且從勘災經驗中可得知，剪力
破壞常主控這些柱之破壞行為與模式，但剪力破壞屬於脆性破壞，對建築物而言
必須盡量避免之。因此本研究以實驗及分析方法，來探討鋼筋混凝土柱在固定軸
力載重下受剪破壞之耐震行為。 
    本研究第一年主要探討純剪破壞柱之耐震性能曲線。執行 8 座鋼筋混凝土試
體測試，藉以了解鋼筋混凝土短柱受剪破壞之行為，以及剪力破壞後之崩塌行
為。第二年度主要探討撓剪破壞柱之耐震性能曲線。執行 6 座鋼筋混凝土柱試體
測試，藉以了解鋼筋混凝土柱受撓剪破壞之行為，以及剪力破壞後之崩塌行為。
第三年度研究重點將放在高軸力對柱之耐震性能曲線之影響上，執行 3 座鋼筋混
凝土柱試體，藉以了解承受高軸力之鋼筋混凝土柱，其受撓剪破壞之崩塌行為。 
關鍵字:鋼筋混凝土、柱、剪力破壞、崩塌 
Abstract 
    During the 921 Chi-Chi Earthquake, a lot of reinforced concrete buildings failed 
in shear. According to the experience in Chi-Chi Earthquake, we found that columns 
with inadequate transverse reinforcement are vulnerable to shear failure. Shear failure 
is brittle and therefore should be avoided. Thus, this research is aimed to study the 
seismic resistant behavior of column failed in shear under constant axial force through 
observation in laboratory experiments and analysis. 
    First year, eight specimens will be tested to observe the behavior of shear failure 
columns and its collapse behavior. Second, six specimens will be tested to observe the 
behavior of flexural-shear failure columns and its collapse behavior. Third year, three 
specimens will be tested to observe the behavior of high axial load columns and its 
collapse behavior. 
Keywords: reinforced concrete, column, shear failure, collapse 
二.計畫緣由及目的 
1999 年 9 月 21 號凌晨 1 點 47 分，台灣發生芮氏規模 7.3 級的集集大地震，
造成全國人民生命與財產上嚴重的損失，許多鋼筋混凝土建築物倒塌和不計其數
的人員傷亡，世界各地的學者也關注到此事件。地震後勘災的結果[1]，我們可以
了解混凝土建築物的損毀，絕大原因在於柱桿件的破壞，特別是低矮型鋼筋混凝
土建築物，一樓的柱破壞的十分嚴重。一般而言，早期的建築物設計缺乏抗震概
念（包括校舍及台灣地區典型的臨街式街屋），在柱體內的配筋，箍筋的放置不
符合耐震規範，彎鉤的角度沒達到 135 度、箍筋的間距過大等問題，造成這些柱
無法提供良好之消能與變形能力，而導致耐震能力與結構韌性嚴重不足。 
一般而言柱桿件受剪破壞可分為兩大模式，一為純剪破壞，一為撓剪破壞。

本研究之所有試體都有達到標稱撓曲強度，顯示這些試體在強度階段均為撓
曲主控之行為模式。當所有試體達到其撓曲強度後，均有類似撓曲降伏平台之行
為，並隨著位移之增加，而發生剪力破壞，亦即所謂之撓剪破壞。本研究之試驗
在到達撓剪破壞點後，試體之側力強度開始迅速衰減，隨即喪失軸向承載能力。
垂直承載能力喪失點與撓剪破壞點接近，並無在達到撓剪破壞點後，還經歷一段
較大位移，才喪失軸向承載能力。在Lynn et al. [4]之試驗結果當中，也有與本研
究之試驗結果相類似之情形。本研究試體之主筋比皆為1.43%，而Lynn et al. [4]
試體之主筋比約為2%，主筋比皆較為偏小。而由Sezen [5]、翁樸文[2]與黃益堂[6]
之試驗結果可知，當主筋比較大時(Sezen [5]為2.5%，翁樸文[2]與黃益堂[6]為
3%)，則無此一現象產生。可見主筋量之高低，可能對柱桿件崩塌之時機有一定
程度之影響。本研究之試體分別承載低軸力(0.1 gc Af ′ )與中軸力(0.3 gc Af ′ )，其均小
於試體之平衡破壞軸力Pb，各試體均為拉力破壞之桿件。由試驗結果可知，當試
體承載中軸力時，試體之初始勁度與最大強度均較承載低軸力之試體為高，但位
移能力與承載低軸力試體相比，明顯較小許多。試體H25-L 與H25-M 之箍筋比
為0.002，試體HT25-L 與HT25-M 之箍筋比為0.003，試體H15-L 與H15-M 之箍
筋比為0.0034。由試驗結果可知，增加試體之箍筋比，確實有助於提升桿件之位
移能力。另外，由HT25-M 與H15-M 之試驗結果可知，若試體之箍筋比接近，
當試體承載之軸力較高時，加繫筋之試體，有助於延緩柱桿件側力強度衰減之時
機。

尖大學聯合舉辦，為一年一度針對土木領域所舉辦之校際間交流研討會。其參加對象主
要為亞洲地區五國五所大學之教授、學生等。本次會議在泰國清邁之 Imperial Mae Ping

為 15 分鐘，問題討論時間為 3 分鐘。學生和其指導老師黃世建教授共同發表論文的題
目為 Shear Strength Prediction of Eccentric Beam-Column Joint Using Softened Strut-and-Tie

ABSTRACT 
 
Several studies showed that the eccentricity between beam and column connections has a detrimental effect on joint 
shear strength. With regard to this issue, current ACI 318-08 code restricts the average shear stress on a horizontal plane 
within the joint, which equals to the effective joint width times column depth. However, the formula of effective joint 
width given in ACI 318-08 is realized to be an empirical equation and may be too conservative for eccentric 
beam-column joints. This paper extends the softened strut-and-tie (SST) model by deriving a more rational formula for 
effective joint width associated with the SST failure criteria for eccentric beam-column joints. The extended model is 
verified for collected joint database of 18 eccentrically connected beam-column joint specimens. 
 
INTRODUCTION 
 
When designing buildings with seismic threats, plastic hinges are expected to be at beam ends to ensure good energy 
dissipation during earthquakes. This causes very large forces to be transferred among elements through the 
beam-column joints. Although concentric connections are more desirable to guarantee a smooth force transfer, eccentric 
connections (Fig. 1) are sometimes unavoidable due to architectural considerations. 
During the Tokachioki Earthquake in 1968, Joh et al. (1991) found that several reinforced concrete (RC) frame 
buildings which had eccentric joints were heavily damaged. Several researchers in Japan also reported similar findings 
from the Hyogoken-Nanbu earthquake in 1995 (Goto & Joh 2003). The ACI 318-08 addresses the issue of joint 
eccentricity by strictly limiting the effective joint width to be equal to the beam width if beams are flush with columns. 
The ACI 352R-02 further introduced the eccentricity between the column and beam axes in the design equations. The 
recent study by LaFave et al. (2005) concluded that the ACI 318-05 code and ACI 352R-02 guideline give conservative 
joint shear strength estimation for the effective joint width. Based on comparisons with test data, LaFave et al. 
suggested that the effective joint width can be taken as the average between the beam and column width for the ACI 
318-08 code design equation, yielding reasonable estimations of shear strength for eccentric beam-column joints. 
This paper extends the applicability of softened strut-and-tie (SST) model in estimating shear strength of 
eccentric beam-column joint. A new and more analytical definition for effective joint width is derived based on the 
failure criteria of this SST model. The accuracy of the proposed model is gauged by the available experimental data for 
eccentric joints collected from literatures.

EXPERIMENTAL VERIFICATION 
 
In order to verify the applicability of SST model together with the analytically derived effective joint width 
(Eq. (2)), as many as 18 specimens of eccentrically connected beam-column joints have been collected (Lim 
2009). All of these joint specimens were designed according to strong column weak beam concept. Moreover, 
all of the specimens have joint transverse reinforcement and failed in joint shear during the tests. 
In the shear strength prediction for eccentric beam-column joints using the SST model, three tiers of 
analysis (referred to as Analysis 1, 2, and 3) in terms of the sophistication of SST solution procedure and the 
accuracy of determination of the depth of the beam and column compression zone (Analysis 1 being the 
simplest and Analysis 3 being the most sophisticated) are performed. Analysis 1 and Analysis 2 predict the 
joint strength by using a simplified SST approach (Hwang & Lee 2002) while Analysis 3 uses a general SST 
approach (Hwang & Lee 1999, 2000).
