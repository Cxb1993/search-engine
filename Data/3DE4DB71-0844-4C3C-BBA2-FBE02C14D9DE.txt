Studies on Fabrication and Sensing Mechanism of Electroless Plated
Palladium-Transistor Hydrogen Sensors

一、摘 要
本研究首度利用無電鍍(EP)技術分
別在磷化銦鎵與砷化鋁鎵基材上鍍覆鈀
閘極層以製備鈀/砷化鋁鎵(Pd/AlGaAs)
及鈀/磷化銦鎵(Pd/InGaP)電晶體式氫氣
感測器，並探討氫氣感測特性，文中並
分別就穩態與暫態分析，探討氫氣在界
面上之吸附行為。實驗結果顯示，由於
砷化鋁鎵基材易受鹼性鍍浴侵蝕，造成
結構破壞，導致電流通道縮小，電晶體
飽和電流變小，電性特性發生改變，造
成氫氣感測特性變差。至於鈀/磷化銦鎵
電晶體對氫氣具有極佳之偵檢能力，偵
測極限低(<5 ppm)、偵檢範圍寬(5 ppm~1
% H2/air)、靈敏度高，且響應快速、閘
極操作溫度廣(303-503 K)。靈敏度受偵
測濃度、閘極操作偏壓與溫度之影響甚
鉅，當增加濃度及降低溫度時皆能提升
元件之靈敏度，且當操作偏壓在-0.75 V
時，呈現最大靈敏度。研究中並發現，
當溫度為 333K 時，響應及回覆速率最
快。
關鍵字：電晶體、無電鍍、鈀、氫氣感
測器、磷化銦鎵、砷化鋁鎵
二、緣由與目的
自從 Lundström[1]成功以鈀作為蕭特基
二極體(Schottky diode)氫氣感測閘極後，各
式場效元件感測器之研究與開發亦陸續被研
究與開發。一般傳統鍍膜技術(熱蒸鍍、濺鍍
法等)由於高溫高能量會造成蕭特基界面之
破壞，導致表面發生費米能階釘住效應
(Fermi-level pinning effect)。近年來研究者[2,
3]認為，低溫低能製程可避免熱破壞金-半界
面，而使二極體電性特性改善。其中，本研
究室以無電鍍法成功開發出鈀蕭特基二極體
氫氣感測器，其具備極佳之界面品質及氫氣
感測性能。在本研究中，吾人以此無電鍍技
術推廣至鈀閘極電晶體式氫氣感測器之製
備，嘗試更小線幅尺寸(1 μm)之析鍍，實驗
中首先確認基材之化學穩定性，再進行鈀之
析鍍，並以此三端電晶體元件進行氫氣感測
實驗，以探討此元件之感測性能。
三、氫氣感測原理
由於鈀金屬對氫氣具有特殊選透性，因
此氫氣於鈀蕭特基二極體上之吸附行為一般
可分為三階段：(1)氫分子於鈀表面進行化學
解離吸附成氫原子；(2)藉由表面與界面之濃
度差，氫原子經擴散至鈀與半導體之界面；
(3)氫原子吸附在界面上。吸附於鈀蕭特基界
面上之氫原子，由於受到接面空乏區之電荷
誘導極化，產生一偶極層(dipole layer)使界面
電荷重新分佈，導致空乏區寬度減小[4]，使
電流上升。因此藉由量測元件之電性變化，
則可檢測出氫氣濃度含量。
基本上，電晶體包含二個歐姆電極與一
蕭特基電極，藉由所施加之閘極偏壓(VGS)來
控制源極與汲極間之電流量(ID)，電晶體飽和
區之電流電壓關係可以式(1)來表示：

Abstract—In this work, the electroless plated (EP) Pd/InGaP 
high electron mobility transistor (HEMT) was firstly employed 
for hydrogen sensing. The current-voltage (I-V) characteristics 
under hydrogen concentrations of 5ppm-1% and temperatures 
of 303-503K were investigated. Experimentally, the Pd gate of 
three-terminal devices were successfully fabricated by the 
electroless plating method, and the studied devices exhibited 
excellent current-voltage characteristics with superior current 
control ability. For hydrogen sensing performances, the studied 
EP device demonstrated low detection limit, high sensitivity, and 
fast response. As compared with the thermal evaporated (TE) 
device, larger current variations can be achieved by the EP 
device. Even at extremely low hydrogen concentration, e.g., 4.3 
ppm H2/air, obvious current modulation was found. The 
maximum relative sensitivity reaches up to 428 % at a optimal 
gate voltage of -0.75 V. Furthermore, the transient detections 
showed that the sensing response was fairly fast, especially at 
high concentrations and high temperatures. At detection 
temperature of 403 K, the time for 90% response at 1 % H2/air 
was within 4 seconds. These excellent sensing performances of 
the EP device indeed made it promising and competitive in 
future developments of smart hydrogen sensors integrated 
microelectronic systems.  
I. INTRODUCTION  
 
Over the past years, many hydrogen-sensitive Pd Schottky 
diodes based on compound semiconductors, e.g., GaAs, InP, 
etc., have been studied and reported. However, due to the 
small bandgap of substrates, they do not allow to operate over 
a wide temperature range, especially in harsh environments. In 
modern trend of development of chemical sensors, it is highly 
expected to use semiconductor materials with large bandgaps. 
For this reason, Schottky diode hydrogen sensor based on 
ternary compound semiconductors, such as AlGaAs[1,2] and 
InGaP[3,4], recently have attracted considerable attention due 
to high hydrogen sensitivity at high-temperature regime. As it 
was known, the sensing performances were strongly 
dependent on the deposition techniques of metal gate, i.e., Pd. 
Conventionally, the catalytic Pd layer was deposited by 
thermal evaporation (TE) or sputtering techniques, which 
would probably cause the Fermi-level pinning effect leading 
to the reduction of sensing capability. Herein, a Pd/InGaP 
transistor is first fabricated by electroless plating in this work. 
Furthermore, the I-V characteristics and hydrogen sensing 
performances of the studied device were investigated and 
demonstrated. 
 
II. EXPERIMENTAL  
 
A. Fabrication of electroless plated Pd/InGaP transistor 
 
The studied Pd-InGaP transistors were fabricated on a 
InGaP substrate with an n-type epitaxial layer grown by a 
metal organic chemical vapor deposition (MOCVD) system. 
The drain-source Ohmic contacts were made by thermal 
evaporation of AuGe/Au layers, followed by an annealing 
process at 320 °C. As to the formation of palladium gate on 
the InGaP  layer, the sensitization and activation technique 
was first deposited palladium active layer. Then EP technique 
was employed for palladium deposition in the defined gate 
area. The compositions of sensitization and activation 
solution are listed in Table 1, and the composition of 
hydrazine-based plating bath (Table 2) has been reported 
elsewhere [5].

B. Transient-state analysis 
Figures 5(a)-(c) illustrate plots of transient current versus 
time at 303, 403 and 503 K, respectively. As can be seen, the 
response current increases with increasing hydrogen 
concentrations and operating temperatures. The response time 
and recovery time are defined as the time required for 
reaching e-1 of the maximum change in the response current 
when the hydrogen gas is flow-in and cut-out, respectively. 
Accordingly, the response time and recovery time are 
corresponding to the hydrogen adsorption and desorption 
rates, respectively. Table 3 summarizes the response time and 
recovery time at various hydrogen concentrations and 
operating temperatures. It is found that both response and 
recovery rates can be speeded up by increasing the hydrogen 
concentrations. However, the maximum response rate occurs 
at around 403K.
