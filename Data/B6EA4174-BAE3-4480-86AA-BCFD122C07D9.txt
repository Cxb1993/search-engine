that the Ti-5Cr-5Mo alloy is the lowest

alloy corrosion potential (Ecorr) are higher than cp 
Ti, the Ti-5Cr-1Mo alloy has the highest corrosion 
potential (Ecorr) -0.853 V, followed by Ti-5Cr-5Mo 
alloy. By using a current density of 0.5 V (I 0.5) 
analysis, it was shown that the current density of 
the Ti-5Cr-xMo alloys was lower than that of c.p. Ti 
and Ti-6Al-4V. Hence, the addition of Mo to Ti-5Cr 
has been shown to improve corrosion resistance. With 
regard to biocompatibility, the fibroblast cell (L-
929) and the osteoblast-like cell (MG-63) co-culture 
with Ti-5Cr-xMo alloys, the SEM observation and WST-1 
assay indicate that added little amount of Mo element 
in Ti-5Cr alloy is not cytotoxic. The ALP test showed 
that Ti-5Cr-3Mo had a more rapid cell differentiation 
than c.p. Ti, but no significant difference was noted 
when the alloy was compared with Ti-6Al-4V. The 
results showed little amount of Mo element (1~11 wt%) 
can increase cell activity and osteoblast 
differentiation.
