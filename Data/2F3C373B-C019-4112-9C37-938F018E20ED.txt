one of the main greenhouse gases contributing to 
enhance global warming. Molar weights of these 
hazardous vapors and gases are very different from 
those of soil gases, i.e., oxygen and nitrogen, as a 
result that mixture of these gases can significantly 
migrate in unsaturated soils due to total density 
variation, in addition to external pressure variation 
(fluctuation of atmosphere), component concentration 
variation and gravitational effect. Modeling 
transport of the concentrated gas mixture in 
subsurface conventionally uses advective-dispersive 
(diffusive) transport equation with various forms of 
Fick＇s law of diffusion.  The equation may further 
be incorporated with an approximated function of 
total density in terms of total pressure and 
component concentrations and/or in advance include a 
slip term to account for the density-driven 
transport. However, these different formulations have 
not yet been consistently assessed through 
experimental study. Besides, it seems that the 
additional slip term does not effectively account for 
the density-driven transport in comparison with the 
corresponding experimental result. This study 
continued the previous project entitled ＇Study on 
density-driven and gravitation-induced transport for 
organic vapor and gas in soils (99-2221-E-035-030-)＇ 
and aims at conducting experimental work on assessing 
density- and gravity-driven transport in a column 
composed of dry sand for a heavier-than-air vapor 
mixed with nitrogen to form a density ranging from 
0.14 to 1.5 kg/m3.  The diffusion coefficient is also 
derived for a column system composed of dry sand 
similar to the material in the transport system.  
Various flux components were calculated from the 
measured pressure and density variations based on the 
dusty gas model theory and Fick＇s law of diffusion.  
Finally, several models based on various forms of 
advective-dispersive (diffusive) transport equation 
were used to simulate pressure and density variations 
along the soil column for the experimental conditions 
to assess their adequacy in predicting coupled 
density- and gravity- driven transport systems.

文獻探討 
近期相關於土壤氣體傳輸的研究歸納如下：Wang et al. (2003)以土壤管柱分
析多組成成份 VOCs 垂直向上擴散的遲滯係數與有效擴散係數，他們的實驗結果
可以以 Fick’s 擴散的型式表示這些多組成成份 VOCs 的擴散量，但實驗結果中有
些成份由管柱頂端隨時間增加而出流的擴散量低於數值計算(Fick’s second law)的
結果，極可能由於重力引致傳輸量減少的緣故。Hamamoto et al. (2008)進行 
isohexane 在火山灰(clay loam)及砂質管柱的水平方向與垂直向下的擴散傳輸實
驗，分析密度改變與重力引致的傳輸量，結果顯示孔隙率大的火山灰反而因孔隙
連結度(pore connectivity)優於砂的特性，使得其透氣性較砂為佳，並使得受密度
改變與重力引致的傳輸量大於砂的結果，而 Fick’s 型式的擴散即低估實驗的結果，
另一方面，它們於水平擴散的實驗中亦獲得 Fick’s 高估實驗的結果，顯示 Fick’s
在水平傳輸的分析上亦有不足之處。Oostrom et al. (2010)認為一維模式分析未飽
和層 VOCs 的氣相傳輸有邊界的限制，故以三維模式分析未飽和層 VOCs 向地下
水層的傳輸量，結果顯示受密度改變與重力引致的向地下水層的傳輸量受到氣相
VOCs 濃度的影響很大，濃度大的氣相 VOCs 受重力影響向下的傳輸量較大。
Nerantzis and Dyer (2010)以實驗分析不同含水率砂土對於 BTEX 蒸氣擴散傳輸的
影響，其以 BTEX 蒸氣垂直向上通過不同含水率的砂質管柱分析其對於擴散量的
影響，結果顯示高含水的土壤可以作為降低 BTEX 蒸氣逸出地表的一個天然的屏
障，並促進微生物降解衰減的作用進行。 
 
研究方法 
本計畫的工作分為(1)實驗研究與(2)資料分析，以下分別說明所使用的方法： 
(1) 實驗研究 
本研究所進行的一維土壤管柱氣體傳輸實驗，簡化為單一方向、均質(土壤孔
隙率固定)的氣體傳輸系統，傳輸實驗的設備如圖一所示。量測的項目分為兩
部份：其一為壓力變化；其二為有機氣體的濃度，並要分析不同位置有機氣
體濃度與氣壓隨時間的變化關係。因要區別有機氣體因與空氣密度的不同，
受重力影響下，在水平、垂直向上與垂直向下的傳輸特性不一樣，故進行這
三種不同傳輸方向的實驗。實驗之前置工作則包括土壤管柱特性(粒徑分布、
滲透係數與有效擴散係數)的分析與氣體吸附土壤特性的分析。 
(i)    土壤管柱特性分析：乾海砂的粒徑分布由購買商家提供。乾海砂的滲透
係數為本研究團隊建置的氣體流動實驗系統所獲得(見 Fen, et al. 2011 的實
驗系統)，因所製作土壤管柱孔隙率不同，對土壤滲透係數會有影響，因之
流動實驗管柱與傳輸實驗管柱製作為同一孔隙率，前者獲得的滲透係數可
用為傳輸分析的參數。同樣，土壤氣體擴散之阻礙係數(obstruction factor)，
或是有效氣體擴散係數，亦要同流動與傳輸實驗管柱孔隙率的土壤管柱進

MATERIALS AND METHODS 
An experimental setup for investigating dense gas transport in a porous system was 
constructed according to the setting presented in Altevogt et al. (2003).   The transport system 
is a soil column packed with sea sand and connected at each end with a chamber, hereafter 
called inlet chamber (IC) and outlet chamber (OC), respectively.  These components are all 
filled with nitrogen gas firstly and an amount of SF6 was then injected into the IC to form a 
dense gas mixture in the chamber with a plunger cover the entrance to the soil column.  
Pressure transmitters are responsible for recording pressure differences between the outside and 
inside of the chambers and between sampling ports at the soil column and the inside of the OC.  
Before the plunger is pulled for allowing the gas mixture to enter the soil column, the whole 
system is maintained at atmospheric pressure by vent tubing on the IC and OC.  Once the 
plunger is pulled, pressure differences were recorded every half an hour and gas samples were 
taken every one hour from the ports at the IC and OC and four sampling ports along the soil 
column (5, 15, 25, 35 cm from the column entrance) for 4.5 to 5 hours.  Concentration of SF6 
of each sample was measured using a gas chromatograph (GC-17A, Shimadzu Corp., 
Kyoto, Japan) equipped with an electron-capture detector (ECD) with a molecular sieve 
5A GC PLOT capillary column (Supelco).  The experiment was conducted with 
different initial SF6 densities in the IC ranging from 0.8 to 1.55 Kg/m
3
.  The transport 
system has three different configurations: horizontally, vertically with the IC on the ground 
(transport in the vertically upward direction) or with the OC on the ground (transport in the 
vertically downward direction) to investigate gravitational effect on the migration of the gas 
mixture.   
Before the transport experiment, batch study was carried out to assess sorptive 
effect of SF6 onto sea sand.  Result shows that the amount of SF6 sorption onto sea sand 
is negligibly small.  Effective diffusion coefficient and air permeability for gas phase 
transport in a soil column were also measured separately to assess diffusive and advective flux 
components of gas transport.  These coefficients were determined from gas flow and diffusion 
experiments in which the setups and methodologies can be referred to Fen et al. (2009) and 
(2011).  Soil columns used in different experiments were all packed with sea sand with a 
porosity of 0.3676.
