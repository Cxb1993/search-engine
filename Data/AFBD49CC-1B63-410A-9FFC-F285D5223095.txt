一、前言
近幾年開始有人將界面活性劑結合空氣形成泡沫，將泡沫應用於受有機污染場址
的整治，Chowdiah et al.[1] 在實驗室利用一維的管柱實驗進行泡沫通過土壤介質的物理
現象觀察，他的研究結果指出泡沫在土壤孔隙間流動呈現黏性流體的流動型態，若是
土壤滲透率越小則黏性流體流動型態就越明顯，並且泡沫可以很均勻地被擠入孔隙
中，少有出現通道效應的情形。Rothmel et al. [2]在實驗室土壤管柱中注入泡沫，觀察注
入的泡沫對土壤中污染物 trichloroethylene (TCE) 的移除情形，實驗結果顯示泡沫的使
用確實對 TCE 有明顯的移除能力。Huang and Chang[3]在實驗室製作受正十五烷
(n-pentadecane) 污染的土壤玻璃管柱，再將泡沫注入管柱中進行污染物移除試驗，實
驗結果顯示泡沫整治正十五烷的效果遠高於土壤淋洗法。Jeong et al. [4] 及 Mulligan and
Eftekhari[5] 分別進行的實驗結果亦顯示界面活性劑泡沫對污染物的移除率高於土壤淋
洗法。
由前人研究結果所示，利用界面活性劑泡沫取代液體整治土壤具有幾項優點：一、
可以大幅減少界面活性劑的使用量；二、可有效降低整治流體的比重，對於重力的克
服有明顯的效果；三、可以增加單位界面活性劑的整治效果。若是將氣提法以微氣泡
氣提法取代，土壤蒸汽抽取法以土壤泡沫整治技術取代。藉著少量界面活性劑的使用，
在地下水中形成穩定微氣泡，對於 LNAPL 類的污染物，污染物可以進入氣泡中被移
除；對於 DNAPL 類的污染物，則可吸附在微氣泡表面被移除。微氣泡向上進入土壤
層時會形成泡沫，此泡沫將自水面向上推進，可以溶出土壤所吸附的污染物，再透過
泡沫抽取單元將泡沫移出地表。透過如此的整治程序即可將地下水及土壤中的有機污
染物(含 LNAPL 及 DNAPL)同步有效移除。
二、研究方法及進行步驟
步驟一：界面活性劑種類的選擇試驗
本計畫擬深入研究界面活性劑的添加對微氣泡整治法之影響，故必須選擇適當的
界面活性劑。除了需考量其對於污染物的移除能力，亦應考慮其所形成氣泡的穩
定性，並且此界面活性劑在地下環境中對於生態所造成的環境負荷、毒性殘留情
形也是必須考慮的因素。此外，此界面活性劑進入土壤中時，該界面活性劑與未
來在土壤中添加的界面活性劑之相容性也是界面活性劑的選擇上必須考慮的重
要因素。
步驟二：界面活性劑對微氣泡穩定度影響試驗
一般而言，界面活性劑會在氣體與水的交界面排列，形成一層較穩固的薄膜。依
照此學理，界面活性劑極有可能可以增加泡沫的穩定度。本計畫將探討添加界面
活性劑對於氣泡穩定度的影響，並探討覆蓋界面活性劑的氣泡在土壤孔隙中因孔
道不規則而產生形變之塑性，以進一步瞭解微氣泡在超微細孔隙（如：黏土）中
的移動可行性。

3-5 界面活性劑泡膜對 LNAPL 吸附之研究
本研究在管柱實驗過程中亦對微氣泡中的泡膜取樣分析，分析當中溶解的甲苯含量，分
析結果顯示實驗剛開始時氣泡中的甲苯濃度大約 90ppm 左右(如圖六)，隨著污染物的移
除出去，泡膜中的甲苯濃度逐漸降低，但是變化並不大，持續維持在 72ppm 附近，顯示
界面活性劑泡膜對於水中溶解的 LNAPL 之吸附並無特別的作用。

3.3 Residual Contaminant Analysis of Sand
(1) Surfactant solution injection test
This study gathered statistics of residual
3-Nitrotoluene of sand after the quantitative analysis
by GC-FID. The results were divided into three
vertical layers (Layer I, Layer II, and Layer III)
according to the segment of Fig. 3. Figure 6 showed
that the removal efficiency of the central zone was
higher for Layer I. The residual 3-Nitrotoluene was
around zero for the central blocks. The maximum
mass of residual 3-Nitrotoluene of sand was 0.14
g/cm3 at (27, 2.5). This result indicated that the
injected solution flow channel concentrated at the
central zone and flushed out most of contaminant in
this zone.
Figure 7 showed the residual 3-Nitrotoluene
distributions in sand of Layer II for the surfactant
solution injection test. This figure showed two flow
channels evidently near central zone. Hence, the
residual 3-Nitrotoluene masses of these two zones
were less than 0.05 g/cm3. But the other zones,
especially at the central and boundary zones, still
contained 3-Nitrotoluene of high mass. The residual
mass were both 0.14 g/cm3 at (15, 7.5) and (27, 7.5),
respectively.
Figure 8 showed the residual 3-Nitrotoluene
distributions in sand of Layer III for the surfactant
solution injection test. Figure 8 was similar to Fig. 6.
Both of them showed a low residual mass at central
zone. The maximum mass of residual 3-Nitrotoluene
of sand was about 0.20 g/cm3 at (3, 2.5). Besides,
The higher residual masses of 3-Nitrotoluene were
0.17 g/cm3 at (3, 7.5) and 0.18 g/cm3 at (27, 17.5).
This result also indicated that the injected solution
flow channel concentrated at the central zone and
flushed out most of contaminant in this zone.
According to results of Fig. 6 to 8, the
preferential flow occurred in this test. The injected
solution did not flow all the zones. Hence, many
sand samples still contained 3-Nitrotoluene greater
than 0.1 g/cm3. This result indicated that the
surfactant solution injection method was low sweep
efficiency in this sandbox test.

segment of Fig. 3 too. The results were very different
with that of the surfactant solution injection test.
Figure 9 showed that 70% of sand samples contained
3-Nitrotoluene less than 0.01 g/cm3. The maximum
mass of residual 3-Nitrotoluene of sand was 0.09
g/cm3 at (27, 17.5). The sand samples at (3, 17.5) and
(21, 17.5) also revealed higher residual mass of
3-Nitrotoluene, 0.087 and 0.086 g/cm3, respectively.
6 For surfactant solution injection test, the
variation of concentration was unstable. This
might indicate that flow style of injected
solution was preferential flow. The injected
solution flushed out 3-Nitrotoluene from pore
which the injected solution flow through.
 For surfactant-enhanced solution injection test,
the result indicated that the preferential flow and
air injection affect the removal of 3-Nitrotoluene
in pore. The injected air could disturb the
solution flow and change the flow channel of
injection solution. Besides, the injected air also
could efficiently flush out 3-Nitrotoluene from
pore and mixed 3-Nitrotoluene with Triton
X-100 solution.
 According to results of sand samples, the
preferential flow occurred in the surfactant
solution injection test. The injected solution did
not flow through all the zones. This result
indicated that the surfactant solution injection
method was low sweep efficiency in this
sandbox test.
 The surfactant-enhanced solution injection
method showed a higher sweep efficiency than
that of surfactant solution injection method. The
injected air might disturb the solution flow and
change the flow channel of injection solution,
increasing the removal efficiency.
This study showed that an effective application of
surfactants for pump and treat, injecting surfactant
solution with air could enhance the mix of DNAPL
and surfactant, and increasing the removal efficiency
for soil remediation.
