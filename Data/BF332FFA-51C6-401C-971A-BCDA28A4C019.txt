中文摘要  
本計畫為三年期的連續性研究，主要嘗試應用流場可視化、具高時間解析之 PIV
速度量測系統及氣泡影像測速儀 BIV 等量測技術，針對孤立波通過水平或斜坡底床時
之流場及底部邊界層進行速度量測，並進一步結合 PIV 及 BIV 量測技術嘗試針對碎波
流場中氣泡區及非氣泡區速度場進行解析，以探討孤立波於斜坡上淺化、碎波過程之
特性，建立一套完整之波浪淺化、碎波流場之量測技術。 
此外，本研究另針對孤立波通過水平底床時底床邊界層水平速度之相似特性進行
探討，本研究依流場特性分成孤立波波峰通過前(T ≦ 0)及最大逆向速度發生時兩部
分，並分別選定邊界層厚度及逆向迴流厚度 b0 為特徵長度尺度，勢能區速度 up 及勢
能區速度與最大負速度之差值(up - um)當作特徵速度尺度，即可獲得該流場之相似曲
線。另根據相似性分析結果，進一步討論孤立波通過時之底床剪應力等特性。 
ABSTRACT 
A systematical three-year investigation is proposed to experimentally study the 
boundary layer flow under a solitary wave. The characteristics of flow field induced by 
solitary wave on the horizontal bottom and sloping bottom are experimentally investigated 
using the flow visualization technique, time-resolved particle image velocimetry (PIV) and 
bubble image velocimetry (BIV). First, flow visualization was conducted to observe the flow 
pattern under solitary wave during the process of shoaling and breaking on 1:10 sloping 
bottom. The internal velocity field and boundary layer flow was measured by using precisely 
time-resolved PIV system. Second, combined time-resolved PIV and BIV technique was 
developed to obtain the velocity fields of both aerated and non-aerated regions of a breaking 
solitary wave. 
Forthermore, this study also focuses on the similarity characteristics of bottom boundary 
layer flow field induced by solitary wave on the horizontal bottom. The velocity distribution 
of boundary layer can be classified into two parts for similarity analysis. Before the passing 
of wave crest of the solitary wave, a similarity curve could be obtained by using boundary 
layer thickness () as a length scale and potential velocity (up) as a velocity scale. The 
similarity of velocity profile of maximum reverse flow is also proposed. Based on the 
similarity profile, the viscous bottom shear stress can be calculated in the study.

(三) 具高時間解析之 PIV 系統量測原理 
為建置具高時間解析度之 PIV 速度量測系統，本研究特利用高速攝影機及高功率氬
離子雷射光源分別替代傳統 PIV 量測系統之 CCD 電子耦合攝影機及脈衝式雷射光源，
增快 PIV 系統之量測速度，並配合數位影像強化技術，彌補高速攝影時質點亮度不足之
現象，另外，利用高速攝影機之連續影像擷取特性，採用多時間間距之速度計算方式，
將有效解析速度場中高速與低速間之變化情形。有關本研究建置之具高時間解析度 PIV
系統的量測原理，如下所述： 
1. PIV 量測原理 
PIV 量測主要包括雷射光源及數位攝影器材兩大部分，該量測原理主要利用雷射光
源激發水中隨流場運動之質點顆粒，使其散射雷射光源而成明亮之光點，再利用攝影機
記錄質點運動情形，以求得水流之速度場。速度場分析方法主要利用兩張連續拍攝之流
場影像，將影像畫面切割成好許多個大小相同的矩形疑問視窗，若流場中的質點顆粒分
佈均勻且所取之疑問視窗大小適當，則任一疑問視窗中應至少有一個或數個質點顆粒存
在。速度之計算方式即在第一張影像中選取任意疑問視窗(interrogation windows)，並與
  7
泡本身之散射現象，以及光頁至相機間氣泡所造成的遮蔽作用，將無法清楚呈現氣泡之
影像。因此，BIV 量測系統中之氣泡影像則採用陰影成像技術記錄流場中氣泡運動情
形，即利用四座 600 W 鹵素燈照射含氣泡之流場，如圖 6 所示，當光線照射在氣泡上，

氣泡所產生之陰影，替代 PIV 影像中之質點，本文特以此原理，再利用上述謝(2008)所

(三) BIV 速度量測檢測 
為瞭解 BIV 技術之精確度，本研究特設計另一單一氣泡量測實驗，即追蹤單一氣泡
於均勻流場中運動情形，並將實驗結果與 BIV 系統量測之氣泡速度結果進行比較，以求
得 BIV 量測系統之準確性。實驗配置如圖 1 所示，於水槽底部裝置一直徑為 1.6 mm 之
銅管，連接至二氧化碳鋼瓶，再於不同流速之均勻流場中注入氣泡，以高速攝影機利用
陰影成像可視化法紀錄氣泡之運動情形。 
由於氣泡受到浮力上升，且上升過程中亦會受到氣泡尾流的影響，導致氣泡速度忽
快忽慢，在運動過程中氣泡呈現左右搖擺且晃動情形，形狀亦不停地改變，如圖 9 所示，
因此，欲明確定義氣泡之移動速度有相當的困難性。本實驗特別根據氣泡可視化影像之
氣泡形狀，首先從氣泡影像上勾勒出氣泡之投影面積，並計算出整顆氣泡之投影重心以
作為氣泡所在之位置，如圖 10(a、b)所示。故可從連續影像中得到氣泡於不同瞬間下所
在的位置，即氣泡運動之軌跡(如圖 10(c))，再利用該軌跡計算氣泡移動之距離，除以兩
張影像拍攝之時間間距即獲得氣泡速度，圖 10(d)即為經過多次重複實驗，氣泡在不同
高度下之水平速度計算結果，由圖中亦可清楚看出氣泡運動過程中有良好的再現性。 
至於 BIV 量測結果如圖 9 所示，由於每個疑問視窗皆可計算出速度，故該量測結果

3. Experimentation 
 
Laboratory experiments were carried out in a recirculating water flume in the Department of Civil 
Engineering, National Chung Hsing University, Taiwan. The dimensions of the test section were 305 
cm long, 50 cm wide and 54 cm deep. The glass sidewalls and glass bottom of test section facilitated 
the optical observations. The detail of the performance of the re-circulating water channel was 
described in Lin et al. [2]. Four vertical drop pools with drop height H = 14 cm, pool length L = 16 cm 
and end sills of height h = 1.7, 6, 10 and 14 cm, made of acrylic plates were used in the study. Fig. 1 
shows the schematic view of a vertical drop pool in an experimental setup. The length of end sills was 
made long enough (60 cm) so that the effect of free overfall at the downstream end does not affect 
flow condition at the drop. The skimming flow condition was created at the vertical drop by providing 
necessary air tightness, which facilitated in maximizing the interface between the sliding jet and pool. 
Total 11 experiments were performed with four end sill ratios h/H = 0.12, 0.43, 0.71 and 1 under 
approaching flow discharge per unit width q = 199.32 cm2/s  513.41 cm2/s with brink depth Yb = 2.01 
cm  5.03 cm, critical flow depth Yc = 3.66 cm  6.95 cm, critical velocity of flow Uc = 59.84 cm/s   
82.64 cm and flow depth at end sill Yt = 1.68 cm  5.03 cm. Two coordinate systems were used in the 
study as shown in Fig. 1. One is the Cartesian coordinate system (X, Y) with the origin at the lower 
corner of the drop, and the other local coordinate system (s, y) along the upper surface of the sliding 
jet having its origin located at the point of intersection of the upper surface of the jet and Y-axis of the 
Cartesian coordinate system. Here, s was measured tangentially along the upper surface of the jet and 
y measured normal to the surface of the jet with positive pointing outwards.  
The PIV system was used to measure the two-dimensional velocity fields in the shear layer. The 
PIV system consisted of 5 W argon-ion laser as a light source. The laser beam emitted from the light 
source was reflected by a mirror and passed through a cylindrical rod to form 1.5 mm thick light sheet, 
which was then redirected in to the flow region through the glass bottom of the water flume. 
Aluminum particles with mean diameter 10 m were used as the seeding in PIV. A high speed camera 
with a high memory, 800  600 pixel resolution, 12-bit dynamic range and 2,000 Hz maximum 
framing rate was used to capture images of the particle laden flow. The camera mounted with 105 mm 
focal lens was operated at 1,000 frames per second throughout the experiments. Velocity fields were 
determined by cross-correlation analysis. A multi-grid process starting from 32  32 pixels and ending 
with 8  8 pixels for interrogation windows with 50 % overlap were used in the cross-correlation 
analysis. The images from three fields of views (FOVs) of size 14.1 cm  11.03 cm were taken using 
PIV [see Fig. 1] with the spatial resolution of velocity distribution in each FOV of about 1.47 mm  
1.47 mm. For the verification of velocity field and turbulence characteristics measured by PIV, the 
velocity profiles and the dimensionless turbulence intensities ( uu  )0.5/Uc profiles along s-direction 
obtained by PIV are compared with those measured by the fiber laser Doppler velocimeter (FLDV). 
The detailed comparative study is presented in Lin [4].  
 
4. Result and Discussions  
 
Flow Pattern in the Skimming Flow 
 
Skimming flow is usually found to occur downstream of drop structures under high approaching flow 
discharge [3], which can be characterized by a dropping flow with a surface jet sliding over the pool. 
Fig. 2 (a) shows the typical flow pattern of a skimming flow for h/H = 0.43 which demonstrate the 
sliding of the jet over the pool with a large vortical structure underneath. This is also substantiated by 
the time-averaged (mean) velocity field presented in Fig. 2(b).

that the velocity gradient between the sliding jet and pool intersection area is very large near the end of

Distribution of Viscous Shear Stress and Reynolds Shear Stress 
 
From the mean velocity distributions [Fig. 3(a)], the profiles of the viscous shear stress, (/)(u/y) 
are calculated by differentiating the mean streamwise velocity u(y) with respect to y, where  = 
dynamic viscosity of water and  = mass density of water. The comparison between the viscous shear 
stress and Reynolds shear stress, )( vu   relative to  is shown in Fig. 5 (b). It can be observed that 
the magnitude of viscous shear stress has maximum value at the beginning of the shear layer and its 
value decreases as the arc length s increases. On the contrary, the value of Reynolds shear stress is 
relatively small at the beginning of the arc length s while its value gradually increases along the s-
direction. However, Reynolds shear stress tends to decrease along the s-direction in the mid-span of 
the arc length s, and then increases near the end sill. Further, occurrence of the maximum value of the 
Reynolds shear stress of shear layer profile is not at the center position of the shear layer. Note that the 
variation of Reynolds shear stress in the shear layer along the s-direction is generally similar to that of 
the turbulence intensities [Fig. 5(a)]. From the variations of both viscous shear stress and Reynolds 
shear stress, it can be concluded that the viscous shear stresses are almost equal to the Reynolds shear 
stress near the drop structure, while the Reynolds shear stresses dominate over viscous shear stresses 
from the mid-span of the arc length s to the end sill.  
 
5. Conclusions   
 
The flow characteristics in the shear layer between the sliding jet and the pool have been studied 
experimentally. The velocity fields in the shear layer structure were measured using PIV for varying 
approaching flow discharges and different end sill ratios. The spatial variations of mean velocity, 
turbulence characteristics and viscous and Reynolds shear stresses were presented. The decrease in 
velocity gradient in the shear layer as the jet slides down the pool represents the growth of the shear 
layer in the downstream direction and also justifies the momentum exchange. The similarity profile for 
the mean streamwise velocity of flow in the shear layer between the sliding jet and the pool is obtained 
using the mean velocity deficit (u1 – u2) and representative thickness of shear layer bs as the 
appropriate characteristic velocity and length scales. This similarity profile shows the promising self 
preservation within the shear layer. The distributions of turbulence intensities along s-direction and y-
direction and turbulent kinetic energy show the asymmetry to the center position of the shear layer. 
The maximum viscous shear stress occurs at the beginning of the shear layer structure, and the 
magnitude of viscous shear stress at each cross-section decreases along the s-direction. However, the 
value of the Reynolds shear stress is relatively small near the drop structure, which reaches its 
maximum value near the end sill. The results show that the Reynolds shear stress dominates the major 
part during the mixing process along the shear layer between the sliding jet flow and the pool.

四位時，則可先安排將缺席者的報告時間均勻分配至其他報告人上，可讓發表人有更充裕的
時間來報告內容）。惟本次主辦單位在網站架設，網路註冊、會程之協調及安排之細緻度均咸
不足，誠為可惜之處。
