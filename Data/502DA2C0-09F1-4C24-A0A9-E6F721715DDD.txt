添加劑對再生瀝青混凝土鋪面績效之影響(I) 
計畫編號：NSC 94-2211-E-006-059 
主持人：陳建旭  國立成功大學土木工程學系 
 
一、摘要 
道路工程所使用的再生瀝青混凝土回收料
（reclaimed asphalt pavement，RAP）具有可回
收再利用的特性，可以直接達到砂石減量及環保資
源再利用等多項有利條件。但是，目前對於適當的
RAP 添加範圍目前仍無明確的學理定義，公路相關
單位也沒有適當的方法檢驗再生瀝青混凝土
（recycled asphalt concrete，RAC）中 RAP 的添
加量，使得承包商過量添加及使用更軟軟化劑的情
形時有所聞。本研究收集分別代表國道、省道、縣
道的回收料，將其老化瀝青萃取及回收，考量施工
單位現場的時效性、經濟性及簡易性，發展一套黏
度預測模式，研究結果顯示使用重油時會過度軟化
回收瀝青，且重油與老化瀝青間並無交互作用產
生，會有相分離的現象產生，因此不建議使用。本
研究提出之 Vis-Mix Model 能較 ASTM 黏度拌合圖
表更準確的預測拌合後再生瀝青膠漿 (recycled 
asphalt binder，RAB)的黏度變化，可有效將黏度
誤差控制在 7%內，且能合理的預測 RAP 的適合用
量，並提供 RAP 材料黏度的容許範圍，檢驗 RAC
中是否有添加比 AC-10 更軟的軟化劑。此外，本
研究提出之 Modified Hirsch Model 也能描述 RAB
之流變特性，有效預測 RAC 的鋪面績效。 
關鍵詞：再生瀝青混凝土、軟化劑、黏度預測模式、
流變性質 
 
Abstract 
This research investigates the effects of 
reclaimed asphalt pavement (RAP) content and 
softening agent categories on the mechanical 
properties of recycled asphalt binder (RAB). The 
viscosity and rheological teats were conducted to 
characterize the engineering properties of RAB, 
and test results indicate the viscosity, complex 
modulus of RABs decreased with increasing 
softening agent content because the additives 
softened it primarily. The viscosity predictive 
model developed in this study was superior to the 
ASTM method and able to examine the 
relationship between RAP content and viscosity 
well. It was also concluded that the allowable 
RAP content could be determined by the 
viscosity predictive model which were dependent 
on the characteristics of aged asphalt as well as 
the softening agent. The modified Hirsch model 
based on the theory of micro-mechanism 
accurately simulated the trend of RAB at wide 
temperature and RAP content ranges, which 
would help the pavement engineer to control the 
performance of recycled asphalt concrete. 
 
二、前言 
瀝青混凝土為目前各公路系統最常使用之路
面材料，具有施工便利、行車舒適及易於修補等優
點，在台灣地區約佔總道路面積之百分之九十七。
然而台灣地區土地狹窄資源有限，在各項公共建設
與各大營建工程大量使用天然砂石料下，導致作為
工程重要材料之河川砂石料源日漸枯竭，加上瀝青
混凝土每年維修刨除的量相當龐大，若不審慎處理
恐成為隨意棄置的建築廢棄物，因此砂石資源有效
再利用成為綠色營建的重要課題；而在道路方面，
由於道路鋪面工程所使用之瀝青混凝土具可回收
再利用特性，其刨除料於加工處理後，經品質管制
即可再鋪築於道路上，如此一來可直接達到砂石減
量之目的，也符合環保意識及落實資源回收再利用
的考量【1、2】。 
瀝 青 混 凝 土 回 收 料 (reclaimed asphalt 
pavement，RAP)係指從現有的瀝青混凝土路面上
刨（挖）起，運送至拌和廠打碎，依顆粒之大小分
成兩種或三種級配分堆儲放，以作為新的回收路面
材料。依照配合設計中添加適量的新鮮粒料，同時
視回收瀝青的老化程度添加適量軟化劑(softening 
agent)或再生劑(recycling agent)，使瀝青回復至未
老化前之特性，經過適當加熱程序處理，予以拌和
均勻，再鋪築於路面成為再生瀝青混凝土(recycled 
asphalt concrete，RAC)【3】。由於 1973 年中的
中東能源危機，使得國內、外學者開始重視再生瀝
青混凝土的重要性。若與傳統瀝青混凝土施工方法
比較，再生瀝青混凝土路面可降低施工成本、節省
能源及資源、維護自然景觀且能與公共設施相配
合，像在美國每年有超過 5 千萬噸的瀝青鋪面材料
被再生利用【4】。若比較工程性質方面，早期在
國內即針對不同 RAP 含量對鋪面的影響進行分析
【5】；國外也有許多研究指出，經由現場道路試
驗的結果顯示再生瀝青混凝土鋪面績效與新拌瀝
青混凝土並無太大差異【6、7】；另有文獻指出添
加適當含量的 RAP 於鋪面中，可以提升鋪面之回
彈模數、間接張力、抵抗車轍及抗水侵害的能力，
不僅有效增加鋪面績效，更能符合環保的需求，代
表再生瀝青混凝土確實有其價值及實用性【8-15】。 
近幾年再生瀝青混凝土在產、官、學致力推動
下，在產製、人員素質及施工技術方面漸趨成熟，
但是仍有許多問題尚待解決。公共工程委員會所訂
定的「再生瀝青混凝土施工綱要規範」明訂瀝青混
凝土挖（刨）除料添加比例不得超過 40%，但是對
於何謂適當的 RAP 添加範圍目前仍缺乏明確的學
理定義，加上添加 RAP 材料能幫助承包商降低工

3-3 再生瀝青複合材料流變特性預測模式

以上的材料結合形成二或多相的材料，當單一種材
料的特性不足以產生更良好的績效時，就必須添加
適當的添加劑以增強原有單一材料的性能，使其綜
合之性質同時兼具其組成成分材料特質上的優
點，而避免原各組成材料的缺點。

四、分析方法 
使用再生瀝青混凝土作為公路路面材料，可以
達到砂石減量、資源回收再利用的目的。但是基於
成本考量及對材料性質缺乏完善的瞭解，大部分的
再生瀝青混凝土路面未能發揮其績效，卻常有提早
破壞的情形發生，造成普遍存有再生瀝青混凝土品
質不如新拌瀝青混凝土的誤解。因此，為了探討國
內再生瀝青混凝土破壞的原因，充分瞭解其材料因
素及提昇國內再生技術，本研究計劃之目的如下： 
 
1. 探討回收瀝青添加軟化劑之物性及流變行為。 
2. 建立再生瀝青混凝土最佳添加比例的方法。 
3. 建立回收料添加比例檢驗模式。 
4. 建立再生瀝青膠漿流變行為預測模式。

黏度預測模式具有下列多項優點： 
1. 比以針入度值推估的 RAP 用量更嚴謹；

3. 能考量 RAP 及添加劑的材料特性，而非

4. 減少多項時間與試驗工作的成本，僅需量

之黏度，即可決定交互作用參數，進而求

5. 提出之黏度預測模式比 ASTM 方法能使
用更多量的 RAP，提高 RAC 之經濟性；  
6. 此適用於軟化劑 AC-10 及各種再生劑。因
此，本研究建議以此兩種黏度預測模式來
決定最佳的 RAP 用量。

5.2.2 再生瀝青膠漿流變行為模式 
在添加劑與回收瀝青拌合形成的 RAB 中，兩
相的微觀組合及相容性為影響 RAB 流變行為的主
要因素，因此必須從組合行為及體積特性來加以
分析，式(7)之 Hirsch Model 即為使用混和法則
(law of mixture)描述複合材料行為的典型模式。模
式中將混和的兩相以簡單的並聯(parallel phase)
及串聯(series phase)的形式，考慮各相的勁度及

六、結論 
本研究針對不同種類及含量的再生瀝青膠漿
(RAB)進行基本物性及流變行為的探討，綜合試驗
結果及預測模式驗證可得下列結論： 
 
1. 重油與回收瀝青間並無作用產生，並且會過
度軟化再生瀝青膠漿，增加 RAB 的溫感性；
重油毫無黏結作用可言，會在 RAB 中形成相
分離現象，顯示重油與老化瀝青間並無作用
產生，因此不建議在 RAC 中使用重油為添
加劑。 
2. RAB 黏度試驗結果顯示，當混摻的兩相黏度
相差越大時，RAB實際黏度與ASTM D 4887
的黏度拌合圖表偏離效果越明顯，因此必須
考慮混摻兩相的材料交互作用。研究結果顯
示，Vis-Mix Model 及 Grunberg Model 當應
用在添加軟化劑之 RAB 年度預測時，可以有
效將 RAB 的黏度預測值及實際值誤差降低
至 7%以內，其中 Vis-Mix Model 有更佳的預
測能力，可以更有效的掌握 RAP 的用量及
RAC 的工程性質。 
3. 本研究提出及選用之黏度預測模式能考量
RAP 及添加劑的材料特性來建議適合用
量，而非如一般文獻僅建議絕對用量，並且
比針入度法更為嚴謹；於現地使用時能兼顧
時效性、經濟性及簡易性，並且能協助公路
主管機關掌控 RAP 用量及評估是否有添加
不合格的軟化劑。 
4. 回收瀝青與添加劑混拌形成的 RAB，可以由
本研究中提出之 Modified Hirsch Model 來
描述其流變特性，探討不同特性之添加劑與
老化瀝青材料組合後的複雜行為表現，以有
效的預測 RAC 的鋪面績效。

ABSTRACT 
 
Storage stability is the one of the most critical issues in polymer-modified asphalt 
(PMA).  Unstable PMA leads to higher maintenance and rehabilitation costs, to shorter 
service life, and to significant reduction in long-term pavement performance.  This study 
describes the effect of sulfur on the mechanical behavior of asphalt modified by 
styrene-butadiene-styrene (SBS).  The viscosity, microscopy and rheological tests were 
conducted to characterize the engineering properties of PMA.  Without sulfur added, the 
polymer-modified asphalt was microheterogeneous and was made up of two distinct finely 
interlocked phases, especially at high SBS concentrations.  After the addition of sulfur, 
PMA showed smaller asphalt domains and a fairly homogeneous dispersion of the asphalt in 
the SBS matrix.  The compatibility between polymer and asphalt produced an elastic 
network into the asphalt.  The addition of sulfur resulted in an excellent elastic system and 
substantially increased the rheological properties of PMA.  Because of the colloidal nature 
of asphalt cements, their engineering properties of asphalt were highly improved for three 
reasons: (1) the reinforcement of the SBS polymer, (2) the physical-chemical interaction 
between SBS and asphalt, and (3) the presence of the dispersed phase resulting from sulfur.  
Therefore, highway agencies as well as contractors will benefit from using storage-stable 
PMA prepared through sulfur vulcanization.  The softening point difference between top 
and bottom layers decreased significantly and the elastic recovery increased when sulfur 
presented.  A viscoelastic model was examined and shown to be appropriate to predict the 
rheological properties of the asphalt-SBS blend mixed with sulfur.  This model could help 
pavement engineers better design the engineering properties of PMA from the individual 
component.

MATERIALS AND METHODS 
 
Materials 
 
AC-10 and AC-20 asphalt binders were mixed with two types of SBS (SBS-l, SBS-r) at 
four different polymer contents.  Table 1 lists the basic properties of asphalts provided by 
the China Petroleum Cooperation.  The molecular weight of asphalt ranges from 1,100 to 
1,200.  Asphalt is traditionally regarded as a colloidal system, and can be separated into four 
groups: asphaltenes, resins, aromatics and saturates.  The average asphaltene content was 
11.9%, while resins, aromatic and saturates consisted of 17.5%, 57.9% and 12.7%, 
respectively.  Such a composition could potentially present the desirable compatibility with 
polymers.  An SBS was supplied in the form of porous pellets by the Chie-Mei Enterprise 
Ltd., Taiwan.  The polymer is a high molecular weight, random block copolymer consisting 
of about 30% styrene and 70% butadiene in mole ratio.  Two types of SBS copolymers were 
used in this study as listed in Table 2.  Sulfur is an industrial grade product made by the 
Formosa Petrochemical Co. Ltd. The sulfur was mixed with SBS-modified asphalt at three 
different levels. 
 
Sample Preparation 
 
SBS-modified asphalt was prepared by melt blending.  The SBS copolymer was mixed 
with asphalt binders by using a preparation method developed in the laboratory to maximize 
the rheological properties and to minimize asphalt degradation.  The mixer, a model of Eyel 
4 produced by the Tokyo Kikakikae Corporation, can apply a constant mixing speed to ensure 
no voids created in the mixtures.  An "X" shape of propeller was used to stir the 
polymer-modified asphalt.  The mixing bath and the temperature control unit are a product 
of the Glas-Col Cooperation.  The SBS samples supplied by the manufacture in the form of 
a fine uniform powder are suitable for mixing with asphalt due to the high surface area of the 
particles.  The SBS contents used were 1, 3, 5 and 7 percent by weight of blend.  The 
mixing temperature was kept constant at 170°C that would produce during the 60-min mixing 
process.  Then, sulfur was added to the blend and the stirring continued at the same 
temperature for another 60 min to ensure homogeneous mixtures.  The sulfur contents used 
were 1, 3 and 5 percent by weight of SBS.  After completion, the asphalt-SBS blend mixed 
with sulfur was removed from the flask and divided into small containers.  The blend was 
cooled to room temperature, sealed with aluminum foil and stored for further testing.  Note 
that the asphalt-SBS blends without sulfur were also mixed for an additional 60 minutes to 
have a fair comparison. 
 
Transmission Electron Microscope (TEM)   
 
The morphology of the asphalt-SBS blend mixed with sulfur was observed by using a 
transmission electron microscope (Zeiss 10C analytical machine).  The samples were 
prepared by dissolving PMA in toluene (Baker, reactive grade), putting on a copper grid 
coated with Formvar, and evaporating the solvent to produce a film with thickness of around 
0.07 μm.  This technique allows researchers to study the microstructure differences between 
SBS and asphalt in PMA.

RESULTS AND DISCUSSION 
 
Phase Compatibility 
 
The compatibility between polymer and asphalt is critical to the engineering properties 
of PMAs.  The morphology was studied by using TEM to characterize the nature of the 
continuous phase and the fineness of the dispersion of the discontinuous phase.  Fig. 1(a) 
show the original micrographs of the asphalt-SBS blend mixed with 0 wt% sulfur.  Note that 
all horizontal bars in these pictures stand for a length of 500 nm.  The light phase in the 
picture represents the SBS polymer and the dark phase is the asphalt.  The particles of the 
SBS-modified asphalt appear to coalescence and their size appears large, as shown in Fig. 
1(a).  At 7 wt% SBS, the polymer-modified binder shows a continuous polymer phase with 
dispersed asphalt globules.  The asphalt particles are dispersed in the white SBS matrix 
coarsely, indicating that SBS is difficult to disperse into asphalt.  In addition, PMA starts the 
phase inversion in which the polymer becomes the dominant phase at 7 wt% SBS.  This 
phase inversion results from the SBS copolymer swollen by the oils and the asphalt enriched 
in asphaltene containing virtually no polymer.  Because of differences in intrinsic structural 
feature and molecular weight, there exists chemical immiscible between asphalt and SBS.  
Like SBS-l, SBS-r is also dispersed as small particles in the asphalt, resulting in an 
incompatible material.  Since SBS-r has more molecular weight than SBS-l, the particle of 
SBS-r tends to be larger in the presence of asphalt.  This incompatibility implies that pure 
SBS-modified asphalt has poor storage stability. 
The micrographs of the asphalt-SBS blends with 1, 3, 5 wt% sulfur are shown in Figs. 
1(b) to (d), respectively.  The size of asphalt particles becomes smaller with increasing 
sulfur, implying that sulfur promotes a uniform dispersion of SBS in asphalt matrix.  As the 
sulfur content increases, the size of asphalt particles approximately decreases from 1 to 0.01 
μm.  The decrease in polymer size means the compatibility has been improved significantly 
through a process called vulcanization.  Vulcanization is widely used in rubber/plastic 
blends to have composite’s properties as good, in some cases, better than those of polymers.  
In PMA, vulcanization occurs when a vulcanizing agent, i.e., sulfur, is added to the 
asphalt-SBS blends.  According to these morphologies, the asphalt-SBS blends mixed with 
sulfur appear to be more stable and compatible than the pure SBS-modified asphalt.  The 
presence of sulfur improves the stability of PMA.  Similar vulcanization effect is also 
observed for AC-20 asphalt and SBS-r polymer. 
 
Physical Properties and Storage Stability 
 
The effect of sulfur content on the physical properties and storage stability of PMA is 
shown in Figs. 2 to 5.  The ring-and-ball (R&B) softening point (TR&B) and the penetration

Modeling Rheological Properties of Asphalt-SBS Blends Mixed with Sulfur 
 
  For inhomogeneous materials like an asphalt-SBS blend mixed with sulfur, the engineering 
property of PMA is strongly influenced by the characteristics of each constituent that forms 
the material.  When asphalt is mixed with SBS and sulfur, this resulting mastic is a 
composite in which the asphalt is the matrix and the SBS is the filler while sulfur is a 
dispersion agent.  A micromechanical model allows the engineer to predict the properties of 
the composite given the volume percentage and properties of components.  Several 
micromechanical models have been developed to calculate the viscosity or modulus of 
viscoelastic composites.  Among them are those developed by Einstein (21), Mooney (22), 
Kerner (23), and Nielsen (24).  These models all assume that the relationship between stress 
and strain is linear.  The theory of composite systems started with the equation developed by 
Einstein.  His equation predicts the viscosity of a suspension of rigid spherical particles in a 
dilute fluid.  It is applicable only for materials filled with low concentrations of 
noninteractive filler, and the equation only provides the steady state viscosity.  Mooney's 
equation expands the Einstein equation to cover a wider concentration range under perfect 
adhesion.  The Mooney equation, however, predicts values for viscosity far too large when 
the matrix is a highly viscous material. 
 Kerner developed an equation to calculate the shear modulus of a composite provided 
there is good adhesion between the two phases.  However, Kerner's equation generally 
predicts a low modulus when the modulus of the filler is close to the modulus of the matrix.  
The equations cited above are not appropriate to predict the modulus of the SBS-modified 
asphalt because (1) they are used to calculate only the viscosity and (2) they predict 
unreliable values for viscoelastic composites when the modulus of the matrix is close to that 
of the filler. 
 Nielsen modified Kerner's equation to take into account the packing properties of the 
filler and the relative modulus of the two phases.  Perfect adhesion between two phases is

CONCLUSIONS 
 
In this study radial and linear styrene-butadience-styrene (SBS-r and SBS-l) copolymers 
were blended with AC-10 and AC-20 asphalt binders to investigate the effect of sulfur on the 
blend’s properties.  At a relatively low sulfur level, a continuous phase was present and the 
composite behaved like asphalt with moderately enhanced elastic response.  Appearance of 
a dispersed structure began at 3 to 5 wt% sulfur by weight of SBS.  At high sulfur 
concentrations the compatibility of SBS-modified asphalt was enhanced.  Micrographs 
obtained from a transmission electron microscope illustrated that the addition of sulfur 
significantly improves the storage stability of SBS-modified asphalt at high temperatures.  
SBS-r exhibited larger particles in the presence of asphalt than SBS-l.  In-situ chemical 
reactions resulting from sulfur could reduce SBS’s coarseness and enhance solubility of the 
polymer in the asphalt.  The storage stability of both SBS-l and SBS-r polymers mixed with 
asphalt was significantly improved after the addition of sulfur.  Because of the colloidal 
nature of asphalt, the mechanical properties of asphalt-SBS-l and asphalt-SBS-r blends were 
enhanced for two reasons: (1) the presence of sulfur, and (2) the swelling of the polymer.  
Increasing sulfur levels led to an increase in the complex modulus ratio of SBS-modified 
asphalt to pure asphalt, and consequently the addition of sulfur changed the mechanical 
behavior of SBS-modified asphalt.  The sulfur reinforcement became pronounced at long 
loading times.  To better understand how the engineering properties of polymer-modified 
asphalt were influenced by the behavior of each individual component, a micromechanical 
model was presented to model the rheological properties of the asphalt-SBS blend mixed 
with sulfur.
