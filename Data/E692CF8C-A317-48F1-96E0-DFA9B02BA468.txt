This work was to prepare the amphiphilic zeolites (that had the capability to adsorb large organic 
molecules on the external hydrophobic surfaces and to adsorb small organic molecules onto the 
inner hydrophilic pore surfaces, simultaneously) by grafting n-octadecyltrichlorosilane into Y, 
mordenite, and ZSM-5 zeolites. The zeolites were also mediated by the cation exchange process 
with the quaternary amine, hexadecyltrimethylammonium (HDTMA) chloride, from which the 
hydrophobic bonding by conglomeration of large C16 alkyl groups associated with HDTMA 
could render a positive charge development on the external surface of zeolites and this coluld 
adsorb anion organic molecules (such as acid dyes) with counter ion forces or the anion exchange 
process. Effects of mediating procedures on both the pore structure and surface chemistry of 
zeolites were identified with the aids of XRD, FTIR, zeta potential analysis, and nitrogen 
adsorption-desorption isotherms. The adsorption capacity of acid and basic dyes, 2-chlorophenol, 
and toluene on the modified and parent zeolites were then measured. Finally, the relationship 
between the adsorption characteristics of modified zeolites and their pore structure and surface 
chemistry was discussed to examine the potential of the above adsorbents for the removal of 
hydrophilic and hydrophobic organic pollutants from wastewater, simultaneously. 
Keywords: zeolites, alkylsilane, surfactant cation, surface modification, amphiphilic, adsorption
