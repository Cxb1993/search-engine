I. 中文摘要 
此計畫中成功合併傳統之淋幕式塗佈
法及垂落膜系統，建立一套複合式液膜產
生系統，藉由此系統產生之穩定可控液膜
來冷卻熱集中度非常高之點熱源，除此之
外定性觀測到在臨界值前後液膜之狀態變
化情形。 
關鍵詞： 淋幕式塗佈、垂落膜、熱點、臨
界熱通量 
 
Abstract 
 In the program, we succeed in 
combining the traditional curtain coating 
system and falling film system and produce a 
stable and controllable liquid film. We also 
utilize it to cool a concentrated hot spot and 
qualitatively observe its phenomena before 
and after the critical value. 
Keywords: curtain coating, falling film, hot 
spot, critical heat flux 
 
II. 前言與研究目標 
目前市面上各式的電子產品中，由於
技術的進步以及運算能力日益增強，內部
的電子迴路設計將朝著高功率元件邁進，
隨之而來的即是棘手的散熱問題。電腦是
現今用途最廣泛的電子產品，在電腦中有
許多發熱源，如中央處理器（CPU）、南北
橋晶片組、顯示介面卡、硬碟機、光碟機
以及電源供應器等，在一般情況來說中央
處理器（CPU）是最重要的發熱元件，又
其易受高溫破壞的影響而導致失效；而產
生高溫的原因，來自於晶片導線高密度低
線寬的設計，在縮小晶片體積的同時，導
線數與電晶體卻以倍數成長，使得單位面
積或體積內的消耗功率與熱通量快速上
升，造成了在晶片封裝之表面將會因高溫
產生不均勻的熱通量分佈，因此形成了熱
點（Hot Spot），再因為局部熱點的熱應力
不均而使晶片效能降低甚至導致損毀。有
鑑於此，尋求更高熱傳效益之冷卻方式將
是未來研究散熱問題的主流趨勢。然而利
用液體的相變化傳遞熱量之熱傳效益為目
前一般強制空冷的 50 倍甚至更高，因此可
以得到極高的熱傳導係數，達到使晶片均
溫的效果。 
如上述，光電產品的熱管理勢必成為
二十一世紀技術研發的一個主要課題與挑
戰；然而目前液體薄膜散熱技術遇到一些
問題，尤其應用在以熱點為發熱形式之熱
源上，容易因為熱點所造成的熱集中效應
致使液體薄膜破壞，進而使整個冷卻系統
失效。為了了解液膜冷卻系統之整體效應
及其散熱極限之瓶頸，吾人欲利用連續流
動之液體薄膜流經由熱點（Hot Spot）發熱
造成之非均勻熱平面，探討薄膜破裂之臨
界條件。 
 
III. 文獻探討 
在一般液體散熱元件設計時，設計者
在選定工作流體時，一般只考慮到流體之
沸點是否高於元件之操作溫度，而未考慮
到溫度效應對流體之表面張力的影響。但
由於現在電子元件越作越小，散熱流體管
道也到達微米尺度，因此在這微小尺度
上，流體之表面張力扮演一重要角色。 
目前文獻中有許多種方式產生表面張
力梯度(surface tension gradient)，大致可分
為下列幾種：(1)改變固體表面化學結構，
例如：在同一表面上各塗佈上不同性質之
物質，而液體會朝向表面張力小的區域移
動[1]。(2)使用光線驅動感光物質改變其構
形，得到不均勻之表面能量[2]。(3)電毛細
 4
液體無法順利填補熱源上之空洞，使得加
熱元件溫度過高而燒毀。因此若希望操作
在最高之熱傳功率而不至於將元件燒
毀，最佳之操作區為 (a)區，即液柱流
(Rivulet flow)尚未生成時。 
  在先前文獻中所有關於液體流過熱源
冷卻之研究，皆直接利用重力拉動流體產
生一垂落膜(falling film)來冷卻熱源，而液
膜厚度 (film thickness) 及其流速 (flow 
velocity)直接受限於所供應之流體流量
(flow rate)，而無法將這兩個重要參數獨立
操作。因此在本研究中，利用一台傳統之
淋幕式塗佈機並加以改良，製造一可獨立
操作液膜厚度 (film thickness)及其流速
(flow velocity)之液膜產生系統。

VII. 結論 
在此計畫中成功合併傳統之淋幕式塗
佈法及垂落膜系統，建立一套液膜產生系
統，藉由此系統產生之穩定可控液膜來冷
卻熱集中度非常高之點熱源。且定性觀測
到在臨界值前後液膜之狀態變化情形。 
而下一步研究目標則將由此計畫結果
延伸，除了定性之觀察，將進一步定量量
測其熱流量之臨界直與液膜流動之厚度與
速度之關係。

DESIGN AND FABRICATION PROCESSES 
 
To design a microvalve, the sealing ability and the manufacturing procedures are the 
most important factors. We have chosen the active valve, which has the best sealing ability, as 
our design. Since the heating is commonly harmful for bio-cells, and the ceramic-based 
valves are also costly. Hence, a PDMS-based pneumatic membrane microvalve, developed by 
the replica molding method, is chosen in this paper.  
 
A two-level channel with a membrane valve in the connecting part has been designed as 
shown in Fig. 1(a), to replace the one level deformable channel (Unger, 2000). The working 
fluid goes up from the inlet port in the bottom layer, flows through the connecting part in the 
middle layer and then going out from channel outlet. When the valve acts, the membrane will 
increase the flow resistance of connecting through hole to reduce or even stop the flow, as 
shown in Fig. 1(b).

EXPERIMENTAL DESCRIPTIONS  
 
Channel Fabrication System 
  Air plasma treatment is used for bonding PDMS channels and the cover layer. A 
plasma cleaner (HARRICK, PDC-32G) has two main functions: cleaning by removing 
organic contamination and modifying surface property by chemical reaction induced by 
plasma. The RF frequency was set from 8 to 12 Hz (RF power range is from 6.8 W to 18 W). 
Besides, vacuum environment which is provided by a vacuum pump (ULVAC, GVD-050A).  
 
Air Supply system 
Air is used as gas source supplied from a gas tank with filter. An electronic pressure 
regulator (TESCOM, ER3000) with resolution sensitivity of ± 0.05 % in full scale output 
and controlled by a computer via RS-485 interface is used. The resolution of pressure control 
by ER3000 is 0.01 psi. The response time of pressure regulator has been tested as 250 
mini-seconds for rising from 10 to 90 psi, and 650 mini-seconds falling from 90 to 10 psi, 
respectively. The control signal is read by the internal gas transducer in ER3000, and the 
output pressure is controlled by means of PID (Proportional Integral Differential) method. 
Pressure signal can be read out by computer and monitored via the software offered by 
TESCOM. 
 
Liquid Supply System  
A syringe pump (KdScientific, KDS 200) is used to infuse liquid into liquid channel. 
Constant dispense volume and flow rate can be controlled by KDS200.  
 
Image Recording System  
To investigate valve action response and the flow controlling process, the image 
recording system has been used. The image recording system included a microscope 
(Finescope, FS-180, 30 fps) or high speed camera, and image record card. The magnification 
factor of FS-180 microscope is 10 to 180 and working distance up to 190 mm. For higher 
quality of recorded image, an extra LED light module below microvalve chip is needed. The 
high-speed camera (Kodak, Motion Corder Analyzer) is used to investigate the valve response 
time. The frame rate of the camera is up to 10,000 fps, and 1/20000 sec shutter speed. It is 
constructed above the channel to analyze the valve operation condition.

Response Time test 
 
High speed camera with 2,200 frames per second is used to catch the water/air interface 
and the valve response time was calculated by analyzing those images. The serial variation of 
the interface moving speed are shown in Fig.11(a) and (b). As shown in previous experiment 
(Fig. 9), it needs at least 1.9 psi to seal the water flow under Pin=2psi. As Pv changes suddenly 
from 0 psi to 1.9 psi, the microvalve should be fully closed by the added pressure. Based on 
such condition, the response time has been measured. It takes about 20 ms for the valve from 
fully open to fully close as shown in Fig. 11(a), and about 120 ms in opposite direction, from 
fully close to fully open status. 
Fig. 11(a) shows another interesting phenomena that the flow speed increases a little bit 
in the suddenly closed transition before decreasing to zero, as the circle part indicates. The 
overshoot phenomena may come from the expansion of the valve membrane and push the 
liquid forward. When the blocking flow rate is more than the draining flow rate from the 
membrane expansion, the flow speed will decay as expected.

Liquid switching test 
 
In μTAS, all the reaction time, spices, and quantity have to be controlled accurately. The 
present pneumatic microvalve, which has high sealing ability and small response time, can be 
utilized to switch the liquid sequentially for transporting the working fluids. In our 
experiments, even only one of the two valves (e.g., Valve 1 in Fig.12 and the other valve is 
keeping open) can also produce a sequentially segment flow. When Valve 1 is opened, Fluid 
1 runs into the cross area and replaces Fluid 2. When closing the valve 1, Fluid 2 would then 
cut off Fluid 1. Based on this principle, we can control the timing of the valve acting sequence 
to get different length ratio of liquid segments.
