劃結果的可靠性，本計畫利用隨機最佳規劃(stochastic
programming)理論，將風險(risk)觀念導入至多目標規劃
中，配合適應性基因演算法(adaptive genetic algorithm)
及高效率隨機電力潮流(probabilistic load flow)演算法
將負載量與設備投資設成本的變動考慮在分析中，以克
服輸入參數變動對分析結果的影響，獲得更合理穩健
(feasible robot)的最佳解。透過本計畫所建立的分析模
式，可有效整合分散型電源至配電系統擴充中，降低其
負面影響並轉化為對配電系統供電有正面助益，以增加
系統擴充規劃的彈性，並提供充裕優質電源，滿足不同
負載區域用電需求。
三、研究方法與成果
本計畫進行的方式如下所示︰
1. 蒐集國內外有關參數不確定性對配電系統擴充運
轉規劃的影響之相關資料，以作為本計畫執行之參
考。
2. 建立含不確定資料之配電負載潮流分析模式。
3. 建立為克服參數不確定性之整合分散型電源之配
電饋線擴充規劃數學模型，並根據不同區域電網負
載用電特性，建立系統運轉規範，作為最佳解計算
之限制條件。
4. 建立考慮不確定參數之隨機配電饋線擴充規劃之
求解數學模型。
5. 彙整IEEE饋線測試系統資料，並研擬不確定參數資
料。
6. 執行參數不確定性對分散型電源最佳配置之影響
分析。
7. 執行整合分散型電源之隨機配電饋線最佳擴充規
劃實例分析。
8. 執行輸入參數，包括不同規劃目標權重值、不同規
劃時程、不同供電品質要求等對規劃結果之敏感度
分析。
9. 系統整合測試與綜合討論。
10. 期末報告。

型

Step 0-輸入資料。輸入程式執行所需資料包括網路參數
資料及負載資料，負載成長需求及變動量資料，配電設

圖 1 本計畫建立之考慮參數不確定性之整合分散型電源之
配電饋線擴充規劃分析模式之求解流程
Step 1-隨機產生族群。利用亂數隨機產生染色體之控制
變數初始值。染色體結構將包括網路重組開關位置狀態、
分散型電源裝置容量及位置、電纜升級容量。然後進行
每組染色體的解碼動作，將二進制碼轉換為所設定的實
際數，此實際數可能為二位元開關控制(即 0 或 1)，或
其代表為一實數。本計畫採用二位元格雷碼基因編碼方
式，染色體的第一個基因部份是透過某些既設或新增開
關來判斷網路重組的可能性及決定線路開關的最適位
置，然後決定最適開關操作狀態，以保持放射狀網路結
構及達成目標函數之要求，這個部分可透過網路饋線重
組演算法來達成。此部分基因將包含兩個位元，第一個
位元用來表示該線段是否安裝開關，第二個位元為開關
狀態。第二與第三個基因部分為在電網中分散型電源裝
設位置與其容量的大小及線路升級的位置與等級，在此
將每個安裝 DG 的候選節點表示成多個位元來表示 DG
不同的安裝容量。線段升級部分則用一個位元來表示，
即表示該線段是否升級。
Step 2-將族群內的每條染色體進行隨機負載潮流計算。
擷取負載成長需求資料及變動量資料，並且將透過放射
狀限制條件下的每一條染色體，使用式(13)至式(16)計
算隨機電力潮流，並取得相對應於每條染色體之節點電
壓及線路電流之統計資料，包括期望值與標準差。此步
驟最後將同時儲存電力潮流計算結果之統計量，作為
Step 4 中的隨機適應性函數計算使用。
Step 3-判斷代表每條染色體之系統運轉物理量之統計
資料是否滿足隨機限制條件。將估算出代表每條染色體
之節點電壓及線路電流之期望值與標準差對應到式(9)
至式(10)之隨機限制條件進行判斷。若符合全部的限制
條件則繼續至 Step 4，若不符合的染色體則跳回 Step 1
重新產生，直到所有染色體皆滿足限制條件為止。
Step 4-計算隨機目標函數之統計量。擷取輸入參數變動
資料，再執行點估測法連同之前 儲存的各運動物理量
的各動差資料，計算每條染色體之目標函數的平均值及
標準差。
Step 5-計算適應性函數。根據 Step 4 之計算結果，使用
式(4)算出各染色體之隨機適應性函數並依序排列，找
外，饋線平均故障率假設為 0.1failures/year-mile，平均
修護時間為 3 小時，開關切換平均時間為 0.2 小時。考
慮之目標函數有缺電指標、線路損失、平均電壓變動、
及成本投資，各目標函數之權重值均設為 1/4。擴充規
劃時程僅考慮 3 年後之短期負載成長，其負載總容量為
2021kW。規劃年之設備成本之期望值如上所述，負載
資料之期望值可由負載成長率推算。強化設備成本及負
載資料的不確定性大小即代表對該資料於未來時段無
法掌握的程度，一般可由變動範圍來表示。假設強化設
備及負載量資料的變動量可由歷史資料統計或工程人
員判斷得知，則於不同規劃年度之設備成本及負載資料
變動量可由不同大小的標準差()來表示。本計畫假設
短期規劃之輸入參數變動量由 1 倍來表示，其中假
設為該規劃年之設備成本或負載資料期望值的 5%。
以本計畫提出的分析模式規劃三年後的配電饋線
擴充規劃，分析結果並與用解析規劃法之分析結果進行
比較。首先於解析規劃法中，我們將強化設備實際成本

資料情況下，另以下列三種可能的負載運轉模式來表示

模式一：低負載運轉量，以低於負載量期望值一個標準
差來表示負載資料。
模式二：中等負載運轉量，負載量等於期望值差。
模式三：高負載運轉量，以高於負載量期望值一個標準
差來表示負載資料。

5. 不確定參數變動程度之敏感度分析
由於每年用戶負載需求增加與配電設備原物料造
價或匯率變化會因經濟、氣候等因素有很大的差異，為
瞭解未來負載量與強化設備投資成本等不確定因素之
不同變動程度對規劃結果之影響，本計畫也執行不確定
參數變動量對隨機配電饋線擴充規劃之敏感度分析。在
本分析測試中，規劃時程假設為短期，隨機規劃目標函
數之期望值與變異量之權重值均設為 0.5，隨機限制條
件參數皆設為 2，分別以不確定參數變動量之標準差與
期望值佔比為 5%、10%、20%、30%進行測試，測試結
果如表 6所示。表 6可看出當不確定參數之變動量增加，
表示輸入資料的正確性無法確認程度提高，所需之強化
設備及投資成本也隨之增加。當不確定量之標準差與期
望值佔比為 5%時，在這種狀況下，為滿足用電需求及
維持系統運轉安全，必須新增 7 個線路開關、5 部總裝
置容量為 1000kW 之 DG、升級 9 條線路，此時強化設
備之投資成本為$3,777,865，DG 滲透率為 49.48％。當
不確定量之標準差與期望值佔比增加至 30%時，新增的
線路開關增加至 9 個、新增 DG 增加至 6 部，但總裝置
容量不變亦為 1000kW、線路升級增加至 11 條，強化
設備之投資成本增加至$4,078,498，DG 滲透率不變。
這是主要是因為不確定參數變動量之標準差與期望值
佔比提高，使得不確定性參數的變動範圍也擴大，即增
加未來規劃的風險程度，為減少因輸入參數變動量造成
的規劃風險，必須投入更多的電網強化設備，以克服參
數的不確定性。至於合理的不確定參數變動量是多少，
則必須由分析者視電力公司投資政策來加以決定。測試
結果也顯示當不確定量增加時，新增線路開關及線路升
級等強化措施之規劃應用也隨之增加，DG 裝設位置會
有所差異，但總裝置容量卻都相同。這主要是因為 DG
設備單位投資成本較其他兩種強化方案要來得高，因此
當不確定量增加時，為使投資成本、線路損失、供電可
靠度、供電品質同時能夠最小化，新增線路開關及線路
升級會比 DG 配置更常被使用於擴充規劃結果中。

Abstract -- To increase the competition in the future global 
shipbuilding markets, CSBC Corp., Taiwan (CSBC) has started 
to do an expansion plan to install more efficient facilities for 
valuing productivity and improving the overall efficiency of 
plant operations. The shipyard, however, has no power SCADA 
system due to old building ages and cost considerations and 
consequently real-time measurements for most load buses in the 
system are not available. For future expansion purposes, CSBC 
tries to develop a methodology for expansion planning of the 
shipyard power system. This paper presents the methodology 
and expansion planning results. A two-stage load estimation 
model that combines typical load characteristics and state 
estimation (SE) theory is developed to provide the daily load 
curves for all buses in the industrial distribution network at a 
sound accuracy with relatively few real-time measurements. It 
makes use of network information to check the validation of 
their estimates and even to eliminate errors in estimated load 
data so that the resulted estimates can match the available 
measurements and comply with power system operation 
relationships and constraints. The load estimation results that 
could reflect true system operation are then used as a base-case 
for expansion planning to determine proper system 
reinforcements to accommodate an increase in the site’s 
electrical demand. The analysis model developed in this paper 
can be used for expansion planning, or possible performance 
measure of similar industrial power systems. 
 
Index Terms-- Industrial Power System, Load Estimation, 
System Planning.  
I.   INTRODUCTION 
The CSBC Corp., Taiwan, (CSBC) manufacturing facility 
is located on south part of Kaohsiung city, Taiwan, and is 
considered one of Asia’s largest shipbuilding facility. New 
building takes the largest portion of the corporation’s 
business and hundreds of various types of ships for both 
local and foreign owners have been delivered. Inherited from 
the past experiences and exerted in developing new 
techniques, CSBC is today a ship builder standing high 
among those leading ones in the world. 
The CSBC shipyard’s electric power system was in 
service in 1976. The shipyard has steadily expanded in the 
past 34 years to include different types of facilities, such as 
cranes, cutting machines, hydraulic bending machines, super 
carriers, and building dock. The initial electrical demand of 
the facilities was about 5,700 kW and grew to 22,800 kW in 
2009. The site was initially supplied from the transmission 
grid by one 12 kV overhead feeder through one main 
transformer. This was later expended to include a second 12 
kV overhead feeder and a second main transformer. 
To deal with the challenges in the future global 
shipbuilding markets, CSBC has started to do an expansion 
plan to install efficient facilities including trolleys, 
centrifugal pumps, and welding machines for valuing 
productivity and improving the overall efficiency of plant 
operations. Furthermore, electrical power consumption at the 
shore side has rapidly grown for commercial ships. The 
environmental impact of the pollution created while at berth 
has many ports restricting the operation of onboard 
generators. Shutting off these on-board generators and 
supplying ship’s power from the shore power system can 
reduce the air pollution emissions. With the development of 
medium voltage power plugs and receptacle assemblies, cold 
ironing projects for larger ships are more prevalent [1]. This 
movement has been recognized by the ship owners and shore 
power connection aspects are introduced into the ship 
building. The expansion plan also includes the construction 
of a new wharf in the shipyard for testing on bard shore 
power connection equipment. All these facilities are being 
designed and will be in-service in the coming few years. 1 
The electric power system expansion planning is a 
decision-making process that evaluates the performance of 
the future system, determines the effectiveness of alternatives 
for modifying the existing system, and identifies the best 
schedule of actions to achieve the system operation’s goals, 
such as system security, power quality, and reliability, at a 
reasonable cost. The system expansion critically depends on 
the availability and accuracy of real and reactive load data 
that reflects the true condition of the current system. These 
data usually can be obtained from power SCADA system 
installed in industrial power systems. The CSBC shipyard, 
however, has no power SCADA system due to old building 
ages and cost considerations and consequently real-time 
measurements for the load buses in the system are not 
available. As the system planning and control functions are 
adopted for economic and operational benefits, a good load 
estimation that can reflect true system operations and provide 
real-time load data becomes more important.

IV.   LOAD ESTIMATION FOR INDUSTRIAL POWER SYSTEMS 
In a large industrial power system network, real-time 
measurements may be unavailable or fewer so that the 
network may be unobservable. In order to overcome this 
problem, a procedure that involves the use of “pseudo-
measurements” is used in the estimator computation for load 
estimation. To determine the values of pseudo-measurements 
without measuring it, it should have some knowledge about 
the power system beyond the measurements currently being 
made. In this paper, load-bus injection powers are 
considered for pseudo-measurements. Then the load-bus 
transformer rating data that show the relationship between an 
individual load and the total system load can be used to 
estimate each load-bus active and reactive injections evenly 
by knowing the total power being consumed and estimating 
the network losses. Using the most recently estimated values 
from the estimator as pseudo-measurements, it can provide 
the state estimator with a reasonable value to use as a 
pseudo-measurement at any load bus in the system. The 
system states obtained from the state estimator with pseudo-
measurements and relatively few real-time measurements 
then are together used to estimate the injection for all load 
buses in the system. The concept of the proposed method is 
similar to that used in [9]. Its mathematical model and 
computation procedure are shown as follows.

In general, for system expansion the network 
reinforcements mainly contain network voltages, power 
flows, and fault level managements. Although the network 
upgrading alternatives have been used separately, issues of 
fault level, voltage control and power flows are often inter-
dependent and in practice need to be considered concurrently 
in the analysis. From the test results in Tables VI, VII, and 
VIII, it can be seen that among all expansion scenarios 
considered in this paper the centrifugal pumps group 
significantly affects the system performance. For maintaining 
system operation security and supply quality, the simplest 
way for network reinforcement is to directly use proper 
alternatives for simultaneously managing network voltages, 
power flows, and fault level in the system with the 
centrifugal pumps group. Another effective way is to change 
its connection location to a bus with higher voltage level 
through proper connection devices. Fig. 7, Fig. 8, and Table 
IX show the analysis results of bus voltages, short-circuit 
currents, and line currents when changing the connection 
location of the centrifugal pumps group from bus B51 to bus 
B2, respectively. Test results have shown that when the 
centrifugal pumps group is connected to the bus B2, the 
network voltages, line flows, and fault level can be 
significantly improved. This maintains the system supply 
quality and operation security against future demand increase.

VI.   CONCLUSIONS 
Load estimation is an important task for industrial power 
systems with no power SCADA data. A two-stage load 
estimation based methodology has been proposed for the 
system expansion planning. At the first stage, power 
consumption measured at outgoing feeders supplied by main 
transformers and large loads, network loss data, and the 
transformer rating and power factor data for load buses are 
taken into account to estimate injection at the load buses. 
The WLS state estimation and standard power flow 
equations are then used in the second stage to refine the 
estimates obtained from the first stage by including the 
relatively few real-time measurements. Test results of a 
practical industrial power system have shown that the 
proposed methodology can provide the daily load curve for 
the load buses at a sound accuracy and consequently the true 
system operation performance can be thoroughly represented 
for providing necessary base case data for system planning. 
The results also showed that it has been easy to integrate the 
proposed load estimation model into the system expansion to 
assist the engineers in planning similar industrial power 
systems.
