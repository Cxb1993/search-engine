I. 簡介 
近年來，車用資訊系統[1]傳輸已經被廣泛的使用
在Vehicular Ad-hoc Network(VANET)環境傳送即時服
務[2][3]，例如即時影音傳送給高移動速度的汽車，較
小的無線傳輸範圍容易造成可靠性(reliability)下降和
無法滿足即時服務應用的Quality of service (QoS)。 車
載資訊系統可適用於多元的無線網路環境包括了： 
WiFi [4]， 3G/3。5G 行動通訊 [5][6]， Worldwide 
interoperability for Microwave Access (WiMAX) 網路
環境 [7]， 或Heterogeneous Wireless Networks(HWNs) 
由不同的無線網路構成[8]。 
根據不同的網路特性，例如：頻寬，無線存取裝置，
無線傳輸範圍，以及傳輸干擾…等， 和移動的特性造
成較多不可靠的無線網路連線。 所以，即時群播傳送
比單點傳送更關鍵， 然而，在文獻[9]即時傳送封包的
延遲時間建議小於100-200(ms)並且為較低優先權的封
包，例如，best effort的網路特性就無法提供給即時傳
送服務[10][11]。 文獻[12]，影像封包(chunks)必須要
在有限可用的頻寬下傳送並且滿足時間延遲的限制。 
車載網路，主要的目標是提供即時串流服務應用， 
並且要能提供封包(chunk)延遲時間滿足即時影音所要
求的範圍; 其他方面，串流影音的品質是非常嚴格避免
品質下降和影像中斷[13][14]。 所以，部份議題主要探
討：有限的頻寬，短暫的連線率，高動態不穩定的拓
樸(topology)，無線傳輸干擾，訊號衰減，多路徑傳輸
衰減，以上在VANET提供即時影音服務都必須考量。 
提供即時串流傳輸在車載網路(VANET)以及(異質網
路環境)，文獻Bucciol et al。 [15]提出一個採用群播傳
送串流影音的方法，從傳送端傳送串流封包給指定的
群組。 此外，文獻[15]考量不同的傳輸封包提供適合
的傳送方式。 在群播中，每一個接收者都處於不同的
網路環境狀況，而造成最差頻寬的接收者明顯造成群
播效能下降[16]。 
此外，在P2P(Peer-to-Peer)網路環境可分成三類
[17]：Pure P2P， Hybrid P2P and Mixed P2P，由下列
描述： 
-Pure P2P：不需要中央伺服器，例如，每一個節點在
Pure P2P都是獨立。 
-Hybrid P2P：可視為採用集中式的架構，並且透過群
組管理可以提供給路由管理以及資源分享等服務。 
-Mixed P2P：結合Pure P2P and Hybrid P2P兩種特性。 
P2P網路，提供低傳送封包延遲以及穩定傳送封
包，給予實現即時串流服務[18][19]， 因為P2P不需要
提供任何feedback機制，所以無需考慮降低feedback的
負載。 文獻[20]，以P2P為基礎串流傳輸可以實現幾個
優點： 低頻寬浪費率，負載平衡， 容易佈署於現行
的網路環境和可適性。 文獻[21]， Zhengye et al。提
出一個典型的P2P串流傳輸架構，由peers和tracker組
成，而 tracker是管理分享的封包資訊和控制公平性
upload/download給每一個節點。 
文獻討討[22][23][24][25][26]提出了在異質網路環
境採用P2P串流傳輸改善的部份，而異質網路是由不同
的網路組成包刮： WiFi， WiMAX and 3。5G。 改善
的部份包刮： 1)增加頻寬的使用率[22]， 2)平衡負載
以及降低時間延遲[23][24]， 3) 群播傳送在MANET
的封包路徑[25]， 和4)在異質無線網路結合p2p即時串
流影音。 
所以，我們提出一個方法來保證可以滿足在車載網
路環境下提供即時串流影音服務，  方法為
Delay-Guarantee chunk Reloading Algorithm(DGR)，採
用從 P2P clustering來補償 lost chunk和 unconfirmed 
chunk。 尤其， DGR可以決定一個最短的傳送延遲時
間來滿足即時串流封包的限制。 所以由我們提出的
DGR方法在車載群播網路下提供即時影音服務並且能
滿足QoS。 
在以下章節可分為。 第二章描述網路環境和定義
執行效率的比較。 在第三章詳細說明方法在車載網路
下提供delay-guarantee real0time streaming ， 第四章評
估方法的表現以及與其他方法比較，最後第五章為結
論以及未來的研究。

3.1.2 Phase 1-2 The cluster tracker determination 
phase 
相關文獻探討[31][32][33]針對在變動的網路環
境下選擇cluster header。 在車網路環境中，當cluster 
header的位置在中心，可以有最短的delay到達在相同
cluster的其他節點。 然而，選擇中心的車輛是非常困
難的，因為會產生大量的訊息負載來計算車輛位置並
且導致非常高的計算複雜度。 為了達到簡化以及有效
率，在此篇文章所以選擇車輛最多的鄰居數(degree)來
推選為cluster head，例如： 車輛有最多的鄰居數量，
推選為tracker。 結果，在cluster中tracker可以容易與其
他節點建立連線。 通常，在車載網路環境中車輛位置
在cluster中間一定會有大量的鄰居數，所以透過決定
tracker演算法以 cluster中心為基礎有效的推選出
tracker。 
Tracker 會管理所有分享的chunk資訊，而這些資
訊是由同一個cluster receivers成功接收chunk資訊更新
給tracker。而Tracker管理分享的chunk資訊包括了：
stream ID， chunk ID，分享節點IP address， cluster ID，
和 multicast group ID 等。

3.2.3 Phase 2-3  Leaving a P2P cluster 
當節點要離開P2P cluster或multicast group，將
傳送一個離開訊息通知tracker。 所以，tracker設定
離開的peer所分享的chunk資訊validate bits為”0”，
Fig.7說明receiver離開P2P的過程，並由三個步驟詳
細說明。 
步驟一，當車輛A(multicast receiver)準備離開
multicast group，車輛A傳送Leave_Multicast_Req訊息
給 multicast server ， 然 後 server 會 回 傳
Leave_Multicast_Rep訊息告知成功離開。 
步驟二，車輛A傳送Leave_Cluster_Notification通
知cluster tracker T通知準備離開cluster。然後進入步驟
三，treacker T將更新兩個部份，第一部分為更新分享
peer的chunk資訊更改validate bits為”0”，之後等待一個
timeout的時間後，無效的chunk資訊將會被移除。第二
部份，tracker會重新分配節點ID資訊(Peer_ID)，最後完
成離開的動作。 
我們所提的real-time補傳方法，所分享的chunk會
逐漸舊，如果分享的peer無預警的離開，則會造成chunk
無效，所以採用tracker validate bits定期檢查，當舊掉
的chunk資訊則更改為 0v ¬ ，並根據此資訊把無效的
chunk資訊移除。

2. NETWORKS MODEL 
This section first defines the network notations and 
through the network model to explain the proposed 
integrated infrastructure of FSSH. Then we define several 
performance metrics, including route distance between 
MH and CN, handoff delay and signaling overhead, to 
compare with other approaches.
Fig. 1 demonstrates a heterogeneous wireless network 
that consists of three heterogeneous ASPs, including CIP, 
MIP, and FSSH. BGW can be used as a protocol gateway 
among heterogeneous ASPs, so the movement of MH can 
be divided into two modes, i.e., Intra-ASP and Inter-ASP. 
First, in the Intra-ASP case, MH moves between 
sun-networks of ECIP or different ECIP networks. MH 
initializes the location update and verifies user ID through 
the servers of core network, such as MIP HA, SIP Proxy, 
and RADIUS etc. Meanwhile, MH fulfills a session setup 
and data transmission with CN in Internet via SCG and 
IGW.
In the Inter-ASP case, there are two different 
situations: 1) MH handoffs in from ASP 1 s CIP to 
ASP2 s FSSH, and 2) MH handoffs in from ASP 3 s MIP 
to ASP2 s FSSH. In both, BGW is adopted to connect to 
the FSSH domain among different domains of CIP and 
MIP, and each MH is registered to individual domain s 
MIP HA and SIP Proxy. 
For considering security and reducing handoff delay, 
a simplified integration signaling overhead of mobility 
protocols is proposed for hierarchical infrastructure during 
MH roaming among Intra-ASP or Inter-ASP. The 
proposed approach is an enhanced semi-soft handoff 
mechanism that achieves two goals: 1) reducing handoff 
delay and 2) providing a policy control to differentiate 
QoS of real-time services and non-real-time services.

Then, we compare FSSH and other approaches by

among all approaches. Less the handoff delay the 
approach yields, better the performance it has.
3. FSSH APPROACH
This session first describes the network structure of 
FSSH domain, and then explains the operation procedure 
of FSSH approach, including Location Registration, 
Session Setup and Handoff Procedure. 
The wireless network structure of FSSH is shown as 
ASP2 in Fig.1. FSSH is a hierarchical mobility network 
structure that consists of a MIP-based macro mobility 
network and a hierarchical micro mobility network 
structure of ECIP. An Integrated SIP and CIP gateway 
(SCG) exists between the MIP-beasd network and any 
ECIP network. The gateway between the MIP-based 
network and Internet is an Internet Gateway (IGW) and 
that between ECIP and other ASPs is a Border Gateway 
(BGW).
Three primary interoperation functions include 
MIP-based network, intra-ECIP networks, and inter-ECIP 
networks. First, the MIP-based netork is the main wireless 
backbone network that integrates with ECIP to form the 
hierachical mobility FSSH network. Two-type gateway, 
SCG and IGW, are within it to interconnect to Internet and 
ECIP, respectively. Meanwhile, a SIP proxy and a Radius 
server are the required components for FSSH to support

3.3. Handoff in FSSH 
In traditional CIP/MIP, an MH sends CIP Routing 
Update (RU) and MIP Binding Update (BU) to update 
location [6] at the same time, when an MH initializes 
handoff. For achieving efficient handoff while minimizing 
handoff delay, FSSH considers two handoff modes: the 
intra-ECIP and inter-ECIP handoff modes, as detailed 
below. 
First, in the intra mode, MH roaming in 
intra-subnetworks of ECIP does not require changing the 
IP address because of using the same CoA. Thus, MH 
initializes handoff in the micro-mobility model is the 
same as CIP. Second, in the inter mode, MH will receive a 
new beacon while roaming to neighbor ECIP domain. 
Thus, the new SCG will act as an agent of the roaming 
MH to update the location information recorded in CN, 
MIP HA and SIP Proxy. As a result, handoff in FSSH is 
very efficient. Fig.5 demonstrates the message flow of 
inter-subnetworks in macro-mobility, in which the handoff

5. CONCLUSIONS 
This paper proposed an integrated micro and macro 
mobility approach for achieving fast seamless handoff in 
heterogeneous wireless networks. To achieve fast, 
seamless, secure handoff, the proposed approach (namely 
FSSH) integrates various secure mechanism and adopts 
the Enhanced Semi-Soft Handoff. Moreover, the policy 
control mechanism is proposed to reduce packet loss rate 
and to quarantee QoS for real-time voice service. In 
addition, the inter-connection of BGW and SCG is 
adopted to achieve fast handoff, while MH roaming from 
other mobility environments. FSSH brings several 
supreme features: 1) simplifying signaling load from the 
integration of various mobility protocols, 2) using SIP 
relay to overcome NAT issues, 3) reducing handoff delay, 
4) decreasing packet loss and 5) providing the 
inter-connection and achieving fast handoff among 
different protocols, e.g., CIP, SIP, etc. Numerical results 
demonstrate that the proposed FSSH approach 
outperforms other compared approaches in signaling cost, 
procedure time, and handoff delay while supporting 
secure authentication.

In the close-loop system, the water-filling theory can yield 
the optimal power allocation. Although the studies [12][13][14] 
adopt the water-filling-based schemes, they cannot lead to 
better results because the accurate CSI is difficult to be 
obtained. Several studies [18][20][21] try to reduce the number 
of feedback messages and to increase the accuracy of the CSI 
estimation, but the results are limited in the realistic wireless 
networks, especially in the high mobility environment. 
This paper thus proposes a MIMO close-loop-based solution 
that consists of two schemes. First, we propose the polynomial 
extrapolation-based SNR prediction (PESR) to estimate CSI 
accurately even though the sender cannot always receive the 
CSI message. Second, we propose the adaptive proportional 
power allocation (ARPA) to allocate transmission power to 
every MIMO antenna according to the determined SNR and 
MCS, and thus maximize the system capacity. 
The remainder of this paper is organized as follows. The 
network model of our strategy is proposed in Section . In 
Section , we describe the proposed adaptive CSI prediction 
and the MCS determination in detail. Numerical results of the 
proposed approach are evaluated in Section . Conclusions 
and future works are given in Section .

B. Adaptive Proportional Power Allocation (ARPA) 
In a close-loop MIMO system, the sender cannot always 
receives the feedback CSI message that includes the 
information of SNR and MCS. If the CSI message is lost, the 
sender easily uses inaccurate SNR and MCS to allocate power 
for wireless channels and then leads to low system capacity and 
high power consumption. Especially, under high mobility or 
high wireless interference, the performance is degraded 
significantly.  
This paper has proposed the PESR and AHA schemes to 
predict SNR and MCS when the CSI message is lost. Based on 
the water-filling theory, we now propose the Adaptive 
Proportional Power Allocation, namely ARPA, to compute the 
new transmission power for channel j , jP , by using the

IV. NUMERICAL RESULTS
This section evaluates the proposed approach by comparing 
various performance metrics: mean relative error, the system 
capacity, the MCS hit ratio, and the MCS change probability. 
The compared approaches include the water-filling scheme 
(WF) [19], the QoS-based water-filling (IWF) scheme [13], and 
the equal power allocation (EP) scheme [19]. The compared 
prediction algorithms include the Exponentially Weighted 
Moving Average (EWMA) [25], Mobile EWMA (M-EWMA) 
[25], Gray Model (GM) [26], and Gray Polynomial (GP) [27]. 
The evaluated parameters, as shown in Table I, of the 
system model are based on the downlink channel of the IEEE 
802.16m Evaluation Methodology Document (EMD) [1]. The 
evaluated system is an 8*8 MIMO system with White Gaussian 
noise variance, 2s . The system adopts several different 
constellation sizes of M-QAM and M-BPSK as the adaptive 
modulation and coding scheme, where 2, 4,16,64,256M . 
In addition, in the close-loop MIMO system, we assume that 
the high mobility feature is applied to the system, and then the 
MIMO channel state is always changed. The one-bit Change 
Indication Flag (CIF) is adopted to notify the sender whether 
the receiver replies the CSI message or not. If yes, CIF is set to 
1; otherwise, CIF is set to 0.
