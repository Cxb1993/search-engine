米碳管上製備出含氮碳層(N/C ratio 3.3-14.6%)的新式觸媒載體(NC-CNT)。接著使用苯甲胺做為白金

相比之下具有較高的表面積(180-252 m2 g-1)，而其擔載白金後的觸媒(Pt/NC-CNT)與商用品 E-TEK

關鍵字： carbon nanotubes, nitrogen-doped, electrocatalysis, fuel cells, oxygen reduction reaction.
ABSTRACT. A novel high nitrogen-containing carbon nanotubes (N-CNT) (14% N) as catalyst support
has been successfully prepared using aniline as a dispersant to CNT and as a source for both carbon and
nitrogen coated on the surface of the CNT. Benzylamine is further used as stabilizer to prepare Pt0
nanoparticle (1.4±0.4 nm) from Pt4+, and as the second source of carbon and nitrogen on CNT. The obtain
N-CNT has higher surface area (252 m2 g-1) than the CNT (225 m2 g-1). The Pt/N-CNT catalyst shows an
significantly high electrochemical activity surface area (111.4 m2 g-1) compared to the commercial E-TEK
catalyst (65.2 m2 g-1). Furthermore, the strong interaction of nitrogen to Pt0 is evidenced by the binding energy
obviously observed at 399.5 eV. These two factors enhanced catalytic activity toward the oxygen reduction
reaction, resulting in an enhancement of ca. 40% in mass activity compared with E-TEK.
Introduction
Nitrogen doped carbon nanotubes (N-CNT) have been studied extensively as substrate materials of
catalysts for fuel cells, due to their unique structure.[1-3] N-doping on CNT can affect the properties such as
conductivity, nanostructure and catalyst activity.[4-6] Several methods for preparing N-CNT have been used,
such as doping directly during the synthesis of carbon materials[7-10] and post-treatment of pre-synthesized
carbon materials with N-containing precursor (N2, NH3, etc.).[11-14] Additionally, Pt supported on N-CNT show
enhanced catalystic activity toward oxygen reduction reaction (ORR). ORR is a main issue in the fuel cell
community, because the over-potential loss due to the slow reaction kinetics of ORR is more than a quarter of
the total ideal electrode potential and greatly decreases the energy efficiency of fuel cells.[15-20]
In this research, a very high nitrogen-containing carbon nanotube was prepared through aniline, which acts
multifunctionally: (1) to disperse the boundle of CNT into separated line; (2) to be polymerized into
polyaniline (PANI) and then calcinated to form the N-dopped carbon char on CNT (N-CNT) for preparing Pt0
nanoparticles on the obtained N-CNT from Pt4+. Benzylamine is further used to act not only as a stabilizer but
also as the second source of nitrogen and carbon char coated on N-CNT after calcination. The obtained N on
N-CNT can reach 14%, significantly higher than the dotted N content reported for the case of pyridine[21],
acetonitrile[22] and benzylamine[7] as precursor, with 1-5% N doped into CNT, and of N2, NH3 as precursor
with 4.8-8.8% N.[11-14] Here, N dotted on N-CNT can act as stabilizer to prepare very small Pt0 particle
(1.4±0.4 nm) and enhance the catalystic activity toward ORR (up to 40%).

4Results and discussion
Fig 1a-c shows the route of the synthesis of N-CNT. First, aniline is used to efficiently separate the CNT
bundle (Fig 1b), then aniline was polymerized to form a polyanilin (PANI) layer coated on CNT in situ (Fig
1c) through the interaction with pristine CNT byπ-π interaction. During pyrolysis, the PANI was carbonized
to generate a nanostructure of nitrogen-doped carbon layer as a shell surrounding around the core of CNT
where PANI acts as the both source of carbon and nitrogen, with nitrogen doped into a carbon structure during
carbonization.
SEM was used to study the morphology and the states of dispersed of N-CNT. Fig 2a shows that the raw
CNT exist mainly in the form of large bundles with diameter of 100 nm or larger. Under the existence of
aniline, the CNT is found to be dispersed into individual nanotubes, with a diameter of 20-30 nm (Fig 2b).
After the aniline adsorbed on CNT is polymerized, it clear that the diameter of N-CNT (50 nm, Fig 2c)
becomes larger than the individual CNT, showing the PANI to be uniformly coated on CNT to produce a
core-shell morphology. Here, the N-CNT are linked by the PANI char, which acts as a new conductive
passageway to enhance the conductivity between the sidewall of CNT. The surface areas of CNT and N-CNT
are measured by N2 adsorption/desorption. It shows that N-CNT has higher surface area (252 m2 g-1) than the
CNT (225 m2 g-1). It indicates that the introduction of the coated carbon layer of PANI char increase the
surface roughness of CNT, agreeing with the observation from SEM. The higher surface area of carbon
support will increase depositing sites to help to disperse the Pt nanoparticles.
For Pt0 loaded N-CNT, the nitrogen group on the surface of CNT was characterized by XPS as shown in
Fig 3. A observed peak is reconverluted into five peaks centered at 398.5, 399.5, 400.8, 401.5 and 403 eV,
which can be assigned to the pyridinic-type, nitrile, pyrrolic-type, graphitic-type and oxidized nitrogen,
respectively.[23] It is clear that Pt/N-CNT contains large percentages of pyridinic and pyrrolic-type of nitrogen,
which are reported to be responsible for the active sites.[5,23] Moreover, the strong interaction of nitrogen to Pt0
is evidenced by the obviously observed peak of nitrile. Furthermore, the XPS analysis was carried out to
estimate the nitrogen amount of N-CNT surface layer. The N/C ratios on the surface of N-CNT were
measured to be 13.4%. Moreover, the N/C ratio determined by EDS analysis is 14.0% coincided with that by
EDS elemental mapping, significantly higher than the amount from other methods such as post-treatment of
pre-synthesized CNT with N-containing precursor (N2, NH3, etc.). It indicates that a high N dotted N-CNT
can be obtained from the carbonization of PANI coated on CNT. For the produced N-CNT and CNT, the
thermal stability was tested in air with TGA, and both start to decompose above 550 oC, attributed to the
degradation and decomposition of carbon backbone. It indicates that the thermal stability of N-CNT in air is
similar to CNT. Thus, the carbonization of PANI on CNT during pyrolysis process at high temperature (800
oC) leds to an effective transformation of PANI into the carbon layers covering the CNT.

技術說明
The present invention is directed to a method of preparing a
carbonized nanotube composite having carbon char layers, and a
method of preparing a metal-carbon nanotube composite catalyst by
loading metal catalyst particles onto well-debundled carbon nanotubes
surrounded by the carbon char layers. With the carbon char layers
serving as shells for the carbon nanotubes, the carbon nanotubes can
have increased surface roughness and surface area, and can be
provided a new conductive pathway to enhance conductivity between
the sidewalls of the nanotubes. Small nanoparticles of the metal
catalyst can be stabilized on the carbon nanotubes with a uniform
distribution when the carbon nanotubes are surrounded by the carbon
char shells.
產業別
燃料電池、鋰電池及其它以碳材為觸媒擔體的電化學系統。
