中文摘要 
本研究建立 Au-S-(CH2)10-CH3與 Au-S- (CH2)15-COOH 的混合自組膜表面，並利用 EDC/NHS
活化表面之 COOH 官能基團以固定化 Polymyxin(PMB)分子。透過調控 PMB 在表面之濃度，探
討其對脂多醣(Lipopolysaccharide)吸附性之影響。研究以橢圓儀量測膜厚，搭配表面電漿共振儀
(SPR)分析各種分子之表面吸附濃度並利用不同理論模型分析 LPS 分子與固定化 PMB 分子間之
即時動態的吸、脫附行為。結果顯示 LPS 與混合自組膜表面的非特異吸附量隨著表面 COOH 濃
度的增加而下降，而 PMB 在混合自組膜上的固定量則隨表面 COOH 濃度之增加而上升。由於
LPS 在 PMB 固定化之混合自組表面上的平衡吸附量隨表面 COOH 濃度的增加而增加，顯示 LPS
與 PMB 固定化之混合自組膜表面的交互作用隨表面 COOH 濃度的增加而趨於由特異性吸附所
主導。LPS 與 PMB 固定化表面間的動態吸、脫附曲線分析結果顯示，無論在 37℃或 25℃下，
在緩衝液 pH=8.41 的環境下進行 PMB 分子的固定化，則 LPS 分子與 PMB 分子間傾向以 1:2 的
比例結合；相對地，當 PMB 在 pH=9.41 進行固定化，則以 1:1 之吸附模型進行迴歸分析才可較
為適切描述 LPS 分子的吸附行為。由此可知，溫度由 25℃提高至 37℃所導致之 LPS 分子的相
轉移變化，並未改變 LPS 與 PMB 分子交互作用時之化學配位數；但是改變 PMB 分子固定化之
pH 值，則會使 LPS 與 PMB 分子間的結合比例產生變化。 
關鍵字：內毒素, Polymyxin B, 混合自組膜表面, 特異性吸附, 吸脫附動力分析 
Abstract 
The present study immobilizes Polymyxin B (PMB) molecules to a mixed self- assembled 
monolayers (SAMs) of alkanethiolates on gold derived from the 1-Undecanethiol and the 
16-Mercaptohexadecanoic acid and studies the binding behaviors of lipopolysaccharide (LPS) to the 
PMB immobilized SAMs with various surface concentration of COOH functional group. The dynamic 
adsorption/desorption curves of film thickness and surface concentration are measured by ellipsometry 
and SPR, respectively. Also, various theoretical models are applied to analyze the dynamic 
adsorption/desorption curves. 
Experimental data shows that the nonspecific adsorption of LPS on these surfaces decreases and 
the amount of PMB immobilized increases with the increase of surface concentration of COOH 
functional group. Since, in equilibrium, the amount of LPS adsorbed increases with the increase of 
surface concentration of COOH functional group: the adsorption of LPS molecules on a PMB 
immobilized SAMs with homogeneous end group of COOH should be mainly specific binding. As for 
the kinetics data from SPR, when PMB molecules are immobilized at pH 8.41, experimental results 
indicate that LPS bind with PMB in the ratio of [LPS]:[PMB]=1:2 for both of T=25℃ and 37℃. 
While for PMB immobilized at pH 9.41, Langmuir model of [LPS]:[PMB]=1:1 gives better results. 
The binding stoichiometry of LPS with PMB remains the same at 25℃ and 37℃ suggesting that LPS 
in gel phase (<30℃) or in liquid crystalline phase (>35℃) does not change its binding stoichiometry.  
Keywords: Polymyxin B, endotoxin, mixed SAMs, specific binding, adsorption/desorption kinetics

Title of paper to be presented: 
 
CHARACTERISTICS OF Ti-6Al-4V ALLOY SURFACE MODIFIED BY 
PLASMA IMMERSION ION IMPLANTATION FOR BIOMEDICAL 
APPLICATIONS  
 
R. Tsay 1, G.P. Chou1, W.F. Tsai 2, C.F.Ai2  
1 Institute of Biomedical Engineering, National Yang Ming University,  #155, Sec. 2, 
Li-Nung St., Taipei, 112,  Taiwan (email:tsay@ym.edu.tw) 
2 Physics Division, Institute of Nuclear Energy Research, Atomic Energy Council, 
Taiwan 
 
Ti-6Al-4V is an alloy widely used in biomedical devices because of its superior 
biocompatibility and desirable mechanical strength.  However, the poor performance 
on wear resistance of this material has greatly hampered its applications in medical 
devices, such as artificial joints.  Numerous studies have shown that wear arising from 
the counter-friction of UHMWPE element against metal is one of the major causes for 
the failure of joint arthroplasty.  The aim of this work is to improve the wear resistance 
of UHMWPE by strengthening its counter Ti-6Al-4V alloy surface using PIII technique. 
In Plasma Immersion Ion Implantation (PIII), the targeted specimen immersed in gas 
plasma is applied with a negative bias voltage, which accelerates ion bombardment 
from all directions and cause 3D ion implantation.  By properly controlling the 
implantation condition, one can increase the surface hardness of the targeted specimen 
and therefore improve its wear resistance.  In this work, treatments were carried out in 
a nitrogen plasma at bias voltages of 15 to 40 kV for 1 to 3 hours. The PIII nitrided 
layers were characterized by a secondary ion spectrometer (SIMS), a grazing incidence 
X-ray diffraction (GIXRD) and a nano-indentor.  Also, wear factor tested under 
loading condition simulating a normal knee joint is measured by a six-station wear 
testing system built based on ASTM F732. The results show that by increasing 
implantation voltage, the penetration depth of nitrogen ions increases and the surface 
hardness enhanced.  Compared to a non-modified sample, the surface hardness of a 35 
kV PIII-treated sample increases with a factor of three. The present results also indicate 
that friction coefficient decreases substantially with the PIII treatment. By increasing the 
bias voltage from 15 to 40 kV or by increasing implantation time from 1 to 3 hours, the 
friction coefficient of UHMWPE-to-PIII treated Ti-6Al-4V alloy surface decreases.

d. Wear tests 
Wear tests shows that the wear factor of an UHMWPE 
pin against a counter surface of a medical grade 
Co-Cr-Mo alloy is less than 4x10-10 mm3/(N‧m) while 
for an unmodified Ti6Al4V surface, the wear factor can 
be as high as 1x10-9 (Figure 8).  PIII nitriding of 
Ti6Al4V at 35KV for 3hrs effectively reduces the wear 
factor to less than 4x10-10 and the implanted layer is 
stable enough to sustain wear tests for more than 4 
million cycles.
