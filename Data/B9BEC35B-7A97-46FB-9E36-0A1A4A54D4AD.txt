Abstract 
The goal of this sub-project is to 
integrate the knowledge of biology, the 
technologies of digital content and digital 
learning to establish a digital learning 
environment for bioinformatics, which, in 
turn facilitates the studying of 
bioinformatics for the students major in 
computer science. 
In this sub-project, we propose a Topic 
Maps-driven framework. In most of the 
current Semantic Web visual authoring 
environments, the topic-oriented concept is 
not considered in their design, and thus the 
interface usability is not intuitive to users. 
The efficiency, quality, and 
comprehensiveness of the construction 
process also need to be enhanced. Thus, to 
propose a framework, called XRVAT 
(XTM-to-RDF Visual Authoring Tool), for 
RDF/XTM ontology construction, is one 
goal of this sub-project. The framework has 
two major design features. First, the Topic 
Maps-driven graphical user interface 
alleviates the cumbersome construction 
procedure and provides users with a clear 
top-down conceptual view. Second, the 
effective XTM-to-RDF translation kernel 
allows users to incorporate topic features 
from Topic Maps into RDF and generate 
RDF/XTM ontologies. In our study, 
usability survey and quantitative analyses 
were conducted to demonstrate the 
improvement of the XRVAT interface upon 
the previous table-based interface. 
In this sub-project, the second 
important task is to propose a graph/image 
legend retrieval method. The proposed 
method consists of two phases: legend 
extract and legend retrieval. The features 
used in our method include aspect ratio, 
number of legend components and spatial 
histograms of pixel number, border length 
and gray level. Since the processed legends 
can be properly divided into two categories: 
graph and image, type-based matching is

(2) 針對數位教學錄影影像之圖例檢索 
由於電腦多媒體技術及儲存功能的先
進發展，使得建置ㄧ以數位視訊為基礎的

本子計畫之第二重點工作是提出一針
對數位教學錄影影像內容，提供方便的圖

(3) 生物晶片之功能性基因體分析工具 
本子計劃的技術主要是應用在微陣列
(microarray)所產生的基因表現圖譜，期望
藉由資訊分類技術在大量的量測資料中找
到腫瘤特徵基因，以達到癌症分類之目
的、並有助於癌症的診斷。 現今許多關於
二元種類癌症晶片資料的分類演算法都可
以使訓練後的分類器達到 90 %-100%的分
類準確性。 然而這些方法面對基因交互作
用更為複雜的異質類(multi-class )癌症晶
片資料時，則無法有如此高的準確性，其
困難大致可歸納為二項原因： (1) 由於無
法預知區別異質類癌症的理想基因樣式，
因此篩選後得到的基因也無法有效率地區
分不同的腫瘤組織，此外這些方法的本質
一直以來也缺乏考慮基因交互作用下對分
類的影響。 (2)異質類癌症晶片資料分類必
須在少量的癌症病人資料中(4~6 樣本/種
類)來分析成千上萬的基因表現。 因此在
少樣本卻多變數的情況下，更增加了同一
類別中基因表現量測值的不確定性，也因
此降低了分類器的準確度。 
在子本計畫中，本研究將針對上述二
項問題提出解決的方法：首先提出
GA/ANN 的方法，用以考慮同時多個基因
共同相互影響下的類神經網路，從而找出
重要的基因組合來代表腫瘤特徵，並評估
用以分類的有效性。

(3) 生物晶片之功能性基因體分析工具 
A. 基因選擇－基因演算法 
基於考慮所選基因的相互作用對分類
的影響，研究方法中提出以基因演算法的
染色體作為基因組合選擇的方法，並在計
算方法過程中同時考慮一條染色體下的基
因共同表現對於分類的效果，這個方法的
優點除了同時考慮多個基因相互作用下的
分類性能，並能藉著基因演算法來演化最
佳的染色體，得到最佳的基因組合樣式用
以求得最大的分類準確性。在基因演算法
中，提供染色體不同的交配方式，包括： (1) 
one point crossover 和  (2) uniform 
crossover 來演化染色體中最佳的基因組
合。 再者，對於染色體基因的選擇將提供 
(1) stochastic universal sampling (SUS) 和 
(2) roulette wheel selection (RWS) 的方法
來選擇基因。 
 
B. 分類器設計－類神經網路

distributed processing model)或連結模式
(connectionist model)。Multilayer Perceptron 
(MLP)在類神經網路中是最常使用的類
型，主要是用倒傳導 (back propagation) 的
方式來進行演算，並且在大部分的領域中
皆可以提供較好的模型。『倒傳遞演算法』
的網路訓練方式包含兩個階段：前饋階段
以及倒傳遞階段。前饋階段：輸入向量由

2 The Topic Maps-driven Framework
The interactive Topic Maps-driven framework is called XRVAT (XTM-to-RDF Vi-
sual Authoring Tool). Users can manipulate and describe the Semantic Web ontologies
with less effort. Figure 1 shows the architecture which consists of two major modules,
namely, the graphical interface and the resource-generating module. The graphical in-
terface provides users with an interactive visual authoring interface and a graph parser
to further analyze the relationships of visual components.
The resource-generating module contains three components: an XTM generator,
an XTM-to-RDF translator, and an RTM generator. The XTM generator processes the
Topic Maps relationships generated from the graph parser and renders the XTM-format
resources. The XTM-to-RDF translator, which adopts the Ogievetsky proposal with
the consideration of its semantic completeness requirement, can then be invoked to
translate XTM syntax into RDF syntax [15]. The RTM generator takes the responsibility
to generate RDF-based resources for interoperability.
Figure 2 shows the interface design. In the XRVAT design, six visual components,
namely, Topic, Association, Occurrence, Member, Scope, and Basename, are designed
to describe the semantic relationships within the Topic Maps [16]. In the XRVAT inter-
face design, each visual component provides an interactive functionality accompanied
with a pop-up form, in which users can fill in to complete the description of Topic Maps
and generate well-formed XTM/RDF ontological resources.

II. RELATED WORK
Bluetooth topology construction has been an important
research issue in the past few years. As proposed in [11],
previous scatternet formation schemes can be classified into
three categories: single-hop, multi-hop, and optimized. In
this paper, we only focus on single-hop schemes and multi-
hop schemes, because the optimized schemes are usually for
theoretical analysis [11].
To form a single-hop scatternet, all devices are required
to be in a radio transmission range of each other. If the
single-hop formation scheme is centralized, all devices must
be in the transmission range of the coordinator. Therefore, the
coordinator has the complete knowledge of the network, and
can assign roles to all participating devices. BTCP [13] and
ASFS [6] are two good examples in which a centralized coor-
dinator or sub-coordinators take the responsibility to construct
the scatternet. Although the centralized single-hop formation
schemes can efficiently construct scatternets and perform self-
healing operations, they suffer from some of the following
three shortages. First, the leader election may take much time
to determine the coordinator at the beginning. Second, the
scale of the scatternet is limited due to the radio transmission
constraint. For example, only 36 nodes can be active in
a BTCP scatternet. Third, the coordinator may become the
bottleneck if scatternet variations frequently occur.
To cope with the disadvantages of the centralized coordina-
tor approach, several distributed single-hop formation schemes
have been proposed. For example, a randomized distributed
protocol (RDSF) uses a probability model to decide the roles
[8]. Some distributed schemes use specialized topological
structures (e.g. TSF [14] and BlueRing [3]) to construct a
scatternet. Due to the distributed nature, most of these schemes
can handle incremental device arrivals and node failures.
However, if some nodes are not within single vicinity, some
distributed schemes, such as RDSF and TSF, cannot properly
maintain the scatternet. The bottleneck problem also exists in
some distributed schemes, such as TSF.

Multi-hop formation schemes do not require that all nodes

according to the centroid distance and the remaining power.

and Bluenet, BlueMesh [12] uses a weighting approach to
rapidly construct a scatternet. Since the weight can be cal-

BlueMesh can be adjusted to meet different requirements. In
addition, BlueMesh keeps that a bridge will not participate
more than 2.3 piconets on average.
In our survey, most of the related studies do not address
the node mobility issue. Therefore, these formation schemes
may choose a node with high mobility as the master, and the
scatternet is not stable. Although BlueMesh can take mobility
into account because of its general weighting approach, it does
not further address this issue. Furthermore, BlueMesh does not
consider a situation in which more than two nodes have the
same weight. In this case, BlueMesh has more piconets than
our proposed FLSF approach.
III. FUZZY LOGIC-BASED SCATTERNET FORMATION
FLSF is a distributed multi-hop formation scheme. To
decide whether a node is appropriate as a master, the node
executes the FLSF scheme locally. The node with high remain-
ing power and high relative connectivity tends to be a master
to construct a piconet. After an election process, the master
can be decided to construct a piconet. When all piconets are
constructed, the bridge nodes are determined according to the
remaining power and the relative connectivity. Since the status
of the remaining power and the relative connectivity of each
node may be changed as time goes on, FLSF dynamically
adjusts the network topology accordingly.

IV. SCATTERNET RECOVERY
Since the devices may roam around out of the communi-
cation range or may be out of battery, the scatternet topology
needs to be adjusted if a master or a bridge is malfunctioned.
Furthermore, a new node may appear to join the scatternet.
Four cases are considered in FLSF to adjust the scatternet.
The first case occurs when a new node wants to join the
scatternet. It begins the FLSF scheme from the Initial state
to see if there is some piconet that can be joined. If no such
a piconet exists, it begins the master election procedure and
tries to connect to other piconets with bridges.
The second case occurs when a slave moves to another
piconet. In this case, the slave goes back to the Initial state
to try to join another piconet. At the same time, its original
master can allow another slave to join the piconet.
In the third case, the movement of a bridge is considered.
If it loses connections to piconets, it will check whether it is
still in a piconet. If not, it goes back to the Initial state. In
this case, the masters in the bridged piconets will be aware of
this change after a predefined period. The masters will restart
the bridge election procedure to choose another bridge.

may move to another piconet. In this situation, two masters

VI. CONCLUSIONS
In this paper, we describe a multi-hop scatternet formation
scheme based on fuzzy logic. FLSF has shown its prominence
in the number of alive node and network stability. Compared
with two related scatternet formation schemes, the proposed
FLSF scheme considers both the remaining power and the
relative connectivity for role decision. As a result, FLSF
shows its prominence in node lifetime and scatternet stability.
In addition, FLSF can effectively construct a large-scaled
scatternet because of its multi-hop formation feature.
Further studies on FLSF include considering more realistic
energy dissipation models and mobility models, conducting
more in-depth research on data transmission performance, and
making comprehensive performance comparisons with other
multi-hop formation schemes. We will also discuss the im-
provements on bridge assignment for bandwidth consideration.
The complexity of FLSF will be also studied in our future plan
to investigate its feasibility in real applications.

4   Conclusion 
The main issue in pattern recognition for microarray data classification is to identify 
useful genes for accurate classification. Our belief is that such a hybrid learning sys-
tem will identify more discriminatory feature subsets than those produced by existing 
methods for two reasons. First, GA/ANN that embedded gene selection process in 
classification task itself can explore more informative genes than rank-based gene 
selection methods. Second, neural networks describing the nonlinear relationships 
between genes for discriminating samples are more powerful than linear discriminant 
analyses. The results not only show good classification accuracy but also need the 
least number of discriminatory genes, and we hope it can be useful for microarray 
data analysis in cancer diagnosis.
