1、 中文摘要 
    本研究系探討以聚焦離子束技術(FIB)
製作之半節距 100nm以下之石英玻璃柵型
結構模仁於高分子材料上的壓印技術。我
們以 FIB 製作不同深度及寬度之奈米溝槽
結構模仁，並使用 PDMS翻印模仁製模，
研究這些模仁對於高分子光阻材料 SU-8
壓印製程的適當壓力。為求達到最佳之壓
印結果以獲得最佳之壓印參數，因此設計
實驗以針對 PDMS不同翻模參數，壓印之
參數進行探討：例如真空的影響，壓印壓
力探討其影響。本研究成功在高分子材料
SU-8上壓印出線寬約 40nm柵型結構。 
關鍵詞：奈米製造、奈米壓印、聚焦離子
束 
  
Abstract 
In this study, half pitch below 100nm 
gate-type structures of quartz glass were 
made by focus ion beam technology (FIB) as 
mold for nanoimprint on the polymer.    
Several nanotrenchs with different depth and 
width were made by FIB.  PDMS molds 
were used to make the SU-8 photoresist 
nanostructures. SU-8 on the embossed outlet 
width of 40nm nanostructures.                    
Keywords：NanoFabrication、
Nanoimprint 、SU-8 
2、 緣由與目的 
隨著製程的尺寸要求越小，光學微影
技術遭受的困難增加[1]，成本將會更高，
因此發展出熱壓成型技術，亦稱為奈米壓
印技術[2]。首先製作出元件的板模，再以
奈米壓印技術大量生產複製元件，有效簡
化生產步驟，降低生產成本，提高產能[3]。
對於奈米[4]與次微米級的微型元件複製亦
可行，為目前最具有可行性的新製程。 
奈米壓印技術已有十多年發展歷史，
在極小線寬上的研究已經十分成熟，目前
已發展出多樣性奈米壓印技術種類[5][6]。
而製作奈米壓印模仁材料的種類，不外乎
矽晶圓，石英，電鑄金屬模，及高分子模。
以PDMS高分子模仁進行奈米壓印，目前雖
有一些學者研究，但在極小線寬上的研究
卻非常有限。PDMS高分子模仁有許多優點，
成本低，製作方便，但要以PDMS高分子為
模仁進行奈米壓印，本身有許多參數需要
探討，如PDMS的成形性，而以PDMS進行
奈米壓印一些參數勢必改變，如原本以石
英模仁進行壓印，所需的成形壓力必需比
較大，但以PDMS為模仁進行壓印，所需的
壓力可能就需要改變。

In this study, we use the FIB as a lithographic 
tool to define the pattern on hard mask, and then 
transfer to ICP etcher to carry out structural 
anisotropic etching in halogen atmosphere. By 
combining the both advantages of two different 
systems, we can provide a simple method for 
nano-fabrication without any optical exposure and 
resist steps. 
2. EXPERIMENTAL DETAILS 
The process flow is shown in Fig. 2. 1cm×1cm 
P-type (100) single crystal silicon wafer are cleaned 
by isopropanol, acetone, and deionized water for 15 
minutes sequentially to remove the particles, organic 
matter, chemical substances attached on the wafer, 
then transfer to 0.5% HF solution until the native 
oxide layer is completely dissolved. After rinsing in 
DI water and subsequent drying in N2 ambient, a 
thickness of 20nm chrome is deposited on the wafer 
by sputtering as conductive layer to prevent charge 
accumulation and hard mask during FIB machining 
and ICP anisotropic etching, respectively. 
  The patterning process for hard mask is carried out 
using a dual-beam FIB with an ion source of liquid 
gallium and a scanning electron microscope which can 
allow observing the process situation synchronously. 
The FIB system consisted of a vacuum system and 
chamber, a liquid metal ion source, an ion column, a 
sample stage, detectors, gas delivery system, and a 
computer control system. The highest accelerating 
voltage is 30 keV, and the ion beam current range 
from 0.98pA to 20nA. The pressure in chamber and 
ion column was 5.7×10-5 Pa and 4.6×10-6 Pa, 
respectively. The main functions of FIB include the 
precise cutting, selective deposition, and enhanced 
etching using XeF2. 
    During the FIB patterning process, we use the 
optimized parameters to suppress structural 
broadening effect. The ion beam current is 0.98pA 
equivalent to spot size of 7nm, dwell time is 10μs, and 
overlap is 50%. 
    After the design of patterning file input FIB 
system and complete the parameter settings, FIB 
system will start to define the pattern on the Cr mask. 
In this study, the setting values of ion dose range from 
69.2×1015 to 553×1015 ion/cm2. In order to confirm 
which value of ion dose can just go through the mask 
layer, we transfer the result of FIB machining as 
shown in Fig. 3. to the ICP etcher to perform 
anisotropic etching as shown in Fig. 4. The 
measurement result is shown in Fig. 5. and the etching 
depth and width are proportional to ion dose due to the 
increase of ion dose and boarding effect. It is clearly 
shown that when the ion dose value is 276.8×1015 
ion/cm2, the FIB patterning image will transfer to 
silicon material and it is also proved the feasibility of 
the 2-step manufacturing technique of ICP etching 
after FIB direct-writing on mask. In this study, we set 
the ion dose value of 276.8×1015 ion/cm2 as the 
optimized parameter value for FIB patterning process. 
  In order to get the idea shape of nanostructure, we 
use the optimized parameters of ICP process to

3. RESULTS AND DISCUSSION 
First, we design larger scale of FIB patterns 
include grating and cylinder. After FIB patterning on 
the Cr mask and ICP anisotropic etching processes, 
the SEM results can be seen in Fig. 7. The grating 
width is 95nm, aspect ratio is about 2.16 and the 
cylinder diameter is about 201nm, and height is up to 
380nm measured by SEM. 
    Finally, we design smaller scale of patterns of 
grating and cylinder. The SEM results can be shown 
in Fig. 7 after ICP etching, the smallest grating width 
is 48nm, aspect ratio can be up to 2.35, and the 
smallest cylinder diameter is about 100nm. The 
results show the technique of direct FIB patterning 
combines with ICP etching would provide another 
simple and rapid method to fabricate nanostructures. 
4. Conclusion 
In this study, nanostructures with dimension 
under 100nm can be obtained by combining both 
advantages of maskless patterning of focused ion 
beam and anisotropic etching of inductively 
coupled plasma etcher in halogen atmosphere. It is 
really time-saving and simple method without extra 
photolithography and complicated process. With 
the finest ion beam, it is possible to fabricate 
smaller scale of nanostructure and if the dry etch 
process can combine with another form of etching 
methods such as the "Bosch process" or "low 
temperature process" with optimized condition, it is 
believed that higher aspect ratio structures could be 
also obtained easily.13
