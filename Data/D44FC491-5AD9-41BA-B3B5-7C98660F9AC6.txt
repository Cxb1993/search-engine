為無污染的能源，使用氫能源可解決地球二氧化碳排放過多、環境污染等問題，以太陽能取代
石化能源則可解決地球石化能源逐漸枯竭的問題。製氫的方法很多，以煤、石油和天然氣等燃
燒所產生的熱或所轉換成的電雖可用來分解水製氫，但顯然在還沒製氫之前就先污染環境了。
因此若能直接將太陽能源轉換成氫能源將是最佳的選擇。Anatase 相 TiO2 光觸媒由於其能隙範
圍 (energy gap, Eg) 涵蓋了「H
+
/H2」及「O2/H2O」之氧化還原電位，藉由光激發的電子與電洞
載子能量可直接分解水形成氫氣與氧氣，但由於 TiO2 能隙太高（Eg = 3.2 eV），只有紫外光可
激發出電子電洞載子，因此太陽能的吸收效率低於 3 %，許多研究皆試圖降低其能隙以提高太
陽能吸收效率[1-10]，其中以摻雜 C、N、S 等取代 O 有較佳的效果[3-5]，但至目前為止其對可
見光吸收率的提升仍然有限。由於摻雜會提高載子復合速率而抵銷部份太陽能轉換效率[5]，因
此摻雜濃度之最佳化及如何利用奈米結構提升對可見光的吸收效率乃當務之急。 
三、研究目的 
利用 C、N、S 等元素摻雜取代 O 已被發現可延伸 TiO2 的光學吸收截止波長至可見光區
[6,7]，但這些摻入的雜質也同時作為電子電洞載子的復合中心，降低了它們的擴散距離，許多
電子電洞載子還來不及擴散至表面參與觸媒反應時即被復合掉了，造成光觸媒效率無法有效提
升。為了讓載子能有效的擴散至表面參與反應，TiO2 薄膜要愈薄愈好，然而降低薄膜厚度卻同
時降低了薄膜對光的吸收率，若能將薄薄的 TiO2 膜沉積在多孔性基板上，相信可以有效提升
整體光學吸收效率，獲得好的光觸媒效果。本研究期望以原子層沉積法成長優良的 TiO2-xNx 薄
膜於多孔性金屬基板上，藉由光在孔隙內的多重反射使薄薄的 TiO2-xNx 膜即能達成高可見光吸
收率，並同時藉由低厚度來降低載子復合速率，達成高太陽能製氫效率的目的。 
四、文獻探討 
以 ALD 成長 TiO2-xNx 薄膜的文獻僅有 Pore 等人[11]以 TiCl4、H2O 與 NH3 為反應源，於 glass
與 ITO coated glass 基材上交錯成長 TiN/TiO2，藉由控制 TiN 循環次數比例製備出氮摻雜 TiO2
薄膜，其製備較耗時，且有均勻度的疑慮。本研究將採用 H2O 與 NH3 同時通入反應室的做法直
接成長氮摻雜 TiO2 薄膜，提高生產力及摻雜均勻度。 
製作多孔性金屬膜的方法不多，本研究使用的方法有斜向沉積法、陽極氧化鋁(AAO)模板
電鍍法。斜向沉積是一種經濟且可行的方法。斜向沉積法是將基板傾斜一個角度，利用斜向入
射原子受基板成核顆粒突起的遮蔽效應，製作出多孔性三維結構薄膜。K. Robbie、J.C. Sit 和
M.J. Brett 對此技術的研究最為透徹[12]，藉由斜向沉積技術已可成長出各種柱狀及螺旋狀多孔
結構，但他們所成長多孔性膜大多為 Si 膜，且底材並非導體，因此不適合作為製氫電極。由於
斜向沉積需要有突起顆粒才能產生遮蔽效應，達成多孔性膜結構，在純斜向沉積下大部份金屬
不易形成優良的多孔性結構，因而有些研究以微影製程製作種子層(Seed)來提高遮蔽效應[13]，
也有利用高分子奈米陣列顆粒來提高遮蔽效應，製作出週期性柱狀結構[14,15]。然而由於奈米
微影製程設備昂貴、生產力低，高分子奈米陣列則有不導電的問題，皆不適合直接應用於本研
究。本研究將尋找一種適合的沉積材料及適當的種子層，製作出高孔隙率金屬膜，以搭配 ALD 
TiO2-xNx形成高效率太陽能製氫電極。 
陽極氧化鋁(AAO)模板電鍍法亦是一可行的方法，將鋁浸泡於合適的酸性溶液中加上電場

模板形成奈米鎳柱陣列。 
3. 電極特性分析 
鍍膜完成後，以光源波長 632.8 nm，型號為 MARY-102 的橢圓儀量測薄膜厚度，量測時 
TiO2 消光係數設為 0；以波長 = 1.5418 Å  的 RIGAKU D/MAX 3.V X-光繞射儀 (X-ray 
Diffractometer, XRD) 分析薄膜的結晶型態，操作條件為電壓 40 kV，電流 30 mA；以 JEOL 公
司 JSM-6701F 場發射型掃瞄式電子顯微鏡 (Scanning Electron Microscopy, SEM) 觀察薄膜表
面型態；光電流響應則以三極式電化學電池量測，以白金為對電極，Ag/AgCl 為參考電極，
N-doped TiO2 薄膜為工作電極，浸泡在 0.1 M KOH 電解液裡，以波長 365 nm、功率 100W 的 
UV 燈照射，量測在 UV 光下的電流-電壓特性曲線；以功率 50 W 的氙燈照射，搭配 435 nm 
濾鏡濾除 UV 光，量測在可見光下的電流-電壓特性曲線。另外，以鹵鎢燈為光源，藉由型號 
TRIAX 320 的光譜儀分離成各種不同波長的光譜掃描照射，在 0 V (vs Ag/AgCl) 偏壓下量測各
波長的光電響應。

本計畫為提高 TiO2 薄膜的可見光吸收能力及其光觸媒化學反應面積，積極研究最佳的氮摻雜
量及開發各種高表面積金屬電極，研究結果已發表在國際知名的鍍膜科技相關期刊。雖然最
佳化氮摻雜的 TiO2 薄膜對可見光響應仍差，商業應用可能性低，但在高表面積金屬膜的開發
上頗具成果，目前正申請專利中，預計未來可應用於光觸媒、感測器、太陽能電池等方面。 
學術成就、技術創新、社會影響等如下： 
1. 完成基板種類與功函數對 ALD TiO2 成長與光觸媒效率之影響。結果已發表於學術期刊
Journal of The Electrochemical Society, 156 (2009) D275-D278。 
2. 完成氮摻雜量的最佳化研究，結果有部份已發表於學術期刊 J. Australian Ceram. Soc., 45 
( 2009) 64-68。 
3. 完成基板的多孔化與高表面積化研究，結果有部份已發表於學術期刊 Electrochemical and 
Solid-State Letters, 13 (2010) D19-D22，有部份（高表面積金屬電極之製備）目前正申請專
利中。 
4. 培養薄膜工程及光電化學專業人才 
本研究計畫三年計有博士生李文仁、碩士生陳宥儒、陳羿志、李俊德、葉柏均、賴忠
和、周承璋、陳家全等，及 8 位大學部專題同學參與，藉由本研究之啟發使他們更瞭解薄

N-doped TiO2 films were grown on n-type Si (100) 
substrates with a resistivity of 1~5 × 10
-3
 Ω-cm in a 
hot-wall ALD system. The precursors of TiCl4 and 
ammonia water were alternatively introduced into the 
ALD chamber through time controlled solenoid valves. An 
ALD cycle with 6 steps of TiCl4 injection, pump-down, Ar 
purge, ammonia water vapor injection, pump-down, and 
Ar purge with pulse time of 1, 1, 1, 1, 2 and 1 s, 
respectively, was adopted. The TiCl4 injection amount was 
fixed at 0.27 cc/pulse for all experiments, but the ammonia 
water vapor injection amount varied in the range of 10-20 
cc/pulse, depending on the reservoir temperature. The ratio 
of NH3 to H2O in the ammonia water vapor, calculated 
from the saturated vapor pressure and ammonia 
concentration in the solution, was between 230 and 420. 
The substrates were cleaned in an ultrasonic bath 
sequentially using acetone, methanol and de-ionized water 
for 5 min separately, and then dried with N2 gas before 
introducing into the vacuum chamber. Prior to deposition, 
the chamber was evacuated to a base pressure of 0.27 Pa. 
For each sample 1000 cycles in total were conducted 
resulting in a film with thickness of around 55 nm. 
The film thickness was measured by a 
Fivelab-MARY-102 ellipsometer. The film structure was 
determined by a RIGAKU D/MAX 3V X-ray diffraction 
(XRD) with voltage of 40 kV and current of 30 mA at a

characterized by a Philips XL-40FEG scanning electron

Hydrophilic properties of as-deposited films were 
investigated by measuring the water contact angle of films 
before and after 365 nm UV light irradiation using a VCA 
performa-200 system in the black box. The intensity of UV

deposited films at various wavelengths were examined in a 
wet-type three-electrode cell using an Autolab PGSTAT12

automatic monochromator were used for the wavelength

electrode, and 1 M HClO4 solution as the electrolyte. The 
measurements were carried out at applied potential of 1 V 
versus reference electrode. 
 
RESULTS AND DISCUSSION 
Fig. 1a and b shows the temperature-dependent XRD 
patterns of N-doped TiO2 films grown by ALD with 
NH3-to-H2O injection ratios of 230 and 420, respectively. 
The XRD patterns demonstrate that the film structure is 
highly influenced by the deposition temperature. The 
structure is amorphous for films grown at 200
 o
C, 
anatase-crystalline for films grown at 300-500
 o
C, and 
rutile-crystalline for films grown at 600
 o
C with 
NH3-to-H2O injection ratio of 230. As the NH3-to-H2O 
injection ratio was increased to 420, however, TiON and 
TiN instead of rutile TiO2 were formed in the 600
o
C 
deposited films. It is noteworthy that regardless of 
NH3-to-H2O injection ratio the 300
o
C deposited films have 
relatively higher crystallinity. The high crystallinity of 
300
o
C deposited films is considered to be due to the 
formation of large plate-like anatase grains. Figure 2 and 3 
show the surface morphologies of these N-doped TiO2 
films. The films grown at 300 
o
C clearly have a relatively 
large grain size, especially for the NH3-to-H2O injection 
ratio of 420. The formation of large plate-like grains at 300 
o
C has been explained by the low nucleation density which 
allows the nuclei to grow laterally.[8] At deposition 
temperature of 200 
o
C, the surface migration of adatoms is 
too low to form crystal nuclei and the deposited films are 
amorphous with smooth surface. At deposition 
temperature of 300 
o
C, the surface migration of adatoms is 
high enough to form some crystal nuclei; however, the 
nucleus density is low and a two-dimensional grain growth 
can proceed. The deposited films are crystalline with large 
plate -like grains. At deposition temperature of 400 
o
C, the 
nucleus density is high and the deposited films consist of 
fine grains. At temperatures above 400 
o
C, however, the

Fig. 4: Wavelength-dependent photocurrent density of 
ALD N-doped TiO2 films grown at the indicated 
temperatures with NH3-to-H2O injection ratios of (a) 230 
and (b) 420. The noise was enlarged by the low

In this study, the thickness of deposited films is around 55 
nm which is too thin to absorb the incident light entirely. 
As the wavelength is close to the intrinsic cutoff of TiO2, 
the light absorption ability of films decreases drastically, 
leading to a decrease of photocurrent with the wavelength. 
Nevertheless, it suggests that the visible light photocurrent 
of ALD N-doped TiO2 films can be further improved by 
growing the films on a substrate with light-trapping 
structure.  
The effect of microstructure on the photoresponse of 
deposited films was explored by the change of 
photocurrent peak intensity with deposition temperature. 
From Fig. 4a, we can find that the 600 and 300 
o
C 
deposited films have the highest UV photocurrent, 
followed by the 500 
o
C deposited film, 400 
o
C deposited 
film, and then 200 
o
C deposited film. The high 
photocurrent of 600 
o
C deposited film is evidently due to 
its rutile structure, which has lower band gap energy than 
the anatase structure, i.e., a greater optical absorption 
coefficient at wavelength of near UV. The high 
photocurrent of 300 
o
C deposited film, however, is due to 
the large grain size, which lowers the probability of carrier 
recombination. On the contrary, the low photocurrent of 
200
 o
C deposited film is due to the amorphous structure, 
which causes a high recombination rate of carriers. In Fig. 
4b, the result is similar to that of Fig. 4a except the 600
o
C 
deposited film which shows no photocurrent due to the 
formation of TiON and TiN.  
The photoactivity of these N-doped TiO2 films was 
further investigated by water contact angle measurements 
shown in Fig. 5a and b. Expectedly, the 300, 400 and 
500
o
C deposited films have higher photoactivity than the 
200 and 600
o
C deposited films due to their anatase 
structure. Among the anatase films, however, the 400 and 
500
o
C deposited films perform rather better than the 
300
o
C deposited film. It may be due to the rougher surface 
of 400 and 500
o
C deposited films. The superhydrophilicity 
of 400 and 500
o
C deposited films in Fig. 5b suggests that 
with an appropriate deposition condition the N-doped 
anatase TiO2 films grown by ALD can possess a high 
photoactivity.
