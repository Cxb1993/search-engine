本研究是以固態反應法製備具有高介電常數之CaCu3Ti4O12(CCTO)陶瓷材料，藉以探討
不同製程參數對此材料其物性及電性之影響。在物性方面，藉由XRD與SEM分析結果得知，
CCTO陶瓷材料須在煆燒溫度900℃以上才會化合反應完成，然而，燒結溫度與持溫時間的
改變，對XRD的結晶方向並沒有明顯相依的變化，且皆呈多晶結構，而銅計量比的增加，
CCTO的結晶繞射峰則會越來越強，當銅計量比增加至75%時，此時的結晶相均完全化合成
CCTO，同時可發現晶格常數會有拉伸近一倍之情形。此外，銅計量比的增加、提高燒結溫
度及較長的持溫時間，可以促使晶粒的成長，並可觀察到液相再結晶的臨界溫度為1010℃。
在電性方面，隨著銅計量比的增加促使晶粒成長，介電常數會有上升之趨勢，並在75%時
達到最大，而改變不同燒結溫度與持溫時間，於頻率102~105 Hz範圍區間，以持溫時間24
小時來探討，在1000℃時其介電常數會比較穩定平直，介電損失為0.2以下，當溫度增加至
1100℃時其介電常數會比1000℃時高，但是，介電損失則轉變為0.8以下。 
 
關鍵詞：固態反應法，鈣銅鈦氧化合物，燒結溫度，介電常數，介電損失。 
 
In this study, we adopt conventional solid-state reaction method to obtain CaCu3Ti4O12 
(CCTO) ceramics. The effect of sintering conditions on the microstructures and the electrical 
properties of CCTO ceramics are also discussed. 
Experimental results reveal that the calcined temperature of CCTO was 900℃. However, 
crystal phases of polycrystalline CCTO ceramics have no obvious differences using by XRD. It is 
found that there are no strong change of crystalline phase intensity at various sintering 
temperature and dwell time. It can be seen that the grain size was significantly increased with an 
increase of sintering temperature and dwell time. However, it is observed that the liquid-phase 
secondary recrystallization of CCTO ceramics at sintering temperature 1010℃. Besides, 75% 
CuO doped in CaTiO3 and then formed CCTO (75% copper ions replaces the position of calcium 
ions)， the lattice constant will extend one-fold. 
The grain size increased with an increase of Cu stoichiometric ratio, 75% Cu stoichiometric 
ratio of CCTO exhibited a high dielectric constant. The dielectric constant was found to be 
constant at sintering temperature 1000℃ for 24 h (ε: about 700, tanδ: below 0.2) at 10 kHz. 
However, sintering temperature at 1100℃ for 24 h exhibited a high dielectric constant (ε：about 
10000, tanδ: below 0.8) at 10 kHz but dielectric constant rapidly decreased with an increase of 
frequency.  
Keyword: Conventional solid-state reaction method, CaCu3Ti4O12, Sintering temperature,

1. Introduction 
Recently, much attention has been paid to the perovskite-like structure compound CaCu3Ti4O12 
(CCTO), which exhibits a large dielectric constant for applications of capacitor, microelectronics 
and microwave devices [1-3]. Both single crystal and polycrystalline ceramic of CCTO exhibit very 
high dielectric constant (in the order of 104) at room temperature (RT) [4,5]. Unlike most undoped 
ferroelectrics and relaxors, the dielectric constant of CCTO showed very little temperature 
dependence between RT and 200℃. However, the measured dielectric constants from 478 to 
300,000 have been reported for this material fabricated in different processes [6-10]. It appears that 
the dielectric properties of CCTO ceramics and thin films are very sensitive to process. In this 
present study, we adopt the radio frequency (rf) magnetron sputtering technique to obtain CCTO 
thin films. Experimental results reveal that the processing parameters of CCTO thin films will affect 
the dielectric properties such as dielectric constant and dielectric loss. The effect of deposition 
conditions on the surface morphology and the dielectric properties of CCTO thin films are reported. 
2. Experimental

U.S.A.) by conventional solid-state reaction method. Stoichiometric amounts of above reagents 
were mixed in a ball mill with de-ionized water using an agate pot and balls for 12 h. The powder 
was dried at 110℃ for 12 h and then milled again prior to calcination at 900℃ for 12 h. The powder 
was confirmed to be CCTO crystalline phases by XRD patterns and pressed into a 2-inch-diameter 
ceramic target. The target was sintered at 1100℃ in air for 24 h and furnace-cooled to RT. The 
However, the polycrystalline CCTO peaks appear obviously at post-annealing temperature higher

XRD patterns also showed that the film annealed at 800℃ has a stronger (220) peak relative to the 
others. This implies that the film is 〈220〉-preferred. Figure 4 shows the surface morphologies of 
CCTO films annealed at various post-annealing temperatures. The as-deposited CCTO film 
exhibited no resolution of microstructural feature as shown in Fig. 4(a). The surface morphology of 
CCTO film annealed at 600℃ was similar to that of as-deposited film. At a post-annealing 
temperature of 700℃, the homogeneous microstructure consisting of uniform grain size of about 
0.1 µm can be observed. When the post-annealing temperature was raised to 800℃, the grains of 
〈220〉-preferred CCTO film became irregular and larger in size (≤ 0.3µm). It is reported that CCTO 
films deposited under higher oxygen pressure or depositing temperature exhibited the square shaped 
grains stacking on the surface [12-14]. It is suggested that the variation in surface microstructure of 
CCTO film is due to the different process.

4. Conclusion 
In summary, CCTO thin films with high dielectric constant were successfully prepared by rf 
magnetron sputtering technique. The crystallization and microstructures of the CCTO films were 
affected by the post-annealing temperature. The 〈220〉-preferred CCTO films can be obtained when 
the post-annealing temperature was above 700℃. The 0.8-µm-thick CCTO films deposited at rf 
power of 80 W for depositing time of 2 h then annealed at 800  (grain size ℃ of ~0.3 µm) have high 
dielectric constant of 500 and dielectric loss of 0.7 at 10 kHz. In order to apply CCTO in the 
application of thin film capacitor, details of the effects of the other parameters on the microstructure 
and the dielectric properties are further studied.

2. Experimental

3. Results and discussion 
The thermolysis behavior of TiO2 gel is shown in Fig. 3. TGA analysis indicated two major weight 
losses occurred and completed at about 400℃. It can be seen that the gel exhibited approximately 
80% weight loss in the temperature range of 100℃ to 400℃ due to the elimination of adsorbed 
water, solvent (b.p. of 2-methoxyethanol is 122℃) and the decomposition of by-products. The 
endothermic peaks appeared at about 135℃ and 320℃ are associated with former weight losses. 
The exothermic peak appeared at about 386℃ is probably due to the crystallization of TiO2 from 
amorphous to anatase phase. XRD patterns of TiO2 films heated at temperatures from 200℃ to 
500℃ for 10 min are shown in Fig. 4. It is found that the film heated at 300℃ was still amorphous. 
However, the crystalline TiO2 films can be obtained at heating temperature higher than 400 . ℃ The 
crystallization of TiO2 film is consistent with the result of TGA-DTA. Furthermore, the increase of 
larger charge capacity [9,15]. Figure 8 shows the transmittance of TiO2 films obtained by various 
oxygen concentrations in heat treatment process. The transmittance of TiO2 film with the oxygen 
concentration of 60% is varied from about 81.78% to 63.78% between colored and bleached at 550 
nm. The optical density change (∆OD) is 0.087. It is suggested that the proper oxygen concentration 
will influence the electrochromic property of the film, and this result depends on oxygen defect and 
color centers [16,17]. Figure 9 shows the transmittance of TiO2 films obtained by various heating 
temperature (RTP). It is found that the charge injected into the crystalline film is much less than 
into the amorphous film [18-20]. That is, Li+ ion intercalation in the amorphous TiO2 film, due to its 
opener microstructure, is much easier to proceed than in the anatase film. In this work, the 
experimental results are reported in Table 1. 
4. Conclusion 
In summary, TiO2 films were deposited on ITO/Glass substrates by the CSD method. TiO2 thin 
films heated at different temperatures from 200℃ to 500℃ and the structure transferred from 
amorphous to crystalline. The cyclic voltammogram of the 350 nm TiO2 film exhibited a reduction 
peak at -3.8 V (0.94 mA/cm2) and an oxidation peak at 0.04 V (-0.46 mA/cm2). As Li+ ion inserted, 
the amorphous film present a dark blue and the crystalline one present a light blue color. In this 
study, the thickness of 350 nm amorphous TiO2 film was obtained by RTP with the oxygen 
concentration of 60% and heating temperature of 300℃ exhibited a large transmittance change 
(∆T%) of 18% (∆OD=0.087) between colored and bleached at the wavelength of 550 nm. The 
amorphous TiO2 film showed higher electrochromic characteristic than the anatase film.
