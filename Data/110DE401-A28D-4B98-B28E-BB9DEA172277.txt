二、前言 
近幾年來高分子光纖之發展，已從步階式高分子
光纖(step-index polymer optical fiber, SI-POF)進步至
漸變式高分子光纖(graded-index polymer optical fiber, 
GI-POF)。漸變式高分子光纖(GI-POF)之傳輸速率已
進歩到可高達 Gb/s，較步階式高分子光纖(SI-POF)幾
百個 Mb/s 之傳輸速率來的更快。因此，過去幾年來
GI-POF 之相關研究已經愈來愈受到國際之重視
[1-3]，研究之重點也有逐漸加溫之趨勢。由於在實際
應用上，資料傳輸速率之要求愈來愈快，GI-POF 勢
必是未來高分子光纖之主流。 
有關 GI-POF 高溫功率損失之探討，文獻上之研
究較少。Koike 等人[4]之研究指出，GI-POF 可以傳
輸更高速率之光訊號。因此高頻寬之 GI-POF，將是
未來多媒體網際網路使用之重點。Arrue 等人[5]探討
影響 GI-POF彎曲損失之參數。結果顯示，曲率半徑
愈小、外型指數(profile exponent)愈大時，彎曲損失愈
大。Ishigure等人[6, 7] 之研究指出，具 PVDF外層之
GI-POF其彎曲損失較低。研究結果顯示，採用高 NA
值及高頻寬之 GI-POF，當其曲率半徑為 5mm時，其
彎曲損失可低於 1dB。此外研究也指出，當核心半徑
從 550減少至 500μm時，其彎曲損失可以從 0.75dB
減少至 0.4dB。Makino 等人[8]則是探討，GI-POF核
心直徑和數值孔徑之大小對光纖彎曲功率衰減之影
響。結果指出，當核心直徑小於 200µm 以及數值孔
徑大於 0.25時，光纖彎曲功率之衰減幾乎沒有。Sato
等人[9]則是探討隨意扭曲之 GI-POF於高溫環境中之
衰減情形。結果顯示，數值孔徑對於高溫環境下 GI 
POF 之功率衰減影響很大。其功率衰減之主要原因
是，高溫環境中扭曲造成高分子結構鬆弛現象所引
起。此外，Ishigure 等人[10]也提出，溼度將導致高
分子材料之異質性，造成 GI-POF產生過量之散射損
失。雖然文獻上有關於 GI-POF之研究不少，但均只
是單純探討數值孔徑、光纖核心直徑及不同曲率半徑
之彎曲損失問題。關於 GI-POF在高溫環境下，受彎
曲拉伸變形時，其傳輸功率之衰減情形以及材料機械
特性之改變為何？並無相關之研究。且 GI-POF內部
之應力及應變、功率衰減隨拉伸位移之改變情形也沒
有相關之深入研究。因此，本計畫將針對 GI-POF之
高溫功率衰減作進一步之深入探討。 
有關 SI-POF 變形衰減之研究，主持人過去幾年
之研究 [11-15]算是這個領域相當領先的。個人之研
究指出，彎曲拉伸變形的確對 SI-POF 之功率衰減有
明顯之影響。且也提出一預估 SI-POF 功率衰減之公
式，與實驗量測結果之誤差小與 5%。 
 
三、研究目的 
本計畫之目的首先是要建立GI-POF高溫衰減量
測之實驗平台，量測高溫彎曲拉伸變形對於GI-POF
功率衰減之影響，建立相關之實驗數據作為GI-POF
未來相關週邊設備研發及改善之參考。計畫之執行將
包含兩部分，一是實驗量測，另一則是是有限元素分
析。本計畫探討之參數將包括：(1)不同溫度、(2)不同
彎曲半徑、(3)不同拉伸位移及(4)隨溫度變化之材料參
數等。利用實驗所建立之GI-POF拉伸位移曲線，來建
立GI-POF在不同溫度下之有限元素分析模型。探討
GI-POF在高溫拉伸彎曲變形時，相關變形及應力變化
與功率衰減之關係，藉此釐清GI-POF功率衰減之原因
及建立GI-POF高溫之分析模式，期能建立一經驗公式
作為設計之參考。對於GI-POF之設計、其他領域之應
用，及在未來汽車影音及資料傳輸使用上將有相當之
助益。

Fig. 2. Finite element model of drill bit and bone 
drilling operation. The computational efficiency of the explicit 
method used in the numerical simulation procedure was 
enhanced by applying a mass scaling technique. Figure 1 
shows the drill bit and the bone contact geometry used in this 
study. 
The region of interest is the immediate surrounding of the drill 
hole where the temperature is highest. Hence the domain for 
the numerical simulation is chosen to be a circular disc. Since 
the mechanical properties of cortical bone are larger than those 
of cancellous bone, a greater temperature rise is induced within 
the cortical bone as the drilling operation proceeds. Only 
cortical bone is considered in the finite element model. As Fig. 
1 shown, the diameter, point angle and helix angle of the drill 
bit used in this study are 2.5 mm, 118 o  and 30 o , respectively. 
The diameter and thickness of cortical bone are taken as 6.5 
mm and 4 mm, respectively. The point of the drill hole edge, 
i.e. x=1.25 mm and z=0 mm, is taken as the origin of the 
coordinate system. The corresponding finite element model is 
shown in Fig. 2. The contact behavior between the drill bit and 
the bone is modeled using approximately 10,475contact 
elements in each simulation. The mesh is constructed using 
eight-node three-dimensional brick elements. The finite 
element model comprises a total of 56,116 elements and 
64,342 nodes. To explore the effects of drilling speed and 
applied force of the drill bit on the temperature rise, the 
simulations are performed using three applied forces F, i.e. 20, 
40 and 60 N, and four rotating speeds N, i.e. 800, 1200, 1600 
and 2000 rpm. The initial temperature of bone and drill bit is 
taken as 37 Co  and 22 Co  (a typical temperature in a surgical 
operation room). The mechanical properties used in the finite

III. RESULTS AND DISCUSSIONS 
Figure 3 shows the variation of bone temperature with the 
drilling time for different measurement depths. Four different 
depths, i.e. z=1, 2, 3 and 4 mm, are presented in Fig. 3. The 
measurement point is located at a distance of 0.1 mm from the 
edge of the drilled hole (x=0.1 mm). The applied force F and 
rotating speed of the drill bit is 20 N and 800 rpm, 
respectively. The results presented in Fig. 3 show that the 
bone temperature distributions vary noticeably with the 
measurement depth. The bone temperature decreases as the 
measurement depth z increases. In Fig. 3, the peak bone 
temperature occurs at approximately 5 s after drilling starts in 
all the cases.  The peak temperatures at measurement depths z 
= 1, 2, 3 and 4 mm are found to be 52 oC , 46 Co , 44 Co  and 
40 Co , respectively. The temperature difference can reach 
12 Co  for these four different measurement depths.

peak bone temperature is reduced as the rotating speed 
increases from 800 rpm to 1200 rpm. The effect of rotating 
speed on the bone temperature distribution is summarized in

V. CONCLUSION 
This study has performed numerical investigations into the 
bone temperature distribution within the bone during drilling. 
Various numerical analyses have been conducted using an 
elastic-plastic dynamic temperature-displacement finite 
element model. The effect of the rotating speed and the applied 
force of the drill bit on the temperature rise has been examined. 
Based upon the numerical results, the following conclusions 
can be drawn: 
1. The bone temperature distributions vary noticeably with 
the measurement depth. The temperature difference can 
reach 12 oC  for four different measurement depths in this 
study. 
2. A drill bit with a higher rotating speed can have a 
noticeable reduction on the bone temperature rise. 
3. The size of the thermal affected zone can be reduced under 
a higher rotating speed. 
4. A larger applied force can reduce the bone temperature 
rise effectively.
