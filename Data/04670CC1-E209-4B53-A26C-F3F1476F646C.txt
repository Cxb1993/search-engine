摘要 
    薄膜電池夾帶著若干傳統鋰電池無法望其項背的特性，諸如超薄、全固態、溫變性小、
不怕擠壓、高使用壽命(上千次)、快速充電、可撓曲、可施工在各種幾何形狀及高功率密度
等，因而在近幾年以極快的研發速度使其製造技術逐漸逼近實用化的角度。有鑑於此，本計
劃乃構思將薄膜電池及電致色變元件整合成一元件，結合薄膜電池使電致色變的應用朝向 
“可攜＂、“微型＂以及“主動＂的發展趨勢。 
    計劃去年目標在於構建完整的薄膜電池元件，藉由增加第三極熱燈絲電將系統的輔助，
沉積出LiCoO2正極薄膜，試圖在相對低溫下獲得更高性能的電池正極，用以取代必要的後熱
處理。實驗結果顯示，當沉積達到一定厚度(>1 μm)時，在使用第三極熱燈絲輔助沉積的條件
中，所得的LiCoO2 正極薄膜結晶性會隨著沉積溫度上升，超過300℃可獲得目標相結構R3m，
所沉積鍍膜之結晶方位達到400℃以上時，可獲得Li+離子嵌出、入反應較佳的 (101)及(104)
平面。其中以功率200 W，沉積時間3 h 之正極膜所組成的半電池，充電時的飽合電壓為4 V，
放電時電容量可達約70 μAh/cm2。 
    本年度目標完成電致色變/薄膜電池積層元件設計製作。計畫完成之際分別達成以Ta2O5
為固態電解質之「單側型全固態電致色變元件」與LiCoO2 為正極之「單側型薄膜電池元件」。
前者尺寸為2.5 cm ×4.0 cm，其著、去色響應時間分別為33 s及15 s，特定波長600 nm穿透度差
△T達50%以上與高達0.8之光學密度差；而單側型薄膜電池件亦有穩定放電電壓平台。本研究
發展出低溫沉積LiCoO2 正極薄膜的製程，並省去電致色變元件一側的ITO玻璃，成功的將薄
膜電池及電致色變元件整合成一元件。 
     計畫完成之際發展完成的技術包括「熱燈絲輔助低溫化沉積技術」、「最佳化氧化鉭固
態電解質濺鍍製程」、「全固態電致色變元件」、「單側型全固態電致色變元件」以及「單
側型薄膜電池元件」。 
關鍵詞：氧化鉭、電致色變元件、鈷酸鋰、薄膜電池、磁控濺鍍、熱燈絲輔助沉積 
Abstract 
Thin film batteries with slim feature, all solid state construction, operating temperature 
insensitivity, long durability, rapid recharging capability, availability on flexible substrates, 
conformability to any shape/size and high power density, are promptly researched and developed 
into a circumstance of ready-to-use recently. In respect to this, the subsidiary project raises a 
concept to integrate thin film battery into electrochromic device. To combine thin film battery and 
electrochromic device is the key issue of miniaturization of display and is the goal of this subsidiary 
project. 
    In last year, thermionic emission technique was applied in sputter deposition of LiCoO2 
positive electrode thin film for achieving better battery performance without post annealing. The 
result shows that when film thickness reaches 1 μm with thermionic emission enhamcement, the 
LiCoO2 film crystalinity (R3m) increases with deposition temperature and eventually crystallized at 
300 ℃. Such films present a (101) and (104) preferred orientation and show higher ion intercalation 
and deintercalation efficiency as expected. The assembled thin film battery with LiCoO2 positive 
electrode deposited at 200W for 3 h exhibits a saturated charge potential of 4 V and 70 μAh/cm2 of 
discharge capacity.

三、研究方法 
為了將電致色變元件及薄膜電池兩種元件整合在一個元件中，必須將兩種元件的疊層分
別在一片ITO玻璃的上、下兩側，故先依「單側型全固態電致色變元件」及「單側型薄膜電
池元件」兩部份來探討，最後再建構電致色變/薄膜電池元件積層設計，其研究方法分別說明
如下： 
3-1 單側型全固態電致色變元件製作 
 單側型全固態電致色變元件在本研究中設計成如圖1所示，首先製作採用反應性磁控濺鍍
系統，先將片電阻10Ω/□之ITO導電玻璃切成2.5 cm ×4 cm的規格，置入盛有丙酮之燒杯中，
於超音波震盪洗淨器內震盪五分鐘後取出，再置於去離子水中震盪五分鐘後取出陰乾備用。
將清洗後ITO玻璃置於真空腔中依序沉積電致色變層氧化鎢、固態電解質層氧化鉭(於沉積氧
化鉭後，需利用恆電位儀於1M過氯酸鋰之碳酸丙烯液態溶液中，預先注入鋰離子於半元件
中)，接續濺鍍輔助變色層氧化鎳，最後濺鍍氧化銦錫透明導電層，疊層中的各層濺鍍參數如
表1所示，即完成單側型全固態電致色變元件製作，並進行後續電致色變元件量測分析。

3-3 電致色變/薄膜電池元件積層設計建構 
    藉由成功建製單側型全固態電致色變元件與單側型薄膜電池元件，預期可建構的「電致
色變/薄膜電池積層元件」如下所述：首先將玻璃基材蝕刻出兩個通道，如圖 4(a)所示；再鍍
上白金電極層，讓蝕刻的兩個通道亦填滿白金電極，作為單側型全固態電致色變與薄膜電池
元件的聯接導線，如圖 4(b)所示；接下來，於白金電極上堆疊串聯的薄膜電池模組，並於玻
璃基材的背面沉積電致色變元件，如圖 4(c)所示；之後將元件右側鍍膜蝕刻出一寬度，如圖
4(d)所示；之後在電致色變/薄膜電池元件上鍍一層SiO2(該SiO2鍍膜是作為隔離層)，並將元件
上多餘的SiO2鍍膜蝕刻掉留下所需的部份，用以杜絕電致色變元件和薄膜電池元件各別的
上、下電極接觸如圖 4(e)、(f)所表示，最後在薄膜電池元件鍍上另一層白金電極層，讓另一
通道也填滿了白金電極，完成電致色變與薄膜電池元件的另一連接導線，此外亦須在電致色
變元件上鍍上ITO電極，如圖 4(g)所示，必要時在Pt層連接控制模組，即完成了單側型全固態
電致色變與薄膜電池元件整合型之建製。

3.2. Coating property changes with thermal cycle testing  
The macrographs of TBC coated specimen after a number of thermal cycles are shown in Fig. 
4.  Initial damage of the YSZ top coat occurs at the edge of the samples, due to local stresses 
generated by the geometry [16]. The damage subsequently extends to the flat side of the sample 
with increasing number of thermal cycles.  After 700 cycles, there is still 90% of the top coat 
remaining on the specimen, which reveals the strong adhesion of TBCs deposited by this hybrid arc 
ion plating and magnetron sputtering system.  The durability is better than that of air plasma 
sprayed TBC but inferior to that of EB-PVD TBCs [17,18].  The surface and cross-sectional 
morphology of the TBC coated specimen after thermocyclic testing is shown in Fig. 5 and Fig. 6.  
Due to the dense structure of the YSZ top coat, it is unable to accommodate the tensile thermal 
stresses occurring in the TBC during thermal cycle testing.  This leads to many cracks being 
induced in the top coat surface that grow with the increasing number of cycles.  The coating 
structure does however appear to aid in directing cracks perpendicular to the surface along the 
boundary with the TGO layer.  These parallel cracks are the major cause of top coat spallation.  
This failure mode has features analogous to the “stick-slip” behavior of plasma-sprayed TBCs and 
the so called “pinch-off” cones in EB-PVD TBC, in which rumpling in the boundary between the 
TGO and top layer occurs due to the thermal stress [20-22].  Kirkendall voids can also be observed 
between the bond coat and the substrate.  These are caused by the interdiffusion of aluminum and 
nickel between bond coat and substrate during thermocyclic testing.  From the element mapping 
results, as shown in Fig. 10, internal oxides are seen to be formed in these voids. This prevents 
further interdiffusion between the bond coat and substrate.  Although these internal TGOs are 
beneficial in suppressing the depletion of aluminum from the bond coat, they do however cause 
delamination between bond coat and substrate, as shown in Fig. 11.  This internal oxidation may 
be attributed to the fast diffusion of oxygen along the pores formed in the vicinity of droplets.  
Both parallel cracks in the YSZ top coat and internal oxides beneath the bond coat are the principal 
causes of the failure of TBC’s deposited by the hybrid arc ion plating and magnetron sputtering 
system.

1. Introduction 
Thermal barrier coating (TBC) systems are widely used to provide thermal and oxidation protection 
of nickel-based superalloy components used in gas turbine engines.  These systems consist of a 
thermally insulating yttria-stablized zirconia (YSZ) top coat and a metallic bond coat.  The bond coat 
contains sufficient aluminum to provide an oxidation resistant α-Al2O3 interlayer at high temperatures [1, 
2].  Furthermore, NiAl intermetallic compounds, with a stable B2 structure, posses a high melting point, 
low density and high oxidation resistance. These properties result in its widespread use as the bond coat 
in TBC systems [3-5]. 
Electron beam physical vapor deposition (EB-PVD) is currently the preferred method for producing 
the YSZ top coat in TBC systems due to extended service life, compared to air plasma sprayed coatings 
[1].  The NiAl bond coats are commonly synthesized by high temperature alumization techniques such 
as pack cementation or chemical vapor deposition [6-8].  These methods can successfully fabricate the 
desired YSZ and NiAl layers; nevertheless, they lead to the whole TBC preparation process being 
complex and time consuming.  For additional alloying, an extra electroplating or sputtering operation 
must be incorporated, further increasing the process complexity and cost.  To overcome these difficulties, 
some PVD techniques, including sputtering and electron beam evaporation, have been tried in the 
preparation of the NiAl bond coat [9-11].  These processes however often employ temperatures of over 
700°C in order to facilitate the desired crystallinity and adhesion of the top and bond coat.  Arc ion 
plating is a technique known to be able to deposit crystalline NiAl and YSZ coatings at substrate 
temperatures below 400 °C [10, 12].   
Nevertheless, deposition from arc sources alone can lead to difficulties in the controlled doping of 
yttrium and necessitate the fabrication of complex and expensive alloy source materials.  To overcome 
these limitations, a hybrid ion plating system consisting of a magnetron sputtering yttrium source and an 
arc zirconium source, with in-situ substrate heating, was developed to co-deposit YSZ films [13].  In the 
present work a hybrid arc/sputter deposition process was used to produce the complete Ni-Al/YSZ TBC 
on a Ni-based superalloy substrate.  The thermocyclic performance of the TBC and bond coat system

2. Experimental procedure 
Fig. 1 shows a schematic of the hybrid arc ion plating and magnetron sputtering system employed 
for Ni-Al/YSZ thermal barrier coating preparation.  The specimens were sand blasted to a surface 
roughness value of Ra = 3.5 μm to enhance the adhesion of the deposited films.  After distilled water 
washing, acetone rinsing in an ultrasonic vibrating bath and air-drying, the specimens were positioned on 
the rotating substrate table.  A DC argon discharge (pressure = 10 Pa, substrate bias = -800 V) was used 
to sputter clean the Alloy600 substrates for five minutes, prior to deposition.  After DC argon discharge 
activation, the argon pressure was decreased to 0.54 Pa and the arc spots ignited on the NiAl source with 
an average arc current of 90 A.  The substrate was maintained at -800V for five minutes to induce Ni and 
Al ion bombardment for enhanced bonding strength between Ni-Al coating and substrate.  The substrate 
bias was then lowered to -100 V and the NiAl coating was deposited for a further 240 min.  The arc 
current to the zirconium source was set at an average of 60 A.  The magnetron sputtering target power 
was kept at 1300 W with a bipolar pulse waveform of +78 V for 1 μs and -200 V for 9 μs.  The 
deposition parameters of Ni-Al and YSZ coatings are provided in Table 1.   
The thermocyclic tests of the deposited coatings were carried out at 1100 °C.  Details of the 
equipment can be found in previous work [14].  The test conditions are shown in Fig. 2.  Each cycle 
consisted of 30 min at the 1100 °C, followed by air cooling for 10 min in ambient.  
The thermal conductivity was measured by a thermal conductivity analyzer with model name- Omicron 
Multiprobe Compact (Hot Disk AB- a Swedish company).  The crystal structure of the YSZ top coat was

From the XRD patterns shown in Fig. 7, it is seen that, after thermocyclic testing, peaks 
corresponding to the cubic zirconia phase are now present among those of the original monoclinic phase 
of the as-deposited YSZ top coat.  The formation of the cubic phase in the top coat is as a result of 
annealing effects of thermocyclic temperature and further oxidation to form a completely stoichiometric 
zirconia structure.   
 
3.3. Failure mechanisms of TBCs during thermocyclic testing 
The left and right sides of Fig. 8 show the surface morphology and elemental distributions of the 
remaining cracked and spalled regions of the YSZ top coat, respectively, after 700 cycles.  From the 
elemental mapping, it can be seen that most of the exposed area is covered by the bright zirconia debris 
with only small traces of the dark, thermally grown oxide (TGO) alumina.  This indicates that the bond 
strength between the TGO and top coat is higher than the cohesive strength of the top coat.  SEM 
back-scattered electron (BSE) cross-sectional observation has been performed to further understand the 
failure mechanisms of the TBC system, as shown in Fig. 9.  It is seen that the TGO layer between the 
YSZ top coat and Ni-Al bond coat grows with an increasing number of thermal test cycles.  During the 
thermal cycling tests there are also seen to be cracks propagating through the top coat in the direction of 
the columns, parallel to (and just above) the boundary with the TGO layer.  These parallel cracks are the 
major cause of top coat spallation.  This failure mode has features analogous to the “stick-slip” behavior 
of plasma-sprayed TBCs and the so called “pinch-off” cones in EB-PVD TBC, in which rumpling in the 
boundary between the TGO and top layer occurs due to the thermal stress [20-22].  Kirkendall voids can 
also be observed between the bond coat and the substrate.  These are caused by the interdiffusion of 
aluminum and nickel between bond coat and substrate during thermocyclic testing.  From the element 
mapping results, as shown in Fig. 10, internal oxides are seen to be formed in these voids. This prevents 
further interdiffusion between the bond coat and substrate.  Although these internal TGOs are beneficial

4. Conclusion 
Ni-Al/YSZ thermal barrier coating systems were synthesized using NiAl and Zr arc sources and an 
yttrium magnetron sputtering target, in a hybrid ion plating system.  By observing the thermocyclic 
behavior at 1100°C it was concluded that:   
1. The as-deposited YSZ top coat exhibited a completely monoclinic structure.  After initial 
thermocyclic testing, due to annealing effects, cubic phase was also formed.   
2. Spallation of the YSZ top coat was attributed to the connecting of perpendicular cracks along 
column boundaries and to parallel cracks propagating above the thermally grown oxide layer.  This 
spallation behavior exhibited features similar to both plasma-sprayed and EB-PVD TBCs, with a 
rumpling in the TGO boundary.   
3. As well as the thermally grown oxide layer at the interface of the bond and top coats, there was 
internal oxidation in the region between bond coat and substrate.  This internal oxidation resulted 
in delamination of the bond coat from the substrate.
