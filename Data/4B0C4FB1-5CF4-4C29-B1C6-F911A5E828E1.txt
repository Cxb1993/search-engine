中文摘要 
    全世界的工業正朝向「更精密」與「更
微小」的領域邁進，台灣也不例外。半導 
體加工精度與加工也隨之的不斷縮小，所
以微細加工的精度需求極高精密且不得容
許些許的誤差。另外半導體產業、微機械
元件以及微機電系統等元件的迅速發展，
各類元件射出用模具、成型自具的尺寸也
越來越小，更加突顯微細切削的重要性。
本計劃擬以微細鑚切為研究主題。在實際
半導体電路板製程中，因封裝越來越精密
與小型化，使得鑚針也朝向越來越細小甚
至直徑小於 0.1mm，也造成進刀過程中微鑚
針常常斷裂。所以探討以超音波振動改善
微細鑚針之切削與動態特性與挫曲斷針機
構。因微鑽針加工十分精密，一但有不穩
定現象將導致加工不良、挫曲甚至鑽針斷
裂。 
故本計畫先以理論分析再配合實驗
量測此微小鑽針鑽切時挫曲問題與動態特
性分析，來探討鑽針問題與改良鑽切製
程。音波振動式鑽切對鑽切動態與切削特
性與挫曲之影響。希望藉由理論、實驗與
實際設計刀具夾具以期能改善超微細鑽針
鑽切的動態特性與挫曲斷針等問題，以達
到正確且精密的鑽切加工，期使業界能節
省大量成本與使我國精密製造業能更進一
步且技術可國內生根。 
 
關鍵字：超音波振動、微鑽針、動態特性。 
 
Abstract 
The ultrasonic vibration is employed to 
depress the drill fracture in high speed 
drilling process. The dynamic characteristics 
and buckling of a micro drill in drilling 
process is also investigated in this study. Due 
to the high speed and micro drilling, the dill 
fracture in the high-speed micro milling is 
much more significant than the traditional 
milling. To proceed farther into an analysis 
of drill fracture, the drilling dynamic 
characteristics and buckling is worthy to pay 
attention to study, and then the ultrasonic 
vibration is used to improve the dynamic and 
buckling performance in drilling process. By 
using pre-twisted beam in this model, the 
micro dill can be simulated. The Dynamic 
response of the traditional drilling has been 
frequently studied, but only few investigators 
paid attention to the instability and fracture 
in a micro-drilling process. Hence, an 
investigation on the theoretical and 
experimental analysis on dynamics and 
buckling performances in a micro milling is 
studied in this research. Finally, a micro drill 
with ultrasonic vibration drilling is employed

Abstract—The dynamic model of a drill in drilling process 
was proposed and investigated in this study. To assure a good 
drilling quality, the vibration variation on the drill tips during 
high speed drilling is needed to be investigated. A pre-twisted 
beam is used to simulate the drill. The moving Winkler-Type 
elastic foundation is used to characterize the tip boundary 
variation in drilling. Due to the variation of the drill depth, a 
time dependent dynamic model for the drill is proposed. 
Results simulated from this proposed model indicate that an 
abrupt natural frequencies drop are experienced as the drill tip 
tough the workpiece, and a severe vibration is induced. The 
effects of parameters, e.g. drilling speed, depth, drill size and 
thrust force on the drill tip responses studied.  
 
Keywords—drilling, vibration of drill, twisted beam.  
I. INTRODUCTION 
N manufacturing, drilling has been used widely to drill holes. 
Due to the severe drill tip vibration induced in the drilling, 
the high accuracy and quality drilling still be a difficult task. 
The reliability in drilling process may affect the production 
capacity and the yield rate significantly, especial in the 
electronic board manufacturing. To improve drilling quality 
and capabilities, it is necessary to understand the dynamic 
drilling process characteristics. In this study, a time dependent 
dynamic model of the drill is proposed to explore the effect of 
drill size, drill speed and tip thrust force on the dynamic 
responses of a drill during the drilling process.  
Only a few studies have been conducted on the time varying 
dynamic properties of a drill during the drilling process 
Traditional drilling analysis has always focused on the drill 
itself, such as Rosard [1], Jarrett and Warner [2], Tekinalp and

In previous published papers, different vibration was

geometry shape and the cutting chip on the drilling and

that even a small variation in the geometry or symmetry can 
introduce a very strong influence on the drilling stability and 
dynamic responses of a drill [10]. These proposed drill models 
have provided very useful information for drill bit design. 
Some investigators extended their research to the instability 
analysis of a drill under different drilling conditions [6], 
[11]-[12]. Results indicated that the spinning speed, pre-twisted 
angle and axial force may change the distribution of instability 
zooms. 
Recently, a number of structural models with moving 
masses, forces and boundaries have been developed [13]-[17]. 
It should be noted that the dynamic characteristics of these time 
dependent systems are quite different from the time invariant 
problems. Most important of all, the traditional time invariant 
natural modes and frequencies become meaningless because 
the system and its corresponding boundary conditions are time 
dependent. In this article, a dynamic model of the drill with 
time varying drilling depth and thrust force is proposed to 
simulate the variation of dynamic response during the drilling 
process.
