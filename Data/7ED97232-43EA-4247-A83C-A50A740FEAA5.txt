Abstract: We experimentally demonstrated a novel wavelength-preserved all-optical 2R regeneration system 
at 10-Gb/s in an SOA-based cross-gain compression scheme with improved power efficiency in generating 
the desired optical complementary signals by counter-propagating XGM-based wavelength conversion. 
 
1.  Introduction 
With the advent of all optical switching technologies, all optical signal process becomes mandatory to meet the 
urgent requirements of system upgradability with reduced cost. Due to the bandwidth limit of the electronic circuits, 
all-optical signal regeneration is an effective approach to eliminate accumulated signal impairments in high speed 
optical communication systems without the need of optical/electronic/optical (O/E/O) conversion to execute signal 
processing in the electric domain. Numerous all optical signal regeneration approaches had been demonstrated and 
theoretically studied recently, including nonlinear self-phase modulation [1], four-wave mixing [2], cross-gain 
modulation (XGM) [3], cross-phase modulation [4], optical parametric regenerator [5], and delayed interference 
signal wavelength converter [6]. However, due to the wavelength conversion nature of these regenerated signals, 
serious pattern-dependent gain and/or frequency chirping problems will be introduced in the wavelength conversion 
process. On the other hand, by introducing a pair of complementary signals into an SOA, cross-gain compression 
(XGC) technique is becoming a promising candidate for 2R regeneration system in terms of preserving signal 
wavelength as well as reducing the inherent pattern-dependent gain and chirping problems. However, to generate the 
required complemented signal and preserve the original positive logic signal under co-propagation XGM scheme, 
we typically have to use too many passive optical couplers and optical filters, which wastes too much precious 
optical power. In our proposed scheme, we employ the counter-propagation scheme in XGM-based wavelength 
converter and properly route the desired complementary signals to the second SOA to achieve XGC function. Due to 
the nature of the counter-propagating scheme, the original signal and wavelength-converted complemented signal 
can be discriminated easily, thus the requirement of passive components can be minimized and power efficiency can 
be enhanced. Moreover, because the propagating paths of the both complementary signals are perfectly symmetric in 
the proposed architecture, theoretically no delay line is needed for synchronization in the 2
nd
 SOA to achieve XGC. 
In this paper, we experimentally demonstrate the proposed 2R regeneration system at 10-Gb/s with excellent 
performance.

3.  Experimental Results and Discussions 
In the system performance evaluation, we adopt the error-vector magnitude (EVM) measurement defined in IEEE 
802.11a-1999TM standard, as well as the related bit error rates (BERs) derived from the measured EVMs [6]. A 
guard band between optical carrier and the first band is applied to accommodate the intra-band beating noise. With 
simple and low-cost optical bandpass filters, the outmost signal band within the filtering spectrum always survives 
with the least beating interference after O/E conversion, as we stated above. Therefore, all the received signal bands 
can achieve the same performance with the proposed mechanism. Fig. 3 depicts the BER measurements with respect 
to the received OSNR. For all four signal bands, the OSNR sensitivity at BER = 10-3 is about 24 dB in simulations, 
while the experiments demonstrate similar results, at around 25 dB. The sensitivity differences among the four 
bands are both less than 1 dB. Moreover, due to the imperfection of the applied optical bandpass filter, i.e., slow 
roll-off in its filtering spectrum, we found the best performance is achieved when the signal’s CSPR is around 5 dB. 
By matching the measured filter shape with a Gaussian filter of order 1.1 in numerical simulations, the experimental 
results well match the numerical simulations, as depicted in Fig. 4.

我們依照計劃書內容，我們利用高速波長可調雷射(Tunable Laser)、半導體光學放大器
(Semiconductor optical amplifier)與陣列波導光柵(Array waveguide grating)結合形
成一個高速的光學開關，並且利用光纖當緩衝器(buffer)，讓進到光學交換機系統的光學
封包可以依據交換機中的交通狀況進行適當的排隊(Queue)，不致產生交通衝突。同時，
我們提出的架構可以同時具有先進先出(FIFO MUX)，光學多工塞取(OADM)，封包交換
(Optical Packet Switch)等任意封包排程。我們成功地展示了 10 G/s 的高速光學交換機
系統。這個系統發表在 ECOC 與 Optics Express 上。 
另外我們發展了一個全光學式 2R OOK signal regenerator。我們在 SOA 上利用 Cross Gain 
Suppression 技術，針對光學 OOK 訊號，進行兩次波長轉換，使得受到放大器與光纖色散
污染的 OOK 訊號，經過這個全光學 2R regenerator 重新整形與放大，改善 10Gb/s OOK 訊
號品質，降低誤碼率。這個論文成果，我們發表在 IPC'2011 上。 
最後我們在光學 OFDM 技術上，發展在直接接收(direct detection)機制下的
super-channel 架構，產生超高速光學信號。初步成果發表在 CLEO'2012。最近我們更將
訊號速率提昇至 172Gb/s，頻譜效率達到 2.73 bit/s/Hz。我們相信這個訊號速率應該是
目前台灣光纖通訊傳輸速率的紀錄。成果已經投稿至 ECOC'2012 與 Optics Express。
