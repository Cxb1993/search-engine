計畫類別：一般型計劃
計畫編號：NSC 95-2211-E-003-017

一、中文摘要
本報告合成兩種兩性共聚合物–PAD 與
PAMD，評估其作為混凝土工作維持劑的可行性。
PAD 係以丙烯醯胺(AM)與 DAE 作為反應物，經由
適當的反應條件下合成；PAMD 則以 2-丙烯基胺基
-2-甲基丙烷磺酸(AMP)、甲基丙烯酸(MA)與 DAE
作為反應物，經由適當的反應條件下合成。研究過
程中改變反應物比例、反應條件，合成數種 PAD
與 PAMD，探討其對水泥(砂)漿流動性或混凝土工
作性的影響。結果顯示添加 PAD 之水泥漿體有甚
佳的後期(60 min)迷你坍度，但 PAD 與 SNF(磺酸
系強塑劑)之相容性卻不好。適當單體比例與分子量
下的 PAMD 對水 泥 漿 的流動 性 與 流 動 維持
性有良好的促進效果，以 PADM 取代部份 SNF
添加於含 SNF 的水泥 (砂) 漿體中，可增加漿體之
流 動 維 持 性 。 混 凝 土 中 添 加 混 合 摻 料
(0.2PAMD+0.8SNF)的劑量低於添加 SNF 的劑量便
可達到設計的坍流度值。
關鍵詞：兩性共聚合物、合成、水泥質材料、流動
維持劑。
Abstract:
This report has prepared two amphoteric
copolymers, i.e., PAD and PAMD, and evaluates them
as workability-retention agents. PAD was prepared
from AM and DAE. PAMD was prepared from AMP,
MA and DAE. Experimentally, several PAD and
PAMD samples with different reactant ratios and
molecular weights were prepared and their effects on
the fluidity of cement pastes or mortars, and on the
workability of concrete were studied. The results
indicate that cement pastes or mortars with PAD show
good fluidity at 60 min. Unfortunately, PAD is not
compatible with SNF. PAMD with proper reactant
ratio and molecular weight could promote the fluidity
and fluidity retention of cement pastes or mortars, and
the workability of concrete greatly. The performance
of PAMD is better than that of SNF. PAMD could
improve the fluidity retention of cementitious
materials with SNF. Besides, less amount of the
blended admixture (0.2PAMD+SNF) than that of the
pure SNF admixture was required in reaching the
designed workability of the resulting concrete.
Keywords ： amphoteric copolymer, synthesis,
cementitious material, fluidity-retention agent.
二、緣由與目的
強塑劑由於可以大幅提昇混凝土的工作性
能，使得產製高性能混凝土、自充填混凝土變得可
能，因此其製造與應用被認為是二十世紀混凝土技
術的一項重要突破性發展。第一代的強塑劑包括改
質的磺化木質素與磺化萘甲醛縮合物(SNF)等，其
分散效果以靜電排斥為主。新型的強塑劑則以丙烯
酸鹽或共聚物(PCA)為主，其分散效果則綜合了靜
電排斥與立體障礙兩種方式，故在應用時所需的水
量低於前者，且所形成的流動性混凝土的坍度可維
持至 2hr 以上。
儘管添加 PCA 的混凝土在工作維持性方面表
現較佳，但是由於 PCA 較 SNF 的價格昂貴，故目
前國內外在工程實際應用上還是以使用 SNF 為
主，惟所拌製的混凝土須留意不能有工作性過度變
差的現象。
為改善添加有 SNF 的混凝土之工作維持性，曾
有學者或廠商嘗試將 PCA 取代部分 SNF 添加於材
料中，結果發現同時添加有 SNF 和 PCA 的混凝土
之工作性與工作維持性均變很差。因此，本報告的
目的在於合成兩性共聚物以作為工作維持劑。實驗
過程為改變反應物比例與條件，合成數種 PAD 和
PAMD，探討其與 SNF 的相容性，並將其部份取代
SNF 而添加於含 SNF 之水泥 (砂) 漿和混凝土中，
探討對水泥(砂)漿流動(維持)性、或混凝土工作(維
持)性的影響。

五、結論
1. 成功合成出兩種兩性摻料：PAD 與 PAMD，並
經由 IR 及 NMR 鑑定確認其結構，利用 GPC 測
定分子量。
2. 添加 PAD 之水泥漿體有甚佳的後期迷你坍度，
但初始的迷你坍度則不佳，且 PAD 與 SNF 之相
容性不好。
3. 添加適當單體比例與分子量的 PAMD 之水泥
(砂)漿體的流動性與流動維持性均優於添加
SNF 者。
4. 適當單體比例與分子量的 PAMD 可與 SNF 相
容，例如以 PDAM3 取代部份 SNF 添加於含
SNF 的水泥 (砂) 漿體中，可增加漿體之流動
維持性。
5. 混凝土中添加混合摻料(0.2PAMD3+0.8SNF)的
劑量低於添加 SNF 的劑量便可達到設計的坍
流度值。

EXPERIMENTAL WORK
Materials
The materials used include Type I Portland cement, fine and coarse aggregate, fly ash and
blast-furnace slag, and two chemical admixtures. Cement is from the Taiwan Cement and complies
with ASTM C150. Fine aggregate is from the bank of Pearl River, China. The specific gravity of the
sand is 2.59 and its fineness modulus is 2.8. Coarse aggregate is from the bank of Da-An River. It has
a specific gravity of 2.62 and maximum-size aggregate of 12.5 mm. Both fine and coarse aggregates
meet the standard of ASTM C33, and were washed and dried before use. Fly ash comes from the
Hsin-Da Thermo-Power Plant, which is a subsidiary of Taiwan Electric Power, and has an LOI value
of 4%. Slag is obtained from China Steel and has a Blaine surface area of 400 m2/kg. Both the fly ash
and slag comply with ASTM C311. Table 1 lists the properties of cement, slag, and fly ash. One
chemical admixture used is PDA, which was prepared from -N,
N-dimethyl-N-(3-(-carboxylate)acrylamino) propylammonium ethanate (DAE) and acrylamide
(AAM) in basic condition through free radical polymerization. Details of the preparation procedure
were described elsewhere.[5] Figure 1 shows the chemical structure of PDA. In this study, polymers
with different reactant ratios and weight-average molecular weights were prepared and tested. As
shown in Table 2, the AAM/DAE ratio ranged from 1/1 to 10/1, and molecular weight was ranging
from 1.8x104 to 1.1x105. P11, P12, and P13 have the same reactant ratio, but different molecular
weight; P12, P22, and P32 have close molecular weight, but different DAE/AAM ratio. Another
chemical admixture is a NSF superplasticizer; it was used for comparison.
Preparation and Fluidity Test of Cementitious Materials
PDA was first dissolved in water to form an aqueous solution with 20 % by weight in solid
content. Cement pastes were made by mixing water and cement, with or without addition of polymer
solution. The water/cement ratio (W/C) was fixed at 0.3; the polymer/cement ratio (SP/C) ranged from
0 to 1 % by mass of cement, expressed in terms of dry material. The fluidity of cement pastes was
indicated by the measured mini-slump value, which was determined from a mini-slump test.[9] The
apparatus for this test consists primarily of a hollow mold in the form of a frustrum of a minicone with
the base 40 mm in diameter, the top 20 mm in diameter, and the height 60 mm. The minicone was sat
on a plexiglass plate and filled with cement pastes. Ten strokes were given on the top of the minicone
before it was raised rapidly. The mini-slump value is indicated by the measured spread diameter of
pastes on the plate. Higher spread diameter value represents greater fluidity of materials.
Concrete was made by mixing water, binder (cement + fly ash + slag), sand, and coarse
aggregate, with or without the presence of polymer solution. The water/binder ratio (W/B) was 0.37;
the polymer/binder ratio (SP/B) ranged from 0.54 to 0.8 % by weight. Table 3 lists the mixture
proportions of concrete made with various PDA and NSF. The workability of concrete was indicated
by the slump and slump flow values determined shortly after the end of mixing (0 min) and 60 min
after using the slump test according to ASTM C143.
Adsorption Measurements
Cement pastes (W/C = 0.6) with polymer (SP/C = 0.5 wt %) were prepared. After the samples
were mixed and centrifuged, the clear solutions on the top of the paste samples were taken. The
residual dispersant concentration in the clear solutions was determined by an RI dector.[5] The amount
of polymer adsorbed onto cement particles was calculated from the difference in the admixture
concentration before and after adsorption.
RESULTS AND DISCUSSION
Fluidity of Cement Paste
The major function of superplasticizer is to disperse cement particles and improve workability
not flow if it did not incorporate any chemical admixture. As the polymer was added, both the slump
and slump flow values of the concrete increased due to the dispersing effect of the admixture. For
concrete made with P22 polymer (CP1) at 0.54% by mass of binder, the slump and slump flow value
were about 230 mm and 370 x 370 mm2 at 0 min, and 275 mm and 640 x 630 mm2 at 60 minutes,
respectively. It is noted that the latter slump flow value is higher than the former one. This agrees with
the test results of cement pastes and confirms that PDA indeed could provide slump-releasing effect.
Besides, concrete made with higher admixture dosage showed greater initial workability. When the
polymer dosage was increased to 0.6%, both the initial and 60-min slump values of the concrete (CP2)
were about 265 mm, and the initial and 60-min slump flow values of the concrete (CP2) were about
650x650 mm2. In contrast, concrete made with NSF required higher superplasticizer dosage to achieve
similar slump and slump flow. Both CN1 and CN2 had worse workability-retention properties than the
CP1 and CP2, because concrete containing NSF shows some slump loss along with elapsed time.
Table 4 also shows that slump and slump flow values of concrete (CM1) containing a blended
admixture (P22/NSF = 2/8), which presented improved slump flow and flow retention characteristics
over those made only with NSF. This is consistent with the test results of cement pastes, suggesting
that PDA could blend with NSF without losing its performance. More importantly, the synergetic effect
could occur for the blended admixtures with proper PDA/NSF ratio.
CONCLUSIONS
A new amphoteric copolymer (PDA) has been prepared and examined as a superplasticizer for
concrete. The results indicate that this polymer is as effective as NSF in dispersing cement particles and
enhancing fluidity of cement paste. The use of PDA yields better fluidity-retention properties than
similar system made with NSF. Limited test data indicate that this new admixture can exhibit good
performance in terms of workability and workability retention of the resulting concrete.
