2前言:
由於一些環境污染問題與多成份(multicomponent)氣體在地表下的傳輸行為有重要的
相關性，故近十年來有很多這一方面的研究，例如，岩層中自然產生之致癌性氡(radon)氣
體在土壤與地下結構建築物中的傳輸行為(Loureiro et al., 1990; Kozak et al., 2003)；廢棄物
掩埋場甲烷與二氧化碳氣體的產生與在掩埋場周邊土壤中的傳輸行為 (Poulsen et al., 1996;
Nastev et al., 2001); 再者，受揮發性有機物(volatile organic compounds, VOCs)與非水相有機
液，如汽油屬之碳氫化合物與含氯有機化合物，污染之地下水或未飽和土壤層，亦包含多
成份揮發性有機污染物(Ostendorf et al., 2000)。過去幾年來，有很多研究探討未飽和土壤層
多成份氣體的傳輸機制與行為 (Thorstenson and Pollock, 1989; Massmann and Farrier, 1992;
Sleep, 1998; Fen and Abriola, 2004)，這些研究均指出氣體擴散(vapor diffusion)是造成污染物
移動(contaminant migration)一個很重要的途徑，必須深入了解多成份氣體傳輸的機制與行
為，才能夠有效的進行後續污染整治系統的設計、整治技術、健康風險的評估、土壤氣體
監測資料的分析(Rivett, 1995) 與監測技術的開發等工作。
尤其近年來，地下水污染整治技術中，污染物之自然降解(natural attenuation)方式已成
為大多數污染場址在技術不可行(technically impracticable)情況下的污染整治手段之一。美
國針對地下水與地表以下的污染問題，已發展數個技術型草案，其目的在指導污染整治與
設計者如何證明污染場址確實具有有效的自然降解作用，並針對特性迴異的碳氫化合物及
含氯有機溶劑污染問題，分別規範不同的檢測與評估方式(Bedient et al., 1999)。美國環保署
則於 1997 年發布地表下污染團自然降解監測準則，企圖制定一套原則以規範應用自然降解
為整治方式的必要條件與措施，及所須履行的監測項目與頻率(Bedient et al., 1999)。對於揮
發性有機污染物(volatile organic compounds, VOCs)而言，氣體擴散是污染物自然降解中一
個非常重要的機制與途徑，不論是前置污染物自然降解評估，亦或後續污染場址之污染監
測規畫與執行的工作，皆須針對污染質的揮發與擴散程度做有效的評估。
研究目的:
本研究計畫主要探討與分析未飽和土壤層中氣體的有效擴散係數 (effective diffusion
coefficient)，其不同於大氣系統中的氣體分子擴散係數 (air-filled molecular diffusion
coefficient)，主要差別在於氣體分子在土壤系統中的擴散傳輸會受到土壤介質與其它不同
類之氣體分子的影響，故如何修正此擴散係數，以有效應用於不同之土壤條件與成份氣體
的傳輸系統，將是本研究主要討論的重點。本研究預計分為兩年完成，並分為下列三個方
向進行：其一為理論推導有效擴散係數與土壤、氣體種類特性參數的關係式；其二為文獻
回顧與現有有效擴散係數資料的整理與分析；其三為實驗分析有效擴散係數關係函數與檢
驗必要之土壤氣體滲透係數與 Knudsen 擴散係數，

驗此差異情況，應進行滲透係數小的土壤實驗，其乾土滲透係數應在 10-9 cm2 以下。
由以上的分析結果顯示，以Fick’s law擴散理論所分析的阻礙係數，在土壤氣體擴散系
統之滲透係數小於 10-9 cm2 時，其 Knudsen 擴散的影響將開始顯著，此阻礙係數應該與
土壤滲透性與氣體特性有一些相關性。過去有很多的實驗分析探討此阻礙係數與土壤孔
隙率與含水率的關係，如 Penman (1940)、Grable and Siemer (1968)、Currie (1970)、Lai
et al. (1976)等；Troeh et al. (1982)與 Moldrup et al. (1997)則加入一些經驗常數以考慮土
壤成份與組成的影響；另外，Millington and Quirk (1961)、Ball (1981)、Friejer (1994)與
Schaefer et al. (1997)以連結管束(joint tubes)的觀點，用理論的方式分析氣體有效擴散係
數；還有 Van Duin (1956)、Wesseling (1962)、Lahvis et al. (1999,2004)以率定的方式，
推導阻礙係數與土壤含水率的關係。然而這些文獻資料，有些並未提供或確實紀錄土壤
組成資料，而可能所依據的資料為包含部份需考慮 Knudsen 擴散的影響，故影響阻礙
係數的率定結果，此亦使得該關係式可能無法應用於其它的土壤條件。

important for assessment of natural attenuation, remediation and monitoring network

Fick’s first law of diffusion is conventionally applied to compute diffusive flux of
a gas component in soils. In the application, the air-filled gas-phase diffusion
coefficient is modified by a diffusibility factor, leading to the so-called effective
diffusion coefficient, to account for the impedance to diffusion caused by the tortuous
nature of pores in soils. Determination of the diffusibility factor is important in
predicting the magnitude of gas diffusion, and thereby delineating other transport and
fate processes in soils.
In the past, numerous experimental studies proposed various relationships of the
diffusibility factor with soil porosity, air-filled porosity and some empirical constants,
e.g., [22, 8, 26, 5]. Millington and Quirk [18], Ball [4], Friejer [10] and Schaefer et
al. [24] treated the porous structure of soil as joint tubes and developed theoretical
models for this factor. Van Duin [27] and Lahvis et al. [17], however, used
experimental and/or field data to determine the diffusibility factor as functions of
air-filled porosity and several fitting constants. Moldrup et al. [19] and Weerts et al.
[30] assessed the dependence of this factor on soil water characteristic parameters of
disturbed and undisturbed soils.
A rigorously constitutive relationship of component gas diffusive flux in soils is
described by the Dusty Gas Model (DGM) equations [7]. The component diffusive
flux for multicomponent gas diffusion in soils can be a Fickian expression only under
specific conditions. For example, Jaynes and Rogowski [12], Amali et al. [2] and
Webb and Pruess [29] mentioned a Fickian expression is valid only for a binary gas
diffusion system with a trace gas and for a ternary gas diffusion system with a
stagnant gas. Fen and Abriola [9] used the DGM equations to obtain the component
diffusive flux in a Fickian form for a two-component gas transport system with gas
components of equal molecular masses under isothermal and isobaric conditions. A
correction factor, called the obstruction factor, is defined in the DGM, to account for
the obstruction of soil particles to molecular diffusion. Brugeman [6], Neale and
Nader [21] and Abu-El-Sha’r and Abriola [1] applied the DGM theory and conducted
equimolar countercurrent diffusion experiments in porous systems to determine this
factor as functions of air-filled porosity. Abu-El-Sha’r and Abriola [1] also
determined the Knudsen diffusion coefficient for single gas flow systems with the
same soil samples as the ones used for determining the obstruction factor in
two-component gas diffusion experiments. However, the Knudsen effect is not
pronounced in their diffusion experiments, as a result that the diffusibility factor is
equivalent to the obstruction factor.

Results and discussion
The diffusibility factor was calculated based on eqns (2), (3) and (7) for several
hypothetical diffusion systems, in which CO, TCE or CH4 is considered as a diffusing
gas and N2 is an ambient gas. This calculation also considers a range of soil
permeabilities from 10-10 to 10-16 m2 and water saturation levels between 0.4 and 0.8.
The obstruction factor was calculated based on [18]. A reduction of gas permeability
due to partial saturation of soil is evaluated according to Brook and Corey’s
relationship. Figure 2 presents the percentage difference between the calculated
values of the diffusibility and the obstruction factors related to water saturation of soil
and the corresponding soil effective permeability. The difference is minimal for the
diffusion systems with dry soil permeabilities greater than 10-13 m2. This implies the
Knudsen effect can be neglected for gas diffusion in soil systems within this dry soil
permeability and the tested saturation ranges. For soil systems with dry soil
permeabilities less than 10-13 m2, the difference starts to be significant, which is 6% at
least and 75% at most for these studied cases. Soils in this permeability range have
high silt and clay contents. Besides, the diffusibility values are less than those of the
obstruction factor when considering the Knudsen effect in the diffusibility. Different
diffusing gas also makes the difference changed even though the gas is dilute in the
system. For all of the studied cases, the difference for the CO/N2 system is the
greatest and for the CH4/N2 system is the least. As dry soil permeability decreases,
discrepancies of the difference between different diffusing gas systems also increase.
Note that the difference does decrease as the soil water saturation increases.
Eqn (7) is also used to evaluate the diffusibility factor based on the experimental data
given in articles by assuming that the corresponding diffusion experiments all involve
a dilute diffusing gas. Results were compared to the diffusibility values provided in
the articles as shown in figure 2. Values of the obstruction factor in eqn (7) for [31,
28, 4] were calculated based on the correlation provided in [1] and for the others are
based on [18]. Soil permeability values for the tested soil cores in these experiments
were not provided in the referred articles. Hence, they were determined by fitting
the diffusibility values obtained from eqn (7) to the ones provided in these articles.
The permeability values obtained by the fitting are in the range between 10-13 and
10-20 m2, which is consistent with the permeability range for soils having high silt and
clay contents. In this permeability range the difference between the diffusibility
and obstruction factors is substantial as shown in figure 1. This implies that soil
permeability (or the Knudsen diffusion coefficient) is an important factor affecting
gas diffusion in these experiments and should be considered in the diffusibility factor.
Permeabilities of the soil cores in these diffusion experiments are also evaluated
according to eqn (8) and compared to the permeability values obtained by the fitting.
