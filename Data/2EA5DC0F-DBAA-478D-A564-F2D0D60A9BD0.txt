1g 落門試驗(Trapdoor test)以及有限元素法，進行未
壓密傾斜層狀堆積土層中隧道開挖之模擬，分別針
對不同落門寬度、不同土層厚度與不同傾斜角度的
土層進行探討。研究發現傾斜堆積土層的土壓力分
佈受到傾斜層堆積角度的影響。另外，作用於落門
之垂直土壓力隨著覆土層厚度增加而降低，且於落
門微量向下位移時，作用於落門上之垂直土壓力迅
速降低。當落門向下位移量到達 0.5mm 時，垂直土
壓力則趨於定值；層狀堆積土層傾斜角愈小時，落
門上方所量得之垂直土壓力也愈小。Nakai et al. 
(1997)利用二維與三維有限元素法進行隧道開挖之
模擬，於模型底部設置了隧道元素。隧道開挖的模
擬方式乃是利用增加隧道元素之長度以及利用隧道
頂拱向下位移，來模擬開挖時頂拱上方土壤解壓的
過程。數值模擬分別使用砂土與黏土進行分析，土
壤性質假設砂土為中等緊密砂，黏土為正常壓密黏
土。結果顯示，位於隧道上方之垂直土壓力於隧道
開挖時皆小於初始垂直土壓力，於隧道邊緣附近之
垂直土壓力皆驟然增加，然而隨著距離隧道愈遠垂
直土壓力則逐漸回歸於初始值，上述情況在砂土層
中更為顯著。Hoyaux 與 Ladanyi (1970)利用有限元
素法，探討在正常壓密黏土層中開挖隧道時，隧道
周圍土壤的應力場分佈。結果可以發現，隧道上方
之垂直土壓力受到地拱效應影響而降低，愈靠近隧
道處趨勢更為明顯，而側向土壓力則呈現增加的情
況。隧道兩側水平斷面之垂直土壓力亦受到地拱效
應影響而增加，隨著距離隧道中心愈遠垂直土壓力
亦逐漸歸於初始土壓力大小。Terzaghi (1943) 提出
地拱效應理論與隧道地壓的評估方式，假設矩形隧
道開挖時，隧道頂拱上方土壤失去部分支撐力而產
生破壞滑動面(假設為鉛直的平面)，進行微元體的
垂直向力平衡分析，可以得到隧道頂拱所受之垂直
覆土應力。 
 
綜觀上述前人的研究成果，大部分的模型試驗
都屬於 1g 的試驗，對於隧道周圍土壓力的演化與土
壤漏失量的關係，亦尚未有深入的探討，因此本研
究利用離心隧道模型試驗，模擬隧道開挖時，隧道
體積漏失所導致之隧道周圍土壓力重新分佈情形，
希冀釐清地拱效應導致土壓力改變與隧道所受壓力
之間的複雜關係。 
 
三、離心模型試驗設備、配置與試驗步驟 
 
本研究所採用之地工離心機，其有效旋轉半徑
為 3m，荷載容量為 100g-ton。所有試驗均在離心加
速度為 100g 之離心力場環境下進行。試驗配置圖中
之尺度標示為模型尺寸，而文中各組試驗所量測得

則用來量測試體內的孔隙水壓力；隧道體積漏失量
則利用體積量測儀進行量測。另外，利用土壓計量

3-2 模型隧道 
模型隧道直徑為 60mm，主要是 1.5mm 厚之圓
柱橡皮囊袋，橡皮囊袋的表面則以 0.1mm 之加勁膠
帶包裹。該模型隧道在 100g 之重力加速度場下進行
試驗，可以模擬原型隧道直徑為 6m 之情況。本文
以體積控制之方式來模擬隧道體積漏失的過程，隧
道體積漏失量測設備如圖 1 所示。量測原理係利用
充滿水之模型隧道，於試驗進行過程中，將隧道內
部之水量排出到量測設備中之集水桶中，來模擬隧
道開挖產生的土壤漏失量。土壤漏失量之定義即為
模型隧道所排出之水量體積與隧道總水量體積之比
值，以百分比來表示。為了確保離心機增加繞行 g
數時，模型隧道內之體積保持不變，而到達預定 100 
g 後才允許隧道內部的水排出，藉以模擬隧道的土
壤漏失，故本模型隧道裝置中裝設一電子閥門，用
以控制排水時機。此外，於模擬隧道體積漏失過程
中，為了使土壤漏失模擬過程能夠按照預定的速率
進行，於設備的排水通路間裝置了一個流量調節
閥，以控制隧道內水的漏失速率。而模擬隧道體積
漏失所排出之水量，則藉由儲水桶收集，以量測隧
道體積漏失量。儲水桶由兩段不同截面積之壓克力
管所組成，上半段儲水桶內徑 40mm，下半段內徑
為 15mm。儲水桶之下半段壓克力管可以收集 3％以
內之隧道體積漏失量，此量值約為現地施工之最大
體積漏失量值(1%~2%)，下半段直徑較小乃是為了
使所量之體積漏失量有較高的精度；儲水桶上半段
有較大的截面積可以收集較多之排出水量，以模擬
隧道有較大的土壤漏失量，使隧道周圍土壤產生較
大的變位，以了解其破壞機制。儲水桶所收集之水
量，藉由與儲水桶相連之差壓計(differential pressure 
transducer)來量測，可間接求得排出水量之體積。另
外，模型隧道內裝設一孔隙水壓計，用以量測隧道
內部的支撐壓力值。

4-2 隧道側壁上方有效水平土壓力的分佈 
本研究為了解隧道崩垮過程中，隧道上方有
效水平土壓力的分佈情況，及所受之影響範圍，

B15 及B17 等位置處，及圖 5(C/D=4)中B-Line上
B15 及B18 等位置處，進行水平土壓力之量測。圖
10 及圖 11 分別為C/D=3 與C/D=4 的正規化有效水
平土壓力隨深度之分佈情況，分別表示出不同體
積漏失量VL=1%、2%與 20%之正規化有效水平土
壓力分佈。所謂正規化有效水平土壓力乃為量測
位置處，在不同土壤漏失量時所量得之有效水平
土壓力與隧道開挖前之初始有效水平土壓力之比
值。圖 10 及圖 11 均顯示，除了土壤漏失量的大
小，會影響正規化有效水平土壓力之分佈型態；
另外，地盤深度也是重要的影響因素。以C/D=3
為例，大約以深度 15m處為臨界深度，即深度小
於 15m的範圍內，其正規化有效水平土壓力隨著
土壤漏失量之增加而增加；相反的，深度大於 15m
到隧道軸的範圍內，其正規化有效水平土壓力反
而隨著土壤漏失量之增加而減少，此乃深度大於
15m到隧道軸的範圍內為隧道開挖引致之塑性鬆
動區。另外，C/D=4 之情況，大約以深度 17m處
為臨界深度。同理，即深度小於 17m的範圍內，
其正規化有效水平土壓力隨著土壤漏失量之增加

Using Neural Network and Their Sensitivity Analysis 
Chung-Jung Lee, Ta-Kang Hsiung 
Department of Civil engineering, National Central University 
Chungli, TAIWAN, China 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
ABSTRACT  
 
In this paper, a well-trained MLP model (9×11×1) was developed to 
recognize the liquefaction and non-liquefaction cases. The excellent 
performance with a 96.6% high success recognition rate for the total 
cases can be made. The model enables one to map all influence factors 
to predict liquefaction occurrence. A useful index, average sensitivity 
factor, SFi is proposed to quantify the relative importance of the 
influence factors affecting the 2-class liquefaction recognition problem.  
 
KEY WORDS: liquefaction; neural network; sensitivity analysis. 
 
INTRODUCTION 
 
Soil liquefaction is known as one of the most severe seismic hazards 
and it may damage structures founded on both shallow and deep 
foundations and disrupt buried lifelines as well. In engineering practice, 
it is very essential to identify a site vulnerable to liquefaction-induced 
damages and then to mitigate possible damages by taking appropriate 
measures in advance. A methodology termed the “simplified 
procedure” has evolved as a standard of practice for evaluating the 
liquefaction potential since Seed and Idriss (1971) developed and 
published the basic simplified procedure. Since that time, various 
simplified methods (SPT-based, CPT-based, and Vs-based methods) have 
been proposed and become a standard practice throughout the world. 
These simplified methods were generally presented in a chart that 
defines the boundary of liquefaction and non-liquefaction in an 
empirical plot of cyclic resistance ratio (CRR) versus the corrected SPT-
N values, the normalized CPT tip resistance, and the normalized Vs, 
respectively. Recently more liquefaction cases are available for 
calibrating and modifying the boundary of liquefaction and non-
liquefaction cases in the empirical plots (Hwang and Yang, 2001; Lee, 
2005).  
Evaluation of liquefaction potential is a complicated multivariable 
problem and needs to examine the key parameters of controlling 
liquefaction occurrence and to evaluate the relative importance among 
them. In recent years, a useful and powerful computation tool, Artificial 
Neural Network (ANN), has been introduced for solving the problem of 
recognizing liquefaction cases (2-class pattern recognition). Many 
researchers (Juang et al. 2000; Chern et al. 2000) have reported that the 
same as or even more accuracy than the simplified methods can be 
achieved by using ANN to recognize liquefaction or non-liquefaction 
cases. In this study, a total of 644 SPT-based cases of liquefaction and 
non-liquefaction were compiled and used for training and testing a 
multi-layer perceptron (MLP) with 9 input parameters. The final optimal 
architecture of the MLP with the optimal interconnection weights and 
threshold values was identified through many trial-and-error processes. 
A new sensitivity factor was derived to reveal the variation of output 
subject to a small change of the input in the architecture of the MLP. 
The average sensitivity factor for the well-trained MLP with the highest 
performance was then calculated to quantify the relative importance for 
each input parameter used in the liquefaction analysis.  
 
NEURAL NETWORK  
 
Multi-layer Perceptron 
An ANN is a highly simplified model of the biological structures found 
in a human brain. The layered structure is composed of a large number 
of interconnected processing elements to mimic the biological neurons. 
One of the most popular types of ANN is the multi-layer perceptron 
with the back propagation algorithm. Figure 1 shows a typical 
architecture of MLP consisting of three layers of interconnected neuron 
(Input layer, hidden layer and output layer). This architecture is 
represented as “ pnm ×× ”. Here “m” is the number of neuron in the 
input layer, “n” is the number of neuron in the hidden layer, and “p” is 
the number of neuron in the output layer. Each neuron in the layer is 
fully connected to all neurons in the next higher layer, and each 
connection has a weight (a scalar) associated with it. These weights 
determine the nature and the strength of the influence between the 
interconnected neurons. The output signals from one layer are 
transmitted to the subsequent layer through links that amplify or 
attenuate or inhibit the signals using the associated interconnection 
weights. Except for the neuron in the input layer, the net input to each 
neuron is the sum of the weighted outputs of the neuron in the previous 
layer. An activation function such as the sigmoid logistic function is 
used to calculate the output of the neutron in the hidden and output 
layers. The neurons in the hidden layer play the roles of nonlinear 
transformation that enable the MLP to simulate a more complex and

For a complicated problem, understanding which “causes” play major 
roles on “effects” is very helpful for solving problems correctly and 
effectively. Yang and Zhang (1997) proposed a concept to identify the 
significance of each “cause” factor (input) on the “effect” (outputs) in a 
well-trained neural network. They defined a “relative strength of effects 
(RSE)” to evaluate the sensitivity for each input to the outputs. This 
enables ANN users to recognize the most sensitive “factors” affecting 
“cause”. An input with higher RSE has the stronger influence on the 
final output. In this study, the modified concept from Yang and Zhang’s 
is used to evaluate the sensitivity of input variables for recognizing the 
liquefaction potential problem (2-class pattern recognition problem).

A total of 730 SPT-based field case records were extracted from the 
literatures compiled by Youd et al. (1997), Nobel et al. (1999), 
Boulanger et al. (1997), and Hwang and Yang (2001). Those case 
records cover a wider range of earthquake magnitudes (Mw=5.3-9.2) 
and of peak ground accelerations (PGA) and those events occurred at 
different countries or regions. Most of the earthquake magnitudes used 
in the study were in the moment magnitude that came from official 
website of USGS for consistent comparisons. In the study, 644 cases 
were selected from 730 cases. The discarded cases were the cases of 
some effective parameters missing or of liquefaction not occurring in a 
level ground. There were 382 liquefied cases and 262 non-liquefied 
cases. A total of 450 cases (70% of 644 cases, randomly selected) were 
used in the training phase and 194 cases (30% of 644 cases) in the 
testing phase.

Three experiments were performed using different architectures of 
MLP model (9×10×1, 9×11×1, and 9×13×1) to assess the performance 
of the model. These experiments used the same input data sets (644 
data pairs) for training and testing the neural networks to determine the 
best performance model architecture. The result of experiment shows 
that the 9×11×1 architecture of MLP model can give the highest 
success rates (overall success rate 96.6%, training success rate 98.9%, 
and testing success rate 91.2%). The Seed’s simplified method revised 
by NCEER (Youd and Idriss, 2001) was also conducted to perform the 
liquefaction potential analysis for the same set of cases. Only 550 cases 
were correctly identified; the recognition success rate was about 85.4%. 
The results indicate that the 9×11×1 MLP model has been very 
successful in learning the mapping function between the input and 
output data. The calculated connection weights and the thresholds are 
listed in Tables 2-a and 2-b for the well-trained 9×11×1 model.
