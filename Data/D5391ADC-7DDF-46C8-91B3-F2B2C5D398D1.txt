中文摘要
本研究主要是利用斷面水槽進行水工模型試驗，探討透水複合式結
構物之消波特性。此種結構物是將礫石填充於兩片垂直孔隙板之間所組
成，主要是利用波浪通過前、後孔隙板，會使波浪產生相位差，及前後
水位高差不同達到消能的功效，並利用水流在通過兩板間填充礫石時，
能因水的黏滯性與礫石粗糙度產生能量損失，進而達到消減波浪的能量
之效果。
關鍵字:透水、反射率、能量損失、複合式透水結構物
ABSTRACT
In this investigation, we conducted a series of physical experiments to
estimate the wave dissipation characteristic of wave attacking the composite
breakwater which is consisted of two vertical porous plates and gravel filled
in the gap of plates. The wave energy dissipation can be not only attributed
to the wave phase lag due to waves pass through the porous plates but also
the friction between the viscosity of fluid and gravel. Besides, the difference
of water head between water wave pass through the composite breakwater is
also an effective approach to dissipate wave energy. The wave energy of
wave can be dissipated efficiently by above approaches.

本文主要以斷面水槽中進行水工
模型試驗，探討透水複合式結構物之消

波能的效果，由於礫石間太緊密造成波

當 σ2h/g 為 2.484~4.106 時，能量損失

Wave Scattering by a Submerged Porous Plate Wave Absorber 
 
Ching-Yun Yueh and Shih-Hsuan Chuang 
Department of Harbor and River Engineering, National Taiwan Ocean University 
Keelung, TAIWAN, ROC 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
ABSTRACT 
 
The wave scattering of normal incident waves by absorbing-type 
breakwaters is investigated in this paper. The absorbing-type 
breakwaters consist of a submerged permeable structure, a solid back 
wall and a submerged horizontal or slightly inclined porous plate. The 
whole fluid domain is divided into four regions: a permeable structure 
region and three pure water regions. Under the assumptions of linear 
wave theory, the Darcy’s law in the porous plate, and the pore velocity 
potential theory of Sollitt and Cross (1972) in the permeable structure 
region, a multi-domain BEM model is created to calculate the reflection 
coefficients and the wave forces of water waves by several properties 
of the breakwaters. The numerical model is calibrated by previous 
numerical studies of the limiting cases of a partially submerged 
impermeable structure and a horizontal porous plate with a solid back 
wall. From the numerical results, the wave dissipation effect of 
permeable structure was found as expected better than that of 
impermeable structure. In the cases of horizontal porous plate, the 
smaller submergence depth of the plate makes the smaller reflection 
coefficient. However, the reflection coefficient increased as inclined 
angle increased in the cases of inclined porous plate. 
 
KEY WORDS:  Porous plate; permeable structure; BEM; reflection. 
 
INTRODUCTION 
 
Breakwaters that are widely used along shorelines, beaches, harbors, or 
marinas may vary in type according to their use. The general trend of 
breakwater development is from gentle slope breakwaters to vertical 
ones, from rubble-mound breakwaters to composite breakwaters. It is 
customary to use rock or concrete blocks mound at the front of the 
upright section of a vertical breakwater. This type of breakwater is 
considered as a defence structure not only reduces the incident wave 
energy but also uses as the toe protection. Recently, many new types of 
breakwaters have been proposed and extensively studied for controlling 
ocean waves efficiently. Application of submerged structures as 
breakwaters in coastal engineering practice has been emphasized during 
the recent years. Since a submerged breakwater does not visually 
partition the natural sea, it is favored from the point of view of marine 
scenery. Actually, a submerged horizontal plate has been suggested as a 
breakwater for a long time. The previous studies show that when an 
incident wave encounters the plate, depending on the dimension and the 
submergence depth of the plate as well as the incident wave height and 
wavelength, part of the wave energy is reflected, part is dissipated in 
the vicinity of the plate, and the remainder is transmitted into the region 
behind the plate. This leads to conjecture that such a plate could be 
used as an effective device for wave force reduction. In recent decades, 
perforated breakwaters have become popular as they can allow water 
waves to transmit through it, reduce the wave reflection and wave run-
up in front of the structures. This paper presents the wave scattering of 
normal incident waves by absorbing-type breakwaters. The absorbing-
type breakwaters consist of a submerged permeable structure, a solid 
back wall and a submerged horizontal or slightly inclined porous plate. 
The interaction of water waves with a rigid porous plate was 
investigated by several researchers. Sollitt and Cross (1972) used 
Lorentz’s principle of equivalent work to analyze the problem about 
ocean wave reflection and transmission at a permeable breakwater. 
Chwang (1983) developed a porous wave-maker theory and found that 
the porous effect reduces not only the wave amplitude but also the 
hydrodynamic force acting on the wave-maker. Patarapanich (1984, a) 
studied wave reflection from a submerged horizontal plate in shallow 
water. For a particular submergence, the reflection coefficient increases 
alternately to a peak value and then decreases to zero. Patarapanich 
(1984, b) used the finite element method to study the wave forces and 
moment on a horizontal plate due to wave scattering. Patarapanich and 
Cheong (1989) found that the optimum plate width may be about 
0.5~0.7 times that of the wavelength above the plate for the plate 
submergence of around 0.05~0.15 times the water depth, for minimum 
transmission of waves. Yueh and Kuo (1992) applied the boundary 
element method (BEM) to examine the wave pressure acting on a 
vertical quay and horizontal force and the uplift force exerted on a 
submerged inclined plate which is placed before a vertical quay and 
inclined at an angle of θ. Chwang and Wu (1994) solved the 
modification of the incident wave field by a rigid submerged porous 
disk and found that the wave scattering by a disk includes a process of 
focusing wave energy. Yu and Chwang (1994) applied the boundary 
element method to investigate the reflection and transmission of surface 
waves by a horizontally submerged rigid/porous plate. They found that 
a plate with proper porosity can suppress significantly the wave 
reflection. Wu et al. (1998) investigated the phenomenon of wave

the R  implies the energy.
