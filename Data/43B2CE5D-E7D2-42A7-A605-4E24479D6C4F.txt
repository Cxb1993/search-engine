The construction of 3D information of object is widely applied in various applications. For the 
commercial 3D information construction systems, the software and hardware are expensive and not easily 
modified/maintained. Therefore low-cost dual webcams incorporating self-developing software are developed 
to construct the 3D information of object.First the calibration plate is used to obtain the intrinsic parameters 
(focal length), scaling factors, and extrinsic parameters (rotation matrix and translation vector) for each 
webcam. Then dual webcams are used to take the image of the specific objects, find the image coordinates of 
object’s feature points, apply the camera parameters obtained above and the epipolar line geometry to 
compute the correspondences between stereo images, and derive their scaling factors to construct the 3D 
object information. Finally the low-cost webcam based 3D object information construction system can be 
achieved. 
 
Keyword: digital image processing, coordinate transformation, epipolar line geometry, 3D object 
information construction

Abstract—Secret data delivery is an important application in
the modern world. When sending secret data over public com-
puter networks, it has been found that using the Steganography
technique is more suitable than the Cryptography technique,
because encrypted data looks like random noise that an unex-
pected user may not pay attention to. This paper presents a (2, 3)
threshold secret sharing scheme to achieve the goal of secret data
delivery with the high visual quality of a stego image and good
security. The concept of Sudoku was adopted in the proposed
method. According to the method’s design, secret data can only
be extracted when a minimum number of two shadow images are
received. Furthermore, a large number of solutions of Sudoku
are an advantage to increase secret data delivery security.
Index Terms—Data hiding, secret sharing, steganography,
Sudoku
I. INTRODUCTION
Because of advanced information techniques development,
how to securely deliver secret data over public computer
networks has become a popular research issue. Cryptography
and Steganography are two popular methods used to achieve
the goal of secret data delivery. The Steganography technique
uses a cover medium as a carrier to carry secret data and
send it to the receiver [1], [2], [3], [8], [9]. Multimedia,
such as a text, audio, video, or image ﬁle, is suitable as a
cover medium, because it contains a signiﬁcant amount of
redundant space that can be used to carry secret data. In this
paper, the grayscale image is used as a cover medium for
the proposed secret sharing scheme. (t, n) threshold secret
sharing is one of the popular secret data delivery techniques.
The (t, n) secret sharing technique is used to separate secret
data into many parts and embed these parts into n shadows
(i.e., a modiﬁed cover image generated by secret embedding).
The secret data can then be extracted when it is equal to, or
greater than, t shadows cooperating with the secret data being
extracted. In other words, if the receiver obtains less than
t shadows, the receiver cannot extract any secret data from
the shadows. In this way, the security of secret data delivery
can be increased. Tsai et al. (2002) proposed sharing multiple
secrets using the digital image method to achieve the goal of
secret data delivery [7]. Their proposed multiple secret sharing
scheme utilizes four cover images to carry six secret images.
Chang et al. (2008) proposed a data embedding method using
Sudoku [2]. Chang et al.’s method is a spatial domain method
which utilizes the properties of Sudoku to modify the pixel
value to imply secret data. Chang et al.’s method requires less
computation cost with the acceptable visual quality of stego
images. However, Chang et al.’s method can be improved upon
in terms of security. A (2, 3) secret sharing scheme is proposed
in this paper. The proposed method was inspired by Chang
et al. (2008)’s method to design a novel secret data sharing
scheme. The proposed method uses a cover image to generate
three stego images (also called shadow images) to carry secret
data. Secret data can be extracted when a receiver receives
a minimum number of two shadow images. The proposed
method not only achieves the goal of secret data delivery, but
also improves upon the security of secret data delivering.

numbers from 1 to 9 into a 9 × 9 grid. Garns invented the
Sudoku puzzle and published it in Dell Magazines in 1979.

Nikoli substantially advertised the puzzle game. In 2005, the
Sudoku puzzle became popular worldwide [5]. A Sudoku grid
contains 3×3 sub-blocks, where each block contains different
digits, from 1 to 9. Each row and each column in the grid are
also composed of digits from 1 to 9. Fig. 1 is a simple example
