重要發現結果摘要: 
Cyclic-loading effects on a nickel-based superalloy are investigated with in-situ neutron-diffraction 
measurements. The temperature evolution subjected to cyclic loading is estimated based on the 
lattice-strain evolution. The calculated thermoelastic responses are compared with the measured bulk 
temperature evolution. Two transitions in the temperature-evolution are observed. The first transition, 
observed with the neutron-measurement results, is associated with the cyclic 
hardening/softening-structural transformation. The second transition is observed at larger number of 
fatigue cycles.  It has a distinct origin and is related to the start of irreversible structural transformations 
during fatigue. A lattice-strain asymmetry behavior is observed. The lattice-strain asymmetry is 
quantified as a grain-orientation-dependent transverse/loading parameter. This stain-asymmetry 
evolution reveals the irreversible plastic deformation subjected to the fatigue. The irreversible fatigue 
phenomena might relate to the formation of the microcracks. At elevated temperatures the cyclic 
hardening/softening transition starts at lower fatigue cycles as compared to room temperature. A 
comparison between the room-temperature and the elevated-temperature fatigue experiments is 
performed. The asymmetry-parameter evolutions show the same irreversible trends at both the room and 
the elevated temperatures.

重要發現結果摘要: 
This book chapter addresses three issues concerning the applications of neutron diffractometers to 
investigate the metallic structural materials: (1) how to characterize the microstructure with neutron 
diffractometers, (2) how to correlate the diffraction results to the deformation behavior of the structural 
materials, and (3) where may we apply these advanced neutron diffractometers. So far, many successful 
deformation mechanisms of the metallic materials have been reported via the understanding of the 
microscopy results and theoretical calculations. However, due to the  inherently limited small gauge 
volume of the microscopy, there are still various uncovered behaviors underneath the deformation. On 
the other hand, the high-penetration of the neutrons can penetrate the bulk materials, which can give 
ensemble average for complimentary theoretical modeling and calculations. There are already several 
publications reporting new findings based on the combinations of neutron and microscopy capabilities 
combined with comprehensive modeling. This chapter describes an introduction to the neutron 
diffraction of the mechanical behavior studies and also selects several useful examples in the review of 
the published papers.
