numerical simulation and experimental study of spray nozzle parameters, we find that the spray 
evaporation performance and pressure drop increase as the nozzle size decreases.  We conclude 
the optimum nozzle diameter is about 0.23 mm, which maintain the pressure drop within 100 
kPa.  The effect of inclination is insignificant for pin-finned surfaces.  The heat transfer 
coefficient increases with increasing flow rates.  The critical heat flux increases with increasing 
fin height/width ratio as a result of the total surface area enhancement.  However, the longer 
fins also results in reduction of the flow velocity and convection heat transfer efficiency.  From 
the experiments of spray evaporation in a confined evaporator, the comparison of five versus 
nine nozzles showed that evenly distributed nine nozzle device results in better performance 
than the five nozzle device.  The pin-finned surface having 0.2 fin gap and 0.8 mm fin height 
results in 0.161 K/W thermal resistance with 150 ml/min flow rate.  A prototype of the spray 
evaporation cooling device has been designed based on the findings of the present project.  
Compared with a commercial water cooling device having multiple channels, the pumping flow 
rate of the commercial device is nine times of the present prototype, yet the thermal resistance 
of the commercial device is 3~5 times of the spray cooling device using water.  The thermal 
resistance of the spray cooling prototype using FC-72 is about 0.2 K/W at 80~140 W heat input.  
Although its thermal resistance is significantly greater than that using water, it offers the 
advantage of di-electricity.  Hence, the FC-72 is still a promising working fluid for cooling of 
electronics.

二、緣由與目的 
半導體技術的進步使電路集積程度提高、電腦運算速度倍增，造成晶片整體發熱量與
單位面積之熱通量同步增加，電腦的散熱問題已成為電腦設計中極重要的一環。根據
ITRS(International Technology Roadmap for Semiconductors)的預估，晶片熱通量將於西元
2010 年超過 70 W/cm2。「液體冷卻」是將晶片的熱以液體對流方式帶離晶片較遠的散熱鰭
片再將熱散至空氣，目前的發展趨勢是以微小管徑之多渠道(multiple micro channels)散熱
器、或將液體加壓以噴嘴(Nozzle)噴擊在發熱表面上之「噴擊散熱」(Jet impingement)。 
「滴淋蒸發」(Falling film evaporation)或「噴淋蒸發」(spray evaporation)之熱傳模式與
「噴霧冷卻」(spray cooling)十分相似，但流體通過「滴淋蒸發」之滴淋裝置的壓損較小、
流至加熱表面的速度也較小。流體在通過「噴霧冷卻」(spray cooling)的噴嘴時產生較大壓
降的同時因膨脹作用而降溫，因此可提高其散熱能力，但對於電腦的散熱元件而言，其壓
力過大形成實際應用上的困難；而反觀「滴淋蒸發」，雖然其強制對流效應較「噴霧冷卻」
弱，在低熱通量時的熱傳性能隨流速下降而變差（Bartoli et al., 1997）；但在高熱通量時因
為是以核沸騰為主要的熱傳機制。因此若輔以高沸騰性能之表面，其整體性能應可與「噴
霧冷卻」媲美。而且滴淋蒸發的各元件承受壓力較小，較易於實際用在電腦散熱上。核沸
騰本身即是一種具有高熱傳效率的散熱方式，但因為電腦運作時發熱量忽大忽小，因此若
單以核沸騰進行散熱，則會在發熱量突然上升時因為熱傳機制由自然對流轉為核沸騰時，
產生「沸騰起使過熱溫度」(Incipient superheat)過高的問題。然而在「滴淋蒸發」的散熱模
式中，當核沸騰尚未發生時，是以熱傳效率較高的滴淋蒸發的機制傳熱，因此筆者認為以
高沸騰效率的表面進行滴淋蒸發的散熱模式，應該是可行性極高的電腦散熱模式。本計畫
將研究以滴淋蒸發方式之電腦散熱裝置，針對 CPU 之散熱條件，探討滴淋蒸發在電腦散熱
應用上的各種特性與效能。 
 
2.1 文獻探討與國內外相關研究 
噴擊冷卻適用於高熱通量，因此被用在許多不同工業中，如核能電廠、離子束之散熱、
冷凍空調、冶金等（Gonzalez and Black, 1997）。早期的應用偏重於冶金之高溫急速冷卻，
而以高溫純水之薄膜沸騰(Film boiling)為主要研究對象，如 Oliphant et al (1998) 發現在無
核沸騰發生的中、低發熱條件下，流量越大性能越好，而以空氣加壓噴出水霧之冷卻模式
所需之流量比單相噴擊冷卻之流量小。在電腦散熱與雷射冷卻等方面的應用溫度則低許
多，所用之工作流體則包含純水與非導電性流體(dielectric fluids), 如 FC-72、FC-87)。Lin and

他們比較 FC-72、甲醇、與純水之噴淋熱傳性能與臨界熱通量(CHF)，並且以 FC-72 比較系

朝上與朝下噴擊表面的實驗，發現其噴擊方向沒有明顯影響。噴霧冷卻也應用在航空器材

2.1.3  流量及過冷度對熱傳性能與臨界熱通量之分析 
 Sung and Mudawar (2006)發現增加流量可提升單相熱傳性能，且延遲沸騰起始時間，
CHF 也會較高。增加過冷度，對單相熱傳性能影響較小，但可延遲沸騰起始時間。 
Mudawar and Estes (1996)認為噴淋冷卻之 CHF 與體積流率有關，而不是平均液滴速
度，原因為在不同體積流率下，可能因不同噴淋裝置而有一樣的平均液滴速度；當噴淋範
圍剛好與表面四個邊相切時，會有最大的 CHF，且隨著過冷度由 13℃增至 33℃時，CHF
也會由 80 W/cm2 增加至 109 W/cm2。 
 Rybicki and Mudawar (2005)以 PF-5052 向上噴淋 1 cm2 的熱源表面，研究指出增加流量
可以增加單相熱傳性能和提升 11.1 % ~ 13.5 %的 CHF，但對核沸騰機制則無太大影響。而
過冷度由 12℃增加至 27℃時，CHF則可提升 7.6 % ~ 55.5 %。此實驗數據與先前噴淋實驗(以
FC-72、FC-87、水朝下噴淋)加以比較，發現朝上或朝下噴淋對於熱傳性能及 CHF 沒有太
大的影響。

噴淋式蒸發模型都忽略了熱通量或核沸騰之影響，因此無法有效預測高熱通量之實驗數
據；筆者並提出以疊加式模型為基礎之改良模型，可有效預測噴淋式蒸發之熱傳係數，預
測光滑管誤差約在 -20% ~+25﹪以內，預測增強管(Tu-B)之誤差則不超過 ±30% 。筆者
(Chien and Lin, 2007)並曾進行滴淋蒸發實驗，將強化表面製作於在平板上，並且改變平板
傾斜角度與滴淋流量，以研究傾斜角與表面幾何參數對滴淋薄膜蒸發之影響。綜合前述各
項文獻，雖然有不少電腦散熱之池沸騰研究及噴擊(噴霧)冷卻研究，但對於以高效率沸騰表
面進行滴淋蒸發的研究卻被忽視。筆者認為利用高效率沸騰表面在高熱通量時的高熱傳效
率，並搭配滴淋蒸發在中、低熱通量時的高熱傳效率，以及滴淋蒸發設備壓力低於噴擊冷
卻的優勢，在電腦散熱等熱集中發熱元件的散熱上應該是十分可行的。 
 
2.2 本研究計畫之目的 
    本計劃的整體目標，在於將滴淋蒸發方式應用在晶片散熱、雷射散熱、等熱集中元件
之散熱器；研究滴淋蒸發在高熱通量、低噴淋高度、狹小蒸發空間之下的各種熱傳現象與
流場狀況。本年度之研究目標為：以滴淋非導電性流體 FC-72 於水平及傾斜之加熱面，探
討滴淋孔徑、流量、熱通量、表面結構及傾斜角度等參數對熱傳性能的影響。

3.1.2 實驗程序 
一、測試表面清洗 
    實驗中所使用之測試表面均為紅銅之材質，因此在實驗前需先清洗測試表面。表面清
潔液採用重量濃度百分比為 15 %之鹽酸(HCl)與 85 %之純水(H2O)所調製而成。將表面浸至
清潔液中約 15 ~ 30 秒，再以清水沖洗，並使用柔軟之刷毛將表面之氧化物去除。重複此
一步驟約 4 ~ 5 次，至表面氧化層除去為止。 
二、環路測漏 
    將測試表面置於加熱塊上方並將其固定於滴淋孔的正下方；將管路各部位之接合處鎖
緊且確認各部位均緊密接合後即灌入高壓氮氣，其灌入之量為 202.65 kPa(約為二大氣壓之
量)，經過 24 小時之站壓過程後觀察壓力值之變化，若壓力值維持不變則代表環路無洩漏
之狀態，故將高壓氮氣排放並利用真空泵將腔體抽真空至 10-3 Torr，以確保腔體及環路內
無殘存之水分或氣體。 
三、系統絕熱 
    絕熱主要之功能在於盡量降低腔體內之熱量與外界溫度做熱交換，而絕熱在本實驗中
分成兩個部份： 
(1)加熱塊之絕熱：將加熱塊外部以石綿網格包覆，由於石綿為一防火隔熱之材質，熱
傳係數極低(約 0.15 W/mK)，因此加熱塊之熱量能隔絕在內而不與外界之空氣做熱交換導致
熱大量散失。 
 (2)環路與腔體之絕熱：腔體及管路均在其外圍包覆保溫棉，使液體在流經管路時其熱
量不與外界做熱交換。 
四、工作流體充填 
    待腔體確定保持真空後即將工作流體自充填閥灌入。實驗若是以核沸騰為主，則充填
之量為 200 ml；若實驗是以滴淋為主，則充填之量約為 150 ml。核沸騰實驗之液面需高出
待測表面之 5 mm，因此充填之量較滴淋蒸發實驗來得多。

3.2.2 實驗程序 
(I) 實驗容器之測漏標準 
    實驗設備組裝完成後，或有更換測試表面時，在實驗進行前均需先行測漏。測漏標準
及程序為： 
1. 測漏前先充填 3 kg/cm2 之氮氣至測試容器內部，站壓兩小時後，檢測壓力是否有下降，
若有容器內壓力有降低，則代表測試容器有洩漏，因此必須排除洩漏狀況。若無洩漏時，
可進行抽真空之動作。 
2. 利用真空泵浦以 60 l/min 之抽氣流率，將系統抽至真空(使用之真空幫浦可抽至約 10-3 
Torr)，並持續約三十分鐘以期能將系統內水氣抽掉。 
3. 測漏時系統須維持真空狀態至少 24 小時以上。其真空狀態若依本實驗所使用之壓力計
精確度而定，則為 1 Tor 以下。 
 
(II) 滴淋與沸騰實驗之操作程序如下述： 
1. 實驗開始前需先清洗紅銅製的測試表面，表面清潔液採用重量百分濃度 15%之鹽酸
(HCl)及 85%之純水(H2O)所調置而成。將表面浸至清潔液中約 15~30 秒，即以清水沖
洗之。重複此一步驟數次直至表面氧化層除去為止。 
2. 實施系統測漏，整個實驗約需在 50oC 下進行約 12 個小時才可完成，故測漏極為重要，
若系統在測漏後 24 小時仍維持壓力為 1 Torr 以下，則視為符合實驗要求。 
3. 利用真空泵浦將系統抽至真空(約 10-3 Torr)，並持續約三十分鐘以期能將系統內水氣抽
掉。 
4. 調整實驗所需之測試表面傾斜角度(0o, 30o, 45o, 60o or 90o)，實驗順序由 0 o 至 90o。 
5. 充填工作液體(FC-72：非導電液體)：(i)池沸騰實驗：須先關閉測試容器入出口之閥門，
並將 FC-72 充至液面高出測試表面約 5mm 為止。(ii)滴淋蒸發實驗：工作液體需低於
測試表面，以避免產生池沸騰，最後再調整實驗所需之測試流量，實驗順序由高流量
至低流量。 
6. 開啟輔助加熱器(16 Watt)，再輸入測試表面所需之熱通量，將工作液體加熱至 50oC 後，
使其溫度維持在待測溫度附近(50±0.5℃)沸騰 1~3 小時，使溶於工作液體的殘存氣體釋
出。 
7. 再次抽真空(約 2~3 秒)，以抽出系統殘餘的氣體，若液面過高則持續抽出蒸氣至所需液
面高出測試表面約 5mm 為止。 
8. 若測試表面則靜待系統穩定在±0.3℃約三十分鐘後即可量測。 
9. 利用冷凝器盤管與恆溫槽之溫度設定來控制沸騰空間的溫度，待池溫穩定(±0.5℃內)
且與待測溫度相差在±0.3℃以內，維持五分鐘後便可開始連續擷取三筆以上之數值，
取最後三筆之平均值為所需之數據。 
10. 漸增 (或遞減)電源供應器輸入的熱量，每測試一組加熱量後，重複步驟 9、10。本實
驗先以漸增熱通（increasing heat flux）之方式量測到最大熱通量後，再遞減熱通量
(decreasing heat flux)完成實驗量測。

熱通量(q” 小於 240 kW/m2)下，熱傳性能以最大流量(99.1 ml/min)所呈現之性能為最佳，原
因為衝擊效應帶走表面之熱量，因此熱傳性能在此流量下產生最佳之效益；在熱通量為 240

流量下熱傳性能均較核沸騰來得佳；當熱通量大於 640 kW/m2 後，滴淋熱傳中最大流量(99.1 
ml/min)所呈現之性能曲線開始下降，唯核沸騰曲線保持其熱傳性能，原因為核沸騰實驗
中，其液面約高出測試表面 5 mm，因此在高熱通量下表面不會形乾涸之狀況，相較之下滴

乾涸，則性能開始下降，並隨著乾涸之面積增加，性能降幅亦增加，因此在圖 4.4 中形成
此現象。

4.3.2 九個滴淋孔對熱傳性能之影響 
圖4.21為不同測試表面在九個滴淋孔且流量為150 ml/min下之性能曲線圖，其性能最佳
的表面是P-0.2-0.8表面，接著依序為S-0.2-0.8、SC-0.4-0.4、PC-0.4-0.4與光滑表面。光滑表
面與上述之原因一樣，其表面積小且無法把液體留在表面上，故性能最差。而PC-0.4-0.4與
SC-0.4-0.4表面之性能在九個滴淋孔則有不同的結果，可以由圖中得知九孔的SC-0.4-0.4表面
之熱傳性能均比PC-0.4-0.4表面來得高，這是因為有更多的滴淋孔直接衝擊流道，讓
SC-0.4-0.4表面有更多流道受衝擊推力的影響，讓液體迅速填補至各個位置；而PC-0.4-0.4表
面雖因孔數增加使得受衝擊的流道變多，但九孔之衝擊推力變小，讓流體在流道內流動的
距離變短，故在九個滴淋孔下SC-0.4-0.4表面之性能會高於PC-0.4-0.4表面。C-822表面因滴
淋孔數變多，使其受推力影響之流道也變多，故其性能不像五孔時與P-0.2-0.8表面差異甚
大，但S-0.2-0.8表面之渠道總體積還是低於P-0.2-0.8表面，當發生沸騰狀態時，P-0.2-0.8表
面因流道內之液體量較多，使其藉由大量的潛熱來帶走表面的熱量，故P-0.2-0.8表面在高
加熱量時之性能高於S-0.2-0.8表面。 
九個滴淋孔對槽道表面的影響比五孔大，當滴淋孔越多，受到液柱衝擊的流道也就越
多，受衝擊之流道因液體流速較快，讓液體能迅速填補至流道各個位置，增加熱傳性能。
滴淋孔分佈越平均，熱傳性能越佳。

六、結論 
1. 依滴淋孔徑幾何尺寸與滴淋高度測試模組之實驗結果可知，孔徑為 0.17 mm 之衝擊對流
性能雖然較佳，但其壓降過大，並不符合經濟效益；0.41 mm 壓降值小，但其熱傳性能
中衝擊效應不佳，在低熱通量下呈現之性能亦較低；0.23 mm 時壓降值均能控制於 100 
kPa 以下，且其整體之熱傳性能在低熱通量下呈現強烈之衝擊效應；高熱通量下沸騰效
應亦能維持一定之性能。於本實驗中提出此三種孔徑中，以 0.23 mm 為滴淋散熱中較佳
之選擇。 
2. 在 10 mm 與 30 mm 兩種滴淋距離的比較下，低熱通量下兩種滴淋距離所呈現之性能幾
乎一致，高熱通量下則以滴淋距離為 10 mm 所呈現之熱傳性能較佳。 
3. 在 5 種傾斜角度下之滴淋熱傳性能，以三滴淋流量之測試結果。發現在三種滴淋流量
下，光滑表面之滴淋熱傳性能以 0o 與 30o 傾斜角度最佳，與其他角度之性能差異最大約
為 30％。當傾斜角度高過 30 度後，工作液體不易佈滿測試表面，因此降低熱傳性能。
而鰭片表面因表面結構可以液體均勻的分佈在表面上，因此不易受傾斜角度的影響。所
有測試表面之熱傳性能皆隨著流量增加而提昇。 
4. 採用 0.24mm 之滴淋孔徑進行滴淋傾斜角度與增強表面之實驗結果顯示，鰭片間之渠道
寬度應大於 0.24mm，以便滴淋液柱沖擊至鰭片根部，促使測試表面增加對流效應。在
鰭片高度方面，雖較長的鰭片高度可因表面積增加而延遲臨界熱通量，但過長的鰭片高
度，也會抑制滴淋液柱沖擊至鰭片根部的流速，進而降低滴淋熱傳性能。 
5. 在滴淋系統中，九個滴淋孔對槽道表面的影響比五孔大，當滴淋孔越多，直接受到液柱
衝擊的流道也就越多，增加平均熱傳性能。滴淋孔分佈越平均，熱傳性能越佳。 
6. P-0.2-0.8 表面在九個滴淋孔 150 ml/min 下之平均熱阻為 0.161 K/W，其熱源面積及流體
接觸面積均為 12×12 mm2，如將其流體接觸面積改為與晶片大小相同，便可再降低其熱
阻值；故 P-0.2-0.8 表面之滴淋蒸發散熱器適合應用於實際電腦散熱。 
7. 本計畫依各分項研究之結果，完成滴淋散熱模組原型機之設計，並與市售之多渠道晶片
散熱器進行比較。當以水為工作流體時，巿售晶片散熱器所使用的流量是 Prototype 的
9 倍，但熱阻卻是 Prototype 的 3~5 倍，表示滴淋冷卻比多渠道對流之熱傳效果好。此滴
淋散熱模組原型機以 FC-72 為工作流體時的熱阻明顯大於水，但在 80~140W 加熱量之
熱阻值約 0.2 K/W，已達到可實際應用之散熱水準，且優於市售水冷散熱器。雖然 FC-72
應用於噴淋冷卻方式中效果不比水好，但考慮 FC72 之非導電性，故筆者認為 FC-72 可
實際應用於電子散熱中。 
 
七、計畫成果自評

EXPERIMENTAL SETUP 
 
Apparatus 
The internal convection test system is shown in Figure 1.  
The working fluid, which was driven by a variable speed gear 
pump, passed through a flow meter and a pre-heater before 
entering the test section.  Then, the working fluid entered a 
plate-type heat exchanger, which was cooled by coolant from a 
constant temperature bath.  For the tests of FC-72, the fluid 
evaporated in the test section, and the plate type heat exchanger 
served as a condenser.  A pressure transducer and two 
thermocouples were installed near the inlet of the test section.  
The working fluid inlet temperature was maintained by the 
adjustment of the pre-heater heat input and the coolant flow 
rate and temperature of the plate type heat exchanger. An 
accumulator collected the cooled water or condensed FC-72 
liquid from the plate type heat exchanger. The liquid in the 
accumulator passed through a sight glass and flows back to the 
gear pump.  Care was taken to ensure no vapor bubble 
entering the pump by inspecting through the sight glass 
between the accumulator and the pump. 
The top view of the test section is shown in Figure 2.  
The channel is 20 mm wide and 2 mm high.  The total length 
of the test channel is 113 mm, with a heated length of 25mm, 
shown by the shaded region on Figure 2.  The bottom and the 
two sides is made of a piece of copper plate.  The top cover of 
the test section is made of Plexiglas for flow distribution 
observations.  A high speed camera system, Redlake 2000C, 
whose maximum resolution = 1280×1024 and maximum frame 
rate = 2000 frame/sec, was used in the present visual 
observation study.   The test surface was made on a 2.0 mm 
thick copper plate.  The back side of the test surface was 
soldered on a copper heating block.  The copper block having 
test surface was fixed on the back of the test section.  For the 
purpose of preventing leakage, a 2.0 mm thick rubber gasket 
was inserted between the heating block and the test section.  
As shown on Figure 3, two cartridge heaters were inserted into 
the bottom of the heating block, and three 0.5 mm thick 
stainless-steel sheathed copper-constantan (T-type) 
thermocouples were inserted into the block for measurement of 
temperature variation at two locations under the test surface.  
The test surface temperatures near the flow inlet and outlet 
(Tw,in and Tw,out) were extrapolated by the three temperatures 
(X1~X3) at each of the two locations.  The distance between 
two thermocouples is 5mm m (0.1968 inch), and the 
thermocouple closest to the test surface is 3.7 mm from the 
surface.  Thermal transfer compound was applied on the 
thermocouples and the cartridge heaters to ensure good thermal 
contact with the copper block.

ABSTRACT 
This study investigates a new enhanced boiling surface, 
which is made by wrapping wire mesh on finned tubes.  Pool 
boiling performance of the new enhanced tubes has been tested 
in Refrigerant-134a at 5, 10, 20, and 26.67oC saturation 
temperatures.  Brass or copper mesh of 80, 100, or 120 
meshes per inch was wrapped on finned tubes of 42 or 60 FPI 
(fins per inch).  The fin heights were either 0.2 mm or 0.4 
mm.  The test results show that the mesh covered fin tubes 
significantly enhanced the boiling performance by forming 
many pores of proper sizes on the surface and sustaining vapor 
in the tunnels formed by the mesh and fins.  The preferred 
mesh size decreases with decreasing heat flux.  The mesh 
covered on 60FPI fin tube having 0.4 mm fin height and 100 
mesh per inch yields the best boiling performance. It enhances 
the boiling heat transfer coefficient by 7~8 folds at 5oC as 
compared with the smooth tube.

1. INTRODUCTION 
Boiling is a very efficient and important heat transfer mode 
on many applications in refrigeration, processing and cooling 
of electronic devices.  The boiling heat transfer efficiency can 
be improved by using enhanced boiling surfaces.  Webb [1], 
Webb and Kim[2] and Thome [3] had surveyed the boiling 
enhancement techniques.  The “structured surface” and 
“porous surface” are the most important types of boiling 
enhanced surfaces.  The structured surface was manufactured 
by cold metal working to form reentrant nucleation sites and 
inter-connected channels on the surface.  All refrigeration 
“water chillers” presently use such enhanced tubes.  Webb and 
Pais [4] tested four commercial boiling enhanced tubes in R-11, 
R-12, R-123, and R-134a at 4.4oC and 26.7oC.  Chien and 
Webb [5, 6] designed an enhanced boiling surface by piercing 
pores on a thin copper sheet, which was soldered on fin tips of 
a low-fin-tube.  They performed systematic tests of geometric 
parameters including surface pore diameter/pitch, subsurface 
tunnel height, tunnel width, and tunnel pitch in R-123, R-134a 
and R-22 at 26.7oC.   Figure 1 is a composite diagram 
showing the separate and combined effects of pore diameter 
(dp) and pore pitch (Pp) for the 1378 fins/m tube having 0.9 mm 
fin height.  This figure is based on detailed observations in 
Chien and Webb [5, 6].  Data were obtained only for the dp = 
0.23 mm, Pp = 1.5 and 3.0 mm cases.  The dashed lines are the 
authors’ qualitative estimates of the effect of pore pitch.  On 
Figure 1, the solid lines are for fixed pore pitch (Pp = 1.5 mm) 
with increasing pore diameter (dp).  The dashed lines show the 
effect of pore pitch (0.75 ≤ Pp ≤ 4.0 mm) for fixed pore 
diameter (dp = 0.23 mm).  The salient features shown on 
Figure 1 are: 
1. As shown by the solid curves for fixed Pp, the DHF 
(Dryout Heat Flux) increases as the pore diameter 
increases.  A lower DHF occurs for the smaller pore 
diameters, because they cannot supply enough liquid 
to the tunnel at high heat flux. 
2. For fixed Pp and heat fluxes less than the DHF (e.g., 
50% of the DHF), the heat transfer coefficient 
increases as dp increases, except for the largest dp 
(0.28 mm).  The lower heat transfer coefficient for 
the largest dp occurs because too much liquid is 
supplied to the tunnels, and floods the intermittent 
regions.  Hence, there is an optimum pore diameter 
for operation at low-to-moderate heat flux. 
3. For fixed dp (0.23 mm), the DHF decreases as the Pp 
increases.  This is because the tunnel needs greater 
liquid supply at the higher heat flux.  However, the 
more widely spaced pores are unable to supply 
sufficient liquid.

4. EXPERIMENTAL RESULTS 
4.1 Comparisons of the Smooth Tube Data  
Figure 4 shows boiling heat transfer coefficients of the 
smooth tube at 5oC and 26.7oC saturation temperature in the 
present tests.  The smooth tube data of Webb and Pais [4] are 
also shown in Fig. 4 for comparison.  The present data at 5oC 
are very similar to the data of Webb and Pais [4] at 4.4oC.  At 
26.7oC, the heat trasnfer coefficient of the Webb and Pais[4] is 
similar to the present data at q” < 30 kW/m2, but slightly 
greater than the present data at higher heat fluxes.  Figure 5 
shows the boiling curves of the smooth tubes at top, center and 
bottom locations at 5oC, 10oC and 26.7oC saturation 
temperatures.  Only one tube is heated for each curve.  As 
shown in Fig. 5, the boiling heat transfer curves for the three 
locations are almost identical at each saturation temperature. 
4.1 Effect of Mesh Size  
Figure 6 shows that boiling heat transfer curves at 20oC of 
the smooth tube, 42 FPI tube, and three mesh-covered-fin tubes 
having #80 copper mesh, #100 brass mesh, or #120 brass mesh.  
All the three mesh-covered-fin tubes yielded higher boiling 
heat transfer coefficients than the 42 FPI fin-tube or the smooth 
tube.  The 42 FPI_100 tube having #100 brass mesh yields the 
highest heat transfer coefficients in every heat flux in the tests.  
The 42 FPI_80c has the second best performance in q＂ > 24 
kW/m2, yet the 42 FPI_120b tube performs better than the 42 
FPI_80c tube at q＂ < 24 kW/m2.  These results imply that the 
#100 mesh provides the best combination of the pore size and 
pore pitch.  The pore of the #80 mesh is too big to prevent 
flooding in the tunnel at low heat flux, while the #120 mesh is 
too small to prevent dry-out.  Similar results are found in the 
results of 10oC and 5oC as shown in Figure 7 and 8, 
respectively.  However, the performance of the 42FPI_120b 
performed better at a lower temperature.  At shown by the 5oC 
data in Figure 8, the 42FPI_120b even yielded a greater heat 
transfer coefficient than the 42FPI_100b at q＂ < 15 kW/m2.  
This indicates that the optimum pore size of boiling enhanced 
surface decreases as the saturation temperature decreases. 
The 42FPI_100b is also tested at 26.7oC and compared 
with the data of Turbo-B™ tested by Webb and Pais [4] as 
shown in Fig. 9.  The Turbo-B™ tube is a commercial tube 
having boiling enhanced structures with 42 fins/inch fin 
density.  As shown in Fig. 9, the present 42FPI_100b tube 
yields greater boiling heat transfer coefficient than the Turbo-
B™ tube at q＂ < 30 kW/m2.  The Turbo-B™ tube has better 
boiling performance at high heat fluxes.  Compared with the 
commercial structured tubes, the mesh covered fin tube has the 
advantage of flexibility in setting pore sizes for the designed 
heat flux.  The smaller pores (#120 mesh) are preferred at low 
heat flux, yet the larger pores (#80~#100 mesh) are preferred at 
high heat flux.

Several researches have been reported on boiling of pin-fins,
manufactured by various methods, including etching and electric
discharge machining. Yu and Lu [6] experimentally
investigated the effect of the pin-fin geometry parameters (fin
spacing and fin length) on boiling performance. The test surfaces
were manufactured on a copper block with a base area of 10
mm×10 mm with three fin spacing (0.5 mm, 1.0 mm and 2.0mm)
and four fin lengths (0.5 mm, 1.0 mm, 2.0 mm and 4.0 mm). The
results also showed that overall heat transfer coefficient decayed
rapidly as the fin spacing decreased or the fin length increased.
The maximum value of CHF on the base area was 9.8 ×105
W/m2 for the test surface with a 0.5 mm fin spacing and a 4.0
mm fin length, which has a several times greater than that of the
plain surface.
Wei and Honda [7] studied the effects of the height and
thickness of square micro-pin-fin on boiling heat transfer from
silicon chips immersed in a pool of degassed or gas-dissolved
FC-72. Six kinds of micro-pin-fins with the dimensions of 30 ×
60, 30 × 120, 30 × 200, 50 × 60, 50 × 200 and 50 × 270 μm2
(thickness, t · height, h) were fabricated on the surface of a
square silicon chip with the dimensions of 10 × 10 × 0.5 mm3 by
using the dry etching technique. The chip with 270μm fin height
and 50μm thickness yielded the maximum value of allowable
heat flux ( 84.5 W/cm2), which was 4.2 times as large as that
for the smooth chipat ΔTsub = 45 K.
This work seeks to find a high boiling performance pin-finned
surface in FC-72, and to investigate the effect of surface
inclination on pin fins. The fin width (= 200 μm) of the present
work is greater than that of Wei and Honda [7], yet smaller than
Yu and Lu’s study [6].
EXPERIMENTAL SETUP
Apparatus
A schematic of the test facility is shown in Fig. 1. The boiling
test vessel was made from a rectangular stainless steel block
having a 120 mm× 120 mm front area and 90 mm in depth. The
boiling surface plate was located on the middle of boiling test
vessel and connected a shaft of 8 mm diameter to control the
inclination angle of heating plate. A reflux condenser located
above the test surface. The condenser cooling water temperature
was maintained by a constant temperature bath. The condenser
cooling capacity was controlled by the water flow rate and the
temperature in the constant temperature bath. The temperature of
the boiling test chamber was measured by three 0.5 mm sheathed
thermocouples, as shown on Fig. 1. The saturation temperature
(Tsat) was taken as the average temperature of Tsat1, Tsat2 and
Tsat3. During the tests, the saturation temperature of the test
section was kept at 50 oC. A cartridge heater was located in the
bottom of the chamber to maintain the bulk temperature. One
pressure transducer fixed in the side of vessel was used to
measure system pressure.
The heating power is controlled by a DC power supply. The
voltages across the heater and the current values were recorded
by the data acquisition system, and the power input was
calculated subsequently. For each value of the power input, the
steady state was assumed to be reached when the difference
between the temperatures measured by the two thermocouples
embedded in the test surface was less than 0.2 K during a period
of 5 minutes. In the steady state, the averaged temperature
gradient between the surface and bulk liquid and the heat input
were determined and recorded by the data acquisition system.
The details of the heating module are shown in Fig. 2. The
test surface was made on a 10×10×2 mm3 copper plate, heated
by a 10×10 mm2 film heater from the bottom. The copper plate
and the film heater are fixed with a bakelite frame insulator and
a Teflon (k≒0.35 W/mK) substrate to prevent side heat loss as
shown in Fig. 2. All test surfaces used herein were manufactured
from high-purity oxygen-free copper. The base temperatures
were measured using two T-Type thermocouples which were
inserted in two holes from one side the copper block. The center
of the hole is 1.0 mm underneath the test surface. The distance
between the tips of the two thermocouples is 3.3 mm.
Scope of the tests
The parameters in the present boiling tests are listed in Table
1. FC-72 was tested at 50oC saturation temperature on a plain
surface, straight fin surface and pin-finned surfaces. The test
fluid, FC-72, is a highly wetting dielectric perfluorocarbon has
been determined to be a good candidate fluid for immersion
cooling applications because it is chemically stable, dielectric,
and has a relatively low boiling point (Tsat= 56
oC at atmospheric
pressure). The boiling inclination angles are: 0o, 45o and 90o.
The average surface roughness of the plain surface is 0.25μm. 
The designed pin-fins size is 0.2 mm × 0.2 mm × 0.5 mm (width
× thickness × height) in 0.4 mm pitch. The actual size of the pin-
fins is measured from the top and side view photos of the pin-
fins surface as shown on Fig. 3. The uncertainty of the fin size,
resulted from the electric discharge wire cutting machining
process is within1%.

Effect of pin-fins
The boiling curves of the three surfaces are compared in Fig.
9. With 10K superheat, the straight fin surface(C-0.2-0.5) and
pin-finned surface (P-0.2-0.5) enhanced the boiling performance
by approximately 6.7 and 7.5 folds, respectively. The total
surface area of these two surfaces (C-0.2-0.5 and P-0.2-0.5) is
similar (approximately 3.5 times of the projected area). The
boiling performance of the pin-fin surface is slightly better than
straight fin surface. The better nucleate boiling performance of
the pin fin is attributed to the increased nucleation sites in the
corners of pin-fins. FC-72 Boiling data of several pin fin
surfaces and a porous surface are compared in Fig. 10. The
sources [6, 7, 8, 9 and 10] and the geometric parameters of the
data in Fig. 10 are compared in table 2. Generally, the boiling
performance of the pin-finned surface increases with increasing
(fin height/fin width) ratio. This trend is in agreement with the
finding of Yu and Lu [6], who suggested that the boiling heat
transfer enhancement of the pin fins are mainly attributed to the
increased total surface area. The P-0.2-0.5 surface in the present
surface yields the best performance among the pin-fins because
of its high (fin height/fin width) ratio. However, the
enhancement ratio (7.5) of the P-0.2-0.5 is greater than the
surface area increase ratio (3.5). Therefore, the proper fin and
gap size is also an important factor to yield high boiling
performance for pin fins. Its boiling curve is similar to that of
the porous surface in El-Genk [9]. However, its critical heat flux
is smaller than that of the pin fin surface in Rainey and You [8].
Bubble growth mechanisms for straight fin surface
Boiling phenomena are recorded at 800 frames per second by
a high speed video system in the present tests. The observations
showed that the bubble size depends on surface geometries. The
typical departure bubble sizes on the plain surface in Fig. 11 are
much greater than those on the finned surface in Fig. 12 at the
same heat flux. Fig. 12 shows the sequence of boiling
phenomena of FC-72 on the horizontal finned surface at ΔTws =
6.07 K and q” = 2.94 W/cm2. The arrow in each figure indicates
one of the active bubble sites. At time τ= 0 ms, a bubble leaves
the site on the surface. The bubble moves upward thereby
increasing its volume. At τ= 3.75 ms, a small bubble emerges
from the gap between adjacent fins. The bubble continues to
grow on the surface moving slowly upward. At τ= 6.25 ms, the
second bubble detaches the site and the next cycle of bubble
growth starts. The average period of bubble emission from the
site is about 8.75 ms.
The bubble departure diameter of the finned surface (typically
0.2~0.5 mm) is smaller than that of the plain surface (0.5~1.0
mm). The mechanism of bubble growth for finned surface is
different from that of a plain surface because that the liquid is
evaporated inside the tunnel for finned surface, while
evaporation occurs on the micro-layer for the plain surface.
Liquid inertia due to the bubble expansion is important during
the growth period for the small bubbles on the finned surfaces.
The bubble size is expected to be influenced by the size of the
pin fins and the gaps between fins. As compared in Fig. 10, the
P-0.2-0.5 surface, having 0.2 mm fin gap, yields the best boiling
performance at 10~25 W/cm2 among the pin-fins of different
sizes. The pin fins of 0.32 mm gap in Rasmaswamy [10] yields
the best performance at q < 10 W/cm2. The fin width and gap
width are between 0.2 and 0.32 mm in these two studies. The
width of the gap is similar to the bubble departure size observed
in the present work. The convective flow induced by the bubbles
