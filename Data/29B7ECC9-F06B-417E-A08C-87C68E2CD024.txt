In this paper, we consider the identical parallel machines problem with the objective to minimize 
makespan and include the dynamic arrivals of jobs and the sequence-dependent setup time. We 
apply support vector machine (SVM) and neural network to estimate the rescheduling criterion and 
the parameter used in the cost function of the modified parallel insertion algorithms developed by 
Hsiao [4] and Tsai [12]. Besides, the SVM and the neural network are also adopted to make the 
algorithm selection decision based on the features of problems. In such dynamic environments, we 
develop an idle preventing mechanism to reduce the idle time of machines. We also adopts 
rescheduling criterion to regenerate the schedule of parallel machines according to the number of 
jobs in the waiting buffer.  
Section 2 describes the proposed approach to solve the dynamic parallel machine problem with 
minimizing makespan, in which some critical decisions are made by SVM or neural network to 
facilitate the execution of scheduling algorithms. Section 3 presents the modified parallel insertion 
algorithms, and then designs the reactive mechanisms such as the rescheduling policy and the idle 
preventing mechanism to deal with the dynamic events. The problem design and computational 
testing are presented in section 4. Finally, we give the conclusions. 
2 PROPOSED APPROACH 
Hsiao [4] and Tsai [12] proposed parallel insertion algorithms for solving the parallel-machine with 
minimizing the total workload and minimizing the makespan. However, for the problems with 
various characteristics, they still set the same parameters, which are used in the cost function of 
algorithms. Considering the set of parameters may relate to problem features, we extend the 
modified parallel insertion algorithms proposed by Hsiao [4] and Tsai [12] with support vector 
machine (SVM) and neural network to determine the appropriate parameters of cost function and 
the rescheduling criterion used in the considered algorithms, then apply the algorithm selecting 
decision to choose an appropriate algorithm for a problem with some specific features. The 
appropriate parameters used in the modified parallel insertion algorithm may vary according to the 
features of the problem to be solved. Therefore, the problem features will be defined as follows.

3 THE MODIFIED PARALLEL INSERTION ALGORITHMS 
3.1 Parallel Insertion Algorithms 
Parallel insertion algorithm (PIA) is looking for a best position with the minimum insertion cost and 
applies the notion of regret to decide the job first to be processed. It computes the insertion costs of 
every job for any permitted inserted position of all machines and chooses the minimal value as C1. 
The insertion cost can be defined as the additional setup time which indicates to subtract the setup 
time before inserting the job from the setup time after inserting the job and be denoted as S∆ . The 
regret value, C2, indicates the loss and affections in the future if the job is not processed 
immediately, and therefore the job with maximal value of C2 will be processed first. The process of 
the PIA is described ad below. 
Step 1. Select K jobs with first K largest initial setup time for K parallel machines. 
Step 2. If the number of arriving jobs achieve to the condition of rescheduling, then go to Step 
3. 
Step 3. For each unscheduled job, find its best allowable insertion position by computing C1. 
Step 4. Calculate the regret value C2 for each unscheduled job. Select the job with max C2 and 
insert it to the optimal position of machine got by C1 without violating the machine 
capacity and its due date restriction. 
Step 5.  Repeat Step 3 and Step 4 until all unprocessed jobs are scheduled. 
Step 6.  If all jobs are scheduled, then stop. Otherwise, go to Step 2. 
The PIA is designed to reduce the setup time, but it does not consider some important influences, 
such as due date. Hsiao [4] and Tsai [12] developed four modified parallel insertion algorithms to 
make the PIA more efficient and will be described in the following paragraphs. 
Parallel Insertion with New Initial Criteria (PIA-I) 
PIA-I initially selects the product type containing maximal number of jobs and picks the job of the 
same type and with minimal latest starting time as the initial job to be processed on each machine. 
Once the job of a specific product type is selected for a specific machine, other jobs of the same 
product type can not be selected as the initial job processed on other machines. After initializing K 
jobs, the following steps of PIA-I are the same as PIA. 
The job-type feature, JR, includes three levels, 15, 30, and 60; the setup time severity feature, ST, 
includes three levels, 1, 2, and 4; the setup time range feature, SR, includes three levels, 0.5, 1, and 
2; and finally the tightness feature, T, includes three levels and describe as below. 
We divide the due dates of jobs into three levels, which are 20, 30 and 40 hours. If the tightness 
feature, T, is 1975, which indicates there are 30 jobs assigned for 20 hours, 60 jobs assigned for 30 
hours, and 90 jobs assigned for 40 hours. If the feature T is 1775, which indicates there are 60 jobs 
assigned for 20, 30, and 40 hours respectively. If the feature T is 1708, which indicates there are 70 
jobs assigned for 20 hours, 60 jobs assigned for 30 hours, and 50 jobs assigned for 40 hours. The 
tightness feature gets tighter as the values of T decreasing.  
Four modified parallel insertion algorithms are applied to solve these problems respectively. The 
rescheduling criterion is set as 10 and 20 respectively, which indicates the job sequences will be 
rescheduled when the number of jobs in the unscheduled buffer reaches the rescheduling criterion. 
Parameters α and β using in the considered algorithms, PIA-II, PIA-III and PIA-IV, are divided into 
five levels, 0.1, 0.3, 0.5, 0.7, and 0.9. For all problems corresponding to all combinations from the 
different rescheduling criteria and the different levels of α or β, each considered algorithm is 
applied to find the best rescheduling criterion and the best value for α or β.
