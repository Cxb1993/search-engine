2. Experimental 
PBI synthesis and preparation of PBI-functionalized MWCNTs 
About 15 g of 3,3'-diaminobenzidine (DAB) and 250 g of polyphosphoric acid (PPA) were added into a 1 L 
round bottom flask equipped with magnetic stirrer. The solution was heated to 140°C under a nitrogen stream 
to dissolve DAB in PAA. 11.63 g of isophthalic acid (IPA) was then added and the temperature was raised to 
200°C. After 15 h, the viscous solution was poured into a large amount of cold water. The fibers formed were 
kept in water for at least 3 days. The excess acid was neutralized with a sodium bicarbonate aqueous solution 
(1 M). The polymer was then collected from filtration, washed thoroughly with water and methanol, and then 
dried under vacuum at 130°C overnight [3]. 
 
PBI and PBI/CNT composite preparation 
Appropriate amount of PBI polymer was dissolved in 22 g of dimethylacetamide (DMAc) and mixed for 3 h 
to form a homogeneous polymer solution. This solution was poured into a petri dish with a diameter of 9 cm 
and dried in a vacuum oven at 120°C for 8 h. The dry film was peeled off from the petri dish and soaked in 
500 mL of methanol solvent at 60°C. The methanol solvent was changed every 3 h for a total of 12 h so that 
the methanol would place the residual DMAc in the film. The film was then dried at 200°C for 4 days.  
 
The PBI-functionalized CNT [3] was suspended in 5 g of DMAC under ultrasonication at room temperature 
for 8 h. The PBI solution in 17 g of DMAc was prepared according to the aforementioned procedure. The 
CNT suspension and PBI solution were mixed for additional 2 h under ultrasonication and poured into a petri 
dish. The mixture was dried; the film was peeled off, soaked in methanol, and dried again as the procedure 
similar for the PBI preparation. The resulting PBI composites consisted of 0.05% (weight basis) of 
PBI-functionalized CNT, unless stated otherwise. The thickness of the dry composite membrane was 40-50 
μm. The PBI and PBI/CNT composites were immersed in 6 M KOH solution for 24 h. These KOH-doped PBI 
and PBI composite were referred as PBI/KOH and PBI/CNT/KOH.

4. Conclusion 
The alkali-doped PBI is a promising electrolyte for alkaline fuel cell due to the thermal stability and excellent 
mechanical strength of the polymer matrix. Incorporation of small amount of PBI-functionalized carbon 
nanotubes significantly improves the fuel cell performance. The gas diffusion electrode prepared without 
hydrophobic layer is beneficial and results in a higher cell power density than the commercial E-tek products. 
The direct alkaline methanol fuel cell reaches a peak power density of 104 mW/cm2 when the cell was fed 
with 2 M methanol in 6 M KOH at 90C and equipped with PBI/0.05%CNT.
