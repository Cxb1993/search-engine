2中文摘要
本研究的目的是希望能藉由應力波法及透地雷達法檢測混凝土於火害後的劣化深度，混凝
土受損深度是否超過鋼筋層面對鋼筋握裹力評估有重要的影響，下年度將藉本年度研究成果
建立完整之非破壞檢測預估劣化深度及鋼筋握裹力關係性。本研究之試體分為兩種，一為大
小 40*40*15cm 之小型混凝土試體，受到火害溫度為 300 到 800℃；另一為大小為
240×130×15cm3 之大型混凝土板，火害溫度為 600℃。
在應力波檢測方面，檢測方法是於混凝土表面以鋼珠做敲擊源敲擊，將接收器設置於 20cm
之距離，以所得到之頻譜中的主要反應頻率來評估混凝土的劣化程度。先以小型混凝土試體
利用應力波法量測尖峰頻率後再鑽心，劣化深度是由鑽心試體切片之動態彈性模數判定，將
所得之頻率與劣化深度的關係代至大型混凝土板驗證。由研究結果顯示，劣化深度低於 2cm
時頻譜仍由試塊整體共振頻率控制，因此較無法判斷劣化深度，然而在劣化深度大於 2cm 時，
最低頻率越低劣化深度越深，且呈線性關係。
在雷達波檢測方面，本研究是以雷達天線置於試體表面上方 30cm 處量測，接收電磁波由
混凝土反射訊號之前 1 又 1/2 個振幅所圍成面積來評估混凝土品質，並以同配比劣化與常溫
之面積對數比例(單位為 dB)定義衰減指數(AI)。研究結果顯示 AI 與火害溫度有正向的關連性
的關係，普通混凝土在表面無嚴重剝離的狀況下，當 AI 介於 0-0.5 間可評估火害溫度在 300°C
或以下，AI 介於 0.5-1.0 間，火害溫度可能在 400-600°C，AI 在 1.0 以上火害溫度可達 800°C。
而 AI 與混凝土表面彈性模數也有正比的關係，因此此法可用來評估火場各區域所經歷之火害
溫度。
關鍵詞：應力波、雷達波、火害、混凝土。
Abstract
The aim of this study is to develop techniques using stress wave method and short-pulse radar
to investigate the depth of deteriorated concrete after exposing to high-temperature. For assessing
bond condition of steel reinforcement in structural concrete, the accurate estimation of the depth of
deterioration is very important. The depth of deterioration evaluated by present study will be
applied to assessment of loss of bond of reinforcement in the project conducted next year. Two
types of specimens were investigated. One is the small specimens with dimensions 40*40*15 cm3.
These specimens were heated with the temperatures 300 to 800℃. The other is a large concrete
plate with the size 240*130*15 cm3. The specimen is heat by fire with temperature 600℃.
For stress wave method, a steel ball is used as the impactor and the frequency of the principal
response obtained by a receiver 20 cm away is used for evaluation. The principal frequencies for the
small specimens were compared to the depth of deterioration examed by drilled core. The core
specimens were sliced into disks and the depth of the deterioration was evaluated by the dynamic
elastic modulus of the disks. The results obtained from the small specimens were used to evaluate
the depth of deterioration of the big plate. The test results showed the depth of deterioration can not
be evaluated while it is less than 2 cm. A linear relationship can be found between the lowest peak
frequency and the depth of deterioration while the depth is larger than 2 cm.
For radar wave method, the signals corresponding to the reflections from the concrete surface
transmitted by an antenna 300 mm above were used for analysis. The area enveloped by the
response reflected from the concrete surface in time domain was calculated. The attenuation index
(AI), with dB as the unit, was obtained from the enveloped areas after and before high-temperature
exposure. The AI was compared to the Elastic Modulus of concrete near damaged surface obtained

英文：
The aim of this study is to develop techniques using stress wave
method and short-pulse radar to investigate the depth of deteriorated
concrete after exposing to high-temperature.
For stress wave method, a steel ball is used as the impactor and
the frequency of the principal response obtained by a receiver 20 cm
away is used for evaluation. The test results showed the depth of
deterioration can not be evaluated while it is less than 2 cm. A linear
relationship can be found between the lowest peak frequency and the
depth of deterioration while the depth is larger than 2 cm.
For radar wave method, the signals corresponding to the
reflections from the concrete surface transmitted by an antenna 300 mm
above were used for analysis. The area enveloped by the response
reflected from the concrete surface in time domain was calculated. The
attenuation index (AI), with dB as the unit, was obtained from the
enveloped areas after and before high-temperature exposure. The AI
was compared to the Elastic Modulus of concrete near damaged surface
obtained from drilling core. Results show AI can be used as the
indicators on estimating the concrete having literally no damage
(temperature below 300°C), minor damages (between 400 and 600°C),
or major damages (above 800°C). Linear relation was found between
AI and Elastic Modulus on the concrete surface.
