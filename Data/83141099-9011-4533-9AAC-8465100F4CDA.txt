可將氫氣的化學能轉化成電能，且可降低
現行石化燃料所產生的污染，此外，燃料
電池還有高效率（40~50 %)、低噪音等優
點。 
從熱力學的觀點來看，乙醇水蒸氣重
組及直接分解反應均為吸熱反應，需要在
較高的操作溫度下進行，所以必須提供額
外的熱源。部份氧化反應為放熱反應，唯
氫氣產率比水蒸氣重組反應低，且散熱問
題不易處理。因此，我們採用具部份氧化
與水蒸氣重組功能的自熱化蒸氣重組反
應，它具有不錯的氫氣產率，且可於較低
的溫度下進行。 
本實驗選擇 MDC-3 商用觸媒進行反
應，並以含浸法製備 Cu-Zn/CeO2 及
Cu-Zn/Al2O3 觸媒，根據不同的實驗條件

度、反應壓力、掃流氣體流量與通氧比率
等條件對轉化率的影響。本實驗氣相產物
中亦看到少許的一氧化碳的生成，此結果
初步證明二氧化碳的生成並非全然靠水
氣轉移反應而來，反應物生成量與觸媒反
應的活性狀況都將受到反應條件的影
響，這些結果將一一探討。 
三、實驗方法 
(一) 觸媒的製備-含浸法 
本實驗以含浸法（impregnation）作為
金屬植入的方法，添加促進劑於銅觸媒，
製成雙金屬(Cu-Zn)觸媒。為配合銅的來源
為硝酸銅，促進劑的來源亦使用硝酸化合
物(硝酸鋅)。銅觸媒或載體在使用前，都
先放入烘箱以120℃烘乾12 小時以上。 
含浸法：精秤所需的硝酸銅、硝酸鋅溶於
適量的去離子水中，至入超音波震盪器 30
分鐘，使其完全溶解，即完成前趨溶液之
配製，後將前趨溶液加入載體當中，並微
微加熱並攪拌，將水蒸乾。放入烘箱 120℃
烘乾 12 小時以上。烘乾後冷卻再將其粉
末研磨均勻，放入高溫爐煅燒以 1℃/min 
的速率昇到適當的溫度，維持定溫，進行
鍛燒數小時。冷卻後再次將粉末研磨均
勻，即得所要的雙金屬觸媒。 
 
(二) 鈀銀合金膜管之氫氣/氮氣滲透選擇
率 
本實驗使用之鈀銀合金膜管是以無
電鍍法製備的支撐式鈀銀合金膜管，利用
H2 / N2 氣體測試，測試後膜管內通過之氫
氣濃度即為此膜管對氫氣之滲透選擇
率，氫氣對氮氣的滲透選擇率越高，代表
鈀膜管對氫氣的分離效果越好，分離出的
氫氣純度越高。而氫氣滲透通量的大小，
則代表單位面積鈀銀膜所能分離的氫氣
量。滲透通量越大，則高純度的氫氣產量
越大。

反應研究

Effect of Oxygen Addition on the Reaction Schemes of Ethanol Reforming in a 
Pd/Ag Membrane Reactor 
 
Ying-Chi Liu   Hsin-Fu Chang* 
 
Department of Chemical Engineering, Feng Chia University 
Taichung, Taiwan 407, R.O.C. 
* hfchang@fcu.edu.tw (H.-F. Chang) 
Abstract 
In this study, oxygen was cofed with ethanol and water into a palladium-silver alloy 
membrane reactor for the autothermal reforming of ethanol in order to collect high purity 
hydrogen on the permeation side. Pd/Ag membrane was prepared by the sequential electroless 
plating of palladium and silver on the porous stainless-steel tube, to reach a final film 
thickness of 20μm and a Pd/Ag weight ratio of 78/22.  
 The reformer was packed with the commercial zinc-copper catalyst MDC-3 for the steam 
reforming of ethanol with reaction temperatures ranged from 593 to 723 K and pressures 
between 2 to 9 atm. The reactant feed consisted of ethanol, water and oxygen, with a 
H2O/EtOH molar ratio of 2.2, O2/EtOH molar ratio of 0.3 to 0.4 and WHSV of 5h-1. It is 
found that the effect of oxygen addition to the ethanol steam reforming is dramatic. When 
O2/EtOH is 0.3 the overall reaction is endothermic, while O2/EtOH is 0.4 the reaction turns to 
be exothermic, and both of which exhibit a higher ethanol conversion ratio at higher 
temperatures and lower pressures; the ethanol conversion is higher at O2/EtOH =0.4 than at 
O2/EtOH =0.3. The selectivities and yields to CO and CO2 at O2/EtOH =0.4 are higher than at 
O2/EtOH =0.3. 
Key words: Sequential electroless plating; Pd-Ag/PSS membrane reactor; Auto-thermal 
reaction of ethanol   
Introduction 
Due to the exacerbated pollution problems on burning fossil fuels as energy source, people are 
interested in clean energy resource and hydrogen has become one of the most potential energy 
carriers [1]. Hydrogen is mostly obtained from steam reforming of methane and other 
hydrocarbons which inevitably produces carbon dioxide as side product and aggravates the 
green house effect. Ethanol, obtained commonly through the fermentation from agricultural 
products, can be reformed to produce hydrogen and maintain a CO2 ecological balance. In the 
last years, some attention has been devoted to investigate processes for the production of 
hydrogen and synthesis gas from bio-ethanol through catalytic steam reforming [2,3]. 
In this study, the steam reforming of ethanol was conducted in a Pd/Ag membrane reactor 
to produce high purity hydrogen. The effect of oxygen addition associated with ethanol and 
water on the performance of reforming reactions is thoroughly discussed.

Fig. 3 shows the dependences of hydrogen and nitrogen fluxes on temperature at various 
transmembrane pressure differences for Pd-Ag/PSS alloy membrane. The H2/N2 
permselectivity, which is defined asαH2/N2= (JH2/JN2), was about 253 at 623K and 10atm

Fig. 4 illustrates that when the pressure increases from 2 atm to 9 atm, the ethanol conversion 
decreases from 91~99 % to 76%~81%. In 623 K, ethanol conversion in steam reforming 
reaction is well low when low oxygen (O2/EtOH = 0.3) is fed into the reactor, indicating that 
the steam reforming reaction is not triggered at low temperature. The conversion increases 
remarkably when the O2/EtOH increases from 0.3 to 0.4, especially at high pressures. This 
can be attributed to the exothermity of oxidation reaction providing energy to steam reforming 
reaction. Stoichiometrically, when O2/EtOH is 0.3 the overall reaction is endothermic, while 
O2/EtOH is 0.4 the reaction turns to be exothermic, and both of which exhibit a higher ethanol 
conversion ratio at higher temperatures and lower pressures; the ethanol conversion is higher 
at O2/EtOH =0.4 than at O2/EtOH =0.3. Fig. 5 shows when O2/EtOH is 0.4 and at 723 K and 9 
atm, the highest hydrogen flux through the permeation side is 67.72 mole/m2*h. At different 
pressures the selectivities to CO and CO2 varying with the amount of oxygen added are 
depicted in Fig. 6. Both CO and CO2 selectivities increases with increasing temperature and 
the increasing amount of oxygen addition, which can be inferred that at high O2/EtOH the 
oxidation reaction becomes the dominant reaction.
