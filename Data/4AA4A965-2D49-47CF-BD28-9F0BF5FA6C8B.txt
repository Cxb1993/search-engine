 2
self-consolidating concrete (SCC) beams with low coarse aggregate 
content, and the third were high-workability concrete (HWC) beams. The 
size of specimens was 240 × 360 × 3500 mm . The parameters included 
the concrete strength, splice length and spacing of transerverse 
reinforcement. The results show that: 
1. The HWC specimens have better ductility than NC, but SCC 
spcimens have inferior ductility than NC. 
2. The HWC specimens have slightly higher bond strength than NC, 
while SCC specimens have less bond strength than NC. 
3. The HWC and SCC specimens have better crack control over NC 
specimens. 
4. The O. J. B. model becomes unconservative when stirrups are used in 
the development length.
