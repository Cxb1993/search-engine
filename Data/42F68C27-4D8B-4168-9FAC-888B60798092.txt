The Evaluation of Hand Exertion for Manual Lifting Tasks 
Lifting is a complex dynamic task involving not only the spine but also the upper and 
lower limbs. The object is basically handled during manual handling by one-handed or 
two-handed exertions and the weight of body and the object is supported by the lower 
extremities. When overexertion occurred at hands or upper extremities, musculoskeletal 
injuries may occur. Few literatures were found for the injuries of hands and lower extremities 
during materials handling. Two 3-component force transducers, EMG, and motion analysis 
were applied in Human Factors Lab to conduct the experiments for the changes of object 
characteristics and one-handed handling tasks for this 3-year research project. The study in 
first year focused on the weight distribution of object, on one-handed lifting in the second year, 
and on the dimension change of object in the third year. The hazard occurs during the change 
of object dimension and handle in lifting tasks and some suggestions are made to avoid or 
prevent the risks of hands and upper extremities. 
 
 
Keywords: Manual Materials Handling, Hand exertion, Biomechanics, EMG

二、研究方法 
2.1 受測者 
本研究目前共邀請 2 位健康男性參與實驗，此二受測者過去均無肌肉骼系統方面的疾病
與傷害。受測者平均年齡 26 歲、平均身高 174.9 公分、平均體重 71.5 公斤。受測者皆以右手
為慣用手。受測者於實驗開始進行前，研究人員針對受測者進行相關人體計測資料測量，並
向受測者說明本次實驗目的及實驗過程所使用的儀器設備，提供受測者進行實驗的相關資訊。 
2.2 實驗設備 
本研究使用的設備包含：三軸力荷重計（LSM-B-500NSA1, Kyowa）2 個、EMG 量測系
統、及實驗用壓克力箱底（長 45cm、寬 25cm，重量 4.98kg）。本實驗將荷重計裝置於實驗用
箱底上，並在荷重計上安裝握把（如圖 1 所示），用以量測受測者抬起箱底時手部之作用力。

四、討論 
本研究在實驗過程中利用荷重計所量測左右邊重量比率為 2：1 或 1：2 之物件時，其所
量測出之左右兩邊作用力的比率並非 2：1 或 1：2，而是約 2：3 或 3：2 左右，在 2 種抬舉
姿勢之間並無明顯的差別。基本上，抬舉的過程中手部各肌肉所施的力本來即不會完全一致，
因此與由荷重計所量測出之作用力值亦會不全然相同，而其結果的顯示也有可能是因為本研
究目前所收集的樣本數太少，其中也許會有若干條肌肉會隨荷重計所量測出之作用力呈現規
律的變化。 
當手臂外展角度較大時，三角肌的肌電訊號也會較強，亦即會施出較大的力。本研究發
現，當荷重計 Y 方向之正值較大時（荷重計之座標軸如圖 1 所示），三角肌之 MVC%也相對
較大，可推測三角肌應與荷重計 Y 軸方向的值有顯著相關，但因本研究目前樣本數少，尚未
能進行統計分析，待增加樣本後，可進一步證實。 
本研究發現，不管在任何型態下，肱橈肌之 MVC%最大值均係利用背舉式且負重位置為
前：後 2：1 時，其最小值亦均在利用腿舉式且負重位置為前：後 1：2 時，本研究推測當負
重位置離人體越時，對於肱橈肌的負擔越大，但 Brandon et al.[12]的研究中提到，當負荷最接
近身體時，上背直肌和下背直肌產生最高的 EMG 峰值，因此負荷重心位置該靠近或遠離身
體，值得進一步探討。 
在三角肌方面，其最大 MVC%均出現在利用腿舉式進行抬舉時，其原因可能在於當利用
腿舉式進行抬舉時，手臂外展角度會較背舉式時大，因此使得三角肌之施力變大。再者，斜

一、緣由與目的 
科技進步的今日，產業強調機械自動化，然而由於經濟成本及工作環境的考量，人工物
料搬運（manual materials handling, MMH）仍然無法完全避免[1-3]。搬運時由於用力不當、姿
勢不良或重複性等因素，易導致肌肉骨骼傷害發生。許多文獻曾針對人體背部[2, 4]、上肢[5]
及下肢[6]等部位肌肉骨骼傷害進行探討。 
抬舉是一項複雜的動態作業，需要包括背部、上肢及下肢共同完成[7]。在上肢部分，主
要係透過手部與物件的接觸，將物件進行移動。作業場所設計或物件握持形狀，是決定使用
雙手或單手抬舉的重要因素[8-10]。單手抬舉研究大多在於探討坐姿水平面抬舉[11-13]，雙手
及單手抬舉比較大多在於探討腰椎受力情形[9-10]，而手部施力則缺乏相關探討。以往針對手
部施力量測，最常使用肌電訊號作為施力情形收集方法 [7, 11, 14]，利用荷重計進行手部施力
量測與評估者相對地較少[15-17]。利用手部施力進行力學探討者，如江昇修[5, 16]、林久翔
等[3]。 
日常生活中以及工作場所內均可見到單手抬舉情形，以往針對單手抬舉研究，比較偏向
持續性以及背部受力情況，對於偶發性、垂直面抬舉及上肢肌群施力情形的研究仍屬少量。
因此，本研究主要在於收集受測者上肢肌電訊號，並利用三軸向荷重計，探討單手與雙手抬
舉時手部施力情形，並比較單手及雙手抬舉作業時，其手部肌群肌電訊號及受力情形。 
二、研究方法 
2.1 受測者 
本研究邀請 15 位年輕男性受測者參與實驗，平均年齡 23.9yr、平均身高 173.9cm、平均
體重 67.8kg，均無肌肉骨骼或心血管疾病史，右手均為其慣用手。單手抬舉時，以受測者右
手為實驗用手。受試者身體質量指數(Body mass index, BMI)介於 19.3~24.9 kg/m2 之間。 
2.2 實驗設備 
主要實驗設備包括：肌電訊號量測模組(Biopac system, Inc., MP150)、三軸力荷重計
（LSM-B-500NSA1, Kyowa）2 個及實驗用木製箱底 2 塊（長 45cm、寬 25cm、厚 1.7cm）。
本實驗將荷重計安裝於實驗用箱底上，並在荷重計上安裝握把（如圖 1 所示）。握把之間距離
為 37.0cm、高度為 17cm。握把之間距離設定為 37.0cm 係依據台灣地區人體計測資料庫手冊
[17]國內 50%ile 男性肩寬為 370.04mm。箱底選擇中密度纖維板材質，主要在於中密度纖維板
的均質性（homogeneity）較佳[18]。

中文摘要 
本研究利用肌電訊號量測模組及三軸力荷重計，收集 15 位男性受測者右手上肢肌群肌電
訊號、抬舉時產生之手部三軸作用力等資料。本研究實驗因子為物件箱長度的改變，物件箱
有三種，其左右握把間距分別為 18 公分、37 公分及 56 公分。利用實驗室開發的 Motion 
Processor 軟體，處理並計算各肌群 EMG 之均方根及%MVC；三軸作用力方面，則計算其合
力與峰值，觀察三軸作用力之趨勢。結果發現，抬舉時物件箱長度改變，對於肱橈肌之 EMG
具有顯著差異，當箱子長度增加時，肱橈肌施力降低。肱二頭肌、肱三頭肌、前三角肌及上
斜方肌之 EMG 均無顯著差異，但肱二頭肌 EMG 雖會因箱子長度而增加施力。抬舉時物件箱
長度改變對於三軸作用力之影響，無顯著差異。 
關鍵詞：人工物料搬運、物件尺寸、肌電訊號、手部作用力 
Abstract 
The EMG measurement module and load cell system were used to explore the biomechanical 
behavior of lifting tasks performed by 15 male subjects in the study. The measurements of EMG 
signal were obtained from 5 muscle groups of subjects’ dominant (right) hand and hand exertion 
was collected with load cell system. The dependent parameter in the experiment was the object 
dimensions with three kinds of box length of 18, 37 and 56cm. The root mean square and 
percentage of maximum voluntary contraction (MVC) of EMG signals and the resultant and peak 
values of hand exertion were calculated. The experimental result showed that the significant 
difference on brachioradials was obtained with the dimension changes of object lifted. The strength 
of brachioradials was decreased when the length of object increased. The muscle groups of biceps 
brachii, triceps brachii, anterior deltoid, and upper trapezius presented no significant difference with 
the object dimension change. However, the strength of biceps brachii increased with the increase of 
object length. The hand exertion showed no significant difference when object length changed. 
Keywords: Manual materials handling, Object dimension, EMG, Hand exertion

3.3 主觀知覺施力評量 
在執行完抬舉實驗後，受測者填寫主觀施力量表。本研究主觀施力量表計有 6 個等級，
由分數 0 為完全不用力至分數 5 為非常用力。 
在進行抬舉作業時，受測者對於前三角肌、肱二頭肌及肱橈機的主觀施力評估較其他二
肌群為大，其 RPE 平均值介於 2.07 至 2.61 之間；而斜方肌及肱三頭肌則相對較小，介於 1.23
至 1.69 之間。針對 RPE 進行變異數分析，探討不同實驗情境對主觀施力評估的影響，結果發

四、討論 
4.1 箱子尺寸對肌群 EMG 之影響 
本研究針對物件箱尺寸改變，量測抬舉作業時之 5 條肌群 EMG 進行變異數分析，結果
顯示除了肱橈肌之 EMG 有顯著差異之外，其他肌群均無顯著差異。本研究觀察抬舉影片時
發現，在抬舉握把間距為 18 公分的箱子時，由於物件箱長度（握把間距）小於肩膀寬度，因
此在開始抬舉時，肘關節呈現較大的屈曲（flexion）角度，手腕呈現較大尺偏（ulnar deviation），
在抬舉握把間距 18 公分箱子時，肱橈肌施力最大，隨著箱子長度（握把間距）增加，屈曲角
度減小，手腕尺偏亦減小，肱橈肌施力逐漸下降。本實驗為過肩抬舉，因此在抬至最高點向
終點位置（肩高）前進時，肘關節呈現伸展（extension）情況，但因箱子長度增加，力臂變
長，因此在相同物重下，握把間距為 56 公分的箱子相對會有較大力矩，使肱二頭肌向物件箱
重心方向施力尋求抬舉的穩定，因而會施出較大力量。 
 24
本研究主要目的在於利用肌電訊號系統、荷重計（load cells）及主觀知覺量表，探討搬運作
業時箱子握距長度改變，對於手部施力情況及上肢肌電訊號的變化。結果顯示，抬舉作業時
物件尺寸改變，對右手肱橈肌 EMG%MVC 有顯著影響，進行卸下作業時左手肱二頭肌之
EMG%MVC 有顯著差異。抬舉握距 56 公分箱子時，左手及右手上斜方肌施力均大於
50%MVC，卸下時肱二頭肌超過 50%MVC，因此不適合搬運作業。卸下握距 18 公分箱子時，
4 條肌群 EMG%MVC 均小於 40%MVC，為 3 種尺寸中較佳者。不論抬舉或卸下作業，物件
尺寸改變對三軸作用力均無顯著影響。依研究結果提出下列建議：(1)建議應避免物件過大及
過小，造成某部分肌肉施力不當；(2)各種抬舉型態，肱二頭肌、前三角肌及上斜方肌之 EMG
均在 30 至 50%MVC 之間，對該三條肌群易造成疲勞，因此未來可針對改善策略進一步研究；
(3)未來可進一步探討關節角度變化及其他搬運作業之變化。

This study evaluates the effects of glovebox glove, layer, and thickness on maximum voluntary contraction grip 
strength and forearm muscle activity for gripping exertion with maximal and sub-maximal efforts. Six volunteered male 
comprised the subject pool. Butyl, hypalon, and neoprene gloves in 0.015” and 0.03” thicknesses were selected for 
evaluation. Grip strength and muscles activity were measured using a transducer equipped hand dynamometer and an 
electromyography system. Based upon the observations made in this experiment, 0.015” inch butyl glove in single 
gloving setting should be used as selection considerations for glovebox works to minimize effects on maximum grip 
strength. In addition, 0.015” inch butyl glove in single or triple gloving setting should be used as selection 
considerations for glovebox works to minimize the forearm muscle activity. This study recommends 0.015” inch butyl 
glove in single gloving setting to provide the desired hand protection against ergonomic-related disorders in the 
glovebox environment involving gripping with maximal and sub-maximal efforts. 
 
Keywords: Glovebox, Glove, Muscle activity, Grip 
 
INTRODUCTION 
Work-related musculoskeletal disorders (WRMSDs) of upper extremities are among the most prevalent lost-time 
injuries and illnesses in almost every industry (BLS, 2007). Forearm muscles are common sites and sources for the 
development of upper extremity disorders even though the normal muscles function is essential in protecting ligaments 
and other tissues against injury or disorders (Nicolay et al., 2007). Reduced strength (weakness) of the musculature 
surrounding a joint also contributes to increased risk of injuries or disorders (Aagaard et al., 1998). In addition to 
muscle weakness, hand force has also been acknowledged by epidemiologic evidence as a mechanical risk factor for the 
development of upper extremity disorders (NIOSH, 1997). Gripping is one of the most commonly applied hand exertion 
to accomplish complex tasks for industrial jobs and has been implicated as a risk factor for several WRMSDs (Bao and

Apparatus 
Gloves and Gloving Conditions  
Butyl, hypalon and neoprene gloves in two different thicknesses (0.015” and 0.03”) that are commercially available for 
glovebox use were selected for evaluation. The cuff diameter and length of the gloves are 8" and 32". Two hand sizes, 
8.5"and 9.75" were provided and the subject wore both of the gloves to pick the best fit glove. 
For the single gloving condition, the subject donned only a pair of glovebox gloves. The double gloving condition 
includes a pair of glovebox glove as outer glove and a pair of natural rubber (Trionic© size 8, 0.02” thickness, 43.5 gm) 
gloves as the inner glove which was used in glovebox work to facilitate donning the outer glove. The triple gloving 
condition adds another pair of cotton gloves (median size, 0.008” thickness, 9.3 gm) as the innermost glove which is 
used in glovebox work for perspiration absorption purpose.  
Electromyography (EMG) 
The BIOPAC MP150 EMG System which includes amplifier & A/D converter (BIOPAC Systems UIM100C) and data 
acquisition software (Acq Knowledge 3.7.2) installed in a laptop were used to collect and process the EMG signals. 
After shaving and scrubbing the recording sites with alcohol, surface EMG electrodes were positioned over the 
following forearm muscles of dominant arm: (1) flexor digitorum superficialis (FDS), (2) flexor carpi radialis (FCR), (3) 
extensor carpi radialis longus (ECR), and (4) extensor digitorum (ED) of the subjects’ dominant arm involved with 
gripping tasks (Larivière et al., 2004). A ground electrode was also placed at the lateral epicondile of the subjects’ 
dominant arm. The EMG signals were bandpass filtered (20-450 Hz) and preamplified (gain: 1000) with 1k Hz 
sampling rate. 
The root mean square (RMS) amplitude which is proportional to the energy content of the muscle activity was used in 
this experiment for analysis of the EMG signals. The RMS value increases indicating an increase in muscle activity in 
gripping exertions. The RMS amplitude for the jth gripping exertion (RMSij) for ith session was normalized for each 
subject using equation 1. The RMSmax,i determined from three replicates of barehanded MVC gripping and RMSbase,i 
determined when the forearm is in rest were measured 30 minutes before the starting of ith session. The normalized 
RMS (nRMS) values of EMG represent the relative level of muscle activation for the corresponding muscles.

Procedures 
Upon the starting of this experiment, MVC grip strength and corresponding EMG for each subject were recorded for 
bare hand condition. The MVC grip strength for bare and gloved hands were measured according to Caldwell’s regimen 
(Caldwell et al., 1974) using a Jamar hand dynamometer with Transducer (Lafayette Instrument Company). The subject 
was instructed to increase to maximum exertion (without jerk) in about one second and maintain this effort during a 
four second count. The strength datum is the mean score recorded during the first three seconds of the steady exertions. 
RESULTS 
Effects on Grip Strength 
The results show that the mean MVC grip strength for all gloved hands is significantly lower (p<0.05) than that of bare 
hand and the amount of reduction for gloved hands compared to bare hand (36.9±3.3 Kg) ranges from 13.1% to 31.8% 
in this study. 
The results of the repeated-measured ANOVA (Table 1) show that there are significant differences between glove 
material (F=350.33, p<0.001), layer (F=510.23, p<0.001) and thickness condition (F=2753.31, p<0.001) on MVC grip 
strength. In addition, there are statistically significant interaction effects between glove material and layer, glove 
material and thickness, and among all three factors on the MVC grip strength. Bonferroni pairwise comparisons in 
glove material effect show that there were statistically significant differences (p<0.001) for all three glove materials. 
The butyl glove can retain significantly higher maximum grip strength followed by hypalon and neoprene gloves. In 
layer effect, statistically significant differences (p<0.001) were found for single vs. double, single vs. triple, and double 
vs. triple. The results show that more the layers of gloves are worn, the greater the grip strength reduction. In addition, 
the thinner (0.015”) gloves retain significantly higher maximum grip strength (2.8 kg in mean differences) than the 
thicker (0.03”) glove.

For the effects of glove material, layer, and thickness on muscle activity for four forearm muscles, the findings of the 
present investigation are:  
(1) The muscle activity is significantly higher when donned hypalon gloves than butyl gloves on FDS and ECR 
muscles. Hypalon gloves also generated significantly higher muscle activity than butyl and neoprene gloves on 
FCR. In addition, neoprene gloves generated significantly higher muscle activity than butyl gloves on ED. 
(2) The double gloving allowed significantly higher muscle activity than single and triple gloving on FDS and than 
triple gloving on ECR.  
(3) The thicker (0.03”) gloves generated significantly higher muscle activity than thinner (0.015”) gloves.  
To minimize effects of glovebox gloves on muscle activity based upon the observations made in this experiment, 
thinner butyl glove in single or triple gloving setting should be used as selection considerations for glovebox works.  
Kovacs et al. (2002) found no significant effect of glove type on EMG activity of FDS and ED when exerting MVC 
grip strength. This study also shows similar results for the MVC grip exertions. Since the glovebox activities may 
regularly require gripping with sub-maximal efforts in terms of maximal efforts. The results of this study recommend 
thin (0.015”) butyl glove in single gloving setting to provide the desired hand protection against ergonomic-related 
disorders in the glovebox environment involving gripping exertions.
