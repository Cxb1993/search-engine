在奈米尺度之動態應力下薄閘極氧化層之退化與崩潰
Degradation and Breakdown of Thin Gate Oxides under Nano-scaled Dynamic Stress
計畫編號：NSC97-2221-E-260-024
執行期間：97 年 08 月 01 日 至 98 年 07 月 31 日
主持人：國立暨南國際大學電機工程系 吳幼麟教授
一、中文摘要
雖然，在文獻中奈米尺度直流應力(DC stress)
下的SiO2 及高k 值絕緣層之退化及崩潰都曾
經被加以探討，但大部份的探討僅止於受應力
施加後之I-V 特性及表面形態的觀察。到目前
為止，全球的研究學者均未曾探討過SiO2及高
k值氧化層在奈米尺度交流應力作用下所展現
的行為。本計畫的目的即在嘗試瞭解閘極氧化
層在奈米尺度動態應力作用下之退化及崩潰
特性。透過原子力顯微鏡與Agilent 4156C半導
體參數量測儀對薄閘極氧化層施加奈米尺度
之單極性及雙極性方形脈波並觀測閘極氧化
層之崩潰特性。我們的實驗發現氧化層的崩潰
時間一開始是隨所施加方波的頻率增加而增
加，但當頻率超過10KHz時，氧化層的崩潰時
間反而隨頻率增加而降低。
關鍵詞：動態應力、原子力顯微鏡、氧化層崩
潰、電荷捕捉及反捕捉
英文摘要
Although the nanoscale degradation and
breakdown behaviors of SiO2 and high-k gate
dielectrics under DC stresses have been largely
investigated, most of them are basically focused
on post-stress I-V characteristics and surface
morphology changes. It is rarely found any
discussion about the nanoscale degradation of
gate dielectrics under ac stresses. In this project,
we used conductive atomic force microscopy
(C-AFM) in conjunction with semiconductor
parameter analyzer Agilent 4156C to study the
nanoscale degradation and breakdown behaviors
of thin SiO2 and high-k gate dielectrics under
unipolar and bipolar square waves. We found
that, initially, the time to breakdown of thin gate
oxide increases with increasing square wave
frequency, then it decreases with frequency as
the frequency is larger than 10kHz.
Keywords: dynamic stress, atomic force
microscopy, oxide breakdown, charge trapping
and detrapping
二、計畫的緣由與目的
過去對於金屬-氧化層-半導體元件的研究通常
以靜態直流電應力為主，如：定電壓應力
(CVS)、定電流應力 (CCS)與斜坡電壓應力
(RVS)等方法。但在實際的電路操作中，
MOSFET的閘極與汲極經常承受到的是瞬變
的電壓而非直流電壓。過去的文獻[1]提出金氧
半元件在交流應力下的退化情況比直流應力
作用下還要來的嚴重，且閘極暫態電壓 (Gate
Voltage transient)對金氧半元件的退化影響遠
大於汲極暫態電壓(Drain Voltage transient)，也
就是說當交流應力施加於金氧半元件上時，主
要的退化還是發生在閘極氧化層。而且在DC
Stress的作用下，金氧半元件主要的損壞是發
生於interface states，但AC Stress所產生的損壞
卻主要是電洞注入(hole injection)所產生的中
性電子缺陷(neutral electron traps)。然而目前所
探討的交流應力所觀測到的現象，都是在閘極
面積之下的平均資訊。因此，我們基於過去對
薄氧化層於奈米尺度直流應力下的了解，希望
能更近一步得知其在奈米尺度動態應力作用
下的特性。透過傳導式原子力顯微鏡的探針針
尖與裸露的氧化層直接接觸，取代了傳統金氧
半電容的金屬閘極，且探針與氧化層接觸面積
極小，大約只有幾十到幾百nm2，且側向解析
度亦可達幾個nm的範圍[2,3]。在本計畫中，我

result indicates that the HfO2 and SiO2 samples have different
post-irradiated charge trapping properties. Oxide trapped
charges can be annealed out at temperature below 500℃. Thus
a 400℃, 20 min furnace post-irradiation anneal was carried out
in N2 ambient for those irradiated samples, and their nanoscale
I-V characteristics were measured as shown in Fig. 5. The
post-irradiation annealed hysteresis vs. preset current levels as
the stopping points of forward sweeps for SiO2 and HfO2
samples are shown in Fig. 6. Comparing with Figs. 3 and 4, it is
evident that the charge trapping has been altered after the
post-irradiation anneal.
In order to find out how the charge trapping properties between
HfO2 and SiO2 are different, we plotted the delta hysteresis
(Hysteresis = Hysteresis (after) –Hysteresis (initial)) as a
function of the maximum stressed current level for
post-irradiated and post-irradiation annealed samples as shown
in Fig. 7. We found that the post-irradiated Hysteresis of
HfO2 is smaller than that of SiO2, which means that HfO2 is
more radiation hardened than SiO2. It is also obvious that the
post-irradiated Hysteresis for the HfO2 samples is positive
while that for the SiO2 samples is negative irrespective of the
maximum stressed current level. This indicates that the oxide
trapped charge in HfO2 is more acceptor-like while that in SiO2
is more donor-like after irradiation. As observed, these oxide
trapped charges can be annealed out by post-irradiation
annealing.
IV. CONCLUSION
A new technique has been developed in this work for
determining the nanoscale oxide charge trapping properties by
combining the CAFM and a semiconductor parameter analyzer
Agilent 4156C. Forward and backward sweep nanoscale I-V
characteristics with different preset maximum stressed current
levels were measured. The hysteresis was taken as the voltage
differences between the forward and backward sweeps at a
fixed current level of 1 10-12 A. It was found that HfO2 has
less hysteresis than SiO2 unless the maximum stressed current
level is smaller than 10-11 A. This result indicates that initially
HfO2 has a higher level of oxide traps. However, HfO2 is more
stress-resistant than SiO2 when the stressed current level
becomes higher. From the post-irradiated nanoscale I-V
characteristics, we observed that the oxide trapped charge in
HfO2 is more acceptor-like while that in SiO2 is more
donor-like after irradiation. We also concluded that HfO2 is
more radiation hardened than SiO2.
