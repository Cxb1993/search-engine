摘要 
有別於一般常用之正齒輪組及螺旋齒輪組，球形齒輪組(Spherical Gear Set)
具有可容許軸交角變動與軸裝配誤差之傳動特性而不會導致齒緣接觸(Edge 
Contact)，同時球形齒輪組在傳動時也不會產生軸向推力(Axial Thrust Force)。雖
然球形齒輪之加工技術很早就已經被發展出來，但有關球形齒輪的研究則至今只
有很少數的研究可查。因此，若能建立出球形齒輪的齒面數學模式，則可利用此
數學模式來進行球形齒輪之相關分析與研究，更進一步了解球形齒輪的特性及應
用上之限制。本專題研究主要係探討凸狀球形齒輪(Convex Spherical Gear)及凹狀
球形齒輪(Concave Spherical Gear)所組成之球形齒輪組的特性，以提供工業界在
球形齒輪應用上的參考。 
本專題研究計畫為三年型研究計畫，計畫之第一年(95 年 8 月 1 日至 96 年 7
月 31 日)，係利用齒輪嚙合原理與電腦輔助繪圖技術，針對以假想齒條刀創成之
球形齒輪進行其齒面數學模式之建立。此外，亦依據所推導之齒面數學模式，建
立球形齒輪之齒面過切條件式，並探討球形齒輪在不同設計參數條件下，探討其
過切線之位置與齒面過切(Tooth Undercutting)的情況。 
計畫之第二年(96 年 08 月 1 日至 97 年 07 月 31 日)，係利用第一年所發展之
球形齒輪之齒面數學模式，透過齒面接觸分析(Tooth Contact Analysis)之方法，推
導出球形齒輪組之齒面接觸數學模式。再利用此齒輪嚙合模型發展球形齒輪組之
齒面接觸電腦分析軟體，探討球形齒輪組在不同的組裝條件與齒輪設計參數下的
運動誤差。而球形齒輪組之接觸比則利用齒面接觸分析程式並配合不同的齒輪設
計參數來計算，以探討不同齒輪設計參數對接觸比(Contact Ratio)的影響。另外，

Abstract 
Different from those of the conventional spur gear set and helical gear set, the 
spherical gear set can allow variable shaft angles and axial misalignments without 
edge contact and axial thrust force. Although the manufacturing techniques of 
spherical gears had been developed already, however only very few of researches on 
the spherical gears up to now. Therefore, in order to understand the characteristics and 
limits of usage on spherical gears, it is necessary to set up the mathematical model of 
spherical gears and to perform various analyses on spherical gears. This research 
project studies the characteristics of spherical gear sets that composed of the convex 
and concave tooth of spherical gears. The research results provide important 
references for industry to the applications of spherical gears. 
The project is a three-year NSC research project. In the first year of the NSC 
research project, a mathematical model for the spherical gear, which has been 
generated by an imaginary rack cutter, is developed based on the theory of gearing 
and techniques of computer aided design. According to the developed mathematical 
model of tooth surfaces, the conditions of tooth undercutting have been investigated. 
Further, the location of undercutting line and the undercutting status have also been 
studied. 
In the second year’s research report, based on the mathematical model of tooth 
surfaces developed by the first year’s research, a meshing model of spherical gear set 
has been derived by applying the tooth contact analysis (TCA) method. The kinematic 
errors of the spherical gears have been performed by the developed analysis programs 
with different assembly conditions and design parameters. The contact ratios of the 
spherical gear with convex tooth mating with concave tooth have also been 
investigated. Moreover, the sizes and positions of contact patterns of spherical gears 
have been studied by using the tooth surface topology method.

研究目的 
球形齒輪其最大的特色是具有容許變動軸交角之傳動特性，然而正齒輪組及
螺旋齒輪組，因其組裝嚙合時無法容許齒輪旋轉軸角之變化，可能會產生齒輪在
嚙合時卡死的現象。因此，若利用球形齒輪此一特性於變動軸交角之使用場合
中，如齒輪聯軸器(Gear-Type Coupling)[1]，將是一個不錯的應用方向。過去對
於球形齒輪之相關研究並不多[2-4]，楊義雄及山崎隆[5]提到球形齒輪之實體已
經被加工出來，但相關之研究則未進行，2000年Mitome[6]提出的一種新型之球
形齒輪，因此，進一步瞭解球形齒輪在幾何上及嚙合上之特性，將可提供工業界
在設計及加工球形齒輪時，選取適當之刀具參數及加工條件。 
齒輪之齒形過切對齒輪嚙合之接觸比及齒根應力有很大的影響。過大的齒形
過切將使得齒輪之齒根容易發生斷裂之現象。因此，本專題研究計畫將利用
Litvin[11-13]所發展之齒輪嚙合原理及應用電腦輔助繪圖技術，進行球形齒輪齒
面數學模式之建立及齒形過切之研究。以探討球形齒輪之齒形過切現象與刀具參
數之間的關係，以提供工業界在加工球形齒輪時之參考依據。 
齒輪在製造及組裝時，多少會產生齒形製造誤差及裝配誤差，進而造成齒輪
在傳遞運動的過程中的傳動誤差。當齒輪組在進行裝配的過程中，具有水平軸向
偏差、垂直軸向偏差或是中心距的偏差時，將會影響組裝後整體齒輪組的傳動性
能。因此，本專題研究計畫將利用Litvin[11-13]所發展之齒面接觸分析(Tooth 
Contact Analysis)方法，來推導球形齒輪組之齒面接觸分析數學模式，並用以探
討球形齒輪組之運動誤差及接觸比。此外，亦利用Janninck[17]所發展之齒面外
形法(Surface Topology Method)來求解球形齒輪組之接觸齒印的大小及位置。 
齒輪組嚙合時實際的接觸狀況十分複雜，需考慮嚙合齒面之曲率變化、嚙合
齒面間摩擦力、齒輪組所承受之負載與齒輪的材料性質等。因此，為研究球形齒

第一章  前言 
球形齒輪其最大的特色是具有容許變動軸交角之傳動特性。對正齒輪組及螺
旋齒輪組來說，因其組裝嚙合時無法容許齒輪旋轉軸角之變化，可能會產生齒輪
在嚙合時卡死的現象。此外，球形齒輪亦能在兩個齒輪軸為交叉軸的情況下傳遞
動力，此一性質與錐形齒輪[2]相似。因此，若利用球形齒輪此一特性於變動軸
交角之使用場合中，如齒輪聯軸器(Gear-Type Coupling)[1]，將是一個不錯的應用
方向。若以外型來區分，球形齒輪可分為凸狀球形齒輪及凹狀球形齒輪。凸狀球
形齒輪有如球體之一部份，而凹狀球形齒輪則與蝸輪相似。凸狀球形齒輪可視為
滾齒機在創成齒輪時，刀具由齒輪之齒面端部往齒輪之齒面中央滾削時，呈現連
續二次方程式變化之正轉位所創成出來之齒輪。反之，凹狀球形齒輪則為刀具連
續二次方程式變化之負轉位所滾削而成之齒輪。如圖 1.1 所示，當球形齒輪互相
嚙合時，球形齒輪組可以有以下三種之配對型式：凸狀球形齒輪搭配凸狀球形齒
輪、凸狀球形齒輪搭配凹狀球形齒輪及凸狀球形齒輪搭配正齒輪。

第四章  球形齒輪組之有限元素應力分析 
4.1  前言 
由於齒輪組實際嚙合時的接觸狀況十分複雜，需考慮嚙合齒面之曲率變
化、齒面嚙合區域受力的大小、嚙合齒面間摩擦力以及齒輪的材料機械性質等。
在本研究第三章中，係以剛體模型來分析球形齒輪的接觸齒印分布情形，然而
剛體模型分析，並沒有考量到負載、摩擦和材料性質等因素，而這些因素都與
實際齒印的大小和分布情況有著密切的影響，為了模擬更接近實際的齒輪嚙合
狀況，本章利用有限元素法(Finite Element Method)[29]建立三齒接觸分析模
型，探討齒輪組負載下的接觸情形，亦即所謂的負載下的齒面接觸分析
(LTCA)，包括齒面接觸應力與齒根彎曲應力，以了解齒輪組在負載下的接觸區
域。分析方法係依據第二章所推導之球形齒輪齒面方程式與第三章所推導之球
形齒輪組之齒面接觸分析條件式，自行發展三維齒面網格分割軟體，建立有限
元素接觸模型，並於軟體中設定齒輪組承受之負載、齒面間的摩擦力、齒輪材
料性質及邊界條件，藉由 ABAQUS/Standard[31]分析軟體進行齒面接觸應力與
齒根彎曲應力之分析。 
4.2  球形齒輪組之有限元素網格模型

CONTACT CHARACTERISTICS OF THE ZK-TYPE WORM AND ZN-TYPE 
WORM WHEEL MESHING WITH A NON-NINETY-DEGREE CROSSING ANGLE 
*Kuan-Yu CHEN1 and Chung-Biau TSAY2
1Department of Mechanical Engineering, National Chiao Tung University, 
Hsinchu 30010, Taiwan 
2Corrosponding autherDepartment of Mechanical Engineering, Minghsin University of Science and Technology, 
Hsinfong, Hsinchu 30401, Taiwan
Keywords: Worm gear set, Non-ninety-degree crossing angle, Tooth contact analysis, Average contact ratio, Instantaneous 
contact teeth. 
ABSTRACT
Mathematical models of the ZK-type worm and 
ZN-type worm wheel are developed based on their 
respective generating mechanisms. Kinematic errors  
(KEs) of the proposed worm gear drive under a 
non-ninety-degree crossing angle are calculated by 
applying the tooth contact analysis (TCA) method. 
Contact ratios (CR), instantaneous contact teeth (ICT) and 
average contact ratio (ACR) are investigated by applying 
the TCA method. It is found that the CR, ICT and ACR of 
the proposed worm gear drive are increased significantly 
when the crossing angle is chosen as 60 degrees. The 
effects of gear parameters on the ICT, CR, ACR and KEs 
under various crossing angle of the worm gear drive are 
demonstrated by an illustrative example. 
1. Introduction
The worm gear set is composed of a worm and worm 
wheel and is one of the most important devices for 
transmitting torques between spatial crossed axes. Due to 
its high transmission ratio, low noise and compact 
structure, the worm gear set is widely used in 
gear-reduction mechanisms. Characteristics and contact 
nature of the worm gear set have been investigated by 
many researchers. Winter and Wilkesmann [1] proposed a 
calculation method of obtaining different tooth profiles. 
Simon [2-3] studied the manufacture of a high precision 
hob for worm surface generations. Litvin and Kin [4] 
proposed a generalized tooth contact analysis (TCA) 
algorithm to determine the position of transfer points 
where an ideal contact line will turn into a real contact 
point. The influences of axial misalignments and 
center-distance errors on the conjugate worm gear set were 
also investigated in their study. Kin [5] studied the surface 
deviations of the produced worm gear tooth surfaces due 
to the cutting-edge deviation of hob cutters. Tsay [6] 
applied TCA techniques to simulate the meshing 
conditions of involute helical gears, and proposed a 
compensation method to reduce the kinematic error (KE) 
induced by horizontal axial misalignments. Lin et al. [7] 
performed TCA for hypoid gears. Fang and Tsay [8-10] 
derived mathematical models of the ZK, ZN and ZE-type 
worm gear sets based on their respective hobbing 
mechanisms. They also investigated bearing contacts and 
KEs of these types of worm gear sets. Bair and Tsay 
[11-12] studied the bearing contact, KE, contact teeth and 
contact ratio (CR) of the ZK-type dual-lead worm gear 
drives. Janninck [13] proposed a contact surface topology 
method to predict the initial contact pattern on worm gear 
teeth. Litvin et al. [14] developed a method for 
localization of bearing contacts of gear drives and also 
proposed a method to eliminate the discontinuous linear 
KE induced by gear axial misalignments. Colbourne [15] 
investigated the tooth undercutting, interference and 
non-conjugate contact of the ZK-type worm gear set. 
Shigley and Mischke [16] defined the CR of a gear pair as 
the average numbers of contact teeth during the gear 
meshing. The CR can also be defined by the gear rotation 
angle, measured from the starting point to the end point of 
contact, divided by the angle formed by two successive 
teeth.
Although the previous studies have significantly 
improved the characteristics of worm gear drives, they all 
focused on analyses of worm gear drives with a 
ninety-degree crossing angle. In this study, the worm gear 
drive is meshed under a non-ninety-degree crossing angle, 
and thus it provides a higher contact ratio when it is 
compared with that of the conventional worm gear drive 
with a ninety-degree crossing angle. The proposed gear set 
is composed of the ZK-type worm ground by a grinding 
wheel and the ZN-type worm wheel cut by a ZN-type 
worm-type hob cutter. Mathematical models of the 
ZK-type worm and ZN-type worm wheel are developed 
based on their respective generating mechanisms. 
According to the developed mathematical models, the 
meshing simulations can be performed to investigate the 
KE, instantaneous contact teeth (ICT) and average contact 
ratios (ACR) of the worm gear drive under a 
non-ninety-degree crossing angle.

6. Contact Ratios of the Gear Drive 
The contact ratio (CR) of a gear pair is generally 
defined as the rotation angle, measured from the starting 
point of contact to the end point of contact, divided by the 
angle formed by two successive teeth. The instantaneous 
contact teeth (ICT) of a spur gear, helical gear, spiral bevel 
gear or hypoid gear usually is one to three teeth. However, 
the ICT of a worm gear drive can have more than two, 
three, four or even five teeth. In this study, TCA method is 
applied to calculate the KEs and find all the instantaneous 
contact points of the worm gear drive at every selected 
contact instant (i.e. computed point), where the selected 
contact instant is chosen as every 0.01° of the worm 
rotational angle. Meanwhile, ICT is also calculated by 
allowing an amount of 3 m�  for worm wheel surface 
elastic deformation, then checking how many worm teeth 
are actually in contact with the mating worm wheel at 
every computed point. By doing so, ICT can be 
determined by the numbers of instantaneous contact teeth 
of the gear drive. Herein, average contact ratio (ACR) is 
defined as the sum of ICT of the selected contact instants 
(computed points) divided by the total numbers of selected 
contact instants within one meshing cycle.

8. Conclusions 
In this study, TCA is performed for simulations of KE, 
ICT, CR and ACR of the ZK-type worm and ZN-type 
worm wheel drive with non-ninety-degree crossing angles. 
The ICT and ACR are obtained by applying the numerical 
method and the developed TCA computer simulation 
programs. The value of CR calculated by conventional 
definition is slightly smaller than that of ACR. The CR and 
ACR are increased for the worm gear drive with a 
non-ninety-degree of crossing angle, and it can increase 
the absorbability of the impact energy subjected to 
machines, and thus can reduce the design safety factor of a 
worm gear drive for dynamic loads. Based on the 
illustrated example discussed in this study, the ZK-type 
worm and ZN-type worm wheel drive has multiple contact 
teeth. The ACR is 3.791 for the gear drive with the 
conventional assembly crossing angle of ��=90.0º and 
ACR can be increased to�4.526 with a non-ninety-degree 
crossing angle of ��=60.0º under ideal assembly conditions. 
The maximum KE of the proposed worm gear drives with 
crossing angles ��=30.0º, 45.0º and 60.0º are still kept by 
0.0 arc-second under ideal assembly conditions, and this is 
the same as that of the crossing angle of ��=90.0º. 
Therefore, The ZK-type worm and ZN-type worm wheel 
drive with a non-ninety-degree crossing angle belongs to a 
high-contact-ratio gear set. Besides, parameters of the 
worm gear drive, such as the crossing angle, will also 
influence the contact ratio of the worm gear drive. The 
main advantage of the proposed ZK-type worm and 
ZN-type worm wheel drive is that it can mesh in a very 
low level of kinematical errors with a high contact ratio 
even under assembly errors.
