計畫英文摘要。 
Ecosystems have changed more rapidly in the past 50 years than at any other time in recorded history, 
often resulting in a degradation of ecosystems services (MEA 2005). Lakes are exemplars of change, 
as a variety of social, landscape and climate factors drive globally pervasive degradation of lake 
water quality and quantity. These are issues that transcend geographic and political boundaries. A 
better understanding of the fate of lakes in the face of change requires generalized understanding of 
lake-landscape-climate interactions, and yet each lake is a story by itself. Its observable 
characteristics represent complex and changing physical, chemical, and biological processes that 
respond in unique ways to a local history of drivers. Capturing that complexity in a few wells-studied 
lakes and extracting key indicators of change will help us scale from a few well-studied lakes to 
broader gradients more representative of global diversity. There is a paradigm shift underway, in 
which ecologists envision lakes as detectors for measuring broader environmental change 
 4 
 
Through lakes-as-detectors we make new kinds of comparisons among ecosystems, allowing us to address 
questions, such as:  
 
 What are the patterns and surprises in sensor data, and what do they tell us about how external drivers 
influence lake physical, chemical, and biological processes? 
 How do large gradients in geology, hydrology, and climate influence lake responses to external drivers? 
 What are the essential emergent characteristics from lakes that allow us to generalize processes from a 
few, highly instrumented lakes to regional and global scales? 
 
Discovery science requires cyber infrastructure (CI) embedded in the daily activities of the distributed 
GLEON community. Dependable infrastructure and middleware are needed to deliver observed and 
simulated data to models and scientists through a web of complex steps. Standards for data access, 
transformation, and delivery need to be developed to facilitate use of multiple models. Computing resources 
are needed to support complex and varied modeling tasks. The analytical models innovated to interpret the 
data will be many and varied, exploiting the creativity of multiple disciplines in ways that are new to 
ecologists and advance the state of the art in distributed computing. 
   
二、研究目的 
In this proposal, we have three broad goals that include several of the principles for Cyberinfrastructre 
Vision for 21st Century Discovery (NSF 2007): Advance the science: The lakes-as-detectors paradigm forms 
the nexus of partnerships among ecologists, physical limnologists, and computer scientists. The synergies 
obtained from alternative analytical approaches ultimately lead to exploitation of data in the creation of new 
knowledge. Use computational thinking: Integrating advanced computing technologies into the daily routine 
of ecologists by leveraging proven workflow and distributed computing capabilities offered by the Condor 
project in support of a suite of analytical models. Middleware from NCHC interacts with data repositories, 
provides data transport, and manages analytical models. These technologies will increase the accessibility 
and usability of NSF-funded, as well as international sensing networks. Expand partnerships: New 
partnership between GLEON and Condor brings critical computing expertise and resources to the network, 
and expanded leadership by existing GLEON international partners at NCHC (Taiwan) provides desperately 
needed software.  
 
三、研究方法 
The science of lakes-as-detectors 
 
Lakes-as-detectors focuses research on how the flux of energy and material into lakes propagates through 
physical dynamics mediated by feedbacks from chemical and biological components of the system. The flow 
of energy and nutrients is complex and its manifestations are multi-scale and multi-dimensional. We propose 
a novel strategy that embraces this complexity in its representation of physical, chemical, and biological 
components and have assembled our team toward this strategic end. Physical limnology emphasizes the 
 6 
complexity of sensor network data. 
 
The complementary approaches of physical and ecosystems science together deliver emergent characteristics 
of lakes that demonstrate the utility of the lakes-as-detectors paradigm. The process for achieving these goals 
will feed back to the respective disciplines in important ways. Operating in a near-continuous, 
mutli-dimensional world will be new to most environmental scientists. All scientists will have new 
appreciation for what constitutes a disturbance event for lakes. Ecosystems ecologists will gain new 
understanding of the role of physical processes in contributing to ecological processes. Physical limnologists 
will gain better understanding of how biology feeds back to physics, as well as a better understanding of the 
circumstances in which their models predict well. Computer science will be improved through learning how 
complementary visions by environmental scientists are reflected in alternative processes for conducting 
research. The products for facilitating shared visions will also inform all of the CI requirements for further 
advancement of science from sensor networks.  
 
Rationale – Innovative Use of Computational Thinking 
 
GLEON‟s “grass-roots” approach to science, which empowers individuals to contribute to the productivity 
of the network, has largely been responsible for the enthusiasm and growth in GLEON (Hanson 2007). 
Individuals commit their time and data for the common good of the network. What GLEON gives in return 
are the CI expertise and resources needed to connect an individual‟s lake sensor network to the global 
infrastructure. The distribution of CI to individuals through the network has led to the explosive growth in 
GLEON data from lakes around the world. This proposal takes the next step, which introduces compute 
technologies into the research activities of all GLEON members, taking science to the next level for 
individuals and for the network. Through this activity, a global community of aquatic scientists will be 
exposed to another level of lake ecology.  
 
四、結果與討論 
The CI required to support the science will: (1) access large repositories of data, and move data seamlessly 
through a web of models and repositories; (2) accomplish a complex series of tasks in dependable ways; (3) 
support the interconnection of models, some of which are extremely compute intensive, in flexible and fast 
ways; (4) provide on-demand access to GLEON scientists from around the world. This functionality extends 
existing GLEON technology by leveraging proven workflow and distributed technologies available through 
Condor and data access and transport technologies through NCHC.  
 
Condor (http://www.cs.wisc.edu/condor/) is a specialized workload management system for 
compute-intensive jobs in a High-Throughput Computing (HTC) environment. Condor provides a job 
queuing mechanism, scheduling policy, priority scheme, resource monitoring, and resource management. 
Users submit their serial or parallel jobs to Condor, Condor places them into a queue, chooses when and 
where to run the jobs based upon a policy, carefully monitors their progress, and ultimately informs the user 
upon completion. Condor provides an ideal environment for complex collections of computing tasks (Fig. 1).

一、背景與目的 
    本次美國行主要是執行參與之國科會計畫「全球湖泊學研究之觀測網路
虛擬化研究」(主持人為林芳邦博士)參與國際學術會議的項目。相關研究主題約
自 2008 夏季開始，至今已有部份執行成果並開始進行論文撰寫。因我國在湖泊
代謝能屬於剛起步階段，缺乏相關經驗，相關資料有賴美方湖泊學家協助確認與
解釋，因此，本人年初便著手規劃此次的行程。此行目的有二：第一，與美方合
作的研究人員與其他國家成員，研討我們在鴛鴦湖代謝能力上的最新研究成果；
第二，將藉由我方的研究資料協及資訊技術助提供建構大尺度湖泊概念模式，並
希望藉由此次研討會與各國研究人員交換研究上的心得、增進彼此認識，並為未
來的國際合作及學生交換計畫建立互信的基礎。 
二、行程報告 
   本次會議在 University of Wisconsin-Madison, Trout Lake Station 研究站舉辦，
 3 
了解美方新建立的新模式為此行最重要的目的之ㄧ，Dr. Ryan 向我說明該新模
式為一以機制為基礎的生態模式(非經驗模式)，主要是為了彌補傳統的 Odum 模
式對於湖泊代謝機制假設過於簡化的缺點修正而來，藉由此一新修正模式擬合野
外監測資料來推估影響湖泊代謝能力主要作用機制的量化方式。該模式涵蓋了垂
直光剖面、水溫剖面、葉綠素 a (Chl a)、溶解性有機態碳(DOC)、表層水溫與溶
氧量及風速與 PAR。其中溫度剖面資料可提供計算湖泊的浮力因子(buoyancy 
frequency, BF)，代表湖水水層的分層強度，其值越高代表強度越大，也就是表層
水越不易與下層水層產生垂直混合；而光剖面資料則可提供推估不同水深處之光
線衰減係數(light extinction coefficient, LEC)，Chl a、DOC 濃度由研究人員定期
於野外採樣分析所得，而水溫、溶氧、風速等環境資料則由 Buoy 上的儀器量測
獲的，這些湖泊參數為此模式的主要輸入參數，統一了參數的時間間距之後便可
輸入 DoMaster 這個主模式中進行湖泊溶氧動態變化的描述與預測。圖二說明該
新模式的基本架構。
