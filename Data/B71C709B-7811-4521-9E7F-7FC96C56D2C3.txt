Abstract 
New white polymeric light-emitting diodes 
(WPLEDs) from phosphorescent single 
polymer systems have been developed using 
a blue-light-emitting fluorene monomer 
copolymerized with a red-light-emitting 
phosphorescent dye, and end-capped with a 
green-light-emission dye. All of the 
copolymers have good thermal stability with 
5 % weight loss temperatures at 380-413°C 
and glass transition temperatures at 75-137°C. 
We obtained white-light-emission devices by 
adjusting the molar ratio of the co-monomers 
with a structure of indium tin 
oxide/poly(3,4-ethylenedioxythiophene): 
poly(styrene sulfonic 
acid)/polyvinylcarbazole (PVK)/emission 
layer/Ca/Ag. The highest brightness in such a 
device configuration is 300 cd/m2 at a current 
density of 2900 A/m2 with Commission 
Internationale de l’Eclairage (CIE) 
coordinates of (0.33, 0.34). 
 
Keywords:   light-emitting diodes ; 
copolymerization  ; films; polyfluorene

EL Properties  
The devices were fabricated in the 
configuration of ITO/PEDOT:PSS/PVK/ 
emission layer/Ca/Ag. Figure 3 shows the EL 
spectra of the copolymers. The EL spectrum 
of P1 only exhibits green-light emission 
(λmax = 480 nm) from the NTI unit. P2 
exhibits blue-light emission (λmax = 430 
nm/460 nm) from the fluorene segment, and 
red-light emission (λmax = 600 nm) from the 
iridium complex. P3 and P4 show co-existent 
blue-, green-, and red-light emissions in their 
EL spectra. The devices of P3 and P4 emit 
light with CIE coordinates of (0.27, 0.31) and 
(0.33, 0.34) respectively. The light emitted 
from P4 is close to the pure white-light 
emission of (0.33, 0.33). We can observe a 
pure white-light emission from the device of 
P4 presented in the inset of Figure 3B. The 
peak intensity of red-light emission in EL 
spectra is much stronger than that in the PL 
spectra. This can be attributed to the 
charge-trapping mechanism. There are more 
charges recombined on the iridium complex. 
The CIE coordinates of P4 changed from 
(0.33, 0.34) to (0.28, 0.32), when the bias was 
raised. The EL spectra (Figure 4) changed 
slightly with the applied voltage, and the 
red-light emission decreased with the 
increasing current density. Table 3 shows the 
EL properties of copolymers. 
 
四、計畫成果自評 
In summary, we have obtained white-light- 
emitting devices from phosphorescent single 
polymer systems based on fluorene, NTI, and 
iridium complex monomers. The EL spectra 
can be adjusted to white-light emission by 
changing the molar ratio of the monomers. 
The device of P4 emits white light with CIE 
coordinates of (0.33, 0.34) and exhibits a 
maximum luminance of 300 cd/m2 at a 
current density of 2900 A/m2. This study 
demonstrates an effective way to prepare pure 
polymer white-light-emitting diodes. 
本研究成果已投稿且刊登在 Journal of 
Polymer Science, Part A; Polymer Chemistry 
vol. 46, 464-472 (2008).
