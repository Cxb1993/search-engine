 III 
 
 
ABSTRACT 
 
In the present study, we synthesized Fe3O4@SiO2 nanocomposites by 
sol-gel methods, as well as systematically investigated the influences of 
the preparation conditions on the characteristics of nanocomposites. The 
physical and magnetic properties were characterized by TEM 
(Transmission Electron Microscope), EDX(Energy-dispersive X-ray 
spectroscopy), XRD(X-ray Diffraction) and VSM(Vibrating Sample 
Magnetometer) measurements. The results showed that well-dispersed 
Fe3O4@SiO2 nanoparticles with high magnetization could be obtained by 
mixing 25 mL 3 wt% Fe3O4 solution and 0.5 mL tetraethyl orthosilicate 
(TEOS) under 750 r.p.m for 10 min. The average size of Fe3O4@SiO2 
nanoparticle was 17 nm and surface of nanoparticle showed a thin and 
uniform SiO2 layer with 2 nm, as shown in Fig. 1. We also found that the 
size and morphology of nanocomposites were dependent on the stirring 
rate and reaction time. In addition, the thickness of SiO2 layer and 
amounts of Fe3O4 nanoparticles embedded in nanocomposites were 
dependent on the dosage of TEOS and Fe3O4. Accordingly, we can 
facilely prepare the appropriate Fe3O4@SiO2 nanocomposites for the real 
application by controlling these factors.

一、前言 
氧化鐵磁性奈米粒子的應用近年來受到高度重視，因為氧化鐵磁性奈米粒
子有若多優異特性，例如超順磁性、高飽和磁化量與磁化率，良好的生物相容
性和低毒性，所以常應用於用在生物分離、藥物傳輸、標靶藥物治療、核磁共
振顯影與腫瘤溫熱法[1-5]。但單純磁性奈米粒子易受到外來物影響或接觸空氣
而造成氧化，使粒子團聚、飽和磁化量下降等缺點。 
在磁性奈米粒子表面包覆一層保護層，是近年來許多學者研究的方向，像
SiO2、TiO2等，其中以 SiO2最為熱門，因 SiO2 具有良好的生物相容性及表面易
改質的優點，且在酸性環境能保護磁性奈米粒子[6]。 
利用 SiO2易改質的特性，以無電電鍍法在 SiO2 表面鍍上銀奈米粒子，因銀
奈米粒子的特殊光學性質-表面電漿共振，可做為近紅外線吸收材，在近紅外線
的激發下會產生熱量，殺死癌細胞，且近紅外線對人體傷害性低，可做為生醫
上光熱治療法之材料[7]。 
許多研究顯示，雖然能以 SiO2包覆磁性粒子，但所合成出之磁性/SiO2複合
粒子的飽和磁化量與原先磁化量相比，下降 50%以上，造成 Fe3O4/SiO2 複合粒
子無法完全吸附，應用於溫熱法時，發熱量不足；以無電電鍍法在 Fe3O4/SiO2
奈米複合材料表面鍍上銀奈米粒子，卻因包覆量太低，發熱量低。 
本研究以化學共沉法與溶膠-凝膠法，以 SiO2 為包覆材，製備 Fe3O4/SiO2奈
米複合材料後，以無電電鍍法在其表面鍍上銀奈米粒子，利用交流磁場與近紅外
線測試發熱量，觀察兩者的發熱能力。

Abstract 
The hyperthermia using superparamagnetic nanoparticles, magnetite (Fe3O4), as a thermoseed is a promising solution for tumor therapy. The aim of this study was to investigate the heating 
of superparamagnetic nanoparticles influenced by physical and environmental factors under alternating current field. We simulate the physiological factors such protein, blood sugar, 
electrolyte and viscos body fluid with bovine serum albumin, dextrose, commercial norm-saline or glycerol, respectively. The lauric acid was used as the surfactant to modify the surface of

Introduction 
Some studies have shown that the self-heating characteristics of magnetic nanoparticles 
under an alternating magnetic field can not only kill tumor cells. In practical application, 
Magnetic nanoparticles (MNPs) coated by a biocompatible surfactant must mix in to 
medium or inject them into an organism for in vitro or in vivo testing. However, previous 
studies showed that the aggregates composed of MNPs were formed with time and 
reduced the heating ability. Although we could know that the synthesized MNPs under a 
specific condition were not suitable as a good thermal seed, the major influences often 
could not been identified. In this study, a facile, rapid and low cost method was provided 
to evaluate initially the heating ability of MNPs in physiological environments, such as 
protein, blood sugar, electrolyte and viscos body liquid. We used bovine serum albumin, 
dextrose, commercial norm-saline or glycerol respectively to test the heating ability of 
MNPs and found the major influence factor to act as the reference for improving the 
preparation of MNPs.   
Experimental 
1. Preparation and characteristics of magnetic Fe3O4 
Nanosized magnetite was prepared by chemical precipitation method using Fe2+, 
Fe3+ salt, and ammonium hydroxide under a nitrogen atmosphere. The 
nanoparticles were characterized by transmission electron microscopy, X-ray 
diffraction, Fourier transformation infrared spectroscopy and superconducting 
quantum interference device magnetometer. 
2. The measurements of the heating abilities 
Heating A 0.5-mL sample in a 2 ml tube was placed at the center of the coils as 
shown in Fig. 1 for each experiment. The temperature was measured by fiber optic 
thermometer (Neoptix, Nomad type) under an alternating magnetic field. The 
heating ability was then reported as an SAR value. The influence of simulated 
physiological conditions, such as protein (bovine serum albumin, 0~4g/L), blood 
sugar (dextrose, 0~1000mg/L), and electrolytes (commercial norm-saline, 
containing NaCl 150mg/L) for chemical effects, and viscosity (glycerol, 
0.9~43.2cP) for physical effect on the heating capacity of magnetic nanoparticles 
synthesized at an Fe3+:Fe2+ ratio of 3:2 was investigated at a sample concentration 
of 0.025 g/ml, and alternating magnetic field parameters of 215 kHz and 3.8 kA/m. 
The heating mechanism was investigated by comparing the SAR values between 
magnetic nanoparticles dissolved in pure water and mixture of 
poly(dimethylsiloxane) (PDMS) and magnetic nanoparticles.

Results and Discussion 
1. Fig. 2 showed that the XRD spectra from four black magnetic nanoparticle samples in 
color with different Fe3+:Fe2+ ratios. The dominant  crystalline structures were 
Fe3O4 (comparison with reference value from JCPDS No.82-1533). The

particle size was 14.1 nm (4:3) and the smallest average particle size was 11.9 
nm (2:1). This result suggest that FeCl2・4H2O could influence the average size.  
2. Fig. 3 showd the near superparamagnetic property for four samples from magnetization 
curves. The saturation magnetization was about 57 emu/g for 2:1 ratio powder 
and about 63 emu/g for the other three powders.  
Conclusions 
In our examination, magnetic nanoparticles coated by lauric acid were investigated the 
respective influence of different physiological factors on heating ability to realize the 
characteristics of synthesized magnetic nanoparticles. The results showed that no influence 
from BSA or blood sugar, but, was greatly affected by a norm-saline solution environment 
due to lauric acid interaction with sodium cations. In addition, the heating capacity was 
reduced with increasing viscosity. Further experiments on magnetic nanoparticles 
embedded in PDMS were carried out and showed that the Brownian mechanism 
contributes ~53% to the total heating capacity. Thus, it suggested that the contribution 
from the Brownian mechanism reduced was major reason.

The heating ability of magnetic nanoparticles prepared in a 3:2 ratio was higher than 
others, as shown in Fig 4 and unaffected by the protein and blood sugar environment, 
as shown in Fig 5 and 6. Therefore it could act as a suitable thermal seed. However, 
aggregates of MNPs were formed in the electrolyte solutions and the heating abilities 
were dramatically reduced due to lauric acid interaction with sodium cations.. In 
addition, the heating abilities were reduced gradually with increasing visosity. Further 
test exhibited that the heating ability was from 53% Brownian mechanism. Such the 
result suggested that the influence of electrolyte and viscos environments on the 
heating ability were due to the reduced contribution from Brownian mechanism. The 
result suggested that synthesized MNPs generated much heat from Brownian 
mechanism and the usage of lauric acid were not the suitable conditions for 
preparations. For evaluating magnetic nanoparticles, this method provides a fast and 
low cost method for determining qualitative and quantitative information 
measurement for the effect of physiological interference and could greatly reduce the 
cost and time by in vitro or animal test.
