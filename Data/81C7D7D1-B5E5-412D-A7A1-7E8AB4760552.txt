linear encoder systems, and a PC； 2. Development of tool wear 
measurement system for a PCD and a micro end mill, 
respectively； 3. Fabrication of a micro-structure using a single 
crystal diamond (SCD) tool； 4. Design, fabrication, and the 
assembly test of a new precision micro-milling machine tool； 5. 
Integration of a CNC controller with the developed micro-milling 
machine tool； 6. Configuration of the matrix experiments for high 
speed micro-milling. The circular positioning tests with the radius 
of 10 μm、1 mm  and 7 mm using the developed stage have been 
tested, and the overall roundness error was about 0.059、0.083 and 
0.094  m, respectively. The shaping tests using a SCD tool under 
various combination of the depth of cut and cutting speed have been 
carried out. It revealed that the cutting speed had no great influence 
on the cutting force. The measured cutting forces for the depth of 
cut of 5, 10, 15 μm were 1.2 N, 1.6 N and 2.4 N, respectively. Base 
on these results, the developed micro machine tools will be applied 
to fabricate some precision micro parts in the future.

本子計畫之主要目的為於兩年期間研製兩台具有與微型三次元量測儀同型機台架構之
高精度奈米級微型工具機，可分別於該機台進行以鑽石刀具作微結構之鉋切加工及高速微
銑削。該 PC-based 微型工具機主要核心技術元件包括：安裝於精密花崗岩平台之 XY 共平
面平台、安裝於寶塔式(Pagoda)結構之主(Z)軸結構、雷射繞射式光學尺、驅動控制器與軟
體等。XY 共平面平台、繞射光學尺、驅動控制器與軟體乃由子計畫一所研製提供。研究
重點為利用 ELC2200 進行高精度奈米級微型工具機之 XY 共平面平台整合與測試；利用三

由於工業產品朝向輕薄短小的發展趨勢，3C 產業之蓬勃發展以及未來充滿生機之生技

視。事實上數十微米（μm）至數毫米（mm）尺寸之二次元／三次元微小元件已被廣泛應

另外的一個發展重點，則是感測元件微小化，因為微小後的感測元件熱常數很小不易
受到外界溫度干擾；元件共振頻率很高，工作頻帶加寬；且感知區間變小，空間解析度提
高。

費性電子產品及一般機械工業等方面。

6. 本研究計畫之目的: 奈米精度微型工具機之研製 
由於超精密加工機之價格，一般均在台幣千萬以上，且大部分為輸出管制品，有鑑於
此，本子計畫研製一經濟型且具奈米精度之微型工具機；此外，為了分別研究以鑽石刀具
鉋切不同材料及高速微銑切之加工研究，並考慮鑽石刀具夾持與高速主軸更換所造成之可
能誤差，故本計畫乃分別研製完成鑽石刀具鉋切微結構與高速微銑削之奈米精度微型工具

(vi) 控制理論介紹 
(1) PID 控制理論 
    在工程實際中，應用最為廣泛的調節器控制規律為比例、積分、微分控
制，簡稱 PID 控制，又稱 PID 調節。PID 控制器問世至今已有近 70 年歷史，
它以其結構簡單、穩定性好、工作可靠、調整方便而成為工業控制的主要技
 26
統的相對穩定度及穩態誤差。通常增益值變大時，可以增快系統的響應速度，
不過會降低系統的穩態誤差，甚至破壞系統的穩定度。 
微分增益控制（Derivative control） DK ： 
積分控制動作，如同在原點的位置加入一個極點，使系統的階數提高一階，
可消除穩態誤差，並且有利於雜訊的抑制。但是積分動作卻有可能使系統變為
不穩定，即使系統仍維持穩定，其暫態響應性能通常會變得較差。 
比例增益控制（Proportional control） PK ： 
微分控制動作可以改善系統的阻尼特性及暫態響應，並能增加系統的相對
穩定度。但是微分控制的動作不利於高頻雜訊干擾，且無法改善穩態誤差。  
而 PID 控制器的設計（ PK 、 IK 、 DK 的調整）有時需要依據經驗法則來當
調整的依據。而有關 PID 控制參數的調整已有許多的參數調整法被提出【40】，
其中最常被使用的是 Ziegler-Nichols 於 1942 年針對誤差積分準則（IAE）的性
能規格極小化，提出的最佳調整方法。其方法簡述如下：

(2) 多晶鑽石刀具面積量測 
分別讓兩點從最右側以及最左側往中間靠近，由於邊緣為白線，灰階值為
255，以此為條件，當兩點碰到灰階值為 255 的點便記錄下來，此兩點即為邊緣點，
藉由這兩個邊緣點便可計算出刀具中心線，由於影像邊緣是呈現一鋸齒狀，故以
中心線為基準，對兩邊的斜線各分別取 200 個點， 然後用最小平方法，分別對應
兩斜邊的最小平方線，以此這兩最小平方線可計算出多晶鑽石刀具之夾角。由於
鑽石為非常硬的物質，不容易磨耗，故面積量測不須計算整個多晶鑽石刀具，只
需計算刀尖的一小區塊即可，故以此這兩最小平方線交點為基準，劃出一個
50Pixel×50Pixel 的方框，接著計算方框內的刀具面積，即為所求，其計算法示意
 46
成的變化非常微小，可能是由於多晶鑽石刀具本身材料硬脆，加工材料又為質地較軟
的無氧銅的關係，故捨棄角度變化量當作磨耗檢測的標準，而以面積檢測為主。在進
行微鉋切前，其面積為 2985.1851 μm2，然後在微鉋切後，再對多晶鑽石刀具的刀腹進
行面積量測，面積為 2764.1975 μm2。多晶鑽石刀具在微鉋切前後的面積相減，即為磨
耗面積，其值為 220.9876 μm2。

結論 
經由兩年之研究，本計畫已完成一台PC-based高精度三軸以單晶鑽石刀具微鉋切之工
具機與一台高精度CNC高速微銑切工具機之設計、組裝與測試，並進行其相關實驗，完成
之工作項目如下所述： 
1. 本計劃已完成 ELC2200 控制器與高精度奈米級微型工具機之整合與測試，並進行半徑
分別為 10 μm、1 mm 和 7 mm 循圓定位實驗，以最適圓法計算出最大半徑值，得知其
循圓誤差值分別約 0.059、0.083 與 0.094 μm。 
2. 高速微切削黃銅實驗，並利用田口法找出最適於黃銅之切削參數，其參數為刀間距 15 
μm、主軸轉速 60000 RPM，進給速率 200 mm/min 及切削深度 36μm，未來將應用此參
數做為微型工具機系統進行高速微銑切之實驗依據。 
3. 本計劃開發多晶鑽石刀具與微銑刀之磨耗影像量測系統，進行微銑刀經高速銑削加工
後所產生之刀腹磨耗情況分析，以及多晶鑽石刀具經微鉋切加工後所產生之刀腹磨耗
情況以及角度變化，經實驗結果後，可得知微銑刀以及多晶鑽石刀具之刀腹磨耗影像
處理量測系統，經影像濾波、二值化、影像尋邊與細線化後，可求得實際刀腹面積；
此外，由於多晶鑽石刀具本身材料硬脆，不易磨耗，加工無氧銅此類較軟的材料，其
磨耗情況不明顯，無法詳細得到其加工參數與角度變化之間的關係。 
4. 本計劃已於高精度奈米級微型工具機進行單晶鑽石刀具之位置監測系統開發，未來將
繼續應用此系統於高精度奈米級微型工具機之刀具定位，並配合本計劃所開發之刀具
磨耗影像量測系統，進行刀具磨耗之即時檢測。 
5. 本計劃已於高精度奈米級微型工具機進行單晶鑽石刀具之微結構加工切削實驗分析研
究，經由實驗得知，本系統加工之定位精度為 99.520 μm，並利用微動力計於不同深度
量測其切削力如圖 25~37 所示，其分別測得加工深度為 5、10 與 15 μm 之切削力分別
為 1.2、1.6 與 2.4 N。 
6. 本計劃已額外開發完成另一高速微銑切之微型工具機之設計、製作與組裝測試，近期
將於該微型工具機進行高速微銑削實驗。

Development of a meso-scale machine tool and the preliminary cutting tests 
using a polycrystalline diamond tool 
 
Fang-Jung Shiou1, Kuang-Chao Fan2, Kai-Ming Pan1, Zhi-Yuan Ke2, and Kun-Jin Wu1 
1 Department of Mechanical Engineering, 
National Taiwan University of Science and Technology 
No. 43, Sec.4, Keelung Road, Taipei, 106, Taiwan (R.O.C) 
2 Department of Mechanical engineering, 
Nation Taiwan University 
No. 1, Sec. 4, Roosevelt Road, Taipei, 106, Taiwan (R.O.C.) 
shiou@mail.ntust.edu.tw 
 
Abstract 
This work presents the development of a 
meso-scale 3-axis milling machine with a nanometer 
resolution. Regarding the Z-axis design of the 
meso-scale 3-axis milling machine, it mainly includes a 
pagoda structure, the high speed air bearing spindle, two 
HR8 ultrasonic motors, a laser diffraction grating 
interferometer system (LDGI), and a coaxial 
counter-balance system for the spindle. The optimal 
geometrical dimensions of the pagoda structure have 
been determined by ANSYS software. According to the 
simulation results, the maximum static deformation 
along the z-axis of the pagoda was about 5.05 nm. The 
designed meso-scale 3-axis milling machine is equipped 
with an X-Y coplanar positioning stage with nanometer 
resolution, including the low-cost components and two 
ultrasonic motors, in order to reduce the complexity and 
error resulted from the combination of a long-stroke 
stage and short-travel stage. The coplanar stage 
developed by Nation Taiwan University was integrated 
with two laser diffraction grating interferometer system 
as displacement feedback sensors, so that a two-axis 
closed-loop control was possible. The positioning 
accuracy of the coplanar stage was about 50nm after 
error compensation using the gate mode, base on the test 
results. A circular positioning test with the radius of 100 
μm using the developed stage was tested, and the 
overall roundness error was about 4.01 μm based on the 
preliminary test results. A preliminary cutting tests for 
the aluminium alloy (5052) using a polycrystalline 
diamond tool have been investigated with respect to 
different depth of cut and cutting speed on the 
developed meso-scale machine tool. 
Keywords: Meso-scale 3-axis milling machine, pagoda 
structure, laser diffraction grating interferometer system 
(LDGI), X-Y coplanar positioning stage, polycrystalline 
diamond tool 
1. Introduction 
The demands in the micro-mechanical have been 
increased such as digital cameras, mobile phones, 
sensors, actuators and so on in the past decade [1]. The 
precision manufacturing process would be able to 
manufacture microstructures with 3D complex shapes or 
free-form surfaces [2]. Therefore, there are more and 
more researches focus on high precision machine tool 
design and manufacturing such as precision turning 
machine [3], precision grinding machine [4] and 
micro-milling machine [5]. 
When design a precision machine, four major 
subsystems have to be taken into account, including 
mechanical structure, drive, spindle and control system. 
The precision machine mechanical structure design and 
analysis have been discussed in [6]. 
Regarding diamond tool machining, J.H. Lee et 
al.[7] used diamond tool to fabricate v-groove on the 
optical fiber connector. Jiwang Yan et al. [8] used single 
crystal diamond tool for machining v-groove on an 
electroless-plated Nip surface and used as molds for 
hot-press glass. Gi Dae Kim et al. [9] discussed the new 
method using single crystal diamond tool to 
manufacture v-groove machining, in which an 
ultrasonic elliptical vibration method was applied to 
reduce the burrs formation and reduce the cutting force. 
The price of an ultra-precision manufacturing 
machine generally more than NTD 10 million, and most 
of them are export-restricted products. For that reason, 
the objective of this research is to develop an economy 
meso-scale 3-axis milling machine with a nanometer 
resolution that can be applied to do shaping and high 
speed milling machining. 
2. Design and analysis of the meso-scale 
3-axis milling machine tool 
The purpose of this study is to develop a 
meso-scale 3-axis machine tool with nanometer 
resolution. The schematic illustration of the meso-scale 
3-axis machine tool, the Z-axis design and its static 
deformation analysis, the design of high speed air 
baring spindle clamp and driving mechanism, the design 
of the XY-coplanar stage, the system integration and 
some test results of the assembled machine tool were 
introduced in the next sections.

2.2 Z-axis design and analysis 
2.2.1 Optimal geometrical dimensions of the pagoda 
structure design  The design of the structure of 
meso-scale machine tool must take into account the 
rigidity to withstand the weight of the spindle system. 
As a result, according to the former design of several 
different shapes of the machine tool structure, and using 
ANSYS finite element analysis software to analyze the 
best machine structure, the result of the pagoda-type 
structure was the most stable [10]. This work follows 
previous pagoda-type structure design, and makes some 
improvements design to clamp and integrate the new 
Z-axis for a high speed air bearing spindle. The optimal 
geometrical dimensions of the pagoda structure have 
been determined by ANSYS software. Figure 2 shows 
the new design of Z-axis of the granite pagoda. The 
optimal geometrical dimensions of the newly designed 
pagoda structure parameters are listed in Table 2.

4. Conclusion  
A prototype of the meso-scale 3-axis milling machine 
tool with a nanometer resolution has been newly 
developed in this work. Some preliminary test results of 
the coplanar stage have been carried out in this work. 
The maximum tilting angles of the new coplanar stage 
for X- and Y-axis within the travel of 22 mm, were 
about 5 and 4 arc-seconds, respectively. The open loop 
positioning error for X- and Y-axis within the travel of 
20 mm was about 0.205 μm. The closed loop 
positioning errors of the X- and Y-axis, were about 0.1 
μm within the travel of 11 mm. The roundness error of 
the preliminary circular positioning test was about 4.01 
μm. A preliminary cutting tests for the aluminium alloy 
(5052) using a polycrystalline diamond tool have been 
executed with respect to different depth of cut and 
cutting speed on the developed meso-scale machine tool. 
The induced cutting force measured by the micro 
dynamometer has also been investigated. The accuracy 
of the developed machine tool will be further improved 
in the future using an appropriate control strategy.
