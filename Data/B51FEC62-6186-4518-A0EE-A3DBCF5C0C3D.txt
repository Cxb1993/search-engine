壹、前言
根據內政部消防署統計[1]臺灣地區民
國 86-92 年間，每年平均發生一萬四千起火
災，總計損失 6 億 5 千多萬，多起高科技廠
房及辦公大樓發生火災，造成巨額財產損
失，對於人民生命和國家經濟等產生極大影
響，故對於國內結構物在火災後之結構公共
安全評估，為相當重要之研究議題。
混凝土由於具有低導熱性，因此也具有
良好的耐火性質，目前國內的住宅及商務建
築物主要係以鋼筋混凝土所建造，當火災發
生時，由於各建築物室內隔間與擺放物品不
同，使得混凝土材料在火場中有著不同程度
之損害。對於局部受損或火害較輕微的結構
構件，在適當的補強修復後都可繼續使用。
因此，決定火災後建築結構是否需拆除或經
適當修復補強後再使用，需要有一套快速且
具有適當可靠度的評估方法。
貳、試驗原理
混凝土係以水泥漿體與粗細骨材所組
成，兩者因高溫作用會有不同之變化。目前
用以估算火害後混凝土內部不同深度火害
溫度變化及影響深度之通用方法，大都為破
壞性檢測，包含鑽心切片吸水率試驗[2]、
劈裂強度[2-3]、燒失量法[4]、X光繞射法
[5]、壓汞孔隙試驗[6]，上述方法在取樣及
試驗過程中皆較為費時。
沈等學者[7]曾經以有限元素模式分析
方法，針對鋼筋混凝土梁柱構件，模擬在不
同最高溫度及不同火害延時作用後，其內部
之溫度變化，結果顯示混凝土在受高溫作用
時，內部溫度會隨著高溫面距離而減少，即
形成一溫度梯度，因此預期將造成混凝土由
原本可視為均質材料，將形成層狀結構
(layered structure)，而本研究採用表面波譜
法 及 單 邊 波 速 量 測 法 (one-sided wave
velocity)之兩種不同應力波檢測法，正適合
評估這種層狀構造物的材料性質變化。而表
面波譜法(Spectral Analysis of Surface Wave,
SASW)為其中一種應力波檢測方法，並適用
於檢測層狀結構物[8-15]。
SASW法係於偵測物之表面安裝至少
兩個收波器，使用鐵鎚敲擊偵測物表面，使
其產生應力波在偵測物體內傳播，再利用頻
率域法推求雷利波 (Raleigh wave)相速度
(phase velocity) 與 視 波 長 (apparent
wavelength)間之關係，以建立受測體雷利波
之頻散曲線(dispersion curve)，再經由頻散
曲線反算程式，計算受測體之剪力波速，並
反算推估受測體內之材料性質（彈性模數及
其層厚等動態參數）等特性。
此法最早由Jones應用於探測土層分
佈、深度、動態剪力模數等[8]。在國內外
之研究中被廣泛應用於探討土層動態特
性，此方法也用在量測表層金屬的塗覆厚
度、探測地下管線參數、評估道路瀝青舖面
品質等評估各種層狀材料的性質、厚度與地
下物位置探測；此方法主要觀念為以表面波
（R 波）在一定波長對應深度內傳播及多

RETROFITTING MATERIAL FOR CONCRETE MEMBERS 
 
Ta-Peng Chang(1), Bo-Tsun Chen(2), Chao-Lung Hwang(1), Jeng-Ywan Shih(2), Chung-Sheng 
Wu(2)
 
(1) Professor, Dept. of Construction Eng., National Taiwan Univ. of Science and Tech., Taipei, 
Taiwan, R.O.C. 
 
(2) Graduate student, Dept. of Construction Eng., National Taiwan Univ. of Science and Tech., 
Taipei, Taiwan, R.O.C. 
 
 
 
Abstract 
 
In this study, the performance of reactive powder concrete (RPC) used as a retrofitting material 
is evaluated by the ultimate compressive strengths and flexural strengths (moduli of rupture) of the 
resulting composite concrete specimens.  Experimental results show that flows of fresh RPC are 
between 155 to 205%.  Typical average compressive strength of RPC cylinder increases from 
110.7 MPa at age of 7 days to 157.9 MPa at 28 days with moist-curing and no applied pressure.  
When the cylindrical concrete specimens were wrapped with 10 and 20 mm thicknesses of RPC 
layers, the average increases of compressive strengths for such composite concrete members are 
28.7% and 40.0%, respectively.  For beam concrete specimen retrofitted by the RPC plates of 10 
and 20 mm thickness, respectively, the increase of moduli of rupture are 89.2% and 121%, 
respectively.  More significant strengthening effects of RPC retrofit on the flexural behavior than 
those on compressive behavior are observed. 
1. INTRODUCTION 
The reactive powder concrete (RPC) has been well-known as a high-performance material with 
high strength, modulus of elasticity, toughness, tightness, and durability since its debut to public in 
1994 [1-2].  As a result, it has been used for applications to an industrial scale of real construction, 
for examples, the storage of nuclear waste [3], the pedestrian/bikeway bridge [4-6], etc., and to the 
structural members like beam [7], composite slabs [8].  Although there are plenty of other various 
prospective applications with this super material, its utilization is still limited due to the 
consideration on the cost-effectiveness provided an excessive amount of RPC is required to 
complete the construction task.  Along this line, one of other possible economic and effective 
applications is in the field of strengthening and rehabilitation of concrete structures. This study 
presents the preliminary evaluation on the performance of RPC used as a retrofitting material.

(CFRP) wrap which has a significant enhancement to the strength, ductility, and energy absorption

the cylindrical concrete specimen also provides the additional capability to sustain the compressive

2.3 Preparation of specimens and experimental methods 
Dry RPC components of cement, quartz sand, silica fume and quartz powder were mixed for 3 
to 5 minutes with low-speed gear, then total pre-mixed water and SP were added and mixed for 
another 2 to 3 minutes to let the resulting paste obtain a proper consistency and flowability.  The 
steel fibers are then spread into the paste and mixed for 2 minutes with middle-speed gear to finish 
the mixing of RPC.  The fresh RPC was cast into cylindrical paper molds of 50 mm in diameter 
and 100 mm long and cured in ambient laboratory environment for 24 hours.  Afterwards, these 
RPC cylindrical specimens were demolded and cured with two kinds of curing treatment: in a 
steaming chamber at a temperature of 85oC for three days; in an ambient temperature for three days.  
Then they were submerged in the saturated lime water until the day of testing.  Normally, 
additional pressure was applied to the fresh RPC during setting with elevated temperature of 90oC 
or between 250-400oC in order to improve its strengths [1-2].  But on account for the compatibility 
of material properties between the base NC member and retrofitting RPC as stated previously, the 
traditional RPC curing treatment was modified with a lower curing temperature of 85oC or an 
ambient temperature and without applied pressure. 
On the other hand, two types of NC specimens were cast: cylindrical specimen for compressive 
strength test and beam specimen for flexural strength test as shown in Fig. 1(a).  Three different 
sizes of cylindrical specimens were cast: φ70×200 mm, φ80×200 mm and φ100×200 mm according 
to the ASTM C 31 procedure.  Three different sizes of beam specimens were also cast: 
130×150×500 mm, 140×150×500 mm and 150×150×500 mm following the procedure of ASTM C 
78.  All NC specimens were moist cured for at least 28 days.  Meanwhile, two kinds of thin plate 
specimen of RPC were also cast as the retrofitting material: 10×150×500 mm and 20×150×500 mm.  
The outer circumferential layers of these two smaller sizes of cylindrical NC specimens of φ70×200 
mm and φ80×200 mm were then cast by fresh RPC with thickness of 15 mm and 10 mm, 
respectively, such that both of their final dimensions become φ100×200 mm, and then moist cured 
in ambient temperature.  Similarly, the bottom surface of these two smaller NC beam specimens of 
130×150×500 mm and 140×150×500 mm were bonded with RPC plate sheets of 20×150×500 mm 
and 10×150×500 mm, respectively, using an epoxy-based adhesive.  Both of their final sizes also 
become the standard size of 150×150×500 mm for flexural test.   
The flowability test of RPC was conducted by the flow table method modified from the 
procedure of ASTM C 1437.  This procedure was originally designed for the measurement of 
consistency of mortars expressed as mortar flow, defined as the percentage of the spread diameter of 
mortar sample after the table is dropped 25 times in 15 seconds over the original bottom diameter of 
truncated cone sample.  For some of the high flowability of RPC, the numbers of drops may not be 
necessary.  The compressive test of RPC and NC cylinders followed ASTM C 192 and C 617.

3.3 Cylindrical specimen strengthened by RPC 
As a result of the extra bonding capability between the fiber and concrete, the incorporation of 
steel fiber in RPC increases its tensile strength and resistance to the impact loading such that an 
original brittle failure mode is transformed into the ductile mode for RPC. Normally, the 
strengthening mechanism of cylindrical specimen wrapped by an outer ring layer of retrofitting 
material can be divided into two parts: axial composite effect and circumferential confining effect.  
In this study, since the Poisson’s ratios of RPC and NC are 0.22 and 0.20, respectively, the 
circumferential confining effect is not significant.  Thus, the concept of transformed composite 
section was used to account for axial composite effect due to strengthening layer of RPC.  Thus,
