一、 前言 
近年來，先進國家已將垃圾焚化灰渣視為一種資源，予以資源化再利用，不但減少了
灰渣掩埋的處理成本，同時也增加了經濟效益。我國環保署將都市垃圾焚化底渣歸為一般
廢棄物，可進入衛生掩埋場掩埋，但在新設衛生掩埋場越來越困難的情況下，資源再生利
用實為未來重要趨勢。環保署自去年起推動垃圾焚化底渣再利用，並期以四年再利用率達
80%為目標。 
都市垃圾焚化底渣之重金屬溶出量較少，而被視為一般廢棄物，但重金屬會因底渣之
粒徑大小而有不同之分布，較小粒徑之部分因含有較高之重金屬溶出量，具有相當之危害
潛勢。本研究團隊多年來在研究都市焚化灰渣之無害穩定化中發現，以水洗方式可去除可
溶性物質，增加後續處理之穩定性。焚化底渣約占 90%的粗粒徑部份，目前廣泛作為路基、
覆土、堤防等材料之低經濟價值的再生利用。本研究團隊累積多年的灰渣處理研究經驗，
發展出利用粗粒徑焚化底渣燒結再生成為常重骨材之技術，研究結果顯示再生產品可做為
結構性骨材使用。為了更進一步提高再生骨材使用範圍與其經濟價值，以粗粒徑焚化底渣
作為研究材料，探討其受熱發泡條件與燒製為高強度輕質骨材的成效。 
 
二、 文獻回顧 
歐美等國家在二十世紀初即開始生產人造輕質骨材，1917 年美國首先研究輕質骨材之
特性，利用膨脹性黏土及頁岩等原料，在高溫條件下其內部產生氣體所燒成的小孔。英國
於 1954 年利用倫敦黏土陸續燒製輕質骨材，並應用於建築物及橋樑工程。日本則於 1968
年首次使用膨脹性頁岩燒製輕質骨材。 
國內針對於輕質骨材的研發與應用，王氏(1971)以膨脹性頁岩材料利用旋窯燒製技術，
開創國內研製輕質骨材之先驅；高氏(1987)則採用破碎頁岩材料，燒製非造粒型的輕質骨
材。一般而言主要是以頁岩、火山玻璃石、黏土及生雲母等天然資源材料，經過高溫燒製
而成的膨脹黏土、膨脹頁岩、珠石及蛭石等，稱為改良型人造輕質骨材。但因改良型的輕
質骨材所使用之材料仍需採掘天然礦物資源，對於自然資源缺乏的台灣，實非長久之計，
基於自然資源保育、替代材料開發及環保等觀點考量，遂發展出利用工業製程的廢棄物，
作為原料燒製為輕質骨材，如 Zhang 及 Gjorv (1991)，分別以膨脹性黏土粉及煤灰、飛灰等
材料，以燒結法製造輕質骨材。國內在 1990 年時，台灣科技大學營建工程系黃教授開發利
用飛灰與底灰的冷結型輕質骨材造粒技術(專利字號 54384)，許氏(1991)利用底渣與飛灰冷
結型造粒技術製成輕質骨材，首開工業廢棄物再利用的途徑。 
近年來，國內多位學者及廠商利用水庫淤泥，或工業污泥混合水庫淤泥，進行造粒與
高溫燒結骨材，並有具體不錯成果。基於天然輕質與常重骨材之天然料源逐漸匱乏，且因
經濟發展下產生大量的工業廢棄物，國內以煤灰及爐石等廢棄資材作為原料之研究，已累
積相當多的實用成果與經驗，民眾接受度普遍提昇，顯見以廢棄資材產製輕質化建材具有
市場潛力及工程優越性。張氏(1999)、余氏(2000)及楊氏(2001)等人研究指出都市下水污泥
焚化灰具有燒製輕質骨材之潛力。陳氏(2003)則以水庫淤泥添加料方式燒製輕質骨材，並成
功燒結具有工程價值輕質骨材。 
早期之研究皆以自然資源的天然岩石礦物(浮石、溶岩、膨脹黏土)做為材料來燒製輕質
骨材，近十年來逐漸發展利用以水庫淤泥、淨水污泥、下水污泥與飛灰等廢棄材料作為燒
製再生輕質骨材之研究，惟獨缺乏利用焚化底渣做為燒製輕質骨材之主體材料研究。 
 
三、 研究目的 
本研究所使用的粗底渣先經過水洗前處理，主要目的是為了洗去底渣中的鹼性氯化
物、硫酸鹽類和未完全燃燒之有機物，以免影響再利用產品的材料性質。由於在 1,000~1,350 
oC 高溫時 Fe2O3 會釋出 O2、CO、CO2 等氣體(洪氏，2005)，使燒結體膨脹進而降低骨材密
度，而 SiO2 的添加能夠將氣體包覆在骨材內，且幫助骨材表面的熔融使表面緻密無孔隙，

 (b)底渣熱重分析 
一般而言於室溫到 105℃為吸附水(hygroscopic water)揮發，其存在礦物的宏觀細孔中，
藉由吸附力連接於孔壁。而 350~650℃為有機物燃燒，硫化物及硫酸鹽分解為 SO2 ；大於
450℃則為化學鍵結水(chemically combined water)之揮發，以 H2O 型態或 OH-或 H+的形態
與化合物結合。600~750℃為 CaCO3 分解產生 CO2；大於 700℃會產生新結晶，Al2O3 和 SiO2
化合產生複雜的矽鋁酸鹽相。 
文獻中指出輕質骨材於高溫時膨脹溢出之氣體以 CO2、O2 及 CO 為主， 於粗底渣之
TG/DTA(圖 1)中，在 105~800℃產生較大之熱失重，應為化學鍵結水、有機質、碳酸鹽等
產生揮發或分解造成重量損失。於最初 100℃的重量損失原因為內部水分蒸散，而在 550℃
的重量損失於文獻中指出大多為 CO2 釋放出來的關係。於 600～750℃之間，在 700℃左右
有一個明顯的吸熱峰，造成的主要原因為 CaCO3 轉變成 CaO 伴隨著 CO2 的釋出。於 1,100℃
的重量損失則可推測為底渣已達玻璃化的現象，斷定此點應為其熔融點。

(c)粗粒徑底渣之粒徑分析 
粗底渣用於燒結前之前處理除了兩段式水洗、顎碎之外，由於其粒徑仍然過大，而後
又將其濕式球磨，目的為使粒徑均勻，避免燒結時因粒徑過大降低燒結效果。由圖 2 中觀
察出球磨後粒徑大都集中於 20 μm 左右，由肉眼觀察出磨後大都呈泥狀，而烘乾後呈細小
的粉塵狀，已達粒徑均勻的粉末狀，而當粒徑分布愈廣者燒結時的收縮率較穩定且孔隙較
小，可在高溫下得到較均勻的晶相分布，且經由球磨可以降低燒結溫度減小能量的損耗。

(b) 吸水率  
骨材的含水狀態常受環境乾濕、溫度變化等之影響，故於實際施工拌和時必須修正，
否則將對混凝土之均勻性及穩定性會造成不良的影響。雖然沒有明確指出混凝土強度與骨
材吸水率的關係式，但骨材顆粒表面存在的孔洞將會影響骨材與水泥漿的鍵結，進而影響
混凝土強度，使得新拌輕質混凝土的工作度降低，對硬固混凝的抗拉強度、抗凍性及保熱
性等性能有不利的影響。 
輕質骨材的吸水率較一般骨材高，此因多孔性質的關係，骨材表面孔隙的存在會增加
吸水率、降低其強度和硬度，對混凝土工作性不利，目前國內對於結構用輕質骨材 1 hr 吸
水率是規定須小於 10%。 
骨材 1 小時吸水率如圖 4 所示，添加 1 克和 2.5 克 SiO2 吸水率皆小於 10%，符合輕質
骨材吸水率標準。添加較多量的 SiO2 因其試體內部的連通狀孔隙減少且變小而朝緻密化發
展，因此骨材密度增加、吸水率則因此降低。 
而未添加 SiO2 、Fe2O3 添加 0.1~1 克之燒結體雖然表面已達熔融狀，但因包覆力不佳
導致試體表面有許多孔洞，雖然密度小但吸水率高，在添加 0.1~0.4 克時吸水率都大於 10%
不適用做為結構性輕質骨材。而添加 5 克 SiO2 試體表面緻密且內部連通狀孔隙少，故吸水
率皆低於 1%，但其密度未小於 1.6 g/cm3 不符合輕質骨材的規範，故不予以討論。整體而
言以顆粒密度和吸水率兩者來看，較適合做結構性輕質骨材者為添加 1 和 2.5 克 SiO2所燒
結出的試體，而添加 2.5 克 SiO2 者因添加較多量 SiO2 以至於熔融點提高，試體表面來不及
包覆產生的氣體導致密度提高，雖然氣體逸散但粒子與粒子之頸部成長現象持續進行，燒
成試體呈現緻密且吸水率低的現象。由圖八可以發現吸水率的整體趨勢是 SiO2 的量添加越
多會造成吸水率明顯降低。

八、計畫成果自評 
本研究內容與原計畫相符，探討粗粒徑焚化底渣受熱膨脹特性和焚化底渣添加最適合
發泡種類之燒製輕質骨材成效，以及輕質骨材燒結溫度、燒結時間與發泡劑對於底渣資源
化影響關係。本研究已確立添發泡劑類與不同配比之燒製輕質骨材最佳範圍並獲得粗底渣
依不同燒製溫度及時間之膨脹發泡特性。而本計畫在學術研究之貢獻 
(1) 水洗程序與底渣粒徑大小之重金屬含量關係探討。 
(2) 篩選適合之焚化底渣並燒製輕質骨材之最佳溫度與時間條件。 
(4) 探討燒成反應過程之元素成分環境、pH 環境、濃度環境、時間變化、重金屬物種變化。 
(5) 研究最佳發泡劑種類添加底渣，在不同燒成溫度操作條件下，試體受熱膨脹現象之影響。 
在國家發展及其他應用方面預期之貢獻 
(1) 將都市垃圾焚化底渣資源化，解決大量廢棄物之處理處置問題。 
(2) 協助解決國內大量底渣之處置問題，並將提高資源化經濟效益。

ABSTRACT 
This study discussed the feasibility of sintering municipal solid waste incinerator 
bottom ash to produce lightweight aggregate. This recycling process included the 
water-extracting, screening, wet-milling, vesicant-adding and sintering. In addition, 
adding 0.1-0.4 g Fe2O3 into 10 g coarse grain (particle size higher than 74.5μm) was 
found to generate lightweight aggregate with density lower than 1.6 g/cm3 and 
compressive strength greater then 100 kgf/cm2 when the sintering temperature was 
1100 oC and sintering time was 30 minutes. Therefore, the coarse grain of municipal 
solid waste incinerator bottom ash is shown to have a good potential material to be 
sintered into a lightweight aggregate product. 
Keywords ： Coarse grain MSWI bottom ash, lightweight aggregates, sintering, 
water-wash, foaming agent 
 
INTRODUCTION 
With high population density and limited available land in Taiwan, the handling of 
municipal solid waste incinerator (MSWI) ash has become increasingly important. 
There are currently 23 incineration plants producing 1,024,208 tons of ash in 2005, of 
which 86,000 tons was bottom ash. The Environmental protection agency in Taiwan 
(EPA) has categorized bottom ash as general industrial waste and thus can be directly 
disposed in incinerator ash landfill sites. However, with greater focus on waste 
recycling, the recycling of incinerator bottom ash (IBA) has gained important status in

Analysis 
The physical and chemical properties of the bottom ash and sintered samples were 
investigated and analyzed. Physical properties investigated included moisture content, 
weight loss, and bulk density.  The chemical properties studied here were chemical 
compositions, heavy metal contents, and crystalline phases. Following the USEPA 
SW3050 method, the bottom ash was digested by HNO3/HClO4/HF and analyzed for 
its main chemical constituents (Si, Al, Fe, and Ca, for example) by inductively 
coupled plasma atomic emission spectroscopy (ICP-AES, Ultima-2000, JOBIN 
YVON HORIBA). Thermal analysis (TG/DTA) and X-ray powder diffraction (XPRD) 
were used to study the crystalline phases of the coarse grain and fine grain bottom ash; 
their chemical compositions were identified by comparing the results with the JCPDS 
database. The morphology of lightweight aggregate was studied using scanning 
electron microscopy (SEM, LV-SEM/EDS, JSM - 5400). The leaching characteristics 
of heavy metals were determined using by the toxicity characteristic leaching 
procedure (TCLP) according to SW846-1311, USEPA.  
 
RESULTS AND DISCUSSION 
 
Basic characteristics of coarse and fine grain bottom ash 
 
Lightweight aggregates made using the coarse grain bottom ash were analyzed for 
chemical composition after washing and screening. The chemical compositions of the 
coarse and fine grain bottom ash were analyzed by ICP and the results are shown in 
Table 1. The main constituents of the coarse grain bottom ash were Si, Fe, and Al 
accounting for 19.17%, 5.32%, and 4.14%, respectively, significantly higher than 
those of its fine grain counterpart. These elements are known to be beneficial to the 
sintering of aggregates. On the other hand, Ca made up the largest percentage (35.95 
%) in the fine grain bottom ash. During sintering, high Ca content led to insufficient 
melting and collapse on the aggregate surface. It is therefore clear that the fine grain 
bottom ash was not suitable for sintering in its primitive form and modification and 
certain additives were necessary before reutilization. Consequently, the bottom ash 
used in the present study was first screened to separate the fine grain bottom ash and 
the resulting coarse grain bottom ash was then modified with Fe2O3 and sintered to 
form lightweight aggregates.

Density  
The density of the sintered samples with 0.1~1 g of Fe2O3 addition is shown in Figure 
6, in which those with 0.1~0.4 g Fe2O3 addition had a density less than 1.6 g/cm3. The 
results also indicate that higher Fe2O3 addition resulted in higher density since 
excessive Fe2O3 not only enhanced the strength of samples but also increased their 
sintering temperature. Since higher sintering temperature could dissipate the gas on 
the surface before liquefying occurs, and thus little gas was trapped within samples 
regardless of the amount of gas produced, the necks between particles could further 
grow after the escape of gas, leading to a higher density of the sintered samples.

Compressive strength 
The shaping process for lightweight aggregates in the present study was conducted 
manually and the resulting spherical samples were then sintered. However, since the 
manual production rate was limited and spherical samples were not suitable for 
compressive testing, press molding was used for shaping to provide cylindrical 
samples for compressive testing. In general, lightweight aggregates are porous and 
thus have a lower strength. However, with lightweight aggregates, it is easier for 
cement mortars or hydration products to fill in the cavities or large pores on the 
surfaces to form a stronger adhesion between aggregates and concrete and in turn, a 
higher chemical bonding between them. The strength of lightweight aggregates has 
been stipulated to be between 20~850 kgf/cm2 in ROC standards. For the general 
application of aggregates, those with a compressive strength higher than 1,000psi (70 
kgf/cm2) are considered highly useful, falling into the category of medium strength 
aggregates. Those with a compressive strength over 2,500psi (175 kgf/cm2) are high 
strength aggregates, which can be used in structural applications12. In the present 
study, lightweight aggregates with 0.1 g Fe2O3 addition had a somewhat low 
compressive strength; however, those with over 0.2 g Fe2O3 addition had a 
compressive strength over 100 kgf/cm2, which can be used as aggregates in concrete 
for insulating purposes. Figure 8 also shows several sintered aggregates with strength 
higher than 175 kgf/cm2, indicating that the coarse grain bottom ash had a great 
potential to be sintered into lightweight aggregates with low density and high 
strength.

absorption and thus decreased strength and hardness.

CONCLUSIONS 
 
(1) MSWI bottom ash was first water washed to remove soluble salts and then 
screened to separate the coarse and fine grain bottom ash fractions. ICP element 
analysis showed that the major elements present in the fine grain bottom ash were 
Ca (35.95%)、Si(7.89%)、Fe(2.17%) and in the coarse grain bottom ash, Si 
(19.1%)、Ca(18.58%)、Fe(5.32%).  
 
(2) From the TD/DTA study, the melting point of the coarse grain bottom ash was 
1100 oC and the fine grain counterpart showed no sign of melting until 1300 oC. 
Therefore, the sintering temperature of the fine grain bottom ash was higher than 
its coarse grain counterpart. Consequently, the coarse grain bottom ash was 
favorably disposed to be sintered as lightweight aggregates in terms of sintering 
temperature. 
 
(3)The bottom ash with the addition of 0.1~0.4 g Fe2O3 can be sintered to produce 
lightweight aggregates with density lower than 1.6 g/cm3, indicating that small 
Fe2O3 addition was able to encourage foaming; however, the water absorption rate 
was somewhat too high. The bottom ash, with over 0.5 g Fe2O3 being added, 
showed a higher density of the aggregate, but the water absorption rate went below 
10%. Future study will further examine the effects of SiO2 in enhancing the 
surface denseness to further reduce the water absorption rate of aggregates. 
 
(4) In general, most sample exhibited a compressive strength higher than 100 kgf/cm2 
with some exceeding over 175 kgf/cm2, which can be used as raw material for 
lightweight aggregates in concrete.
