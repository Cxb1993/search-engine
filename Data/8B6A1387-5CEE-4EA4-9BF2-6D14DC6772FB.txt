2一、摘要
本研究針對具矽酸鈣板外覆材及底部錨定之冷軋型鋼牆進行試驗與研究，試體使用的矽酸鈣板分
為 9 mm 及 12 mm 兩種厚度，並以牆體底部二端有無錨定裝置(hold down)及不同的加載方法(循環載重
與直剪載重)進行比較。
而根據實驗結果，討論其極限強度、勁度、勁度衰退、延性比(μ)、結構系統過強因子(Ω)、韌性容
量(R)和試體破壞的形式。實驗結果發現底部二端有無錨定對於試體的極限強度影響約兩倍以上；外覆
材的厚度越厚其所能承受的剪力強度越高，雙面外覆材所能抵抗的側向力相對的也比單面外覆材來的
高。對於具有錨定裝置的牆體比較其循環載重與直剪載重，發現其抵抗剪力的強度差異不大，加裝錨
定裝置對於牆體的極限強度、勁度、延性比皆有較好的貢獻。
關鍵字：冷軋型鋼牆體、剪力實驗、循環載重、延性比、韌性容量
Abstract
This research is concentrated on the strength and behavior of the cold-formed steel wall frames anchored
at base corners and sheathed with calcium silicate board under monotonic shear and cyclic loads. The test
specimens were assembled with two different thicknesses of calcium silicate board, 9 mm and 12 mm, and
one-side or two-side of sheathing attachment.
The ultimate strength, stifness, stifness dissipation, ductility ratio (μ), structure overstrength factor (Ω), 
and ductility (R) of each test specimen were discussed according the experimental results. Test results show
that the shear strength of the specimen anchored at base corners is more than twice the shear strength of the
specimen without hold down device. The thicker of the calcium silicate board sheathing is used, the higher
shear resisting strength of wall frames is obtained. In addition, the wall frames with two-side calcium silicate
board provide higher lateral resistance than those attached with one-side sheathing. There was a slight
difference of tested ultimate loads for test specimens subjected to either monotonic shear load or cyclic load.
It was found that the test wall frames anchored at base corners have better values of ultimate strength,
stiffness, and ductility ratio as comparing to the specimens without hold down device.
Keywords: cold-formed steel wall frame, shear test, cyclic loading, ductility ratio, ductility
二、前言
在歐美、日本、澳洲等國家對於低層建築的結構型態，已發展使用冷軋型鋼結構來替代過去常應
用的木構造及鋼筋混凝土結構。輕型鋼構冷軋型鋼構造在設計上可分為：構架式結構系統、牆板式結
構系統等，在輕型鋼構建築型態中又以牆板式結構系統為設計主流，在以牆板式結構系統為主的冷軋
型鋼構造中，結構牆體即是主要之受力單元，冷軋型鋼牆體除了可承受垂直方向之承載力外，亦可抵
抗橫向的地震力與風力。雖然國外冷軋型鋼建築已有許多的研究成果與規範，但國內的設計規範或
結構設計直接引用國外的研究與技術，較不符國內實際工程的施工狀況，因此國內在冷軋型鋼建築
的相關設計參數則無跡可循，因此本研究的成果乃嘗試提供冷軋型鋼建築在設計時的參考依據。
配置剪力牆之結構比純構架式結構具有較好之耐震特性，可以有效抵抗地震力的作用。根據過去
4子、延性修正因子、韌性容量等。
2.2 研究內容
一般而言，牆構造就功能而言，可分隔間牆與外牆，而隔間牆則可分為承載型與非承載型(承重
牆與分間牆)。在目前國內建築與營造的設計生態，通常皆不考慮牆體可否提供結構強度(抗壓、抗彎
或抗剪)，在分析或設計時大都以樑柱系統為考慮設計承載能力的對象。在 921 地震對建築物破壞現
象中發現，事實上，牆體提供結構物相當的支撐能力。因此，相信深入探討與暸解冷軋型鋼牆體系統
對結構物強度的影響，對於國內在冷軋型鋼相關研究領域上有所加強，對於建築設計業界將可提供一
有效率且經濟的設計方向。本研究在分析上具有相當之挑戰性，希望藉由實驗，尋找出結構之行為模
式，但在分析此類結構體之問題時，除非藉由實驗之驗證與相關推導等，否則較難以予分析計算，所
以本計畫的研究方式包括了分析與實驗二大重點。而本計畫的進行可以推展的目標為：研究冷軋型鋼
牆體受橫向力狀況下各項行為表現(如延性比、超強因子、延性修正因子、韌性容量與各參數間之影
響等)；探討外覆材及其影響參數對牆體結構行為與其強度之影響；提供牆體之剪力計算流程與預估
強度，以提供設計者或業者在計算上之參考；提出牆體其耐震能力之佐證，以供學術與業界對於建築
設計之考量；牆體破壞行為模擬。

鋼片斜撐，兩側單根立柱。CD1 為雙面鋼片斜撐，兩側雙根立柱，四個角落皆有角條(Bracket)，如圖 1

最大剪力的能力都小於其他類型的鋼片斜撐，而且因為其他類型的鋼片斜撐框架的有較大的柔度，才
能使鋼片斜撐到達最大的側向位移；鋼片斜撐接合在有雙根兩側立柱的內部立柱與外部立柱，會影響
他的強度，接合在內部立柱會比接合在外部立柱抵抗較高的剪力強度，底部槽鋼與底梁的錨定(Hold
down)，而錨定冷軋型框架試體內部比錨定在試體外部所抵抗的剪力強度較大。

5.4 延性比
本研究計算延性比採用ECCS(European Convention for Construction Steelwork)及AISI (American Iron
and steel Institute)的建議來計算，其定義為極限位移除以降伏位移(Du/Dy)，延性比的值越大則代表牆體延
性越好，地震發生時提供較好的耐震效果。表2為採用ECCS的方式所得之延性比，表3為採用AISI的方式
所得之延性比。比較先前國科會研究(NSC-97-2221-E-324-049)【9】與本研究的延性比，在直剪載重試驗
中發現具hold down的牆體延性比相差5%~14%，無hold down的試體延性比相差11%~37%；循環載重試驗
發現具hold down的牆體延性比相差18%~23%，無hold down的試體延性比相差10%~35%。ECCS與AISI
所計算的延性比發現有裝設hold down的牆體平均值比沒有裝設hold down的延性比大，可知道有裝設hold
down的牆體可以提高延性比，且雙面外覆材的牆體的延性比也比單面外覆材之試體好。其中ECCS比AISI
延性比的平均值小，故較為保守。

In this study, cold-formed steel wall frames sheathed with one-side or two-side calcium silicate
board were tested to evaluate the structural behavior of failure type, ultimate strength, and
ductility ratio when subjected to monotonic shear load and cyclic load. This research finding
can be used as a design reference for applicable specification.
2. EXPERIMENTAL INVESTIGATION
2.1 Materials
The cold-formed steel wall frame assemblies contain three kinds of materials, i.e.,
cold-formed steel sections, calcium silicate board sheathing, and self-drilling screw. The
cold-formed steel sections designated as SSC 400 conform to Chinese National Standard No.
6183 [4] for general light-gauge steel. The yield strength, Fy, and tensile strength, Fu, of CFS
sections were obtained from the tensile test and were equal to 330.1 MPa and 414.5 MPa,
respectively. The sheathing material of calcium silicate board having two different thicknesses
(9 mm and 12 mm) complies with CNS 13777 [5]. Typical No.10 self-drilling screws as given
in AISI [6] were used to connect the sheathing and wall frame. The minimum screw length and
spacing of screws were provided to meet the requirements.
2.2 Test specimens
Cold-formed steel studs, 95 mm x 65 mm x 12 mm x 1.6 mm, were used to assemble the wall
frame test specimens as shown in Figure 1(a). These sections were perforated with web
openings, 39 mm x 39 mm @ 50 cm o.c., to provide space for utility conduits. Two studs
connected back to back were used at two sides of the wall frame. The studs were placed
perpendicularly into top and bottom channel tracks as shown in Figure 1(b).

3.1 Failure types of test specimens
Four different types of failure of test specimens were observed in this study, i.e., cracking of
sheathing, peeling of sheathing, local buckling on top track, and bearing failure of sheathing
and shear failure of screw. Detailed description of failure types is summarized as follows:
(1) Cracking of sheathing.
When tested wall specimen was applied by monotonic shear loads, it resisted not only the
shear force but also additional compression or tension forces due to the moment action. It is
because the test specimen was anchored at bottom corners and the sheathing board was
connected by self-drilling screws. Consequently, the lower portion of sheathing was subjected
to tension force which caused crack at the board around the connected screw. The test
specimen failed when the cracking extended further to the bottom screw as shown in Figure 6.
(2) Peeling of sheathing
The anchored test specimens can resist the overturning moment produced from the applied
lateral loads. However, the attached sheathing was not strong enough to resist the shear
force transferred from the wall frame. Thus, the sheathing material was peeled off from the
wall frame end stud as shown in Figure 7. This type of failure occurred for test specimen with
9 mm thick and one-side sheathing.
Figure 6: Cracking of sheathing (HM-C12-HO-2) Figure 7: Peeling of sheathing (HM-C09-HO-1)
(3) Local buckling on top channel track.
The top channel track subjected to compressive force when the test specimen induced
bending moment under the applied lateral loads. This channel section is not capable to take
such force, and the channel web deformed shape caused by the local buckling.
(4) Bearing failure of sheathing and shear failure of screw.
The sheathing material was attached to the cold-formed steel wall frame by using self-drilling
screws. The applied forces were transferred between the steel frame and sheathing through
these screws. After applying further cyclic loads, the sheathing was damaged at the
connection area. In addition, the self-drilling screw became misshaped when additional load is
applied. The test specimen finally failed by fracture of sheathing.

4. CONCLUSIONS
A total of eight test specimens of cold-formed steel wall frames were conducted in this study,
which included three tests subjected to monotonic shear loads and five tests subjected to
cyclic loads. Calcium silicate board sheathing with two different thicknesses, 9 mm and 12
mm, and having one-side or two-side sheathing were used to assemble the test wall frames.
Based on the test results, the failure types, the ultimate loads, and the ductility ratio of test
specimens were discussed in detail. The following conclusions were made.
(1) The wall frames with two-side calcium silicate board provide higher lateral resistance and
ductility ratio than those attached with one-side sheathing
(2) The thicker of the calcium silicate board sheathing is used, the higher shear resisting
strength of wall frames is obtained for both monotonic and cyclic loading conditions.
(3) For test specimens under cyclic loads, the ultimate loads are similar to those tested under
the monotonic shear loads.
(4) For test specimens with one-side sheathing under cyclic loads, the ductility ratios for
specimens with 9 mm thick sheathing are similar to those of having 12 mm thick sheathing.
(5) If the cold-formed steel wall with calcium silicate board was employed to resist shear force,
it is suggested that the design ductility ratio of wall may be assumed to be 2.19.

ABSTRACT
An experimental study of cold-formed steel wall frames anchored at base corners and
sheathed with calcium silicate board under monotonic shear and cyclic loads is presented in
this paper. The test specimens were assembled with two different thicknesses of calcium
silicate board, 9 mm and 12 mm, and one-side or two-side of sheathing attachment. The
monotonic shear and cyclic loads are applied through the MTS testing machine. Test results
show that the wall frames with two-side calcium silicate board provide higher lateral
resistance than those attached with one-side sheathing. In addition, the thicker of the calcium
silicate board sheathing is used, the higher shear resisting strength of wall frames is obtained.
There was a slight difference of tested ultimate loads for test specimens subjected to either
monotonic shear load or cyclic load. In summary, the failure type, maximum load, and ductility
ratio of test wall frames are discussed in detail and conclusions are made.
1. INTRODUCTION
Cold-formed steel (CFS) structures have been widely used for quite many years in the
building construction industry [1]. In particular, wall frame systems attached with sheathings,
such as gypsum board, calcium silicate board, and oriented-strand board, are commonly used
as major structural element to resist not only the vertical loads but also the horizontal forces
induced from the wind and earthquake. Previous researches indicated that the shear
resistance of CFS wall frames can be increased by using sheathing, bracing, and anchors at
wall base.
Fulop and Dubina [2] studied a 12 ft 8 ft cold-formed stud frame. The experimental shear
walls included frame without sheathing, frame sheathed with corrugated sheet, frame braced
with steel strap, sheathed frame with opening. Each series consisted of identical wall panels
tested statically, both monotonic and cyclic. Results showed that the shear-resistance of wall
panels is significant both in terms of rigidity and load bearing capacity, and can effectively
resist lateral loads. Failure started at the bottom track in the anchor bolt region, therefore
strengthening of the corner detail is crucial.
Moghimi and Ronagh [3] investigated wall frame assemblies having diagonal bracing, bracket,
gusset plates and base anchors under lateral cyclic loads. It found that the CFS wall frames
provided better shear resistance when the diagonal strap bracing or the bracket at four

sheathing attached, and the loading type on the ultimate load can be obtained. The failure

3.3 Ductility ratio
In this study, the ductility ratio of wall specimen is determined on the basis of ECCS report [9],
which recommended that the ductility ratio (μ) is calculated from the ratio of the ultimate 
displacement to the yield displacement, Du/Dy. The calculated ductility ratios for test
specimens are given in Table 2. Theμvalue for Specimens HM-C09-HT and HC-C09-HT are
much higher than those for Specimens HM-C09-HO and HC-C09-HO. And the ductility ratios
between 9-mm sheathed wall and 12 -mm sheathed wall are not much different for the
specimens with one-side sheathing. The average ductility ratio for all specimens without
considering the two-side sheathed specimens is 2.19. Similar test having the same specimen
configuration without installing the hold-down device were conducted by Gu and Pan [10]. It is
found that the ultimate strength of the wall can be increased by installing the hold-down
device. However, the average values of ductility ratio for both the wall specimens with
hold-down device and specimens without hold-down device are quite similar.
