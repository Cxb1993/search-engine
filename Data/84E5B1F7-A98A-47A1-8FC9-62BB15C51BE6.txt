1.Foreword 
 Welcome to the 22nd International Conference on Indium Phosphide and Related 
Materials from May 31st to June 4th in 2010 at Takamatsu Symbol Tower, Kagawa 
Prefecture, Japan. It is our great pleasure to hold the 22nd IPRM in Takamatsu City 
facing the beautiful Seto Inland Sea. We have invited two Plenary Speakers on 
important research fields for IPRM. The first speaker, Prof. Hiroo Yonezu of 
Toyohashi University of Technology, will review material approaches for 
optoelectronics in his talk entitled “Convergence of III-V compound and Si 
technologies: photonic-electronic convergence”. 
The second speaker, Prof. Sanjay Raman of DARPA MTO will give a talk entitled 
“The DARPA COSMOS Program: The Convergence of InP and Silicon CMOS 
Technologies for High-Performance Mixed- Signal”. Following the plenary talks, in 
18 technical sessions including a poster-viewing session, 19 invited speakers will 
review the state-of-the art of achievements in each field and 122 contributed papers 
will be presented. Prior to the main program, Dr. Shinji Matsuo of NTT and Prof. 
Peide Ye of Purdue University will respectively conduct Short Courses covering 
photonic integration technologies and III-V MOS device technologies. The excellent 
papers and presentations will definitely make the 22nd IPRM fruitful. I would like to 
express my thanks to all contributors. 
In addition to the traditional organization of IPRM, it is a great pleasure to announce 
collocation with the 37th International Symposium on Compound Semiconductors, 
ISCS, for the first time. The purpose of this collocation is to make IPRM and ISCS as 
informative as possible by enhancing communication and collaboration between the 
two societies. I expect that all participants will be able to deepen their expertise in the 
technical sessions and expand their technical interests and human networks through 
other related sessions, the rump session, vendor exhibition, and social events, such as 
the welcome reception, banquet, and 
excursions.
