中文摘要 
電解拋光技術是屬於一種精密拋光技術，拋光後可以去除材料表面的破壞
層，得到平坦、光澤和無殘留應力的表面。而且又具有潔淨、高抗蝕性、無拋光
應力、不產生毛邊等優點，廣泛的運用於半導體製程設備、生醫、精密機械製造
等領域。 
黏稠層被破壞是造成製程不穩定的主因，影響黏稠層的原因主要有電化學作
用、流體擾動和氣泡。尤其，反應過程中所產生的氣泡，造成溶液被過度擾動，
嚴重破壞擴散層的穩定性。所以，在製程穩定性不佳的情況下，製程精度不好掌
控，並容易產生流痕、孔蝕等缺陷於工件表面上，降低表面品質。 
為了改善製程的穩定性，本研究針對目前使用的電解液進行改良，透過實驗
設計法的協助，找出減少氣泡生成，增加穩定性的添加劑，與最佳添加量。透過
長效時間實驗，以表面粗糙度和電解液金屬濃度為指標，推測出電解液的壽命，
達到提高對電解液性能與穩定度的目標。 
本研究建立電解拋光物理模型，討論電荷分布與擴散作用對電解拋光製程的
影響。採用商用軟體，FEMLAB，建立 3 維的物理模型，模擬在不同陰極電極面
積、陰極形狀和間隙下，表面電荷濃度分布的情況。再以表面輪廓儀，量測去除
量的分布，探討去除量和電荷濃度分布的關係，釐清參數真正影響的關係。並找
出改善加工精度的參數範圍，提高參數控制與治具設計的能力。針對物理模型所
找出的參數範圍，進行實驗，以表面機械性質與表面化學電化學性質為評估指
標，評斷表面品質改善的效果，藉由實驗的方式，找出最佳參數，以獲得高精度
與品質的拋光效果。 
透過本研究所建立之物理模型，與對參數作用的了解，可以快速評斷陰極設
計的效果，加快陰極設計的流程，並找出加工精度較高的參數設定區間，再對此

1.前言 
電解拋光技術是屬於一種精密拋光技術，拋光後可以去除材料表面的破壞層
得到平坦、光澤和無殘留應力的表面，而且又具有潔淨、無拋光應力、不產生毛
邊等優點，而拋光過程中所生成的鈍化膜將可以提升抗蝕的效能並改變表面的極
性為疏水性減少液體的殘留並使表面的電位一致化，減少表面電位差造成局部腐
蝕的發生。由這樣的特性來看，電解拋光技術可以滿足高科技業對於零組件平
滑、抗蝕和無殘留應力的需求，屬於關鍵的製程技術。目前國外應用的範圍相當
的廣泛，舉凡半導體製程設備、生醫、精密機械製造等領域的零組件製造上都有
廣泛的應用。 
除了上述的用途之外，目前亦有數個新應用正在發展中，如可撓式顯示器、
超高解析度顯示器和薄膜太陽能電池，都需要藉助此項技術的幫助。以可撓式顯
示器為例，已有許多文獻指出金屬薄片具有 1.耐高溫製程，2.低熱膨脤係數，3.
阻水氣、阻氧氣，4.低靜電效應，5.適合 Roll-To-Roll 製程，6.低成本，7.取得容
易等優點，在可撓式顯示器的基板應用上相當有潛力。但是為了符合後段製程需
要改善表面品質，傳統的機械研磨無法有效的處理薄片材料，使得改善表面品質
成為金屬薄片應用的重要技術瓶頸。電解拋光屬於非接觸的製程，經過電解拋光
處理之後，可以得到平滑、抗蝕無殘留應力的表面，對於克服上述的瓶頸有很大
的助益。此外由於電解拋光無殘留應力的問題，所以在半導體銅製程的研磨上可
以改善 CMP 所造成導線破損和偏移的問題，所以電解拋光技術可應用的範圍相
當廣，未來發展的潛力相當的大。 
電解拋光技術是一種電化學陽極溶解的拋光技術，在反應過程中電化學作
用、電場、電流分布和流體皆同時發生作用，且過程中陰極、陽極都會產生大量
氣體使電解液具有雙相流的現象。拋光所使用的電解液是濃稠的酸，所以氣體的
排除不易，會破壞製程的穩定性，對拋光結果造成影響如產生流痕、孔蝕等缺陷，
這樣的現象以陽極最為嚴重，是發展此技術所面臨的技術瓶頸。 
所以電化學拋光技術普遍都存在加工精度不佳、品質穩定度不均勻和參數不
響且不穩定，並造成製程參數如電壓、間隙和拋光時間的難以掌握，影響製程的
加工精度，亦無法預測去除量的分布。本研究將以商用軟體為工具探討電荷、擴
散等影響電解拋光的物理現象，建立去除量分布方程式，探討去除量分布和參數
的關係以提高製程參數的了解與控制能力。 
2. 表面品質改善研究 
在製程過程中，陽極伴隨產生的氣泡，是造成黏稠層破壞的主因，並導致表面有
孔洞、流痕和鈍化膜生成不均等缺陷。所以，本研究將對電解液配方進行改良，
降低氣泡生成的量與氣泡尺寸，以改善鈍化膜生成的均勻度，並建立鈍化膜均勻
度與品質的評估方式，以作為鈍化膜品質與均勻度評估的指標。

何形狀、陽極幾何形狀、擾動參數和拋光時間等，而且參數之間存在交互作用，
很難清楚釐清參數直接和互相的影響關係，造成不同的研究者，在環境設定不同
時會得到截然不同的結果。 
由上述文獻，電解拋光相關的研究大致分為理論現象探討與參數實驗的方
式，但兩者很難互相配合與驗證。在理論現象探討的部份，需要作簡化以符合理
論假設和現象的觀察，但是卻造成和實際製程有差距。在實驗觀察的部份，則受
制於參數的多樣性且具交互作用，很難對參數作用進行全面的了解。使得實驗所
得到的參數應用範圍狹小，每當有參數須修改則須重新進行實驗，浪費許多不必
要的時間與成本。所以，本研究將發展一界面，整合理論與實驗，以改善製程的
穩定度，提升拋光的精度與品質。 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
電解拋光的製程牽涉的因素相當的多，系統穩定性不夠強健，綜觀先前的研
究，電解拋光過程大概可以分為三個部份，不同的階段有不同的主要影響因子，
分別說明如下： 
1. 初始作用區 
當在陰陽極間供給電位差，此時金屬離子開始自電極表面溶解，受到電
極幾何形狀、陰陽極材料和溶液電化學特性等影響，一開始溶解的速率
很高，但並不均勻。一般來說，邊界有電荷集中的現象，使得加工速率
會高於其他部位。此階段，電極幾何形狀、陰陽極材料和溶液電化學特
性是造成電流密度分布不均為主要的影響機制。 
2. 擴散區 
在初始作用時所產生的電荷會朝向電解液擴散，並發生活性極化。隨著
時間的增加發生，陽極溶解出來的金屬離子數和擴散到溶液的金屬離子
數相等時，電極表面附近溶液中，任一點的離子濃度不在隨著時間改變，
反應速率降低並達到動態平衡。此時的擴散行為已經達到穩態擴散的狀
態，擴散層已於電極表面生成，成為電解拋光的作用機制。然而此時的
系統非常容易受影響，例如過電壓升高、電解液更新不良和氣泡生成等
因素將會破壞此平衡，造成製程的不穩定。 
3. 流體作用區 
隨著擴散層的生成，此時反應的過電壓增加，電解水的副反應開始出現，
在陰極出現氫氣氣泡，陽極出現氧氣泡。產生的氣泡，因為密度差和濃
度差，會在體溶液發生自然對流現象，使得溶解速率變得不穩定，破壞
擴散層的穩定性與均勻性，使得加工的速率變動很大且不均，是造成製
程穩定性不足的主要原因。

陰陽極材料和溶液電化學特性所造成的電荷集中現象；第二部份為破壞擴散層的
因素如電解液更新不良和氣泡。所以，如何抑制上述現象的影響將是提升電化學

項需求進行鈍化膜的性能和組成評估，以了解品質改善的效果。在鈍化膜性能分
析的部份，評估指標有抗蝕性、鈍化膜阻抗和均勻度，以這些指標評估鈍化膜性
能的改善，分別敘述於下。 
在抗蝕性研究的部份、本研究採用 Solartron 1285 Potentiostat 對試片分別進
行均勻腐蝕測試和晶界腐蝕測試，以均勻腐蝕測試評估鈍化膜對抗環境腐蝕的能
力。局部腐蝕(Localized corrosion)問題一直是奧斯田鐵系不銹鋼應用必須面臨的
問題，而晶界腐蝕(Intergranular corrosion, IGC)是屬於局部腐蝕的一種，好發生
於不銹鋼焊接的過程，由於焊接過程中的高溫，易使鉻和碳反應沈積於晶界，引
發後續的局部腐蝕 [21] 。晶界腐蝕測試的方法有很多種，根據 [22-23]  
Electrochemical Potentiokinetic Repassivation (EPR)測試法是目前最常用的方式，
所以，本研究將透過晶界腐蝕測試，以了解鈍化膜對改善此晶界腐蝕的作用，並
作為評估製程改善的參考。

5.2.7 小結 
由計算結果，不管陰極形狀和陰陽極面積比，間隙 3mm 時，穩定區的電荷
個數為 16660;間隙 5mm 時，穩定區的電荷個數為 10000;間隙 15mm 時，穩定區
電荷個數為 3360 個。電荷個數和間隙放大倍率成反比，間隙越小穩定區內兩側
受影響的區域越小，但影響區內的電荷數卻越高，集中程度相當明顯，且受影響
區長度，亦會隨間隙增加而增加。間隙較大時，電荷以較緩慢的速度增加，呈現
圓滑曲線的分布，受影響區長度會增加。但大部分結果都顯示，在間隙 15mm 時，
影響區增加至 20%，且電荷強度變化很大，穩定的區間很少，所以，間隙大的參
數不適用高加工精度的應用。 
由計算結果，不管陰極面積，長方形陰極，對降低電荷集中現象，效果有限，
當面積放大到 2 倍後，還會大幅降低穩定區電荷數。圓形陰極，除了面積比為 1
的時候，分布相當不均之外，皆能有效的降低受影響區長度，且不同量測位置之
影響區，長度皆一致，有效的提高電荷分布之均勻性，所以圓形陰極較長方形陰
極，更有效的提升電荷分布均勻度。 
當陰極面積放大為 2 倍後，和放大 1.5 倍的時候相比，對於提高電荷分布的
均勻性改善有限，反而容易使分布的圖形呈現大弧度的圓滑曲線形態，且降低穩
定區的電荷量，增加兩側和穩定區之電荷量的差值，會降低加工時精度。但是較

5.6 電解液改善研究 
由上述的問題，電解液改善研究主要分為 2 部份，第一部份是降低氣泡的生
成量，第二部份為電解液壽命的評估。但是，要找到合適的添加劑種類與濃度，
相當的困難，所以，本研究以實驗設計的方式，對可能的添加劑進行研究，以找
出最有貢獻的參數與最佳的添加量，以達到提升電解液品質與穩定性的目標。 
5.6.1 降低氣泡影響研究 
目前，電解拋光所使用的電解液為磷酸和硫酸的混合液，根據所蒐集的專利
和文獻，整理出可能的添加劑有 A、B、C 和 D 四種。但是，並不知道是不是真
的有作用，所以，先進行 2 水準的 24 實驗，以找出有貢獻的因子。根據所蒐集
的資料，A 參數分別設為 5%和 10%，B 參數設為 3%和 6%，C 參數設為 2%和
5%，D 參數設為 1%和 3%。並依實驗設計法，進行拋光實驗，完成拋光實驗後，
以粗糙度(Ra)作為檢測指標，評估各添加劑的貢獻度，並找出最佳添加量，實驗
結果如表 5-3。 
將實驗之結果進行 ANOVA 分析，分析 A、B、C、D、AB、AC、AD、BC、
BD 和 CD 對粗糙度望小的影響，透過 ANOVA 分析，可判斷各參數的貢獻，作
為更詳細實驗的基礎，分析結果如表 5-4 所示。 
由 ANOVA 分析結果，A、B、AB、BC 之 P 值皆小於 0.05，所以基本上，
A、B、C 三個參數對實驗都是有影響的。但是，A 和 B 的貢獻度相對於參數 C 來
的顯著，所以，本研究將針對參數 A 和 B，進行反應曲面法配適實驗，利用反
應曲面法，求出最佳因子水準組合。

5.6.4 表面缺陷評估研究 
所謂的缺陷，就是以試片上非平整的部份來定義，包括圓孔、凸點、凹洞等，
因為平坦的區域較亮、凹陷的區域較暗，可以有效的定義缺陷的輪廓。所以本實
驗，主要是利用缺陷在影像上的色階值不同，作為缺陷輪廓定義的依據。再藉由
調整影像的門檻值(Threshold)[33][34]，來區別缺陷與非缺陷的區域，並進行圈選
 
 
率為 90 倍與 180 倍兩種，並在實驗之前，先依據硬體的設定範圍，以測試的方
式調整至最佳的光源均勻度，以此值作為接下來實驗操作之光源強度。 
以商用軟體 IMAQ 為缺陷辨識工具，處理的步驟是將原始影像轉換為
HSL(Extract color planes: Luminance plane)，接著進行二值化步驟，選取適當的門
檻值(Threshold)以抓取缺陷的特徵值，再進行色彩反轉將缺陷部份轉為可計算的
色階(Invert Binary image)，同時濾除細小的雜點(Morphology: Remove small 
objects)，最後，再計算缺陷的總像數值(pixels)。 
在有圓孔的情況下，如圖 5-46 所示，其周圍突起部分的色階值與平面部份
差異太小，因此，不易利用門檻值的選取將整個缺陷部份抓取出來，只能抓取成
月弧形的形狀，如此就無法完整的計算缺陷真正所佔有的面積。因此，為了減少
計算的誤差，進行計算前，必須先將實驗觀察中有出現圓孔形缺陷的試片獨立出
來，並以另一套的設定程序分析，除此之外，亦利用更低倍率觀察表面，使圓孔
變小來減少色階誤判的情形，無圓孔時使用 180 倍，有圓孔時使用 90 倍。

將會破壞製程穩定，形成缺陷。所以，本研究針對表面缺陷的檢測，建立影像評
估系統，為了營造出不同的表面缺陷，選取了九組不同參數的拋光實驗，作為系
    經過 IMAQ 處理過的影像，可以明顯的將缺陷部份表示出來並予以量化，

整度就越高，自然缺陷數也就越少。另外亦可以發現 Rmax 為 1.0 時可視為一臨界
點，Rmax<1.00 以下的缺陷比例大約在 2% 以下，但是，Rmax 超過 1 時，可以發

組有明顯的不同(Rmax = 1.94µm，缺陷比 4.12%)，這是因為圓孔過多而造成軟體
辨識度較低，但粗糙度值卻不會受此影響，所以兩者的相關性較不準確。 
另外，由第 10 組實驗，可以發現，缺陷尺寸明顯的變少，顯示製程改良之
後，有效的降低表面缺陷的尺寸，僅為檢測面積的 0.07%，但是，受制於氣泡無
法快速離開表面，在總缺陷數目的改善上，效果較不明顯。 
由上述的檢測模式，透過軟體的計算，可以得到缺陷的形狀、尺寸和所佔的
比例，並可快速且大範圍的量化評估拋光後的表面品質，將可提升對拋光品質的
掌握，增加對製程品質掌控的能力。 
透過影像處理的方式，所建立之表面缺陷數評估指標，有效的判定表面品質
差異，比較粗糙度指標(Rmax)，可以發現，在粗糙度相差甚少的組別，在其表面
缺陷比例上，卻有明顯的差異。所以，表面缺陷數評估指標具有較佳敏感性，可
放大差異性，輕易的辨識表面品質的差異，更適合運用於表面品質的鑑別。

5.6.7 晶界腐蝕腐(Intergranular corrosion, IGC)蝕測試實驗 
不銹鋼表面在高溫(480ºC-815ºC)時，在晶界鉻會和碳發生反應形成金間產物
(Chromium carbides)，造成晶界缺鉻易被破壞，此現象亦稱為晶界腐蝕(IGC)，屬
於局部腐蝕的一種。缺鉻的區域，鈍化膜被破壞後所露出之表面，與還有鈍化膜
保護的地方形成電位差，進而發生局部腐蝕的現象。 
經過電解拋光製程處理過後的試片表面，在抗 IGC 腐蝕能力有大幅度的改
善，改善率為 85%-91%之間，由實驗數據來看，在製程穩定後配合適當參數，
皆可大幅降低發生 IGC 的趨勢。同時亦可以發現，在經過電解拋光製程改善之
後，表面生成的鈍化膜具有較大量的鉻，雖然無法抑制金間產物的生成，但是可
以減少鈍化膜被破壞的情形，有效降低 IGC 現象的發生。

ABSTRACT 
 
In this study, the effect of pulse current on the EMM process is studied by both 
numerical simulation and experimental measurement. The commercial software 
FEMLAB was employed to establish a 3D model for the simulation of the 
relationship between the operational potential, the electrolyte, the pulse rate and the 
duty cycle. The original specimen was digitized by a digital scanning machine to 
obtain the point cloud. The initial surface profile was established with the point cloud 
by reverse engineering technology. By the same procedures, the surface profile of the 
processed specimen was also established. Comparing these two surface profiles, it is 
easy to compute the material removal rate and the formation of the feature. It will also 
verify the improvement of machining accuracy and process stability from the pulse 
current. Through the results, the effects of pulse current are better understood. The 
simulation model can be employed as a predictive tool to provide optimal parameters 
for better machining accuracy and process stability of the EMM process.   
 
KEYWORDS: electrochemical Micro-Machining (EMM), pulse current, numerical 
simulation 
 
1. INTRODUCTION 
 
The increasing demand of squeezing more functions into ever smaller spaces 
drives the development of micro-fabrication technology such as CMOS and MEMS 
technologies. However, the silicon substrate lacks some desirable mechanical 
properties for MEMS applications. As a result the technologies of micro-machining 
on a metal substrate have emerged as promising research areas for micro/nano 3D 
structure fabrication.  
The EMM process is an anodic dissolution process of electrochemical reaction. It is a 
zero stress process suited for any conducting material, especially traditionally 
hard-to-machine metals. Compared to other micro-machining technologies, the 
characteristics of high machining efficiency, non-attritional electrode and good 
surface quality [1, 2] are some of the special merits of the EMM process. 
The anode (specimen) dissolves during the process creating an inverse image of 
the cathode. During the process, reactants, which are composed of metallic 
compounds, are created in between the electrode gap (IEG) and are refreshed by the 
diffusion mechanism and flow field of the electrolyte. Hydrogen and oxygen are also 
created on the surfaces of the anode and the cathode. When the shape of the cathode is 
the desired surface geometry of the final product, the processing time will be short. 
However, it is difficult to maintain a rapid and uniform flow of the electrolyte, 
making dimensionality control difficult. If the shape of the cathode is similar to a 
milling tool, then the final surface geometry will be generated by the CNC path of the 
cathode similar to the CNC milling of the metal cutting process. The machining time 
The prototype of the EMM system is built for machining sheet metal. The 
schematic picture of the EMM apparatus is demonstrated in Figure 2. The system 
includes a machine platform, feeding mechanism of the electrode, fixture of the 
specimen, electrolyte tank and filter, and a power supply [8]. The design variables for 
both simulation and experiment are pulse rate and operating potential. The duty cycle, 
IEG value and processing time remain constant. After the experiments, the profile of 
the processed rectangular slot were measured by a Keyence digital microscope, 
VK-8550. 
 
3. NUMERICAL SIMULATION MODELS 
 
The Multi-physics models for diffusion and current distributions were 
established using FEMLAB commercial software. The design variables are pulse rate, 
operating potential and concentration of the electrolyte. The fixed variables are the 
electrode gap, composition of the electrolyte, and duty cycle. Controlling the proper 
electrode gap is important in the EMM process. A smaller gap may provide better 
machining precision, but it may also result in a non-uniform and/or insufficient flow 
of the electrolyte, causing non-uniform metal removal [9]. The electrode gap is set at 
200µm. 
Bhattacharyya [7] reported that an operating potential between 6 and 10 V and a 
concentration of the electrolyte below 20 g/L provide better metal removal rate and 
machining precision. Therefore, two levels of operating potential, 5 V and 7 V, and 
10g/L of electrolytic concentration, were used in this study. The coefficient of 
diffusion obtained from the diffusion tests is 1.0E-6cm2/S. Four levels of pulse rates, 
15ms, 30ms, 60ms, and 100ms were tested in order to investigate the effect of the 
pulse rate on the EMM process. 
The cathode is stationary during the simulation as well as during EMM 
machining. In order to simplify the numerical simulation, a 2D model is assumed. 
Geometry of the numerical simulation is shown in Figure 3. The electrolyte is 
assumed to be stationary, and as a result there will be no convection due to fluid flow 
of the electrolyte.

3.2 The effects of the pulse rate on the reactant concentration in the IEG 
The trends of the reactant concentration in between the electrode gap were similar 
between the operating potential of 5 V and 7 V. The results of the operating potential 
of 7V are presented for discussion. The simulation results demonstrate that the 
reactant concentration reaches dynamic balance in just a few seconds. Their low and 
high values and the envelopes of concentration variation are shown in Figure 4. At a 
pulse rate of 15 ms, the reactant concentration diffuses effectively from the anode and 
decreases to 50% at the middle of the IEG, Gap = 100µm. At the middle of the IEG, 
the low and high values of the reactant concentration are 3.2E4 and 7.0E4, 
respectively. The difference is 218%. This indicates that the refreshing of the 
electrolyte is efficient and stable which results in a stable electrochemical process. As 
the pulse rate reduces to 30 ms, the diffusion rate slows down and reduces only 20% 
at the middle of the IEG where the low and high values of the reactant concentration 
are 1.4E4 and 9.0E4, respectively. The difference is 643%. Hence, the uniformity and 
stability of the electrolyte has degraded with the decreases in pulse rate. 
When the pulse rate decreases to 60 ms, the polishing time in each duty cycle is 
longer. The diffusion rate becomes even slower and the reactant starts to accumulate. 
At the middle of the IEG, the reactant concentration decreases only by 2%. The low 
and high concentration values are 0.4E4 and 1.04E5, respectively. The difference is 
961%. The reactant may have difficulty in refreshing by diffusion during the time-on 
period. The diffusion takes place during the time-off period. It will increase the 
electrical resistance and decrease the process effectiveness. When the pulse rate 
decreases to 100 ms, the diffusion mechanism is completely ineffective during the 
time-on period. 
From the above analysis results, it can be concluded that a faster pulse rate 
promotes a uniform and fresh reactant concentration, provides stable process 
conditions, a better metal removal rate and higher level of machining precision.

5. CONCLUSIONS 
 
1. The current distribution and the diffusion models have been established for the 
EMM process. The simulation results showed the effectiveness of the pulse 
current on the terminal effect. This was verified by the EMM experiments and the 
measurement of the profile of the processed specimen. 
2. From the simulation results it is evident that a pulse rate shorter than a few tens of 
ms can greatly improve the stability of the process conditions. A shorter pulse rate 
can further improve the process conditions and their uniformity. 
3. The pulse current increases the metal removal rate by 50% and reduces the ratio of 
the width on the top and at the bottom by a factor of 3, which improves machining 
accuracy. It is also consistent with the numerical simulation. 
4. The effects of the pulse rate reduces the effects of the terminal effect resulting in 
an improved over-cutting control and a higher machining accuracy.

The Performance of Miniature Metallic PEM Fuel Cells 
 
Shuo-Jen Lee, Yu-Ming Lee, Chi-Yuan Lee, Feng-Hui Kuan and Chih-Wei Chuang 
Department of Mechanical Engineering, Yuan Ze University 
135 FarEast Road, NeiLi, TaoYuan, Taiwan, ROC 
Email: mesjl@saturn.yzu.edu.tw 
 
ABSTRACT 
 
 A prototype of metallic PEM fuel cell with thin stainless bipolar plates was tested for their 
potential applications in portable electronic products. The flow field pattern was grown from the 
stainless steel plates by the electroforming process. The main flow channel has the dimensions of 300 
µm (width) by 300 µm (depth). The dimensions of the micro-features were 100 µm width by 50 µm 
depth and 50 µm width by 50 µm depth. The material of the electroformed flow field pattern is nickel. 
A prototype of a single cell with total thickness of 2.5 mm and overall reaction area of 16 cm2 was 
assembled for this study. In order to improve its corrosion resistance, the bipolar plates were coated 
with 5 µm thick of multi-layered corrosion resistant material. 
 In this study, indices of cell performance, with and without micro-features, of flow field 
concentration distribution, water draining capability were studied by both numerical simulation and 
experimental measurement. The results show that the micro-features will provide a more uniform gas 
concentration environment in the bipolar plate flow field. Also, the bipolar plate with micro-features 
could greatly improve cell draining capability. The results of cell performance tests show that the cell 
average power density reaches 173 mW/cm2. The long-term tests verify their performance were very 
stable.  
 
Keywords: electroforming, micro-features, metallic bipolar plate, numerical simulation, water draining 
capability 
 
1. Introduction 
    Recently, because of the rapid development 
of green technologies and 3C products, the small 
fuel cells as portable power sources are gaining 
more attentions. 
    The miniaturized fuel cells require thinner 
and smaller bipolar plates. The current approach 
in developing miniature fuel cells and its bipolar 
plates cells is based on adoption of 
micro-fabrication technologies like MEMS, 
long-term test was conducted to verify its 
performance stability. The research procedure 
are shown in Figure 1.  
The shape and size of the bipolar plate was 
4.0 cm by 4.0 cm square with effective area of 
2.0 cm by 2.0 cm square. The substrate material 
is 316L stainless steel. The width of the main 
flow channel was selected to be 300 µm. The 
depth of the channel was 250 µm taking pressure 
drop and flow resistance into consideration. 
Micro-features are designed with depth of 50µm 
and width of 50 µm and 100 µm. Figure 2 
presents the geometrical dimensions of the 2D 
schematic plot. 
 
2.1 Models for numerical simulation  
2D multi-physics models of bipolar plate 
flow field for hydrogen and concentration 
distributions were established using FEMLAB 
commercial software. The fuel’s mass 
transportation of an ideal fuel cell system is 
composed of complex process such as flow of 
gas into the bipolar plate, diffusion into the MEA 
and flew in the MEA. The focus of the numerical 
simulation is to analyze the gas mass 
transportation of the bipolar plate with and 
without micro-features. Therefore, the gas 
diffusion of the MEA is ignored.  
The resulting transport equations governing 
the flow in the channels of the bipolar plate are 
the steady version of the well known 
Navier–Stokes equations for mass and 
momentum conservation:

Variables in this study include hydrogen

settings and related coefficients such as gas 
density (kg/m3, ρ), gas dynamic viscosity (Ns/m2,

2.2 Micro-fabrication of bipolar plates 
    The bipolar plates are fabricated by the 
UV-LIGA and electroforming processes. The 
thickness of the bipolar plates is around 250 µm 
to 300 µm. Therefore, Conventional photo resist 
of the MEMS process is not suitable. After many 
trials, the SU-8 photo resist is found to meet our 
around 0.65. But the lowest concentration 
channel is around 0.55. The difference reaches 
0.1. 
    The problem could be effectively 
eliminated by micro-features. Figures 7 (a) and 8 
(a) show there are no obvious concentration 
difference between each channel from the gas 
inlet to the outlet. These results indicate that the 
micro-features could help gas diffusion between 
each channel. Thus, the flow field has more 
uniform gas concentration and the cell could 
perform better. 
 
3.2 Single cell and results of water draining test 
    The prototype of a single cell is shown in 
Figure 9. The optical microscopy, micrometer 
and laser displacement profiler were employed 
to exam the surface profile and height of the 
flow channel. The results showed that the overall 
size of the bipolar plate is around 4 cm by 4 cm 
square. The height of the flow field channel is 
around 250 to 300 µm. However, the shape is not 
very sharp which is caused by the chemical 
residues left on the pattern before electroforming. 
There is around 20 µm difference in height over 
the whole bipolar plates. This might be caused 
by the initial flatness error of the SS304 plate. It 
could also be resulted from the non-uniformity 
of the photo resist.  
The water draining tests were conducted 
with 50 and 100 SCCM of pure oxygen and the 
flow channels are full of red color stain. The 
water draining time is 3 seconds Figures 10 and 
11 shows the results of 50 and 100 SCCM.  
At 50 SCCM, Figure 10 (b) shows the 
bipolar plate without micro-features still has 
water flooding situation. The flooding area is 
around a quarter of the flow field. On the other 
hand, the flooding area of the micro-feature flow 
field is just at the bottom of the flow field. The 
area is less than half of the flow field without 
micro-features. Therefore, the water draining 
capability of the bipolar plate with 
micro-features seems much better than that 
without micro-features.  
At 100 SCCM, the oxygen flow rate is 
increased. The water flooding situation is less 
damaging than 50 SCCM. Figure 11 (b) shows 
the flooding situation of the flow filed without 
micro-features. It is still serious than that with 
micro-featured flow field.  
From these results, the water draining 
capability of the micro-feature bipolar plate is 
better than that without micro-features. 
 
3.3 Cell performance tests 
Cell performance tests were conducted 
with 50, 100, 150, 200, 250 and 300 SCCM 
(Standard Cubic Centimeter Per minute), 
respectively. Pure dry oxygen and hydrogen and 
prototype of single cell is 4 cm2. The overall 
size is 16 cm2 with thickness of 2.6 mm.  
2. Through numerical simulation, it is 
concluded that the micro-features will 
provide a more uniform gas concentration 
environment in the bipolar plate flow field. 
Thus, the cell performance could be 
improved. 
3. From results of water draining tests, 
regardless of gas flow rate, the bipolar plate 
with micro-features could greatly improve 
cell draining capability. 
4. The results of cell performance tests show 
that the cell average power density has 
reached 173 mW cm-2. The long-term tests 
also verify their stable performance.  
5. The proposed manufacturing procedures 
prove to be a promising technology in 
producing metallic bipolar plates with 
micro-features.
