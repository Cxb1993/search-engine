Abstract 
In this project, the laser surface scanner 
supported point based registration method 
for guiding the surgical tool in the robotic 
neurosurgical system is studied. The laser 
surface scanner is not only used to capture 
the fiducial markers on patient head in order 
to register the image space to the patient 
space, but it is also used to the registration 
of the patient-to-robot spaces. The iterative 
closed point algorithm is utilized in the 
image-to-patient registration, in which the 
parameters of rigid-body transformation 
matrix are iteratively searched until the sum 
of square distance of all paired fiducial 
makers is minimized. As for the 
patient-to-robot registration, the fiducial 
marker attaching on the tool tip was driven 
to different position, whose coordinates can 
be known from the control panel, then the 
iterative closed point algorithm is again 
used to align the both coordinates of the 
patient and robot. Quality of the 
image-to-patient registration are first 
reported, the positioning accuracy for 
guiding the surgical tool is also estimated. 
The result of 37 experiments shows the 
registration based on the fiducial markers is 
0.3±0.22mm(1σ), and the ability of tool 
positioning are 1.7±0.17mm(1σ). The 
results show that the proposed tool 
positioning procedure is valid for robotic 
neurosurgery system with accuracy and 
repeatability. 
Keywords?Robotic neurosurgery system, 
Laser surface scanning system, Registration, 
Positioning
