verifying the properties of the generated networks, especially, as in the recent situation of diversified research
criteria, some theoretical aspects as well as new features need to be addressed.
In this paper, an algorithm is proposed to generate a general and consistent directed flow network. This
algorithm is not an extension of NETGEN. Some theoretical points of view like the relationship among nodes,
arcs, and outdegrees are addressed. The proposed algorithm is based on the linked path structure to improve
the efficiency. Some features are included in the algorithm such as: multi-sources, multi-sinks, capacitated or
uncapacitated links. Other new features are also addressed like specified/unspecified maximal outdegree for
all nodes, specified/unspecified outdegree (indegree) for sources (sinks), naturally deployed feasible cycles as
in the real cases, and new attributes such as budgets, time, weight, and etc. To evaluate the proposed algorithm,
the 40 benchmarks in NETGEN are investigated. The limits of nodes and arcs are not constrained in the
proposed algorithm as long as the computer memory affordable. NETGEN had a limited size of 8000 nodes
and 35000 arcs in different situations. However, a network of 9000 nodes with 34000 arcs was generated by
the proposed approach. Unlike NETGEN involving thousand lines of codes, a sample code of no more than 40
lines in PROLOG is tested for examples. The remainder of the work is described as follows: The notations
and assumptions for the approach discussed here are presented in Section 2. Some mathematic preliminaries
and the theoretical aspects are addressed in Section 3. Section 4 describes the algorithm of network generation.
Then, the undergoing of the algorithm is illustrated by some numerical examples in Section 5. The 40
benchmarks plus one extra case are also drawn in Section 5.
