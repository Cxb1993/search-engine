英文摘要 
 
In this study, tin oxide powder and long-chained fatty acid (oleic acid) were used to prepare a tin 
oleic complex. The tin oleic complex thermal decomposed to synthesis different types of SnO and 
SnO2 nanoparticles by dissolution and precipitation method. Tri-n-octylamine (TOA) and 
Oleylamine (OLA) as used as the surfactants and co-solvents. The as-received tin oxide powder was 
characterized by X-ray diffractometer, scanning electron microscopy and high resolution 
transmission electron microscopy. The optical property and energy gap was measured by UV-Vis 
spectrometer.  
Various low-dimensional tin-oxides and tin-sulfides particle including square SnO platelet, 
truncated octahedral SnO crystal, and the monodispepersed SnO2 nanoparticles were synthesized by 
dissolution and recrystallization of bulk SnO powder via thermal decomposition of tin oleate in the 
coordinating solvent of tri-n-octylamine, 1-Octadecanol, Oleyamine etc. at the different temperatures. 
The results revealed that the temperature, surfactants, atmosphere and reaction time are important 
factors to control the shape, size and the phases of the tin oxides and tin-sulfices. Thus, the thermal 
decomposition method shows a novel and convenient route to prepare tin oxides and sulfides 
nanoparticles and nanostructures. These low-dimensional nanomaterials have potential in the 
application of solar cell, Li-ions rechargeable battery and transparent conductive oxide and gas 
sensor etc.

1-2 研究背景 
 
1-2-1 氧化錫材料 
 
氧化錫材料，其常見的相有 SnO, SnO2 兩種，另外也有 Sn3O4[1]與 Sn5O6[2]曾被報導過。
因此可以得知陽離子，主要是以 Sn(II)離子與 Sn(IV)離子方式存在。在許多應用的氧化錫材料
中，大部分都包含這兩種離子。因此在本文中，有時候以 SnOx來特冸表示此包含缺陷的化合
物。 
SnO2 是一種寬能隙的半導體材料，其能隙較常見的 TiO2, ZnO 等寬能隙半導體材料更高，
如 Fig. 1 所示[3]，能隙高達 Eg=3.8 eV。因此具有高化學穩定性、高熱穩定性、高氣體敏感性
及於可見光區有高透光度等特性。純的 SnO2（化學當量）其導電性不佳，因為高能隙的緣故，
電子不易躍上傳導帶形成自由電子。但一般製備的 SnO2 晶體中，大多形成非化學劑量比的化
合物，即 SnOx (1≦x≦2)。也就是有部分的 Sn
2+ 
(SnO)取代 SnO2 晶格中於 Sn
4+的位置，形成氧
空位或格隙 Sn 離子，因此可視為是一種 n-type 半導體材料。 
 
Fig. 1 Band positions of several semiconductors in contact with aqueous electrolyte at pH 1.[3] 
 
由於SnOx的導電機制主要是控制在氧空缺的濃度，在不同的還原性氣氛之下，會改變材
料內氧空缺的濃度，大幅影響其導電度。因此，一直以來，SnOx就被廣泛的應用在氣體感測
器上[4]，主要檢測對象是丙烷、酒精、CO、H2、H2S等氣體。另外，由於SnO2的高能隙，使
其光吸收光譜落在紫外光的範圍，而在可見光區域具有高透明性，加上具有可調整的導電特
性，因此成為理想的光電透明導電膜材料[5]、抗靜電塗佈層等，應用逐年成長的帄陎顯示器
(Flat-panel display, FPD)產品上。在其他的顯示技術上，SnO2亦可作為有機發光二極體(OLEDs, 
organic light-emitting diodes)的陰極材料[6]，可以減少銦(In)的使用以避免In擴散至高分子材料
中的問題，並增進OLED的發光效率。

分；另一端為疏水性(hydrophobic)的尾鏈，主要是由非極性的碳氫鏈或碳氟鏈所組成。界陎活
性劑的分類可由親水基的頭部所攜帶之電荷，區分為陰離子性(anionic) 、陽離子性(cationic)

2-2 SnO 型態控制 
2004年Chem. Mater國際期刊中Mark G. White等人發表以高溫熱合成的方式製備SnO晶
體，以SnO粉末並添加苯酚當反應溶劑，經攪拌後再由高溫爐在氰氣氣氛下以400~1100℃範圍
做高溫合成，即形成產物，而燒結溫度會影響SnO形狀與其產物的晶相[38]。 
 
2005年Chem. Commun國際期刊Minghua Qiao等人發表SnO板塊(20~25 μm)，以氯化亞錫與
檸檬酸鈉和界陎活性劑十六烷基三甲基溴化胺（CTAB）溶解在25毫升去離子水，然後加入10
毫升的氫氧化鉀溶液氫氧化鉀並加熱，經離心後SnO板塊形成，而不同CTAB的濃度會使SnO
成長速率改變，造成SnO型態不同[39]。 
 
而在2008年國際期刊ACS, Langmuir中Hiroaki Imai等人發表，以沉澱法合成片狀SnO，使
用SnF2做為前驅物與NaOH水溶液混合，經攪拌一段時間後形成中間產物為Sn6O4(OH)4，再冺
用酸性和鹼性溶液和純水混合調配不同pH值，並置於烘箱以溫度60℃氣氛下經長時間後沉澱
形成產物，而使用不同SnF2濃度與pH值所製備出的SnO型態也會隨之變化[40]。 
 
2009年國際期刊ACS, J. Phys. Chem. C.中Yingjin Wei等人發表具有層次的SnO晶體，以氯
化亞錫(SnCl2)與溶液油胺(OLA)和十八烯(ODE)混合攪拌，會先形成中間產物Sn6O4(OH)4，然
後在製程中添加少許水的目的，是由於在過程中水會產生水解而影響SnO型態，進而達到SnO
晶體型態的控制，而後其進行鋰電池的測試，其最佳達到535.3 mAh/g的電容量，高於一般石
墨342 mAh/g之電容量[41]。

Abstract
Various types of tin oxide particle, including square SnO platelets, truncated octahedral SnO crystals, and monodispepersed SnO2 nanoparticles,
were synthesized by the dissolution and recrystallization of bulk SnO powder via the thermal decomposition of tin oleate in a coordinating solvent
of tri-n-octylamine at 340 ◦C. The results reveal that the atmosphere and reaction time are important factors that affect the shape of tin(II) oxide
and the oxidation state of tin(II or IV) oxide. As tin oleate decomposed in a N2 atmosphere, truncated octahedral SnO crystals formed. When the
thermal decomposition was conducted in air, square SnO platelets formed after a 30-min reaction time. When the reaction time was extended to
180 min, the square SnO platelets decomposed and transformed into nanocrystalline SnO2 particles.

1. Introduction
Nanostructured metal oxide and semiconductor materials
are highly desirable for advanced electronic, magnetic, opto-
electronic and sensing applications.1,2 The performance of
nanoparticles strongly depends on their phase, size, shape, and
dimension. Hence, the control of these properties is of great
interest.
Tin oxides, such as tetragonal SnO and rutile-type SnO2,
have received a lot of attention as functional materials. SnO is
used in p-type semiconductors, high-energy-density recharge-
able lithium batteries,3,4 and solar energy storage.5 SnO2 is
widely used for various devices, such as transparent conduc-
tive electrodes,6 gas sensors,7,8 electrochromic devices,9 and
anodes for batteries.10,11 A lot of effort has thus been devoted
to the synthesis of SnO and SnO2 nanoparticles and nanostruc-
tured materials. Moreover, SnO is a versatile intermediate to
metallic Sn, Sn3O4, and SnO2.12 However, the steady growth of
single-crystal SnO is relatively difficult because Sn(II) is not a
thermodynamically stable phase and is easily oxidized to Sn(IV)
in the atmosphere.

Tin oxide nanostructures of various sizes, phases, and shapes
have been prepared through various gas-phase and liquid-phase
routes. Zero-dimensional nanoparticles,13–17 one-dimensional

3.3. Growth mechanism
Base on experimental results, we propose a formation mech-
anism for tin oxide. The less stable SnO powder dissolved in the
OA solvent and formed a tin oleate complex. When TOA was
added into the tin oleate complex solution and heated to 340 ◦C,
the tin oleate molecules decomposed into SnO monomers. The
SnO monomers are very difficult to isolate in high tempera-
ture and recrystallization occur to growth as stable particle. The
coordinating solvent TOA works as an activator that triggers the
decomposition reaction as well as a surfactant that controls the
shape of the SnO particles. The SnO monomers are like build-
ing blocks which assemble into SnO particles on a large scale
to minimize the surface energy. According to Wang’s22 report,
the shape of a tetragonal SnO single crystal is determined by
the growth rates along the 〈0 0 1〉, 〈1 0 0〉, and 〈1 1 0〉 directions.
When the thermal decomposition process was conducted in a N2
atmosphere, the TOA surfactant was stable and adsorbed onto
the surface of the SnO nuclei isotropically. The growth rates of
the tetragonal SnO single crystal along the 〈0 0 1〉, 〈1 0 0〉, and
〈1 1 0〉 directions are equal, leading to a decahedral structure.
However, the TOA molecules in air during the decomposition
process is unstable and oxide to form cation radical, which
selectively adsorbs onto the (0 0 1) plane of the SnO crystal
and suppresses the growth rate of the (0 0 1) plane. Finally, the
anisotropic assembled into a large square SnO sheet crystal.
4. Conclusions
Single-crystalline SnO in the forms of square nanoplatelets
and a truncated octahedral shape was synthesized from the
thermal decomposition of tin oleate precursor, prepared using
commercial SnO powder dissolved in oleic acid. The morphol-
ogy and oxidation state of tin oxide varied with atmosphere
and reaction time of the decomposition process. Truncated
octahedral and square nanoplatelets of SnO were selectively
synthesized under air and nitrogen atmospheres, respectively.
Nanocrystalline SnO2 particles with a size of 6–7 nm formed
after a prolonged decomposition time. The shape of SnO crys-
tals is controlled by the TOA anisotropic adsorption behavior in
N2 or air. The air atmosphere tends to suppress the growth rate
of SnO in the [0 0 1] direction, whereas N2 leads to isotropic
growth.

3. Results and discussion
3.1. Structure and composition of Ta2O5 ﬁlm
As the Ta2O5 ﬁlm deposited in an Ar–O2 mixture with a working
pressure of 20 mTorr and sputter power of 50W, the deposition rate
of the Ta2O5 ﬁlms decreases with increasing oxygen ﬂow rate and
becomes stable for oxygen ﬂow rates higher than 10 sccm. The strong
decrease of the sputtering efﬁciency is probably due to surface oxida-
tion of the metallic Ta target. Fig. 1 shows the glancing-angle X-ray
diffraction pattern of the as-deposited Ta2O5 thinﬁlmwhich is prepared
at the ratio of oxygen and argon of 1/10. The absence of any sharp
diffraction peaks and the presence of a hump indicate the amorphous
nature of the as-deposited ﬁlm.
The chemical compositions of Ta2O5 ﬁlms prepared at various
oxygen ﬂow rates are listed in Table 2. All the ﬁlms prepared were
non-metallic in appearance and even a 1-sccm oxygen ﬂow rate was
sufﬁcient to oxidize Ta metal to its oxide. The atomic ratio of O/Ta
increases slightly with the increase in oxygen ﬂow rate. The ﬁlm depos-
ited at a 3-sccm ﬂow rate has the same O/Ta atomic ratio as that of stoi-
chiometric Ta2O5. The XPS spectra of as-deposited ﬁlms with binding
energies of Ta4f and O1s are shown in Fig. 2. The Ta4f core level
exhibits two peaks, centered at 26.2 and 28.1 eV, that can be assigned
to the 4f7/2 and 4f5/2 core levels of Ta+5, respectively. The band shift
of these two peaks is 1.9 eV and the ratio of their areas is about 1.3,
which indicates that the ﬁlm is fully oxidized to stoichiometric Ta2O5
[22]. The Ta 4f7/2 line position at 26.2 eV is very close to the typical
26.3 eV of the Ta chemical state in Ta2O5 [22,23]. Fig. 2 (b) shows a
binding energy of 530.3 eV corresponding to the characteristic binding
energy of O2−anions in the oxide [24].
The representative microstructures of the as-deposited Ta2O5 ﬁlm
characterized by SEM and AFM are shown in Fig. 3. The surface mor-
phology has ﬁbrous grains, separated by a voided boundary (Fig. 3(a)).
The AFM image (Fig. 3 (b)) shows a relatively smooth ﬁne-dome top
structure for the ﬁbrous grain. The average surface roughness (Ra) is
about 2.5 nm. The cross-sectional SEM micrograph (Fig. 3(c)) shows ﬁ-
brous grains with a porous and loose-packing structure. According to
the Thorton zone model [25], the ﬁbrous and loose-packing structure
forms under coating conditions of low deposition temperature and
high Ar pressure. These conditions result in a low kinetic energy of the
sputtered atoms from the target and a low surfacemobility of ad atoms.
The inﬂuence of the oxygen ﬂow rate on the microstructure was
further quantiﬁed by refractive index measurements. A ﬁlm's refrac-
tive index is inversely proportional to its porosity [9,24]. Fig. 4
shows the refractive index of Ta2O5 ﬁlms as a function of the ratio
of oxygen and argon. The refractive index mostly increases with
increase in oxygen ﬂow rate, but the minimum is found at 3 sccm.

Morphological and phase control of tin oxide single-crystals synthesized by
dissolution and recrystallization of bulk SnO powders
Sheng-Chang Wang a,∗, Ray-Kuang Chiang b,∗, Pin-Jie Hu a
a Department of Mechanical Engineering, Southern Taiwan University, Yungkang City, Tainan 710, Taiwan, ROC
b Department Materials Science and Engineering, Far East University, Tainan 744, Taiwan, ROC

Abstract
Various types of tin oxide particle, including square SnO platelets, truncated octahedral SnO crystals, and monodispepersed SnO2 nanoparticles,
were synthesized by the dissolution and recrystallization of bulk SnO powder via the thermal decomposition of tin oleate in a coordinating solvent
of tri-n-octylamine at 340 ◦C. The results reveal that the atmosphere and reaction time are important factors that affect the shape of tin(II) oxide
and the oxidation state of tin(II or IV) oxide. As tin oleate decomposed in a N2 atmosphere, truncated octahedral SnO crystals formed. When the
thermal decomposition was conducted in air, square SnO platelets formed after a 30-min reaction time. When the reaction time was extended to
180 min, the square SnO platelets decomposed and transformed into nanocrystalline SnO2 particles.

1. Introduction
Nanostructured metal oxide and semiconductor materials
are highly desirable for advanced electronic, magnetic, opto-
electronic and sensing applications.1,2 The performance of
nanoparticles strongly depends on their phase, size, shape, and
dimension. Hence, the control of these properties is of great
interest.
Tin oxides, such as tetragonal SnO and rutile-type SnO2,
have received a lot of attention as functional materials. SnO is
used in p-type semiconductors, high-energy-density recharge-
able lithium batteries,3,4 and solar energy storage.5 SnO2 is
widely used for various devices, such as transparent conduc-
tive electrodes,6 gas sensors,7,8 electrochromic devices,9 and
anodes for batteries.10,11 A lot of effort has thus been devoted
to the synthesis of SnO and SnO2 nanoparticles and nanostruc-
tured materials. Moreover, SnO is a versatile intermediate to
metallic Sn, Sn3O4, and SnO2.12 However, the steady growth of
single-crystal SnO is relatively difficult because Sn(II) is not a
thermodynamically stable phase and is easily oxidized to Sn(IV)
in the atmosphere.

Tin oxide nanostructures of various sizes, phases, and shapes
have been prepared through various gas-phase and liquid-phase
routes. Zero-dimensional nanoparticles,13–17 one-dimensional
nanowires,7 nanorods,18 and nanotubes, two-dimensional
nanoplates,19 nanoribbons,1 and nanofilms,13 and three-

However, the preparation methods generally involve the use of
tin salts dissolved in aqueous or polar solvents. The present study
reports that SnO can be easily dissolved in hot oleic acid and that
the resulting tin(II) oleate complex can be used as an efficient
precursor for the synthesis of nanoparticles of SnO and SnO2.
Furthermore, the shape of the obtained SnO single-crystals can
be easily controlled by reaction atmospheres. Alternatively, the
phase of tin oxides can be controlled by the reaction time.

3.3. Growth mechanism
Base on experimental results, we propose a formation mech-
anism for tin oxide. The less stable SnO powder dissolved in the
OA solvent and formed a tin oleate complex. When TOA was
added into the tin oleate complex solution and heated to 340 ◦C,
the tin oleate molecules decomposed into SnO monomers. The
SnO monomers are very difficult to isolate in high tempera-
ture and recrystallization occur to growth as stable particle. The
coordinating solvent TOA works as an activator that triggers the
decomposition reaction as well as a surfactant that controls the
shape of the SnO particles. The SnO monomers are like build-
ing blocks which assemble into SnO particles on a large scale
to minimize the surface energy. According to Wang’s22 report,
the shape of a tetragonal SnO single crystal is determined by
the growth rates along the 〈0 0 1〉, 〈1 0 0〉, and 〈1 1 0〉 directions.
When the thermal decomposition process was conducted in a N2
atmosphere, the TOA surfactant was stable and adsorbed onto
the surface of the SnO nuclei isotropically. The growth rates of
the tetragonal SnO single crystal along the 〈0 0 1〉, 〈1 0 0〉, and
〈1 1 0〉 directions are equal, leading to a decahedral structure.
However, the TOA molecules in air during the decomposition
process is unstable and oxide to form cation radical, which
selectively adsorbs onto the (0 0 1) plane of the SnO crystal
and suppresses the growth rate of the (0 0 1) plane. Finally, the
anisotropic assembled into a large square SnO sheet crystal.
4. Conclusions
Single-crystalline SnO in the forms of square nanoplatelets
and a truncated octahedral shape was synthesized from the
thermal decomposition of tin oleate precursor, prepared using
commercial SnO powder dissolved in oleic acid. The morphol-
ogy and oxidation state of tin oxide varied with atmosphere
and reaction time of the decomposition process. Truncated
octahedral and square nanoplatelets of SnO were selectively
synthesized under air and nitrogen atmospheres, respectively.
Nanocrystalline SnO2 particles with a size of 6–7 nm formed
after a prolonged decomposition time. The shape of SnO crys-
tals is controlled by the TOA anisotropic adsorption behavior in
N2 or air. The air atmosphere tends to suppress the growth rate
of SnO in the [0 0 1] direction, whereas N2 leads to isotropic
growth.
