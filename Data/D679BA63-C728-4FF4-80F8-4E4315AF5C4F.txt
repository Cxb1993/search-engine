二、 遊走行為及介入措施所面臨的問題 
    遊走行為是一種沒有目標、沒有目的地而又不知道自己身處何地的行走，遊 
走行為的發生並不僅限於失智症患者定向感的能力減退，同時也有可能是其他因 
素所造成。過去的研究將遊走行為現象分為尋找出口、靜坐不能、自我刺激、模 
仿等四種型態，其中尋找出口者是失智個案有其理由要尋找門以便走出目前的環 
境，靜坐不能者可能是長期服用抗精神病藥物，個案會出現無法休息且無目的地 
行走，自我刺激者可能只是希望有一些身體的刺激，是不是可以離開目前的環境 
對他們不重要，而模仿者只是一味地跟隨他人，若別人離開他也會跟著離開 (    
 Hussain,1985)。遊走是失智症患者尋找安全、熟悉的地方之過程所產生的自然結 
 果，可以為患者提供身體刺激及運動的機會，然而遊走也許表達了患者的不安， 
 對於持續的、激動的遊走行為除了可能是前述四種型態之現象外，也可能是因為 
 找不到路或處於新的環境而感到不安，也可能是滿足精力旺盛者「必須做什麼」 
 的需求，或者可能是需要上廁所的訊號，但有些遊走是患者一種災難式的反應， 
 可能與腦部受損有關 ( Nancy et al., 1996)。 
 
    照護人員面對失智症患者的遊走行為將存在著既要保護他們免於受傷，又要 
容許他們有行動自由的矛盾之中 ( Hope & Fairborn, 1990 )；然而遊走行為將面臨 
高風險的傷害，如跌倒、骨折等狀況，因此針對這些風險採取適當的介入及管理 
係必要的考量 ( Mayer & Darby, 1991)。過去對於遊走行為所採取的不當措施包含 
身體的約束和藥物的控制，其中身體的約束容易造成失智症患者心理嚴重的傷害 
而導致其尖叫或產生攻擊性行為 ( Cohen-Mansfield et al., 1991 )，而藥物的控制 
則容易產生副作用，如妄想或幻覺 ( King & Mallet, 1991)，因此環境設計被期待 
能夠發揮功能而提供失智症患者可以安全遊走的機制。Mc Growder-Lin & Bhatt 
(1988) 更進一步建議應提供一個有意義的徘徊空間 (wandering space)，讓失智症 
患者除了可以安全地遊走而且行動上受到最小的限制之外，並可以具有復建的功 
能。適當的徘徊空間設計可以對失智老人在遊走行為及情緒上有正面的影響，如 
喜好花較多的時間在徘徊場而多於其他地方，呈現較少尋找出口及穿越出去的行 
為，在徘徊場有較少的攻擊行為，坐的時間比走動的時間多，情緒上有較多的愉 
快與興趣，而較少的生氣與焦慮(Cohen, et al., 1998)。 
 
三、徘徊空間建構之相關理論探討 
    對於徘徊空間的建構，在路徑形式上應符合迴路形式而無交叉口與端點，以
免遊走者走失；徘徊空間的迴路可能有多種組成，包含室內單迴路、室內雙迴路、
室內外相連之雙迴路等類型 ( Thomas, 1995)。徘徊空間的建構除了路徑形式外 
，在路徑上需運用哪些環境元素來構成始符合一個有意義的徘徊空間，讓失智 
症患者除了可以安全地遊走並且可以具有復建的功能，而發揮情境治療的機制。 
在過去的研究文獻中，Cohen (1991) 認為在徘徊路徑上陳列過去的物件，如早 
期人物的照片、過去重要的生活事件、失智症患者個人早期使用的用品或家具

(二)   觀察記錄法：針對各遊走個案進行三階段觀察，每一階段為期一星期，該 
星期中針對每一個案進行兩次觀察，觀察方式配合個案遊走
時段，採全天式記錄單一個案在徘徊空間之遊走現象，記錄
內容包含遊走時段、和徘徊空間之環境元素互動情形、和其
他遊走者或照護人員之互動情形、停留、休息或穿越出口等。
每一機構有 2‐4 個個案，研究人員在該星期內進行觀察記錄。 
(三) 問卷調查法：  針對各遊走個案之照護人員採結構式問卷，進行徘徊空間中
對遊走個案具吸引力之環境元素調查。 
        意見調查結果之分析採 SPSS 進行輕度失智症患者、中度失智症患者對於徘
徊空間之環境元素需求描述性統計分析，而針對不同徘徊空間形式之環境元素需
求採 t 檢定，以瞭解需求差異性。有關各類環境元素中各種元素項目之重要程度
採多元回歸法之逐步回歸分析，以瞭解各種元素項目之權重關係。

Although visually impaired people may find it difficult to achieve a survey representation from their direct 
experience, they may be able to compensate with spatial information gained from indirect source. Hence,  
the study of more effective methods for teaching visually impaired people to move about in space is a primary 
objective. Tactile map being recognized as effective source of information about both immediate and distant 
places in the environment contributes to successful wayfinding ( Passini et al., 1986). However, more recent 
efforts have focused on assembly devices and systems to provide support tools for mobility. How to develop 
an assembly device by the components of building to form a sequence of landmarks without carrier for blind 
pedestrian and the cost of construction available for public building are urgent issues. The purpose of this 
experiment is to testify the effectiveness of a local assembly device. There were 60 visually impaired persons 
with one-year orientation and mobility training participating in this experiment. A special education school  
and a government office were selected as experimental locations of simple environment and complicate 
environment. Each case including 8 objects in the environment was set for participant to identify the location 
of space in the order. The procedure of experiment included four phases, the test of origin environment, origin 
environment with a tactile map, origin environment with a tactile map and handrails, origin environment with  
a tactile map, handrails and door signs to evaluate the accurate ratio of testing objects and how much time 
participant taking to complete task. The demographic of participant, ages of persons with visual impairments 
ranged from 20 to 60, 53.3% were men, and they had education from elementary school, junior school, senior 
high school to university. The result of experiment showed that the differences of accurate ratio of testing  
objects between origin environment and origin environment with a tactile map, accurate ratio of testing objects 
between origin environment with a tactile map and origin environment with a tactile map and handrails were 
significant in the location both of simple environment and of complicate environment. It had no significant 
difference of accurate ratio of testing objects between simple environment and complicate environment when 
the components of landmark were applied. With reference to travel time, it took less time for people with  
visual impairments to complete a trip after component of landmark was applied to the route for each phase 
of experiment both in simple environment and in complicate environment even if they walking through open 
space.  
 
Introduction 
Mobility refers to one’s ability to know where one is in the pedestrian environment and to locate 
waypoints and destinations. A major problem for a blind pedestrian is to know exactly where he 
is, where his destination is located, and how to get there from where he is. The “orientation” device, 
表 Y04 5
mandate within building code to provide access to public buildings. How to develop an assembly 
device by the components of building, such as tactile map, handrail and sign with braille, to form 
a sequence of landmarks without carrier for blind pedestrian and the cost of construction available 
for public building are urgent issues. The purpose of this experiment is to testify the effectiveness  
of a local assembly device for the orientation and mobility of people with visual impairments.  
 
Methods 
Participants and Locations 
There were 60 visually impaired persons with one-year orientation and mobility training from the 
Association of People with Visual Impairments participating in this experiment, blind individual  
had the experiences of using Braille and reading tactile map. This experiment included four phases 
and 15 persons were arranged for each phase. A special education school with continuous space  
and simple route (see Figure 1) and the Construction and Administration Office composed of open 
space and complicate route (see Figure 2) were selected as experimental locations of simple 
environment and complicate environment. Both locations were popular public buildings for visually 
impaired people in Taipei city.  
 
Assembly Device 
The ground floor plan of each building was made as tactile map which had a scale of 45*60 
centimeters and consisted of raised print wall-line and Chinese characters with braille signage (see 
Figure 3, Figure 4). Handrail located on the wall about 80 centimeters height from the ground and 
had coding landmarks by braille signage at the end of handrail to identify the location where 
pedestrian was (see Figure 5, Figure 6). The door sign showed the location of space by Chinese 
characters with braille signage (see Figure 7, Figure 8).

Procedure and Survey 
The content of this experiment designed a route composed of a variety of washroom, storage, 
staircase, classroom, audio-video room, meeting room, library, and elevator. Each case of route 
including 8 objects in the environment (see Figure 9, Figure 10) was set for participant to identify 
the location of space in the order.  
 
The procedure of experiment included four phases. For the first phase, test was taken to evaluate 
origin environment in terms of the accurate ratio of testing objects and how much time participant 
taking to complete task. For the second phase, tactile map was fixed on the wall which located at 
the start point of route in origin environment for participant perceiving the spatial layout of the 
route, and test was taken to evaluate the accurate ratio of testing objects and the time participant 
taking to complete task. As to the third phase, handrails were added to the wall along testing route 
for assisting participant to identify the location except for an existing tactile map, and the test of 
accurate ratio and time was taken. Finally, door signs were put on doors along testing route for 
participant to judge the location of space except for existing tactile map and handrails at the fourth 
phase, and the test of accurate ratio and time was taken as well. The record of photographs in 
terms of simple environment (see Figure 11, Figure 12, Figure 13, Figure 14) and complicate 
environment (see Figure 15, Figure 16, Figure 17, Figure 18 ) has showed the testing process.

Description and Analysis 
By the criteria, effectiveness is measured by predetermined objective factors of performance, such  
as the time it took to complete a trip or how many errors the participant made. As to “travel error”,  
it was defined as veering, stop and other disorienting effects when walking (Ungar et al., 1997). 
Nevertheless, the situation of participant being unable to identify self-location for the test object  
was also recognized as “error”. The description of this measurement included the accurate number 
of testing objects and travel time the participant took to complete whole trip.  
 
Descriptive and analytic statistics were performed with the SPSS computer program. A one-way 
analysis of variance was applied to compare the difference of accurate ratio of testing objects 
between phases of experiment and significance was established by using Paired-Sample t test 
criterion. Another analysis was applied to compare the difference of accurate ratio of testing objects 
between simple environment and complicate environment by using Independent Samples t test 
criterion.
