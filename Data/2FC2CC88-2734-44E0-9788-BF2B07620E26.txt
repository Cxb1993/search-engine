一、中文摘要 
    因為利用多個相似的共振模態可以完
成雙頻或寬頻的天線設計，因此如何讓微
帶天線激發出多個相似的共振模態，成為
一個雙頻或寬頻平板天線的研究重點。從
相關的文獻可得知，在一片金屬上，嵌入
適當形狀的槽縫(slit)，只要能不破壞原有
的共振特性，將可以激發出一個新的相似
共振模態，而且只要調整此槽縫的參數，
即可完成不同頻率比(frequency ratio)之雙
頻微帶天線設計，或結合成一個具有寬頻
功能的寬頻微帶天線之設計。再者，從寬
頻寬槽孔微帶天線 (broadband wide-slot 
microstrip antenna )的設計可知，此種天線
主要是由「接地面的寬槽孔(wide slot)」與
「具有寬調整截線(widened tuning stub) 的
微帶饋入線」所組成，並以寬槽孔及單極
(monopole)的操作模態，完成寬頻微帶天線
之設計。因為這種微帶天線具有結構簡
單、容易製作、阻抗頻寬極佳、容易與微
波電路結合等優點，因此甚受產學界的重
視。本計畫的研究重點在於提出一種全新
的微帶天線的設計概念，希望結合「嵌入
式槽縫」與「寬頻寬槽孔微帶天線」的技
術，完成雙頻與更大寬頻的平板天線設計。 
 
關鍵詞： 嵌入反U 型槽縫，寬調整截線，
寬槽孔天線，雙頻與寬頻設計 
  
Abstract  
Because the dualband/broadband microstrip 
antenna can be achieved by using multiple 
adjacent resonant modes with similar 
radiation characteristics, how to excite 
multiple adjacent resonant modes with 
similar radiation characteristics for a 
microstrip antenna is getting to be important. 
From the relative technical literature it can be 
found that if the original resonant mode of 
the conventional patch (without an embedded 
slit) does not be interfered after adding an 
embedded slit, this new design patch will 
generate a new resonant mode which 
frequency will be higher than that of the 
original resonant mode of the conventional 
patch. By selecting appropriate slit 
parameters, dual-band microstrip antennas 
with various frequency ratio or broadband 
microstrip antennas can be implemented. The 
microstrip-fed wide slot antenna with a 
widened tuning stub comprises wide slot and 
microstrip-fed line with a widened tuning 
stub and is operated at both wide slot mode 
and monopole mode, therefore it has a wide 
impedance bandwidth if let the two resonant 
frequencies be close to each other. Because 
these kinds of the microstrip antennas have 
the following advantages, simple structure, 
easy fabrication, wide impedance bandwidth, 
and easy integration with microwave circuit, 
they are being paid much attention by 
antenna designers. To propose and 
implement a novel microstrip antenna design

Fig 2 shows the measured reflection coefficient (S11) for the proposed antenna 
with various flare angles of the tapered triangular stub. From the results it can 
easily be found that if the flare angle θ is getting larger, the length of the 
triangular stub (ℓ1) and the dual-band bandwidths will be getting shorter and 
wider, respectively; that is, a compact dual broadband monopole antenna can be 
achieved. Because the width (W) of the ground plane is fixed at 30 mm, the 
maximum experimental flare angle is set at 39º. When θ is set at 39º, the 
antenna protruding length (ℓ1) can be reduced from 31 mm to 22.8 mm, which 
miniature ratio is about 25%, the bandwidths for the lower and higher bands are 
improved, respectively, from 8.3% to 15.5% and from 20.1% to 32.3%. It needs 
to be noted that the center frequency of the higher band has been increased 
slightly due to having a better impedance matching for the higher-frequency 
side. Fig. 3 shows the measured radiation patterns for the proposed antenna 
with flare angle 39º at 1.81 GHz and 2.65 GHz, which are the center 
frequencies of the dual bands, respectively. It can be found that they are similar 
to the radiation patterns of the CPW-fed monopole antennas and good 
omnidirectional radiation pattern in the x-z plane is seen. The antenna gains of 
the lower and higher bands are 0.3-2.7 dBi and 0.8-1.8 dBi, respectively.  
Conclusions 
A novel compact dual broadband monopole antenna design with a triangular 
stub has been achieved and demonstrated. For the maximum flare angle of 39º 
(the width of ground plane is fixed at 30 mm), the bandwidths for the lower and 
higher bands can be increased about 1.8 and 1.5 times, respectively, the 
protruding length of the longer stub can be reduced 25%, and monopole-like 
radiation patterns are observed. More experimental results for the proposed 
dual-frequency designs have been obtained and will be described in the 
presentation.

omnidirectional radiation pattern in the x-z plane is seen. The antenna gains of 
the lower and higher bands are 0.3-2.7 dBi and 0.8-1.8 dBi, respectively.  
Conclusions 
A novel compact dual broadband monopole antenna design with a triangular 
stub has been achieved and demonstrated. For the maximum flare angle of 39º 
(the width of ground plane is fixed at 30 mm), the bandwidths for the lower and 
higher bands can be increased about 1.8 and 1.5 times, respectively, the 
protruding length of the longer stub can be reduced 25%, and monopole-like 
radiation patterns are observed. More experimental results for the proposed 
dual-frequency designs have been obtained and will be described in the 
presentation.
