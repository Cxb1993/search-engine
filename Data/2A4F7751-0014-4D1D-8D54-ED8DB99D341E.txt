1. 摘要 
   本研究計畫以開發新穎的高分子改質
方法為目標，並據以合成可控制化學結構
的高分子。以不含反應基的高性能聚偏二
氟乙烯 PVDF 高分子為標的，開發可以控
制化學結構的高分改質方法，並據以將
PVDF 高分子鍵結至碳奈米管表面，合成碳
奈米管/PVDF 奈米混成材料。 
關鍵詞:聚偏二氟乙烯、高分子改質、奈米
材料 
Abstract 
 The objective of this proposal is to 
develop novel approaches of polymer 
modification. PVDF was used as the target. 
PVDF possessed modified groups with 
controlled chemical structures was obtained. 
Moreover, PVDF was also incorporated onto 
carbon nanotube (CNT) surface to prepare 
CNT-PVDF nanohybrids.  
Key words: PVDF, polymer modification, 
nanohybrid 
 
2. 研究背景 
高分子材料廣泛地應用於各種工業與
民生用品之中，高分子材料的性質和其所
能應用的範疇和效能息息相關，設計新的
高分子化學結構往往能得到具有新性質與
高效能的高分子材料，這可以從新的單體
設計開發或是新的高分子聚合反應著手，
但都牽涉到繁複且耗時的過程。另一個改
變高分子形質的方式是進行高分子改質，
在已有的高分子主鏈結構上對其進行化學
反應，則可以依分子設計的觀念引入特殊
且具有特定功能的化學團基進入高分子化
學結構之中，這樣只牽涉到簡單的反應，
且可以保有原來高分子的特殊性能或是性
質，可謂一舉兩得。 
對於鈍性高分子，也就是化學結構上
沒有具有化學反應活性的高分子，通常無
法以一般的化學反應加以改質，於是此類
高分子的改質往往必須借助一些高能量的
活性程序作為輔助，以高能量的方式，透
過自由基聚合反應來改質高分子的方式，
則往往無法達到精準控制所反應上去高分
子鏈化學結構的目標。 
PVDF 為性能優異應用廣泛的高分
子，因此，本研究以開發 PVDF 高分子改
質技術為目標。 
 
3. 研究成果 
3.1. PVDF 薄膜的表面精準聚合改質 
 本研究首先進行 PVDF 薄膜的表面改
質，期望以表面引發原子轉移自由基聚合
（ATRP）的方式來達成。首先我們以臭氧
處理 PVDF 薄膜的表面，使之帶有過氧化
基，再加熱過氧化基使之分解，在空氣與
水的存在下使過氧化基轉變為羫基，這是
改質 PVDF 的第一步。在 PVDF 的表面引
入羫基之後，便可以利用羫基廣泛的化學
反應性以進行後續的改質。如圖一所示，
我們利用 2-bromoisobutyrate bromide 與
PVDF 表面的羫基進行酯化反應，便可以將
2-bromoisobytulate 團基引入 PVDF 的表
面。2-bromoisobytulate 團基為有效的 ATRP
的聚合起始基，可以在適當的催化劑和
ligand 的存在下引發 ATRP。我們使用雙性
SBMA 化合物作為單體，進行 PVDF 薄膜
表面引發的 ATRP 反應，並將 PSBMA 的高
分子鏈段改質至 PVDF 的表面。 
 圖二為改質PVDF以FTIR-ATR進行表
面化學結構分析的光譜圖，可以看出SBMA
高分子鏈在改質PVDF表面呈現出的特徵
吸收峰，包括C=O (1754 cm-1)、C-N(1485 
cm-1)、-SO3- (1070 cm-1)等等，以重量改變
計算PSBMA的表面接枝率為 320 μg/cm2。

4. 檢討與展望 
本研究的後續工作仍在進行中，第一部分
我們期待能將樹枝狀的高分子接枝到
PVDF 的薄膜表面，並進行所得到的改質薄
膜的蛋白質吸附分析，此部份實驗因需與
其他研究室共同洽談合作，故仍在積極進
行 中 ； 第 二 部 分 我 們 則 期 待 能 在
CNT-PVDF 的表面繼續進行聚合與改質，
目前已有初步成果，仍將繼續進行。

Abstract 
 Difunctional (BATMS-Bz) and monofunctional (MTMSS-Bz) benzoxazine 
monomers containing siloxane groups were prepared using 
1,3-bis(3-aminopropyl)-tetramethyldisiloxane (BATMS) and monofunctional 
3-aminopropyltris(trimethylsiloxy)silane (MTMSS) as raw materials.  The chemical 
structures of the prepared benzoxazines were characterized with FTIR, 1H NMR, 
molecular mass, and elemental analysis.  The siliconized benzoxazines are liquid at 
ambient conditions and exhibit good solubility in common organic solvents to ensure 
their convenience in processing.  Cross-linked benzoxazine resins prepared from 
BATMS-Bz and MTMSS-Bz showed a glass transition temperature of 87 and 152 oC, 
respectively.  Incorporation of siloxane groups to benzoxazine resins significantly 
improve their toughness and anti-oxidative thermal stability. 
Keywords: benzoxazine, silicon, thermosets

2.3 Preparation of difunctional silicon-containing benzoxazine (BATMS—Bz) 
 In contrast to the solventless method reported in the US patent [32], here a solution 
system was utilized in benzoxazine preparation.  Phenol (7.53 g, 80 mmole), 
formaldehyde (37% by weight in water, 12.99 g, 160 mmole), and BATMS (9.96 g, 40 
mmole) were dissolved in 20 mL dioxane, respectively. The formaldehyde solution was 
charged into a 250 mL round-bottom flask with ice-bathing. The BATMS solution was 
then slowly added to keep the reaction temperature below 10 oC. The reaction mixture 
was stirred for 30 min.  The phenol solution was then added into the flask and the 
reaction temperature was raised to 85 oC and reacted for 24 h. Dioxane was removed 
out with using a rotary evaporator, and the residual was dissolved in 100 mL diethyl 
ether. The ether solution was washed with 2N NaOH(aq) and distilled water to remove 
out the oligomer byproducts and the un-reacted monomers.  The ether phase was 
collected and dried with anhydrous magnesium sulfate. Solvent removal was done with 
a rotary evaporator and the residual was dried under vacuum at 50 oC to give the final 
BATMS-Bz product: 80 % yield; viscous yellow liquid; Anal. Calcd for C26H40N2O3Si2: 
C, 64.22; H, 8.23, and N, 5.76; Found: C, 63.73, H, 8.49, and N, 5.81. MS (m/z): M+ 
=485.  
2.4. Preparation of monofunctional silicon-containing benzoxazine (MTMSS—Bz) 
Using MTMSS to replace BATMS, MTMSS-Bz was obtained with the same 
manner of preparation of BTAMS-Bz: 78 % yield; yellow liquid; Anal. Calcd for 
C20H41NO4Si4: C, 51.74; H, 8.04, and N, 2.80; Found: C, 50.86, H, 8.69, and N, 2.97. 
MS (m/z): 470 (M-1)+. 
2.5. Preparation of cross-linked benzoxazine resins 
The homogenous benzoxazine monomer solutions in acetone (10 wt%) were 
poured into a stainless mold.  Solvent was removed out at room temperature under 
vacuum for 12 h.  The residuals were cured stepwise at 160 oC (1 h), 200 oC (1.5 h), 
and 230 oC (2 h) and at 160 oC (1 h), 190 oC (1.5 h), and 210 oC (2 h) for BATMS-Bz 
and MTMSS-Bz, respectively.  The cross-linked benzoxazine resins cured from 
BATMS-Bz and MTMSS-Bz, were coded as P-BATMS-Bz and P-MTMSS-Bz,, 
respectively. All of the cured samples are transparent and bright yellow in color.  The 
prepared sample was directly applied for property evaluation.

3.1. Preparation and characterization of BATMS-Bz and MTMSS-Bz monomers

3-aminopropyltris(trimethylsiloxy)silane (MTMSS) as raw materials  (Figure 1).  
Both benzoxazines were obtained in high yields of about 80 %.  The chemical

also indicate that MTMSS-Bz polymerization rates were highly temperature-dependent.  
3.3. Thermal and mechanical properties of cross-linked benzoxazine resins 
 Benzoxazine polymers were applied to DSC measurements to probe their phase 
transition behaviors (Figure 6).  Generally, cross-linked benzoxazine resins cured from 
difunctional benzoxazine monomers exhibit relatively high Tg’s, due to their high 
cross-linking densities. However, this result was not observed in this work.  
P-MTMSS-Bz exhibited an obvious glass transition behavior at 152 oC, which is much 
higher than the glass transition temperature of P-BATMS-Bz (87 oC).  The glass 
transition temperature of bisphenol-A/aniline based cross-linked benzoxazine resin was 
ca. 171 oC [17], which was close to the Tg of P-MTMSS-Bz.  The relatively low Tg of 
P-BATMS-Bz might arise from the flexible propyl and dimethylsiloxane linkages in the 
polymer.  The high glass transition temperature of P-MTMSS-Bz could be attributed 
to the presence of bulky trimethylsilane pendants, which seriously prohibit 
P-MTMSS-Bz chain motions so as to increase its Tg.   
 Figure 7 shows the P-BATMS-Bz DMA thermograms, in which P-BATMS-Bz 
exhibited a glass transition temperature of 116 oC (read from the tanδ peak), a 
maximum tanδ value of 0.43, and a storage modulus (G’) of 2.5 GPa at 50 oC.  The 
storage modulus value of P-BATMS-Bz was comparable to the values reported to other 
cross-linked benzoxazine resins [2,12,17].  Figure 8 shows P-BATMS-Bz tensile 
stress-tensile strain curve measured by an Instron.  P-BATMS-Bz exhibited a 7.3% 
elongation to break, which is much higher than the value (1.3%) reported to the 
bisphenol-A based cross-linked benzoxazine resin (P-Ba).  The toughness in terms of 
the area under the stress-strain curve [36] was measured to be 54,750 N/m.   The 
tensile strength of P-BATMS-Bz (50 MPa) is comparable to the value of P-Ba (43 MPa) 
[26].  It can be concluded that the introduction of siloxane linkages enhances the 
toughness of cross-linked benzoxazine resins without mechanical strength sacrifice.  
On the other hand, mechanical measurements on P- MTMSS-Bz was failure due to its 
brittleness. 
3.4. Thermal degradation behavior of cross-linked benzoxazine resins        
 Figure 9 shows the TGA thermograms of cross-linked benzoxazine resins and the 
collected data is list in Table 1.   P-BATMS-Bz exhibited relatively good thermal 
stability either in nitrogen or in air atmosphere.  The poor thermal stability of P- 
MTMSS-Bz could arose from the trimethylsilyl groups which degraded at low 
temperatures.  It is noteworthy that the degradation temperature of P-MTMSS-Bz is 
close to the curing temperatures of MTMSS-Bz (210 oC).  Some degradation reactions 
might accompany with the P-MTMSS-Bz curing process.  Practically a curing 
reaction promoter, like p-toluenesulfonic acid, can be added to MTMSS-Bz curing 
compositions to lower their curing temperatures. While heated in nitrogen, the presence 
of siloxane contributed to the high char yield (55 wt%) of P-BATMS-Bz.  
P-MTMSS-Bz lost its silicon groups at low temperatures to result in its low char yield.
