  
摘   要 
    現今科技日新月異，產品也跟著五花八門、如何掌握消費者難以捉摸的需求，一直
是企業面臨的問題。根據 Rogers(1962)所提出的創新採用行為模式，將消費者對新產品
接受程度的快慢作描述，Moore(1995)則指出市場中的新產品在擴散尚未成熟時必須跨
越這「鴻溝」才可能成為市場主流；如何從創新產品接受程度高的消費者成功的擴散至
創新產品接受程度低的消費者，跨越「鴻溝」成為成功的產品是一大挑戰。本研究藉由
比較對新產品接受程度不同的採用者，以探討消費者對創新產品採用行為之影響因素。  
其步驟如下: (1)把握需求因素:對消費者創新產品的購買原因進行調查，透過評價
構造法(EGM)，整理出 23 個創新產品需求因素，進一步使用因素分析將 23 個創新產品
需求因素歸納為：「產品資訊來源因素」、「產品外觀設計因素」、「產品功能與品牌形象
因素」、「產品耐用及操作愉悅因素」四大構面；(2)以新產品採用行為分群:本研究採用
Innovativeness Scale 與 23 個創新產品需求因素當成量測的標準，用以區分受測者對
新產品接受速度，並根據 Rogers(1983)的創新擴散理論，把受測者區分為創用者、早期
採用者、早期大眾、晚期大眾、落後者等五類型的採用者；(3)不同採用者的差異分析：
採用單因子變異數分析，以檢測五種類型的採用者對四構面之 23 個創新產品需求因素，
是否有認知差異；(4)鴻溝差異分析:採用獨立樣本 T檢定，以檢測早期採用者與早期大
眾在創新需求因素的認知上的差異。  
研究結果顯示，五類型之族群對產品外觀設計因素、產品資訊來源因素、產品耐用
及操作愉悅因素、產品功能與品牌形象因素的認知皆有顯著差異存在：(1)產品外觀設
計因素影響創新採用者的程度最高。(2)創新採用者、早期採用者及早期大眾對產品資
訊來源因素的認知沒有差異；但是早期採用者與早期大眾受到的影響，大於晚期大眾及
落後者。(3)創新採用者與早期採用者受到產品耐用及操作愉悅因素的影響，大於早期
大眾、晚期大眾及落後者。(4)創新採用者與早期採用者產品功能與品牌形象因素受到
的影響，大於早期大眾與晚期大眾及落後者。另外，鴻溝的差異分析來看：(1)早期採
用者受到「DM 及雜誌裡，有詳細比較產品的規格表的影響」比早期大眾來的多；(2)早
期採用者比早期大眾更在意產品的外觀特質;(3) 早期採用者比早期大眾更在意產品功
能與品牌形象的特質;(4)早期採用者比早期大眾更意在產品的耐用性及操作愉悅的特
質。

一、前言 
1.1 研究背景 
現今科技日新月異，產品五花八門、如
何掌握消費者難以捉摸的需求，一直是企業
面臨的問題。Rogers(1983)的創新擴散理論
提出:當一項新產品的問市時，剛開始只有某
一部份的消費者（創新者）會採用，過一段
時間才會藉由這些先採用新產品的消費族群
（創用者）來影響其他的採用族群（早期採
用者、早期大眾、晚期大眾、落後者）。後續
有學者 Moore(1995)，提出了「跨越鴻溝」的
理論，認為許多新技術產品在發展的早期都
只受到創新者與早期採用者的親睞,但其中
卻只有少數新產品能成為市場的主流產品。
原因是大部份的新技術產品很難跨越早期市
場（創用者加上早期採用者稱為早期市場）
與主流市場（早期大眾加上晚期大眾稱為主
流市場）間的巨大鴻溝。因此市場中的新產
品在擴散尚未成熟時必須跨越「鴻溝」才可
能成為市場主流。行銷研究學者對創新擴散
的議題一直很感興趣，希望能量測消費者的
創新性，以便能分配消費者至某一個採用者
的類型，或檢視量測創新性與其他變數的關
係(Goldsmith & Hofacker, 1991)。 
在過去的研究中，創新性的建構，常被
用來量測先進科技的消費性產品的消費者的
採用行為(Jonathan et al., 2004)。從文獻
探討中可以看出，許多學者已經提出了量測
消費者創新性的量表，而這些量表的內容，
大多數都是屬於量測消費者人格本質的量
表，是以人格特質為基礎來判斷消費者的創
新性。 在各式的創新性量表中，我們可以發
現一些共同的問題： 
1. 所有的量表都是以量測人之「心理特質」
為出發點，來看消費者對新產品的採用行
為；從來沒有學者以「使用產品」的角度
為出發點，來探討產品本身的特質對消費
者採用行為的影響，其實，產品本身的特
色，也會影響消費者的採用行為。曾有學
者（George，1975）調查新產品吸引力的
決定因素，與吸引力的反應或者消費者個
人屬性與情境的關聯性。檢視情境與個人
屬性對新產品吸引力的影響。學者
(Joseph ,1999)提出新產品上市策略
(product launch) ,來增加產品對消費者
的吸引力，以減低風險。 
2. 消費者購買不同的產品，一定會有不同的
採用行為，例如，有些消費者在3C產品的
採用上屬於創用者，在其他種類產品的採
用上，也許可能屬於落後者。同樣的量
表，幾乎不可能量測消費者對不同種類產
品的採用行為。所以，才有學者Foxall, 
Goldsmith, and Brown (1999)提出了特
定了領域的創新量表，以量測消費者對某
一種產品的創新性。 
    有鑑於此，本研究目的將針對特定種類
的3C產品為研究對象，加入以產品特性之影
響採用因素，來探討消費者採用新產品的需
求因素與差異。 
 
1.2 研究目的 
本研究藉由比較對新產品接受程度不同的採
用者，以探討消費者對創新產品採用行為之
影響因素。其步驟如下: (1)把握需求因素: 
對消費者創新產品的購買原因進行調查，整
理創新產品需求因素，進一步使用因素分析
將創新產品需求因素歸納其構面。(2)以新產
品採用行為分群: 
採用 Innovativeness Scale 與創新產品需求
因素當成量測的標準，用以區分受測者對新
產品接受速度，並根據 Rogers(1983)的創新
擴散理論，把受測者區分為創用者、早期採
用者、早期大眾、晚期大眾、落後者等五類
型的採用者。(3)不同採用者的差異分析：採
用單因子變異數分析，以檢測五種類型的採
用者與各構面之創新產品需求因素，是否有
認知差異。(4)鴻溝差異分析:採用獨立樣本 T
檢定，以檢測早期採用者與早期大眾在創新
需求因素的認知上的差異。

2.2 新產品對消費者的需求 
  當創造一個新產品時，如何萃取出產品
對消費者的吸引力是很重要的。日本學者
Sanui(1996)便提出一個方法，稱之為EGM 
(Evaluation Grid Method)，是以消費者的
偏好為基礎（Preference-based Design），
所發展來的方法，目的是為了要出萃取消費
者對產品的需求偏好，並有系統的建立架
構，這個架構包含：原始評價項目（請受訪
者以自己的辭彙描述其喜好的原因與理由，
將這些原因或理由記錄下來，作為原始評價
項目）；原始評價項目之上位（將所得到的
原始評價項目，對受訪者追問其背後的抽象
意義，作為其上位項目）、原始評價項目之
下位（對受訪者追問其形成「原始評價項
目」，具體條件與特徵，作為下位項目）。
所以，本次研究也會將訪談的結果，建構出
上位、中位及下位的評價內容，並有系統的
整理成消費者採用新產品的評價構造網絡
圖，最後再發展為「創新採用需求因素｣。 
 
2.3 消費者創新性特質的量測 
消費者創新性之量表最早是由 Kirton 
於 1976 年提出的「KAI 量表」（Kirton＇s 
Adaption-Innovation Inventory），其主要
在探討個人解決問題或進行決策時所傾向於
創新的程度，後來研究發現單獨將此量表應
用在消費者研究上，結果看法並不一致， 
Foxall, et al.(1991)提出使用此量表同時
需考慮到涉入程度來進行探析。1977 年 Hurt, 
Joseph & Cook 提出「接受創新程度量表」
（Innovativeness Scale），是第一套根據
Rogers 與 Shoemaker（1971）對創新觀念接
受度所劃分的五大類接受創新者之特性為基
礎所發展出來的量表，在台灣最早是被運用
在教學領域，近年來開始被運用在數位產品
及網路科技領域，都有相當不錯的信度，相
較於其他量表運用在實證研究中，算是表現
出信度最高的一種。1990 年 Venkatraman 
and Price 與 1991 年 Venkatraman 的研究
中發展了另ㄧ套創新雙向度模式衡量方法，
首次將創新度又再分為認知創新度及感觀創
新度兩個面向來測量，適合業者針對特定商
品鎖定創新消費者後，想更深入瞭解創新消
費者之人格特質的一種量測方式，目前台灣
研究較少使用此種量表。1991 年 Goldsmith 
and Hofacker 提出了「特定領域創新性量表」 
（Domain Specific Innovativeness Scale，
DSI），是第一個針對特定領域所發展出來的
量表，被使用在很多商品和服務上，均獲得
良好的效度。Goldsmith, Freiden & Eastman
（1995）指出特定領域的創新性衡量指標較
一般創新性衡量指標與新產品採用的相關性
較高。DSI 量表適合特定領域創新性的研
究，目前台灣也很多研究使用此種量表。1996 
年 Baumgartner & Steenkampy 提出探索性消
費者購買行為傾向（Exploratory Consumer 
Buying Behavior Tendency, EBBT）尺度，
將探索行為分為產品之探索性獲取（EAP）及
資訊之探索性搜尋（EIS）兩種，來衡量實際
探索性消費者行為傾向。此量表所探討的構
面與前述的其他量表不同，研究者可以依據
自身研究欲探討之創新消費者之傾向，決定
採用之適用性。 
  Innovativeness Scale 及 DSI 量表，都
是以量測人格特質的方式，來評估消費者對
創新產品接受程度的高低，這二者皆廣泛被
學者採用。因此，會從這二個創新性量表選
擇一個，當成是正式實驗用的人格特質量表。

(2) 一般來說，在我的朋友圈中，我是最晚

II. LITERATURE REVIEW
Literary references affecting innovation adoption attributes 
will be discussed with two parts. The first part is perceived risk 
(Bauer, 1960) and the five perceived product attributes (Rogers, 
1995). The five perceived product attributes were suggested by 
product diffusion literature.  A lot of researchers think that 
perceived product attributes belong to abstract dimensions, so 
they develop related research based on perceived product 
attributes (or dimensions) and apply them on diverse areas. The 
other part is an exploration of concrete factors affecting 
innovation adoption, such as price, word-of- mouth, receiving 
someone else's advice, the reputation of the firms, a good store, 
salesperson, etc. 
A. Perceived product attributes  
In 1962, Everett Rogers published the first edition of 
Diffusion of Innovations, which brought together diffusion 
research traditions from many fields. Diffusion is ‘‘the process 
by which an innovation is communicated through certain 
channels over time along the members of a social 
system’’(Rogers, 1995).  
   Product diffusion literature suggests that the following five 
perceived product attributes have an impact on the rate of 
innovation adoption (1)relative advantage, (2)compatibility, 
(3)complexity, (4)divisibility(trialability) and (5) 
communicability or observability of item (Fliegel et al., 1966 ; 
Ostlund, 1973 ; Robertson, 1971; Rogers,1995 ). Bauer 
contributed a sixth dimension, perceived risk ( Bauer, 1960) , 
later used by Ostlund (Ostlund, 1974). Rogers’s desire for 
dimensions “as universally relevant as possible” yielded very 
abstract dimensions. Yet, for practical usefulness, the attributes 
should be “actionable,” meaning “that the attributes indicate 
specific actions the manufacturer must take to build such a 
product” ( Shocker and Srinivasan, 1974 ).
B. Extended Application of Perceived product attributes 
Ostlund indicated that compatibility and relative advantage 
predominate as purchase predictors for low-ticket food 
products. Relative advantage and perceived risk ratings 
received highest importance weights in the case of oven 
cooking bags. Compatibility, relative advantage and perceived 
risk ratings to have a consistently significant impact on 
reported purchase intention for several durable and nondurable 
product innovation categories (Holak, 1988). Besides, several 
studies suggest that perceived innovation attributes dominate 
psychological and/or demographic variables with respect to 
adoption behavior( Holak, 1988; Labay, 1981; Ostlund 1973; 
Ostlund 1974). 
It is believed that consumers want products not for the 
physical products themselves, but for the benefits derived from 
using the products (Ratch- ford, 1975). Haley urged marketing 
managers to focus on abstract benefits rather than on 
descriptive product features when segmenting consumer 
markets ( Haley, 1968 ). 
The degree to which the desired demand outcome is realized 
is shown to be dependent on buyers’ perceptions of the new 
product’s relative advantage and of its compatibility with 
buyers’ values and experiences. Given an understanding of 
these perceptions, managers can then select launch tactics 
designed to clarify or leverage relative advantages or to 
demonstrate or enhance compatibility to the target market 
(Guiltinan, 1999). The particular features and relative 
innovativeness of the product—attributes, benefits, procedures, 
and occasions for usage, etc.—are the primary determinants of
relative advantage and compatibility (Guiltinan, 1999). 
Thus, a new product may have distinctive features or 
benefits, but if buyers do not perceive these to offer significant
gains in utility over alternative products, the relative advantage 
remains low. New benefits that go beyond buyers’ expectations 
may also fail to result in a perceived relative advantage
(Guiltinan, 1999; Veryzer, 1998). If the new product demands 
new knowledge or destroys the value of a customer’s prior 
experience, compatibility is reduced (Dhebar, 1995).  
C. Concrete attributes  affecting adoption 
In a typical model of diffusion, the incremental number of
new adopters over time is assumed to be normally distributed. 
Whereas innovators will adopt on the basis of their own 
evaluations (much like those buying low-risk products), as one 
moves through the curve, the role of word-of-mouth becomes 
increasingly important (Guiltinan, 1999). 
Information from noncommercial, personal sources has been 
found to be particularly influential in consumer decision 
making (Gilly, 1998). Word-of-mouth communications have 
more influence on product judgments than less vivid printed 
information (Herr, Kardes, and Kim 1991). Interpersonal 
influence has also been suggested as a major component of 
opinion leadership (Rogers, 1995).An individual's 
predisposition to innovate is modified by socially transmitted 
messages about the innovation, as well as by other situation 
specific factors (Midgley and Grahame, 1993).
The delay closure reasons are related to the consumers 
decision making. Many delay closure reasons, such as 
receiving word-of- mouth advice, finding a lower price, finding 
a good store or salesperson, and receiving someone else's 
advice, are likely to lower perceived risk (Greenleaf, 1995). 
Price is one type of information that is generally believed to 
be correlated with quality. Therefore, a satisficing consumer 
may rely on the product’s price as a form of readily available 
information, rather than nudertake a more arduous search for 
information form other sources ( e.g., obtaining information on 
product quality form consumer reports) (Olshavsky et al., 
1995). Generally speaking, the result of this stream of research 
suggest that consumers perceived price to be a good indicator 
of quality ( Monroe, 1990 ; Rao and Monroe, 1989; Zeithaml, 
1998).

V. DISCUSSION AND CONCLUSIONS
In this study, we try to apply two different ways to classify the 
attributes of innovation adoption. Furthermore, we rethink 
those attributes suggested by researchers and review all of them. 
Managerial scholars have studied innovative product cognitive 
attribute for many years, but very few experts with design 
background join this kind of research. Therefore, we asked 
some experts with design background to participate in the 
experiment. We wish to discover differences between 
managerial scholars and design-background experts in attribute 
classification to make up for the lack of studies in the field. The 
major contributions of this study are: 
A. Results of KJ method 
The classification result of KJ method divides the attributes 
into seven factors: “social value”, “operational features”, 
“manufacture”, “maintain service”, “usage cost”, “hardware 
design”, “preference”. Comparing them with the five product 
perceived attributes suggested by the innovation diffusion 
studies and the perceived risk indicated by Bauer. “Operational 
feature” and “hardware design” could be grouped in relative 
advantage. However, the former two are more concrete to 
explain the last one which is abstract. “Operational feature” and 
“hardware design” to consumer are both advantages when they 
adopt new products. Dimension of compatibility relates to 
“manufacture” and “social value”—the method of manufacture, 
the stability of the product, social responsibility and the 
influence of family and friends are the features of compatibility. 
Dimension of complexity relates to “operational feature”, 
“manufacture” and “hardware design”. Dimension of 
divisibility relates to the “usage cost” including cost of 
purchase, free samples, presents… etc, and those features 
affecting the market diffusion rate. Dimension of 
communicability relates to “maintain service” and “preference”. 
A new product should have excellent communicability: easy to 
fix, guarantee service to conform to customers’ preference and 
necessity. The perceived risk of the customer should be 
lowered. To lower the perceived risk we have to look into all 
attributes and promote the new product in all ways; we believe 
that perceived risk is involved in all seven factors. 
B. Results of quantitative research 
The result of the quantitative research also divides all 
attributes into seven factors, including social value, installation 
technology, usage cost, manufacture and service, 
want-satisfaction, quality features and software/hardware 
design.   The classification result of the quantitative research is 
similar to the one applied by the KJ method. There are some 
differences between them: “manufacture dimension” (means 
manufacture technology and the method of manufacture), 
“dimension of maintain service” (means the service and 
guarantee provided by the manufacturer), and the dimension of 
service manufacture of the quantitative research (means the 
service and manufacture way provided by the manufacturer). 
And, the “dimension of operation feature” of the KJ method 
(the function features of the product and the operational 
situation) is different from the installation technology factors of 
the quantitative research (means the state of installation and the 
amount of information). Eventually, the hardware design factor 
of the KJ method differs from the hard/soft wares design factor 
of the quantitative research (means the accessibility of 
operation, product function). 
Besides, we can take four dimensions divided by X axis and 
Y axis to explain the purchase attributes affecting consumers.  
Please refer to the fig. 8 to fig. 14.  The first quadrant is 
consisted of Psychological Level and Functional Level.  The 
second quadrant is consisted of Requirement Level and 
Functional Level.  The third quadrant is consisted of Technical 
Level and Requirement Level.  The third quadrant is consisted 
of Technical Level and Requirement Level.  The forth quadrant 
is consisted of Psychological Level and Technical Level.  The 
research discovers coincidently that the result can be authority 
to classify consumers’ adoptions to further researches.
