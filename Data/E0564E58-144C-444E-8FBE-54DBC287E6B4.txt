 ABSTRACT 
  In this study, the plasma deposition methods were used to obtain thin films on the surface of 
polyetheretherketone?PEEK?. Subsequently, the UV-light induced surface graft polymerization 
can provide useful functional group for immobilized Heparin to improve biocompatibility. After 
plasma deposition HMDSZ film, the surface of PEEK become hydrophobic. The HMDSZ plasma 
deposition PEEK subsequent to grafting AAm becomes hydrophilic. After immersed PEI and 
immobilized Heparin, the surface of PEEK still keeps hydrophilic. From Micro-IR spectrum, 
Heparin were successful immobilized on the surface of PEEK. The element of sulfur on specimen 
were confirmed by XPS. From the results of fibroblast cell culture, the specimens of immobilized 
Heparin enhances cell attachment. 
Keyword?PEEK, Heparin, and surface modification
