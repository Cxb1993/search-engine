 III
Abstract 
Keywords : particle deposition rates, wedge flow, electromagnetic effect, inertial 
effect, Brownian diffusion, 2nd-grade non-Newtonian fluids 
The problems of aerosol particles deposition onto surface are a commonly 
observed phenomenon and their studies have gained importance for engineering 
applications or indoor environments. Owing to nonisothermal convection gaseous 
flow; thermophoresis plays a significant role in enhancing small particle moving to 
clod surface. The study in the deposition by combined effects of inertia, 
thermophonesis and other mechanisms is important. In addition, particles suspended 
in the non-Newtonian fluids deposition onto a surface in plate flow are of significance 
in a wide range of physical applications, such as the polymer welding and chemical 
industry processes. 
The goal of this proposal is to examine the coupling effects of Brownian 
diffusion, inertia, thermophoresis and electromagnetism on particle deposition onto 
surfaces. The aerosol flow is considered as electromagnetic fluids and 2nd-grade 
non-Newtonian fluids flow over wedges. Governing partial differential equations are 
transformed to be a system of similar or nonsimilar equations by using similarity 
analysis. Block-elimination method and finite difference scheme are used to solve 
those equations and determine the deposition rates. This project is taken two years to 
 V
velocity profiles, temperature and concentration distributions and then calculate 
particle deposition velocity. In the zero-grade part, the results are agreement 
with the case of Newtonian fluid; in the first-grade part, it has a greater effect on 
angle. In addition, we can find that the particle deposition rates are controlled by 
Brownian diffusion and thermophoresis effect for ultra-small particle sizes. 
However, when the particle size increases, the inertial and elastic effect becomes 
more and more important and Brownian diffusion effect maybe negligible. 
Furthermore, the elastic parameter enhances the effect of inertia.
