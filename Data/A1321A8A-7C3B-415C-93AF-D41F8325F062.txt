RESULT AND DISCUSSION 
Fig.2 (a)-(d) illustrate the SEM pictures of microring P.R. pattern exposed by variable shaped beam 
lithography system. The main reason for microring pattern with wiggly sidewall is described as follow: During 
exposing process, the LEICA WEPRINT 200 system drives the shaped beam, which is formed by two dynamic 
masks in the electron gun, to locate the desired positions such that the P.R. was exposed with segmented 
patterns. From exposing one segmented pattern to another one, the electron beam deflection system deflected 
the shaped beam to the desired position. AS a result, the shape beam has some inaccuracy in the deflection 
location and in the formed size. Those make the margin of the microring wiggly. Fig. 3 (a)-(d) show the SEM of 
microring filter exposed by variable shaped beam lithography system and etched by Lam TCP9400 etcher. The 
rough sidewall will increase the waveguide propagation loss and affect the power coupling coefficient between 
bus and microring waveguide.

With the same exposed process by Gaussian beam lithography systems, Fig. 4 (a)-(d) illustrate the SEM 
pictures of microring P.R pattern. From these pictures, the P.R patterns show that the roughness of sidewall is

wrote the microring patterns on the wafer with the 10nm electron beam by raster-like scanning in the horizontal 
direction according to the segmented pattern with ECA software, so it got smoother sidewall than that with
