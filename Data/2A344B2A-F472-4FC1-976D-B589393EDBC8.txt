研發應更加快腳步開發較新、功能性較強、被取代性較低、保有特殊專利性之配方，以使得

的量隨著體積分率改變而增加，其指出整體失效機率對 率 高靈敏度的
之下，在曲線平坦的區域，其系統誤差對於估算失效機率有較小的影響。事實上，在這些平
坦區中的體積分率 f 是在 V 型曲線尖端或靠近尖端體積分率 tipf 的位置。很明顯的，當設計

6.結果與討論 
一、 在結合完成後的強度分析方面：含包覆金屬顆粒之異向性導電膠其整體結構具有複合
材料的應力緩和效果，惟其顆粒分佈的最終位置決定、顆粒含量的多寡及體積百分比，
如何降低封裝體之內應力的形成及分散殘餘應力，必須採用顆粒複合材料力學的觀點
加入顆粒隨機分佈的概念，以得到量化的數據，並據以由材料配方推估成形的結構力
學特性。 
二、 在機率計算性能提升方面：對於機率可靠度分析方面，需要作數量龐大的迴圈運算，
且我們希望所開發出來的程式能夠整合不同功能的計算，因此在導電電極數量方面將
會強烈的影響到運算的速率與收斂的可行性。已發展二元及四元事件為單元之計算模
式，大大的降低運算項及運算量，顯著地提高運算速度。因此未來對於分析更多更密
的電極陣列應該沒有計算容量的問題。 
三、 在含(或不含)顆粒膠體材料的熱性質方面：含顆粒之導電膠與純膠體的熱傳導性質的
差異，也可透過導電顆粒含量來評估，可由 DSC 實驗比較其差異；由複合材料的觀點，
含導電顆粒的熱傳導性應該比純膠材要佳，但是定量上的結果還是必須做 DSC 熱分析
而得到。對於商業配方膠材的熱性質及熱反應性質均是透過多次的試驗及客戶驗證，
其參數之可信度及精確度較高，目前採用商業膠材為基底材料。 
四、 在多層膠膜設計方面：由計劃申請人所設計之配方模型，其特點是採用兩種封膠材料，
黏度大的膠材其功能是牽制導電顆粒避免過度分散影響導電墊片下方之顆粒數量達到
較為經濟的特性；黏度較小的膠材易於流動負責填充絕緣部分。

在晶片與基板中上下電極高度對稱時是最好的幾何參數選擇。 
九、 新修正之短路模型：本文所建立的短路模型的估算方法比立方模
更好，但是目前的模型仍然不是最準確的計算模型。一個準確的計算必須考慮所有可
能短路的路徑，應該導入滲透定理(Percolation model)來計算，目前正在研究發展中。

5. Conclusion
This study has utilized the V-shaped curve method to examine
the effects of the package geometry and ACF composition on the
reliability of ACF-packaged IC/substrate assemblies. The investiga-
tions have focused particularly on the effects of the upper-to-lower
pad-height ratio (referred to within this study as the asymmetry
ratio). The major ﬁndings of the study can be summarized as
follows:
 The failure probability increases as the sum of the upper and
lower pad heights increases.
 The failure probability increases as the pad-height asymmetry
ratio increases.
 The failure probability reduces as the pad length and pad dis-
tance are increased.

as those found in flip-chip applications. ACF-based techniques
offer a number of key advantages, including a fine-pitch capa-
bility, low processing temperatures (i.e., lower thermal stresses
within the packaged assembly), high compatibility with a wide
range of different surface materials, and the use of nontoxic
environmentally friendly bonding agents [2]. As a result, the
literature contains many investigations into various aspects of
ACF, including the effects of different particle volume frac-
tions, optimal ACF processing conditions, typical electrical and
bond-strength characteristics of ACF packages, ACF-assembly
reliability, and so forth.
However, relatively few researchers have addressed the prob-
lem of predicting the probability of opening and bridging
defects in ACF-based assemblies. Williams and Whalley [3]
estimated the probabilities of nonuniform conductivity and
shorting by assuming that the conductive particles within the
ACF resin were arranged on the pads in accordance with the
Poisson distribution. Yamada et al. [4], [5] reported that, in
IC/substrate assemblies with an interpad spacing of 50 mm,
the presence of five particles per pad was sufficient to maintain
a stable contact resistance under high-temperature (85.8 ◦C)
and relative-humidity (85%) conditions. It was shown that the
corresponding failure probability was less than 10−9, which
was sufficient to ensure a stable electrical performance of
the IC/substrate assembly. However, in practice, the conduc-
tive particles tend to crowd together, and hence, it has been
suggested that the particle distribution on the pads is more
accurately described by a binominal distribution [6].
Mannan et al. [7] developed a computational model referred
to as the box model to predict bridging events in ACF packages.
However, in estimating the probability of bridging failures, the
model assumed that shorting took place only in the pitch direc-
tion. In practice, however, bridging may actually take place in
many different directions within the assembly, and hence, the
actual shorting probability is higher than that predicted by the
model. Accordingly, Lin et al. [8] applied probability theory to
enhance the accuracy of the box-model predictions by taking
into account the occurrence of bridging in multiple directions.
Applying a Poisson distribution to describe the opening circuit
and the modified box model to describe the bridging circuit,
the authors proposed the use of a V-shaped curve (see Fig. 3)
to estimate the probability of bridging or opening failures.
The authors referred to their method as the V-shaped-curve
method and demonstrated its application in a series of failure-
probability analyses [9]–[11]. For example, in [10], the au-
thors applied various probability-based methods, including the
Poisson distribution, the original box model [7], and the mod-
ified box model [8], to analyze the reliability of ACF IC/
substrate packages. Four different models were proposed in
predicting the failure probability of the assembly, and in every
case, it was shown that the volume fraction corresponding to
the tip of the V-shaped curve minimized the risk of package
failure.

PO∩B(f) = PO(f) · PB(f). (7)

C. Stochastic Error
As discussed previously, the conductive particles in the
original ACF compound are distributed randomly throughout
the resin matrix. Furthermore, the particles are redistributed
within the resin following the bonding process. Consequently,
in contrast to the analysis presented above for the systematic
errors, the effects on the estimated failure probability of the
stochastic errors associated with the indeterminate random
distribution of the particles cannot be modeled using an ex-
plicit mathematical formulation but must be determined using
statistical procedures. For a specified volume fraction f , the
distribution of the particles in the pad and interpad regions of
the ACF assembly can be expressed in terms of the mean µ,
the standard deviation σ, and the coefficient of variation (CV)
of the distribution, where these parameters are defined, res-

A. ACF Pattern Generator
In this paper, the effects of stochastic errors on the estimated

the required volume fraction, and the desired CV, the pattern
generator responds by outputting the corresponding 2-D or

regions of the assembly. The pattern generator provides a
quick and convenient means of producing a series of particle
distributions for different input conditions. Overall, the steps
involved in generating the V-shaped curves corresponding to

2) Compute the total number of particles required based

3) Compute the average number of particles in each opening
and bridging region of the assembly.
4) Using the results obtained in step 3), randomly distribute
the required number of particles in each region in accor-

5) Compute the corresponding opening- and bridging-

particle distributions with varying degrees of nonuniformity. In
general, the results show that the failure probability increases as
the particle distribution becomes more random, i.e., the value of
CV increases. The results also show that the tip positions of the
V-shaped curves are significantly dependent on the geometry
parameters of the ACF package. In general, the tip position is
determined primarily by the pad-length parameter l∗2. In Fig. 9,
it is shown that the tip position shifts to a lower value of the
volume fraction as the value of l∗2 increases.
The V-shaped curves for different values of the CV parame-
ter plotted in Fig. 10 enable the stochastic probability error
to be calculated for volume fractions of f = 0.1, 0.15, 0.2,
0.25, 0.3, and f = ftip, as shown in Fig. 11 and Table V.
Fig. 11 shows the variation of the stochastic probability er-
ror with the CV. It is evident that the stochastic error re-
duces as the volume fraction approaches the tip value, i.e.,
f = ftip. Furthermore, for a constant volume fraction, it can
be seen that the stochastic probability error increases with an
increasing CV.
