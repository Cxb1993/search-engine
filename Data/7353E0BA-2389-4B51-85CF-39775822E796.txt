摘要 
本計畫的研究目標為發展先進綠色產
品創新設計方法, 以加強題升我國廠商採
取綠色設計措施到「功能創新 (Function 
Innovation) 」，以及「系統創新 (System 
Innovation)」的層次。將以前期研究提出的
產品綠色創新設計方法為基礎, 配合較先
進的 TRIZ 創新設計方法，如創新演算法
(ARIZ), 發明想法統一結構法(USIT), 仿生
學, 以及綠色演化趨勢等, 並整合綠色創新
設計其他相關方法, 以進行發展先進產品
綠色創新設計方法, 作為設計工程師在進
行產品創新設計時, 可發明出『新, 有用又
對環境無害』的產品及製程, 以提昇我國的
產品創新設計能力及產品的競爭力。 
第一年將進行先進綠色產品創新設計
方法研發, 由 TRIZ 創新設計方法之發明想
法統一結構法(USIT)為基礎, 整合綠色品質
機能展開與 TRIZ 方法, 以發展先進產品綠
色創新設計方法。 
 
Abstract 
The goal of this project is to develop advanced 
product eco-innovative design methods to 
help Taiwan’s company improving their 
eco-design strategies toward the level of 
“function innovation” and “system 
innovation”. The advanced methods, such as 
USIT method, ARIZ method, biomimetics and 
evolutional trends methods, will integrated 
several related eco- design methods based on 
the framework of proposed product 
eco-innovative design methods from previous 
projects.  These advanced methods can be 
used as supporting tools for designer’s 
innovative design task to invent “novelty, 
usefulness and no environmental burden” new 
products or processes. 
     The first year of this project is 
developing first kind of advanced product 
eco-innovative design method. This new 
method is based on USIT method and 
integrated with the green QFD method. 
關鍵詞：USIT、綠色創新設計、產品設計、
環境效率要素、 發明創新問題解決方法
(TRIZ)。 
一、前言 
科技進步已明顯改善人類的生活，帶來
蓬勃發展的社會經濟，卻也帶來環境及生態
的破壞。科技進步的現有成果在未來可能被
環境及生態的破壞逐漸抵消，目前產品的環
境保護訴求趨向於延長生產者的責任，希望
生產者所製造產品的環境影響，從原料選
擇、製程、包裝運輸、使用過程到產品生命
終了後的廢棄與回收，整個生命週期都納入
生產者的責任。 
因為責任延長而考慮更多環保因素
後，企業創新將走向綠色設計，既落實環境
保護的精神與規範，也尋求高創新價值，滿
足新的客戶與國際貿易規範要求。 
為了方便企業主及設計師從事綠色設
計，中華民國對外貿易發展協會設計推廣中
心發表綠色設計手冊，”從減低環境衝擊的
角度提供各種設計上的守則與實例”【1】。

變；分布針對功能(function)的來源物體作改
變；轉化針對屬性-功能-屬性的相互影響作
用。 
 
三、研究設計方法架構與步驟 
3.1 環境效率要素之設計守則與工程參數對照表 
在前述 USIT 方法中，解答技巧需要配
合分析問題得到的靈感來指引技巧的創新
方向。難以捉摸，所以本文根據綠色設計特
有的條件，提出環境效率要素之設計守則與
工程參數對照表做為輔助。 
世界企業永續發展協會(WBCSD) 【8】
指出，願意發展對環境友善的商品及服務的
企業應考慮下列七項環境效率要素。 
(1)減少商品和服務的原料密集度。 
(2)減少商品和服務的能源密集度。 
(3)減少有毒物的擴散。 
(4)提高原料的可回收性。 
(5)使可更新的資源達到最極限的永續使用。 
(6)延長產品的耐久性。 
(7)加強產品和服務的服務性。 
任何一項環境效率要素，都是許多相關
綠色設計守則概念的歸納結果。 
綠色設計守則條列出來其實有很多
條，每一條都是一個概念，在某一情況下可
以用來解決問題。靈感就是使用者針對問題
想出來的概念，所以這裡利用圖形將環境效
率因素背後原本考量的綠色設計守則，一一
指出，作為產生靈感的輔助。 
當 USIT 在進行分析問題階段，如果使
用者利用生命週期評估、毒性潛能指標、本
身經驗，確認產品或服務最需要改善的環境
效率要素，即可利用環境效率要素之設計守
則與工程參數對照表在閉世界圖形中尋找
設計守則提供靈感。按照前述 USIT 方法所
使用的物體-屬性-功能的分類概念，某些設
計守則偏重物體，可以用來尋找產品中具有
綠色創新價值與機會的子系統或零件。某些
設計守則偏重屬性，可查出應優先搜尋產品
的哪些工程參數對環境之衝擊。環境效率要
素之設計守則與工程參數對照表請參照附
錄。

Abstract 
 
This paper applies biomimetics in product eco-innovative 
design task.  A biomimetic design process for 
eco-product design is proposed to develop a biomimetic 
design based on observing creatures, analyzing their 
characteristics, applying the results, and finally 
completing the biomimetic design.  Existing bionic 
design cases are collected and categorized as a database, 
called “Bio-Tree”.  Bio-Tree is used by designers to find 
solutions from the nature when facing eco-innovative 
design problems.  The idea of adding the biomimetic 
design cases into “Innovative Principles for Single 
Engineering Parameter” TRIZ approach as a “Biomimetic 
Principle” to offer biomimetic solutions for designers is 
proposed in this paper. 
 
Key words: eco-innovation, biomimetics, bionics, TRIZ. 
 
1. Introduction 
 
The development of technology plays a crucial role in 
modern economic growth but it also is the key factor of 
environmental crisis.  It is usually emphasizing the 
novelty and economic usefulness of an innovation product 
but neglects its environmental impacts.  Currently, many 
eco-design methods have been developed to support the 
designer for reducing the environmental impact of the 
product throughout its life cycle.  However, those 
methods are focused on the redesign or optimization of 
existing products.  Therefore, there is a need to develop 
a product eco-innovative design method for this situation.  
Currently, there were many TRIZ based eco-innovative 
design tools and methods available for designers in 
designing eco-products [1-6].  This paper describes a 
new method of applying biomimetics into product 
eco-innovation. 
Biomimicry Institute [7] proposed “The Design Spiral” 
concept to find solutions of design from nature.  Hacco 
and Shu [8] used key word to find corresponding biology 
features to generate bionic idea and applied to design for 
remanufacture tasks.  Vincent and his co-authors [9] 
collected biological effects database and integrated with 
TRIZ method.  Mann [10] tried to develop biological 
TRIZ contradiction matrix. 
 
2. Biomimetics 
 
Biomimetics [11-12] is a subject to mimic the organic 
characteristics and to practice these in product design.  
From ancient to present, there have been a lot of 
successful bionic designs made.  These design concepts 
from nature are becoming more and more important 
recently.  Furthermore, more and more designers search 
the design solution of their problem from nature.  
 
3. Biomimetic design process for product 
development 
 
The first idea is how to apply the organic 
characteristics in product designs. A proposed method is 
to develop a biomimetic design process of developing 
innovative product.  The design procedures begin from 
living things, then analysis the characteristic of living 
things and finally apply it in product design.  
Furthermore, this method adds the concepts of eco-design 
into this process to confirm that the new developed 
biomimetic designs do not harm the environment, as 
shown in Figure 1.

3. Definition of product function evaluation 
 
The definition for product’s functions for different 
products and similar products from different companies 
are diversity.  Unification of definition of product’s 
function evaluation is necessary for each kind of product.  
One product function for each product [4] is too simple 
and can not representing the actual product value.  Using 
QFD method to define some (2 to 5) product functions or 
specifications is suggested in this paper for the definition 
of product’s function in Factor X indicator. 
 
4. Calculation of product function evaluation 
 
The numerical calculation method for product’s function 
for different products and similar products from different 
companies are also diversity.  Therefore, unification of 
calculation method for product’s function evaluation is 
also necessary for each kind of product.  This paper 
suggests to including the weighting value of every 
functions in any summation methods for the calculation of 
product’s value in Factor X indicator. 
 
5. Selection of benchmark/base product 
 
Each company used old product of different production 
year as the “benchmark/base product” for denominator 
calculation in Factor X indicator.  For eco-products 
comparison of different companies, it is necessary to 
provide an old product for Factor X calculation for all 
companies.  Three kinds of old products are proposed in 
this paper to provide the unification of “benchmark/base 
product” in Factor X calculation. 
(1) Oldest in production year. 
(2) Worst in environmental load. 
(3) Specify in production year. (For example: 2000 year in 
guideline of reference 4) 
 
6. Suggestions and conclusions 
 
Current Factor X method makes it misleading for 
customers to make comparisons using numerical values.  
Therefore, unification of factor definition, benchmark 
products and function calculation methods are necessary.  
Furthermore, including cost into indicator is very 
important for product comparison.  Total performance 
index (TPI) [6] can be one of calculation method for 
Factor X indicator in the future.

5. Toxicity Hazard Categories (THC) 
 
Toxicity is the potential ability of a substance to cause 
harmful health effects on humans or animals. The 
descriptions of toxicity, e.g. low, moderate, and severe, 
etc., depend on the amount needed to exert an effect or the 
severity of the effect. Acute toxicity involves harmful 
effects in an organism through a single or short-term 
exposure and refers to those adverse effects occurring 
following oral or dermal administration of a single dose of 
a substance, or multiple doses given within 24 hours, or 
an inhalation exposure of 4 hours. LD50 and LC50 are the 
most common indicators of acute toxicity. We could 
access to the acute toxicity values expressed as LD50 or 
LC50 from the 11th section (Toxicological Information) of 
MSDS. This section shall contain a concise but complete 
and comprehensible description of the various 
toxicological effects of the chemical product which can 
arise if the user comes into contact with the chemical 
product. Based on the first revised edition of the Globally 
Harmonized System of Classification and Labeling of 
Chemicals (GHS) published by United Nations Economic 
Commission for Europe (UNECE) in 2005 [5], the 
chemicals can be allocated to one of five toxicity 
categories based on acute toxicity by the oral, dermal or 
inhalation route according to the numeric cut-off criteria. 
The standard scale factors for different categories are 
ranked by rating from 2 to 7.
