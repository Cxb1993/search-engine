Key words: resistive switching, unipolar, filament. 
Abstract. The unipolar resistive switching (URS) properties were investigated for NiO thin film 
prepared by thermal oxidation of the deposited Ni layer on Pt substrate. The turn-on voltage (Vset) 
shows apparent variation, at which switching from a high-resistance state (off) to a low-resistance 
state (on) occurs. The formation and rupture of a conducting channel are commonly proposed to 
explain the bistable conduction states, so called the filament model. The Vset polarity and instability 
are discussed in the light of the filament model and the results agree with the p-type carriers known in 
NiO film. Current-voltage data of the on-state conduction in full-log scale follow the ohmic behavior 
and the temperature dependence shows the relatively stable essence. The off-state current displays 
space-charge limited (SCL) relation at higher field and reveals a positive temperature dependence. 
The dominance of the off-state conduction for resistance swtich effect is emphasized and analyzed 
using the impedance spectroscopy. 
Introduction 
Resistance switching phenomena have been observed in many oxide films, and these effects have 
been investigated widely for potential application in the resistive random access memory (RRAM) 
[1,2]. By applying appropriate programming voltage sweeps or pulses, the RRAM memory cell in its 
high-resistance state (off) can be SET to a low-resistance state (on), while the RESET operation 
functions oppositely and is sometimes called an erase action. The threshold switching voltages can be 
denoted by Vset and Vreset, respectively. Unipolar resistance switching (URS) is usually observed for 
transition metal oxides, in which the Vset and Vreset are of the same polarity. 
Great efforts have been exerted to elucidate the mechanism resposible for the on/off conduction 
and Vset/Vreset switching. The filament model explains the R-switch effect based on the rupture and 
formation of a conducting channel between top and bottom electrodes [3]. The direct observation on 
the filament and the pad-size independence of the conduction current give strong support for this 
model [4,5]. In this work, the URS property of NiO film was examined, including switching voltage 
polarity and instability. The temperature dependence of the current-voltage data in full-log scale was 
examined to probe the transport nature, and stability in on/off conduction was compared [6,7]. The 
impedance spectroscopy was adopted to analyze the off-state resistance. 
Experiment 
A 100-nm-thick Pt layer as the bottom electrode (BE) was thermal evaporated on Ti/SiO2/Si 
substrate, and then the Ni film of 350-nm thickness was deposited on BE also by an evaporator. 
Subsequent oxidation under ambient atomosphere to form NiO was performed at 500°C for 7 h. The 
final step was to make the top electrode (TE) via 100-nm-thick Cu by thermal evaporation and 
patterned by a metal mask of diameter around 150 µm. Electrical properties were measured on this 
metal-film-metal sandwiched structure with Agilent 4155A and 4194A analyzer. The voltage polarity 
and current direction were shown in the inset of Fig.2.
