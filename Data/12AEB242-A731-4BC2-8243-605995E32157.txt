understanding of physical and ecological process and

best be viewed as an experiment, from which

objectively documented and evaluated for scientific

2. PPAs 修訂 
PPAs 乃是一套以系統化資料蒐集為基礎之評估法，針對已完工之河川復育
計畫，逐一評估其復育成果是否符合其設計內容、目標以及在地貌上對河川流域、
泥砂運輸過程長期的效益達成概況，並藉由 PPAs 之評估結果建立一個溝通的平
台；經過其在美國 Maryland 的 Deep Run、California 的 Moore’s Gulch 及英國
Nottinghamshire 的 River Idle、Hertfordshire 的 Kitswell Brook 的研究證明，使用
PPAs不但能定義各復育計畫的成功度，還能應用在適應性管理的學習上。Kondolf
等於 2007 年進一步在美國 California 透過 NRRSS 的研究紀錄，建立了四千多個
河川復育計畫的基本資料概述，從中隨機選擇了 44 個計畫做深入的研究，並進
行分析及歸納其相同與相異之處，給予不足之處建議與改善之方法。另外，
Tompkins 與 Kondolf 於 2007 年在美國 California 利用 PPAs 進行了七個複合河道
形式的河川復育計畫比較，並在其成功標準上判定其是否達成效益。 
由於 PPAs 於台灣地區之適用性因河川環境與美國不同而須予以適當修訂，
本研究以歷年執行研究計畫之收穫為基礎，嘗試進行 PPAs 修訂，提出”學習度”
及”成功度”二項評估參數，希冀修訂後之 PPAs 能更符合台灣地區河川環境及社
會特性；人們可透過評估某河川復育計畫以獲得”學習度”及”成功度”兩個面向的
相關知識，而「成功度」的評估可藉由復育目標與相對之評估工具間分析而了解，
例如復育目標為水質改善，透過河川汙染指數 RPI 之評估，便可瞭解復育目標是
否達成，計畫是否成功；而「學習度」的評估則需透過河川復育計畫 PPAs 組成
因子所相關之紀錄文件中，可用資料的量來加以定義，例如：河川復育計畫中
PPAs 組成因子的”基本資料調查”，當針對計畫目標所施行之相關基本資料調查
愈是詳盡完備，則其學習度較高。修訂版 PPAs 各多的細節和此兩個面向需進一
步研究和確定。

(中文) 臺灣山區河流大型防砂壩移除策略後之泥砂衝擊評估 
(英文) Sediment Impact Assessment of Check-dam Removal Strategies on

 3 
Geomorphic responses to large check-dam removal on a mountain river in Taiwan 
Hsiao-Wen Wang, Colin Stark, Kristen Cook, Wei-Cheng Kuo 
 
Dam removal has become an important aspect of river restoration in recent years, but studies documenting the 
physical and ecological response to dam removal are still lacking – particularly in mountain rivers and 
following major floods.  This presentation documents the recent removal of a large dam on a coarse-grained, 
steep (an order of magnitude greater than on the Marmot) mountain channel in Taiwan.  
 
The Chijiawan river, a tributary of the Tachia River draining a 1236 km
2
 watershed, is the only habitat in 
Taiwan of the endangered Formosan landlocked salmon.  The habitat of this fish has been cut significantly 
since the 1960s following construction of check dams designed to prevent reservoir sedimentation 
downstream.  The largest and lowermost barrier on Chijiawan creek is the 15m high, “No. 1 Check Dam” 
built in 1971.  Forty years later, in early 2011, the sediment wedge behind the dam had reached an estimated 
0.2 million m
3
 and the dam toe had been scoured about 4m below its foundation, posing a serious risk of dam 
failure.  For these reasons, the Shei-Pa National Park removed the dam in late May 2011. 
 
To monitor the response of the river to dam removal, we installed video cameras, time-lapse cameras, stage 
recorders, and turbidity sensors, conducted surveys of grain size distributions and longitudinal profiles, and 
carried out repeat photography.  Channel changes were greatest immediately following removal as a result of 
the high stream power, steep energy slope, and unconsolidated alluvial fill behind the dam.  Headcut 
propagation caused immediate removal of the sand-grade sediment and progressive channel widening.  One 
month after dam removal, a minor flood event excavated a big wedge of sediment from the impoundment. 
Most of the subsequent downstream deposition occurred within 500m of the dam, with alluviation reaching up 
to 0.5m in places.  Two months after dam removal, erosion had propagated 300m upstream into the 
impounded sediment along a bed profile of gradient at 1.4% at a headcut with a local gradient of 5.1%.  The 
change in grain size was a fining of the sediment at the two downstream sites and a slight coarsening at the 
upstream site from April 2010 to July 2011.  This is likely due to the increase in energy upstream of the dam 
post-removal, which has transported the fine-grained sediments downstream.   
 
As the river adjusts over coming months and years, we anticipate that observations such as these will help 
generate an important resource for all those concerned with dam removal and river restoration.
