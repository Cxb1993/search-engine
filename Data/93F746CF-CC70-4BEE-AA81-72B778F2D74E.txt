三、研究方法 
(1) 為了有效地定性和定量齲齒之狀況，本研究使用一般牙醫學中模擬嚴重齲齒
之條件模式，以無齲齒的恆齒，塗附臨床上所使用的濃度為 34 % 之磷酸膏
進行去礦化作用；去礦化反應後，將試片置入超音波震盪器中清洗，以去除
殘留於試片表面之磷酸。 
(2) 將一半的牙釉質進行再礦化反應之處理，另外一半試樣則不進行再礦化反
應，直接分析化學成分和觀察其顯微結構等，做為實驗之對照組，以利比較
有經再礦化與無再礦化作用後各項性質之差異。其中，再礦化反應部分，選
取重複有序的谷氨酸-絲氨酸-絲氨酸(ESS, Glu-Ser-Ser)及天冬醯胺-絲氨酸-
絲氨酸(NSS, Asn-Ser-Ser)胜肽塗佈於牙釉質，再將試片置於人工唾液中進行
再礦化作用，反應溫度為模擬成人正常之體溫 37 ℃。 
(3) 完成再礦化反應之試片，以超音波震盪器洗淨表面後，利用掃瞄式電子顯微
鏡（Scanning Electron Microscopy, SEM) 和原子力顯微鏡（Atomic Force 
Microscopy, AFM）分析試片表面之顯微結構，並藉由能量質譜分析儀
（Energy Dispersive Spectrometer, EDS）、傅立葉轉換紅外線光譜儀（Fourier 
Transform Infrared Spectroscopy, FTIR）和低掠角 X 光繞射儀（Grazing 
Incidence XRD, GIXD）等分析化學成分、鍵結情形和組成相之結構。

Human enamel is a highly mineralized extracellular matrix including 96 % inorganic mineral and 4 % 
organic material with water. The inorganic mineral is a crystalline hydroxyapatite (HA) organized in a highly 
complex three dimensional micro- and nano-architecture that contributes to enamel’s remarkable hardness[1].  
Enamel is relatively stable in the healthy oral environment, where saliva and oral fluid continuously promote 
the balance between dissolution and deposition of minerals.  However, if dissolution occurs at a rate 
kinetically greater than that of mineral deposition, demineralization or dental caries can result[2].   
Currently, the most common treatment for clinically detectable caries is to fill the decayed tissue with 
artificially restorative materials. While this method is effective, a preferred approach is to promote the 
remineralization of small, incipient demineralized lesions before they advance into full lesions[3]. At a 
minimal, this latter approach is thought to reduce the likelihood for decay by slowing demineralization.  
Several strategies involving the use of fluorides, mineral precursors such as amorphous calcium phosphates 
and silicas are currently available[4,5]. Also, casein phosphopeptide-amorphous calcium phosphate 
(CPP-ACP) has been shown to promote mineral deposition and to increase enamel hardness[6]. However, the 
effect of CPP-ACP remineralization is not universally reproduced[7]. Therefore, a need exists for alternative 
peptides that can promote mineral deposition onto dental tissues. 
Dentin phosphoprotein (DPP) is the most abundant non-collagenous extracellular matrix component in 
dentin and is known to play a critical role in tooth mineralization since it is unusually specific to calcium 
ions[8,9]. Human DPP contains numerous repetitive nucleotide sequences of aspartate-serine-serine that are 
believed to promote the formation of hydroxyapatite[10]. Several small peptides have been designed based on 
the sequence of the dentin phosphoprotein, and these peptides, consisting of multiple repeats of the tripeptide 
aspartate-serine-serine, bind with high affinity to calcium phosphate compounds. Of these, the octuple repeats 
of aspartate-serine-serine are the most active in the mediation of biologically directed mineral 
deposition[11-14]. However, the ability of 8DSS peptides to promote mineral deposition remains unknown.

Abstract 
    GeTe0.91PbTe0.09-xBiTex p-type thermoelectric (TE) samples were prepared by hot-pressing under the 
pressure of 333 MPa and 1333 MPa at 380 ℃ for 10, 30 and 60 mins. The thermoelectric properties were 
obtained in the temperature range of 27 – 77 ℃ and the mechanical were determined using nanoindentation 
with a Berkovich diamond tip at room temperature. The sample hot pressed under the pressure of 333 MPa at 
380C for 30 mins possesses the highest ZT value of 0.17. The hardness slightly increases with the hot pressing 
time under the pressure of 333 MPa; however, the hardness significantly decreases of the specimen pressing 
under the pressure of 1333 MPa.   
 
 
 
Introduction 
    The awakening concern worldwide over our reliance on fossil fuels is driving the need for alternative 
energy sources and novel energy conversion techniques. Among them, the 
thermoelectric (TE) technique has several unique features such as its all solid-state assembly without moving 
parts, ease of switching between the power generation mode (based on the Seebeck effect) and the 
refrigeration mode (based on the Peltier effect), low maintenance cost, and its ability to easily couple to other 
energy conversion devices [1-3]. The performance of TE materials is quantified by the dimensionless figure of 
merit ZT = (S2σ/k)T, where S is the Seebeck coefficient, σ the electrical conductivity, k the thermal 
conductivity, and T the absolute temperature. 
    For the industrial applications, the coordination of the appropriately peripheral circuit design and the 
characteristics of thermoelectric materials is required.  Thus, the thermoelectric material itself should possess 
the optimal mechanical properties such as hardness, modulus, fracture toughness to develop the whole 
thermoelectric system.
