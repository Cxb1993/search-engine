multidimensional or multimodal function with good 
probability. Due to its effectiveness, DE was widely 
applied to mechanical units design, data mining, and 
decision support problems. Each individual of the 
population is a vector to DE. DE perturbs vectors 
with scaled difference of two randomly selected 
population vectors and adds the scaled, random vector 
difference to a third randomly selected population 
vector to avoid trapping in the local optima. The 
advantages of DE are its simplicity, easy to use, and 
fast convergence. However, it has the same problems 
of the instability for global convergence and easily 
trapping to local optima as most of the evolutionary 
algorithms. Therefore, many researchers try to 
improve the deficiencies by studying the number of 
differential vectors, evolution mechanism, and 
parameters adjustment. We have applied DE to solve 
the fuzzy transportation problem and compare the 
efficiency obtained with that of GAs. We found that 
DE has the fast convergence capability and 
outperforms GA over some types of complex problems. 
In this research proposal, we would like to study the 
effectiveness of applying DE to solve constrained 
optimization problems, and to compare the result 
obtained with that of GA. Also, we would like to find 
out the effects and performance of dynamically 
adjusting parameters in DE while solving constrained 
optimization problems. Finally, a dynamic, adaptive 
DE strategy will be presented for solving constrained 
optimization problems efficiently.

一、前言 
   過去我們曾經提出「退火演化法」(Annealing-Genetic algorithm) 來求解組
合最佳化問題。此法有兩個特點：(1) 它可視為模擬退火 (Simulated Annealing, 
SA) 但保持一群狀態作為狀態轉移基點，同時存在多條同質馬可夫鏈結 
(Homogeneous Markov chains)，(2) 它可視為一個基因演算法 (Genetic Algorithm, 
GA) 但具有波茲曼分佈 (Boltzmann distribution) 的挑選方式。實驗結果顯示：
退火演化法可平行處理高困難度的組合最佳化問題，提升找到全體最佳解的機
率。1994 年 Kenneth Price 在 Dr. Dobb Journal 發表名叫 Genetic Annealing 文
章，此法是在 GA 中加入 annealing criterion，與我們的退火演化法有異曲同工之
處。Kenneth Price 發表此文後，Rainer Storn 找他討論是否可利用 Genetic 
Annealing 求解切比雪夫多項式問題 (Chebyshev Polynomials)，因為一般演算法
很難找到切比雪夫多項式的係數。於是 Kenneth Price 設計出可解決五維度切比
雪夫多項式問題的 Genetic Annealing 演算法，但是收斂速度非常緩慢，當時也
很難知道如何設計有效的控制參數可縮短需要的計算時間。之後 Kenneth Price 
開始修改 Genetic Annealing 演算法不用 bit string 而是使用 floating-point，發
覺可使用微分突變運算子 (Differential mutation operator) 將求解組合最佳化演
算法變成求解連續性數值最佳化演算法，這也就是微分演化  (Differential 
Evolution, DE) 的前身。1995 年 Price and Storn 將初步研究結果整理成一本技
術報告，名叫 Differential Evolution – A simple and efficient adaptive scheme for 
global optimization over continuous spaces。1997 年，兩人在 Dr. Dobb Journal 發
表 “Differential Evolution – a simple evolution strategy for fast optimization” 文
章，此時  DE 已吸引眾多研究學者的關注。之後他們在  Journal of Global 
Optimization 正式發表 ”Differential Evolution – A simple and efficient heuristic for 
global optimization over continuous spaces”，此文提出更具體的數據證明 DE 對於
廣泛且眾所皆知的測試函數 (benchmarks) 具備執行績效的穩健性。 
在 1990 年代中後期，許多混合式改良的演化計算方法陸續出現，它們大
都是混合 GA、SA、禁忌搜尋法 (Tabu Search)、粒子粒子群最佳化法 (Particle 
Swarm Optimization)、與蟻群最佳化法 (Ant Colony Optimization) 等，其目的就
是希望能加速演化計算的收斂速度而找到最佳解或最佳近似解。而 DE 的出現
給了演化式計算的研究社群注入一股新的研究動力，從此引發更多學者的研究
興趣。我們於數年前就研讀過 Ken and Rainer 的文章，只是當時先後投入做求

四、研究方法 
微分演化 (DE) 與演化策略 (Evolution Strategy, ES) 相近，因此本計畫從 
ES 切入探討。ES 是 1960 年代德國柏林工業大學的 I. Rechenberg 為了解決流
體力學 (hydrodynamics) 做模型控制的參數最佳化問題而提出第一個實驗性的
演化策略方法。Rechenberg 提出的 ES 最初只有挑選機制與突變，其群體規模
只有 1 個個體。之後再加入了重組機制以及群體規模是 1 個以上的個體，這
使得 ES 比起傳統的最佳化技巧有明顯的優勢。然而，他們的研究是建立在數
值分析與機率的基礎上，所以 ES 的每一個單元是由一組實數參數構成的。ES 
的做法是：隨機產生第 0 代的群體後，評估每一個個體得到適應值。假設群體
的規模為 N，使用均一隨機方式每次挑選兩個個體當做雙親，將雙親經過重組
後產生子代，子代再進一步做突變。這個方法所產生的子代數目將會超過 N 
個，接著有兩種方法來挑選其中的 N 個個體組成下一代：第一、只從子代中挑
選 N 個最佳個體，第二、從上一代與子代中挑選 N 個最佳個體。 
早期的 ES 非常的簡單，稱之為 (1+1)-ES。(1+1)-ES 是說群體規模為 1，
每一代只有 1 個個體，經過突變後也只有 1 個子代，”+” 號表示從親代與子代
中挑選出一個最佳的個體繼續演化，這種行為很像隨機搜尋。以後發展出多元
的演化策略：(, )-ES 與 ( + )-ES，其中  是親代的群體規模， 是產生的
子代的個體數目，1    。(, )-ES 是從產生的  個子代中選出較優的  個
個體，完全取代原來的親代成為下一代群體。然而 ( + )-ES 是從子代與親代
中 (一共有  +  個) 挑選出其中較優的  個個體來組成下一代。在 ES 的機
制中，個體的重組是發生在表現型 (實數參數) 而非在基因型 (構成實數的位
元)。重組是以實數為單位而非以位元為單位，因此對於實數的突變作用，產生
了突變強度的問題，引發了演化窗的研究理論。演化窗主要是考慮個體突變的
分佈情形，通常使用標準常態分布 (standard normal distribution) 或對數常態分
布 (log-normal distribution) 來討論突變的分佈情形。ES 具有自我適應的特性，
這是因為可將策略參數直接置入染色體之故。ES 最著名的理論結果是 
Rechenberg 提出的五分之一成功規則 (1/5 success rule)，這是說於平均情況下，
五次突變會有一次能夠改進收斂速率，可縮短接近最佳解的時間。五分之一成
功規則是用來控制標準差，於演化過程中成功的突變次數與所有的突變次數維
持 1/5 的比率，如果大於 1/5 時則增加標準差，小於 1/5 則減少標準差。1981 
年 Schwefel 提出應經過 10N 次演化運算後才需要去維持 1/5 的比率，N 是群

微分演化是快速發展中的新方法，擁有結構簡單、容易使用、與快速收斂
等特性，文獻上記載與其他演化式方法比較，微分演化保留了群體的全域搜索
策略，採用實數編碼，利用微分突變 (differential mutation) 的操作，可降低演化
計算的複雜性。我們曾應用微分演化求解模糊運輸問題，並與先前使用的基因
演算法做比較，發覺微分演化確實有快速收斂之特性，對於某些情況它的表現
優於基因演算法，此結果已發表在期刊上。本計畫應用微分演化求解限制性最
佳化問題，考慮探索 (exploration) 與開發 (exploitation) 的議題，研究結果顯
示，F 對 exploration 與 exploitation 有一定的調節作用，F 較大時有利於保持群
體多樣性和全域搜索的潛力，然而 F 小時則有利於局部搜索和提高收斂速度。
而 CR 值對於收斂速度、找尋最佳值的成功率、以及演算法的穩定性也有極高
的關聯性，大的 CR 收斂速度快但易陷入局部最佳解。本研究確定 F  [0.1, 0.5]

同的 DE 策略有最好的執行績效。現在我們還在使用動態參數調整策略求解困

Abstract. This study investigates how to apply modified Differential Evolution algo-
rithms to solving the transportation problem with fuzzy coefficients in an efficient way.
The fuzzy coefficients including fuzzy demands and fuzzy supplies are represented as fuzzy
numbers. The signed-distance ranking method is used to rank fuzzy numbers in the eval-
uation as well as in the selection processes of the proposed algorithm. The proposed
Differential Evolution algorithm is not only to simulate those fuzzy numbers represent-
ing the fuzzy coefficients, but to find near optimal solutions to the fuzzy transportation
problem. In addition, the efficiency of three modified Differential Evolution algorithms
are evaluated and discussed.
Keywords: Differential evolution, Transportation problem, Fuzzy demand, Fuzzy sup-
ply, Signed-distance ranking method
1. Introduction. The transportation problem deals with the situation in which a com-
modity is shipped from sources to destinations and the objective is to determine the
amounts shipped from each source to each destination that minimize the total cost while
satisfying both the supply limits and the demand requirements [14]. Many efficient algo-
rithms have been developed over the past decades for solving the transportation problem
when the cost coefficients and the supply and demand values are already known exactly.
However, in some real world application scenarios, the supply and demand quantities are
sometimes difficultly specified precisely because of changing weather, social, or economic
conditions. Over the past years, fuzzy sets have been used as a powerful tool to model
and solve this kind of imprecision problems [7,8].
Kaufmann and Gupta [4] first examined the fuzzy transportation problem. Their
straightforward approach is to apply the existing fuzzy linear programming techniques
directly to the fuzzy transportation problem. Several investigators have also discussed
using different approaches to solving this problem. Chanas et al. [1] investigated the
transportation problem with fuzzy supplies and fuzzy demands and solved them via the
parametric programming technique in terms of the Bellman-Zadeh criterion. Chanas and
Kuchta [2] proposed a concept of the optimal solution of the transportation problem with
fuzzy coefficients and an algorithm determining this solution. Tada and Liu and Kao [11]
developed a method to find the membership function of the fuzzy total transportation
cost when the unit shipping costs, the supply quantities, and the demand quantities are
fuzzy numbers. Their method was based on Zadeh’s extension principle to transform the
fuzzy transportation problem to a pair of mathematical programs.
The studies of applying genetic algorithms (GAs) to solving the fuzzy transportation
problem are reviewed as follows. Li et al. [6] presented an evolutionary program for solving
the fuzzy multicriteria solid transportation problem in which the coefficients of objective

3. The Proposed Differential Evolution. The basic concept of DE is that it is a
parallel direct search method which utilizes NP D-dimensional parameter vectors as a
population for each generation [13]. The initial vector population is chosen randomly
and should cover the entire parameter space. As a rule, we assume a uniform probability
distribution for all random decisions unless otherwise stated. DE generates new parameter
vectors by adding the weighted difference between two population vectors to a third vector.
This operation is called differential mutation. In this operation, a mutation vector for
altering an individual is generated with reference to a difference vector. It enables DE
to explore the search space and maintain the population diversity. The mutated vector’s
parameters are then mixed with the parameters of another predetermined vector, the
target vector, to yield the so-called trial vector. Parameter mixing is often referred to
as crossover in the Evolution Computation community [12]. If the trial vector yields a
lower cost function value than the target vector, the trial vector replaces the target vector
in the following generation. This last operation is called the selection. Each population
vector has to serve once as the target vector so that NP competitions take place in one
generation.
Before the population can be initialized, both upper and lower bounds for each pa-
rameter must be specified. These 2D values can be collected into two, D-dimensional
initialization vectors, bL and bU , for which subscripts L and U indicate the lower and
upper bounds, respectively. Once initialization bounds have been specified, a random
number generator assigns each parameter of every vector a value from within the pre-
scribed range. For example, the initial value (g = 0) of the jth parameter of the ith
vector, xj,i,g, is generated by xj,i,0 = randj(0, 1) · (bj,U − bj,L) + bj,L. The random number
generator, randj(0, 1), returns a uniformly distributed random number from within the
range [0, 1), i.e., 0 ≤ randj(0, 1) < 1. The subscript j indicates that a new random value
is generated for each parameter.
Once initialized, DE mutates and recombines the population to produce a population
of NP trial vectors. The differential mutation adds a scaled, randomly sampled, vector
difference to a third vector. Equation (7) is called DE1 which combines three different,
randomly chosen vectors to create a mutant vector.

5. Conclusion. This study investigated using DE algorithm to solve the transportation
problem with fuzzy demands and fuzzy supplies in an efficient way. Unlike GA, DE per-
turbs the current-generation population members with the scaled differences of randomly
selected and distinct population members. We conducted several experiments on the fuzzy
problem for performance comparisons and for convergence analysis between DE and GA.
Three different mutation strategies, DE1, DE2 and DE3, are used to evaluate the perfor-
mance of the problem. The experimental results show that DE has faster convergence rate
than GA on the fuzzy transportation problem and DE3 has the better steady performance
than the others to the fuzzy problem.
