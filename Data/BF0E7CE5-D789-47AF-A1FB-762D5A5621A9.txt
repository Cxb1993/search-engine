摘要 
 
    在本篇報告中，我們繼續頻譜預編碼正交分頻多工系統的後續研
究。在報告一中，我們研究頻譜預編碼採用功率負載，藉由數值結果
顯示，採用功率負載的頻譜預編碼訊號比未採用功率負載的頻譜預編
碼訊號有更好的錯誤率位元率。在報告二中，我們研究正交分頻分碼
多工的研究，我們探討展頻碼與頻譜的關係，藉由選擇有快速旁波衰
減的展頻碼，產生更好的頻譜效能。在報告三中我們證明之前所設計
給頻譜預編碼正交分頻多工訊號的預編碼限制於相同、均勻分布及零
平均數的輸入統計資料下也可以適用並維持良好的頻譜特性於任意
的輸入統計特性。接著，我們研究了頻譜預編碼正交分頻多工與循環
字首及其多重存取。我們設計給新提出的頻譜預編碼正交分頻多工與
循環字首及其多重存取訊號的預編碼限制於任意的輸入統計特性下，
以至於其功率頻譜旁波下降的速率能達到݂ିଶ௃ିଶ，其中J為可設計的
正整數。我們並設計給頻譜預編碼正交分頻多工與循環字首的預編碼
矩陣並滿足了預編碼限制，並藉由數值結果展現比之前所設計的頻譜
預編碼正交分頻多工及區塊分割訊號有更好的頻譜緊密度。 
關鍵字:正交分頻多工，頻譜預編碼，功率負載，多載波分碼多重存
取，正交頻率分碼多工，多重存取，循環字首，保護比例，旁波抑制。

功率負載技術 (power loading) 是一種用來改善多載波系統效能的方法，它的
基本觀念是，在固定的區塊 (block) 傳輸功率限制下，用最理想的方式去分配每
個子載波的傳輸功率，其目的主要可分為兩種：第一，降低系統的位元錯誤率 [7] 
(Bit Error Rate, BER)，其作法是假設所有子載波上所傳送的位元數及其使用的調
變皆相同，然後去調整每個子載波的傳輸功率；第二，增加系統的處理容量 [6] 
(capacity)，其作法是假設所有子載波之位元錯誤率皆相同，然後去調整每個子載
波的傳輸功率及其所傳送的位元數。在本篇報告中，由於頻譜預編碼式正交分頻
多工系統假設所有子載波上所傳送的位元數及其使用的調變皆相同，因此我們的
討論將著重在如何利用功率負載技術去降低系統的位元錯誤率。  
在 [8] 中，最小位元錯誤率 (Minimum BER, MBER) 功率負載技術被提出，
它的準則 (criterion) 是在一個傳輸功率限制下，去最小化系統的平均位元錯誤
率，其目標函數 (objective function) 即為系統的平均位元錯誤率，它是由一些Q
函數所組成，這些Q函數分別代表每個子載波上的位元錯誤率。如此一來，我們雖
然可以求得一組理想解 (optimal solutions)，但由於要去解非線性方程式，所以會
有很高的計算複雜度，另外，Q函數並不是封閉 (closed-form) 的形式，所以我們
得到的解也不是封閉的。因為上述的兩個缺點，作者又提出了一個近似最小錯誤
率 (Approximate MBER, AMBER) 功率負載技術，它的準則與最小位元錯誤率功
率負載技術相同，只是利用一些指數函數去近似原本的目標函數，由於指數函數
是封閉的形式，所以可以去求得一組次理想 (suboptimal) 但卻是封閉形式的解。
在這裡，我們所說的解即為每個子載波上的功率負載係數 (power loading 
coefficient)，這些係數代表每個子載波上所應負載的功率。 
上述所提到的近似最小錯誤率功率負載技術，由於它並未反覆地檢查所求得
的解，所以可能會造成一些子載波負載著小於零的功率，而我們知道功率固有的

第二節 頻譜預編碼技術的介紹 
 
正交分頻多工系統中，脈衝整形（pulse shaping）仍然是主要影響頻譜效能的
因素，如果使用不恰當的脈衝整形函數，會使得位在給定頻寬邊緣的子載波能量
容易干擾到鄰近的通道。而大部分正交分頻多工系統的脈衝整形都是採用方波脈
衝（rectangular pulse），因為使用方波脈衝可以有效地運用快速傅立葉轉換 (Fast 
Fourier transform, FFT) 及插入循環字首（Cyclic Prefix, CP）或補零（Zero Padding, 
ZP）的保護區間 （guard interval）。從頻譜的角度來看，使用方波脈衝當作脈衝整
形函數的傳輸訊號，其頻譜是由一個個sinc函數所組合而成的，因此其功率頻譜 
（Power Spectral Density, PSD）函數的形式見圖（1.3），這是一個使用方波脈衝當
作脈衝整形函數的正交分頻多工訊號的頻譜，其中虛線的部分是將所有子載波的
能量加總起來所形成的包跡（envelope），在這裡我們會觀察到一個問題，由於sinc
函數的旁葉（sidelobe）部分是以 2f − 的速度做衰減，這樣一來包跡的旁葉衰減幅
度其實相當的緩慢，因此溢出給定頻寬且干擾到鄰近通道的功率相當大。

第三節 研究動機 
     
    在上述的簡介中，我們知道功率負載技術可以降低正交分頻多工系統的位元
錯誤率，而頻譜預編碼技術可以使正交分頻多工系統的頻譜能量更集中，所以我
們想到把頻譜預編碼式正交分頻多工系統與功率負載技術做結合，希望能讓系統
在有效降低錯誤率的情況下，還同時保有極佳的頻譜效率。另外，由於 [8] 中的
近似最小錯誤率功率負載技術並未對其所求得的解做反覆地檢查，所以可能會造
成一些子載波負載著小於零的功率，為了解決這個問題，我們將在第三章中提出
一個加入檢查機制的反覆式近似最小錯誤率功率負載技術。 
   在第二章中，我們會去介紹系統架構、訊號模型和雙路徑衰減通道模型。第
三章是介紹使用在頻譜預編碼式正交分頻多工系統之功率負載技術，其中包括反
覆式近似最小錯誤率、相等增益以及最小均方誤差三種功率負載技術。第四章中，
我們分別在只加入預功率負載技術 (pre-power loading) 和同時加入預與後功率負
載技術 (pre and post-power loading) 的情況下，比較這三種功率負載技術在頻譜預
編碼式正交分頻多工系統與未編碼之正交分頻多工系統中的錯誤率表現、頻帶外
功率特性以及複雜度大小。最後的第五章是結論，我們將對此系統所選用的預編
碼在加入不同功率負載技術後的優缺點做總結。

第二節 雙路徑衰減通道模型 
 
首先，我們介紹多路徑衰減通道的由來。在無線通訊系統中，經由天線傳輸
出去的電波在傳輸的過程中，很容易受到周遭環境的干擾，因此載在電波中的訊
號會嚴重扭曲或變形。常見的例子如圖（2.4），環境中如果有牆壁、傢俱或其他
障礙物就容易反射和折射電波訊號，造成接收端會同時收到傳送訊號的直接路徑
與傳送訊號的反射路徑所組成的合併訊號，而通常這個合併訊號因為破壞性加成
的關係，通常強度都會比原本傳送訊號之直接路徑的訊號小，這意味著會造成接
收端訊號的衰減，這就是所謂的多重路徑干擾。另外，在傳輸的過程中，傳送端
與接收端有相對的運動，就會造成訊號載波的偏移（offset），換言之就是訊號頻
譜會因此擴散（spread），再加上通道的效應會隨時間變化，造成所謂的都卜勒效
應（Doppler effect）。此外，傳送端與接收端相對運動所造成通道的效應會隨時
間有增強衰弱的情形，此情形稱為通道的衰減（fading）。而上述的這些干擾與效
應，都會對系統效能產生不好的影響，因此，瞭解通道的特性對設計一個通訊系
統是非常重要的一環。而無線通道的特性可由同調頻寬（coherent bandwidth, 
cB ）、延遲擴展方均根（root-mean-squared delay spread, τσ ）、同調時間（coherent 
time, cT ）、與都卜勒擴展（Doppler spread, dB ）等參數來描述。而所謂的同調頻
寬 cB，一般定義為兩個頻域上不同頻率的訊號，彼此能夠維持相關係數（correlation 
coefficient）大於 0 .9  的最大頻率差。而延遲擴展方均根 τσ 通常定義為同調頻寬
cB 的倒數。至於同調時間，一般定義為兩個時域上不同時間的訊號，彼此能夠維
持相關係數（correlation coefficient）大於 0.9 的最大時間差。而都卜勒擴展 dB 與
同調時間 cT 成反比。根據這幾項參數與傳送訊號的頻寬 sB 及符元時間（symbol time, 
sT ）的關係，我們可以將時變多重路徑衰減通道四種型態 [11]：

Chapter 1
Introduction
In this chapter, the key features of 4G cellular communication systems are briey reviewed,
along with the basic concepts of CDMA and OFDM techniques. To reach the extremely
high data rate in future 4G systems, a new wireless access technique utilizing a much wider
bandwidth is introduced in the rst section. After that, an overview of the spreading codes
employed in these systems is given in the second section. Finally, the motivation is drawn in
the last section.
1.1 Combining CDMA and OFDM
Future 4G wireless communication systems aim to provide extremely high-rate transmis-
sion over a broadband channel to accommodate forthcoming high-QoS applications such as
multimedia messaging service and high-speed wireless internet access, etc. Specically, the
objective of data throughput dened by the 4G wireless communication standard is to achieve
100 Mb/s peak data rate for wide area coverage with full mobility and 1 Gb/s peak data rate
for local area coverage with low mobility, especially in the downlink. To meet the tremen-
dously high data-rate requirements, a new wireless access technique utilizing 50 to 100MHz
1
suitable under this consideration.
On the other hand, orthogonal frequency-division multiplexing (OFDM) is a technique
in which a large number (sometimes, hundreds) of spectrally overlapping but coherently or-
thogonal subcarriers are employed simultaneously to modulate a block of transmitted data
symbols from a single source. By dividing a broadband channel into a number of narrow-
band at fading channels, OFDM seems to be a solution for the future 4G cellular systems. In
fact, it has been widely adopted as the modulation method in many modern broadband com-
munications because of its attractive features such as high spectral efciency, simple one-tap
equalization, and robustness against narrow-band interference. Furthermore, in the context
of rectangularly pulsed OFDM, the baseband modulation and demodulation involve simply
IFFT/FFT, and the guard interval (zero padding or cyclic prex) can be inserted to eliminate
the inter-symbol interference (ISI) resulted from multipath fading channels. Consequently,
most popular OFDM approaches are standardized in the form of rectangularly pulsed OFDM
block transmission. Despite these advantages, however, ordinary OFDM systems provide no
frequency diversity for transmitted data symbols. Some data symbols in each data block may
encounter deep fades in frequency-selective fading (FSF) channels. Moreover, in mobile cel-
lular systems, OFDM signals suffer from adjacent cell interference (ACeI) unless a non-unity
frequency reuse factor is adopted, which results in degradation in spectral efciency.
CDMA and OFDM can be combined to combat their respective obstacles, and in the
meanwhile to maintain the main advantages of both techniques to meet the requirements of
future 4G communication systems. The idea of combining CDMA and OFDM is rst pro-
posed as the multi-carrier (MC-) CDMA transmission scheme [1], where transmitted data
symbols are spread in the frequency domain by a pre-assigned spreading code. In recent
years, the orthogonal frequency and code-division multiplexing (OFCDM) system has been

Traditionally, code selections are conducted in connection with the following two quanti-
ties: the MAI due to imperfections of channels and the peak-to-average power ratio (PAPR)
of the transmitted signals. As mentioned in Section 1.1, orthogonal codes are usually em-
ployed in synchronous transmission to combat the MAI over Gaussian channels. In FSF
channels, the received MC-CDMA or OFCDM signal suffers from non-negligible MAI due
to the deviation of the spreading codes from perfect orthogonality. Fortunately, at a given
receiver end, the desired signal and disturbing signals are affected by the same channel dis-
tortions. Thus, by proper channel equalization, it is easy to benet from the orthogonality
between spreading codes and hence the MAI can be eliminated. Generally, only bipolar or-
thogonal codes such as OVSF and OCG codes are employed in the downlink OFCDM system
as both the time- and frequency-domain spreading codes due to their simplicity. As for asyn-
chronous transmission, the orthogonality of spreading codes is not a sufcient condition for
the purpose of multiple accesses. In this case, polyphase codes are usually considered because
of their good correlation properties that can be exploited to reduce the MAI resulted from the
time inconsistency between desired and disturbing signals, as will be shown in Chapter 3.
BesidesMAI, the potentially high PAPR of the transmittedMC-CDMA or OFCDM signal
is also an important issue that needs to be taken into account. For both synchronous and
asynchronous transmissions, the time-domain spreading code is usually chosen to have a
constant envelope (either complex- or real-valued) to prevent the signal dynamic range or
PAPR from exploding. For example, the envelopes of OVSF, OCG, and polyphase codes
are all constant. On the other hand, the frequency-domain spreading codes may cause high

1.3 Motivation
When input data are not properly correlated, the power spectrums of rectangularly pulsed
OFDM signals exhibit large power spectral sidelobes rolling off as f 2, with f being the
frequency in Hertz. Troublesomely, large power spectral sidelobes lead to severe adjacent
channel interference (AChI) between communication systems utilizing adjacent frequency
bands. In order to reduce AChI, insertion of guard bands is commonly applied between
adjacent bands, which results in degradation in spectral efciency. On the other hand, in
the signal-processing point of view, various techniques have been considered to suppress
sidelobe powers, such as frontend ltering, data weighting, and spectral precoding. However,
these techniques inevitably raise the implementation complexity or bit-error probability to a
greater or lesser degree. Since spreading operations are already conducted in MC-CDMA
and OFCDM systems for the purpose of multiple accesses, it is worth to relate the spectral
characteristics with the employed spreading codes. Although several assigning principles
for spreading codes have been proposed in [2]-[5], none of these works concerns about the
AChI resulted from the spectral sidelobes of the MC-CDMA or OFCDM signal. This report
investigates the general spectral sidelobe properties of MC-CDMA and OFCDM signals,
aiming to propose new assigning principles for OVSF, OCG, and polyphase codes subject to
spectral sidelobe suppression.
Furthermore, when OVSF codes are employed in MC-CDMA or OFCDM systems for
a certain user to separate its multiple transmissions, proper management is needed to avoid
the OVSF code tree becoming too fragmented. The fragmentation of the OVSF code tree
may cause a severe code-blocking problem which leads to inefcient bandwidth utilization,
as will be shown in Section 4.3. In [9], three code tree management strategies are proposed
to reduce the code-blocking probability, such as random, leftmost, and crowded-rst strate-
gies. However, since none of the works in [9] concerns about the spectral sidelobes of the

When OVSF codes are employed in MC-CDMA or OFCDM systems for a certain infor-
mation source (base-station or user-equipment) to separate its multiple transmissions, proper
management is needed to avoid the OVSF code tree becoming too fragmented (i.e., all oc-
cupied codes being widely distributed over the tree.) The fragmentation of the OVSF code
tree may result in too many blocked codes and hence inefcient bandwidth utilization. More
precisely, when a new call arrives requesting for a code with a certain data rate, it might be
rejected even though there still have enough capacity. This is called the code-blocking prob-
lem. For example, when a newly arrived call requests to the OVSF code tree in g. 4.5 for
a code with data rate 2R, it will be rejected for no space available. On the other hand, if the
formerly occupied code h8;0 has been replaced by h8;6 to support the same transmission, then
the newly arrived call can be supported by h4;0.
In [9], three strategies of OVSF code tree management are proposed to reduce the code-
blocking probability. That is, the random, the leftmost, and the crowded-rst strategies. For
a newly arrived call, the random strategy randomly picks one code with the demanded data
45
succeeding call requesting to a partially occupied OVSF code tree, the following strategy can
be applied.
Ancestor-First (AF) Strategy If the tree has enough capacity, code assignment is conducted
according to table 1 for those unmasked codes (rather than just unblocked codes); Otherwise,
the call is rejected. After the assignment, code replacement is needed for those formerly
occupied codes being masked by the newly assigned code. Whenever a code is released, code
replacement is also needed to avoid the codes with larger sidelobe-decaying orders being idle.
Although the above scenario considered a dynamic code assignment for each incoming
call, the AF strategy is actually equivalent to a static code assignment for each new state of
the OVSF code tree. Given any state of an OVSF code tree, the AF strategy results in an
optimal assignment for those codes with the shortest length in the sense of spectral sidelobe
suppression, then an optimal assignment for those unmasked codes in the next layer, and so
on. That is to say, the AF strategy guarantees the priority of higher layers to choose their
codes with larger sidelobe-decaying orders, which leads to the highest spectral compactness
of the overall transmitted MC-CDMA signal, as can be seen from the following proposition.
Proposition 5 The AF strategy yields the maximal global sidelobe-decaying order for each
state of the OVSF code tree (see Appendix F.)
Note that in the AF strategy, instant code replacement is applied not only to achieve good
spectral sidelobe suppression, but also to solve the code-blocking problem completely, at the
expense of too much times of code replacement.

Chapter 5
Conclusion
This report investigates the spectral sidelobe properties of MC-CDMA and OFCDM signals.
By expressing the equivalent baseband PSD of MC-CDMA and OFCDM signals, it is shown
that only the frequency-domain spreading codes can affect the sidelobe powers, whereas the
time-domain spreading codes have nothing to do with the decaying rate of the power spec-
tral sidelobes. For the frequency-domain spreading codes, a general fast-sidelobe-decaying
property is observed to provide small power spectral sidelobes decaying asymptotically as
f 2K 2, withK being an integer-valued parameter. Given the length of the frequency-domain
spreading code, a limitation on the resulted sidelobe-decaying rate is also obtained.
By proper arrangement, it is shown that the spectral sidelobe properties of theMC-CDMA
or OFCDM signal with (frequency-domain spreading) OVSF and OCG codes can be com-
pletely described by their code indices. According to this description, new assigning princi-
ples for (frequency-domain spreading) OVSF and OCG code are proposed subject to spectral
sidelobe suppression. Also, all polyphase codes are shown to yield the same spectral sidelobe
property, and hence a new assigning principle is not necessary.
Furthermore, a new code tree management strategy for OVSF code is proposed to max-
imize the sidelobe-decaying rate of the overall transmitted MC-CDMA or OFCDM signal,
