Abstract 
The quality of opto-electronic products has been improved greatly and continuously, and has a 
trend to be complicated and multifunction recently. The complicated optical simulation software 
and ultra-precision CNC machine technology has been possible to realize freeform optics, 
because of the massive progress of computer technology. In this sub-project (subproject 2) “The 
Development of Ultra-precision Machining Technology for the Components of Medical Lighting 
and Bio-medical Inspection”, we develop the surface machining and inspection technologies 
based on the optical design of subproject 1. The optical design of subproject 1 is LED reflection 
mirror with off-axis freeform surface which has a large sag variation. After surface measurement, 
we compensated the form error to the machining tool path to get a high form accuracy surface 
that matched the requirement of the subproject 1. 
Keywords: freeform optics, ultra-precision machining.

Development of the micro displacement measurement 
system based on astigmatic method 
Wei-Yao Hsu,* Zong-Ru Yu, Po-Jui Chen, Ching-Hsiang Kuo, and Chi-Hung Hwang 
Instrument Technology Research Center,  
National Applied Research Laboratories 
Hsinchu, Taiwan 
hsuweiyao@itrc.narl.org.tw 
 
 
Abstract—A non-contact micro displacement measurement 
system with a long working distance is presented based on the 
astigmatic technology. The astigmatic method principle is to 
detect the shape change of the reflected laser beam on a four-
quadrant photodiode. The laser beam shape changes with respect 
to the positions of the reflect mirror, because of the focal length 
difference of the astigmatic lens in sagittal and tangential planes. 
The test results of the implemented micro displacement 
measurement system show good performances of accuracy and 
reliability. 
Keywords- displacement measurement; astigmatic 
I.  INTRODUCTION  
The development of the components and devices of 
precision manufacturing and inspection system is important for 
increasing product competition. Metrology technology is 
always the vital factor of the precision engineering. After the 
laser applications are popular and the progress of 
optoelectronic technologies grows rapidly, there are many 
optical methods for dimensional metrology [1], such as laser 
interferometer for large range displacement [2], laser triangular 
method [3] and its combination with diffraction grating [4], 
confocal chromatic method [5], and the simultaneous 
measurement of linear and angular displacement using laser 
interferometer and four-quadrant photodiode [6]. 
We developed a non-contact micro displacement 
measurement system with a long working distance based on 
astigmatic method. The astigmatic method has been widely 
adapted for autofocusing of the compact disk/digital versatile 
disk (CD/DVD) pickup head. There are many studies about the 
astigmatic theory [7-9] and its applications [9-15]. The 
astigmatic model has been formulated based on the analytical 
geometric theory in documents [7, 9] and the effect of light 
intensity distribution was introduced in paper [8]. The 
applications of astigmatic method, such as confocal compact 
scanning optical microscope, optical accelerometers, 
autofocusing function for probe profile measuring and 
microscope, and thickness measurement for transparent plates 
have been introduced in documents [9-15]. 
Fig. 1 shows the configuration of the displacement 
measurement system based on the astigmatic method. The 
system is constructed by a collimated laser, a polarized beam 
splitter (PBS), a 1/4 wave plate, an objective lens, an astigmatic 
lens, and a four-quadrant photodiode. As shown in Fig. 1, the 
collimated laser beam passes sequentially through the polarized 
beam splitter, 1/4 wave plate, and the objective lens, and 
arrives the target plane. The laser beam is reflected by the 
target back to the PBS and then passes through the astigmatic 
lens. Finally, the reflected laser beam strikes a four-quadrant 
photodiode. Because of the focal length difference in sagittal 
and tangential plane of the astigmatic lens, the target plane 
position change brings a corresponding shape change of the 
reflected beam on the four-quadrant photodiode. There is a 
one-to-one relationship between the shape change of the 
reflected laser beam and the target displacement inside the 
working range. Therefore, the system can find the displacement 
of the target by detecting the shape change of the reflected laser 
beam.

astigmatic theory of the micro displacement system and its 
design criteria; the implementation and measurement data are 
revealed in section 3; and conclusions are given in section 4.

IV. CONCLUSIONS 
A new micro displacement measurement system with a 
long working distance of 37.5mm and a working range of f
100µm has been implemented. We introduced the design rules 
and alignment procedure of the micro displacement 
measurement system. The results of the measurement show 
that the system has error less than 0.6µm after the polynomial 
parameter fitting of the FES curve is conducted..
