行政院國家科學委員會專題研究計畫成果報告
競爭電力市場下短期風能預測之研究及其在市場設計與操作成本的影響
計畫編號：NSC 96-2221-E-346-006-
執行期限：96年8月1日至97年7月31日
主持人：吳元康 博士 國立澎湖科技大學電機工程學系
共同主持人：洪景山 博士 交通部中央氣象局
中文摘要
由於風機技術的進步以及裝機成本的降低，風力發電預期將對全球再生能源目標產值產生巨大的貢
獻。然而，它相對也帶來系統規劃與操作許多新的挑戰。在平衡發電及負載之管理上，風能的可預
測性扮演非常重要的角色。假如風能變化能有效的預測，即使是風能占比高的系統，其額外所需增
加的操作成本仍將可減少。因此，精確及可靠的風力預測可為是否增加風機容量的最主要因素。本
論文將逐一研究目前全球風力預測的技術與現況，並針對澎湖本島進行實際的風力發電量預測，其
中包括極短時間及短時間風力預測。預測模組輸入包含數值氣象預測值以及即時量測數據，而輸出
為多機組總風能之預測值。此外，亦分析風力預測技術應用於高佔比風力系統上的效益。
Abstract
Wind power is expected to contribute significantly to the renewable energy targets owing to advancements
in wind technologies, falling capital costs, abundance of free resource and commercial viability. However,
it also brings into a lot of new challenges for the power network planning and operation. The predictability
of wind power in managing load and generation balance is very important. If the fluctuations of wind were
perfectly predictable, the additional cost of operating the system with a large penetration of wind power
would be reduced. Therefore, accurate and reliable forecasting systems for wind power are widely
recognized as a major contribution for increasing wind penetration. The target of this paper is to
investigate the state-of-the-art wind power forecast technologies all over the world and predict wind power
generated by Penghu wind farm. The short term and very short term forecasts have been taken into
account. This prediction module will be developed with several statistical models and ANN structures to
capture the relations between input valuables (numerical weather prediction (NWP) based on
meteorological information and online measurement data) and the output valuable (future total wind park
power).
壹、前言與研究目的
全球風力占比逐年提高，然而相對亦衍生
許多技術與經濟上的挑戰：如電力交易市場設
計、電力調度、併聯技術、輔助服務、傳輸容
量擴充、電力系統品質、穩定度、以及可靠度
等問題，其中風力輸出的不穩定性是風力發展
的最大障礙。近年來由於監控系統、風力預測、
儲能系統、分散式電源，以及負載管理技術不
斷進步，已大幅降低風力輸出不穩定性對系統
所造成的衝擊，特別是風力預測技術，已被公
認是增加風力占比最主要的因素之一。優質的
風力預測可降低備載容量成本、協助系統操作
者網路管理及防止傳輸容量壅塞、並可建立良
好的市場交易機制。在歐洲，結合 7 個國家共
22個學術研究單位以 4年的時間執行ANEMOS
計劃[1]，該計劃的目標為發展先進的風力預測
模型：包括人工智慧、統計模型、物理模型，
以及互相結合的方法。而從 2008 年 1 月開始，
歐洲更衍生相關持續性計劃 ANEMOS.plus，進
一步研發新的智慧型風力管理預測技術，並搭
配電力市場的交易機制[2]，可見此一議題的重
要性。此外，全球各主要電力市場（特別是風
能占比高的地區），包括北歐（Nord Pool）、加
州、西班牙等電力系統操作者均已開始執行相
關的風力預測工作，一般搭配該電力市場的運
行架構分別進行一日前、一小時前，以及其他
領前時間的預測工作。
相較於負載及電價波形，風速並不明顯存
在每日、每週、甚至每月的週期性，使得風力
預測過程中並不易發現過去何筆歷史資料對未
來預測的重要性，進而增加預測的困難度。此
外，由於大氣環境及地形的影響，風力預測模
型及預測經驗存在高度的地域性，換言之，某
一地區的風力預測模型可能僅適用於當地，並
無法應用於其他地形或地區的預測工作[3]。舉
(如 AR、ARMA、ARIMA、ARMAX)及非線性
的時間序列模型。一般文獻主要應用此類模型
進行 1 小時至數小時前的風速或風力預測[4]。
相較於 NWP 預測模式，此類模型的優點及主要
訴求在於提供簡易、快速，以及便宜的預測方
式，因為並不需要高成本的計算機與數值分析
軟體，然而當預測領前時間愈長，相對的預測
精確度也愈差。加州 ISO 初期即是以 ARMA 統
計模型作為短期風力預測的相關工具。
三、人工智慧預測
典型人工智慧預測模型大略包含類神經網
路（ANN）以及結合類神經與模糊系統的預測
模式。ANN 模式的優點在於能學習輸入與輸出
間的關係而不需要提供轉換的數學函式，這種
特性特別適合風力預測的工作，因為風力預測
具有不同變數的多輸入（包括各種大氣因子如
壓力及溫度、歷史量測風速及風力資訊）以及
相異變數間轉換的特性，而此種特性並不容易
以特定的數學函式來呈現。進一步來說，風力
預測涵蓋風速預測以及風速與風力輸出間的轉
換。早期部份預測模式先進行風速預測，接著
藉由轉換特性曲線（manufacturer power curve）
將風速預測值對應至可能的風力輸出。然而隨
著風機數量的增加而形成風場，加上風機運轉
老化影響，風速與風力間的對應關係相對複雜
許多，很難以固定的轉換特性曲線來表示，因
此可以ANN技術來轉換此一複雜的非線性且時
變的關係，進而獲得較佳的預測結果。
建構ANN風力預測模型首重評估輸入變數
的個數與種類，特別應選擇與未來高度相關的
預測因子。換言之，輸入變數的選擇應先進行
相關性（correlation）分析，找出重要的影響因
子並藉此架構預測模型的輸入格式。大部分的
文獻指出[4][7]，類神經網路輸入應包含氣象預
報資訊以及即時量測數值。此外，選擇神經網
路的架構以及學習方法是另一項重點，一般較
常應用的網路架構為前向式（feed forward）之
倒傳遞網路(BPN)以及回饋式（recurrent）架構，
而訓練演算法常採用 Levenber-Marquardt 方
法。一般應用 ANN 預測模式進行的預測時間範
圍相當廣，由數秒鐘至數小時前的風速或風力
預測均有文獻報導[7]。
四、混合型（hybrid）預測模式及其他方法
大部分商業應用的預測模式屬於混合型模
式，典型的方法包括結合 NWP 與統計模型（或
人工智慧預測方式）、結合神經網路及模糊系統
的預測模式（如 ANFIS）[8]，以及結合短期與
中期的完整預測模式。舉例而言，典型混合式
預測模型首先接受來自數值天氣預報結果以及
即時風速測量訊號，並利用統計模型及人工智
慧程序轉換為風力預測結果。近年來亦有學者
利用灰色模型 GM(1,1)[9]、利用鄰近風能測量
及相關性分析推估法[10]、以及機器學習演算法
（Support Vector Machine, SVM）等預測風力。
五、高風力佔比系統的因應對策
當風力佔比逐漸提高，上百台的風力機組
發電量逐漸可取代傳統火力機組，然而這些風
機分佈於輸電及配電系統各處，因此必須強化
風力機組間的在線（on line）監控及管理能力，
例如溫度、風速及風力、負載，以及其他系統
變數的監控。
目前許多歐洲國家風機採用群聚式的併接
方法，並成立風力調度中心來統一調度管理所
屬風機，特別在西班牙，其風力系統調度中心
採用階層式（hierarchical）控制架構，如圖 1 所
示。而葡萄牙風力系統亦採用相類似的中央控
制架構。許多風機製造商以及系統操作者已經
強調遠端監控風機的重要性，因此國際電工協
會 IEC （ International Electrotechnical
Commission）已開始發展新的國際通訊標準 IEC
61400-25-XX，以協助建立風機間的通訊聯繫準
則。此外，若系統中的風機分別併接於輸電端
與配電端，則輸電系統操作者(TSO)與配電系統
操作者(DSO)之間的聯繫與通訊規範亦有其必
要性[11][12]。

（三）、10 分鐘前風力預測
全球許多電力市場為每間格 10 分鐘或數十
分鐘依據市場最新情況與系統安全考量重新執
行機組調度（re-dispatch），因此在市場交易環
境下，極短時間如 10 分鐘前的風力預測亦有其
必要性。雖然澎湖目前僅靠尖山及中屯風力電
廠發電，且台灣電力並未存在市場機制，但是
未來一旦澎湖風力佔比提高，且台澎海底電纜
架設完成，則澎湖數分鐘的短期風力變動將可
能對整個電力系統產生某一程度的影響。若能
及早預測未來數分鐘的風力變動，將有助於系
統操作者的應變能力，維護系統的安全與穩定
性。
一般極短期（如數分鐘前）的預測不易使

在高風力佔比的系統中，除了風能變動的
不確定性因素需要妥善因應外，目前在技術面

路故障，系統所產生的電壓突降(voltage dip)很

制能力，以降低頻率驟降的衝擊。而為了確保
風力機組在系統電壓突降時可提供足夠虛功的
能力，以維持系統電壓在某一限制範圍之內，
部分國外系統操作者要求風機必須具備虛功支
援能力。總之，為了安全併接風力機組至電網，
系統操作者已陸續進行併接相關之研究工作，
其中 FRTC 需求、系統頻率響應需求、輸電電
壓及虛功能力需求，以及通訊需求均是重要的
議題，且被列入 Grid Code 規範中。這些都是
未來台灣面對高佔比風力系統環境下有待努力
解決的課題。
準確的風力預測技術所帶來的效益評估是
一個複雜的問題：它將可提高電網穩定性、風
能佔比提高、節省燃料成本、減少污染排放。
因此實際上在計算效益時應納入所有可能的收
益評價。本文僅就風力預測對於系統發電不確
定度做一評估分析，證明風力預測可大幅降低
機組調度的不確定風險，特別是高佔比風能的
系統中。
目前大部分國外文獻在風力預測的研究中
並未考慮機組可靠度（reliability）的問題，實際
上由於機組故障等因素將會導致有風卻無法發
電的情況。因此在預測上，若能再考慮機組的
可靠度問題，可獲得更為客觀的預測結果。

II. RENEWABLE ENERGY TRADING IN THE NORDIC ELECTRICITY MARKET
The Nordic electricity market was established on 1st January 1996 as the first international electricity market in
the world. It is divided into three main parts: the physical market, the financial market, and clearing services. Fig. 1
presents an overview of the essential periods under the Nordic electricity market.
Fig. 1. Essential trading periods under the Nordic electricity market
The financial market includes Eltermin and Eloptions markets. The Eltermin is a financial market for trading
contracts used primarily for price hedging and risk management. Contracts can be traded for delivery up to 4 years in
advance; different contract series cover days, weeks, blocks, seasons, and years. There are two main categories of
contracts: futures and forwards. Another financial market is Eloption. It provides financial instruments,
European-style power options, for price hedging of future income. The combined use of electric power options and
forward and futures contracts would offer greater opportunities for spreading and handling risk associated with
power trading.
The spot (Elspot) and balancing (Elbas) markets are referred to as the physical markets. The Elspot is a contractual
market where contracts are traded daily in the Nordic Power Exchange for next-day physical delivery. Its function is
similar to the day-ahead energy market. The system spot price is determined by the intersection point of the

Fig. 2 presents an overview of the essential periods under NETA: In the long-term forward and short-term spot
markets, most bilateral trades of electricity for physical delivery are conducted by OTC (over the counter) and power

their expected outputs and demands, which is termed the final (firm) physical notifications (FPNs). They may also

At the core under BETTA are the Balancing Mechanism and Imbalance Settlement processes. During the Balancing
Mechanism period, the SO balances actual demand and generation by accepting appropriate bids and offers. The SO
attempts to minimize balancing costs while recognising system constraints. The only compulsory part of BETTA
system is the imbalance settlement process. In the process, market participants would be paid or charged for
electricity on the basis of the difference between their ex-ante contract volumes and metered physical volumes. Two
cash-out prices are calculated for each settlement period. One price, the System Sell Price (SSP), is paid to trading
parties who have a net surplus of imbalance energy, and the other, the System Buy Price (SBP), is paid by the trading
parties who have a net deficit of imbalance energy. These prices are designed to reflect the prices associated with the
Balancing Mechanism Offers and Bids selected by the SO to balance the energy flows in the system.
NETA has created an environment where 98% of electricity is sold like a commodity, and only 2% of electricity is
traded in the balancing mechanism, which enhances competitive pressure since it provides the freedom for
participants to enter contractual arrangements. The NETA has proved that an electricity market without a centralized
clearing market can operate effectively and efficiently. However, it also brings some impacts on generators, suppliers,
and environment, especially for renewable energy resources.
Fig.3 shows an example of different types of generators that meet either the reliability or the flexibility
requirements [7]. Reliability is the ability to generate electricity in a predictable manner, which is rewarded in a
contract-based market. Flexibility is the ability to rapidly change the amount of electricity in response to changes in
demand, which is rewarded in a balancing market. Based on the operation experiences under BETTA, flexible
generators and suppliers have earned premium prices in the Balancing Mechanism because they shared most of
accepted bids and offers. In addition, reliable generators can avoid penalty in the balancing market. However, most
of distributed generators, especially for renewable generation, have proven less profitable under BETTA [7] because
they lack the two technical characteristics: reliability and flexibility. For example, some renewable generators cannot
readily predict their outputs for a given period so that they have been subject to the risk under the imbalance
settlement.
Under BETTA, trading can be conducted in the same way by wind power generators as other energy resources.
Therefore, forecasting errors would result in a reduction in the short-term contracted revenue due to the ex-post
imbalance prices. Therefore, a large number of wind generators in the UK have opted not to operate directly in the
power market but have instead chosen to operate through their local suppliers using simpler contracts [8]. However,
these contracts can offer low prices to wind generators while penal imbalance charges can be passed on.
Alternatively, the UK government has responded to the plight of renewable generators by introducing financial
incentives, Renewable Obligation Certificates (ROCs) [9]. The ROCs scheme requires licensed local suppliers to buy
an increasing percentage of their supply from eligible renewable resources. However, this policy mechanism would
not facilitate the competitive trading options.

In fact, many wind generators in California have signed a bi-lateral contract (qualifying facility) with a load
serving entity (LSE) to sell all the energy that they can produce [13]. Under these contact trades, wind generators are
paid a fixed price. These LSEs have to schedule their energy in CAISO’s market system (market redesign and 
technology upgrade program, MRTU) using the PIRP mechanism. Therefore, the wind generators are not subject to
the real time market price but suffer from the risk of wind power forecasting errors.
Recently, the CAISO just proposed a new method of financing transmission lines to serve wind resources. If there
is significant wind resource in an area, a transmission builder can move forward with a project to serve that area and
apply for cost recovery under the Transmission Access Charge (TAC). As a new wind generator connects to the new
lines, the generator will begin to pay for a pro-rated share of the costs based on the capacity it needs. The generator
payments would reduce the amount that will be recovered under the TAC. If adopted, this proposal would remove a
significant roadblock for wind generation investment.
V. RENEWABLE ENERGY TRADING IN OTHER ELECTRICITY MARKETS IN THE WORLD
A power pool EEX is operated for the German market [12]. However, it covers much less of the electricity trade
than Nord Pool. The tradition in Germany for electricity trading is one of bilateral contracts for longer periods. Some
incentive mechanisms, as the feed-in tariff (FIT) currently used in Germany, completely remove market risks for
wind investments, which explains why considerable amounts of wind capacity are being added to some competitive
power system [18]. Under the feed-in tariff scheme, the facility typically has two Kw-hr meters: one is to measure
the energy produced by renewable sources and the other is to measure the energy consumed by the load at this
location. The owner is paid a fixed energy price for the energy produced by renewable resources and then pays a
different price for the energy serving local loads. Currently implemented in EU member states, FIT mechanisms
are the most widespread renewable energy promotion schemes in Europe. Different design choices exist within FIT
schemes, including fixed feed-in versus fixed premium systems, base calculation to fix the level of support,
technologies supported and technology-specific support, and others [19].
In Australia, wind power is currently non-scheduled and accepted straight into the National Electricity Market
(NEM) with wind power generators able to receive the market pool price. By law, purchasers of wholesale electricity
in Australia are required to ensure a percentage of electricity from renewable sources. This is achieved through the
creation of a renewable energy market and the trading of renewable energy certificates (RECs). The RECs are an
innovative approach to support renewable energy technologies and they allow for a certificate market to be separated
from the physical electricity market. Wind farm developers can sell RECs which suppliers are required to purchase
under Australia’s Mandatory Renewable Energy Target (MRET) scheme [20].

二、與會心得
I. INTRODUCTION
lectricity markets are being made more competitive
in attempt to reduce the price and to increase the
efficiency of its delivery. In competitive markets,
electricity can be traded through a number of market
mechanisms with time scopes ranging from several years,
months, to a few hours prior to physical delivery.
Although long- and medium-term markets are expected
to gain importance, electricity spot markets have played
the most relevant role, which is organized as a sequence
of market mechanisms, typically including a day-ahead
market, a congestion management procedure, an
adjustment market and a market for ancillary services.
One of the most important targets for designing these
market mechanisms is to achieve efficient use of
transmission facilities with the lowest cost of generation
dispatch. However, any form of transmission constraints
will prevent perfect competition. This ultimately gives
rise to market power and leads to price spikes above the
marginal costs. Therefore, reasonable pricing scheme
and effective transmission system operation become very
important.
In general, efficiency, simplicity, transparency and
accuracy are important factors to pricing system. A
well-functioning pricing scheme should in theory give a
clear ‘price signal’ that generate optimal investments in 
new power generation and transmission capacity.
Moreover, it should be easy to understand and make
social welfare increase. More importantly, a pricing
scheme should take congestion and transmission costs
into account [1]. The congestion cost is the cost of not
having enough exchange capacity as required to obtain
an unconstrained system price [2]. An overall aim in
congestion management should be to minimize the sum
of added costs in operation due to network constraints
and to allocate congestion costs into the responsible
participants based on an optimal pricing scheme.
Competitive electricity markets over the world has
performed a variety of pricing schemes, such as uniform
marginal price (UMP) [3], zonal marginal price (ZMP)
[4], nodal marginal price (NMP) [5], and
bilateral/multilateral contract price (BCP) [6]. This
research has examined the characteristic of each pricing
system by using an 8-bus system [7]. A comparative
analysis in the four representative pricing schemes has
been studied so as to assess their efficiencies and the
effectiveness of the market pricing signals. In addition,
the risk assessment with these pricing methods has been
evaluated, which takes dynamic loads and system reserve
margins into account.

III. RISK ASSESSMENT IN THE FOUR REPRESENTATIVE
PRICING APPROACHES
In a short term, random uncertainties are deviation of
those parameters which are repeatable and have a known
probability distribution. As a result, their statistics can be
derived from the past observations. In a deregulated
power system, the expected values, the standard
deviation (Std), the Value at Risk (VaR), and the
Conditional VaR (CVaR) [11] of system prices are
important information for the risk analysis of market
trading, planning and operation. Therefore, this research
measures the risk related to the hourly system load
charges based on these risk estimates. The main
contribution for the risk assessment is to provide market
designers and participants with a tool able to capture the
related risk, examine their pricing schemes, and finally
handle market-price uncertainties.
The Std is a simple way to characterize risk. However,
according to its definition, the positive deviations and
negative deviations from the expected value are given
the equal weighting, which would lead to an incorrect
risk assessment, especially for the distribution of
electricity prices. Therefore, there is a need for downside
risk measure to evaluate the characteristics of heavy-tail
distribution. As a result, the VaR and CVaR were
proposed to provide more appropriate risk measurement
techniques in the electric industry [12].
The VaR summarizes the expected maximum loss
over a target horizon within a given confidence interval.
However, it cannot provide a measure of the potential
losses exceeding the VaR value. Instead, the CVaR
provides an estimate of the average loss exceeding the
VaR value. For instance, the 0.95-CVaR is the average
of the expected monetary loss for potential loss values
that exceed the 0.95-VaR threshold [13]. For comparison,
