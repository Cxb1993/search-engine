The brand new polymer/surfactant systems containing poly(vinylformamide)-based polymers 
and various surfactants have been systematically studied. An integration of several important
techniques (NMR, LS, and pyrene fluorescence) has been established to reveal the detailed and 
dynamic pictures of the polymer-surfactant complexes, which never have been done before. The 
results obtained from the investigations of PNVF-based/surfactant systems can be published in 
the top-ranked Journals in the fields of polymer science, physical chemistry, and colloidal 
science and engineering. We expect that the unique solution properties of the new 
polymer/surfactant systems may open new applications. Furthermore, the knowledge of the 
interaction characteristics of the particular polymer/surfactant systems, which are obtained in 
this research, will greatly aid the understanding and facilitate optimization of the properties of 
the polymer/surfactant systems.

Interactions of SDS with Statistical Poly(acrylamide-co-N-vinylformamide) Copolymers: Effects of 
Surfactant-phobic Units on Intermolecular Association between Polymer and Surfactant 
 
Sheng-Shu Hou*, Hou-Ju Huang, Jia-Hsien Lin, and Shiuan-Fu Lin 
Department of Chemical Engineering  
National Cheng Kung University, Tainan, Taiwan 
 
 
The influence of "surfactant-phobic" unit (AAm) on the interaction and the association behavior between 
the anionic surfactant (SDS) and the AAm-containing copolymer is presented. A series of random 
poly(acrylamide-co-N-vinylformamide) (PAAm-ran-PNVF) copolymers with different microstructures have 
been synthesized. PNVF is chosen as the model polymer because the PNVF homopolymer has significant 
interaction with SDS (cac = 3 mM) and also because the PNVF chain penetrates deeply through the 
PNVF-induced SDS aggregate. We used pyrene fluorescence to examine the strength of interaction and the 
association behavior between copolymers and SDS. The interaction is weakened gradually with increasing the 
amount of AAm units in the copolymer chain. As the random copolymer contains 66 mol% of the AAm units, 
no obvious binding of SDS with the copolymer chain is observed. Two-dimensional 1H-1H NOESY NMR 
was applied to elucidate the microstructure of the SDS aggregates which is associated with the random 
copolymers. The random copolymer chain still can penetrate deeply into the polymer-bound SDS aggregates 
even if the copolymer (AN(20/80)) comprises 20 mol% of AAm units. However, the random copolymer chain 
is completely expelled from the SDS aggregate as the amount of AAm units in the copolymer is higher than 
55 mol%. The lack of cross-peak between the AAm unit and SDS reveals that, in the interior of 
AN(20/80)-SDS complex, SDS molecules cannot be close (< 0.5 nm) to the AAm units. The interesting result 
suggests that the AAm unit has a dislike for SDS, probably as a result of strong repulsion between them.

Study of Interactions between Poly(N-Vinylacetamide) and Sodium 
Dodecylsulfate  
Meng-Han Chuang (莊孟翰) and Sheng-Shu Hou* (侯聖澍) 
國立成功大學化學工程學系 
Abstract 
The interaction of poly(N-Vinylacetamide) (PNVA) with anionic surfactant, 
Sodium Dodecylsulfate (SDS), has been studied by pyrene fluorescence and 
two-dimensional nuclear overhauser effect spectroscopy (2D NOESY). With using 
pyrene as a probe, from low to high concentration of SDS the pyrene I1/I3 plot 
exhibits three stages of association of PNVA with SDS. The I1/I3 plot vs. [SDS] 
shows a slope (I1/I3 ~ 1.40) at the SDS concentrations ranged between 6 mM and 12 
mM, indicating that in this range of SDS concentration the size of polymer-bound 
SDS aggregates increase with SDS concentration increase. The ambiguity in 
determining the critical aggregation concentration (cac) of SDS from the I1/I3 plot vs. 
[SDS], which resembles the typical profile of surface tension versus surfactant 
concentration, has been clarified by the 2D NOESY experiment. Also, just beyond the 
cac ([SDS] = 6 mM), the definite proof of formation of the PNVA-SDS complex 
bound on the polymer chain is provided by the 2D NOESY experiments. On the basis 
of the inter- and intramolecular cross relaxation between the protons of PNVA and 
SDS, the microstructure of PNVA-induced SDS aggregates is proposed. Moreover, 
the association behavior of PNVA with SDS is compared to that of the 
poly(N-vinylformamide, PNVF)/SDS poly(vinylpyrrolidone, PVP)/SDS, and 
poly(ethylene oxide, PEO)/SDS systems. When SDS concentration is above cac, the 
viscosity of PNVA solution increase with SDS concentration, but the viscosity of 
PNVF solution decrease and then increase with SDS concentration. 
Keywords: poly(N-vinylacetamide); SDS; polymer-surfactant interaction; NOESY; 
pyrene, viscosity. 
Introduction 
Interactions between water-soluble 
synthetic polymers and surfactants have been 
extensively explored for more than four 
decades. Researches on mixed 
polymer/surfactant systems still receive 
increasing interest in the past few years because, 
on the one hand, many current and promising 
applications grow in pharmaceutical 
formulations, food products, detergency, paints 
and inks and enhanced oil recovery, etc.,1 and 
on the other the nature of association between 
the polymer and surfactant, especially at a 
concentration far below the critical micelle 
concentration (cmc) of the surfactant, remains 
equivocal. 
From the previous result of experiment,2

Evidence of short-range interaction between 
PNVA and SDS by 2D NOESY. The plot of 
pyrene I1/I3 vs. [SDS] shows that, in the

This question can be tackled by the 2D NOESY

The one-dimensional 1H spectra and 
assignments of chemical shifts for the 
PNVA/SDS/D2O mixture are shown in Figure 
2. The isotope effect on the aggregation 
properties of most nonionic polymer/surfactant 
systems will not affect the experimental results 
and can be neglected.26 The chemical shifts of 
PβH and S2 of SDS are overlapped; therefore, 
for the following 2D NOESY spectra the 
cross-relaxation between PNVA and SDS will 
be focused on the proton pairs of PαH-S3~11, 
PαH-S1, ,PCH3-S3~11, and PCH3-S1 
respectively.  
The cross peaks of two specific protons of 
macromolecules shown in the 2D NOESY 
spectrum at a sufficiently short mixing time (tm) 
means that they are close within a distance of 
0.5 nm. However, except for the direct 
dipole-dipole interaction, indirect 
magnetization transfer due to spin diffusion 
also gives rise to cross peaks between adjacent 
protons in the 2D NOESY spectrum. Spin 
diffusion occurs mainly for larger molecules 
and for long mixing times outside the initial 
rate regime, i.e. “linear approximation”.27 In

can reach the alkyl protons first.

曾鎔塏[6]等人以 Pyrene 螢光探針與 2D

互作用力存在。 
本實驗合成不同比例的 PNVF-co-PAA
無規共聚物，並以其單體活性比計算推測此
高分子共聚物的組成結構情形；以 Pyrene螢
光探針探討在不同組成比例 PNVF-co-PAA
下對不同濃度的 CTAB水溶液中的相互作用
力；以分光光度計觀察水溶液的相分離情
形；並使用 2D NOSEY NMR 實驗探討
PNVF-co-PAA中 NVF、AA單元與 CTAB疏
水 端 之 間 的 距 離 ， 進 一 步 了 解
PNVF-co-PAA/CTAB 系統為何種聚集型態
存在；而電位測定法是利用界面活性劑選擇
電極（Surfactant selective electrode，SSE）可
分別偵測系統中 CTAB 開始吸附於
PNVF-co-PAA 高分子鏈上以及開始形成

Interactions of SDS with Statistical Poly(acrylamide-co-N-vinylformamide) Copolymers: Effects of 
Surfactant-phobic Units on Intermolecular Association between Polymer and Surfactant 
 
Sheng-Shu Hou*, Hou-Ju Huang, Jia-Hsien Lin, and Shiuan-Fu Lin 
Department of Chemical Engineering  
National Cheng Kung University, Tainan, Taiwan 
 
 
The influence of "surfactant-phobic" unit (AAm) on the interaction and the association behavior between 
the anionic surfactant (SDS) and the AAm-containing copolymer is presented. A series of random 
poly(acrylamide-co-N-vinylformamide) (PAAm-ran-PNVF) copolymers with different microstructures have 
been synthesized. PNVF is chosen as the model polymer because the PNVF homopolymer has significant 
interaction with SDS (cac = 3 mM) and also because the PNVF chain penetrates deeply through the 
PNVF-induced SDS aggregate. We used pyrene fluorescence to examine the strength of interaction and the 
association behavior between copolymers and SDS. The interaction is weakened gradually with increasing the 
amount of AAm units in the copolymer chain. As the random copolymer contains 66 mol% of the AAm units, 
no obvious binding of SDS with the copolymer chain is observed. Two-dimensional 1H-1H NOESY NMR 
was applied to elucidate the microstructure of the SDS aggregates which is associated with the random 
copolymers. The random copolymer chain still can penetrate deeply into the polymer-bound SDS aggregates 
even if the copolymer (AN(20/80)) comprises 20 mol% of AAm units. However, the random copolymer chain 
is completely expelled from the SDS aggregate as the amount of AAm units in the copolymer is higher than 
55 mol%. The lack of cross-peak between the AAm unit and SDS reveals that, in the interior of 
AN(20/80)-SDS complex, SDS molecules cannot be close (< 0.5 nm) to the AAm units. The interesting result 
suggests that the AAm unit has a dislike for SDS, probably as a result of strong repulsion between them.
