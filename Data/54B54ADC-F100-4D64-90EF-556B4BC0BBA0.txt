摘要 
微擠製成形是製造微型金屬元件的重要技術。其複雜之材料流動行為受晶粒尺寸、溫度、摩擦與
潤滑效應等多項因素影響。然而，大部分之研究仍以冷成形或只針對單一因素之影響進行研究，對於
高溫成形與各因素間之交互作用對微擠製之材料流動行為仍缺乏深入之研究。 
本研究建構一個可控制溫度的微擠製實驗平台，針對微逆向擠製、微雙杯擠製與微前後向擠製成
形，經模擬與實驗方法，探討純銅(JIS C1100)與黃銅(JIS C2600)之製程溫度、晶粒尺寸、胚料表面粗
糙度與潤滑條件對微擠製成形之影響。 
本研究使用退火與等徑轉角擠製技術備製平均晶粒尺寸為 4、36 與 62 m 之三種純銅胚料，經切
削為直徑與高度分別為 2 與 1.5 mm 之試片後，於成形溫度 25、200 與 400 ºC 下，利用三種不同直徑
沖頭，以逆向擠製製造壁厚分別為 0.05、0.1 與 0.2 mm 之微型圓杯。微前後向擠製實驗是利用直徑與
高度分別為 2 與 2.5 mm 之胚料，置於模穴直徑 2 mm 與前擠直徑為 1 mm 之模具內，以兩種不同直
徑沖頭製作壁厚分別為 0.1 與 0.2 mm 之成品，完成具有逆擠圓杯與前擠圓棒之成品。以上兩組實驗結
果顯示，晶粒細化有助於改善材料流動，使得擠製後成品杯高差與壁厚差較小，但成形負荷較高。另
外，提高製程溫度可降低成形負荷並改善成形性，但材料會有再結晶的現象，尤其晶粒細化材料有明
顯晶粒再成長現象發生。 
為了探討微擠製成形時的摩擦效應，本研究利用三種晶粒尺寸，於三種成形溫度下進行微雙杯擠
製實驗。胚料之直徑與高度分別為 2 與 2.5 mm，沖頭直徑為 1.0 mm，在無潤滑與有潤滑條件下進行
實驗，觀察杯高變化。同時，利用有限元素模擬微雙杯擠製成形，建立校正曲線，以做為評估摩擦因
子之參考依據。結果顯示，晶粒尺寸在壁厚較厚的情形下對於杯高比的影響並不明顯。提高製程溫度
使得界面摩擦因子也會隨之升高。 
本研究也針對直徑與高度皆為 1.1 mm 之黃銅胚料，分別進行雙杯擠製與前後向擠製實驗，探討
不同製程參數對於摩擦效應的影響。利用三種不同形貌直徑 0.55 mm 之沖頭進行雙杯擠製實驗，預估
母模與工件間之定剪摩擦因子值。實驗結果顯示，沖頭前緣形狀對成品杯高差沒有明顯影響，母模與
工件之接觸摩擦是影響摩擦校正曲線主要因素。依據前述預估結果，針對沖頭為 0.8 mm 與前擠直徑
為 0.55 mm 之前後向擠製進行模擬，建構沖頭摩擦校正曲線。前後向擠製實驗之沖頭係經不同的加工
程序後獲得四種表面形貌，進行微擠製成形實驗，經量測成品之前擠桿件與後擠杯高尺寸，並與模擬
校正曲線比對以評估摩擦效應，探討沖頭形貌對摩擦效應之影響。結果顯示，摩擦效應由小到大依序
為拋光、研磨、溝槽加工與放電加工之形貌。綜合前述兩種擠製實驗結果顯示，使用雙杯擠製與前後
向擠製預估之摩擦因子值可得到精確的負荷預估。雙杯擠製適合用於預估母模與工件間之摩擦值，而
前後向擠製則適合用於預估沖頭與工件間之摩擦效應。

1999 年 Tiesler 等人[19]利用雙杯擠製討論成形中的摩擦現象。直徑 4、2 與 1mm，高度與直徑
比為 1 之銅(E-Cu-57)試片，試片表面以電化學拋光至表面粗度 Ra 0.18μm，晶粒尺寸為 62μm，於
室溫下使用潤滑劑，根據試片大小分別以 10、5、與 2.5mm/min 速度進行微雙杯擠製。比對實驗結果
與 DEFORM 2D 進行模擬製作之摩擦校正曲線，發現摩擦因子值隨著試片直徑縮小而增加，經量測表
面粗糙度後，顯示造成此現象的原因在於試件尺寸變化會使得潤滑區分布情形不同，隨試件尺寸縮小，
封閉潤滑區(closed lubricant pockets)由大變小，而開放潤滑區(open lubricant pockets)增加，由於開放潤
滑區無法將成形負荷透過儲存的潤滑劑均勻地傳至試片上，導致成形負荷增加，摩擦力也因而明顯上
升。 
2004 年 Fereshteh-Saniee 等人[20]利用物理仿真的方式分別以純鉛與黏土進行圓環壓縮、雙杯擠
製與前後向擠製，目的在觀察不同潤滑條件對界面摩擦的影響。研究結果發現:(a)利用物理仿真方法可
以降低實驗負荷、簡化實驗步驟並降低實驗成本；(b)圓環壓縮試驗之模具架構最簡單、最容易進行實
驗，在某些潤滑條件下實驗結果與其他兩種擠製法之結果類似；(c)利用鉛與黏土在不同潤滑條件下進
行實驗，能表現出不同成形製程的摩擦因子變化。 
2005 年毛慶中[21]利用車削、研磨與放電加工技術製作不同表面形貌之模具，進行簡單壓縮與環
形壓縮試驗，實驗結果顯示，車削後模具有方向性特徵紋路能有效的儲存潤滑劑，對於改善摩擦現象
有良好的效果。車削製作之模具可以達到近似完全潤滑，所以對於胚料成形後表面粗糙度影響很小，
而研磨與放電加工之模具因表面為隨機與開放之特徵，無法與形成有效潤滑，導致實驗後胚料表面粗
糙值略微上升。 
2006 年 Engel [22]探討微成形中的磨潤現象，以直徑 4 mm 至 0.5 mm、高度直徑比為 1 之 CuZn15 
棒材進行雙杯擠製實驗。試片表面粗度在 Ra 0.025 至 0.05 μm 間，於室溫下添加潤滑劑進行微雙杯
擠製。實驗完成後再與 DEFORM 2D 之模擬結果比對。比對實驗與模擬結果發現，直徑 4 mm 試件
所測得之摩擦因子值為 0.02，隨著試片直徑縮小至 0.5 mm，測得的摩擦因子值提升為 0.4。該研究提
出，造成此現象的原因，在於不同試件尺寸變化對不同潤滑區分布情形的影響。因為，隨試件尺寸縮
小，潤滑區分布比例，由多變少，而開放潤滑區比例則增加。因此，開放潤滑區無法將成形負荷透過
潤滑劑均勻地傳至試片上，以致於成形負荷只作用於試件部分位置，造成表面產生局部塑性變形、摩
擦力因而明顯上升。 
2007 年 Jeon 和 Bramley[23]提出了利用模具表面粗糙度做為摩擦模型的方法進行微圓環壓縮試
驗模擬，並與一般僅設定定剪摩擦因子之模擬進行比對，比較兩者之摩擦校正曲線以及成形負荷之差
異。結果發現這種新的模擬模型可以表現出和傳統方法差不多的結果。 
2007 年 Altan 等人[24]利用有限元素軟體 DEFORM 探討不同模擬參數對於雙杯擠製校正曲線的
影響。研究結果發現增加胚料的長度、擠製比或是胚料非均質的應變分布皆會使得杯高比值增加，且
杯高比值隨著材料應變硬化指數的減少而增加。沖頭與胚料間的接觸摩擦並不影響杯高比值以及校正
曲線的建立。 
2008 年黃建國等人[25]利用不同表面粗糙度之純鋁材料，在有潤滑以及無潤滑的條件下，進行直

三、摩擦與潤滑效應 
3.1 微雙杯擠製 
微雙杯擠製係將試片置於母模內孔及兩支對向沖頭之間，並以上沖頭向下擠壓試片，下沖頭為靜
止，使材料在模具內產生塑性流動，最後製作具有雙圓杯之成品，如圖 3-1 所示。由於下部材料是靜
止，因此材料承受之摩擦力比較趨近於靜摩擦，而上半部之杯部由於沖頭與材料之間有相對速度，其
摩擦現象比較趨近於動摩擦，因此上杯深度會大於下杯。利用上與下杯高度比值(HU/HL)和進給行程與
原試片高比值的關係，將可瞭解摩擦效應對微擠製成形之影響。 
本研究針對 C1100 純銅線材，經 800、580 ºC 退火製程與 ECAE 技術製作平均晶粒尺寸為 62、
36 與 4 m、直徑與高度為 2 與 2.5 mm 之純銅胚料(如圖 3-1 所示)，胚料表面以 1000 號砂紙進行研磨
其平均表面粗糙度為 Ra 0.144 m，進行無潤滑與有潤滑條件下的微雙杯擠製成形實驗，觀察製程溫
度為 25、200 與 400ºC 下的杯高變化。本研究也利用有限元素模擬微雙杯擠製成形，測量上下杯高比
與行程之關係，建立校正曲線，作為評估摩擦因子之參考依據。

5.4 黃銅微擠製 
5.4.1 黃銅微雙杯擠製 
為了準確地預估母模摩擦值，本研究利用 DEFORM 2D 進行了考慮沖頭摩擦的雙杯擠製模擬，模
擬結果如圖 5-20 所示 ，由模擬圖可得知影響杯高比值的主要原因為母模的摩擦值變化，而沖頭的摩
擦因子變化對於材料上下杯高比值造成了些許的影響，在沖頭摩擦小時材料較容易於沖頭與胚料界面
間流動，使得材料較容易往上杯方向流動，造成上下杯高比值增加。由於實驗所用沖頭為球狀沖頭，
比起一般平坦型沖頭造成較大的表面擴張，可能是造成此結果的原因，若改變形狀為帶有錐角之平坦
沖頭可以使得沖頭摩擦造成的差異略為改善(如圖 5-21 )，但整體而言沖頭摩擦值造成的影響遠比母模
之影響小，所以對於雙杯擠製而言，沖頭之前緣形狀與摩擦值大小對於摩擦校正曲線的建立影響不
大。 
 
         
為了驗證沖頭摩擦值對於杯高比值之影響，本研究除了利用研磨沖頭進行實驗，也分別利用拋光、
放電沖頭進行微雙杯擠製，將實驗後胚料分別進行量測後與校正曲線進行比對(如圖 5-22 所示)，根據
此圖可看出，若沖頭表面較光滑，在擠製初期材料較容易朝上杯方向流動，導致杯高比增加；反之較
粗糙的表面使得材料較容易往下杯方向流動，使得杯高比降低，但隨著擠製行程增加，各沖頭、潤滑
條件之杯高比值皆趨近於一定值，另外可發現拋光及研磨沖頭之實驗結果相近，僅有放電沖頭之差異
較大，顯示進行微雙杯擠製實驗時，若上下沖頭表面過於粗糙將造成實驗差異。根據圖 5-22 可發現
雖然不同形貌沖頭會造成實驗差異，但根據模擬與實驗的結果，可發現造成杯高比變化的主要原因為

Micro metal pins have been widely used in electronics devices. The 
pins are mainly fabricated by precision machining processes. 
However, the production rate of the machining process is low and 
cannot fulfill the requirements for fast manufacturing and low cost.
The developed technology of the manufacturing of micro metal pins is 
based on a micro extrusion to produce micro cylindrical pins. The 
technology with characteristics of high product rate and low cost is 
able to produce 150 to 200 pieces per minute. The both ends of the

diameter of the pin can be less than 1 mm and its length is in a range 
from 1.5 to 5 mm or longer. The dimension accuracy is in a range less 
than 0.01 mm. The materials can be carbon steels, copper alloys, other

Effects of Temperature and Grain Size on Combined Micro Forward and Backward Ex-
trusion of Copper  
Chao-Cheng Chang, Jian-Chen Lin, Cheng-Ping Siao 
Department of Mold and Die Engineering, National Kaohsiung University of Applied Sciences, Kaohsiung / Taiwan, ccchang@kuas.edu.tw  
Abstract. This study develops a micro extrusion system with temperature control in order to study the effects of temperature and grain 
size on the combined forward and backward extrusion of copper at the micro scale. The study employed equal channel angular extrusion 
and annealing techniques to refine the grains of copper, after which the treated copper was machined to prepare the billets with 2 mm 
diameter and 2.5 mm height for conducting micro extrusion experiments. The 1.6 and 1.8 mm diameter punches were used to compress 
the billets into the die with 2 mm diameter in the portion of backward extrusion and 1 mm diameter in the portion of forward extrusion at 25, 
200 and 400 ºC, respectively. The billets were thus forced to deform backwards and forwards, and the micro parts with a backwards-
extruded cup and a forwards-extruded pin were fabricated. The results show that the grain refinement reduces the differences in both the 
rim height and the wall thickness of the cup, and an appropriate forming temperature is important for reducing the dimension variation of 
the extruded part.   
Keywords: Micro metal forming, Extrusion, Temperature, Grain size 
 
1. INTRODUCTION 
The micro extrusion process plays an important role in 
manufacturing micro metal parts, such as connector pins 
and micro cups, which are widely used in portable devices 
in the fields of consumer electronics including mobile 
phones, tablet computers, and digital cameras. However, 
the existing knowledge of conventional extrusion cannot 
be simply transferred to the micro scale because so-called 
size effects appear and can cause inhomogeneous material 
characteristics [1, 2]. In the micro extrusion process, it is 
possible that only a small number of grains are directly 
involved. Therefore, a single grain characterized by its size, 
orientation, and position significantly affects the extrusion 
process and results in inhomogeneous material behaviour. 
This situation can lead to inaccuracy of shape and scatter 
of product properties.  
Using hydraulic bulge testing at micro and macro scales,  
Mahabunphachai and Koc [3] showed that the flow curve 
behaviour was clearly affected by the ratio of the sheet 
thickness to the material grain size and the ratio of the 
bulge die diameter to the sheet thickness. Moreover, ac-
cording to the micro hardness evaluations together with the 
X-ray texture and microstructure analyses, Parasiz et al. [4] 
reported that the micro brass parts extruded from the bil-
lets with coarse-grained structure may result in higher 
hardness than the ones with the fine-grained structure as 
the grain size becomes comparable to the specimen feature 
size. The phenomenon was inconsistent with the Hall-
Petch relationship which typically appears in macro scale 
processes, and was caused by the penetration of shear 
deformation occurring for the coarse-grained structure. For 
reducing the drawback of inhomogeneous material charac-
ter, grain refinement increasing the number of grains in-
volved in the process is one of the methods [5, 6]. Fur-
thermore, an increase of forming temperature may be per-
formed in the micro forming to minimize the effect of 
inhomogeneous material flow behaviours because the 
thermally activated recovery process and additional slip 
system can improve formability [7]. However, the interac-
tion between temperature and grain size in the micro extru-
sion of copper has not been fully established. Further in-
vestigation on theirs effects on the process could help to 
develop technologies for producing micro metallic parts. 
This study develops a micro extrusion system with tem-
perature control in order to study the effects of temperature 
and grain size on the combined forward and backward 
extrusion of copper at the micro scale. The equal channel 
angular extrusion (ECAE) and annealing techniques were 
employed to produce three kinds of coppers with different 
grain sizes for the use in the micro extrusion processes 
conducting at three different temperatures. In this manner, 
it was possible to investigate the effects of temperature and 
grain size on material flow, forming load, and dimension 
variation. 
2. GRAIN REFINEMENT 
This study used ECAE and annealing techniques to ad-
just grain size in copper for the micro extrusion experi-
ments. Commercially available copper (JIS C1100) was 
first machined to prepare specimens with a diameter of 4 
mm and length of 16 mm. The specimens were then an-
nealed at 580 ºC for 1 hour and later processed via ECAE 
in which two channels equal in cross-section had been 
arranged to form an L-shaped channel in the die as illus-
trated in figure 1. The channel angle,I, and the angle of 
curvature of the outside corner,T, were 90º and 40º, re-
spectively. As the punch was pressed forwards, the billet 
passed through the channel and shearing process occurred 
in the crossing region of the channels. To refine the grains 
and improve strain homogeneity, the billets lubricated with 
grease containing molybdenum disulfide (MoS2) were 
processed with six passes through ECAE, and rotated 90º 
longitudinally between successive passes at room tempera-
ture. The extruded specimens were later annealed at 160 
ºC for 1 hour to obtain a stable microstructure. These bil-
lets are referred to as ECAE processed in this study. A 
number of billets prepared with commercially available 
copper had also been annealed at 580 and 800 ºC, respec-
tively, for 2 hours to produce a microstructure with coarse 
grains. To quantify the changes in the microstructure of the 
specimens, the grain size and hardness were measured in

 Effect of Grain Size on Micro Ball Punch Deformation of SUS 304 
Stainless Steel  
Chao-Cheng Changa and Li-Yi Linb 
Department of Mold and Die Engineering, National Kaohsiung University of Applied Sciences,  
No. 415, Chien-Kung Road, Kaohsiung, Taiwan 
accchang@kuas.edu.tw, ba163082@gmail.com 
Keywords: Micro metal forming, Ball punch deformation, Grain size. 
Abstract. Micro metal forming is widely interested due to its potential in manufacturing micro parts 
with low costs and high production rate as the demands of compact devices have been increasing in 
many fields. This study uses micro ball punch deformation tests to investigate the effect of grain size 
on the formability of SUS 304 stainless steel sheets. The study employed annealing treatments to 
change the grain size of the sheets. By using three punches in different diameters in associated with 
three dies having an inner diameter of 2 mm and different die radii, it was able to perform micro 
bulging tests and obtain the bulging depths which were used to assess the formability of the sheets. 
The study shows that the grain size does affect the depth of the deformed dome in the ball punch 
deformation test at the micro scale.  
Introduction 
The applications of micro features on mechanical components have been increasing due to the trend 
of compact devices in the fields of consumer products, electronic equipments, medical and other 
devices. These compact devices contain not only electronic components but also mechanical parts 
such as connector pins, miniature screws, contact springs, chip lead frames and micro cups. These 
micro parts may be produced by Micro electro-mechanical system (MEMS) technology. However, 
the difficulty in fabricating a complex shape product with low costs at a high production rate is an 
obstacle to apply the MEMS technology for manufacturing the micro devices [1-3]. Alternatively, 
metal forming is one of potential technologies especially suitable for the mass production of metallic 
parts because of the advantages of high production rate, minimized material loss, excellent 
mechanical properties of the final product and close tolerances. These characteristics also make it 
suitable for manufacturing micro parts, especially when high-volume low-cost production is desired. 
However, so-called size effects appear in scaling down a conventional metal forming process to the 
micro scale level and influence not only material flow behavior but also formability, dimension 
accuracy, tooling and assembly [4-7]. The factors used to investigate the size effects include the size 
of grain and the size of feature. The effect of grain size on the strength of metal tends to follow the 
Hall-Petch equation [8], which describes that the strength increases with the reduction of grain size. 
As the size of the grain is relatively close to the size of feature, or the size of the features has been 
reduced to the micro-scale, the effect of feature size can have a significantly impact on material 
behavior, and is thus susceptible to deformation. A study of tensile and bending tests on aluminum 
and brass [9] showed how the yield strength and tensile strength decreased with a decrease in ratio, in 
cases where the ratio was greater than 1. This trend reversed when the ratio was less than 1. The study 
also concluded that at micro scales, formability decreased as the ratio decreased, and suggested that 
size effect had to be taken into account for forming processes with a ratio less than or approaching 10. 
Moreover, a study using hydraulic bulge tests [10] to investigate the effects of grain and feature sizes  
on the material flow curve of thin stainless steel sheets with different grain sizes showed that the 
critical ratio of bulge diameter to the thickness of sheet fell between 100 and 200. As the critical ratio 
dropped below 200, flow stress increased significantly. The influence of size effects on the material 
flow curve was also observed in various test conditions using a variety of materials including 
aluminum, copper and brass [11-15].

Effect of Grain Size on Micro Ball Punch Deformation of SUS 304 Stainless Steel

An increase in forming temperature could reduce forming load 
and improve material flow and die filling. However, the micro 
backward extrusion of stainless steel at elevated temperatures has 
not been fully understood. Further investigation on the effect of 
temperature on micro backward extrusion could help to develop 
technologies for producing micro metallic parts. This study used a 
metal forming system comprising an electrical heater in order to 
conduct the micro backward extrusion of SUS 304 stainless steel 
at 400 ºC and was able to investigate the material flow, forming 
load and dimension variation for the extrusion of stainless steel at 
the elevated temperature. 
Experimental Setup 
The rod of SUS 304 stainless steel was carefully machined to prepare billets with a diameter of 2 mm 
and height of 3 mm with the highest possible dimensional accuracy. Fig. 1 shows the dimensions of 
the billet and extruded cup. This study used two punches with diameters of 1.6 mm and 1.8 mm to 
extrude the billets inside the die with an inner diameter of 2 mm for producing 0.2 mm and 0.1 mm 
wall-thickness cups, respectively. To overcome the difficulty of rapid heat dissipation during transfer 
from the furnace to the die for experiments at elevated temperatures due to a high surface to volume 
ratio in micro-scale parts, the billets were directly placed inside the die for heating in a forming 
system comprising an electrical heater, as illustrated in Fig. 2. The temperature of the die was 
measured by a thermocouple and fixed at a particular value by the controller. In this study, the billets 
were applied by water-based graphite and placed inside the die after the temperature of the die and 
punch had reached the desired level. By means of heat conduction at the contact interface between the 
billet and die, approximately 5 min was required to raise the billet temperature from room temperature 
to 400 ºC. Therefore, the specimen, die and punch had the same temperatures before extrusion started, 
i.e. the process was under an isothermal forming condition. As the desired temperature was obtained, 
the punch was compressed by a press with a load capacity of 50 kN and a position accuracy of 0.01 
mm. The speed of the punch was kept constant at 0.01 mm per second for all experiments. Finally, the 
dimension and microstructure of the extruded cups were examined through an optical microscope 
equipped with an image-capturing device and dimension-measuring software.

Summary 
This study developed a metal forming 
system comprising an electrical heater to 
investigate deformation, material flow 
behaviour and load in the micro backward 
extrusion processes of SUS 304 stainless 
steel at 400 ºC. Two punches with 
diameters of 1.6 mm and 1.8 mm were used 
to extrude the billets inside the die with an 
inner diameter of 2 mm. The results show 
that the stainless steel components with a 
micro cup-shaped profile have been 
successfully fabricated. The variation in 
the rim height of the cups is greater in the 
case with a smaller clearance between the 
punch and die than the other with a larger 
clearance. This implies that the material 
flow was more inhomogeneous in the case 
with a smaller clearance. The results could 
form the base of developing of a micro 
backward extrusion process used in the 
production of micro metallic parts with a 
cup-shaped profile.

技術說明
(中文) 微型金屬探針廣泛地用於電子產品，目前以精密車削技術製造，其生產速率低，
無法滿足此類產品之快速量產與低成本的需求。 
微型金屬探針之製造是利用微型擠製與多道次成形技術，製造微型圓柱狀探針的
一種技術。本技術利用微金屬成形具有高生產效率之特性，可於每分鐘生產150
至200個元件。微型探針兩端可為不同外形，包括球形或圓柱。探針直徑可小於1 
mm，長度可以介於1.5至5 mm或更長，尺寸精度可達0.01 mm。元件材質可為碳鋼、
銅合金、其他金屬或合金。
(英文) Micro metal pins have been widely used in electronics devices. The pins are mainly 
fabricated by precision machining processes. However, the production rate of the 
machining process is low and cannot fulfill the requirements for fast manufacturing and 
low cost. 
The developed technology of the manufacturing of micro metal pins is based on a micro 
extrusion to produce micro cylindrical pins. The technology with characteristics of high 
product rate and low cost is able to produce 150 to 200 pieces per minute. The both ends 
of the micro pin can have different shape, including sphere and cylinder. The diameter of 
the pin can be less than 1 mm and its length is in a range from 1.5 to 5 mm or longer. The 
dimension accuracy is in a range less than 0.01 mm. The materials can be carbon steels, 
copper alloys, other metals or alloys.
