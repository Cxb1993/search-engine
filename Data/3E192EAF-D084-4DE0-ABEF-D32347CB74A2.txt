Weibull distributions, respectively. In 
practical applications, Weibull and 
lognormal distributions may fit the lifetime 
data well. However, their predictions may 
lead to a significant difference. It is a pity 
that the impacts of mis-specification 
between these two distributions on the 
optimal test plans are not studied in the two 
articles. The main purpose of this project is 
to address this issue. Under the same model 
assumptions used in Yu & Tseng (2004), we 
will investigate the impacts of 
mis-specification by computing the optimal 
test plans and the corresponding mean 
squared errors for both mis-specifications: 
(1) if the degradation rate follows a 
lognormal distribution, but get 
mis-identified as a reciprocal Weibull 
distribution and (2) if the degradation rate 
follows a reciprocal Weibull distribution, 
but get mis-identified a lognormal 
distribution. 
 
Keywords: Mis-specification; Reciprocal
