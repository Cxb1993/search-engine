「需求轉變」及「發展歷程」等，進

(一) 供給面向 
1970 年之前的建築投資均著重於
追求快速及大量生產，相對而言對於
「建築品質」與「建物性能」方面則
較不重視，且由於當時建築物之相關
設備較為單純，對於建築物整體之防
災意識亦不普遍，同時未建立設備維
護與建築管理等相關概念；因此，如
何透過機制之建立與策略的管理作為
改善當前之窘境，即為本研究之重要
關鍵處；除上述經常年所衍生出之建

2. 研究目的 
以建築使用觀點而言，以往經由
契約約定所提供之一般傳統的大樓管
理方式，僅著重於建築物清潔、環境
美化、機電維護、保全管理型態的例
行性建物管理，此種較為固定性的管
理方式，不僅於其管理績效與服務品
質上令人產生疑慮，另檢視現行之服
務性質而言，亦僅止於協助住戶間生
活事項的處理，並已無法滿足 21 世紀
建築物業主或使用者的需求與變化；
因此，如何跳脫傳統「物」的思維角
度，著重於人與建築物之間的互動，
並以使用者觀點作為規劃與設計之理
念[1]，將建築物視為與人組合而成的
有機體，並針對此有機體本身及其所
產生的行為，研擬出現今新型態的建
築維護管理模式，即為本研究首要之
階段任務。 
另透過施行物業管理而促使建築
物之資產價值有效提昇而言，如何以
物業管理作為經營管理建築物之手
法，並於建築物生命週期之各階段，
除探討出修繕重點項目、更新應用時
程、資產評價方案（REITS）及投資效
益評估等執行要件，並以研究案例作
為實際驗證，以獲得本研究之實用價
值。

6. DISCUSSION 
This study provides essential data analysis and key 
items of maintenance costs by examining 9,410 historical 
maintenance records of the 2400-bed medical center 
NTUH in Taiwan.  An 86 year–old West Site building 
and a 17 year-old East Site building were compared.  
The results of this study can provide significant 
references for the hospital facility managers around the 
world.  Three major findings are discussed as follows. 
In Shoet’s study in 2003, 17 hospitals in Israel with an 
average age of 38 years and 658 sickbeds were analyzed.  
The mean annual maintenance budget is $37.20 per 
square meter and about half of this was spent on 
permanent maintenance staff.  Thus, the mean annual 
maintenance cost will be less than $18.60 per square 
meter.  For the case study of NTUH, which has a much 
larger scale (2400 beds vs 658), the average annual 
maintenance costs were $29.50 per square meter for the 
older West Site building and $15.40 for the younger adult 
East Site building.  For different building life cycle 
stages, it is reasonable that the older the buildings, the 
higher the maintenance cost.  The findings of the NTUH 
study provide valuable information about the 
maintenance costs during various life cycle stages.  In 
Shoet’s later study in 2004, a 23 year-old hospital 
building with 1065 beds was studied and the annual 
maintenance expenditure was $38.50 per square meter 
(half was for maintenance staff).  Combining the results 
of NTUH with Shoet’s studies, the relationship between 
the age of hospital buildings and the annual maintenance 
costs is be plotted as in figure 8.  Although this 
relationship may require further detailed analysis, this 
finding provides precious information for hospital facility 
managers in making decisions regarding maintenance 
budgets.     
Regarding to the key sub-items and associate 
maintenance cost percentage, in Uhlik and Hinze’s study, 
the questionnaire showed that the majority of the 
maintenance work consisted of HVAC (including 
plumbing, 53%) and roofing projects (20%).  Neely and 
Neathmmer (1991) identified interior finishes (32%) and 
HVAC (29%) as the major maintenance sources.  This 
research on the NTUH demonstrated different key items 
of maintenance work for the older West Site building and 
East Site building accordingly.  As discussed in section 
5, the key sub-items for periodic maintenance, repair, and 
demand change are identified respectively.  For various 
life cycle stages, the key sub-items may be different.  
However, it’s interesting to note that HVAC and low 
voltage systems remain the key sub-items for all the 
periodic maintenance, repair, and demand change 
categories.  Regardless the various life cycle stages of 
the West Site and East Site buildings, these two sub-items 
continue to be essential items for hospital maintenance 
work.  This finding coincides with the results of Uhlik’s 
studies.  However, the percentage of HVAC in NTUH’s 
historical maintenance data is relatively low compared to 
Uhlik’s questionnaire results.  Another difference is that

significant in Uhlik’s study.  Lack of the information of
