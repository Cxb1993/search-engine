Abstract 
  The friction stir welding (FSW) process 
has been developed by the Welding Institute 
(TWI) since 1991. Extensive applications of 
FSW on different materials and on 
aerospace on automobile industries have 
been explored. One of the most potential 
applications of FSW should be the 
manufacturing of car body structures 
including frames and doors using aluminum 
alloys to achieve lightweight purpose. 
  The feature of FSW is to join materials 
by a friction stir motion between a high 
speed rotation stir probe and the base metal. 
The base metal behaves similar to a viscous 
material so that the “weld metal“ can be 
stirred together to form a sound weld. Since 
there is no melting and solidification during 
welding, the limited heat input results in a 
narrow heat affected zone (HAZ). The 
integrity of aluminum frame structure can be 
improved by applying FSW in 
manufacturing. In this study, our main 
objective is focused on the metal matrix 
composite (MMC) material such as Al2O3 
and SiC. The main effort was to mix the 
Al2O3 powder into the weld track to enhance 
physical properties of the weld track and 
also to study the flow direction of the stirred 
material in the weld track. 
KeywordsLight-weight vehicle, friction 
stir welding, particulate 
reinforcement.
