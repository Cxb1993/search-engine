前言 
肌肉系統是同時滿足頸椎活動及維持穩定功能的主要角色。肌肉作用能夠有
效地增加脊椎穩定性。然而亦可能同時造成脊椎負載快速上升，而導致椎體周邊
組織的傷害 (Tchako and Sadegh, 2009)。一般認為脊椎負載與穩定性的平衡失常，
可能與非創傷性脊椎退化的進程有關。脊椎退化包括了脊椎椎體本身的改變 (如
椎間盤退化、小面關節炎、韌帶退化)以及脊椎肌肉功能下降 (Fujiwara et al., 
2000)。如果初期的椎體退化、或是肌力下降沒有給予適當的治療，持續過度的
脊椎活動會造成周邊韌帶中的機械感受器 (mechanoreceptor)和小面關節裡的關
節感受器 (articular receptor)一直累積微小的傷害。這些受損的感受器會傳給中樞
神經系統錯誤的資訊，而造成中樞控制模式的改變 (Armstrong et al., 2008)。異
常的中樞控制模式則會引起肌肉本身的性質發生變化 (Falla and Farina, 2008)、
以及破壞小面關節傳導應力的能力，脊椎的受力因而大部分轉嫁到椎間盤和周邊
韌帶上 (Kong et al., 1996)。這樣的惡性循環使得脊椎負載大量增加、脊椎穩定
度持續惡化、加速脊椎退化過程，最後導致各種慢性症狀的產生。 
直接量測人體的脊椎負載相當困難，但是可以藉由量測椎間盤內的壓力來
推論負載的大小。Nechemson (1964)首先使用針式壓力感測器，配合醫學影像引
導，在 19 位病患身上量測椎間盤的尺寸、及其所承受的壓力。結果顯示臥躺時
椎間盤負載最低 (約 100~200牛頓)、站立時較高 (約 900~1200牛頓)，而懶散的
坐姿時負載最高 (約 1000~1750 牛頓)。在行走時因為軀幹的加速度所產生的慣
性力，亦會導致脊椎負載增加為約 2.5倍的體重 (Cappozzo, 1984)。後續的研究
提供了更多人體腰椎在各種日常生活動作下的負載，其量值可由正躺時約 250
牛頓，至身體前彎合併旋轉時的2100牛頓 (Nachemson, 1981, Wilke et al., 2001)。
而在抬舉不同重物時，腰椎負載甚至可達 5000~8000 牛頓 (van Dieen et al., 
1999)。 
頸椎負載的研究相對於胸腰椎而言較少。Snijder 學者 (1991)以生物力學模
型配合最佳化方式，計算出頸椎在正中姿勢下，第五-六節椎間盤的負載約為 85
年頓。Panjabi 等學者 (1998)以體外實驗，量得頸椎產生挫曲前的臨界負載 
(critical load)平均為 10.5牛頓。Pospiech學者 (1999)則以體外實驗模擬三對肌肉
的作用，得到在頸椎第五-六節的壓力約為 0.43 MPa。至目前為止，惟一在人體

計畫成果自評部份 
1. 研究內容與原計畫相符程度。 
本研究原提出三年期的長程計畫，後核定為一年期的計畫。第一年計畫主要
為發展可供估算表層及深層頸部肌力和椎間盤負載的模型，工作重點在於模
型的建構與程式撰寫。原計畫研究目的均已達成，與計畫內容相符。 
 
2. 達成預期目標情況。 
本計劃的第一年預期目標為: 
1) 比較不同頸部角度下頸椎負載的變化。 
2) 比較表層肌肉及深層肌肉作用對於脊椎負載影響的比例。 
目標皆已達成，研究發現詳述於內容中。 
 
3. 研究成果之學術或應用價值。 
本計劃之特殊性在於提出新的肌電圖整合最佳化的肌肉模型，同時計算表層
肌肉和深層肌肉的肌力，可完整考慮所有肌群對於脊椎負載的影響，對於肌
力的估算也更精確。可提供臨床上簡易且有效地評估脊椎系統的健康狀況。
亦可幫助了解中樞系統因應各種生理條件改變時，對於肌肉控制策略的調
節。 
 
4. 是否適合在學術期刊發表或申請專利。 
期刊論文撰寫中。 
 
5. 主要發現或其他有關價值。 
本研究的主要發現為: 1) 頸部運動會伴隨著前後向剪力負載增加，而在四個
方向的運動中，只有頸屈曲動作會伴隨著軸向負載的增加。2) 頸部深層肌群
在慢速的頸部運動時，相較於較最大自主出力時，會有更大的肌肉活動。此
與其做為維持姿勢肌群 (posture muscle) 的角色一致。3) 當執行頸部運動使
得頸部偏離正中姿勢時，頸部表層肌群的活動會隨著運動角度的增加而加大。
此與其做為動作肌群 (movement muscle) 的角色一致。將來此模型可望應用
於臨床上評估分析椎間盤退化機制等議題。

Estimation of the deep muscle activation and spinal load of human cervical spine during neck movements 
 
+1Cheng, C H; 2Wang, J L; 3Chen, C P C;  3Chang, Y L 
+1Chang Gung University, Taoyuan, Taiwan,  2National Taiwan University, Taipei, Taiwan,  3Chang Gung Medical Foundation, Taoyuan, Taiwan 
chcheng@mail.cgu.edu.tw 
 
INTRODUCTION: 
 Muscle activations could augment the stiffness of the spine at the cost 
of increased spinal load which may deteriorate the peripheral tissues. 
The coordination of the deep and superficial neck muscles, which 
commonly function as postural and focal muscles, is important to 
maintain proper spinal load of cervical spine in daily life activities. The 
purpose of this study is to establish a new EMG-assisted optimization 
model based on the clinically accessible superficial muscles to estimate 
the deep muscle activation as well as to examine the effect of muscle 
activation level on the cervical spinal load during the neck movements. 
 
METHODS: 
 There were 7 healthy subjects (3 males and 4 females,  24.6±2.4 years 
old) participated in this study. All subjects were right-hand dominant 
without neck or shoulder disorders. A six-channel EMG recorder 
(Trigno Wireless, Delsys Systems, Boston, USA) was used to collect the 
surface electromyographic (EMG) activities, including the 
sternocleidomastoid, splenious and paraspinal muscles (trapezious, and 
capitis groups). The subjects were instructed to perform two kinds of 
tasks while sitting on the chair with trunk firmly restrained. First, the 
participants performed maximal voluntary isometric contraction (MVIC) 
against a fixed surface to a plateau for 3 seconds in anterior, posterior, 
left, and right directions. Isometric forces were measured by an S-type 
load cell (STC-50kgSE, Vishay, USA). Secondly, the participants 
performed voluntary movement from neutral to terminal position in four 
directions with slow speeds (i.e., 10s in duration). The terminal range 
was reached when the subject felt mild resistance. The order of tests was 
randomized and three trials of each test were recorded. 
 
  A three-dimensional musculoskeletal model of the cervical spine was 
built based on the anatomical data obtained from the literatures. The size 
of vertebrae was scaled by the length of C7-tragus. The model was 
equipped with 26 paired muscles which were divided into the superficial 
(sternocleidomastoid , splenious, trapezious, and semispinalis capitis) 
and deep groups (suboccipital, longus capitis and colli, scalenes, erector 
spinae, and cervicis muscles). Muscle forces of the superficial group 
were determined by the muscle activation level [1]. The deep muscles 
were assumed to be maximally activated, and then were adjusted by 
introducing the gain with the following optimization formula:

 The final muscle forces were calculated by multiplying the initial 
muscle forces by the gain value. The spinal loads at the C4-5 
intervertebral disc and ratio of the total deep muscle forces to the total 
superficial muscle forces (FR) during the MVIC and movement tasks 
were calculated. ANOVA was used to determine the effect of movement 
on the spinal loads and FR. Statistical significance was set at p < 0.05. 
  
RESULTS: 
 The mean peak force (N) developed by the subjects during the 
isometric efforts were 71.5±21.5, 83.6±45.8, 51.5±19.1, and 47.0±18.1 
in  anterior, posterior, left, and right directions. The mean ROM during 
the flexion, extension, left, and right side movements were 42.4°±5.9°, 
50.9°±5.0°, 31.5°±6.0°, and 31.5°±5.3° respectively.  
 
 The mean C4-5 compressive loads during peak efforts was greatest  
in extension (1652±232 N). The maximal mean shear forces were 91±40 
N in anterior-posterior direction and 102 ± 75 N in medial-lateral 
direction. The FR were all close to 1 in all directions. The comparative 
data reported by Choi et al. [2] were also shown in Table 1.  
     With increasing neck flexion, the compressive loads were 
significantly increased and the shear forces were toward posterior 
direction, while the trends were opposite during neck extension (p < 
0.01). The anterior shear force increased with increasing left and right 
side bending (marginally significant, p = 0.06 and p = 0.07). The medial-
lateral shear forces increased toward the opposite direction during the 
side bending movements (p < 0.01). The FR generally decreased away 
from the neutral position, and there were significant differences among 
postures during flexion and extension (p < 0.05, Table 2).

DISCUSSION: 
 This study showed that: 1) the neck movements accompany with 
increased anterior-posterior shear force, and only the flexion shows 
increased compressive loads; 2) the deep muscles are more activated 
during slow neck movements than during peak efforts, which coincide 
with the role of posture muscles; 3) the superficial muscle activations 
could increase with movements away from the neutral position.  
 
     In conclusion, this study used the surface EMG technique to predict 
the deep muscle forces and showed their effects on the spinal load. It 
may further be used to assess the issues of spinal degeneration clinically.
