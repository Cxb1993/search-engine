2鋼筋混凝土連續深梁之剪力強度評估
Shear Strength Prediction for Reinforced Concrete Continuous Deep
Beams
編號：NSC 99-2211-E-163-002
執行期限：99 年 8 月 1 日至 100 年 7 月 31 日
主持人：呂文堯 中國科技大學室內設計系
共同主持人：林英俊 國立台灣科技大學營建工程系
一、摘要
本研究建議一混凝土對角壓碎破壞之鋼
筋混凝土連續深梁剪力強度之分析方法。根
據本研究建議之分析方法與所蒐集之兩跨連
續深梁之試驗資料詳加比對。研究結果顯
示，對於不同之剪力跨度與有效深度比，不
同之混凝土強度，不同之水平箍筋及垂直箍
筋，以本研究所建議之分析方法可皆以正確
地預測連續深梁之剪力強度。
關鍵詞：連續深梁、鋼筋混凝土、剪力強度、
壓-拉桿
Abstract
An analytical method for determining the shear
strengths of reinforced concrete continuous deep beams
under the failure mode of concrete crushing is proposed.
The shear strength predictions of the proposed method are
compared with the collected experimental data for
two-span continuous deep beams. The results of this study
indicatethat theproposedmethodcanaccuratelypredict the
shear strengths of reinforced concrete continuous deep
beams for a wide range of shear span-to-depth ratios,
concretestrengths,horizontalhoops,andverticalhoops.
Keywords: continuous deep beams; reinforced concrete;
shearstrength;strut-and-tie.
Introduction
Most typically, deep beams occur as transfer girders,
whichmaybeeither singlespanorcontinuous(MacGregor
and Wight 2005). Deep beams support the load from
columns, transferring it laterally to other columns. Because
of their proportions, they are likely to have strength
controlled by shear. The shear strengths of simply
supported deep beams had been accurately predicted by
Hwangetal. (2000),Russoetal(2005)andLuetal. (2010).
Anupper-boundanalysisofreinforcedconcretecontinuous
deep beams has been presented by Ashour and Moreley
(1996). Calibration of the computed failure loads against
the experimental failure loads for 20 test beams produces
the value of the effectiveness factor for concrete in
compression for continuous deep beams (1996). Typically,
reinforcement for the reinforced concrete continuous deep
beam has consisted of positive and negative
moment-resisting steels and horizontal and vertical hoops
as shown in Fig. 1. The shear action in the reinforced
concrete continuous deep beams leads to a diagonal
compression and tension in a direction perpendicular to it.
Thecontinuousdeepbeamsdonot fail immediatelydue to
the formation of the diagonal cracks (Manicka and
Natarajan 1985, Rogowsky et al. 1986, Ashour 1997).
After diagonal cracking, the concrete between the diagonal
crackscanbeconstitutesthediagonalcompressionstrut, the
external shear thus transferred by the strut-and-tie
mechanisms, this force transferred behavior has been
reported by Hwang et al. (2000) as well as Lu and Lin
(2009). The shear strength of a reinforced concrete
continuous deep beam is reached when the
diagonal-compression strength of concrete is exceeded at
ultimate state. The failure mode of a reinforced concrete
continuous deep beam could be the crush of concrete as
showninFig.1.

Experimentalverification
A total of 51 tests of two-span continuous deep
beams and their test results in the available literature are
used toverifytheproposed model. The specimens of loads
applied through columns are those tested by Rogowsky et
al. (1986), and are shown in Table 1. The specimens of
loads applied through bearing plates are listed in Table 2,
11 of them were tested by Manicka and Natarajan (1985),
and 8 of them were tested by Ashour (1997). In selecting
these test data, a number of screens were applied. They
wereasfollows:
1. The test specimens are reported to have
prematurefailurewereomitted;and
2. The shear span-to-depth ratio da was less
than2.5.

Following Rogowsky et al. (1986), each beam of
loads applied through columns was tested twice. After
thefirst shearspanfailed, itwasexternallyreinforcedwith
a yoke above and below the beam and twelve 20 mm

The support reactions were then remounted to allow for
any deformations due to adding the external stirrups.
The beams were then retested (Rogowsky et al.1986).
However, each beam of loads applied through bearing
plates (Manicka and Natarajan 1985, Ashour 1997) was
onlytestedonce.
