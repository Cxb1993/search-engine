一、中文摘要 
 
在此研究中提出對圓型極化貼片天線
達到提升增益的方法，此法是在覆板的兩
邊蝕刻藕合方型分離式環狀共振器
(CSSSRRs)，而且覆板跟貼片之間相隔著一
空氣層，使天線罩維持在一空氣層高度為
0.1λ的低厚度狀況下，對於覆板的結構，
使每個上下相對的 CSSSRR 單元，有著兩側
缺口相互正交的擺設，這種特別的方向排
列使天線罩不只增加增益，也維持住圓形
型極化的效果。 
 
關鍵詞： 
圓型極化,藕合方型分離式環狀共振器,天
線罩 
Abstract 
 
A method to enhance gain of a 
circularly polarized (CP) microstrip patch 
antenna is proposed. We etch coupled 
square-shaped split ring resonators 
(CSSSRRs) on both sides of a superstrate 
which is separated from the patch by an air 
layer. Thickness of the air layer is around 0.1, 
which keeps the radome in low profile. Open 
gaps of each CSSSRR on opposite sides of 
the superstrate are orthogonally oriented to 
each other. This unique orientation allows 
the radome not only enhance gain but also 
maintain good CP performance. 
 
Keywords:  
circularly polarized(CP), square-shaped split ring 
resonators (CSSSRRs), radome 
 
二、研究目的 
 
本 研 究 是 利 用 簡 易 一 雙 層  n*n 
CSSSRRs 元素懸浮在貼片(patch)天線正上
方架構，實現的操作頻率為 2.5GHz 之圓型
極化天線。此天線的圓形極化效果是利用
底層正方形 patch 截角的方式來達到軸比
(axial ratio)<3dB。另外雙層 CSSSRRs
元素與貼片天線之間的空氣層高度亦會影
響天線增益及其它參數特性，若取得適當
的空氣層高度將可以保持原有的圓形極化
特性並得到最佳的天線增益。 
  
三、文獻探討 
近年來微帶天線被廣泛應用其優點為
重量輕且成本低，但是頻寬較窄且低增益
為兩個主要的缺點，此缺點限制了微帶天
線的應用。在過去的研究中，研究者嘗試
著克服這些因素，如:提出堆疊型微帶天線
[1]，此天線具有高增益或較寬的頻寬；當
結構上層的貼片與餽入貼片之間間距為
0.1 個波長時，具有頻寬增加的特性；當上
述之距離約為半個波長，增益將會提高。
另外，使用互補式隙環共振器(CSRR)可有
效的增加天線增益，論文所提出的結構中
包含了在貼片及餽入線周圍增加(CSRR)結
構[2]，此種結構可以改善回波損耗及增加
天線增益及天線效率。使用隙環共振器
(SRR)用以設計超寬頻天線，在文章[3]所
提出的結構中，作者使用隙環共振器(SRR)
使其達到頻段阻帶的特性，阻斷超寬頻天
線所不需要的頻段，同時維持天線較小的
尺寸且在所須頻段保持高 Q值。

五、討論與實驗結果 
針對上述結果，對於 CSSSRRs 數量與
空氣層高度，以電流分佈來觀察其變化特
性，首先討論在 1*1 元素到 7*7 元素
CSSSRRs 在空氣層為 0.1λ下，貼片天線會
將能量耦合至上方的 CSSSRRs，當 CSSSRRs
越多得到的增益便越高，如圖十四所示，
但對於 6*6 及 7*7 的 SRR 元素而言，由上
視圖來看已經有若干 SRR 位於貼片天線之
外，能耦合到能量便不多，所以從圖六(c)
增益曲線圖中，所得到增益也可知道元素
再增加對於增益的改善並不大，所以選定
5*5 元素 CSSSRR 主要是要取高增益且軸比
(<3dB)的特性。 
 
由之前的分析中，可以在低厚度下達
成高增益的天線，但對於空氣層的高度確
是值得討論的，如圖七(a)及(c)結果中對
於 0.01λ的空氣層可以得到寬的阻抗頻
寬，對於 0.1λ卻是得到高的增益，對於這
特點從場的分佈來觀察，其不同高度的分
佈狀況。

七、結論 
在此研究中，利用CSSSRRs結構蝕刻在
覆板兩邊，蝕刻的CSSSRRs結構在覆板兩邊
使其開口其呈現正交方向，此法適合於增
加圓型極化之天線增益，且可達到將貼片
天線與CSSSRRs元素之間的空氣層高度降
至0.11λ，還保有高的圓型極化增益，若
想得到較寬的阻抗頻寬，亦可將空氣層高
度降至0.01λ。

二、與會心得 
        The symposium covers many advanced topics in electromagnetic. There are both regular 
and post sessions. In regular session, many new fields are added, For example, there are Plasmonics, 
Meta-materials, and Magneto Optics. Several traditional topics such as antennas, transmission lines, 
EM theory, and computational techniques are still the largest part among all topics. In PIERS 2009 
in Moscow, there are 1231 submissions and 886 accepted abstracts, 640 registered authors. 
Full-length paper is not required by PIERS. However, accepted and registered full-length papers 
will be included in PIERS Proceedings (ISTP) and online accessed after the conference. My 
presentation is on a retrodirective array. My paper has already been accepted to be published in 
PIERS. The paper can be classified as antenna array technology. However, it contains only four 
elements. The purpose of our design is to reduce ground reflection while the array is used to 
retro-direct a circularly polarized wave. 
         During my talk with other attendees, I found that not many people understand 
operational principle of a retro-directive array. I spend some time to explain the details. Perhaps, the 
most difficult part is that why our design can reduce ground reflection even for a circularly 
polarized incident wave. In fact, it may be easy to understand that for a linearly polarized wave. For 
a circularly polarized wave, it would be better to decompose the scattering fields into one 
right-hand and one-left hand circularly polarized component. Usually, one component is larger than 
the other component for oblique incident wave. However, the two components have almost the 
same field strength when the wave is incident from (0, 0) direction.  It takes a long time to explain 
that although they have almost the same field strength, they can be distinguished through the 
polarization-dependent technique. I am glad that I can successfully explain it to those who are 
interested in my topics.  
       Many other topics also cause my interest. For example, the potential application of using 
far field. The incident angle increase gradually from 0° to 40° in 10° step and the scattered field in 
each case is observed. The scattered field can be divided into right-hand and left-hand (E-right & 
E-left) components. Fig. 3(a) and (b) shows E-left and E-right pattern respectively. We found that 
E-right component in Fig.3 can successfully retro-direct the incident RHCP wave. The retro-direct 
direction follows direction of the incident wave from 0° to 40° in 10° step. At 10, 20, 30, and 40°, 
E-left component is relatively smaller than E-right component. 
At 0° incident angle, it is shown that the scattered E-left component is comparable with the 
E-right component (due to retro-directive connection). However, the E-left component is mainly 
due to ground reflection. The two components can still easily be separated as they are orthogonally 
polarized. One import advantage using connecting scheme shown in Fig.2 is that the polarization 
direction of scattered field caused by ground reflection is orthogonal to that of scattered field caused 
by retro-direction. This is vital especially for incident a CP wave at normal direction. To say in 
another word, we can easily separate ground reflection from the desired retro-direct field using a 
receiving antenna having the same polarization state as the incident transmitting antenna.  
The above operational principle can be extended to a two-dimension array. Fig.4 shows 
measured and simulated scattered patterns of the array. The measured results are quite agreeable 
with simulated predictions. The measured relative 10-dB beam width is about 130° which is a little 
wider than simulated beam width. 
Conclusion 
In this paper, we design a retro directive array with 2-D retro directivity. We validate that 
circular polarization retro-directivity can be achieved by choosing both transmitting and receiving 
antenna having the same polarization state (either both of RHCP or both of LHCP). The ground 
reflection can automatically be rejected from reception as its polarization state is orthogonal to that 
of the transmitting antenna.
