?????Abstract? 
 
This study, a two-year research project, was aimed at investigating (1) the use of zero-valent 
aluminum-based bimetals as remedial reagents for effective remediation of groundwater 
contaminated with chlorinated organic solvents and (2) the development of a novel 
electro-reactivation technology for extending the longevity of permeable reactive barriers.  We 
report here a summary of the findings and contributions obtained from the first year study.  In 
the first-year study, aluminum-based bimetals (e.g., Fe/Al, Ni/Al, Pd/Al) were developed and 
used to dechlorinate carbon tetrachloride under various pH conditions.  The optimum metal ratio 
was determined in the study.  Characterization of the metal surface was also conducted using 
XRD and SEM-EDS.   
The results of this two-year project are summarized as below: Studies indicated that the 
degradation rate of carbon tetrachloride by aluminum-based bimetals is a function of metal 
content and solution pH.  Under acidic conditions, the order of degradation rate was 
Fe/Al>Pd/Al≈Ni/Al; whereas, under alkaline conditions, the order of degradation rate was 
Pd/Al>>Ni/Al>Fe/Al.  The result of the Fe/Al study is consistent with the previous studies on 
zero-valent iron that reaction rates increased at low pH and decreased significantly at high pH.  
XRD analysis indicated a minimum full surface coverage of iron at the aluminum surface was 
determined when the iron to aluminum ratio was 15% where Fe/Al particles also exhibited 
optimum performance.  The high iron content suggested that iron did not serve as a catalyst.  
On the other hand, the optimum performance of Ni/Al and Pd/Al for degradation of carbon 
tetrachloride was observed at the metal content of 1%, suggesting that Ni and Pd serve as a 
catalyst.  Degradation products included chloroform, dichloromethane, and methane.  In the 
case of Fe/Al, the mass balance of carbon was poor and methane accounted for about 25% of the 
carbon tetrachloride lost; nevertheless, the mass balance of carbon was about 60-75% for the 
Ni/Al system where methane accounted for about 45% of the carbon tetrachloride lost.  The 
yield of methane was 80-85% in the use of Pd/Al systems. The column study showed that TCE 
was degraded by bimetallic Fe/Al during the operation period of 720 h. The effluent TCE 
concentration was less than 0.1 mg/L. This indicates that bimetallic Fe/Al can serve as an 
excellent reactive material. 
Overall speaking, this project was conducted properly according to the schedule.  Major 
objectives were successfully achieved.  The outcome of the study include: two SCI papers, a 
peer-review paper in Journal of the Chinese Institute of Environmental Engineering, three 
international conference papers (ACS national meeting), and six domestic conference papers. In 
addition, one graduate and three undergraduate students were involved and well-trained in the 
project, which provides a high quality of man power for research.  
 
Keywords: Aluminum, Bimetal, Groundwater remediation, Permeable reactive barrier, 
Zero-valent iron, Chlorinated organic solvent, Dechlorination
