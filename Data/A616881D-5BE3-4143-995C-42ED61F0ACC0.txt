三、英文摘要 
  A new proposed gas sensor with stack 
electrodes was built to archive an ultra small gap 
of electrodes by using the metal layers in the 
standard CMOS process. Beyond the 
conventional finger-type electrodes, the stack 
electrodes formed from the inter metal layers of 
CMOS process provide a lower resistance 
contact with the sensing material. The proposed 
gas sensor was built with array of floating 
membranes and serially connected over an 
etched cavity. A micro heater of n-type 
polysilicon with 5.81 kΩ is situated beneath each 
active area of the membrane to obtain a stable 
working temperature with several power 
consumptions conditions. The sensing material 
SnO2 for CO detection was deposited onto the 
stack electrodes after sol-gel formation with 
deionized water and ammonia (NH3) to promote 
the hydrolysis and condensation. After the sensor 
fabricated, a test wire-bond and TO-5 package 
was performed and the sample was test and 
verified inside a CO gas chamber. With careful 
investigation of measurement results, the 
sensitivity of proposed monoxide sensor is 
6.25%/ppm without heating and the sensitivity 
reaches to 15.7%/ppm under 172 W heating 
power which is highly beyond the result of 
previous research work. The experimental 
measurement shows the research is applicable 
for a low cost CO sensor with high sensitivity.

A. Design of Proposed Gas Sensor  
The design and fabrication of CO gas sensors are realized 
by using 0.35µm CMOS IC technology of TSMC combined 
with a subsequent RIE dielectric layers etching which forms 
the finger electrodes shown in Fig. 1. To obtain a high aspect 
ratio and more surface area of electrodes, the metal and via 
layers are inherently been used and apparently a dry etching 
processes of dielectric materials are been used. However, we 
have to adopt one more etching stop layer M1 (1st metal) 
during the drying etching to maintain a sensing working plate 
and the embedded polysilicon heater without the sequent 
etching of the floating membrane. The electrodes include the 
2nd ~ 4th metal layers and via layers between these metal 
layers. The only exclusive metal layer is the 1st metal layer 
which is acts as the etching stop layer for the dielectric RIE 
etching and defines the working plate but electrical isolated 
from the other metal layers. Therefore, the via12 isn’t defined 
without any pattern. 
By using CoventorWare 5.0, we demonstrate the design of 
our proposed gas sensor with top electrodes, embedded micro 
heater and large floating membrane with seven sensing 
elements connected with each others as shown in Fig. 2. The 
etching windows surround around each element with a size of 
width 30µm and length 60µm. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Figure 1.  Demonstration of proposed structure after MEMS post processes. 
The whole active area is 500µm×500µm and the sensing 
electrodes of all the elements are connected in series with two 
different polar types to provide a large sensing area. 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Figure 2.  Electrodes and etching windows of proposed structure. 
 
B. Fabrication Processes  
The thermal isolated based gas microsensors which we 
propose here can be made by combining industrial CMOS 
technology with additional compatible processing steps, e.g., 
anisotropic etching of silicon after completion of the regular 
IC process sequence. The design and fabrication of CO gas 
sensors are realized by using 0.35µm CMOS IC technology of 
TSMC combined with a subsequent RIE dielectric layers 
etching which forms the finger electrodes shown in Fig. 3. 
After the electrodes formation, the partly isotropic and 
anisotropic front-side etching is followed. Several etching 
windows are opened by the RIE processes and the silicon 
substrate etching release membrane-type structures for thermal 
insulation of gas sensor with more efficiency. We use a post 
micromachining process of the silicon isotropic etching with a 
“RLS” mask to define the etching area of V-groove windows 
which are opened by a dry etching of dielectric materials and 
then part of silicon substrate underneath the membrane is 
etched away by isotropic etching of post-process subsequently. 
Using TMAH etching solution the silicon substrate is 
anisotropic etched and removed more clearly. Fig. 4 shows the 
floating structure after substrate etching. The whole chip with 
six identical gas sensors after the post micromachining is 
shown in the Fig. 5.

研究相關主題 
本次會議報告題目為" A New Stack Electrode Type CMOS Compatible Gas 
Sensor "。此次報告我們最新的研究，提出一新穎的堆疊式電極氣體感測器，有別於
傳統之指叉狀電極，能更大幅有效地降低高電阻率之感測材料。同時利用台積電
TSMC 0.35μm 2P4M標準 CMOS製程製作的新穎式氣體感測晶片。藉由改變感測
元件的操作條件來分析，大致可分為工作溫度及環境照度這兩部分，當感測元件操
作於工作溫度 350℃時，其靈敏度高達 0.157%/ppm，表示感測元件操作的工作溫度
越高，其對一氧化碳氣體感測的能力越好。在照光實驗結果發現，當環境照度從 250lx
提高至 1000lx時，其對氣體偵測的靈敏度會由 0.085%/ppm提升三倍至 0.25%/ppm。
