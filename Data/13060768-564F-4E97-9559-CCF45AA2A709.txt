Abstract 
Sulfonated polystyrenes(PSS) possessing various 
extents of sulfonation from 5.2 mol% to 23.5 
mol% were synthesized using polystyrene and 
acetyl sulfate.  PSS membranes were prepared by 
solution casting method.  The conductivity 
coefficients of various sulfonated polystyrene 
membranes were obtained utilizing AC-impedance 
measurements. The conduc- tivity coefficient of 
PSS membrane of proton type was enhanced from 
1.86 x 10-4 Scm-1 to 1.42 x 10-2 Scm-1 while the 
extent of sulfonation was increased from 5.2 
mole% to 23.5 mole%.  The conductivity 
coefficient of membrane with higher sulfonation 
content was in the same order of magnitude as that 
obtained for Nafion 117 membrane (7 x 10-2 
Scm-1).  An apparatus of proton exchange 
membrane fuel cell was constructed for H2/O2 fuel 
supply.  The membrane electrode assembly 
(MEA) was manufactured using Pt/C as both 
catalyst and electrode, followed by hot-pressing 
sulfonated polystyrene membranes in- between 
cathode and anode as the solid electrolyte. The 
performances of PSSH membrane-based MEA in 
the proton exchange membrane fuel cell at various 
experimental conditions are under investigation.  
The comparison of the performance for both PSSH 
membrane-based MEA and commercial Nafion- 
based MEA is explored. 
 
Keywords: Proton exchange membrane fuel cell, 
sulfonated polystyrene membrane, membrane 
electrode assembly, H2/O2 fuel cell
