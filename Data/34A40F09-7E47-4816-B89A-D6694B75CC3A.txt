Abstract 
 
There have been many eutrophication cases of reservoirs and lakes reported all over 
the world.  Excess nutrient supplies may cause eutrophication and high cost of water 
treatment due to high concentration of algal cells.  Some algae, for instance, Microcystis 
aeruginosa, may produce toxins.  There have been Microcystis blooms during late spring 
and early summer every year in Sin-san Reservoir recently.  However, the reason of the 
sudden growth of Microcystis is not clear.  There is still lack of decision-supporting tools 
for identification of the causes of Microcystis blooms nor for the controlling of the 
eutrophication and the dominance of hazardous algae.   
 
Through the investigation of a reservoir ecosystem, a dynamic growth model for 
Microcystis by taking the effects of physical and chemical factors of reservoirs and the 
floating regulatory mechanisms of cyanobacteria into consideration is to be established.  
Through the results of this three-year project, we will be able to predict the outbreaks of 
algal blooms and the dominance of some hazardous algal species by using this developed 
model.  The integrated model will be able to predict the seasonal variation of the 
concentration of Microcystis and to be a predicting tool for the dominance of the 
hazardous algal species. 
 
In the first and second year of the project, in addition to the monthly water quality, 
the floating regulatory mechanisms and the phosphorus uptake and growth mechanisms 
for Microcystis were investigated, and a growth model was established.  Some 
preliminary achievement of the project includes, (1) continuous monitoring of the water 
quality and algae density seasonally; (2) the establishment of major part of the algae 
model; (3) monitoring of the diurnal variation of the vertical distribution of the algal 
species.   
 
In the third year of the project, a trajectory model for predicting the temporal and 
spatial distribution of Microcystis cells was developed.  The predicted concentration 
variation with time and depth matched the observed pattern in the field well.  he present 
model has not yet include the uptake and growth processes of Microcystis, which are 
critical on the dominance of Microcystis and greatly affected by the colony size.  It is 
more reasonable to simulate the migration or behaviour of Microcystis population by 
using trajectory approach rather than advection-diffusion approach, not to mention that 
using the trajectory model can simulate population with a particular colony-size

第一章  前言  
1-1 研究緣起  
水庫水質之良窳一直是國內外環境保護及水資源相關單位關切的問題，而其中藻類優
養問題適為其最。近年世界各地許多水庫湖泊均面臨到水質優養化問題。台灣澄清湖、鳳
山、蘭潭、永和山、石門、翡翠等許多水庫水質也面臨優養化問題。吾等必須建立預測其
發生趨勢的能力，方能採取適當措施，預防優養問題發生。近年來由於過量的營養鹽造成
水庫優養化，藻類滋生增加自來水廠處理成本，更有甚者有些藻類形成之藻華可能產生毒
素或嚴重危害水源水質，如銅綠微囊藻(Microcystis aeruginosa Kutz.) (顏等，2006；吳等，
1997；Wicks and Thiel，1990)及二角多甲藻（Peridnium bipes Stein）（柳，1991；李，1998；
Graham and Wilcox, 2000）將造成飲用水之疑慮。這些具有特殊危害性的藻種，常出現在
某些特別時段，或偶爾爆發大量生長情形。例如新山水庫在每年四月至六月間微囊藻大量
增加。但是此類特殊藻種優勢生長之原因及外在條件，至今仍不甚明白，以致無法確切的
採取預防的策略(吳等，2006；許，2006)。 
目前國內改善水庫水質之手段分為集水區整治及庫內整治工程。在改善集水區污染源
的研究方面，如在集水區設置人工濕地處理污水，以期減少進入庫內之營養鹽（邱，2004）；
針對水理水質傳輸進行模擬，期能掌握河川進入水庫之水質與水量（李，2003；黃，2002）；
以及建立集水區管理機制（鄧，2003）或建立集水區水質改善策略系統（戎，2003）做為
擬定水庫水質改善方案之依據。而庫內整治研究部分，曾以曝氣工程（劉，1993）、人工浮
島及生態工程進行改善水庫優養化的研究（李，2002）。另外亦有許多研究著重於水文水理
及水質模擬（柯，1997；羅，1999；吳，2003），以期先了解水庫之水質變化特徵；庫內工
程改善水質之研究，則朝向水庫與集水區整合水質模式，模擬分析水庫與集水區主要河川
之流量與各項水質因子，擬定集水區整體治理最佳管理作業，評估其對水庫水質改善的效
益（王，1999）。近年來環保署亦積極進行水庫內生態工法之示範研究。 
然而近年來對水庫水文水質及藻類生長的研究發展，發現水庫內部營養源的存在，在
某些時段成為特定藻種生長的關鍵因素（吳，2006；陳，2004）。使得利用集水區污染源削
減以控制優養的效果受到影響。某些藻種似可利用光線梯度及空間非均勻分佈之營養鹽取
得優勢，其原因至今不甚清楚。 
近年來亦有提倡嘗試以生物控制方法抑制優養。由於生物控制方法牽扯整個水庫水體
生態系統之改變，例如加入食藻魚種或貝類等（林等，2006；吳等，2006）。對於生態系統
可能發生之改變，應事先建立模式加以預測及評估，以免實施後得不到預期效果或甚至反
效果。

2-2 藻類浮力調節模式相關文獻  
2-2-1 浮力調節機制 
藍綠藻經常在分層明顯之水體成為優勢藻種，主要原因可能是因為具有氣囊可以增加
浮力減少沈降(Reynolds and Walsby, 1975)，以及團聚在適當的光區(Humphries and Lyne, 
1988)。Ganf及Oliver (1982)認為浮力調節機制使得藍綠藻可以垂直移動以克服光線和營養
鹽的垂直分離，充分利用光線及營養資源，因為在分層明顯的水體其表層的營養鹽濃度是
很貧乏的，然而在深層因為底泥釋出，使其富含營養鹽。藍綠藻便會利用其上浮能力潛游
於最適合光合作用及攝取營養之區域(Brookes et al., 1999)。 
 
一般認為藍綠藻控制沈浮之機制為氣囊控制與載重物控制兩種方式。藍綠藻會藉膨脹
壓（turgor pressure）之改變調節氣囊的大小；另一種氣囊大小不改變，但藍綠藻藉由合成
或代謝體內儲存物質來改變本身的比重(Oliver et al., 1982)。浮力調節機制主要受營養鹽及
光線影響(Oliver, 1994)。例如在氮營養鹽限制環境下，藍綠藻體內氣囊的體積會減小，使
其浮力變小(Klemer, 1978; Klemer et al., 1982; van Rijn and Shilo, 1983; Spencer and King, 
1985, 1989)。再添加氮鹽於氮限制水體可以使Oscillatoria spp.之浮力快速增加(van Rijn and 
Shilo, 1983; Konokpa, et al., 1993)。在CO2為限制因子時，浮力則會增加(Klemer et al., 
1982)。Walsby(1971)發現在光線較弱時，隨著光和作用較不旺盛，此時Oscillatoria spp.之
膨脹壓減少使得浮力增加；在光線較強時假如提供足夠的CO2，Oscillatoria spp.的氣囊會減
小。Brookes及Ganf (2001)發現Microcystis aeruginosa浮力調控並不會因外在營養鹽濃度缺
乏而馬上減少其浮力，而是與體內儲存營養鹽、營養鹽攝取率及可以利用的資源有關。浮
力增加（調控）可以增加藻類捕捉光線的能力（Walsby et al., 1997）。另一個重要的可能機

4-1 九十六年度成果  
4-1-1 水質與藻類垂直分佈之季節性變化 
本研究以新山水庫為研究場址，並且對其進行定期採樣，以做為將來模式校正及驗證
所需之背景資料建立。本計畫去年度共計三次採樣，其採樣日期為2007年8月23日、2007
年12月27日及2008年4月14日。監測項目包括溫度、導電度、溶氧、pH、總磷、溶解性總
磷、硝酸鹽氮、氨氮、總藻數量及微囊藻數量。圖4-1至圖4-8為部分監測項目隨深度變化
之情形。圖4-1為三次採樣之溫度分佈情形，由圖中得知於8月時水體分層最為明顯；12月
時其水體完全無分層現象；隔年4月雖表層水溫度略高於12月份，然4月份之下層水其溫度
較12月份之下層水溫度大約低3℃，可見冬天下層水可達15℃，並在4月溫度回升，漸漸形
成新的水體分層。 
 
圖4-2為定期採樣之溶氧隨深度分佈情形，表層水溶氧極高，即使為冬季溶氧仍高達
7.97 mg/L，此為藻類行光合作用之結果。夏季時因水體分層穩定，雖表層溶氧高達9.33 
mg/L，然下層為低溶氧狀態，至水下8米深其溶氧驟減至2.27 mg/L；12月時水體呈現混合
較為良好情形，可見其溶氧為均一分佈之高溶氧狀態，僅有底泥上層水溶氧偏低（冬天水
位較低，水深約32公尺左右）；4月雖漸漸形成分層，但下層溶氧狀態和12月（冬季）情形
相似。 
 
在過去監測調查發現，水庫水體其反應性磷濃度相當低，甚至低於偵測極限（2 μ
g/L），本研究團隊認為無機磷可能極易被藻類利用，使得水體無機磷濃度極低，故水中反
應性磷並不合適作為藻類磷營養之代表，並以溶解性總磷作為磷營養之代表（溶解性總磷
轉換為無機磷，再被藻類利用）。圖4-3及圖4-4為總磷及溶解性總磷隨深度分佈情形，新山
水庫的磷濃度偏低，三次採樣中可以大致發現下層水之總磷及溶解性總磷濃度略高於上層
水，12月之總磷及溶解性總磷相較8月及4月低，4月時底層之總磷高達93 μg/L。

Microcystis aeruginosa, one of the popular species of Microcystis, tends to 
form colonies.  Large colonies may contain a million cells, but small ones can be 
clusters of only a few dozen cells, and solitary cells may also occur (Dubelaar and 
Reijden, 1995; Joung et al, 2006).  The impacts of colony size on growth and 
movement of Microcystis have been discussed in modeling, experimental or 
theoretical aspects.  Colony size has a large effect on migration velocity (Reynolds et 
al., 1987; Rabouille and Salençon, 2005), grazing pressure (Oliver & Ganf, 2000) and 
surface area to volume ratio. In addition, surface area to volume ratio affects light 
attenuation (Morel and Bricaud, 1981; Robarts & Zohary, 1984; Kirk, 1994; Bricaud 
et al., 1995; Fujiki and Taguchi, 2002), growth rate (Reynolds, 1997b) and nutrient 
uptake.  We believe that Microcystis would be dominating in certain ecological 
system with a competitive colony size distribution.  However, in the past modeling 
of the behavior of Microcystis were based on single colonies or on the averaged 
behavior of many algal colonies, without the consideration of colony size distribution.   
 
Trajectory simulation of the movement of individual particles in a dynamic 
environment is a useful tool for investigating ecological process in the ocean (Werner 
et al., 1993; Hare et al., 1999).  In the last two decades many attempts have been 
done to quantitatively describe the movement behavior of Microcystis in water bodies 
(Okada and Aiba, 1986; Visser et al., 1997; Wallace and Hamilton, 1999; Wallace 
and Hamilton, 2000; Wallace et al, 2000; Rabouille and Salençon, 2005; Rabouille et 
al., 2005).  However, there is rare field data of diurnal distribution of Microcystis so 
far.  Validation of the ability of these particle trajectory models to simulate the 
distribution of the populations in vertically stratified water body is needed. 
The purpose of this study is to simulate the migration behaviors of Microcystis 
of single colonies or colonies with a distribution of size by using the trajectory 
approach.  The population with a distributed radius rather than uniform radius should 
reflect more closely the actual situation in lakes or reservoirs.  The advantages of 
using trajectory approach in simulating the population distribution of Microcystis will 
be discussed. Mathematical model will be established to simulate the vertical 
distribution of the population of Microcystis over time scales of hours and days in a 
calm water body.  Various physical factors such as diffusion, thermal stratification, 
temperature and sunlight intensity which affect the movement of Microcystis will be 
considered through inputting real field data from field investigation in this study.

The positions show no diurnal pattern and are controlled mainly by turbulent mixing 
for colonies with radius of 50 μm (Figure 4.a1).  After a short period of time in the 
dark (from 0 hr to 6 hr) when the density of colonies dropped uniformly, the rates of 
the increase of density are diversified due to different radiation intensity at distributed 
positions for the next 12 hours in the day time(Figure 4. a2).  Colonies with slightly 
larger radii (e.g. 100 μm and 200 μm) obtain enough momentum to sink and to 
float up in spite of turbulent diffusion (Figure 4.b1), however, the velocity of sinking 
or floating cannot catch up the diurnal change of the irradiation intensity.  Most 
colonies can only return to the strong irradiated near-surface approximately three days 
after the colonies started to sink at the end of the first dark period.  The migration 
cycles of colonies are longer than one day and not well synchronized. The simulation 
results indicate that Microcystis with colony radius lager than 300 μm is able to 
resist the turbulent mixing and migrate up and down diurnally (Figure 4.c1, Figure 
4.d1 and Figure 4.e1).  The migration trajectories of the colonies of radius of 300 μ
m are almost similar to that of 400 μm.   During the first 6 hours, densities of all 
colonies reduced to the same density due to darkness (Figure 4.d2 and Figure 4.e2).  
After sunrise, colonies become denser and start sinking.  The colonies with larger 
size get higher speed of migration, according to Stokes’s Law, to overcome the 
turbulence diffusion.   A radius of 300 μm is the minimum size required to achieve 
active diurnal migration for Microcystis with the environmental settings of this study.

Stability which concerns the effects of vertical density stratification and is defined by 
minimum amount of work that the wind must perform to turn a given stratified

better understanding of the internal response of the reservoir to wind forcing the 
Wedderburn number, was computed for varying conditions on the reservoir.

Results and Discussion 
The variance of nitrate was significantly related with stability.  The value of 
nitrate concentration in the whole reservoir was about 0.7 mg/L during lower stability, 
but that the value of nitrate concentration was down to 0.2 mg/L when higher stability 
as result of the distinct concentration difference between epilimnion and hypolimnion.

The concentrations of total phosphorous of epilimnion and hypolimnion were similar

total phosphorous (about 15-30 µg/L) in the reservoir.

observed that Microcystis population increases as stability increasing, but   
Microcystis blooms decline at higher stability.  There one exception for the

exception seems to be related with the special condition of high level phosphorous 
and strong light intensity.  The key factors of limiting growth of Microcystis 
population has been investigated by Principal Component Analysis.  The principal 
factor of affecting Microcystis population was stability, and the variance of light 
intensity was the second key factor; then the nutrient was third key factor.  
 
Conclusion  
In summary, succession in Microcystis is related with stability, light intensity and 
nutrient.  It is important not to explain what the limiting factor is but also to predict 
when the blooms occur. A model based on the feature of Sin-san Reservoir will be 
developed to predict the observed successional changes.

Abstract 
Shin-san Reservoir is a subtropical off-channel reservoir, and has developed a well 
stratified structure in terms of temperature and density from June to October annually.  
Microcystis blooms can be observed during this period of time every year.  It is 
believed that Microcystis cells are capable of catching sun light in the euphotic zone 
and obtaining sufficient nutrients for growth in a well stratified water body, while 
other species of phytoplankton are not.  The diurnal migration of Microcystis 
regulated by the change of buoyancy, which has been reported in the literature, may 
play a key role on this advantageous growth pattern.  Cell density determines the 
velocity of floating or sinking in addition to the random movement due to turbulent 
mixing.  It has been suggested that there are two mechanisms controlling the density 
of Microcystis cells, producing ballast material or compressing the gas vescles, which 
are both regulated by radiation intensity.  A trajectory model which takes the 
diurnally changing solar radiation intensity and its influence on the movement of 
Microcystis cells into consideration has been developed.  The results of simulation 
well describe the buoyancy regulated vertical migration within a 24-hour period.  
The verification of the model has also been done based on the field data of the diurnal 
change of the vertical profile of Microcystis collected in Shin-san Reservoir.  The 
established trajectory modeling approach would enable us to study the effects of 
desynchronized nutrient uptake and growth processed on the growth of Microcystis in 
stratified water bodies in the future.
