in automated food processing. The best way to avoid 
contamination in quality measurement of food 
processing is to use non-contact detectors. The 
progress of gelation determines the physical 
structure and the firmness of tofu product. A firmer 
product has a denser texture, less water content, and 
a larger modulus of elasticity. The behaviour of 
ultrasound in tofu correlates with the fineness of 
the aggregated protein network structure. In 
gelation, the aggregation of coagulant and protein 
alters the concentration of free ions and therefore 
the conductance of the soya emulsion. After gelation, 
the fineness of the protein network structure 
determines secretion of whey residue and hence the 
conductance of the whey.  
 
This project will use ultrasound and conductance in 
measuring the fineness of tofu structure. In 
gelation, ultrasonic attenuation is used as a 
calibrator for the conductance of soya emulsion to 
explore the gelation dynamics. After gelation, 
scanning electronic microscopy (SEM) and textural 
analysis are used to calibrate ultrasonic and whey 
conductance measurements in tofu fineness indication. 
The gelation model can be used to predict the product 
quality.

英文摘要
Tofu is made from soymilk mixed with a coagulant followed by either heat
treatment or high pressure treatment. The taste of coagulated tofu is affected by its
final texture. The final texture is dominated by the concentration of soya milk,
concentration of the coagulant, coagulation pressure and temperature, type of
coagulant, and coagulation time. The firmness of a tofu gel-network determines the
texture and hence the sensation of the tofu. The structure of tofu network can be
observed with a microscope and the mechanical properties are obtainable through
textural analysis or rheological study. These approaches are destructive and
sometime prohibitively expensive and hence they are unsuitable for on-line
measurement of tofu properties in a production line. Thus, this study exploits
ultrasound and the electric conductance of soya milk and of whey for
non-destructive measurement of tofu firmness with scanning electronic microscopy
and textural analysis as calibrators.

Keywords: tofu; textural analysis; ultrasound; microscopic analysis; quality
monitoring and control

圖4中，因為嫩豆腐與板豆腐在凝固成型時所使用凝固劑的不同，致使嫩豆腐孔
洞比板豆腐孔洞還要小，因此，超音波通過小孔洞所造成的衰減會比通過大孔洞
所造成的衰減更少，符合超音波的物理意義，既有的研究成果可以驗證此理論，
超音波有機會成為豆腐質地的檢測工具。然而，孔洞尺寸影響到豆腐的含水量，
進而影響乳清導電度；而整個豆乳導電度會因為凝固過程中，凝固劑與蛋白質的
結合而產生變化(Kohyama et al, 1995)。因此，觀察導電度的變化，可以獲得凝固
過程雨凝固後的豆腐質地。而導電度的測量比超音波更簡單，有必要針對豆乳與
乳清導電度進行系統化分析。
以下對「導電度」的定義：凝固過程中，指的是豆乳導電度；凝固後，指的
是乳清導電度。

結果與討論
電導度變化
豆乳內的離子量會因豆乳濃度、加熱溫度及時間、及加入添加物濃度或種類
等原因而改變。使用電導度計量測三種豆乳濃度(7 °Brix，9 °Brix，11 °Brix)
由表 2可知，豆乳濃度(A1)對豆乳電導度(B1)及乳清電導度(B2)的相關係數
分別為 0.95 及 0.92，A1對豆腐 TPA 硬度為 0.75，屬於高度相關性，原因為豆乳
濃度增加，且豆乳中離子量也增加導致電導度及濃度上升影響其硬度，這些結果
明顯的說明豆乳及乳清電導度不但可以觀察豆乳濃度也可推測豆腐的品質。豆乳
凝固劑濃度與硬度及凝聚度有較大的相關性分別為 0.57 與 0.55，表示凝固劑濃
度越高，則豆腐質地越硬，凝聚度也越高。
豆乳電導度(B1)與乳清電導度(B2)對硬度間相關係數分別為 0.72 及 0.86 有
高度相關；對彈性度為 0.54 及 0.49； 歸納其原因為豆乳濃度增加時，豆乳中
離子濃度也增加，導致凝結過程中的豆乳電導度隨之上升，擠壓後的乳清導電度
也跟著提高。這些結果說明利用豆乳電導度及乳清電導度這種非破壞性的量測不
但可以推知豆乳濃度，也可以預測豆腐的品質。
板豆腐微結構孔面積與超音波衰減係數具有很高的相關性，從圖 15 所示，
板豆腐的孔面積愈大，所造成的超音波衰減相對地也愈大。

與豆腐性質的相關性，綜合以上實驗結果與分析，歸納出以下幾點結論：
1. 隨豆乳濃度增加其豆乳電導度值越高；凝固劑添加後，豆乳濃度 7 °Brix 在凝
固劑濃度 0.5 %時豆乳電導度最小，豆腐產量明顯隨豆乳濃度的增加而有所提
高；而當豆乳濃度越高時乳清產量則越少。
2. TPA 試驗得知硬度的相關係數分別為 0.72 及 0.86 有高度相關；彈性度為 0.54
及 0.49；豆乳凝固劑濃度與硬度及凝聚度有較大的相關性分別為 0.57 與
0.55，表示凝固劑濃度越高，則豆腐質地越硬，凝聚度也越高 ；凝固劑濃度
及豆腐濃度越高則硬度及彈性度越高；而凝聚度明顯的隨著凝固劑濃度增加
而升高。
3. 豆腐在擠壓時間（10 min）及擠壓壓力（10.94 g/cm2）下利用豆乳電導度 EC600
及乳清電導度 EC600 這種非破壞性的量測不但可以推知豆乳濃度，也可以預
測豆腐的產量。
4. 嫩豆腐與板豆腐的口感之所以會有差異，根據顯微影像分析與超音波檢測分
析的實驗結果，可得知大部分的板豆腐孔洞是大於嫩豆腐的，而在超音波的
衰減係數中，板豆腐的衰減比嫩豆腐的衰減大。因此，證明出超音波在豆腐
中傳播時，會受到豆腐內部膠狀組織的關係影響其物理特性。
5. 般的嫩豆腐是不需要擠壓就可以形成完整的盒裝豆腐；而板豆腐必需要在凝
固期結束時，將豆乳舀入鋪了布塊的正方形木箱內，再予以擠壓，將水份擠
壓出來，形成板豆腐。基於製作過程有所差異，使得嫩豆腐的含水率比起板
豆腐還來的高。

2.  SYSTEM FRAMEWORK 
Measurement based on the direct piezoelectric effect can 
be realised as contact or non-contact according to how the 
induced mechanical energy is picked. The contact scheme 
picks mechanical deformation with an adequate probe and 
the non-contact scheme senses acoustic waves emitted from 
the stimulated material. The intensity of the induced 
electric charge can be converted from the detected 
mechanical quantity. Popular contact implementation 
schemes include normal load, cantilever, periodic 
compression force, pneumatic pressure for force application, 
and optical interferometer for deformation detection [6].  
Among the above, force applied as normal load or in 
periodic compression deserves the simplicity and mutual 
compatibility. Thus, we used a loud speaker to generate 
reciprocal movements and forces, in normal load and 
period compression pattern, onto piezoelectric samples. An 
electric charge is induced from the sample by the force. The 
piezoelectric characteristics of the sample are derived from 
the applied force and the induced charge. 
Based on the above arguments, the developed 
measurement system is conceptualised as Fig. 3 using the 
Unified Modelling Language (UML). The framework 
shows signal flows between two functional blocks. 
Processor (personal computer) instructs Stress Actuator, via 
Data Acquisition Device, to exert a certain force on a target 
piezoelectric sample. The intensity of the exerted force is 
picked by Force Meter and then sent back to Processor 
through Data Acquisition Device. Electric charge generated 
from the material is amplified and conditioned by Charge 
Amplifier and Low-Pass Filter and sent back to Processor 
through Data Acquisition Device for on-line analysis. Time 
responses are recorded for subsequently off-line analysis.

framework of Fig. 3. A complete measurement system 
consists of a Microsoft Windows-XP computer, an NI 
USB-6009 DAQ for data acquisition, a stress actuator, a 
force meter, a charge amplifier, and a low-pass filter.

C. System Responses 
PZT and thin film (FS-2513S) samples were used to test 
system responsiveness. Each measurement was repeated 3 
times. Fig. 12 shows step responses to different magnitudes 
of applied forces from the stress actuator. The PZT material 
exhibits first-order dynamics and the thin film exhibits 
second-order dynamics, as expected.

square waveforms of frequencies between 10 Hz and 200

repeated 3 times. Fig. 13 shows sinusoidal responses from
