日治時期台灣西式木屋架構造類型之研究 
 The Study on the Typology of Western Wooden Trusses During the  
Japanese Period in Taiwan 
一、中文摘要 
西式木屋架（Wooden Truss）為台灣
日治時期大量被採用的屋頂構造技術之
一，廣泛應用於木造、磚造及鋼筋混凝土
造等構造中。日本於明治維新之後向西方
習得西式木屋架構造技術，並將其應用於
台灣的建築構造上，但由於日本地震、颱
風頻繁之地理特性，加上尺寸計畫、木材
供給亦具有地區性，隨著日本建築科學研
究之進展，西式木屋架構造技術在日本亦
有所調整以提供建築物更好的安全性與經
濟性。而日本屋架構造技術之發展是否也
應用於台灣日治時期西式木屋架上，為本
研究所欲探討的課題。 
本研究首先企圖蒐集日治時期台、日
兩地之相關「構造書籍」、「建築雜誌」、「建
築教材」、「建築法規」，由相關文獻分析以
釐清日治時期西式木屋架之相關發展脈
絡；另外亦針對台灣現存日治時期西式木
屋架「構造實體史料」及「構造設計圖面」
從事構造調查與分析，調查內容將包括屋
架類型、構材尺寸與建造時間等事項，調
查結果將與上述文獻分析結果相互比對。 
研究成果包括，（1）整理台灣總督府
公文檔案中西式木屋架構造類型、構件尺
寸等之基本資料。（2）藉由文獻史料之相
互比對、分析，了解西式木屋架構造類型
於台灣的演變與應用概況。研究結果將可
作為未來古蹟、歷史建築保存與修護之調
查研究、修復設計之參考。 
關鍵詞：日治時期、木構造、西式木屋架 
 
Abstract 
Western wooden roof truss is one of the 
main roof building systems in Taiwan during 
the Japanese period. The previous literature 
shows that Japanese learned the new building 
system from western world and then applied 
them to the building system in Taiwan during 
1895 and 1945. Nevertheless, Japanese 
modified the system so as to resist the natural 
disasters, such as earthquake and typhoon, 
and meet the economical condition. The main 
purpose of this study is therefore to explore if 
the western wooden trusses in Taiwan were 
changed in accordance with the development 
of this kind of trusses in Japan.  
To improve the knowledge on the 
development of the western wooden trusses 
in Japan and Taiwan, historical materials 
about this kind of wooden buildings before 
1945 in Japan and Taiwan, including text 
books, magazines, architectural codes and 
regulations, will be thoroughly reviewed and 
analyzed. Moreover, existing western 
wooden trusses and their design drawing 
before 1945 in Taiwan will be collected and 
analyzed so as to find out the building 
characteristics of these western wooden 
trusses, such as types of trusses, dimensions 
of components, and their joints. 
The result of this study can be references 
not only for the future study on western 
wooden trusses, but also for conservation of 
these historical buildings in Taiwan during 
1895~1945. 
Keywords：Japanese period, wooden（timber）
construction, wooden（timber）truss

3. 西式木屋架在台灣的應用與演變 
關於上述屋架的補強構造，皆是震災
豫防調查會及相關書籍中提出的補強建議
案，實際執行上仍經過演變之過程，本研
究乃蒐集台灣總督府公文檔案中 76 組西式
木屋架案例，並以台灣現存古蹟及歷史建
築中 24 組西式木屋架案例做為參考，推論
1895年到1938年台灣西式木屋架應用上之
發展過程。 
（1）屋架形式與跨距之應用 
就屋架形式對應跨距應用上，台灣案
例與日本相關書籍上的說明差異不大，棟
束小屋主要應用於 4.5m 到 10m 間（日文書
籍說明為 6m 到 9m），對束小屋主要應用
於 10m 到 18m 的範圍內（日文書籍說明為
9m 到 27m），變形的棟束小屋則用於 10m
到 12m 左右的範圍內（日文書籍說明用於
大於 9m 的跨距中）。 
但台灣的棟束小屋中，尚可依是否安
裝釣束或釣ボルト構件區分為兩種。未安
裝的案例主要應用於 4.5m 到 7.2m 的跨距
範圍（圖 9）。有安裝的案例，在 1924 年
以前主要應用於7.2m到10m左右的跨距範
圍中；1925 年以後逐漸應用於跨距在 6.6m
以下的屋架中（圖 10），是為強化屋架構
造之發展過程。

4. 木材節約與西式木屋架之發展 
日本在濃尾大地震之後，提出木構造
及屋架的補強構造，實際上多為原則性的
補強。1923 年關東大地震之後，日本木構
造建築仍然不斷受到風災、地震之破壞，
其中以 1934 年在日本關西地區因颱風造成
的暴風雨災害（關西風水害）最為嚴重。
同時因為國際局勢上的影響，原使用於鋼
骨或鋼筋混凝土構造的金屬材料價格不斷
上揚，促使日本建築學界有機會透過力學
實驗，陸續掌握木造建築材料強度、接點
強度等基礎資料。 
在建築材料基本強度試驗方面，由森
徹開始於 1931 年《建築雜誌》發表〈關於
木材的機械性能的 2-3 個研究〉，陸續討論
節點與木材受壓、拉張、彎曲強度之影響，
促使 1937 年改正市街地建築法施行規則
時，可以提高木材的容許應力強度（日本
建築學會，1972：p117）。 
在舊有的木構造系統方面，有田邊平
學、狩野春一等人，希望由實驗成果提出
耐震、耐風木造建築結構規範（田邊平學，
1934：pp1087~1110），其研究首先透過
1934、1935 年的實驗了解以螺栓或鐵釘將
筋違或方丈安裝在立柱上的最佳配置、破
壞行為與最大荷重。在台灣的震災調查
中，屋架與立柱間的方丈補強，往往也是
該建築受震災時造成立柱破壞的因素之
一。而日本也有相同的情形，田邊平學的
實驗，即欲先解決此方面的問題，並予以
量化。 
其後經由壁體、木構骨架等一系列木
構造結構實驗的成果，提供 1942 年日本建
築學會中成立「木構造計算規準委員會」
之契機，進一步在 1944 年發表「木構造計
算規準」和「木造建築的強度計算案」（日
本建築學會，1972：p119）。 
而 1937 年到 1945 年中日戰爭及太平
洋戰爭其間，在有限的材料下，日本乃發
展包括剪力環（ジベル）、釘打、膠合等
各種接點構造以降低木材斷面損失，並藉
以增大木造建築跨距空間。於 1940 年到
1941 年乃進行 30-45 公尺的大跨距木構造
實驗（日本建築學會，1972：p116）。台
灣現存屏東東港大鵬灣國家風景區中的原
日軍海軍航空隊停機坪（屋架跨距約
20m，建於 1938 以後）、台中酒廠 8 號倉
庫（屋架跨距約 17m，建於 1939 年）、台
中酒廠禮堂（屋架跨距約 14.5m，建於 1941
年）等建築之屋架形式，即類似當時發展

Tectonic Characteristic of Taiwan’s Chuandou Timber 
Framing on the Basis of Cases in Tainan 
 
Fion, Yi-Chun Lin1, Min-Fu Hsu2 
1Ph.D. student, Department of Architecture, National Cheng Kung University, Taiwan. 
2Professor, Department of Architecture, National Cheng Kung University, Taiwan. 
E-mail: hsiao_na@hotmail.com 
 
 
 
1. Introduction 
The traditional Chuandou timber framing system was mainly applied in vernacular 
dwellings in Taiwan during the late Manchu Dynasty (which ended in 1911) and the 
Japanese Occupation Period (1895-1945). Various examples of this framing system still 
exist. Fieldwork has shown that this framing system is generally used as two main 
frames which identify the central bay of the main hall in vernacular buildings in central 
and southern Taiwan. 
This paper is therefore concerned with tectonic characteristics of Chuandou timber 
framing of the central room in the main hall. Identification of the origin of this frame 
from ancient historical documents, field surveys tracing its distribution in Tainan county, 
and research into the period during which it was in use, all help to elucidate the tectonic 
characteristics of this frame.  
The earliest extant edition of the Lu Ban jind dates from the late Ming Dynasty (15th 
century) and has been a Han traditional carpenter’s manual ever since. The Lu Ban 
jind‘s illustrations of the typical Chuandou system show a frame with long columns and 
short columns, and three-purlin, five-purlin type, seven-purlin and nine-purlin types 
were depicted. However, the Lu Ban jind said : “there might be three, five, seven, nine 
or eleven purlins, according to the wish of the owner” [1]. The fieldwork carried out for 
the present study in Taiwan shows that various constructions of seven-purlin type, 
eight-purlin type, and nine-purlin still exist; overhanging eaves in the front and rear or 
overhanging a rear passageway at the back show how the nine-purlin and eleven-purlin 
types came into being. 
 
2. Tectonic Characteristic of Chuandou Timber Frame 
This Chuandou timber framing system is mainly composed by beams penetrate the 
columns at different heights to assemble the framing system. The infill wall materials 
include wattle and daub, timber panels, and or bricks. 
2.1 Columns  
The long and short columns may be cylindrical or square. They are typically made by 
wood, but in the size around 16 to 20 centimeters wide for the cylindrical ones, and 
around 12 to 18 centimeters for the square ones. The woods were imported from the 
Chinese mainland as ballast from the eighteenth-century to the beginning of the 
Japanese Occupation Period. After 1913, the local forests were exploited to supply the 
woods for building materials, the modern tools and technologies were then used to 
made the columns be square in standard. The long columns stood mostly on pedestals of

Assessment of Wooden structural Components  
by Nondestructive Methods  
 
Yi Jen Tseng1, Min-Fu Hsu2 
1Ph. D. Candidate, 2Professor 
Department of Architecture, National Cheng Kung University, Tainan, Taiwan 
E-mail: tyijen@ms5.hinet.net 
 
 
 
1. Introduction 
For the traditional historic buildings in Taiwan, most of the wooden components 
covered with paintings are considered to be the objectives in conservation, so that it is 
very difficult to assess their deterioration and the safety of wooden structure before 
dismantling. Consequently, an adequate nondestructive testing is essential to diagnose 
these structural components. Not until recently, several modern diagnostic devices were 
introduced for assessing the deterioration of old wooden components, including the 
ultrasonic wave measurement and the drill resistance method. The aim of this study is, 
therefore, to understand the reliable information and the accuracy of damage assessment 
of wooden structural components by these two methods. 
 
2. Materials and methods 
2.1 UV method 
It is the convenient method to assess the damage of the wooden component by using 
ultrasonic velocity (UV) measurement in radial direction [1]. In Taiwan, China fir 
(Cunninghamia lanceolata) is the main material for wooden structure of traditional 
buildings. This study chose new ones of this material, in the size of 14.6×14.6×18cm, so 
as to evaluate the property of wood by using different measurements. For the purpose to 
find out some factors, the experiment uses different UV method and impact of the 
wooden component covered with ground layer.  
(1) on the raw wooden component using different UV method:  
A. surface normal pressure,        B. surface complete pressure,  
C. drilling one small hole (1 cm depth) and using normal pressure,  
D. drilling one hole (1 cm depth) with Vaseline coupling and using complete pressure 
(2) on the same component covered with ground layer using the same ways of 
measurement as shown in (1). 
2.2 RST method  
The drill resistance method, RESISTOGRAPH system (RST), has been considered to be 
a suitable tool for detecting decay in timber structures. In Taiwan, the decay inside the 
the post is the major damage of wooden historic buildings. The goal of this experiment 
was to evaluate wooden posts within a listed historic building, a pavilion in Ze-Sian 
Vegetarian Hall, by RST method. In general, the deterioration of posts were mainly the 
decay of the bottom and their decay range were mostly under 30cm of the wooden post. 
Therefore, the RST drilling started from the bottom of the post, and drilled 10cm up 
each time (Fig1).
