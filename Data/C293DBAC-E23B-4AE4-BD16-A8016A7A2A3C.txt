資金額不是一般台灣公司可以承擔的。因此，在欠缺
資金支持且著重短期回收的情況下，台灣企業從事產
品研發時，所採取的作法通常以「快速追隨領先者」
的研發方式進行。 
當今在資訊快速的流通下，競爭族群間可透過相
關電子工具搜集並監視所需的資訊，再經由專業的分
析，即可充分掌握對手的商業情報與研發動向。資訊
的發展使彼此間營業秘密接近透明，另一方面研發的
速度也因為電子工具的協助而加快。企業為確保競爭
優勢，必須將研發的成果進一步轉化成為公開登記的
智慧財產權（Intellectual Property Right, IPR）加以保
護，才可以確保企業本身的利益。在知識經濟的時
代，智慧財產權的運用已成為各企業在市場攻防時的
利器，縱使是具規模的大企業，也往往必須經由法律
上的保障，才能維護自己的辛苦成果，延續競爭優勢。 
無論是市場領先族群間之競爭，或者是委託代工
與委託設計製造廠商想要切入市場，其進入的時間點
通常是在該技術的成長期，甚至是成熟期。尤其是後
兩者性質的廠商想要切入市場，本身即屬於技術落後
者，若想進入市場獲取利益，必須面對的是領先者佈
下的專利障礙。 
而在面對專利障礙時企業有三種安全進入市場
的方式因應，分別為尋求授權、投入能量進行創新研
發、以及專利迴避設計（Design-Around）方式閃避領
先者之專利。其中，若為相互競爭之企業，其尋求授
權之機會甚微；而且此方式缺乏自主性，處處受制於
人，並可能付出龐大的授權金。投入能量進行創新研
發的方式，雖有充分的自主權，但因投入的金額相對
龐大，研發時間較久，風險高將造成投資不確定性，
而且可能會因時效問題而喪失市場。相較之下，進行
專利迴避設計是最佳的選擇，雖可能負擔風險，進行
專利迴避設計有快速且擁有高主控權之優點，同時迴
避設計之成果可回饋為研發設計與企業經營策略之
參考。 
 
三、文獻回顧 
專利迴避概念係衍生自於法律學理中的專利侵權
判定程序，目前Nydegger與Richards【1,2】已提出專
利迴避設計可遵循的步驟。黃【3】採行元件迴避設計
方法、等價變換方式、技術手段迴避設計法與一般發
明思想從事專利迴避設計。羅【4】提出元件失蹤法、
複數規一法、隱善揚惡法、環伺覓食法、復古還原法、
化零為整法、異取同工法、檢拾回收法、集其大成法、
一箭雙鵰法。 
劉【5】與陳【6】結合專利策略，搭配 TRIZ 與
QFD 等方法實行於自行車飛輪之創新迴避設計，劉 
等人【7】利用 TRIZ 方法與公理設計於滑鼠設計之個
案研究，林【8】運用 TRIZ 原理探討專利開發實例。
張【9】運用 TRIZ 與可拓等一系列方法進行人體發電
裝置之產品之創新迴避設計及綠色創新設計，王等人
【10】利用可拓方法分析產品結構與技術，套用新型
式油壓缸之迴避設計，並提出兩個可供產出新專利之
參考方向。張與周【11, 12】利用專利機能矩陣與 TRIZ
方法，以元件關聯分析進行專利群之迴避設計程序，
針對一群嬰兒車產品群進行收折裝置之迴避設計。 
 
四、研究目的 
本研究將提出一個主要以避免全要件侵權判定
成立為原則之專利迴避設計程序，作為產品研發人員
之參考依據。該專利迴避設計程序將區分為專利分
析、設計解題與成果評估等三階段進行研究，如圖 
1。本研究將透過系統化創新設計工具將此三階段加
以連貫整合，使該程序於專利迴避分析階段時，可以
得到明確的設計建議或設計條件；於設計解題階段，
可以順利完成設計解答並檢驗設計達成度；於設計成
果評估階段時，可以評估檢驗法理上的迴避效果。

評分方式主要比對元件功能項，如圖 4，表之直
行代表各產品之功能的傳遞途徑，拆解成元件、元件
執行功能項，表之橫列欄數目即代表本產品群之所有
功能項數目，行列兩者間行成空格矩陣，進行功能交
集比對，若該空格之行列之功能項相同，則在該格內
註記「＊」，將所有空格依次進行判定註記。接著進
行單一功能項得分計算，將各功能項欄之「＊」加以
統計，每一「＊」得 1 分，再檢查功能項是否皆為專
利範圍內之相同特徵元件，如果是（Y），則進行加權
將該分數乘以 2 倍；若功能項為專利範圍內，但不為
相同特徵之元件（N），則不作加權；另若功能項本身
不在專利範圍內亦不作加權（X）。在範例中，功能項
FA11與 FB11有 2 個「＊」得 2 分，再檢查其功能項皆
為專利範圍內且為相同特徵元件，進行加權將該分數
乘以 2 倍，故功能項 FA11與 FB11各有 4 分。 
將計算後各功能項之得分，填入功能得分欄位
中，然後依各元件擁有之功能項計算各元件之得分，
最後可以得到各功能項與各元件之權重排名，依權重
產生迴避設計之建議，本例結果以 A2元件包含 F21、
F22功能項，得分 9 分最高，列為第一優先迴避對象，
且功能項 FA21被包含於 A2、B3，C2三個元件中，故
本設計例只要針對此功能項進行迴避設計或針對
A2、B3，C2三元件作迴避設計即可達成迴避效果。

AN ECO-INNOVATIVE PROBLEM-SOLVING DESIGN PROCESS 
COMBINING TRIZ SU-FIELD MODEL AND STANDARDS 
 
Hsiang-Tang Chang1, Ya-Chuan Ko2 
 
1Department of Industrial Design, Tunghai University, Taichung, TAIWAN 
2Graduate School of Applied Art & Design, Shu-Te University, Kaohsiung, TAIWAN 
 
 
 
Abstract: Design engineers often spend a lot of time to resolve problems.  They sometimes 
must compromise with the problem despite they have made their efforts to search for all the 
possible solutions.  This study will propose an eco-innovative problem-solving design 
process combining TRIZ tools, including Su-Field Model and Standards.  A design 
engineers can construct his problem model according to the properties and rules of Su-Field 
Model, then through several check criteria, the classified Standards could be led in for 
resolve the problem.  Moreover, the TRIZ tools will be integrated into the new updating 
conflict-problem-solving CAD software “Eco-Design Tool,” the more fascinating 
animations and convenient function would be conducive to eco-innovation. 
 
 
 
 
1. INTRODUCTION 
 
Recently, eco-innovative products have been 
popularized a couple of years because of the 
environmental consciousness.  Most companies 
always make their efforts to develop their novel and 
eco-friendly products.  New ideas, processes and 
technologies dominate the present development of 
eco-innovative products, even the trend of future 
change.   
However, an excellent design needs human 
talent, and the design may needs more experience 
and knowledge; in other words, a good design 
engineer may have the abilities of biological science, 
chemical science, material science, electrical 
engineering, mechanical engineering, etc.   
But, eco-innovative product design tasks could 
often perplex rookie design enginers.  In order to 
resolve the perplexity, the design engineer may need 
an efficient integrative process to accomplish the task.  
Currently, some eco-innovative design methods, 
which support designers in reducing the 
environmental impact of the product throughout its 
life cycle, have been developed [1-3].  However, 
these methods mostly focus on redesign or 
modification of existing products.  Design engineers 
may need a faster and systematic method to develop 
their eco-innovative products, especially in the stage 
of idea incubation.   
Further, if there is the software package which 
can assist design engineers to resolve the problem 
when they develop ideas, design engineers will spend 
less time and obtain the solutions more efficiency. 
 
2. TRIZ THEORY 
 
TRIZ (the Russian acronym for the “Theory of 
Inventive Problem Solving”) was constructed in the 
former Soviet Union by Altshuller [4-6], he spent 
more than fifty years working on his theory.  TRIZ 
provides some helpful tools to assist design engineers 
resolving most design dilemmas.  The TRIZ theory is 
composed of several pillar tools, including the 
contradiction matrix, Su-Field model, laws of 
evolution, ideal final result, stansards, and ARIZ (the 
Russian acronym for the “Algorithm for Inventive 
Problem Solving”).  The contradiction matrix is 
composed of the 39 engineering parameters and 40 
inventive principles and is as known to most TRIZ 
users or researchers, and it. 
Some eco-innovative design processes are based 
on the TRIZ contradiction matrix [7-9].  However, 
actually, other TRIZ tools are probably more useful 
than the contradiction matrix for achieving 
breakthrough innovation [10].  Therefore, in this 
article, we will try to apply and integrate the Su-field 
model theory and satndard solutions into the 
problem-solving process during the development of 
eco-innovative product.  Furthermore, in order to run 
the design process more conveniently, applying these 
TRIZ tools to computer aided software package is 
another part of the achievement in this article.

4. STANDARDS 
 
“Standards” is another one of the TRIZ tools, and 
they are also named “Standard Solutions.”  Standards 
and Su-Field model theory were incubated in 1975, 
and published in 1985 [7].  As similar to the 
contradiction matrix, the Standards are the ways to 
resolve problem Su-Field models.  There are 76 
Standards, which are classified according to their 
attributes, as shown in Table 2.   
However, it is too complicated to use Standards 
with Su-Field model theory.  According to the 
description of existing TRIZ books, as the 
Savransky’s or Fey’s book, a design engineer must 
run a large process for obtaining the recommended 
Standard.  It not only takes the design engineer a lot 
of time, but also lowers his willing to use Standards 
and Su-Field model.

5.2. Design example-new shower drain 
 
The above-mentioned proposed design process is 
illustrated by the design example of shower drain.  
As is known to all, the traditional house shower drain 
and pipes are often clogged with grease and hairs, 
and people must use much chemical cleaner and 
water to make them flow without obstruction.  
However, the chemical cleaner could make negative 
impact to the environment.  If a new shown drain can 
efficiently keep the hairs entering the pipe, the pipe 
will be not easily clogged and the chemical cleaner 
will never be used.  Through applying the proposed 
process, we can obtain a new design step by step: 
1. Receive a design problem, and we will make 
our effort to avoid the pipe clogging. 
2. List all probable Su-Field models which are 
related to the problem.  In the first model, as 
shown in Figure 7, we can set the hydraulic 
field FH (by water) in the model, S1 is the 
traditional shower drain and S2 is hairs.   
The hair S2 could adhere to the drain S1, and
