摘要 
本研究嘗試將雷射深雕技術引入
商標模具或金屬幣模之製作，利用雷射非接
觸加工特性克服傳統 CNC 模具製程在非規
則性曲度設計模型加工限制。以脈衝式
Nd:YAG 雷射機製作商標樣品模具，藉由實
務開發完成商標模型樣品過程，探討雷射三
維雕刻技術之加工特性與限制。研究中提供
雷射深雕產業案例與雷射雕刻參數校正、分
析過程，期盼能提供後續需接觸相關產業之
研究人員，快速釐清雷射能量、雕刻掃描速
度、雷射脈衝頻率、雕刻線間距、雷射切層
參數、切層角度變化對加工特性實務影響經
驗。整個研究顯示若使用雷射深雕製程製作
加工三維模型時，若欲得最佳模型曲型精度
加工，其雷射切層參數越小越好但該雷射強
度必須符合該加工材料最低安全汽蝕門
檻；脈衝式雷射參數調校建議順序為先調整
雷射輸出脈衝頻率，然後再進行雷射電流
（功率）的變化，最後再調整雕刻掃描速
度，以利達成最佳加工效果。 
關鍵詞：雷射深雕、金屬幣模、雷射參數 
 
1. 前言 
隨人類文明的發展，對於產品品質需求
不斷增長，為因應供給品質需求與成本考
量，常見以「模具」來大量生產標準化之產
品，就模具產業加工角度而言，對製作模具
的加工精度與一致性要求遠比產品來得嚴
苛。傳統模具製作加工方式以 CNC、放電加
工、電化學腐蝕等為大宗，對於飾品模具而
言產業中則以 CNC 加工為主。回顧過去文獻
中學者 Wang 等人[1]利用 3D 雷射雕刻加工
方式，雕刻於陶瓷，藉由更改雷射輸出功
率、雷射掃描速度、雷射脈衝頻率三個參數
變化探討雷射雕刻後之深度變化，由研究成
果得到影響 3D 雷射雕刻深度品質最重要的
參數為雷射輸出功率，剩下兩個參數可能需
仔細選擇才能使雕刻深度品質達到最佳效
果；學者 Dubey 等人[2]研究提供建議給予初
進入雷射加工相關領域之研究者，提出雷射
光束加工特性和使用雷射光束加工後對材
料產生缺陷可能形成之原因；學者周芳銘等
人[3]利用數值模擬分析法，模擬雷射深雕時
造成的溫度梯度對 SUS304 不鏽鋼材料所發
生的溫度場與應力場之變化，藉由模擬結果
得到提高雷射輸出能量進行雕刻時，因提高
能量造成材料吸熱(預熱)效果提升反而降低
材料因雷射深雕產生之熱應力影響，同時也
藉由模擬雷射雕刻掃描路徑不同所產生之
溫度場與應力場變化，找出最佳雷射雕刻路
徑移動方式；學者 Navas 等人[4]利用雷射光
束加工特性在不銹鋼材料表面披覆熔化塗
層合金元素，目的得到兼具耐腐蝕、耐磨耗
特性材料；學者 Santos 等人[5, 6, 7, 8]利用三維
圖檔與搭配雷射細密切層加工，燒結粉末材
熔覆成型，得到高精度細小模型並兼具快速
制模加工特性；學者 Cicalǎ 等人[9]利用實驗

度求得。學者王仁傑、陳譽文等人[10,11]以
CNC 工具機刀具路徑為研究主軸，探討工

飞秒激光应用加工玻璃基材之生物芯片微管道 
 
洪廷甫 1，朱伟中 2，张金龙 3，郭静男 4，傅龙明 1  
(1.屏东科技大学材料工程系，屏东，91201；2.成功大学工程科学系，台南，70101；3.屏东科技大学车辆工程系，屏东，91201；
4.工业技术研究院，台南, 73401) 
 
摘要：本研究提出一种使用的飞秒激光系统，以直接读写烧蚀的方式，进行快速的玻璃基材之微流体芯片的制造。利用此方法所
制造之微流道，将以电子显微镜(SEM)及原子力显微镜(AFM)表面轮廓仪进行个各项表面性质分的析。利飞秒激光加工玻璃基材
时，有较少的热会产生，因此有相对少的脆裂行为。此微流体芯片是利用便利的软件包绘制而成后，再传输至飞秒激光系统
以直接读写烧蚀的方式在玻璃基材上进行微管道的烧蚀，并利用 650℃并持温 5 小时的退火处理来改善微管道的壁面质量。利
用飞秒激光制造微管道可提供一个便利且迅速的玻璃基材之微流体芯片的制造。 
关键词：微流体芯片；飞秒激光；玻璃；微管道

Rapid Microfluidc Biochips Fabrication by Femtosecond Laser on Glass Substrate 
 
HONG Ting-Fu1, JU Wei-Jhong2, CHANG Chin-Lung3, KUO Ching-Nan4, FU Lung-Ming 1 
(1. Department of Materials Engineering, Pingtung University of Science and Technology ,Pingtung 91201； 
2.Department of Engineering science,Cheng Kung University, Tainan 70101;  
3. Department of Vehicle Engineering, Pingtung University of Science and Technology, Pingtung 91201； 
4. Associate Researcher, Laser Application technology Center,Tainan 73401) 
 
Abstract：This paper uses a femtosecond laser scriber to perform the direct-writing ablation of glass substrate for the development of 
microfluidic biochips. The surface quality of the ablated microchannels and the presence of debris and distortion were examined by 
scanning electron microscopy (SEM) and atomic force microscopy (AFM) measurement techniques. The developed femtosecond laser 
ablation system provides a versatile and economic approach for the fabrication of glass-base microfluidic chips. In the laser writing 
process, the desired microfluidic patterns are designed using commercial computer software and are then transferred to the laser scriber to 
ablate the trenches. The results show that a very smooth channel wall can be achieved through the annealing process at the temperature 
650℃and 5 hour. The system provides an economic and powerful means of rapid glass microfluidic biochips development. 
 
Keywords：Microfluidic biochip, Femtosecond laser, Glass, Microchannel

Fluorescence Light with Passive Focusing 
 
Yao-Nan Wang1, Jik Chang Leong1,Chin-Lung Chang1, Chang-Hsien Tai1, 
 Chien-Hsiung Tsai1, Lung-Ming Fu2, Jr-Ming Miao2

ABSTRACT 
In this paper, a microflow cytometer for particles/cells 
passive focusing, fluorescence detection, and sizing is 
demonstrated with a convergent geometry of 50 µm detection 
gate channel and a pair of external optic fibers on poly-
dimethylsiloxane (PDMS) microchip. A laser (635 nm) is used 
for fluorescent excitation. The amplitudes of the backward 
fluorescence signal and forward scattered light signal indicate 
the corresponding fluorescent intensity and size of the detected 
fluorescent particles and labeled white blood cells, respectively. 
In the experiment, the uniform fluorescent light intensity was 
measured by focusing the fluorescent particles in a 
200×50×30µm detection gate channel. However, the emitted 
fluorescence and forward scattered light intensity of different 
size particles/cells are also measured simultaneously by 
external optic fibers. 
INTRODUCTION 
 Flow cytometer has become an important diagnostic 
tool used in research and clinical laboratories for broad 
application in various fields, such as cell biology, immunology, 
oncology, food science [1-4]. In these system that performs 
multiple analyzing functions on individual bio-particles 
including counting, sorting, characterizing of size distribution, 
and fluorescent detection. To achieve accurate and reliable 
detection results, flow cytometer utilizes sheath flow caused by 
hydrodynamic focusing effect to confine samples within a 
narrow stream and then the cell samples are forced to pass 
solely through the focal point of the laser. In recent years, the 
rapid advances of lab-on-a-chip technology have made great 
impact on biomedical sciences and healthcare industry [5-7]. 
One of the most important applications of lab-on-a-chip 
technology is the on-chip flow cytometric analysis of cell and 
particles [8]. Compared to labor-intensive and expensive 
commercial flow cytometer, micro-flow cytometers have some 
critical advantages of lower fabrication and operating costs, 
reduced sample and reagent consumption, greater portability, 
and the potential for integration with other miniaturized 
devices.  
In the past decade, many research groups developed 
microflow cytometers by microfabrication technologies and 
focusing approaches to miniaturize the devices for bioparticle 
detection. Many successful micro-flow cytometers were 
demonstrated for bioparticles detection utilizing hydrodynamic 
focusing [9-13], electrokinetic focusing [14-16], and 
dielectrophoretic focusing[17-19] techniques in recent years. 
The sample focusing in these proposed microflow cytometer 
can be realized by bulky syringe pumps or embedded 
microelectrode in the microchip integrating with high voltage 
power supply. 
The sample focusing is one of the key techniques for 
bioparticle detection in microflow cytometer, which can make 
the bioparticles pass through the center of interrogation area 
one-by-one in order to optimize detection signals. Typically, 
two-dimensional hydrodynamic focusing utilizes two sheath 
flows on both sides to confine and squeeze the samples into a 
thin stream in the center of microchannel. For example, Tung et

SAMPLE PREPARATION 
The commercial calibration fluorescent polystyrene beads 
are used as reference standards in flow cytometer experiments 
that can be used to estimate relative fluorescence intensity of 
labeled cells and size by comparing the scatter signals. In the 
experiment, commercial fluorescent microparticles of three 
different sizes were initially used to evaluate the performance 
of the proposed microfluidic system. FluoSpheres® 
polystyrene microparticles (Scarlet, 645/680, Invitrogen, 
Carlsbad, CA) of 15 μm (actual size of 15.41 μm) are 
originally supplied as suspension in distilled water with 0.15 M 
NaCl, 0.05% Tween 20, and 0.02% Thimerosal. The particle 
suspension has original number concentration of 1×106 / ml 
and is further diluted with DI water. The ultra rainbow 
fluorescent particles of mean diameter 10.2 μm (8.1-12 µm) 
(Bangs Laboratories Inc.) with a concentration of 2×106 
beads/ml were originally supplied as a suspension in deionized 
water with 0.02 % sodium azide and 0.01% NP4O. Rainbow 
fluorescent particles 3.0-3.4 µm (BD Biosciences) have a 
concentration of 2×106 beads/ml. All of the particles were re-
suspended into appropriate concentrations using 7.5 mM 
sodium borate buffer (with a pH value of 9.39) before the test 
in 1 ml aliquots. 
The white blood cells are PBMC (Peripheral Blood 
Mononuclear Cell) separated from blood of healthy donors 
through Ficoll-HypaqueTM (Pharmacia Biotechnology Group, 
Uppsala, Sweden) centrifugation. For fluorescent staining, the 
freshly isolated or thawed PBMC were resuspended at 20 × 106 
/ml in PBS (phosphate buffered saline) containing 5 µM 
SYTO-62 (Molecular Probes) for 20 min. Cells were then 
washed 3 times in PBS and re-suspended at a density of 10 × 
106 /ml in PBS. The cells were spun down and re-suspended in 
7.5 mM sodium borate buffer with a final density of 105~106 
/ml. 
DETECTION PRINCIPLE 
The operation principle of the optical detection system is 
similar to that of the conventional flow cytometer, using a laser 
light as the excitation source in the forward scattered light for 
particle size discrimination, and backward fluorescent light 
takes the place of the side scatter light for fluorescent detection. 
For the particles fluorescent and sizing detection, the 200 μm 
optical fiber was fixed in a movable cartridge on top of the 
microchip to transmit excitation laser light and receive 
fluorescence emission light. The excitation was done by a 25 
mW single mode diode laser (wavelength of 635 nm, Blue Sky 
Research, CA). The laser beam went through a filter cube (OZ 
Optics, Canada) and was reflected into the fluorescence 
detecting fiber by the dichroic mirror. The laser beam was 
focused at the detection gate channel by the fluorescence 
detection fiber that transmitted both excitation laser light and 
sample emission light concurrently. In the micro-flow 
cytometer microchip, both the glass substrate and PDMS 
microchip are transparent so that the optical detection window 
can transmit optical signals in the interrogation region. The 
schematic diagram outlining the principle of the system and 
cross-sectional dimensions of the microchip were shown in 
Figure 2.

System throughput is one of the key factors of the on-chip 
flow cytometric function. In the proposed micro-flow 
cytometer, an optical fiber of 200 μm in diameter was utilized 
for evaluating the counting efficiency based on particles 
fluorescent light detection. As shown in Figure 7, for 3.2 μm 
fluorescent beads a counting rate of 8 particles within 500 ms 
was recorded by the detected fluorescent light signals. The 
shortest time for a single particle to pass through the detection 
region is less than 50 ms and consequently a maximum 
throughput of 1200 particles per minute can be achieved. 
However, Sample migration velocity in the detection region is 
one of parameters to determine the system throughput. The 
shorter time period for single particle to pass through the 
detection region, the more particles can be counted and more 
signal overlap will be reduced. In the proposed configuration, 
the moving speed of the sample particles will increase up to 
four times when it passes through the convergent channel.
