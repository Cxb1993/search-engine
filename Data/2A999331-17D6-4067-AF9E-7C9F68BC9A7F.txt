心物法評估小心輕放抬舉作業之抬舉能力
Determining the lifting capacity of handling with care by psychophysical method
計畫編號：NSC 98-2221-E-211-005
執行期限：98 年 8 月 1 日至 99 年 7 月 31 日
主 持 人：鍾綉貞 助理教授 華梵大學工業工程與經營資訊學系
計畫參與人員：郭佳珍、洪詠彧 華梵大學工業工程與經營資訊學系
一、中文摘要
本研究共徵募 10 位男性華梵大學
學生參與實驗，研究主要目的為探討
抬舉方式(小心輕放抬舉與一般抬舉)
與抬舉頻率(1 次/分鐘、4 次/分鐘)以
及握把角度(30°, 45°, 60°, 75°, 90°)對
受試者最大可接受抬舉重量(maximal
acceptable weight of lift, MAWL)、心搏
率增量(△HR)、握力差與手腕姿勢(橈/
尺偏與屈曲/伸展)的影響。實驗過程
中，受試者之心搏率（HR）、最大可
接受抬舉重量與手腕姿勢同時一併收
集。經由統計分析顯示：受試者對最
大可接受抬舉重量、心搏率增量以及
手腕姿勢等應變項皆有顯著影響
(P<0.01)。抬舉方式對手腕姿勢之最大
右手伸展有顯著影響(P<0.01) 。手腕
姿勢之左手平均與最大尺偏、最大屈
曲與抬舉能力─最大可接受重量以及
生理負荷之心搏率增量顯著受抬舉頻
率所影響(P<0.05)。最大可接受重量與
手腕姿勢也隨著握把角度不同而有明
顯差異(P<0.01)。至於握把角度與抬舉
方式之交互作用則對手腕姿勢變項有
顯著效應(P<0.01)。
關鍵詞：握把角度、圓柱型握把、小心
輕放、最大可接受抬舉重量、抬舉頻
率
Abstract
Ten male subjects were recruited from
Huafan University. This study aimed to evaluate
the maximal acceptable weight of lift (MAWL) in
dynamic lifting tasks with care of different
combinations of lifting frequency (1 lift/min and 4
lift/min),andhandleangle(30°,45°,60°,75°,90°).
The subject’s heart rate (HR), MAWL, wrist
posture (ulnar/radial deviation and
flexion/extension), decrement in the strength of
power grip, ratings of perceptive exertion (RPE)
weremeasuredandanalyzed.TheANOVAresults
indicate that all of responses are significantly
affectedbysubject(p<0.01).Theeffectofhandling
w/wo care on wrist posture (maximal right hand
flexion) is significant (p<0.01).Also the wrist
posture ( the average and maximal ulnar deviation
of left hand), MAWL, and increment in heart rate
(ΔHR) are significantly affected by lifting
frequency(p<0.05). MAWLand wrist posture are
significantly affected by handle angle (p<0.01). In
addition, the interaction of lifting w/wo care and
handle angle have significant influence on wrist
posture(p<0.01).
Keywords: Handle Angle, Cylindrical Handle,
LiftingWithCare,MAWL,LiftingFrequency

（二）實驗設計
本研究以心物法進行實驗，因此抬舉實
驗的自變數組合不宜過多，以降低受試者無法
完成所有實驗的風險。
本研究之實驗設計採完全隨機化集區設
計（completelyrandomizedblockdesign），受試
者(10名)視為集區效應。除了受試者之外，5
種握把角度(30°,45°,60°,75°,90°)、2種抬舉方
式(小心輕放抬舉與一般抬舉)與2種抬舉頻率
(1 次/分鐘、4 次/分鐘)為實驗自變數，共有20
(5×2×2=20)種組合，換言之，每位受試者必須
完成 20次實驗。每次實驗過程中詳實紀錄受
試者之最大可接受抬舉重量 (maximal
acceptable weight of lift, MAWL)、手腕姿勢(尺
偏/橈偏、屈曲/伸展)、主觀施力知覺評量(ratings
of perceptive exertion, RPE)以及心搏率(heart
rate, HR)等資料。此外本實驗為避免其他非本
研究想探討的因素干擾研究結果，實驗控制變
項為抬舉高度，每次實驗高度皆為膝到肩高。

(1) 訂製抬舉作業架：此為可調式之模擬抬舉
作業架，用以調整物料箱握把高度至實驗所需
之高度( 含平台，鏈條，鋼索，扣環等)。
(2) 實驗用物料箱：為自行設計之訂製木製物
料箱，木箱含有握把角度調整機構，以利調整
握把角度，如圖1所示。

(3) 心搏率量測儀(PolarHeartRateMonitor)：記
錄實驗過程之心搏率變化。主要組件包含錶型
記錄器與心搏率偵測傳送器(HRtramsmitter)。
(4) 關節角度量測系統：採用Biometrics 公司

要組件包括身高計、展開式測徑器、滑動式兩
腳規與捲尺等，再加上電子磅秤，即可用來量
測人體計測項目。
（四）實驗步驟
本研究之每次實驗步驟，如以下所述：
(1) 根據實驗隨機順序，於受試者到來前完成
物料箱握把角度與抬舉高度之調整工作。
(2) 簡介實驗自變項組合，幫助受試者了解實
驗之作業條件。
(3) 量測五分鐘休息心搏率(HRrest)
(4) 量測實驗前最大握力(力握)。
(5) 閱讀心理物理法指示語。
(6) 做暖身操五分鐘。
(7) 調整重量：以心理物理法進行重量調整，
直至最大可接受抬舉重量為受試者尋獲並確
認(重量調整時間至少20分鐘)。
(8)MAWL 抬舉作業：受試者進行MAWL 抬
舉作業(10 分鐘)，同時記錄受試者之生理反
應。
（三）左/右手握力差異
左/右手握力差異在不同抬舉方式與不同
握把角度在統計上並不顯著，但實驗前後的左
/右手握力仍有差異。小心輕放抬舉作業的實
驗前後握力衰減程度較高，可能是因為對準而
引起的握力衰降情形。
握把角度由30˚增加至90˚，握力差異值先
增加後降低；角度為30˚~60˚，握力差隨握把
角度增加而提升，角度超過60˚以後，握力差
隨角度增加而變小(圖 9)，此變化情形與
MAWL 相似，由此可知握把角度對△HR 的
效應顯然受MAWL的變化影響。此外研究結
果也指出左手握力差衰降情形較為明顯，可能
是因為受試者皆為右手慣用者，所以在抬舉作
業後，左手感到疲勞程度較高(圖10)。
抬舉頻率增加，左手握力衰降較右手明顯
(圖11)，但握力衰降值在統計上並沒有顯著差
異(P>0.05)。
五、結論與建議
根據本研究之實驗結果，可以獲得以下的
結論：
1. 小心輕放或一般抬舉適用的握把角度相
同。
2. 抬舉高度範圍為膝高至肩高，握把角度
建議為30˚~60˚。
3. 抬舉頻率越大所造成的生理負荷越大，
因此所能承受的重量越低。
4. 握把角度愈趨近極端(90˚)時，所能承受
的重量越小，所造成的生理負荷也越重。
5. 小心輕放抬舉作業的實驗前後握力差較
高，會造成非慣用手握力負荷衰退情形較為明
顯。

Apparatus and Materials
A steel-frame box with two cylindrical handle was
mounted a load cell (Sensotec, Model 81, 250kg; compression/
tension) under the bottom of the box to measure the lifting
strength. Each cylindrical handle composed of six pieces of
metal load cells was design to measure the stress on the handle
while the subject deforming lifting task (Figure 1 ). To provide
different handle angles for this experiment, an adjusta ble
device was fabricated, as shown in Figure 2. In addition, a
Heart Rate Monitor (ADInstruments, MLT1020EC IR
Plethysmograph - Ear Clip) was applied to measure work
pulses.
Experimental Procedure
The experimenter explained the aims and procedures to a ll
the subjects and scheduled several lifting practices for each
subject, executed before the formal measurements, to
familiarize with how to perform the lifting task. For the formal
measurements, subjects were asked to face a given box with
handles and to align their toes to a line marked on the floor,
use the power grip to grasp handles, and then lift the box as
great as possible to reach MVC and to maintain about six
seconds. To avoid accumulated fatigue, several pauses were
scheduled in the experimental  plan according to the findings of
Peterfsky and Lind (1980), who found that the EMG recovered
within 4-5 minutes after a contraction. A three -minute break
was allowed between two consecutive repetitions. A
ten-minute break was scheduled between two experi mental
conditions. At noon, a one-hour pause was given.
RESULTS
Maximal Voluntary Contraction (MVC)
According to the ANOVA results, the subject effect and
lifting height effect are significant on all responses (P<0.0001),
the main effect of handle angle and the lifting height*handle
angle interaction affect all of the responses (P<0.0001), but not
the ΔHR (P>0.05). A further statistical test, Duncan’s multiple
range test, was conducted to discriminate the mutual
differences for the significant effects, MVC (P<0.05).
Two main effects, lifting height and handle angle, and
lifting height*handle angle interaction are significant on MVC.
The MVC at eye height (17.2 kg) is significantly lower than
those at floor and elbow height (31.0 and 28.9 kg) (Figure 3).
For handle angle effect, the greatest MVC (28.2 kg) appears at
90°, the smallest MVC (22.8 kg) occurs at 0°. Based on the
Duncan multiple range test result, the MVC of 0° is
significantly lower than those of 45°and 90° ( Figure 4).
The interaction of lifting height*handle angle on MVC is
shown in Figure 5. The MVCs of the two above elbow heights
(elbow and eye height) increase with the increase in handle
angle. Of particular interest is the fact that the MVC of 90° at
floor is markedly smaller than those o f 0° and 45°.
Stress on Handles
The higher stress occurs on right handle than on left handle.
The average stress on left handle is about 90% of on right
handle. Significant effects on the stress on left/right handle
include lifting height, handle angle a nd lifting height*handle
angle. The lifting height effect on the stress on the handles is
similar to on the MVC. The stress on handles at eye height is
significantly lower than those at floor and elbow  (Figure 6).
The stress increases with the increasing h andle angle (Figure
7).
The lifting height*handle angle interaction is plotted in
Figure 8. It is just like the MVC. Only at floor, the stress on
90° handle is significantly lower than those on 45°and 0°. For
the other lifting height, elbow and eye height , the stress
increases with the increasing handle angle. Whether left or
right handle, the lifting height*handle angle interaction on the
handles is similar to on MVC.
ΔHR (work pulse)
The only significant effect on ΔHR is lifting height. The
further test, Duncan multiple ranges test, reveals that ΔHR at
floor (21.2 beats/min) is significantly lower than those at
elbow and eye height (34.5 and 34.2 beats/min respectively)
(Figure 9).
DISCUSSION
The MVC is smaller as the lifting height is above elbow
level. The highest MVC is almost 2 folds of the lowest MVC.
Such result indicates that the adequate initial height of lifting
tasks should not set above elbow level. However, a lower
lifting height induces higher stress on the lumbar disc ( Lee,
2004), and increases the risk of low back pain. Thus, the elbow
height is recommended to ease the low back strain and to
perform lifting task with their maximal lifting strength (MVC).
The small handle angle, 0°, results in the small MVC
compared with the large handle  angles, 45°and 90°. There is
no significant difference in MVC between 45°and 90°. The
aforementioned result is not in agreement with the findings of
Wang et al. (2000), who found that a 30° handle angle induced
significant higher lifting capacity than tho se of handle
angles,0°, 15° 45°, 60°, and 75°. This is because that this study
included three different lifting heights. The result of the
present study indicates that the great handle angles (45°and
90°) seems to be more adequate to recommend for lifting tasks
which the lifting height varies dramatically.
For the handle angle*lifting height interaction, the MVCs
for floor height are greater than those for elbow and eye height.
In addition, the elbow height and eye height result in the
increase in MVC as the handle angle increase. These results
provide evidence that the strength of upper limbs limit the
lifting capacity as the lifting height above elbow height. On the
other hand, the exertion capacity of trunk and lower limbs are
the primary factors affecting the lifting capacity, and the upper
limbs are the minor factors. He highest MVC appears at 90°
for elbow height and eye height, followed by a 45° , since a
great handle angle keeps the joint of upper limbs, especially
the wrists, near neutral posture whi le performing lifting task at
