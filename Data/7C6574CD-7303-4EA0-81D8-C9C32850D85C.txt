This report covers six areas that we have 
worked on for this project: 1. Realization of 
continuous wave 1.32 µm quantum dot (QD) 
lasers with low threshold current and high output 
power. 2. 1.5 µm InAs/InGaAs QDs for long 
wavelength lasers have been investigated. The 
luminescence efficiency of InAs QDs at 1.5 µm 
is greatly improved by an AlGaAs carrier 
blocking layer 3. The enhancement of thermal 
stability and emission intensity of InAs/GaAs 
QDs. InGaAsSb and InAlAsSb are used as the 
overgrown layers of InAs QDs. 4. Growth of 
low-density QDs for single photon sources 5. 
Realization of high-efficiency single photon 
source based on a photonic crystal microcavity. 6. 
Realization of single photon emission from an 
InGaAs quantum dot precisely positioned on a 
nano-plane
