因此，除了將量子點紅外線偵測器結構最佳化後作為正面入射吸收之量子紅外線偵測器
外，新型元件結構的開發對於徹底解決此一問題有極迫切的需要，因此在量子井結構較易於
成長的前提下，若能將量子井結構由原本的垂直基板表面改變為平行基板表面，那麼此偵測
器便能由不吸收正面入射光轉而為僅吸收正面入射光，在先前其他研究團隊的研究結果指
出，在偏角度的砷化鎵基板上成長快速切換之 GaAs/AlAs 超晶格結構 [J. Cryst. Growth 80, 
425 (1987)]，由於半導體在平行基板之成長速度遠較垂直基板之成長速度為快 (ten times 
faster)，因此在快速切換垂直覆蓋 0.5 原子層半導體材料的情況下，此超晶格之電子波函數
侷限方向將由原本的垂直基板平面轉而成為平行基板方向，其結構圖如圖二之

I. INTRODUCTION 
 
Due to their pronounced modifications 
for optical and electrical characteristics, 
self-assembled semiconductor quantum dots 
(QDs) grown on a lattice mismatched 
substrate have attracted much attention in 
recent years [1-6]. Among all the reports, the 
fabrication of InGaAs QDs grown on GaAs 
substrates by molecular beam epitaxy (MBE) 
has been extensively studied [7-9]. For the 
InAs/GaAs QD structures, due to the 6.7 % 
lattice mismatch between InAs and GaAs 
materials, a transition from two-dimensional 
(2-D) to three-dimensional (3-D) growth 
would be observed with InAs coverage 
exceeds critical thickness ~ 1.75 ML. With 
additional Ga adatoms added in the QD 
region, increasing critical thicknesses and 
decreasing QD optical quality and uniformity 
are usually observed for the structures. 
 
In the report, the surface morphologies 
and optical characteristics of InGaAs/GaAs 
with different In compositions prepared by 
molecular beam epitaxy (MBE) are 
investigated. Uniform QD and good optical 
characteristics are observed for all the 
samples. we report the surface morphology 
and optical characteristics of dots formed 
under different indium composition. 
All samples discussed in this report are 
grown on (100) oriented semi-insulating 
GaAs substrate using a solid-source Riber 
P32 MBE system. Three samples are 
prepared under As4 flux with In composition 
100, 75 and 50 % denoted as samples A, B 
and C, respectively. The sample structures 
are shown in Table I. As shown in the table, 
for the measurements of photoluminescence 
(PL) and atomic force microscopy (AFM) 
images, samples with two InGaAs QD 
structures are prepared. One quantum-dot 
layer grown in GaAs matrix is sandwiched 
between two additional AlAs carrier 
confinement layers for PL measurement. The 
other quantum-dot layer is grown on the 
wafer surface under the same conditions for 
AFM measurement. For PL measurement, a 
532 nm green laser is used as the pumping

III. CONCLUSIONS 
 
The surface morphologies and optical 
characteristics of InGaAs/GaAs quantum-dot 
(QD) structures prepared by molecular beam 
epitaxy are investigated in this report. 
Uniform QDs are obtained for samples with 
different In compositions. Strong state filling 
effect observed from PL spectra has proved 
the high optical qualities of all the 
InGaAs/GaAs QD samples.

increasing QD doping density, the response

remain unchanged at 0.4 under different 
applied voltages. The results suggest that 
besides the intrinsic QD normal incident 
absorption characteristics, the doping density 
at the QD region plays another important 
role for the property. For a QDIP with higher 
doping density at the QD region, the 
response ratio for s- and p- polarized lights 
would be reduced to close the performances 
of QWIPs. 
To explain the phenomenon, the response 
ratio of s/p –polarized lights under positive 
applied voltages for sample C are shown in 
Fig. 2. As shown in the figure, decreasing 
response ratio of s/p –polarized lights with 
increasing applied voltage is observed. A 
minimum value 0.34 is observed at 2.2 V. 
For applied voltage higher than 2.2 V, the 
ratio will increase with increasing voltages. 
Based on the observations, a model is 
purposed. Due to the fact that only parts of 
the photo-excited electrons are pulled out of 
the QDs under low applied voltage, it is 
assumed that there are two portions of 
photocurrents: (1) photocurrent composed of 
photo-excited electrons pulled out of the 
QDs with an external voltage and (2) 
induced photocurrent of photo-excited 
electrons remained in the QDs by the 
accelerated electrons. The two parts of the 
photocurrent are proportional to 
photo-excited electron number N and N2, 
respectively. And due to the intrinsic 
absorption ratio for s- and p- polarized lights 
is smaller than one. The results would be a 
decreasing response ratio with increasing 
voltage as observed in the low-voltage 
region at Fig. 2. For QDIPs under 
high-applied voltages, only few of the 
photo-excited electrons remained in the QDs. 
Therefore, the influence for the second part 
of the photocurrent will decrease, which lead 
to a critical voltage with minimum response 
ratio.
