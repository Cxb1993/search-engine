 2
結構考量結構-基礎-土壤互制下承受地震波之反應分析 
 
摘 要 
 
結構、基礎、週遭土壤及地震波空間效應之土壤結構互制分析為地震工程、科技
廠房微振分析及減振對策、交通振動波傳分析等方面急需要且必要之研究工作，
對於此一問題，本研究採用將結構-基礎-土壤整體系統拆解為內域以有限元素分
析及外域以級數解分析之混合法。對於外域部份，採用級數解來展開外域土層之
散射場，並使用動態 Lamb 奇異解為級數之基函數(basis function)，及利用快速陡
降積分法來進行數值積分，此方法之優點在於其可以採用較傳統邊界積分法(元
素法)少之級數即可達到所要求之準度，另由於配合快速陡降積分法，在計算速
度上相當快速，較合適於進行參數分析。對於內域部份，則採用傳統有限元素法
來模擬多變化性之結構物、基礎及內域土層，並利用所提之變分原理進行內外域
勁度矩陣之結合，使整體勁度矩陣為對稱形式而優於其他混合法。 
 
 
 4
第一章 概論 
由於結構物之受震反應及耐震性能會受到基礎與土壤之變形的影響而不
同，所以於分析中須將基礎與土壤之受震變形效應正確的加以考慮才可得到上部
結構之受震反應。對於一般之土壤-基礎-結構互制分析方式，常常將基礎本身視
為一較剛性結構體，土壤視為等值彈簧，並採用單重輸入(uniform input)地震之
方式進行分析，此種分析方式對於基礎長度與寬度尺寸較小之情況下為相對可行
的，其地震波空間效應採用 5%意外偏心應該即可含蓋，但對於具較大基礎尺寸
之建築物或基礎寬深比較大之情況下此種分析方式並不一定為完全正確的，因為
基礎平面尺寸過大時，其長度與地震波波長比例並不是一個相當小而可以忽略之
值，所以必須要考慮到基礎兩端地震波輸入之時間差(既地震波之空間分佈)，此
與長跨度橋梁分析時需考慮多重輸入(multiple input)效應之原理相同，另若基礎
之長度與深度比(或長度與寬度比)值過大時，則其於地震表面波(surface wave)
作用下之行為類似於一細長梁之行為，可能會引起不小之撓曲變形，亦因此而造
成與其銜接柱之額外變形轉角，此些行為皆非現有常被採用之分析方式所能準確
預估到的行為。 
在圖 1.1 中為台北縣某集合住宅，此結構為地上 5 棟 37 層住宅，地下 9 層。
此 5 棟結構地上層為獨立的，但地下室則為共用結構，其地下室尺寸為長 135 
m、寬 29m 及深 27m，如此之結構系統，其基礎長向兩端所受之地震波必然有
所不同，在我國現行之耐震設計規範中，雖然有規定需要考慮 5%建物尺寸之意
外偏心扭矩之規定，此 5%偏心扭矩若以地上層各別結構來考量的話，其相當於
將地上 5 棟結構分開固接於地面之行為，並無法完全反應基礎因地震波空間分佈
所造成之旋轉(torsional component)與翻轉(rocking component)的效應，因為以一
般建物結構來看，上部結構相較於基礎而言仍為相當之柔性，所以基礎整體繞垂
直軸的旋轉與繞水平軸的翻轉，或基礎(地下室)本身之撓曲變形可能會擺動上部
結構，造成上部結構慣性力於大小及分佈上之明顯不同，所以非採用 5%偏心扭
矩所可以含蓋的。於此所舉之案例下，5%偏心扭矩僅能視為考慮建物施作及設
計階段時，真實質心與設計質心位置偏移所造成之扭矩，而不能考量到地震波空
間變異性之效應。

「美國國家地震工程模擬實驗整合網路計畫(Network for Earthquake Engineering

進方案之一。NEES 計畫雖然其重點著眼於網路與先進儀器設備結合及研究設備
之提昇，但其在土壤-基礎-結構互制數值模型方面，仍被視為試驗所需要之基礎
研究。對於地震工程或結構系統之研究與發展的進程，每一研發成果均必須經過
理論推導、數值模擬、實驗驗證及實體結構成效評估，以至歸納整合為設計規範，
提供工程實務界之廣泛應用。其中理論推導與數值模擬之研究環境及所需設備，
較為簡單且經濟；雖然簡易之土壤-基礎-結構互制模型的探討可以採用試驗方式
來進行，但對於複雜土壤-基礎-結構互制模型之分析則以計算模擬方式來進行較
適當，且我們現在對於土壤-基礎-結構三者互制之行為所了解的並不夠多，所以
唯有先透過數值模擬方式來對此一複雜物理問題進行了解，發現問題之主要控制
因素及參數為何，才了解到若要進行簡易土壤-基礎-結構互制模型的試驗要如何
規劃及試驗中重要的參數為那些，否則一昧的進行試驗，將造成時間及經費上之
嚴重耗費。 
對於如何進行模擬包括結構、基礎、週遭土壤及地震波空間效應之土壤結構
互制分析為地震工程、科技廠房微振分析及減振、交通振動波傳分析等方面急需
要且必要之研究工作，所以本計畫之研究為相當具有前瞻性及重要之研究工作。
本研究方向之最主要目的在於整合及發展電腦模擬技術以解決此一地震工程上

第二章 研究方法 
 
2.1 前言 
本研究所分析問題之物理模型概念圖則如圖2.1中所示。由於本計畫所進行
之結構-基礎-土壤互制系統受震行為分析需要考量到地震波輸入之時間差，因此
採用混合模式法(hybrid method)來進行此類問題的分析，將問題拆解為內域(結
構、基礎及部份近域土壤)以有限元分析及外域(遠域土壤)以級數解展開分析之混
合法。 
對於外域部份，採用級數解來展開外域土層之散射場，即將外域散射場之位
移與應力場以級數來展開，所使用之級數為以動態Lamb奇異解及其高階微分項
所產生之基函數(basis function)，對於內域部份(基礎及部份近域土層)，則採用有
限元素法來模擬多變化性之結構、基礎及其附近土壤，使能方便進行分析並應用
於基礎-土壤系統受震分析、地下結構耐震分析、不規則地形受震反應及車輛振
動傳播等各方面工程實務問題上。

第三章 外域場之奇異解與數值結果 
 
3.1 前言 
如第二章所述，對於外域部份，採用級數解來展開外域土層之散射場，即將
外域散射場之位移與應力場以級數來展開，所使用之級數為以動態 Lamb 奇異解
及其高階微分項所產生之基函數(basis function)，此方式可以有效且準確的模擬
外域散射場的行為，另對於採用 Lamb 奇異解及其高階微分項為基函數來建構外
域散射場級數所產生之水平波數域數值積分問題，則採用修改之快速陡降積分法
來加以處理。上述外域部份之分析方法的優點在於其可以採用較傳統邊界積分法
(元素法)少之級數即可達到所要求之準度，另由於其配合快速陡降積分法，在計
算速度上相當快速，其速度可較一般適應性數值積分方法快數倍之多，所以為一
相當優良的分析方法。對於內域部份(結構、基礎及部份近域土層)，則採用有限
元素法來模擬多變化性之結構、基礎及其附近土壤，使能方便進行分析，對於內
域與外域之介面，則利用內外域勁度矩陣之結合，使整體勁度矩陣為對稱形式而
優於其他混合法。此一部份研究主要的成果為發展出能進行二維與三維分析程
式，使能應用於結構-基礎-土壤系統受震分析、地下結構耐震分析、不規則地形
受震反應及車輛振動傳播等各方面工程實務問題上。

ABSTRACT 
This paper first studies the transition matrix formulation for the analysis of responses of an elastic half- 
space with a buried tunnel subjected to obliquely incident waves.  The basis functions are constructed 
using the moving P-, SV-, and SH-wave source potentials and to represent the scattered and refracted 
wave fields in series forms.  The associated T-matrix expression of elastic inclusion is derived using 
Betti’s third identity.  Second, this study proposes a technique for calculating the integral representation 
of basis functions in the wave-number domain using the method of steepest descent.  Finally, typical 
numerical results obtained under incident plane waves are presented for verification. 
Keywords : T-matrix, Elastic wave, Steepest descent, Tunnel. 
1.  INTRODUCTION 
The transition matrix (T-matrix) theory for the scat-
tering of acoustic waves by a single scatterer in an infi-
nite medium was first proposed by Waterman [1,2].  
Initially, the theory was derived using Helmholtz inte-
gral formula.  Pao [3,4] has shown that the derivation 
can be greatly simplified if Green’s identity is applied to 
the acoustic wave problem and Betti’s third identity is 
applied to the elastic wave problem.  Since then, there 
have been numerous publications on the applications 
and extensions to electromagnetic and elastic wave 
propagation problems.  Detailed references for elec-
tromagnetic and elastic wave propagation problems can 
be found in (Varatharajula and Pao [5]; and Varadan and 
Varadan [6]).  Yeh and Pao [7] have proposed the T- 
matrix formulation for the scattering of acoustic waves 
by a multiple layered inclusion in an infinity medium.  
Chai, et al. [8] have successfully employed the cylin-
drical wave functions as basis functions for SH-wave 
scattering in a two-dimensional alluvial valley to study 
its resonant phenomenon according to the property of 
the T-matrix.  It should be noted that only Neuman’s 
boundary condition can be satisfied by the angular part 
of the cylindrical wave function.  
As for research on dynamic responses of a buried 
layered inclusion (tunnel or pipeline) in an elastic semi- 
infinity medium subjected to incident plane waves, Lee 
and Trifunac [9] have studied the scattering of two-  
dimensional anti-plane problem using the method of 
series expansion.  The cylindrical pipeline subjected to 
incident SH-waves was also analyzed and discussed.  
El-Akily and Datta [10] have used the Fl ugge shell 
theory to simplify the behavior of the cylindrical pipe-
line, and expand the scattering fields of the exterior re-
gion by circular cylindrical wave functions for their 
analyses.  Wong et al. [11] combined the finite element 
method with wave function expansion to analyze the 
responses of a buried pipeline with non-circular cross- 
sections.  Luco and de Barros [12] used the indirect 
boundary integral method to construct the scattering 
field and combined it with the Donell shell theory to 
study the responses of a cylindrical pipeline subjected to 
obliquely incident plane waves.  Yeh et al. [13] have 
recently proposed a hybrid method for analyzing the 
dynamics responses of shells buried in an elastic half- 
plane.  Recently, Chen et al. [14-16] used the boundary 
integral method with degenerate kernel of fundamental 
solutions to transform the improper boundary integrals 
to a series sum for study the exterior radiation and scat-
tering problems with circular boundaries. 
In this paper, Yeh and Pao’s [7] concept is extended 
to analyze the scattering of elastic waves by a lined

NUMERICAL RESULTS  
  For the three-dimensional problems, the current approach is 
applied to study the waves scattered from a semi-spherical 
basin. For all cases studied, the Poisson's ratios for both the 
surrounding half-plane and alluvium are taken to be 1/3, and 
the ratio of the density and shear modulus were taken to be 
5.1=oρρ  and 0.6=oµµ , respectively. Since the semi- 
spherical basin is symmetric about the z-axis, the direction of 
x-axis can be defined to be coincident with the projection of 
incident waves on the ground surface for convenience. The 
numerical results of the amplitudes of the surface 
displacement corresponding to the incident P-, SV- and 
Rayleigh waves with excitation frequency sca πωη = =0.5 
are illustrated in Figs. 4, 5 and 7, respectively, where a  is the 
radius of the semi-circular alluvium. From these figures, the 
reasonable symmetry properties of the components of ground 
displacements can be found to be in accordance with the 
specified incident waves. Furthermore, the continuity 
conditions between the exterior and interior fields can be 
found to be satisfied at the boundary of the semi-spherical 
basin. Hence, we believe that the proposed T-matrix method 
can be applied successfully to the three-dimensional scattering 
problems.  
 
 
CONCLUSION 
On the basis of the Betti's identity in the theory of elasticity, 
we have shown that the basis functions of elastic wave in an 
elastic half-space satisfy the orthogonality conditions. The 
transition matrix, which relates the unknown scattering 
coefficients to the free field coefficients, can be derived 
directly by the orthogonality conditions as well as the 
continuity conditions at the interface between the surface 
obstacle and the surrounding half-space. The basis functions 
and their regular parts are constructed in a systematic manner 
for three-dimensional problems, and their properties are 
discussed thoroughly. Based on a linear transformation, a new 
set of orthogonal basis functions has been constructed. Based 
on the new orthogonal basis functions, the unitary and 
symmetric properties of the associated scattering matrix are 
shown by applying the conservation of energy and time 
reversal requirement. The symmetry property of the associated 
T-matrix is further shown to resemble the reciprocity relation. 
Some results for wave scattering in an elastic half-space were 
calculated. Although only a few numerical results are 
presented here, applications of the present method to wave 
scattering in multiple dipping layers, embedded cavity and 
other problems are also possible. In addition to the elastic 
waves, it should be noted that the proposed T-matrix method 
could also be extended to the coupled field problems, such as 
the thermoelasticity and poroelasticity.
