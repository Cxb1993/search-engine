 Patch Antenna with Reconfigurable Polarization 
Abstract  —  In this paper, polarization reconfigurable antenna is proposed through 
voltage controlling. The studied antenna comprising of a rectangular patch, a varactor, a 
power divider circuitry, and a finite ground plane is designed for Global Position System 
(GPS) operation. The patch is simultaneously coupling fed by two T-strip along the vertical 
edge and horizontal edge. The two feeding mechanism are the same except for a varactor 
which is embedded into one of them. An antenna with triple-polarization diversity, 
including one linear polarization, one elliptical polarization, and one circular polarization, 
is developed from the structure, and only one varactor is required. Details of the antenna 
design are shown, and the simulated and measured results for the constructed prototype are 
also presented and discussed. 
Index Terms —patch antenna, reconfigurable polarization, power divider, circular 
polarization. 
I. INTRODUCTION 
For the rapidly growing wireless communication systems, microstrip antennas have been 
widely developed owing to their attractive features of low profile, light weight and easy 
integration with RF circuitry [1]. Generally, antenna operation in linear polarization (LP) is 
suitable for land-mobile communication.  
In addition, a circular polarization (CP) antenna is suitable for some mobile wireless 
communications because of its insensitivity to signal orientations and polarization. To achieve CP 
radiation with single feed, an L-strip and a cross-slot can provide orthogonal proximity feeding. 
Usually, the feeding module should level at the radiating element is a considered problem. To 
achieve CP radiation with single feed, an attached portion, such as cross-slot and L-strip can 
provide orthogonal proximity feeding. 
 Another key factor of 90 phase difference can be obtained by adapting strip/slot length. In 
addition, sequential quadrature feed network introduced by branched power dividers [2-3] and an 
adapting perturbation in radiating element [4-5] can work in CP sense. Moreover, a polarization 
reconfigurable antenna has received considerable attention because it can reduce the required 
antenna amount coped with the unknown relative positions of transmitter/receiver. To electrically 
switch in CP and LP, RF MEMS switch [6] and PIN diodes [7] were employed for single feed.  
In this paper, an antenna with triple-polarization diversity, including one linear polarization, one 
elliptical polarization (EP), and one circular polarization, is developed from the conventional 
patch antenna, and only one voltage-controlled varactor is required.

As Figure 1 shown, one diagonal linear polarizations are achieved as the varactor is forward 
biased. In this case, no difference between two branched feeding mechanisms except for the 
orthogonal excitation to the patch. However, such function is altered as a reverse bias considered. 
The reversely biased varactor conducts a capacitance varied with DC bias. Also, the phase 
difference between two feed strips caused by the embedded capacitance is adjustable. Therefore, 
CP and EP operations are achieved as the phase difference is about 90 and other degree, 
respectively.

IV. CONCLUSIONS 
A patch antenna with reconfigurable polarization has been developed. The prototypes for LP, 
CP, and EP switching directly proceeded by a varactor with a DC bias is demonstrated. Moreover, 
a prototype whose polarization can be switched among LP and CP modes has successfully 
implemented and studied. Although inherent path loss caused by nonidentity feeding mechanism 
imperfects the CP performance, simple compensating strategy can be applied to resolve the 
shortcoming. The easy polarization sense switching indicates that the proposed antenna has 
highlighted potential for wireless systems requiring polarization diversity.

and two degenerated modes can be achieved by conducting

usually occurred for CP sense. The effect of capacitance 
location is also studied. Figure 4 shows the measured S11 in 
frequency with varied d. Experimental results indicate that 
the capacitance location affects equivalent input reactance 
and then impedance matching between antenna and input.

IV. CONCLUSIONS 
A patch antenna with reconfigurable polarization has been 
developed. The prototypes for LP, CP, and EP switching 
directly proceeded by a varactor with a DC bias is 
demonstrated. Moreover, a prototype whose polarization can 
be switched among LP and CP modes has successfully 
implemented and studied. Although inherent path loss caused 
by nonidentity feeding mechanism imperfects the CP 
performance, simple compensating strategy can be applied to 
resolve the shortcoming. The easy polarization sense 
switching indicates that the proposed antenna has highlighted 
potential for wireless systems requiring polarization diversity.
