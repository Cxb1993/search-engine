波浪與系列彈性透水潛堤互相作用之理論解析
計畫編號：NSC 95-2221-E-432-008
執行期限：95年 8月 1日至 96年 7月 31日
主持人：藍元志 興國管理學院資訊科學學系 助理教授
中文摘要
本文主要針對系列彈性透水潛堤結構與
波浪互相作用之問題，提出一理論解析模
式。波浪運動採用線性波浪理論；彈性透水
結構則假設為均質、等向、具高透水性之彈
性介質。利用 Biot 理論之動量方程式以及
Verruijt 提出之保存方程式，並引用 Sollitt
and Cross(1972)對於透水性介質波浪運動的
描述，加入紊流摩擦阻力效應，在週期性運
動的假設下，推導出孔隙水壓力之四階微分
方程式以及彈性體位移之六階微分方程式，
將波浪運動與系列彈性透水潛堤運動聯立求
解，配合潛堤與流體交界面處之壓力與運動
速度連續之邊界條件，據此波浪運動以及彈
性透水潛堤之解析解可以求出。在模式驗證
上，則與歐等(2004)之雙列不透水潛堤、丁等
(2002)之雙列透水潛堤之試驗結果比較，均得
到合理的結果。文中再探討波浪通過雙列彈
性透水潛堤，在不同柔軟性及滲透效應，對
於波浪反射與透過的影響。
關鍵詞：波浪、彈性透水結構、系列潛堤、
互相作用
Abstract
An analytical model is developed for the
problem of periodic waves interaction with a
series of poro-elastic submerged structures. The
fluid motion is described by the linear wave
theory. The poro-elastic structures are assumed
to be homogeneous, isotropic, and can be
elastic. The poro-elastic medium theory is
redeveloped by means of that the momentum
equation of Biot’s theory is modified for 
adopting the turbulent fricative effect presented
by Sollitt and Cross (1972). Based on the
modified momentum equation and the storage
equation presented by Verruijt, the governing
equations of sixth-order differential equations
for the displacements of the porous medium,
and a forth-order differential equation for the
fluid pressure are derived. Continuity of
dynamic pressure and flow flux at the
interfacial structures surface are used to solve
the problem. General solution forms for the
wave motion and the poro-elastic submerged
structures are derived, and the coefficients in
the solutions can be determined from the
boundary conditions. The present analytic
solutions compare favorably well with
experimental results by Ou et al.(2004) for two
impermeable submerged structures, and Ting et
al.(2002) for two rigid permeable submerged
structures. Using the present analytic solution,
effects of elasticity and permeability of two
poro-elastic submerged structures on wave
reflection and transmission are studied.
Key Word：waves; poro-elastic structures; a
series of submerged structures;
interaction
