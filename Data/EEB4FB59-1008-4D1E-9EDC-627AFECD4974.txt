transited anchor, Z/D>17.4 is belong to a deep 
anchor. The coefficient of friction K decreases with 
increases in both fixed length and overburden depth 
of an anchor in gravel formation, but increases a 
little bit with an increase in excavating depth. The 
load transfers from the top to the bottom within the 
anchor body； a rectangular shaped distribution of 
friction stress along fixed end can be found except 
the top and bottom of fixed end. As the horizontal 
spacing of a dual anchors Sh=13D, the efficiency of 
dual anchors is more than 90%, and the behavior of 
dual anchors behave like two separate anchors.

卵礫石層中永久性抗浮群錨互制行為之研究(2/3) 
 
摘  要 
本研究利用數值分析與現地試驗
的方式探討卵礫石土中單錨與雙錨之
垂直受力行為。數值分析的方式係利
用卵礫石土三軸試驗結果，套用
SHASOVOD 模式(A Continuous Strain 
Hardening/Softening and Volumetric 
Dilation Model)的架構，求取該模式所
需之參數，再利用數值分析軟體
FLAC3D 進行單錨與雙錨之受力行為
分析。並以現場地錨試驗結果驗證數
值分析之正確性，再進行各項地錨影
響參數之研究，以瞭解卵礫石土中垂
直地錨之錨碇行為。 
由研究結果發現，地錨拉拔力隨
著覆土深度、錨碇段長度及地錨錨頭
於開挖面深度的增加而提高。以地錨
面承力係數區分淺層與深層地錨可
得，覆土深徑比 Z/D<8.7 之地錨定義
為淺層地錨，Z/D=8.7~17.4 為轉換地
錨，Z/D>17.4 為深層地錨。地錨摩擦
力之土壓力係數 K 隨著錨碇段長度和
覆土深度之增加而減少，但會隨著地
錨錨頭位於開挖面深度略為增加。承
拉式地錨之荷重傳遞方式係由錨頂向
錨底傳遞，除了錨頂與錨底附近外，
其餘摩擦應力大致呈矩形分佈。對於
深層地錨(Z/D>17.4)而言，當雙錨水平
間距達 13D 時，群錨效益係數皆大於
90%，而漸趨於單錨的行為。 
關鍵詞：卵礫石層、面承力係數、摩
擦力係數、雙錨 
 
ABSTRACT 
To evaluate the uplift behavior of 
single anchor and dual anchors installed 
in gravel formation, a series of triaxial 
test results were utilized to obtain the 
parameters of a continuous strain 
hardening-softening and volume dilation 
model named “SHASOVOD”. In 
conjunction with FLAC3D, a numerical 
study was carried out to analyze the 
behavior of vertically pulled out 
behavior of single anchor and anchor 
groups in gravel formation. A field test 
program was conducted in gravel within 
excavating site to verify the applicability 
of the numerical program. Finally, 
parametric studies on the factors 
affecting the anchorage behavior were 
executed. 
The numerical results indicate that, 
when overburden depth, fixed length 
and excavating depth which is the 
location of anchor head increase, the 
anchorage capacity also increases. Based 
on the development of end resistance 
coefficient Nq, it can be defined as a 
shallow anchor as overburden-diameter 
ratio Z/D<8.7, Z/D=8.7~17.4 of an 
anchor is classified to a transited anchor, 
Z/D>17.4 is belong to a deep anchor. 
The coefficient of friction K decreases 
with increases in both fixed length and 
overburden depth of an anchor in gravel 
formation, but increases a little bit with 
an increase in excavating depth. The 
load transfers from the top to the bottom 
within the anchor body; a rectangular 
shaped distribution of friction stress 
along fixed end can be found except the 
top and bottom of fixed end. As the 
horizontal spacing of a dual anchors 
Sh=13D, the efficiency of dual anchors 
is more than 90%, and the behavior of 
dual anchors behave like two separate 
anchors. 
Keyword: Gravel formation, End 
resistance coefficient, Coefficient of 
friction and Dual anchors

第六章 結論 
本研究利用沈崑章[10]卵礫石土
之三軸試驗結果來求取 SHASOVOD
模式所需之參數，並利用 SHASOVOD
模式，配合 FLAC3D軟體撰寫成數值分
析程式，進行卵礫石層中之垂直地錨
單錨與雙錨之錨碇行為研究，並利用
現場試驗結果驗證數值分析的可行性
後，再探討不同覆土深度、不同錨碇
段長度、地錨錨頭位於不同開挖深度
與不同水平間距條件下現地尺寸地錨
之受力行為。於研究過程中可得下列
結論： 
1. 以沈崑章[10]之卵礫石土三軸試驗
結果，求得 SHASOVOD 模式所需
之參數，配合 FLAC3D 軟體模擬現
地尺寸地錨的荷重-位移關係，具有
一定之準確性。 
2. 由數值分析結果可得知，增加覆土
深度和錨碇段長度皆可提高地錨之
錨碇力，其中又以增加錨碇段長度
對地錨之錨碇力提高效果較佳。 
3. 以相同尺寸地錨而言，錨頭置於地
表與置於開挖深度為 20m 的開挖
面，其地錨之極限荷重相差約
10%~15%。 
4. 以地錨面承力係數區分淺層與深層
地錨可得：覆土深徑比 Z/D<8.7 之
地 錨 地 定 義 為 淺 層 地 錨 ，
Z/D=8.7~17.4 為 轉 換 地 錨 ，
Z/D>17.4 為深層地錨。 
5. 以摩擦應力比較不同錨碇長度的地
錨，結果發現當錨碇段長度≧4m
時，地錨則有較明顯之漸進式降伏
行為發生。 
6. 地錨摩擦力係數 K 隨覆土深度與錨
碇段長度之增加而減少，尤其是增
加錨碇段長度最為明顯，原因在錨
碇段產生漸進式降伏。 
7. 深層地錨於間距達 13.0D(3m)時，群
錨效應係數皆高於 93%以上。 
8. 相同地錨水平間距下，覆土深度愈
深，其群錨效應越小。 
9. 相同地錨水平間距下，錨頭埋置深
度對群錨效應沒有顯著的影響。

Numerical Modeling of Earth Structures: Frictional Anchors in Sand  
Wen-Chi Hu 1,a and  Shih-Tsung Hsu 2,b   
1 162, East Ho-Ping Rd., Sec. 1, Taipei, 10610, Taiwan, R.O.C 
2 168, Jifeng E. Rd., Wufeng District, Taichung, 41349, Taiwan, R.O.C. 
a whu@ntnu.edu.tw, b sthsu@cyut.edu.tw 
Keywords: Frictional anchor; Numerical modeling; Field test; Sand. 
Abstract. This research performed a series of triaxial tests on sandy specimens to obtain the 
parameters needed for a constitutive model. The model is capable to simulate the strain 
hardening/softening and the volumetric dilation of sandy soil during stressing. The model and the 
related parameters were then employed in the commercial software FLAC2D to analyze the uplift 
behavior of various frictional anchors in sandy soil. Analysis results indicate that the friction stress 
along the fixed end of the anchor with a long fixed length exhibits progressive yielding under not 
only for a tension but also for a compression anchor. The progressive yielding behavior could be 
eliminated using a compound anchor because of the upward and downward transfer of load within 
the anchor shaft from the anchorage body. Therefore, a compound anchor can generate a higher 
anchorage capacity of all frictional anchors. 
Introduction 
Vertical anchors are usually employed to tie down basements against ground-water uplift pressure 
or to counterbalance eccentric loads that act on foundation mats. However, the pullout mechanisms 
of full-scale anchors are not well understood. 
The stress-strain behavior of sandy soil typically includes initial strain hardening, followed by 
strain softening; the volume of the sandy soil dilates during shearing. However, strain softening and 
volumetric dilatancy of the soil are frequently ignored in numerical analyses, even though these two 
phenomena markedly influence the behavior of earth structures, such as when an anchor is used in 
sandy soil. To handle these issues, this investigation employs a constitutive model of sandy soils, 
which was proposed by Hsu [1]. The model considers both strain hardening and softening and 
volumetric dilation of sandy soil. In conjunction with the FLAC2D software, a numerical program 
was written to investigate the uplift behavior of anchors in sand.  
Soil Conditions and a Constitutive Model of the Test Soil  
The sandy specimens used in triaxial tests were sampled from Taipei Basin. This soil is classified as 
silty sand, SM. Table 1a summarizes the constitutive model for the sandy soil that was developed by 
Hsu [1]. The model was built up with the reference to the theory of plasticity. Hsu’s investigation [1] 
presented additional information and details of the model. Table 1b presents the parameters that are 
obtained from the results of triaxial test and used in the model. To demonstrate the reliability of this 
constitutive model, the stress-strain behaviors of silty sand with a relative density of 50%, 
determined from the triaxial test, are compared with those calculated using the proposed model. As 
illustrated in Fig. 1, the calculated results are perfectly consistent with the triaxial test results.

several categories according to the load transfer mechanism along the fixed anchor. This research 
concerns the tension anchor, the compression anchor and the compound anchor (Fig. 2a).

Load-displacement relationship of shaft anchors 
The numerical results reveal that the variation in pullout load with anchor displacement clearly 
varies with the type of anchor, when the length of the anchor exceeds a certain threshold. Figure 4a

state. The frictional resistances from the top of the fixed end to 140D were finite. Figure 6b 
demonstrates that the peak friction moves from the bottom to the top of the fixed end of a 
compression anchor. When the maximum load was applied, the peak friction stress was located at 
35D from the bottom of the anchor. Then, the friction stress rapidly declined toward the top of the 
fixed length; no friction stress was perceived from the top of the fixed length to 160D along the 
fixed length. Therefore, although the ultimate loads of the tension and compression anchors do not 
differ by much, the anchorage mechanics of a tension anchor differ from those of compression 
anchor. The friction stress of a tension anchor can easily develop from top to bottom; in contrast, 
the friction stress of a compression anchor develops from bottom to top, but not easily. An 
important conclusion can be drawn: tension and compression anchors with long fixed ends exhibit 
the progressive yield, and so have a non-effective fixed length. In this scenario, the anchorage 
capacity does not increase linearly with fixed length; this phenomenon is similar to that found by 
Barley [3]. However, the effective fixed length in this research is longer than that recommended by 
Barley [3]. 
Figure 7 plots the load transfer and the friction stress along the fixed end of a compound anchor 
of L/D=333. Since the anchorage body (anchorage point) is in the middle of fixed length, load can 
be transferred upward and downward simultaneously. The upper part of the fixed end bears 
compressive force, while the bottom bears tensile force (Fig. 7a). The friction stress reaches its peak 
at the anchorage point before the peak load is reached; when the load reaches its maximum value, 
friction stress at the anchorage point decreases to a residual state; two peak friction stresses exist at 
120D and 210D from the top of the fixed end(Fig. 7b).  
Figure 8 shows the load transfers and friction stresses of tension, compression and compound 
anchors to compare their behaviors under peak load. As displayed in Fig. 8a, the load transfers of 
these three anchors vary substantially; Fig. 8b demonstrates that a compound anchor can generate 
friction resistance than the other anchors. Consequently, a long compound anchor has greater 
anchorage capacity. In Taiwan, the full length of a tension anchor is usually grouted, free end 
thereby provides anchorage capacity, and the tension anchor becomes a compound anchor, with 
higher anchorage capacity. However, the function of the free end is to fix the distance of the fixed 
end.
