中文摘要 
本研究使用非水解性溶膠－凝膠法(non-hydrolytic sol-gel process, NHSG)成功地製
備表面修飾三辛基氧化磷(trioctylphosphine oxide, TOPO)的二氧化鈦(titanium dioxide, 
TiO2)，經鑑定後可確定此 TOPO-TiO2 為 4.9 nm 的銳鈦礦(anatase phase)單晶顆粒，且
TOPO 以共價鍵化學吸附方式在 TiO2 表面。TOPO-TiO2 對 3 種不同親疏水性的環境荷
爾蒙 (endocrine disrupting chemicals, EDCs)包括酚 (phenol， log Kow = 1.6)、雙酚
A(bisphenol A，log Kow = 3.3)和雌激素(estrone，log Kow = 4.5)的吸附行為符合 Langmuir 
等溫吸附，其吸附常數分別為 0.18、3.94 與 12.1，可知 TOPO-TiO2 對於疏水性較高的
EDCs 具有較好的吸附能力。TOPO-TiO2 在 EDCs 溶液中的平均半徑為 215.38-216.84 
nm，略大於吸附前的大小(182.04 nm) ，且半徑變化與吸附量呈正相關。TOPO-TiO2 對
於 5-20 ppm EDCs 均能在 135 分鐘內完成光降解反應，同時，光催化反應後，表面仍有
C-H 與 P=O 的 IR 振動吸收，且 C/P 值在 5.29-6.61 間，與反應前的值相當(C/P= 7.62)，
可知表面 TOPO 在光催化反應中不易受到破壞。TOPO-TiO2 對 phenol、bisphenol A 與
estrone 光降解反應遵循擬一階動力方程式，其本徵反應常數分別為 3.81×10-3、3.67×10-3
及 2.68×10-2 g-m-2-min-1，在相同光催化反應情況下，此反應活性分別為商用光觸媒 P25 
TiO2 的 2、4 及 22 倍，因此表面修飾有機 TOPO 能大幅提高 TiO2對疏水性 EDCs 的催
化效率。

1. 前言 
二氧化鈦(Titanium dioxide, TiO2)為一種無機半導體，其能隙約為 3.0-3.2 eV，可被
波長於 387 nm 以下的紫外光激發生電子-電洞對，進而引發氧化還原的光催化反應，由
於 TiO2 的傳導帶與價帶相對的還原電位分別為-0.2 與 3.0 V，能氧化與還原大多數有機
污染物，且具有高化學穩定性，因此 TiO2 為目前最廣被研究與應用的光觸媒 1-3。現今
對於 TiO2 的研究重點主要著重於如何提高其光催化活性，以及降低能隙，延長激發光
譜的波長，使其能於可見光發下產生光催化反應，策略上可分為三種方式，其一為製備
高比表面積的奈米粉體或多孔結構材，其二為採取摻雜不純物於 TiO2 晶格內的方式，
在晶體結構中導入缺陷，抑制電子-電洞再結合的速率，以提高光量子產率，同時，在
能帶間產生額外的能階，降低能隙，而達到可見光敏感的目的，其三為在表面修飾無機
或有機物質，此方式除了具有第二種方式的優點外，更能提高反應物於表面接觸的機
會，並增進表面電荷轉移，提高光催化活性 4-8。 
表面修飾的物種可分為離子型，如無機金屬陽離子與非金屬陰離子，或含碳的有機
或無機物質，如界面活性劑與奈米碳管。Zhang et al.9利用dextran膠體為模版，將TiO2
鍍膜於有機模版表面後移至N2環境中於600 °C鍛燒3小時後，得到高比表面積的TiO2/C
複合物(334 m2/g)，在對RhB的光催化分解實驗中，此複合物比單純TiO2有更高的光催化
效率。Cho et al.10將染劑RuIIL3與TiO2混合製成染劑敏化TiO2，可使光催化還原CCl4反應
在波長大於420nm可見光下進行，後續有許多研究探討不同染劑的光催化反應，這些染
劑包括porphyrin11、phthalocyanine12、erythrosin B13、thionine14等。Ghosh15添加界面活
性劑如dodecylbenzenesulphonate (DBS)於TiO2系統中，由於DBS能吸附於TiO2表面使其
表面成為疏水性，進而促進有機污染物與TiO2表面的接觸，因此能提昇TiO2的光催化活
性，另外，DBS的磺酸基螯合TiO2在表面形成的缺陷可以捕捉電荷，降低電荷在結合的
機會。Chang和Doong16利用非水解性溶膠-凝膠法製備TiO2奈米晶粒，並於製備同時於
表面以錯合方式將有機物TOPO修飾於TiO2表面，對於RhB的光降解研究顯示，TOPO
修飾的TiO2比商用光觸媒(Degussa P25)的光催化活性高2倍。這些正面的光催化結果，
使表面修飾含碳化合物的研究備受重視。 
環境荷爾蒙(Endocrine Disrupting Chemicals，EDCs)為一種存在於環境中並且會干
擾生物體內分泌系統正常運作(合成、分泌、結合、運輸、新陳代謝等)之化學物質，即
使是極微量的環境荷爾蒙，長時間的接觸也會影響生物的生長、發育、生殖等生命現象，
例如：妨礙體內正常的生理機能、引起乳癌或是睪丸癌等腫瘤疾病、雄性精蟲數目銳減、
婦女不孕、胎兒畸形等嚴重變異 17-19。環境荷爾蒙種類包括有機氯(organochlorine)、磷
苯二甲酸鹽(phthalates)、壬機苯酚(nonylphenol)、雙酚 A(bisphenol)等，一般來自於煉油、
絕緣材料與塑膠製造、製藥、造紙、染料工業的廢水與農藥的使用。目前處理環境荷爾
蒙的方式包括有紫外光直接光降解 20、photo-Fenton 法 21、臭氧分解及光催化分解，其
中光催化反應由於可利用太陽光進行觸媒活化降解、無須提供額外能源、具有永續使用

2.2 TOPO-TiO2 之合成 
本研究以非水解性溶膠-凝膠法製備 TOPO-TiO2 光觸媒，反應步驟為將異丙基氧化
鈦(Ti(OC3H7)4，0.7325 克，2.5 毫莫耳)與四氯化鈦(TiCl4，0.236 克，2.5 毫莫耳)在 80 °C
下加到已被熔化的三辛基氧化磷(TOPO, 5.22 克, 13.5 毫莫耳)中混合均勻，將此溶液加
熱到 150 °C 反應 15 分鐘，爾後於氮氣環境中將此溶液加熱到 400 °C 持續 3 小時，此
時可發現原先金黃色的溶液轉為濃稠的乳白色，反應後將此溶液降溫至 80 °C，再將此
稠狀乳白色液體中加入丙酮，以離心機在轉速 11000rpm 下離心 10 分鐘，用以洗掉多餘
TOPO，此離心步驟重複 3 次，最後將離心完的固體使用熱水將丙酮快速蒸發並且完成
乾燥，即可得到表面修飾 TOPO 的二氧化鈦。 
 
 
2.3 材料鑑定

於室溫下乾燥。為了要暸解材料平均的結晶型態，利用 X 光粉末繞射儀(X-ray powder

3.5 光催化後的觸媒表面特性 
為了解表面有機修飾物在光催化反應後是否會被破壞，本研究對光催化反應後的
TOPO-TiO2 進行 FTIR 及 XPS 分析。圖 10 為光催化反應後 TOPO-TiO2 的 FTIR 圖譜，
由圖中可發現 TOPO-TiO2 在光降解 EDCs 後，在波數 2760-3150 cm-1 與 1064-1174 cm-1 
處仍有 C-H 與 P=O 的振動吸收，因此可知有機碳鏈及磷化物在催化反應後仍存在觸媒
表面。為釐清光催化反應前後觸媒表面化學組成的差異，本研究進而利用 XPS 分析光
催化反應後 TOPO-TiO2 表面 C/P 及 P/Ti 比例，表 4 為光催化降解 EDCs 後 TOPO-TiO2
表面 C/P 及 P/Ti 比例，由結果可知反應前 C/P 為 7.62，此數值小於理論值 24，因此部
份 TOPO 的碳鏈在合成 TOPO-TiO2 時即發生裂解，光催化反應後 C/P 值介於 5.29-6.61，
此數值皆為小於催化反應前的 C/P 值，因此催化反應並未嚴重破壞有機碳鏈，此現象有
利於觸媒重覆使用時維持高催化活性，而 P/Ti 值在反應前為 0.64，顯示 TiO2 表面的 Ti4+
並未全部與 P=O 錯合，原因為 TOPO 的有機碳鏈形成立體障礙，光催化反應後的 P/Ti
值仍介於 0.53-0.64，可知催化反應並不影響 P=O 與 Ti4+間的配位鍵。總而言之，以非
水解性溶膠－凝膠法製備的TOPO-TiO2其表面有機物種在催化反應仍能維持原有狀態。

Abstract 
 
The trioctylphosphine oxide (TOPO) capped ZrxTi1-xO2 anatase nanocrystals (x ~ 0.03) were 
successfully prepared by a non-hydrolytic sol-gel route.  To understand the effect of thermal 
treatment on the microstructures, electronic structures, and surface properties of the TOPO-capped 
ZrxTi1-xO2, the samples calcined at elevated temperatures (250-950 °C) under air atmosphere were 
examined by means of HRTEM, XRD, TG-DSC, XPS, FTIR and UV-vis spectroscopy.  
Calcination converted the surface of the TOPO-capped ZrxTi1-xO2 from hydrophobicity to 
hydrophicility as a result of the decomposition of the surface TOPO to phosphate species.  The 
introduced P5+ and Zr4+ enhanced the stability of the anatase form till 950 °C without any phase 
transformation.  Moreover, the crystallite sizes of the samples were maintained at 10.2-10.8 nm at 
250-750 °C and were slightly increased to 16.1 nm as the calcination temperature rose to 950 °C.  
Consequently, the band gaps of the samples declined from 3.4 to 3.2 eV upon the increasing 
crystallite sizes.  The calcined samples exhibited the highest specific photoactivies toward the 
decoloration of Rhodamine B over the as-prepared samples and commercial TiO2 (Degussa P-25).  
This high photoactivity is mainly due to the phosphated surface formed by thermal-decomposition 
of TOPO during calcination.

3. Results and discussion 
3-1 Thermal analysis.  To investigate the thermal behaviors including thermal decomposition of 
TOPO and changes in microstructures of the TOPO-capped ZrxTi1-xO2 nanorystals during 
calcinations, the TGA/DSC analysis were conducted.  Figure 1 shows the TGA/DSC curves of the 
TOPO-capped ZrxTi1-xO2 nanorystals in air.  The TGA curve demonstrates a continuous weight 
loss was encountered at 25-950 °C, and this process underwent four stages. at elevated temperatures.  
At 25-90 °C, about 2 % weight lost accompanied with endothermic process occurred and it was 
ascribed to the withdrawal of adsorbed water molecules.  Subsequently, about 2 % decrease in the 
weight at 90-310 °C is associated with the pyrolysis of TOPO into carbonaceous species.  In fact, 
significant declined to 2.1-3.0 at 450-950 °C.  This result clearly indicates the TOPO are deformed 
and carbonaceous parts of TOPO are burnout during calcinations.  To further examine the changes 
in the P speciation, the binding energy of P(2p) are determined using XPS.  Figure 3 shows the 
high-resolution scanned P(2p) XPS spectra of the samples calcined at elevated temperatures.  The 
TOPO has the P(2p) peak centered at 132.5 eV.  The binding energy of the P(2p) of the 
as-prepared sample centered at 133.1 eV.  Compared to binding energy of P (2p) of TOPO, the 
chemical shift of the P (2p) of the as-prepared sample to high binding energy reveals the chelating 
of TOPO to the metal centers on the surface.  Further shifts of the P(2p) peak to 133.7-134.0 eV 
were observed when the sampled were cacined at 350-750 °C.  These binding energies are close to 
that of phosphate salt, indicating the phosphated TiO2 are formed after thermal treatment of the 
TOPO-capped ZrxTi1-xO2.  At 950 °C, the P (2p) peak centered at 133.4 eV, implying the P=O 
were regenerated from dehydroxylation at high temperature.  To further identify the changes in the 
surface functional groups, the FTIR analysis is conducted.  Figure 4 shows the IR absorption 
spectra of the TOPO-capped ZrxTi1-xO2 before and after calcinations at various temperatures in air.  
The as-prepared sample exhibited an absorption peak centered at 1065 cm–1 corresponding to the 
stretching absorption of P=O groups which were chelated to the surface Ti4+ centers.  However, 
this absorption peak diminished at 250 °C and disappeared when the samples were calcined at the 
temperatures over 450 °C.  This results reveals that the P=O functional groups are converted into 
Ti-O-P type and incorporated into surface lattice of TiO2 after burn out of the TOPO.  Instead of 
disappearance of P=O absorptions, the absorption peaks at 1640 and 3400 cm-1 were observed over 
450 °C.  These two absorptions correspond to adsorbed water and hydroxyl groups, indicating the 
surface become hydrophilic after calcination.19  At 250 °C, all absorptions of the P=O, adsorbed 
water and surface hydroxyl groups were not significant, indicating that P=O was defunctional and 
alkyl chains of TOPO were decomposed at 250 °C.  Since the new formed carboneous species still 
deposited on the surface, the surface still kept hydrophobility.  This phenomenon is in agreement 
with the observation of weight loss in TGA.

system showed the fastest degradation rate with the rate constant of 0.11 min-1.  However, the 
specific rate constant of P25 TiO2 (3.7×10-3 min-1m-2) was far less than that of the TOPO-capped 
ZrxTi1-xO2 (0.05 min-1m-2), indicating that the surface of TOPO-capped ZrxTi1-xO2 was more active 
than the commercial TiO2.  We attribute the high activities to the existence of hydrophobic TOPO 
which can assist the adsorption of organic compounds toward to the surface of photocatalysts.  
Moreover, the photodegradation of RhB is presumably induced by the radicals diffused from the 
surface of TOPO-capped photocatalysts.  A slight decrease in degradation rate occurred at 250 °C.  
This phenomena suggests that carbonization via calcination either inhibit the adsorption ability of 
the surface of the catalysts toward to RhB or limit the diffusion of radicals to decolorize RhB.  As 
the carbonaceous parts of TOPO-capped ZrxTi1-xO2 were burned out completely at 550 °C, the 
photoactivities of the catalysts increased.  The specific photoactivities of the samples calcined at 
550 °C was higher than that of as-prepared one, indicating the phosphated photocatalysts are more 
effective to photodegradation of polar dye than organic modified ones.  Contrast to the 
photocatalysts calcined below 550 °C, the photocatalysts calcined at 950 °C performed the lowest 
photoactivies and zero-order kinetics.  This result is mainly due to the significant loss of surface 
area at high temperatures.

3. Results and discussion 
Colloidal crystal films were fabricated by various diameters of polystyrene particles at different 
temperatures from 4 to 90 °C. Figure 1 shows the SEM image of the colloidal crystal films prepared 
with various concentrations of surfactant or PS.  Addition of surfactant has different effects on the 
fabrication of ordered colloidal crystals.  Low PS concentration at 0-1 % has little effect on 
affecting the repulsion force between particles, while high PS concentration in the range 3-4% 
hampered the fabrication of ordered crystal films. Highly ordered crystal films can be obtained at 
1-2 CMC, which may well-disperse of PS particles in the solution.  In addition, the polystyrene 
concentration is an important factor influencing the properties and thickness of the colloidal crystals. 
The cross-sectional analysis showed a linear relationship between PS concentrations and the 
number of layers.  A 20- to 70-layer colloidal crystal film can be obtained at PS concentration of 
1-2 wt%.  
The temperature effect was also examined, and a highly ordered photonic crystal film can be 
fabricated at 60 °C.  Figure 2 shows the UV-vis spectra of the colloidal crystals fabricated with 
various temperatures.  As the temperature increased, the assembly of polystyrene particles became 
more ordered but the cracks also increased.  Also, the stop bands shifted to higher wavelength at 
high temperature.
