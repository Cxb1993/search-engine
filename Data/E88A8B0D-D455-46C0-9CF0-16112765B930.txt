Albatross模式，此模式是屬於一種啟發式(heuristic)學習導向的模擬系統，其假設活動者

體決策之原則納入考量。Gliebe & Koppelman(2000)利用「家戶成員效用相加函數
(additive household utility function)」，將家戶成員的互動予以模式化；其假設家戶裡某位
成員的效用，係由「其它成員的效用」及「他和家人一同參加活動的效用」所組成，透
過此模式便可以瞭解「個人活動」與「家戶共同參與活動」之間的背反效果。從既往的
研究可以發現，「個人需求」及「家戶其它成員需求」之交互影響，確實會對工作者之
生活排程造成影響。 
四、 研究方法 
(一) 活動選擇模式 
為了驗證「態度」及「個人社經屬性(socioeconomic attribute)」是否會對時間的運用
及分配造成影響，本研究假設透過某些交通管理策略，可縮短交通時間「一小時」，亦
即，受訪者將多出一小時可以自由使用，但這「一小時」係假設為不可分割。進而，以
此假設情境為基礎，詢問受訪者會將節省下來的交通時間分配在何項活動上，此係利用
陳述性偏好法(stated preference)問卷進行調查。如此一來，「活動時間分配行為」將可透
過「活動選擇模式」表現出來。 
本研究係採用「多項羅吉特模式(multinomial logit model )」來建構活動選擇模式，
替選方案的設定則沿用既有的六項活動分類，分別為：(1)生理照護活動；(2)家務活動；
(3)家庭照護活動；(4)社會化活動；(5)工作活動；(6)休閒娛樂活動。 
解釋變數則包含 3 類變數，分別為：(1)個人屬性變數，如性別、年齡、有無同居人
等；(2)對各活動之優先性評價；(3)對各活動之滿意度評價。在變數的設定方式上，(1)
將設定為方案特定變數(alternative specific variable)；(2)與(3)將嘗試設定為共生變數
(generic variable)或方案特定變數。

(四) 平日活動時間分配模式之建構 
此方法的主要概念係為：在考慮時間和預算的限制下，工作者會透過分配時間到各
種不同活動，使自身獲取最大效用。本研究建構之平日活動時間分配模式如表 10 所示。 
從表 10 可知，不同社經屬性的工作者會有不同的活動時間分配行為。此外，「活動
安排的優先性」的確也會對「活動時間分配」產生顯著的影響，特別是「生理照護活動」、
「工作活動」、「休閒娛樂活動」之優先性會對「活動時間分配」產生正向的影響；而「家
務活動」、「社會化活動」之優先性則是對「活動時間分配」產生負向的影響。同時，不
同活動之活動安排優先性的係數值均不相同，表示活動的優先性亦會產生不同的邊際效
用，此亦得到和「活動選擇模式」一致之結論。 
六、 結論與建議 
隨著理論的發展及模式的開發，近年規劃界與學術界也逐漸瞭解到對「交通/活動行
為」進行更細緻探討的重要性。在此情況下，利用活動研究法（activity-based）或旅程
研究法（tour-based）所建構之「活動時間分配模式」即能顯現出其重要性。緣此，本研
究透過對台南地區家戶平日與週末活動旅運之調查，來瞭解活動主體之活動時間分配行
為，及其對時間分配行為的「主體意識」。透過分析，可以得到以下的結論：

有「契約」的特性，是故工作者必須將其視為優先安排的活動；此也顯示出「工作

Abstract 
Value of time plays a significant role in the evaluation of investment effectiveness of 
transportation plans. Therefore, it is a prerequisite to accurately estimate value of time 
savings to meaningfully evaluate transportation policies. In this study, the focus is on 
weekday activities and associated travel behaviors. The evaluation of individuals’ time 
allocation behavior on weekdays and an activity choice model has been investigated in 
this study for analyzing the preference of individuals’ time allocation. The activity choice 
model specifically takes into account the state of the individual time allotment on weekdays 
and subjective evaluation of time use on weekdays. There results show that : 1) The 
socioeconomic conditions have strong effects on workers’ time allocation. 2) The priority of 
activity time allocation would affect time allocation of workers significantly. 3) Furthermore, 
priority of different activities will produce different marginal utility. Therefore, the priority of 
activity should be considered before establishment of a model of activity time value in the 
future.

2. LITERATURE REVIEW 
Generally, the allocation of workers’ living times is subject to the interactive effects 
between the individual demands and the demands of other household members. In view of 
the influences by individual demands, Yamada et. al., (1999) noted that healthcare and 
work have different effects on the allocation of individual times. To be specific, young 
workers would spend more time on their works; while elder workers would spend more 
time on health care. In his study, he also discovered that, in addition to individual 
properties, the cultural conventions also brought great impacts on the timing of individual 
times. Men in Japan whether employed or unemployed, for example, spend the same time 
on household works, which is caused by Japanese cultural conventions.  
Besides individual demands, the demands of household members also have impacts 
on the allocation of workers’ living times. For instances, in households with both husbands 
and wives working, the task to cart their children to and from school will affect the 
allocation of husbands’ or wives’ times. For this reason, to understand the allocation of 
workers’ living times, it is essential to know first the mutual decision behavior of household 
members and the interactive effects of task allocation. To learn the interactive effects 
amongst household members, Algers et al.(1997) modeled the commuting modes and 
trips of household members using the nested logit model. Wen & Koppelman(1999) also 
used the nested logit model to analyze issues concerning the stopping location choice and 
tour formation choice of households . The studies foresaid more often than not focused on 
the analysis of activity-travel behavior, seldom involving the group decision of household 
members. The Albatross model system established by Arentze & Timmermans(2000) 
belongs to a heuristic simulation system. It assumes that the choice behavior of individuals 
belongs to a continuous decision behavior, based on different rules. Through interaction 
with environment and communication with other persons, individuals can acquire 
experiences and correct the existing rules with the acquired experiences, and form 
different decision behaviors. With this model, we can forecast what types of activities the 
individuals is going to do, when, where, with whom and what commuting mode will be 
used. This model analyzes the activity-travel behavior from multiple aspects, but it does 
not consider the group decision. Gliebe & Koppelman(2000) used the additive household 
utility function of household members to model the interactions among household 
members. In this model, it is assumed that the effectiveness of a household member is

4. ACTIVITY CHOICE MODEL 
According to the above analysis of Section 3, individuals with different 
social-economic attributes will hold different approaches to the same kind of activity and 
such finding is preliminarily demonstrated by foregoing analysis on priority of activity 
arrangement and satisfaction of activity time allocation. Such different attitudes also 
construct different lifestyles that will specifically reflect in utilization and allocation of time 
for daily activities (Prasetyo et al., 2003). 
In order to examine whether “attitude” and “socioeconomic attribute” can affect 
utilization and allocation of time, the study assumed travel time could be reduced by “one 
hour” by implementation of some strategies of transport management; that is interviewees 
would have one more hour to use, at the same time the study assumed the hour couldn‘t 
be divided. Then the study asked interviewees what type of activity they would allocate the 
saved travel time to. In this way, activity time allocation model will be turned into activity 
choice model. As for setting of alternatives, the existing six types of activity were continued 
to be the alternatives, including: (1) physical care activity. (2) homemaking activity. (3) 
family care activity. (4) socialization activity. (5) working activity. (6) pleasure activity. 
Explanatory variables contain three kinds involving: (1) personal attribute variable, such as 
gender, age and marriage etc.. (2) priority rank of each kind of activity. (3) satisfaction
