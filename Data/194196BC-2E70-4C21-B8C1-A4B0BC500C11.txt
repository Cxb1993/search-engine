關鍵成功因素（Key Success Factors, 
KSF）為對應環境中重要的要求條件，此
觀念最早始於經濟學者 Commons（1938）
提出之限制因子（limited Factor）。後來，
美國學者 Barnard(1948)將其用於管理決策
理論上以找尋「策略因子」，隨後也陸續
吸引有許多學者注意而逐漸加以研究
(Rockart, 1979；Porter, 1980；大前, 1985；
Aaker, 1988；黃, 1993；Steele, 2000；Darrow, 
2001；)。 
本計畫先利用模糊層級分析法(Fuzzy 
Analytic Hierarchy Process, Fuzzy AHP)分
析出影響風力發電的種種重要相關因子的
重要性排序找出關鍵因素，以供國內發展
離岸式風力場之參考評估。接著，佐以歐
洲風能協會(EWEA)之量化指標針對「海水
的深度」、「距海岸線的距離」、「風速」、
「環保」、「生態」、「休閒」、「土地
利用」、「合適地點規模大小」，以及台
灣常受影響的因子如「颱風」、「地震」、
「斷層」、「地質」及「飛安」來一起進
行量化指標的計算，進行評估適合設立離
岸風力機的場址。 
  
三、結果與討論 
本研究層級分析之程序架構共分為三
大層級因素，以下分別說明： 
一、第一層因素 
本研究所謂「關鍵成功因素」乃定義
為「所有影響風力發電的種種重要相關因
子以評估台灣離岸風力發電之設置」，並
依此發展出第二及第三層架構。 
二、第二層因素 
歸納相關文獻及專家意見後，第二層
架構分有「氣象與海象」、「地質與地形」、
「位置與規模」及「人為與生態保護」等
四個面向。 
三、第三層因素 
本層級再依四種不同的面向進行其影
響之變數的分析，每層含有四項變數，共
計十六項因素。 
（一）氣象與海象 
1.颱風, 2.雷雨, 3.氣溫, 4.潮汐 
（二）地質與地形 
1.風能, 2.地質, 3.地形, 4.地震 
（三）位置與規模 
1.海水深度, 2.離海岸的距離 
3.風力場發展規模, 4.土地利用 
（四）人為與生態保護 
1.航行活動, 2.飛航安全 
3.生態保護, 4.漁業資源 
一般各要素間權重判斷是否有一致
性，以確定其判斷是否可信，本研究採用
一致性檢定來分析，而一致性檢定的指標

A COMBINED HARMONIC ANALYSIS-NEURAL NETWORK METHODOLOGY FOR 
STORM SURGE PREDICTIONS 
 
 
T.-L. Lee 1, W.-H. Shih 2 and W. -P. Huang3 
 
 
ABSTRACT: To avoid properties losses and reduce risk caused by the typhoon surge, accurate 
prediction of surge deviation is an important task. There are many conventional numerical 
methods and experienced methods for typhoon surge forecasting have been investigated, but it is 
still a complex ocean engineering problem. This paper applied the neural network in forecasting 
the storm surge. The original data of Longdong station at Taiwan under the Aere typhoon invaded 
directly will be verify the present modal. Comparisons with the numerical methods indicate that 
the storm surge and surge deviation can be efficiently predicted using neural network. 
 
 
1. Introduction 
 
Storm surge is caused primarily by high winds pushing on the ocean's surface. The wind causes 
the water to pile up higher than the ordinary sea level. Low pressure at the center of a weather 
system also has a small secondary effect, as can the bathymetry of the body of water. It is this 
combined effect of low pressure and persistent wind over a shallow water body which is the most 
common cause of storm surge flooding problems. Storm surges are particularly damaging when 
they occur at the time of a high tide, combining the effects of the surge and the tide. While high 
impact events will undoubtedly occur in the future, the advent and further improvement of the 
prediction of the storm surge may greatly reduce the loss of lives, and potentially reduce the 
property damage. 
The storm surge models were developed in the 1950s. Hansen (1956) proposed a fluid 
dynamic model to describe the storm surge phenomenon in the North Sea. The National Weather 
Service (NWS) adopted the SPLASH model (Jelesnianski, 1972) for storm surge forecasting. 
However, this model worked well only in regions that are not too close to the shoreline. Holland 
(1980) used for wind forcing to drive storm surge models. The SLOSH model (Jelesnianski et al., 
1992) followed SPLASH and considered wetting and drying processes. Emergency managers use 
this SLOSH data to determine which areas must be evacuated to avoid storm surge consequences. 
Current publications indicate a renewed interest in storm surge modeling (Bode and Hardy, 1997). 
Hsu et al. (1999) developed a storm surge model where analytical expressions of both the

3. Back-propagation neural networks 
 
The primary characteristics of ANNs are ability to learn, distributed memory and parallel 
operation eventually leading to fault tolerance. Thanks to these advantages, ANNs has been 
widely applied in geosciences. Since the principle of ANNs has been well documented in the 
literature (Bishop, 1995), only a brief outlook is given in this section. 
A typical three-layered network with an input layer, a hidden layer and an output layer (see 
Figure 1) is adopted in this study. Each layer consists of several neurons and the layers are 
interconnected by sets of correlation weights. The neurons receive inputs from the initial inputs 
or the interconnections and produce outputs by transformation using an adequate nonlinear 
transfer function. A common transfer function is the sigmoid expressed by f(x)=(1+e-x ) −1, it has 
a characteristics of df/dx = f(x)[1- f(x)]. The training processing of neural network is essentially 
executed through a series of patterns. In the learning process, the interconnection weights are 
adjusted within input and output value.  
The Back-Propagation Network (BPN) is the most representative learning model for the 
artificial neural network. The procedure of the BPN is the error at the output layer propagates 
backward to the input layer through the hidden layer in the network to obtain the final desired 
outputs. The gradient descent method is utilized to calculate the weight of the network and adjust 
the weight of interconnections to minimize the output error. The error function at the output
