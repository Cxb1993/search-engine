Interestingly, the as-deposited Ag10.8Sb63.1Te26.1 was difficult to be crystallized by blue laser pulses, as shown in 
Fig. 2(d). However, the ablations were observed in Ag10.8Sb63.1Te26.1 after irradiated by pulsed lasers with higher 
powers and longer durations. Apparently, the area of outer crystallization region decreased with increasing Ec 
while inner melt-quenched region increased with decreasing Em while increasing the Ag content. When Ec/Em 
reached a critical value, the Ag-doped Sb70Te30 recording film will be difficult to crystallize by a blue laser pulse. 
Fig. 3(a) and (b) show the TEM images of the annealed Sb69.9Te30.1 and Ag10.8Sb63.1Te26.1 recording films after 
irradiated by blue laser pulses of powers ranging from 1 to 12 mW with 1mW step (Y-axis) for the durations 
ranging from 6 to 606ns with 50ns step (X-axis). It was found that the melt-quenched amorphous marks were 
observed in Ag10.8Sb63.1Te26.1, however, the amorphous marks were only observed nearby the ablation region in 
Sb69.9Te30.1. Uhlmann et al. have asserted that for congruently melting glass-forming materials, the crystallization 
velocity at low temperature will be limited by the viscosity of the glass forming liquid. The activation energy for 
crystallization can be interpreted as low temperature viscous flow, while the activation energy for melting could 
be interpreted as the high temperature viscous flow. [7,8] It should have an optimum element-doped concentration 
of the Sb70Te30 recording film to obtain an adequate value of Ec/Em for smoothly reversible switching between 
amorphous and crystalline marks.  
4. Conclusions 
Adding Ag into the fast-growth type Sb70Te30 recording film can increase crystallization and melting 
temperatures, increase activation energy for crystallization, but decrease activation energy for melting. The laser 
powers required to initiate crystallization increase, however, laser powers required to form melt-quenched 
amorphous decrease, for the Sb70Te30 recording film with increasing Ag content. Accordingly, we can improve 
the archival stability and recording sensitivity of the Sb70Te30 recording film by doping Ag, however, the 
initialization (crystallization) sensitivity of the as-deposited film will become more difficult. It should have an 
optimum element doping concentration for the Sb70Te30 recording film to obtain an adequate value of Ec/Em for 
smoothly switching between amorphous and/or crystalline marks reversible by using a blue-laser-pump.
