25-60 分鐘之時間誤差[8-10]。因此當考慮鄰近電力公司
間經由通訊網路傳輸資料時，可以考慮傳遞的時間
差，以減小內部網路與外部網路資料的不一致性。另
外，在建立整體網路模式上，穩態狀態估計 (state
estimation, SE)往往假設系統中的所有測量值都是在同
一時間，時間不同所引起的誤差都視為量測時之誤
差。但當鄰近的區域彼此互連成一個大的系統(數千個
至數萬個匯流排)時，各電力公司之系統量測資料可能
會發生在不同時間，這將造成整體網路模式估計
(complete model Estimation, CME)中所採用的測量值在
時間上會發生誤差。由於在讀取資料庫的第一個和最
後一個測量值之瞬間有時間差(time skew)，在這時間中
可能由於調度員的要求改變，發電量的改變，網路結
構的改變及因擾動而動態調整的改變等，系統狀態也
會因此而隨之發生變化。
若電力公司間的資料交換已成為例行性的工作，
由長期的歷史資料，可以建立量測資料預測模式以修
正測量值，來解決整體電網模式估計中量測資料時間
差問題。但這種方式卻無法充分利用各自系統狀態估
計所解出的即時狀態解，且大系統的測量值數量龐
大，易造成通訊線路過重的負荷，降低資料傳輸效能，
增加通訊線路成本。目前許多電力公司已裝設同步相
量量測單元(Phasor Measurement Units, PMUs)來量測系
統動態運轉訊息。這些具有”時間同步”特性的同步相量
量測資料若能被充分利用的話，對於整體電網模式估
計之時間差問題與通訊線路效能與成本上，或許是一
個有效且可行的解決方案。因此互連系統資料交換可
以直接傳送鄰近電力公司各自系統所計算出的內部網
路狀態解與所量測的同步相量資料至區域控制中心
(Regional Transmission Organization, RTO)，再藉由適當
的狀態估計解整合模式，修正大區域系統量測資料時
間延遲，包含通訊傳輸延遲與量測資料發生在不同時
間，所造成的誤差。
本計畫主要目的為在不改變原有EMS狀態估計功
能與降低通訊線路負荷下，以資料交換及整合方式，
提出一個新的 CME 及即時 ATC 計算架構。各電力公
司的同步相量量測資料與 EMS 狀態估計解被傳送至中
央控制中心，透過延伸卡曼濾波 (Extended Kalman
Filtering, EKF)理論所建構之動態狀態估計(Dynamic
State Estimation, DSE)來減少測量值時間差效應，提高
整體網路模式估計結果的正確性，提供即時 ATC 計算
精確的基礎解，使電力交易參與者對在電力傳輸開放
即時資訊系統 (Open Access Same-time Information
System, OASIS)上公佈的 ATC 有更高的信賴度。
二、研究方法與成果
本計畫首先瞭解各電力公司間資料交換之種類、
格式及方式，並研擬電力資料交換機制。同時，建立
互連電網估計模式及穩態狀態估計計算模式，並建構
系統狀態資料交換模式。然後，建立系統電壓離散數
學模型及互連電網動態狀態估計模式來整合不同電力
公司的穩態狀態估計解及同步相量資料，以修正因測
量值時間差所造成的估計誤差，並發展 ATC 計算程式
供後續的測試分析使用。最後，收集及彙整 IEEE 標準
輸電測試系統及台電輸電系統之網路架構、線路參
數、及連續負載量，並研擬其同步相量運轉資料、
SCADA 運轉資料、及狀態估計結果，以作為建立互連
電網估計模式的基礎，並以彙整的測試系統進行整體
網路估計及即時 ATC 計算之實例模擬。本計畫採用之
進行步驟，執行進度，及工作項目如下：
1. 收集及彙整國外電力公司間資料交換之種類、格
式及方式，以作為本計畫執行之參考。
2. 建立互連電網估計模式分析模式。
3. 建立穩態狀態估計模式。
4. 研擬互連電網穩態狀態估計解及同步相量量測資
料交換模式。
5. 建立系統電壓離散數學模型，並應用延伸卡曼濾
波技術建立大區域系統動態狀態估計模式，同時
利用所建立的狀態估計模式修正互連系統狀態
解。
6. 發展 ATC 計算程式。
7. 收集及研擬 IEEE 輸電標準測試系統及台電公司
輸電網路架構、線路參數、連續負載量，以作為
本計畫執行所需之測試系統。
8. 以 IEEE 測試系統及台電輸電網路來分析同步相
量量測資料的整合對即時整體電網估計模式之影
響，並分析及比較所建立的互連電網動態狀態估
計與傳統靜態 SE 之執行效能，同時測試狀態估計
執行週期對計算結果的影響。
9. 以所提出的互連電網動態狀態估計模式，對 IEEE
測試系統及台電輸電網路進行整體電網即時狀態
解對 ATC 計算誤差之實例模擬。
10.綜合歸納測試結果，並作結論。
2.1 問題描述
現今由於電腦軟硬體及通訊技術的進步，許多電力
公司紛紛建置高效能的通訊線路作為資料交換分享使
用。為增加互連系統 SE 計算的精確性，外部網路即時
的測量值資料可透過廣域資訊分享（Wide Area
Information Sharing, WAIS）系統來傳送。這種即時資
料的傳輸架構使得各個系統的控制中心能夠收集到整
個網路的即時資訊。圖 1 所示為互連電網資料交換之
一般化架構。如圖 1 所示，假設一個互連電網中有四
個電力公司，這些電力公司各自擁有自己的系統。在
每個子區域系統中都有一個控制中心，稱為子區域控
制中心(subarea control center, SACC)，負責管控自己電

2.5 測試結果及討論
本計畫所發展的整合同步相量資料之動態狀態估
計模式是以 MATLAB 程式語言撰寫，在 Pentium IV 電
腦上進行測試。為了瞭解該動態估計模式之執行效
能，我們以 IEEE 30-bus 標準測試系統及台電公司輸電
系統進行實例模擬。IEEE 30-bus 測試系統被分割成 3
個區域，表示 3 個電力公司(SACC1、SACC2、SACC3)
系統互連，假設 SCC 位於 SACC1 內，SACC2 及 SACC3
必須將 SE 解傳送至 SACC1。然後以每隔 1 分鐘的系
統匯流排注入值，在每個時間點執行負載潮流分析以
作為各個時刻系統狀態的實際值。利用以上所求得的

1 分鐘的系統狀態解，以內插法及數位訊號處理方法

A. Probabilistic Load-Flow Solution Considering Network
Reconfiguration

generation patterns are not certain, future load characteristics
become more unpredictable, and network configurations are
more diverse. The PLF takes these uncertain factors into
account in the load flow computations and evaluates
probability density functions and/or statistical moments of all
nodal voltages and network flows to indicate the possible
ranges of the load flow result. Many probabilistic simulation

2006 International Conference on Power System Technology
Visualization of Large-Scale Power System
Operations Using Phasor Measurements
Chun-Lien Su, Member, IEEE, and Bo-Yuan Jau
Abstract-- Many countries around the world had experienced
large-scale blackout incidents in recent years. Preventing the
large-scale blackout or reducing its impact requires an effective
monitoring of the system state of the entire grid during
emergency operations. Visualizations that provide a wide-scale
and animating display to graphically show important system
quantities are an effective way to assist the operators in assessing
system status during emergency operations. The availability of
real-time system operating data is the main problem when
visualizing the emergency operations. To provide the adequate
"real-time" system operating information for monitoring system
operations, a new procedure for monitoring large-scale power
system operations is proposed in this paper. Instead of using the
system state solution obtained from state estimation (SE),
synchronized phasor measurements are used in this paper for
monitoring the entire grid operations. Since the phasor
measurements only exist at a few buses, a linear SE is used to
obtain more phasor data. The voltages of the estimated phasor
data are then visualized using contours. The proposed method is
tested on an actual disturbance event occurred in Taiwan power
system. Performance of the proposed method is ensured through
several sensitivity analyses.
Index Terms-Power system operations, Blackout, Voltage
contours, Phasor measurements.
I. INTRODUCTION
OWING to the deficiency of system stability and reliability,
many utilities worldwide had experienced large-scale
blackouts in recent years [1-3]. The blackout covering a wide-
area affects numerous peoples' life and causes huge financial
losses. Form a system operating security point of view, it has
been desired to effectively present the system state of the
entire grid to let the operators quickly assess the actual system
condition to determine proper control actions for avoiding the
occurrence of the blackout or reducing the impact of the
blackout. However, in an existing Energy Management System
(EMS) system operational quantities are usually represented as
either analog fields on a set of substation on-line diagrams, or
numeric fields on tabular displays. Such a representation that
might be desired for normal and routine operations may be

assessing system status during emergency operations [4].
Voltage is an important parameter in the operation of power
systems. The nodal voltage magnitude information is useful for
indicating the system security condition during extreme
emergency operations. Therefore, many visualization
techniques for designing voltage visualizations have been
developed. Among the emerging voltage visualizations,
contours widely used for the display of spatially distributed
continuous data in many other fields have been used for
visualizing nodal voltages in large-scale power systems with
powerful computer capability. The voltage contours that
handle a vast amount of node voltage information effectively
improve the time required for perceiving patterns in large
amounts of data displayed by using the traditional
representation methods. By the voltage contours, the
identification and discernment of the voltages in the entire grid
become easy and effective [5].
Many literatures are relevant to the voltage contour designs
on various applications of electric power systems [6-9].
Reference [6] proposed an efficient computation algorithm for
contouring bus voltage data in a real-time environment. The
application of voltage contours to competitive electric power
markets is presented in [7,8]. An experiment was conducted in
[9] to determine the effectiveness of using color contours to
visualize electric power system bus voltage information.
This paper presents the voltage contours of large-scale
power systems for on-line monitoring system operating
conditions during times of emergency operations. The
availability of real-time system operating data is the main
problem when voltage information contours are designed for
extreme emergency operations [4]. To provide the adequate
"real-time" system operating information for monitoring

power system operations is proposed in this paper. Instead of
using the system state solution obtained from state estimation
(SE), synchronized phasor measurements are used for voltage
contours. Since the phasor measurements only exist at a few
buses, the data about real-time system states may be
inadequate and thus, a linear SE model is used to obtain more

B. Overall Procedure for Monitoring Large-Scale Power
System Operations
The overall procedure for monitoring large-scale power
system operations is shown in Fig. 1. The weighting control
parameter is assigned first. The scope and outline of the grid to
be monitored is plotted and the locations of PMUs are
identified. The voltage and current phasor measurements are
recorded for the linear state estimation. The voltage vector is
then computed by using (3). The voltage magnitude data are
extracted from these estimated data. For each virtual point in
the grid, the buses with voltage data are used for calculating
the distances between these buses and the virtual point. The
distance obtained is then used for computing weighted factors
by using (5) and the virtual voltage data is computed by using
(4). When all virtual points in the grid are taken care of, the
voltage contour plot module is executed. The voltage contours
of the entire grid are displayed with a color map. Monitoring
of the entire grid can be achieved through the view of the
contour map.
The execution time of the proposed method for monitoring
large-scale power system operations is the main issue when it
is used for the on-line application. When the phasor data are
estimated with the linear state estimation, it will increase the
overall execution time of presenting operating conditions. This
execution time is strongly relevant to how many PMUs are
used; therefore, it is important to know the execution times of
the proposed method under different numbers of PMUs. In
addition, the performance of displaying voltage contours
significantly depends upon the value of the weighting control
factor and the number of sub-grid divided from the entire grid.
It is necessary to understand the effects of different values of
weighting control parameter and different numbers of sub-grid
on voltage contours display. The information obtained is
useful for selecting the proper control parameter and number
of sub-grid for voltage contouring plotting. These factors that
affect voltage contours are tested in the following section.

practical Taiwan power system is used for the study. The
scope of the monitored Taiwan power system covers the
longitude of 119 to 123 degrees and the latitude of 21.5 to

visualization development software [12] on a personal

of 128 Mbytes.

An actual disturbance occurred in Taiwan power system
due to a lightning event is also tested. The position of the
event is located at Taiwan south area. The event that started at
16:45:03 and stopped at 16:50:28 lasted about 5 minutes. In
this time period, the traditional SE solution about the Taiwan
power system is unavailable. In this time, the disturbance
information for this event was captured by the PMUs
dispersedly located in the Taiwan north (top of the figure),
central (middle part of the figure), and south (bottom of the
figure) areas. Using the computation procedure shown in Fig.
1, the more phasor data about this event are obtained by using
the linear state estimation. The voltage data are extracted from
these estimated data for plotting voltage contours. The results
are shown in Fig. 5 and Fig. 6, which depict the voltage
information for the event without and with considering the
linear state estimation, respectively. In the test, the weighting
control parameter of 4 and the sub-grids of 100*100 are used
for a better visual display purpose. In the computation of the
linear SE, a total of 29 phasor data is obtained while only 9
true phasor data are considered.
Fig. 5(a) shows that the voltage information of the entire
Taiwan power grid before the event. The change in system
operating condition after the event can be seen in Fig. 5(b), Fig.
5(c), and Fig. 5(d), which show the consequent system
operating conditions. As can be seen from Fig. 5(a) that before
the event the bus voltages in the system are around within a
range of 0.98 to 1.02 p.u.. After the event, the bus voltages in
the south and central areas in Fig. 5(b), Fig. 5(c), and Fig. 5(d)
are smaller than those for the north area. This is because the
location of the event is near the south area. The voltages at
these two areas are below about 0.95 p.u. at this time period.
Observing the dynamic change in these figures, the operators
can more easily and quickly to know the system operating
condition. The result shows that the voltage contouring
algorithm used in this paper is accurate. The result also implies
that the location of the disturbance event may be roughly
identified through the observation of animating displays of
voltage contours.
The dynamic change in voltages information about the
event with considering the linear state estimation is shown in
Fig. 6. Comparisons of Fig. 5 and Fig. 6, it can be seen that
since more voltage data are used in Fig. 6, the visual displays
of Fig. 6 are fairly more obvious and clearer than those for Fig.
5. From the comparisons, it is also found that the voltage
information in some buses in the south area of Fig. 6(c) and
Fig. 6(d) are significantly different from those for Fig. 5(c) and
Fig. 5(d). This fact has indicated that the PMUs based linear
state estimation is very helpful to monitor the entire grid
operations. In Fig. 5 and Fig. 6, the execution times of each
display without and with considering linear state estimation are
0.42 seconds and 1.02 seconds, respectively. The response
time may be acceptable for the operators for on-line
monitoring extreme emergency operating conditions. If it is
not accepted, the response time can be further improved
through the use of a high-level computer or a concise
computation program. From Fig. 5 and Fig. 6, it has shown
that the real-time measurements used for monitoring system
operating conditions can be enhanced by using a linear state
estimation. The voltage contours using the estimated phasor
data can provide adequate information about the system
operations to let the operators intuitively and quickly know the
entire grid operating conditions during emergency operations.
