1. Introduction 
     In order to relieve the energy crises, environment pollution and global warming, 
the development of accessible renewable energy productions has received substantial 
attention.  Solar power is the most noteworthy among renewable and sustainable 
energy resources because it is a clean and unlimited resource of energy and has the 
global availability.  In recent years, several alternatives to Si-based solar cells or 
photovoltaic (PV) cells have become available, and considerable research is ongoing 
towards substantially reducing the cost of electricity generation [1].  The 
dye-sensitized solar cell (DSSC), as proposed by O’Regan and Grätzel [2], is an 
attractive alternative because of its properties, such as low production cost, the 
various choices of substrates that can be used, and low environmental impact during 
fabrication [3-5].  However, a comparison with conventional solid-sate junction 
devices made of crystalline silicon indicates that the DSSC has lower power 
conversion efficiency [6]. 
     Adding semiconductor materials, which can absorb light in the visible region, 
to the meso-porous TiO2 electrode is one of promising approaches to enhance light 
harvest of a DSSC.  For example, metal chalcogenide semiconductors such as 
cadmium sulfide (CdS) [7-11], cadmium selenide (CdSe) [12-15], lead sulfide (PbS) 
[16-17], and CdS/CdSe [18] were used for quantum dot-sensitized solar cell 
applications.  Luther et al. indicated the advantages of lead chalcogenides quantum

Applications of P-Type NiO in Dye-Sensitized Solar Cells 
Ting-Lung Chiang, Chuen-Shii Choua, Der-Ho Wu and Chin-Min Hsiung 
Department of Mechanical Engineering 
National Pingtung University of Science and Technology, Pingtung 912, Taiwan 
acschou@mail.npust.edu.tw

Abstract. This study investigates the applicability of p-type NiO in the working (or counter) 
electrode of a dye-sensitized solar cell (DSSC). The working electrode was designed and 
fabricated by depositing a film of TiO2/NiO composite particles, which were prepared by mixing 
the Ni powder with TiO2 particles using dry mixing method. The counter electrode was 
fabricated by depositing a NiO film on top of a Pt film, which has been deposited on a FTO glass 
using an ion-sputtering coater (or E-beam evaporator). This study shows that the power 
conversion efficiency of the DSSC with TiO2/NiO composite particles (3.80%) substantially 
exceeds that of the conventional DSSC (3.16%) due to the effects of the NiO barrier and the n-p 
junction. Aside from this, the power conversion efficiency of a DSSC with a Pt(E)/NiO counter 
electrode (4.28%) substantially exceeds that of a conventional DSSC with a Pt(E) counter 
electrode on which a Pt film was deposited using an E-beam evaporator. This result is attributed 
to the fact that the NiO film coated on the Pt(E) counter electrode improves the electrocatalytic 
activity of the counter electrode. 
Introduction 
  Dye sensitized solar cells (DSSCs), as proposed by O’Regan and Grätzel [1], have attracted 
considerable interest since 1991 because of its properties, such as low production cost and low 
environmental impact during fabrication [2, 3]. In the past few years, several methods have 
utilized the p-type oxide semiconductors, such as NiO [4-7], CuAlO2 [8], and CuO [9] to be as 
the hole collector and a barrier for charge recombination. The n–p junction electrodes, such as 
SnO2/NiO [10], TiO2/NiO [11], were fabricated to promote the performance of the DSSC, and 
[11] claimed that the efficiency of the TiO2/NiO solar cell is 30% more than that of the bare TiO2. 
Aside from this observation, Kay and Grätzel reported that a small amount of platinum might be 
dissolved in the electrolyte by oxidation and complex formation with iodide/tri-iodide (such as 
PtI4 or H2PtI6) [12]. This may degrade the performance of counter electrodes after a certain 
period of exposure to light. 
Therefore, in this study, the dye-sensitized solar cell with a working electrode of TiO2/NiO 
composite particles (Fig. 1(a)) and the dye-sensitized solar cell with a Pt/NiO counter electrode 
(Fig. 1(b)) were fabricated. The effect of P-type NiO on the power conversion efficiency of a 
DSSC was investigated. A comparison of a DSSC with the proposed working (or counter) 
electrode with the conventional DSSC was also made in this study. Aside from this, the results 
presented in this paper are cited from Chou et al. [13-14].

3.2 DSSC with a counter electrode of Pt/NiO film 
Fig. 2(b) shows the presence of a NiO film enhances the current density during the redox 
process.  For example, in the oxidation process, the highest current density of Pt(E)/NiO (7.2 
mA/cm2) exceeds that of Pt(E) (6.1 mA/cm2). In the reduction process, the lowest current density 
of Pt(E)/NiO (-5.2 mA/cm2) also exceeds that of Pt(E) (-3.9 mA/cm2). This result is attributed to 
the fact that a larger active surface area due to a deposited NiO film corresponds to a more 
energetic electrocatalytic activity. A similar tendency was also observed by Kim et al. [15] and 
Yoon et al. [16]. 
Therefore, the presence of a NiO film remarkably promotes the power conversion efficiency of 
DSSC. For example, the power conversion efficiencies of DSSC in tests S3 (with a Pt (E) counter 
electrode) and S5 (with a Pt (E)/NiO counter electrode) are 3.16% and 4.28%, respectively.  
Furthermore, the power conversion efficiencies of DSSC in tests S4 (with a Pt (S) counter 
electrode) and S6 (with a Pt(S)/NiO counter electrode) are 2.34% and 2.92%, respectively. These 
results are due to the following facts: (1) the NiO film deposited on top of Pt (E) (or Pt (S)) 
substantially enhances the surface roughness average (Ra) of the counter electrode; (2) the 
increased roughness improves the light scattering as well as the electroactive area of a counter 
electrode. Kim et al. [15] showed that the overall conversion efficiency of DSSC increased to 
3.62% and 4.21% through the use of Pt/NiO and Pt/TiO2 bi-phase counter electrodes, 
respectively. 
Conclusion 
The power conversion efficiency of the DSSC with TiO2/NiO particles exceeded that of the 
conventional DSSC due to the effects of the NiO barrier and the n-p junction. Further, the power 
conversion efficiency of the DSSC with a Pt(E)/NiO counter electrode exceeds that of the DSSC 
with a Pt(E) counter electrode because the Pt(E)/NiO counter electrode has better electrocatalytic 
activity.  
Most interestingly, this study supports the application of NiO particles in improving the 
performance of a DSSC.
