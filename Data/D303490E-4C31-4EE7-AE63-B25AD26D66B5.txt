隨著網路 3D虛擬技術的快速發展，3D虛擬商店的應用將越來越普及；由於高齡人口的行
動不便，勢必將有越來越多的高齡人口會進行相關網路瀏覽與消費活動。3D虛擬商店不同
於一般網路商店，其吸引消費者最大的誘因在於逼真的商品感受性；所以，如何提升 3D
虛擬商店的臨場感是商家與技術人員最大的目標。但在臨場感的提升之下，透過介面的操
作，是否也因此更易誘發空間症候群，特別是高齡人口這一族群。然而我們發現，過去有
關空間症候群的研究，多著重顯示介面與控制模式之研究，而忽略虛擬環境氛圍(如：知覺
線索與臨場感)之探討，當虛擬商店臨場感感受越高或越低時，實際感覺訊息與預期經驗產
生的差異是否更加擴大，進而造成知覺衝突的加劇與空間症候群之誘發，這是在高齡人口
將成為大量使用網路 3D 虛擬商店潛在族群趨勢下，應立即探討與改善之問題。故本研究
之目的包括: (一)深入瞭解 3D虛擬商店深度知覺線索對於高齡人口空間認知能力與臨場感
感受的影響；(二)在深度知覺主效果下，立體顯像模式對於臨場感感受之邊際效果；(三)探
討不同臨場感感受程度對於誘發空間症候群之影響。結果顯示，以 3D偏光眼鏡瀏覽 3D虛
擬商店，搭配高度深度知覺線索，所產生的臨場感效果最高；但臨場感愈高並未相對提升
對於空間認知能力，對於購物能力亦無顯著的影響，但是受試者對於商店購物偏好是增加
的。其次，深度知覺線索越強烈，因為臨場感與融入性的增加，對於高齡受試者誘發空間
症候群之效應，以 TFT-LCD 顯示裝置較輕微，所以，以 TFT-LCD 顯示裝置建構 3D 虛擬
環境瀏覽環境，除了可增加高齡人口的偏好與實用性，相對產生的空間症候群症狀亦較輕
微。

53.2 空間症候群的症狀
虛擬環境若具有較佳的臨場感，可以讓使用者有沉浸於另一個虛擬世界的感覺，過去
的研究發現，經由使用 3D立體即時圖形展示裝置，例如：頭盔顯示器 (HMD)或多視窗顯
示器，已被證明在教育、娛樂、工程、駕駛模擬化、以及模擬飛行的應用上，有不錯的效
果(Cruz-Neira et al., 1993)。然而，隨著虛擬環境廣泛的應用，在一些情況，使用者在沉浸
過程中會逐漸產生類似動暈症(motion sickness)的類似症狀，此種不舒適的疾病症狀，被稱
為空間症候群(Cybersickness)。空間症候群，可能發生的一些癥狀，包括：眼睛過勞、頭痛、
臉色蒼白、出汗、嘴巴乾燥、胃脹、方向知覺的喪失、眩暈、運動失調、反胃、嘔吐。
雖然動暈症和空間症候群產生相同類型的症狀，他們卻不是相同的事物。前者，僅由
(內耳)前庭刺激即能引起動暈症(Money, 1970)， 偶而也會因幻覺引起(Kennedy et al.,
1988)。後者，病症完全因視覺的刺激引起，而非前庭刺激。然而，至今尚沒有一確切的原
因可以描述此症狀為何會發生(Kennedy & Fowlkes, 1992)？
在虛擬環境中，空間症候群的確會產生一些後果和影響。其中一個潛在危險是空間症
候群可能在經歷虛擬環境幾個小時以後發生。在許多情況下，癥狀可能持續長達數小時之
久，更有一些個案長達數天之久(Palmisano & Chan, 2004)。對於空間症候群如何發生及發
生原因，有三個主要的理論：感覺衝突理論，中毒理論和姿勢的不穩定理論。此三個論點，
不論是哪一項理論，都可得到一致的結論：3D虛擬環境與空間症候群的誘發是有絕對的關
係，當高齡人口瀏覽 3D 虛擬商店，除了享受網路購物樂趣外，高度的臨場感將帶給另一
番不同的視覺感受，但是可能引發的空間症候群後遺症，是不容忽視的。
4. 研究方法
為達成前述之目的，研究方法上，以探討 3D 虛擬商店深度知覺線索與立體顯像模式
對於臨場感感受之交互影響，其次探討臨場感對於高齡人口空間認知能力之影響及誘發空
間症候群之程度評估，具體作法包括：文獻資料的探討、系統發展工具的評估、虛擬商店
實驗環境設計、臨場感與空間症候群之實驗設計，及建構 3D 虛擬商店臨場感的最佳設計
策略。以下分別就內容說明之：
4.1文獻探討與發展工具評估
文獻探討的目的在於了解過去相關的研究與背景，藉以建立本研究的研究架構，以及
架構中主要的概念及變數。本研究文獻探討的重點與範圍包括：網路應用對高齡人口
之影響、3D 虛擬環境之應用技術、3D 虛擬環境空間知覺與臨場感的相關研究、高齡
人口在瀏覽 3D虛擬商店過程中可能遭遇之問題與空間症候群的相關研究。
4.2 3D虛擬商店測試系統設計
本研究虛擬商店之設計，因考量高齡人口網路購物行為，多以家居用品為主，及為建
立實驗中的購物行為，乃規劃四大商品展示區，四大商品展示區分別為：清潔用品區、
手工具區、文具區與盥洗用品區，每一商品區至少包含 15個種類以上商品(如文具區包
括：訂書機、膠帶、墨水、橡皮擦、計算機、筆記本、筆、膠水、剪刀、修正液、尺、
檔案匣、量規、畫筆及紙張等 15項)。在場景方面，分為三種不同的氛圍：低度深度線
索、中度深度線索、高度深度線索。在低度深度線索情境中，商品僅用 2D圖像線索方
式呈現，且僅有展示櫃與商品呈現，當瀏覽者操作畫面於較大視角時，畫面之物品會
出現平面失真的感覺；在中度深度線索情境中，商品以立體圖像線索方式呈現，商品
隨著瀏覽角度，會呈現出 3D不同的面向；在高度深度線索情境中，商品除了以立體圖
像線索方式呈現外，整個環境加上一些豐富的環境素材，如：櫃檯、盆景、畫飾…等，
增加環境的豐富性。

6. 結論
當 3D 虛擬情境愈來愈與人們生活結合後，的確帶來視覺上的不同感受，對於高齡人口也
產生了若干吸引力，這由本實驗受試者的較高的新奇感受與再次使用性，可看出此一趨勢，
然而當顯示裝置愈來愈 3D化(3D電視)，搭配深度知覺線索(立體圖像、氛圍)越強烈，對於
高齡人口在操作瀏覽 3D 虛擬商店時，雖產生相當高的臨場感，但是另一個誘發生理的現
象：空間症候群，卻是不爭的事實。對於高齡受試者誘發空間症候群之效應，以 TFT-LCD
顯示裝置較輕微，所以，以 TFT-LCD顯示裝置建構 3D虛擬環境瀏覽環境，除了可產生某
種程度的臨場感，增加高齡人口的偏好與實用性，相對產生的空間症候群症狀亦較輕微。

2. A FUZZY VIGILANCE-MEASURING MODEL
2.1. Model development
When an atypical signal appears in a system, the external stimulus will
generate human neural activity in the brain. Therefore, on average, there will be
more sensory or neural activity in the brain when a signal is present than when it
is absent [8]. We refer to this quantity X of neural activity as the evidence
variable. If there is enough neural activity, X exceeds the critical threshold XC1
of preliminary vigilance, and the operator will decide “yes.” If there is too litle, 
the operator wil decide “no,” as shown in Figure 1. After a period of time, the 
signal gradually strengthens until X exceeds criterion XC1, and the operator will
say “yes.” Sometimes, even when no signal is present, X wil exceed the
criterion XC1because of random variations in the environment and the operator’s 
own “baseline” level of neural firing, and the operator wil say “yes” (generating 
a false alarm in interval α of Figure 1). When the operator detects an unusual or
infrequent condition in the preliminary vigilance, then he/she must maintain
concentration to detect any resulting emergency signals by extended vigilance.
If the system approaches a dangerous situation (interval B of Figure 1) and there
is enough neural activity, then X exceeds the critical threshold XC2 of the
extended vigilance, and the operator wil decide “Yes.” If there is not enough 
activity, the operator wil decide “No.”

3. ALARM FOR IMPROVING EXTENDED VIGILANCE
3.1. Alarm development and experiment 2
In the vigilance-measuring model, we define y = 50 (%) as good status, and
in the first experiment the average performance of preliminary vigilance is 47.31
(%). Therefore, the performance value 50 (%) of y* in the preliminary vigilance
is defined as the threshold for producing an alarm signal. If the value of y* is
smaller than 50 (%), the system will produce an alarm signal to warn the
participant to maintain his/her extended vigilance. This experiment used the
same set of participants as in the first experiment. The two experiments were
separated by approximately one month.
3.3. Results and discussion
In the second experimental process, we found that the
preliminary vigilance performances of participants #4 and #11 to
#18 were poor (the performance of these participants were also
poor in the first experiment, except for #12), so the fuzzy alarm
activated. A differences of means test showed that the means of the
extended vigilance performance of nine participants in the second
experiment was significantly larger than in the first experiment (t =
4.9662 > t (0.95, 8) = 1.8595 and p <0.0006). However, we also
found that the means of the preliminary vigilance performance of
these nine participants in the second experiment was significantly
smaller than in the first experiment (t = 1.8770 > t (0.95, 8) =
1.8595 and p < 0.05). These findings indicate that the effect of an
alarm for improving the extended vigilance performance is
significant when the performance of preliminary vigilance of
participants is poor.

D. Objectives
Although the momentum changes in fatigue are
not easy to detect accurately by vehicle-based
methods, there are some tendency indices could be
considered to use to detect the momentum changes.
In this study the vehicle-based approach by
monitoring the momentum of driving behavior was
used to develop a neuro-fuzzy system for detecting
driver fatigue, and the solution of the system was
applied to produce an adaptive alarm for wake
driver up.
There are two phases to develop the neuro-fuzzy
detecting and combating fatigue system. In phase 1,
the tendency indices were surveyed for detect
driving momentum changes while the driver
produced the fatigue gradually, and then assessing
and selecting the adaptive tendency indices by a
driving simulation experiment. In phase 2, a
neuro-fuzzy detecting and combating fatigue
system was developed with the adaptive tendency
indices and verified by the second experiment.
ADAPTIVE TENDENCY INDICES ON MOMENTUM
OF FATIGUE DRIVING
Adaptive tendency indices
In general, there are two categories of
quantitative tendency predicting methods: moving
averages and oscillators. Oscillators take
quantitative analysis one step beyond moving
averages. They are designed to provide signals
concerning the general strength of trend and
over-strong and over-weak conditions. In fact, the
occurrence of driver fatigue could be seen as
behavioral trend. For example, Santana-Diaz et al.
found that the standard deviation of steering wheel
angle is decreasing 208% and speed is decreasing
25% in fatigue situation to compare with normal
situation. The oscillation of driving behavior is
smaller and smaller when in fatigue [7]. Therefore,
the oscillators could be considered to be used to
predict the tendency of driver fatigue. Generally,
there are three oscillators been used extensively: 1)
relative strength index (RSI); 2) stochastic
oscillators (KDL); 3) moving average convergence
divergence (MACD). In general, RSI is a short
term index for momentum, KDL is middle term and
MACD is long term. Formula and specify are
shown as follows.

In the second experiment subjective fatigue is measured
through the use of the Matthews and Desmond’s Fatigue
Rating Scales as the first experiment. Following the main
drive, subjects must complete the subjective state measures
(‘post-drive’).
In sensitivity measurement, there is a characteristic task
required participant to perform. In the position of 50 miles, a
series of 30 road-signs were presented to subjects, at a
distance of 80 meters apart. Subjects were required to detect
target sign which shows 50 miles of speed limits as shown in
Fig. 1, and to press the button set on the steering wheel when
they detected the target. However, there is a situation
designed as noise to make participant in confusion, dense fog.
In dense fog (occurred from the 5th road sign to the 20th road
sign), the road signs will be unclear. Thus, the participant
may make a wrong detection (misses, and false alarms), if
he/she was in poor concentration. For example, because the
dense fog, the road sign is unclear. Which is 50 or 58 miles of
speed limit? If participants believe the number is 50 to press
the button set, they will make a wrong detection “false
alarm.” If not, they will make correct detection “correct
rejection.”
