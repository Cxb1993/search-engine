壹、緣由與目的 
近年來傳統活性污泥程序結合薄膜單元發
展出薄膜生物反應槽（Membrane bioreactor, 
MBR），利用薄膜取代沉澱池，達到固液分離
的效果。由於 MBR 處理程序能有效去除廢水中
污染物，且出流水已接近水回收再利用標準，
不過 MBR 出流水仍受到不同的反應器型式、薄
膜種類、薄膜孔徑大小與進流水質特性所影
響，使得出流水中仍有或多或少的污染物
[1-10]，例如 MBR 出流水中常含有溶解性微生
物產物（Soluble Microbial Products, SMP）及無
機鹽類，因而若要將 MBR 出流水回收再利用，
則仍須透過其他處理程序進一步處理。由於薄
膜分離程序可去除水中各種有機及無機物質，
可有效地替代高級物化程序的處理功能，為現
階段最具潛力的廢水回收再利用技術[11]。因
此，若欲以薄膜程序探討 MBR 出流水的水回收
再利用，則上述 SMP 及無機鹽類對薄膜程序的
行政院國家科學委員會專題研究計畫成果報告 
 
MBR出流水SMP對薄膜程序操作與膜面積垢之探討 
The effect of SMP in MBR effluent on membrane process performance 
and membrane fouling 
 
計畫編號：NSC 96-2221-E-238-004- 
執行期限：96 年08 月01 日至97 年07 月31 日 
主持人：莊連春(萬能科技大學環境工程學系) 
 
 
摘要 
本計畫利用超濾（UF）與逆滲透（RO）薄膜程序處理人工染料廢水(SDW)、都市污水(DW)、
食品廢水(FPW)及 TFT-LCD 製程廢水(TFT-LCD)經薄膜生物反應槽（Membrane Bioreactor, MBR）
處理之出流水，評估 UF、RO 程序回收再利用 MBR 出流水之可行性，並探討 SDW MBR 出流水
水質特性對 RO 薄膜過濾機制的影響。 
研究結果顯示，四種原水的 MBR 出流水中，僅有微量的懸浮物質，濁度介於 0.01~1.82 NTU，
大多以溶解性有機物及無機鹽類存在，其中有機物主要為溶解性微生物產物(Soluble Microbial 
Product, SMP)，其分子量分佈受到原飼水及不同 MBR 程序所影響，與傳統生物程序產生的 SMP
分子量分佈有所差異。MBR 出流水經薄膜程序處理後，可有效去除出流水中溶解性有機物及無機
鹽類，SDW、DW、TFT-LCD 經由 RO 處理之滲透液導電度、TOC 及 TS 之去除率，分別為 85~98%、
85~94%及 94~99%，而 FPW 經由 UF/RO 處理後，滲透液導電度、TOC 及 TS 之去除率，分別為
91.1%、93.8%、90.9%，此外，RO 對 SMP 有很好的去除效果，尤其對分子量較大的含芳香族 SMP，
有較高的去除率。 
在以 RO 處理 SDW 過濾機制之探討中，發現 RO 膜表面累積微量有機物，且溶液擴散模式可
有效推測滲透液濃度，誤差範圍小於±30%以內，顯示 RO 膜過濾 SDW 仍以溶液擴散為主要去除
機制。此外，利用模式推測滲透液的 TOC 濃度均比實際滲透液中的 TOC 濃度低，其原因可能是
微量有機物於膜面發生濃度極化，使得模式有低估的現象。 
 
關鍵詞：超濾、逆滲透、薄膜生物反應槽、溶液擴散模式、溶解性微生物產物 
 
指數皆可達 80~90%去除率(如表 4)，操作期
間的操作壓力及通量並無下降之趨勢(如圖
5)，且處理水可用於半導體Ⅳ級純水，或可
作為超純水進料水。但若以工業取水成本評
估，其造水成本仍偏高，未來若能實施水污
費徵收、調整水價及以法規規範各產業之水
回收再利用率後，以 RO 程序回收 MBR 出
流水，仍然具有優勢與可行性。

Using Reverse Osmosis Separation to Treat and Reuse the 
Membrane Bioreactor Effluent from Dye Wastewater 
 
Lain-Chuen Juang*, Dyi-Hwa Tseng** and Ching-Chieh Lin** 
*Department of Environmental Engineering, Vanung University, Chung-Li, 320, Taiwan 
**Graduate Institute of Environmental Engineering, National Central University, Chung-Li, 320, Taiwan 
 
Keywords: Reverse Osmosis, Membrane Bioreactor, Soluble Microbial Product, Solution-Diffusion Model 
 
For sustainable development, it will be necessary to gradually phase in water reclaiming 
and reusing technologies to ensure the “zero discharge” of wastewater into the 
environment. Membrane processes are promising technologies for the removal of many 
types of organic and inorganic pollutants. The use of membranes appears to be a 
suitable alternative to conventional wastewater treatment processes because of their 
high flexibility and excellent performance in the removal of organic [e.g., soluble 
microbial products (SMP)] and inorganic (e.g., nutrients) pollutants from wastewater. The 
aim of this study was to determine the filtration behavior of a reverse osmosis (RO) 
process for treating the synthetic dye wastewater (SDW) effluent from a membrane 
bioreactor (MBR). The filtration mechanism was investigated through analyses of the 
quality of the permeate water and the characteristics of membrane surface. 
The results show that there was a slight amount of suspended solid in the effluent of 
the MBR, with the major contaminants being salt and organic substances comprising the 
SMP as shown in Figure 1. The water quality of the RO permeate did not vary 
significantly when performing the experiments using different recovery rates (20, 40, 60, 
and 80%). Indeed, all of the removal efficiencies—measured in terms of the water quality 
in the permeate treated through RO processing—were over 70%; thus, these permeates 
meet the water reuse standards required for the industry and for use in water purification 
machines. From Table 1, we find that the organic substances did not appear to 
accumulate on the membranes—as determined through analysis of the membrane 
surfaces using SEM, AFM, SEM-EDS, and ATR-FTIR spectroscopy—with increasing 
recovery rate. On the other hand, we determined that the permeate flux was affected 
only by the difference between the osmosis pressures of the feedwater and the 
permeate. 
Using a solution-diffusion model to simulate the quality of the permeate water, we 
calculated concentrations for the components in the permeate that were within 30% of 
the experimental values (Figure 2). During the solution-diffusion of salt in the solute, the 
negative charge of NO3– ions in the SDW was most likely rejected by the negative 
charge of the membrane surface, resulting in a small solute permeability constant for 
these ions (Table 2). With respect to the solution-diffusion of organic compounds in the 
solute, the removal of the total organic carbon (TOC), i.e., the SMP, occurred efficiently 
when using a hydrophobic membrane in the RO process, resulting in the permeability 
constant of organic compounds being considerably smaller than that of salt.
