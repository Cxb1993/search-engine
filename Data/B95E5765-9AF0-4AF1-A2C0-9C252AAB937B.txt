The Development and Testing of Hexagonal Wire Mesh 
Taiwan is located the contact interfaces among three major earth plates. The 
development of mountain phenomenon is very active. Therefore, more than 60% areas 
in Taiwan are mountains and hills. Earthquakes are frequently observed. In addition, 
typhoon carrying heavy rainfall several times a year is a common event in Taiwan. 
The large amount of rains and floods create many nature disasters in Taiwan. Metal 
wire mesh is commonly practice for river bank protection, rock fall protection, and 
slope stabilization applications. Currently, the use of wire mesh for river bank 
protection and slope stabilization applications is more than 15 million square meters. 
Only the materials cost is more than 2 trillion New Taiwan dollars. Currently, the 
replacement of wire mesh for these applications is too often. To develop a better 
mechanism to construct metal wire mesh is an important task.  
The early age hand made 4-twisted hexagonal wire meshes were observed to be 
more durable then those current machine made triple-twisted hexagonal wire meshes. 
Therefore, the objective of this study is to study a better structural pattern to produce 
durable hexagonal wire mesh.  
Failure cases for the use of wire mesh in the applications of river bank protection 
and slope stabilization were investigated. Triple-twist and 4-twist model hexagonal 
wire mesh panels were built for tensile and punch tests using well made test panel or 
one cut wire panel. Binding-joint and twist-joint for the connection of gabion panel 
were also evaluated. The results of the study indicated that the ultimate tensile 
strength or punch resistance for triple-twist or 4-twist hexagonal wire mesh panel was 
similar. However, the 4-twist hexagonal wire panels showed better tensile and punch 
resistance after the wire broken at center or other locations. Reverse wire twisting 
mechanism was observed near the broken wires during tensile and punch tests. Twist 
joint showed better tensile resistance than that for binding joint. Based upon the 
results, a better wire mesh structure pattern was proposed to manufacturer. Large 
reaction beam with hydraulic machine and lifer were designed and ordered for future 
detail study.  
    Keywords: hexagonal wire mesh, River bank protection, Slope stabilization, 
Rock-fall protection.
