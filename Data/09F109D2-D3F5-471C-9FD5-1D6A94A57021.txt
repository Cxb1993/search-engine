中文摘要 
「以微型觀測管及透地雷達於邊坡現況調查之研究」為三年期之計畫，第一
年的主要任務為微型觀測管的研發及室內驗證試驗、微型觀測管的挖孔埋設裝備
研發以及尋找適合的邊坡作為未來兩年現地實驗的場所，第二年度的工作重點則
是微型觀測管的持續開發並實際製作觀測管埋入試驗邊坡，進行微型觀測管的初
步觀測，同時並利用透地雷達探測實際地下水位、攝影測量技術量測邊坡的初始
位置，第三年度則是持續利用微型觀測管、透地雷達對於試驗邊坡之地下水位進
行長期觀測，同時亦以攝影測量技術對邊坡進行監測，最後成功綜合上述結果進
行試驗邊坡的穩定性的評估。 
 
關鍵字：邊坡、地下水位、微型觀測管、透地雷達、攝影測量 
 
ABSTRACT 
This project is a three-years project. The main goal of the project in the first year 
was to develop the micro-pipe, verify the equipment in the laboratory; develop the 
hardware of drilling and find the slopes for the next two-year research. The key works 
in the second year were to accomplish the development of the micro-pipe and install it 
into the test slope in situ. Meanwhile, the GPR technique was applied to estimate the 
water table observed by the micro-pipe and the Photogrammetry technique was also 
be utilized to measure the slope movement. In the last year of the project, the water 
table of the slope is continually and regularly measured by the micro-pipe and verified 
through the GPR technique. And, the slope movement is also measured through 
Photogrammetry during this year. Finally, the collected data, including the water table 
of the slope and the movement of the slope, is utilized to evaluate the stability of the 
slope. 
 
Keywords：slope, water table, micro-pipe, GPR, photogrammetry

2-1  邊坡調查 
興建公路或開發社區、農林產業所形成的人工邊坡在崩壞時其規模多屬不
大，以阿里山公路(台 18 公路)在集集地震前後的崩塌記錄為例(如表 2-1-1)[1]，
可見阿里山公路不管在平時或地震時，每年崩塌處數最多者為崩塌量為 100～
500m3 之崩壞，其次是 0～100m3。顯見大部份的人工邊坡發生崩壞時，其破壞
範圍約在 10～20m × 10～30m，破壞面的深度約在 2～4m 之間。因此要了解邊
坡現況就須對邊坡表面到破壞面之間 2～4m 部份的土體特性加以確實掌握。毛
子文等人於 2005年著文介紹香港土力工程處(Geotechnical Engineering Office, 其
前身為 Geotechnical Control Office, 簡稱 GCO)於 1978 年所研發之貫入試驗，該
貫入設備為香港土力工程處於 1978 年發展出的簡易貫入儀器，GCO Probe(相關
設計尺寸如圖 2-1-1 所示)[2]，該設備之貫入方式與標準貫入試驗(Standard 
Penetration Test, SPT)相同，屬於動力式貫入[3]，惟其使用的重錘、重錘落距以
及貫入器之尺寸均小於 SPT，其貫入能量估計約為 SPT 之 1/10~1/11，同時試驗
所求得之打擊次數與 SPT 之打擊次數(N 值)亦有所不同。因該儀器簡單輕便、攜
帶容易、在坡地上操作方便且所需費用低廉[3]，故本計畫參考 GEO 之設計製作
「GCO Probe 鑽孔設備」，並另開發製作ㄧ套拔桿設備供操作時輔助使用，該套
儀器具輕量化、易於搬運與操作容易、且試驗費用低廉，同時試驗結果能反映地
層細微變化，今年度本計畫持續利用該設備在試驗邊坡上進行鑽孔作業。

2-3  透地雷達 
透地雷達(Ground Penetration Radar，簡稱 GPR)為地球物理探測的方法之
一，其乃是利用高頻電磁脈衝波的反射以探測目的體及地質現象等。透地雷達技
術的開發，主要乃因美國在越戰中需要偵測越共地道的技術，於是委託麻省理工
學院開發而成。後來學術機構、工程單位紛紛投入透地雷達的研究，其應用的範
圍相當廣泛。目前透地雷達研究成果如下：[11] 
(1) 冰層或堆雪厚度的調查 
(2) 鹽穹(salt dome)、礦脈的厚度調查 
(3) 土壤及土層構造的調查 
(4) 岩體中裂隙、岩盤中斷層等的調查 
(5) 地下埋設物的調查(管線、樁) 
(6) 地基下或壩體中掏空的調查 
(7) 地下水位的深度及水分的變化調查 
(8) 混凝土構造物和道路品質的調查 
(9) 地下污染物的調查 
(10)河床、湖底地形的描繪 
(11)隧道工程之調查 
(12)鋼筋檢測 
(13)古蹟遺址探測

3-2-2  土壤飽和感測器 
由於含水量感測器在含水量改變時其電阻值雖也會同時改變，但電阻值和含
水量之間似無一穩定關係，故本計畫改以利用透水材料將水導入感測器內開發新
的感測器，其原理是利用透水材料製造水份可流通的孔隙，使在地下水位面以下
土層中的自由水得以流入所開發的感測器內形成電流通路，並在地下水位面下降
時，感測器內的水可同樣經由透水材料重新滲入土體內，由於感測器是利用土壤
達到飽和狀態後孔隙內所存在的自由水進行地下水位面的監測，故將感測器名稱
定為「土壤飽和感測器」，其設計示意圖如圖 3-2-2 所示。 
 
圖 3-2-2 土壤飽和感測器示意圖 
由於鑽孔儀器 GCO-Probe 最大直徑為 25mm，因此所製作的土壤飽和感測器
的最大寬度亦不能超過 25mm，在考量材料的經濟性、製作的便利性以及使感測
器具有一定的強度的條件下，本研究採用厚 2mm 的壓克力板作為感測器外殼的
材料(感測器內部尺寸為 2cm×1cm×0.8cm)，透水材料則選用三種不同的材料進行
試驗，分別是(a)石膏拌合真珠石、(b)透水石及(c)樹脂拌合有孔玻璃珠。 
石膏拌合真珠石是將石膏(水與石膏粉之重量比為 1:1)與過篩後的真珠石進
行拌合(通過標準篩號#10，停留標準篩號#20)，再填裝入利用壓克力板所製成的
中空矩形塊內；透水石則是利用車床將市售的透水石自行加工成所需之尺寸；樹
脂拌合玻璃珠則利用市售工藝品材料之有孔玻璃珠(外徑約 2mm)，與適量之 AB
膠進行拌合填裝至中空矩形壓克力塊待其硬固。利用三種透水材料製作完成的感
測器如照片 3-2-3 所示。

3-2-3  微型觀測管室內實驗 
在完成土壤飽和感測器後，本計畫利用土壤飽和感測器製作微型觀測管，將
所開發之土壤飽和感測器裝設在市售的電線壓條上，並利用燈號顯示感測器的狀
況，由於微型觀測管預定放置於邊坡上，考慮到電源設置的便利性以及燈泡的耐
久性，本計畫採用具有高耐震性、壽命長，同時耗電量少、發熱度小的 LED 燈
泡作為土壤飽和感測器狀態顯示之用。利用並聯電路將微型觀測管上不同埋設深
度的土壤飽和感測器及與其連接的 LED 燈和供電之電源加以連接後，即完成微
型觀測管。而後於室內進行砂箱試驗，檢覈微型觀測管的功能，室內實驗的設備
配置圖如圖 3-2-3 所示，首先在室內實驗製作兩支觀測管，觀測管的最上方為顯
示不同位置土壤飽和感測器的 LED 燈泡，1 號觀測管上感測器之間距為 10cm；
2 號觀測管上感測器的間距則為 5cm，而後將觀測管置入壓克力製的試驗砂箱中
(砂箱的尺寸為 45cm×30cm×60cm)並在砂箱內填入土壤，砂箱內所填土壤為取自
台南縣龍崎鄉崎頂村兵仔舍邊坡的地表土，填入之土樣以孔隙比進行控制，分別
設定最大孔隙比γd min與最小孔隙比γd max兩種情況進行實驗；利用相對密度實
驗所獲得之最小乾土單位重γd min=1.27 g/cm3 及最大乾土單位重γd max=1.38 
g/cm3，來計算砂箱中應填入之土樣重量。 
完成填土後的砂箱如照片 3-2-4，而後控制水位高度變化(最低 10cm，最高
55cm)，以 5cm 為一間距進行水位上升和下降的控制，並記錄水位升降時微型觀
測管 1 號和 2 號上 LED 燈號的顯示情形，照片 3-2-5 為砂箱開始注水以及水位
上升到最高位置的情形，詳細的實驗結果記錄如表 3-2-4 和表 3-2-5，由表中可知

實的反應，因此本計畫把此微型觀測管裝設在試驗邊坡進行現地實驗。

第七章 結果與討論 
本計畫利用簡單的電學原理以及容易取得的材料，成功利用三種不同材料開
發完成土壤飽和感測器，分別為石膏拌合真珠石、透水石及樹脂拌合玻璃珠，第
一種石膏拌合真珠石的成品強度過低，易在貫入土層內時損壞；第二種透水石則
價格較高；第三種樹脂拌合玻璃珠則兼具有強度及製作便利的特點，故最後選用
第三種材料製作土壤飽和感測器並與市售壓條結合，再利用 LED 燈泡作為顯示
裝置，完成可觀測土層內飽和層的微型觀測管，由室內實驗證明微型觀測管可準
確的反應出土層內的水位面。

1. INTRODUCTION 
In the foothill of western Taiwan, most rock strata 
belong to sedimentary rocks formed in Pliocene and 
Pleistocene.  As the function of lithification with particle 
cementation and perfect lock-up cannot be achieved due 
to short period of lithification age, the cementation of the 
rock is quite poor and susceptible to weathering, slaking 
and erosion. Based on the uniaxial compression strength, 
the rocks are classified as low to very low strength rock.  
The Taiwan High Speed Railway route passes through 
the foothill of western Taiwan.  Bridges and viaducts are 
the main structures in this project. Cast in site reinforced 
concrete bored piles are used as foundation supports for 
bridge and viaduct type structures.  Determination of the 
side resistance and end bearing of drilled shaft or bored 
pile in the poorly cemented rock was one of the 
important geotechnical topics. 
Several methods are available to estimate the bearing 
capacity of a pile.  Pile load test is the most accurate 
method to evaluate the pile bearing capacity[1]. In this 
study, one set with one instrumented compression (CP) 
and one tension (TP) pile load tests was conducted to 
investigate the he load transfer behavior and the magnitude 
of pile bearing capacity of the pile in the poorly cemented 
rock.   
2. SUBSURFACE CONDITIONS 
The site of pile load test was located at Shinchu County, 
northern Taiwan. The test area is near by the Shinchu 
Science Based Industrial Park, a high-tech industry 
center in Taiwan.  The major geomorphology feature of

area are Pleistocene Toukoshan Formation. In northern

ABSTRACT: There were few loading test cases for pile socketed into the poorly cemented rock in the world. In this study, one 
set of compression and tension instrumented pile loading tests was conducted to investigate the load transfer behavior of the 
reinforced bored pile in the poorly cemented rock.  The results show that the end bearing resistance and its related base settlement 
are significant. The end bearing of compression pile is linearly increased up to about 54% of the maximum test load.  The related 
base settlement and unit end bearing resistance is about 3.5D% and 10.5MPa respectively.  Besides, the pile ultimate unit skin 
friction in compression and in tension are in same range, i.e.,  about 90kPa to 103kPa.

In this study, the undrained ring shear test was 
conducted to investigate the slide phenomenon occurring 
in a soft sandstone slope after a rainfall. In past literature 
([8], [9], [14]), the pore water pressure and the material 
grain size of the specimen shear zone have been shown 
change under the shearing condition. Thus, the sliding-
surface liquefaction and the shear zone failure theory are 
discussed below. 
2.1. Sliding-surface liquefaction 
The “sliding-surface liquefaction” concept states that the 
grain size of the sliding-surface will be broken then 
liquefaction occurs when the slide occurs and the 
sandstone is sheared. It is proposed by Sassa(1996), 
when they studied earthquake induced slope slides using 
the undrained ring shear test. This concept is different 
from the definition of the traditional liquefaction. Figure 
1 illustrates the difference between the soil liquefaction 
and the sliding-surface liquefaction. 
According to both Figure 1(A) and 1(B), the stress path 
of the sandy material will move from the initial P0 to the 
steady state point when the pore water pressure increases. 
In the “Liquefaction” (Fig1.(A)), the stress path does not 
touch the peak failure line (P.F.L.) and the residual 
failure line (R.F.L.). On the contrary, no matter under 
loose or dense construction, the stress path of the 
“Sliding-surface liquefaction” touches the P.F.L. or 
R.F.L. and then arrives the steady state finally (Fig.1 
(B)). The difference between these two different 
liquefaction phenomena is shown in Fig.1. The 
“Liquefaction” usually takes in the loose sandy material 
structure and saturated water content. As such structure 
is shaken, the pore water pressure is generated to 
decrease the effective normal stress and result in 
structure destroys. Such destruction belongs to the whole 
structure destroy, and the sandy material particles are 
rearranged but not broken, so the stress path can get in to 
the steady state without touching failure lines. On the 
other hand, if the sandy material structure is dense, like 
the sandstone. When the sandstone is sheared, the shear 
zone will procedure the particle broken phenomenon, 
and then the volume change will generate the pore water 
pressure Δu to decrease the effective normal stress. This 
phenomenon is the same with the “Liquefaction”, but 
belongs to the local structure destroy. Finally, the 
“Sliding-surface liquefaction” happens when the stress 
state arrive the stable state.

2.2. Shear Zone 
To obtain the residual strength of soil or rock materials, 
laboratory tests usually hope test apparatus can provide a 
large shear displacement.  Taylor (1952) carried out the 
constant volume direct shear test to measure the shear 
strength on Boston Blue Clay. Test results showed that 
the undrained residual strength cannot get because of the 
limited continuous shear displacement allowed in single 
direction by the direct shear apparatus. Thus, the clay 
particles cannot rearrange along the shear direction to 
achieve the residual condition. Skempton and Petley 
(1967) observed the shear zone of the clay mineral and 
found that the clay particles will be arranged parallel to 
the sheared direction gradually by direct shear test. Stak 
and Eid (1992) introduced the use of a reversal shear test 
to obtain the residual strength of soil. But the residual 
condition is unable achieve, because of the same reason 
above. Agung et al,(2004) took ring shear test in sandy 
material pointed out the shear zone of the material can 
divide into three stages(Fig.2).

Before carrying out the ring shear test, physical test; 
permeability test, unconfined pressure test and slaking 
test will be proceed to investigate the essential properties 
of Kuan-Miao sandstone. Test results show that Kuan-
Miao sandstone is a porous and high permeable material. 
Its void ratio is 0.39 and the coefficient of permeability 
is 5.9*10-5 (cm/sec). Moreover, the uniaxial compressive 
strength of the aired-dry specimen is 1.98 MPa and the 
saturated one is 0.36 MPa. The slaking index (Id1) is 0%. 
Therefore, Kuan-Miao sandstone is extremely sensitive 
to the water and is classified to be a poorly cemented 
and low durability rock by Gamble (1971).  
4.2. Prepare Specimen 
Generally, using the water-drilled method to take rock 
specimens, but in this study because Kuan-Miao 
sandstone will be collapsed by water, so we use the air-
drilled method to take specimens. The step of this 
method is firstly drills the inner diameter of the ring 
shape specimen by 11.2 cm drill and then drills the outer 
diameter by 14.8 cm drill. Finally, use sandpapers to 
adjust the specimen size to satisfy test needed.  
In order to avoid the plate effect and ensure the shear 
stress can distribute uniform when specimens sheared. 
The inner radius, the outer radius and height of samples 
must be limited. In this study, the size of the ring shape 
specimen are inner radius is 5.6 cm; outer radius is 7.4 
cm and the height is 8.0 cm respectively. All these three 
conditions can satisfy the formulation presented by 
Saada (1981).

4.3. Undrained ring shear test 
Firstly, the sandstone specimen is set in the shear box 
with epoxy, and then put the Teflon plate and O-rings 
down on the shear surface as show in Fig.6. After 
waiting for 24 hrs, we replace the air by CO2 in the 
specimen. Secondary, the aired-water will be pumped 
into the specimen from valves located at the lower shear 
box to saturate the specimen. Then, observing the water 
outflow and inflow of the shear box, if the values equal 
to each other, the specimen is defined to be saturated. 
Thirdly, close water valves, open the volume strain tube, 
and apply certain normal force on the specimen to 
consolidate the sample at the same time. When 
consolidation is completed, turn on the serve motor to 
shear the specimen for 24 hrs with the shearing speed of 
1.5mm/min. Subsequently, the shear resistance, and the 
variations of the water pressure, and the specimen height 
are observed.
