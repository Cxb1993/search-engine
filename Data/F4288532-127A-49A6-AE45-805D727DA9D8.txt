Abstract 
A new triphenylamine-containing AB2 type monomer with one carboxylic acid and two amino 
groups,4-(5-(4-(bis(4-aminophenyl)amino)phenyl)-1,3,4-oxadiazol-2-yl)benzoic acid (3), was 
synthesized and used for the preparation of hyperbranched poly(triphenylamine-1,3,4-oxadiazole 
amide)s. The self-polycondensation of the AB2 monomer (3) afforded a hyperbranched 
poly(triphenylamine amide) with amino end groups. The molecular weight of the hyperbranched 
was 20,146 Da determined by Gel permeation chromatography. End-capped hyperbranched 
polyamides were isolated by the chemical modification of unreacted amino groups with various 
acid chlorides. Subsequently, a hyperbranched poly(triphenylamine oxadiazole amide) having 
approximately a 62% degree of branching (DB), as determined by a combination of model 
compound studies and 1H NMR, was obtained. All the end-capped hyperbranched 
poly(triphenylamine oxadiazole amide)s exhibit excellent solubility in organic solvents such as 
NMP, DMF, DMSO, and DMAc at room temperature. 
 
Keywords: Polyamide, Hyperbranched, Triphenylamine, 1,3,4-Oxadiazole

Introduction 
      Dendritic polymers have received considerable attention due to their unique chemical and 
physical properties [1–7]. Hyperbranched polymers show similar properties to those dendrimers, 
such as low viscosity, high solubility in organic solvents, and lack of significant entanglement in 
the solid state. However, hyperbranched polymers are readily synthesized by one-step 
polymerization of ABn monomers [1–4]. This is advantageous over the multi-step synthesis of 
dendrimers because of the rapid production in large quantities. It is also well known that the 
properties of the dendritic polymers depend on terminal functional groups in addition to 
backbone structure [1–7]. Therefore, the properties of dendritic polymers can be tuned to some 
extent by the chemical modification of end functional groups [1–7]. On the other hand, wholly
