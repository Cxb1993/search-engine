英文摘要 
In this project a new elastic-wave-based NDT method combing the 
time-frequency domain analysis and the synthetic aperture focusing technique, called 
SAFT, was developed. It can reveal the defects inside the concrete elements with 
resultant image. With the help of developed process method in time-frequency 
domain, the reflected signals from defects can be enhanced and thus higher quality 
resultant image that can disclose the information of defects was produced. The 
outcome of this project can expand the application scope of the elastic-wave-based 
imaging method in the field of civil NDT. In the whole research, a finite difference 
program was used as the numerical simulation tool and some experiments on concrete 
specimens were used to verify the findings. In the first year the SAFT imaging 
method was improved by the band-pass filtering and reconstructing process. 
Hilber-Huang Transform, called HHT, was used to transform the time domain signals 
into time-frequency ones. The resultant images show good improvement in resolution 
and contrast. In the second year the SAFT imaging method was improved by 
changing the time-amplitude signals with the time-frequency ones as the input traces. 
The computation cost is reduced owing to the removal of the band-pass filtering and 
reconstructing process. However, the image quality is still good enough. In the third 
year the research focused on the feasibility evaluation of the SAFT method applying 
on the reinforced concrete elements. The result about the correlation between the 
image quality and the wavelength-to-rebar-diameter ratio could be a guideline for 
selecting a proper wave source when applying the SAFT method to image the defects 
inside reinforced concrete elements. Not only the numerical simulations but also the 
experimental results show good result to reveal the defects inside the reinforced 
concrete specimens when the wave source was properly selected. The result of this 
project could be very helpful as a reference of future research about civil NDT 
technologies and also the in-situ applications.

1.2.1 應力波檢測技術 
    超音波(Ultrasound)是一種頻率高於 20kHz 之應力波，可透過各種介質(固、
液、氣)進行傳播。通常超音波在均質的介質中行進時，會維持固定的波速，但
當遇到不同介質時，就會因不同的聲阻抗(Acoustic Impendence)而產生反射或折
射現象，就此可用以檢測結構物內部的不連續現象。早在 1940 年代超音波便開
始用於檢測缺陷，而根據超音波在行進過程中遇到異質缺陷時所產生的反射、
折射或是繞射現象從而衍生出許多不同檢測方法，如衛星波法 (Satellite 
Pulse)
[2]、波行時間繞射法(Time-of-Flight Diffraction, TOFD)[3,4]、複式波觀察法
(Multiple Mode Observation)…等。 
    然而超音波法使用於土木工程領域時，在混凝土材料上會有應用波源能量
不足的嚴重問題。於是，在 1983 年時美國國家標準及科研院(National Institute of 
Standards and Technology)及康乃爾大學(Cornell University)資助下，由 Carino 及
Sansalone 博士研究發展敲擊回音法(Impact-Echo Method)[5]，與超音波法同樣是
利用應力波動原理，但其改變了應力波激發源為機械性的敲擊方式，接收器改
成由點接觸之位移訊號接收器改善了以往超音波法應用於混凝土結構物時能量
衰減嚴重之問題，此一問題的改善大大增加了結構物可檢測的深度及範圍，且
此一方法的提出也為土木工程的非破壞檢測領域帶來了一個新的方向。而此一
技術在 1990 年後更多方的運用於混凝土結構物各項性質檢測，例如：混凝土內
部裂縫及空隙、夯實不良混凝土、預力套管灌漿不良所形成之孔隙…等[6~12]。 
    1995 年 Wu 等人[13,14]利用暫態彈性波理論，發表點波源-點接收器為基礎之
波速量測方法使其能夠在現地混凝土結構物單一表面進行量測，即可準確求得
待測試體之彈性常數。此外，利用物理學中的 Fermat 最短走時定理結合裂縫尖
端繞射波的時間訊號量測，亦可求得混凝土開口表面裂縫深度，若採用兩組訊
號則可以利用雙橢圓交線定理進一步求得裂縫尖端的座標及角度[15]。表面波頻
譜分析法(Spectral Analysis of Surface Wave, SASW)，利用量測彈性波在層狀材
料上傳播之訊號所求得的頻散曲線，常用於地層或道路鋪面的檢測，在一般混
凝土結構中，常因邊界反射造成訊號的干擾，影響量測結果頻譜解析度[16]。2002
年 K. Mori 等人發展一新的非接觸(non-contact)非破壞檢測技術，其原理主要是
運用一可發射衝擊波的機械距混凝土結構物表面一定距離施加衝擊力，再使用
雷射震動計量測該試體的表面速度並結合敲擊回法的分析概念進行檢測，其非
接觸式的檢測方式對於欲檢測難以接觸的結構物時相當便利，但由於輸出能量
的關係也會使其能檢測範圍較為侷限[17]。2005 年 Andrew J. Boyd 等人運用敲擊
回音法進行表面縱波波速與混凝土內部縱波波速的波速比較[18]。2006年Markus 
KRUGER 提出以能量衰減來檢測裂縫深度，此法雖然可以避免波到時間判定的
問題，但因混凝土屬非均質材料不同的混凝土結構衰減能量也不相同[19]。以上
所述各種不同應用的應力波檢測方法，均需經由有經驗的檢測人員加以判定結
果，並以特定的方法進行分析，對於現地工程人員而言，其檢測結果顯得較為
不直觀，也難以了解。因此，許多的專家學者便開始朝向結合影像技術利用影
像將檢測結果作呈現，使檢測結果能夠更為直觀地進行檢視與探討。

2.2.1 希爾伯特-黃轉換(Hilbert-Huang Transform, HHT)原理 
希爾伯特-黃轉換(Hilbert-Hung transform, HHT)是由黃諤院士於 1998 年所
提出[49~50]，其最大的貢獻即是可分析非穩態以及非線性訊號，有別以往傳統的
傅立葉轉換只能分析穩態以及線性之訊號。而希爾伯特-黃轉換主要包含了兩個
部份：經驗模態分解(Empirical Mode Decomposition, EMD)以及希爾伯特轉換
(Hilbert transform, HT)。經驗模態分解可將一複雜的訊號分解成數個內建模態函
數(Intrinsic Mode Function, IMF)，其分解步驟如下： 
1 定義原始訊號 x(t)之最大值包絡線以及最小值包絡線，並求取兩包絡線之
平均得出均值包絡線 m(t)。 
2 將原始訊號 x(t)以及 m(t)相減得出一分量 h(t)。 
3 重複步驟 1 以及步驟 2 直到 h(t)滿足內建模態函數之條件，則可得 c1(t)為
h(t)，c1(t)即為第一個內建模態函數(IMF)。 
4 將原始訊號 x(t)減去 c1(t)後可得出一剩餘的訊號 r1(t)。 
5 將 r1(t)當作新的資料，重複步驟 1 ~ 4 即可得出新的剩餘量 r2(t)，如此重覆
n 次。 
當第 n 個剩餘量 rn(t)已成為單調函數(monotonic function)，將無法再分解
IMF 時，整個 EMD 的分解過程完成。因此，原始訊號可以表示成為下列式子：

2.2.2 帶通濾波合成法 
    帶通濾波合成法的分析方式，主要是將位移訊號(如圖 2.2.3)經由 HHT 處
理，將位移-時間域的訊號轉換至頻率時間域，即含有時間-頻率-能量相互關係
的空間。而後，將反射波型假設為半個 sin 波，並取其Δt 再利用公式(2.2.7)計
算所需之暫態彈性波的中心頻率。然而在中心頻率的取得上，若單純以計算所
得的單一頻率為中心頻率值會容易造成反射波能量曲線選取產生誤差，使取得
之能量曲線非屬缺陷中心頻率上之能量曲線。此一誤判將會造成取得之能量曲
線中反射波的能量不足，對後續影像合成後之結果有極大的影響。為避免此一
現象，本法結合帶通濾波(Band-Pass Filter)的概念，將上述所求得之中心頻率值
做為基準，加減一Δf 成為一頻率寬帶後取一頻寬帶如圖 2.2.4，再將此一頻寬
帶中之能量寬帶加以平均後取得其能量曲線，避免以單一頻率代表中心頻率所
取得能量曲線錯誤的情況發生，如圖 2.2.5。 
    此法為了消除計算中心頻率上的誤差須取一頻率寬帶進行平均，然而其Δf
之大小取決便顯得相當重要。因此，本研究也針對此一部份進行深入探討。在
經過不同的Δf 比較後，發現當Δf 在 2kHz~3kHz 之間為最佳，一但低於此範
圍會使得展顯缺陷特徵之能量明顯不足，可能無法完整突顯缺陷影像；而高於
此範圍則會導致其他反射訊號的能量產生，進一步影響缺陷影像的辨識。

乃由於應力波傳遞時波行路徑不同所造成。在沒有鋼筋存在情況下，P 波的波

無鋼筋相同，實際缺陷影像可經由此路徑計算後獲得，而第二種則是 P 波行經
鋼筋時會先造成反射至表面後再往下傳遞，也因此會造成時間的延遲，由於時
間的延遲在圖像上便會形成實際缺陷影像下方會有假缺陷影像的存在。所幸，
根據彈性波基本特性來判讀，此類亮帶很容易被歸類為假缺陷。因為該亮帶正

抵達假缺陷面，再傳回接收器。縱使有少量繞射波還能由真正缺陷邊緣繞過，

第5章  結論與建議 
    本研究為三年期計畫，主要工作是以暫態彈性波為基礎，結合時頻域訊號分
析技術與合成聚焦影像法，進行混凝土缺陷成像技術之研究。並以時頻域訊號分
析結果，發展適當的訊號處理方法，藉以凸顯缺陷反射訊號，進而獲得更佳品質
的混凝土結構缺陷成像，以提升土木非破壞檢測之實用範圍。 
    本研究第一年，利用 HHT 法對於非穩態及非線性訊號的時頻分析優勢，發
展帶通濾波法，將接收的振幅-時間訊號轉換成能量-時間訊號，進而完成合成
聚焦成像。在數值模擬及實驗結果，皆顯示該技術可有效改善暫態彈性波合成
聚焦顯像法所形成之影像品質，不但消除影響缺陷判斷的鬼影之外，亦提升試
體淺層檢測範圍。然而，研究中發現，帶通濾波合成法之頻率範圍選定，會明
顯影響成像品質，而頻率範圍選定並不夠直覺，因而需進一步訂定可靠的頻率
範圍選取機制，方能施用於現地試體檢測。 
    計畫第二年，本研究改以瞬時頻率的依時改變(時間-主頻率曲線)為基礎，
進行合成聚焦影像改良。在數值模擬與實驗結果均顯示，該方法可有效改善彈
性波合成聚焦影像品質。該方法除具備帶通濾波法優點-提升影像解析度、去除
鬼影、增加檢測範圍等，並減少帶通濾波法之頻率寬帶分析程序，使訊號處理
方式更便利，而適用於現地檢測。上述成像技術，突破傳統利用能量譜進行成
像之思維，其成果已刊登於 IEEE Transactions on Ultrasonics, Ferroelectrics, and 
Frequency Control 之國際知名期刊。 
    計畫第三年，針對混凝土試體中，鋼筋的存在對彈性波合成聚焦成像影響。
因為帶過濾合成法及時間-主頻率曲線法，對鋼筋的反射訊號過於敏感，反而不
適合應用於鋼筋混凝土之缺陷成像。此部分改成以時間-速度曲線進行成像，探
討鋼筋存在對合成聚焦成像的影響。由數值模擬及試體實驗結果得知，彈性波
波長對於檢測結果有極大的影響。當使用較短波長之彈性波進行檢測時，來自
鋼筋的大量反射將使成像過亂而無法判讀缺陷所在；反之，提升彈性波波長時，
的確可藉由合成聚焦成像法，成功的顯像鋼筋混凝土缺陷資訊。此結果在現有
彈性波為基礎之非破壞檢測研究中，可視為一突破性之發展。主持人已將該研
究成果，投稿至 NDT&E International 國際期刊。值得注意的是，在不同波長的
影像結果中亦可發現，採用大波長之敲擊源檢測，雖然可以降低鋼筋的影響，
但亦會降低成像解析度。因此，如何選定適當的波源進行檢測，將是後續所需
持續進行的相關研究。

木非破壞檢測技術之跨領域開發應用之種子人才。

according to the elastic wave propagation theory, the

different acoustic impedances. The reflection point can be

the transducer. In the general case, however, there are so 
many reflections from boundaries and defects that deter-

difficult. also, the dimensions of a defect cannot be ob-
tained from a single response signal. In the saFT-imaging 
process, geometric information about a defect can be con-
structed not only from one batch of the response signals 
but from all of them. The image intensity I corresponding 
to each cell is determined by summing the amplitudes of 
all the tests at the time ti and then taking the average 
value. The time ti is taken as the theoretical traveling time 
for the longitudinal wave to propagate from an impacting 
source to the target cell and then back to a receiver. In 
the summation, the intensity of the cells associated with 
defects or interfaces will be enhanced whereas that of the 
cells associated with uniform matrix will be reduced be-
cause of cross-interference. The next step of the process 
is to map the intensity value of each cell to pixels with 
corresponding brightness on the grayscale image. after 
the image processing, defects and interfaces embedded in 
matrix material can be exposed. In this paper, high im-
age intensity associated with defects and interfaces will be 
displayed as bright white, whereas low intensity associated 
with uniform matrix will be displayed as dark black. The 
resultant image will be similar to that scanned with a 
phased array system.
The significance of saFT-imaging method developed in 
this paper is to enhance the snr of received signals and 
then transform them into a visualized result. When com-
pared with conventional a-scan or b-scan technologies 
which can be obtained by simply using the point-source/
receiver technique, the saFT-imaging method could pro-
vide more useful information about defects including loca-
tion, front-end shape, and size, so the influence of defects 
upon a concrete structure could be estimated more accu-
rately. It is worth noting that it needs little effort to trans-
form a conventional b-scan diagram into a saFT image. 
When evaluating the influence of defects upon concrete 
structures, this technique to image the defect would be 
more useful than simply using the point-source/receiver 
technique to find the damage without trying to create an 
image.
III. Time-Frequency curve
The Hilbert-Hung transform (HHT) is an effective 
method for decomposing a signal into so-called intrinsic 
mode functions (ImF) and obtaining instantaneous fre-
quency data. It uses the empirical mode decomposition 
(Emd) method to decompose a signal into intrinsic mode 
functions, and uses the Hilbert spectral analysis (Hsa) 
method to obtain instantaneous frequency data. It is de-
signed to work well for data that are nonstationary and

assume the first impact S1 is applied at the point which

In this paper, a new elastic-wave-based NDT system was developed to reveal the defects inside 
concrete structures by imaging. It integrates the point-source/point-receiver scheme with the 
SAFT method to achieve results like scanned with a phase array system; hence it is equipped 
with large functioning depth because of high-energy feature that transient elastic wave method 
possessed over traditional ultrasonic method. To evaluate the feasibility of this system on NDT 
for concrete structures, a concrete slab with artificial void inside were built for experiment. The 
2D plane stress FEM numerical simulations were performed for further comparison. 
Experimental result shows good agreement with the numerical result not only on the B-scan 
diagram but also on the processed scanning image. The elastic-wave-based NDT system 
proposed in this paper exhibits high potential in inspecting the defects of in-situ concrete 
structures by imaging for civil engineering applications.

1 Introduction 
Among the present civil NDT technologies, 
the elastic-wave-based application always 
plays a very important role. The point-
source/point-receiver scheme is especially 
suitable for the inspection of on-site civil 
infrastructures (Wu 1995, Tong 2002, Liao 
2006, Tong 2007). It overcomes the limitation 
of transmission distance caused by the nature 
of the traditional ultrasonic probing, which is 
featured by low output power. However, when 
it is applied to detect the defects inside the 
tested concrete structures, especially with 
complex boundary conditions or multiple 
inclusions, successful results are usually hard 
to obtain with analyzing a single batch of 
response signals. For metallic materials the 
synthetic aperture focusing technique (SAFT) 
is often used as a signal processing strategy in 
ultrasonic testing as a NDT method, good 
results of high S/N ratio can be obtained as 
those obtained with the phase array system 
(Doctor 1986, Wasazak 1990). In 2007, Tong 
et al. proposed a new NDT method, which 
combines the point-source/point-receiver 
scheme and SAFT method, to image the 
defects inside the concrete structure (Tong 
2007). It could be a practical and effective 
NDT method for defect inspection on civil 
infrastructures. However, it is indispensable to 
develop a new NDT system for in-situ 
applications basing on the new method. In this

3 Composition of System  
As shown in Fig. 5, the system is mainly 
composed of an impact source generator, a 
vertical displacement transducer, a signal 
capturing unit and an operation software. The 
transient elastic wave is first generated by a 
point impact on the surface of the specimen, 
and then propagates inside the material. The 
transducer converts the surface response into 
an electrical signal. The signal capturing unit 
amplifies the small signal and converts the 
analog signal into digital data for further 
process. The operation software controls all 
the measurement procedure and finally 
develops the resultant image.

4 Experimental Results 
To evaluate the feasibility of the new system 
developed in this paper, a defective concrete 
slab, whose thickness is 3cm, was cased for 
experiment as shown in Fig. 2.  A horizontal 
void is placed at the center of the block. The 
experiment was then carried out with a series 
of impact-and-receive operations on the top 
surface of the concrete slab. The distance 
between the impacting source and the receiver 
is always set to be 5cm and the distance to 
move from the current impacting point to the 
next one is 5cm. The first measurement was 
made at 30cm from the side surface of the 
block. The B-scan diagram shown in Fig. 8 is 
the 13 displacement traces recorded when the 
concrete slab is impacted by a falling steel 
ball of 8mm in diameter. The reflections from 
the surface of the void can be found in the B-
scan diagram. Using the image processing 
scheme to process the 13 traces in Fig. 8, a 
scan-like picture is shown in Fig. 9. It can be 
seen that a bright zone appears at the place of 
the void. Another bright strip corresponding to 
the bottom boundary of the specimen can also 
be found beneath the defect. As compared to 
the traditional B-scan diagram, this method 
can provide better results to identify the 
number of the defects and their locations, 
sizes, and front-end shapes. The experimental 
result shows good agreement with numerical 
simulation shown before. 
 
5 Conclusions 
A well developed instrument is indispensible 
for a new NDT method when it is applied to 
in-situ concrete structures inspection. The 
state-of-the-art technologies could provide 
enough assistance to the development of the 
instrument on reducing the size and 
simplifying the operation procedure. In this 
paper, integrating the point-source/point-
receiver scheme with SAFT process, a brand 
new NDT system for defects imaging was 
developed. It combines the interdisciplinary 
technologies, including mechanics, electronics, 
computer science and so on, to form a 
portable instrument. This system can expose 
the defect embedded inside the concrete 
structures immediately after a series of 
impact-and-receive operations. The 
experimental result inspected by this system 
shows very good agreement with the result 
simulated by FEM method not only on the B-
scan diagram but also on the SAFT image. 
The location, size, and front-end shape of the 
defect can be clearly identified from the image. 
It shows great potential of this new NDT 
system on detecting the defects of the in-situ 
concrete infrastructures.

Mobile wireless technologies provide wireless information transport solutions that

technologies that have been implemented in the real-time bridge scouring monitoring

stability of our approach and section 5 concludes the work. 
 
 
COMPOSITION OF SYSTEM 
 
The scour monitoring system proposed in this paper is shown in Fig. 1. In the 
system, a steel tube containing 16 sensor nodes at an equal distance of 0.5 m is installed 
into the river bed by drilling a hole on the downstream side. Each sensor has a wire used 
to activate its corresponding digital switch contained in a switch box under scouring. A 
control unit located on the top portion of the bridge pier is used to detect the on/off 
status of the digital switch for monitoring the sensor status.  
All the sensors are initially covered by soil and all their corresponding switches are 
off. When a flood induces scouring, the sensors above the scouring surface vibrate due 
to water flow and they turn on their corresponding switches, which can be detected by 
the control unit. The scouring depth is obtained by monitoring the change of switches’ 
status. The mobile wireless technology is used to instantly report the scoring 
information to a remote station.  
 
Control unit 
 
The system control unit consists of computer and monitoring software. To get rapid 
monitoring data, we use laptops, smart-phone or PDA with WiFi communicable device 
in field monitoring, while in remote control monitoring, we use desktops or laptops 
instead. Monitoring software comprises Graphical User Interface (GUI), as shown in 
Fig. 3, controlled by mouse and touch panel with the setting of argument. It may also 
record monitoring data with time history diagram for long term.

Networking 
 
Generally, the mobile telecommunication operators have offered several alternative 
mobile wireless network solutions to support long distances wireless communications, 
such as, 3G (3rd-generation), HSDPA (High-Speed Downlink Packet Access) and 
GPRS (General Packet Radio Service). On the other hand, Wireless Local Area 
Networks (WLANs) provide wireless network communication over short distances 
using radio signals instead of traditional network cabling (Ethernet). Both mobile 
wireless networks and WLANs have provided the significant broadband capability in 
different methods. In describing our real-time bridge scouring safety monitoring 
solution, we observe that the improvement of both reliability and efficiency of wireless 
communications is necessary. In order to achieve the long distances of wireless 
communications, a 3G network card has been deployed on our system that enabled the 
TCP/IP communication between the bridge monitoring system and the remote control 
unit. However, there were several challenges regarding the mobile wireless networks. 
The 3G network was designed for broadband data and voice users and not for 
application awareness or the need to support the remote real-time control for the bridge 
monitoring system. To overcome some of these shortcomings, one approach 
considered from our case study is a capability for hand-over with several wireless 
network interfaces. Technologies such as ad-hoc and multi-hop wireless networks were 
also considered to extend the coverage of the wireless transmission range. For example, 
if the primary mobile wireless network is unable to allocate a data channel, perhaps 
because of an outage or all channels consumed, then the device is able to transmit over 
an alternative WLANs. The alternative WLANs can provides the short range of 
wireless communications and be able to extend as ad-hoc and multi-hop wireless 
networks by using the proper wireless routing protocols.
