中文摘要 
本計畫為氧化鎂鋅薄膜應用於深紫外光電元件之特性研究三年期之研究計畫，主要是研
究利用磁控式射頻共濺鍍系統沉積氧化鎂鋅(MgxZn1-xO)薄膜之特性，並研究於不同薄膜沉積
條件之特性，並藉由摻雜不同的鎂含量(x=0~x=0.36)於氧化鎂鋅之中，藉以調變氧化鎂鋅薄膜
的能隙(3.37eV~4.25eV)，使其可偵測或發光波長由 376nm 分佈至 291nm，以達要深紫外光波
段之要求，再將其應用於紫外光波段之光電元件如光檢測器及發光二極體，另一部分研究是
將氧化鎂鋅薄膜應用於平面顯示器之透明薄膜電晶體。 
利用 X 光繞射分析不同鎂含量之氧化鎂鋅薄膜及晶格結構為六角柱狀鎢彩結構，製作氧
化鎂鋅紫外光檢測器，因氧化鎂鋅為一寬能隙的材料，所以所製作之光檢測器具有相當低的
暗電流，元件在偏壓 20V 時，其元件之暗電流皆小於 0.6nA，可偵測之光波長會隨著鎂含量
之增加而有往短波長偏移之現象，氧化鎂鋅薄膜之鎂含量由 0 調變至 0.36 時，其光檢測器之
截止波長由 380nm 調變至 310nm，其光拒斥比由 769 變至 701，由此部份之研究成果可以說
明利用射頻磁控式濺鍍系統所製作之氧化鎂鋅光檢測器，可容易利用調變鎂的含量即可控制
紫外光檢測器之檢測波段。 
利用氧化鎂鋅薄膜製作發光二極體，n 型氧化鎂鋅(x=0~0.3)/p 型氮化鎵與 n 型氧化鎂鋅
(x=0~0.3)/i 型氧化鎂鋅(x=0~0.3)/p 型氮化鎵發光二極體，i型及 n型之氧化鎂鋅之鎂含量皆相
同。由電激發光的量測結果顯示，n 型氧化鎂鋅(x=0~0.3)/p 型氮化鎵之四個發光二極體元件
的電激發光量測結果明顯發現其發光波段皆在 430nm 左右，並非隨著氧化鎂鋅的鎂含量而改
變，此部分最主要的原因是因為 n 型氧化鎂鋅(x=0~0.3)的電子濃度皆大於 1019cm-3，而 p 型
氮化鎵的電洞濃度僅有 5×1017cm-3，所以當 n 型氧化鎂鋅(x=0~0.3)沉積於 p 型氮化鎵之上，
兩者接面所形成的空乏區坐落在 p 型氮化鎵之上，所以 n 型氧化鎂鋅(x=0~0.3)/p 型氮化鎵結
構之四個發光二極體元件的發光波段皆為 p 型氮化鎵的發光波長 430nm。n 型氧化鎂鋅
(x=0~0.3)/i 型氧化鎂鋅(x=0~0.3)/p 型氮化鎵之四個發光二極體元件的電激發光量測結果明顯
發現其發光波段會隨著氧化鎂鋅之不同鎂含量變大時，其發光波長往短波長漂移，此部分與
結構一的不同之處是在於 n 型氧化鎂鋅(x=0~0.3)及 p 型氮化鎵之間再加入一層 i 型氧化鎂鋅
(x=0~0.3)，因 i 型氧化鎂鋅(x=0~0.3)的電子濃度約為 1013cm-3，所以所有空乏區皆落在 i 型氧
化鎂鋅層，故其發光波長會隨著鎂含量增加而往短波長漂移。 
本計畫主要是製作透明薄膜電晶體，從電極、通道層及絕緣層皆使用寬能隙材料，皆由
磁控式射頻共濺鍍系統所沉積。本計畫元件使用通道寬度為 100μm，通道長度為 10μm，其中
通道層使用氧化鋅摻雜微量鋁金屬，提昇元件的載子遷移率達到 9.1 cm2/V-s，接著將通道層
減薄，減少壓降得浪費且可以減少熱效應的產生，使得元件特性改善，本計畫元件載子遷移
率達到 12.5 cm2/V-s。並利用鎂含量為 0.1 之氧化鎂鋅薄膜作為薄膜電晶體之通道層，此電晶
體元件具有極佳的等效載子移動率約為 161cm2/V-s，且元件之電流開關比達 108，由此元件的
特性表現，其可應用於主動式平面顯示器，作為開關元件。

四、 研究方法 
1、氧化鎂鋅薄膜之材料分析[31-34] 
本研究利用磁控式射頻濺鍍系統，在室溫環境下沉積不同鎂含量(x=0、0.1、0.2、0.3 與
0.36)之氧化鎂鋅薄膜於藍寶石基板上，藍寶石基板以丙酮、甲醇及去離子水各震洗五分鐘，
背景真空度控制在 4×10-6 Torr 以下，使用氬氣作為濺鍍反應氣體，氬氣流量為 10sccm，且通
入不同氧流量 0~10sccm，濺鍍壓力控制在 10mtorr，射頻濺鍍功率為 100W，靶材與藍寶石基
板間隔 70mm，薄膜厚度為 200nm。沉積完成的試片利用 X 光繞射分析儀(XRD)量測氧化鎂
鋅薄膜結晶品質，並量測薄膜之穿透率及計算其光學能隙。 
圖 1 為不同鎂含量之氧化鎂鋅薄膜，在不同氧氣流量下最佳條下之 X 光繞射圖，分析其
結果得到在 x=0、0.1、0.2、0.3 及 0.36 鎂濃度下最佳濺鍍氧氣流量比分別為 4、5、6、8 及 10sccm。
由圖顯示在未摻雜鎂的氧化鋅有其良好的(002)繞射強度，表示結晶為六方晶形的鎢彩結構。
當鎂含量增加至(x=0.36)時，薄膜結晶仍維持單一(002)繞射方向，將有無通氧氣流量之不同鎂
含量的氧化鎂鋅薄膜的(002)晶向的強度作圖於圖 1 中，明顯發現有通入氧氣所沉積的不同鎂
含量之氧化鎂鋅薄膜(002)繞射強度皆有明顯增強。從 X 光繞射圖觀察，鎂含量增加則(002)
繞射峰角度往高角度移動，由圖 2 觀察出不同鎂含量(x=0~0.36)氧化鎂鋅薄膜繞射峰值角度從

從以上趨勢說明我們成功製備出不同比例鎂含量的氧化鎂鋅薄膜，也由此可證實氧化鎂掺入
氧化鋅的確可改變能隙值，使薄膜吸收波長朝向更低紫外光波段。

(2) 共濺鍍氧化鎂鋅:鋁薄膜特性分析 
由熱處理氧化鎂鋅薄膜之研究結果顯示，雖然適當熱處理確實能有效提升薄膜載子濃
度，並且改善原子排列情形與晶格結構，但導電特性並未能達到理想之目標，故再進一步採
用鋁原子摻雜的方式，由於鋁原子具有三個價電子，若取代二價的鋅原子，即會成為施體
(donor)原子的角色，提升載子濃度[38]，故可提高薄膜導電特性。本研究利用氧化鎂鋅(x=0~0.3)
靶材分別與鋁靶進行共濺鍍；濺鍍條件為固定條件為氬氣流量 10sccm，濺鍍壓力 10mtorr 及
氧化鎂鋅(x=0~0.3)濺鍍功率 100W。其中氧化鎂鋅(x=0)而言則鋁靶濺鍍功率由 15W~25W、氧
化鎂鋅(x=0.1)鋁靶濺鍍功率為 20W~30W、氧化鎂鋅(x=0.2)鋁靶濺鍍功率為 30W~40W、氧化
鎂鋅(x=0.3)鋁靶濺鍍功率為 30W~40W，由此方式得到各組最佳導電特性之氧化鎂鋅:鋁薄
膜。其結果與討論如下: 
圖 8 至圖 11 分別為不同鎂含量之氧化鎂鋅薄膜與不同鋁靶濺鍍功率之電特性分析；固定
條件為氬氣流量 10sccm，濺鍍壓力 10mtorr 及氧化鎂鋅濺鍍功率 100W。。由一般文獻可得
知電阻率與載子移動率及載子濃度呈  ρ-1 =nqμ(q 為電荷量)關係，故影響電阻率的因素主要
有載子移動率與載子濃度。如圖 8 所示為氧化鎂鋅(x=0)與不同鋁靶濺鍍功率關係圖，當鋁靶
濺鍍功率上升時，薄膜載子濃度也有大幅上升的趨勢，薄膜電阻率則呈現下降，當鋁靶濺鍍
功率為 20W 時薄膜具有最低電阻率 1.55×10-3 Ω cm，這是由於以共濺鍍方式時，有較多鋁原

鋅薄膜載子濃度，進而使得薄膜電阻率可以有效降低[39]。當鋁靶濺鍍功率達到 25W 時電阻
率則又上升，由圖中得知在鋁靶濺鍍功率為 20W 上升至 25W 時載子濃度方面並未有明顯的
變化，因此本研究則近一步觀察其薄膜載子移動率，當鋁靶濺鍍功率由 20W 上升至 25W 時
移動率也由 13.53 cm2/V-s 降至 3.34 cm2/V-s。由一般文獻可得知理論上鋁靶濺鍍功率提升，薄
膜導電特性應越高，因為可提供載子來源增加，但過多的鋁原子則造成在晶界析出，不但無

二為 n 型氧化鎂鋅(x=0~0.3)/i 型氧化鎂鋅(x=0~0.3)/p 型氮化鎵之四個發光二極體元件的電激
發光量測結果明顯發現其發光波段會隨著氧化鎂鋅之不同鎂含量而有所改變，當鎂含量變大
時，其發光波長往短波長漂移，此部分與結構一的不同之處是在於 n 型氧化鎂鋅(x=0~0.3)及
p 型氮化鎵之間再加入一層 i 型氧化鎂鋅(x=0~0.3)，因 i 型氧化鎂鋅(x=0~0.3)的電子濃度約為
1013cm-3，所以所有空乏區皆落在 i 型氧化鎂鋅層，故其發光波長會隨著鎂含量增加而往短波
長漂移。

五、 結果與討論 
本計畫利用磁控式射頻濺鍍系統沉積不同鎂含量之氧化鎂鋅薄膜，沉積過程中通入不同
氧氣流量比，研究結果在鎂含量 x=0、0.1、0.2、0.3 及 0.36 下最佳濺鍍氧氣流量比分別為 4、
5、6、8 及 10sccm，薄膜表面平整度分別為 1.58、1.51、1.42、1.37 及 1.09nm，由於氧氣流
量比隨鎂含量增加而提高，可降低氧空缺存在，提升薄膜結晶品質，使得薄膜形成較為緊密、
晶粒較小且均勻的表面，進而薄膜表面平整度趨於平整，則氧化鎂鋅薄膜在可見光穿透率達
90%。 
經實驗結果顯示有氧氣分流量比光檢測器(MSM-B)，在 10V 偏壓下暗電流皆比無氧氣流
量比所製作之光檢測器(MSM-A)低約 2.8 個數量級，說明通入氧氣成長之不同鎂含量氧化鎂
鋅薄膜具有較佳的結晶性，較少之氧缺陷密度，且其表面平整度更好，使金屬與氧化鎂鋅接
觸，可獲得較佳之介面，故可降低元件之暗電流。MSM-B 光檢測器的波長分辨率值分佈由
701 至 769，約 MSM-A 光檢測器六倍，結果顯示出，有氧氣分流量比之氧化鎂鋅吸收層光檢
測器波長分辨率值明顯提高，這可歸咎於薄膜在通入氧氣及鎂的摻雜條件下成長，並使得氧
空缺減少則薄膜特性變好。 
利用磁控式射頻共濺鍍系統在室溫沉積之氧化鎂鋅薄膜載子濃度普遍偏低且電阻率均較
高，故藉由對薄膜熱處理以提升薄膜之電子濃度，由熱處理後之氧化鎂鋅薄膜載子濃度由
1.96×1020Ω cm(x=0)至 1.74×1019Ω cm(x=0.3)，且電阻率也從 2.98×10-3Ω cm (x=0)至 1.20×10-1Ω

The μFE, Vth and S parameters of the TFTs deposited with 
various RF powers of the In2O3 target are listed in Table 1. 
It can be seen that the effective field-effect mobility 
increased from 1.42 to 90.2 cm2/V-s with increase of the 
RF power of the In2O3 target. As is known, the carrier 
transport path in IGZO film is formed by In 5s orbital 
overlapping with each other [5], thus more In ions in IGZO 
films benefit the carrier transport and hence result a better 
field-effect mobility. The threshold voltage was 2.7, 2.2, 
and 1.3 V when the RF power of the In2O3 target was set at 
25, 50, and 75 W. The subthreshold swing decreased 
accordingly from 0.41 to 0.24 V/decade with the increase 
of the RF power of the in2O3 target, indicating a better 
switch rate at higher In ions content. However, the off-state 
of the TFTs fabricated with In2O3 RF power of 75 W was at 
negative voltage (-0.8 V), which means that the devices had 
larger leakage current at VGS=0 V and was not suitable for 
normally off operation. This can be explained as follows. 
The increase in the content of indium ions causes a larger 
number of oxygen vacancies and zinc interstitials, which 
results in a larger free electrons density [10] and hence a 
larger drain-source leakage current as observed here for 
TFT fabricated with the In2O3 RF power of 75 W, for 
which the IDS was about 10 nA at VGS=0 V. Therefore, the 
In2O3 RF power of 50W was the best one among the three 
values on the whole and was chosen to be used in the other 
deposition series of samples. 
Figure 3 shows the representative transfer 
characteristics of the TFTs with the a-IGZO channels 
deposited at various RF powers of the Ga2O3 target, when 
the In2O3 RF power and Zn DC power were fixed at 50 W 
and 10 W, respectively. The Ga2O3 RF powers of 25 50, 
and 75 W were used for the deposition of the a-IGZO films. 
The characteristics of the TFTs deposited with the Ga2O3 
RF power of 75 W could not be obtained. This 
phenomenon can be attributed to the fact that the Ga ions in 
a-IGZO films would suppress carrier generation via oxygen 
vacancy formation, because the Ga ion forms stronger 
chemical bonds with oxygen than Zn and In ions. However, 
too much Ga2O3 content leads to a low carrier 
concentration in a-IGZO films. Therefore the formation of 
the channel is limited even at a positive VGS applied on the 
device. 
The μFE, Vth and S parameters of the TFTs with 
Ga2O3 RF powers of 25 W and 50 W were listed in Table 2. 
The effective field-effect mobility of the TFTs deposited 
with the Ga2O3 RF power of 50 W and 25 W were 7.38 and 
63.8 cm2/V-s, respectively. The mobility increase was 
attributed to the existence of more indium ions which 
formed a good transport path in the a-IGZO films, when the 
RF power of the Ga2O3 target was lower. Furthermore, less 
gallium ions would lead more oxygen vacancies in which a 
higher carrier concentration was induced. Higher carrier 
concentration in turn caused the threshold voltage shifted 
from 2.2 to 1.9 V and the subthreshold swing changed from 
0.28 to 0.25 V/decade. Less gallium ions resulted in an 
increase of effective field-effect mobility and kept the 
channel layer remained in the off state at VGS=0 V. 
Therefore, the Ga2O3 RF power of 25W was selected for 
third series deposition parameter, where only the DC power 
of the Zn target was varied.

3. Experimental results and discussion  
Hall measurement was used to measure the carrier type, 
concentration, mobility and resistivity of the resultant films. 
The results are shown in Table 1. As usual, the n-type 
conductivity was obtained for all undoped ZnO films 
post-annealed with various temperatures and the carrier 
concentration increased with the post-annealing 
temperature. However, it was evidently found that the 
electronic concentration of the as-growth N-doped ZnO 
films was three orders lower than that of the as-growth 
undoped ZnO films. This phenomenon can be ascribed to 
that a part of nitrogen atoms replace the oxygen atoms in 
the deposited films. In addition to NH3 molecules, NH2 
molecules and H atoms were produced in the plasma 
reaction [16]. The deposition processes of N-doped ZnO 
films could be described by the following equations: 
O-Zn-C2H5+ NH3 → O-Zn-NH2+C2H6              (1) 
O-Zn-C2H5+ NH2 → O-Zn-NH+C2H6               (2) 
O-Zn-C2H5+H-OH → O-Zn-OH+C2H6              (3) 
O-Zn-NH2+C2H5-Zn-C2H5→O-Zn-NH-Zn-C2H5+C2H6 (4) 
O-Zn-NH+C2H5-Zn-C2H5→O-Zn-N-Zn-C2H5+C2H6   (5) 
O-Zn-OH+C2H5-Zn-C2H5→O-Zn-O-Zn-C2H5+C2H6   (6) 
Eq. (1) and Eq. (2) show the reactions that the NH3 and 
NH2 react with the C2H5 to form C2H6 gas. Eq. (4) to Eq. (6) 
describes the reactions of the DEZn with NH2, NH and OH, 
respectively, in which the NH and N replace the oxygen to 
combine with the Zn. Therefore, the electronic 
concentration of the as-grown N-doped ZnO films was 
lower than that of the as-grown undoped ZnO films. The 
stable p-type conductivity of the N-doped ZnO films was 
obtained when the resultant films were post-annealed at 
900 oC in O2 ambient for 10 min, for which the carrier 
concentration, Hall mobility and resistivity were 3.10×1017 
cm-3, 0.92 cm2/V-s and 26.3 Ω-cm, respectively. Here, the 
post-annealing treatment was used to remove the hydrogen 
atoms from NH radicals, leaving single nitrogen atoms as 
acceptors in the N-doped ZnO films. 
The acceptor activation energy (EA) of the resultant 
films was estimated from the Arrhenius equation 
σ=σ0exp(-EA/KBT), where σ is the conductivity, σ0 is the 
pre-exponential factor, KB is the Boltzmann constant and T 
is the absolute temperature. Figure 2 shows the 
temperature-dependent conductivity of the N-doped ZnO 
films with post-annealing treatment at 900 oC, which was 
derived from Hall measurement carried out at different 
temperatures. By linear fitting, the acceptor activation 
energy was estimated to be approximately 86 meV. This 
value is close to the reported activation energy of 90 meV 
for nitrogen occupying the oxygen site (NO) in N-doped 
ZnO films [17].

Silicon-based solar cells on the oblique-evaporated textured ITO layer with an AZO 
coating 
 
Hsin-Ying Lee1*, Li-Wen Lai2, Kun-Wei Lin2, and Jiun-Ting Chen2  
 
1.Department of Electro-Optical Engineering, National Cheng Kung University, Tainan 701, Taiwan 
2.ITRI South, Industrial Technology Research Institute, Tainan 701, Taiwan 
*Phone: 886-6-2757575-63923, Fax: 886-6-2082368  E-mail: hylee@ee.ncku.edu.tw 
 
Abstract-The ITO/AZO double layers were used as the 
textured transparent conductive oxide (TCO) in the Si solar 
cells. The oblique evaporation method was used to deposit 
the textured ITO films. The AZO films were deposited on 
the textured ITO films to avoid the damages from the 
plasma processes. The resistivity, root mean square (RMS) 
of surface roughness, and transmittance of the obtained 
textured ITO/AZO (300 nm/50 nm) are 6.1×10-4 Ω-cm, 68 
nm and 88%, respectively. The best conversion efficiency 
of the a-Si solar cells with the textured ITO/AZO films was 
4.85 %.  
 
1. Introduction 
Recently, amorphous silicon (a-Si) is suitable to be used 
as the solar cell active material owing to its high 
absorptance. To obtain high conversion efficiency, the 
improvement of both internal quantum efficiency and light 
trapping efficiency of the solar cells are needed [1-3]. 
Among these methods, textured transparent conductive 
oxide (TCO), such as SnO2 and ITO, with a randomly 
distributed surface morphology is typically effective and 
widely employed in conventional cells [4]. However, the 
conductivity of ITO films decreases due to the plasma 
effect during the deposition process of the Si-based films. 
Therefore, the performance of the solar cells using textured 
ITO films is still difficult to elevate. In this work, for the 
improvement of light trapping efficiency, various textured 
ITO/AZO layers were deposited on the glass substrates 
with the oblique evaporation method using an electron 
-beam evaporator and an atomic layer deposition (ALD) 
system. The dependences of the amorphous Si solar cell on 
the various textured ITO/AZO layers were measured and 
investigated. 
 
2. Experimental procedure 
To get better efficiency, the solar cell with textured 
ITO/AZO layer was fabricated as follows. First, 10 
nm-thick ITO thin film was deposited on the glass 
substrates to perform a self-shadow region. The oblique 
angle set at 45o was used to fabricate the 300 nm-thick 
texture morphology of the ITO layer. With the aim to 
promote the conductivity, the samples were post-annealed 
at 450oC for 2 minutes in a N2 ambient using rapid thermal 
annealing (RTA) system. Then the various-thick AZO thin 
films of 10, 30 and 50 nm were deposited on the textured 
ITO using an atomic layer deposition (ALD) system. The 
textured ITO/AZO layers with various-thick AZO films of 
0, 10, 30 and 50 nm are labeled with letters sample A-D for 
abbreviation, respectively. These layers can improve the 
conductivity and avoid the damage to the ITO film by the 
plasma in the deposition process of Si films.  
The resistivity, root mean square (RMS) of roughness, 
surface morphology and transmittance of the obtained 
textured ITO/AZO films were measured using Hall 
measurement, atomic force microscopy (AFM), scanning 
electron microscope (SEM), and UV/VIS 
spectrophotometer, respectively. Finally, the p-type, i-type 
and n-type a-Si active layers were deposited on ITO and 
ITO/AZO films using plasma enhanced chemical vapor 
deposition (PECVD) system. The thicknesses of each layer 
were optimized. The photovoltaic conversion efficiency of 
the resultant silicon-based solar cells under various TCO 
structures will be measured and compared. 
 
3. Experimental results and discussion  
Table 1 shows the root mean square of surface 
roughness, resistivity, transmittance and carrier 
concentration of the sample A-D were measured by AFM, 
UV/VIS Spectrophotometer and Hall measurements, 
respectively. It can be seen that the as-deposited textured 
ITO films have the maximum roughness compared to all 
the ITO/AZO double layers. The conductivity and 
concentration of the ITO/AZO layers increases with the 
thickness of AZO layers. However, the resistivity of the 
sample C and D were nearly unchanged. Moreover, sample 
D exhibited the highest conductive behavior. This implied 
that this textured TCO with a randomly distributed surface 
morphology is suitable to employ in Si solar cells.
