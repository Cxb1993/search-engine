Abstract 
 
This project intends to develop a sensor device using carbon nanotubes (CNTs) as the base material for 
detecting phenolic compounds in the air. Our previous studies have shown CNTs are capable of selectively 
adsorb ring-bearing compounds because of the -bonding between the graphitic structure of the CNT and 
the benzene rings of the compounds, enabling the possibility of using this property as the fundamental of 
selective sensing. At present, environmental monitoring of these phenolic pollutants is almost exclusively 
done by sampling and analysis with expensive instruments which are highly sensitive and accurate. These 
chemical analyses are usually done ex situ and time consuming, thus bringing the potential application of a 
quick sensing device to determine the environmental (e.g., indoor air) quality.  
 
This proposed project includes a device fabrication process and a testing procedure. The objective of the 
fabrication is to embed CNTs onto a substrate, followed by doping with copper, which enhances the 
sensitivity of the CNT sensing device, through chemical bonding and copper coordination. The device 
fabrication process begins with acid washing with concurrent ultrasonication of the CNT, which will 
functionalize their surface, primary opening up the carbonyl functional groups on the surface. We then take 
advantage of the functional group by initiating acyl chloride reactions that in turn facilitates bonding of an 
amide group. The presence of the amide groups will further facilitate copper coordination to complete the 
base material of the device. On the alternative, we will also attempt to directly grow CNTs on the silicon 
substrate through thermal vapor deposition (CVD) process using CH4 as the carbon source. The grown CNTs 
would then be prepared by following the aforementioned procedure to complete copper doping. Finally, the 
prepared CNT device will be tested in a vaccum chamber equipped with a feed inlet of a pre-determined 
concentration of the target phenolic compounds, which includes phenol, chlorophenol, and nitrophenol. As a 
result, we will be able to study the adsorption behavior of the phenolic compounds on the prepared CNT, and 
to establish the correlation between the extent of adsorption with the change of material resistivity. 
 
Keywords: carbon nanotubes, sensing device, phenol, copper coordination, thermal vapor deposition

2.1 奈米碳管之基本性質與做為吸附劑之應用 
  含碳的吸附材料具有化學及熱穩定等優點，故能廣泛的應用於工業廢氣處理、分析樣品的前濃縮
及前處理中。目前較常使用的含碳吸附劑有活性碳、碳分子篩、石墨炭黑與奈米碳管。活性碳表面上
具有複雜的結構及許多不同的官能基，且利用官能基與待分析物之間各式不同的鍵結機制[3]來有效地
捕捉各式各樣不同的質。但活性碳吸附劑對於許多有機污染物的吸附力太強，易於造成不易脫附而影
響回收率，由於活性碳吸附設備較低的設置成本與不錯的吸附能力；因此活性碳吸附被廣泛運用於工
業廢氣的管末處理中。碳分子篩則是由許多細小的晶粒互相交連 (Cross-linking) 而產生許多不規則的
微孔洞。碳分子篩由於具有微孔洞與高比表面積，因此對於分子大小與孔洞大小接近的有機物具有優
異的滯留能力，故常用來捕捉高揮發性有機物，但太強的捕捉力對於低揮發性的有機物而言，則容易
發生脫附不完全而造成「記憶效應」影響後續分析，因此較少單獨使用[4]。石墨炭黑表面不具孔洞結
構而是具有一致的層狀結構，其利用層狀結構的表面的極性與非極性兩種不同吸附位置與分子作用來
捕捉有機分子[5]。石墨碳黑主要是利用表面上的非極性位置，如排列整齊的碳原子與分子間所產生的
凡得瓦力來吸引非極性分子；或是利用極少數的極性位置來吸附極性分子。此外石墨炭黑不易吸附空
氣中的水分子，使得分析結果更加的準確並改進碳分子篩的缺點，因此常被利用在多重床吸附管。碳
分子篩與石墨碳黑雖常當做吸附劑而運用在分析上，但都有價錢昂貴且低選擇性等缺點。 
 
在以奈米碳管吸附芳香族化合物的相關研究中；在 2001 年發表的文章[6]指出對於戴奧辛；奈米
碳管有著比活性碳更好的吸附能力，Xing 等人[7]則是利用奈米碳管對 PAHs (Polycyclic Aromatic 
Hydrocarbons) 物質  (pyrene、phenanthrene、naphthalene)做吸附測試，並將實驗結果與 PMM 
(Polanyi-Manes model) 做 fitting，其研究指出 PMM 對於奈米碳管吸附 PAHs 的現象有不錯的模擬，
而由 PMM 之相關參數可推斷 PAHs 的分子大小將會影響碳管的吸附率。而吸物質分子大小與其在奈
米碳管上吸附位置不同所造成的吸附效率差異，則是肇因於大分子物質由於較難進入碳管內部孔洞故
多為表面吸附，但小分子物質擴散進入碳管內部孔洞速率卻極低，因此奈米碳管對大分子物質吸附率
較佳，另外分子結構的型態也會對吸附效率產生影響，平面型的分子由於能與奈米碳管表面有較佳之
接觸，故對於吸附也有正面之幫助[8]。除了吸附物質的性質影響；奈米碳管本身特性對於芳香族化合
物的吸附也會造成影響，例如表面積越大即對吸附有利[9]，而奈米碳管之比表面積範圍可由 50至 1315

2.3 生成奈米碳管之方法 
奈米碳管的生成，從最早發現奈米碳管所使用的方法電弧放電法開始，之後陸陸續續的發展出各
種生成奈米碳管之方法。而奈米碳管的生成目前主要分為三大方法：1. 電弧放電法（arc- discharge 
method）、2. 雷射氣化法（laser ablation method）、3. 化學氣相沉積法（chemical vapor deposition，簡
稱 CVD）。以下分別介紹三大生成奈米碳管方法之過程及方式。 
 6 
 
空化學氣相沉積法（ultrahigh vacuum CVD，簡稱 UHVCVD）是指於非常低壓之環境下進行 CVD 反應，
其壓力大約為 10-8 torr。若依溫度作區分，則可分為（I）熱化學氣相沉積法（thermal CVD，簡稱 TCVD）
是指不以輔助方式，可直接由熱輻射提供之反應能量；（II）電漿化學氣相沈積法（plasma enhanced 
CVD，簡稱 PECVD）是指利用電漿之能量增加前驅物之反應速率，因而允許於低溫環境下產生反應。
TCVD 與 PECVD 尚可藉由射頻（radio frequency，簡稱 RF）、微波、熱燈絲等方法輔助來增加其效能。 
利用此方法生成奈米碳管時，催化劑為必要的生成因素。反應溫度、反應時間、氣體之流量（比
例）、催化劑的大小及多寡、載體特性、環境壓力等因素，也會影響生成的奈米碳管之品質，這些因
素於反應過程中是值得被探討的。於本研究過程中，係於常壓環境下的熱化學氣相沉積法製造奈米碳
管於載體上。 
在上述提到之三大生成奈米碳管生成方法中，其中以化學氣相沉積法相對另外兩大方法來說具有
成本低廉、操作簡易、高產率之優點，因此，許多研究都採用此種方法來合成與製作奈米碳管。奈米
碳管於 CVD 中生成，會因種種條件而有所影響，包含催化劑種類、催化劑分布情形、還原氣體流量（比
例）與種類、碳源流量（比例）與種類、反應時間、反應溫度、載體種類、環境壓力等。 
本研究主要目的是在常壓環境下，於 TCVD 系統進行奈米碳管生成。一般利用化學氣相沉積法生成奈
米碳管以負壓或超真空環境條件下操作居多。但還是有些許是於常壓環境條件下作碳沉積反應。於常
壓環境下，反應溫度生成奈米碳管時，反應溫度亦有被作考慮的，若反應溫度高於 700℃時，因催化
劑活性較高亦有凝聚現象，漸而生成大型顆粒，造成 CNT 的產率下降(Sharma and Lakkad, 2009)。另外，
反應時間亦有當作參數調整重點，反應時間長短影響其奈米碳管與 CNC 的生成有相關，當反應時間越
長時，其時間約為 30 min 以上，有助於奈米碳管生成，但對 CNC 之生成反之減少(Kang et al., 2008)。
上述以常壓環境下生成 CNT，其反應溫度及反應時間，均會造成生成的影響；本研究於常壓環境下，
改善高於 700℃不易生成奈米碳管而生成其他碳沉積物，在本研究反應溫度除高於 700℃亦可生成 CNT
外，生成密度亦優於文獻中之結果。另外，在反應時間過程，文獻指出，生成時間越長，生成 CNT 效
果越佳，但其反應時間冗長，與本研究相較之下，本研究僅需以文獻中的反應時間之一半，即可生成
CNT，且生成效果與文獻中結果相像，故本研究在常壓環境下，不僅改善於高溫可生成 CNT 外，亦改
善以短時間生成 CNT。

4.2.1 不同披覆方法於載體表面之分析結果 
去年之計畫，曾以溶膠凝膠法（Sol-gel）披覆載體，披覆後進行熱化學氣相沉積法之碳沉積反應，
雖然以此方法可生成奈米碳管於載體表面上，但因生成後，易與載體表面做脫離，無法密合的與載體
做結合，而使生成於載體上之奈米碳管，不具本研究中製作電極元件使用；而為改善其方法，本研究
利用兩種不同披覆催化劑於載體之方法做其嘗試，分別為蒸鍍法及有機金屬裂解法等兩種方法。這兩
種方法不同於Sol-gel法，蒸鍍法是直接將催化劑之金屬以真空蒸鍍，將金屬催化劑汽化，而附著於載
體表面上；而以有機金屬裂解法則是選用前年度有生成奈米碳管之催化劑前驅物（氯化鎳）搭配甘油
為其溶劑，以達到有機金屬裂解法披覆於載體表面上。其結果分別如下，蒸鍍法之披覆結果如圖十五

覆較不易產生金屬結晶顆粒，對後續碳沉積之實驗，似乎有影響其反應作用，至於是否有影響，須於
下段結果作其解釋。另外，以有機金屬裂解法披覆結果如圖十七及十八，由這兩張圖觀察，其披覆均

 Reclamation of Copper from Polishing Wastewater by Electrodialysis 
 
Yu-Nung Su and Walter Den 
 
Since the development of copper interconnect technology for advanced 
microelectronics device manufacturing, the environmental impact of copper contamination 
and higher water consumption are serious challenges facing for the IC and Semiconductor 
industries nowadays. Traditional wastewater treatments processes typically require high 
chemical consumption and generate heavy metal sludge volume. With the rising 
environmental awareness and high raw material cost, the reclamation processes have come 
into public attention. This research focuses on the efficiency of an integrated water reclaim 
system with ultrafiltration (UF) and electrodialysis (ED) for treatment and reclamation of 
copper wastewater. In this work, the permeate flux for the ultrafiltration membrane are 
investigated using artificial solutions containing nano-scale silica particles and Cu ions. 
Using different operating parameters we studied the separation efficiency, produced water 
quality and the optimal conditions applied to SiO2 and Cu raw material recovery. 
Experiments are conducted using commercial polyvinylidenefluoride (PVDF) membrane, 
ion exchange membranes (SKS-CGSKS-A), and graphite electrodes in both batch and 
continuous operations with three parameters: concentration (SiO2: 500, 1000 mgL-1; Cu 
10, 25, 50, 120 mgL
-1
), pH (3, 4, 5) and voltage gradient (1, 1.5, 2.5 Vcm
-1
). The UF 
membrane system could effectively separate the particles from the solution with a rejection 
rate over 99.7%, then the ED process would reclaim Cu ions from permeate solution. The 
removal efficiency of copper can reach above 95.6%, when the operating conditions in the 
well-stirred batch reaction process are constant voltage 50V and reaction time 2 hr. Results 
from the desorption experiment presented the release amount of 93.5% of copper from 
ion-exchanger membrane. The improvement of adsorption efficiency by the modified ED 
system is an emerging technique for recycling heavy metal from Chemical Mechanical 
Polishing (CMP) wastewater because it is capable to attenuate problems such as membrane 
fouling, block of ion exchange resin, and microbial activities in a batch reactor. The 
combination of membrane systems could treat the copper wastewater to the level that 
easily meets the Taiwan EPA’s effluent regulations (3 mgL-1). With efficient desorbing 
solution, from this study presents the feasibility for removal and recovery of copper from 
wastewater.

Syntheses, Acidichromic and Hg2+-Sensing Properties of Novel Naphthalimide Derivatives  
Pei-Yu Kuo, Ya-Chi Jang and Walter Den, Environmental Science and Engineering, Tunghai

Two novel fluorescent naphthalimide-based sensors as ion sensor and acidichromic colorant are 
synthesized. This naphthalimide fluorophore is functionalized via a condensation reaction in which

visual detection provides a simple, rapid and sensitive method and was used for the detection of 
gaseous acids and Hg2+ ion in environmental applications.

Synthesis of Carbonaceous Nano-Materials and Their Solid-Phase Adsorptivity  
Walter Den, Gang Chen and Chia-Hung Yeh, Environmental Science and Engineering, Tunghai 
University, Taichung City, Taiwan  
 
This study was intended to develop a solid-phase adsorbent based on carbonaceous materials 
embedded on zirconia (ZrO2) spherical carriers. This study used thermal chemical vapor deposition 
(TCVD) process at atmospheric pressure for growth of carbon nano-material, including carbon 
nanosphere (CNS), carbon nanocapsule (CNC), carbon onion, and carbon web-like structures. In the 
preliminary study, wet impregnation process was conducted to coat nickel (Ni) as catalyst precursor 
on the surface of substrates, followed by using TCVD process with methane as carbon source, argon 
as carrier gas, and hydrogen as initiator. The structural and physical characteristics of the carbon 
products were determined using scanning electron microscopy, transmission electron microscopy, 
energy dispersive spectrometry, X-ray diffractometry, and Raman scattering spectroscopy. The 
results of these micro-analyses were used to evaluate the effects of individual process parameter on 
the carbonaceous material characteristics. The ensuing solid-phase adsorption studies were then 
performed to characterize the sorption properties of the materials associated with their physical 
characteristics. In this work, phthalic acid esters (PAEs) were used as the compounds of interest for 
the solid-phase adsorption studies. PAEs are commonly used in plastic industry, and are most widely 
used as plasticizer in products such as plastic toys, medical devices, food wrap, perfume and nail 
polisher. They are categorized as endocrine disrupting compounds (include sex hormone and the 
thyroxine) and some of them are suspected carcinogens. In Taiwan, PAEs reportedly exist in 
seawater and freshwater in very low concentration range (0.1 ~ 300 μg/L). The work therefore uses a 
representative PAE, namely diethyl phthalate (DEP) as the testing compound. Preliminary 
adsorption test results indicate that CNC and carbon onion materials both achieved DEP adsorptivity 
in the range of 80 μg/g at DEP solution concentration of 5 mg/L, and 110 μg/g at DEP solution 
concentration of 10 mg/L. The web-like carbon structure attained approximately 50 μg/g at DEP 
solution concentration of 5 mg/L. Additionally, DEP adsorption capacities of CNC and carbon onions 
were both higher (12.0 μg) than that of commercial adsorbent Tenax TA (4.1 μg).

performed to characterize the sorption properties of the materials associated with their physical 
characteristics. In this work, phthalic acid esters (PAEs) were used as the compounds of interest for 
the solid-phase adsorption studies. PAEs are commonly used in plastic industry, and are most widely 
used as plasticizer in products such as plastic toys, medical devices, food wrap, perfume and nail 
polisher. They are categorized as endocrine disrupting compounds (include sex hormone and the 
thyroxine) and some of them are suspected carcinogens. In Taiwan, PAEs reportedly exist in 
seawater and freshwater in very low concentration range (0.1 ~ 300 μg/L). The work therefore uses a 
representative PAE, namely diethyl phthalate (DEP) as the testing compound. Preliminary 
adsorption test results indicate that CNC and carbon onion materials both achieved DEP adsorptivity 
in the range of 80 μg/g at DEP solution concentration of 5 mg/L, and 110 μg/g at DEP solution 
concentration of 10 mg/L. The web-like carbon structure attained approximately 50 μg/g at DEP 
solution concentration of 5 mg/L. Additionally, DEP adsorption capacities of CNC and carbon onions 
were both higher (12.0 μg) than that of commercial adsorbent Tenax TA (4.1 μg).
