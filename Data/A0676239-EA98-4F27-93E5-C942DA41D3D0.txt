The experimental data of water contact angle showed that in the hierarchically
structured superhydrophobic particulate films fabricated by LB strategy,  the
advancing contact angle is above 170?, hysteresis is as low as 6?and compared
with the hydrophobicity of micro-sized structured hydrophobic surfaces, the enhanced
superhydrphobic effect of hierarchical structure is conspicuous. Beside, transparent
and superhydrophobic surface could be created by using ELbL strategy on appropriate
proportion of body layer with 22nm/7nm mixing-sized nanoparticles. Among these
films, superhydrophobic and transparent thin films with water advancing contact
angle values of 169-171?, contact angle hysteresis values of 7-9?and average
transmittance values of 90.2%-91% in the visible-light region were exhibited by 5
bilayers of adhesion-layer, 20 bilayers of body-layer with 22 nm / 7 nm volume ratio
values of 1/4 – 1/6, and 3 layers of top-layer.
Superhydrophilic self-cleaning surfaces could also be fabricated by ELbL
strategy. The particulate films with 1~30 bilayers were achieved by assembling of
positive charged 7 nm TiO2 nanoparticles and negative charged 22nm SiO2
nanoparticles aqueous on glass substrate alternately. After sintering treatment and
hydrophobic modification with alkylsilane,  superhydrophilic self-cleaning surfaces
with photocatalytic property and high then 90% average transmittance in the
visible-light wavelength can be obtained.

On the Stability of Liposomes and Catansomes in Aqueous Alcohol Solutions
Yu-Min Yang*, Kuo-Chang Wu, Zheng-Lin Huang, and Chien-Hsiang Chang
Department of Chemical Engineering, National Cheng Kung University, Tainan 701
Taiwan
Ethosomes are vesicular carriers composed of lipids and relatively high concentrations (20-
50%) of ethanol and are designed to facilitate drug penetration to the underlying skin strata. In
this work, effects of three cosolvents (methanol, ethanol, and 1-propanol) on the stability of
liposomes formed from soybean phosphatidylcholine (PL-90) by a semi-spontaneous
process[1]was carried out. The experimental results revealed that significant enhancement of
PL-90 liposome stability could be achieved by cosolvent addition with suitable amounts. A
similar phenomenon was also observed for catansomes formed from ion-pair amphiphiles
(IPAs) as demonstrated by decyltrimethylammonium dodecylsulfate (DeTMA-DS),
decyltrimethylammonium tetradecylsulfate (DeTMA-TS), and dodecyltrimethylammonium
dodecylsulfate (DTMA-DS). In general, with keeping increase in the cosolvent concentration,
the stability of liposomes and catansomes first increased, reached a maximum, and then
decreased. Ethosomes therefore turn out to be examples of liposomes promoted by means of
cosolvent effects. Furthermore, it was realized that the cosolvent effects on the liposome
