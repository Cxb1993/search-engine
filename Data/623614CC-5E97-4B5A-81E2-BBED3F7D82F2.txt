一、中文摘要 
此計畫在執行的第三年已成功展示一
種高功率、高速的黃綠光二極體。其可應
用在外科手術或是塑膠光纖通信。我們也
成功展示了一種新穎的次毫米波光電發射
器，其可應用於生醫影像系統。除此之外
我們更展示了一種在紅外光波段並具世界
紀錄高的增益頻寬乘積的光檢測器。我們
也在計畫的最後一年順利將綠光高速二極
體的技術往更高溫、更高速操作邁進。 
 
關鍵詞：發光二極體，DNA 分析，單光子
檢測器，光動力療法，生醫晶片 
 
Abstract 
The third year of this project has 
successfully demonstrated novel structure of 
high-speed and high-power green-yellow 
light-emitting diodes, which can be applied 
to the surgery or the plastic optical fiber 
communication. We also demonstrated a 
novel sub-millimeter wave photonic 
transmitter, which can be applied to the 
bio-imaging system. In addition, we 
demonstrated a novel infrared photodetector 
with record high gain-bandwidth product      
 
Keywords: Light-emitting-diode, DNA 
analysis, Single-Photon Detector, 
Photodynamic therapy, Bio-Chip 
 
二、緣由與目的 
此計畫目的將會展示以半導體為材料
且應用於 DNA 分析生醫晶片光源和光動
力療法光源的高速發光二極體和高響應度
的檢光器。在生醫應用方面，黃綠光
(~540nm)和紅光波段(~650nm)的應用不勝
枚舉，例如說: DNA 分析生醫晶片光源，
光動力療法光源，生物體螢光激發光源等
等..。在傳統的這些生醫系統中，所使用的
黃綠光光源大部份是利用半導體雷射激發
的固態雷射(Diode pumped solid state laser) 
所組成，並不是使用直接電激發的發光二
極體或半導體雷射。由於近年來氮化鎵半
導體材料的成熟，使得半導體黃綠光光源
的技術也已商品化。和其他所使用的光源
相比，(例如說:氣體雷射，固態雷射)半導
體光源有體積小，高效率，可直接調變的
優點。此計畫中我們已利用氮化鎵的材料
系統，成功製作出可操作在波長~500nm(黃
綠光)和~400nm(UV 光)的高速和高功率發
光二極體。利用這些元件可高速調變的特
性，我們可以改變其工作週期 (duty cycle)
和振幅來量測生物體或 DNA 的螢光動態
反應，或者是降低系統的低頻(1/f)噪音。也
可研究光源特殊的工作週期和功率對螢光
體吸收飽合(absorption bleach)的影響，或是
光動力療法的治療效果。除此之外，我們
也開發了可應用在兆赫波影像系統的新潁
光源:光電發射器。和傳統光電發射器相
比，其具有更低的操作電壓和幅射效率。

High-Responsivity, High-Speed, and High-Saturation-Power Performances 
of Evanescently Coupled Photodiodes with Partially p-Doped Photo-absorption 
Layer 
 
J.-W. Shi*, Y.-S. Wu, F.-H. Huang, Y.-J. Chan 
Department of Electrical Engineering, National Central University, Taoyuan, Taiwan, R.O.C 
*TEL: 886-3-4227151 ext. 4528 
*FAX: 886-3-4255830 
*E-mail: jwshi@ee.ncu.edu.tw 
Abstract 
Recent advancements in 40-Gbs optical communication systems have focused on increasing 
the bandwidth-responsivity product and obtaining a high saturation output power of p-i-n 
photodiodes in order to relieve or even eliminate the burden imposed on the electrical preamplifier 
[1].  However, the electrical bandwidth, responsivity, and saturation output power are usually 
three trade-off parameters in the design of high-speed photodiodes [1].  Here, we demonstrate an 
evanescently-coupled photodiode [2] with the partially p-doped photo-absorption layer [3].  Very 
high responsivity (1.01A/W), high electrical bandwidth (>40GHz), and high saturation power 
performances (over 6.5 dBm at 40GHz) have been achieved simultaneously at 1.55μm 
wavelength. 
Fig. 1 shows the top-view and cross-sectional view of the fabricated edge-coupled 
photodiodes.  Table 1 gives the corresponding epi-layers structures. We adopted the evanescently 
coupled optical waveguide structure in the fiber guide layer, which is a stack of five periods of 
undoped InP-InGaAsP layers.  The numbers of periods and their thickness have been optimized 
to achieve high coupling efficiency with an input fiber and low polarization dependence.  Two 
kinds of devices were fabricated.  Device A has partially graded p-doped photo-absorption layer 
(150nm) and device B has the same geometry size and epi-layer structures as device A except for 
the photo-absorption layer, which is pure intrinsic.  Fig. 2 shows the simulated electrical 
bandwidth of the fabricated photodiodes versus the ratio of p-doped photo-absorption layer 
thickness to undoped photo-absorption layer thickness.  According to the simulated results, we 
can thus choose the desired ratio of thickness (0.15/0.35) to achieve near 40GHz electrical 
bandwidth performance.  Fig. 3 shows the measured photocurrent of device A and B versus 
optical pumping power.  A very high responsivity of 1.01A/W has been achieved [2], and the 
obtained values of responsivity of device A and B are almost the same.  These results indicate 
that the partially p-doped photo-absorption layer of our structure will not degrade the performance 
of quantum efficiency.  The electrical bandwidth and output radio-frequency (RF) power of 
fabricated photodiodes were measured with a heterodyne beating setup [2], which includes two 
tunable lasers at the wavelength of 1550nm, an optical 3-dB coupler, and a high-power optical 
amplifier connected with an optical attenuator to adjust the input optical power from a coupling 
lens pairs to the photodiodes.  The photo-generated electrical signal is extracted via a 40GHz 
microwave probe and fed into a 40GHz radio-frequency (RF) spectrum analyzer or a 40GHz RF 
power sensor. Fig. 4 represents the frequency responses of device A under 5V dc bias voltage and 
0.28mA output photocurrent.  The measured electrical bandwidth is over 40GHz and consistent 
with the simulated results as shown in Fig. 2.  Fig. 5 shows the measured frequency responses of 
device A and B under the same dc bias (5V) and the same high output photocurrent regime 
(5.5mA).  Compared with device B, device A exhibits much superior bandwidth performance 
(26GHz vs. 8GHz) in the high output power regime due to its partially p-doped photo-absorption 
layer.  Trace A and B in Fig. 6 represent the photo-generated RF power at the frequency of 
40GHz versus dc photocurrent of device A and B, respectively.  We can clearly see that the 
photodiode with partially p-doped photo-absorption layer has significant improvement in RF 
power generation.  Fig. 7 shows the photo-generated RF power of device A versus dc 
photocurrent for different reverse bias: 1, 3, and 5V.  We can clearly see that under 5V bias 
voltage, the -1-dB compression current and RF power at 40GHz operating frequency are over 
23mA and 6.5 dBm, respectively.  The obtained power and saturation current are much higher 
than the previous work on p-i-n evanescently coupled photodiodes with integrated tapers [1,2,4,5] 
at the same operating frequency (40GHz) [2,4].
