具溫度補償功能之光纖振動感測器 
何旭川、林威男、蔡志倫、何孟翰 
元智大學機械工程學系 
計畫編號：NSC 99-2221-E-155-012 
 
摘要 
  光纖光柵傳感器具有抗電磁干擾、尺寸小、重量輕、
低損耗、傳輸距離遠、易形變等優點，已被廣泛使用
於通訊和感測技術。本文以光纖光柵為感測器結合寬
頻光源在特定波段光強度與波長具線性關係的特性，
將溫度變化與機械負載所產生之光纖光柵反射波長偏
移轉換為光強度變化，再以反應快速之光偵測器檢測
光強度，應用於量測結構在溫度與機械負載之動態響
應。同時為能分離溫度與機械負載，使量測結果獲得
溫度補償，於結構上下表面各貼覆一條相同之光纖光
柵，再將表面光纖光柵反射光強度經相加與相減運算
後，可解調結構承受之溫度變化與機械應變。本文將
此光纖光柵量測方法，應用於懸臂樑自由振動，並將
量測所得與應變規及熱電偶比較，改變試片溫度，振
動振幅與頻率，驗證此光纖感測系統量測結構振動與
溫度的可行性。 
關鍵詞：光纖光柵、光偵測器、自由振動、光強度、
溫度補償 
 
1. 前言 
  光纖光柵技術發展至今已將近三十年，在光纖光柵
製程技術方面，從 1978 年 K.O.Hill[1]等人發現含鍺光
纖的感光性並以內部寫入法（Internal Writing Method）
製作出第一條光纖光柵開始，但由於光柵品質不易控
制，加上難以決定光柵所在位置及長度，使得這種原
始的光纖光柵製作方式難以被實際應用；到 1989 年
G.Meltz[2]等人發展出由光纖外部以兩道紫外光干涉
形成光纖光柵的橫向全像法（Transverse Holographic 
Method），此方式可將光纖光柵的共振波長調整至想
要的波長而大大的提高其應用的範圍，但其缺點是光
路架設困難、無法任意改變光柵週期；直到 1993 年
K.O.Hill[3]等人提出相位光罩法（Phase Mask Method）
的光纖光柵製作方式，其對雷射光源同調性要求低、
光路架設簡單、光柵品質穩定，所以為現今大量採用
的光纖光柵製作方式。理論方面，1957 年 Nye[4]對電
光效應及光彈效應等結晶體受外在因素影響造成的
介電常數變化加以研究，並推導出應變場對光學性質
影響的關係式；1979 年 Takahashi 和 Shibata 找出溫度
增量對光纖之折射率變化間的關係； 1997 年
Erdogan[5]引入光纖模態耦合理論（Coupled-mode 
Theory）解釋光纖光柵的原理。以光纖光柵作為感測
器已廣泛地應用在各種領域中， 1992 年 Kersey 和
Berkoff [6]、[7]提到光纖光柵干涉波長飄移對動態應
變偵測的原理介紹，1996 年 Kersey [8]回顧整理先前
一些光纖感測器的應用，1997 年 Kersey、Davis [9]等
人又共同提出以 FBG 結合干涉系統作為感測器來量
測動態應變，並找出波長飄移量和相位差之理論關係
式；2007 年 H.Tauda[10]以 FBG 為感測器應用光柵反
射波長偏移量搭配長短週期光纖光柵的組合來量測
碳纖維複合材料受到衝擊複合材料表面脫落之情
況；2008 年 Wang[11]等人以雙光纖布拉格光柵的方式
使溫度變化能夠自動補償。本研究即根據此理論基礎
開始探討此感測系統量測溫度與振動的準確度、可行
性，以短週期光柵隨著形變，反射波長會有所漂移，
結合寬頻光源在特定波段光強度與波長具線性關係
的特性，讓在不同應變量對應出不同的光強度。 
 
2. 應用光強度解調結構溫度與應變關係 
  感測光路如圖 1 所示，以兩條相同之光纖光柵黏貼
於結構上下表面，上表面光柵反射波長為 1524.59nm
如圖 2，下表面光柵反射波長為 1524.89nm 如圖 3，藉
由量測上下表面光纖光柵反射光強度，寬頻光源特定
波段為 1522.5nm~1527.5nm 如圖 4，解調結構承受之
溫度變化與機械應變。 
量測原理為樑試片受到溫度影響產生熱漲冷縮的變
形，貼覆於試片上下表面的光纖光柵亦會隨之壓縮或
伸長，而懸臂樑受負載向下彎曲，貼覆於懸臂樑上表
面光纖光柵承受拉伸應變，下表面光纖光柵承受壓縮
應變，使光纖光柵反射波長產生偏移，選用之光纖光
柵其反射光強度與波長具線性關係，故藉由量測光強
度變化求得結構承受之溫度變化與機械應變。 
理想狀況上下表面光纖光柵承受相同溫度變化或機械
應變，反射光強度應相同，然實務上兩條光路可能由
於接頭損耗不同，或因光纖內衰減不同，使上下表面
光纖光柵反射光強度不同。本節先以實驗方式校正上
下表面光纖光柵反射光強度差異，校正方式為將懸臂
樑試片置於真空烘箱內，設定不同溫度，量測上下表
面光纖光柵在相同熱應變之反射光強度電壓如圖 5 和
表 2 所示，結果顯示在相同熱應變下表面光纖光柵反
射光強度電壓 V2 與上表面光纖光柵反射光強度電壓
V1 之比值約為定值，表 2 所示之 V2/ V1 之比值其平
均值為 1.096。將上表面光纖光柵反射光強度 V1 乘以
1.096 即與下表面光纖光柵反射光強度相同。本章後續
實驗貼覆於試片上表面光纖光柵量測所得光電壓均乘
上此係數 1.096，使修正後通過上下表面光柵之光路，
在不受應變溫度作用下其光強度相等。

4. 結論 
懸臂樑不同初始位移與長度之自由振動應用(6)
式計算之溫度變化與熱電偶量測值比較，光纖光柵量
測值因受雜訊影響有些許誤差，當溫度變化提高時，
整體誤差均提高，是由於真空烘箱加熱時，並非絕熱
狀態，在每次施加振動時，都會影響烘箱內溫度，使
得誤差提高。此外應用(10)式計算之應變響應與應變歸
量測值比較，可看出初始位移 2mm 之自由振動應變規
與光纖光柵計算所得應變誤差較大，而初始位移
10mm 之自由振動其誤差較小，是因為當初始位移較
小時，所產生的訊號較小，容易受雜訊影響，而懸臂
樑不同長度產生不同頻率，其整體誤差則較平均。此
外使用真空烘箱加熱時，需要相當的電力，使得電壓
不穩定，造成光源強度的改變，因此造成整體誤差提
高。

1. Introduction 
The use of optical fiber sensors has grown considerably over the past decade in applications ranging 
from the aerospace industry to civil engineering [1,2]. Optical fiber sensors have been developed to 
measure a variety of physical quantities such as strain, temperature and vibration. Smart structures 
which integrate both sensors and actuators have the ability to sense environmental changes within or 
around the structure and are able to interpret and react to these changes. Optical fiber sensors possess 
several advantages over conventional electrical sensors such as light weight, small dimensions, high 
temperature endurance, dielectric nature, immunity to corrosion, electromagnetic interference and 
electric hazards, that meet the basic sensing requirements of smart structures to a large extent. The 
direct application of smart structures in structural health monitoring has been studied intensively over 
the last few years, particularly in buildings and bridges [3-5]. Several fiber optic technologies have 
been proposed for strain/temperature measurements, such as fiber Bragg grating (FBG) sensors [6-8], 
high-polarimetric fiber optic sensors [9,10] and white-light interferometric fiber sensors [11]. Fiber 
Bragg grating based optical fiber sensors have been demonstrated successfully in monitoring structures. 
Liu et al. [12] used polymer-coated FBG sensors to measure pressure and temperature simultaneously. 
Chau et al. [13] experimentally demonstrated the effectiveness of using the FBG system for structural 
vibration control. Interferometric type fiber optic sensors have the advantages of high sensitively and 
high spatial resolution. Therefore, they can be used for damage detection in structural members [14,15]. 
As a sensor, it is expected that the strains between the optical fiber and host structure are the same. 
However, due to the existence of the adhesive layer and protective coating, part of the energy is 
converted into shear deformation. Thus, the strain of the optical fiber is different from that of the host 
structure. Lau et al. [16] developed a simple model to calculate the percentage of strain applied to the 
host structure actually transferred to the embedded fiber optic sensor. Zhou et al. [17] investigated the 
influence of coating and adhesive on the strain transmission of embedded fiber optic sensors. Most of 
the existing works that have studied the strain transfer between the optical fiber and host material have 
focused on the embedded optical fiber. In reality, the surface bonded optical fiber is much easier to use 
in structural monitoring applications then embedded optical fibers. Ansari and Libo [18] proposed a 
theoretical model to study the strain transferred from the host material to the surface bonded optical 
fiber. They assumed that the optical fiber (with coating) was perfectly bonded with the host material. 
The effect of adhesive layer was neglected. Wan et al. [19] investigated the influence of four geometric 
parameters of the adhesive, side width, top thickness, bonded length and bottom thickness, on the 
strain of the surface-attached optical fiber. In this investigation, the fiber optic sensor is surface 
bonding on the host structure. The theoretical model includes the adhesive layer and protective coating. 
An analytical formula is proposed to evaluate the real strain in the host structure using the surface 
bonded optical fiber. This formula provides accurate prediction of the strain transformation rate from 
the host material through the adhesive layer and protective coating to the optical fiber strain sensor. A 
numerical study is performed to evaluate the influence of the coating and bonded length on the strain 
transferred from the host structure to the optical fiber. Experimental tests are conducted based on the 
Mach-Zehnder interferometery to reveal the differential strains between the fiber-optic sensor and host 
structure.

The coefficient of strain transformation between the optical fiber and host material can be obtained

Figure 12 shows the results of the coefficient of strain transformation versus the bonded length for 
polymer and acrylate coatings. The coefficient of strain transformation for the optical fiber without

transformation for optical fibers without coating shown in Figure 12 is close to 1, which means the

coating on the strain transformation rate between the optical fiber and surrounding host material is
