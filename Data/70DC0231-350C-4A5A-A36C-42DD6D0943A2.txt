六、計畫成果自評 
一、與原計畫相符程度 
本計畫預計發展之目標為正面入射吸收量子紅外線偵測器陣列研製，而透過本計畫之
支持，在本年度我們將重心放在量子點紅外線偵測器之正面吸收特性及偵測器陣列之研
製，此計畫在執行之初設定為三年計畫而核可下來變成一年計畫，因此在有限的時間內，
我們成功的探討了量子點結構的尺寸對於正面吸收特性的影響，同時我們也建立了完整的
紅外線熱像儀之製作及測試系統，以結果而言，我們本年度之研究成果完全符合計畫目標
之要求。 
 
二、達成預期目標情況 
透過本計畫之支持，我們於本年度發表的論文目錄如後所述，此外，我們成功的以不
同的成長方式製備出不同尺寸的量子點結構，在我們最新的成果中，我們亦探討了量子點
結構的尺寸對於正面吸收特性的影響，此一結果有助於對量子點紅外線偵測器之正面入射
吸收特性更進一步的瞭解。同時我們也建立了完整的紅外線熱像儀之製作及測試系統，以
結果而言，我們已達成本年度之預期目標。 
 
三、研究成果之學術或應用價值 
    由於理論上，量子井結構並不吸收正面入射光，因此在元件製作及量測上往往增加許
多不必要之困難而影響其應用之空間 (如需額外的導光機制或是需要較高的響應度以補償
不吸收正面入射光之損失)。而相較於量子井紅外線偵測器，理論上由於量子點結構為一不
具體積之半導體結構，因此在大部份的文獻中皆預期其正面入射吸收特性較量子井紅外線
偵測器較佳，在我們今年的成果中，我們亦證明了較小尺寸的量子點具有較高的正面入射
吸收，再加上我們所建立的完整紅外線熱像儀之製作及測試系統，我們認為此計畫的成果
在學術及實際應用上均具有極大之價值。

The Influence of Fermi Level Position on The Performances of Single-Period InAs/GaAs 
Quantum-Dot Infrared Photodetectors 
 
Shu-Ting Chou and Shih-Yen Lin* 
Research Center for Applied Sciences, Academia Sinica, Taipei, Taiwan 
Jheng-Nan Chen, Chi-Che Tseng, Yi-Hao Chen and Meng-Chyi Wu 
Department of Electrical Engineering, National Tsing Hua University, Hsinchu, Taiwan 
* Corresponding author: shihyen@gate.sinica.edu.tw 
 
In this report, the influence of flat-band Fermi level position on the performances of the single-period 
QDIPs prepared by molecular beam epitaxy (MBE) is investigated. With the Fermi level raised from near/below 
the ground state to close/above the excited state, devices with no response, highest response and depressed 
response are observed. The phenomenon is attributed to three different electron occupancy situations at the QD 
region resulted from the position of the flat-band Fermi level: (a) electrons fully depleted from the QDs, (b) 
electron fully occupied the ground states with most of excited states remained empty and (c) electron partially 
occupied the excited states. The results suggest that unlike quantum-well infrared photodetectors (QWIPs), wide 
Fermi level tuning range is available for QDIPs, which is advantageous for dark current depression to achieve 
high-temperature operation. Also observed is the decrease of barrier heights with increasing Fermi level position 
in the QD structure. The phenomenon is attributed to the acceptor-like behavior of the depleted QD such that the 
p-type GaAs barrier height would be pushed higher. In this report, three samples with different Fermi level 
positions prepared by solid-source Riber P32 molecular beam epitaxy (MBE) system on (100) oriented 
semi-insulating GaAs substrates are investigated. The sample structures and flat-band diagrams of the samples 
are shown in Fig. 1. Samples with different (d1, d2) parameters (210, 90), (240, 60) and (270, 30) are referred to 
as samples A, B and C, respectively. As shown in Fig. 1, with similar flat band diagram, the change of QD 
position in the device structure would force electrons to fully deplete from QDs, fully occupied ground states and 
partially occupied excited states of the QDs for samples A, B and C, respectively. Therefore, no response would 
be observed for sample A, while 10 K spectral responses could be observed for samples B and C as shown in Fig. 
2. For devices B and C, no responses could be observed under positive applied voltages due to the charge 
neutrality could not be achieved under such operation conditions. The same mechanism has also limited 
photovoltaic (PV)- mode operation of the QDIPs even for the device structure with build-in electric field as 
discussed in this report. Another interesting phenomenon is that the depleted QDs seem to act as acceptors in the 
structures such that the barrier height Ea would change from 458, 425 to 402 meV for samples A, B and C, 
respectively [1]. In this case, device B would be able to operate under higher applied voltage due to dark current 
depression resulted from the higher barrier height compared with device C. This work was supported in part by 
National Science Council, Taiwan under Grant No. NSC 95-2218-E-002-067.

The Surface Morphologies and Optical Characteristics of In0.5Ga0.5As/GaAs Quantum 
Dots with different InGaAs coverage 
 
Chi-Che Tseng, Yi-Hao Chen, Jheng-Nan Chen and Meng-Chyi Wu 
Department of Electrical Engineering, National Tsing Hua University, Hsinchu, Taiwan 
Shu-Ting Chou and Shih-Yen Lin* 
Research Center for Applied Sciences, Academia Sinica, Taipei, Taiwan 
* Corresponding author: shihyen@gate.sinica.edu.tw 
 
The surface morphologies and optical characteristics of In0.5Ga0.5As/GaAs quantum dots (QDs) with 
different InGaAs coverage prepared by molecular beam epitaxy are investigated. Uniform QDs with dot 
densities up to 7x1010 cm-2 are observed for the samples with InGaAs coverage 5 and 6 ML. Excited-state 
luminescence of the two samples resulted from state-filling effect have revealed the high crystal qualities of the 
QD samples. For the sample with 7 ML InGaAs coverage, QD density reduction and PL wavelength blue shift 
are observed. The decrease of QD density is attributed to the QD coalescence with nearby dots and the PL blue 
shift of the sample is attributed to the dominant luminescence of the wetting layer instead of the relaxed InGaAs 
islands. The samples discussed in this report are grown on (100) oriented semi-insulating GaAs substrate by 
Riber P32 solid-sources MBE system under As4 overpressure. Three QD samples with different In0.5Ga0.5As 
coverage of 5, 6 and 7 ML grown at 510 oC denoted to as samples A, B and C are prepared. The wafer structures 
consist of two QD layers with one embedded in 60 nm GaAs matrix for PL measurements and the other on top of 
the samples for AFM measurements. The 1 x 1 μm2 AFM images of three samples are shown in Fig. 1. As shown 
in the figure, QDs densities 7.06x1010, 7.55x1010 and 3.55 x1010 cm-2 with average QD height 7, 7.3 and 9.3 nm 
are observed for sample A, B and C, respectively. The reduced QD density and large QD height for sample C are 
resulted from QD coalescence with nearby dots resulted from the increase of QD size with increasing InGaAs 
coverage [1]. Normalized 10 K PL spectrums of the three samples are shown in Fig. 2 (a). PL peak red shift is 
observed for samples A and B. However, for sample C, PL peak blue shift is observed. The phenomenon is 
attributed to the dominant luminescence of the wetting layer instead of the relaxed InGaAs islands as shown in 
the AFM image of sample C. The multi-peak spectra of samples A and B are attributed to the state-filling effect 
of the QD structures [2]. Excited-state luminescence is observed due to ground state fully occupied with 
photo-excited electrons. To investigate the phenomenon, normalized 10 K PL spectra for sample B measured 
under different laser pumping powers 4.33 and 1.27 W/cm2 are shown in Fig. 2. (b). Decreased excited-state 
luminescence ratio with decreasing laser pumping power is attributed to less pronounced state-filling effect with 
decreasing laser pumping power. The state-filling effect observed for samples A and B under such a low 
excitation power density also suggests the high crystal qualities of the QD samples. This work was supported in 
part by National Science Council, Taiwan under Grant No. NSC 95-2218-E-001-006.
