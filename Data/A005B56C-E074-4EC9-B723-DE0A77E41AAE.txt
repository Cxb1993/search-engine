本研究嘗試利用輕型的即時粒狀物採樣設備，以高樓、通量塔及繫留氣球進行懸浮微粒濃度垂直
分布監測與 PM 陰陽離子成分分析結果，有助於了解近地表懸浮微粒分布狀況及修正模式參數，發展
出更完善、較佳模式性能的空氣品質模式。實驗地點通量塔部分選擇在惠蓀林場中興大學莊秉潔教授
之通量塔上進行，而高樓及繫留氣球則選擇在高雄市大寮區和春技術學院校園內。在 2011 年 9 月 15 
日-17 日及 2011 年 12 月 10 日-12 日惠蓀林場通量塔的懸浮微粒之濃度觀測可以發現，在 26 公尺處量
測的懸浮微粒濃度普遍較 1.5 公尺處量測的濃度稍低，但不顯著，而標準差明顯得較大，顯示植被之
上尚受到植被擾動的影響。懸浮微粒於 2011 年 9 月及 12 月 6 次的量測結果，26 公尺處的懸浮微粒濃
度約為 1.5 公尺處濃度的 0.90 倍。由渦流協變系統量測與計算的參數，2011 年 12 月 10 日、11 日及 12
日的平均摩擦風速為 0.315 m s-1、計算出來的細粒與粗粒乾沉降速度平均為 0.013 cm s-1 及 0.198 cm

能量收支平衡，能量通量向上，垂直擴散較好阻抗較小。而粒狀物濃度梯度實驗結果，因受到採樣高

予以修正。粉塵量測儀以繫留氣球為載具量測懸浮微粒濃度垂直分布的結果顯示，大部分的實驗顯示，

一、前言 
一般的空氣品質模式考量了污染物排放量、乾溼沈降、擴散、傳輸、氣態及固態物質的化學反應、
氣固間的轉換、氣象與複雜地形，模式中包含了許多模組與參數的設定，這些機制與輸入值必須考量
到其適用性及本土化，否則無法獲得良好的模擬結果，也無法確知誤差產生的來源。空氣品質模式常
常被用來模擬污染物濃度，進而找出主要貢獻污染源，並為減少空氣污染事件提供管制規劃，而乾沉
降速度的模擬，可提供不同植被地區對於污染物淨化能力的推估。這是目前空氣品質模擬與應用，以
及在空氣品質環境影響評估上所常發生的問題，否則無法獲得良好的模擬結果，也無法確知誤差產生
的來源。 
過去本人空氣品質軌跡模式模擬與應用的研究發現，沉降為粒狀物主要的去除機制。乾沉降速度
對粒狀污染物濃度模擬的敏感度約為-20%，直接影響植被對污染物淨化能力的推估，也對污染物的去
除速率與濃度模擬有相當大的影響(Chen et al., 2002)，在 1998 年-2002 年於宜蘭縣、台中市、台中縣梧
棲、台中縣霧峰、南投草屯鎮及南投魚池鄉，利用繫留氣球、採樣袋與分析儀器，進行了大氣邊界層
內氣狀污染物垂直分布的研究，並協助規劃與建立實驗方法，對於大氣污染物的垂直分布狀況、污染
物質特性以及氣狀污染物垂直濃度分布有一定的認知。 
而環保署空氣品質測站例行的監測近地表約 20 公尺內的懸浮微粒、CO、SO2、NOx、O3、CH4及
非甲烷碳氫化合物(NMHC)，但對於邊界層內污染物的垂直分布狀況，相關的研究卻很少，在國內外雖
然有一些研究報告描述大氣污染物的垂直分布狀況，但因為有許多造成此現象的因素，如氣象條件、
污染排放、複雜的物化機制等，污染物質特性、採樣方式與採樣方法的不同，使得污染物垂直濃度會
隨時間與空間存在許多變異。 
尤其在模擬污染濃度與貢獻量時，位於空氣品質管制區交界附近，或是海拔位置較高的受體點，
其結果通常較都會地區來得差，除在受體點附近缺乏氣象資料庫或污染源資料庫失真的影響，一方面
可能是模式各模組機制系統本身的缺失(如模式垂直擴散機制、沉降機制)，另一方面邊界觀測資料的缺
乏也是一個因素。尤其污染事件日污染來源的模擬，對於上風處邊界濃度以及高空沉降(上邊界)貢獻量
的解析往往受到限制。三維的空氣品質在進行模擬時，需要邊界濃度，這些都是欠缺的實驗數據，有
待進一步觀測、研究與探索，才不致造成解析粒狀污染物質污染來源貢獻與模擬結果的誤差。 
 
二、研究目的 
本計畫之研究目的在發展及建立一種新穎的懸浮微粒垂直剖面量測技術，一方面進行粒狀物及其
成分近地表垂直剖面分析，一方面修正空氣品質模式粒狀物乾濕沉降機制。計畫研究目的概述如下： 
1. 建立利用通量塔(60m)量測近地表粒狀物濃度垂直分布。在通量塔量測不同高度的 PM10 濃
度，觀察粒狀物在植被層內的垂直剖面。 
2. 配合通量塔微氣象觀測及粒狀物垂直剖面實驗結果，將地表、阻尼參數及乾沉降模組加以修
正並對本土化軌跡模式持續改良，以了解模式對各輸入參數之敏感度特性，以及實驗地點植
被對粒狀物的淨化能力推估。 
3. 利用建築物樓層及繫留氣球，將輕巧的粒狀物濃度量測儀器懸繫於氣球下，量測近地表約 100
公尺內的懸浮微粒濃度，以了解不同污染狀況下，粒狀物垂直分布特性與變異以及垂直擴散
傳輸現象。

Abstract—This paper presents the relationship between the indoor 
and outdoor concentrations and the vertical profiles of PM10 
concentrations near the ground in Fortune Institute of Technology, 
Daliao, Kaohsiung, Taiwan. Sampling was carried out using portable 
optical aerosol monitor automated near real-time measurement. The 
hourly PM10 concentrations measured from Daliao station and 
portable aerosol monitor were compared on 24th and 25th August 
2011. The absolute bias of the PM10 concentrations is 6.6%. The 
measured PM10 data of the portable aerosol monitor agrees well with 
observed data at the Daliao station. The correlation coefficient (R2) is 
0.98. Three sampling sites were chosen, near the ground at 1.5 m 
height (site A), the classroom on the fifth floor (site B), and the 
verandah of the classroom (site C). Experiments were conducted on 
2nd September, 8th September, and 9th September 2011. Twenty four 
hourly average concentration data were collected for each of the three 
sampling sites. The average indoor/outdoor ratio is 0.8980. All ratios 
were below unity for the classroom without any indoor sources. The 
linear regression analysis shows a good agreement with the 
relationship between outdoor and indoor PM10 concentrations. The 
correlation coefficient (R2) is 0.97. The PM10 concentrations gradients 
below 17.5 m were always negative. The highest PM10 concentration 
measured 124.8 µg/m3 occurred at 13:00 on 9th September 2011. The 
PM10 concentrations decreased at 17.5 m height and had a ratio of 
about 0.90 to its surface concentration. The largest vertical 
concentration decline with -19.6% to the near ground concentration 
occurred at 12:00 on 9th September 2011. 
 
Keywords— indoor air quality, particle matter, vertical profile. 
I. INTRODUCTION 
ARTICULATE matter, especially fine aerosol, has been 
linked with a wide range of adverse health effects [1]–[5]. 
As most time many epidemiological studies have shown the 
results that begun to focus on the influence of the suspended 
particles for human exposure estimates and human health. Some 
scholars measured and estimated the exposure risk of air 
pollution where people crowd, such as hospitals, home, schools, 
railway stations and other indoor and outdoor air pollutants hot 
spots [6]–[7]. The measurement results will be used as a 
warning of the health of the people living in these spaces. These 
data can also be used as the basis for the physical and mental 
health risk assessment. 
 
Chien-Lung Chen.  Chen is with the Department of Industrial Engineering 
and Management, Fortune Institute of Technology, Kaohsiung 831, Taiwan 
(phone: +886955343966; fax: +88677889777; e-mail: clchen@fotech.edu.tw).  
Ying I. Tsai.  Tsai is with the Department of Environmental Engineering and 
Science, Chia Nan University of Pharmacy & Science, Tainan 717, Taiwan 
(e-mail: mtsaiyi@mail.chna.edu.tw ).. 
In recent years, many studies have found that the vertical 
profiles of pollutant concentrations were different from those 
measured at air quality monitoring station [8]–[11]. To 
understand the vertical distributions of the pollutant 
concentrations near the ground over the first few meters of the 
boundary layer is important in improving the air quality model 
and the human exposure risk estimates. Currently some air 
quality model and exposure estimates have been assumed that 
the pollutant concentration is uniform over some 
three-dimensional space, such as the vegetation canopy of the 
forest trees, the volume of a workshop or the confines of a street 
[12]–[16]. The assumption of spatial uniformity can be 
erroneous for concerning the vertical contributions of pollutant 
concentrations near the ground. In this study we used a novel 
automated particle sampling equipment, measured the particle 
concentrations at different levels and indoor/outdoor 
environments, are presented and discussed. 
II. DESCRIPTION OF THE EXPERIMENT AND MEASURING 
TECHNIQUE 
A. Description of the experiment 
The study was conducted between 24th August and 9th 
September 2011. The period was selected as meteorologically 
stable without high pollution episodes. An experimental 
location was chosen at the lecturing building with seven floors 
in Fortune Institute of Technology, Daliao, one of the major 
townships in Kaohsiung, Taiwan (shown in Fig. 1). The first 
sampling site (noted as A in Fig. 1) was near the ground at 1.5m 
height. The second sampling site (noted as B in Fig. 1) was a 
classroom on the fifth floor (17.5 m above the ground) with a 
capacity of about 50 persons was chosen for the indoor particle 
concentration measurements. The room with dimensions of 14m 
× 8m × 4m (length × width × height) was naturally ventilated 
through 10 large double-glazed windows (no forced ventilation 
with air conditioning system but was not used in the classroom). 
The third sampling site (noted as C in Fig. 1) was the verandah 
of the classroom for the outdoor particle concentration 
measurements at elevated level. 
The classroom was occupied from Monday through Friday, 
not all the time usually from 8 AM to 5 PM. To avoid the indoor 
activities and the occupancy usually changed may represent a 
major and uncertain source of particle matter, the sampling 
periods were conducted during the classroom was not occupied.
