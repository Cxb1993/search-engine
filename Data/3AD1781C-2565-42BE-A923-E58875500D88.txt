Abstract 
 
This study investigate the feasibility of cleaning oil-contaminated sand by coal particles which 
utilization attrition mass transfer and oil agglomeration mechanisms.  The attrition action produced 
by ball-milling with steel ball provided the shearing force for liberation of coal-oil agglomerates 
and clean sands.  Eventually the coal-oil agglomerates were coated on the surface of steel-ball and 
were separated from clean sand.  The cleaning efficiency and sand recovery was 90% and 95% for 
fresh oil contaminated sand respectively.  However for aged (heated) oil contaminated sand, the 
cleaning efficiency dropped to below 60%.  In this study we soften the aged oil on sand surface by 
adding kerosene, isoamyl acetate, cyclohexane, and short-chain alkenes to improve the cleaning 
efficiency.  The results indicated that cyclohexane demonstrated the best cleaning efficiency.  
Using a mixture of 5-mm and 10-mm steel balls the cleaning efficiency increase to over 80%. 
 
Keywords: oil-contaminated sand, coal particle, oil agglomeration, attrition mass transfer
