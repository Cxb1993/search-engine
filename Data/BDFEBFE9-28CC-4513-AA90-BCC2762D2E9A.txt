  “改良型異質接面雙極性電晶體之製作與研究”

Abstract 
As an alternative to InGaP/GaAs material systems 
for applications in heterojunction bipolar transistors 
(HBTs), InGaP/GaAs0.93Sb0.07 heterostructure grown 
by metalorganic chemical-vapor deposition is 
demonstrated. The studied InGaP/ GaAsSb/GaAs 
double HBT (DHBT) device structures are identical 
to the control HBTs except for the addition of Sb to 
the base layer. The InGaP/GaAsSb/GaAs DHBT 
exhibits a turn-on voltage reduction when compared 
to the control InGaP/GaAs HBT. The dc current gain 
 of the InGaP/GaAsSb/GaAs DHBT is virtually 
independent of collector current at 300 K.  variation 
with temperature is slight at medium currents over 
the range 300 K to 390 K. 
 
I. Introduction 
InGaP/GaAs heterojunction bipolar transistors 
(HBTs) have become a mainstay device technology 
for accomplishing high-speed integrated circuits. 
InGaP/GaAs HBTs provide various advantages over 
AlGaAs/GaAs HBTs stemming from the inherent 
properties of the InGaP/GaAs material system [1], 
[2].  
In the last several years, significant effort has been 
devoted to the development of small-bandgap 
materials that can be grown on GaAs [3]-[6] or InP [7] 
substrates. An alternative scheme involves GaAsSb 
alloys grown compressively strained on GaAs 
substrates. The staggered (“type II”) band lineup [8], 
[9] of GaAs and GaAsSb facilitates the unhindered 
transport of electrons from the GaAsSb base to the 
GaAs collector of a GaAsSb/GaAs HBT. To date, 
however, there has been little work assessing 
InGaP/GaAsSb/GaAs HBTs so as to allow a 
judicious comparison with competing InGaP/GaAs 
HBTs [4]-[6]. Thus, though InGaP/GaAsSb/GaAs 
has been shown to be promising HBT material, its 
entire potential has not yet been demonstrated. 
Variation and stability of device characteristics at 
elevated temperatures is important for using HBTs in 
high-speed integrated circuits. Although there have 
been several publications on HBTs [1], [10]-[12], no 
attention has been devoted to investigating the 
influence of temperature on GaAsSb/GaAs HBTs. 
Moreover, no efforts have been made to study the 
offset voltage VCE,offset of InGaP/GaAsSb/GaAs 
double heterojunction bipolar transistors (DHBTs). 
In this study, the temperature dependence of current 
gain in InGaP/GaAsSb/GaAs DHBTs is reported and 
the results are compared with that of InGaP/GaAs 
HBTs having the same device geometry and 
 3
currents. At low collector currents (Region 1) the 
reduction of current gain with increasing temperature 
is brought about the space-charge recombination. 
Furthermore, in Region 3, since the studied DHBTs 
have sharp base-emitter heterojunction and they are 
operated at high collector currents, the space-charge 
recombination is negligibly small. Consequently, it 
can be argued that at high collector currents (Region 
3) the current gain is dominated by the base bulk 
recombination. 
 
IV. Conclusion 
DHBTs are fabricated from InGaP/GaAsSb 
heterostructures grown by MOCVD on 
semi-insulating GaAs substrates. The 
InGaP/GaAsSb/GaAs DHBT device characteristics 
exhibit the anticipated decrease in VBE,on. The 
InGaP/GaAsSb/GaAs DHBT reveals normalized 
gain variations of less than 1% over a temperature 
range of 300 K to 390 K. The observed temperature 
dependence of the current gain is much smaller than 
what is observed in the control InGaP/GaAs HBTs at 
medium collector currents. A very weak dependence 
of the current gain on VCB is observed, whish 
suggests the absence of the potential barrier at the 
base-collector interface.
