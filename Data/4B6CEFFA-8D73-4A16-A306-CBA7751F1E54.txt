一、前言 
隨著工業的發展，製程技術不斷地往微型尺寸邁進，奈米技術匯集力學、電學、光學、
材料、化工、製造、量測、生醫工程、微機電等技術，隨著工業技術的奈米化發展，對於
產品製造、機件對位的高精確度、高解析度要求也不斷地提升，這也使得量測技術扮演著
舉足輕重的角色。奈米量測使人類得以窺視並控制奈米結構，也加速了奈米科技的進展，
奈米量測儀器設備都需要高精密度、高解析度與長行程的掃描平台支援，因此奈米定位技
術乃是奈米研發科技的關鍵技術。奈米尺寸意指 100 nm~0.1 nm 的尺寸範圍。傳統接觸式
量測系統可能會對待測物造成破壞，或者因為接觸式量測系統與待測物之間相互接觸相互
影響而產生量測誤差；有鑑於此，非接觸性的量測技術因而較受到青睞，其中光學干涉技
術以其高精確度、高解析度及高靈敏度而備受矚目。 
二、研究目的 
 本研究預計開發一種以二極體雷射波長調制的外差干涉術為基礎的奈米級位移量測及
定位控制技術。首先於第一年的研究期間，建立二極體雷射波長調制的外差干涉術，並且
利用此外差架構搭配高密度全像光柵的面內精密位移量測系統－「波長調制外差干涉儀」。
利用光柵刻劃的間距來定量位移而與光源的波長無關，不但量測精確度高而且可避免光源
波長不穩定等環境影響。此系統擁有高精密度、高解析度與高靈敏度之量測能力，可滿足
奈米定位技術的要求。 
  第二年研究期間，以第一年建立的波長調制外差光源架構為基礎，搭配散斑干涉技術，
建立一套滾轉位移量測技術－「波長調制外差散斑干涉儀」。此系統以任意的光散射面作為
待測物，可針對面內位移及滾轉進行量測。 
三、文獻探討 
 近二十年間，雷射二極體開始受到重視[1]。雷射二極體有小尺寸、低驅動功率、高光
電轉換效率、高調制頻率與可量產化等特性，因此有研究提出利用其波長可調變性取代常
見的外差移頻架構來獲得外差光源[2][3]。於 1962 年問世的雷射二極體起初因為波長範圍很
小且同調性很短，量測解析度與量測範圍受到限制；近期雷射技術發達，有外腔式的可調
波長雷射出現，使得量測的解析度與量測範圍大幅提升，有研究[4]將波長調制技術應用到
塊規的長度量測或校正上。雷射二極體在量測上的應用研究多數集中在絕對距離或表面形
貌的量測，其中又以日本研究員對此興趣尤甚。日立公司[2]與東京理科大學 Y. Ishii 的研究
團隊[3][5]等至今提出為數不少的論文與專利。 
 光柵干涉術從 90 年代開始被科學家應用，其利用光柵刻劃的間距來定量位移，與光源
的波長無關，不需要穩頻的光源，不但大幅將低成本，亦能減少環境因素對儀器產生的影
響。光柵干涉儀的高解析度及不受環境影響的優越性能，使得有愈來愈多的研究利用光柵
四、研究方法

方法。

本計畫研究成果與原計畫目標相符合、順利達成預期目標情況，研究成果一部份已整理完
    本研究提出利用波長調制式雷射二極體，配合設定的光程差產生外差光源。其中，配
合高密度光柵建構出「波長調制外差光柵干涉儀」；以及結合散斑干涉技術的「波長調制外
差散斑干涉儀」兩套量測系統。兩系統利用新開發的相位演算法可取代鎖相放大器擷取相
位變化，經過計算即可得到面內位移與滾轉角，由實驗得到的數據，可歸納出以下結論： 
1. 根據式的理論計算：「波長調制外差光柵干涉儀」當光柵線距為 1.6 微米時，系統的解析
度約可達 1 pm，靈敏度為 1.728 °/nm，所以光柵線距愈小，便可提高系統的靈敏度；「波
長調制外差散斑干涉儀」當入射角為 45°時，系統電子解析度為 1 pm，靈敏度為
0.802°/nm，欲提昇量測解析度與靈敏度，可將入射角增加以達到目標。 
由目前的實驗數據與電容式位移計相互驗証，本系統有能力偵測到奈米級的位移變化或
是微小的機械振盪，具有高精確性及高靈敏度的優點。未來將可應用於高精密移動平台
之位移及定位之感測等。 
3. 實際的量測環境會造成量測的雜訊，包括了空氣擾動、機械振盪等低頻雜訊，或是雷射
光源、感測器等電子高頻雜訊，以及外差干涉術本身存在的非線性週期性誤差。 
目前所完成之量測系統與實驗步驟，尚有許多地方需要改進，以提高系統之完整性與
便利性。在未來之研究方向上，根據目前所遭遇到的困難以及可改進之處提出以下幾點建
議： 
1. 實驗架構體積：在本研究中未來將朝向將架構縮小的方向去實行，可設計一微型化的機
構，並加入稜鏡與光纖配合收光，以期可將整體的實驗架構體積微型模組化。 
2. 多維度或多功能線上即時量測：目前程式設計僅對於平台做非即時性量測，將來可進一
行研發一線上即時的量測軟體工具，撰寫出一完整的人機介面程式，必能帶給儀器步自
使用 利。

presented. The phase difference variation between s and p polarization resulting from the SPR was 
detected by the polarization interferometry. In the polarization interferometry, the light reflected from 
the SPR sensor was divided into four phase quardrature parts by polarization components. By means 
of an algorithm similar to phase shifting interferometry, the phase distribution of SPR bio-sensor was

Introduction 
Traditional biochemical analysis or biomolecular detection often relies on fluorescence microscopy, 
which can provide high throughput screening. However, the detection process of fluorescence 
microscopy is quite time-consuming, so that it is hard to do real-time measurement. Recently, the 
surface plasmon resonance (SPR) [1-4] with optical phase detection technique has emerged as an 
important and powerful biochemical analysis tool, because SPR is label-free and has high sensitivity 
to the surface effect. Several methods have been proposed to image the full field phase of the 
excitation of SPR, such as the phase shifting interferometry (PSI)[1]. Nevertheless, conventional PSI 
is too easily affected by surrounding noise, such as mechanical vibration, because of the 
non-common-path optical configuration. 
In this paper, a full field polarization interferometer (PI) [5-7] is developed to image the optical 
phase of SPR bio-sensor. Our measurement system includes an expanded collimated laser source, a 
SPR bio-sensor, and a PI. The collimated linear polarized light reflected from the SPR bio-sensor is 
sent to the PI, in which some optical components (e.g., beam splitters and polarizers) divide the light 
into four phase quardrature parts. These four phase quardrature light beams are used to estimate the 
optical phase resulting from SPR. Because our PI has a common-path optical configuration, our 
method can get rid of the surrounding noise. So far, we have successfully observed the local phase 
difference, which resulted from the surface effect of difference materials within 1×1 mm
2
 full field 
range. If only the electronic noise is considered, the measurement resolution of the refractive index of 
our system is estimated to be 1×10
-7

Abstract. The precision positioning device becomes significant requirement in scientific instruments 
used for the applications of nanotechnology for a few decades. To achieve the high resolution 
positioning, the sensing methods of displacement sensor become more important in positioning 
device. In this paper, a novel method is presented for measuring both in-plane/out-plane 
displacements with the single heterodyne grating interferometry (HGI). We demonstrated the 1D, 2D, 
and 3D measurement results respectively and the smallest displacement can be detected was better 
than 6 pm. Furthermore, the nanometer resolution can be ensured within 20 µm displacement. Hence, 
the in-plane/out-plane measurements with single apparatus can be realized by our method and might 
be a displacement sensor using in the motorized stage with suitable opto-mechanics structure 
minimization. 
Introduction 
The precision positioning device, which are in-plane or out-plane positioning device or both, becomes 
significant requirement in scientific instruments used for the applications of nanotechnology for a few 
decades. The piezoelectric actuator and the displacement sensor will play the role of the positioning 
and the feedback control of the positioning apparatus. To achieve the high resolution positioning, the 
sensing methods of displacement sensor become more important. The homodyne sensing method [1, 
2] and heterodyne sensing method [3] are the two popular categories in displacement sensor methods. 
Salbut [1] summarized a few articles with grating interferometry as the in-plane displacement sensor 
and also proposed five different types of the sensing head using in the waveguide grating 
interferometry. Based on the fringe pattern analysis technology, the 20 nm accuracy of the in-plane 
displacement sensing system could be achieved. Wu et al. [3] developed dual frequency heterodyne 
interferometry for the out-plane displacement measurement which is based on the moving grating 
with constant speed to induce the Doppler shift frequency in diffracted beams and the heterodyne 
signal is generated. In Wu’s method, the standard deviation was about 1.8 nm for the movement range 
within 0.18 µm. Both of them can not provide the three dimensional (3D) displacement measurements 
with single detection apparatus. Hence, the number of sensor, alignment procedures, and 
manufactured cost will be larger and complicated. 
To overcome these drawbacks, a novel method is presented for measuring both in-plane/out-plane 
displacements with the single heterodyne grating interferometry (HGI). The novel setup of the optical 
configuration can reduce the airstreams disturbance and maintain the environmental vibration at 
minimum level, allowing high stability and low uncertainty to be achieved. Resulting from the 
theoretical calculation, our method can be sensitive to the picometer level and the reliability of 
picometer displacement measurement is assured. In this paper, we demonstrated the 1D, 2D, and 3D 
measurement results respectively and the smallest displacement can be detected was better than 6 pm. 
Furthermore, the nanometer resolution can be ensured within 20 µm displacement. Hence, the 
in-plane/out-plane measurements with single apparatus can be realized by our method and might be a 
positioning sensor using in the motorized stage with suitable opto-mechanics structure minimization.

 Experiments and Results 
A heterodyne light source consisted of a linearly polarized He-Ne laser at 632.8 nm with frequency 
stabilized control and an electro-optic modulator EO (model: 4001, New Focus, Inc.) driven by a 
function generator with 1 kHz sawtooth signal. The diffraction grating was mounted on the precision 
x-y-z stage (model: 8095, New Focus, Inc.). The driver (model: LS-773, New Focus, Inc.) of this 
stage was designed as the open-loop circuit without feedback control. The grating pitches were 
pre-determined by atomic force microscopy (AFM) and they were 1.6 µm (1D grating) and 1.1 µm 
(2D grating). The 1D, 2D, and 3D measurement results are shown in Figs. 2, 3, and 4, respectively. In 
the figure 2, the smallest displacement can be detect in our method is about 6 pm. This displacement 
might be come from the electronics noise of the stage. And the total difference between our method 
and HP 5529A was within 10 nm (except the priori noise coming from electronic signal). In figure 3
