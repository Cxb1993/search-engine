摘 要
國際海事組織（IMO）理事會所屬海洋環境保護委員會（Marine Environment Pollution
Committee, MEPC）已於 1997 年 7 月制定溫室氣體排放規範，各國海運界亦已積極提出
各種減排與節能之有效措施，以我國航運之發達現況及經濟對海運之依存關係，如何減
少船舶廢氣排放，為當前重要議題。減排與節能是一體兩面，在維持等功輸出的同時，
可一併降低耗能，減少包含 CO2在內的廢氣排放。
本研究除彙整航商已提出之節能與環保之措施外，並透過問卷調查方式，獲知一般
船員、岸上管理階層及相關產業界，對節能減碳的必要性，已達九成以上的共識。同時，
藉由能源盤查，了解船舶電動機耗能結構，並針對船用電動機之現況，透過實驗提出藉
並聯電容提高其功率因數，以提高船舶整體能源使用效率，進而達到節能減排目標。
關鍵字：國際海事組織、溫室效應、節能減排、能源盤查、功率因數。
Abstract
International Maritime Organization (IMO) has set the norm system about the
greenhouse gas emissions, the maritime industry in developed countries has also set
effective policies for emission-reducing and energy-conservation for ships. This issue is
essential for Taiwan’s trade-depended economy. This research is to seek the opportunities for
merchant ships in reducing fuel consumption and emissions without sacrificing operating
performance. Currently applied measures and technologies foe energy conservation on-board
ship are complied and analyzed. Background information and an overview of green-shipping
issues are also developed. The result of a survey performed in this study indicated that there is
a high degree of willingness for marine industry to improve energy efficiency and reduced
emissions from ship. The structures of electrical energy use were established through energy
audit, and measures for conservation were suggested. The experimental results indicated that
paralleled capacitors can be used to enhance power factor for motor and further improve
energy efficiency as a whole.
Key words： IMO, greenhouse effect, energy-conservation and reduce-emissions, energy
audits, power factor.

船用電動機
交流感應電動機為船舶電力之主要負載，如泵送流體維持主機和輔機之正常運轉，舵
機、錨機、起貨機、舷梯及冷凍貨櫃等甲板機械裝置之操作使用，均應用交流感應電
動機。其多置放於密閉船艙內部或甲板上，由於長年的海上航行必須面對海洋天候的
特性和發電機負載變動的影響以及操作人員更替不定等特殊狀況。
受發電機負載變動的影響
 發電機負載的變動：發電機負載急劇變動將導致供電品質不佳，電源額定電壓及
頻率低下。
 負載電流的變化：電動機在額定電流以上運轉時，線圈溫度會持續增高，成為絕
緣劣化、壽命縮短或線圈燒損之主因。
5船舶能源盤查（Energy Audit）的意涵是指所有應用在使船舶正常推進狀況下所必須運
轉的主機、輔機、電動馬達、照明及其它耗用能源的裝備及機具，將其日常運轉的狀
況及耗能情形進行統整，預防異常現象的發生並針對能源之供應與使用，加以分析、
統計及評估，從中發現能源消耗的異常或尋求可以改善之機會，藉以制訂節約能源措
施，進而提高能源使用效率，降低船舶營運成本。
初級盤查標的
本研究初級盤查以柴油機為主機及發電機之學生實習船、貨櫃船、散裝貨輪為主要目
標船舶。能源初級盤查首先針對目標船型、主機、發電機原動機及發電機容量概況做
一般性之彙整。
航行摘要統計
柴油機船舶耗能結構，以主推進引擎（柴油主機）和柴油發電機燃用重油或船用柴油，
用以推進船舶及供應輔機運轉所需之電能、照明以及船員生活上所需電力的需求。為
了解船舶耗能之實際情況，針對具有完整摘要紀錄之貨櫃輪及鋼料散裝輪做輪機日誌
摘要統計及圖表分析。本研究調查即以柴油機為船舶主機之貨櫃船、散裝貨輪、客貨
輪及實習船為主要目標船舶進行節能研究。

發電機發電量與耗油量之關係
大型船舶主機多採用大型低速、二衝程、單動、十字頭型活塞及排氣過給式柴油機，
發電機則多採用中高速、四衝程、單動、筒型活塞及排氣過給式柴油機。晚近燃油資
源日趨短絀，取得成本日趨增加。船舶在營運策略上，早已朝向發展單一且較低廉價
格的船用重油（MFO）。以目標船鋼料散裝輪為例，其發電機 75%負載之耗油率為
214g/kw-h、50%負載之耗油率為 225.1g/kw-h，參考該輪輪機日誌記載資料，在港負載
約於 230kw~270kwf 區間，故模擬其耗油率為 225.1g/kw-h。
主要輔機能源盤查
馬達在重柴油引擎船舶上就扮演著輔助引擎運轉的重要角色，各式泵均須配置功率大
小不同的馬達，作為動力來源，馬達運轉的特性是屬於電感性負載，其功率因數一般
在改善前約為 0.5~0.85，其更低者也有監測到 PF=0.2 者。假如船用電動機能依其使用
特性之不同，應用電容性負載的投入，將「功率因數」提升，即能降低其使用電流，
減低發電機之負載，進而節省燃油之消耗。

結論與建議
 針對輔機（含鍋爐）進行能源效率提昇，散裝船所可能獲致的效益優於貨櫃船，
其中靠泊時間長的船舶尤為顯著。
 適當並聯進相電容器，可以改善功率因數降低耗能，在營運中之船舶，檢查其感
應馬達之 PF 值，考慮汰舊 PF 值太低的機器或改善，可減少不當氣體排放、減緩
地球暖化速度。
 新建或規劃中之船舶，可研議將感應馬達做個別化的提昇功率因數之裝置。
 以本研究抽樣之貨櫃輪為例，對航行或泊港中之船舶，每一航次能節省燃料費
98126 元（673.7 小時）對於提升營運績效，每年計算可節省 1715500 元，值得再作
進一步的探討。
 以本研究問卷調查之結果可得知，船舶具有節能之空間且為船公司必須面對之ㄧ
項議題。

2.1 Energy Saving Strategies
There are so-caled “bolt-on” performance improvers, of which typical suggestions include slow steaming, power take-offs,
exhaust gas recovery, use of blended or residual fuel in diesel generators, upgrading existing main engine, ducted and other
propellers.[2,3] This category of improvement measures usually involves retrofitting existing vessels and thus requires additional
capital investment. It is therefore necessary to measure the financial attractiveness before the adoption. There are other measures that
require not a large capital outlay compared with the above mentioned bolt-on improvers. Examples of this category include minimum
ballast and optimal trim, self-polishing underwater paint, propeller polishing, underwater scrubbing, reduction of electrical load,

Owing to recent hikes of oil prices, major players in the maritime industry have renewed the important task of reducing fuel
consumption on board their fleets. For example, in a recent speech, Robert Pedersen of A.P. Moller–Maersk has included operation
optimization and innovation in the list of energy efficiency improvement strategies adopted by the maritime giant.[4] A performance
control system, which has been in operation since 1975, was the key monitoring tool to facilitate daily reporting and follow-up on
performance data from all ships. Optimization of operations for lowest possible fuel consumption was thus pursued based on the data,
weighing factors such as condition of under water paint system, surface condition of propeller, mechanical losses in the engines,
ignition properties in the engines, power consumption onboard, route planning, weather routing, trim, and sharing of sea current
information.
Maersk also looked for improvement opportunities that would enhance the energy consumption of a ship, i.e. technologies or
devices that could achieve more effective engine, more effective propeller, less friction between hull and water, or less wave
resistance, or utilize alternative energy sources. Innovation projects currently undergoing in the company include hot engines for
improved efficiency, alternative propeller designs, micro bubbles for less friction, and flexible hull shapes.[4]
Höegh Autoliners, one of the largest global Ro/Ro operators, has taken similar measures to ease energy consumption onboard its
fleet of 70 Ro/Ro vessels. One aspect of the improvement was through optimization of voyage performance, which took into account
hull condition, propeller condition, autopilot, trim and draft, voyage planning, and weather routing. Reduction in fuel consumption
was also achieved by tuning or other efficiency improvement measures applied on major energy consumers aboard, such as main
engine, aux engines, boiler, and thruster operation. Höegh also utilized the concept of total energy management in fuel procurement
and hedging, and to oversee bunkering process.[5]
2.2 Factors Affecting Marine Fuel Consumptions
In searching for measures to save fleet fuel consumption, studies had looked into factors that affect the consumption.
Speed of a vessel
Speed of vessel is among the mostly mentioned factors influencing fuel consumptions of an ongoing merchant ship. Many
studies demonstrated a positive relationship between ship’s speed and the fuel consumption per unit of time.[2,6,7]. Several also
developed a positive relationship between per unit distance fuel consumption and speed.[2,7] To be exact, Taylor (1996) postulated
that fuel consumption (per unit of time) is positively related to 2/3 power of displacement and cube of speed, while per unit distance
fuel consumption is directly related to the square of speed, or:

(1) Weather impact
Weather is also widely acknowledged as an important factor influencing the consumptions of bunkers. Weather will affect the
voyage duration, which, in turn, has a significant impact on total bunker consumption during a voyage. The effects of weather on the
duration of a cross-ocean voyage usually channel to changes in ship’s speed of advance. The speed reduction can be remarkable; as a 
result, the chance of voyage delay due to weather conditions is very high. A study into the duration of voyages between selected ports
over a period of five years demonstrates clearly the considerable variance en route times due to the influence of weather actually
encountered.[6]

The result confirms a positive relation between ship speed and fuel consumption. The elasticity of fuel consumption with respect
to speed is estimated to be 0.938724. In other words, a 1% reduction in the speed of the vessel is estimated to result in 0.94% savings
in marine fuel consumption. This energy savings prospect is surprisingly less than predicted by the marine engineering literature.
Marine engineering theory usually postulates a two-degree direct relation between ship speed and fuel consumption. A unit increase
in speed is expected to trigger two-degree increase in the fuel consumption. In other words, increasing rate of savings in fuel
consumption is expected from an additional decrease of speed.[7] Our result, however, depicts no such increasing rate of energy
savings that may result from lowering ship speed.
Nevertheless, ship speed reduction is still a priority energy saving strategies. The elasticity of fuel consumption with respect to
ship speed is relatively high, comparing with other factors considered in the model. Cost savings from ship speed reduction can be
very significant, especially during the era of high energy prices. This result has important implication to ship operating practice. It
warrants serious consideration a careful voyage planning which emphasizes shorter port stay so as to allow more at-sea time. The
faster port turnaround time gives possibility to decrease the vessel speed at sea. This is especially true for schedule operation ships,
such as ferries and container vessels. Turnaround time can be reduced, for example, by improving maneuvering performance or
enhancing cargo flows with innovate ship design, ramp arrangement or lifting arrangements.[9]
