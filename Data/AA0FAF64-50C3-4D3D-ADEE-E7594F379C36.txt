這一年申請人不僅以國立交通大學奈米中心(NFC)以及國立台灣科技大學竹北潔淨室來
做製程研發的精進，同時在絕緣層上覆矽(SOI; Silicon-on-insulator)波導的設計與量測上皆有
顯著地成效。
對於從廠商來的絕緣層上覆矽的晶片，可以藉由標準 RCA 製程步驟去除掉附著在晶圓表
面的顆粒。而促進黏著性的六甲基二矽氮烷 Hexamethyldisilazane（HMDS），則普遍使用在
矽晶圓工業中，並促進黏著性，並增加光阻以及基材間的附著力。光波導的圖案和形狀可藉
由光學接觸式微影和顯影方式定義。簡易的製程步驟可由圖一闡述。之後矽基板要做晶圓切
割和端面研磨，並個別用掃描式電子顯微鏡（SEM）和光學顯微鏡檢測其品質好壞。

在光纖與波導之間的空隙填入 newprort 的耦合液（matching fluid index），其折射係數
與二氧化矽略同，可有效的降低反射損失（reflection loss）。實際量測中，光纖與光纖耦合損

極化相依損耗(PDL; Polarization dependent loss)為光電元件在不同偏振極化下所造成的
損失，尤其在長距離通訊領域中所要求的損耗極為嚴格下，任何損失都必須是被關注的課題。
在小尺度(微米)光被動元件中，極化相依損耗為測量元件特性好壞的方式，也是測試設計、
製程、測量這一系列過程是否有缺陷的良好機會。
極化相依損耗的定義為一光電被動元件導以各種可能極化狀態的光，測量其輸出功率最
大值與最小值的差再取對數即為極化相依損耗。

Wavelength-Insensitive Tap Waveguide Using
Mach-Zehnder Structures on Silicon-on-insulator Platform
Shih-Hsiang Hsu
Department of Electronic Engineering, National Taiwan University of Science and Technology
No. 43, Sec. 4, Keelung Rd., Taipei, Taiwan
E-mail: shsu@mail.ntust.edu.tw
Abstract: A wavelength-insensitive tap waveguide using a relative phase shift between two
directional couplers was demonstrated to get 3.7% coupling ratio and 0.25 dB chromaticity across
the whole C-band via 12m thick silicon-on-insulator waveguide.

Introduction
One or more tap waveguides, implemented in the optical components for monitoring, is usually utilized to extract a
portion of the light signal traveling alone a primary waveguide. Furthermore, a photodetector can be positioned onto
tap ports to receive the light signals on the tap waveguide. A portion of the light signal from the primary channel is
carried by the tap waveguide, which output can be utilized to indicate characteristics on the primary waveguide. A
wavelength-insensitive Mach-Zehnder interferometer (MZI), composed of two synchronous directional couplers
(DC) of different coupling lengths separated by an uncoupled region in which a relative phase-shift is introduced,
can make the optical power divider insensitive to the deviations in wavelength, polarization, and fabrication [1].
Recently silicon-on-insulator (SOI) is a critical platform for integrated optoelectronic circuits since it offers the
potential of monolithic integration for photonic and electronic functions on a single substrate. Integrating photonics
functions on a silicon platform will be a low cost solution if integrated optoelectronics circuits are feasible.
Birefringence is a serious problem faced by many photonics devices, which results in polarization dependent
wavelength (PDW) shift and polarization dependent loss (PDL). However the beam propagation method (BPM)
with semi-vectorial capability to simulate SOI waveguides shows that the waveguide height of a few microns has
lower birefringence than one micron or less height waveguide, which is approximately 10-2, two orders of
magnitude larger [2]. A bigger core size for SOI waveguide is also preferred by the interconnection coupled with the
conventional fiber.
Study of wavelength-insensitive MZI tap and thick SOI waveguide
The configuration of MZ structures constructs optical couplers, which consist of two couplers connected by two
MZ waveguide arms, serving as phase shifters. When light is launched into one of the input ports, it is split into two
MZ arms by the first coupler. As light travels through the MZ arms, the phase difference can be altered due to the
optical path difference between two waveguides. After passing through the second coupler, two beams recombine
either constructively or destructively at either of two output ports, depending upon the exact phase difference
between the two MZ arms controlled by path difference. The wavelength insensitive MZI is comprised of a pair of
DCs with a relative phase shift between them, shown in Fig. 1.
