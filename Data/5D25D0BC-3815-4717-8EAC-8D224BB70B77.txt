總計畫：周邊神經修復組織工程整合計畫 
周邊神經是人體控制和感覺的管線通道，其構造精密而特殊。也由於其細微
精緻，神經也特別脆弱。周邊神經受損不但使運動能力喪失，所造成之感覺異常
和痛楚也影響生活品質。因此對於修補神經的需求和品質要求與日俱增。在台灣
由於地小人稠且交通擁塞，車禍造成的外傷病例不斷增加、經濟蓬勃發展造成營
養過剩，糖尿病人口數目增加、且近來社會槍械暴力案件增多，因此神經受損的
病歷不斷上升。國外也有這些問題，但是在台灣卻日趨嚴重。目前糖尿病和事故
傷害皆為國人十大死因。傳統上，神經傷害的診斷只能依靠電生理檢查，沒有活
體組織結構的診斷工具。而治療上，對於斷裂的神經可以接回，但是由於神經生
長緩慢而效果不佳。對於僅僅受損卻未斷裂的神經則沒有任何有效的治療方式。 
基於本土臨床醫學之需求和提升醫療之品質，本團隊係整合光電、影像處
理、機械、生醫材料、醫學工程和臨床醫學研究人員提出一垂直連貫的神經修復
之研究計畫。計畫目標包括預防神經傷害之擴大、神經傷害之組織結構診斷、神
經傷害之修復治療和相關之基礎生物力學研究。 在本計畫中，創新改良的部分
包括：在預防為運用電刺激、在診斷為光學同調性斷層掃描術(Optical Coherence 
Tomography)、在治療為複合導管併用電刺激，以及運用生物力學分析輔助診斷
和複合導管之設計。 
本三年計劃將主要研究標的放在創傷所造成之神經傷害，次要目標則是延續
完成先期研究在糖尿病性神經病變的研究。如此，主計畫可採用先期計畫發展之
研究技術和經驗，而先期糖尿病性神經病變的研究則可受惠於主計畫之接續創新
研究成果，將先期結果進一步擴展充實，彼此相輔相成。最後希望研究成果對於
創傷和糖尿病之神經病變，兩者的診斷治療皆有實際貢獻。    
研究依原先之架構，分為五個子計畫進行： (1) 神經組織之多種顯微成像
分析與診斷應用，(2) 偏振式光纖同調斷層掃描系統於神經雙折變性質之研究，
(3) 周邊神經生物力學與再生模型，(4) 具誘導生長功能之神經修復導管研究，
以及(5) 電刺激對於周邊神經修復之微觀研究。周邊神經損傷之動物模型統一由
子計畫五負責運用標準方式，以手術及先前注射方式分別誘發小鼠外傷及糖尿病
性神經病變。注射後開始進行實驗。實驗之標的神經為小鼠股骨段之坐骨神經。

本計畫研究分為三年。在第一年的部分，子計畫一應用子計畫五所提供之小

之生長，然後評估神經再生的優劣。另外以電流刺激纖維化遭抑制之神經再生，
比較觀察神經再生的優劣。 
第二年的部分，子計畫一前期針對神經細胞顯微影像的特質，提出一個自動
化且具準確性之分割系統，用來偵測細胞輪廓與計算其個數。後期針對原子力顯
微鏡(AFM)儀器，所掃瞄出來的細胞表面高度影像，利用對位方式找出相對應位
置的組織，重構其表面形貌。此結果作為子計畫三用來模擬細胞受力時形變之模

的結果，並以三角波週期壓縮實驗數據驗證模型的準確性。後期則針對細胞於不
同培養基材下量測其機械性質的分佈與變化趨勢，並配合子計畫五電刺激的結

Abstract 
The main purpose of this subproject was to estimate the biomechanical 
properties of the peripheral neural tissue and cells through in vivo mechanical testing. 
The modeling and simulation of growth of peripheral nerves could be used to design 
better conduits for nerve regeneration. 
This project was divided into three years. In the first year, the dynamic circular 
compression of sciatic nerve of Wistar rats was conducted to yield the stress and strain 
curves of the nerve. Quasi-linear viscoelastic models were fitted to the data obtained 
from constant compression rate tests and parameters of the models were estimated. 
The models were verified by utilizing the results obtained from triangular wave 
compression tests. In subproject 1, the image of the microstructure of the sciatic nerve 
was analyzed and reconstructed from the stained microtome sections of the sciatic 
nerve provided from subproject 5. Based on the reconstructed images, 
two-dimensional finite element models of the sciatic nerve have been built. The goals 
of the second year were twofold.  First was to improve the fitting results between the 
QLV model and the experimental data obtained from the constant compression rate 
tests. Second was focused on the cell mechanics analysis.  An apparatus and 
experimental method were designed for accurately measure the cell mechanical 
properties on different substrates as well as the change of cell elasticity when 
subjected to compressive strain.  The change in cell mechanical properties due to 
longitudinal compression was investigated. In the third year, a two-dimensional finite 
element model of nerve was built and simulations were performed. The model was 
verified by using the experimental data. In the part of cell mechanics, the QLV model 
of PC-12 neuron-like cells was first built by utilizing the atomic force microscopy 
indentation and model fitting methods developed in the second year. The growth 
behavior of the axons was also observed to find the relationship between the 
mechanical properties and the internal structure of cells during differentiation. 
The main contribution of the subproject were: (1) estimate the parameters of 
QLV model from the dynamic circular compression of sciatic nerve of Wistar rats, (2) 
develop a two-dimensional elastic finite element model of sciatic nerve from the 
transverse section image, (3) develop an experimental method by using the AFM 
indentation for 3T3 fibroblast and apply it to PC-12 neuron-like cells, (4) evaluate the 
mechanical properties for each layers of sciatic nerve by using the inverse finite 
element method, (5) combine the QLV theory and Bilodeau contact mechanics model 
to estimate the distribution of viscoelastic properties of the soma and axons of PC-12 
cells.  
一、 研究背景、動機及目的 
人體的周邊神經系統扮演著非常重要的生理角色，一旦神經系統因為外傷或
病變而受損，便會影響病人的日常生活，造成家人與社會的負擔。隨著時代進步，
意外傷害與糖尿病等文明病例不斷增加，周邊神經傷害與病變的發生率亦愈趨上
升。傳統上，周邊神經傷害的診斷只能靠電生理檢查，缺乏活體組織結構的診斷
工具；在治療方面，神經再生則是目前相當受重視且可行性極高的研究方向，以
現今的技術，雖然已經可以接回斷裂的神經，但生長緩慢且效果不佳，同時對於
僅受損而無斷裂的神經並無有效的治療方式。 
神經損傷後如果沒有經過適當的處理，很容易產生神經瘤，使得受損神經無
法恢復功能。近年來為了避免神經瘤的發生及協助受損神經的再生，已發展出不
同的神經修補及再生技術；在周邊神經的再生方面，目前常用神經導管來誘導和
支持神經纖維的生長，利用顯微手術將神經兩斷端縫入一個由適當生醫材料製成
之神經導管的兩端。基本上，這個圓形神經管對神經組織來說就如同一個溫室
般，除了可以導引及支持再生神經纖維成長之外，同時也可以將阻礙神經再生的
細胞及其分泌物阻擋在管外。透過對導管的材料性質最佳化設計、導管內各結締
組織間的力學分析與適當的電刺激輔助，即有可能促進受損神經的重建[1]。 
近年來由於組織工程(tissue engineering)與神經科學(neuroscience)逐漸受到
重視，神經再生的研究已不再限於理論的階段而進入了實際臨床的應用；然而甚
多研究著重於神經再生的方法與技術，關於神經受損後再生的微觀機轉和生長過
程中與周邊結締組織間的力場影響研究不多，所以本研究的動機則是希望藉由神
經的力學研究分析，建立周邊神經再生的模型與模擬，期望能提供臨床上周邊神
經再生技術的突破與做為再生結果評估的理論基礎。 
本研究為三年期計畫。目標為建構神經再生生物力學模型，第一年先建立白
鼠坐骨神經之巨觀構成方程式( constitutive equation )與類線性黏彈模式，第二年
再由微系統技術和結合子計畫五之電刺激探討神經受損後再生微觀機轉以及機
械特性的改變對於再生的影響；第三年輔以子計畫一之神經橫切面影像擷取與處
理，由巨觀與微觀的模式，建立二維及三維再生力學有限元素模型及模擬，並以
實驗數據驗證，提供神經再生導管的設計理論和改進臨床神經再生修復技術。

第二年 
I. 簡介 
本計畫目標是周邊神經橫向力學特性，先前研究由巨觀的角度，引用 Fung
於 1972 年提出之類線性黏彈（Quasi-Linear Viscoelasticity, QLV）理論用以分析
和估測活體周邊神經的機械性質[5]，本研究室范家霖與陳榮健的研究中，觀察
周邊神經應力與應變的關係圖，發現神經為非線性黏彈性材料[8, 9]。針對細胞
力學模型的建構，1996 年 Satcher 等人利用微/奈米結構方法將貼附型細胞當作承
受預應力之網狀結構來研究細胞骨架，進而推導細胞模型[10, 11]，近 10 年來研
究細胞力學特性的利器莫過於原子力顯微鏡（Atomic force microscope），1997 年
Radmacher 提出利用原子力顯微鏡量測細胞的彈性係數的方法[12]。同年，Ricci
等人也運用原子力顯微鏡量測 3T6 細胞的機械特性[13]。1999 年 Rotch 等人發現
纖維母細胞不同部位的機械性質不盡相同[14]。到了 2001 年 Mathur 等人針對血
管內皮細胞(endothelial)與心肌細胞(cardiac muscle)的機械性質進行測量，並與先
前研究成果加以比較 [15]。這些文獻提供了量測細胞力學性質的方法，進而可
以由量測到的數據來提供建構細胞力學模型所需的機械性質。

（一）神經組織力學分析 
本年度的計畫延續前一年度的研究並提出改善類線性黏彈模型嵌合的方

由實驗結果可看出以每秒 10％壓縮率的實驗數據嵌合而得的類線性黏彈模
型，可以完整描述每秒 5％和 1％壓縮率的實驗結果，顯示本年度所提出之快速
一個有效的參數估測方法求得白鼠坐骨神經之類線性黏彈模型，並成功地驗證此

第三年 
I. 簡介 
本年度目標為配合子計畫一和子計畫五之神經橫切面影像擷取與處理，由巨
觀與微觀的模式，建立二維再生力學有限元素模型及模擬，並與實驗數據作驗
證，以提供神經再生導管的設計理論和臨床神經再生修復技術的進步。周邊神經
是由機械性質不同的分層結構所組成，包括神經外膜（epineurium）、神經束膜
（perineurium）和神經內膜（endoneurium）（如圖 17），不規則的幾何輪廓與複
雜的分層結構更增加了模型建立的困難度。本研究應用逆向有限元素法，結合前
兩年在位活體生物力學的實驗結果，來估測小鼠坐骨神經各層結構包括神經內、
外膜及神經束膜之機械特性。 
微觀的研究方面，神經再生的關鍵在於如何引導神經元往正確方向再生，
且期待此再生程序能夠快速完成。近年來雖然已發展出輔助的神經導管以及許多
促進再生的藥物，但是針對神經細胞的機械特性及外在物理性刺激對神經再生影
響的研究仍在多方發展中。本年度的研究著眼於細胞力學的領域，利用原子力顯
微鏡建立一套能準確量測到細胞力學特性的實驗方法，以估測細胞的機械特性，
進而評估細胞整體力學模型。從微觀的角度，瞭解神經細胞再生時其機械性質與
內部結構變化的相關性，同時為了分析周邊神經的生長行為，將觀察軸突的生長
表現和進行相關力學性質的量測，除了提供神經導管設計上的參考外，進一步地
能找到促進神經再生效率的方法。 
II. 研究方法 
（一） 神經組織力學有限元素分析 
逆向有限元素分析之流程如圖 18 所示，首先進行在位活體生物力學實驗，
以自行開發之環形壓縮系統施予坐骨神經環形壓力，得到神經組織之應力-應變
關係。實驗後將坐骨神經段取下進行切片及染色，得到神經各層結構之幾何輪
廓，由影像處理定義出各層的邊界，並建立有限元素模型（圖 19）。逆向有限元
素分析是藉由事先假設材料參數，以有限元素模型分析並模擬材料之力學響應，
與實驗結果相比對，再優化材料參數來求得合理的材料參數值。 
在材料性質設定方面，假設神經組織為均質（homogeneous）和等向性
（isotropic）的材料，且有相關研究指出神經束膜為主要應力承受區[2]，因此本
研究將活體生物力學實驗所求得之應力-應變關係曲線，由應變大於 0.3 之後的線
性範圍求其斜率得到之視楊氏係數（apparent Young’s modulus），定義為神經束
膜之楊氏係數。神經外膜部分是由鬆軟的結締組織所構成，已有相關文獻估測出
鬆軟結締組織之楊氏係數範圍約為 12 kPa [16]，因此在本模型中設定其值為神經
外膜之楊氏係數。在給予神經內膜楊氏係數適當的假設後，藉由有限元素分析結

Transverse Quasi-Linear Viscoelastic Modeling of Rat Sciatic Nerves and Its Applications 
 
Rung-Jian Chena, Ming-Shaung Jua, Chou-Ching K. Linb 
a Dept. of Mechanical Engineering, National Cheng Kung University, Tainan, Taiwan, 
b Dept. of Neurology, National Cheng Kung University Hospital, Tainan, Taiwan 
 
The quasi-linear viscoelastic (QLV) theory has been frequently used to model the nonlinear time- and 
history-dependent viscoelastic behavior of many soft tissues such as tendons, ligaments, and skin [1, 2].  
Most of existing studies on biomechanics of peripheral nerves are focused on the axial tensile 
properties and very few works investigate the transverse biomechanics of peripheral nerves.  In our 
previous work, a custom-designed compression system was developed for in situ static compression of 
the sciatic nerves of Wistar rats [3].  In this work, stress-strain relationships at three different strain 
rates, stress relaxation, and cyclic compression tests were performed.  QLV models were fitted into 
the experimental data and the reduced relaxation function, G(t) and the instantaneous elastic response 
 of sciatic nerves were obtained for normal rats.  The preliminary results reveal that the 
sciatic nerve is a bioviscoelastic solid with nonlinear elasticity and the transverse elastic modulus is 
three orders smaller than that of the axial direction.  It shows that the sciatic nerve is relatively weaker 
on the transverse plane.  This finding may have a contribution to injury prevention of peripheral 
nerves in neural engineering such as neural prostheses.  The other application is minimally invasive 
surgery in which the nerve tissue can be identified with forced response by employing the methodology 
presented in this study.

The main goal of the current study is to investigate the 
biomechanical properties of nerves under in situ circular 
compression.  In past work, an axis-symmetric finite element 
simulation of the aspiration experiment was proposed [1].  A 
fiber-reinforced composite model composed of viscoelastic 
fiber surrounded by viscoelastic matrix was used to model the 
biological architecture of the brainstem to its anisotropic 
mechanical response [2].  In this work, the biomechanical 
parameters of the component layers were estimated by fitting 
the experimental data to the simulation of finite element 
models.  The finite element model may be applied for 
assessment of peripheral nerve repairing and regeneration after 
injury and designing of efficient nerve conduit. 
 
Materials and Methods 
A plane strain model of the nerve, which consists of three 
homogenous components, namely, epineurium, perineurium 
and endoneurium is developed.  The endoneurium is 
composed of a large number of axons surrounded by the 
Schwann cells.  Two different staining methods were utilized 
to mark these layers and the axons. 
The digital images of the transverse cross-section are 
processed by a custom-made image-processing program for 
recognizing the contour of the whole nerve and individual 
fascicles.  The program is written so that the output image 
file is compatible with the finite element software.  We 
hypothesize, according to the previous reports, that the main 
stress-bearing component is the perineurium [3].  The 
viscoelastic properties are described by a generalized Maxwell 
model.  The simulation results obtained from the finite 
element analysis are compared with the experimental data to 
estimate the values of material parameters of each component 
layers. 
 
Results and Discussion 
Figure 1 showed the staining of a sciatic nerve of rat and 
three component layers could be differentiated.  Four 
fascicles were marked in the nerve.  The axons surrounded 
by the Schwann cells and other connective tissues comprised 
the endoneurium which was encircled by the perineurium.  
The epineurium was the outermost layer of connective tissue 
surrounding the sciatic nerve. 
Figure 2 showed a single fascicle and the axons could be 
formulated.  The density of the axons of a single fascicle 
could be calculated to estimate the number of the axons for the 
four fascicles in Figure 1.  A finite element model was built 
by integrating the information of axons to the fascicles in the 
three component layers. 
The material parameters derived by the inverse finite 
element method could be used to describe the viscoelastic 
response of the whole sciatic nerve to the mechanical load 
from the nerve conduit.  By this combination of numerical 
simulation and in situ experiment, better design for nerve 
conduit could be achieved.

Conclusions 
Three component layers and axons were differentiated and 
reconstructed by a custom-made image-processing program. 
From the processed image a finite element model of the sciatic

could be estimated.
