氮化鉻緩衝層對光電元件特性 
之實驗與電腦模擬研究 
 
 
計畫編號：NSC 98-2221-E-426-001- 
執行期限： 98 年 8 月 1 日至 99 年 7 月 31 日 
主持人：林建德 立德大學資訊傳播學系

一、中文摘要 
本研究計劃的主要目的是以 DC與 RF濺鍍機在
矽(Si)、藍寶石(Sapphire)、及石英玻璃(Quartzes)上，
成長氮化鉻(Chromium Nitride, CrN)薄膜以作為緩衝
層，再利用分子束磊晶系統(Molecular Beam Epitaxy 
System, MBE)於該緩衝層上成長氮化鎵(GaN)薄膜，並
且利用成長後的薄膜來製作各種不同的光電元件，如
光感測器、發光二極體、或是太陽能電池等。氮化鉻
的優點在於其結構為岩鹽結構(Rocksalt)，故可以有效
的遏止來自基板的錯位(Dislocation)，減少漏電流的產
生。本研究計畫利用濺鍍方式得到氮化鉻薄膜，其為
隨機成長方向。然而，本研究計畫利用分子束磊晶的
方式可以控制成長時的參數，進而得到不同的薄膜成
長方向，更可以控制所成長的 GaN 為 m-plane 或是
c-plane；而 m-plane 的 GaN 更為非極性面，對於光電
元件特性有很好的改進。分子束磊晶法是利用在超高
真空的環境，精確地控制由噴射槽(Effusion Cell)加溫
後噴出的分子束，使其沉積在基板上。此方法有許多
的優點，其一為可在低溫下成長，對於某些基板而言，
過高的沉積溫度，會對基板產生破壞，或是使其表面
能料過高，容易吸附太多雜質；其二，由於分子束磊
晶沉積速率較慢，可以準確的控制磊晶層的厚度；其
三，由於是在超高真空的環境(約為 10-10torr)不但可以
減少吸附在基板上的雜質，更可得到與預想中較為接
近的磊晶層結構，且可在磊晶的同時，同時量測其材
料結構以及特性，利用此方法，可以得到品質及為優
良 III-V材料以及元件 
 
關鍵詞：分子束磊晶、光偵測器、氮化鎵、磊晶、氮
化鉻。 
 
Abstract 
This study grew GaN epilayers on a (111)-oriented 
silicon substrate by molecular beam epitaxy with a thin 
CrN interlayer fabricated through a nitridation process. 
High-resolution X-ray diffraction results showed (200) 
and (0002) oriented peaks in the CrN interlayer and GaN 
film, respectively. Hall measurements identified carrier 
concentrations in the GaN film, with and without a CrN 
interlayer, as 2.1×1020 cm-3 and 1.23×1022 cm-3, 
respectively. The results of auger electron spectroscopy 
showed that the concentration of electrons was relatively 
high in the samples grown without a CrN interlayer, 
which could be due to the diffusion of Si atoms into the 
GaN epilayer. CrN prevented Si atoms from diffusing into 
the GaN epilayer, thereby reducing the concentration of 
electrons. With a lower concentrations of electrons and 
surface defects, the photoluminescence spectra of GaN 
without CrN exhibits two peaks, located at 363 nm and 
380 nm corresponding to band-edge emission and 
donor-acceptor pair recombination (DAP) emission, 
respectively. The DAP emission was not generated in the 
GaN with a CrN interlayer, due to improved crystalline 
quality, and a reduction in the concentration of electrons. 
 
 
Keywords: Molecular Beam Epitaxy, Photodetector, InN, 
Epitaxial layer, CrN. 
 
二、計畫緣由及目的 
Gallium nitride (GaN)-based compound 
semiconductors have been widely utilized in 
optoelectronic devices, due to their wide energy band-gap 
(~3.4 eV), and excellent chemical and thermal stability.1 
Recent technical advances have enabled the production of 
increasingly thin liquid crystal displays through the use of 
high-brightness GaN-based light emitting diodes (LEDs) 
as backlights. A sapphire substrate is usually used for the 
growth of GaN-based LEDs; however, silicon substrates 
have a number of attractive advantages compared to 
sapphire. These advantages include good thermal and 
electrical conductivity, low cost, and well-developed 
fabrication processes. Optoelectronic devices including 
LEDs, 2, 3 photo detectors4 and field-effect transistors5 
based on III-nitride have been grown on Si substrates.

Nevertheless, it was possible to conduct longitudinal

Figure 4 (a) and (b) show the AES results of the 
GaN grown on Si substrate without and with a CrN

role in obstructing the diffusion of Si atoms from the

四、結論與成果 
In summary, this study grew a GaN epilayer on an 
Si (111) substrate with a CrN interlayer formed by the 
nitridation of sputter-coated Cr metal film. The GaN 
epilayer exhibited a crystal orientation of [0002], and 
XRC results demonstrated that the growth of a CrN 
interlayer between the Si substrate and the GaN epilayer 
reduces the density of dislocations and increases 
crystalline quality. The TEM results also confirmed that 
CrN reduced the number of dislocations in the same 
pattern as the sapphire substrate. In the sample with the 
CrN interlayer, the concentration of electrons was reduced 
100-fold compared with the GaN grown without CrN. In 
addition, the FWHM was also improved in the GaN 
grown with CrN interlayers. Photoluminescent spectra 
indicated donor-to-acceptor transition in the GaN films 
grown without a CrN interlayer, which may have been the 
result of a diffusion of Si atoms from the Si substrate. The 
unintended doping of Si led to vacancies of Ga as an 
acceptor material. The PL intensity and FWHM of the 
band edge emission were improved in the GaN film 
grown with a CrN interlayer. This effect could be 
attributed to the prevention of Si diffusion by the CrN 
interlayer, which in turn reduced the Ga vacancy and 
improved the crystallinity of the grown GaN epilayer. The 
results of this study show that a CrN interlayer plays an 
important role in the growth of GaN epilayers.
