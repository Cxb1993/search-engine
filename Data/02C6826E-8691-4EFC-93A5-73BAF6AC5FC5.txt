model that can be widely applied among institutes to
follow up these patients.
Keywords: Alzheimer's disease、senile dementia、
electroencephalography、EEG、spectral analysis
二、緣由與目的
阿茲海默病又稱為老人失智症，是一種漸進
性的腦部神經退化疾病，主要表現為認知及記憶
退化、日常生活功能障礙、及神經心理和行為異
常，年齡越大，發生機會越高。隨著社會人口結
構老化，患者將會越來越多，對家庭及社會都是
很大的負擔。在過去，阿茲海默病的藥物治療效
果並不顯著。但近十年來，數種上市藥物已被證
實能夠有效控制病患症狀，更有新藥物被研發以
期能夠阻止病理變化。
目前有多種國際通用的量表在臨床上被廣泛
應用，以評估阿茲海默病的嚴重程度。評估量表
雖然經過實驗證明具備可接受的信度與效度，但
因牽涉觀察者之判斷，主觀因素無法完全避免。
因此，我們希望能夠利用較為客觀的評估工具來
追蹤病患的病程，與目前臨床使用中的評估量表
相輔相成，提供更有價值的資訊以為臨床決策之
用。
腦波是一種簡單、安全、敏感、客觀、普及
及成本低廉的檢查工具，它被運用在阿茲海默病
的研究已有超過 20 年的歷史。典型阿茲海默病
的腦波變化為慢波增加、快波減少，但這些現象
為非特異性變化[1]。過去文獻研究發現：腦波有
助於區別不同原因造成的痴呆症、早期診斷疾
病、預測疾病預後、評估疾病嚴重程度、追蹤病
程與評估藥物治療效果。然而，臨床上腦波在阿
茲海默病的運應用卻相當侷限。筆者訪問各個醫
學中心，臨床醫師多不清楚腦波於評估阿茲海默
病可帶來的貢獻，文獻搜尋也未發現台灣本地的
研究報告或是使用經驗。
理想的腦波指標需要符合下列條件：區分疾
病嚴重程度的敏感度和鑑別度高、適用於不同機
構的腦波機，避免個體間生理差異的影響、計算
容易、及具有神經生理意義。本研究假設能夠尋
得理想的腦波指標，不僅可以呈現疾病嚴重程
度，也足以協助區別病患的臨床分級。期望以此
指標，建立以腦波為基礎的阿茲海默病評估模
式，提供醫師客觀的臨床輔助工具，幫助醫療團
隊有效掌握病患狀況，未來能應用於藥物治療效
果的評估，並預測病患預後。
三、材料與方法
 研究對象及收案流程
本研究的研究對象為符合 NINCDS-ADRDA
準則診斷中可能（probable）阿茲海默病[2]及
Petersen 診斷準則[3]的輕度知能障礙（MCI）的
個案。採用回溯研究法，利用三家機構(台北市立
聯合醫院仁愛院區、臺北市立聯合醫院松德院區
及博仁綜合醫院)腦波室各別的腦波檢查登錄系
統，篩選以阿茲海默病、痴呆症、失智症、及記
憶障礙，或 ICD-9-CM 診斷編碼為 290（Senile /
Presenile psychosis）、 290.0 （Senile dementia）、
290.1（Presenile dementia）、及 331.0 (Alzheimer's
disease) 為適應症，接受腦波檢查個案。根據完整
病歷資料，由神經內科專科醫師重新檢視病歷紀
錄 及 各 項 檢 查 報 告 ， 選 擇 其 中 符 合
NINCDS-ADRDA 診斷準則的可能阿茲海默及
Peterson 診斷準則的輕度知能障礙個案。針對年
齡、性別、腦波檢查日期、腦波實行前後兩週內
之 MMSE[4]、CDR[5]、 GDS[6]、及用藥加以紀
錄。若病例具有下列排除條件，雖診斷符合可能
阿茲海默病例，仍不納入實驗之中，以避免疾病
之外因素造成可能的腦波影響。排除條件：使用
膽鹼酯解脢抑制劑、NMDA 受體拮抗劑、抗憂鬱
藥物、抗精神病藥物、抗乙醯膽鹼藥物、持續使
用鎮靜安眠藥、腦波檢查期間處於瞻妄狀態、或
處於嚴重全身性代謝性疾病。符合條件之病例依
其智能狀態（CDR 或 GDS）區分為四組：非常
輕微組（very mild，GDS＝3，CDR＝0.5)、輕度
組（mild，GDS＝4，CDR＝1)，中度組（moderate，
GDS＝5，CDR＝2），及重度組（severe，GDS＝6，
CDR＝3）。
 腦波紀錄與分析
受測者於機構內之腦波檢查室以 Stellate
Harmonie 腦波機檢查，檢查時間為上午 9 時至
下午 4 時。受測者於清醒安靜狀態下，坐於有高
靠背之單人沙發。腦波紀錄採頭皮電極，依照國
際標準 10-20 系統排列，包括 FP1、FP2、F3、
F4 、F7、F8、Fz、T3、T4、T5 、T6、C3、C4、
Cz、P3 、P4、Pz、O1、O2 十九個活動電極，及
兩耳上之參考電極。表面肌電圖及心電圖同時紀
錄，但不納入訊號分析。保持頭皮電極之
impedance 小於 5 kohm 以下以達良好訊號紀
錄。腦波取樣頻率為 200 Hz（松德院區為 500
Hz），band pass 為 1-70 Hz，交流電干擾過濾
notch filter 60 Hz。腦波紀錄過程中，受測者遵照
腦波技術員指示，執行張眼或閉眼動作，或加入
過度換氣及光刺激誘發試驗，由技術員在腦波紀
錄上加上註記，平均紀錄時間約在 10-15 分鐘。
在未知個案實驗分組前提下，由有腦波判讀
經驗的醫師以傳統目視方式重新檢視數位腦波資
料。統一選擇以雙耳為參考電極的 Montage，包
含 FP1、FP2、F3、F4、F7、F8、Fz、T3、T4、
重度兩等級區分法，而是採用更為精確的四個等
級區分法，目的是為了研究腦波能否反映更為細
微的腦部變化。
本研究所設計的 power ratio 具備以下特性：
單一指標、同時考慮快波及慢波的變化、避免不
同儀器或不同病患間 absolute power 的差異。我
們發現此一比值確實和疾病嚴重度有良好相關
性：疾病程度越嚴重、比值越高。此發現不僅符
合研究假設，也能和傳統腦波判讀的經驗相互應
証，顯示 power ratio 適合用來作為腦波判讀的指
標之一。
此外，本研究 interhemispheric alpha coherence
中 P3-P4 和疾病嚴重相關性尤其明顯，我們認為
可能和阿茲海默病最重要的病症－認知障礙關係
密切。而在解剖構造上，P3-P4，也就是兩側大腦
頂葉的連接，主要依賴胼胝體尾部。多項影像學
證據顯示，阿茲海默病的胼胝體病變以尾部萎縮
為其特徵[9,10]。解剖學、影像學、和神經電生理
學的證據可說是不謀而合。

五、結論
腦波是一個客觀、安全、普及性高、及成本
低廉的檢查工具，可以用來評估阿茲海默病疾病
嚴重程度，本研究發現 power ratio 和 P3-P4
alpha coherence 是理想的腦波指標，可以反映
病患的病程變化。我們提議臨床及研究機構將此
兩項腦波指標納入對阿茲海默病患的評估模式，
輔助現行精神量表的不足。並以此模式進行後續
研究，追蹤病患的治療反應。

A Semi-Automatic QT Interval
Measurement Based on Digital
Filters
CY Wu, HW Chiu
Taipei Medical University, Taipei, Taiwan
Abstract
The QT interval of ECG signal, a representative
duration of cardiac cell activation, has been evidenced to
correlate with cardiac disease or drug. Therefore the
accurate QT measurement is important for clinical
diagnosis and prognosis. In this paper we developed and
presented a semi-automatic QT interval measurement
method to participate the 2006 computers in cardiology
challenge. This method locates the major markers of ECG
signals (the P, Q, R, S T waves) based on digital filtering
and predefined conditions in waveform. First a proper
lead was select to measure the QT interval by manually
inspection. The proper lead was defined as in which there
are obvious upward R and T waves after 0.4-50 Hz band-
pass filtering to diminish the noise and baseline
wandering. Then R waves were found as local maxima
after applying thresholding techniques. After the
approximated first deviation of the filtered ECG (dECG)
was obtained by difference filter, the Q onsets were
located as the second cross-zero point of dECG 2-120 ms
upstream R waves and the ends of T waves were located
as the intercept of the filtered ECG signal and the line
passing through T peak and maximum slope point on T
wave. A visualization interface to display the
characteristic markers on the ECG waveform was used to
inspect the primary results and thus a representative beat
could be manually selected as a final result. This method
involves tow-steps manually intervention to raise the
accuracy of QT measurement. However, these tow-steps
are not time-consumed and easy for users with minimum
knowledge of interpreting ECG. Finally an automatic
method was developed by using the mean of 12 leads as
processed signal.
1. Introduction
The QT interval (from the Q wave onset to the T wave
end) represents the duration of ventricular depolarization
and subsequent repolarization. A delay in cardiac
repolarization has clinical relevance to cardiac
arrhythmias.[1,2]
Automated methods, in comparison with manual
methods, offer advantages in terms of absolute
repeatability of measurements, immunity from errors
related to observer fatigue, lapses of attention, and
transcription, as well as efficiency and cost considerations
that permit either more extensive and rigorous testing for
the same cost as manual methods, or more rapid testing at
lower cost.[3,4]
There are four automated methods that have been
around by McLaughlin et al.[5]: the Technique TH
(threshold), DTH (differential threshold) Technique SI
(slope intercept), Technique PSI (peak slope intercept).
The mean of the QT interval determined by the four
methods described above may vary of up to 62 ms, in
particular, the results of Technique TH are highly
undesirable.
In addition, filtering has proven to be more difficult
than we ever anticipated, because the program has to be
robust enough regardless of the quality of the recordings.
With more processing power in today’s computers, an
order higher than two can be done with acceptable
efficiency.[6]
The aim of the study is participate the Computers in
Cardiology Challenge 2006 and do a delicate task of
performing automated QT interval measurements with a
high reproducibility, based on the 549 recordings of the
PTB Diagnostic ECG Database.
2. Methods
The PTB Diagnostic ECG Database, which includes
549 records from 294 subjects (each subject is
represented by one to five records). Each record includes
15 simultaneously measured signals: the conventional 12
leads (I, II, III, avr, avl, avf, v1, v2, v3, v4, v5, v6)
together with the 3 Frank lead ECGs (vx, vy, vz). Each
signal is digitized at 1000 samples per second, with 16 bit
resolution over a range of ± 16.384 mV.
2.1. Filtering and R-peak finding
A forth order Butterworth IIR digital filter with a
bandwidth of 0.4 Hz to 50 Hz was used to eliminate high
frequency noise and baseline wandering. A lead was
selected for further processing by manually inspection.

method is semi-automatic needing some manual
processes.

2.3. Automatic method involving 12 leads
The proposed semi-automatic method had a manual
procedure for the selection of a proper lead to process.
Hence we developed another automatic method using 12
leads data instead of manual selection of leads. The first
process involved screens the twelve leads for inverted
leads, and inverting them to let them with positive R
waves. The second process was to calculate the mean and
median of 12 lead ECGs including uninverted leads and
the inverted leads which had been processed upon. The
final signal was put through the procedures of finding R
peak, Q onset, and T end by methods described
previously.
3. Results
The criteria for manually lead selection was straight
forward, lead II was always top on our priority, only
when the peaks had inadequate amplitude, or the number
of peaks abnormal. When lead II was unusable, other
leads we selected for their recognizable PQRS complex,
independent P and Q waves (P and Q waves not merged),
and sufficient amplitude of the T wave.
Finally, of all the 482 sets of data we performed, lead
II accounted for 320 sets, lead I was the second most
favorable, accounting for 80 sets. We got a final score
46.96 ms based on the scoring rule of this challenge.

Generally zero-slope method produced a longer QT
interval, on the other hand, MS-ISO method produced a
shorter result.
