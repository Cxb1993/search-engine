3. 文獻討論
依據調閱國內、外相關於護膝等護具的學術研究文獻極為有限，但可以發現業界愈
來愈重視人體生物力學的研究，護具支架的設計不斷地更新改良，國外利用跑步器和量
度腳板踏地反應的儀器，以及利用高速攝影機在三度空間拍攝運動員在運動時的姿勢，
來設計更好更合適的護具和支架[37]，或是使用豬的膝關結骨做為人體膝關節的替代研
究[36]。目前國內除了相關的學術性研究之外，業界對於護膝的性能上尚未制定一套檢
驗標準。國內對於護膝的使用雖然已較被接受且相當普遍，但在沒有健全的性能檢驗評
估標準的情況下，無論對於防撞擊或是防扭傷拉傷用之護具來說，對於使用者的膝關節
都是相當不利的。因此本研究對於建立護膝使用的安全性評估與規範有相當的重要性。
過去關於遠紅外線功能性之研究，多偏重於物理醫學與臨床醫學之領域，其中以日
本及中國大陸最為熱烈。自 20 世紀 80 年代中期，日本率先進行遠紅外纖維及紡織品的
研究開發，經過 10 年的研製，已向市場推出了一批遠紅外織物。中國在 90 年代初中期，
江蘇、上海、天津、北京等地科研院校相繼開發這類纖維，台灣則較少[38]。日本對各
種用途或樣式的遠紅外線產品經由各種管道，而廣為一般家庭所接受，其中多數應用中
醫學的觀點，照射頭頂的百會穴和腳底湧泉穴，對老年人睡眠障礙如失眠或焦慮均有幫
助。中國大陸關於遠紅外線基礎研究很多，臨床應用也很廣泛，從失眠、類風濕性關節
炎、骨關節炎、運動傷害的輔助治療、術後傷口癒合和燒燙傷治療，都有相關研究報告。
目前遠紅外線發射體已進入多源（multi-field）的研發，在材料科學的進展下，已能
把遠紅外線輻射集中在 7.5 至9.5 μm波長間，而沒有其它短波如χ射線、γ射線等對人
體有害的射線，對人體發揮「細胞共振」的作用外，也維護使用的安全性。遠紅外線除
了「熱效應」外，還有一種「生物效應」（non-thermal effect）正受到學術研究者注目，
雖然目前醫界對生物效應所知不多，但物理醫學界認為，這將是一道新的曙光。

4.3.2. 針軋原理
將混棉、開棉、清棉、梳棉後得到的蓬鬆纖網，經成千上萬枚針刺入纖網，刺針上
的鉤刺就帶住纖網表面的一些纖維隨刺針穿過纖網而產生位移，同時由於摩擦作用而使
纖網受到壓縮，當刺入一定深度後，刺針回升，此時由於鉤刺順向的緣故，這些纖維脫
離鉤刺而以近乎垂直狀態留在纖網內，猶如許多的纖維束釘入纖網，使纖維與纖維間互
相緊緊地抱合在一起並使纖網的密度大為提升，如圖 4.6 所示。
4.3.3. 熱固結原理
在熱黏合生產過程中，當熱熔纖維加熱到一定溫度和具有一定的壓力時，整個大分
子鏈相互滑移，即熱熔纖維軟化、熔融，發生黏性流動，再經由冷卻作用使得纖維粘合。
在主體纖維中加入適量熱熔纖維使之與主體纖維接觸，則在纖維交叉點處形成黏合點，
如圖 4.7 所示。

5.2. 實驗流程說明
本研究分別利用 PLA 與聚酯紅外線機能纖維製造非織物，並針軋固結複合兩種不
同型態非織物。最後利用熱黏合法將高強力尼龍非織物與蜂巢氣墊複合，製做三明治複
合材料。並在複合之後檢測其避震效果與軟硬度，以尋找穿戴時同時符合緩衝性能與舒
適的最佳條件。
1. 高強力 PLA 非織物：以非織物成型設備製作混有低熔棉之預軋 PLA 棉網。
2. 疊層：以蜂巢氣墊置入兩層 PLA 針軋棉網之間。
3. 熱壓固結：將疊層過後棉網在利用熱壓固結後形成一複合非織物。
4. 落重式衝擊測試：將醫護用功能性護膝緩衝材料，經過落重式衝擊測試所得之
衝擊能量、最大荷重及凹陷深度來獲取複合非織物之最佳製程條件。
5. 遠紅外線測試：委託國立海洋大學進行遠紅外線性能測試。

膝緩衝試片裁成 10 cm × 10 cm，衝頭落下高度為 8 cm，重鎚之重量為 6.5 kg，同一種

之抗衝擊性能。

6.1.8. 不同疊層方式之 TPU蜂巢式氣墊對於衝擊吸收之影響
圖 6.7 為使用 2 mm、4 mm、6 mm、8 mm 之蜂巢氣墊變化其疊層方式為 2 mm + 8

的原因是由於較薄的蜂巢氣墊因為其垂直結構具較高的剛性，所以在衝頭接觸 TPU 表
面的瞬間，能將力分散到整個平面上，再由下方較厚的 TPU 提供吸收衝擊的效果。

8. 計劃結果自評
本計畫以PLA不織布所製備之基布複合TPU蜂巢結構之氣墊研製護膝
之緩衝材料，以基布本身的物性評估探討以求得最佳製程參數設計與適用
於本計畫之不織布基布，並以探討 TPU 衝擊吸收之性能以取得良好的衝擊
吸收疊層方式。TPU 以其優良的物理性質配合蜂巢結構所提供的緩衝性
能，為護膝的設計提供一個全新的選擇方向，其設計出之護膝不但可以擁
有良好的緩衝性能，更可以增加透氣性，以減少穿戴者的不適，進而增加
使用意願降低運動傷害的產生，將護膝的使用提升至令一種發展層次。

PET/PP Blend with Bamboo Charcoal to Produce Functional Composites 
 
Ching-Wen Loua*, Ching-Wen Linc, Chen-Hwan Leib, Kuan-Hua Sub, Chan-Hung Hsub, Zheng-Horng Liub, Jia-Horng Linb 
aGeneral Education Center/ Institute of Biomedical Engineering and Material Science, Central Taiwan University of Science and 
Technology, Taiwan, R.O.C. 
bLaboratory of Fiber Application and Manufacturing, Institute of Textile Engineering and Department of Fiber and Composite

Abstract 
 
Polymer products belong to petrochemical compounds and uneasily degrade in the natural environment. Besides, the process of 
extraction and purification of oil consume resources, energy and result in environmental pollution. For recycling of plastic wastes and 
environmental preservation, this study focused on blending and reprocessing of polyester (PET) and polypropylene (PP) due to their large 
amount of disposal. In this study, thermoplastic composites were made from blending virgin PET and PP pellets with the bamboo 
charcoal by melt compounding and then injection molding. The mechanical properties and functions of these composites were 
investigated. The compositions of blended ratio were also studied in this work. The results show that the mechanical properties of the 
PET/PPext (extrusion grade polypropylene) composites were better than that of the PET/PPinj (injection grade polypropylene) composites
with 10 % and 20 % of polyester composition. After three extrusion processes, the tensile strength, impact strength, and flexural modulus
of the PET/ PPext composites were still respectively maintained about 32 MPa, 35 J/m, and 2000 MPa. While the content of polyester is 30 
%, the serious phase separation between PET and PP resulted in poor mechanical properties of the PET/PPext composites. While the 
content of polyester was 20 %, the mechanical properties of the PET/PPinj composite weren’t significantly influenced by the addition of 
bamboo charcoal.  
 
Keywords: bamboo charcoal; melt compounding; Injection molding; far infrared ray 
 
 
 
1. Introduction 
 
Polymer products such as synthetic fibers, plastics and 
rubbers belong to petrochemical compounds and uneasily 
degrade in the natural environment. Besides, the process of 
extraction and purification of oil consume resources, energy 
and result in environmental pollution. Some statistical data in 
this area has shown that the wastes from the polymer industry 
are 25 % of the raw materials by volume [1]. Therefore, 
recycling and reusing polymeric waste is one of the most 
important environmental tasks that face the world, to reduce 
environmental loading and promote the most effective use of 
resources. 
Recycling consists of several stages, the first of which is 
separation. This stage is often the major factor for the high cost 
of the process. This cost could often be avoided if recycling of 
plastics in mixtures were technically and economically viable. 
Actually, to recycle the commingled plastic waste in the form 
of blends is very attractive because it also avoids the difficult 
task of separation.  
Bamboo charcoal is come from bamboo which 
carbonized under high temperature. There are innumerable 
holes crosswise in its structure. It is said that the inner surface 
of bamboo charcoal reach 250 to 300 m2/g. In such large space, 
microbe can easy absorb on it, and the volatile bamboo 
charcoal contained rich nutriment for microbe, so bamboo 
charcoal possessed great absorption effect and the microbe it 
absorbed can decompose the absorbed ingredients clearly. If 
processing bamboo charcoal under very high temperature 
(beyond 1200 °C), it will emit far infrared ray, release minus 
ion, absorb electromagnetic wave. Bamboo charcoal also 
possesses rich trace elements and natural mineral, such as 
calcium, potassium, sodium and iron etc. 
Bamboo charcoal has countless small holes lengthwise 
and crosswise. If compared with wood charcoal, the number of 
holes is about five times, the mineral constituent eight times 
and absorption efficiency ten times than wood charcoal. If 
compared the inner surface of it, wood charcoal is about 50 
m2/g, bamboo charcoal is about 250 to 300 m2/g, so bamboo 
charcoal has better absorption effect. 
In this study, virgin polyester (PET) and polypropylene 
(PP) pellets were first mix in proportion, and then melt 
compounding with the bamboo charcoal powder by a single 
screw extruder.

3.2 The influence of PET blend ratio and the processing cycles 
on the flexural modulus of the PET/PP composites for 
injection and extrusion grade PP matrix 
Flexural modulus is usually used to evaluate the rigidity 
of polymeric materials. Figs. 3 shows the influences of PET 
blend ratio and the processing cycles on the flexural modulus 
of the PET/PP composites, which were made from different 
grade PP and the ratio of PET were 10 %, 20 % and 30 %. As 
can be observed, the flexural modulus of the PET/PP 
composites almost keeps constant with the number of 
processing cycles. Besides, with an increase in PET ratio, the 
flexural modulus is increased for the PET/PPinj composites. 
However, it doesn’t result in any obvious variation for the 
PET/PPext composites.

3.3 The influence of PET blend ratio and the processing cycles 
on the impact strength of the PET/PP composites for injection 
and extrusion grade PP matrix 
The influences of reprocessing cycles and the ratio of 
PET on the Izod impact strength of the PET/PPinj and 
PET/PPext composites are shown in Fig. 4. As can be seen, the 
impact strength of the PET/PP composites is decreased with 
the increasing processing cycles for both the PPext and PPinj 
matrix. A maximum decrement in the Izod impact strength can 
be found in the PET/PPext composites with 20 % PET ratio. 
Processing induced degradation could be accounted for the 
decrement in impact strength of the composites. 
The impact strength of the PET/PPinj composites doesn’t 
change with the increasing PET ratio, but it is obviously 
decreased for the PET/PPext composites with 30 % PET ratio. 
It should be resulted from lacking of well interfacial adhesion 
which leads to stress concentration at the interface between the 
PET filler and PP matrix, and further reduces the impact 
resistance of the PET/PPext composites with higher PET 
content.

The influences of bamboo charcoal content and the 
processing cycles on the flexural modulus of the PET/PPinj 
composites are shown in Fig 7. A slightly increment in the 
flexural modulus of the PET/PPinj composites can be found as 
a result of adding the bamboo charcoal. It might be attributed 
to higher crystallinity induced by the bamboo charcoal particle 
in the PP matrix. The existence of the inorganic micro particle 
can promote the PP matrix crystallized at higher temperature 
and increased its crystallinity, as well as further increased the 
flexural modulus of the composites. Besides, further increase 
in bamboo charcoal content doesn’t result in an increase in the 
flexural modulus of the PET/PPinj composites. 
Fig. 8 shows the influences of bamboo charcoal content 
and the processing cycles on the impact strength of the 
PET/PPinj composites. The incorporation of the bamboo 
charcoal slightly decreased the impact strength of the 1st 
processed PET/PPinj composites. However, further increase in 
bamboo charcoal content and in processing cycles doesn’t 
result in a decrease in the impact strength of the composites. 
 
3.5 SEM observation of the fracture surface of the PET/PP 
composites 
The SEM photographs of the fracture surface of the 1st 
injected PET/PPinj and PET/PPext composites with 10 % PET 
ratio are shown in Figs. 9 ~ 14. As shown in Fig. 9, several 
PET particles and voids with approximately 10 µm can be 
found in the fracture surface of the PET/PPinj composites. By 
contrast, smaller PET particles and voids size are found in the 
fracture surface of the PET/PPext composites. Significant 
difference in fluidity between PET and PP would lead to 
severe phase separation in the composites.
