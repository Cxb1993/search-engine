2.常用之噴滴設備 
一般文獻中常使用的液滴產生方式大
致有兩種，第一種即所謂之 VODG 
(Vibrating Orifice Droplet Generator)的方
式，另一種則為脈衝作動式噴嘴。VODG
式之液滴產生器原理乃是先加壓於工作流
體使其經由一孔口(orifice)射出形成液柱，
再利用壓電陶瓷於孔口處提供穩定的振動
以使液柱表面產生波動，由於液體本身表
面張力所引致之不穩定增強了液柱表面波
動程度，進而促使此液柱斷裂生成均勻大
小之液滴串。為了保持生成液滴之穩定
性，其液滴的生成頻率通常維持在 1 kHz
以上。脈衝作動式噴嘴主要有熱氣泡型噴
嘴與壓電型噴嘴兩類；前者利用局部加熱
工作流體產生蒸汽泡將部份流體自噴嘴噴
出；此對於加熱後有可能產生變質的流體
樣品(如蛋白質)，或是沸點較高(如融熔錫)
的流體不適用，且最高噴出率較壓電型噴
嘴低，故在實驗中使用後者，利用其簡單、
易操作與可用的工作流體也廣泛的特性。

(4) 改善蒸發的問題 
經過實驗後含有螢光劑之液滴產生於
玻蓋玻片上，其大小介於 165μm 之間，並
且會逐漸蒸發縮小，而液滴直徑的縮小與
時間並非成線性關係，從實驗測試結果於
圖十七中可以得知隨著時間的增加，液滴
直徑縮小愈多；也就是每秒縮小速率增
加，因此對於液滴的觀察即增加了困難
度。從液滴動態變化圖，亦發現液滴的輪
廓並不成正圓形，原因可能是玻璃表面不
完全乾淨，尚有顆粒或雜質在其上，使液
滴在縮小時，因液體與玻璃表面的黏附作
用力不均勻，形成多邊形的輪廓，可以藉
著製程標準步驟清洗玻璃表面改善此問
題。

A Wireless Bio-MEMS sensor for C-Reactive Protein Detection Based on Nanomechanics 
L.-S. Huang, M.C Kuo1, C.-M. Hung1, T.H Chen2, R.-Z. Hwang1, S. Lin3, Y. H. Wang4 and 
S.-S.  Lu1 
1Institute of Applied Mechanics, National Taiwan University, Taiwan 
2GRADUATE INSTITUTE OF ELECTRONICS ENGINEERING AND DEPARTMENT OF

Bio-sensing tools have been moving towards miniaturization, high sensitivity, portability and 
wireless networking. While fluorescent labeling of nucleic acids is becoming a standard procedure, 
protein labeling techniques are not yet as well established. Moreover, the complex structure of 
proteins renders themselves much more sensitive to chemical modifications. Label-free methods 
offer specific advantage that unmodified samples can be used in real-time and in-situ monitoring. 
Label-free detection of one of the most clinically used protein biomarker—C reactive protein 
(CRP)—using microcantilevers has not yet been demonstrated. In addition, wireless networking and 
safe reusability of bio-sensors are also required to enhance patients’ mobility without the sacrifice of 
health monitoring. CRP is an acute-phase reactant produced in the liver normally present in healthy 
human serum with a concentration < 1μg/mL. CRP levels may rise up to 100 or even 500 times 
during acute infection or inflammation anywhere in the body [4]. Recent studies have also shown 
that high levels of CRP in the bloodstream raise the risk of a heart attack [4] [5]. In this paper, we 
report the first demonstration of wireless real-time label-free detection of C-reactive proteins based 
on nanomechanics at clinically relevant concentrations (1-500 μg/mL). Furthermore, it is found that 
a low frequency ac electric field can be applied to the sensor to dissociate the CRP from the 
microcantilever for reusable purpose in contrast to the traditional methods of using strong acids [6]. 
The sensing principle of the CRP using a microcantilever is illustrated in Fig.1. The V-shape MEMS 
silicon nitride microcantilever with a length of 200 um and each leg 20 um wide was fabricated 
in-house by micromachining technology on a silicon substrate. On the top side of the 
microcantilever, four layers consisting of chromium, gold, biolinker, and monoclonal anti-CRP were 
deposited sequentially for the detection of CRP.  
In summary a fast real-time wireless label free detection of CRP at clinically relevant concentrations 
using a CMOS compatible bio-MEMS sensor is demonstrated for the first time. In addition, the 
bio-MEMS CRP sensor has proven to be reused by dissociating the CRP from the microcantilever
