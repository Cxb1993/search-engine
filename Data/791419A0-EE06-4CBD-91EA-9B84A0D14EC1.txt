γ-FeOOH均是由鐵氧八面體所組成，α-FeOOH結構是
由鐵氧八面體以鏈狀的方式排列，中間以氫鍵連結，
γ-FeOOH結構是由鐵氧八面體已層狀方式排列，層間

赤鐵礦(α-Fe2O3)，其結構為六方晶系(HCP)，在自

鐵原料，一般自然界中較古老的赤鐵礦沉積岩，因粒

小所以呈現紅色，因此常被拿來作紅色顏料使用。其
除了作為顏料之外，也可以當作固態反應法製作鐵氧
磁體的原料。

水解過程(hydrolytic process)既是指鐵與水離子作

Fe3+ ＋ 2H2O → Fe(OH)2+ ＋ 2H+
Fe3+ ＋ 2H2O → FeOOH ＋ 3H+
Fe3+ ＋ 3H2O → Fe2O3 ＋ 6H+

型態卻會與轉換前的型態相似，例如：纖鐵礦

將離心完成後的紅褐色膏狀物加入去離子水中，

Abstract 
In this work, a clear sol was prepared from a mixed aqueous solution of NH3 and 
Al(NO3)3·9H2O, and peptized by nitric acid. Alumina films were fabricated by spin-coating the 
clear sol on Si and glass substrates and annealing at 50 to 800°C. A strong blue photoluminescence 
emission at 450 nm was observed to vary with the pH of the peptizing solution, annealing 
temperature, and atmosphere. An ultra-strong, blue-emitting alumina film was obtained at a low 
temperature of 50°C. It is believed that the observed strong photoluminescence is associated with 
inherent defect centers in the material, which are related to phase transformation and chemical bond 
cleavage. Therefore, the phase evolution, microstructure, and photoluminescence properties of 
alumina films, fabricated at low temperatures, will be discussed in this paper. 
This experiment uses a low temperature, thermally assisted sol-gel method to fabricate 
alumina nano-films. The effects of oxygen heat theories and the pH of the processing solution on 
the blue light fluorescent properties are investigated. The intent of this investigation is to extend the 
knowledge of intense, blue light emitting alumina films, and their potential contributions to the 
current LED industry. 
 
2. Experimental  
Al(NO3)3 9H2O was dissolved in deionized water and then mixed with strong ammonia water 
to adjust the alkalinity of the resultant solution to a pH of 8 to 10, resulting in a white porodine 
solution. The porodine solution was stirred, and the resultant sedimentation was removed and 
washed with deionized water. This stirring and washing step was repeated several times to eliminate 
all remaining ammonia and nitrate base. The sedimented product was then diluted to a density 
where the resultant solution was still a white porodine form. A micro-nitric acid solution was then 
added to adjust the solution alkalinity pH to a value between 2 and 10 in order to peptize. As nitric 
acid is added, the solution gradually changes from a white opaque color to transparent appearance. 
The nitric acid causes the originally combined colloid granules to separate due to the repulsion of 
electronic charges, reducing the granule size. The solution’s bottom half is composed of white 
sediment, while the upper half is transparent. The tansparent solution was used to coat alumina thin 
film onto a substrate and dried at 50 oC, resulting in an alumina film. The as-fabricated film was 
subjected to different annealing temperatures and atmospheres to investigate any structural changes 
From Fig. 1, it can be observed that when films were annealed at 150 oC for 1 hour, there was 
no evident change in the composition of the alumina film, and the film composition remained a 
combination of pseudo-boehmite and bayerite. When the alumina films were annealed at 200°C for 
1 hour, the peaks corresponding to bayerite in the XRD spectra disappeared, and only peaks 
corresponding to pseudo-boehmite remained. Further, this data also suggests that at an annealing 
temperature of 200°C, all remaining water in the pseudo-boehmite (AlOOH) in the bayerite was 
removed from the alumina film. This water removal was a contributing factor in the transition to a 
single-phase film.   
When the alumina films were annealed at 400°C for 1 hour, the alumina film phase changed 
to γ-alumina [12].At annealing temperatures of 400 to 800°C, the phase of the alumina film slowly 
transitioned from γ-alumina to θ-alumina. 
FTIR was used to observe OH bond and Al-O bond in the alumina. Fig. 2 depicts the FTIR 
spectra of alumina films annealed at temperatures of 400 to 800°C for 1 hour. From Fig. 2, it can be 
observed that the broad peak at 3300 to 3600 cm-1significantly decreases as a higher annealing 
temperature is used. This decreasing broad peak corresponds to a decreasing concentration of 
hydroxyl bonds as the annealing temperature is increased.. This hydroxl bond decrease is due to the 
alumina film phase transforming from pseudo-boehmite to γ-alumina, and then later to θ-alumina. 
A sufficiently high annealing temperature provides the hydroxyl bond sufficient energy to break 
and be removed from the film. The broad peak at 1650 cm-1 corresponds to the concentration of 
water molecules in the alumina film. Similarly, this peak decreases as the annealing temperature is 
shown that boehmite and bayerite exhibit cubic-closed packing (CCP), while gibbsite exhibits 
hexagonal closed packing (HCP).

of air, oxygen, and nitrogen. The reaction temperature was 400°C, and was maintained for 20 
minutes. Fig. 4 depicts the XRD spectra of alumina films subjected to different annealing 
treatments. The XRD spectra depicted in Fig. 4 shows clearly the alumina film annealing at 400oC 
as a function of RTA atmosphere; however, it could not be seen obvious phase changes of alumina 
film annealing in different atmosphere from the XRD analysis. Peak A corresponds to the (400) 
plane of γ-alumina, while peak B corresponds to the (051) plane of pseudo-boehmite. Alumina 
films under certain atmospheric annealing conditions would be affected between pseudo-boehmite 
and γ-aluminum. Air is composed of 20% oxygen, and correspondingly, the intensities of peaks A 
and B for films annealed under air are higher than peaks for films annealed under nitrogen. With the 
increase of annealing temperature, the OH band in pseudo-boehmite structure tends to be broken, 
and the boehmite phase (octahedrally coordinated structure) would transfer to γ-phase aluminum 
oxide (tetrahedrallly coordinated structure), favoring pentahedrally coordinated aluminum and the 
occurrence of singly ionized oxygen vacancies which are regarded to the F+ center. Du et al. [14] 
and Li et al. [15] observed that it could be seen blue emission in the alumina film due to F+ center. 
 
the alumina film has transitioned to γ-alumina, aluminum atoms with five coordinates are produced, 
along with oxygen defects. Hence, the blue emission is still observable. When the annealing 
temperature is 600°C under an oxygen atmosphere, the ambient oxygen would compensate the 
oxygen vacancy of the alumina film and the blue emission would be reduced. Thus, as oxygen 
availability increases, the strength of the blue emission correspondingly decreases. As the annealing 
temperature rises to 800°C, it film composition slowly changes from the γ-alumina phase to the 
θ-alumina phase, decreasing the oxygen defect density, and correspondingly, quenching blue light 
emission. Similarly, if the solution used to fabricate the alumina film has a pH of 5, the resultant 
film is primarily composed of boehmite and gibbsite phases, wherein the gibbsite phase is Al(OH)3 
and has a HCP structure. In contrast, the boehmite phase has a CCP structure. These two structures 
are incompatible and will separate.  This incompatibility is another potential source of blue 
emission, and is primarily dependent on the dehydration of boehmite and the corresponding oxygen 
defect density, as shown in Fig. 5(b). Thus, when the annealing temperature is 50°C, blue emission 
is lower. When the annealing temperature rises to 400°C, the alumina film phase transforms from 
boehmite to γ-alumina, and the subsequent oxygen deficiency increases the intensity of blue light 
emission. When the annealing temperature reaches 600°C, the intensity of the blue light emission is 
similarly quenched as was observed in the case where the film solution had a pH of 10. Fig. 5(c) 
compares the PL spectra of solutions with a pH of 10 and 7 at 50°C.
