二、緣由與目的 
數位學習之ㄧ主要特色為個人化學
習，其學習環境須能達到適性化以符合個
人化學習的需求。要讓一數位學習環境具
有適性化教學能力，並且可以及時、自主
式地(autonomously)調整教材的難易度，則在
教學策略、教學物件、及學習路徑之間須
有ㄧ整合的智慧型溝通、調適機制。而各
方具有一標準之辭書 (Ontology)以便溝同
及施行語意查詢(Semantic Query)則是不可
或缺的需求。因此在適性化的數位學習環
境中，首要的工作便是各種知識辭書
(Knowledge Ontology)的訂定及實做了。使
用辭書 Ontology 可以達到知識共享
(Knowledge sharing)、知識重用(Knowledge 
reuse)、智慧型溝通及合作 (Intelligent 
Communication and Corporation)、以及建構
知識豐富(knowledge rich)的系統。 而這些
功能需求也正是一個適性化數位學習環境
所急迫需要的。 
本計畫主要目的在賦予整個數位學習
環境ㄧ個適性化數位學習的能力。透過本
計畫的成果，適性化數位學習平台可藉由
追蹤及分析學習者的學習路徑及學習行為
來了解一學習者的學習狀態，並且能據以
調整適性之課程、亦即微調其學習路徑及
選擇恰當教學物件。更可收集學習者的學
習歷程來廣義地調整適性之學習策略，也
就利用不同之資料探勘(Data Mining)技術
去分析所收集到的所有學習者學習歷程紀
錄，產生ㄧ個Meta-策略庫，來提供予教學
系統按照學生之特徵(Profile)和學習缺失
(Deficiency)作為選擇更適當教學策略及選
擇更恰當教學路徑之依據。 
有鑑於數位學習未來之發展性與影響
力，行政院國科會於 91年通過「數位學習
國家型科技計畫」，希望借助政府政策引
導，推動全民數位學習，縮減數位落差，
以提昇在知識經濟時代國家整體競爭力。
「數位學習」指的是以數位工具，透過有
線或無線網路，取得數位教材，進行線上
或離線之學習活動。因而數位學習產業涵
括數位學習工具（載具及輔具）研發、數
位學習網路環境建置、數位教材內容開
發、以及數位學習活動的設計等。目前國
際上已有許多流通的標準，其中針對"學習
元件(Learning Object)"與"學習元件元資料
(Learning Object Metadata, LOM)"，在國際間
最具份量的標準為美國國防部 ADL 的
SCORM(Sharable Content Object Reference 
Model)的規格，此規格已被國內各研究單位
與產業界廣泛採用。 
目前國內數位學習管理系統的開發與
應用已極為普遍，但相關的發展與研究以
整合數位學習標準居多，對於適性化數位
學習的研究仍略顯不足。本計劃的主要目
的 在 建 立 一 個 適 性 化 框 架 (Adaptive 
Framework)，透過所訂定的協定(Protocol)
及介面(Interface)，此框架可賦予一數位學
習環境適性化數位學習的能力。適性化數
位學習的主要特色為個人化學習，數位學
習環境須依據個別學員的學習狀況給予最
適切的學習策略、學習路徑與學習元件。
因此一個適性化框架需具備兩階層(Two 
Layer)功能，第一階層為適性化教學策略之
推薦，而第二階層則為適性化學習路徑之
微調。第一階層最主要的功能為利用學生
的特徵(Profile)和學習缺失(Deficiency)作為
選擇更適切的教學策略及選擇更適當的教
學路徑之依據。而第二階層最主要的特色
為自動分析與推論學生的學習狀況，並即
時(Real Time)及自主性地(Autonomously)調
整教材難易度、微調與學習路徑。要達成
上述適性化數位學習的目的，在適性化框
架中各個功能模組之間需有一統合的智慧
型溝通、調適機制。因此在各功能模組間
建置標準化知識辭書（Knowledge Ontology）
以便進行語意查詢(Semantic Query)與推論

 RDF/RDFS 
RDF/RDFS(Resource description framework) 
/( Resource description framework schema) 
[4]。 RDF 基 本 上 是 一種基於 XML 
(eXtensible Markup Language) 的語言格式，
主要用於製作交換以及自動處理網際網路
資源之描述（物件可利用 URIs來定址）。
RDF 用以載明“資源描述”之語法和模式。
它可利用 XML來描述之，但它在 XML上
加上了直接標籤模式。RDF 並載明了特定
應用領域的 Schema，以宣告該領域自己的
資源描述語彙，利用該 Schema，該領域的
應用將可由語法的層級提昇至語意層面。 
 DAML+OIL 
DAML+OIL (DARPA Agent Markup plus 
Ontology Inference Layer)[5]是由歐洲與美洲
聯合委員會所發展的語意語言。其能力是
建 立 在 XML 及 RDF(S)的基礎上。
DAML+OIL依據路徑表示文件的資料及資
訊。類似 XML及 RDF的文件，DAML+OIL
亦提供規則(rules)及定義(definition)。此外，
DAML+OIL 提供了描述資源間的關係
(relationships) 及 限 制 (constraints)， 所 以
DAML+OIL的文件不僅可讓機器理解，更
可對內容進行推理(reasoning)。 
 
 OWL 
OWL(Ontology Web Language)[6]是 W3C 
Web Ontology Group (Web Ont)延伸自
XML、RDF、以及 RDFS 所發展的 Web 
Ontology Language。 OWL 的語法是以
DAML+OIL為基礎，主要功能是用來定義
Web Ontologies及 Ontologies 的相關知識基
礎。其最大的特色是允許資源的描述反推
(negative information)。 
 
目前 OWL存在三種規格，描述如下: 
 
 OWL Lite : 可描述類別階層及簡單的
限制。亦即最簡單的 OWL語言。 
 OWL DL : 具有類似 OIL 的抽象語
法，是一種高階的描述語言特徵的語
法。 
 OWL Full : 具有完整的 OWL詞彙，可
使用更多的 OWL 描述邏輯來表達資
訊。

層面來看，目前學生使用系統學習課程的
方式主要是由課程設計者事先規畫課程章
節，在每個章節中，系統提供相關教材供
學習，這樣的方式可視為一種線性的學習

教材，比較這樣的學習模式與傳統的課堂
學習，可以發現不同之處只是將教材由原
本實體課本轉變成數位內容而已，實際
上，他們並未解決學生的真實需求，例如
學生對於教材的內容呈現方式的偏好，不
同學生有不同的學習需求與學習順序。因
此，基於這樣的觀察，本計畫預期提出幾
項功能來支援或輔助目前的目前學習系
統: 
 
1. 提供推論機制了解學習者需求。 
2. 提供個人化的學習課程。 
3. 提供合適的學習路徑。 
 
針對前一項功能，已於先前計畫中實
現[3,4]，本計畫主要將致力於第二、三項
功能。在動態組合個人化課程上，最大的
挑戰在於如何從教材庫中快速地挑選合式
教材作為課程內容，觀察此一問題，可以
發現此問題可以換成多目標組合的問題，
亦即如何在多條件要求下選擇出合適的教
材，此問題已知為 NP問題，當教材庫過大
時，找出最佳組合的時間將成指數成長，
因此如何利用有效的挑選機制在有效時間
內挑選合適的教材便成了一大挑戰，因此
本計畫將發展一基於粗粒子群集最佳化
(Particle Swarm Optimization，PSO)[2]的挑選
機制，達成上述目標。

動態課程的設計與產生，最大的問題
在於系統如何得知學生的學習需求，並根
據此學習需求挑選合適的教材與組織課

者的查詢語句能推論學習者需要學習的概

討如何動態組織教材來客制化的課程以滿
足不同學習者的需求。

四、計畫成果 
 
經過查準率和查全率的效能評比，並
以 F-measure統合評比，透過 DPSO所選擇
的教材所包含的概念多能符合系統要求的
概念集合與權重分布，同時透過問卷的調
查，所提供的教材也能滿足學習者的需
求，此外檢驗系統的效能，使用 DPSO 的
機制，系統能在有效的時間內，找出儘可
能符合的教材，儘管所選擇的教材不見得
是真正的最佳解，但是透過分析所找出的
解已趨近真正最佳解。另外透過使用不同
大小的教材庫，我們也發現儘管 DPSO 能
解決挑選的效能問題，但是當教材庫過大
時仍會出現效能下降的問題，此問題在於
當教材過大時，粒子的搜尋空間維度也將
等比變大，因此如何改進此問題，也是未
來值得繼續研究之議題。

For the majority of E-learning systems their important role is the electronic teachers who can assist stu-
dents to learn and review courses enrolled. Unfortunately, an E-learning system does not resemble a tea-
cher who can evaluate the current students’ learning status by the immediate class observations and
adjust his/her policy accordingly. Hence, to be able to dynamically compose tests to examine students’
learning status and clear their confusions becomes an important issue. This paper argues that how to for-
malize test requirements dynamically and how to select suitable items effectively are two main chal-
lenges in the current researches of computerized test generation. Hence, this article proposed a novel
approach that can compose suitable tests to easily and efﬁciently evaluate the learning statuses of stu-
dents. The proposed approach obeys the Discrete Particle Swarm Optimization (DPSO) scheme and uses
partial course ontology to generate tests according to the studied materials. Besides, it also harnesses the
notion of testlets to construct testlet-based tests for students. In the experiments, the results show that
the proposed approach can effectively formalize the tested concepts and their weights, and also generate
the well-scrutinized test in accordance with the test requirements in the acceptable time.

1. Introduction
A test which contains well-scrutinized test items is greatly ben-
eﬁcial and valuable for teachers who want to inspect whether stu-
dents indeed comprehend relevant courses and to diagnose
learning confusions and learning bottlenecks of students (Yin,
Chang, Hwang, Hwang, & Chan, 2006). Especially in the E-learning
systems, students usually learn relevant courses through mutual
interactions; therefore, concretely understanding the current
learning statuses of students becomes more essential, because
E-learning systems do not resemble teachers who can evaluate
the current learning statuses of students by the immediate class
observations. Adopting the automatically computerized tests has
been proved more intuitional and easier an approach for acquiring
the students’ learning statuses and misconceptions (Hwang, Chu,
Yin, & Lin, 2008).
Previous investigations have shown that there are several
important challenges in the computerized test systems needed to
be improved, in which how to formalize the requirement of tested
concepts and how to select suitable items to generate tests are two
most critical issues in our opinion. In order to effectively examine a
student’s learning status and diagnose his/her misconceptions, the
majority of current computerized test systems take multiple test
requirements as the criteria of test generation. Among test require-
ments, deciding which concepts should be tested and what weight
each tested concept should be assigned, is the most critical, but
only few attempts were made for this problem. In fact, by selecting
correct and effective concepts, it is usually very helpful and bene-
ﬁcial to understand students’ learning status and also quite easy to
ﬁgure out their misconceptions at the same time. Hence, formaliz-
ing the concepts which need to be tested and deciding their
weights in the selected scope of courses becomes a very urgent is-
sue. In the second challenge, in order to satisfy such multiple test
requirements, some computerized test systems utilized dynamic
programming approach to minimize the requirement discrepan-
cies between the test requirements and the information of picked
items, and some heuristic approaches are also proposed to gener-
ate a suitable test automatically in accordance with the multiple
requirements. But these approaches reveal weaknesses when gen-
erating a test from the large-scale item bank; it’s also recognized
they take longer execution time in the test generation if there is
a huge amount of items in the item bank. Hence, a new strategy
of item selection, which can dynamically generate a suitable test
from a large-scale item bank in a sensible interval, becomes very
essential for E-learning systems.
In this paper, to cope with the above challenges, a novel ap-
proach for the dynamic testlet-based computerized test generation
is proposed. This approach, according to the designated scope of

3.1. Dynamic partial course ontology construction
The ontology plays an important role in determining what con-
cepts, with their weights, should be appeared in a test. A con-
structed ontology contains two basic elements, concepts and
their relations. Concepts can be regarded as the topic or subject
on which the courses describe, and their relations can be consid-
ered as the correlations between concepts in a course. By analyzing
the structure of a course, the constructed partial course ontology
will contain three main relationships, the Single Relationship, Hier-
archical Relationship, and Interactive Relationship. The dynamic
ontology construction algorithm will be based on Formal Concept
Analysis (FCA) (Hashemi, De Agostino, Westgeest, & Talburt,
2004; Yaohua & Yiyu, 2005) and build the relevant concepts and
relationships for some selected materials. The major four proce-
dures of the algorithm, i.e. (1) Relevant concept determination, (2)
Context matrix construction, (3) Hierarchy of partial course ontology
construction, and (4) Interactive relationships construction, are ex-
plained in detail as follows.
Step 1: Determine relevant concepts of the speciﬁc course
For the construction of dynamic partial course ontology, one
preparative work is to determine what concepts a course should
cover. In this paper, the study case, the Sun Company’s Java Tuto-
rial (Sun, 2009), is analyzed to determine relevant concepts of a
JAVA Course, which, according to the opinions of experts, appear
in the Java Tutorial are actually opted as the JAVA concepts.
Step 2: Construct the context matrix
Based on the selected materials or a partial content of a course,
the algorithm slices them into sentences. Subsequently, the objec-
tive concepts are extracted, possibly, from each sentence and all
triple attributes (C, S, I) are constructed to form a context matrix,
presented as the collection of objective concepts C, sentences S,
and relationships I in the Table 1. For example, in the table, the
ﬁelds c1–c8 stand for the objective concepts that should be tested
and, in the rows, the sentences s1–s6 are obtained from the mate-
rials. In such a context matrix, the relationship value is set to 1 if
the concept c appears in the sentence s; otherwise, the value is
set to zero.

4.3.5. Terminal condition of DPSO scheme
After updating the velocities of all the particles, they will move
to new positions. The same cycles of ﬁtness value evaluation, local
best solution search, global best solution, and particles update are
performed repeatedly until the default number of generations is
reached. Only the corresponding index in the position of the ﬁnal
global best particle has value 1 will an item be picked into the spe-
ciﬁc testlet. Through DPSO, the related items would yield for each
speciﬁc testlet. Hence, if there are k TSs, which are generated by
the partial course ontology, DPSO will be performed k times to gen-
erate the complete and suitable test in accordance with the multi-
ple test requirements.

5.2.4. Experiment 4 – Organized type evaluation in the different
categories
According to the cognitive type of learning in Bloom’s taxonomy
(Anderson & Krathwohl, 2001; Bloom, Engelhart, Furst, Hill, & Kra-
thwohl, 1956), to fully learn concepts in a course, three important
learning types, Knowledge, Comprehension, and Application, have to
be included in a learning track of students. In such a learning track,
a student knows each concept in the Knowledge learning type,
subsequently, comprehends relevant concepts in the Comprehen-
sion learning type, and then nimbly uses them better in the Appli-
cation learning type. Correspondingly, in order to understand and
examine the learning status of students, a test should also contain
the items that can examine whether students know, comprehend
and is able to apply speciﬁc concepts. In this paper, the items of
the Single Type (S), the Hierarchical-Concepts (HC) Type, and the
Interactive-Concepts (IC) Type can be seamlessly regarded as of
the learning types of Bloom’s taxonomy respectively where a gen-
erated test can be fully conﬁgured to test the learning status of
students.
An item of the Single type associates only with a single concept
and consequently it is regarded as the Knowledge learning type. For
example, an item may test a single speciﬁc concept of ‘‘how to de-
clare an Array”, so it is usually to test whether students know how
to use a programming construct or not only. In the second type, the
HC, an item may associate with several hierarchically related con-
cepts. Such an item can examine whether a student understands
several relevant concepts belonged to a speciﬁc topic; for instance,
an item can test a topic, ‘‘Data type” which includes several con-
cepts like ‘‘int”, ‘‘long”, ‘‘ﬂoat”, ‘‘double”, and ‘‘char” in Java. That
is, items belong to the HC type can examine whether students can
comprehend the deﬁnitions and relations of several relevant con-
cepts and, in the mean time, can classify these concepts and know
how to use them in suitable occasions. Thus, an item of the HC type
is regarded as the Comprehension type. In the third type, the IC, an
item contains some interactive concepts; in fact, in the program-
ming learning, all the submitting items of IC type play a rather
essential role. For example, a concept, ‘‘for statement”, may be re-
lated to several concepts, ‘‘increment Operator”, ‘‘conditional state-
ment”, ‘‘for loop”, and etc. In another example, the ‘‘sorting” may
contain two concepts, ‘‘array” and ‘‘for loop”. Accordingly, each
item of IC type can be used to test whether students can blend har-
moniously and grasp thoroughly the objective concepts and can
nimbly apply the studied concepts in new situations, and thus
can be regarded as of the Application type in the Bloom’s taxonomy.

二、 KES-AMSTA 2009 簡介 
KES-AMSTA每年選擇由國際知名大學主辦，KES-AMSTA2009則由在瑞典的烏普蘇
拉大學(Uppsala University)主辦，烏普蘇拉雖是斯堪地納維亞(Scandinavian) 
的一個中世紀既建立的古城，目前卻也是一工業重鎮，整個烏普蘇拉就是一個烏
普蘇拉大學城，相當於英國的牛津及劍橋。其位置在斯德哥爾摩(Stockholm)北
方七十公里處。烏普蘇拉大學是歐洲的古老大學之一，也是斯堪地納維亞半島所
有國家中最古老的大學，在研究及教學上都具有世界一流水準。烏普蘇拉大學共
計有國內及國際學生 40,000人，國內及國際教師 6,000人，目前是世界大學百
大排名第六十四的國際知名大學，近一百年來總共製造了八位諾貝爾獎得主，其
中甚至有父子檔，烏普蘇拉無可置疑地可說是全世界的一個學術重鎮。 
而代理者及多代理者系統可以說是近代軟體技術之典範(paradigm)，並且長久以
來一直被視為是建造具自主性(autonomous)的複雜及智慧型系統的下一代不二
人選技術。因此 KES-AMSTA-2009可說是代理者及多代理者系統領域的研究人員
一個能討論他們在代理者及多代理者系統的先進技術及應用的一絕佳國際性論
壇機會。
