一、前言 
我國一直是以稻米為主食的國家，稻米的供應安全與台灣的民生穩定息息相關。隨著快
速的產業經濟轉變以及加入 WTO 後農業開放的衝擊，台灣自身農業已無法迴避外來稻米進口
競爭。關貿總局統計數據顯示，2010 年我國自東南亞湄公河委員會國家進口量已達 91,294
公噸，且有可能逐年增加該區的進口配額。此外，東南亞地區的稻米生產，除了自給之外，
其生產出口已達世界首位，其產量亦與世界的糧食供需平衡息息相關。以越南而言，越南為
東南亞僅次泰國的第二大米產輸出國，該國 80%以上的稻米產自於湄公河三角洲。因此在該
區域的農作偵測與產量估計，對於台灣未來的糧食輸入政策制定，更顯重要。稻米亦為台灣
地區重要的糧食作物，其種植面積與產量、生產查報等，皆為政府制定農糧政策的重要參考
指標，1980年開始，由台灣省糧食處 (現已改隸為行政院農委會農糧署) 依行政院核定「台
灣地區稻米生產調查改進要點」，逐年施行稻作面積調查。由農糧署委託林務局農林航空測
量所進行航空攝影，並以人工方式結合耕地坵塊圖藉以辨識水稻田之種植範圍，實為一件費
時且費力之工作。近年來，隨著衛星遙測技術的發展與普及化，利用資源衛星進行稻作偵測，
成為進行水稻田調查時重要參考資訊來源之一。 
從同一地多時期累積的大量衛星影像，必包含地物類別時序變化的資訊，因此如何從多
時期衛星影像中萃取出土地類別時序變化的資訊，並加以分類建模，是非常具理論及應用價
值的研究。故此，本研究將從時間序列的衛星影像中萃取作物生長周期資訊，進行稻作生長
階段的偵測與種植型態分類，以提升衛星影像在時間尺度上處理與分析技術，未來可據此分
析區域尺度的衛星影像土地類別時序變化，拓展時間序列衛星影像的應用層面及價值。 
二、研究目的 
傳統地物偵測常以單一時間衛星影像中的地物光譜特徵做為辨識地物的依據，然而僅憑
單一時間的影像，實難區分光譜特徵相似的地物，以水稻田為例，成熟水稻與牧草地之光譜
資料特徵十分相似，若僅以單一時間之影像資料，將難以對水稻與牧草進行辨識 (陳益凰，
1998)；此外，若使用水稻收割後的單時段影像，單憑光譜特徵僅能得知土地覆蓋類型為土壤，
卻無法判斷其土地利用類型為水稻田，因此周昶成（1997) 選擇稻作插秧期與稻作分蘗期兩
個時間點的 SPOT 影像，以確定的水稻範圍為訓練區進行監督式分類，但是只選擇兩個時間
點的影像，必須準確估計兩個生長時期的時間以選擇影像，若是部分區域有雲，則被雲遮蔽
的區域無法進行判釋，而且部分水稻田仍然容易與其他地物混淆；由於水稻在不同生長期具
有不同的光譜反應，利用水稻在不同生長期各波段影像灰度值的光譜變化曲線，可避免與當
地的其他地物混淆的情形，以求得較高的分類精度，所以結合多時期影像能有效改善上述之
情形，並且多時期衛星影像也能提供更多用來辨別單張影像受到雲霧覆蓋的像元點分類所需
的資訊，因此使用多時期衛星影像所產生的時間序列資料進行稻米偵測與生長期之研究，成
為近年來衛星偵測稻米分類之熱門課題。 
回顧這十年來遙測與稻米相關的熱門研究中，常被拿來應用的多時期衛星影像的時間序
列資料，大致上可分為短周期(小於一年)與長周期(一年或一年以上)的時間序列資料兩種，受
限於早期的硬體設備等限制，利用短周期的時間序列資料多為較早期的研究，如：陳哲俊(1994)
利用半年期的 SPOT時間序列資料進行進行稻作插秧日期的評估，陳益凰(1998)利用半年期的
多時段 SPOT衛星影像結合耕地坵塊之地理資訊，統計坵塊內各像元之 NDVI隨時間的變化
情形，分別以 NDVI 差分影像分類法、時間剖面匹配法與波峰偵測法結合地理資訊進行辨識
 4 
 
模態函數（Intrinsic Mode Functions，IMFs），進而萃取與分析其具有物理意義與時間周期的
訊息。由於在同一區域內累積多時期之衛星影像可能存在時序上的觀測地表資訊，如地物在
光譜上的周期性變化，但同時在同一位置的時間序列資料中，亦可能來自參雜了一些其他類
別之雜訊，如雲霧、陰影等，此種混合像元的時序資訊，本身大多為非線性與非穩態的時間
序列，因此可藉由訊號處理分解代表各具物理特性的不同頻率信號，吾人透過經驗可以分析
而得到個別類別之週期變化，最後排除其時序上之雜訊或不必要之資訊，進而萃取出有關稻
作時序變化的資訊，並將其資訊應用於辨識稻米的插秧與收割時間的偵測與稻作類型的分類。
因此本研究的主要目的為以 MODIS 與 SPOT 等長時間系列衛星影像，探討經驗模態分解法
與小波轉換萃取稻作耕種生長曲線的資訊已進行生長資訊偵測與型態分類。研究欲達成目標
包含了研發時間序列衛星影像資訊萃取的方法、探討 EMD 濾噪與目前通用的相關小波函數
濾噪之比較、將濾噪後之作物生長曲線資訊應用於台灣地區水稻生長之偵測與分類並擴大至
如越南湄公三角洲地區的區域尺度，提升衛星影像時序資料在區域尺度上的應用。 
三、研究資料來源 
3.1 衛星影像資料 
3.1.1 MODIS 產品影像 
MODIS影像由美國航太總署(National Aeronautics and Space Administration ,NASA)所公
開的衛星影像產品，在本研究使用的測試資料為 2005年MODIS地表參數產品MOD09Q1 是
一個以 MOD09GQ 7個光譜為架構所組製的 level-3產品。地面解析度為 250m，時間解析度
為 8天，所使用的波段為紅光波段(620 ~ 670 nm)與近紅外光波段(841 ~ 876 nm)。影像產品的
中的每個像素皆以 8天觀測其中最好的 L2G觀測值來記錄，包含了最大觀測幅合區、較低視
角與最小雲量與雲影及大氣膠(Vermote et al. 2008)。影像產品在品質控制資訊圖層中提供了雲
量資訊，這項產品經過 stage 2的驗證並可以提供科學性的研究發表，相較於 SPOT衛星影像，
衛星的時序規律，但空間解析力較差，亦受混合像元的干擾。MODIS資料取得的原始投影格
式為正弦等積投影，因此影像資料將被拼合剪裁並重投影成符合需求的座標系統。 
3.1.2 SPOT 影像 
SPOT 系列衛星為法國所發射的資源遙測衛星，研究採用的多光譜解析度影像地面解析
度為 20公尺， SPOT衛星影像標準產品分為原始影像(1A)、系統改正影像(2A)、以及精密幾
何改正影像(3A)等三個等級，由於進行時間序列資料分析，不同時期影像間的各像元必須準
確對應，故本研究選擇經過精密幾何改正的 3A 等級 SPOT 影像做為測試資料的來源。由於
SPOT 的高空間解析與可調整的任務需求，同區衛星重訪週期與影像覆蓋程度較不穩定，亦
為本研究之可行性探討主題。

4.3 時序資料濾噪 
從衛星影像推導的植生指數 NDVI 時間序列，通常具有時間雜訊，如圖 4-4 所示，且幾
乎無法藉由大氣校正將之根除。對時間序列的資料處理與分析而言，移除這些雜訊是很重要
的步驟。一些常見的移除雜訊的方法，如傅立葉轉換(Fourier Transform)或小波轉換等，都常
見於應用植生時間訊號的文獻中。經驗模態分解法(Emprical Mode Decomposition ,EMD)則是
目前較新的研究嘗試。 
 
4.3.1 傅立葉轉換

4.3.2 小波轉換

4.3.4 濾噪分析小結 
從衛星影像推導的 NDVI 植生指數時間序列數據具有的時間雜訊，必須藉由一些訊號處
理的方法來移除這些雜訊。一些移除雜訊的方法，如傅立葉轉換或小波轉換等，都常見於應
用植生時間訊號的文獻之中；其中傅立葉轉換很容易因訊號的非線性因素而產生無意義的諧
波資訊(Huang et al. 1999)；小波轉換則需要先決定適合的母函數來濾除雜訊，在資訊不足的
情況下很難操作。許多研究已經證明，對於非穩態的訊號，小波轉換比傅利葉轉換更具解析
能力，見表 4-1。因此本研究將僅比較小波轉換與內建經驗模態分解濾噪的差異。

6.1 研究總結 
台灣農家之平均耕地面積小於一公頃，前期研究所使用 MODIS 的產品影像為 250 公尺
解析度。因此針對台灣地區，小面積與非均質的稻作型態，易產生混合像元問題。因此本次
加入較高解析度的影像 SPOT，利用一年期的 SPOT長時間衛星影像 (每個測試區約取 70張
影像)，以期增加分類之精度，但是高空間解析度的影像，往往更容易受到採樣時間週期不穩
定，以及更多地表現象所產生的輻射變化以及大氣干擾等誤差，因此本研究更進一步探討其
應用的可行性以及如何應用。 
由 SPOT影像推估稻作插秧日的偵測成果顯示，EMD與三種不同的母波作為基底的小波

Abstract.Rice is themost important food crop in Taiwan.During recent decades, rice production
in Taiwan has sharply declined because of industrialization and urbanization. Monitoring the
areas of rice cultivation thus becomes important due to the official initiatives to ensure food
supply. This study aims to develop a remote sensing classification approach for mapping double-
cropped irrigated rice fields in Taiwan using time-series SPOT (Satellite Pour l’Observation
de la Terre) data. Three study sites with different farming conditions in Taitung, Chiayi, and
Taoyuan counties were chosen to test the new classification method. Data processing steps
include: 1. filtering time-series SPOT-based normalized difference vegetation index (NDVI)
using empirical mode decomposition (EMD) and wavelet transform, 2. classifying double-
cropped irrigated rice fields using statistical methods (i.e., correlation analysis and sign-test
statistics), and 3. assessing classification accuracy. The comparisons between the classification
maps and ground-truth maps in 2005 indicated that classification using the EMD-based filtered
NDVI time-series data yielded more accurate results than did the wavelet transform-based

1 Introduction
Rice is a globally important food crop, ranked third behind maize and wheat
(http://www.wordiq.com/definition/Rice). It occupies more than 11% of the global cropland
area1 and provides food for more than three billion people around the world.2 Rice cropping
creates the main source of employment and livelihood for large populations in rural areas and
forms the economic basis of many developing countries in Asia. The rapid growth in human
consumption, economy, and population have driven environmental degradation problems such
as climate change.3 The global mean temperature is estimated to increase beyond preindustrial
temperatures by at least 1.5◦C, and possibly up to 6◦C or slightly more, by 2100.4 Interactions
between environmental and social changes are rapidly leading to profound impacts, such as loss
of habitat and biodiversity5 on major ecosystems.4
Temperature increases lead to heat stress and rising sea levels and, consequently, more
frequent and severe climate extremes. Climate change significantly impacts crop production,6
especially in low-lying deltas and coastal areas where rice is predicted to experience the brunt of

High resolution satellites, such as Satellite Pour I’Observation de la Terr (SPOT), seem to be

The use of high resolution data, however, has some disadvantages, such as the high cost of
data acquisition and a limited range of coverage. Therefore, studies often use single images for
land-use or land-cover mapping.30–33 The use of single images is not sufficient for mapping
crop patterns due to the similarities in spectral characteristics among different types of crops.
In this study, under a contract between the Center for Space and Remote Sensing Research

the French Space Agency, the CSRSR receiving station is able to receive satellite images when

This study is thus aimed to investigate the feasibility of using high-resolution time-series
SPOT satellite data for mapping and updating information of rice growing areas in Taiwan.
The data were processed using EMD for smoothing time-series SPOT NDVI data, and wavelet
transform was used to compare the performance of EMD in noise filtering of the time-series
data. A new classification system based on statistical analyses of filtered time-series data was
developed to classify the double-cropped irrigated rice in Taiwan.
2 Study Area
Rice is mainly cultivated in three regions of Taiwan, the southeast, southwest, and north-
ern areas, and is dependent on irrigation availability. The double-cropping system (double-
cropped irrigated rice) using short-term varieties of rice (approximately 110 days) is com-
monly practiced in Taiwan. This cropping system consists of two rice crops per year; the
first is grown from January to April and the second from May to September.7 We selected
three study sites that were representative of these three main rice growing regions: Taitung,
Chiayi, and Taoyuan counties (Fig. 1). These study sites were selected because the rice fields
in each have distinct characteristics useful for testing the performance of the crop classification
method based on SPOT NDVI time-series data filtered using EMD and the wavelet transform
methods.
 Taitung location: This 100-km2 study site was located in the southeast region [Fig. 1(a)]
and was characterized by rice fields (reddish tones) uniformly distributed along a large
river (cyan tones).
 Chiayi location: This 25-km2 study site was located in the southwest region in an urban
area with a dense road network developed for transportation [Fig. 1(b)]. Rice fields in this
area were relatively small, and many were located along small roads (light cyan color).
 Taoyuan location: This 25-km2 study site was situated in the northern region and was
characterized by developed areas and small ponds (black color) [Fig. 1(c)]. The rice fields
in this county were small and scattered throughout the region.

3.2 Ground-Truth Data
Three vector format rice crop maps of the three study sites (Taitung, Chiayi, and Taoyuan) in

and used for classification accuracy assessments. These maps were originally constructed by
digitizing aerial photos (scale 1:5000) that were captured twice per year during the rice-heading
stages of each crop season. The digitized rice crop maps were validated using field survey data.
These maps were converted to a raster format at 20-m resolution and used as ground-truth data
for accuracy assessments (Fig. 3).

In the first step of the correlation analysis, we examined the linear relationship be-

(Table 1; Sec. 4.2). Pixels that were highly correlated with the mean pattern were further
used for sign-test analyses. In the second step, the sign-test statistic was used to test the
similarity of two double-cropped irrigated rice patterns (training pattern and filtered NDVI
time-series pattern). The second step was implemented because, in correlation analysis, the
calculated correlation coefficient is a single number that describes the overall relationship
between two variables rather than the similarity of two patterns. Therefore, a non-rice pixel
may have a strong correlation with the mean pattern due to the similarity of its pattern. Three
signatures extracted from the filtered NDVI data for the double-cropping system, forest and
a water body in the study area (Fig. 6), clearly show that although the magnitudes of the
NDVI values for these land-use types were different, their temporal patterns were nearly
identical. A strong correlation between the patterns was observed, and the correlation co-
efficient was greater than 0.85 in all cases (Table 2). Therefore, correlation analysis alone
was insufficient to differentiate between the double-cropped irrigated rice and other land-use
types.

medians were similar. In this study, we used an exact algorithm43 to compute p-values. The sign
test was actually a special case of the one-sample binomial test. In this case, S+ and S− were the

5 Results
5.1 Temporal NDVI Profiles
The EMDmethod was applied to generate smoothed time-series SPOT NDVI data. An example
of a smoothed NDVI profile of the double-cropping system that was extracted from the filtered
NDVI data (Fig. 7) shows that the smooth curve was able to preserve the magnitude of the
temporal NDVI data. The smoothed pattern reveals temporal characteristics of seasonal changes
in the rice fields as illustrated by the NDVI responses. In general, the rice pattern increased
in NDVI intensity after the crop was transplanted. Rice reached the highest NDVI value (rice
heading date) at approximately day of year (DOY) 101 for the first crop and DOY 252 for the
second crop. After the heading phase, the NDVI intensity started to level off. This information
highlighted the differences between patterns of double-cropped irrigated rice fields and those
of other land-use types throughout the study area, which allowed us to inspect proper temporal
training patterns that were used for later classification.
5.2 Accuracy Assessment
5.2.1 Taitung
Rice fields within the Taitung study site were spatially concentrated along rivers. Taitung is one
of the most important rice growing regions in Taiwan where rice cultivation provides income
and livelihoods for local people. The rice fields in this county are relatively homogeneous. Thus,
most of the rice fields could be clearly identified using the filtered data derived from either the
EMD or wavelet transform (Fig. 8). The results of pixel-by-pixel comparisons between the
classification maps and the ground-truth maps indicated that classification using EMD for data
filtering gave a slightlymore accurate result than did thewavelet transform. The overall accuracy
and Kappa coefficient using EMD were 93.7% and 0.83, respectively (Table 3). Among the
different mother wavelets used for data filtering, the data derived from Daubechies 13 gave
the highest rate of classification success. The overall accuracy using the wavelet transform was
93.6%, and the Kappa coefficient was 0.83.
5.2.2 Chiayi
The classification results (Fig. 9) showed that the double-cropped irrigated rice was spatially
distributed throughout the Chiayi study area, which was characterized by a dense and well-
developed road network. Rice fields at this site were relatively small, and many fields were
located along small roads such that problems with mixed pixels were evident in this region. The
comparison between the classification maps and the ground-truth map showed that the highest
overall classification accuracy (85.8%) and Kappa coefficient (0.70) were achieved using the
EMD-based filtered data (Table 4). The classification using the data derived from the Symlet 6
wavelet transform gave the highest accuracy relative to that of the Coiflet 4 and Daubechies 13.
The overall accuracy was 83.5%, and the Kappa coefficient was 0.65.
5.2.3 Taoyuan
The spatial distribution of the double-cropped irrigated rice within the Taoyuan study site
(Fig. 10) shows that, in general, rice fields were scattered throughout the study area. When

HAZE REMOVAL FOR FORMOSAT-2 SATELLITE IMAGES 
 
Chi-Farn Chen and Li-Yu Chang 
 
 Center for Space and Remote Sensing Research 
National Central University 
No.300, Jungda Rd, Jhongli City, Taoyuan, 320, Taiwan 
e-mail: cfchen@csrsr.ncu.edu.tw 
 
ABSTRACT: 
Atmospheric scattering caused by haze usually generates path radiance and degrades the quality of remotely sensed 
images. Traditionally, Dark Object Subtraction (DOS) is a commonly used approach to reduce such disadvantage on 
images. Nevertheless, when the distribution of haze is not spatially homogeneous, DOS may no longer applicable. 
Haze Optimized Transformation (HOT) defines a Haze Vector that can be used to estimate the haze condition for each 
particular image pixel. Accordingly, a correspondent gray value correction for reducing the influence of path radiance 
can be obtained as well. However, for images with higher spatial resolution, the results of HOT algorithm become 
spatially unstable because the complicated ground objects are more observable. In this study, for suppressing the 
drawbacks caused by detailed ground objects, a filtering process is introduced to smooth the original output of HOT. 
Furthermore, in order to decide a reasonable window size for medium filter, a high frequency noise analysis in 
frequency domain is also included. After the proposed method is tested by using a FORMOSAT-2 high resolution 
satellite image, the results show that the haze removed image by proposed method can have better visual quality and 
applicability. 
 
KEY WORDS:  Haze Removal, Haze Optimized Transformation, FORMOSAT-2 Satellite Image 
 
 
1. INTRODUCTION 
It is well known that the haze with constituents of 
water droplet, ice crystals and fog/smoke particles will 
affect the quality of image acquired by optical remote 
sensing sensors (Kaufman, 1989). Basically, atmospheric 
scattering contributed by haze such as Rayleigh 
scattering, Mie scattering and Nonselective scattering can 
generate excessive path radiance and increase the total 
radiance received by sensors (Schott, 1997). Dark Object 
Subtraction (DOS) is a commonly used approach to 
reduce such excessive path radiance (Chavez, 1988, 
1989; Teillet and Fedosejevs, 1995). Basically, DOS uses 
some specific targets whose corresponding reflectance is 
almost zero as the “dark objects” (for example, clear 
water objects). As a result, the gray value of the pixels 
corresponding to these “dark objects” can be regarded as 
the contribution of path radiance. Then, by assuming that 
the distribution of haze is spatially homogeneous, the 
influence of haze can be reduced by subtracting the gray 
value of “dark objects” from original images.  
 
However, when the distribution of haze is not spatially 
homogeneous, DOS may have difficulties. Instead of 
using a specific target as a reference in DOS, Haze 
Optimized Transformation (HOT) (Zhang, 2002) defines 
a Clear Line in spectral feature space to describe the 
radiometric property of ground objects under the clear 
sky. By using Clear Line as a reference, a Haze Vector 
can be carried out to estimate the haze condition for each 
pixel. Moreover, a correspondent gray value correction 
for reducing the drawbacks caused by excessive path 
radiance is then derived by the estimated haze condition. 
In practice, using HOT to reduce the influence of haze on 
Landsat 7 ETM+ images has been tested and discussed in 
literature (Zhang, 2003). 
 
In this study, the HOT algorithm is tested by using a 
higher resolution image acquired by FORMOSAT-2 
satellite. In this case, because the complicated and 
detailed ground objects are more observable in 
FORMOSAT-2 satellite image, the results of original 
HOT algorithm become spatially unstable. Nevertheless, 
if a medium filter can be introduced to smooth the output 
of HOT in advance, a more reasonable result can be 
obtained. 
 
2. METHODOLOGY 
2.1  Haze Optimized Transformation (HOT) 
In HOT, at first a Clear Line should be defined in 
“blue-red” spectral space (Zhang, 2002). Basically, Clear 
Line can be obtained by using the linear regression of the 
pixels without haze contamination. Then, in spectral 
space, the haze condition for a particular image pixel can 
be represented by its shortest distance to the Clear Line, 
i.e., the HOT of that pixel. It implies that a pixel is hazier 
if it is further away from Clear Line. Therefore, if the 
slope angle of Clear Line is Θ , the HOT image can 
obtained by  Eq. (1). 
 
HOT = B1SinΘ- B3SinΘ     (1) 
 
where B1 and B3 are image pixel values in blue band and 
red band respectively.  
  
power spectrum is needed to be considered in the noise 
analysis. Therefore, according to the high frequency 
power differences between the source and haze removed 
images, their power RMSE can be used to measure the 
increased high frequency noise due to the problem of 
HOT image. Based on this point, we can use medium 
filters with various window sizes to generate a series of 
haze removed images. Then, if a medium filter with a 
suitable windows size is selected to filter the original 
HOT image, the high frequency power RMSE obtained 
from that specific haze removed images should be 
reasonably small.

Rice crop classification in Taiwan from time-series SPOT data using empirical 
mode decomposition and statistical methods 
  
Chi-Farn Chen and Nguyen-Thanh Son 
 
Abstract : This study aims to investigate the feasibility of using time-series SPOT NDVI data for mapping double rice fields in 
Taiwan. The data was processed using empirical mode decomposition (EMD) and statistical methods (i.e., correlation analysis 
and sign-test statistics). Two study sites with different farming conditions situated in Taitung and Taoyuan counties were chosen 
for verification of the classification algorithm. The results showed that EMD performed a good filter at smoothing time-series 
SPOT NDVI data. The comparison results between the classification maps and the ground-truth maps indicated that the overall 
and Kappa coefficient for the Taitung study site were 93.7% and 0.84, while that for the Taoyuan were 90.8 and 0.74, 
respectively. 
Key words: time-series SPOT data, empirical mode decomposition (EMD), rice crop.
1. Introduction 
Rice is an important food crop in Taiwan. Rice crop 
monitoring is important for devising timely plans for 
ensuring food security. Rice cultivation in Taiwan 
should be examined each year as the government 
estimates the harvest (Chou et al. 2006). The SPOT data 
are found suitable for this monitoring purpose. In 
previous crop mapping studies, commonly-used noise 
filtering techniques, such as Fourier and wavelet 
transforms, were often applied prior to data analysis. 
One demerit of Fourier transform analysis of time-series 
data is that as the degree of nonlinearity in a time series 
increases, the decomposition often generates large sets 
of physically meaningless harmonics (Huang et al. 
1999). This matter can be overcome by wavelet 
transform. However, the demerit of wavelet transform is 
to determine a proper filter function prior to the 
decomposition (Li 2006). In this study, we used the 
empirical mode decomposition (EMD)  (Huang et al. 
1998) for noise filtering of the time-series SPOT NDVI 
data. A classification algorithm based on statistical 
analyses of filtered time-series data was developed to 
classify irrigated double-cropped rice fields in Taiwan.  
 
2. Study area  
Two study sites with different farming conditions 
located in Taitung and Taoyuan counties were selected 
for testing our classification algorithm. Rice cultivation 
in these two sites is dependent on irrigation availability. 
Double irrigated rice cropping using short-term varieties 
of rice (110 days) is commonly practiced. The first crop 
is grown from January to April, and the second crop is 
grown from May to September. 
 
 
 
 
 
3. Data  
The SPOT-2, -4, and -5 satellite data for 2005 were 
collected from the receiving station of the Center for 
Space and Remote Sensing Research, National Central 
University, Taiwan. A total of 75 images was acquired 
for the Taitung study site, and 69 for the Taoyuan study 
site. NDVI was used in this study. Two vector format 
rice crop maps of these study sites in 2005 (scale: 
1/5,000) were provided collected and used for accuracy 
assessments. 
 
4. Methods 
i) Data filtering, the EMD applied to the SPOT NDVI 
time-series data adaptively decomposed the signal into 
intrinsic mode functions (IMFs) and a residue (Huang et 
al. 1998). In this study, we developed the low-pass filter 
by summing the last two IMFs and the residue and 
applying the filter to our time-series data. 
ii) Image classification, double irrigated rice cropping 
system was classified through two steps: 1) selection of 
training patterns: training data were obtained from the 
filtered NDVI data. We used the 2005 rice crop map as a 
reference map for randomly extracting 100 rice pixels 
from the filtered data and then calculated the mean 
pattern for these sampling pixels. The correlation 
coefficients between the mean patterns with 100 
sampling pixels were computed. A histogram of 
correlation coefficients was then plotted. Based on this 
histogram, a correlation coefficient threshold was set to 
identify 95% of the extracted training pixels. The 
thresholds derived from the correlation analysis of 
sampling pixels for the Taitung and Taoyuan study sites 
were 0.88 and 0.79, respectively; and 2) rice crop 
classification: there were two main steps: i) correlation 
analyses with training patterns; and ii) sign-test statistics.
