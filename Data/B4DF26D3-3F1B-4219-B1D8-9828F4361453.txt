ABSTRACT
Because of the rapid developments in micro-electro-mechanical systems and
microfluidics such as microchannel heat sinks for electronic cooling, micro fluid pumps,
and microfluidic devices for chemical and biomedical analyses, etc., it is highly desirable
to understand the essentials of various fluid flows in microchannel. Electroosmosis has
been used extensively to move packets of fluid through lab-on-a-chip based
microsystems. Most of the previous studies pertinent to microchannel flows were
concerned with Newtonian fluids. However, microfluidic devices are usually used to
analyze biofluids which may not be treated as Newtonian fluids. Also, he flow rate
induced by electroosmotic force is usually small, and therefore even a small pressure
gradient applied along a microchannel may cause velocity distributions and
corresponding flow rates that deviate from the pure electroosmotic flow. The pressure
gradient may arise from several reasons, such as the presence of alternative pumping
mechanism, placement of mechanical value in the flow path, and the existence of
variations in the wall zeta potential.
Keeping these in mind, the objective of this investigation is to study non-Newtonian
fluid flow and heat transfer introduced by combined electroosmosis and pressure forces
in microchannels. The governing system of equations includes the electrical potential
field, flow field, and energy equations. Effect of Joule heating is taken into account in
the energy equation. Analytical solutions are presented for mixed flow of a Newtonian
fluid and for purely electroosmotic flow with specific values of non-Newtonian
behavioral behavior index, numerical solutions are generated by an accurate
finite-difference method because of the highly non-linear nature of the governing
differential equations. Representative results fore the electrical potential distribution
within the electric double layer, the fluid velocity and temperature profiles, the volume
flow rate, the friction factor, and the Nusselt number are presented for different values of
governing parameters.
Keywords: Microchannel; Non-Newtonian fluids; Electroosmosis; Joule Heating.

計畫結果自評
本計畫研究使用非牛頓流體作為工作流體，並討論在微流道中受壓力與電滲所
引發的熱流機制，重要結果包括速度分佈、溫度分佈以及紐塞爾賽等，是以更清楚
瞭解微流道中非牛頓流體之熱流機制。研究內容與原計畫相符，達成預期研究目標。
本研究讓吾等可更深入認識微流道中以動電力及壓力共同驅動之熱流行為，並提供
有助於微流體晶片設計及微全分析系統發展之參考，以期能對微流體學的發展上有
更大的幫助。研究成果經整理後適合發表於國際學術期刊。
