一、中文摘要: 
本計畫利用一年時間組裝建構一可線上檢測半導體晶片光學特性之調制光譜量測系統。此系統可
使用各種不同非破壞性光學量測方法，包括光子調制、電場調制、壓電調制、及表面光電壓光譜來檢
測新穎光電半導體材料及其奈米微細結構元件晶片，本期工作內容主要以上述光學量測方法檢測含銻
(antimony)之 GaAsSb 塊材及其量子井、II-VI 族 ZnCdMgSe 之特性。藉由比較各種不同調制方式之半
導體晶片光學檢測結果，並分析量測結果，可瞭解不同半導體材料其異質接面狀態及其奈米微細結構
和合金成分，以鑑定其界面品質及特性，藉此探討可能影響的因素。配合光學檢測結果及晶片成長條
件，掌握各種外加參數對元件品質及性能之影響，進而提昇元件的性能。並擬將此技術發展成為非接
觸及非破壞性元件構造與品質鑑定之有利工具。 
 
關鍵詞：調制光譜、表面光電壓、銻化物。 
 
二、英文摘要: 
    In this proposal, we build a modulation spectroscopy and surface photovoltage spectroscopy system to 
characterize the novel optoelectronic semiconductors materials wafer in one year. The optical characterization 
on novel optoelectronic semiconductors and their nanostructures such as III-V Sb containing bulk materials 
and their quantum well structure, II-VI ZnCdMgSe materials by using various modulation techniques 
including photomodulation, piezomodulation, and surface photovoltage spectroscopy were carried out in this 
work. The sharp, derivative like features will be fit and the origins of the various spectral features will be 
identified by comparing different modulation mechanism. The results of optical investigation can be used for 
optimizing the parameters of semiconductor wafer fabrication. 
 
Keywords：modulation spectroscopy、surface photovoltage spectroscopy、antimonide。

An optical characterization of two wurtzite ZnCdMgSe crystalline alloys grown by the modified high-pressure Bridgman method 
was carried out by temperature-dependent photoluminescence (PL) and contactless electroreflectance (CER) in the temperature 
range of 10–300 K and photoreflectance (PR) measurements between 300–400 K. Low temperature PL spectra of the investigated 
samples consisted of the excitonic line, the “edge-emission” due to radiative recombination of shallow donor-acceptor pairs and a 
broad band related to recombination through deep level defects. Three excitonic features, A, B, and C, in the vicinity of band edge 
were observed in the CER and PR spectra. The peak positions of band edge excitonic features in the PL spectra were shifted slightly 
toward lower energies as compared to the lowest corresponding transition energies of A exciton determined from CER and PR data. 
The increase in the CER-PL shift with the increasing of Mg content in the investigated crystals was explained by the rising of 
compositional disorder causing the smearing of the band edge energies. In addition, the parameters that describe the temperature 
dependence of the transition energies and broadening parameters of the band edge excitonic transitions were evaluated and 
discussed.

Experimental 
The deposition of RuO2 NCs on TiO2 NCs 
template was carried out in a home-made high 
vacuum RFMS system. The sputtering gun has a 
standard circular planar magnetron. The sputtering 
targets were Ru (99.95%) and Ti (99.95%) metal. 
The sputtering chamber was evacuated with a 
turbo-molecular pump and had a base pressure of 
~3×10−5 Pa. Reactive sputtering was carried out in 
a mixture of oxygen and argon. The sputtering 
parameters for TiO2 (RuO2) were: flux of oxygen 
and argon O2 : Ar = 10 : 10 sccm and 2 : 10 sccm 
corresponding to sputtering pressures of 14 and 8 
Pa, denoted as Pa-I and Pa-II, respectively (O2 : Ar 
= 10 : 10 sccm); RF power 230 W (50W); 
substrate temperature Ts as 400°C (200°C). 
A JEOL-JSM6500F FESEM was used to 
study the morphology of samples. Crystal 
structures were analyzed using a Rigaku 
D/Max-RC X-ray diffractometer equipped with 
Cu K radiation source and Ni filter. TEM images 
and SAED patterns were recorded to characterize 
the structure of the individual RuO2 V-shaped 
RuO2 NW by a Phillips Tecnai G2 F20 FE-TEM at 
working voltage of 200 kV.  
 
Results and discussion 
As shown in Fig. 1(a) and (b) are 30° perspective 
view and cross-sectional view FESEM images of 
TiO2 NCs deposited on SA(100) substrate under 
Pa-I. Fig. 1(c) shows XRD pattern of TiO2 NRs 
deposited on the SA(100) under Pa-I, where the 
two reflection peaks are identified to be from the 
R-TiO2(002) and SA(300). The FESEM images 
and XRD pattern of the TiO2 NRs grown on 
SA(100) under Pa-I reveal the uniquely single-directional growth of rutile phase TiO2 NRs along [001] 
with smooth tops. FESEM micrographs depicted in Fig. 1(d) and (e), respectively, are 30° 
perspective view and cross-sectional view images of RuO2 NCs deposited on top of TiO2 NRs. As can 
be seen in Fig. 1(d) and (e), the vertically aligned RuO2 nanotubes (NTs) are grown on top of R-TiO2 
NRs. The XRD pattern of RuO2 NTs grown on top of TiO2 NRs (see Fig. 1(f)) indicates that a single 
directional growth of RuO2(001) NTs has been deposited on the smooth top of TiO2 NRs. The close 
lattice constant and identical crystal structure with the c-direction growth mechanism are the main 
driving force for the formation of well-aligned RuO2 NTs on top of R-TiO2 NRs.

 Summary 
Well-aligned RuO2/R-TiO2 
heteronanostructures on SA(100) substrates has 
been deposited by RFMS under different 
conditions. The FESEM images and XRD patterns 
indicated that the growth of vertically aligned 
RuO2(001) NTs and twinned V-shaped RuO2(101) 
NWs on top of R-TiO2 nanorods under different 
sputtering pressures. TEM and SAED 
characterizations of the V-shaped RuO2 NWs 
showed that the NWs are crystalline RuO2 with 
twin planes of (101) and twin direction of [101] at 
the V-junction. The close lattice constant and 
identical crystal structure with the c-direction 
growth mechanism are the main driving force for 
the formation of well-aligned RuO2 NCs on top of 
R-TiO2 NRs.
