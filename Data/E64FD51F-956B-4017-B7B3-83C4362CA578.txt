Abstract 
Fabrication of a complicated microchannel system that is integrated with arrays of pressure 
sensors is presented. This channel can allow either gas or liquid flow through for basic 
micro-fluidic studies. This fabrication process developed is almost the reverse of the surface 
micromachining process usually used for the microchannel system. This allows the use of SU-8 
to fabricate, by lithography, the diaphragm and the cavities used for these pressure sensors, and 
the channel. The height for the diaphragm, the cavities and the channel can be readily varied from 
few to hundreds of microns by spin coat different thickness of SU-8 layer. This will allow much 
wider measurement range and flow conditions. The fabrication process has completely absence of 
diaphragm sitction. More detailed design and fabrication techniques developed, and the 
calibration of sensors for this channel system is presented. The pressure distributions measured 
along the channel are compared with the analysis.
