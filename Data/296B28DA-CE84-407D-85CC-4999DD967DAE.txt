Abstract 
 
 In this study we apply the Trebble-Bishnoi-Salim equations of state (TBS EOS) and a 
general four-parameter cubic EOS (C4EOS) with different approaches for obtaining the 
parameters to calculate the liquid- liquid equilibria of hydrocarbon-water mixtures over wide 
range of temperature and pressure. Incorporated with the traditional two-parameter van der 
Waals mixing rules the mutual solubilities of n-hexane/water, n-octane/water and 
n-decane/water are studied.  
 For pure compounds, the TBS EOS is more accurate than the C4EOS. Both of the 
four-parameter EOS improve the overall liquid-liquid equilibria correlation of 
hydrocarbon-water systems, especially the solubility of hydrocarbon in water. The water 
solubility in hydrocarbon can be correlated by TBS EOS and C4EOS with similar accuracy. 
However, the C4EOS EOS calculates the solubility of hydrocarbon in water more precisely. 
For n-hexane-water system, the C4EOS EOS can describe the phenomena of the minimum 
solubility of hydrocarbon in water but not the TBS EOS. 
 
 
Keywords: water, hydrocarbon, equation of state, liquid- liquid equilibrium
