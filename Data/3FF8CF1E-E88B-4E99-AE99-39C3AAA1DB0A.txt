This proposal devises a realization circulator-free technique of 
optical CDMA spectral-coding with fiber-Bragg-grating (FBG) -based 
codecs was proposed and experimentally demonstrated. Based on the 
modulation and detection principles that we have developed 
correlation subtractions of nearly orthogonal codes with balanced 
photodetectors, this proposed FBG encoder/decoder configuration is 
much different from those published before. We introduce the 
transmissive spectral characteristics of FBGs to construct our FBG 
encoder/decoder. Instead, it provides several important advantages. 
First, the cost is lowered due to our circulator-free architecture. 
Second, the round-trip time problem is resolved. Besides, we also can 
reduce the power loss issue slightly. The testbed consists of two active 
encoders and three decoders. Both matched and unmatched cases are 
demonstrated. In order to counter the multiple-access-interference 
(MAI) effects may caused by non-flattened optical sources, the spectral 
amplitude is sliced in the more flattened central region of broadband 
optical sources for partial compensation in our experiment. Our 
measurements verify that our proposed scheme is successful and 
practicable.
