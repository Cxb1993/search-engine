一、中文摘要 
本研究的目的在於建立一個擁有不同跟
高變化的 3D動態腳型資料庫並分析其腳型尺
寸的變化。本研究使用 3D 腳型掃描儀來量測
總共 270 位受測者（男女性各 135 位）的右腳
在不同腳跟高度時（0-cm,3-cm,6-cm,9-cm）的
腳型。利用軟體半自動辨識出 3D 腳型上的 6
個重要腳型標記點，並萃取出 10 個關鍵的 1D
腳型尺寸值。同時本研究以 0 cm 腳型做為正
規化的基準來分析 3-, 6-, 9-cm 的腳型尺寸，
並藉以分析這 10 個腳型尺寸值的變化情形及
其估算迴歸公式。本研究結果建立了 270 位受
測者在不同跟高下的 3D 動態腳型資料庫，萃
取並分析出各腳型尺寸值的變化與估算迴歸
公式，這些迴歸公式的 R2 大抵在 0.7 左右。
這些量測結果可以提供給傳統鞋傳師父或 3D
動態腳型模型研究做為設計參考。 
關鍵詞：3D 動態腳型資料庫，腳跟高度、腳
型尺寸變化 
Abstract 
The purpose of this study is to collect 3-D 
foot forms at various heel heights and extract the 
measurements to analyze their variations. A 3-D 
foot scanner was used. 135 males and 135 
females were recruited. For each subject, 4 scans 
of the right foot at 0-, 3-, 6- and 9-cm heel 
heights were taken. For each scan, 6 landmarks 
were identified and then 10 1-D measurements 
were extracted semi-automatically by Anthro3D 
software. In data analysis, foot form in 
measurements in different heel heights are then 
normalized by the ones in 0-cm heel height. 
Regression models are analyzed. A 3D foot 
database with 270 people is established. 
Regression models of the measurements under 4 
different heel heights are also analyzed. The R2s 
of these models are about 0.7. The results are 
useful for the estimation of the measurements 
changed in heel-raised position and for the 
validation to the dynamic foot models. 
Keywords: 3D dynamic foot form database, 
heel height, foot measurements variation

三、研究方法 
本研究以我國成人人口為抽樣母體，在兼
顧母體代表性的原則下依身高及體重分佈比
例抽樣，男女各 135 人。每位受測者的腳型外
觀上並沒有異常。 
本研究採用 3D腳型量測儀來進行不同跟
高的 3D 腳型量測。這部 3 腳型掃描儀配備有
4 個量測測頭（如圖 3）。此 3D 掃描儀安裝有
一塊強化玻璃位於下方測頭的上方處，量測時
受測者的腳型放置於這塊強化玻璃上方（量測
平面）。每次的腳型量測時間大約是 6 秒鐘。
同時，本研究亦同時設計了一組的腳跟墊輔具
與兩組架設在 3D腳型量測儀上的雷射指示裝
置，以協助維持受測者腳型的腳跟高度，並且
避免受測者的腳型因為腳踝關節角度變化而
影響量測結果。

本研究針對每位受測者的右腳在 4 種不
同腳跟高度的狀態下進行量測，這 4 種腳跟高
度分別是 0 cm，3 cm，6 cm，9 cm。首先量
測的是 0 cm 腳跟高度的狀態（0-cm）。受測者
坐在油壓式高度可調整座椅上，此時腳部自然
垂放而腳趾則觸碰到量測平面（強化玻璃）。
接著調整油壓式座椅的高度，直到腳跟完全平
放在量測平面上，再進行腳型量測（如圖 4）。
利用同樣的程序，本研究量測受測者在 3 
cm，6 cm，9 cm 的腳型。在調整座椅高度的
同時，腳部的荷重會自然落在足球關節和腳趾
上，此時分別將三種不同高度（3 cm，6 cm，
9 cm）的跟高量測器（heel-like gauge）暫時
置於受測者的腳跟下方。高度調整結束後，移
開跟高量測器來進行腳型量測。 
腳型量測結束後，本研究利用 Anthro3D
軟體以半自動的方式辨視出 6 個解剖標記點
和萃取出 10 個 1D 尺寸值，如圖 4 所示。。
由這 6 個解剖標記點可以萃取出 10 個 1D 尺
寸值，這些標記點的定義是參考 Webb 
Associates（1978）[16]以及 Du Bois 和 Du Bois
（1916）[4]來決定的。

Abstract—The 3-D foot scanning technology has become an 
important application in foot studies of large scale recently. 
Nevertheless 3-D foot forms scanned usually consist of many 
holes because of missing views and grazing angle views. These 
holes are significant drawbacks of 3-D foot anthropometry. To 
overcome this problem, this study is to demonstrate a method to 
fill holes using template matching method and extract the foot 
measurements from it as well. One 3-D foot form was developed 
without any holes as the 3-D foot template in this study, 
accompanying with two groups of points, 14 characteristic 
landmarks and 9 anatomical landmarks. Using template 
matching method, the 3-D foot template can be transformed into 
the 3-D foot form of the subject with characteristic landmarks on 
both of them correspondingly. After transformation, holes of the 
subjects’ 3-D foot form were filled, and concurrently the 
anatomical landmarks on the 3-D foot template would also be 
transferred onto it. The foot measurements could be extracted 
based on the anatomical landmarks as well. The results showed 
the template matching method is useful to fill the holes and to 
extract foot measurements of the 3-D foot form. The 
configuration of the groups of landmarks could be further 
studied. 
Keywords-3-D foot form; template matching method; foot 
measurement extraction 
I.  INTRODUCTION 
The 3-D foot scanning technology has become an important 
application in foot studies of large scale recently [1] [2][ 3][ 4]. 
It can measure the 3-D foot form precisely and quickly. 
Nevertheless 3-D foot forms scanned usually consist of many 
holes because of missing views and grazing angle views [5]. 
These holes are significant drawbacks of 3-D foot 
anthropometry, for example, extraction of 1-D of 
measurements, foot area, foot volume, and etc. 
Three methods are usually used to fill the holes of 3-D foot 
forms: plantar triangulation method, iterative closet points 
(ICP) [6], and template matching method [7]. The plantar 
triangulation method is to fill the holes with plantar 
triangulation meshes by manual effort in computer. The ICP 
Algorithm was developed by Besl and McKay [6] and is 
usually used to register two given point sets in a common 
coordinate system. The algorithm calculates iteratively the 
registration. In each iteration step, the algorithm selects the 
closest points as correspondences and calculates the 
transformation for minimizing some equations. Many 
variations were developed to improve its efficiency, for 
example, Pajdla and Gool proposed ICRP (Iterative Closest 
Reciprocal Point) [8], Rusinkiewicz and Levoy [9] made some 
changes from the original ICP method, and Chetverikov et al. 
proposed TrICP (Trimmed Iterative Closest Point) [10]. Plantar 
triangulation method and ICP method is time-consuming and 
exhibit significant distortion. 
Then the template matching method was proposed by Allen 
et al. [7] to fix the 3-D anthropometrical database in CAESAR 
project (Civilian American and European Surface 
Anthropometry Resource). It employs affine transformation 
algorithms to project all the characteristic points of the 
template onto the control points of the original data, and then 
all the meshes on the template as well. As a result, the holes on 
the original data were filled. This method is much preferable 
than the planar triangulation method and ICP method both in 
speed and fidelity. 
The most important application of hole-filled 3-D foot 
forms is to extract foot measurements from them. And the foot 
measurements are usually extracted in base of anatomical 
landmarks of foot form. Many researches focused on the 
identification of the anatomical landmarks [11][12][13][14]. 
Generally, these methods could identify the anatomical 
landmarks on foot form precisely, but they are also usually 
accompanying with time-consuming effort. For all these, the 
purpose of this study is to demonstrate a method to fill holes 
using template matching method and extract the foot 
measurements from it as well. 
II. METHOD 
An existing 3-D anthropometrical database was used to 
select an initial foot template in this study [14]. A 
representative female subject was selected for the initial foot 
template. Two groups of landmarks were set on it. Finally, 10 
females beyond the database were recruited and the foot 
measurements were extracted from their foot form by template 
matching method and by palpation to validate this method.

D. Validation 
10 female subjects conforming to M-Medium were 
recruited to validate hole-filling and measurement extraction by 
the template matching function. Their right feet were scanned 
by 3-D foot scanner and were applied by template matching 
method to fill the holes and extract the foot measurements. 11 
foot measurements resulting from 3D foot forms were 
compared to those extracted by expert’s palpation on the feet of 
these 10 female subjects to see whether they were correct or 
not. 
III. RESULTS 
One of the resulting 3-D foot forms whose holes are filled 
by the template matching method is shown as Fig. 5. It can be 
seen that the holes are filled well. The comparison of the 11 
foot measurements extracting from the anatomical landmarks 
of the foot template and those by expert’s palpation is listed as 
Table II. The results show that there is no statistically 
significant difference between two values extracted by 
template matching method and palpation in each foot 
measurement. 
IV. DISCUSSION AND CONCLUSION 
A useful method to fill the holes and to extract foot 
measurements of the 3-D foot form concurrently were 
proposed in this study. The validation results showed that there 
was no statistically significant difference between two values 
extracted by template matching method and palpation in each 
foot measurement. 
One configuration of 14 characteristic landmarks and 9 
anatomical landmarks was set in the foot template in this study, 
and was validated to be able to fill holes and extract foot 
measurement. It would seem not to be time efficient that 14 
characteristic landmarks were used to identify 9 anatomical 
landmarks. The efficiency could be easily increased by adding 
the number of anatomical landmarks, but, essentially this is the 
issue of the compromise between number of characteristic 
landmarks and the correctness of the anatomical landmarks on 
the subjects’ foot forms. The further work would be on the 
optimization of the configuration of the characteristic 
landmarks.

技術說明
(中文) 本研究的目的在於建立一個擁有不同跟高變化的3D動態腳型資料庫並分析其腳型
尺寸的變化。本研究使用3D腳型掃描儀來量測總共270位受測者（男女性各135
位）的右腳在不同腳跟高度時（0-cm,3-cm,6-cm,9-cm）的腳型。利用軟體半自動
辨識出3D腳型上的6個重要腳型標記點，並萃取出10個關鍵的1D腳型尺寸值。同
時本研究以0 cm腳型做為正規化的基準來分析3-, 6-, 9-cm的腳型尺寸，並藉以
分析這10個腳型尺寸值的變化情形及其估算迴歸公式。本研究結果建立了270位
受測者在不同跟高下的3D動態腳型資料庫，萃取並分析出各腳型尺寸值的變化與
估算迴歸公式，這些迴歸公式的R2大抵在0.7左右。這些量測結果可以提供給傳
統鞋傳師父或3D動態腳型模型研究做為設計參考。
(英文) The purpose of this study is to collect 3-D foot forms at various heel heights and extract 
the measurements to analyze their variations. A 3-D foot scanner was used. 135 males 
and 135 females were recruited. For each subject, 4 scans of the right foot at 0-, 3-, 6- and 
9-cm heel heights were taken. For each scan, 6 landmarks were identified and then 10 1-
D measurements were extracted semi-automatically by Anthro3D software. In data 
analysis, foot form in measurements in different heel heights are then normalized by the 
ones in 0-cm heel height. Regression models are analyzed. A 3D foot database with 270 
people is established. Regression models of the measurements under 4 different heel 
heights are also analyzed. The R2s of these models are about 0.7. The results are useful 
for the estimation of the measurements changed in heel-raised position and for the 
validation to the dynamic foot models.
