Correlation between Direct Methanol Alkaline Fuel Cell Performance and 
Characteristics of Potassium Hydroxide-doped Polyvinyl Alcohol-Fumed Silica 
Electrolyte  
 
Shingjiang Jessie Lue*, Wei-Ting Wang, K.P.O Mahesh, Wen-Han Pan 
 
Department of Chemical and Materials Engineering 
Chang Gung University, Kei-shan,Taoyuan 333, Taiwan 
 
Abstract 
 
This study reports on the cell performance of direct methanol alkaline fuel cells (DMAFCs) employing 
hydroxide conducting membranes. The membrane electrode assemblies (MEA) consisted of carbon-supported 
platinum/ruthenium for the anode, platinum for the cathode, and potassium hydroxide (KOH)-doped 
poly(vinyl alcohol)-fumed silica (PVA-FS) nano-composites as the electrolytes. The maximum power density 
and open-circuit voltage was monitored for DMAFC’s at various operating conditions. The effects of 
nano-filler loading, KOH concentration, methanol/KOH ratio and operating temperature on the cell 
performance are discussed and correlated with the electrolyte characteristics. The performance was higher 
than previous results available in the literature at comparable operating conditions for DMAFCs. 
 
Keywords: Direct methanol alkaline fuel cell (DMAFC); Nano-composites; Cell performance; Methanol 
permeability; Conductivity 
 
1. Introduction 
There has been increasing interest in direct methanol fuel cells (DMFCs) as alternative, portable, 
power-converting sources [1,2]. The proton-exchange membrane used in hydrogen fuel cells can be employed 
in DMFCs, but it suffers from methanol crossover through the electrolytes. This leads to fuel loss, unwanted 
methanol oxidation potential and poisoning by CO formation on the Pt cathode catalyst. In our previous report 
[3], we showed that fumed silica (FS)-modified polyvinyl alcohol (PVA) composites exhibit higher thermal 
stability and lower crystallinity than the pristine polymer (Table 1). The resulting enlarged free volume [4] 
may increase water and ion transport in the PVA/FS composite and increase the ionic conductivity. The scope 
of the present work is to prepare KOH-doped PVA/FS electrolytes for direct methanol alkaline fuel cells 
(DMAFC). The membrane characteristics and fuel cell performance are reported as functions of the operating 
variables.

4. Conclusion 
The cell performance of the PVA/FS/KOH electrolyte was higher than literature results at comparable 
operating conditions for DMAFCs. The increase was due to the two-fold benefits from the increased 
conductivity and supressed methanol cross-over by FS incorporation. The PVA/CNT/KOH electrolyte had a 
higher conductivity and its DMAFC rendered a high power density than the PVA/KOH counterpart. This 
study shows that enhanced DMAFC performance can be achieved by appropriate nano-composite designs.
