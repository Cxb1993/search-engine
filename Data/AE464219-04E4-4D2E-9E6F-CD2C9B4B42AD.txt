一、中文摘要 
近年來發展迅速的創新構思問題解決法
(TRIZ)為一系統化創新性問題解決理論，其技術
方法中的矛盾矩陣廣泛使用在取捨問題
(trade-off)解決，能協助發明者快速找到更佳的
研發創新思考途徑。本研究計畫第一年使用
1000筆中華民國綠色節能專利為標的，先採用
文字探勘中之自動斷詞 (auto-Tag system)將專
利資料文句斷成數個字詞並標示詞性，並以人
工方式將部分字詞加以適度標籤，而關鍵字與
主要特徵擷取則採用卡方統計與相關係數作為
字詞篩選排序之準則，且計算其字詞頻率(TF)、
字詞頻率反轉文件頻率(TFIDF)與權重-字詞頻
率反轉文件頻率(w-TFIDF)值，將中華民國綠色
節能專利和TRIZ創新原則加以分類關聯，並期
以多標籤、卡方統計來改善國際專利分類、傳
統文字探勘之不適性，使分類結果更能符合
TRIZ研發創新者之應用，即為結合文字探勘技
術與TRIZ進行有效率之中華民國綠色節能專利
分析，也達到令滿意的成果。 
   本研究計畫第二年將使用支持向量機(SVM)
與最近鄰居法(KNN)為演算法進行專利分類，後
藉由準確率、精準度、回想率及 F 值四個評估
指標，比較 SVM 與 KNN 系統之分類結果與績
效何者較佳，較優的分類系統做為後續 TRIZ 專
利檢索機制之自動專利分類工具。接著建立一
TRIZ 專利檢索機制，TRIZ 矛盾矩陣為基礎，將
1000 筆綠色節能產業相關專利依 TRIZ40 創新
原則做自動分類、及建立與整合 39TRIZ 工程參
數間之關聯，分析問題找出其中取捨矛盾後，
透過整合 TRIZ 39 工程參數的選擇對應 TRIZ 創
新原則，檢索出高關聯綠色節能產業專利，並
製作一簡易之 TRIZ 40 創新原則之高關聯性綠
色節能產業專利檢索操作介面，可直接提供綠
色節能產業 TRIZ 使用者或研發者更具體的思
考捷徑，亦為本研究計畫最主要之目的與貢獻。 
關鍵詞：創新構思問題解決法(TRIZ)；40 創新

Abstract 
In recent years, TRIZ have been developed 
rapidly and known as a useful tool to solve the 
contradictions on engineering problems. The 
aim of this research is to map R.O.C green & 
energy conservation patents into TRIZ 
applications by using text mining technique for 
further innovative product concepts and 
solutions of conflicts. In the first year, using 
on-line auto-tag system provided by Academia 
Sinica to break every sentence in a document into 
several keywords and label these keywords 
manually. Calculating text frequency (TF) and 
inverse document frequency (IDF) in the 
corresponding document. Secondly, chi-square 
statistics and correlation coefficient approaches 
are used to select and sort word features which 
are highly correlated to 40 TRIZ innovative 
principles, the results are satisfactory. Then, 
calculating the TFIDF and weight-TFIDF values 
for selected words, these values are inputs for 
further classifiers such as support vector machine 
(SVM) and K-Nearest neighbor classifier (KNN). 
Finally, SVM and KNN are implemented to 
evaluate the performances of 1000 green & 
energy conservation R.O.C patents mapping into 
40 TRIZ innovative principles. This research 
would like to significantly help R&D personnel 
to solve green & energy conservation trade-off 
contradictions by fast searching the correlation

利文獻的檢索，然而相同的技術主題，經由不
同的研究人員或發明者做資料檢索，由於專利
分類所涉及的經驗的累積及深層的知識，以國
際專利分類方法(IPC)來說，便多達六萬九千個
主次目，包括的技術內容繁複，故非專業人員
皆可能因為對專利分類邏輯與準則的不了解或
認知上的落差，檢索出與目標差異大的專利文
獻，故如何選擇適切的專利關鍵詞與專利分
類，達到專利文獻的準確度及回想率，已是研
究人員及發明者的主要議題。另外，和傳統發
明者形成對比，使用 TRIZ 做為創新研發工具的
發明者，不僅於自身領域中尋找已發明技術，
而且對在其他領域利用相同方法解決類似技術
矛盾(technical contradiction)之發明更感興趣，然
而國際專利分類方法尚不能滿足這些需求，也
促使本計畫意欲開啟 TRIZ、文字探勘與中華民
國綠色節能專利三者結合之新興研究議題，以
達成 TRIZ 使用者與創新研發者所需之綠色節
能產業專利分類與檢索機制。 
    本研究計畫第一年將使用 1000 筆中華民國
綠色節能專利為標的，先採用文字探勘中之自
動斷詞剖析系統(auto-Tag system)將專利資料文
句斷成數個字詞並標示詞性，並以人工方式將
部分字詞加以適度標籤(label)。而關鍵字與主要
特徵擷取則採用卡方統計 ( 2  statistics ，
Chi-square)與相關係數(correlation coefficient)作
為字詞篩選排序之準則，且計算其字詞頻率
(TF)、字詞頻率反轉文件頻率(TFIDF)與權重-字
詞頻率反轉文件頻率(w-TFIDF)值，將中華民國
綠色節能專利和 TRIZ 創新原則加以分類關
聯，並期以多標籤、卡方統計來改善國際專利
分類、傳統文字探勘之不適性，使分類結果更
能符合 TRIZ 研發創新者之應用，即為結合文字
探勘技術與 TRIZ 進行有效率之中華民國綠色
節能專利分析，目前本研究計畫第一年之執行
成果至此。 
三、研究方法 
創新問題解決理論(TRIZ)，源自蘇俄工程師
兼發明家 Genrich Altshuller (1926-1998) ，譽為
TRIZ 之父。將其原俄文：теории решения 
изобретательских задач 縮寫「ТРИЗ」， 翻譯
為「解決發明家任務的理論」；用英語標音可讀
為 Teoriya Resheniya Izobreatatelskikh Zadatch，
TRIZ；英文說法：Theory of Inventive Problem 
Solving，TIPS）。TRIZ 系統化創新流程，即透
過某事物需要被改善的認知時，以 TRIZ 為工具
或方法進行問題之最適解決方法探索，其主要
由定義問題、選擇工具、解決問題、評估四個
步驟來進行。而在第二步驟中，TRIZ 提供 40
創新性原則、矛盾矩陣(Contradiction matrix)、
演化趨勢(Trends of technology evolution)、理想
最終結果(Ideal final result，IFR)等最常被使用的
工具。 
   如前所述，TRIZ 解決問題的工具很多，其
中以矛盾矩陣最常被應用在解決技術矛盾之取
捨(Trade-off)問題，亦為本研究主要研究對象，
矛 盾 矩 陣 由 39 工 程 參 數 (Parameter for 
engineering；見表一)以及 40 創新性原則(見表
二)兩個要素所構成。參數是物體(object)的特徵
(feature)，可以是物體的重量、長度、面積、或
體積等，物體可以是靜止物體也可以是移動中
物體。我們可以針對某個物體的某項特徵來加
以改善，但有時候某項特徵改善後，卻可能造
成另項特性的惡化，因而產生了不希望之結
果，所以針對某一結果有時某些特徵是為相互
矛盾(conflict)。了解對某項物體在某種的改善情
形下，那些特徵(參數)是相互矛盾的，是應用矛
盾矩陣作創新改善時很重要的輸入資料；也就
是要清楚想要改善的特徵(feature to improved)
為何，以及矛盾的效應(特徵)為何，創新性原則
為解決這類問題的最佳途徑，也是主要的解決
問題思考建議，更是創新研發之具體化方案。 
TRIZ 問題解決之思考模式；即將特定問題
透過 TRIZ 工具之協助，找出主要矛盾點，再對
應至標準的解決方案，最後依照這些推薦的方
案(如相似案例或相似專利)進一步思考出原問

(3)主要特徵擷取 
    在機器學習探討文件分類領域中，由於容
易產生相當高維度且稀疏之資料集，對分類之
效能及運算時間皆是相當大之考驗，因此有許
多討論學習效能之文件，期均著重於使用主要
特徵值，而非全部的特徵值，因此如何使用特
徵選取工具進行節錄出有效特徵便是此階段中
重要的目標，而近年來主要有下列公認較佳之
特徵擷取方法 [40][47-49]： 
(a) 文件頻率(document frequency，DF) 
    指一個字詞出現於文件中之個數，方法為
將全部類別中，所有字詞取出後統計其文件頻
率，字詞文件頻率低於門檻值時則刪除，反之
留下。主要觀念為 DF 低於門檻值時對該類別較
不具代表性且無影響力，也有可能為雜訊。對
於將字詞降低，此方法是最容易使用之技術，
同時於處理複雜運算之訓練文件時，DF 能表現
相當之效率。

5. 研究結論 
本研究利用自動斷詞方法將綠色產業 1000
筆中國民國專利文件轉為數個特徵字詞，並透過
卡方統計與相關係數特徵選擇方法，將專利文件
中與 40 TRIZ 創新原則具關聯性之有效字詞篩
選出來，以利後續支持向量機與最近鄰居法進行
專利自動分類工作，經由此自動分類流程及不同
實驗設計參數重覆實驗，整合歸納出下列研究結
論與貢獻： 
1. 本研究針對綠色產業相關之中華民國專利
進行 40 TRIZ 創新原則之自動專利分類研
究，各產業皆具其產業特性，專利自動分類
流程中所選擇之各創新原則代表特徵字詞
可能依產業不同而有不同，代表性字詞及字
詞涵義可能皆有些許差異，因此本研究整理
出綠色產業適用之字詞(附錄 C)，期許可幫
助未來對此領域有興趣之專家學者進行更
深入研究；或增加綠色產業相關領域專家之
介入，修正擴充字詞集使其更為完備。 
2. 近年專利創新有屬於創新層級二之專利量
逐漸增加的趨勢，也代表著適用於此種創新
之 40 TRIZ 創新原則之重要性也隨之上
升，但 TRIZ 創新性原則一直有著偏概念
性、敘述較為抽象的使用門檻，如能透過範
例、知識、專利等資源提供 TRIZ 創新原則
具體的應用方向，比起尚需理解並經由腦力
激盪之發想更具使用便利性。本研究透過綠

一、參加會議經過 
2011 International Conference on Modelling, Identification and Control於2011 6月26日至2日
在中國上海交通大學舉行。會議超過400名代表參加，三天的會議，全體代表對專TRIZ和四
階段品質機能展開的應用，進行了深入而廣泛的交流和探討，而各國學者豐富多彩的研究
思路與方法，對研究人員具有啓發與指導意義。 
二、與會心得 
TRIZ 之源由於蘇俄短語(Teorija rezhenija izobretatelskih zadach)，蘇俄學者 Genrich 
Saulovich Altshuller 發明，英文翻譯為:“Theory of Inventive Problem Solving”亦即 “創新構思
問題解决”，他發現很多種不同的工程系統與科技問題，具有共同之演化與趨勢，運用此特性

Abstract—This paper proposes a four-phase quality function deployment (QFD) involving TRIZ 
thinking strategy to enhance breakthrough capabilities for preventative and proactive design in 
product R&D process. Continuously developed ‘opportunities for breakthrough innovation’ are 
highlighted. In QFD1, identified customer needs are translated into technical specifications / design 
requirements. QFD2 is responsible for defining product structure, takes an initial DFA approach 
that provides a minimalist structure and drafts a rough schematic diagram that demonstrates visually 
the position of modules and their interactions.  Optimal design parameters for the in-house made 
components are planned in the QFD3.  QFD4 considers problems that may result from all 
manufacturing process parameters and then delivers recommendations for quality control and site 
supervision that reflect appropriately differing levels of stringency. Leveraging the introduction and 
execution of this four phase QFD, we can quickly and efficiently certify whether design changes or 
innovative product concepts meet customer specifications, target quality level and cost. In addition, 
this paper pinpoints the areas the TRIZ can be used to propose innovative methods for problem 
solving.  As contradictory requirements appeared for the system, TRIZ methodology helps 
decrease negative effects and improves performance of existing design.

II. METHODOLOGY  
2.1.  Identify customer needs and their relative importance
Purposive discussions with key current and potential 
customers provide information that may be recorded and 
organized into useful data.  Traditional approaches typically 
see summarized results of market and/or business interviews 
forwarded to a project team for action.  However, this study 
highlights the significant benefits to be gained by including 
project team sales and R&D staff members in the interview / 
data collection process.  On the one hand, such staff can help 
organize and classify information to maximize accuracy and 
completeness.  On the other, they can help streamline 
questions asked to customers and capture the true intent in 
responses.  Roughly 80% of said benefits are attributed to the 
former (listening and organizing data), while 10% each are 
derived from question streamlining and confirming response 
intent.  Professional staff can listen closely to responses and 
ensure proper classification as well as observe interviewee 
attitudes and non-verbal communication, which, together, can 
convey interviewees’ true attitudes in 80% or more of cases.  
As such, in addition to defining customer needs more 
accurately, the interview process is able to explore potential 
needs (i.e., needs a customer is unable to express clearly).  
Potential needs represent opportunities for innovation because, 
whereas competitors must inevitably come to a clear 
understanding of customer needs, they will not necessarily be 
able to uncover potential needs. 
A series of 12 to 15 interviews should be able to capture 
about 90% of customer needs (including potential needs).  In 
order to capture the remaining 10%, one needs to conduct 
hidden observations of consumption characteristics of 
ultimate end users (consumers).  This 10% represents 
potential needs and, as such, presents opportunities for 
breakthrough innovation. The company conducting business

customer needs in order to maintain effective competitiveness

DFM can reduce overall per-unit costs, which include 
costs of materials (outsourced / in-house), assembly and 
administration (indirect costs).  Fixed and administrative costs 
should be distributed across all products.  The Formosa 
Plastics Corporation (FPC), one of Taiwan’s most important 
and successful manufacturers, places significant emphasis on 
internal systems designed to control itemized costs, which 
allow them to remain profitable even during times of 
economic downturn.  It is relatively common for 
manufacturers to use multiples of 1.15~1.20 of materials costs 
to calculate overall cost of production.  If a product is 
manufactured in regular, high volumes (e.g., annual volumes 
in the 100 thousands or millions), the dilution affect achieved 
by distributing fixed costs across all products may still 
provide opportunities of profitability.  On the other hand, if 
products are highly customized and produced in small / 
medium lots (typically between 100 and 100 thousand), even 
when employing mass customization production strategies, 
many fixed costs remain tied up in the customization process.  
Therefore, total unit cost estimates face a significantly higher 
risk of financial loss. Thus, there is a distinct advantage to 
gaining control of itemized cost details in order to both 
stabilize quality and reduce costs (opportunity for 
breakthrough innovation).  In terms of DFM, in addition to 
using industry-accepted standards as much as possible when 
outsourcing modules / components, manufacturers should pay 
particular attention to decision-making details related to 
materials (especially high-value inputs) used in in-house 
manufacturing processes. 
Administrative costs accrue in many, often hidden ways, 
making accurate calculation of such difficult [12-13]. As 
inefficient or complex administrative mechanisms have been 
known to raise related costs up to 40~50% of total unit 
production costs, the importance of administrative costs to the 
cost of production and competitiveness cannot be overlooked.  
Traditional approaches to calculating administrative costs 
frequently assume a fixed relationship with cost source; for 
example 10% outsourced materials costs + 70% in-house 
manufacturing costs. However, such formulae present large 
margins of error and prevent reduction of hidden costs, which 
ultimately elevate risk of financial loss.  Activity-based 
costing (ABC) represents a more precise approach to 
calculating costs.  Due to the highly complex nature of 
semiconductor fabrication, Taiwan Semiconductor 
Manufacturing Corporation (TSMC), the world’s largest 
manufacturer of semiconductors, employs ABC to consider 
different cost bases on an activity by activity basis (level of 
complexity).  For example, if a production process covers 10 
steps, relevant administrative costs (water / utilities, materials 
/ inventory, HR management, supplier management, shop 
floor control, etc.) are considered for each step separately.  
Thus, the final calculation for total cost should be more 
accurate, and it should be easier to identify / improve hidden 
costs involving wasted resources.

III. CONCLUSION 
Before target specifications and costs are met, applying 
the four-phase QFD and DFM/A described in this paper can 
assist manufacturers to understand the various factors related 
to quality characteristics, product structure, manufacturability 
and producibility in order to reduce overall manufacturing 
costs and stabilize quality.  Such represents a front-end 
preventive design approach.  During this stage, methods by 
which key problems will be resolved must be integrated into 
the product concept.  Using TRIZ to propose innovative 
methods (integration of QFD1~4, DFM/A and TRIZ) to 
resolve such problems, we may anticipate the end result of the 
product development process will be both innovative and 
successful.  Engineering analysis simulations, theoretical 
formulae, experience and prototypes may then be employed to 
verify the feasibility of all problem resolution-related target 
specifications.  In the event that a resolution approach, while 
able to achieve target specifications for a particular critical 
problem, is nonetheless expensive with little hope of 
significantly reducing costs, a possible approach is to look at 
reducing costs elsewhere in order to achieve the overall cost 
target.  If not possible, consideration should then be given to 
request the R&D committee or customer (for those in the 
ODM / OEM business) to permit a relaxation of target 
specifications.  However, this last approach should be used 
only as a last resort. We would like to stress again that, the 
concept development described here represents a group effort 
across departments supported by regular discussion and 
general agreement, with customers and key suppliers involved 
as necessary.  Such is essential to ensuring that problems are 
given comprehensive consideration.  Expanding discussions 
beyond a tight circle of project managers and other individuals 
directly involved in development work will help ensure all 
links are considered and avoid the need for in-process 
reengineering to resolve oversights.

VI. CONCLUSION 
 
QFD and TRIZ synergy had been known as an innovative 
and break-through scheme to solve engineering conflicts in 
recent years. However, not like engineering, a simple, fast, 
feasible, and effective conflict-solving skill is more 
welcomed by the design management area. Defining the 
problem can be a breakthrough innovation for more specific 
follow-ups of the TRIZ application to give priority to the 
prevention of certain designs.  In the conceptual stage of the 
development, the traditional development process to save 
about 33% of the time, this is the development time of the 
significant breakthroughs in innovative opportunities for the 
medical care bed. The designers shall study QFD and TRIZ 
synergy for savings of about 40% of the time, reduce product 
development time and accelerate the time to market time, 
increasing market competitiveness. Future study suggests that, 
using the QFD and TRIZ skills to integrate the sensor system 
for the intelligent body-turning, back-petting medical care 
bed can reduce the cause of pressure sores.   
From the patient’s perspective, under greater discomfort 
of excess warmth, humidity, or greater skin pressure, the bed 
would activate automatically to perform body-turning and 
back-petting heat ventilation functions. And under a more 
comfortable setting for tranquil resting, it allows the patient to 
rest comfortably without being disturbed by the body-turning, 
back-petting timer movements, bringing greater comfort and 
convenience to the patient.

Abstract－ The aim of this paper is to show how to implement 
TRIZ to resolve conflicts in planning phase of project 
management, using smart phone project as an example. TRIZ 
has been well-known as a creative and innovative thinking 
theory in solving engineering and technology contradictions in 
the last two decades. However, few studies and practical usage 
were proposed in management area. Six conflicts occurring in 
project planning stages including schedule, budget, risk, 
procurement, manufacturing, and communication ones at 
smart phone R&D process are discussed to demonstrate the 
ideas guided by TRIZ features, 40 innovative principles, and 
contradiction matrix. The results show that TRIZ provides 
quick and effective alternatives to resolve the conflicts in 
planning stage of project management. In this manner, huge 
effort and cost can be saved in further project execution stage. 
The quality of project management can be ensured as well to 
achieve desired project goal.

I.  INTRODUCTION 
In practical, project management (PM) plays an 
important role for new product development (NPD) process. 
Poor defining, planning, executing, and delivering in PM 
usually result in delay and over-budgeted project. In general, 
the less definition is, the more uncertainties occur in 
planning phase. However, uncertainty can come in different 
forms and is unavoidable for all aspects of projects [1-2]. 
Therefore, planning phase should be given more detailed

procurement, bidding, quality, communication, 
configuration, system, manufacturing, training, and safety 
areas to ease the impact of uncertainties and ensure smooth 
further execution. Many studies had addressed the critical 
factors for project success, no matter in local [3-5] or 
international environment [6-7]. They highlighted the key 
activities that require more attentions and effort to achieve 
successful projects. On the contrast, numerous researches 
pointed out failure causes [8] and conflicts in procurement 
[9-11], culture [12], human resource [13], stakeholders’ 
confidence [14], resource allocation [15], and schedule 
problems under constrained resource [16], etc. in PM. 
Researches 9-16 provided specific skills or methodologies 
to solve certain conflicts in PM, the outcomes were 
impressive and satisfactory. However, there has been very 
little study concerning one single approach to solve most of 
conflicts in PM.

In this section, a new smart phone project are planned in

approaches in 6 detailed planning, including schedule, 
budget, risk, procurement, manufacturing, and 
communication ones. Although many creative thinking are 
quickly inspired by TRIZ 39 engineering features, 39×39

in each planning is presented in this paper to save the effort 
spent in PM.

IV.  CONCLUSION 
 
As surveyed, 40% R&D projects will fail under the 
restricted resource, limited budget, unpredictable risk and 
time deadline. Intensive conflicts will occur in PM 
execution phase if the conflicts can not be solved or avoided 
in advance at PM planning phase. That may be the major 
reason to result in project failure. TRIZ has been known as 
an innovative and break-through scheme to solve 
engineering conflicts. However, not like engineering, a 
simple, fast, feasible, and effective conflict-solving skill is 
more welcomed by management area. In this study, fast, 
feasible and effective TRIZ-based approaches to solve 
conflicts in smart-phone R&D PM are proposed in project 
schedule, budget, risk, procurement, manufacturing, and 
communication planning stages. This study is the first real 
case study to implement TRIZ into PM area as well. Notice 
that although a lot of solutions inspired by TRIZ are created, 
but the simplest and most feasible approaches are proposed 
in this paper. TRIZ is verified to be helpful in offering better 
performance but avoiding worsen effects concerning 
management strategy or tactic, it is also feasible and 
effective in PM area based on the results proposed by this 
practical study. A well organized and less conflict PM 
tactics could create huge cost saving or profit for long term 
running.
