4.1 Interbay雙載量 OHS承載作業程序 
本研究中 OHS 在內圈以順時針方向與在外圈

1. 車子全空時：車子必定執行載取的任務，依先

1. 最早到達系統：無人搬運車在完成卸載作業
後，系統會分別去檢查各個工作站輸出端暫存
區內所有系統的到達時間，選出最早到達系統

3. 最長在等候線等候時間：OHT無人搬運車依據

最長等候時間的工作站做為下一個搬運車到
達的工作站，依據這個法則可使工件在工作站
的等待時間減小，避免工件一直停留在工作站
中不被揀取加工。 
4. 最少剩餘加工時間：OHT無人搬運車依據工作
站輸出端暫存區的工件最少剩餘加工時間來
決定派車決策，無人搬運車會選擇工件剩於最
少剩餘加工時間的工作站做為下一個搬運車
到達的工作站，依據這個法則可使工件超出交
期的時間最小化，減少工廠因為延誤時間而造
成損失。 
5. 阻塞機台優先+最早到達系統：OHT無人搬運
車依據工作站輸出端暫存區的工件阻塞來決
定派車決策，無人搬運車會選擇輸出端工件已

響產能利用。 
 
6.2 Intrabay單載量 OHT系統之負載揀取法則 
負載揀取法則是指當 OHT 根據載取派車法則
或是運送派車法則分派到某個工作站，當 OHT在
該工作站要進行揀取工件的動作時，OHT 會根據
負載揀取法則在該工作站的出料暫存區中選擇出
符合條件之工件，然後揀取此一工件。本研究發展
出數種不同的負載揀取法則來搭派各種不同的派
車法則： 
 
1. 晶舟最早到達系統：當工件在某一工作站加工
完成後，工件會進入該站的輸出端暫存區，當
每個工件進入輸出端暫存區等候揀取時，系統
會去紀錄下每個工件進入輸出端暫存區的時
間，當 OHT 在某個工作站的輸出端暫存區進
行揀取作業時，系統會去自動檢視出該輸出端
暫存區上各個工件在此暫存區最早到達系時
間，再將此資訊傳達給 OHT，OHT 再依造所
給的資訊去揀取所挑選出在該輸出端暫存區
等候最久的工件，運用此法則可以避免某些工
件在暫存區中閒置過久，造成延遲的浪費。 
2. 晶舟最少剩餘作業數目：當 OHT 在某個工作
站的輸出端暫存區進行揀取作業時，系統會去
自動檢視出每個晶舟剩於的作業數目，再從中
挑選最少剩於加工數目的晶舟，此加工此方法
能提高產出率，進而提昇系統的生產效率。 
3. 晶舟離到期時間最小：OHT無人搬運車依據工
作站輸出端暫存區的工件到期時間來決定派
車決策，無人搬運車會選擇此輸出端工件剩於
最小到期時間的晶舟揀取它作為運輸對像，依
據這個法則可使工件超出交期的時間最小
化，減少工廠因為延誤時間而造成損失。 
4. 晶舟最少剩餘加工時間：OHT無人搬運車依據
工作站輸出端暫存區的工件最少剩餘加工時
間來決定派車決策，無人搬運車會選擇此輸出
端工件剩於加工時間最小的晶舟揀取它作為
運輸對像，依據這個法則可使產出量提升工件
超出交期的時間最小化，減少工廠因為延誤時
間而造成損失。

z 工件產出量：離目的地最近法則之所以在工件
產出量的績效最好其主要原因：軌道佈置為單
方向，OHS只能在軌道上以單方向來運行，故
只要揀取離下一工作站最近的工件，可以大量
的減少工件在 OHS 的被運送時間。B.一旦工
件在 OHS上的時間縮短了，則 OHS有更多的
時間快速的運送各工件往來於各加工中心
間，相對的使工件的移動效果增加（即做對的
事情）相形之下產出數量也會增加。 
z 提早工件之平均提早完成時間：離目的地最近
法則最好其主要原因：以運送距離取勝。 
z 延遲工件之平均延遲完成時間：延遲工件之平
均延遲完成時間其延遲時間最少的是相同目
的地法則，延遲時間最多為離目的地最近法
則，其原因是此晶圓廠的軌道佈置為單方向，
OHS只能在軌道上以單方向來運行，故加工區
域的位置會影響到晶舟停在加工中心的時
間，也因此晶舟待在此區域的時間就被拉長
了，晶舟的延遲時間反而增加。  
z 總平均 Lateness：總平均延誤時間最小為相同
目的地法則等以時間為考量的法則，總平均延
誤時間最大的子集為離目的地最近法則，當一
個系統很關心每一個工件是否準時交貨，或是
否能提早交貨，那以時間為考量的七個法則是
值得我們使用的，離目地的最近法則雖然會有
較多的總平均延誤時間，而不像那些以時間為
考量的法則來得理想，但控制系統的 WIP 在
先前所提的 100之下亦能提早交貨，故此法則
亦值得吾人所採用。

1. Introduction 
Semiconductor manufacturing (SM) is an important aspect of the manufacturing industry in 
Taiwan, as its total production rate is ranked fourth in the world. In recent years, Taiwanese SM 
companies have begun to establish many 300-mm fabs. In early 1996, the International 300 mm 
Initiative (I300I) started developing a vision for 300 mm factories (SEMATECH, 2000); its initial 
factory integration vision was expressed in the original I300I Factory Guidelines. The general layout 
envisioned equipment being located in several bays that stem from a central aisle. A storage buffer 
(stocker) resided at the junction of the main aisle and each bay. Each bay also had its own central 
aisle with space for equipment on either side of the aisle. SM is a highly sophisticated operation with 
various processes to be performed, and SM factories typically have reentrant flow to deal with the 
increased number of process steps. With the limited land availability in Taiwan, large footprints not 
only pose constraints for the facility designer but present challenges in the design of the automatic 
material handling system (AMHS). In response to such challenges, it has become necessary to 
eliminate the manual handling of the wafers. Yet optimizing the AMHS is most effective when done 
in conjunction with the design of the fabrication facility’s (fab) layout. Therefore, the layout of the 
fab and the design of the AMHS are critical tasks in the early planning phase. As indicated in Peters 
and Yang (1997), most new fabs use an overhead monorail system for moving material between bays. 
This type of AMHS is usually designed with a spine or perimeter type of configuration, which forms 
a material flow loop within the facility. An AMHS with a spine configuration is the most common. 
The main purpose of having an AMHS with spine configuration is to improve the performance of the 
fabs by reducing cycle time and wafer damage during transportation, as well as increasing throughput. 
Therefore, the facility layout and how the AMHS is incorporated in it are critical factors in designing 
an efficient fab. According to J. T. Lin et al. (2001), there are two types of AMHS in wafer fabs. One 
is the interbay system, which transports cassettes or boxes of wafers between process bays, and the 
other is the intrabay system, which transports cassettes or boxes of wafers within one process bay 
(also called tool-to-tool AMHS). Research done by Castillo and Peters (2004) points out that a 
common problem in multi-bay manufacturing facility layouts is that interbay material handling costs 
dominate intrabay material handling costs. Thus, at the facility layout design stage, efficiency is 
measured in terms of interbay material movement. 
A common approach to modeling facility layout problems is to look at them as quadratic 
assignment problems (QAPs), known as NP-complete. When the number of departments is more than 
15, it is not easy to obtain the optimal solution with short computations. For this reason, most 
approaches resort to heuristic algorithms to obtain near-optimal solutions.  The purpose of this paper 
is to use an approach that incorporates heuristic algorithms and a mathematical programming model 
to improve the current layout of fabs and their AMHS. It is hoped that an optimal solution with good 
quality can be obtained with less computing time. In this study, a multi-bay layout with central aisle 
design methodology for SM factories will be proposed, along with three main objectives to be 
achieved. The first objective is to determine the relative positions of bays by analyzing the flow 
sequences of various products. Since the flow sequence is not unique, multiple layouts can be 
generated and evaluated. The second objective is to develop a hybrid approach using heuristic 
algorithms and mathematical models that can solve a facility layout design problem. The last 
objective is to minimize the total flow distance.

4.1.1 Stage 1- FLA Method 
This paper’s focus is on investigating the problem of bay positioning on a bidirectional loop 
flow path. Ho & Moodie (1998) modified several FLA methods proposed by Hollier (1963) and 
proposed two new methods of their own. Here we adopt a method proposed by them. The main 
objective of this method is to maximize the total in-sequence movements specifically designed in a 
bidirectional flow line. It has been proven to be particularly efficient at minimizing the flow distance 
in a bidirectional loop. The construction rules of this method are listed as follows.   
Step 1: The total flow interactions between every pair of bays are calculated. 
Step 2: Among the bay pairs, select and connect a pair of bays that has the largest amount of flow 
interactions to generate a semiconstructed flow line. If more than one pair of bays is qualified, 
arbitrarily select one pair.  
Step 3: Find a pair of candidate bays that has the largest number of flow interactions and does not 
belong to the same semiconstructed flow line. A bay is candidate if it is not connected to any 
 8 
In this example, we adopt the FLA method proposed by Ho and Moodie (1998) to determine the 
initial flow line design. The sequence of each bay in the initial flow design of loop is Bay 2-Bay 
3-Bay 1-Bay 4-Bay 5. At this point, SA is applied to improve the initial flow line design. The 
sequence of each bay is changed to the following: Bay 2-Bay 3-Bay 1-Bay 5-Bay 4. 
 
4.1.2 Stage 2- Simulated Annealing (SA) 
At this stage, SA is used to improve the solution obtained in the first phase. SA is an 
optimization technique that allows non-improving moves in the search process. SA was first proposed 
by Metropolis et al. (1953) and has received a considerable amount of attention since then. To 
implement SA in this stage, the solution obtained from FLA is used as the initial solution. Two bays 
are then randomly selected and their positions are exchanged to obtain a new solution. To apply SA, 
an evaluation criterion, a neighboring solution generation method, and a temperature reduction 
strategy are needed. In addition, the values of several parameters need to be defined before starting, 
such as Imax (the maximum number of iterations), Ti (the initial temperature) and Tf (the freezing 
temperature). For a detailed derivation of these value ranges for Ti , Tf  and temperature reduction 
strategy, please see Ho and Moodie (1998). 
 
4.2  Phase II: Adjust the layout of bays 
After the sequence of bays along the loop has been determined, a method is used to adjust the 
layout of bays into a double-row configuration with a central aisle. This method is to find two pairs 
of neighboring bays (or two segments) from the original loop, and then calculate additional flow 
distance to divide the loop into a double-row configuration with a central aisle. In this configuration, 
bays can be placed in the upper and lower row, but no bay can be placed on the right or left end of the 
aisle. The MSDR between bays in the same row will be the same as original specified distance; 
however, for the two pairs of bays placed on both ends of the aisle the distance will be stretched some. 
This stretched distance is the segment to connect upper and lower rows. Once the relative positions of 
all bays are determined and placed in these two rows, there will naturally be a difference in the total 
length of each row. Therefore, in order to make the length of the two rows identical, the difference in
