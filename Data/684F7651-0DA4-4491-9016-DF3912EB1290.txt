Density, viscosity, refractive index, surface tension, 
and electrical conductivity of various salt (sodium 
sulfate, sodium acetate, sodium nitrate, sodium 
carbonate, potassium sulfate, potassium nitrate, 
ammonium chloride, and ammonium sulfate) aqueous 
solutions up to saturation were measured at T = 
(298.15, 313.15, 328.15) K. .  Densities were 
measured with an Anton Paar DMA-5000 
vibrating-tube density meter with a stated accuracy of 
5×10-6 g⋅cm-3, which was thermostatically controlled 
to within ±0.01 K.  Calibration was performed 
periodically at atmospheric pressure, in accordance 
with specifications, using double-distilled water and 
dry air.  The uncertainty of the density data was 
estimated to be less than ±3×10-5 g⋅cm-3. 
The viscosities were determined with an Anton Paar 
automated microviscometer (AMVn).  The AMVn 
uses the rolling ball principle, where a steel ball rolls 
down inside an inclined, sample filled glass capillary 
with an inner diameter of 1.6 mm.  The viscosity of the 
test fluid was determined by measuring the rolling 
time of the steel ball with a diameter of 1.5 mm.  The 
accuracy of the flow-time measurement in the range (0 
to 250) s is less than 0.002 s.  The temperature was 
maintained by the Peltier effect with accuracy within 
0.05 K.  Calibration was performed periodically under 
atmospheric pressure, in accordance with 
specifications, using pure water.  The uncertainty of 
the viscosity data was estimated to less than ±0.8%. 
Refractive indices, nD, were measured with an 
automatic Anton Paar RXA-156 refractometer, which 
works with the wavelength (589 nm) corresponding to 
the D-line of sodium.  The applied temperature range 
of the refractometer is from 283.15 K to 343.15 K with 
accuracy of 0.03 K.  Calibration was performed 
periodically under atmospheric pressure, in 
accordance with specifications, using pure water and 
dry air.  The uncertainty of the refractive index 
measurement was estimated to within ±0.00002. 
Surface tensions were measured with an automatic 
surface tension meter Model CBVP-A3 (Kyowa, 
Japan), which works with the Wilhelmy-plate method.  
The platinum plate was thoroughly cleaned and 
flame-dried before each measurement.  Calibration 
was performed periodically under atmospheric 
pressure, in accordance with specifications, using two 
200 mg calibration masses.  All liquids were 
thermostatically controlled to within ±0.05 K with 
circulating thermostat water to a jacketed sample 
vessel.  The uncertainty of surface tension 
measurement was estimated to less than ±0.3%. 
Electrical conductivities, κ, were carried out using 
an QiS model M340 conductivity meter (Oosterhout, 
The Netherlands) by measuring the electrical 
resistance between two electrodes facing each other in 
the sample solution.  Temperature was 
thermostatically controlled to within ±0.05 K with 
circulating thermostat water to a jacketed sample 
vessel.  Calibration was performed periodically at 
atmospheric pressure, in accordance with 
specifications, using a 0.01 M standard KCL solution.  
The uncertainty of the conductivity measurement was 
estimated to within ±0.6 mS⋅cm-1.
