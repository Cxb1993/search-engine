控制錫枝晶以提升錫基微電子銲點機械性能之研究 
計畫編號：NSC 98-2221-E-259-007 
執行期間：98/08/01~99/07/31 
計畫主持人：宋振銘 
計畫參與人員：曾傳政、黃柏融、蔡齊航、王鼎翔 
國立東華大學 材料科學與工程學系 
 
本年度計畫針對低 Ag 含量的 Sn-1.0wt%Ag-0.5wt%Cu(SAC)銲錫合金，探討過渡元素添加
對塊材與銲點之微結構組織與振動性質影響。結果顯示 Mn 和 Ti 添加可大幅降低 SAC 銲錫合金
之過冷度、粗化共晶金屬間相顆粒，並分別生成 MnSn2、Ti2Sn3 異質金屬間化合物。值得注意的
是 β-Sn 枝晶尺寸變小但其面積率增加。含 0.5wt%Ti 及 0.5wt%Mn 之塊材試料，因具高 β-Sn
枝晶面積比例，有助於其以差排滑移方式吸收振動能，加上異質介金屬化合物提供介面內摩耗，
因而具有較高的制振性。當測試溫度提升至 100oC 時，塊材制振性增加但初始偏移量不降反增。
值得注意的是，室溫時主要為穿晶破壞，而 100oC 振動破壞裂縫有沿次晶界傳播的傾向。銲點
試料振動試驗結果顯示，銲點破壞路徑可分為沿著介面 Cu6Sn5 發生破壞的 Type1 模式，與破壞
路徑部份沿介面 IMC 以及部份穿越銲料內部的 Type2 混合破壞模式。兩模式發生的機率約略相
當，但添加微量 Mn(0.05wt%)之銲點試片因其銲料組織因具較佳吸收振動能與變形能力，因而
提升其發生 Type 2 破壞模式時之臨界破壞次數以及平均振動壽命。 
關鍵字：錫銀銅、凝固、錫枝晶、過渡金屬、機械性能、可靠度 
 
Abstract 
In this project, we have investigated the alloying effects of transition metals on the microstructure and 
vibration properties of low Ag SAC alloys and joints.  Results show that alloying of Mn and Ti 
resulted in dramatically reduced undercooling, coarse eutectic structure and extended volume fraction 
of proeutectic Sn of which the dendritic size was refined.  Those thermal and microstructural changes 
might be ascribed to the formation of MnSn2 and Ti2Sn3 intermetallic compounds (IMCs) appeared 
respectively in the Mn-doped and Ti-doped samples.  With the additions of 0.5wt% Mn or Ti, the 
damping capacity of SAC was improved because of the higher area fraction of Sn which dissipated 
vibration energy through dislocation glide and the appearance of heterogeneous IMCs which 
contributed to higher internal fraction. When the test temperature was raised to 100oC, the damping 
capacity of bulk solders was increased but the initial deflection amplitude was lowered. Notably, the 
fracture mode was transgranular at room temperature, while the cracks tended to propagate along 
subgrain boundaries within Sn dendrites. With respect to the vibration test of the joint samples, the 
fracture modes could be classified into two types with similar probabilities to occur.  Those are mode 
type 1 with interfacial cracking and type 2 of which the cracks grew along interface and through the 
solder as well.  Joint samples with a minor addition of 0.05wt% Mn exhibited better vibration life 
especially when Type 2 fracturing occurred.  That is because the greater ability for energy absorption 
of the solder structure mentioned above.

2-2-3 振動壽命分析 
  首先尋找試片之共振頻率，即由低頻掃描
至高頻(10~300Hz)，振幅偏移量最大時所對應
之頻率即為該試片之共振頻率。固定振動器之
出力值，並將頻率固定於共振點，進行試片之
振動測試，並記錄試片末端所反映之偏移量，
取得 D-N 曲線(D：試片末端偏移量，N：振
動次數)。振動壽命定義為 D-N 曲線由平臺區
開始下降時 0.95DMax 所對應之振動次數，亦
稱為臨界破壞振動次數[7]。

    圖 23為銲點 SAC及 SAC0.05Mn之Type1
振動破斷剖面，銲料組織並未觀察到明顯變
形。圖 25 為圖 23 中試片破斷面與下介面放大
組織，其中破斷面可觀察酒窩狀(Dimple)破壞
形態，銲料被拉長，酒窩底部裸露 Cu6Sn5(圖
25(a)(c))，圖 25(b)、 (d)中剖面顯示裂縫沿
Cu6Sn5 與銲料介面，以及破碎之 Cu6Sn5 傳播。
推論銲點在振動負載反覆 push-pull 下，易在
介面處造成應力集中，而於硬脆 Cu6Sn5 相發
生破斷。 
    圖 26為銲點 SAC及 SAC0.05Mn之Type2
的振動破斷剖面，銲料外觀呈現彎曲或傾斜，
以 SAC0.05Mn 較為嚴重。由破斷俯視圖可觀
察到介面 Cu6Sn5 與銲料介面，以及塑性變形
的 β-Sn，如圖 27 所示。研判銲點振動負載反
覆 push-pull 下，介面處產生裂縫後，在傳播
過程中因負載失去重心，而改變裂縫傳播路徑
轉向銲料，導致 β-Sn 產生塑性變形。文獻[20]
指出，造成 β-Sn 塑性變形，是慢速的裂縫成
長所致；研究[5]亦提及β-Sn 塑性變形，代表
能夠吸收大量的振動能，使得裂縫傳播能量減
少。由圖 24 及圖 26 與圖 19 相較可觀察到，
銲點經振動破壞後，不同方向的β-Sn 枝晶明顯
增加，且以添加 Mn 元素後更為顯著。推論 
β-Sn 為 BCT 具有多組可能的滑移面及滑移方
向，因振動產生的反覆應力，使得β-Sn 枝晶吸
收振動能後往不同方向滑移(silp)，產生層狀變
形，導致β-Sn 枝晶方向改變。且 Mn 元素添加
造成β-Sn 枝晶尺寸減少，有助於β-Sn 枝晶的
吸收振動能。

4 計畫結果自評 
本研究於本年度進行以微量過渡金屬控制
微電子銲料與銲點樹枝晶組織並成功提升機械
性能以及振動可靠度，達成本計畫實驗項目及
目標。初期研究成果已發表在 Microelectronics 
Reliability [21]一篇(附於報告後)，其它數據經
整理後除將陸續投稿至國際期刊外，由本計畫
衍生之研究構想亦將繼續進行探討。

Effects of Mn and Ti additives on the microstructure and solidiﬁcation behavior of Sn–1.0Ag–0.5Cu alloys
(SAC105), as well as mechanical properties, were investigated in this study. Results show that alloying of
Mn and Ti resulted in dramatically reduced undercooling, coarse eutectic structure and extended volume
fraction of proeutectic Sn of which the dendritic size was reﬁned. Those thermal and microstructural
changes might be ascribed to the formation of MnSn2 and Ti2Sn3 intermetallic compounds (IMCs) which
appeared respectively in the Mn-doped and Ti-doped samples. Nanoindentation tests demonstrated that
the heterogeneous IMCs produced by alloying were harder and stiffer than the inherent IMCs, Ag3Sn and
Cu6Sn5. Worthy of notice is that the elastic modulus of SAC105 alloys decreased with only a minor alloy-
ing addition due to the shrunken eutectic regions and coarsened eutectic microconstituents. With a larger
quantity of additives, an ascending elastic modulus could be obtained because of the strengthening effect
by hard heterogeneous compounds.

1. Introduction
Sn–Ag–Cu alloys (SAC) are considered one of the most favorable
systems as a lead-free solder. However, there are still some short-
comings needing to be overcome. Numerous studies have revealed
that additions of transition metals (TM) to Sn–Ag–Cu alloys pro-
vided a marked improvement in microstructural modiﬁcation
and mechanical properties, and have attracted considerable atten-
tion. Suganuma et al. [1] reported that TM elements can form var-
ious IMC precipitates and reduce undercooling in solidiﬁcation to
various extents. Kang et al. [2] demonstrated that a small amount
of Zn addition has the ability to reduce undercooling efﬁciently and
suppress the formation of massive primary Ag3Sn plates. Anderson
et al. [3,4] proposed that adding Co and Fe can effectively reﬁne
microstructure, modify interfacial Cu–Sn compounds and increase
the shear strength of joints with Cu. Kariya et al. [5] suggested that
an addition of 0.05 wt%Ni can enhance the low cycle fatigue endur-
ance of ﬂip-chip interconnections. Also, it has been reported that
for the joints of Ni-doped Sn–Ag solders and Cu, interfacial IMC
was irregular (Cu,Ni)6Sn5 rather than scalloped Cu6Sn5 and did
not show an observable change in thickness after long time aging
[6]. Song et al. [7] found that TM additions can effectively suppress
the formation of the Ni–Sn–P phase between Ni3P and interfacial
compounds in SAC solder joints with Cu/Ni(P)/Au substrate.
In addition to a reduced Ag content, a recent research suggested
that doping with either a small amount of Mn or Ti brings a pro-
nounced improvement in drop test performance of SAC solder
joints with electroplated Ni/Au surface ﬁnish [8] (Fig. 1). Since no
distinct changes in the morphology and composition of the interfa-
cial IMCs are observed, it can be assumed that microstructure of
the solders likely acts an important role in the resistance to impact
fracturing. Therefore, this study aims to clarify the effect of Mn and
Ti dopants on the microstructure and mechanical performance of
low Ag SAC alloys. Nanoindentation test was also employed to
investigate the mechanical characterization of intermetallic
phases.
2. Experimental procedures
The Sn–Ag–Cu–X alloys (X = Mn and Ti) investigated were Sn–
1.0 wt%Ag–0.5 wt%Cu alloy and those with 0.15 wt% or 0.5 wt%
alloying addition. The specimens are designated according to their
compositions and are referred to as SAC105, 0.15Mn, 0.5Mn, 0.15Ti
and 0.5Ti, respectively. Near-eutectic Sn–3.0Ag–0.5Cu alloy was
also prepared for comparison (designated as SAC305). All the alloys
were prepared by melting pure metal in a vacuum arc melting fur-
nace and subsequently remelting and casting into a Y-shaped
graphite mold.
All the specimens were soaked in an oil bath at 120 C for 1 h to
stabilize the microstructure before testing. Using an image ana-
lyzer, the microstructural characteristics, secondary dendrite arm
spacing (SDAS) of Sn and the area fraction of individual phase, were

In this study, the microstructural observation shows that the
additions of Mn or Ti to SAC105 resulted in a higher volume frac-
tion of proeutectic Sn, as well as changes in the morphology and
distribution of eutectic IMCs. In order to elucidate this, we shall
consider the solidiﬁcation history of SAC alloys. The supercooled li-
quid ﬁrst encounters the onset of b-Sn nucleation. Under this cir-
cumstance, the growth rate of b-Sn dendrites is expected to be
extremely rapid. This induces two effects. First, since the b-Sn is
nearly pure Sn, the Ag and Cu content of remaining liquid increase
substantially. Second, the latent heat for the formation of b-Sn
raised the temperature of remaining liquid. For the near-eutectic
alloy, these two effects bring the residual liquid to the ternary eu-
tectic point. However, for the low Ag SAC105 alloy the situation is
more complicated. Fig. 10a illustrates the solidiﬁcation career of a
low Ag alloy was ﬁrst a deep undercooling for b-Sn and an increase
in temperature by the release of latent heat, followed by a
descending curve which is believed to have resulted from the crys-
tallization for more Sn dendrites, and ﬁnally a small undercooling
signal accompanied by a plateau representing ternary eutectic
reaction.
It has been reported that primary IMCs might act as heteroge-
neous nucleation sites for Sn dendrites upon solidiﬁcation and
are able to suppress the undercooling of Sn–Ag–Cu alloys [14,15].
In this case, MnSn2 and Ti2Sn3 made a similar contribution and
thus the undercooling of proeutectic Sn of Ti or Mn added samples
was decreased drastically. Those IMCs had an additional effect.
Based on our results, a larger volume fraction occupied by Sn den-
drites and coarsened eutectic IMCs, we can speculate that hetero-
geneous IMCs induced by TM alloying may bring about a more
deviated eutectic point than the unalloyed one as shown in the
pseudo-binary diagram, Fig. 10b. Under the same cooling and com-
positional conditions, TM-doped non-equilibrium system, C, is ex-
pected to have a more extended volume for proeutectic Sn and to
be undercooled less for ternary eutectics which leads to a coarse
microconstituents, in comparison with unalloyed non-equilibrium
system ‘‘B” and the equilibrium system ‘‘A”.
