 III
Abstract 
 
Due to high power generation efficiency and better reliability, the solid oxide fuel cell (SOFC) 
has been considered a candidate as next generation energies. SOFC is also suitable to use in the 
community to afford power. The anode material is Nickel-Yttria Stabilized Zirconia cermet 
(Ni-YSZ). The stainless steels are one of the popular interconnect materials in the SOFC systems 
recently. In this study, phase equilibria of the Fe-Cr-Ni ternary system and interfacial reactions 
between Fe-Cr alloys and Ni-YSZ were experimentally investigated at specific temperatures by 
using the vacuum brazing method. Meanwhile, Ag-Cu and Ag-based alloys as active fillersf 
Ni-YSZ are used to connect the stainless steels and NiYSZ..  
No ternary intermetallic compounds (IMCs) can be found at 750?, and only one ternary IMC, 
σ phase, existed at 850? in the Fe-Cr-Ni ternary systems. In the Fe-16 wt%Cr and Fe-25 wt%Cr 
couples, layer-shape α and γ phases, and the island-shape α' phase were formed on the Fe-Cr/Ni 
interface at 750?. As the reaction temperature rose to 850?, only the α' and γ phases can be 
observed. In Fe-80 wt%Cr and Fe-94 wt%Cr couples, α' and γ phases are formed on the Fe-Cr 
interface at both 750 and 850?. The thickness of all reaction couples increased with longer reaction 
time and less Cr content in Fe-Cr alloys. All the IMCs growth mechanism can be described by the 
parabolic law. The IMCs growth is diffusion controlled. 
The liquid/solid reaction couples were used to investigate interface reactions between anode 
and stainless steel interconnect material when Ag-Cu alloys were used as a filler. The Cu atoms 
diffused to the Ni side, then the (Cu, Ni) solid solution was formed on the Ag-Cu/Ni interface. The 
(Cu, Ni) solid solution is as a diffuse barrier to prevent Cu atoms diffuse to the Ni side. In the 
Ag-Cu/Electroplating Ni/ 430 stainless steel system, the reaction layer is the γ(Fe,Ni) and the 
interface is smooth if the Cu content is lower than 20 at.% in Ag-Cu alloys. If the Cu content is 
higher than 20 at.%, the interface morphology became rougher and the reaction layer would 
be(Cu,Ni)+γ(Fe,Ni). 
The interfacial reaction of Ag based active filler-silver ABA (Ag-5.00 wt%Cu-1.25 
wt%Ti-1.00 wt%Al, Tl: 921?) to connect with commercial 430 stainless steel (430SS) and Ni-YSZ 
were investigated by using a vacuum brazing process.The TiO/Cu2(Ti,Al)4O could be observed on 
YSZ interface in the YSZ/ABA/430SS system. Increasing the reaction temperature and time, the 
formation phase would convert into TiO. Al2O3/Cu2(Ti,Al)4O, then Cu2(Ti,Al)4O would take place 
by TiO and its morphology became to be island shape. A Ti super-saturation solid solution, 
AlCu2Ti/Ti-rich phases, was formed at the interface of the 430SS in the short reaction time. Then, 
the AlCu2Ti/Ti-rich would convert into the Ti-rich phase in long reaction time. Three reaction layers, 
AlNi3/TiNi3/AlNi2Ti, were formed in short reaction time in the Ni interfcae. Finally, only the AlNi3 
phase could stably exist at the Ni side, and only the Ti-rich phase was formed at the 430SS in a long 
reaction time. 
Joining of 8YSZ to 430SS has been carried out using Cusil ABA filler. The microstructure at 
the interface between filler and Ni were the (Cu,Ni) solid solution; In the filler matrix, a lot of 
acicular TiNi3 phase spread over the interface. The (Cu,Ni) solid aolution was form with an island 
sharp. No clear interface can be observed at the braze/430SS interface.   
The similar results are found in the 8YSZ/430SS by using Ag50Cu48Ti2 fillers. 
 
Keywords: Solid Oxide Fuel Cell (SOFC), Nickel-Yttria stabilized Zirconia cremet (Ni-YSZ), 
interconnect material, 430 stainless steel (430SS), Ag based active filler-silver ABA
