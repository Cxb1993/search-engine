計畫中文摘要 
 
關鍵詞：有機發光二極體，磷光材料，磷光主體材料，銥、鉑錯合物 
 
 OLED 元件是否能具有高效能特性其最基礎與關鍵的要求仍在於所使用材
料的本質效能，再加上各功能性材層間的相互搭配。為提升發光元件之效能，本
子計劃將目標集中在關鍵材料的設計與合成，其主要的著眼點便是提升各功能性
材料的本質效能，再透過與子計劃二中的放光元件設計與製造來驗證材料之特
質，經由探討分子結構與光電等物理性質的關聯性，可取得增進相關性質所需的
分子設計之資訊，再透過合成策略完成更高效能材料。 
 本子計劃中策重於以下幾種關鍵材料之分子設計與高效率合成路徑的開
發、昇華純化方法的標準化、材料相關特性的鑑定分析、磷光 OLED 材料光電
特性與物理探討。(1) 高效能磷光主體材料：在此子題中將利用本研究團隊過去
幾年來對含芴基材料的開發經驗，充分發揮其高螢光量子率的結構特性並結合
Carbazole 基團或適當的非共軛骨架結構如 C、Si 開發高效能紫光同時具有高三
重態能階之磷光主體材料；(2) 高效率磷光發光材料：將利用分子設計與合成開
發具有立體阻隔效果的配位基以及新型高缺電性配位基再與過渡金屬如 Ir 及 Pt 
等結合發展可忍受較高濃度、可真空蒸鍍之紅、藍磷光放光材料，並結合子計劃
二共同合作發展高效能全磷光之全彩 OLED 顯示元件。

OLED 元件放光的主要機制為電子與電洞的再結合產生激子(exciton,即分子
激發態)。電子與電洞再結合時會產生單重態(singlet)激子與三重態(triplet)激子，
大部分有機發光材料中只有單重態(singlet)激子會放光（即螢光），三重態激子
則以熱輻射方式回到基態。由於結合機率使然，形成單重態激子進而發光的理論
效率只約為 25%，因此如何充分利用其餘 75%三重態激子發光成為近年來研究
高效能 OLED 元件的主軸。利用重金屬促進自旋與軌道偶合(spin-orbital coupling)
所產生系統間轉換(intersystem crossing)，可使受激分子由單重激發態轉換至三重
態激發態進而放射出磷光，利用此特性所開發出來的磷光放光材料近來在紅光及
綠光部份已獲致相當進展，將成為未來發展高效能 OLED 顯示器的主要趨勢。
此類材料中所引入的重金屬中心大致集中在 Ir, Ru, Os, Pt, Re 等過渡金屬，經由
合成的設計調控引入適當的配位基與這些金屬結合形成化合物，放光之波色可以
經由配位基的結構加以調控，例如增加配位基的共軛長度、改變配位基的電子特
性等。然而磷光材料目前存在的一項問題，在於高濃度激發態時之自身淬熄效應
(self-quenching)，其放光效率會因本身之淬熄效應而顯著下降，因此本子計劃首
先針對此問題提出進一步的解決方案，利用分子設計與合成開發具有立體阻隔效
果的配位基與過渡金屬結合發展可承受較高濃度、可真空蒸鍍之紅光及綠光磷光
材料。在此領域中，藍光的磷光材料之設計與開發的進展較為緩慢，主要原因乃
在於藍色磷光的能量較高，目前的發展是在於配位基上引入強拉電子基團，但其
放光波色仍為藍綠光，欲得到波長更短的藍色磷光材料其配位基的三重態能階也
需相對提升，對於分子設計上是一個巨大的挑戰，本子計劃利用分子設計與合成
為此一挑戰提出解決的方法，開發出藍色磷光材料。

 9,9-雙芳香基二芴 (9,9-diarylbifluorenes) (B2)經本研究團隊研究發現具有極
高之薄膜紫外光量子產率，良好之雙載子電荷傳輸特性、成膜特性、薄膜形態穩
定性等，具有高度應用於有機紫外光元件之潛力。在 OLED 元件中利用磷光材
料作為放光層，其放光機制可以經由能量轉移或是直接於磷光材料層中進行載子
捕捉形成電子–電洞再結合之 exciton 而放光。此二種機制所需的主體材料之特性
有所不同，若經由能量轉換機制，其所需的主體材料除須搭配其他材料層之能階
外，其放光波長需與磷光材料在紫外光區吸收特性作充分的搭配且需具有極佳的
紫外光螢光量子效率。若磷光之產生是經由生成於磷光材料上之激子，此時所需
之主體材料除其能階需與磷光材料配合外，另需具有較佳的電荷載子傳輸特性，

ABSTRACT
A novel host material for efficient green and red electrophosphorescence devices is obtained by adopting the new molecular strategy of
nonconjugated linkage of carbazole and fluorene moieties. The new host combines characteristics of both carbazole and fluorene, giving a
large-gap host material suitable for green and red phosphorescent OLEDs. Green and red phosphoresecent OLEDs with external quantum
efficiencies up to 10% have been achieved with this new host material.
Phosphorescent emitters containing transition metals are of
substantial interest for highly efficient organic light emitting
diodes (OLEDs).1 In devices using such materials, both
electrogenerated singlet and triplet excitons contribute to the

quantum efficiency are made possible.2 In phosphorescent

excited-state lifetimes of triplet emitters and triplet-triplet

are normally used as emitting guests in a host material, and
thus effective host materials are of equal importance for
efficient phosphorescent OLEDs. The development of ef-
fective host materials for triplet emitters imposes some
challenges to molecular designs. For preventing reverse
energy transfer from the guest back to the host and for
effectively confining triplet excitons on guest molecules, the

ABSTRACT
Remarkable UV OLED devices using novel spiro-configured bifluorenes as UV emitters have been successfully achieved with external quantum
efficiencies up to 2.6−3.1% and emission wavelengths as short as 370 nm. In addition, these large-gap materials have been shown to be

molecules can serve as an efficient host for red electrophosphorescence devices.
Organic materials with large energy gaps are of increasing
importance due to rapid development of organic optoelec-
tronic devices. For instance, although the emission wave-
lengths of organic light-emitting devices (OLEDs) have
covered almost the whole visible range, extending OLED
emission into the true ultraviolet (UV) wavelength is still
challenging. In addition, the development of highly efficient
phosphorescent OLEDs requires wide-gap materials as
effective host materials for triplet dopant emitters. Existing
wide-gap materials used for UV OLEDs or hosts of
phosphorescent OLEDs are mainly limited to a few func-
tionalized structural features, such as materials containing
arylamines, mainly carbazoles,1 and silane moieties.2 Up to
date, pure hydrocarbons of low molecular weight have rarely
been successful in these aspects and purposes.

Based on 3,6-Bis(triphenylsilyl)carbazole as the Host Material**
ByMing-Han Tsai,Hao-Wu Lin, Hai-Ching Su, Tung-Huei Ke, Chung-chih Wu,* Fu-Chuan Fang,
Yuan-Li Liao, Ken-Tsung Wong,* and Chih-I Wu
The recent development of efficient phosphorescent emit-
ters that contain transition metals renders possible the har-
vesting of both electrogenerated singlet and triplet excitons
for emission from organic light-emitting devices (OLEDs)
and the realization of nearly 100% internal quantum efficien-
cies of electroluminescence (EL).[1–8] In phosphorescent
OLEDs, to reduce the quenching associated with relatively
long excited-state lifetimes of triplet emitters and triplet–trip-
let annihilation etc., triplet emitters are normally used as
emitting guests in a host material. Effective host materials are
thus of equal importance for efficient phosphorescent
OLEDs. For efficient electrophosphorescence from triplet
guests, it is essential that the triplet level of the host be larger
than that of the triplet emitter to prevent reverse energy
transfer from the guest back to the host, and to effectively
confine triplet excitons on guest molecules. Such a require-
ment becomes particularly challenging when blue electropho-
sphorescence is of interest,[3–8] in which the conjugation in the
host molecules must be extremely confined to achieve a trip-
let energy level larger than photon energies of blue light (i.e.,
≥ 2.7 eV). As such, host materials reported for blue electro-
phosphorescence have thus far been rare,[4–8] and, among
them, materials based on the carbazole moiety are the only
electrically active systems that have been determined to meet
such a requirement.
For molecules to form morphologically stable and uniform
amorphous films using typical processing techniques, the mol-
ecule size must be extended beyond one carbazole monomer
to obtain bulky and sterically hindered molecular configura-
tions. Since conjugation beyond one carbazole would result in
substantial reduction of the triplet energy,[9,10] thus far there
exists only a very limited number of carbazole-based com-
pounds suitable for blue electrophosphorescence (i.e., having
both large enough triplet energies and acceptable morpholog-
ical stability).[4–7] In general, these rare cases involve connect-
ing the carbazole moieties through their 9-position (i.e., the
nitrogen atom) to a central linkage with even more limited
conjugation (e.g., benzenes or phenylsilanes), since such a

ular configuration has proven successful in improving mor-
phological stability, all these materials have left their highly
electrochemically active sites (C3 and C6 of the carbazoles)
