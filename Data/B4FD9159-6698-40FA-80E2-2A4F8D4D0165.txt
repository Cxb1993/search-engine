行政院國家科學委員會專題研究計畫成果報告 
 
模板式生長二氧化鈦奈米管陣列之染料敏化太陽能電池研究  
Arrayed TiO2 Nano-wire Growth via Template for Dye-sensitized Solar Cell  
 
摘要 
本研究計畫第三年主要是以乾淨、製程簡單、成本低廉的電弧離子鍍(Arc ion plating, 
AIP)在透明導電玻璃上預鍍鈦金屬層作為前驅層，再利用陽極處理法將鈦金屬層置換成透光
的二氧化鈦奈米管陣列電極。並封装成 ITO glass/TiO2 barrier layer/[TiO2-nanotube (N3 
dye)]/I2+LiI electrolyte/Pt/ITO glass 之染料敏化太陽電池(Dye-sensitized solar cell, DSSC)元
件後，量測其光電轉換效率，且找出一最佳化參數來作為大面積模板式生長二氧化鈦奈米
管陣列之染料敏化太陽能電池。 
研究結果顯示；以電弧離子鍍製備鈦金屬模板時，施鍍時間為 45 min，可得厚度 5 μm
的鈦金屬層。在未添加水之電解液中進行 2 h 陽極處理，可使鈦金屬層完全置換成管長達
11.5 μm 的非晶二氧化鈦奈米管陣列，內、管徑分別為 77 nm 及 108 nm。經 350oC 退火熱
處理後，可得富含銳鈦礦相的二氧化鈦奈米管陣列，使光電轉換效率明顯大幅提昇。在大
尺寸測試面積(8 cm2)下進行測試，最高可達到 1.88%的光電轉換效率。 
 
關鍵詞：二氧化鈦模板、染料敏化太陽電池、電弧離子鍍、陽極處理 
 
Abstract 
An attempt in the 3rd-year project is to construct a dye-sensitized solar cell (DSSC) device 
by using arc ion plated (AIP) deposition system to produce metal titanium pre-layer on 
transparent conducting oxide glass, followed by anodically oxidizing into TiO2-nanotube array 
electrode. Task in this year mainly focused on the growth control of anodic oxidation grown 
TiO2-nanotube on the titanium template. A DSSC full-scale device was then assembled as ITO 
glass/[TiO2-nanotube (N3 dye)]/I2+LiI electrolyte/Pt/ITO glass.  Photovoltaic efficiency of the 
device and growth control of crystalline anatase phase titanium dioxide to optimize its 
performance was evaluated. AIP known to be a clean, cheap and easy-operation process is 
potentially to be applied for growing titanium film to serve as the template for growing titanium 
dioxide nanotube and large-area dye-sensitized solar cell. 
Experimental results show that a 5μm thick titanium pre-layer can be obtained for 45 min 
AIP deposition. After anodic oxidation grown in the electrolyte for 2 h, metal titanium pre-layer 
can be completely convert into amorphous TiO2-nanotube array, which the tube length reaches 
11.5 μm with the tube inner and outer diameter 77 nm and 108 nm, respectively. Thermal 
annealing at 350oC enables the formation of anatase phase TiO2-nanotube array, which greatly 
increases the photo-conversion efficiency. Under the effective test area of 8 cm2, the device 
produced an ultimate photovoltaic efficiency up to 1.88%. 
 
Keywords: Titanium dioxide template, dye-sensitized solar cell, arc ion plating, anodic oxidation

三、研究目的 
本研究計畫分為三年期，在第一年已成功建置出高純度的銳鈦礦相二氧化鈦模板，並
且以製備最佳化的二氧化鈦模板，作為成長奈米線狀結構的平台以及電子擴散阻礙層。加
入了二氧化鈦擴散阻礙層【2】後，可讓染料激發的電子快速的由奈米線狀結構傳遞到二氧
化鈦模板，以減低電子-電洞再結合所造成的光電流損失。 
在第二年則以最佳化的二氧化鈦模板透過同質成長的方式，以水熱法生長二氧化鈦奈
米線，進而獲得具高比表面積的奈米線叢集來作為染料敏化太陽能電池之二氧化鈦電極。
雖然以水熱法製備之奈米線並未如預期呈陣列形態，但在高比表面積的奈米線和二氧化鈦
電子擴散阻礙層的共同作用下，使其所組成之染料敏化電池元件之光電效率可達 3.63%。 
本年度研究之原先計畫是利用前兩年的研究成果，以水熱法奈米線發展一大面積高效率模
板式生長二氧化鈦奈米管陣列之染料敏化太陽能電池，但已知水熱法所製備之奈米線並非
原先所預期之有序排列奈米管陣列，故改採以陽極處理法於電弧離子鍍所沉積之鈦金屬模
板上，製備一具有高比表面積且有序排列之二氧化鈦奈米管陣列。陽極處理法不僅在製程
操作簡單，並且可藉由參數來控制管長、管壁厚度，使獲得最佳化的奈米管陣列。後續再
將二氧化鈦奈米管陣列進行後熱處理，使奈米管陣列具有高純度之銳鈦礦相結構。因此，
在一有序排列之奈米管陣列與最佳化參數的共同作用下，預期將可發展出具大面積模板式
生長二氧化鈦奈米管陣列之染料敏化太陽能電池。

D. 後熱處理溫度對 DSSC 元件光電轉換效率之影響 
鈦金屬層以1 L乙二醇+3 g/l氟化銨+20 g/l水之電解液經60 V陽極處理2 h所得二氧化鈦
奈米管，經不同退火溫度處理後，封裝成ITO glass/TiO2 barrier layer/[TiO2-nanotube (N3 
dye)]/I2+LiI electrolyte/Pt /ITO glass之DSSC元件。由圖九可看出未經退火處理的非晶二氧化
鈦奈米管其光電轉換效率僅為0.44%，隨著退火處理溫度提昇，非晶二氧化鈦轉為銳鈦礦
相，則光電轉換效率隨之提昇。當退火溫度達350oC時，可得光電轉換效率1.88%。溫度增
高至450oC時，轉換效率反而下降至0.53%。 
此外，從圖九也顯示出二氧化鈦奈米管經不同退火溫度熱處理後，整體DCCS元件之
Voc會隨著溫度增加而下降，其可能是因為熱處理溫度增加而造成ITO電極的電阻提昇之故
【50-51】，故ITO電極之電阻為元件之串聯電阻Rs的最大來源。而當退火溫度增加時，由於
可促使非晶二氧化鈦奈米管的結晶性，故電子在具結晶性的銳鈦礦二氧化鈦奈米管可更直
接注入到導電電極，增加輸出電流，故Jsc上升。所以光電轉換效率明顯受二氧化鈦奈米管
的結晶性影響；當溫度達450oC時，由於會破壞ITO電極的導電性，因而降低Jsc，故在350oC
可得最高光電轉換效率。

1. Introduction 
 
With its sustainable, self-cleaning, photocatalytic, and antimicrobial characteristics [1–4], 
TiO2 has been extensively developed and applied in various fields. Immobilization of the TiO2 [5] 
over a wide range of support materials has become a key issue for wide-spread applications. A 
combination of an appropriate deposition process and an exact set of deposition parameters, to 
prepare anatase TiO2 films with a photocatalytic nature and diverse properties, has been a 
common goal for many researchers. Through wet or dry approach routes, many deposition 
processes including sol-gel [6–8], chemical vapor deposition (CVD) [9,10] and various physical 
vapor deposition (PVD) techniques – including sputter deposition [11–13], ion beam assisted 
deposition (IBAD) [14] and arc ion plating (AIP) [15–18] – have been proposed. TiO2 films 
deposited with rutile (equilibrium stable phase) and anatase (non-equilibrium metastable phase) 
structures were reported and have been found to form under certain growth conditions (or with 
additional post-annealing) intentionally manipulated to form rutile as the prime phase in the early 
development for optical purposes in contrast to the anatase (with increased attention being paid in 
this decade), a phase structure necessary for photocatalytic applications such as antimicrobial 
purposes [19,20], decomposition of organic substances [21], heavy toxic ion handling [22], 
purification of exhaust gases [23,24] and water electrolysis for hydrogen fuel generation [25,26]. 
Among the aforementioned processes, PVD techniques in particular have received much 
attention due to their environmental friendliness, the ability for accurate phase structure control 
and most important the energetic ionized species involved in the process to obtain crystalline film 
without post-annealing. Extensive research into coating condition effects on film structure 
indicated that it is closely related to the working pressure, substrate temperature, substrate bias 
and oxygen partial pressure. The amorphous structure, rutile and anatase phase have been found 
to form during PVD processes [17]. Bendavid et al. [16] found that crystalline anatase TiO2 films 
can be grown at zero substrate bias in a filtered arc system. Films become amorphous once a 
substrate bias is applied until, at –100 V, the rutile phase appears and crystallinity depends 
strongly on the energy of the arriving titanium ions. The optical and mechanical properties of 
were used as substrates for the TEM study. The deposition work involved i) argon glow discharge 
precleaning, ii) Ti interlayer growth and iii) TiO2 film deposition. The first two steps remained 
identical for all the deposition work, while the oxygen flow rate was varied to form different 
oxygen partial pressure conditions. The deposition conditions are shown in Table I. It has been 
shown that the rutile phase prevails once the substrate is biased [16,27]. The bias was 
intentionally set to zero to observe the oxygen partial pressure effect. 
 
1.2 Morphological Observation and Microstructural Characterization of TiO2 Films 
 
After the TiO2 film was prepared, specimens were studied using a MAC–MXP3 X-ray 
diffractometer (XRD), using Cu Kα (λ=1.5405 nm) radiation to analyze the crystal structure of 
the films. The cross-sectional morphology observation was established using a cold field 
emission scanning electron microscope (FESEM). 
A Philips Tecnai 20 field emission transmission electron microscope (FETEM) was used to 
observe detailed cross-sectional morphology of the films and detailed crystal structure through 
the entire thickness of the deposited films obtained at different oxygen partial pressures. Prior to 
TEM analysis work, TEM sample were thinned manually and ion milled. A significant difference 
in etching rate for the anatase and rutile phases was found during ion milling due to their 
difference in intrinsic density (4.25 g/cm3 for rutile phase, and 3.89 g/cm3 for anatase phase). This 
increases the difficulty in obtaining a large and observable thinning area. Bright field images (BFI) 
in film cross-section were first obtained, followed by selected area diffraction (SAD) patterns at 
specific areas through the entire film thickness to further characterize detailed crystal structure. 
Additionally, a JEOL JEM-2010 Cryo high resolution transmission electron microscope operated 
in HRTEM and fast Fourier transformation (FFT) mode was employed to conduct lattice imaging 
analysis and consequent structural identification.
