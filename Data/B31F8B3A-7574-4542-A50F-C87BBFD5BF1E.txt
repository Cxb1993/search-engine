Abstract
The growth of porous ZnO nanowires
(NWs) via phase transformation of ZnS NWs
at 500-850˚C in air was studied. The ZnS 
NWs were first synthesized by thermal
evaporation of ZnS powder at 1100˚C in Ar. 
On subsequent annealing at 500˚C in air,
discrete ZnO epilayers formed on the surface
of ZnS NWs. At 600˚C, polycrystaline ZnO 
and the crack along the (0001) interface
between the ZnO epilayer and ZnS NW were
observed. At 700-750˚C ZnS NWs 
transformed to ZnO NWs, meanwhile
nanopores and interfacial cracks were
observed in the ZnO NWs. Two factors, the
evaporation of SO2 and the stress induced by
the incompatible structure at the interface of
ZnO epilayer and ZnS NW, are responsible
for the formation of porous ZnO NWs from
ZnS templates on annealing in air. Rapid
growth of ZnO at 850˚C could heal the pores 
and cracks and thus resulted in the

結論
當 ZnS 奈米線在 500-600℃空氣中退火
後將生成 ZnO 磊晶膜和 ZnS 奈米線，中間
伴隨以多晶 ZnO 和單晶 ZnO 之間沿著
(0001)平面形成裂縫。而在 700-750℃退火
時ZnS奈米線將轉變為ZnO奈米線在此溫
度區間奈米孔洞和界面的裂縫都轉變為
ZnO 奈米線。孔洞的生成是由 SO2 的蒸發
所造成，而裂縫是由 ZnO 磊晶膜和 ZnS 奈
米線在 (0001)平面間的不匹配結構所造
成。850℃時 ZnO 奈米線會快速成長修復
孔洞和裂縫而生長好的結晶性 ZnO 奈米
線。
使 用 熱 蒸 鍍 法 於 Ar 的 氣 氛 下 在
1000-1100℃加熱 ZnS 和 Ge 的粉末在
650-800℃在 Si 基板上將生成 ZnGe2O4 奈
米線。當使用鍍 Cu 的 Si 基板會促進生長
Cu 摻雜 ZnGe2O4 奈米線。ZnGe2O4 奈米線
和 Cu 摻雜 ZnGe2O4 皆遵循自催化 VLS 的
生長機制過程。較高的氧分壓在 Ar 中將傾
向生成 GeO2 奈米線。和純 ZnGe2O4 奈米
線相比 Cu 摻雜 ZnGe2O4 奈米線發綠光並
且產生藍位移。

Abstract
The growth of porous ZnO nanowires (NWs) via phase transformation of ZnS NWs
at 500-850˚C in air was studied. The ZnS NWs were first synthesized by thermal 
evaporation of ZnS powder at 1100˚C in Ar. On subsequent annealing at 500˚C in air, 
discrete ZnO epilayers formed on the surface of ZnS NWs. At 600˚C, polycrystaline 
ZnO and the crack along the (0001) interface between the ZnO epilayer and ZnS NW
were observed. At 700-750˚C ZnS NWs transformed to ZnO NWs, meanwhile 
nanopores and interfacial cracks were observed in the ZnO NWs. Two factors, the
evaporation of SO2 and SO3 and the stress induced by the incompatible structure at
the interface of ZnO epilayer and ZnS NW, can be responsible for the formation of
porous ZnO NWs from ZnS NW templates on annealing at 700-750˚C in air. Rapid

The formation enthalpies of ZnO, SO2, SO3, and ZnSO4 are -83, -71, -94, and -235
kcal/mol, respectively, all of which are more than that, -46 kcal/mol, of ZnS.20 In
addition, the boiling points of SO2 and SO3 are -10 and 45˚C, respectively, while 
ZnSO4 decomposes at 600˚C.20 It is expected that on annealing in air at temperatures
above  600˚C ZnS would readily react with O2 to form ZnO, SO2, and SO3, and then
SO2 and SO3 evaporate away to form voids in ZnO. In the present study, therefore,
ZnS NWs were first grown via thermal evaporation of ZnS powder in Ar and then
subjected to annealing in air at 500-850˚C. At 600-750˚C the porous ZnO NWs were 
indeed formed. Meanwhile, from transmission electron microscopy (TEM)
examination, the viable formation mechanisms of porous ZnO NWs grown by
thermal oxidation of ZnS NWs in air were elucidated.
2. EXPERIMENTAL DETAILS
The ZnS (99.99%) powder, about 0.5 g, on an alumina boat was loaded into a tube
furnace and then heated at 1100˚C. The Si substrates were put at the downstream side 
of ZnS powder. The ZnS NWs were grown on the Si substrates at a temperature of
750-850˚C in flowing Ar at 50 sccm for 2-3 h. Then the as-grown ZnS NWs
subjected to annealing at 500-850 C˚ for 10 minin air. The microstructure of the
samples was observed using scanning electron microscopy (SEM) and TEM, both of
which are equipped with a field-emission gun. The chemical compositions of the

{0110}ZnS//{0110}ZnO, and <2110>ZnS//<2110>ZnO, forming a serrated structure,
as shown in Fig. 4. At 600˚C, the ZnO epilayers became thick and polycrystalline
ZnO grains also grew on the surface of ZnS NWs, forming the porous ZnS-ZnO
core-shell NWs as shown in Fig. 5(a), where the fracture along the interface, i.e.
(0001) plane, of ZnO epilayers and ZnS core is evident. At 700-750˚C, nanopores
with 10-200 nm in size and cracks uniformly distributed in ZnO NWs as shown in
Fig. 5(b). It is worth noting that on annealing at 850˚C in air no pores were observed 
in ZnO NWs as shown in Fig. 5(c), indicating that high temperature annealing
suppressed the formation of porous structures in ZnO NWs.
The as-grown ZnS NWs are of single crystal, one expects the rapid oxide growth
to initiate on their surface. On annealing in air at 500˚C, the growth of ZnO epilayers 
preferably initiates on the (0001) plane of ZnS NWs since the (0001) plane has the
lowest surface energy. At 600˚C, the ZnO epilayers become thick and polycrystaline 
ZnO grains also grow on the surface of ZnS NWs. A stress can be induced at the
interface of ZnO epilayer and ZnS NW because of the large difference in the lattice
mismatch, 16% for the (0001) plane, and the thermal expansion coefficient, 7.1 x
10-6/K and 8.6 x 10-6/K,21 between ZnS and ZnO. For the thick ZnO epilayer grown at
600˚C, the stress is large enough to induce the fracture along the interface between
the ZnO epilayer and ZnS NW. However, such cracks were not observed in the

polycrystalline ZnO and epitaxial ZnO associated with the crack along the (0001)
