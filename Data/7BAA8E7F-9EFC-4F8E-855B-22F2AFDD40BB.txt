1.3 研究方法 
本研究以面積616 km2之俄羅斯Komarovka流域與面積21.2 km2之美國
Goodwin Creek集水區為研究集水區。研究中首先收集研究集水區之水文紀
錄資料與地文資料，利用統計尺度理論與運動波-地貌瞬時單位歷線模式進
行研究集水區尖峰流量與面積之尺度分析，探討Komarovka流域內不同逕流
運行路徑尺度關係之變異性，以瞭解尺度效應與集水區地文特性之關係。 
本研究藉由選取河川網路之不同逕流運行路徑，探討集水區面積尺度
與尖峰流量之尺度關係，分析逕流運行路徑對集水區尺度變異臨界點發生
位置之影響。另外，利用兩段式迴歸法(two-segment regression)逐步分析沿
主流下游往上游方向考慮不同面積範圍之尺度關係，探討集水區尖峰流量
與面積尺度關係之變化情形，期能藉此瞭解尺度效應與流域範圍大小之關
係，藉以建立架構完整的水文模擬模式。

能在於數學關係、統計關係或者描述物理現象之觀測關係的轉換，而與尺
度轉換有關者則稱之為尺度問題(scale issues)。若將尺度區分為時間尺度與
空間尺度，時間尺度是依時間長短區分，空間尺度則依空間大小區分。當
空間尺度增大時，集水區水文反應將由線性關係轉為非線性關係，其非線
性關係可分為降雨之非線性特性與逕流之非線性特性，逕流部分可再區分
為地形與流力(水力)之考量(Blöschl and Sivapalan, 1995)。 
近年來水文學上的尺度問題愈來愈受重視，藉由尺度化可減少水文量
推估之誤差，以利水資源工程規劃或設計工作之進行。尺度之推估方式，
可分為增大尺度(upscaling)或降低尺度(downscaling)，增大尺度是指利用小
尺度描述大尺度之情形，例如利用10年水文紀錄資料推估100年之洪水量；
而降低尺度是指利用大尺度描述小尺度之情形，例如利用大區域範圍之逕
流參數進行小型集水區涵洞設計。假設集水區水文與地文特性相似時，利
用增大尺度或降低尺度方式推求所需之水文量是適宜的；但若是集水區之
水文與地文特性相異時，則須考慮尺度效應，以減少推估逕流量時誤差的
產生，因此必須考慮集水區水文反應之非線性關係，其中集水區的異質性
(heterogeneity)與水文過程之變異性(variability)是造成尺度問題困難的因素
之一。

3.3 運動波-地貌瞬時單位歷線理論 
由前一節所推導之地貌瞬時單位歷線理論可知，若能推求出集水區各
級序漫地流與渠流之逕流運行時間，再配合(3-12)式即可以推導出集水區之
瞬時單位歷線。本研究之目的即在探討適用於集水區河川網路之逕流運行
時間公式，冀能應用水力學理論，在不仰賴集流時間經驗公式之情況下，
完全以解析解方式推導出集水區之瞬時單位歷線，使得模式能夠真正應用
於無水文紀錄之地區。

4.1 研究集水區概述 
本研究選用俄羅斯 Primorye 地區的 Komarovka 流域為應用區域；俄羅
斯東南部之 Primorye 地區是典型的季風氣候區，鄰近山區年平均降雨量約
為 1200 mm，湖泊附近年平均降雨量約為 600mm；颱風季為每年 7 月至 9
月，其日降雨量為 150~200mm，11 月至 3 月之氣溫變化為-10~-24.9oC，其
餘月份氣溫變化為 15.4~20.9oC。Primorye 地區有 7 條主要河川，其河川長
度由 301 km 至 1000 km，其中 11 月至 3 月河川為結冰狀態，4 月至 5 月為
融雪季節，6 月至 10 月則為降雨季節。研究中採用集水區內 11 個次集水區
之水文紀錄資料進行分析與驗證，集水區面積範圍為 5.64 至 616 km2。整個
研究集水區平均坡度約為 0.0896，集水區之河川網路與水文站分布圖，如

集水區之尖峰流量通常與集水區降雨特性或地文特性相關，降雨特性
相關因子為降雨強度、空間與時間分布之變異性等等，而地文特性則為土
壤特性、入滲率、地下水位、土地利用與地形等條件，上述因子的重要性
則與逕流產生機制與集水區尺度相關。小型集水區之水文反應幾乎是由降
雨、截留或入滲主控。而於中型或大型集水區，河道與渠流效應則是愈加
重要而不可忽略。隨著集水區面積尺度增大情況下，集水區水文與地文特
性皆會影響集水區水文反應，故需將尺度效應加以考慮。前人研究顯示，
當降雨事件可達全流域供水，則表示降雨逕流關係接近線性；若是降雨逕
流關係為非線性，表示尚未達全區域供水(Leopold et al., 1964; Ogden and 
Dawdy, 2003)。事實上集水區水文反應很難達到平衡狀態，圖 4-3 顯示

變異臨界點，需要更多的資料以作為驗證之用。

之尖峰流量與紀錄大致相近。本研究以兩段式指數迴歸法(two-segment 
power-law regression method)分析集水區面積與尖峰流量之尺度關係，此法
是利用模式推估值與指數迴歸式推估值之殘差(residual)達最小，以決定尺度
變異臨界點(Goodrich et al., 1997)，故由圖 4-8 顯示集水區面積尺度在 156.73

斯 Komarovka 集水區當面積大於 294.41 km2時，在尖峰流量與集水區面積

尺度關係發生轉變，巢狀集水區之尺度關係由線性轉為非線性關係。研究

4.4 沿主流河川匯流面積變化分析 
上節利用運動波-地貌瞬時單位歷線模式與數值高程模式為輔助工具，
分析集水區之尺度關係，研究結果顯示尺度關係依線性與非線性特性，分
別將集水區面積尺度劃分為小面積尺度區與大面積尺度區。故本研究則分
析沿主流上各河川格點之集水區上游匯流面積之變化情形，以瞭解集水區
尺度關係由線性轉為非線性關係之變異臨界點(圖 4-8 與圖 4-10)。 
本研究藉由沿著主流河川格點往下游逐步分析各控制點至集水區出口
之距離，分別與上游匯流面積以及其尖峰流量之變化情形，以深入瞭解尺
度變異臨界點發生之位置。如圖 4-11 所示，Komarovka 集水區主流河川長
度為 53.4 km，由主流之最上游格點而下，當有支流流入主流河川時，該格

水區出口點 26310 m 處(圖 4-11a)，此時上游匯流面積為 156.73 km2(a 點處)，
因有南側 4 級序之次集水區匯入( A′=137.68 km2)，集水區上游匯流面積因
而增大為 294.41 km2(b 點處)。圖 4-11b 為沿著主流最上游格點至集水區出

公式(2-12)所示。 
由圖 4-8 與圖 4-10 顯示，兩段式指數迴歸分析之上半段面積大於 294.41 
km2與下半段面積小於 156.73 km2，而 156.73~294.41 km2即為尺度關係不

Background (1/2)
The dynamic of rainfall-runoff relationship is resulted from 
watershed rainstorm characteristics and watershed 
geomorphological properties. 
The nonlinearity at small scales is dominated by the hillslope 
response, and the nonlinearity at large scales is dominated by 
channel network hydrodynamics (Robinson et al., 1995). 
  
3
Background (2/2)
The linearity can be attained when the watershed rainfall-
runoff approaches equilibrium state; the linearity cannot be 
attained when the watershed rainfall-runoff doesn’t approach 
equilibrium state.
The condition of whole watershed contributing to runoff can 
be attained for some storm events in the small and midsize 
watersheds, which have high rainfall intensity and long 
rainfall duration.
4
Objective of the Study
The frequent occurrence of disastrous floods have shown the 
urgent requirement to develop new approach to analyze the 
extreme floods. 
The dynamic and nonlinear watershed response should be 
adequately simulated in considering the nonlinearity 
hydrologic response.
The objective is to develop a new basis for hydrologic 
predictions in contemporary changing condition, which 
introduce non-stationary in floods.

Theory, parameters and verification of Flood Cycle Model  
Abstract: A flood cycle model is a lumped balance model oriented to integral 
description of small river basin dynamics near the saturated conditions. The model 
reflects the processes of soil water motion and evaporation very roughly. It uses a 
variable structure of runoff generation for description of co-transformations of 
different parts of basin storage. As a result three different types of runoff regime are 
obtained: subsurface, surface (overland) and “falling through”. FC-model have a 
small count of parameters with clear physical sense and demonstrate a strong 
non-linearity. It is very promising for predictions and assessment both maximum and 
seasonal runoff of rivers with floods caused by heavy rainfall, even under lack of data.

Probabilistic methods of the hydrological extreme assessment: generalization of 
international experience  
Abstract: The topic of our report is to review different probabilistic distribution 
models of extreme discharges and methods of estimation their parameters. In the 
world there are many distribution functions is used for hydrometeorological values. In 
case of estimation of summarized runoff characteristics like annual discharges and so 
on, there are no problem as a rule. But the task to estimate precisely and reliably a 
design flood on the base of stationary, one-dimensional, homogeneity probabilistic 
model is unsolved yet. Analysis of European, American, Russian methodological 
schools is show that there is abundant different types of distribution functions and 
there is no a general physical based recommendation how we can estimate extreme 
hydrological events. The same time it become very evident that extreme series are not 
really stationary and homogeneous, and multi-dimensional engineering tasks become 
actual.

2Background (2/2)
Several general considerations motivate the approach: 
• Mountain relief cause a large variety of runoff forming 
conditions but it is compensated by homogeneity of complex 
landscape combinations in basin scale (tens-thousands km2), 
• Huge deficit of observation data results in the fact that applying 
the distributed rainfall-runoff models is absolutely impossible,
• Determinative role of intensive rain floods increases the 
importance of routing processes (drainage system dynamic) and 
decreases the importance of other aspects, like interception, 
evaporation, ground inflow etc. 
• Modern ideas about key role of river (drainage) network in basin
structure and dynamic were tried to apply, both in form of non-
linearity of basin response and existence of back-relations 
between channel and slope systems.
As a result, the FC-Model is something intermediate between 
simple “runoff formula” and real continuous rainfall-runoff 
model. It can be considered as a lumped model of the drainage 
system dynamic within basin under strong idealized conditions. 
The paper includes the model description, algorithms, the 
methodology of numerical testing and the results of the model 
verification in some river basins in Russia and Taiwan.
FC-model imitates the behavior of different parts of basin 
storage around to total moisture (saturated) conditions. 
Four parts of basin storage are described – channel, ground and 
temporary upper storages are the forms of gravitational 
(flowing&filtering) water; non-gravitational soil storage 
(evaporated) is represented as unit, formed by capillary water 
predominantly. 
Following assumptions (hypothesises) determine the 
characteristic values of basin storage parts, it’s free dynamics 
and interrelations:

8FC-model algorithm (1/3)
Precipitation are directed separately to non-gravitational (C) 
and gravitational (V) storages – kpre determines the effective 
precipitation (runoff losses).
FC-model algorithm (2/3)
Basic system of water balance equations for interaction of gravitational (V) 
and channel (W) storages. 
Connection between gravitational and channel storages is not sequential and 
not parallel but “nested’ – channel storage is variable part of gravitational one, 
relate to it’s value. Two types of recession curves and phase solution (“phase 
portrait”) are derived with the system solution.

CONCLUSION 
In this study, we present the results of hydrograph simulations in different 
climatic-topographic regions of Taiwan and Russia by using the KW-GIUH model. The 
KW-GIUH model, which can reflect the watershed geomorphology and rainfall intensity and 
can have good simulating results for different climatic-topographic watersheds. It has shown 
that the KW-GIUH model combined with the synthetic hyetograph can be used to estimate 
extreme floods in gauged or ungauged watersheds for different recurrence conditions.
