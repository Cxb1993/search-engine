用的材料，但是近幾年來氧化鋅薄膜(ZnO)的研究也是眾受矚目的議題，因為它的材
料價格低廉、相對的沉積溫度較低、無毒性且氧化鋅薄膜亦具備良好的光學性質與

阻率較高，所以一般通常會藉著摻入雜質(doping)來增加氧化鋅薄膜的載子濃度及遷
移率，進而降低電阻率，所以本計劃為探討氧化釕(RuO2)和氧化鋁(Al2O3)摻雜於氧
化鋅之薄膜的性質研究，藉著釕和鋁的摻雜，以期能獲得較佳的電阻率。
一般成長氧化鋅薄膜的方法有很多，例如射頻 /直流磁控濺鍍法 (RF/DC
Magnetron Sputter)，脈衝雷射鍍膜法(PLD)，化學氣相沈積法(CVD)，溶膠-凝膠法
(Sol-gel)：旋轉塗佈法(Spin-coating)、噴霧法(Spray)及浸漬法(Dip-coating)等。不同
方法所沈積之氧化鋅薄膜，其結晶性、電性、光學特性、表面粗糙度均有所不同，
其中以射頻/直流磁控濺鍍法最常被用來沈積氧化鋅薄膜的技術，其優點為製程參數
精確且容易控制，獲得薄膜的品質較佳，設備成本也較CVD與PLD來的低，加上所
需的製程溫度較低，在室溫下即可進行薄膜沈積的優點，故可以搭配塑膠基板的製
程，因此濺鍍法是適合被使用來沈積氧化鋅薄膜於塑膠基板的技術。且根據許多文
獻指出，以濺鍍法所沈積之氧化鋅薄膜，薄膜容易有多晶（polycrystalline）形成，
且容易具高度的c-axis 優先取向、表面平坦及透光性高的優點。
以濺鍍法製備氧化鋅薄膜的特性通常受到製程參數的影響甚大，包括濺鍍壓
力、RF功率、沈積時間、基板的種類等。基板的種類控制氧化鋅薄膜特性時也扮演
著重要的角色，因為基材和薄膜材料間晶格參數和熱膨脹係數不同，對於所沈積薄
膜的結晶類型也有顯著的相關性。故本年度的計劃使用由另一子計劃合成的塑膠基

ABSTRACT
Zinc oxide co-doped with aluminum and ruthenium transparent
conducting thin films were grown on polyethylene terephthalate substrate
at room temperature by RF magnetron sputtering method. The crystal
growth and physical properties of the films were investigated with respect
to variation of discharge power density from 1.5 to 6.1 W/cm2 and
sputtering pressure from 1 to 15 mtorr. The XRD and FESEM results
show that the films with 3.6 W/cm2 power density and sputtering pressure
of 3 mtorr have the best crystallinity and larger pyramid-like grains,
therefore the resistivity reached to lowest value of 8.6 x 10-4 and 9.1 x
10-4 Ωcm,respectively. The low carrier mobilities of the films (3 ~ 8.9
cm2V-1s-1) not only were limited by ionized impurity scattering, but
chemisorption of oxygen on the film surface and grain boundary has to be
taken into account. The transmittances of the films in the visible range are
greater than 80%, while the optical band gaps are in the order of 3.337 to
3.382 eV.
1. Introduction
Recently, zinc oxide has emerged as one of the most promising oxide
materials because of its numerous technological applications [1]. For
example, gas sensor [2], varistors [3], surface acoustic wave devices [4],
optical wave guides [5] as well as blue/UV light emitting devices [6].
More recently, ZnO is considered as an excellent alternative to replace
indium tin oxide (ITO) and tin oxide (SnO2) as transparent conducting
electrodes in flat panel display and solar cell devices [7-9]. The
advantages of zinc oxide include low cost of raw material and relative
ease of lithography. However, the electrical conductivity of un-doped
zinc oxide is not high enough for practical application. Further
reduction of resistivity of zinc oxide can be achieved either by doping

Fig. 6. Optical transmittance spectra of AZO:Ru films deposited atdifferent
(a) discharge power densities and (b) sputtering pressure. The insert
photo gives the optical band gap for corresponding AZO:Ru films.
The optical transmission curves of the samples at discharge power
density from 1.5 to 6.1 W/cm2 and sputtering pressure from 1 to 15
mtorr are grater than 80 % in the visible range. However, the film
deposited at 6.1 W/cm2 showed the lowest transmission. This variation
can be related to film structure. The film surface becomes rough as the
power density is increased and resulted in more light scattered and then
a low transmittance in the visible region. The inserted figures in the Fig.
8 and Fig. 9 are the photon energy dependence of squared absorption
coefficient,α2, for the corresponding AZO:Ru films [43]. The linear
relationship of hνtoα2 at higher photo energy indicates that AZO:Ru
films are direct transition type semiconductor. The extrapolation of the
linear region to α2 = 0 gives the direct band gap of 3.337 to 3.382 eV
for discharge power density from 1.5 to 6.1 W/cm2 and 3.352 to 3.371
eV for sputtering pressure from 1 to 15 mtorr. These values are little
higher than previously reported ZnO thin films that were only doped
with Al [44]. This is possibly because of more free electrons
contributed from Ru doping in our samples. The films with widening in
the band gap is known as the Moss-Burstein shift [45] which provide
higher free electrons that filled up of low lying energy levels and the
valence electrons required extra energy to be excited to higher energy
state in the conduction band.
4. Conclusions
It was found that AZO:Ru thin films with the optimized properties have
pyramid structure. This microstructure revealed a competing growth
characteristic of pyramid-like grains with a dimension of 100 to 150 nm
long and 75 to 100 nm width. As sputtering power further increased,
the size of pyramid-like grain was started to decrease and finally
became to a loose and irregular shape with few pores microstructure.
Meanwhile, the microstructure of the films also revealed smaller grain
and more porous morphology as sputtering pressure increased. The
optimized electrical resistivity of 8.6 x 10-4 Ωcm and 9.1 x 10-4 Ωcm
were obtained at discharge power density of 3.6 W/cm2 and sputtering
pressure of 3 mtorr, respectively. The improvement of electrical
conductivity of the films was attributed to better crystallization.
However, the relative low mobilities can not only explain by ionized
impurity, chemisorption of oxygen on the surface and grain boundary
also play a significant on the electrical behavior f AZO:Ru films.
Optical transmittance spectra in visible region of AZO:Ru films with
different discharge power density and sputtering pressure are all higher
than 80 %. The band gaps of the AZO:Ru films at discharge power
density of 1.5 to 6.1 W/cm2 and sputtering pressure from 1 to 15 mtorr
were calculated and the values were within the range from 3.337 to
3.382 eV.
