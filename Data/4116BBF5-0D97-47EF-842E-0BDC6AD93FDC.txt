四、結論與成果 
本計畫歸納出光學塑膠透鏡最大問
題為雙折射存在，藉由田口實驗設計調整
射出成形參數降低殘留應力之效果有
限，無法大幅降低殘留應力，利用塑膠模
壓製程可有利降低殘留應力所產生的雙
折射現象，經實驗設計分析後最高持溫溫
度及其持溫時間為主要影響因子，其中又
以最高持溫最為明顯，溫度愈高對於殘留
應力的問題有明顯的改善，當選擇較高溫
度的水準因子時，殘留應力之條紋級數可
明顯由五條減少至一條以下。而由模壓後
形狀精度量測結果分析，發現形狀精度主
要影響因子為有無氣冷、最高溫度及脫模
溫度，其中以快速降溫所造成形狀精度不
佳的問題可經由兩段式冷卻解決，以便獲
得良好成品品質並能有效縮短模壓成形
時間。比較最高持溫對於鏡片殘留應力與
形狀精度的影響，溫度較高雖可明顯消除
殘留應力，但形狀精度也因此下降，因此
溫度的選擇上可依實際對於鏡片成品的
品質要求做適當的調整，進而消除射出成
形中所產生之殘留應力並改善塑膠鏡片
的幾何尺寸誤差。根據研究結果所歸納的

Chung Yen Wang1, Yi Heng Sun1, Yuan Chieh Cheng2 and Pei Jen Wang1

Abstract 
This paper investigates the process of precision 
compression-molding of plastics lenses with near-zero 
residual birefringence in the molded lenses.  First, plastics 
preforms are injection molded by a conventional injection 
molding machine by net-shape molding technology.  The 
preforms are then re-heated by infrared heaters and 
compressed under hydrostatic pressure conditions for 
form-finishing and stress-relieving.  Once the preforms 
are heated above glass transition temperature, the moldings 
would release the frozen-in residual-stresses and retain the 
form accuracy via low compression pressure employed for 
shrinkage compensation.  The compression molding 
process exhibits advantages of low process costs, high 
production efficiency, and good dimensional accuracy with 
zero-birefringence.  First, experiments incorporated with 
Taguchi method are conducted for parameter optimization 
in residual stresses and form accuracy.  The preliminary 
results show that plastic lenses optimized for form accuracy 
could have p-v values smaller than 0.35 micron plus RMS 
surface roughness less than 60 nm.  More importantly, the 
plastic lenses are molded with little birefringence 
throughout the process. 
 
Introduction 
 
Since the early years in 18th century when Issac 
Newton first published basic theories for light and optics, 
many optical instruments have been invented for 
accomplishing the research goals in discovery of minute 
organisms, distant stars, light spectrum, and etc.  As the 
progress in fundamental science has been made in the past 
few years, optical lens systems have been commonly 
utilized in consumer products accompanying with high 
development of microelectronics industry.  Typical 
examples are the 3rd generation (3G) mobile phones, 
digital still/motion cameras, and pick-up heads in DVD 
drives.  Since all the above systems are mass-produced in 
thousands or millions of unit per month, it is necessary to 
choose a manufacture process producing the systems not 
only in good optical quality but also at affordable prices.  
Among the most popular manufacture processes, injection 
molding process is widely accepted by the manufacturers 
for meeting the aforementioned basic requirements. 
In the injection molding process dedicated for 
manufacture of plastic lenses, factors affecting the qualities 
of optical image are generally classified into two 
categories; the one induces birefringence in the lens 
materials, whereas the other influences the form accuracy 
in geometric dimensions.  The birefringence in materials 
defocuses optical images of the object mainly via blurring 
the focus distance.  Adjusting the injection process 
parameters could lower birefringence and improve form 
accuracy; however, it is hopefully possible to make 
near-zero birefringence lenses, especially for lenses 
without birefringence near the gate area [1,2]. In order to 
make near-zero birefringence lenses, this paper adopts a 
two-step manufacture process.  At the first step, injection 
molding with the optimal process parameters produces 
preforms with regular form accuracy; and at the second 
step, compression-molding employed for release of 
residual stresses preserves the net-shape form accuracy 
under the sub-micron scale.
