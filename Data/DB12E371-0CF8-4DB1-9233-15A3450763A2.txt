Effect of High Temperature on the Microstructure and

一、中文摘要
本研究第三年係延續前二年之研究成
果，主要探討高性能混凝土(HPC)受高溫作
用時(最高溫度800℃，升溫速率10℃/min)
之傳熱行為及孔隙壓力變化，並與輕質混
凝土(LWAC)進行比較。試驗結果顯示：因
輕質混凝土之熱傳導係數較高性能混凝土
低，故在相同位置下輕質混凝土版溫度升
高的幅度皆較高性能混凝土版小。高性能
混凝土版內距加熱面1 cm、3 cm及5 cm之
最高溫度分別為325℃、218℃及176℃；而
輕質混凝土版則分別為245℃、204℃及
55℃。另外，雖然輕質骨材混凝土之熱穩
定性較佳、熱傳導性與熱膨脹係數較低，
但相同強度等級下輕質混凝土之水灰比
(w/c)較低，其漿體緻密性較高，故可能導
致較高的孔隙壓力。高性能混凝土版最大
孔隙壓力為123 kPa (距加熱面1 cm)；而輕
質混凝土版則為133 kPa (距加熱面3 cm)。
關鍵詞：高性能混凝土、輕質混凝土、孔
隙壓力、火害行為
Abstract
The third-year research is extended from
the first two year results to investigate the
heat transfer behavior and the pore pressure
of high performance concrete (HPC) under
high temperature (max. temperature of 800℃
and heating rate of 10℃/min) and which are
compared to those of lightweight aggregate
concrete (LWAC). The experimental results
showed that, because of the lower thermal
conductivity of LWAC in contrast with HPC,
the arising temperature in LWAC slab was
lower than that in HPC slab at the same posi-
tion. The maximum temperature in HPC slab
were 325℃, 218℃ and 176℃ respectively
corresponding to the distance from heated
face of 1 cm, 3 cm and 5 cm. However, those
were 245℃, 204℃ and 55℃ in LWAC slab.
In addition, even if the better thermal stabil-
ity, the lower thermal conductivity and ex-
pansion coefficient of LWAC, the higher
pore pressure may be developed due to the
tightness of paste resulting from the lower
water to cement ratio (w/c) of LWAC for the
same strength level. The maximum pore
pressure in HPC slab was 123 kPa (1 cm
form heated face) and was 133 kPa (3 cm
from heated face) in LWAC slab.
Keywords: high performance concrete,
lightweight aggregate concrete, pore pressure,
and fire behavior
二、研究目的
混凝土遭受火害時，其微結構及性質
會隨溫度之增加而改變[1,2]，當混凝土受
熱溫度超過200℃以上時，水泥漿體中的水
化物會開始逐漸分解，同時水泥漿與骨材
之間熱變形差異所導致的應力集中，將使
混凝土強度明顯地下降，下降的幅度則依
不同來源骨材性質(如矽質骨材及輕質骨
材)以及混凝土內部溫度場的分佈情形而
定。此外，高溫作用也會造成混凝土體積
4時，以高溫爐施加熱源於版沿厚度方向之
側面(升溫速率10℃/min)，試體周圍則包覆
絕熱用之防火棉，至最高火害溫度(800℃)
後，熱源停止加熱，使試體自然降溫。
試驗過程中，採用內徑1.6 mm之不銹
鋼管，於灌漿前固定於欲量測壓力之位置(
距加熱面1 cm、3 cm及5 cm)，量測混凝土
版內部之孔隙壓力發展。試驗進行前，須
先將不銹鋼管內填滿水，使混凝土內的水
汽壓力得以順利傳遞，並由鋼管將氣壓引
至高溫爐外部之壓力計。另外，在試體內
部孔隙壓力量測處旁及試體外部，安置熱
偶計，以測得試體內部溫度及爐溫變化。
試驗進行時，同時紀錄孔隙壓力及溫度，
取樣速率為每10 sec擷取1筆數據。

四、結果分析與討論
4-1升溫歷程
高性能及輕質混凝土版內部之溫度變
化，如圖3所示。由圖中可看出，混凝土版
內部溫度會隨熱源之加熱而增加，而達到
最大火害溫度800℃後，則開始逐漸降溫。
此外，由於溫度傳遞之延遲現象，距加熱
面的距離愈遠，試體的升溫速率則愈慢，
所測得之最高溫度亦愈低，同時最高溫度
發生的時間也會延後。高性能混凝土版內
距加熱面1 cm、3 cm及5 cm之最高溫度分
別為325℃、218℃及176℃；而輕質混凝土
版則分別為245℃、204℃及55℃。
因輕質骨材顆粒內部存在較多的孔
隙，傳熱效率降低，使輕質混凝土之熱傳
導係數較高性能混凝土低很多，故在相同
位置下輕質混凝土版隨溫度升高而增加的
幅度皆較高性能混凝土版小，顯示輕質混
凝土版具有較佳的隔熱性。由數值分析所
得高性能及輕質混凝土版內部最終(火害
6會產生較高孔隙壓力。高性能及輕質混凝
土版內部之孔隙壓力發展，如圖5所示。高
性能混凝土版最大孔隙壓力為123 kPa，發
生在距加熱面1 cm處；而輕質混凝土版則
為133 kPa，距加熱面3 cm。雖然輕質骨材
混凝土之熱穩定性較佳、熱傳導性與熱膨
脹係數較低，相較於高性能混凝土有較佳
的阻熱效果，即熱-力(thermo-mechanical)
性質較佳。但由於相同強度等級下輕質混
凝土之水灰比(w/c)較低，故其漿體強度會
高於高性能混凝土者。輕質混凝土中漿體
較高的緻密性及較低的滲透性，將可能導
致較高的孔隙壓力。
五、結論
1. 輕質骨材較多的孔隙，使輕質混凝土之
熱傳導係數較高性能混凝土低，故在相
同位置下輕質混凝土版之溫度增加皆較
高性能混凝土版小。高性能混凝土版內
距加熱面1 cm、3 cm及5 cm之最高溫度
分別為325℃、218℃及176℃；而輕質混
凝土版則分別為245℃、204℃及55℃。
2. 由數值分析所得高性能及輕質混凝土版
內部之升溫歷程，其趨勢與實驗結果相
似，惟在相同位置試驗所量測之溫度皆
低於計算值，可能是因實際環境條件與
數值邊界條件的差異所導致。
3. 雖然輕質骨材混凝土之熱穩定性較佳、
熱傳導性與熱膨脹係數較低，相較於高
性能混凝土有較佳的阻熱效果。但相同
強度等級下輕質混凝土之水灰比(w/c)較
低，故漿體緻密性較高，可能導致較高
的孔隙壓力。高性能混凝土版最大孔隙
壓力為123 kPa (距加熱面1 cm)；而輕質
混凝土版則為133 kPa (距加熱面3 cm)。

Based on the load spectrum for various types of constructions during their service life, the
different classes of fatigue loadings including super-high cycle loadings are summarized in Table
1 [Hsu, 1981]. Super-high cycle loadings involves the application of stress lower than or close to
the fatigue limit with extremely large numbers of cycles (107~109). It corresponds to the long
fatigue life region in the S-N curve. For concrete structures, it has been evidenced that there is no
an apparent fatigue limit exiting. In most cases, the adequate fatigue limit could be chosen as the
fatigue strength corresponding to 2×106 cycles. In regard to the testing manner, the hydraulic
machine is conventionally adopted for fatigue tests. However, the achievable frequency of
loading would decrease with the magnitude of loading. Since the achievable frequency for
hydraulic machines for testing reinforced concrete members generally ranges from 2 to 10 Hz, it
would be time-consuming during the process of fatigue tests. Besides, the long duration is also
likely to result in a breakdown of machinery. For this reason, a substitute testing method is
proposed in this paper. The vibrating motors in place of the hydraulic machine are used to
conduct fatigue tests. Because of the higher frequency provided by vibrating motors, it is
expected to shorten the duration of fatigue tests.

Beam stiffness
For fatigue testing by hydraulic machine, the load-deflection relationships of beams at specific
number of cycles (N = 100,103, 104, 105 ,106 and 2×106) are measured and plotted in Figure 7. At
first cycle, there is a change in slope as the applied load reaches to the cracking load in the
ascending part (loading). As the maximum cyclic load (Pmax) is reached, the applied load starts to
reduce to the minimum cyclic load (Pmin). This descending part (unloading) is approximately a
straight line and within the elastic range of the beam (Figure 4(b)). From Figure 7, the additional
deflection and reduced slope resulting from fatigue loading (compared to Figure 4(b)) can be
observed. Because the start of fatigue-induced inelastic deformation is at the descending part of
first cycle, the beam stiffness at specific number of cycles should be defined as the slope of the
secant line between Pmax and Pmin at the unloading stage.
The stiffness as a function of number of cycles for all beams is shown in Figure 8. For
hydraulic machine system, the beam stiffness is determined from the load-deflection curve at
specific number of cycles (Figure 7). The tendency of stiffness degradation is similar to the
results of midspan deflection (Figure 6) with two main stages. For vibrating motor system, the
beam stiffness is tested by static loading only at initial state and 2×106 cycles. As mentioned
above, the rapidly cracking during fatigue testing by vibrating motor would cause abruptly not
only the increase of midspan deflection but also the degeneration of beam stiffness. Compare
Figure 6 with Figure 8, although the midspan deflection development of beam tested by vibrating
motors with loading frequency 30 Hz is lower than that by hydraulic machine, the degree of
decay for beam stiffness is more serious at 2×106 cycles (6.33 kN/mm for vibrating motors with
30 Hz and 9.32 kN/mm for hydraulic machine with 2 Hz). Therefore, the effect of the
appearance of extra cracking on beam stiffness is greater than that on midspan deflection.
On the other hand, due to the application of mass to provide the mean cyclic load (Pm), the
beam tested by vibrating motors is able to be considered as a dynamically simple harmonic
system. The dynamic effects can be estimated by the dynamic magnification factor (Rd), which is
principally dominated by the frequency ratio of cyclic load frequency (ωp) to system’s natural
frequency (ωn). As shown in Figure 9, the reduced beam stiffness due to suddenly cracking
would accompany the natural frequency decreasing and the frequency ratio (ωp /ωn) increasing.
