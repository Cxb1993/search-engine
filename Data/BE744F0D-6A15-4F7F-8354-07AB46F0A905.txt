In this work, the synthesis and characterization of 
the nitrogen-doped UNCD (UNCD:N) films have been 
investigated in order to employ as a n-type layer for 
developing the hybrid solar cells. Furthermore, the 
synthesis and characterization of nitrogen-doped 
nanocrystalline diamond (NCD:N) thin films were also 
investigated in order to further understand the 
effects of nanostructure including the diamond grain 
size and grain boundary on the application in n-type

that introduce of N2 into the plasma jet would cause 
the agglomerate formation of diamond nanograins in 
the UNCD:N and NCD:N films. With a high N2

films. Therefore, UNCD:N thin films were considered

developing the solar cells. The result also 
demonstrated that the MPJCVD system could improve the 
synthesis of UNCD:N and NCD:N films with a higher 
nitrogen doping efficiency for the industrial 
production in future.

In this research project, 
ultra-nanocrystalline diamond (UNCD) films 
have successfully prepared using our 
home-made microwave plasma jet chemical 
vapor deposition (MPJCVD) system. The 
surface roughness of UNCD film could be 
significantly decreased due to the ultra-fine     
grain size (< 10 nm). The diamond films 
consisted of ultra-fine dimond nanograins 
induced the increase in the proportion of 
grain boundary in films and hence the 
potential application in photonics devices 
would be promoted.  
In this work, the synthesis and 
characterization of the nitrogen-doped 
UNCD (UNCD:N) films have been 
investigated in order to employ as a n-type 
layer for developing the hybrid solar cells. 
Furthermore, the synthesis and 
characterization of nitrogen-doped 
nanocrystalline diamond (NCD:N) thin films 
were also investigated in order to further 
understand the effects of nanostructure 
including the diamond grain size and grain 
boundary on the application in n-type (buffer) 
layer for solar cells. The results showed that 
introduce of N2 into the plasma jet would 
cause the agglomerate formation of diamond 
nanograins in the UNCD:N and NCD:N films. 
With a high N2 concentration would even 
form the vacant defects in the films. The 
UNCD:N films possess higher optical 
transmittance, lower surface roughness, 
higher sp3 bonding content, and superior 
electric conductivity and carrier concentration 
as compared with the NCD:N films. 
Therefore, UNCD:N thin films were 
considered as an appropriate candidate of 
n-type layer for developing the solar cells. 
The result also demonstrated that the 
MPJCVD system could improve the synthesis 
of UNCD:N and NCD:N films with a higher 
nitrogen doping efficiency for the industrial 
production in future. 
In the deposition of diamond-like carbon 
(DLC) films, we developed a 
room-temperature process underlying the 
deposition of highly transparent and 
hermetical DLC films by low-pressure radio 
frequency (RF) unbalanced magnetron 
sputtering system with a unique target 
consisted of UNCD powders. The results 
indicated that the structural density, surface 
smoothness, and sp3 carbon content in DLC 
films decreased with increasing working 
pressure due to the increase in local heat and 
resputtering formation during the 
high-pressure deposition. The DLC film with 
a thickness ~80 nm was synthesized under a 
unique low-pressure/low-temperature (5 
mTorr/50 ℃) sputtering deposition with a 
high-purity diamond target, which produced 
films with smooth surface morphology 
(~1.1 nm rms), high sp3 carbon content 
(~65%), and dense films structure to offer 
high optical transmittance (> 90% in the 
near-IR region) and excellent hermeticity with 
low leakage current in the 0.6 M NaCl 
solution (< 1.5 × 10-5 A/cm2). DLC films 
were systematically investigated to correlate 
with the anti-reflective and protective 
performances. The above findings show that 
DLC/UNCD:N composite film structure has a 
very high potential for application in the 
thin-film solar cells as the n-type and 
anti-reflective layer, respectively, enhancing 
the performance and life-time of solar cells.

NCD:N 薄膜，目的在於探討奈米級鑽石
(UNCD 及 NCD)薄膜其晶粒尺寸與晶界比
例之變化對於應用至太陽能電池的 n-type

數固定不變情況下，改變電漿中氮氣濃度

NCD:N 薄膜結構與性質之影響，最後與

此現象。 
在太陽能電池之 n-type材料使用
UNCD:N薄膜及NCD:N薄膜進行相互比
較，當電漿中氮氣濃度為3%所成長的
UNCD:N薄膜具有最高的氮含量，N/C比為
3.25%，表面粗糙度為11.1 nm rms，可見光
光穿透達到83%，其電子傳導率為90.7 
Ω-1cm-1，而載子濃度可達 1.559 ×  1018 
cm-3，可釋出大量電子；另一方面，當電漿
中氮氣濃度為3%所沉積之NCD:N薄膜，表
面粗糙度為19.5 nm rms，可見光穿透率為
81%，其電子傳導率為7.12 Ω-1cm-1，而載
子濃度8.70 × 1017 cm-3。經由上述分析發
現其UNCD:N薄膜擁有較佳的sp3含量、較
低表片平整度、較高的光穿透率、較高電
子傳導率及載子遷移率等優點，可釋放出
大量電子，因此可提高太陽能電池發電之
16 
子濃度皆優於 NCD:N薄膜，為一較合適之
n-type層(緩衝層)候選材料。本研究之另一
重點 -製備出高穿透且低反射之 (Diamod 
Like Carbon, DLC)薄膜，使用獨特之鑽石靶
材進行射頻磁控濺鍍可再較一般DLC薄膜
製程低壓與低溫條件完成高功能性抗反射
層沉積，研究結果指出此獨特低壓低溫(5 
mTorr/50 ℃)製備之 DLC 薄膜擁有高結構
密度、表面平整度及碳原子 sp3鍵結含量，
不謹使光穿透率提升(> 90% in the near-IR 
region)，同時亦使其保護性功能提高 
(leakage current < 1.5 × 10-5 A/cm2 in the 
0.6 M NaCl solution)，在促進光穿透入太陽
電池供發電反應同時，亦可最為外層封裝
中保護材料，縱合 UNCD:N 及 DLC 薄膜
研究結果指出 DLC/UNCD:N 複合體薄膜
結構極具潛力作為新型薄膜太陽電池之
n-type層及抗反射層之應用，同時提升薄膜
太陽電池之性能與壽命。
