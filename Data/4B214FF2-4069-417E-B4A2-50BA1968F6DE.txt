摘     要 
    本計劃利用離心模型試驗，探討隧道掘進過程，周圍地盤土壤變形或應力釋
放，對緊鄰隧道的樁基礎（包含單樁及群樁基礎）的樁表皮摩擦力(Unit skin 
friction)及樁尖承載力(End bearing)的影響。模型隧道、模型樁均埋置於飽和砂
土中。本計劃探討在不同試驗條件下（包括不同的土壤漏失量、隧道中心與樁
的水平距離、隧道深徑比及樁的工作載重比），單樁及群樁基礎的軸向荷重傳遞
機制以及地盤側向位移導致的側向壓力分布，來預測單樁及群樁的反應（包括
樁身最大軸力、最大彎矩及樁頭沉陷），並提出一套評估樁基礎安全性的方法。
模型隧道（直徑 6 cm）以體積控制的方式，在 100g 的試驗條件下，模擬隧道開
挖，在不同的土壤漏失量，觀察對緊鄰隧道的單樁基礎及群樁基礎的反應。本
計畫主要探討：(1)在不同的土壤漏失量下，於鄰近隧道旁之單樁及群樁基礎，
進行樁載重試驗，探討單樁及群樁在自由場（樁旁並無隧道）以及不同土壤漏
失量下，樁基礎之荷重傳遞行為；(2) 距隧道中心不同水平距離處的單樁或群樁
基礎，因隧道開挖引致周圍地盤移動，對鄰近單樁及群樁基礎之影響；(3)含不
同工作荷載之單樁基礎及群樁基礎，受到隧道開挖影響，其荷重傳遞行為的差
異。 
    研究結果顯示，在新挖隧道正上方進行單樁載重試驗，當土壤漏失量達 2%
時，基樁承載力減少 60%，而樁尖承載力則減少 95%。土壤漏失量的大小會影
響樁身軸向力的分布、樁身表皮摩擦力的分布以及樁頭沉陷量。當土壤漏失量
發生時，樁底處的樁身軸向力會因為樁尖點承力減小而隨著減少，使得樁身表
面摩擦力逐漸發揮作用。隨著土壤漏失量的增加，樁頭沉陷量也會逐漸增加。
基樁距隧道中心軸之水平距離逐漸拉大，則樁身軸向力分布、樁身表面摩擦力
的變化量與樁頭沉陷量會逐漸變小，當基樁距隧道中心軸之距離為 10m 時，則
基樁受到隧道開挖的影響不大。

 III
ABSTRACT 
    A series of centrifuge model tests were performed to assess tunneling-induced 
ground deformations or stress releases around the tunnel and their detrimental effects 
on adjacent pile foundations in saturated sandy ground. The responses of single piles 
under various test conditions to nearby tunneling were investigated and an evaluation 
method was proposed to enable construction engineers to prevent structure failure in 
piles and excessive pile settlement. A volume-controlling method was used to 
simulate the ground loss during tunneling event in the model tests. The following 
topics are discussed: (1) Pile load tests were carried out for understanding the pile 
load transfer mechanisms at various ground loss ratios for the single pile and 
grouped piles. Two pile load tests were also conducted in a field without tunneling 
nearby; (2) pile responses caused by nearby tunneling at various horizontal distances 
from the tunnel; (3) responses of piles which were subjected to various working 
loads on the pile head when nearby tunneling for the single pile and grouped piles. 
    The test results show that a 60% loss of the ultimate bearing capacity and a 95% 
loss of the pile end bearing capacity were observed when the ground loss reached 2% 
for the single pile embedded above the tunnel center. The distribution of axial force 
along a pile will decrease with decreasing pile point bearing capacity if the ground 
loss increased. The settlement of pile head will also increase with increasing the 
ground loss ratio. The increase in the horizontal distance between the pile and the 
tunnel center will lead to the decrease in the settlement of pile head and the axial 
force and the skin friction on a pile. The tunneling will not affect the pile if the 
horizontal distance between the pile and the tunnel center was larger than 10 m.  
    The major influences of nearby tunneling on the bearing capacity of the short

1-1 緣起 
都會區施作大規模之地下工程時，將會影響鄰近建物之安全。諸
如大樓基地及隧道明挖覆蓋段之深開挖、地下空間的開發及潛盾隧道
從建物基礎旁邊或底下穿越時，都會對既有建物基礎造成不利的影
響。國內捷運工程的施工過程中亦曾造成若干的災害，在可見的未來
諸如此類的問題亦難以避免重複發生。 
國內主要都會區之地盤大部分為軟弱土層，因此在進行軟地通隧
施工時，隨著隧道的開挖過程，將造成隧道周圍地盤之應力釋放，致
使周圍土壤向開挖區移動，形成上方地表沉陷槽(surface settlement 
trough)及地下沉陷槽(subsurface settlement trough)。隧道周圍土壤移
動，造成隧道附近土壤應力狀態重新分布，此時位於沉陷槽內鄰近建
物的基礎，會受到影響進而危及結構物的安全。故軟地通隧施工所造
成之地盤變位及其對周圍鄰近基礎之影響，是值得深入探討與評估
的，期能定出適宜評估方式以供設計者遵循。 
此類工程問題之分析可用數值分析、模型試驗或現場施工監測來
進行探討。數值分析可模擬現場較為複雜之邊界條件及施工過程，並
以不同參數及條件來模擬原型的行為。但因土壤組成律之研究至目前

3.有限元素法 
近年來，無論學術界或工程界常使用有限元素法(Finite Element 
Method)來探討力學上之相關問題。理論上，只需假設適當的邊界條
件以符合基樁承受側壓力之狀況及樁體、土體性質，即可對基樁承受
側向土壤移動後之力學變化，加以研究探討。不過在分析上，除了需
有合適之電腦程式外，最主要的是需有符合實際情況的土壤位移量，
才能得到合理又具正確性的解。Poulos et al.(1997)即以 FEM 來計算
深開挖所引發之側向土壤位移，並將此位移量輸入以邊界元素法
(Boundary Element Method)所撰寫而成之程式 PALLAS，以探討深開
挖引致之側向土壤移動對鄰近基樁的影響。Stewart et al.(1993)亦曾利

(1)De Beer 法 
De Beer(1967)指出，將試樁之數據繪於雙對數座標上，可得兩條
近似之直線段，其交點則為降伏承載力。 
 (2)Terzaghi 法 
Terzaghi(1942)依照土壤力學之塑性破壞觀念，取沉陷量為樁徑
之 10%，所對應的載重當做極限承載力。 
(3)Fuller 與 Hoy 法 
Fuller 與 Hoy(1970)建議將試樁曲線與斜率 1.27mm/ton 之交點
視為極限承載力。 
(4)Chin 法 
又稱逆斜率法，將試樁曲線視為雙曲線，因此可繪製成一直線，
而該斜率的倒數則為極限承載力。

3-2-3 移動式霣降機 
霣降法是最常被用來製作乾砂試體的方法，其優點是能在短時間
內製作出試體，尤其是大型的離心模型試體。空氣中霣降法所使用的
方法一般可分為固定式霣降法及移動式霣降法，然而固定式霣降法所
製作出的砂土試體常會造成相對密度不均及大小顆粒析離的現象。
Frettiet al.(1995)指出以移動式霣降機霣降出之砂試體混合較為均
勻，其水平向與垂直向骨材顆粒間之相互影響可忽略，每霣降完一
層，所上升之高度非常小，因此砂層與砂層間之影響(Layering Effect)
可忽略，由此可知移動式霣降法可改善固定式霣降法之缺點。 
為了製作均勻的砂土試體，本研究採用由陳泓文(1998)所設計之
移動式霣降機(Traveling Pluviation Assembly，TPA)來霣降砂試體，如
圖 3-5所示。其細部構造包括支撐鋼架、滑輪組(提供z方向移動)、盛
土漏斗、柔性塑膠管(管徑d=30 mm；管長L=110 cm)、八支不同直徑

第五章 群樁試驗結果與分析 
圖 5-1 與圖 5-2 分別為短群樁與長群樁載重試驗所得的群樁試樁曲

所對應的垂直載重視為群樁的極限承載力(Qu)，本試驗所得之結果，
可推估本研究中短群樁之基樁極限承載力(Qu)約為 8400kN，而長群
樁之極限承載力(Qu)約為 12000kN。 
圖 5-3 與圖 5-4 分別為短群樁與長群樁量測所得樁身軸向力隨深度的
變化圖，即樁身荷重傳遞曲線（load transfer curve）。這兩個圖中分別
包含前排樁與後排樁所量得之結果。試樁之荷重傳遞曲線顯示，當樁
頂施以荷載時，接近地表之樁位置處，所受之軸力值較樁深處位置之
值大，主要因為基樁與土壤之間產生了正摩擦力的影響。換句話說越
接近樁尖位置的軸向力被樁身表皮摩擦力所抵抗。另外，試驗結果也
顯示基樁的極限承載力是由樁周圍表面所提供正摩擦力及由樁尖端
點提供承載力所組成，其機制通常是由樁周圍表面摩擦阻力先發揮至
極限狀態。待基樁沉陷量達相當程度時，樁尖端點支承力才達到極限
狀態，換言之這兩部分的極限承載不會同時發生。 
另外，由圖 5-3 與圖 5-4 之樁身軸向力隨深度之變化分布，外插可以
得到每一加壓階段之樁尖承載力，分別將每一階段之樁尖承載力占每
一階段之全部樁頂荷載(Qu)之百分比與樁頭沉陷之關係繪於圖 5-5 與
圖 5-6。由圖 5-5 與圖 5-6 之短群樁與長群樁結果可得知，當樁尖承
載力(Qp)占樁頂全部荷載(Qu)分別約為 40%與 13%時，則樁頭之沉陷
量會達到樁直徑之 10%，若由Terzaghi 之試樁詮釋法進行分析，此時
之樁頭沉陷量已超過其容許值。

第五章 群樁試驗結果與分析 
由圖 5-39(Etest5)前排樁之試驗結果可看出，靠近地表下 15m 處之樁
身彎矩值會受到鄰近隧道開挖之影響，而產生負彎矩。而當隧道位置
逐漸加深時，如圖 5-37(Etest3)前排樁之試驗結果顯示，因為此組試
驗之隧道深徑比為 C/D=3 之情況，其隧道中心位置位於地表下 21m
處，所以由圖 5-37(a)前排樁之試驗結果顯示，在地表下 21m 處，樁
身彎矩值會受到隧道開挖之影響而產生負彎矩，即代表，此處之樁體
變形受到隧道開挖之影響而周圍地盤變位，致使土體推前排樁使其往
隧道方向變形，因而會在此處產生負彎矩。另外，圖 5-40 則為隧道
最深之情況，其隧道深徑比為 C/D=4 之情況，由於此組試驗其隧道
中心位置與樁尖之位置屬相同水平高程，所以從圖 5-40 之前排樁試
驗結果可看出，其樁身彎矩值並無產生負彎矩之情況，其主要原因為
隧道開挖所引致之地盤變位，並無對前排樁產生往隧道方向的側向推
力，反而因為樁尖位置靠近隧道開挖區，所以因為受到隧道開挖，引
致周圍地盤變位，再加上樁尖承載力嚴重喪失，致使基樁產生巨大沉
陷，致使前排樁之樁身彎矩值並無產生負彎矩，反而產生正彎矩，主
要原因乃受到樁頭荷載與基樁產生沉陷之影響。而至於後排樁之試驗
結果可看出，樁頂之彎矩值亦受到隧道開挖之影響，其樁頂彎矩值隨
著土壤漏失量之增加而增加，即樁頂樁身彎矩值因為隧道開挖而產生
正彎矩，其主要原因乃前排樁因為下方隧道開挖，引致其承載力降
低，進而產生沉陷，所以造成整體群樁往隧道方向前傾，致使前後排
樁之樁頂彎矩值受到隧道之開挖影響而往正號發展，即產生正彎矩。 
      綜合以上之試驗結果可得知，長群樁與隧道開挖之相關位置亦
會影響到長群樁之受力行為，主要原因為樁尖與隧道開挖區之相對位
置關係不同，亦會對基樁整體之行為產生不同之結果。

第六章 結 論 
第六章 結 論 
本文利用離心模型試驗，模擬隧道掘進過程中，由於周圍土壤變形
及應力釋放，對鄰近隧道的樁基礎（包括單樁基礎、短群樁基礎及長
群樁基礎）的影響，分別探討不同試驗條件下，樁身軸向力及樁身彎
矩的分布和樁頭沉陷的變化。彙整離心模型試驗之結果得到以下結
論：  
6-1 單樁基礎 
隧道開挖引致基樁荷重傳遞機制的改變，其主要影響因素為土壤漏
失量、隧道深徑比、工作載重比及基樁距離隧道中心的遠近。位於隧
道中心軸正上方之短基樁，進行樁載重試驗時，可發現其荷重傳遞行
為與隧道未開挖時完全不同。當土壤漏失量為 2%，基樁極限承載力
會減少 60%，而樁尖承載力則大幅減少 95%。因此當隧道進行開挖，
應盡量減少土壤漏失量的產生，以確保鄰近建物的安全。 
在固定土壤應力場下，基樁距離隧道之不同水平距離會影響基樁之
荷重傳遞行為，當基樁位於隧道中心軸正上方時，基樁極限承載力為
在 x=6m 處的 0.8 倍，而樁尖承載力為 x=6m 的 0.5 倍，由此可見，基
樁距離隧道中心軸越近時，其樁尖承載力的喪失會比樁極限承載力來
得明顯。隧道正上方進行樁載重試驗時，其下方之隧道所受之壓力為
原本隧道壓力之 1.5 倍，而 x=4.5m 及 x=6m 處之基樁，其隧道要能承
受原本的 1.2 倍及 1.17 倍。因此鄰近隧道的樁基礎載重，會對下方隧
道襯砌產生不利的影響。

Responses of single piles to tunneling-induced
soil movements in sandy ground
Chung-Jung Lee and Kuo-Hui Chiang
Abstract: The responses of single piles under various working loads to nearby tunneling were investigated using centrifuge
model tests. First, the tunneling-induced soil movements and the tunnel stability in saturated sandy ground were examined.
Two instrumented piles with penetration depths of 27 m were located either side of, and at various distances from, tunnels
embedded at depths with various cover-to-diameter ratios, and used to measure the bending moments and axial forces at
various depths for various ground loss ratios during tunneling simulations. The test results show that in the case of shallow
tunneling near a long pile the unit skin frictions on the pile from the tunnel axis to an elevation of 1.5 tunnel diameters
above the tunnel axis rapidly decrease with increases in the ground loss ratio. A significant degradation of the end bearing
capacity results in a large settlement of the pile if the pile tip is near the tunnel. The depth ratio was found to be a signifi-
cant influence on the bending moment profiles along the piles, but both the depth ratio and the working loads on the pile
head determine the axial load profile and the pile settlement. A mechanism for pile load transfer during new tunneling is
proposed to enable construction engineers to prevent structure failure in piles and excessive pile settlement.
Key words: pile response, tunneling, load transfer, ground loss ratio, centrifuge modeling.

Introduction
Tunneling in soft ground is increasingly being used in
subway construction because of the benefit of reduced inter-
ference with congested traffic in dense urban environments.
However, subway authorities have also been forced to make
use of limited public areas for building tunnels because of
the high density of development and the difficulty of land
expropriation in crowded cities. Thus new tunnels are fre-
quently constructed beneath streets. They are driven very
close to or even under existing buildings. Tunneling is likely
to cause both vertical and lateral soil movements and the
major concern is to prevent or minimize damage to adjacent
buildings or underground utilities. In current design practice,
the characteristics of the structure are neglected and the ex-
pected deformed shape of a greenfield site is applied to as-
sess the building damage. Recently Burland et al. (2004) and
Franzius et al. (2004) investigated the soil structure interac-
tion effects during tunneling. Ground movements during
tunneling may adversely affect the nearby pile foundation
systems of buildings. This is because the soil displacements
and stress relief around a newly driven tunnel may induce
both extra forces and moments on existing piles and also
cause piles to experience extra settlements. Lee et al.
(1994) reported the case of a tunnel driven between the pile
foundations supporting a seven-storey building with a two-
storey basement in London. This structure is founded on
piles passing through 28 m of London clay and end bearing
on a hard soil. They found that the piles deformed in the
same manner as the surrounding soils. However, the level

Test procedures
Four series of model tests were conducted. Details of the
test arrangements and test conditions are given in Table 2.
In test series A, the axial instrumented pile was used to per-
form the pile load test to investigate the ultimate bearing ca-
pacity of a pile embedded in the ground without the
presence of a tunnel. A loading device (an actuator driven
with compressed air) was used to apply axial loads step by
step until the pile experienced a large displacement.
In test series B, the model tunnel was embedded (no piles
were present) at various cover-to-diameter ratios (C/D = 1, 2,
3, and 4, where C is the distance between the ground surface
and the tunnel crown, and D is the tunnel diameter), and tun-
nel collapse tests were conducted to investigate tunnel stabil-
ity and ground movements.
The model tunnel was connected to an air pressure line
and the centrifuge was then accelerated at 5g per step until
it reached an acceleration of 100g. The air pressure was cau-
tiously regulated to balance the surrounding earth pressure
so that no radial deformations occurred during centrifuge ac-
celerating.

increases in the ground loss ratio, decreasing to zero or

The variation of pile settlement with distance from the
tunnel axis
Figure 25 shows the variation of the pile settlement with
distance ratio, x/R, with respect to the vertical axis of the
tunnel for a long pile near shallow tunneling (Lp/h = 1.8).
Here the distance ratio is defined as the distance between
the pile and the tunnel axis divided by the radius of the tun-
nel, R. The pile settlements were found to increase rapidly
when the pile was close to the newly driven tunnel (i.e.,
with a distance ratio less than 1.5), whereas they did not in-
crease significantly when the distance ratio was larger than
1.5. Moreover, the pile experienced small settlements if the
ground loss ratio was less than 1.0%, regardless of the mag-
nitude of the working load.
Summary and conclusions
A series of centrifuge model tests were performed to as-
sess the tunneling-induced ground deformations and their
detrimental effects on neighboring single piles in saturated
sandy ground. It was found that only the depth ratio has a
significant influence on the distributions of the bending mo-
ments along the piles, but both the depth ratio and the work-
ing load on the pile determine the profile of the axial forces
along the pile. The maximum bending moment and axial
force on the pile increase rapidly when the ground loss
reaches 3%–3.5%. Shallow tunneling near a long pile indu-
ces both positive and negative bending moments, whereas
deeper tunneling induces large negative bending moments.
For shallow tunneling near a long pile, the unit skin frictions
on the pile between the elevation of 1.5D above the tunnel
axis and that of the tunnel axis rapidly decreased with in-
creases in the ground loss. More loads were transferred to
the pile tip resulting in pile settlement, especially for dis-
tance ratios less than 1.5. The pile experiences only small
settlements if its distance ratio is larger than 1.5. For deep
tunneling near a pile, the pile settlement increased rapidly
with the result that a large amount of end bearing resistance
disappeared due to ground loss. Hence the construction engi-
neer must take measures to prevent the piles from structure
failures and from suffering excessive pile settlements. We
conclude that the larger the working load on the existing
pile before tunneling, the larger the settlements that the pile
experiences after tunneling.
