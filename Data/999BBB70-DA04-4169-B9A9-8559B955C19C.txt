中文摘要 
本研究的目的在於發展一以 DFX(Design for eXcellence)為基礎的電子化(Intranet-based)
績效管理系統。 
本計畫使用之方法包括下述諸項： 
1. 正向設計鏈分析：包括有設計屬性的靜態表現機制 (Static Presentation 
Mechanism)、自動發掘跨部門 /設計屬性衝突的系統 (Automatic Conflict 
Identification System)及爭議自動解決機制 (Intelligent Conflict Resolution and 
Coordination System) 
2. 反向設計鏈分析：包括有擴大型失效模式與效應分析(Extended FMEA)及特性要
因圖(Cause-and-Effect diagram)的整合分析系統及以品質、製造機能展開(Quality 
& Manufacture Function Deployment; QFD & MFD)。 
3. 設計鏈績效評估系統：包括設計部門自我評估系統 (R&D Self-Measurement 
System)及跨部門績效評估系統 (Cross-Functional Performance Measurement 
System)。 
4. 整合上述方法建構一電子化(Intranet-based)的 DFX績效管理系統。 
本計畫為一三年期之整合型計畫，本子計畫今年之工作目標建立一個同步設計審閱平
台(Concurrent Design Review Platform, CDRP)，此平台可經由整合性優質設計(Design For 
eXcellence, DFX)架構支援新產品導入(New Product Introduction, NPI)生命週期及跨功能部
門進行同步審閱。其中，本研究以三家代表性筆記型電腦公司的新產品導入參考流程為例，
經由標竿管理手法進行比較，得到一個改良性的新產品導入流程參考模型。利用此新產品
導入流程參考模型與跨功能部門或跨審閱角度的關聯性，可以得到同步設計審閱平台的審
閱矩陣。此矩陣提供跨功能部門或跨審閱角度之同步屬性呈現及審閱的功能，藉由同時匯
入各資料/知識庫中製造、行銷及服務、採購及研發與設計等部門的限制及思考角度，使設
計部門能將所有設計出來的相關屬性有系統、清楚的呈現於同步設計屬性審閱平台上，以
有利於各相關部門或審閱角度去審閱各自相關的 DFX屬性，不同的部門可能看到不一樣的
屬性組合。 
本研究之貢獻包括： 
(1) 提供一個改良式的新產品導入流程參考模型，其目的在改良現有新產品導入流
程中因同步多面向的優質設計審閱(simultaneous multiple DFX reviews)之無效率
而衍生的研發問題。 
(2) 建立一個以生命週期及流程為基礎的審閱平台，此平台在網路環境中運作並支
援跨功能部門及跨審閱角度在跨地域環境下作業。 
關鍵字：優質設計、同步設計審閱、新產品導入參考流程

2.2 設計考量組裝(Design for assembly；DFA) 
DFA最早是由 Boothroyd et al.在 1980年代開始研究，其理論基礎是基於「最低的組裝
成本可經由 DFA 的產品設計方式產生」 的假設前提而來，並認為組裝系統的運作成本主
要受產品的零件總數及零件的組裝的難易度兩項因素的影響，而 Boothroyd el al. 所開發的
DFA導入方法概述如下： 
1. 設計利用簡單的規則重新檢討現有的零組件，決定現有的零組件間的獨立性是否需
保留或進行整合為模組化零件，但在進行過程中，設計人員需將理由予以記錄。 
2. 必須利用工作研究的方法建立標準工時資料庫以利估計正確的組裝時間。 
DFA的另一考慮方向為採用模組化的設計，這是一種利用單一或少數幾項功能之零件
組或使用標準化零件，設計時兼顧不同顧客需求但設計相同的組裝介面使整體系統又不會
變動太大的一種設計方式，此種設計方式在系統組裝業者常又將其稱為延遲產品差異化
(Delayed product differentiation, DPD)設計(He, Kusiak, & Tseng, 1998)，其零組件設計原則如

2.4 設計考量製造/組裝(Design for Manufacture/Assembly, DFM/A) 
Boothroyd et al. (1992)提供一個設計考量製造/組裝(DFM/A)的七步驟導入實務程序(流
程如圖 4所示)： 
1. 設計人員進行概念設計； 
2. 進行 DFA，其目的在使產品結構單純化，然後將產品在結構或是組裝上的建議回
饋至概念設計； 
3. 進行物料及流程的選擇並進行初步的成本評估，而後將成本分析的結果和回饋建
議回饋設計單位進行設計概念之修改； 
4. 進行選擇一最佳設計概念時的評選； 
5. 開始導入 DFM/A，針對最小製造成本，進行細部的評估與設計，然後將結果回饋
至概念設計。 
6. 完成 DFA與 DFM之後，開始進行產品原型(Prototype)發展； 
7. 進行工程變更(Engineering Change Notice, ECN)，完成設計，進行量產。

2. 標竿管理(Benchmarking Management)： 
標竿管理最早起源於 1970年代末期的 Xerox公司，當時 Xerox公司在影印機市場上失
去其領導地位，故該公司在 1979年開始對其製造成本實施標竿管理，並針對製造活動進行
產品品質及特性之改進計畫。當在製造活動標竿管理成功之後，Xerox 更進一步將其運用
至各項企業活動，使 Xerox 在市場上再度取得優勢地位。此時許多美國企業也都感受到強
大的競爭壓力，同時也體會到領先群雄的重要，紛紛起而效尤(Boxwell, 1994; Bogan & 
English, 1994; Karlöf & Östblom, 1993; Spendolini , 1992; Zairi, 1996)。 
當標竿管理廣泛地被應用在不同行業、不同公司中時，常常會將「標竿」的定義改寫
成各種不同的面貌，但它們所代表的內涵卻都是相同的：標竿是一項持續的過程，用來評
估企業本身的活動或作業，並將其與最佳之企業相互比較，嘗試以有系統、有組織的方式
學習其經驗，以期與之並駕齊驅，甚至超越之標竿制度的種類依有不同的分類方法
(Spendolini, 1992)。 
1. 依標竿制度內容來分類 
(1) 策略性的標竿制度：藉由分析競爭對手的績效和策略，尋找改善的契機與策
略，重點在確認何項流程必須進行改善。

3. 田口方法(Taguchi Method)： 
田口博士對產品品質的定義為「產品在使用期間對社會所造成的損失最小」，所以產品
製造者的社會責任為必須儘量做出對社會造成損失最小的產品，因此，品質的要求不應僅
是合於規格而已，還須符合以下兩項條件： 
(1). 品質特性值與目標值一致； 
(2). 品質特性之變異越小越好。  
根據上述品質定義，Taguchi(1993)將設計區分為(1).系統設計(system design)；(2).參數
設計(parameter design)及(3).容差設計(tolerance design)等三個階段，並對各階段的設計特性
說明如下 (Kackar, 1985; Phadke, 1989)： 
(1). 系統設計階段：在此階段，設計人員主要是以科學或工程的原則決定產品的架構。 
(2). 參數設計階段：在此階段，設計人員將決定產品的工程規範(如尺寸、規格…等)。 
(3). 容差設計階段：在此階段，設計人員將決定產品參數的公差。

process development, of design issues associated with environment safety and health over the 
product life-cycle)

三項歐盟的環保法規「強制」規定筆記型電腦生產廠商須在設計時考量環保
的需求： 
(1). 2004 年 8 月歐盟會員國將批准「廢棄電子設備法(Waste Electrical and 
Electronic Equipment (WEEE) Directive )」，緩衝期為 1年; 
(2). 2005年 8月起，歐盟將強制 PC製造業者負起電腦廢棄物處理的多數責任。 
(3). 2006年 7月起，歐盟會員國將實施「有毒物質限制法 (Toxic Substances 
Control Act, TSCA)」，法規中明文規範電子設備含有毒物質的上限水準。 
為配合上述三項法令，各國亦紛紛制定類似的環保法規。在這些規定下，預期將對筆
記型電腦的經營模式(Business Model)及研發重點產生下列的影響： 
1. 往後電腦製造業者必須針對自家生產的電腦一一做紀錄，售出後還要持續追蹤後
端的處理回收情況，因主管機關將針對各廠商的市佔率課徵回收費用。 
2. 公司將在研發業務中加重環保相關事項，而其目的是期望藉由設計的創新而降低
產品在其生命週期中所造成的環境人體傷害，甚或在研發專案進行前即針對將會
使用到的研發及製程技術進行環境影響衝擊評估，因此近年來有許多研究人員投
入設計考量環境(Leonard 1991)、設計考量回收能力(Henstock 1988)。 
3. 由於無鉛(Pb-free)銲料無論是在學術上或是商業上的應用目前均不成熟，但因銲
接仍是筆記型電腦組不可缺少的製程工序之一，使用無鉛銲料除造成錫膏原材料
成本之增加外，更增加製程的不良率，故目前有相當多的人員投入無鉛銲料的開
發及商業化應用的研發工作上。

A  Model for Preventive Maintenance Operation and Forecasting  
 
D. Daniel Sheu1, Jun Yuan Kuo1 and His-Lo Lo2 
1. Department of Industrial Engineering & Engineering Management National Tsing Hua University 
Hsinchu, Taiwan, R.O.C. 30013 
2. Powerchip Semiconductor Company 
Hsinchu, Taiwan, R.O.C. 30013 
 
 
Abstract 
Equipment costs constitute the greatest majority of overall manufacturing costs for Semiconductor Therefore, 
maintaining high equipment availability has been regarded as one of the major goals in the industry. The ability to 
forecast correctly equipment preventive maintenance (PM) timing requirements not only can help optimizing equipment 
uptime but also minimizing negative impacts on manufacturing production efficiency. This research used grey theory 
and evaluation diagnosis to construct a PM forecasting model for prediction of PM timing of various machines. The 
results showed significant improvement of PM timing predictions by accumulated wafers processed (＃of wafers) . The 
overall accuracy average is 93.30% and the mean day error is 1 day, which is significantly more accurate than a recently 
published method proposed by Li & Chang [1].  
 
Keywords：Preventive Maintenance Forecast, Grey Forecast Theory, Wafer Manufacture, Equipment Maintenance  
 
1. INTRODUCTION 
Semiconductor equipment is very expensive and constitutes a high proportion of the manufacturing cost. Therefore, the 
full usage of equipment is an important goal for wafer fabs. When repair or maintenance takes place, equipment will be 
idled and the utilization reduced so does the production capacity impacted.  
 
One issue of concern in PM is when and how long to maintain what pieces of equipment so that the machines’ 
production capability can be kept at their optimal situation while the impact of machine unavailability on production can 
be minimized. Prediction of the needed PM timing is critical in this regard. Yet, the timing for a piece of equipment to 
perform needed PM may not be straight-forward. It depends on the operational mode of the equipment, window of 
allowance for the PM timing, loading of the particular machine, resource availability of maintenance staff, etc. Different 
types of machines have different measures to identify when the machine shall be maintained. In a typical semiconductor 
fabrication plant, thin film equipment such as chemical vapor deposition (CVD) machines and physical vapor deposition 
(PVD) machines may use “number of cumulated wafer processed (in pieces)” as the measure for PM timing; Implanters 
may use total power wattage applied since last PM as the measure; Furnace may use accumulated film thickness (in nano 
meters) as the measure, etc.  
 
The issue is to predict the timing for a set measure of PM specification at certain machine within a given window so that 
associated resources can be arranged and the impact on production can be minimized. 
This paper proposes a model to predict the PM timing for machines based on cumulated number of wafer processed in 
pieces (pcs) since last PM. The same idea may also be extended to other measures of maintenance requirements.  
 
Past studies on the timing of preventive maintenance, such as Love and Guo [2], Jayabalan and Chaudhuri [3], Wu and 
Jonathan [4], Lee [5], Guo [6], Park [7], Marquez & Heguedas [8], and Degbotse [9] are for general industrial facilities 
(aging, failed and general equipment) instead of the PM for machines in various wafer fabs. According to Li & Chang 
[1], most PMs are performed based on the personal experience and weighted average. Inaccurate predictions are 
common and different people make different judgments.  
 
Inaccurate forecast of facility maintenance will affect preventive maintenance arrangements and may give rise to 
conflicts between production personnel who needs equipment up for production and maintenance personnel who needs 
to takedown equipment from maintenance. Accurate prediction of the PM timing requirements is important not only to 
maintain the optimal life-cycle productivity of the equipment but also to alleviate the conflicts between the production 
people and the maintenance people. The current practice of PM is shown in Figure 1. As a result, interdepartmental 
conflicts arise easily and the PM timing proposed by different personnel will be quite different.

2.1 Grey model for machine PM forecast 
The grey theory was proposed by Deng [10]. It is good for relational analysis and model construction when the 
system model is uncertain and data are incomplete. The grey theory has been successfully applied to many different 
fields, such as environmental engineering, agriculture, traffic, meteorology, engineering, transportation, economics, 
medicine, education, geology, management, physical education etc. [11] In this research, the Authors used a grey 
forecasting method based on the GM (1,1) model. It is a forecasting model constructed to find out the future dynamic 
correlation and development pattern of series of data with small number of data available. [11].

2.2 Predicting the timing for next PM 
Predicting the timing for next PM when the equipment reaches the PM specification is simply by accumulating the 
predicted wafer moves in each period since last PM until the PM specification is reached. In this method, the MFK value, 
the accumulative # of wafers processed in period k, is calculated by obtaining the forecast of period )(ˆ(0) kx  in Stage 1. 
If MAk < MPM, calculate )1(ˆ(0) +kx  again with the calculation formula of the grey forecast model. If MF (k+1) > MPM 
consider the PM execution of the period. If MF (k+1) < MPM, calculate )2(ˆ(0) +kx  again with the calculation formula of the 
grey forecast model and so on.
